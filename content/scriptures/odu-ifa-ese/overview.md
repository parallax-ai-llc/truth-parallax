## Overview

The Ese Ifá (Ifá verses) constitute the vast oral literary corpus of the Yoruba Ifá divination system, one of the most elaborate and intellectually sophisticated systems of knowledge in the world. The Ese Ifá are sacred poems, narratives, proverbs, and prescriptions associated with the 256 Odu (sacred signs or figures) of the Ifá divination system. Each Odu contains numerous Ese — ranging from brief proverbs to elaborate mythological narratives — that are memorized and recited by Ifá priests (Babalawo, "Father of Secrets") during divination consultations.

The Ifá system, centered on the deity Orunmila (also called Ifá), is a comprehensive system of knowledge encompassing theology, philosophy, medicine, history, ethics, and cosmology. UNESCO recognized the Ifá divination system as a Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity in 2005. The Ese Ifá collectively constitute an encyclopedic oral text — potentially hundreds of thousands of verses — that transmits Yoruba wisdom across generations.

The Ese Ifá tradition remains vibrant not only in Yorubaland (southwestern Nigeria and Benin Republic) but also in the African diaspora, where it forms the foundation of related traditions including Cuban Santería/Lucumí, Brazilian Candomblé, and Trinidad Orisha.

## Structure

The Ese Ifá are organized around the 256 Odu Ifá, arranged in a hierarchical system:

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| 1 | The Sixteen Major Odu (Olodu/Oju Odu) — the sixteen principal sacred signs |
| 2 | The 240 Minor Odu (Omo Odu/Amulu Odu) — combinations of the major signs |
| 3 | Creation narratives — the role of Orunmila in the creation of the world |
| 4 | Ethical teachings — proverbs, moral instruction, and social wisdom |
| 5 | Medicinal and ritual prescriptions — ebo (sacrifice) and healing knowledge |
| 6 | Historical narratives — accounts of ancient kings, wars, and migrations |
| 7 | Philosophical discourses — cosmology, destiny (ori), and the nature of existence |
| 8 | Diaspora traditions — the transmission and adaptation of Ifá in the Americas |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Indigenous**: A living oral tradition central to Yoruba religious practice and cultural identity
- **Academic**: A major subject of African studies, oral literature, and comparative philosophy
- **Comparative**: Parallels with other divination systems (I Ching, geomancy) and encyclopedic oral traditions
- **Theological**: Contains sophisticated theological reflection on creation, destiny, and divine-human interaction
- **Anthropological**: Central to understanding Yoruba social organization, healing practices, and knowledge systems
- **Interfaith**: Represents a major world religious tradition deserving of equal recognition in global dialogue

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Divination | The systematic consultation of Orunmila for guidance in human affairs |
| Ori (Destiny) | The concept of individual destiny chosen before birth and its negotiation through life |
| Ebo (Sacrifice) | Ritual offerings as the means of aligning human will with divine purpose |
| Oral Literature | The Ese as a vast corpus of poetry, narrative, and wisdom transmitted orally |
| Orunmila | The deity of wisdom and divination, witness to creation |
| Community | The role of Ifá in governing social relations, conflict resolution, and communal life |
