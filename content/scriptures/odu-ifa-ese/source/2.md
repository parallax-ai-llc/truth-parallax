---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 2"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Oyeku Meji, the second Odu, speaks of darkness and transformation, of death and rebirth.

**2** "When darkness covers the earth, the wise man does not despair. He knows that after the night comes the dawn."

**3** "Oyeku Meji divined for the Night before Night came into being. Night was told: 'You shall cover the earth, but you shall not prevail forever. The Day shall always follow you.'"

**4** "And Night accepted her role, knowing that rest is as necessary as labor, and that the seed germinates in darkness before it sprouts into the light."

**5** "When Oyeku Meji appears in divination, it speaks of endings that lead to new beginnings. It says: 'Do not fear the darkness. Within it lies the seed of your transformation.'"

**6** "It was Oyeku Meji who taught the ancestors the rites of the dead — how to honor those who have crossed to the realm of the ancestors, how to seek their guidance, how to maintain the bond between the living and the departed."

**7** "The ancestors are not gone. They live in the wind, in the water, in the earth. They speak through the rustling of leaves and the voices of children."

**8** "Oyeku Meji warns: 'He who forgets his ancestors forgets himself. He who dishonors the dead dishonors the living.'"

**9** "Sacrifice: a black cloth, kola nuts, and cool water offered at the crossroads at nightfall. This is the offering that opens the way."

**10** "The babalawo chants: 'Oyeku Meji, owner of the night, owner of the mysteries, open the way for your child. Let the darkness be a womb, not a grave.'"

**11** These are the words of Oyeku Meji, the Odu of transformation and ancestral wisdom.
