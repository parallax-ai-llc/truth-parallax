---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 1"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Orunmila, the witness of fate, the historian of Ife, the great diviner who was present when Olodumare created the universe, speaks through the sacred palm nuts.

**2** When the world was young, Olodumare, the Supreme Being, sent sixteen Irunmole to the earth to establish order. Among them was Orunmila, the deity of wisdom and divination.

**3** Orunmila brought with him the sacred system of Ifa — the corpus of verses that contains all knowledge, all wisdom, all solutions to human problems.

**4** The sixteen principal Odu are the foundation of Ifa: Eji Ogbe, Oyeku Meji, Iwori Meji, Odi Meji, Irosun Meji, Owonrin Meji, Obara Meji, Okanran Meji, Ogunda Meji, Osa Meji, Ika Meji, Oturupon Meji, Otura Meji, Irete Meji, Ose Meji, and Ofun Meji.

**5** From these sixteen principal Odu arise two hundred and forty derivative Odu, making a total of two hundred and fifty-six. Each Odu contains hundreds of Ese — verses of wisdom and narrative.

**6** Eji Ogbe, the first Odu, speaks: "The day breaks with certainty. The sun rises without fail. Olodumare keeps His promise to the world."

**7** "It was Eji Ogbe who divined for the sun before the sun chose to bring light to the world. The sun was told to sacrifice, and the sun sacrificed. Since that day, the sun has never failed to rise."

**8** "The babalawo who consults Ifa walks in the light of Orunmila. He sees what others cannot see. He knows what others cannot know."

**9** "When Eji Ogbe appears in divination, it speaks of new beginnings, of leadership, of the dawn of good fortune. It says: 'Your path is clear. Walk forward with confidence.'"

**10** "But it also warns: 'He who sees the dawn and does not give thanks will not see the next dawn.' Gratitude is the foundation of all blessing."

**11** These are the words of Orunmila, passed from babalawo to babalawo, from generation to generation, since the beginning of the world.
