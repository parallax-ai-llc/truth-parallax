---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 4"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Odi Meji, the fourth Odu, speaks of obstacles, boundaries, and the feminine principle.

**2** "The door that is closed protects what is within. Not every door should be opened. Not every secret should be told."

**3** "It was Odi Meji who divined for the tortoise before the tortoise received its shell. The tortoise was told: 'Carry your house upon your back. Protect yourself. Not all who smile are friends.'"

**4** "The tortoise sacrificed, and received its shell. And to this day, the tortoise carries its protection wherever it goes."

**5** "When Odi Meji appears, it says: 'Be cautious. There are obstacles on your path, but they are there for your protection. Do not remove the fence until you know why it was built.'"

**6** "Odi Meji governs the mysteries of women — the cycles of the moon, the power of the womb, the wisdom of the mothers."

**7** "The great mothers — Iya Mi, the ancient ones — are honored in this Odu. Without their blessing, nothing prospers. Without their permission, no child is born."

**8** "He who disrespects women disrespects the source of all life. The earth herself is a woman, and she nourishes all."

**9** "Odi Meji warns: 'Close the door against gossip. Close the door against envy. Close the door against evil. But open the door to wisdom, to generosity, and to love.'"

**10** "Sacrifice: a hen, red palm oil, and bitter kola, offered to the mothers at the foot of an ancient tree."

**11** These are the words of Odi Meji, the Odu of protection, boundaries, and the sacred feminine.
