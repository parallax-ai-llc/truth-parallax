---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 5"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Irosun Meji, the fifth Odu, speaks of blood, lineage, and the power of sacrifice.

**2** "Blood is life. Blood is memory. Blood carries the stories of the ancestors from generation to generation."

**3** "It was Irosun Meji who divined for the palm tree before the palm tree came to earth. The palm tree was told: 'You shall give life to all. From your fruit shall come oil. From your sap shall come wine. From your fronds shall come shelter.'"

**4** "The palm tree sacrificed, and it became the tree of life among the Yoruba. Every part of the palm tree serves a purpose."

**5** "When Irosun Meji appears, it speaks of heredity and destiny. It says: 'The blood remembers what the mind forgets. Honor your lineage, for it has made you who you are.'"

**6** "But it also says: 'You are not bound by the mistakes of your ancestors. Each generation has the power to heal the wounds of the past.'"

**7** "Irosun Meji teaches the power of sacrifice — not as loss, but as exchange. You give something of value, and you receive something of greater value."

**8** "The greatest sacrifice is the sacrifice of selfishness. He who gives freely receives abundantly. He who hoards loses everything."

**9** "Irosun Meji warns: 'Do not spill blood carelessly. Every drop of blood cries out to the earth, and the earth remembers.'"

**10** "Sacrifice: palm oil, roasted yam, and a rooster, offered at the shrine of the ancestors with songs of remembrance."

**11** These are the words of Irosun Meji, the Odu of blood, sacrifice, and ancestral memory.
