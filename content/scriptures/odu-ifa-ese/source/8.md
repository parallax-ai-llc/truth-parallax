---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 8"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Okanran Meji, the eighth Odu, speaks of conflict, courage, and the warrior spirit.

**2** "There are battles that must be fought. Not every conflict can be avoided. But the wise warrior fights only when necessary, and even then, fights with honor."

**3** "It was Okanran Meji who divined for Ogun, the deity of iron and war, before Ogun cleared the path from heaven to earth. Ogun was told: 'Your strength shall open the way, but your wisdom must know when to rest the sword.'"

**4** "Ogun sacrificed, and he cleared the path through the primeval forest with his iron machete. And all the Orishas followed behind him on the road he had made."

**5** "When Okanran Meji appears, it speaks of the need for decisive action. It says: 'The time for deliberation has passed. Act now, or the opportunity will be lost.'"

**6** "But it also says: 'Do not use your strength to oppress the weak. The truly strong man protects the vulnerable. The truly powerful man lifts up the fallen.'"

**7** "Okanran Meji teaches the way of the warrior: courage without cruelty, strength without arrogance, resolve without stubbornness."

**8** "It speaks of Ogun's oath: 'I shall fight for justice. I shall protect the innocent. I shall clear the path for those who come after me.'"

**9** "The conflict spoken of is not always physical. It may be the inner battle against one's own weaknesses, against laziness, against fear, against despair."

**10** "Okanran Meji warns: 'He who draws the sword without cause shall perish by the sword. But he who sheathes the sword when justice demands action is a coward.'"

**11** "Sacrifice: a rooster, palm wine, and iron implements, offered at the shrine of Ogun at the edge of the forest."

**12** These are the words of Okanran Meji, the Odu of courage, conflict, and righteous action. Thus concludes the first cycle of the Ese Ifa, the verses of the sacred oracle of the Yoruba people.
