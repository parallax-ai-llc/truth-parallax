---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 6"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Owonrin Meji, the sixth Odu, speaks of change, disruption, and the unexpected turns of fate.

**2** "The river does not flow in a straight line. It bends and turns, and yet it reaches the sea. So it is with the path of destiny."

**3** "It was Owonrin Meji who divined for the whirlwind before the whirlwind came to earth. The whirlwind was told: 'You shall disrupt and disturb, but from your disruption shall come renewal.'"

**4** "The whirlwind clears the dead branches so that new growth may come. The flood washes away the old so that the new may take root."

**5** "When Owonrin Meji appears, it says: 'Expect the unexpected. Your plans may be disrupted, but the disruption carries a hidden blessing. What seems like destruction is preparation.'"

**6** "Owonrin Meji teaches that chaos is not the opposite of order but the prelude to a new order. The seed must break open before the plant can grow."

**7** "It speaks of trickster energy — the power of Eshu, the divine messenger, who stands at the crossroads and turns things upside down."

**8** "Eshu is not evil. Eshu is the principle of change, of communication, of the unexpected. Without Eshu, the world would be stagnant."

**9** "Owonrin Meji warns: 'Do not be rigid. Do not cling to the old ways when the new ways are calling. The tree that bends survives the storm; the tree that is rigid breaks.'"

**10** "Sacrifice: a rooster, palm wine, and cowrie shells, offered at the crossroads to Eshu, the opener of the way."

**11** These are the words of Owonrin Meji, the Odu of change, disruption, and unexpected blessing.
