---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 3"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Iwori Meji, the third Odu, speaks of inner vision and the contrast between appearance and reality.

**2** "The eye that sees outward sees the world. The eye that sees inward sees the truth. Iwori Meji opens the inner eye."

**3** "It was Iwori Meji who divined for the chameleon before the chameleon came to earth. The chameleon was told: 'You shall see in all directions at once. But do not be so busy looking outward that you forget to look within.'"

**4** "The chameleon sacrificed and came to earth. That is why the chameleon can look forward and backward at the same time — but it walks slowly, for it is always contemplating."

**5** "When Iwori Meji appears, it says: 'Things are not what they seem. Look again. Look deeper. The truth is hidden beneath the surface.'"

**6** "A man who appeared wealthy was poor in character. A woman who appeared poor was rich in wisdom. Iwori Meji teaches: 'Do not judge by appearances.'"

**7** "The inside of the house is more important than the outside. The character of a person is more important than the clothing."

**8** "Iwori Meji also speaks of the womb — the inner chamber where life begins, where secrets are kept, where the future is formed."

**9** "It warns against hypocrisy: 'The mouth that blesses by day and curses by night will be exposed. Ifa sees all.'"

**10** "Sacrifice: two pigeons, palm oil, and prayers spoken facing the rising sun. Let the inner and outer be made one."

**11** These are the words of Iwori Meji, the Odu of inner vision and hidden truth.
