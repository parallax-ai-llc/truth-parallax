---
title: "Ese Ifa - Chapter 7"
scripture: "odu-ifa-ese"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Obara Meji, the seventh Odu, speaks of abundance, generosity, and the power of the spoken word.

**2** "The word spoken with authority creates reality. The curse spoken in anger destroys. The blessing spoken in love builds up."

**3** "It was Obara Meji who divined for the mouth before the mouth came to dwell in the face. The mouth was told: 'You shall be the gateway between the inner world and the outer world. Guard well what passes through you.'"

**4** "For the mouth can bless or curse, nourish or poison, build or destroy. The mouth is the most powerful weapon and the most powerful healer."

**5** "When Obara Meji appears, it speaks of prosperity and the proper use of wealth. It says: 'Wealth that is shared multiplies. Wealth that is hoarded diminishes.'"

**6** "The generous man is like the rain that falls on all fields equally. The miser is like the drought that kills everything it touches."

**7** "Obara Meji teaches that true wealth is not in money or possessions but in relationships, in community, in the bonds of love and mutual support."

**8** "A man who has a thousand cowries but no friends is poorer than a man who has one friend but no cowries."

**9** "Obara Meji warns: 'Watch your tongue. The word that escapes the mouth cannot be called back. It flies like an arrow, and once released, it strikes its target.'"

**10** "Sacrifice: kola nuts, honey, and a goat, offered with prayers of gratitude for the blessings already received."

**11** These are the words of Obara Meji, the Odu of abundance, speech, and generosity.
