Section 1 of the Ese Ifá — The Sixteen Major Odu — has been the subject of extensive academic analysis across multiple disciplines: anthropology, archaeology, linguistics, art history, comparative religion, and cultural studies. Scholars examine this section's content, structure, and cultural context, seeking to understand both what it communicates and the conditions under which it was produced and transmitted.

Key scholarly questions concerning this section include its relationship to other sections of the Ese Ifá, its historical and cultural context, the reliability and transmission history of the text, and its significance for understanding the broader religious and intellectual tradition from which it emerges. Academic engagement with this section contributes to the ongoing scholarly project of recognizing the Ese Ifá as a major text of world religious and literary heritage.
