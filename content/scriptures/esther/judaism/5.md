## The First Banquet

Jewish interpretation of Esther's banquet strategy draws on midrashic explanations for why she delayed her request. The Talmud (Megillah 15b) records multiple opinions:

| Rabbi | Explanation for Delay |
|-------|----------------------|
| R. Eliezer | To make the king jealous of Haman |
| R. Joshua | To keep Haman close so he could not plot rebellion |
| R. Meir | To prevent the Jews from saying "We have a sister in the palace" and becoming complacent |
| Rabbah | So that Haman's downfall would come at the height of his pride |

## Key Themes

Haman's construction of the gallows (5:14) at Zeresh's suggestion is noted by the rabbis as the beginning of his end. The Talmud observes that "from the moment the righteous Mordecai was destined for the gallows, Haman began to fall."

## Theological Significance

- **Strategic wisdom**: Multiple explanations honor the text's complexity
- **Divine timing**: Delay serves God's purposes
- **Pride before fall**: Haman's elation is the prelude to destruction
- **Rabbinic plurality**: Multiple valid interpretations coexist
