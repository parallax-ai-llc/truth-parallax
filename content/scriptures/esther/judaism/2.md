## Esther and Hester Panim

Jewish interpretation finds profound theological meaning in Esther's name, connecting it to *hester panim* — the hiding of God's face (Deuteronomy 31:18). Just as Esther hides her Jewish identity, God hides His presence throughout the book. The Talmud (Chullin 139b) asks: "Where is Esther indicated in the Torah? In the verse, 'And I will surely hide (*haster astir*) My face'" (Deuteronomy 31:18).

## Key Themes

### Mordecai's Lineage

Mordecai is identified as a Benjaminite, descendant of Kish (2:5) — the same tribal and family affiliation as King Saul. The midrash sees the Mordecai-Haman conflict as completing unfinished business from 1 Samuel 15, where Saul failed to destroy the Amalekite king Agag.

### Finding Favor

The phrase "Esther found favor" (*chen*) recalls other figures who found divine favor (Noah, Joseph). Jewish tradition attributes this not merely to physical beauty but to divine grace operating through her.

## Theological Significance

- **Hester panim**: God's hidden face is the book's deepest theological theme
- **Saul-Agag reprise**: The ancient conflict between Israel and Amalek continues
- **Divine chen**: Favor is ultimately from God, not from human merit
- **Hidden providence**: Concealment is itself a divine strategy
