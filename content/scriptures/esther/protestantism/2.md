## Esther Enters the Palace

Esther 2 introduces the book's protagonist: Esther (Hadassah), a young Jewish orphan raised by her cousin Mordecai. Through a kingdom-wide beauty selection, Esther becomes queen of Persia — while concealing her Jewish identity.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Mordecai's Instruction (2:10)

Mordecai instructs Esther not to reveal her nationality. Protestant interpreters debate this: was it prudent wisdom or faithless concealment? Most conclude it was providential positioning — God placing Esther in the royal court for a future purpose she cannot yet see.

### "Esther found favor" (2:15, 17)

The repeated motif of Esther finding favor echoes the language used of Joseph, Daniel, and other figures whom God elevated in foreign courts. Protestant interpreters see this as evidence of divine grace operating through natural means.

### Mordecai Saves the King (2:21-23)

Mordecai discovers a plot against the king and reports it through Esther. The event is recorded in the royal chronicles but not immediately rewarded — another providential detail that will prove critical in chapter 6.

## Theological Significance

- **Hidden identity**: God positions His people strategically before the crisis
- **Favor as grace**: Divine grace operates through natural attraction
- **Unrewarded faithfulness**: Good deeds are recorded for future vindication
- **Preparation**: God prepares His instruments before revealing His purpose
