## Mordecai's Exaltation

Esther 10, the book's brief epilogue, records Mordecai's elevation to second-in-command of the Persian Empire and his ongoing work for the welfare of his people.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Mordecai's Position (10:2-3)

"Mordecai the Jew was second in rank to King Ahasuerus, and he was great among the Jews and popular with the multitude of his brothers, for he sought the welfare of his people and spoke peace to all his offspring."

Protestant interpreters see Mordecai as a Joseph-like figure: a Jew elevated in a foreign court who uses his position to benefit his people. The phrase "sought the welfare of his people" echoes Jeremiah 29:7 — seeking the welfare of the place of exile.

### The Book's Conclusion

The brevity of the epilogue is itself significant. Protestant commentators note that the story's resolution is not a return to Israel but a thriving Jewish community within the diaspora — a reality that continues to this day.

## Theological Significance

- **Exaltation of the faithful**: God elevates those who serve Him courageously
- **Diaspora blessing**: Jews can flourish and bless others in foreign lands
- **Public service**: Political power used for the welfare of the vulnerable
- **Peace**: The ultimate fruit of faithfulness
