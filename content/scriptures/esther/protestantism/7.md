## Haman's Downfall

Esther 7 brings the dramatic climax: at her second banquet, Esther reveals her identity and Haman's plot. The king's rage seals Haman's fate, and he is hanged on the very gallows he built for Mordecai.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Esther's Revelation (7:3-4)

"If I have found favor in your sight, O king, let my life be granted me, and my people." Esther identifies herself with her people's fate. Protestant interpreters see a Christological parallel: the mediator identifies with those under threat of death and intercedes before the throne of power.

### Haman Exposed (7:5-6)

"A foe and enemy! This wicked Haman!" The moment of exposure is dramatic and complete. Protestant theology of judgment sees evil ultimately unmasked and condemned.

### The Gallows (7:9-10)

"So they hanged Haman on the gallows that he had prepared for Mordecai." The principle of *lex talionis* (measure for measure) reaches its narrative climax. Protestant interpreters cite Psalm 7:15-16: "He who digs a pit will fall into it."

## Theological Significance

- **Mediation**: Esther intercedes for her people before the king
- **Evil exposed**: Sin cannot remain hidden forever
- **Poetic justice**: The wicked fall into their own trap
- **Reversal completed**: The gallows serves the opposite of its intended purpose
