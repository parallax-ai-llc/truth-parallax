## Esther's First Banquet

Esther 5 records Esther's approach to the king and her strategic decision to invite the king and Haman to not one but two banquets before revealing her request. Meanwhile, Haman's pride builds the gallows for his own destruction.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Esther Before the King (5:1-3)

Esther puts on her royal robes and stands in the inner court. The king extends his golden scepter, sparing her life. Protestant interpreters see divine favor at work: Esther's beauty, her timing, and the king's receptivity all converge providentially.

### The Delayed Request (5:4-8)

Rather than immediately revealing the crisis, Esther invites the king and Haman to a banquet, then delays again to a second banquet. Protestant commentators see this as Spirit-guided patience and strategic wisdom.

### Haman's Gallows (5:9-14)

Haman, elated by the invitation yet infuriated by Mordecai's continued refusal to bow, builds a 75-foot gallows at his wife's suggestion. The irony is devastating: Haman constructs the instrument of his own execution. Protestant theology of reversal sees God's providential hand preparing the judgment.

## Theological Significance

- **Strategic patience**: Wisdom knows when to wait before acting
- **Divine favor**: God provides access at the critical moment
- **Pride before fall**: Haman's arrogance prepares his own destruction
- **Dramatic irony**: The reader sees what the characters cannot
