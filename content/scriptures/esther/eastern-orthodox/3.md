## The Face of Evil

Orthodox interpretation reads Haman's plot as an expression of the demonic forces that oppose God's people throughout history. The casting of lots reveals the enemy's attempt to use fate and chance against divine providence, but even the lots fall within God's sovereign plan.

## Theological Significance

- **Spiritual warfare**: Behind Haman's malice lies a deeper spiritual reality
- **Providence over chance**: God governs even the fall of lots
- **Corporate threat**: Evil targets the community of faith as a whole
- **Historical pattern**: The persecution of God's people recurs throughout history
