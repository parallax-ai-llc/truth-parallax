## The Fulfillment of the Divine Economy

Orthodox interpretation reads Mordecai's exaltation as the completion of the divine economy in this narrative. The Septuagint's Addition F interprets Mordecai's dream, revealing that every detail of the story was part of God's plan. The book ends in peace, reflecting the eschatological peace that is the goal of all divine action.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine economy fulfilled**: God's plan reaches its appointed end
- **Exaltation of the humble**: Faithfulness is rewarded with honor
- **Peace**: The ultimate fruit of God's providential governance
- **Septuagint completeness**: The additions reveal the story's full theological meaning
