## The Persian Court and the Divine Economy

Orthodox interpretation reads Esther 1 within the framework of the divine economy. The extravagant Persian court, with all its material glory, serves as the unwitting stage for God's saving purpose. Vashti's removal is an act of divine providence preparing the way for Esther's appointment.

## Key Themes

The Septuagint version, which the Orthodox Church uses, includes Addition A with Mordecai's prophetic dream, framing the entire narrative as a divine revelation. The contrast between imperial power and divine sovereignty is a recurring Orthodox theme — the Byzantine Empire itself was understood as an instrument of the divine economy.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine economy**: God orchestrates all events for salvation
- **Imperial power**: Human kingdoms serve divine purposes unknowingly
- **Septuagint framing**: Mordecai's dream provides the theological key
- **Providence**: Seemingly random events serve God's plan
