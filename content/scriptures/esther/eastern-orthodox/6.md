## The Night of Reversal

Orthodox interpretation reads the king's sleepless night as a moment of divine intervention that, while operating through entirely natural means, reveals God's sovereign governance of history. The Orthodox tradition recognizes divine humor in the exquisite irony of Haman's humiliation.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine intervention**: God works through ordinary means
- **Holy irony**: The reversal of the proud reveals divine humor
- **Vindication**: The faithful are rewarded in God's timing
- **Providence**: Insomnia becomes the instrument of salvation
