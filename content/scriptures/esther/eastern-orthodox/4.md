## Fasting, Prayer, and Courage

Orthodox interpretation of Esther 4 emphasizes the ascetical dimension. Esther's three-day fast (expanded beautifully in Addition C) models the Orthodox practice of rigorous fasting before major spiritual undertakings. The fast is not merely abstinence but a total surrender of self to divine will.

## Key Themes

Addition C's prayer reveals Esther's interior struggle — she abhors the pagan court yet accepts her vocation within it. Orthodox ascetical theology recognizes this tension: the saint lives in the world while maintaining interior detachment. "For such a time as this" is read as the moment when ascetical preparation meets divine calling.

## Theological Significance

- **Ascetical fasting**: Rigorous preparation precedes decisive action
- **Interior prayer**: The soul struggles in hiddenness before God
- **Vocational obedience**: Accepting a difficult calling for the sake of others
- **Courage through prayer**: Spiritual strength comes from encounter with God
