## Esther's Elevation

Catholic interpretation sees Esther's selection as queen as a providential appointment. The deuterocanonical additions provide Esther with a rich inner life of prayer and devotion that transforms her from a passive beauty contestant into a woman of deep faith.

## Key Themes

Addition C reveals Esther's private prayer, in which she expresses her loathing of the gentile court and her crown, and pleads with God for courage. This transforms the Catholic reading: Esther is not merely fortunate but faithfully enduring a difficult vocation for her people's sake, making her a type of Mary in her faithful submission to God's plan.

## Theological Significance

- **Providential appointment**: God places His servants in strategic positions
- **Inner devotion**: External beauty conceals deep interior faith
- **Marian typology**: Esther's hidden identity prefigures Mary's hidden life
- **Vocational endurance**: Faithful service sometimes means bearing difficult circumstances
