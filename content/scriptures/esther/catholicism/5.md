## The First Banquet

Catholic interpretation reads Esther's strategic patience as guided by the Holy Spirit. The delay in revealing her request reflects the Catholic principle of discernment — waiting for God's timing rather than acting precipitously.

## Key Themes

Esther's approach models the Catholic virtue of prudence. Haman's construction of the gallows illustrates the Catholic teaching on the self-destructive nature of sin — evil ultimately turns against itself. The irony of the gallows is providential justice in its most dramatic form.

## Theological Significance

- **Prudence**: Wisdom guides the timing of action
- **Self-destructive sin**: Evil prepares its own downfall
- **Divine timing**: God's schedule supersedes human impatience
- **Ironic justice**: The instruments of oppression become instruments of judgment
