## The Vindication of the Righteous

Catholic interpretation of Haman's downfall reads it as divine justice operating through human agents. Esther's intercession before the king models the Catholic theology of mediation — one person's courage and faith securing deliverance for many.

## Key Themes

Esther's self-identification with her people ("my people") echoes the Catholic theology of the communion of saints. Haman's execution on his own gallows fulfills the Psalm: "The wicked are snared in the work of their own hands" (Psalm 9:16). The Catholic tradition of praying for the conversion of sinners recognizes that even Haman was a soul in need of grace.

## Theological Significance

- **Mediation**: Esther intercedes effectively for her people
- **Poetic justice**: The wicked fall into their own trap
- **Communion of saints**: Solidarity with one's people in their hour of need
- **Divine judgment**: God's justice operates through human agents
