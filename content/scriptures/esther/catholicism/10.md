## Mordecai's Exaltation

Catholic interpretation reads Mordecai's elevation as a vindication of faithful service. Like Joseph in Egypt and Daniel in Babylon, Mordecai demonstrates that faithful service to God can coexist with faithful service in a pagan court.

## Key Themes

The deuterocanonical Addition F provides a concluding interpretation of Mordecai's dream from Addition A, making explicit the theological meaning of the entire narrative. Catholic canonicity affirms these additions as inspired Scripture that illuminates the book's divine dimension.

## Theological Significance

- **Vindication**: God rewards faithful service in His timing
- **Public service**: Political authority can serve God's purposes
- **Canonical fullness**: The deuterocanonical additions complete the theological picture
- **Peace**: The ultimate fruit of God's providential governance
