## Victory and Memorial

Catholic interpretation reads the institution of Purim as a model of liturgical memorial. The Church's own calendar of feasts serves the same function: commemorating God's saving acts so that each generation may participate in the reality they celebrate.

## Key Themes

The reversal (*v'nahafokh hu*) is the book's theological climax. Catholic liturgical theology understands memorial (*anamnesis*) not as mere remembrance but as making present the reality commemorated. The integration of feasting, charity, and gift-giving reflects the Catholic understanding that worship overflows into concrete acts of love.

## Theological Significance

- **Liturgical memorial**: Commemorating God's acts makes them present
- **Reversal**: God transforms death sentences into occasions for life
- **Charity**: Celebration includes care for the poor
- **Communal worship**: Deliverance is remembered corporately
