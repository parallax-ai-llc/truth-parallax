## Esther's Call to Intercession

Catholic interpretation of Esther 4 centers on the theme of intercession. Mordecai's appeal and Esther's response model the Catholic understanding of intercessory prayer: one person, placed in a position of access, risks everything for the sake of others.

## Key Themes

The deuterocanonical Addition C provides Esther's prayer in full — one of the most moving prayers in Catholic Scripture. She removes her royal garments, puts on mourning, and prays: "O my Lord, you only are our King; help me, who am alone and have no helper but you." This prayer transforms Esther's approach to the king from political strategy into an act of faith.

"For such a time as this" (4:14) is read through the lens of Catholic vocation theology: every person is placed in their circumstances by divine providence for a purpose.

## Theological Significance

- **Intercessory prayer**: Esther models the mediator who risks everything for others
- **Marian parallel**: Esther's intercession prefigures Mary's mediation
- **Fasting**: Three days of fasting prepare for a decisive act of faith
- **Vocational calling**: Each person is placed "for such a time as this"
