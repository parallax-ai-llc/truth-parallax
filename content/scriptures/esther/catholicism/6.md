## Divine Providence Unveiled

Catholic interpretation of Esther 6 reads the king's sleepless night as the most vivid demonstration of divine providence in the book. God works through ordinary events — insomnia, bureaucratic record-reading — to accomplish extraordinary purposes.

## Key Themes

The Catechism (CCC 303) teaches that God "in his almighty providence can bring a good from the consequences of an evil." The king's insomnia, Mordecai's unrewarded loyalty, and Haman's self-inflated suggestion all converge in a moment of stunning reversal. Catholic theology of Mary's Magnificat finds an echo here: "He has brought down the mighty from their thrones" (Luke 1:52).

## Theological Significance

- **Providence in the ordinary**: God works through sleepless nights and records
- **Ironic reversal**: The proud are humiliated by their own designs
- **Delayed justice**: God's vindication comes in His timing
- **Magnificat theme**: The mighty are brought low, the humble exalted
