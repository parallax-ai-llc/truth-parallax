## The Plot Against God's People

Catholic interpretation of Haman's plot reads it within the broader biblical pattern of threats against God's chosen people, from Pharaoh to Herod. The casting of lots (*pur*) to determine the date demonstrates that even instruments of divination serve God's purposes — the lot falls where God determines.

## Key Themes

The Catholic reading emphasizes the corporate dimension of the threat: this is not persecution of individuals but attempted genocide of an entire people. Catholic social teaching on the rights of peoples and the evil of genocide finds biblical warrant in the horror of Haman's decree. The Catechism (CCC 597-598) explicitly condemns collective guilt and persecution directed at any people.

## Theological Significance

- **Corporate evil**: Genocide threatens an entire people, not merely individuals
- **Providential timing**: Even the lots fall according to God's will
- **Pattern of persecution**: The threat to Israel recurs throughout salvation history
- **Catholic social teaching**: The rights of peoples against genocidal persecution
