## Victory and Gratitude

Islamic interpretation reads the institution of Purim through the lens of *shukr* (gratitude). The Quran teaches that gratitude for divine blessings is obligatory: "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you" (14:7). The annual commemoration of deliverance models the proper response to God's saving acts.

## Theological Significance

- **Shukr**: Gratitude for divine deliverance is obligatory
- **Memorial**: God's saving acts deserve perpetual commemoration
- **Communal celebration**: Joy is shared, not private
- **Charity**: Celebration includes care for the poor
