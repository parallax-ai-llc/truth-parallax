## The Persian Court in Islamic Perspective

Islamic engagement with Esther 1 focuses on the theme of divine sovereignty over earthly kingdoms. The Quran teaches that God grants dominion to whom He wills and takes it from whom He wills (3:26). The extravagant Persian court, while not referenced in the Quran, illustrates the transience of worldly power.

## Key Themes

The fall of Vashti demonstrates the instability of worldly status. Islamic theology emphasizes that all earthly power is temporary and granted by God. The luxury and excess of the Persian court contrasts with the Islamic virtue of *zuhd* (ascetic simplicity). The narrative's unfolding demonstrates God's ability to use even pagan courts for His purposes.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine sovereignty**: God grants and removes worldly power
- **Transience of power**: Earthly status is inherently unstable
- **Zuhd**: Material excess contrasts with spiritual simplicity
- **Divine instrumentality**: God uses all institutions for His purposes
