## Courage and Trust in God

Islamic interpretation of Esther 4 resonates with the Quranic emphasis on *tawakkul* (trust in God) and the willingness to sacrifice for others. Esther's decision to risk her life parallels the Islamic concept of self-sacrifice (*fida'*) for the welfare of the community.

## Key Themes

Esther's fast before approaching the king parallels the Islamic practice of fasting as spiritual preparation. The Quran teaches that "whoever submits his face to Allah while being a doer of good will have his reward with his Lord" (2:112). Esther's willingness to risk death models complete submission to divine purpose.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawakkul**: Trust in God enables courageous action
- **Fasting**: Spiritual preparation for decisive moments
- **Self-sacrifice**: Willingness to risk oneself for the community
- **Divine reliance**: Human courage is empowered by trust in God
