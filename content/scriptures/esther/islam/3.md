## Evil Plotting and Divine Counter-Plan

Islamic interpretation of Haman's plot connects to the Quranic principle that "they plan, and Allah plans, and Allah is the best of planners" (8:30). Note: the Haman of the Quran (associated with Pharaoh) is a different figure from the Haman of Esther, though the name's association with tyranny and evil planning creates an interesting resonance.

## Key Themes

The Quran repeatedly demonstrates that the plots of the wicked are ultimately overturned by God. Haman's scheme to destroy the Jews fits this pattern perfectly. God's protection of vulnerable communities is a recurring Quranic theme.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine planning**: God's plan supersedes human plotting
- **Protection of the vulnerable**: God shields threatened communities
- **Haman resonance**: The name evokes tyranny in both biblical and Quranic traditions
- **Justice**: Oppressors are ultimately defeated
