## Mordecai's Exaltation

Islamic interpretation reads Mordecai's elevation as an example of God rewarding faithful service. The Quran teaches that "whoever does righteousness, whether male or female, while being a believer — those will enter Paradise and will not be wronged" (4:124). Mordecai's political success serves his community's welfare — the Islamic ideal of public service.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine reward**: God elevates the faithful
- **Public service**: Political power should serve the community's welfare
- **Peaceful conclusion**: The ultimate fruit of faithfulness is peace
- **Diaspora service**: Serving God is possible in any political context

## References

- Quran 4:124 on the reward of the righteous.
