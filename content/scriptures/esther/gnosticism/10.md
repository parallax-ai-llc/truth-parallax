## Greatness Within the Material Realm

Gnostic interpretation reads Mordecai's exaltation as the highest achievement possible within the archontic system — political power, social honor, and communal peace. Yet from the pneumatic perspective, this greatness is entirely material. Mordecai is second to a pagan king in a material empire; he has not transcended the system but risen within it.

## Key Themes

The book of Esther ends with peace and prosperity — the best the material world can offer. Gnostic readers respect this outcome while recognizing its inherent limitation. True peace, in Gnostic thought, is found only beyond the archontic spheres, in the Pleroma of the transcendent God.

## Theological Significance

- **Material success**: The highest worldly achievement is still material
- **Limited peace**: Temporal peace is not the peace of the Pleroma
- **Archontic promotion**: Rising within the system is not transcending it
- **Honest ending**: The book concludes without pretending to offer spiritual liberation
