## Material Evil and Archontic Fate

Gnostic interpretation reads Haman's genocide plot as an expression of the violence inherent in the material system. The casting of lots represents the archons' use of *heimarmene* (fate) to control events. In Gnostic thought, fate is an archontic tool that keeps humanity trapped in determined cycles of suffering.

## Key Themes

The *pur* (lot) that gives Purim its name is, from a Gnostic perspective, an instrument of *heimarmene* — the cosmic fate that the archons impose on the material world. True liberation requires transcending fate through *gnosis*, not merely surviving within its framework.

## Theological Significance

- **Heimarmene**: Fate is an archontic tool of control
- **Material violence**: The material system produces genocide and oppression
- **Transcending fate**: Liberation comes through gnosis, not through political reversal
- **Archontic evil**: Haman operates within the Demiurge's system of power
