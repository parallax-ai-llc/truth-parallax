## Overview

Academic study of the Rehatnamas focuses on their historical context, textual transmission, and role in the construction of Khalsa identity. Scholars such as W.H. McLeod have demonstrated that the various Rehatnama texts were composed over an extended period, with some elements dating to the time of Guru Gobind Singh and others reflecting later developments. The critical analysis of these texts illuminates the process by which the Khalsa community defined its boundaries, articulated its distinctive practices, and negotiated its relationship to broader North Indian religious culture.

Key scholarly questions include the dating and attribution of specific Rehatnama texts, the relationship between the Rehatnamas and earlier Sikh ethical teachings (such as those found in the Guru Granth Sahib), and the sociological dynamics of identity formation in a persecuted minority community. The Rehatnamas also provide valuable evidence for the study of eighteenth-century Punjabi social history, material culture, and the intersection of religious and political authority.
