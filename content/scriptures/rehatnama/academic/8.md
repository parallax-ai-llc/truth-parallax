Section 8 of the Rehatnama addresses violations, transgressions, and restoration, which academic scholarship examines through the lenses of the sociology of transgression, punishment, and reconciliation. Scholars analyze these prescriptions in their historical context — the turbulent eighteenth century in Punjab, marked by Mughal persecution, Afghan invasions, and the Khalsa's struggle for survival — as well as their ongoing significance for contemporary Sikh communities.

Critical analysis of this section considers questions of textual authenticity (which prescriptions can be reliably attributed to Guru Gobind Singh), historical development (how these norms evolved over time), and sociological function (how they serve to maintain communal boundaries and reinforce collective identity). The study of Rehatnama literature illuminates broader questions about the relationship between religious authority, communal discipline, and identity formation in minority communities under pressure.
