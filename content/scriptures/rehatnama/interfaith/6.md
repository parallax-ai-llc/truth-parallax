Section 6 of the Rehatnama addresses economic ethics and stewardship, which raises questions of vital importance in contemporary interfaith dialogue. The Khalsa code's approach to economic justice as a shared concern across traditions reflects a distinctive vision of the integration of faith and daily practice that both challenges and enriches other traditions' perspectives. In a world where the relationship between religious commitment and public life is contested, the Sikh model offers valuable insights.

Interfaith engagement with this section of the Rehatnama often reveals surprising common ground. Beneath the diversity of specific prescriptions, all traditions share a conviction that the spiritual life must be embodied in concrete practices of daily living. The universal questions raised by this section — How should one live? What are the marks of authentic spiritual commitment? How does a community of faith sustain its identity while remaining open to others? — provide rich material for cross-traditional conversation.
