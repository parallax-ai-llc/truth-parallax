Section 6 of the Rehatnama addresses economic ethics and stewardship, which Sufi readers relate to Sufi teachings on detachment from wealth and the ethics of generosity. The parallels between the Khalsa code and Sufi practice reflect the shared spiritual and cultural milieu of medieval Punjab, where Sikh and Sufi traditions developed in close proximity and mutual influence. Both traditions understand that the ordering of daily life according to spiritual principles is not merely a matter of external compliance but a means of inner transformation.

The Sufi tradition's own rich literature of *adab* (spiritual etiquette) and ethical conduct provides a comparative framework for understanding the Rehatnama's prescriptions. Both traditions recognize that spiritual realization must be embodied in every aspect of daily life — that the inner journey of the soul toward God must be accompanied by a corresponding outer discipline of conduct, character, and community.
