Section 6 of the Rehatnama addresses economic ethics and stewardship, a subject of central importance to the Khalsa way of life. The Sikh tradition understands *Dasvandh* (tithing) and the practice of honest livelihood (*Kirat Karni*) as integral to the spiritual discipline prescribed by Guru Gobind Singh. These regulations are not arbitrary impositions but expressions of a comprehensive spiritual vision in which every aspect of daily life — from dress and diet to prayer and economic activity — becomes an arena for the practice of devotion and the cultivation of the Khalsa virtues of courage, compassion, and commitment.

The contemporary Sikh community continues to engage with these teachings through the *Sikh Rehat Maryada*, the modern code of conduct that synthesizes and updates the various historical Rehatnamas. Questions of interpretation and application in changing social contexts ensure that the Rehatnama tradition remains a living conversation within the Sikh community rather than a static set of rules.
