## Overview

For the Sikh community, the Rehatnamas represent the authoritative expression of Guru Gobind Singh's vision for the Khalsa way of life. They provide the foundation for the *Sikh Rehat Maryada* — the modern code of conduct ratified by the Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC) in 1950, which continues to govern the religious and social life of observant Sikhs worldwide. The Rehatnamas are understood not as arbitrary rules but as a comprehensive spiritual discipline designed to cultivate the qualities of courage, compassion, and devotion that define the Khalsa.

The Sikh community's relationship with the Rehatnamas involves ongoing interpretation and adaptation. Questions about the applicability of specific regulations to contemporary life, the relative authority of different Rehatnama texts, and the balance between the letter and spirit of the *rehat* are matters of continual communal reflection. The central principle, however, remains constant: the Khalsa Sikh is called to embody a distinctive way of life that integrates spiritual devotion, moral integrity, communal solidarity, and readiness to defend the oppressed.
