---
title: "Duties to the Community"
scripture: "rehatnama"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Every Sikh has a duty to the Panth, the community of the faithful. The individual exists within the community, and the community supports the individual.

**2** Seva, selfless service, is the duty of every Sikh. Service in the Langar, service in the Gurdwara, service to the poor and the sick—all are forms of worship.

**3** The Sikh shall defend the weak and the oppressed. This is the duty of the Khalsa, established by Guru Gobind Singh.

**4** The Sikh shall contribute to the welfare of society. Building schools, hospitals, and roads—all service to humanity is service to God.

**5** The Sikh shall promote peace and harmony among all people. The message of the Guru is universal love and brotherhood.

**6** The Sikh shall never turn away from duty, whether in peace or in war. Courage in the face of injustice is the mark of the Khalsa.
