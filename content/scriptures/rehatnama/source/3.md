---
title: "Prohibitions"
scripture: "rehatnama"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The four cardinal prohibitions for the Khalsa are these: cutting of hair, use of tobacco, adultery, and eating meat that has been ritually slaughtered.

**2** A Sikh shall not steal, gamble, or cheat. These are the acts of the faithless.

**3** A Sikh shall not consume alcohol or any intoxicant. The mind must remain clear and devoted to God.

**4** A Sikh shall not practice caste discrimination. In the Guru's court, all are equal. The high and the low are one before God.

**5** A Sikh shall not commit infanticide, nor discriminate between sons and daughters. Boys and girls are equal gifts from God.

**6** A Sikh shall not bow before any grave, shrine, or sacred place other than the Guru Granth Sahib. Worship belongs to the one God alone.
