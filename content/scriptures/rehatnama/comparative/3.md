Section 3 of the Rehatnama addresses moral conduct and ethical living, which invites comparison with virtue ethics across traditions. Comparative analysis reveals both the universal dimensions of this ethical domain — every religious tradition develops norms and practices related to moral conduct and ethical living — and the distinctive features of the Khalsa approach, shaped by its particular historical circumstances and theological commitments.

The comparative study of these regulations illuminates fundamental questions about the relationship between faith and practice, individual conscience and communal authority, and the role of discipline in the spiritual life. Each tradition's approach to moral conduct and ethical living reflects its deeper theological vision — its understanding of the human condition, the nature of the divine, and the path to liberation or salvation.
