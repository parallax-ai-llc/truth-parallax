Section 1 of the Rehatnama addresses Khalsa identity and the Five Ks, which invites comparison with religious dress codes and visible identity. Comparative analysis reveals both the universal dimensions of this ethical domain — every religious tradition develops norms and practices related to Khalsa identity and the Five Ks — and the distinctive features of the Khalsa approach, shaped by its particular historical circumstances and theological commitments.

The comparative study of these regulations illuminates fundamental questions about the relationship between faith and practice, individual conscience and communal authority, and the role of discipline in the spiritual life. Each tradition's approach to Khalsa identity and the Five Ks reflects its deeper theological vision — its understanding of the human condition, the nature of the divine, and the path to liberation or salvation.
