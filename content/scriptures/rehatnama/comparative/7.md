Section 7 of the Rehatnama addresses martial ethics and righteous warfare, which invites comparison with just war traditions across religions — crusade, jihad, dharma yudh. Comparative analysis reveals both the universal dimensions of this ethical domain — every religious tradition develops norms and practices related to martial ethics and righteous warfare — and the distinctive features of the Khalsa approach, shaped by its particular historical circumstances and theological commitments.

The comparative study of these regulations illuminates fundamental questions about the relationship between faith and practice, individual conscience and communal authority, and the role of discipline in the spiritual life. Each tradition's approach to martial ethics and righteous warfare reflects its deeper theological vision — its understanding of the human condition, the nature of the divine, and the path to liberation or salvation.
