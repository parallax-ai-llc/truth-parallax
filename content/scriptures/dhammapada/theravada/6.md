The Panditavagga presents 14 verses on wisdom and the characteristics of the wise person (pandita). In Theravada understanding, the wise are those who see things as they truly are: impermanent, unsatisfactory, and without self. The chapter instructs seekers to associate with the wise as one would follow a guide who reveals hidden treasure.

The Theravada commentarial tradition identifies the wise person with one who has at minimum attained stream-entry (sotapanna), the first stage of awakening. The wise person's willingness to accept correction and criticism is particularly valued in the monastic context, where the Vinaya discipline depends on mutual accountability.
