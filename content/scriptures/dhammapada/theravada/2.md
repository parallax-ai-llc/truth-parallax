The Appamadavagga, comprising 12 verses on heedfulness (appamada), presents what the Theravada tradition considers the single most important quality for spiritual progress. The Buddha's own last words, "Strive with heedfulness," echo the central message of this chapter. Heedfulness is understood not merely as carefulness but as the sustained, vigilant awareness that prevents the arising of unwholesome states.

Theravada commentaries emphasize that heedfulness is the root of all wholesome qualities, just as the footprint of the elephant encompasses all other footprints. This teaching is frequently invoked in monastic discipline, where continuous mindfulness in all postures and activities is the ideal.
