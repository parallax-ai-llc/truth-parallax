The Jaravagga's 11 verses on old age confront the reality of physical decay with unflinching directness. The Theravada tradition uses contemplation of aging as one of the formal meditation subjects (kammatthana) designed to overcome attachment to the body and generate spiritual urgency (samvega).

The chapter's comparison of the body to a worn-out chariot and its warning that old age and death come without regard for social status reinforce the universality of impermanence. The teaching that one who has practiced the Dhamma in youth has a refuge in old age motivates early engagement with the path.
