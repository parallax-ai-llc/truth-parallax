The Tanhavagga's 26 verses on craving (tanha) address the second Noble Truth. The Theravada analysis distinguishes three types: craving for sense pleasure, for existence, and for non-existence. This extensive treatment reflects craving's central importance.

Theravada practice addresses craving through vipassana insight into impermanence. The vivid imagery of craving as a creeping vine motivates sustained effort. The teaching that craving can be completely uprooted distinguishes Buddhism from mere asceticism.
