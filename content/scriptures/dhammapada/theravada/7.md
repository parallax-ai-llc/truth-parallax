The Arahantavagga's 10 verses describe the arahant, the fully liberated being who is the goal of the Theravada path. The arahant has destroyed all defilements (kilesa), is free from craving and attachment, and will not be reborn. This chapter provides a portrait of the ideal that inspires all Theravada practice.

Theravada commentators emphasize that the arahant's state is characterized by the complete eradication of greed, hatred, and delusion. The chapter's imagery of the arahant as one whose path cannot be traced, like birds in the sky, conveys the freedom and unboundedness of full liberation.
