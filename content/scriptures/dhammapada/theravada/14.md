The Buddhavagga's 18 verses celebrate the qualities of the Awakened One. The Theravada tradition understands the historical Buddha as the supreme teacher whose awakening revealed the timeless truth of the Dhamma. The chapter's declaration that "the Buddhas only point the way; you yourselves must make the effort" encapsulates the Theravada understanding of the teacher-student relationship.

The verses on the rarity of a Buddha's appearance motivate practitioners to seize the opportunity presented by the Dhamma's availability. Theravada interpretation emphasizes the Buddha's role as teacher and exemplar rather than savior.
