The Papavagga's 13 verses on evil examine the nature and consequences of unwholesome action. The Theravada understanding of evil (papa) is rooted in the three unwholesome roots: greed, hatred, and delusion. The chapter teaches that evil actions inevitably ripen into suffering.

Theravada commentators connect these verses to the detailed analysis of karma found in the Abhidhamma. The metaphor of evil accumulating "drop by drop, like a water pot" illustrates how habitual patterns of unwholesome action create deep mental tendencies (anusaya) that bind beings to samsara.
