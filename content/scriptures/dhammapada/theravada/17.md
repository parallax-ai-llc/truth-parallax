The Kodhavagga's 14 verses on anger address one of the three unwholesome roots (dosa). Anger is analyzed as a destructive mental force that obscures wisdom and generates harmful karma. The chapter teaches that overcoming anger with patience is among the highest spiritual achievements.

Theravada practice addresses anger through mindfulness of its arising, cultivation of loving-kindness, reflection on karma, and development of patience (khanti). The commentarial tradition provides extensive guidance on anger management.
