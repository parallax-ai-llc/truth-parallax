The Brahmanavagga's 41 verses redefine the brahmin from birth-based caste identity to spiritual attainment. The Theravada tradition reads this as the Buddha's challenge to the Brahmanical order: true nobility is determined by conduct, wisdom, and liberation from defilements.

The qualities of the true brahmin—patience, non-violence, freedom from attachment—describe the arahant. This chapter provides scriptural support for rejecting caste discrimination and affirming that the spiritual path is open to all.
