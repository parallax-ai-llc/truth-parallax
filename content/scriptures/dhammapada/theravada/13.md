The Lokavagga's 12 verses on the world examine samsaric existence. The Theravada understanding of 'the world' (loka) encompasses both external phenomena and internal experience. The teaching to "look upon the world as a bubble, look upon it as a mirage" is used in vipassana meditation to develop insight into the insubstantial nature of phenomena.

The chapter's vision of the wise person who has transcended the world while still dwelling in it describes the arahant's relationship to ordinary experience. Theravada interpretation connects these verses to cosmological teachings where the world arises through collective karma.
