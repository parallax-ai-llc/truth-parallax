The Cittavagga's 11 verses on the mind are central to Theravada psychology (Abhidhamma). The chapter describes the mind as flickering, difficult to guard, and subtle, yet capable of being trained and directed toward liberation. Theravada meditation practice, particularly samatha and vipassana, is grounded in the understanding that the undisciplined mind is the source of suffering while the disciplined mind is the source of happiness.

The Theravada commentarial tradition connects these verses to the detailed analysis of consciousness found in the Abhidhamma Pitaka. The mind's impermanent, conditioned nature is emphasized: each moment of consciousness arises and passes away, offering continuous opportunities for wholesome redirection.
