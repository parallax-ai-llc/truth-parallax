The Sukhavagga's 12 verses on happiness distinguish between worldly and spiritual wellbeing. The Theravada tradition identifies multiple levels of happiness, from sense pleasure through meditative bliss (jhana) to the supreme happiness of nibbana.

The verse declaring that health is the highest gain, contentment the greatest wealth, trust the best kinsman, and nibbana the highest happiness summarizes the graduated understanding of wellbeing. The chapter's teaching that victory breeds enmity points toward the peace that transcends worldly competition.
