The Sahassavagga's 16 verses teach that quality surpasses quantity in spiritual practice. A single meaningful verse understood and practiced is worth more than a thousand meaningless words recited without comprehension. This teaching is central to the Theravada emphasis on understanding (pariyatti) leading to practice (patipatti) and realization (pativedha).

The Theravada tradition uses this chapter to distinguish genuine spiritual attainment from mere ritualistic observance. The verses emphasize that a single day of moral effort or meditative insight surpasses a hundred years of heedless living.
