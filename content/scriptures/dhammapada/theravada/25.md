The Bhikkhuvagga's 23 verses describe the ideal Buddhist monastic. The Theravada Sangha is considered the preserver of the Buddha's teaching, and this chapter outlines the virtues sustaining that mission.

Theravada interpretation reads these verses alongside the Vinaya Pitaka's monastic code. The monk who guards the senses, practices restraint, delights in meditation, and is content with simple provisions embodies the ideal of renunciation.
