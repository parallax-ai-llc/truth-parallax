The Malavagga's 21 verses on impurity examine the defilements (kilesa) that obstruct liberation. The Theravada tradition identifies a detailed taxonomy of mental impurities, from gross defilements to subtle impurities in advanced practitioners.

Theravada practice uses these verses in connection with meditation on the body's impurity (asubha bhavana). The chapter's teaching that impurity arises from within aligns with the emphasis on mental purification as the heart of the spiritual path.
