The Piyavagga's 12 verses on affection examine the relationship between love and suffering. The Theravada tradition distinguishes between attachment (upadana), which leads to suffering, and loving-kindness (metta), which liberates. The chapter teaches that from affection springs grief and fear.

Theravada meditation includes the systematic cultivation of loving-kindness (metta bhavana) as an antidote to possessive attachment. The chapter's teaching is not an injunction against love but an invitation to transform possessive love into boundless compassion.
