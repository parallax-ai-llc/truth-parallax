The Dhammatthavagga's 17 verses on the just person describe one who lives in accordance with the Dhamma. The Theravada tradition understands justice as alignment with the natural moral order. The just person acts with wisdom, impartiality, and compassion.

Theravada commentators connect these verses to the concept of dhammacakka, the wheel of righteousness. The chapter's warning against hasty judgment and its praise of thorough investigation reflect the commitment to careful discernment.
