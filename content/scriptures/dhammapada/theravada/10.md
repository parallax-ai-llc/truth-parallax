The Dandavagga's 18 verses on violence present the Buddhist ethic of non-harming (ahimsa) with particular force. The Theravada tradition reads these verses as supporting the first precept of abstaining from killing and the broader commitment to compassion for all sentient beings.

The chapter's statement that "all beings tremble before violence, all fear death" establishes empathy as the foundation of ethical conduct. In Theravada societies, these verses have informed attitudes toward capital punishment, warfare, and the treatment of animals.
