The Attavagga's 10 verses on the self present a nuanced Theravada teaching. While ultimate Buddhist doctrine denies a permanent self (anatta), these verses address the conventional self as the agent of moral effort. The chapter teaches that one should be one's own refuge, training oneself as a merchant trains a noble horse.

Theravada commentators resolve the tension between these verses and the anatta doctrine by distinguishing between conventional and ultimate levels of discourse. The chapter's emphasis on self-reliance aligns with the core teaching that each person must walk the path for themselves.
