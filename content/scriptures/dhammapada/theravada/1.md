The Yamakavagga, or Twin Verses, opens the Dhammapada with one of the most celebrated declarations in Theravada Buddhism: "Mind is the forerunner of all actions." These 20 verses establish paired contrasts between wholesome and unwholesome mental states, showing how the quality of mind determines the quality of experience. The Theravada commentary by Buddhaghosa provides elaborate Jataka-style stories for each verse, connecting them to specific incidents in the Buddha's life.

For Theravada practitioners, these opening verses lay the foundation for the entire path. The emphasis on mental causation undergirds the practice of satipatthana (mindfulness meditation) and the ethical life of sila. The twin structure of the verses reflects the Theravada understanding that liberation is a matter of personal choice and sustained effort.
