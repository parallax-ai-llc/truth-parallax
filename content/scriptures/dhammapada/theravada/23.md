The Nagavagga's 14 verses use the elephant as a metaphor for patience, endurance, and self-mastery. The Theravada tradition values the image of the great elephant enduring arrows without flinching, comparing it to the practitioner who bears hardship with equanimity.

The elephant metaphor connects to the Buddha's journey: a wild elephant must be tamed, just as the untrained mind must be disciplined through meditation. The elephant's solitary nature illustrates the ideal of the forest-dwelling monk.
