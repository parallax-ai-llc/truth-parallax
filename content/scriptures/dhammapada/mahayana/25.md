Mahayana Buddhism engages with these 23 verses through the lens of the bodhisattva ideal and Madhyamaka emptiness. The Dhammapada's teachings are understood as valid at the conventional level, supplemented by universal compassion and the understanding that all phenomena are empty of inherent existence.

The The Monk chapter's teachings are embraced as foundational practices supporting the six perfections (paramitas). Mahayana readers find seeds of the great vehicle's expansive vision, recognizing that individual liberation naturally blossoms into aspiration for universal awakening.
