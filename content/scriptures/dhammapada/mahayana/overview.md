## Overview

While the Dhammapada originates from the Pali canon, Mahayana Buddhism has its own versions and has engaged deeply with the text's teachings. Chinese translations such as the Fa Ju Jing were among the earliest Buddhist texts rendered into Chinese and exercised significant influence on East Asian thought.

## Mahayana Engagement

Mahayana interpreters read the Dhammapada's ethical teachings as foundational but not final. The text's emphasis on personal liberation is understood as the Sravakayana perspective, which the Mahayana supplements with the bodhisattva ideal of universal compassion. The teachings on mental discipline, non-hatred, and impermanence are fully embraced as essential foundations for the bodhisattva path.

## Textual History

Multiple Dhammapada recensions exist, including the Gandhari Dharmapada and the Udanavarga preserved in Sanskrit and Tibetan. These variant collections demonstrate that the core teachings were treasured across all Buddhist schools.
