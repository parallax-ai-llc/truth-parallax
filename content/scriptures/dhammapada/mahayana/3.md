The 11 verses on the mind take on special significance in the Mahayana context, where the nature of mind is a central philosophical concern. The Yogacara school's teaching that all phenomena are mind-only (cittamatra) and the Madhyamaka school's analysis of mind's empty nature both find resonance in the Dhammapada's description.

Mahayana practitioners read these verses as pointing toward the Buddha-nature (tathagatagarbha) beneath adventitious defilements. The teaching that the disciplined mind brings happiness is understood as a preliminary glimpse of the luminous mind revealed through the bodhisattva path.
