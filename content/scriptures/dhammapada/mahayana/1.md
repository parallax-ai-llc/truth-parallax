Mahayana readers find in these 20 opening verses a foundation that the later tradition builds upon. The declaration that mind is the forerunner of all states is read through the lens of Yogacara philosophy's analysis of consciousness, including the alaya-vijnana (storehouse consciousness) that underlies all experience.

Mahayana interpretation adds the dimension of bodhicitta, the mind of awakening directed toward the liberation of all beings. The twin contrasts are understood not merely as individual moral choices but as reflecting the choice between samsara's self-centered orientation and the bodhisattva path's other-centered orientation.
