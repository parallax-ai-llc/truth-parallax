The Mahayana reading of these 12 verses extends heedfulness beyond individual vigilance to encompass the bodhisattva's tireless attention to the welfare of all sentient beings. Heedfulness includes maintaining awareness of one's bodhisattva vows and constant readiness to respond to suffering.

Shantideva's Bodhicaryavatara echoes many of the Dhammapada's themes on heedfulness, applying them to the bodhisattva's training. The Mahayana understanding transforms personal heedfulness into universal responsibility.
