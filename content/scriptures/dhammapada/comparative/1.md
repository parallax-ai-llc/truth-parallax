The Twin Verses' opening declarations on the primacy of mind invite comparison with foundational texts across traditions. The teaching that mind is the forerunner of all actions parallels Proverbs 23:7 and the Upanishadic teaching that consciousness is the ground of reality, though with the crucial Buddhist distinction that no permanent self underlies mental causation.

The paired structure echoes the "Two Ways" motif in the Didache, the Qumran Community Rule, and Deuteronomy's blessings and curses. Yet the Dhammapada grounds this moral dualism not in divine command but in natural law (dhamma).
