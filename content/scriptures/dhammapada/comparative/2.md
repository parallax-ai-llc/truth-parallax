The 12 verses on heedfulness find resonance across contemplative traditions. Appamada parallels the Christian monastic virtue of vigilance (nepsis), the Sufi concept of muraqaba (watchfulness), and the Confucian emphasis on cautious self-examination (shen du).

Comparative analysis reveals important differences. Christian vigilance is oriented toward resisting temptation, Sufi watchfulness toward divine presence, while the Dhammapada's heedfulness is directed toward the impermanent nature of all phenomena and the avoidance of unwholesome mental states.
