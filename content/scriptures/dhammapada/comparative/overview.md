## Overview

The Dhammapada offers rich material for comparative study across religious traditions. Its ethical teachings on non-violence, mental discipline, and self-mastery find parallels in many traditions, from Hebrew Proverbs to Marcus Aurelius's Meditations.

## Cross-Traditional Parallels

The opening verses on the primacy of mind echo the Sermon on the Mount's emphasis on inner disposition, the Bhagavad Gita's teaching on detached action, and the Daoist emphasis on wu wei. Its teachings on non-hatred parallel the command to love one's enemies and the Jain principle of ahimsa.

## Distinctive Contributions

Comparative study highlights what is distinctive: the thoroughgoing analysis of suffering, the rejection of a permanent self, and the identification of craving as suffering's root. The vision of liberation through individual mental cultivation represents a distinctive contribution to world religious heritage.
