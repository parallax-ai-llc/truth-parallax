The 11 verses on the mind invite comparison with other traditions' teachings on consciousness. The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, defining yoga as the cessation of mental fluctuations, share the Dhammapada's understanding of the undisciplined mind as the fundamental problem.

While Buddhism, Hinduism, and contemplative Christianity all emphasize mental training, they differ on what is revealed when the mind is stilled. The Upanishadic tradition discovers eternal atman, the Christian contemplative encounters God, while the Buddhist practitioner realizes the mind's empty, dependently arisen nature.
