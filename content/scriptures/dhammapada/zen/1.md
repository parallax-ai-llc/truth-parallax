Zen encounters these 20 Twin Verses with characteristic directness. The opening declaration that mind precedes all phenomena is treated not as a philosophical proposition but as a living truth to be realized in immediate experience. A Zen master might ask: "Before mind, before thoughts, what is this?"

The paired structure resonates with Zen's understanding that delusion and awakening are not two separate states but the same mind seen differently. The chapter's ethical teachings are embraced as the natural expression of an awakened mind that perceives the interconnection of all beings.
