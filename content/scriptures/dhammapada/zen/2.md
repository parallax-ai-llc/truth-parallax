Zen reads these 12 verses on heedfulness as pointing toward continuous, alert presence. The Zen concept of mindfulness in daily activities is grounded in the same unwavering attention the Dhammapada calls appamada.

The contrast between heedful and heedless has a particular edge in Zen, where awakening can turn on a single moment. Zen stories illustrate sudden awakening triggered by complete presence: a shout, a slap, the sound of a pebble striking bamboo.
