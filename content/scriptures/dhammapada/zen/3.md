For Zen, these 11 verses on the mind go to the heart of practice. The mind described as flickering and restless is precisely what zazen addresses. In seated meditation, the practitioner faces the unruly mind directly, allowing it to settle into natural clarity.

The instruction to "guard the mind" does not mean controlling it with force but illuminating it with awareness. The mind described as "hard to restrain" becomes the vehicle of awakening when its true nature, empty and luminous, is directly perceived. This is the meaning of "The ordinary mind is the Way."
