## Overview

Tibetan Buddhism engages with the Dhammapada through the Udanavarga, a related collection preserved in the Tibetan Kangyur. While not identical to the Pali Dhammapada, the Udanavarga shares many verses and themes, and the core teachings on karma, mental discipline, and liberation are fully integrated into Tibetan education.

## Tibetan Interpretation

Tibetan scholars situate the Dhammapada's teachings within the graduated path (lamrim) framework. The ethical teachings correspond to the initial scope, mental cultivation to the intermediate scope, and liberation to the advanced scope. Tibetan commentators interpret these through Madhyamaka philosophy, understanding references to impermanence as pointing toward emptiness of inherent existence.

## Pedagogical Use

In Tibetan monastic education, verses from the Dhammapada and Udanavarga introduce fundamental concepts to young monastics. The accessible poetry provides an entry point into the vast philosophical literature of Tibetan Buddhism.
