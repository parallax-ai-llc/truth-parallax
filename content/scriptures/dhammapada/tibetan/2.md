The Tibetan tradition deeply values these 12 verses on heedfulness, connecting them to conscientiousness (bag yod), one of the eleven virtuous mental factors in Abhidharma psychology. Heedfulness is the active guarding of the mind against defilements combined with enthusiasm for virtue.

Shantideva's treatment of conscientiousness in the Bodhicaryavatara draws on the same spirit, extending heedfulness to the bodhisattva's vigilant protection of bodhicitta. Tibetan masters emphasize that without heedfulness, even advanced tantric practices cannot bear fruit.
