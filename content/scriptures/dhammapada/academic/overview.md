## Overview

Academic scholarship on the Dhammapada spans philological, historical, literary, and comparative dimensions. The text is one of the most widely studied in Buddhist Studies, valued for its antiquity and for the multiple recensions surviving in Pali, Gandhari, Sanskrit, Chinese, and Tibetan.

## Textual Criticism

Scholars such as John Brough, K.R. Norman, and others have undertaken detailed comparative studies. The Gandhari Dharmapada revealed a version significantly different from the Pali in arrangement and occasionally content, demonstrating that the collection took shape over time.

## Historical Context

The Dhammapada's verses are generally dated to the earliest period of Buddhist literary composition, though the collection was likely assembled over several centuries. Academic scholars study the text for insights into early Buddhist ethics, psychology, and soteriology.
