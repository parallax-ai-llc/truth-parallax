The Yamakavagga's 20 verses are among the most studied in academic Buddhist scholarship. The opening verses, declaring that mind (mano) precedes all states (dhamma), have been the subject of extensive philological analysis. Scholars debate whether 'dhamma' refers to mental states specifically or to all phenomena.

Comparative analysis across the Pali, Gandhari, and Chinese recensions reveals both striking agreements and instructive differences. K.R. Norman's translation provides detailed linguistic analysis, while scholars such as Mark Siderits have explored the verses' implications for Buddhist theories of mind.
