Academic analysis of these 11 verses engages with fundamental questions in early Buddhist psychology. The characterization of mind (citta) as flickering, far-ranging, and solitary provides evidence for the earliest Buddhist understanding of consciousness.

Comparative philological work examines how the Cittavagga's verses relate to parallel passages in the Samyutta Nikaya and Anguttara Nikaya. The chapter's teachings on mental training have also been examined in light of contemporary cognitive science.
