---
title: "Dhammapada - Chapter 22"
scripture: "dhammapada"
chapter: 22
---

**305** If a man has done wrong and knows it, if repenting he goes down into hell, he is freed from the evil path, as he is freed from the punishment for killing.

**306** He who, without being asked, tells his fault — how much more, if he is asked! He is indeed freed from the evil path.

**307** "I will not do this again," if a man says so and changes, there is no better repentance.

**308** As a man who gathers flowers, and whose mind is distracted, him death carries off, as a flood carries off a sleeping village.

**309** As a man who gathers flowers, and whose mind is distracted, him the destroyer catches before he is satiated in his pleasures.

**310** Let a wise man blow off his own impurities, as a smith blows off the impurities of silver, one by one, little by little, and from time to time.

**311** As rust sprung from iron, eats into its own source, so do their own deeds lead transgressors to an evil state.

**312** Non-repetition is the bane of prayers; neglect is the bane of a house; sloth is the bane of beauty; thoughtlessness is the bane of a watchman.

**313** Bad conduct is the bane of woman; greediness is the bane of a benefactor; bad qualities are indeed banes, in this world and in the next.

**314** But there is a bane worse than all banes — ignorance is the greatest bane. O mendicants! throw off that bane, and become bane-free!

**315** Life is easy for a man who is without shame, bold as a crow, a backbiter, forward, arrogant, and dissolute.

**316** But life is hard for a modest man, who always looks for what is pure, who is free from attachment, quiet, spotless, and intelligent.

**317** He who destroys life, who speaks untruth, who in this world takes what is not given him, who goes to another man's wife;

**318** And the man who gives himself to drinking intoxicating liquors, he, even in this world, digs up his own root.

**319** O man, know this, that the unrestrained are in a bad state; take care that greediness and vice do not bring thee to grief for a long time.
