## Overview

The Hermetic reading of the Book of the Dead approaches these ancient texts as repositories of esoteric wisdom that prefigure and inform the later Hermetic tradition. Hermeticists see in the Egyptian funerary spells a sophisticated system of spiritual transformation, initiatory knowledge, and theurgic practice that was later systematized in the Corpus Hermeticum and related texts.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Spiritual Alchemy
Hermetic interpreters read the journey through the Duat as an allegory for the alchemical process of spiritual transformation — the dissolution and reconstitution of the soul through stages corresponding to nigredo, albedo, and rubedo.

### Initiatory Knowledge
The spells of the Book of the Dead are understood as encoding initiatory knowledge — secret teachings about the nature of the soul, the structure of the cosmos, and the path to divine union that were transmitted through temple mysteries.

### Theurgic Practice
The performative dimension of the spells — their power to effect change through ritual utterance — is seen as an early form of theurgy, the practice of invoking divine powers to achieve spiritual transformation.
