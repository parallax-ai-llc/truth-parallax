## Overview

Archaeological study of the Book of the Dead examines these texts in their material context — the tombs, coffins, papyri, and burial goods with which they were deposited. This approach treats the texts not merely as literary compositions but as physical objects embedded in specific funerary practices and social contexts.

## Key Archaeological Approaches

### Papyrus Studies
Archaeologists study the physical characteristics of Book of the Dead papyri — their manufacture, dimensions, quality, script, and illustrations — to understand production methods, dating, provenance, and the social status of their owners.

### Tomb Context
The placement of papyri within the burial assemblage, alongside other funerary goods, provides crucial evidence for understanding how these texts functioned within the broader funerary ritual.

### Regional Variation
Archaeological evidence reveals significant regional variation in the selection, ordering, and illustration of spells, reflecting local religious traditions and workshop practices.

### Chronological Development
Material evidence tracks the evolution of the Book of the Dead from the earliest New Kingdom examples through the Ptolemaic period, documenting changes in format, content, and artistic style.

## Key Archaeological Sites

| Site | Significance |
|------|-------------|

| Saqqara | Important for later period examples |

| Valley of the Kings | Royal examples and tomb decoration |
