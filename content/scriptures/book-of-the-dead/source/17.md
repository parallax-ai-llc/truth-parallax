**Chapter XVII: The Chapter of the Liturgy of Funeral Offerings**

**1** The Osiris Ani says: "I am Atum when I am alone in Nu; I am Ra when he rises in the beginning, when he begins to rule that which he has made.

**2** What is this? It is Ra when he begins to rule that which he has made. It is when Ra began to appear as king, as one who existed before the Pillar of Shu had been raised.

**3** He was on the high ground which is in Khemenu. He had received the word of destruction of the enemies from the dwellers in Khemenu.

**4** I am the great god who came into being by himself. Who is he? The great god who came into being by himself is Nu; he is water, he is Nu, father of the gods.

**5** Another says: He is Ra. He who created the names of the parts of his body—these came into being as the gods who are in the following of Ra.

**6** I am the god who cannot be repulsed among the gods. Who is he? He is Atum in his sun-disk.

**7** Another says: He is Ra when he rises on the eastern horizon of heaven.

**8** I am Yesterday, I know Tomorrow. Who is he? As concerning Yesterday, that is Osiris. As concerning Tomorrow, that is Ra, on the day when he shall destroy the enemies of Neb-er-tcher.

**9** I am the divine Soul which dwells among the gods. Who is he? He is Osiris. When he goes to Tattu and finds there the soul of Ra, there the one god embraces the other, and divine souls spring into being within the divine twin-souls."
