## Overview

The Book of the Dead (*Pert Em Hru*, "Book of Coming Forth by Day") is a collection of ancient Egyptian funerary texts consisting of spells, prayers, and incantations designed to assist the deceased in navigating the afterlife. Composed over a period spanning roughly from the 16th century BCE to the 1st century BCE, these texts were typically written on papyrus scrolls and placed in the coffin or burial chamber with the dead.

The collection represents the culmination of a long tradition of Egyptian funerary literature, drawing upon earlier Pyramid Texts and Coffin Texts while adapting them for use by non-royal individuals. The spells address every conceivable challenge the deceased might face in the Duat (underworld), from hostile creatures to the final judgment before Osiris.

## Structure

The Book of the Dead does not have a single canonical form; the selection and ordering of spells varied by period, region, and the wealth of the deceased. However, Egyptologist Karl Richard Lepsius established a conventional numbering system of approximately 190 spells based on a Ptolemaic-period manuscript. For the purposes of this collection, 42 representative spell groupings have been organized thematically:

1. **Spells 1-10**: Entering the Duat and initial protections
2. **Spells 11-20**: Knowledge of the gods and sacred geography
3. **Spells 21-30**: Transformation spells and divine powers
4. **Spells 31-42**: The judgment hall, the weighing of the heart, and final transfiguration

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Book of the Dead holds significance across multiple scholarly and spiritual traditions:

- **Academic**: A primary source for understanding Egyptian religion, language, and funerary practices
- **Comparative**: Parallels with Greek, Tibetan, and Mesoamerican afterlife literature
- **Hermetic**: A foundational influence on later Hermetic and esoteric traditions
- **Theological**: Raises profound questions about justice, immortality, and cosmic order
- **Archaeological**: Provides key evidence for burial customs and religious evolution
- **Interfaith**: Offers insights into universal human concerns about death, judgment, and the afterlife

## Key Themes

| Protective Magic | Spells and incantations to ward off dangers in the underworld |
