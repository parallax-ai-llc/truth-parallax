## Overview

The Prose Edda is the single most important primary source for the academic study of Norse mythology. Scholars analyze Snorri Sturluson's literary methods, his relationship to earlier sources (especially the Poetic Edda and skaldic verse), and the degree to which his Christian perspective shaped his presentation of pre-Christian traditions. Academic study involves careful source criticism, philological analysis, and consideration of the text's manuscript tradition and historical context.
