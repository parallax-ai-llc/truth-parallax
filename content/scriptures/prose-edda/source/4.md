---
title: "Skáldskaparmál (The Language of Poetry)"
section: 4
---

There was one called Ægir, or Hlér. He dwelt on the island which is now called Hlésey. He was deeply versed in magic. He took his way to Ásgard, and when the Æsir became aware of his journey, he was received with great hospitality; yet many things had illusory appearances. And at evening, when they had begun to drink, Odin had swords brought into the hall, and they were so bright that light radiated from them; and no other light was used while they sat at drinking.

Then the Æsir established their banquet, and twelve Æsir who were appointed to be judges seated themselves in their high-seats. Their names are as follows: Thor, Njördr, Freyr, Týr, Heimdallr, Bragi, Vídarr, Váli, Ullr, Hoenir, Forseti, Loki.

Then Ægir spake to Bragi: "Whence did that art of poetry which ye call poesy come?"

Bragi answered: "The origin of it was this. The gods had a dispute with the folk called Vanir, and they appointed a peace-meeting between them and established truce in this way: they each went to a vat and spat their spittle therein. Then at parting the gods took that sign of truce and would not let it perish, but shaped thereof a man. This man is called Kvasir, and he was so wise that none could question him concerning anything but that he knew the solution.

"He went up and down the earth to give instruction to men; and when he came upon invitation to the dwelling of certain dwarves, Fjalar and Galar, they called him into a secret parley with them, and killed him, letting his blood run into two vats and a kettle. The kettle is named Ódrerir, and the vats Són and Bodn; they blended honey with the blood, and the outcome was that mead by the virtue of which he who drinks becomes a skald or scholar. The dwarves reported to the Æsir that Kvasir had choked on his own knowledge, since there was none so learned as to be able to question his wisdom."

Thus all the art of poetry descends from the blood of Kvasir, mixed with honey and transformed into the mead of inspiration which Odin himself stole from the giants and brought to Asgard, spraying it from his mouth for gods and men.
