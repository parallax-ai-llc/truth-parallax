---
title: "Gylfaginning (continued) — The Ash Yggdrasil and the Doom of the Gods"
section: 3
---

Then said Gangleri: "Where is the chief abode or holy place of the gods?"

High One answered: "That is at the Ash of Yggdrasil; there the gods must give judgment every day."

Then said Gangleri: "What is to be said concerning that place?"

Just-as-High said: "The Ash is greatest of all trees and best: its limbs spread out over all the world and stand above heaven. Three roots of the tree uphold it and stand exceeding broad: one is among the Æsir; another among the frost-giants, where the Yawning Void once was; the third stands over Niflheim, and under that root is Hvergelmir, and Nithhogg gnaws the root from below.

"But under that root which turns toward the frost-giants is the well of Mimir, wherein wisdom and understanding are stored; and he is called Mimir, who keeps the well. He is full of ancient lore, since he drinks of the well from the Horn Gjallarhorn. Allfather came there and craved one drink of the well, but he got it not until he had laid his eye in pledge."

Then said Gangleri: "What tidings are to be told concerning Ragnarök? I have not heard tell of this before."

High One said: "Great tidings are to be told of it, and much. First shall come the Fimbulwinter, in which snow shall drive from all quarters; frosts shall be great then, and winds sharp; there shall be no virtue in the sun. Those winters shall proceed three in succession, and no summer between. But first shall come three other winters, during which there shall be great battles throughout all the world. Then brothers shall slay each other for greed's sake, and none shall spare father or son in manslaughter and in incest.

"The Wolf shall swallow the Sun; that shall seem a great loss to men. Then the other wolf shall seize the Moon, and he also shall work great ruin. The stars shall vanish from the heavens. Then shall come to pass these tidings also: all the earth shall tremble so that mountains shall be shattered, and trees uprooted, and all fetters shall be broken and burst."
