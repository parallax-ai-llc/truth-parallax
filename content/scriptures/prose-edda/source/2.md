title: "Gylfaginning (continued) — The Making of the World"
section: 2
---

Then said Gangleri: "How were things wrought, ere the races were and the tribes of men increased?"

High One said: "The sons of Borr slew Ymir the giant. And when he fell, there gushed from his wounds so great a sea of blood that all the race of frost-giants was drowned therein, save one who escaped with his household; him the giants call Bergelmir. He went up on his boat, and his wife with him, and there they were preserved. And from them are come the races of frost-giants."

Then said Gangleri: "What did the sons of Borr do then, if thou believest that they are gods?"

High One said: "There is not a little to be told thereof. They took Ymir and bore him into the middle of the Yawning Void, and made of him the earth: of his blood the sea and the waters; the land was made of his flesh, and the crags of his bones; gravel and stones they made of his teeth and his grinders and those bones that were broken."

Just-as-High said: "Of the blood, which ran and welled forth freely from his wounds, they made the sea, when they had formed and made firm the earth together, and laid the sea in a ring round about her; and it may well seem a hard thing to most men to cross over it."

Third said: "They took his skull also, and made of it the heaven, and set it up over the earth with four corners; and under each corner they set a dwarf. They are named thus: East, West, North, and South. Then they took the glowing embers and sparks that burst forth and had been cast out of Muspellheim, and set them in the midst of the Yawning Void, in the heaven, both above and below, to illuminate heaven and earth. They assigned places to all fires: to some in heaven, some wandered free under the heavens; nevertheless, to these also they gave a place, and shaped their courses."

Then said Gangleri: "This is a great work of craft. How was the earth fashioned?"

High One said: "She is ring-shaped without, and round about her without lies the deep sea; and along the strand of that sea they gave lands to the races of giants for habitation. But on the inner earth they made a citadel round about the world against the hostility of the giants, and for their citadel they raised up the brows of Ymir the giant, and called that place Midgard."
