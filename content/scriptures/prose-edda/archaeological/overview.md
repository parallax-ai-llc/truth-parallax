## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Prose Edda connect Snorri's literary mythography to material evidence from the Viking Age and earlier periods. While Snorri wrote in thirteenth-century Iceland, the myths he records reflect traditions stretching back centuries, and archaeological discoveries — from cult sites and ritual deposits to iconographic representations on metalwork and stone — provide independent evidence for the religious practices and beliefs that underlie his literary account.
