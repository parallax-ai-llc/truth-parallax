## Overview

The Prose Edda is a masterwork of medieval Icelandic literature and one of the most important texts in the history of poetic theory. Snorri's frame narratives — the dialogue between Gylfi and the enthroned figures of Asgard, the conversation between Aegir and Bragi — are sophisticated literary devices that allow him to present mythological knowledge in an engaging dramatic form. Skáldskaparmál constitutes a comprehensive treatise on poetic diction, and Háttatal demonstrates extraordinary metrical virtuosity.
