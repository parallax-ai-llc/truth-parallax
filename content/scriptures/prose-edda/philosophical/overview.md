## Overview

The Prose Edda raises profound philosophical questions about the nature of myth, cultural transmission, and the relationship between religion and literature. Snorri's euhemeristic Prologue — which reinterprets the Norse gods as deified human kings from Troy — represents a philosophical strategy for reconciling Christian monotheism with respect for the pagan literary tradition. The mythological narratives themselves explore fate and free will, the relationship between wisdom and sacrifice, and the meaning of existence in a cosmos destined for destruction.
