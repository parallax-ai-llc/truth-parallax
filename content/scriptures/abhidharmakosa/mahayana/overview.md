## Overview

The Abhidharmakosa became a foundational text for Mahayana Buddhist education, particularly in East Asia, where it was translated into Chinese by Xuanzang (602-664) and Paramartha (499-569). In Chinese, Korean, and Japanese Buddhist monasteries, the Kosa (*Jushe lun* / 俱舍論) was studied as the essential philosophical foundation upon which Mahayana doctrines — emptiness (*sunyata*), Buddha-nature, and the bodhisattva path — were built. The text was understood as providing a rigorous analysis of conventional reality that Mahayana philosophy then transcended.

Mahayana engagement with the Kosa is particularly enriched by the fact that Vasubandhu himself later became one of the founders of the Yogacara (Mind-Only) school. The Kosa thus represents the earlier, more analytically realist phase of a philosopher who would subsequently develop a radically idealist position. East Asian Buddhist traditions have long been fascinated by this philosophical trajectory and its implications for understanding the relationship between Abhidharma analysis and Mahayana insight.
