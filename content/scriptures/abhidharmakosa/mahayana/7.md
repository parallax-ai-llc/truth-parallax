Chapter 7 of the Abhidharmakosa addresses the types of knowledge (*jnana*) and wisdom (*prajna*), a subject that Mahayana Buddhism engages with from the perspective of the *sunyata* (emptiness) and *bodhisattva* doctrines. While the Kosa's analysis represents the Sarvastivada (and Sautrantika) perspective, Mahayana commentators read it as providing a rigorous analysis of conventional reality that the Madhyamaka and Yogacara philosophies then transcend or reinterpret.

In the East Asian Buddhist tradition, the epistemological framework of Buddhist soteriology — how knowledge leads to liberation was studied as foundational philosophical training for monks and scholars. The Chinese translation by Xuanzang ensured that the Kosa's systematic analysis became the standard philosophical vocabulary for Buddhist discourse in China, Korea, and Japan. Mahayana readers engage with this chapter as a sophisticated but ultimately provisional analysis — a map of conventional reality that must be complemented by the insight into emptiness that characterizes the Mahayana path.
