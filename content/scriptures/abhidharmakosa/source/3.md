---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 3: Loka-nirdesa (Analysis of the World)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The world is constituted by beings and their receptacles. The receptacle-world is the external environment in which beings live. The being-world consists of the sentient beings themselves, classified according to their realms of existence.

**2** There are three realms: the desire realm, the form realm, and the formless realm. The desire realm includes the hells, the animal realm, the hungry ghost realm, the human realm, and the six heavens of desire.

**3** The form realm consists of seventeen heavens arranged in four meditation levels. The first meditation level has three heavens: Brahma-parisadya, Brahma-purohita, and Maha-brahma. The second, third, and fourth meditation levels each have their own heavens.

**4** The formless realm has four levels: the sphere of infinite space, the sphere of infinite consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception.

**5** At the bottom of the world lies the great circle of wind, upon which rests the circle of water, upon which rests the circle of gold — the earth. Mount Sumeru stands at the center, surrounded by seven mountain ranges and four continents.

**6** The hells are located beneath this continent of Jambudvipa. There are eight hot hells and eight cold hells. The duration of suffering in each hell is immensely long, increasing from one to the next.

**7** Beings are born into these various realms according to their karma. Wholesome actions lead to birth in the higher realms; unwholesome actions lead to birth in the lower realms. The heaviness, proximity, habitual nature, and priority of the deed determine the specific result.

**8** The world undergoes periodic cycles of destruction and formation. A great aeon consists of four intermediate aeons: the aeon of destruction, the aeon of remaining destroyed, the aeon of formation, and the aeon of continuance.

**9** During the aeon of destruction, beings gradually disappear from the lower realms, being reborn in higher realms. At the end of the process, the receptacle-world itself is destroyed by fire, water, or wind, depending on the cycle.

**10** After the aeon of remaining destroyed, the world-system is reformed through the collective karma of beings. First the receptacle-world appears; then beings gradually descend from the higher realms to populate it. Thus the cycle of formation and destruction continues without discoverable beginning.
