---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 5: Anusaya-nirdesa (Analysis of the Latent Defilements)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The latent defilements are six in number: attachment to sensual pleasure, attachment to existence, aversion, pride, ignorance, and wrong view. Some enumerate them as seven, dividing wrong view into view and doubt.

**2** More extensively, there are ninety-eight latent defilements, classified according to the three realms and the nine stages. Ten belong to the stage of the truths to be abandoned by the path of seeing in the desire realm, and further defilements are enumerated for each subsequent stage.

**3** The latent defilements are called "latent" because they lie dormant in the mental series, ready to manifest when the appropriate conditions arise. Even when not actively occurring, they have not been abandoned and continue to bind beings to the cycle of existence.

**4** Attachment to sensual pleasure is the craving for the objects of the five senses. Attachment to existence is the craving for continued being, whether in the form or formless realms. These two forms of attachment propel beings into rebirth.

**5** Aversion is ill-will, hostility, and displeasure toward beings and conditions. It exists only in the desire realm, for in the form and formless realms, beings have temporarily suppressed aversion through meditation.

**6** Pride is of seven kinds: pride proper, excessive pride, pride of pride, pride of thinking "I am," conceit, false pride of inferiority, and wrong pride. Pride operates through the comparison of oneself with others.

**7** Ignorance is the non-knowledge of the Four Noble Truths. It accompanies all unwholesome mental states and is the root of the entire round of existence. Without ignorance, no other defilement can arise.

**8** Wrong view is fivefold: the view of a self in the five aggregates, extreme views of eternalism or annihilationism, the denial of cause and effect, the grasping of wrong views as supreme, and the grasping of rules and vows as sufficient for purification.

**9** The defilements are abandoned at two stages: some are abandoned by the path of seeing — that is, the initial insight into the Four Noble Truths — and some are abandoned by the path of cultivation — that is, the repeated development of insight and meditation.

**10** When all ninety-eight latent defilements are abandoned, one attains Arahatship and is no longer bound to the cycle of rebirth. The Arahat has completed the task, lived the holy life, done what had to be done, and laid down the burden.
