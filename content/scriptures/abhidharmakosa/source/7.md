---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 7: Jnana-nirdesa (Analysis of Knowledge)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Knowledge is of ten kinds: knowledge of dharmas, knowledge of subsequent application, conventional knowledge, knowledge of suffering, knowledge of its origin, knowledge of its cessation, knowledge of the path, knowledge of the minds of others, knowledge of extinction, and knowledge of non-arising.

**2** Knowledge of dharmas is the undefiled knowledge that takes as its object the dharmas of the desire realm. Knowledge of subsequent application is the undefiled knowledge applied to the dharmas of the form and formless realms.

**3** Conventional knowledge is the worldly correct knowledge — the understanding of things as they conventionally are. It is the only knowledge that is both defiled and undefiled, and it extends to all objects without limitation.

**4** Knowledge of suffering is the penetrative understanding that the five aggregates of clinging are suffering: impermanent, unsatisfactory, and without self. This knowledge arises through contemplation of the three characteristics.

**5** Knowledge of the origin is the penetrative understanding that craving is the origin of suffering, together with delight and lust, finding delight here and there — craving for sensual pleasure, craving for existence, craving for non-existence.

**6** Knowledge of cessation is the penetrative understanding that the complete fading away and cessation of craving is the cessation of suffering: the relinquishment of craving, the liberation from craving, the detachment from craving.

**7** Knowledge of the path is the penetrative understanding that the Noble Eightfold Path is the way leading to the cessation of suffering, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, to Nirvana.

**8** Knowledge of the minds of others is the ability to know directly the mental states of other beings — whether their minds are with lust or without lust, with hatred or without hatred, with delusion or without delusion, concentrated or unconcentrated, liberated or not liberated.

**9** Knowledge of extinction is the Arahat's knowledge: "Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being." This knowledge belongs only to the Arahat.

**10** Knowledge of non-arising is the knowledge that the defilements which have been abandoned will never arise again. This certain knowledge that the defilements are permanently extinguished is the final security and the ground of the Arahat's unshakeable peace.
