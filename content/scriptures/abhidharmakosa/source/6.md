---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 6: Marga-pudgala-nirdesa (Analysis of the Path and the Noble Persons)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The path leading to the cessation of suffering is the Noble Eightfold Path: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These are the path in the sense of leading to Nirvana.

**2** The path of seeing consists of fifteen moments of consciousness, during which the practitioner gains the first direct insight into the Four Noble Truths. At each truth, there is a moment of patient acceptance and a moment of knowledge, for both the desire realm and the higher realms.

**3** The sixteenth moment is the first moment of the path of cultivation. At this point, the practitioner has become a Stream-enterer and has irrevocably entered the path leading to liberation.

**4** The Stream-enterer has abandoned the three fetters and is destined to attain Nirvana within at most seven more existences. The Stream-enterer will never again be born in the lower realms and possesses unshakeable confidence in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha.

**5** The Once-returner has weakened the defilements of sensual desire and aversion. After returning to the human world once more, the Once-returner will attain final liberation.

**6** The Non-returner has completely abandoned the five lower fetters. After death, the Non-returner is reborn in the Pure Abodes of the form realm and attains Nirvana there without returning to the desire realm.

**7** The Arahat has abandoned all defilements without remainder. The Arahat is of two kinds: the one liberated by wisdom alone, and the one liberated in both ways — that is, through both wisdom and the formless attainments.

**8** The Pratyekabuddha attains enlightenment independently, without the guidance of a teacher, in an era when no Buddha's teaching is available. The Pratyekabuddha is superior to the Arahat-disciple in the depth and independence of realization.

**9** The Buddha is supreme among all beings. The Buddha has perfected the six paramitas over three incalculable aeons, accumulated immeasurable merit and wisdom, and attained the unsurpassed, complete enlightenment. The Buddha possesses the ten powers, the four fearlessnesses, and the eighteen special qualities of a Buddha.

**10** The four fruits of the path are: Stream-entry, Once-returning, Non-returning, and Arahatship. These are attained through the progressive abandonment of the defilements and the development of the eightfold path. Through these fruits, beings are freed from the cycle of birth and death.
