---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 4: Karma-nirdesa (Analysis of Action)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The variety of the world arises from action. Action is volition and what is done through volition. Volition is mental action. What is done through volition is bodily and vocal action.

**2** Bodily and vocal actions are of two kinds: informative and non-informative. Informative action is the bodily gesture or vocal utterance that makes one's intention known to others. Non-informative action is the imperceptible form that arises from informative action and continues in the series.

**3** The courses of action are tenfold: three of body — killing, stealing, sexual misconduct; four of speech — lying, divisive speech, harsh speech, idle talk; three of mind — covetousness, malice, wrong view.

**4** Killing is the intentional taking of the life of a living being. The full course of action requires five factors: a living being, the perception that it is a living being, the intention to kill, the effort, and the consequent death.

**5** The ten wholesome courses of action are the opposites: abstaining from killing, abstaining from stealing, abstaining from sexual misconduct, abstaining from lying, from divisive speech, from harsh speech, from idle talk, non-covetousness, non-malice, and right view.

**6** Actions bear fruit in three ways: some ripen in this very life, some ripen in the next life, and some ripen in subsequent lives. No wholesome or unwholesome action is ever lost; each inevitably produces its result when the conditions are ripe.

**7** There are actions whose results are definite and actions whose results are indefinite. Actions done intentionally, with full awareness, and with intensity are definite in their result. Actions done without intention or halfheartedly may be indefinite.

**8** Meritorious action is of three kinds: giving, moral conduct, and meditation. These are the three bases of meritorious action. Giving leads to wealth; moral conduct leads to good birth; meditation leads to the attainments of the form and formless realms.

**9** Demeritorious action arises from the three roots of unwholesome: greed, hatred, and delusion. Of these three, wrong view is the most dangerous, for it can cut off the roots of merit entirely and lead to the avici hell for an incalculable period.

**10** The five actions of immediate retribution are: killing one's mother, killing one's father, killing an Arahat, shedding the blood of a Buddha with evil intent, and causing a schism in the Sangha. These ripen inevitably in the very next existence with birth in the avici hell.
