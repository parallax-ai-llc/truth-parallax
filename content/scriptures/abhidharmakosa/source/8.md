---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 8: Samapatti-nirdesa (Analysis of the Attainments)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The meditative attainments are of two types: worldly and supramundane. The worldly attainments are the four form-realm absorptions and the four formless-realm attainments. The supramundane attainments are the fruits of the path.

**2** The first absorption is attained by one who, secluded from sensual desires and unwholesome states, enters and abides in the first meditation, which is accompanied by applied thought, sustained thought, joy, and happiness born of seclusion.

**3** The second absorption is attained by the subsiding of applied and sustained thought. One enters and abides in the second meditation, which has internal confidence and unification of mind, is without applied thought and sustained thought, and has joy and happiness born of concentration.

**4** The third absorption is attained with the fading away of joy. One abides equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending, and experiences happiness with the body. This is the meditation of which the Noble Ones declare: "One who is equanimous and mindful abides in happiness."

**5** The fourth absorption is attained with the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the previous disappearance of gladness and sadness. One enters and abides in the fourth meditation, which has neither pain nor pleasure and has purity of mindfulness due to equanimity.

**6** The four formless attainments are: the sphere of infinite space, attained by completely surmounting perceptions of form; the sphere of infinite consciousness; the sphere of nothingness; and the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception.

**7** The attainment of cessation is entered by the Non-returner or Arahat who has mastered all eight attainments. In this attainment, all mental activity ceases. It is the closest approximation to Nirvana within the conditioned realm.

**8** The preparatory stages for each absorption involve the suppression of specific hindrances. For the first absorption, the five hindrances — sensual desire, ill-will, sloth-and-torpor, restlessness-and-worry, and doubt — must be suppressed.

**9** There are four kinds of meditators: one who is skilled in entering but not in abiding, one who is skilled in abiding but not in entering, one who is skilled in both, and one who is skilled in neither. The most excellent is the one skilled in both entering and abiding.

**10** Through the mastery of these attainments, the practitioner gains the basis for the development of insight, the supernormal powers, and finally the complete liberation of the mind. The attainments are not liberation itself but are the indispensable foundation for the arising of liberating wisdom.
