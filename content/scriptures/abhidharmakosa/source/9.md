---
title: "Abhidharmakosa Chapter 9: Pudgala-nirdesa (Refutation of the Person)"
scripture: "abhidharmakosa"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Some assert that a real, substantial person exists. The Vatsiputriyas claim that the person is neither identical with the aggregates nor different from them, that it is a real entity apprehended through the aggregates as fuel is apprehended through fire.

**2** We ask: Is this person conditioned or unconditioned? If conditioned, it must be impermanent and fall within the five aggregates. If unconditioned, it cannot act, experience, or transmigrate, and would be like empty space.

**3** The Blessed One has declared: "All conditioned things are impermanent. All conditioned things are suffering. All dharmas are without self." If a real person existed, the third statement would be false.

**4** The Blessed One has also said: "Monks, the body is not self. Feeling is not self. Perception is not self. Mental formations are not self. Consciousness is not self." Each aggregate is examined and shown to be not self. Where then would the person be found?

**5** They argue: If there is no person, who transmigrates? We reply: It is the continuity of the aggregates that transmigrates. Just as a flame passes from one candle to another, so consciousness-series continues from one life to the next, without any unchanging entity passing over.

**6** They argue: If there is no person, who performs actions and experiences their results? We reply: It is the conventional person — the series of aggregates — that acts and experiences. The Buddha used conventional language when speaking of beings performing actions and experiencing results.

**7** The Blessed One has said: "There is action and there is the fruit of action, but there is no agent apart from the action." When wood is burning, we say "fire is burning the wood," but there is no fire apart from the burning of the wood.

**8** They argue: If there is no person, what is it that remembers past lives? We reply: Memory arises from a consciousness conditioned by a previous consciousness. The connection is through causal continuity, not through an enduring entity that was present then and is present now.

**9** The teaching of non-self does not lead to nihilism. The aggregates exist; causes and effects exist; the path exists; liberation exists. What does not exist is an unchanging, independent, substantial self over and above the aggregates.

**10** This refutation of the person is the crown of the Abhidharma. For it is precisely the belief in a self that is the root of all suffering. When this belief is thoroughly abandoned through wisdom, the practitioner attains liberation. As the Blessed One said: "The destruction of the belief in self is the destruction of the entire mass of suffering."
