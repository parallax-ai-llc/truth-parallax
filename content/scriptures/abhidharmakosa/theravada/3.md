Chapter 3 of the Abhidharmakosa addresses Buddhist cosmology and the structure of the universe. From the Theravada perspective, this chapter invites comparison with the corresponding analysis in the Theravada Abhidhamma tradition, particularly as systematized by Buddhaghosa in the *Visuddhimagga* and *Atthasalini*. While the Sarvastivada and Theravada Abhidharma traditions share a common analytical project — the exhaustive classification of the constituents of experience — they diverge on significant philosophical points.

The Theravada tradition's engagement with the realms of existence, world-systems, and the relationship between moral order and cosmic structure differs from Vasubandhu's treatment in several respects, including the enumeration and classification of mental factors (*cetasikas* in Pali, *caitta* in Sanskrit), the ontological status of *dharmas* across the three times, and the precise analysis of the path to liberation. Comparative study of these differences illuminates the range of philosophical options available within the early Buddhist analytical tradition.
