## Overview

The Theravada tradition possesses its own distinct Abhidhamma system, codified in the seven books of the Abhidhamma Pitaka and elaborated by Buddhaghosa in the *Visuddhimagga* and the *Atthasalini*. From the Theravada perspective, the Abhidharmakosa represents a parallel but distinct enterprise — the Sarvastivada systematization of Abhidharma — that diverges from the Theravada system on several key points, including the ontological status of *dharmas* across the three times, the nature of the enlightened mind, and the classification of mental factors.

Comparative study between the Theravada Abhidhamma and Vasubandhu's Kosa has been a fruitful area of scholarship, illuminating both the shared analytical heritage and the distinctive philosophical commitments of each tradition. Theravada scholars engage with the Kosa as a sophisticated alternative systematization that sharpens understanding of their own tradition's positions on consciousness, karma, and the path to liberation.
