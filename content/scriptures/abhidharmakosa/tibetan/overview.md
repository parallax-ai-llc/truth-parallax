## Overview

The Abhidharmakosa (*mngon pa mdzod* in Tibetan) occupies a central position in the Tibetan Buddhist monastic curriculum, where it is one of the "Five Great Treatises" (*bstan bcos chen po lnga*) studied in all major scholastic traditions, particularly the Gelug school. Tibetan monks typically spend one to two years studying the Kosa in detail, memorizing the root verses and engaging in rigorous philosophical debate (*rtsod pa*) on its contents. The text provides the foundational philosophical vocabulary and analytical framework upon which all subsequent Mahayana and Vajrayana study is built.

The Tibetan commentarial tradition on the Abhidharmakosa is vast and sophisticated. Major commentaries include works by Chim Jampaiyang, Yonten Gyatso, and the First Dalai Lama Gendun Drup. Tibetan scholars have been particularly interested in the Kosa's analysis of consciousness and mental factors, its cosmological system, and the debates between Vasubandhu and the Sarvastivada orthodox positions — debates that prefigure the broader Tibetan philosophical engagement with questions of realism and idealism.
