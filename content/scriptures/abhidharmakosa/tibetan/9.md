Chapter 9 of the Abhidharmakosa addresses the refutation of a permanent self (*pudgala*), a subject that occupies a significant portion of the Tibetan monastic curriculum. Tibetan scholars study this chapter as part of the *mngon pa* (Abhidharma) course, typically spending several months on detailed analysis, memorization of the root verses, and philosophical debate (*rtsod pa*). The major Tibetan commentaries — by Chim Jampaiyang, the First Dalai Lama, and Yonten Gyatso — provide extensive explanations that integrate the Kosa's analysis with the broader Tibetan philosophical framework.

The Tibetan tradition's engagement with Vasubandhu's philosophical arguments against the *Pudgalavada* (Personalist) school's claim that a 'person' exists as a real entity is shaped by its commitment to the *grub mtha'* (tenets systems) approach, which presents the various Buddhist philosophical schools as a progressive hierarchy leading to the Madhyamaka view. Within this framework, the Kosa's Sarvastivada-Sautrantika analysis represents a provisional but essential stage of philosophical understanding — a necessary foundation for the subtler analyses of the Madhyamaka and Yogacara schools.
