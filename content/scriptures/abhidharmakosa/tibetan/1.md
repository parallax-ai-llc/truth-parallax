Chapter 1 of the Abhidharmakosa addresses the basic elements (*dhatus*) of existence — the 18 elements, 12 sense bases, and 5 aggregates, a subject that occupies a significant portion of the Tibetan monastic curriculum. Tibetan scholars study this chapter as part of the *mngon pa* (Abhidharma) course, typically spending several months on detailed analysis, memorization of the root verses, and philosophical debate (*rtsod pa*). The major Tibetan commentaries — by Chim Jampaiyang, the First Dalai Lama, and Yonten Gyatso — provide extensive explanations that integrate the Kosa's analysis with the broader Tibetan philosophical framework.

The Tibetan tradition's engagement with Vasubandhu's systematic taxonomy of the fundamental constituents of experience is shaped by its commitment to the *grub mtha'* (tenets systems) approach, which presents the various Buddhist philosophical schools as a progressive hierarchy leading to the Madhyamaka view. Within this framework, the Kosa's Sarvastivada-Sautrantika analysis represents a provisional but essential stage of philosophical understanding — a necessary foundation for the subtler analyses of the Madhyamaka and Yogacara schools.
