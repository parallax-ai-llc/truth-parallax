Chapter 4 of the Abhidharmakosa addresses the theory of karma — intentional action and its consequences, which has generated extensive scholarly analysis. Academic engagement with this chapter spans multiple disciplines: textual criticism (comparing the Sanskrit, Chinese, and Tibetan versions and reconstructing the original text), philosophical analysis (examining Vasubandhu's arguments and their relationship to earlier and later Buddhist philosophical developments), and comparative philosophy (relating Vasubandhu's analysis of moral causation, the types of karmic action, and their results to analogous discussions in Western and other Asian philosophical traditions).

Key scholarly debates concerning this chapter include the relationship between Vasubandhu's stated Sautrantika sympathies and the Sarvastivada framework he expounds, the philosophical implications of his analytical categories for contemporary philosophy of mind and metaphysics, and the historical development of the concepts and arguments presented. The chapter's technical precision and argumentative rigor have made it a focal point for philosophers seeking to engage seriously with Buddhist thought as a living philosophical tradition.
