Chapter 5 of the Abhidharmakosa addresses the latent defilements (*anusaya*) that perpetuate suffering, which has generated extensive scholarly analysis. Academic engagement with this chapter spans multiple disciplines: textual criticism (comparing the Sanskrit, Chinese, and Tibetan versions and reconstructing the original text), philosophical analysis (examining Vasubandhu's arguments and their relationship to earlier and later Buddhist philosophical developments), and comparative philosophy (relating the analysis of the root mental afflictions — desire, aversion, ignorance — and their relationship to suffering and rebirth to analogous discussions in Western and other Asian philosophical traditions).

Key scholarly debates concerning this chapter include the relationship between Vasubandhu's stated Sautrantika sympathies and the Sarvastivada framework he expounds, the philosophical implications of his analytical categories for contemporary philosophy of mind and metaphysics, and the historical development of the concepts and arguments presented. The chapter's technical precision and argumentative rigor have made it a focal point for philosophers seeking to engage seriously with Buddhist thought as a living philosophical tradition.
