Zen engagement with this chapter on Purification of Virtue and the Ascetic Practices reflects an appreciation for practical meditation instruction while maintaining Zen's characteristic emphasis on direct experience over systematic categorization. The Visuddhimagga's detailed treatment of ascetic practices (dhutanga) as voluntary austerities that refine monastic discipline provides analytical precision that can illuminate Zen practice, even though Zen typically avoids such elaborate classification.

Zen readers note that the experiential realities described in this chapter — the purification that comes from ethical discipline — are not foreign to Zen practice but are expressed in Zen through different pedagogical means, particularly through the master-student relationship and the koan tradition.
