Zen engagement with this chapter on the path and not-path reflects an appreciation for practical meditation instruction while maintaining Zen's characteristic emphasis on direct experience over systematic categorization. The Visuddhimagga's detailed treatment of discerning true progress from the subtle corruptions of insight provides analytical precision that can illuminate Zen practice, even though Zen typically avoids such elaborate classification.

Zen readers note that the experiential realities described in this chapter — the progressive refinement of wisdom — are not foreign to Zen practice but are expressed in Zen through different pedagogical means, particularly through the master-student relationship and the koan tradition.
