## Overview

Tibetan Buddhist engagement with the Visuddhimagga involves comparative study alongside Tibetan systematic treatises such as Tsongkhapa's Lamrim Chenmo and Gampopa's Jewel Ornament of Liberation. While these Tibetan works include Mahayana and Vajrayana elements absent from Buddhaghosa's text, the Visuddhimagga's treatment of shamatha (samatha) meditation, the mental factors, and the stages of purification provides valuable comparative material.

## Shared Concerns

Both the Visuddhimagga and Tibetan path literature share the conviction that the spiritual path must be presented systematically, with clear stages and well-defined practices for each stage. The Visuddhimagga's forty meditation subjects have parallels in the Tibetan meditation curriculum, and its analysis of the jhanas informs Tibetan understanding of shamatha attainment.

## Comparative Value

Tibetan scholars have noted both convergences and divergences between the Visuddhimagga's Theravada Abhidhamma framework and the Sarvastivada-Vaibhashika Abhidharma that forms the primary foundation for Tibetan Buddhist philosophy.
