Tibetan Buddhist engagement with this chapter on the path and not-path involves comparison with corresponding treatments in Tibetan systematic literature. The Visuddhimagga's analysis of discerning true progress from the subtle corruptions of insight finds parallels in Tibetan meditation manuals, though Tibetan presentations typically integrate Mahayana and Vajrayana elements that Buddhaghosa's Theravada framework does not include.

Tibetan scholars note both convergences and divergences between the Visuddhimagga's Theravada Abhidhamma categories and the Sarvastivada-Vaibhashika framework that underlies Tibetan philosophical education. These comparisons contribute to a richer understanding of the diversity within the shared Buddhist analytical tradition.
