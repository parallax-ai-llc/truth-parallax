---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 14: Khandha-niddesa (Description of the Aggregates)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Now begins the description of understanding. Understanding is the wisdom that penetrates the characteristics of phenomena. It is the third division of the threefold training, after virtue and concentration. Its soil is the purified mind.

**2** The field of understanding comprises the five aggregates, the twelve sense-bases, the eighteen elements, the twenty-two faculties, the Four Noble Truths, and dependent origination. First, the five aggregates.

**3** The aggregate of material form includes the four great elements — earth, water, fire, and air — and the material form derived from them. The earth element has the characteristic of hardness. The water element has the characteristic of cohesion. The fire element has the characteristic of heat. The air element has the characteristic of distension.

**4** Derived material form includes the five sense-organs, the four sense-objects of visible form, sound, smell, and taste, and other forms such as femininity, masculinity, the life-faculty, the heart-base, bodily intimation, verbal intimation, space-element, lightness, malleability, wieldiness, growth, continuity, decay, and impermanence.

**5** The aggregate of feeling is the experience of objects. It is of three kinds: pleasant, painful, and neither-pleasant-nor-painful. It is of five kinds when divided further: bodily pleasant, bodily painful, mentally pleasant, mentally painful, and equanimous.

**6** The aggregate of perception is the recognizing of objects. It perceives the distinctive marks of objects — blue, yellow, long, short, male, female, and so forth. It is the function of the mind that identifies and labels experience.

**7** The aggregate of mental formations includes all mental factors other than feeling and perception. There are fifty of these, including volition, attention, contact, desire, energy, joy, resolve, and many others. Volition is the most important, for it is the factor that directs the mind and determines karma.

**8** The aggregate of consciousness is the awareness of objects. It is classified by its basis as eye-consciousness, ear-consciousness, nose-consciousness, tongue-consciousness, body-consciousness, and mind-consciousness. It is the knowing element in all experience.

**9** These five aggregates arise and pass away in every moment. There is no moment when they are static. Their arising is conditioned, their passing away is conditioned, and their standing is conditioned. There is no master, no doer, no experiencer behind them.

**10** When a bhikkhu sees the five aggregates as impermanent, as suffering, and as not-self, he becomes disenchanted with them. Becoming disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate. Through dispassion, he is liberated. This is the purpose of understanding the aggregates.
