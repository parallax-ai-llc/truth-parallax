---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 18: Ditthi-visuddhi-niddesa (Purification of View)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 18
---

**1** Purification of view is the correct seeing of name-and-form. A bhikkhu who has developed concentration should emerge from jhana and contemplate the mental and material phenomena that constitute his being.

**2** He should discern material form by attending to the four great elements — the earth element as hardness or softness, the water element as fluidity or cohesion, the fire element as heat or cold, the air element as pushing or supporting — and the material form derived from them.

**3** He should discern name — mentality — by attending to the mind that knows the material form. When material form is discerned, the mind that knows it is also discerned. Thus both name and form are seen.

**4** When name-and-form have been discerned, the bhikkhu sees that there is only name-and-form, with no being, no person, no self behind them. The belief in a self is the view of personality, and this must be abandoned.

**5** The bhikkhu examines: "Is there a self that sees?" He finds only eye-sensitivity, visible form, and eye-consciousness. "Is there a self that hears?" He finds only ear-sensitivity, sound, and ear-consciousness. In each case, there is only the process, with no agent.

**6** This understanding constitutes purification of view. The view is purified of the distortion of seeing a self where there is no self. The bhikkhu now understands: "There is only name-and-form. There is no being, no person, no soul."

**7** The Blessed One illustrated this with the simile of the chariot: just as the word "chariot" is used when there is an assemblage of parts — axle, wheels, frame, yoke — so the word "being" is used when there is an assemblage of aggregates. But apart from the aggregates, no being is found.

**8** This purification of view is also called "the defining of name-and-form" and "the knowledge of delimitation of name-and-form." It is the first of the insight knowledges and the beginning of the development of liberating wisdom.

**9** A bhikkhu who has purified his view is free from three of the sixteen kinds of doubt: he no longer doubts whether he existed in the past, whether he will exist in the future, or what he is in the present.

**10** But purification of view alone is not sufficient for liberation. Having discerned name-and-form, the bhikkhu must proceed to discern their conditions — the causes for the arising of name-and-form — and this is the next purification: purification by overcoming doubt.
