---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 23: Panna-bhavana-anisamsa-niddesa (Benefits of the Development of Understanding)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 23
---

**1** The development of understanding produces great benefits. When understanding is developed, the Noble One has direct knowledge of the Four Noble Truths, has abandoned the defilements, has realized Nibbana, and has developed the path.

**2** The stream-enterer is freed from the fear of the lower realms. He will never again be born in hell, as an animal, as a hungry ghost, or as a demon. This is the first great benefit of the development of understanding.

**3** The once-returner has weakened the bonds of sensual desire and ill-will. He will return to this world only once more. The non-returner has cut these bonds entirely and will never again return to the desire realm. These are great benefits of understanding.

**4** The Arahat has destroyed all fetters, all taints, all defilements. He has laid down the burden, attained his own goal, destroyed the fetters of existence, and is liberated through final knowledge. This is the supreme benefit of the development of understanding.

**5** The Arahat's mind is unshakeable, like a mountain. When praised, he does not exalt; when blamed, he does not despair. When gain comes, he does not rejoice unduly; when loss comes, he does not grieve. He has transcended all the worldly conditions.

**6** The Arahat abides in the bliss of liberation. There is no suffering greater than the round of existence; there is no happiness greater than the peace of Nibbana. The Arahat has passed from suffering to peace, from bondage to freedom, from darkness to light.

**7** The Blessed One said: "As the great ocean has but one taste — the taste of salt — so this teaching has but one taste — the taste of liberation." All the teachings of the Buddha — virtue, concentration, and wisdom — converge upon this single point: liberation from suffering.

**8** This is the path of purification: beginning with the purification of virtue, proceeding through the purification of consciousness, and culminating in the purification of view, the overcoming of doubt, the knowledge of what is and is not the path, the knowledge of the way, and finally the knowledge and vision that is the attainment of the supramundane path.

**9** Seven purifications, sixteen insight knowledges, four paths, four fruits — this is the entire course of the path of purification as taught by Buddhaghosa, following the teaching of the Blessed One and the tradition of the great commentators.

**10** Thus is the Path of Purification completed, composed for the purpose of gladdening good people. By the merit of this work, may all beings be happy, may all beings be free from suffering, may all beings attain the supreme peace of Nibbana. May the true Dhamma long endure for the benefit and welfare of the world.
