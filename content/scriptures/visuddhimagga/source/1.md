---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 1: Sila-niddesa (Description of Virtue)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 1
---

**1** When a wise man, established well in virtue, develops consciousness and understanding, then as a bhikkhu ardent and sagacious, he succeeds in disentangling this tangle.

**2** This was said by the Blessed One. Herein, "virtue" is the beginning. For this has been said: "What is the basis for wisdom? It is purified virtue that is the basis."

**3** Virtue is the states beginning with volition present in one who abstains from killing living beings, and so forth. It is also the restraint, the non-transgression, in the sense of the act of restraining and non-transgressing.

**4** Virtue has the characteristic of composing. Its function is to destroy moral failings. It is manifested as purity of bodily action, verbal action, and mental action. Its proximate cause is conscience and shame.

**5** Virtue is of many kinds. It is classified as virtue of restraint by the Patimokkha, virtue of restraint of the senses, virtue of purification of livelihood, and virtue concerning the four requisites.

**6** Restraint by the Patimokkha is the undertaking and observance of the training rules laid down by the Blessed One. A monk should guard the doors of his senses, be moderate in eating, and be devoted to wakefulness.

**7** Purification of livelihood means that a bhikkhu should not obtain the four requisites — robes, alms-food, lodging, and medicine — by wrong means such as flattery, hinting, scheming, belittling, or pursuing gain with gain.

**8** Virtue concerning the four requisites means that a bhikkhu should reflect wisely on the use of each requisite: "I use this robe only for protection from cold, heat, gadflies, mosquitoes, wind, sun, and creeping things, and only for the purpose of covering my nakedness."

**9** The benefits of virtue are many: it is the foundation of all wholesome states, the destroyer of all self-reproach, the source of blamelessness and happiness, the delight of the virtuous, and the prerequisite for concentration and wisdom.

**10** The Blessed One declared: "Monks, virtue is the ground on which all wholesome states are established, just as all trees, plants, and herbs that grow are established on the earth. Therefore, monks, you should train thus: 'We shall be accomplished in virtue.'"
