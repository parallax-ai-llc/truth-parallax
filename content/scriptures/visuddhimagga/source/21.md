---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 21: Patipada-nanadassana-visuddhi-niddesa (Purification by Knowledge and Vision of the Way)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 21
---

**1** After the mature knowledge of arising and passing away, the knowledge of dissolution arises. The bhikkhu no longer attends to the arising of formations but sees only their passing away. He sees that all formations are dissolving, vanishing, breaking up, moment after moment.

**2** With the knowledge of dissolution, the knowledge of appearance as terror arises. All formations appear as fearful, as a source of danger, as a peril. Like a man who sees danger on every side, the bhikkhu sees peril in all formations — in all existence, in all the three realms.

**3** Then the knowledge of danger arises. The bhikkhu sees that all formations in all states of existence are dangerous, unstable, and unreliable. There is no formation that can provide lasting safety or security.

**4** Then the knowledge of disenchantment arises. The bhikkhu becomes utterly disenchanted with all formations. He finds no delight in any state of existence — not in the desire realm, not in the form realm, not in the formless realm. All seems wearisome and burdensome.

**5** Then the knowledge of desire for deliverance arises. The bhikkhu desires to be delivered from all formations, to be freed from the round of existence, as a frog in the jaws of a snake desires to escape, as a man trapped in a burning house desires to get out.

**6** Then the knowledge of re-observation arises. The bhikkhu once again contemplates formations with the three characteristics, but now with greater penetration: "All formations are truly impermanent, truly suffering, truly not-self." This re-observation strengthens and deepens the insight.

**7** Then the knowledge of equanimity about formations arises. The bhikkhu regards all formations with equanimity — without attraction or aversion, without delight or fear. His mind is perfectly balanced, neither drawn toward any formation nor repelled by any formation.

**8** This equanimity about formations is the highest of the insight knowledges. It is compared to a man who has divorced his wife — he regards her with neither love nor hate, but with complete indifference.

**9** With the maturation of equanimity about formations, the knowledge of conformity arises. This is the culminating moment of insight meditation. The mind takes Nibbana as its object, and the change-of-lineage consciousness arises, marking the transition from the lineage of worldlings to the lineage of the Noble Ones.

**10** Immediately after the change-of-lineage consciousness, the path-consciousness arises — the consciousness of stream-entry, once-returning, non-returning, or Arahatship. This supramundane consciousness directly penetrates Nibbana, eradicating the relevant defilements. This is the attainment of the path, followed immediately by its fruit.
