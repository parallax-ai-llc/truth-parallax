---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 16: Indriya-sacca-niddesa (Description of Faculties and Truths)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 16
---

**1** The twenty-two faculties exercise predominance in their respective domains. The five sense-faculties exercise predominance in the cognition of their respective objects. The mind-faculty exercises predominance over all mental activities.

**2** The five spiritual faculties — faith, energy, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom — must be developed in balance. Faith without wisdom leads to credulity. Wisdom without faith leads to cunning. Energy without concentration leads to restlessness. Concentration without energy leads to sloth.

**3** Mindfulness is always beneficial and need not be balanced against any other faculty. Mindfulness is like salt that is needed in every dish, or like a minister who is needed in all the affairs of state.

**4** The Four Noble Truths are the core of the Buddha's teaching. The truth of suffering must be fully understood. The truth of the origin must be abandoned. The truth of cessation must be realized. The truth of the path must be developed.

**5** The truth of suffering: birth is suffering, ageing is suffering, sickness is suffering, death is suffering, association with the disliked is suffering, separation from the liked is suffering, not getting what one wants is suffering. In brief, the five aggregates of clinging are suffering.

**6** The truth of the origin: it is craving — craving for sensual pleasure, craving for existence, craving for non-existence. This craving leads to renewed being, is accompanied by delight and lust, seeking delight here and there.

**7** The truth of cessation: it is the remainderless fading away and cessation of that same craving, the giving up and relinquishing of it, freedom from it, non-reliance on it. This is Nibbana.

**8** The truth of the path: it is the Noble Eightfold Path — right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These eight factors are grouped into three trainings: virtue, concentration, and wisdom.

**9** The Four Noble Truths are to be penetrated simultaneously, not sequentially. At the moment of stream-entry, the noble disciple penetrates all four truths in a single moment: he understands suffering, abandons craving, realizes cessation, and develops the path.

**10** The Blessed One said: "It is through not understanding, not penetrating the Four Noble Truths that I, as well as you, have wandered through this long round of birth and death. But now that these truths have been understood and penetrated, the craving for existence is cut off, the guide to becoming is destroyed, and there is no more renewed being."
