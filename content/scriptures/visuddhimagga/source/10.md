---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 10: Aruppa-niddesa (The Formless Attainments)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 10
---

**1** A bhikkhu who has mastered the fourth jhana of the form realm and wishes to develop the formless attainments should see the danger in material form — it is the basis for physical suffering, for disease, for the blows of cold and heat — and should aspire to the peaceful formless states.

**2** The sphere of infinite space: the bhikkhu, having attained the fourth jhana using a kasina, should extend the kasina to the limits of the universe and then remove the kasina, attending only to the space it occupied: "infinite space, infinite space."

**3** When the kasina is removed and attention is fixed on the space, the fifth jhana arises — the attainment of the sphere of infinite space. In this attainment, the perception of material form has entirely disappeared, and only the perception of boundless space remains.

**4** The sphere of infinite consciousness: the bhikkhu, having mastered the sphere of infinite space, should see the danger in that attainment — it is still close to the form-realm jhanas — and should attend to the consciousness that pervades that infinite space: "infinite consciousness, infinite consciousness."

**5** When attention is fixed on the consciousness of infinite space, the sixth jhana arises — the attainment of the sphere of infinite consciousness. In this attainment, the perception of infinite space has disappeared, and only the perception of infinite consciousness remains.

**6** The sphere of nothingness: the bhikkhu, having mastered the sphere of infinite consciousness, should see the danger in that attainment and should attend to the absence of that consciousness: "there is nothing, there is nothing."

**7** When attention is fixed on the nothingness — the absence of the consciousness of infinite space — the seventh jhana arises, the attainment of the sphere of nothingness. In this attainment, the perception of infinite consciousness has disappeared.

**8** The sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception: the bhikkhu, having mastered the sphere of nothingness, should see the danger in that attainment and should attend to the peace and subtlety of the consciousness of nothingness, thinking: "this is peaceful, this is sublime."

**9** When attention is fixed in this way, the eighth jhana arises — the attainment of the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. In this attainment, perception is so subtle that it cannot be said to exist, yet it has not entirely ceased. This is the most subtle of all mundane mental states.

**10** These four formless attainments are not sufficient for liberation. Though they bring great calm and subtlety of mind, they do not uproot the defilements. The bhikkhu must proceed from concentration to the development of insight, seeing all formations — including these attainments themselves — as impermanent, suffering, and not-self.
