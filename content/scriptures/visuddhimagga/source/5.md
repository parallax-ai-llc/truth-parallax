---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 5: Sesa-kasina-niddesa (The Remaining Kasinas)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The water kasina: one who wishes to develop the water kasina should contemplate water. He may fill a bowl or basin with clean, clear water and place it before him, or he may use a natural pool of still, clear water, and fix his mind upon it with the thought "water, water."

**2** The fire kasina: one who wishes to develop the fire kasina should kindle a fire and contemplate the flames through a hole in a screen or mat, so that he sees only the fire and not the smoke or the fuel. He fixes his mind upon it with the thought "fire, fire."

**3** The air kasina: one who wishes to develop the air kasina should attend to the wind as it strikes the body or moves the leaves of trees. He may also watch the movement of a flag or the swaying of grass, fixing his mind with the thought "air, air."

**4** The blue kasina: one who wishes to develop the blue kasina should make a disc of blue flowers, blue cloth, or blue-colored material, and contemplate it. Or he may contemplate the blue of the sky. He fixes his mind with the thought "blue, blue."

**5** The yellow kasina is developed with yellow flowers or gold-colored cloth. The red kasina is developed with red flowers or red cloth. The white kasina is developed with white flowers, white cloth, or the moon's disc. The method for each is the same as for the earth kasina.

**6** The light kasina: one who wishes to develop the light kasina should contemplate a bright patch of light — sunlight entering through a window and falling on a wall, or moonlight — and fix his mind with the thought "light, light."

**7** The space kasina: one who wishes to develop the space kasina should contemplate a limited space seen through a hole or an opening in a wall. He fixes his mind upon the space with the thought "space, space."

**8** For each of the ten kasinas, the process is the same: first the learning sign arises through attention to the preliminary object, then the counterpart sign arises, and through the counterpart sign, the jhana is attained.

**9** Of the ten kasinas, the earth kasina, the water kasina, the fire kasina, and the air kasina are the most important. These four are connected with the four great elements that constitute all material form.

**10** Mastery over the kasinas provides the basis for the development of the supernormal powers. One who has mastered the kasinas can develop the ability to fly through the air, walk on water, pass through solid objects, and create multiple forms of oneself. But these powers are secondary; the true purpose of the kasinas is to develop the concentration necessary for insight.
