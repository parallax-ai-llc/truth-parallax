---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 19: Kankhavitarana-visuddhi-niddesa (Purification by Overcoming Doubt)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 19
---

**1** When the bhikkhu has discerned name-and-form, he should next discern their conditions. He should investigate: "What is the cause for the arising of this name-and-form? What conditions produce this body and this mind?"

**2** He understands through dependent origination: with ignorance as condition, formations arise; with formations as condition, consciousness arises; with consciousness as condition, name-and-form arise. He sees that name-and-form do not arise without cause, nor from a creator, but from specific conditions.

**3** He investigates the past: "In the past life, there was ignorance and craving. Because of that ignorance and craving, there were formations and kamma. Because of those formations and kamma, this present consciousness and name-and-form have arisen."

**4** He investigates the future: "If there is ignorance and craving now, there will be formations and kamma now. Because of those formations and kamma, future consciousness and name-and-form will arise."

**5** He sees the three rounds: the round of defilements (ignorance, craving, clinging), the round of kamma (formations, becoming), and the round of results (consciousness, name-and-form, six sense-bases, contact, feeling). These three rounds drive the wheel of existence.

**6** When he sees these conditions clearly, all doubt is overcome. He no longer doubts: "Was I in the past? Was I not in the past? What was I in the past? How was I in the past? Shall I be in the future?" These doubts are dissolved by the clear seeing of conditionality.

**7** This understanding constitutes purification by overcoming doubt. It is also called "the knowledge of discerning the conditions of name-and-form." The bhikkhu now sees that all phenomena arise from conditions and cease when conditions cease.

**8** He understands: "There is no self that passes from one life to the next. There is only the continuity of conditions — ignorance conditions formations, formations condition consciousness, and so forth. The process continues, but no entity transmigrates."

**9** With the overcoming of doubt, the bhikkhu has reached the stage called "the lesser stream-enterer" — not yet a true Noble One, but one whose understanding of the Dhamma is correct and firm.

**10** The bhikkhu should not stop here. Having purified his view and overcome doubt, he should proceed to the contemplation of the three characteristics — impermanence, suffering, and not-self — and this is the beginning of purification by knowledge and vision of what is and what is not the path.
