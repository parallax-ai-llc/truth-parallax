---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 12: Iddhi-vidha-niddesa (The Supernormal Powers)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 12
---

**1** When a bhikkhu has mastered the jhanas, he may develop the supernormal powers. There are five kinds of supernormal knowledge: the supernormal power of psychic abilities, the divine ear, the knowledge of others' minds, the recollection of past lives, and the divine eye.

**2** The supernormal power of psychic abilities includes: being one, he becomes many; being many, he becomes one; he appears and disappears; he goes unhindered through walls, through ramparts, through mountains, as though through space; he dives in and out of the earth as though in water; he walks on water as though on earth; he travels through the sky like a bird.

**3** To develop psychic power, the bhikkhu must master the eight attainments and the eight kasinas, and must be able to enter any jhana at will, for any duration, and emerge from any jhana at will. This is called mastery over the jhanas.

**4** The divine ear: by means of the divine ear-element, purified and surpassing the human, the bhikkhu hears both kinds of sounds — divine and human, whether far or near.

**5** The knowledge of others' minds: the bhikkhu, encompassing the minds of other beings with his own mind, knows directly: a mind with lust as a mind with lust, a mind without lust as a mind without lust, a contracted mind, a distracted mind, a liberated mind, an unliberated mind.

**6** The recollection of past lives: the bhikkhu recollects his manifold past lives — one birth, two births, a hundred births, a thousand births, many aeons of world-contraction and expansion — with their aspects and particulars.

**7** The divine eye: by means of the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, the bhikkhu sees beings passing away and being reborn, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, according to their karma.

**8** These five supernormal powers are mundane and can be attained by non-Buddhist meditators as well. They are not essential for liberation but are natural byproducts of the development of concentration.

**9** The Blessed One declared that the display of supernormal powers should not be used for the purpose of impressing householders or gaining material support. The true miracle is the miracle of instruction — the teaching of the Dhamma that leads to liberation.

**10** Beyond these five mundane powers, there is the sixth supernormal knowledge: the knowledge of the destruction of the taints. This alone is supramundane, attainable only through insight, and it is the knowledge that constitutes Arahatship — the final and complete liberation from all suffering.
