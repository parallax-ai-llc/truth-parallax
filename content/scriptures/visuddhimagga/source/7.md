---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 7: Cha-anussati-niddesa (The Six Recollections)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Recollection of the Buddha: a bhikkhu should recollect the qualities of the Blessed One thus: "He is the Blessed One, the Arahat, the Fully Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, the Fortunate One, the Knower of the World, the unsurpassed trainer of persons to be tamed, the Teacher of gods and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One."

**2** When a bhikkhu recollects the qualities of the Buddha, his mind is not obsessed by lust, hatred, or delusion. His mind becomes upright, inspired by the Tathagata. The hindrances are suppressed, and access concentration is attained.

**3** Recollection of the Dhamma: a bhikkhu should recollect the qualities of the Dhamma thus: "The Dhamma is well-proclaimed by the Blessed One, visible here and now, not delayed in time, inviting inspection, onward-leading, to be experienced individually by the wise."

**4** Recollection of the Sangha: a bhikkhu should recollect the qualities of the Sangha thus: "The Sangha of the Blessed One's disciples has practiced well, has practiced directly, has practiced correctly, has practiced properly — that is, the four pairs of persons, the eight individuals. This Sangha is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverence, the unsurpassed field of merit for the world."

**5** Recollection of virtue: a bhikkhu should recollect his own virtues — unbroken, untorn, unblotched, unmottled, liberating, praised by the wise, not clung to, conducive to concentration. Through this recollection, gladness arises, and through gladness, access concentration.

**6** Recollection of generosity: a bhikkhu should recollect his own acts of generosity, thinking: "It is a gain for me, it is well gained by me, that in the midst of beings obsessed by the stain of miserliness, I abide with a mind free from the stain of miserliness, freely generous, open-handed, delighting in relinquishment."

**7** Recollection of deities: a bhikkhu should recollect the deities, thinking: "There are the devas of the Four Great Kings, the devas of the Thirty-Three, and so forth. These deities were endowed with faith, virtue, learning, generosity, and wisdom, and by reason of those qualities they were reborn there. Such faith, virtue, learning, generosity, and wisdom are found in me too."

**8** Through any of these six recollections, access concentration is attained but not full absorption, because the object is too varied and conceptual. Nevertheless, the mind becomes purified, gladness arises, and the bhikkhu is established in a wholesome state.

**9** These recollections are suitable for all temperaments and serve as a foundation for further development. Even when a bhikkhu is unable to practice other meditations, he can always take up one of these recollections.

**10** The Blessed One said: "Monks, when one thing is developed and cultivated, it leads to great benefit. What one thing? Recollection of the Buddha." And the same was said for each of the other recollections. Through these recollections, the mind is uplifted, purified, and prepared for deeper practice.
