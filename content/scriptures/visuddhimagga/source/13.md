---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 13: Abhinna-niddesa (Direct Knowledge)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 13
---

**1** There are three kinds of direct knowledge: the direct knowledge that is merely understanding, the direct knowledge that is the supernormal powers, and the direct knowledge that constitutes the destruction of the taints.

**2** The direct knowledge that is understanding arises through the practice of insight meditation. When a bhikkhu contemplates the five aggregates as impermanent, suffering, and not-self, direct knowledge arises: "This is suffering, this is the origin of suffering, this is the cessation of suffering, this is the path."

**3** The direct knowledge of the supernormal powers arises through the mastery of concentration, as described previously. These powers are developed as a basis for the practice of insight and for the benefit of beings.

**4** The direct knowledge of the destruction of the taints is the Arahat's knowledge. It arises when all defilements have been completely abandoned, never to arise again. This knowledge is: "Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing more beyond this."

**5** The three taints are: the taint of sensual desire, the taint of existence, and the taint of ignorance. When these three taints are destroyed, the mind is liberated from all bondage.

**6** The taint of sensual desire is the craving for the objects of the five senses. It binds beings to the desire realm and is the most obvious cause of suffering. It is abandoned progressively through the four stages of the path.

**7** The taint of existence is the craving for being, for continued existence in the form and formless realms. It is more subtle than sensual desire and is fully abandoned only at the stage of Arahatship.

**8** The taint of ignorance is the non-knowledge of the Four Noble Truths. It is the most fundamental of the three taints and underlies all other defilements. When ignorance is destroyed, all other defilements are destroyed as well.

**9** The bhikkhu who has destroyed the taints reviews his liberation: "Liberation is mine. Birth is destroyed. The holy life has been lived." He knows directly that the defilements that were once present have been abandoned and will never arise again.

**10** This threefold direct knowledge — understanding, supernormal power, and the destruction of the taints — constitutes the complete range of the Noble One's attainment. Of these, the destruction of the taints is supreme, for it alone brings final and irreversible liberation from all suffering.
