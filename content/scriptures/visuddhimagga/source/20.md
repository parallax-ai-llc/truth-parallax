---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 20: Maggamagga-nanadassana-visuddhi-niddesa (Purification by Knowledge and Vision of What Is and Is Not the Path)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 20
---

**1** The bhikkhu who has overcome doubt should contemplate the three characteristics of all formations. He takes up a formation — any material or mental phenomenon — and contemplates it: "This is impermanent, this is suffering, this is not-self."

**2** The knowledge of comprehension arises: he comprehends all formations through the forty modes — the ten modes of impermanence, the twenty-five modes of suffering, and the five modes of not-self.

**3** Then the knowledge of arising and passing away arises. He sees the momentary arising and passing away of formations: "This formation arises and immediately passes away. This consciousness arises and immediately ceases. This material form arises and immediately dissolves."

**4** At the immature stage of the knowledge of arising and passing away, the ten corruptions of insight may arise: light, knowledge, rapture, tranquility, happiness, resolution, exertion, assurance, equanimity, and attachment. These are called corruptions because the meditator may mistake them for the attainment of the path.

**5** A brilliant light may fill the meditator's perception. Profound knowledge may arise. Intense rapture may pervade the body. Deep tranquility and happiness may be experienced. The meditator may think: "Surely I have attained the path! Surely I have attained enlightenment!"

**6** But these are not the path. They are merely the byproducts of keen insight and strong concentration. A wise bhikkhu, with the help of his teacher, recognizes these experiences as corruptions of insight and does not cling to them.

**7** This discernment — knowing what is the path and what is not the path — constitutes purification by knowledge and vision of what is and what is not the path. The meditator now knows: "These pleasant experiences are not enlightenment. The path is the continued contemplation of impermanence, suffering, and not-self."

**8** Having set aside the corruptions of insight, the bhikkhu returns to the contemplation of the three characteristics with renewed clarity. The mature knowledge of arising and passing away now arises, free from the corruptions.

**9** With the mature knowledge of arising and passing away, the bhikkhu sees formations arising and passing away with great rapidity, like sparks flying from a struck piece of iron, like the popping of sesame seeds in a hot pan.

**10** This mature knowledge is the gateway to the higher insight knowledges. From here, the bhikkhu progresses through the remaining stages of insight toward the attainment of the supramundane path and its fruit.
