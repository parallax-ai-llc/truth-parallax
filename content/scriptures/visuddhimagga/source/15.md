---
title: "Visuddhimagga Chapter 15: Ayatana-dhatu-niddesa (Description of Sense-Bases and Elements)"
scripture: "visuddhimagga"
chapter: 15
---

**1** The twelve sense-bases are the six internal bases and the six external bases. The six internal bases are: the eye-base, the ear-base, the nose-base, the tongue-base, the body-base, and the mind-base. The six external bases are: the visible-form-base, the sound-base, the smell-base, the taste-base, the tangible-base, and the mental-object-base.

**2** The eye-base is the sensitive materiality in the eye, derived from the four great elements, by which visible forms are seen. It is not the gross eye visible to others, but the subtle sensitive element within the eye.

**3** The mind-base includes all types of consciousness — the eighty-nine types of consciousness classified in the Abhidhamma. It is the base for the arising of mind-consciousness and the cognizing of mental objects.

**4** The mental-object-base is the most comprehensive of all the bases. It includes feeling, perception, mental formations, subtle material form, and the unconditioned element — Nibbana. All objects of the mind are included here.

**5** The eighteen elements are an expansion of the twelve sense-bases. The mind-base is divided into seven consciousness-elements: eye-consciousness, ear-consciousness, nose-consciousness, tongue-consciousness, body-consciousness, mind-element, and mind-consciousness-element.

**6** The purpose of teaching the sense-bases is to show how contact and feeling arise. When the internal base, the external base, and consciousness come together, there is contact. Dependent on contact, feeling arises. This is the mechanism of all experience.

**7** A bhikkhu should understand: "The eye is not mine, visible forms are not mine, eye-consciousness is not mine." He should understand this for each of the six sense-doors. When he understands thus, he is not attached to any sense experience.

**8** The Blessed One said: "Monks, all is burning. The eye is burning, visible forms are burning, eye-consciousness is burning, eye-contact is burning, and whatever feeling arises with eye-contact as condition — whether pleasant, painful, or neutral — that too is burning. Burning with what? Burning with the fire of lust, the fire of hatred, the fire of delusion."

**9** When a noble disciple sees this burning, he becomes disenchanted with the eye, disenchanted with visible forms, disenchanted with eye-consciousness. Becoming disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate. Through dispassion, he is liberated.

**10** The sense-bases and elements are taught by the Blessed One to show the conditioned nature of all experience. There is no experiencer apart from the process of experiencing. There is no seer apart from the process of seeing. Understanding this, the bhikkhu abandons the conceit "I am" and is freed.
