## Overview

Academic scholarship on the Visuddhimagga addresses questions of authorship, sources, commentarial methodology, and the text's relationship to the Pali canon. The work has been the subject of major scholarly studies by Bhikkhu Nyanamoli (whose complete English translation remains standard), Pe Maung Tin, and more recently by Maria Heim, Rupert Gethin, and others.

## Scholarly Analysis

Scholars examine how Buddhaghosa systematized disparate canonical teachings into a coherent whole, sometimes introducing interpretive innovations while claiming merely to transmit the orthodox tradition. The question of Buddhaghosa's originality versus his dependence on earlier Sinhalese commentaries remains debated. Academic analysis of the text's meditation instructions has contributed to the modern study of Buddhist meditation practices and their historical development.

## Significance for Buddhist Studies

The Visuddhimagga remains indispensable for any serious study of Theravada Buddhism and provides a crucial data point for comparative studies of Buddhist path literature across traditions.
