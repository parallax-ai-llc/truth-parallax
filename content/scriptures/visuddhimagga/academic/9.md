Academic scholarship on this chapter examines Buddhaghosa's treatment of the divine abidings within the broader context of Indian Buddhist intellectual history. Scholars analyze how Buddhaghosa systematized the Pali canon's scattered teachings on the cultivation of loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity, sometimes introducing interpretive innovations while claiming to transmit established tradition. The chapter's relationship to earlier Sinhalese commentaries, now lost, remains a subject of scholarly investigation.

The chapter's detailed analysis provides important primary source material for the academic study of Buddhist meditation theory. Scholars such as Bhikkhu Bodhi, Rupert Gethin, and Maria Heim have examined these teachings in their historical and philosophical context, contributing to the modern understanding of classical Theravada thought.
