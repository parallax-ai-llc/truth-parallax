## Overview

The Visuddhimagga is the single most authoritative post-canonical work in the Theravada tradition. Buddhaghosa's systematic presentation of the path to purification has shaped Theravada understanding of meditation, ethics, and philosophy for over fifteen centuries. Every subsequent Theravada meditation manual, commentary, and doctrinal summary draws directly or indirectly on this monumental work.

## Interpretive Approach

Theravada readers treat the Visuddhimagga as the definitive guide to practice, integrating the scattered teachings of the Pali canon into a coherent progressive path. The text's organization around sila, samadhi, and panna provides the framework for understanding how the diverse elements of the Buddha's teaching relate to one another and how the practitioner should progress from ethical purification through meditative attainment to liberating wisdom.

## Contemporary Relevance

Modern Theravada meditation movements, including the Burmese vipassana tradition of Mahasi Sayadaw and the forest tradition of Ajahn Chah, draw extensively on the Visuddhimagga's meditation instructions. The text remains required study in Theravada monastic education throughout Southeast Asia.
