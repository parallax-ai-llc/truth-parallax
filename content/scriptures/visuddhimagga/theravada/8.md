Theravada commentary on this chapter treats anapanasati as the supreme meditation subject praised by the Buddha himself. Buddhaghosa's presentation draws on the entire Pali canon and earlier Sinhalese commentaries to provide the most detailed and authoritative treatment of mindfulness of breathing as the most comprehensive concentration practice available in the Theravada tradition. The chapter's systematic approach reflects Buddhaghosa's conviction that the path to liberation requires precise understanding of each component of practice.

For Theravada practitioners, this chapter provides essential guidance for developing the concentration that constitutes the second stage of the threefold training. The detailed instructions have shaped Theravada meditation practice for over fifteen centuries, providing the framework within which teachers across Southeast Asia continue to guide their students.
