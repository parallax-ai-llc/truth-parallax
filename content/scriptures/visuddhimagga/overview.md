## Overview

The Visuddhimagga ("Path of Purification") is the magnum opus of the great Theravada commentator Buddhaghosa, composed in the 5th century CE at the Mahavihara monastery in Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka. This encyclopedic work systematizes the entire Theravada path to liberation according to the threefold training of morality (sila), concentration (samadhi), and wisdom (panna). Its 23 chapters provide the most comprehensive single-volume treatment of Theravada Buddhist practice and philosophy ever composed.

## Structure

The 23 chapters are organized according to the threefold training:

| Chapters | Theme |
|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Sila (Morality): Purification of virtue |
| 3-13 | Samadhi (Concentration): Purification of mind through meditation |
| 14-23 | Panna (Wisdom): Purification of view through insight |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Visuddhimagga's influence extends well beyond Theravada Buddhism:

- **Theravada**: The definitive systematization of Theravada doctrine and practice
- **Mahayana**: Studied comparatively as a comprehensive presentation of foundational Buddhist psychology
- **Zen**: Engaged for its detailed meditation instructions and analysis of mental states
- **Tibetan**: Compared with Tibetan systematic treatises on the path
- **Academic**: A primary source for understanding classical Theravada thought

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Sila | Moral discipline as the indispensable foundation of the path |
| Samadhi | Concentration attained through forty meditation subjects |
| Jhana | The four absorptions and their progressive refinement |
| Panna | Insight wisdom leading to liberation |
| Abhidhamma | Detailed analysis of mind, matter, and their interactions |
| Nibbana | The unconditioned goal of the entire path |
