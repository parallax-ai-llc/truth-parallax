Mahayana engagement with this chapter on recollection of the Buddha acknowledges the shared Buddhist foundations while noting distinctive Mahayana developments. The Visuddhimagga's treatment of recollection of the Buddha and other wholesome qualities as meditation objects provides technical precision that Mahayana practitioners can appreciate, even where the Mahayana path includes additional elements such as the bodhisattva vow and the perfection of wisdom (prajna-paramita) that Buddhaghosa does not address.

Comparative Mahayana scholarship notes parallels between this chapter's teachings and corresponding treatments in Mahayana Abhidharma literature, particularly the Abhidharmakosa of Vasubandhu and the Yogacarabhumi of Asanga. These comparisons illuminate both the common Buddhist heritage and the distinctive developments of each tradition.
