## Overview

Mahayana engagement with the Visuddhimagga reflects an appreciation for its comprehensive treatment of foundational Buddhist practices that all traditions share. While the text represents the Theravada path and does not include specifically Mahayana teachings on bodhicitta or emptiness as understood by Nagarjuna, its detailed treatment of moral discipline, meditation, and the analysis of mental phenomena provides essential groundwork that Mahayana practitioners also require.

## Comparative Study

Mahayana scholars have compared the Visuddhimagga's systematic approach with Mahayana counterparts such as Asanga's Yogacarabhumi and Kamalashila's Bhavanakrama. These comparisons reveal both shared Buddhist foundations and distinctive Mahayana developments, particularly in the understanding of the ultimate goal and the scope of compassion.

## Continuing Value

The Visuddhimagga's detailed meditation instructions, particularly on the kasina practices and the jhanas, provide technical precision that Mahayana traditions have sometimes valued for supplementing their own meditation manuals.
