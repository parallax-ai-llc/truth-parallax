Comparative study of this chapter on the faculties, truths, and dependent origination places Buddhaghosa's treatment of the systematic analysis of the spiritual faculties, Four Noble Truths, and dependent origination alongside parallel teachings in other contemplative traditions. The chapter's analysis of insight and liberation invites comparison with the Christian mystical tradition's stages of union with God, the Hindu Advaita tradition's analysis of self-knowledge, and the Sufi concept of fana (annihilation) and baqa (subsistence in God).

The Visuddhimagga's unprecedented level of systematic detail in this chapter represents a distinctive contribution to the world's contemplative literature, providing analytical precision rarely matched in other traditions' treatments of similar subjects.
