Comparative study of this chapter on Earth Kasina meditation places Buddhaghosa's treatment of the earth kasina as the primary model for developing concentration alongside parallel teachings in other contemplative traditions. The chapter's meditation instructions find parallels in the Christian contemplative tradition's teachings on prayer, the Hindu yoga tradition's analysis of samadhi, and the Sufi tradition's descriptions of spiritual states (ahwal) and stations (maqamat).

The Visuddhimagga's unprecedented level of systematic detail in this chapter represents a distinctive contribution to the world's contemplative literature, providing analytical precision rarely matched in other traditions' treatments of similar subjects.
