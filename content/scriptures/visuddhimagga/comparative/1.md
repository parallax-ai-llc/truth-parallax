Comparative study of this chapter on Purification of Virtue and the Ascetic Practices places Buddhaghosa's treatment of ascetic practices (dhutanga) as voluntary austerities that refine monastic discipline alongside parallel teachings in other contemplative traditions. The chapter's detailed ethical prescriptions invite comparison with the Christian monastic rules of Benedict and Basil, the Jewish mussar tradition, and the Islamic adab literature that governs spiritual conduct.

The Visuddhimagga's unprecedented level of systematic detail in this chapter represents a distinctive contribution to the world's contemplative literature, providing analytical precision rarely matched in other traditions' treatments of similar subjects.
