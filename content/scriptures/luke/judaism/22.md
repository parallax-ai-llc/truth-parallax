## The Last Supper and Passover Seder

Luke 22's account of the Last Supper (22:14–23) takes place in the context of the Passover festival. Jewish scholars debate the precise relationship between the Last Supper and the Passover seder, noting both parallels (reclining at table, wine, bread, the framework of remembrance) and differences. The words "Do this in remembrance of me" parallel the Passover command to retell the Exodus — both rituals involve anamnesis, the re-presentation of a foundational saving event.

## The Arrest and Jewish Legal Procedure

The arrest (22:47–53) and the hearing before the council (22:66–71) raise questions about Jewish legal procedure. Jewish scholars have extensively analyzed whether the Sanhedrin trial as depicted in the Gospels conforms to Mishnaic legal procedures. Many scholars argue that the Gospel accounts reflect either informal proceedings or do not accurately represent actual Sanhedrin practice. The Mishnah's rules (no capital trials at night, no trials on festival days) suggest irregularities in the Gospel accounts.

## Peter's Denial and Human Weakness

Peter's threefold denial (22:54–62) is a narrative of human weakness under pressure that Jewish scholars read with empathy. The portrait of a devoted follower who collapses under fear of persecution resonates with the Jewish experience of communities and individuals who compromised their convictions under threat — a recurring theme in Jewish history from the Maccabean crisis to the Inquisition.
