## Wealth and Accountability

Luke 16's teachings on wealth — the shrewd manager and the rich man and Lazarus — resonate with the Islamic emphasis on financial accountability. The Quran warns: "And those who hoard gold and silver and do not spend it in the way of Allah — give them tidings of a painful punishment" (Surah 9:34). The Islamic principles of zakat (obligatory charity) and the prohibition of riba (usury) reflect the same concern for just economic relationships that underlies Jesus's teaching.

## The Rich Man and Lazarus

The parable of the rich man and Lazarus (16:19–31) has significant parallels with Islamic eschatology. The concept of post-mortem reward and punishment, the unbridgeable gulf between the blessed and the damned, and the moral imperative to attend to the poor in this life are all consistent with Quranic teaching. The Quran describes the akhirah (afterlife) in vivid terms: the righteous in gardens of bliss, the wicked in a fire whose fuel is people and stones (Surah 2:24).

## The Sufficiency of Revelation

Abraham's statement "If they do not hear Moses and the Prophets, neither will they be convinced if someone should rise from the dead" (16:31) is of particular interest to Islamic scholars. Islam affirms the principle that divine revelation, properly received, is sufficient for guidance. The multiplication of signs does not help those whose hearts are hardened — a teaching found throughout the Quran regarding communities that demanded miracles yet still refused to believe.
