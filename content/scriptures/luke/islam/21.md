## Signs of the End

Luke 21's eschatological discourse (21:5–36) covers themes that are central to Islamic eschatology. Wars, earthquakes, famines, and persecution are recognized in Islamic tradition as signs (ashrat al-sa'ah) preceding the Day of Judgment. The hadith literature contains extensive lists of these signs, many of which overlap with Luke 21's description. Islamic scholars find in this discourse confirmation that Isa taught about the approaching end — a teaching consistent with the message of all prophets.

## The Destruction of the Temple

Jesus's prophecy of the Temple's destruction (21:5–6) is understood by Islamic scholars as a genuine prophetic prediction, confirmed by the historical destruction in 70 CE. The Quran itself references the destruction of the Temple (Surah 17:4–7), describing it as divine punishment for Israel's transgressions. Islamic scholars see this convergence as evidence that Isa possessed authentic prophetic foresight.

## Perseverance and Trust

The exhortation to perseverance under trial (21:12–19) and the promise that "not a hair of your head will perish" (21:18) resonate with the Quranic promise of divine protection for the faithful: "Allah is the protector of those who believe" (Surah 2:257). The call to endurance (sabr) in the face of persecution is one of the most emphasized virtues in the Quran and is seen as a mark of genuine faith.
