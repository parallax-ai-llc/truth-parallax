## Repentance and Tawbah

Luke 13's call to repentance (13:1–5) aligns with the Islamic emphasis on tawbah — turning back to Allah in sincere repentance. The Quran declares: "Allah loves those who are constantly repentant" (Surah 2:222) and "Say: O my servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins" (Surah 39:53). The urgency of repentance that Jesus proclaims is consistent with the prophetic message in Islam.

## The Barren Fig Tree and Divine Patience

The parable of the barren fig tree (13:6–9) resonates with the Quranic theme of Allah's patience (hilm) with humanity. Allah gives people time to repent and bear fruit, but this opportunity is not unlimited. The Quran warns: "Let not the deceiver deceive you concerning Allah" (Surah 31:33). Islamic scholars read this parable as consistent with the prophetic warning that divine patience should not be mistaken for divine indifference.

## The Narrow Door

The teaching on the narrow door (13:22–30) is consistent with the Islamic understanding that salvation requires genuine commitment and effort (jihad al-nafs — striving of the soul). The Quran teaches that the path to paradise is demanding: "Do people think they will be left alone because they say 'We believe' and will not be tested?" (Surah 29:2). Not everyone who claims faith will be saved — sincere practice and genuine submission to God are required.
