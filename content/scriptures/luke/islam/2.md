## The Birth of Isa

Luke 2's nativity narrative has points of contact with the Quranic account of Isa's birth (Surah 19:22–26), though the details differ significantly. The Quran places the birth beneath a palm tree rather than in a manger, and Maryam is alone rather than accompanied by Joseph. Both traditions, however, affirm the miraculous nature of the birth and the humble circumstances surrounding it. Islamic scholars see these differences as evidence of the biblical text's alteration (tahrif) from the original revelation.

## The Shepherds and Divine Signs

The angelic announcement to the shepherds (2:8–20) has no direct Quranic parallel, but the concept of divine signs (ayat) accompanying the birth of a prophet is consistent with Islamic theology. The glory of the Lord and the heavenly host declaring peace are understood by Muslim commentators as part of the divine attestation that accompanies prophetic births, comparable to the miraculous signs at the births of other prophets in Islamic tradition.

## The Boy Isa in the Temple

The episode of the twelve-year-old Jesus in the Temple (2:41–52), while not paralleled in the Quran, is consistent with Islamic traditions about Isa's early wisdom. The Quran describes Isa speaking from the cradle (Surah 19:29–33) — a miracle not found in Luke but attested in the apocryphal Infancy Gospel of Thomas. Islamic tradition thus attributes even greater precocity to Isa than Luke does.
