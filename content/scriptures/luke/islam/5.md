## The Calling of Disciples

The calling of disciples in Luke 5 is understood in Islamic perspective as the gathering of followers (hawariyyun) around a prophet — a pattern seen with all major prophets in Islamic tradition. The Quran mentions the disciples of Isa: "When Isa sensed disbelief from them, he said, 'Who will be my helpers for Allah?' The disciples said, 'We are Allah's helpers'" (Surah 3:52). Islamic tradition honors the sincere disciples while noting that later followers corrupted Isa's original message.

## Healing and Divine Power

The healings in Luke 5 — the leper and the paralytic — are consistent with the Quranic affirmation of Isa's healing ministry. Islamic scholars emphasize that these miracles were performed "by Allah's permission" (bi-idhnillah), not through any inherent divine power. The distinction is theologically critical: in Christianity, the miracles demonstrate Jesus's divine nature; in Islam, they demonstrate Allah's power working through his chosen prophet.

## The Forgiveness Controversy

Jesus's claim to forgive sins (5:20) is a point of significant divergence. In Islamic theology, only Allah forgives sins — no prophet, angel, or intermediary possesses this authority independently. Muslim scholars would read this claim either as a later Christian addition to the text or as Isa declaring God's forgiveness on God's behalf, which is within the prophetic function of conveying divine will.
