## The Annunciation to Zakariya and Maryam

Luke 1 contains narratives that have the most extensive parallels with the Quran of any Gospel chapter. The announcement to Zechariah (Zakariya in Arabic) about the birth of John (Yahya) closely parallels Surah 3:38–41 and Surah 19:2–15. In both accounts, Zakariya is an elderly priest whose wife is barren, an angel announces the birth of a son, and Zakariya is struck mute as a sign. Islamic tradition honors Yahya (John) as a righteous prophet.

## The Annunciation to Maryam

The angelic announcement to Mary (Maryam) in Luke 1:26–38 has a direct Quranic parallel in Surah 3:42–47 and Surah 19:16–22. Both traditions affirm the virgin birth and the angelic announcement. However, a fundamental divergence emerges: while Luke's narrative leads toward the identification of Jesus as "Son of the Most High" (1:32) and "Son of God" (1:35), Islamic theology firmly rejects divine sonship as incompatible with tawhid (the absolute oneness of God). The Quran states: "It is not befitting to Allah that He should beget a son" (Surah 19:35).

## Maryam's Exalted Status

Despite rejecting the divinity of Isa, Islam holds Maryam in the highest esteem. She is the only woman named in the Quran, an entire surah (Surah 19) bears her name, and the Quran declares her chosen "above the women of all nations" (Surah 3:42). Muslim scholars find in Luke 1's portrayal of Mary's piety, humility, and submission to God's will a portrait consistent with the Quranic Maryam.
