## The Crucifixion Question

Luke 23 contains the narrative of greatest theological divergence between Christianity and Islam. The crucifixion of Jesus is the central salvific event in Christianity but is explicitly denied in the Quran: "They did not kill him, nor did they crucify him, but it was made to appear so to them" (Surah 4:157). Islamic scholars have proposed various interpretations: that someone else was crucified in Isa's place (the substitution theory), that Isa was raised to heaven before the crucifixion, or that he was placed on the cross but did not die.

## The Trial and Innocence

Islamic scholars affirm that the religious and political authorities conspired against Isa — this much is consistent with the prophetic pattern of rejection. However, Allah's protection of His prophet ensured that the enemies' plan ultimately failed. The trial before Pilate and Herod is read as a human drama in which the perpetrators believed they had condemned Isa, while in reality Allah had already determined his rescue.

## Divine Rescue and Ascension

Rather than dying on the cross, Islamic tradition teaches that Allah raised Isa to heaven: "Allah raised him to Himself" (Surah 4:158). This ascension — without prior death — is a unique honor that distinguishes Isa from other prophets. He remains alive in heaven, awaiting his return before the Day of Judgment. Islamic scholars read Luke 23 as a narrative that accurately describes the apparent events but misunderstands their true nature.
