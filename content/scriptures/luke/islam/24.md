## The Resurrection in Islamic Perspective

Islam does not accept the Christian doctrine of Jesus's resurrection from death, since it does not accept that Jesus died on the cross. The appearances described in Luke 24 — the empty tomb, the Emmaus road, the appearance to the disciples — are interpreted by Islamic scholars either as encounters with the living Isa before his ascension to heaven or as later fabrications that supported the emerging Christian theological narrative.

## The Ascension

The ascension described in Luke 24:50–53 has a partial parallel in Islamic theology: the Quran affirms that Allah raised Isa to Himself (Surah 4:158). However, the Islamic ascension is not preceded by death and resurrection but is a direct divine rescue. Isa was taken up bodily to heaven, where he remains alive. This belief is held by virtually all Muslim scholars and is a point of firm consensus in Islamic theology.

## The Return of Isa

Islamic eschatology assigns Isa a crucial role at the end of time. He will descend from heaven, break the cross (symbolically refuting the doctrine of the crucifixion), kill the Dajjal (Antichrist), establish justice on earth, and live out a natural life before dying and being buried. This second coming of Isa is affirmed in numerous hadith and is part of orthodox Islamic belief. While Luke 24 does not describe this future return, Islamic scholars connect Jesus's departure in Luke with the prophetic traditions about his eventual return.
