## Parables and Prophetic Teaching

The parable of the sower (8:4–15) exemplifies the prophetic teaching method of using illustrative stories to convey spiritual truths. The Quran itself employs parables (amthal): "Allah sets forth parables for people so that they may reflect" (Surah 14:25). Islamic scholars see the parable of the sower as consistent with the Quranic teaching that people respond differently to divine guidance — some accept it, others reject it, and the outcome depends on the condition of the heart.

## Power Over Nature and Spirits

The calming of the storm (8:22–25) and the exorcism of the Gerasene demoniac (8:26–39) are understood in Islamic perspective as prophetic miracles demonstrating Allah's power. The jinn — spiritual beings in Islamic cosmology — are acknowledged in the Quran as real entities that can influence human beings. Isa's authority over demonic forces is understood as a gift from Allah, comparable to Sulayman's (Solomon's) authority over the jinn described in the Quran (Surah 27:17).

## Raising the Dead

The raising of Jairus's daughter (8:49–56) is another instance of the Quranic affirmation that Isa raised the dead. Islamic scholars accept the historicity of such miracles while consistently emphasizing their source in divine power rather than in any inherent ability of Isa himself. These miracles serve as signs (ayat) confirming his prophetic mission.
