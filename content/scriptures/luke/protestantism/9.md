## Mission and the Feeding of the Five Thousand

Luke 9 marks a turning point. Jesus sends the Twelve on mission (9:1–6), demonstrating that his authority is delegable. The feeding of the five thousand (9:10–17) is the only miracle recorded in all four Gospels, and Protestant interpreters see it as a sign of Jesus's messianic provision, echoing Moses and the manna. Peter's confession "The Christ of God" (9:20) is the hinge of the narrative.

## The Transfiguration

The Transfiguration (9:28–36) reveals Jesus's divine glory to Peter, James, and John. The appearance of Moses and Elijah, who speak of Jesus's "departure" (exodus) to be accomplished in Jerusalem, connects Jesus's passion to the redemptive history of Israel. The divine voice — "This is my Son, my Chosen One; listen to him!" — echoes the baptism and reinforces Jesus's unique divine identity.

## The Journey Begins

The pivotal statement "he set his face to go to Jerusalem" (9:51) initiates the great travel narrative that dominates the central section of Luke. Protestant commentators see this as Jesus's deliberate embrace of the cross. The three would-be followers (9:57–62) and Jesus's radical demands — "No one who puts a hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom" — underscore the total commitment required of discipleship.
