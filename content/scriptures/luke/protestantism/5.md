## The Miraculous Catch and the Call of Peter

Luke 5 opens with the calling of Simon Peter through the miraculous catch of fish (5:1–11). Protestant interpreters see Peter's response — "Depart from me, for I am a sinful man, O Lord" — as a paradigm of conviction and grace. Jesus's words "Do not be afraid; from now on you will be catching people" transform Peter's vocation and model the call to evangelism.

## Healing and Controversy

The healing of the leper (5:12–16) and the paralytic lowered through the roof (5:17–26) demonstrate Jesus's power over disease and, more controversially, his authority to forgive sins. Protestant commentators emphasize that Jesus's declaration "Your sins are forgiven" provoked the Pharisees because it claimed a divine prerogative — reinforcing the Protestant conviction that only God can forgive sins, without priestly mediation.

## The Call of Levi and New Wine

The calling of Levi the tax collector (5:27–32) and the ensuing controversy over eating with sinners encapsulates the gospel's radical inclusivity. Jesus's saying "I have not come to call the righteous but sinners to repentance" (5:32) is a cornerstone text for Protestant soteriology. The parable of new wine in old wineskins (5:36–39) signals that the gospel inaugurates something genuinely new.
