## The Empty Tomb

Luke 24 opens with the women discovering the empty tomb on the first day of the week (24:1–12). The angelic announcement "He is not here, but has risen" is the climax of the entire Gospel. Protestant interpreters stress the bodily, physical nature of the resurrection — this is not a spiritual metaphor but a historical event that vindicates Jesus's claims and defeats death.

## The Emmaus Road

The Emmaus road narrative (24:13–35) is one of the most theologically rich passages in Luke. The risen Jesus walks with two disciples, and "beginning with Moses and all the Prophets, he interpreted to them in all the Scriptures the things concerning himself" (24:27). Protestant hermeneutics finds here the warrant for christological reading of the entire Old Testament. The moment of recognition in the breaking of bread (24:30–31) models the pattern of Word and sacrament that shapes Protestant worship.

## Commissioning and Ascension

Jesus's final appearance to the Eleven (24:36–49) confirms the physical reality of the resurrection — "Touch me, and see. For a spirit does not have flesh and bones" — and commissions the disciples as witnesses. The promise of the Holy Spirit points forward to Pentecost in Acts 2. The ascension (24:50–53) concludes Luke's Gospel with the disciples returning to Jerusalem "with great joy," worshipping in the Temple and awaiting the Spirit's coming.
