## Warnings Against Hypocrisy

Luke 12 begins with Jesus warning against the "leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy" (12:1). Protestant interpreters connect this to the Reformation critique of outward religiosity that lacks inward transformation. The encouragement not to fear those who kill the body (12:4–7) and the promise that the Holy Spirit will teach believers what to say (12:11–12) have sustained Protestant martyrs and confessors throughout history.

## The Parable of the Rich Fool

The parable of the rich fool (12:13–21) warns against covetousness and the illusion that material abundance provides security. "Fool! This night your soul is required of you" (12:20) is a dramatic reminder of human mortality and the urgency of being "rich toward God." Protestant preachers frequently cite this parable in sermons on stewardship and eternal perspective.

## Anxiety, Watchfulness, and Division

Jesus's teaching on anxiety (12:22–34) — "Consider the ravens... consider the lilies" — calls for radical trust in God's providential care. The exhortation to watchfulness (12:35–48) uses the imagery of servants awaiting their master's return, a text Protestant eschatology applies to readiness for Christ's second coming. Jesus's sobering declaration that he came to bring "not peace, but division" (12:51) warns that the gospel inevitably creates conflict even within families.
