## The Widow's Offering

Luke 21 opens with the contrast between the rich donors and the poor widow who gives two small coins (21:1–4). Protestant interpreters see this as a lesson on the nature of true giving: generosity is measured not by amount but by sacrifice. The widow's offering exemplifies the radical trust that Jesus has been teaching throughout the Gospel.

## The Olivet Discourse

Jesus's prophetic discourse about the destruction of the Temple and the signs of the end (21:5–36) is central to Protestant eschatology. Protestant interpreters generally distinguish between prophecies fulfilled in the destruction of Jerusalem in 70 CE and those pointing to Christ's second coming. The warning signs — wars, earthquakes, persecutions, cosmic disturbances — are read as characterizing the entire period between Christ's ascension and return.

## Exhortation to Watchfulness

The discourse concludes with the call to "stay awake at all times, praying that you may have strength to escape all these things" (21:36). Protestant commentators emphasize that eschatological awareness should produce not anxiety but faithful vigilance and prayerful readiness.
