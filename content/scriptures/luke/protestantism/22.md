## The Last Supper

Luke 22 narrates the final hours before Jesus's arrest. The institution of the Lord's Supper (22:14–23) is foundational for Protestant sacramental theology. Jesus's words "This is my body, which is given for you. Do this in remembrance of me" (22:19) have been interpreted variously across Protestant traditions — from Zwingli's memorial view to Luther's real presence to Calvin's spiritual presence — but all agree that the Supper proclaims Christ's atoning death.

## Servant Leadership and Peter's Denial

The dispute about greatness (22:24–30) and Jesus's prediction of Peter's denial (22:31–34) reveal the disciples' continuing failure to grasp the nature of Jesus's kingdom. Jesus's words "I am among you as the one who serves" (22:27) establish servant leadership as the model for the Church — a principle invoked repeatedly in Protestant ecclesiology.

## Gethsemane and Arrest

Jesus's prayer on the Mount of Olives (22:39–46) — "Father, if you are willing, remove this cup from me. Nevertheless, not my will, but yours, be done" — displays both his full humanity and his obedient submission to the Father's redemptive plan. The arrest, Judas's betrayal with a kiss, and Peter's threefold denial unfold as fulfillments of Jesus's predictions, reinforcing the sovereignty of God over the events of the passion.
