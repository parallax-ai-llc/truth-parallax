## The Parable of the Sower

Luke 8 begins with Jesus's itinerant ministry accompanied by the Twelve and several women (8:1–3) — a detail unique to Luke that highlights the role of women in Jesus's movement. The parable of the sower (8:4–15) is interpreted by Protestants as illustrating the varied responses to the preached Word, with the emphasis falling on the good soil that "hearing the word, hold it fast in an honest and good heart, and bear fruit with patience" (8:15).

## Power over Nature and Demons

The calming of the storm (8:22–25) and the healing of the Gerasene demoniac (8:26–39) demonstrate Jesus's sovereign authority over creation and the demonic realm. Protestant interpreters read the disciples' question "Who then is this?" as Luke's way of pressing the reader toward the confession that Jesus is Lord over all. The restored demoniac who is sent to "declare how much God has done for you" (8:39) becomes a model of witness.

## Jairus's Daughter and the Hemorrhaging Woman

The interwoven accounts of Jairus's daughter and the woman with a hemorrhage (8:40–56) display Jesus's power over illness and death. The woman's healing through touching Jesus's garment and Jairus's daughter raised from death both emphasize faith as the channel through which divine power operates — "Your faith has made you well" (8:48).
