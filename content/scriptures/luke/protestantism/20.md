## The Question of Authority

Luke 20 opens with the Jewish leaders challenging Jesus's authority (20:1–8). Jesus's counter-question about John's baptism exposes their unwillingness to commit, and Protestant interpreters see this exchange as illustrating the danger of intellectual evasion when confronted with divine truth.

## The Parable of the Wicked Tenants

The parable of the wicked tenants (20:9–19) allegorizes Israel's history of rejecting God's messengers, culminating in the killing of the beloved son. The concluding citation of Psalm 118:22 — "The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone" — is a central christological text in Protestant theology, affirming that God's purposes triumph through and despite human rejection.

## Render to Caesar and the Resurrection

The question about paying taxes to Caesar (20:20–26) produces Jesus's famous "render to Caesar" principle, which has shaped Protestant political theology from Luther's two-kingdoms doctrine onward. The Sadducees' challenge about the resurrection (20:27–40) elicits Jesus's affirmation that God "is not God of the dead, but of the living" — a key text for the doctrine of the resurrection and the afterlife.
