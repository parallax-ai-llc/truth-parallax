## The Trial and the Way of the Cross

Luke 23 narrates the trial, crucifixion, and burial of Jesus. Catholic interpretation emphasizes Jesus's innocence, declared three times by Pilate, and the salvific meaning of his voluntary suffering. The encounter with Simon of Cyrene (23:26) and the weeping women of Jerusalem (23:27–31) are commemorated in the Stations of the Cross, the devotional practice that traces Jesus's journey to Calvary.

## The Crucifixion and the Seven Last Words

Luke records three of the traditional "Seven Last Words" from the cross: "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do" (23:34), "Today you will be with me in paradise" (23:43), and "Father, into your hands I commit my spirit" (23:46). Catholic theology sees in the prayer of forgiveness the supreme expression of divine mercy. The promise to the penitent thief is understood as an assurance that sincere repentance, even at the last moment, receives God's mercy — a principle that informs Catholic pastoral care of the dying (the Sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick and Viaticum).

## The Burial

Joseph of Arimathea's burial of Jesus (23:50–56) and the women's preparation of spices are commemorated on Holy Saturday. Catholic liturgy marks this day as a time of silence and waiting, reflecting the mystery of Christ's descent among the dead (the "Harrowing of Hell") before the glory of Easter.
