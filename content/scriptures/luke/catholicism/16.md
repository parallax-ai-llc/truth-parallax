## The Shrewd Manager and Catholic Social Teaching

The parable of the shrewd manager (16:1–13) has been read in Catholic tradition as teaching the proper use of material goods for spiritual and social purposes. The maxim "You cannot serve God and mammon" (16:13) is a foundational text for Catholic teaching on the relationship between faith and economics. Catholic social encyclicals from Rerum Novarum (1891) to Fratelli Tutti (2020) echo this Lukan warning against allowing wealth to become an idol.

## The Rich Man and Lazarus

The parable of the rich man and Lazarus (16:19–31) is the most vivid depiction of post-mortem justice in the Gospels. Catholic interpretation draws several theological conclusions: the reality of particular judgment immediately after death, the existence of an unbridgeable gulf between the blessed and the damned, and the moral imperative to attend to the poor in this life. The image of the rich man in torment has influenced Catholic artistic depictions of the afterlife and Catholic preaching on the works of mercy.

## Abraham's Response

Abraham's concluding words — "If they do not hear Moses and the Prophets, neither will they be convinced if someone should rise from the dead" (16:31) — affirm the sufficiency of divine revelation and the responsibility of each person to respond to it. Catholic interpreters note the irony: even Christ's own resurrection did not convince all, confirming Abraham's warning.
