## John the Baptist and Sacramental Preparation

Catholic interpretation of Luke 3 reads John the Baptist's ministry as a prototype of sacramental preparation. His "baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins" (3:3) is understood not as a complete sacrament but as a preparatory rite that anticipates Christian baptism. John's ethical exhortations (3:10–14) prefigure the examination of conscience that precedes the Sacrament of Reconciliation.

## The Baptism of Jesus

The baptism of Jesus (3:21–22) is a theophany revealing the Trinity — the Father's voice, the Son in the water, and the Holy Spirit descending as a dove. Catholic sacramental theology sees this event as the institution or sanctification of the sacrament of Baptism. The Catechism of the Catholic Church teaches that by submitting to John's baptism, Jesus "allows himself to be numbered among sinners" in anticipation of the cross.

## The Genealogy

Luke's genealogy (3:23–38) traces Jesus's descent back to Adam and God, emphasizing the universality of redemption. Catholic commentators note that this genealogy complements Matthew's by highlighting different aspects of Jesus's human lineage, and that the inclusion of both genealogies in the canon reflects the Church's embrace of theological diversity within unity.
