## The Question of Authority

The challenge to Jesus's authority by the chief priests and scribes (20:1–8) is read by Catholic interpreters as illustrating the conflict between divinely given authority and institutional resistance. Catholic ecclesiology affirms that the teaching authority of the Church (Magisterium) derives from Christ's own authority, transmitted through the apostles and their successors.

## The Parable of the Wicked Tenants

The parable of the wicked tenants (20:9–19) is understood as an allegory of salvation history: God sends prophets who are rejected, and finally his Son who is killed. Catholic interpretation reads the transfer of the vineyard as the extension of God's covenant to include the Gentiles, not the replacement of Israel. The Second Vatican Council's document Nostra Aetate (1965) clarified that the Jewish people remain in a covenant relationship with God.

## Render to Caesar and the Resurrection

The question about taxes (20:20–26) informs Catholic political theology, particularly the distinction between temporal and spiritual authority. The Sadducees' question about the resurrection (20:27–40) elicits Jesus's affirmation of life after death, a doctrine central to Catholic faith as expressed in the Creed: "I look for the resurrection of the dead, and the life of the world to come."
