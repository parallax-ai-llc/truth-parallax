## The Empty Tomb and the Women's Witness

Luke 24 opens with the women at the empty tomb (24:1–12). Catholic interpretation highlights that the first witnesses to the resurrection are women — Mary Magdalene, Joanna, and Mary the mother of James — affirming the dignity and apostolic role of women in the Church. The angels' reminder "Remember how he told you" connects the resurrection to Jesus's prior passion predictions, demonstrating the trustworthiness of his word.

## The Road to Emmaus

The Emmaus narrative (24:13–35) is the supreme biblical paradigm for the Catholic Mass. The two movements — Jesus opening the Scriptures (the Liturgy of the Word) and being recognized in the breaking of the bread (the Liturgy of the Eucharist) — mirror the structure of every eucharistic celebration. The disciples' burning hearts ("Did not our hearts burn within us while he talked to us on the road?") describe the experience of encountering the risen Christ through Word and Sacrament.

## The Ascension and the Great Commission

Jesus's final appearance (24:36–49) confirms the bodily reality of the resurrection and commissions the disciples as witnesses. The promise of the Holy Spirit points to Pentecost (Acts 2). The ascension (24:50–53) marks the completion of Jesus's earthly ministry and the beginning of the Church's mission. Catholic liturgy celebrates the Ascension as a holy day of obligation, and the narrative concludes with the disciples returning to Jerusalem "with great joy" — the joy that characterizes authentic Catholic faith.
