## The Widow's Offering

The widow's offering of two small coins (21:1–4) is a cherished text in Catholic teaching on stewardship and sacrificial giving. Catholic interpretation emphasizes that the widow gives "all she had to live on," making her offering a total self-gift that mirrors Christ's own self-offering on the cross. This passage has inspired generations of Catholic charitable giving and the spirituality of poverty.

## The Eschatological Discourse

Jesus's prophecy of the Temple's destruction and the signs of the end (21:5–36) is central to Catholic eschatology. Catholic interpretation, following the Church Fathers, generally distinguishes between the destruction of Jerusalem (fulfilled in 70 CE) and the signs of the Parousia. The Catechism teaches that the Church must pass through a final trial before Christ's second coming, and Luke 21 provides key imagery for understanding that trial.

## Perseverance and Hope

The promise "not a hair of your head will perish" (21:18) and "by your endurance you will gain your lives" (21:19) ground the Catholic virtue of perseverance — the grace to remain faithful through suffering and persecution. The concluding exhortation to "stay awake at all times, praying" (21:36) connects eschatological readiness to the life of prayer that is central to Catholic spirituality.
