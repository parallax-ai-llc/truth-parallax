## Zacchaeus and Conversion

The story of Zacchaeus (19:1–10) is a beloved narrative in Catholic preaching on conversion and restitution. Catholic moral theology notes that Zacchaeus's response to Jesus's visit — giving half his goods to the poor and restoring fourfold to those he defrauded — models the fruits of genuine conversion, including the obligation of restitution that is part of the Sacrament of Reconciliation. Jesus's declaration "Today salvation has come to this house" is understood as illustrating the transformative power of personal encounter with Christ.

## The Parable of the Ten Minas

The parable of the ten minas (19:11–27) teaches responsible stewardship of the gifts entrusted by God. Catholic interpretation applies this to the faithful use of charisms, talents, and material resources for the building up of the Church and the service of the world. The severe judgment on the wicked servant warns against spiritual laziness and the failure to develop the gifts received in baptism and confirmation.

## The Triumphal Entry

Jesus's entry into Jerusalem (19:28–40) is celebrated on Palm Sunday at the beginning of Holy Week. The liturgical procession with palms reenacts this scene. Jesus's weeping over Jerusalem (19:41–44) expresses the prophetic grief of God over his people's refusal of peace, and Catholic interpreters read it as a call to the Church to be attentive to God's "time of visitation" (kairos) in every age.
