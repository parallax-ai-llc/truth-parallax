## The Institution of the Eucharist

Luke 22 contains the institution narrative that is the foundation of the Catholic Mass. Jesus's words "This is my body, which is given for you. Do this in remembrance of me" (22:19) and "This cup that is poured out for you is the new covenant in my blood" (22:20) are the words of institution spoken by the priest at every eucharistic celebration. Catholic doctrine holds that through these words, the bread and wine are transubstantiated into the body and blood of Christ — the Real Presence.

## The New Covenant and Servant Leadership

Jesus's establishment of the "new covenant" echoes Jeremiah 31:31–34 and is foundational for Catholic covenant theology. The dispute about greatness (22:24–30) and Jesus's teaching "I am among you as the one who serves" establish the model for ordained ministry as service, not domination — a principle reaffirmed by the Second Vatican Council.

## Gethsemane and the Passion

Jesus's agony in the garden (22:39–46), unique in Luke for mentioning his sweat "like great drops of blood" (22:44), reveals the full humanity of Christ in the face of suffering. Catholic devotional practice — the Stations of the Cross, the Sorrowful Mysteries of the Rosary, Holy Thursday adoration — draws heavily on this chapter. Peter's denial and Jesus's compassionate glance (22:61) model the dynamics of sin, repentance, and restoration that characterize the sacramental life.
