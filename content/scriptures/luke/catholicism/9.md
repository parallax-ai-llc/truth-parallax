## The Mission and the Eucharistic Feeding

The sending of the Twelve (9:1–6) is read by Catholic interpreters as the institution of apostolic ministry — the foundation of the hierarchical structure of bishops, priests, and deacons. The feeding of the five thousand (9:10–17) is the Gospel's most eucharistic miracle: Jesus takes bread, blesses, breaks, and gives — the same four actions that structure the eucharistic liturgy. Catholic tradition sees this miracle as anticipating the Eucharist, where Christ continues to feed his people with his own body.

## Peter's Confession and the Transfiguration

Peter's confession "The Christ of God" (9:20) is understood as the foundational confession of the Church, and the Transfiguration (9:28–36) reveals the glory that is hidden beneath the veil of Christ's humanity — and, by extension, beneath the eucharistic species. Moses and Elijah discuss Jesus's "exodus" (departure), linking the passion to the great acts of salvation history. The feast of the Transfiguration (August 6) is an important celebration in the Catholic calendar.

## The Way of the Cross

Jesus's prediction of his passion and the call to take up one's cross daily (9:22–27) are foundational for Catholic spirituality. The great travel narrative beginning at 9:51 — "he set his face to go to Jerusalem" — initiates a spiritual pilgrimage that Catholic devotional practice mirrors in the Stations of the Cross.
