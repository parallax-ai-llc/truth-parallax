## The Nativity

Catholic interpretation of Luke 2 is deeply interwoven with the liturgical celebration of Christmas. The birth of Jesus in Bethlehem (2:1–7) fulfills the Micah prophecy and underscores the paradox of divine glory manifested in poverty. The Catholic tradition has developed rich artistic, musical, and devotional traditions around this narrative, from the Christmas crèche (attributed to St. Francis of Assisi) to the solemn Midnight Mass.

## The Shepherds and the Presentation

The angelic announcement to the shepherds (2:8–20) illustrates God's preferential concern for the humble, a recurring theme in Catholic social doctrine. The Presentation in the Temple (2:22–40) is celebrated as the Feast of the Presentation (February 2) and includes the Nunc Dimittis of Simeon — "Lord, now let your servant depart in peace" — prayed at Compline (Night Prayer). Simeon's prophecy that a sword would pierce Mary's heart (2:35) is the foundation of the Catholic devotion to Our Lady of Sorrows.

## The Finding in the Temple

The finding of the twelve-year-old Jesus in the Temple (2:41–52) is the fifth Joyful Mystery of the Rosary. Catholic interpreters emphasize both Jesus's obedience to his earthly parents and his higher obedience to his heavenly Father, modeling the proper ordering of earthly and divine authority.
