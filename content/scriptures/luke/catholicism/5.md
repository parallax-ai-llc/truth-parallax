## The Call of Peter and Apostolic Ministry

The miraculous catch of fish and the calling of Peter (5:1–11) is read by Catholic interpreters as the foundation of apostolic ministry. Peter's boat, from which Jesus teaches the crowds, is a traditional symbol of the Church (the "barque of Peter"). Peter's confession of sinfulness and Jesus's commission to "catch people" prefigure the Petrine ministry that Catholic ecclesiology traces from this moment to the papacy.

## Healing and the Sacrament of Forgiveness

The healing of the paralytic (5:17–26) is particularly significant for Catholic sacramental theology. Jesus's declaration "Your sins are forgiven" demonstrates his authority to forgive sins — an authority that Catholic teaching holds was transmitted to the apostles and their successors through the Sacrament of Reconciliation. The visible healing serves as a sign of the invisible spiritual healing, mirroring the sacramental principle of outward signs conveying inward grace.

## The Call of Levi

The call of Levi (5:27–32) and Jesus's table fellowship with tax collectors and sinners exemplify the mercy of God that lies at the heart of Catholic pastoral practice. Pope Francis has repeatedly cited Jesus's words "I have not come to call the righteous but sinners to repentance" as the guiding principle for a Church that functions as a "field hospital" for wounded humanity.
