## The Widow's Offering

The widow's two coins (21:1–4) are understood in Orthodox teaching as revealing the inner disposition that God values above all external display. The widow gives "all she had to live on" — a total self-offering that mirrors Christ's own sacrifice. Orthodox monastic tradition holds up this widow as a model of the poverty and trust to which all Christians are called.

## The Eschatological Discourse

Jesus's prophecy of the Temple's destruction and the signs of the end (21:5–36) is read during Orthodox Holy Week and in the Lenten period. Orthodox eschatology, following the Fathers, generally avoids speculative timetables for the end times, focusing instead on the spiritual preparedness that Jesus demands. The "distress of nations" and cosmic signs (21:25–26) are understood as characterizing the entire period of the Church's earthly pilgrimage.

## Watchfulness and Prayer

The concluding exhortation to "stay awake at all times, praying" (21:36) captures the essence of Orthodox eschatological spirituality. The watchful vigilance (nepsis) taught by the hesychastic tradition — constant attention to the movements of the heart and mind — is the practical expression of this Lukan command. The faithful are called to live each day as if it were the last, maintaining the alertness that the Desert Fathers called "sobriety" of spirit.
