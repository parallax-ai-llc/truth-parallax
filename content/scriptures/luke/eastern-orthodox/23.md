## The Trial and the Innocent Lamb

Luke's passion narrative emphasizes Jesus's innocence — declared by Pilate, uncontested by Herod. Orthodox theology reads the trial as the condemnation of the sinless Lamb of God, whose voluntary self-offering fulfills the Paschal sacrifice of Exodus. The Orthodox Paschal liturgy proclaims "Christ our Passover has been sacrificed for us" — the innocent one who bears the sins of the world.

## The Crucifixion

The crucifixion in Luke (23:26–49) is commemorated on Great and Holy Friday, the most solemn day of the Orthodox calendar. Jesus's prayer "Father, forgive them" (23:34) and his promise to the penitent thief "Today you will be with me in paradise" (23:43) are among the most cherished texts in Orthodox devotion. The penitent thief (known as Dismas in Western tradition, but simply "the wise thief" in Orthodox hymnography) is commemorated as a saint, and his prayer "Remember me when you come into your kingdom" is incorporated into Orthodox worship.

## The Burial and Holy Saturday

The burial of Jesus (23:50–56) is commemorated in the Orthodox Epitaphios service on Holy Friday evening, when a cloth icon of the dead Christ is carried in procession. Holy Saturday — the day between crucifixion and resurrection — is understood in Orthodox theology as the day Christ descended into Hades, trampling death and liberating the righteous dead. This is the subject of the most iconic image in Orthodox art: the Anastasis (Resurrection) icon.
