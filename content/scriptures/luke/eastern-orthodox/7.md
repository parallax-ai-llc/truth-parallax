## The Centurion's Faith

The healing of the centurion's servant (7:1–10) is significant in Orthodox theology for demonstrating that faith transcends ethnic and religious boundaries. The centurion's words are echoed in the Orthodox pre-Communion prayer: "I am not worthy, Master and Lord, that you should enter under the roof of my soul." His recognition of Jesus's authority over sickness is understood as an act of faith in Christ's divine power.

## The Widow of Nain and Victory Over Death

The raising of the widow's son at Nain (7:11–17) is read by Orthodox interpreters as a prefiguration of Christ's own resurrection and the general resurrection of the dead. Jesus's compassion for the widow and his command "Young man, I say to you, arise" reveal the life-giving power of the incarnate Word. Orthodox funeral hymnography draws on this hope: death is not the final word, for Christ has trampled death by death.

## The Sinful Woman and Repentance

The anointing by the sinful woman (7:36–50) is a beloved text in Orthodox Lenten hymnography. The hymn of Kassiani (sung on Holy Wednesday) meditates on this woman's tears and her encounter with divine forgiveness. Orthodox interpretation emphasizes that her great love flows from the experience of being forgiven, and her story is a model for all who approach Christ in repentance.
