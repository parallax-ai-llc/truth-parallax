## The Parable of the Sower

Orthodox interpretation of the parable of the sower (8:4–15) applies it to the spiritual life. The four types of soil represent different dispositions of the soul: the hardened heart closed to grace, the shallow heart that receives the Word without depth, the distracted heart choked by worldly cares, and the prepared heart that bears fruit through patient endurance. Orthodox spiritual directors use this parable to guide the faithful in cultivating receptivity to the Word through prayer, fasting, and repentance.

## Mastery Over Creation and the Demonic

The calming of the storm (8:22–25) and the exorcism of the Gerasene demoniac (8:26–39) reveal Christ's lordship over both the natural world and the spiritual realm. Orthodox theology sees the storm as symbolizing the turbulence of the passions and the demonic as representing the enslaving power of sin. Christ's authority to still both nature and demons demonstrates the comprehensive scope of salvation.

## Jairus's Daughter and the Power of Faith

The intertwined stories of Jairus's daughter and the hemorrhaging woman (8:40–56) illustrate the Orthodox teaching that faith is the means by which divine healing power is received. The woman's touch of Jesus's garment has informed Orthodox veneration of icons and relics — material objects through which the divine presence and power are communicated.
