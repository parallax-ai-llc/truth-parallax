## The Lord's Prayer in Orthodox Worship

The Lord's Prayer (11:1–4) occupies a central place in Orthodox liturgical life, recited in every service of the Daily Office and in the Divine Liturgy before the reception of Communion. Orthodox interpretation, following the Fathers, reads each petition as encompassing both the material and spiritual dimensions of life. "Give us each day our daily bread" is understood as referring to both physical sustenance and the "supersubstantial bread" (epiousion) of the Eucharist.

## The Beelzebul Controversy and Spiritual Discernment

The Beelzebul controversy (11:14–26) and Jesus's teaching about the unclean spirit (11:24–26) are significant for Orthodox demonology and the practice of spiritual discernment. Orthodox theology holds that Christ's exorcisms inaugurate the defeat of the demonic powers, a victory that continues through the Church's sacramental and prayer life. The warning about the swept and empty house teaches that moral reformation without the indwelling of the Holy Spirit leaves the soul vulnerable to worse spiritual corruption.

## The Woes and Prophetic Critique

The woes against the Pharisees and lawyers (11:37–54) are read by Orthodox interpreters as a prophetic warning against the danger of reducing religion to external observance. The Fathers apply these warnings to the Church itself, reminding the faithful that piety without mercy, knowledge without humility, and tradition without living faith are all forms of spiritual death.
