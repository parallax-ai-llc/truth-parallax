## The Shrewd Manager

The parable of the shrewd manager (16:1–13) is interpreted by Orthodox Fathers as teaching prudent use of earthly goods for eternal benefit. St. John Chrysostom applied this parable to almsgiving: wealth that is shared with the poor becomes spiritual treasure stored in heaven. The concluding saying "You cannot serve God and mammon" is a foundational text for Orthodox monastic poverty and for the Church's critique of economic injustice.

## The Rich Man and Lazarus

The parable of the rich man and Lazarus (16:19–31) is one of the most important texts for Orthodox eschatology and the theology of the afterlife. The "bosom of Abraham" where Lazarus rests is understood as the state of the righteous dead before Christ's resurrection — a place of consolation and peace. The "great chasm" (16:26) represents the irreversibility of one's spiritual state after death, grounding the Orthodox urgency of repentance in this life.

## The Word of God and the Saints

Abraham's response — "They have Moses and the Prophets; let them listen to them" (16:29) — affirms the sufficiency of divine revelation for salvation. Orthodox interpretation extends this to include the entire witness of Scripture and Tradition. The refusal to believe even if someone rises from the dead (16:31) is read as a sober commentary on the hardness of the human heart when it is closed to grace.
