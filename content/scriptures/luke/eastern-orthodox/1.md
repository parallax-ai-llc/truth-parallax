## The Annunciation and the Theotokos

In Orthodox theology, Luke 1 is the chapter that reveals the mystery of the Incarnation through the Theotokos. The Annunciation (1:26–38) is one of the Great Feasts of the Orthodox liturgical calendar (March 25), and the entire event is understood as the moment when heaven and earth are united through Mary's free consent. Her response "Be it done to me according to your word" is not passive acceptance but an active, willing participation in divine providence — the supreme act of human synergy with divine grace.

## The Magnificat and the Visitation

The Magnificat (1:46–55) is sung at Matins in the Orthodox tradition and expresses the theology of divine reversal that pervades Luke's Gospel. The Visitation (1:39–56) is understood as the meeting of the old and new covenants — the prophetic child John leaps in the womb in recognition of the incarnate Lord. Orthodox hymnography draws extensively on this scene, celebrating Mary as the living Ark of the Covenant who carries within her the Word of God.

## The Benedictus and Prophetic Tradition

Zechariah's Benedictus (1:68–79) is chanted in Orthodox morning services. Orthodox exegesis reads it as a prophetic summary of salvation history: God has "visited and redeemed his people," fulfilling the oath sworn to Abraham. The birth of John the Baptist is celebrated on June 24, one of the oldest feasts in the Orthodox calendar.
