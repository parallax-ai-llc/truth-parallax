## The Empty Tomb and Pascha

Luke 24's account of the resurrection is the climax of the Gospel and the foundation of the Orthodox celebration of Pascha (Easter) — the "Feast of feasts." The women at the empty tomb discover that "He is not here, but has risen" (24:6). The Orthodox Paschal liturgy proclaims "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life" — the most joyful hymn in the Orthodox tradition.

## The Emmaus Road and the Divine Liturgy

The Emmaus narrative (24:13–35) is read by Orthodox interpreters as a model of the Divine Liturgy. Jesus opens the Scriptures (the Liturgy of the Word), and the disciples recognize him in the breaking of the bread (the Liturgy of the Eucharist). The phrase "their eyes were opened" (24:31) describes the illumination that comes through sacramental encounter with the risen Christ. Every celebration of the Eucharist is an Emmaus experience — the risen Lord making himself known through his Word and his Body.

## The Ascension and the Promise of the Spirit

Jesus's final appearance and ascension (24:36–53) are foundational for Orthodox Christology and pneumatology. The bodily resurrection — "Touch me, and see. For a spirit does not have flesh and bones" — affirms the redemption of the material world. The ascension, celebrated forty days after Pascha, is a Great Feast of the Orthodox Church. The promise of the Holy Spirit (24:49) points to Pentecost, when the Spirit descends to constitute the Church as the continuing Body of Christ in the world.
