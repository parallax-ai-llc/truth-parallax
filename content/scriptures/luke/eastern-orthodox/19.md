## Zacchaeus Sunday

The story of Zacchaeus (19:1–10) is read on the Sunday before the Triodion begins (the Sunday of Zacchaeus) in the Orthodox liturgical calendar, making it the opening text of the Church's preparation for Great Lent. Zacchaeus's eager desire to see Jesus, his willingness to be transformed, and Jesus's initiative in calling him embody the dynamics of conversion that the Lenten season cultivates. His fourfold restitution models the fruits of genuine repentance.

## The Parable of the Ten Minas

The parable of the ten minas (19:11–27) teaches faithful stewardship of the gifts received from God — the talents, charisms, and opportunities entrusted to each person at baptism. Orthodox interpretation emphasizes that these gifts are not private possessions but are given for the building up of the Church and the service of the world. The severe judgment on the wicked servant warns against the spiritual sloth (acedia) that the Desert Fathers identified as one of the most dangerous passions.

## The Triumphal Entry and Palm Sunday

Jesus's entry into Jerusalem (19:28–40) is celebrated as Palm Sunday, the beginning of Holy Week in the Orthodox calendar. The Orthodox liturgy for this feast is rich with hymnography celebrating Christ as the humble King who comes in peace. Jesus's weeping over Jerusalem (19:41–44) is understood as an expression of divine grief over human resistance to salvation.
