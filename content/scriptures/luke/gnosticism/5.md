## The Calling of the Pneumatics

Gnostic interpreters read the calling of disciples in Luke 5 as the gathering of pneumatic individuals — those who possess the divine spark and are capable of receiving gnosis. Not all humans are equally receptive: Valentinian Gnosticism distinguished between pneumatics (spiritual), psychics (soulish), and hylics (material). The disciples who leave everything to follow Jesus are the pneumatics who recognize the call of the divine and abandon the material world's claims.

## Healing as Spiritual Liberation

The healings in Luke 5 — the leper, the paralytic — were understood by Gnostic interpreters as allegories of spiritual liberation rather than literal physical cures. Leprosy represented the corruption of the material body; paralysis symbolized the soul's imprisonment in matter. The true healing that Jesus brings is not physical restoration but the awakening of gnosis — the knowledge that liberates the divine spark from its material prison.

## New Wine in New Wineskins

The saying about new wine in old wineskins (5:36–39) was particularly important for Gnostic hermeneutics. The "old wineskins" represented the religion of the Demiurge — the Jewish law, the Temple cult, the material creation. The "new wine" was the gnosis that Jesus brought from the Pleroma. Marcion especially favored this passage as evidence that Jesus's message was fundamentally incompatible with the Old Testament religion.
