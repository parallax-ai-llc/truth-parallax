## The Beatitudes and Gnostic Reversal

Gnostic interpreters read the Beatitudes and Woes of Luke 6 as describing the cosmic reversal that gnosis accomplishes. The "poor" who are blessed are the pneumatics — poor in material terms but rich in divine knowledge. The "rich" who receive woes are the hylics — wealthy in the Demiurge's world but spiritually bankrupt. The Gnostic reading radicalizes the reversal theme: it is not merely social but ontological, describing the inversion of the entire material order.

## Love of Enemies and the Unknown God

The command to love enemies (6:27–36) was interpreted by Marcion as evidence that Jesus's God is fundamentally different from the God of the Old Testament. While the Demiurge operates by justice, retribution, and law, the true God revealed by Jesus operates by unconditional love and mercy. This teaching, for Marcion, proved that Jesus brought a revelation from a previously unknown deity whose nature is pure grace — entirely unlike the jealous, wrathful creator of Genesis.

## The Two Builders

The parable of the two builders (6:46–49) was read by Gnostics as contrasting two types of spiritual foundation: the pneumatic foundation of gnosis (rock) and the psychic or hylic foundation of mere faith or material religion (sand). True spiritual stability comes only from the deep knowledge of one's divine origin and destiny, not from outward religious observance.
