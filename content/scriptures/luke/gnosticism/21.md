## The Destruction of the Temple as Cosmic Symbol

Jesus's prophecy of the Temple's destruction (21:5–6) was embraced by Gnostic interpreters as confirming the impermanence and ultimate futility of the Demiurge's religious system. The Temple — the supreme material expression of the Demiurge's relationship with his chosen people — is destined for annihilation. Gnostic cosmology envisioned the eventual dissolution of the entire material order, and the Temple's destruction was a foretaste of this cosmic dissolution.

## The Signs of the End and Liberation

The apocalyptic signs described in Luke 21 — wars, earthquakes, cosmic disturbances — were interpreted by Gnostic readers not as catastrophes to be feared but as the birth pangs of cosmic liberation. The dissolution of the material world is the necessary precondition for the pneumatic sparks to return to the Pleroma. What the proto-orthodox feared as the end of the world, the Gnostics welcomed as the beginning of true existence.

## The Son of Man

The coming of the "Son of Man in a cloud with power and great glory" (21:27) was read through varying Gnostic lenses. Some interpreted the Son of Man as the divine Anthropos — the primal heavenly Human whose image the pneumatic sparks bear. His coming in glory represents the final gathering of all pneumatic fragments back into the Pleroma, completing the cosmic drama that began with Sophia's fall.
