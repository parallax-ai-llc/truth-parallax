## The Sabbath and the Demiurge's Law

The Sabbath controversy in Luke 14 was interpreted by Gnostic readers as demonstrating Jesus's freedom from the Demiurge's legal system. The Sabbath, instituted by the creator god in Genesis 2, belongs to the material order that the divine Christ transcends. Jesus's willingness to heal on the Sabbath shows that the true God's mercy operates outside and above the Demiurge's regulations.

## The Great Banquet and Cosmic Invitation

The parable of the great banquet (14:15–24) was given cosmic scope by Gnostic interpreters. The original invited guests who refuse are the psychics — those who are comfortable within the Demiurge's religious system and see no need for the radical gnosis that Jesus offers. The poor, crippled, blind, and lame who are brought in from the streets are the pneumatics — marginalized by the religious establishment but possessing the divine spark that makes them receptive to the true invitation.

## Renunciation of the Material World

The teaching on the cost of discipleship (14:25–35) — hating family, carrying the cross, renouncing possessions — was embraced by Gnostic interpreters as confirming the necessity of radical detachment from the material world. Family ties, social obligations, and material possessions all bind the pneumatic soul to the Demiurge's realm. True discipleship requires severing these bonds and embracing the ascetic path that leads back to the Pleroma.
