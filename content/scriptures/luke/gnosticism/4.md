## Marcion's Gospel Begins Here

Marcion's edited version of Luke began at what corresponds to Luke 4:31: "He went down to Capernaum, a city of Galilee." For Marcion, this was the true beginning of the gospel — the unknown God's emissary descending directly from heaven into the Demiurge's world without birth, childhood, or baptism. The abruptness of this beginning was theologically deliberate: Jesus appears out of nowhere, from a God previously unknown, bringing a message of pure grace that has no connection to the Old Testament.

## The Temptation and the Archons

Gnostic interpreters who retained the temptation narrative (4:1–13) read it as a confrontation between the divine Savior and the archons (rulers) of the material world. Satan was identified with or subordinated to the Demiurge — the ignorant creator god who rules the material realm. Jesus's victory over the tempter demonstrated the superiority of pneumatic knowledge over the powers that imprison humanity in matter.

## The Nazareth Proclamation

The reading of Isaiah 61 in the Nazareth synagogue (4:16–21) was interpreted by some Gnostics as Jesus proclaiming liberation not from political oppression but from the bondage of material existence itself. The "captives" are the divine sparks imprisoned in matter; the "blind" are those who lack gnosis; the "oppressed" are those enslaved by the Demiurge's law. The "year of the Lord's favor" is the moment when gnosis breaks into the world of ignorance.
