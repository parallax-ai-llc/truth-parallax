## The Last Supper and Gnostic Sacrament

Luke 22's Last Supper account (22:14–23) was interpreted variously by Gnostic groups. Some Gnostic communities practiced their own eucharistic rites with different theological meanings — the bread and wine representing not the body and blood of a crucified savior but the gnosis and pneumatic life that the divine Christ imparts. Valentinian Gnostics are known to have practiced a "bridal chamber" sacrament that they considered superior to the ordinary eucharist.

## The Betrayal and the Archons' Plot

The betrayal by Judas (22:47–53) was read as part of the archons' plot against the divine emissary. In some Gnostic texts, Judas is portrayed not as a villain but as a figure who cooperates with the divine plan — the Gospel of Judas presents him as Jesus's most trusted disciple who facilitates the liberation of the divine Christ from its material body. The "power of darkness" that Jesus references (22:53) was identified with the archonic rulers of the material realm.

## Gethsemane and the Human Jesus

The agony in Gethsemane (22:39–46) presented a problem for docetic Gnostics. If the divine Christ cannot truly suffer, then the anguish described here belongs only to the human Jesus — the material vessel that the Christ-Aeon inhabits. Some Gnostic texts describe the divine Christ watching the human Jesus suffer from above, even laughing at the archons who believe they can harm the divine being by torturing the body.
