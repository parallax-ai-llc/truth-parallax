## The Question of Authority and Cosmic Sovereignty

The confrontation over Jesus's authority (20:1–8) was read by Gnostic interpreters as a clash between the representatives of the Demiurge (the chief priests and scribes) and the emissary of the true God. The religious authorities cannot recognize Jesus's authority because it comes from a source entirely outside their cognitive framework — the unknown God above the Demiurge. Their inability to answer Jesus's counter-question reveals the limitations of psychic consciousness.

## The Wicked Tenants and the Gnostic Myth

The parable of the wicked tenants (20:9–19) was mapped onto the Gnostic cosmic narrative. The vineyard is the material world; the tenants are the archons who rule it; the servants sent by the owner are the prophets and divine messengers sent from the Pleroma throughout history. The killing of the "beloved son" represents the archons' attempt to destroy the divine Savior — an attempt that, in Gnostic theology, fails because the true divine Christ cannot be killed (only the material body suffers).

## The Cornerstone and Gnostic Ecclesiology

The rejected stone that becomes the cornerstone (20:17) was understood by Gnostic interpreters as the gnosis rejected by the mainstream religious establishment but destined to become the foundation of the true spiritual community. The Gnostic ecclesia is built not on the Demiurge's law but on the pneumatic knowledge brought by the divine Christ.
