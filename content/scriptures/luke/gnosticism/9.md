## The Transfiguration and Divine Light

The Transfiguration (9:28–36) was one of the most important passages for Gnostic Christology. The radiant glory that the disciples witnessed was understood as the momentary unveiling of the divine Christ's true nature — the light of the Pleroma shining through the veil of flesh. Valentinian Gnostics taught that this light was the same divine light that existed before the material creation, the uncreated luminosity of the true God.

## Moses, Elijah, and the Levels of Revelation

The appearance of Moses and Elijah was interpreted differently by various Gnostic groups. For Marcion, their presence was problematic since they represented the Demiurge's covenant. Valentinian Gnostics offered a more nuanced reading: Moses and Elijah represented the psychic level of revelation (Law and Prophets) that was being superseded by the pneumatic revelation of the divine Christ. Their conversation about Jesus's "departure" (exodus) referred to the Christ's eventual return to the Pleroma.

## The Cross and Gnostic Soteriology

Jesus's teaching on taking up the cross (9:23–27) was reinterpreted in Gnostic terms. The cross was not primarily a symbol of suffering but of the intersection between the material and spiritual realms. To "take up the cross" was to embrace the pneumatic path — renouncing the material world and its attachments in pursuit of gnosis. Losing one's life to save it meant dying to the false material self to discover one's true pneumatic identity.
