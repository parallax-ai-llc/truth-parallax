## Nothing Hidden That Will Not Be Revealed

Jesus's teaching that "nothing is covered up that will not be revealed" (12:2) was a key text for Gnostic hermeneutics. Gnostic interpreters saw this as Jesus's promise that the esoteric knowledge he transmitted to his inner circle would eventually be made known to all pneumatics. The surface meaning of the Gospels conceals a deeper, hidden teaching that only the spiritually advanced can discern — and that hidden teaching is gnosis itself.

## The Rich Fool and Material Illusion

The parable of the rich fool (12:13–21) reinforced the Gnostic contempt for material existence. The fool's error is not merely greed but a fundamental ontological mistake: he treats the material world as real and permanent when it is, in Gnostic cosmology, a temporary and illusory realm. "Being rich toward God" means accumulating spiritual knowledge — recognizing one's divine origin and pursuing liberation from the prison of matter.

## Vigilance and Pneumatic Readiness

The parables of watchfulness (12:35–48) were read as exhortations to pneumatic vigilance — maintaining awareness of one's true spiritual identity in the midst of the material world's distractions. The master's return represents the consummation of the cosmic drama when the pneumatic sparks are gathered back into the Pleroma. The unfaithful servant represents those who, despite possessing the divine spark, fall back into material slumber.
