## The First Book of Jeu: Text, Diagrams, and Emanative Structure

The First Book of Jeu presents scholars with a unique combination of textual instruction and visual representation. The sixty emanations of Jeu are each accompanied by a diagram — a schematic representation of the emanation's seal and character. These diagrams constitute one of the earliest known traditions of illustrated esoteric Christian literature, and their relationship to the text raises important questions about the transmission of religious knowledge in late antiquity.

Carl Schmidt's 1892 edition reproduced the diagrams alongside the Coptic text, but their interpretation remains contested. Some scholars read them as mnemonic devices for initiates memorizing the system of seals; others suggest they functioned as ritual objects in their own right, inscribed on amulets or drawn during liturgical ceremonies. The relationship between the diagrams in the Bruce Codex and the "diagrams" criticized by Plotinus and Irenaeus in other Gnostic contexts is uncertain but suggestive.

The emanative structure of the First Book — sixty emanations proceeding from a single divine source — has been compared to Valentinian systems (the thirty aeons of the Pleroma) and to Middle Platonic theories of emanation (particularly those of Numenius of Apamea). The precise number sixty may reflect astronomical or calendrical symbolism, though no scholarly consensus has been reached on this point.
