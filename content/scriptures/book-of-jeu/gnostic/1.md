## The Sixty Emanations of Jeu

The First Book of Jeu presents the most detailed emanative system in the Gnostic corpus. Jesus reveals to his disciples the sixty emanations that proceed from the figure of Jeu, whom he identifies as "the true God" — the overseer of the Treasury of Light and the being through whom the highest God expresses itself in differentiated form. Each emanation is characterized by three elements: a seal (*sphragis*), a number, and a unique character or cipher.

For Gnostic readers, this elaborate system is not abstract speculation but practical soteriology. The seals and numbers are the keys that the ascending soul must present to the guardians of each heavenly level. Without this knowledge, the soul cannot pass through the archontic barriers that separate the material cosmos from the Treasury of Light. The sixty emanations thus function as a celestial map — each station on the soul's journey home is marked, named, and equipped with the specific token that grants passage.

The diagrammatic tradition accompanying the text — visual representations of each emanation — underscores the practical orientation of the work. These are not decorative illustrations but functional tools, visual aids for memorizing the complex system of seals and correspondences that the initiate must internalize before death. The combination of text and image in the Books of Jeu anticipates later esoteric traditions (Kabbalistic diagrams, alchemical emblems, Tantric mandalas) in which visual representation serves as a vehicle for spiritual knowledge.
