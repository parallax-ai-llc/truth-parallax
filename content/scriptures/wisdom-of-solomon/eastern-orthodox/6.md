## Royal Accountability

Chapter 6 warns rulers that their authority derives from the Most High and that strict judgment awaits those who abuse power. Orthodox political theology, expressed in the Byzantine concept of symphonia between Church and State, holds that temporal authority is delegated and accountable to God. The chapter provides scriptural grounding for the Orthodox tradition of prophetic witness to rulers — from John Chrysostom confronting Empress Eudoxia to modern hierarchs speaking truth to power.

## Wisdom Seeks the Seeker

The beautiful passage describing Wisdom sitting at the gates, going to meet those who are worthy, and appearing on every path (6:12-16) resonates with the Orthodox theology of divine energies. God is not passive but actively reaches out to creation through His uncreated energies. This Palamite distinction — between the unknowable divine essence and the experienceable divine energies — finds an early echo in the Wisdom of Solomons portrait of Wisdom permeating and penetrating all things.

## The Ladder of Wisdom

The sorites argument (6:17-20) — from desire to love to obedience to immortality to nearness to God — mirrors the Orthodox understanding of the spiritual life as an ascent. John Climacus Ladder of Divine Ascent presents a similar graduated path from earthly attachments to divine union. The Wisdom of Solomon provides a scriptural model for this progressive understanding of spiritual growth.
