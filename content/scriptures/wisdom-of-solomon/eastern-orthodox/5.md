## The Wicked at Judgment

Chapter 5 portrays the ungodly at the last judgment, confessing their folly in having persecuted the righteous. Orthodox eschatology, while less systematized than Western eschatology, affirms the reality of divine judgment. The kontakia and stichera of the Sunday of the Last Judgment (Meatfare Sunday) echo the themes of this chapter — the reversal of earthly fortune and the vindication of those who suffered for righteousness.

## God as Cosmic Warrior

The vision of God arming Himself with creation as weaponry (5:17-23) resonates with the Orthodox celebration of Christs victory over death, sin, and the devil. The Paschal troparion — "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death" — proclaims the same reality in liturgical form: God enters the cosmic battle not with the weapons of worldly power but with the transformative power of self-giving love, which reconfigures creation itself.

## The Righteous Live Forever

The declaration that "the righteous live forever" (5:15) is echoed throughout Orthodox hymnography and theological reflection on the saints. The Orthodox veneration of relics, icons, and the memory of the saints presupposes this conviction: death does not end the life of the righteous but transforms it. The saints remain active members of the Body of Christ, interceding for the living and participating in the divine liturgy celebrated in heaven.
