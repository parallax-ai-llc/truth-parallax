## Virtue over Length of Days

Chapter 4 teaches that a virtuous life cut short surpasses a long life of wickedness. Orthodox interpretation connects this to the veneration of young martyrs and saints who died early — Saints Boris and Gleb, Saint Barbara, and many child martyrs. The Church does not measure holiness by years but by conformity to Christ, and this chapter provides scriptural support for that conviction.

## The Translation of Enoch

The passage about the righteous one taken early (4:10-14) is read alongside the story of Enochs translation and Elijahs ascent. Orthodox theology sees these as anticipations of the Resurrection and Ascension of Christ. The icon of the Ascension depicts Christ taken up into glory, and the Wisdom of Solomons language about the righteous snatched away from corruption contributes to the theological vocabulary of divine translation.

## Eschatological Reversal

The chapters closing vision of the wicked recognizing the exaltation of the righteous anticipates the Orthodox teaching on the Last Judgment. Orthodox iconography of the Final Judgment, found in the narthex of many churches, depicts precisely this reversal — the humble exalted, the proud brought low. The Wisdom of Solomon provides literary and theological depth to these iconic representations.
