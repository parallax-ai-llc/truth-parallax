## The Paschal Night

Chapter 18 retells the Passover night, contrasting Israels light with Egypts darkness and death. Orthodox Paschal theology reads the original Passover as a type of Christs Resurrection — the definitive passage from death to life, from slavery to freedom. The Paschal Liturgy, celebrated at midnight, reenacts this transition: the church moves from darkness to blazing light as the priest proclaims "Christ is risen!"

## The All-Powerful Word

The dramatic image of the all-powerful Word leaping from heaven (18:14-16) is interpreted by Orthodox exegesis as a theophany — a manifestation of the divine Logos in judgment. While Western tradition applied this passage to the Incarnation, Orthodox interpretation more commonly reads it in its original context of divine judgment, while recognizing that the same Logos who judges is the one who saves. The liturgical hymnography of Pascha celebrates precisely this unity of judgment and salvation.

## Aarons Priestly Intercession

The account of Aaron standing between the living and the dead (18:20-25) is a powerful type of priestly mediation in Orthodox tradition. The Orthodox priest, vested in liturgical garments bearing the symbols of Christs high priesthood, stands before the altar interceding for the living and the dead. The Wisdom of Solomons portrait of Aaron — armed with prayer and the covenant rather than weapons — defines the Orthodox understanding of priestly ministry as fundamentally intercessory.
