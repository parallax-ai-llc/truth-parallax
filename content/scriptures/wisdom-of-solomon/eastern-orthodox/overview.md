## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox Church accepts the Wisdom of Solomon as part of its Old Testament canon, following the Septuagint tradition. Orthodox interpretation is deeply rooted in patristic exegesis, and the book holds a prominent place in liturgical worship, theological reflection, and iconographic tradition.

## Sophiology

The personification of Wisdom (Sophia) in the Wisdom of Solomon has had a profound impact on Orthodox theology and culture. Great cathedrals — most notably the Hagia Sophia in Constantinople — were dedicated to Holy Wisdom. Orthodox theologians read Sophia as pointing both to Christ (the Logos and Wisdom of God) and to the uncreated divine energies through which God acts in the world.

## Patristic Reception

The Church Fathers drew extensively on the Wisdom of Solomon:

- **Clement of Alexandria** and **Origen** used Sophia language to articulate early Christological formulations
- **Athanasius** cited Wisdom 7:25-26 in defense of the Son's consubstantiality with the Father
- **Gregory of Nyssa** employed the book's language of immortality and virtue in his ascetical theology

## Liturgical Use

The Wisdom of Solomon is read at Vespers for major feasts of the Theotokos and of saints, and its themes permeate hymnography. The opening exhortation to love righteousness (1:1) resonates with the Orthodox emphasis on *theosis* — the transformative participation in divine life that is the goal of human existence.
