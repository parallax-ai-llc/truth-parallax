## Prophetic History

Chapter 10 traces Wisdoms role in guiding the righteous from Adam through Moses. Islamic tradition recognizes all these figures as prophets (*anbiya*) sent by God with divine guidance. The Quran recounts the stories of Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Lut, Yaqub, Yusuf, and Musa in similar terms — as recipients of divine guidance who were protected and delivered by God. The parallels between the Wisdom of Solomons survey and Quranic prophetic narrative are extensive.

## The Pattern of Divine Guidance

The Wisdom of Solomons presentation of a consistent pattern — Wisdom guiding, protecting, and vindicating the righteous in every generation — mirrors the Quranic teaching that God sent prophets to every nation: "There was no community to which a warner was not sent" (35:24). Both texts affirm the continuity of divine guidance throughout human history, though Islam sees this guidance as culminating in the final revelation to Muhammad.

## The Exodus as Divine Deliverance

The chapters climax in the Exodus, where Wisdom delivers Israel through Moses. The Quran devotes extensive attention to Musas story (mentioned more than any other prophet), presenting it as a paradigm of divine deliverance from tyranny. The Wisdom of Solomons identification of Wisdom as the agent of the Exodus resonates with the Quranic emphasis that it was God alone — not Moses or any human power — who delivered the people from Pharaoh.
