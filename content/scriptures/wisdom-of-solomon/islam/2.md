## The Denial of the Afterlife

Chapter 2 presents the reasoning of those who deny life after death and pursue pleasure without restraint. Islamic theology identifies this attitude as *kufr* (disbelief) in the *akhirah* (hereafter) — one of the most fundamental errors a person can make. The Quran repeatedly warns against those who say "there is nothing but our life in this world — we die and we live, and nothing destroys us except time" (45:24), language remarkably similar to the speech of the ungodly in this chapter.

## The Persecution of the Righteous

The depiction of the righteous man persecuted for his piety resonates with the Islamic tradition of prophetic suffering. The Quran recounts how nearly every prophet was rejected, mocked, and persecuted by his people. The pattern described in the Wisdom of Solomon — the arrogant oppressing the faithful — mirrors the Quranic narratives of Nuh (Noah), Ibrahim (Abraham), Musa (Moses), and ultimately Muhammad, all of whom faced opposition for proclaiming Gods truth.

## The Devils Envy

The attribution of death to the devils envy (2:24) parallels the Quranic account of Iblis, who refused to bow to Adam out of pride and envy (7:11-18, 15:28-42). Islamic theology teaches that Iblis became humanitys avowed enemy, seeking to lead people astray. The Wisdom of Solomons identification of envy as the devils primary motivation aligns with the Quranic portrayal of Iblis as one consumed by arrogance and resentment.
