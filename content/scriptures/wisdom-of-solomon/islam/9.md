## The Prayer of Sulayman

Chapter 9 presents Solomons extended prayer for wisdom. Islamic tradition records Sulaamans prayers in the Quran, including his request that God inspire him to be grateful and to do righteous deeds (27:19) and his prayer for a kingdom unlike any other (38:35). The Wisdom of Solomons prayer for wisdom rather than power resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *dua* (supplication) as the essence of worship and the proper orientation of the human heart toward God.

## Human Limitation and Divine Revelation

The confession that human reasoning is feeble and human plans uncertain (9:14-16) corresponds to the Quranic acknowledgment that human knowledge is profoundly limited: "You have not been given of knowledge except a little" (17:85). Islamic epistemology holds that genuine knowledge of God and the unseen (*al-ghayb*) comes only through divine revelation (*wahy*), not through unaided human reason. The Wisdom of Solomon articulates this same conviction in philosophical terms.

## The Spirit of Wisdom from Above

Solomons request that God send wisdom from the holy heavens (9:10) parallels the Islamic understanding of revelation as descending from God through angelic mediation. Jibril (Gabriel) brought the Quran from the Preserved Tablet (*al-Lawh al-Mahfuz*) in the highest heaven to the Prophet Muhammad. While the mechanisms differ, both traditions affirm that true wisdom originates with God and must be transmitted to humanity through divine initiative.
