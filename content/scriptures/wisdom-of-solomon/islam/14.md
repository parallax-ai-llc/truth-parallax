## The Origin of Idolatry

Chapter 14 proposes that idolatry originated from grief over a dead child and from political ruler-worship. Islamic tradition offers its own account: the Quran and hadith describe how the people of Nuh (Noah) originally honored righteous ancestors, and Shaytan gradually led them to worship the images they had made. Both explanations identify the same dynamic — the corruption of legitimate human sentiments into false worship.

## Idolatry and Moral Collapse

The chapters catalog of moral corruptions flowing from idolatry (14:22-31) mirrors the Quranic description of pre-Islamic *jahiliyyah* (age of ignorance). The Arabs before Islam practiced idol worship alongside infanticide, tribal warfare, exploitation, and sexual immorality. The Quran, like the Wisdom of Solomon, identifies false worship as the root cause of social and moral disorder: when God is displaced from the center, everything else falls apart.

## The Wisdom of Solomons Anti-Shirk Polemic

Islamic scholars engaging in comparative study appreciate the Wisdom of Solomons sustained argument against idolatry as consistent with the prophetic message that Islam sees as running from Adam through Muhammad. The books philosophical sophistication — analyzing the psychological and political origins of idolatry — complements the Qurans more direct prophetic condemnation. Together they constitute a comprehensive case for pure monotheism.
