## The Night of Deliverance

Chapter 18 retells the night of the Exodus — the death of Egypts firstborn while Israel was spared. Islamic tradition acknowledges this event as part of Musas prophetic mission. The Quran states, "We took retribution from them and drowned them in the sea because they denied Our signs" (7:136). The Wisdom of Solomons emphasis on the proportionality of punishment — Egypt decreed the death of Hebrew children and suffered the loss of their own — aligns with the Quranic principle that God repays evil in kind.

## The Descending Word

The dramatic image of Gods all-powerful word descending as a warrior (18:14-16) resonates with Islamic conceptions of divine power manifested through the divine command (*amr*). The Quran teaches that when God decrees a thing, He says "Be" and it is (*kun fa-yakun*, 2:117). The Wisdom of Solomons personification of the divine word as an irresistible force reflects this same theology of divine command, though Islamic theology would avoid the degree of personification found here.

## Priestly Intercession

The account of Aarons intercession to halt a plague resonates with the Islamic concept of *shafaah* (intercession). Islamic theology teaches that on the Day of Judgment, the Prophet Muhammad will intercede for his community with Gods permission. The pattern of a righteous mediator standing between the people and divine wrath, armed with prayer and devotion rather than weapons, is shared between the Wisdom of Solomons portrait of Aaron and the Islamic understanding of prophetic intercession.
