## The Accountability of Rulers

Chapter 6 warns rulers that their authority derives from the Most High and will be strictly judged. Islamic political theology (*siyasah shariyyah*) makes the same claim: all authority is delegated from God, and rulers are accountable for their stewardship. The Quran declares that God grants sovereignty to whom He wills (3:26), and the hadith literature records the Prophet Muhammad warning that every shepherd will be questioned about his flock.

## Seeking Wisdom as Religious Duty

The chapters exhortation to seek wisdom resonates with the Islamic emphasis on seeking knowledge (*ilm*) as a religious obligation. The Prophet Muhammad declared, "Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim" (Ibn Majah). The Wisdom of Solomons portrait of Wisdom as accessible, gracious, and actively seeking the worthy mirrors the Islamic conviction that divine guidance is available to all who sincerely pursue it.

## Wisdom and Nearness to God

The sorites argument leading from desire for wisdom to nearness to God (6:17-20) parallels the Islamic concept of *qurb* (nearness to God) achieved through worship, knowledge, and obedience. The Quran declares, "We are nearer to him than his jugular vein" (50:16), affirming divine proximity. The Wisdom of Solomons graduated path from desire to divine nearness resonates with the Sufi tradition of spiritual stations (*maqamat*) through which the seeker progresses toward God.
