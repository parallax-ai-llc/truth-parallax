## The Accountability of the Archons

Gnostic interpreters read the warning to rulers and kings (6:1-8) as addressed not merely to earthly potentates but to the archons — the cosmic rulers whose authority derives from the Demiurge rather than the true God. Their strict judgment reflects the Gnostic expectation that the archons will be held accountable for their tyranny over the material realm and their imprisonment of the divine sparks.

## Sophia Seeks the Worthy

The portrait of Wisdom actively seeking those worthy of her (6:12-16) takes on mythological significance in Gnostic reading. Sophia — the fallen Aeon whose error generated the material world — seeks to recover the divine sparks she inadvertently scattered. Her search for the worthy mirrors the Valentinian teaching that Sophia sends messengers (including, ultimately, Christ) into the material realm to awaken the pneumatics and guide them home to the Pleroma.

## The Ascent to the Pleroma

The sorites argument — from desire to discipline to love to immortality to nearness to God (6:17-20) — is read as a map of the souls ascent through the archonic spheres back to the Pleroma. Each step represents a stage of Gnostic initiation: the awakening of desire for *gnosis*, the discipline of ascetic practice, the love of divine wisdom, the attainment of immortality through knowledge, and finally union with the transcendent God beyond all material existence.
