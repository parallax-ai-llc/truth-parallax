## Prayer as Cry of the Entrapped Spark

Gnostic interpreters read Solomons prayer for wisdom as the cry of the divine spark trapped in matter, calling out to the transcendent God for liberation. The prayer acknowledges the radical limitation of human knowledge — "the reasoning of mortals is worthless, and our designs are likely to fail" (9:14) — which Gnostic readers interpret as a description of the minds imprisonment in the material world created by the Demiurge.

## Sophia Descending from the Throne

The petition for God to send Wisdom "from the throne of your glory" (9:10) is mapped onto the Gnostic myth of Sophias descent. In Valentinian theology, Sophia is sent (or falls) from the Pleroma into the lower realms, and her recovery is the central drama of cosmic history. Solomons prayer enacts in miniature what the Gnostic myth describes in cosmic scope: the pneumatic calls out, and Sophia descends to bring liberating knowledge.

## The Holy Spirit and Gnosis

The near-identification of Wisdom and Holy Spirit in verse 17 supports the Gnostic teaching that *gnosis* comes through pneumatic illumination — the Spirit reveals what the rational mind cannot discover. Gnostic texts such as the Gospel of Philip describe the Spirit as the divine Mother who gives birth to spiritual understanding. The Wisdom of Solomons linking of Sophia and Spirit provides scriptural support for the Gnostic association of feminine divine figures with salvific knowledge.
