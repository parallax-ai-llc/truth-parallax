## Solomon as Gnostic Initiate

Gnostic readers interpret Solomons emphasis on his common humanity (7:1-6) as the starting point of Gnostic awakening: every human being, regardless of worldly status, possesses the potential for *gnosis*. The recognition of ones mortal, material condition is the first step toward recognizing the divine spark within. Solomon becomes a model of the pneumatic who moves from ordinary human awareness to extraordinary divine knowledge.

## The Supremacy of Gnosis

Solomons preference for Wisdom over all material goods (7:7-12) expresses the fundamental Gnostic valuation: *gnosis* surpasses every worldly possession because it alone liberates the soul from material bondage. The Gnostic Gospel of Truth declares that "knowledge of the Father" is the supreme good, and the Wisdom of Solomons passage provides scriptural warrant for this radical prioritization of spiritual knowledge over material wealth.

## The Twenty-One Attributes of Sophia

The great hymn to Wisdom (7:22-30) is the single most important passage in the Wisdom of Solomon for Gnostic theology. The description of Sophia as "a breath of the power of God, a pure emanation of the glory of the Almighty, an image of his goodness" (7:25-26) provided Gnostic mythmakers with the vocabulary of emanation that structures their cosmological systems. Sophia is not a creation but an emanation — she flows from the divine source as light flows from a lamp, sharing its nature while extending its reach. Valentinus built his entire system on this concept of divine emanation, with Sophia as the last Aeon of the Pleroma whose desire to know the Father precipitated the creation of the material world.
