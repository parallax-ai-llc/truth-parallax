## The God Who Loves All Existence

The theological excursus on divine mercy (11:17-26) presents a portrait of God that Gnostic interpreters identify with the transcendent Father of the Pleroma rather than the Demiurge. The God who "loves all things that exist and hates nothing he has made" cannot be the same deity who, in the Gnostic reading of Genesis, expelled Adam from paradise, sent the Flood, and destroyed Sodom. The Wisdom of Solomons merciful God is the true God above the jealous, wrathful creator of the material world.

## Measure for Measure and Archonic Justice

The synkrisis structure — comparing Egypts punishments with Israels blessings — is read by Gnostic interpreters as illustrating the crude justice of the Demiurge, who operates by mechanical retribution. The true God transcends this measure-for-measure logic, offering unconditional mercy and love. Gnostic systems typically distinguished between the Demiurges justice (rigid, retributive) and the true Gods grace (unconditional, liberating).

## The Mercy That Leads to Gnosis

The declaration that God "overlooks the sins of men so that they may repent" (11:23) is reinterpreted as the true Gods patience in waiting for the pneumatics to awaken. The divine sparks trapped in matter need time to discover their true identity and turn toward the Pleroma. The mercy described here is not merely moral forbearance but the cosmic patience of a God who will not rest until every fragment of divine light has been recovered from the material world.
