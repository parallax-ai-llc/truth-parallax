## Address to Kings and Rulers

Chapter 6 resumes the direct address to rulers that opened the book, warning that those in authority will face stricter judgment because power was given to them by the Most High. Protestant interpreters note the echo of Jesus teaching that to whom much is given, much will be required (Luke 12:48), and observe that this chapter articulates a robust theology of political accountability grounded in divine sovereignty.

## Wisdom Accessible to All

The chapter presents Wisdom as actively seeking those who are worthy of her (6:12-16). She is found sitting at the gates, going about seeking those who desire her, and graciously appearing to them in their paths. Protestant commentators appreciate this portrayal of prevenient grace — Wisdom initiates the relationship, anticipating the seeker. This resonates with Reformed theology emphasis on Gods prior action in salvation.

## The Chain of Wisdom

Verses 17-20 present an elegant logical chain: desire for instruction leads to love of Wisdom, love of Wisdom means keeping her laws, keeping her laws ensures immortality, and immortality brings one near to God. Protestant readers recognize a sorites argument (chain syllogism) reflecting Greek rhetorical training, illustrating how the author synthesized Hebrew and Hellenistic thought forms.
