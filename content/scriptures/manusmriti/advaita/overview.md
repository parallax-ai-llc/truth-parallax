## Overview

Advaita Vedanta's engagement with the Manusmriti operates through the fundamental distinction between conventional truth (vyavaharika) and ultimate truth (paramarthika). While the Manusmriti's dharmic prescriptions are valid at the conventional level — governing the conduct of individuals who have not yet attained self-knowledge — they are ultimately transcended by the jnani (knower of Brahman) who has realized the non-dual reality.

## Interpretive Approach

Shankara and later Advaita teachers accept the authority of the Manusmriti for those operating within the framework of varnashrama-dharma but insist that the highest truth lies beyond all social categories. The renunciant (sannyasi) who has realized Brahman is no longer bound by the Manusmriti's prescriptions, having transcended the very categories of caste, stage of life, and social obligation that the text legislates.

## Philosophical Integration

Advaita reading integrates the Manusmriti within a hierarchical understanding of knowledge: the text provides valid guidance for the conduct of worldly life (pravritta dharma) while pointing beyond itself to the path of renunciation (nivritta dharma) that leads to liberation through self-knowledge.
