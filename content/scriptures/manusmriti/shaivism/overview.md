## Overview

Shaiva engagement with the Manusmriti has been characterized by creative tension. While acknowledging the Manusmriti as an important dharmic text, several Shaiva traditions have offered alternative visions of dharma that transcend or challenge the Manusmriti's social hierarchies. The Virashaiva (Lingayat) movement and aspects of Kashmir Shaivism have been particularly critical of caste-based dharmic prescriptions.

## Interpretive Approach

Shaiva interpreters tend to emphasize the inner dimensions of dharma over external social observance. The Manusmriti's detailed prescriptions for caste duties are understood as belonging to the conventional realm of social order, while Shaiva soteriology emphasizes direct experience of Shiva-consciousness as the ultimate dharma that transcends all social categories.

## Alternative Frameworks

The Virashaiva tradition, founded by Basavanna in the 12th century, explicitly rejected caste distinctions and proposed an alternative dharma based on devotion to Shiva, equality of all devotees, and ethical conduct regardless of birth. This represents one of the most significant internal Hindu challenges to the Manusmriti's social vision.
