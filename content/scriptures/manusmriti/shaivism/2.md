Shaiva engagement with this chapter on the sources of dharma, the sacraments from conception through initiation, and the beginning of Vedic education reflects the creative tension between the Manusmriti's social prescriptions and the Shaiva emphasis on inner transformation and the transcendence of conventional categories. While acknowledging the text's authority at the level of conventional dharma, Shaiva interpreters point to the limitations of external social regulation as a means of spiritual liberation.

Shaiva traditions such as Kashmir Shaivism and the Virashaiva movement have offered alternative readings that emphasize the supreme value of Shiva-consciousness over social conformity. The Virashaiva saint-poets (vachanakaras) explicitly challenged aspects of the Manusmriti's social hierarchy, insisting that devotion to Shiva transcends all distinctions of birth, caste, and gender. This chapter's prescriptions are thus read within a framework that acknowledges their conventional validity while pointing beyond them to a higher spiritual reality.
