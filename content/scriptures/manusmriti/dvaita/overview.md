## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta, following Madhvacharya, accepts the Manusmriti as an authoritative text reflecting the divinely ordained structure of dharmic society. The text's prescriptions for the duties of different varnas and ashramas are understood as expressions of God's will for the proper ordering of human life, and their observance is regarded as a form of devotion to Vishnu.

## Interpretive Approach

Dvaita interpretation emphasizes the Manusmriti's theistic foundations, reading its dharmic prescriptions as reflecting the eternal and divinely established order (sanatana dharma) of the cosmos. Madhva's hierarchical metaphysics — which posits inherent gradations among souls — finds support in the Manusmriti's differentiation of duties according to social position. The text's emphasis on dharma as the supreme governing principle of human life aligns with the Dvaita conviction that adherence to God's commands is the foundation of spiritual progress.

## Contemporary Application

Modern Dvaita scholars continue to affirm the Manusmriti's general principles of dharmic order while acknowledging the need for contextual interpretation of its specific social prescriptions.
