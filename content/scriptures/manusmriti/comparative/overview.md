## Overview

Comparative study of the Manusmriti places it alongside other ancient and religious legal codes, including the Code of Hammurabi, the Torah's legal sections, Roman law, Islamic sharia, and Confucian ritual law (li). Each of these systems integrates religious authority with social regulation, providing rich material for cross-cultural comparison.

## Interpretive Approach

Comparative scholars examine the Manusmriti's treatment of social hierarchy, gender, property, punishment, and religious obligation alongside similar themes in other legal traditions. The text's integration of cosmology, ethics, and law — deriving social order from the structure of creation itself — finds parallels in the biblical understanding of law as divine command and in the Islamic concept of sharia as God's path for humanity.

## Contemporary Significance

The Manusmriti raises important comparative questions about the relationship between religious authority and social justice, the status of women in religious legal systems, and the possibility of reforming sacred law traditions while maintaining their spiritual authority. These questions are equally relevant for Jewish halakha, Islamic fiqh, and Christian canon law.
