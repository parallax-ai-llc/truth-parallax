Comparative study of this chapter on the duties of the king (raja-dharma), including statecraft, military policy, taxation, and justice places its prescriptions alongside similar treatments in other religious and legal traditions. The chapter's integration of cosmological, ethical, and legal elements invites comparison with the biblical legal codes of Exodus and Deuteronomy, the Islamic legal tradition's derivation of sharia from divine revelation, and the Confucian integration of ritual propriety (li) with cosmic order.

The chapter raises important comparative questions about the relationship between divine authority and social law, the universality or cultural specificity of moral prescriptions, and the challenges of applying ancient religious codes in modern contexts. Comparative analysis reveals both common patterns — the tendency of religious traditions to ground social order in cosmic or divine authority — and significant differences in how specific traditions understand the nature and scope of sacred law.
