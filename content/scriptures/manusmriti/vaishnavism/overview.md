## Overview

Vaishnava engagement with the Manusmriti treats it as an authoritative text on dharma within the broader framework of devotion to Vishnu/Krishna. While the Manusmriti does not emphasize bhakti as the supreme path, Vaishnava interpreters integrate its dharmic prescriptions within a devotional framework, understanding the performance of one's prescribed duties (svadharma) as a form of service to God.

## Interpretive Approach

Vaishnava readers of the Manusmriti emphasize the text's affirmation that dharma is divinely ordained and that its observance maintains cosmic order. The Bhagavad Gita's teaching that one should perform one's own dharma (svadharma) rather than another's is read as complementary to the Manusmriti's detailed specification of duties for each varna and ashrama. The text's prescriptions for righteous conduct are understood as preparing the soul for the higher path of bhakti.

## Contemporary Relevance

Modern Vaishnava thinkers have selectively engaged with the Manusmriti, affirming its general principles of dharmic conduct while acknowledging the need to reinterpret its social prescriptions in light of contemporary values and the universal accessibility of bhakti.
