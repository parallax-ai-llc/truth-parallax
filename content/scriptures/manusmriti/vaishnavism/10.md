Vaishnava interpretation of this chapter on the origins and duties of mixed castes, occupations in times of distress, and social adaptation situates the Manusmriti's prescriptions within the broader framework of devotion to Vishnu. The text's dharmic prescriptions are understood as establishing the moral and social order within which the devotional life flourishes. Vaishnava commentators emphasize that the performance of one's prescribed duties (svadharma) as outlined in this chapter constitutes a form of worship when dedicated to the Lord.

The Bhagavad Gita's teaching that action performed without attachment and offered to God becomes a means of spiritual purification provides the Vaishnava hermeneutical key for reading the Manusmriti's detailed prescriptions. What might otherwise appear as mere social regulation is transformed into a comprehensive devotional practice when performed with the right intention and dedication to Vishnu.
