---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 7"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 7
---

**1** I will declare the duties of kings, and show how a king should conduct himself, how he was created, and how he can obtain highest success.

**2** A Kshatriya, who has received according to the rule the sacrament prescribed by the Veda, must duly protect this whole world.

**3** For, when these creatures, being without a king, through fear dispersed in all directions, the Lord created a king for the protection of this whole creation.

**4** Taking eternal particles from the essence of Indra, of the Wind, of Yama, of the Sun, of Fire, of Varuna, of the Moon, and of the Lord of wealth,

**5** Because a king is formed of particles of those lords of the gods, he therefore surpasses all created beings in lustre.

**6** And, like the Sun, he burns eyes and hearts; nor can anybody on earth even gaze on him.

**7** Through his power he is Fire and Wind, he is the Sun and the Moon, he is the Lord of justice, he is Kubera, he is Varuna, he is great Indra.

**8** Even an infant king must not be despised, from an idea that he is a mere mortal; for he is a great deity in human form.

**9** The king has been created as the protector of the castes and orders, who, all according to their rank, discharge their several duties.

**10** Let the king, after rising early in the morning, worship Brahmanas who are well versed in the threefold sacred science, and learned in polity, and follow their advice.

**11** Let him daily worship aged Brahmanas who know the Veda and are pure; for he who always worships aged men, is honoured even by Rakshasas.

**12** Let him, though he may already be modest, constantly learn modesty from them; for a king who is modest never perishes.

**13** Through a want of modesty many kings have perished, together with their belongings; through modesty even hermits in the forest have gained kingdoms.

**14** Through a want of humility Vena perished, likewise king Nahusha, Sudas the son of Pijavana, Sumukha, and Nemi.

**15** But by humility Prithu and Manu gained sovereignty, Kubera obtained the position of the Lord of wealth, and the son of Gadhi the rank of a Brahmana.

**16** Let him appoint seven or eight ministers who are natives of the country, whose ancestors have been servants of the state, who are versed in the sciences, heroes skilled in the use of weapons, of noble descent, and who have been tried.

**17** Even an act which is easily performed is difficult for one man alone; how much more difficult is the government of a kingdom, especially if there are no helpers.

**18** Let him daily consider with them the ordinary business of the state: peace and war, his army, his revenue, the protection of his subjects, and the bestowing of rewards.

**19** Having thus consulted with his ministers, let him take exercise; then let him bathe and enter the inner apartments for his midday meal.

**20** The king must exert himself to the utmost for the protection of the people; for the king who protects his subjects receives from each the sixth part of their spiritual merit.
