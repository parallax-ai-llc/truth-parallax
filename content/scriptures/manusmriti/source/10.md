---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 10"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 10
---

**1** The three twice-born castes, discharging their prescribed duties, shall study the Veda; but among them the Brahmana alone shall teach it, not the other two; that is an established rule.

**2** The Brahmana must know the means of subsistence prescribed by law for all, instruct the others, and himself live according to the law.

**3** On account of his pre-eminence, on account of the superiority of his origin, on account of his observance of particular restrictive rules, and on account of his particular sanctification, the Brahmana is the lord of all castes.

**4** Brahmana, Kshatriya, and Vaishya, these three castes are called twice-born. The fourth, the Sudra, has one birth only; there is no fifth caste.

**5** In all castes, those children only which are begotten in the direct order on wedded wives, equal in caste and married as virgins, are to be considered as belonging to the same caste as their fathers.

**6** From a Brahmana a Kshatriya woman bears a son called Murdhavasikta; from a Brahmana a Vaishya woman bears a son called Ambashtha; from a Brahmana a Sudra woman bears a son called Nishada, also called Parasava.

**7** From a Kshatriya a Vaishya woman bears a son called Mahishya; from a Kshatriya a Sudra woman bears a son called Ugra.

**8** From a Vaishya a Sudra woman is born a son called Karana.

**9** In the inverse order of the castes are born the following: from a Kshatriya father and a Brahmana mother a son called Suta; from a Vaishya father and Brahmana mother, Vaidehaka; from a Vaishya father and Kshatriya mother, Magadha.

**10** From a Sudra father and a Brahmana mother, Chandala — the lowest of men; from a Sudra and a Kshatriya, Kshattri; from a Sudra and a Vaishya, Ayogava.

**11** Let the three twice-born castes, devoting themselves to their prescribed duties, study the Veda; but among them the Brahmana alone shall teach it.

**12** Teaching, studying, sacrificing for himself, sacrificing for others, making gifts, and receiving them are the six acts prescribed for a Brahmana.

**13** But among the six acts ordained for him, three are his means of subsistence: sacrificing for others, teaching, and accepting gifts from pure men.

**14** Passing from the Brahmana to the Kshatriya, three acts incumbent on the former are forbidden, namely, teaching, sacrificing for others, and thirdly, the acceptance of gifts.

**15** The same are likewise forbidden to a Vaishya; that is a settled rule. For Manu, the lord of creatures, has not prescribed these to men of those two castes.

**16** To carry arms for striking and for throwing is prescribed for Kshatriyas as a means of subsistence; to trade, to rear cattle, and agriculture for Vaishyas; but their duties are liberality, the study of the Veda, and the performance of sacrifice.

**17** Among the several occupations, the most commendable are: teaching the Veda for a Brahmana, protecting the people for a Kshatriya, and trade for a Vaishya.

**18** But a Brahmana, unable to subsist by his peculiar occupations, may live according to the law applicable to Kshatriyas; for the latter is next to him in rank.

**19** If it be asked, how shall it be if he cannot maintain himself by either of these occupations? The answer is: he may adopt a Vaishya's mode of life, employing himself in agriculture and rearing cattle.

**20** A man of low caste who through covetousness lives by the occupations of a higher one, the king shall deprive of his property and banish.
