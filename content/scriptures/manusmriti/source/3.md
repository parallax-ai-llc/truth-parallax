---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 3"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The vow of studying the three Vedas under a teacher must be kept for thirty-six years, or for half that time, or for a quarter, or until the student has perfectly learnt them.

**2** A student who has studied in due order the three Vedas, or two, or even one only, without breaking the rules of studentship, shall enter the order of householders.

**3** He, being famous for the strict performance of his duties, having received his heritage from his father, shall, after marrying, dwell in his own house, keeping control of himself.

**4** Let him who is about to take a wife approach the daughter of a family of equal status, who is endowed with good qualities, who has not been married before, whose limbs are complete, and who is younger than himself.

**5** A twice-born man shall marry according to the rules of sacred law a wife of equal caste who is endowed with auspicious bodily marks.

**6** It is declared that a Sudra woman alone can be the wife of a Sudra, she and one of his own caste the wives of a Vaishya, those two and one of his own caste the wives of a Kshatriya, those three and one of his own caste the wives of a Brahmana.

**7** There are eight forms of marriage: the rite of Brahma, of the gods, of the Rishis, of the Prajapatis, of the Asuras, of the Gandharvas, of the Rakshasas, and the eighth, that of the Pisachas.

**8** The gift of a daughter, after decking her with costly garments and honouring her by presents of jewels, to a man learned in the Veda and of good conduct, whom the father himself invites, is called the Brahma rite.

**9** The gift of a daughter, whom the father has decked with ornaments, to a priest who duly officiates at a sacrifice, during the course of its performance, is called the Daiva rite.

**10** When the father gives away his daughter according to the rule, after receiving from the bridegroom a cow and a bull or two pairs, for the fulfilment of the sacred law, that is named the Arsha rite.

**11** The gift of a daughter to the bridegroom by the father after he has addressed the couple with the text: "May both of you perform together your duties" — this marriage is called Prajapatya.

**12** The voluntary union of a maiden and her lover must be known as the Gandharva rite, which springs from desire and has sexual intercourse for its purpose.

**13** The forcible abduction of a maiden from her home, while she cries out and weeps, after her kinsmen have been slain or wounded and their houses broken open, is called the Rakshasa rite.

**14** When the lover secretly embraces the maiden, either sleeping, or flushed with drink, or disordered in intellect, that is the eighth, the most base of marriages, called the Paisacha.

**15** For a Brahmana the first four are approved; for a Kshatriya the Rakshasa form is also allowed; and for a Vaishya and a Sudra, all but the Paisacha.

**16** The mutual duties of the husband and wife are declared to be the foundation of all Dharma: the husband shall honour his wife, and the wife shall honour her husband.

**17** Where women are honoured, there the gods are pleased; but where they are not honoured, no sacred rite yields rewards.

**18** Where the female relations live in grief, the family soon wholly perishes; but that family where they are not unhappy ever prospers.

**19** The houses, on which female relations, not being duly honoured, pronounce a curse, perish completely, as if destroyed by magic.

**20** Hence men who seek their own welfare should always honour women on holidays and festivals with gifts of ornaments, clothes, and food.
