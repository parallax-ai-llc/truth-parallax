---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 2"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Know that the sacred law, which is set forth in each Veda, is the real principle of Dharma; and hence a wise man should always act according to it.

**2** The whole Veda is the first source of the sacred law, next the tradition and the virtuous conduct of those who know the Veda further, also the customs of holy men, and finally self-satisfaction.

**3** Whatever law has been ordained for any person by Manu, that has been fully declared in the Veda: for that sage was omniscient.

**4** But a learned man after fully scrutinizing all this with the eye of knowledge, should, in accordance with the authority of the revealed texts, be intent on the performance of his duties.

**5** For the human being who fulfils the law prescribed in the revealed texts and in the sacred tradition, gains fame in this world and after death unsurpassable bliss.

**6** By Shruti is meant the Veda, and by Smriti the institutes of the sacred law: those two must not be called into question in any matter, since from those two the sacred law shone forth.

**7** Every twice-born man who, relying on the institutes of dialectics, treats with contempt those two sources of the law, must be cast out by the virtuous, as an atheist and a scorner of the Veda.

**8** The Veda, the sacred tradition, the customs of virtuous men, and one's own pleasure — they declare to be visibly the fourfold means of defining the sacred law.

**9** The knowledge of the sacred law is prescribed for those who are not given to the acquisition of wealth and to the gratification of their desires; to those who seek the knowledge of the sacred law, the supreme authority is the revelation of the Veda.

**10** But when two sacred texts are conflicting, both are held to be law; for both are pronounced by the wise to be valid law.

**11** The Veda has declared that the sacred law is settled both by the prescriptive and the prohibitory texts.

**12** He who has settled his mind on the sacred law, as declared by the Veda and by the institutes of Manu, is to be known as the wise man who acts righteously.

**13** Thus the sacred and auspicious rules of conduct have been declared for the twice-born; hear now the rules of conduct for kings which are productive of bliss.

**14** The initiation of a Brahmana shall be in the eighth year from conception, of a Kshatriya in the eleventh, and of a Vaishya in the twelfth.

**15** The initiation of a Brahmana who desires to attain the lustre of sacred knowledge may take place in the fifth year, that of a Kshatriya who wishes to gain power in the sixth, and that of a Vaishya who wishes for success in business in the eighth.

**16** Until the sixteenth year the time for the initiation of a Brahmana has not passed, until the twenty-second that of a Kshatriya, and until the twenty-fourth that of a Vaishya.

**17** After those periods men of these three castes who have not received the sacrament at the proper time become Vratyas, degraded from the Savitri; let no one initiate such men, nor teach them, nor let any one form a matrimonial alliance with them.

**18** Let students, according to the order of their castes, wear garments of different materials: the Brahmana of hemp or flax, the Kshatriya of cotton, and the Vaishya of wool.

**19** The girdle of a Brahmana shall be made of Munja grass, in three strands; that of a Kshatriya a bowstring of Murva fibres; that of a Vaishya a hempen thread.

**20** The staff of a Brahmana shall be of Palasha wood, that of a Kshatriya of Udumbara wood, and that of a Vaishya of Pilu or Bilva wood.
