---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 11"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Him who wishes to perform a penance, let him first restrain his organs, and, having increased his sacred learning, let him perform the penance.

**2** A twice-born man who wishes to attain the highest good, shall perform penances in accordance with the rules prescribed for them.

**3** Performing austerities is a means of purification for a Brahmana; for a Kshatriya, the protection of the people; for a Vaishya, wealth through trade; and for a Sudra, service.

**4** The great sages, through the power of austerities, acquired knowledge; through knowledge they obtained the sacred law; through the sacred law they produced the purifications.

**5** A Brahmana who desires to attain the highest state shall study and teach the Veda, shall perform sacrifices, and shall give and receive gifts.

**6** Among all these meritorious acts, the study of the Veda is the highest; for that is declared to be the foundation of the sacred law and the means of attaining final liberation.

**7** A Brahmana who has fallen from his duties and has performed no austerities, who is addicted to vices, and whose sacred fires have gone out, shall perform these penances.

**8** If a twice-born man, without being in distress, performs his duties according to the law, merely for the sake of a reward in the next world, he obtains after death the fulfillment of all his wishes.

**9** Five great sins are: killing a Brahmana, drinking Sura, stealing the gold of a Brahmana, defiling the bed of the guru, and associating with those who have committed such acts.

**10** For killing a Brahmana, let him make a hut in the forest and dwell in it during twelve years, subsisting on alms and making the skull of a dead man his flag, in order to obtain purification.

**11** Or let him voluntarily become, in a battle, the target of archers who know his purpose; or he may cast himself headfirst into a blazing fire three times.

**12** Or, to purify himself, let him walk one hundred leagues, reciting one of the Vedas, eating little, and controlling his organs.

**13** Or let a Brahmana who has committed any of these five great sins, feed a Brahmana and walk after the cow, going by the road it takes.

**14** Having committed a minor offence by slaying a cow, the offender shall drink for three months a decoction of barley-grains; having had his head shaved and covering himself with the hide of the slain cow, he shall stay in a cow-pen.

**15** During two months he shall eat a small quantity of food, without any seasoning, once a day, bathing at the time of the three savanas, and three times a day he shall attend upon the cows.

**16** When the cows rise, he shall also rise; and when they stand, he shall also stand; when they rest, he shall rest beside them, attending upon them constantly.

**17** If they be stolen, or lost, or injured by snakes or wild beasts, or fall into pits or rivers, or are killed by lightning — he is freed from his guilt by fasting for three days.

**18** For stealing grain, a man shall subsist on a measured quantity of rice for three years. For stealing honey or oil or butter, he shall subsist on alms for twelve months.

**19** He who has committed a minor offence shall confess it; for, being confessed, it becomes lighter.

**20** The penance for all offences, both great and small, is correctly performed when a man controls his mind, speech, and body, and always speaks the truth.
