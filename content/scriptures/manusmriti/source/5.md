---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 5"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The sages, having heard the duties of a Snataka, thus spoke to the great-souled Bhrigu, who sprang from fire: "How can death gain power over Brahmanas who know the sacred science?"

**2** The righteous Bhrigu, the son of Manu, thus addressed the great sages: "Hear, I shall declare the rules of purification to the best of my understanding."

**3** He may eat the flesh of animals killed by hawks, by other birds of prey, by dogs, or by anyone skilled in hunting, whether those animals are five-toed or not.

**4** Garlic, leeks, onions, mushrooms, and all plants springing from impure substances are not fit to be eaten by the twice-born.

**5** The red sap of trees, sap flowing from an incision, the Shelu fruit, and the milk of a cow within ten days after calving must be avoided.

**6** Rice boiled with sesame, wheat mixed with butter, milk and sugar — these may be eaten, though they have stood for a long time, if they are not sour.

**7** The food of the twice-born, which is mingled with hair, insects, or filthy substances, must be purified by strewing earth and water upon it.

**8** As long as the smell and the moisture of an impure substance cling to it, so long must earth and water be applied in purifying all things.

**9** Three ablutions suffice for purification of oily things, of things through which liquid has seeped, and of things smeared with impure substances.

**10** Offerings of food to the gods, and of water to the departed, and the hospitality shown to guests — all these depend upon the wives; therefore the husband should cherish his wife.

**11** The husband receives his wife from the gods, not according to his own will; doing what is agreeable to the gods, he must always support her while she is faithful.

**12** No sacrifice, no vow, no fast must be performed by women apart from their husbands; if a wife obeys her husband, she will for that reason alone be exalted in heaven.

**13** A faithful wife, who desires to dwell after death with her husband, must never do anything that might displease him who took her hand, whether he be alive or dead.

**14** She must always be cheerful, clever in household affairs, careful in cleaning her utensils, and economical in expenditure.

**15** Him to whom her father may give her, or her brother with the father's permission, she shall obey as long as he lives, and when he is dead, she must not insult his memory.

**16** The husband who wedded her with sacred texts is always a source of happiness to his wife, both in season and out of season, in this world and in the next.

**17** Though destitute of virtue, or seeking pleasure elsewhere, or devoid of good qualities, yet a husband must be constantly worshipped as a god by a faithful wife.

**18** No sacrifice and no penance and no fasting is allowed to women separately from their husbands; only by obeying the husband does a woman gain exaltation in heaven.

**19** A faithful wife who wishes to attain the abode of her husband, must never do anything disagreeable to him, be he living or dead.

**20** Let her emaciate her body by living on pure flowers, roots, and fruit; but let her not, when her lord has died, even mention the name of another man.
