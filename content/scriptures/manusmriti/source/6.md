---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 6"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Having thus passed the first quarter of his life in the teacher's house, let the twice-born man live in his house during the second quarter of his existence, after marrying a wife.

**2** When a householder sees his skin wrinkled, and his hair white, and the sons of his sons, then he may resort to the forest.

**3** Abandoning all food raised by cultivation, and all his belongings, he may depart into the forest, either committing his wife to his sons, or accompanied by her.

**4** Taking with him the sacred fire and the implements required for domestic sacrifices, he shall go forth from the village into the forest and reside there, duly controlling his senses.

**5** With many kinds of pure food fit for ascetics, or with herbs, roots, and fruit, let him perform the five great sacrifices, adding to each more and more austerity.

**6** Let him wear a skin or a tattered garment; let him bathe morning and evening; let him always wear his hair in braids, and let the hair of his body, his beard, and his nails remain unclipped.

**7** Let him perform the Bali offering with such food as he eats, and give alms according to his ability; let him honour those who come to his hermitage with gifts of water, roots, and fruit.

**8** Let him be always industrious in privately reciting the Veda; let him be patient of hardships, friendly toward all, of collected mind, ever liberal, and never a receiver of gifts, and compassionate toward all living creatures.

**9** Let him offer, according to the law, the Agnihotra with three sacred fires, never omitting the new-moon and full-moon sacrifices at the proper time.

**10** Making no effort to procure things pleasant, nor to avoid things unpleasant, let him bear with all that comes, abstaining from worldly engagements.

**11** Let him not desire to die, let him not desire to live; let him wait for his appointed time, as a servant waits for the payment of his wages.

**12** Departing from his house fully provided with the means of purification, let him wander about absolutely silent, and caring nothing for enjoyments that may be offered.

**13** Let him always wander alone, without any companion, in order to attain final liberation, fully understanding that the solitary man, who neither forsakes nor is forsaken, gains his end.

**14** He shall neither possess a fire, nor a dwelling; he may go to a village for his food; he shall be indifferent to everything, firm of purpose, meditating and concentrating his mind on Brahman.

**15** A potsherd instead of an alms-bowl, the roots of trees for a dwelling, coarse worn-out garments, life in solitude, and indifference toward everything — are the marks of one who has attained liberation.

**16** Let him not desire death, let him not desire life; let him wait for the time appointed, as a servant awaits his wages.

**17** Let him step on his way, keeping his eyes on the ground; let him drink water purified by straining with a cloth; let him utter speech purified by truth; let him keep his heart purified by knowledge.

**18** Let him patiently bear hard words, let him not insult anybody, and let him not become anybody's enemy for the sake of this body.

**19** Against an angry man let him not in return show anger; let him bless when he is cursed; and let him not utter speech which is deceitful, or which concerns another's faults.

**20** Delighting in what refers to the Atman, sitting in the postures prescribed by the Yoga, independent of external help, entirely abstaining from sensual enjoyments, with himself for his only companion, he shall live in this world, desiring the bliss of final liberation.
