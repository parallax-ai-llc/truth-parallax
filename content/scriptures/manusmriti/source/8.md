---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 8"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 8
---

**1** A king, desirous of investigating law cases, must enter his court of justice, preserving a dignified demeanor, together with Brahmanas and with experienced councillors.

**2** There, either seated or standing, raising his right arm, without ostentation in his dress and ornaments, let him examine the causes of suitors.

**3** Let him, one by one, decide the causes brought before him according to the law, making use of the principles drawn from local usage and from the institutes of the sacred law.

**4** The causes of suits are eighteen: non-payment of debts is the first, deposit and pledge, sale without ownership, concerns among partners, and resumption of gifts.

**5** Non-payment of wages, non-performance of agreements, rescission of sale and purchase, disputes between the owner of cattle and his servants.

**6** Disputes regarding boundaries, assault, defamation, theft, robbery and violence, adultery.

**7** Duties of man and wife, partition of inheritance, gambling and betting — these are in this world the eighteen topics which give rise to lawsuits.

**8** Among litigants the king must ascertain the truth by means of witnesses and the like; looking at the nature of the case, at the country, at the time, and at the customs.

**9** What is consistent with the word and character of the plaintiff, what is confirmed by the evidence of others, and what is not denied by the defendant, the king must consider as true.

**10** In cases where no witnesses are available, the judge must decide, after careful examination of all circumstances, the matter according to equity.

**11** Evidence is declared to be of two kinds: human and divine. Human evidence consists of documents, possession, and witnesses. Divine evidence consists of the ordeal.

**12** A creditor shall receive his debt with interest from a debtor. The interest on money lent shall be, at the most, one and one-quarter percent per month.

**13** A witness who speaks truth in his evidence gains, after death, the most excellent regions; in this world he obtains unsurpassable fame: such testimony is revered by Brahma himself.

**14** He who speaks falsehood as a witness is firmly bound by Varuna's fetters, helpless during one hundred existences; therefore let one speak truth as a witness.

**15** By truthfulness a witness is purified, through truthfulness his merit grows, truth must, therefore, be spoken by all witnesses.

**16** Let the king punish all those who give false evidence, or who mislead the court; and let him banish them.

**17** A just king must punish those men who give false evidence with the punishment of a thief.

**18** The theft of men of different castes shall have different punishments: the lighter for the higher castes and the heavier for the lower.

**19** Let the king, having fully ascertained the motive, the time and place, and having duly considered the ability of the criminal and the nature of the crime, justly inflict punishment on the evil-doers.

**20** Unjust punishment destroys reputation among men, and fame after death, and causes even in the next world the loss of heaven; let him, therefore, be careful in awarding punishment.
