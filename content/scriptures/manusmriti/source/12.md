---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 12"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 12
---

**1** The great sages thus addressed Bhrigu, the son of Manu: "O Lord, kindly declare to us the fruits of action in their various forms."

**2** Bhrigu, the wise son of Manu, spoke to the great sages: "Hear the decision regarding the fruit of actions."

**3** Action, which springs from the mind, from speech, and from the body, produces either good or evil results; by action are caused the conditions of men — the highest, the middling, and the lowest.

**4** Know that the mind is the instigator here below, even to that action which is connected with the body, and which is of three kinds, has three seats, and falls under ten heads.

**5** Coveting the property of others, thinking in one's heart of what is undesirable, and adherence to false doctrines are the three kinds of sinful mental action.

**6** Abusing others, speaking untruth, detracting from the merits of all men, and talking idly shall be the four kinds of evil verbal action.

**7** Taking what has not been given, injuring creatures without the sanction of the law, and holding criminal intercourse with another man's wife are declared to be the three kinds of wicked bodily action.

**8** A man obtains the result of a good or evil mental act in his mind, that of a verbal act in his speech, that of a bodily act in his body.

**9** In consequence of many sinful acts committed with his body, a man becomes in the next birth something inanimate; in consequence of sins committed by speech, a bird or a beast; and in consequence of mental sins, he is reborn in a low caste.

**10** That man is called a Tridandin who has placed the three-fold control on his speech, on his thoughts, and on his body. He who exercises this three-fold control with respect to all created beings and wholly subdues desire and wrath, thereby assuredly obtains salvation.

**11** Him who impels this body to action, they call the Kshetrajna, the knower of the field; him who does the acts, the wise call the Bhutatman, the elemental self.

**12** Another internal self, called Jiva, is born together with all living creatures, and it is through that Jiva that the knower of the field perceives pleasure and pain in all existences.

**13** Those two, the great soul and the individual soul, being closely united with the five elements, dwell in the supreme soul, which pervades all beings, high and low.

**14** From his body a multitude of forms are produced without cessation, which give life to the animate creatures.

**15** Thus after death, those sinful men, who have committed crimes, must go through dreadful hells, and those who acted righteously gain entry to heaven.

**16** One must know the soul to be a thousand times subtler than a hair's tip, and the divine one to pervade all things.

**17** Through the fault of not understanding the soul, and through the fault of carelessness, the deluded soul obtains birth as a beast, as an insect, and in the various hells.

**18** Through the ripening of the fruit of each act committed in various existences, beings obtain in this world different conditions in each successive existence.

**19** By the study of the Veda, by austerities, by the acquisition of knowledge, by self-control, by the performance of sacrificial rites, and by meritorious acts, this body is made fit for Brahman.

**20** The Atman alone is the god of all beings. All worlds rest in the Atman. The Atman produces the actions of beings united with their bodies. He who knows this reaches Brahman, the highest goal. Thus is the whole of Manu's law declared.
