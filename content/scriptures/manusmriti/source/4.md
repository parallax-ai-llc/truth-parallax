---
title: "Manusmriti - Chapter 4"
scripture: "manusmriti"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Having dwelt with a teacher during the fourth part of a man's life, a Brahmana shall live during the second quarter of his existence in his house, after he has wedded a wife.

**2** A Brahmana must seek a means of subsistence which either causes no, or at least little, pain to others, and live by that — except in times of distress.

**3** For the purpose of gaining bare subsistence, let him accumulate property by following those irreproachable occupations which are prescribed for his caste, without unduly fatiguing his body.

**4** He should never, for the sake of subsistence, follow the ways of the world; let him live the pure, straightforward, honest life of a Brahmana.

**5** He who desires happiness must strive after a perfectly contented disposition and control himself; for happiness has contentment for its root, the root of unhappiness is the contrary disposition.

**6** Let him never, even in times of distress, do anything offensive or sinful; let him not draw near to anything forbidden, for he who adheres to the path of righteousness does not come to ruin.

**7** Let him say what is true, let him say what is pleasing, let him utter no disagreeable truth, and let him utter no agreeable falsehood; that is the eternal law.

**8** Let him say "well and good" or let him say "well" only; let him not engage in useless enmity or dispute with anyone.

**9** Let him not journey too early in the morning, nor too late in the evening, nor near the middle of the day, nor with an unknown companion, nor alone, nor with men of low caste.

**10** Let him not insult those who are deficient in limbs or in learning, or in age, or in beauty, or in wealth, or in family, or in conduct.

**11** A Brahmana who is impure must not touch with his hand a cow, a Brahmana, or fire; nor, being in good health, let him look at the luminaries in the sky, while he is impure.

**12** Let him, though in a hurry, not run meanly after a departing guest; let him never look at the sun when it rises or sets.

**13** Let him not point at a rainbow and say, "that is a rainbow"; nor at the stars, nor at their reflections in water, lest his life be shortened.

**14** He shall not dwell in a country where the rulers are Sudras, nor in one which is surrounded by unrighteous people, nor in one which has become subject to heretics, nor in one swarming with men of the lowest castes.

**15** Let him not eat food which has been offered by a drunken man, or by a madman, or by an unclean person.

**16** Let him never eat food given unwillingly, or food which is stale, or impure.

**17** Let him not honour, even by a greeting, heretics, men who follow forbidden occupations, men who live like cats, rogues, logicians, and those who make their living by public disputations.

**18** Let him not form an acquaintance with outcasts, nor with Chandalas, nor with the lowest of men, nor with Pukkasas, nor with fools, nor with overbearing men, nor with men of the lowest castes.

**19** Single is each being born; single it dies; single it enjoys the reward of its virtue; single it suffers the punishment of its sin.

**20** Leaving the dead body on the ground like a log of wood, or a clod of earth, the relatives depart with averted faces; but virtue accompanies the soul.
