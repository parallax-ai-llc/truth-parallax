Academic analysis of this chapter on the doctrine of karma, the process of transmigration, the nature of the gunas, and the path to liberation examines its historical context, literary sources, and social implications. Scholars study the chapter's relationship to other dharmashastra literature, identifying both common traditions and distinctive innovations. Patrick Olivelle's critical edition has clarified numerous textual difficulties and identified likely interpolations.

The chapter's prescriptions are analyzed not as timeless spiritual truths but as products of a specific historical and social context — Brahmanical North India during the early centuries of the Common Era. Scholars examine how the chapter's rules reflect, legitimate, and sometimes challenge existing social arrangements. The gap between textual prescription and actual social practice is a recurring theme in academic analysis, as is the chapter's reception and interpretation in subsequent Indian legal and social history.
