---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 2"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The second way is called Nangshen, the Way of the Shen of the Visible World. This is the path of purification, exorcism, and the subduing of demons.

**2** The Nangshen practitioner knows the names and natures of the three hundred and sixty classes of spirits that inhabit the world — the gods above, the nagas below, and the demons of the middle realm.

**3** When a person falls ill through the influence of malevolent spirits, the Nangshen performs rituals of ransom and substitution.

**4** He creates effigy figures called "to" — models of the afflicted person — and offers them to the demons in exchange for the patient's life.

**5** He performs the rite of "sel" — the purification ceremony in which smoke offerings of juniper, rhododendron, and other aromatic plants are burned to cleanse the environment of negative energies.

**6** The Nangshen also performs the rite of "lu" — offerings to the water spirits and earth spirits that dwell in springs, lakes, rivers, and underground passages.

**7** When the nagas are offended — by pollution of their waters, by digging in their earth, or by other disturbances — they send illness and misfortune. The Nangshen appeases them with offerings of milk, grain, and precious substances.

**8** The ritual drum, the bell, the mirror, and the ritual dagger are the implements of the Nangshen. With these he invokes the protecting deities and drives away the forces of harm.

**9** The Nangshen also performs the rite of "dud" — the summoning and binding of demons. Through the power of mantra and visualization, he captures the malevolent spirits and binds them with sacred cords.

**10** Thus the second way protects the community from the invisible forces of harm and maintains the balance between the human world and the spirit world.
