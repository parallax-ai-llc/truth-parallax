---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 7"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The seventh way is called A-kar, the Way of Pure Sound. This is the path of mantra, tantra, and the practices of the higher vehicles.

**2** The practitioner of the seventh way receives initiation from a qualified master and enters into the practice of the deity yoga — the visualization of oneself as a divine being.

**3** Through the recitation of mantras, the performance of mudras, and the visualization of sacred forms, the practitioner transforms ordinary perception into sacred perception.

**4** The body is seen as the body of the deity, speech is heard as mantra, and mind is recognized as the wisdom mind of the enlightened one.

**5** The central practice is the generation stage — "kyerim" — in which the practitioner visualizes the mandala of the deity, invokes the wisdom beings, and merges with them.

**6** This is followed by the completion stage — "dzogrim" — in which the visualization is dissolved and the mind rests in its natural state of clarity and emptiness.

**7** The deity practiced may be peaceful or wrathful, male or female, single or in union. Each deity represents a particular aspect of wisdom and compassion.

**8** The mandala offering — the visualization of the entire universe offered to the enlightened beings — is a central practice for the accumulation of merit.

**9** Through these practices, the practitioner purifies the three doors — body, speech, and mind — and transforms them into the three kayas of the enlightened state.

**10** The seventh way is called the Way of Pure Sound because the mantra — the sacred syllable — is the vehicle by which ordinary sound is transformed into the sound of truth.
