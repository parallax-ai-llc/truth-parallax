---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 4"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The fourth way is called Sichen, the Way of the Shen of Existence. This is the path concerned with the world of the dead and the rites of the afterlife.

**2** When a person dies, the consciousness departs from the body and enters the bardo — the intermediate state between death and rebirth.

**3** The Sichen practitioner guides the consciousness of the dead through the bardo, directing it toward a favorable rebirth or toward liberation.

**4** The ritual of "dur" — the funeral rite — is the central practice of the Sichen. It includes the preparation of the body, the chanting of sacred texts, and the performance of offerings.

**5** The practitioner creates a "namkha" — a thread cross of colored threads — which serves as a trap for negative energies and a support for the consciousness of the deceased.

**6** Offerings of food, drink, clothing, and precious substances are made to the dead, to ease their passage through the bardo and to provide them with merit for their journey.

**7** The Sichen also performs the rite of "pho-wa" — the transference of consciousness. At the moment of death, through the power of mantra and visualization, the consciousness is directed upward through the crown of the head toward the pure lands.

**8** Animal sacrifice was practiced in the ancient Bon tradition as a means of providing a mount for the dead to ride into the next world. But Tonpa Shenrab reformed this practice, substituting symbolic offerings for living beings.

**9** The Sichen knows the geography of the afterlife — the paths that lead to the various realms of rebirth, the judges of the dead, and the bridges that must be crossed.

**10** Thus the fourth way guides beings through the most fearful transition — from life to death — with wisdom and compassion, ensuring that the journey continues toward the light.
