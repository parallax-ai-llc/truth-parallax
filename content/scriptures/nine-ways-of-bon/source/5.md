---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 5"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The fifth way is called Genyen, the Way of the Virtuous Lay Practitioners. This is the path of ethical conduct for those who live in the world.

**2** The Genyen takes vows to refrain from the ten non-virtuous actions: three of body — killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct; four of speech — lying, divisive speech, harsh speech, and idle gossip; and three of mind — covetousness, malice, and wrong views.

**3** In place of these, the Genyen cultivates the ten virtues: protecting life, practicing generosity, maintaining proper conduct, speaking truth, promoting harmony, speaking gently, speaking meaningfully, cultivating contentment, practicing loving-kindness, and holding right views.

**4** The Genyen also observes special vows on auspicious days — the eighth, fifteenth, and thirtieth of each lunar month — when additional practices of fasting, prayer, and meditation are undertaken.

**5** The practice of generosity is central to this way. The Genyen gives food to the hungry, clothing to the cold, shelter to the homeless, and medicine to the sick.

**6** The merit generated by virtuous action is dedicated to all sentient beings, that they may be freed from suffering and attain the state of liberation.

**7** The Genyen also practices the recitation of mantras and the circumambulation of sacred sites, generating merit and purifying the mind.

**8** Compassion for all living beings is the foundation of this way. The Genyen regards all beings as having been, in some past life, his own mother, and thus he treats all with kindness and respect.

**9** Through the accumulation of merit and the purification of negative karma, the Genyen prepares the ground for the higher practices of the later ways.

**10** Thus the fifth way establishes the moral foundation upon which the spiritual life is built, making it possible for ordinary people to walk the path of liberation while living in the world.
