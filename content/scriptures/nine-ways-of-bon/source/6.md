---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 6"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The sixth way is called Drangsong, the Way of the Ascetic Sages. This is the path of monastic discipline and renunciation.

**2** The Drangsong renounces the worldly life — family, possessions, and pleasures — and takes up the robes and begging bowl of the mendicant.

**3** He dwells in remote places — mountains, forests, and caves — practicing meditation and austerity, seeking the direct realization of the nature of mind.

**4** The monastic vows are two hundred and fifty in number, governing every aspect of the monk's life: eating, sleeping, walking, speaking, and relating to others.

**5** The monk eats one meal a day before noon, sleeps little, speaks only what is necessary, and avoids all forms of entertainment and distraction.

**6** He practices the four foundations of mindfulness: mindfulness of the body, mindfulness of feelings, mindfulness of mind, and mindfulness of phenomena.

**7** Through these practices, the Drangsong gradually purifies the mind of all defilements — desire, hatred, and ignorance — and develops the qualities of wisdom and compassion.

**8** The ascetic also practices the "tummo" — the inner heat meditation — by which the body's subtle energies are awakened and directed upward through the central channel.

**9** Through this practice, the monk gains mastery over the physical body and is able to withstand extreme cold, sitting in snow-covered mountains wearing only a thin cotton cloth.

**10** The goal of the Drangsong is the complete liberation from the cycle of birth and death, the attainment of the state of the Awakened One. Thus the sixth way leads from the world of appearances to the threshold of ultimate reality.
