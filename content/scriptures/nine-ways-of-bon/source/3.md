---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 3"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The third way is called Trulshen, the Way of the Shen of Magical Power. This is the path of the wonder-worker, who commands the forces of nature.

**2** The Trulshen practitioner has mastered the art of controlling the elements — earth, water, fire, air, and space. He can summon rain, calm storms, and direct the course of rivers.

**3** Through the power of mantra, mudra, and concentration, the Trulshen can walk on water, fly through the air, and make himself invisible.

**4** He practices the rite of "phur" — the destruction of enemies through the ritual dagger. But this power is used only against the enemies of the Dharma, not for personal gain.

**5** The Trulshen also practices the art of "jinsek" — the fire ceremony in which offerings are burned to invoke the protection of the wrathful deities.

**6** Through these ceremonies, obstacles are removed, enemies are pacified, wealth is increased, and the power of the practitioner grows.

**7** The Trulshen can also perform the rite of "cha" — the calling back of the soul. When a person's life-force has been stolen by demons or has wandered away through shock or fright, the Trulshen calls it back with drums, chanting, and the waving of the ritual arrow.

**8** He can also perform the "yang-gug" — the calling of fortune and prosperity. Through this rite, the wealth and good luck of the household are restored.

**9** The powers of the Trulshen are great, but they must be used with compassion and wisdom. Power without compassion leads to the path of the demons.

**10** Thus the third way demonstrates the mastery of the visible forces through the invisible powers of the mind and spirit.
