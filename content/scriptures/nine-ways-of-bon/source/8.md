---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 8"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The eighth way is called Yeshen, the Way of the Primordial Shen. This is the path of the highest tantric practices, concerned with the union of wisdom and method.

**2** The practitioner of the eighth way works directly with the subtle body — the channels, the winds, and the drops — that form the invisible architecture of the human being.

**3** There are three main channels: the central channel, called the "uma," and two side channels, the right and the left. Through these channels flow the subtle winds that carry consciousness.

**4** By directing these winds into the central channel through the practice of breath control, visualization, and physical yogas, the practitioner awakens the innate wisdom that lies dormant at the heart center.

**5** The practice of "tsa-lung" — channel-wind yoga — is central to this way. Through specific postures, breathing patterns, and visualizations, the knots in the channels are untied and the winds flow freely.

**6** When the winds enter the central channel, extraordinary experiences arise: bliss, clarity, and non-conceptuality. These are not the goal but signs of progress on the path.

**7** The practitioner also works with the "tigle" — the essential drops of consciousness that reside at specific points in the body. By activating these drops, the practitioner gains access to states of consciousness beyond ordinary experience.

**8** The ultimate goal of this way is the attainment of the Rainbow Body — the dissolution of the physical body into pure light at the time of death, leaving behind only the hair and nails.

**9** This attainment has been demonstrated by many masters throughout the history of the Bon tradition, providing tangible proof that the practices of this way can lead to the complete transformation of matter into spirit.

**10** Thus the eighth way works with the most subtle levels of body and mind, transforming the practitioner from an ordinary being into an enlightened one within a single lifetime.
