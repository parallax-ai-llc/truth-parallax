---
title: "Nine Ways of Bon - Chapter 9"
scripture: "nine-ways-of-bon"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The ninth way is called Lame, the Supreme Way, the Way of Dzogchen — the Great Perfection. This is the pinnacle of all the ways, the direct path to the realization of the nature of mind.

**2** Dzogchen is the natural state of mind, which has been present from the very beginning, before any practice, before any effort, before any thought.

**3** The nature of mind is described as having three aspects: its essence is emptiness — it cannot be found anywhere; its nature is clarity — it is luminous and knowing; and its energy is unceasing — it manifests as the infinite display of appearances.

**4** These three aspects are not separate but are the single nature of mind itself, present in every moment of experience.

**5** The practice of Dzogchen begins with the "pointing out instruction" — the direct introduction to the nature of mind by a qualified master. In a single moment, the master reveals to the student what has always been present.

**6** Having received this introduction, the student practices "trekchod" — cutting through to the primordial purity. This is the practice of simply resting in the natural state, without altering, without fabricating, without meditating on anything.

**7** Whatever arises — thoughts, emotions, perceptions — is recognized as the natural display of the mind's energy and is allowed to self-liberate, like a snake uncoiling its own knots.

**8** The advanced practice is "togal" — the direct leap to spontaneous presence. Through gazing at the sun, the sky, or a lamp flame, the practitioner sees the spontaneous display of light that is the expression of the mind's innate clarity.

**9** Through sustained practice, the visions increase and the physical body is gradually transformed into light. At death, the accomplished practitioner dissolves entirely into the Rainbow Body.

**10** Tonpa Shenrab himself taught this supreme way as the crown of all his teachings. He said: "The nature of mind is like the sky — boundless, clear, and unchanging. All clouds that appear in it arise and dissolve without affecting its nature."

**11** "The one who recognizes this nature in this very moment, without searching elsewhere, has found the Buddha within himself."

**12** Thus the ninth way completes the journey that began with the first way. From the reading of signs in the outer world, the path leads inward, through purification, transformation, and direct realization, until the practitioner awakens to the primordial nature that was never absent.
