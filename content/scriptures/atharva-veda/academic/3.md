The marriage, love, and domestic hymns provide invaluable evidence for social history, gender relations, and family structure in Vedic India. Scholars note that the marriage hymns describe a relatively egalitarian ideal of conjugal partnership, with both bride and groom receiving blessings and taking mutual vows. The love charms and fertility hymns offer rare glimpses into the emotional and intimate dimensions of ancient Indian life.

Stephanie Jamison's work on women in the Vedic ritual system draws on these Atharvan domestic hymns to reconstruct the roles and status of women in Vedic society. While the solemn *shrauta* rituals were predominantly male domains, the *grihya* (domestic) ceremonies documented in the Atharva Veda reveal women as active participants in religious life, with specific roles in marriage, childbirth, and household worship.
