The Atharva Veda's healing hymns invite comparison with medical-magical traditions worldwide. The Egyptian Ebers Papyrus (c. 1550 BCE), the Akkadian diagnostic texts, the Greek Hippocratic corpus, and the Chinese *Huangdi Neijing* all share the Atharvan approach of combining empirical observation of disease with spiritual or cosmological explanatory frameworks. The transition from magico-religious healing to empirical medicine is a cross-cultural phenomenon documented in each of these traditions.

The figure of the Atharvan healer-priest compares with healing specialists in other traditions: the Mesopotamian *ashipu* (exorcist-healer), the Greek *iatromantis* (seer-physician), the Navajo hatali (medicine man), and the Siberian shaman. All these figures combine spiritual authority with medical knowledge, reflecting the ancient and cross-cultural understanding that physical and spiritual health are inseparable.
