The Atharva Veda's statecraft hymns compare with political theology traditions across cultures. The ancient Egyptian concept of *ma'at* (cosmic order) as the foundation of kingship, the Chinese Mandate of Heaven, the biblical anointing of kings, and the Confucian theory of governance by virtue all share the Atharvan principle that political authority is grounded in cosmic order and maintained through right action.

The Atharvan hymns' invocation of divine power for political purposes compares with state religion across civilizations. The Roman imperial cult, the Byzantine symphony of church and state, the Japanese emperor's divine status, and the Islamic caliphate all reflect varying models of the relationship between spiritual authority and political power — a relationship that the Atharva Veda addresses in its earliest known form.
