The Atharva Veda's philosophical hymns on cosmic origins compare with creation speculation across world traditions. The Skambha Sukta's inquiry into the cosmic support compares with the pre-Socratic search for the *arche* (first principle), the Daodejing's meditation on the Dao that precedes heaven and earth, and the Kabbalistic concept of the *Ein Sof* as the infinite ground of reality. All these traditions ask the same fundamental question: what is the ultimate foundation of existence?

The Kala Sukta's personification of Time compares with temporal cosmologies across cultures: the Greek Chronos, the Zoroastrian Zurvan, the Buddhist understanding of dependent origination in time, and the Aboriginal Australian Dreamtime. The Prana Sukta's identification of breath with the cosmic principle parallels the Chinese concept of *qi*, the Greek *pneuma*, the Hebrew *ruach*, and the Polynesian *mana* — all pointing to a cross-cultural intuition that the animating force of life is itself divine.
