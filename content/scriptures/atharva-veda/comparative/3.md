The Atharva Veda's marriage hymns compare with wedding liturgies across world religions. The seven steps around the sacred fire parallel the Jewish *sheva brachot* (seven blessings), the Christian exchange of vows, the Islamic *nikah* ceremony, and the Shinto *san-san-kudo* (three-three-nine). All these traditions ritualize the transition from individual to conjugal life through sacred formula, divine witness, and communal celebration.

The Atharvan love charms and fertility hymns participate in a cross-cultural tradition of ritual intervention in romantic and reproductive life. Greek love spells, medieval European love potions, Chinese matchmaking rituals, and African fertility ceremonies all share the Atharvan assumption that the sacred dimension of love and procreation can be accessed and influenced through ritual means.
