Shaiva reading of the royal power hymns connects political authority to Shiva's cosmic sovereignty as Maheshvara, the great lord. The Atharvan formulas for royal consecration invoke a divine power that Shaivas identify with Shiva's *aishvarya* (lordship) — the supreme authority that governs all realms of existence. The righteous king rules as Shiva's agent, maintaining *dharma* through the exercise of divinely sanctioned power.

The Sri Rudram's enumeration of Rudra-Shiva's presence in every social role — including king, warrior, and administrator — provides the theological basis for the Shaiva political theology implicit in these Atharvan hymns. Shiva is present in the ruler and the ruled, the protector and the protected, making all political relationships expressions of the divine play (*lila*) of consciousness.
