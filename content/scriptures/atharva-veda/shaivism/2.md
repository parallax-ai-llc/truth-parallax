Shaiva reading of the protection hymns connects them to Rudra's fierce protective aspect — the deity whose very name (*Rudra*, 'the howler') inspires terror in hostile forces. The Atharvan counter-sorcery mantras invoke the same terrifying divine power that Shaivas worship as Bhairava, the fierce form of Shiva who destroys evil and protects his devotees from all dangers.

The protective amulets (*mani*) and defensive rituals described in these hymns find continuity in Shaiva practice: the wearing of *rudraksha* beads, the application of sacred ash (*vibhuti*), and the recitation of Rudra mantras for protection are all living extensions of the Atharva Veda's protective tradition. The Shaiva understanding is that Shiva's *anugraha* (grace) is the ultimate shield against all hostile forces.
