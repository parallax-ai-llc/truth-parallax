The philosophical hymns of the Atharva Veda are read by Shaivas as intimations of the supreme consciousness that is Paramashiva. The Skambha Sukta's cosmic pillar is identified with the *Shiva Linga* — the axis of the universe, the pillar of consciousness around which all creation revolves. The hymn's exploration of what supports the cosmos finds its Shaiva answer in the self-standing (*svayambhu*) nature of Shiva-consciousness.

The Kala Sukta's meditation on Time resonates with Shiva's identity as Mahakala — the lord of time who transcends the temporal order while governing it. The Prana Sukta's identification of breath as the cosmic principle connects to Shaiva *pranayama* practice and the understanding that the breath (*prana*) is a vehicle of Shakti through which the practitioner can access the still point of Shiva-consciousness at the center of all movement.
