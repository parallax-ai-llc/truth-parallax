Shaiva interpretation reads the healing hymns of the Atharva Veda through Rudra-Shiva's identity as the lord of healing herbs and the divine physician (*Vaidyanatha*). The Rig Veda's Rudra hymns already establish that 'Rudra possesses a thousand remedies' (2.33.4), and the Atharva Veda's detailed healing incantations develop this aspect of the deity. Shaivas understand these hymns as invoking Shiva's compassionate aspect — the healer who cures not only physical disease but the spiritual illness of bondage.

The Atharva Veda's combination of herbal knowledge with mantric power resonates with the Shaiva tradition of *siddha* medicine, particularly the Tamil Siddha medical tradition that traces its origins to Shiva's teaching. The healing hymns' invocation of specific plants and their divine powers reflects the Shaiva understanding that the natural world is charged with Shiva's energy (*shakti*), and the knowledgeable practitioner can channel this energy for healing.
