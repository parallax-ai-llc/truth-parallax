Shaiva interpretation of the marriage and domestic hymns acknowledges the importance of *grihastha dharma* while viewing it as one stage in the soul's journey toward the ultimate renunciation (*sannyasa*) that characterizes the Shaiva ascetic ideal. Shiva himself is both the model householder — married to Parvati, father of Ganesha and Kartikeya — and the supreme renunciant who sits in eternal meditation on Mount Kailasa.

The Atharvan love and fertility hymns are read by Shaivas in light of the Shiva-Shakti dynamic: the creative union of consciousness (Shiva) and energy (Shakti) that generates and sustains all life. The domestic rites sanctify human love and reproduction as participations in this divine creative activity, giving cosmic significance to the most intimate dimensions of human experience.
