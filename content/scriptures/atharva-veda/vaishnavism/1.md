Vaishnavas interpret the healing hymns of the Atharva Veda as expressions of Vishnu's role as the supreme healer and preserver of life. The Lord who sustains the cosmos also sustains the individual body, and the Atharvan healing mantras channel his preserving energy through the medium of sacred sound. The divine physicians invoked in these hymns — the Ashvins, Dhanvantari — are understood as agents of Vishnu's healing grace.

The Vaishnava reading emphasizes that true healing comes from surrender to the Lord, who is the ultimate physician (*Vaidya*). The herbs and rituals prescribed in the healing hymns are effective because they are charged with divine power, and their efficacy depends ultimately on the Lord's will. This theological perspective connects the Atharva Veda's practical healing tradition to the broader Vaishnava framework of divine grace and providence.
