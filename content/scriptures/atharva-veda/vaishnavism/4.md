Vaishnavas interpret the royal power hymns through the theology of Vishnu's *Rama* and *Krishna* avataras, both of whom exercised dharmic kingship. The Atharvan political formulas are understood as encoding the principles of *rama-rajya* (Rama's ideal kingdom) — governance rooted in dharma, compassion, and the protection of all subjects. The king who follows these principles rules as Vishnu's representative on earth.

The royal consecration mantras are connected to the Vaishnava understanding that all legitimate authority derives from Narayana, the king of kings. The Atharvan statecraft hymns complement the Yajur Veda's royal rituals by providing formulas for the day-to-day exercise of dharmic governance, including the resolution of disputes, the management of resources, and the protection of the vulnerable.
