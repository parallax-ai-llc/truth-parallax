Vaishnavas read the philosophical hymns of the Atharva Veda as pointing to Vishnu-Narayana as the cosmic reality behind all philosophical categories. The Skambha (cosmic pillar) that supports heaven and earth is identified with Narayana, the foundation of all existence. The hymn's enumeration of cosmic principles — *tapas*, *rta*, *satya* — is understood as describing attributes of the Supreme Person rather than impersonal abstractions.

The Prana Sukta's identification of breath as the supreme principle is read within the Vaishnava framework that identifies Narayana as the indwelling breath of all beings — the *antaryamin* (inner controller) who sustains life from within. The philosophical hymns' quest for the one reality behind the many thus finds its Vaishnava answer in the personal God who is simultaneously transcendent and intimately present in every living being.
