Vaishnavas find in the marriage and domestic hymns a celebration of *grihastha dharma* (householder duty) as a path of devotion. The Vaishnava ideal of the sanctified household — where husband and wife serve the Lord together through daily worship, hospitality, and the raising of dharmic children — has its Vedic roots in these Atharvan ceremonies. Marriage is understood not merely as a social contract but as a spiritual partnership in the service of God.

The love hymns of the Atharva Veda are interpreted by Vaishnavas as reflecting the soul's innate longing for union with the divine beloved. The human experience of love, when properly directed, becomes a vehicle for understanding the soul's eternal relationship with Krishna. The domestic rites sanctify the household as a temple of the Lord, where every daily act becomes an offering.
