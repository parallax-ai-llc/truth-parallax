Vaishnavas read the protection hymns through the theology of Vishnu as the supreme protector — the deity who descends as avatara to defend dharma and destroy the forces of evil. The Atharvan protective mantras invoke a power that Vaishnavas identify with Narayana's protective grace (*raksha*), the same power that manifests as the Sudarshana Chakra (discus) that destroys all hostile forces.

The counter-sorcery hymns are understood within the Vaishnava framework as defense against the forces of *adharma* that oppose the Lord's cosmic order. The devotee who takes refuge in Narayana (*sharanagati*) receives the ultimate protection that no hostile power can overcome. The Atharvan protective formulas are thus preliminary expressions of the same divine protection that is fully realized through exclusive devotion to Vishnu.
