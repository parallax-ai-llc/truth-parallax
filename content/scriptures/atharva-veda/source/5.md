---
title: "Atharva Veda - Chapter 5"
scripture: "atharva-veda"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Brahman was born first of all, from the east the radiance spread. He revealed the depths of his own beauty, of this world the womb supreme, the lowest and the highest.

**2** That which is beyond the sky, beyond the earth, beyond the gods, beyond the Asuras — what was that first embryo the waters received, in which all the gods were seen together?

**3** He who is the source of all, the origin of all forms, the one upholder of this world — He is that into which this world will dissolve at the end.

**4** The wise declare that Being which exists unperceived, eternal, existing in all creatures, the universal soul, the goal of all — that verily is Brahman.

**5** He who knows Brahman, who is all knowledge, whose austerity consists of knowledge — from that Brahman are born breath, mind, all organs of sense, ether, air, fire, water, and earth, the support of all.

**6** Fire is His head, His eyes the moon and the sun, the quarters His ears, His speech the Vedas disclosed, the wind His breath, His heart the universe; from His feet came the earth. He is indeed the inner Self of all things.

**7** From Him comes Agni, the sun being the fuel; from the moon comes rain, from rain come herbs upon the earth; and man gives seed unto woman. Thus many creatures are begotten from the Purusha.

**8** From Him come the Rik verses, the Saman chants, the Yajus formulae, the rites of initiation, all sacrifices, ceremonies, and gifts; the year too, the sacrificer, and the worlds in which the moon shines and the sun.

**9** From Him were born the gods manifold, the celestial beings, men, cattle, horses, goats and sheep, rice and barley. Thus from this imperishable source all things proceed.

**10** All this is Brahman. This Self is Brahman. This Self has four quarters. The waking state is the first quarter, the dreaming state the second, the state of deep sleep the third, and Turiya, the fourth, is the state of pure consciousness — beyond all states, peaceful, blissful, the non-dual.

**11** That Turiya is to be known. It is the lord of all, the knower of all, the inner controller. It is the source of all, the beginning and end of beings.

**12** He who knows this, with his self meditates on the Self — to him Brahman gives liberation. He who knows this — verily he knows the Veda.
