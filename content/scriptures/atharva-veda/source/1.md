---
title: "Atharva Veda - Chapter 1"
scripture: "atharva-veda"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Ye three and thirty gods, whose breath is life, who guard the world with care, who are the lords of all that is — come hither and partake of these oblations.

**2** From time the waters were produced, from time the Brahman, holy fire; from time arose the sun, the lord; from time the moon and constellations.

**3** From time the many-formed arose, the lord of creatures manifold; the past and future both proceed from time, and likewise sacrifice.

**4** From time the earth and sky were born, the atmosphere, the wind, the rain; from time what is and what shall be — all this was born, all this was spread.

**5** Time drives like a horse with seven reins, a thousand-eyed, undecaying stallion. Sages and poets mount him; all beings are his chariot wheels.

**6** Time carries within himself seven wheels, seven naves has he; immortality is the axle. He is now the first of the gods. A full jar is placed before us; we see him who in many forms exists.

**7** Time encompasses all beings in the world. He who knows him as the father of the year, to him the gods and celestial beings offer worship.

**8** On time is established this universe; on time all this world rests. That which is called Brahman — in time the Brahman is established.

**9** By time the earth was created; by time the sun burns hot. In time are all beings; in time the eye looks abroad.

**10** In time the mind exists, in time the breath, in time the name is fixed. When time has come, all these creatures rejoice.

**11** In time there is holy fervour; in time is the highest treasure. In time is the great Brahman; time is the lord of all things.

**12** He urged forth this universe and he encompassed it. Being their father, he became their son. There is no other power higher than he.
