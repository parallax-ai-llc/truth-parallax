---
title: "Atharva Veda - Chapter 4"
scripture: "atharva-veda"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship? He who gives life, He who gives strength; He whose command all the bright gods revere; whose shadow is immortality, whose shadow is death.

**2** He who through His power is the one God of the breathing and awakening world. He who governs all, man and beast. Who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship?

**3** He whose greatness these snowy mountains, whose greatness the sea proclaims, with the distant river — His are these arms, these quarters of the sky.

**4** He through whom the sky is strong and the earth is steadfast, He through whom the heaven was established — nay, the highest heaven. He who measured out the light in the air — who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship?

**5** He to whom heaven and earth, standing firm by His will, look up, trembling inwardly — He over whom the rising sun shines forth. Who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship?

**6** When the great waters came, carrying the universal germ, producing the flame of life, then dwelt there in harmony the One Spirit of the gods. Who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship?

**7** He who by His might looked even over the waters which held power and generated the sacrifice — He who alone is God above all gods. Who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship?

**8** May He not destroy us — He the creator of the earth; or He the righteous, who created the heaven. He also created the bright and mighty waters. Who is the God to whom we shall offer our worship?

**9** Prajapati, Lord of generation! No other than thou encompasses all these created things. That for which our prayers rise, may that be ours; may we be lords of riches.

**10** Thou art the guardian of bodies, O Prajapati. Let not what is hostile come near us; let us be masters of unfailing vigour. Grant to us the medicine of the body.
