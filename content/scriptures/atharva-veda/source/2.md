---
title: "Atharva Veda - Chapter 2"
scripture: "atharva-veda"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The earth is vast and firm; the earth, upheld by Order, is the support of all that lives. Upon the earth, the ground, the soil, I offer my oblation.

**2** Whatever I dig from thee, O Earth, may that have quick growth again. O purifier, may we not injure thy vitals or thy heart.

**3** Thy summer, O Earth, thy rainy season, thy autumn, winter, early spring, and spring — thy seasons duly ordered, thy years made of days and nights — may they pour upon us their blessings.

**4** The earth which has many heights, and slopes, and the unconfined plain that binds men together — the earth that bears plants of various healing powers, may she spread wide for us and thrive.

**5** The earth on which the sea, and the rivers, and the waters, on which food and the tribes of men have arisen — on which this breathing, moving life exists — may she grant us the foremost draught of sustenance.

**6** The earth whose heart is in the highest heaven, encompassed by truth, immortal — may that earth bestow upon us splendour and strength in the loftiest kingdom.

**7** The earth on which the noisy mortals sing and dance, on which they fight, on which resounds the roaring drum — may she drive off our enemies, may the earth make us free from rivals.

**8** The food-bearing earth, the treasure-chamber, the sustainer — may she bestow on us wealth, giving us everything, a dwelling without fear.

**9** May the earth with people who speak various tongues, with various rites according to their dwellings — milk for me, as a great cow that never fails, a thousand streams of wealth.

**10** The serpent, the scorpion with thirsty fangs, and the winter-numb that lie hidden — the worm, each thing that stirs in the rains — may it, creeping, not creep on us. With what is gracious on the earth, may we associate.

**11** Her paths that extend afar, her lanes on every side, the ways on which men meet — on them may we walk with honey and ghee. Free from foes may the earth be to us.

**12** The earth that bears within her bosom all treasures, wealth, jewels, and gold — the giving goddess, bountiful, may she bestow wealth upon us.
