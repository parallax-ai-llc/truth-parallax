The Advaita reading of the healing hymns sees them as addressing the fundamental disease of *avidya* (ignorance) through the medium of physical healing metaphors. While the hymns operate at the practical level of curing bodily ailments, Advaitins interpret the deeper significance: all disease is ultimately rooted in the ignorance of one's true nature as Brahman, and the supreme healing is the knowledge that 'I am not this body; I am the eternal, immutable Self.'

The Mundaka Upanishad's distinction between *para vidya* (higher knowledge) and *apara vidya* (lower knowledge) places the healing hymns in the category of useful but ultimately limited knowledge. They serve the essential function of maintaining bodily health — a prerequisite for spiritual practice — but the Advaitin recognizes that the final cure for all suffering is the self-knowledge that transcends the bodily identification altogether.
