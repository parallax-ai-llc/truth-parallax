The Advaita reading of the marriage and domestic hymns recognizes the *grihastha* (householder) stage as a necessary phase in the fourfold *ashrama* system, one that prepares the individual for the eventual renunciation (*sannyasa*) that leads to liberation. The domestic rites purify and mature the individual through the discipline of dharmic responsibility, creating the conditions for the dispassion (*vairagya*) that arises naturally from fulfilled worldly life.

The Mundaka Upanishad (1.2.12) teaches that the wise householder, having experienced the limitations of action-based religion, seeks a teacher for the knowledge of Brahman. The Atharva Veda's domestic hymns thus serve the Advaitic framework by establishing the experiential ground from which the aspirant can recognize the insufficiency of worldly happiness and turn toward the permanent bliss of self-realization.
