The philosophical hymns of the Atharva Veda are regarded by Advaitins as the Samhita's closest approach to the non-dual truth that is fully revealed in the Upanishads. The Skambha Sukta's question about the cosmic pillar that supports everything is answered by the Advaitic identification of that pillar with Brahman — the one reality upon which the entire phenomenal universe is superimposed.

The Kala Sukta's meditation on Time as the ultimate creative power is reinterpreted through the Advaitic teaching that time itself is a superimposition (*adhyasa*) upon the timeless Brahman. The Prana Sukta's identification of breath as the supreme principle anticipates the Upanishadic teaching on *prana* as a manifestation of Brahman, while the overall philosophical trajectory of these hymns points toward the Mandukya Upanishad's revelation of *turiya* — the fourth state beyond waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.
