Advaitins interpret the protection and counter-sorcery hymns as addressing the phenomenal experience of threat and hostility that arises from the fundamental error of duality. In the non-dual vision, there is no 'other' to threaten the self, and no hostile force that can touch the *atman*. However, at the *vyavaharika* (conventional) level, protective practices have their place in maintaining the peace of mind necessary for spiritual inquiry.

The deeper Advaitic reading sees the 'enemies' and 'hostile sorcery' of these hymns as symbols of the internal enemies — desire, anger, greed, delusion, pride, and envy — that obstruct the path to self-knowledge. The mantras for 'destroying enemies' become, in this interpretation, tools for overcoming the mental afflictions (*kleshas*) that bind the aspirant to the cycle of suffering.
