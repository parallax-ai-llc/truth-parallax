Advaitins read the royal power hymns as describing the *atman*'s sovereignty over the inner kingdom of mind and senses. The true king is the self (*atman*) who rules the body-mind complex with the authority of self-knowledge. The Atharvan political formulas are reinterpreted as instructions for establishing the self's dominion over the forces of desire, fear, and delusion that usurp its rightful sovereignty.

The Brihadaranyaka Upanishad's teaching that 'the self is the king of everything' (2.5.15) provides the Advaitic framework for reading these political hymns. The cosmic sovereignty that the hymns seek to establish for the earthly king is, in its highest meaning, the absolute sovereignty of Brahman — the self-luminous awareness that is the ground and ruler of all experience.
