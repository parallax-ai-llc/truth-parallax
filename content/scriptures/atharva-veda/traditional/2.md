The protection and counter-sorcery hymns of the Atharva Veda provide mantras for defending the household, village, and individual against hostile forces — both human and supernatural. These include charms against witchcraft (*krityapratiharana*), incantations to repel enemies, and prayers for the destruction of hostile sorcery. Traditional understanding treats these not as offensive magic but as legitimate spiritual defense authorized by divine revelation.

The protective hymns include mantras for the consecration of amulets (*mani*), the preparation of protective herbs, and the ritual establishment of defensive boundaries around the home and property. The Brahman priest's knowledge of these hymns was essential to the welfare of the community, as he alone possessed the Vedic authority to counter hostile spiritual forces and restore harmony to disrupted households.
