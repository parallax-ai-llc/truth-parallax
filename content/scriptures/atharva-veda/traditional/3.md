The marriage, love, and domestic rites section of the Atharva Veda provides the mantras for the most intimate and joyful ceremonies of Vedic life. The marriage hymns (*vivaha sukta*) prescribe the formulas for every stage of the wedding ceremony: the giving of the bride, the seven steps around the sacred fire, the vows of fidelity and mutual support, and the prayers for progeny, harmony, and long life together.

Beyond marriage, this section includes hymns for conception, safe childbirth, the welfare of the newborn, the naming ceremony, and the ongoing harmony of the household. Traditional interpretation emphasizes that the domestic sphere is as much a domain of Vedic dharma as the solemn sacrifice, and the Atharva Veda's attention to these daily concerns reflects the comprehensive scope of divine revelation that addresses every dimension of human experience.
