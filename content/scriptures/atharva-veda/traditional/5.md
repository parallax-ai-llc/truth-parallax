The philosophical hymns of the Atharva Veda represent some of the most profound speculations in the entire Vedic corpus. The Skambha Sukta meditates on the cosmic pillar that supports heaven and earth, asking 'In which of his limbs does *tapas* stand? In which is *rta* established?' The Kala Sukta personifies Time as the supreme creative force, while the Prana Sukta identifies breath as the fundamental principle of life.

Traditional interpretation treats these philosophical hymns as the Atharva Veda's equivalent of the cosmogonic speculation found in Rig Veda 10. They represent the seers' contemplation of the ultimate principles underlying the practical content of the other sections — the unity that encompasses healing, protection, love, and power. The progression from practical hymns to philosophical speculation mirrors the Vedic journey from ritual action to liberating knowledge.
