The royal power and statecraft section contains mantras for the consecration of kings, the strengthening of royal authority, the defeat of enemies in battle, and the governance of the realm. The Atharva Veda's political hymns complement the Yajur Veda's Rajasuya and Ashvamedha by providing additional formulas for specific political situations: disputes over succession, threats from rival kings, and the maintenance of civil order.

Traditional interpretation understands these hymns as establishing the principle that legitimate political authority derives from divine order (*rta*) and is maintained through righteous governance (*dharma*). The king who follows the Atharvan political formulas rules not by brute force but by the cosmic power channeled through properly performed rituals and the blessing of the Brahman priest who serves as the kingdom's spiritual guardian.
