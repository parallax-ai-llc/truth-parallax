The healing hymns of the Atharva Veda represent the Vedic foundation of the Ayurvedic medical tradition. These mantras address specific ailments — fever (*takman*), jaundice (*harimana*), cough, worms, snakebite, and fractures — combining herbal prescriptions with ritual incantation. The traditional understanding holds that disease arises from both physical and spiritual causes, and therefore effective healing requires both material remedies and the recitation of divinely revealed mantras.

The healing hymns invoke specific divine physicians — the Ashvins, Rudra, and various herb deities — whose power is channeled through the priest's chanting. The Atharvan healer (*bhishaj*) occupies a distinct role in traditional society, combining the functions of physician, herbalist, and ritual specialist. These hymns are still recited in traditional Ayurvedic practice and in healing ceremonies performed in Hindu temples and homes.
