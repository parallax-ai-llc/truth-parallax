---
title: "Life of Adam and Eve - Chapter 8"
scripture: "life-of-adam-and-eve"
chapter: 8
---

**1** And after the burial of Adam, Eve, knowing that she too was about to die, gathered together all her sons and daughters, Seth with thirty brothers and thirty sisters, and Eve said to them all:

**2** "Hear me, my children, and I will tell you what the archangel Michael said to us when I and your father transgressed the command of God."

**3** "On account of your transgression, our Lord will bring upon your race the anger of His judgement, first by water, the second time by fire; by these two will the Lord judge the whole human race."

**4** "But hearken unto me, my children. Make ye then tablets of stone and other tablets of clay, and write on them all my life and your father's which ye have heard and seen from us. If by water the Lord judge our race, the tablets of clay will be dissolved and the tablets of stone will remain; but if by fire, the tablets of stone will be shattered and the tablets of clay will be baked."

**5** When Eve had said all this to her children, she spread out her hands to heaven in prayer, and bent her knees to the earth, and while she worshipped the Lord and gave Him thanks, she gave up her spirit. Thereafter all her children buried her with loud lamentation.

**6** And when they had been mourning four days, then Michael the archangel appeared and said to Seth: "Man of God, mourn not for thy dead more than six days, for on the seventh day is the sign of the resurrection and the rest of the age to come; on the seventh day the Lord rested from all His works."

**7** Then Seth made the tablets of stone and clay and wrote on them the life of Adam and Eve, as the angel Michael had instructed. And he placed them in the place where Adam used to pray to the Lord.

**8** And after the flood, many men could see them but no man could read them, until Solomon the wise. Then an angel of the Lord appeared to Solomon and said: "Solomon, son of David, thou hast found the tablets which Seth, the son of Adam, made. Read them and understand."

**9** And when Solomon had read the tablets, he glorified God and gave thanks, saying: "Blessed art Thou, O Lord, who hast made Adam in Thy image, and who didst form Eve to be a helpmate for him. From them the human race has come forth over all the earth."

**10** "And even when they transgressed Thy commandments, Thou didst not abandon them, but in Thy mercy Thou didst promise them the resurrection and the restoration of all things."

**11** "For Thou art merciful and compassionate, O Lord, slow to anger, and plenteous in mercy. Thou dost not desire the death of the sinner, but rather that he should turn from his wickedness and live."

**12** Thus was the life of Adam and Eve made known to the children of men, that they might remember the beginning of all things, and the fall from grace, and the promise of redemption.

**13** For as through the first Adam death came into the world, so through the mercy of God shall life be restored. And as the earth received Adam into her bosom, so shall she give him up again at the last day.

**14** These are the words concerning the life of Adam and Eve, the first man and the first woman, as they were revealed by the archangel Michael and written upon tablets of stone. He who reads them with understanding shall find wisdom, and he who keeps them in his heart shall be blessed. Amen.
