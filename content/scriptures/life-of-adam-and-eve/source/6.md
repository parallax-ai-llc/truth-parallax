---
title: "Life of Adam and Eve - Chapter 6"
scripture: "life-of-adam-and-eve"
chapter: 6
---

**1** When Adam had said this to all his sons, he was seized with great pains, and he cried aloud and said: "What shall I do? I am in great distress." And Eve also wept and said: "My lord Adam, rise up and give me half of thy disease and let me bear it; because it is on my account that this hath happened to thee, on my account thou art beset with toils and troubles."

**2** And Adam said to Eve: "Rise up and go with my son Seth to the neighborhood of paradise, and put dust on your heads and throw yourselves on the ground and lament in the sight of God. Perhaps He will have pity and send His angel to the tree of His mercy, from which floweth the oil of life, and will give you a drop of it, to anoint me with it, that I may have rest from these pains, by which I am being consumed."

**3** Then Seth and his mother Eve went towards the gates of paradise. And while they were walking, lo, suddenly there came a beast, a serpent, and it attacked and bit Seth.

**4** And as soon as Eve saw it, she wept and said: "Alas, wretched woman that I am. I am accursed since I have not kept the commandment of God." And Eve said to the serpent in a loud voice: "Accursed beast, how is it that thou wast not afraid to fling thyself on the image of God, but hast dared to fight with it?"

**5** The beast answered in the language of men: "Is it not against you, Eve, that our malice is directed? Are not ye the objects of our rage? Tell me, Eve, how was thy mouth opened to eat of the fruit? But now if I shall begin to reproach thee thou canst not bear it."

**6** Then Seth said to the beast: "The Lord God rebuke thee. Be silent, be dumb, shut thy mouth, accursed enemy of Truth. Get back and depart from the image of God till the day when the Lord God shall order thee to be brought to the trial." And the beast said to Seth: "See, I depart from the presence of the image of God, as thou hast said." And straightway he left Seth, wounded by his teeth.

**7** But Seth and his mother walked to the regions of paradise for the oil of mercy to anoint the sick Adam; and they arrived at the gates of paradise. And they took dust of the earth and placed it on their heads, and bowed themselves with their faces to the earth, and began to lament and make loud moaning, imploring the Lord God to pity Adam in his pains and to send His angel to give them the oil from the tree of His mercy.

**8** But when they had been praying and imploring for many hours, behold, the angel Michael appeared to them and said: "I have been sent to thee from the Lord. I am set over the bodies of men."

**9** "I tell thee, Seth, man of God, lament not, nor pray and entreat on account of the oil of the tree of mercy to anoint thy father Adam for the pains of his body."

**10** "For I tell thee that in no wise wilt thou be able to receive thereof save in the last days. But thou, Seth, go to thy father Adam, since the time of his life is fulfilled. Six days hence, his soul will leave his body, and when his soul goes out, thou shalt see great marvels in the heaven and in the earth and in the luminaries of heaven."

**11** With these words, the angel Michael departed from Seth. And Seth and Eve returned, bearing with them herbs of fragrance, that is, nard and crocus and calamus and cinnamon.
