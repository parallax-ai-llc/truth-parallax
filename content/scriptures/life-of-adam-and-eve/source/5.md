---
title: "Life of Adam and Eve - Chapter 5"
scripture: "life-of-adam-and-eve"
chapter: 5
---

**1** When Adam heard these words of the Lord, and the Holy One spoke thus to him, Adam fell down before Him and said: "O Lord, Almighty and Merciful God, Holy and Righteous One, let not the name that is mindful of Thy Majesty be blotted out, but convert my soul, for I die and my breath will go out of my mouth."

**2** "Cast me not out from before Thy face, whom Thou didst form of the clay of the earth. Do not banish from Thy favour him whom Thou didst nourish."

**3** And lo, the word of the Lord came unto me and the Lord said to me: "Since thy days were fashioned, thou wast made with a love of knowledge; therefore there shall not be taken from thy seed for ever the will to serve Me."

**4** And when Adam heard these words, he said: "Let it be so, O Lord, as Thou hast said. Thy will be done."

**5** And when Adam had lived nine hundred and thirty years, he knew that his days were coming to an end, and he said: "Let all my sons assemble themselves to me, that I may bless them before I die, and speak to them."

**6** And they were assembled in three parts, before his sight, in the house of prayer, where they used to pray to the Lord God.

**7** And they asked him, saying: "What concerns thee, Father, that thou shouldst gather us? And why dost thou lie on thy bed?" Then Adam answered and said: "My sons, I am sick and in pain."

**8** And all his sons said to him: "What does it mean, Father, this illness and pain?" And Seth his son said: "O my lord, perchance thou hast longed after the fruit of paradise which thou wast wont to eat, and therefore thou liest in sadness? Tell me and I will go to the nearest gate of paradise and put dust on my head and throw myself down on the earth before the gates of paradise and lament and make entreaty to God with loud groaning; perchance He will hearken to me and send His angel to bring me the fruit for which thou hast longed."

**9** Adam answered and said: "No, my son Seth, but I have sickness and pain." Seth answered: "And how have they come upon thee?"

**10** And Adam said to him: "When God made us, me and your mother, and placed us in paradise and gave us every tree bearing fruit to eat, He also gave us a commandment not to eat of one tree, for if we ate of it, we would die."

**11** "And the hour drew near for the angels who were guarding your mother to go up and worship the Lord. Then the enemy, the devil, gave to her, and she ate of the tree, knowing that I was not near her, nor the holy angels. Then she gave also to me to eat."

**12** "And when we had eaten, God was wroth with us, and the Lord came into paradise and called us and said: 'Where are ye?' And we hid ourselves, because we were ashamed. And He said to us: 'Because ye have forsaken my commandment and have not kept my word, I will bring upon your body seventy strokes; the trouble of the first stroke is a pain of the eyes; the second stroke an affliction of hearing; and likewise all the strokes shall betide you in turn.'"
