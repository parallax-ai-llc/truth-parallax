## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox encounter with the Life of Adam and Eve resonates with the tradition's theology of ancestral sin — the understanding that Adam's transgression introduced corruption and mortality into human nature without imputing personal guilt. The cosmic dimensions of the Fall, the angelic involvement in human affairs, and the promise of resurrection through Christ all connect with central Orthodox theological themes.
