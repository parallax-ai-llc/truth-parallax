## Overview

The Academic study of the Life of Adam and Eve navigates the complex relationship between the Latin, Greek, Armenian, Georgian, and Slavonic versions, seeking to reconstruct the earliest form of the tradition. Scholars analyze the text's contribution to Second Temple Jewish theology of sin, death, and angelic rebellion, and trace the evolution of the Satan mythology that became foundational for later Christian and Islamic traditions.
