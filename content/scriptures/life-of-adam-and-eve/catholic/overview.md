## Overview

The Catholic reading of the Life of Adam and Eve values the text for its contribution to the theology of original sin and its Marian typology. Eve's role in the narrative — both as participant in the Fall and as penitent — provides the antitype against which Mary's obedience is understood. The quest for the 'oil of mercy' anticipates the Catholic understanding of sacramental grace and the anointing of the sick.
