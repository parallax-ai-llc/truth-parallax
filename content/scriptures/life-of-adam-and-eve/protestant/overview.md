## Overview

The Protestant engagement with the Life of Adam and Eve draws on the text for its elaboration of the Genesis 3 narrative. The expanded account of Satan's motivation illuminates Protestant demonology, while the depiction of death's entrance into the world resonates with Pauline hamartiology (Romans 5:12). The text's emphasis on repentance after the Fall provides a narrative framework for the Protestant doctrine of contrition.
