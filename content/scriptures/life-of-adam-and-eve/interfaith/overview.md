## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Life of Adam and Eve draws on the text's power as a narrative shared — in various forms — across Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. The story of Satan's refusal to honor Adam provides common ground for interreligious dialogue about the nature of evil, while the themes of repentance, mortality, and eschatological hope unite the Abrahamic traditions in their reflection on the human condition.
