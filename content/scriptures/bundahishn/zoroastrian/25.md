## Zoroastrian Reading

The Bundahishn's account of the legendary kings traces the history of Iran from the primordial monarchs through the Kayanid dynasty. These kings — Hoshang, Tahmurath, Jamshid, and others — are presented as agents of Ahura Mazda's plan, each contributing to the advancement of civilization and the containment of evil. Their reigns establish the Zoroastrian understanding of legitimate kingship as a divine trust.
