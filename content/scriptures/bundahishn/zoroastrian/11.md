## Zoroastrian Reading

The creation of the wind describes the breath of life that Ahura Mazda breathed into the material world — the invisible force that animates all living things and maintains the cosmic order. Wind's association with the soul (*urvan*) and with the divine breath establishes its sacred character. The Zoroastrian reader understands atmospheric phenomena — wind, storms, rain — as manifestations of the ongoing cosmic struggle.
