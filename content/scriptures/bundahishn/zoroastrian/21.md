## Zoroastrian Reading

The Bundahishn's account of the righteous and the wicked provides the Zoroastrian ethical framework: human beings are defined not by birth or status but by their moral choices — their alignment with *asha* (truth/righteousness) or *druj* (falsehood/deception). This chapter articulates the fundamental Zoroastrian conviction that moral choice is the defining feature of human existence.
