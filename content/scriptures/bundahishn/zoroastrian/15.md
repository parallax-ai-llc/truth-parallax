## Zoroastrian Reading

The planets (*stargaran*) occupy a uniquely problematic position in Zoroastrian cosmology — they are classified as demonic beings whose irregular movements disrupt the orderly procession of the fixed stars. This negative evaluation of the planets contrasts sharply with the Babylonian and Greek traditions that regarded planetary movements as expressions of divine order, revealing a distinctively Zoroastrian cosmological perspective.
