## Zoroastrian Reading

The Bundahishn's account of rivers and seas describes the hydrological cycle as a manifestation of cosmic order. The rivers are daughters of the cosmic sea, and their flow from mountain to ocean recapitulates the cycle of purification that is the material world's response to Angra Mainyu's corruption. The sacredness of water in Zoroastrian practice is thus grounded in cosmological principle.
