## Zoroastrian Reading

The Zoroastrian reader begins the Bundahishn's cosmic narrative with the creation of the spiritual world (*menog*) — the primordial act by which Ahura Mazda brought into being the invisible realm of pure thought and spiritual forms that precedes and undergirds the material creation. The spiritual world is perfect, luminous, and wholly good — the direct expression of Ahura Mazda's wisdom and goodness. This chapter establishes the foundational Zoroastrian teaching that spirit precedes matter and that the material world is the embodiment of spiritual intention.
