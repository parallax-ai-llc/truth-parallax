## Zoroastrian Reading

Fire, the most sacred element in Zoroastrian worship, is described in its cosmic origin as a direct emanation of Ahura Mazda's creative energy. The Bundahishn distinguishes several grades of fire — from the fire in the body to the fire of the hearth to the fire that blazes before God — each serving a specific function in the cosmic order. The Zoroastrian reverence for fire, maintained in the sacred fire temples, is grounded in this cosmogonic account.
