## Zoroastrian Reading

The creation of the material world (*getig*) represents Ahura Mazda's response to the challenge of the Destructive Spirit. The Zoroastrian reader understands the material world not as a fallen or inferior realm but as the arena specifically designed for the final defeat of evil. Matter, in the Zoroastrian vision, is inherently good — it is the corruption introduced by Angra Mainyu that produces suffering and decay. The restoration of matter to its original purity is the goal of the cosmic process.
