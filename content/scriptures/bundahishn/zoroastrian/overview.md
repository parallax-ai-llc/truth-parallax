The Zoroastrian reading of the Bundahishn receives it as the definitive account of how Ahura Mazda created the world as an arena for the cosmic battle against Angra Mainyu (the Destructive Spirit). For the Zoroastrian community, the Bundahishn is not merely a mythological text but a revelation of the deepest structure of reality — explaining why the world exists, why evil is present within it, and how the faithful can participate in the ultimate triumph of good.

The text provides the cosmological framework within which all Zoroastrian ritual, ethics, and eschatology operate. Every aspect of creation — from the stars to the rivers to the animals — has a spiritual significance and a role in the cosmic struggle. The Zoroastrian who reads the Bundahishn gains a comprehensive understanding of the world as Ahura Mazda intended it and as the forces of evil have distorted it, along with the eschatological promise that creation will be restored to its original perfection.
