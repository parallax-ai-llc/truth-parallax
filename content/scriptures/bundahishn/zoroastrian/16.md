## Zoroastrian Reading

The Bundahishn's account of the earth's geography — its mountains, valleys, and landforms — interprets physical geography as the record of the cosmic struggle. Mountains rose when Angra Mainyu assaulted the originally flat earth; valleys were carved by the rushing of waters seeking to escape the Destructive Spirit's pollution. The Zoroastrian reader sees in every landscape feature the marks of the cosmic battle.
