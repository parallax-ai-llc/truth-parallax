## Comparative Reading

Comparative analysis of the Bundahishn's cosmographic treatment of the planets reveals parallels with other traditions' sacred geographies and astronomies: biblical cosmography, Hindu Puranic geography, Buddhist cosmology of Mount Meru, and medieval Islamic cosmography. Each tradition maps the cosmos according to its theological convictions, and the Bundahishn's distinctive feature — the moral valuation of astronomical and geographical phenomena as agents of good or evil — provides a unique perspective in this comparative conversation.
