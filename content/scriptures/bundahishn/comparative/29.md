## Comparative Reading

Comparative study of the Bundahishn's treatment of nature of evil creatures (khrafstars) places Zoroastrian demonology alongside other traditions' accounts of evil spiritual beings: Jewish *shedim*, Christian fallen angels, Islamic *jinn* and *shayatin*, Hindu *asuras*, and Buddhist *maras*. Each tradition develops a systematic account of evil's agents and their methods, and the comparison reveals both shared patterns (the personification of specific vices) and culturally distinctive features.
