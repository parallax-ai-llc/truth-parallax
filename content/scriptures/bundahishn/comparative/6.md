## Comparative Reading

Comparative study places the Bundahishn's creation account of the earth alongside parallel creation narratives from other traditions: the Genesis creation, the Babylonian Enuma Elish, the Hindu Purusha Sukta, the Egyptian cosmogonies, and Norse creation mythology. The analysis reveals both shared ancient Near Eastern and Indo-European motifs — such as creation through cosmic combat, primordial sacrifice, and the ordering of chaos — and distinctively Zoroastrian elements, particularly the ethical dimension of creation as an act of strategic goodness against evil.
