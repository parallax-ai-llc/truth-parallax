Comparative study of the Bundahishn reveals it as one of the most complete cosmogonic narratives from the ancient Near East — a text that spans the entire arc from creation to final renovation with a comprehensiveness matched by few other traditions' cosmogonic accounts. The Bundahishn's creation narrative is compared with Genesis, the Babylonian Enuma Elish, the Hindu Purusha Sukta, and Egyptian cosmogonies, revealing both shared motifs (primordial sacrifice, creation through combat, the ordering of chaos) and distinctive features.

The text's eschatological sections invite comparison with Jewish apocalyptic literature (Daniel, 1 Enoch), Christian Revelation, Islamic eschatology, and Hindu concepts of the *yugas* and *pralaya*. The Bundahishn occupies a pivotal position in the comparative study of eschatology, as Zoroastrian end-time concepts demonstrably influenced the development of Jewish, Christian, and Islamic eschatological traditions.
