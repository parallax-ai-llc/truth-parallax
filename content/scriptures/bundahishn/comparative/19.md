## Comparative Reading

Comparative study of the Bundahishn's treatment of varieties of animals reveals parallels with other traditions' systems of natural classification, social organization, and ritual calendars. The universal human tendency to organize knowledge of the natural and social worlds into systematic categories — reflecting cosmological and theological convictions — is illustrated by comparing the Bundahishn's classifications with Hindu varna, Chinese five-phases correlative thinking, and medieval European natural philosophy.
