## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader finds in the Bundahishn's cosmographic treatment of trees and plants parallels with Jewish cosmological traditions preserved in the Talmud, midrash, and Jewish apocalyptic literature. The shared features of Jewish and Zoroastrian cosmography — multi-layered heavens, cosmic waters, luminaries with moral significance — reflect the common Near Eastern cosmological inheritance of both traditions and the specific influence of Zoroastrian ideas on Jewish thought during the Persian and Hellenistic periods.
