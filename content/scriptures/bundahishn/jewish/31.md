## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader of the Bundahishn's treatment of nature of demons and their works encounters a demonological system that parallels and may have influenced the development of Jewish demonology in the Second Temple and Talmudic periods. The proliferation of named demons with specific functions in post-exilic Jewish literature — a development largely absent from pre-exilic biblical texts — suggests Zoroastrian influence during the Persian period, and the Bundahishn provides the fullest account of the Zoroastrian demonological tradition.
