## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader of the Bundahishn's treatment of nature of human faculties discovers parallels with Jewish systems of classification, calendrical organization, and ritual practice that may reflect shared Near Eastern cultural patterns or direct Zoroastrian influence on Jewish thought. The comparison illuminates both the distinctiveness of Jewish approaches and the cosmopolitan intellectual environment in which post-exilic Judaism developed.
