## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader of the Bundahishn's creation account of the sky encounters a cosmogony that may have influenced the development of Jewish creation theology during the Persian period. The parallels between the Bundahishn's creation narrative and elements of Genesis, the Psalms, and Job's cosmological passages invite reflection on the extent to which Jewish and Zoroastrian thought shared a common Near Eastern cosmological vocabulary and mutually influenced each other during the centuries of Persian rule.
