## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader of the Bundahishn's eschatological teaching on signs of the end times encounters the possible source of concepts that became central to Jewish messianic and eschatological expectations. The development of Jewish concepts of resurrection, final judgment, the Messiah, and *olam ha-ba* (the world to come) during the Persian and Hellenistic periods corresponds chronologically with Jewish exposure to Zoroastrian eschatological ideas, and the Bundahishn's detailed account provides the fullest context for understanding these possible influences.
