## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader of the Bundahishn's historical narrative of history of the faith through the centuries finds parallels with the biblical narrative of sacred history — the conviction that historical events unfold according to divine purpose and that the community of faith is connected to its origins through a chain of divinely guided leaders. The Protestant concept of *providentia Dei* (divine providence) finds a parallel in the Zoroastrian understanding of history as the progressive unfolding of Ahura Mazda's plan.
