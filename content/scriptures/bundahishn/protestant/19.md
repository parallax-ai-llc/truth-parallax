## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader of the Bundahishn's treatment of varieties of animals discovers parallels with biblical systems of classification and ordering. The universal human impulse to organize the created world into meaningful categories — reflected in Adam's naming of the animals, the Levitical dietary laws, and the Zoroastrian classifications — reveals a shared conviction that the world is ordered by divine purpose and that human beings are called to discern and respect that order.
