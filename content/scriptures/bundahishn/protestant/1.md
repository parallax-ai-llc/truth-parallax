## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader of the Bundahishn's creation account of the spiritual world discovers ancient parallels to the biblical creation narrative that illuminate the shared Near Eastern context of Genesis. The Zoroastrian concept of creation as a purposeful act of a good God resonates with the biblical affirmation that God saw everything He had made and declared it 'very good.' The presence of a destructive antagonist in the Zoroastrian account invites reflection on the biblical understanding of evil's intrusion into God's good creation.
