## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader finds in the Bundahishn's cosmographic treatment of the planets a window into the ancient Near Eastern worldview that also shapes biblical cosmography. The shared cosmological assumptions — a structured cosmos with heaven above, earth below, and waters surrounding — remind the Protestant reader that the biblical writers operated within the same cultural-cosmological framework as their Zoroastrian neighbors, even as they expressed distinctive theological convictions about God's sovereignty and purpose.
