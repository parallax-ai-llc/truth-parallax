## Islamic Reading

The Islamic reader of the Bundahishn's treatment of nature of human faculties discovers the pre-Islamic Persian systems of classification and organization that influenced the development of Islamic natural philosophy and social thought. The Zoroastrian interest in systematic knowledge — classifying animals, plants, human groups, and ritual practices — anticipates the Islamic encyclopedic tradition that would flourish in the Abbasid period, when Persian intellectual traditions were actively translated and assimilated.
