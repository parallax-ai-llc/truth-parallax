The Islamic reading of the Bundahishn engages with Zoroastrian cosmogony as part of the pre-Islamic Persian heritage that profoundly shaped Islamic civilization, particularly in its eastern domains. The Bundahishn's cosmological and eschatological concepts influenced Islamic thought through multiple channels: direct transmission from Zoroastrian scholars, the translation movement (*naql*) of the Abbasid period, and the general cultural continuity of Persian civilization across the Sasanian-Islamic transition.

Islamic scholars recognize Zoroastrian influence in Islamic descriptions of creation, the structure of the heavens, the battle between angels and demons, the signs of the end times, and the final renovation of the world. The Bundahishn provides the pre-Islamic context for these Islamic concepts, revealing the deep roots of Persian Islamic culture in the Zoroastrian intellectual tradition.
