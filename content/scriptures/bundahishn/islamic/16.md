## Islamic Reading

The Islamic reader finds in the Bundahishn's cosmographic treatment of the earth and mountains the pre-Islamic Persian cosmological tradition that influenced Islamic cosmography. Many features of Islamic descriptions of the cosmos — the seven heavens, the cosmic mountain, the celestial bodies as agents of divine will — have parallels in the Bundahishn that may reflect direct Zoroastrian influence or shared Near Eastern cosmological heritage. Persian Islamic cosmographical texts, such as those of Biruni, drew on both Islamic and pre-Islamic Persian sources.
