---
title: "Chapter 2 – The Assault of the Destructive Spirit"
chapter: 2
---

**1.** The Destructive Spirit, when his stupor was ended, arose with his demons. He beheld the creation of Ohrmazd, and the creatures in their material form appeared to him of a wonderful nature.

**2.** He surveyed the sky, which the Fravashis of the righteous defended, and it seemed to him of the strongest fortification; and he thought: "The Fravashis of the righteous are strong."

**3.** He surveyed the creation, one by one: the stars and the moon and the sun, and the form of the Beneficent Animal and the form of Mankind, even Gayomard, and the form of all that Ohrmazd had shaped forth.

**4.** Then the Destructive Spirit, with his demons, rushed upon the creation. He assailed the sky, which was the first. He leapt upon it like a snake, and dragged the sky which was above the earth down into the Void.

**5.** He came upon the water. He came upon the earth. He came upon the plants. He came upon the Ox. He came upon Gayomard. He came upon the fire. Like a fly he rushed upon the whole creation.

**6.** At midday he attacked, and the world became dark as night. He darkened the sky, which was above and below the earth.

**7.** He let loose upon the earth noxious creatures — serpents and scorpions and lizards and tortoises — so that not so much as a needle's point was left free from the crawling of the noxious creatures.

**8.** He brought greed and want, pain and disease, hunger and thirst, grief and decay, foulness and lethargy upon the creation.

**9.** Before his assault upon the Ox, Ohrmazd gave the Ox healing herbs to eat, and rubbed its eyes with the juice of the white hom plant, that the pain and torment of the assault might be less.

**10.** It immediately became ill and lean, and the milk dried up. At last it died. Then the bull said: "The creatures ought to be saved from the Enemy." Before Gayomard was afflicted, Ohrmazd brought sleep over Gayomard for the same purpose.

**11.** When Gayomard was afflicted, Ohrmazd said: "From thee shall arise the Family of the Man, and thou art the seed of Mankind, and from thy seed shall men be born."

**12.** The Destructive Spirit thought: "All the creatures of Ohrmazd are made powerless save Mankind alone." Then he assailed Gayomard. The Destructive Spirit and the demon of death assaulted him.

**13.** But Gayomard's appointed time had not yet come, and he lived on for thirty years after the assault. And Ohrmazd said: "Though the adversary has come upon my creation, mankind shall arise from thee, and shall smite back the Lie."
