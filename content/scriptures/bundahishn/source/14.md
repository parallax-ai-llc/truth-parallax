---
title: "Chapter 14 – On the Nature of the Body of Man"
chapter: 14
---

**1.** On the nature of the body it is declared in the Religion that Ohrmazd created the body of man from the material world, and the soul from the spiritual world. He joined them together.

**2.** The body of man is composed of these: bone from stone, marrow from water, blood from water, hair from plants, and the vital heat from fire.

**3.** Ohrmazd created the body of man in the likeness of his own form. The head is the likeness of the sky, the feet of the earth, the breath of the wind, the blood of water, and the eyes of the luminaries.

**4.** In the body there are five senses: sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch. Through these five senses the soul perceives the material world.

**5.** The soul has five faculties: the vital spirit, the conscience, the intellect, the will, and the innate wisdom. Through these faculties the soul governs the body.

**6.** The vital spirit maintains the body in life, as fire maintains fuel. When the vital spirit departs, the body dies.

**7.** The conscience distinguishes between good and evil. It is the voice of Ohrmazd within man.

**8.** The intellect understands and comprehends all things. Through the intellect man knows Ohrmazd and the Religion.

**9.** The will enables man to choose between good and evil. Man's free will is the gift of Ohrmazd, so that through it he may choose the Right and reject the Lie.

**10.** The innate wisdom is that which was placed in man at creation. It recognizes the Good without instruction, as the infant knows to suckle milk.

**11.** When a man dies, the body returns to the earth, the vital spirit to the wind, the likeness and form to the sun, and the soul goes to its appointed place to await the judgment.

**12.** For three days the soul sits at the head of the body. On the dawn of the fourth day it reaches the Chinvat Bridge, where it is judged by Mithra, Sraosha, and Rashnu. If its good deeds outweigh the evil, it crosses the Bridge to paradise. If its evil deeds outweigh the good, it falls from the Bridge into hell.
