---
title: "Chapter 26 – On the Spiritual Chieftainship of the Regions"
chapter: 26
---

**1.** On the spiritual chieftainship of the regions it is declared in the Religion that each of the seven regions of the earth has a chieftain, both material and spiritual.

**2.** Over the land of Iran, the chieftain is Zarathushtra in the spiritual realm, and in the material realm the righteous king whom Ohrmazd has chosen.

**3.** Each region has its own Yazad as protector, and its own constellation as guardian. The constellation of the Ram guards Iran and its waters.

**4.** The righteous kings of Iran, from Hooshang to Vishtaspa, each received the royal glory from Ohrmazd, and through that glory they were able to rule with justice.

**5.** The royal glory, called Khvarenah, is a spiritual fire that descends upon the chosen of Ohrmazd. When a king acts with righteousness, the glory remains with him. When he turns to evil, the glory departs.

**6.** Jamshid possessed the greatest glory of all kings. When he grew proud and spoke falsely, the glory left him in three portions: one went to Mithra, one to Faridun, and one to Keresaspa.

**7.** Through the glory, the righteous kings established civilization: they built roads and bridges, irrigated fields, founded cities, taught arts and sciences, and upheld the Religion.

**8.** At the end of time, the glory shall be gathered together and given to the Saoshyants, who through its power shall accomplish the Renovation and renew creation.
