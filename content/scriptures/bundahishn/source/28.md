---
title: "Chapter 28 – On the Nature of the Bird Karshipt and Others"
chapter: 28
---

**1.** On the nature of the bird Karshipt it is declared in the Religion that the Karshipt dwells in the sacred precinct of the Var made by Yima, and there it recites the Avesta in the language of birds.

**2.** When Yima built the Var to protect the best of creation from the deadly winter, the Karshipt was among the birds brought within, and it maintained the Religion there.

**3.** The Saena bird, called also Simurgh, is the greatest of all birds. Its nest is upon the tree of all remedies. It is of the good creation, and it nourishes the tree.

**4.** The Chamrosh bird is its helper. It gathers the seeds that fall from the tree of all seeds and carries them to where Tishtar may take them with the rain.

**5.** The Ashozushta bird is the bird that is called by the people the "owl." It cries aloud in the night, warning men against the demons of darkness.

**6.** The rooster is the bird of Sraosha. It crows before dawn and wakes men for their prayers, driving away the demon Bushyasta. He who keeps a rooster gains merit, for the rooster fights the demons of the night.

**7.** The hawk and the falcon are of the good creation, for they prey upon noxious creatures — the mice and the rats and the serpents.

**8.** All birds of the good creation serve Ohrmazd by their nature: some by guarding the crops, some by destroying noxious creatures, some by carrying seeds, and some by waking men for prayer.
