---
title: "Chapter 34 – On the Reckoning of the Years"
chapter: 34
---

**1.** On the reckoning of the years it is declared in the Religion that from the creation of Gayomard to the coming of Zarathushtra was a span of time which they reckon in the Religion.

**2.** From Gayomard to Hooshang was forty years. Hooshang ruled for forty years. Tahmuras ruled for thirty years. Jamshid ruled for six hundred and sixteen years, of which three hundred years were in glory.

**3.** Dahak, the servant of Ahriman, ruled for a thousand years, with tyranny and oppression, feeding the serpents on his shoulders with the brains of men.

**4.** Faridun ruled for five hundred years. He divided the earth among his sons and bound Dahak on Mount Damavand.

**5.** Manushchihr ruled for one hundred and twenty years. He avenged Iraj and drove back the Turanians from the land of Iran.

**6.** Under the kings of the Kayanian dynasty — Kay Kobad, Kay Kavad, Kay Khosrov, Kay Lohrasp, and Kay Vishtaspa — the land prospered and the Religion was upheld.

**7.** In the reign of Kay Vishtaspa, Zarathushtra came forth and proclaimed the Religion. He was thirty years old when he received the revelation, and he was forty-seven when Vishtaspa accepted the Good Religion.

**8.** From the acceptance of the Religion to the coming of Alexander was three hundred years. Alexander destroyed many of the sacred books and the fire temples, and the Religion was grievously harmed.

**9.** After Alexander, the Arsacid kings ruled, and then the Sasanian kings restored the Religion and gathered the scattered texts and rebuilt the fire temples.

**10.** The Religion teaches that the reckoning of years is important, for it shows how the world moves toward the Renovation, and how the power of the Destructive Spirit diminishes with each age.
