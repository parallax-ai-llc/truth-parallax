---
title: "Chapter 16 – On the Nature of Winds and Clouds"
chapter: 16
---

**1.** On the nature of the wind it is declared in the Religion that the wind was created by Ohrmazd to maintain the creatures. Without wind there would be no breath in the body, and all that lives would die.

**2.** There are many kinds of wind. The good wind blows gently and brings rain and makes the plants grow. The evil wind blows violently and brings destruction.

**3.** The wind blows from every direction. When it blows from the south, it brings warmth; from the north, cold. From the east it brings light; from the west, moisture.

**4.** The Destructive Spirit corrupted the wind, making hurricanes and dust-storms and whirlwinds, which destroy the works of man and uproot the trees.

**5.** On the nature of clouds it is declared that when Tishtar rises, he seizes the water from the sea of Vourukasha and turns it into clouds. The wind drives the clouds over the earth, and the rain falls upon the lands.

**6.** Three times in each year Tishtar produces rain. The first rain nourishes the grain; the second rain fills the rivers; the third rain causes the seeds of plants to ripen.

**7.** The cloud is the vessel of rain. Tishtar, in the form of a white horse, fights with the demon Apaosha, who is in the form of a black horse. When Tishtar prevails, rain falls upon the earth. When Apaosha prevails, there is drought.

**8.** So Ohrmazd appointed Tishtar as the guardian of rain, and through his victory over Apaosha, the earth is watered and the creatures of Ohrmazd are sustained.
