---
title: "Chapter 7 – On the Nature of the Earth"
chapter: 7
---

**1.** On the nature of the earth it is declared in the Religion that there are thirty-three kinds of land. On the day when Tishtar produced the rain, the water stood on the whole earth to the height of a man.

**2.** All the noxious creatures that were on the earth were destroyed by the water, and the holes and dens in which they had burrowed were covered over.

**3.** Afterwards, the wind swept the water to the ends of the earth. Three great seas arose, and the water of the small seas was made salty. The great ocean, called Vourukasha, occupies one-third of the earth, to the south.

**4.** At the beginning of creation, the earth was flat and without mountains. When the Destructive Spirit assailed the earth, the earth shook, and mountains grew from the ground.

**5.** First, Mount Alburz grew up around the earth. It grew for eight hundred years: two hundred years up to the star station, two hundred years up to the moon station, two hundred years up to the sun station, and two hundred years up to the Endless Light.

**6.** From Mount Alburz all other mountains grew, in number two thousand two hundred and forty-four. They are like the roots and branches of a tree, whose root is Alburz.

**7.** The land of Iran is in the middle of the seven regions, and it is the best of the regions. The six other regions that surround it are: Arzah to the east, Savah to the west, Fradadhafshu and Vidadhafshu to the south, and Vourubareshti and Vourujareshti to the north.

**8.** Between every two regions there is a sea, so that passage from one to the other is not possible except by the guidance of the spiritual beings.

**9.** In the middle of the world is the peak of Mount Alburz, around which the sun and moon and stars revolve. On one side it is always day and on the other it is always night, as the shadow of Mount Alburz falls.

**10.** Eighteen great rivers flow from Mount Alburz, and all the other rivers and streams branch from them. The greatest of these is the Arang River, and the Veh River, which flow one to the east and one to the west and pour into the great ocean.
