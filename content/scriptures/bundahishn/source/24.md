---
title: "Chapter 24 – On the Chieftainship of Men and Animals"
chapter: 24
---

**1.** On the chieftainship of men and animals it is declared in the Religion that Ohrmazd created a chieftain for every species, that each might be governed and protected.

**2.** Of men, the chieftain is the Prophet Zarathushtra, for through him the Religion was revealed and men were shown the path of righteousness.

**3.** Of oxen, the chieftain is the white ox called Sarsaok. Of horses, the white horse with golden ears. Of camels, the white camel. Of dogs, the white dog.

**4.** Of birds, the chieftain is the Karshipt, which recites the Avesta. Of fish, the Kara fish, which guards the tree of all seeds.

**5.** Of trees, the chieftain is the Gaokerena, the white Hom tree, whose juice gives immortality. Of grains, wheat is the chief.

**6.** Among countries, the chieftain is Iranvej, the best and noblest of all lands. Among mountains, Alburz. Among rivers, the Arang.

**7.** In every class and every species, Ohrmazd has established a chief and a leader, so that order may prevail and the creation may prosper.

**8.** The chieftain of the Amesha Spentas is Ohrmazd himself. The chieftain of the Yazads in the material world is Zarathushtra, and after him the righteous kings who uphold the Religion.

**9.** As each species has its chieftain, so each human community must have its righteous leader, who governs with good thought and justice, according to the ordinances of the Religion.
