---
title: "Chapter 6 – On the Contest of the Creations"
chapter: 6
---

**1.** On the nature of the contest they say: when the Destructive Spirit produced the creatures of his assault, they were full of malice and destructiveness, and the world was in darkness.

**2.** The Destructive Spirit, by his nature of destruction, was unable to create anything beneficial. His nature is to smite, to destroy, and to make desolate. He has no share in creation except that which is harmful.

**3.** When the Destructive Spirit rushed upon the Ox, which was the sole-created being, it first bellowed, then trembled, and then died. And from the Ox as it died the fifty-five species of grain and the twelve species of healing plants grew forth.

**4.** From the seed of the Ox, which was kept in the station of the moon, after being purified by the light of the moon, every species of animal came into being: two of each kind, one male and one female, from which arose two hundred and eighty-two species of animals.

**5.** First, two oxen, then two horses, two camels, two dogs, then all the other animals, each two by two, so that the world should be replenished.

**6.** When Gayomard died, from his left hand came the seed of mankind. It fell upon the earth, and from it grew a plant in the form of a rhubarb, with one stem and fifteen leaves. This was Mashya and Mashyane, the first man and the first woman.

**7.** Ohrmazd said to them: "You are human beings, the parents of the world. I have created you with the best righteousness. Perform your duties with good thought, speak good words, do good deeds, and worship no demon."

**8.** And at first they believed, and said: "Ohrmazd created the water and the earth, the plants and the animals, the stars and the moon and the sun, and all the good things whose origin and growth are from the manifestation of righteousness."

**9.** But the Destructive Spirit assaulted their minds with lies, and they said: "The Destructive Spirit created the water, the earth, the plants, and all things." This was the first falsehood they uttered, and it was the desire of the demons.

**10.** By that falsehood they were made wicked, and their souls went to hell until the Renovation and the future body. And for thirty days they ate no food; then they first drank water, then ate plants, then milk, and then meat.
