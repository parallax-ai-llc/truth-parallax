---
title: "Chapter 1 – On the Beginning of Creation"
chapter: 1
---

**1.** As revealed by the Religion of the Mazdayasnians, so it is declared that Ohrmazd was on high in omniscience and goodness; for boundless time he was ever in the light.

**2.** That light is the throne and place of Ohrmazd; some call it Endless Light. The omniscience and goodness of the Almighty Ohrmazd is what they call the Religion.

**3.** Ahriman was in the depth, in darkness and ignorance, and the desire to smite. That desire to smite is what they call the Fiendish Religion.

**4.** Between them was the Void. Some call it Vay, in which the two spirits mingle with each other.

**5.** Both are limited and unlimited: for the highest is that which they call Endless Light, in which is the throne of Ohrmazd; and the deepest is Endless Darkness, which is Ahriman's abode. Both are spirits.

**6.** Ohrmazd, in his omniscience, knew that the Destructive Spirit existed, that he would mingle with his creatures and attack, and that this conflict would last long. He shaped forth by his mind that which was needful for the contest.

**7.** For three thousand years creation was in a spiritual state: it was without thought, without motion, without touch. The Destructive Spirit, on account of his ignorance, was not aware of the existence of Ohrmazd.

**8.** Then he rose up from the depth, and went to the border of the lights. When he saw the light of Ohrmazd, of a nature opposed to his, he rushed forward to destroy it.

**9.** Because he saw valour and supremacy greater than his own, he fled back to the darkness, and shaped many demons and fiends, and the host arose for the fight.

**10.** Ohrmazd, seeing the creatures of the Destructive Spirit, frightful and putrid and evil — deemed them not praiseworthy. Then the Destructive Spirit, seeing the creatures of Ohrmazd, they appeared many and worthy of praise, and he coveted them.

**11.** Ohrmazd, in spite of his knowledge of creation, approached the Destructive Spirit with peace. He said: "O Destructive Spirit! Aid my creatures, and offer them praise, so that in return thou mayest be immortal and undecaying and without hunger and without thirst."

**12.** But the Destructive Spirit cried out: "I shall not aid thy creatures, nor shall I offer them praise. I shall destroy thee and thy creatures for ever and ever. I shall persuade all thy creatures to hatred of thee and to love of me."

**13.** And Ohrmazd said: "Thou art not omniscient and almighty. Thou canst not destroy me, and thou canst not so influence my creatures that they will not return to me."

**14.** Then Ohrmazd, through omniscience, knew that if he did not fix a time for the contest, then what Ahriman threatened he would be able to do; the struggle would last forever and without end.

**15.** And Ohrmazd said to the Destructive Spirit: "Fix a time, so that by this agreement the struggle may be for nine thousand years." For he knew that by fixing a time, the Destructive Spirit would be rendered powerless.

**16.** Then the Destructive Spirit, not seeing the end, agreed to that covenant, as even now two combatants who fight fix a time, saying: "Let us fight on such a day till night."

**17.** This too, Ohrmazd knew through omniscience: that within these nine thousand years, three thousand would pass in the will of Ohrmazd, three thousand in the mingling of the wills of Ohrmazd and Ahriman, and that in the last three thousand years the Destructive Spirit would be made powerless, and they would keep the adversary from attacking the creatures.

**18.** Then Ohrmazd chanted the Ahunavar prayer — "Yatha Ahu Vairyo" — and showed the Destructive Spirit his own final victory, the powerlessness of the Destructive Spirit, the destruction of the demons, the resurrection, the future body, and the freedom of creation from aggression for ever and ever.

**19.** The Destructive Spirit, seeing his own powerlessness and the annihilation of his demons, was confounded and fell back into the darkness. For three thousand years he lay in stupor.

**20.** During that stupor of the Destructive Spirit, Ohrmazd created his creatures. First he created the Sky, bright and visible, from Endless Light, in the shape of an egg, of shining metal.

**21.** Second, from the Sky, he created Water. Third, from the Water, he created Earth. Fourth, he created Plants. Fifth, he created the Beneficent Animal. Sixth, he created Mankind.
