---
title: "Chapter 17 – On the Nature of the Chinvat Bridge"
chapter: 17
---

**1.** On the Chinvat Bridge it is declared in the Religion that it extends from the peak of Mount Alburz, which is called the Peak of Judgment, over to the other world.

**2.** The Bridge is of many sides: for the righteous it is broad, nine javelins wide; for the wicked it is narrow, like the edge of a razor. The righteous man crosses with ease; the wicked man falls from it into hell.

**3.** When a man dies, for three days and three nights the soul sits at the head of the body, reciting the Ustavaiti Gatha, and experiencing joy or sorrow according to its deeds.

**4.** On the dawn of the fourth day, the soul of the righteous man, accompanied by the sweet-scented wind from the south, reaches the Chinvat Bridge. There comes to meet him his own Religion, in the form of a maiden, beautiful and radiant.

**5.** The soul asks: "Who art thou, that I have never seen a maiden more beautiful than thee?" She answers: "I am thy good deeds. I am thy good thoughts, thy good words, thy good actions, thy good religion. When thou wast on earth, thou didst worship and praise and make offering. Thou didst pursue good and forsake evil. I am thy own self."

**6.** Then the soul of the righteous crosses the Chinvat Bridge with ease. He comes first to the star station, where Good Thought receives him; then to the moon station, where Good Word receives him; then to the sun station, where Good Deed receives him; then to the Endless Light, the dwelling of Ohrmazd.

**7.** But the soul of the wicked, on the dawn of the fourth day, surrounded by the stench of a foul wind from the north, reaches the Bridge. There comes to meet him his own religion, in the form of a hag, ugly and terrible.

**8.** The soul asks: "Who art thou, that I have never seen anything more ugly?" She answers: "I am thy evil deeds. I am thy evil thoughts, thy evil words, thy evil actions. When thou wast on earth, thou didst worship demons and do evil. I am thy own self."

**9.** The soul of the wicked steps upon the Bridge, and it becomes narrow as the edge of a razor. The demon Vizarsh seizes him and drags him down into hell, where he shall suffer until the Renovation.

**10.** There are also those whose good and evil deeds are equal. They go to the place called Hamistagan, which is between heaven and hell. There they experience neither joy nor sorrow, but only cold and heat.

**11.** At the Renovation, all souls shall be raised, and all shall pass through the river of molten metal. The righteous shall pass through as through warm milk, and the wicked shall be purified by it, and all shall be made immortal, and creation shall be renewed.
