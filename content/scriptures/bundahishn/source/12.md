---
title: "Chapter 12 – On the Nature of Mankind"
chapter: 12
---

**1.** On the nature of mankind it is declared in the Religion that Gayomard, the first man, was created on the bank of the river Daiti, in the middle of the earth, bright as the sun.

**2.** When Gayomard was about to die, his seed fell to the earth. Neryosang, the Yazad, preserved the seed, and Spandarmad received it.

**3.** After forty years, Mashya and Mashyane grew up from the earth in the form of a rhubarb plant, with one stem and fifteen leaves, and they changed from plant to human form.

**4.** Ohrmazd said to them: "You are human beings, the parents of the world. Do your work with righteous thought; speak good words; do good deeds; and worship no demons."

**5.** At first they thought that each was created by the other, and they said: "Who created us?" But then they said: "Ohrmazd created us." And this was the truth.

**6.** But the Destructive Spirit deluded their minds, and they spoke a lie. They said: "The Destructive Spirit created the water and the earth and the plants and all good things." Through this lie the Destructive Spirit gained power, and their souls went to hell.

**7.** For thirty days they ate no food. After thirty days they went to a white-haired goat and sucked the milk. The milk tasted sweet and good to them.

**8.** Then the Destructive Spirit spoke to them again, corrupting their minds, and they drank of the milk and said: "We have drunk the milk, but it does not profit us." This was the second lie, and through it the demons gained strength.

**9.** After thirty days more, they came to a sheep, white-jawed and fat, and they slaughtered it. By the instruction of the spiritual beings they kindled a fire of wood, using the fire from the dry branches, for in those first days fire was easy to kindle.

**10.** They roasted the sheep and ate of it, and left a portion for the fire, saying: "This is the share of the fire." They threw a portion to the sky, saying: "This is the share of the Yazads." But a vulture came and carried away the meat.

**11.** Then they ate more meat and put on clothing of skins. They dug a pit in the earth and found iron. They beat it with stones and made for themselves an axe, and they cut wood, and made a shelter.

**12.** In fifty years they produced children: a pair, a boy and a girl. From tenderness toward them, the mother devoured the boy and the father the girl.

**13.** Then Ohrmazd took away that tenderness, so that thereafter parents reared their children. From those first children seven pairs were born, and each pair became husband and wife.

**14.** From one pair, Siamak and Neshak, came a son Fravak and a daughter Fravakain. From them came fifteen pairs, each pair becoming a people, and from them the different peoples of the seven regions of the earth descended.

**15.** From Fravak all the races of mankind spread across the earth, each to their appointed region, and the human race multiplied until it filled all the lands.
