---
title: "Chapter 29 – On the Measurement of Time"
chapter: 29
---

**1.** On the measurement of time it is declared in the Religion that Ohrmazd created time when he created the creatures. Time is the lord of all that is born and dies.

**2.** From Boundless Time, Ohrmazd fashioned Bounded Time for a period of twelve thousand years, during which the battle with the Destructive Spirit should be waged and won.

**3.** Of these twelve thousand years, the first three thousand passed in the spiritual creation, when Ohrmazd created all things in their spiritual form.

**4.** The second three thousand years passed in the material creation, when Ohrmazd brought the spiritual forms into material being. During this time the Destructive Spirit lay in stupor.

**5.** The third three thousand years began with the assault of the Destructive Spirit, when good and evil became mingled in the material world. This is the time of conflict.

**6.** The fourth and last three thousand years is the period of the Renovation, when the Saoshyants shall come and the Destructive Spirit shall be defeated. At its end, Bounded Time shall return to Boundless Time.

**7.** Within each year there are twelve months, and in each month thirty days, and in each day five watches. The day belongs to Ohrmazd, and the night to Ahriman, though even in the night the Yazads keep watch.

**8.** The six seasonal festivals, the Gahanbars, mark the six stages of creation: the creation of the sky, of water, of earth, of plants, of animals, and of man.

**9.** The first Gahanbar is Maidyozarem, in mid-spring, which celebrates the creation of the sky. The second is Maidyoshahem, in mid-summer, which celebrates the creation of water.

**10.** The third is Paitishahem, at harvest time, for the creation of earth. The fourth is Ayathrem, in autumn, for the creation of plants. The fifth is Maidyarem, in mid-winter, for the creation of animals. The sixth is Hamaspathmaidyem, at the end of winter, for the creation of man.

**11.** By the keeping of these festivals and the observance of the sacred calendar, the righteous maintain the order of creation and hasten the Renovation.
