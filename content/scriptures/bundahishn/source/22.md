---
title: "Chapter 22 – On the Nature of Rivers"
chapter: 22
---

**1.** On the nature of rivers it is declared in the Religion that two great rivers flow from the north, from Alburz: the Arang to the west and the Veh to the east. They flow around the earth and pour back into the sea of Vourukasha.

**2.** Eighteen rivers of navigable waters flow from Mount Alburz, and from each of these many smaller rivers branch. The number of all the rivers that flow is beyond counting.

**3.** The Arang River is called also Aras, and it flows through many lands to the west. The Veh River flows eastward through the lands of the east.

**4.** The river Helmand flows through Seistan; the river Marv flows through Khorasan; the river Tigris flows through Babylon; the river Euphrates flows through the lands of the Arabs.

**5.** The river Oxus is among the great rivers, and the river Indus, and the river Nile, which flows through Egypt.

**6.** All these rivers carry the waters of Ohrmazd to the fields and pastures of men, that the crops may grow and the cattle may drink. The rivers are the veins of the earth, as the blood-vessels are the veins of the body.

**7.** The Destructive Spirit has polluted the rivers with salt and bitterness, so that some waters are unfit for drinking. But the waters that flow from the mountains are sweet, for they come from the pure source.

**8.** At the Renovation all waters shall be purified, and the rivers shall flow sweet and clear as they did in the beginning.
