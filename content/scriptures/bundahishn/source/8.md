---
title: "Chapter 8 – On the Nature of Waters"
chapter: 8
---

**1.** On the nature of waters it is declared in the Religion that two rivers flow from the north, from Alburz: one to the east, which is the Veh River, and one to the west, which is the Arang River. They flow continuously around the earth.

**2.** Eighteen rivers of navigable waters flow from the summit of Alburz, and all these join the Vourukasha sea. Every year Tishtar draws up the water from the sea and distributes it in the form of rain.

**3.** Besides the eighteen rivers there are others, smaller ones, which flow from each of these. They say: "These waters all flow back to the great sea Vourukasha, and from Vourukasha they flow again into the rivers."

**4.** Of the origin of the sea of Vourukasha it is said: when Tishtar produced rain, the water collected in the lowlands of the earth and formed the sea. One-third of this earth is covered by the Vourukasha sea.

**5.** It is warm at the time of summer and cold at winter. In the middle of the sea of Vourukasha stands the tree of many seeds, which is called the tree of all remedies, and there is the white Hom tree.

**6.** They say all the seeds of plants in the world are on that tree. Every year, when Tishtar takes up water, he takes the seeds of all these plants along with it and rains them upon the earth.

**7.** Near the tree of many seeds, the Kara fish circles continually around it, so that no harmful creature may approach. The Kara fish is created as the protector of that tree.

**8.** Of the sea of Putik it is said that it is between Sagastan and Pars; it has the peculiarity that it keeps rising and falling, and many rivers flow into it, yet it does not increase.

**9.** There is a river called Nahvtak, through which all the foulness and pollution of the world are carried to the sea of Putik, where it is purified.

**10.** Water is the life of creation; all the creations of Ohrmazd were made to grow through water, and without it they would not survive.
