---
title: "Chapter 11 – On the Nature of Animals"
chapter: 11
---

**1.** On the nature of animals it is declared in the Religion that when the sole-created Ox died, from its marrow grew fifty-five species of grain and twelve species of healing plants.

**2.** From the seed of the Ox, which was carried to the moon station and purified in the light of the moon, came two animals, one male and one female. From them came two hundred and eighty-two species of animals on the earth.

**3.** Among animals, the dog is created foremost. Of dogs there are ten species: the shepherd's dog, the house dog, the bloodhound, the young dog, the water-beaver, the fox, the weasel, the hedgehog, the ichneumon, and the porcupine.

**4.** Of horses there are ten species; and the noblest among them is the white horse with yellow ears, on whose forehead is a mark. Of camels there are two species: the swift one and the two-humped.

**5.** Of oxen there are fifteen species: the white, the dun, the red, the yellow, the black, and the spotted. The ox is the foremost of creatures for its usefulness to men.

**6.** Of birds there are many species. The chief among them is the Karshipt bird, which knows the Religion and recites the Avesta in the language of birds. The eagle, the hawk, the crane, the magpie, and others.

**7.** The Chamrosh bird is of the nature that when it perches on the top of a tree, all the seeds and branches are shaken from the tree. When it settles on a mountain, the mountain trembles.

**8.** The Saena bird, some call it the Simurgh, is the chief of birds. It sits on the tree of all seeds. When it rises from the tree, a thousand branches spring forth; when it sits, a thousand branches are broken and the seeds are scattered.

**9.** Of fish there are ten species; the most important is the Kara fish, which guards the white Hom and the tree of all seeds in the middle of the sea Vourukasha.

**10.** All animals of the beneficent creation are created for the aid and protection of man and for the increase of the good creation. The noxious creatures — wolves, serpents, scorpions, lizards — all these are of the Destructive Spirit and exist to harm creation.
