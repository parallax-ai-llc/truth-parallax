---
title: "Chapter 5 – On the Conflict Between the Creations"
chapter: 5
---

**1.** As it is declared in the Religion: when the Destructive Spirit came upon the creation, each creation had a spiritual adversary who opposed it. The sky was opposed by the demon of evil thought, the water by the demon of drought, the earth by the demon of discord, the plant by the demon of withering, the Ox by the demon of greed, the fire by the demon of darkness.

**2.** He produced for every one of the Amesha Spentas a demon of wickedness. Opposite to Ohrmazd stood the Destructive Spirit himself; opposite to Vahman stood Akoman the demon of evil thought; opposite to Ardwahisht stood Indar; opposite to Shahrevar stood Savar; opposite to Spandarmad stood Taromat the demon of presumption and contempt; opposite to Hordad stood Tairev; opposite to Amurdad stood Zairicha.

**3.** The other demons were created by the Destructive Spirit — demons of wrath, demons of sloth, demons of greed, demons of envy, demons of pain, demons of hunger, demons of thirst, demons of shame, demons of old age, demons of sorrow, demons of stench, demons of pollution.

**4.** They are many and prolific, the progeny of the evil one, that rush upon the creation of Ohrmazd. But the righteous creation fights against them, and through the power of the sacred word and through good thoughts, good words, and good deeds, they shall be overcome.

**5.** The Destructive Spirit could produce nothing that was good. All that he made was evil, dark, and stinking. But Ohrmazd's creation was good and fragrant, light and pure.

**6.** So the two creations were mingled in the world, and the battle continued unceasingly between the good and the evil. And this mingling shall endure until the Renovation, when the Destructive Spirit shall be rendered powerless, and the good creation shall be purified and made immortal.
