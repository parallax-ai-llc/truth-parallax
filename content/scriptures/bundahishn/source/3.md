---
title: "Chapter 3 – The Fashioning of the Lights"
chapter: 3
---

**1.** Ohrmazd created the lights between the sky and the earth. The stars of the fixed firmament and those that are not fixed, and then the moon, and then the sun.

**2.** First he created the celestial sphere, and the stars of the fixed firmament are attached to it, especially these twelve constellations: the Ram, the Bull, the Twins, the Crab, the Lion, the Husk of Corn, the Balance, the Scorpion, the Bow, the Sea-Goat, the Bucket, and the Fish.

**3.** These twelve constellations he distributed into four quarters. Three constellations in each quarter he appointed, and he fixed for every constellation six hundred thousand small stars as helpers.

**4.** Over these constellations he appointed four chieftains, in four quarters, as generals and guardians. He appointed Tishtar as chieftain of the east, Sataves as chieftain of the south, Vanand as chieftain of the west, and Haftoring as chieftain of the north.

**5.** Above them all he appointed the Great One, Merag, which they call the Pole Star, as the chieftain over all the chieftains, in the middle of the sky, above the summit of Hara Berezaiti, around which the stars and moon and sun revolve.

**6.** He tied all the celestial beings and creation of the material world to the luminaries, so that when the Destructive Spirit should come, they might fight against him and his creation, and the creatures of the material world would be saved from the assault.

**7.** The moon and sun he placed in motion. The moon has its own light, which comes from a different source, and the sun which moves in brightness illumines all the creatures of Ohrmazd.

**8.** The first month belongs to the Ram. When Ohrmazd created the creatures, they were in the month of Fravardin, the day Ohrmazd, at noon, for at noon the affairs of the world are most active.

**9.** He created first the firmament, and its motion was appointed from that place where the sun was created. For the sun was in the constellation of the Ram when he fashioned the creation.

**10.** As a fortress which men build over against an enemy so that they may not make an attack from that quarter — so all the stars are created as a fortress against the Destructive Spirit, the planets, and the other demons.
