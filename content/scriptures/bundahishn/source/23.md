---
title: "Chapter 23 – On the Nature of Lakes"
chapter: 23
---

**1.** On the nature of lakes it is declared in the Religion that the lake Chechast is in Atropatakan. It is said that the spring of that lake is connected to the sea of Vourukasha.

**2.** The lake Sovbar is in the land of Airan-vej. There the Kayansih, the seed of Zarathushtra, is preserved. From it the future mothers of the three Saoshyants shall conceive.

**3.** The lake Frazdanav is in Seistan. The lake Zarmanand is in Hamadan. The lake Husruv is in the land of the Khwarezmians.

**4.** Every lake has a purpose in the creation of Ohrmazd. Some provide water for the lands; some are the habitation of sacred fish; some preserve that which is needful for the Renovation.

**5.** The lake Sadves is in the land of the east. It is said that each drop of rain that falls into it produces ten drops in return, so that it is never exhausted.

**6.** In the lake Sovbar, the seed of Zarathushtra is guarded by ninety-nine thousand nine hundred and ninety-nine Fravashis of the righteous, so that no demon may approach or corrupt it.

**7.** At the appointed time, a maiden shall bathe in the lake Sovbar and shall conceive from the seed of Zarathushtra. She shall bring forth the first Saoshyant, Hushedar, who shall restore the Religion and advance the Renovation.

**8.** After him the second Saoshyant, Hushedar-Mah, shall be born in the same manner; and after him the third, Soshyant, who shall bring about the final Renovation and the defeat of the Destructive Spirit.
