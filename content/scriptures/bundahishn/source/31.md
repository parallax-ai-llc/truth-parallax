---
title: "Chapter 31 – On the Evil-Doing of Ahriman and the Demons"
chapter: 31
---

**1.** On the evil-doing of Ahriman it is declared in the Religion that the Destructive Spirit, after his stupor, rushed upon creation from every side.

**2.** He produced darkness to oppose the light, stench to oppose fragrance, disease to oppose health, death to oppose life, winter to oppose summer, and every manner of evil to oppose the good.

**3.** The demons, as they assaulted the creation, each attacked that which was appointed to him: Akoman attacked Good Thought, Indar attacked Righteousness, and so each demon fought against its opposing Amesha Spenta.

**4.** Ahriman himself entered the earth from below, piercing it like a snake, and the earth trembled and grew dark. He polluted the waters, blighted the plants, and struck the sole-created Ox, and struck Gayomard.

**5.** In every generation the demons continue their assault. They tempt men with lies, with greed, with anger, with sloth, with envy, and with all the vices.

**6.** But in every generation the Yazads and the Fravashis of the righteous oppose them. Through good thoughts, good words, and good deeds, the faithful resist the demons and advance the cause of Ohrmazd.

**7.** The power of the Destructive Spirit grows weaker with each age. In the beginning he was strongest, but as the Renovation approaches, his strength diminishes.

**8.** At the end he shall be utterly powerless, and the demons shall be destroyed, and creation shall be freed from their grip forever.
