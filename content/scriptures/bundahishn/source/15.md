---
title: "Chapter 15 – On the Nature of Sleep and Wakefulness"
chapter: 15
---

**1.** On the nature of sleep it is declared in the Religion that Ohrmazd created sleep for the protection of the body. When the body grows weary, the soul takes counsel with the spiritual beings.

**2.** Sleep is of the nature of death, as wakefulness is of the nature of life. When a man sleeps, the vital spirit remains in the body to guard it, but the soul rises and communes with the spiritual world.

**3.** The Destructive Spirit created nightmares and evil dreams to torment the sleeping man, and through fear and terror to weaken his soul.

**4.** But the Fravashi of the man and the guardian spirit watch over him during sleep, and they keep the demons at bay. When a man dreams of good things, it is the Fravashi that shows him these visions.

**5.** They say that a man should sleep the last third of the day and the first third of the night, and should rise before the sun to perform his prayers. He who rises before the sun gains merit.

**6.** Excessive sleep is of the demon of lethargy, Bushyasta, who is long-handed. She lies upon men and makes them sleep on, that they may not perform their duties and prayers.

**7.** When a man wakes, he should first recite the sacred words, and wash his hands and face, and tie on the sacred girdle, and offer praise to Ohrmazd. Then he should go about his duties.

**8.** The greatest wakefulness is that of the soul — to be ever alert to the promptings of Good Thought, to keep the Lie at bay, and to act with righteousness. This wakefulness is the true life.
