---
title: "Chapter 21 – On the Nature of Mountains"
chapter: 21
---

**1.** On the nature of mountains it is declared in the Religion that in the beginning the earth was smooth and without mountains. When the Destructive Spirit assailed the earth, mountains grew from the earth through the shaking.

**2.** First Mount Alburz grew up. It grew for eight hundred years, and it encircles the earth. Its summit reaches to the Endless Light.

**3.** From Alburz all other mountains grew, and they are connected to Alburz like the roots and branches of a tree. In number they are two thousand two hundred and forty-four mountains.

**4.** The Peak of Hara Berezaiti is on the summit of Alburz. Around it the sun, moon, and stars revolve. From it the Chinvat Bridge extends to the other world.

**5.** Mount Ushidarena is that from which the light of dawn comes forth, for the sun rises from behind it. It is connected to Alburz.

**6.** Mount Aparsen is in Khurasan. Mount Manusha is in Seistan. Mount Bakhtigan is in Pars. Each mountain has its own guardian spirit.

**7.** When the Destructive Spirit assaulted the earth, the mountains grew up as a defense, for within the mountains are veins of water and metal and precious stones, which are for the use of mankind.

**8.** At the Renovation, the mountains shall be leveled, and the earth shall again be smooth and without elevations, as it was in the beginning before the assault.
