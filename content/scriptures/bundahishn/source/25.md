---
title: "Chapter 25 – On Noxious Creatures"
chapter: 25
---

**1.** On the nature of noxious creatures it is declared in the Religion that all noxious creatures are of the creation of the Destructive Spirit. Not one of them was made by Ohrmazd.

**2.** The Destructive Spirit created them to destroy the good creation: to kill the beneficent animals, to harm the crops, to bite and sting mankind.

**3.** Of noxious creatures, the serpent is the chief. The Destructive Spirit created the serpent first, and from it many species arose: vipers, cobras, scorpions, poisonous spiders, and all venomous things.

**4.** The wolf is the chief of noxious animals that walk on four legs. He is the enemy of the sheep and the dog. The Destructive Spirit created him to prey upon the flocks.

**5.** The frog is created to pollute the waters. The locust is created to devour the grain. The fly is created to carry corruption and disease.

**6.** The ant that carries off grain is of the Destructive Spirit. So also the wasp, the mosquito, and the rat.

**7.** All these noxious creatures multiply rapidly, for the Destructive Spirit wished them to overrun the creation. But the beneficent animals, especially the dog and the cat and the birds of prey, were created to keep the noxious creatures in check.

**8.** It is the duty of every man to slay noxious creatures wherever he finds them. He who kills a snake or a scorpion or a frog gains merit equal to a righteous deed. He who allows noxious creatures to multiply serves the Destructive Spirit.

**9.** At the Renovation, all noxious creatures shall be destroyed. There shall be no serpent, no scorpion, no wolf, no frog. The earth shall be cleansed of all evil creatures, and only the beneficent animals shall remain.
