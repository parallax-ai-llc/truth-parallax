## Academic Reading

Academic study of this chapter examines the Bundahishn's treatment of varieties of animals as evidence of Zoroastrian social organization, ritual practice, and intellectual culture during the Sasanian and early Islamic periods. Scholars analyze the text's classifications and categories in relation to comparable systems in other ancient civilizations, assessing both indigenous Zoroastrian traditions and possible external influences.
