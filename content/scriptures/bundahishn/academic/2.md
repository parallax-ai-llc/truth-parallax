## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze the Bundahishn's account of Angra Mainyu's assault as a mythological elaboration of the Gathic doctrine of the two spirits (Yasna 30). The narrative is compared with other ancient combat myths — the Chaoskampf of Babylonian and Canaanite mythology, the Titanomachy of Greek myth — while noting the distinctive Zoroastrian feature that the combat is ethical rather than merely physical. The text's dualism is examined in relation to scholarly debates about the nature and development of Zoroastrian theological thought.
