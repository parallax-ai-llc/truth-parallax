## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of this chapter examines the Bundahishn's account of the creation of the wind in relation to comparable accounts in other ancient Near Eastern cosmogonies and Indo-Iranian mythological traditions. Scholars trace the text's concepts to Avestan sources while noting the influence of Babylonian, Greek, and Indian scientific and mythological traditions that entered Zoroastrian thought during the Achaemenid and Sasanian periods. The chapter's Pahlavi vocabulary is analyzed for evidence of both archaic and innovative elements.
