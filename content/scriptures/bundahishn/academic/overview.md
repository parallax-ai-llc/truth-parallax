Academic study of the Bundahishn encompasses Iranian linguistics, comparative mythology, history of science, and religious studies. The text is analyzed as a compilation that preserves materials from diverse periods — some reflecting Indo-Iranian cosmogonic traditions of considerable antiquity, others showing the influence of Hellenistic, Babylonian, and Indian scientific concepts that entered Zoroastrian thought during the Sasanian period.

Textual scholars compare the two recensions (Greater and Lesser Bundahishn) to trace the text's compositional history and identify later interpolations. The natural-historical sections are examined for their relationship to Aristotelian, Ptolemaic, and Indian astronomical and geographical knowledge. The eschatological sections are compared with Jewish, Christian, and Islamic apocalyptic literature to trace the complex web of mutual influence.
