## Overview

The Bundahishn (Bundahišn, "Primal Creation" or "Original Creation") is one of the most important Zoroastrian texts in Pahlavi (Middle Persian), providing a comprehensive account of cosmogony, cosmography, natural history, and eschatology from the Zoroastrian perspective. Extant in two recensions — the Greater or Iranian Bundahishn (36 chapters) and the Lesser or Indian Bundahishn (34 chapters) — the text was compiled in the 9th century CE from earlier Sasanian-era materials, many derived from lost sections of the Avesta.

## Structure

The Bundahishn unfolds as a cosmic narrative in 36 chapters, moving from creation through history to the end of time:

- **Chapters 1–11**: The creation of the spiritual and material worlds — sky, water, earth, plants, animals, fire, and humanity
- **Chapters 12–18**: Cosmography — the nature of celestial bodies, the earth's geography, rivers, seas, and vegetation
- **Chapters 19–24**: Natural history, human classification, sacred fires, and the ritual calendar
- **Chapters 25–28**: Legendary history and the genealogy of Zoroaster
- **Chapters 29–32**: Demonology and the battle between good and evil in nature
- **Chapters 33–36**: Eschatology — the signs of the end, the coming of the Saoshyant, the final renovation, and the eternal state

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Zoroastrian**: The primary cosmogonic and cosmographic text of the tradition
- **Academic**: An indispensable source for Zoroastrian studies, Iranian mythology, and comparative cosmogony
- **Comparative**: Reveals deep connections between Zoroastrian and Abrahamic creation and eschatological narratives
- **Protestant**: Illuminates the ancient Near Eastern context of biblical creation and end-times theology
- **Jewish**: Provides crucial context for understanding Persian influences on Jewish cosmology and eschatology
- **Islamic**: Documents the pre-Islamic Iranian worldview that shaped Persian Islamic culture

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Creation | The good creation of Ahura Mazda as an act of cosmic strategy against evil |
| Cosmic Struggle | The ongoing battle between Ahura Mazda and Angra Mainyu |
| Natural Order | The classification and spiritual significance of all created beings |
| Sacred History | The unfolding of divine purpose through legendary and historical time |
| Renovation | The final defeat of evil and the restoration of creation to its original perfection |
