## Overview

The Drasha d-Yahia holds special significance for interfaith dialogue because it centers on John the Baptist, a figure revered across Christianity, Islam, and Mandaeism. Interfaith engagement with this text invites participants from different traditions to encounter a familiar figure through unfamiliar eyes — to see John not as the forerunner of Christ or the prophet Yahya but as the supreme teacher of the Mandaean Light-religion. This encounter challenges assumptions about religious boundaries and reveals the complex, overlapping histories of Abrahamic and Gnostic traditions.

Interfaith readers approach the Drasha d-Yahia's polemical passages — particularly its critique of Christianity and Judaism — with sensitivity, understanding them as expressions of a minority community's need to define and defend its distinctive identity. The text's emphasis on baptism as a universal sacrament of purification and spiritual renewal offers common ground for dialogue about the meaning of water, purity, and spiritual rebirth across traditions. The Mandaean community's survival as a small, often persecuted minority makes engagement with the Drasha d-Yahia an exercise in honoring marginalized religious voices.
