## Interfaith Reading

Interfaith engagement with Drasha d-Yahia Section 9, "Yahia's Disputes with the World," creates space for encounter across religious boundaries through a shared interest in a figure revered by multiple traditions. This section's treatment of Yahia's Disputes with the World invites Christians, Muslims, Jews, and Mandaeans to sit together with a text that challenges the assumptions of each tradition — presenting familiar figures in unfamiliar light and revealing the complex, interwoven histories of the Abrahamic and Gnostic religious families.

Interfaith readers approach this section with particular sensitivity to the power dynamics of inter-religious interpretation: the Mandaean community, as one of the world's smallest and most endangered religious minorities, has rarely had the opportunity to have its scriptures read with the same respect and scholarly rigor accorded to the Bible or the Quran. Engaging the Drasha d-Yahia in an interfaith context means honoring the Mandaean community's self-understanding while also recognizing the text's value as a window into the religious diversity of the ancient Near East and as a source of spiritual insight for all seekers.

## Key Themes

- Shared prophetic figures across Mandaean, Christian, Islamic, and Jewish traditions
- The sacredness of water and baptism as common ground for dialogue
- Honoring minority religious voices in the interfaith conversation
- The challenge of reading polemical texts with mutual respect
- Discovering unexpected spiritual kinship across tradition boundaries

## Key Passages

> "Yahia prayed beside the flowing water and said: 'Praised be the Great Life, who planted the good in the midst of the world, who raised up the truthful and brought low the liars.'"

This prayer, with its universal themes of gratitude, truth, and divine justice, offers common ground for interfaith reflection. Participants from different traditions can recognize in Yahia's words echoes of their own prayers while also encountering the distinctive Mandaean emphasis on "the Great Life" and "living water" as primary symbols of the sacred.

## Commentary Tradition

Interfaith engagement with the Drasha d-Yahia is a relatively recent development, facilitated by the work of scholars and community leaders who bridge Mandaean studies and interreligious dialogue. Organizations dedicated to the preservation of Mandaean culture and religion have increasingly participated in interfaith forums, bringing texts like the Drasha d-Yahia to wider attention.
