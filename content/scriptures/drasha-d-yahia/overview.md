## Overview

The Drasha d-Yahia (Mandaic: "Teachings of John"), also known as the Book of John, is one of the most important scriptures of the Mandaean religion. This text presents the Mandaean account of John the Baptist (Yahia Yuhana), whom the Mandaeans revere as the last and greatest prophet sent from the World of Light. Unlike the Christian portrayal of John as a forerunner to Jesus, the Mandaean tradition presents Yahia as the supreme teacher in his own right, the true representative of *manda d-hiia* (knowledge of life).

The Drasha d-Yahia recounts Yahia's miraculous birth, his baptismal ministry in the Jordan River, his confrontations with Jewish and Christian authorities, and his eventual martyrdom. The text is notable for its polemical stance against both Judaism and Christianity, particularly in its depiction of Jesus (Yeshu) as a false messiah who distorted Yahia's true teachings. Interwoven with the narrative are cosmological teachings, ethical exhortations, and liturgical prayers that connect the life of Yahia to the broader Mandaean understanding of the cosmic struggle between Light and Darkness.

Written in Mandaic, the text preserves traditions that scholars believe may reach back to the early centuries of the Common Era, making it an invaluable source for understanding the religious landscape of late antique Mesopotamia and the Jordan Valley.

## Structure

The Drasha d-Yahia is organized into twelve main sections:

1. **Birth and Early Life**: Yahia's miraculous birth and calling
2. **Baptismal Ministry**: Teaching and baptizing along the Jordan
3. **Ethical Teachings**: Instructions for righteous Mandaean living
4. **Polemical Sections**: Confrontations with Jewish priests and Jesus
5. **Cosmological Teachings**: The nature of the World of Light and creation
6. **Miracles and Signs**: Yahia's divine powers
7. **Martyrdom**: Yahia's death and his soul's ascent
8. **Eschatological Prayers**: Final teachings and hopes for the community

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Mandaean**: The primary narrative scripture for understanding the life and teachings of the greatest prophet
- **Academic**: A crucial text for studying Gnostic Christianity, Baptist movements, and Aramaic traditions
- **Comparative**: Offers a unique non-Christian perspective on John the Baptist and early religious polemics
- **Gnostic**: Contains distinctive Gnostic cosmology and soteriology within a baptismal framework
- **Jewish**: Illuminates Second Temple period baptist sects and their relationship to mainstream Judaism
- **Interfaith**: Presents an alternative scriptural voice on figures shared across Abrahamic traditions
