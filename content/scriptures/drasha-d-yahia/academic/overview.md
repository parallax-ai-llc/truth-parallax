## Overview

Academic study of the Drasha d-Yahia focuses on the text's significance for understanding the religious diversity of late antiquity, the history of Baptist movements in the Jordan Valley and Mesopotamia, and the development of Mandaean literary traditions. Mark Lidzbarski's critical edition and German translation (1915) remains foundational, though subsequent scholars have refined the understanding of the text's philology, dating, and compositional layers.

Scholars debate the historical relationship between the Drasha d-Yahia's portrait of John the Baptist and the New Testament accounts. While the Mandaean text clearly represents a later literary composition, some elements — particularly the baptismal theology and the polemical stance toward Jerusalem-centered religion — may preserve authentic traditions from first-century Baptist communities. The text's Mandaic language is studied for its linguistic features, and its anti-Christian passages are analyzed as evidence for Mandaean-Christian interaction in the Sassanian and early Islamic periods.
