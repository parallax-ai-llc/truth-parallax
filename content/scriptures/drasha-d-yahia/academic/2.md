## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of Drasha d-Yahia Section 2, "Yahia's Baptism and Calling," examines the text through the lenses of philology, history of religions, and literary criticism. Scholars scrutinize the Mandaic language for clues about dating and provenance, noting both archaic features that may reflect early traditions and later linguistic strata indicating redactional activity. This section's relationship to parallel accounts in the New Testament, Josephus, and other ancient sources is carefully assessed to distinguish historical kernel from theological elaboration.

The literary structure of this section reveals characteristic features of Mandaean narrative composition: the interweaving of biographical narrative with cosmological teaching, the use of formulaic prayers and hymns, and the rhetorical deployment of dialogue to convey theological points. Scholars debate whether the traditions preserved here originated in the Jordan Valley in the first century CE or developed in the Mesopotamian diaspora, or — most likely — represent a layered composition incorporating elements of both settings.

## Key Themes

- Philological analysis of the Mandaic text and its linguistic strata

## Key Passages

> "And Yahia went to the banks of the Jordan, and the waters rose up to greet him, and the *uthras* descended from the World of Light to witness."

Scholars note the characteristic Mandaean literary device of cosmic participation in earthly prophetic events. The rising waters and descending *uthras* serve as literary markers of Yahia's prophetic legitimacy, paralleling similar techniques in Jewish, Christian, and Manichaean hagiographic traditions.

## Commentary Tradition

Key academic studies include Lidzbarski's critical edition and translation, Rudolph's analysis of Mandaean literary forms, Buckley's sociological and textual studies, and Häberl's linguistic research. Recent scholarship increasingly emphasizes the need to read the Drasha d-Yahia within its own Mandaean context rather than merely as a source for reconstructing Christian origins.
