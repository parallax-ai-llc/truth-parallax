## Overview

Within the framework of Gnostic studies, the Drasha d-Yahia represents one of the few surviving scriptures of a living Gnostic tradition. Unlike the Nag Hammadi texts, which are the products of extinct communities, the Drasha d-Yahia continues to be read and revered by the Mandaean community. Gnostic interpreters focus on the text's characteristic themes: the fundamental dualism between the World of Light and the world of darkness, the soul's entrapment in material existence, and the role of divine knowledge (*manda*) in achieving liberation.

Yahia's role in the Drasha d-Yahia corresponds to the Gnostic archetype of the celestial revealer — the divine messenger who descends into the material world to awaken souls to their true origin in the Light. His baptismal practice is understood not merely as ritual purification but as a sacramental act that reconnects the soul with its heavenly source. The text's polemic against Jesus and the Jewish authorities reflects the Gnostic tendency to critique institutional religion as a distortion of authentic spiritual knowledge.
