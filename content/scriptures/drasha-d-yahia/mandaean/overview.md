## Overview

For the Mandaean community, the Drasha d-Yahia is the definitive account of their greatest prophet, Yahia Yuhana (John the Baptist). This text is not historical biography but sacred narrative — a revelation from the World of Light that establishes Yahia's supreme prophetic authority and the eternal validity of the Mandaean way of life. Mandaean priests (*tarmide*) study and recite passages from the Drasha d-Yahia during baptismal ceremonies, connecting each *masbuta* performed today to the original baptisms performed by Yahia in the Jordan River.

The Mandaean reading emphasizes Yahia's role as the final link in a chain of prophets stretching back to Adam (*Adam Kasia*, the hidden Adam), through Seth (*Shitil*), and Noah (*Nuh*). Yahia's confrontation with the false teachings of Judaism and Christianity is understood as the definitive prophetic witness to the truth of *manda d-hiia* — the saving knowledge that flows from the World of Light. The text's polemical passages are received not as mere historical disputes but as eternal warnings against the distortions of truth that threaten the Mandaean community in every age.
