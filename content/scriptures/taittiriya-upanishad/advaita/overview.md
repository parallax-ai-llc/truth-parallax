## Overview

Shankara's commentary on the Taittiriya Upanishad provides the classic Advaita analysis of the five sheaths (*pancha kosha*). Each sheath — food, vital breath, mind, intellect, and bliss — is systematically shown to be "not the self" because it is an object of awareness, subject to change, and dependent on conditions. The true self is the witness-consciousness that illuminates all five sheaths while being identical with none of them. This analysis became the template for Advaita meditation practice (*atma vichara*).

## Theological Emphases

The definition "satyam jnanam anantam brahma" ("Brahman is truth, knowledge, infinite," 2.1) is Shankara's key text for establishing that Brahman is pure consciousness without qualities. These three terms are not attributes of Brahman but definitions by mutual implication — each excludes what Brahman is not. The bliss sheath (*anandamaya kosha*) is particularly important: Shankara argues it is still a sheath (an object of awareness), not the self itself, against those who identify Brahman with the bliss experienced in deep sleep. The self transcends even bliss as ordinarily experienced.
