## Overview

Madhva's interpretation of the Taittiriya Upanishad emphasizes that the five sheaths describe real layers of the soul's embodied existence, not illusions to be negated. Each sheath is sustained by God's power, and the innermost bliss sheath points to Vishnu as the source of all joy. Unlike Shankara, Madhva holds that the bliss sheath is not merely a covering but genuinely reflects the soul's participation in divine bliss — though this bliss remains infinitely less than God's own.

## Theological Emphases

The definition "satyam jnanam anantam brahma" is read by Madhva as describing Vishnu's essential attributes: He is supremely real, omniscient, and infinite. The scale of bliss presented in Chapter 2 — where each successive order of beings experiences exponentially greater joy — culminates in Brahman's infinite bliss, demonstrating the absolute hierarchy that pervades reality. Bhrigu's progressive discovery (Chapter 3) teaches that deeper meditation reveals increasingly subtle aspects of God's nature, with bliss (*ananda*) as His highest characteristic accessible to the devoted seeker.
