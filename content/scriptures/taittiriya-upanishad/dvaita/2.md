Madhva reads "satyam jnanam anantam brahma" as describing three of Vishnu's infinite attributes: He is supremely real (not illusory), omniscient (not limited in knowledge), and infinite (not bounded by space, time, or quality). Unlike Shankara, Madhva treats these as genuine attributes of a personal God, not mere definitions by negation. Vishnu really possesses truth, knowledge, and infinity as aspects of His nature.

The five sheaths are real layers of the soul's embodied existence, each sustained by Vishnu's power. The bliss sheath is a genuine experience of divine joy, not an illusion to be transcended. The graduated scale of bliss (2.8) establishes the hierarchy (*taratamya*) central to Dvaita philosophy: there are real differences in the degree of bliss experienced by different orders of beings, with Vishnu's bliss at the unsurpassable summit. The soul at liberation experiences God's bliss in a derivative, dependent way — genuinely sharing in divine joy while remaining ontologically distinct from the Lord.
