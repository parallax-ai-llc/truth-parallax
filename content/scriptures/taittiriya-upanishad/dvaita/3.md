Madhva reads Bhrigu's progressive discovery as demonstrating that deeper meditation reveals increasingly profound aspects of Vishnu's nature. The identification of Brahman with food shows God as the sustainer; with breath, as the life-giver; with mind, as the source of thought; with intellect, as the fount of wisdom; with bliss, as the supreme joy. Each level is a real aspect of Vishnu's nature, not a sheath to be discarded. The progression reveals an ever-richer understanding of God, culminating in the recognition of His essential nature as infinite bliss.

The declaration "I am food" (3.10) is read not as a claim of identity with the Absolute but as the soul's recognition of its role in God's cosmic economy. The soul is "food" in the sense that it is entirely dependent on God and exists for God's purposes. The ecstatic tone expresses the devotee's joy at discovering their proper place in the divine order — not the joy of becoming God, but the joy of knowing oneself as God's servant, sustained by His bliss and participating in His creative play.
