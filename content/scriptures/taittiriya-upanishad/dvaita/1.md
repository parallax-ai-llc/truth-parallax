Madhva reads the Shiksha Valli as establishing the practical and ethical framework within which devotion to Vishnu takes root. The phonetic instruction ensures the devotee can properly recite the Vedas and Vishnu's names, since incorrect recitation is considered an offense against the Lord. The meditations on cosmic correspondences (1.3-7) demonstrate Vishnu's ordering presence at every level of reality — from the individual body to the cosmic totality.

The convocation address (1.11) is read as divine instruction through the guru: the commandments to speak truth, practice dharma, and honor one's teachers are not merely ethical suggestions but expressions of Vishnu's will for His devotees. The remarkable tolerance of the closing advice — "if in doubt, follow the practice of thoughtful, devout persons" — demonstrates that devotion admits of variety in practice while maintaining unity in its object (Vishnu). The chapter establishes that right knowledge begins with right conduct.
