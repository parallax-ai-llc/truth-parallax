## Overview

Shaiva interpreters read the Taittiriya Upanishad's five-sheath doctrine as mapping the layers of manifestation through which Paramashiva expresses Himself. Each sheath — from the gross food-body to the subtle bliss-body — represents a level of Shiva's creative self-concealment. The spiritual journey is one of progressive recognition, piercing through each layer to discover that the bliss at the core is Shiva's own nature of consciousness-bliss (*chidananda*).

## Theological Emphases

The Taittiriya's ecstatic declaration "anando brahmeti vyajanat" ("he knew bliss as Brahman," 3.6) resonates with the Kashmir Shaiva teaching that ultimate reality is *ananda* — not mere absence of suffering but the dynamic, creative joy of consciousness recognizing itself. The convocation address's instruction to treat the teacher as God aligns with the Shaiva guru tradition, where the teacher is an embodiment of Shiva's grace. The five sheaths correspond to the Shaiva tattvas (categories of existence) through which consciousness progressively manifests as the material world.
