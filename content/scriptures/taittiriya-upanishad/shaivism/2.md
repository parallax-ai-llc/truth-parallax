The Brahmananda Valli's definition of Brahman as "truth, knowledge, infinite" resonates with the Shaiva understanding of Shiva as *sat-chit-ananda* (being-consciousness-bliss). The five sheaths describe the progressive self-concealment of Shiva-consciousness through layers of manifestation: from the bliss of pure awareness through intellect, mind, and vital breath to the gross food-body. The spiritual path is a journey inward — peeling back each sheath to recognize the luminous consciousness at the core.

The graduated scale of bliss (2.8) demonstrates that ordinary happiness is a faint reflection of Shiva's infinite *ananda*. Each level of celestial joy represents a less contracted form of the same consciousness-bliss that is Shiva's nature. The realization "He knew bliss as Brahman" (culminating the Bhrigu narrative in Chapter 3 but anticipated here) is the moment of *pratyabhijna* — recognizing that the bliss at the core of one's being is Shiva's own joy, not something separate from universal consciousness.
