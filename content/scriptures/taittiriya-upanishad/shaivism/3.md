Bhrigu's progressive meditation through five levels — food, breath, mind, intellect, bliss — maps the Shaiva practice of *tattva-shuddhi* (purification of categories), in which the practitioner systematically transcends each level of manifestation to recognize the pure consciousness that underlies all. Each identification that Bhrigu achieves and then surpasses represents the dissolution of a limiting contraction (*mala*) that conceals Shiva's nature.

The final realization of bliss (*ananda*) as Brahman is the moment of recognition (*pratyabhijna*) — Bhrigu discovers that the consciousness-bliss at the core of his being is Shiva's own nature. The ecstatic utterance "I am food" (3.10) expresses the Shaiva insight that the liberated being recognizes itself as both the consumed and the consumer — consciousness absorbing all experience into itself while simultaneously offering itself to the play of manifestation. This is the *spanda* (vibration) of Shiva: the pulsation between self-expression and self-absorption.
