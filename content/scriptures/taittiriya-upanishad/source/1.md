---
title: "Taittiriya Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "taittiriya-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. May Mitra be propitious to us, and Varuna, Aryaman, Indra, Brihaspati, and the wide-striding Vishnu. Adoration to Brahman! Adoration to thee, O Vayu! Thou indeed art the visible Brahman. I will declare thee as the visible Brahman. I will declare the right. I will declare the true. May it protect me. May it protect the teacher. Om. Peace, peace, peace.

**2** Om. Let us explain Siksha, the doctrine of pronunciation. It deals with sound, pitch, quantity, force, modulation, and combination. Thus much for the chapter on pronunciation.

**3** May glory come to both of us together. May the light of Brahman shine alike for both of us. Now let us explain the Upanishad on Combination under five heads: with respect to the worlds, with respect to the heavenly lights, with respect to knowledge, with respect to progeny, and with respect to the Self.

**4** With regard to the worlds: the earth is the first element, heaven the last element, the ether is the union, and the air is the medium. With regard to the heavenly lights: fire is the first element, the sun the last element, water is the union, lightning the medium. With regard to knowledge: the teacher is the first element, the pupil the last element, knowledge is the union, instruction the medium. With regard to progeny: the mother is the first element, the father the last element, the offspring is the union, procreation the medium. With regard to the Self: the lower jaw is the first element, the upper jaw the last element, speech is the union, the tongue the medium.

**5** These are the great combinations. He who knows these combinations, as here explained, becomes united with offspring, cattle, the light of Brahman, food, and the heavenly world.

**6** He who is there in the sun and here in the right eye — that one is established in the verses, in the sacrifice, in the duties, in Brahman, in Indra. This self, indeed, is the self of both. He who knows this goes from this world, and having attained this self made of food, having attained this self made of breath, having attained this self made of mind, having attained this self made of understanding, having attained this self made of bliss, travels over these worlds, eating what he wishes, assuming what form he wishes, and sits singing this song:

**7** Oh wonderful! Oh wonderful! Oh wonderful! I am food! I am food! I am food! I am the food-eater! I am the food-eater! I am the food-eater! I am the combining agent! I am the combining agent! I am the combining agent! I am the first-born of the world-order, earlier than the gods, in the navel of immortality! He who gives me away, he alone preserves me. He who eats food — I, as food, eat him. I have overcome the whole world. He who knows this, knows. My light is like the sun. This is the Upanishad.

**8** May He protect us both. May He nourish us both. May we both acquire strength. May our study be brilliant. May we not cavil at each other. Om. Peace, peace, peace.

**9** He who knows Brahman attains the highest. On this the following verse is recorded: He who knows Brahman which is reality, knowledge, and infinity, hidden in the cave of the heart and in the highest heaven, he enjoys all desires simultaneously, being one with the omniscient Brahman.

**10** From that Self sprang ether; from ether, air; from air, fire; from fire, water; from water, earth; from earth, herbs; from herbs, food; from food, the person. That person is indeed the essence of food. This indeed is his head; this is the right arm; this is the left arm; this is the trunk; this is the tail which is his support.

**11** From food are produced all creatures which dwell on earth. Then they live by food, and in the end they return to food. For food is the eldest born of all beings, and therefore it is called the medicine for all.

**12** Those who worship food as Brahman obtain all food. For food is the eldest born of all beings, and therefore it is called the medicine for all. From food all creatures are produced; by food, when born, they grow. Food is eaten by beings and food also eats beings; therefore it is called food.
