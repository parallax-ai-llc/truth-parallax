---
title: "Taittiriya Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "taittiriya-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** From bliss all these beings are born, by bliss they all live, and into bliss they all return.

**2** He who knows the bliss of Brahman, whence all words, together with the mind, turn away, unable to reach it — he never fears.

**3** He does not distress himself with the thought: Why did I not do what is good? Why did I do what is evil? He who thus knows these two, frees himself. He who knows thus, frees himself. This is the Upanishad.

**4** Bhrigu Varuni went to his father Varuna, saying: Sir, teach me Brahman. He told him this, namely: food, breath, the eye, the ear, the mind, speech. Then he said to him: That from which these beings are born, that by which, when born, they live, that into which they enter at their death, try to know that. That is Brahman.

**5** He performed penance. Having performed penance, he perceived that food is Brahman. For from food these beings are produced; by food, when born, they live; and into food they enter at their death. Having perceived this, he went again to his father Varuna, saying: Sir, teach me Brahman. He said to him: Try to know Brahman by penance, for penance is Brahman.

**6** He performed penance. Having performed penance, he perceived that breath is Brahman. For from breath these beings are born; by breath, when born, they live; into breath they enter at their death. Having perceived this, he went again to his father Varuna, saying: Sir, teach me Brahman. He said to him: Try to know Brahman by penance, for penance is Brahman.

**7** He performed penance. Having performed penance, he perceived that mind is Brahman. For from the mind these beings are born; by the mind, when born, they live; into the mind they enter at their death. Having perceived this, he went again to his father Varuna, saying: Sir, teach me Brahman. He said to him: Try to know Brahman by penance, for penance is Brahman.

**8** He performed penance. Having performed penance, he perceived that understanding is Brahman. For from understanding these beings are born; by understanding, when born, they live; into understanding they enter at their death. Having perceived this, he went again to his father Varuna, saying: Sir, teach me Brahman. He said to him: Try to know Brahman by penance, for penance is Brahman.

**9** He performed penance. Having performed penance, he perceived that bliss is Brahman. For from bliss these beings are born; by bliss, when born, they live; into bliss they enter at their death. This is the knowledge of Bhrigu and of Varuna, exalted in the highest heaven. He who knows this becomes exalted. He becomes rich in food and able to eat food. He becomes great by offspring, cattle, and the splendour of sacred knowledge. He becomes great by fame.

**10** Let him never abuse food; that is the rule. Breath is food. The body is the food-eater. The body rests on breath. Breath rests on the body. Thus food rests on food. He who knows this food resting on food is established. He becomes rich in food and able to eat food. He becomes great by offspring, cattle, and the splendour of sacred knowledge. He becomes great by fame.
