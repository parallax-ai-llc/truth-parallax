---
title: "Taittiriya Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "taittiriya-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** He who knows Brahman attains the highest. Different from this self consisting of food, but within it, is the self consisting of breath. By that this is filled. It too has the shape of a man. Like the human shape of the former is the human shape of the latter. Prana is its head. Vyana is its right arm. Apana is its left arm. Ether is its trunk. The earth is its tail, its support.

**2** The gods breathe after breath, so do men and cattle. Breath is the life of beings. Therefore it is called the life of all. Those who worship Brahman as breath attain a full life. For breath is the life of all beings, and therefore it is called the life of all.

**3** Different from this self consisting of breath, but within it, is the self consisting of mind. By that this is filled. It too has the shape of a man. Like the human shape of the former is the human shape of the latter. The Yajur-Veda is its head. The Rig-Veda is its right arm. The Sama-Veda is its left arm. The teaching is its trunk. The hymns of Atharva and Angirasa are its tail, its support.

**4** He from whom all speech turns back, together with the mind, not having reached it — he who knows the bliss of that Brahman fears nothing at any time.

**5** Different from this self consisting of mind, but within it, is the self consisting of understanding. By that this is filled. It too has the shape of a man. Like the human shape of the former is the human shape of the latter. Faith is its head. Righteousness is its right arm. Truth is its left arm. Yoga is its trunk. The great intellect is its tail, its support.

**6** Understanding directs the sacrifice, and understanding also directs all actions. All the gods worship understanding as Brahman, the eldest. If a man knows understanding as Brahman, and if he does not swerve from it, he leaves behind in the body all evils and attains all his desires.

**7** Different from this self consisting of understanding, but within it, is the self consisting of bliss. By that this is filled. It too has the shape of a man. Like the human shape of the former is the human shape of the latter. Joy is its head. Delight is its right arm. Great delight is its left arm. Bliss is its trunk. Brahman is its tail, its support.

**8** If anyone knows Brahman as non-existing, he becomes himself non-existing. If anyone knows that Brahman exists, him they know as existing. The embodied self of this bliss is the same as that of the former.

**9** Thereupon the following questions arise: Does anyone who knows not, after departing this world, ever go there? Or does he who knows, after departing this world, go there? He desired: May I be many, may I grow forth. He brooded over himself. Having brooded, he created all this, whatever there is. Having created, he entered into it. Having entered into it, he became what is manifest and what is not manifest, what is defined and what is not defined, what has support and what has not, what is conscious and what is not conscious, what is real and what is false. The True became all this. Therefore they call it the True. On this there is this verse.
