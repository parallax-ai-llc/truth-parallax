The Brahmananda Valli has received more scholarly attention than perhaps any other section of the Taittiriya. The definition "satyam jnanam anantam brahma" (2.1) represents one of the earliest attempts at a systematic definition of the absolute in Indian philosophy. Scholars debate whether the three terms were originally intended as attributes, definitions, or something else — a question with significant implications for understanding the development of Indian metaphysics.

The five-sheath doctrine represents a sophisticated model of the human person that some scholars (Deussen, Nakamura) have compared with early Samkhya categories and Buddhist aggregates (*skandhas*). The graduated scale of bliss (2.8), which multiplies human joy by factors of one hundred to arrive at progressively higher celestial joys, has been analyzed as an early form of mathematical-cosmological thinking. Whether this scheme is original to the Taittiriya or borrowed from an earlier source is debated.
