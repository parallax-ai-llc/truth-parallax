The Bhrigu Valli narrates the sage Bhrigu's progressive discovery of Brahman under his father Varuna's guidance. Instructed to discover Brahman through meditation (*tapas*), Bhrigu successively identifies Brahman with food, vital breath, mind, intellect, and finally bliss (*ananda*). Vaishnavas read this as the devotee's progressive deepening of understanding — from recognizing Vishnu's presence in physical sustenance to realizing that Vishnu's own nature is infinite bliss.

The ecstatic declaration at the chapter's conclusion — "Oh wonderful! Oh wonderful! Oh wonderful! I am food! I am food! I am food!" (3.10) — is read by Vaishnavas not as the dissolution of the self into an impersonal principle but as the soul's rapturous recognition of its complete participation in Vishnu's creative economy. The soul is "food" in the sense that it is completely given over to God's purposes, sustaining and being sustained within the divine order. This is *prapatti* — total surrender to the Lord.
