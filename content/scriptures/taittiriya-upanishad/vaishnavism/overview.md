## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters regard the Taittiriya Upanishad's teaching on the five sheaths (*pancha kosha*) as describing the soul's progressive layers of connection to Vishnu. The innermost sheath — the bliss sheath (*anandamaya kosha*) — is identified not with impersonal Brahman but with Vishnu-Narayana, who is the ultimate source and ground of all joy. Ramanuja argues that the bliss described here is the infinite bliss of the Lord Himself, in which the devotee participates through grace.

## Theological Emphases

The Taittiriya's definition of Brahman as "satyam jnanam anantam" ("truth, knowledge, infinite," 2.1) is read by Vaishnavas as describing Vishnu's essential attributes — He is eternally true, omniscient, and limitless. The convocation address (Chapter 1), with its injunctions to speak truth, practice dharma, and honor one's teachers, aligns with the Vaishnava emphasis on ethical conduct as an expression of devotion. Bhrigu's progressive discovery of Brahman through food, breath, mind, intellect, and finally bliss (Chapter 3) is read as the devotee's journey toward realizing Vishnu's supreme nature.
