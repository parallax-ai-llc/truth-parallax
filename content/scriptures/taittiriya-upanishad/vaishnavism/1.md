The Shiksha Valli opens with prayers for auspiciousness and proceeds to teach phonetics, meditation on Om, and ethical conduct. Vaishnava interpreters read the invocatory prayers — "May Mitra be propitious, may Varuna be propitious" — as appeals to Vishnu in His various cosmic functions. The detailed instruction on phonetics (*shiksha*) is understood as preparation for the proper recitation of Vishnu's sacred names, since correct pronunciation is essential for the efficacy of devotional chanting.

The celebrated convocation address (1.11) — "Speak the truth. Practice dharma. Do not neglect your study. Give to the teacher his fee and do not cut off the line of progeny" — is read as the foundation of Vaishnava householder life. The instruction "Do not swerve from truth, do not swerve from dharma" establishes that ethical living is the indispensable context for devotion. The address's remarkable conclusion — "If in doubt about conduct, follow the practice of Brahmanas who are thoughtful, devout, and devoted to dharma" — affirms the authority of the devotional *sampradaya* (tradition).
