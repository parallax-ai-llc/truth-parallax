Bhrigu's guided discovery — meditating on progressively subtler realities until reaching the ultimate — enacts a pattern of spiritual inquiry found across traditions. Plato's *Symposium* describes the soul's ascent from love of particular beautiful bodies to the vision of Beauty itself. The Buddhist *jhana* progression moves from coarse to refined states of consciousness. The Sufi *maqamat* (stations) describe the seeker's progressive purification from worldly attachment to divine intimacy.

The ecstatic conclusion "I am food!" resonates with mystical utterances across traditions that express the dissolution of ordinary boundaries between self and world. The Sufi *shath* (ecstatic utterance) of Hallaj — "I am the Truth" — the Zen Buddhist's spontaneous expression of *satori*, and the Pentecostal experience of being "filled with the Spirit" all share the quality of a consciousness that has broken through its ordinary limits and recognizes itself as participating in something vastly larger.
