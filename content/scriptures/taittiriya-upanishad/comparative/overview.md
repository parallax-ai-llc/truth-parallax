## Overview

The Taittiriya Upanishad's five-sheath doctrine presents a model of the human person that invites comparison with layered anthropologies across traditions. The progression from food-body to bliss-body resembles Neoplatonic emanation theory, the Kabbalistic four worlds (*olam*), and the Buddhist distinction between form and formless realms. In each case, the innermost or highest level is identified with the divine or transcendent ground.

## Comparative Themes

The convocation address (Chapter 1) — urging truth, dharma, and respect for teachers — resonates with wisdom literature worldwide, from Proverbs in the Hebrew Bible to Confucian instruction and Stoic ethical pedagogy. The ecstatic declaration "Aha! I am food! I am food!" (3.10) has been compared with eucharistic theology in Christianity and the Sufi concept of *fana* — the mystic consumed by and becoming one with the divine. The graduated scale of bliss anticipates Dante's ascending circles of paradise and the Pure Land Buddhist concept of progressive stages of enlightenment.
