## Historical Context

Section 41, dated to approximately 1831-1832 and associated with Kirtland, Ohio, and Independence, Missouri, belongs to the Missouri-Kirtland period of dual church headquarters. Historical-critical scholars situate this revelation within the specific social, political, and religious circumstances of its production, examining both the immediate occasion for the revelation and the broader cultural forces shaping its language and concerns.

## Source Criticism and Compositional Analysis

The text of Section 41 shows dependence on biblical language and imagery, a characteristic feature identified by Philip Barlow in *Mormons and the Bible*. The language of divine calling echoes biblical call narratives (Isaiah 6, Jeremiah 1, Acts 9) while adapting them to an early American context. Form-critical analysis identifies this as a commissioning genre.

## Form Criticism and Sitz im Leben

Form-critical analysis identifies this section within the genre of prophetic oracle adapted to the institutional needs of a new religious movement. The Sitz im Leben encompasses both the immediate community context and the wider currents of American religious culture in the 1831-1832 period.

## Redaction History

Comparison of manuscript sources (preserved in the Joseph Smith Papers) with the 1833 Book of Commandments and the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants reveals editorial changes consistent with the evolving theological and institutional needs of the early church. Robin Scott Jensen and others have documented these textual variants, which provide evidence of the kind of redactional process familiar from biblical criticism.
