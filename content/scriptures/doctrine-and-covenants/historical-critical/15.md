## Historical Context

Section 15 was received in June 1829 for John Whitmer, brother of David Whitmer. It is notably brief and nearly identical to Section 16 (addressed to Peter Whitmer Jr.), with the two sections forming a matched pair.

## Source Criticism and Form Criticism

The virtual identity of sections 15 and 16 — differing only in the recipient's name — represents the most extreme example of the formulaic revelatory template in the D&C. This pattern has been analyzed from two perspectives: critics cite it as evidence of mechanical composition, while defenders read it as evidence that the same divine message applied equally to both brothers.

## Sitz im Leben

The Whitmer family's hospitality was critical to the completion of the Book of Mormon. The revelations to multiple Whitmer family members (David in Section 14, John here, Peter Jr. in Section 16) reflect the social network that supported the translation project.

## Historical Evidence

John Whitmer later served as church historian (per Section 47) and was one of the Eight Witnesses to the Book of Mormon. His subsequent excommunication in 1838 and refusal to return church records raise questions about the long-term effectiveness of revelatory commissioning as an institutional binding mechanism.
