## Historical Context

Section 22, dated to approximately 1830-1831 and associated with Fayette, New York, and Kirtland, Ohio, belongs to the initial organization and expansion of the early church. Historical-critical scholars situate this revelation within the specific social, political, and religious circumstances of its production, examining both the immediate occasion for the revelation and the broader cultural forces shaping its language and concerns.

## Source Criticism and Compositional Analysis

The text of Section 22 shows dependence on biblical language and imagery, a characteristic feature identified by Philip Barlow in *Mormons and the Bible*. Claims to divine authority are examined by scholars such as Jan Shipps within the broader context of antebellum American religious contestation, where multiple groups claimed exclusive access to authentic Christianity.

## Form Criticism and Sitz im Leben

Form-critical analysis identifies this section within the genre of prophetic oracle adapted to the institutional needs of a new religious movement. The Sitz im Leben encompasses both the immediate community context and the wider currents of American religious culture in the 1830-1831 period.

## Redaction History

Comparison of manuscript sources (preserved in the Joseph Smith Papers) with the 1833 Book of Commandments and the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants reveals editorial changes consistent with the evolving theological and institutional needs of the early church. Robin Scott Jensen and others have documented these textual variants, which provide evidence of the kind of redactional process familiar from biblical criticism.
