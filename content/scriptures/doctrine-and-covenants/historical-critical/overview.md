## Overview

Historical-Critical engagement with the Doctrine and Covenants brings distinctive interpretive methods and concerns to this foundational text. This approach illuminates dimensions of the text that may be less visible from other perspectives.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Source Criticism
Historical-critical scholars examine the Doctrine and Covenants's sources, identifying the literary, oral, and documentary materials that contributed to its composition.

### Redaction History
Analysis of the Doctrine and Covenants's editorial history reveals how the text was shaped by successive editors, compilers, and tradents over time.

### Social-Historical Context
Historical-critical interpretation situates the Doctrine and Covenants within its original social, economic, and political context, examining how these factors shaped the text's composition and reception.
