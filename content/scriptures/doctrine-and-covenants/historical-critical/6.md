## Historical Context

Section 6 was received in April 1829 when Oliver Cowdery arrived in Harmony, Pennsylvania, to serve as scribe. Historical-critical scholars examine Cowdery's background as a schoolteacher with possible connections to the Smiths through the Palmyra-Manchester community.

## Source Criticism

The revelation's promise that Cowdery possesses "the gift of Aaron" draws on Exodus while adapting priestly language to a new context. Richard Bushman notes the parallel structure with Section 4 (to Joseph Smith Sr.) and Section 11 (to Hyrum Smith), suggesting a formulaic revelatory template adapted to individual recipients. The phrase "in your mind and in your heart" as the mechanism of revelation echoes patristic and Quaker epistemologies of inner witness.

## Sitz im Leben

Cowdery's arrival was pivotal for the translation project, which had stalled. The revelation functions to bind Cowdery to the work through promises of spiritual gifts, a strategy that form-critical analysis identifies as covenant initiation. Contemporary letters and journals corroborate Cowdery's enthusiasm and the progress of translation after his arrival.

## Redaction History

The text underwent revisions between manuscript, Book of Commandments, and Doctrine and Covenants editions. Marquardt has documented changes that strengthened claims about Cowdery's pre-ordination awareness and clarified the nature of his spiritual gifts.
