## Historical Context

Section 14, received in June 1829, is directed to David Whitmer, who would become one of the Three Witnesses to the Book of Mormon. The revelation was received at the Peter Whitmer Sr. farm in Fayette, New York.

## Source Criticism

The text shares extensive formulaic language with sections 4, 6, 11, and 12, continuing the pattern of a standardized commissioning template. The distinctive element is the conditional promise of witnessing the plates: "if you will ask of the Father in my name, in faith believing, you shall receive the Holy Ghost, which giveth utterance." This links the witness experience to charismatic spiritual gifts rather than empirical observation.

## Sitz im Leben

David Whitmer's farm provided the location for completing the Book of Mormon translation after the move from Harmony, Pennsylvania. His family's hospitality was essential to the project, and the revelation promising him a witness role may be read as both spiritual calling and acknowledgment of material support.

## Historical Evidence

Whitmer maintained his testimony of the Three Witnesses experience throughout his life, even after leaving the church in 1838. His 1887 *Address to All Believers in Christ* provides a late but detailed account. Historical-critical scholars debate whether the experience was visionary (internal) or empirical (external), with the D&C's own language supporting the former.
