## Historical Context

Section 16, received in June 1829 for Peter Whitmer Jr., is the twin of Section 15 (to John Whitmer). The text is identical except for the name, making it a key piece of evidence in debates about the compositional nature of D&C revelations.

## Source Criticism

The identical language in sections 15 and 16 is the most compressed instance of what source critics identify across dozens of early D&C sections: a common textual substrate adapted for individual recipients. This phenomenon invites comparison with form-critical observations about template compositions in biblical literature, such as the standardized covenant formulae in Hittite treaty patterns.

## Sitz im Leben

Peter Whitmer Jr. would serve as one of the Six Elders at the church's organization on April 6, 1830, and later participated in the mission to the Lamanites. His early death in 1836 at age 27 means less documentary evidence survives about his response to this revelation.
