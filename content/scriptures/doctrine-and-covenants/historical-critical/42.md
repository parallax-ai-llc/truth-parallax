## Historical Context

Section 42, dated to approximately 1831-1832 and associated with Kirtland, Ohio, and Independence, Missouri, belongs to the Missouri-Kirtland period of dual church headquarters. Historical-critical scholars situate this revelation within the specific social, political, and religious circumstances of its production, examining both the immediate occasion for the revelation and the broader cultural forces shaping its language and concerns.

## Source Criticism and Compositional Analysis

The text of Section 42 shows dependence on biblical language and imagery, a characteristic feature identified by Philip Barlow in *Mormons and the Bible*. The law of consecration described here has been analyzed by Leonard Arrington as reflecting both communitarian ideals common in early American utopian movements (cf. Shakers, Rappites) and biblical precedents in Acts 2:44-45.

## Form Criticism and Sitz im Leben

Stewardship language mediates between full communalism and private property, a compromise that Arrington documented as evolving through practical failure of earlier, more radical economic experiments.

## Redaction History

Comparison of manuscript sources (preserved in the Joseph Smith Papers) with the 1833 Book of Commandments and the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants reveals editorial changes consistent with the evolving theological and institutional needs of the early church. Robin Scott Jensen and others have documented these textual variants, which provide evidence of the kind of redactional process familiar from biblical criticism.
