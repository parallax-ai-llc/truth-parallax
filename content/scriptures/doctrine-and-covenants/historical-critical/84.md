## Historical Context

Section 84, dated to approximately 1832-1834 and associated with Kirtland, Ohio, belongs to the Kirtland temple-building period. Historical-critical scholars situate this revelation within the specific social, political, and religious circumstances of its production, examining both the immediate occasion for the revelation and the broader cultural forces shaping its language and concerns.

## Source Criticism and Compositional Analysis

The text of Section 84 shows dependence on biblical language and imagery, a characteristic feature identified by Philip Barlow in *Mormons and the Bible*. The priesthood language in this section reflects evolving ecclesiology. Historical-critical scholars such as Gregory Prince have documented how priesthood concepts developed progressively within early Mormonism, with the Melchizedek/Aaronic distinction crystallizing only gradually. The text's priesthood claims must be read against contemporary Protestant debates about ministerial authority and apostolic succession.

## Form Criticism and Sitz im Leben

The concept of 'keys' of authority shows dependence on Matthew 16:19 while developing distinctive Mormon theological content. Prince's *Power from On High* traces how key-holding language expanded from simple ordination authority to a comprehensive hierarchy of delegated divine powers.

## Redaction History

Comparison of manuscript sources (preserved in the Joseph Smith Papers) with the 1833 Book of Commandments and the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants reveals editorial changes consistent with the evolving theological and institutional needs of the early church. Robin Scott Jensen and others have documented these textual variants, which provide evidence of the kind of redactional process familiar from biblical criticism.

## Theological Development

Claims to divine authority are examined by scholars such as Jan Shipps within the broader context of antebellum American religious contestation, where multiple groups claimed exclusive access to authentic Christianity.
