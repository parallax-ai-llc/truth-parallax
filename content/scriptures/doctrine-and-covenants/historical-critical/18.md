## Historical Context

Section 18, received in June 1829, establishes the foundation for the church's apostolic organization and articulates the doctrine of the worth of souls. Historical-critical scholars date the section's composition to the period immediately before the church's formal organization.

## Source Criticism

The section's language about the "worth of souls" (v. 10) draws on Luke 15 (the parables of the lost sheep and lost coin) while its organizational directives about the Twelve Apostles anticipate structures that would not be formalized until 1835. Scholars debate whether the apostolic material was part of the original revelation or represents later editorial expansion.

## Sitz im Leben

The directive to choose twelve apostles reflects the restorationist imperative to reconstitute New Testament church structures. The immediate context is the transition from a small translation project to an organized religious movement, a process that required institutional forms.

## Redaction History

Comparison with the Book of Commandments version reveals significant additions in the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants, particularly regarding the apostleship. These additions coincide with the actual calling of the Quorum of the Twelve in 1835, raising questions about whether revelatory text was updated to match institutional development.
