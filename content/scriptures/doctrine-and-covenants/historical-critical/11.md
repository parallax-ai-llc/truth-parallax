## Historical Context

Section 11 was received in May 1829, addressed to Hyrum Smith, Joseph's older brother. Historical-critical scholars note the near-identical language shared with sections 4, 6, and 12, suggesting a formulaic template adapted for different recipients.

## Source Criticism

The instruction to "seek not to declare my word, but first seek to obtain my word" reflects a practical concern: Hyrum's enthusiasm outpaced his preparation. Barlow notes the heavy biblical borrowing, with phrases drawn from Isaiah, Matthew, and the Pauline epistles woven into a composite text. The "treasure" language echoes both the parable of the pearl of great price (Matthew 13:46) and the treasure-seeking culture of the Smith family.

## Form Criticism

The commissioning form — divine call, conditional promise, warning, encouragement — parallels prophetic call narratives across multiple traditions. The specific instruction to wait and prepare before preaching represents what form critics identify as a preparatory oracle, distinct from the commissioning oracle proper.

## Sitz im Leben

Hyrum Smith's role as loyal elder brother and eventual church leader gives this revelation retrospective significance. The instruction to study scripture before preaching reflects the practical need for doctrinal education in a movement whose leaders had minimal formal theological training.
