## Historical Context

Section 13 records the restoration of the Aaronic Priesthood by John the Baptist on May 15, 1829, near Harmony, Pennsylvania. This event is central to Mormon restorationist claims but raises significant historical-critical questions.

## Source Criticism

The earliest accounts of the church's founding (1830-1834) do not mention the Aaronic Priesthood restoration as a distinct event. Dan Vogel, Grant Palmer, and others have argued that the priesthood restoration narrative was retrospectively constructed, possibly as late as 1834-1835, to legitimate institutional authority. Defenders such as Larry Morris argue the event was simply not publicly reported until later.

## Form Criticism

The ordination formula ("Upon you my fellow servants, in the name of Messiah...") employs archaic biblical language and follows the form of an apostolic commission. The attribution to John the Baptist as ordaining agent establishes a chain of authority reaching to the New Testament, a legitimation strategy common in Christian history (cf. apostolic succession claims in Catholic and Orthodox traditions).

## Redaction History

The dating and circumstances of this event differ across Joseph Smith's various histories. The 1832 history does not clearly distinguish Aaronic from Melchizedek priesthood restoration. The fuller narrative emerged in the 1838 history, suggesting progressive theological articulation. This developmental pattern is comparable to what source critics identify in the Torah's progressive elaboration of Aaronide priestly authority.
