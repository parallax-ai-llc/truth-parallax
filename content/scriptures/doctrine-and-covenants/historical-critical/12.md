## Historical Context

Section 12 was received in May 1829 for Joseph Knight Sr., an early supporter who provided material assistance during the Book of Mormon translation. Historical-critical scholars examine this revelation's function as a reciprocal gesture — spiritual authority conferred in exchange for material support.

## Source Criticism

The text is nearly identical to sections 4, 6, and 11, with minimal personalization. This formulaic quality suggests what source critics would call a revelatory Gattung (genre) — a standardized form that could be deployed for multiple recipients with minor adjustments.

## Sitz im Leben

Knight's provision of food, paper, and other supplies during the translation period is well documented in his own account and in the Smith family's records. The revelation's promise of spiritual reward for temporal service reflects the patron-prophet dynamic characteristic of charismatic religious movements.

## Historical Evidence

Joseph Knight Sr.'s 1833 account provides independent corroboration for the translation period's practical circumstances and confirms his supportive role. His account is valued by historians as one of the earliest non-Smith witnesses to the Book of Mormon's production.
