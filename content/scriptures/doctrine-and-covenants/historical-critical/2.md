## Historical Context

Section 2 records the angel Moroni's quotation of Malachi 4:5-6, reportedly delivered on September 21, 1823. Historical-critical scholars note that this section was not written down until years after the reported event, raising questions about the reliability of retrospective accounts common in form-critical analysis.

## Source Criticism

The text is a variant of Malachi 4:5-6, but with significant departures from both the KJV and the Hebrew Masoretic Text. Scholars such as Grant Hardy and Philip Barlow have analyzed these departures, noting that the rendering "plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to the fathers" diverges from any known biblical version, suggesting creative prophetic reinterpretation rather than direct quotation.

## Form Criticism and Sitz im Leben

The angelic visitation narrative follows a well-documented form in early American visionary literature. Richard Bushman has situated Moroni's visits within the folk magic and treasure-seeking culture of upstate New York, while Jan Shipps reads them within the conventions of biblical theophany narratives. The Sitz im Leben includes both the "burned-over district" revivalism and the family's engagement with divining practices.

## Redaction History

The earliest account of Moroni's visit (Joseph Smith's 1832 history) differs substantially from later versions (the 1835 and 1838 accounts). The progressive elaboration of detail across these accounts is a classic subject for redaction-critical analysis, paralleling the synoptic problem in New Testament studies.
