## Historical Context

Section 3 was received in July 1828 after Martin Harris lost 116 manuscript pages of the Book of Mormon translation. Historical-critical analysis examines the situational context: Joseph Smith faced a credibility crisis that threatened the entire translation project.

## Source Criticism and Sitz im Leben

The rebuke language ("You should not have feared man more than God") parallels biblical prophetic formulae, particularly Jeremiah's call narratives. Scholars note the text functions as a theodicy, explaining failure through divine displeasure rather than human fraud. The Sitz im Leben is the immediate crisis of the lost manuscript pages, documented in Lucy Mack Smith's biographical sketches and other contemporaneous sources.

## Redaction History

Grant Palmer and others have noted that the section underwent revision between the Book of Commandments (1833) and the Doctrine and Covenants (1835). Changes include strengthened language about Smith's prophetic authority and clarified theological claims, consistent with a pattern of editorial refinement that parallels Deuteronomistic redaction of prophetic texts.

## Historical Evidence

The lost manuscript episode is well-documented in multiple independent sources, making it one of the better-attested events in early Mormon history. Lucy Mack Smith's account, Martin Harris's later statements, and Joseph Smith's own narratives provide overlapping but divergent testimony, offering material for the kind of source analysis familiar from Synoptic Gospel criticism.
