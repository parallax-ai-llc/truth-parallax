## Overview

The Doctrine and Covenants is a collection of divine revelations and inspired declarations given primarily through Joseph Smith, the founding prophet of the Latter-day Saint movement. Unlike the Book of Mormon, which is presented as an ancient text, the Doctrine and Covenants is a modern scripture — its revelations span from 1823 to 1978, addressing the organization, doctrine, and governance of the restored Church of Jesus Christ.

The collection contains 138 sections and two Official Declarations. The revelations address a wide range of topics: the nature of God, the plan of salvation, priesthood authority, temple ordinances, the gathering of Israel, the establishment of Zion, church organization, and practical instructions for early church members. Many sections are directed to specific individuals, providing personal counsel alongside universal doctrine.

The Doctrine and Covenants holds canonical status in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and the Community of Christ (formerly RLDS Church), though the two traditions maintain slightly different editions. It is unique among scriptures in that it is not a narrative or collection of ancient writings but a compilation of revelations received in the modern era, making it a key text for understanding the theology and history of the Latter-day Saint movement.

## Structure

The Doctrine and Covenants is organized into 138 sections plus two Official Declarations:

1. **Early Revelations** (Sections 1–40): Revelations surrounding the translation of the Book of Mormon, church organization, and initial missionary work (1828–1831)
2. **Ohio Period** (Sections 41–100): The law of consecration, priesthood organization, the Word of Wisdom, and the School of the Prophets (1831–1833)
3. **Missouri and Nauvoo** (Sections 101–132): Zion's Camp, temple revelations, plural marriage, and the Nauvoo period (1833–1843)
4. **Later Additions** (Sections 133–138): The Appendix, prophetic declarations, and Joseph F. Smith's vision of the spirit world
5. **Official Declarations**: OD-1 (ending plural marriage) and OD-2 (extending priesthood to all races)

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Doctrine and Covenants holds significance across multiple interpretive traditions:

- **LDS**: The voice of the Lord to His restored church, containing the fullness of the gospel
- **Community of Christ**: An inspired collection used alongside continuing revelation through their prophet-president
- **Academic**: A primary source for understanding American religious history and new religious movements
- **Protestant**: Evaluated in light of biblical theology and the principle of *sola scriptura*
- **Comparative**: Studied alongside other modern revelatory texts and prophetic traditions
- **Historical-Critical**: Analyzed through textual, historical, and sociological methodologies

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Restoration | The re-establishment of Christ's original church and priesthood authority |
| Revelation | God's continuing communication with humanity through prophets |
| Priesthood | The authority to act in God's name, organized in Aaronic and Melchizedek orders |
| Zion | The establishment of a righteous community and the New Jerusalem |
| Temple | Sacred ordinances including endowment, sealing, and baptism for the dead |
