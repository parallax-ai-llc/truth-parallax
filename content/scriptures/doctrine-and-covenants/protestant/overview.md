## Overview

Protestant engagement with the Doctrine and Covenants brings distinctive interpretive methods and concerns to this foundational text. This approach illuminates dimensions of the text that may be less visible from other perspectives.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Biblical Evaluation
Protestant interpreters evaluate the Doctrine and Covenants against the standard of biblical teaching, examining its claims in light of the principle of *sola scriptura* and the sufficiency of the Bible.

### Theological Assessment
Protestant analysis examines the Doctrine and Covenants's theological claims, comparing its doctrines with historic Christian orthodoxy as expressed in the ecumenical creeds and Reformation confessions.

### Historical Analysis
Protestant scholars employ historical methods to examine the Doctrine and Covenants's origins, authorship, and development, assessing its claims in light of available evidence.
