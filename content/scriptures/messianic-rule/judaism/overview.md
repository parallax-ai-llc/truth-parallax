## Overview

Jewish engagement with the Messianic Rule situates it within the broader landscape of Second Temple Jewish literary and religious creativity. The text provides evidence for the remarkable diversity of Jewish thought and practice in the centuries before the destruction of the Temple in 70 CE — a period that gave birth to the foundations of rabbinic Judaism.

## Interpretive Approach

Jewish scholars read these texts as evidence of the rich pluralism of Second Temple Judaism, illuminating debates about law, worship, messianic expectation, and communal identity that shaped the Judaism that would emerge from the destruction of the Temple.
