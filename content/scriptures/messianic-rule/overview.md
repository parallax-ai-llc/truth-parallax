## Overview

The *Messianic Rule* (1QSa, also known as the Rule of the Congregation or 1Q28a) is another appendix to the Community Rule found in Cave 1 at Qumran. This brief but theologically significant text describes the organization and regulations of the eschatological community — the congregation of Israel at the end of days — including the famous description of the messianic banquet at which both the priestly messiah and the messiah of Israel preside.

The Messianic Rule is one of the most important texts for understanding Second Temple Jewish messianic expectations. Its vision of two messiahs — a priestly messiah of Aaron who takes precedence and a royal messiah of David — differs from later Jewish and Christian conceptions of a single messianic figure and provides crucial background for understanding the diversity of messianic belief in the period that gave birth to both rabbinic Judaism and Christianity.

## Structure

The three sections address:

1. **Section 1**: The organization of the eschatological congregation and the stages of life
2. **Section 2**: The council of the community and the role of the messiahs
3. **Section 3**: The messianic banquet and the precedence of the priestly messiah

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Essene**: Messianic Rule as a document of the community's self-understanding and eschatological hope
- **Judaism**: Witness to the diversity and creativity of Second Temple Jewish thought and practice
- **Protestantism**: Illuminates the Jewish background of the New Testament and early Christian theology
- **Catholicism**: Part of the salvation-historical development that prepared the way for Christ
- **Eastern Orthodox**: Anticipation of the liturgical and mystical realities fulfilled in the Church
- **Academic**: Primary source for understanding Second Temple Jewish religion, literature, and social organization
