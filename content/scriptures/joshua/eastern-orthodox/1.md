## The New Moses and the Theosis of the Leader

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Joshua 1 emphasizes the divine-human synergy (synergia) in Joshua's commission. God's command to "be strong and courageous" is not mere psychological encouragement but a transformative word that effects what it commands — a principle central to Orthodox sacramental theology.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "As I was with Moses, so I will be with you" (1:5)

**Theodoret of Cyrus** understood this as a guarantee of the divine energies (energeiai) operative in Joshua's leadership. The Orthodox distinction between God's essence and energies allows for a real participation in divine power without confusion of natures — Joshua is truly empowered by God while remaining fully human.

### The Transition of Authority (1:1-2)

Orthodox theology does not read the transition from Moses to Joshua merely as a change in management. The Fathers saw here a typological movement: Moses represents the Law, Joshua (bearing the name of Jesus) represents Grace. **Origen**, received in Orthodox tradition, taught that passing from Moses to Joshua signifies passing from the letter of the Law to its spiritual fulfillment.

### "This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth" (1:8)

The command to meditate on Scripture day and night anticipates the Orthodox practice of *lectio divina* and the Jesus Prayer. **John Chrysostom** insisted that Scripture meditation is not intellectual exercise but a means of union with God — an entry into the divine life.

## Theological Significance

- **Typology of Moses and Joshua**: The Law yields to Grace; the old dispensation to the new
- **Theosis**: Joshua's empowerment by God models the deification of the human person
- **Synergia**: Divine grace and human cooperation work together in the fulfillment of God's purposes
- **Living Tradition**: The transmission of authority from Moses to Joshua mirrors the Apostolic Tradition
