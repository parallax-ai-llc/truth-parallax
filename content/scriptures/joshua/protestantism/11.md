## The Northern Campaign

Joshua 11 records the defeat of the northern Canaanite coalition led by Jabin king of Hazor. The chapter concludes with a summary statement that Joshua "took the whole land," bringing the active conquest narrative to its climax. Protestant interpretation sees here the faithfulness of God to complete what He began.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Do not be afraid because of them" (11:6)

Before the massive northern coalition, God again reassures Joshua. **Spurgeon** preached that the size of the opposition never alters God's promise — the same God who toppled Jericho now defeats Hazor.

### "Joshua did just as the LORD told him" (11:9)

The refrain of obedience runs throughout Joshua. **Calvin** noted that Joshua's military success was inseparable from his theological obedience — he hamstrung the horses and burned the chariots as God commanded, refusing to trust in military technology.

### "So Joshua took the whole land" (11:23)

This summary statement is qualified later by references to land remaining (13:1), but the theological point is that God's promise is fundamentally fulfilled. **Woudstra** sees an "already/not yet" dynamic that anticipates the New Testament's eschatological tension.

## Theological Significance

- **God's faithfulness to complete His work**: What God begins, He finishes (Philippians 1:6)
- **Obedience in detail**: Joshua obeys even commands that seem strategically disadvantageous
- **Already/not yet**: The conquest is both complete and incomplete, modeling the Christian's present experience
- **No enemy too great**: The largest coalition falls before God's power
