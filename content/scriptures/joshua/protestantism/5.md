## Circumcision, Passover, and the Cessation of Manna

Joshua 5 marks a series of transitions: the circumcision of a new generation, the celebration of Passover in the land, the end of manna, and the mysterious appearance of the Commander of the Lord's army. Protestant theology reads these events as covenantal markers signaling that the wilderness period is over and the era of promise-fulfillment has begun.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Make flint knives and circumcise the sons of Israel a second time" (5:2)

The wilderness generation had not been circumcised — a sign of the covenant's suspension during the years of judgment. **Calvin** interpreted this as a renewal of covenant identity, noting that circumcision in the New Testament is replaced by baptism (Colossians 2:11-12).

### "Today I have rolled away the reproach of Egypt from you" (5:9)

The name Gilgal (from *galal*, "to roll") commemorates this moment. Protestant commentators see the "reproach of Egypt" as the stigma of slavery and disobedience now removed — a picture of justification, where the believer's shame is taken away in Christ.

### The Manna Ceases (5:12)

When the people eat the produce of the land, the manna stops. **Spurgeon** preached that God's provision changes form but never ceases — miraculous provision gives way to providential provision, but both come from the same gracious hand.

### The Commander of the Lord's Army (5:13-15)

This mysterious figure — who accepts Joshua's worship and commands him to remove his sandals (echoing Exodus 3:5) — is widely identified in Protestant theology as a Christophany, a pre-incarnate appearance of Christ. He comes not to take sides but to take command.

## Theological Significance

- **Covenant renewal**: Circumcision and Passover renew Israel's covenant identity before the conquest begins
- **Christophany**: The Commander of the Lord's army is the pre-incarnate Christ
- **The end of wilderness and the beginning of inheritance**: A paradigm for the Christian life of appropriating God's promises
- **Providence replaces miracle**: God's care continues in ordinary means
