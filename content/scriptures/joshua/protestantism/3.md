## Crossing the Jordan

Joshua 3 recounts one of the great miracles of the Old Testament: the parting of the Jordan River as the priests bearing the Ark of the Covenant step into the flood-stage waters. Protestant theology reads this event as both historical miracle and theological statement — God who parted the Red Sea for Moses now parts the Jordan for Joshua, confirming the new leader and the continuity of God's saving power.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "When the soles of the feet of the priests who bear the ark of the LORD... shall rest in the waters of the Jordan" (3:13)

**Spurgeon** preached that the priests had to step *into* the water before it parted — faith must act before the miracle comes. This is not works-righteousness but the obedience of faith, a distinction central to Protestant soteriology.

### "The waters coming down from above stood and rose up in a heap" (3:16)

The language deliberately echoes the Red Sea crossing (Exodus 14-15). **Woudstra** notes that this parallel establishes Joshua's authority as parallel to Moses' and confirms God's ongoing commitment to His people.

### The Ark Leading the Way (3:3-4)

The Ark goes before the people into the Jordan. Reformed commentators see the Ark — representing God's presence and His covenant — as a type of Christ who goes before His people through death and into the promised inheritance.

## Theological Significance

- **God's presence precedes His people**: The Ark enters the Jordan first, just as Christ entered death before us
- **Continuity of redemption**: The Red Sea and Jordan crossings form a theological pair — deliverance from Egypt and entrance into Canaan
- **Baptismal typology**: Paul's Red Sea typology (1 Corinthians 10:1-2) extends naturally to the Jordan crossing
- **Faith requires action**: The priests had to step into the water, modeling faith that acts on God's word
