## Remaining Tribes' Territories

Joshua 19 completes the tribal allotments, assigning territory to Simeon, Zebulun, Issachar, Asher, Naphtali, and Dan, and concluding with the personal inheritance given to Joshua himself. The chapter demonstrates that every tribe — and even the leader himself — receives its promised portion.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Simeon Within Judah (19:1-9)

Simeon's territory is carved from within Judah's allotment because "the portion of the people of Judah was too large for them" (v. 9). **Calvin** saw this as fulfillment of Jacob's prophecy that Simeon would be "scattered in Israel" (Genesis 49:7), demonstrating the reliability of prophetic word.

### "They gave Joshua the city that he asked" (19:49-50)

Joshua receives his inheritance last — after all the tribes are settled. **Davis** finds this profoundly significant: the leader serves first and receives last, modeling servant leadership that anticipates Christ's own pattern.

### "Timnath-serah in the hill country of Ephraim" (19:50)

Joshua's inheritance is modest — a single city in the hills. **Matthew Henry** contrasted this with the vast territories Joshua conquered for others, seeing in his humility a rebuke to self-serving leaders.

## Theological Significance

- **Every tribe receives its portion**: God's faithfulness extends to each member of the community
- **Prophetic fulfillment**: Simeon's scattered inheritance fulfills Jacob's ancient prophecy
- **Servant leadership**: Joshua takes last and takes least, modeling Christ-like humility
- **God's impartial care**: From the largest tribe to the smallest, each receives what God appointed
- **Completion of the division**: The exhaustive cataloguing shows that God's promise is thoroughly fulfilled
