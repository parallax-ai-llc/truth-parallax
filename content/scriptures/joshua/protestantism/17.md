## Manasseh's Territory

Joshua 17 describes the allotment for the half-tribe of Manasseh west of the Jordan. The chapter includes the notable story of Zelophehad's daughters claiming their inheritance and the Joseph tribes' complaint that their allotment is too small.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Zelophehad's Daughters (17:3-6)

The daughters of Zelophehad, who had successfully petitioned Moses for an inheritance (Numbers 27:1-11), now receive their land from Joshua. **Hess** notes that this demonstrates continuity between Moses' legal decisions and Joshua's administration — the law is faithfully applied.

### "The hill country is not enough for us" (17:14-18)

The Joseph tribes complain that their allotment is insufficient. Joshua's response is striking: if you are a great people, clear the forested hill country. **Calvin** saw in Joseph's complaint the sin of ingratitude — they wanted the blessing without the labor. Joshua's reply models wise leadership: he acknowledges their greatness but redirects it toward productive effort.

### "You shall drive out the Canaanites, though they have chariots of iron" (17:18)

Joshua's encouragement directly contradicts the people's fear. **Davis** observes that faith sees iron chariots differently than sight does — what intimidates human calculation is irrelevant to divine power.

## Theological Significance

- **Women's inheritance rights**: Zelophehad's daughters demonstrate that God's justice extends to all
- **Ingratitude and entitlement**: The Joseph tribes' complaint reveals a demanding spirit inappropriate for recipients of grace
- **Wise leadership**: Joshua neither dismisses the complaint nor enables passivity
- **Faith over fear**: Iron chariots are no obstacle to God's purposes
- **The call to effort**: Inheritance is given by God but appropriated through human labor
