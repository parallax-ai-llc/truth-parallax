## Rahab and the Spies

Joshua 2 introduces one of the most remarkable figures in Scripture: Rahab the prostitute, whose faith in the God of Israel earns her a place in the genealogy of Christ (Matthew 1:5) and in the hall of faith (Hebrews 11:31). Protestant interpretation emphasizes that her story illustrates salvation by grace through faith — irrespective of moral background, ethnicity, or social standing.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "She had brought them up to the roof and hid them with the stalks of flax" (2:6)

Rahab's actions demonstrate living faith. **James** cites her as proof that faith without works is dead (James 2:25). Protestant theology resolves the apparent tension with Paul's teaching by distinguishing between justification (by faith alone) and the evidence of justification (works that flow from faith).

### "I know that the LORD has given you the land" (2:9)

Rahab's confession reveals genuine faith. **Calvin** marveled that this pagan woman possessed clearer spiritual perception than many in Israel. Her knowledge came from hearing what God had done (v. 10) — faith comes by hearing (Romans 10:17).

### "The LORD your God, He is God in the heavens above and on the earth beneath" (2:11)

This is a remarkable monotheistic confession from a Canaanite woman. **Luther** saw in Rahab a paradigm of the gospel's universal scope — God saves from every nation, class, and moral condition.

### The Scarlet Cord (2:18)

The scarlet cord that marks Rahab's house for salvation has been compared by countless Protestant preachers to the blood of Christ. While some scholars caution against over-allegorizing, the typological parallel to the Passover lamb's blood (Exodus 12) is widely acknowledged.

## Theological Significance

- **Salvation by grace through faith**: Rahab's story demolishes any notion of salvation by merit
- **The universality of the gospel**: A Canaanite prostitute is welcomed into Israel — and into Christ's lineage
- **Faith and works**: Rahab illustrates the Protestant understanding that genuine faith necessarily produces obedient action
- **Typology of the scarlet cord**: A foreshadowing of redemption through blood
