## Caleb's Inheritance

Joshua 14 records Caleb's bold claim to Hebron, based on the promise Moses made forty-five years earlier. At eighty-five years old, Caleb declares himself as strong as ever and requests the most challenging territory — the hill country inhabited by the Anakim (giants). Protestant theology celebrates Caleb as a model of persevering faith.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "I wholly followed the LORD my God" (14:8)

This phrase, repeated in verse 14, defines Caleb's character. **Spurgeon** preached that "wholehearted" following is the distinguishing mark of genuine faith — not perfection, but undivided loyalty.

### "I am still as strong today as I was in the day that Moses sent me" (14:11)

Caleb's vigor at eighty-five is attributed to God's sustaining power. **Calvin** noted that God preserves strength for those He has purposes for — Caleb's physical endurance reflects his spiritual endurance.

### "Give me this hill country" (14:12)

Rather than requesting easy territory, Caleb asks for the most difficult assignment. **Davis** observes that mature faith does not seek comfort but challenge — Caleb wants the land where the giants live because God promised it.

## Theological Significance

- **Persevering faith**: Caleb waited forty-five years and never wavered
- **Wholehearted devotion**: The repeated emphasis on following God "wholly" challenges half-hearted religion
- **Faith that seeks challenge**: True faith does not avoid difficulty but embraces it in God's strength
- **God keeps His promises**: Every word spoken to Caleb is fulfilled
- **Aging in faith**: Caleb's story encourages lifelong spiritual vitality
