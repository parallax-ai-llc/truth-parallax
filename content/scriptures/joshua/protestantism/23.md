## Joshua's Farewell Address

Joshua 23 records Joshua's final speech to Israel's leaders — a blend of gratitude for God's past faithfulness, warning against compromise, and exhortation to continued obedience. Protestant theology reads this as a model of pastoral farewell, anticipating Paul's address to the Ephesian elders (Acts 20) and his final letters.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "The LORD your God is He who has fought for you" (23:3)

Joshua attributes all success to God. **Spurgeon** preached that this is the mark of true spiritual leadership: giving God all the credit for every victory.

### "Be very strong to keep and to do all that is written in the Book of the Law of Moses" (23:6)

The same charge given to Joshua in chapter 1 is now passed to the next generation. **Calvin** observed the pattern: each generation must receive the word afresh. Sola Scriptura is a perpetual principle, not a one-time event.

### "If you turn back and cling to the remnant of these nations" (23:12-13)

Joshua warns that compromise with the Canaanites will lead to disaster. The language is graphic: they will become "snares and traps... whips on your sides and thorns in your eyes." **Davis** notes that Joshua does not minimize the danger — covenant infidelity has consequences as certain as covenant faithfulness has blessings.

## Theological Significance

- **Past faithfulness as ground for future trust**: What God has done guarantees what He will do
- **The danger of compromise**: Tolerance of sin leads to spiritual destruction
- **Leadership's final responsibility**: A leader's last words should point people to God's word
- **Conditional blessings**: While God's covenant is unconditional, the experience of blessing depends on obedience
- **Intergenerational faithfulness**: Each generation must be charged anew
