## Overview

Protestant interpretation of Joshua centers on the fulfillment of God's promises to Abraham regarding the land. The book demonstrates that God is faithful to His covenant — what He promised to the patriarchs, He delivers through Joshua. Reformed and evangelical scholars emphasize that Joshua functions as a typological bridge: Joshua (Yehoshua) bears the same name as Jesus and leads God's people into their inheritance, just as Christ leads His people into the heavenly rest (Hebrews 3-4).

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Promise and fulfillment**: Joshua demonstrates God's covenant faithfulness — every promise to Abraham finds its initial fulfillment here
- **Typology of Christ**: Joshua as a type of Jesus who leads God's people into rest (Hebrews 4:8-9)
- **Holy war and divine sovereignty**: The conquest is understood as God's sovereign judgment on Canaanite sin (Genesis 15:16)
- **Faith and obedience**: Rahab's faith (Hebrews 11:31; James 2:25) and Achan's disobedience illustrate the gospel dynamic
- **The land as a type of spiritual inheritance**: Protestant typology reads Canaan as a picture of the believer's rest in Christ
