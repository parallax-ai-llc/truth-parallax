## The Conquest of Ai

Joshua 8 records Israel's second attempt against Ai — this time successful after the purging of Achan's sin — followed by the covenant renewal ceremony at Mount Ebal. Protestant interpretation sees a twofold lesson: after repentance, God restores His people to victory, and military conquest is immediately followed by the reading of God's law.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Do not fear and do not be dismayed" (8:1)

After the devastating defeat of chapter 7, God renews the commission. **Luther** emphasized that God does not abandon His people after their failure — He disciplines, restores, and recommissions.

### The Ambush Strategy (8:3-8)

Unlike Jericho, Ai is taken by military strategy — but strategy directed by God. **Howard** notes that this is not a contradiction: God works through both miraculous intervention and human agency. The Protestant doctrine of means (that God works through secondary causes) is illustrated here.

### The Altar on Mount Ebal (8:30-35)

Joshua builds an altar and reads the entire law to the assembled people, including women, children, and sojourners. **Calvin** noted that this fulfilled Moses' command in Deuteronomy 27 and demonstrated that the conquest was not merely political but covenantal. The centrality of the public reading of Scripture anticipates the Reformation's emphasis on vernacular Bible reading.

## Theological Significance

- **Restoration after repentance**: God does not permanently reject His people after sin
- **God works through means**: Military strategy and divine sovereignty are not contradictory
- **Law follows victory**: Conquest without covenant is meaningless; the people need God's word
- **Obedience to Deuteronomy**: Joshua faithfully executes Moses' instructions, modeling submission to prior revelation
