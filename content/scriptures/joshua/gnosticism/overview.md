## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Joshua is deeply allegorical, reading the conquest narratives as descriptions of the soul's struggle against archontic powers rather than literal military history. The Demiurge — the inferior creator god of Gnostic cosmology — is often identified with the wrathful deity who commands the destruction of the Canaanites. The true spiritual meaning, Gnostics argued, lies hidden beneath the literal text: Joshua represents the pneumatic (spiritual) self leading the soul into the Pleroma (divine fullness), while the enemies of Israel represent the passions and archons that enslave the spirit in matter.

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Allegory over literalism**: The conquest is a spiritual drama, not a historical record of genocide
- **Joshua as the Aeon**: Joshua/Jesus represents the divine emanation who liberates sparks of light from material imprisonment
- **The Demiurge**: The god who commands herem (destruction) is the inferior creator, not the true transcendent God
- **Archons as Canaanites**: The nations to be conquered represent the archontic powers that rule the material world
- **Gnosis through narrative**: Hidden knowledge (gnosis) is embedded in the text for those with spiritual perception
