## The Fall of Jericho and the Collapse of Archontic Walls

Gnostic interpretation of Joshua 6 reads the fall of Jericho as a cosmic drama: the walls represent the barriers erected by the archons to imprison divine sparks within the material world. Their collapse through sacred sound (the trumpets and the shout) represents the power of the divine Word — the Logos — to shatter the structures of cosmic ignorance.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Walls as Archontic Barriers

In Gnostic cosmology, the material world is enclosed within concentric spheres ruled by archons. Jericho's walls represent these cosmic barriers. The seven-day march corresponds to the seven planetary spheres that the soul must traverse to return to the Pleroma.

### The Sacred Sound

The trumpets and the great shout represent the power of the divine Name — the ineffable Word that dissolves illusion. **Valentinian** teaching held that certain sacred sounds and names possessed liberating power, reflecting the Gnostic emphasis on the potency of revealed knowledge.

### Rahab as the Awakened Soul

In Gnostic reading, Rahab represents the divine spark trapped in the most degraded material condition (a prostitute in a condemned city) who nevertheless recognizes the truth and is liberated. She is the *pneumatikos* (spiritual person) who perceives what others cannot and escapes the destruction of the material order.

### The Herem as Dissolution of Matter

The total destruction of Jericho represents not genocide but the ultimate dissolution of the material world — the eschatological event when matter, which is the Demiurge's creation, is consumed and the divine sparks are freed.

## Theological Significance

- **Cosmic liberation**: Jericho's fall represents the breaking of archontic bondage
- **The power of sacred sound**: The divine Word shatters the structures of ignorance
- **The awakened soul**: Rahab models the pneumatic self's liberation from matter
- **Eschatological destruction**: The herem symbolizes the end of the material order
