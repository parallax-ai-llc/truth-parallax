## The Conquest of Jericho

Islamic tradition preserves accounts of the conquest of Jericho (Ariha) as a demonstration of Allah's power granted to the faithful. The city's miraculous fall — through divine intervention rather than conventional siege — illustrates the Quranic principle that victory belongs to Allah alone.

## Quranic Parallels

### "How many a small company has overcome a large company by permission of Allah" (Surah al-Baqarah 2:249)

The fall of Jericho exemplifies this Quranic principle: military victory depends on Allah's permission, not human strength. **Ibn Kathir** connects the conquest narratives to this verse, demonstrating that faith overcomes material disadvantage.

### The Ark of the Covenant

Islamic tradition acknowledges the *Tabut* (Ark) mentioned in Surah al-Baqarah 2:248 as containing relics of Musa and Harun (Aaron). Its role in the Jericho narrative demonstrates that sacred objects serve as signs of Allah's presence and authority, though worship belongs to Allah alone.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Divine Intervention, Not Human Strategy

Muslim commentators emphasize that the unconventional method of conquest — marching rather than fighting — proves that Allah alone grants victory. This teaching reinforces *tawakkul* and warns against reliance on military technology or human planning.

### The Ethics of Warfare

Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) on warfare draws on multiple sources, including the prophetic narratives. The classical rules of Islamic warfare — prohibition of harming women, children, the elderly, and religious practitioners — represent the Sharia's ethical framework, which Muslims apply to all armed conflict.

### Rahab's Faithfulness

**Al-Tha'labi** includes Rahab in his accounts of the prophets, noting that her faith in God saved her household. Her story illustrates the Islamic teaching that sincere faith (*iman*) transcends ethnic and social boundaries.

## Theological Significance

- **Allah alone grants victory**: Human effort is secondary to divine will
- **Tawakkul in action**: Obedience to unconventional divine commands demonstrates true trust
- **Ethics of warfare**: Islam establishes moral boundaries even in legitimate combat
- **Universal faith**: Rahab's inclusion demonstrates that faith is not restricted by birth
