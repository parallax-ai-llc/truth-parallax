## Salvation and Return

The salvation teaching describes the reversal of the fall — the gathering of scattered sparks back to their source. This pattern of dispersion and return parallels the Hindu concept of moksha, the Buddhist ideal of nirvana, the Neoplatonic epistrophe (return), and the Sufi concept of fana (annihilation in God).
