## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Sophia of Jesus Christ represents the authoritative Christianized form of the cosmological knowledge found in Eugnostos the Blessed. By placing the same cosmological revelations in the mouth of the risen Jesus, the text claims the highest possible authority for its Gnostic cosmology. The post-resurrection setting is significant: the risen Christ does not merely repeat the public teachings of his earthly ministry but reveals the hidden truths about the divine realm that could only be communicated after his triumph over the material world. The inclusion of female disciples (including Mary) among the questioners reflects the Gnostic insistence that gnosis is available to all spiritual persons regardless of gender.
