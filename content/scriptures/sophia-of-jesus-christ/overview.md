## Overview

The Sophia of Jesus Christ (NHC III,4 and BG 8502,3) is a Gnostic revelation dialogue in which the risen Jesus appears to his disciples on a mountain in Galilee and instructs them about the nature of the divine realm, the origin of the cosmos, and the path to salvation. The text is a Christianized adaptation of Eugnostos the Blessed (NHC III,3), transforming that non-Christian cosmological epistle into a post-resurrection dialogue by inserting the figure of Jesus as the revealer and adding questions from the disciples — including Matthew, Philip, Bartholomew, Thomas, and Mary. The existence of both versions allows scholars to observe precisely how non-Christian Gnostic material was Christianized, making this text-pair one of the most important witnesses to the process of Christian Gnostic composition.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | The Risen Savior Appears | Jesus appears to the twelve disciples and seven women on a mountain after the resurrection |
| 2 | The Nature of the Father | The Savior reveals the nature of the ineffable Father, the First Father, and the divine hierarchy |
| 3 | The Emanation of the Aeons | Description of the emanation of the aeons from the divine source, adapted from Eugnostos |
| 4 | The Fall and the Material World | The origin of the material world through Sophia's error and the creation by the archons |
| 5 | Salvation and Return | The Savior explains the means of liberation from the material realm and return to the Pleroma |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: A key text showing how Gnostic cosmology was transmitted through the authority of the risen Christ
- **Academic**: Central to the study of literary dependence and Christianization processes in Gnostic literature
- **Comparative**: Illustrates how cosmological knowledge is transmitted through revelation dialogue across religious traditions
- **Christian**: Raises questions about the genre of post-resurrection revelation and its relationship to canonical resurrection appearances
- **Jewish**: Demonstrates the transformation of Jewish concepts of divine Wisdom (Sophia/Hokhmah) in Gnostic Christology
- **Interfaith**: Addresses the universal pattern of divine wisdom transmitted through an enlightened teacher to prepared disciples

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Revelation Dialogue | Cosmic knowledge is communicated through questions and answers between the risen Jesus and his disciples |
| Christianization | The text demonstrates the process by which non-Christian Gnostic material was adapted for Christian communities |
| Sophia Mythology | The figure of divine Wisdom (Sophia) and her role in both the fall and the redemption of the cosmos |
| Post-Resurrection Teaching | The risen Christ's primary function is not salvation through death but revelation of hidden knowledge |
