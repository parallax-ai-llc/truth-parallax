## Overview

The Sophia of Jesus Christ is of exceptional scholarly importance because it allows direct comparison with its source text, Eugnostos the Blessed. By placing the two texts side by side, scholars can observe precisely how a non-Christian Gnostic cosmological treatise was transformed into a Christian revelation dialogue: the epistolary frame is replaced by a post-resurrection appearance, the anonymous teacher becomes the risen Christ, and questions from the disciples are inserted to create a dialogue format. This text-pair provides the clearest evidence in the Nag Hammadi library of the process of Christianization at work.
