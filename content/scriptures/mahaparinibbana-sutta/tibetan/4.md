The events at Kusinara are read through the lens of the Tibetan emphasis on the teacher's final instructions (zhal chems). In Tibetan tradition, the words spoken by a realized master at the time of death carry special weight, as they represent the distilled essence of a lifetime of practice and realization. The Buddha's final instructions about the Dhamma and Vinaya serving as teacher are understood alongside the Tibetan tradition of terma — hidden teachings revealed at the appropriate time — suggesting that the Buddha's teaching continues to unfold beyond the historical moment of his death.
