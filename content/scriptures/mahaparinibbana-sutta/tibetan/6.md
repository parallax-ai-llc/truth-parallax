The distribution of relics is understood in Tibetan Buddhism through the tradition's extensive culture of relic veneration. Tibetan Buddhism has developed elaborate practices around the relics of realized masters — their remains are often enshrined in stupas (chorten), their clothes and personal items preserved as sacred objects, and ringsel (relic pearls) found in their cremation ashes are regarded as signs of realization. The Mahaparinibbana Sutta's account provides the scriptural precedent for these practices, connecting Tibetan relic veneration to the earliest Buddhist tradition of honoring the physical remains of awakened beings.
