The journey from Rajagaha to Kusinara is read in Tibetan tradition as a model of the teacher's compassionate activity — continuing to teach and guide even while approaching death. The Tibetan understanding of the guru's role as the living embodiment of the dharma gives special poignancy to this travelogue: every word spoken along the way is precious because time is running out. The Mirror of the Dhamma teaching is understood in relation to the Tibetan practice of taking stock of one's spiritual progress, a regular feature of Tibetan contemplative training.
