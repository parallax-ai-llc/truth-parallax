The Buddha's passing through the jhanas and his entry into parinibbana are studied in Tibetan Buddhism as the supreme example of conscious dying. The detailed description of the meditative progression at the moment of death provides a model for the Tibetan practice of phowa (consciousness transference) and for the more advanced practice of dying in meditation. The Tibetan understanding of the stages of dissolution at death — described in the tantric literature as the progressive dissolution of the elements — adds a Vajrayana dimension to the Pali account, enriching the understanding of what occurs as a realized being releases the physical body.
