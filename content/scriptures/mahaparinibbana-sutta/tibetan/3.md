The Buddha's illness and his teaching on self-reliance are understood in Tibetan Buddhism through the framework of the guru-disciple relationship. While the Buddha teaches self-reliance, the Tibetan tradition also emphasizes the indispensability of the teacher — a tension resolved by understanding that the ultimate guru is the Buddha-nature within. The decision to release the life force resonates with Tibetan teachings on the conscious death of realized masters, who choose the moment and circumstances of their passing. Ananda's failure to request the Buddha to remain parallels the Tibetan practice of requesting the guru to live long (zhabs brtan), which Tibetan practitioners perform regularly.
