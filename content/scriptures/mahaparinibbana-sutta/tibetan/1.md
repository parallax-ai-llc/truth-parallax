The opening chapter's discourse on communal welfare resonates with the Tibetan Buddhist emphasis on the sangha as one of the Three Jewels. The seven conditions for a community's prosperity parallel the guidelines for monastic community found in the Tibetan Vinaya tradition and in the instructions of Tibetan lamas for maintaining harmony in dharma centers. The chapter's practical wisdom about institutional governance has been applied by Tibetan teachers to the challenges of maintaining Buddhist institutions in exile, where the conditions for communal welfare described by the Buddha take on renewed urgency.
