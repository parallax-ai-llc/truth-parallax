The Buddha's decision to release the life force and his admonition to 'be a lamp unto yourselves' go to the heart of Zen. The teaching of self-reliance is Zen's foundational principle: no external authority can awaken you; you must see your own nature directly. Yet the Buddha's impending death also functions as the ultimate koan: the great teacher is leaving — now what? This question, pressing and unanswerable by intellect, drives the practitioner beyond dependence on words and teachers into direct encounter with reality. Ananda's failure to ask the Buddha to remain is understood as the failure of conceptual mind to grasp the living moment.
