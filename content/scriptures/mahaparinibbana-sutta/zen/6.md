The distribution of relics raises the question that Zen addresses directly: where is the Buddha now? If you seek the Buddha in relics and stupas, you will not find him. If you seek him in the teaching, you will not find him there either. The true relic of the Buddha is the awakened mind — present in every being, available in every moment. The chapter's account of competing claims for the relics is a cautionary tale about the danger of grasping at forms rather than realizing the formless truth to which all forms point. As Linji taught: 'If you meet the Buddha, kill the Buddha' — let go of all attachment, even to the most sacred objects.
