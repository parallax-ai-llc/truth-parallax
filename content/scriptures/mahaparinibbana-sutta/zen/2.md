The journey narrative is read in Zen as a model of the teacher's life: every encounter, every conversation, every ordinary moment is an occasion for dharma. The Buddha does not reserve his teaching for formal settings but teaches along the road, in groves, and at meals. This resonates with the Zen understanding that awakening is not confined to the meditation hall but pervades every activity of daily life. The encounters along the way function as proto-koans: each situation presents a unique opportunity for direct pointing, and each listener's response reveals their level of understanding.
