At Kusinara, the Buddha's last instructions take on particular urgency for Zen: 'The Dhamma and Vinaya shall be your teacher.' Zen interprets this not as an instruction to rely on texts but as a pointing to the living reality that texts indicate. The 'true teacher' is not a book but the dharma realized in direct experience. Subhadda's conversion — the last disciple made through personal encounter with the living Buddha — poignantly illustrates what will be lost and what will continue: the historical person will die, but the dharma he transmitted lives on in each practitioner who realizes it.
