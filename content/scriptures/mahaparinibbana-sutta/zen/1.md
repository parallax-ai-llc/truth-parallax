Zen reads the opening chapter's discourse on communal welfare as practical wisdom for sangha life — the ongoing community of practice that sustains Zen transmission. The seven conditions for welfare resonate with the Zen emphasis on harmony in the monastery, respect for the teacher-student relationship, and the maintenance of established forms of practice. The chapter's political context — the threat to the Vajjian republic — is understood in Zen terms as a reminder that practice communities face constant challenges and must be actively maintained through the collective commitment of their members.
