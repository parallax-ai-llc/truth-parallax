The Mahayana tradition interprets the Buddha's illness and his announcement of parinibbana through the lens of the Mahayana Mahaparinirvana Sutra, which radically reinterprets these events. In the Mahayana version, the Buddha's apparent death is itself a form of skillful means — the eternal Tathagata only appears to pass away in order to inspire a sense of urgency among his followers. The teaching of self-reliance is reinterpreted not as reliance on one's individual capacity alone but as reliance on the Buddha-nature within oneself, which is none other than the eternal Buddha. This interpretation transforms the Pali sutta's emphasis on impermanence into a teaching about the permanence, bliss, selfhood, and purity of the Buddha-nature.
