The events at Kusinara are read in Mahayana tradition with attention to the Buddha's final instructions about the Vinaya and the teaching. The statement about abolishing lesser rules is interpreted in light of the Mahayana emphasis on the spirit rather than the letter of the precepts — the bodhisattva's commitment to liberating all beings may sometimes require flexibility in applying specific rules. The conversion of Subhadda demonstrates the Buddha's compassion extending to the very last moment, embodying the bodhisattva ideal of never abandoning any being. The declaration that the Dhamma shall be the teacher is understood in Mahayana terms as pointing to the living dharma transmitted through lineage rather than to the letter of any specific text.
