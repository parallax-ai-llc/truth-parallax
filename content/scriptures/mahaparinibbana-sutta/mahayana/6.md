The distribution of relics takes on additional significance in Mahayana Buddhism through the understanding that the true relic of the Buddha is the Dharma itself. While not dismissing physical relic veneration, Mahayana texts such as the Diamond Sutra teach that wherever the sutras are present, there the Buddha is present. This 'Dharma-relic' understanding expanded the concept of the stupa from a container of physical remains to any site where the teaching is preserved and transmitted. The chapter's account of rival claims for the relics is read as an illustration of the dangers of attachment — even attachment to sacred objects — and as a call to focus on the living teaching rather than on material remnants.
