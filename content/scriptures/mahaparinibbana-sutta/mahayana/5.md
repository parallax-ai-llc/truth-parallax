The passing through the jhanas and the final entry into parinibbana are read by Mahayana tradition as a demonstration rather than a permanent departure. The Buddha's progression through all meditative states and his final passing from the fourth jhana — the state of perfect equanimity — models the complete mastery of a fully awakened being. However, the Mahayana understanding, developed in the Mahaparinirvana Sutra and the Lotus Sutra, holds that the Buddha's physical death does not represent the cessation of his activity. The Dharmakaya — the ultimate reality body of the Buddha — is eternal and continues to teach and guide beings through countless manifestations across space and time.
