The journey narrative provides Mahayana readers with examples of the Buddha's skillful means (upaya). Each teaching along the way is adapted to the specific audience and circumstances, demonstrating the same pedagogical flexibility that the Lotus Sutra celebrates as the Buddha's supreme quality. The encounter with Ambapali is read as an illustration of the bodhisattva's non-discriminating compassion — the Buddha accepts offerings from all beings regardless of their social standing. The Mirror of the Dhamma teaching is understood in Mahayana terms as describing the bodhisattva's irreversible commitment to the path, which parallels the stream-entry of the Pali tradition but extends toward the goal of complete Buddhahood.
