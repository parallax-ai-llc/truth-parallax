Mahayana Buddhism reads the opening chapter's discourse on communal welfare through the lens of the bodhisattva ideal. The seven conditions for a community's welfare are understood not merely as institutional guidelines but as expressions of the bodhisattva's compassionate concern for the well-being of all beings. The Mahayana reading extends these principles beyond the monastic sangha to encompass all of society, reflecting the tradition's broader social vision. The chapter's setting in Rajagaha — a great city and seat of political power — is read as indicating that the Dhamma's relevance extends to governance and politics, a theme developed more fully in Mahayana texts such as the Sutra of Golden Light.
