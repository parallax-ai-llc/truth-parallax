The account of the Buddha's parinibbana has been compared across all extant versions — Pali, Sanskrit fragments, Chinese, and Tibetan — revealing both a stable core narrative and significant variations in detail. The description of the Buddha's meditative progression at death has been analyzed in relation to Buddhist theories of meditation and consciousness, with scholars debating whether it represents an authentic early tradition or a later schematization. The supernatural events — earthquakes, celestial flowers — have been studied as literary conventions that parallel accounts of great events in other Indian religious texts, raising questions about genre and historicity.
