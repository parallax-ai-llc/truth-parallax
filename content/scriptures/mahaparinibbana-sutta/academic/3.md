The account of the Buddha's illness and his decision to relinquish the life force has generated extensive scholarly debate. Andre Bareau argued that this passage reflects a later legendary tradition that sought to explain why the Buddha died, while other scholars have defended its historical plausibility. The 'be a lamp unto yourselves' passage (attadipa viharatha) has been philologically analyzed — Rhys Davids and K.R. Norman debated whether 'dipa' means 'lamp' or 'island' — with significant implications for understanding the Buddha's teaching on self-reliance. The textual history of this chapter reveals multiple layers of composition, with some passages appearing to be later additions to an earlier core narrative.
