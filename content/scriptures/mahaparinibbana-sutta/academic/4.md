The events at Kusinara have been subjected to detailed textual and historical analysis. The conversion of Subhadda has been studied as a literary device establishing the continuity of the Buddha's teaching activity to the very end. The instruction about abolishing lesser rules has been analyzed in relation to the First Council narrative, where the sangha's decision not to abolish any rules established a precedent for institutional conservatism. Scholars have examined how this tension between flexibility and conservatism shaped the subsequent development of Buddhist monastic codes across different schools.
