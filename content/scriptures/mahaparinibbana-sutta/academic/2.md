The journey narrative has been studied for its historical and geographical information. Scholars have mapped the Buddha's route from Rajagaha to Kusinara and examined the archaeological evidence for the sites mentioned along the way. The encounter with Ambapali has been analyzed in relation to the social status of courtesans in ancient Indian society and the Buddha's revolutionary inclusion of marginalized groups. The Mirror of the Dhamma passage has been studied as a possible later insertion that reflects the formalization of the concept of stream-entry in early Buddhist doctrinal development.
