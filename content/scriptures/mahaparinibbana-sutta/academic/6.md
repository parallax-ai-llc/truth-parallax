The relic distribution narrative has been studied as the foundation for the Buddhist stupa cult, one of the most important developments in Buddhist material culture. John Strong's work on relics has examined how the narrative established the precedent for relic veneration that spread throughout the Buddhist world. Archaeological evidence from sites such as the Piprahwa stupa has been compared with the textual account, with some scholars arguing that the inscriptions confirm aspects of the narrative while others remain skeptical. The role of the Brahmin Dona as mediator has been analyzed in relation to the relationship between Buddhist and Brahmanical communities in the post-Buddha period.
