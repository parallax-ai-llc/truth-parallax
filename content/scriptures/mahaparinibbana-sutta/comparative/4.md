The final instructions at Kusinara parallel the farewell discourses found across religious traditions: Jesus' farewell discourse in John 13-17, Muhammad's farewell sermon at Arafat, and Moses' final addresses in Deuteronomy. All share a structure of last testament, ethical exhortation, and provision for the future community. The conversion of Subhadda — a last-minute disciple — parallels the conversion of the thief on the cross in the Christian narrative, suggesting a common motif in which the founder's redemptive power extends to the very last moment.
