The Buddha's illness and his teaching on self-reliance after the teacher's departure find poignant parallels in other traditions. Jesus' promise of the Holy Spirit as Comforter after his departure (John 14-16) addresses the same crisis: what happens when the teacher leaves? The Sikh tradition's transfer of authority to the Guru Granth Sahib — a text rather than a person — parallels the Buddhist decision to take the Dhamma and Vinaya as teacher. Each tradition must solve the problem of succession, and the solutions reveal fundamental assumptions about the nature of authority and the source of spiritual guidance.
