The distribution of relics and construction of stupas parallel relic veneration practices across world religions. The Christian cult of saints' relics, the Islamic preservation of Muhammad's personal effects (including the sacred cloak and staff), the Hindu veneration of sacred sites associated with divine activity, and the Jewish preservation of Torah scrolls all reflect the human need to maintain physical connection with the sacred. The Mahaparinibbana Sutta's account provides the earliest detailed narrative of how such a relic cult originated, offering valuable comparative material for understanding the universal dynamics of sacred materiality in religion.
