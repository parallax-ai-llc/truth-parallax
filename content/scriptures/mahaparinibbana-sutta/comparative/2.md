The journey narrative — a teacher traveling, teaching, and preparing for death — finds parallels in the final journeys of other religious figures. Jesus' journey to Jerusalem, Socrates' final days narrated in the Phaedo, and the prophetic journey of Muhammad to Medina all share the structure of a final teaching journey. The encounters along the way function in each tradition as crystallizations of the teacher's essential message, delivered with heightened urgency in the awareness of approaching death. The inclusion of marginalized figures such as Ambapali parallels Jesus' association with tax collectors and sinners, suggesting a shared pattern in which religious founders challenge social boundaries.
