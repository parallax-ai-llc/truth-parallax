---
title: "Mahaparinibbana Sutta - Chapter 4: The Last Meal"
scripture: "mahaparinibbana-sutta"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Then the Blessed One went with a large company of monks to Bhandagama, and then to Hatthigama, then to Ambagama, then to Jambugama, and arrived at Bhoganagara.

**2** There the Blessed One said to the monks: "There are four great references, monks. If a monk should say, 'Friends, I heard this in the presence of the Blessed One, I received this from his lips,' then his words should not be rejected nor accepted. Without rejecting or accepting, the words and phrases should be carefully noted and checked against the Suttas and reviewed in the light of the Vinaya."

**3** "If they are not found in the Suttas and are not in accordance with the Vinaya, you should conclude: 'This is certainly not the word of the Blessed One.' If they are found in the Suttas and are in accordance with the Vinaya, you should conclude: 'This is indeed the word of the Blessed One.'"

**4** Then the Blessed One went to Pava and stayed in the mango grove of Cunda the smith. Cunda prepared a special meal, including a quantity of sukaramaddava. He served it to the Blessed One.

**5** The Blessed One said: "Cunda, serve me with the sukaramaddava, and serve the monks with the other foods." Cunda did so. Then the Blessed One said: "Cunda, bury the remaining sukaramaddava in a pit. I see no one in the world of men and gods who could digest it properly, except the Tathagata."

**6** After eating Cunda's meal, the Blessed One was attacked by a severe illness, with sharp pains and dysentery, with blood flowing. But the Blessed One endured the pains, mindful and clearly comprehending, without complaint.

**7** The Blessed One said to Ananda: "Come, let us go to Kusinara." On the way, the Blessed One was weary and said: "Fold my robe in four, Ananda. I am weary and wish to sit down."

**8** He sat down at the foot of a tree and said: "Bring me water, Ananda, I am thirsty." Ananda said: "Lord, five hundred carts have just crossed the stream, and the water is muddy." But the Blessed One said again: "Bring me water, Ananda." And when Ananda went, the water was clear and pure.

**9** Then the Blessed One said: "Ananda, if anyone should blame Cunda the smith, saying, 'It is a loss to you, friend Cunda, it is unfortunate that the Tathagata attained final Nibbana after eating his meal,' any such remorse should be dispelled."

**10** "Two offerings of food are of equal fruit and greater fruit than any other: the offering of food after which the Tathagata attains supreme enlightenment, and the offering of food after which the Tathagata attains final Nibbana. Cunda the smith has accumulated meritorious action conducive to long life, beauty, happiness, fame, rebirth in heaven, and sovereignty."
