---
title: "Mahaparinibbana Sutta - Chapter 3: The Relinquishment of the Life Force"
scripture: "mahaparinibbana-sutta"
chapter: 3
---

**1** At Vesali the Blessed One fell severely ill, with sharp pains as if he were close to death. But the Blessed One, mindful and clearly comprehending, endured the pain without complaint.

**2** The Blessed One thought: "It would not be fitting for me to pass into final Nibbana without addressing my attendants, without taking leave of the order of monks. Let me suppress this illness by energy and live on."

**3** Then the venerable Ananda came to the Blessed One and said: "Lord, I have seen the Blessed One in comfort, and I have seen the Blessed One in suffering. My body seemed as if drugged. I could not find my bearings. The teaching seemed unclear to me. But one thing gave me some comfort: I thought, 'The Blessed One will not pass into final Nibbana until he has given some instruction concerning the order of monks.'"

**4** The Blessed One said: "What does the order of monks expect from me, Ananda? I have taught the Dhamma without making a distinction between inside and outside. With respect to the Dhamma, the Tathagata has no teacher's fist—that is, he holds nothing back."

**5** "If there is anyone who thinks, 'I will lead the order,' or 'The order depends on me,' then he might give some instruction concerning the order. But the Tathagata does not think in such terms."

**6** "Therefore, Ananda, be islands unto yourselves, be your own refuge, with no other refuge. Let the Dhamma be your island, let the Dhamma be your refuge, with no other refuge."

**7** "And how does a monk dwell as an island unto himself? Here, a monk dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and grief in regard to the world. He dwells contemplating feelings in feelings... mind in mind... phenomena in phenomena."

**8** "Those monks, Ananda, either now or after I am gone, who dwell as their own island, their own refuge, with the Dhamma as their island, the Dhamma as their refuge—they will be the highest of those monks who are desirous of training."

**9** Then the Blessed One went to the Capala shrine. He said to Ananda: "Ananda, whoever has developed the four bases of spiritual power could, if he wished, remain for a kalpa or the remainder of a kalpa. The Tathagata has developed the four bases of spiritual power." But Ananda did not understand the hint and did not ask the Blessed One to remain.

**10** Then Mara, the Evil One, approached the Blessed One and said: "Let the Blessed One now pass into final Nibbana." The Blessed One said: "Do not worry, Evil One. Before long the Tathagata will attain final Nibbana. Three months from now, the Tathagata will attain final Nibbana."

**11** And the Blessed One, mindful and clearly comprehending, relinquished the life force at the Capala shrine. When the life force was relinquished, there was a great earthquake and a terrifying thunder.

**12** The Blessed One uttered this inspired verse: "What has been produced—whether gross or subtle—the sage has renounced, becoming one who produces nothing more. Inwardly calmed, he has destroyed the produced self. Like a coat of mail that is shattered, one is at peace."
