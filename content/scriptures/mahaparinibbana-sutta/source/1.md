---
title: "Mahaparinibbana Sutta - Chapter 1: The Journey Begins"
scripture: "mahaparinibbana-sutta"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Rajagaha, on the mountain called Vulture Peak. At that time King Ajatasattu Vedehiputta of Magadha wished to make war on the Vajjians.

**2** He sent his chief minister, Vassakara the brahman, to the Blessed One, saying: "Go to the Blessed One and say: 'King Ajatasattu wishes to make war on the Vajjians. What does the Blessed One say?'"

**3** The Blessed One said to the venerable Ananda: "Ananda, have you heard that the Vajjians hold frequent and well-attended assemblies?" "I have heard so, Lord." "Ananda, so long as the Vajjians hold frequent and well-attended assemblies, only growth is to be expected for them, not decline."

**4** "So long as the Vajjians meet in harmony, break up in harmony, and carry on their business in harmony, only growth is to be expected for them, not decline."

**5** "So long as the Vajjians do not establish new laws that were not established, do not abolish laws already established, and proceed according to the ancient Vajjian laws as they have been established, only growth is to be expected for them."

**6** "So long as the Vajjians honour, respect, venerate, and salute the Vajjian elders, and consider them worth listening to, only growth is to be expected for them."

**7** "So long as the Vajjians do not forcibly abduct women and girls of good families and compel them to live with them, only growth is to be expected."

**8** "So long as the Vajjians honour, respect, venerate, and salute the Vajjian shrines in town and country, and do not withdraw the customary offerings, only growth is to be expected for them."

**9** "So long as the Vajjians provide proper provision and protection for the Arahants among them, so that such Arahants may come to their territory and live comfortably, only growth is to be expected."

**10** Vassakara said: "If the Vajjians keep to even one of these principles, growth is to be expected, let alone all seven. The Vajjians cannot be defeated by King Ajatasattu in battle, except through diplomacy or by sowing dissension among them."

**11** Then the Blessed One said to Ananda: "I will now teach the seven conditions of welfare for the monks." And he spoke of regular assemblies, harmony, adherence to training, respect for elders, protection from craving, love of forest dwellings, and mindfulness.

**12** "So long as these seven conditions prevail among the monks, only growth is to be expected for the monks, not decline."
