---
title: "Mahaparinibbana Sutta - Chapter 5: The Final Instructions"
scripture: "mahaparinibbana-sutta"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Then the Blessed One went to the Sala Grove of the Mallas at Kusinara, between two sala trees. He said: "Ananda, make a bed for me between the twin sala trees, with the head to the north. I am weary and wish to lie down."

**2** Ananda did so. The Blessed One lay down on his right side, in the lion posture, with one foot placed on the other, mindful and clearly comprehending.

**3** At that time the twin sala trees burst into bloom out of season, and the blossoms rained upon the body of the Tathagata, scattering and strewing upon it as a mark of respect. Celestial mandarava flowers and sandalwood powder rained from the sky. Celestial music sounded.

**4** The Blessed One said: "Ananda, it is not by this that the Tathagata is worshipped, venerated, or honoured. But the monk or nun, the layman or laywoman, who practises the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, who enters upon the proper way, who walks in the Dhamma—that person worships, venerates, and honours the Tathagata with the supreme worship."

**5** Then the venerable Ananda went inside the dwelling, leaned against the doorpost, and wept: "Alas, I am still a learner, with much to do. And the Teacher is about to pass away, he who was so compassionate to me."

**6** The Blessed One said: "Enough, Ananda, do not grieve, do not weep. Have I not already told you that it is in the nature of all things near and dear to us that we must separate from them? How could it be, Ananda, that what is born, come into being, formed and conditioned, should not pass away?"

**7** "For a long time, Ananda, you have attended the Tathagata with loving, helpful, and wholehearted bodily acts, with loving, helpful, and wholehearted verbal acts, with loving, helpful, and wholehearted mental acts. You have done meritorious deeds, Ananda. Make effort, and you too shall soon be free from the taints."

**8** Then the Blessed One said to the monks: "Monks, if there is any monk who has doubt or perplexity regarding the Buddha, the Dhamma, the Sangha, the path, or the practice, ask now. Do not later feel remorse, thinking: 'The Teacher was before us and we did not ask him.'"

**9** He said this a second time, and a third time. But the monks were silent. Then Ananda said: "It is wonderful, Lord! Not one of these monks has any doubt or perplexity."

**10** The Blessed One said: "You speak out of faith, Ananda. But the Tathagata knows: not one of these five hundred monks has any doubt. The least advanced of them is a stream-enterer, not destined for the lower realms, certain of eventual enlightenment."
