---
title: "Mahaparinibbana Sutta - Chapter 2: The Last Journey"
scripture: "mahaparinibbana-sutta"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Then the Blessed One, having stayed at Rajagaha as long as he wished, said to Ananda: "Come, Ananda, let us go to Ambalatthika." And they went.

**2** At Ambalatthika the Blessed One spoke to the monks: "Monks, it is through not understanding, not penetrating four things that you and I have wandered so long in the round of rebirth. What four? Noble morality, noble concentration, noble wisdom, and noble liberation."

**3** "But now, monks, these have been understood and penetrated. The craving for existence has been cut off. That which leads to rebirth has been destroyed. There is no more coming to any state of being."

**4** Then the Blessed One went to Nalanda, and there the venerable Sariputta came to him and said: "Lord, I have such faith in the Blessed One that I believe there has not been, will not be, and is not now any ascetic or brahman who is superior to the Blessed One in enlightenment."

**5** The Blessed One said: "Grand and bold is this utterance of yours, Sariputta, a veritable lion's roar! But have you known all the Buddhas of the past, comprehending their minds?" "No, Lord." "Then, Sariputta, how can you make such a statement?"

**6** Sariputta said: "I do not have knowledge of the minds of all the Buddhas. But I know the principle of the Dhamma. It is as if a king's frontier city had strong foundations and a single gate, and the gatekeeper knew that no creature could enter except through the gate."

**7** "So I know that all those who have attained supreme enlightenment have done so by abandoning the five hindrances, by establishing the four foundations of mindfulness, and by developing the seven factors of enlightenment."

**8** Then the Blessed One went to Pataligama. There he taught the householders: "Five perils await the immoral person: great loss of wealth, an evil reputation, timidity in assemblies, death in a state of confusion, and rebirth in a lower realm."

**9** "Five blessings await the moral person: great increase of wealth, a good reputation, confidence in assemblies, death without confusion, and rebirth in a happy realm."

**10** Then the Blessed One crossed the river Ganges and came to Kotigama. There he addressed the monks: "It is through not understanding, not penetrating the Four Noble Truths that you and I have wandered so long in the round of rebirth. But now they have been understood, and the craving for becoming has been cut off."
