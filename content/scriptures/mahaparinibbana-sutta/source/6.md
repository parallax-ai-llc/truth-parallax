---
title: "Mahaparinibbana Sutta - Chapter 6: The Final Nibbana"
scripture: "mahaparinibbana-sutta"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Then the Blessed One addressed the monks for the last time: "Monks, I declare to you: all conditioned things are of a nature to decay. Strive on with diligence." These were the last words of the Tathagata.

**2** Then the Blessed One entered the first jhana. Emerging from the first jhana, he entered the second jhana. Emerging from the second jhana, he entered the third jhana. Emerging from the third jhana, he entered the fourth jhana.

**3** Emerging from the fourth jhana, he entered the base of infinite space. Emerging from that, he entered the base of infinite consciousness. Emerging from that, he entered the base of nothingness. Emerging from that, he entered the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. Emerging from that, he entered the cessation of perception and feeling.

**4** Then Ananda said to the venerable Anuruddha: "The Blessed One has attained final Nibbana." Anuruddha said: "No, friend Ananda, the Blessed One has not yet attained final Nibbana. He has entered the cessation of perception and feeling."

**5** Then the Blessed One, emerging from the cessation of perception and feeling, entered the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. Emerging from that, he entered the base of nothingness, then the base of infinite consciousness, then the base of infinite space, then the fourth jhana, then the third jhana, then the second jhana, then the first jhana.

**6** Emerging from the first jhana, he entered the second jhana. Emerging from the second jhana, he entered the third jhana. Emerging from the third jhana, he entered the fourth jhana. Immediately upon emerging from the fourth jhana, the Blessed One attained final Nibbana.

**7** When the Blessed One attained final Nibbana, at the moment of his passing, there was a great earthquake and terrifying thunder. Brahma Sahampati uttered this verse: "All beings in the world will finally lay aside the body, for even such a one as the Teacher, the peerless person in the world, the Tathagata endowed with the powers, the Buddha, has attained final Nibbana."

**8** Sakka, lord of the devas, uttered this verse: "Impermanent are all formations. Their nature is to arise and pass away. Having arisen, they cease. Their stilling is bliss."

**9** The venerable Anuruddha uttered this verse: "There was no more breathing of one whose mind was firm, when the sage, the Peaceful One, passed away."

**10** The venerable Ananda uttered this verse: "Then there was terror, then there was a horrifying dread, when the Fully Enlightened One, the one endowed with all excellent qualities, attained final Nibbana."

**11** Among the monks who were not yet free from passion, some raised their arms and wept, some fell headlong on the ground. But those monks who were free from passion endured it mindfully and clearly comprehending, saying: "Impermanent are all formations. What is the use of grieving?"

**12** Then the venerable Anuruddha addressed the monks: "Enough, friends, do not grieve, do not weep. Has not the Blessed One already told us that it is in the nature of all things near and dear to us that we must be separated from them? Whatever is born, come into being, formed and conditioned—that it should not pass away is impossible."
