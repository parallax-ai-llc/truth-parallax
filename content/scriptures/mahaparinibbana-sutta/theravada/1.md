The opening chapter of the Mahaparinibbana Sutta, set in Rajagaha, presents the Buddha's discourse on the seven conditions for a community's welfare (satta aparihaniya dhamma). These conditions — including regular assembly, harmony in decision-making, respect for elders, and adherence to established institutions — were delivered in the context of the Vajjian republic's defense against King Ajatasattu's planned invasion. Theravada tradition reads these conditions as the Buddha's blueprint for sangha governance, applicable not only to the monastic community but to any group seeking to thrive. The chapter establishes the practical, institutional dimension of the Buddha's final teaching: the Dhamma is not only a path of personal liberation but a framework for communal flourishing.
