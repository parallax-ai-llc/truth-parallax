## Overview

The Mahaparinibbana Sutta holds supreme importance in the Theravada tradition as the canonical account of the Buddha's final days, last teachings, and passing into parinibbana. As part of the Digha Nikaya, it carries full canonical authority and is regularly chanted, studied, and referenced in Theravada communities.

## Doctrinal Authority

The sutta's instruction to "be a lamp unto yourselves" (attadipa viharatha) and to take the Dhamma and Vinaya as teacher after the Buddha's passing have become foundational principles of Theravada Buddhism. These instructions establish the primacy of the canonical texts and the monastic code as the ultimate authorities, rather than any living teacher or later revelation.

## Relic Veneration

The sutta's account of the distribution of the Buddha's relics provides the scriptural basis for stupa worship and relic veneration throughout the Theravada world. The great stupas of Sri Lanka, Myanmar, and Thailand trace their spiritual authority to the relics described in this text, making the sutta foundational for Buddhist material culture and pilgrimage.
