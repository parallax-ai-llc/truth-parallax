The chapter narrating the Buddha's severe illness at Vesali and his decision to relinquish the life force is among the most doctrinally significant passages in the Pali canon. The Buddha tells Ananda that he could, if asked, remain alive for an entire age (kappa), but Ananda, his mind clouded by Mara, fails to make the request. When the Buddha subsequently announces his coming parinibbana, Ananda is stricken with grief. The teaching 'Be a lamp unto yourselves' (attadipa viharatha) — delivered in this context of impending loss — becomes the Theravada tradition's foundational statement of self-reliance. The practitioner must rely on the Dhamma itself, not on any external authority, a principle that has shaped Theravada institutional structure and practice ever since.
