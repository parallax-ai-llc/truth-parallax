The Buddha's journey from Rajagaha toward his final destination passes through various towns and villages, each stop providing an occasion for teaching. The Theravada tradition gives particular weight to the 'Mirror of the Dhamma' (dhammadasa) teaching, in which the Buddha provides criteria for recognizing one's own level of attainment: a stream-enterer possesses unshakable confidence in the Buddha, Dhamma, and Sangha, and is endowed with the virtues dear to the Noble Ones. The encounter with Ambapali, the courtesan who donates her mango grove, demonstrates the Buddha's teaching that the Dhamma is available to all regardless of social status. The chapter's travelogue structure gives it historical and geographical specificity valued by the Theravada tradition as evidence of the sutta's authenticity.
