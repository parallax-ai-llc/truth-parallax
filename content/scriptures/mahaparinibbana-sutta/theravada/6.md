The cremation of the Buddha's body and the distribution of relics among eight kingdoms provides the scriptural foundation for stupa worship throughout the Theravada world. The Brahmin Dona's role in mediating the competing claims for the relics and his establishment of a stupa over the measuring vessel reflect the political and devotional dynamics that shaped early Buddhist material culture. The Theravada tradition traces the great stupas of Sri Lanka, Myanmar, and Thailand to relics distributed at this time or subsequently obtained from the original eight shares. The chapter establishes relic veneration as a legitimate and important practice within Theravada Buddhism, alongside — but distinct from — the practice of meditation and study.
