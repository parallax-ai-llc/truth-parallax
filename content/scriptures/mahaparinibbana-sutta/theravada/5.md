The account of the Buddha's passing through the four jhanas and the formless attainments, followed by the reversal and final entry into parinibbana from the fourth jhana, is one of the most carefully preserved passages in the Pali canon. The Theravada tradition understands this as demonstrating the Buddha's complete mastery of all meditative attainments and his conscious, deliberate entry into final nibbana. The earthquakes, celestial offerings, and the lamentation of gods and humans that follow express the cosmic significance of this event. The elder Anuruddha's composure and his verses — 'With mind unshaken... the sage has gone to peace' — model the appropriate response: grief acknowledged but transcended through understanding of impermanence.
