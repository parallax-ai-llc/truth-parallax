The arrival at Kusinara and the events preceding the parinibbana are narrated with emotional intensity. The Buddha's final conversion — of the wanderer Subhadda, who becomes the last direct disciple — demonstrates that the teaching power remained undiminished to the end. The Theravada tradition particularly values the Buddha's instruction regarding the Vinaya: 'After my passing, the sangha may abolish the lesser and minor rules if it wishes.' This statement became the subject of extensive commentary, as the First Council ultimately decided not to abolish any rules, establishing the conservative principle that has characterized Theravada monastic discipline. The chapter also contains the famous declaration that the Dhamma and Vinaya shall be the teacher after the Buddha's passing.
