## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Leviticus is profoundly critical. The entire sacrificial and priestly system is read as the Demiurge's mechanism for binding human souls to the material world through ritual obligation. Where orthodox readers see holiness laws as divine instruction, Gnostics see the chains of the archons — regulations designed to keep the divine spark imprisoned in matter through fear, guilt, and endless ritual performance.

## The Gnostic Inversion

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Sacrifices draw Israel near to God | Sacrifices feed the Demiurge's appetite for material worship |
| Purity laws sanctify the people | Purity obsession traps the soul in bodily concerns |
| The priesthood mediates divine presence | The priests are agents of the Demiurge |
| "Be holy" is a call to divine life | "Be holy" means submission to the Demiurge's arbitrary rules |
| The Day of Atonement reconciles with God | Atonement rituals perpetuate the illusion that the Demiurge deserves worship |

## Key Gnostic Themes in Leviticus

### Sacrifice as Entrapment
For Valentinian and Sethian Gnostics, the entire sacrificial system reveals the Demiurge's true nature — a being who demands blood, smoke, and material offerings. True worship is interior gnosis, not external ritual.

### The Body as Prison
The elaborate purity laws regarding the body — skin diseases, bodily emissions, food — reflect the Demiurge's obsession with matter. The pneumatic (spiritual) person recognizes that the body is a temporary prison, not a temple to be regulated.

### The Anti-Priestly Stance
Gnostic texts frequently oppose the established priesthood. The *Testimony of Truth* and the *Apocalypse of Peter* criticize those who serve "a god of error" through ritual sacrifice.
