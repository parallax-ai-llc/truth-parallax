## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Leviticus emphasizes the continuity between the Old Testament priesthood and sacrificial system and the sacramental life of the Church. The Levitical priesthood prefigures the ministerial priesthood; the sacrifices anticipate the Eucharist; and the Holiness Code informs Catholic moral theology. The Catechism frequently references Leviticus in its treatment of worship, priesthood, and the moral life.

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Sacramental continuity**: The Levitical sacrifices are fulfilled but not abolished — they continue in the Eucharistic sacrifice
- **Priesthood**: The ordained priesthood stands in continuity with the Aaronic priesthood, perfected in Christ the eternal High Priest
- **Natural law**: The moral precepts of Leviticus (especially ch. 19) express the natural law accessible to all human beings
- **Liturgical formation**: Catholic liturgy draws heavily on Levitical patterns of sacred space, sacred time, and ritual action
