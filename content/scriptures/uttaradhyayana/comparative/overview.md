## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Uttaradhyayana illuminates striking parallels with "last teachings" and ethical literature across traditions. The text's combination of urgency, comprehensive moral instruction, and soteriological vision invites rich cross-traditional analysis.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Final Discourses Compared

The Uttaradhyayana as Mahavira's last teaching parallels the Buddha's Mahaparinibbana Sutta, Jesus's farewell discourse in John's Gospel, and Moses's final addresses in Deuteronomy. Each tradition preserves the last words of its founder with special reverence.

### Virtue Lists

The Uttaradhyayana's ten supreme virtues can be compared with Buddhist paramitas, Christian cardinal and theological virtues, and Confucian virtues, revealing both universal moral intuitions and tradition-specific emphases.

### Monastic Ideals

The text's portrait of the ideal monk invites comparison with Buddhist monastic ideals, Christian religious life, Hindu sannyasa, and Sufi asceticism.

## Comparative Framework

| Concept | Jainism (Uttaradhyayana) | Buddhism | Christianity |
|---------|------------------------|----------|-------------|
| Last teaching | Mahavira's final discourse | Mahaparinibbana | Farewell discourse (John) |
| Virtue list | Ten virtues | Paramitas | Cardinal virtues |
| Monastic ideal | Complete renunciation | Middle way | Evangelical counsels |
