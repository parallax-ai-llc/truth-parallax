## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Uttaradhyayana finds in Mahavira's final teachings a wisdom that transcends sectarian boundaries. The text's emphasis on non-violence, truthfulness, compassion, and self-discipline speaks to values recognized across all major religious traditions.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Final Wisdom

Every major religious tradition preserves the last teachings of its founder with special reverence. The Uttaradhyayana's position as Mahavira's farewell gives it a universal poignancy that resonates across religious boundaries.

### Shared Virtues

The ten supreme virtues of the Uttaradhyayana -- forgiveness, humility, uprightness, contentment, truthfulness, self-restraint, austerity, renunciation, non-attachment, and celibacy -- overlap significantly with virtue traditions in Buddhism, Christianity, Islam, and other faiths.

### Monastic Common Ground

The text's portrait of the ideal ascetic life finds parallels across traditions, creating natural connections with Christian, Buddhist, Hindu, and Sufi monastic practitioners.

## Interfaith Connections

| Jain Virtue | Parallel in Other Traditions |
|-------------|------------------------------|
| Forgiveness (uttama kshama) | Christian forgiveness, Buddhist metta |
| Humility (mardava) | Christian humility, Islamic tawadu |
| Truthfulness (satya) | Buddhist right speech, Jewish emet |
| Non-attachment (akincanya) | Buddhist non-clinging, Christian poverty |
