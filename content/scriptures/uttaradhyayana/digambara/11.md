## On Non-Violence

The Digambara tradition reads this non-violence as the supreme ethical principle with characteristic doctrinal strictness, emphasizing the necessity of complete renunciation and the precise application of Jain principles. Digambara interpreters stress that non-violence as the supreme ethical principle must be understood within the framework of absolute non-attachment and rigorous ascetic practice that defines the Digambara path.
