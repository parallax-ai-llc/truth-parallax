## Overview

The Digambara tradition approaches the Uttaradhyayana with deep reverence as a record of Mahavira's final teachings before his liberation. While the Digambara recension may differ from the Shvetambara version, the text's emphasis on complete renunciation and the urgency of the spiritual quest resonates powerfully with Digambara values.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Last Teachings

Digambara interpreters regard the Uttaradhyayana as Mahavira's most urgent and essential teachings, delivered with the authority of one about to achieve complete liberation. The text carries the weight of final words.

### Complete Renunciation

The chapters exhorting monks to abandon all worldly attachments are read as confirming the Digambara insistence on total renunciation, including the abandonment of clothing, as necessary for the highest spiritual attainments.

### The Ten Virtues

The Uttaradhyayana's enumeration of the ten supreme virtues -- forgiveness, humility, uprightness, contentment, truthfulness, self-restraint, austerity, renunciation, non-attachment, and celibacy -- is regarded as the definitive summary of the Digambara ethical ideal.
