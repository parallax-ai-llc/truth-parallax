## Overview

Philosophical interpretation of the Uttaradhyayana examines the text's ethical theory, its philosophy of liberation, and its account of the virtues. The text presents a comprehensive moral philosophy grounded in Jain metaphysics but addressing universal ethical questions.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Virtue Ethics

The Uttaradhyayana's enumeration and analysis of the ten supreme virtues constitutes a sophisticated virtue ethics that can be compared with Aristotelian, Buddhist, and Confucian approaches to the moral life.

### Philosophy of Liberation

The text's account of how ethical conduct, ascetic practice, and right knowledge lead to liberation raises fundamental philosophical questions about the relationship between morality and metaphysics.

### Moral Urgency

The framing as Mahavira's final teaching introduces an existential urgency that raises philosophical questions about finitude, mortality, and the meaning of spiritual aspiration.
