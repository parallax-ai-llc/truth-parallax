---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 24"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 24
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of time and use it wisely, for time, once lost, can never be recovered.

**2** The present moment is the only moment in which we can act. The past is gone, the future has not yet come. Only the present is real.

**3** The wise monk uses every moment for spiritual practice — for meditation, for study, for the observance of the vows, for the cultivation of virtue.

**4** He does not waste time in idle talk, in reminiscence, in daydreaming, or in planning for a future that may never come.

**5** The duration of human life is uncertain. Death may come at any moment — in youth, in middle age, or in old age. No one knows the hour of his death.

**6** Therefore, the monk should practise as if each day were his last. He should not postpone his spiritual practice, for there may be no tomorrow.

**7** Time is the great devourer. It consumes youth, health, beauty, wealth, and life itself. Nothing can resist the power of time.

**8** But the soul is beyond time. It is eternal, unchanging, and imperishable. He who has realized the soul has conquered time.

**9** The contemplation of time and its power should motivate the monk to practise with urgency and sincerity. Every moment of life is precious.

**10** Let the monk use this precious human life wisely. Let him dedicate every moment to the purification of his soul and the attainment of liberation.
