---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 12"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Mahavira said: The story of Harikeshiya. There was once a monk named Harikeshiya who was born of low caste but possessed great virtue and learning.

**2** He was treated with contempt by some on account of his birth. But he said: "The soul has no caste. Caste belongs to the body, not to the soul. The soul is pure consciousness."

**3** He practised the severest austerities. He fasted for long periods. He meditated day and night. He conquered all his passions.

**4** When the gods saw his spiritual attainment, they honoured him, though men of higher caste had scorned him. The gods said: "This monk is greater than kings, greater than gods, for he has conquered himself."

**5** This story teaches that the Jain path is open to all, regardless of birth. The soul of the outcast is the same as the soul of the king. Both can attain liberation.

**6** He who judges another by birth is deluded by the illusion of the body. He who judges by character and spiritual attainment sees with the eye of truth.

**7** Mahavira Himself accepted disciples from all backgrounds. He said: "In my order, there is no distinction of high and low. All who sincerely seek liberation are welcome."

**8** The Jain community is built upon the principle of spiritual equality. What matters is not where one comes from but where one is going — toward liberation or toward bondage.

**9** The monk of low birth who practises the vows with sincerity is greater than the Brahmana who neglects them. The vows, not the birth, determine the spiritual status of a person.

**10** Let all seekers take heart from this teaching. The path of liberation is open to all. No barrier of birth, caste, or gender can prevent the sincere seeker from attaining the supreme goal.
