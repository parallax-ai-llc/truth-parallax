---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Mahavira said: The soul that is overcome by attachment is like a bird caught in a net. The more it struggles with passion, the more entangled it becomes.

**2** But the soul that conquers attachment is like a bird that has broken free from the net. It soars into the sky of liberation, unbounded and free.

**3** Attachment to the body is the root of all bondage. The wise monk knows that the body is not the self. The body is born, grows old, and dies. But the self is eternal.

**4** He who identifies with the body experiences the sufferings of the body — hunger, thirst, heat, cold, disease, and death. He who knows the self is free from these sufferings.

**5** The monk should contemplate: "This body is not mine. It is a temporary dwelling, given to me for the purpose of spiritual practice. I shall use it wisely and not be attached to it."

**6** The monk should not adorn the body, nor should he neglect it. He should maintain it as a means of spiritual practice, neither pampering it nor tormenting it beyond what is necessary.

**7** He who has conquered attachment to the body is free from the fear of death. Death is merely the shedding of the body, as a snake sheds its skin. The self continues.

**8** The wise monk looks forward to the shedding of the body, as a prisoner looks forward to the opening of the prison gates. For the body is the prison of the soul.

**9** But the monk should not hasten death, for the body is the instrument of spiritual practice. He should endure life with patience and use every moment for the purification of the soul.

**10** This is the teaching on attachment: conquer it gradually, through the practice of renunciation, through the contemplation of the soul, and through the grace of the Jinas.
