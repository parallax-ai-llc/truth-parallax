---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 21"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 21
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should practise the confession and repentance of his faults, for this is the means of purifying the soul of accumulated sin.

**2** The monk should examine his conduct daily and ask himself: "Have I committed any transgression today — in thought, word, or deed?"

**3** If he finds that he has transgressed, he should confess the transgression before his teacher and sincerely repent for it.

**4** Confession should be complete and honest. The monk should not hide any fault, for a hidden fault festers and grows, while a confessed fault is healed.

**5** Repentance should be sincere and heartfelt. The monk should resolve not to repeat the transgression and should take steps to prevent its recurrence.

**6** The teacher should listen with compassion and assign an appropriate penance. The penance should be proportionate to the transgression and should be performed with sincerity.

**7** The practice of confession and repentance is not a sign of weakness but of strength. The monk who confesses shows that he values truth above his own ego.

**8** Regular confession prevents the accumulation of small faults that can gradually undermine the monk's spiritual progress.

**9** The practice of confession also strengthens the bond between the monk and his teacher. It creates a relationship of trust and mutual support.

**10** He who practises regular confession and sincere repentance purifies his soul of accumulated karma and hastens his progress toward liberation.
