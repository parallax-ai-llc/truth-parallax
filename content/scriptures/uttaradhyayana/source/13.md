---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 13"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 13
---

**1** Mahavira said: The story of Chitragupta. A householder named Chitragupta lived righteously, observing the minor vows, giving charity, and worshipping the Jinas.

**2** When the time came for him to die, the messengers of both merit and demerit appeared before him. His good karma and bad karma were weighed upon the scales of justice.

**3** His acts of charity, non-violence, and devotion were weighed against his moments of anger, attachment, and carelessness.

**4** Because his good karma outweighed his bad, he was reborn in a celestial realm, where he enjoyed the fruits of his virtue.

**5** But celestial life is not permanent. When his merit was exhausted, he fell again to the human realm, where he had the opportunity to resume his spiritual practice.

**6** This story teaches that the fruits of good karma, though pleasant, are temporary. Only liberation is permanent. Therefore, the wise seek liberation, not heavenly pleasures.

**7** The householder should practise the vows and charities not for the sake of heavenly rewards but for the purification of the soul.

**8** He should give up attachment to the results of his actions. He should act rightly because it is right, not because he expects a reward.

**9** The true fruit of righteous action is the purification of the mind and the progress of the soul toward liberation. All other fruits are secondary.

**10** Let the householder remember this teaching and practise his duties with the right motivation — not for heaven, but for liberation.
