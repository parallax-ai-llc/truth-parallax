---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 19"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 19
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should reflect upon the cycle of transmigration and the suffering it entails.

**2** The soul has wandered through the cycle of birth and death since beginningless time. It has been born and has died innumerable times.

**3** There is no relationship that the soul has not had. There is no form that the soul has not taken. There is no place where the soul has not been born.

**4** In this vast wandering, the soul has experienced every kind of pleasure and every kind of pain. Yet it has not attained satisfaction, for worldly pleasure is never satisfying.

**5** The pleasures of the senses are like salt water — the more one drinks, the thirstier one becomes. They promise satisfaction but deliver only more craving.

**6** The sufferings of samsara are innumerable: the suffering of birth, the suffering of old age, the suffering of disease, the suffering of death, the suffering of separation from loved ones, the suffering of association with the unloved.

**7** The wise monk, seeing the endless cycle of suffering, resolves to break free. He applies himself to the practice of non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation with renewed determination.

**8** He knows that every moment spent in spiritual practice brings him closer to liberation and further from the cycle of suffering.

**9** He does not postpone his practice, saying "Tomorrow I shall begin." He begins now, in this very moment, for the moment of death is uncertain.

**10** This contemplation of transmigration and suffering is the great motivator. It drives the monk to practice and keeps him on the path when difficulties arise.
