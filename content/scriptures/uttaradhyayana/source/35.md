---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 35"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 35
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the fourteen stages of spiritual development through which the soul progresses on its way to liberation.

**2** The first stage is wrong faith. In this stage, the soul is completely deluded and has no understanding of the true nature of reality.

**3** The second stage is the downfall from right faith. In this stage, the soul has a taste of truth but loses it due to the power of its karma.

**4** The third stage is mixed faith — a combination of right and wrong faith. The fourth stage is right faith without self-control — the soul understands the truth but has not yet begun to practise.

**5** The fifth stage is partial self-control. The sixth stage is complete self-control with occasional lapses. The seventh stage is complete self-control without lapses.

**6** The eighth, ninth, and tenth stages involve the progressive suppression of the passions — first the grosser passions, then the subtler ones, then the subtlest.

**7** The eleventh stage is the stage of suppressed passions. The twelfth stage is the stage of destroyed passions. In this stage, the soul has completely eliminated all passions.

**8** The thirteenth stage is the stage of omniscience with activity. The soul has attained Kevala Jnana but still has a body and performs minimal activities.

**9** The fourteenth stage is the stage of omniscience without activity. The soul's physical activities have ceased, and it is about to shed its last karma and attain liberation.

**10** At the conclusion of the fourteenth stage, the soul sheds its last karma and rises to Siddha Loka, attaining final and irrevocable liberation.
