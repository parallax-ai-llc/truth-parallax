---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 14"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of food, for food is connected with violence and must be accepted with great care.

**2** The monk should not cook his own food, for cooking involves the killing of fire-bodies and the destruction of plant-bodies.

**3** He should accept only food that has been prepared by householders for their own use, of which a portion is freely offered to the monk.

**4** He should not beg for food, nor should he indicate his preferences. He should accept whatever is placed in his bowl with gratitude and equanimity.

**5** He should not eat food that has been specially prepared for him, for this would make the householder's violence attributable to the monk.

**6** He should not eat at night, for in the darkness, insects and other small creatures may fall into the food unseen.

**7** He should eat only once a day, in moderation, taking only enough to sustain the body for spiritual practice. He should not eat for pleasure or for taste.

**8** Before eating, the monk should reflect: "This food sustains the body, which is the instrument of spiritual practice. I eat not for pleasure but for the continuation of my spiritual work."

**9** The restrictions on food are not arbitrary rules but expressions of the principle of non-violence applied to the act of eating.

**10** He who eats with awareness and restraint generates less karma than he who eats with attachment and indulgence. The monk should always eat mindfully.
