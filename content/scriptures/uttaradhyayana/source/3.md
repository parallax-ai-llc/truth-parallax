---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Mahavira said: There are four states of existence through which the soul wanders: celestial, human, animal, and hellish.

**2** In the celestial state, the soul enjoys great pleasures and long life. But even the gods are bound by karma and must eventually fall to a lower state.

**3** In the human state, the soul has the unique opportunity to practise the path of liberation. Only in the human state can the threefold path be followed to its completion.

**4** In the animal state, the soul is driven by instinct and passion. It has little capacity for self-control and spiritual progress. Yet even animals accumulate and shed karma.

**5** In the hellish state, the soul suffers the consequences of its most severe negative karma. The sufferings of hell are terrible, but they are not eternal; they last only as long as the karma that caused them.

**6** The soul moves from state to state, from birth to birth, driven by its karma. It has been a god, a human, an animal, and a hell-being countless times.

**7** In every state, the soul carries with it the accumulated karma of all its past lives. This karma determines the circumstances of each new birth.

**8** The wise monk contemplates these four states and resolves to free himself from the cycle of transmigration. He practises non-violence, self-control, and meditation.

**9** He knows that the pleasures of heaven are temporary, the sufferings of hell are not permanent, and the human life is the precious opportunity for liberation.

**10** Therefore, let the monk not waste this human life in the pursuit of worldly pleasures. Let him use it for the purpose for which it was given — the liberation of the soul.
