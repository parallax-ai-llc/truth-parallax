---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 8"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Mahavira said: The story of Kapila. There was once a Brahmana named Kapila who performed many sacrifices, thinking that the killing of animals in sacrifice was righteous.

**2** A Jain monk approached him and said: "O Brahmana, how can the killing of innocent creatures be righteous? The soul of the animal is the same as your soul. It desires to live, just as you desire to live."

**3** Kapila said: "The scriptures prescribe animal sacrifice. It is sanctioned by tradition." The monk replied: "Tradition that causes suffering is not true dharma. True dharma is non-violence."

**4** The monk continued: "Would you consent to be sacrificed for the benefit of another? Then why do you sacrifice others for your own benefit?"

**5** Kapila was moved by these words. He said: "I have been blind, following tradition without thinking. I see now that non-violence is the true dharma."

**6** He abandoned the sacrificial rites and adopted the path of non-violence. He became a monk and eventually attained liberation.

**7** This story teaches that blind adherence to tradition is not virtue. True virtue requires the exercise of reason, compassion, and the willingness to change when one sees the truth.

**8** The Jinas teach that no ritual, however ancient, is justified if it involves the taking of life. The soul of every creature is sacred.

**9** He who abandons violence in the name of religion and adopts non-violence in the name of truth has taken the first step on the path of liberation.

**10** Let all seekers examine their traditions in the light of non-violence. Let them abandon what is harmful and embrace what is beneficial to all beings.
