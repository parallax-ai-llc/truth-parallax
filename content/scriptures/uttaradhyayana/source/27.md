---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 27"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 27
---

**1** Mahavira said: The story of the king and the four friends. A king had four friends, each of whom advised him differently when he fell into trouble.

**2** The first friend said: "Fight your enemies with force." The second said: "Negotiate with cunning." The third said: "Flee to safety." The fourth said: "Look within yourself for the solution."

**3** The king followed the advice of the first three friends and found only temporary relief. His enemies returned, the negotiations failed, and there was nowhere left to flee.

**4** Finally, he followed the advice of the fourth friend and looked within himself. He found the strength to face his troubles with equanimity and the wisdom to resolve them without violence.

**5** This story teaches that the solutions to the soul's problems are found within, not without. External remedies provide only temporary relief.

**6** The soul's true enemies are not external foes but internal passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed. These must be conquered from within.

**7** The monk who looks within finds the infinite resources of the soul — infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy.

**8** These resources are available to every soul, for they are the soul's inherent nature. They are obscured by karma but never destroyed.

**9** The practice of meditation is the means of looking within. Through meditation, the monk discovers the treasures hidden within his own soul.

**10** He who has found the treasure within has no need of external wealth, external power, or external protection. He is self-sufficient, self-contained, and self-luminous.
