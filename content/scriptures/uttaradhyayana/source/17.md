---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 17"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 17
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should reflect upon the impermanence of all things, for the contemplation of impermanence is a powerful means of overcoming attachment.

**2** All that is composed will be decomposed. All that is born will die. All that rises will fall. Nothing in this world is permanent.

**3** Youth is followed by old age. Health is followed by disease. Life is followed by death. Beauty is followed by decay.

**4** The body that is cherished today will be food for worms tomorrow. The wealth that is hoarded will pass to others. The fame that is sought will be forgotten.

**5** The kingdoms that once flourished have turned to dust. The kings who once ruled are no more remembered. The cities that once prospered are now ruins.

**6** Seeing this, the wise monk does not attach himself to the body, to wealth, to fame, or to power. He knows that all these are transient and will pass away.

**7** He fixes his heart upon the eternal — the soul, which is beyond birth and death, beyond decay and destruction.

**8** The contemplation of impermanence is not pessimism. It is realism. It is the clear-eyed seeing of things as they are.

**9** From this clear seeing arises detachment. From detachment arises peace. From peace arises the clarity of mind in which the soul can be perceived.

**10** He who contemplates impermanence daily, who reminds himself of the transient nature of all worldly things — he loosens the bonds of attachment and hastens his progress toward liberation.
