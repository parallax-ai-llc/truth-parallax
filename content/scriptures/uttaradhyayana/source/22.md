---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 22"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 22
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of attention and carefulness, for these are the guards against the influx of karma.

**2** Attention to walking: The monk should walk with his eyes on the ground, watching each step, placing his feet carefully to avoid harming any creature.

**3** Attention to speaking: The monk should speak only what is necessary, true, and beneficial. He should avoid unnecessary speech, for careless words can cause harm.

**4** Attention to eating: The monk should examine his food before eating, ensuring that it is free from insects and other living beings. He should eat mindfully.

**5** Attention to placing and removing objects: The monk should look carefully before placing anything down or picking anything up, lest he harm a creature hidden beneath.

**6** Attention to the disposal of waste: The monk should dispose of waste in a clean place, away from living beings, ensuring that no creature is harmed in the process.

**7** These five forms of attention, the Samitis, are the practical expression of non-violence in daily life. They transform every ordinary action into a spiritual practice.

**8** In addition to the five Samitis, the monk should observe the three Guptis — the guards of body, speech, and mind.

**9** The guard of the body means moving with care and restraint. The guard of speech means speaking with care and restraint. The guard of the mind means thinking with care and restraint.

**10** He who practises the five Samitis and the three Guptis lives a life of complete awareness. Every moment becomes an opportunity for spiritual progress.
