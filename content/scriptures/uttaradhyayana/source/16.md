---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 16"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 16
---

**1** Mahavira said: The ten virtues of dharma are the foundation of all spiritual practice. He who cultivates them progresses rapidly on the path of liberation.

**2** Forgiveness: The monk should forgive all beings for all offences. He should harbor no grudge, no resentment, no desire for revenge.

**3** Humility: The monk should be free from pride in his learning, his austerities, his family, and his spiritual attainments. He should regard himself as the least of all beings.

**4** Straightforwardness: The monk should be free from deceit and hypocrisy. His inner life and his outer life should be in harmony.

**5** Contentment: The monk should be satisfied with whatever comes to him, without craving more and without complaining about less.

**6** Truth: The monk should speak what is true, what is pleasant, and what is beneficial. He should avoid all forms of falsehood.

**7** Self-restraint: The monk should control his senses and his mind. He should not be swayed by desire or aversion.

**8** Austerity: The monk should practise penance for the purification of the soul, enduring hardship with equanimity.

**9** Renunciation: The monk should give up all possessions and attachments. He should live without any sense of ownership.

**10** Non-attachment and chastity complete the ten. He who cultivates these ten virtues is established in dharma and progresses toward the supreme goal.
