---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 15"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should cultivate solitude, for in solitude the mind is stilled and the soul can be contemplated.

**2** Solitude is of two kinds: external and internal. External solitude is the withdrawal from the company of others. Internal solitude is the withdrawal of the mind from its objects.

**3** External solitude without internal solitude is of little value. The monk who sits alone in the forest but whose mind is full of worldly thoughts has not attained solitude.

**4** Internal solitude can be maintained even in the midst of a crowd. The monk whose mind is fixed upon the soul is in solitude even when surrounded by thousands.

**5** The ideal practice combines both. The monk should seek a quiet place, sit in meditation, and withdraw his mind from all objects, fixing it upon the self alone.

**6** In solitude, the passions are weakened, for they are fed by contact with the world. In solitude, the mind becomes clear, for it is not disturbed by external stimuli.

**7** In solitude, the soul begins to shine with its own light, as the sun becomes visible when the clouds are dispersed.

**8** The monk should not fear solitude. He should welcome it as a friend, for it is in solitude that the deepest spiritual discoveries are made.

**9** He should not seek company out of loneliness, nor should he shun it out of pride. He should accept company when it comes and solitude when it comes, with equal equanimity.

**10** The perfected monk is in solitude at all times, for his consciousness is always turned inward, always resting in the self, regardless of external circumstances.
