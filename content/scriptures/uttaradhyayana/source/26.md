---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 26"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 26
---

**1** Mahavira said: The parable of the arrow. A man was struck by a poisoned arrow. His friends called a physician to remove it.

**2** But the man said: "I will not let the physician remove the arrow until I know who shot it, what his caste was, what kind of bow he used, and what the arrow was made of."

**3** The man died before his questions were answered. The arrow killed him while he was occupied with irrelevant inquiries.

**4** So it is with the soul in this world. The arrow of suffering is embedded in it, and the poison of karma is spreading. But the soul, instead of seeking the remedy, wastes time in speculative questions.

**5** The wise man does not ask: "When was the universe created? Who created it? Why does evil exist?" He asks: "How can I free myself from suffering? What is the path of liberation?"

**6** The teaching of the Jinas answers these practical questions. It shows the way out of suffering. It reveals the path of liberation.

**7** Let the seeker not waste time in fruitless speculation but apply himself to the practical work of self-purification.

**8** The five vows, the practice of austerities, the cultivation of the ten virtues, the daily meditation — these are the practical means of removing the arrow of suffering.

**9** He who applies these means with sincerity and perseverance will find the arrow removed and the poison neutralized. He will be healed of the disease of samsara.

**10** This is the practical wisdom of the Jain teaching: focus on what can be done, practise what leads to liberation, and leave aside what is merely speculative.
