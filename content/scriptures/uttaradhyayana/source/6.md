---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 6"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should practise austerities for the purification of the soul and the shedding of accumulated karma.

**2** External austerities are: fasting, eating less than one's fill, restricting the variety of food, giving up delicacies, sleeping in isolated places, and mortifying the body.

**3** Fasting is the most powerful external austerity. By fasting, the monk weakens the hold of the body upon the soul and strengthens the soul's dominion over the body.

**4** The monk should fast with a peaceful mind, without complaint, without pride, without expecting praise. He who fasts with attachment to the act of fasting gains little benefit.

**5** Internal austerities are: repentance, reverence, service, study, meditation, and renunciation of the body. These are more powerful than external austerities.

**6** Repentance purifies the soul of past transgressions. The monk should regularly examine his conduct and sincerely repent for any lapses.

**7** Reverence strengthens the spiritual bond between the student and the teacher, between the seeker and the tradition. Without reverence, progress is impossible.

**8** Service to the monks and the community generates merit and cultivates humility. The monk who serves others purifies his own soul.

**9** Study of the scriptures illumines the mind and strengthens right faith. The monk should study diligently, reflecting on the meaning of what he reads.

**10** Meditation is the direct means of shedding karma and realizing the soul. The monk should practise meditation daily, gradually deepening his concentration and his insight.
