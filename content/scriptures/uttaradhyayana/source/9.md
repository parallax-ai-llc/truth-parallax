---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 9"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Mahavira said: The parable of the traveller. A traveller, walking through the forest, came upon a well covered with creepers. He fell into it and caught hold of a branch.

**2** Below him, at the bottom of the well, a great serpent waited with open mouth. On two sides, two mice — one white and one black — gnawed at the branch he held.

**3** Above him, bees had built a hive, and drops of honey fell upon his lips. Forgetting the serpent below and the mice above, he tasted the honey and thought: "How sweet!"

**4** This is the condition of the worldly being. The well is the cycle of birth and death. The serpent is death itself. The white mouse is day, and the black mouse is night — they gnaw away the branch of life.

**5** The drops of honey are the pleasures of the senses. The foolish man, enchanted by these pleasures, forgets the danger of death and the suffering of samsara.

**6** The wise monk, seeing through the illusion of sense-pleasure, lets go of the branch and faces the truth. He conquers his fear of death and his craving for pleasure.

**7** He who clings to the branch of worldly life, tasting the honey of sense-pleasure, is doomed to fall into the mouth of death again and again.

**8** He who lets go of the branch, who renounces sense-pleasure, who conquers the fear of death — he alone escapes the well of samsara and attains the freedom of liberation.

**9** Therefore, O monks, be not deceived by the honey of worldly pleasures. See through the illusion. Recognize the danger. Practise renunciation.

**10** The pleasures of the senses are like poison disguised as nectar. They attract the soul but bind it with chains of karma. The wise monk avoids them as he would avoid a deadly serpent.
