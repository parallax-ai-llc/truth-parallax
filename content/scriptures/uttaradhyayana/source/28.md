---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 28"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 28
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of speech and its consequences, for speech is a powerful instrument of both bondage and liberation.

**2** Speech that is true, pleasant, and beneficial leads to the shedding of karma. Speech that is false, harsh, and harmful leads to the accumulation of karma.

**3** The monk should think before speaking: "Is what I am about to say true? Is it necessary? Is it kind? Will it benefit the hearer?"

**4** If the answer to any of these questions is no, the monk should remain silent. Silence is better than harmful speech.

**5** The monk should avoid four kinds of harmful speech: anger-laden speech, pride-laden speech, deceit-laden speech, and greed-laden speech.

**6** Anger-laden speech wounds others and creates enemies. Pride-laden speech alienates others and creates resentment. Deceit-laden speech destroys trust and creates suspicion. Greed-laden speech manipulates others and creates bondage.

**7** The monk who has mastered his speech is a master of himself. For speech reveals the state of the mind, and the state of the mind determines the state of the soul.

**8** Let the monk practise right speech daily. Let him speak only what is true, necessary, and kind. Let him restrain himself from all harmful speech.

**9** In time, right speech becomes natural, and the monk no longer needs to struggle against the impulse to speak harmfully.

**10** The perfected monk's speech is like honey — sweet, nourishing, and healing. His words bring comfort to the afflicted, guidance to the confused, and inspiration to the seeker.
