---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 20"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 20
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should reflect upon the nature of the body and its impurity, for attachment to the body is the root of all bondage.

**2** The body is composed of flesh, blood, bone, and various fluids. It is sustained by food, which is itself the product of violence. It produces waste and decay.

**3** The body, which appears beautiful on the outside, is filled with impurity on the inside. It is like a painted pot filled with filth.

**4** The body is subject to disease, old age, and death. It is a constant source of trouble and anxiety. Yet the deluded soul clings to it as its most precious possession.

**5** The wise monk sees the body as it truly is — a temporary vehicle for the soul's spiritual journey. He does not despise it, for that would be a form of violence. But he does not worship it.

**6** He uses the body as an instrument for spiritual practice — for fasting, for meditation, for the study of the scriptures, for service to others.

**7** When the body is worn out and can no longer serve its purpose, the wise monk lets it go without regret, as a man lets go of a worn-out garment.

**8** The contemplation of the body's impurity is not an expression of self-hatred but of wisdom. It frees the soul from the delusion that happiness depends upon the body.

**9** True happiness belongs to the soul, not to the body. When the soul is purified of its karmic obstructions, it experiences a happiness that no bodily pleasure can approach.

**10** Let the monk contemplate this truth daily and gradually free himself from the attachment to the body, the root of all bondage.
