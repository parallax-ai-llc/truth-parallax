---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 10"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Mahavira said: I shall now describe the nature of the Tirthankara, the ford-maker, who shows the way across the ocean of worldly existence.

**2** A Tirthankara is a soul that has attained omniscience through its own spiritual practice and that establishes the fourfold order of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen.

**3** Twenty-four Tirthankaras appear in each half of each cosmic cycle. The first of the present set is Rishabhadeva, and the last is Mahavira.

**4** A Tirthankara is distinguished from other liberated souls by the fact that He teaches the path of liberation to others. Other souls may attain liberation but do not establish a religious order.

**5** The body of a Tirthankara is extraordinary. It is adorned with the thirty-four auspicious signs and is free from all physical defects.

**6** When a Tirthankara attains omniscience, the gods create a divine assembly hall, the Samavasarana, in which He delivers His teaching. Gods, humans, and animals all attend.

**7** The Tirthankara's teaching is in the form of a divine sound that is understood by all beings in their own language. His teaching is without error, without contradiction, and without omission.

**8** After teaching for many years, the Tirthankara attains final liberation, Nirvana, shedding the last karma and ascending to Siddha Loka.

**9** The teaching of the Tirthankara is preserved by His chief disciples, the Ganadharas, who organize it into the canonical scriptures.

**10** He who follows the teaching of the Tirthankaras with sincerity and devotion, who practises the vows and austerities they prescribed, attains the same liberation that they attained.
