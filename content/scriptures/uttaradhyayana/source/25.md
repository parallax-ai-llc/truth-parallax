---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 25"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 25
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should reflect upon the rarity of human birth and the even greater rarity of encountering the true teaching.

**2** The soul has wandered through countless births in countless forms. Among all these forms, the human birth is the rarest and most precious.

**3** Among human births, birth in a land where the true teaching is available is even rarer. Among such births, the desire for liberation is rarer still.

**4** He who has obtained a human birth, who has encountered the true teaching, and who has the desire for liberation — he should consider himself the most fortunate of all beings.

**5** Let him not waste this rare opportunity. Let him apply himself to spiritual practice with the utmost diligence and sincerity.

**6** As a merchant who has obtained a ship to cross the ocean would not delay but would set sail at once, so the seeker who has obtained the ship of the true teaching should set sail toward liberation without delay.

**7** The human body is the raft, the teaching is the wind, the guru is the helmsman, and liberation is the far shore. All the conditions are met; what remains is the effort.

**8** He who wastes this human birth in the pursuit of worldly pleasures is like a man who has found a diamond but throws it away, thinking it is a pebble.

**9** The pleasures of the world are available in every birth — as a god, as a human, even as an animal. But the opportunity for liberation is available only in the human birth.

**10** Therefore, let the seeker seize this opportunity with both hands and dedicate his life to the attainment of liberation. This is the wisest course of action.
