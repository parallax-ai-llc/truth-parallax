---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 18"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 18
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should reflect upon the loneliness of the soul, for the soul is truly alone in its journey through samsara.

**2** The soul is born alone. It dies alone. It experiences the fruits of its karma alone. No one can share its suffering or its joy.

**3** The friends and family of this life were strangers in the last life and will be strangers in the next. The relationships of this world are temporary and illusory.

**4** The soul has wandered through countless births, taking countless forms — as a god, as a human, as an animal, as a hell-being. In all these births, it has been essentially alone.

**5** No one accompanies the soul at death. The wealth, the family, the friends — all are left behind. Only karma accompanies the soul to the next birth.

**6** Realizing this, the wise monk does not depend upon others for his happiness. He does not seek companionship to fill the void within. He finds his companionship in the self.

**7** He who has found the self has found the only true companion — a companion that never leaves, never betrays, never disappoints.

**8** The monk should not grieve for those who have died, nor should he cling to those who are living. He should regard all relationships with equanimity.

**9** This contemplation of aloneness is not a cause for sadness but for freedom. When the monk realizes that he is alone, he is freed from the burden of expectations and dependencies.

**10** He stands on his own feet, relying on his own strength, following his own path. He is the master of his own destiny, the architect of his own liberation.
