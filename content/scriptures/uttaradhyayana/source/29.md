---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 29"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 29
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the importance of the teacher, the guru, in the spiritual life.

**2** The guru is the lamp that lights the way in the darkness of ignorance. Without the guru's guidance, the seeker stumbles in the dark.

**3** The guru is one who has walked the path before the student. He knows its dangers, its pitfalls, and its rewards. He guides the student with wisdom and compassion.

**4** The student should approach the guru with humility, reverence, and a sincere desire to learn. He should serve the guru with devotion and follow his instructions faithfully.

**5** The guru does not create knowledge in the student. He removes the obstacles that prevent the student from seeing the truth that is already within him.

**6** The relationship between guru and student is the most sacred of all relationships. It is built on trust, respect, and the shared commitment to the truth.

**7** The guru should teach by example as well as by word. His life should embody the virtues he teaches. His conduct should be impeccable.

**8** The student should not blindly follow the guru but should verify the teaching through his own experience. The guru illumines the path; the student must walk it himself.

**9** When the student has attained the same level of realization as the guru, the student becomes a guru for others. Thus the chain of transmission continues.

**10** He who honours the guru, who serves him with devotion, who follows his teaching with sincerity — he makes rapid progress on the path of liberation.
