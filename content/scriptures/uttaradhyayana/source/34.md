---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 34"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 34
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should contemplate the vastness of the universe and the smallness of the self within it, and thereby cultivate humility.

**2** The universe extends infinitely in all directions. It contains innumerable souls, innumerable worlds, innumerable atoms of matter.

**3** The soul of the monk is but one among these innumerable souls. His body is but one among the innumerable bodies. His life is but a moment in the vastness of cosmic time.

**4** Yet this tiny soul, in its essence, is infinite. It possesses infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy — though these are obscured by karma.

**5** The contemplation of the universe's vastness produces humility. The contemplation of the soul's infinite potential produces courage and aspiration.

**6** The monk should hold both truths simultaneously: he is infinitely small in the scheme of the universe, and infinitely great in the potential of his soul.

**7** This paradox is the essence of the spiritual life. The greatest power lies in the smallest vessel. The infinite is hidden in the finite.

**8** The monk who understands this paradox is both humble and confident. He does not think too highly of himself, nor does he think too lowly.

**9** He approaches the path of liberation with the humility of a student and the confidence of one who knows that the goal is attainable.

**10** Let the monk contemplate the vastness of the universe and the greatness of the soul daily, and let these contemplations guide him on his path.
