---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 31"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 31
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should be free from the four great passions: anger, pride, deceit, and greed.

**2** Anger is the fire that burns the soul. It destroys the merit of years of practice in a single moment. The monk should extinguish the fire of anger with the water of forgiveness.

**3** Pride is the wall that separates the soul from others. It creates the illusion of superiority and prevents the monk from seeing the truth. The monk should dissolve the wall of pride with the practice of humility.

**4** Deceit is the fog that obscures the truth. It creates confusion in the mind and prevents the monk from seeing clearly. The monk should disperse the fog of deceit with the light of straightforwardness.

**5** Greed is the chain that binds the soul to the world. It creates insatiable desire and prevents the monk from experiencing contentment. The monk should break the chain of greed with the practice of renunciation.

**6** These four passions are the root causes of all karmic bondage. All other negative qualities — jealousy, hatred, fear, anxiety — are branches of these four roots.

**7** The conquest of these four passions is the central task of spiritual practice. All vows, all austerities, all meditations are directed toward this conquest.

**8** The passions are not conquered all at once but gradually. First they are weakened, then controlled, then subdued, and finally destroyed.

**9** The monk who has destroyed all four passions has reached the tenth stage of spiritual development — the stage of total suppression, from which omniscience can be attained.

**10** Let the monk wage constant war against these four enemies. Let him be vigilant, persistent, and courageous. The victory is certain for the determined seeker.
