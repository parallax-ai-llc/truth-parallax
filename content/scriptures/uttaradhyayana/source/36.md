---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 36"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 36
---

**1** Thus spoke the venerable Mahavira in His final teaching: O long-lived ones, listen well, for this is the essence of all that I have taught.

**2** The soul exists. It is eternal. It is bound by karma. It can be freed. The path of liberation is the threefold path of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct.

**3** Non-violence is the supreme dharma. Truthfulness is the foundation of all virtue. Non-attachment is the key to freedom. Self-control is the means of spiritual progress.

**4** The five great vows — non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness — are the practical expression of right conduct.

**5** The practice of austerities, both external and internal, is the means of shedding accumulated karma. Meditation is the direct path to the realization of the soul.

**6** The guru is the guide on the path. The scriptures are the map. The sangha is the community of fellow travellers. The soul itself is the traveller.

**7** The goal is Moksha — liberation — the state of infinite perfection that is the birthright of every soul. It is attainable by all who sincerely seek it.

**8** Let every monk and every householder strive toward this goal with sincerity, perseverance, and devotion. The path may be long, but the goal is certain.

**9** May all beings be happy. May all beings be peaceful. May all beings be free from suffering. May all beings attain liberation.

**10** Thus ends the Uttaradhyayana, the last teaching of the venerable Mahavira, the twenty-fourth Tirthankara. Parasparopagraho Jivanam — souls render service to one another. Om, peace to all beings.
