---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 30"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 30
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the importance of the sangha, the spiritual community, in supporting the individual's practice.

**2** The sangha consists of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen who follow the teaching of the Jinas. Together they form a community of mutual support and encouragement.

**3** The monk in isolation may falter, but the monk in the company of fellow monks is strengthened. The sangha provides accountability, inspiration, and fellowship.

**4** The laypeople support the monks with food, clothing, shelter, and medicine. The monks support the laypeople with teaching, guidance, and spiritual example.

**5** This mutual support is the foundation of the Jain community. Without the monks, the laypeople would lack spiritual guidance. Without the laypeople, the monks could not sustain their practice.

**6** The sangha should be united by the common commitment to non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation. Internal disputes should be resolved through dialogue and forgiveness.

**7** The sangha should welcome all sincere seekers, regardless of their background. The teaching of the Jinas is for all who wish to follow it.

**8** The sangha should maintain the integrity of the teaching. It should not compromise the core principles of non-violence and truthfulness for the sake of popularity or convenience.

**9** The sangha should also engage with the wider world, sharing the message of non-violence and compassion with all who will listen.

**10** He who is a faithful member of the sangha, who supports it with his time, his resources, and his devotion, acquires great merit and progresses on the path of liberation.
