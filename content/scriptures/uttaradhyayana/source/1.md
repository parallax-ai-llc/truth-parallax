---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus spoke the venerable Mahavira: O long-lived ones! Wander not heedlessly. A monk who wanders heedlessly does not reach the path of liberation.

**2** As a tortoise draws in its limbs within its shell, so the wise monk should restrain his senses, his mind, and his speech.

**3** He who has conquered anger and pride, who has overcome deceit and greed, who is free from all attachment — he alone is on the right path.

**4** As a bird on a tree, when it sees the hunter approaching, spreads its wings and flies away, so the wise monk, seeing the hunter called Attachment, should spread the wings of renunciation and fly to safety.

**5** The wise monk should not desire praise, nor should he fear blame. He should bear both with equanimity, as the earth bears both the pure and the impure.

**6** Hard is the life of a monk. It is a life of discipline, of self-denial, of constant watchfulness. But the fruits of this life are beyond all measure.

**7** He who lives the monk's life with sincerity and devotion, who follows the teaching of the Jinas without compromise — he reaches the shore of liberation.

**8** Therefore, O monks, be earnest in your practice. Be diligent in your vows. Be faithful to the teaching. The opportunity of a human birth is rare; do not waste it.

**9** This human life is like a dew-drop on the tip of a blade of grass. It lasts but a moment and then is gone. Use it wisely.

**10** He who uses this life for the purification of his soul, who practises non-violence and truthfulness, who conquers his passions — he achieves the purpose of human birth.
