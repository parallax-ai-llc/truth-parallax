---
title: "Uttaradhyayana - Chapter 33"
scripture: "uttaradhyayana"
chapter: 33
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of death and prepare for it through the practice of Santhara — the holy death.

**2** Death comes to all beings. It is certain and unavoidable. The wise man does not fear death but prepares for it through lifelong spiritual practice.

**3** When a monk, due to old age, terminal illness, or famine, can no longer maintain his vows and his spiritual practice, he may undertake the fast unto death — Santhara or Sallekhana.

**4** This is not suicide, for suicide is motivated by passion — despair, anger, or attachment. Santhara is motivated by detachment — the serene acceptance of death and the desire to meet it in a state of spiritual purity.

**5** The monk who undertakes Santhara gradually reduces his food and water intake while maintaining his meditation, his vows, and his equanimity.

**6** He asks forgiveness of all beings for any harm he may have caused them, and he forgives all beings for any harm they may have caused him.

**7** He contemplates the nature of the soul, the impermanence of the body, and the bliss of liberation. His mind is fixed upon the eternal, not upon the transient.

**8** He dies in a state of perfect peace, free from anger, free from attachment, free from fear. His death is the culmination of his spiritual life.

**9** The death of such a monk is not a cause for mourning but for celebration. He has achieved the highest goal of human life — a death in full consciousness, without passion, without attachment.

**10** This is the ideal of the Jain tradition: to live well, to die well, and to attain liberation through the practice of non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation, even unto the moment of death.
