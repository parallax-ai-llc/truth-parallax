The Orthodox tradition, following Basil the Great's Hexaemeron and John of Damascus's Exposition of the Orthodox Faith, teaches that the material world is the good creation of the one true God, brought into being through the divine Logos. The Apocryphon's portrayal of Yaldabaoth as a monstrous, ignorant creator who fashions the cosmos in arrogance stands in absolute contradiction to this teaching. The Orthodox liturgy repeatedly celebrates creation as a gift of God's goodness: "Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights."

Sophia's fall — the event that generates Yaldabaoth — also has no analogue in Orthodox theology. While Orthodoxy acknowledges the reality of the fall (the rebellion of angelic and human beings), it does not locate the origin of evil in a mistake within the divine realm itself. The Apocryphon's suggestion that a divine being could err and that this error could produce the entire material world implies a deficiency within the divine Pleroma that Orthodox theology cannot accept. God's creation is the free, deliberate act of the Trinity, not the unintended consequence of a cosmic accident.
