## Overview

The Catholic Church categorically rejects the Apocryphon of John as a heretical text. Irenaeus of Lyon, writing around 180 CE in *Adversus Haereses*, provided a detailed refutation of the very cosmological system this text presents. The Catholic tradition holds that the God who created the material world is the same God who redeems it — a position directly contradicted by the Apocryphon's portrayal of the creator as the ignorant and malevolent Yaldabaoth.

## Patristic Response

The Apocryphon of John is significant in Catholic history precisely because the early church defined its orthodoxy partly in opposition to the theology this text represents. Irenaeus's summary of the "Barbeloite" system closely matches the content of the Apocryphon, making it one of the few Gnostic texts for which we possess both the original and a patristic refutation. The Catholic Church's insistence on creation ex nihilo by a good God, the goodness of matter, and the real incarnation and suffering of Christ were all articulated in direct response to Gnostic teachings like those found here.

## Contemporary Catholic Scholarship

Modern Catholic scholars study the Apocryphon of John as an important historical document that illuminates the theological challenges of the second and third centuries. While firmly rejecting its theology, Catholic academics recognize its value for understanding the intellectual world of early Christianity and the process by which the Church clarified its doctrinal boundaries through conciliar and patristic teaching.
