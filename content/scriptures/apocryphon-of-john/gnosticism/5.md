The final chapter of the Apocryphon of John recounts the repeated interventions of the divine realm in human history to awaken the sleeping divine sparks. The Savior descends into the material world not to suffer and die but to bring gnosis — the liberating knowledge of humanity's true divine origin. This descent is described as an act of compassion by the Pleroma, responding to Sophia's repentance and the groaning of the trapped light.

For the Gnostic reader, chapter 5 provides the ultimate assurance of salvation. The Savior's descent demonstrates that the Pleroma has not abandoned the divine sparks imprisoned in matter. The text describes different categories of souls and their fates: those who receive gnosis and are immediately liberated, those who receive it after a period of ignorance, and those who reject it entirely. This soteriological framework reinforces the urgency of the Gnostic message — gnosis is available, but it must be received and acted upon.

The chapter concludes with the Savior's commission to John to transmit this knowledge to his fellow spirits. The Apocryphon itself becomes part of the salvific process — a written vehicle of the gnosis that liberates. The Gnostic reader who receives and understands this text participates in the same chain of revelation that began with the Monad's self-contemplation and will end with the restoration of all light to the Pleroma.
