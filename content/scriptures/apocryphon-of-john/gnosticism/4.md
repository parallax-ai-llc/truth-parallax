Chapter 4 describes how the archons create Adam's body according to the image of the divine Anthropos reflected on the waters of chaos. The Gnostic reader understands this as a pivotal moment: the archons attempt to capture and contain the divine light by imprisoning it within a material form. Each archon contributes a part of the psychic body, binding the divine spark in layers of material and psychic substance.

The breathing of life into Adam — an event the archons did not intend to produce spiritual awakening — results from Sophia's hidden influence. Her light, trapped within the archontic creation, animates Adam with a power that exceeds what the archons had planned. When the archons recognize that Adam possesses intelligence and luminosity beyond their own, they cast him into the lowest region of matter, seeking to further obscure the divine spark within him.

For the Gnostic, this chapter explains the paradox of the human condition: human beings carry within them a divine spark of extraordinary power, yet this spark is buried beneath layers of material and psychic substance designed to keep it unconscious. The counterfeit spirit — the archontic imitation of true spiritual life — further deceives humanity, making them forget their luminous origin. Only gnosis can penetrate these layers and awaken the sleeper to their true identity.
