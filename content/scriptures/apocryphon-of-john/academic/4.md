The anthropogony of chapter 4 — the detailed account of how the archons construct Adam's body — has attracted considerable academic attention. The longer recension contains an extensive list of archons responsible for individual body parts, a catalog that may draw on Greco-Egyptian anatomical-astrological traditions in which body parts are assigned to planetary rulers. Jorunn Buckley and others have analyzed these lists for their relationship to ancient medical and astrological texts.

The concept of the "counterfeit spirit" (antimimon pneuma) introduced in chapter 4 is unique to the Apocryphon and has been the subject of significant scholarly analysis. Michael Williams has argued that this concept represents the Sethian answer to the problem of why not all humans recognize their divine origin — the counterfeit spirit functions as a mechanism of ongoing deception that keeps the divine sparks unconscious. The concept also provides the Apocryphon's explanation for the persistence of evil and ignorance in a world that contains divine light.
