Academic analysis of chapter 2's emanation scheme has focused on its structural relationship to Platonic metaphysics. John Turner has argued that the triad of Father (Monad), Mother (Barbelo), and Son (Autogenes) maps onto the Middle Platonic triad of the One, Mind (Nous), and Soul (Psyche). This mapping suggests that the Sethian system was not merely a mythological fantasy but a serious engagement with the dominant philosophical framework of its era, expressed in mythological rather than discursive terms.

The Four Luminaries — Harmozel, Oroiael, Daveithai, and Eleleth — have been the subject of detailed comparative study. Their names appear across multiple Sethian texts, suggesting a stable mythological tradition. The aeons associated with each Luminary vary between texts and between recensions of the Apocryphon, providing evidence for the evolution of the Sethian system. Birger Pearson and others have traced the Jewish angelological roots of these figures, connecting them to traditions about the four archangels in Second Temple Judaism.
