## Overview

The Apocryphon of John (Secret Book of John) is one of the most important texts in the Nag Hammadi library, preserved in four Coptic manuscripts — two longer and two shorter recensions. Framed as a post-resurrection dialogue between the risen Christ and the apostle John, the text presents an elaborate Sethian Gnostic cosmogony that reinterprets the opening chapters of Genesis. It describes the nature of the supreme, unknowable Monad, the emanation of the divine Pleroma through Barbelo, and the catastrophic fall of Sophia that results in the birth of the ignorant Demiurge, Yaldabaoth.

## Structure

The text unfolds as a revelatory dialogue covering the full scope of Gnostic cosmology and soteriology:

1. **The Monad and the Invisible Spirit** (Chapter 1): The transcendent, unknowable God beyond all categories
2. **Barbelo and the Divine Emanations** (Chapter 2): The first emanation and the unfolding of the Pleroma
3. **Yaldabaoth and the Creation of the Material World** (Chapter 3): Sophia's error and the Demiurge's false creation
4. **The Creation of Adam and the Imprisonment of Sophia's Light** (Chapter 4): The entrapment of divine sparks in human bodies
5. **Salvation History and the Descent of the Savior** (Chapter 5): The repeated interventions of the divine realm to rescue humanity

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Apocryphon of John holds vastly different status across traditions:

- **Gnosticism**: A foundational revelatory text, arguably the most systematic Sethian cosmological treatise
- **Catholicism / Eastern Orthodoxy**: Rejected as heretical; the theology it promotes was combated by Irenaeus in *Against Heresies*
- **Protestantism**: Not canonical; studied for its historical witness to early Christian diversity
- **Judaism**: Not part of Jewish tradition, though it extensively reinterprets Genesis and Jewish angelology
- **Academic**: A primary source for reconstructing Sethian Gnostic thought and its relationship to Platonism

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| The Monad | The utterly transcendent, unknowable divine source beyond all predication |
| Barbelo | The first emanation, the divine Mother-Father and beginning of the Pleroma |
| Sophia's Fall | The origin of evil through Sophia's unauthorized creative act |
| Yaldabaoth | The ignorant Demiurge who claims to be the only God |
| Divine Sparks | Fragments of Sophia's light trapped in human bodies |
| Salvation through Gnosis | Liberation comes through knowledge of one's true divine origin |
