Chapter 3 represents the most directly offensive content for Jewish readers. The text takes YHWH — the sacred, unutterable Name of the God of Israel — and assigns it to Yaldabaoth, a monstrous, ignorant being. Yaldabaoth's declaration "I am God; there is none besides me" deliberately echoes Deutero-Isaiah's monotheistic affirmations (Isaiah 45:5, 46:9), transforming Judaism's most exalted theological claims into evidence of demiurgic arrogance and blindness.

From a Jewish perspective, this chapter constitutes a wholesale assault on the theological foundations of Judaism. The God who liberated Israel from Egypt, gave the Torah at Sinai, and established an eternal covenant with his people is recast as a cosmic villain. Jewish scholars studying the Apocryphon see in this chapter evidence of the deep anti-Jewish currents within certain Gnostic movements — traditions that appropriated Jewish scripture and concepts while systematically demonizing the God at their center. The text illustrates the extremes to which anti-cosmic theology could go in its polemic against the creator.
