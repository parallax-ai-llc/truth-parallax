---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 6"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 6
---

**1** He bears the Self in two ways, as he who is Prana the breath, and as he who is the Aditya the sun. And there are two paths for him, one within, one without, and both return in a day and a night. The Sun is the outer Self, the inner Self is Breath.

**2** Hence the motion of the inner Self is inferred from the motion of the outer Self. For thus it is said: "He who knows, who has thrown off all evil, the overseer of the senses, the pure-minded, firmly grounded, looking away from worldly things, he is indeed the Self." And likewise it is said: "He who, having thrown off all evil, rests in the most excellent, undecaying, unfailing, immortal light, he is the Self."

**3** Now it has been said elsewhere: This body is produced by copulation, and grown in darkness; it is then pushed out through the urinary opening, and grown up through bones, flesh, sinew, marrow, blood, membrane, skin, hide and cuticle. The whole body is overlaid with impurities. As a sword is sheathed in its case, so is he.

**4** And it has been said elsewhere: The darkness, which some call Tamas, is truly the nature of this Self. What was called Tamas is indeed Rajas. And Rajas is truly Sattva. For the Self pervades all this; therefore it is said: This trinity of qualities enters the one Self.

**5** And it has been said: The Self is overcome by the qualities of Nature, and because he is overcome he falls into bewilderment; and because he is bewildered he sees not the Creator, the holy Lord, who dwells within himself. Carried along and defiled by the stream of the qualities, unsteady, wavering, bewildered, full of desire, distracted, he enters into the state of self-consciousness, thinking "This is I" and "That is mine." He binds himself by himself, as a bird is caught in a net.

**6** He is indeed overcome by the fruits of his actions. Being overcome he enters a good or bad womb, so that his course is downward or upward, and he wanders about, overcome by the pairs of opposites.

**7** And it has been said: Now then, that which is the subtile essence, in it all that exists has its self. It is the True. It is the Self. And thou, O Svetaketu, art it.

**8** Now it has been asked: When he who knows has withdrawn from this body, and is freed, what belongs to him then?

**9** Sakayanya replied: Let a man, after he has gained knowledge, strive after fulness of the Self. Let him not seek after a host of words, for that is mere weariness of the tongue.

**10** Now there are indeed three worlds, the world of men, the world of the Fathers, the world of the Devas. The world of men can only be gained through a son, not by any other work. By sacrifice the world of the Fathers is gained. By knowledge the world of the Devas is won. The world of the Devas is the best of worlds. Therefore they praise knowledge.

**11** Now next the initiation. He should meditate on the syllable Om, the Udgitha. For the Udgitha is sung beginning with Om. The essence of all things is earth; the essence of earth is water; the essence of water are plants; the essence of plants is man; the essence of man is speech; the essence of speech is the Rik; the essence of the Rik is the Saman; and the essence of the Saman is the Udgitha.

**12** That Udgitha is the best of all essences, the supreme, deserving the highest place, the eighth. By meditating on the syllable Om, one is released. One rises upwards into the sun. This is the door of the world, an entrance for the knowing, a bar for the unknowing.

**13** There is this verse: Om is the bow, the Self is the arrow, Brahman is called its aim. It is to be hit by a man who is not thoughtless; and then, as the arrow becomes one with the target, he will become one with Brahman.

**14** He in whom the sky, the earth, and the interspace are woven, the mind also, with all the senses, know him alone as the one Self, and leave off other words. He is the bridge of the Immortal.

**15** Where the arteries of the body are brought together, like the spokes in the nave of a wheel, there he moves about, becoming manifold. Meditate on the Self as Om. Hail to you, that you may cross beyond the sea of darkness!

**16** He who understands all and who knows all, he to whom all this glory in the world belongs, the Self, is placed in the ether, in the heavenly city of Brahman. He assumes the nature of mind, and becomes the guide of the body of the senses. He subsists in food, in close proximity to the heart. By the knowledge of that which gleams, the wise perceive the blissful Immortal, which shines forth.

**17** The fetter of the heart is broken, all doubts are solved, all his works perish when He has been beheld who is the higher and the lower.
