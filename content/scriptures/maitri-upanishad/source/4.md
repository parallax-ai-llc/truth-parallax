---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Now it has been said elsewhere: The body in which the Self dwells is like a cart without intelligence. To such a being of great power has this body been made subservient, and therefore it is said:

**2** Savitri verily is Prana, for from the Sun all this is born; the Sun is yoked by Time, and the time-measure is the month.

**3** There are indeed two forms of Brahman, time and non-time. That which was before the existence of the sun is non-time and has no parts. That which had its beginning from the sun is time and has parts. Of that which has parts, the year is the form, and from the year these creatures are produced; through the year, after having been produced, they grow; in the year they disappear.

**4** Therefore the year is Prajapati, is time, is food, is the nest of Brahman, is Self. Thus it is said: "Time ripens and dissolves all beings in the great Self, but he who knows into what time itself is dissolved, he is the knower of the Veda."

**5** This manifested time is the great ocean of creatures. He who is called Savitri dwells in it, from whence the moon, stars, planets, the year, and the rest are produced. From them again comes all this, and whatever of good or evil is seen in this world. Therefore Brahman is the Self of the sun, and a man should worship the sun under the name of time. Some say the sun is Brahman.

**6** Thus it has been said: "The sacrificer, the deity that enjoys the sacrifice, the oblation, the hymn, the sacrifice, Vishnu, Prajapati, all this is the Lord, the witness, that shines in yonder orb."
