---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The laying of the fires is indeed the sacrifice of Brahman. Therefore let the sacrificer meditate on the Self as the fire. The fire is perfect and complete by meditation. Who is it then that is to be meditated on? He who is called Prana, the breath of life.

**2** A story is told of King Brihadratha, who, after having established his son in the sovereignty, went into the forest, because he considered that the body is not eternal, and there he reached the highest knowledge of the Self. Having performed penance, he stood with his arms lifted up, looking at the sun.

**3** At the end of a thousand days, the Lord Sakayanya, who knew the Self, came near, burning with splendour, like a fire without smoke. He said to the king: "Rise, rise! Choose a boon!"

**4** The king, bowing before him, said: "O Lord, I know not the Self. We have heard that thou knowest the nature thereof. Please teach it to us." Sakayanya replied: "This was achieved in former times; but this question is indeed difficult. O son of the race of Ikshvaku, choose other pleasures."

**5** The king, touching the sage's feet with his head, recited this lament: "O Lord, in this foul-smelling, unsubstantial body, which is a conglomerate of bone, skin, sinew, muscle, marrow, flesh, semen, blood, mucus, tears, rheum, ordure, urine, wind, bile, and phlegm, what is the good of enjoyment of desires?"

**6** "In this body, which is afflicted with desire, anger, covetousness, delusion, fear, despondency, envy, separation from the desirable, union with the undesirable, hunger, thirst, senility, death, disease, sorrow, and the like, what is the good of enjoyment of desires?"

**7** "And we see that all this is perishable, as these gnats, mosquitoes, and the like, the grass, and the trees that grow and decay. But what of these? There are others superior, great warriors, some kings, such as Sudyumna, Bhuri-dyumna, Indradyumna, Kuvalayasva, Yauvanasva, Vadhryasva, Asvapati, Sasabindu, Harischandra, Ambarisha, Nahusha, Ananata, Saryati, Yayati, Anaranya, Ukshasena, and the rest; and kings such as Marutta, Bharata, and others, who before the eyes of their whole family surrendered the greatest happiness, and passed on from this world to that."

**8** "But what of these? There are others superior. We see the destruction of the Gandharvas, Asuras, Yakshas, Rakshasas, Bhutas, Ganas, Pisachas, snakes, and vampires. But what of these? Among other things, there is the drying up of great oceans, the falling of mountains, the moving of the pole-star, the cutting of the wind-ropes, the submergence of the earth, the departure of the gods from their station. In such a world as this, what is the use of enjoyment of desires? He who has fed on them is seen again and again to return to this world. Deign therefore to deliver me. In this world I am like a frog in a waterless well. O Lord, thou art our way, thou art our way."
