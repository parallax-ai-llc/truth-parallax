---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Then the Lord Sakayanya, well pleased, said to the king: "Great king Brihadratha, thou banner of the race of Ikshvaku, quickly obtaining a knowledge of Self, thou art happy, and art renowned by the name of Marut, the wind. This is indeed thy Self."

**2** "Which one?" asked the king. Then Sakayanya said to him:

**3** "He who, without stopping the out-breathing, proceeds upwards, and who, modified, moves about in this world; this is thy Self, the Inner Self, the ancient, who never dies, who is free from hunger, who is free from thirst, who is free from sorrow, free from old age, free from death."

**4** "By the serenity of thought deeds, good and evil, cease! With Self serene, staying in the Self, he obtains imperishable happiness."

**5** "The self which is the knower, the inner man, the lord, the enjoyer, the ruler, who rests in the supreme Self, which is a place of shelter, is like a mass of consciousness. He, the Atman, is to be known."

**6** "He who, having been afflicted by the things of the body, goes forth from the body, attains the highest light, and appears in his own form. He is the highest Self. He is the man who moves about, laughing, eating, and rejoicing in his mind, though not attached to these activities. And as the wind-driven smoke of a fire, spread over the sky, cannot be found again, so the one who has realized the Self and who has cast off the body, cannot be pointed out."

**7** "This Self, indeed, is the Lord of all beings, the king of all beings. And as all the spokes are contained in the axle and the felly of a wheel, so all beings, all gods, all worlds, all breathing things, and all these selves are contained in that Self."
