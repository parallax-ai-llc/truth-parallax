---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Now it has been said elsewhere: "The Self, which is free from sin, free from old age, from death, from grief, from hunger, from thirst, which desires nothing but what it ought to desire, and imagines nothing but what it ought to imagine, that it is which we must search out, that it is which we must try to understand. He who has searched out that Self and understands it, obtains all worlds and all desires."

**2** Thus spoke Prajapati. The Devas and the Asuras both went forth to study. Having studied, they returned. Virochana, satisfied in his heart, went to the Asuras and preached to them the doctrine: The Self alone is to be worshipped here, the Self alone is to be served. He who worships the self alone as his treasure, obtains all worlds.

**3** And therefore even now on earth they say of a man who does not give alms here, who has no faith, and offers no sacrifices, he is an Asura; for this is the doctrine of the Asuras.

**4** But Indra, even before he had returned to the Devas, saw this difficulty: "As this self is well adorned when the body is well adorned, well dressed when the body is well dressed, well cleaned when the body is well cleaned, that Self will also be blind if the body is blind, lame if the body is lame, crippled if the body is crippled, and will perish in fact as soon as the body perishes. Therefore I see no good in this."

**5** Having come back, Prajapati taught him further: "He who moves about happy in dreams, he is the Self, this is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman." Then Indra went away satisfied in his heart. But even before he had returned to the Devas, he saw this difficulty: "True it is that this self is not blind even if the body is blind, nor lame if the body is lame; but in dreams it does suffer as if it were struck, as if it were chased. It becomes conscious of pain, and sheds tears. Therefore I see no good in this."

**6** He went back again. Prajapati said to him: "When a man being asleep, reposing, and at perfect rest, sees no dreams, that is the Self, this is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman." Then Indra went away satisfied in his heart. But even before he had returned to the Devas, he saw this difficulty: "In truth he does not know himself that he is, nor does he know anything that exists. He is gone to utter annihilation. I see no good in this."

**7** He came back once more. Prajapati said: "O Maghavan, this body is mortal and always held by death. It is the abode of that Self which is immortal and without body. When in the body, the Self is held by pleasure and pain. So long as he is in the body, he cannot get free from pleasure and pain. But when he is free of the body, then neither pleasure nor pain touches him."

**8** "The wind is without body, the cloud, lightning, and thunder are without body. Now as these, arising from that heavenly space, reach the highest light and appear each in its own form, thus does that serene being, rising from this body, reach the highest light and appear in its own form. He is the highest Person."

**9** He there moves about, eating, playing, and rejoicing, be it with women, carriages, or relatives, never minding that body into which he was born. As an animal is attached to a cart, so is the spirit attached to this body.

**10** Now where the sight has entered into the void, there is the person of the eye; the eye itself is the instrument of seeing. He who knows: Let me smell this, he is the Self; the nose is the instrument of smelling. He who knows: Let me say this, he is the Self; the tongue is the instrument of saying. He who knows: Let me hear this, he is the Self; the ear is the instrument of hearing.

**11** He who knows: Let me think this, he is the Self; the mind is his divine eye. He, the Self, seeing these pleasures by his divine eye, the mind, rejoices.

**12** The Devas who are in the world of Brahman meditate on that Self. Therefore all worlds belong to them, and all desires. He who knows that Self and who understands it, obtains all worlds and all desires. Thus said Prajapati, yea, thus said Prajapati.
