---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 7"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 7
---

**1** In the beginning this was non-existent. It became existent, it grew. It turned into an egg. The egg lay for the time of a year. The egg broke open. The two halves were one of silver, the other of gold.

**2** The silver one became this earth, the golden one the sky, the thick membrane the mountains, the thin membrane the mist with the clouds, the small veins the rivers, the fluid the sea.

**3** And what was born from it, that was Aditya, the sun. When he was born, shouts of hurrah arose, and all beings arose, and all things which they desired. Therefore whenever the sun rises and whenever it returns, shouts of hurrah arise, and all beings and all desires arise.

**4** He who, knowing this, meditates on the sun as Brahman, pleasant shouts will approach him and will continue, yea, will continue.

**5** Now what is the true nature of that which was there before the creation? It was neither what is called existent nor what is called non-existent. Prana issued from it. That Prana united with that other, the Atman.

**6** The form of that being is the golden person who is in the sun, who looks down upon the earth from his golden place. He it is who, dwelling within the lotus of the heart, eats food. He who dwells within the lotus of the heart eats food.

**7** And he who, knowing this, eats food, turns it into prana and apana, clothes himself therewith, covers himself therewith, nourishes himself therewith.

**8** Now therefore, that fire, which is the foundation of the year, is the Self of all existing things. This is the Self. This is Brahman. And this Brahman has four feet. His feet are the four quarters.

**9** And therefore it has been said: The sun is Brahman, this is the teaching. Further explanation: The thinking Self, after having been dwelling in the body of wakefulness and having enjoyed the gross, then going forth, having enjoyed the subtle, having reached the state of deep sleep, and having found rest, the Self returns again.

**10** Thus there are two states for that person, the state in this world and the state in the other world; and there is a third, the twilight state. While in the twilight state, he sees both states together, this world and the other world.

**11** Now whatever approach he makes to the other world, that approach he gains, and sees before him both evil and blessed results. And when he falls asleep, then having taken from this all-containing world the timber of good and evil, he himself builds up a new body by his brilliant light.

**12** In that state he is self-illuminated; there are no chariots, no spans, no roads, but he himself sends forth chariots, spans, roads. There are no blessings, no happiness, no joys, but he himself sends forth blessings, happiness, and joys. There are no tanks, no lakes, no rivers, but he himself sends forth tanks, lakes, and rivers. He is indeed the maker.

**13** Concerning this there are the following verses: "Having by sleep overcome all that belongs to the body, he, not asleep himself, looks down upon the sleeping senses. Having taken to himself light, he goes again to his place, the golden person, the lonely bird."

**14** "Guarding with the breath his low nest, the immortal moves away from the nest; that immortal one goes wherever he likes, the golden person, the lonely bird."

**15** "Going up and down in his dream, the god makes manifold shapes for himself, either rejoicing together with women, or laughing, or seeing terrible sights."

**16** "People see his playground but himself no one ever sees." Therefore they say: "Let no one wake a man suddenly, for it is not easy to cure him who does not return rightly to his body."

**17** Now this same is also the Bhautika-Agnihotra. The first offering is called exhalation, the second inhalation, the third diffusion. Therefore the wise man should be cautious, serene, knowing, free, and dwelling in what is the true Self. He who seeks the Self and obtains it, for him there is freedom in all worlds. Yea, for him there is freedom in all worlds.
