---
title: "Maitri Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "maitri-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The sages say: "What is the shadow of that of which this is the body?" When this body, being without intelligence, was like a cart, by what super-sensuous power was such an intelligent body produced?

**2** And what is it that having made that wise becomes the driver? Whatever that is, know it as the Self. As it is said: "He who rests in the highest self, in the midst of the body, sleepless, who is a ruler, a mover, who is diffused everywhere, he indeed is the Self."

**3** Now it has been said elsewhere: "He who knows the Self and has become a thinker, being the lord of the past and the future, does not try to hide himself from anything."

**4** Of this, this is the proof: "That Self which was hidden in the Upanishads, the old secret; through Yoga it is now understood. For this body arose through Yoga, and Yoga leads to its dissolution."

**5** He whose being is mind, whose body is Prana, whose form is light, whose thought is truth, whose nature is like the ether, from whom all works proceed, who contains all good desires, who comprehends this whole world, the speechless, the careless; he is my Self within the heart, smaller than a grain of rice, smaller than a grain of barley, smaller than a mustard seed, smaller than a grain of millet, smaller than the kernel of a grain of millet; he also is my Self within the heart, greater than the earth, greater than the sky, greater than heaven, greater than all these worlds.

**6** He from whom all works, all desires, all scents, all tastes proceed, who embraces all this, who never speaks and who is never surprised; he is my Self within the heart; that is Brahman. He who departs from this life will, on departing, reach that Self. He who believes this will have no doubt. Thus spoke Sandilya, thus spoke Sandilya.
