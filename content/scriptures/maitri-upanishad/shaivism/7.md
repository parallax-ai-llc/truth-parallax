Chapter 7 describes the Supreme Brahman in terms that resonate powerfully with Shaiva theology: beyond all duality, self-luminous, the source and dissolution of all that exists. Shaiva interpreters identify this Brahman with Paramashiva — the absolute consciousness that transcends even the distinction between Shiva and Shakti, resting in its own nature (*svasvarupa*).

The Maitri's final teaching that Brahman is both the knower and the known, the seer and the seen, points to the Shaiva principle of self-recognition (*pratyabhijna*): ultimate reality is consciousness knowing itself. There is nothing outside consciousness that could limit or define it. This is the Shaiva *anuttara* — the unsurpassable.
