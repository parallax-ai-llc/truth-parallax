## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Maitri Upanishad a comprehensive teaching on consciousness and its liberation from the binding qualities (*gunas*) of nature. The Upanishad's extensive yoga and meditation instructions anticipate the Shaiva practices of *dhyana* (meditation) and *dharana* (concentration) that lead to recognition of one's true nature as Shiva.

## Theological Emphases

The two forms of Brahman — manifest and unmanifest — correspond to the Shaiva understanding of Shiva as both the dynamic creator (*Sadashiva*) and the transcendent absolute (*Paramashiva*). The three gunas that bind consciousness are overcome through yoga, which in Shaiva tradition means the direct recognition of awareness as unlimited and free. The Maitri's detailed meditation techniques — concentration on the heart, meditation on Om, control of breath — form the practical foundation upon which later Shaiva meditation systems were built.
