The two forms of Brahman in Chapter 3 — temporal and eternal — correspond to the Shaiva understanding of Shiva as both the dynamic creator within time (*Sadashiva*) and the timeless absolute beyond all manifestation (*Paramashiva*). The Upanishad's teaching that time itself is Brahman resonates with the Shaiva concept of *kala* as one of the five coverings (*kancukas*) that limit infinite consciousness.

The identification of the sun with the manifest Brahman connects to the Shaiva understanding of light as the primary metaphor for consciousness. Just as the sun illuminates the external world, consciousness illuminates all experience. The unmanifest Brahman beyond time is the Shaiva absolute — pure awareness prior to all temporal expression.
