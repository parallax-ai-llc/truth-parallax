The inner self in the heart described in Chapter 4 is read by Shaiva interpreters as the contracted form of Shiva's infinite consciousness dwelling at the center of each being. The five pranas through which this inner self operates are expressions of Shiva's five cosmic functions (*panchakritya*): creation, sustenance, dissolution, concealment, and grace.

The heart as the seat of the self resonates with the Shaiva emphasis on the *hridaya* (heart) as the center of consciousness — not the physical organ but the core of awareness from which all experience radiates. Meditation on the inner self in the heart is the foundation of Shaiva contemplative practice.
