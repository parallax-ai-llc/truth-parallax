The Maitri's teaching on the self in Chapter 2 resonates with the Shaiva understanding of consciousness as inherently free yet apparently bound. The self described here — eternal, conscious, yet entangled in the body and senses — is what Kashmir Shaivism calls the *anu* (contracted self), Shiva appearing to be limited by His own creative power (*maya-shakti*).

The metaphor of the self as untouched by bodily conditions mirrors the Shaiva teaching that consciousness is never truly contaminated by its objects. Bondage is not a real change in the nature of awareness but a contraction (*sankoca*) that can be reversed through recognition. The self's entanglement in the gunas is Shiva's own play (*lila*), and its liberation is Shiva recognizing Himself.
