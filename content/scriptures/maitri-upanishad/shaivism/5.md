The three gunas described in Chapter 5 are understood in Shaiva traditions as aspects of Shiva's creative power (*maya-shakti*) that produce the apparent limitations of consciousness. The gunas do not truly bind the self — they create the appearance of bondage through the contraction of unlimited awareness into limited cognitive and emotional patterns.

Kashmir Shaivism reads the three gunas as three modes of Shiva's self-obscuration: tamas obscures consciousness completely, rajas agitates it into constant activity, and sattva allows its partial reflection. Full liberation transcends all three gunas through the direct recognition that consciousness was never truly bound — the gunas were always Shiva's own play.
