Chapter 6 presents meditation techniques that Shaiva interpreters recognize as early forms of the yogic practices elaborated in later Shaiva texts. The meditation on the inner light, the use of Om, and the breath control techniques anticipate the Shaiva practices of *dharana* (concentration), *dhyana* (meditation), and *pranayama* (breath regulation) described in texts like the Vijnanabhairava Tantra.

The Maitri's instruction to concentrate on the inner sun — the light of consciousness within the heart — is central to Shaiva meditation. This inner light is not a physical phenomenon but consciousness itself, luminous and self-aware. The chapter's progressive meditation technique — from external sound to internal silence, from gross object to subtle awareness — mirrors the Shaiva journey from contraction to expansion.
