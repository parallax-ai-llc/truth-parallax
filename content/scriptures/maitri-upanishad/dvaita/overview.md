## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Maitri Upanishad's two forms of Brahman as describing the Supreme Lord's accessible and transcendent aspects — not as two levels of reality (as Advaita holds) but as two genuine dimensions of God's being. The elaborate discussion of bondage through the *gunas* confirms the Dvaita teaching that the soul's entanglement in material nature is real and requires God's real grace for liberation.

## Theological Emphases

The Maitri's meditation instructions are read as devotional exercises aimed at developing the concentration and purity needed to receive Vishnu's grace. The three *gunas* are real qualities of *prakriti* (material nature) that genuinely bind the soul — not mere illusions to be dispelled by philosophical analysis. Liberation comes when Vishnu, pleased by the soul's devoted meditation, frees it from the gunas and grants it eternal bliss in His presence.
