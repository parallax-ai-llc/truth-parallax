Madhva reads Chapter 2 as confirming the real distinction between the self, matter, and God. The individual soul is genuinely bound by the three gunas of material nature — not illusorily or apparently but actually and experientially. The self's bondage is real, its suffering is real, and therefore its need for God's grace for liberation is genuine.

The Dvaita reading insists that the self described here is the individual soul (*jiva*), eternally distinct from both the material world and the Supreme Lord. The self's eternal nature does not mean it is identical with Brahman but that it is an enduring entity whose ultimate destiny depends on its relationship with God.
