Madhva reads the two forms of Brahman not as two levels of reality but as two real dimensions of God's being. Vishnu is genuinely both temporal (as the cosmic sustainer operating within time) and eternal (as the transcendent Lord beyond time). Neither form is more real than the other — both are authentic aspects of the Supreme Lord.

The identification of time with Brahman demonstrates God's sovereign power over the temporal order. Time is not an independent force but an instrument of divine will. The manifest Brahman (the sun, the cosmic order) is God's self-expression in creation, while the unmanifest Brahman is His transcendent nature that exceeds all created forms.
