Madhva's Dvaita school reads Brihadratha's despair as the genuine suffering of a soul separated from God. The king's disillusionment with worldly power and pleasure is not the recognition of an illusion but the real experience of a finite being who cannot find fulfillment apart from the infinite Lord. His approach to the sage represents the soul's turn toward God through the guidance of a *guru*.

The chapter establishes that spiritual seeking arises from real suffering in a real world — not from the recognition that the world is unreal. For Dvaita, the solution to Brihadratha's despair is not the negation of the world but the discovery of God's sustaining presence within it.
