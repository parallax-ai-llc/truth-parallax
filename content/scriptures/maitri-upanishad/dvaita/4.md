Madhva reads the inner self in the heart as the Supreme Lord dwelling within each soul as the inner controller (*antaryamin*), distinct from and superior to the individual soul. The five pranas are the Lord's instruments for governing the body — further evidence of His intimate yet sovereign presence. The soul and the Lord coexist in the heart, but they are never identical.

This chapter provides key scriptural support for the Dvaita doctrine of *bimba-pratibimba* — the Lord as the original and the soul as the reflection. The inner self that illumines the heart is God Himself, and the individual soul's experience of consciousness is a reflection of His light.
