Madhva reads Chapter 7 as the culmination of the Maitri's teaching on God's infinite nature. The Supreme Brahman described here is Vishnu in His most exalted aspect — infinitely perfect, infinitely powerful, infinitely loving. The negation of all finite attributes is not the negation of personality but the affirmation of God's transcendence of all human categories.

For Dvaita, the statement that Brahman is both material and efficient cause means that God creates the world from the material He controls (*prakriti*) and by His own will. The world is real because its cause is real, and the distinction between the creator and the creation remains even at the highest level of understanding.
