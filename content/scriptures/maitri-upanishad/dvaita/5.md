Madhva reads the three gunas as real qualities of material nature that genuinely bind the individual soul. Unlike Advaita, which treats bondage as merely apparent, Dvaita insists that the soul's entanglement in the gunas is a real condition requiring real divine intervention. The gunas are aspects of *prakriti* controlled by Vishnu, and only His grace can free the soul from their grip.

The chapter's classification of consciousness according to the gunas — clear in sattva, agitated in rajas, dull in tamas — reflects the Dvaita understanding of the soul's varying spiritual states. Progress toward liberation involves the real cultivation of sattva through devotion, study, and righteous action, culminating in God's gracious intervention.
