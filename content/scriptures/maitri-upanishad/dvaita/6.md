Madhva's tradition reads Chapter 6's meditation instructions as practical disciplines for developing the concentration and devotion needed to receive God's grace. Meditation on Om is meditation on Vishnu, and the inner light upon which the practitioner focuses is the Lord's presence in the heart. These practices are not mere psychological exercises but genuine acts of worship.

The chapter's detailed breath control techniques are understood as physical supports for mental concentration. The Dvaita tradition values these practices not as ends in themselves but as means to cultivate the inner focus required for *bhakti* (devotion). The fruit of meditation is not the dissolution of the self but the clarification of the self's relationship to God.
