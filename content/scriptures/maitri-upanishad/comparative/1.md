Brihadratha's royal despair echoes the motif of the disillusioned king across world literature. The Buddha's departure from his palace upon witnessing old age, sickness, and death; Solomon's declaration in Ecclesiastes that "all is vanity"; and Marcus Aurelius's meditations on impermanence all share this fundamental recognition that worldly power cannot shield one from the realities of transient existence.

The king's turn from power to wisdom parallels Plato's philosopher-king who ascends from the cave of shadows to the light of truth, and the medieval Christian concept of *contemptus mundi* (contempt for the world) that motivated monastic withdrawal. The teacher-student relationship between Shakayanya and Brihadratha mirrors the guru-disciple dynamic found across traditions.
