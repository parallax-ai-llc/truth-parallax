The three gunas as fundamental qualities constituting material nature invite comparison with other elemental or quality-based cosmologies. The four elements of Greek philosophy (earth, water, air, fire), the five phases (*wuxing*) of Chinese thought (wood, fire, earth, metal, water), and the four humors of Galenic medicine (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, black bile) all attempt to reduce material complexity to a small number of fundamental principles.

The psychological application of the gunas — classifying mental states as pure, agitated, or dull — compares with the Buddhist analysis of wholesome, unwholesome, and neutral mental factors, the Christian tradition of the seven capital virtues and vices, and modern psychological theories of temperament.
