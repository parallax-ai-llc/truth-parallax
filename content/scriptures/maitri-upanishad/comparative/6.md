Chapter 6's meditation techniques invite comparison with contemplative practices across traditions. The use of a sacred syllable (Om) as a meditation focus parallels the Christian use of the Jesus Prayer, the Islamic practice of *dhikr* (repetition of divine names), and the Pure Land Buddhist invocation of Amitabha's name (*nembutsu/nianfo*).

The progressive internalization of attention — from external sound to internal silence, from gross to subtle — mirrors the stages of Christian contemplative prayer (from vocal prayer through meditation to contemplation), the Sufi progression from *dhikr* of the tongue to *dhikr* of the heart to *dhikr* of the soul, and the Buddhist *jhana* (absorption) sequence. The visualization of inner light compares with the Quaker concept of the "Inner Light" and the Eastern Orthodox experience of the Taboric Light.
