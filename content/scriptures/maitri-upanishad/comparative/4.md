The inner self dwelling in the heart resonates with heart-centered spirituality across traditions. The Christian mystical tradition of the "prayer of the heart" (Eastern Orthodox hesychasm), the Sufi concept of the *qalb* (spiritual heart) as the seat of divine knowledge, and the Egyptian understanding of the heart (*ib*) as the center of intelligence and morality all place the heart at the center of spiritual experience.

The five vital breaths compare with the Chinese concept of *qi* in its various forms, the Tibetan Buddhist subtle body with its five winds (*lung*), and the Greek pneuma circulating through the body. The mapping of cosmic functions onto bodily processes reflects the universal microcosm-macrocosm principle.
