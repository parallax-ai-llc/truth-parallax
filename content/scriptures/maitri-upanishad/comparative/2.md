The Maitri's vision of the self as pure but temporarily bound by material qualities invites comparison with dualistic anthropologies worldwide. The Platonic distinction between the immortal soul and the mortal body, the Gnostic teaching on the divine spark trapped in matter, and the Manichaean light-particles imprisoned in darkness all share the basic structure of a pure spiritual principle entangled in an alien material environment.

The three gunas as qualities constituting material nature compare with the four humors of Greek medicine, the five phases (*wuxing*) of Chinese philosophy, and the three alchemical principles of Western esotericism. Each system attempts to reduce the complexity of the material world to a small number of fundamental qualities.
