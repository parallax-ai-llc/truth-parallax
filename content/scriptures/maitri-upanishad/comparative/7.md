The Maitri's description of the Supreme Brahman beyond all qualities parallels apophatic (negative) theology across traditions. The Christian via negativa (Pseudo-Dionysius, Meister Eckhart), the Islamic understanding of God's *tanzih* (transcendence beyond all human categories), the Kabbalistic *Ein Sof* (the Infinite beyond all Sefirot), and the Daoist *wu* (the nameless, formless Dao) all approach ultimate reality through the systematic negation of finite attributes.

The teaching that Brahman is both the material and efficient cause of the universe compares with the Neoplatonic concept of the One as the source from which all things emanate, the Stoic *logos* as both the rational principle and the material substance of the cosmos, and the Kabbalistic understanding of creation as God's self-contraction (*tzimtzum*) making room for the world within Himself.
