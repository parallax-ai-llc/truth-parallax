The Maitri's two forms of Brahman — temporal and eternal — parallel dualisms in other traditions. The Kabbalistic distinction between *Ein Sof* (the infinite, unknowable God) and the *Sefirot* (God's manifest attributes) mirrors this structure. So does the Islamic theological distinction between God's essence (*dhat*) and His attributes (*sifat*), and the Neoplatonic contrast between the transcendent One and its temporal emanations.

The identification of time with the divine resonates with the Zoroastrian concept of *Zurvan* (infinite time as a deity), the Greek identification of *Chronos* with cosmic order, and the Buddhist teaching on dependent origination (*pratityasamutpada*) as the temporal structure of reality.
