Chapter 2 presents the sage Shakayanya's initial teaching on the nature of the self. The individual self (*jiva*) is described as distinct from the body and senses, yet bound by its attachment to material qualities (*gunas*). Vaishnava commentators read this as confirming the soul's eternal nature — the self is not the body but a spiritual entity temporarily entangled in material nature through forgetfulness of its relationship with God.

The teaching that the self is "like a drop of water on a lotus leaf" — present in the body yet not touched by it — illustrates the Vaishnava understanding of the soul as inherently pure and divine, only apparently contaminated by contact with material nature. Liberation comes through remembering one's true identity as a servant of Vishnu.
