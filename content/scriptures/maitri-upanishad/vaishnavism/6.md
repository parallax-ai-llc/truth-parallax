Chapter 6 provides the Maitri Upanishad's most detailed practical instructions — meditation on Om, breath control, and concentration on the inner light as means to realize Brahman. Vaishnava commentators read these techniques as devotional practices (*upasana*) directed toward the Supreme Lord. Meditation on Om is understood as meditation on Vishnu, since Om denotes the Lord in His fullest manifestation.

The chapter's instruction to meditate on the self within the heart using Om as the vehicle is read as the foundation of *mantra-yoga* — the devotional repetition of God's name that is central to Vaishnava practice. The goal of meditation is not the dissolution of the self into an impersonal absolute but the direct vision (*darshana*) of the Lord dwelling in the heart.
