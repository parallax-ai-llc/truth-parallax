Chapter 1 opens with King Brihadratha's disillusionment with worldly life. Sitting in meditation, he reflects on the impermanence of great empires, mighty kings, and cosmic ages. His despair drives him to approach the sage Shakayanya for spiritual knowledge. Vaishnava commentators read this as illustrating the *vairagya* (detachment) necessary for spiritual life — the recognition that material existence, however grand, cannot satisfy the soul's longing for the eternal.

Brihadratha's approach to the sage mirrors the devotee's surrender to the spiritual master (*guru*), who represents the Lord's grace. His question — "What is the use of enjoyment in this body, which is afflicted by desires?" — expresses the Vaishnava insight that material pleasure is ultimately unsatisfying because the soul is made for communion with God.
