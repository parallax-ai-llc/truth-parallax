Chapter 4 teaches about the inner self dwelling in the heart — the *antaryamin* (inner controller) who operates through the five vital breaths (*pranas*). Vaishnava commentators identify this inner self with Vishnu as Paramatma (Supersoul), the divine presence within every being who sustains the body's functions through the five pranas.

The five vital breaths — *prana*, *apana*, *vyana*, *samana*, and *udana* — are understood as instruments through which the Lord maintains the bodily vehicle for the soul's embodied experience. The teaching that the inner self is the light within the heart anticipates the Bhagavad Gita's teaching (15.15) that the Lord dwells in the heart of all beings.
