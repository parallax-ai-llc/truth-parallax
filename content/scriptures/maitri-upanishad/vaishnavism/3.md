Chapter 3 introduces the crucial distinction between the two forms of Brahman: the temporal and the eternal, the manifest and the unmanifest. Vaishnava commentators read this as describing Vishnu's dual nature — He is both the immanent sustainer of the time-bound cosmos and the transcendent Lord beyond all temporal limitations. The sun as the manifest form of Brahman is understood as one of Vishnu's expressions.

The teaching that time (*kala*) is a form of Brahman is significant for Vaishnavism: Krishna declares in the Bhagavad Gita (11.32), "I am time, the great destroyer of worlds." The Maitri's identification of time with Brahman confirms that even the most powerful cosmic force — the flow of time that destroys all things — is an expression of the Supreme Lord's nature.
