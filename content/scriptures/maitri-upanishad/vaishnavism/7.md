Chapter 7 culminates the Maitri Upanishad's teaching with the revelation of the Supreme Brahman beyond all distinctions — beyond time, beyond the gunas, beyond even the two forms described earlier. Vaishnava commentators identify this Supreme Brahman with Vishnu-Narayana in His most transcendent aspect — the Lord who is simultaneously the origin of all and beyond all.

The chapter's assertion that Brahman is both the material and efficient cause of the universe is read by Vaishnavas as confirming that Vishnu creates the world from Himself (as material cause) and by His will (as efficient cause). This *abheda-bheda* (identity-in-difference) relationship between God and creation is central to Vaishnava theology.
