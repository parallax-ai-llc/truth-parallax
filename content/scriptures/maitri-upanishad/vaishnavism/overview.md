## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Maitri Upanishad's teaching on the two forms of Brahman — manifest and unmanifest, temporal and eternal — as pointing to Vishnu's dual nature as both the immanent sustainer of the cosmos and the transcendent Lord beyond creation. The Upanishad's detailed account of bondage through the three *gunas* confirms the Vaishnava understanding that the soul is trapped in material nature until liberated by God's grace.

## Theological Emphases

The Maitri's systematic meditation instructions are read as devotional practices directed toward the Supreme Lord. The meditation on Om as a means to reach Brahman is understood as a form of *upasana* (worship) that purifies the heart for receiving divine grace. The Upanishad's acknowledgment that the self is bound by *sattva*, *rajas*, and *tamas* until freed by higher knowledge confirms the need for the Lord's intervention in liberation.
