Chapter 5 presents a detailed analysis of the three gunas — *sattva* (purity, light), *rajas* (passion, activity), and *tamas* (darkness, inertia) — and their effects on the individual self. Vaishnava commentators read this as describing the soul's entanglement in material nature (*prakriti*), from which only the Lord's grace can free it. The Bhagavad Gita's guna teachings (chapters 14 and 17) expand on this Upanishadic foundation.

The chapter's analysis of how each guna affects consciousness — sattva producing clarity and happiness, rajas producing restlessness and desire, tamas producing delusion and sloth — provides the basis for the Vaishnava understanding of spiritual development as a progressive shift from tamas through rajas to sattva, and ultimately beyond all three gunas through devotion to God.
