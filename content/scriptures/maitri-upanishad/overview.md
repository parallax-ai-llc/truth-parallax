## Overview

The Maitri Upanishad (also known as Maitrayaniya Upanishad) belongs to the Krishna Yajur Veda and is one of the later principal Upanishads. It presents a comprehensive synthesis of earlier Upanishadic teachings, integrating cosmology, the nature of the self, the theory of the three *gunas* (qualities), and detailed instructions on meditation and yoga. Its syncretic approach and systematic organization make it a bridge between the older Upanishads and the later philosophical systems of Samkhya, Yoga, and Vedanta.

## Structure

The Maitri Upanishad consists of seven chapters:

1. **Chapter 1 — The King's Despair**: Brihadratha's disillusionment and his approach to the sage Shakayanya
2. **Chapter 2 — The Nature of the Self**: The self as distinct from the body and bound by the gunas
3. **Chapter 3 — The Two Forms of Brahman**: Time and the timeless, the manifest and unmanifest
4. **Chapter 4 — The Inner Self**: The self within the heart, the five vital breaths
5. **Chapter 5 — The Three Gunas**: Sattva, rajas, and tamas and their effects on consciousness
6. **Chapter 6 — Meditation and Yoga**: Detailed techniques for meditation on Brahman, including Om
7. **Chapter 7 — The Supreme Brahman**: The nature of ultimate reality beyond all distinctions

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: Brahman in two forms — with and without qualities — points to Vishnu's dual nature
- **Shaivism**: The yoga teachings anticipate Shaiva meditation practices
- **Advaita Vedanta**: A systematic presentation of non-dual Brahman beyond the gunas
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: The two forms of Brahman demonstrate God's transcendence and immanence
- **Academic**: An important transitional text between early Upanishads and classical philosophy
- **Comparative**: The guna theory parallels quality-based cosmologies in other traditions

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| The Two Brahmans | The manifest (saguna) and unmanifest (nirguna) aspects of the absolute |
| The Three Gunas | Sattva, rajas, and tamas as the qualities binding the self |
| Meditation Techniques | Systematic instructions for meditation on Om and Brahman |
| Time and Eternity | The relationship between temporal existence and timeless reality |
| Liberation Through Knowledge | Freedom from the gunas through self-knowledge and yoga |
