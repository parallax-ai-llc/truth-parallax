## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Maitri Upanishad provides a systematic exposition of the relationship between the conditioned self (bound by the *gunas*) and the unconditioned Brahman (beyond all qualities). The Upanishad's distinction between the two forms of Brahman — *saguna* (with qualities) and *nirguna* (without qualities) — is central to Shankara's theology, even though Shankara did not write a separate commentary on this text.

## Theological Emphases

The Maitri's teaching that the self is bound by *sattva*, *rajas*, and *tamas* and freed through knowledge provides scriptural support for the Advaita understanding of bondage as ignorance and liberation as knowledge. The detailed meditation instructions serve as *upasana* — preparatory practices that purify the mind for the direct recognition of non-dual Brahman. The Upanishad's final teaching on the supreme Brahman beyond all distinctions points to the *nirguna* absolute that is Advaita's ultimate reality.
