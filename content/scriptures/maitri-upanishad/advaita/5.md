For Advaita, the three gunas belong entirely to *prakriti* (material nature) and have no connection with the true self (*atman*), which is pure consciousness beyond all qualities. The self appears to be affected by the gunas only through ignorance (*avidya*) — the mistaken identification of the witness-consciousness with the body-mind complex.

Chapter 5's detailed analysis of the gunas serves the pedagogical purpose of helping the seeker discriminate between the self and the not-self. By understanding how sattva, rajas, and tamas shape one's mental states, the seeker learns to identify with the unchanging witness rather than with the changing mental content. This discrimination (*viveka*) is the preliminary step toward the direct recognition of non-dual Brahman.
