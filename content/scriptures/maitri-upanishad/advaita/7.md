Chapter 7 presents the Maitri Upanishad's highest teaching from the Advaita perspective: the Supreme Brahman beyond all qualities, distinctions, and determinations. This *nirguna* (qualityless) Brahman is the sole reality — all forms, names, and qualities are superimpositions (*adhyaropa*) upon it. The chapter's systematic negation of all attributes culminates in the recognition that Brahman is pure being-consciousness-bliss (*sat-chit-ananda*).

The teaching that Brahman is both the material and efficient cause of the universe is read by Advaita as *vivarta* (apparent transformation): Brahman appears as the world without actually changing, as a rope appears as a snake without really becoming one. The ultimate truth is that Brahman alone exists, and the world is its apparent manifestation.
