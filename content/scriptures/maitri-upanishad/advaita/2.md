For Advaita, Chapter 2 establishes the fundamental distinction between the self (*atman*) and the body-mind complex (*anatman*). The self is eternal, unchanging, and inherently free; its apparent bondage is due to ignorance (*avidya*) — the mistaken identification of the self with the body, senses, and mind. The three gunas that bind belong to *prakriti* (nature), not to the self.

Shankara's tradition reads the chapter's teaching on the self's bondage as a preliminary description that will be transcended by the higher teaching of non-duality. The self appears to be bound by the gunas, but in reality — from the absolute standpoint — the self is Brahman, and Brahman is never bound. The purpose of the teaching is to motivate discrimination (*viveka*) between the self and the not-self.
