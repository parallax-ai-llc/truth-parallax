For Advaita, the inner self of Chapter 4 is Brahman appearing as the individual soul (*jiva*) due to limiting adjuncts (*upadhis*) — the five pranas, the body, and the senses. The self in the heart is not a miniature deity but the universal Brahman viewed through the lens of individuality. The teaching on the five pranas describes the mechanism of apparent limitation but does not define the self's true nature.

The significance of this chapter lies in pointing the seeker inward — from the external world to the inner reality. The self in the heart is a stepping stone to the recognition that this very self is Brahman, unlimited and free.
