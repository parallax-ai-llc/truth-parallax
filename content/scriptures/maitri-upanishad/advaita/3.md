Chapter 3's distinction between the two forms of Brahman is foundational for Advaita Vedanta. The temporal, manifest Brahman (*saguna*, with qualities) is Brahman as it appears to the ignorant mind — associated with time, form, and cosmic functions. The eternal, unmanifest Brahman (*nirguna*, without qualities) is Brahman as it truly is — timeless, formless, and beyond all determination.

Shankara's tradition reads this distinction as pedagogical: the lower Brahman is a stepping stone to the higher. Meditation on the manifest form — the sun, time, the cosmic order — prepares the mind for the recognition of the unmanifest absolute. Ultimately, there is only one Brahman, and its apparent duality is resolved through knowledge.
