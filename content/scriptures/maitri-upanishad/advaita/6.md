For Advaita, Chapter 6 presents the most important *upasana* (meditative worship) techniques in the Maitri Upanishad. These practices — meditation on Om, concentration on the inner light, breath regulation — serve to purify the mind and prepare it for the direct recognition of non-dual Brahman. They are not liberation itself but necessary preliminaries.

The meditation on Om is particularly significant: Om represents both the conditioned and unconditioned Brahman. The three syllables (A-U-M) correspond to the three states of consciousness (waking, dreaming, deep sleep), while the silence after Om represents the fourth state (*turiya*) — pure awareness beyond all states, which is Brahman itself. This teaching parallels the Mandukya Upanishad's analysis.
