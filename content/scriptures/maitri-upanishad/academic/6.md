Chapter 6 contains the Maitri Upanishad's most extensive practical instructions and represents some of the earliest systematic accounts of meditation technique in Indian literature. Scholars note the sophistication of the meditation method: concentration on Om, regulation of breath, visualization of the inner light, and progressive internalization of awareness.

The relationship between these techniques and Patanjali's later Yoga Sutras is debated. Some scholars see the Maitri as a direct precursor to classical Yoga; others argue that both draw on a common pool of contemplative practices circulating in the late Vedic and early post-Vedic period. The chapter's references to the inner sun and the *nadi* (energy channel) system suggest familiarity with proto-tantric physiological concepts.
