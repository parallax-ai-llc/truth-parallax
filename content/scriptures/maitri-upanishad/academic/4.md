Chapter 4's teaching on the inner self and the five pranas connects the Maitri to the broader Upanishadic and medical traditions. The five-fold classification of prana appears in earlier Upanishads (Prasna 3, Chandogya 3.13) and becomes standard in Ayurvedic medicine. Scholars note that the Maitri's version is more systematic than earlier accounts, suggesting refinement through engagement with medical knowledge.

The concept of the self dwelling in the heart as a light or flame — found across multiple Upanishads — may derive from actual observations during meditation or from metaphorical reasoning about consciousness as illumination. The heart as the seat of consciousness contrasts with later Indian (and modern Western) identification of consciousness with the brain.
