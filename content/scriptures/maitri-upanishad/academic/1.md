Chapter 1's narrative frame — a disillusioned king seeking wisdom from a sage — follows a pattern common in Upanishadic literature (cf. Janaka in the Brihadaranyaka, Pravahana in the Chandogya). Scholars note that the Maitri's version is more elaborate and psychologically detailed than earlier examples, reflecting a later period in which the existential dimension of spiritual seeking was more explicitly thematized.

The list of fallen empires and dead kings may reflect historical awareness of political instability in the late Vedic or early post-Vedic period. The emphasis on the body's decay and the futility of sensory pleasure has been compared to Buddhist teachings on suffering (*dukkha*) and impermanence (*anicca*), suggesting possible Buddhist influence or parallel development.
