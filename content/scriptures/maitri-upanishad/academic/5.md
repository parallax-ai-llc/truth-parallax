Chapter 5's guna theory is one of the most philosophically significant elements of the Maitri Upanishad. Scholars debate its relationship to classical Samkhya: does the Maitri transmit a pre-Samkhya version of the guna doctrine, or does it reflect knowledge of an already developed Samkhya system? Van Buitenen argues for the former, seeing the Maitri as a transitional text that contributed to the crystallization of Samkhya philosophy.

The psychological dimension of the guna theory — its analysis of how different mental states arise from different combinations of the three qualities — anticipates later Indian psychological classification systems and may have influenced Buddhist Abhidharma analysis of mental factors (*cetasikas*).
