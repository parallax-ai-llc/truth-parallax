## Overview

The Maitri Upanishad is generally dated to the 3rd-2nd century BCE, making it one of the later principal Upanishads. Scholars note its syncretic character — it draws on earlier Upanishadic teachings while incorporating elements of proto-Samkhya (the three *gunas*), early yoga (systematic meditation techniques), and possibly Buddhist influence (the emphasis on suffering and disillusionment as the starting point of spiritual inquiry).

## Research Themes

Van Buitenen's critical edition identifies multiple layers of composition, suggesting that the Maitri grew through successive additions. The guna theory, which becomes central to classical Samkhya, appears here in a relatively developed form, raising questions about the chronological relationship between the Upanishad and the Samkhya system. The yoga instructions in Chapter 6 — including concentration, breath control, and meditation on Om — represent some of the earliest systematic accounts of meditative practice in Indian literature, predating Patanjali's Yoga Sutras.
