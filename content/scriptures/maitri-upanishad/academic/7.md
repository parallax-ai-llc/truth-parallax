Chapter 7's description of the Supreme Brahman beyond all determinations represents the most abstract and philosophically developed passage in the Maitri Upanishad. Scholars note its use of the *neti neti* ("not this, not this") strategy familiar from the Brihadaranyaka, combined with positive descriptions of Brahman as the source of all existence. This dialectical approach — negating all finite descriptions while affirming infinite reality — anticipates the sophisticated metaphysics of later Vedanta.

Van Buitenen suggests that Chapter 7 may be a later addition to the Maitri, as its highly abstract philosophical language contrasts with the more practical and narrative character of earlier chapters. Its reference to Brahman as both material and efficient cause engages a debate that becomes central in the Brahma Sutras and subsequent commentarial tradition.
