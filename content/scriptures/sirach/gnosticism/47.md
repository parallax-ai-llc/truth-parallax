## Gnostic Reading of Sirach 47

Sirach chapter 47, "David, Solomon, and Rehoboam," with 25 verses, presents a complex case for Gnostic interpretation. Ben Sira's celebration of conventional wisdom, Torah observance, and the goodness of the created order stands in tension with core Gnostic convictions about the material world's deficiency. Nevertheless, Gnostic readers found subversive possibilities within the wisdom tradition — Sophia (Wisdom) is central to both Sirach's theology and Gnostic mythology, creating an unexpected bridge between the two.

## Counter-Reading and Appropriation

David and Solomon present a complex case. David's Psalms were used by Gnostic communities (the *Pistis Sophia* extensively interprets the Psalms), suggesting a more-than-psychic origin. Solomon's association with esoteric wisdom made him a figure of interest to Gnostic circles despite his place in the Demiurge's genealogy.

## The Gnostic Hermeneutic of Suspicion

Gnostic interpreters approached texts like Sirach 47 with a "hermeneutic of suspicion," reading against the grain to discover hidden knowledge beneath conventional piety. Where Ben Sira celebrates the heroes of Israel's covenant, the Gnostic reader asks what deeper reality lies concealed. The Valentinian tradition in particular, as described by Irenaeus and Clement of Alexandria, was adept at finding pneumatic (spiritual) meanings in texts that appeared to teach only psychic (soulish) or hylic (material) wisdom.
