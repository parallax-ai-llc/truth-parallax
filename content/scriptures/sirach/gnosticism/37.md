## Gnostic Reading of Sirach 37

Sirach chapter 37, "True and False Friends and Counselors," with 31 verses, presents a complex case for Gnostic interpretation. Ben Sira's celebration of conventional wisdom, Torah observance, and the goodness of the created order stands in tension with core Gnostic convictions about the material world's deficiency. Nevertheless, Gnostic readers found subversive possibilities within the wisdom tradition — Sophia (Wisdom) is central to both Sirach's theology and Gnostic mythology, creating an unexpected bridge between the two.

## Counter-Reading and Appropriation

The search for true counselors is reframed as the search for a Gnostic teacher — one who has received the secret tradition and can awaken the pneumatic spark. The *Gospel of Thomas* (logion 108) promises that the one who drinks from Jesus' mouth will become like him.

## The Gnostic Hermeneutic of Suspicion

Gnostic interpreters approached texts like Sirach 37 with a "hermeneutic of suspicion," reading against the grain to discover hidden knowledge beneath conventional piety. Where Ben Sira celebrates social order and ethical conduct, the Gnostic reader asks what deeper reality lies concealed. The Valentinian tradition in particular, as described by Irenaeus and Clement of Alexandria, was adept at finding pneumatic (spiritual) meanings in texts that appeared to teach only psychic (soulish) or hylic (material) wisdom.
