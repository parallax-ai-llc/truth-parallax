## The Demiurge's Social Order

Sirach 7's comprehensive catalogue of social duties represents, for Gnostic readers, the moral order established by the Demiurge to maintain control over the material world. While these duties may serve a pragmatic purpose — maintaining social harmony — they do not address the soul's deepest need: liberation from the material realm itself.

## The Priesthood of Knowledge

The instruction to honor priests (7:29-31) is reinterpreted: the true priests are not the Levitical functionaries of the Temple but the Gnostic teachers who mediate saving knowledge. The *Gospel of Philip* describes a Gnostic priesthood that administers sacraments of knowledge rather than animal sacrifices — a spiritual priesthood that transcends the material cult.

## Compassion as Awakening

The call to visit the sick and comfort the grieving (7:33-36) is read at two levels: on the surface, as conventional compassion; at a deeper level, as the Gnostic's mission to awaken those who are "sick" with ignorance and "grieving" because of their separation from the Pleroma.
