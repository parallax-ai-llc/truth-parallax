## Gnostic Reading of Sirach 44

Sirach chapter 44, "Praise of the Ancestors — Introduction," with 23 verses, presents a complex case for Gnostic interpretation. Ben Sira's celebration of conventional wisdom, Torah observance, and the goodness of the created order stands in tension with core Gnostic convictions about the material world's deficiency. Nevertheless, Gnostic readers found subversive possibilities within the wisdom tradition — Sophia (Wisdom) is central to both Sirach's theology and Gnostic mythology, creating an unexpected bridge between the two.

## Counter-Reading and Appropriation

The 'Praise of the Ancestors' is subjected to Gnostic re-evaluation. Some ancestors — particularly those who upheld the Demiurge's law — are seen as psychic heroes at best. The Gnostic tradition had its own alternative genealogy, tracing the bearers of gnosis through Seth.

## The Gnostic Hermeneutic of Suspicion

Gnostic interpreters approached texts like Sirach 44 with a "hermeneutic of suspicion," reading against the grain to discover hidden knowledge beneath conventional piety. Where Ben Sira celebrates the heroes of Israel's covenant, the Gnostic reader asks what deeper reality lies concealed. The Valentinian tradition in particular, as described by Irenaeus and Clement of Alexandria, was adept at finding pneumatic (spiritual) meanings in texts that appeared to teach only psychic (soulish) or hylic (material) wisdom.
