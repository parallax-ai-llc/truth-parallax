## Sophia's Self-Revelation

Sirach 24 is the Gnostic reader's most treasured chapter. Wisdom's first-person speech — describing her origin with God, her procession from His mouth, her search for a dwelling place, and her establishment on earth — is read as Sophia's own autobiography. The passage encodes the central Gnostic myth: a divine aeon who descends from the Pleroma, wanders through creation, and finds a resting place — though for Gnostics, that resting place is not Torah but the awakened pneumatic soul.

## Wisdom's Descent as Cosmic Drama

Where Ben Sira reads Wisdom's descent as a celebration of divine providence, Gnostics read it as tragedy: Sophia's fall from the Pleroma into the material world, where she becomes entangled in the very creation she helped produce. The *Pistis Sophia* elaborates this myth extensively: Sophia is deceived, falls, repents, and is ultimately rescued by the Logos.

## Beyond Torah

Ben Sira's identification of Wisdom with Torah (24:23) is the point where Gnostic and conventional readings decisively diverge. For Gnostics, Torah is the Demiurge's instruction manual — the rules of a limited world. True Sophia transcends Torah entirely, offering not a law to obey but a knowledge that transforms. The Gnostic reads 24:23 as Ben Sira's misidentification — a mistake that confines cosmic Wisdom within the narrow boundaries of Mosaic legislation.
