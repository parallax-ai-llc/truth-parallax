## The Pneumatic Community

Sirach 6's distinction between true and false friends is read by Gnostic interpreters as distinguishing between pneumatic companions (those who share *gnosis*) and the uninitiated (those still bound to the material world). The "faithful friend" who is "beyond price" (6:14-16) is the fellow Gnostic who shares the journey of awakening.

## Wisdom's Fetters as Initiation

The imagery of submitting to Wisdom's yoke and fetters (6:24-31) describes the Gnostic initiation process. The student must undergo discipline, purification, and the surrender of worldly attachments before receiving the liberating knowledge. The paradox — that chains become ornaments — mirrors the Gnostic conviction that what appears as constraint to the uninitiated is actually freedom.

## The Gnostic Teacher

The counsel to seek wise elders (6:34-37) is read as instruction to find a Gnostic teacher — one who has received the secret tradition from the apostles or from Sophia herself. The Valentinian and Sethian schools each claimed chains of esoteric transmission, and Sirach's educational ideal mirrors their model of master-disciple instruction.
