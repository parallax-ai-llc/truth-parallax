## Providence Reconsidered

Sirach 11's teaching that both good and evil come from God (11:14) is deeply problematic for Gnostic theology, which insists that the true God is purely good. The alternation of fortune — prosperity and adversity — is attributed to the Demiurge's capricious governance of the material world, not to the transcendent Father's benevolent will.

## The Reversal of Worldly Values

The theme of divine reversal — the lowly exalted, the mighty cast down — resonates with the Gnostic inversion of conventional values. The pneumatic person, though despised by the world, possesses the greatest treasure: knowledge of the divine. The hylic (material) person, though wealthy and powerful, is truly impoverished.

## Detachment from Material Fortune

The warning against trusting in riches (11:14-28) aligns with the Gnostic contempt for material attachments. The material world is not the true home of the pneumatic soul, and its goods — wealth, health, pleasure — are transient illusions. True security lies in the knowledge of one's divine origin and destiny.
