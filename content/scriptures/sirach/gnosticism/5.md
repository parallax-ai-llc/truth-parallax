## Presumption on the Demiurge's Mercy

Sirach 5:6-7 warns against presuming on divine mercy. Gnostic interpreters read this in two ways: on the surface, it is the Demiurge's warning to his subjects to stay in line; at a deeper level, it warns pneumatics not to become complacent about their spiritual status. Even those with the divine spark must actively cultivate *gnosis* — spiritual awakening is not automatic.

## The Tongue and Authentic Speech

Verses 9-15 on speech ethics are read through the Gnostic concern with authentic expression. The "double-tongued" person is one who speaks the language of the archons while secretly knowing the truth — or worse, one who has forgotten the truth entirely. The Gnostic ideal is *parrhēsia* — bold, truthful speech that proclaims hidden knowledge regardless of social consequences.

## Beyond Conventional Morality

Gnostic interpreters note that Sirach's moral instruction, while not without value, operates within the Demiurge's system of rewards and punishments. The pneumatic person, while perhaps observing conventional morality for practical purposes, recognizes that true liberation lies beyond moral categories — in the direct knowledge of the divine.
