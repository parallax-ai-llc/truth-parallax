## Gnostic Reading of Sirach 46

Sirach chapter 46, "Joshua, Caleb, and the Judges," with 20 verses, presents a complex case for Gnostic interpretation. Ben Sira's celebration of conventional wisdom, Torah observance, and the goodness of the created order stands in tension with core Gnostic convictions about the material world's deficiency. Nevertheless, Gnostic readers found subversive possibilities within the wisdom tradition — Sophia (Wisdom) is central to both Sirach's theology and Gnostic mythology, creating an unexpected bridge between the two.

## Counter-Reading and Appropriation

Joshua's military conquests represent the Demiurge's violent assertion of territorial control. Gnostic pacifism, attested in several Nag Hammadi texts, stands in tension with the celebration of military heroes. The judges' cycles of sin and deliverance reflect the Demiurge's system of control through crisis and rescue.

## The Gnostic Hermeneutic of Suspicion

Gnostic interpreters approached texts like Sirach 46 with a "hermeneutic of suspicion," reading against the grain to discover hidden knowledge beneath conventional piety. Where Ben Sira celebrates the heroes of Israel's covenant, the Gnostic reader asks what deeper reality lies concealed. The Valentinian tradition in particular, as described by Irenaeus and Clement of Alexandria, was adept at finding pneumatic (spiritual) meanings in texts that appeared to teach only psychic (soulish) or hylic (material) wisdom.
