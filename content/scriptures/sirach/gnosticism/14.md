## The Awakened Conscience

Sirach 14:1-2 blesses the person whose heart does not condemn him. For Gnostic readers, the uncondemned heart is the heart that has received *gnosis* — the knowledge that dissolves guilt by revealing its roots in the Demiurge's false moral system. True freedom of conscience comes not from obedience to the law but from transcending the law through direct knowledge of the divine.

## Death and the Divine Spark

The reminder of mortality (14:11-19) is read through the Gnostic understanding of death as liberation. For the pneumatic, physical death is not a tragedy but a homecoming — the divine spark returning to the Pleroma from which it fell. The urgency is not to enjoy material pleasures before death but to attain *gnosis* before the opportunity is lost.

## Dwelling with Sophia

The closing image of the seeker camped near Wisdom's house (14:20-27) is read as the Gnostic's pursuit of Sophia. The pneumatic person arranges life around the quest for hidden knowledge — studying, meditating, and seeking the divine feminine who reveals the mysteries of the Pleroma.
