## Gnostic Reading of Sirach 48

Sirach chapter 48, "Elijah, Elisha, and Hezekiah," with 25 verses, presents a complex case for Gnostic interpretation. Ben Sira's celebration of conventional wisdom, Torah observance, and the goodness of the created order stands in tension with core Gnostic convictions about the material world's deficiency. Nevertheless, Gnostic readers found subversive possibilities within the wisdom tradition — Sophia (Wisdom) is central to both Sirach's theology and Gnostic mythology, creating an unexpected bridge between the two.

## Counter-Reading and Appropriation

Elijah's ascent to heaven in a chariot of fire resonated with Gnostic ascent narratives — the soul's journey through the archontic spheres back to the Pleroma. However, Elijah's zealous violence against the prophets of Baal aligns him with the Demiurge's jealousy, complicating his Gnostic reception.

## The Gnostic Hermeneutic of Suspicion

Gnostic interpreters approached texts like Sirach 48 with a "hermeneutic of suspicion," reading against the grain to discover hidden knowledge beneath conventional piety. Where Ben Sira celebrates the heroes of Israel's covenant, the Gnostic reader asks what deeper reality lies concealed. The Valentinian tradition in particular, as described by Irenaeus and Clement of Alexandria, was adept at finding pneumatic (spiritual) meanings in texts that appeared to teach only psychic (soulish) or hylic (material) wisdom.
