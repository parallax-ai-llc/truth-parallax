## Testing as Material Entrapment

Sirach 2's call to prepare for testing is read by Gnostic interpreters not as a summons to endure suffering faithfully but as a description of the soul's condition in the material world. The pneumatic self is "tested" by its imprisonment in matter — the body, with its passions and limitations, is itself the trial from which the soul seeks liberation.

## Trust in the True God

The appeal to trust God (2:6-10) is reframed: trust not in the Demiurge, whose "mercy" is merely the continuation of material existence, but in the transcendent Father who sends messengers of *gnosis* to awaken the sleeping souls. The "generations of old" who trusted and were not disappointed (2:10) are reinterpreted as the pneumatic lineage — those who possessed secret knowledge from Seth through the prophets.

## Mercy Beyond the Cosmos

The closing affirmation that God's mercy equals His majesty (2:18) is embraced by Gnostic readers who identify the "God" in question with the true, unknown Father rather than the Demiurge. The mercy of the transcendent God is precisely the sending of Sophia and the Logos to rescue the divine sparks trapped in matter.
