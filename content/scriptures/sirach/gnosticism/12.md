## Discernment Between Pneumatics and Hylics

Sirach 12's counsel to know the recipient of one's generosity (12:1-7) is read by Gnostic interpreters as instruction to share *gnosis* selectively. The pearls of spiritual knowledge must not be cast before those incapable of receiving them — a principle Jesus articulates in Matthew 7:6, which Gnostic teachers frequently cited.

## Enemies as Archonic Agents

The warning about enemies who feign friendship (12:8-18) is interpreted as describing the archons and their agents who infiltrate spiritual communities. The Gnostic teacher must exercise discernment to identify those who truly seek knowledge and those who are agents of the Demiurge — sent to suppress or distort the truth.

## Spiritual Warfare

Sirach 12's realism about enmity reflects the Gnostic understanding of cosmic conflict between the forces of light (the Pleroma) and the forces of darkness (the archons). The spiritual life is warfare, and discernment is the primary weapon of the pneumatic warrior.
