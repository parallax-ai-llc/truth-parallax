## The Poor as the Spiritually Unawakened

While Sirach 4:1-10 addresses material poverty, Gnostic interpreters read "the poor" as those who lack *gnosis* — the spiritually impoverished who have not yet recognized their divine origin. True compassion, in this reading, is not primarily material charity but the transmission of saving knowledge to those still trapped in ignorance.

## Sophia as Initiator

In 4:11-19, Wisdom tests and disciplines her students before revealing her secrets. Gnostic readers find here a description of the initiatory process: Sophia leads the pneumatic soul through trials of purification before granting access to the hidden mysteries. The *Pistis Sophia* elaborates this pattern in its account of Sophia's own journey through suffering toward restoration.

## The Shame of Ignorance

The discussion of shame (4:20-31) is reinterpreted: the true shame is not social disgrace but the ignorance of one's divine origin. The person who lives without *gnosis* lives in a state of perpetual shame — unaware of who they truly are. Awakening to self-knowledge is the cure for this fundamental shame.
