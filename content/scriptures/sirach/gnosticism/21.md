## Sin as Ignorance

Sirach 21's command to flee from sin is reframed by Gnostic interpreters: the fundamental "sin" is not moral transgression but ignorance (*agnoia*) of one's divine origin. The *Gospel of Truth* declares that ignorance of the Father brought anguish and terror into existence. To "flee from sin as from a snake" (21:2) is to flee from the ignorance that keeps the soul imprisoned.

## The Wise as Pneumatics

The contrast between the wise and the foolish (21:11-28) maps onto the Gnostic distinction between pneumatics and hylics. The wise person's heart as a spring of living water describes the pneumatic whose inner divine spark flows with the knowledge of the Pleroma. The fool's broken jar represents the hylic — unable to retain spiritual truth.

## The Two Paths Reinterpreted

The smooth path ending in Hades (21:10) is read as the path of the Demiurge's religion — comfortable, conventional, socially approved, but leading only to continued cycles of material existence. The path of *gnosis*, though difficult and socially marginal, leads to genuine liberation from the cycle of birth and death.
