## The Gnostic Reading of Human Creation

Sirach 17's account of humanity's creation echoes Genesis but receives a radical reinterpretation in Gnostic hands. The Demiurge formed the body from earth, but the divine spark — the pneumatic element in humanity — originates from the Pleroma, secretly implanted by Sophia. The human person is thus a battleground between two origins: the material (from the Demiurge) and the spiritual (from the true God).

## Knowledge as Gift and Danger

The endowment of humanity with reason and moral discernment (17:6-10) is read ambivalently. The Demiurge gave humans enough intelligence to serve him but not enough to discover the truth. It is Sophia — the serpent in Eden, according to some Gnostic texts — who bestows the higher knowledge that the Demiurge fears, enabling humans to see beyond the material veil.

## Repentance as Awakening

The call to repentance (17:25-32) is reinterpreted as a call to *metanoia* in its literal sense: a change of mind, a transformation of consciousness. True repentance is not sorrow for violating the Demiurge's rules but the radical reorientation of the self toward the transcendent God — the awakening from the sleep of ignorance.
