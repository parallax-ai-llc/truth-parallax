## The Demiurge's Pride

Sirach 10's denunciation of pride as "the beginning of sin" (10:13) is read by Gnostic interpreters as a description of the Demiurge himself — the arrogant creator god who declared "I am God, and there is no other" (echoing Isaiah 45:5). In Gnostic mythology, the Demiurge's pride is the original sin, the foundational delusion that produces the flawed material world.

## The Overthrow of the Archons

The theme of God casting down the proud (10:14-18) is embraced as prophecy: the archons who rule this world will be overthrown when the divine sparks are gathered and the Pleroma is restored. The Gnostic reads these verses eschatologically — the reversal is not merely historical but cosmic.

## True Honor as Gnosis

The conclusion that those who fear God deserve honor regardless of status (10:19-31) is reinterpreted: true honor belongs not to those who fear the Demiurge but to those who possess *gnosis* of the true God. Social status, wealth, and worldly power are irrelevant categories in the pneumatic world — what matters is spiritual knowledge.
