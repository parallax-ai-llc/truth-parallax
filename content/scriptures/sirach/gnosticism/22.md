## Spiritual Torpor

Sirach 22's condemnation of laziness is reinterpreted by Gnostic readers as a warning against spiritual torpor — the condition of the soul that has fallen asleep in the material world and forgotten its divine origin. The Gnostic "call" — the summons from the Pleroma that awakens the sleeping soul — addresses precisely this condition of spiritual inertia.

## Mourning the Unawakened

The comparison between mourning the dead and mourning the fool (22:7-18) takes on cosmic significance: the spiritually "dead" are those who have never received *gnosis*, and their condition is more tragic than physical death. The Gnostic teacher mourns for the hylics and psychics who cannot hear the call — even as they try to awaken any who may be pneumatic without knowing it.

## The Bond of Shared Gnosis

The meditation on friendship and its fragility (22:19-26) describes the bonds of the Gnostic community. Fellowship among those who share *gnosis* is the deepest possible human connection — but it is fragile, threatened by persecution, apostasy, and the constant pressure of the material world to dissolve spiritual bonds.
