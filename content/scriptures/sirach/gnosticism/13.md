## Material Wealth as Archonic Power

Sirach 13's observation about the exploitative dynamics between rich and poor is read by Gnostic interpreters as a description of the archonic power structure. The rich — those who benefit from the material world's arrangements — are aligned with the Demiurge, while the poor — those marginalized by the system — may be closer to spiritual truth precisely because they have less investment in the material order.

## The Three Classes of Humanity

The social dynamics Sirach describes — the rich ignoring the wise poor, the powerful exploiting the weak — are mapped onto the Gnostic tripartite anthropology: pneumatics (spiritual), psychics (soulish), and hylics (material). The pneumatic may be poor in worldly terms but rich in *gnosis*; the hylic may be wealthy but spiritually impoverished.

## Beyond Social Categories

Gnostic soteriology ultimately transcends social categories. While Sirach's social analysis is valued for its realism, the Gnostic solution is not social reform but spiritual awakening — the recognition that both wealth and poverty belong to the transient material world from which the soul seeks liberation.
