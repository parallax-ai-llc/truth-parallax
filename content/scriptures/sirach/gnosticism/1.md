## Wisdom's True Origin

Sirach 1 declares that all wisdom comes from the Lord (1:1), but Gnostic readers ask a crucial question: which Lord? If the "Lord" is the Demiurge — the ignorant creator god of the material world — then the wisdom he offers is limited, a partial truth that conceals the higher *gnosis* available only through the transcendent, unknown God. True Sophia originates beyond the visible cosmos, in the Pleroma (divine fullness).

## Fear as Bondage or Beginning

The fourfold description of the fear of the Lord (1:11-20) is read ambivalently in Gnostic traditions. Valentinian interpreters distinguished between the fear appropriate to psychics (soul-level believers who serve the Demiurge) and the love that characterizes pneumatics (spiritual beings who know the true God). Fear is a beginning, but those who remain in fear never reach *gnosis*.

## The Hidden Wisdom

Sirach's claim that wisdom was "poured out" on all creation (1:9) is reinterpreted by Gnostic readers as describing Sophia's fragmentation — her divine light scattered throughout the material world, hidden in the "sparks" of pneumatic souls awaiting awakening. The wise person is not one who fears the Demiurge but one who recognizes the divine spark within.
