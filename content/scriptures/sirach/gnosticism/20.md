## Silence and the Unspoken

Sirach 20's meditation on the wisdom of silence resonates with the Gnostic valuation of the unspeakable (*arrētos*). The deepest truths cannot be communicated in ordinary language — they are transmitted through silence, symbol, and direct experience. The *Gospel of Thomas* (logion 17) promises "what no eye has seen, what no ear has heard" — truths beyond the reach of speech.

## The Paradox of Gnosis

The paradoxes of fortune (20:9-17) — loss as gain, poverty as wealth — mirror the central Gnostic paradox: what the world calls knowledge is ignorance, and what the world calls madness is wisdom. The pneumatic person appears foolish to the world precisely because they have rejected the Demiurge's value system.

## Speech as Revelation or Concealment

The catalogue of foolish speech (20:18-31) is read as a meditation on the dual nature of language: it can reveal the divine or conceal it. The Gnostic teacher speaks in riddles and paradoxes — like Jesus in the *Gospel of Thomas* — using language to disrupt ordinary understanding and open the way to higher perception.
