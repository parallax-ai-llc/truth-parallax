## The True Father and Mother

Sirach 3's instruction to honor one's parents is read allegorically by Gnostic interpreters. The true "Father" is the transcendent, unknown God; the true "Mother" is Sophia, the divine feminine principle. Honoring these spiritual parents means recognizing one's heavenly origin and refusing to identify exclusively with the material body and its earthly parentage.

## Seeking Hidden Things

The warning "Seek not what is too difficult for you" (3:21) is subverted in Gnostic reading. While Ben Sira counsels intellectual humility, Gnostic interpreters see here the Demiurge's attempt to prevent humanity from discovering the secrets of the Pleroma. The Gnostic path is precisely the pursuit of what is "too difficult" — the hidden knowledge that the archons seek to conceal.

## Almsgiving and the Material Order

The teaching on almsgiving (3:30) belongs to the Demiurge's moral economy — a system of deeds and rewards that operates within the material world. Gnostic soteriology offers a different path: liberation not through moral works but through knowledge (*gnosis*) of one's true divine nature. The pneumatic person transcends the economy of merit.
