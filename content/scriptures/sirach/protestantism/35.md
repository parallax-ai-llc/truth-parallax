## Protestant Perspective on Sirach 35

Sirach (Ecclesiasticus) chapter 35, titled "Sacrifices and Divine Justice," contains 24 verses of practical wisdom attributed to Jesus ben Sira (c. 180 BCE). While Protestant churches classify Sirach as part of the Apocrypha rather than canonical Scripture, the book has historically been valued for edification and instruction. The 1647 Westminster Confession acknowledges that apocryphal books may be read "for example of life and instruction of manners," though not used to establish doctrine.

## Historical and Theological Context

This chapter explores the relationship between sacrifice and justice, teaching that true worship requires ethical conduct. Protestant commentators connect this with the prophetic tradition (Amos 5:21-24, Micah 6:6-8) and the Reformation principle that outward ritual without inner faith is empty.

## Significance for Protestant Readers

Although Sirach lacks canonical authority in Protestant traditions, this chapter's emphasis on practical godly living resonates with themes found throughout the canonical wisdom literature. Reformers like Luther included Sirach in his Bible translation, noting its usefulness for moral guidance despite its non-canonical status. The chapter's teachings on wisdom and ethics can be profitably compared with Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and the canonical Psalms.
