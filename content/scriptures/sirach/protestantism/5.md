## Warning Against Presumption

Sirach 5:1-8 warns against presuming on God's mercy: "Do not say, 'His mercy is great, He will forgive the multitude of my sins'" (5:6). Protestant interpreters recognize here a warning against what Bonhoeffer would later call "cheap grace." The passage affirms that God's mercy is genuine but must not be treated as a license for continued sin — a theme echoed in Romans 6:1.

## The Discipline of Speech

Verses 9-15 address the ethics of speech with characteristic precision: be quick to hear, slow to answer, and never be called a double-tongued person. Protestant commentators note the strong parallel with James 1:19 and 3:1-12. The warning against being "double-tongued" anticipates Paul's requirement for deacons in 1 Timothy 3:8.

## Practical Wisdom for Christian Living

Though Sirach lacks canonical authority in Protestant theology, this chapter exemplifies why the Reformers valued it for edification. Its practical counsel on presumption and speech addresses perennial temptations with clarity and brevity.
