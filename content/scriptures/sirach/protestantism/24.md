## Wisdom's Self-Praise

Sirach 24 is the theological heart of the book: Wisdom speaks in the first person, describing her origin with God before creation, her search for a dwelling place among the nations, and her ultimate establishment in Israel (24:1-22). Protestant interpreters recognize this as the most developed wisdom hymn outside the canonical Proverbs 8, and they note both continuities and significant differences.

## Wisdom Identified with Torah

The pivotal verse is 24:23: "All this is the book of the covenant of the Most High God, the law that Moses commanded us." Here Ben Sira explicitly identifies cosmic Wisdom with the Mosaic Torah — a move that Protestant scholars consider theologically significant as a bridge between the universal wisdom tradition and the particularism of the covenant. This identification anticipates the Johannine prologue, where the Logos — the eternal Word — becomes flesh (John 1:14).

## Protestant Christological Reading

While Protestant exegetes are careful not to read later christology directly into Sirach, they note that the trajectory from Proverbs 8 through Sirach 24 to John 1 and Colossians 1:15-20 reveals a developing theology of divine Wisdom that finds its fulfillment in Christ. Sirach 24 is thus valued as a witness to the theological ferment of Second Temple Judaism that prepared the way for the New Testament.
