## A Catalogue of Duties

Sirach 7 offers a comprehensive catalogue of moral duties spanning public and private life: avoid evil, practice humility, do not seek political office for its own sake, and be faithful in worship. Protestant interpreters appreciate the practical specificity, noting parallels with the ethical lists in Pauline epistles (Romans 12, Colossians 3).

## Family and Social Obligations

The chapter addresses duties to wives, children, slaves, livestock, and the dead with a directness characteristic of ancient Near Eastern wisdom. Protestant commentators read these instructions within their historical context, recognizing that while some specifics reflect the social structures of the ancient world, the underlying principles — faithfulness, kindness, and justice in all relationships — remain applicable.

## Generosity to the Priestly and the Poor

Verses 29-36 urge generosity toward priests, the poor, the sick, and the grieving. Protestant readers note that while the specific context is the Levitical priesthood, the broader principle of supporting those who serve God and caring for the vulnerable is reaffirmed throughout the New Testament (Galatians 6:6, 1 Timothy 5:17-18).
