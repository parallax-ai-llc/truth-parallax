## The Testing of Friends

Sirach 6 contains one of the ancient world's most perceptive treatments of friendship. Ben Sira distinguishes between fair-weather friends who vanish in adversity (6:8-12) and the faithful friend who is "a sturdy shelter" and "beyond price" (6:14-16). Protestant interpreters value this practical wisdom, noting its consistency with Proverbs 17:17 ("A friend loves at all times") and 18:24.

## The Search for Wisdom

The second half of the chapter (6:18-37) shifts to an extended exhortation to pursue wisdom with the discipline of a farmer and the patience of a student. The imagery of putting one's feet into wisdom's fetters and neck into her collar (6:24-25) — only to discover they become ornaments — resonates with the paradox of Christian freedom through obedience that Paul articulates in Romans 6:17-22.

## Seeking Wise Counsel

The chapter closes with counsel to seek the company of the wise and to listen to godly discourse (6:34-37). Protestant readers connect this to the Reformation emphasis on preaching and catechesis as the ordinary means by which wisdom is transmitted in the community of faith.
