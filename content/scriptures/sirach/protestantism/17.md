## Humanity Created in God's Image

Sirach 17 offers a theological anthropology drawing on Genesis 1-2: God created humanity from the earth, gave them authority over creation, endowed them with reason and moral discernment, and revealed His covenant to them (17:1-14). Protestant interpreters find this consistent with the canonical doctrine of the *imago Dei*, though they note that Ben Sira's optimistic assessment of human moral capacity differs from the Reformers' emphasis on the fall's devastating effects.

## The Covenant at Sinai

Verses 11-14 describe God's revelation at Sinai, where Israel received the law and witnessed divine glory. Protestant commentators note the significant link between wisdom and Sinai — Ben Sira grounds universal moral knowledge in the specific historical event of Torah revelation.

## The Call to Repentance

The chapter closes with an urgent call to repentance (17:25-32): "Turn back to the Lord and forsake your sins." Protestant readers hear in these verses an anticipation of the gospel call to repentance, while noting that Sirach's framework remains within the covenant of works rather than the gospel of grace.
