## Caution with Women

Sirach 9:1-9 contains warnings about sexual temptation — avoid jealousy toward one's wife, do not give oneself to prostitutes, and guard against the allure of beauty. Protestant interpreters read these alongside Proverbs 5-7, noting the shared concern with sexual fidelity. While some of the language reflects patriarchal assumptions of the ancient world, the underlying call to marital faithfulness and moral self-control remains a constant in Protestant ethics.

## Choosing Companions Wisely

Verses 10-16 counsel careful selection of friends and associates: forsake not an old friend, envy not the sinner, and keep company with the wise. The warning to distance oneself from those with the power to kill (9:13) reflects the political realities of Second Temple Judea but also speaks to the perennial need for discernment in relationships of power.

## Wise Governance

The chapter concludes with advice about rulers and governance (9:17-18): "Skilled workers are sought out for the council of the people." Protestant commentators connect this to the Reformed tradition's theology of vocation, where competence and godliness together qualify one for public service.
