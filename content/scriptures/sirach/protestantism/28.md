## Protestant Perspective on Sirach 28

Sirach (Ecclesiasticus) chapter 28, titled "Forgiveness and the Power of the Tongue," contains 26 verses of practical wisdom attributed to Jesus ben Sira (c. 180 BCE). While Protestant churches classify Sirach as part of the Apocrypha rather than canonical Scripture, the book has historically been valued for edification and instruction. The 1647 Westminster Confession acknowledges that apocryphal books may be read "for example of life and instruction of manners," though not used to establish doctrine.

## Historical and Theological Context

This chapter addresses forgiveness, grudge-bearing, and the destructive power of the tongue. The parallel with Jesus' teaching in Matthew 6:14-15 on forgiving others is striking and frequently noted by Protestant commentators. The extended metaphor of the tongue as fire (28:22-23) directly anticipates James 3:5-6, making this one of the most pastorally relevant Sirach passages for Protestant readers.

## Significance for Protestant Readers

Although Sirach lacks canonical authority in Protestant traditions, this chapter's emphasis on practical godly living resonates with themes found throughout the canonical wisdom literature. Reformers like Luther included Sirach in his Bible translation, noting its usefulness for moral guidance despite its non-canonical status. The chapter's teachings on wisdom and ethics can be profitably compared with Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and the canonical Psalms.
