## Preparing for Testing

Sirach 2 addresses those who would serve the Lord with a sobering call: "Prepare yourself for testing" (2:1). Protestant interpreters find this counsel remarkably consistent with James 1:2-4 and 1 Peter 1:6-7, where trials refine faith as fire refines gold. The chapter assumes that faithful living does not exempt believers from suffering but rather prepares them for it.

## Trust in God's Faithfulness

The chapter builds toward a crescendo of trust: "Consider the generations of old and see: has anyone trusted in the Lord and been put to shame?" (2:10). Protestant readers note the anticipation of Hebrews 11, where the same logic — the retrospective survey of faithful ancestors — undergirds perseverance. The passage affirms the Protestant conviction that faith, not works, is the proper response to divine testing.

## Mercy and Judgment

The closing verses (2:15-18) distinguish between those who fear the Lord and those who do not, with a final appeal to fall into God's hands rather than human hands — echoing David's words in 2 Samuel 24:14. Protestant commentators value this as a powerful expression of trust in divine mercy.
