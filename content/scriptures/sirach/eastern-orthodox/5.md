## Against Spiritual Presumption

Sirach 5:1-8 warns against presuming on God's mercy without repentance. Orthodox theology distinguishes between confidence in God's love (*parrhēsia*) and sinful presumption that treats grace as a license. The Fathers taught that genuine repentance involves the whole person — not merely intellectual acknowledgment but the turning of the heart (*metanoia*).

## The Discipline of the Tongue

Verses 9-15 counsel consistency and truthfulness in speech. Orthodox ascetical theology treats the governance of the tongue as a primary spiritual discipline. The hesychastic tradition — rooted in the practice of inner stillness — begins with external silence and the careful monitoring of speech, recognizing that the tongue reveals the state of the heart.

## Wholeness of Heart

The warning against being "double-tongued" (5:14) connects to the Orthodox emphasis on integrity (*akeraiotes*) — singleness of heart and speech. The divided heart is the fundamental spiritual illness; healing comes through the unification of the person in the love of God, achieved through prayer, fasting, and the sacramental life.
