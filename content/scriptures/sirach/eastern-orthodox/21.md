## Sin and Repentance

The command to "flee from sin as from a snake" (21:1-2) resonates with the Orthodox emphasis on vigilance (*nēpsis*). The Fathers taught that the spiritual life requires constant watchfulness — identifying temptation at its earliest stage and cutting it off before it takes root. The Jesus Prayer serves as the primary weapon in this ongoing spiritual combat.

## The Wise Person's Heart

The contrast between the wise and the foolish (21:11-28) — the wise person's heart as a spring, the fool's as a broken jar — illustrates the Orthodox understanding of the *nous* (the spiritual intellect). When the nous is purified through ascetical practice, it becomes a vessel of divine light; when corrupted by passions, it cannot retain wisdom.

## The Two Ways

The image of the smooth path ending in destruction (21:10) evokes the Orthodox understanding of the two ways — a theme from the Didache through the entire patristic tradition. The narrow way of ascetical discipline leads to life; the broad way of self-indulgence leads to spiritual death.
