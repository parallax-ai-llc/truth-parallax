## Orthodox Reading of Sirach 27

The Wisdom of Sirach chapter 27, "Sin, Foolishness, and Retribution," with its 30 verses, is received as canonical Scripture in Eastern Orthodox tradition. The Septuagint text, which includes Sirach, has always been the Old Testament of the Greek-speaking Church. The Fathers frequently cite this book — St. John Chrysostom, St. Clement of Alexandria, and St. Basil the Great all drew upon its wisdom for homiletic and catechetical purposes.

## Patristic and Liturgical Dimensions

The Fathers' extensive teaching on guarding the tongue — particularly St. John Climacus in *The Ladder of Divine Ascent* (Step 10, on slander) — provides the ascetic context for reading this chapter. The connection between speech and spiritual condition is fundamental to Orthodox hesychast practice.

## Theosis and the Wisdom Tradition

Orthodox theology reads Sirach's wisdom teaching as preparation for the fullness of revelation in Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24). The chapter's themes of virtue, discipline, and divine order are understood within the framework of theosis — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature. The liturgical use of Sirach during Great Lent and other penitential seasons underscores its role in the ascetic and spiritual formation of the faithful.
