## Orthodox Reading of Sirach 45

The Wisdom of Sirach chapter 45, "Moses, Aaron, and Phinehas," with its 26 verses, is received as canonical Scripture in Eastern Orthodox tradition. The Septuagint text, which includes Sirach, has always been the Old Testament of the Greek-speaking Church. The Fathers frequently cite this book — St. John Chrysostom, St. Clement of Alexandria, and St. Basil the Great all drew upon its wisdom for homiletic and catechetical purposes.

## Patristic and Liturgical Dimensions

The Aaronic priesthood is read typologically as prefiguring both Christ's eternal priesthood and the ordained ministry of the Church. The detailed description of priestly vestments resonates with the rich liturgical vestiture of Orthodox clergy.

## Theosis and the Wisdom Tradition

Orthodox theology reads Sirach's wisdom teaching as preparation for the fullness of revelation in Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24). The chapter's themes of the righteous ancestors and their faith are understood within the framework of theosis — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature. The liturgical use of Sirach during Great Lent and other penitential seasons underscores its role in the ascetic and spiritual formation of the faithful.
