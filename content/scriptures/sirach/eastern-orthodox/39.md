## Orthodox Reading of Sirach 39

The Wisdom of Sirach chapter 39, "The Scribe and Praise of God," with its 35 verses, is received as canonical Scripture in Eastern Orthodox tradition. The Septuagint text, which includes Sirach, has always been the Old Testament of the Greek-speaking Church. The Fathers frequently cite this book — St. John Chrysostom, St. Clement of Alexandria, and St. Basil the Great all drew upon its wisdom for homiletic and catechetical purposes.

## Patristic and Liturgical Dimensions

The ideal scribe who combines study, prayer, and public service embodies the Orthodox integration of theology and doxology. The hymn of praise (39:12-35) anticipates the Great Doxology and other liturgical canticles that structure Orthodox worship.

## Theosis and the Wisdom Tradition

Orthodox theology reads Sirach's wisdom teaching as preparation for the fullness of revelation in Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24). The chapter's themes of virtue, discipline, and divine order are understood within the framework of theosis — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature. The liturgical use of Sirach during Great Lent and other penitential seasons underscores its role in the ascetic and spiritual formation of the faithful.
