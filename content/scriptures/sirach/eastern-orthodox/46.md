## Orthodox Reading of Sirach 46

The Wisdom of Sirach chapter 46, "Joshua, Caleb, and the Judges," with its 20 verses, is received as canonical Scripture in Eastern Orthodox tradition. The Septuagint text, which includes Sirach, has always been the Old Testament of the Greek-speaking Church. The Fathers frequently cite this book — St. John Chrysostom, St. Clement of Alexandria, and St. Basil the Great all drew upon its wisdom for homiletic and catechetical purposes.

## Patristic and Liturgical Dimensions

Joshua's conquest and the judges' deliverances are read as types of spiritual warfare. Orthodox hymnography celebrates these figures, and the tradition of naming churches after Old Testament saints reflects their ongoing veneration.

## Theosis and the Wisdom Tradition

Orthodox theology reads Sirach's wisdom teaching as preparation for the fullness of revelation in Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24). The chapter's themes of the righteous ancestors and their faith are understood within the framework of theosis — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature. The liturgical use of Sirach during Great Lent and other penitential seasons underscores its role in the ascetic and spiritual formation of the faithful.
