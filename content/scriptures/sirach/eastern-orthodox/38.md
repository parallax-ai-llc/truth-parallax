## Orthodox Reading of Sirach 38

The Wisdom of Sirach chapter 38, "Physicians, Mourning, and Artisans," with its 34 verses, is received as canonical Scripture in Eastern Orthodox tradition. The Septuagint text, which includes Sirach, has always been the Old Testament of the Greek-speaking Church. The Fathers frequently cite this book — St. John Chrysostom, St. Clement of Alexandria, and St. Basil the Great all drew upon its wisdom for homiletic and catechetical purposes.

## Patristic and Liturgical Dimensions

The Orthodox Church has always affirmed the legitimacy of medicine alongside the healing ministry of prayer, anointing, and the saints. The chapter's integration of physician and prayer (38:1-15) mirrors the Orthodox practice of Holy Unction and the veneration of physician-saints (Sts. Cosmas and Damian, St. Panteleimon).

## Theosis and the Wisdom Tradition

Orthodox theology reads Sirach's wisdom teaching as preparation for the fullness of revelation in Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24). The chapter's themes of virtue, discipline, and divine order are understood within the framework of theosis — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature. The liturgical use of Sirach during Great Lent and other penitential seasons underscores its role in the ascetic and spiritual formation of the faithful.
