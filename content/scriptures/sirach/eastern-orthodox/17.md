## The Image of God

Sirach 17's account of humanity's creation — endowed with reason, moral discernment, and dominion over creation — resonates with the Orthodox theology of the *imago Dei*. The Fathers distinguished between the "image" (given at creation) and the "likeness" (the goal of theosis). Every human being bears God's image; the call to holiness is the call to grow into His likeness.

## The Covenant and Divine Glory

The description of Israel witnessing God's glory at Sinai (17:11-14) connects to the Orthodox theology of divine light. The Taboric light — the uncreated light that shone on Mount Tabor at the Transfiguration — is understood as the same divine glory that appeared at Sinai. Gregory Palamas defended this teaching, affirming that humans can truly participate in God's uncreated energies.

## The Urgency of Repentance

The call to repentance (17:25-32) echoes through Orthodox Lenten worship. The reminder that the dead cannot praise God is read not as a denial of the afterlife but as an exhortation to repent now — the same urgency expressed in the Lenten hymn "Open to me the doors of repentance."
