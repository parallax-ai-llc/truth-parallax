## Orthodox Reading of Sirach 26

The Wisdom of Sirach chapter 26, "The Good and Bad Wife," with its 29 verses, is received as canonical Scripture in Eastern Orthodox tradition. The Septuagint text, which includes Sirach, has always been the Old Testament of the Greek-speaking Church. The Fathers frequently cite this book — St. John Chrysostom, St. Clement of Alexandria, and St. Basil the Great all drew upon its wisdom for homiletic and catechetical purposes.

## Patristic and Liturgical Dimensions

St. John Chrysostom's homilies on marriage provide the patristic framework for reading this chapter's portrait of the virtuous wife. Orthodox wedding liturgy and the theology of marriage as a 'little church' (*kat' oikon ekklesia*) resonate with Ben Sira's teaching on the blessedness of a faithful spouse.

## Theosis and the Wisdom Tradition

Orthodox theology reads Sirach's wisdom teaching as preparation for the fullness of revelation in Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24). The chapter's themes of virtue, discipline, and divine order are understood within the framework of theosis — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature. The liturgical use of Sirach during Great Lent and other penitential seasons underscores its role in the ascetic and spiritual formation of the faithful.
