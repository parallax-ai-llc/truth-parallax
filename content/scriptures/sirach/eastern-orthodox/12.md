## Discernment in Charity

Sirach 12's counsel to exercise discernment in giving (12:1-7) is read in Orthodox theology alongside Christ's command to love enemies. The Fathers held these in tension: discernment does not cancel love but directs it wisely. St. John Chrysostom taught that while we must give to all who ask, we should give in ways that truly help rather than enable harm.

## The Reality of Spiritual Enemies

The warning about enemies who feign friendship (12:8-18) connects to the Orthodox understanding of spiritual combat. The Church Fathers taught that discernment of spirits — the ability to distinguish between genuine and counterfeit goodness — is a spiritual gift cultivated through prayer, fasting, and obedience to a spiritual father.

## Love with Open Eyes

Orthodox ethics does not demand naivete but rather love with open eyes. Sirach 12's realism about human duplicity complements the Orthodox insistence on love: the Christian is called to love all people while exercising the spiritual discernment necessary to navigate a world affected by sin.
