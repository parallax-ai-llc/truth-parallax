## Wisdom as Bride and Mother

Sirach 15:1-6 describes Wisdom welcoming the faithful like a bride and a mother. Orthodox hymnography echoes this nuptial imagery, particularly in the services for the feast of the Elevation of the Cross and the great feasts of the Theotokos, where the Church is presented as the bride adorned with divine wisdom.

## Synergy: Divine Grace and Human Freedom

Verses 11-20 — "Before each person are life and death; whichever one chooses will be given" — express the Orthodox doctrine of synergy. Against both Pelagian self-sufficiency and fatalistic determinism, Orthodoxy teaches that salvation involves the genuine cooperation of human freedom with divine grace. The Fathers of the Second Council of Orange and later Orthodox synods affirmed this balance.

## Moral Responsibility

Orthodox theology insists on genuine moral responsibility: God does not predestine anyone to sin. This chapter's vigorous defense of free choice aligns with the Orthodox rejection of double predestination and affirms the goodness of the human will as created by God — wounded by sin but not destroyed, and capable of responding to grace.
