## Sophia and the Logos

Sirach 24 is the theological heart of the book and a foundational text for Orthodox Sophia theology. Wisdom's self-description — her origin with God, her presence at creation, her search for a dwelling place — is read christologically: the pre-incarnate Logos who "was with God" and through whom "all things were made" (John 1:1-3). The great cathedrals of Hagia Sophia (Holy Wisdom) in Constantinople and Kiev embody this theological vision in stone and light.

## Wisdom's Dwelling in Israel

Wisdom's establishment in Jerusalem (24:8-12) prefigures the Incarnation: the eternal Logos taking flesh and dwelling among His people. Orthodox theology sees a continuous line from Wisdom's tent in Zion to the tabernacle of Christ's body to the Church as the ongoing dwelling of the Spirit.

## The Inexhaustible River

The closing image of Wisdom as an overflowing river (24:25-34) resonates with the Orthodox understanding of divine grace as inexhaustible. As the Paschal hymn proclaims, "From Thy pierced side, O Christ, flowed streams of grace" — the same inexhaustible abundance that Sirach attributes to Wisdom.
