## Bechirah Chofsheet: Free Will

Sirach 15:11-20 contains one of the strongest defenses of human free will (*bechirah chofsheet*) in pre-rabbinic literature: "Before each person are life and death; whichever one chooses will be given" (15:17). This aligns with Maimonides' emphatic affirmation that free will is a fundamental principle of Torah (Hilkhot Teshuvah 5:1-3) and with the Deuteronomic source: "I have set before you life and death, blessing and curse; therefore choose life" (Deuteronomy 30:19).

## Torah and Wisdom United

The opening verses (15:1-6), where Wisdom welcomes the Torah-observant like a bride and a mother, embody Ben Sira's central theological move: the identification of cosmic wisdom with the particular revelation of the Torah. This fusion became foundational for rabbinic Judaism, where *talmud Torah* (Torah study) is understood as the highest form of wisdom.

## Against Fatalism

The insistence that God does not cause sin — "Do not say, 'It was the Lord's doing that I fell away'" (15:11) — aligns with the rabbinic rejection of fatalism. The Talmud (Berakhot 33b) teaches: "Everything is in the hands of Heaven except the fear of Heaven." Human moral choice is genuine and consequential.
