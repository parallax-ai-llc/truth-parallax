## Against Delaying Teshuvah

Sirach 5:6-7 warns against presuming on God's patience: "Do not say, 'His mercy is great, He will forgive the multitude of my sins.'" The Mishnah (Yoma 8:9) makes the same point: "If one says, 'I will sin and repent, I will sin and repent,' he is not given the opportunity to repent." Ben Sira and the rabbis agree that genuine *teshuvah* (repentance) must not be indefinitely postponed.

## Shemirat ha-Lashon

Verses 9-15 address the governance of the tongue — a theme that became central in Jewish ethics under the rubric of *shemirat ha-lashon* (guarding the tongue). The Chofetz Chaim (Rabbi Israel Meir Kagan) devoted his life's work to systematizing the laws of proper speech, building on a tradition that includes Ben Sira's warnings against double-tongued speech and careless words.

## Consistency of Character

The warning against being "double-tongued" resonates with the Jewish ideal of *tokhho ke-varo* — that a person's inside should match their outside (Berakhot 28a). Integrity of character, where speech matches thought and both align with action, is a fundamental Jewish virtue.
