## Numerical Mashalim

Sirach 25's numerical sayings follow the pattern established in Proverbs 30:15-31 — a form of *mashal* (proverb) that groups observations by number for rhetorical and mnemonic effect. Ben Sira celebrates the goods of harmony, friendship, and marital love, reflecting the Jewish ideal of *shalom bayit* (peace in the home).

## The Crown of Old Age

The praise of wisdom acquired in old age (25:3-6) resonates with the Jewish reverence for the *zaken* (elder) who has accumulated both Torah learning and life experience. The Talmud (Kiddushin 32b) defines *zaken* as *zeh she-kanah chokhmah* ("one who has acquired wisdom"), making wisdom the defining characteristic of honored age.

## Shalom Bayit

The chapter's treatment of marital difficulties (25:13-26), while reflecting the patriarchal assumptions of the ancient world, touches on the Jewish ideal of *shalom bayit* (domestic peace). Rabbinic literature extensively discusses the husband's and wife's mutual obligations, and the Talmud teaches that when peace reigns in the home, the Shekhinah dwells there (Sotah 17a). Ben Sira's concern for domestic harmony, however imbalanced in its expression, reflects this deep Jewish conviction.
