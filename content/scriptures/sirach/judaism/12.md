## Discernment in Tzedakah

Sirach 12's counsel to know the recipient of one's generosity (12:1-7) connects to the rabbinic discussion of priorities in tzedakah. While Jewish law obligates giving to all who ask, it also establishes a hierarchy: family before strangers, one's own community before outsiders (Bava Metzia 71a). This is not selfishness but prudent stewardship of limited resources.

## The Treachery of Enemies

The warning about enemies who feign friendship (12:8-18) resonates with the Talmudic observation (Sanhedrin 37a) about the difficulty of knowing the human heart. Jewish wisdom recognizes that people are complex and that discernment — the ability to read character and intention — is essential for navigating social life.

## Chesed with Sekhel

Jewish ethics teaches *chesed with sekhel* — kindness with intelligence. Sirach 12 does not counsel suspicion or misanthropy but informed generosity — the kind of giving that takes into account both the needs of the recipient and the realities of human nature.
