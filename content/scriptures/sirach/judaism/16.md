## Divine Justice in Jewish Theology

Sirach 16's affirmation that God's judgment is inescapable reflects the Jewish belief in *middat ha-din* (the attribute of justice). The Talmud teaches that God judges with both justice and mercy, and that these two attributes are held in dynamic tension. Ben Sira's survey of historical judgments — the flood, Sodom, the Canaanites — echoes the Passover Haggadah's retelling of God's mighty acts in history.

## Quality over Quantity

The claim that one righteous child is better than a thousand wicked ones (16:1-4) resonates with the rabbinic emphasis on the quality of spiritual life. Pirkei Avot (4:13) teaches that a single moment of repentance and good deeds in this world is better than all of the world to come — the qualitative over the quantitative.

## The Order of Creation

The meditation on creation's order (16:24-30) connects to the Jewish understanding of the natural world as reflecting God's wisdom. The daily liturgy praises God who "renews the work of creation every day" — the same orderly, purposeful creation that Sirach celebrates.
