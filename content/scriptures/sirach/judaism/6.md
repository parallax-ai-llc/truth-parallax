## The Value of a True Friend

Sirach 6's teaching on friendship — "A faithful friend is a sturdy shelter" (6:14) — resonates with the rabbinic emphasis on companionship in Torah study. The Mishnah (Avot 1:6) teaches: "Acquire for yourself a friend" (*kneh lekha chaver*), understanding that the life of Torah requires a study partner and companion. The *chavruta* (study partnership) institutionalizes this wisdom.

## The Discipline of Torah Study

The exhortation to submit to Wisdom's discipline (6:18-31) — bearing her yoke and fetters until they become ornaments — parallels the rabbinic concept of *ol Torah* (the yoke of Torah). The Mishnah (Avot 3:5) teaches: "Whoever accepts the yoke of Torah, the yoke of government and worldly care is removed from him."

## Seeking a Rabbi

The counsel to seek the company of elders and attach oneself to a wise teacher (6:34-37) reflects the rabbinic institution of discipleship. Pirkei Avot 1:6 instructs: "Make for yourself a teacher" (*aseh lekha rav*). Ben Sira himself was such a teacher, and his school in Jerusalem was a prototype of the later bet midrash.
