## A Catalogue of Mitzvot

Sirach 7 reads as a practical catalogue of interpersonal obligations that anticipates the rabbinic systematization of *bein adam la-chavero* (commandments between person and person). The chapter addresses duties to family, servants, the community, the priesthood, and the poor — the full range of social relationships that halakhah later codifies.

## Support for the Kohanim

Verses 29-31 command generosity toward priests, reflecting the pre-Maccabean Temple culture in which Ben Sira lived. Jewish tradition maintained the obligations to the priesthood through tithes and priestly gifts, and even after the Temple's destruction, the memory of these obligations shaped Jewish communal ethics and the continued honor accorded to kohanim.

## Bikkur Cholim and Chesed shel Emet

The instructions to visit the sick and mourn with the grieving (7:33-36) correspond to the Jewish mitzvot of *bikkur cholim* (visiting the sick) and *chesed shel emet* (true kindness to the dead). The Talmud (Shabbat 127a) lists these among the acts whose reward is enjoyed in this world and whose principal endures in the world to come.
