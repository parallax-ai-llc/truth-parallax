## The Prohibition of Lashon ha-Ra

Sirach 19's treatment of gossip — "Have you heard something? Let it die with you" (19:10) — addresses the sin of *lashon ha-ra* (evil speech), which Jewish law treats with extraordinary seriousness. The Chofetz Chaim's systematic treatment of speech ethics (*Shemirat ha-Lashon*) builds on the same foundation that Ben Sira establishes: the tongue's power to destroy or heal.

## Tokhechah: The Duty of Reproof

The instruction to confront others directly rather than spread rumors (19:13-17) corresponds to the mitzvah of *tokhechah* (reproof) derived from Leviticus 19:17: "You shall not hate your brother in your heart; you shall surely rebuke your neighbor." Jewish law requires direct, private confrontation before public disclosure.

## Wisdom and Fear of God

The conclusion that true wisdom is inseparable from the fear of the Lord (19:20-30) reiterates the Jewish conviction that *yirat shamayim* (fear of Heaven) is the prerequisite for all genuine knowledge. The Talmud (Shabbat 31a) compares it to the outer chamber that one must enter before reaching the inner chamber of Torah.
