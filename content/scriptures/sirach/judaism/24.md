## The Identification of Torah and Wisdom

Sirach 24 represents the most explicit pre-rabbinic identification of Wisdom with Torah: "All this is the book of the covenant of the Most High God, the law that Moses commanded us" (24:23). This theological move — equating cosmic wisdom with the particular revelation at Sinai — became foundational for rabbinic Judaism. The midrash (Bereishit Rabbah 1:1) teaches that God "looked into the Torah and created the world," making Torah the blueprint of creation.

## Wisdom's Dwelling in Israel

Wisdom's search for a dwelling place and her establishment in Jerusalem (24:8-12) reflects the Jewish theology of *Shekhinah* — God's indwelling presence. The Temple was the site where divine Wisdom took up residence among the people of Israel, and the destruction of the Temple raised the question of where Wisdom now dwells. The rabbinic answer: in the Torah and among those who study it.

## The Inexhaustible Torah

The closing image of Wisdom as an overflowing river (24:25-34) resonates with the rabbinic teaching that the Torah is inexhaustible: "Turn it over and turn it over, for everything is in it" (Avot 5:22). Ben Sira's declaration that he could never exhaust Wisdom's riches anticipates centuries of Torah commentary and interpretation.
