## The Preferential Option for the Poor

Sirach 4:1-10 commands care for the poor with prophetic urgency: do not grieve the hungry, do not anger the desperate, do not turn away from the needy. Catholic social teaching draws on these verses as part of the biblical foundation for the "preferential option for the poor," a principle articulated in modern Catholic social encyclicals from *Rerum Novarum* to *Laudato Si'*.

## Wisdom as Mother

In 4:11-19, Wisdom is personified as a mother who tests and disciplines her children before exalting them. Catholic Marian theology has drawn on the maternal imagery of Wisdom throughout Sirach, seeing in the figure of the wise mother a type of the Blessed Virgin who forms Christ in the faithful.

## Godly Shame and Courage

The chapter's treatment of true and false shame (4:20-31) informs Catholic moral theology's distinction between healthy guilt (which leads to repentance) and scrupulosity (which paralyzes). The counsel to "never be ashamed to confess your sins" (4:26) anticipates the sacrament of reconciliation.
