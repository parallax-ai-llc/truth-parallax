## Prudent Generosity

Sirach 12's counsel to "know to whom you do good" (12:1) represents the wisdom tradition's emphasis on prudent charity. Catholic moral theology does not read this as contradicting the universal call to love but as advocating discernment in how that love is expressed. The Church distinguishes between the obligation to love all people and the prudential judgment about the most effective forms of assistance.

## The Reality of Enmity

Verses 8-18 warn about the duplicity of enemies with striking realism. Catholic spiritual theology acknowledges the reality of spiritual combat and the need for discernment in relationships. The tradition of spiritual direction partly serves this function: helping the faithful distinguish between true and false friends, genuine and counterfeit counsel.

## Charity and Justice Together

Catholic social teaching insists that charity and justice must go together. Sirach 12's realism about human duplicity does not cancel the call to generosity but situates it within a realistic assessment of human nature affected by sin — the Catholic understanding of concupiscence and its effects on social relationships.
