## Wisdom's Self-Revelation

Sirach 24 presents Wisdom speaking in the first person, describing her divine origin, her presence at creation, and her establishment in Israel. Catholic theology reads this chapter christologically and ecclesiologically: Wisdom's dwelling in Israel prefigures the Incarnation, when the eternal Word took flesh and "dwelt among us" (John 1:14).

## Wisdom and Torah, Wisdom and Christ

The pivotal identification of Wisdom with "the book of the covenant of the Most High God, the law that Moses commanded us" (24:23) is read in Catholic theology as pointing beyond Torah to Christ himself, who is "the wisdom of God" (1 Corinthians 1:24). The trajectory from personified Wisdom to incarnate Logos is a foundational Catholic hermeneutical principle.

## Marian Readings

Catholic devotional tradition has applied many of Sirach 24's images to the Blessed Virgin Mary. Wisdom's description of herself as a cedar of Lebanon, a cypress, a palm tree, and a vine (24:13-17) appears in the liturgy for Marian feasts. Mary, as the Seat of Wisdom (*Sedes Sapientiae*), is the one in whom divine Wisdom found its most perfect human dwelling — the one who bore the incarnate Logos in her womb.
