## Wisdom and the Faithful

Sirach 15 opens with the promise that the one who fears the Lord will find Wisdom like a bride and a mother (15:1-6). Catholic theology reads this within the nuptial imagery that runs from the Song of Songs through the Pauline letters: the soul's union with Wisdom prefigures the Church's union with Christ.

## The Catholic Defense of Free Will

Verses 11-20 provide one of the strongest biblical defenses of human free will: "Before each person are life and death, and whichever one chooses will be given" (15:17). The Council of Trent cited this passage explicitly in its defense of free will against what it perceived as the Protestant overemphasis on predestination. Catholic theology affirms that grace does not destroy but perfects nature — including the natural capacity for moral choice.

## Grace and Freedom

Catholic doctrine holds Sirach 15 in balance with the Pauline teaching on grace: human freedom is real, but effective only when cooperating with divine grace. This synergistic understanding — the interplay of human choice and divine enablement — is the foundation of Catholic soteriology and finds scriptural support in this chapter.
