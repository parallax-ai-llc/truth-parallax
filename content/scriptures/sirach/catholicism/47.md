## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 47

Sirach chapter 47, "David, Solomon, and Rehoboam," comprises 25 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

David's establishment of liturgical worship and Solomon's Temple prefigure Catholic liturgy and sacred architecture. The frank assessment of Solomon's fall reinforces the Church's teaching on the universal need for grace and vigilance.

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of the communion of saints and salvation history are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
