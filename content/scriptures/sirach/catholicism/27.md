## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 27

Sirach chapter 27, "Sin, Foolishness, and Retribution," comprises 30 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

Ben Sira's teaching on the testing of character through speech and the certainty of divine retribution informs Catholic moral theology regarding the sins of the tongue — detraction, calumny, and gossip. The Catechism (CCC 2477-2479) echoes these concerns in its treatment of offenses against truth.

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of moral instruction and virtue are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
