## Theological Anthropology

Sirach 17 presents a rich theological anthropology: God created humanity from the earth, clothed them with strength, made them in His image, granted dominion over creation, and endowed them with intellect and moral discernment (17:1-10). Catholic theology finds here a comprehensive account of the *imago Dei* — the image of God in which every human person is created and which confers inalienable dignity.

## The Covenant and Moral Law

Verses 11-14 describe the Sinai revelation, where God showed Israel His glory and gave them the law. Catholic theology connects this to the natural law, which is "inscribed in the heart" of every person (Romans 2:15), and to the revealed law given through Moses. The two are complementary, not contradictory — both express the one eternal law of God.

## The Call to Repentance

The closing appeal to repentance (17:25-32) is used in Catholic penitential practice. The reminder that the dead cannot praise God (17:28) has been interpreted not as denying the afterlife but as urging the urgency of repentance in this life — the same urgency that informs the sacrament of reconciliation and the Catholic teaching on purgatory.
