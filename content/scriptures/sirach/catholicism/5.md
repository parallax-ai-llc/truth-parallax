## Against the Sin of Presumption

Sirach 5:1-8 warns against presuming on God's mercy: "Do not say, 'His mercy is great, He will forgive the multitude of my sins'" (5:6). Catholic moral theology identifies presumption as a sin against hope — the opposite of despair — and cites this passage in its catechetical instruction. The Catechism teaches that presumption involves either counting on one's own capacities or counting on God's mercy without conversion.

## The Governance of the Tongue

Verses 9-15 counsel consistency and truthfulness in speech, warning against being "double-tongued." Catholic moral theology treats the sins of the tongue — gossip, detraction, calumny, and perjury — as violations of the eighth commandment, and Sirach's practical instruction has served as a guide for examination of conscience and confessional practice for centuries.

## The Integration of Mercy and Justice

The chapter holds together what Catholic theology insists must never be separated: God's mercy and God's justice. Forgiveness is real and available, but it demands genuine repentance — not the presumption that cheapens grace by eliminating conversion.
