## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 39

Sirach chapter 39, "The Scribe and Praise of God," comprises 35 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

The portrait of the ideal scribe who studies the Law, prays, and serves the community is seen as prefiguring the Catholic understanding of the theologian's vocation. The hymn of praise (39:12-35) reflects the sapiential tradition that culminates in the Church's liturgical prayer.

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of moral instruction and virtue are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
