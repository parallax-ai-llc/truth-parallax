## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 48

Sirach chapter 48, "Elijah, Elisha, and Hezekiah," comprises 25 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

Elijah's prophetic ministry is read in light of his appearance at the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:3) and his expected return before the Parousia (Malachi 4:5). Catholic tradition venerates Elijah as a model of prophetic zeal and contemplative prayer.

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of the communion of saints and salvation history are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
