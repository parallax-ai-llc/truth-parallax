## A Moral Catechism of Duties

Sirach 7 reads as a catechetical summary of duties spanning the whole of human life. Catholic moral theology has valued this chapter for its systematic treatment of obligations to God, family, servants, the poor, the sick, and the dead. The chapter's comprehensive scope anticipates the structure of Catholic moral manuals that organize ethics by state of life and relationship.

## Support for the Priesthood

Verses 29-31 command generosity toward the priesthood: "With all your soul fear the Lord, and honor His priests" (7:29-31). Catholic ecclesiology cites such passages in support of the ordained ministry and the obligation of the faithful to support the clergy. The distinction between priestly and lay vocations is grounded in this biblical vision of sacred office.

## Care for the Suffering and the Dead

The instruction to visit the sick and remember the dead (7:33-36) resonates deeply with Catholic practice. The corporal works of mercy — visiting the sick and burying the dead — find scriptural support here. The verse "do not neglect the discourse of the sages" (7:35, alternate reading) has also been applied to Catholic educational and intellectual traditions.
