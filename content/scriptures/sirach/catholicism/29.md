## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 29

Sirach chapter 29, "Lending, Almsgiving, and Surety," comprises 28 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

Catholic social teaching draws on passages like these regarding lending, almsgiving, and care for the poor. The principle of the universal destination of goods (CCC 2402-2406) finds a precursor in Ben Sira's insistence that wealth carries social obligations.

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of moral instruction and virtue are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
