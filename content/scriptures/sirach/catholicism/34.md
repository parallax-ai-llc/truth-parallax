## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 34

Sirach chapter 34, "Dreams, Travel, and True Worship," comprises 26 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

The critique of empty dreams and the affirmation of genuine worship support the Catholic distinction between authentic devotion and superstition. The Council of Trent's decrees on the sacrifice of the Mass find a distant echo in Ben Sira's insistence that sacrifice must be accompanied by justice.

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of moral instruction and virtue are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
