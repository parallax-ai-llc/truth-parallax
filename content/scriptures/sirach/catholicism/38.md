## Catholic Interpretation of Sirach 38

Sirach chapter 38, "Physicians, Mourning, and Artisans," comprises 34 verses and holds full canonical authority in the Catholic Church. The Council of Trent (1546) definitively included Sirach in the canon of Sacred Scripture, affirming a tradition of usage stretching back to the earliest centuries. The Nova Vulgata and the New American Bible provide the standard Catholic texts, and Sirach appears frequently in the Church's liturgical readings.

## Doctrinal and Moral Teaching

The chapter's affirmation of physicians (38:1-15) is foundational for Catholic bioethics, supporting the legitimacy of medical care as cooperation with God's healing providence. The dignity of manual labor (38:24-34) anticipates the Church's social teaching on the value of work (*Laborem Exercens*).

## Liturgical and Catechetical Use

The Catechism of the Catholic Church draws on Sirach at multiple points, and this chapter's themes of moral instruction and virtue are well represented in the lectionary. Catholic exegetes such as Patrick Skehan and Alexander Di Lella (*Anchor Bible Commentary on Sirach*) situate these verses within the broader context of Second Temple wisdom theology, emphasizing the integration of Torah observance and experiential wisdom that is characteristic of Ben Sira's thought.
