## Wise Generosity

Sirach 12's counsel to exercise discernment in giving (12:1-7) resonates with the Islamic emphasis on wise charity. While Islam commands generosity (*sadaqah*) to all, it also recognizes that some forms of giving are more beneficial than others. The Quran says: "And do not give the weak-minded your property" (4:5) — an acknowledgment that charity requires prudence.

## Awareness of Enmity

The warning about enemies who feign friendship (12:8-18) corresponds to Quranic teachings about the reality of human enmity. The Quran warns: "O you who believe, do not take as intimates those other than yourselves, for they will not spare you any ruin" (3:118). Islam teaches both the obligation to be just to all people and the wisdom to recognize genuine threats.

## Ihsan with Discernment

Islamic ethics combines *ihsan* (excellence in doing good) with *firasat* (spiritual discernment). Sirach 12 embodies this combination: generosity is commanded, but it must be directed by wisdom. The believer gives from a position of knowledge, not naivete.
