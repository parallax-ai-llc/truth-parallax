## Birr al-Walidayn: Honoring Parents

Sirach 3:1-16 on honoring parents finds a powerful parallel in the Quran, which places filial duty immediately after the worship of God: "Your Lord has decreed that you worship none but Him, and that you be kind to parents" (17:23). The detailed practical counsel — patience with aging parents, bearing with their weakness — mirrors the Quranic instruction: "Lower to them the wing of humility out of mercy" (17:24).

## Tawadu: The Virtue of Humility

The call to humility (3:17-24) resonates with the Islamic concept of *tawadu'* (humility). The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) taught: "No one humbles himself for the sake of Allah but Allah raises him in status" (Sahih Muslim). Both Sirach and Islamic teaching warn against intellectual arrogance and self-sufficiency.

## Sadaqah as Spiritual Purification

The teaching that almsgiving atones for sin (3:30) finds direct parallel in the Quranic concept of *sadaqah* (charitable giving): "Take from their wealth a charity by which you purify them and cause them increase" (Quran 9:103). Islam teaches that charitable giving purifies both the giver's wealth and soul.
