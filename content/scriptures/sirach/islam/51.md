## Islamic Perspective on Sirach 51

Sirach chapter 51, "Ben Sira's Prayer and Hymn of Thanksgiving," contains 30 verses of wisdom teaching. While Sirach is not recognized as a revealed text in Islam, Islamic scholarship engages with it under the broader category of Isra'iliyyat — traditions from Jewish and Christian sources that may illuminate shared moral and theological concerns. The Quran affirms that wisdom (*hikmah*) was granted to many prophets and sages, and Ben Sira's ethical teachings often parallel Quranic moral instruction.

## Parallels with Quranic and Prophetic Teaching

Ben Sira's concluding prayer of gratitude resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *shukr* (gratitude). The Quran connects gratitude with faith: 'If you are grateful, I will surely increase you' (14:7). The pursuit of wisdom echoes the Islamic elevation of *'ilm* (knowledge) as worship.

## Scholarly Assessment

Muslim scholars approach Sirach with the same critical framework applied to all biblical literature: what aligns with the Quran and Sunnah may be accepted, what contradicts them is rejected, and what falls into neither category is neither affirmed nor denied (the principle articulated in hadith narrated by Abu Hurayra). This chapter's emphasis on the legacy of the prophets and righteous leaders resonates with Islamic ethical teaching while remaining subject to the standard of Quranic authority.
