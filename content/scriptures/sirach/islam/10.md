## Kibr: The Sin of Pride

Sirach 10's denunciation of pride as "the beginning of sin" (10:13) resonates with the Islamic condemnation of *kibr* (arrogance). The Prophet taught: "No one who has an atom's weight of pride in his heart will enter Paradise" (Sahih Muslim). In Islamic theology, Iblis (Satan) fell through pride — refusing to bow before Adam — making arrogance the primordial sin.

## God's Sovereignty over Nations

The theme of God overthrowing the proud and exalting the humble (10:14-18) aligns with the Quranic teaching on divine sovereignty: "Say, 'O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty from whom You will'" (3:26). Islam teaches that all human power is delegated and provisional.

## True Honor in God's Sight

The conclusion that those who fear God deserve true honor regardless of status (10:19-31) mirrors the Quranic declaration: "Indeed, the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous of you" (49:13). Neither wealth, lineage, nor social rank determines a person's worth before God — only *taqwa*.
