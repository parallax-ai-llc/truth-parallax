## Guarding Against Temptation

Sirach 9:1-9 warns against sexual temptation, a concern shared by Islamic ethics. The Quran commands: "Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their chastity" (24:30). Islam's ethic of modesty (*haya'*) — in dress, behavior, and social interaction — addresses the same vulnerabilities that Ben Sira identifies, though the specific prescriptions differ.

## Righteous Companionship

The counsel to choose companions wisely (9:10-16) is a major theme in Islamic ethics. The Quran warns: "On that Day, friends will be enemies to one another, except the righteous" (43:67). The Prophet compared a good companion to a perfume seller — one benefits simply by being near them — and a bad companion to a blacksmith's bellows (Sahih al-Bukhari).

## Just Governance

The chapter's closing comments on governance (9:17-18) resonate with the Islamic political theology of just rule (*siyasah shar'iyyah*). The Quran commands: "Indeed, Allah commands you to render trusts to whom they are due and when you judge between people to judge with justice" (4:58). Competent and righteous governance is a religious obligation in Islam.
