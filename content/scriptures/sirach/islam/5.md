## Against Presuming on God's Mercy

Sirach 5:6-7 warns against delaying repentance in presumption of divine mercy. The Quran similarly warns: "Let not the worldly life delude you, and let not the Deceiver deceive you about Allah" (31:33). Islam teaches that while God's mercy is vast, it must not be presumed upon as a license for continued sin. Genuine *tawbah* (repentance) requires sincerity and urgency.

## The Ethics of Speech

Verses 9-15 on the governance of the tongue correspond to extensive Islamic teaching on speech ethics. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) taught: "Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day, let him speak good or remain silent" (Sahih al-Bukhari). The warning against being double-tongued echoes the hadith: "You will find among the worst people on the Day of Resurrection the two-faced person" (Sahih al-Bukhari).

## Sincerity and Integrity

The call for consistency between speech and character resonates with the Islamic ideal of *ikhlas* (sincerity) — the alignment of outward behavior with inner conviction. Hypocrisy (*nifaq*) is among the most condemned sins in the Quran, and Sirach's warning against double-tongued speech targets the same moral failing.
