## Prayer for Protection

Sirach 23's opening prayer for deliverance from sinful desires corresponds to the Islamic practice of seeking God's protection (*isti'adhah*). Muslims regularly pray: "I seek refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan." The recognition that self-mastery requires divine assistance is fundamental to both Sirach's prayer and Islamic spirituality.

## The Gravity of Oaths

The chapter's teaching on oaths (23:7-15) corresponds to the Islamic jurisprudence of oaths (*ayman*). The Quran commands: "Guard your oaths" (5:89) and provides rules for expiation when oaths are broken. Islam, like Sirach, treats the invocation of God's name with extreme seriousness and warns against habitual or frivolous swearing.

## Zina: The Prohibition of Adultery

The adultery passage (23:16-27) resonates with Islam's absolute prohibition of *zina* (adultery/fornication). The Quran commands: "Do not approach unlawful sexual intercourse. Indeed, it is ever an immorality and is evil as a way" (17:32). Sirach's emphasis that God sees what the adulterer tries to hide aligns with the Quranic assurance that "Allah is ever a careful observer" (33:52).
