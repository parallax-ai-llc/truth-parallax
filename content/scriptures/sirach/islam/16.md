## The Certainty of Judgment

Sirach 16's warning that no one is hidden from God's sight (16:17) resonates deeply with Islamic eschatology. The Quran teaches: "Not an atom's weight escapes your Lord in the earth or in the heaven" (10:61). On the Day of Judgment (*Yawm al-Qiyamah*), every deed — hidden or public — will be brought to light. Ben Sira's warning against spiritual complacency echoes this Quranic urgency.

## Lessons from History

The chapter's survey of God's punishments — the flood, Sodom, the Canaanites — parallels the Quranic *qisas* (stories of previous peoples). The Quran repeatedly warns about the fate of peoples who rejected God's prophets: 'Ad, Thamud, the people of Lot, Pharaoh's army. Both Sirach and the Quran use sacred history as moral instruction.

## The Order of Creation

The meditation on creation's order (16:24-30) aligns with the Quranic emphasis on the *ayat* (signs) of God in nature: "Indeed, in the creation of the heavens and the earth and the alternation of the night and the day are signs for those of understanding" (3:190). Nature testifies to divine wisdom and power.
