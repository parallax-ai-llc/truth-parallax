## Islamic Perspective on Sirach 47

Sirach chapter 47, "David, Solomon, and Rehoboam," contains 25 verses of wisdom teaching. While Sirach is not recognized as a revealed text in Islam, Islamic scholarship engages with it under the broader category of Isra'iliyyat — traditions from Jewish and Christian sources that may illuminate shared moral and theological concerns. The Quran affirms that wisdom (*hikmah*) was granted to many prophets and sages, and Ben Sira's ethical teachings often parallel Quranic moral instruction.

## Parallels with Quranic and Prophetic Teaching

David (*Dawud*) and Solomon (*Sulayman*) are major Quranic prophets, but the Islamic tradition categorically rejects the biblical accounts of their sins. David is given the *Zabur* (Psalms), and Solomon is granted dominion over the jinn — without the moral failings attributed to them in Israelite tradition.

## Scholarly Assessment

Muslim scholars approach Sirach with the same critical framework applied to all biblical literature: what aligns with the Quran and Sunnah may be accepted, what contradicts them is rejected, and what falls into neither category is neither affirmed nor denied (the principle articulated in hadith narrated by Abu Hurayra). This chapter's emphasis on the legacy of the prophets and righteous leaders resonates with Islamic ethical teaching while remaining subject to the standard of Quranic authority.
