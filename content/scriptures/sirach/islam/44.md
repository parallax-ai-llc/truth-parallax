## Islamic Perspective on Sirach 44

Sirach chapter 44, "Praise of the Ancestors — Introduction," contains 23 verses of wisdom teaching. While Sirach is not recognized as a revealed text in Islam, Islamic scholarship engages with it under the broader category of Isra'iliyyat — traditions from Jewish and Christian sources that may illuminate shared moral and theological concerns. The Quran affirms that wisdom (*hikmah*) was granted to many prophets and sages, and Ben Sira's ethical teachings often parallel Quranic moral instruction.

## Parallels with Quranic and Prophetic Teaching

The commemoration of past figures resonates with the Quranic *qisas al-anbiya'* (stories of the prophets) tradition. Both traditions use ancestral narratives to teach moral and theological lessons. Al-Tha'labi's *Qisas al-Anbiya'* is the Islamic counterpart to Ben Sira's 'Praise of the Ancestors.'

## Scholarly Assessment

Muslim scholars approach Sirach with the same critical framework applied to all biblical literature: what aligns with the Quran and Sunnah may be accepted, what contradicts them is rejected, and what falls into neither category is neither affirmed nor denied (the principle articulated in hadith narrated by Abu Hurayra). This chapter's emphasis on the legacy of the prophets and righteous leaders resonates with Islamic ethical teaching while remaining subject to the standard of Quranic authority.
