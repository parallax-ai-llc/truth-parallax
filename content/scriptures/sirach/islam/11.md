## Qadr: Divine Decree

Sirach 11's teaching that both prosperity and adversity come from the Lord (11:14) corresponds to the Islamic doctrine of *qadr* (divine decree). Belief in qadr is one of the six articles of faith in Islam. The Muslim is called to accept both good and ill as coming from God, responding with gratitude (*shukr*) in prosperity and patience (*sabr*) in adversity.

## Against Hasty Judgment

The counsel against hasty judgment (11:7-13) resonates with Islamic *adab* (etiquette) in interpersonal relations. The Quran commands: "O you who believe, avoid much suspicion; indeed, some suspicion is sin" (49:12). Both Sirach and the Quran counsel restraint in judging others.

## Zuhd: Detachment from Wealth

The warning against trusting in riches (11:14-28) aligns with the Islamic virtue of *zuhd* (ascetic detachment from worldly things). While Islam does not prohibit the enjoyment of lawful wealth, the Quran warns: "Know that the life of this world is but amusement and diversion and adornment and boasting to one another" (57:20). Sirach's warning that fortune can reverse overnight reinforces the Islamic call to spiritual priorities.
