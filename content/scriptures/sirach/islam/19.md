## The Prohibition of Intoxicants

Sirach 19's warning about wine leading to ruin (19:1) parallels Islam's prohibition of intoxicants (*khamr*): "O you who believe, indeed intoxicants... are but defilement from the work of Satan, so avoid them" (Quran 5:90). Islam goes further than Sirach's warning, prohibiting alcohol entirely rather than merely cautioning against excess.

## Ghibah: The Sin of Backbiting

The instruction to let rumors die (19:10) corresponds to the Islamic prohibition of *ghibah* (backbiting). The Quran likens backbiting to eating the flesh of one's dead brother (49:12) — a vivid image that conveys the horror of this seemingly minor sin. Both Sirach and the Quran treat gossip as a serious moral failing.

## True Wisdom and Fear of God

The chapter's conclusion that true wisdom requires the fear of God (19:20-30) aligns with the Islamic conviction that *'ilm* (knowledge) without *taqwa* (God-consciousness) is dangerous. The Quran warns against those who are clever in wrongdoing, and the Islamic scholarly tradition insists that moral character is the prerequisite for teaching.
