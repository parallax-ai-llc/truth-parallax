## Islamic Perspective on Sirach 26

Sirach chapter 26, "The Good and Bad Wife," contains 29 verses of wisdom teaching. While Sirach is not recognized as a revealed text in Islam, Islamic scholarship engages with it under the broader category of Isra'iliyyat — traditions from Jewish and Christian sources that may illuminate shared moral and theological concerns. The Quran affirms that wisdom (*hikmah*) was granted to many prophets and sages, and Ben Sira's ethical teachings often parallel Quranic moral instruction.

## Parallels with Quranic and Prophetic Teaching

The chapter's teachings on marital relations find broad parallels in Islamic ethics. The Quran (4:19, 30:21) and Prophetic hadith extensively address the mutual rights and duties of spouses. The underlying concern for marital harmony resonates with Islamic teaching on *mu'ashara bil-ma'ruf* (living together in kindness).

## Scholarly Assessment

Muslim scholars approach Sirach with the same critical framework applied to all biblical literature: what aligns with the Quran and Sunnah may be accepted, what contradicts them is rejected, and what falls into neither category is neither affirmed nor denied (the principle articulated in hadith narrated by Abu Hurayra). This chapter's emphasis on practical wisdom and moral conduct resonates with Islamic ethical teaching while remaining subject to the standard of Quranic authority.
