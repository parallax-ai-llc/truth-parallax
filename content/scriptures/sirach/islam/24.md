## Wisdom as Divine Gift

Sirach 24's grand hymn, where Wisdom describes her origin with God and her establishment among Israel, resonates with the Quranic concept of divine guidance sent to specific peoples. Islam affirms that God sent revelation to every nation: "For every community there is a messenger" (Quran 10:47). Wisdom's dwelling in Israel represents, in Islamic perspective, one instance of God's universal pattern of guidance.

## Torah as Revelation

The identification of Wisdom with the Torah (24:23) is acknowledged in Islamic theology as reflecting a genuine moment in salvation history. Islam affirms that the Torah (*Tawrat*) was a true revelation given to Moses (*Musa*), though it holds that the text was subsequently altered. The reverence Ben Sira shows for Torah mirrors the Islamic reverence for the Quran as God's final, preserved revelation.

## The River of Wisdom

The closing image of Wisdom as an inexhaustible river (24:25-34) resonates with the Islamic understanding of divine knowledge as infinite: "Say, 'If the sea were ink for the words of my Lord, the sea would be exhausted before the words of my Lord were exhausted'" (Quran 18:109). God's wisdom exceeds all human capacity to comprehend or record it.
