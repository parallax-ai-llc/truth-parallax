## The Dignity of Human Creation

Sirach 17's account of humanity's creation parallels the Quranic narrative. The Quran teaches: "We have certainly created man in the best of stature" (95:4), and "He taught Adam the names — all of them" (2:31). Both Sirach and the Quran affirm that God endowed humanity with unique dignity, intellect, and moral responsibility among all creation.

## The Covenant and Divine Guidance

The description of God revealing His covenant (17:11-14) resonates with the Islamic understanding of divine guidance through prophets and scriptures. Islam teaches that God sent messengers to every people (Quran 10:47) and that the Torah was a genuine revelation — though Muslims believe it was later altered (*tahrif*). Ben Sira's account of Sinai reflects the same God who sent guidance to all nations.

## Tawbah: The Call to Repentance

The urgent call to repentance (17:25-32) echoes the Quranic summons: "Turn to Allah in repentance, all of you, O believers, that you might succeed" (24:31). Islam emphasizes that the door of *tawbah* remains open as long as a person lives — the same urgency that Ben Sira expresses.
