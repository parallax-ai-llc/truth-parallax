## Comprehensive Social Ethics

Sirach 7's catalogue of duties — to family, community, the poor, and the sick — mirrors Islam's comprehensive ethical vision. The Quran and Sunnah address the full range of human relationships, from domestic life to commerce to governance. The concept of *huquq* (rights and duties) in Islamic jurisprudence systematizes the kind of relational ethics that Ben Sira presents.

## Family Obligations

The chapter's attention to family duties resonates with Islam's strong emphasis on family as the fundamental unit of society. The Quran commands kindness to parents, spouses, and children, and the Prophet said: "The best of you is the one who is best to his family" (Sunan al-Tirmidhi). Ben Sira's household ethics, while reflecting ancient social structures, share this Islamic conviction.

## Visiting the Sick and Honoring the Dead

The instruction to visit the sick and honor the dead (7:33-36) corresponds to well-established Islamic obligations. Visiting the sick (*'iyadat al-marid*) and attending funeral prayers (*salat al-janazah*) are communal duties in Islam. The Prophet identified visiting the sick as one of the five rights of a Muslim over another Muslim (Sahih al-Bukhari).
