## Fleeing from Sin

Sirach 21's command to "flee from sin as from a snake" (21:2) echoes the Islamic imperative to avoid sin and its occasions. The Prophet taught: "That which is lawful is clear and that which is unlawful is clear, and between them are doubtful matters... whoever falls into doubtful matters falls into the unlawful" (Sahih al-Bukhari). Islam counsels avoiding even the vicinity of sin.

## The Wise and the Ignorant

The contrast between the wise and the foolish (21:11-28) mirrors the Quranic distinction between those who know and those who do not: "Are those who know equal to those who do not know?" (39:9). Islam elevates the status of the scholar (*'alim*) and treats ignorance (*jahl*) as a spiritual danger, not merely an intellectual deficiency.

## The Two Paths

The image of the smooth path ending in destruction (21:10) resonates with the Quranic imagery of the *sirat al-mustaqim* (the straight path) that Muslims pray for in every *rak'ah* of prayer. The Fatiha's plea — "Guide us to the straight path" — acknowledges that the way of righteousness requires divine guidance.
