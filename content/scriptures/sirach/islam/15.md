## Wisdom and Obedience

Sirach 15:1-6 promises that Wisdom welcomes the obedient. Islam similarly teaches that true wisdom is the fruit of obedience to God. The Quran links knowledge and practice: "Are those who know equal to those who do not know?" (39:9). In Islam, knowledge without action is incomplete, just as Ben Sira links wisdom to Torah observance.

## Ikhtiyar: Human Free Will

Sirach 15:11-20 — "Before each person are life and death; whichever one chooses will be given" — resonates with the Ash'ari and Maturidi discussions of human freedom in Islamic theology. While Islam affirms divine sovereignty (*qadr*), it also insists on human responsibility (*taklif*). The Quran declares: "Whoever wills, let him believe; and whoever wills, let him disbelieve" (18:29) — a verse that, like Sirach 15:17, places moral choice squarely on the individual.

## God Does Not Command Sin

Sirach's insistence that God does not cause sin (15:11-12) aligns with the Quranic declaration: "Indeed, Allah does not order immorality" (7:28). Both traditions reject the blasphemous excuse of attributing one's sin to God, insisting on the reality of human moral agency.
