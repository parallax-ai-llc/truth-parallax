## Overview

The Heart Sutra's teaching on emptiness invites comparison with apophatic traditions. Its systematic negation parallels the via negativa of Christian mysticism, the neti neti of the Upanishads, and the Daoist teaching that the Dao that can be spoken is not the eternal Dao.

## Cross-Traditional Parallels

The declaration "form is emptiness" has been compared to the coincidentia oppositorum of Nicholas of Cusa and Meister Eckhart's "Godhead beyond God." The sutra's insistence that ultimate reality transcends all categories resonates across mystical traditions.

## Distinctive Features

Unlike the Upanishads, which negate attributes to reveal Brahman, the Heart Sutra's emptiness is itself empty. This "emptiness of emptiness" distinguishes Buddhist understanding from substantialist mysticism and represents a unique philosophical contribution.
