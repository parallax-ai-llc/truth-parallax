These 16 verses invite rich comparative study with apophatic traditions. The method of systematic negation parallels the via negativa of Pseudo-Dionysius and Meister Eckhart, where God is approached by denying all attributes. The Upanishadic neti neti similarly strips away predication to reveal the unconditioned Brahman.

However, the Heart Sutra's emptiness differs fundamentally. The Upanishadic negation reveals a positive ultimate reality, and the Christian apophatic tradition affirms a God beyond names. The Heart Sutra's emptiness is not a hidden positive reality but the absence of inherent existence in all phenomena, and emptiness itself is empty.

The concluding mantra finds echoes worldwide: in the Sufi concept of fana, the Daoist wu wei, and the Jewish mystical tradition of devekut. These parallels suggest a shared human capacity for transcending conceptual categories, even as traditions differ on what lies beyond.
