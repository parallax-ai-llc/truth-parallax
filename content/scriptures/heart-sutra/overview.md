## Overview

The Heart Sutra (Prajnaparamita Hridaya) is the shortest and most widely recited text of the Prajnaparamita (Perfection of Wisdom) literature. In approximately 260 Chinese characters or 16 verses, it distills the essence of the vast Prajnaparamita corpus into a concentrated teaching on emptiness (sunyata). The sutra presents Avalokiteshvara Bodhisattva teaching Shariputra that all phenomena are empty of inherent existence.

## Structure

The Heart Sutra follows a concise structure:

1. **Setting**: Avalokiteshvara practicing deep Prajnaparamita
2. **Core teaching**: "Form is emptiness, emptiness is form"
3. **Systematic negation**: All Buddhist categories shown to be empty
4. **Result**: The bodhisattva dwells without fear, far from delusion
5. **Mantra**: "Gate gate paragate parasamgate bodhi svaha"

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Heart Sutra is chanted daily in Mahayana and Zen monasteries across East Asia, studied philosophically in Tibetan monastic universities, and examined by academic scholars as a key text for understanding Mahayana thought.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Sunyata | All phenomena are empty of inherent, independent existence |
| Form and Emptiness | Conventional reality and emptiness are inseparable |
| Prajnaparamita | The perfection of wisdom that sees through all conceptual elaboration |
| Non-duality | Transcendence of all dualistic categories |
| Liberation | Freedom from fear through the realization of emptiness |
