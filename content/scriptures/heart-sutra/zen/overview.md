## Overview

The Heart Sutra is arguably the single most important text in the Zen tradition. Recited daily in virtually every Zen monastery, it serves as liturgical centerpiece and philosophical distillation of Zen's vision.

## Zen Interpretation

Zen masters read the Heart Sutra as direct pointing to reality. "Form is emptiness" is not an intellectual proposition but a description of what is perceived in awakening (satori/kensho). The systematic negation is embraced as ultimate freedom from attachment to any fixed view.

## Liturgical Practice

In Japanese Zen, the Hannya Shingyo is chanted in Sino-Japanese pronunciation. The act of chanting itself is practice, creating a contemplative experience embodying the unity of form and emptiness.
