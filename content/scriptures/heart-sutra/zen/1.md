In Zen practice, these 16 verses are enacted as direct experience. The Heart Sutra is chanted daily, its rhythmic recitation creating concentrated awareness that embodies the emptiness it describes. When Avalokiteshvara perceives that the five skandhas are empty, Zen understands this as direct seeing that occurs when discursive thought drops away.

The radical negations are embraced as the cutting sword of prajna severing attachment to fixed views. A Zen master hearing "no suffering, no origin, no cessation, no path" might respond: "Now you are free to truly practice." The negation does not eliminate these realities but liberates practitioners from clinging to them as solid entities.

The concluding mantra, chanted with full energy, is the sound of awakening itself. "Gone, gone, gone beyond" expresses the movement from conceptual understanding to direct realization that is Zen's heart.
