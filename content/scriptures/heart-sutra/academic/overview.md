## Overview

The Heart Sutra is one of the most intensely studied texts in academic Buddhist scholarship. Jan Nattier's 1992 article arguing Chinese composition and subsequent back-translation into Sanskrit generated vigorous debate.

## Textual Criticism

Scholarly analysis identifies the core passage as drawn from the Large Prajnaparamita Sutra, with the framing narrative and mantra added later. The shorter and longer recensions continue to be compared using manuscript evidence from Dunhuang and Gilgit.

## Philosophical Analysis

Scholars such as Edward Conze, Donald Lopez, and Kazuaki Tanahashi have produced influential studies illuminating the sutra's treatment of emptiness and its apparent paradoxes.
