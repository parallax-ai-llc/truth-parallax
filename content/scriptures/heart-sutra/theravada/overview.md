## Overview

The Heart Sutra occupies an ambiguous position within the Theravada tradition. As a Mahayana text, it is not part of the Pali canon. However, its central teaching on emptiness resonates with the Theravada doctrine of non-self (anatta).

## Theravada Engagement

Some Theravada scholars engage with the Heart Sutra as a concise expression of insights also found in the Pali Abhidhamma. The declaration that "form is emptiness" can be read as an intensification of the teaching that all formations are impermanent and without self.

## Contemporary Reception

Increased Theravada-Mahayana dialogue has led some Theravada practitioners to study the Heart Sutra with appreciation, even without according it canonical status. Its brevity and profundity give it cross-traditional appeal.
