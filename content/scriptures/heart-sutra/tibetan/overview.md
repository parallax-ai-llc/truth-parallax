## Overview

The Heart Sutra, known as Sherab Nyingpo (Essence of Wisdom), holds great importance in Tibetan Buddhism. The tradition preserves extensive commentarial literature by Kamalashila, Vimalamitra, Atisha, and numerous Tibetan masters.

## Philosophical Analysis

Tibetan scholars analyze the sutra through Madhyamaka philosophy, distinguishing explicit teaching on emptiness from implicit teaching on the stages of the path. The systematic negations refute inherent existence while the structure maps onto the five paths and ten bodhisattva grounds.

## Ritual and Protective Use

The Heart Sutra is recited as a protective text. The concluding mantra is used in tantric practice, and the sutra is recited to avert obstacles and establish auspicious conditions for spiritual practice.
