Tibetan Buddhism approaches these 16 verses with philosophical rigor and devotional reverence. The Heart Sutra is analyzed through the two truths framework: conventional appearances and ultimate emptiness. Avalokiteshvara's teaching reveals these truths as inseparable.

Tibetan commentators map the systematic negations onto the five paths and the progressive realization of emptiness at each stage. The negation of the aggregates corresponds to insight on the path of seeing, while negation of the Noble Truths points to non-dual realization of the highest bodhisattva grounds. Tsongkhapa's interpretation emphasizes that the sutra teaches emptiness of inherent existence, not nonexistence of conventional phenomena.

The Heart Sutra is also a powerful protective text in Tibetan ritual. Its recitation pacifies obstacles and creates merit. The combination of philosophical depth and ritual efficacy makes it one of the most versatile texts in the Tibetan repertoire.
