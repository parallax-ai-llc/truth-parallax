## Overview

Mahayana Buddhism engages with the Majjhima Nikaya through its Chinese parallel, the Madhyama Agama, and recognizes the collection as preserving foundational teachings upon which the Mahayana philosophical tradition builds. Suttas on emptiness, non-self, and the analysis of experience provide the doctrinal ground for later Madhyamaka and Yogachara developments.

## Key Themes

Mahayana scholars identify in the Majjhima Nikaya early expressions of ideas that later Mahayana philosophy develops. The emphasis on non-self, the analysis of experience as empty of permanent substance, and the Buddha's use of skillful means all anticipate Mahayana themes.

## Relevance to Mahayana

The Majjhima Nikaya's meditation instructions and ethical teachings remain foundational for Mahayana practice, complementing the specifically Mahayana emphasis on compassion and the bodhisattva path.
