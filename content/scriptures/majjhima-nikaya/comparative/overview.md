## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Majjhima Nikaya provides rich material for cross-traditional analysis of meditation, ethics, and philosophy. The collection's detailed treatment of contemplative practice invites comparison with meditation traditions worldwide.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Meditation Compared

The Majjhima Nikaya's meditation instructions can be compared with Hindu yoga (Patanjali's Yoga Sutras), Christian contemplative prayer, Sufi dhikr, and Jewish meditation, revealing both shared contemplative experiences and distinctive Buddhist approaches.

### Philosophical Dialogue

The collection's philosophical discourses on non-self, dependent origination, and emptiness invite comparison with Advaita Vedanta, process philosophy, and phenomenological traditions.

## Comparative Framework

| Practice | Buddhism (MN) | Hinduism | Christianity |
|----------|--------------|----------|-------------|
| Meditation | Satipatthana | Dhyana yoga | Contemplative prayer |
| Self-knowledge | Non-self analysis | Self-inquiry | Examination of conscience |
| Ethics | Five precepts | Yamas/niyamas | Ten commandments |
