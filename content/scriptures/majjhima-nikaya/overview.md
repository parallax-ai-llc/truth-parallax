## Overview

The Majjhima Nikaya ("Collection of Middle Length Discourses") is the second of the five nikayas of the Sutta Pitaka in the Pali Canon. Containing 152 suttas of moderate length, it is widely regarded as the single most important collection for understanding the Buddha's teachings on meditation, philosophy, and the path to liberation. The discourses combine doctrinal precision with practical guidance in a format accessible to serious practitioners.

## Structure

The 152 suttas are organized into 15 section groups (pannasas and vaggas):

1. **Mulapannasa 1**: Root discourses on right view and the foundations of practice
2. **Mulapannasa 2**: The simile of the cloth, the heartwood, and other foundational teachings
3. **Mulapannasa 3**: The lion's roar discourses and debates with other ascetics
4. **Mulapannasa 4**: The paired discourses on various doctrinal topics
5. **Mulapannasa 5**: The shorter and longer discourses on the destruction of craving
6. **Majjhimapannasa 1**: Discourses to householders and lay followers
7. **Majjhimapannasa 2**: Discourses on monks, conduct, and meditation
8. **Majjhimapannasa 3**: Discourses on wanderers and other ascetics
9. **Majjhimapannasa 4**: Discourses on kings, Brahmins, and debate
10. **Majjhimapannasa 5**: Advanced teachings on meditation and insight
11. **Uparipannasa 1**: Discourses on the aggregates and elements
12. **Uparipannasa 2**: Discourses on emptiness and non-self
13. **Uparipannasa 3**: Discourses on the analysis of action and rebirth
14. **Uparipannasa 4**: Discourses on the stages of meditation
15. **Uparipannasa 5**: Final discourses and recollections

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Majjhima Nikaya has extensive parallels in the Chinese Madhyama Agama and other early Buddhist collections. Key suttas such as the Satipatthana Sutta (MN 10) and the Anapanasati Sutta (MN 118) are foundational across all Buddhist traditions.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Meditation | Detailed instructions for mindfulness, concentration, and insight |
| Non-Self | The analysis of experience as five aggregates without a permanent self |
| Dependent Origination | How suffering arises through conditions and ceases through their removal |
| Right View | The correct understanding that underlies the entire path |
| The Middle Way | Avoiding extremes of self-indulgence and self-mortification |
