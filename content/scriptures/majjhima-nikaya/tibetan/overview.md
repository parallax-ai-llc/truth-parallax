## Overview

Tibetan Buddhism engages with the Majjhima Nikaya's teachings as part of the foundational sutra vehicle upon which the complete Tibetan Buddhist path is built. The collection's analytical approach to meditation and doctrine provides the basis for the lam-rim (stages of the path) tradition.

## Key Themes

Tibetan scholars value the Majjhima Nikaya's systematic treatment of meditation stages, its analysis of mental factors, and its detailed descriptions of the path from ordinary consciousness to awakening. These teachings inform the preliminary analytical meditations (vipassana) that precede shamatha and tantric practices.

## Relevance to Tibetan Study

The Majjhima Nikaya's discourses provide textual support for key concepts in Tibetan Buddhist education, including the analysis of afflictive emotions, the cultivation of right view, and the progression through the stages of meditation.
