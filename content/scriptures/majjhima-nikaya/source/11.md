---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 11: Cula-Malunkya Sutta (The Shorter Discourse to Malunkyaputta on Sense Restraint)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi. There he addressed the venerable Malunkyaputta: "Malunkyaputta, what do you think? Those forms cognizable by the eye that you have not seen, that you never saw before, that you do not see now, and that you have no expectation of seeing—do you have any desire or lust or affection for them?"

**2** "No, Lord." "Those sounds cognizable by the ear... those odours... those tastes... those tangibles... those mental objects that you have not cognized—do you have any desire or lust for them?" "No, Lord."

**3** "Then, Malunkyaputta, regarding things seen, heard, sensed, and cognized: in the seen there will be merely the seen; in the heard, merely the heard; in the sensed, merely the sensed; in the cognized, merely the cognized."

**4** "When for you, in the seen there is merely the seen, you will not be 'with that.' When you are not 'with that,' you will not be 'in that.' When you are not 'in that,' you will be neither here nor there nor in between. This itself is the end of suffering."

**5** The venerable Malunkyaputta, being instructed by the Blessed One, rose from his seat and went to a secluded place. He practised as instructed. Before long he attained Arahantship."
