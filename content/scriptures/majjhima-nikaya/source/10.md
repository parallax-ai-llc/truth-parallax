---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 10: Ratthapala Sutta (On Ratthapala)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was travelling in the Kuru country with a large company of monks, and he came to a Kuru town called Thullakotthita. The Kuru brahman householders heard of his arrival and went to him.

**2** Among them was the clansman Ratthapala, who heard the Blessed One's Dhamma teaching and was deeply moved. He asked the Blessed One's permission to go forth from home to homelessness.

**3** The Blessed One said: "Do you have your parents' permission?" "No, Lord." "The Tathagatas do not give the going forth to one whose parents have not given permission."

**4** Ratthapala went home and told his parents: "I wish to go forth." They refused, saying: "You are our only son." But Ratthapala lay on the ground and said: "Right here I will either die or go forth." For seven days he lay without eating.

**5** His friends pleaded with his parents, and finally they gave permission, saying: "But when you have gone forth, come back to visit us."

**6** After going forth and attaining Arahantship, the venerable Ratthapala returned to visit his parents. His father offered him great wealth to return to the household life. Ratthapala refused.

**7** The king asked him: "Ratthapala, there are four reasons people go forth: decline through aging, decline through sickness, loss of wealth, and loss of relatives. None of these applies to you. Why did you go forth?"

**8** Ratthapala said: "Great king, I went forth because the Blessed One taught four summaries of the Dhamma. The first: 'The world is unstable and swept away.' The second: 'The world has no shelter and no protector.' The third: 'The world has nothing of its own; one has to leave everything and pass on.' The fourth: 'The world is deficient and insatiable, a slave to craving.'"

**9** "Great king, you rule this kingdom, but can you say: 'Let no aging come to me, let no sickness come, let no death come'? You cannot. This is what is meant by the world being unstable."

**10** "And who is your protector? Can your army protect you from aging and death? No. Therefore the world has no protector. And when you die, will your wealth go with you? No. You will leave everything behind. Therefore the world has nothing of its own."
