---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 13: Vitakkasanthana Sutta (The Removal of Distracting Thoughts)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 13
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi. He addressed the monks: "When a monk is pursuing the higher mind, from time to time he should attend to five signs."

**2** "When evil unwholesome thoughts connected with desire, hate, and delusion arise while he is attending to some sign, he should attend to some other sign connected with what is wholesome. Just as a skilled carpenter drives out a coarse peg by means of a fine one, so when he attends to another sign connected with what is wholesome, the evil thoughts are abandoned."

**3** "If evil thoughts still arise, he should examine the danger in those thoughts: 'These thoughts are unwholesome, blameworthy, and result in suffering.' Just as a young woman or man would be horrified if the carcass of a dead snake or dog were hung around their neck, so he should see the danger."

**4** "If evil thoughts still arise, he should try to forget those thoughts and not give attention to them. Just as a man who does not wish to see forms that come within his range of vision would close his eyes or look away."

**5** "If evil thoughts still arise, he should attend to the stilling of the thought-formation of those thoughts. Just as a man walking fast might consider, 'Why am I walking fast? Let me walk slowly.' Then, 'Why am I walking? Let me stand.' Then, 'Why am I standing? Let me sit.' Then, 'Why am I sitting? Let me lie down.'"

**6** "If evil thoughts still arise, he should, with teeth clenched and tongue pressed against the palate, beat down, constrain, and crush mind with mind. Just as a strong man might seize a weaker man by the head and beat him down."
