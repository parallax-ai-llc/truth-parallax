---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 15: Bahudhatuka Sutta (The Many Kinds of Elements)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Thus have I heard. The Blessed One said: "Monks, whatever fears arise, all arise on account of the fool, not the wise person. Whatever calamities arise, all arise on account of the fool, not the wise person."

**2** "Just as a fire that starts in a hut of reeds or grass burns even a house with a peaked roof, plastered inside and out—so whatever fears and calamities arise, all arise on account of the fool."

**3** "The fool brings peril, the wise person brings no peril. The fool brings calamity, the wise person brings no calamity. No peril comes from the wise person, no calamity comes from the wise person."

**4** "Therefore, monks, you should train yourselves thus: 'We will be wise persons, we will be inquirers.'"

**5** "Monks, when a monk knows the elements, knows the bases, knows dependent origination, and knows what is possible and what is impossible—that monk is called wise."

**6** "And what elements does he know? The eighteen elements: the eye element, visible form element, eye-consciousness element; the ear element, sound element, ear-consciousness element; the nose, tongue, body, and mind elements with their respective objects and consciousnesses."

**7** "He also knows the six elements: the earth element, water element, fire element, air element, space element, and consciousness element."

**8** "He also knows the three elements: the sense-desire element, the form element, and the formless element. He knows the two elements: the conditioned element and the unconditioned element."

**9** "And what does he know regarding what is possible and what is impossible? He knows that it is impossible for a person possessing right view to regard any formation as permanent, as pleasurable, or any phenomenon as self."

**10** "He knows that it is impossible for two Arahants, fully enlightened Buddhas, to arise simultaneously in one world-system. He knows that it is possible for one fully enlightened Buddha to arise in a single world-system."
