---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 14: Mahacattarisaka Sutta (The Great Forty)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Thus have I heard. The Blessed One said: "Monks, I will teach you noble right concentration with its supports and its requisites. What is noble right concentration with its supports? Right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness—these seven factors are the supports and requisites of right concentration."

**2** "Right view comes first. And what is right view? Knowledge of suffering, knowledge of the origin of suffering, knowledge of the cessation of suffering, and knowledge of the way leading to the cessation of suffering."

**3** "And what is wrong view? 'There is nothing given, nothing offered, nothing sacrificed. There is no fruit or result of good and bad actions. There is no this world, no other world.' This is wrong view."

**4** "Monks, there are two kinds of right view: right view that is affected by taints, partaking of merit, ripening in the acquisitions; and right view that is noble, taintless, supramundane, a factor of the path."

**5** "The right view affected by taints is this: 'There is what is given, there is what is offered, there are fruits and results of good and bad actions, there is this world and the next.' The right view that is noble and taintless is the wisdom, the faculty of wisdom, the power of wisdom, the investigation-of-phenomena factor of enlightenment, the path factor of right view."

**6** "When one makes an effort to abandon wrong view and to enter upon right view, that is one's right effort. When one mindfully abandons wrong view and enters upon right view, that is one's right mindfulness. Thus these three states run and circle around right view."
