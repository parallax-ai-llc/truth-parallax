---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 6: Culamalunkya Sutta (The Shorter Discourse to Malunkyaputta)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi. Then the venerable Malunkyaputta, while alone in meditation, thought: "These speculative views have been left undeclared by the Blessed One: whether the world is eternal or not, whether the world is finite or infinite, whether the soul and body are the same or different, whether the Tathagata exists after death or not."

**2** "If the Blessed One does not declare these to me, I will abandon the training and return to the lower life."

**3** He went to the Blessed One and stated his concern. The Blessed One said: "Malunkyaputta, did I ever say to you, 'Come, live the holy life under me, and I will declare these things to you?'" "No, Lord."

**4** "Suppose a man were wounded by a poisoned arrow, and his friends brought a surgeon. But the man said: 'I will not let the surgeon pull out this arrow until I know whether the man who wounded me was tall or short, dark or fair, from this village or that.'"

**5** "That man would die, Malunkyaputta, without ever learning those things. In the same way, if anyone should say, 'I will not live the holy life under the Blessed One until he declares these things to me,' that person would die with those things still undeclared."

**6** "The holy life does not depend on the view that the world is eternal or not eternal. Whether or not the world is eternal, there is still birth, aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair, the destruction of which I teach here and now."

**7** "Therefore, Malunkyaputta, bear in mind what I have declared and what I have not declared. And what have I declared? 'This is suffering'—I have declared. 'This is the origin of suffering'—I have declared. 'This is the cessation of suffering'—I have declared. 'This is the way leading to the cessation of suffering'—I have declared."

**8** "And why have I declared this? Because it is beneficial, it pertains to the fundamentals of the holy life, it leads to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, to Nibbana. That is why I have declared it."
