---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 5: Kayagatasati Sutta (Mindfulness of the Body)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Thus have I heard. The Blessed One said: "Monks, there is one quality which, when developed and cultivated, leads to a great sense of urgency, to great benefit, to great security from bondage, to mindfulness and clear comprehension, to the attainment of knowledge and vision, to a pleasant abiding in this very life, to the realization of the fruit of knowledge and liberation. What one quality? Mindfulness directed to the body."

**2** "And how is mindfulness of the body developed? Here a monk, gone to the forest, sits down and, having set up mindfulness before him, breathes in and breathes out mindfully."

**3** "Again, when walking, a monk knows 'I am walking.' When standing, he knows 'I am standing.' In whatever posture the body is disposed, he knows it."

**4** "Again, a monk is one who acts with clear comprehension when going forward and returning, when looking ahead and looking away, when bending and stretching his limbs, when wearing his robes and carrying his bowl, when eating, drinking, chewing, and tasting."

**5** "Again, a monk reviews this body from the soles of the feet upward and from the crown of the head downward, enclosed in skin and full of many kinds of impurities."

**6** "Again, a monk reviews this body as consisting of elements: 'In this body there are the earth element, the water element, the fire element, and the air element.' Just as a skilled butcher, having slaughtered a cow, were to sit at a crossroads with the pieces of the cow, so a monk reviews this body as elements."

**7** "Again, as though he were to see a corpse thrown aside in a charnel ground—one dead for one day, or two days, or three days, bloated, livid, and oozing—he compares this body with it: 'This body too is of the same nature, it will become like that.'"

**8** "Monks, when mindfulness of the body has been developed and cultivated in this way, whatever wholesome states pertain to higher knowledge, they are included in it."

**9** "Just as one who encompasses the great ocean with his mind includes all the streams that flow into it, so whoever has developed mindfulness of the body includes all wholesome states."

**10** "Monks, one thing, when developed, leads to the abandoning of lust, of hatred, of delusion: mindfulness directed to the body."
