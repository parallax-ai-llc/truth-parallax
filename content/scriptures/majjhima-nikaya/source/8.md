---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 8: Cakkavattisihanada Sutta (The Discourse on the Lion's Roar of the Wheel-Turning Monarch)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 8
---

**1** "Monks, be islands unto yourselves, be your own refuge, with no other refuge; let the Dhamma be your island, let the Dhamma be your refuge, with no other refuge."

**2** "In the past, monks, there was a wheel-turning monarch named Dalhanemi, a righteous and law-abiding king. He ruled with righteousness by the wheel-treasure that appeared to him."

**3** "And the wheel-treasure, monks, when it appeared, rolled to the east, and the great king followed it with his fourfold army. And wherever the wheel-treasure stopped, the king set up his camp."

**4** "The kings of the eastern region came to King Dalhanemi and said: 'Come, great king! Welcome, great king! We are at your service. Rule over us.' The king said: 'Do not kill. Do not take what is not given. Do not commit sexual misconduct. Do not tell lies. Do not drink intoxicants. And eat moderately.'"

**5** "When the king's son took over the kingship, the wheel-treasure disappeared. The new king grieved. He went to the royal sage and asked: 'How can I make the wheel-treasure appear?' The sage said: 'Rule by righteousness. Provide protection for all beings. Let no wrongdoing prevail.'"

**6** "But the king did not give wealth to the needy, and poverty spread. With poverty, stealing spread. With stealing, violence spread. With violence, killing spread. With killing, the human lifespan decreased."

**7** "As the lifespan decreased, moral decline increased. People became more and more immoral until the human lifespan fell to ten years. At that time, the world was plunged into a seven-day period of slaughter."

**8** "Then some people, having taken shelter, thought: 'Let us stop killing.' They came out and resolved to practise virtue. Their lifespan increased. Gradually, as virtue increased, the human lifespan rose again."

**9** "When the lifespan reaches eighty thousand years, a Buddha named Metteyya will arise in the world. He will teach the Dhamma, lovely in the beginning, lovely in the middle, lovely in the end."

**10** "Therefore, monks, dwell with yourselves as your island, with the Dhamma as your island. Walk in the way of righteousness. Guard yourselves well."
