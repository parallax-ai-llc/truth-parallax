---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 7: Culasuññata Sutta (The Shorter Discourse on Emptiness)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu. Ananda asked: "Lord, on one occasion the Blessed One said, 'I often dwell in emptiness.' Did I hear this correctly?"

**2** The Blessed One said: "Certainly, Ananda. I often dwell in emptiness. Here, a monk, not attending to the perception of village, not attending to the perception of people, attends to the singleness dependent on the perception of forest."

**3** "His mind enters into the perception of forest and acquires confidence, steadiness, and resolution. He understands: 'Whatever disturbances might exist dependent on the perception of village, dependent on the perception of people, those are not present. There is present only this amount of disturbance, namely, the singleness dependent on the perception of forest.'"

**4** "He understands: 'This field of perception is void of the perception of village, void of the perception of people. There is only this non-voidness: the singleness dependent on the perception of forest.'"

**5** "Whatever is not there, he regards as void with regard to it. Whatever remains, he understands: 'This is present.' Thus, Ananda, this is his genuine, undistorted, pure descent into emptiness."

**6** "Again, not attending to the perception of forest, not attending to the perception of earth, a monk attends to the singleness dependent on the perception of the base of infinite space."

**7** "Again, not attending to the base of infinite space, he attends to the base of infinite consciousness. Then to the base of nothingness. Then to the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception."

**8** "Then, not attending to the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, he attends to the signless concentration of mind. His mind enters into the signless concentration and acquires confidence. He understands: 'This signless concentration of mind is conditioned and volitionally produced. But whatever is conditioned and volitionally produced is impermanent, subject to cessation.'"

**9** "When he knows and sees in this way, his mind is liberated from the taint of sensual desire, from the taint of becoming, and from the taint of ignorance."

**10** "He understands: 'This is void of the taint of sensual desire, of becoming, of ignorance. There is present only this non-voidness: that which is dependent on this body with its six sense bases, conditioned by life.' This is his genuine, undistorted, pure descent into emptiness—the supreme, unsurpassed emptiness."
