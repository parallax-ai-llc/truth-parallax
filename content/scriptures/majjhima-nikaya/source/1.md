---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 1: Mulapariyaya Sutta (The Root of All Things)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Ukkattha, in the Subhaga Grove, at the root of a royal sala tree. There he addressed the monks: "Monks, I will teach you the root of all things. Listen and attend carefully."

**2** "Here, monks, an untaught ordinary person, who has no regard for noble ones, who is unskilled in their Dhamma, perceives earth as earth. Having perceived earth as earth, he conceives himself as earth, he conceives himself in earth, he conceives himself apart from earth, he conceives earth to be 'mine,' he delights in earth. Why is that? Because he has not fully understood it, I say."

**3** "He perceives water as water, fire as fire, air as air. He perceives beings, devas, Pajapati, Brahma, the gods of streaming radiance, the gods of refulgent glory, the gods of great fruit, the Overlord, the base of infinite space, the base of infinite consciousness, the base of nothingness, the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, the seen, the heard, the sensed, the cognized, unity, diversity, all, Nibbana—he conceives himself in relation to each of these."

**4** "But a monk who is an Arahant, with taints destroyed, who has lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, reached the true goal—he directly knows earth as earth. Having directly known earth as earth, he does not conceive himself as earth, does not conceive himself in earth, does not delight in earth. Why? Because he has fully understood it."

**5** "A Tathagata, an Arahant, a Perfectly Enlightened One, directly knows earth as earth. Having directly known earth as earth, he does not conceive himself as earth. Why? Because he has fully understood it. And also because the Tathagata has understood that delight is the root of suffering, that with existence there is birth, and that for what has come to be there is aging and death."

**6** "Therefore, monks, through the complete destruction of craving, the Tathagata has attained supreme full enlightenment."
