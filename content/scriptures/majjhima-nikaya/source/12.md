---
title: "Majjhima Nikaya - Chapter 12: Culagopalaka Sutta (The Shorter Discourse on the Cowherd)"
scripture: "majjhima-nikaya"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Vajjians at Ukkacela, on the bank of the river Ganges. There the Blessed One addressed the monks:

**2** "Monks, once upon a time, in the land of Magadha, a foolish cowherd, in the last month of the rains, without examining the near shore or the far shore, drove his cattle across the Ganges at a spot that had no ford. The cattle became confused in midstream, and they met with calamity and disaster."

**3** "In the same way, monks, those ascetics and brahmans who are unskilled in this world and the other world, in Mara's realm and what is outside Mara's realm, in the realm of Death and what is outside the realm of Death—whoever thinks they should listen to those ascetics and brahmans, that will lead to their harm and suffering for a long time."

**4** "But, monks, a wise cowherd, in the last month of the rains, examining the near shore and the far shore, drove his cattle across the Ganges at a spot with a ford. He drove the bulls and the senior oxen first, and they cut across the current. Then the trained cattle, then the young steers and heifers. Then the calves, who swam across following their mothers' lowing."

**5** "In the same way, those monks who are Arahants, with taints destroyed—they have crossed the flood. Those who have abandoned the five lower fetters—they too are crossing. Those who are stream-enterers—they too are crossing. Those who are Dhamma-followers and faith-followers—they too are crossing."

**6** "Even the calves, following their mothers' lowing, cross the flood. In the same way, those who have nothing more than faith in me, nothing more than love for me, all of them are destined for heaven."
