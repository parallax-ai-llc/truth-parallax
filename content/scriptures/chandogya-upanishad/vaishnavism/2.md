Chapter 2 elaborates the Saman chant's structure and its cosmic correspondences, teaching that every element of the liturgical performance mirrors a dimension of Vishnu's creation. The fivefold and sevenfold Saman meditations link the ritual chant to natural phenomena — rain, seasons, animals, the human body — revealing Vishnu's presence in the entire fabric of existence. For Vaishnavas, this is not mere symbolism but a real demonstration that all of creation is Vishnu's body (*vishvarupa*).

The chapter's progressive identifications — Saman with earth and sky, with speech and breath, with sun and moon — illustrate the Vaishnava principle that the Lord's glory is manifest in every aspect of nature. The detailed ritual instructions serve as a form of worship (*archana*) in which the devotee recognizes and honors Vishnu's presence in every dimension of experience, from the cosmic to the intimate.
