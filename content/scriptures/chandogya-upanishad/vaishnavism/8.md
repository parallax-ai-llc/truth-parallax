Chapter 8 begins with the teaching of the "small space" (*dahara*) within the heart, which contains within itself the entire cosmos. Vaishnava interpreters identify this inner space with the dwelling place of Vishnu as the inner controller (*antaryamin*). The Lord resides in the lotus of the heart, and the meditator who enters this space encounters not emptiness but the fullness of Vishnu's presence. The teaching that this space is as vast as the external cosmos (8.1) reveals that Vishnu's presence within the devotee is as infinite as His cosmic sovereignty.

The chapter's dialogue between Prajapati and Indra about the nature of the true self progresses through four stages — the waking self, the dreaming self, the self in deep sleep, and finally the transcendent self. Vaishnavas read this as Prajapati guiding Indra toward recognition of Vishnu as the true self of all selves. The final teaching that the self is "free from evil, free from old age, free from death" (8.7) describes the liberated soul dwelling eternally in Vishnu's presence.
