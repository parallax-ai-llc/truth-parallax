The Chandogya Upanishad opens with an extended meditation on the Udgitha — the syllable Om as chanted in the Sama Veda rituals. Vaishnava interpreters understand this meditation as directing the devotee's consciousness toward Vishnu, who is the ultimate referent of Om. The teaching that Prana is the essence of the Udgitha (1.2) is read as revealing that Vishnu, as the supreme Prana, is the life-force behind all sacred chanting. Just as the Udgitha is the highest part of the Saman chant, Vishnu is the highest reality to which all worship points.

The narrative of the contest between the gods and demons over the Udgitha (1.2) teaches that when the faculties — speech, sight, hearing — are contaminated by evil, only Prana remains pure. Vaishnavas read this as demonstrating that Vishnu alone is untouched by the imperfections that afflict all created beings. The devotee who meditates on Om as Vishnu's sacred syllable purifies all faculties and directs them toward divine service.
