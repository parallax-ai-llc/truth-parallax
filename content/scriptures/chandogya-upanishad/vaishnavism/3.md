Chapter 3 presents the Madhu Vidya (honey doctrine) and the meditation on the sun as the honey of the gods. Vaishnava interpreters read the sun as a manifestation of Vishnu — Surya-Narayana — whose golden brilliance nourishes all beings as honey nourishes those who consume it. The teaching that the rays of the sun are channels through which all the Vedas and all beings receive their sustenance (3.1-5) reveals Vishnu's role as the cosmic provider through His solar form.

The chapter's famous teaching on Brahman as the golden person within the sun and within the heart (3.13-14) is central to Vaishnava theology. The person (*purusha*) shining within the heart is Vishnu as the inner controller (*antaryamin*), and the meditation on this golden being is an act of devotion leading to union with the Lord. The Gayatri meditation (3.12) is understood as a prayer to Vishnu in His solar manifestation.
