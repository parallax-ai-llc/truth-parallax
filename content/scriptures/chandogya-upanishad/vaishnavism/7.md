Chapter 7 records the dialogue between Narada and Sanatkumara, in which the celestial sage Narada — despite his vast learning — confesses that he has not attained peace and seeks instruction from Sanatkumara. The progressive teaching that follows — from name to speech, from speech to mind, through will, thought, meditation, understanding, strength, food, water, fire, space, memory, hope, to Prana and finally to the Infinite (*Bhuma*) — is read by Vaishnavas as a graduated devotional ascent toward Vishnu.

The Bhuma (the Infinite or the Full) that Sanatkumara reveals as the ultimate reality is identified with Vishnu-Narayana. The teaching that "where one sees nothing else, hears nothing else, knows nothing else — that is the Infinite" is read not as impersonal absorption but as total immersion in the divine presence. The devotee who reaches the Bhuma dwells entirely in awareness of Vishnu, seeing nothing apart from the Lord.
