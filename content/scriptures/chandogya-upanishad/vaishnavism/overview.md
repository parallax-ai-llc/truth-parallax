## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters regard the Chandogya Upanishad as one of the most important Upanishadic texts, particularly because of the "Tat Tvam Asi" teaching in Chapter 6. Ramanuja reads this great declaration not as asserting identity between the individual self and Brahman but as revealing that the self has Brahman (Vishnu-Narayana) as its inner essence and controller. The soul is real, Brahman is real, and the relationship between them is one of inseparable dependence — like body to soul.

## Theological Emphases

The Chandogya's teaching that "all this has its self in That" (*aitadatmyam idam sarvam*) is central to Vishishtadvaita theology. Ramanuja's commentary argues that the examples Uddalaka uses — clay and pot, gold and ornament — demonstrate that effects are real transformations of their cause, not illusions. The Panchagni Vidya (Chapter 5) and its doctrine of two paths after death are read as describing the soul's journey toward or away from Vishnu's eternal abode.
