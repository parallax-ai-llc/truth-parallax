Chapter 5 presents the Panchagni Vidya (five-fire doctrine) and the teaching of the two paths after death — the *devayana* (path of the gods) leading to non-return, and the *pitriyana* (path of the ancestors) leading to rebirth. Vaishnava interpreters read the *devayana* as the path to Vishnu's eternal abode (*Vaikuntha*), from which the liberated soul never returns to the cycle of birth and death. The five fires represent the cosmic process by which Vishnu creates and sustains embodied existence.

The chapter's narrative of the five householders questioning King Ashvapati about the Vaishvanara (universal self) is read as teaching that Vishnu pervades the entire cosmos as the cosmic person (*vishvarupa*). Each householder's partial knowledge — identifying the self with heaven, sun, air, space, or water — is corrected by the king, who reveals the complete Vaishvanara as the totality of the universe, which is Vishnu's body.
