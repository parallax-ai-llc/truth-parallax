Shankara treats the Panchagni Vidya (5.3-10) as an important teaching on the mechanism of transmigration (*samsara*), which belongs to the domain of empirical reality (*vyavahara*). The five fires describe how the soul, driven by karma, passes through cosmic stages to take birth in a body. This teaching motivates the desire for liberation by revealing the painful mechanics of rebirth. The two paths — *devayana* and *pitriyana* — are both within *samsara*; only the knowledge of Brahman leads to *moksha* beyond both paths.

The Vaishvanara teaching (5.11-18) is read by Shankara as a meditation (*upasana*) on Brahman as the cosmic totality. Each householder's partial view — identifying the self with heaven, sun, or other elements — represents the error of limiting Brahman to a particular form. The full Vaishvanara meditation encompasses all forms but ultimately points beyond them to the formless Brahman that is the meditator's own self.
