Shankara treats Chapter 2's elaborate Saman meditations as *upasanas* (contemplative exercises) designed to expand the aspirant's awareness beyond narrow identification with the body. By correlating the elements of the Saman chant with cosmic realities — earth, atmosphere, heaven, seasons, vital breaths — the meditator progressively identifies with larger and larger spheres of existence. This expansion prepares the mind for the ultimate realization that the self is identical with the totality of Brahman.

The chapter's teaching that the Saman pervades everything — from the elements to the human body to the celestial realms — is read by Shankara as a preliminary illustration of the non-dual insight that will be made explicit in Chapter 6. The multiplicity of correspondences ultimately points to a single underlying reality. These meditations are valuable as preparatory disciplines but must eventually be transcended in favor of direct self-knowledge.
