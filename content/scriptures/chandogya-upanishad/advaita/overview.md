## Overview

Shankara's commentary on the Chandogya Upanishad is among his most extensive and important works. The text provides the *mahavakya* "Tat Tvam Asi" ("That Thou Art"), which Shankara considers the direct statement of the identity of the individual self (*atman*) with the universal reality (*Brahman*). This single sentence, repeated nine times in Chapter 6, is the core of Advaita teaching: what you truly are is not the body, mind, or ego, but Brahman itself.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara reads Uddalaka's examples — clay and pot, gold and ornament — as demonstrating that all names and forms are merely verbal designations (*vacharambhanam vikaro namadheyam*), while the underlying substance alone is real. The Chandogya's teaching on deep sleep (Chapter 8) reveals the self's true nature as pure consciousness, temporarily free from the superimpositions of waking and dreaming. The Panchagni Vidya is treated as preparatory knowledge (*aparavidya*) leading to the higher knowledge of Brahman.
