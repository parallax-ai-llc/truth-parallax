Shankara's commentary on Chapter 1 explains that the meditation on the Udgitha is a form of *upasana* (meditative worship) that purifies the mind and prepares it for the higher knowledge of Brahman. The identification of Om with Prana, the sun, and the essence of all beings (1.1-3) is read as a progressive demonstration that a single reality underlies the diversity of names and forms. Each identification strips away another layer of multiplicity to reveal the one Brahman.

The contest between gods and demons over the Udgitha (1.2) illustrates a key Advaita principle: individual faculties (speech, sight, hearing) are limited and corruptible because they operate within the realm of duality, while Prana — here a symbol for the universal life-principle — transcends these limitations. Shankara treats these meditations as auxiliary practices (*aparavidya*) that prepare the aspirant for the direct knowledge of Brahman taught in later chapters.
