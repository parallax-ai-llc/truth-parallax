Shankara reads the Madhu Vidya (3.1-11) as teaching that the diverse elements of the world — the Vedas, natural phenomena, cosmic directions — are all "honey" extracted from the one Brahman. The analogy suggests that just as honey loses the distinct identities of the flowers from which it was gathered, the realized sage sees all phenomena as expressions of the one undifferentiated Brahman. This foreshadows the "Tat Tvam Asi" teaching of Chapter 6.

The meditation on the golden person within the sun and within the heart (3.13-14) is read by Shankara as pointing to the identity of the cosmic self and the individual self — the same Brahman shines in the sun and in the heart. The instruction to meditate on Brahman as *satyam* (truth) in 3.14 — "All this is Brahman; let one meditate on it as *tajjalan* (arising from, dissolving into, and sustained by That)" — is one of the most important Advaita proof-texts, establishing that the entire universe is a manifestation of the one Brahman.
