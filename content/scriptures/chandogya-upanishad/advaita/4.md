Shankara's commentary on Chapter 4 focuses on the teaching of *samvarga* (absorption) given by Raikva. Vayu absorbs all cosmic phenomena, and Prana absorbs all individual faculties — both point to Brahman as the ultimate reality into which all appearances are resolved. This teaching of absorption foreshadows the full Advaita realization that multiplicity is resolved into the one Brahman.

The Satyakama narrative is significant for Shankara's argument that Brahman-knowledge is accessible to the qualified student (*adhikari*) regardless of birth. The four quarters of Brahman revealed by the bull, fire, swan, and water-bird — identified with the directions, worlds, luminaries, and cosmic waters — teach that Brahman manifests as everything. The teaching is graduated, moving from gross to subtle, but the ultimate realization is that these are not parts of Brahman but aspects of the one undivided reality.
