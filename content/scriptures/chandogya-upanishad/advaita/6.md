Chapter 6 is the heart of the Chandogya Upanishad and the foundation of Advaita Vedanta. Shankara treats Uddalaka's teaching as the direct revelation of the *mahavakya* "Tat Tvam Asi" — the great sentence that declares the identity of the individual self (*atman*) with the universal reality (*Brahman*). The teaching begins with the cosmological assertion that in the beginning there was Being alone (*sat*), one without a second (6.2), which then projected fire, water, and food — the entire manifest universe.

The examples of clay and pot, gold and ornament (6.1) establish Shankara's principle of *vacharambhanam vikaro namadheyam* — "all modification is merely a name based on speech; the reality is only the material cause." Pots are nothing but clay; ornaments are nothing but gold; the world is nothing but Brahman. The nine repetitions of "Tat Tvam Asi" are read as addressing progressively subtler levels of misidentification until the student realizes that he is — and has always been — Brahman itself.
