---
title: "Chandogya Upanishad Chapter 8"
scripture: "chandogya-upanishad"
chapter: 8
---

**1** There is this city of Brahman, the body, and in it the palace, the small lotus of the heart, and in it that small ether. Now what exists within that small ether, that is to be sought for, that is to be understood.

**2** And if they should say to him: "Now with regard to that city of Brahman and the palace in it, the small lotus of the heart, and the small ether within the heart—what is there within it that deserves to be sought for, or that is to be understood?"

**3** Then he should say: "As large as this ether is, so large is that ether within the heart. Both heaven and earth are contained within it, both fire and air, both sun and moon, both lightning and stars; and whatever there is of him in this world, and whatever is not, all that is contained within it."

**4** And if they should say: "If everything that exists is contained in that city of Brahman, all beings and all desires—then what is left of it when old age reaches it and scatters it, or when it falls to pieces?"

**5** Then he should say: "By the old age of the body, that ether does not age; by the death of the body, that is not killed. That is the true Brahma-city. In it all desires are contained. It is the Self, free from sin, free from old age, from death and grief, from hunger and thirst, whose desire is the True, whose thoughts are the True."

**6** Now as here on earth people follow as they are commanded, and depend on the object which they are attached to, be it a country or a piece of land,

**7** And as here on earth whatever has been acquired by exertion perishes, so perishes whatever is acquired for the next world by sacrifices and other good actions performed on earth. Those who depart from hence without having discovered the Self and those true desires, for them there is no freedom in all the worlds. But those who depart from hence, after having discovered the Self and those true desires, for them there is freedom in all the worlds.

**8** If a man desires the world of the fathers, by his mere will the fathers come to receive him, and having obtained the world of the fathers, he is happy.

**9** And if he desires the world of the mothers, by his mere will the mothers come to receive him, and having obtained the world of the mothers, he is happy.

**10** And if he desires the world of the brothers, by his mere will the brothers come to receive him, and having obtained the world of the brothers, he is happy.

**11** And if he desires the world of the sisters, by his mere will the sisters come. And if he desires the world of friends, by his mere will the friends come. And if he desires the world of perfumes and garlands, by his mere will perfumes and garlands come. And if he desires the world of food and drink, by his mere will food and drink come. And if he desires the world of song and music, by his mere will song and music come.

**12** These true desires, though true, are covered with what is false, and while the desires themselves are true, they are covered with what is false. Therefore whoever belonging to us has departed this life, him we cannot gain back, so that we should see him with our eyes.

**13** "Those who are his, both who are living and who have died, and whatever else there is which he wishes for and does not obtain, all that he finds by going there, into the Self; for there his true desires are, covered by what is false."

**14** "As people who do not know the country walk again and again over gold that has been hidden somewhere in the earth without discovering it, thus do all these creatures day after day go into the Brahma-world without discovering it, carried away by untruth."

**15** That Self abides in the heart. And this is the etymological explanation: the Self abides in the heart, hence it is called Hridayam. He who knows this goes day by day into the heavenly world.

**16** Now that serene being which, after having risen from out this earthly body, and having reached the highest light, appears in its own form, that is the Self. That is the immortal, the fearless, that is Brahman. The name of that Brahman is the True.

**17** Now Prajapati said: "The Self which is free from sin, free from old age, from death and grief, from hunger and thirst, whose desires are true, whose thoughts are true—that is to be sought for, that is to be understood. He who has searched out that Self and understands it, obtains all worlds and all desires."

**18** The Devas and the Asuras both heard these words and said: "Well, let us search for that Self by which, if one has searched it out, all worlds and all desires are obtained."

**19** Thus saying, Indra went from the Devas and Virochana from the Asuras, and both, without having communicated with each other, approached Prajapati, holding fuel in their hands, as is the custom for pupils approaching their master.

**20** They dwelt there as pupils for thirty-two years. Then Prajapati asked them: "For what purpose have you both dwelt here?"

**21** They replied: "A saying of yours is being repeated: 'The Self which is free from sin, free from old age, from death and grief, from hunger and thirst, whose desires are true, whose thoughts are true—that is to be sought for, that is to be understood. He who has searched out that Self and understands it, obtains all worlds and all desires.' Now we both have dwelt here because we wish to learn that Self."

**22** Prajapati said to them: "The person that is seen in the eye, that is the Self. This is what I have said. This is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman."

**23** They asked: "Sir, he who is perceived in the water, and he who is perceived in a mirror, who is he?" He replied: "He himself indeed is seen in all these." He said: "Look at yourself in a pan of water, and whatever you do not understand of yourself, come and tell me."

**24** They looked in the water-pan. Then Prajapati said to them: "What do you see?" They said: "We both see the self thus altogether, a picture even to the very hairs and nails."

**25** Prajapati said to them: "After you have adorned yourselves, have put on your best clothes and cleaned yourselves, look again into the water-pan." They, after having adorned themselves, put on their best clothes and cleaned themselves, looked into the water-pan.

**26** Prajapati said: "What do you see?" They said: "Just as we are, well adorned, with our best clothes and clean, thus we are both there, Sir, well adorned, with our best clothes and clean."

**27** Prajapati said: "That is the Self, this is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman." Then both went away satisfied in their hearts.

**28** And Prajapati, looking after them, said: "They both go away without having perceived and without having known the Self, and whoever of these two, whether Devas or Asuras, will follow this doctrine, will perish."

**29** Now Virochana, satisfied in his heart, went to the Asuras and preached that doctrine to them, that the self alone is to be worshipped, that the self alone is to be served. He said: "He who worships the self and serves the self, he gains both worlds, this and the next."

**30** Therefore they call even now a man who does not give alms here, who has no faith, and who offers no sacrifices, an Asura, for this is the doctrine of the Asuras. They deck the body of the dead with perfumes, flowers, and fine raiment by way of ornament, and think they will thus conquer that world.

**31** But Indra, even before he had returned to the Devas, saw this difficulty. "As this self is well adorned when the body is well adorned, well dressed when the body is well dressed, so that self will be blind if the body is blind, lame if the body is lame, crippled if the body is crippled, and will perish in fact as soon as the body perishes. I see no good in this."

**32** Taking fuel in his hand he came again as a pupil to Prajapati. Prajapati said: "Maghavat, as you went away with Virochana satisfied in your heart, for what purpose did you come back?" Indra said: "Sir, as this self is well adorned when the body is well adorned, so that self will perish as soon as the body perishes. I see no good in this."

**33** "So it is indeed, Maghavat," replied Prajapati. "But I shall explain it to you further. Live with me another thirty-two years." He lived with him another thirty-two years, and then Prajapati said:

**34** "He who moves about happy in dreams, he is the Self, this is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman." Then Indra went away satisfied. But before he had returned to the Devas, he saw this difficulty: "Although it is true that self is not blind even if the body is blind, yet in dreams that self seems to suffer and to weep. I see no good in this."

**35** He came back again to Prajapati and lived with him another thirty-two years. Then Prajapati said: "When a man being asleep, reposing, and at perfect rest, sees no dreams, that is the Self, this is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman."

**36** Then Indra went away satisfied. But before he had returned to the Devas, he saw this difficulty: "In truth he does not know himself that he is, nor does he know anything that exists. He is gone to utter annihilation. I see no good in this."

**37** He came back again to Prajapati and lived with him another five years. In all, Indra dwelt there for one hundred and one years, and therefore people say that Indra dwelt with Prajapati for one hundred and one years.

**38** Prajapati said to him: "Maghavat, this body is mortal and always held by death. It is the abode of that Self which is immortal and without body. When in the body, the Self is held by pleasure and pain. So long as he is in the body, he cannot get free from pleasure and pain. But when he is free of the body, then neither pleasure nor pain touches him."

**39** "The wind is without body, the cloud, lightning, and thunder are without body. Now as these, arising from this heavenly space, appear in their own form as soon as they have approached the highest light, thus does that serene being, arising from this body, appear in its own form as soon as it has approached the highest light. He is the highest Person."

**40** "There he moves about, laughing, playing, and rejoicing, be it with women, carriages, or relatives, never minding that body into which he was born. As an animal is attached to a cart, so is life attached to this body."

**41** "Now where the sight has entered into the void, there is the person of the eye; the eye itself is the instrument of seeing. He who knows, let me smell this, he is the Self; the nose is the instrument of smelling. He who knows, let me say this, he is the Self; the tongue is the instrument of saying. He who knows, let me hear this, he is the Self; the ear is the instrument of hearing."

**42** "He who knows, let me think this, he is the Self; the mind is his divine eye. He, the Self, seeing these pleasures through his divine eye, the mind, rejoices."

**43** "The Devas who are in the Brahma-world meditate on that Self. Therefore all worlds belong to them, and all desires. He who has found out and who understands that Self, he obtains all worlds and all desires." Thus said Prajapati. Thus said Prajapati.
