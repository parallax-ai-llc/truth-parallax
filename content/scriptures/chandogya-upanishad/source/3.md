---
title: "Chandogya Upanishad Chapter 3"
scripture: "chandogya-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The sun is indeed the honey of the gods. The heaven is the cross-beam from which the sky hangs as a hive, and the bright vapours are the eggs of the bees.

**2** The eastern rays of the sun are the honey-cells on the eastern side. The Rik verses are the bees; the Rig-Veda is the flower; the water of the sacrificial libations is the nectar. Those Rik verses brooded over the Rig-Veda; and from it, thus brooded over, sprang as its essence, fame, splendour of countenance, vigour, strength, and health.

**3** That essence flowed forth and went towards the sun. And that is what forms the red appearance of the sun.

**4** The southern rays of the sun are the honey-cells on the southern side. The Yajus verses are the bees; the Yajur-Veda is the flower; the water of the sacrificial libations is the nectar.

**5** Those Yajus verses brooded over the Yajur-Veda; and from it, thus brooded over, sprang as its essence, fame, splendour, vigour, strength, and health.

**6** That essence flowed forth and went towards the sun. And that is what forms the white appearance of the sun.

**7** The western rays of the sun are the honey-cells on the western side. The Saman verses are the bees; the Sama-Veda is the flower; the water is the nectar.

**8** Those Saman verses brooded over the Sama-Veda; and from it, thus brooded over, sprang fame, splendour, vigour, strength, and health.

**9** That essence flowed forth and went towards the sun. And that is what forms the dark appearance of the sun.

**10** The northern rays of the sun are the honey-cells on the northern side. The hymns of the Atharvangiras are the bees; the Itihasa-Purana is the flower; the water is the nectar.

**11** Those hymns of the Atharvangiras brooded over the Itihasa-Purana; and from it, thus brooded over, sprang fame, splendour, vigour, strength, and health.

**12** That essence flowed forth and went towards the sun. And that is what forms the exceedingly dark appearance of the sun.

**13** The upper rays of the sun are the honey-cells above. The secret doctrines are the bees; Brahman is the flower; the water is the nectar.

**14** Those secret doctrines brooded over Brahman; and from it, thus brooded over, sprang fame, splendour, vigour, strength, and health.

**15** That essence flowed forth and went towards the sun. And that is what seems to stir in the centre of the sun.

**16** Verily, these are the essences of the essences. For the Vedas are the essences, and of them these are again the essences. They are the nectar of the nectars. For the Vedas are the nectars, and of them these are the nectars.

**17** On the first of these nectars the Vasus live, with Agni at their head. Verily, the gods do not eat or drink, but they are satisfied by seeing that nectar.

**18** They enter into that colour and they rise from that colour.

**19** He who thus knows this nectar becomes one of the Vasus, with Agni at their head; he is satisfied by seeing the nectar. He enters into that colour and rises from that colour.

**20** So long as the sun rises in the east and sets in the west, so long does he obtain the lordship of the Vasus.

**21** On the second of these nectars the Rudras live, with Indra at their head. The gods do not eat or drink, but they are satisfied by seeing that nectar. He who knows this becomes one of the Rudras.

**22** On the third of these nectars the Adityas live, with Varuna at their head. He who knows this becomes one of the Adityas.

**23** On the fourth of these nectars the Maruts live, with Soma at their head. He who knows this becomes one of the Maruts.

**24** On the fifth of these nectars the Sadhyas live, with Brahma at their head. He who knows this becomes one of the Sadhyas.

**25** Now, in the beginning this was non-existent. It became existent. It grew. It turned into an egg. The egg lay for the period of a year. The egg broke open. The two halves were one of silver, the other of gold.

**26** That which was of silver is this earth; that which was of gold is the sky. What was the outer membrane is the mountains; what was the inner membrane is cloud and mist. What were the veins are the rivers; what was the fluid within is the ocean.

**27** Now what was born from it, that is yonder sun. When it was born, shouts and hurrahs, all beings and all desires arose. Therefore, whenever the sun rises and whenever it returns, shouts and hurrahs, all beings and all desires arise.

**28** He who, knowing this, meditates on the sun as Brahman—pleasant sounds will approach him and continue to delight him. Yea, they will delight him.
