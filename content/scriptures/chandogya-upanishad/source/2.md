---
title: "Chandogya Upanishad Chapter 2"
scripture: "chandogya-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Meditation on the whole of the Saman is good, and people, whenever anything is good, say it is Saman, and whenever anything is not good, it is not Saman.

**2** Thus they also say, he approached him with Saman, meaning he approached him well; and they say he was well-decked, meaning it was done with Saman.

**3** This world is Hinkara, fire is Prastava, the sun is Udgitha, the stars are Pratihara, the moon is Nidhana. This is the Rajana Saman, as interwoven in the deities.

**4** He who thus knows this Rajana Saman as interwoven in the deities, obtains the same world, the same happiness, and the same company as these very deities; he reaches the full life, he lives long, becomes great with children and cattle, great by fame. His rule is, "Never speak ill of Brahmins."

**5** Rain is Hinkara, the cloud is Prastava, lightning is Udgitha, thunder is Pratihara, and the ceasing of rain is Nidhana. This is the Raivatam Saman, as interwoven in the rain.

**6** He who thus knows this Raivatam Saman as interwoven in the rain, obtains all kinds of rain; he reaches the full life, he lives long, becomes great with children and cattle, great by fame. His rule is, "Never speak ill of rain."

**7** Spring is Hinkara, summer is Prastava, the rainy season is Udgitha, autumn is Pratihara, winter is Nidhana. This is the Vairupam Saman, as interwoven in the seasons.

**8** He who thus knows the Vairupam Saman as interwoven in the seasons, shines with children, cattle, and the splendour of sacred knowledge. He reaches the full life, he lives long. His rule is, "Never speak ill of the seasons."

**9** The earth is Hinkara, the sky is Prastava, heaven is Udgitha, the regions are Pratihara, the sea is Nidhana. These are the Sakvari Samans, as interwoven in the worlds.

**10** He who thus knows the Sakvari Samans as interwoven in the worlds, becomes possessed of the worlds; he reaches the full life, he lives long. His rule is, "Never speak ill of the worlds."

**11** Goats are Hinkara, sheep are Prastava, cows are Udgitha, horses are Pratihara, man is Nidhana. These are the Revati Samans, as interwoven in animals.

**12** He who thus knows the Revati Samans as interwoven in animals, becomes rich in animals; he reaches the full life, he lives long. His rule is, "Never speak ill of animals."

**13** Hair is Hinkara, skin is Prastava, flesh is Udgitha, bone is Pratihara, marrow is Nidhana. This is the Yajnayajniya Saman, as interwoven in the members of the body.

**14** He who thus knows the Yajnayajniya Saman as interwoven in the members of the body, becomes possessed of strong limbs; he reaches the full life, he lives long. His rule is, "Do not eat marrow for a year," or "Do not eat marrow at all."

**15** Fire is Hinkara, air is Prastava, the sun is Udgitha, the stars are Pratihara, the moon is Nidhana. This is the Rajana Saman, as interwoven in the deities.

**16** He who thus knows this Rajana Saman, as interwoven in the deities, obtains the same world, the same happiness, and reaches the full life. His rule is, "Never speak ill of the Brahmins."

**17** The threefold knowledge is Hinkara, the three worlds are Prastava, fire, air, and sun are Udgitha, stars, birds, and rays are Pratihara, serpents, Gandharvas, and fathers are Nidhana. This is the Saman, as interwoven in everything.

**18** He who thus knows this Saman as interwoven in everything, he becomes everything.

**19** There is a verse on this: There are the fivefold ones, and five and three, and with the three the whole universe is known. He who knows this, knows all, all quarters bring him tribute.

**20** Let him meditate: "I am everything." This is the rule.

**21** Verily, this Udgitha is the best of all the essences, the highest, holding the highest place—the eighth. The Udgitha is excellent. For as gold is superior to all other metals, so is this Udgitha.

**22** When one understands the Udgitha, one comes to know the essence. Let a man worship the Saman, devoted to the idea: the Saman is all.

**23** What is good work, that is Saman. What is Saman, that is good work. Thus the chanter of the Saman should know.
