---
title: "Chandogya Upanishad Chapter 4"
scripture: "chandogya-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** There lived once upon a time Janasruti Pautrayana, who was a pious giver, giving much and preparing much food for the people. He built places of rest everywhere, wishing that people should everywhere eat of his food.

**2** Once in the night, some flamingoes flew over his house, and one flamingo said to another: "Ho, ho, short-sighted one, the light of Janasruti Pautrayana has spread like the sky. Do not go near, that it may not burn thee."

**3** The other answered him: "Who is he whom thou praisest as if he were like Raikva with the cart?" "What is this Raikva with the cart?" asked the first.

**4** The other replied: "As in a game of dice, when the highest score is hit, all the lower scores belong to the winner, so whatever good deeds creatures do, all that goes to Raikva. I say the same of whosoever knows what Raikva knows."

**5** Now Janasruti Pautrayana overheard this. Rising from his couch, he said to an attendant: "Friend, dost thou praise me as if I were Raikva with the cart?" "What is this Raikva with the cart?" the attendant asked.

**6** Janasruti said: "As in a game of dice, when the highest score is hit, all the lower scores belong to the winner, so whatever good deeds creatures do, all that goes to Raikva." The king sent the attendant to search for Raikva.

**7** The attendant searched for him but returned, saying: "I could not find him." Then the king said to him: "Where a knower of Brahman should be searched for, go there and seek him."

**8** The attendant came to a man who was sitting under a cart and scratching his sores. He said to him: "Sir, are you Raikva with the cart?" "I am," he answered. Then the attendant returned and told the king: "I have found him."

**9** Then Janasruti Pautrayana took six hundred cows, a necklace, and a chariot with mules, went to Raikva, and said:

**10** "Raikva, here are six hundred cows, a necklace, and a chariot with mules. Teach me the deity which thou worshippest."

**11** The other replied: "Take back thy gifts, O Shudra, keep them with the cows and the chariot." Then Janasruti took again a thousand cows, a necklace, a chariot with mules, and his own daughter, and went to him.

**12** He said: "Raikva, here are a thousand cows, a necklace, a chariot with mules, this wife, and this village in which thou dwellest. Teach me, Sir."

**13** He, lifting up her face, said: "He brought these cows—but only by this face, O Shudra, thou didst make me speak." Thus are the places called Raikvaparnas in the country of the Mahavrishas, where Raikva dwelt under his patronage. And Raikva said to him:

**14** "Air is indeed the end of all. For when fire goes out, it goes into air. When the sun goes down, it goes into air. When the moon goes down, it goes into air."

**15** "When water dries up, it goes into air. Air indeed swallows them all. This is with reference to the gods."

**16** "Now with reference to the body: Breath is indeed the end of all. When a man sleeps, speech goes into breath, the eye goes into breath, the ear goes into breath, the mind goes into breath. Breath indeed swallows them all."

**17** "These are the two ends, air among the gods, breath among the senses."

**18** Once Satyakama Jabala addressed his mother and said: "Mother, I wish to become a Brahmachari. Of what family am I?"

**19** She said to him: "I do not know, my child, of what family thou art. In my youth, when I had to move about much as a servant, I conceived thee. I do not know of what family thou art. I am Jabala by name; thou art Satyakama. Say that thou art Satyakama Jabala."

**20** He going to Gautama Haridrumata said to him: "I wish to become a Brahmachari with you, Sir. May I come to you, Sir?"

**21** He said to him: "Of what family are you, my friend?" He replied: "I do not know, Sir, of what family I am. I asked my mother, and she answered: 'I do not know of what family thou art. I am Jabala by name, thou art Satyakama.' I am therefore Satyakama Jabala, Sir."

**22** He said to him: "No one but a true Brahmana would thus speak out. Go and fetch fuel, friend. I shall initiate you. You have not swerved from the truth."

**23** Having initiated him, he chose out four hundred lean and weak cows, and said: "Tend these, friend." He drove them out and said to himself: "I shall not return unless it be with a thousand." He dwelt a number of years in the forest, till the cows had increased to a thousand.

**24** Then the bull of the herd said to him: "Satyakama! We have reached a thousand. Carry us to the teacher's house. And I will declare to thee one foot of Brahman."

**25** "Declare it, Sir," he replied. The bull said: "The eastern region is one quarter, the western region is one quarter, the southern region is one quarter, the northern region is one quarter. This, O friend, is one foot of Brahman, consisting of four quarters, and called Prakasavat, the Luminous."

**26** "He who, knowing this, meditates on this foot of Brahman as the Luminous, becomes luminous in this world. He conquers the luminous worlds—he who, knowing this, meditates on this foot of Brahman as the Luminous."
