---
title: "Chandogya Upanishad Chapter 1"
scripture: "chandogya-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Let a man meditate on the syllable Om, called the Udgitha; for the Udgitha is sung beginning with Om. The full account, however, of Om is this:

**2** The essence of all beings is the earth, the essence of the earth is water, the essence of water is the plants, the essence of the plants is man, the essence of man is speech, the essence of speech is the Rik, the essence of the Rik is the Saman, the essence of the Saman is the Udgitha.

**3** That Udgitha is the best of all essences, the highest, deserving the highest place, the eighth.

**4** What is the Rik? What is the Saman? What is the Udgitha? This is the question.

**5** The Rik indeed is speech, the Saman is breath, the Udgitha is the syllable Om. Now speech and breath, or Rik and Saman, form one couple.

**6** And that couple is joined together in the syllable Om. When two people come together, they fulfil each other's desire.

**7** Thus he who, knowing this, meditates on the syllable Om, the Udgitha, becomes indeed a fulfiller of desires.

**8** That syllable is a syllable of permission, for whenever we permit anything, we say Om. Now permission is gratification. He who, knowing this, meditates on the syllable Om, the Udgitha, becomes indeed a gratifier of desires.

**9** By that syllable does the threefold knowledge proceed. When the Adhvaryu priest gives an order, he says Om. When the Hotri priest recites, he says Om. When the Udgatri priest sings, he says Om—all for the glory of that syllable. The threefold knowledge and also this syllable are the same.

**10** Now, with this syllable both he who knows this and he who does not know perform. Knowledge and ignorance, however, are different. What is performed with knowledge, with faith, and with the Upanishad is more powerful. This then is the full account of the syllable Om.

**11** The gods and the demons, both having sprung from Prajapati, were once contending against each other. The gods took hold of the Udgitha, thinking they would vanquish the demons by means of it.

**12** They meditated on the Udgitha as the breath in the nose, but the demons pierced it with evil. Therefore we smell by the breath in the nose both what is sweet-smelling and what is bad-smelling, for the breath was pierced with evil.

**13** Then they meditated on the Udgitha as speech, but the demons pierced it with evil. Therefore we speak both truth and untruth, for speech was pierced with evil.

**14** Then they meditated on the Udgitha as the eye, but the demons pierced it with evil. Therefore we see both what is sightly and what is unsightly, for the eye was pierced with evil.

**15** Then they meditated on the Udgitha as the ear, but the demons pierced it with evil. Therefore we hear both what should be heard and what should not be heard, for the ear was pierced with evil.

**16** Then they meditated on the Udgitha as the mind, but the demons pierced it with evil. Therefore we conceive both what should be conceived and what should not be conceived, for the mind was pierced with evil.

**17** Then they meditated on the Udgitha as the chief breath. When the demons rushed at it, they were destroyed, as a clod of earth would be destroyed by striking against a solid stone.

**18** As a clod of earth is destroyed when striking against a solid stone, so will he be destroyed who wishes evil to one who knows this, or who persecutes him; for he is a solid stone.

**19** By it he does not discern what is sweet-smelling and what is bad-smelling, for that breath is free from evil. Whatever we eat and whatever we drink, by that breath we support the other vital breaths. And when at death that breath does not find this, it departs. At the moment of departing, it opens the mouth.

**20** Angiras meditated on the Udgitha as that breath. People regard it as Angiras, because it is the essence of the members of the body.

**21** Therefore Brihaspati meditated on the Udgitha as that breath. People regard it as Brihaspati, because speech is great, and it is its lord.

**22** Therefore Ayasya meditated on the Udgitha as that breath. People regard it as Ayasya, because it goes from the mouth.

**23** Baka Dalbhya knew it. He became the Udgatri priest of the people of the Naimisha forest, and he sang out their desires for them.

**24** He who knows this and meditates on the syllable Om, the imperishable Udgitha, obtains all his wishes by his singing. So much with regard to the Udgitha as meditated upon with reference to the body.
