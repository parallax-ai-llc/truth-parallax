---
title: "Chandogya Upanishad Chapter 5"
scripture: "chandogya-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** He who knows the oldest and the best, becomes himself the oldest and the best. Breath indeed is the oldest and best.

**2** He who knows the richest, becomes himself the richest. Speech indeed is the richest.

**3** He who knows the firm resting-place, becomes firm in this world and in the next. The eye indeed is the firm resting-place.

**4** He who knows success, his wishes succeed, both his divine and human wishes. The ear indeed is success.

**5** He who knows the home, becomes the home of his people. The mind indeed is the home.

**6** The vital breaths once disputed among themselves as to who was the best, saying: "I am best, I am best."

**7** They went to their father Prajapati and said: "Sir, who is the best of us?" He replied: "He by whose departure the body seems worse than worst, he is the best of you."

**8** Speech departed, and having been absent for a year, it came back and said: "How have you been able to live without me?" The rest answered: "Like mute people, not speaking, but breathing with the breath, seeing with the eye, hearing with the ear, thinking with the mind. Thus we lived." Then speech entered in again.

**9** The eye departed, and having been absent for a year, it came back and said: "How have you been able to live without me?" The rest answered: "Like blind people, not seeing, but breathing with the breath, speaking with the tongue, hearing with the ear, thinking with the mind. Thus we lived." Then the eye entered in again.

**10** The ear departed, and having been absent for a year, it came back and said: "How have you been able to live without me?" The rest answered: "Like deaf people, not hearing, but breathing with the breath, speaking with the tongue, seeing with the eye, thinking with the mind. Thus we lived." Then the ear entered in again.

**11** The mind departed, and having been absent for a year, it came back and said: "How have you been able to live without me?" The rest answered: "Like children whose minds are not yet formed, but breathing with the breath, speaking with the tongue, seeing with the eye, hearing with the ear. Thus we lived." Then the mind entered in again.

**12** When the breath was about to depart, it tore up the other vital breaths, as a great excellent horse of the Sindhu country would tear up the pegs to which he is tethered. They said to him: "Sir, do not depart. We shall not be able to live without thee." He said: "Then make me an offering." "So be it," they replied.

**13** Then speech said to him: "That wherein I am the richest, thou art the richest thereby." The eye said: "That wherein I am the firm resting-place, thou art the firm resting-place thereby." The ear said: "That wherein I am success, thou art success thereby." The mind said: "That wherein I am the home, thou art the home thereby."

**14** And verily, people do not call them speeches, nor eyes, nor ears, nor minds. They call them breaths, for breath indeed is all these.

**15** Now then follows the teaching of Pravahana Jaivali. He said to Svetaketu Aruneya: "What has thy father taught thee?"

**16** "He has taught me," Svetaketu replied. Pravahana asked: "Dost thou know where created beings go to from here?" "No, Sir." "Dost thou know how they return again?" "No, Sir." "Dost thou know where the path of the gods and the path of the fathers diverge?" "No, Sir."

**17** "Dost thou know why that world is never full?" "No, Sir." "Dost thou know why in the fifth libation water is called Man?" "No, Sir."

**18** Then the king said: "How then couldst thou say thou hadst been taught? For how can he who does not know these things say he has been taught?" The boy went back afflicted to his father's place, and said: "Sir, without having taught me, you said you had taught me."

**19** The father said: "I too do not know all this. Let us go together and learn." Gautama went to King Pravahana Jaivali and the king received him with honour.

**20** In the morning the king went out. He said to Gautama: "Sir, ask a boon of worldly wealth." Gautama replied: "Keep thy worldly wealth, O king. Tell me the speech which thou addressedst to the boy."

**21** The king was perplexed, and bade him stay with him a long time. Then he said to him: "As you have told me, O Gautama, this knowledge did not go to any Brahmana before you, and therefore this teaching belonged in all the worlds to the Kshatriya class alone."

**22** He then said: "Those who know this, and those who in the forest follow faith and austerities, go to the light, from light to day, from day to the increasing half of the moon, from the increasing half to the six months when the sun goes to the north."

**23** "From those months to the year, from the year to the sun, from the sun to the moon, from the moon to the lightning. There a person who is not a man leads them to Brahman. This is the path of the gods."

**24** "But those who living in a village practise sacrifices, works of public utility, and alms, they go to the smoke, from smoke to night, from night to the decreasing half of the moon, from the decreasing half to the six months when the sun goes to the south. But they do not reach the year."

**25** "From those months they go to the world of the fathers, from the world of the fathers to the ether, from the ether to the moon. That is Soma, the king. That is the food of the gods. The gods do eat it."

**26** "Having dwelt there till their merit is consumed, they return again that way as they came, to the ether, from the ether to the air, from the air to the rain, from the rain to the earth. And when they have reached the earth, they become food, they become grain, and are offered again in the altar-fire which is man, and thence are born in the fire of woman. Thus they rise up towards the worlds and go the same round as before."

**27** "But those who know neither of these two paths, become worms, birds, and creeping things."
