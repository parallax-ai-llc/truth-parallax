The Chandogya's opening meditation on Om as the essence of all reality and all speech resonates with logos theology in multiple traditions. The Gospel of John's declaration that "In the beginning was the Word" parallels the Upanishadic teaching that the sacred syllable precedes and underlies all creation. The Sufi practice of *dhikr* (repetitive chanting of divine names) and the Christian hesychast tradition of the Jesus Prayer share the Chandogya's conviction that repeated sacred utterance can transform consciousness.

The contest between gods and demons, resolved through the supremacy of Prana, finds parallels in the cosmic battles of Zoroastrian mythology (Ahura Mazda vs. Angra Mainyu), Norse mythology (Aesir vs. Jotnar), and the Christian narrative of angelic warfare. In each case, the ultimate victory belongs to the life-sustaining principle rather than to any particular power or faculty.
