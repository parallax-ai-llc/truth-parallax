The Madhu Vidya's teaching that all sacred knowledge is "honey" gathered from the cosmic source resonates with the concept of perennial wisdom — the idea that diverse traditions draw from a common spiritual reservoir. The metaphor of the sun as the source of all nourishment parallels solar theology in ancient Egypt (Ra), Zoroastrianism (the sun as Ahura Mazda's eye), and Plato's analogy of the Good as the sun illuminating all truth.

The golden person within the sun and the heart simultaneously present in the macrocosm and the microcosm echoes the Hermetic maxim "As above, so below," the Kabbalistic correspondence between *Adam Kadmon* (the cosmic human) and the individual soul, and the Sufi concept of *al-insan al-kamil* (the perfect human as microcosm). The *tajjalan* formula — all things arise from, exist in, and return to Brahman — parallels Neoplatonic emanation and return, and the Buddhist concept of dependent origination.
