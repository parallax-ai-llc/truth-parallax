The *dahara vidya* — the teaching that the entire cosmos is contained within the heart's inner space — resonates with mystical traditions worldwide. The Sufi concept of the *qalb* (heart) as the locus of divine encounter, the Christian mystic's "castle of the soul" (Teresa of Avila), and the Kabbalistic *lev* (heart) as the meeting point of upper and lower worlds all share the conviction that interiority is the path to the Infinite.

The Prajapati-Indra dialogue's progressive elimination of false selves parallels phenomenological methods across traditions. The Buddhist analysis of the five aggregates (*skandhas*) systematically removes every component that might be mistaken for the self. Descartes' methodical doubt strips away all uncertain knowledge to reach the *cogito*. The Sufi stages of *nafs* (self/ego) describe progressive purification from the commanding self to the satisfied self. Each tradition recognizes that the true self is discovered not by addition but by subtraction.
