The Chandogya's correlation of ritual elements with cosmic phenomena belongs to a widespread pattern in ancient religions. The Egyptian temple as microcosm of the universe, the Chinese concept of *li* (ritual propriety) as cosmic harmony, and the Pythagorean music of the spheres all share the conviction that ritual performance participates in and mirrors the structure of reality. The Saman meditations represent a particularly elaborate example of this sacred-cosmic correspondence.

The idea that sacred sound structures the cosmos resonates with the Aboriginal Australian understanding of songlines as paths of creation, the Navajo healing chants that restore cosmic harmony, and the medieval Christian understanding of liturgical chant as participating in the angelic *musica celestis*. In each tradition, the human voice engaged in ritual becomes a bridge between the mundane and the transcendent.
