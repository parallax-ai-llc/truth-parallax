The Panchagni Vidya's doctrine of the two paths after death — one leading to liberation, the other to rebirth — parallels afterlife geographies across traditions. The Tibetan *bardo* teachings describe multiple post-mortem pathways determined by the dying person's consciousness. Plato's "Myth of Er" presents souls choosing their next lives based on past experience. The Zoroastrian Chinvat Bridge separates the righteous from the wicked into different posthumous destinations.

The Vaishvanara teaching — that the cosmic self encompasses the totality of existence — resonates with the concept of the cosmic human found in Kabbalistic *Adam Kadmon*, the Sufi *al-insan al-kamil*, and the Pauline doctrine of the Body of Christ. Each tradition envisions a macrocosmic person whose dimensions encompass the universe, and each teaches that the individual participates in this cosmic totality.
