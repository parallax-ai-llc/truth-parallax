Chapter 8 contains two major teachings: the *dahara vidya* (meditation on the space within the heart) and the Prajapati-Indra dialogue on the nature of the self. The *dahara vidya* represents an internalization of Vedic cosmology — the external cosmos is mirrored within the body, specifically within the heart. Scholars have connected this to broader Vedic ideas about the correspondence between microcosm and macrocosm.

The Prajapati-Indra dialogue, in which Indra returns four times over a period of 101 years to receive progressively deeper teachings about the self, has been analyzed as a sophisticated pedagogical narrative. The four stages — waking self (rejected because it perishes), dreaming self (rejected because it is illusory), deep-sleep self (rejected because it is unconscious), and the transcendent self — may represent an early phenomenological analysis of consciousness. Scholars debate whether the final teaching points to a state of pure consciousness or to a transformed mode of embodied experience.
