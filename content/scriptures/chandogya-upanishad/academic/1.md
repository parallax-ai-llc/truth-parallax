Chapter 1 opens with ritual instruction on the Udgitha chant, the central element of Sama Veda liturgy. Scholars note that the opening sections preserve valuable evidence of how Vedic ritualists understood the metaphysical significance of their chanting practices. The identification of Om with Prana and with the essence of all reality (1.1) represents an early stage of the philosophical process by which ritual elements were reinterpreted as symbols of cosmic principles.

The myth of the gods and demons contesting the Udgitha (1.2) belongs to a widespread Vedic narrative pattern in which cosmic conflicts are resolved through ritual knowledge. Paul Deussen and others have noted that this chapter demonstrates the transition from purely liturgical instruction to proto-philosophical speculation — the ritual chant becomes a vehicle for cosmological teaching. The verse counts and structure suggest this chapter may represent one of the earlier strata of the Chandogya.
