Chapter 2 preserves some of the most detailed information available about the structure and performance of the Saman chant in Vedic ritual. The fivefold (*panchavida*) and sevenfold (*saptavida*) analyses of the Saman provide evidence for how Sama Veda ritualists understood the internal structure of their liturgical performances. The systematic correlation of chant elements with natural phenomena reflects the Vedic practice of *bandhu* (homology) — linking ritual and cosmos.

Scholars have noted that this chapter's material likely predates the more philosophical sections of the Chandogya and may represent an early stratum of the text in which ritual instruction was the primary concern. The cosmological correspondences, while appearing fanciful to modern readers, represent a coherent system of analogical thinking that was central to Vedic intellectual culture. The chapter's structure also provides evidence for the training methods used in Sama Veda schools.
