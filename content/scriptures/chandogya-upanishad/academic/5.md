Chapter 5 contains the Panchagni Vidya, one of the most important and widely studied teachings in the Upanishads. Scholars have analyzed it as early evidence for the Indian doctrine of rebirth (*punarjanma*), which may not have been fully developed in the earliest Vedic texts. The two paths — *devayana* (path of the gods) and *pitriyana* (path of the ancestors) — appear in both the Chandogya and the Brihadaranyaka, suggesting a common source. H.W. Bodewitz has argued that these doctrines represent a transformation of earlier Vedic ideas about the afterlife.

The Vaishvanara narrative is notable for its dramatic structure — five learned householders discover their knowledge is incomplete when tested by a king. This reversal of the expected teacher-student hierarchy (Brahmins learning from a Kshatriya) recurs in several Upanishads and has been interpreted as reflecting actual tensions between priestly and warrior-class intellectuals in late Vedic society.
