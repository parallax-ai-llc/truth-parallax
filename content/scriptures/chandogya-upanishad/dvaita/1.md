Madhva's interpretation of Chapter 1 emphasizes that the Udgitha meditation is a practice of devotion to Vishnu through the medium of Om. The identification of Om with Prana, the essence of all beings, demonstrates Vishnu's all-pervasive lordship — He is the life and meaning behind every sacred utterance. The Udgitha is not merely a ritual sound but a means of connecting with the supreme Person who gives all sounds their power and significance.

The narrative of the gods and demons (1.2) demonstrates the hierarchy of being that is central to Dvaita philosophy. Individual faculties fail when corrupted, but Prana — representing Vishnu's sustaining power — cannot be overcome. This teaches that God's sovereignty is absolute and unchallengeable, and that all other powers are derivative and dependent. The chapter establishes the pattern the Chandogya will develop: all reality points to and depends upon the supreme Lord.
