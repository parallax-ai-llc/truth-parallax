## Overview

Madhva's interpretation of the Chandogya Upanishad is most famous for its radical rereading of "Tat Tvam Asi." Where Advaita reads identity between self and Brahman, Madhva splits the sentence differently — *tat tvam asi* becomes *tat atvam asi* ("thou art not that") or is read with an implied qualification: "thou art [dependent on] that." This interpretive move preserves the fundamental Dvaita principle that God and souls are eternally distinct.

## Theological Emphases

Madhva reads Uddalaka's examples of clay and pot as demonstrating God's role as the material and efficient cause who remains distinct from His effects. The honey doctrine (Chapter 3) teaches that all experiences are offerings to the supreme Lord, not that all things are identical. The Panchagni Vidya reveals the hierarchy of beings and their dependence on Vishnu, who alone is independent (*svatantra*). The progression of the Chandogya from ritual to knowledge is read not as superseding devotion but as deepening it.
