Madhva reads the Panchagni Vidya as revealing the real process by which Vishnu governs the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth. The five fires are not metaphors but descriptions of actual cosmic stations through which the soul passes under God's direction. The two paths after death demonstrate God's justice: those who have practiced devotion and knowledge travel the *devayana* to the Lord's presence, while those bound by desire follow the *pitriyana* to rebirth. Neither path operates independently of Vishnu's sovereign will.

The Vaishvanara teaching corrects the householders' partial understandings of the universal self. For Madhva, their error is not that they saw God in heaven, sun, or space, but that they limited God to only one manifestation. The complete Vaishvanara is Vishnu in His cosmic form, encompassing all dimensions of reality while remaining the personal Lord who governs each aspect. Knowledge of this cosmic Vishnu is a form of devotion, not a stepping-stone to impersonal realization.
