Chapter 2's systematic correlation of the Saman chant with cosmic phenomena demonstrates, in the Dvaita reading, Vishnu's sovereign presence in every dimension of creation. Each correspondence — the Saman with rain, with seasons, with the human body — reveals another facet of God's creative power and governance. The chapter teaches that nothing in creation is self-subsistent; every element exists because Vishnu sustains it and is meaningless apart from Him.

The elaborate ritual structure of the Saman, with its precisely ordered elements, reflects the Dvaita principle of *taratamya* (gradation) — a divinely ordained hierarchy pervading all of reality. Just as the Saman has higher and lower parts in a structured relationship, so the cosmos is organized in a hierarchy of beings, all dependent on Vishnu as the supreme apex. The devotee who performs the Saman with this understanding engages in true worship.
