Madhva reads the Raikva narrative as demonstrating that knowledge of Vishnu's supremacy is the highest attainment, surpassing all worldly wealth and power. King Janashruti's offerings of cattle, gold, and his daughter are accepted by Raikva only when accompanied by humility — teaching that devotion requires the surrender of ego. The *samvarga* (absorption) doctrine reveals Vishnu as the Lord into whom all things are gathered, not dissolved into nothingness but organized under His sovereignty.

The Satyakama narrative demonstrates that Vishnu reveals Himself to the sincere seeker through the hierarchy of His creation. The four quarters of Brahman taught by the bull, fire, swan, and water-bird represent four aspects of Vishnu's cosmic governance. Each teacher in the natural world points beyond itself to the supreme Lord, illustrating the Dvaita principle that all beings serve as instruments of God's self-revelation to the devoted soul.
