Madhva reads the Madhu Vidya as teaching that all Vedic knowledge and all cosmic phenomena are sustained by and directed toward Vishnu, just as honey is the purpose and product of the bees' labor. The hierarchical structure of the doctrine — different Vedas corresponding to different directions and cosmic elements — reflects the Dvaita principle of *taratamya* (ordered gradation) in which all things occupy their proper place in relation to God.

The golden person within the sun and the heart (3.13-14) is Vishnu, who is simultaneously the cosmic Lord governing the universe and the inner controller guiding each individual soul. The meditation on this person is devotion (*bhakti*), not an attempt to merge with God but a practice of recognizing His presence and submitting to His governance. The passage "All this is Brahman" (3.14.1) is read not as identity but as dependence — all things belong to, are sustained by, and exist for the sake of Brahman (Vishnu).
