Madhva reads the *dahara vidya* as teaching that Vishnu dwells within the heart of every being as the inner controller. The small space is not empty or abstract but is the Lord's chosen dwelling place, from which He governs the individual soul. The cosmic vastness contained within this tiny space demonstrates Vishnu's inconceivable power — He is fully present in every heart while simultaneously governing the entire universe.

The Prajapati-Indra dialogue's progressive elimination of false selves leads not to an impersonal absolute but to the recognition of Vishnu as the true self of all selves. The final teaching that the self is "free from evil" describes the Lord's absolute perfection — He alone possesses these qualities inherently. The liberated soul attains a reflection of these qualities through Vishnu's grace but never becomes identical with God. The chapter's closing image of the self "reaching the highest light" is the soul arriving at Vishnu's eternal abode.
