## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Chandogya Upanishad a teaching on the nature of consciousness that aligns with Kashmir Shaivism's non-dual theology. The Udgitha meditation on Om (Chapters 1-2) is read as a practice of recognizing the universal vibration (*spanda*) that is Shiva's creative self-expression. The teaching that all beings emerge from Sat (Being) and return to it (Chapter 6) is understood as describing Paramashiva's cycle of emanation and reabsorption.

## Theological Emphases

The "Tat Tvam Asi" teaching resonates with the Shaiva recognition (*pratyabhijna*) doctrine — the individual self discovers its identity with the universal consciousness. The Chandogya's exploration of deep sleep (Chapter 8) as a state where the self merges with Being parallels the Shaiva teaching on *turiya* (the fourth state) as pure Shiva-consciousness beyond waking, dreaming, and sleeping. The text's emphasis on meditation and inner experience over external ritual aligns with Shaiva contemplative priorities.
