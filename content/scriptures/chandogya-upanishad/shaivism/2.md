The Saman meditations of Chapter 2 reveal the cosmos as a symphony of correspondences in which every natural phenomenon echoes the primordial vibration of Shiva's consciousness. The fivefold Saman structure — linking chant elements to earth and fire, atmosphere and wind, heaven and sun — maps the Shaiva understanding of creation as layers of Shiva's self-manifestation. Each level of reality is a *tattva* (category) through which consciousness progressively densifies into material form.

The teaching that the Saman has seven elements corresponding to the seven cosmic dimensions parallels the Shaiva system of tattvas, where reality unfolds in stages from pure consciousness to gross matter. The ritual chanter who understands these correspondences is practicing a form of *pratyabhijna* (recognition) — seeing Shiva's creative presence in every sound, every season, every breath.
