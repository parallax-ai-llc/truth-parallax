The opening chapter's meditation on the Udgitha as the essence of Om resonates with Shaiva teachings on *nada* — the primordial sound-vibration that is Shiva's creative self-expression. Kashmir Shaivism identifies Om with the *pranava*, the vibratory energy (*spanda*) that pulsates at the heart of all manifestation. The Chandogya's teaching that Om is the essence of all speech, all beings, and all Vedas (1.1) parallels the Shaiva understanding that all reality is the self-expression of Paramashiva through sound.

The parable of the gods and demons contesting the Udgitha, where only Prana remains undefeated, is read through the Shaiva lens as demonstrating that pure consciousness (*chiti*) cannot be overcome by limitation. While individual faculties can be corrupted, the fundamental awareness that underlies them — Shiva-consciousness — remains ever-pure and triumphant.
