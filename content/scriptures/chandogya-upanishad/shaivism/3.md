The Madhu Vidya's teaching that all the Vedas are honey collected by the bees of the sun resonates with the Shaiva understanding that all sacred knowledge is an expression of Shiva's light (*prakasha*). The sun as the cosmic source of illumination parallels Shiva as the light of consciousness that makes all knowing possible. The honey metaphor suggests that just as honey is the concentrated essence of countless flowers, all diverse teachings are concentrated expressions of the one consciousness.

The golden person (*purusha*) within the sun and within the heart (3.13-14) is read as Shiva — the luminous consciousness dwelling at the core of every being and at the center of the cosmos simultaneously. The Shaiva recognition that "I am Shiva" (*shivoham*) is enacted in the meditation on this inner golden being. The Gayatri mantra's invocation of the solar light is understood as a practice of recognizing Shiva's *prakasha* (luminosity) as the ground of all perception.
