## Overview

The Ramayana, attributed to the sage Valmiki, is one of the two great Indian epics (alongside the Mahabharata). Consisting of approximately 24,000 verses (*shlokas*) in seven books (*kandas*), it narrates the life of Prince Rama of Ayodhya — his exile, the abduction of his wife Sita by the demon king Ravana, and the war to rescue her. Far more than a heroic narrative, the Ramayana serves as a treatise on dharma, presenting Rama as the ideal man (*maryada purushottama*) and embodiment of righteous conduct.

## Structure

The seven books trace Rama's journey from birth to apotheosis:

1. **Bala Kanda (Youth)**: Rama's birth, education, and marriage to Sita
2. **Ayodhya Kanda (Ayodhya)**: The court intrigue leading to Rama's fourteen-year exile
3. **Aranya Kanda (Forest)**: Life in the forest and Sita's abduction by Ravana
4. **Kishkindha Kanda (Monkey Kingdom)**: Alliance with the monkey king Sugriva and Hanuman
5. **Sundara Kanda (Beautiful)**: Hanuman's leap to Lanka and discovery of Sita
6. **Yuddha Kanda (War)**: The great battle against Ravana and rescue of Sita
7. **Uttara Kanda (Epilogue)**: Rama's reign, Sita's trial, and final departure

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Ramayana resonates across Hindu traditions and far beyond India:

- **Vaishnavism**: Rama as an avatar of Vishnu and supreme object of devotion
- **Shaivism**: Rama as the ideal devotee of Shiva, with the Ramayana reflecting Shaiva values
- **Advaita Vedanta**: The epic as allegory for the soul's journey from ignorance to self-knowledge
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Rama's sovereignty illustrates God's absolute independence and supremacy
- **Academic**: A foundational text for studying Indian literature, culture, and religious history
- **Comparative**: The Ramayana tradition across Southeast Asia offers rich comparative material

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Dharma | Righteous conduct under extreme circumstances |
| Bhakti | Devotion exemplified by Hanuman's selfless service |
| Exile and Return | The archetypal journey of loss, trial, and restoration |
| Ideal Kingship | Rama Rajya as the model of just governance |
| Feminine Virtue | Sita's steadfastness and the contested politics of her trials |
