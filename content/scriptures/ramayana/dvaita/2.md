The Dvaita reading of Ayodhya Kanda emphasizes Rama's sovereignty (*svatantrya*) even in apparent adversity. Rama does not accept exile because he is powerless to resist but because he freely chooses to honor dharma. This voluntary submission demonstrates the Dvaita principle that God's freedom includes the freedom to accept limitation for a higher purpose. Rama's independence is most fully displayed not in acts of power but in his ability to act rightly under any circumstance.

Madhva's followers pay particular attention to the different characters' responses to Rama's exile as illustrations of the *taratamya* (spiritual hierarchy). Lakshmana's immediate, unquestioning decision to accompany Rama reflects the highest devotion. Bharata's anguished rejection of the throne demonstrates profound love. Dasharatha's death from grief shows the overwhelming power of attachment — even noble attachment — to God's incarnate form. Each response reveals the soul's innate nature and rank, confirming the Dvaita teaching that different souls are endowed with different spiritual capacities.
