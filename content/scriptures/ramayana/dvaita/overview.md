## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta, following Madhvacharya, reads the Ramayana as a historically real account of Lord Vishnu's incarnation as Rama. The events of the epic are not allegorical but actual demonstrations of God's sovereignty, justice, and grace in the material world. Madhva's tradition emphasizes that Rama's actions reveal the five eternal differences (*pancha-bheda*) that structure reality — especially the absolute difference between God and all other beings.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Madhva's Realism
Madhva and his followers insist on the literal reality of the Ramayana's events. Rama's exile, the war against Ravana, and the rescue of Sita are genuine historical events that demonstrate divine power and purpose. The miraculous elements are real exercises of God's omnipotence.

### Hierarchical Reading
The Dvaita tradition reads the Ramayana's characters as exemplifying the *taratamya* (hierarchy) of souls. Hanuman, as an incarnation of Vayu (the wind god and Madhva's own prior incarnation), occupies a uniquely elevated position in the spiritual hierarchy.

## Theological Emphases

- **Rama's Svatantrya**: Rama's independence and sovereignty in every situation demonstrates God's absolute freedom
- **Real Events**: The epic narrates genuine historical events, not metaphorical or mythological narratives
- **Soul Hierarchy**: Different characters attain different spiritual outcomes based on their innate nature
- **Grace**: Rama's protection of those who surrender to Him demonstrates the primacy of divine grace
