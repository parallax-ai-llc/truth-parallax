Dvaita Vedanta reads Aranya Kanda's events as real occurrences that reveal the hierarchical structure of creation. Rama's protection of the forest sages demonstrates God's commitment to supporting those engaged in genuine spiritual practice. The encounters with demons like Viradha and Khara illustrate the real conflict between divine and demonic forces that structures Dvaita cosmology — a conflict that is not allegorical but ontologically fundamental.

Sita's abduction is understood in the Dvaita tradition as a genuine event in which Ravana, driven by his inherently *tamasa* (dark) nature, commits the supreme act of defiance against God. The golden deer episode is not illusion but the real use of demonic power (*maya* in the sense of magical ability) by Maricha. Sita's vulnerability, despite being Lakshmi incarnate, demonstrates that even the highest beings are subject to the conditions of incarnate existence when God permits it for His larger purposes. Jatayu's heroic death fighting Ravana is particularly honored in the Dvaita tradition as exemplifying the soul that fights for God with its full, innate capacity.
