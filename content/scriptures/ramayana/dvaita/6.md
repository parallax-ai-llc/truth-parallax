Yuddha Kanda is the ultimate demonstration of God's sovereignty and justice in the Dvaita reading. The great war is a real cosmic event in which divine and demonic forces clash, and the outcome is determined by God's will alone. Vibhishana's defection to Rama illustrates the Dvaita principle that souls of inherently *sattvic* (pure) nature will eventually orient themselves toward God, even if born into adverse circumstances. Rama's acceptance of Vibhishana demonstrates that God receives all who sincerely surrender.

The climactic battle between Rama and Ravana is the confrontation between the independently real Supreme Being and the most powerful of the *tamasa* (darkly natured) souls. Ravana's defeat is inevitable — not because of Rama's martial skill alone but because God's power is infinite while the soul's power, however great, is always finite and dependent. The Dvaita tradition reads every detail of the battle — the use of divine weapons, the intervention of celestial beings, the final destruction of Ravana with the *brahmastra* — as confirming the absolute gap between God's unlimited independence and the creature's inherent limitation.
