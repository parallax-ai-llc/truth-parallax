The Shaiva reading of Ayodhya Kanda focuses on the theme of renunciation (*vairagya*), which is central to Shaiva spiritual life. Rama's calm acceptance of exile — leaving behind kingdom, comfort, and power — mirrors the Shaiva ideal of the ascetic who renounces worldly attachments to discover the inner kingdom of consciousness. Rama becomes, in this reading, the supreme renunciant who demonstrates that true sovereignty is internal.

The forest exile is understood as a passage from the outer world of social roles and obligations to the inner world of spiritual awakening. Shaiva interpreters note that the forest (*aranya*) is traditionally the domain of Shiva — the god of wild places, ascetics, and those who dwell outside conventional society. Rama's entry into the forest thus symbolizes his entry into the realm of Shiva's transformative power. The suffering of Dasharatha, Kausalya, and the citizens of Ayodhya represents the pain of attachment that the spiritual seeker must move beyond.
