Yuddha Kanda's great battle receives a multivalent Shaiva reading. The construction of the bridge to Lanka by the *vanara* army symbolizes the building of a path between the ordinary world and the realm of illusion where Shakti is held captive. The war itself is the inner battle (*antaryuddha*) between the forces of awareness and the forces of ignorance, a theme central to tantric Shaiva practice.

A pivotal episode for the Shaiva reading is Rama's worship of Shiva at Rameshwaram before crossing to Lanka. This act of devotion — the avatar of Vishnu worshipping Shiva — is understood not as subordination but as the recognition that all divine forms are expressions of one supreme consciousness. The Shiva lingam established by Rama at Rameshwaram remains one of the holiest Shaiva pilgrimage sites, embodying the unity of Rama and Shiva. The destruction of Ravana represents the final annihilation of the ego-principle, after which consciousness and Shakti are reunited.
