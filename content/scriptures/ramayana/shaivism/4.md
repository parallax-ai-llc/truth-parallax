Shaiva interpreters read Kishkindha Kanda's alliance between Rama and the *vanaras* as illustrating the universal accessibility of divine grace. The monkey kingdom, existing outside Brahmanical social structures, represents the Shaiva principle that spiritual realization is available to all beings regardless of birth or convention. Shiva is *Pashupati* — lord of all creatures — and His grace extends to every form of life.

The conflict between Sugriva and Vali, and Rama's controversial killing of Vali from concealment, receives a distinctive Shaiva interpretation. Rather than focusing on the ethical debate, Shaiva commentators read Vali's death as the destruction of pride (*ahankara*) by the hidden power of consciousness. Vali's extraordinary strength, which automatically absorbed half the power of any opponent, represents the ego's capacity to co-opt every challenge into its own reinforcement. Only the unexpected arrow of grace, arriving from beyond the ego's visual field, can destroy this self-reinforcing pattern.
