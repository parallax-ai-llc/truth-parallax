Sundara Kanda is considered the most auspicious book of the Ramayana in the Vaishnava tradition, and its recitation is believed to bring divine blessings. Hanuman's leap across the ocean to Lanka is the supreme demonstration of what devotion can accomplish. His extraordinary physical feat is powered entirely by his love for Rama — he himself declares that he crosses the ocean not by his own strength but by the strength of Rama's name.

Hanuman's discovery of Sita in the Ashoka grove, his delivery of Rama's ring as a token of reassurance, and his message of hope are read as allegories of the guru or divine messenger who brings God's grace to the suffering soul. Sita's unwavering faith in Rama despite her captivity exemplifies the steadfastness required of the devotee in adversity. Hanuman's subsequent burning of Lanka demonstrates that divine power, when channeled through devotion, can overcome any obstacle. Vaishnavas recite this kanda particularly for the removal of difficulties and the strengthening of faith.
