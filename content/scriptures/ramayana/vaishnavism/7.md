Uttara Kanda presents significant theological challenges for Vaishnava interpreters. Rama's banishment of the pregnant Sita based on public rumor is the most debated episode in the entire Ramayana. Some Vaishnava commentators argue that Rama acted according to his duty as king (*raja dharma*), placing public welfare above personal desire. Others, particularly in the Gaudiya tradition, question the authenticity of this kanda, noting that its tone and theology differ markedly from the core narrative.

Despite these difficulties, Vaishnavas find profound meaning in Uttara Kanda's conclusion. Sita's final vindication — the earth itself receiving her as a testimony of her purity — demonstrates that divine truth ultimately triumphs over worldly judgment. Rama's eventual departure from the earthly realm, walking into the Sarayu River and returning to his eternal form as Vishnu, confirms the Vaishnava teaching that the avatar's earthly career is a voluntary, temporary descent. The Lord's departure does not diminish his presence, for Vaishnavas, he remains eternally accessible through devotion, scripture, and the sacred name.
