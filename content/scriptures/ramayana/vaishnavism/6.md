Yuddha Kanda's great battle between Rama's forces and Ravana's armies is the fulfillment of the incarnation's purpose — the destruction of adharma and the restoration of cosmic order. Vaishnavas read every battle sequence as the triumph of divine grace over demonic pride. Vibhishana's defection from Ravana to Rama models the Vaishnava principle that surrender to God (*sharanagati*) is available even to those born in adverse circumstances.

Rama's acceptance of Vibhishana — "He who comes to me in friendship, to him I give assurance of safety" — is one of the most quoted verses in Vaishnava theology, establishing the unconditional nature of divine refuge. The climactic battle with Ravana demonstrates that evil, however powerful, cannot ultimately prevail against the Lord. Rama's use of the *brahmastra* to slay Ravana is not merely a military victory but a cosmic act of purification. The reunion of Rama and Sita and the triumphant return to Ayodhya celebrate the restoration of dharma and the establishment of Rama Rajya — the ideal kingdom.
