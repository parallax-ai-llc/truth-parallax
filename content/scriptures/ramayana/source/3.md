---
title: "Ramayana Aranya Kanda (Book 3)"
scripture: "ramayana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana wandered through the great Dandaka forest, visiting the hermitages of sages and protecting them from the demons who terrorized the wilderness.

**2** At the hermitage of Agastya, the great sage gave Rama the bow of Vishnu, an inexhaustible quiver, and a divine sword. He said: "O Rama, take these weapons. You shall need them, for great trials lie ahead."

**3** They established their dwelling at Panchavati, on the banks of the river Godavari. There Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana lived in peace, subsisting on fruits and roots, devoted to prayer and the duties of forest life.

**4** Shurpanakha, the sister of Ravana, came to Panchavati and saw Rama. She was overcome with desire. She assumed a beautiful form and approached him, saying: "O handsome one, I am a princess. Be my husband."

**5** Rama, smiling, said: "I am already married. Go to my brother Lakshmana." Lakshmana, understanding the jest, sent her back to Rama. Enraged at being mocked, Shurpanakha attacked Sita in her true demonic form.

**6** Lakshmana, on Rama's command, drew his sword and cut off Shurpanakha's nose and ears. She fled, howling, to her brother Khara, who led fourteen thousand demons against Rama. Rama alone destroyed them all in a single battle.

**7** Shurpanakha then went to Lanka and told her brother Ravana of her humiliation and of Sita's extraordinary beauty. Ravana, the ten-headed king of the demons, resolved to abduct Sita and make her his queen.

**8** Ravana enlisted the help of the demon Maricha, who assumed the form of a golden deer and appeared near the hermitage. Sita, enchanted by the deer, begged Rama to capture it for her. Rama, though suspicious, went after it.

**9** Maricha, struck by Rama's arrow, cried out in Rama's voice: "O Sita! O Lakshmana!" Sita, hearing this cry, was terrified and urged Lakshmana to go to Rama's aid. Lakshmana protested that Rama could not be in danger, but Sita's harsh words drove him away.

**10** Before departing, Lakshmana drew a line around the hermitage — the Lakshman Rekha — and said: "O Sita, do not cross this line. As long as you remain within it, no harm can befall you."

**11** Ravana appeared at the hermitage in the guise of a wandering ascetic and asked Sita for alms. When she crossed the line to offer him food, he seized her in his arms, assumed his true form, and carried her away through the sky in his aerial chariot.

**12** Sita cried out: "O Rama! O Lakshmana!" The great vulture Jatayu, an old friend of King Dasharatha, heard her cries and attacked Ravana in the sky. He fought bravely but Ravana cut off his wings, and Jatayu fell to earth mortally wounded.

**13** Sita, as she was carried over the mountains, dropped her ornaments, hoping to leave a trail for Rama. She was taken to Lanka, the island kingdom of the demons, and imprisoned in a grove of Ashoka trees. Ravana gave her one year to submit to him or face death.

**14** Rama, returning to find the hermitage empty, was overcome with anguish. He found the dying Jatayu, who told him that Ravana had taken Sita toward the south. Rama performed the funeral rites for the noble bird and set out in search of his beloved wife.
