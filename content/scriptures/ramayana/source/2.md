---
title: "Ramayana Ayodhya Kanda (Book 2)"
scripture: "ramayana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** King Dasharatha, grown old, resolved to install Rama as the crown prince and heir to the throne. The announcement filled all of Ayodhya with joy. The city was decorated, and preparations began for the great consecration ceremony.

**2** But Manthara, the hunchbacked maid of Queen Kaikeyi, poisoned her mistress's mind. She said: "O queen, if Rama is crowned, your son Bharata will be reduced to a servant. You must act now to save your son's future."

**3** Kaikeyi, overcome by Manthara's venom, remembered the two boons Dasharatha had once granted her when she saved his life in battle. She went to the anger-chamber and lay upon the floor, determined to use those boons.

**4** When Dasharatha came to her, Kaikeyi demanded: "Let Bharata be crowned king in Rama's place, and let Rama be exiled to the forest for fourteen years. These are my two boons. If you are a man of truth, fulfill your word."

**5** Dasharatha was struck as if by a thunderbolt. He pleaded, he wept, he raged, he fell at Kaikeyi's feet. But she was immovable. "A king must keep his word," she said. "Or all the world will know that the king of Ayodhya is a liar."

**6** Rama, when told of the exile, showed no anger and no grief. He said: "I shall gladly go to the forest for fourteen years. My father's word must be honored. A son's duty is to obey his father, even when the command is unjust."

**7** Sita said to Rama: "Where you go, I go. The forest with you is heaven; Ayodhya without you is hell. A wife's place is beside her husband, whether in a palace or in the wilderness."

**8** Lakshmana declared: "I shall accompany my brother to the forest. I shall serve as his protector and companion. Without Rama, Ayodhya is a desert to me." And so the three prepared to depart.

**9** Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana departed Ayodhya on a chariot driven by Sumantra. The entire city came out weeping. The people cried: "We shall follow Rama! A city without Rama is no city at all!" But Rama bade them return and care for his aged father.

**10** Dasharatha, watching Rama's chariot disappear, collapsed in grief. He cried out: "O Rama, O my son!" Remembering an old curse — that he would die of grief separated from his beloved son — he knew his end was near.

**11** On the sixth night after Rama's departure, King Dasharatha died of a broken heart, calling Rama's name with his last breath. Kausalya and Sumitra wept beside him, and all of Ayodhya was plunged into darkness.

**12** Bharata, summoned from his grandfather's kingdom, returned to find his father dead and his brother exiled. He was filled with rage at his mother Kaikeyi. He said: "You have destroyed our family! I did not desire this kingdom. I shall go to the forest and bring Rama back."

**13** Bharata journeyed to the forest of Chitrakuta where Rama dwelt. He fell at Rama's feet and begged him to return. But Rama refused: "I have given my word to our father. I shall complete the fourteen years. Take the kingdom and rule with justice."

**14** Bharata said: "I shall not sit upon the throne. I shall place your sandals upon it and rule as your regent until you return." He took Rama's wooden sandals, placed them on the throne of Ayodhya, and governed from the village of Nandigrama, living as an ascetic until Rama's return.
