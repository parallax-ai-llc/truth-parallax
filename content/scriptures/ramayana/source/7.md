---
title: "Ramayana Uttara Kanda (Book 7)"
scripture: "ramayana"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Rama ruled Ayodhya with perfect justice. During his reign, known as Rama Rajya, there was no disease, no drought, no crime, and no sorrow. Every citizen was content, and dharma prevailed throughout the land.

**2** But rumors spread among the people that Sita had lived in another man's house for many months, and some questioned her purity despite the fire ordeal. A washerman quarreling with his wife said: "I am not like Rama, who took back a wife who had lived with another."

**3** When these words reached Rama, he was torn between his love for Sita and his duty as a king. He said to Lakshmana: "A king must be above reproach. The people must have no cause to question the honor of the royal house. Take Sita to the forest and leave her at the hermitage of Valmiki."

**4** Lakshmana, weeping, carried out his brother's command. Sita, who was with child, was left at the hermitage of the sage Valmiki. She did not curse Rama. She said: "If Rama believes this is his duty, I accept it. A wife must support her husband's dharma."

**5** In the hermitage of Valmiki, Sita gave birth to twin sons, Lava and Kusha. The sage raised them and taught them the entire Ramayana, which he had composed through divine inspiration. The boys grew into accomplished warriors and scholars.

**6** Rama performed the Ashvamedha sacrifice. During the ceremony, two young boys appeared and sang the Ramayana before the assembled court with voices of extraordinary beauty. Rama recognized in them the features of his own face.

**7** Valmiki brought Sita before the assembly and declared: "O Rama, these are your sons. And Sita is as pure as the day she was born from the earth. I speak this truth by the merit of my ten thousand years of austerity."

**8** Rama said: "I have never doubted Sita's purity. But let her prove it before the assembly so that the world may be satisfied." The people called for her to undergo another trial.

**9** Sita, weary of proving her virtue again and again, spoke to the earth: "If I have been faithful to Rama in thought, word, and deed, then O mother Earth, receive me back into your bosom."

**10** The earth split open, and a golden throne rose up, carried by the serpent kings. The goddess Earth seated Sita upon the throne, and together they descended into the earth. The ground closed above them, and Sita was gone.

**11** Rama cried out in anguish and begged the earth to return his wife. But Brahma appeared and said: "O Rama, do not grieve. You shall be reunited with Sita in your eternal abode. Your task on earth draws to its close."

**12** Rama ruled for many more years, but his heart was never whole after Sita's departure. When the time came, he walked into the river Sarayu, accompanied by the people of Ayodhya who would not be separated from him. He laid down his mortal form and returned to his divine abode as Vishnu, where Sita awaited him as Lakshmi, and they were united for all eternity.

**13** Valmiki declared: "He who reads this Ramayana, the story of Rama, is freed from all sins. He who listens to it with devotion obtains long life, prosperity, and happiness. The Ramayana endures as long as the mountains stand and the rivers flow upon the earth."
