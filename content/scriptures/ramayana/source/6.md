---
title: "Ramayana Yuddha Kanda (Book 6)"
scripture: "ramayana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Rama marched to the southern shore with the vast army of monkeys and bears. The ocean lay before them, impassable and vast. Rama fasted and prayed to the ocean god for three days, but received no answer.

**2** In anger, Rama raised the Brahmastra to dry up the ocean. The ocean god appeared in terror and said: "O Rama, I cannot alter my nature, but Nala, the son of Vishvakarma, can build a bridge across me. I shall support it."

**3** Nala built the bridge to Lanka in five days. The monkeys carried mountains and trees and hurled them into the sea. The bridge stretched one hundred yojanas across the waters, and Rama's army crossed to the shores of Lanka.

**4** Ravana's brother Vibhishana, a righteous demon, came to Rama seeking refuge. He said: "O Rama, I have counseled Ravana to return Sita, but he will not listen. I abandon my brother and seek your protection." Rama accepted him and promised him the throne of Lanka.

**5** The siege of Lanka began. The monkey army attacked the golden walls, and the demons poured forth in thousands. Battle after battle raged, and the earth was drenched with the blood of monkeys and demons alike.

**6** Indrajit, the son of Ravana, used the Nagastra, the serpent weapon, and bound Rama and Lakshmana in a mass of venomous snakes. They lay upon the ground as if dead. But Garuda, the divine eagle, came and devoured the serpents, and the brothers were freed.

**7** Kumbhakarna, the giant brother of Ravana, who slept for six months at a time, was awakened for the war. He strode onto the battlefield like a moving mountain and devoured monkeys by the thousands. Rama fought him in a terrible duel and severed his head with the Aindra weapon.

**8** Indrajit performed a sacrifice to become invisible and attacked the monkey army with devastating weapons. He struck Lakshmana with the Shakti weapon, and the prince fell mortally wounded. Hanuman flew to the Himalayas and, unable to identify the healing herb, carried the entire mountain of Dronagiri back to Lanka. The herb Vishalyakarani restored Lakshmana to life.

**9** Rama and Ravana met in the final battle. Their combat shook the three worlds. Ravana, with his ten heads and twenty arms, fought with the fury of the cosmic dissolution, wielding weapons given to him by Brahma and Shiva.

**10** Rama, guided by the sage Agastya, chanted the Aditya Hridayam, the hymn to the Sun, to gain divine strength. Then he took up the Brahmastra, the weapon of Brahma, and released it at Ravana. The arrow blazed through the sky and pierced Ravana through the chest.

**11** Ravana, the lord of the three worlds, the conqueror of the gods, fell from his chariot and lay upon the earth. As his life ebbed, a divine light issued from his body, for even in his wickedness he had been a great devotee of Shiva and a master of the Vedas.

**12** Sita was brought forth from captivity. But Rama said: "O Sita, I have rescued you for the sake of my honor, not merely from affection. You have lived in another man's house. How can I accept you without proof of your purity?"

**13** Sita, with dignity and anguish, ordered a funeral pyre to be built. She walked into the flames, saying: "If I have been faithful to Rama in thought, word, and deed, let Agni protect me." The fire god Agni rose from the pyre bearing Sita unharmed, testifying to her absolute purity.

**14** Rama embraced Sita with tears. He said: "I never doubted you, O beloved. But the world needed to witness your purity. You are as pure as the flame itself." The gods appeared and praised Rama, and he was reunited with Sita.

**15** Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana flew back to Ayodhya in the aerial chariot Pushpaka. As they approached the city, the fourteen years of exile had ended on that very day. Bharata met them with joy beyond measure, and Rama was crowned king of Ayodhya in a ceremony of unparalleled splendor.
