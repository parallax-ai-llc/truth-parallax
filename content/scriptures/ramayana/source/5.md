---
title: "Ramayana Sundara Kanda (Book 5)"
scripture: "ramayana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Hanuman, reminded of his divine power, expanded his body to enormous size. He climbed to the summit of Mount Mahendra and, with a mighty leap, launched himself across the ocean toward Lanka.

**2** As Hanuman flew over the sea, the mountain Mainaka rose from the ocean and offered him rest. Hanuman touched the peak in courtesy but would not pause: "I shall not rest until my mission is accomplished."

**3** The demoness Surasa opened her mouth to swallow Hanuman. He shrank to the size of a thumb, darted into her mouth and out again in an instant. Surasa blessed him: "Go, O noble one. You shall succeed in your mission."

**4** The shadow-grasping demoness Simhika seized Hanuman's shadow from below the waves. He struck her down and continued his flight. At last the golden city of Lanka rose before him, magnificent beyond description.

**5** Hanuman reduced himself to the size of a cat and entered Lanka by night. He searched the palaces, the gardens, and the inner chambers of Ravana. He saw Ravana sleeping in his bedchamber, surrounded by his wives, but Sita was not among them.

**6** At last Hanuman found Sita in the Ashoka grove, seated beneath a Shimsapa tree, thin and pale from grief, surrounded by fierce demonesses who guarded her day and night. She wore a single garment and her hair was unadorned.

**7** Ravana came to the grove and urged Sita to accept him. He said: "O beautiful one, forget Rama. He is a mortal. He cannot cross the ocean or defeat my armies. Become my queen and all Lanka shall be yours."

**8** Sita replied: "O wretch, you are like a hare that desires the moon. I am the wife of Rama, the mighty-armed, the destroyer of demons. He shall come for me, and your death approaches with his coming."

**9** After Ravana departed, Sita despaired and considered ending her life. At that moment, Hanuman, sitting in the branches above, began to softly chant the story of Rama. Sita looked up in wonder.

**10** Hanuman descended and showed Sita the signet ring of Rama. She was overcome with joy and tears. Hanuman said: "O queen, Rama sends this ring as proof. He grieves for you day and night. Soon he shall come with a mighty army and rescue you."

**11** Sita gave Hanuman her crest-jewel, the Chudamani, to carry back to Rama as proof that he had found her. She said: "Tell Rama that I shall wait for him. My life endures only because of my faith in his coming."

**12** Before departing, Hanuman wished to test the strength of the demons. He ravaged the Ashoka grove, slew many warriors, and allowed himself to be captured and brought before Ravana. The demons set fire to his tail, but Hanuman broke free and leaped from rooftop to rooftop, setting all of Lanka ablaze.

**13** Hanuman then leaped back across the ocean with a shout of triumph: "I have seen Sita! She lives!" The monkeys rejoiced, and they all hastened back to Rama with the news. When Rama received the Chudamani, he pressed it to his heart and wept tears of joy and sorrow.
