---
title: "Ramayana Kishkindha Kanda (Book 4)"
scripture: "ramayana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Rama and Lakshmana, searching for Sita, came to the shores of Lake Pampa and then to the mountain Rishyamuka, where the monkey chief Sugriva lived in exile, having been expelled by his brother Vali.

**2** Hanuman, the minister of Sugriva and the son of Vayu the wind god, was sent to meet the strangers. He assumed the form of a Brahmin student and approached Rama. Seeing Rama's divine form, he was filled with devotion and revealed his true identity.

**3** Hanuman brought Rama and Lakshmana to Sugriva. An alliance was formed: Rama would slay Vali and restore Sugriva's kingdom, and in return Sugriva would help Rama find Sita with his vast army of monkeys.

**4** Sugriva showed Rama the ornaments that Sita had dropped from the sky. Rama recognized them and wept. He said: "These are Sita's jewels. I know them as the stars know the sky. O Sugriva, help me find my wife, and I shall be forever in your debt."

**5** Rama challenged Vali to combat through Sugriva. While the two monkey brothers fought, Rama hid behind a tree and shot Vali with an arrow. Vali fell, mortally wounded, and rebuked Rama for this seemingly unrighteous act.

**6** Rama replied: "O Vali, you have sinned by taking your brother's wife and kingdom. A king has the right to punish wrongdoers, whether they come before him or not. I acted as the instrument of dharma."

**7** Vali accepted Rama's judgment and died in peace, entrusting his son Angada to Rama's care. Sugriva was crowned king of Kishkindha and promised to send search parties in every direction to find Sita.

**8** But Sugriva, intoxicated by his recovered kingdom and pleasures, forgot his promise. The rainy season passed, and no search had been made. Lakshmana, sent by Rama, came to Kishkindha in great wrath.

**9** Lakshmana said: "O Sugriva, my brother trusted you and fulfilled his part of the alliance. Yet you sit here indulging in pleasures while Sita languishes in captivity. If you do not act now, the arrows of Rama shall find you as they found Vali."

**10** Sugriva, humbled and ashamed, assembled millions of monkeys and bears and dispatched them in four directions. To the southern party, led by Angada and Hanuman, he gave special instructions, for all signs pointed southward.

**11** The southern party searched far and wide but found no trace of Sita. As they sat despairing by the ocean, the aged vulture Sampati, brother of Jatayu, told them that he had seen Ravana carrying Sita across the sea to Lanka, one hundred yojanas away.

**12** The monkeys were dismayed, for who could cross the vast ocean? One by one they declared their abilities, but none could leap the full distance. Then Jambavan the bear king turned to Hanuman and said: "O son of the wind, you alone have the power to cross the ocean. Remember your strength, for you were born to accomplish this deed."
