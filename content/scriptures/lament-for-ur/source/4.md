**Section IV: The Destruction**

**1** The storm, like a flood, devoured the city.
**2** The Lament for Ur—the storm devoured.
**3** The people mourn.

**4** The great storm howled above—the people mourn.
**5** The storm which knows no mother, the storm which knows no father,
**6** the storm which knows no wife, the storm which knows no child,
**7** the storm which knows no sister, the storm which knows no brother,
**8** the storm which knows no neighbour, the storm which knows no companion—

**9** the storm devoured Ur.

**10** Large boats carrying grain were swamped.
**11** The storm tore the mooring of the boats of the city.

**12** The storm ordered by Enlil in hate,
**13** the storm which wears away the land,
**14** covered Ur like a garment, wrapped it like a linen sheet.

**15** Ur, which had been built on joy—
**16** its joy was turned to lament.
**17** Ur, which had been built on prosperity—
**18** its prosperity was turned to distress.

**19** Its people who had filled the feasting places were overthrown.
**20** Its young men, who had paraded the streets, were laid low.
**21** The song of the city was turned into weeping.
**22** Where there was feasting, now there was the dust of ruin.

**23** Its trees were scorched by fire.
**24** Its plantations were felled like cucumbers.
**25** Its strong ones were cast down.

**26** Ur—its weak and its strong perished through hunger.
**27** Mothers and fathers who did not leave their houses were overcome by fire.
**28** The young lying in their mothers' laps—
**29** like fish were carried off by the waters.

**30** In the sturdy nurseries the children lay scattered.
**31** The city's song of motherhood was silenced.
**32** The city's children were laid waste, like broken pottery.
