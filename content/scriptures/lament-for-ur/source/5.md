**Section V: The Desolate City**

**1** O city, a bitter lament set up as your lament.
**2** Your lament is a bitter lament. O city, set up your lament.
**3** His righteous city which has been destroyed—bitter is its lament.
**4** His Ur which has been destroyed—bitter is its lament.

**5** Its people who filled its broad squares—where are they?
**6** Its people who filled its market places—where are they?
**7** O Nanna, the righteous city has been destroyed—its people are dead.

**8** Its refugees are unable to flee, unable to get away.
**9** Ur, like a hunted ox, has no place to go.

**10** The houses of the city have been delivered to the pickaxe.
**11** The walls of the city have been torn down.
**12** On the walls—where the ceremonies of An were performed—
**13** an unkindly eye has been cast.

**14** The temple has been delivered to the wind.
**15** The lofty gates, the places of wonder, have been torn down.
**16** The shrine Ekishnugal has been delivered to the wind.

**17** The Anunnaki, the lords of judgment,
**18** stepped outside their city.
**19** Nanna has abandoned Ur.

**20** The faithful shepherd has abandoned his sheepfold.
**21** The wild ox has left his stable.
**22** The mistress of the city has abandoned her city.
**23** Ningal has abandoned her beloved dwelling.

**24** The Elamite, the wicked foe, set up his dwelling in the city.
**25** The Elamite, the wicked foe, set up his dwelling in Ur.
**26** The guardian of Ur has been carried off.

**27** Its king sits immobilized in a foreign land.
**28** The elders of the land who gave counsel have been carried off.
**29** The wise women of the land who uttered speeches have been carried off.

**30** The singers have been carried off.
**31** The music of the city has been turned to discord.

**32** O Nanna, your city weeps before you.
**33** Like the children of a ruined city, your city seeks your aid.
