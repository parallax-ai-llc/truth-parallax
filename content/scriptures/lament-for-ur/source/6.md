**Section VI: The Temple Destroyed**

**1** Its temple, like the temple of a destroyed city, has been torn down.
**2** Its canals, like canals from which water has been withdrawn, carry no water.
**3** No barge howls at its quay;
**4** its towpath grows only weeds.

**5** No chariot rumbles along its highway;
**6** no wagon makes its way.
**7** Ur, at the place where the boats were launched,
**8** grass grows long.

**9** Ur, where the festival songs were sung,
**10** the people lie dead.
**11** No one walks in the streets;
**12** no one seeks the highways.

**13** Its canal-boat towpath has been destroyed;
**14** no barges traverse its course.
**15** Long grass grows on its path;
**16** the "lamenting reeds" grow on its waterway.

**17** No one tends the garden;
**18** no one plants the seed.
**19** Ur, where the barley grew tall,
**20** wild grass grows.

**21** Ur, where the rich harvest was garnered,
**22** thorns and brambles grow.
**23** Its fine trees, which stood in their glory, have been felled.
**24** Its garden hedges have been hacked down.

**25** The city that was filled with song—
**26** its song has been silenced.
**27** The city that was filled with merriment—
**28** its merriment has been stilled.

**29** The temple's lyre, which used to echo—
**30** its strings are cut.
**31** The song of the temple that once gave joy—
**32** it is no more.

**33** The temple drums that thundered—
**34** they are silent.
**35** The sweet singing of the ala-drums—
**36** it has been ended.

**37** Ur—the shrine has been given to the wind.
