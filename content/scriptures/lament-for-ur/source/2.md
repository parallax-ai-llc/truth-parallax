**Section II: The Storm Ordered**

**1** After An had frowned upon all the lands,
**2** after Enlil had looked favourably on an enemy land,
**3** after Ningal had been taken away from her city,
**4** after she had been removed from her temple,

**5** after the lady had been abducted from her house—
**6** the city was destroyed by the storm.
**7** The storm ordered by Enlil in hate, the storm which wears away the land,
**8** covered Ur like a garment, wrapped it like a linen sheet.

**9** On that day, the storm was removed from the city,
**10** that city was a ruin.
**11** O father Nanna, that city was a ruin;
**12** the people mourn.

**13** On that day, the storm left the city in ruins.
**14** Its people, not potsherds, littered the approaches.
**15** The walls were breached; the people mourn.
**16** In its lofty gate, where they were wont to promenade, dead bodies were piled.

**17** In its boulevards, where the feasts were celebrated, scattered they lay.
**18** In all its streets, where they were wont to promenade, dead bodies were piled.
**19** In its places where the festivities of the land took place, the people lay in heaps.

**20** The blood of the land filled its holes, like metal in a mould;
**21** bodies dissolved like fat left in the sun.
**22** Its men who were brought by the mace were not covered with cloth;
**23** like a gazelle trapped by the snare, their mouths bit the dust.

**24** Its men who were struck by the spear were not bandaged;
**25** as if in the place where their mothers had given birth to them,
**26** they lay in their own blood.
**27** Its men who were brought by the battle-axe were not bandaged—
**28** they lie about as drunkards, drenched in drink.

**29** He who was struck by the storm had no one to clasp his hand;
**30** he who was seized by the storm had no one to carry him.
