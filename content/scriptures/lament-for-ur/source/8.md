**Section VIII: Ningal's Lament**

**1** Woe is me! The city is destroyed.
**2** Woe is me! The house is destroyed.

**3** O Nanna, the city is destroyed; the people are dead.
**4** The people, not potsherds, filled its sides.
**5** Its walls have been destroyed; the people mourn.

**6** At its gate where the ala-drum used to stand, now there is desolation.
**7** The city gives no joy; there is no cause for celebration.

**8** Ningal spoke:
**9** "I am one whose house has been made desolate.
**10** I am the woman for whom all has been taken away.
**11** O Nanna, the city has been destroyed.
**12** I am one to whom no comfort has been given.

**13** Where I could say 'My dwelling!'—
**14** now I am a wretched exile.
**15** I, Ningal, am an exile from my city.
**16** The bird has its nest; I have no nest.

**17** The fish has its pool; I have no pool.
**18** The little mongoose has its den; I have no den.

**19** I am the queen, but my city has been destroyed.
**20** I am Ningal, but my house has been laid waste.
**21** O Nanna, the city which gave you joy has been destroyed.

**22** The sacred house of Ur—the enemy has laid it bare.
**23** The shrine Ekishnugal—the enemy has laid it bare.
**24** The dwellers of the house have been driven out.
**25** The treasures of the house have been scattered.

**26** The house which knew not the cry of mourning—
**27** mourning has been set up in it.
**28** The house which knew not the cry of distress—
**29** distress has been established in it."

**30** Her tears were endless. She would not be comforted.
**31** Her city—she could not cease the mourning of her city.
**32** Ningal, whose house had been laid to ruin, wept bitterly.
