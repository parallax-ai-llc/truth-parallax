**Section III: Ningal's Plea**

**1** After the storm had raged, after the people had mourned—
**2** Ningal, like a flying bird, left her city.
**3** Like a flying bird she left her city.

**4** "The storm has laid waste my city—
**5** my city is no more.
**6** The storm has laid waste my house—
**7** my house is no more.

**8** O Nanna, the sacred city has been destroyed,
**9** the people have been led to slaughter.
**10** In the place that was my delight,
**11** in the place where I bound my head-cloth,
**12** strangers walk."

**13** Before the storm had approached,
**14** Ningal pleaded with Anu and Enlil.
**15** She clutched at the garment of Anu:
**16** "O An, my father, change the destructive storm!
**17** Do not let the day be darkened by the flood!"

**18** She clutched at the garment of Enlil:
**19** "O Enlil, turn a favourable eye upon my city!
**20** Turn a favourable eye upon Ur!"

**21** But Anu did not change his word.
**22** Enlil did not soothe her heart with the words "It is enough."

**23** The destruction of Ur was decreed.
**24** Its fate was determined.
**25** Who could change it? Who could overturn it?

**26** The word of An and Enlil:
**27** who could oppose it?
**28** An frightened Ur's inhabitants from their homes.
**29** Enlil transformed its sweet lyre into discord.

**30** Ningal wept for her city.
**31** Before the day of the storm she wept bitterly.
**32** "That day, approaching me in its fury,
**33** laid its heavy hand upon me.

**34** It did not let me flee from its terror.
**35** On that day, though I trembled on account of the storm,
**36** the storm did not turn from me.

**37** The storm's fury crushed me;
**38** I could not sleep peacefully through the night,
**39** nor could the grief of the night bring me rest."
