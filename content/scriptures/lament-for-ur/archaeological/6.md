The institutional context of divine assembly decisions is reflected in the administrative archives from Nippur and other cities, where collective decisions by councils of elders are recorded in similar formal language. The legal terminology used in the assembly's rejection — terms for 'verdict,' 'decree,' and 'irrevocable judgment' — appears in both divine and human legal contexts, confirming that Mesopotamian theological language drew on institutional practice.
