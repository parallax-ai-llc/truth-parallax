The temples and shrines named in the first kirugu correspond to structures documented in the archaeological record at Ur. Leonard Woolley's excavations identified the Ekishnugal (temple of Nanna), the Giparu (residence of the high priestess), and other structures referenced in the text. The correlation between literary description and archaeological reality confirms that the lament draws on detailed knowledge of Ur's sacred topography.
