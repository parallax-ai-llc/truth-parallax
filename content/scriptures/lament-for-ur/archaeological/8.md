The agricultural infrastructure of the Ur III state has been extensively studied through administrative texts. Tens of thousands of documents recording grain production, canal maintenance, livestock management, and labor allocation have been recovered from sites across southern Mesopotamia. The collapse of this bureaucratic and agricultural system — as described in the eighth kirugu — would have been visible in the archaeological record as a sudden cessation of administrative activity, consistent with the textual evidence.
