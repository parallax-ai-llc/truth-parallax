The practice of ritual mourning over destroyed sites is attested in Mesopotamian textual and material evidence. Inscriptions from the Isin dynasty describe kings visiting the ruins of Ur and performing restoration rituals. The ninth kirugu may reflect an actual liturgical practice in which a priestess representing Ningal processed through the ruins as part of a ceremony of lamentation and, eventually, restoration.
