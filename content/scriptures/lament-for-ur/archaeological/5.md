Ningal was worshipped at Ur as the consort of Nanna and was served by a high priestess (*entu*) who resided in the Giparu complex. Archaeological evidence of the Giparu — including dedicatory inscriptions and administrative texts — confirms Ningal's prominent position in Ur's cult. The high priestess Enheduanna (daughter of Sargon of Akkad), the world's first known author, was an earlier occupant of this office, linking the lament tradition to a centuries-long history of female religious authority at Ur.
