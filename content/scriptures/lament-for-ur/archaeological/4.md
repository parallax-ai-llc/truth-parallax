Woolley's excavations at Ur documented the Ekishnugal's architectural history across multiple periods. The Ur III-period temple showed evidence of damage consistent with the events described in the lament, followed by rebuilding under the Isin dynasty. The archaeological record confirms that the temple was not totally destroyed — its massive mud-brick core survived — but was damaged, looted, and temporarily abandoned, consistent with the poem's description.
