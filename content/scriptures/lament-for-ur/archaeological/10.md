The Isin dynasty, which succeeded the Ur III dynasty, actively promoted the restoration of Ur's temples as part of its legitimation strategy. Archaeological evidence confirms rebuilding activity at the Ekishnugal and other structures under Isin rulers, particularly Ishme-Dagan. The tenth kirugu's petition thus reflects a historical reality: the lament was composed in a context where restoration was not merely hoped for but politically and architecturally underway.
