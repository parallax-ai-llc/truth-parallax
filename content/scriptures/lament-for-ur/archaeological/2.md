The concept of the divine assembly is reflected in Mesopotamian temple architecture, where large courtyards may have hosted assemblies mirroring the divine model. At Nippur, the Ekur (temple of Enlil) served as the symbolic meeting place of the gods, and administrative texts use assembly language for both divine and human deliberative bodies. The second kirugu thus draws on institutional realities familiar to its ancient audience.
