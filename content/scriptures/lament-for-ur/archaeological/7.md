Archaeological evidence from Ur's destruction levels confirms the kinds of devastation the seventh kirugu describes. Woolley documented destruction layers with evidence of burning, collapsed structures, and signs of violent disruption to daily life. While individual bodies are rarely preserved in the archaeological record at these levels, the material evidence of sudden, violent disruption is consistent with the text's account.
