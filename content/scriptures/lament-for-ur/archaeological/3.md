Southern Mesopotamia is susceptible to severe storms, including sandstorms and the intense weather systems of the Persian Gulf. Archaeological evidence at Ur includes layers of wind-blown sand between occupation levels, consistent with periods of abandonment and exposure. The poem's storm imagery, while clearly theological in intent, is grounded in genuine environmental experience. Woolley documented evidence of violent destruction at the end of the Ur III period consistent with both military assault and environmental degradation.
