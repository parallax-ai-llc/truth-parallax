The fourth kirugu narrows the poem's focus from the city as a whole to its most sacred spaces. This literary zoom — from panoramic destruction to intimate devastation — heightens the emotional impact. The listing of specific sacred objects and spaces creates an inventory of loss, each named item carrying associations of worship, memory, and divine presence now extinguished. The technique transforms a catalogue of things into a catalogue of meanings.
