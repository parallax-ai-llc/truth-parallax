The eighth kirugu shifts the poem's setting from the urban to the pastoral, expanding the scope of devastation beyond the city walls. This geographic expansion creates a sense of total loss: not only the center but the periphery, not only the buildings but the fields. The pastoral imagery — empty sheepfolds, untended gardens, dry canals — carries a particular poetic resonance, evoking the absence of what should be present, the silence where there should be sound.
