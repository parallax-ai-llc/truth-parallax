The fifth kirugu marks the poem's emotional center, shifting from third-person narration to first-person speech. Ningal's voice — passionate, desperate, eloquent — introduces a human (or divine-human) perspective on the catastrophe that the earlier kiruguS described from a cosmic distance. The literary effect is immersive: the audience hears not about grief but grief itself, spoken in the voice of a goddess who is also a mother mourning her children.
