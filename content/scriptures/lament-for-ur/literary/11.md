The eleventh kirugu brings the poem to a close with a shift from the past tense of destruction to the future tense of hope. The final lines create a sense of openness — the poem does not describe a restored city but points toward one, leaving the fulfillment to the future. This open ending is a sophisticated literary choice: by ending with hope rather than resolution, the poem retains its emotional power and its liturgical function — it can be performed again because the restoration it seeks is always still in process.
