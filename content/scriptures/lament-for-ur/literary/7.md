The seventh kirugu represents the poem's most emotionally intense passage. The poet deploys a relentless accumulation of suffering images, each more specific and harrowing than the last. Dead bodies are compared to 'fat melting in the sun'; streets 'where the people once promenaded' are filled with corpses. The literary technique is one of overwhelming witness: the poet does not editorialize or theologize but simply describes, allowing the images to carry their own terrible weight.
