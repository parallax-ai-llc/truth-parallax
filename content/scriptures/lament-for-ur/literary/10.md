The tenth kirugu shifts the poem's emotional trajectory from despair to hope. After nine kiruguS of unrelenting grief, the introduction of petition creates a new literary dynamic. The language shifts from past tense (what was destroyed) to imperative and optative moods (what should be restored). This modulation of tense and mood is the poem's most significant literary turn — it transforms the lament from a document of grief into an instrument of renewal.
