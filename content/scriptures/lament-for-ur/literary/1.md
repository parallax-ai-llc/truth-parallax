The opening kirugu establishes the poem's dominant literary technique: the anaphoric catalogue. Deity after deity, temple after temple, city after city — the poet creates an overwhelming sense of totality through accumulation. The effect is one of comprehensiveness: not a single god remains, not a single shrine is spared. The repetitive structure also serves the poem's performative function, each line presumably accompanied by ritual gesture or musical response.
