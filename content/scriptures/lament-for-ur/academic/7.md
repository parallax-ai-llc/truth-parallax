The seventh kirugu turns from the divine realm to the human, describing in graphic detail the suffering of Ur's population. Bodies lie in the streets, children are separated from parents, the living hide among the dead. Scholarship has noted the documentary quality of these descriptions — their specificity suggests eyewitness reporting or, at minimum, access to detailed accounts of the city's fall. The passages describing bodies in the streets and the breakdown of social order are among the most cited in studies of ancient literature and warfare.
