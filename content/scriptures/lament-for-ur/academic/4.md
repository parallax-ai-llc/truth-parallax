The fourth kirugu focuses specifically on the destruction of Ur's temples and sacred precincts, with particular attention to the Ekishnugal (temple of Nanna). The text describes the violation of sacred space in graphic terms: temple furnishings smashed, ritual vessels scattered, the inner sanctum profaned. Scholarship has noted the theological precision of this section — the poet distinguishes between the temple's physical destruction and the more profound violation of its sacred character.
