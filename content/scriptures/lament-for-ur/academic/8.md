The eighth kirugu extends the lament beyond the city walls to describe the devastation of the surrounding countryside. Fields lie fallow, canals are choked, pastures are empty, and the herds have scattered. This section reflects the economic realities of southern Mesopotamia, where the city depended on irrigated agriculture and pastoral herding. Scholarship has noted that the destruction of irrigation infrastructure would have had longer-lasting effects than the sacking of the city itself, as the rebuilding of canals required coordinated labor over extended periods.
