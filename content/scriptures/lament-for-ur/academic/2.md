The second kirugu describes the divine assembly's formal decree of Ur's destruction. Anu and Enlil, the supreme gods, pronounce the verdict that cannot be overturned. Scholarship has emphasized the legal character of this scene: the assembly functions as a court, and the decree is presented in formulaic legal language. Michalowski notes the use of juridical terminology (*di*, judgment; *inim*, word/verdict) that grounds the cosmic catastrophe in institutional procedure.
