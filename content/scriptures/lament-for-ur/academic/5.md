The fifth kirugu introduces the voice of Ningal, consort of Nanna and mother goddess of Ur, who speaks before the divine assembly to plead for her city's survival. Ningal's speech is one of the longest and most emotionally powerful passages in the lament. Scholarship has analyzed the speech's structure: it combines legal argumentation (citing Ur's faithful service to the gods), emotional appeal (Ningal's tears and self-abasement), and theological reasoning (questioning the justice of the decree).
