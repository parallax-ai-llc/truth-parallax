The first kirugu catalogues the departure of the gods from their temples in Ur and the surrounding cities. Each deity is named alongside the shrine they abandon, creating a systematic inventory of divine withdrawal. Piotr Michalowski's critical edition identifies this section as establishing the theological framework for the entire lament: destruction is caused not by enemy action alone but by the deliberate withdrawal of divine protection.
