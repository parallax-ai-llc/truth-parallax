The ninth kirugu returns to Ningal's voice as she walks through the ruins of Ur and laments over what she sees. This section is distinguished from her earlier plea (kirugu 5) by its tone: where the plea was directed at the divine assembly in hope of reversal, this lament is addressed to the ruins themselves — a retrospective grief without hope of changing the outcome. Scholars have noted the shift from argumentation to elegy, from public speech to private mourning.
