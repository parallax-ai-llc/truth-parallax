The sixth kirugu describes the divine assembly's rejection of Ningal's intercession. Anu and Enlil's word stands: the destruction of Ur will proceed. Scholarship has noted the formal parallel between this scene and legal proceedings — the appeal is heard, considered, and denied. Michalowski emphasizes that the text does not present the rejection as arbitrary but as the fulfillment of an irrevocable cosmic law: once the word of Anu and Enlil is spoken, it cannot be recalled.
