The third kirugu describes the storm sent to execute the divine decree. The destruction is portrayed as a supernatural tempest — a devastating wind sent by Enlil that levels buildings, scatters populations, and renders the city uninhabitable. Scholarly analysis has focused on the meteorological language: the 'storm' (*u4*) functions as both a literal weather event and a cosmic force of divine judgment. The imagery draws on the real experience of devastating storms in southern Mesopotamia.
