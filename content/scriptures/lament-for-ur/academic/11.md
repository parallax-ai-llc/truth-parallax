The eleventh and final kirugu concludes the lament with an appeal for and expectation of renewed divine favor. The text addresses Nanna directly, asking him to look with favor upon Ur once more. Scholars debate whether the concluding lines represent a prophetic assurance of restoration or a petitionary prayer for a restoration still hoped for. The liturgical rubric at the end — 'It is a kirugu of Nanna' — confirms the text's cultic function and its attribution to the worship of Ur's patron deity.
