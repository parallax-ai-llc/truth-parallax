The tenth kirugu marks the poem's turn from lament to petition, as the speaker appeals to the gods — particularly Nanna — to restore their favor to Ur. Scholars have identified this section as the liturgical climax of the composition, the moment at which the ritual function of the lament becomes most explicit: the poem was likely performed during temple restoration ceremonies, and this petition represents the community's formal request for divine return.
