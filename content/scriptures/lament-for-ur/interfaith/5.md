Ningal's plea for mercy resonates with intercessory traditions across religions. In Judaism, Moses intercedes for Israel after the golden calf (Exodus 32:11-14). In Christianity, Mary intercedes for humanity before Christ. In Islam, Muhammad intercedes for his community on the Day of Judgment. In Mahayana Buddhism, bodhisattvas intercede for all sentient beings. The fifth kirugu presents perhaps the earliest literary depiction of intercessory prayer — and its failure raises the most difficult question: what happens when intercession is not enough?
