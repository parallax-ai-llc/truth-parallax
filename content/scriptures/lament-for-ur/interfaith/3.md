The storm as divine instrument appears across religious traditions: the flood in Genesis, the wind that parts the Red Sea, the whirlwind from which God speaks to Job. In Hinduism, Rudra and Shiva are associated with destructive storms. In Islam, the people of 'Ad are destroyed by a devastating wind (Quran 41:16). The third kirugu's depiction of divine destruction through storm participates in a universal pattern in which the most powerful natural forces are understood as expressions of divine will.
