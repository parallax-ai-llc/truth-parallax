The act of mourning over ruins connects to spiritual practices across traditions. Jewish pilgrimage to the Western Wall, Christian Stations of the Cross, Muslim visit to the destroyed sites of early Islam, and Buddhist practice of walking meditation among ruins all affirm that sacred space retains its significance even in destruction. Ningal's lament teaches that mourning is not weakness but witness — the insistence that what was lost mattered.
