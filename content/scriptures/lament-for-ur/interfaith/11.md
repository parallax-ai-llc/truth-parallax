The promise of renewed divine favor — the hope that the sacred can be restored after catastrophe — is perhaps the most universal theme in the lament. Every tradition that has survived persecution, exile, or destruction has drawn on this hope. Judaism's prayer 'Next year in Jerusalem' during centuries of exile, Christianity's faith in resurrection after crucifixion, Islam's confidence that truth will prevail, Buddhism's teaching that suffering can be transformed into wisdom — all share the conviction that destruction is not the final word. The Lament for Ur, composed four thousand years ago, is the earliest surviving literary expression of this indomitable hope.
