The destruction of sacred space is one of the most traumatic experiences a religious community can endure. The fourth kirugu's grief over ruined temples resonates with Jewish mourning for the Temple on Tisha B'Av, Christian reflection on the desecration of churches, Muslim grief over destroyed mosques, and Buddhist sorrow over lost monasteries. Sacred architecture represents centuries of devotion made material; its destruction is experienced as a wound to the community's spiritual body.
