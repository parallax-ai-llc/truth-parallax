The mourning of the land for its lost community resonates with ecological and spiritual traditions that understand the natural world as a participant in human affairs. Indigenous traditions worldwide affirm the reciprocal relationship between communities and their land. Judaism's concept of the sabbatical year (Leviticus 25:4) and the land 'vomiting out' inhabitants who defile it (Leviticus 18:28) express a similar theology. The eighth kirugu suggests that the devastation of a people is also a wound to the earth.
