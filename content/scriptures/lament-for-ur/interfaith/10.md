The turn from lament to hope is one of the most universal patterns in religious experience. Jewish liturgy moves from mourning to hope on Tisha B'Av. The Christian liturgical year moves from Good Friday to Easter. The Islamic tradition affirms that 'with hardship comes ease' (Quran 94:5-6). Buddhist practice transforms suffering into the path of awakening. The tenth kirugu embodies this universal spiritual movement: the insistence that destruction is not the final word.
