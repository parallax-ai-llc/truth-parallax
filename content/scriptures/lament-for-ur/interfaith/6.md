The failure of intercession raises a question that haunts every tradition: when prayers go unanswered, does it mean God does not care, cannot help, or has a purpose beyond human understanding? The sixth kirugu offers one answer: the cosmic order has its own integrity that even divine compassion cannot override. This resonates with traditions that emphasize God's inscrutability (Job 38-41), the Buddhist acceptance of suffering as intrinsic to existence, and the Islamic emphasis on *qadr* (divine predestination).
