The decree of a divine council — the gods collectively deciding humanity's fate — invites comparison with traditions that present divine governance differently. Judaism, Christianity, and Islam affirm a single God whose decisions are sovereign; the biblical prophets are granted insight into God's 'council' (Jeremiah 23:18) but do not participate in it. Hindu tradition includes divine assemblies (*devasabha*), and the Norse tradition features the gods' *thing* (assembly). The second kirugu offers the earliest literary depiction of this universal concept.
