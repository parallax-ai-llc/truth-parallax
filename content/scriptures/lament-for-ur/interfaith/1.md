The image of gods abandoning their temples speaks to a universal religious fear: what happens when the divine withdraws? This fear resonates across traditions. In Judaism, the destruction of the Temple raises the question of God's presence in exile. In Christianity, Christ's cry from the cross — 'My God, why have you forsaken me?' — expresses the terror of divine abandonment. In Hinduism, the concept of the *Kali Yuga* describes an age when dharma withdraws. The first kirugu gives this universal fear its earliest literary voice.
