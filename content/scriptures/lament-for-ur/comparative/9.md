Ningal's solitary lament over the ruins of Ur parallels some of the most moving passages in world literature. Hecuba's lament over fallen Troy (Euripides, *The Trojan Women*), the figure of Daughter Zion weeping over Jerusalem (Lamentations 1-2), and Andromache's grief in the Iliad all share the pattern of a woman mourning a destroyed city. The gendering of this grief — it is almost always a woman who gives voice to the city's suffering — appears across cultures and periods.
