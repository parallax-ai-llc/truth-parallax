The storm as instrument of divine destruction appears across ancient Near Eastern literature. In the Atrahasis, the flood is preceded by destructive storms; in the Gilgamesh flood narrative, 'the storm god roared in the clouds.' The biblical tradition uses storm imagery for divine judgment (Nahum 1:3, Psalm 83:15). The Baal Cycle's identification of the storm god with cosmic sovereignty provides a theological framework for understanding the storm as an expression of divine will rather than mere natural disaster.
