Ningal's intercession parallels other scenes of divine or human figures pleading before supreme authority to avert destruction. Abraham's negotiation with God over Sodom (Genesis 18:22-33) is the closest biblical parallel — both involve a figure arguing against divine judgment on the grounds that the righteous should not perish with the wicked. In Greek tradition, Thetis pleads with Zeus on behalf of Achilles. The motif of the intercessor who challenges divine authority appears across cultures.
