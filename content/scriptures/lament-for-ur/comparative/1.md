The catalogue of divine abandonment in the first kirugu parallels the biblical motif of God's glory departing from the Temple (Ezekiel 10:18-19). In both traditions, the departure of the divine presence is the precondition for destruction — the enemy is merely the instrument. This shared theological pattern suggests a common ancient Near Eastern understanding that sacred space is protected by divine presence rather than military strength.
