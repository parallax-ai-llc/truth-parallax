The turn from lament to petition is a structural feature shared across lamentation literature. Lamentations 5:21 ('Restore us to yourself, O LORD, that we may return; renew our days as of old') parallels the tenth kirugu's appeal. In the Psalms of lament (Psalm 44, 74, 80), the same pattern appears: description of suffering followed by petition for restoration. The structure suggests a universal liturgical logic: grief is not merely expressed but directed toward a hoped-for future.
