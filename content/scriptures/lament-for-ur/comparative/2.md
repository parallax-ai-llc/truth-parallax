The divine council's decree in the second kirugu parallels the heavenly court scenes found across ancient Near Eastern literature: the divine assembly in the Baal Cycle, the council of gods in the Atrahasis, and the heavenly court in Job 1-2 and Psalm 82. In each case, cosmic decisions are made through collective deliberation. The biblical tradition increasingly centralizes authority in a single God, but the conciliar model persists in the 'divine council' imagery of the prophets.
