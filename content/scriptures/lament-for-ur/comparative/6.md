The rejection of divine intercession is comparatively rare in ancient literature. In the biblical tradition, intercessory prayer is often effective — Abraham's negotiation saves Lot, Moses's plea averts total destruction. The Lament for Ur's insistence that the decree cannot be overturned even by a goddess's tears represents a more pessimistic theology that finds its closest biblical parallel in the unalterable decrees of the Medes and Persians (Daniel 6:8, Esther 1:19).
