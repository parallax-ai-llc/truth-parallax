The concluding appeal for divine return parallels the endings of other ancient Near Eastern lament compositions. The Lament for Sumer and Ur, the Lament for Nippur, and the Lament for Eridu all conclude with similar petitions for restoration, suggesting a genre convention. In biblical tradition, the book of Lamentations ends not with resolution but with an ambiguous plea (5:22), while many Psalms of lament conclude with confident assertions of divine faithfulness. The Lament for Ur's conclusion falls between these poles — hopeful but not yet resolved.
