The first kirugu presents one of the most devastating theological images in ancient literature: gods voluntarily abandoning the temples they inhabit. The text does not attribute the abandonment to divine anger or human sin but to the irresistible decree of the assembly. Even Nanna, Ur's patron, must leave — not because he wants to but because the cosmic order requires it. This theology of reluctant divine abandonment differs from prophetic traditions where God departs in anger.
