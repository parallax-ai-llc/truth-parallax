The final kirugu resolves the poem's theological tension — or rather, holds it in suspension. The decree of destruction has been executed, the suffering has been witnessed, and now the community appeals for a new decree: one of restoration rather than destruction. The theology of this conclusion is one of cyclical renewal — destruction is not the end but a passage through which the community moves toward a new beginning. This theology of survival through catastrophe is foundational to the Mesopotamian religious worldview.
