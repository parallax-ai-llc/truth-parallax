The seventh kirugu confronts the reader with the concrete reality of theological abstraction. The divine council's decree, discussed in formal terms in earlier kiruguS, is revealed in its human consequences: starvation, violence, separation of families, unburied dead. The theological question is no longer 'Why did the gods decree destruction?' but 'What does that destruction mean for the old woman dying in the street, for the child crying for its mother?' The text does not answer this question — it simply presents the evidence.
