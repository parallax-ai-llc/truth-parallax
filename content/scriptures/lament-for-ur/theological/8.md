The desolation of the countryside extends the theological implications of Ur's destruction to the natural world. The land itself mourns — fields that should produce grain lie empty, canals that should carry water are choked with debris. This theology of sympathetic nature — in which the natural world reflects divine displeasure or absence — appears across traditions: Romans 8:22 ('the whole creation groans'), the Baal Cycle's cosmic drought, and the Hindu concept of *adharma* disrupting natural order.
