Ningal's plea raises the theological question of intercession: can divine judgment be changed by appeal? The fifth kirugu presents the most sympathetic case possible — a goddess weeping for her own city, presenting arguments of justice and mercy — and the text will answer in the next kirugu: no. The decree stands. This theological outcome is devastating: even divine compassion and eloquent argument cannot alter cosmic law once spoken.
