The third kirugu presents destruction as cosmic storm — not a human army but a divine force. This theological choice is significant: by attributing Ur's fall to a supernatural storm rather than (primarily) to human enemies, the text locates the cause of destruction in the divine realm rather than the political. The Elamite invaders are instruments, but the storm is the true agent. This theology insists that history's catastrophes are ultimately caused by divine decision, not human agency alone.
