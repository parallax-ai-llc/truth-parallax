The destruction of the temple represents the collapse of the cosmic order made visible on earth. In Mesopotamian theology, the temple is not merely a building but the point where heaven and earth meet — the dwelling place of the god on earth. Its destruction severs the connection between divine and human realms, reducing sacred space to rubble. The fourth kirugu's detailed inventory of destroyed sacred objects — altars, offering tables, ritual vessels — emphasizes the totality of this cosmic rupture.
