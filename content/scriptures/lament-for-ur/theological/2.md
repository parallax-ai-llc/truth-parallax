The second kirugu presents the most theologically difficult aspect of the lament: the destruction of Ur is not punishment for sin but the execution of an inscrutable decree. Anu and Enlil speak, and their word is irreversible. This theology of absolute divine sovereignty — in which the gods' decision requires no justification — raises the question of theodicy in its sharpest form: if destruction is not punishment, then there is no moral logic to the cosmos. Or perhaps the moral logic operates at a level beyond human comprehension.
