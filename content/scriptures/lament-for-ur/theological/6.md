The rejection of Ningal's plea is the lament's theological nadir. If even a goddess cannot save her own city through appeal, then the cosmic order is one in which compassion is subordinate to law. This theology of cosmic jurisprudence — in which decrees once made are absolutely binding — contrasts with traditions that emphasize divine freedom to relent (Jonah 3:10, 'God relented from the evil he had planned'). The theological vision here is deterministic: once fate is spoken, it is sealed.
