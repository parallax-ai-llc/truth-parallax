## Overview

Jami at-Tirmidhi is one of the six canonical hadith collections (*Kutub al-Sittah*) in Sunni Islam, compiled by Imam Abu Isa Muhammad ibn Isa at-Tirmidhi (824–892 CE). The collection is uniquely valued for its systematic grading of hadiths — categorizing each as *sahih* (authentic), *hasan* (good), *da'if* (weak), or *gharib* (rare) — making it an essential reference for students of hadith methodology and authentication.

## Structure

The collection is organized thematically into major books covering the breadth of Islamic teaching:

1. **Faith and Purification** — Foundational beliefs, ritual cleanliness, and the prerequisites of worship
2. **Prayer and Fasting** — The daily prayers, voluntary devotions, and the disciplines of Ramadan
3. **Trade and Judgment** — Commercial ethics, judicial processes, and legal rulings
4. **Virtues and Manners** — Noble character traits, the merits of the Prophet and his companions
5. **Tafsir and Supplications** — Quranic commentary through prophetic explanation, and prayers for every occasion

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Jami at-Tirmidhi occupies a distinctive place across interpretive traditions:

- **Sunni Islam**: Valued for its hadith grading system and its inclusion of diverse jurisprudential opinions
- **Shia Islam**: Engaged selectively, with interest in traditions concerning Ahl al-Bayt
- **Sufi traditions**: Prized for its rich collection of hadiths on virtues, manners, and spiritual excellence
- **Salafi movement**: Appreciated for its transparency in hadith authentication and grading
- **Academic scholarship**: Studied as a key source for understanding early hadith classification methodology
