Shia engagement with at-Tirmidhi's tafsir section examines how prophetic commentary on the Quran aligns with or diverges from the interpretations transmitted through the Imams. Shia exegetical tradition holds that the Imams possessed a special knowledge of the Quran's inner meanings, and traditions from at-Tirmidhi that touch on the same verses are compared with Imami interpretations. Where at-Tirmidhi records a prophetic explanation through an Ahl al-Bayt narrator, Shia scholars treat this as particularly reliable.

The supplication chapters are engaged with genuine appreciation, as the Shia tradition possesses its own rich legacy of devotional literature — most notably *al-Sahifah al-Sajjadiyyah*, the prayer collection attributed to Imam Zayn al-Abidin. Shia readers compare at-Tirmidhi's prophetic supplications with those preserved in their own tradition, often finding striking parallels that attest to a shared devotional heritage. The supplications for times of distress and tribulation resonate especially with Shia piety and its emphasis on patience through suffering.
