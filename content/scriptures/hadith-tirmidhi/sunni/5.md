At-Tirmidhi's final major sections address Quranic commentary (*tafsir*) through prophetic hadith and the comprehensive collection of prophetic supplications (*du'a*). For Sunni scholars, the tafsir section is especially valuable because it preserves the Prophet's own explanations of specific Quranic verses, providing authoritative interpretive guidance that takes precedence over later scholarly opinion in matters of exegesis.

The supplication chapters gather prophetic prayers for every occasion — morning and evening, travel, illness, distress, entering the mosque, and countless other situations. Sunni devotional practice draws heavily on these traditions, with many Muslims memorizing and reciting these prayers as part of their daily routine. At-Tirmidhi's grading of each supplication allows practitioners to know which prayers rest on the strongest evidence. The combination of tafsir and du'a in the collection's final sections creates a natural arc from understanding God's word to responding to it in prayer, a structure that Sunni commentators regard as reflecting the organic relationship between revelation and devotion.
