At-Tirmidhi's treatment of prayer and fasting is valued in the Sunni tradition for its comprehensive coverage of both obligatory and voluntary devotional acts. He records the prophetic traditions governing the five daily prayers — their times, conditions, pillars, and recommended practices — while noting the positions of the major jurists on disputed points such as the placement of hands during standing and the recitations during bowing and prostration.

The fasting section covers the regulations of Ramadan, the conditions that permit breaking the fast, and the merits of voluntary fasting on specific days. Sunni scholars appreciate at-Tirmidhi's practice of including variant narrations alongside his gradings, which allows students to understand both the strength of the evidence and the range of scholarly opinion on each issue. His inclusion of hadiths on the night prayer (*tahajjud*) and the witr prayer provides Sunni worshippers with detailed prophetic guidance for devotions beyond the obligatory minimum, enriching the spiritual life of the committed Muslim.
