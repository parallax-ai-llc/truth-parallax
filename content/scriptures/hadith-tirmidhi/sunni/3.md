At-Tirmidhi's section on trade and judgment assembles prophetic traditions relevant to commercial ethics and judicial proceedings — areas of particular importance for Sunni jurisprudence. He records hadiths prohibiting usury (*riba*), fraud, and exploitative practices while establishing the permissibility of honest trade, partnership, and advance payment arrangements.

Sunni scholars find at-Tirmidhi's jurisprudential notes especially valuable in this section, as commercial law is an area where the four legal schools frequently diverge. His practice of indicating which scholars permitted a given transaction and which prohibited it provides a concise survey of the early legal landscape. The judgment chapters include traditions on the qualifications of judges, the requirements of testimony, and the importance of justice in arbitration. These hadiths form a significant portion of the textual basis for Sunni legal procedure and reinforce the prophetic emphasis on fairness and equity in all dealings.
