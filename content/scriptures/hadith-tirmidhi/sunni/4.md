The sections on virtues and manners constitute one of the most distinctive portions of at-Tirmidhi's collection. Sunni tradition treasures these chapters for their vivid portrayal of the Prophet's character, the merits of his companions, and the ethical ideals of Islam. At-Tirmidhi records traditions praising specific virtues — generosity, patience, modesty, truthfulness — and providing prophetic guidance on social etiquette, from the manner of greeting to the conduct of meals.

Sunni scholars draw upon these chapters to develop a comprehensive ethic of prophetic character (*akhlaq nabawiyyah*). The traditions on the virtues of the companions serve as models of piety and devotion, while those on manners establish the social norms of an Islamic community. At-Tirmidhi's related work, *al-Shama'il al-Muhammadiyyah*, expands on this material with detailed physical and behavioral descriptions of the Prophet, and many of the hadiths in the Jami prefigure that devotional masterpiece. These sections remain widely read in Sunni educational settings as essential texts for moral formation.
