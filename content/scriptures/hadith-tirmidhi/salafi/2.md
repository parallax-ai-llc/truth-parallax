Salafi engagement with at-Tirmidhi's chapters on prayer and fasting prioritizes the identification of authentic prophetic practice stripped of later accretions. Each tradition is evaluated through the lens of its grade and chain of narration, with Salafi scholars seeking to reconstruct the prayer and fasting practices of the Prophet and his companions as precisely as possible, free from the modifications introduced by subsequent generations.

At-Tirmidhi's jurisprudential commentary is utilized but not treated as binding; Salafi scholars prefer to follow the hadith evidence directly rather than deferring to the opinions of later jurists. When at-Tirmidhi notes that "the scholars differ on this point," Salafi readers evaluate which position rests on the strongest hadith evidence rather than following a particular legal school. This approach extends to fasting practices, where Salafi scholars distinguish carefully between authentically established Sunnah practices and customs that may have become widespread but lack strong hadith support.
