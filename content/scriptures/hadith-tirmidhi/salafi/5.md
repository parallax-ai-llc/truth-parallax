Salafi scholars accord special importance to at-Tirmidhi's tafsir section, as it preserves the Prophet's own explanations of Quranic verses. In the Salafi methodology of Quranic interpretation, the prophetic tafsir takes precedence over all later commentary, and at-Tirmidhi's compilation of these traditions provides direct access to the Prophet's understanding of God's word. Each tradition is evaluated for authenticity before being used as an interpretive authority.

The supplication chapters are engaged with emphasis on practicing only those prayers established through authentic narrations. Salafi scholars caution against the widespread use of supplications whose chains of narration are weak or whose texts contain formulations not attested in the prophetic Sunnah. At-Tirmidhi's grading system is appreciated here as a guide for separating authentic prophetic prayers from later additions. The result is a curated collection of supplications that Salafi practitioners can recite with confidence that they are following the exact words and formulas of the Prophet, uncontaminated by later devotional innovations.
