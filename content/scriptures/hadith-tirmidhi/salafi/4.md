Salafi scholars engage with at-Tirmidhi's chapters on virtues and manners with careful attention to the authentication of each tradition. While appreciating the devotional value of these sections, the Salafi approach insists on distinguishing between hadiths that meet the standards of authenticity and those that are weak or fabricated. This is particularly important in the virtues sections, where Salafi scholars note that weak hadiths praising specific individuals or practices have historically proliferated.

The manners traditions are valued as prophetic guidance for daily life, covering everything from greetings and meals to dress and speech. Salafi scholars emphasize that these prophetic manners (*adab*) are not merely cultural customs but divinely guided practices that Muslims should strive to follow. At-Tirmidhi's detailed recording of the Prophet's habits — how he ate, how he walked, how he spoke — is treated as a comprehensive guide to the Sunnah in daily life, distinguishing authentic prophetic practice from later cultural accretions that entered Muslim societies.
