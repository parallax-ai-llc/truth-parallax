## Prophet Like Moses

Deuteronomy 18 addresses Levitical provision, prohibits pagan divination practices, and delivers one of the most important messianic prophecies in the Pentateuch: God will raise up "a prophet like Moses." Protestant Christology has consistently identified this prophet as Christ.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Levitical Provision (18:1-8)

The Levites have no territorial inheritance; the LORD is their portion. They are supported by the offerings of the people:

- **Calvin** applied this to the maintenance of gospel ministers — those who serve the word should be supported by the congregation
- **Luther** distinguished this from the Roman system of benefices, arguing for direct congregational support of pastors

### Prohibition of Divination and Sorcery (18:9-14)

A comprehensive list of forbidden practices — child sacrifice, divination, sorcery, mediums, necromancy. Protestant theology draws firm lines here:

- **Block** notes that these practices represent attempts to access divine knowledge apart from God's appointed means
- The Reformers applied this against astrology, fortune-telling, and superstitious practices that persisted in medieval Christianity
- **Calvin** argued that seeking knowledge through forbidden channels is a form of unfaith — it bypasses God's word

### "The LORD your God will raise up for you a prophet like me from among your brothers" (18:15, 18)

This is the central messianic text of the chapter:

- **Peter** explicitly applies this to Jesus in Acts 3:22-23
- **Stephen** references it in Acts 7:37
- **Calvin** argued that while the passage has a secondary reference to the prophetic office generally, its ultimate fulfillment is in Christ alone
- **Luther** taught that Christ is the final Prophet whose word supersedes all others
- **Block** notes the parallels: like Moses, Jesus mediates a covenant, speaks God's words directly, and leads a new exodus

### Testing Prophets (18:21-22)

A prophet whose predictions fail is a false prophet. Protestant theology extends this to doctrinal testing — any teaching that contradicts Scripture is false, regardless of the teacher's credentials.

## Theological Significance

- **Christ as Prophet**: The threefold office of Christ (prophet, priest, king) begins here — Jesus is the prophet like Moses
- **Sufficiency of revelation**: Divination is forbidden because God provides sufficient guidance through His word and His prophet
- **Ministerial support**: Those who serve God's word deserve material provision
- **Discernment**: The community bears responsibility for testing prophetic claims
