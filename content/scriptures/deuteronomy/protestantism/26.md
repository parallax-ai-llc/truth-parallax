## Firstfruits

Deuteronomy 26 prescribes two liturgical acts — the offering of firstfruits and the declaration of completed tithing — and closes with a summary declaration of the covenant relationship. This chapter contains one of the most important confessional texts in the Old Testament: the "wandering Aramean" creed.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Firstfruits Confession (26:1-11)

When Israel enters the land, each person must bring the first produce to the priest and recite a historical confession:

### "A wandering Aramean was my father" (26:5)

This brief creed summarizes Israel's story — from Jacob's vulnerable wandering, through Egyptian slavery, to divine deliverance and the gift of the land:

- **Calvin** saw this confession as the model of all true worship: remembering God's saving acts and responding with gratitude
- **Luther** emphasized that the worshiper confesses unworthiness ("a wandering Aramean") before acknowledging God's grace
- **Wright** calls this Israel's "gospel in miniature" — a narrative of helplessness, oppression, divine intervention, and inheritance
- **Von Rad** identified this as one of the oldest credal statements in the Old Testament

### The Tithe Declaration (26:12-15)

Every third year, the worshiper declares before God that all tithe obligations have been fulfilled — nothing withheld, nothing misused:

- **Block** notes the remarkable transparency required: the worshiper invites divine scrutiny of their economic life
- **Calvin** connected this to the Protestant examination of conscience — honest self-assessment before God

### The Covenant Summary (26:16-19)

"The LORD has declared today that you are a people for his treasured possession." **Reformed theology** sees here the essence of the covenant: God claims a people, and they pledge obedience.

## Theological Significance

- **Worship as remembrance**: True worship recounts what God has done — the Reformation insisted on gospel-centered liturgy
- **Gratitude as foundation**: The firstfruits offering acknowledges that every good gift comes from God
- **Narrative identity**: Israel's creed is a story, not an abstraction — Protestant theology has similarly emphasized salvation history
- **Mutual commitment**: God declares Israel His own; Israel declares the LORD their God — the bilateral structure of the covenant
