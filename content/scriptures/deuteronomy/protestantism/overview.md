## Overview

Protestant interpretation of Deuteronomy is deeply shaped by the Reformation's law-gospel dialectic. The book presents the fullest expression of the Mosaic covenant's demands — total love for God, radical obedience, and the terrible consequences of apostasy. Yet Protestants also find in Deuteronomy the seeds of the gospel: the promise of a prophet like Moses (18:15-18), the call for circumcision of the heart (10:16; 30:6), and the acknowledgment that Israel cannot keep the covenant in its own strength.

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Law and gospel**: Deuteronomy reveals both the beauty and the impossibility of the law, driving the reader to grace
- **Covenant theology**: The suzerainty treaty structure of Deuteronomy informs Reformed covenant theology
- **Christ as the prophet like Moses**: Deuteronomy 18:15-18 is a key messianic prophecy fulfilled in Christ (Acts 3:22)
- **Jesus quotes Deuteronomy**: Christ's temptation responses all come from Deuteronomy (6:13, 6:16, 8:3), establishing it as central to Christian ethics
