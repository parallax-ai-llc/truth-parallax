## Tablets Restored

Deuteronomy 10 records the restoration of the covenant after the golden calf disaster. God commands new tablets, the ark is built, the Levites are set apart, and Moses calls Israel to circumcise their hearts. The chapter moves from covenant mechanics to the grand character of God.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### New Tablets and the Ark (10:1-5)

God commands Moses to cut new tablets and build an ark to house them. Protestant commentators draw significant lessons:

- **Calvin** noted that God's willingness to rewrite the covenant demonstrates His patience and commitment to His promises
- The ark containing the law becomes a picture of Christ, in whom the fullness of God's will is embodied
- **Luther** saw the replacement tablets as evidence that God restores what sin destroys

### The Levitical Calling (10:8-9)

The tribe of Levi is set apart to carry the ark, minister before the LORD, and bless in His name. The LORD Himself is their inheritance. **Reformed theology** applies this principle spiritually to all believers under the priesthood of all believers — every Christian's true inheritance is the Lord Himself.

### "Circumcise therefore the foreskin of your heart, and be no longer stubborn" (10:16)

This command for internal transformation is central to Protestant theology:

- **Luther** argued this proves the law demands more than outward compliance — it demands a changed heart, which only the gospel can produce
- **Calvin** saw here anticipation of the new covenant promise in Jeremiah 31 and Ezekiel 36
- Paul picks up this theme in Romans 2:28-29 and Colossians 2:11

### The Character of God (10:17-18)

"The LORD your God is God of gods and Lord of lords, the great, the mighty, and the awesome God, who is not partial and takes no bribe. He executes justice for the fatherless and the widow, and loves the sojourner."

**Wright** emphasizes that God's justice for the vulnerable is not optional social ethics but flows from His very nature. Protestant theology grounds social concern in the character of God, not in human ideology.

## Theological Significance

- **Covenant perseverance**: God renews what Israel broke — grace triumphs over failure
- **Heart religion**: Outward ritual without inward transformation is worthless
- **God's impartiality**: He cannot be bribed or manipulated — a principle the Reformers applied against indulgences
- **Care for the vulnerable**: Justice for orphans, widows, and sojourners is a covenant obligation
