## Remember God in Prosperity

Deuteronomy 8 warns Israel against the spiritual danger of material abundance. Moses recalls the wilderness period as a time of divine discipline and provision, then warns that prosperity in the Promised Land could become a snare if Israel forgets the source of every blessing.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Man does not live by bread alone, but man lives by every word that comes from the mouth of the LORD" (8:3)

Jesus quoted this verse during His temptation (Matthew 4:4). This is a cornerstone of Protestant theology:

- **Luther** saw here the supremacy of God's word over all earthly sustenance — the basis for *sola Scriptura* as a lived reality
- **Calvin** taught that physical provision is merely instrumental; the word of God is the true sustenance of the soul
- **Evangelical tradition**: This verse grounds the priority of Bible reading, preaching, and meditation

### The Purpose of Wilderness Testing (8:2-5)

God led Israel through the wilderness for forty years to humble them, test them, and know what was in their hearts:

- **Block** observes that testing reveals character rather than creates it
- The imagery of fatherly discipline (8:5) was important to **Calvin**, who taught that God's afflictions are pedagogical, not punitive, for His children

### "Beware lest you say in your heart, 'My power and the might of my hand have gotten me this wealth'" (8:17)

This warning against self-reliance is central to Protestant soteriology. **Luther** saw this as the root of works-righteousness — claiming credit for what God alone provides.

### Manna as Sacramental Type (8:3, 16)

Protestant commentators see manna as a type of Christ, the Bread of Life (John 6:31-35). The daily dependence on manna pictures the believer's daily dependence on Christ and His word.

## Theological Significance

- **Word over bread**: Spiritual life depends on God's word — the Reformation principle of *sola Scriptura* in practice
- **Prosperity as spiritual danger**: Wealth tempts to self-sufficiency and forgetfulness of God
- **Divine discipline**: God tests and refines His people for their good
- **Gratitude as duty**: All wealth comes from God's hand, and acknowledging this is basic piety

## The Reformation and Material Simplicity

While the Reformers did not embrace monastic poverty, they warned constantly against the spiritual dangers of wealth. Deuteronomy 8 was a key text in Reformed ethics of stewardship and generosity.
