## The Shema

Deuteronomy 6 contains the most foundational confession in all of Scripture: the Shema — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one" (6:4). This chapter moves from creed to command, demanding that Israel love God with total devotion and transmit faith to coming generations.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one. You shall love the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might" (6:4-5)

Jesus identified this as the greatest commandment (Mark 12:29-30). **Calvin** argued that loving God totally is the summary of the entire law — every commandment flows from this single obligation.

**Luther** emphasized that this love is not a natural human capacity but must be created by the Holy Spirit. The command exposes our inability and drives us to grace.

### Teaching the Next Generation (6:6-9)

Moses commands that God's words be taught diligently to children, spoken of at home and abroad, bound on hands, placed on doorposts:

- **Reformed tradition**: This passage grounds the Protestant emphasis on catechesis, family worship, and Christian education
- **Block** notes that transmission of faith is not delegated to institutions alone but is primarily the responsibility of parents
- **Wright** observes that the entire household is conceived as a learning environment

### "You shall not put the LORD your God to the test" (6:16)

Jesus quoted this verse when tempted by Satan in the wilderness (Matthew 4:7). Protestant theology sees here a prohibition against demanding signs — faith trusts God's word without requiring proof.

### The Danger of Prosperity (6:10-12)

When Israel inherits cities they did not build and vineyards they did not plant, the temptation is to forget the Giver. **Matthew Henry** saw this as a warning relevant to every prosperous generation.

## Theological Significance

- **Monotheism as foundation**: The Shema is the doctrinal bedrock on which all else rests; the Reformers fought to preserve worship of God alone
- **Total devotion**: Heart, soul, and might — Protestant theology insists no compartment of life is exempt from God's claim
- **Faith transmitted, not inherited**: Each generation must hear, learn, and love; **Calvin** connected this to the necessity of preaching and catechism
- **Memory and gratitude**: Forgetting God is the root of all apostasy

## The Shema in Protestant Worship

The Reformation's emphasis on the sovereignty and oneness of God draws directly from Deuteronomy 6. Luther placed the Shema alongside the Lord's Prayer and the Decalogue as pillars of Christian instruction in his catechisms.
