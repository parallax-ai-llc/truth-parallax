## Destroy the Nations

Deuteronomy 7 commands Israel to utterly destroy the Canaanite nations, make no treaties with them, and tear down their altars. The chapter raises some of the most difficult ethical questions in Scripture while simultaneously revealing foundational truths about election and grace.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Command to Destroy (7:1-5)

Seven nations are listed for total destruction (*herem*). Protestant commentators have addressed this difficult command in several ways:

- **Calvin** argued this was a unique, unrepeatable divine judgment against specific peoples whose iniquity was "full" (cf. Genesis 15:16)
- **Wright** emphasizes that the command was about religious purity, not ethnic hatred — intermarriage would lead to idolatry
- **Block** notes that the text itself gives the rationale: "for they would turn away your sons from following me"

### "For you are a people holy to the LORD your God. The LORD your God has chosen you" (7:6)

This is one of the clearest election texts in the Old Testament. **Reformed theology** finds here the doctrine of unconditional election:

1. God chose Israel — they did not choose Him
2. The choice was not based on Israel's size or merit (7:7)
3. Election flows from God's love and His oath to the fathers (7:8)

**Luther** connected this to justification: God's choice of Israel mirrors His choice of sinners in Christ — "not because of your righteousness" (cf. 9:4-6).

### "It was because the LORD loved you" (7:8)

Divine love is the sole ground of election. **Calvin** saw here confirmation that grace precedes all human response.

### God's Faithfulness and Judgment (7:9-10)

God keeps covenant to a thousand generations with those who love Him, but repays those who hate Him. Protestant theology sees both gospel and law here — mercy for believers, justice for the impenitent.

## Theological Significance

- **Unconditional election**: God's choice is grounded in His own love, not human worthiness
- **Radical separation from idolatry**: The Reformers applied this principle to their rejection of images and relics in worship
- **Grace precedes obedience**: Israel is holy because God declared them so — sanctification flows from justification
- **Judgment as divine prerogative**: Only God has the right to command *herem*; this is not a model for human initiative
