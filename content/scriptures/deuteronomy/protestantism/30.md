## Choose Life

Deuteronomy 30 moves from the dark warnings of chapters 28-29 to a remarkable promise of restoration. Even after exile, God will gather His scattered people, circumcise their hearts, and restore them. The chapter culminates in the great summons: "Choose life, that you and your offspring may live."

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Restoration After Exile (30:1-5)

"When all these things come upon you... and you return to the LORD your God... then the LORD your God will restore your fortunes and have mercy on you":

- **Calvin** saw this as a promise that goes beyond national restoration to anticipate the spiritual restoration accomplished in Christ
- **Wright** notes the pattern of grace: even the covenant curses do not have the last word — God's mercy follows judgment
- **Reformed theology** reads this as evidence that God's ultimate purpose is redemptive, not merely punitive

### "The LORD your God will circumcise your heart" (30:6)

This is one of the most important Old Testament anticipations of the new covenant:

- **Luther** argued this proves that heart transformation is God's work, not human achievement — the very thing the law demands, God Himself must provide
- **Calvin** connected this directly to Jeremiah 31:33 and Ezekiel 36:26 — the promise of a new heart is the essence of the new covenant
- **Reformed theology** sees here the clearest Pentateuchal statement of regeneration — God changes the heart so that love for Him becomes possible

### "The word is very near you. It is in your mouth and in your heart" (30:14)

Paul quotes this in Romans 10:6-8 to explain the accessibility of salvation by faith:

- **Luther** emphasized that the gospel does not require heroic effort — it is near, in the word preached
- **Calvin** saw Paul's use of this text as demonstrating continuity between Old and New Testaments — salvation has always been by grace through faith

### "I have set before you life and death, blessing and curse. Therefore choose life" (30:19)

The climactic appeal of the entire Deuteronomic sermon:

- **Arminian theology** sees here genuine human freedom in spiritual matters
- **Reformed theology** maintains that the command to choose reveals what ought to be done, while 30:6 reveals that God must enable the choosing
- **Block** observes that "life" is defined relationally — loving the LORD, obeying His voice, holding fast to Him (30:20)

## Theological Significance

- **Grace beyond judgment**: Exile is not the end — God's restorative purpose transcends His punitive sanctions
- **Divine regeneration**: Circumcision of the heart is God's work, anticipating new covenant transformation
- **Accessibility of the word**: Salvation does not require extraordinary effort — God has brought His word near
- **Life as relationship**: Choosing life means choosing God Himself — not merely obedience but love
