## Against Idolatry

Deuteronomy 13 addresses three scenarios of temptation to idolatry: a false prophet performing signs, a close family member enticing privately, and an entire city going astray. The penalties are severe in each case. The chapter establishes principles of spiritual discernment that Protestant theology has applied to doctrine and church discipline.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The False Prophet with Signs (13:1-5)

Even if a prophet performs a genuine sign or wonder, if he says "Let us go after other gods," he must be rejected and put to death:

- **Calvin** drew from this the principle that miracles alone do not authenticate a message — doctrine must be tested by God's revealed word
- **Luther** applied this against Rome's appeal to miracles supporting practices absent from Scripture
- The Reformers used this text to argue that the church must be reformed by the word, regardless of impressive spiritual experiences

### "The LORD your God is testing you, to know whether you love the LORD your God with all your heart" (13:3)

God permits false prophecy as a test of loyalty. **Block** notes this is consistent with the testing theme throughout Deuteronomy — the wilderness (8:2), prosperity (8:12-14), and now false teaching.

### Family Members Who Entice (13:6-11)

Even the closest relations — spouse, child, friend — must not be followed into idolatry. **Calvin** taught that covenant loyalty to God takes precedence over every human bond, a principle Jesus echoed in Luke 14:26.

### The Devoted City (13:12-18)

An entire city that turns to idolatry must be placed under the ban (*herem*). Protestant commentators note:

- This represents the seriousness of corporate apostasy
- **Wright** observes that the passage envisions careful judicial inquiry before action ("you shall inquire and make search and ask diligently," 13:14)
- The Reformers did not advocate literal application but drew the principle that false teaching must be confronted with urgency

## Theological Significance

- **Scripture over experience**: Signs and wonders do not validate doctrine; God's word is the final authority
- **The testing of faith**: God uses temptation to reveal and strengthen loyalty
- **Covenant above kinship**: No human relationship supersedes allegiance to God
- **Discernment as duty**: The congregation is responsible for testing teaching (1 John 4:1)
