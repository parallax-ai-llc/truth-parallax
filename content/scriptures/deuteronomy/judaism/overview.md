## Overview

Deuteronomy (*Devarim*, "Words") holds a unique place in Jewish tradition as *Mishneh Torah* — the "repetition of the Torah." It contains the Shema (6:4-9), Judaism's foundational confession of faith, recited twice daily. The book's emphasis on teaching Torah to children, loving God, and choosing life has shaped Jewish piety, education, and ethics for millennia.

## Key Interpretive Themes

- **The Shema**: The most important prayer in Judaism — "Hear, O Israel: the LORD our God, the LORD is one" (6:4)
- **Ve'ahavta**: The command to love God (6:5) and teach Torah to children (6:7) is the foundation of Jewish education
- **Teshuvah**: Deuteronomy 30:1-10 is the Torah's primary text on repentance and return to God
- **Choosing life**: "I have set before you life and death, blessing and curse; therefore choose life" (30:19)
