## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Deuteronomy draws heavily on its moral and social teaching. The Catechism's treatment of the Ten Commandments follows the Deuteronomic formulation, and the book's vision of a just society — care for the poor, the stranger, the widow, and the orphan — informs Catholic social teaching. The Shema's command to love God totally is read as the foundation of the First Commandment and the basis of Christian spirituality.

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **The Decalogue**: The Catechism follows the Deuteronomic numbering and exposition of the commandments (CCC 2052-2557)
- **Natural law**: Deuteronomy's moral precepts express the natural law inscribed in every human heart
- **Social justice**: The Deuteronomic Code's concern for the marginalized informs Catholic social doctrine
- **Covenant and sacrament**: The covenant renewal ceremony prefigures the liturgical renewal of baptismal promises
