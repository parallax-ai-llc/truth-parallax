## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Deuteronomy reads Moses' farewell as a type of Christ's farewell discourse (John 13-17). The Shema's call to love God with all one's being is understood as the foundational movement toward theosis. The Church Fathers — especially Origen, Chrysostom, and Theodoret — developed rich spiritual readings of Deuteronomy's laws, seeing in them the soul's journey toward God.

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Moses as Christ-type**: Moses' farewell, his death outside the land, and his designation of a successor all prefigure aspects of Christ's ministry
- **The Shema and theosis**: Total love for God is the beginning of deification — the soul's participation in divine life
- **Patristic moral reading**: The Fathers read Deuteronomy's social laws as guides for the Christian community's life
- **Liturgical use**: Deuteronomy readings appear in the Great Lent parimiae and other liturgical contexts
