## Overview

Islamic engagement with Deuteronomy centers on several key themes: the Shema's affirmation of God's oneness (paralleling the shahada), the prophecy of a prophet like Moses (18:15-18, which Muslim scholars identify as Muhammad), and Moses' farewell as a model of prophetic succession. The book's emphasis on monotheism, justice, and covenant resonates deeply with Quranic theology.

## Key Parallels

| Deuteronomy Theme | Islamic Parallel | Significance |
|------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| The Shema (6:4) | The Shahada | Foundational monotheistic confession |
| Prophet like Moses (18:15-18) | Muhammad as the promised prophet | Central to Islamic prophetology |
| Blessings and curses | Consequences of obedience/disobedience | Quranic pattern of warning |
| Love of God | Hubb Allah / Taqwa | Spiritual foundation |
| Care for the poor | Zakat / Sadaqah | Social justice imperative |

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Tawhid**: The Shema confirms the original monotheism that Islam sees as the core of all prophetic revelation
- **The promised prophet**: Muslim scholars argue that Deuteronomy 18:18 ("a prophet from among their brethren") refers to Muhammad, from the Ishmaelite branch
- **Tahrif**: The current text of Deuteronomy is seen as containing alterations to the original divine revelation
