## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Deuteronomy finds in the book the fullest self-revelation of the Demiurge. The jealous God who demands exclusive worship ("You shall have no other gods before me"), who threatens terrible curses for disobedience, and who binds his people through law — this is precisely the portrait of Yaldabaoth that Gnostic texts describe. Deuteronomy's blessings-and-curses framework is read as the Demiurge's ultimate mechanism of control: obey and prosper, disobey and be destroyed.

## The Gnostic Inversion

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| The Shema affirms God's unity | The Demiurge demands exclusive worship to prevent souls from seeking the true God |
| Covenant love is the highest calling | "Love" the Demiurge or face destruction — this is coercion, not love |
| The curses warn against sin | The curses reveal the Demiurge's true vindictive nature |
| Moses' death is tragic and noble | Moses served the Demiurge faithfully and received death as his reward |
| "Choose life" is gracious invitation | The "choice" is illusory — both paths lead deeper into the material world |

## Key Gnostic Themes

### The Jealous God
"I the LORD your God am a jealous God" (5:9) — this self-description is, for Gnostics, the Demiurge's most damning confession. The *Apocryphon of John* uses precisely this verse to unmask Yaldabaoth.

### Law as Total Control
Deuteronomy's comprehensive legislation — governing food, clothing, warfare, family, worship — represents the Demiurge's attempt to regulate every aspect of material existence, leaving no space for gnosis.
