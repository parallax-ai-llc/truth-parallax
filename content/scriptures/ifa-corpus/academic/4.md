Odu 4 — Odi Meji — Feminine Power and Re-creation — is a major division of the Ifa literary corpus that has attracted significant scholarly attention in the fields of Yoruba studies, African oral literature, religious studies, and anthropology. Academic analysis of this Odu examines its constituent verses (*ese*), their narrative structures and poetic features, the knowledge systems they encode, and their performance contexts in Ifa divination practice.

Scholars have studied Odi Meji's position within the hierarchical structure of the 16 principal Odu, its thematic associations with feminine power and re-creation, the range and diversity of its verses, and its role in Ifa divination consultations. The Odu serves as a repository of Yoruba intellectual heritage, encoding historical, botanical, medical, ethical, and theological knowledge in narrative and proverbial form. Academic engagement with this Odu contributes to the broader recognition of Ifa as one of the world's great knowledge systems.
