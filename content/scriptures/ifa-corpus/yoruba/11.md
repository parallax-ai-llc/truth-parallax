Odu 11 — Ika Meji — Wisdom Through Adversity — holds fundamental significance within Yoruba religious practice as one of the 16 principal Odu that structure the entire Ifa divination system. When this Odu appears in divination, the babalawo (Ifa priest) recites appropriate verses (*ese*) from the Odu's extensive oral literary corpus, providing the client with guidance, warnings, and prescribed sacrifices (*ebo*) relevant to their situation.

Within Yoruba tradition, Ika Meji is associated with wisdom through adversity and carries specific ritual, medicinal, and ethical prescriptions. The Odu's verses narrate stories of the Orisha (divinities), ancestors, and mythological figures whose experiences illuminate the client's circumstances. The babalawo's mastery of Ika Meji's verses — their narratives, proverbs, songs, and sacrificial prescriptions — represents years of intensive training and memorization, reflecting the depth and complexity of the Ifa intellectual tradition.
