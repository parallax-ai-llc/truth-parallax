Odu 7 — Obara Meji — Speech and the Power of the Word — holds fundamental significance within Yoruba religious practice as one of the 16 principal Odu that structure the entire Ifa divination system. When this Odu appears in divination, the babalawo (Ifa priest) recites appropriate verses (*ese*) from the Odu's extensive oral literary corpus, providing the client with guidance, warnings, and prescribed sacrifices (*ebo*) relevant to their situation.

Within Yoruba tradition, Obara Meji is associated with speech and the power of the word and carries specific ritual, medicinal, and ethical prescriptions. The Odu's verses narrate stories of the Orisha (divinities), ancestors, and mythological figures whose experiences illuminate the client's circumstances. The babalawo's mastery of Obara Meji's verses — their narratives, proverbs, songs, and sacrificial prescriptions — represents years of intensive training and memorization, reflecting the depth and complexity of the Ifa intellectual tradition.
