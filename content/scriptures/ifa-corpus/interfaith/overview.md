## Overview

The Ifa corpus offers an invaluable African contribution to global interfaith dialogue, presenting perspectives on the divine, creation, destiny, ethics, and human purpose that both complement and challenge the assumptions of the Abrahamic and Asian traditions that have historically dominated interreligious conversation. Ifa's inclusion in interfaith discourse is essential for a truly global and representative dialogue among the world's religious traditions.

Key interfaith themes in the Ifa corpus include the concept of destiny and free will (resonating with theological debates in Christianity, Islam, and Hinduism), the role of sacrifice in maintaining the relationship between the human and the divine (paralleling Vedic, biblical, and other sacrificial traditions), the ethical emphasis on good character (*iwa pele*) as the foundation of a meaningful life (connecting with virtue ethics traditions worldwide), and the vision of a cosmos sustained by reciprocal relationships between all beings (resonating with indigenous and ecological spiritualities globally).
