## Overview

The Ifa Corpus (Odu Ifa) is the sacred oral literary tradition of the Yoruba people of West Africa, centered on the system of divination presided over by Orunmila (also known as Ifa), the deity of wisdom, knowledge, and destiny. The corpus consists of 256 Odu (chapters or signs), each containing a collection of *ese* (verses) — stories, proverbs, songs, and prescriptions that encode the accumulated wisdom of Yoruba civilization. The corpus is traditionally organized around 16 principal Odu (*Odu Meji*), from which the remaining 240 are derived through combination.

Ifa divination is the central intellectual and spiritual tradition of Yoruba religion, recognized by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity. Through the casting of palm nuts (*ikin*) or a divining chain (*opele*), the Ifa priest (*babalawo*, "father of secrets") determines which Odu is relevant to a client's situation and then recites the appropriate verses, which contain guidance, warnings, and prescribed sacrifices (*ebo*). The corpus addresses every aspect of human existence — from cosmogony and theology to medicine, ethics, governance, and daily conduct.

## Structure

The 16 principal Odu and their associations:

| Odu | Name | Key Themes |
|-----|------|------------|
| 1 | Eji Ogbe | Light, clarity, purity, divine consciousness, the head |
| 2 | Oyeku Meji | Darkness, death, ancestors, the night, hidden knowledge |
| 3 | Iwori Meji | Inner vision, the womb, transformation, duality |
| 4 | Odi Meji | Stubbornness, feminine power, re-creation, persistence |
| 5 | Irosun Meji | Bloodline, family, witchcraft, the power of women |
| 6 | Owonrin Meji | Chaos, unexpected change, the trickster, instability |
| 7 | Obara Meji | Deception, wealth, speech, the power of the word |
| 8 | Okanran Meji | The heart, courage, conflict, resolution of disputes |
| 9 | Ogunda Meji | War, the forge, Ogun, clearing of paths, surgery |
| 10 | Osa Meji | Water, Oya, sudden change, spiritual power, divination |
| 11 | Ika Meji | Conspiracy, poison, the serpent, wisdom through adversity |
| 12 | Oturupon Meji | Disease, infection, the earth, healing, medicine |
| 13 | Otura Meji | The sky, mystical vision, the sacred, Ifa itself |
| 14 | Irete Meji | Pressing forward, determination, the imprint of destiny |
| 15 | Ose Meji | Abundance, sweetness, Oshun, fertility, prosperity |
| 16 | Ofun Meji | Completion, purity, the origin, the womb of creation |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Academic**: Studied as one of the world's great oral literary traditions and knowledge systems
- **Comparative**: Rich parallels with divination systems, wisdom literature, and oral traditions worldwide
- **Yoruba**: The central spiritual and intellectual tradition of Yoruba religion and civilization
- **Diasporic**: Preserved and adapted in the African diaspora — in Cuban Santería/Lucumí, Brazilian Candomblé, and other traditions
- **Theological**: Presents a sophisticated theology of destiny, free will, sacrifice, and divine-human relationship
- **Interfaith**: Offers an African perspective on universal questions of meaning, purpose, and the sacred

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Ori (Destiny) | Each person chooses their destiny before birth, which can be fulfilled or obstructed |
| Ebo (Sacrifice) | The offering of sacrifice to align oneself with one's destiny and the will of the Orisha |
| Iwa (Character) | Good character as the foundation of a successful and meaningful life |
| Ase (Power) | The divine power or authority that enables all things to come into being |
| Ayanmo (Fate) | The interplay between individual destiny and cosmic order |
| Ogbon (Wisdom) | The pursuit of wisdom through divination, study, and moral reflection |
