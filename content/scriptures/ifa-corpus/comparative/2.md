Odu 2 — Oyeku Meji — Darkness and Ancestral Knowledge — provides rich material for comparative analysis with divination traditions, wisdom literature, and oral literary traditions worldwide. The Odu's thematic focus on darkness and ancestral knowledge connects to analogous concepts in other religious and philosophical traditions, revealing both shared patterns in how human cultures understand and navigate these fundamental aspects of existence.

Comparative analysis examines parallels between Oyeku Meji's teachings and related concepts in other traditions: Chinese I Ching hexagrams with similar thematic associations, biblical wisdom literature addressing comparable concerns, and indigenous knowledge systems worldwide that similarly encode practical and spiritual wisdom in oracular form. The Odu's integration of narrative, proverb, prescription, and song within a single divination sign invites comparison with the I Ching's combination of images, judgments, and commentaries.
