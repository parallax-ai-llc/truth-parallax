## Overview

The Ifa corpus provides exceptionally rich material for comparative analysis with divination systems, wisdom traditions, and oral literatures worldwide. The binary structure of the Odu (based on combinations of single and double marks) has invited comparison with the Chinese I Ching, another ancient binary divination system, though the two traditions developed independently. The corpus's function as a repository of proverbial wisdom connects it to wisdom literature traditions such as the biblical Proverbs, the Confucian Analects, and West African griot traditions.

Comparative study reveals both structural parallels and distinctive features. Like the I Ching, Ifa uses a binary system to generate a finite set of symbolic configurations, each with accumulated interpretive tradition. Like Greek and Roman oracular traditions, Ifa mediates between human uncertainty and divine knowledge through ritualized consultation. However, the Ifa corpus's extraordinary breadth — encompassing creation myths, ethical teachings, medical knowledge, and historical narratives — gives it a comprehensiveness that distinguishes it from most other divination traditions.
