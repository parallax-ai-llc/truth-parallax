Odu 12 — Oturupon Meji — Disease and Healing — carries profound theological significance within the Ifa religious system, articulating fundamental truths about disease and healing and their relationship to the broader Yoruba understanding of divinity, destiny, and cosmic order. The theological content of this Odu encompasses creation narratives, accounts of the Orisha, ethical teachings, and reflections on the nature of human destiny (*ori*) and its fulfillment.

Theologically, Oturupon Meji reveals aspects of the relationship between Olodumare (the Supreme Being), the Orisha (divinities), and human beings. Its verses articulate the principle that proper sacrifice (*ebo*), good character (*iwa pele*), and the guidance of Ifa divination are essential for navigating the challenges associated with disease and healing and fulfilling one's destiny. The Odu thus functions as theological discourse in narrative form — communicating fundamental truths about the divine-human relationship through story, proverb, and prescription.
