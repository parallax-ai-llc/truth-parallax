## Overview

The Ifa corpus presents a sophisticated theological system addressing the fundamental questions of human existence: the nature of the divine, the origin and purpose of creation, the relationship between destiny and free will, and the role of sacrifice in maintaining cosmic harmony. At the summit of the Yoruba theological universe stands Olodumare (the Supreme Being), the source of *ase* (creative power) who delegates authority to the Orisha (divinities), each governing specific domains of existence.

Central to Ifa theology is the concept of *ori* (destiny or inner head) — the belief that each soul chooses its destiny before birth in the presence of Olodumare. This destiny is not rigidly deterministic; through proper sacrifice (*ebo*), moral conduct (*iwa*), and the guidance of Ifa divination, individuals can overcome obstacles and fulfill the potential encoded in their chosen destiny. The theology of sacrifice — offering to the Orisha and to one's ori — reflects a vision of the cosmos as a web of reciprocal relationships between humans, divinities, ancestors, and the natural world.
