Odu 16 — Ofun Meji — Completion and the Origin — carries profound theological significance within the Ifa religious system, articulating fundamental truths about completion and the origin and their relationship to the broader Yoruba understanding of divinity, destiny, and cosmic order. The theological content of this Odu encompasses creation narratives, accounts of the Orisha, ethical teachings, and reflections on the nature of human destiny (*ori*) and its fulfillment.

Theologically, Ofun Meji reveals aspects of the relationship between Olodumare (the Supreme Being), the Orisha (divinities), and human beings. Its verses articulate the principle that proper sacrifice (*ebo*), good character (*iwa pele*), and the guidance of Ifa divination are essential for navigating the challenges associated with completion and the origin and fulfilling one's destiny. The Odu thus functions as theological discourse in narrative form — communicating fundamental truths about the divine-human relationship through story, proverb, and prescription.
