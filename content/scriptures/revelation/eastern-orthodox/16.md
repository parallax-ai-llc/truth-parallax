## The Seven Bowls of Wrath

The bowl judgments of Revelation 16 represent the most severe and comprehensive divine interventions in the book. Orthodox interpretation, following St. Andrew of Caesarea, reads these judgments as the ultimate expression of divine justice against impenitent humanity. The parallels with the Egyptian plagues — sores, water to blood, darkness — establish a typological connection with the Exodus: God's final judgments recapitulate and surpass his earlier acts of deliverance and justice.

## Armageddon

The gathering at "Armageddon" (16:16) has been interpreted in Orthodox tradition not as a geographic prediction but as a symbolic designation for the final confrontation between God and the forces of evil. Orthodox eschatology, while affirming the reality of a final conflict, resists detailed speculation about its timing, location, and participants. St. Andrew reads the drying of the Euphrates (16:12) as the removal of divine restraints that had held back destructive forces — a final test of humanity before the end.

## The Seventh Bowl

The climactic "It is done!" (16:17) echoes Christ's cry from the cross and marks the completion of divine judgment within history. Orthodox theology sees in this parallel a profound connection between the cross and the consummation — the same divine love that embraced the cross now brings history to its conclusion. The unprecedented earthquake and the hundred-pound hailstones express the totality and irreversibility of this judgment in the symbolic language of prophetic tradition.
