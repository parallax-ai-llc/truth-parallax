## The Lamb Who Was Slain

Revelation 5 is the Paschal chapter of the Apocalypse. The appearance of the Lamb "standing as though slain" (5:6) is the central christological image of the book, and Orthodox theology reads it through the lens of the Paschal mystery. The Paschal troparion — "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life" — captures the paradox of the slain-yet-standing Lamb: death has been conquered not by force but by the voluntary self-offering of the God-Man.

## The Scroll and Divine Providence

The sealed scroll represents God's providential plan for the cosmos. That only the Lamb is worthy to open it affirms the Orthodox teaching that Christ is the key to all of history — a conviction expressed in the Paschal homily of St. John Chrysostom and in the Orthodox theological understanding of Christ as the *Logos* through whom all things were made and in whom all things find their meaning. The weeping of John (5:4) because no one is found worthy represents the despair of a cosmos without Christ.

## Cosmic Worship

The expanding circles of worship — the four living creatures, the twenty-four elders, myriads of angels, and finally "every creature in heaven and on earth and under the earth and in the sea" (5:13) — express the Orthodox vision of cosmic *doxologia* (glorification). All creation is destined to participate in the praise of God, a destiny that is anticipated in every celebration of the Divine Liturgy and will be fully realized in the age to come.
