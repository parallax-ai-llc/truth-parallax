## The Two Beasts

Orthodox interpretation of Revelation 13 reads the two beasts as symbols of political and ideological power arrayed against God and his Church. St. Andrew of Caesarea interprets the beast from the sea as the anti-Christian political authority that demands worship — a reading that the Orthodox Church has applied across centuries of confrontation with imperial, Islamic, and communist regimes that sought to suppress or co-opt the Church.

## The Antichrist in Orthodox Theology

Orthodox theology affirms the expectation of a personal Antichrist who will appear before the second coming of Christ. St. John of Damascus, St. Cyril of Jerusalem, and other Fathers describe the Antichrist as a human being empowered by Satan who will establish a counterfeit kingdom, perform false miracles, and demand universal worship. The beast's blasphemous names and demand for worship (13:4-8) depict this figure. However, Orthodox theology also recognizes a broader "spirit of antichrist" (1 John 4:3) operative in every age.

## The Number 666

St. Andrew of Caesarea discusses several possible interpretations of 666, including various names yielded by Greek *gematria*, while ultimately counseling caution about definitive identifications. He interprets the number as representing the fullness of human evil — falling short of the divine perfection symbolized by seven. Orthodox tradition resists the speculative identification of 666 with contemporary figures, emphasizing instead the spiritual discernment needed to recognize the beast's spirit in all forms of idolatrous power.
