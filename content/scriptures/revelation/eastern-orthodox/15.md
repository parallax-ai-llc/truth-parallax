## The Song of the Victorious

Revelation 15 presents the victorious saints standing on a sea of glass mingled with fire, singing the song of Moses and the Lamb. Orthodox interpretation reads this as a prefiguration of the Paschal celebration — the greatest feast in the Orthodox calendar. Just as Israel sang at the Red Sea after God's deliverance from Egypt, the redeemed sing after their deliverance from the beast. The "song of Moses and the song of the Lamb" affirms the unity of the two Testaments and the continuity of God's saving action.

## The Heavenly Temple

The opening of "the temple of the tabernacle of testimony in heaven" (15:5) reveals the heavenly archetype of earthly worship. Orthodox liturgical theology holds that every church building is an icon of the heavenly temple, and the iconostasis (icon screen) represents the boundary between the visible and invisible realms that will be dissolved at the eschaton. The seven angels emerging in "pure, bright linen" (15:6) echo the priestly vestments, reinforcing the liturgical character of the vision.

## The Glory of God Fills the Temple

The temple filling with smoke from God's glory (15:8) — so that no one can enter until the plagues are complete — echoes the Sinai theophany and the dedication of Solomon's temple. Orthodox theology reads this as a moment of terrible divine majesty: God's holiness is both attractive and overwhelming, drawing worship and inspiring awe in equal measure.
