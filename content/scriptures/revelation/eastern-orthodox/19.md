## The Hallelujah and the Marriage Supper

Revelation 19 opens with heaven's fourfold "Hallelujah," the only New Testament occurrence of this Hebrew praise word. Orthodox liturgical tradition preserves the "Alleluia" as a central element of worship — it accompanies the reading of the Gospel and is intoned during the most solemn moments of the Divine Liturgy. The "marriage supper of the Lamb" (19:7-9) is read as the eschatological fulfillment of the Eucharist — the heavenly banquet anticipated in every celebration of the Divine Liturgy.

## The Rider on the White Horse

The vision of Christ as the victorious rider (19:11-21) embodies the Orthodox understanding of *Christus Victor* — Christ's triumph over sin, death, and the devil. The Paschal homily of St. John Chrysostom celebrates this victory: "Christ is risen, and the demons are fallen. Christ is risen, and the angels rejoice." The rider's name — "The Word of God" (19:13) — echoes the Johannine prologue and the Nicene Creed's affirmation that Christ is the eternal *Logos*.

## The Sword of the Word

That Christ's weapon is the sword proceeding from his mouth — not a military instrument — affirms the Orthodox conviction that divine victory comes through truth and sacrificial love, not through coercion. The beast and false prophet are defeated by the Word, not by an army. This pattern is reflected in the Orthodox theology of martyrdom: the martyrs conquer not by fighting but by bearing witness (*martyria*) to the truth, even unto death.
