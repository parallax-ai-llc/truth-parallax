## The Hallelujah and Pleromatic Rejoicing

The fourfold Hallelujah of Revelation 19:1-6 was read by Gnostic interpreters as the celebration of the Pleroma itself — the divine fullness rejoicing at the imminent restoration of the divine sparks to their original home. The destruction of Babylon-as-material-cosmos is cause for cosmic celebration because it marks the end of the pneumatics' exile. The heavenly worship described here is not directed at the Demiurge but at the true God — the transcendent Father whom the Demiurge never knew.

## The Rider as Pneumatic Christ

The rider on the white horse (19:11-16) — named "The Word of God," "Faithful and True," and "King of kings" — was identified in Gnostic reading with the pneumatic Christ, the divine Aeon who descends to liberate the trapped sparks. His robe "dipped in blood" (19:13) was reinterpreted: not the blood of sacrifice (which Gnostics rejected) but the residue of his passage through the archontic spheres, where the material elements attempt to stain the pure Aeon. The "sword" from his mouth is *gnosis* itself — the liberating knowledge that cuts through archontic deception and awakens the sleeping pneumatics.

## The Defeat of the Beasts

The beast and false prophet thrown into the lake of fire (19:20) represent the definitive destruction of the archontic system — both its political power (beast from the sea) and its ideological apparatus (beast from the earth / false prophet). Their destruction by fire, the purifying element, returns their borrowed existence to the void from which the Demiurge fashioned them. The armies destroyed by the sword of the Word (19:21) are the hylic forces that have no existence apart from the material cosmos — when *gnosis* is fully revealed, ignorance simply ceases to exist.
