## The Lamb and the Gnostic Christ

The Lamb "standing as though slain" (5:6) presents a complex challenge for Gnostic interpretation. Most Gnostic systems rejected the notion that the true Christ actually suffered and died — the *Apocalypse of Peter* (NHC VII,3) describes the spiritual Christ laughing above the cross while only his fleshly substitute suffers. Yet the Lamb imagery was too powerful to simply dismiss. Some Gnostic interpreters read the "slain" Lamb as the pneumatic Christ who descended through the archontic spheres, appearing to be killed but in reality liberating the divine sparks trapped in matter.

## The Sealed Scroll and Hidden Gnosis

The sealed scroll that only the Lamb can open (5:1-5) was read as the hidden knowledge (*gnosis*) concealed from the Demiurge and his archons. The seven seals represent the seven archontic barriers that prevent the ignorant from accessing the truth about the Pleroma, the Fall of Sophia, and the divine origin of the human spirit. Only the true Christ — the Aeon who descends from above — possesses the knowledge to break these seals and reveal the hidden content.

## The New Song

The "new song" celebrating the Lamb's worthiness (5:9-10) was reinterpreted in some Gnostic circles as the hymn of pneumatic liberation — the joyful recognition that the divine messenger has broken through the archontic barriers and revealed the path of return to the Pleroma. The declaration that the redeemed are made "a kingdom and priests" (5:10) was read not as an earthly vocation but as the restoration of the pneumatics to their original status as inhabitants of the divine realm.
