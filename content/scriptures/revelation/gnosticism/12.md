## The Woman as Sophia

Revelation 12 is arguably the most important chapter for Gnostic interpretation. The Woman clothed with the sun (12:1) was identified by Gnostic readers with Sophia — the fallen Aeon whose tragic error produced the Demiurge and set the entire cosmic drama in motion. In the *Apocryphon of John*, Sophia's premature emanation without her consort produces Yaldabaoth, the lion-headed serpent who becomes the creator of the material world. Revelation 12's Woman in labor pains mirrors Sophia's agonized production of the Demiurge — a birth that is simultaneously a cosmic catastrophe and the hidden beginning of redemption.

## The Dragon as the Demiurge

The great red dragon (12:3) was identified not merely with Satan (the orthodox reading) but with the Demiurge himself — the jealous, wrathful creator who opposes the redemptive work of the true God. The dragon's seven heads and ten horns represent the totality of archontic power, the full apparatus of material oppression that the Demiurge deploys against the pneumatic community. His attempt to devour the male child (12:4) represents the Demiurge's effort to prevent the descent of the saving *gnosis* into his cosmos.

## The War in Heaven

The war between Michael and the dragon (12:7-9) was read as the primordial conflict between the Pleromatic powers and the archontic forces — a cosmic battle that precedes and underlies all earthly conflicts. The dragon's expulsion from heaven represents the Demiurge's confinement to the material realm, cut off from the Pleroma that he never truly knew. The woman's flight to the wilderness (12:14) represents Sophia's exile in the material world, sustained by the true God while she awaits the completion of the pneumatic harvest.
