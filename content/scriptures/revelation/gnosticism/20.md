## The Binding of Satan and the Archons

The binding of Satan for a thousand years (20:1-3) was read in Gnostic cosmology as the temporary restraining of the chief archon — the Demiurge or his most powerful agent — allowing the pneumatic harvest to proceed unimpeded. The abyss into which he is cast is the lowest depth of the material cosmos, the most dense and opaque region of the Demiurge's creation. The thousand years represent a symbolic period of relative pneumatic freedom — an era in which *gnosis* can be transmitted and received without the full force of archontic opposition.

## The First Resurrection

The "first resurrection" (20:4-6) — in which the saints come to life and reign with Christ — was interpreted in Gnostic terms as the awakening of *gnosis*, not a bodily resurrection. The pneumatic who achieves *gnosis* experiences a "resurrection" from the death of material ignorance into the life of spiritual knowledge. This resurrection is available now, in the present life, to those who receive the secret teaching. The *Gospel of Philip* (NHC II,3) states: "Those who say they will die first and then rise are in error. If they do not first receive the resurrection while they live, when they die they will receive nothing."

## The Great White Throne

The final judgment (20:11-15) was read by Gnostic interpreters as the definitive sorting of the three types of human beings: pneumatics (saved by nature through *gnosis*), psychics (saved conditionally through faith and works), and hylics (destroyed with the material cosmos). The "books" opened represent the record of each soul's spiritual attainment — not moral behavior in the orthodox sense, but the degree of *gnosis* achieved. The "lake of fire" is the dissolution of the material cosmos itself, into which everything that lacks the divine spark simply disappears.
