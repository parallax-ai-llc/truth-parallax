## The Two Witnesses as Bearers of Gnosis

The two witnesses of Revelation 11, who prophesy, perform signs, are killed by the beast, and rise again, were read in some Gnostic circles as representatives of the pneumatic tradition — the bearers of *gnosis* who are persecuted by the archontic powers but cannot ultimately be silenced. Their death and resurrection after three and a half days mirrors the pattern of Gnostic revelation itself: true knowledge is repeatedly suppressed by the Demiurge's agents but always re-emerges.

## The Temple Measured

The command to measure the temple while excluding the outer court (11:1-2) was read in Gnostic terms as the distinction between the inner, spiritual reality accessible to the pneumatics and the outer, material form that is given over to the archons and their servants. The "nations" who trample the outer court are the hylic (material) forces that can affect the body and the external world but cannot touch the inner sanctum of the divine spark.

## The Seventh Trumpet and Cosmic Transformation

The seventh trumpet's announcement — "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord" (11:15) — was read by Gnostics as the proclamation of the Demiurge's impending overthrow. The "kingdom of the world" is precisely the Demiurge's domain, and its transfer to "our Lord" represents the restoration of cosmic authority to the true God of the Pleroma. The Ark of the Covenant glimpsed in the heavenly temple (11:19) was interpreted as a symbol of the hidden divine presence that has always existed within the Demiurge's cosmos, concealed from the archons but accessible to the pneumatics.
