## The Gnostic Christ Unveiled

Revelation 1's vision of the glorified Christ — radiant, otherworldly, bearing attributes of cosmic authority — resonated deeply with Gnostic sensibilities. The Christ of this vision is not the earthly Jesus of the Synoptic Gospels but a transcendent, luminous being whose appearance overwhelms the seer (1:17). Gnostic interpreters read this figure as the pneumatic Christ — the divine Aeon who descended temporarily into the material world and has now returned to his true form, free of fleshly limitation.

## The Seven Lampstands and the Archontic Spheres

The seven golden lampstands (1:12) and seven stars (1:16) were mapped in some Gnostic systems onto the seven planetary archons — the rulers of the material spheres through which the soul must ascend to reach the Pleroma. The letters to the seven churches (chapters 2-3) could then be read as instructions for navigating the archontic obstacles, with each church representing a sphere and each promise to "the one who conquers" representing the password or knowledge needed to pass through that sphere.

## The Alpha and Omega

The self-designation "Alpha and Omega" (1:8) was embraced by Gnostic interpreters as evidence that the true Christ transcends the Demiurge's limited creation. The first and last letters of the Greek alphabet encompass all knowledge — a totality of *gnosis* that the Demiurge does not possess. Valentinian interpreters, as reported by Irenaeus (*Against Heresies* 1.14-15), developed elaborate systems correlating letters, numbers, and Aeons, finding in Revelation's alphabetic symbolism confirmation of their cosmological speculations.
