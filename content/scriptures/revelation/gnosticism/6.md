## The Seals and the Dissolution of the Material World

Gnostic interpreters read the seal judgments of Revelation 6 through the lens of their fundamental conviction: the material world is a flawed creation of the Demiurge, and its destruction is not a tragedy but a liberation. The four horsemen — conquest, war, famine, and death — are not aberrations in an otherwise good creation but the natural consequences of a cosmos built on ignorance and error. The horsemen reveal what the material world truly is: a realm of suffering, domination, and death.

## The Martyrs and the Pneumatic Cry

The martyrs under the altar (6:9-11) were read by Gnostic interpreters as the pneumatic souls trapped in the material world, crying out for release. Their cry "How long?" is the cry of the divine spark imprisoned in matter, longing for the dissolution of the cosmic prison. The white robes given to them represent the luminous garment of the Pleroma — the spiritual body that will replace the material one when the soul ascends through the archontic spheres.

## The Cosmic Upheaval

The sixth seal's cosmic destruction (6:12-17) — sun darkened, stars falling, sky rolled up — was welcomed in Gnostic reading as the beginning of the material cosmos's disintegration. The terror of the powerful who cry to the mountains to hide them is the terror of the archons and their servants who sense that their rule is ending. The question "Who can stand?" is answered differently by Gnostics than by orthodox interpreters: only the pneumatics, who possess the divine spark that transcends the material order, can stand when the Demiurge's creation collapses.
