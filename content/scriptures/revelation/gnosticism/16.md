## The Bowls and the Demiurge's Undoing

The seven bowl judgments of Revelation 16, which destroy the Demiurge's creation systematically — land, sea, fresh water, sun, the beast's kingdom, the Euphrates, and the atmosphere — were read by Gnostic interpreters as the progressive dismantling of the archontic cosmos. Each bowl targets a specific domain of the Demiurge's rule, stripping away the layers of material illusion that imprison the divine sparks. The destruction is not tragic but salvific — each layer removed brings the pneumatics closer to liberation.

## Armageddon as the Final Archontic Stand

The gathering at Armageddon (16:16) was interpreted as the archons' desperate, final attempt to consolidate their power against the forces of liberation. The "kings of the whole world" assembled by demonic spirits (16:14) represent the full mobilization of the material order against the incoming Pleromatic reality. This battle is doomed from the outset — the archons fight against the source of their own existence, and their defeat is assured by the ontological superiority of the Pleroma.

## "It Is Done"

The climactic "It is done!" (16:17) was read by Gnostic interpreters as the announcement that the Demiurge's creation has reached its end — the cosmic experiment in ignorance and separation is finally concluded. The great earthquake (16:18) and the splitting of the "great city" (16:19) represent the shattering of the archontic infrastructure that has sustained the material illusion. The hailstones provoke blasphemy from the survivors (16:21) — the hylic beings who, lacking the divine spark, can only respond to liberation with incomprehension and rage.
