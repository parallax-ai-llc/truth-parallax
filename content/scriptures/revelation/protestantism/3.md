## Letters to Three Churches

Revelation 3 completes the seven letters with messages to Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea. These three letters have been among the most frequently preached texts in Protestant tradition, particularly the letter to Laodicea with its famous rebuke of lukewarmness.

## Sardis: The Dead Church

The letter to **Sardis** (3:1-6) addresses a church with a reputation for being alive but actually dead. Protestant interpreters have applied this warning to churches that maintain institutional structures and cultural respectability while lacking spiritual vitality. The promise of "white garments" and the assurance that overcomers' names will not be blotted from the book of life have fueled significant debate about the perseverance of the saints.

## Philadelphia and Laodicea

The letter to **Philadelphia** (3:7-13) is one of only two letters (along with Smyrna) that contain no rebuke. The "open door" imagery has been applied in Protestant missions theology as a mandate for evangelism. The promise to keep them from the "hour of trial" (3:10) is a key proof text in debates about the pre-tribulation rapture. The letter to **Laodicea** (3:14-22) — "neither hot nor cold" — has become one of the most quoted passages in Protestant preaching. Christ standing at the door and knocking (3:20) is widely used in evangelistic contexts, though scholars note it is addressed to the church, not to individual unbelievers.
