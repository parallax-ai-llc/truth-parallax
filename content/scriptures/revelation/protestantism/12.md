## The Woman and the Dragon

Revelation 12 introduces a new section of cosmic drama featuring a woman clothed with the sun, a great red dragon, and a male child destined to rule the nations. Protestant interpreters generally identify the woman as Israel (or the faithful community from which the Messiah comes), the male child as Christ, and the dragon as Satan. Unlike Catholic interpretation, which strongly identifies the woman with the Virgin Mary, most Protestant commentators read the woman as a corporate symbol — the people of God who give birth to the Messiah and are subsequently persecuted.

## War in Heaven

The war between Michael and the dragon (12:7-12) results in Satan being cast down to earth. Protestant theology reads this as depicting a decisive spiritual victory connected to Christ's death and resurrection — the dragon is defeated "by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony" (12:11). This text is central to Protestant spiritual warfare theology. The devil's fury is intensified precisely because his time is short, explaining the escalation of evil in the world.

## The Persecution of the Woman

After being cast down, the dragon pursues the woman, who is given "two wings of a great eagle" to flee into the wilderness for "a time, times, and half a time" (12:14) — the same three-and-a-half-year period found in Daniel 7:25. Protestant interpreters see this as God's providential preservation of his people during intense persecution, whether during a specific future tribulation or throughout the present age.
