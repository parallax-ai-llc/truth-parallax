## The Four Horsemen and the Opening of the Seals

Revelation 6 records the Lamb opening the first six of the seven seals, unleashing a sequence of judgments upon the earth. The four horsemen — white (conquest or false peace), red (war), black (famine), and pale/green (death) — are among the most iconic images in Western culture. Protestant futurists read these as specific tribulation-era events; historicists see them as recurring patterns throughout church history; idealists view them as symbols of the perennial forces of human suffering.

## The Cry of the Martyrs

The fifth seal (6:9-11) reveals the souls of martyrs under the heavenly altar, crying "How long, O Lord?" Protestant theology sees this as affirming both the conscious existence of believers after death and the legitimacy of crying out for divine justice. The martyrs are told to rest "a little longer" until the full number of their fellow servants is complete — a text that has been applied to the persecuted church throughout Protestant history, from the Huguenots to modern underground churches.

## Cosmic Upheaval

The sixth seal (6:12-17) describes a great earthquake, the sun turning black, the moon becoming like blood, stars falling, and every mountain and island being moved. Protestant interpreters divide on whether this is literal cosmic destruction (futurist), symbolic of the collapse of political powers (preterist/idealist), or Old Testament judgment language drawn from Isaiah 34 and Joel 2. The chapter closes with the terrified cry: "Who can stand?" — answered in chapter 7.
