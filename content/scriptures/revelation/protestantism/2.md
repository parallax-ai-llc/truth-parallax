## Letters to Four Churches

Revelation 2 contains four of the seven letters dictated by the risen Christ to the churches of Asia Minor. Protestant interpreters read these letters as combining historical specificity with universal application — each letter addresses a real congregation while also diagnosing conditions found in every church age.

## Key Messages

The letter to **Ephesus** (2:1-7) commends doctrinal fidelity but diagnoses the loss of "first love" — a warning that Reformed and evangelical preachers have frequently applied to congregations that prioritize orthodoxy over devotion. The letter to **Smyrna** (2:8-11) promises the "crown of life" to those who endure persecution and poverty, a text central to Protestant theologies of suffering and martyrdom. The reference to the "synagogue of Satan" (2:9) has been the subject of careful scholarly attention, with most Protestant commentators now reading it as referring to a specific local conflict rather than a blanket condemnation of Judaism.

## Pergamum, Thyatira, and Moral Compromise

The letters to **Pergamum** (2:12-17) and **Thyatira** (2:18-29) address the danger of compromising with pagan culture — eating food sacrificed to idols and sexual immorality. Protestant commentators see these as warnings against cultural accommodation. The figure of "Jezebel" in Thyatira has been interpreted as a false prophetess promoting syncretism, and Protestants have historically applied this imagery to what they perceive as doctrinal corruption in other traditions.
