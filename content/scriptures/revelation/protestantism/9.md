## The Fifth and Sixth Trumpets

Revelation 9 describes two terrifying trumpet judgments. The fifth trumpet (9:1-12) releases locust-like creatures from the abyss, led by a king named Abaddon (Hebrew) or Apollyon (Greek), meaning "Destroyer." Protestant interpreters have identified these variously as demonic forces, invading armies, or symbolic of spiritual torment. The locusts are forbidden to kill but inflict five months of agony so severe that people seek death and cannot find it.

## The Sixth Trumpet and the Euphrates Army

The sixth trumpet (9:13-21) releases four angels bound at the Euphrates River, who mobilize an army of 200 million. Protestant futurists have often identified this with a literal military force from the East (sometimes specifically China), while idealists read the number as symbolically representing an overwhelming demonic host. The Euphrates reference evokes the eastern boundary of the Roman Empire and the ever-present threat of Parthian invasion — a connection that preterist interpreters emphasize.

## The Refusal to Repent

The chapter closes with a devastating observation: despite these catastrophic judgments, the rest of humanity "did not repent of the works of their hands" (9:20-21). Protestant theology sees this as confirming the doctrine of human depravity — that judgment alone, without the regenerating work of the Holy Spirit, cannot produce genuine repentance. The catalog of sins — idolatry, murder, sorcery (*pharmakeia*), sexual immorality, theft — represents a comprehensive indictment of unregenerate humanity.
