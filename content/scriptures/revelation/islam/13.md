## The Beast and the Dajjal

Revelation 13's beast that demands universal worship invites direct comparison with the Islamic figure of the Dajjal (the False Messiah or Antichrist), one of the major signs of the Hour in Islamic eschatology. The hadith literature describes the Dajjal as a one-eyed deceiver who will appear before the Day of Judgment, perform false miracles, claim divinity, and lead many astray before being killed by Isa ibn Maryam at the gate of Ludd (Lydda) in Palestine. Like Revelation's beast, the Dajjal demands worship and exercises quasi-universal power.

## The Mark and the Inscription

The "mark of the beast" required for buying and selling (13:16-17) has been compared to the hadith tradition that the word *kafir* (disbeliever) will be inscribed on the Dajjal's forehead, readable by every believer. Both traditions envision a visible mark that distinguishes the followers of the deceiver from the faithful. In both cases, the fundamental sin is idolatry — giving ultimate allegiance to a created being rather than to God.

## The Number 666 and Islamic Disinterest

Islamic scholarship has shown relatively little interest in the number 666, as the entire system of apocalyptic numerology belongs to a textual tradition that Islam does not accept as authoritative. Muslim scholars who engage comparatively note that *gematria*-style calculations exist in the Islamic *abjad* numerical tradition, but they are applied to the Quran and hadith, not to the Book of Revelation. The fundamental Islamic concern with Revelation 13 is not its numerology but its implications for *tawhid* — any figure that demands worship other than Allah represents the ultimate *fitna* (trial) of faith.
