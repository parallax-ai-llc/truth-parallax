## The Final Three Letters

Revelation 3's letters to Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea continue the pattern of spiritual diagnosis and moral exhortation. Islamic interpretation engages with the ethical content while maintaining its foundational objections to the christological framework.

## Spiritual Vigilance

The letter to **Sardis** (3:1-6), warning a community that appears alive but is spiritually dead, resonates with the Quranic concept of *ghafla* (heedlessness) — the state of those who go through the motions of religious life without genuine *taqwa* (God-consciousness). The Quran warns: "Do not be like those who forgot Allah, so He made them forget themselves" (59:19). Both traditions emphasize that outward religious observance without inner transformation is spiritually dangerous.

## The Lukewarm and *Nifaq*

The letter to **Laodicea** (3:14-22), with its famous rebuke of lukewarmness, parallels the Quranic condemnation of *nifaq* (hypocrisy). The *munafiqun* (hypocrites) are those who profess faith outwardly while lacking genuine commitment — a condition the Quran considers worse than open disbelief (Quran 4:145: "Indeed, the hypocrites will be in the lowest depths of the Fire"). Islamic scholars note that both Revelation and the Quran treat spiritual complacency as a graver danger than outright opposition.
