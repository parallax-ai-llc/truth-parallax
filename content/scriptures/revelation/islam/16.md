## The Bowl Judgments and the Signs of the Hour

The seven bowl judgments of Revelation 16, with their escalating destruction, parallel the "signs of the Hour" described in Islamic hadith literature. Muslim eschatological compilations — such as Ibn Kathir's *Al-Nihayah fi al-Fitan wa al-Malahim* — describe natural disasters, moral collapse, and cosmic upheaval as precursors to the Day of Judgment. While the specific imagery differs, the underlying theology is similar: the created order itself responds to human sin and divine judgment.

## Armageddon and the Malhama

The gathering at Armageddon (16:16) invites comparison with the Islamic concept of *al-Malhama al-Kubra* (the Great Battle), described in hadith as a cataclysmic end-times conflict. Various hadith locate this battle in the region of Syria (al-Sham), and it involves the Muslim forces, the Romans (understood variously as Byzantines or Western powers), and the eventual appearance of the Dajjal. While the geographic and narrative details differ from Revelation's Armageddon, both traditions anticipate a definitive military confrontation as part of the eschatological sequence.

## "It Is Done"

The climactic declaration "It is done!" (16:17) parallels the Quranic descriptions of the Hour's arrival as sudden and irrevocable: "And the command of Allah is ever a destiny decreed" (33:38). Islamic theology emphasizes the suddenness and finality of the Day of Judgment — when the command comes, there is no delay and no appeal.
