## The Demonic Locusts and the World of the Jinn

Revelation 9's terrifying locusts from the abyss — with human faces, women's hair, lions' teeth, and scorpion tails — find a distant parallel in the Islamic concept of the *jinn*: invisible beings created from smokeless fire (Quran 55:15) who can be either good or evil. The Quran devotes an entire surah (72, Al-Jinn) to these beings and describes how some among them accepted Islam while others remained in rebellion. The locust-beings of Revelation, emerging from the abyss under demonic leadership, would be classified in Islamic demonology as evil jinn or *shayatin* (devils) operating under Iblis (Satan).

## The Euphrates and Islamic Geography

The Euphrates River (9:14) holds significance in Islamic eschatological tradition as well. Several hadith describe events associated with the Euphrates in the end times. A hadith in Sahih Muslim reports that the Prophet Muhammad said: "The Hour will not come until the Euphrates uncovers a mountain of gold, for which people will fight. Ninety-nine out of every hundred will die, and every one of them will say, 'Perhaps I might be the one who will be saved.'" While the specific context differs from Revelation 9, both traditions associate the Euphrates with eschatological upheaval.

## The Refusal to Repent

The observation that humanity "did not repent" despite catastrophic judgments (9:20-21) resonates with the Quranic theme of *istikbar* (arrogant refusal to submit to God). The Quran repeatedly describes communities that rejected their prophets despite clear signs: "And We had certainly sent to them messengers before you, and We presented them with clear proofs, but they were not to believe in that which they had denied before" (30:47). Both traditions recognize the mystery of human obstinacy in the face of divine warning.
