## The Sealed and the Multitude

Revelation 7's vision of the 144,000 sealed from the twelve tribes and the great multitude from every nation addresses themes that resonate with Islamic theology: divine protection of the faithful and the eschatological reward of those who endure tribulation. However, the specifically Israelite framing (twelve tribes) and the christological basis of salvation (robes washed in the blood of the Lamb) mark fundamental points of divergence.

## Paradise Imagery

The promise that those before the throne "shall hunger no more, neither thirst anymore; the sun shall not strike them" (7:16) parallels Quranic descriptions of Jannah (Paradise). The Quran describes a paradise with "rivers of water unaltered, rivers of milk the taste of which never changes, rivers of wine delicious to those who drink, and rivers of purified honey" (47:15). Both traditions envision the eschatological reward as the removal of all earthly suffering and the provision of abundance beyond imagination.

## Tears Wiped Away

The promise that "God will wipe away every tear from their eyes" (7:17) echoes the Quranic assurance that the inhabitants of Paradise "will not be grieved" (3:170) and that God will "remove whatever is within their breasts of resentment" (7:43). Islamic eschatological teaching emphasizes the completeness of heavenly joy — no residue of earthly sorrow will remain. This shared hope for the eschatological elimination of suffering represents a point of convergence between the two traditions.
