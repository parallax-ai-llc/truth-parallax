## The Seal Judgments and Quranic Eschatology

The judgments unleashed by the six seals — conquest, war, famine, death, martyrdom, and cosmic upheaval — find broad thematic parallels in Quranic eschatology. The Quran describes the "signs of the Hour" (*ashrat al-sa'ah*) that precede the Day of Judgment: wars, earthquakes, the spread of injustice, and cosmic dissolution. However, the Quran presents these signs in a non-narrative, non-sequential form, in contrast to Revelation's structured unfolding of sealed judgments.

## The Cosmic Signs

The sixth seal's phenomena — sun darkened, moon like blood, stars falling, sky rolled up like a scroll (6:12-14) — closely parallel Quranic descriptions of the end times. Surah At-Takwir (81:1-6) declares: "When the sun is wrapped up, and when the stars fall, dispersing, and when the mountains are removed." Surah Al-Infitar (82:1-3) adds: "When the sky breaks apart, and when the stars fall, scattering, and when the seas are erupted." Islamic scholars note that both traditions draw on a common stock of Near Eastern apocalyptic imagery.

## The Cry of the Powerful

The powerful crying out to the mountains "Fall on us and hide us" (6:16) parallels the Quranic description of the Day when "every nursing mother will be distracted from that which she was nursing, and every pregnant woman will abort her pregnancy, and you will see the people intoxicated while they are not intoxicated; but the punishment of Allah is severe" (22:2). Both traditions emphasize the terror of divine judgment and the futility of seeking refuge from God.
