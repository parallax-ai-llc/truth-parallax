## The Letters and Islamic Moral Teaching

Revelation 2's letters to four churches contain moral exhortations — warnings against idolatry, sexual immorality, false teaching, and spiritual complacency — that resonate broadly with Islamic ethical principles. The Quran similarly addresses communities of believers, warning against the dangers of deviation from pure monotheism and moral laxity.

## Points of Convergence

The letter to **Smyrna** (2:8-11), which promises the "crown of life" to those who endure persecution unto death, echoes the Islamic concept of *shahada* (martyrdom). The Quran promises that those who are killed in God's cause are "alive with their Lord, receiving provision" (3:169). The letter to **Pergamum** (2:14), which condemns eating food sacrificed to idols, aligns with the Islamic prohibition of food offered to anything other than Allah (*dhabiha*). These convergences reflect the shared Abrahamic heritage of both traditions.

## Points of Divergence

The central divergence remains christological: the letters are presented as dictated by the risen, glorified Christ who exercises divine prerogatives — judging the churches, knowing their inner hearts, and promising eternal rewards. In Islam, such authority belongs to Allah alone, and even the greatest prophets serve as messengers, not as sovereign judges of their communities' spiritual condition.
