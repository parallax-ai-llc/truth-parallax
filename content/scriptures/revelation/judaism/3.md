## The Final Three Letters

Revelation 3 completes the seven letters with messages to Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea. The Philadelphia letter contains the second "synagogue of Satan" reference (3:9), which is of particular concern to Jewish scholars studying the history of anti-Jewish polemic in Christian texts.

## The Philadelphia Conflict

The promise that those of the "synagogue of Satan" will "come and bow down before your feet" (3:9) reverses the eschatological expectation found in Isaiah 60:14 and 49:23, where the Gentile nations bow before Israel. Jewish scholars note that this reversal — claiming that Jews will bow before Christians — represents an extraordinary theological claim by a marginal community seeking to establish its legitimacy. This text has been used, tragically, to justify centuries of Christian triumphalism over Judaism, though responsible scholarship recognizes its original context as a local, intra-communal dispute.

## The Book of Life

The reference to the "book of life" in the Sardis letter (3:5) connects to a concept deeply rooted in Jewish theology. The *Sefer ha-Hayyim* (Book of Life) figures prominently in the High Holy Days liturgy, particularly on Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur, when Jews pray to be "inscribed and sealed" in the Book of Life. Revelation's use of this imagery reflects its thoroughly Jewish theological vocabulary, even as the author applies it within a christological framework.
