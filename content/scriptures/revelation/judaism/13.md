## The Beast and Daniel 7

Revelation 13's beast from the sea combines features of all four beasts in Daniel 7 — the lion, bear, leopard, and the terrible fourth beast with ten horns. Jewish scholars read Daniel 7 as a cipher for the succession of empires that oppressed Israel (Babylonian, Median/Persian, Greek, and Roman), and Revelation's composite beast represents the culmination of imperial evil in Rome. The beast's demand for universal worship (13:4, 8) corresponds to the imperial cult — the requirement to offer sacrifice to the emperor's genius — which created a severe crisis of conscience for both Jews and Christians in Asia Minor.

## 666 and Jewish Gematria

The number 666 (13:18) is best explained through Hebrew *gematria*, the practice of assigning numerical values to letters. "Neron Qesar" (the Hebrew transliteration of Nero Caesar: nun-resh-vav-nun qoph-samekh-resh) totals 666. Some manuscripts read 616, which corresponds to "Nero Caesar" without the final nun — "Nero Qesar" in Latin form. Jewish scholars note that *gematria* was a well-established interpretive technique in both rabbinic and apocalyptic literature, making this identification highly probable.

## Anti-Imperial Resistance

Jewish historians read Revelation 13 alongside other anti-Roman texts from the same period, including 4 Ezra's eagle vision (4 Ezra 11-12), the Sibylline Oracles' attacks on Rome, and the rabbinic tradition's use of "Edom" as a code name for Rome. These texts share a common posture of theological resistance to imperial power, even when military resistance was no longer feasible after 70 CE. Revelation 13's portrait of the beast functions within this tradition of anti-imperial dissent.
