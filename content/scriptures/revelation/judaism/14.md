## Mount Zion and Jewish Eschatology

The vision of the Lamb on Mount Zion (14:1) draws on the rich tradition of Zion theology in the Hebrew Bible and Second Temple Judaism. Psalm 2:6 declares that God has set his king on Zion, and Joel 2:32 (Hebrew 3:5) promises that deliverance will come from Mount Zion. The gathering of the righteous on Zion is a standard feature of Jewish eschatological expectation — 4 Ezra 13:35-36 describes the Messiah standing on Mount Zion, and 2 Baruch 40:1 envisions the messianic reign centered there.

## The Eternal Gospel and the Fear of God

The angel's proclamation to "fear God and give him glory" (14:7) echoes Ecclesiastes 12:13 ("Fear God and keep his commandments, for this is the whole duty of man") and the prophetic call to worship the Creator. Jewish scholars note that this "eternal gospel" is essentially the basic monotheistic message of the Hebrew Bible — not a specifically Christian kerygma but the foundational call to acknowledge the one God.

## The Winepress of Wrath

The winepress imagery (14:19-20) derives from Isaiah 63:1-6, where God treads the winepress of judgment alone, and Joel 3:13 (Hebrew 4:13), which commands: "Put in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe; come, tread, for the winepress is full." The grape harvest as divine judgment is a thoroughly Jewish metaphor, and Revelation's application of it continues the prophetic tradition of envisioning God's definitive intervention against oppressive nations.
