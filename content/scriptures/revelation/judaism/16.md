## The Bowl Judgments and the Exodus Pattern

The seven bowl judgments of Revelation 16 represent the most concentrated use of exodus plague typology in the book. Sores (cf. Exodus 9:8-12), water to blood (Exodus 7:14-24), darkness (Exodus 10:21-23), and the drying of a great river (cf. the Red Sea) all recapitulate the Egyptian plagues. Jewish scholars note that this pattern of eschatological recapitulation — the last things mirroring the first things — is deeply embedded in Jewish theological thinking. The prophet Isaiah declared, "As in the days of your coming out of the land of Egypt, I will show them marvelous things" (Micah 7:15).

## Armageddon and Megiddo

The name "Armageddon" (16:16) derives from *Har Megiddon* — the hill/mountain of Megiddo. The Jezreel Valley near Megiddo was the site of numerous decisive battles in Israelite history: Deborah's victory over Sisera (Judges 5:19), Gideon's defeat of the Midianites (Judges 7), and the death of King Josiah at the hands of Pharaoh Necho (2 Kings 23:29-30). Jewish scholars note that Megiddo is actually a plain, not a mountain — the "Har" prefix is puzzling and may indicate a symbolic or composite reference rather than a literal geographic prediction.

## The Euphrates

The drying of the Euphrates (16:12) inverts the Red Sea crossing and recalls the crossing of the Jordan (Joshua 3). In Jewish tradition, the Euphrates marked the ideal boundary of the promised land (Genesis 15:18). Its drying to permit the advance of "kings from the east" would have evoked the Babylonian and Assyrian invasions that devastated Israel — the return of the ancient eastern threat in eschatological guise.
