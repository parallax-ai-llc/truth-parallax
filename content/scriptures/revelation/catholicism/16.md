## The Seven Bowls of Wrath

The seven bowls of Revelation 16 represent the most intense and universal judgment sequence in the book. Catholic interpretation reads the Egyptian plague parallels (sores, water to blood, darkness, frogs) as demonstrating the continuity of God's salvific acts: the same God who judged Egypt to liberate Israel now judges the whole world in its final act of rebellion.

## Armageddon and Spiritual Warfare

The gathering at Armageddon (16:16) has been read in Catholic tradition not as a literal geographic prediction but as a symbol of the ultimate confrontation between good and evil. Catholic theology, while affirming the reality of a final conflict, resists the detailed speculative timelines common in some Protestant traditions. The *Catechism* (CCC 677) teaches that the Church "will follow her Lord in his death and Resurrection" — the final battle is won not by military power but by the cross.

## The Seventh Bowl

The climactic seventh bowl — "It is done!" (16:17) — echoes Christ's cry from the cross, "It is finished" (John 19:30). Catholic theology sees this as more than coincidence: the judgments of Revelation consummate the victory that Christ achieved on the cross. The great earthquake and the division of Babylon anticipate the detailed judgment of chapters 17-18. Catholic interpretation reads the hundred-pound hailstones and cosmic upheaval as prophetic-symbolic language expressing the totality and finality of divine judgment on all structures of sin.
