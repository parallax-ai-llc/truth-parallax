## The River and the Tree of Life

Revelation 22 completes the scriptural narrative by restoring what was lost in Eden. The river of the water of life and the tree of life (22:1-2) reverse the expulsion from the Garden, and the declaration that "no longer will there be anything accursed" (22:3) lifts the curse of Genesis 3. Catholic sacramental theology sees the water of life as the eschatological fulfillment of baptismal grace, and the tree with its twelve fruits as the eucharistic nourishment that sustains the Church in her earthly pilgrimage toward this heavenly reality.

## The Beatific Vision Fulfilled

"They will see his face, and his name will be on their foreheads" (22:4) — this is the definitive statement of the beatific vision in Scripture. Catholic eschatological teaching holds that the blessed in heaven see God directly, not through images or symbols, and that this vision constitutes the fullness of human happiness (CCC 1028). The promise that "they will reign forever and ever" (22:5) fulfills the royal destiny announced in Genesis 1:26-28 and restored through Christ.

## "Come, Lord Jesus"

The closing dialogue — "Surely I am coming soon" / "Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!" (22:20) — expresses the eschatological longing that animates Catholic worship. The *Maranatha* prayer is preserved in the Didache, one of the earliest Christian liturgical documents, and resonates in every celebration of the Eucharist, which proclaims the Lord's death "until he comes." The warning against altering the words of this prophecy (22:18-19) is read by Catholic tradition as affirming the integrity of sacred Scripture within the broader framework of Sacred Tradition and the Magisterium.
