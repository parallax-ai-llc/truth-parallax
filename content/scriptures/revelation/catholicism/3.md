## Letters to Three Churches

The final three letters in Revelation 3 complete Christ's pastoral address to his churches. Catholic interpretation draws particular attention to the sacramental and ecclesial imagery embedded in each letter's promises to "the one who conquers."

## Sardis and the Call to Vigilance

The letter to **Sardis** (3:1-6) warns a church that appears alive but is spiritually dead. Catholic preaching has applied this to communities that maintain outward liturgical observance while lacking interior conversion. The promise of "white garments" evokes the baptismal garment (*alba*), reinforcing the sacramental dimension of the Christian life.

## Philadelphia and Laodicea

The letter to **Philadelphia** (3:7-13) promises to make the faithful "a pillar in the temple of my God" — language that Catholic ecclesiology connects to the Church as the living temple of God and the apostles as foundational pillars (Galatians 2:9). The letter to **Laodicea** (3:14-22) is perhaps the most frequently cited in Catholic preaching on spiritual complacency. The famous image of Christ knocking at the door (3:20) has been interpreted in Catholic tradition as an invitation to deeper eucharistic communion — Christ seeks to enter and "eat" with the believer, evoking the language of the Last Supper.
