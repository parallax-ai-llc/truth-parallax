## The Opening of the Seals

Catholic interpretation of the six seals in Revelation 6 emphasizes both the historical and spiritual dimensions of the judgments. The four horsemen — conquest, war, famine, and death — are read not as a blueprint for future events but as perennial realities of fallen human existence. Catholic social teaching has drawn on these images to address the systemic causes of war, poverty, and injustice.

## The Martyrs Under the Altar

The fifth seal (6:9-11) is particularly significant for Catholic theology. The souls of the martyrs are located "under the altar" — a direct connection to the Eucharistic altar and the Catholic practice of placing relics of martyrs beneath or within the altar at consecration. Their cry for justice ("How long, O Lord?") affirms the Catholic belief in the conscious existence of the saints after death and their ongoing intercession. The white robes given to them anticipate their glorified state and connect to baptismal and liturgical symbolism.

## The Sixth Seal

The cosmic upheaval of the sixth seal (6:12-17) draws on Old Testament theophany language (Isaiah 34, Joel 2). Catholic interpretation, guided by the Pontifical Biblical Commission's principles, reads these images as prophetic-symbolic rather than literal predictions, expressing the truth that God's justice will ultimately prevail over all human pretension and power. The terrified cry of the powerful — "Who can stand?" — underscores the Catholic teaching that no human status or achievement provides shelter from divine judgment apart from the mercy of God.
