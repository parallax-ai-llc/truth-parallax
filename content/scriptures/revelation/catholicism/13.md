## The Two Beasts

Catholic interpretation of Revelation 13 reads the two beasts as symbols of political and religious power that oppose God. The beast from the sea, with its blasphemous names and demand for worship, represents totalitarian political authority in any age — not merely a single future figure. The beast from the earth (later called the false prophet) represents the ideological and religious apparatus that legitimizes and enforces the first beast's claims.

## Catholic Social Teaching and the Beasts

Catholic social doctrine has applied Revelation 13's imagery to any system that claims absolute authority over human persons. Pope Pius XI's *Mit brennender Sorge* (1937) implicitly invoked apocalyptic categories in condemning National Socialism's divinization of race and state. The Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church affirms that no political authority may demand what belongs to God alone — a principle that Revelation 13 dramatizes in its starkest form.

## The Number 666

Catholic scholarship has generally favored the *gematria* interpretation, identifying 666 with Nero Caesar (*Neron Qesar* in Hebrew numerals). This reading connects the beast to the historical reality of Roman imperial persecution while allowing the symbol to transcend its original referent. The *Catechism* (CCC 675-677) teaches that the Church will undergo a final trial — a "religious deception offering men an apparent solution to their problems at the price of apostasy from the truth" — that resonates with Revelation 13's portrait of a beast who offers security in exchange for worship.
