## The Sealed and the Triumphant

Revelation 7 presents two complementary visions that Catholic theology reads through the lens of ecclesiology and the communion of saints. The 144,000 sealed from the twelve tribes (7:1-8) represent the Church in its earthly pilgrimage — numbered, known to God, and divinely protected. The great multitude from every nation (7:9-17) represents the Church triumphant in heaven, joined in eternal worship.

## The Communion of Saints

Catholic interpretation sees in this chapter a vivid portrait of the communion of saints (*communio sanctorum*): the Church on earth (the sealed) and the Church in heaven (the great multitude) form one body under the Lamb. The white robes, washed in the blood of the Lamb (7:14), signify the sacramental reality of baptism and the purifying effect of Christ's sacrifice. The great multitude's worship — with palm branches evoking both the Feast of Tabernacles and Christ's triumphal entry — connects Old Testament liturgy, Gospel narrative, and heavenly worship.

## Eschatological Hope

The promise that God "will wipe away every tear from their eyes" (7:17) and that "the Lamb in the midst of the throne will be their shepherd" is central to Catholic eschatological hope. The paradoxical image of a Lamb who is also a shepherd affirms the Catholic understanding that Christ's sacrificial death and his pastoral care are inseparable — realized in the Eucharist, where the Lamb feeds his flock with his own body and blood.
