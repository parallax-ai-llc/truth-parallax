## Overview

Theological analysis of the Enuma Elish examines this creation epic as a systematic expression of Babylonian theology — its understanding of cosmic origins, divine sovereignty, human purpose, and the relationship between the heavenly and earthly orders.

## Key Theological Themes

### Creation Theology
The Enuma Elish presents creation not as an act of benevolence but as a consequence of cosmic conflict. The world emerges from the body of the defeated chaos goddess Tiamat, establishing a theology in which order is won through divine combat.

### Divine Sovereignty
Marduk's ascent to kingship among the gods through demonstrated power raises theological questions about the nature of divine authority — is it inherent or earned? The epic suggests that sovereignty must be demonstrated and ratified by the divine assembly.

### Human Purpose
The creation of humanity from the blood of the rebel god Kingu establishes a theological anthropology in which humans are created for service — to relieve the gods of labor. This contrasts significantly with biblical anthropology, where humans are created in God's image.

### Cosmic Order
The Enuma Elish presents the ordering of the cosmos — the establishment of celestial bodies, the calendar, and the division of cosmic realms — as a divine act that imposes structure on primordial chaos.
