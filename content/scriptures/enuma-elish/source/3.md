**Tablet III**

**1** Anshar opened his mouth and
**2** to Gaga, his vizier, a word he addressed:
**3** "O Gaga, my vizier, who gladdens my spirit,
**4** to Lahmu and Lahamu I will send you.

**5** You know how to discern, are skilled in counsel.
**6** Produce the gods, my fathers, before me!
**7** Let all the gods come to me,
**8** let them hold converse, sit down to a banquet,

**9** let them eat festive bread, let them pour wine,
**10** that for Marduk, their avenger, they may fix the decrees.
**11** Be on your way, Gaga, stand before them,
**12** and that which I shall tell you repeat unto them:

**13** 'Anshar, your son, has sent me hither,
**14** charging me to give voice to the dictates of his heart,
**15** saying: "Tiamat, she who bore us, detests us.
**16** She has set up the Assembly and is furious with rage.

**17** All the gods have rallied to her;
**18** even those whom you brought forth march at her side.
**19** They throng and march at the side of Tiamat,
**20** angry, scheming, restless day and night.

**21** They are bent on fighting, rage and prowl like lions.
**22** They have established the Assembly and created battle.
**23** Mother Hubur, she who fashions all things,
**24** has added matchless weapons, has born monster-serpents,

**25** sharp of tooth, unsparing of fang.
**26** With venom for blood she has filled their bodies.
**27** Roaring dragons she has clothed with terror,
**28** has crowned them with haloes, making them like gods.

**29** She has set up the Viper, the Dragon, and the Sphinx,
**30** the Great-Lion, the Mad-Dog, and the Scorpion-Man,
**31** mighty lion-demons, the Dragon-Fly, the Centaur—
**32** bearing weapons that spare not, fearless in battle.

**33** Firm are her decrees, past withstanding are they.
**34** Withal eleven of this kind she has brought forth.
**35** From among the gods, her firstborn, who formed her Assembly,
**36** she has elevated Kingu, has made him chief among them.

**37** She has given him the Tablet of Destinies, fastened on his breast:
**38** 'Your command shall be unchangeable, your word shall endure!'

**39** I sent forth Anu; he could not face her.
**40** Nudimmud was afraid and turned back.
**41** But Marduk has set out, the director of the gods, your son.
**42** His heart has prompted him to set out against Tiamat.

**43** He opened his mouth, saying unto me:
**44** 'If I indeed, as your avenger,
**45** am to vanquish Tiamat and save your lives,
**46** set up the Assembly, proclaim supreme my destiny!

**47** When in Ubshukinna jointly you sit down rejoicing,
**48** let my word, instead of yours, determine the fates.
**49** Unalterable shall be what I may bring into being;
**50** neither recalled nor changed shall be the command of my lips.'

**51** Now hasten hither and promptly fix for him your decrees,
**52** that he may go forth to face your mighty foe!"'"

**53** Gaga departed, pursuing his way.
**54** Before Lahmu and Lahamu, the gods, his fathers,
**55** he made obeisance, kissing the ground at their feet.
**56** He bowed low as he stood up, saying unto them:

**57** [He repeats the entire message of Anshar]

**58** When Lahmu and Lahamu heard this, they cried out aloud,
**59** all the Igigi wailed in distress:
**60** "How strange that they should have made this decision!
**61** We cannot fathom the doings of Tiamat!"

**62** They made ready to leave for the journey,
**63** all the great gods who decree the fates.
**64** They entered before Anshar, filling Ubshukinna.
**65** They kissed one another in the Assembly.

**66** They held converse as they sat down to the banquet.
**67** They ate festive bread, poured the wine,
**68** they wetted their drinking-tubes with sweet intoxicant.
**69** As they drank the strong drink, their bodies swelled.

**70** They became very languid as their spirits rose.
**71** For Marduk, their avenger, they fixed the decrees.
