**Tablet V**

**1** He constructed stations for the great gods,
**2** fixing their astral likenesses as constellations.
**3** He determined the year by designating the zones:
**4** he set up three constellations for each of the twelve months.

**5** After defining the days of the year by means of heavenly figures,
**6** he founded the station of Nebiru to determine their heavenly bands,
**7** that none might transgress or fall short.
**8** Alongside it he set up the stations of Enlil and Ea.

**9** Having opened up the gates on both sides,
**10** he strengthened the locks to the left and the right.
**11** In her belly he established the zenith.
**12** The Moon he caused to shine, the night to him entrusting.

**13** He appointed him a creature of the night to signify the days:
**14** "Monthly, without cease, form designs with a crown.
**15** At the month's very start, rising over the land,
**16** you shall have luminous horns to signify six days,

**17** on the seventh day reaching a half-crown.
**18** At full moon stand in opposition in mid-month.
**19** When the sun overtakes you at the base of heaven,
**20** diminish your crown and retrogress in light.

**21** At the time of disappearance, approach the course of the sun,
**22** and on the thirtieth you shall again stand in opposition to the sun."

**23** [Gap in text—passage dealing with further astronomical arrangements]

**24** After he had appointed the days to Shamash,
**25** and had established the precincts of night and day,
**26** taking the spittle of Tiamat
**27** Marduk created—

**28** He formed the clouds and filled them with water.
**29** The raising of winds, the bringing of rain and cold,
**30** making the mist smoke, piling up her venom—
**31** these he appointed to himself, took into his own charge.

**32** Putting her head into position he formed therefrom the mountains,
**33** opening the deep which was in flood,
**34** he caused to flow from her eyes the Euphrates and Tigris.
**35** Stopping her nostrils he left—

**36** He formed from her breasts the lofty mountains,
**37** therein he drilled springs for the wells to carry off the water.
**38** Twisting her tail he bound it to Durmah,
**39** [Gap in text]

**40** He set her thigh to make fast the heavens,
**41** with half of her he roofed the sky as a covering,
**42** and established the earth.

**43** [Gap in text]

**44** After he had designed its plan,
**45** he placed the gods in their proper stations.
**46** He opened the Euphrates and the Tigris from her eyes,
**47** stopped her nostrils, and—

**48** From her udder he formed the lofty mountains,
**49** he drilled the springs to channel the water.
**50** He opened the courses of her two eyes,
**51** where the Euphrates and Tigris were formed.

**52** He stopped her nostrils,
**53** he accumulated the far mountains on her breasts,
**54** he bored wells to channel the springwater.
**55** He twisted her tail and wound it into the Great Band,

**56** [Gap in text]

**57** He set her crotch as the brace of heaven,
**58** spreading half of her as a ceiling.
**59** He established the earth over the waters,
**60** solidifying the ground.

**61** When he had designed its inner workings,
**62** he organized its structure well.
**63** He laid out the precincts and fashioned their shrines.

**64** After he had completed his labours
**65** and had accomplished his designs,
**66** he divided the sky into regions
**67** and assigned the three hundred gods their celestial posts.

**68** After he had ordained the year
**69** and fashioned the design of its divisions,
**70** he established the three stars each for the twelve months.

**71** After the days of the year he had fashioned in their design,
**72** he fixed the stand of Nebiru as the pole star to determine the celestial bands.
**73** That none should transgress or stray,
**74** alongside Nebiru he fixed the positions of Enlil and Ea.

**75** Gates he opened on both sides,
**76** and strong locks he made on the left and right.
**77** At the centre he placed the zenith.
**78** Nannar he caused to shine, entrusting the night to him.

**79** He established him as a body of the night
**80** to mark the days, and month by month,
**81** without ceasing, with his crown he should—

**82** "At the beginning of the month, when shining over the land,
**83** display thy horns to mark six days.
**84** On the seventh day, be thou a half-crown.
**85** At the full moon thou shalt stand in opposition.

**86** When the sun overtakes thee on the horizon,
**87** diminish thy crown and retrograde.
**88** At the time of waning, approach the sun;
**89** then on the thirtieth thou shalt stand in opposition to the sun."
