**Tablet II**

**1** Tiamat made weighty her handiwork,
**2** evil she wrought against the gods her children.
**3** To avenge Apsu, Tiamat planned evil.
**4** When she had equipped her army, it was revealed to Ea.

**5** Ea heard this matter,
**6** he was benumbed with silence, sat in deep gloom.
**7** The days went by, his anger subsided,
**8** he went to his father Anshar, his begetter.

**9** He went before Anshar, the father who begot him.
**10** All that Tiamat had plotted he repeated unto him:
**11** "My father, Tiamat, she who bore us, detests us.
**12** She has set up the Assembly and is furious with rage.

**13** All the gods have rallied to her;
**14** even those whom you brought forth march at her side.
**15** They throng and march at the side of Tiamat.
**16** Angry, scheming, restless day and night,

**17** they are bent on fighting, rage and prowl like lions.
**18** They have established the Assembly and created battle.
**19** Mother Hubur, she who fashions all things,
**20** has added matchless weapons, has born monster-serpents,

**21** sharp of tooth, unsparing of fang.
**22** With venom for blood she has filled their bodies.
**23** Roaring dragons she has clothed with terror,
**24** has crowned them with haloes, making them like gods.

**25** She has set up the Viper, the Dragon, and the Sphinx,
**26** the Great-Lion, the Mad-Dog, and the Scorpion-Man,
**27** mighty lion-demons, the Dragon-Fly, the Centaur—
**28** bearing weapons that spare not, fearless in battle.

**29** Firm are her decrees, past withstanding are they.
**30** Withal eleven of this kind she has brought forth.
**31** From among the gods, her firstborn, who formed her Assembly,
**32** she has elevated Kingu, has made him chief among them.

**33** The leading of the ranks, command of the Assembly,
**34** the raising of weapons for the encounter, advancing to combat,
**35** in battle the command-in-chief—
**36** these to his hand she has entrusted.

**37** She has given him the Tablet of Destinies, fastened on his breast:
**38** 'Your command shall be unchangeable, your word shall endure!'"

**39** When Anshar heard that Tiamat was sorely troubled,
**40** he smote his loins and bit his lips.
**41** Gloomy was his heart, restless his mood.
**42** He covered his mouth to stifle his outcry:

**43** "Battle! You shall engage and turn back her powers!
**44** Go, my son, you who know all wisdom,
**45** compose Tiamat with your holy spell.
**46** On the storm-chariot proceed at once!"

**47** [Ea goes but cannot face Tiamat. Anu goes but retreats.]

**48** Anshar was speechless, staring at the ground,
**49** hair on end, shaking his head at Ea.
**50** All the Anunnaki gathered to that place.
**51** Their lips closed tight, they sat in silence.

**52** "No god can go to battle and,
**53** facing Tiamat, escape with his life."

**54** Lord Anshar, father of the gods, arose in grandeur,
**55** and having pondered in his heart, he said to the Anunnaki:
**56** "He whose strength is potent shall be our avenger,
**57** the mighty in combat, Marduk, the hero!"

**58** Ea called Marduk to his place of seclusion.
**59** Giving counsel, he told him what was in his heart:
**60** "O Marduk, consider my advice. Hearken to your father,

**61** for you are my son who comforts his heart.
**62** When facing Anshar, approach as though in combat;
**63** stand up as you speak; seeing you, he will be at rest."

**64** The lord rejoiced at the word of his father,
**65** he approached and stood up facing Anshar.
**66** When Anshar saw him, his heart filled with joy.
**67** He kissed his lips, his own gloom lifted.

**68** "Anshar, be not muted; open wide your lips.
**69** I will go and attain your heart's desire.
**70** What male is it who has pressed his fight against you?
**71** It is but Tiamat, a woman, that flies at you with weapons!

**72** O my father-creator, be glad and rejoice;
**73** the neck of Tiamat you shall soon tread upon!
**74** O my father-creator, be glad and rejoice;
**75** the neck of Tiamat you shall soon tread upon!"

**76** "My son, you who know all wisdom,
**77** compose Tiamat with your holy spell.
**78** On the storm-chariot proceed at once!
**79** From her presence they shall not drive you! Turn them back!"

**80** The lord rejoiced at the word of his father.
**81** His heart exulting, he said to his father:
**82** "Creator of the gods, destiny of the great gods,
**83** if I indeed, as your avenger,
**84** am to vanquish Tiamat and save your lives,

**85** set up the Assembly, proclaim supreme my destiny!
**86** When in Ubshukinna jointly you sit down rejoicing,
**87** let my word, instead of yours, determine the fates.
**88** Unalterable shall be what I may bring into being;
**89** neither recalled nor changed shall be the command of my lips."
