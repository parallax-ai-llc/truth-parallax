**Tablet IV**

**1** They erected for him a princely throne.
**2** Facing his fathers he sat down, presiding.
**3** "You are the most honoured of the great gods,
**4** your decree is unrivalled, your command is Anu.

**5** You, Marduk, are the most honoured of the great gods,
**6** your decree is unrivalled, your word is Anu.
**7** From this day your pronouncement shall be unchangeable.
**8** To raise or bring low—these shall be in your hand.

**9** Your utterance shall be true, your command shall be unimpeachable.
**10** No one among the gods shall transgress your bounds!

**11** O Marduk, you are our avenger.
**12** We have granted you kingship over the universe entire.
**13** When you sit in Assembly your word shall be supreme.
**14** Your weapons shall not fail; they shall smash your foes!

**15** O lord, spare the life of him who trusts you,
**16** but pour out the life of the god who seized evil."

**17** Having placed in their midst a piece of cloth,
**18** they addressed themselves to Marduk, their firstborn:
**19** "Lord, truly your decree is first among gods.
**20** Say but to wreck or create; it shall be.

**21** Open your mouth: the cloth will vanish!
**22** Speak again, and the cloth shall be whole!"
**23** At the word of his mouth the cloth vanished.
**24** He spoke again, and the cloth was restored.

**25** When the gods, his fathers, saw the fruit of his word,
**26** joyfully they did homage: "Marduk is king!"
**27** They conferred on him sceptre, throne, and vestment;
**28** they gave him matchless weapons that ward off the foes:

**29** "Go and cut off the life of Tiamat.
**30** May the winds bear her blood to places undisclosed."

**31** The lords fixed Marduk's destiny,
**32** they made him set out on the path of prosperity and success.

**33** He constructed a bow, marked it as his weapon,
**34** attached thereto the arrow, fixed its bowstring.
**35** He raised the mace, made his right hand grasp it;
**36** bow and quiver he hung at his side.

**37** He placed the lightning in front of him.
**38** With a blazing flame he filled his body.
**39** He then made a net to enfold Tiamat therein.
**40** The four winds he stationed that nothing of her might escape:

**41** the South Wind, the North Wind, the East Wind, the West Wind.
**42** Close to his side he held the net, the gift of his father, Anu.
**43** He brought forth Imhullu, the Evil Wind, the Whirlwind, the Hurricane,
**44** the Fourfold Wind, the Sevenfold Wind, the Cyclone, the Matchless Wind;

**45** then he sent forth the winds he had brought forth, the seven of them.
**46** To stir up the inside of Tiamat they rose up behind him.
**47** Then the lord raised the flood-storm, his mighty weapon.

**48** He mounted the storm-chariot, irresistible and terrifying.
**49** He harnessed and yoked to it a team of four,
**50** the Killer, the Relentless, the Trampler, the Swift.
**51** Sharp were their teeth, bearing poison.

**52** They were versed in ravage, skilled in destruction.
**53** On his right he posted the Smiter, fearsome in battle,
**54** on his left the Combat, which repels all the zealous.

**55** For a cloak he was wrapped in an armour of terror;
**56** with his fearsome halo his head was turbaned.
**57** The lord went forth and followed his course,
**58** towards the raging Tiamat he set his face.

**59** In his lips he held a spell,
**60** a plant to put out poison was grasped in his hand.
**61** Then they milled about him, the gods milled about him,
**62** the gods, his fathers, milled about him, the gods milled about him.

**63** The lord approached to scan the inside of Tiamat,
**64** and of Kingu, her consort, the scheme to perceive.
**65** As he looks on, his course becomes upset,
**66** his will is distracted and his doings are confused.

**67** And when the gods, his helpers, who marched at his side,
**68** saw the valiant hero, blurred became their vision.

**69** Tiamat emitted a cry, without turning her neck,
**70** framing savage defiance in her lips:
**71** "Too important are you for the lord of the gods to rise up against you!
**72** Is it in their place that they have gathered, or in your place?"

**73** Thereupon the lord, having raised the flood-storm, his mighty weapon,
**74** to enraged Tiamat he sent word as follows:
**75** "Why are you risen, why have you arrogantly exalted?
**76** You have charged your own heart to stir up conflict,

**77** so that sons reject their own fathers,
**78** and you, who have born them, hate—
**79** You have aggrandized Kingu to be your consort;
**80** a rule, not rightfully his, you have substituted for the rule of Anu.

**81** Against Anshar, king of the gods, you seek evil;
**82** against the gods, my fathers, you have confirmed your wickedness.
**83** Though drawn up be your forces, girded on your weapons,
**84** stand up, that I and you might meet in single combat!"

**85** When Tiamat heard this,
**86** she was like one possessed; she took leave of her senses.
**87** In fury Tiamat cried out aloud.
**88** To the roots her legs shook both together.

**89** She recited a charm, she cast her spell,
**90** while the gods of battle sharpened their weapons.
**91** Then joined issue Tiamat and Marduk, wisest of gods.
**92** They strove in single combat, locked in battle.

**93** The lord spread out his net to enfold her,
**94** the Evil Wind, which followed behind, he let loose in her face.
**95** When Tiamat opened her mouth to consume him,
**96** he drove in the Evil Wind that she close not her lips.

**97** As the fierce winds charged her belly,
**98** her body was distended and her mouth was wide open.
**99** He released the arrow, it tore her belly,
**100** it cut through her insides, splitting the heart.

**101** Having thus subdued her, he extinguished her life.
**102** He cast down her carcass to stand upon it.

**103** After he had slain Tiamat, the leader,
**104** her band was shattered, her troupe broken up;
**105** and the gods, her helpers who marched at her side,
**106** trembling with terror, turned their backs about,

**107** in order to save and preserve their lives.
**108** Tightly encircled, they could not escape.
**109** He made them captives and he smashed their weapons.
**110** Thrown into the net, they found themselves ensnared;

**111** placed in cells, they were filled with wailing;
**112** bearing his wrath, they were held imprisoned.

**113** And the eleven creatures which she had charged with awe,
**114** the whole band of demons that marched on her right,
**115** he cast into fetters, their hands he bound.
**116** For all their resistance, he trampled them underfoot.

**117** And Kingu, who had been made chief among them,
**118** he bound and accounted him to Uggae.
**119** He took from him the Tablet of Destinies, not rightfully his,
**120** sealed it with a seal and fastened it on his breast.

**121** When he had vanquished and subdued his adversaries,
**122** had wholly established Anshar's triumph over the foe,
**123** had attained the purpose of Nudimmud, valiant Marduk
**124** strengthened his hold on the vanquished gods,

**125** and turned back to Tiamat whom he had bound.
**126** The lord trod on the legs of Tiamat,
**127** with his unsparing mace he crushed her skull.
**128** When the arteries of her blood he had severed,

**129** the North Wind bore it to places undisclosed.
**130** On seeing this, his fathers were joyful and jubilant,
**131** they brought gifts of homage, they to him.

**132** Then the lord paused to view her dead body,
**133** that he might divide the monster and do artful works.
**134** He split her like a shellfish into two parts:
**135** half of her he set up and ceiled it as sky,

**136** pulled down the bar and posted guards.
**137** He bade them to allow not her waters to escape.
**138** He crossed the heavens and surveyed the regions.
**139** He squared Apsu's quarter, the abode of Nudimmud,

**140** as the lord measured the dimensions of Apsu.
**141** The Great Abode, its likeness, he fixed as Esharra,
**142** the Great Abode, Esharra, which he made as the firmament.
**143** Anu, Enlil, and Ea he made occupy their places.
