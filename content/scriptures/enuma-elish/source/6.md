**Tablet VI**

**1** When Marduk hears the words of the gods,
**2** his heart prompts him to fashion artful works.
**3** Opening his mouth, he addresses Ea
**4** to impart the plan he had conceived in his heart:

**5** "Blood I will mass and cause bones to be.
**6** I will establish a savage, 'man' shall be his name.
**7** Truly, savage-man I will create.
**8** He shall be charged with the service of the gods
**9** that they might be at ease!

**10** The ways of the gods I will artfully alter.
**11** Though alike revered, into two groups they shall be divided."

**12** Ea answered him, speaking a word to him,
**13** giving him another plan for the relief of the gods:
**14** "Let but one of their brothers be handed over;
**15** he alone shall perish that mankind may be fashioned.

**16** Let the great gods be here in Assembly,
**17** let the guilty be handed over that they may endure."

**18** Marduk summoned the great gods to Assembly;
**19** presiding graciously, he issued instructions.
**20** To his utterance the gods pay heed.
**21** The king addressed a word to the Anunnaki:

**22** "If your former statement was true,
**23** do you now also truth on oath by me declare!
**24** Who was it that contrived the uprising,
**25** and made Tiamat rebel, and joined battle?

**26** Let him be handed over who contrived the uprising.
**27** His guilt I will make him bear. You shall dwell in peace!"

**28** The Igigi, the great gods, replied to him,
**29** to Lugaldimmerankia, counsellor of the gods, their lord:
**30** "It was Kingu who contrived the uprising,
**31** and made Tiamat rebel, and joined battle."

**32** They bound him, holding him before Ea.
**33** They imposed on him his guilt and severed his blood vessels.
**34** Out of his blood they fashioned mankind.
**35** He imposed the service and let free the gods.

**36** After Ea, the wise, had created mankind,
**37** had imposed upon it the service of the gods—
**38** that work was beyond comprehension;
**39** as artfully planned by Marduk, did Nudimmud create it—

**40** Marduk, the king of the gods, divided
**41** all the Anunnaki above and below.
**42** He assigned them to Anu to guard his instructions.
**43** Three hundred in the heavens he stationed as a guard.

**44** In like manner the ways of the earth he defined.
**45** In heaven and on earth six hundred thus he settled.
**46** After he had ordered all the instructions,
**47** to the Anunnaki of heaven and earth had allotted their portions,

**48** the Anunnaki opened their mouths
**49** and said to Marduk, their lord:
**50** "Now, O lord, you who have caused our deliverance,
**51** what shall be our homage to you?

**52** Let us build a shrine whose name shall be called
**53** 'Lo, a chamber for our nightly rest'; let us repose in it!
**54** Let us build a throne, a recess for his abode!
**55** On the day that we arrive we shall repose in it."

**56** When Marduk heard this,
**57** brightly glowed his features, like the day:
**58** "Like that of lofty Babylon, whose building you have requested,
**59** let its brickwork be fashioned. You shall name it 'The Sanctuary.'"

**60** The Anunnaki applied the implement;
**61** for one whole year they moulded bricks.
**62** When the second year arrived,
**63** they raised high the head of Esagila equalling Apsu.

**64** Having built a stage-tower as high as Apsu,
**65** they set up in it an abode for Marduk, Enlil, and Ea.
**66** In their presence he adorned it in grandeur.
**67** To the base of Esharra its horns look down.

**68** After they had achieved the building of Esagila,
**69** the Anunnaki themselves erected their shrines.
**70** All of them gathered, three hundred of heaven and of earth.
**71** Enlil raised high the goblet before them in the place they had built as his dwelling.

**72** "This is Babylon, the place that is your home!
**73** Make merry in its precincts, occupy its broad places."
**74** The great gods took their seats,
**75** they set up festive drink, sat down to a banquet.

**76** After they had made merry within it,
**77** in Esagila, the splendid, had performed their rites,
**78** the norms had been fixed and all their portents,
**79** all the gods apportioned the stations of heaven and earth.

**80** The fifty great gods took their seats.
**81** The seven gods of destiny set up the three hundred in heaven.

**82** Enlil raised the bow, his weapon, and laid it before them.
**83** The gods, his fathers, saw the net he had made.
**84** When they beheld the bow, how skilful its shape,
**85** his fathers praised the work he had wrought.

**86** Raising it, Anu spoke up in the Assembly of the gods,
**87** as he kissed the bow: "This is my daughter!"
**88** He named the names of the bow as follows:
**89** "Longwood is the first, the second is Accurate;
**90** its third name is Bow-Star, in heaven I have made it shine."

**91** He fixed a place which the gods, its brothers, should—
**92** After Anu had decreed the fate of the Bow,
**93** and had placed the exalted royal throne before the gods,
**94** Anu seated it in the Assembly of the gods.

**95** When the great gods had assembled,
**96** and had duly exalted the destiny of Marduk,
**97** they pronounced among themselves a curse,
**98** swearing by water and oil to place life in jeopardy.

**99** When they had granted him the exercise of kingship of the gods,
**100** they confirmed him in dominion over the gods of heaven and earth.

**101** Anshar pronounced supreme his name Asarluhi:
**102** "Let us make humble obeisance at the mention of his name;
**103** when he speaks, the gods shall pay heed to him.
**104** Let his utterance be supreme above and below!"

**105** "Most exalted be the Son, our avenger;
**106** let his sovereignty be surpassing, having no rival.
**107** May he shepherd the black-headed ones, his creatures.
**108** To the end of days, without forgetting, let them acclaim his ways.

**109** May he establish for his fathers the great food-offerings;
**110** their support they shall furnish, shall tend their sanctuaries.
**111** May he cause incense to be smelled, their spells—
**112** a likeness on earth of what he has wrought in heaven.

**113** May he order the black-headed to revere him,
**114** may the subjects ever bear in mind their god,
**115** and may they at his word pay heed to the goddess.
**116** May food-offerings be borne for their gods and goddesses.

**117** Without fail let them support their gods!
**118** Their lands let them improve, build their shrines,
**119** let the black-headed wait on their gods.
**120** As for us, by however many names we pronounce, he is our god!

**121** Let us then proclaim his fifty names!"
