## Overview

Comparative study of the Enuma Elish examines this Babylonian creation epic alongside other ancient cosmogonies, theogonies, and creation narratives. The text has been central to comparative mythology and biblical studies since its discovery in the 19th century.

## Key Comparative Themes

### Creation from Chaos
The motif of creation through the defeat of a chaos monster appears widely:
- **Genesis 1**: God subduing the primordial waters (tehom/Tiamat)
- **Ugaritic mythology**: Baal's defeat of Yam (Sea)
- **Norse mythology**: The creation of the world from Ymir's body
- **Hindu mythology**: Indra's defeat of Vritra

### Theogony and Divine Succession
The succession of divine generations parallels:
- **Hesiod's Theogony**: Ouranos > Kronos > Zeus
- **Hurrian-Hittite myths**: Kumarbi cycle
- **Phoenician cosmogony**: As preserved by Philo of Byblos

### Creation of Humanity
The creation of humans from divine material parallels:
- **Genesis 2**: Adam formed from dust, animated by divine breath
- **Atrahasis**: Humans made from clay mixed with a slain god's blood
- **Greek mythology**: Prometheus fashioning humans from clay
