## Overview

The Enuma Elish ("When on High") is the Babylonian creation epic, composed in Akkadian cuneiform on seven clay tablets. Dating to approximately 1100 BCE in its written form, though likely drawing on older oral traditions, it was discovered among the ruins of the library of Ashurbanipal at Nineveh in the mid-19th century by Austen Henry Layard and later published by George Smith in 1876.

The epic narrates the creation of the world through a cosmic battle between the god Marduk and the primordial sea goddess Tiamat. It serves simultaneously as a cosmogony (creation account), a theogony (account of divine origins), and a political-theological justification for Marduk's supremacy as king of the gods and for Babylon's preeminence among cities.

## Structure

The Enuma Elish is inscribed on seven cuneiform tablets:

1. **Tablet I**: Primordial chaos, the birth of the gods, and the conflict between generations
2. **Tablet II**: The threat of Tiamat and the search for a champion among the gods
3. **Tablet III**: Anshar's embassy to the elder gods
4. **Tablet IV**: Marduk's battle with Tiamat and the creation of the heavens and earth from her body
5. **Tablet V**: The ordering of the cosmos — stars, calendar, and celestial bodies
6. **Tablet VI**: The creation of humanity from the blood of Kingu, and the building of Babylon
7. **Tablet VII**: The fifty names of Marduk and the establishment of his supreme authority

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Enuma Elish holds significance across multiple fields:

- **Academic**: A key text for understanding Babylonian religion, politics, and cosmology
- **Comparative**: Extensively compared with Genesis 1, Hesiod's Theogony, and other creation accounts
- **Theological**: Raises fundamental questions about creation, divine power, and cosmic order
- **Literary**: A masterwork of ancient Mesopotamian mythological poetry
- **Archaeological**: Its discovery transformed the study of biblical and ancient Near Eastern origins
- **Interfaith**: Illuminates universal human attempts to understand cosmic origins and divine purpose
