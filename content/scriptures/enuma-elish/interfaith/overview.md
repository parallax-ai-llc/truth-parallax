## Overview

An interfaith approach to the Enuma Elish examines this Babylonian creation epic alongside other religious traditions' accounts of cosmic origins, divine sovereignty, and human purpose. The text raises questions about creation, order, and meaning that have parallels in virtually every religious tradition.

## Key Interfaith Themes

### Creation and Cosmic Origins
The Enuma Elish's creation account invites comparison with:
- **Judaism and Christianity**: Genesis 1-2 and creation ex nihilo
- **Islam**: The Quran's creation accounts
- **Hinduism**: The Nasadiya Sukta (Hymn of Creation) and Puranic cosmogonies
- **Chinese religion**: Pan Gu and the cosmic egg

### Order from Chaos
The triumph of order over chaos resonates with:
- **Judaism**: God's mastery over the primordial waters (tehom)
- **Hinduism**: The churning of the cosmic ocean (Samudra Manthan)
- **Norse religion**: The creation from Ginnungagap
- **Zoroastrianism**: The cosmic struggle between Ahura Mazda and Angra Mainyu

### Human Purpose and Service
The creation of humanity to serve the gods raises questions addressed by many traditions:
- **Christianity**: Humanity created for relationship with God, not mere servitude
- **Islam**: Humans as God's khalifah (vicegerent) on earth
- **Buddhism**: The preciousness of human birth as an opportunity for liberation
- **Judaism**: Humanity as partner in tikkun olam (repair of the world)
