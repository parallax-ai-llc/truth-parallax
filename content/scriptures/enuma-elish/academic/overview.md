## Overview

Academic study of the Enuma Elish encompasses cuneiform philology, Babylonian religion, ancient political theology, and comparative mythology. The text is a cornerstone of Assyriology and has generated extensive scholarly debate about its date, purpose, and relationship to other ancient creation accounts.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Philological Study
Scholars work with the Akkadian cuneiform text to establish reliable readings, analyze grammatical structures, and understand the specialized religious vocabulary employed in the composition. W.G. Lambert's critical edition remains the standard reference.

### Political-Theological Analysis
The Enuma Elish is studied as a work of political theology that legitimizes Marduk's supremacy among the gods and, by extension, Babylon's preeminence among cities. Scholars debate whether the text was composed to support specific political agendas.

### Comparative Mythology
Extensive academic attention has been devoted to comparing the Enuma Elish with Genesis 1, Hesiod's Theogony, Ugaritic myths, and other ancient creation accounts. These comparisons illuminate both shared ancient Near Eastern traditions and distinctive features of each account.
