The Maitreyi dialogue's teaching that "it is for the sake of the self that all is dear" resonates with Augustine's insight that all desire is ultimately desire for God, and with the Buddhist teaching that attachment to impermanent things causes suffering precisely because what we truly seek — the unconditioned — cannot be found in them. Each tradition recognizes that human longing points beyond its immediate objects to something ultimate.

The Madhu Vidya's vision of all things as mutually nourishing "honey" resonates with the Buddhist doctrine of *pratityasamutpada* (dependent origination), the Confucian concept of cosmic harmony, and the ecological insight that all living systems are interconnected webs of mutual dependence. The Stoic concept of *sympatheia* — the cosmic sympathy linking all parts of the universe — provides another parallel. Each tradition perceives an underlying unity connecting the diverse elements of the world.
