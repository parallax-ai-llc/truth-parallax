The lineage lists of Chapter 6, tracing knowledge transmission through chains of named teachers, parallel the *isnad* (chain of transmission) in Islamic hadith scholarship, the apostolic succession in Christianity, and the Zen Buddhist lineage charts tracing dharma transmission from the Buddha. Each tradition recognizes that authentic spiritual knowledge requires not merely texts but living transmission through qualified teachers.

The rites of passage described — marking conception, birth, and death — belong to the universal human practice of sacralizing life's transitions. Arnold van Gennep's classic study of *rites de passage* identified the tripartite structure (separation, transition, incorporation) that appears in these Vedic rituals as in rituals worldwide. The death ritual's description of the self's faculties being withdrawn one by one resonates with the Tibetan Buddhist description of the dissolution of elements at death and the Egyptian funerary texts' account of the soul's departure from the body.
