## Overview

The Brihadaranyaka Upanishad's "neti neti" method — approaching the Absolute through systematic negation — stands as one of the world's earliest and most influential examples of apophatic (negative) theology. It invites direct comparison with Pseudo-Dionysius's *Via Negativa* in Christianity, Maimonides' negative theology in Judaism, and the Mu'tazilite rejection of anthropomorphism in Islam. The insight that the ultimate reality transcends all categories of thought appears independently across civilizations.

## Comparative Themes

Yajnavalkya's debates at King Janaka's court parallel the Socratic dialogues in method if not in content — both employ rigorous questioning to expose the inadequacy of conventional knowledge. The Maitreyi dialogue's teaching that love is ultimately self-referential resonates with Augustine's insight that all love is ultimately love of God. The Brihadaranyaka's teaching on deep sleep as a state of undifferentiated awareness has been compared with the Plotinian One, the Buddhist *nirodha samapatti*, and the Christian mystical "dark night of the soul" — all pointing to a reality beyond subject-object duality.
