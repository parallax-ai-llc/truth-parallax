The teaching of the three Da's — self-control, generosity, and compassion — presents a universal ethical triad that resonates across traditions. The Buddhist *paramitas* (perfections) of *sila* (morality), *dana* (generosity), and *karuna* (compassion) closely parallel these three virtues. The Christian theological virtues of temperance, charity, and mercy provide another correspondence. The convergence suggests that contemplative traditions worldwide identify similar prerequisites for spiritual realization.

The chapter's meditation on truth as rooted in Being (*sat*) resonates with Plato's identification of the Good with Being, the Islamic concept of *al-Haqq* (the Real/True) as a name of God, and the Buddhist teaching on *sacca* (truth) as fundamental to the path. The insight that truthfulness is not merely an ethical virtue but an ontological alignment with reality itself appears independently in traditions that had no historical contact.
