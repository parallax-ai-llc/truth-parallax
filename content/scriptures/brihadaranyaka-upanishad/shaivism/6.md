The lineage lists of Chapter 6 resonate with the Shaiva tradition's emphasis on *guru-parampara* — the unbroken chain of teachers through which Shiva's wisdom is transmitted. The Shaiva understanding of initiation (*diksha*) as the fundamental rite of spiritual transformation gives special significance to the chapter's account of how sacred knowledge passes from teacher to student. Each teacher in the lineage is a vessel of Shiva's grace, not merely a transmitter of information.

The rites of passage described in this chapter — particularly the death rituals — are understood through the Shaiva lens as moments of transition when the soul's relationship to consciousness is transformed. The withdrawal of faculties at death mirrors the dissolution of the *tattvas* (categories of manifestation) in Shaiva cosmology — a progressive return from multiplicity to the unity of Shiva-consciousness.
