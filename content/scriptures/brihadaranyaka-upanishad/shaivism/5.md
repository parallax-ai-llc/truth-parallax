Chapter 5's meditations and ethical teachings resonate with the Shaiva understanding of spiritual practice as the progressive refinement of awareness. The teaching of the three Da's (5.2) — self-control, generosity, and compassion — represents the ethical preparation necessary for the recognition of Shiva-consciousness. Without the purification achieved through ethical living, the mind remains too turbulent for the subtle perception required by *pratyabhijna* (recognition).

The meditations on truth, the Gayatri, and the vital breaths are understood as *dharanas* (concentrative practices) that focus awareness on increasingly subtle aspects of Shiva's manifestation. The chapter's emphasis on the relationship between ethical conduct and spiritual realization aligns with the Shaiva teaching that liberation requires the removal of *karma mala* (the impurity of accumulated actions) through righteous living and contemplative practice.
