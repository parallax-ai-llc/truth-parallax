---
title: "Brihadaranyaka Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "brihadaranyaka-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Janaka, king of Videha, performed a sacrifice at which gifts were freely distributed. Brahmanas from the Kuru and Panchala countries were assembled there. Janaka wished to know which of these Brahmanas was best versed in the Vedas. He enclosed a thousand cows, and to the horns of each ten padas of gold were fastened.

**2** He said to them: "Ye venerable Brahmanas, whoever among you is the wisest, let him drive away these cows." No one dared. Then Yajnavalkya said to his pupil: "Dear Samasravas, drive them away." He drove them away. The Brahmanas were angry: "How dare he call himself the wisest among us?"

**3** Then Asvala, the Hotri priest of Janaka, asked him: "Yajnavalkya, are you indeed the wisest among us?" Yajnavalkya replied: "I bow to the wisest, but I do want the cows."

**4** Then Asvala asked: "Yajnavalkya, since everything here is overtaken by death, since everything is overcome by death, by what means is the sacrificer freed beyond the reach of death?" Yajnavalkya said: "By the fire-priest, by fire, by speech. For speech is the fire of the sacrifice. Speech is this fire, speech is this freedom, speech is perfect liberation."

**5** Then Artabhaga asked: "Yajnavalkya, when a man dies, what does not leave him?" Yajnavalkya said: "The name. Endless is the name, and endless are the gods. He who knows this wins an endless world."

**6** Then Bhujyu asked: "Yajnavalkya, where did the Parikshitas go?" Yajnavalkya answered: "They went where the performers of horse-sacrifices go — to the world of the sun, which extends as far as thirty-two days' journey of a horse-drawn chariot. The earth surrounds it on every side, twice as wide. And the earth is surrounded by the ocean on every side, twice as wide."

**7** Then Ushasta asked: "Yajnavalkya, explain to me the Brahman that is present and not beyond our ken, that is the Self within all." Yajnavalkya said: "This is your Self which is within all. That which breathes through the breath is your Self within all; that which breathes down through the out-breath is your Self within all; that which pervades through the pervading breath is your Self within all; that which breathes upward through the up-breath is your Self within all. This is your Self which is within all."

**8** Ushasta said: "You have explained it as one might say, 'This is a cow, this is a horse.' Explain to me the Brahman that is present and not beyond our ken, that is the Self within all." Yajnavalkya said: "You cannot see the seer of seeing, you cannot hear the hearer of hearing, you cannot think the thinker of thinking, you cannot know the knower of knowing. This is your Self which is within all. Everything else is of evil."

**9** Then Gargi asked: "Yajnavalkya, if all this is pervaded by water, by what then is water pervaded?" He said: "By air." "By what is air pervaded?" "By the sky." "By what is the sky pervaded?" "By the worlds of the Gandharvas." And she continued asking, and he answered through the worlds of sun, moon, stars, gods, Indra, Prajapati, up to the worlds of Brahman.

**10** Yajnavalkya said: "That, O Gargi, which is above the sky, which is beneath the earth, which is between these two — sky and earth — which people call the past and present and future: that is woven, like warp and woof, in the Imperishable." Gargi said: "Venerable Brahmanas, you should consider it a great thing if you get off from this man by merely bowing to him. None of you will ever defeat him in arguments about Brahman."
