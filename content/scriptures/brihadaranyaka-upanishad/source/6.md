---
title: "Brihadaranyaka Upanishad - Chapter 6"
scripture: "brihadaranyaka-upanishad"
chapter: 6
---

**1** He who knows the first and the best becomes the first and best of his people. Breath is the first and the best. He who knows this becomes the first and best of his people.

**2** The vital breaths once disputed among themselves as to who was the best. They went to Brahma and asked: "Who is the most excellent among us?" He said: "He by whose departure the body seems worse than worst, he is the most excellent among you."

**3** Speech departed. Having been absent for a year, it came back and said: "How have you been able to live without me?" The other breaths said: "Like mute people, not speaking, but breathing with the breath, seeing with the eye, hearing with the ear, thinking with the mind. Thus we lived." Speech entered in again.

**4** The eye departed. Having been absent for a year, it came back and said: "How have you been able to live without me?" They said: "Like blind people, not seeing, but breathing with the breath, speaking with the tongue, hearing with the ear, thinking with the mind. Thus we lived." The eye entered in again.

**5** The ear departed and returned similarly, and the mind, and each time the others had survived. But when the vital breath was about to depart, it tore up the other breaths, as a great horse, going to start, might tear up the pegs to which he is tethered.

**6** They said: "Sir, do not depart. We shall not be able to live without you." The vital breath said: "Then make me an offering." They said: "So be it."

**7** Therefore the vital breath is called Ayasya, for it is the essence of all the members. From the vital breath all these are born, into the vital breath they enter at death.

**8** Now follows the line of teachers. This knowledge was handed down from Brahma to Prajapati, from Prajapati to Manu, from Manu to mankind.

**9** Lead me from the unreal to the real. Lead me from darkness to light. Lead me from death to immortality. Om. Peace, peace, peace.

**10** He who knows this — his self becomes the Self of all beings. As all spokes are contained in the axle and in the rim of a wheel, all beings and all selves are contained in that Self. Thus ends the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad.
