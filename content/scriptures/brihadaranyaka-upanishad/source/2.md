---
title: "Brihadaranyaka Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "brihadaranyaka-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** There was once Gargya Balaki, a man of great learning. He said to Ajatasatru, the king of Kasi: "Shall I tell you about Brahman?" Ajatasatru said: "We give a thousand cows for that speech. People would rush to us, crying 'A Janaka! A Janaka!' — meaning a truly liberal patron."

**2** Gargya said: "The person who is in the sun, that one I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said: "Do not make me argue on this. I worship him as the supreme, as the head of all beings, as the king. He who worships him thus becomes supreme and the head of all beings."

**3** Gargya said: "The person who is in the moon, that one I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said: "Do not make me argue on this. I worship him as the great, clad in white, as Soma the king. He who worships him thus — soma is pressed out for him day by day."

**4** Gargya said: "The person who is in the lightning, that one I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said: "Do not make me argue on this. I worship him as the luminous. He who worships him thus becomes luminous, and his offspring become luminous."

**5** Then Gargya was silent. Ajatasatru said: "Is that all?" Gargya said: "That is all." Ajatasatru said: "With so much only one cannot know Brahman."

**6** Then Gargya said: "Let me come to you as a pupil." Ajatasatru said: "It is against the grain that a Brahmana should come to a Kshatriya, saying, 'Tell me about Brahman.' However, I shall make you know Him clearly."

**7** He took him by the hand, and they went to a person who was asleep. He called him by these names: "O great one, clad in white, O Soma, O King." He did not rise. Then rubbing him with the hand, he woke him, and he arose.

**8** Ajatasatru said: "When this man was thus asleep, where was then the person of intelligence, and from whence did he thus come back?" Gargya did not know.

**9** Ajatasatru said: "When this man was thus asleep, the person of intelligence, having through the intelligence of the senses absorbed within himself all intelligence, lies in the space within the heart. When he takes in these senses, the person is said to be asleep. Then the breath is kept in, speech is kept in, the ear is kept in, the eye is kept in, the mind is kept in."

**10** As a spider comes out with its thread, as small sparks come forth from fire, thus from this Self come forth all vital breaths, all worlds, all gods, all beings. Its secret meaning is "the truth of truth." The vital breaths are truth, and their truth is the Self.
