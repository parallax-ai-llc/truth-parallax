---
title: "Brihadaranyaka Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "brihadaranyaka-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** That is full, this is full. From the full the full is taken. When the full is taken from the full, the full still remains. Om. Peace, peace, peace.

**2** The threefold offspring of Prajapati — gods, men, and demons — dwelt as students with their father Prajapati. Having completed their studentship, the gods said: "Tell us something, Sir." He told them the syllable "Da." "Did you understand?" he asked. "We did," they said. "You told us Damyata — be self-controlled." "Yes," he said, "you have understood."

**3** Then the men said to him: "Tell us something, Sir." He told them the same syllable "Da." "Did you understand?" he asked. "We did," they said. "You told us Datta — give." "Yes," he said, "you have understood."

**4** Then the demons said to him: "Tell us something, Sir." He told them the same syllable "Da." "Did you understand?" he asked. "We did," they said. "You told us Dayadhvam — be compassionate." "Yes," he said, "you have understood."

**5** The divine voice of thunder repeats the same: Da-Da-Da, that is, Be self-controlled, Give, Be compassionate. Therefore let a man learn these three: self-control, charity, and compassion.

**6** This, verily, is that great unborn Self, who is undecaying, undying, immortal, fearless — Brahman. Fearless is Brahman, and he who knows this becomes verily the fearless Brahman.

**7** In the beginning this was Self alone, one only. He desired: "Let there be a wife for me that I may have offspring, and let there be wealth for me that I may offer sacrifices." Verily, this is the whole extent of desire. Even if one wishes, one cannot get more than this.

**8** There is the city of Brahman, and in it the palace, the small lotus of the heart, and in it that small ether. What exists within that small ether, that is to be sought, that is to be understood.

**9** As large as this ether is, so large is that ether within the heart. Both heaven and earth are contained within it, both fire and air, both sun and moon, both lightning and stars; whatever there is of him in this world and whatever is not, all that is contained within it.

**10** That Self is not this, not this. He is unseizable, for He is not seized; He is indestructible, for He cannot be destroyed; He is unattached, for He does not attach Himself; He is unbound, He does not tremble, He is not injured. Him who knows this, these two thoughts do not overcome: "For this I did an evil deed," and "For this I did a good deed." He overcomes both. What he has done and what he has not done do not affect him.
