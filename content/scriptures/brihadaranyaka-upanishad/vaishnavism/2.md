Chapter 2 contains two landmark teachings: the Madhu Vidya (honey doctrine) and Yajnavalkya's first dialogue with his wife Maitreyi. The Madhu Vidya (2.5) teaches that every element of the cosmos — earth, water, fire, air — is "honey" for every other element, meaning that all things are interconnected in mutual nourishment. Vaishnavas read this as revealing Vishnu's role as the sustainer (*bharta*) who connects all beings through His immanent presence.

The Maitreyi dialogue (2.4) is among the most profound passages in the Upanishads. When Yajnavalkya prepares to renounce the world, Maitreyi asks whether wealth can give her immortality. Yajnavalkya's answer — "It is for the sake of the Self that all is dear" — is read by Vaishnavas as teaching that all love is ultimately love of the Lord who dwells within. The self that makes all things dear is Vishnu as the *antaryamin*, and true love means recognizing this divine presence in every relationship.
