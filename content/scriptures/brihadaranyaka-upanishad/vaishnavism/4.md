Chapter 4 contains Yajnavalkya's systematic final teaching to King Janaka, progressing from the nature of the self to the states of consciousness and culminating in the teaching on liberation. The Maitreyi dialogue reappears in an expanded form (4.5). Vaishnavas read this chapter as Yajnavalkya's revelation of Vishnu as the true self who illuminates waking, dreaming, and deep sleep. The teaching that the self is "the light of lights" (*jyotisham jyotih*, 4.4.16) is identified with Vishnu's self-luminous nature.

The detailed teaching on the states of consciousness (4.3) — where the self creates dream worlds from its own light and in deep sleep "is embraced by the all-knowing Self" — is read as describing the soul's relationship to Vishnu in different states. In deep sleep, the individual soul rests in the Lord's embrace, temporarily freed from karma's effects. The teaching on liberation (4.4) describes the soul that has known the Lord departing at death and reaching Vishnu's eternal abode, never to return.
