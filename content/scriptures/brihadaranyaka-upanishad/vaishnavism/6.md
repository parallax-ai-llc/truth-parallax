Chapter 6 presents lineage lists (*vamsha*), rites of passage, and practical rituals, which Vaishnavas read as demonstrating the importance of tradition (*sampradaya*) in transmitting divine knowledge. The teacher-student lineages traced here show how knowledge of Vishnu has been preserved and transmitted through an unbroken chain of devotees. The chapter's rites — including those for conception, birth, and death — are understood as sacraments that mark the soul's journey through embodied life under Vishnu's care.

The ritual for the dying person (6.1) — in which the self's faculties are withdrawn one by one — is read as the process by which Vishnu gathers the soul's capacities back to Himself at the moment of death. The devotee who has lived in awareness of the Lord faces this process with confidence, knowing that Vishnu will guide the soul to its proper destination.
