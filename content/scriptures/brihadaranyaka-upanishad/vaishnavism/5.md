Chapter 5 presents a series of meditations and ethical teachings that Vaishnavas read as practical instructions for the devotee's daily life. The famous teaching of the three "Da's" (5.2) — *dama* (self-control), *dana* (generosity), and *daya* (compassion) — is read as the ethical foundation of Vaishnava devotion. These virtues prepare the heart for the reception of Vishnu's grace. The meditation on the Gayatri as the protector of all life (5.14) points to Vishnu as the solar deity who sustains and protects all beings.

The chapter's meditations on truth (*satya*) as having the syllable *sat* (being) at its root (5.5) reveal that truthfulness is grounded in the nature of Vishnu, who is Sat (Being) itself. The devotee's commitment to truth is not merely ethical but ontological — to speak truth is to align oneself with the fundamental nature of the Lord. The various *upasanas* (meditations) prescribed in this chapter are understood as devotional practices that deepen the devotee's awareness of Vishnu's presence.
