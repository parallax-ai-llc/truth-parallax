## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters regard the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad as a foundational text for the doctrine of the inner controller (*antaryamin*). The famous Antaryami Brahmana (3.7), in which Yajnavalkya describes a being who dwells within all elements, senses, and the self — controlling them from within while remaining unknown to them — is read as a direct description of Vishnu-Narayana. Ramanuja's commentary makes this passage the cornerstone of Vishishtadvaita theology.

## Theological Emphases

The "neti neti" ("not this, not this") teaching is read not as negating all attributes of Brahman but as denying that any finite description can exhaust God's infinite nature. Yajnavalkya's dialogue with Maitreyi — "It is for the sake of the Self that all is dear" (2.4) — is interpreted as teaching that love ultimately derives from the Lord who indwells all selves. The Madhu Vidya (honey doctrine) reveals that all beings are interconnected through their common dependence on Vishnu as their inner essence.
