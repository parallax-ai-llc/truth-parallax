Shankara treats Chapter 5 as a collection of *upasanas* (meditative practices) and ethical teachings that prepare the aspirant for the higher knowledge of Brahman. The teaching of the three Da's (5.2) — "control yourselves, give, have compassion" — addresses the three classes of beings (gods, humans, and demons) and establishes the moral foundation without which self-knowledge is impossible. These are preparatory disciplines (*sadhana-chatushtaya*) rather than the goal itself.

The meditation on the Gayatri (5.14) and the various cosmological meditations are classified as *aparavidya* (lower knowledge) — valuable for purifying the mind and producing merit but not sufficient for liberation. The chapter's teaching on truth (5.5) is significant: Shankara reads *satya* (truth) as containing both *sat* (being) and *ti/ya* (becoming), suggesting that empirical truth participates in but does not fully express absolute Being. Only the direct knowledge of Brahman — "I am That" — constitutes the liberating truth.
