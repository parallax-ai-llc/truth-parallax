Shankara treats Chapter 6's lineage lists as establishing the authoritative transmission (*sampradaya*) of Vedantic knowledge. The unbroken chain of teachers ensures that the teaching of Brahman has been faithfully preserved from its original revelation. The rites of passage and practical rituals belong to the domain of *karma kanda* (the section of the Veda dealing with action) and serve to maintain social and cosmic order, but they do not directly produce liberation.

The death rituals described in 6.1 are significant for Shankara's teaching on the departure of the self at death. The *jnani* (knower of Brahman) who dies does not undergo the usual process of faculty-withdrawal because the self's identification with the body has already been dissolved through knowledge. For the *ajnani* (ignorant person), the withdrawal of faculties describes the mechanism by which the subtle body departs to take on a new embodiment according to karma.
