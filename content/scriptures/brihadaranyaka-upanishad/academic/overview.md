## Overview

The Brihadaranyaka Upanishad is generally dated to the 7th-6th century BCE, making it among the earliest Upanishads. Its enormous length and complex structure suggest multiple layers of composition. The Madhu Kanda, Yajnavalkya Kanda, and Khila Kanda likely represent different periods of redaction, with the Yajnavalkya debates possibly preserving memories of actual philosophical contests in the courts of Videha (modern Bihar).

## Research Themes

The historicity of Yajnavalkya has been debated extensively. Joel Brereton and others argue that while a historical figure may underlie the tradition, the dialogues are literary constructions reflecting the philosophical concerns of their composers. The text provides unparalleled evidence for the culture of philosophical debate in ancient India — including the high stakes involved (Yajnavalkya's opponents risk their heads). The Maitreyi dialogue is significant for gender studies, as one of the rare Upanishadic passages featuring a woman as a serious philosophical interlocutor. The "neti neti" method anticipates aspects of later Buddhist and Madhyamaka dialectics.
