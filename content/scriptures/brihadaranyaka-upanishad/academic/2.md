Chapter 2 is notable for the Maitreyi dialogue (2.4), one of the most philosophically significant passages in the Upanishads. Scholars have debated whether Maitreyi's role represents genuine female participation in Vedic philosophical discourse or a literary device. The passage also contains the earliest clear formulation of the idea that all value is ultimately self-referential — a philosophical insight with implications for both ethics and epistemology.

The Madhu Vidya (2.5) presents an elaborate system of cosmic correspondences in which every element is "honey" for every other. Joel Brereton has analyzed this as a development from the Vedic concept of *bandhu* (cosmic homology) into a more genuinely philosophical teaching about the interconnectedness of reality. The "neti neti" formula, which first appears at 2.3.6, has been the subject of intense scholarly attention regarding whether it originally meant "not this, not that" (a method of negation) or "not thus, not thus" (indicating the inadequacy of description).
