Chapter 5 belongs to the Khila Kanda (supplementary section) of the Brihadaranyaka and is generally considered later than the Yajnavalkya material in Chapters 3-4. It contains a heterogeneous collection of meditations, ethical teachings, and cosmological speculations that scholars have analyzed for evidence of the social and intellectual context of late Vedic religion.

The teaching of the three Da's (5.2) — in which Prajapati's thunderclap "Da" is interpreted differently by gods, humans, and demons — is one of the most famous passages in the Upanishads and has attracted scholarly attention for its literary sophistication. T.S. Eliot's use of this passage in *The Waste Land* brought it to wide Western attention. The chapter's Gayatri meditation and various *upasanas* preserve evidence of late Vedic ritual and contemplative practices that bridge the gap between the older Brahmana literature and the emerging philosophical traditions.
