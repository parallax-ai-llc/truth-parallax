Chapter 6 is the most heterogeneous section of the Brihadaranyaka, containing teacher lineages (*vamshas*), ritual instructions, and practical teachings that scholars assign to the Khila Kanda (supplementary section). The lineage lists are valuable historical documents, providing evidence of the chains of transmission through which Upanishadic knowledge was preserved. Scholars have used these lists to establish relative chronologies and to identify geographical and social contexts of the Upanishadic tradition.

The ritual instructions — for conception, rites of passage, and funeral ceremonies — preserve evidence of domestic religious practices in late Vedic society. These passages bridge the gap between the high philosophical speculation of Chapters 3-4 and the everyday religious life of Vedic householders. The chapter demonstrates that the Brihadaranyaka was not merely a philosophical treatise but a practical religious text embedded in the living traditions of Vedic ritual life.
