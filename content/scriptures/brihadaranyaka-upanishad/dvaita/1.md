Madhva reads Chapter 1's cosmic horse sacrifice as revealing the hierarchy of Vishnu's creation. The identification of the horse with the cosmos demonstrates that every element of the universe has its designated place and function within God's sovereign order. The cosmogony is a real account of creation by Vishnu through His agent Brahma — the world is real, not illusory, and its ordered structure reflects divine intelligence and purpose.

The Prana narrative (1.3) teaches that Vishnu, as the cosmic Prana, is supreme over all other powers. When the gods representing individual faculties are overcome by demons, only Prana remains unconquered — demonstrating that God alone is independent (*svatantra*) while all other beings are dependent (*paratantra*). The prayer "from death to immortality" is the soul's petition to Vishnu for liberation from *samsara*, which comes through God's grace, not through the soul's own power.
