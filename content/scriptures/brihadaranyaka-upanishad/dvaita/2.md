Madhva reads the Maitreyi dialogue as teaching devotion to Vishnu rather than impersonal self-knowledge. When Yajnavalkya says "it is for the sake of the self that all is dear," the "self" refers primarily to Vishnu, the Supreme Self who is the source of all joy. All human love is a faint reflection of the soul's natural love for God, and the instruction to "see, hear, and meditate upon the self" is an injunction to devotional contemplation of Vishnu.

The Madhu Vidya demonstrates the real interconnection of all elements through their common dependence on Vishnu. The immortal *purusha* present in each element is the Lord as the inner controller — He is the honey that makes each element nourishing and meaningful. The chapter's "neti neti" teaching (2.3.6) is read by Madhva not as negating all qualities of God but as affirming that no finite description can capture His infinite perfections — a statement of divine transcendence, not of attributelessness.
