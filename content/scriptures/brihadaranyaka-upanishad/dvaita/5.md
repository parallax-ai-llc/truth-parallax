Madhva reads Chapter 5's ethical and meditative teachings as essential components of the devotional life, not merely preparatory stages to be transcended. The three Da's (5.2) — self-control, generosity, and compassion — are permanent virtues that characterize the devoted soul both before and after liberation. Ethical conduct is not a stepping-stone to impersonal knowledge but an expression of the soul's proper relationship to God and to other souls in God's creation.

The chapter's various meditations are understood as forms of devotion (*bhakti*) through which the soul deepens its relationship with Vishnu. The Gayatri meditation (5.14) is devotion to the Lord in His solar form. The meditation on truth reflects the Dvaita principle that God is the ultimate truth (*paramarthika satya*), and all truthfulness participates in and derives from His nature. The chapter's placement after the philosophical climax of Chapter 4 demonstrates that practice and devotion remain necessary even after intellectual understanding is achieved.
