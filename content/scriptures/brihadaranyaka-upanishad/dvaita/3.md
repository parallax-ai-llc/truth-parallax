The great debate of Chapter 3 demonstrates, in the Dvaita reading, the systematic hierarchy of realities that culminates in Vishnu. Each challenger's question points to a level of reality, and Yajnavalkya's answers reveal that every level depends on a higher one, with Vishnu as the ultimate, self-dependent reality. The Antaryami Brahmana (3.7) is Madhva's most important proof-text: it describes Vishnu dwelling within all beings as their inner controller while remaining eternally distinct from them.

Madhva emphasizes that the Antaryami passage explicitly says the inner controller is unknown to the beings He indwells — proving that God and souls are distinct. If they were identical (as Advaita claims), the soul would know the inner controller as itself. The *akshara* (imperishable) described in 3.8 is Vishnu in His transcendent aspect — beyond all finite descriptions not because He lacks qualities but because His qualities are infinite. The debate format itself illustrates the Dvaita principle that truth is established through rigorous reasoning and scriptural authority.
