Madhva reads Chapter 4 as Yajnavalkya's progressive revelation of Vishnu's sovereignty over all states of the soul's existence. In waking, Vishnu governs the soul's engagement with the world; in dreaming, He permits the soul to experience the fruits of its karma in subtle form; in deep sleep, He holds the soul in His care as it rests from activity. The teaching that the self is "embraced by the all-knowing Self" in deep sleep (4.3.21) proves, for Madhva, that God and soul are distinct — one embraces, the other is embraced.

The teaching on liberation (4.4) describes the soul that has known Vishnu through devotion departing at death along the path of light to the Lord's eternal abode. Madhva rejects the Advaita reading of *jivanmukti*: liberation occurs at death, when the soul, purified by devotion and knowledge, enters Vishnu's presence. The self's identity as "the light of lights" (4.4.16) refers primarily to Vishnu, who illuminates all other sources of light, including the soul's own consciousness.
