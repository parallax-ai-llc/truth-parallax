Chapter 6's lineage lists and ritual instructions demonstrate, in the Dvaita reading, the importance of authorized tradition in preserving knowledge of Vishnu. The teacher-student chain is not merely a human institution but a divinely ordained means by which God ensures that true knowledge continues in the world. Each teacher transmits not just information but the devotional relationship with Vishnu that makes knowledge salvific.

The rites of passage — for conception, birth, and death — are real sacraments through which Vishnu governs the soul's embodied journey. The death ritual describes the actual process by which the soul's faculties are withdrawn under God's direction, preparatory to the soul's journey to its next destination. For the devotee, this journey leads to Vishnu's eternal abode; for others, it leads to rebirth in accordance with God's justice and the soul's accumulated karma.
