## Overview

Madhva's reading of the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad consistently emphasizes the eternal distinction between God (Vishnu), individual souls, and matter. The Antaryami Brahmana (3.7) is a key text for Dvaita, demonstrating that the Lord dwells within all beings as their inner controller while remaining absolutely distinct from them. The soul is real, the world is real, and God is supremely real — all three exist eternally.

## Theological Emphases

Madhva reads "Aham Brahmasmi" not as the soul's claim of identity with Brahman but as a statement made by the Lord Himself or as the soul recognizing Brahman as its ruler. The "neti neti" teaching is interpreted as negating any limitation on God's infinite perfections — not negating all attributes but affirming that no description can encompass His greatness. Yajnavalkya's teaching to Maitreyi that "the self should be known" is read as an instruction for devotional meditation on Vishnu, who is the true Self of all selves.
