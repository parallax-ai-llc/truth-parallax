## Overview

Jewish interpretation of Divrei HaYamim Bet chapter 20 engages the narrative of jehoshaphat's victory through the lenses of rabbinic commentary, midrash, and halakhic implication. The Chronicler's distinctive theological perspective — emphasizing the Temple, the Leviim, and the principle of divine retribution — shapes the tradition's reading.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### Classical Commentators

**Rashi** provides concise peshat (plain sense) commentary, often supplemented by midrashic material that illuminates the theological dimensions of the narrative. **Radak** offers grammatical precision and contextual analysis. **Ralbag** (Gersonides) draws practical moral lessons (*to'aliyyot*) from the events described.

### The Chronicler's Theology

The Chronicler's emphasis on retribution — blessing for faithfulness, punishment for apostasy — reflects a theological framework that the rabbis engaged critically. The Talmud and Midrash sometimes qualify this framework, noting that righteous suffering and wicked prosperity also exist.

## Theological Significance

- **Torah and kingship**: Kings are measured by their fidelity to the Torah
- **The Beit HaMikdash**: The Temple is the center of Israel's religious life
- **Teshuvah**: Repentance is always possible, as the Chronicler repeatedly demonstrates
- **Divine justice**: HaShem rewards faithfulness and disciplines apostasy

### Haftarah and Liturgical Connection

The narratives of Divrei HaYamim, while not directly assigned as haftarah readings, inform the liturgical consciousness of the Jewish community. The themes of Temple worship, divine justice, and the possibility of teshuvah resonate throughout the prayer book, particularly in the High Holy Day liturgy and the kinot of Tisha B'Av.

### Ethical Implications

The Midrash draws from the narratives of Judah's kings practical ethical lessons that apply beyond the royal court. The principles of just governance, faithful worship, and accountability before HaShem are binding on every Jew, not only on rulers.
