## Overview

Jewish interpretation of Divrei HaYamim Bet chapter 30 engages the narrative of hezekiah's passover through the lenses of rabbinic commentary, midrash, and halakhic implication. The Chronicler's distinctive theological perspective — emphasizing the Temple, the Leviim, and the principle of divine retribution — shapes the tradition's reading.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### Classical Commentators

**Rashi** provides concise peshat (plain sense) commentary, often supplemented by midrashic material that illuminates the theological dimensions of the narrative. **Radak** offers grammatical precision and contextual analysis. **Ralbag** (Gersonides) draws practical moral lessons (*to'aliyyot*) from the events described.

### The Chronicler's Theology

The Chronicler's emphasis on retribution — blessing for faithfulness, punishment for apostasy — reflects a theological framework that the rabbis engaged critically. The Talmud and Midrash sometimes qualify this framework, noting that righteous suffering and wicked prosperity also exist.

## Theological Significance

- **Torah and kingship**: Kings are measured by their fidelity to the Torah
- **The Beit HaMikdash**: The Temple is the center of Israel's religious life
- **Teshuvah**: Repentance is always possible, as the Chronicler repeatedly demonstrates
- **Divine justice**: HaShem rewards faithfulness and disciplines apostasy


### The Pesach Invitation

**Radak** notes the remarkable nature of Chizkiyahu's invitation: he sends couriers throughout "all Israel and Judah" — including the territory of the former northern kingdom. This is an act of extraordinary religious diplomacy, extending the hand of reconciliation to communities that had been separated for generations.

### Grace for the Ritually Impure (30:18-20)

The Talmud discusses Chizkiyahu's prayer for those who ate the Pesach without proper purification. **Rashi** notes that Chizkiyahu invokes HaShem's goodness rather than the people's merit — a model of intercessory prayer that prioritizes divine grace over human performance.
