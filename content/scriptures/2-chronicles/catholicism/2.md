## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Chronicles 2 reads the account of preparations for the temple within the framework of Sacred Tradition and the Church's liturgical and moral theology. The Church Fathers provide interpretive keys that connect this narrative to Christ, the Church, and the sacraments.

## Patristic and Magisterial Reading

### Typological Connections

The Fathers read the events of preparations for the temple as part of the larger divine economy leading to the Incarnation. Every faithful king points toward Christ, the true King; every act of worship anticipates the liturgy of the Church.

### Moral Theology

Catholic moral theology draws lessons from the conduct of Judah's kings and the people's response. The interplay of faithfulness and apostasy illustrates the Church's teaching on the necessity of grace, the reality of human freedom, and the possibility of repentance.

## Theological Significance

- **Christological typology**: The events of this chapter find their fulfillment in Christ
- **Ecclesiological significance**: The Temple and its worship prefigure the Church
- **Moral instruction**: The choices of kings and people provide lasting ethical guidance
- **Sacramental connection**: The worship described here anticipates the Church's sacramental life


### International Cooperation (2:3-16)

Catholic exegesis notes that Hiram's cooperation with Solomon prefigures the universality of the Church. The Temple is built with materials and labor from beyond Israel's borders, illustrating the Catholic principle that God's salvific plan encompasses all peoples.

### The Dignity of Labor (2:17-18)

The Chronicler's detailed attention to the organization of laborers reflects the Catholic social teaching on the dignity of work. Every worker contributes to the sacred project, and their labor participates in the construction of God's dwelling place.
