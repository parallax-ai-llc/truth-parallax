## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Chronicles draws on both Sacred Scripture and Sacred Tradition, reading the Chronicler's history within the canonical context and the teaching authority of the Magisterium. The Church Fathers provided extensive commentary on Solomon's Temple, the liturgical reforms of Judah's faithful kings, and the theology of divine dwelling. Catholic exegesis emphasizes typological connections to Christ, the Church, and the sacraments.

## Key Interpretive Emphases

### Patristic Temple Theology
The Church Fathers read Solomon's Temple as a type of the Church. Bede's commentary on the Temple's construction (*De Templo*) is among the most extensive patristic treatments, interpreting every architectural detail as symbolic of the Church's structure and mission.

### Liturgical Continuity
The Chronicler's intense focus on Levitical worship, psalms, and sacrificial order resonates deeply with Catholic liturgical theology. The Temple liturgy is read as a forerunner of the Mass.

### Moral Theology of Kingship
The contrast between reforming kings (Hezekiah, Josiah) and apostate kings (Ahaz, Manasseh) provides case studies in Catholic moral theology regarding the duties of Christian rulers.
