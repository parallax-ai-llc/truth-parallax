## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Chronicles 9 reads the account of the queen of sheba within the framework of Sacred Tradition and the Church's liturgical and moral theology. The Church Fathers provide interpretive keys that connect this narrative to Christ, the Church, and the sacraments.

## Patristic and Magisterial Reading

### Typological Connections

The Fathers read the events of the queen of sheba as part of the larger divine economy leading to the Incarnation. Every faithful king points toward Christ, the true King; every act of worship anticipates the liturgy of the Church.

### Moral Theology

Catholic moral theology draws lessons from the conduct of Judah's kings and the people's response. The interplay of faithfulness and apostasy illustrates the Church's teaching on the necessity of grace, the reality of human freedom, and the possibility of repentance.

## Theological Significance

- **Christological typology**: The events of this chapter find their fulfillment in Christ
- **Ecclesiological significance**: The Temple and its worship prefigure the Church
- **Moral instruction**: The choices of kings and people provide lasting ethical guidance
- **Sacramental connection**: The worship described here anticipates the Church's sacramental life


### The Queen's Witness (9:1-8)

Catholic interpretation sees the Queen of Sheba as a model of the *sensus fidei* — the capacity to recognize truth when encountered. Her declaration that Solomon's wisdom surpasses what she had heard illustrates the principle that direct encounter with divine wisdom exceeds all secondhand reports.

### The Universal Scope (9:23-24)

"All the kings of the earth sought audience with Solomon to hear the wisdom God had put in his heart." Catholic missiology reads this as a prefiguration of the Church's universal mission — all nations are drawn to the wisdom of God.
