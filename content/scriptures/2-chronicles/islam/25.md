## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 25 reads the narrative of amaziah's reign through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### Partial Obedience

Islamic moral theology teaches that *iman* (faith) requires the heart, tongue, and limbs to act in harmony. Amaziah's half-hearted devotion illustrates the danger of outward compliance without inner conviction — a condition the Quran describes as *nifaq* (hypocrisy).

### The Folly of Idolatry

Amaziah's worship of the defeated Edomite gods is read as an illustration of the Quran's critique of idolatry: "Those you invoke besides Allah are servants like you. So invoke them and let them respond to you, if you should be truthful" (7:194). The absurdity of worshipping powerless gods is self-evident.
