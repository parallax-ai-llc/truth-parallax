## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 21 reads the narrative of jehoram's wicked reign through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### The Consequences of Tyranny

Islamic political theology reads Jehoram's reign as an illustration of the principle that tyrannical rulers bring destruction upon themselves and their people. The Quran warns: "Do not be like those who forgot Allah, so He made them forget themselves" (59:19). Jehoram's apostasy and brutality lead to personal and national catastrophe.

### The Prophetic Letter

Elijah's letter to Jehoram demonstrates the prophetic function of warning (*indhar*) — one of the two primary roles of prophets in Islamic theology (the other being glad tidings, *tabshir*). The prophet's responsibility is to deliver the message; the recipient's response determines the outcome.
