## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 2 reads the narrative of preparations for the temple through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### Sulayman and the King of Tyre

Islamic commentators note that Sulayman's correspondence with a pagan king illustrates the principle that prophets interact with all peoples, not only with believers. **Al-Tabari** records traditions about Sulayman's international relations, presenting them as evidence of his God-given authority over nations and jinn alike.

### The Scale of the Project

The massive scale of the Temple project — involving thousands of workers and vast quantities of materials — demonstrates, in Islamic reading, that Allah gives His prophets extraordinary means to accomplish their missions. Sulayman's construction projects are attributed in the Quran to the labor of jinn (34:12-13).
