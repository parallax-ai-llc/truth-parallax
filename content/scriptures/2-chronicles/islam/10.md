## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 10 reads the narrative of the kingdom divided through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### The Division as Divine Will

Islamic interpretation reads the division of the kingdom through the Quranic principle of divine sovereignty: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (3:26). The division is not merely a political event but a manifestation of Allah's decree.

### Lessons for the Ummah

Muslim scholars draw practical lessons from the division: the importance of just leadership, the danger of oppression, and the consequences of abandoning prophetic guidance. **Ibn Kathir** notes that the division of Bani Isra'il serves as a warning to the Muslim *ummah* against internal division.
