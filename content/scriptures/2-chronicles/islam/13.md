## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 13 reads the narrative of abijah's reign through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### The Battle and Divine Aid

Islamic tradition affirms that Allah aids those who fight in His cause. Abijah's victory despite being outnumbered is read as an instance of divine intervention — similar to the Quranic account of the Battle of Badr, where the Muslims, outnumbered, prevailed through Allah's support (Quran 3:123-125).

### The Defense of True Worship

Abijah's argument that Judah maintains legitimate worship while the north does not resonates with the Islamic emphasis on proper worship (*ibadah*). Islamic theology insists that worship must follow the form prescribed by Allah through His prophets.
