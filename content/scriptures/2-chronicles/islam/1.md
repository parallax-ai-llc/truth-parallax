## Sulayman's Wisdom from Allah

Islamic reading of 2 Chronicles 1 engages the narrative of Solomon's wisdom through the Quranic account of Sulayman. The Quran affirms that Sulayman was granted extraordinary wisdom and knowledge: "And We gave understanding of the case to Sulayman, and to each We gave judgment and knowledge" (Quran 21:79).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Sulayman in the Quran

The Quranic Sulayman is a prophet-king to whom Allah grants not only wisdom but also command over the wind, authority over jinn, and understanding of animal speech (Quran 27:16). Islamic scholars reading 2 Chronicles 1 affirm the core narrative — God grants wisdom to a humble petitioner — while situating it within the Quranic framework.

### Wisdom as Trust (*Amanah*)

**Ibn Kathir** emphasizes that Sulayman's wisdom is an *amanah* (trust) from Allah, not a personal possession. The prophet-king is accountable for how he exercises this gift — a principle that applies to all who hold authority.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: Wisdom comes from Allah alone
- **Prophetic kingship**: Sulayman is both prophet and king, a unique status in Islam
- **Gratitude (*shukr*)**: The proper response to divine gifts is gratitude and service
- **Accountability**: Power and wisdom carry divine accountability

### Lessons for the Ummah

Muslim scholars consistently draw lessons from the history of Bani Isra'il for the Muslim community. The patterns of faithfulness and apostasy, divine blessing and punishment, and prophetic warning that characterize 2 Chronicles are understood as universal principles that apply to all communities entrusted with divine revelation.

### The Sunnah of Allah in History

The Quran teaches that Allah's way (*sunnat Allah*) does not change (33:62). The rise and fall of Judah's kings illustrates this unchanging divine practice: nations that obey God's messengers prosper, while those that reject them face consequences. This historical pattern serves as both warning and encouragement.
