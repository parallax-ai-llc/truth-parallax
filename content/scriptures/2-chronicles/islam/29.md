## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 29 reads the narrative of hezekiah purifies the temple through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### Temple Purification and Tahara

Hezekiah's purification of the Temple resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *tahara* (purity) as a prerequisite for worship. The Quran commands: "And purify My House for those who perform *tawaf* and those who stand and those who bow and prostrate" (22:26). Hezekiah's cleansing of the Temple fulfills this divine commandment in its original context.

### The Priority of Worship

Islamic interpretation affirms Hezekiah's decision to prioritize the restoration of worship above all other concerns. The Prophet Muhammad taught that the most beloved act to Allah is prayer performed at its proper time — worship cannot be deferred for political or practical considerations.
