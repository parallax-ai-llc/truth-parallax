## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 22 reads the narrative of ahaziah and athaliah through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### The Destruction of the Wicked

Islamic reading of Ahaziah's fate and Athaliah's usurpation illustrates the Quranic principle that wickedness ultimately destroys itself. "And the evil plot does not encompass except its own people" (35:43). Those who ally with the wicked share their fate.

### Divine Protection of the Faithful

The preservation of the infant Joash demonstrates Allah's protection of those designated for His service. Islamic tradition contains parallel narratives — the preservation of Musa (Moses) in infancy, the protection of Ibrahim (Abraham) from fire — illustrating the same principle.
