## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 6 reads the narrative of solomon's prayer of dedication through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### Sulayman's Du'a (Supplication)

Islamic reading of Solomon's prayer of dedication finds deep resonance with the Islamic theology of *du'a* (supplication). The Quran teaches: "And your Lord says, 'Call upon Me; I will respond to you'" (40:60). Sulayman's extended prayer illustrates the prophetic model of turning to Allah in every circumstance.

### Prayer Toward the Sacred

The concept of directing prayer toward a sacred site resonates with the Islamic practice of facing the *qiblah* (direction of Mecca) during *salah*. **Al-Qurtubi** notes the parallel: just as Israel prayed toward the Temple, Muslims pray toward the Ka'bah — both practices acknowledge that Allah designates sacred directions for worship.
