## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 32 reads the narrative of sennacherib's invasion through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### Faith Against Empire

Hezekiah's confidence in Allah against the Assyrian empire illustrates the Quranic principle of *tawakkul* (trust in God). "And whoever relies upon Allah — then He is sufficient for him" (65:3). The miraculous deliverance confirms that human military power cannot prevail against divine decree.

### The Angel of Destruction

The angel who destroys the Assyrian army is read in Islamic angelology as an expression of Allah's power executed through the angelic hierarchy. The Quran affirms that angels carry out Allah's commands in the physical world (35:1).
