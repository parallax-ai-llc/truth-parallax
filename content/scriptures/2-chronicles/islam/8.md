## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 8 reads the narrative of solomon's achievements through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples


### Sulayman's Governance

Islamic tradition presents Sulayman as a model of just governance. His administrative achievements — building cities, organizing trade, maintaining worship — illustrate the Quranic principle that prophets are entrusted with both spiritual and temporal authority. **Al-Tabari** records extensive traditions about Sulayman's just administration.

### The Completion of Worship

The establishment of regular worship at the Temple corresponds to the Islamic emphasis on the regularity of *salah* (prayer). The five daily prayers, the Friday prayer, and the festival prayers create a rhythm of worship analogous to the daily, weekly, and festival offerings described in Chronicles.
