## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Chronicles 20 reads the narrative of jehoshaphat's victory through the principles of *tawhid* (divine unity), prophetic authority, and the Quranic framework for understanding the history of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel).

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Principles

The Quran establishes that God raises and abases nations according to their conduct: "Say, O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will" (Quran 3:26). The events of this chapter illustrate this principle.

### Historical Engagement

Classical Muslim historians like **al-Tabari** include material from 2 Chronicles in their universal histories, drawing on *Isra'iliyyat* (Israelite traditions) while filtering them through Quranic theology. **Ibn Kathir** is more selective, accepting only what aligns with the Quran and authentic hadith.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All sovereignty belongs to Allah alone
- **Prophetic warning**: God sends messengers before sending judgment
- **Divine justice**: Allah's dealings with nations are just and purposeful
- **Lessons for the ummah**: The history of Bani Isra'il provides cautionary examples

### Lessons for the Ummah

Muslim scholars consistently draw lessons from the history of Bani Isra'il for the Muslim community. The patterns of faithfulness and apostasy, divine blessing and punishment, and prophetic warning that characterize 2 Chronicles are understood as universal principles that apply to all communities entrusted with divine revelation.

### The Sunnah of Allah in History

The Quran teaches that Allah's way (*sunnat Allah*) does not change (33:62). The rise and fall of Judah's kings illustrates this unchanging divine practice: nations that obey God's messengers prosper, while those that reject them face consequences. This historical pattern serves as both warning and encouragement.
