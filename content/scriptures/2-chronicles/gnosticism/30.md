## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 30 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of hezekiah's passover. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Passover as Material Meal

Gnostic interpretation views the Passover celebration as a material sacrament of the Demiurge's religion. The meal commemorates liberation from one archontic power (Egypt) through submission to another (the Demiurge). True liberation, in Gnostic understanding, is escape from the entire material system, not merely from one oppressor.

### Unity Without Gnosis

Hezekiah's attempt to unite all Israel in Passover worship creates a powerful community experience, but the Gnostic reader notes that communal solidarity within the Demiurge's system is not the same as spiritual awakening. Unity in material religion is still material.

### The Irregular Passover

The most intriguing Gnostic detail is the participation of ritually unclean worshippers whom Hezekiah prays for (30:18-20). The Demiurge's own purity rules are bent to accommodate sincere seekers. This crack in the ritual system suggests that even within the archontic framework, the impulse toward authentic encounter sometimes overrides institutional regulation — a hint of the *gnosis* that transcends all rules.

## Theological Significance

- **Material commemoration**: The Passover celebrates liberation from one bondage into another
- **Communal religion**: Collective worship strengthens the material-religious system without awakening individuals
- **Bent rules**: The system's willingness to relax purity requirements hints at a reality beyond ritual
- **Joy without knowledge**: The great joy of the celebration is emotional, not pneumatic
