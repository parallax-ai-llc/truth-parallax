## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 22 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of ahaziah and athaliah. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### Ahaziah's Evil Counselors

The Chronicler attributes Ahaziah's wickedness to "the counsel of the house of Ahab" — particularly his mother Athaliah's influence. In Gnostic terms, the transmission of spiritual blindness through family systems illustrates how the archontic order perpetuates itself across generations. The soul trapped in matter inherits not only a body but an entire system of false knowledge.

### Athaliah as Material Power

Athaliah's seizure of the throne represents material power at its most ruthless. She destroys even the royal family — the Demiurge's chosen line — to maintain her position. Gnostic interpretation sees this as the self-devouring nature of archontic authority: power unchecked by *gnosis* destroys even its own instruments.

### The Hidden Child

The preservation of the infant Joash in the Temple has Gnostic resonance: the divine spark (represented by the legitimate heir) is hidden within the Demiurge's own house, protected by those who recognize its value. The hidden child represents the hidden *gnosis* preserved within the material-religious system. Jehoshabeath, a priest's wife, becomes the unlikely guardian of this spark — a feminine figure preserving what the archontic powers would destroy.

## Theological Significance

- **Inherited blindness**: The archontic system transmits ignorance across generations through family and counsel
- **Self-devouring power**: Athaliah's massacre of the royal family shows material authority consuming its own
- **The hidden spark**: True *gnosis* survives even when the archontic system destroys everything around it
- **The feminine guardian**: A woman preserves the divine seed within the Demiurge's own Temple
