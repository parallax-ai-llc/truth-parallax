## Solomon's Esoteric Wisdom

Gnostic reading of 2 Chronicles 1 focuses on Solomon's reception of wisdom — not as mundane political skill but as *gnosis*, direct knowledge of divine realities. In Gnostic tradition, Solomon is associated with esoteric knowledge and authority over spiritual powers, as reflected in texts like the *Testament of Solomon*.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### Wisdom as Gnosis

The Gnostic hermeneutic distinguishes between the Demiurge's offering of material blessings (wealth, honor) and the higher gift of *sophia* (wisdom). Solomon's request for wisdom rather than wealth suggests a pneumatic orientation — he seeks knowledge of divine realities rather than material advantage. The *Gospel of Thomas* (saying 2) teaches that the one who seeks will find and be astonished — Solomon's request hints at this deeper seeking.

### Solomon and the Archons

The *Testament of Solomon* portrays Solomon commanding demons during the Temple's construction. In Gnostic terms, this represents the pneumatic individual's authority over the archontic powers that govern the material world. Solomon's wisdom is not merely administrative competence but a form of *gnosis* that gives power over the archons.

### The Night Vision at Gibeon

Solomon's encounter with God at Gibeon — at night, in a dream — resonates with Gnostic revelatory literature. The *Apocryphon of John* and other Gnostic texts present divine knowledge as received in visionary states, outside the normal structures of material religion. The high place at Gibeon, notably, is not the Temple — suggesting that genuine encounter with the divine occurs outside institutional frameworks.

## Theological Significance

- **Gnosis over material blessing**: True wisdom is knowledge of the divine realm, not worldly success
- **Solomon as pneumatic figure**: The wise king's request reveals a soul oriented toward the Pleroma
- **The Demiurge's counter-offer**: Material prosperity follows as the Demiurge's attempt to recapture the seeker
- **Visionary knowledge**: Direct encounter with the divine bypasses priestly mediation
