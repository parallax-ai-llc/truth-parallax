## Solomon's Esoteric Wisdom

Gnostic reading of 2 Chronicles 1 focuses on Solomon's reception of wisdom — not as mundane political skill but as *gnosis*, direct knowledge of divine realities. In Gnostic tradition, Solomon is associated with esoteric knowledge and authority over spiritual powers, as reflected in texts like the *Testament of Solomon*.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### Wisdom as Gnosis

The Gnostic hermeneutic distinguishes between the Demiurge's offering of material blessings (wealth, honor) and the higher gift of *sophia* (wisdom). Solomon's request for wisdom rather than wealth suggests a pneumatic orientation — he seeks knowledge of divine realities rather than material advantage.

### Solomon and the Archons

The *Testament of Solomon* portrays Solomon commanding demons during the Temple's construction. In Gnostic terms, this represents the pneumatic individual's authority over the archontic powers that govern the material world.

## Theological Significance

- **Gnosis over material blessing**: True wisdom is knowledge of the divine realm
- **Solomon as pneumatic figure**: The wise king transcends the material order
- **The Demiurge's offer**: Material prosperity is the Demiurge's gift, not the highest reality
- **Esoteric tradition**: Solomon becomes a bearer of hidden knowledge

### The Pneumatic Reader

The Gnostic approach to this chapter — as to all Old Testament narrative — is fundamentally about the reader's spiritual orientation. The pneumatic individual reads the same text as the psychic or hylic person but perceives different meanings. Where the material reader sees history, the pneumatic reader sees allegory; where the literalist sees divine commands, the Gnostic sees the Demiurge's attempts to maintain control.

### The Path of Gnosis

Every narrative in 2 Chronicles can be read as a map of the soul's journey. The faithful kings represent moments of spiritual awakening, while the apostate kings represent the soul's descent into material darkness. The ultimate goal — escape from the cycle of material religion — is the Gnostic hope that transcends any particular chapter or narrative.
