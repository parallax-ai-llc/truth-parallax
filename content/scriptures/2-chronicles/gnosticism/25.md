## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 25 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of amaziah's reign. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### Half-Hearted Gnosis

Amaziah's partial devotion illustrates the Gnostic concept of the psychic — the person who is neither fully pneumatic (spiritual) nor fully hylic (material) but exists in a middle state. The psychic individual practices the Demiurge's religion sincerely but without the deeper knowledge that transforms.

### The Absurdity of Captured Gods

Amaziah's worship of gods he has defeated is, in Gnostic terms, the ultimate demonstration of the material mind's confusion. The hylic person, unable to distinguish between power and divinity, worships anything that seems impressive — a critique the Gnostics apply to all material religion.
