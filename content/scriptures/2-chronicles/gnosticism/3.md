## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 3 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of building the temple. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### The Holy of Holies as Sealed Chamber

The construction of the Most Holy Place — sealed, dark, and entered only once per year — represents the Gnostic concept of the hidden divine. The Demiurge conceals the true light behind veils and restrictions, ensuring that only the privileged few access it. Gnostic interpretation inverts this: the veil must be torn for *gnosis* to be universal.

### The Cherubim as Archons

The golden cherubim guarding the Holy of Holies can be read as archontic guardians — spiritual powers that prevent unauthorized access to the divine realm. In the *Apocryphon of John*, archons guard the boundaries between cosmic spheres, restricting the soul's ascent.
