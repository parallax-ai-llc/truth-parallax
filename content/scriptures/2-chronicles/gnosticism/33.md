## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 33 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of manasseh and amon. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### Manasseh's Journey

Gnostic reading finds in Manasseh's trajectory a complex narrative. His initial apostasy — rejecting the Demiurge's worship for other archontic cults — is not, in Gnostic terms, necessarily worse than the worship he rejected. Both are forms of material religion. His repentance, however, raises an interesting possibility: did Manasseh, in his suffering, encounter something deeper than the Demiurge's system?

### The Prison as Initiation

Manasseh's captivity and humiliation in Assyria can be read as a Gnostic initiation narrative. Stripped of all material power and dignity, the king is forced inward — where the divine spark resides. His prayer from prison may represent a genuine encounter with the inner light.
