## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 33 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of manasseh and amon. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### Manasseh's Journey

Gnostic reading finds in Manasseh's trajectory a complex narrative. His initial apostasy — rejecting the Demiurge's worship for other archontic cults — is not, in Gnostic terms, necessarily worse than the worship he rejected. Both are forms of material religion. His repentance, however, raises an interesting possibility: did Manasseh, in his suffering, encounter something deeper than the Demiurge's system?

### The Prison as Initiation

Manasseh's captivity and humiliation in Assyria can be read as a Gnostic initiation narrative. Stripped of all material power and dignity, the king is forced inward — where the divine spark resides. His prayer from prison may represent a genuine encounter with the inner light. The apocryphal *Prayer of Manasseh* — a text that resonates with Gnostic sensibility — captures this moment of crisis-born awareness.

### Amon's Brief Reign

Manasseh's son Amon reverts immediately to idol worship and is assassinated by his own servants (33:21-25). Gnostic interpretation sees in this rapid cycle the futility of reform within the Demiurge's system — even genuine insight in one generation fails to transmit to the next. The divine spark, once recognized, must be individually discovered; it cannot be inherited.

## Theological Significance

- **The complex soul**: Manasseh's trajectory defies simple categories of pneumatic, psychic, or hylic
- **Suffering as doorway**: The destruction of material supports can open the path to inner *gnosis*
- **Non-transferable knowledge**: Manasseh's insight dies with him — *gnosis* cannot be institutionalized or inherited
- **The cycle continues**: Amon's rapid apostasy demonstrates the system's resistance to lasting change
