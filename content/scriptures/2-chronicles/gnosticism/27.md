## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 27 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of jotham's reign. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### The Unremarkable Chapter

Jotham's brief, quiet reign is of minimal interest to Gnostic interpretation. A king who faithfully serves the Demiurge without dramatic events provides no hermeneutical leverage for Gnostic reading. The psychic individual who lives within the system without questioning it is, from the Gnostic perspective, spiritually asleep.

### The Silence of Gnosis

The absence of prophetic confrontation in Jotham's reign may suggest, in Gnostic terms, a period when *gnosis* is dormant — no voice challenges the established order, and the material-religious system operates without interruption.
