## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 24 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of joash restores the temple. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The King Without a Guide

After Jehoiada's death, Joash immediately turns to idol worship, revealing that his faithfulness was dependent on his mentor, not on inner knowledge. In Gnostic terms, Joash is the psychic individual par excellence — capable of right action under external guidance but lacking the interior *gnosis* that sustains the pneumatic individual independently.

### The Temple Repaired — The System Maintained

Joash's repair of the Temple is, from a Gnostic perspective, the maintenance of the Demiurge's central institution. The material structure is patched and preserved, but no spiritual transformation occurs. The pneumatic individual recognizes that repairing the Temple's stones does not repair the soul's relationship with the true God.

### The Murder of Zechariah

The murder of the prophet Zechariah in the Temple court reveals the violent nature of the Demiurge's system. When the system's own prophet threatens the established order with uncomfortable truth, the system destroys him. Gnostic tradition sees this as characteristic of the archontic response to authentic *gnosis*. Zechariah's dying words — "May the Lord see and avenge" — invoke the Demiurge's justice even as the system kills him: the prophet cannot imagine a reality beyond the Demiurge's retributive order.

## Theological Significance

- **Dependent faith**: Without interior *gnosis*, faithfulness collapses when external guidance is removed
- **Institutional maintenance**: Repairing the Temple perpetuates the Demiurge's central structure
- **Prophetic martyrdom**: The system destroys its own truth-tellers when they become inconvenient
- **The cycle of apostasy**: The rapid shift from reform to idolatry reveals the instability of psychic religion
