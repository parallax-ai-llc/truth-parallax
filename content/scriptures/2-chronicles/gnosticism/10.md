## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 10 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of the kingdom divided. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### The Shattering of the Demiurge's Order

The division of the kingdom exposes the fragility of the Demiurge's political-religious system. The united kingdom, built on material power and religious coercion, cannot sustain itself. In Gnostic terms, all structures of the Demiurge are inherently unstable because they are built on matter rather than spirit.

### Rehoboam as Archontic Tyrant

Rehoboam's refusal to lighten the people's burdens embodies the archontic principle: material power maintains itself through coercion. The Gnostic reader sees in Rehoboam's arrogance the characteristic behavior of the archons, who demand submission and punish resistance.
