## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 23 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of joash crowned king. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### Jehoiada the Priest-King

Jehoiada functions as both priest and political organizer — the Demiurge's ideal servant, wielding both religious and political power to restore the archontic order. His careful planning of the coup demonstrates the sophisticated machinery of the Demiurge's system, which can regenerate itself even after near-total destruction.

### Restoration Within the System

Jehoiada's coup restores the Davidic monarchy, but from a Gnostic perspective, this is merely a rearrangement within the Demiurge's political order. The "rightful king" replaces the usurper, but the fundamental structure of material power remains unchanged. True liberation requires not political restoration but spiritual awakening.

### The Covenant as Binding

The covenant made between Jehoiada, the king, and the people represents the binding power of the Demiurge's law. In Gnostic terms, covenants and oaths are mechanisms of control that keep the soul attached to the material order.

### The Destruction of Baal's Temple

The people's destruction of the temple of Baal and the killing of its priest Mattan illustrates the Demiurge's jealousy made violent. One archontic cult is destroyed to reinstate another. The Gnostic reader notes that the violence directed at Baal worship could just as easily be directed at any group that challenges the dominant archontic system.

## Theological Significance

- **System regeneration**: The Demiurge's political-religious order can rebuild itself from a single hidden child
- **Priestly power**: Jehoiada demonstrates how religious authority orchestrates political outcomes
- **Covenant as chain**: Binding oaths keep the soul attached to the archontic order
- **Rival cults destroyed**: The Demiurge's jealousy expresses itself through the destruction of competing worship
