## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 23 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of joash crowned king. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### Restoration Within the System

Jehoiada's coup restores the Davidic monarchy, but from a Gnostic perspective, this is merely a rearrangement within the Demiurge's political order. The "rightful king" replaces the usurper, but the fundamental structure of material power remains unchanged. True liberation requires not political restoration but spiritual awakening.

### The Covenant as Binding

The covenant made between Jehoiada, the king, and the people represents the binding power of the Demiurge's law. In Gnostic terms, covenants and oaths are mechanisms of control that keep the soul attached to the material order.
