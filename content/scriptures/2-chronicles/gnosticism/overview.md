## Overview

Gnostic reading of 2 Chronicles applies the characteristic Gnostic hermeneutical inversion to the Chronicler's narrative of Solomon's Temple, the Judean monarchy, and the eventual destruction of Jerusalem. While no surviving Gnostic text provides a systematic commentary on 2 Chronicles, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of Genesis and the Wisdom literature illuminate how these communities would have engaged the material. The Temple — as both a material structure and a symbol of divine dwelling — is a particularly rich site for Gnostic interpretation.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### Solomon's Temple as Material Trap
The grandeur of Solomon's Temple presents an ambiguity for Gnostic thought. On one hand, the Temple represents the Demiurge's attempt to localize and control access to the divine. On the other hand, the Temple's inner sanctum — where God's glory dwells — may symbolize the hidden divine spark trapped within material creation.

### Solomon's Wisdom
In some Gnostic traditions, Solomon is associated with esoteric wisdom. The *Testament of Solomon* and other pseudepigraphical texts portray Solomon commanding demons, which resonates with Gnostic ideas about the pneumatic individual's authority over archontic powers.

### The Fall of the Temple
The destruction of Jerusalem in Chapter 36 can be read as the inevitable collapse of the Demiurge's political-religious order. True worship cannot be contained in material structures but must be an interior *gnosis*.

## Relevant Gnostic Texts

- **The Testimony of Truth**: Critiques Temple sacrifice as worship of the Demiurge
- **The Gospel of Philip**: Reinterprets Temple imagery as symbols of inner spiritual realities
- **The Apocryphon of John**: Provides the cosmological framework for reading Old Testament narrative
