## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 2 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of preparations for the temple. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### Material Preparations for a Material Temple

Gnostic reading of the Temple preparations questions the entire enterprise. The gathering of stones, timber, and metals from across the known world is an act of material accumulation — the Demiurge commanding the material world to construct his dwelling. True divinity, in Gnostic understanding, does not require material housing. The *Gospel of Thomas* (saying 77) declares: "Split a piece of wood; I am there. Lift up a stone, and you will find me" — a direct subversion of the idea that God requires a constructed house.

### Hiram as Archontic Collaborator

The collaboration between Solomon and Hiram of Tyre represents, in Gnostic terms, the cooperation of archontic powers in constructing the material-religious system. Both kings serve the Demiurge's purposes, building a structure that will localize and control access to the divine. Hiram's letter acknowledging Solomon's God reads as one archontic administrator recognizing another's patron deity — a diplomatic courtesy within the Demiurge's world order.

### The Labor Force

The conscription of 153,600 laborers for the Temple project reveals the coercive underpinning of material religion. The Demiurge's house is built not through spiritual awakening but through forced labor — a system of material compulsion that echoes the archontic enslavement of divine sparks within material bodies.

## Theological Significance

- **Material accumulation as false worship**: Gathering the world's resources for the Demiurge's dwelling
- **Archontic cooperation**: Political alliances serve the material-religious system
- **Coerced labor**: The Demiurge's worship requires the subjugation of bodies, not the liberation of souls
- **The true temple**: The pneumatic individual recognizes that no material structure can house the infinite
