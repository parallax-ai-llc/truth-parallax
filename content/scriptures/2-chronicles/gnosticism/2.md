## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 2 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of preparations for the temple. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### Material Preparations for a Material Temple

Gnostic reading of the Temple preparations questions the entire enterprise. The gathering of stones, timber, and metals from across the known world is an act of material accumulation — the Demiurge commanding the material world to construct his dwelling. True divinity, in Gnostic understanding, does not require material housing.

### Hiram as Archontic Collaborator

The collaboration between Solomon and Hiram of Tyre represents, in Gnostic terms, the cooperation of archontic powers in constructing the material-religious system. Both kings serve the Demiurge's purposes, building a structure that will localize and control access to the divine.
