## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 9 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of the queen of sheba. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### The Queen of Sheba as Sophia

Some Gnostic traditions associate the Queen of Sheba with *Sophia* (Wisdom) — the divine feminine principle whose descent into the material world is a central Gnostic myth. In this reading, the Queen's visit to Solomon represents Sophia encountering the Demiurge's greatest creation and finding it insufficient: "the half was not told me" takes on new meaning — material wisdom, however impressive, is only half the truth.

### The Limits of Material Glory

The elaborate catalog of Solomon's wealth — gold, ivory, apes, peacocks — represents the apex of material accumulation. Gnostic interpretation reads this as the Demiurge's proudest display, which is nevertheless a pale shadow of the Pleroma's true glory.
