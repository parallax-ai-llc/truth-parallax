## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 9 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of the queen of sheba. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Queen of Sheba as Sophia

Some Gnostic traditions associate the Queen of Sheba with *Sophia* (Wisdom) — the divine feminine principle whose descent into the material world is a central Gnostic myth. In this reading, the Queen's visit to Solomon represents Sophia encountering the Demiurge's greatest creation and finding it insufficient: "the half was not told me" takes on new meaning — material wisdom, however impressive, is only half the truth. The Queen comes from outside the Demiurge's covenant system, yet possesses the capacity to test and evaluate its wisdom.

### The Limits of Material Glory

The elaborate catalog of Solomon's wealth — gold, ivory, apes, peacocks — represents the apex of material accumulation. Gnostic interpretation reads this as the Demiurge's proudest display, which is nevertheless a pale shadow of the Pleroma's true glory. The 666 talents of gold (9:13) — a number later associated with the Beast in Revelation — hints at the ultimately beastly nature of material splendor.

### Solomon's Death

The chapter closes with Solomon's death, a reminder that even the Demiurge's most favored agent cannot escape the material condition. The pneumatic individual reads this ending as confirmation: no amount of wisdom, wealth, or divine favor within the Demiurge's system overcomes the fundamental problem of material existence.

## Theological Significance

- **Sophia's visit**: The divine feminine tests and surpasses the Demiurge's wisdom
- **Material apex as spiritual nadir**: The greatest worldly glory remains infinitely distant from the Pleroma
- **The insufficiency of half-truth**: Material wisdom is only partial — *gnosis* completes it
- **Death as the Demiurge's final word**: The material ruler cannot offer eternal life
