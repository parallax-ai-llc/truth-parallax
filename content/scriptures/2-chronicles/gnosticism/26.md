## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Chronicles 26 applies the characteristic hermeneutical inversion to the narrative of uzziah's reign. While no surviving Gnostic text directly comments on this chapter, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of the Hebrew Bible — the Demiurge, the divine spark, archontic powers, and the primacy of *gnosis* — provide an interpretive framework.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Demiurge's Kingdom

The Chronicler presents the kingdom of Judah as God's chosen political order. Gnostic hermeneutics questions this framing: the God who establishes earthly kingdoms and demands material sacrifices may be the Demiurge, not the true transcendent God.

### Pneumatic and Hylic Figures

Gnostic interpretation classifies the characters of this narrative according to their spiritual orientation. Those who demonstrate knowledge, insight, and transcendence of material concerns are read as pneumatic figures. Those bound to power, ritual, and material wealth are hylic.

## Theological Significance

- **The Demiurge's order**: Earthly kingdoms serve the lower god's purposes
- **Interior knowledge**: True religion is *gnosis*, not institutional worship
- **The divine spark**: Even within the Demiurge's narrative, traces of the true God appear
- **Material impermanence**: All political and religious structures are ultimately temporary


### Pride and the Pneumatic Boundary

Uzziah's attempt to burn incense in the Temple can be read, in Gnostic terms, as an attempt to cross the boundary between the material and spiritual realms without proper *gnosis*. The leprosy that results represents the archontic defense mechanism — the Demiurge punishes unauthorized access to the sacred.

### The King's Leprosy

In Gnostic interpretation, Uzziah's visible disease represents the spiritual contamination that comes from attempting to manipulate sacred power without genuine knowledge. The material body manifests what is true of the soul.
