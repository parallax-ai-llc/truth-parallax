## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 26 reads the account of uzziah's reign within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads uzziah's reign as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### Uzziah's Transgression

The distinction between royal and priestly functions is fundamental to Orthodox ecclesiology. The *symphonia* model — cooperation between civil authority and the Church — requires that each sphere respect the boundaries of the other. Uzziah's attempt to usurp the priestly role illustrates what happens when these boundaries are violated.

### Leprosy and Spiritual Diagnosis

Orthodox spiritual theology reads Uzziah's leprosy as a visible manifestation of an invisible spiritual condition. The Fathers distinguish between bodily illness and spiritual disease, teaching that pride — the root of Uzziah's transgression — is the most dangerous of all spiritual maladies.
