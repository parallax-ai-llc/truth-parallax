## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 4 reads the account of temple furnishings within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads temple furnishings as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### The Bronze Sea and Baptism

Orthodox baptismal theology reads the bronze sea as a type of the baptismal font. Just as the priests washed in the sea before performing their sacred duties, Christians are washed in baptism before participating in the divine mysteries. **Cyril of Jerusalem** develops this typology in his catechetical lectures.

### Sacred Vessels and Liturgical Practice

The consecration of Temple vessels for sacred use establishes the principle that underlies the Orthodox consecration of liturgical objects. The chalice, diskos, and other implements of the Divine Liturgy are not ordinary objects but vessels set apart for the sacred mysteries.
