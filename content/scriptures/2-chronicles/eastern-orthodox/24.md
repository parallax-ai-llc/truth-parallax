## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 24 reads the account of joash restores the temple within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads joash restores the temple as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### The Two Phases of Joash

Orthodox interpretation distinguishes sharply between Joash under Jehoiada's guidance (faithful) and Joash after Jehoiada's death (apostate). This pattern illustrates the Orthodox teaching on the importance of spiritual direction — the *staretz* (elder) or spiritual father guides the believer, and removing that guidance exposes one to spiritual danger.

### The Blood of Zechariah

The martyrdom of Zechariah in the Temple court has deep liturgical significance. Orthodox hymnography commemorates the holy prophets and martyrs whose blood was shed for the faith, recognizing their sacrifices as seeds of the Church's growth.
