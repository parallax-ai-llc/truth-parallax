## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 27 reads the account of jotham's reign within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads jotham's reign as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### The Hiddenness of Holiness

Jotham's short, unremarkable chapter illustrates the Orthodox teaching that holiness is often hidden. The most faithful servants of God are not always the most visible. The hesychastic tradition values the hidden life of prayer over public acclaim.

### Steady Faithfulness

Orthodox monasticism prizes the virtue of *perseverance* — the quiet, daily fidelity that sustains the spiritual life over decades. Jotham's reign embodies this virtue: no dramatic highs or catastrophic lows, simply the steady walking before God.
