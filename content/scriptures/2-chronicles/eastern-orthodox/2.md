## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 2 reads the account of preparations for the temple within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads preparations for the temple as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### The Cosmic Temple

Orthodox theology reads the Temple's construction as a cosmic event. The materials gathered from Lebanon, Tyre, and beyond represent creation offering itself for the divine dwelling. **Maximus the Confessor** teaches that the Temple is a microcosm — a miniature cosmos ordered around the divine presence.

### The Cooperation of Nations

The collaboration between Solomon and Hiram prefigures the gathering of all peoples into the worship of the true God. Orthodox ecclesiology sees the Church as the fulfillment of this vision — a community that transcends ethnic and national boundaries.
