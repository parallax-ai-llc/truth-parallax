## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 31 reads the account of hezekiah's religious reforms within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads hezekiah's religious reforms as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### The Fruits of Revival

The generous giving that follows Hezekiah's Passover illustrates the Orthodox teaching that genuine worship produces material generosity. The connection between the Divine Liturgy and charitable action is not incidental but essential — the Eucharist transforms hearts, and transformed hearts produce works of mercy.

### Ordered Ministry

Hezekiah's reorganization of the Levitical divisions reflects the Orthodox emphasis on canonical order in ministry. The Church's hierarchical structure is not merely administrative but participates in the heavenly order.
