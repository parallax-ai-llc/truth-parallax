## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 6 reads the account of solomon's prayer of dedication within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads solomon's prayer of dedication as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### Solomon's Prayer and Liturgical Intercession

Orthodox liturgical prayer follows the pattern established in Solomon's prayer of dedication. The Great Litany of the Divine Liturgy addresses the same concerns: peace, protection from enemies, fruitful seasons, the sick, travelers, and the departed. Solomon's comprehensive prayer is the Old Testament template for the Church's intercessory ministry.

### The Temple as Place of Encounter

Orthodox theology emphasizes that the Temple is not merely a building but a place of *synaxis* — the gathering of God's people in God's presence. The Church inherits this function, with every Divine Liturgy re-enacting the encounter between heaven and earth.
