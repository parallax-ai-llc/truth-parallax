## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles is grounded in the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Chronicler's emphasis on Temple worship, divine glory, and the sanctification of the people resonates with the Orthodox theology of *theosis* (divinization). The Fathers — particularly John Chrysostom, Theodoret of Cyrus, and Ephrem the Syrian — read these narratives within the broader divine economy of salvation.

## Key Interpretive Emphases

### Liturgical Context
The Chronicler's detailed attention to Levitical singers, musicians, and liturgical order finds deep resonance in Orthodox worship. The Temple liturgy is understood as a prefiguration of the Divine Liturgy, with the Holy of Holies corresponding to the sanctuary behind the iconostasis.

### Patristic Typology
- **Solomon's Temple**: A type of the Church as the dwelling place of God's glory
- **The cloud filling the Temple (5:13-14)**: A theophany prefiguring the Incarnation
- **Hezekiah's Passover (ch. 30)**: Unity of worship as a model for ecclesial communion

### Divine Economy
The rise and fall of Judah's kings is read as part of God's providential plan (*oikonomia*) leading to the Incarnation. The exile is not merely punishment but a purification preparing Israel for Christ's coming.
