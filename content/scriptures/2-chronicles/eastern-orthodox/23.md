## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Chronicles 23 reads the account of joash crowned king within the patristic tradition and the liturgical life of the Church. The Fathers — particularly the Antiochene and Cappadocian schools — provide the hermeneutical framework for understanding this narrative within God's divine economy (*oikonomia*).

## Patristic Reading

### Typological Significance

Orthodox exegesis reads joash crowned king as part of the unfolding plan of salvation leading to the Incarnation. The faithfulness or unfaithfulness of Judah's kings reveals the human condition's need for the divine-human King, Jesus Christ.

### Liturgical Connection

The worship described in Chronicles finds its fulfillment in the Divine Liturgy. The Chronicler's attention to Levitical order, sacred music, and proper sacrifice resonates with Orthodox liturgical theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Theosis**: The goal of human life is participation in the divine nature
- **Divine economy**: God works through history to accomplish salvation
- **Liturgical worship**: True worship participates in the heavenly liturgy
- **Patristic continuity**: The Church reads Scripture with the Fathers


### The Priestly Role in Restoration

Jehoiada's leadership in restoring the Davidic monarchy illustrates the Orthodox understanding of the priesthood's role in safeguarding the faith. The priest does not seek political power but acts when the covenant order is gravely threatened.

### Liturgical Restoration

The restoration of the king is accompanied by the restoration of Temple worship. Orthodox theology insists on the inseparability of right governance and right worship — a kingdom without proper liturgy is a kingdom without its foundation.
