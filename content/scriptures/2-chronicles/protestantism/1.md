## Solomon's Petition at Gibeon

2 Chronicles opens with Solomon's visit to the tabernacle at Gibeon, where God appears to him in a dream and offers him anything he desires. Solomon asks for wisdom and knowledge to govern God's people. Protestant interpreters read this as a model of prayer: the king subordinates personal ambition to the call of service.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Give me wisdom and knowledge" (1:10)

**Calvin** notes that Solomon's request reveals a proper understanding of human limitation — the wise ruler acknowledges dependence on God. This aligns with the Protestant emphasis on grace over human merit.

### Wealth as Divine Blessing (1:14-17)

God grants Solomon not only wisdom but also wealth and honor. Protestant theology reads this not as a prosperity gospel but as an illustration of Matthew 6:33 — "Seek first the kingdom of God, and all these things will be added to you."

## Theological Significance

- **Prayer as the foundation of leadership**: Solomon begins with worship, not politics
- **Divine sovereignty**: God initiates the encounter and sets the terms
- **Wisdom as gift**: True wisdom is received, not achieved
- **Typology**: Solomon's wisdom prefigures Christ, "in whom are hidden all the treasures of wisdom" (Colossians 2:3)

### Historical Context

Protestant historical-critical scholarship situates this chapter within the broader context of the Chronicler's post-exilic theology. Writing after the return from Babylon, the Chronicler shaped the narrative to address the needs of the restored community — particularly questions about the Temple, the Davidic dynasty, and the conditions for divine blessing. Understanding this authorial context enriches the theological reading without diminishing the text's authority.

### Application

The Protestant tradition of applying Old Testament narratives to the life of the church finds rich material here. The patterns of faithfulness and failure, divine initiative and human response, judgment and mercy all speak directly to the church's ongoing experience.
