## Covenant Renewal Under Asa

The prophet Azariah son of Oded meets Asa with a message: "The LORD is with you when you are with him. If you seek him, he will be found by you, but if you forsake him, he will forsake you" (15:2). Asa responds by intensifying his reforms and leading the people in a solemn covenant renewal ceremony.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "The LORD Is with You When You Are with Him" (15:2)

This verse encapsulates the Chronicler's retribution theology in its most direct form. Protestant interpreters note both the promise and the warning. **Selman** observes that this is not a doctrine of works-righteousness but a description of covenant relationship: God's presence is not earned but can be forfeited through unfaithfulness.

### Covenant Renewal (15:12-15)

The people enter into a covenant "to seek the LORD, the God of their fathers, with all their heart and soul." Protestant theology reads this as a model of genuine revival — a corporate turning to God marked by solemn commitment.

## Theological Significance

- **Seeking God**: The central call of the Chronicler
- **Prophetic encouragement**: God sends prophets to call His people back
- **Corporate renewal**: True reform involves the whole community
- **Joy in worship**: The people rejoice because they have sought God wholeheartedly (15:15)

### Historical Context

Protestant historical-critical scholarship situates this chapter within the broader context of the Chronicler's post-exilic theology. Writing after the return from Babylon, the Chronicler shaped the narrative to address the needs of the restored community — particularly questions about the Temple, the Davidic dynasty, and the conditions for divine blessing. Understanding this authorial context enriches the theological reading without diminishing the text's authority.

### Application

The Protestant tradition of applying Old Testament narratives to the life of the church finds rich material here. The patterns of faithfulness and failure, divine initiative and human response, judgment and mercy all speak directly to the church's ongoing experience.
