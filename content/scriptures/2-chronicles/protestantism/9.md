## The Queen of Sheba Visits Solomon

The Queen of Sheba travels to Jerusalem to test Solomon's wisdom with hard questions. She is overwhelmed by his wisdom, the magnificence of his court, and the worship at the Temple. The chapter concludes with a summary of Solomon's wealth and his death.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "The Half Was Not Told Me" (9:6)

Protestant interpreters read the Queen of Sheba as a type of the nations coming to acknowledge God's wisdom. Jesus himself draws this connection: "The Queen of the South will rise at the judgment with this generation and condemn it, for she came from the ends of the earth to listen to Solomon's wisdom, and now something greater than Solomon is here" (Matthew 12:42).

### Solomon's Glory and Its Limits

Protestant exegesis notes the Chronicler's deliberate juxtaposition of Solomon's glory with the kingdom's division in the very next chapter. Earthly splendor is transient; only God's kingdom endures.

## Theological Significance

- **Witness to the nations**: Solomon's wisdom draws the world to Israel's God
- **Christological typology**: Christ is the greater Solomon
- **Transience of earthly glory**: Human kingdoms rise and fall
- **Wisdom and worship**: True wisdom leads to worship of the true God

### Historical Context

Protestant historical-critical scholarship situates this chapter within the broader context of the Chronicler's post-exilic theology. Writing after the return from Babylon, the Chronicler shaped the narrative to address the needs of the restored community — particularly questions about the Temple, the Davidic dynasty, and the conditions for divine blessing. Understanding this authorial context enriches the theological reading without diminishing the text's authority.

### Application

The Protestant tradition of applying Old Testament narratives to the life of the church finds rich material here. The patterns of faithfulness and failure, divine initiative and human response, judgment and mercy all speak directly to the church's ongoing experience.
