## Overview

Chapter 25 of 2 Chronicles presents the Chronicler's account of amaziah's reign. Protestant interpreters read this material within the framework of covenant theology, emphasizing divine sovereignty and human responsibility.

## Key Interpretive Emphases

### Covenant Faithfulness

The Chronicler's presentation of amaziah's reign illustrates the recurring pattern of 2 Chronicles: obedience brings blessing, while apostasy invites judgment. Protestant exegesis reads this not as mechanical retribution but as the outworking of covenant relationship.

### The Davidic Promise

Even through the vicissitudes of individual reigns, God's promise to David endures. The Chronicler preserves the continuity of the Davidic line as evidence of God's faithfulness to His covenant.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine sovereignty**: God directs the course of history through and despite human choices
- **Human responsibility**: Kings are accountable for their response to God's word
- **The prophetic word**: God sends prophets to call rulers to faithfulness
- **Typological significance**: The successes and failures of Judah's kings point to the need for a perfect King


### Amaziah's Partial Obedience (25:2)

"He did what was right in the eyes of the LORD, but not wholeheartedly." This assessment is programmatic for the Chronicler's evaluation of Amaziah. Protestant interpreters note the danger of partial obedience — Amaziah follows God when convenient but turns to Edomite idols after military victory.

### The Unnamed Prophet (25:15-16)

When Amaziah worships the gods of Edom, a prophet confronts him: "Why do you consult this people's gods, which could not save their own people from your hand?" Amaziah's dismissive response — "Have we appointed you an adviser to the king?" — illustrates the pattern of rejecting prophetic correction.
