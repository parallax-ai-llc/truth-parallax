## Overview

2 Chronicles is the second half of the Chronicler's history, originally a single work with 1 Chronicles in the Hebrew Bible (*Divrei HaYamim*). While 1 Chronicles focused on David's reign, 2 Chronicles narrates the era from Solomon's accession through the fall of Jerusalem to Babylon (586 BCE) and concludes with Cyrus's decree permitting the exiles to return.

## Structure

2 Chronicles may be divided into three broad sections:

1. **Solomon's Reign** (Chapters 1--9): Solomon's wisdom, the construction and dedication of the Temple, and the visit of the Queen of Sheba
2. **The Kings of Judah** (Chapters 10--35): The divided monarchy told exclusively from Judah's perspective, highlighting reform and apostasy cycles through Rehoboam, Asa, Jehoshaphat, Hezekiah, and Josiah
3. **The Fall of Jerusalem** (Chapter 36): The final kings, the Babylonian conquest, and the edict of Cyrus

## Cross-Traditional Significance

2 Chronicles holds importance across multiple religious traditions:

- **Judaism**: Part of the Ketuvim (Writings) and the last book in the traditional Jewish ordering of the Tanakh; its final verses (36:22-23) echo Ezra 1:1-3, linking exile to restoration
- **Christianity**: The Temple theology of 2 Chronicles shapes New Testament worship language; Solomon's Temple as a type of Christ's body (John 2:19-21)
- **Islam**: Solomon (Sulayman) is a major prophet and king in the Quran; the Temple narrative is engaged through prophetic tradition
- **Academic**: A critical source for understanding post-exilic Judean theology and its reinterpretation of Israel's monarchic past

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Temple Worship | The Temple as the center of Israel's relationship with God |
| Davidic Covenant | God's enduring promise to David's dynasty |
| Retribution Theology | Faithfulness brings blessing; apostasy brings judgment |
| Reform and Renewal | The great reforms of Asa, Jehoshaphat, Hezekiah, and Josiah |
| Prayer | Key prayers answered by God (Solomon, Jehoshaphat, Hezekiah) |
| Hope | Cyrus's decree at the close signals restoration and continuity |
