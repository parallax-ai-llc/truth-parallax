---
title: "Valentinian Exposition Section 2"
scripture: "valentinian-exposition"
chapter: 2
---

**1** And from the Mind and the Truth there emanated the Word and the Life. And from the Word and the Life there emanated the Human Being and the Church. And this is the Ogdoad, the root of all things.

**2** The Ogdoad is the fullness of the fullness. It is the foundation upon which all the aeons rest. And the aeons emanated from the Ogdoad, each pair proceeding from the pair above it.

**3** And the Word and the Life produced ten aeons. And the Human Being and the Church produced twelve aeons. And together with the Ogdoad, there are thirty aeons in all.

**4** And the thirty aeons constitute the Pleroma, the fullness of the divine. Each aeon has its own character and its own function, but all are united in the Father through the Son.

**5** And the last of the aeons, the youngest, is Sophia, which is Wisdom. And Sophia dwelt in the outermost region of the Pleroma, at the boundary between the fullness and the void.

**6** And Sophia desired to know the Father, to comprehend him as the Mind comprehends him. But this was beyond her capacity, for only the Only-Begotten Son comprehends the Father.

**7** And in her desire, Sophia overreached herself. She brought forth a thought without the consent of her consort. And this thought, being produced without the masculine element, was formless and without substance.

**8** And Sophia was filled with grief when she saw the formlessness of that which she had produced. She feared and she repented, and she turned back toward the Father.

**9** And the Father, in his compassion, sent forth Horos, the Boundary, to separate Sophia from her formless offspring. And Sophia was restored to her place in the Pleroma.

**10** But her formless offspring was cast outside the Pleroma, into the void. And this is the origin of the material world: the formless passion of Sophia, cast into the emptiness.
