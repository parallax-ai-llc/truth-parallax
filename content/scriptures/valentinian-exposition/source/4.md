---
title: "Valentinian Exposition Section 4"
scripture: "valentinian-exposition"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Now concerning the anointing: the anointing is the mercy of the Father, who will have mercy upon them. But those whom he has anointed are the ones who have been perfected.

**2** For full jars are the ones that are usually anointed. But when the anointing of one jar is dissolved, it is emptied. And the cause of its deficiency is the condition through which its anointing goes.

**3** For at that point a breath draws it, a breath of the power of the one who is with him. But from the one who is without deficiency no seal is removed, nor is anything emptied.

**4** But what he lacks, the perfect Father fills again. He is good. He knows his plantings, because it is he who planted them in his paradise. Now his paradise is his place of rest.

**5** This is the perfection in the thought of the Father, and these are the words of his reflection. Each one of his words is the work of his one will, in the revelation of his Logos.

**6** Now concerning the baptism which exists in the fullness: it is not only a washing but it is a redemption as well. The baptism of truth is something else; it is by renunciation of the world that it is found.

**7** But those who say only with the tongue that they renounce it are liars, and they go down to the water and come up having received nothing. They say: We renounce, but their hearts have not renounced.

**8** The baptism of truth is different. It is the descent into the water of the living truth, where the spiritual seed is washed clean of the passions of the material world.

**9** And the one who receives this baptism puts off the garment of the Demiurge and puts on the garment of the Pleroma. He is no longer a servant of the archons but a child of the Father.

**10** And the anointing which follows the baptism is the confirmation of the Spirit. For the Spirit descends upon the one who has been baptized, and the Spirit seals the spiritual seed, so that it cannot be corrupted by the powers of the material world.
