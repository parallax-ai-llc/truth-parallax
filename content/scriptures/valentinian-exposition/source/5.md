---
title: "Valentinian Exposition Section 5"
scripture: "valentinian-exposition"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Now concerning the Eucharist: the bread of truth is the body of the Son, which was given for the life of the world. And the cup is the blood of truth, the outpouring of the Pleroma into the void.

**2** And those who receive the bread and the cup in truth receive the body and the blood of the Son. And the Son dwells within them, and they dwell within the Son.

**3** For the Eucharist is not merely a memorial but a participation. Through it, the spiritual seed is nourished and strengthened, and the bond between the human and the Pleroma is renewed.

**4** And the prayer of thanksgiving is: We give thanks to you, Father of all things, through your Son, whom you have sent as a savior and redeemer and messenger of your will.

**5** We give thanks to you, for you have shown us the truth and you have revealed the Pleroma. We give thanks to you, for you have planted your seed within us and watered it with the rain of gnosis.

**6** We give thanks to you, Father, Son, and Spirit, for you have brought us forth from ignorance into knowledge, from darkness into light, from death into life.

**7** And we pray that the seed which you have planted may bear fruit. That the spiritual nature within us may grow to its fullness. That we may be gathered into the Pleroma at the consummation of the age.

**8** And the Father hears this prayer, for it proceeds from the seed which he himself has planted. The seed prays to the sower, and the sower hears, for the voice of his planting is not foreign to him.

**9** This is the exposition of the Valentinian teaching, concerning the Father, the Pleroma, the fall of Sophia, and the restoration of all things through the Son and the Spirit.

**10** May the grace of the ineffable Father, and the light of the Only-Begotten Son, and the communion of the Holy Spirit be with all who seek the truth. For the truth is the way of return, and the return is the restoration, and the restoration is the fullness of the Father.

**11** A Valentinian Exposition.
