---
title: "Valentinian Exposition Section 1"
scripture: "valentinian-exposition"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The Father exists in the Monad, dwelling in silence, he who is the Monad. And the Monad is the silent one, dwelling in the ineffable, the one who dwells in silence.

**2** And the Father is he over whom no one exists. He is the one who dwells in the depth. He is the one whose fullness no one can grasp, the one about whom no thought can conceive.

**3** He is the one who is above every name that is named, the unmeasurable light, the pure, holy, immaculate one.

**4** He is the ineffable one. He is the perfect one who is imperishable. He is the one who exists, who came into existence before all things, and who is the cause of all things.

**5** The Father exists in the Monad, and the Monad is in the Father. They are two and they are one. The Monad is the dwelling place of the Father, and the Father is the fullness of the Monad.

**6** And the Father looked within himself, in the light which surrounds him, which is the spring of the living water. And he produced all the aeons which exist in the Thought, the Logos, the Ennoia.

**7** Before anything became visible of those that exist, and are set up from the source of his own fullness, the depth of the Father of all things was set up by himself.

**8** It was he who willed and it was he who brought forth: the Father. And the fruit of his will appeared as the Son. But first, before anything was manifest, the dyad existed: the Father and the Silence.

**9** And in the Silence, the Thought of the Father took shape. And the Thought is Ennoia, which is also Barbelo, the first power, the glory, the perfect one.

**10** And from the Father and the Silence, through the mediation of Ennoia, there emanated the first Tetrad: the Father, the Silence, the Mind, and the Truth. And this is the root of all things.
