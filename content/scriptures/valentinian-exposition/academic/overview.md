## Overview

The Valentinian Exposition is critically important for reconstructing the Valentinian system in its own terms, rather than relying solely on the hostile reports of heresiologists. The theological section can be compared with Irenaeus's account (Adversus Haereses 1.1-8) and with other Valentinian texts (the Gospel of Philip, the Tripartite Tractate) to assess the reliability and completeness of the heresiological reports. The liturgical supplements are among the most important evidence for Valentinian sacramental practice, providing actual ritual texts rather than descriptions from outsiders.
