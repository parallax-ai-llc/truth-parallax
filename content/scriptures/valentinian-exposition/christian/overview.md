## Overview

For Christian readers, the Valentinian Exposition provides direct evidence that Valentinian Christians practiced forms of the same sacraments as the proto-orthodox church — anointing, baptism, and eucharist — though within a fundamentally different theological framework. The question of whether these shared sacramental forms reflect a common origin or parallel development is important for understanding the relationship between Gnostic and orthodox Christianity. The text challenges the orthodox claim to exclusive possession of valid sacraments.
