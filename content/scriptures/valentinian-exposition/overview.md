## Overview

The Valentinian Exposition (NHC XI,2) is a systematic Valentinian theological treatise followed by five short liturgical texts — prayers and rituals for anointing, two forms of baptism, and two forms of the eucharist. The theological section describes the emanation of the Pleroma from the ineffable Father, the fall of Sophia through her desire to know the Father without the mediation of the Son, and the subsequent redemption of the spiritual seed. The appended liturgical supplements are among the most important evidence for Valentinian sacramental practice, providing actual ritual texts that Valentinian communities may have used in worship. Together, the exposition and its supplements present a complete Valentinian system: theology, cosmogony, soteriology, and liturgy.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | The Father and the Pleroma | The ineffable Father, the generation of the Son (Monogenes), and the emanation of the aeons |
| 2 | The Emanation of the Aeons | The progressive unfolding of the Pleroma through pairs (syzygies) of masculine and feminine aeons |
| 3 | The Fall of Sophia | Sophia's unauthorized desire to know the Father directly causes a rupture in the Pleroma and the origin of deficiency |
| 4 | The Redemption of the Seed | The Savior's descent to redeem the spiritual seed scattered in the material world and restore it to the Pleroma |
| 5 | The Liturgical Supplements | Five ritual texts: prayers for anointing, two baptismal formulas, and two eucharistic prayers |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: The most complete surviving presentation of Valentinian theology together with its corresponding ritual practice
- **Academic**: Critical for reconstructing Valentinian sacramental practice and comparing it with heresiological reports
- **Comparative**: The sacramental supplements offer parallels to initiation rites in mystery religions, Hindu diksha, and Buddhist tantra
- **Christian**: Provides evidence of how Valentinian Christians practiced baptism, anointing, and eucharist alongside (or as alternatives to) proto-orthodox rites
- **Jewish**: The concept of Pleroma and syzygy has been compared to Kabbalistic sefirot and the Shekhinah
- **Interfaith**: Addresses the universal relationship between theological understanding and liturgical practice

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| The Pleroma | The divine fullness consists of paired aeons emanating from the ineffable Father |
| Sophia's Fall | Sophia's unauthorized desire introduces deficiency into the divine realm and triggers the creation of the material world |
| Sacramental Practice | The liturgical supplements provide actual ritual texts for anointing, baptism, and eucharist |
| Theological System | The text presents a comprehensive Valentinian system from first principles to liturgical practice |
