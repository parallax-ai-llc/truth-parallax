## The Fall of Sophia — Jewish Perspectives

Jewish readers encounter in this section of Valentinian Exposition evidence of how Jewish traditions were appropriated and transformed in the Gnostic context. Jewish readers find in the Valentinian Exposition's concept of the Pleroma a possible precursor or parallel to the Kabbalistic concept of the sefirot — the ten emanations through which the infinite God (Ein Sof) manifests and interacts with the created world.

The section "The Fall of Sophia" draws on Jewish sources — Genesis narratives, prophetic imagery, wisdom traditions, and possibly early mystical speculation — but deploys them within a framework that diverges from rabbinic Judaism. This transformation is not simple distortion but a creative engagement with traditions that remained fluid and contested in the second and third centuries.

Reading this passage against rabbinic parallels illuminates both shared concerns and fundamental divergences — revealing what was at stake theologically when Jewish concepts entered Gnostic systems.
