## Overview

The Thought of Norea's celebration of a feminine figure who communicates directly with the highest God resonates with traditions worldwide that honor the feminine dimension of the divine. The Hindu goddess traditions (Shakti, Durga, Kali), the Buddhist figure of Tara who responds to the cries of suffering beings, the Kabbalistic Shekhinah as the feminine presence of God, and the Christian veneration of Mary all affirm that the feminine has a central place in the divine economy. Norea's cry and the divine response enact a universal pattern: the soul in distress calls out, and the divine answers.
