## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Thought of Norea a transformation of a minor figure from Jewish tradition into a protagonist of divine drama. In rabbinic literature, Naamah (sometimes identified with Norea) appears as the sister of Tubal-Cain (Genesis 4:22) or as the wife of Noah. The Gnostic text elevates her into a cosmic figure — the daughter of Eve who represents the spiritual race and communicates directly with the highest God. The text's positive portrayal of a feminine figure in direct relationship with God finds parallels in the Jewish Shekhinah concept and in the personification of Wisdom in Proverbs 8.
