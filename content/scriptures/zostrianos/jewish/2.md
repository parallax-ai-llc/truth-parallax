## The Initial Ascent — Jewish Perspectives

Jewish readers encounter in this section of Zostrianos evidence of how Jewish traditions were appropriated and transformed in the Gnostic context. Jewish readers find in Zostrianos a text that closely parallels the Jewish Hekhalot (heavenly palaces) mystical tradition.

The section "The Initial Ascent" draws on Jewish sources — Genesis narratives, prophetic imagery, wisdom traditions, and possibly early mystical speculation — but deploys them within a framework that diverges from rabbinic Judaism. This transformation is not simple distortion but a creative engagement with traditions that remained fluid and contested in the second and third centuries.

Reading this passage against rabbinic parallels illuminates both shared concerns and fundamental divergences — revealing what was at stake theologically when Jewish concepts entered Gnostic systems.
