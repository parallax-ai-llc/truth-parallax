## Overview

Zostrianos is a text of exceptional scholarly importance for multiple reasons: it is the longest text in the Nag Hammadi library; it represents the most elaborate fusion of Sethian mythology with Platonic metaphysics; and it is one of only two Nag Hammadi texts (along with Allogenes) whose reception in philosophical circles is directly attested — Porphyry reports that these texts were read and refuted in Plotinus's seminar in Rome around 263 CE. The text's damaged condition (only about a quarter of the original survives intact) makes reconstruction challenging but has not prevented scholars from identifying its basic structure and theological content.
