## Overview

Zostrianos's journey from despair to divine encounter speaks to one of the most universal patterns in spiritual autobiography: the dark night that precedes illumination. The text begins with a man contemplating suicide and ends with the same man transmitting divine knowledge to others — a transformation that parallels the Buddha's journey from the suffering of samsara to enlightenment under the Bodhi tree, the Sufi's passage through annihilation (fana) to subsistence in God (baqa), and the Christian mystic's 'dark night of the soul' preceding union with God. The message is universal: the deepest despair can be the gateway to the highest knowledge.
