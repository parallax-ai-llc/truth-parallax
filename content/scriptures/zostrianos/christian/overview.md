## Overview

For Christian readers, Zostrianos illuminates the broader phenomenon of ascent mysticism in early Christianity and its philosophical milieu. The text's Sethian-Platonic synthesis represents one approach to the question of how the human soul can ascend to God — a question that Christian theologians from Origen through Gregory of Nyssa to Maximus the Confessor also addressed. The successive baptisms described in Zostrianos raise questions about the relationship between ritual and spiritual transformation that are central to Christian sacramental theology.
