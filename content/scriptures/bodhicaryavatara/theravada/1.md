Theravada engagement with Chapter 1 notes the parallels between Shantideva's ecstatic praise of bodhicitta and the Theravada tradition's own exaltation of wholesome mental states. While Theravada does not employ the term "bodhicitta" in the same Mahayana sense, the aspiration to attain liberation and the recognition that wholesome intention transforms the quality of mind resonate with the Theravada understanding of kusala citta (wholesome consciousness). The chapter's imagery of bodhicitta as a supreme medicine, a sheltering tree, and a guiding star parallels Theravada devotional literature's praise of the Dhamma.

The Theravada reader finds common ground in Shantideva's insistence that the awakening mind arises through moral effort and mental cultivation, a position consistent with the Theravada emphasis on self-reliance in spiritual development.
