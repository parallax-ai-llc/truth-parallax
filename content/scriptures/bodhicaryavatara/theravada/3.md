Theravada engagement with Chapter 3's formal embrace of the awakening mind distinguishes between the Mahayana bodhisattva vow and the Theravada aspiration for arahantship while noting structural parallels. Both traditions recognize the transformative power of a resolute spiritual commitment. The Theravada tradition's own practice of taking precepts (sila) and resolving to follow the Noble Eightfold Path shares the chapter's conviction that public commitment strengthens spiritual resolve.

The chapter's universal scope — aspiring to benefit all sentient beings — extends beyond Theravada soteriology but is appreciated as an expression of the boundless compassion that the Theravada tradition also values.
