Theravada engagement with Chapter 10's dedication of merit finds resonance with the Theravada practice of pattidana (sharing of merit). While the Mahayana understanding of merit dedication is cosmically more expansive — aspiring to relieve the suffering of all sentient beings throughout all world-systems — the Theravada tradition similarly practices the sharing of merit with departed relatives, devas, and all beings. Shantideva's majestic aspiration that his merit may benefit all who suffer echoes the spirit of the Theravada metta (loving-kindness) meditation.

The chapter's concluding verses represent a universal aspiration for the welfare of all beings that transcends sectarian boundaries.
