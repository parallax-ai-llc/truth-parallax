Theravada readers approach Chapter 2's confession of wrongdoing with recognition of shared Buddhist penitential practices. The Theravada tradition has its own established forms of confession (patidesana) within the monastic code, and the spirit of honest self-examination that pervades this chapter is fully consistent with Theravada moral psychology. Shantideva's acknowledgment that karmic consequences follow from unwholesome actions mirrors the Theravada understanding of kamma.

The chapter's emphasis on taking refuge in the Three Jewels and requesting the blessings of all Buddhas represents a Mahayana devotional framework that differs from Theravada practice, yet the underlying attitude of moral humility is shared.
