Theravada engagement with Chapter 6's perfection of patience (khanti-paramita) draws on the rich Theravada literature on patience and forbearance. The Khantivadi Jataka, which tells of the Bodhisatta's patience under torture, represents the Theravada tradition's own celebration of patience as a supreme virtue. Shantideva's systematic analysis of the causes of anger and his detailed prescriptions for cultivating patience complement the Theravada understanding while adding philosophical depth.

The chapter's argument that anger destroys the merit accumulated through other practices resonates with the Theravada concern for protecting one's store of kusala kamma (wholesome karma).
