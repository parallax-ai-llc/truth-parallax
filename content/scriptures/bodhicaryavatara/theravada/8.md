Theravada engagement with Chapter 8's meditation teachings acknowledges both common ground and significant divergence. The chapter's initial instructions on abandoning attachment to worldly life and cultivating tranquility align with the Theravada samatha (calm) tradition. However, the chapter's central teaching on the exchange of self and other — the practice of imaginatively taking on others' suffering and offering them one's happiness — represents a distinctively Mahayana meditative innovation.

Theravada readers note that while the specific practice of exchange differs from Theravada meditation, the underlying compassion (karuna) that motivates it is one of the four Brahmaviharas that the Theravada tradition also cultivates extensively.
