Theravada readers find in Chapter 7's treatment of heroic effort (viriya) a direct parallel with the Theravada paramita of viriya (energy). The Theravada tradition's emphasis on sustained effort as essential for spiritual progress — captured in the Buddha's final exhortation to "strive with diligence" — aligns closely with Shantideva's analysis of the causes of laziness and his prescriptions for cultivating enthusiastic perseverance.

The chapter's practical psychology of motivation, including its analysis of the mental factors that undermine effort, enriches the Theravada understanding of viriya as one of the five spiritual faculties (indriya).
