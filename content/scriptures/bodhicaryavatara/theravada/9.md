Theravada readers approach Chapter 9's philosophical arguments about emptiness with particular interest and some reservation. The chapter's Madhyamaka analysis challenges the Theravada Abhidhamma's acceptance of ultimate realities (paramattha dhamma), arguing that even the smallest constituents of experience lack inherent existence. While mainstream Theravada does not accept the full Madhyamaka position, contemporary Theravada scholars have engaged with these arguments, finding points of contact with the Theravada understanding of anatta (non-self).

The chapter's philosophical rigor is respected even where its conclusions differ from Theravada orthodoxy.
