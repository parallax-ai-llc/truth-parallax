Chapter 5's detailed instructions on guarding awareness represent perhaps the strongest point of convergence between the Bodhicaryavatara and Theravada practice. The teaching on continuous mindful observation of one's own mental states parallels the Satipatthana Sutta's instructions for mindfulness of mind (cittanupassana). Shantideva's prescriptions for bodily conduct, speech, and mental discipline echo the Theravada training rules (sikkhapada) that structure monastic life.

Theravada readers appreciate this chapter as evidence that the Mahayana tradition shares the foundational Theravada commitment to mental discipline as the prerequisite for all higher attainments.
