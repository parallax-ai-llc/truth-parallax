Theravada readers find Chapter 4's teachings on conscientiousness (apramada) highly compatible with their own tradition's emphasis on appamada (heedfulness). The Dhammapada's opening chapters on heedfulness parallel Shantideva's warnings about the dangers of moral carelessness. The chapter's analysis of how mental afflictions arise and overwhelm the unguarded mind mirrors the Theravada Abhidhamma's detailed mapping of unwholesome mental factors.

The practical advice on maintaining moral vigilance in daily life resonates with the Theravada monastic ideal of continuous mindfulness as the foundation of the spiritual life.
