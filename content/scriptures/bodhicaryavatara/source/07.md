---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara - Chapter 7: Enthusiasm"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Having thus been patient, I should develop enthusiasm, for awakening is found through enthusiasm, and without enthusiasm there is no merit, just as without wind there is no movement.

**2** What is enthusiasm? It is finding joy in what is wholesome. Its opposing forces are explained as laziness, attachment to what is vile, and self-contempt born of discouragement.

**3** Laziness develops through a taste for idleness and sleep, and through having no disenchantment with the misery of cyclic existence.

**4** Caught in the hunter's trap of the defilements, the beings have entered the net of birth. Why am I still unaware that I have entered the mouth of Death?

**5** Do you not see that those around you are dying, one by one? And yet you slumber on, like a buffalo beside its butcher!

**6** When the Lord of Death has eyed you and every path of escape has been blocked, how can you enjoy your food? How can you take delight in sleep?

**7** Once death comes rushing in, even if you then cease being idle, what can be done at that untimely point?

**8** "This I have not done, and this I have just begun, and this is only half completed"—then suddenly the Lord of Death arrives, and one thinks, "Alas, I am done for!"

**9** For those who, through laziness, have failed to accumulate merit—it is as if an arrow had been shot and one stands helpless at the brink of the hells, the face bathed in tears.

**10** So, having found the leisure of this human life, if I then fail to cultivate virtue, there could be no greater self-deception, no greater folly.

**11** And if, on understanding this, I am still foolishly lazy, when the hour of death arrives, my sorrow will be boundless.

**12** If my unbearable body will long burn in the fires of hell, then of course my mind will be tormented by the fires of unendurable remorse.

**13** Somehow I have found this beneficial state, so difficult to find. And now, if, while I am aware of this, I am once again led off to the hells—

**14** It is as if I had been bewildered by a spell. I have been made mindless; I do not even know by what I am confused. What is it that dwells within me?

**15** Therefore, I shall transport this burden of accumulated wholesome deeds across the waters of defilement and, mounting the steed of the awakening mind, I shall progress from joy to joy. What weary person would not delight in this?
