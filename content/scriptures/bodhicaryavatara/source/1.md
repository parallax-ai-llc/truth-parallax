---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara Chapter 1"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 1
---

**1.** To those who go in bliss, the Dharma they have gained, and to all their offspring, I reverently bow. As prescribed I shall now briefly describe the entry to the Bodhisattva discipline.

**2.** What I have to say has all been said before, and I am destitute of learning and of skill with words. I therefore have no thought that this might be of benefit to others; I wrote it only to sustain my understanding.

**3.** My faith will thus be strengthened for a little while, that I might grow accustomed to this good. But others who now chance upon my words may also profit, equal to myself in fortune.

**4.** So hard to find the ease and wealth whereby the aims of beings may be gained. If now I fail to turn it to my profit, how could such a moment come again?

**5.** As when a flash of lightning rends the night, and in its glare shows all the dark black clouds had hid, likewise rarely, through the Buddhas' power, virtuous thoughts rise, brief and transient, in the world.

**6.** Thus behold the utter frailty of goodness! Except for the supreme awakening mind, there is nothing that can withstand the great and overwhelming strength of evil.

**7.** The mighty Buddhas, pondering for many ages, have seen that this, the awakening mind, alone is beneficial, whereby the limitless masses of beings will easily attain the supreme bliss.

**8.** Those who wish to crush the many sorrows of existence, those who wish to quell the pain of living beings, those who wish to have experience of a myriad joys should never turn away from the awakening mind.

**9.** The moment the awakening mind is born in those fettered and weak in the jail of cyclic existence, they will be named a "child of the Sugatas," and will be revered by all the world of gods and men.

**10.** It is the supreme ambrosia that overcomes the sovereignty of death. It is the inexhaustible treasure that eliminates all poverty in the world.

**11.** It is the supreme medicine that quells the world's disease. It is the tree that shelters all beings wandering and tired on the path of conditioned existence.

**12.** It is the universal bridge that leads to freedom from unhappy states of birth. It is the dawning moon of the mind that dispels the torment of disturbing conceptions.

**13.** It is the great sun that finally removes the misty ignorance of the world. It is the quintessential butter from the churning of the milk of Dharma.

**14.** For all those who go upon life's way and wish to taste the sustenance of happiness, this awakening mind will satisfy them with every joy and seat them in the highest bliss.

**15.** Today in the presence of all the Protectors, I invite the world to be my guests at the feast of temporal and ultimate delight. May gods, asuras, and all the rest rejoice!
