---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara Chapter 4"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 4
---

**1.** The children of the Conqueror who thus have firmly grasped the awakening mind should never turn aside from it. They should strive to keep their discipline.

**2.** If something is not well considered, and if what is undertaken is not properly pursued, then even though a promise has been made, it is right to ask: should I act or should I not?

**3.** But what has been examined by the Buddhas and their offspring, and by myself according to my ability — why should I now delay in carrying it out?

**4.** But if I make this promise and then fail to carry out my action, then since I will have deceived those multitudes of beings, what destiny must be in store for me?

**5.** As it has been taught: a person who intended to bestow some small and trifling thing but then did not is reborn as a hungry ghost.

**6.** Then how much more so if, with loud and clear proclamation, I invite all beings to the unsurpassed and highest bliss, and then deceive every one of them — what fate could then be mine?

**7.** The all-knowing Lord has said that even those who harbour just a harmful thought will linger long in the Avici hell.

**8.** Then what need to speak of those who perpetrate ill deeds against so great a benefactor as a Bodhisattva?

**9.** For if they turn against the awakening mind, evil will come to them in all their future lives, and their advancement on the paths of liberation will be long delayed.

**10.** Therefore I shall act according to my word, for if I fail to strive in this, I shall descend from low to lower still.

**11.** Though countless Buddhas who have helped all beings have passed, yet I, because of my wrong deeds, have not been reached by their compassionate treatment.

**12.** And if I continue as before, again and yet again I'll suffer in the lower realms, with sickness, bondage, laceration, and the rest.

**13.** The appearance of the Tathagata, the gaining of the human form, the faith to practise virtue — all are rarely found. When shall I find them once again?

**14.** Today I'm well and sound, I have enough to eat. This life is fleeting and deceptive; this body is but lent to me.

**15.** And yet through actions such as mine, how can I ever win a human birth again? And without human birth, there will be only evil, never good. If, when I have the chance to live a wholesome life, my actions bring no virtue, then what will become of me when dazed by pain in lower realms?
