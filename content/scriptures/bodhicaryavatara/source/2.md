---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara Chapter 2"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 2
---

**1.** To seize upon this precious awakening mind, I offer now to all the Tathagatas, to the sacred Dharma — that faultless jewel — and to the Buddhas' offspring, vast oceans of all excellence.

**2.** Whatever flowers and fruits there are, whatever kinds of medicine, whatever jewels exist within this world, whatever waters, clear and pleasant —

**3.** Mountains of jewels and likewise forest groves, quiet and joyful places, celestial trees bedecked with flowers, and trees whose branches bow with fruit —

**4.** Fragrances of the celestial realms, wish-granting trees and trees of gems, spontaneous harvests, and all ornaments worthy of those who are revered —

**5.** Lakes and ponds adorned with lotuses, resounding with the sweet-voiced cries of water-birds, things without an owner reaching to the margins of the boundless sky —

**6.** I hold them all before my mind and to the supreme Buddhas and their children make a perfect offering. Think of me with compassion, holy and most venerable beings! Accept these offerings that I make to you.

**7.** For I am destitute of merit, I have nothing more to offer. Therefore may the Lords who think of others' good accept this for my sake out of their compassion.

**8.** My body too I give entirely to the Conquerors and to their offspring. Supreme and brave ones, please accept me! Respectfully I place myself in your service.

**9.** For if you take me wholly in your care, in fearlessness I'll work for beings' benefit. The former evils I have done I shall transcend entirely, and evermore shall evil be avoided.

**10.** In bathing chambers, sweetly scented, where the crystal floors are radiant and gleaming, with graceful pillars all ablaze with gems, with canopies above of shining pearls —

**11.** I bathe the bodies of the Tathagatas and of their offspring with fragrant vases brimming over, to the strains of music and melodious song. With towels beyond compare, with scented cloths I dry them.

**12.** And robes well-dyed and finest scented, I offer to these holy beings.

**13.** With various garments, finely woven, and a hundred beautiful adornments, I will dress the noble Samantabhadra, Manjusri, Avalokitesvara, and all the rest.

**14.** With the most precious ointments, whose fragrance permeates a thousand million worlds, I will anoint the bodies of all the Buddhas, gleaming bright like pure, refined, and burnished gold.

**15.** And to the highest objects of my veneration, the Buddhas, I will offer flowers: mandarava, blue lotuses, and jasmine — all the fairest blossoms, and the sweetest garlands skillfully arranged.
