---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara Chapter 3"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 3
---

**1.** With joy I celebrate the virtue that relieves all beings from the sorrows of the lower realms, and places those who suffer in the state of happiness.

**2.** I celebrate the gathering of merit, the cause of awakening, and I rejoice in the deliverance of beings from the sufferings of conditioned life.

**3.** I celebrate the Buddhahood of the Protectors, and their stages of realisation. And I rejoice in the ocean of good intention, the awakening mind, that strives for every being's happiness and acts for every being's benefit.

**4.** And now, with hands pressed together, I beseech the Buddhas of all quarters: kindle the Dharma's light for those who grope bewildered in the darkness of their pain.

**5.** With hands pressed together I implore the Conquerors who wish to pass beyond all sorrow: remain for countless ages here, do not leave this world in blindness!

**6.** Through the merit of all the acts of good that I have done, may all the pain of every being be wholly scattered and destroyed!

**7.** For all those ailing in the world, until their every sickness has been healed, may I myself become for them the doctor, nurse, the medicine itself.

**8.** Raining down a flood of food and drink, may I dispel the ills of thirst and famine. And in the ages marked by scarcity and want, may I myself appear as food and drink.

**9.** May I become an inexhaustible treasure for those who are poor and destitute. May I turn into all things they could need, and may these things be placed close beside them.

**10.** My body, thus, and all my goods besides, and all my merits gained and to be gained, I give them all and do not count the cost, to bring about the benefit of beings.

**11.** Nirvana is attained by giving all, and it is Nirvana that my spirit seeks. If all must be surrendered, let me give it to the beings I must serve.

**12.** I give this body up for all the joy of living beings. Let them strike it, let them smite it, let them cover it with filth!

**13.** Let them toy with my body, let them laugh, let them make sport! I have given them my body — why should I now care?

**14.** Let them do to me whatever does not harm them. And when anyone should see me, let that never be in vain.

**15.** Whether those who see me are seized with anger or with faith, may the one or other be, for them, a cause by which they ever find their benefit.
