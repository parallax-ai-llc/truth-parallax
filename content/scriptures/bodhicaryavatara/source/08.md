---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara - Chapter 8: Meditation"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Having thus developed enthusiasm, the mind should be settled in meditation; for the person whose mind is distracted dwells between the fangs of the defilements.

**2** Through solitude of body and of mind, no distractions will occur. Therefore, having forsaken the worldly life, one should abandon mental wandering.

**3** Because of attachment and craving for gain and the like, the worldly life is not forsaken. Thus, in order to abandon these, the wise should reason as follows.

**4** Insight firmly grounded in tranquillity destroys the defilements. Knowing this, one should first seek tranquillity. And tranquillity arises from a genuine indifference to the world.

**5** What transient being would cling to other transient beings? Thousands of lives will pass before one meets them once again.

**6** Not seeing them, I am unhappy; and my mind cannot rest in meditation. But even should I see them, there is no satisfaction—I am tormented by desire, just as before.

**7** Through attachment to living beings, the view of reality is completely obscured, and disenchantment is destroyed. In the end, there is only grief.

**8** If I spend all my time thinking about others, my life will pass without purpose. Because of impermanent friends and kin, the Dharma that truly helps will be lost.

**9** If I behave as do the childish, I shall surely go to lower realms. And if I am led there by the childish, what is the use of keeping their company?

**10** One moment they are friends, and in the next moment they are enemies. And when the occasion is for joy, they are displeased. Ordinary people are hard to gratify!

**11** Therefore I shall dwell alone, in forests where the trees bear flowers and fruit, in peaceful, happy solitude, where there is little care, with a joyful mind.

**12** In pleasant caves and empty shrines and at the foot of trees, without attachment and without aversion, I shall dwell in equanimity.

**13** And now I shall meditate on the exchange of self and other. Since all beings, just like myself, want happiness and do not want suffering, what is so special about myself that I strive for my happiness alone?

**14** All the suffering in the world arises from seeking happiness for oneself. All the happiness in the world arises from seeking happiness for others.

**15** What need is there to say more? The childish work for their own benefit; the Buddhas work for the benefit of others. Just look at the difference between them!
