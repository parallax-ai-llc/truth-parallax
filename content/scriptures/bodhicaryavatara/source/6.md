---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara Chapter 6"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 6
---

**1.** All the good works gathered in a thousand ages, such as deeds of generosity, and offerings to the Blissful Ones — a single flash of anger shatters them.

**2.** No evil is there similar to anger, no austerity to be compared with patience. Steep yourself, therefore, in patience, in various ways, insistently.

**3.** Those tormented by the pain of anger will never know tranquillity of mind. Strangers they will be to every pleasure; they will neither sleep nor feel secure.

**4.** Even those dependent on them for their comfort and their wealth — even they will rise against the one consumed by hatred.

**5.** Friends will also weary of them; gifts and honours fail to win them loyalty. There is no one who, when rage possesses them, is happy — this in brief is what I have to say.

**6.** Anger, thus, the enemy that brings about such harms — this enemy I undertake to slay. What other foe produces such distress?

**7.** Whatever is it that I do, I should never allow my cheerfulness to be disturbed. For if I am unhappy, I will be unable to accomplish anything. And what I strive for will be left undone.

**8.** If there is a remedy when trouble strikes, what reason is there for dejection? And if there is no help for it, what use is there in being glum?

**9.** Pain, humiliation, insults, or rebukes — these I do not want, either for myself or for those I love. For those I do not like, it is the contrary!

**10.** The causes of happiness sometimes occur; the causes of suffering are very many. But without suffering there is no determination to be free. Therefore, mind, be strong!

**11.** The devotees of Durga and the men of Karnata inflict upon themselves the meaningless austerities of cuts and burns. If this they do for nothing, why am I so timid on the path that leads to freedom?

**12.** There is nothing that remains difficult when it is practised. So, through practice with the minor pains, the great pains too become endurable.

**13.** Irritation, stings of biting insects, the pangs of hunger and of thirst, and other such afflictions, rashes, sores — have I not seen that these are insignificant?

**14.** Heat and cold, wind and rain, sickness, prison, beatings — I should not be oversensitive to them, for if I am, the pain increases.

**15.** And so when enemies or friends are seen to act improperly, remain serene and tell yourself that all arises from conditions. This reflection I should always keep in mind.
