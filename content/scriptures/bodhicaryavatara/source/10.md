---
title: "Bodhicaryavatara Chapter 10"
scripture: "bodhicaryavatara"
chapter: 10
---

**1.** By all the virtue I have now amassed by composition of this work, may every being's pain be wholly scattered and destroyed!

**2.** For all those ailing in the world, until their every sickness has been healed, may I myself become for them the doctor, nurse, the medicine itself.

**3.** Raining down a flood of food and drink, may I dispel the ills of thirst and famine. And in the ages marked by scarcity and want, may I myself appear as food and drink.

**4.** May I be an inexhaustible treasure for those who are poor and destitute. May I turn into all things they could need, and may these things be placed close beside them.

**5.** My body, thus, and all my goods besides, and all my merits gained and to be gained, I give them all and do not count the cost, to bring about the benefit of beings.

**6.** May I be a guard for those who are protectorless, a guide for those who journey on the road. For those who wish to cross the water, may I be a boat, a raft, a bridge.

**7.** May I be an isle for those who yearn for land, a lamp for those who long for light. For all who need a resting place, may I be a bed. For every being, may I be the servant they desire.

**8.** May I be the wishing jewel, the vase of wealth, the word of power and supreme healing. May I be the tree of miracles, and for every being the abundant cow.

**9.** Just like the earth and the pervading elements, enduring as the sky itself endures, for the boundless multitude of living beings, may I be the ground and vessel of their life.

**10.** Thus, for everything that lives, as far as are the limits of the sky, may I provide their livelihood and nourishment until they pass beyond the bonds of suffering.

**11.** May all embodied creatures in all the hells throughout all the world-systems find happiness and relief from their sufferings. May those suffering from cold find warmth, and those from heat be cooled by the boundless waters of the Bodhisattvas' clouds.

**12.** May the forest of sword-leaves become a beautiful pleasure grove. May the trees of thorns be transformed into wish-fulfilling trees. May the animals be free from the fear of being devoured by one another.

**13.** May the hungry ghosts be satisfied, bathed, and refreshed forever by the streams of milk flowing from the hand of the noble Avalokitesvara. May the blind see forms, may the deaf hear sounds.

**14.** May the naked find clothing, the hungry find food, and the thirsty find water and delicious drink. May the frightened cease to be afraid, and those who are bound be freed. May the powerless find strength, and may people think of benefiting one another.

**15.** As long as space endures, as long as sentient beings remain, until then, may I too remain and dispel the sufferings of the world. Whatever suffering there is in the world, may it all ripen upon me. And through the virtues of the Bodhisattvas, may all beings find happiness. May the teaching that is the sole remedy for the suffering of the world, the source of all happiness and joy, endure for ages, accompanied by riches and renown!
