## Overview

Academic scholarship on the Bodhicaryavatara spans philological, philosophical, and comparative dimensions. The text has attracted significant attention from Western philosophers interested in its arguments about personal identity, altruism, and the foundations of ethics, as well as from Buddhologists working on Madhyamaka philosophy and the history of Indian Buddhism.

## Philosophical Analysis

Chapter 9 has been the focus of intensive philosophical study, particularly its arguments against the Madhyamika-Svatantrika and Yogacara positions and its defense of the Prasangika-Madhyamaka view. Western analytic philosophers have engaged with the text's reductionist arguments about personal identity, comparing them with the views of Derek Parfit and other contemporary philosophers.

## Textual Studies

Scholars have examined the textual history of the Bodhicaryavatara, noting significant differences between the Sanskrit manuscripts and the Tibetan translations. The Dunhuang manuscripts have provided important evidence for the text's early transmission. Questions about the historical Shantideva, including the legendary account of his composition of the text at Nalanda, continue to generate scholarly discussion.
