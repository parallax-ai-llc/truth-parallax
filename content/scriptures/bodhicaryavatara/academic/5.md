Chapter 5 has received significant attention from scholars interested in Buddhist ethics and moral psychology. The chapter's detailed instructions on self-monitoring have been compared with Michel Foucault's concept of "technologies of the self" and with Aristotelian accounts of practical wisdom. Academic analysis examines how the chapter constructs the ideal of the self-regulating moral subject through practices of constant self-surveillance.

Scholars have also studied the chapter's treatment of bodily deportment as evidence for understanding Indian Buddhist monastic culture and its norms of comportment.
