Chapter 8 has attracted extensive academic attention for its radical teaching on the exchange of self and other. Philosophers such as Derek Parfit and Mark Siderits have engaged with the chapter's arguments about personal identity and their implications for ethics. The chapter's claim that the distinction between self and other is merely conventional — and that therefore one has as much reason to alleviate others' suffering as one's own — represents one of the most provocative philosophical arguments in Buddhist literature.

Scholars debate whether the exchange of self and other is best understood as a meditation technique, a philosophical argument, or both.
