Scholarly treatment of Chapter 4 examines Shantideva's moral psychology in comparison with Western philosophical accounts of weakness of will (akrasia). The chapter's analysis of how mental afflictions overcome moral resolve parallels Aristotle's discussion of akrasia and contemporary philosophical debates about self-control. Academic work by Charles Goodman and others has explored the philosophical implications of Shantideva's account of moral motivation.

The chapter's use of vivid imagery and emotional appeals raises questions about the relationship between philosophical argument and rhetorical persuasion in Buddhist ethical literature.
