Academic study of Chapter 10 examines the dedication of merit as both a religious practice and a philosophical concept. Scholars analyze the chapter's implicit theory of moral causation — how merit can be "transferred" to other beings — and its compatibility with Buddhist karma doctrine. The chapter's literary qualities, including its sweeping aspirational rhetoric and vivid imagery, have been studied as examples of Indian Buddhist devotional poetry.

Comparative scholars note parallels between the dedication of merit and intercessory prayer in theistic traditions, while acknowledging the significant metaphysical differences.
