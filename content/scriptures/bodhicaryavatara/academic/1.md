Academic analysis of Chapter 1 examines the concept of bodhicitta within the broader context of Indian Buddhist philosophy. Scholars trace the development of the bodhicitta doctrine from its origins in early Mahayana sutras through its systematic treatment by Shantideva. The chapter's rhetoric of praise has been studied as a literary genre, with comparisons drawn to devotional poetry in other Indian religious traditions. Questions about the relationship between aspirational and engaged bodhicitta have generated significant philosophical discussion.

Paul Williams and Mark Siderits have examined the chapter's implicit arguments about motivation and moral psychology, comparing them with Western philosophical accounts of altruism.
