Academic scholarship on Chapter 9 is among the most extensive in Buddhist Studies. The chapter's Madhyamaka arguments have been analyzed by scholars including Paul Williams, Tom Tillemans, and Jay Garfield, with particular attention to the refutation of Yogacara idealism and the defense of conventional truth. The chapter's philosophical methodology — using reductio ad absurdum arguments to establish emptiness — has been compared with Pyrrhonian skepticism and Wittgensteinian philosophical therapy.

Debates about whether Chapter 9 represents a Svatantrika or Prasangika form of Madhyamaka continue to generate scholarly discussion.
