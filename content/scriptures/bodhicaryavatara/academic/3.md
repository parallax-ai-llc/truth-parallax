Academic analysis of Chapter 3 examines the bodhisattva vow as a speech act with performative power. Scholars compare the formal generation of bodhicitta with vow-taking ceremonies in other religious traditions, including Christian baptism, monastic profession, and Hindu diksha. The philosophical question of how a verbal commitment can transform one's spiritual trajectory has been examined from both Buddhist and Western philosophical perspectives.

Historical scholarship traces the evolution of the bodhisattva vow ceremony from early Mahayana sources through its codification in texts like the Bodhicaryavatara.
