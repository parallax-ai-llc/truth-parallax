Academic analysis of Chapter 6 on patience has generated extensive philosophical discussion. The chapter's argument that anger is never justified — even toward those who cause deliberate harm — has been debated by Western philosophers, some of whom question whether anger might serve legitimate moral functions. Martha Nussbaum's engagement with the chapter's arguments about anger has brought Shantideva's views into dialogue with contemporary emotion theory.

Scholars have also examined the chapter's implicit philosophy of moral responsibility, noting its compatibility with compatibilist and hard determinist positions in Western philosophy.
