Scholarly study of Chapter 2 examines the confession genre in Indian Buddhist literature and its relationship to earlier Brahmanical penitential practices. The chapter's liturgical structure — offering, prostration, confession, and supplication — has been analyzed as a ritual sequence with specific performative functions. Academic work on Buddhist confessional practices by scholars such as Susanne Mrozik examines how these rituals construct moral subjectivity.

Textual critics note differences between the Sanskrit and Tibetan versions of this chapter, suggesting editorial development over time.
