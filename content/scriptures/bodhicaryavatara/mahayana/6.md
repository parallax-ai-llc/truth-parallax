Chapter 6 on patience is regarded by many Mahayana practitioners as the heart of the entire text. Shantideva's argument that anger is the greatest obstacle to the bodhisattva path — destroying in a single moment the merit accumulated over countless lifetimes — establishes patience as the indispensable virtue. The chapter's systematic analysis considers patience toward those who harm us, patience in bearing suffering, and patience with the difficulties of dharma practice.

The revolutionary teaching that one's enemy is actually a precious spiritual teacher, providing the essential opportunity to practice patience, represents one of the most distinctive and challenging insights in Mahayana ethics.
