Mahayana interpretation of Chapter 5 treats the guarding of awareness as the indispensable practice that sustains all other perfections. Shantideva's detailed instructions on monitoring one's body, speech, and mind moment by moment provide a practical manual for the bodhisattva's daily life. The chapter's famous teaching that "all the fears and immeasurable sufferings of this world arise from the mind alone" locates the entire spiritual project in the transformation of consciousness.

The integration of external conduct (rules for bodily behavior) with internal monitoring (introspective awareness) reflects the Mahayana understanding that ethical behavior and mental cultivation are inseparable aspects of a single path.
