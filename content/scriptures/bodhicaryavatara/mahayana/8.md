Mahayana readers regard Chapter 8's meditation chapter as containing some of the most profound and transformative teachings in Buddhist literature. The initial sections on cultivating solitude and tranquility prepare for the chapter's revolutionary core: the practice of exchanging self and other (paratmaparivartana). The argument that all suffering arises from self-cherishing and all happiness from cherishing others inverts ordinary self-centered psychology.

The tonglen practice of imaginatively taking on others' suffering and offering them one's happiness, derived from this chapter, has become one of the most widely practiced Mahayana meditations, particularly in Tibetan Buddhism.
