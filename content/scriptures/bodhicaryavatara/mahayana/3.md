Mahayana interpretation of Chapter 3 treats the formal embrace of bodhicitta as a momentous event of cosmic significance. Shantideva's declaration that the person who generates bodhicitta becomes "an object of reverence for the world" expresses the Mahayana conviction that bodhicitta transforms the practitioner's entire being and spiritual trajectory. The chapter's vivid imagery of the bodhisattva as a hero entering the battlefield of samsara to rescue all beings captures the Mahayana ideal of spiritual courage.

The vow to maintain bodhicitta across all future lifetimes establishes the infinite temporal horizon that distinguishes the Mahayana path from paths aimed at individual liberation.
