Mahayana interpretation treasures Chapter 10's dedication of merit as the culmination and completion of the bodhisattva's practice. Shantideva's majestic aspirations — that all beings be free from suffering, that the hells be emptied, that the dharma endure forever — represent the fullest expression of the bodhisattva ideal. The chapter's scope encompasses all realms of existence, all periods of time, and all categories of sentient beings.

The dedication of merit is understood not as a mere formal conclusion but as an essential practice that transforms personal spiritual attainment into universal benefit, embodying the Mahayana principle that individual liberation is inseparable from the liberation of all beings.
