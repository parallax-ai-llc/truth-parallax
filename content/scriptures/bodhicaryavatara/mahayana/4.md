Mahayana readers understand Chapter 4 on conscientiousness as providing the practical foundation for maintaining the bodhisattva vow. Shantideva's analysis of the mental factors that undermine spiritual commitment — attachment, anger, delusion, and their many variants — draws on the Mahayana Abhidharma tradition's detailed psychology of afflictive emotions. The chapter's urgent tone reflects the conviction that the opportunity for spiritual practice is rare and precious.

The teaching that the bodhisattva must guard the awakening mind as carefully as a person crossing a precipice guards their balance establishes the standard of vigilance that the following chapters elaborate.
