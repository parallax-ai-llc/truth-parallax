Mahayana interpretation treasures Chapter 1 as one of the supreme expressions of the bodhisattva aspiration in all of Buddhist literature. Shantideva's ecstatic verses on the preciousness of bodhicitta — "It is the supreme medicine that allays the sickness of the world" — have inspired generations of Mahayana practitioners. The chapter establishes the fundamental Mahayana conviction that the aspiration for universal enlightenment is the most precious and transformative mental event possible.

The distinction between aspiring bodhicitta (the wish for enlightenment) and engaging bodhicitta (the commitment to practice) provides the structural foundation for the entire text and for the Mahayana path itself.
