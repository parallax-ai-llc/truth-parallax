Mahayana readers understand Chapter 2's confession as an essential purification practice that prepares the mind for the generation of bodhicitta. The elaborate offering, prostration, and confession sequence reflects the Mahayana Seven-Limb Prayer that became central to devotional practice across traditions. The chapter's acknowledgment that one has harmed countless beings across beginningless lifetimes reflects the Mahayana understanding of samsara's vast scope.

The confession practice is understood not as mere guilt but as a radical acknowledgment of the interconnected web of karma that binds all beings, and a commitment to transform one's relationship with that web.
