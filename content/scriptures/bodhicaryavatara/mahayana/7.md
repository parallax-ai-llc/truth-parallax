Mahayana interpretation of Chapter 7 celebrates Shantideva's analysis of heroic effort as essential for sustaining the bodhisattva's commitment across the vast time scales of the Mahayana path. The chapter identifies three types of laziness — attachment to worldly pleasures, self-deprecation, and discouragement — and prescribes specific antidotes for each. The teaching that the bodhisattva should take joy in virtuous activity "like an elephant plunging into a lake" captures the Mahayana ideal of enthusiastic engagement.

The chapter's emphasis on the power of aspiration to overcome all obstacles reflects the Mahayana conviction that the bodhisattva's resolve is ultimately stronger than any form of suffering or adversity.
