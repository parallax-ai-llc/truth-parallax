Chapter 9 represents the philosophical summit of the Bodhicaryavatara and one of the most important Madhyamaka texts in all of Buddhist literature. Shantideva presents a systematic defense of the Prasangika-Madhyamaka view that all phenomena are empty of inherent existence, engaging and refuting the positions of Yogacara, Sautrantika, Vaibhashika, and non-Buddhist schools. The chapter's argument that conventional truth and ultimate truth are both necessary for the path integrates philosophical analysis with practical soteriology.

Mahayana readers understand this chapter as demonstrating that the perfection of wisdom — the direct realization of emptiness — is the indispensable complement to the compassion developed in the preceding chapters.
