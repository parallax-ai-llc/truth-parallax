Zen readers approach Chapter 3's formal embrace of bodhicitta with characteristic directness. While the elaborate vow and aspiration framework belongs more to Indian and Tibetan Buddhism, Zen appreciates the chapter's core insight: that awakening is not a private attainment but necessarily includes all beings. The Zen Bodhisattva Vows — "Beings are numberless; I vow to save them" — express the same aspiration in Zen's characteristic economy of expression.

The chapter's teaching that the person who generates bodhicitta immediately begins to benefit all beings resonates with the Zen understanding that realization is instantaneously effective.
