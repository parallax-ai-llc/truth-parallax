Zen interpretation of Chapter 7 on heroic effort resonates with the Zen emphasis on sustained practice (shugyo) and the "great doubt" that propels the practitioner forward. The chapter's analysis of laziness and its antidotes parallels the Zen tradition's rigorous training schedule and the master's encouragement to maintain fierce determination. Shantideva's teaching that the bodhisattva should take delight in practice reflects the Zen ideal of joyful engagement.

The chapter's emphasis on the power of aspiration to overcome all obstacles aligns with the Zen understanding that sincere practice-intention is the decisive factor in realization.
