Zen engagement with Chapter 6 on patience draws on the Zen tradition's own emphasis on equanimity in the face of hardship. The Zen training environment, with its physical austerity and the teacher's challenging methods, cultivates the kind of patience Shantideva describes. The teaching that one's adversaries are actually spiritual benefactors resonates with the Zen understanding that obstacles are opportunities for awakening.

The chapter's analysis of anger as ultimately groundless — arising from causes and conditions rather than from an inherent self — aligns with the Zen insight into the empty nature of emotional reactivity.
