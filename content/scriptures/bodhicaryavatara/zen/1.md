Zen engagement with Chapter 1 appreciates Shantideva's direct pointing to the transformative power of the awakening mind while noting that Zen typically expresses this insight with greater economy. The chapter's teaching that bodhicitta transforms suffering into the path resonates with the Zen understanding that enlightenment is not separate from the ordinary mind. The famous teaching that bodhicitta arises "like a flash of lightning in a dark cloud-covered sky" captures the sudden quality of awakening that Zen emphasizes.

Zen readers value the chapter's experiential tone: bodhicitta is not a concept to be understood but a reality to be realized in direct experience.
