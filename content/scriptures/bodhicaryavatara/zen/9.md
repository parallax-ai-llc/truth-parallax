Zen engagement with Chapter 9's philosophical analysis of emptiness recognizes the same truth that koans and direct pointing seek to communicate, expressed in a more discursive form. While Zen typically avoids extended philosophical argumentation, the chapter's conclusion — that all phenomena are empty of inherent existence — is precisely the insight that Zen meditation aims to realize directly. Zen masters have noted that understanding emptiness intellectually is not the same as realizing it, a point Shantideva himself makes.

The chapter's integration of philosophical analysis with meditative realization reflects the Zen conviction that wisdom and practice are ultimately inseparable.
