Zen interpretation of Chapter 2 recognizes the value of confession as a practice of radical honesty about one's own mind. The Zen tradition has its own forms of repentance, including the Verse of Repentance recited in many Zen centers. The chapter's unflinching acknowledgment of one's past wrongdoing parallels the Zen emphasis on seeing things as they are, without self-deception or rationalization.

The directness of Shantideva's confession — its refusal to minimize or explain away moral failings — reflects the Zen commitment to uncompromising truthfulness in self-examination.
