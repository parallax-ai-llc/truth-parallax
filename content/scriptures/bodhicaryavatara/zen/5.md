Chapter 5 on guarding awareness is perhaps the most naturally "Zen" chapter in the Bodhicaryavatara. Its detailed instructions on watching the mind moment by moment parallel the core Zen practice of shikantaza (just sitting) and the broader Zen emphasis on mindfulness in all activities. Shantideva's teaching that one should observe the mind "like a spy watching a thief" captures the quality of alertness without aggression that characterizes mature Zen practice.

Zen readers appreciate the chapter's integration of physical conduct with mental awareness, reflecting the Zen understanding that body and mind are not separate.
