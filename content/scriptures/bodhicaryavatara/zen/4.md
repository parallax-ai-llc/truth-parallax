Zen engagement with Chapter 4's treatment of conscientiousness aligns closely with the Zen emphasis on continuous mindful attention. The teaching that spiritual practice requires unwavering vigilance parallels the Zen instruction to maintain awareness in every moment, whether sitting in meditation, walking, eating, or working. Shantideva's warning against complacency echoes the Zen master's constant challenge to students who believe they have "understood."

The chapter's urgency — its insistence that the opportunity for practice is rare and easily lost — reflects the Zen emphasis on the preciousness of this present moment.
