Zen readers engage with Chapter 8's meditation teachings while noting differences in approach. While the chapter's elaborate visualization practices of exchanging self and other differ from typical Zen meditation, the underlying principle — dissolving the boundary between self and other — is central to Zen realization. The chapter's teaching on solitude and renunciation of worldly attachments resonates with the Zen monastic ideal.

The fundamental insight that self-cherishing is the root of suffering and that liberation comes through genuine concern for others aligns with the Zen understanding that realization naturally expresses itself as compassionate action.
