Comparative interpretation of Chapter 3's vow places the bodhisattva commitment alongside religious vows in other traditions. Christian religious profession, Jewish covenantal commitment, Muslim shahadah, and Hindu sannyasa vows all represent formal commitments that reshape the practitioner's identity and trajectory. The bodhisattva vow is distinctive in its infinite temporal horizon and its explicit dedication to the welfare of all beings without exception.

The chapter raises comparative questions about the nature of religious commitment and the power of formal vows to transform moral motivation.
