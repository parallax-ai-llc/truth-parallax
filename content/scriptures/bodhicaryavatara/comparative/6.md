Comparative interpretation of Chapter 6 places Shantideva's treatment of patience alongside teachings on forgiveness and forbearance across traditions. The Christian command to forgive seventy times seven, the Stoic practice of apatheia, the Jewish concept of savlanut (patience), and the Islamic virtue of sabr (patient endurance) all counsel restraint in the face of provocation. Shantideva's contribution is distinctive in providing a comprehensive philosophical argument for why anger is never rational, based on the analysis of causes and conditions.

The chapter raises important comparative questions about whether anger can ever be morally justified — a question answered differently across traditions.
