Comparative analysis of Chapter 4 places Shantideva's treatment of moral conscientiousness alongside similar concerns in other traditions. The Christian examination of conscience, the Stoic practice of daily self-review, the Jewish concept of cheshbon hanefesh (accounting of the soul), and the Islamic practice of muhasabah (self-reckoning) all emphasize vigilant self-monitoring as essential to the moral life. The Buddhist framework is distinctive in attributing moral failure to specific mental afflictions (kleshas) rather than to sin or weakness of will.

The chapter reveals cross-cultural convergence on the importance of sustained moral attention.
