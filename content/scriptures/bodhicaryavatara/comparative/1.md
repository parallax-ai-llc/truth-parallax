Comparative analysis of Chapter 1 places Shantideva's praise of bodhicitta alongside comparable expressions of transformative spiritual aspiration in other traditions. The Christian concept of metanoia (fundamental change of heart), the Sufi notion of tawbah (turning toward God), and the Jewish concept of teshuvah (return) all describe moments of radical spiritual reorientation. What distinguishes the Mahayana Buddhist concept of bodhicitta is its explicit orientation toward all sentient beings: the awakening mind is from its inception universal in scope.

The chapter invites reflection on how different traditions understand the relationship between personal transformation and universal concern.
