Comparative study of Chapter 2's confession practice examines parallels across religious traditions. Christian sacramental confession, Jewish vidui, Muslim tawbah, and Hindu prayaschitta all provide formal frameworks for acknowledging moral wrongdoing and seeking purification. Shantideva's confession is distinctive in its cosmic scope — confessing wrongs committed across infinite lifetimes — and in its orientation toward all sentient beings rather than a personal God.

The chapter illuminates the widespread human recognition that honest self-examination is a prerequisite for moral and spiritual growth.
