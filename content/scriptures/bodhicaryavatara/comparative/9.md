Comparative analysis of Chapter 9's philosophical treatment of emptiness places it alongside other traditions' explorations of ultimate reality. The Christian apophatic tradition's insistence that God transcends all concepts, the Sufi doctrine of wahdat al-wujud (unity of being), the Advaita Vedanta teaching of maya and Brahman, and the Daoist concept of the nameless Dao all address the relationship between conventional experience and ultimate reality. Shantideva's Madhyamaka approach is distinctive in denying inherent existence to all phenomena without positing any positive ultimate.

The chapter invites comparative reflection on the different ways traditions navigate the relationship between ordinary experience and ultimate truth.
