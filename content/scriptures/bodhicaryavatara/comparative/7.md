Comparative analysis of Chapter 7 on heroic effort examines the concept of spiritual perseverance across traditions. The Christian virtue of fortitude, the Islamic concept of jihad an-nafs (struggle against the self), the Hindu discipline of tapas (austerity), and the Stoic endurance of hardship all value sustained effort in the face of difficulty. Shantideva's treatment is distinctive in its detailed psychological analysis of the obstacles to effort and its emphasis on joy as the proper accompaniment to spiritual striving.

The chapter illuminates the cross-cultural recognition that spiritual growth requires sustained and disciplined effort.
