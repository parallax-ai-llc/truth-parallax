Comparative study of Chapter 5's teachings on guarding awareness places them alongside contemplative practices in other traditions. The Christian practice of "custody of the senses," the Stoic discipline of prosoche (attention), and the Hindu practice of pratyahara (withdrawal of the senses) all prescribe vigilant monitoring of one's mental and sensory experience. Shantideva's instructions are distinctive in their systematic comprehensiveness and their integration of bodily conduct with mental awareness.

The chapter illuminates the cross-cultural recognition that unguarded consciousness is the root of moral and spiritual failure.
