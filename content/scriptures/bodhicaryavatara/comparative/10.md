Comparative interpretation of Chapter 10's dedication of merit examines the concept of spiritual generosity across traditions. Christian intercessory prayer, Jewish prayer for the welfare of others, Muslim du'a (supplication) for all humanity, and Hindu puja offerings all direct spiritual merit or blessing toward others. Shantideva's dedication is distinctive in its cosmic scope — encompassing all sentient beings throughout all world-systems and all time — and in its philosophical framework, which understands merit as transferable through the interconnection of all beings.

The chapter represents one of the supreme expressions of the universal compassion ideal and invites reflection on the common human aspiration to benefit others through spiritual practice.
