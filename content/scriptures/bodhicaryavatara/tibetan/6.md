Tibetan Buddhism regards Chapter 6 on patience as one of the most practically important chapters in the text. The Dalai Lama frequently teaches this chapter, emphasizing its relevance to daily life and its systematic approach to transforming anger. Tibetan commentators provide extensive meditation instructions based on the chapter's arguments, including the reflection that one's enemies provide the indispensable opportunity for practicing patience.

The chapter's famous verse — "If something can be remedied, what is the use of being unhappy about it? And if there is no remedy, what is the use of being unhappy?" — is among the most frequently quoted teachings in Tibetan Buddhism, providing a practical framework for emotional resilience.
