Tibetan interpretation of Chapter 2 integrates the confession practice into the broader framework of preliminary practices (ngondro) that prepare the practitioner for advanced meditation. The Seven-Limb Prayer, derived in part from this chapter, is recited daily in Tibetan monasteries and meditation centers worldwide. Tibetan commentators emphasize that confession purifies the obscurations that prevent the arising of bodhicitta, making it an essential prerequisite rather than a mere formality.

The chapter's visualization of offering to all Buddhas and bodhisattvas connects with the elaborate offering practices central to Tibetan devotional life.
