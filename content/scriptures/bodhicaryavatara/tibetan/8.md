Chapter 8 is regarded by Tibetan commentators as containing some of the most transformative meditation instructions in all of Buddhist literature. The tonglen practice of "giving and taking" — imaginatively taking on others' suffering and sending them happiness with each breath — derives directly from this chapter's teaching on the exchange of self and other. Tibetan teachers from Atisha through the present Dalai Lama have made tonglen a cornerstone of compassion training.

The chapter's philosophical argument that the boundary between self and other is a mere convention, not an ultimate reality, provides the theoretical justification for the practice of exchanging self and other that is distinctive to the Tibetan lojong tradition.
