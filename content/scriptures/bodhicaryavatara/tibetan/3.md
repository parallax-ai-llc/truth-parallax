Tibetan commentary on Chapter 3 treats the formal generation of bodhicitta as a ceremony of cosmic significance, celebrated with joy by all Buddhas and bodhisattvas. The distinction between aspiring bodhicitta (the wish for enlightenment) and engaging bodhicitta (the commitment to practice) structures Tibetan understanding of the stages of the bodhisattva path. Tibetan teachers emphasize that the vow taken in this chapter creates an unbreakable karmic connection to the path of awakening.

The chapter's call to rejoice in one's own commitment — "Today my life has borne fruit" — is frequently quoted in Tibetan teachings as an expression of the joy that accompanies genuine spiritual commitment.
