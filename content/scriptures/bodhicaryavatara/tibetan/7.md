Tibetan interpretation of Chapter 7 on heroic effort integrates the text with the lamrim teaching on the paramita of joyful effort (virya). Tibetan commentators identify the three types of laziness described by Shantideva — comfort-seeking, self-deprecation, and distraction — as the principal obstacles to sustained practice. The chapter's teachings on cultivating enthusiasm through reflecting on the benefits of practice and the rarity of the human opportunity form part of the standard Tibetan contemplative curriculum.

The chapter's imagery of the bodhisattva as a warrior joyfully entering battle against the kleshas resonates with the Tibetan tradition's martial metaphors for spiritual practice.
