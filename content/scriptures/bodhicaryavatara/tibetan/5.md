Chapter 5 on guarding awareness receives extensive commentary in Tibetan traditions. Tibetan interpretation integrates Shantideva's instructions with the lojong (mind training) tradition that traces its lineage to Atisha and later to Geshe Chekawa. The chapter's teachings on monitoring body, speech, and mind form the basis for detailed meditation manuals used in Tibetan monastic education.

Tibetan commentators emphasize the chapter's teaching that the mind must be observed with samprajanya (introspective awareness) while being stabilized through smriti (mindfulness), providing the theoretical foundation for Tibetan meditation practice.
