Tibetan scholarship on Chapter 9 represents one of the most extensive bodies of philosophical commentary in Buddhist literature. The chapter's Madhyamaka arguments are studied in detail in the Tibetan monastic curriculum, with debates (rtsod pa) on its key points forming a central component of geshe training. Tibetan commentators, including Minyak Kunzang Sonam and Gyaltsab Je, have provided verse-by-verse analyses of the chapter's refutations of non-Buddhist and Buddhist philosophical positions.

The chapter's distinction between the two truths (conventional and ultimate) and its defense of the Prasangika-Madhyamaka position that all phenomena lack inherent existence have been central to Tibetan philosophical education for centuries.
