Tibetan interpretation of Chapter 4 on conscientiousness draws on the lamrim (graduated path) literature's extensive treatment of the precious human rebirth (dalwa rinpoche). Shantideva's urgent warnings about the difficulty of attaining human birth and the ease of losing it are integrated into the Tibetan contemplative tradition's preliminary reflections on impermanence and the rarity of the spiritual opportunity. Tibetan commentators provide detailed meditation instructions based on this chapter's teachings.

The chapter's analysis of the mental afflictions that undermine spiritual commitment is correlated with the Tibetan Abhidharma tradition's classification of klesha (mental afflictions).
