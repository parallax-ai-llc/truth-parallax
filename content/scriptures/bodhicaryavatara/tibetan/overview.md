## Overview

The Bodhicaryavatara holds an unparalleled position in Tibetan Buddhism as perhaps the single most cherished text after the Buddha's own words. The Dalai Lama has called it his favorite Buddhist text, and it forms a core component of monastic education across all four major Tibetan schools (Nyingma, Kagyu, Sakya, and Gelug). The text's influence on Tibetan Buddhism's distinctive emphasis on compassion training (lojong) and the exchange of self and other (tonglen) has been profound and enduring.

## Key Themes

Tibetan commentators have produced an extensive literature on the Bodhicaryavatara, with particular attention to Chapter 6 (Patience) and Chapter 9 (Wisdom). The patience chapter's systematic analysis of anger and its antidotes has shaped Tibetan approaches to emotional transformation, while Chapter 9's Madhyamaka philosophical arguments have been central to Tibetan debates about the nature of emptiness.

## Lineage and Transmission

The text entered Tibet through the translations of Kawa Paltsek and Rinchen Zangpo and has been continuously taught and commented upon. Major Tibetan commentaries include those by Kunzang Pelden, Pawo Tsuglag Trengwa, and the contemporary teacher Kunzang Pelden. The Dalai Lama regularly teaches the text to large public audiences worldwide.
