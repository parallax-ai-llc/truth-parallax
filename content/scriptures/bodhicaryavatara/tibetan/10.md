Tibetan tradition regards Chapter 10's dedication of merit as both the culmination of the text and a model for the dedication practice that concludes every Tibetan Buddhist meditation session. The chapter's sweeping aspirations — that all beings be free from suffering, that the dharma endure, that the hells be emptied — are recited daily in Tibetan monasteries. The Dalai Lama has described this chapter as expressing the essence of the bodhisattva's motivation.

Tibetan commentators emphasize that the dedication of merit is not optional but essential: without proper dedication, the merit generated through practice remains unstable and can be destroyed by anger or wrong views. The chapter's extensive aspirations provide a template for transforming personal practice into universal benefit.
