Tibetan commentary on Chapter 1 treats the generation of bodhicitta as the defining moment of the Mahayana path. Kunzang Pelden's detailed commentary explains the distinction between relative bodhicitta (the aspiration for enlightenment) and ultimate bodhicitta (the direct realization of emptiness), both of which are introduced in this chapter. The Dalai Lama frequently teaches this chapter as an introduction to the bodhisattva path, emphasizing that bodhicitta is not merely a thought but a profound transformation of one's entire motivation.

Tibetan tradition regards this chapter's imagery of bodhicitta as supremely precious — more valuable than a wish-fulfilling jewel — as expressing a literal truth about the nature of the awakening mind.
