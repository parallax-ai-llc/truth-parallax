Hosea 4 opens God's formal lawsuit against the inhabitants of the land: "There is no faithfulness, no steadfast love, no knowledge of God in the land" (4:1). Catholic moral theology identifies these three failures as the root of all social and spiritual disorder. The catalogue of sins that follows — swearing, lying, murder, stealing, adultery — mirrors the Decalogue, indicating that Israel has systematically violated the covenant.

The indictment of the priesthood in 4:4-10 has been read with particular seriousness in Catholic tradition, where the ordained ministry carries grave responsibility. "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge" (4:6) is applied to clergy who fail in their teaching office. The Council of Trent cited the prophetic tradition, including Hosea, in reforming seminary education and clerical formation.

The chapter's description of religious prostitution and Baal worship (4:11-19) illustrates the Catholic understanding of idolatry as a fundamental disorder of worship — directing to creatures the devotion owed to the Creator. The interweaving of sexual and religious infidelity reflects the Catholic conviction that liturgical and moral life are inseparable.
