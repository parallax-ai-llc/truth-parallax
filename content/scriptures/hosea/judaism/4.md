Hosea 4 opens God's lawsuit with a devastating indictment: the land lacks *emet* (faithfulness), *hesed* (covenant love), and *da'at Elohim* (knowledge of God). Jewish commentators note that these three terms encapsulate the entire covenantal obligation — truth in speech, love in action, and intimacy with God in worship. Their absence explains the moral catalog that follows: cursing, lying, murder, theft, and adultery.

The indictment of the priesthood (4:4-10) struck deep in Jewish consciousness. Rashi reads "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge" as an accusation against the kohanim who failed to teach Torah. The Talmud draws from this verse the principle that a scholar who withholds Torah teaching is cursed (Sanhedrin 99b). Jewish educational tradition, with its emphasis on universal Torah study, can be seen as a response to this prophetic warning.

The syncretistic worship described in 4:11-19 — consulting wooden idols, sacrificing on hilltops, engaging in ritual prostitution — reflects the Canaanite influence that the Torah had warned against. Jewish interpretation emphasizes that idolatry is not merely an error of belief but a corruption of the entire moral order, as the chapter makes clear by connecting false worship directly to social disintegration.
