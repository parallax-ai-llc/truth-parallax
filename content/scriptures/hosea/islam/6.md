Hosea 6 opens with a call to repentance that resonates with the Quranic emphasis on *tawbah*. The confidence that God "has torn, and he will heal us" (6:1) parallels the Quranic teaching that tribulation from God is a form of purification: "And We will surely test you with something of fear and hunger and a loss of wealth and lives and fruits, but give good tidings to the patient" (2:155).

Verse 6 — "I desire mercy and not sacrifice" — aligns with the Islamic understanding that ritual acts (*ibadat*) are meaningless without sincere intention (*niyyah*) and moral conduct. The Quran states that "their meat and blood do not reach Allah, but what reaches Him is piety from you" (22:37), echoing Hosea's insistence that external religious performance without inner transformation is worthless.

The lament that Israel's devotion is fleeting like morning dew (6:4) resonates with the Quranic distinction between sincere and hypocritical faith. The Quran describes the *munafiqun* (hypocrites) who profess faith but lack genuine commitment (63:1-4). Hosea's portrait of superficial repentance that evaporates under pressure describes precisely this condition of spiritual unreliability.
