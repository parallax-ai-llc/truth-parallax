Hosea 7 describes Israel's political corruption and moral decay through vivid imagery. The oven metaphor — burning passions, sleeping baker, assassinated kings — portrays a society in terminal decline. Islamic political ethics, grounded in the Quranic concept of *amr bil-ma'ruf wa-nahy 'an al-munkar* (commanding good and forbidding evil), identifies the abdication of moral responsibility by leaders as a primary cause of social collapse.

The image of Ephraim as "a cake not turned" (7:8) — half-done, neither one thing nor the other — parallels the Quranic warning against half-hearted faith. "O you who believe, enter into Islam completely and do not follow the footsteps of Satan" (2:208). The Quran demands wholeness of commitment, and the prophetic tradition records Muhammad's warning against mixing Islamic practice with pre-Islamic customs, just as Hosea condemns Israel's mixing of divine worship with Baal rituals.

The statement "they do not cry to me from the heart, but they wail upon their beds" (7:14) distinguishes authentic prayer from mere emotional display. Islamic theology likewise emphasizes *khushu* (humility and concentration in prayer) as essential. The Prophet Muhammad taught that prayer without presence of heart is like a body without a soul — outwardly complete but spiritually empty.
