## Overview

Hosea is not explicitly named in the Quran, but Islamic tradition recognizes the broader prophetic mission described in the book as consistent with the Quranic pattern of divine warning and mercy. Muslim scholars who engage with the biblical text approach Hosea through the lens of *tawhid* (divine oneness) and the principle that the Torah (*Tawrat*) originally contained authentic revelation that has undergone *tahrif* (alteration).

## Islamic Engagement with Hosea's Themes

The central theme of Hosea — God's faithful love for an idolatrous people — aligns with the Quranic portrayal of Allah as *al-Wadud* (the Loving) and *al-Tawwab* (the Accepter of Repentance). Hosea's condemnation of Ba'al worship parallels the Quran's repeated denunciations of *shirk* (associating partners with God), the gravest sin in Islam. The prophetic call to abandon idols and return to the one true God is a universal theme in Quranic revelation.

## Critical Concerns

Islamic commentators raise concerns about the marriage narrative. The command for a prophet to marry a promiscuous woman conflicts with the Islamic doctrine of *ismah* (prophetic infallibility and moral purity). Muslim scholars typically interpret such passages as either allegorical, as evidence of textual corruption, or as belonging to a prophetic tradition whose details have been distorted. The broader theological message of divine patience and the call to repentance, however, is affirmed as consistent with Islamic teaching.
