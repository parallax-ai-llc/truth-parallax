Hosea 12 returns to the Jacob traditions — the patriarch who deceived, struggled, and was renamed. In Gnostic interpretation, Jacob's wrestling with the divine being at Peniel represents the pneumatic individual's struggle with archontic powers. The fact that Jacob prevails and receives a new name suggests that the human spirit can overcome the archons — a theme developed in Gnostic texts like the Hypostasis of the Archons.

The prophetic tradition referenced in 12:10 and 12:13 — God speaking through visions and prophets — raises the Gnostic question of prophetic reliability. If the prophets serve the Demiurge, their visions may be archontic deceptions. But some Gnostic traditions acknowledged that authentic divine revelation could penetrate the archontic veil, reaching prophets who transmitted truth without fully understanding its source. Hosea himself might be such a prophet — speaking the Demiurge's words while inadvertently revealing deeper truths.

The charge that Ephraim has accumulated wealth through dishonest commerce (12:7-8) and claims innocence resonates with the Gnostic critique of the material world. The pursuit of material wealth is the ultimate archontic distraction — it binds the pneumatic individual more deeply to the material realm while providing the illusion of security and accomplishment. True wealth, in Gnostic terms, is gnosis alone.
