Hosea 5 announces judgment on priests, people, and kings, demonstrating the Demiurge's comprehensive control over every level of Israelite society. The Gnostic reader notes that no one escapes the system — religious authorities, political leaders, and ordinary people are all trapped within the archontic matrix of covenant obligation and punitive consequence.

The critique of seeking Assyria's help (5:13) reveals the Demiurge's demand for exclusive dependence. In Gnostic terms, the jealous god cannot tolerate his subjects seeking any external source of help, not because foreign alliances are inherently wrong, but because the Demiurge cannot maintain control over those who look beyond his domain. The archontic system requires total dependence; any external reference point threatens its hegemony.

God's withdrawal to "his place" (5:15) presents an interesting Gnostic possibility: perhaps this withdrawal creates a space in which the Demiurge's oppressive presence is temporarily lifted, allowing the divine spark within pneumatic individuals to awaken without interference. The moments when the archontic god seems absent may paradoxically be the moments when the true God of the Pleroma becomes accessible.
