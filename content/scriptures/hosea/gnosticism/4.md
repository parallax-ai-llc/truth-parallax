Hosea 4 presents God's lawsuit against Israel, but Gnostic readers note that the charges — "no faithfulness, no steadfast love, no knowledge of God" — inadvertently describe the Demiurge's own deficiencies. The creator who demands knowledge of himself has withheld the true knowledge (*gnosis*) that would liberate his subjects. The accusation is, in Gnostic terms, a projection: the Demiurge accuses Israel of lacking the very thing he has suppressed.

The indictment of the priesthood (4:4-10) aligns with the Gnostic critique of institutional religion. The priests who "feed on the sin of my people" represent the archontic hierarchy that sustains itself through the ignorance and guilt of its subjects. The more the people sin, the more sacrifices are required, and the more the priestly class profits — a self-perpetuating system of spiritual exploitation.

The sexual-religious syncretism described in 4:11-19, while condemned by the prophet, might be read by Gnostic interpreters as an attempt to access spiritual power outside the Demiurge's approved channels. The "spirit of whoredom" that leads Israel astray (4:12) could alternatively be understood as Sophia — the wayward wisdom that leads pneumatic seekers beyond the boundaries of the archontic covenant toward dangerous but liberating knowledge.
