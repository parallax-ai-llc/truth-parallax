## Overview

Hosea is the first of the twelve Minor Prophets in the Hebrew Bible and Christian Old Testament. The prophet Hosea ministered in the northern kingdom of Israel during the eighth century BCE, a period of political instability and religious syncretism. His prophetic career spanned the reigns of several kings, from the prosperity of Jeroboam II through the final collapse of Samaria in 722 BCE.

## Structure

The book is typically divided into two main sections:

1. **Hosea's Marriage and Family** (Chapters 1–3): The prophet's marriage to Gomer, an unfaithful wife, serves as a living parable of God's relationship with Israel
2. **Oracles of Judgment and Restoration** (Chapters 4–14): A collection of prophetic speeches denouncing Israel's idolatry, injustice, and false reliance on foreign alliances, interspersed with promises of eventual restoration

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Judaism**: Part of the Twelve (*Trei Asar*); Hosea's marriage metaphor informs Jewish understandings of the covenant relationship between God and Israel
- **Christianity**: Hosea 11:1 ("Out of Egypt I called my son") is applied to Christ in Matthew 2:15; the marriage imagery shapes ecclesiology and Christology
- **Islam**: The prophetic themes of warning, repentance, and divine mercy resonate with Quranic teaching on God's dealings with unfaithful communities
- **Gnosticism**: The Demiurge-as-jealous-husband reading inverts Hosea's theology, casting Israel's "adultery" as spiritual seeking beyond the false creator

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Covenant Love (*Hesed*) | God's steadfast, loyal love despite Israel's unfaithfulness |
| Divine Marriage | The relationship between God and Israel portrayed as a marriage covenant |
| Judgment | The consequences of idolatry and social injustice |
| Restoration | God's ultimate purpose to heal and restore the broken relationship |
| Knowledge of God | True intimacy with God versus superficial religion |
