---
title: "Padma Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "padma-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the sacred places and the merit obtained by visiting them, as revealed in the Padma Purana.

**2** The Lord said: The earth is studded with sacred places, as the sky is studded with stars. He who visits these places with faith and devotion acquires great merit.

**3** Pushkara, the lake of Brahma, is the most ancient of all sacred places. He who bathes there during the month of Kartika acquires the merit of performing a thousand horse sacrifices.

**4** Varanasi, the city of Shiva, is the eternal abode of liberation. He who dies there is whispered the mantra of liberation by Shiva himself and attains the supreme state.

**5** Mathura, the birthplace of Krishna, and Vrindavana, the place of His divine play, are the most sacred places for the devotees of the Lord.

**6** Ayodhya, the birthplace of Rama, and Dvaraka, the city of Krishna, are among the seven sacred cities that grant liberation to those who die within their boundaries.

**7** The confluence of the Ganga and the Yamuna at Prayaga is the king of all pilgrimage sites. He who bathes there during the sacred month is freed from all sins accumulated over countless lifetimes.

**8** But the Lord said: "O Narada, the external pilgrimage is for those whose minds are not yet purified. For the pure in heart, every place is sacred."

**9** "He who carries Me in his heart, who sees Me in all beings, who serves all creatures as My forms — he has visited all the sacred places without moving a single step."

**10** "The body is the greatest temple. The Self within is the highest deity. The mind is the sacred water. Devotion is the flower offered. He who worships thus attains Me."
