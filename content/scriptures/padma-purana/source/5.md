---
title: "Padma Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "padma-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The Lord said: I shall now describe the duties of women, who are the foundation of the family and the upholders of dharma in the household.

**2** A woman who is devoted to her husband, who serves him with love and faithfulness, who maintains the sacred fire of the household — she is the goddess of the home.

**3** The wife should participate in all the religious duties of the household. Without her presence, the sacrifice is incomplete, for the wife is the other half of the husband.

**4** She should be honoured and protected by the family. Where women are honoured, there the gods are pleased. Where they are dishonoured, all sacred rites are fruitless.

**5** The woman who is devoted to the Lord, who chants His name, who serves the saints and the poor, who raises her children in dharma — she attains the supreme goal.

**6** The mother is the first teacher. It is she who shapes the character of the child, who imparts the first lessons of virtue, who plants the seeds of devotion in the young heart.

**7** The sacred texts declare: "The mother is greater than the father, greater than the teacher, greater than the entire earth. He who serves his mother serves the world."

**8** A woman may attain liberation through devotion, through service, through knowledge, through the performance of her duties with sincerity and love.

**9** The great women of the scriptures — Savitri, Anasuya, Sita, Draupadi, Mandodari — are models of virtue, devotion, and strength. Their stories inspire all who hear them.

**10** He who honours women, who protects them, who serves them with respect — he is blessed by the Lord and prospers in all his undertakings.
