---
title: "Padma Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "padma-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The Lord said: I shall now describe the nature of the Tulasi plant, which is most dear to Me of all plants and trees.

**2** Tulasi, the holy basil, is the embodiment of the goddess Lakshmi. She assumed this form out of devotion to Me, to remain always at My feet.

**3** He who worships Me with Tulasi leaves acquires merit beyond measure. Even a single Tulasi leaf offered with devotion is dearer to Me than a mountain of gold.

**4** He who plants and tends a Tulasi garden purifies the land around him. The air that passes over Tulasi becomes sacred. The water that touches Tulasi becomes holy.

**5** At the time of death, he who holds Tulasi leaves in his hand or wears them upon his body is freed from all sins and attains My supreme abode.

**6** He who lights a lamp before the Tulasi plant in the evening acquires the merit of lighting lamps in all the temples of the world.

**7** The place where Tulasi grows is a place of pilgrimage. The house where Tulasi is worshipped is sanctified. The family that tends Tulasi is blessed.

**8** Tulasi is the mother of devotion, the sister of compassion, the daughter of purity. She sanctifies all who come near her.

**9** In the month of Kartika, the worship of Tulasi is especially powerful. He who offers lamps to Tulasi during this month attains all his desires and is freed from all bondage.

**10** Thus the Lord has declared the glory of Tulasi. He who honours her, who worships her, who tends her with devotion — he is dear to the Lord and attains the supreme goal.
