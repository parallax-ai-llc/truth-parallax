---
title: "Padma Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "padma-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Lord said: I shall now describe the glory of the divine name, which is the supreme means of liberation in the age of Kali.

**2** In the age of Satya, liberation was attained through meditation. In the age of Treta, through sacrifice. In the age of Dvapara, through temple worship. In the age of Kali, through the chanting of the divine name alone.

**3** The name of the Lord is more powerful than the Lord Himself, for the Lord is bound by His name. Wherever His name is chanted, there the Lord is present.

**4** The name of the Lord purifies the tongue that utters it, the ear that hears it, and the mind that contemplates it. It destroys all sins, removes all obstacles, and bestows all blessings.

**5** Even the unconscious utterance of the Lord's name — in jest, in anger, in fear, or by accident — produces benefit. How much more powerful is the deliberate, devoted chanting!

**6** The Lord's name is the boat that carries the devotee across the ocean of worldly existence. It is the light that dispels the darkness of ignorance. It is the medicine that cures the disease of birth and death.

**7** Ajamila, the great sinner, was saved at the moment of death by uttering the name Narayana, though he called to his son and not to the Lord. Such is the power of the divine name.

**8** The Lord says: "My name is Myself. There is no difference between Me and My name. He who chants My name with love dwells in Me and I dwell in him."

**9** "In this age of Kali, there is no easier, no surer, no more effective means of liberation than the chanting of My name. I have given this gift to mankind out of compassion."

**10** Therefore, O wise ones, chant the name of the Lord at all times — in joy and in sorrow, in health and in sickness, in prosperity and in adversity. The name is the supreme refuge.
