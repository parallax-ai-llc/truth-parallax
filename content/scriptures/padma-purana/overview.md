## Overview

The Padma Purana is one of the major Mahapuranas, named after the lotus (padma) from which Brahma is said to have emerged at the beginning of creation. It is an encyclopedic text of immense proportions, containing extensive material on cosmology, mythology, pilgrimage, festivals, dharma, and devotional theology. The text is strongly Vaishnava in orientation.

The Padma Purana exists in multiple recensions that differ substantially in content and extent. The text as it exists today is clearly a composite work that grew over many centuries, with scholars dating various portions from the 4th to the 15th century CE. It is notable for its classification of the Puranas into sattvika, rajasika, and tamasika categories — a system that reflects sectarian competition.

## Structure

The text is traditionally divided into six Khandas (sections):

1. **Srishti Khanda — Creation**: Cosmogony, the emergence of the lotus, and the creation narrative
2. **Bhumi Khanda — Earth**: Sacred geography, holy places, and the greatness of Pushkara
3. **Svarga Khanda — Heaven**: Celestial realms, the devas, and heavenly pleasures
4. **Patala Khanda — Netherworld**: The subterranean realms, nagas, and the story of Rama
5. **Uttara Khanda — Final Section**: Festivals, vratas, dana, dharma, and philosophical teachings
6. **Kriya Yoga Sara — Essence of Ritual Practice**: Ritual procedures, worship methods, and devotional practices

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Padma Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: A major Vaishnava encyclopedia containing extensive devotional material and the classification of sattvika Puranas
- **Shaivism**: Critically engaged for its sectarian classifications while acknowledging shared cosmological frameworks
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Referenced for philosophical passages that describe the ultimate reality beyond sectarian forms
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Cited for its strong Vaishnava orientation and its affirmation of Vishnu's supremacy over all deities
- **Academic**: Studied for its complex textual history, multiple recensions, and encyclopedic scope

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Lotus Cosmogony | Creation symbolized through the cosmic lotus emerging from Vishnu's navel |
| Puranic Classification | The categorization of Puranas into sattvika, rajasika, and tamasika types |
| Pilgrimage | Detailed descriptions of sacred sites and their spiritual significance |
| Festivals and Vratas | Religious observances and their rewards |
| Bhakti | Devotion to Vishnu as the supreme path to liberation |
