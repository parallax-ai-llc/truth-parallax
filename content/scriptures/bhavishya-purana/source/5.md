---
title: "Bhavishya Purana Concluding Section (Book 5)"
scripture: "bhavishya-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The concluding section of the Bhavishya Purana describes the ultimate nature of time, the purpose of the Purana, and the path to liberation in the Kali age.

**2** The sage said: "Time is the supreme power of the Lord. It creates, it sustains, it destroys. No being can escape the power of time. Even the gods are subject to it. Only the Supreme Brahman is beyond time."

**3** "Time moves in cycles. The four ages revolve like the seasons of a cosmic year. In each cycle, dharma rises and falls, civilizations are built and destroyed, and the soul journeys through countless lives, seeking liberation."

**4** "The purpose of the Puranas is to make the truths of the Vedas accessible to all. The Vedas are like the ocean; the Puranas are like the rivers that bring the ocean's water to every village and field. Through stories, through prophecies, through descriptions of worship, the Puranas nourish the souls of all beings."

**5** The Purana describes the duties specific to the Kali age: "In the Kali age, elaborate Vedic rituals are no longer possible. The lifespans of men are too short, their memories too weak, their resources too limited. Therefore, the Lord has ordained simpler paths."

**6** "In the Kali age, the chanting of the divine names replaces all other forms of worship. The name of the Lord is the Veda, the sacrifice, the pilgrimage, the austerity, and the meditation. By the name alone, all can be achieved."

**7** "In the Kali age, the devotee need not withdraw to the forest or perform great sacrifices. He can attain liberation while living in the world, simply by surrendering to the Lord with a pure heart and chanting his name at every moment."

**8** The Purana describes the signs that precede the end of the Kali age: "Mountains will crumble, rivers will dry up, the earth will tremble, and great storms will sweep the land. Men will be small in stature, short-lived, and devoid of virtue."

**9** "But in the darkest hour, the Lord Kalki will appear. Born at the conjunction of two ages, he will ride forth on a white horse, blazing like the sun at the end of the world. In a single campaign, he will destroy all evil and purify the earth."

**10** "After Kalki's victory, the survivors will be pure in heart. They will become the seed of the new Krita age. Dharma will stand on four legs once more. Truth, compassion, austerity, and charity will flourish. Men will live for thousands of years in peace and plenty."

**11** The Purana declares: "The Bhavishya Purana is unique among the Puranas, for it reveals not only what has been but what will be. It teaches that the future is not random but follows the laws of dharma and karma. Knowing the future, the wise prepare themselves."

**12** The sage concluded: "Thus ends the Bhavishya Purana. He who hears it with faith obtains the knowledge of the three times — past, present, and future. He is freed from the fear of death, the sorrow of loss, and the anxiety of the unknown. He lives in the eternal present, at peace with the Lord."
