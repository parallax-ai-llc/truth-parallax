---
title: "Bhavishya Purana Pratisarga Parva (Book 3)"
scripture: "bhavishya-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Pratisarga Parva describes the secondary creation, the dynasties of future kings, and prophecies concerning the ages to come.

**2** The sage said: "After the great flood, Manu established the new creation. From him descended the races of gods and men. The solar dynasty and the lunar dynasty arose, and from them came the kings who would rule the earth through the four ages."

**3** The Purana describes the lineage of the solar dynasty in detail: from Ikshvaku through Harishchandra, Sagara, Bhagiratha, Dilipa, Raghu, Dasharatha, and Rama. Each king upheld dharma according to the standards of his age.

**4** The lunar dynasty is traced from Budha, the son of Soma, through Pururavas, Nahusha, Yayati, and Puru to the Bharata line. From the Bharatas came the Kauravas and Pandavas, whose war marked the transition from the Dvapara to the Kali age.

**5** The Purana prophesies the kings of the Kali age: "In the Kali age, kings will be of Shudra origin. They will be greedy, cruel, and given to falsehood. They will tax their people heavily and squander the revenue on their own pleasures."

**6** "Dharma will progressively decline. Men will abandon the Vedas and the sacred fire. They will eat impure food, practice no austerity, and worship wealth as the highest god. Families will be broken, and the bond between teacher and student will be dissolved."

**7** The Purana describes the marks of the Kali age: "In the Kali age, a man's worth will be judged by his wealth alone. Marriage will be a matter of mutual consent, without regard for family or dharma. Brahmins will neither study nor teach. Justice will be determined by the length of one's purse."

**8** "Cities will be infested with thieves. The Vedas will be contaminated by empty speculation. Women will be abandoned by their husbands. Rain will be scanty. The earth will yield little. People will migrate to countries where wheat and barley grow."

**9** Yet the Purana offers redemption: "Even in the darkest period of Kali, the name of the Lord remains efficacious. He who chants 'Narayana' or 'Krishna' with devotion is immediately freed from all the evils of the age."

**10** The Purana prophesies the coming of Kalki: "At the end of the Kali age, when adharma has reached its peak, Lord Vishnu will incarnate as Kalki, born in the village of Shambhala, in the family of Vishnuyasha. Riding a white horse and wielding a blazing sword, he will destroy the wicked and establish a new Krita age."

**11** "After Kalki's victory, dharma will be restored. The four varnas will resume their duties. The Vedas will be honored. Truth will prevail. The earth will be abundant, and all beings will live in happiness. The cycle of the four ages will begin anew."

**12** The sage concluded: "The Pratisarga Parva teaches that history is cyclical, not linear. The ages revolve like a great wheel. Dharma rises and falls, but it is never permanently destroyed. The divine always intervenes to restore the balance."
