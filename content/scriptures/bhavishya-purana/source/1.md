---
title: "Bhavishya Purana Brahma Parva (Book 1)"
scripture: "bhavishya-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. Salutation to the sun god Surya, the eye of the world, the soul of all beings, the source of all light and life. The Bhavishya Purana, the Purana of the future, is now revealed.

**2** Sumantu, the sage, said to King Shatanika: "O king, I shall narrate to you the Bhavishya Purana, which describes the events of the future, the duties of men, the worship of the gods, and the path to liberation."

**3** In the beginning, the Supreme Brahman manifested as Surya, the sun. From the sun came all creation. The sun is the visible form of the divine, the source of all energy, and the sustainer of all life on earth.

**4** The Purana prescribes the worship of the sun: "He who worships Surya at dawn, at noon, and at sunset, with the Gayatri mantra and with offerings of water, is freed from all diseases, all sins, and all fears."

**5** The twelve forms of the sun are described, one for each month of the year: Dhata, Aryaman, Mitra, Varuna, Indra, Vivasvan, Tvashta, Vishnu, Amsuman, Bhaga, Pusha, and Parjanya. Each form governs a month and has its own hymns and rituals.

**6** The Purana describes the duties of the four varnas as established by Brahma. The Brahmin must study, teach, perform sacrifice, and accept gifts. The Kshatriya must protect the people, administer justice, and wage righteous war. The Vaishya must tend cattle, practice agriculture, and conduct trade. The Shudra must serve the other three with devotion.

**7** The laws of marriage are described: eight forms of marriage are enumerated — Brahma, Daiva, Arsha, Prajapatya, Asura, Gandharva, Rakshasa, and Paishacha. Of these, the Brahma marriage, in which the father gives his daughter with ornaments to a learned man, is the most meritorious.

**8** The Purana describes the rites of passage: the naming ceremony, the first feeding, the tonsure, the initiation, the marriage, and the funeral. Each ceremony is accompanied by specific mantras and offerings that sanctify the transition.

**9** The duties of the king are described at length: "The king is the representative of the divine on earth. He must protect dharma with his strength, feed the hungry with his wealth, and guide the people with his wisdom. A kingdom without a righteous king is like a body without a soul."

**10** The Purana describes the construction of fire altars and the performance of Vedic sacrifices. The Agnihotra, the daily fire sacrifice, is the foundation of all ritual. He who maintains the sacred fire and offers oblations morning and evening attains the world of the gods.

**11** The sage narrated prophecies concerning future ages: "In the Kali age, the varnashrama dharma will be corrupted. Men will abandon the Vedas. Kings will be tyrants. Wealth will be the measure of all virtue. But at the end of the Kali age, the Lord will restore dharma."

**12** The Purana concludes this section: "The Brahma Parva teaches the fundamentals of dharma — the worship of the divine, the duties of each varna, and the rituals that sustain the cosmic order. He who follows these teachings lives in harmony with the universe."
