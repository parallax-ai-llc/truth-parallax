Joel 3 describes the gathering and judgment of the nations in the Valley of Jehoshaphat. Jewish tradition identifies this as the eschatological judgment when God will vindicate Israel against the nations that scattered, enslaved, and divided the people and the land. The Targum expands the description with specific references to the nations' crimes against Israel.

The verse "Multitudes, multitudes in the valley of decision!" (3:14) is interpreted in Jewish tradition as the moment when all human history reaches its conclusion and every person and nation stands before the divine tribunal. Radak connects this to the Rosh Hashanah liturgy's affirmation that God judges the entire world — *kol ba'ei olam* — passing before him like sheep before a shepherd.

The closing vision of Jerusalem's security and the fountain from the LORD's house (3:17-21) shapes the Jewish messianic hope for the restoration of Zion. The image of the stream flowing from the Temple (cf. Ezekiel 47) became a powerful symbol of the messianic age, when the Temple would be rebuilt and God's presence would radiate outward to heal the nations. The final affirmation — "the LORD dwells in Zion" — is the ultimate promise of Jewish eschatology: God's permanent, uninterrupted dwelling with his people.
