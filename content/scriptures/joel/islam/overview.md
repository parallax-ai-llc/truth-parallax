## Overview

Joel is not mentioned by name in the Quran, but the book's central themes — the Day of Judgment, cosmic upheaval, divine warning through natural catastrophe, and the call to repentance — are among the most prominent motifs in Islamic scripture. Muslim scholars who engage with Joel find in it echoes of the Quranic vision of *Yawm al-Qiyamah* (the Day of Resurrection) and *Yawm al-Din* (the Day of Judgment).

## Quranic Parallels

Joel's apocalyptic imagery finds extensive parallels in the Quran. The darkened sun and blood-red moon (Joel 2:31) parallel Quran 75:8-9 ("When the sight is dazzled, and the moon is eclipsed") and 81:1 ("When the sun is wrapped up"). The locust plague as divine warning resonates with Quran 7:133, which lists locusts among the signs sent to Pharaoh. The call to communal repentance before the Day of the LORD aligns with the Quranic emphasis on *tawbah* (repentance) before the door of mercy closes.

## Theological Assessment

Islamic theology affirms Joel's core message — that God sends warnings through nature and prophets, calling communities to repentance before final judgment — while maintaining that the specific text has undergone *tahrif*. The promise that God will "pour out my Spirit on all flesh" is reinterpreted through an Islamic lens: prophetic inspiration (*wahy*) is God's prerogative, given to chosen messengers rather than universally distributed. The eschatological framework of Joel, with its cosmic judgment and restoration, is broadly consistent with Islamic teaching on the Last Day.
