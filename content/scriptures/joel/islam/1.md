Joel 1 describes a devastating locust plague sent as a divine warning, a theme that resonates deeply with Quranic teaching. The Quran lists locusts among the signs sent to Pharaoh: "So We sent upon them the flood and locusts and lice and frogs and blood as distinct signs" (7:133). Both Joel and the Quran present natural catastrophes as divine communications — calls to repentance addressed to communities that have strayed from God's path.

The call to communal fasting and lamentation (1:13-14) aligns with the Islamic practice of collective worship during times of crisis. The Prophet Muhammad prescribed special prayers (*salat al-istisqa'*) during drought and the prayer for rain, reflecting the same prophetic impulse that Joel expresses — when natural order is disrupted, the community must turn to God in corporate supplication.

The theological principle underlying Joel 1 — that material blessings depend on spiritual faithfulness — is affirmed throughout the Quran: "If only the people of the cities had believed and feared Allah, We would have opened upon them blessings from the heaven and the earth" (7:96). The locust plague is not arbitrary punishment but a sign (*ayah*) calling the community to recognize its dependence on God and return to the path of righteousness.
