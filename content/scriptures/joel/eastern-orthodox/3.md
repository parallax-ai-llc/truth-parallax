Joel 3 presents the cosmic judgment of the nations and the final restoration of God's people. Orthodox eschatology reads this chapter as an icon of the Last Judgment — not merely a prediction of future events but a revelation of the eternal principles by which God governs history. The Valley of Jehoshaphat ("the LORD judges") represents the place where all human actions are weighed before the divine tribunal.

The cosmic imagery — the sun and moon darkened, the stars withdrawing their shining (3:15) — is understood in Orthodox theology not as the annihilation of creation but as its transfiguration. The old creation passes through judgment to emerge renewed, just as the baptized person passes through the waters of death to rise into new life. The Orthodox icon of the Last Judgment depicts not destruction but the restoration of the divine order that sin has distorted.

The concluding vision of Jerusalem as the place where "the LORD dwells" (3:17, 21) anticipates the Orthodox understanding of the Church as the place of divine indwelling. Every Orthodox temple is a Zion in miniature — the place where heaven and earth intersect in the Divine Liturgy. The fountain flowing from the LORD's house (3:18) is the baptismal water and eucharistic cup through which the life of the age to come is already communicated to the faithful.
