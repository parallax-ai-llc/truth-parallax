Joel 2 moves from cosmic terror to transformative promise. The approaching army — whether locust swarm or apocalyptic host — darkens the sun and shakes the earth. Catholic eschatology reads this imagery as a typological anticipation of the Last Judgment, when all creation will tremble before the returning Christ. The chapter provides readings for Ash Wednesday and other penitential occasions in the Roman liturgy.

The call to repentance (2:12-17) is among the most important penitential texts in Catholic tradition. "Rend your hearts and not your garments" (2:13) grounds the Catholic distinction between external observance and interior conversion — both are necessary, but the interior must animate the exterior. The Sacrament of Reconciliation embodies this principle: confession is not merely verbal acknowledgment but involves genuine contrition (*contritio cordis*) and firm purpose of amendment.

The Spirit-outpouring passage (2:28-32) is foundational for Catholic pneumatology. Pentecost is the birthday of the Church, and the Catholic Charismatic Renewal finds in Joel's promise scriptural warrant for the ongoing charismatic gifts. Pope John XXIII's prayer for a "new Pentecost" at Vatican II explicitly invoked this Joeline vision, and the Council's emphasis on the laity's participation in the prophetic office of Christ echoes the democratization of prophecy announced by Joel.
