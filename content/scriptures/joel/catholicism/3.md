Joel 3 presents the final judgment of the nations assembled in the Valley of Jehoshaphat. Catholic eschatology reads this chapter within the framework of the general judgment — the definitive separation of righteous and unrighteous that follows Christ's return. The Catechism (CCC 1038-1041) teaches that all nations will be gathered before the Son of Man, echoing Joel's vision of universal accountability.

The agricultural imagery of judgment — "Put in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe" (3:13) — connects directly to Jesus's parables of the harvest (Matthew 13:39) and the Book of Revelation's vision of the winepress of God's wrath (Revelation 14:19-20). Catholic interpretation reads these texts as a unified eschatological vision in which the patience of God eventually gives way to definitive judgment.

The concluding promise of abundance and divine dwelling (3:17-21) anticipates the Catholic vision of the new creation. The fountain flowing from the house of the LORD (3:18) is read typologically as the grace flowing from the sacraments — particularly baptism and the Eucharist — which already anticipate in time the eternal communion of heaven. "The LORD dwells in Zion" (3:21) finds its ultimate fulfillment in the heavenly Jerusalem, where God will be "all in all."
