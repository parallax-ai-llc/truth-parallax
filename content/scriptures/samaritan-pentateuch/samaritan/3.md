## Samaritan Reading

The Samaritan community reads Leviticus as the divinely ordained system of priestly law that governs the sacrificial worship at Mount Gerizim. Although the destruction of the Samaritan temple in 128 BCE and the subsequent cessation of regular sacrifice altered the practical application of Levitical law, the Samaritan community continues to observe the Passover sacrifice on Mount Gerizim according to the instructions preserved in their Pentateuch.

The Samaritan Leviticus differs from the Masoretic Text in relatively few significant variants, as the book's priestly legislation offered less opportunity for sectarian modification than the narrative and legal sections of other books. The purity laws, dietary restrictions, and festival calendar prescribed in Leviticus remain binding in Samaritan practice, interpreted through the community's own oral tradition and the rulings of its high priests.
