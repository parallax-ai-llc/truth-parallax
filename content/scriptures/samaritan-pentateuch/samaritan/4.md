## Samaritan Reading

The Samaritan reading of Numbers traces the wilderness journey as a narrative of faithfulness and rebellion that prefigures the Samaritan community's own history. The census data, tribal organization, and journey stations described in Numbers establish the organizational structure of the Israelite community as it moved toward the promised land and the sacred mountain.

The Samaritan Numbers contains several harmonistic expansions that bring its narrative into closer agreement with parallel passages in Deuteronomy. The story of Balaam (chapters 22-24) is of particular interest, as the Samaritan text's version of Balaam's oracles contains references to Mount Gerizim that are absent from the Masoretic Text. The Samaritan community reads these chapters as confirmation of Gerizim's election by God.
