## Samaritan Reading

The Samaritan reading of Genesis emphasizes the continuity between the creation of the world and the election of the patriarchs as the foundation for the eventual revelation at Sinai and the establishment of worship on Mount Gerizim. For the Samaritan community, Genesis is not merely history but the theological prologue to the Torah's central commandment: to worship the one God at the place He has chosen — Mount Gerizim.

The Samaritan text of Genesis differs from the Masoretic in numerous details, particularly in chronological data (the ages of the patriarchs in chapters 5 and 11), where the Samaritan figures often agree with the Septuagint against the Masoretic Text. The Samaritan community reads these chapters during its annual liturgical cycle, understanding the patriarchal narratives as prefigurations of the Samaritan community's own experience of faithfulness, exile, and return. The creation account establishes God's sovereignty and the sanctity of the seventh day — the Sabbath — which the Samaritans observe with particular rigor.
