## Overview

For the Samaritan community — one of the oldest and smallest religious groups in the world, numbering fewer than 900 members — the Samaritan Pentateuch is the sole scripture, the complete and final revelation of God to Moses. Unlike Jewish and Christian traditions, the Samaritans accept no prophetic books, wisdom literature, or later canonical additions. The Torah alone, in its Samaritan recension, constitutes sacred scripture.

The community traces its textual tradition to the high priest Abisha, grandson of Aaron, who is believed to have written the original scroll in the thirteenth year after the Israelite entry into the land. The Abisha Scroll, preserved in the Samaritan synagogue on Mount Gerizim, is venerated as the oldest Torah manuscript in existence. The Samaritan reading of the Pentateuch is inseparable from the community's identity as the faithful remnant of Israel, centered not on Jerusalem but on Mount Gerizim — the mountain chosen by God.
