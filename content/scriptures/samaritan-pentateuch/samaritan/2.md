## Samaritan Reading

The Samaritan reading of Exodus centers on the revelation at Sinai and the establishment of the covenant — events that, in the Samaritan understanding, point directly to the sanctity of Mount Gerizim. The Samaritan Exodus contains several significant expansions not found in the Masoretic Text, particularly the addition of material from Deuteronomy into the Sinai narrative to create a more complete and harmonized account of the revelation.

The Ten Commandments in the Samaritan Exodus include the distinctive Tenth Commandment — an instruction to build an altar on Mount Gerizim — which the Samaritan community regards as an integral part of the original revelation, not a later addition. The Exodus narrative of liberation from slavery and covenant at the mountain is liturgically central to Samaritan life, especially during the annual Passover sacrifice performed on Mount Gerizim to this day.
