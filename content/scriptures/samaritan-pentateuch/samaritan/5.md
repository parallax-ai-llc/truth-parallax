## Samaritan Reading

The Samaritan reading of Deuteronomy reaches the theological climax of the entire Pentateuch with the Tenth Commandment — the commandment to build an altar on Mount Gerizim. This commandment, absent from the Masoretic Text, is for the Samaritan community the keystone of the Torah's legal architecture, the culmination of the covenant relationship established at creation and renewed through the patriarchs and at Sinai.

Deuteronomy's repeated phrase 'the place that the LORD will choose' is rendered in the Samaritan text as 'the place that the LORD has chosen' — a past tense that identifies the chosen place as Mount Gerizim, already revealed and established, rather than a future, unnamed location that Jewish tradition identifies with Jerusalem. This single grammatical difference encapsulates the fundamental theological divergence between the Samaritan and Jewish traditions and grounds the Samaritan community's entire religious identity.
