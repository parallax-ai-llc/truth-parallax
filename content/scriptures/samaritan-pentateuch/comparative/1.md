## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Samaritan Genesis alongside other versions reveals how textual transmission functions in communities that share a common scripture. The fact that Samaritan and Jewish communities preserved the same book of Genesis for over two millennia yet with thousands of minor variations illustrates the tension between fidelity and adaptation in scriptural transmission.

Genesis provides a particularly interesting case for comparison because its theological content — creation, flood, patriarchal election — is shared across Jewish, Christian, Islamic, and Samaritan traditions, each interpreting the same narratives through distinct theological lenses. The Samaritan Genesis reminds comparative scholars that scriptural identity is not determined solely by content but also by the community that reads, transmits, and interprets the text.
