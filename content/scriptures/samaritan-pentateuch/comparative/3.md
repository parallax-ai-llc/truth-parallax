## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Samaritan Leviticus raises questions about the universality and particularity of priestly religion. The detailed sacrificial legislation — shared in its essentials between Samaritan and Jewish traditions — invites comparison with priestly systems in other ancient cultures: the Vedic *yajna* rituals of Hinduism, the temple sacrifices of ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia, and the ritual prescriptions of Zoroastrianism.

The persistence of the Samaritan Passover sacrifice on Mount Gerizim — one of the few surviving examples of ancient Israelite sacrificial practice — offers comparative scholars a living window into the priestly religion described in Leviticus, a tradition otherwise accessible only through texts and archaeological remains.
