## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Samaritan Numbers highlights the role of wilderness narratives in forming communal identity. The Israelite journey through the wilderness — with its themes of divine guidance, human rebellion, and covenantal fidelity — functions as a foundational myth for both Samaritan and Jewish communities, much as the Hijra serves for Islam or the Exodus itself for African American Christianity.

The Balaam narratives invite comparison with prophetic traditions in other cultures where foreign seers or prophets are compelled to speak truth about a people not their own. The Samaritan version of these oracles, with its Gerizim references, demonstrates how communities adapt shared prophetic traditions to support their own theological claims.
