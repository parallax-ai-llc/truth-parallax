## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Samaritan Exodus highlights how liberation narratives and covenant traditions function differently within related but distinct communities. The Exodus story — deliverance from slavery, revelation at a sacred mountain, establishment of a covenant community — is a shared cultural inheritance, but its meaning diverges sharply when the identity of the chosen mountain is contested.

The Samaritan Exodus demonstrates that the same foundational narrative can support competing claims to religious legitimacy, a pattern observable across traditions: Catholic and Orthodox disputes over papal authority, Sunni and Shia disagreements about legitimate succession, and Theravada and Mahayana debates about authentic Buddhist teaching all reflect similar dynamics of shared text with divergent interpretation.
