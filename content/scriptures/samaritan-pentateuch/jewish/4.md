## Jewish Reading

Jewish scholars examine the Samaritan Numbers for its harmonistic expansions and its treatment of the Balaam narratives. The insertion of Deuteronomic material into Numbers — particularly in the wilderness narrative and the Balaam cycle — reveals how Samaritan scribes sought to create a more unified Pentateuchal narrative.

The Balaam oracles present a particularly interesting case, as the Samaritan text's references to Gerizim have been compared with the Deir Alla inscription (discovered in Jordan in 1967), which mentions a seer named Balaam. Jewish scholars debate whether the Samaritan readings preserve ancient traditions about Balaam's connection to the central highlands or represent later Samaritan modifications to support the Gerizim cult.
