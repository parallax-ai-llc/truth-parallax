## Jewish Reading

Jewish scholars examine the Samaritan Genesis alongside the Masoretic Text to identify variant readings and assess their significance. The chronological differences in the patriarchal genealogies of chapters 5 and 11 are among the most discussed, as the Samaritan figures create a different timeline for early human history — one that often agrees with the Septuagint, suggesting that the Masoretic chronology may represent a later revision.

The theological variants in Genesis are relatively few compared to later books, but scholars note subtle differences in divine names and epithets that may reflect different theological sensibilities. The Samaritan Genesis provides Jewish textual critics with an independent witness to the pre-Masoretic text, helping to distinguish between original readings and later scribal modifications in both traditions.
