## Jewish Reading

Jewish scholars find the Samaritan Exodus particularly interesting for its expansions of the plague narratives and the Sinai theophany. The Samaritan text inserts speech formulae and transitional material that smooth the narrative flow, suggesting a scribal tradition that valued narrative clarity and completeness over the terse style preserved in the Masoretic Text.

The most theologically significant variant is the Samaritan version of the Decalogue, which includes the Gerizim commandment. Jewish scholars generally regard this as a sectarian addition designed to legitimate the Samaritan sanctuary, though the question of whether the Masoretic text might have undergone a corresponding deletion remains debated. The Samaritan Exodus provides valuable comparative material for understanding how different communities understood and transmitted the Sinai covenant.
