## Overview

Jewish scholars engage with the Samaritan Pentateuch as both a textual witness and a theological challenge. The approximately 6,000 variant readings between the Samaritan and Masoretic texts raise fundamental questions about the transmission history of the Torah and the processes by which the biblical text was standardized in the late Second Temple period.

Many Jewish scholars note that a significant number of Samaritan variants are shared by the Septuagint or the Dead Sea Scrolls, suggesting that the Samaritan text preserves ancient readings that were once part of a broader textual plurality. The theological variants — particularly the Samaritan references to Mount Gerizim — are understood as sectarian modifications, but the non-theological variants are treated as valuable evidence for reconstructing the pre-Masoretic text of the Torah.
