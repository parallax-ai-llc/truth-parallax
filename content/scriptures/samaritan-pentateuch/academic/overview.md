## Overview

Academic study of the Samaritan Pentateuch has undergone a revolution since the discovery of the Dead Sea Scrolls. Whereas earlier scholars often dismissed the Samaritan text as a late, tendentious recension of the Masoretic Text, the Qumran manuscripts revealed that many Samaritan readings are attested in pre-Christian Jewish manuscripts, demonstrating that the Samaritan textual tradition is far more ancient and complex than previously assumed.

Contemporary textual critics recognize the Samaritan Pentateuch as one of three major pre-Masoretic textual families — alongside the proto-Masoretic and the Vorlage of the Septuagint — each preserving ancient readings lost in the other traditions. The study of Samaritan scribal practices, including their distinctive expansionist tendency to harmonize parallel passages, illuminates the editorial processes that shaped all versions of the Pentateuch.
