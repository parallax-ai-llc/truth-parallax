## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the Samaritan Exodus reveals the text's most extensive harmonistic expansions. The plague narratives contain inserted passages from Deuteronomy that provide Moses with advance knowledge of each plague, creating a more coherent narrative structure. These expansions are attested in pre-Samaritan manuscripts from Qumran, demonstrating their antiquity.

The Decalogue presents one of the most important text-critical problems in Pentateuchal studies. The Samaritan Tenth Commandment — commanding the construction of an altar on Mount Gerizim — is composed of material drawn from Deuteronomy 11 and 27. Scholars debate whether this represents a Samaritan addition to an original nine- or ten-commandment text, or whether the proto-Masoretic tradition deleted a Gerizim reference that once stood in the text.
