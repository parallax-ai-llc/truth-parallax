## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the Samaritan Genesis focuses on textual criticism, comparing its readings with the Masoretic Text, the Septuagint, and the Dead Sea Scrolls. The chronological data in the genealogies of chapters 5 and 11 present one of the most complex text-critical problems in biblical studies, with three distinct numerical traditions (Masoretic, Samaritan, and Septuagintal) each claiming antiquity.

Scholars have identified several categories of Samaritan variants in Genesis: harmonistic additions that bring parallel passages into agreement, phonological variations reflecting Samaritan pronunciation, and a small number of ideological changes related to the Gerizim tradition. The discovery of Genesis manuscripts at Qumran that share readings with the Samaritan text has demonstrated that the Samaritan textual tradition was not an isolated sectarian production but part of a broader stream of pre-Masoretic textual plurality.
