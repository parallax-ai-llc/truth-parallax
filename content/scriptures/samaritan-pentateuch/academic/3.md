## Academic Reading

Academic study of the Samaritan Leviticus concentrates on its contribution to understanding Priestly (P) source material and the history of Israelite sacrificial law. The relative textual stability of Leviticus across the Samaritan and Masoretic traditions suggests that the Priestly legislation achieved its essential form before the Samaritan-Jewish split.

Text-critical analysis reveals that the Samaritan Leviticus preserves several readings that appear more original than their Masoretic counterparts, particularly in formulaic legal passages where scribal errors in one tradition can be corrected by reference to the other. The Samaritan Leviticus also provides evidence for the pronunciation and understanding of technical priestly terminology in a tradition independent of the Masoretic vocalization.
