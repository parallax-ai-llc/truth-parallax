## Protestant Reading

Protestant interpreters approach the Samaritan Leviticus through the lens of the Reformation's complex relationship with Old Testament law. While the Reformers distinguished between the moral, ceremonial, and civil dimensions of the Mosaic law — generally regarding the ceremonial law as fulfilled in Christ — the Samaritan Leviticus reminds Protestant scholars that for some communities, the Levitical system remains a living religious obligation.

The Samaritan text of Leviticus contributes to Protestant Bible translation in technical areas where the Masoretic Text is difficult or ambiguous, particularly in the description of skin diseases (traditionally rendered as 'leprosy'), sacrificial procedures, and purity regulations. Understanding Samaritan readings helps translators render these passages more accurately for modern readers.
