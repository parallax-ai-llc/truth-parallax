## Overview

Protestant scholars have engaged with the Samaritan Pentateuch since the seventeenth century, when European scholars first gained access to Samaritan manuscripts. The text's relevance to the Protestant tradition centers on two concerns: the accuracy of Bible translation and the authority of the Pentateuch.

For Bible translators working in the Protestant tradition, the Samaritan Pentateuch provides alternative readings that sometimes clarify obscure passages in the Masoretic Text. Modern critical editions of the Hebrew Bible, used by Protestant translators worldwide, routinely cite Samaritan variants in their critical apparatus. The Samaritan Pentateuch also contributes to Protestant debates about the relationship between textual criticism and biblical authority, challenging simplistic assumptions about a single original text.
