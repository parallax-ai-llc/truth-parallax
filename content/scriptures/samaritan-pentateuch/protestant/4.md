## Protestant Reading

Protestant scholars engage with the Samaritan Numbers primarily through its contribution to understanding the wilderness narrative and the Balaam cycle. The text's harmonistic expansions illustrate scribal methods that inform Protestant debates about the composition and unity of the Pentateuch.

The Balaam oracles have been important in Protestant typological interpretation, where Balaam's prophecy of a 'star out of Jacob' (Numbers 24:17) is read as a messianic prediction. The Samaritan text's version of these oracles, with its additional Gerizim references, reminds Protestant interpreters of the complex textual history underlying familiar proof-texts and the importance of textual criticism for responsible biblical interpretation.
