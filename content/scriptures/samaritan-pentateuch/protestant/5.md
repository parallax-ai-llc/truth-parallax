## Protestant Reading

Protestant scholars engage with the Samaritan Deuteronomy as a test case for the relationship between text, tradition, and theology. The Tenth Commandment raises fundamental questions about the original form of the Decalogue — questions that bear on Protestant theology's emphasis on the moral law as the foundation of Christian ethics.

The verb tense dispute — 'will choose' versus 'has chosen' — also resonates with Protestant debates about the relationship between Old and New Testaments. If the chosen place was already identified in Moses' time, the theology of Deuteronomy points to a specific historical sanctuary; if the choice is future, it opens the possibility of typological fulfillment — a reading that Protestant interpreters have historically applied to Christ and the church as the ultimate 'place' of God's presence.
