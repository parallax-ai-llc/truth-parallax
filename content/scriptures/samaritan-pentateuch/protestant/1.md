## Protestant Reading

Protestant engagement with the Samaritan Genesis has historically centered on its value for Bible translation and textual reconstruction. When the Samaritan Pentateuch first became available to European scholars in the seventeenth century, its variant readings sparked intense debate about the reliability of the Masoretic Text and the possibility of recovering a more original form of the biblical text.

Modern Protestant translators consult the Samaritan Genesis when the Masoretic Text is obscure or appears corrupt, particularly in the genealogical data of chapters 5 and 11. The Samaritan readings occasionally inform footnotes in major Protestant Bible translations such as the NIV, ESV, and NRSV, demonstrating the text's ongoing relevance to the Protestant commitment to accurate, accessible scripture.
