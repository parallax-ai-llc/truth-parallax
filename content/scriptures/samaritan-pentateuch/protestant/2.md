## Protestant Reading

Protestant scholars have given particular attention to the Samaritan Exodus for its implications for understanding the Decalogue. The Samaritan Tenth Commandment raises questions about the original form and number of the commandments that are relevant to Protestant theological reflection on the law of God.

The Samaritan Exodus's harmonistic expansions in the plague narratives illustrate scribal techniques that illuminate the composition of the Pentateuch as a whole. Protestant commentators note that understanding these textual dynamics does not undermine biblical authority but rather deepens appreciation for the complex processes through which God's word was transmitted and preserved across centuries.
