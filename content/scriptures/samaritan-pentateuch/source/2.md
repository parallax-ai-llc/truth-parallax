title: "Samaritan Pentateuch Section 2"
scripture: "samaritan-pentateuch"
chapter: 2
---

**1** [Key Variant — Exodus 12:40] Now the sojourning of the children of Israel and their fathers, who dwelt in the land of Canaan and in the land of Egypt, was four hundred and thirty years. [Note: The Samaritan adds "and their fathers" and "the land of Canaan," including the patriarchal period in the 430 years.]

**2** [Key Variant — Exodus 20:17, the Tenth Commandment] The Samaritan Decalogue includes an additional commandment after the tenth, which reads: "And it shall come to pass when the Lord thy God shall bring thee into the land of the Canaanites whither thou goest to possess it, thou shalt set up for thyself great stones and plaster them with plaster,

**3** and thou shalt write upon them all the words of this law. And when ye are passed over the Jordan, ye shall set up these stones, which I command thee this day, in Mount Gerizim. And thou shalt build there an altar unto the Lord thy God, an altar of stones."

**4** [Note: This commandment to build an altar on Mount Gerizim is the most significant theological difference between the Samaritan and Masoretic texts, establishing Mount Gerizim rather than Jerusalem as the divinely chosen place of worship.]

**5** [Key Variant — Exodus 20:21] In every place where I cause My name to be remembered, I will come unto thee and I will bless thee. [Note: The Masoretic future tense "I will choose" becomes past tense in the Samaritan, "I have chosen," implying the site was already designated.]
