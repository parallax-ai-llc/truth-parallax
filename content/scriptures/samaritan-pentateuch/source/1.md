---
title: "Samaritan Pentateuch Section 1"
scripture: "samaritan-pentateuch"
chapter: 1
---

**1** In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth. And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters. [Genesis 1:1-2, as in the Masoretic Text]

**2** [Key Variant — Genesis 2:2] And God finished on the sixth day His work which He had made; and He rested on the seventh day from all His work which He had made. [Note: The Samaritan reads "sixth day" where the Masoretic Text reads "seventh day," avoiding the implication that God worked on the Sabbath.]

**3** [Key Variant — Genesis 5 Chronology] The genealogies from Adam to Noah differ in the ages of the patriarchs. In the Samaritan reckoning, Adam lived 130 years and begat Seth, and after begetting Seth lived 800 years. The total years from creation to the Flood are shorter in the Samaritan reckoning than in the Masoretic Text.

**4** [Key Variant — Genesis 11 Chronology] Similarly, the genealogies from Shem to Abraham show different ages at the birth of each patriarch's son. The Samaritan chronology yields a different total for the age of the world.

**5** [Key Variant — Genesis 22:2] And God said, Take now thy son, thine only son, whom thou lovest, even Isaac, and get thee into the land of Moreh. [Note: The Samaritan reads "Moreh" where the Masoretic reads "Moriah," identifying the place of binding with the Samaritan sacred site near Shechem rather than Jerusalem.]
