**1** [Harmonistic Expansions] Throughout the Samaritan Pentateuch, commands given by God are followed by expanded narratives of their fulfillment, often borrowing from Deuteronomy to supplement Exodus and Numbers.

**2** [Example — Exodus 7-11] In the account of the plagues upon Egypt, each plague narrative in the Samaritan text includes the full speech of God to Moses and then the fulfillment, harmonizing the account so that no detail is left implicit.

**3** [Example — Numbers 10:10] After God's command to make silver trumpets, the Samaritan inserts the corresponding fulfillment narrative: "And Moses made two trumpets of silver, of beaten work he made them."

**4** [Example — Numbers 13:33] The report of the spies in the Samaritan omits the mention of the Nephilim, sons of Anak, which appears in the Masoretic Text.

**5** [Phonological and Orthographic Differences] The Samaritan script preserves the paleo-Hebrew alphabet used before the Babylonian exile, while the Masoretic Text is written in the square Aramaic script adopted during the exile.

**6** The Samaritan text contains approximately six thousand variants from the Masoretic Text, most of which are minor orthographic or grammatical differences. About two thousand of these agree with the Septuagint against the Masoretic Text.
