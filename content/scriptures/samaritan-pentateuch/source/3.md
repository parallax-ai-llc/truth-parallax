title: "Samaritan Pentateuch Section 3"
scripture: "samaritan-pentateuch"
chapter: 3
---

**1** [Key Variant — Deuteronomy 5:6-21, the Decalogue] The Samaritan version of the Ten Commandments in Deuteronomy harmonizes with the Exodus version, including the additional commandment concerning Mount Gerizim.

**2** [Key Variant — Deuteronomy 11:30] Are they not on the other side of the Jordan, by the way where the sun goeth down, in the land of the Canaanites, which dwell in the Arabah, over against Gilgal, beside the oaks of Moreh, over against Shechem? [Note: The Samaritan adds "over against Shechem," further identifying the sacred site.]

**3** [Key Variant — Deuteronomy 12:5] But unto the place which the Lord your God hath chosen out of all your tribes to put His name there, even unto His habitation shall ye seek, and thither thou shalt come. [Note: The Samaritan reads "hath chosen" (past tense) rather than "shall choose" (future tense), asserting that the place — Mount Gerizim — was already chosen.]

**4** [Key Variant — Deuteronomy 27:4] And it shall be when ye are gone over the Jordan, that ye shall set up these stones which I command thee this day, in Mount Gerizim. [Note: The Masoretic Text reads "Mount Ebal" while the Samaritan reads "Mount Gerizim."]

**5** This variant in Deuteronomy 27:4 is of great importance, for it directly determines where the altar of unhewn stones shall be built and where the blessings shall be pronounced.
