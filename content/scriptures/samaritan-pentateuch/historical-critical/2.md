## Historical-Critical Reading

Historical-critical analysis of the Samaritan Exodus focuses on its harmonistic expansions as evidence of editorial activity in the Pentateuch. The insertion of Deuteronomic material into the Exodus narrative — particularly in the plague cycle and the Sinai theophany — suggests that scribes actively worked to integrate the parallel accounts found in Exodus and Deuteronomy.

This harmonistic tendency, attested in pre-Samaritan Qumran manuscripts (especially 4QpaleoExod^m), predates the crystallization of the Samaritan community and reflects broader scribal practices in the Second Temple period. Source critics find in these expansions evidence that the distinction between Exodus and Deuteronomy sources (traditionally assigned to E/J and D respectively) was recognized by ancient scribes who sought to resolve the tensions between them.
