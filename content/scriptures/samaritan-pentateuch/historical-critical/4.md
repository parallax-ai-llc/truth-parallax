## Historical-Critical Reading

Historical-critical scholars use the Samaritan Numbers to trace the editorial history of the wilderness traditions. The text's harmonistic expansions, particularly the insertion of Deuteronomic material, provide evidence for the scribal processes by which the Pentateuch's parallel accounts were gradually brought into closer agreement.

The Balaam cycle in the Samaritan Numbers is of particular interest for source criticism. Scholars have long recognized multiple literary strands in the Balaam narratives (traditionally assigned to J and E), and the Samaritan text's expansions may preserve or reflect pre-redactional arrangements of this material that differ from the Masoretic compilation.
