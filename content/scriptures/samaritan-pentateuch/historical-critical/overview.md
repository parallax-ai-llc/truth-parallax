## Overview

Historical-critical scholars approach the Samaritan Pentateuch as a crucial witness to the editorial history of the Pentateuch. The text's distinctive harmonistic expansions — in which parallel passages from Exodus and Deuteronomy are supplemented with material from each other — illuminate the scribal processes by which the Pentateuch was compiled and edited.

The Samaritan Pentateuch's relationship to the Documentary Hypothesis is complex. Some scholars argue that Samaritan variants preserve pre-redactional readings that support the identification of distinct source documents (J, E, D, P), while others contend that the Samaritan expansions represent a secondary stage of editorial harmonization. The discovery of proto-Samaritan texts among the Dead Sea Scrolls has added new complexity to these debates.
