## Historical-Critical Reading

Historical-critical scholars value the Samaritan Leviticus for its witness to the Priestly source and the history of Israelite cult. The relative agreement between Samaritan and Masoretic texts of Leviticus suggests that the Priestly legislation was substantially complete and widely accepted before the Samaritan-Jewish split, supporting a pre-exilic or early post-exilic dating for much of P.

The few significant variants — particularly in the festival calendar and purity regulations — may preserve alternative priestly traditions that coexisted in the Second Temple period. Historical-critical scholars use these variants to reconstruct the diversity of priestly practice before the standardization imposed by the Second Temple establishment in Jerusalem.
