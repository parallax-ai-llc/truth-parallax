## Historical-Critical Reading

Historical-critical scholars examine the Samaritan Genesis for evidence of the Pentateuch's editorial history. The text's harmonistic expansions — in which parallel accounts are supplemented with material from elsewhere in the Pentateuch — illuminate the scribal techniques used in the compilation and transmission of the Torah.

Source critics have noted that some Samaritan variants may preserve pre-redactional readings that support the identification of distinct documentary sources. The Samaritan Genesis's relationship to the proto-Samaritan texts found among the Dead Sea Scrolls suggests that the harmonistic tendency was already present in pre-Samaritan manuscripts of the second century BCE, indicating that this editorial approach predates the Samaritan-Jewish split and reflects a broader scribal culture.
