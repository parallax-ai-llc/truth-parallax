## The Feast on the Mountain

Isaiah 25 contains one of the Old Testament's most luminous visions of salvation. "On this mountain the LORD Almighty will prepare a feast of rich food for all peoples, a banquet of aged wine" (25:6). Protestant interpreters see this as a pivotal eschatological promise: salvation is depicted not as austere spirituality but as abundant feasting — a theme Jesus develops in His parables of the wedding banquet and the great supper (Matthew 22; Luke 14).

## Death Swallowed Up

"He will swallow up death forever. The Sovereign LORD will wipe away the tears from all faces" (25:8). This is the Old Testament's most explicit promise of the abolition of death, directly quoted by Paul in 1 Corinthians 15:54 as fulfilled in Christ's resurrection. Protestant theology regards this verse as the hinge between the Old Testament's shadowed hope of life beyond death and the New Testament's confident proclamation of resurrection. The personal tenderness of God wiping tears from faces anticipates Revelation 21:4.

## The Prayer of the Redeemed

"Surely this is our God; we trusted in him, and he saved us. This is the LORD, we trusted in him; let us rejoice and be glad in his salvation" (25:9). Protestant interpreters hear in this confession the voice of the church triumphant — all those who trusted God through the darkness of judgment now vindicated in the light of redemption. The repetition of "we trusted" grounds eschatological joy in present faith.
