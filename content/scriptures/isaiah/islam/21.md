## The Burden of Babylon

Isaiah 21 contains oracles against Babylon, Edom, and Arabia. Islamic engagement with these oracles is particularly notable regarding the Arabian oracle (21:13-17), which some Muslim scholars have read as referencing the Arabian Peninsula's future significance in sacred history.

## The Watchman

The image of the watchman stationed to report what he sees (21:6-8) resonates with the Islamic concept of *da'wah* — the duty to watch, observe, and communicate truth. The Prophet Muhammad was described as a *nadhir* (warner) and *bashir* (bearer of good news) — functions that correspond to the prophetic watchman of Isaiah.

## "Babylon Has Fallen"

"Babylon has fallen, has fallen! All the images of its gods lie shattered on the ground!" (21:9). Islamic theology celebrates the destruction of idolatry as the triumph of *tawhid*. The Prophet Muhammad's destruction of the idols in the Ka'bah echoes this prophetic pattern: the shattering of false gods before the proclamation of the One True God.

## The Arabian Oracle

"A prophecy against Arabia" (21:13). Muslim scholars have paid particular attention to this oracle, noting that the reference to the people of Tema (21:14) and the warriors of Kedar (21:16-17) directly concerns Arabian peoples. Some interpret this in light of the pre-Islamic Arabian context, seeing the oracle as anticipating the transformation of Arabia through Islam.
