## The Strong City of Trust

Isaiah 26 sings of a city whose walls are salvation (26:1). Islamic theology sees the community of believers (*ummah*) as God's protected city — not a physical fortress but a spiritual community sheltered by divine mercy. The Quran affirms: "Allah defends those who believe" (Al-Hajj 22:38).

## Perfect Peace Through Trust

"You will keep in perfect peace those whose minds are steadfast" (26:3). The Islamic concept of *tawakkul* (trust in God) produces *sakinah* (divine tranquility) — the peace that descends upon the hearts of believers. The Quran describes this: "It is He who sent down tranquility into the hearts of the believers" (Al-Fath 48:4). Isaiah's "perfect peace" corresponds to the Islamic experience of *sakinah*.

## The Resurrection of the Dead

"Your dead will live, LORD; their bodies will rise" (26:19). Islamic belief in bodily resurrection (*ba'th*) is one of its most fundamental doctrines. The Quran declares: "Does man think that We will not assemble his bones? Yes, We are able to proportion even his fingertips" (Al-Qiyamah 75:3-4). Isaiah's resurrection promise aligns with the Quranic insistence on physical resurrection.

## Shelter Until the Wrath Passes

"Enter your rooms and shut the doors behind you" (26:20). Islamic tradition includes supplications (*du'a*) for protection during times of tribulation, and the concept of *isti'adhah* (seeking refuge in God) is a fundamental practice. The faithful shelter in God's protection as Isaiah describes.
