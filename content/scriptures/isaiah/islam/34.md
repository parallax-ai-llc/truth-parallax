The Islamic reading of Isaiah 34 focuses on its eschatological dimensions, finding strong parallels with the Quran's descriptions of the Day of Judgment (Yawm al-Qiyamah). The cosmic upheaval described in verses 1-4 -- stars falling, heavens rolling up -- echoes Quran 81:1-6 (At-Takwir): "When the sun is wrapped up, when the stars fall, when the mountains are removed." Muslim commentators see in these biblical passages confirmation that the original revelation given to the Israelite prophets contained the same eschatological warnings that the Quran preserves in their uncorrupted form.

The judgment on Edom (34:5-17) is read through the principle of divine justice (adl) that the Quran emphasizes throughout. Allah's judgment falls on those who oppress and reject His messengers -- a pattern repeated from Ad and Thamud (Quran 7:65-79) through Pharaoh to the nations Isaiah addresses. Al-Tabari notes that the biblical prophets, like the Quranic messengers, warned their people of specific consequences for specific sins, and the fulfillment of these warnings demonstrates the truthfulness of the prophetic message.

The ecological devastation described -- land becoming burning pitch, overrun by wild creatures -- resonates with Quranic descriptions of destroyed civilizations. The Quran repeatedly invites readers to "travel through the land and observe how was the end of those who denied" (Quran 3:137). Islamic commentators see Isaiah 34 as one such testimony to divine judgment in history, confirming the Quranic pattern while acknowledging that the present biblical text may contain elements of tahrif (textual alteration).
