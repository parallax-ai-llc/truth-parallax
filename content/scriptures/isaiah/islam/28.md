## The Woe Against Drunkenness

Isaiah 28 opens with a condemnation of drunkenness. The Quran's progressive prohibition of intoxicants culminates in: "O you who believe, indeed intoxicants, gambling, stone altars, and divining arrows are but defilement from the work of Satan, so avoid them" (Al-Ma'idah 5:90). Isaiah's prophetic critique of drunkenness is fully consistent with the Quranic prohibition.

## The Sure Foundation

"See, I lay a stone in Zion, a tested stone, a precious cornerstone" (28:16). Islamic theology sees the prophetic tradition as the sure foundation for human life. Each prophet lays a stone in the edifice of divine guidance, with Muhammad as the final stone — the "seal of the prophets" (*khatam al-nabiyyin*) who completes the structure. The Quran uses the building metaphor: "Muhammad is not the father of any of your men, but the Messenger of Allah and the seal of the prophets" (Al-Ahzab 33:40).

## Divine Wisdom in Varied Methods

The farmer parable (28:23-29) illustrates God's wisdom in applying different methods to different situations. Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) recognizes this principle through the concept of *maslahah* (public interest) — the adaptation of divine guidance to varying circumstances while maintaining core principles. God's wisdom, as Isaiah describes, is both wonderful and practical.
