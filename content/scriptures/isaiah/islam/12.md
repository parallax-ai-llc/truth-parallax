## Songs of Praise and Gratitude

Isaiah 12 contains psalms of thanksgiving that express the proper human response to divine salvation. Islamic theology places *shukr* (gratitude) among the highest virtues. The Quran declares: "If you are grateful, I will surely increase My blessings upon you" (Ibrahim 14:7). Isaiah's songs model the posture of gratitude that Islam identifies as essential to the believing life.

## Trust and Not Fear

"I will trust and not be afraid" (12:2). The Islamic concept of *tawakkul* (trust in God) is one of the defining characteristics of the believer (*mu'min*). The Quran teaches: "In Allah let the believers put their trust" (Al Imran 3:160). Isaiah's declaration of trust aligns with the Quranic instruction to rely on God rather than on worldly securities.

## Making Known God's Deeds

"Make known among the nations what he has done" (12:4). Islamic *da'wah* (invitation to God) echoes this prophetic call to proclaim God's greatness among all peoples. The universality of the prophetic message — addressed to all nations, not just one people — is a principle shared by Isaiah and the Quran.
