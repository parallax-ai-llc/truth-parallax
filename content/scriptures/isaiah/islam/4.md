## The Branch of the Lord

Isaiah 4:2 speaks of "the Branch of the LORD" that will bring beauty and glory. Islamic interpretation reads this through the lens of prophetic succession: each prophet is a "branch" from the tree of divine guidance, culminating in Muhammad. While the specific identification varies among Muslim scholars, the theme of divine renewal after judgment is consistent with the Quranic pattern of prophets sent after periods of corruption.

## Purification Through Judgment

The vision of a purified remnant washed "by a spirit of judgment and a spirit of fire" (4:4) resonates with the Islamic concept of divine testing (*ibtila'*). The Quran teaches: "Do people think that they will be left alone because they say, 'We believe,' and not be tested?" (Al-Ankabut 29:2). Purification through trial is a consistent theme in both prophetic traditions.

## The Divine Canopy

The cloud and fire over Zion (4:5) recall the Quranic descriptions of divine protection: God shelters the righteous under His mercy. The concept of God as protector (*al-Hafiz*) — shielding the faithful through trials — is affirmed throughout the Quran and echoed in Isaiah's vision of the divine canopy over the purified community.
