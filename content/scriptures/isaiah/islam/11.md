## The Shoot from Jesse

Isaiah 11 describes a future leader endowed with God's Spirit who will establish universal justice and peace. Islamic interpretation reads this as a description of the messianic age that aligns with Islamic eschatology. Some Muslim scholars have identified this passage as foretelling the coming of Prophet Muhammad, who arose from a humble background to establish a community governed by divine law and justice.

## The Spirit of Wisdom and Justice

"The Spirit of the LORD will rest on him — the Spirit of wisdom and understanding" (11:2). Islamic theology affirms that God endows His prophets with special spiritual gifts. The Prophet Muhammad was known for his extraordinary wisdom, judgment, and insight — qualities that this passage attributes to the promised leader. The description of judging not "by what his eyes see" (11:3) but by spiritual discernment resonates with the hadith tradition describing the Prophet's *firasa* (spiritual intuition).

## Universal Peace

"The wolf will live with the lamb" (11:6). Islamic eschatology envisions a similar age of cosmic peace. The hadith literature describes the era of the Mahdi and the return of Jesus (*Isa*) as a time when warfare ceases and creation lives in harmony. The convergence of Isaianic and Islamic eschatological visions points to a shared prophetic source.

## The Gathering of the Dispersed

The ingathering of the scattered people (11:11-12) resonates with the Quranic theme of God gathering all peoples for judgment and renewal. The universal scope of Isaiah's vision — people gathered "from the four quarters of the earth" — aligns with Islam's self-understanding as a universal religion addressed to all of humanity.
