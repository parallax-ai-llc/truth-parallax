## The Prophet's Warning

Isaiah 1 opens with a divine indictment against Judah — a pattern that resonates deeply with Quranic narratives of prophets sent to warn their peoples. Islamic tradition recognizes Isaiah (*Sha'ya*) as one of the prophets of Bani Isra'il who called his people to repentance and monotheism. The chapter's central message — that God rejects worship unaccompanied by justice — aligns perfectly with the Quranic principle that faith (*iman*) must be inseparable from righteous deeds (*'amal salih*).

## Ritual Without Righteousness

"I have no pleasure in the blood of bulls and lambs and goats" (1:11). Islamic theology affirms this prophetic critique. The Quran declares: "It is not their meat nor their blood that reaches Allah: it is your piety that reaches Him" (Al-Hajj 22:37). The prophetic tradition from Isaiah through Muhammad consistently teaches that outward observance without inner sincerity and social justice is worthless before God.

## The Call to Repentance

"Come now, let us reason together" (1:18). Islamic theology of *tawbah* (repentance) resonates with Isaiah's offer of cleansing. The Quran affirms that God is *al-Tawwab* (the Oft-Returning, the Acceptor of Repentance) who welcomes the repentant regardless of the gravity of their sins. The prophetic message across all dispensations is consistent: sincere repentance is always accepted.
