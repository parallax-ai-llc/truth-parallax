## The Defeat of the Serpent

"In that day the LORD will punish Leviathan" (27:1). Islamic eschatology includes the final defeat of *Iblis* (Satan) and all forces of evil. The Quran promises that on the Day of Judgment, Satan will be exposed and punished: "And Satan will say when the matter has been decided, 'Indeed, Allah had promised you the promise of truth'" (Ibrahim 14:22). Isaiah's vision of Leviathan's destruction aligns with the Islamic expectation of evil's final defeat.

## The Restored Garden

The vineyard tended by God (27:2-3) resonates with the Quranic descriptions of Paradise as a garden (*jannah*) cultivated by divine care. The image of God watering and guarding His vineyard parallels the Quran's promise: "Gardens of Eden, beneath which rivers flow, wherein they will abide eternally" (Al-Bayyinah 98:8).

## The Gathering Trumpet

"In that day a great trumpet will sound" (27:13). Islamic eschatology describes the trumpet (*sur*) blown by the angel Israfil at the end of time: "And the trumpet will be blown, and whoever is in the heavens and whoever is on the earth will fall dead... then it will be blown again, and at once they will be standing, looking on" (Al-Zumar 39:68). The Isaianic trumpet and the Islamic *sur* serve the same eschatological function.
