## The Sign of Trust

Isaiah 7 presents a crisis of faith: will Ahaz trust God's promise or seek human alliances? Islamic theology identifies *tawakkul* (trust in God) as one of the highest virtues. The Quran declares: "And whoever puts their trust in Allah — He will be sufficient for them" (Al-Talaq 65:3). Ahaz's failure to trust God exemplifies the sin of relying on created things rather than the Creator.

## The Immanuel Sign

"The *almah* will conceive and give birth to a son" (7:14). Islamic interpretation does not accept the Christian doctrine of the virgin birth of a divine being. While Islam affirms the virgin birth of Jesus (*Isa*) — "She said, 'How can I have a son when no man has touched me?'" (Maryam 19:20) — Islamic theology insists that Jesus was a created prophet, not God incarnate. The Isaiah passage is read as referring to a sign for Ahaz's own time.

## The Consequences of Misplaced Trust

The chapter's warning that Assyria will devastate the land illustrates the Quranic principle that those who seek protection from other than God will be disappointed. "Do they seek honor through them? Indeed, honor belongs entirely to Allah" (Al-Nisa 4:139). The prophetic message is consistent: security comes from God alone, and trusting in human powers leads to destruction.
