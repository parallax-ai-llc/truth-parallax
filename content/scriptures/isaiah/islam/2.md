## The Mountain of God

Isaiah 2:2-4 envisions the nations streaming to God's holy mountain. Islamic eschatology parallels this vision: the Quran describes the Day of Judgment as a time when all peoples will be gathered before God (Al-Kahf 18:47-48). The concept of universal divine judgment and the establishment of justice is central to both Isaianic prophecy and Quranic teaching.

## Swords into Plowshares

"They will beat their swords into plowshares" (2:4). Islamic tradition values peace (*salam*) as both a divine attribute and the ultimate goal of human society. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Spread peace among yourselves" (Sahih Muslim). Isaiah's vision of universal peace accords with the Islamic understanding that true submission to God (*islam*) produces harmony among peoples.

## The Judgment of Pride

The "day of the LORD" against all that is proud and exalted (2:12-17) resonates with the Quranic condemnation of *kibr* (arrogance). The Prophet Muhammad said: "No one who has an atom's weight of arrogance in his heart will enter Paradise" (Sahih Muslim). Isaiah's prophetic denunciation of pride aligns with the consistent Islamic teaching that arrogance is the fundamental sin that separates creatures from their Creator.
