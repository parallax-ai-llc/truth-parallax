## Seeking Refuge

Isaiah 16 envisions Moab seeking shelter under Judah's protection. Islamic theology affirms that true refuge is found only in God: "Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind'" (Al-Nas 114:1). While human compassion and hospitality are commanded, ultimate security comes from God alone.

## The Destructiveness of Pride

"We have heard of Moab's pride" (16:6). Islamic moral theology identifies *kibr* (arrogance) as the sin that prevents entry into Paradise. The Quran warns: "Do not turn your cheek in contempt toward people and do not walk through the earth exultantly. Indeed, Allah does not like everyone self-deluded and boastful" (Luqman 31:18). Isaiah's diagnosis of Moab's pride aligns with the Quranic analysis of the root cause of human destruction.

## Prophetic Grief

Isaiah's weeping over Moab (16:9) models the compassion that the Prophet Muhammad also displayed. When visiting a Jewish boy who was ill, when mourning the death of his son Ibrahim, and when weeping over the suffering of his community, the Prophet demonstrated that prophetic authority is inseparable from prophetic tenderness.
