## The Prophet's Body as Warning

Isaiah 20 records Isaiah's three-year sign-act of walking "stripped and barefoot." Islamic tradition recognizes that prophets sometimes performed dramatic acts to communicate divine messages. The Quran records various ways God communicated through His prophets — dreams, visions, angelic messengers, and direct speech. The prophetic sign-act represents a form of embodied revelation.

## The Humiliation of False Protectors

The sign demonstrates that Egypt and Cush will be humiliated by Assyria. Islamic theology teaches that all who set themselves up as alternatives to God will eventually be exposed: "And those who take protectors besides Him — 'We only worship them that they may bring us nearer to Allah' — Allah will judge between them concerning that over which they differ" (Al-Zumar 39:3).

## The Futility of Worldly Reliance

"How then can we escape?" (20:6). Islamic theology answers this question definitively: escape is found only through submission to God (*islam*). No human power, alliance, or strategy can substitute for divine protection. The Prophet Muhammad taught: "Know that if the whole world were to gather together to benefit you, they could not benefit you except with something that Allah had already prescribed for you" (Jami' al-Tirmidhi).
