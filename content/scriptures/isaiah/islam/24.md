## The Day of Cosmic Judgment

Isaiah 24 presents a vision of universal judgment that strongly parallels Quranic descriptions of *Yawm al-Qiyamah* (the Day of Resurrection). "The LORD is going to lay waste the earth" (24:1) echoes the Quran's depiction of cosmic upheaval: "When the earth is shaken with its final earthquake and the earth discharges its burdens" (Al-Zalzalah 99:1-2). Both traditions envision a comprehensive divine judgment that encompasses all peoples and the entire created order.

## The Broken Covenant

"The earth is defiled by its people; they have broken the everlasting covenant" (24:5). Islamic theology identifies the primordial covenant (*mithaq*) — God's covenant with all humanity before creation (Al-A'raf 7:172) — as the foundation of moral obligation. When humanity violates this covenant through *shirk* (polytheism) and oppression, the consequences extend to the earth itself.

## The Remnant's Praise

Even amid devastation, praise is heard from the ends of the earth (24:16). Islamic theology affirms that *dhikr* (remembrance of God) persists through every trial. The faithful who maintain their worship through catastrophe exemplify the Quranic principle: "Those who remember Allah standing, sitting, and lying on their sides" (Al Imran 3:191).
