## God's Sovereignty Over Nations

Isaiah 10 declares that Assyria is "the rod of my anger" (10:5) — a pagan empire serving God's purposes unknowingly. Islamic theology strongly affirms this principle of divine sovereignty (*qadr*): God governs all nations and events, and nothing occurs outside His will. The Quran states: "To Allah belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth, and Allah has power over all things" (Al Imran 3:189).

## The Arrogance of Power

The Assyrian king's boast — "By the strength of my hand I have done this" (10:13) — exemplifies the *kibr* (arrogance) that Islam identifies as the root of all sin. It was arrogance that caused Iblis (Satan) to refuse God's command to bow before Adam (Al-Baqarah 2:34). The prophetic critique of imperial pride is consistent with the Quranic condemnation of Pharaoh, Nimrod, and every ruler who claims autonomous power.

## The Axe Cannot Boast

"Does the axe raise itself above the person who swings it?" (10:15). Islamic theology uses similar analogies to affirm God's absolute sovereignty and to expose the absurdity of creaturely pride. The creature exists and acts only by God's sustaining will — a principle affirmed by the Ash'ari theological tradition that all events are directly sustained by God's creative power.

## The Righteous Remnant

The promise that "a remnant will return" (10:21) resonates with the Quranic concept of the faithful community (*ummah*) that persists through every trial. God always preserves a group of believers who maintain the truth — "There will always be a group from my ummah who are manifest upon the truth" (hadith, Sahih Muslim).
