## The Sign Beyond the Demiurge

Isaiah 7 presents the sign of Immanuel — "God with us." Gnostic Christology reads this as the announcement of divine descent: the true God, who has been absent from the material cosmos, sends a representative into the Demiurge's domain. The "sign" is not a political prediction but a signal that the transcendent divine is breaking into the archonic world.

## The Mystery of the Virgin Birth

"The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son" (7:14). Valentinian Gnosticism developed an elaborate theology of spiritual birth: the divine Revealer enters the world not through ordinary generation but through a process that bypasses the Demiurge's mechanisms of reproduction. The virgin birth represents a mode of entry into the material world that is not under archonic control.

## Ahaz's Refusal of Knowledge

Ahaz's refusal to ask for a sign (7:12) is read by Gnostic interpreters as a refusal of *gnosis* — the choice to remain in comfortable ignorance rather than face the challenging truth. This refusal is the fundamental sin in Gnostic anthropology: not moral transgression but the willful rejection of knowledge when it is offered.
