## The Light of Gnosis

"The people walking in darkness have seen a great light" (9:2). Gnostic soteriology identifies this light as *gnosis* itself — the salvific knowledge that illuminates the darkness of material existence. The metaphysics of light and darkness is central to Gnostic thought: the material world is darkness, and the divine Pleroma is light. Isaiah's language maps directly onto this fundamental Gnostic cosmology.

## The Names of the Revealer

"Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace" (9:6). Gnostic Christology reads these as names of the divine Revealer who descends from the Pleroma. Each name reveals an aspect of the transcendent God that the Demiurge lacks: true counsel (not the Demiurge's deceptive schemes), genuine power (not the Demiurge's borrowed authority), eternal being (not the Demiurge's contingent existence), and authentic peace (not the Demiurge's enforced order).

## The Hidden King

"Of the increase of his government and peace there will be no end" (9:7). The Gnostic "king" rules not a material kingdom but the kingdom of light — the Pleroma itself, which is infinite and eternal. The throne is not David's earthly throne but the transcendent throne glimpsed in Isaiah 6. The pneumatic beings who recognize this king enter into his limitless dominion.

## The Judgment of Ignorance

The judgment section (9:8-21) is read as describing the self-destructive nature of ignorance. Those who refuse *gnosis* do not need to be actively punished — their ignorance generates its own consequences. The outstretched hand is not primarily wrathful but revelatory: it continues to offer knowledge that the people continue to reject.
