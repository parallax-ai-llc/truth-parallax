## The City of Light

"We have a strong city; God makes salvation its walls" (26:1). Gnostic cosmology identifies this city not with any earthly location but with the Pleroma itself — the transcendent realm whose "walls" are the divine emanations (aeons) that constitute its structure. The "strong city" is the ultimate destination of the pneumatic journey.

## Peace Beyond the Archons

"You will keep in perfect peace those whose minds are steadfast" (26:3). Gnostic soteriology understands this peace as the state of the pneumatic being who has achieved *gnosis* — who knows their true identity and origin and is therefore undisturbed by the chaos of the material world. The "steadfast mind" is the *nous* (spiritual intellect) that remains fixed on the Pleroma despite the distractions of matter.

## The Rising of the Sparks

"Your dead will live; their bodies will rise" (26:19). Gnostic interpretation reads "bodies" not as physical flesh but as the spiritual bodies that the pneumatic beings will assume upon return to the Pleroma. The "dead" are those whose divine sparks have been dormant within matter — they will "rise" when *gnosis* awakens them to their true nature. This is spiritual resurrection, not the resuscitation of physical corpses.

## Hiding from the Archonic Storm

"Enter your rooms and shut the doors behind you" (26:20). Gnostic interpreters read this as instruction for the pneumatic to withdraw into the inner sanctuary — the place within the self where the divine spark burns. The "rooms" are the interior spaces of contemplation and *gnosis*, protected from the archonic "wrath" that rages in the external world.
