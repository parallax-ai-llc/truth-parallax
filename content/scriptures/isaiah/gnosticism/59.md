Isaiah 59's diagnosis -- "Your iniquities have separated you from your God" (59:2) -- is reinterpreted in Gnostic theology as describing the ontological separation between the pneumatic seed and the Pleroma caused by Sophia's fall and the archons' imprisonment. The "iniquities" are not primarily moral failures but the state of ignorance (agnoia) that results from incarnation in the archonic cosmos. The separation is real but not permanent -- it is precisely the condition that gnosis overcomes.

The corporate confession (59:9-15) is read as a description of the pneumatic community's experience of exile in the material world. "We look for light, but all is darkness" (59:9) describes the condition of those who intuit their divine origin but cannot yet perceive it clearly. The Gnostic community, marginalized and persecuted, experienced this darkness as both cosmic and social: the truth they carried was suppressed by the archonic powers and rejected by the wider society.

The divine warrior passage (59:16-20) is interpreted as the Pleroma's decision to intervene directly when the archonic system has made human self-liberation impossible. The Revealer who "put on righteousness as a breastplate" (59:17) is the pleromatic being who descends into the material world armed with gnosis. The archons cannot defeat this warrior because his armor is made of pleromatic substance, which is indestructible by material forces. The Redeemer who "comes to Zion" (59:20) is the Luminous Messenger whose arrival awakens the sleeping pneumatics and begins the process of cosmic restoration.
