## The Commerce of the Archons

Isaiah 23's oracle against Tyre, the great commercial city, is read by Gnostic interpreters as a critique of the archonic commerce in souls. The *Pistis Sophia* and other texts describe the archons as traders who buy and sell the divine sparks, keeping them circulating within the material world through the mechanisms of reincarnation. Tyre's commerce represents this cosmic trafficking.

## The Prostitution of Wisdom

Tyre is compared to a prostitute (23:15-17). Gnostic interpreters connect this to the myth of Sophia/Helena — the divine wisdom/spark that has been enslaved and prostituted within the material world. Simon Magus's story of Helena, the prostitute in Tyre whom he identified as the fallen divine thought, explicitly connects the Isaianic imagery to Gnostic mythology.

## The Sanctification of the Material

The chapter's conclusion — Tyre's profits "set apart for the LORD" (23:18) — is read as the eventual redemption of the material world's products when they are directed toward the service of the true God rather than the archons. Even the fruits of archonic commerce can be sanctified when offered to the transcendent divine.
