## The Altar-Hearth Under Siege

"Woe to you, Ariel, Ariel" (29:1). Gnostic reading sees "Ariel" (altar hearth) as the place where the divine spark burns within the material world — besieged by archonic forces but never fully extinguished. The siege of Jerusalem represents the archons' attempt to suppress the flame of *gnosis* within the human being.

## The Sealed Book of Gnosis

"The whole vision is nothing but words sealed in a scroll" (29:11). This verse is of great importance to Gnostic hermeneutics: the scriptures contain hidden *gnosis* that is "sealed" from ordinary readers. Only those who possess the spiritual key — the illumination granted by the divine Revealer — can unseal the book and read its true meaning. The *Apocryphon of John* presents itself as just such an "unsealing" of the hidden meaning of Genesis.

## Hearts Far from the True God

"These people honor me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me" (29:13). Gnostic interpreters read this as the Demiurge's own unwitting confession: the worship he receives is directed at a false image of God. The people's hearts are "far" from the Demiurge because their divine sparks instinctively sense that the true God is elsewhere — beyond the Demiurge's heaven, in the Pleroma they have forgotten.

## The Wonderful Reversal

The chapter's concluding reversal (29:17-24) is read as the eschatological reversal when the hidden becomes manifest: the true God is revealed, the Demiurge is exposed, the pneumatics awaken, and the archons lose their power. This is the *apokatastasis* — the restoration of all things to their original plerotic state.
