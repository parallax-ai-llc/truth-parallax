## The Naked Prophet

Isaiah 20's account of Isaiah walking "stripped and barefoot" for three years resonates with Gnostic themes of stripping away material attachments. The *Gospel of Thomas* (saying 37) records Jesus's instruction: "When you strip without being ashamed and take your clothes and put them under your feet like little children and trample them, then you will see the Son of the Living One and you will not be afraid." Isaiah's nakedness prefigures the Gnostic stripping of material illusion.

## Exposure of the Material

The sign's meaning — Egypt and Cush will be led away naked — represents, in Gnostic reading, the eventual exposure of all material pretensions. The powers and civilizations that seem impressive will be revealed as empty and powerless when measured against the Pleroma's reality.

## The Futility of Material Security

"How then can we escape?" (20:6). Gnostic soteriology answers: through *gnosis* alone. No political alliance, military strategy, or material arrangement can provide genuine security. Only the knowledge of one's true origin and destiny — one's identity as a divine spark temporarily imprisoned in matter — offers authentic liberation.
