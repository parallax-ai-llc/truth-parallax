Isaiah 60's vision of Zion radiant with divine light is read in Gnostic theology as a description of the Pleroma or of the pneumatic community transformed by gnosis. "Arise, shine, for your light has come" (60:1) is the call to awaken to the divine light that has always been present within the pneumatic but was obscured by archonic darkness. The light that shines is not created light (which belongs to the Demiurge's domain) but the uncreated light of the Pleroma -- the luminous reality that is the pneumatic's true home.

The nations streaming to Zion's light (60:3) is interpreted as the gathering of scattered pneumatic sparks from throughout the material cosmos. Wherever pneumatics are found -- in every nation, culture, and religion -- they are drawn by their innate affinity to the divine light. The Valentinian system described this gathering as the progressive collection of the "spiritual seed" (sperma pneumatikon) until all sparks have been recovered and the Pleroma is complete. The wealth the nations bring (60:5-7) represents the gnosis that each pneumatic community contributes to the whole.

The promise "The LORD will be your everlasting light" (60:19) describes the final condition of the restored Pleroma, where no created luminaries are needed because the divine light itself fills everything. In the Apocryphon of John, the Pleroma is described as pure light -- not the light of sun or stars but the self-luminous radiance of the divine mind. Isaiah's vision of a world where God's light replaces all created sources of illumination is, for the Gnostic reader, a vision of the Pleroma restored to its original fullness.
