## The Valley of False Vision

Isaiah 22's oracle against "the Valley of Vision" is read by Gnostic interpreters as a critique of institutional religion that claims visionary authority but lacks genuine *gnosis*. Jerusalem — the religious center of the Demiurge's worship — is a "valley" (low place) that calls itself a place of "vision" (insight). The irony exposes the fundamental Gnostic critique: the Demiurge's religion offers the appearance of knowledge while withholding its substance.

## The Key of Knowledge

The "key of the house of David" transferred to Eliakim (22:22) is read by Gnostic interpreters through Luke 11:52: "Woe to you lawyers! For you have taken away the key of knowledge." The "key" is not political authority but the key of *gnosis* — access to the hidden knowledge that unlocks the path to the Pleroma. The transfer from Shebna to Eliakim represents the passage of authentic esoteric knowledge from unworthy custodians to worthy ones.

## Eating and Drinking Before Death

"Let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die!" (22:13). Gnostic interpreters read this not merely as moral failure but as a description of the hylic condition — the purely material person who, having no awareness of the divine spark, sees life as nothing more than physical existence followed by annihilation. The person without *gnosis* is already dead in the only sense that matters.
