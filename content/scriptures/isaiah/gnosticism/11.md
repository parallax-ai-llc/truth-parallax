## The Pneumatic Shoot

"A shoot will come up from the stump of Jesse" (11:1). Gnostic Christology sees this as the descent of the divine Revealer into the material world. The "stump" represents the apparently dead material realm from which, impossibly, the living branch of the Pleroma emerges. The shoot is not a political messiah but a pneumatic being who enters the archonic domain to awaken the sleeping sparks.

## The Fullness of the Spirit

"The Spirit of the LORD will rest on him" (11:2). Gnostic pneumatology identifies this Spirit not with the Demiurge's spirit (which animates the material world) but with the Spirit of the true God — the *Pneuma* that descends from the Pleroma. The seven qualities listed represent the fullness (*pleroma*) of divine knowledge that the Revealer brings into the world of ignorance.

## The Peaceable Kingdom as Pleroma Restored

"The wolf will live with the lamb" (11:6-9). Gnostic reading sees this not as political peace or even cosmic harmony but as the restoration of the Pleroma — the state of original unity before Sophia's fall introduced division and conflict. In the Pleroma, all contradictions are reconciled, all opposites united. Isaiah's vision of predator and prey living together is a poetic description of the Plerotic state where all divine emanations exist in perfect harmony.

## The Second Gathering

The gathering from "the four quarters of the earth" (11:12) is the eschatological ingathering of the divine sparks — the final return of all pneumatic elements to the Pleroma. This gathering is the purpose of cosmic history in Gnostic theology: when all the light has been extracted from matter, the material cosmos will dissolve and the Pleroma will be restored to its original fullness.
