## The Ruin of False Knowledge

Isaiah 17's oracle against Damascus is read by Gnostic interpreters as a judgment against false knowledge — the wisdom of the world that mimics genuine *gnosis* but lacks its transformative power. Damascus, as a great ancient city, represents the accumulated "wisdom" of the archonic world — impressive in appearance but ultimately empty.

## Turning to the True Maker

"In that day people will look to their Maker" (17:7). Gnostic theology distinguishes between the "Maker" (*poietes*, the Demiurge who fashioned the material body) and the true God who implanted the divine spark. Isaiah's vision of people turning to their Maker is reread as the awakening of the pneumatic being to the true God beyond the Demiurge — recognizing that the ultimate "Maker" is not the creator of matter but the source of spirit.

## The Roar and the Silence

The nations roaring "like the roaring of great waters" before being rebuked by God (17:13) represents, in Gnostic reading, the noise of the material world that obscures the still, small voice of *gnosis*. The true God's rebuke silences the chaos, creating the inner stillness (*hesychia*) in which the pneumatic being can hear the call of the Pleroma.
