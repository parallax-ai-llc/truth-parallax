Isaiah 51's call to "look to the rock from which you were cut" (51:1) is interpreted in Gnostic theology as a call to remember one's pleromatic origin. The "rock" is the divine substance from which the pneumatic seed was "quarried" -- the luminous reality of the Pleroma that is the pneumatic's true home. The forgetfulness (lethe) that the archonic world imposes makes this remembering (anamnesis) essential. The Hymn of the Pearl narrates exactly this recovery of memory: the prince remembers who he is and where he came from, breaking the spell of Egypt (the material world).

The creation-combat imagery (51:9) -- "Was it not you who cut Rahab to pieces?" -- is given a distinctive reading. In Gnostic cosmology, the "sea monster" is not merely a mythological creature but a representation of the archonic powers that govern the material ocean (matter, hyle). The divine act of piercing the monster represents the Pleroma's ongoing struggle against the archonic forces that maintain the material cosmos. Every act of gnosis is a blow struck against these powers.

The "cup of wrath" (51:17, 22) is reinterpreted as the "cup of forgetfulness" that the archons administer to incarnating souls. In the Apocryphon of John, the archons give Adam a "drink of forgetfulness" so that he will not remember his divine origin. When God takes this cup from Jerusalem's hand (51:22), it represents the Revealer's act of breaking the spell of forgetfulness and restoring the pneumatic's awareness of the Pleroma. Gnosis is, at its core, the reversal of the archonic intoxication that keeps souls trapped in material existence.
