## The Heavenly Ascent

Isaiah 6 is the single most important chapter for Gnostic interpretation of Isaiah, because it provides the template for the *Ascension of Isaiah* — one of the most significant Gnostic-influenced texts. The prophet's vision of God enthroned in the Temple is read as an ascent through the heavenly spheres, each governed by archons, to a vision of the true divine reality beyond the Demiurge's heaven.

## The Seraphim and the Archons

The seraphim who cry "Holy, holy, holy" (6:3) are read by different Gnostic schools in different ways. Some identify them as genuine servants of the transcendent God who have penetrated the Demiurge's realm; others see them as high-ranking archons whose worship, though sincere, is directed at a deity who is not the ultimate God. The *Ascension of Isaiah* describes Isaiah encountering angelic beings at each level of heaven, with only the highest level revealing the true God.

## The Burning Coal and Spiritual Transformation

The coal from the altar (6:6-7) is interpreted not as forgiveness of sin but as the ignition of the divine spark within the prophet. The fire of *gnosis* burns away the material dross that encases the pneumatic seed, enabling the prophet to perceive what ordinary consciousness cannot. This transformative encounter is the model for all Gnostic initiation.

## The Paradoxical Commission

"Make the heart of this people calloused" (6:10). Gnostic interpreters read this commission as confirming the divide between the pneumatic few who can receive *gnosis* and the hylic/psychic majority who cannot. The prophet is not causing blindness but acknowledging a pre-existing condition: most people are not equipped to receive the highest knowledge. The "holy seed" (6:13) is the pneumatic remnant that preserves *gnosis* through the ages.
