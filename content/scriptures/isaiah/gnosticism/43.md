Isaiah 43's declaration "I have called you by name, you are mine" (43:1) is read in Gnostic theology as the true God's address to the pneumatic seed, claiming ownership of what the Demiurge mistakenly believes belongs to him. In Gnostic cosmology, the divine spark within the human being was smuggled into the archonic creation against the Demiurge's will. When the true God says "you are mine," He is asserting His prior claim to the pneumatic element that the archons cannot permanently possess.

The "new thing" of verse 19 is interpreted as the irruption of gnosis into the material order -- an event that the Demiurge did not plan and cannot comprehend. The Apocryphon of John describes how Epinoia (Afterthought), a pleromatic being, descended into the material world and "raised up" the knowledge that the archons had suppressed. This "new thing" is not a modification of the existing order but a disruption of it -- the introduction of a qualitatively different reality that the archonic system was designed to prevent.

The divine self-identification "I, even I, am he who blots out your transgressions" (43:25) is read through the Gnostic understanding of sin as ignorance rather than moral transgression. What needs to be "blotted out" is not a legal record of offenses but the veil of forgetfulness that prevents the pneumatic from recognizing its true nature. Forgiveness, in the Gnostic sense, is the removal of agnoia (ignorance) through the bestowal of gnosis (knowledge). The Gospel of Truth describes error (plane) dissolving like a dream when the sleeper awakens.
