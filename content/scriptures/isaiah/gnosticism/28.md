## The Intoxication of Ignorance

Isaiah 28's condemnation of Ephraim's drunkenness is read by Gnostic interpreters as a description of the state of spiritual intoxication — the forgetfulness (*lethe*) that afflicts the divine spark in the material world. The *Gospel of Truth* describes the human condition as a "nightmare" from which the pneumatic must awaken. Ephraim's drunken priests and prophets represent religious leaders who are themselves trapped in archonic ignorance.

## The Stone of Gnosis

"See, I lay a stone in Zion, a tested stone, a precious cornerstone" (28:16). Gnostic Christology identifies this stone with the divine Revealer — the one sent from the Pleroma to serve as the foundation of authentic *gnosis*. The "tested stone" has been proven in the fires of descent through the archonic realms. Those who build upon this foundation — who receive the Revealer's teaching — will not be shaken.

## The Covenant with Death Unmasked

"Your covenant with death will be annulled" (28:18). Gnostic soteriology reads the "covenant with death" as the archonic compact that binds the soul to the cycle of reincarnation and material existence. The coming of *gnosis* annuls this compact, freeing the pneumatic from the Demiurge's jurisdiction. The "lie" and "falsehood" in which the people have taken refuge (28:15) are the Demiurge's religious system itself.

## The Farmer Who Knows

The farmer parable (28:23-29) is read as an allegory of the true God's wisdom: unlike the Demiurge, who creates clumsily and ignorantly, the transcendent God knows exactly how to cultivate each soul, applying the precise measure of knowledge and experience needed to liberate the spark from its material husk.
