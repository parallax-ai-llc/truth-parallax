## The Branch of the Lord

"The Branch of the LORD will be beautiful and glorious" (4:2). Jewish messianic interpretation connects the Branch (*tsemach*) to the Davidic line but emphasizes the communal dimension: the "fruit of the land" will be the pride of "the survivors of Israel." Rashi reads the "branch" as referring to the righteous remnant rather than a single messianic figure. The Targum Jonathan, however, renders this as "the Messiah of the LORD."

## The Purified Remnant

Those who survive judgment are called "holy" (4:3). Jewish theology understands holiness (*kedushah*) as separation for divine purpose rather than moral perfection. The concept of the remnant (*she'erit*) is central to Jewish self-understanding through centuries of persecution: even when the majority is lost, a faithful core is preserved to continue the covenant relationship.

## The Cloud and Fire

The imagery of cloud and fire over Zion (4:5) recalls the Exodus and anticipates the messianic restoration. Rashi sees this as a promise that God will once again manifest His presence visibly among His people. The Talmud (Bava Batra 75a) describes the messianic age as a time when the divine presence (*Shekhinah*) will be directly experienced by all, not just by prophets and holy men.
