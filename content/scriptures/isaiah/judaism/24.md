## The Lord Devastates the Earth

Isaiah 24 marks a shift from national oracles to cosmic vision. "The LORD is going to lay waste the earth" (24:1). Jewish eschatological interpretation reads this as describing the transformation that precedes the messianic age. The Talmud (Sanhedrin 97a) records debates about whether this transformation will be catastrophic or gradual, but agrees that the present world order must be fundamentally altered before the messianic kingdom can emerge.

## The Broken Everlasting Covenant

"The earth is defiled by its people; they have broken the everlasting covenant" (24:5). Rashi identifies this as the Noahide covenant — the universal moral law given to all humanity after the Flood. Jewish theology teaches that all human beings are bound by the seven Noahide laws, and that the violation of these laws has consequences for the entire earth.

## Songs from the Ends of the Earth

"From the ends of the earth we hear singing: Glory to the Righteous One!" (24:16). Jewish interpretation sees this as the response of the righteous among all nations when God's justice is finally made manifest. The passage supports the Jewish conviction that the messianic age will bring universal recognition of God — not through conversion to Judaism but through acknowledgment of God's sovereignty by all peoples.
