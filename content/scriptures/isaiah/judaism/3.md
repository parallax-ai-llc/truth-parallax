## The Removal of Leaders

Isaiah 3 describes God's removal of competent leadership as punishment. Rashi identifies the specific types of leaders removed — judges, prophets, elders, military commanders — as corresponding to the pillars of Jewish communal life. The Talmud (Chagigah 14a) uses this passage to discuss the qualities of authentic Torah leadership and the consequences of their absence.

## The Oppression of the Poor

"The LORD enters into judgment against the elders and leaders of his people" (3:14). Jewish social ethics draws heavily on this passage. The prophetic insistence that leaders are accountable for the condition of the poor is foundational to the Jewish concept of *tzedakah* (justice/charity) as obligation rather than voluntary generosity. The Talmud (Shabbat 54b) teaches that one who has the ability to protest against wrongdoing and fails to do so is held accountable for that wrongdoing.

## The Daughters of Zion

Rabbinic commentary on 3:16-26 sees the detailed description of women's ornaments as a moral parable. The Talmud (Shabbat 62b) provides an elaborate interpretation of each ornament, associating them with specific sins. The midrashic reading is not anti-feminine but uses the daughters of Zion as representative of the entire nation's pursuit of superficiality over substance.
