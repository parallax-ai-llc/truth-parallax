## The Feast on God's Mountain

"On this mountain the LORD of hosts will make for all peoples a feast of rich food" (25:6). Jewish eschatological interpretation reads this as describing the great banquet of the messianic age. The Talmud (Bava Batra 75a) elaborates this vision with vivid detail: God will prepare a feast of Leviathan's flesh and aged wine preserved since the six days of creation. The feast is "for all peoples" — reflecting the universal scope of messianic salvation.

## The Shroud Destroyed

"He will destroy on this mountain the shroud that enfolds all peoples, the sheet that covers all nations" (25:7). Rashi reads the "shroud" as the veil of ignorance and idolatry that prevents the nations from knowing God. In the messianic age, this veil will be removed, and all peoples will recognize the God of Israel. The passage supports the Jewish conviction that universal enlightenment — not universal conversion to Judaism — characterizes the messianic age.

## Death Swallowed Up

"He will swallow up death forever" (25:8). Jewish interpretation of this verse varies. Some commentators (Radak, Abarbanel) read it as promising the literal abolition of death in the messianic age or the world to come. Others interpret it more modestly as the end of premature death or of the fear of death. The Talmud (Moed Katan 25b) uses this verse to comfort mourners with the promise of future reunion.
