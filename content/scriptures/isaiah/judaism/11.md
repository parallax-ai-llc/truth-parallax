## The Shoot from Jesse's Stump

"A shoot will come up from the stump of Jesse" (11:1). Jewish messianic interpretation reads this as a description of the future Messiah who will arise from the Davidic line. The Rambam (Maimonides) includes this passage in his discussion of the Messiah's characteristics (*Hilkhot Melakhim* 11:1-4): the Messiah will be a human king from David's line who restores the Davidic kingdom, gathers the exiles, and rebuilds the Temple. The mention of Jesse (rather than David) suggests that the dynasty will have been reduced to obscurity before the Messiah emerges.

## The Spirit of the Lord

"The Spirit of the LORD will rest on him" (11:2). Jewish interpretation sees the six qualities listed — wisdom, understanding, counsel, might, knowledge, and fear of the LORD — as describing the ideal qualities of the Messiah as a righteous judge and king. The Talmud (Sanhedrin 93b) states that the Messiah will judge "by smell" — by intuitive spiritual discernment — rather than by external evidence, based on 11:3: "He shall not judge by what his eyes see."

## The Peaceable Kingdom

"The wolf will live with the lamb" (11:6-9). Jewish messianic theology takes this vision of cosmic peace seriously as a description of the world to come. The Rambam interprets this allegorically — the "wolf" and "lamb" represent nations that currently prey upon Israel living together in peace. Abarbanel, however, takes the passage literally: the messianic age will bring a genuine transformation of nature in which predation ceases.

## The Ingathering of Exiles

"He will assemble the scattered people of Judah from the four quarters of the earth" (11:12). The ingathering of exiles (*kibbutz galuyot*) is a central tenet of Jewish messianic belief. This passage, along with Deuteronomy 30:3-5, forms the scriptural basis for the prayer for the ingathering in the daily *Amidah*. The return to the land of Israel remains a defining feature of Jewish eschatological hope.
