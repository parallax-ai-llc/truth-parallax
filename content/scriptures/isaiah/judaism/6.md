## The Throne Vision and the Kedushah

Isaiah 6 provides the foundational text for the *Kedushah*, the central prayer of the *Amidah* in which the congregation joins the angelic praise: "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; the whole earth is full of His glory" (6:3). The Talmud (Chullin 91b) debates whether Israel or the angels are more beloved, with one opinion holding that the angels cannot sing the *Kedushah* until Israel initiates it below. This passage anchors the Jewish conviction that human worship is cosmically significant.

## Unclean Lips and Prophetic Humility

Isaiah's confession — "I am a man of unclean lips, and I live among a people of unclean lips" (6:5) — is understood by Rashi as a specific sin: Isaiah included the people in his confession because a prophet should not speak against Israel. The Talmud (Yevamot 49b) records a tradition that Isaiah was punished for calling Israel "a people of unclean lips." Prophetic authority does not exempt the prophet from solidarity with his people.

## The Hardening Commission

"Make the heart of this people calloused; make their ears dull and close their eyes" (6:10). Jewish interpretation generally reads this not as God causing unbelief but as Isaiah being warned that the people will not respond — the hardening is a description of what will happen, not a command to make it happen. Radak explains that the prophet's message will have the effect of hardening those who are already resistant, because exposure to truth deepens the guilt of those who refuse it.

## The Holy Seed

"The holy seed is its stump" (6:13). Rashi identifies the "holy seed" with the righteous remnant who survive judgment. The image of the stump that retains life has sustained Jewish hope through centuries of persecution: the people may be cut down, but the root survives and will produce new growth. This verse is a foundational text for the Jewish theology of survival.
