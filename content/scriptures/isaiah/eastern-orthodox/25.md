## The Messianic Banquet

"On this mountain the LORD Almighty will prepare a feast of rich food for all peoples" (25:6). Orthodox Eucharistic theology reads this as the definitive Old Testament anticipation of the Divine Liturgy. The feast on God's holy mountain is the heavenly banquet that every Eucharistic celebration participates in sacramentally. The "rich food" and "aged wine" are the bread and wine that become the Body and Blood of Christ.

## Death Swallowed in Victory

"He will swallow up death forever" (25:8). This promise is the heart of the Orthodox Paschal proclamation. The Paschal troparion — "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life" — is the liturgical fulfillment of Isaiah 25:8. Death is not merely defeated but consumed, swallowed by Life itself. This is the central mystery of Orthodox theology.

## The Wiping Away of Tears

"The Sovereign LORD will wipe away the tears from all faces" (25:8). Orthodox eschatology describes the final state not merely as the absence of suffering but as the presence of perfect communion with God — the beatific vision in which all creation participates in divine joy. The tenderness of the image — God personally wiping tears — reveals the intimate, pastoral character of divine love.
