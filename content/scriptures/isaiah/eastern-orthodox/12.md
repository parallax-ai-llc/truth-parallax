## The Doxology of the Redeemed

Isaiah 12 functions as a liturgical response to the preceding messianic oracles. Orthodox worship incorporates these canticles into the cycle of biblical odes that accompany the canon at Matins. The chapter models the proper human response to divine salvation: not anxious striving but joyful thanksgiving and confident praise.

## The Wells of Salvation

"With joy you will draw water from the wells of salvation" (12:3). Orthodox baptismal theology connects this verse to the blessing of waters at Theophany (Epiphany), when the Church commemorates Christ's baptism in the Jordan. The "wells of salvation" are the sacramental waters through which divine grace flows into the life of the faithful. The Great Blessing of Waters explicitly invokes the imagery of Isaiah.

## Proclamation to the Nations

"Make known among the nations what he has done" (12:4). Orthodox missiology, while historically less expansionist than Western Christianity, affirms the universal scope of God's saving purpose. The translation of Scripture and liturgy into vernacular languages — a principle established by Saints Cyril and Methodius — embodies this Isaianic call to make God's deeds known among all peoples.
