## The Banquet on the Mountain

Isaiah 25 presents the eschatological feast: "On this mountain the LORD Almighty will prepare a feast of rich food for all peoples" (25:6). Catholic Eucharistic theology reads this as the Old Testament anticipation of the Eucharistic banquet. The "rich food" and "aged wine" correspond to the bread and wine of the Mass, and the universal scope — "for all peoples" — reflects the Catholic conviction that the Eucharist is offered for the salvation of the whole world.

## The Destruction of Death

"He will swallow up death forever" (25:8). Catholic theology reads this as the decisive eschatological promise, fulfilled in principle in Christ's resurrection and to be consummated at the general resurrection. The Easter liturgy quotes this verse (through Paul's citation in 1 Corinthians 15:54) as the Church's triumphant proclamation over death. Catholic funeral liturgy likewise draws on this promise: death is not the final word but the penultimate one.

## God Wipes Away Tears

"The Sovereign LORD will wipe away the tears from all faces" (25:8). Catholic eschatology connects this intimate image to Revelation 21:4, seeing it as a description of the beatific vision — the ultimate encounter with God in which all suffering is not merely forgotten but transformed and healed. The Catechism (CCC 1028) describes heaven as the state of supreme definitive happiness — the fulfillment of Isaiah's prophetic vision.
