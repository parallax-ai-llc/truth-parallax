## The Eschatological Vision

Isaiah 2 presents a vision of the nations streaming to "the mountain of the LORD's temple" (2:2). Catholic ecclesiology reads this as a prophecy of the universal Church, the spiritual Zion to which all peoples are called. *Lumen Gentium* (1964) echoes this Isaianic vision when it describes the Church as the sacrament of unity for the whole human race.

## Peace Among Nations

"They will beat their swords into plowshares" (2:4). Catholic social teaching has made this verse a cornerstone of its peace theology. The passage has been cited by popes from John XXIII (*Pacem in Terris*) to Francis (*Fratelli Tutti*) as the scriptural basis for the Church's commitment to disarmament and international reconciliation. God's peace is not merely the absence of conflict but the establishment of justice (*iustitia*).

## The Day of the Lord Against Pride

The second half of the chapter depicts God's judgment against every form of human pride (2:12-22). Catholic spiritual theology reads this through the lens of the first capital sin: *superbia* (pride) as the root of all other sins. St. Augustine's interpretation of the fallen angels in *De Civitate Dei* draws on the same prophetic logic — what exalts itself against God will inevitably be brought low.
