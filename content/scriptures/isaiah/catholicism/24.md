## The Lord Lays Waste the Earth

Isaiah 24 marks the transition from national oracles to cosmic apocalypse. "See, the LORD is going to lay waste the earth and devastate it" (24:1). Catholic eschatology reads this as one of the Old Testament's clearest anticipations of the final judgment. The universal scope — affecting priest and people, master and slave alike (24:2) — corresponds to the Catholic teaching that all human beings will face divine judgment without exception.

## The Broken Everlasting Covenant

"The earth is defiled by its people; they have violated the everlasting covenant" (24:5). Catholic moral theology identifies this covenant with the natural law inscribed in creation — the moral order that all human beings can know through reason. When this universal moral law is systematically violated, the consequences are cosmic. *Laudato Si'* (2015) draws on this prophetic tradition to argue that environmental destruction is a form of covenant-breaking with creation itself.

## The Remnant's Song

Even amid cosmic devastation, praise is heard "from the ends of the earth: Glory to the Righteous One!" (24:16). Catholic liturgical theology sees in this the indestructibility of worship: even in the worst catastrophe, some faithful community continues to praise God. The Church's daily celebration of the Eucharist — in wartime, in plague, in persecution — embodies this prophetic conviction that worship outlasts every disaster.
