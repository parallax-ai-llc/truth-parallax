## The Liturgical Year

Catholic scholars read Chapter 3's festival calendar in light of the rich liturgical year of the Catholic Church. The Temple Scroll's expansion of the biblical festival calendar with additional celebrations resonates with the Catholic development of the liturgical cycle — from the biblical feasts of Passover and Pentecost to the Christological seasons of Advent, Christmas, Lent, and Easter, and the proliferation of saints' days and solemnities. Both traditions share the conviction that sacred time must be elaborately structured to sanctify the passage of the year.

## Sacrifice and Eucharist

The chapter's detailed sacrificial prescriptions are read by Catholic scholars as anticipations of the Eucharistic sacrifice. The Catholic understanding of the Mass as a true sacrifice — the re-presentation of Christ's offering on the cross — stands in continuity with the Temple Scroll's conviction that proper sacrifice is essential for maintaining the covenant relationship between God and his people. The specific animals and offerings prescribed in the scroll are understood as types of the one perfect sacrifice of Christ.

## The Calendar Controversy

The chapter's insistence on a solar calendar, against the lunisolar calendar of the Jerusalem Temple, is analyzed by Catholic scholars as evidence for the diversity of liturgical practice in ancient Judaism. Catholic tradition, which adopted its own distinctive calendar through centuries of development, recognizes that the ordering of sacred time is both essential and subject to legitimate variation — a principle the Temple Scroll's very existence helps to illustrate.
