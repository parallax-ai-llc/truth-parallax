## Overview

Catholic scholarship approaches the Temple Scroll with particular interest in its theology of sacred space, its liturgical calendar, and its understanding of the relationship between divine law and human authority. The scroll's vision of concentric zones of holiness radiating outward from the sanctuary resonates with Catholic sacramental theology, in which grace flows from the altar into the world.

## Interpretive Approach

Catholic interpreters read the Temple Scroll as a witness to the developing understanding of sacred space, liturgical time, and legal authority that forms part of the preparation for the full revelation in Christ and the Church. The scroll's innovations are studied in light of the Catholic principle that the Old Testament contains "shadows" of the realities fully disclosed in the New.

## Key Emphases

Catholic scholarship highlights the Temple Scroll's expansion of the festival calendar, which resonates with the Catholic liturgical year's own development beyond the biblical festivals. The scroll's concern for the spatial organization of holiness — with the temple at the center, surrounded by courts of decreasing sanctity — anticipates the Catholic understanding of the church building as sacred space ordered around the tabernacle. The Law of the King's subordination of royal authority to divine law finds echoes in Catholic social teaching on the limits of political power.

## Significance

The Temple Scroll demonstrates to Catholic scholarship that the impulse toward liturgical elaboration, sacred architecture, and the ordering of communal life according to divine law is deeply rooted in the biblical tradition. It provides historical depth to Catholic practice while illustrating the theological trajectory that finds its fulfillment in the Church's sacramental system.
