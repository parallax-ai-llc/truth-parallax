## Canon Law and Divine Justice

Catholic scholars read Chapter 6's statutes of communal law in light of the Catholic tradition of canon law, which similarly seeks to order the life of the community according to divine principles. The Temple Scroll's comprehensive legal code — covering criminal justice, family relations, and judicial procedure — anticipates the Catholic conviction that the community of faith requires a structured legal framework to promote justice, protect the vulnerable, and maintain order.

## The Dignity of the Human Person

The chapter's judicial procedures, including its requirements for evidence, witness testimony, and proportionate penalties, reflect a concern for justice that Catholic social teaching grounds in the dignity of the human person. Catholic moral theology insists that every person, regardless of status, is entitled to fair treatment under the law — a principle that the Temple Scroll's procedural safeguards implicitly acknowledge.

## Restorative Justice

While the chapter prescribes specific penalties for offenses, Catholic scholars note the broader trajectory toward restorative justice that the Catholic tradition has developed. Catholic teaching emphasizes that the purpose of punishment is not mere retribution but the restoration of the offender to right relationship with God and the community. The Temple Scroll's integration of legal and spiritual categories — where justice serves holiness — anticipates this integrated understanding of the purpose of law.
