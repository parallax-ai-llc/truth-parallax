## Concentric Holiness and the Liturgy

Catholic scholars read Chapter 2's concentric courts of graded holiness in light of the spatial theology embodied in the traditional Catholic church building. The progression from narthex to nave to sanctuary to tabernacle mirrors the Temple Scroll's movement from outer court to middle court to inner court to Holy of Holies. Catholic liturgical space is designed to lead the worshipper progressively deeper into the mystery of God's presence, reflecting the same theology of graded approach that the Temple Scroll articulates.

## The Altar and the Holy of Holies

The chapter's central concern — the proper arrangement of sacred space around the divine presence — resonates with the Catholic theology of the altar. In Catholic understanding, the altar is not merely a table but the place where heaven meets earth, where Christ's sacrifice is made present in the Eucharist. The Temple Scroll's elaborate provisions for protecting and approaching the sacred center anticipate the Catholic reverence for the altar and the tabernacle.

## Access and Preparation

The chapter's detailed regulations about who may enter which court, and under what conditions of purity, resonate with the Catholic practice of preparing for Holy Communion through examination of conscience, confession, and the Eucharistic fast. While the specific conditions differ, the underlying principle — that approaching the holy God requires appropriate preparation — is shared by the Temple Scroll and Catholic sacramental practice.
