## The Holy City and Catholic Urban Theology

Catholic scholars read Chapter 4's purity laws for the holy city within the context of Catholic urban theology and the tradition of sacred cities. Rome, as the city of the Apostles Peter and Paul and the seat of the papacy, carries a special sanctity in Catholic tradition, as do pilgrimage cities like Jerusalem, Santiago de Compostela, and Lourdes. The Temple Scroll's conviction that the city surrounding the temple shares in its holiness resonates with the Catholic understanding that certain places are set apart for encounter with the divine.

## Exclusion and Inclusion in the Church

The chapter's detailed regulations about who may enter the holy city raise questions about inclusion and exclusion that Catholic ecclesiology addresses. While the Temple Scroll excludes various categories of impure persons from the sacred precincts, Catholic teaching emphasizes the Church's universal mission: "Go and make disciples of all nations" (Matthew 28:19). Catholic scholars read the Temple Scroll's exclusionary regulations as part of the old covenant dispensation that Christ's sacrifice has transformed.

## The Purification of Space

The chapter's concern for maintaining the purity of the sacred city anticipates the Catholic practice of blessing and consecrating buildings, objects, and spaces for sacred use. The rite of dedication of a church, the blessing of holy water, and the consecration of altars all reflect the conviction that physical space can be sanctified — a conviction that the Temple Scroll articulates in its own way through its purity regulations for the holy city.
