## Purity and Spatial Holiness

Academic study of Chapter 4 focuses on the scroll's distinctive extension of purity requirements from the temple precinct to the holy city as a whole. Scholars analyze this expansion in light of the broader Second Temple debate about the boundaries of sacred space. The Temple Scroll's position — that the entire city shares in the temple's holiness — is compared with the more restricted views attested in rabbinic literature and the more expansive views found in other Qumran texts.

## Comparison with 4QMMT

The chapter's purity regulations are extensively compared by scholars with those found in 4QMMT (Miqsat Ma'ase ha-Torah), a halakhic letter that presents specific legal positions as points of disagreement between the Qumran community and the Jerusalem establishment. The overlaps and differences between the Temple Scroll and 4QMMT help scholars reconstruct the development of the community's legal traditions and its relationship to other Jewish groups.

## The Archaeology of Purity

Chapter 4's regulations are analyzed alongside archaeological evidence from Qumran and Jerusalem, including ritual baths (*miqva'ot*), stone vessels (which cannot contract impurity according to rabbinic halakhah), and the spatial organization of settlements. The correspondence between literary prescriptions and material remains helps scholars assess the extent to which the Temple Scroll's regulations were actually practiced and how they related to the broader purity culture of Second Temple Judaism.
