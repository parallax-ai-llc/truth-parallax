## Legal Hermeneutics

Academic scholars analyze Chapter 6's communal laws as evidence for the legal hermeneutics employed by the Temple Scroll's author. The chapter's treatment of witness law, judicial procedure, and criminal penalties demonstrates several interpretive techniques: the harmonization of conflicting biblical passages, the extension of specific laws to cover new cases, and the modification of biblical penalties to reflect changed circumstances. These techniques are compared with the later rabbinic hermeneutical rules (*middot*) attributed to Hillel and Ishmael.

## The Witness Controversy

The chapter's treatment of witness requirements has been the subject of particular scholarly debate. The Temple Scroll appears to allow conviction on the testimony of fewer witnesses in certain cases than the rabbinic tradition requires, raising questions about the development of Jewish evidence law. Scholars analyze these differences as evidence for competing legal traditions in Second Temple Judaism, each claiming biblical authority for its positions.

## The Temple Scroll and Early Jewish Law

Chapter 6 contributes to the broader academic project of reconstructing the history of Jewish law in the pre-rabbinic period. The chapter's legal material, combined with evidence from other Qumran legal texts (4QMMT, the Damascus Document, the Community Rule's penal code), provides an invaluable window into the diversity of Jewish legal thought before the rabbinic period. Scholars debate whether the Temple Scroll's legal positions represent an independent tradition, a development of known biblical law, or a polemic against positions held by rival groups.
