## Overview

Eastern Orthodox scholars read the Temple Scroll through the lens of the Orthodox theology of divine presence and sacred space. The scroll's vision of an ideal temple, meticulously designed according to divine revelation, resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the church building as an icon of heaven and the Divine Liturgy as a participation in the worship of the angels.

## Interpretive Approach

Orthodox interpretation views the Temple Scroll typologically, as a witness to the human longing for God's dwelling among his people — a longing fulfilled in the incarnation and made present in every celebration of the Divine Liturgy. The scroll's detailed architectural prescriptions are read as anticipations of the rich iconographic and architectural tradition of Orthodoxy, in which every element of the church building carries theological meaning.

## Key Emphases

Orthodox scholars note the Temple Scroll's understanding of the temple as the point where heaven meets earth, which corresponds to the Orthodox theology of the church as a window into the divine realm. The scroll's elaborate festival calendar, with its expanded cycle of sacred celebrations, prefigures the rich liturgical calendar of Orthodoxy. The purity requirements for approaching the sanctuary resonate with Orthodox preparation for the reception of Holy Communion, including fasting and confession.

## Significance

For Eastern Orthodoxy, the Temple Scroll testifies to an ancient understanding that worship requires beauty, order, and reverent preparation — principles that remain at the heart of Orthodox liturgical life. The scroll's vision of the ideal temple as a divinely revealed pattern finds its ultimate realization in the Orthodox conviction that the church building is a true icon of the Kingdom of God.
