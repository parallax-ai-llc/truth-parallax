## Divine Justice and Human Law

Eastern Orthodox scholars read Chapter 6's statutes of communal law within the context of the Orthodox theology of divine justice, which understands human legal systems as reflections of the eternal justice of God. The Temple Scroll's integration of civil and religious law, in which judicial procedures serve both human welfare and divine holiness, resonates with the Orthodox understanding that all law derives its authority from God and must be ordered toward the flourishing of the human person in community.

## The Canons of the Church

The chapter's comprehensive legal code finds a parallel in the canonical tradition of the Orthodox Church, which governs the life of the community through canons promulgated by ecumenical and local councils. Like the Temple Scroll's statutes, the Orthodox canons address matters of worship, morality, governance, and discipline, seeking to order the community's life according to the divine will revealed in Scripture and Tradition.

## Mercy and Justice

Orthodox scholars note that while the Temple Scroll's legal code emphasizes strict justice, the Orthodox tradition has consistently emphasized the primacy of mercy within the framework of justice. The Orthodox practice of *oikonomia* (pastoral economy) allows for the flexible application of canonical rules in specific pastoral situations, reflecting the conviction that the law exists to serve persons rather than persons to serve the law. This pastoral principle, while not explicit in the Temple Scroll, represents a development of the compassionate impulse that underlies all divinely ordered law.
