## God's Blueprint for the Sanctuary

The opening chapter of the Temple Scroll presents God speaking directly to Moses, revealing the design of the ideal temple. For the Essene community, this was not a secondary interpretation but the original divine instruction — a blueprint that superseded the actual Second Temple in Jerusalem, which they regarded as built by an illegitimate priesthood following corrupted specifications. The scroll's use of first-person divine speech ("I will dwell among them") gave its architectural prescriptions an authority that the community held to be equal to the Torah itself.

## Rejection of the Jerusalem Temple

The chapter implicitly condemns the existing Temple by proposing a radically different design. The Essene community believed that the Jerusalem priesthood, beginning with the Hasmonean usurpation of the high priesthood, had defiled the sanctuary through improper construction, an incorrect calendar, and lax purity standards. The Temple Scroll's ideal temple represented God's true intention — a sanctuary that would one day be built when the community's vision prevailed in the eschatological age.

## The Community as Interim Temple

Since the ideal temple did not yet exist, the Essene community understood itself as a living substitute — a "temple of men" in which righteous living and communal prayer served as spiritual sacrifices. This self-understanding, rooted in the Temple Scroll's vision, allowed the community to maintain its identity as a priestly society even in the absence of an acceptable physical sanctuary.
