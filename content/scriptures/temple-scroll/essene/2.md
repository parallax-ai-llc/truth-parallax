## Concentric Courts of Holiness

Chapter 2 provides precise measurements for the temple courts, arranged in three concentric squares of escalating holiness. The outer court was accessible to ritually pure Israelites, the middle court to purified male Israelites, and the inner court to the priests alone. For the Essenes, these spatial gradations of holiness were not merely architectural features but theological statements about the nature of the divine presence: God's holiness radiates outward from the sanctuary, and proximity to the sacred center requires correspondingly greater purity.

## Measurements and Divine Order

The specific dimensions given for each court, wall, and chamber reflect the Essene conviction that divine order is expressed through mathematical precision. Every measurement was understood as divinely revealed, carrying symbolic as well as practical significance. The community's meticulous attention to these details reflected their broader commitment to exactitude in all matters of Torah observance — the same precision that governed their calendar, their purity practices, and their communal organization.

## Exclusion and Inclusion

The chapter's regulations about who may enter which court reveal the community's understanding of graded holiness. Women, children, and proselytes faced restrictions on access to the inner courts, while specific categories of impurity — skin disease, genital discharge, recent sexual contact — excluded individuals from the sacred precinct entirely. These regulations were more restrictive than those observed in the actual Second Temple, reflecting the Essene community's intensification of biblical purity requirements.
