## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Exegesis on the Soul is a powerful allegory of the pneumatic soul's journey: her original wholeness in the heavenly realm, her catastrophic fall into the material world where she is degraded and prostituted by the archons, her awakening through repentance, and her restoration to her divine bridegroom in the bridal chamber. The feminine personification of the soul is not incidental but essential — the soul's fall is narrated as a story of sexual violation, and her restoration as a sacred marriage, drawing on prophetic imagery of Israel as God's unfaithful wife while transforming it into a Gnostic key.
