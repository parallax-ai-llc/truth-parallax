**4.** Therefore it is fitting to pray to the Father and to call on him with all our soul—not externally with the lips but with the spirit, which is inward, which came forth from the depth—sighing, repenting for the life we lived, confessing our sins, perceiving the empty deception in which we were, and the empty zeal, perceiving the darkness in which we dwelt.

And when we pray, God will surely hear us, for he receives the repentant soul as an offering. As he said through the Spirit to the prophet, "It is not sacrifice that I desire but mercy; not burnt offerings but the knowledge of God."

For as it is written in Homer, "Odysseus sat on the island weeping, afflicted and broken-hearted, turning his eyes toward the sterile sea, shedding tears without ceasing." So also it is fitting for the soul to weep and to cry out to the Father with all her might, so that he may have mercy on her.

And when the soul has recovered her divine nature, the Father sends the bridegroom to the soul. And the bridegroom comes down to the bride, and she gives up her former prostitution and cleanses herself of the pollutions of the adulterers. And she is renewed so as to be a bride. She is filled with the seed of the Father.

This is the ransom from captivity. This is the upward journey. This is the deliverance from slavery. This is the Exegesis on the Soul.
