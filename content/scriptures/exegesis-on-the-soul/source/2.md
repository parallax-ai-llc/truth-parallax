**2.** She then became a poor desolate widow, without help. She had nothing left from the time of her affliction. For she gained nothing from them except the defilements they gave her while they had sexual intercourse with her. And her offspring by the adulterers are dumb, blind, and sickly. They are feeble-minded.

But when the Father who is above visits her and looks down upon her and sees her sighing—with her sufferings and disgrace—and repenting of the prostitution in which she engaged, and when she begins to call upon his name so that he might help her—then he will have mercy on her, for the Father is merciful and he listens to the soul that calls upon him.

For as he said through the prophet Jeremiah, "If a husband divorces his wife and she goes from him and becomes another man's wife, can he return to her? Would not that woman be utterly defiled? But you have prostituted yourself with many shepherds, and you returned to me! says the Lord."

And again it is written in the prophet Hosea, "Come, let us return to the Lord, for he has torn and he will heal us; he has struck and he will bind us up. After two days he will revive us, and on the third day we shall rise and live before him."
