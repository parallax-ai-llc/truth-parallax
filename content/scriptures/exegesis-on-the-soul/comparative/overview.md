## Overview

The Exegesis on the Soul tells a story that resonates across the world's mystical traditions: the soul's separation from her divine source, her suffering in exile, and her eventual reunion with the beloved. The Sufi poets (Rumi, Hafiz) narrate the same journey as the reed flute's lament for the reed bed, or the moth's journey toward the flame. Hindu bhakti literature describes the soul's longing for union with Krishna. The Platonic tradition (Phaedrus, Symposium) describes the soul's fall from the heavenly realm and her ascent through love. The bridal chamber imagery connects to the Jewish Song of Songs tradition and its mystical interpretations in Jewish, Christian, and Sufi exegesis.
