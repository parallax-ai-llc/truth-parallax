## Overview

The Exegesis on the Soul is notable for its eclectic hermeneutical method: the text weaves together quotations from the Hebrew Bible (Genesis, Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, Hosea), the New Testament (Paul, 1 John), and Homer's Odyssey (Odysseus's return from Troy) into a unified allegorical narrative. This cross-cultural citation practice has attracted scholarly attention as evidence of the literary sophistication of Gnostic authors and their familiarity with both Jewish scriptural and Greek literary traditions. The text's relationship to Valentinian bridal chamber theology (as attested in the Gospel of Philip) has also been extensively debated.
