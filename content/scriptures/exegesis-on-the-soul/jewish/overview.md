## Overview

Jewish readers will immediately recognize the prophetic imagery that pervades the Exegesis on the Soul. The metaphor of Israel as God's unfaithful wife who prostitutes herself with foreign gods (Hosea 1-3, Ezekiel 16, Jeremiah 3) is one of the most powerful images in the Hebrew prophetic tradition. The Gnostic text appropriates this imagery but transforms its meaning: the 'husband' is no longer the God of Israel but the heavenly bridegroom of the Pleroma, and the soul's 'prostitution' is not idolatry but entrapment in the material world created by the demiurge. The Homeric citations (Odyssey) alongside biblical texts demonstrate the Hellenistic cultural milieu in which this transformation occurred.
