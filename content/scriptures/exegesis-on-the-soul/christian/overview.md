## Overview

For Christian readers, the Exegesis on the Soul engages deeply with biblical texts — particularly the prophetic imagery of Israel as an unfaithful wife who returns to her husband (Hosea, Ezekiel 16, Jeremiah 3) — while developing them in a Gnostic direction. The text's call to repentance resonates with the core Christian message, but the nature of the repentance differs: it is not turning from sin to obedience but turning from material attachment to spiritual self-knowledge. The bridal chamber as the locus of salvation challenges the orthodox emphasis on the cross and resurrection.
