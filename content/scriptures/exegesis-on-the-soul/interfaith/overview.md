## Overview

The Exegesis on the Soul tells a story of separation and reunion that resonates with the deepest longings of the human spirit across cultures. The experience of feeling exiled from one's true home — alienated, degraded, lost — and the hope of return and restoration speak to what the Sufis call the soul's homesickness for God, what the Buddhists describe as the recognition of one's true nature, and what Christians understand as the prodigal's return to the Father. The bridal chamber imagery — the sacred marriage of the soul with her divine partner — connects to the universal mystical theme of union: yoga (union) in Hinduism, unio mystica in Christianity, fana (annihilation in God) in Sufism.
