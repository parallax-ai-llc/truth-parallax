## The Penal Code and Final Exhortation

The penal code of the Damascus Document provides a pre-Christian model of community discipline that invites comparison with the New Testament's instructions for dealing with sin within the community. Jesus's teaching in Matthew 18:15-17 establishes a graduated process of confrontation and, ultimately, exclusion, that shares structural similarities with the Damascus community's system of penalties.

Paul's instructions for community discipline (1 Corinthians 5; 2 Thessalonians 3:6-15) likewise envision a community that maintains its holiness through the possibility of exclusion — though Paul's stated purpose is restorative ("hand this man over to Satan for the destruction of the flesh, so that his spirit may be saved on the day of the Lord," 1 Corinthians 5:5). The Damascus community similarly balanced punitive and restorative aims, with many penalties being temporary rather than permanent.

The final exhortation's eschatological framework — faithfulness now determines destiny in the coming judgment — resonates with the New Testament's own eschatological urgency. The assurance that those who "hold fast" to the covenant will see God's salvation parallels the letter to the Hebrews' repeated call to perseverance (Hebrews 3:14; 10:36) and Revelation's promises to "the one who conquers" (Revelation 2-3).
