## The Penal Code and Final Exhortation

The final chapter of the Damascus Document contains the community's penal code — a structured system of punishments for various infractions — and a concluding exhortation to faithfulness. Penalties range from ten days of exclusion for falling asleep during assembly to permanent expulsion for murmuring against the authority of the community's leadership.

For the Essene community, the penal code was essential to maintaining the purity and cohesion of the group. Each infraction — lying about property, bearing a grudge, speaking foolishly, walking naked before a companion — threatened the community's status as a living temple. The graduated system of penalties (temporary exclusion from "the purity of the many," reduced food rations, or permanent banishment) reflected a concern for both justice and rehabilitation.

The final exhortation calls the community to steadfastness in the face of the coming judgment. Those who hold fast to the covenant will see the salvation of God; those who turn back will be destroyed with the wicked. This eschatological frame transforms the preceding legal material from mere regulation into a covenant of life and death — the community's daily obedience is nothing less than preparation for the final age.
