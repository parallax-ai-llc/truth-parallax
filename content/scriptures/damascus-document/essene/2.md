## The Admonition: Lessons from Israel's Past

Chapter 2 draws upon biblical history to warn the community against apostasy. The Watchers of heaven fell through stubbornness; the generation of the Flood perished; the sons of Jacob went astray. Each example reinforces the same lesson: those who walk in the stubbornness of their hearts, following their own eyes and inclinations, will face destruction.

For the Essene community, these historical warnings carried immediate relevance. The "builders of the wall" who followed "Zaw" (a derisive nickname for a false teacher) represented contemporary opponents whose smooth interpretations of the law led Israel astray. The three nets of Belial — fornication, wealth, and defilement of the Temple — were not abstract categories but specific accusations against the Jerusalem establishment.

The admonition concludes with an assurance that God's covenant with the fathers remains intact for those who hold fast to it. The community understood itself as the beneficiary of this enduring covenant, the generation for whom God's promises would finally be fulfilled. Their separation from mainstream Israel was not a withdrawal but a preservation — they were the faithful kernel from which God would reconstitute His people.
