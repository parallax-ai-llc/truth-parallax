scripture: "damascus-document"
chapter: 3
---

**4:1** And he opened before them His holy Sabbaths and His glorious feasts, the testimonies of His righteousness and the ways of His truth, and the desires of His will "which a man must do in order to live by them."

**4:4** He built them a sure house in Israel whose like has never existed from former times till now. Those who hold fast to it are destined to live forever and all the glory of Adam shall be theirs.

**4:7** As God ordained for them through the Prophet Ezekiel, saying, "The Priests, the Levites, and the sons of Zadok who kept the charge of my sanctuary when the children of Israel strayed from me, they shall offer me fat and blood."

**4:12** The "Priests" are the converts of Israel who departed from the land of Judah, and the "Levites" are those who joined them. The "sons of Zadok" are the elect of Israel, the men called by name who shall stand at the end of days.

**5:1** And this is the exact statement of their names according to their generations, and the time of their standing, and the number of their afflictions, and the years of their sojourning, and the exact statement of their deeds.

**5:6** None of those brought into the Covenant shall enter the Temple to light His altar in vain. They shall bar the door, forasmuch as God said, "Who among you will bar its door? And you shall not light my altar in vain."

**5:11** And all who have been brought into the Covenant, agreeing not to come to the Sanctuary to light the altar in vain, shall be as those who shut the door, of whom God said, "Who among you will shut My door?"

**6:1** But all who have held fast to these precepts, going and coming in accordance with the Law, who have listened to the voice of the Teacher, and have confessed before God, saying, "Truly we have sinned, we and our fathers," who have walked in the opposite direction from the way of the people, and whose way God has loved—

**6:5** For He has established His Covenant with Israel forever, to reveal to them the hidden things concerning which all Israel had strayed: His holy Sabbaths and His glorious feasts, His testimonies of righteousness and His ways of truth, and the desires of His will.
