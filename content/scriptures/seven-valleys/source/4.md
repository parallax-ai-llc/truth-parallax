---
title: "Seven Valleys - Chapter 4"
scripture: "seven-valleys"
chapter: 4
---

**1** If the mystic knowers be of those who have attained the beauty of the All-Glorious, this station is the apex of consciousness and the secret of divine guidance. This is the center of the mystery: "He doeth whatsoever He willeth, He ordaineth whatsoever He pleaseth."

**2** Were all the denizens of earth and heaven to gather together, not one could ever explain a single letter of this mystery.

**3** Astonishment here is highly prized, and utter poverty essential. Wherefore hath it been said: "Poverty is My glory."

**4** And again: "God hath a people beneath the dome of glory, whom He hideth in the clothing of poverty."

**5** These are they who see with His eyes, hear with His ears, as it is written in the well-known tradition.

**6** Whatsoever He hath decreed, that same do they perform. Whatsoever task He hath assigned, that same do they carry out.

**7** In their sight, learning and the knowledge of the world are a veil and a barrier; and the fruit of the Tree of Knowledge is the cause of their banishment.

**8** They are the ones who dwell in the tabernacle of glory, and are established upon the seats of supreme honour.

**9** One of their number hath said: "The knowledge of God is my capital, and reason is the root of my faith."

**10** This is the Valley of Wonderment, where at every moment the wayfarer beholdeth a wondrous world, a new creation, and goeth from astonishment to astonishment.
