---
title: "Seven Valleys - Chapter 5"
scripture: "seven-valleys"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Now hath the wanderer reached the Valley of True Poverty and Absolute Nothingness.

**2** This station is the dying from self and the living in God, the being poor in self and rich in the Desired One.

**3** Poverty as here referred to signifieth being poor in the things of the created world, rich in the things of God's world.

**4** For when the true lover and devoted friend reacheth to the presence of the Beloved, the sparkling beauty of the Loved One and the fire of the lover's heart will kindle a blaze and burn away all veils and wrappings.

**5** Yea, all he hath, from heart to skin, will be set aflame, so that nothing will remain save the Friend.

**6** "When the qualities of the Ancient of Days stood revealed, then the qualities of earthly things did fade away."

**7** This is the realm of full awareness, of utter self-effacement. Even love is no path to this region, and longing hath no dwelling here.

**8** Wherefore is it said: "Love is a veil betwixt the lover and the loved one." Here love becometh an obstruction and a barrier, and all else save Him is but a curtain.

**9** The wise Sanai hath written: "Never shall mortal eye recognize the everlasting Beauty, nor the hand of the diver bring up the pearl from the depthless sea."

**10** Ecstasy alone can encompass this theme, not utterance nor argument. And whosoever hath dwelt at this stage of the journey, or caught a breath from this garden land, knoweth whereof We speak.
