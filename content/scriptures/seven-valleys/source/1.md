---
title: "Seven Valleys - Chapter 1"
scripture: "seven-valleys"
chapter: 1
---

**1** In the Name of God, the Clement, the Merciful. Praise be to God who hath made being to appear from nothingness; graven upon the tablet of man the secrets of pre-existence.

**2** The stages that mark the wayfarer's journey from the abode of dust to the heavenly homeland are said to be seven. Some have called these Seven Valleys, and others, Seven Cities.

**3** The first is the Valley of Search. The steed of this Valley is patience; without patience the wayfarer on this journey will reach nowhere and attain no goal.

**4** In this journey the seeker reacheth a stage wherein he seeth all created things wandering distracted in search of the Friend. How many a Jacob will he see, hunting after his Joseph; he will behold many a lover, hastening to seek the Beloved.

**5** At every moment he findeth a weighty matter, in every hour he becometh aware of a mystery; for he hath taken his heart away from both worlds, and set out for the dwelling of the Beloved.

**6** At every step, aid from the Invisible Realm will attend him and the heat of his search will grow.

**7** One must judge of search by the standard of the Majnun of Love. It is related that one day they came upon Majnun sifting the dust, and his tears flowing down. They said: "What doest thou?" He said: "I seek for Layli." They cried: "Alas for thee! Layli is of pure spirit, and thou seekest her in the dust!" He said: "I seek her everywhere; haply somewhere I shall find her."

**8** The true seeker hunteth naught but the object of his quest, and the lover hath no desire save union with his beloved.

**9** And if by the help of God he findeth on this journey a trace of the traceless Friend, he shall pass at once from this valley into the Valley of Love.

**10** He shall be stirred by the breezes of love and shall forget all save the Beloved. He shall see neither ignorance nor knowledge, neither doubt nor certitude; he shall find the fire of love and the heat of longing.
