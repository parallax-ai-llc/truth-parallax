---
title: "Seven Valleys - Chapter 3"
scripture: "seven-valleys"
chapter: 3
---

**1** In this Valley, the wayfarer is content with the decree of God, and seeth in poverty the essence of wealth, and in affliction the pleasure of prosperity.

**2** He burneth away the veils of want with a single spark, and with inward and outward eyes discovereth within and without the mysteries of return.

**3** From sorrow he turneth to bliss, from anguish to joy. His grief and mourning yield to delight and rapture.

**4** This station hath many signs, unnumbered proofs. Hence it is said: "Hath not each soul its own station before God?" And again, "Each one acteth according to his own nature."

**5** In this Valley he feeleth the winds of the divine contentment blowing from the plane of the spirit. He burneth away the veils with a single spark.

**6** Although to outward view, the wayfarers in this Valley may dwell upon the dust, yet inwardly they are throned in the heights of mystic meaning; they eat of the endless bounties of inner significances.

**7** The tongue faileth in describing these three Valleys, and speech falleth short. The pen steppeth not into this region, the ink leaveth only a blot.

**8** In these journeys the traveller, if he strayeth a hair's breadth from the path, will be consumed. Only the lamp of divine knowledge can light the way.

**9** He who hath attained this station is sanctified from all that pertaineth to the world. Wherefore, if those who have come to the sea of His presence are found to possess none of the limited things of this perishable world, whether it be outer wealth or personal opinions, it mattereth not.

**10** For whatever the creatures have is limited by their own limits, and whatever the True One hath is sanctified from this limitation. This journey hath no visible end.
