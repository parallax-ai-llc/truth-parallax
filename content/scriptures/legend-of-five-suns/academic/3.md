Sun 3 of the Legend of the Five Suns — Nahui Quiahuitl (Four Rain) — has been the subject of extensive academic analysis across multiple disciplines: anthropology, archaeology, linguistics, art history, comparative religion, and cultural studies. Scholars examine this sun's content, structure, and cultural context, seeking to understand both what it communicates and the conditions under which it was produced and transmitted.

Key scholarly questions concerning this sun include its relationship to other sections of the Legend of the Five Suns, its historical and cultural context, the reliability and transmission history of the text, and its significance for understanding the broader religious and intellectual tradition from which it emerges. Academic engagement with this sun contributes to the ongoing scholarly project of recognizing the Legend of the Five Suns as a major text of world religious and literary heritage.
