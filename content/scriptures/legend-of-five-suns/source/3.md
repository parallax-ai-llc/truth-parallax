---
title: "Legend of Five Suns - Chapter 3"
scripture: "legend-of-five-suns"
chapter: 3
---

**1** When it was still night, when there was still no light, when there had not yet dawned, the gods gathered themselves together at Teotihuacan and spoke among themselves.

**2** They said: "Come hither, O gods. Who will take it upon himself? Who will carry the burden? Who will be the Sun? Who will be the dawn?"

**3** And there one of them, Tecuciztecatl, the Lord of Snails, came forward. He said: "O gods, I shall be the one. I shall be the Sun."

**4** Then the gods spoke again: "Who else? Who will be the other one?" And they looked upon one another. No one dared. All were afraid.

**5** Then one of the gods, Nanahuatzin, the Pimply One, the Scabby One, the diseased and humble god, was addressed. They said to him: "Thou shalt be the one, O Pimply One."

**6** And he gladly received the command. He answered: "It is well, O gods. You have been good to me." And he accepted the burden.

**7** Then both of them began to do penance. They fasted for four days. Then the fire was lit in the hearth, in the rock called teotexcalli, the divine rock-hearth.

**8** Now Tecuciztecatl offered precious things for his penance. Instead of fir branches, he offered quetzal feathers. Instead of grass balls, he offered balls of gold. Instead of maguey spines, he offered spines of precious jade. Instead of spines reddened with blood, he offered spines of coral.

**9** And the incense he offered was of the very finest quality. But Nanahuatzin offered green water rushes tied in bundles of three, and all of them together nine. And he offered grass balls and maguey spines, and he anointed them with his own blood. And his incense was the scabs from his own sores.

**10** A tower was made for Tecuciztecatl, like a mountain. And a tower was made for Nanahuatzin, and it was only a mound of rushes and grass. Each of them remained four nights on his tower, doing penance.

**11** And when the penance was completed, they cast down their towers. And all things with which they had done penance were laid aside. It was time. The moment had come.

**12** Then at midnight the gods assembled around the fire. On either side they stationed themselves, and between them the two penitents stood, facing the fire.
