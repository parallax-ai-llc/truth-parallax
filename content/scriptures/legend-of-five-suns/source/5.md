---
title: "Legend of Five Suns - Chapter 5"
scripture: "legend-of-five-suns"
chapter: 5
---

**1** And when the Sun had begun to move and the Moon had followed, the world was still empty of people. And the gods said: "Who shall inhabit the earth? The Sun has been created, but who will live upon the earth?"

**2** Then Quetzalcoatl, the Feathered Serpent, descended to the underworld, to Mictlan, the land of the dead. He went to see Mictlantecuhtli, the Lord of the Dead, and Mictlancihuatl, the Lady of the Dead.

**3** And Quetzalcoatl said to them: "I come for the precious bones that you keep here, the bones of past generations. I wish to take them, for the gods are concerned about who shall live upon the earth."

**4** And Mictlantecuhtli answered: "Very well. Sound my shell horn and carry the bones four times around my circle of jade." But the shell horn had no holes. And so Quetzalcoatl called upon the worms to bore holes in it, and he called upon the bees to enter the horn and make it sound.

**5** When Mictlantecuhtli heard the horn sounding, he said: "Very well, take them." But then he said to his servants: "Tell him, O gods of the underworld, that he must leave them behind."

**6** But Quetzalcoatl answered: "No, I take them with me forever." And his nahual, his spirit double, said to the Lord of the Dead: "He shall leave them." But to Quetzalcoatl he whispered: "Tell them you will leave them, but take them and go."

**7** And so Quetzalcoatl said: "Very well, I shall leave them." But he took the precious bones and wrapped them in a bundle. The bones of man were in one bundle and the bones of woman in another.

**8** Then Quetzalcoatl took the bones and ascended from the underworld. But Mictlantecuhtli said to his servants: "O gods, is Quetzalcoatl truly carrying away the precious bones? Go and make a pit for him."

**9** They made a pit, and Quetzalcoatl stumbled and fell into it. He fell as if dead. And the quails, which guard the underworld, startled him. And the precious bones scattered and fell. And the quails gnawed on them and chewed them.

**10** Quetzalcoatl came to himself and wept. He said to his nahual: "O my nahual, what shall become of this?" And his spirit double answered: "What shall become of it? It was spoiled. But let it be as it is."

**11** Then Quetzalcoatl gathered up the bones, made a bundle of them again, and carried them to Tamoanchan, the place of origin. And when he arrived, the goddess Cihuacoatl, the Snake Woman, ground them up in a jade bowl, a precious vessel.

**12** And Quetzalcoatl bled his member upon the ground bones. And all the gods did penance over them. They bled themselves upon the bones that had been ground.

**13** And from this came forth the common people, the macehuales. For the gods did penance for us, they gave their blood for us.

**14** And the gods said: "People have been born, the common people, the macehuales, for the gods did penance on their behalf."

**15** And then the gods said: "What shall the people eat? What shall be their food?" And the red ant had maize. She carried it from the interior of the Food Mountain, Tonacatepetl.

**16** Quetzalcoatl met the red ant and said: "Where did you find that maize? Tell me." But the ant would not tell. Quetzalcoatl asked again and again. At last the ant said: "Over there," and pointed to the Food Mountain.

**17** Quetzalcoatl changed himself into a black ant and followed the red ant into the mountain. He entered and carried out the maize. He brought it to Tamoanchan, and the gods chewed it and placed it in the mouths of the infant people, and they were nourished.

**18** Then the gods said: "What shall we do with the Food Mountain? It must be opened." And Nanahuatzin, the Sun, struck the mountain with lightning. And the rain gods, the Tlaloque, came and scattered the grain: white maize, yellow maize, red maize, dark maize, and beans and amaranth and chia.

**19** And so the people were given their food. This is the Sun under which we live, the Sun called Four Movement. It is our Sun, the fifth Sun. And as the old ones say, in this Sun the earth shall move, there shall be famine, and we shall perish.

**20** This is the story of how the world was created five times, and how the people were given life and sustenance by the gods, who sacrificed themselves so that we might live. And for this we owe them honor, and blood, and praise.
