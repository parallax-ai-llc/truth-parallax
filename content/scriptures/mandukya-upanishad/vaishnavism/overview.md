## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Mandukya Upanishad as revealing the omnipresence of Vishnu across all states of consciousness. The sacred syllable AUM is understood as the primordial sound-form of Vishnu, and the four states of consciousness — waking, dreaming, deep sleep, and turiya — are the four ways in which the Supreme Lord manifests His presence.

## Theological Emphases

The correlation of AUM with Vishnu's creative, preserving, and dissolving functions provides a devotional framework for understanding the Upanishad's abstract analysis. The fourth state (*turiya*) is identified with Vishnu Himself in His transcendent aspect — beyond the three states of material experience. The Vaishnava reading emphasizes that turiya is not an impersonal void but the personal presence of the Lord in His fullness, beyond the limitations of material consciousness.
