The Mandukya Upanishad opens with the declaration "AUM — this syllable is all this" (verse 1), which Vaishnavas read as affirming that the sacred sound AUM encompasses the entirety of Vishnu's creation. AUM is not merely a meditative device but the primordial vibration through which Vishnu brings the universe into being and sustains it. The three phonetic elements — A, U, M — correspond to Vishnu's three primary functions: creation, preservation, and dissolution.

The Upanishad's analysis of the four states of consciousness reveals Vishnu's omnipresence. In the waking state (*vaishvanara*), He is the Lord of the external world; in the dreaming state (*taijasa*), He is the Lord of the inner world of impressions; in deep sleep (*prajna*), He is the undifferentiated ground of bliss. The fourth state (*turiya*) is Vishnu in His transcendent, personal fullness — not an impersonal void but the Supreme Person beyond the three material states, accessible only through pure devotion. The silence after AUM — the unspoken fourth element — represents this transcendent reality that words cannot reach but the devoted heart can touch.
