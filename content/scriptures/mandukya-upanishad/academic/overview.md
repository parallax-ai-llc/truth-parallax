## Overview

The Mandukya Upanishad's dating is debated among scholars, with estimates ranging from the 5th century BCE to the early centuries CE. Its extreme brevity (12 verses) and systematic structure have led some scholars to view it as a later, highly distilled summary of Upanishadic consciousness theory rather than an early composition. Its influence, however, has been enormous, particularly through Gaudapada's *Karika*.

## Research Themes

Scholarly attention has focused on Gaudapada's apparent use of Buddhist Madhyamaka and Yogachara concepts in his *Karika*, raising questions about the relationship between late Vedanta and Mahayana Buddhism. The Mandukya's analysis of consciousness states has been compared with Husserl's phenomenological reduction, William James's "stream of consciousness," and modern neuroscientific models of consciousness. Some scholars view the Upanishad as offering one of the world's earliest systematic analyses of subjective experience.
