Madhva reads the Mandukya Upanishad as revealing four real aspects of God's relationship to the individual soul and the cosmos. AUM, as God's primary name, represents Vishnu's creative, sustaining, and sovereign power. The three phonetic elements are not arbitrary correspondences but genuine expressions of divine attributes.

The four states of consciousness are real and distinct modes of the soul's experience, all governed by Vishnu. In waking, God sustains the soul's engagement with the external world; in dreaming, He governs the soul's internal impressions; in deep sleep, He maintains the soul in a state of latent consciousness. Turiya is not the dissolution of these states into undifferentiated awareness (as Advaita claims) but Vishnu's transcendent mode of being — the Supreme Lord in His own abode, beyond material consciousness but fully personal and fully real. Madhva specifically rejects Gaudapada's *ajativada* (non-origination): the world is genuinely created, the states are genuinely experienced, and the God who transcends them is a genuine Supreme Person.
