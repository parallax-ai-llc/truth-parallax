## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita reading of the Mandukya Upanishad emphasizes that the four aspects of consciousness described in the text reveal four genuine modes of God's relationship to the world. The states of waking, dreaming, and deep sleep are real experiences of the real individual soul, governed and sustained by the real Supreme Lord in His various aspects.

## Theological Emphases

Against Gaudapada's *ajativada* (non-origination doctrine), Madhva insists that the three states of consciousness represent genuine experiences of genuinely existing souls. The waking world is real, not a dream within a dream. Turiya is not the dissolution of all states into undifferentiated consciousness but the highest mode of God's being — Vishnu in His transcendent glory, beyond the material states but fully real and personal. AUM as the sacred syllable points to Vishnu's three primary attributes: creation (A), sustenance (U), and sovereignty (M).
