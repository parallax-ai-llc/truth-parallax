The Mandukya Upanishad's 12 verses constitute what Kashmir Shaivism considers a complete map of consciousness. The opening declaration — "AUM is all this" — is read as expressing the vibratory nature (*spanda*) of consciousness itself. AUM is not a word referring to something external but the self-expression of awareness — consciousness vibrating as the entire universe. The three phonetic elements represent the three movements of consciousness: expansion (A), contraction (U), and equilibrium (M).

The four states receive distinctive Shaiva treatment. Waking (*vaishvanara*) is consciousness fully externalized; dreaming (*taijasa*) is consciousness turned inward but still creating forms; deep sleep (*prajna*) is consciousness at rest, undifferentiated but unaware of itself. Turiya is consciousness fully awake to itself — *prakasha vimarsha samarasya* (the perfect equilibrium of luminosity and self-awareness). Kashmir Shaivism adds *turiyatita* — "beyond the fourth" — where turiya is not a separate state but saturates all three ordinary states. The silence after AUM is not emptiness but the fullness of Paramashiva recognizing Himself in every sound, every state, every experience.
