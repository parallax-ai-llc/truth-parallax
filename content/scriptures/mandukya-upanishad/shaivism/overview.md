## Overview

The Mandukya Upanishad's analysis of consciousness states aligns remarkably with Kashmir Shaivism's own four-state model. The progression from waking through dreaming and deep sleep to turiya maps onto the Shaiva categories of *jagrat*, *svapna*, *sushupti*, and *turiya*. Kashmir Shaivism adds a fifth state — *turiyatita* (beyond the fourth) — where turiya pervades all three ordinary states.

## Theological Emphases

The Shaiva reading sees the Mandukya's analysis as a map of consciousness's own self-exploration. AUM is not merely a sacred sound but the vibratory essence (*spanda*) of consciousness itself. The three phonetic elements represent consciousness's contraction into progressively denser states, while the silence after AUM — turiya — is consciousness recognizing its own unlimited nature. This recognition transforms all three ordinary states into expressions of freedom rather than bondage.
