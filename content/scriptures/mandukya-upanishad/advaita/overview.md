## Overview

The Mandukya Upanishad holds a unique position in Advaita Vedanta. Gaudapada (Shankara's grand-teacher) wrote his *Mandukya Karika* — the earliest systematic exposition of Advaita philosophy — as a commentary on this Upanishad. Shankara himself called the Mandukya sufficient for liberation: "If somehow one cannot study all the Upanishads, the Mandukya alone is enough."

## Theological Emphases

The Advaita reading identifies turiya not as a fourth state alongside the other three but as the continuous consciousness that pervades and grounds all three states. Waking, dreaming, and deep sleep arise within turiya and dissolve back into it. Gaudapada's radical contribution (*ajativada* — the doctrine of non-origination) argues that nothing has ever been created; the world of multiplicity is an appearance within the one non-dual consciousness. The Mandukya's 12 verses thus contain, in seed form, the entire Advaita system.
