---
title: "Pesher Habakkuk - Chapter 3"
scripture: "pesher-habakkuk"
chapter: 3
---

**1** "Write down the vision and make it plain upon the tablets, that he who reads may read it speedily." The interpretation of this concerns the Teacher of Righteousness, to whom God made known all the mysteries of the words of His servants the Prophets.

**2** "For there shall be yet another vision concerning the appointed time. It shall tell of the end and shall not lie." The interpretation of this: the final age shall be prolonged, and shall exceed all that the Prophets have said; for the mysteries of God are astounding.

**3** "If it tarries, wait for it, for it shall surely come and shall not be late." The interpretation of this concerns the men of truth who keep the Law, whose hands shall not slacken in the service of truth when the final age is prolonged.

**4** For all the ages of God reach their appointed end as He determines for them in the mysteries of His wisdom.

**5** "Behold, his soul is puffed up and is not upright." The interpretation of this: they shall double their guilt upon themselves, and it shall not be forgiven when they are judged.

**6** "But the righteous shall live by his faith." The interpretation of this concerns all those who observe the Law in the House of Judah, whom God will deliver from the House of Judgment because of their suffering and because of their faith in the Teacher of Righteousness.

**7** "Moreover, wealth is treacherous; the arrogant man shall not abide." The interpretation of this concerns the Wicked Priest who was called by the name of truth when he first arose. But when he ruled over Israel his heart became proud.

**8** He forsook God and betrayed the precepts for the sake of riches. He robbed and amassed the riches of the men of violence who rebelled against God.

**9** And he took the wealth of the peoples, heaping sinful iniquity upon himself. And he lived in the ways of abominations amidst every unclean defilement.

**10** "Because you have plundered many nations, all the remnant of the peoples shall plunder you." The interpretation of this concerns the last Priests of Jerusalem, who shall amass money and wealth by plundering the peoples. But in the last days, their riches and their plunder shall be delivered into the hands of the army of the Kittim, for it is they who are the remnant of the peoples.
