---
title: "Pesher Habakkuk - Chapter 2"
scripture: "pesher-habakkuk"
chapter: 2
---

**1** They come for violence; the host of their faces is as the east wind, and they gather captives as the sand.

**2** The interpretation of this concerns the Kittim, who trample the earth with their horses and beasts. They come from afar, from the islands of the sea, to devour all the peoples like a vulture which cannot be satisfied.

**3** And they deal with all the nations in fury and anger and wrath and indignation. For it is what He said: "They heap scorn upon kings, and rulers are their laughing-stock."

**4** The interpretation of this: they mock the great ones and despise the venerable. They ridicule kings and princes and scoff at a mighty people.

**5** "And they laugh at every fortress, and heap up earth and take it." The interpretation of this concerns the rulers of the Kittim, who despise the fortresses of the peoples and with a laugh they surround them and encompass them with a great army to seize them.

**6** And out of dread and fear they are delivered into their hands, and they destroy them because of the iniquity of their inhabitants.

**7** "Then the wind sweeps on and passes through, and they whose might is their god are guilty." The interpretation of this concerns the rulers of the Kittim, who pass before one another by the counsel of the House of Guilt. Each one comes after the other to destroy the earth.

**8** "And they whose might is their god." The interpretation of this concerns all the peoples who serve stone and wood.

**9** "Art thou not from everlasting, O Lord, my God, my Holy One? We shall not die." The interpretation of this: God will not destroy His people by the hand of the nations; God will execute the judgment of the nations by the hand of His elect.

**10** And through their chastisement all the wicked of His people shall expiate their guilt, who keep His commandments in their distress. For He said: "Too pure of eyes to behold evil."
