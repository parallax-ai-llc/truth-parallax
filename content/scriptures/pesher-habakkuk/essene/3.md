## The Righteous Shall Live by Faith

The third chapter contains the pesher's most theologically significant interpretation: Habakkuk 2:4b, "the righteous shall live by his faith," is read as referring to "all the doers of the Torah in the house of Judah, whom God will rescue from the house of judgment, on account of their toil and their faithfulness to the Teacher of Righteousness." Faithfulness to the Teacher — accepting his interpretive authority and following his teaching — is the criterion of salvation.

For the Essene community, this interpretation established the Teacher as the indispensable mediator of salvation. It was not Torah observance alone that saved, but Torah observance as interpreted by the Teacher. Those who followed other interpretive authorities — however sincere their observance — were outside the covenant of salvation. This exclusive claim to salvific authority was the foundation of the community's separatist identity.

The chapter concludes with reflections on the delay of the end, explaining that "the final age will be prolonged beyond all that the prophets said, for the mysteries of God are wondrous." This acknowledgment of delayed fulfillment, combined with the assurance that God's purposes will ultimately prevail, provided the community with a theological framework for perseverance in the face of continued Roman power and the Wicked Priest's apparent impunity.
