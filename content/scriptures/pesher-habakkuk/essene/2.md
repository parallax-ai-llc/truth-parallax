## The Fate of the Treacherous

Chapter 2 interprets Habakkuk's series of woes against the arrogant and unjust as specific prophecies about the Wicked Priest and other enemies of the community. The biblical woes against those who "heap up what is not their own" and "build a city with bloodshed" are applied to the Wicked Priest's accumulation of wealth and his violent persecution of the faithful.

For the Essene community, these interpretations confirmed that the prophets had foreseen the very events they were experiencing. The Wicked Priest's crimes — plundering the wealth of the people, defiling the Temple with impure practices, and attacking the Teacher on the Day of Atonement — were not random acts of violence but divinely foretold stages in the cosmic drama of the last days.

The chapter's description of the Wicked Priest's attack on the Teacher during the Day of Atonement is one of the most historically significant passages in the Scrolls. Because the community followed a different calendar from the Jerusalem Temple, their Day of Atonement fell on a different date, making them vulnerable to attack on what was for them the holiest day of the year. This calendrical divergence was both a theological conviction and a practical reality with life-and-death consequences.
