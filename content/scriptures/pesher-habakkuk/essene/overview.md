## Overview

For the Essene community, the Pesher Habakkuk was the supreme demonstration of their interpretive authority and theological vision. The pesher method — reading biblical prophecy as coded references to contemporary events — was not merely a literary technique but a claim to divine revelation. God had spoken through Habakkuk, but the full meaning of the prophet's words was disclosed only to the Teacher of Righteousness, "to whom God made known all the mysteries of the words of His servants the prophets."

## Key Interpretive Emphases

The Pesher Habakkuk establishes the Teacher of Righteousness as the definitive interpreter of Scripture, whose understanding exceeds even that of the original prophets. Habakkuk wrote under divine inspiration but did not fully comprehend his own words; their true meaning was revealed to the Teacher for the "final generation" — the community living in the last days before God's judgment.

The identification of the Kittim with a powerful military force — almost certainly the Romans — placed the community's experience within an eschatological framework. The Roman armies sweeping through the Near East were not merely a political development but the fulfillment of ancient prophecy, a sign that the final age had begun. The Wicked Priest's persecution of the Teacher was similarly understood as part of a cosmic drama in which the forces of darkness temporarily prevailed before God's decisive intervention.
