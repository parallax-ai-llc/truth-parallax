## Commentary on Habakkuk 1: Scripture and Its Fuller Sense

The Pesher Habakkuk's conviction that biblical prophecy contains a deeper meaning revealed only in later times resonates with the Catholic concept of *sensus plenior* — the fuller sense of Scripture that the divine Author intended but that may not have been fully understood by the human author. The Pontifical Biblical Commission has acknowledged this concept as a legitimate dimension of Catholic biblical interpretation, and the pesher provides an ancient Jewish antecedent.

Catholic interpreters note the pesher's emphasis on authoritative interpretation as a necessary complement to the prophetic text itself. The raw text of Habakkuk is insufficient without the Teacher's interpretive key — a principle structurally analogous to the Catholic teaching that Scripture must be read within the living tradition of the Church and under the guidance of the Magisterium. The pesher thus provides evidence that the principle of authoritative interpretation was operative in Judaism before it was developed in Catholic ecclesiology.

The chapter's description of the Kittim as instruments of divine judgment reflects a theology of providence that Catholic teaching affirms. The Catechism of the Catholic Church (CCC 302-314) teaches that God directs all of history toward its ultimate purpose, using even the actions of those who oppose Him to accomplish His will. The pesher's reading of Roman expansion as providential judgment anticipates this Catholic understanding of history as guided by divine purpose.
