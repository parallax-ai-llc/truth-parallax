## Solomon's Wisdom as Divine Illumination

Orthodox interpretation of 1 Kings 3 reads Solomon's gift of wisdom through the theological framework of divine illumination — the second stage of the spiritual life in Orthodox ascetical theology, between purification and theosis.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Dream at Gibeon (3:5-15)

Orthodox theology reads the Gibeon theophany as an experience of divine illumination (*photismos*). Solomon's encounter with God in a dream corresponds to the patristic teaching on spiritual vision: God reveals himself to those whose hearts are prepared through humility and prayer.

**St. Gregory the Theologian** taught that true wisdom is not acquired through study alone but is a gift of the Holy Spirit, received in the posture of humble receptivity. Solomon's request — "I am but a little child" — exemplifies this disposition.

### Solomon's Humble Self-Assessment

The phrase "I am but a little child; I do not know how to go out or come in" resonates with the Orthodox understanding of *nepsis* (spiritual watchfulness) and its prerequisite: the recognition of one's own inadequacy before God. The Fathers consistently taught that self-knowledge is the beginning of wisdom.

### The Judgment of the Two Mothers (3:16-28)

Orthodox interpretation reads Solomon's discernment as an exercise of the spiritual gift of *diakrisis* (discernment of spirits) — one of the charismata most valued in the monastic tradition.

**St. John Cassian** listed discernment as the highest of the virtues, without which all others can be corrupted. Solomon's ability to perceive the truth beneath deception illustrates this gift in its judicial application.

### The People's Response

"They perceived that the wisdom of God was in him, to do justice." The people's recognition of divine wisdom at work in their king parallels the Orthodox concept of the sensus fidelium — the faithful people's ability to recognize authentic spiritual authority.

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine illumination**: Wisdom is a stage in the spiritual life, received through humility and prayer
2. **Discernment**: Solomon's judgment exemplifies the charismatic gift of *diakrisis*
3. **Self-knowledge**: True wisdom begins with honest recognition of one's limitations
4. **Christological fulfillment**: Solomon's wisdom points to Christ, the Wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24)
