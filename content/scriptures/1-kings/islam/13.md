## The Man of God from Judah

Islamic engagement with 1 Kings 13 centers on the themes of prophetic obedience and the reliability of God's word — principles strongly affirmed in Islamic theology.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Prophetic Obedience

The man of God's divine commission and subsequent disobedience illustrate a fundamental Islamic principle: obedience to God's command is non-negotiable. The Quran emphasizes total submission (*islam*) to divine instruction: "It is not for a believing man or a believing woman, when Allah and His Messenger have decided a matter, that they should have any choice about their affair" (Al-Ahzab 33:36).

### The Test of False Revelation

The old prophet's lie — claiming an angel commanded him to bring the man of God back — resonates with the Islamic concern about false prophecy. The Quran warns against those who "invent a lie about Allah" (Al-An'am 6:93) and establishes strict criteria for authentic revelation.

Islamic jurisprudence (*usul al-fiqh*) addresses the hierarchy of sources: Quran, Sunnah, consensus (*ijma*), and analogical reasoning (*qiyas*). A later claim that contradicts an established divine command is automatically suspect.

### The Severity of Divine Judgment

The man of God's death for disobedience — even deceived disobedience — illustrates the Islamic principle that accountability is proportional to knowledge and responsibility. Those who have received direct divine communication are held to the highest standard.

## Theological Significance

1. **Total submission**: Obedience to divine commands is absolute and non-negotiable
2. **Discerning false revelation**: Claims of divine authority must be tested against established truth
3. **Accountability**: Those with greater knowledge bear greater responsibility
4. **The reliability of prophecy**: Authentic prophetic declarations are fulfilled without fail
