## Ilyas (Elijah) and the Drought

Islamic tradition recognizes Elijah as the prophet Ilyas, mentioned by name in the Quran (As-Saffat 37:123-132). His mission to call the people away from Baal worship to pure monotheism aligns with the universal pattern of prophetic ministry in Islamic theology.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Ilyas in the Quran

The Quran provides a concise account of Ilyas's mission: "And indeed, Ilyas was among the messengers, when he said to his people, 'Do you not fear God? Do you call upon Baal and leave the best of creators — Allah, your Lord and the Lord of your first forefathers?' But they denied him, so indeed, they will be brought [for punishment], except the chosen servants of Allah. And We left for him among later generations: 'Peace be upon Ilyas'" (As-Saffat 37:123-130).

### The Drought

The drought announced by Elijah in 1 Kings 17:1 is understood in Islamic context as a form of divine punishment (*adhab*) for a community that has rejected its prophet. The Quran records similar punishments for nations that rejected their messengers — drought, famine, and natural disasters as signs calling people to repentance.

### Divine Provision

Islamic tradition affirms God's care for his prophets in times of trial. The ravens feeding Elijah and the widow's inexhaustible supplies illustrate *tawakkul* (complete trust in God's provision):

"And whoever relies upon Allah — then He is sufficient for him" (At-Talaq 65:3).

### The Widow's Son

The raising of the widow's son is consistent with the Quranic affirmation that God has power over life and death. While Islamic tradition does not attribute the power of resurrection to prophets independently, it affirms that God may grant such miracles (*mu'jizat*) through his prophets as signs of divine authority.

## Theological Significance

1. **Ilyas as Quranic prophet**: Elijah is recognized and honored in Islamic tradition
2. **Anti-idolatry mission**: Ilyas's primary calling is to oppose Baal worship and establish *tawhid*
3. **Tawakkul**: Divine provision for the faithful illustrates complete trust in God
4. **Miracles as signs**: God grants miracles through prophets as evidence of divine authority
