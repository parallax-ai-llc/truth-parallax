## Building the Temple

Islamic engagement with 1 Kings 6 relates to the construction of Sulayman's masjid (place of worship), which Islamic tradition connects to Masjid al-Aqsa in Jerusalem.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Sulayman and Masjid al-Aqsa

Islamic tradition connects Sulayman's Temple to Masjid al-Aqsa — the third holiest site in Islam. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "The Masjid al-Aqsa was built forty years after the Masjid al-Haram [in Mecca]" (Bukhari and Muslim). Islamic scholars debate whether this refers to the original construction by Adam or Ibrahim, or to Sulayman's renovation and expansion.

**Al-Tabari** records that Sulayman built his masjid with the help of jinn, who quarried stone, smelted metal, and performed labor that would have been impossible for humans alone. The construction was completed through supernatural means, reflecting Sulayman's unique prophetic gifts.

### Sacred Architecture in Islam

While Islam does not attribute the same sacramental significance to physical structures as Jewish and Christian traditions, the mosque is regarded as a house of worship that should reflect the greatness of God:

| Islamic Principle | Connection to Temple Narrative |
|------------------|-------------------------------|
| Beauty in worship (*ihsan*) | The Temple's elaborate decoration reflects devotion to God |
| Simplicity (*zuhd*) | Excessive decoration can become distraction from worship |
| Direction of prayer (*qiblah*) | The Temple as a directional focus for prayer |
| Sacred space (*haram*) | Certain locations carry special sanctity |

### God's Conditional Promise (6:11-13)

The conditional nature of God's promise resonates with Islamic theology: God's blessings are contingent on obedience. The Quran states: "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you; but if you deny, indeed, My punishment is severe" (Ibrahim 14:7).

## Theological Significance

1. **Masjid al-Aqsa**: Sulayman's Temple connects to the third holiest site in Islam
2. **Supernatural construction**: Jinn, not forced human labor, built the sacred structure
3. **Conditional blessing**: God's promises require ongoing obedience — a shared Quranic principle
4. **Sacred architecture**: Physical beauty in worship spaces reflects devotion but must not become idolatry
