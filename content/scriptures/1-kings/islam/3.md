## Sulayman's Wisdom

Islamic tradition celebrates Sulayman's wisdom as one of his most distinguished prophetic gifts. The Quranic account of his divinely granted judgment resonates strongly with 1 Kings 3.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Divine Gift of Wisdom

The Quran states: "And We had already given knowledge to Dawud and Sulayman, and they said, 'Praise be to Allah, who has favored us over many of His believing servants'" (An-Naml 27:15). Islamic tradition regards Sulayman's wisdom not as requested but as divinely bestowed — a prophetic charism granted without human petition.

**Al-Tha'labi** in *Qisas al-Anbiya* records that Sulayman was granted the ability to understand the speech of all creatures — birds, animals, and jinn — surpassing the wisdom described in the biblical account.

### The Judgment of the Two Women

Islamic tradition preserves a version of the famous judgment story. **Ibn Kathir** records the narrative with minor variations, affirming Sulayman's extraordinary discernment. The Quran references this judgment indirectly: "And We gave understanding of it to Sulayman, and to each We gave judgment and knowledge" (Al-Anbiya 21:79).

The phrase "to each We gave judgment" is significant: both Dawud and Sulayman received divine wisdom, but Sulayman's understanding in this particular case surpassed his father's — a sign of God's sovereign distribution of gifts.

### Wisdom Beyond the Biblical Account

Islamic tradition attributes to Sulayman wisdom far exceeding what 1 Kings describes:

| Gift | Quranic Source |
|------|---------------|
| Speech of birds | An-Naml 27:16 |
| Command over the wind | Saba 34:12 |
| Authority over jinn | An-Naml 27:17 |
| Knowledge of all creatures | An-Naml 27:16 |

These gifts place Sulayman among the most uniquely endowed of all prophets — a status that the biblical account, limited to human wisdom, only partially conveys.

## Theological Significance

1. **Wisdom as prophetic gift**: True wisdom is divinely bestowed, not humanly acquired
2. **Sulayman's unique endowment**: His gifts exceed those of any other prophet-king
3. **God's sovereign distribution**: God gives wisdom as he wills, to whom he wills
4. **Gratitude**: Sulayman's first response to wisdom is praise and thanksgiving
