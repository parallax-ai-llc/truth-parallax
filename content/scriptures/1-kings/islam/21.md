## Naboth's Vineyard

Islamic engagement with 1 Kings 21 centers on the themes of justice, the sanctity of property, and the prophetic obligation to confront tyranny — principles deeply embedded in Islamic jurisprudence and ethics.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### The Sanctity of Property

Islamic law (*shariah*) establishes strong protections for private property. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said in his Farewell Sermon: "Your blood, your property, and your honor are inviolable." Ahab's desire to seize Naboth's vineyard and Jezebel's judicial murder to accomplish it violate fundamental principles of Islamic justice.

**Islamic jurisprudence** recognizes *eminent domain* under certain conditions but requires just compensation and legitimate public purpose. Ahab's motivation — personal desire for a garden — falls far short of these requirements.

### False Witness and Perverted Justice

Jezebel's use of false witnesses directly violates the prohibition of false testimony, which Islam shares with all Abrahamic traditions. The Quran states: "O you who have believed, be persistently standing firm in justice, witnesses for Allah, even if it be against yourselves or parents and relatives" (An-Nisa 4:135).

The corruption of legal institutions for personal ends is identified in Islamic ethics as *fasad fi'l-ard* (corruption in the land) — among the gravest categories of sin.

### Prophetic Confrontation of Injustice

Elijah's confrontation with Ahab models the Islamic obligation of *amr bi'l-ma'ruf wa nahy 'an al-munkar* (commanding the good and forbidding the evil). This is a collective obligation (*fard kifayah*) upon the Muslim community, and individual scholars and leaders bear particular responsibility.

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "The best jihad is a word of truth before a tyrannical ruler" (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi).

## Theological Significance

1. **Property rights**: Islamic law strongly protects private property against unjust seizure
2. **Justice as obligation**: Speaking truth to power is a religious duty, not merely a political act
3. **False witness**: The corruption of legal proceedings is a grave sin in Islamic law
4. **Amr bi'l-ma'ruf**: Commanding good and forbidding evil is an obligation upon the community
