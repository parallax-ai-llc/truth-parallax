## The Queen of Sheba (Bilqis)

The visit of the Queen of Sheba is one of the most elaborated narratives in Islamic tradition. The Quran devotes significant attention to this encounter in Surah An-Naml (27:20-44), providing details not found in the biblical account.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### The Quranic Account

The Quran's version of the story is substantially different from 1 Kings 10:

**The Hoopoe's Report** (27:20-26): Sulayman's hoopoe bird discovers a queen in Sheba who rules a great people but worships the sun. Sulayman sends a letter inviting her to submit to God.

**Bilqis's Response** (27:32-37): The queen consults her advisors and sends gifts to test Sulayman. He rejects the gifts, demanding her presence.

**The Throne Transported** (27:38-40): Sulayman asks his court who can bring the queen's throne before her arrival. A powerful jinn offers to do it, but "one who had knowledge of the Book" brings it in the blink of an eye.

**Bilqis's Conversion** (27:42-44): The queen is tested by the disguised throne and the glass floor she mistakes for water. She submits to God: "My Lord, indeed I have wronged myself, and I submit with Sulayman to Allah, Lord of the worlds."

### Key Differences from 1 Kings

| 1 Kings 10 | Quran (An-Naml 27) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| The queen tests Solomon with riddles | Sulayman summons the queen to submit to God |
| No conversion is mentioned | Bilqis converts from sun worship to monotheism |
| The visit is primarily about wisdom and wealth | The visit is about *tawhid* — submission to the one God |
| The queen is unnamed | Islamic tradition names her Bilqis |

### Bilqis in Islamic Tradition

**Al-Tha'labi** and **Al-Kisa'i** elaborate the story with extensive detail: Bilqis's mixed human-jinn ancestry, the glass floor revealing her legs, and Sulayman's proposal of marriage. The narrative serves as a *da'wah* (call to faith) story — the paradigm of a ruler who abandons false worship and submits to God.

## Theological Significance

1. **Da'wah**: The encounter is fundamentally about calling a ruler from polytheism to monotheism
2. **Sulayman's unique authority**: Command over jinn, birds, and the wind demonstrates prophetic power
3. **Bilqis's conversion**: Her submission to God is the story's climax — not wealth or wisdom
4. **Critique of sun worship**: The Quran specifically condemns the queen's people for worshipping the sun
