## Solomon's Wisdom as Divine Gift

Catholic interpretation of 1 Kings 3 centers on wisdom as a gratuitous divine gift, connecting Solomon's prayer at Gibeon with the Church's theology of grace and the sacramental life.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Dream at Gibeon (3:5-15)

"Ask what I shall give you." Catholic theology reads God's invitation as an illustration of prevenient grace — God initiates the encounter and disposes the heart to ask rightly. Solomon's humble request for "an understanding heart" exemplifies the prayer that aligns with God's will.

**The Book of Wisdom** (7:7-14), a deuterocanonical text treasured in Catholic tradition, elaborates on this encounter: "I prayed, and understanding was given me; I called on God, and the spirit of wisdom came to me."

**St. Augustine** saw Solomon's prayer as a model for all Christian prayer: seeking spiritual goods over material ones, trusting that God "will add all these things" (cf. Matthew 6:33).

### The Judgment of the Two Mothers (3:16-28)

The famous judgment demonstrates the gifts of counsel and wisdom — two of the seven gifts of the Holy Spirit in Catholic theology. Catholic interpreters have also read the two women allegorically:

| Allegorical Reading | Interpretation |
|-------------------|----------------|
| **Origen** | The true mother is the Church; the false claimant is heresy |
| **St. Ambrose** | The living child is truth; Solomon's sword divides truth from falsehood |
| **Medieval tradition** | Solomon's discernment prefigures the Church's magisterial authority in disputed matters |

### Solomon's Wisdom and Natural Law

Catholic theology connects Solomon's wisdom with the natural law tradition: true wisdom perceives the moral order inscribed in creation by God. The Thomistic tradition sees Solomon as exemplifying the harmony of faith and reason.

## Theological Significance

1. **Grace and prayer**: Wisdom is a pure gift of God, given to those who ask in humility
2. **The gifts of the Holy Spirit**: Solomon's discernment illustrates the gifts of wisdom and counsel
3. **Magisterial authority**: Solomon's role as wise judge prefigures the Church's teaching office
4. **Faith and reason**: True wisdom integrates divine revelation with natural understanding
