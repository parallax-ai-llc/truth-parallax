## The Succession of Solomon

Catholic interpretation of 1 Kings 1 reads the succession narrative through the lens of divine providence and the theological significance of anointing. The chapter's political intrigues are understood within God's providential ordering of history toward the establishment of Solomon's peaceful kingdom.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Decline (1:1-4)

The Church Fathers read David's old age allegorically alongside its historical sense. **St. Gregory the Great** saw in the aging David a figure of the Old Covenant itself — venerable but unable to produce warmth (spiritual vitality) without the New Covenant's coming.

Abishag the Shunammite, who ministers to David, has been read typologically by patristic commentators as a figure of Wisdom attending the throne of the righteous king.

### The Anointing of Solomon (1:28-40)

Catholic theology places special emphasis on the sacramental character of Solomon's anointing. The triple authority of king (David's command), priest (Zadok's anointing), and prophet (Nathan's involvement) establishes a pattern of legitimate authority that Catholic ecclesiology sees reflected in the threefold office of Christ — and, by extension, in the Church's ordained ministry.

**Cornelius a Lapide** noted that Solomon's anointing at the Gihon spring, with flowing water, prefigures baptism, where the Christian is anointed as priest, prophet, and king.

### Adonijah's Presumption (1:5-10)

Catholic moral theology reads Adonijah's self-exaltation as an illustration of the sin of pride — "I will be king" — contrasted with Solomon's receptive posture of being called and anointed by legitimate authority. The principle of legitimate succession resonates with Catholic theology of apostolic succession.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sacramental anointing**: Solomon's anointing prefigures the sacraments of baptism and confirmation
2. **Legitimate authority**: God works through established channels of priestly and prophetic authority
3. **Providence**: God's plan unfolds through the complex interplay of human agents
4. **Typology**: Solomon's peaceful accession foreshadows Christ's eternal kingship
