## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 1 Kings draws on the patristic and medieval exegetical tradition, reading the book through the lens of Christological typology, sacramental theology, and the Church's Magisterium. The Temple of Solomon holds particular significance as a type of the Church, and the narrative of apostasy and prophetic confrontation illustrates the ongoing tension between worldly power and divine authority.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### The Four Senses of Scripture
Following the medieval tradition, Catholic exegesis of 1 Kings operates on multiple levels:

1. **Literal**: The historical account of Solomon's reign, the kingdom's division, and the prophetic ministries
2. **Allegorical**: Solomon's Temple as a type of Christ's body and the Church; Elijah as a type of Christ
3. **Moral**: The dangers of wealth, power, and compromise with idolatry
4. **Anagogical**: The Temple as a foretaste of the heavenly Jerusalem

### Tradition and Magisterium
Catholic interpretation integrates the insights of the Church Fathers, particularly Origen, Ambrose, and Gregory the Great, who saw in 1 Kings rich spiritual and moral instruction for the life of the Church.

## Theological Emphases

- **Temple as sacramental space**: The Temple foreshadows the Eucharistic presence of Christ in the Church
- **Solomon and Wisdom**: The book of Wisdom (deuterocanonical) enriches Catholic reading of Solomon's story
- **Marian typology**: The Ark of the Covenant in the Temple as a type of Mary, the Ark of the New Covenant
- **Royal authority and limits**: The prophetic critique of kings models the Church's relationship to temporal power
