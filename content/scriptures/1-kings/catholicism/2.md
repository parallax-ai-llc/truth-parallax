## David's Testament and Solomon's Kingdom Established

Catholic interpretation of 1 Kings 2 attends to David's deathbed charge as a blend of spiritual counsel and political wisdom, read through the tradition of the *ars moriendi* — the art of dying well.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Spiritual Charge (2:1-4)

"Walk in his ways and keep his statutes, his commandments, his rules, and his testimonies." Catholic exegesis emphasizes the totality of David's injunction — obedience encompasses the whole of divine law, not merely selected commandments.

**St. Ambrose** drew from David's deathbed words the principle that a father's final obligation is to transmit the faith to his children. This resonates with Catholic catechetical theology: the family as the "domestic church."

### The Consolidation of Power (2:13-46)

The execution of Adonijah, Joab, and Shimei raises moral questions that Catholic moral theology addresses through the distinction between legitimate and illegitimate uses of authority. The Thomistic tradition evaluates these acts within the framework of just punishment and the common good.

**Aquinas** held that a ruler has both the right and the duty to maintain civil order, though the means must be proportionate and just.

### "The kingdom was established in the hand of Solomon" (2:46)

Catholic interpretation sees in the establishment of Solomon's kingdom a type of the Church's founding — a kingdom of peace built upon the authority of the greater Son of David.

## Theological Significance

1. **The art of dying well**: David's death models the transmission of faith across generations
2. **Justice and the common good**: The consolidation serves the stability necessary for the Temple's construction
3. **Typological kingdom**: Solomon's established kingdom prefigures the Church as Christ's kingdom on earth
4. **Obedience to the whole law**: Catholic moral theology emphasizes comprehensive fidelity to God's commands
