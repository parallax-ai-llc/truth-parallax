## Solomon's Wisdom as Gnosis

Gnostic interpretation of 1 Kings 3 reads Solomon's divinely granted wisdom as a form of *gnosis* — salvific knowledge that originates from beyond the material realm and gives its possessor power over the hidden structures of reality.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### The Dream as Visionary Ascent

Solomon's encounter with God at Gibeon, occurring in a dream, resonates with the Gnostic motif of the visionary ascent — the soul's journey beyond the material realm to receive knowledge from the divine source. In texts like the *Apocryphon of John*, the seer receives hidden knowledge through a revelatory vision that transcends normal consciousness.

### Sophia and Solomon's Wisdom

The *Odes of Solomon*, a collection of hymns with strong Gnostic affinities, present wisdom as a feminine divine principle — Sophia — who initiates the seeker into the mysteries of the Pleroma. Solomon's wisdom is not merely intellectual discernment but participation in Sophia herself.

In Valentinian Gnostic theology, Sophia is the lowest aeon whose fall generates the material world. Solomon's possession of wisdom thus represents a connection to the aeon whose drama underlies all material existence.

### The Judgment of the Two Mothers

Gnostic reading of the famous judgment offers an allegorical layer:

| Element | Gnostic Reading |
|---------|----------------|
| The living child | The divine spark trapped in matter |
| The true mother | Sophia, who seeks to reclaim her offspring |
| The false mother | The material world (hyle), which claims the spark as its own |
| Solomon's sword | The discernment of gnosis that separates truth from illusion |
| The true mother's sacrifice | Sophia's willingness to suffer for the salvation of the divine sparks |

### Knowledge as Power

Solomon's wisdom gives him power over hidden realities — a theme elaborated extensively in the *Testament of Solomon*, where the king's knowledge of angelic and demonic names grants him authority over the spiritual forces that govern the material world.

## Theological Significance

1. **Wisdom as gnosis**: Solomon's wisdom is salvific knowledge from beyond the material realm
2. **Sophia connection**: Solomon's wisdom links him to the aeon Sophia
3. **Discernment as liberation**: True knowledge separates the divine spark from material illusion
4. **Power through knowledge**: Gnosis grants authority over the hidden structures of reality
