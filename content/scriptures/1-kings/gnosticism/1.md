## The Succession as Demiurgic Drama

Gnostic interpretation reads the succession narrative of 1 Kings 1 as a drama unfolding within the Demiurge's domain. The struggle for earthly kingship reflects the archontic powers' competition for authority over the material realm — a pale imitation of the divine order of the Pleroma.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### The Aging David: The Demiurge's Decline

David's physical decline — old, unable to keep warm, dependent on human care — resonates with the Gnostic understanding of the Demiurge as a figure of diminishing power. In Sethian cosmology, the creator god's authority is inherently limited and waning, even as he clings to the illusion of supreme power.

The Shunammite woman brought to warm David has been read in some Gnostic-adjacent texts as a figure of Sophia attempting to sustain the material order she inadvertently created.

### Earthly Kingship as Archontic Rule

Adonijah's attempt to seize the throne through political maneuvering exemplifies the archontic mode of governance: power seized through force, alliances, and deception rather than through authentic spiritual authority. Gnostic texts consistently portray the archons as rulers who maintain their power through ignorance and coercion.

### Solomon as Possessor of Secret Knowledge

Gnostic tradition, particularly as reflected in the *Testament of Solomon*, regards Solomon not primarily as a political king but as a possessor of *gnosis* — secret knowledge that gives him power over demons (archons) and access to heavenly mysteries. His anointing, in this light, is less a political investiture than a spiritual initiation.

### The Gnostic Solomon in Later Tradition

The *Testament of Solomon* (likely 1st-3rd century CE) presents Solomon as a master of demons who compels them to reveal their names, powers, and weaknesses. This text, while not strictly Gnostic, reflects the Gnostic interest in Solomon as an adept of esoteric knowledge.

## Theological Significance

1. **Earthly kingship as imitation**: Material sovereignty imitates but cannot replicate the divine order
2. **The decline of the Demiurge**: David's aging symbolizes the waning power of the material creator
3. **Gnosis over politics**: True authority comes from spiritual knowledge, not political maneuvering
4. **Solomon as initiate**: The Gnostic Solomon possesses knowledge that transcends earthly kingship
