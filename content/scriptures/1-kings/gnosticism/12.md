## The Kingdom Divided: The Fracture of the Material World

Gnostic reading of 1 Kings 12 interprets the kingdom's division as an inevitable expression of the material world's fundamental fragmentation — a cosmos that was flawed from its creation.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### Division as Cosmic Principle

The division of Solomon's united kingdom into two warring states reflects the Gnostic understanding of the material world as inherently dualistic and fragmented. Unlike the Pleroma, where the aeons exist in harmonious syzygy (pairs), the material world is characterized by opposition, conflict, and division.

### Rehoboam and Jeroboam: Archontic Rivalry

The two kings represent rival archontic powers, each claiming authority but both operating within the same flawed system. Gnostic cosmology describes the archons as competitive and jealous, each ruling a portion of the material realm and claiming supremacy.

### The Golden Calves: Demiurgic Worship

Jeroboam's golden calves represent the Demiurge's self-representation in material form — idols that localize divine power in created objects. From the Gnostic perspective, all material worship is ultimately idolatry: any attempt to worship God through material means mistakes the copy for the original.

The *Testimony of Truth* argues that all sacrificial worship is a form of archontic manipulation — the Demiurge demands sacrifice to maintain his power, not because the true God requires it.

### The People's Cry: "What Portion Have We in David?"

The northern tribes' rejection of the Davidic dynasty echoes the Gnostic soul's rejection of archontic authority: "What portion have we in the Demiurge's order?" The assertion of independence, while politically catastrophic in the narrative, mirrors the pneumatic's refusal to submit to the material order.

## Theological Significance

1. **Inherent fragmentation**: The material world is fundamentally divided and unstable
2. **Archontic rivalry**: Material rulers compete for power within a flawed system
3. **Material worship as idolatry**: All worship through created forms risks confusing copy with original
4. **Rejection of archontic authority**: The pneumatic soul refuses submission to the material order
