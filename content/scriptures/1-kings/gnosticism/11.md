## Solomon's "Apostasy": The Descent into Matter

Gnostic reading of 1 Kings 11 radically reinterprets Solomon's apostasy. Rather than a simple moral failure, it represents the entrapment of the enlightened soul in the material realm — the spiritual person drawn down from *gnosis* into *hyle* (matter) through attachment to the passions.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### The Pneumatic's Fall

Solomon, the possessor of divine wisdom, descends into idolatry through attachment to the material world (foreign women, foreign gods). In Gnostic terms, this parallels Sophia's own fall: the divine aeon who "turned aside" from the Pleroma through desire and produced the material world as a consequence.

The *Gospel of Philip* states: "Those who say they will die first and then rise are in error. If they do not first receive the resurrection while they live, when they die they will receive nothing." Solomon's failure is precisely this: he does not maintain his spiritual resurrection while alive in the material world.

### The Many Wives as Material Entanglements

Solomon's 700 wives and 300 concubines represent, in Gnostic terms, the soul's entanglement with the material world's endless multiplicity. The Pleroma is unified; the material world is fragmented. Solomon's movement from the one God to many gods mirrors the Gnostic drama of the One becoming the Many through Sophia's error.

### The Division as Inevitable Consequence

The coming division of the kingdom (announced in 11:11-13) is the natural consequence of material entanglement: the unity that wisdom provides is shattered when the pneumatic person succumbs to the passions. Division, fragmentation, and conflict are the hallmarks of the material realm.

### The "Apostasy" Reframed

Some Gnostic readers might question whether Solomon's exploration of foreign religions constitutes apostasy at all — or whether it represents a genuine (if dangerous) quest for knowledge beyond the Demiurge's boundaries. The Ophite and Naassene traditions celebrated transgression of the Demiurge's commands as a path toward *gnosis*.

## Theological Significance

1. **The pneumatic's fall**: Even the enlightened soul can be drawn into material entanglement
2. **Fragmentation**: Movement from unity to multiplicity characterizes the material descent
3. **Sophia's shadow**: Solomon's fall mirrors Sophia's own descent from the Pleroma
4. **Ambiguity of transgression**: Is exploration beyond boundaries apostasy or a quest for deeper knowledge?
