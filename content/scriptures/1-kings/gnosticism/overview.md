## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 1 Kings is more allusive than systematic, drawing on the book's rich symbolism rather than providing verse-by-verse commentary. The Temple of Solomon, the figure of Wisdom personified, the contrast between true prophets and false ones, and the theme of a hidden God who cannot be contained in material structures — all provide raw material for Gnostic theological reflection.

## The Gnostic Lens

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Solomon's Temple is God's dwelling | The Temple represents the Demiurge's attempt to localize the divine |
| Solomon's wisdom is divine gift | True wisdom (Sophia) transcends the material realm |
| Elijah defeats the prophets of Baal | Elijah represents the pneumatic who perceives beyond material illusion |
| The kingdom divides as punishment | The material world is inherently fractured and dualistic |
| Apostasy is turning from God | "Apostasy" may mean awakening to the Demiurge's deception |

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### Solomon and Sophia
In Gnostic tradition, Solomon's legendary wisdom connects to the figure of Sophia (Wisdom). The *Odes of Solomon* and the *Testament of Solomon* — both texts with Gnostic affinities — portray Solomon as one who possessed secret knowledge of the spiritual realm, including power over demons (archons). His "fall" in 1 Kings 11 is read not as moral failure but as entanglement in the material world.

### The Temple as Cosmic Symbol
The detailed Temple construction in 1 Kings 5-8 resonates with Gnostic cosmological architecture:
- The Holy of Holies corresponds to the Pleroma — the fullness of the divine realm
- The veil separating the inner sanctum mirrors the boundary between the Pleroma and the lower world
- The cherubim represent aeons guarding the divine mysteries

### Elijah as Pneumatic Prophet
Elijah's encounters with the divine — the still small voice, the fire from heaven, the miraculous sustenance — mark him as a *pneumatikos* (spiritual one) who transcends the psychic and hylic levels of existence.

## Theological Significance

- **Critique of material religion**: The Temple, however glorious, cannot contain the true God
- **Wisdom beyond institutions**: Solomon's wisdom surpasses institutional religion but is lost when he succumbs to material attachments
- **The hidden God**: God's revelation to Elijah in the "still small voice" resonates with the Gnostic hidden deity beyond the Demiurge
- **Dualism**: The divided kingdom mirrors the Gnostic dualism between spirit and matter
