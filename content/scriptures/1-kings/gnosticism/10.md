## The Queen of Sheba: Encounter with the Feminine Divine

Gnostic reading of 1 Kings 10 interprets the Queen of Sheba's visit as a meeting between Solomon (the possessor of *gnosis*) and a figure who represents the Sophia principle — wisdom seeking wisdom, the feminine divine encountering the masculine.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### The Queen as Sophia Figure

In Gnostic interpretation, the Queen of Sheba represents Sophia — the divine Wisdom who has fallen into the material world and seeks restoration. Her journey from the distant south mirrors Sophia's descent from the Pleroma and her quest to return.

The "hard questions" (*chidot*) she poses to Solomon are, in Gnostic terms, the riddles of existence that only *gnosis* can solve: Why does suffering exist? Why is the soul trapped in matter? How can the divine spark return home?

### "The Half Was Not Told Me"

The Queen's exclamation — that the report she heard was less than half the truth — resonates with the Gnostic emphasis on hidden knowledge. The surface narrative (what was "told") is always less than the esoteric reality. *Gnosis* reveals depths that no secondhand report can convey.

### Wealth as Material Abundance

Solomon's extraordinary wealth — 666 talents of gold, ivory thrones, golden vessels — represents the Demiurge's realm at its most impressive. Yet in Gnostic perspective, all this material splendor is a gilded cage. The number 666 (10:14) carries ominous connotations that later tradition (Revelation 13:18) would develop.

### The Exchange of Gifts

The mutual exchange of gifts between Solomon and the Queen of Sheba represents, in Gnostic terms, the exchange between the pneumatic soul and Sophia: each recognizes the divine spark in the other, and their meeting produces a mutual enrichment that transcends material transaction.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sophia's quest**: The Queen's journey represents the divine Wisdom seeking restoration
2. **Hidden knowledge**: The "half not told" points to esoteric truth beyond surface narrative
3. **Material splendor as cage**: Even the most impressive material abundance cannot satisfy the soul
4. **Mutual recognition**: The meeting of Solomon and the Queen represents the pneumatic encounter with Sophia
