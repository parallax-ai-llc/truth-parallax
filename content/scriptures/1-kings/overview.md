## Overview

1 Kings narrates the history of Israel's monarchy from the final days of King David through the reign of Solomon, the division of the united kingdom, and the turbulent early history of the two resulting kingdoms — Israel in the north and Judah in the south. The book concludes with the death of King Ahab of Israel.

## Structure

1 Kings is traditionally divided into three major sections:

1. **Solomon's Accession and Reign** (Chapters 1–11): David's death, Solomon's wisdom, the building and dedication of the Temple, and Solomon's eventual apostasy
2. **The Division of the Kingdom** (Chapter 12): Rehoboam's folly and Jeroboam's rebellion
3. **The Two Kingdoms** (Chapters 13–22): Parallel histories of Israel and Judah, climaxing in the Elijah narratives and Ahab's reign

## Cross-Traditional Significance

1 Kings holds a distinctive place across religious traditions:

- **Judaism**: Part of the Nevi'im Rishonim (Former Prophets), read in the annual Haftarah cycle
- **Christianity**: A key historical and theological bridge between the united monarchy and the prophetic era
- **Islam**: Solomon (Sulayman) is a major prophet; the Temple and the Queen of Sheba feature in Quranic tradition
- **Gnosticism**: The Temple as a symbol of the material world's relation to the divine Pleroma

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Wisdom | Solomon's divinely granted wisdom and its eventual corruption |
| Temple | The construction and dedication of the Jerusalem Temple as God's dwelling |
| Covenant | The conditional nature of God's promises tied to obedience |
| Apostasy | The recurring pattern of kings turning from God to idols |
| Prophetic Authority | The emergence of prophets (Elijah, Micaiah) as God's voice against royal power |
| Divine Judgment | The consequences of disobedience at both personal and national levels |
