## Elijah on Mount Carmel

Chapter 18 records one of the most dramatic confrontations in the Bible: Elijah versus 450 prophets of Baal on Mount Carmel. Fire falls from heaven, the people confess "The Lord, he is God," the false prophets are executed, and rain returns to the land. Protestant theology regards this as a defining text for the sovereignty of God.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Obadiah and the Hidden Prophets (18:1-16)

Obadiah, Ahab's steward, has hidden 100 prophets of the Lord in caves. Protestant interpreters see in Obadiah the possibility of faithfulness within compromised institutions — a "believing bureaucrat" who preserves the remnant.

### "How long will you go limping between two opinions?" (18:21)

Elijah's challenge to Israel demands a decision: Yahweh or Baal. **Protestant theology** reads this as the paradigmatic call to commitment:

- **Luther** preached on this verse as the demand for *sola fide* — faith cannot be divided between God and idols
- **Calvin** saw it as a rebuke of all syncretism — the attempt to combine true and false worship

### The Contest (18:22-39)

The structure of the contest emphasizes Yahweh's sovereignty:

| Detail | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| 450 prophets of Baal vs. 1 prophet of the Lord | God's power does not depend on numbers |
| Baal's prophets cry out from morning to evening | Silence reveals Baal's impotence |
| Elijah's mockery: "Perhaps he is asleep" | Satire against false gods |
| Water poured on the sacrifice | God's power is not diminished by obstacles |
| "The fire of the Lord fell" | Yahweh alone is God |

### The People's Confession (18:39)

"The Lord, he is God! The Lord, he is God!" — the confession that defines Israel's faith. Protestant interpreters note that this declaration corresponds to the meaning of Elijah's own name (*Eliyyahu* = "My God is Yahweh").

### The Return of Rain (18:41-46)

Elijah prays seven times before the rain cloud appears. **Protestant interpreters** emphasize persevering prayer: the answer is certain because God has promised, but faith must persist in seeking it.

## Theological Significance

1. **The exclusive claims of God**: There is no middle ground between Yahweh and idols
2. **God answers by fire**: The living God demonstrates his reality through decisive action
3. **The remnant**: God preserves a faithful remnant even in the darkest times (the 100 hidden prophets)
4. **Persevering prayer**: Elijah's sevenfold prayer models the persistence of faith
