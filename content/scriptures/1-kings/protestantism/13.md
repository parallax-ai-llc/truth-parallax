## The Man of God from Judah

Chapter 13 tells the strange and unsettling story of an unnamed prophet from Judah who delivers a prophecy against Jeroboam's altar at Bethel, performs signs, and then is killed by a lion for disobeying God's direct command — deceived by an old prophet.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Prophecy Against the Altar (13:1-3)

The man of God prophesies that a future king named Josiah will desecrate Jeroboam's altar — a prophecy fulfilled some 300 years later (2 Kings 23:15-16). Protestant interpreters see this as remarkable evidence of God's sovereign foreknowledge and the reliability of prophetic Scripture.

### Jeroboam's Withered Hand (13:4-6)

When Jeroboam stretches out his hand to seize the prophet, his hand withers. The king pleads for restoration but does not repent of his idolatry. Protestant interpreters note the pattern: miraculous signs alone do not produce repentance.

### The Old Prophet's Deception (13:11-22)

An old prophet of Bethel lies to the man of God, claiming an angel told him to bring the man home. The man of God accepts the false word and is condemned for disobedience.

**This passage has troubled Protestant interpreters**:
- Why was the man of God punished for being deceived by another prophet?
- **Calvin** argued that the man of God had a clear word from the Lord and should not have accepted any contradictory message, regardless of the source
- **Luther** saw it as a warning against false teachers who claim divine authority

### The Lion and the Donkey (13:23-32)

The miraculous tableau — the lion kills the man but does not eat the body or harm the donkey — demonstrates that this was divine judgment, not a random animal attack.

## Theological Significance

1. **Obedience to God's word is non-negotiable**: Even prophets are accountable to the word they receive
2. **Discernment against false prophecy**: No subsequent "revelation" can override a clear divine command
3. **The severity of God**: God's servants are held to a higher standard
4. **Prophetic fulfillment**: The Josiah prophecy confirms God's sovereignty over history
