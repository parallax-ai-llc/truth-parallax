## David's Final Charge and Solomon's Consolidation

Chapter 2 records David's deathbed instructions to Solomon and the new king's systematic removal of threats to his throne. Protestant interpreters grapple with the tension between David's spiritual counsel and his political pragmatism.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Charge to Solomon (2:1-4)

David's dying words combine covenantal theology with political advice. "Be strong, and show yourself a man, and keep the charge of the Lord your God" — this injunction grounds royal authority in Torah obedience.

**Luther** emphasized that David's charge demonstrates the priority of God's word over political expediency. A king's first duty is faithfulness to the covenant.

### The Execution of Joab and Shimei (2:5-9, 28-46)

David instructs Solomon to deal with Joab (who murdered Abner and Amasa) and Shimei (who cursed David). Protestant interpreters are divided:

| View | Argument |
|------|----------|
| Justice served | Joab and Shimei had committed capital offenses that David was unable to punish |
| Political pragmatism | David used Solomon to settle old scores he could not address |
| Divine providence | God used even these acts to establish Solomon's kingdom firmly |

### "So Solomon sat on the throne of David" (2:12)

The transition of power fulfills the Davidic covenant of 2 Samuel 7. Protestant theology sees in this a partial fulfillment pointing toward Christ, the Son of David who reigns forever.

## Theological Significance

1. **Covenant faithfulness as the foundation of kingship**: David's charge prioritizes Torah above politics
2. **Justice and mercy in tension**: The consolidation involves difficult moral judgments
3. **The kingdom established**: Solomon's throne is "firmly established" (2:46) — a foretaste of the messianic kingdom
4. **Human frailty**: Even David's final acts mix piety with vengeance
