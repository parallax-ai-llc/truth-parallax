## Building the Temple

Chapter 6 provides detailed architectural specifications for Solomon's Temple. Protestant interpreters approach this chapter both as historical record and as theological symbol, seeing in the Temple's structure a revelation of God's holiness, presence, and covenant relationship with Israel.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Dimensions and Design (6:1-10)

The Temple was 60 cubits long, 20 cubits wide, and 30 cubits high — modest by ancient standards but rich in symbolic meaning. Protestant interpreters note that the proportions echo the tabernacle's ratios, establishing continuity between the wilderness sanctuary and the permanent structure.

### God's Promise Amid Construction (6:11-13)

"Concerning this house that you are building, if you will walk in my statutes... I will dwell among the children of Israel and will not forsake my people." This conditional promise interrupts the construction narrative:

**Reformed theologians** emphasize that the promise is covenantal: God's presence is not guaranteed by the building itself but by the king's obedience. This principle undermines any sacramental view that ties God's presence to physical structures.

### The Inner Sanctuary (6:19-28)

The Most Holy Place (*debir*) was overlaid with pure gold and contained two enormous cherubim of olive wood. Protestant interpreters see this as pointing to God's transcendent holiness — the innermost chamber where heaven and earth meet.

### "No hammer, axe, or any tool of iron was heard in the house while it was being built" (6:7)

This detail has fascinated Protestant commentators:
- **Practical interpretation**: Stones were prepared at the quarry to maintain the site's sanctity
- **Typological reading**: The Church is built silently by the Spirit, not by human force

## Theological Significance

1. **God's holiness requires careful approach**: The elaborate design reflects the distance between holy God and sinful humanity
2. **Conditional presence**: The Temple does not guarantee God's favor apart from obedience
3. **Christological fulfillment**: Jesus declared, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up" (John 2:19)
4. **The Church as temple**: Believers are now the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19)
