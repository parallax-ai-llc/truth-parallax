## Preparations for the Beit HaMikdash

Jewish interpretation of 1 Kings 5 reads the preparations for the Temple (*Beit HaMikdash*) with particular attention to halakhic details of Temple construction and the theological significance of international cooperation in building God's house.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Solomon's Alliance with Hiram (5:1-12)

**Rashi** notes that Hiram was a genuine friend to David and Solomon — one of the few Gentile figures regarded positively in the narrative. The rabbis discuss the permissibility of accepting Gentile assistance for the Temple: Gentiles may contribute materials and labor for the Temple (Arakhin 6a), but there are limits on Gentile participation in the sacred service itself.

**Radak** observes that Solomon's message to Hiram contains a theological explanation — "the Lord my God has given me rest" — demonstrating that even diplomatic correspondence should acknowledge God's sovereignty.

### The Cedar of Lebanon

The cedar became in rabbinic literature a symbol of Torah scholarship and the righteous: "The righteous flourish like the palm tree and grow like a cedar in Lebanon" (Psalm 92:12). Its use in the Temple connects the natural world to sacred space.

### The Labor Force (5:13-18)

The Talmud (Sanhedrin 21b) discusses the halakhic category of *mas* (forced labor) and its relationship to the king's authority. The rabbis distinguish between:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| *Mas oved* | Permanent servitude (applied to Canaanites) |
| *Mas* | Temporary conscription (applied to Israelites) |

**Abarbanel** argues that the labor system, while legal, sowed the seeds of discontent that would erupt in chapter 12.

## Theological Significance

1. **Gentile contributions**: Non-Jews may contribute to sacred purposes within halakhic limits
2. **Diplomatic theology**: Even political communication should acknowledge divine sovereignty
3. **The king's authority**: Royal power to conscript labor operates within halakhic parameters
4. **The seeds of discontent**: Excessive demands on the people, however legally justified, carry consequences
