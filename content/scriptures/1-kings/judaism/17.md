## Eliyahu HaNavi (Elijah the Prophet) and the Drought

Elijah (*Eliyahu HaNavi*) holds a unique place in Jewish tradition — greater than any figure except Moses. He appears at every circumcision, is awaited at every Passover seder, and is expected to herald the messianic era. 1 Kings 17 introduces this towering figure.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Elijah's Declaration (17:1)

**Rashi** identifies Elijah as a priest from the tribe of Gad (based on 1 Chronicles 6), though this identification is debated. The Talmud (Sanhedrin 113a) records that Elijah's authority to withhold rain was self-initiated — he invoked the curse of Deuteronomy 11:17 ("he will shut up the heavens so that there will be no rain") and God supported his declaration.

The Midrash adds that God initially resisted Elijah's severity — withholding rain punishes the innocent along with the guilty — but ultimately honored the prophet's oath because of his zeal for God's honor.

### Fed by Ravens (17:2-7)

**Radak** reads the ravens (*orvim*) literally, while **Rashi** notes the midrashic tradition that *orvim* may refer to inhabitants of a place called *Orvim* or to Arabs (*Aravim*). The peshat reading — ravens carrying bread and meat — emphasizes God's ability to use any means to sustain his servants.

### The Widow of Zarephath (17:8-16)

The rabbis note that God sends Elijah to a Gentile widow, demonstrating that divine providence extends beyond Israel's borders. **Ralbag** draws the lesson that faith is rewarded regardless of ethnic or national identity.

The inexhaustible flour and oil become in rabbinic tradition a *mashal* (parable) for divine blessing: when God provides, the supply never fails.

### The Raising of the Widow's Son (17:17-24)

The Talmud (Sukkah 27b, Sanhedrin 113a) discusses the extraordinary nature of this miracle: Elijah was given the "key of resurrection" — one of three keys that God retains for himself and entrusts to no permanent agent (the others being rain and childbirth).

## Theological Significance

1. **Prophetic authority**: Elijah's word carries divine power to withhold and restore rain
2. **Universal providence**: God's care extends to Gentiles as well as Israel
3. **The three divine keys**: Resurrection, rain, and childbirth are exclusively in God's hands
4. **Elijah in Jewish tradition**: The prophet who will herald the messiah and resolve all unresolved halakhic questions
