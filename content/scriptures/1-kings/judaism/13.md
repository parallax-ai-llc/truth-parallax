## The Man of God from Judah

Jewish interpretation of 1 Kings 13 engages with this enigmatic story as a lesson about prophetic obedience, the reliability of God's word, and the complex moral questions surrounding true and false prophecy.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Prophecy Naming Josiah (13:1-3)

**Radak** notes the remarkable precision of this prophecy: a specific king is named some 300 years before his birth. This prophetic foreknowledge is taken as evidence of the divine origin of prophecy — God knows the future and can declare it through his servants.

The Midrash discusses whether naming a future individual constitutes a stronger or weaker form of prophecy compared to predicting events without naming agents. The consensus is that naming demonstrates a higher degree of divine communication.

### The Old Prophet's Deception (13:11-22)

This passage raises acute halakhic questions about prophetic authority:

**Rashi** explains that the old prophet lied, and the man of God sinned by accepting a word that contradicted his original commission from God. The rabbinic principle is clear: a prophet who receives a direct divine command must not abandon it based on another prophet's claim, even if that claim invokes angelic authority.

**Rambam** (Maimonides) in *Hilkhot Yesodei HaTorah* (Laws of the Foundations of Torah) 9:1-4 codifies this principle: no prophet can contradict a prior divine command through a new "revelation." This is one of the criteria for distinguishing true from false prophecy.

### The Lion's Miraculous Restraint (13:23-32)

**Malbim** reads the lion's unusual behavior — killing but not eating the body, standing calmly beside the donkey — as a sign that this was divine judgment, not a natural event. The miraculous detail confirms the prophetic narrative's coherence.

## Theological Significance

1. **Prophetic obedience**: A clear divine command must not be abandoned for any contradictory claim
2. **Criteria for true prophecy**: Rambam's codification of prophetic authenticity draws on this narrative
3. **The severity of disobedience**: Even prophets are held to the strictest account
4. **Prophetic foreknowledge**: The naming of Josiah demonstrates divine sovereignty over history
