Chapter 2 demonstrates prana's supremacy over all other faculties through the famous contest narrative: when each faculty (speech, sight, hearing, mind) departs the body, the person continues to live, but when prana departs, all the other faculties are dragged out with it. Vaishnava interpreters see this as illustrating Vishnu's position as the sustainer of all life — prana is the Lord's energy operating within the body.

The narrative establishes a hierarchy of dependence that mirrors the Vaishnava theological hierarchy: all beings depend on God as the faculties depend on prana. Just as speech, mind, and senses cannot function without the vital breath, so no entity can exist independently of the Supreme Lord who sustains it.
