Chapter 4 teaches the internalization of the *agnihotra* (fire sacrifice) — transforming the external ritual of offering oblations into the inner offering of breath. Vaishnava commentators read this as the elevation of devotion over ritualism: true worship is not the mechanical performance of sacrificial rites but the constant inner awareness of offering everything to the Lord through each breath.

The teaching that eating, drinking, and even breathing can become acts of sacrifice when performed with awareness of God anticipates the Bhagavad Gita's teaching (4.24-30) that all actions can be transformed into sacrifice when offered to the Lord. The Kaushitaki thus provides Scriptural authority for the Vaishnava transformation of daily life into continuous worship.
