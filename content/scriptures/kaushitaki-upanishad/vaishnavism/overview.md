## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Kaushitaki Upanishad's teachings on *prana* and consciousness as pointing to Vishnu-Narayana as the supreme life-force sustaining all beings. The journey of the soul after death described in Chapter 1 — passing through the moon to the world of Brahman — is understood as the *devayana* (path of the gods) leading the devoted soul to Vishnu's eternal abode.

## Theological Emphases

Indra's declaration to Pratardana — "Know me alone" — is read as the Supreme Lord's call for exclusive devotion. The supremacy of *prana* over the senses demonstrates the Lord's position as the inner controller (*antaryami*) who animates all faculties. The internalized *agnihotra* of Chapter 4 prefigures the Vaishnava emphasis on internal devotion over external ritual.
