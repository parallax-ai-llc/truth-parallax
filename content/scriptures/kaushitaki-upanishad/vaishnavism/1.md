Chapter 1 describes the soul's journey after death — the *devayana* (path of the gods) through the moon to the world of Brahman. Vaishnava commentators understand this as the devoted soul's ascent to Vishnu's eternal abode (Vaikuntha). The questions posed to the soul at each stage — "Who are you?" — are answered correctly only by those who know themselves as belonging to the Supreme Lord.

The Upanishad's vivid description of Brahma-loka, with its lake of nectar and throne of glory, is read by Vaishnavas as a symbolic description of the transcendent realm where liberated souls enjoy eternal communion with God. The soul that reaches this realm never returns to the cycle of birth and death, confirming the Vaishnava teaching that liberation is permanent and irreversible.
