Chapter 4's teaching on the inner *agnihotra* reflects a broader trend in Upanishadic literature toward the internalization and reinterpretation of Vedic ritual. Scholars see this as a response to the growing tension between the elaborate ritual system of the Brahmanas and the contemplative ideals of the forest-dwelling seekers.

Jan Heesterman's analysis of the "inner conflict of tradition" illuminates how the Kaushitaki transforms the external *agnihotra* — the most basic daily fire sacrifice — into a meditation on breath, effectively democratizing a ritual that was the exclusive preserve of ritually initiated Brahmins. This internalization may have contributed to the development of yoga and meditation practices that eventually superseded Vedic ritual as the primary spiritual discipline.
