## Overview

The Kaushitaki Upanishad is associated with the Kaushitaki Brahmana of the Rig Veda, though its precise relationship to the Brahmana text is debated. Scholars date it to roughly the 6th-5th century BCE. Its distinctive emphasis on *prana* as the supreme principle, combined with its detailed afterlife mythology, makes it an important source for understanding the diversity of early Upanishadic thought.

## Research Themes

The afterlife journey in Chapter 1 — the soul traveling to the moon, answering questions, and proceeding to Brahma-loka — preserves one of the most elaborate descriptions of the post-mortem path in early Indian literature. The Indra-Pratardana dialogue in Chapter 3 raises important questions about the relationship between breath, consciousness, and identity. Olivelle notes that the Kaushitaki's identification of *prana* with *prajna* represents a distinctive philosophical position that was largely superseded by later Upanishads' emphasis on pure consciousness independent of breath.
