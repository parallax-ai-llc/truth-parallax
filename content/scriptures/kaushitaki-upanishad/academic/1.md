Chapter 1 preserves one of the most detailed afterlife narratives in early Indian literature. The *devayana* (path of the gods) and *pitryana* (path of the fathers) — the two posthumous paths — appear also in the Brihadaranyaka and Chandogya Upanishads, but the Kaushitaki version includes unique elements such as the detailed interrogation of the soul and the elaborate description of Brahma-loka.

Scholars note the mythological richness of this chapter — the lake Ara, the moments Yeshtiha, the river Vijara ("Ageless"), the palace Aparajita ("Unconquered") — which suggests an older stratum of belief underlying the more abstract philosophical teachings of later chapters. The lunar waystation, where the soul may be sent back to rebirth, reflects the ancient Indian association of the moon with fertility and cyclical regeneration.
