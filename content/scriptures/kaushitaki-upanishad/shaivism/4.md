The inner *agnihotra* of Chapter 4 resonates deeply with Shaiva traditions of internal worship (*antarayaga*). The transformation of external fire sacrifice into breath-based meditation mirrors Kashmir Shaivism's teaching that all ritual is ultimately an expression of consciousness recognizing itself. The fire of awareness (*chiti-agni*) consumes all offerings — not in a physical hearth but in the space of consciousness.

The identification of everyday acts (eating, drinking, breathing) with sacrificial offerings reflects the Shaiva principle that the entire world is Shiva's sacrifice — every moment of experience is an offering of awareness to itself. This teaching dissolves the boundary between sacred and profane, ritual space and ordinary life.
