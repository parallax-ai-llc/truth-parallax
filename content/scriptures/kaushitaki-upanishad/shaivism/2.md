The prana contest in Chapter 2 demonstrates that the vital force is the thread connecting all faculties — a teaching that Shaiva interpreters read as pointing to consciousness (*chiti*) as the ground of all experience. Prana is not merely biological breath but the dynamic expression of Shiva's consciousness animating the body and its faculties.

The departure and return of the faculties mirrors the Shaiva cosmology of emission (*srishti*) and withdrawal (*samhara*). When consciousness contracts, the faculties cease; when it expands, they resume. The supremacy of prana over the individual faculties illustrates the Shaiva principle that the universal is always more fundamental than the particular.
