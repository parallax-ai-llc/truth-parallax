The afterlife journey in Chapter 1 receives a distinctive interpretation in Shaiva traditions. The soul's passage through the moon — the gateway between the mortal and immortal realms — symbolizes the transition from limited awareness to expanded consciousness. The questions asked of the departing soul mirror the self-inquiry (*atma-vichara*) that a practitioner undertakes during life.

From a Kashmir Shaiva perspective, the soul's journey to Brahma-loka represents the progressive shedding of limiting adjuncts (*upadhis*) until pure consciousness stands revealed. The teaching that the knowledgeable soul transcends the moon while the ignorant soul returns to rebirth illustrates the Shaiva principle that recognition (*pratyabhijna*) of one's true nature as Shiva determines one's destiny.
