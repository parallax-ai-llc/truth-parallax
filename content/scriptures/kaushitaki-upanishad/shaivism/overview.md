## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Kaushitaki Upanishad a teaching on *prana* as the expression of Shiva's animating consciousness. The vital breath that sustains the body and senses is not merely a biological function but the dynamic aspect of pure awareness — the *spanda* (vibration) of Shiva's creative energy.

## Theological Emphases

The Upanishad's demonstration that *prana* is superior to all other faculties (Chapter 2) resonates with the Shaiva understanding that consciousness (*chiti*) is the ground from which all powers arise. Indra's teaching to Pratardana — that one should know *prana-prajnatman* (the breath-consciousness-self) — is read as pointing to Shiva's nature as the vital awareness pervading all experience. The internalization of sacrifice mirrors the Shaiva emphasis on inner worship (*antarayaga*).
