Madhva reads the prana contest as demonstrating the Lord's sovereign organization of the body's faculties. The hierarchy — prana supreme over speech, mind, hearing, and sight — reflects the Dvaita understanding that all entities exist in a graded order established by God. Prana's supremacy is not absolute but derives from Vishnu's will, who has appointed breath as the chief faculty.

The narrative confirms that individual faculties are real and distinct, each with its own function and relative status. This refutes the Advaita reading that reduces them all to an undifferentiated consciousness. For Dvaita, the multiplicity of faculties is genuine, and their hierarchical ordering demonstrates the Lord's intelligent design.
