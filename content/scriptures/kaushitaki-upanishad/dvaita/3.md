Madhva reads Indra's self-revelation to Pratardana as God's direct communication to the devoted seeker. Indra, as a manifestation of Vishnu's power, commands exclusive devotion: "Know me alone." The identification of the self with prana-prajna is not a metaphysical identity statement but a declaration of God's sovereignty over both the vital and cognitive dimensions of the individual.

The chapter's teaching that knowledge of prana-prajna brings longevity and fame is understood literally by Dvaita: knowledge of God produces real worldly and spiritual benefits. The hierarchical relationship between Indra and Pratardana — god teaching mortal — reflects the eternal distinction between the Supreme Lord and the dependent soul.
