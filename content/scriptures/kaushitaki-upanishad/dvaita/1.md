Madhva reads the afterlife journey as a real description of the soul's passage from the material world to God's eternal abode. The questions posed to the soul at each stage test the soul's knowledge and devotion — only those who have cultivated genuine understanding of their relationship to Vishnu can answer correctly and proceed. The soul's arrival in Brahma-loka is the culmination of a lifetime of devotion and righteous action.

For Dvaita, the journey confirms the reality of individual souls and their post-mortem destiny. The soul does not merge into Brahman upon arrival but enjoys eternal, blissful communion with the Supreme Lord while retaining its individual identity. The realm of Brahman is not a metaphor but a real transcendent dimension where liberated souls dwell in God's presence.
