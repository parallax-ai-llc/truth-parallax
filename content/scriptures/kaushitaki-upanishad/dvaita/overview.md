## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Kaushitaki Upanishad as affirming Vishnu's supreme lordship over all faculties and all beings. The hierarchy demonstrated in Chapter 2 — prana superior to speech, mind, and senses — reflects the Dvaita principle that all entities exist in a graded order of dependence on the Supreme Lord.

## Theological Emphases

Indra's instruction to Pratardana, "Know me alone," is read by Madhva as God's direct command for exclusive devotion. The *prana* that sustains all faculties is ultimately Vishnu's energy (*shakti*) operating through the body. The afterlife journey in Chapter 1 describes the real passage of the soul toward Vishnu's abode, accessible only to those who have cultivated devotion and knowledge during their embodied life.
