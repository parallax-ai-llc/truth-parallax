Madhva reads the inner *agnihotra* as demonstrating the proper attitude for all ritual activity: conscious offering to the Supreme Lord. The internalization of sacrifice does not negate external ritual (as Advaita might suggest) but transforms it by infusing it with devotion. Every breath becomes a prayer, every meal an offering, every act of life a sacrifice to Vishnu.

For Dvaita, the inner *agnihotra* confirms that devotion (*bhakti*) should pervade all aspects of life. The distinction between sacred and secular dissolves not because everything is illusory but because everything genuinely belongs to God and should be offered to Him. This teaching anticipates the devotional transformation of daily life that is central to Vaishnava practice.
