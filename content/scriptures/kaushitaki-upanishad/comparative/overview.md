## Overview

The Kaushitaki Upanishad's identification of breath (*prana*) with consciousness and ultimate reality finds remarkable parallels across traditions. The Sanskrit *prana*, the Greek *pneuma*, the Hebrew *ruach*, and the Latin *spiritus* all connect breath with spirit, life-force, and the animating principle of existence.

## Comparative Themes

The afterlife journey in Chapter 1 — the soul crossing waters, being questioned, and ascending to the divine realm — parallels the Egyptian *Book of the Dead*, the Zoroastrian Chinvat Bridge, and the Tibetan *Bardo Thodol*. The internalization of sacrifice in Chapter 4 — transforming external ritual into inner worship through breath — compares with the Christian shift from temple sacrifice to prayer, the Sufi concept of *dhikr* (remembrance), and the Daoist practice of internal alchemy (*neidan*).
