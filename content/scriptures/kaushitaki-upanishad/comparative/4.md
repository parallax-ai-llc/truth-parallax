The internalization of sacrifice in Chapter 4 parallels a universal pattern in religious history: the transformation of external ritual into inner spiritual practice. The Christian shift from temple sacrifice to the "sacrifice of praise" (Hebrews 13:15), the Sufi transformation of *salat* (prayer) into continuous remembrance (*dhikr*), and the Daoist internalization of alchemical processes all follow a similar trajectory.

The identification of breath with sacrifice connects to the broader phenomenon of breath-based spiritual practices across traditions — from Buddhist *anapanasati* (mindfulness of breathing) to the Jesus Prayer synchronized with breathing in Eastern Orthodox hesychasm. The Kaushitaki's teaching that ordinary acts become sacred through conscious awareness anticipates Brother Lawrence's *Practice of the Presence of God* and the Zen concept of everyday mind as the Way.
