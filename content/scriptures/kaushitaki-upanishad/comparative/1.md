The Kaushitaki's afterlife journey invites rich comparison with post-mortem narratives worldwide. The soul's passage through successive realms, answering questions at each gateway, parallels the Egyptian *Book of the Dead* (the weighing of the heart and passage through gates) and the Zoroastrian crossing of the Chinvat Bridge. The lunar waystation where souls may be turned back echoes Greek mythology's Lethe (river of forgetfulness) at the boundary of Hades.

The two paths — one leading to the gods and liberation, the other to the ancestors and rebirth — compare with the Christian distinction between heaven and purgatory, the Buddhist differentiation of rebirth realms, and the Platonic myth of Er in the *Republic*. The soul's interrogation at cosmic checkpoints anticipates the Gnostic concept of *archons* guarding celestial gates.
