Indra's self-revelation to Pratardana — a god disclosing his true nature to a mortal warrior — parallels theophanic traditions worldwide. Krishna's self-revelation in the Bhagavad Gita, Yahweh's appearance to Moses in Exodus, and Zeus's manifestation to Semele all share the motif of divine self-disclosure that overwhelms ordinary human comprehension.

The identification of breath with consciousness finds parallels in Stoic philosophy (the *pneuma* as both physical breath and rational principle governing the cosmos), Chinese philosophy (*qi* as both vital energy and cosmic ordering principle), and the Hebrew understanding of *ruach* as simultaneously wind, breath, and spirit. The chapter's refusal to separate life-force from consciousness anticipates modern debates about the relationship between biological life and subjective experience.
