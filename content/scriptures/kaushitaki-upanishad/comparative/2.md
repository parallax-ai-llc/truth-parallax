The contest of faculties — each claiming supremacy and being proven subordinate to prana — has structural parallels in fable traditions worldwide. Aesop's fable of the belly and the limbs, the biblical parable of the body's members (1 Corinthians 12), and the Roman story of Menenius Agrippa all use faculty-competition narratives to teach about unity and hierarchy.

The primacy of breath over the senses anticipates yogic and contemplative traditions that use breath control as the gateway to altered states of consciousness — from Indian pranayama to Daoist breath cultivation (*tuna*) to the hesychast breathing practices of Eastern Orthodox Christianity.
