---
title: "Kaushitaki Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kaushitaki-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Breath is Brahman, thus says Kaushitaki. Of this breath, which is Brahman, the mind is the messenger, speech the housekeeper, the eye the guard, the ear the informant. He who knows the mind as the messenger of breath, which is Brahman, becomes possessed of the messenger. He who knows speech as the housekeeper, becomes possessed of the housekeeper. He who knows the eye as the guard, becomes possessed of the guard. He who knows the ear as the informant, becomes possessed of the informant.

**2** Now to that breath, which is Brahman, all these deities bring an offering, though he asks not for it. In the same manner to him who knows this, all creatures bring an offering, though he asks not for it. For him who knows this, there is this Upanishad: "Do not beg." This is like a man who has begged through a village and got nothing sitting down and saying, "I shall never eat anything given by those people," and then those who formerly refused him press him to accept their alms. This is the rule for him who does not beg, but the charitable will press him and say, "Let us give to thee."

**3** Breath is Brahman, thus says Paingya. And in that breath, which is Brahman, the eye stands firm behind speech, the ear stands firm behind the eye, the mind stands firm behind the ear, and the spirit stands firm behind the mind. To that breath, which is Brahman, all these deities bring an offering, though he asks not for it.

**4** And in the same manner to him who knows this, all creatures bring an offering, though he asks not for it. For him who knows this, there is this Upanishad: "Do not beg." This is like a man who has begged through a village and got nothing sitting down and saying, "I shall never eat anything given by those people," and then those who formerly refused him press him to accept their alms. This is the rule for him who does not beg, but the charitable will press him and say, "Let us give to thee."

**5** Now next the obtainment of the highest treasure by means of prayer. If a man desires to become dear to any man or woman, or to any men or women, then at one of these joints of time he offers, having poured melted butter into the fire: "I offer thy speech in me, this I, svaha." "I offer thy ear in me, this I, svaha." "I offer thy mind in me, this I, svaha." "I offer thy thought in me, this I, svaha." "I offer thy breath in me, this I, svaha."

**6** Then going out of the fire, and having wiped off the ashes round his eyes, with ointment on, he should walk silently to the person he wishes to attract, or he should speak to him, or should send a message to him.

**7** He becomes dear to that person. Now he who knows this, and worships the Self, he is dear to all the world, and all the world is dear to him.

**8** Now next the internal Agnihotra. So long as a man speaks, he cannot breathe; he then sacrifices breath to speech. And so long as a man breathes, he cannot speak; he then sacrifices speech to breath. These two unending immortal oblations he offers always, whether waking or sleeping. Whatever other oblations there are, they have an end, for they consist of works. The ancients, knowing this, did not offer the Agnihotra sacrifice externally.

**9** Uktha is Brahman, thus said Sushkabhringara. Let a man meditate on it as the same with the Rik, and all beings will praise him as the greatest. Let him meditate on it as the same with the Yajus, and all beings will be joined to him as the greatest. Let him meditate on it as the same with the Saman, and all beings will bow before him as the greatest.

**10** Let him meditate on it as beauty, as glory, as splendour. For as these are the attributes of the hymn, so are they the attributes of him who meditates.

**11** The chest is the altar, the hairs the grass upon it, the heart the Garhapatya fire, the mind the Anvaharya-pachana fire, the mouth the Ahavaniya fire.

**12** Whatever a man eats or drinks, this is his Huta offering. What he swallows first is his Prana offering. What he swallows in the middle is his Vyana offering. What he swallows last is his Apana offering. Thus the body is furnished with these oblations. He who knows this does not fall into sickness.

**13** Pratardana, the son of Divodasa, came by means of fighting and strength to the beloved abode of Indra. Indra said to him: "Pratardana, let me give you a boon." And Pratardana said: "Do you yourself choose that boon for me which you deem most beneficial for man." Indra said to him: "No one who chooses, chooses for another; choose thyself." "Then," said Pratardana, "that boon is no boon to me."

**14** But Indra did not depart from the truth, for Indra is truth. Indra said to him: "Know me only, that is what I deem most beneficial for man, that he should know me. I slew the three-headed son of Tvashtri; I delivered the Arunmukhas, the devotees, to the wild dogs. Transgressing many compacts, I killed the people of Prahlada in heaven, the people of Puloma in the sky, the people of Kalakanja on earth. And not one hair of me was harmed there. And he who knows me thus, by no deed of his is his life harmed."
