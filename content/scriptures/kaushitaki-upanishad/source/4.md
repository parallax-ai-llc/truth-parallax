---
title: "Kaushitaki Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "kaushitaki-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** There was Gargya Balaki, famous as a man of great learning; for it was said of him that he lived among the Ushinaras, among the Satvat-Matsyas, among the Kuru-Panchalas, among the Kasi-Videhas. Having gone to Ajatasatru, the king of Kasi, he said to him: "Shall I tell you Brahman?" Ajatasatru said to him: "We give a thousand cows for that speech of yours, for verily all people run away, saying, Janaka, Janaka is our father."

**2** Gargya said: "The person who is in the sun, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as the supreme, the head of all beings, the king. Whoso worships him thus, becomes supreme, the head of all beings, a king."

**3** Gargya said: "The person who is in the moon, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as the great, clad in white raiment, as Soma, the king. Whoso worships him thus, Soma is poured out and poured forth for him day by day, and his food does not fail."

**4** Gargya said: "The person who is in the lightning, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as the luminous. Whoso worships him thus, becomes luminous, and his offspring becomes luminous."

**5** Gargya said: "The person who is in the ether, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him as what is full, and quiescent. Whoso worships him thus, becomes filled with offspring and cattle, and his offspring does not cease from this world."

**6** Gargya said: "The person who is in the wind, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him as Indra Vaikuntha, as the unconquerable army. Whoso worships him thus, becomes indeed victorious, unconquerable, conquering his enemies."

**7** Gargya said: "The person who is in the fire, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him as the powerful. Whoso worships him thus, becomes powerful, and his offspring becomes powerful."

**8** Gargya said: "The person who is in the water, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him as the likeness. Whoso worships him thus, what is like comes to him, not what is unlike; what is born from him is like him."

**9** Gargya said: "The person who is in a mirror, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as the brilliant. Whoso worships him thus, becomes brilliant, and his offspring becomes brilliant, and with whomsoever he comes together, he outshines them."

**10** Gargya said: "The sound that follows a man while he moves, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as life. Whoso worships him thus, reaches his full age in this world; breath does not leave him before the time."

**11** Gargya said: "The person who is in space, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as the second who never leaves us. Whoso worships him thus, becomes possessed of a second, his party is not cut off from him."

**12** Gargya said: "The person who consists of the shadow, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as death. Whoso worships him thus, reaches his full age in this world; death does not come to him before the time."

**13** Gargya said: "The person who is in the body, that I worship as Brahman." Ajatasatru said to him: "No, no! Do not speak to me on this. I worship him verily as embodied. Whoso worships him thus, becomes embodied, and his offspring becomes embodied." Then Gargya became silent.

**14** Ajatasatru said: "Thus far only?" "Thus far only," he replied. Ajatasatru said: "This does not suffice to know it, the true Brahman." Gargya said: "Let me come to you as a pupil."

**15** Ajatasatru said: "Verily, it is unnatural that a Brahmana should come to a Kshatriya, hoping that he should tell him the Brahman. However, I shall make you know him clearly," thus saying he took him by the hand and went forth.

**16** Then the two together came to a person who was asleep. Ajatasatru called him, saying: "Thou great one, clad in white raiment, Soma, King." He did not rise. Then rubbing him with the hand, he woke him, and he arose.

**17** Ajatasatru said: "When this man was thus asleep, where was then the person, the intelligent, and from whence did he thus come back?" Gargya did not know this.

**18** Ajatasatru said: "When this man was thus asleep, the person consisting of knowledge had taken to himself the knowledge of these senses and lay in that space which is within the heart. When he takes in these different kinds of consciousness, then it is said that the man is asleep. The breath is kept in, speech is kept in, the eye is kept in, the ear is kept in, the mind is kept in."

**19** "But when he moves about in sleep, these are his worlds. He is, as it were, a great king; he is, as it were, a great Brahmana; he rises, as it were, and he falls. And as a great king might keep in his own subjects, and move about according to his pleasure within his own domain, thus does that person keep in the several senses and move about according to his pleasure within his own body."

**20** "Next, when he is in profound sleep, and knows nothing whatsoever, having crept out through the seventy-two thousand arteries called Hita, which lead from the heart to the body, he rests in the body. And as a young man, or a great king, or a great Brahmana, having reached the summit of bliss, might rest, so does he then rest."
