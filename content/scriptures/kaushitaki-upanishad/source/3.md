---
title: "Kaushitaki Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "kaushitaki-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Indra said: "I am Prana, meditate on me as the conscious Self, as life, as immortality. Life is Prana, Prana is life. Immortality is Prana, Prana is immortality. As long as Prana dwells in this body, so long surely there is life. By Prana he obtains immortality in the other world, by knowledge true conception."

**2** "He who meditates on me as life and immortality, gains his full life in this world, and obtains in the heavenly world immortality and indestructibility."

**3** Now it has been said by some that the pranas all become one in that Prana, for otherwise no man could at the same time make known a name by speech, see a form with the eye, hear a sound with the ear, think a thought with the mind. After having become one, the pranas perceive all these together, one by one.

**4** While speech speaks, all pranas speak after it. While the eye sees, all pranas see after it. While the ear hears, all pranas hear after it. While the mind thinks, all pranas think after it. While the prana breathes, all pranas breathe after it.

**5** "Thus it is indeed," said Indra. "But there is a pre-eminence among the pranas."

**6** A man lives deprived of speech, for we see dumb people. A man lives deprived of sight, for we see blind people. A man lives deprived of hearing, for we see deaf people. A man lives deprived of mind, for we see infants. A man lives deprived of his arms, deprived of his legs, for we see this likewise. But Prana alone is the conscious Self, and having laid hold of this body, it makes it rise up. Therefore it is said, Let man worship it alone as Uktha.

**7** What is Prana, that is Prajna, consciousness; what is Prajna, that is Prana. Together they live in this body, and together they go out of it. This is the proof, this is the understanding thereof.

**8** When a man is thus asleep, and sees no dream, he becomes one with that Prana alone. Then speech goes to him with all names, the eye with all forms, the ear with all sounds, the mind with all thoughts. And when he awakes, then, as from a burning fire sparks proceed in all directions, thus from that Self the pranas proceed, each towards its place; from the pranas the gods, from the gods the worlds.

**9** Of this, this is the proof, this is the understanding. As long as Prana dwells in this body, so long surely there is life. When Prana leaves this body, together with all the pranas it goes out. This is the proof.

**10** Speech goes into Prana, the eye goes into Prana, the ear goes into Prana, the mind goes into Prana; for Prana indeed absorbs them all.

**11** Now Prana indeed is Prajna, the conscious Self, bliss, undecaying, immortal. He does not increase by a good action, nor decrease by a bad action. For he, Prana, the conscious Self, makes him whom he wishes to lead up from these worlds do a good deed; and the same makes him whom he wishes to lead down do a bad deed.

**12** He is the guardian of the world, he is the king of the world, he is the lord of the universe; and he is my Self; thus let it be known, thus let it be known.
