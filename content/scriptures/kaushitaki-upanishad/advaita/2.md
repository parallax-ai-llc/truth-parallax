Shankara reads the prana contest as a preliminary teaching pointing toward the unity of Brahman. The supremacy of prana over the individual faculties demonstrates that a single principle underlies the apparent multiplicity of cognitive and sensory functions. However, prana itself is not the ultimate reality — it is a pointer toward the consciousness that animates even prana.

The pedagogical strategy is characteristic of the Upanishadic method: begin with the most obvious vital function (breath), demonstrate its priority over the senses, and then lead the student to recognize the pure consciousness that is prior to breath itself. This preparatory teaching sets the stage for the deeper identification of self with Brahman in later chapters.
