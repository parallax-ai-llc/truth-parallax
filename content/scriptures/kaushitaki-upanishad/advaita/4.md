For Advaita, Chapter 4's inner *agnihotra* represents the highest stage of ritual understanding: the recognition that the true sacrifice is the offering of the ego into the fire of self-knowledge. External ritual is superseded not by abandonment but by internalization — the fires are within, the offerings are the breath, and the result is the knowledge of Brahman.

Shankara's tradition sees this teaching as confirming the Upanishadic trajectory from ritual (*karma*) through worship (*upasana*) to knowledge (*jnana*). The inner *agnihotra* belongs to the middle stage — it transcends external ritual but has not yet reached the direct knowledge of the non-dual self. Its value lies in purifying the mind and preparing it for the final recognition.
