Shankara treats the afterlife journey of Chapter 1 as describing the path of *krama-mukti* (gradual liberation) — the posthumous ascent of those who have meditated on the conditioned Brahman (*saguna*). These souls reach Brahma-loka and eventually attain liberation when the world-cycle ends. However, Shankara insists that the knower of the unconditioned Brahman (*nirguna*) does not travel any path at all — such a person is immediately liberated at death because there is nowhere to go; Brahman is already everywhere.

The chapter thus illustrates two levels of teaching: for those not yet ready for the highest truth, the afterlife journey provides a consoling and motivating narrative; for the mature seeker, the journey itself is transcended through direct knowledge of the non-dual self.
