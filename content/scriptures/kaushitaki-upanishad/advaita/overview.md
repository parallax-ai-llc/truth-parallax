## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Kaushitaki Upanishad contributes to the cumulative Upanishadic teaching that Brahman — the non-dual absolute — is the sole reality behind all appearances. The identification of *prana* with *prajna* (consciousness) in Indra's teaching demonstrates that the vital force and awareness are ultimately one, both being expressions of Brahman.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara's tradition reads the supremacy of *prana* as a pedagogical device: breath is closer to the self than the external senses, and thus serves as a stepping stone to the recognition of pure consciousness. The soul's journey after death (Chapter 1) describes the path of gradual liberation (*krama-mukti*) for those who have meditated on the conditioned Brahman (*saguna*). The ultimate teaching, however, is the direct recognition of the unconditioned self.
