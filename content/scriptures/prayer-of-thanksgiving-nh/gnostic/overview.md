## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Prayer of Thanksgiving provides rare and precious evidence that Hermetism was not merely a philosophical school but a living religious community with liturgical practices. The prayer's gratitude for the gift of gnosis — direct knowledge of God — echoes the fundamental Gnostic conviction that salvation comes through knowing rather than believing. The communal dimension — the embrace and the shared vegetarian meal — reveals that Hermetic gnosis was not solitary but communal, not purely intellectual but embodied in ritual practice.
