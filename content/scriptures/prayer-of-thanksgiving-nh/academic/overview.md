## Overview

The Prayer of Thanksgiving is critically important for understanding the social reality of Hermetic religion. The scribal note describing the post-prayer ritual — an embrace followed by a vegetarian meal ('holy food which has no blood in it') — provides one of the only pieces of direct evidence for Hermetic communal practice. A Latin parallel in Asclepius 41 confirms the text's relationship to the broader Hermetic tradition and allows scholars to compare the Coptic and Latin versions for evidence of textual transmission.
