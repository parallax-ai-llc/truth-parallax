**1** When they had said these things in the prayer, they embraced each other, and they went to eat their holy food, which has no blood in it.

**2** And the prayer which they spoke was this: We give thanks to Thee, O Most High, for by Thy grace we have received the light of knowledge. The name that is honored, the name that is blessed, we give thanks to Thee.

**3** We give thanks to Thee, for Thou hast taught us about Thyself. We give thanks to Thee, for through the Son Thou hast made us know Thyself.

**4** We give thanks, O Father, who art above every name that is named. Thou art the one who exists, the Aeon of Aeons, the God of gods, the Lord of lords.

**5** We thank Thee for the knowledge that Thou hast given us, for the vision of Thyself, for the gnosis of Thy truth. We praise Thee, having no other prayer than this: that we may be preserved in the knowledge of Thee.

**6** And this is the one protection that we desire: that we not stumble in this kind of life. Having finished the prayer and the thanksgiving, they turned to their holy meal and to their holy day, practicing purity not of the body alone but of the spirit.

**7** This is the Prayer of Thanksgiving from the Nag Hammadi Library. Written in the grace of the Father, the Son, and the Spirit of Truth.
