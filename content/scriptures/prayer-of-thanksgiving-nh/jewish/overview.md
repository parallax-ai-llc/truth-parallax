## Overview

Jewish readers find in the Prayer of Thanksgiving parallels to the Jewish tradition of berakhah (blessing) — the practice of offering thanks to God before and after eating. The communal meal setting resonates with the Jewish Kiddush, the Passover Seder, and the rabbinic ideal of fellowship at table (the saying attributed to Rabbi Simeon: 'Three who have eaten at one table and have spoken words of Torah are as if they have eaten from the table of God,' Pirke Avot 3:3). The vegetarian character of the meal may reflect Jewish laws about blood consumption (Leviticus 17:10-14).
