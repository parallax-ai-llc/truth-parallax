## Ghazals on the Heart's Journey

**1.** My heart said: "I desire to learn the mysteries of the world." I said: "First learn the mystery of yourself, for that is the key to all other mysteries."

**2.** The heart is a wanderer. It travels from desire to desire, from love to love, from city to city, seeking a home it has never seen but has always known.

**3.** Give your heart to the one who has already stolen it, for the thief and the owner are the same.

**4.** I sent my heart on a journey and told it: "Go find the Beloved." It came back empty-handed and said: "The Beloved was here all along. It was I who was absent."

**5.** The heart that has not been broken is like an uncut gem—it has potential but no radiance. Only the chisel of suffering can reveal its light.

**6.** Every heart contains a garden and a graveyard. In the garden grow the flowers of love; in the graveyard lie the bones of attachment.

**7.** A wise man cultivates the garden and visits the graveyard with equanimity, knowing that both are necessary.

**8.** The heart is wider than the sky and deeper than the ocean. Yet it is small enough to be broken by a single word.

**9.** Do not harden your heart against the world, for a hard heart cannot receive the rain of grace. Be soft, like the earth in spring.

**10.** They asked Hafez: "Where do you live?" He replied: "In the space between two heartbeats, where the Beloved whispers and the world is silent."

**11.** The journey of the heart is the only journey that matters. All other journeys are the movement of the body from place to place—the heart alone can travel from self to God.

**12.** When the heart arrives at its destination, it discovers that it never left. The journey was the illusion; the arrival was always.

**13.** O heart, you have wandered through a thousand markets, buying things that rust and rot. Come home, for the one thing that neither rusts nor rots is waiting for you there.

**14.** The heart that loves is never lost, even when it wanders. For love is the compass that always points to the Beloved.

**15.** Hafez, follow your heart, for it knows the way. The mind calculates, but the heart navigates.
