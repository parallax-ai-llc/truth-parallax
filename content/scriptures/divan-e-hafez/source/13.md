## Ghazals on Wisdom and Folly

**1.** The wise man sits in the corner of the tavern and says nothing. The fool stands on the rooftop and shouts his wisdom to the world.

**2.** True wisdom is the knowledge that you know nothing. False wisdom is the belief that you know everything.

**3.** I went to the scholars and asked: "What is the meaning of life?" They gave me a hundred answers, and none of them agreed. I went to the drunkard and asked the same question. He laughed and poured me a cup of wine. That was the best answer.

**4.** The sage who speaks in riddles is not trying to confuse you—he is trying to wake you up. The straight answer puts the mind to sleep; the riddle forces it to open its eyes.

**5.** A donkey loaded with books is still a donkey. Learning without understanding is a burden, not a blessing.

**6.** The fool builds a tower and calls it wisdom. The wise man sits beneath a tree and calls it shade.

**7.** I have learned more from my mistakes than from my successes. My successes taught me arrogance; my mistakes taught me humility.

**8.** They asked a sage: "What is the greatest folly?" He replied: "To think that you are wise." They asked: "What is the greatest wisdom?" He replied: "To know that you are a fool."

**9.** The river that thinks it knows its destination flows straight into the desert. The river that surrenders to the landscape finds its way to the sea.

**10.** O Hafez, do not boast of your knowledge of the Quran, for many who memorize the Book have forgotten its meaning.

**11.** The bird that sings in the cage has learned the song of captivity. The bird that sings in the field has learned the song of freedom. Both are songs, but they are not the same.

**12.** Wisdom is not the accumulation of knowledge—it is the subtraction of certainty, until only wonder remains.

**13.** The greatest scholar I ever met said: "After fifty years of study, I have arrived at the place where I began: I know that I know nothing, and I am at peace with that."

**14.** Be wary of the man who claims to have all the answers, for he has not yet heard the question that will silence him.

**15.** Hafez, be foolish in the eyes of the world, for the wisdom of the world is foolishness before God, and the foolishness of God is wiser than all the wisdom of men.
