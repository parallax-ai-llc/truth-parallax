## Ghazals on the Tavern and the Wine-Bearer

**1.** Rise, O cup-bearer, and hand the cup around, for love at first seemed easy, but now difficulties abound.

**2.** The breeze of dawn has opened the knot of the musk-pod—what a commotion rises in the hearts of the lovers!

**3.** Bring wine, for the foundation of repentance is as weak as the vow of those who build upon the wind.

**4.** I am not the only one whose heart is caught in those dark tresses; what a long chain of captives this moon-faced one drags behind!

**5.** Last night the angel of the secret tavern knocked at the door. He shaped a cup from the clay of Adam and poured the wine of ecstasy into it.

**6.** The dwellers of heaven could not bear the trust of divine love, and the lot of this madness fell upon me, poor distracted one.

**7.** Seventy-two sects are at war with one another—since they could not see the truth, they have struck the door of fable.

**8.** Thank God that between me and the Beloved there is peace. The dancing Sufis are celebrating the union.

**9.** The candle laughs with fire at every dawn, but the butterfly alone knows the meaning of that flame—the rest are mere spectators.

**10.** Pour the wine and cast aside all idle talk. The rose has bloomed, and the nightingale is drunk with song.

**11.** Not all who carry the prayer-rug are worshippers of God; beneath many a pious cloak hides a heart blacker than night.

**12.** I saw the wise men of the city with their ledgers and their scrolls—they knew the price of everything and the value of nothing.

**13.** In the tavern of the Magians, what do they see in us? The light of truth burns in our cup while their lanterns flicker with self-deception.

**14.** Let us not speak of the defects of others, for we have defects of our own that would fill the pages of a book.

**15.** Hafez, if the wine-bearer offers thee a cup, take it—for this moment will not come again.
