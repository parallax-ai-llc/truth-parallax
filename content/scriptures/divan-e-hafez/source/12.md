## Ghazals on Spring and Renewal

**1.** The spring has come! The rose has opened its petals and the meadow is green again. Come out of your house, for the world has been reborn.

**2.** The bird that was silent all winter has found its voice. The stream that was frozen has begun to flow. The tree that was bare has put on its green garment.

**3.** Bring wine, for the season of austerity is over, and the season of joy has begun. The preacher may frown, but the garden laughs.

**4.** Every spring is a resurrection. The earth that seemed dead reveals that it was only sleeping.

**5.** So it is with the soul: what you think is dead in you is only sleeping, waiting for the spring of awakening to call it forth.

**6.** The tulip said to the morning: "I bloomed in a single night." The morning replied: "But you labored in the dark all winter."

**7.** Nothing blooms without preparation. The seed must split, the root must push through stone, and the stem must bear the weight of the bud before the flower appears.

**8.** Be patient in your winter, for spring is certain. The one who planted the seed of your soul knows exactly when to send the rain.

**9.** They say the New Year begins in spring, and they are right. But the truest new year begins when the heart renews itself—and that can happen in any season.

**10.** The garden does not bloom for itself—it blooms for the one who walks through it. So bloom for others, and your fragrance will return to you on the breeze.

**11.** I saw an old tree that everyone thought was dead. But in spring, a single green shoot appeared. And the people said: "It is alive!" It had been alive all along; they were too blind to see it.

**12.** So it is with the mystic: the world declares him dead, but in the spring of eternity, he is revealed to be the most alive of all.

**13.** O Saki, the garden is in bloom and the wind is sweet. Why do we sit here in the dark? Pour the wine and open the door.

**14.** Every atom of creation is shouting: "I am alive! I am awake!" Only the heedless cannot hear this shout.

**15.** Hafez, it is spring—cast off the heavy cloak of winter-sorrow and dance in the green meadow of the Beloved's mercy.
