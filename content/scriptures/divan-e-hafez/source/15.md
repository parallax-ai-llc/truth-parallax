## Ghazals of Farewell and Eternity

**1.** O friends, the time of farewell has come. The caravan is departing, and the bell has sounded.

**2.** We have drunk from the same cup and shared the same road. Now the road divides, and each must go his own way.

**3.** But remember: all roads lead to the same destination. The mountain has many paths, but the summit is one.

**4.** I leave you these poems as a traveler leaves footprints in the sand. The wind may erase them, but the journey was real.

**5.** Do not mourn the poet when the poems remain. The nightingale dies, but the song lives on in every garden.

**6.** When you read these words, know that a heart beat behind them, a hand trembled writing them, and an eye wept the ink that formed them.

**7.** I was a tongue for the Beloved, nothing more. If the words were beautiful, the beauty was not mine—it was His.

**8.** The rose does not take credit for its fragrance, nor the moon for its light. I do not take credit for these poems, for they passed through me like water through a channel.

**9.** When I am gone, do not look for me in the graveyard. Look for me in the verses that made your heart stir.

**10.** The poet's grave is the page. The poet's resurrection is the reader's eye that falls upon the page and brings the words to life again.

**11.** Eternity is not the continuation of time—it is the depth of a single moment. One moment of true love is worth more than a thousand years of existence.

**12.** I have seen empires rise and fall, and through it all, the only thing that remained was the love between the lover and the Beloved.

**13.** This love has no beginning and no end. It was before the world was created, and it will be after the world is dust.

**14.** O Beloved, I return to Thee as the river returns to the sea—not diminished but fulfilled, not ending but arriving.

**15.** Hafez has said his final word. Now let silence speak, for silence is the truest language of the Beloved, and all else is translation.
