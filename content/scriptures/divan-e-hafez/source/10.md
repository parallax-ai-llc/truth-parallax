## Ghazals on Union and Ecstasy

**1.** Last night, the Beloved came to me in a dream and said: "Why do you seek me? I have been with you all along."

**2.** I said: "But I have searched the world for you!" The Beloved said: "You were looking in the wrong direction. You were looking outward when I was within."

**3.** The eye that sees the Beloved is not the eye of the head—it is the eye of the heart. Close the first, and the second will open.

**4.** When the union comes, all words cease. The pen breaks, the ink dries, and the page is left blank, for the blank page says more than all the words in the world.

**5.** In the moment of union, the candle and the moth are one flame. The drop and the ocean are one sea. The lover and the Beloved are one being.

**6.** I was a drop, and I fell into the sea. Now I cannot find the drop, for it has become the sea.

**7.** Do not ask me where I went, for I have gone nowhere. Do not ask me what I found, for I have found nothing. I simply ceased to be separate, and that was everything.

**8.** In the tavern of union, there are no cups and no wine—there is only the intoxication, and it has no end.

**9.** The sufi dances not to entertain the onlookers but to shatter the cage of the self. When the cage is shattered, the bird of the soul flies free.

**10.** I was once a scholar and knew many things. Then love came, and I forgot them all. And in that forgetting, I found the one thing worth knowing.

**11.** They asked me: "What is the one thing?" I could not answer, for the one thing has no name. Call it love, call it God, call it the Beloved—all names are fingers pointing at the moon, but none of them is the moon.

**12.** In the circle of the lovers, there is no first and no last. The circle has no beginning, and the dance has no end.

**13.** Ecstasy is not the opposite of sorrow—it is sorrow transformed. The grapes that are crushed become wine. The heart that is broken becomes a door.

**14.** When ecstasy takes you, do not resist. Let it carry you where it will. The river that resists the current breaks its banks; the river that surrenders reaches the sea.

**15.** Hafez, the night of union is brief, but its memory lights a thousand nights of separation.
