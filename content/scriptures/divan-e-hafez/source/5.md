## Ghazals on Fate and the Turn of Fortune

**1.** This too shall pass—so be not proud in prosperity, nor despair in adversity.

**2.** The wheel of fortune turns, and he who is below today shall be above tomorrow, and he who is above today shall be below.

**3.** Last night I took my troubles to the old wise man of the tavern. He held a mirror before me and said: "Look."

**4.** I looked, and saw in the mirror the entire universe, and every being in it turning upon the wheel of fate.

**5.** He said: "The wine-cup is round, and fortune is round—and the one who drinks from one drinks from the other."

**6.** The cloud does not ask the earth: "Shall I rain upon you?" Nor does the earth ask the cloud: "Shall you rain?" It rains, and the earth accepts. This is the way of heaven.

**7.** Do not curse thy fate, for fate is the hand of God upon the chessboard of the world, and every move has its purpose.

**8.** A king was dethroned and made a prisoner. He said: "Yesterday I held the world in my hand. Today the world holds me." And he laughed, for he had understood the joke.

**9.** The moth does not complain of the flame, nor the rose of the thorn. Each knows its destiny and embraces it.

**10.** If fate brings thee a lemon, do not ask for sugar. Drink the bitter juice, for it purifies the blood.

**11.** They asked a gambler: "Why do you play when you always lose?" He replied: "Because the next throw may change everything."

**12.** So it is with life: the next morning may bring what all the previous mornings have denied.

**13.** The pen of fate has written, and what is written cannot be unwritten. But the ink is the ink of mercy, and the hand that wrote is the hand of Love.

**14.** Be not anxious about tomorrow, for today is the only day you possess. The bird that worries about the storm of tomorrow misses the sunshine of today.

**15.** Hafez, surrender to the turning of the wheel, for resistance only increases the pain. The leaf that lets go of the branch flies freely in the wind.
