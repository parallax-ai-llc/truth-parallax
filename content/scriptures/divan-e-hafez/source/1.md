## Ghazals on the Wine of Love

**1.** Come, Saki, hand me the cup and pour the wine, for love at first seemed easy, but then the difficulties fell.

**2.** By the scent of the musk which the breeze shall scatter from those dark tresses, what blood dripped in the heart from the twisting curls of the beloved!

**3.** Stain thy prayer-carpet with wine, if the Magian elder bids thee, for the traveler is not ignorant of the ways and customs of the stages of the journey.

**4.** In the station of the beloved, what safety is there for us, when every moment the bell cries: "Bind on the loads!"

**5.** The dark night, and the fear of the wave, and the terrible whirlpool—how can they know our state, those who go light along the shore?

**6.** All my work has brought me to ill repute, yet how can the secret remain hidden when every gathering speaks of it?

**7.** If thou desirest the presence of the Beloved, O Hafez, do not absent thyself. When thou meetest thy Beloved, abandon the world and let it go.

**8.** Last night, from the cypress of the garden, I heard the bulbul singing: "What happened to the pledge of the red rose? Where is the promise of spring?"

**9.** O heart, do not complain of the cruelty of the Beloved, for the fault is thine own—complain of thyself.

**10.** If the Holy Spirit should once again lend its assistance, others too will do what Christ has done.

**11.** I am the slave of the will of the Master of the tavern, whose grace has freed me from every bondage.

**12.** What concern have I with virtue and sin? I have thrown myself upon His mercy, and His mercy has no end.

**13.** Come to the tavern of ruin, that you may know the value of the wine of love, for in the cloister of the pious you will find only the dregs of hypocrisy.

**14.** All the piety of the ascetic is not worth one drop of the wine that the drunkard spills upon the dust.

**15.** Hafez, drink wine, be joyful, and sin—but do not make deception and hypocrisy thy craft, as others do.
