## Ghazals on Beauty and Transience

**1.** The rose blooms for five days and no more. Do not set your heart upon it, for even the garden mourns its passing.

**2.** The beauty of the moon is that it wanes. The beauty of the flower is that it falls. The beauty of life is that it ends.

**3.** If beauty were permanent, there would be no love, for love is born in the shadow of loss.

**4.** Yesterday I sat by the stream and watched the water pass. Not a single drop returned. So it is with the hours of our life.

**5.** The caravan of life passes swiftly. Seize the moment of joy, for it will not come again.

**6.** This handful of dust we call the body was once the face of a beauty, the arm of a warrior, the brow of a sage. All of them are dust now.

**7.** The king and the beggar lie in the same earth. The grave knows no distinction between silk and sackcloth.

**8.** O thou who art proud of thy youth and thy beauty, look at the graveyard and consider. Many a cheek softer than the rose lies beneath the clay.

**9.** They brought me to the garden of the world and said: "Be happy." But how can I be happy in a garden from which I must soon depart?

**10.** Yet there is a beauty that does not fade—the beauty of the soul. The beauty of the face is the flame of a candle; the beauty of the soul is the light of the sun.

**11.** A wise man said: "I have seen the most beautiful woman in the world grow old and wrinkled. But I have seen the soul of a saint grow more beautiful with every passing year."

**12.** The nightingale does not grieve when the rose withers, for it knows that spring will come again. So should we not grieve at the passing of beauty, for the Beloved is eternal.

**13.** Every beautiful thing in this world is a reflection of the One Beauty. Do not cling to the reflection—seek the Source.

**14.** The face of the Beloved is the mirror of all beauty. What you love in the rose, in the moon, in the eyes of the beloved—all of it comes from that one Face.

**15.** Hafez, do not mourn the passing of the flower. Rejoice that it bloomed at all, for not every seed is given the grace to flower.
