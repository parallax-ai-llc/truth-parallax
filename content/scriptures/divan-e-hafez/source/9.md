## Ghazals on the Nightingale and the Rose

**1.** The nightingale sang to the rose at dawn: "O rose, your beauty has stolen my heart. I die at your feet."

**2.** The rose replied: "I am beautiful, it is true, but no lover has ever suffered as much from my beauty as I suffer from the brevity of my life."

**3.** "You, O nightingale, will sing for a hundred roses after I am gone. But I shall bloom only once and then be dust."

**4.** The nightingale said: "I do not sing for beauty alone—I sing for the beauty that is passing, for that is the sweetest beauty of all."

**5.** In the garden of existence, every creature plays its part. The rose blooms, the nightingale sings, the thorn protects, and the gardener watches.

**6.** But the garden belongs to none of them. It belongs to the One who planted it, and He alone knows why each thing is as it is.

**7.** Last night the nightingale said to the bud: "Open, for the night is passing." The bud replied: "I will open when the sun commands me, not when the nightingale asks."

**8.** Even so, the heart opens not at the command of the lover, but at the command of the Beloved.

**9.** The rose said: "I am red with the blood of lovers who have died at my feet." The nightingale said: "And I am hoarse with singing the praises of those who have died for love."

**10.** In this garden, the blood of the lover becomes the color of the rose, and the tears of the lover become the dew upon its petals.

**11.** O nightingale, sing on, for though the rose may wither, your song endures. And in every garden, in every age, a new rose will bloom to hear your ancient melody.

**12.** The cypress stands silent in the garden, making no complaint and asking for nothing. Yet it is the cypress that endures while the rose comes and goes.

**13.** Learn from the cypress: stand tall, be silent, ask for nothing, and you will outlast all the roses of the world.

**14.** But learn from the rose also: bloom fully, hide nothing, give your fragrance to all, even though your time is brief.

**15.** Hafez, be both the nightingale and the rose—sing with all your heart, and bloom with all your soul, for the garden will not wait.
