## Ghazals on the Beloved

**1.** If that beauty of Shiraz would take my heart in hand, for the dark mole on that cheek I would give all of Samarkand and Bukhara.

**2.** O Saki, bring me the wine that remains, for in Paradise thou wilt not find the banks of the Ruknabad or the rose-garden of Musalla.

**3.** Alas, those roving dervishes, lovely and full of mischief, have stolen patience from the heart, as the Turks plunder a feast.

**4.** The beauty of the beloved needs not our imperfect love. What need has the lovely face of rouge and mole and paint?

**5.** By the grace of that increasing beauty with which Joseph was endowed, I knew that Love would bring Zuleikha out of the veils of modesty.

**6.** Even if you speak bitterly to me, I pray for your sweet lips, for the bitter answer well befits the ruby lip.

**7.** O minstrel, sing on—for in the counsel of thy song I find the work of yesterday's evening, and the trace of this morning's promise.

**8.** Hafez, speak of wine and the minstrel, and seek not the mysteries of the world, for no one has solved, nor shall solve, this riddle by means of wisdom.

**9.** My heart is ablaze—where is the wine? Bring the cup quickly, for the fire in my soul has turned the harvest of patience to ash.

**10.** That Friend by whom our house was illumined has departed—God bring back our absent Friend!

**11.** My heart is weary of hypocrisy and the burden of pretense. Where is the tavern, that I may take refuge in the purity of wine?

**12.** The garden is beautiful, but the companionship of the friend is more beautiful still. Bring wine, for the spring will not last, and the garden will not always bloom.

**13.** Do not judge us, O preacher, for the cup we drink and the song we sing are prayers in a language you have not learned.

**14.** The Beloved's lane is narrow, and the rivals are many. What shall I do? The road is long and my feet are tired.

**15.** Though the destination seems far, do not despair, Hafez, for there is no road that has no end.
