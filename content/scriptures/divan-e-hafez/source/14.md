## Ghazals on the Cup-Bearer and the Wine

**1.** O Saki, pour the wine of dawn, for the night has been long and the heart is weary.

**2.** The wine I seek is not the wine of the grape—it is the wine of the spirit, pressed from the grapes of eternity.

**3.** One sip of this wine and the scholar forgets his books, the king forgets his throne, and the lover forgets his sorrow.

**4.** The cup-bearer of heaven pours this wine into the cup of the heart. You cannot buy it, you cannot earn it—you can only receive it.

**5.** And the condition of receiving it is to be empty. The cup that is already full cannot receive another drop.

**6.** I went to the mosque and it was closed. I went to the tavern and it was open. From this I learned where God resides.

**7.** The wine of love makes the blind see and the mute speak. It turns the coward into a lion and the fool into a sage.

**8.** But it also turns the sage into a fool, and this is its greatest gift—for the fool of God is wiser than the sage of the world.

**9.** Pour the wine, O Saki, and do not ask whether it is lawful or forbidden. In the court of love, all laws are suspended.

**10.** The wine that I drink does not intoxicate the body—it liberates the soul. The drunkard of God stumbles through the world with more grace than the sober man of the world walks through his house.

**11.** A preacher said to me: "Wine is forbidden." I replied: "By whom? The One who created the grape and the vine and the sun that ripened them?"

**12.** Do not mistake the symbol for the thing itself. The wine is the love of God, the cup is the heart, and the cup-bearer is the Beloved.

**13.** Drink deeply, for the night is passing and the cup-bearer will not wait forever.

**14.** Every age has its cup-bearer. In one age it is a prophet, in another a poet, in another a madman. But the wine is always the same wine.

**15.** Hafez, drink and be silent, for the wine speaks louder than words, and the silence of the drunkard is more eloquent than the sermon of the preacher.
