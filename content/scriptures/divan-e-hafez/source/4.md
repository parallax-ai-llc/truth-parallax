## Ghazals on Longing and Separation

**1.** O morning breeze, if thou shouldst pass by the garden of the beloved, deliver to her my greetings, and carry back to me the fragrance of her hair.

**2.** Say to her: "O thou who art the candle of the assembly of beauty, for God's sake, remember those who burn like moths around thy flame."

**3.** Since thou art gone, the garden has no color and the nightingale has forgotten its song. Come back, for without thee the world is a prison.

**4.** My heart has become narrow from the pain of separation. O Saki, bring the wide-mouthed cup that I may find relief!

**5.** Every night I make the pillow wet with my tears, and every morning I rise and find that the beloved has not returned.

**6.** I asked the wind: "Where is the beloved?" The wind said: "I passed by her garden and wept, for even the wind cannot reach her."

**7.** The candle weeps all night, not because it is sad, but because it is separated from the moth and cannot call it back.

**8.** They said: "Forget her." I said: "Tell the river to forget the sea, tell the branch to forget the root, tell the moth to forget the flame."

**9.** Separation is the cruelest teacher, but it teaches the deepest lesson: that union is not the meeting of two bodies, but the longing of two souls.

**10.** If I could send my soul to thee on the wings of the morning breeze, I would send it gladly, and it would arrive before the dawn.

**11.** The dove that is caged sings sweeter than the dove that is free, for the caged dove sings of what it has lost, and there is no sweeter music than the music of longing.

**12.** Without thee, the garden of the world is empty. The roses bloom but give no fragrance. The wine is poured but has no taste.

**13.** How strange that the heart, which is no larger than a fist, can contain an ocean of longing that the whole world cannot hold.

**14.** I am patient in this separation, for I know that the night, however long, will end, and the morning will bring the face of the Beloved.

**15.** Hafez, weep not, for thy tears are the rain that waters the garden of reunion.
