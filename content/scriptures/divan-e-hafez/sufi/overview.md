The Sufi tradition receives Hafez as the *lisanu'l-ghayb* — the 'Tongue of the Unseen' — whose ghazals articulate the deepest mysteries of the mystical path in the most beautiful language ever fashioned. For the Sufi, every image in Hafez's poetry operates on multiple levels simultaneously: the wine is divine knowledge, the tavern is the Sufi gathering (*majlis*), the beloved is God, the saqi (cupbearer) is the spiritual master, and the rend (libertine) is the authentic seeker who has abandoned the pretenses of conventional piety for the intoxication of direct encounter with the Real.

Hafez's Divan functions in Persian-speaking Sufi communities as a sacred text in its own right, consulted through the practice of *fal-e Hafez* — opening the book at random to receive divine guidance. This oracular function testifies to the Sufi conviction that Hafez's poetry is not merely human artistry but a channel of divine communication, each ghazal containing inexhaustible layers of meaning that reveal themselves according to the spiritual capacity of the reader.
