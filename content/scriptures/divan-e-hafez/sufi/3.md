## Sufi Reading

Hafez's descriptions of the beloved's beauty are read by the Sufi as *tajalli* — the self-disclosure of divine beauty through the mirror of creation. Every curl, mole, and glance described in these ghazals is a symbol of divine attributes: the beloved's face reflects the light of divine *jamal* (beauty), the tresses represent the mysteries that veil the divine essence, and the mole (*khal*) is the point of divine unity from which all multiplicity emanates. The Sufi's contemplation of beauty is thus a spiritual practice — a form of *mushahada* (witnessing) that trains the eye to see God in all things.
