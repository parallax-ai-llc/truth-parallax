## Sufi Reading

Hafez's love for Shiraz — its gardens, rivers, climate, and people — is read by the Sufi as a model of how the mystic can love the particular without losing sight of the universal. The earthly Shiraz is a reflection of the heavenly paradise, and Hafez's refusal to exchange it for Samarkand and Bukhara illustrates the Sufi teaching that the Divine is encountered not in some distant spiritual realm but in the intimate, concrete, beloved particulars of one's own life and place.
