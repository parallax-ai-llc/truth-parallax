## Sufi Reading

Hafez's reflections on fate and fortune are read through the Sufi concept of *tawakkul* (trust in divine providence). The turning of fortune's wheel represents the ever-changing surface of existence beneath which the unchanging divine will operates. The Sufi finds in Hafez's acceptance of fate not fatalism but the profound serenity of one who has surrendered personal will to the divine decree. Hafez's famous line 'Trust in God and do the good' encapsulates the Sufi integration of active moral effort with passive acceptance of whatever comes.
