## Sufi Reading

The tavern (*maikhaneh*) in Hafez's poetry is the Sufi's sacred space of encounter with the Divine — the *khanqah* or mystical gathering where the wine of gnosis is poured by the master. Wine (*may*) represents the knowledge of divine unity (*tawhid*) that intoxicates the rational mind and dissolves the illusion of separation between self and God. The Sufi reads these poems as celebrations of *sukr* (spiritual intoxication), the ecstatic state in which conventional distinctions between lawful and forbidden, sacred and profane, self and other, dissolve in the overwhelming experience of divine presence.
