## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reads Hafez's ghazals on divine love as direct expressions of the mystic's experience of *ishq-e haqiqi* — true love directed toward the Divine Beloved. The longing that pervades these poems is the soul's primordial homesickness for its divine source, a theme rooted in the Qur'anic primordial covenant (*mithaq*) when all souls testified to God's lordship. Hafez's lover, consumed by desire for the absent Beloved, embodies the Sufi seeker at the station of *shawq* (longing), where the pain of separation is itself a form of intimacy with the One who is both infinitely near and infinitely beyond.
