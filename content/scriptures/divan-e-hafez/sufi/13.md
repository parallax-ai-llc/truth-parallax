## Sufi Reading

Hafez's elaborate descriptions of the beloved's hair, mole, and other features constitute a symbolic vocabulary (*rumuz*) that the Sufi tradition has decoded with great precision. The hair (*zulf*) represents the divine mysteries and the multiplicity that veils unity; the mole (*khal*) is the point of divine unity; the eyebrow (*abru*) is the arch of divine decree; the lip (*lab*) is the source of the water of life. The Sufi's contemplation of these images is a meditative practice that trains the inner eye to perceive spiritual realities through material forms.
