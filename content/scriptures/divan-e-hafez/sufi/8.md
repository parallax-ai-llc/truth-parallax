## Sufi Reading

The garden in Hafez's poetry is the inner garden of the heart — the spiritual landscape that blooms when the winter of ego-consciousness gives way to the spring of divine love. The Sufi reads the roses, nightingales, and flowing streams of Hafez's garden imagery as symbols of the states and stations of the mystical path: the rose is divine beauty, the nightingale is the lover's soul singing in ecstatic praise, and the garden itself is the paradise available to the purified heart in this very life.
