## Comparative Reading

Hafez's nocturnal ghazals connect with a cross-cultural tradition of night as a privileged time of spiritual encounter: the Hebrew psalmist's night watches, the Christian monastic *vigils*, the Buddhist midnight meditation, and the Hindu *sandhya* (twilight) prayers. Darkness, across traditions, creates the conditions for a mode of knowing that daylight consciousness cannot access.
