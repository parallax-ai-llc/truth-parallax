## Comparative Reading

Hafez's critique of religious hypocrisy finds parallels across traditions: Jesus's denunciation of the Pharisees, the Buddhist critique of mere scholasticism, Kabir's attacks on Hindu and Muslim formalism, Kierkegaard's assault on Christendom, and the Zen tradition's irreverent approach to institutional Buddhism. The cross-cultural recurrence of this critique suggests a structural tension in all organized religions between the institution and the spiritual experience it claims to mediate.
