## Comparative Reading

Hafez's praise of spiritual poverty invites comparison with the Franciscan ideal, Buddhist *nekkhamma* (renunciation), the Daoist sage's simplicity, the Cynics' rejection of convention, and the Hindu *vanaprastha* (forest-dwelling) stage. Comparative analysis reveals that the renunciation of worldly goods in favor of spiritual freedom is one of the most persistent themes in human religious thought.
