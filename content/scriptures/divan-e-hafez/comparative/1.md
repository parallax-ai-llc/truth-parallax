## Comparative Reading

Comparative study places Hafez's love ghazals alongside the world's great traditions of love poetry: Sappho's fragments, Catullus's lyrics, the Hebrew Song of Songs, Rumi's love poetry, the troubadour *canso*, Shakespeare's sonnets, and the Japanese *waka*. Each tradition develops distinctive conventions for expressing the experience of love, and Hafez's characteristic ambiguity between human and divine love represents a unique contribution to this global conversation.
