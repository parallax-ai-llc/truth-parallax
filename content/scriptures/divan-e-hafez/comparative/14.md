## Comparative Reading

Hafez's poems on death and remembrance connect with the universal human concern for meaning in the face of mortality. Comparative analysis places his confidence in poetry's immortality alongside the Egyptian desire for eternal fame, Horace's *exegi monumentum*, the Hindu concept of *chiranjeevi* (the immortal), and the Buddhist teaching that enlightened actions create enduring benefit. Each tradition offers a distinctive response to the challenge of death.
