## Comparative Reading

Hafez's Shiraz poems invite comparison with other poets' celebrations of their beloved cities: Cavafy's Alexandria, Neruda's Valparaiso, Whitman's Manhattan, Basho's Edo, and Joyce's Dublin. Comparative analysis examines how the poet's attachment to place becomes a vehicle for expressing identity, spiritual vision, and the relationship between the particular and the universal.
