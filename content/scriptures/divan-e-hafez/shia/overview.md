For the Shia world, and for Iran in particular, Hafez is not merely a great poet but a national cultural treasure whose verses are woven into the fabric of daily life. The *Hafeziyeh* — his tomb and memorial garden in Shiraz — is one of Iran's most visited sites, a place of pilgrimage where visitors perform *fal-e Hafez* (bibliomancy) seeking guidance for life's dilemmas. Hafez's poetry is recited at weddings, Nowruz celebrations, and Yalda Night gatherings, functioning as a secular scripture of Iranian identity.

The Shia reading of Hafez draws on the *irfan* (gnosis) tradition that has flourished within Shia Islam, interpreting his celebration of wine, love, and beauty as expressions of mystical insight transmitted through the spiritual lineage of the Imams. Hafez's critique of religious hypocrisy resonates with the Shia experience of oppression by outwardly pious rulers.
