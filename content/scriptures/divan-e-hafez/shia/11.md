## Shia Reading

The Shia reading of renunciation connects it to the exemplary poverty of Imam Ali, who as caliph lived with radical simplicity, and of Fatima al-Zahra, who gave away her wedding dress to a beggar. Hafez's praise of spiritual poverty over worldly wealth echoes the Ahl al-Bayt's consistent choice of inner richness over external power. The *rend's* rejection of conventional respectability mirrors the Shia stance of principled opposition to unjust worldly authority.
