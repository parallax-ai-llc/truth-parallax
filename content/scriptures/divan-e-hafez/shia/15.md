## Shia Reading

Hafez's love for Shiraz carries particular significance in the Shia context as a celebration of the city that has been a center of Shia learning, art, and devotion for centuries. The Shia reader finds in Hafez's refusal to exchange Shiraz for any other place an affirmation of the value of the Shia cultural world — its poetry, its gardens, its devotional traditions — against any external claim of superiority. Shiraz, in Hafez's vision and the Shia reading, is itself a proof of the beauty and depth of Persian Shia civilization.
