Academic study of Hafez's Divan spans Persian literary criticism, Islamic cultural history, hermeneutical theory, and comparative poetics. Scholars grapple with the fundamental interpretive challenge posed by Hafez's poetry: the systematic ambiguity that allows each verse to sustain multiple, often contradictory readings. Is Hafez a Sufi mystic, a hedonistic libertine, a social critic, or a supreme literary artist who exploits all these possibilities? The scholarly debate continues, generating an enormous secondary literature.

Textual scholarship on the Divan is complicated by the absence of an autograph manuscript and the considerable variation among surviving copies. Scholars such as Qasim Ghani, Parviz Natel Khanlari, and others have produced critical editions that attempt to establish authentic texts, but significant disagreements remain about the canon of genuine Hafez ghazals.
