## Academic Reading

Academic study of Hafez's fate ghazals examines their relationship to Islamic theological discussions about predestination (*qadar*) and free will. Scholars analyze how Hafez navigates the tension between determinism and moral agency through poetic ambiguity, creating texts that can be read as endorsing either fatalism or active engagement with the world. The sociopolitical context of 14th-century Shiraz — marked by frequent changes of dynasty and political instability — is considered as a backdrop to these reflections on fortune's inconstancy.
