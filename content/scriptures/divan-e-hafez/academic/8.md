## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze Hafez's garden imagery in relation to the literary and physical garden traditions of medieval Persia. The *chahar bagh* (fourfold garden) of Islamic landscape architecture provides a material context for the poetic garden, while the Qur'anic descriptions of paradise supply a theological framework. Literary critics examine how Hafez transforms conventional garden motifs — the rose, the nightingale, the stream, the spring breeze — through his distinctive combination of sensory precision and metaphysical suggestiveness.
