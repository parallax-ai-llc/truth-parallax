## Academic Reading

Scholars examine Hafez's poems on death and remembrance in relation to the broader Islamic discourse on mortality and the medieval Persian literary tradition of elegy. Hafez's distinctive treatment — combining acceptance of death with confidence in the immortality of his art — is analyzed in relation to both Islamic theology and the classical literary topos of poetry as a guarantee of fame. The reception history of these poems confirms Hafez's prophetic confidence in his enduring legacy.
