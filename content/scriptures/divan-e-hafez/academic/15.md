## Academic Reading

Academic study of Hafez's Shiraz poems examines the poet's construction of a literary and spiritual geography centered on his beloved city. Scholars analyze how Hafez transforms the topographical *locus amoenus* — a conventional literary device — into a complex symbol that encompasses personal identity, cultural heritage, and spiritual vision. The specific references to Shiraz's landmarks — Roknabad, Mosalla, the Jafarabad garden — are examined for their historical and literary significance.
