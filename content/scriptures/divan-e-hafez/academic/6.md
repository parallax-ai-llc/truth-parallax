## Academic Reading

Hafez's critique of hypocrisy is analyzed by scholars as both a specific response to the social conditions of 14th-century Shiraz and a contribution to a long Islamic tradition of anti-hypocrisy discourse. The figures of the *zahid*, *muhtasib*, and *shaikh* in Hafez's poetry are examined as social types whose literary portrayal reveals tensions within medieval Islamic society between institutional religion and personal spirituality, between public performance and private conviction.
