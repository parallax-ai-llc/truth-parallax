The Sunni tradition values Hafez as one of the supreme artists of Islamic civilization, whose mastery of the ghazal form represents a pinnacle of literary achievement. Sunni scholars have long appreciated how Hafez's poetry draws on and transforms Qur'anic imagery, Prophetic hadith, and the vocabulary of Islamic theology and jurisprudence, creating a poetic language of extraordinary richness and resonance.

While some Sunni authorities have been uneasy with Hafez's celebration of wine and love — questioning whether these images cross the boundary from mystical metaphor into actual antinomianism — the mainstream Sunni literary tradition has embraced the Divan as a treasure of *adab* (refined culture) that demonstrates the capacity of the Islamic aesthetic tradition to create works of transcendent beauty.
