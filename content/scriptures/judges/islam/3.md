## The First Judges: Instruments of Divine Will

Islamic interpretation of Judges 3 reads the stories of Othniel, Ehud, and Shamgar as examples of God's practice of raising up righteous leaders to guide communities that have strayed — a pattern central to the Qur'anic understanding of sacred history.

## Othniel: The Righteous Leader (3:7-11)

The Othniel narrative aligns with the Islamic concept of divinely guided leadership. In Islamic theology, God raises up leaders (*a'immah*) to guide communities back to the straight path. Othniel's empowerment by God's spirit corresponds to the Qur'anic concept of divine *tawfiq* (divine enabling) granted to those who serve God faithfully.

## Ehud: The Question of Means (3:12-30)

Islamic ethics (*akhlaq*) engages with Ehud's deceptive assassination through the framework of warfare ethics:

- Islamic just war theory (*jihad*) permits stratagems in legitimate warfare
- The key question is whether the conflict itself is justified
- If the oppressor (Eglon) is an unjust tyrant, resistance may be legitimate

However, Islamic scholars note that the biblical account's moral ambiguity may reflect *tahrif* — textual corruption that attributes questionable actions to divinely guided figures. A true leader guided by God would act with clear moral authority.

## Shamgar: The Humble Instrument (3:31)

Shamgar's victory with an ox goad resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *tawakkul* (reliance on God). The Qur'an teaches that "victory is only from Allah" (3:126, 8:10), and Shamgar's unconventional weapon illustrates that divine aid, not military technology, determines outcomes.

## The Islamic Framework

Islamic engagement with these narratives emphasizes several principles:

### Divine Sovereignty
Allah alone determines the rise and fall of nations and leaders. The judges are raised up by God's will, not by their own initiative.

### The Role of Righteous Leaders
The Qur'an presents a continuous chain of prophets and righteous leaders guiding humanity. The judges fit within this framework, though their precise prophetic status is not established in Islamic tradition.

### The Conditional Nature of Blessing
Victory and peace follow obedience; oppression and suffering follow disobedience. This conditionality applies to all communities, including the *Ummah* (Muslim community).

## Theological Themes

- **Tawakkul**: Victory comes from reliance on God, not human strength
- **Divine sovereignty**: God raises and removes leaders according to His will
- **The ethics of resistance**: Legitimate resistance to tyranny within moral bounds
- **Universal patterns**: God's dealings with Bani Isra'il illustrate principles applicable to all nations
