## The Levite's Concubine: The Depths of Jahiliyyah

Islamic interpretation of Judges 19 reads this chapter as depicting a state of *jahiliyyah* — the pre-Islamic concept of moral ignorance and barbarism that Islam came to abolish. The events at Gibeah represent the nadir of human moral behavior.

## The Sodom Parallel

The Qur'an addresses the sin of the people of Lut (Lot) extensively (7:80-84, 26:160-175, 29:28-35), condemning their sexual violence and violation of guests. The events at Gibeah parallel this Qur'anic account, demonstrating that Israel has fallen into the same sins for which God destroyed previous nations.

## The Violation of Human Dignity

Islamic theology grounds human dignity in the divine act of creation: "We have certainly honored the children of Adam" (17:70). The treatment of the concubine represents a total negation of this divinely bestowed dignity. Islamic law protects the rights and dignity of every person, regardless of gender, status, or social position.

## The Failure of Hospitality

Islamic tradition places enormous value on *diyafah* (hospitality). The Prophet Muhammad taught: "Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day, let him be generous to his guest" (Bukhari). The violation of hospitality at Gibeah represents a fundamental betrayal of this sacred obligation.

## The Problem of Tahrif

Islamic scholars note that this chapter's attribution of such horrific behavior to a community guided by God's law raises questions of *tahrif*. The moral darkness depicted seems incompatible with a community that had received divine guidance. This either reflects textual corruption or represents a period when divine guidance was completely rejected.

## Theological Themes

- **Jahiliyyah**: The state of moral ignorance that characterizes societies without divine guidance
- **Human dignity**: The inviolability of every person as honored by God
- **The sin of Lut's people**: Sexual violence and violation of hospitality as signs of ultimate moral collapse
- **The need for divine guidance**: Without prophetic leadership, communities descend into barbarism
