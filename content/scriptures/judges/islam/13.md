## Samson's Birth: Divine Announcement and Consecration

Islamic interpretation of Judges 13 engages with the annunciation narrative through the lens of the Qur'anic accounts of miraculous births and divine messengers.

## The Annunciation Pattern

The appearance of an angel to announce a miraculous birth is a familiar pattern in the Qur'an:

| Figure | Qur'anic Reference | Pattern |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| Ishaq (Isaac) | 11:69-73 | Angels announce birth to aged parents |
| Yahya (John) | 3:38-41, 19:7-10 | Angel announces birth to aged Zakariyya |
| Isa (Jesus) | 3:45-47, 19:16-21 | Angel announces birth to Maryam |

The announcement to Manoah's wife fits this pattern, reinforcing the Qur'anic theme that God initiates His saving acts through miraculous births announced by angels.

## The Nazirite Vow and Islamic Consecration

While Islam does not have an exact equivalent of the Nazirite vow, the concept of total dedication to God's service resonates with several Islamic practices:

- **Waqf**: Dedication of property or resources to God's service
- **I'tikaf**: Spiritual retreat in the mosque for intensive worship
- **Ihram**: The state of consecration during Hajj

## The Angel's Identity

Islamic angelology identifies major angels by name — Jibril (Gabriel), Mika'il (Michael), Israfil, and Azra'il. The unnamed angel in Judges 13 corresponds to the Islamic understanding that angels serve as God's messengers, delivering commands and announcements according to divine will.

## The Response of Faith

Manoah's wife demonstrates *iman* (faith) and *tawakkul* (trust in God) that exceeds her husband's — a reminder that spiritual insight is not limited by gender, social status, or formal religious role.

## Theological Themes

- **The pattern of divine announcement**: God's great saving acts are preceded by angelic messages
- **Consecration to God**: Total dedication as a model for the faithful
- **The role of angels**: Messengers carrying out God's commands
- **Women of faith**: Spiritual insight transcends social categories
