## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Judges is reconstructed from the broader Gnostic hermeneutical framework found in texts such as the Nag Hammadi library. While no specifically Gnostic commentary on Judges survives intact, Gnostic readings can be inferred from the movement's distinctive cosmology, its radical reinterpretation of Hebrew scripture, and scattered references in Gnostic texts.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Reverse Hermeneutic
Gnostic interpreters frequently invert the values of the Hebrew Bible, reading the God of Israel (identified as the Demiurge) as a flawed or malevolent creator. In this light, Israel's "disobedience" may be recast as resistance to the Demiurge's tyrannical demands.

### Pneumatic-Allegorical
Gnostic readers distinguish between the "pneumatic" (spiritual), "psychic" (soulish), and "hylic" (material) levels of meaning, seeking hidden wisdom (*gnosis*) beneath the surface narrative.

### Sophia Mythology
Female figures in Judges — Deborah, Jael, Delilah, the women of the final chapters — may be read through the lens of the Sophia myth, as reflections of the divine feminine trapped in or acting within the material world.

## Theological Emphases

- **The Demiurge**: The God who commands conquest and war is identified with the ignorant Demiurge, not the true transcendent God
- **Hidden Gnosis**: The judges who receive divine spirit may be understood as pneumatics who briefly access higher knowledge
- **The Material Trap**: The cycle of violence and moral degradation illustrates the hopelessness of existence under the Demiurge's rule
- **Liberation**: True deliverance comes not through military victory but through *gnosis* — spiritual awakening
