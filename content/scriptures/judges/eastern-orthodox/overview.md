## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Judges is deeply rooted in the patristic tradition, particularly the writings of the Greek Fathers. The book is read liturgically and theologically, with emphasis on the spiritual life, theosis (deification), and the cosmic struggle between good and evil.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Patristic-Liturgical
The Orthodox approach reads Judges through the lens of the Church Fathers and the liturgical tradition. Passages from Judges appear in the Old Testament readings during Great Lent and other festal cycles.

### Typological-Christological
Orthodox interpreters consistently find Christological types in the judges. Gideon's fleece, for example, has been interpreted as a type of the Incarnation — the divine dew descending upon the fleece of human nature.

### Ascetical-Spiritual
The narratives of struggle and deliverance are read as metaphors for the spiritual warfare of the Christian life, with the judges exemplifying both virtue and the dangers of spiritual pride.

## Theological Emphases

- **Theosis**: The judges' empowerment by the Spirit of God points to the transformative work of divine grace
- **Spiritual Warfare**: Israel's battles against the Canaanites are read as allegories of the soul's combat against the passions
- **Divine Economy (Oikonomia)**: God's patient plan of salvation unfolds through imperfect human instruments
- **The Theotokos**: Deborah and Jael have been read typologically as figures of the Mother of God
