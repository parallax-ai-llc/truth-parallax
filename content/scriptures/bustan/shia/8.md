## Shia Reading

Repentance in the Shia reading draws on the distinctive Shia understanding of the Imam's role as intercessor between humanity and God. Sa'di's stories of sinners who find redemption through sincere turning illustrate the Shia conviction that the door of divine mercy is always open, particularly through the intercession (*shafa'a*) of the Prophet and the Imams. The mourning assemblies (*rawda*) of Shia devotional life function as communal exercises in repentance, and Sa'di's chapter provides poetic reinforcement for this practice.
