## Shia Reading

The Shia reading of Sa'di's chapter on justice is animated by the central Shia conviction that legitimate authority derives from divine appointment through the Imamic line. Stories of just rulers recall the paradigmatic governance of Imam Ali at Kufa — his famous letter to Malik al-Ashtar outlining the principles of just administration — while tales of tyrants evoke the Umayyad and Abbasid oppressors of the Ahl al-Bayt. Sa'di's insistence that kingship entails service rather than privilege resonates with the Shia teaching that the Imam's authority is exercised in the service of divine justice.
