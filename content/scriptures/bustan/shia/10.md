## Shia Reading

Sa'di's closing invocation is received in the Shia tradition as a fitting conclusion to a work of spiritual beauty — a final turning of the poet's heart toward the Divine in the company of the Ahl al-Bayt. The Shia reader hears in Sa'di's final verses an expression of the eschatological hope that animates Shia spirituality: the anticipation of the Mahdi's return and the establishment of universal justice. The Bustan's closing prayers thus open onto the infinite horizon of divine promise that sustains the community of the faithful.
