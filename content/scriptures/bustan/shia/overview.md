## Overview

The Shia reading of the Bustan is inseparable from its status as a masterpiece of Persian literature — the language and cultural medium through which Shia Islam has expressed its deepest convictions for centuries. Sa'di's emphasis on justice as the first and foremost virtue resonates powerfully with the Shia theological commitment to divine justice (*'adl*) as one of the five pillars of faith (Usul al-Din).

The Bustan's stories of just rulers and their unjust counterparts are read through the lens of Shia political theology, where legitimate authority derives from divine appointment through the Imamic line. Sa'di's celebration of love, humility, and repentance aligns with the devotional sensibility cultivated in Shia communities through *rawda* (mourning assemblies) and *du'a* (supplicatory prayer). In modern Iran, the Bustan remains a living text, recited at public gatherings, quoted in sermons, and studied in schools as both literary art and moral guidance.
