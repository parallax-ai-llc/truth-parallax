## Shia Reading

Sa'di's chapter on prayer resonates with the rich Shia tradition of *du'a* (supplication), which holds a more prominent place in Shia devotional life than in most other Islamic traditions. The intimate, conversational quality of Sa'di's prayer poetry echoes the style of the great Shia devotional collections — the Sahifa Sajjadiyya, the du'as of Kumayl and Tawassul — in which the worshipper addresses God with a directness and emotional intensity that reflects the model of the Imams' own prayer lives.
