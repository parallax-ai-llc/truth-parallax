## Sunni Reading

The Sunni tradition reads Sa'di's chapter on justice through the framework of *siyasa shar'iyya* — Islamic political theory grounded in Qur'anic principles and Prophetic practice. Sa'di's tales of just and unjust rulers illustrate the hadith that 'the just ruler is the shadow of God upon the earth,' while his stories of tyrants brought low demonstrate the Qur'anic warning that oppressive kingdoms are destined for destruction. The chapter functions as a *nasihatnamah* (mirror for princes) in the tradition of al-Mawardi and Nizam al-Mulk, offering practical ethical guidance for those who exercise political authority.
