## Sunni Reading

The Sunni reading of Sa'di's chapter on education connects it to the Islamic obligation to seek knowledge. The Prophet's statement that 'the seeking of knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim' establishes education as a religious duty. Sa'di's stories of successful and failed education illustrate Sunni pedagogical principles: the importance of good teachers, the necessity of moral formation alongside intellectual training, and the dangers of knowledge without practice. The chapter reinforces the Sunni conviction that authentic *'ilm* (knowledge) must issue in righteous action.
