## Sunni Reading

Gratitude (*shukr*) occupies a central place in Sunni theology, following the Qur'anic promise that 'if you are grateful, I will surely increase you.' Sa'di's poetic treatment of gratitude reinforces the Sunni teaching that thankfulness is both a spiritual state and a moral obligation — owed to God for His blessings and to human beings for their kindness. The chapter serves as a sustained meditation on the Qur'anic concept of *ni'ma* (divine blessing), encouraging readers to cultivate awareness of God's countless gifts.
