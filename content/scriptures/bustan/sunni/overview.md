## Overview

The Sunni tradition receives the Bustan as one of the finest expressions of Islamic moral teaching in poetic form. Sa'di's ten chapters align with virtues emphasized throughout the Qur'an and Prophetic *hadith*: justice, benevolence, humility, gratitude, repentance, and prayer. The Bustan served for centuries as a primary text in Sunni educational institutions, where students memorized its verses as both Persian language models and ethical instruction.

Sunni scholars appreciate how Sa'di grounds exalted spiritual ideals in concrete human situations. His stories of rulers, merchants, dervishes, and ordinary people demonstrate that Islamic ethics operates not in abstraction but in the messy particulars of daily life. The Bustan's sustained verse form gives its moral teachings a memorability and emotional force that prose instruction cannot match, making it an enduringly effective vehicle for the transmission of Sunni ethical values.
