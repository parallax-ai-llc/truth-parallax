## Sunni Reading

The Sunni tradition grounds Sa'di's praise of humility in the Prophetic example. Muhammad, despite being God's final messenger, lived with extraordinary simplicity and refused the trappings of worldly authority. Sa'di's stories of the humble exalted echo the hadith that 'whoever humbles himself for God, God will raise him.' The Sunni reader finds in this chapter a corrective to the spiritual diseases of pride (*kibr*) and self-admiration (*'ujb*), which the Prophet identified as among the gravest dangers to the believer's soul.
