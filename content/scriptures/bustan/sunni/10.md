## Sunni Reading

Sa'di's closing chapter functions as a literary *khatima* (conclusion) that mirrors the Islamic practice of closing any significant undertaking with prayer and praise. The Sunni reader appreciates Sa'di's final invocations as models of *du'a* (supplication) — the personal prayers that complement the prescribed liturgy and express the believer's intimate relationship with God. The Bustan's closing benedictions reinforce the Sunni conviction that all human endeavor, including literary creation, derives its meaning from its orientation toward the Divine.
