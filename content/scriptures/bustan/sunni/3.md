## Sunni Reading

The Sunni reading of Sa'di's love chapter navigates between appreciation for the ghazal tradition's literary beauty and the moral imperative to channel human desire within legitimate bounds. Sunni scholars have traditionally read Sa'di's love poetry as primarily addressing divine love — the believer's passionate attachment to God and the Prophet — while acknowledging the natural human experiences of affection and beauty that Sa'di describes with such artistry. The chapter is valued for its capacity to awaken spiritual longing while maintaining moral seriousness.
