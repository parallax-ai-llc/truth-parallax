## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's contentment is compared with Stoic *apatheia*, Epicurean simplicity, Buddhist *upekkha* (equanimity), Daoist *wu-wei*, and the Jewish concept of *sameach b'chelko* (rejoicing in one's portion). Each tradition offers a distinctive rationale for contentment — rational self-mastery, pleasure calculation, insight into impermanence, harmony with nature, or divine trust — and Sa'di's integration of Sufi mysticism with practical wisdom represents a unique synthesis within this global conversation.
