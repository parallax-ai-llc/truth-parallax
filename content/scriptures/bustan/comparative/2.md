## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's celebration of benevolence invites comparison with the Buddhist *dana paramita* (perfection of generosity), the Christian teaching on *agape*, the Jewish concept of *tzedakah*, and the Hindu practice of *danam*. Across traditions, generosity is understood not merely as a social virtue but as a spiritual discipline that purifies the giver. Sa'di's distinctive contribution is his integration of mystical insight (the lover cannot help but give) with practical social ethics (the ruler is obligated to provide for the people).
