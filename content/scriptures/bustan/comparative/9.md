## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's prayer chapter invites comparison with devotional poetry across traditions: the Psalms, Hindu *bhajans*, Buddhist *gathas*, Christian mystical hymns, and Sikh *kirtan*. Each tradition has developed distinctive forms of poetic prayer that serve both liturgical and literary functions. Comparative analysis examines how the formal features of prayer poetry — rhythm, repetition, direct address to the divine — transcend cultural boundaries while receiving culturally specific expression.
