## Comparative Reading

Comparative analysis places Sa'di's chapter on justice alongside other civilizations' reflections on righteous governance: Plato's philosopher-kings, Confucius's virtuous rulers, the Hindu concept of *dharmaraja*, and the biblical model of the anointed king who rules in covenant with God. Each tradition grapples with the fundamental tension between power and virtue, offering different solutions — institutional, ethical, spiritual — that illuminate Sa'di's distinctively Islamic and Persian approach.
