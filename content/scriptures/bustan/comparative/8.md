## Comparative Reading

Comparative study of repentance reveals it as a central concern of the Abrahamic religions — Islamic *tawba*, Jewish *teshuva*, Christian *metanoia* — while noting analogous concepts in Buddhism (the refuge ceremony), Hinduism (*prayaschitta*), and Confucianism (moral self-correction). Each tradition offers a distinctive account of what repentance involves and what makes it possible, and Sa'di's emphasis on divine mercy as the precondition of human turning resonates particularly with Jewish and Christian theological frameworks.
