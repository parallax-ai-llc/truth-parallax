## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's reflections on education connect with Confucian *xiu shen* (self-cultivation), Platonic *paideia*, Buddhist monastic training systems, and the Jewish emphasis on *Torah* study as moral formation. Comparative analysis reveals a near-universal recognition that education involves both knowledge acquisition and character formation, though traditions differ on the relative importance of innate nature versus nurture. Sa'di's position — that education can polish but not fundamentally alter character — finds both support and challenge across traditions.
