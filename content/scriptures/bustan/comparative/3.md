## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's love poetry participates in a global tradition of sacred erotic literature that includes Plato's *Symposium*, the biblical Song of Songs, the Hindu *Gita Govinda*, the troubadour *fin'amors*, and Japanese *mono no aware* poetry. Comparative analysis examines how different traditions negotiate the relationship between human and divine love — whether they are understood as analogous, continuous, or fundamentally different — and how Sa'di's characteristically Persian approach combines mystical aspiration with sensual appreciation.
