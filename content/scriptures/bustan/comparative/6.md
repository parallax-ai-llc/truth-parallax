## Comparative Reading

Gratitude as a spiritual practice finds expression across traditions: Jewish *berakhot* (blessings), Christian eucharistic thanksgiving, Buddhist *katannuta* (gratitude), Hindu *prarthana* (grateful prayer), and the Confucian virtue of reciprocity (*bao*). Comparative study reveals both universal features — the recognition that gratitude requires awareness of one's dependence on forces beyond oneself — and culturally specific expressions. Sa'di's poetic treatment adds an aesthetic dimension to gratitude that is particularly prominent in the Islamic tradition.
