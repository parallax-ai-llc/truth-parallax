## Comparative Reading

Comparative study reveals humility as a virtue praised across traditions yet conceived in strikingly different ways. Christian *kenosis* (self-emptying modeled on Christ), Buddhist recognition of *anatta* (non-self), Daoist praise of water that seeks the lowest place, and Confucian deference within hierarchical relationships each offer distinctive framings of lowliness. Sa'di's Sufi-inflected humility — the annihilation of the ego before divine majesty — represents a theistic version that resonates particularly with Christian and Jewish mystical traditions.
