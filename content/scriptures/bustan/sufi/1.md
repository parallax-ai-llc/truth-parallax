## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reads Sa'di's opening chapter on justice as an allegory of the soul's governance over its inner kingdom. The just ruler represents the perfected self (*insan al-kamil*) who has aligned every faculty — intellect, passion, desire — under the sovereignty of the spirit. Sa'di's tales of kings who serve their people echo the Sufi teaching that true spiritual authority emerges only through *fana* (self-annihilation) — the paradox that one must lose the self to govern it rightly. The Sufi master governs the community of seekers not through coercion but through the magnetic force of realized truth.
