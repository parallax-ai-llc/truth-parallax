## Sufi Reading

This chapter strikes the deepest chord in the Sufi heart, for love (*ishq*) is the very essence of the mystical path. Sa'di's celebration of love's intoxicating power speaks directly to the Sufi experience of *sukr* — the overwhelming ecstasy that dissolves the boundaries of the rational self. The beloved in Sa'di's verses is simultaneously human and divine, reflecting the Sufi conviction that earthly beauty is a theophany — a manifestation of divine beauty in the world of forms. Love's madness, its disregard for reputation and safety, mirrors the Sufi's willingness to sacrifice everything for a moment of union with the Beloved.
