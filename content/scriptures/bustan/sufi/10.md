## Sufi Reading

Sa'di's closing invocation functions as a spiritual testament — the poet's final offering of his work to the Divine. The Sufi reader hears in these closing verses the voice of one who has traversed the garden of spiritual experience and returns to the beginning: the acknowledgment that all beauty, all wisdom, all love originates in and returns to the One. The Bustan's final prayers express the Sufi aspiration that the work of art itself become an act of worship — that the orchard of poetry bear fruit in the hearts of readers for generations to come.
