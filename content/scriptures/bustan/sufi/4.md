## Sufi Reading

The Sufi tradition treasures humility as the gateway to all spiritual realization. Sa'di's chapter presents humility not as weakness but as the most powerful spiritual posture — the lowliness of the valley that receives all the mountain's rain. In Sufi teaching, the ego (*nafs*) is the greatest obstacle to divine knowledge, and humility is the sword that subdues it. Sa'di's stories of the humble exalted and the proud brought low illustrate the cosmic law that echoes through all Sufi literature: only the empty vessel can be filled with divine wine.
