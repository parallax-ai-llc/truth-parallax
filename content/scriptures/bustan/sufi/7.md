## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reads Sa'di's chapter on education as a treatise on spiritual formation (*tarbiya*). The teacher is the *murshid* (spiritual guide) and the student the *murid* (seeker), bound together in a relationship that aims not merely at the transmission of information but at the transformation of the soul. Sa'di's observations about the limits of education for corrupt natures reflect the Sufi understanding that the path requires both divine grace and human effort — the master can polish the mirror, but the mirror must be capable of reflecting light.
