## Sufi Reading

Sa'di's chapter on prayer brings the Bustan to its spiritual climax, for prayer in the Sufi tradition is not ritual obligation but intimate conversation (*munajat*) with the Beloved. Sa'di's verses on prayer echo the Sufi teaching that the highest form of worship is the prayer of the heart — the continuous, wordless remembrance (*dhikr*) that sustains the mystic's awareness of divine presence through every moment of daily life. The formal prayers are the skeleton; the prayer of the heart is the living spirit that animates them.
