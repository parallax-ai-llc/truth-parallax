## Interfaith Reading

Interfaith readers discover in Sa'di's chapter on justice a moral concern shared across all traditions: that power must serve the common good and be held accountable to a standard higher than itself. The prophetic tradition of speaking truth to power — Moses before Pharaoh, Nathan before David, the Buddhist sangha's moral authority — finds eloquent expression in Sa'di's tales. This shared commitment to justice provides a foundation for interfaith collaboration on issues of governance, human rights, and social equity.
