## Overview

The Bustan holds special value for interfaith dialogue because its moral teachings, while rooted in Islamic and Sufi tradition, address virtues recognized across all major religions: justice, compassion, humility, gratitude, and the transformative power of love. Sa'di's poetic genius gives these universal themes a beauty and emotional force that creates immediate connection across religious boundaries.

Interfaith readers discover in the Bustan a model for how deep commitment to a particular tradition — Sa'di is unmistakably Muslim, unmistakably Persian, unmistakably Sufi — can coexist with genuine moral universalism. His stories feature characters of diverse backgrounds and his ethical judgments rest on shared human values rather than sectarian criteria. The Bustan thus demonstrates that the most culturally specific art can speak most powerfully to the universal human condition.
