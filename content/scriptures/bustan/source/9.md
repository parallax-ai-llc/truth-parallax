## On Repentance

**1.** O thou who hast wasted thy life in folly, rise up before the sun of thy days sets forever.

**2.** The door of repentance is open, but it will not be open always. Hasten to enter while there is still time.

**3.** A man said to a sage: "I have sinned all my life. Is it too late for me to repent?" The sage replied: "Is the sun still in the sky? Then it is not too late."

**4.** "But remember: repentance is not a word spoken by the tongue—it is a fire that burns in the heart. It is the turning of the whole self, like a river that changes its course."

**5.** A thief came to a preacher and said: "Pray for me." The preacher said: "Pray for yourself, and while you pray, put down what is in your hand."

**6.** Repentance without a change of conduct is like sowing on barren rock.

**7.** A merchant lost his fortune and wept bitterly. A sage said to him: "Do not weep for what you have lost, but for the years you spent gaining it, for those years are the greater loss."

**8.** God says: "Turn to me, and I will turn to you." But the turning must be real, not merely a turning of words.

**9.** There was a drunkard who repented every morning and returned to the wine-house every night. A sage said: "Your repentance is the repentance of a fox that promises to stay out of the henhouse."

**10.** "True repentance is the fox that sees the henhouse and walks the other way."

**11.** Do not postpone repentance, for tomorrow is not promised to thee. How many have gone to sleep with the intention of rising to pray, and never risen again?

**12.** The young man says: "I will repent when I am old." But old age is not promised to the young, and death does not send notice before it arrives.

**13.** A drop of water said: "When I reach the ocean, I will become pure." But it evaporated before it reached the ocean. Repent now, for the journey is shorter than you think.

**14.** God accepts the repentance of the sincere heart, even at the last hour. But do not gamble on the last hour, for thou knowest not when it will come.

**15.** Wash the page of thy life clean while the ink is still wet, for once it dries, no water can remove it.
