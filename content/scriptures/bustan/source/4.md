## On Humility

**1.** The tree that is laden with fruit bows its head low. The empty branch holds itself high in arrogance.

**2.** If thou art wise, be lowly and humble, for the stream that runs in the valley waters the garden.

**3.** A man said to a sage: "I have traveled the world and seen many wonders." The sage replied: "But have you seen yourself? That is the greatest wonder."

**4.** Humility is not weakness—it is the strength of one who knows his own place before the Almighty.

**5.** A king once visited a dervish in his cell. The dervish did not rise. The king's attendants were enraged and said: "Do you not know who stands before you?"

**6.** The dervish replied: "Tell your king to seek homage from those who desire something from him. I desire nothing, and therefore I owe him nothing."

**7.** "But tell him also this: a king rules over the bodies of men, but a dervish rules over his own soul. Ask him which is the greater kingdom."

**8.** The king pondered these words and departed humbled, saying: "That dervish is the true king."

**9.** A learned man is like a jar of musk—its fragrance speaks for it. The ignorant man is like a drum—loud in sound, empty within.

**10.** They asked a sage: "How should a man behave among his fellows?" He replied: "Like the earth, which is trodden beneath the feet of all and yet provides sustenance to all."

**11.** Be like the earth—patient, humble, and generous. Those who walk upon it do not diminish it; those who dig into it find treasure.

**12.** The mountain stands tall and proud, yet it is the valley that receives the rain and grows the harvest.

**13.** If you wish to be exalted, humble yourself. The door of heaven is low; only those who bow their heads may enter.

**14.** Pride is the mask that hides the face of ignorance. Remove the mask, and what remains is a frightened child pretending to be a giant.

**15.** The truly great are those who serve, not those who command. The sun gives light to all without asking for gratitude.
