## On Gratitude

**1.** If thou art grateful, God will increase thy blessings. If thou art ungrateful, His punishment is severe.

**2.** A man who has his health, his daily bread, and a roof over his head possesses everything, though he thinks he possesses nothing.

**3.** I heard of a man who complained he had no shoes, until he saw a man who had no feet.

**4.** Every breath thou drawest is a gift. Hast thou given thanks for a single one?

**5.** A king asked a sage: "What is the best act of worship?" The sage replied: "For you, it is to wake at noon and give thanks that your oppression has been suspended for half the day."

**6.** But in truth, the greatest act of worship is gratitude. For gratitude opens the door to more blessings, while complaint closes it.

**7.** The grateful man sees abundance everywhere. The ungrateful man sees scarcity even in the midst of plenty.

**8.** A single grape given with love is worth more than a vineyard given grudgingly. Gratitude is not measured by what you receive but by how you receive it.

**9.** They asked a rose: "From whom did you learn to be so beautiful?" The rose replied: "From the gardener who watered me with patience and sheltered me from the storm. My beauty is my gratitude."

**10.** The nightingale sings not because the garden owes it something, but because the garden has given it everything.

**11.** Be grateful to thy enemies, for they teach thee patience. Be grateful to thy friends, for they teach thee love. Be grateful to the world, for it teaches thee humility.

**12.** A dervish was asked: "What have you to be grateful for? You have neither house, nor food, nor possessions." He replied: "I am grateful that I have been freed from the burden of all these things."

**13.** The eye that sees is itself a cause for gratitude. The ear that hears is itself a miracle. The tongue that speaks is itself a gift beyond reckoning.

**14.** Count thy blessings, and thou shalt find they are beyond counting. Count thy troubles, and thou shalt find they are but the dust on the road.

**15.** He who thanks God for the little will be given much. He who complains of much will be left with little.
