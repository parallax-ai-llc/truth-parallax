## On Love

**1.** The path of love is not a road for the faint of heart. He who enters it must surrender everything he has.

**2.** Love is an ocean without a shore. He who would swim in it must be willing to drown.

**3.** I heard of a lover who sold his house, his garden, and all that he possessed for one glance from the beloved.

**4.** They said to him: "You have been cheated." He replied: "If I had a thousand houses, I would sell them all for half a glance."

**5.** This is not the love of the marketplace—this is the love that burns everything and leaves nothing but ashes, and from those ashes, raises a phoenix.

**6.** The lover said to the physician: "Do not prescribe medicine for me—let me burn, for in this burning is my cure."

**7.** Whoever has not tasted the bitterness of love has never tasted its sweetness. The rose has thorns, but it is still the rose.

**8.** When Layla passed through the streets, the people said: "This is the one for whom Majnun lost his mind." But they could not see in her what Majnun saw.

**9.** For Majnun said: "To see Layla with the eyes of Majnun, you must first become Majnun."

**10.** Love is not something you seek—it is something that seizes you. The hawk does not choose its prey; it is the prey that calls the hawk.

**11.** A drop of rain fell from a cloud and was ashamed when it beheld the vastness of the sea. "What am I," it said, "compared to this immensity?"

**12.** But when it looked upon itself with the eye of humility, a shell took it into its bosom, and fate so shaped its course that it became a royal pearl.

**13.** So it is with the lover who humbles himself before the Beloved—he becomes a jewel in the crown of existence.

**14.** Without love, the world would freeze and all the rivers would run dry. It is love that moves the stars and makes the flowers bloom.

**15.** Love is the astrolabe of the mysteries of God. Whether you love a human being or God, when love has matured, it will lead you to the Beloved.
