## On Prayer and the Closing of the Book

**1.** O God, I am a sinner, and Thou art the Merciful. I bring nothing but my empty hands and my broken heart.

**2.** If Thou shouldst reckon my faults, I am lost. But Thou art the Forgiver, and Thy mercy is wider than the sea.

**3.** The night-traveler who loses his way in the desert calls out for help. Even so do I call upon Thee in the darkness of my ignorance.

**4.** A devotee prayed all night and in the morning said: "I fear that my prayer was not accepted." A voice replied: "Your calling was itself the answer. Your seeking was itself the finding."

**5.** Do not say: "God has not heard me." The very fact that you have turned to Him is proof that He has turned to you.

**6.** The door of prayer is never closed. Even when you think you are speaking to the air, the Listener is nearer to you than the air itself.

**7.** A man wept in prayer, and they said to him: "Why do you weep? You who have so many blessings." He replied: "I weep not for what I lack but for what I have been given and have failed to honour."

**8.** Prayer is not the listing of your wants before the Almighty—it is the silence of the heart in the presence of the Infinite.

**9.** When the tongue is silent and the heart speaks, that is the truest prayer. The loudest prayer is the one that makes no sound.

**10.** Sa'di, thou hast spent thy life in wandering—from Damascus to the Hejaz, from the shores of North Africa to the plains of India.

**11.** Thou hast seen the learned and the foolish, the generous and the mean, the just and the oppressor.

**12.** And what hast thou learned from all thy wandering? That the world is a bridge—cross it, but do not build thy house upon it.

**13.** That the kindest word is more powerful than the sharpest sword, and the gentlest hand can move mountains that no force can budge.

**14.** That the beggar who is content is a king, and the king who is greedy is a beggar.

**15.** O God, grant that the words of this book may bring some small light to those who wander in darkness, and may the light of Thy mercy guide the author as well as the reader.

**16.** This is the end of the Bustan, the Orchard of Sa'di, planted with the fruits of observation and watered by the tears of experience.

**17.** May the reader who enters this garden depart with the fragrance of wisdom upon his garments, and may that fragrance never fade.
