## On Education

**1.** The learned man who does not teach is like a cloud that gathers rain and does not release it upon the earth.

**2.** He who possesses knowledge and withholds it from those who seek it will be questioned on the Day of Reckoning: "Why did you hide the lamp beneath a bushel?"

**3.** A father sent his son to the school of a sage. The sage asked: "What do you wish me to teach him?" The father replied: "Teach him to know himself, for that is the beginning of all wisdom."

**4.** The sage said: "That is a task for which a lifetime is too short. But I will plant the seed."

**5.** The pupil who is humble before his teacher is like the soil that receives rain—from it, the garden grows. The pupil who is proud is like stone upon which rain falls and runs off.

**6.** It is better to teach one thing deeply than a thousand things lightly. The well that is dug deep gives sweet water; the well that is dug wide gives only dust.

**7.** A student asked his master: "How many years must I study before I am learned?" The master replied: "When you stop asking that question, you will be close."

**8.** There was a scholar who spent his whole life in study and never applied his learning. He was like a donkey that carried a load of books but could not read a single page.

**9.** Knowledge that is not practiced is a tree that bears no fruit. The farmer who knows the seasons but does not plant will starve as surely as the ignorant man.

**10.** A wise man teaches not by words alone but by the manner of his life. The candle does not speak, yet it gives light to the room.

**11.** Teach your children not to fear the world but to understand it. The child who fears the dark will hide from it; the child who understands it will carry a lamp.

**12.** Every man has a teacher, even if he does not know it. The ant teaches patience, the bee teaches industry, the spider teaches persistence.

**13.** Open your eyes, and every creature of God is your teacher. Close your eyes, and the whole library of the world cannot teach you a thing.

**14.** The true scholar is known not by the books he has read but by the life he has lived. His learning shines from his face like the light of the moon.

**15.** Education without character is like a ship without a rudder—it may sail far, but it will never reach its destination.
