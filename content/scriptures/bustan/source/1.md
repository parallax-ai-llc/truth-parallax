## On Justice and the Conduct of Government

**1.** In the name of God, the Compassionate, the Merciful, who adorned the human soul with the faculty of speech.

**2.** Praise to God Most High, the Lord of Might, the Bestower of grace and the Author of creation.

**3.** O Lord, deal graciously with the people of the earth, for all are Thy servants and all are Thy creation.

**4.** The foundation of all government is justice. Without justice, the king is no more than a thief with a crown.

**5.** A kingdom built on oppression is a house built on sand. When the flood comes, both the house and the builder are swept away.

**6.** Nushirvan the Just, even as his soul was departing, said to his son Hormuz: "Be a guardian to the poor, my son, and be not engrossed in thine own pleasure."

**7.** "No one among the people rests secure whose watchman is asleep. If thou wouldst have thy subjects safe, be wakeful."

**8.** "A king who permits his officers to oppress the peasants digs up the roots of the tree of his own prosperity."

**9.** A king said to a hermit: "Pray for me." The hermit replied: "Pray for yourself by being just. Prayer without justice is like building without a foundation."

**10.** I have heard that once a conflagration burned half a city. A man asked his neighbor: "Were you not grieved by the destruction?" The neighbor replied: "I was so occupied in saving my own goods that I had no time to think of others."

**11.** So it is with tyrants—they are so occupied with their own treasures that they notice not the ruin of the people.

**12.** If thou holdest thy subjects dear, they will lay their heads beneath thy feet. The country that is justly governed needs no fortress.

**13.** The shepherd who sleeps will find his flock scattered by the wolf. Rule with vigilance, and the wolf will not dare approach.

**14.** The wise ruler takes counsel from the wise, for even the brightest gem needs a jeweler to set it.

**15.** Justice is the first pillar of the state. When the pillar falls, the roof collapses upon the heads of all.
