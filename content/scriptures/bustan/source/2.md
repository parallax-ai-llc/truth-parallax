## On Benevolence

**1.** If thou art wise, sow the seed of benevolence, for the harvest thereof will please thee.

**2.** Be generous while thou hast the means, for the treasure-house of opportunity is not always open.

**3.** There was in Egypt a man of great wealth, as generous as Hatim of old. He kept his door open to all who came, and his table was spread for the hungry.

**4.** It happened that his fortune was lost. A friend said to him: "I told you that your generosity would bring you to ruin."

**5.** The generous man replied: "I have lost my wealth, but I have gained a thousand friends. Which is the greater loss—to lose wealth or to have lived without giving?"

**6.** Be benevolent while you are able. The cloud that gives rain is honoured; the cloud that gives none passes away in shame.

**7.** A sage once passed through a graveyard and saw two tombs side by side. Upon one was written: "Here lies one who amassed wealth." Upon the other: "Here lies one who gave it away." Both were equally covered with dust.

**8.** The man who plants a tree under whose shade he will never sit has begun to understand the meaning of life.

**9.** One year, a great famine struck the land. A rich man opened his granaries to the poor. When the famine was over, his granaries were empty but his name was on every tongue.

**10.** His neighbor, who had kept his granaries locked, still had his grain, but when he walked in the street, the people turned their faces away.

**11.** Give freely, for what you give will come back to you increased a thousandfold. What you keep will rot in the storehouse and be devoured by rats.

**12.** The hand that gives gathers; the hand that withholds scatters. This is the paradox of benevolence.

**13.** Do good, and cast it into the river. One day it will come back to you in the desert, when you are dying of thirst.

**14.** The generous man dies but his name lives. The miser lives but his name is already dead.

**15.** A sage was asked: "What is true wealth?" He replied: "That which remains after you have given it away."
