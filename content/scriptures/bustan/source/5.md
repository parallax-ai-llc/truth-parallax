## On Resignation and Submission to the Will of God

**1.** Whatever comes from the hand of Providence, receive it with open hands. The arrow that strikes thee was aimed by a wise Archer.

**2.** If adversity befalls thee, do not grieve, for within the darkness of affliction lies the dawn of mercy.

**3.** A blind man was asked: "Do you not wish for sight?" He replied: "I am content. That which God has given me is enough; that which He has withheld is not my concern."

**4.** A king once lost his signet ring while hunting. His courtiers were dismayed, but the king said: "The ring that was lost can be replaced. But the tranquility that is lost through grieving cannot."

**5.** Do not weep for what is taken from thee, for what remains is sufficient. The tree that loses its leaves in winter is not dead—it is resting for the spring.

**6.** The farmer sows his seed and entrusts it to the earth and the rain. He does not dig it up each day to see if it has sprouted. This is resignation.

**7.** Trust the Gardener, for He knows which plants need rain and which need sun. He waters the garden according to the needs of each flower.

**8.** A man complained to a sage: "My enemy prospers while I suffer." The sage replied: "Be patient. The candle that burns brightest burns shortest."

**9.** "And remember: the stone that is cut and polished endures hardship, but it becomes a jewel. The stone that lies untouched in the field remains a stone."

**10.** It is said that a pearl was once a common drop of rain. But it fell into the mouth of an oyster and submitted to the darkness, and so it became a pearl.

**11.** Submit to the darkness of trial, for from that darkness comes the light of transformation.

**12.** A dervish said: "I have lost everything." His master replied: "Then you have lost nothing, for you now possess what cannot be lost."

**13.** The river does not choose its course—it follows the path that is carved for it. And yet the river reaches the sea.

**14.** Trust the path, even when you cannot see where it leads. The Guide who leads you through the night knows the way even when you do not.

**15.** What God has written on thy forehead, thou shalt become. Be not troubled, for what is destined for thee will find thee even at the bottom of the sea.
