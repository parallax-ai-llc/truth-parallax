## On Contentment

**1.** The contented man sleeps soundly on the bare earth, while the discontented man tosses on a bed of silk.

**2.** I have seen a king who could not sleep for worry over his kingdom, and a beggar who slept like a child beneath the stars.

**3.** Which of these two was the richer? The one who possessed everything and feared losing it, or the one who possessed nothing and feared nothing?

**4.** A dervish said: "I am the richest man in the world." They said: "How so? You have nothing." He replied: "I want nothing. And he who wants nothing possesses all things."

**5.** Contentment is the philosopher's stone that turns copper into gold. Not the copper of the purse, but the copper of the soul.

**6.** A dog bit a man from the desert. The man ran to a physician who said: "Apply the flesh of the dog that bit you to the wound." The man replied: "I would rather die of the wound than seek a remedy from the dog."

**7.** So should we be with the world: rather endure its wounds than seek its cures, for its cures are worse than its diseases.

**8.** A wise man was asked: "Why are the rich so restless?" He said: "Because wealth is a sea, and the more a man drinks salt water, the thirstier he becomes."

**9.** The pitcher that is full makes no sound when struck. The pitcher that is empty rings loudly. Be full, and you will be quiet.

**10.** A man with one loaf of bread and a place to sleep is richer than a king who lies awake all night counting his enemies.

**11.** The greedy man digs a pit for others and falls into it himself. The contented man walks on solid ground.

**12.** Seek not more than thy need, for the ship that is overloaded sinks while the light vessel sails freely.

**13.** I have seen merchants with storehouses full of grain who lay awake at night fearing thieves, while the sparrow who has nothing sings on the branch at dawn.

**14.** The heart that is content is a garden in every season. The heart that is greedy is a desert in every season.

**15.** God gives to each creature according to its need. The ant does not receive the portion of the elephant. Be content with your portion, and it will suffice.
