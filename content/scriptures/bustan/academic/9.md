## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze the Bustan's prayer chapter as devotional poetry, examining how Sa'di transforms the conventions of Islamic liturgical language into literary art. Prosodic analysis reveals how Sa'di uses the rhythms and sounds of Arabic prayer language within Persian verse to create a distinctive register that bridges the liturgical and the literary. The chapter's structural position — near the end of the work — is studied as a formal strategy for bringing the didactic project to its spiritual culmination.
