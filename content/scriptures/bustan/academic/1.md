## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of Sa'di's chapter on justice situates it within the long tradition of Islamic *nasihatnamah* literature while examining its distinctive features. Scholars note how Sa'di adapts the prose mirror-for-princes genre into sustained *masnavi* verse, achieving effects of emotional persuasion unavailable in prose political treatises. The chapter's historical references are scrutinized for evidence of Sa'di's political environment under the Salghurids and the shadow of the Mongol conquests.
