## Academic Reading

Academic study of this chapter examines its philosophical sources in Islamic, Greek, and Persian ethical traditions. Scholars trace Sa'di's concept of contentment through its genealogy in Aristotelian *autarkeia* (mediated through Islamic philosophy), Stoic *ataraxia*, and the Sufi station of *rida*. The chapter is also read as a social document reflecting the economic disruptions of the post-Mongol period, when praise of contentment may have served practical as well as spiritual purposes.
