## Academic Reading

Scholars examine Sa'di's chapter on humility as a literary topos with deep roots in both Islamic and pre-Islamic Persian literary traditions. The chapter's rhetorical strategies — its use of paradox, reversal, and exemplary narrative — are analyzed as techniques for commending a virtue that resists direct advocacy (since any claim to humility is potentially self-contradicting). The chapter is compared with analogous treatments in Arabic *adab* literature and European devotional writing.
