## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze this chapter's treatment of benevolence in relation to medieval Islamic charitable institutions (*awqaf*) and the social ethics of gift-giving in Persian courtly culture. Literary critics examine Sa'di's narrative techniques — his use of exemplary tales, aphoristic conclusions, and emotional appeals — as rhetorical strategies for motivating generosity in his audience. The chapter provides valuable evidence about social relations and economic conditions in 13th-century Shiraz.
