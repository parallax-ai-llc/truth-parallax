## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of Sa'di's love chapter engages with the conventions of Persian *ghazal* poetry and the medieval Islamic discourse on love ('ishq). Scholars debate the extent to which Sa'di's love poetry should be read as mystical allegory, autobiographical expression, or literary convention — noting that these categories were not as sharply distinguished in Sa'di's milieu as they are in modern scholarship. Gender studies and queer theory approaches have opened new perspectives on the chapter's treatment of beauty and desire.
