## Overview

Academic study of the Bustan encompasses Persian literary criticism, Islamic intellectual history, prosodic analysis, and comparative poetics. Scholars analyze Sa'di's mastery of the *masnavi* form — his management of the *mutaqarib* meter, his narrative pacing, his integration of lyrical and didactic modes — as a technical achievement of the highest order in Persian poetry.

The Bustan's relationship to its prose companion, the Gulistan, raises questions about the complementary functions of verse and prose in medieval Islamic literature. Textual scholars examine the extensive manuscript tradition, comparing recensions to identify later interpolations and establish critical texts. The Bustan is situated within the broader tradition of Persian didactic *masnavi*, from Ferdowsi's Shahnameh to Rumi's Masnavi-ye Ma'navi, illuminating the genre's conventions and Sa'di's distinctive innovations.
