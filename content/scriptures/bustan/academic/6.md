## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze Sa'di's treatment of gratitude in relation to medieval Islamic theological discussions about the nature and obligations of *shukr* — debates that engaged philosophers, theologians, and mystics in different ways. The chapter's prosodic features are examined for their contribution to the emotional force of the teaching, with particular attention to Sa'di's deployment of meter, rhyme, and enjambment to create effects of wonder and thanksgiving.
