## God's Justice and Mercy United

Catholic interpretation of Nahum 1 reads the theophanic hymn as a revelation of the unity of God's justice and mercy. God is not divided between a wrathful Old Testament deity and a merciful New Testament one — the same God who shows patience also executes judgment when patience is exhausted. The Church Fathers, particularly Jerome, emphasized that Nahum's God is the God of the entire Bible, whose justice serves his love and whose love demands justice.

## The Theophany and Creation

The imagery of mountains quaking, hills dissolving, and the sea drying up (1:4-5) reveals God's sovereignty over the created order. Catholic natural theology draws on such passages to affirm that creation is not autonomous but dependent on God's sustaining will. The Demiurge of pagan mythology is replaced by the living God who governs nature and history alike.

## Comfort for the Afflicted

The verse "The LORD is good, a stronghold in the day of trouble; he knows those who take refuge in him" (1:7) is a precious text in Catholic pastoral care. God's judgment on the oppressor is simultaneously God's protection of the oppressed. The chapter assures the afflicted that God is attentive to their suffering and will act on their behalf.
