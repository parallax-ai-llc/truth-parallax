## The Dissolution of Archontic Power

Gnostic interpretation of Nahum 2 reads the fall of Nineveh as a prophetic vision of the archontic powers' collapse. The flood that dissolves the palace (2:6) represents the dissolution of the material structures through which the archons maintain their control. The gates are thrown open, the soldiers flee, the treasures are plundered — the entire apparatus of material power disintegrates when the true God acts against it.

## The Empty Lions' Den

The image of the empty lions' den is read with particular relish in Gnostic interpretation. The archons, who prowled the material cosmos like lions seeking prey (cf. 1 Peter 5:8), are exposed as impotent. Their den — the material world itself — is emptied of its power. The divine sparks that were their captive prey are liberated.

## The Flood of Gnosis

The flood that destroys Nineveh is reinterpreted as the flood of *gnosis* — the liberating knowledge that dissolves the structures of ignorance and material attachment. When true knowledge arrives, the carefully constructed illusions of the archontic order collapse like Nineveh's palace under the rising waters.
