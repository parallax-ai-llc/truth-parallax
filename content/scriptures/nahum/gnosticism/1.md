## The True God Breaks Through

Gnostic interpretation of Nahum 1 reads the theophany as a manifestation of the true God — the God beyond the Demiurge — breaking into the material cosmos with devastating effect. The mountains melting, the sea retreating, and the rivers drying up represent the dissolution of the material order under the pressure of genuine divine reality. The Demiurge's creation cannot withstand the presence of the God who transcends it.

## The "Jealous God" Revisited

The declaration that the LORD is "a jealous and avenging God" (1:2) is read with ambivalence in Gnostic interpretation. Some Gnostic readers identify the jealous God with the Demiurge, whose possessiveness is a mark of his limitation. Others read the verse as describing the true God's righteous anger at the archontic powers that have usurped his place and enslaved the divine sparks.

## The Fall of Nineveh as Cosmic Event

Nineveh, as the seat of the world's most oppressive empire, represents the archontic order at its most brutal. Its fall is not merely a historical event but a cosmic one — a sign that the structures of material power are not eternal and will ultimately be dissolved by the power of the true God.
