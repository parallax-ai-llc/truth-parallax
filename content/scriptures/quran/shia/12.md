## Shia Commentary

Surah Yusuf (Joseph), with its 111 verses from the Meccan period, is called 'the best of stories' by the Quran itself, this surah presents the complete narrative of Prophet Yusuf (Joseph) — his dreams, betrayal by his brothers, enslavement in Egypt, resistance to temptation, imprisonment, rise to power, and ultimate reconciliation with his family. Shia exegesis approaches this surah through the interpretive lens of the Ahl al-Bayt — the Prophet's family — whose teachings, preserved in the hadith collections of Twelver Shi'ism, provide both the exoteric (*zahir*) and esoteric (*batin*) dimensions of the text.

**Allamah Tabataba'i** in *Al-Mizan fi Tafsir al-Quran* offers the most comprehensive modern Shia commentary on this surah, combining philosophical depth with close textual analysis. His distinctive method of "interpreting the Quran by the Quran" (*tafsir al-Quran bil-Quran*) identifies internal cross-references and thematic connections that illuminate the surah's meaning within the broader Quranic discourse. He supplements this with narrations from the Imams that reveal layers of meaning inaccessible through linguistic analysis alone.

**Al-Tabrisi** in *Majma' al-Bayan* provides a balanced commentary that engages with both Sunni and Shia interpretive traditions, making it a valuable resource for understanding how this surah has been read across the sectarian divide. His work demonstrates the Shia commitment to rigorous Arabic linguistics and rhetorical analysis alongside the distinctive emphasis on the authority of the Imams as the Quran's authoritative interpreters.

Shia commentators draw attention to verses within this surah that they understand as allusions to the authority (*wilayah*) of Ali and the Imams, reading the Quranic call to follow divine guidance as encompassing obedience to the divinely appointed leaders. This hermeneutical framework does not replace the surah's broader themes but adds a dimension that Shia scholars consider essential for a complete understanding of the revelation's purpose.
