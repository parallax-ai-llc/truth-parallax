## Shia Commentary

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War), with its 75 verses from the Medinan period, is a surah revealed after the Battle of Badr, addressing the distribution of war spoils, the divine assistance that ensured victory, the ethics of warfare, and the relationship between faith, courage, and trust in God's plan. Shia exegesis approaches this surah through the interpretive lens of the Ahl al-Bayt — the Prophet's family — whose teachings, preserved in the hadith collections of Twelver Shi'ism, provide both the exoteric (*zahir*) and esoteric (*batin*) dimensions of the text.

**Allamah Tabataba'i** in *Al-Mizan fi Tafsir al-Quran* offers the most comprehensive modern Shia commentary on this surah, combining philosophical depth with close textual analysis. His distinctive method of "interpreting the Quran by the Quran" (*tafsir al-Quran bil-Quran*) identifies internal cross-references and thematic connections that illuminate the surah's meaning within the broader Quranic discourse. He supplements this with narrations from the Imams that reveal layers of meaning inaccessible through linguistic analysis alone.

**Al-Tabrisi** in *Majma' al-Bayan* provides a balanced commentary that engages with both Sunni and Shia interpretive traditions, making it a valuable resource for understanding how this surah has been read across the sectarian divide. His work demonstrates the Shia commitment to rigorous Arabic linguistics and rhetorical analysis alongside the distinctive emphasis on the authority of the Imams as the Quran's authoritative interpreters.

The Medinan legislative content of this surah is particularly significant in Shia jurisprudence (*fiqh Ja'fari*), where the rulings of the twelve Imams — especially Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq, the founder of the Ja'fari school — provide authoritative elaboration on the Quranic provisions. Shia scholars emphasize that the Prophet designated the Ahl al-Bayt as the inseparable companion to the Quran (the hadith of *al-thaqalayn*), making their interpretive authority essential for correctly understanding the surah's legal and theological content.
