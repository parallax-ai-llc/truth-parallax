In Shia Islam, Al-Fatiha is equally central to prayer and devotion, but its interpretation carries distinctive emphases tied to the doctrine of the Imamate. **Allamah Tabataba'i** in *Al-Mizan* provides a profound analysis, arguing that Al-Fatiha contains the entire Quran in condensed form, just as the *bismillah* contains Al-Fatiha, and the opening letter *ba'* contains the *bismillah* — a concept reflecting the Shia emphasis on layered, esoteric meaning.

The "straight path" (*al-sirat al-mustaqim*) in Shia tafsir is closely identified with the path of the Ahl al-Bayt. Narrations from Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq identify the "straight path" as the path of Ali ibn Abi Talib and the Imams who follow him. This interpretation does not exclude the broader meanings of guidance and righteousness but adds a specific referent: true guidance requires following the divinely appointed leaders.

"Those whom You have favored" is understood as the prophets, the truthful ones (*siddiqin*), the martyrs, and the righteous — with the Imams occupying the highest rank among the favored. The prayer thus becomes not only a request for moral guidance but an affirmation of the necessity of following the Ahl al-Bayt as the authorized interpreters of God's will.
