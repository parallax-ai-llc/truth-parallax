## Overview

Surah Al-Ahqaf (The Sand Dunes), the 46th chapter of the Quran with 35 verses, holds particular significance in Twelver Shia exegesis. Shia scholars approach this surah through the interpretive tradition established by the Ahl al-Bayt, drawing on narrations from the Twelve Imams that provide layers of meaning beyond the apparent (*zahir*) text.

## The Ahl al-Bayt Interpretive Tradition

In the Shia tafsir tradition, notably works such as Tafsir al-Qummi, Tafsir al-Ayyashi, and the more recent al-Mizan by Allamah Tabataba'i, this surah is read with attention to its esoteric (*batin*) dimensions. Tabataba'i's philosophical-mystical approach situates the surah within a comprehensive Quranic worldview, emphasizing the interconnection of its themes with the broader structure of divine guidance as transmitted through the Prophetic household.

## Imamate and Wilayah

Shia commentators identify within this surah allusions to the authority (*wilayah*) of the Imams and the necessity of divinely appointed leadership for the Muslim community. Narrations from Imam al-Baqir, Imam al-Sadiq, and other Imams illuminate the verses' deeper significance, connecting them to the central Shia doctrines of divine justice (*adl*) and the continuing guidance of the Ummah through the Imam of the age.
