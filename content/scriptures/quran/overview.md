## Overview

The Quran (Arabic: القرآن, *al-Qur'ān*, "the Recitation") is the central religious text of Islam, believed by Muslims to be the literal word of God (Allah) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Jibril (Gabriel) over a period of approximately twenty-three years, from 610 CE until his death in 632 CE. It is regarded as the final and complete revelation, superseding all previous scriptures.

## Structure

The Quran consists of 114 chapters (*surahs*), arranged roughly by length from longest to shortest rather than chronologically:

1. **Meccan Surahs** (approximately 86): Revealed before the Hijra (622 CE), generally shorter, focused on theology, eschatology, and moral exhortation
2. **Medinan Surahs** (approximately 28): Revealed after the Hijra, generally longer, addressing law, community governance, and interfaith relations

Each surah is divided into verses (*ayat*, singular *ayah*, meaning "signs"). The total number of verses is traditionally counted at 6,236.

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Quran holds distinct significance across multiple interpretive traditions:

- **Sunni Islam**: Interpreted through the lens of hadith, consensus (*ijma'*), and the four major schools of jurisprudence
- **Shia Islam**: Interpreted with emphasis on the authority of the Ahl al-Bayt and the Imams as divinely guided exegetes
- **Sufi Islam**: Read for its esoteric (*batin*) meanings alongside the exoteric (*zahir*), seeking spiritual transformation
- **Salafi Islam**: Emphasizes return to the understanding of the *salaf* (early generations), rejecting later theological innovations
- **Academic**: Studied through historical-critical, literary, and comparative methods
- **Comparative**: Examined alongside other scriptures to identify shared themes and divergences

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Tawhid | The absolute oneness of God — the central theological principle |
| Risalah | Prophethood and the chain of messengers from Adam to Muhammad |
| Akhirah | The afterlife, resurrection, judgment, paradise, and hellfire |
| Guidance (*Huda*) | The Quran as a guide for personal conduct and communal life |
| Justice (*'Adl*) | Divine justice and the imperative of social equity |
| Mercy (*Rahmah*) | God's encompassing mercy, invoked at the start of all but one surah |
| Covenant | The primordial covenant between God and humanity (7:172) |
