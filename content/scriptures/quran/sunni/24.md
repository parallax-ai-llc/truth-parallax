## Sunni Commentary

Surah An-Nur (The Light), comprising 64 verses revealed in the Medinan period, is a surah establishing detailed social and sexual ethics, including laws against slander and false accusation, rules of modesty and conduct, the famous 'Verse of Light' comparing God's light to a lamp in a niche, and norms for household privacy. In mainstream Sunni exegesis, this surah is situated within the broader Quranic framework of community law, social legislation, and the practical governance of the nascent Muslim state.

**Al-Tabari** in *Jami' al-Bayan* provides extensive narration-based commentary on this surah, compiling reports from the Companions and the early exegetes (*tabi'in*) to establish the circumstances of revelation (*asbab al-nuzul*) and the intended meanings of its key verses. His methodology of presenting multiple interpretive opinions before indicating his preference exemplifies the classical Sunni approach of grounding exegesis in transmitted authority while exercising scholarly judgment.

**Ibn Kathir** in *Tafsir al-Quran al-Azim* offers a more streamlined hadith-based reading, focusing on authentic prophetic traditions that illuminate the surah's themes. He is characteristically cautious about accepting Isra'iliyyat (Judeo-Christian traditions) without prophetic corroboration, representing the Sunni commitment to methodological rigor in tafsir. **Al-Qurtubi** supplements this with extensive legal analysis, drawing out the juridical implications of the surah's verses across the four Sunni schools of law.

The Medinan context of this surah is significant for Sunni legal methodology (*usul al-fiqh*), as many of its verses contribute directly to the body of Quranic legislation. Sunni jurists from the Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali schools have extracted detailed rulings from its provisions, sometimes reaching different conclusions through their respective methodological frameworks while maintaining a shared commitment to the Quran and Sunnah as primary sources.
