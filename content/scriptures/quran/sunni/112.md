## Overview

Surah Al-Ikhlas (The Sincerity), consisting of 4 verses, is a Meccan surah located in the final portion of the Quran. In the Sunni exegetical tradition, this surah addresses themes central to the Meccan revelations: the oneness of God, the certainty of the Day of Judgment, and the moral accountability of every human being.

## Sunni Exegetical Tradition

The classical Sunni mufassirun — including **al-Tabari**, **Ibn Kathir**, **al-Qurtubi**, and **al-Baghawi** — approach this surah through a combination of linguistic analysis, hadith-based interpretation (*tafsir bil-ma'thur*), and theological reflection. The surah's placement among the short, powerful Meccan surahs reflects its role in the early preaching of Islam, where concise and rhetorically striking passages were essential for conveying the urgency of the monotheistic message.

**Al-Sa'di** in *Taysir al-Karim al-Rahman* emphasizes the surah's capacity to awaken the heedless heart through its direct address and vivid imagery. **Ibn Ashur** in *al-Tahrir wa al-Tanwir* notes the surah's literary cohesion and its contribution to the overall Quranic discourse on divine power and human responsibility.

## Theological Significance

In Sunni aqidah, Surah Al-Ikhlas reinforces foundational beliefs including *tawhid* (divine oneness), *risalah* (prophethood), and *akhirah* (the hereafter). The surah is regularly recited in the five daily prayers and is part of the curriculum in traditional Quran memorization programs across the Sunni world.
