Al-Fatiha holds a uniquely exalted position in Sunni Islam. The Prophet Muhammad declared it "the greatest surah in the Quran" (Sahih al-Bukhari), and it is recited in every *rak'ah* (unit) of the five daily prayers, making it the most frequently recited passage of the Quran. Without its recitation, the prayer is considered invalid according to the majority of Sunni jurists, based on the hadith: "There is no prayer for the one who does not recite the Opening of the Book."

Sunni commentators emphasize that Al-Fatiha is a comprehensive summary of the entire Quran. **Al-Tabari** explains that its seven verses contain the essential themes: praise and glorification of God (verses 1-4), the declaration of exclusive worship and reliance upon God (verse 5), and the supplication for guidance (verses 6-7). **Ibn Kathir** notes that the division between praise and petition reflects a hadith qudsi in which God says: "I have divided the prayer between Myself and My servant into two halves."

The phrase "the straight path" (*al-sirat al-mustaqim*) is understood as the path of the Prophet's Sunnah, the Quran's guidance, and the consensus of the righteous community. The final verse's reference to "those who have earned [Your] anger" and "those who have gone astray" is traditionally interpreted, based on prophetic hadith, as referring to groups who knew the truth but rejected it and those who went astray through ignorance, respectively.
