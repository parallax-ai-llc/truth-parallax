## Overview

Surah Al-Haqqah (The Inevitable Reality) is the 69th chapter of the Quran, containing 52 verses. In mainstream Sunni scholarship, this surah is understood through the lens of classical tafsir works by Ibn Kathir, al-Tabari, and al-Qurtubi, who collectively establish the interpretive framework that has guided Sunni understanding for centuries.

## Key Themes and Classical Interpretation

Sunni exegetes emphasize the surah's contribution to core Islamic theology, particularly its treatment of divine attributes and human responsibility. Al-Tabari's *Jami' al-Bayan* provides extensive linguistic analysis of the Arabic text, drawing on reports from the Companions and Successors to clarify the intended meaning. Ibn Kathir supplements this with hadith-based interpretation, connecting the surah's themes to the broader Prophetic tradition and the circumstances of revelation (*asbab al-nuzul*).

## Jurisprudential and Ethical Dimensions

The legal implications drawn from this surah by the four Sunni madhahib (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali) reflect both consensus and legitimate scholarly disagreement. Al-Qurtubi's *al-Jami' li-Ahkam al-Quran* is particularly valuable for extracting the legal rulings embedded within the verses, while al-Razi's *Mafatih al-Ghayb* provides rational theological analysis consistent with Ash'ari and Maturidi creedal frameworks.
