## Overview

Surah An-Naba, consisting of 40 verses, opens with a question about "the great news" (*al-naba' al-'azim*) — understood in Sunni tafsir as the Day of Resurrection. This surah is also known as *Amma* after its first word, and it begins the 30th *juz'* of the Quran (*Juz' Amma*), perhaps the most widely memorized portion of the Quran across the Muslim world.

## Sunni Exegetical Tradition

**Ibn Kathir** identifies the "great news" as the Day of Resurrection, about which the disbelievers of Mecca disputed. The surah proceeds to marshal evidence of God's creative power — the earth spread out, mountains as pegs, the creation of pairs, sleep as rest, night as a covering, day for livelihood, the seven heavens, and the sun as a blazing lamp — all pointing toward God's ability to resurrect the dead.

**Al-Baghawi** and **al-Sa'di** both emphasize the logical structure: the God who created all these things from nothing can certainly recreate humanity after death. Verses 17-30 describe the Day of Judgment and the fate of transgressors, while verses 31-36 depict the rewards of the righteous.

## Place in Worship

This surah holds a special place in Sunni devotional life as the opening of Juz' Amma. It is among the first surahs children learn, and its vivid eschatological imagery establishes foundational beliefs about the hereafter from an early age.
