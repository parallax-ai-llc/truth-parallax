## Overview

Surah Al-Infitar (The Cleaving), consisting of 19 verses, is interpreted in the Salafi tradition according to the methodology of the righteous predecessors (*salaf al-salih*). The Salafi approach prioritizes the Quran's apparent meaning, supports interpretation through authentic hadith and reports from the Companions, and rejects speculative theology (*kalam*), philosophical interpretation, and esoteric hermeneutics.

## Salafi Exegetical Approach

The primary tafsir references in Salafi scholarship for this surah are **Ibn Kathir's** *Tafsir al-Quran al-Azim*, **al-Sa'di's** *Taysir al-Karim al-Rahman*, and **al-Baghawi's** *Ma'alim al-Tanzil*. These commentaries are valued for their reliance on transmitted reports and their avoidance of unwarranted rationalist speculation. **Shaykh Ibn Baz** and **Shaykh Ibn Uthaymeen** have also provided explanatory notes and lectures on this surah that clarify its meanings according to the Salafi methodology.

The Salafi reading insists on affirming the literal reality of the surah's eschatological descriptions — paradise, hellfire, the scales of deeds, and the events of the Day of Judgment — without reinterpretation (*ta'wil*). This approach is rooted in the principle that the Quran's words are to be understood according to their apparent Arabic meaning unless a clear textual indicator requires otherwise.

## Theological Significance

In Salafi theology, this surah reinforces the foundational elements of *aqidah* (creed): belief in Allah's oneness (*tawhid*), the truthfulness of His Messenger, and the reality of the Last Day. The surah also serves the purposes of *da'wah* (calling to Islam) by providing clear, direct arguments for the truth of the Islamic message that can be conveyed without philosophical elaboration.
