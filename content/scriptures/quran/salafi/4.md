## Salafi Commentary

Surah An-Nisa (The Women), containing 176 verses from the Medinan period, is a surah focused on social justice, particularly regarding women, orphans, and inheritance law, establishing detailed legal rulings on marriage, family relations, and community governance while emphasizing justice and equity. Salafi exegesis approaches this surah with strict adherence to the understanding of the *salaf al-salih* (pious predecessors) — the Companions, their successors, and the generation after them — rejecting later theological innovations and interpretive methodologies that depart from this foundational standard.

**Ibn Taymiyyah** and **Ibn al-Qayyim** provide the theological framework through which Salafi scholars read this surah. Their approach insists on taking the text at its apparent meaning (*zahir*) unless clear evidence from the Quran, authentic Sunnah, or consensus of the Companions requires otherwise. Allegorical interpretations (*ta'wil*) of the type practiced by Ash'ari theologians and Sufi mystics are rejected as unauthorized departures from the prophetic methodology.

**Ibn Uthaymin** in *Tafsir al-Quran al-Karim* provides a detailed Salafi commentary that emphasizes the surah's implications for *tawhid* — the absolute oneness of God in His lordship, His names and attributes, and the exclusive right to worship. Every verse affirming God's sovereignty, creative power, or attributes is read as reinforcing the obligation to direct all worship exclusively to God, without intermediaries, saints, or innovated practices.

The legislative dimensions of this Medinan surah are particularly important in Salafi methodology, which seeks to derive rulings directly from the Quran and Sunnah without excessive reliance on the later juristic traditions of the four *madhabs*. While respecting the classical scholars, Salafi interpreters emphasize that scholarly opinion must always be evaluated against the primary sources, and that blind imitation (*taqlid*) of any single school is discouraged in favor of following the strongest evidence.
