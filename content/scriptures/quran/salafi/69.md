## Overview

Surah Al-Haqqah (The Inevitable Reality) is the 69th surah of the Quran, containing 52 verses. In the Salafi tradition, this surah is interpreted strictly according to the understanding of the *Salaf al-Salih* (the righteous predecessors), comprising the Companions, the Successors, and the generation that followed them. This methodology prioritizes the apparent meaning of the text and authentic narrations over rationalist or mystical speculation.

## Methodology of Interpretation

Salafi scholars such as Ibn Taymiyyah, Ibn al-Qayyim, and in the modern period, Ibn Uthaymeen and Ibn Baz, approach this surah with an emphasis on *tawhid* (divine oneness) in its three dimensions: *Rububiyyah* (lordship), *Uluhiyyah* (worship), and *Asma' wa'l-Sifat* (names and attributes). Every verse is examined for its implications regarding the purity of monotheistic worship and the rejection of *shirk* (associating partners with God) in all its manifest and hidden forms.

## Application and Da'wah

The Salafi reading of this surah emphasizes practical application (*'amal*) over theoretical abstraction. Verses are connected directly to matters of creed (*aqidah*), worship (*ibadah*), and conduct (*mu'amalat*), with particular attention to correcting innovations (*bid'ah*) that have entered Muslim practice. The surah's teachings are presented as a call to return to the authentic prophetic model, free from the cultural accretions and philosophical influences that Salafi scholars view as distortions of the original message.
