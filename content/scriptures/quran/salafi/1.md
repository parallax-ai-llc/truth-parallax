Salafi interpretation of Al-Fatiha emphasizes its foundational role in establishing the three categories of *tawhid* (monotheism) that form the backbone of Salafi theology. **Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab** and subsequent Salafi scholars derive from these seven verses the essential framework of Islamic belief.

"Lord of all worlds" (*Rabb al-'alamin*) establishes *tawhid al-rububiyyah* — the oneness of God in His lordship, sovereignty, and creative power. "The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful" affirms *tawhid al-asma' wa al-sifat* — the oneness of God in His names and attributes, which must be affirmed as they appear without metaphorical reinterpretation. "You alone we worship" establishes *tawhid al-uluhiyyah* — the oneness of God in worship, the category that Salafi theology considers most frequently violated through practices like saint veneration and shrine visitation.

**Ibn Uthaymin** in his commentary emphasizes that "You alone we worship and You alone we ask for help" is the surah's central verse, establishing that all acts of worship — prayer, supplication, vowing, sacrificing — must be directed exclusively to God. Any petition to a deceased person, saint, or intermediary constitutes *shirk* (polytheism) and violates this foundational Quranic principle.
