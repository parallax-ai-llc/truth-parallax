## Salafi Commentary

Surah Al-Isra (The Night Journey), containing 111 verses from the Meccan period, is a surah opening with the Prophet's miraculous night journey from Mecca to Jerusalem, followed by ethical commandments paralleling the Ten Commandments, reflections on the Children of Israel, and the Quran's literary challenge to produce anything like it. Salafi exegesis approaches this surah with strict adherence to the understanding of the *salaf al-salih* (pious predecessors) — the Companions, their successors, and the generation after them — rejecting later theological innovations and interpretive methodologies that depart from this foundational standard.

**Ibn Taymiyyah** and **Ibn al-Qayyim** provide the theological framework through which Salafi scholars read this surah. Their approach insists on taking the text at its apparent meaning (*zahir*) unless clear evidence from the Quran, authentic Sunnah, or consensus of the Companions requires otherwise. Allegorical interpretations (*ta'wil*) of the type practiced by Ash'ari theologians and Sufi mystics are rejected as unauthorized departures from the prophetic methodology.

**Ibn Uthaymin** in *Tafsir al-Quran al-Karim* provides a detailed Salafi commentary that emphasizes the surah's implications for *tawhid* — the absolute oneness of God in His lordship, His names and attributes, and the exclusive right to worship. Every verse affirming God's sovereignty, creative power, or attributes is read as reinforcing the obligation to direct all worship exclusively to God, without intermediaries, saints, or innovated practices.

As a Meccan surah focused on theological fundamentals, Salafi commentators find in its verses the essential arguments against polytheism (*shirk*) in all its forms — whether the worship of idols, saints, graves, or any created being. The surah's warnings to past nations who rejected monotheism are read as directly applicable to contemporary Muslims who have, in the Salafi view, introduced innovations into their worship that compromise the purity of *tawhid*.
