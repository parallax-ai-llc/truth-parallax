## Academic Analysis

Surah Al-Baqarah (The Cow), comprising 286 verses and classified by the traditional Islamic chronology as Medinan, is the longest surah of the Quran, covering a vast range of topics including faith and disbelief, the stories of Adam, Abraham, and Moses, legal rulings on prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, marriage, and financial transactions, and the establishment of the Muslim community in Medina. Academic scholarship examines this surah through historical-critical, literary, and comparative methodologies, seeking to understand its composition, rhetorical structure, and relationship to the broader Late Antique cultural and religious environment.

**Angelika Neuwirth** situates this surah within the evolving Quranic discourse, analyzing its rhetorical features — oath formulae, challenge passages, narrative retellings, and eschatological warnings — as elements of a sophisticated oral-literary composition that engaged its original audience in real-time theological debate. Her work on the surah's internal structure reveals patterns of ring composition and chiastic arrangement that point to deliberate literary design rather than haphazard compilation.

**Gabriel Said Reynolds** in *The Qur'an and the Bible* traces the surah's intertextual connections to biblical and post-biblical literature, identifying points of engagement with Jewish midrashic traditions, Syriac Christian homiletic literature, and other Late Antique religious texts. These connections do not imply simple "borrowing" but rather the Quran's active participation in a shared monotheistic discourse, reworking familiar narratives for its own theological purposes.

The question of the surah's dating and compositional history remains an active area of scholarly debate. While the traditional Medinan classification is generally accepted as a useful framework, scholars like **Nicolai Sinai** and **Fred Donner** have refined the chronological analysis using internal linguistic and thematic criteria, sometimes suggesting that individual surahs may contain material from different periods, complicating the simple Meccan/Medinan binary.
