## Overview

Surah Al-Haqqah (The Inevitable Reality), the 69th surah of the Quran containing 52 verses, has been the subject of significant scholarly analysis within the academic study of the Quran. Modern critical scholarship examines this surah through historical, literary, and comparative methodologies, situating it within the broader context of late antique religious discourse.

## Historical-Critical Analysis

Contemporary scholars such as Angelika Neuwirth, Gabriel Said Reynolds, and Nicolai Sinai have analyzed this surah's compositional structure, identifying literary patterns that reflect both the oral performative context of early Quranic proclamation and its engagement with pre-existing biblical and parabiblical traditions. The surah's rhetorical strategies, including oath formulas, narrative insertions, and polemical passages, are examined as features of a distinctive late antique textual genre.

## Linguistic and Intertextual Dimensions

Academic analysis of this surah's vocabulary reveals connections to Aramaic, Syriac, and Ethiopic lexical fields, suggesting a multilingual cultural environment for early Quranic composition. The *Corpus Coranicum* project and related digital humanities initiatives have enabled more precise mapping of the surah's intertextual relationships with Jewish, Christian, and Manichaean literary traditions circulating in the seventh-century Hijaz and broader Near East.
