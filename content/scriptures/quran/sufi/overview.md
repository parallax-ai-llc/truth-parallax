## Overview

Sufi (*tasawwuf*) interpretation of the Quran seeks the inner, spiritual dimensions of the text. Rather than replacing the literal and legal meanings, Sufi tafsir adds layers of spiritual insight (*ishara*), reading the Quran as a guide for the soul's journey toward God. This approach understands the Quran not merely as a book of law and theology but as a living encounter with the Divine — a text that speaks to the heart of the seeker at every stage of the spiritual path.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Tafsir Ishari (Allusive Interpretation)
This method reads Quranic verses as containing subtle spiritual allusions (*isharat*) beyond their surface meaning. The verse "Guide us on the straight path" (1:6) becomes a prayer for spiritual illumination on the path to God, not merely moral uprightness.

### Experiential Hermeneutics
Sufi interpreters often draw on their own spiritual experiences (*dhawq*, "taste") to illuminate the text. This is considered valid when it does not contradict the apparent meaning, sound Arabic, or established doctrine.

### Symbolic Reading
Quranic narratives are read as allegories of the soul's condition. Moses before the burning bush becomes the seeker encountering divine light; Abraham's sacrifice becomes the surrender of the ego.

## Theological Emphases

- **Divine Love (*'Ishq*)**: The relationship between God and humanity is fundamentally one of love
- **Annihilation (*Fana'*)**: The dissolution of the ego-self in the awareness of God
- **Gnosis (*Ma'rifah*)**: Direct experiential knowledge of God, beyond rational theology
- **The Heart (*Qalb*)**: The Quran addresses the spiritual heart as the locus of divine encounter
- **Unity of Being (*Wahdat al-Wujud*)**: Ibn Arabi's doctrine that all existence is a manifestation of divine reality

## Major Commentators

| Commentator | Era | Work |
|-------------|-----|------|
| Al-Tustari | 9th century | *Tafsir al-Tustari* |
| Al-Sulami | 10th century | *Haqa'iq al-Tafsir* (Realities of Interpretation) |
| Al-Qushayri | 11th century | *Lata'if al-Isharat* (Subtleties of Allusions) |
| Ibn Arabi | 12th-13th century | Various works; *Fusus al-Hikam* |
| Rumi | 13th century | *Masnavi* (extensive Quranic commentary in verse) |
| Ismail Haqqi | 18th century | *Ruh al-Bayan* |
