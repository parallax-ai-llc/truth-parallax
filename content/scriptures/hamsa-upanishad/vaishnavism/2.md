Section 2 provides practical instructions for meditating on the hamsa mantra — the sound of breathing itself. With each inhalation, the sound "ham" naturally arises; with each exhalation, "sa." Vaishnava commentators understand this as the continuous, involuntary chanting of God's name that occurs in every living being. Making this unconscious chanting conscious transforms every breath into an act of devotion.

The meditation practice described — focusing attention on the breath while mentally hearing "hamsa, hamsa" — is read as a form of *nama-japa* (repetition of the divine name) that requires no special equipment or setting, making it accessible to all devotees at all times.
