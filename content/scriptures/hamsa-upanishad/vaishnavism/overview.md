## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters connect the *hamsa* (swan) with Vishnu, who rides the cosmic swan and whose supreme form is the *paramahamsa* — the great swan of divine consciousness. The Upanishad's teaching on the hamsa mantra is read as instruction in meditation on the Lord's presence in every breath. Each inhalation (*ham*) and exhalation (*sa*) is a natural repetition of the divine name.

## Theological Emphases

The swan's legendary ability to separate milk from water symbolizes the devotee's discrimination between the Lord and the material world. The *paramahamsa* — the supreme swan — is the sage who has perfected this discrimination, seeing Vishnu in all things and all things in Vishnu. The hamsa meditation thus becomes a form of constant devotion, transforming every breath into an act of worship.
