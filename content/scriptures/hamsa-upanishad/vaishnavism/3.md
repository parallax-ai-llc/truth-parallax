Section 3 describes the *paramahamsa* — the supreme sage who has perfected hamsa meditation and attained liberation while still alive. Vaishnava commentators identify the paramahamsa as the *maha-bhagavata* — the great devotee who sees Vishnu everywhere and in everything. Such a sage wanders freely, unbound by social conventions, sustained by God's grace alone.

The paramahamsa's characteristics — equanimity, freedom from desire, universal compassion — are read as the fruits of complete surrender to Vishnu. The sage has become a living embodiment of the divine will, acting as God's instrument in the world.
