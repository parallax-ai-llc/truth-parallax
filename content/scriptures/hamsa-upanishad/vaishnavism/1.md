Section 1 introduces the hamsa as the supreme self dwelling in all beings. The swan's ability to separate milk from water symbolizes the divine faculty of discrimination — the ability to discern the eternal from the transient. Vaishnava commentators identify the hamsa with Vishnu, whose cosmic form pervades all creation while remaining pure and untouched by material contamination.

The teaching that the hamsa dwells in the heart of every being confirms the Vaishnava doctrine of the Paramatma (Supersoul) — Vishnu's presence within every creature as the inner witness and guide.
