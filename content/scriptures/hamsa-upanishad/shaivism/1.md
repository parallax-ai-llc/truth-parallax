Section 1 presents the hamsa as the symbol of the supreme consciousness that pervades all beings. Shaiva interpreters read the hamsa as Shiva in His universal aspect — the consciousness that breathes through every creature. The swan's discrimination between milk and water represents consciousness distinguishing itself from its objects, recognizing its own nature amidst the flow of experience.

The teaching establishes that the hamsa is not something to be attained but something to be recognized — it is already present in every breath, silently affirming the identity of the individual and the universal.
