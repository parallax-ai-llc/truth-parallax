Section 2's meditation instructions center on the hamsa mantra as Shiva's breath moving through the body. The reversal of "hamsa" into "so'ham" ("I am That") reveals the mantra's deepest meaning: every breath is an unconscious affirmation of one's identity with Shiva. The meditation practice involves bringing conscious attention to this natural process, transforming automatic breathing into deliberate self-recognition.

Kashmir Shaiva commentators connect the hamsa meditation with the *vikalpa-kshaya* (dissolution of thought-constructs) that occurs when attention rests fully in the breath. As the mind quiets, the hamsa mantra reveals the silence of pure consciousness between the breaths.
