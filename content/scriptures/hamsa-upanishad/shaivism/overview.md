## Overview

Shaiva interpreters read the Hamsa Upanishad as a teaching on the *So'ham* mantra — "I am That" — which is the natural sound of breathing heard when the hamsa mantra (*ham-sa*) is reversed. This mantra expresses the fundamental identity of the individual self with Shiva. Every breath unconsciously affirms this identity; meditation makes it conscious.

## Theological Emphases

In Kashmir Shaivism, the hamsa represents the creative and dissolving breaths of Shiva — the outgoing breath (*prana*) as creation and the incoming breath (*apana*) as dissolution. The hamsa mantra is Shiva's own self-recognition occurring spontaneously in every being. The *paramahamsa* is the one who has recognized this — the sage who knows that the breath of life is Shiva's own breath.
