The paramahamsa described in Section 3 is the Shaiva *jivanmukta* — the one liberated while still alive, whose every action is the spontaneous expression of Shiva-consciousness. The supreme sage has realized that the hamsa — the self breathing through the body — is none other than Shiva, and this recognition has dissolved all limitation.

The paramahamsa's freedom from conventional rules and social expectations reflects the Shaiva understanding that liberation transcends all dharma. The sage lives in the world but is not of it — consciousness has recognized itself as unlimited and can never again be confined by any identity.
