Madhva reads Section 1's hamsa as the Supreme Lord dwelling within all beings. The swan symbolizes both God (the cosmic hamsa) and the individual soul (the migrating bird), with the crucial distinction that God is the original and the soul is the dependent reflection. The swan's discrimination represents the devotee's ability to distinguish between truth and falsehood, God and illusion.

The teaching that the hamsa is present in all beings confirms God's omnipresence without implying that God and the souls are identical.
