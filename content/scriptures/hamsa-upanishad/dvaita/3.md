Madhva reads the paramahamsa as the perfected devotee of Vishnu — one whose love for God has become so complete that it pervades every moment of life. The supreme sage is not one who has lost individuality in an impersonal absolute but one whose individuality has been perfected through intimate relationship with the Lord.

The paramahamsa's freedom from worldly conventions reflects not the transcendence of all dharma but the embodiment of the highest dharma — unconditional love for God. Such a sage acts spontaneously because every action arises from devotion.
