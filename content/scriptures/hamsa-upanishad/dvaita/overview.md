## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the hamsa as a symbol of the individual soul (*jiva*) journeying through creation toward its ultimate goal — God. Just as the swan migrates across vast distances, the soul travels through the cycle of birth and death seeking its true home in Vishnu's presence. The hamsa mantra is read as the soul's constant, often unconscious, affirmation of its relationship with God.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the *paramahamsa* is not one who has realized identity with Brahman but one who has perfected devotion to Vishnu through lifelong practice and divine grace. The swan's discrimination represents the devotee's ability to distinguish between actions that lead toward God and those that lead away. The hamsa meditation is valued as a devotional practice that attunes the practitioner to God's presence in every breath.
