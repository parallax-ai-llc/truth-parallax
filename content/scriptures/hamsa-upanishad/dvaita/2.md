Madhva's tradition reads the hamsa meditation as a devotional practice — each breath offered to God as a prayer. The repetition of "hamsa" is understood as the soul's continuous, often unconscious, calling out to the Lord. Making this calling conscious through meditation deepens the devotee's awareness of God's constant presence.

For Dvaita, the mantra "hamsa" does not assert identity with Brahman but expresses the soul's connection to God. The meditation strengthens this connection, drawing the practitioner into deeper awareness of Vishnu's sustaining presence in every breath.
