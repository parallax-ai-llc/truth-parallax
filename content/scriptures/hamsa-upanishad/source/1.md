---
title: "Hamsa Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "hamsa-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Gautama said to Sanatkumara: O Lord, what is the seat of the Hamsa? What is its meditation? Sanatkumara replied: Listen, O Gautama. He who knows this is not tainted by sin.

**2** The Hamsa dwells in the heart of all creatures. As oil dwells in sesame seeds, as butter in curds, as fragrance in flowers, so dwells the Atman in all.

**3** He who meditates upon the Hamsa, which resides in the lotus of the heart, attains liberation. The wise man should contemplate upon the syllable Om, which is the Hamsa itself.

**4** The Hamsa is the supreme light. It pervades all beings. It is the Self that is in the sun and in fire, in lightning and in the wind.

**5** He who knows the Hamsa in the body, who knows its flight, who knows its resting place, he is freed from the bonds of death. The Hamsa is none other than Brahman itself.

**6** The syllable Ha represents Shiva, the syllable Sa represents Shakti. He who constantly meditates upon the Hamsa becomes one with Brahman.

**7** As a lamp in a windless place does not flicker, so is the yogi who has controlled his mind through meditation on the Hamsa. He sees the Self by the Self.

**8** This Hamsa is without beginning and without end. It is imperishable and eternal. He who knows this knows the Vedas. Thus spoke Sanatkumara.
