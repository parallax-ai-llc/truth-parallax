---
title: "Hamsa Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "hamsa-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Hamsa, which is the Paramatman, assumes the form of the Pranava. It has eight limbs and is endowed with three gunas. It has three seats: the right eye, the left eye, and between the eyebrows.

**2** The Hamsa pervades the body from the sole of the foot to the crown of the head. It moves through the nadis as the breath moves. The wise one should meditate upon it.

**3** When the breath goes out with the sound Ham, and returns with the sound Sa, the creature repeats the mantra Hamsa, Hamsa. Day and night, the living being repeats this mantra twenty-one thousand six hundred times.

**4** He who knows this secret teaching is freed from all sins. There is no knowledge equal to this, no austerity equal to this, and no truth equal to this.

**5** The practitioner should sit in a secluded place, in the lotus posture, and meditate upon the Hamsa in the lotus of the heart. He should withdraw the senses from their objects, as the tortoise withdraws its limbs.

**6** He who practices this meditation sees the light within that is like ten thousand suns. This is the light of the Self, the light of Brahman. He who sees it is freed from all bonds.

**7** The ten sounds arise from this meditation: the first is like the buzzing of a bee, the second like a flute, the third like a harp, the fourth like a bell, the fifth like thunder, the sixth like a drum, the seventh like a war drum, the eighth like a great drum, the ninth like a trumpet, and the tenth like the rolling of thunder.

**8** When the mind is absorbed in the tenth sound, the yogi transcends all phenomena. He becomes one with Brahman, the supreme reality. This is the teaching of the Hamsa.
