---
title: "Hamsa Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "hamsa-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Sanatkumara continued: The Hamsa is the jiva, and the jiva is the Hamsa. As long as the jiva is identified with the body, it is called Hamsa. When it realizes its true nature, it is called Paramahamsa.

**2** The Paramahamsa wanders the earth free of attachment. He has no home, no fixed abode. He is like the sky, untouched by the clouds that pass through it.

**3** For the Paramahamsa, there is no distinction between gold and clay, between friend and foe, between honor and dishonor. He sees the same Self in all beings.

**4** The Paramahamsa has gone beyond the three states of waking, dreaming, and deep sleep. He abides in the fourth state, turiya, the state of pure awareness.

**5** As the rivers lose their names and forms when they merge into the ocean, so the wise man who has realized the Self loses all distinctions and merges into Brahman.

**6** He who is free from desire, who is serene, who has controlled his mind, who sees the Self in all and all in the Self, he is the Paramahamsa. He is Brahman itself.

**7** This knowledge was taught by Brahma to Narada, by Narada to Vyasa, by Vyasa to Sanatkumara, and by Sanatkumara to Gautama and others.

**8** He who studies this Upanishad with devotion attains the state of Brahman. He is not born again. He is not born again. Such is the teaching. Om. Peace, peace, peace.
