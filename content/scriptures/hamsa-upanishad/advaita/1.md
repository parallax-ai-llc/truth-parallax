Section 1 introduces the hamsa as the supreme self — the non-dual Brahman present in all beings as the witness-consciousness. For Advaita, the swan's discrimination between milk and water is the metaphor for *viveka* — the fundamental discernment between the real (Brahman) and the unreal (the phenomenal world) that is the prerequisite for liberation.

The hamsa dwelling in the heart of all beings is the *kutastha* — the unchanging witness that remains the same in all states of consciousness. Recognizing this hamsa as one's own self is the essence of Advaita teaching.
