For Advaita, the paramahamsa of Section 3 is the highest type of sannyasi — one who has realized the identity of atman and Brahman and lives in unbroken non-dual awareness. The title *paramahamsa* is the most exalted designation in Shankara's monastic tradition, reserved for those rare beings who have transcended not only worldly attachment but also the attachment to spiritual practices and titles.

The paramahamsa needs nothing — no rituals, no possessions, no external supports — because the self is recognized as complete and self-sufficient. This is the culmination of the Advaita path: the sage who is Brahman-realized and lives as Brahman in the world.
