For Advaita, Section 2's hamsa meditation is an *upasana* (meditative worship) that purifies the mind and prepares it for the direct recognition of Brahman. The mantra "hamsa" — naturally reversed to "so'ham" — is a contemplative affirmation of non-duality: "I am That." The meditation practice leads the seeker from the gross level of physical breathing to the subtle level of pure awareness.

The instruction to focus on the breath and hear "hamsa" demonstrates the Advaita principle that the truth is already present — it does not need to be created but only recognized. Every breath already proclaims the identity of self and Brahman.
