## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Hamsa Upanishad teaches the identity of the individual self with Brahman through the natural mantra of breathing. The hamsa mantra (*ham-sa*), when reversed, becomes *so'ham* — "I am That" — the spontaneous affirmation of non-duality that occurs with every breath. The *paramahamsa* is the sage who has realized this identity and lives in the unbroken awareness of Brahman.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara's sannyasa tradition uses the title *paramahamsa* for the highest order of renunciants — those who have transcended all external marks and live in direct knowledge of Brahman. The swan's discrimination between milk and water symbolizes *viveka* — the ability to distinguish the real (Brahman) from the unreal (the world of appearances). The Hamsa Upanishad thus provides both a meditation technique and a description of its fruit.
