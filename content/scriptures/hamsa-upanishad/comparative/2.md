The hamsa meditation's use of the natural breath-sound as a mantra parallels breath-based contemplative practices across traditions. The Eastern Orthodox hesychasts synchronized the Jesus Prayer ("Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me") with breathing; Sufi practitioners coordinate the *dhikr* ("La ilaha illa'llah") with inhalation and exhalation; and Buddhist *anapanasati* uses bare attention to the breath as the foundation of mindfulness.

The idea that the body naturally produces a sacred sound finds parallels in the Kabbalistic concept of the breath itself as prayer, the Quaker notion of the Inner Light speaking in silence, and the Catholic tradition of the heart's continuous prayer ("the heart prays even when the mind sleeps").
