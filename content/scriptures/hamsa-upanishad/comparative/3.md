The paramahamsa — the sage beyond all conventions who embodies perfect spiritual freedom — parallels ideals of sainthood across traditions. The Christian *theosis* (divinization), the Sufi *insan al-kamil* (perfect human), the Buddhist *arhat* (the liberated one), and the Daoist sage who moves with the Tao all describe human beings who have so thoroughly realized ultimate truth that their lives become transparent to the divine.

The paramahamsa's transcendence of rules and conventions compares with the Zen master's iconoclasm, the Sufi *majdhub* (divinely intoxicated saint) who violates social norms, and the Christian "holy fool" (*yurodiviy*) tradition in Russian Orthodoxy.
