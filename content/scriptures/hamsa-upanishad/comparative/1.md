The hamsa as a symbol of the soul discerning truth from illusion parallels spiritual symbolism across traditions. The Sufi concept of the soul as a bird caged in the body (Rumi's *Masnavi*), the Christian dove as the Holy Spirit discerning truth, and the Egyptian phoenix rising from the ashes of the old self all use avian imagery for spiritual realities.

The hamsa's discrimination between milk and water compares with the biblical wheat-and-tares parable (Matthew 13:24-30) and the Egyptian weighing of the heart against the feather of Ma'at — both expressing the universal concern with discerning the genuine from the false.
