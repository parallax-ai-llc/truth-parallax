Section 1 draws on the rich symbolism of the hamsa (swan/goose) in Vedic and post-Vedic literature. The hamsa appears as a cosmic bird in Rig Veda 4.40 and as a symbol of the self in the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (4.3.11-12). The Hamsa Upanishad develops this symbolism into a systematic meditation teaching.

Scholars note the multiple layers of the hamsa symbol: it represents the migratory sun crossing the sky, the breath moving in and out, and the self traversing the cycle of rebirth. This polysemy made the hamsa one of the most versatile and enduring symbols in Indian religious culture.
