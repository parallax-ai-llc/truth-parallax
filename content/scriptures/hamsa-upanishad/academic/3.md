Section 3's description of the paramahamsa contributes to the history of the ascetic ideal in India. The paramahamsa represents the highest grade of renunciant — beyond even the ordinary sannyasi — who has transcended all rules and lives in complete freedom. Olivelle traces the development of the paramahamsa ideal from its Upanishadic roots through the medieval sannyasa traditions.

The characteristics attributed to the paramahamsa — wandering without possessions, beyond praise and blame, seeing the same in all beings — became the standard description of the ideal sage in Hindu literature, influencing both hagiography and philosophical texts.
