## Overview

The Poetic Edda represents one of the supreme achievements of medieval European literature. Its poems employ sophisticated verse forms — fornyrðislag for narrative, ljóðaháttr for wisdom sayings, málaháttr for heroic tales — that demonstrate mastery of alliterative technique and mythological allusion. The collection's influence on subsequent literature is immense, from the Prose Edda and Icelandic sagas through Wagner's Ring Cycle to Tolkien's legendarium. Literary analysis explores the poems' narrative structure, dramatic dialogue, imagery, and the interplay between oral tradition and written composition.
