## Overview

The Poetic Edda provides exceptionally rich material for comparative study across world mythological and religious traditions. Its creation narrative in Völuspá — featuring a primordial void (Ginnungagap), a cosmic sacrifice (Ymir), and a world tree (Yggdrasil) — invites comparison with creation myths from Vedic, Greek, Mesopotamian, and other traditions. The wisdom poetry attributed to Odin parallels instructional literature from ancient Egypt, Israel, and India. The eschatological vision of Ragnarök resonates with apocalyptic traditions in Zoroastrianism, Judaism, Christianity, and Hinduism.
