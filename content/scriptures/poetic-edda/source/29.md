---
title: "Hyndluljóð (The Poem of Hyndla)"
section: 29
---

**1** Awake, maid of maidens! Awake, my friend! Hyndla, sister, who in the cave dwells! Now is darkness of darknesses; let us ride together to Valhalla, to the holy temple.

**2** Let us ask the Father of Hosts to be gracious; he gives and bestows gold to the deserving. He gave to Hermod a helm and corslet, and to Sigmund a sword as gift.

**3** He gives victory to some, and to some wealth, eloquence to many, and to men understanding; fair winds he gives to sailors, to poets their art, and valour he gives to many a warrior.

**4** I will sacrifice to Thor, and of him make request, that ever he may be favourably disposed to thee, although he has little love for the brides of giants.

**5** From the stall now take one of thy wolves and let him run with my boar. Slow is thy beast on the gods' highway; I will not distress my noble steed.

**6** Freyja thou art, thou knowest full well; gazing at me with those lecherous eyes. Drive thy boar on the dark path now, and let us discover the lineage of heroes.

**7** Ottar the young is the son of Innstein, and Innstein was son of Alf the Old, Alf was son of Ulf, Ulf of Saefari, and Saefari of Svan the Red.

**8** The Norns I saw who shape the fates of men, by the World-Tree standing. Three maidens wise, in the hall beneath the tree; Urd is one named, Verdandi the next, and Skuld the third. These maidens the laws made, these maidens the lives of the children of men decreed, and their fates allotted.

**9** All will I tell thee, if thou wilt hear; such is the lore of the ages, and the wisdom of the Norns.
