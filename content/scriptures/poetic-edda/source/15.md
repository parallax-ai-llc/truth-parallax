---
title: "Skírnismál (The Lay of Skirnir — For Skírnir's Journey)"
section: 15
---

Freyr, the son of Njorth, had seated himself in Hlithskjalf, and looked over all the worlds. He looked into Jotunheim, and saw there a beautiful maiden, as she went from her father's house to her bower. Forthwith he felt a mighty love-sickness.

Skirnir spake:
"Tell me, Freyr, | foremost of the gods,
One thing I fain would know:
Why sittest thou here | in thy spacious hall,
Alone, my lord, all day?"

Freyr spake:
"How shall I tell thee, | thou hero young,
Of all my grief so great?
Though every day | the elfbeam dawns,
It lights not my longing heart."

Skirnir spake:
"Thy longings, methinks, | are not so large
That thou mayest not tell them to me;
Since in days of old | we were young together,
We two might trust each other well."

Freyr spake:
"In Gymir's courts | I beheld walking
A maiden dear to me;
Her arms gave light | wherewith gleamed all
Both air and all the sea.

"More dear to me | is that maiden sweet
Than to any youth of yore;
But none of the gods | or the elves will grant
That together we two shall live."

Skirnir spake:
"Give me the horse | that goes through the dark
And the fearsome flickering flame;
And the sword as well | that fights of itself
Against the giants' throng."

Then Skirnir rode into Jotunheim to Gymir's courts. There were fierce dogs bound before the gate of the fence which surrounded Gerth's hall.
