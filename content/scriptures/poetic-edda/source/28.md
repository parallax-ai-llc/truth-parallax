---
title: "Rígsþula (The Lay of Rig)"
section: 28
---

**1** In olden days, they say, there went along the green pathways a certain one of the Aesir, wise and mighty. His name was Rig, and he strode swiftly.

**2** He went on his way and came upon a dwelling. The door was ajar; he entered. A fire was on the floor, and a hoary couple sat there, Ai and Edda.

**3** Rig knew how to give them counsel. Then he went from thence, and laid himself down in the middle of the bed, and the couple on each side.

**4** He stayed three nights. Then he went on his way. Nine months later Edda bore a son. They sprinkled him with water and called him Thrall.

**5** He grew and thrived. On his hands was wrinkled skin, knobby knuckles, thick fingers, ugly face, crooked back, long heels.

**6** He began to use his strength, binding bast, making bundles, bearing faggots home. There came to the farm a girl with scarred feet, sunburnt arms, crooked nose. Her name was Thir.

**7** From them come the race of Thralls.

**8** Rig went on his way and came to a hall. The door stood open. He entered. A fire burned on the floor. A man and woman sat there; they were busy working. The man was shaping wood; his beard was trimmed, the hair of his brow well-cut. The woman was spinning. They were called Afi and Amma.

**9** He stayed three nights. Nine months later Amma bore a son. They sprinkled him with water and called him Karl.

**10** He was ruddy-faced, with twinkling eyes. He grew up. He tamed oxen, made ploughs, timbered houses, made carts, drove the plough. A woman came, with keys at her girdle, clad in goatskin. Her name was Snor. From them come the race of Karls.

**11** Rig went further. He came to a hall facing south, its door closed but not locked. He entered. The floor was strewn. A couple sat there, gazing into each other's eyes. The man was twisting bowstrings, bending elm-bows, making arrows. The woman was fair-skinned, bright-browed. She was called Modir, and he Fadir.

**12** He stayed three nights. Nine months later Modir bore a son. They wrapped him in silk. He was fair-haired, bright of cheek, sharp-eyed as a serpent. They called him Jarl. From him come the race of Earls and Kings.
