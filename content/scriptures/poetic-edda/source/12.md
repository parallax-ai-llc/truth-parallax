---
title: "Vafþrúðnismál (continued) — The Wisdom Contest"
section: 12
---

Othin spake:
"Tell me first, | if thy wisdom avails,
And thou knowest it, Vafthruthnir, well:
Whence came the earth | or the sky above?
Giant, tell me thy wisdom."

Vafthruthnir spake:
"Out of Ymir's flesh | was fashioned the earth,
And the mountains were made of his bones;
The sky from the frost-cold | giant's skull,
And the ocean out of his blood."

Othin spake:
"Tell me second, | if thy wisdom avails,
And thou knowest it, Vafthruthnir, well:
Whence came the moon | that men behold,
And whence the sun so fair?"

Vafthruthnir spake:
"Mundilferi is he | who begat the moon,
And fathered the flaming sun;
The heavens they travel | each day of the year,
To tell the time for men."

Othin spake:
"Tell me third, | if wise thou art called,
If thou knowest it, Vafthruthnir, well:
Whence came up Dag, | who brings the day,
Or Nott and her daughter moon?"

Vafthruthnir spake:
"The father of Dag | is Delling called,
And the night was born of Nor;
Full moon and old | the gods created
To tell the time for men."

Othin spake:
"Tell me now, | if thou knowest it well,
Vafthruthnir, forth shalt thou say:
Whence came winter | the cold or summer,
First to the mighty gods?"

Vafthruthnir spake:
"Vindsval he was | who begat the winter,
And Svosuth fathered the summer fair."
