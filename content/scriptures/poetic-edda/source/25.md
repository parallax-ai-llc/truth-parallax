---
title: "Guðrúnarhvöt (Gudrun's Inciting)"
section: 25
---

**1** Then did Gudrun gird herself for death, when she had spoken the songs of sorrow. Heavy were the words which she uttered:

**2** "I pray whoever hears these many woe-begotten words of sorrow, that for my grief they may be told, standing or sitting, as a shield-tale of sorrow."

**3** She summoned her sons, bold warriors, to speech before they went to battle; she told them to be ready with arms.

**4** "Arise now, Hamdir! Arise now, Sorli! Ye sons of the south, the seed of Jonakr! And ye bold helpers, companions of warriors!"

**5** "Go ye forth to find out Jormunrek; seek ye that kinsman and make your onset! For Svanhild your sister has Jormunrek trampled beneath his horses' hooves on the highway, with white and black steeds on the warriors' path."

**6** "You are not like Gunnar and his gallant men, you have not the courage of Hogni. You would seek to avenge her if you had the spirit of my brothers, the hard mood of the Hunnish kings."

**7** Then Hamdir spoke, the high-minded: "Little didst thou praise the deed of Hogni, when they wakened Sigurd from his sleep; thy white-blue bed-clothes were reddened with thy husband's blood, soaked through with gore."
