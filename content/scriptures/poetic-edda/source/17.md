---
title: "Þrymskviða (The Lay of Thrym)"
section: 17
---

Wild was Vingthor | when he awoke,
And when his mighty | hammer he missed;
He shook his beard, | his hair was bristling,
As the son of Jorth | about him sought.

And this the first | of his words he spake:
"Hear now, Loki, | what now I tell thee,
That no one knows | on the earth above,
And no one knows | in heaven as well:
The hammer of the gods is stolen!"

To the dwelling fair | of Freyja went they,
And this the first | of his words he spake:
"Wilt thou me, Freyja, | thy feather-coat lend,
That perchance my hammer I find?"

Freyja spake:
"Thine should it be | though of silver bright,
And I would give it | though 'twere of gold."

Then Loki flew, | the feather-coat rustled,
Till he left behind him | the home of the gods,
And he came at last | to the giants' land.

Thrym sat on a mound, | the giants' master,
Leashes of gold | he laid for his dogs,
And stroked and smoothed | the manes of his steeds.

Thrym spake:
"How fare the gods, | how fare the elves?
Why comest thou alone to the giants' land?"

Loki spake:
"Ill fare the gods, | ill fare the elves!
Hast thou hidden | Hlorrithi's hammer?"

Thrym spake:
"I have hidden | Hlorrithi's hammer
Eight miles deep | beneath the earth;
No man shall ever | win it back
Unless he brings me | Freyja as bride."
