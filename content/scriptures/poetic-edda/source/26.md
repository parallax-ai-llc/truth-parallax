---
title: "Hamðismál (The Lay of Hamdir)"
section: 26
---

**1** Deeds of daring were done of old, when the sons of Gudrun sought to avenge their wrongs, and the brave Niflungs rode forth.

**2** Hamdir spoke, the stout-hearted: "What would the deed of Hogni have profited, or the deeds of Gunnar, if they had avenged the wrong? Then would we also avenge Svanhild, who was our sister, dear to our hearts."

**3** Then answered Sorli with subtle mind: "I will not bandy words with my brother. Let us ride southward to the court of Jormunrek. There shall we try whether we can avenge the maiden."

**4** They rode through the wild woods, over mountains and moors, to the dwelling of the king.

**5** They found Jormunrek sitting in his hall, drinking mead. They drew their swords and smote off his hands and feet.

**6** Jormunrek cried: "Take stones and cast at these warriors, since swords and spears and the sharpest weapons cannot harm the sons of Jonakr."

**7** Then fell Hamdir at the hall's gable, and Sorli sank at the side of the house. There the Niflungs' race was ended, and the tale of Gudrun's kin was told.

**8** Thus end the lays of the heroes of old, and the deeds of daring that were done in the world's first age.
