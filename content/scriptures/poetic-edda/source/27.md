---
title: "Baldrs Draumar (Baldr's Dreams)"
section: 27
---

**1** Once were the Aesir all in council, and the Asynjur all in speech; and this the mighty gods discussed: why Baldr was plagued with baleful dreams.

**2** Up rose Odin, the enchanter old, and on Sleipnir he the saddle laid; thence rode he down to Niflhel, and met a dog that came from hell.

**3** Blood was on its breast, and long it barked at the father of magic songs. On rode Odin -- the earth resounded -- till he came to the high house of Hel.

**4** Then Odin rode to the eastern gate, where he knew the grave of the prophetess lay. He sang spells that woke the dead, till reluctantly she rose and spoke these words:

**5** "What man is this, unknown to me, who has made me travel the troublesome road? I was snowed on with snow, and smitten with rain, and drenched with dew; long was I dead."

**6** Odin said: "Traveller is my name, I am Warrior's son. Tell me of Hel: for whom are the benches decked with rings, the halls so fairly strewn with gold?"

**7** She answered: "Here stands mead brewed for Baldr, the shining drink, with a shield laid over it; but the sons of the gods are sunk in despair. Unwillingly I spoke; now I will be silent."

**8** "Be not silent, prophetess! I will question thee till I know all. Who shall the slayer of Baldr be, and steal the life of Odin's son?"

**9** "Hod shall hither his glorious brother send; he shall of Baldr's bane become. Unwillingly I spoke; now I will be silent."

**10** "Be not silent! Who shall on Hod take vengeance, or raise on the pyre the slayer of Baldr?"

**11** "Rind shall bear Vali in the western halls; one night old, Odin's son shall fight; his hands he shall not wash, nor his hair shall comb, till he bears to the pyre Baldr's foe. Unwillingly I spoke; now will I be silent."

**12** "Thou art not a prophetess, nor a wise woman, rather thou art the mother of three monsters!" She answered: "Ride home, Odin, and be proud! No more shall men come to seek me, till Loki is freed from his bonds, and the doom of the gods draws nigh."
