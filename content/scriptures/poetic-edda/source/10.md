---
title: "Hávamál (concluded) — The Charms of Odin"
section: 10
---

I know a first charm, | that is useful to all
The sons of men on earth:
Help it is named, | for help it can give
In sickness and care and sorrow.

I know a second, | that the sons of men
Must know who would be healers.

I know a third: | if my need be great
In battle to hold my foe,
I can dull the swords | of my deadly foes,
That nor wiles nor weapons avail them.

I know a fourth: | if men shall fasten
Bonds on my bended legs,
So great is the charm | that forth I may go,
The fetters spring from my feet,
Broken are the bonds from my hands.

I know a fifth: | no flying arrow,
Aimed to bring harm to men,
Flies too fast for my fingers to catch it
And hold it in mid-air.

I know a sixth: | if harm is done me
By a hero's roots of strength,
The man who would hurt me | gets the wound,
And I am whole and hale.

I know a seventh: | if I see on fire
The hall above my comrades' heads,
However fierce the flames may be,
I can save them all with a single charm.

I know an eighth: | which all can use
Most needfully, sons of men;
When hatred grows | among heroes' sons,
I can smooth it swiftly away.

Those songs I know, | which nor sons of men
Nor any of royal birth know;
Help one is called, | and help it can give
In sickness and care and sorrow.

Now the sayings of Har | are spoken in the hall,
Needful for sons of men, | harmful for sons of giants:
Hail to the speaker, | hail to the knower,
Joy to him who has learned,
Delight to those who listened.
