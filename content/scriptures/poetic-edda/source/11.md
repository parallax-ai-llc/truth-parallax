---
title: "Vafþrúðnismál (The Lay of Vafthrudnir)"
section: 11
---

Othin spake:
"Counsel me, Frigg, | for I long to fare,
And Vafthruthnir fain would find;
Wisdom old | with the giant wise
Myself would I seek to match."

Frigg spake:
"Heerfather here | at home would I keep,
Where the gods together dwell;
For among the giants | is none so great
In might as Vafthruthnir known."

Othin spake:
"Much have I fared, | much have I found,
Much have I got of the gods;
One thing I wish: | what within his hall
Is the manner of Vafthruthnir's might."

Frigg spake:
"Safe go forth, | safe return again,
Safe be thy way between!
Thy wits shall avail thee | when with the giant
In words thou seekest to win."

Then Othin fared forth | to find the giant,
And to try the wisdom | of Vafthruthnir;
To the hall he came | that Im's father had,
And in forthwith went Ygg.

Othin spake:
"Hail to thee, Vafthruthnir! | Into thy hall have I come
Thyself to behold;
And first would I ask | if wise thou art,
Or art truly the wisest of giants."

Vafthruthnir spake:
"What man is this | that speaks to me
Within my very hall?
Hence shalt thou never go | except the wiser thou art;
So sit thee here and let us try our wits!"
