---
title: "Alvíssmál (The Lay of Alviss — All-Wise)"
section: 20
---

Thor spake:
"What man art thou? | Why so pale about the nose?
Didst thou lie among corpses last night?
To a giant's likeness | thou seemest to me;
Thou wast not born for the bride."

Alviss spake:
"Alviss am I, | and under the earth
My dwelling is, | beneath a stone;
With the wagon-guide | a word have I,
Let none make breach | of the bond that was given."

Thor spake:
"I shall break it, | for over the bride
Greatest right have I, I ween;
At home I was not | when the pledge was given
That among the gods was granted."

Thor spake:
"Tell me, Alviss, | thou knowest all
The fates of men, I deem:
What call they the earth, | that lies before all,
In each and every world?"

Alviss spake:
"Earth to men, | to the gods 'tis Field,
The Wanes say the Ways;
Giants call it Ever-Green, | elves the Growing,
The high ones call it Clay."

Thor spake:
"Tell me, Alviss, | thou knowest all,
What call they the heaven, | beheld of the high one,
In each and every world?"

Alviss spake:
"Heaven to men, | the Height to the gods,
The Wanes call it Wind-Weaver;
Giants the Upper-World, | elves the Fair-Roof,
The dwarfs the Dripping Hall."

Thor spake:
"Tell me, Alviss, | what call they the moon,
That men behold, | in each of the worlds?"

Alviss spake:
"Moon with men, | Flame the gods among,
The Wheel in the house of Hel;
The Goer the giants, | the Gleamer the dwarfs,
The elves name it Time-Teller."
