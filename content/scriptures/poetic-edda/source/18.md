---
title: "Þrymskviða (concluded) — Thor's Disguise"
section: 18
---

Then Loki flew, | the feather-coat rustled,
Till he left behind him | the giants' land,
And he came at last | to the home of the gods.
Before the court | did he meet with Thor,
And this the first | of his words he spake:

"Hast thou tidings as well | as trouble to tell?
Speak forth from the air thy news:
Oft doth the sitter | his story forget,
And he lies who lays himself down."

Loki spake:
"I bring thee tidings | and trouble as well:
Thrym, the king of the giants, has thy hammer,
And no man shall ever | win it back
Unless he brings him | Freyja as bride."

Then Freyja they sought, | and this the first
Of his words he spake:
"Bind on, Freyja, | the bridal veil,
For we two must hasten to Jotunheim."

Wrathful was Freyja, | and fiercely she snorted,
And the dwelling shook | of the gods as she trembled,
And burst was the mighty | Brisings' necklace:
"Most lustful indeed | should I look to all
If I rode with thee | to the giants' land."

Then Heimdall spake, | the whitest of the gods,
Wise he was too, | as the Wanes all are:
"Bind we on Thor | the bridal veil,
Let him bear the mighty | Brisings' necklace.

"Keys let him bear | on his girdle's chain,
And woman's dress | fall down to his knees,
With gems full broad | upon his breast,
And a dainty cap | to crown his head."

Then Thor spake, | the mighty god:
"Me would the gods | unmanly call
If I let bind | the bridal veil."

Yet they bound on Thor | the bridal veil,
And next the necklace | of the Brisings bright.
