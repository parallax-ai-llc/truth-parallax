---
title: "Grímnismál (continued) — Valhalla and the World Tree"
section: 14
---

Grimnir spake:
"Five hundred doors | and forty more,
So I deem, are in Valhall;
Eight hundred warriors | walk through each door
When they go forth to fight the wolf.

"Five hundred halls | and forty more,
I ween, has Bilskirnir with its windings;
Of all the dwellings | I know on earth
My son's the greatest have I seen.

"Heithrun is the goat | who stands by Heerfather's hall,
And the branches of Laerath she bites;
The cruse she fills | with the clearest mead,
Ne'er shall that drink grow dry.

"Eikthyrnir is the hart | who stands by Heerfather's hall
And the branches of Laerath he bites;
From his horns a stream | into Hvergelmir drops,
And thence all waters arise.

"I know I hung | on a windy tree
Nine long nights,
Wounded with a spear, | dedicated to Odin,
Myself to myself,
On that tree | of which no man knows
From where its roots run.

"Ratatosk is the squirrel | who there shall run
On the ash-tree Yggdrasil;
From above the words | of the eagle he bears,
And tells them to Nithhogg beneath.

"Four harts there are, | that the highest twigs
Nibble with necks bent back;
Dain and Dvalin, | Duneyr and Dyrathror.

"More serpents lie | beneath the ash
Than an unwise fool would think;
Goin and Moin, | Grafvitnir's sons,
Grabak and Grafvolluth,
Ofnir and Svafnir | shall ever, methinks,
Gnaw at the twigs of the tree."
