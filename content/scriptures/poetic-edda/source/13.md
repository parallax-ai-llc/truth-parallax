---
title: "Grímnismál (The Lay of Grimnir) — The Worlds and Halls"
section: 13
---

Grimnir spake:
"Rathsvith made | the sun so fair
From the flaming world of the south;
From its shining mane | shall Skinfaxi draw
The light that illumines the land.

"Tis sacred, the land | that lies I see
Near the gods and the elves together;
And in Thruthheim | shall Thor abide
Till the gods to destruction go.

"Ydalir call they | the place where Ull
A hall for himself hath set;
And Alfheim the gods | to Frey once gave
As a tooth-gift in ancient times.

"A third home is there | where the kindly gods
Have decked the hall with silver;
Valaskjalf 'tis named, | for himself the god
Built it in days long gone.

"Sökkvabekk is the fourth, | o'er which cool waves flow,
And amid their murmur it stands;
There daily do Othin | and Saga drink
In gladness from cups of gold.

"Glathsheim is the fifth, | where gold-bright stands
Valhall, broad and tall;
There Hropt selects | each day the men
Who have fallen in fight.

"Easy is it to know | for him who to Othin
Comes and beholds the hall;
Its rafters are spears, | with shields is it roofed,
On its benches are breastplates strewn.

"Easy is it to know | for him who to Othin
Comes and beholds the hall;
A wolf hangs before | the western door,
And o'er it an eagle hovers."
