---
title: "Rígsþula (continued) — Karl and Jarl"
section: 23
---

Further went Rig, | the road he followed,
Came to a hall, | the door stood open;
In went he, a fire | was burning within;
A couple sat there | the hearth beside.

The man was hewing | a beam for a loom;
His beard was trimmed, | o'er his brow a curl,
His clothes fit close; | in the corner a chest.

The woman sat | and the distaff wielded,
Her arms were spread, | for the cloth she wove;
On her head was a band, | on her breast a buckle,
A kerchief fair | was about her neck.

Amma bore a son, | with water they sprinkled him,
Karl they named him; | the woman swathed him,
Ruddy and fresh, | with flashing eyes.

He began to grow | and well he throve,
Oxen he broke, | and plows he fashioned,
Houses he built, | and barns he framed,
Carts he made, | and the plow he managed.

Further went Rig, | the road he followed;
A hall he saw, | south stood its door;
The entrance was closed | with a ring that was hanging.

He went within; | the floor was strewn,
A couple sat there | gazing around,
Fathir and Mothir, | with fingers busy.

There sat the house-lord, | wound strings for the bow,
Bent the elm-bow, | and bolts he fashioned;
And there the lady | in fine linen dressed,
Spread her fingers fair, | and smoothed the cloth.

Her brow was bright, | her breast was shining,
Her neck was white | as new-fallen snow.

Mothir bore a son, | in silk she wrapped him,
With water they sprinkled him, | Jarl they called him;
Blond was his hair, | and bright his cheeks,
Grim as a snake's | were his glowing eyes.
