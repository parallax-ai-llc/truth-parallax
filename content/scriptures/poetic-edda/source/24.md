---
title: "Atlamál in Grænlenzku (The Greenlandic Lay of Atli)"
section: 24
---

**1** Men have heard of the strife of old, when warriors plotted; they sent to Gunnar a knowing messenger, a cunning envoy.

**2** Knefrud was his name, he came to the halls of Gjuki, to Gunnar's hearth and his splendid benches, and the sweet drink flowing.

**3** The warriors drank in silence at the mighty feast, fearing the Huns. Then Knefrud called out, the southern man, seated on the high bench:

**4** "Atli has sent me hither riding on an errand, on a steed bit-champing through Myrkwood the unknown, to bid you both, Gunnar, to come to his benches with helms ring-adorned, to visit Atli.

**5** "Shields you may choose there, and shafts of ash, gold-wound helmets and a host of Huns, silver-gilt saddle-cloths, shirts blood-red, darts and lances, and steeds bit-champing."

**6** Gunnar turned his head and said to Hogni: "What counsel, young kinsman, when such things we hear? Gold I knew not on Gnita-heath that we ourselves had not the like."

**7** "Suspicion I have," said Hogni, "of what she means by this: a wolf's hair was wound in the ring she sent us. Wolfish the way to ride if we fare on this journey."
