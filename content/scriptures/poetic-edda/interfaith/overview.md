## Overview

The Poetic Edda offers valuable perspectives for interfaith dialogue as a primary source for a pre-Christian European religious tradition. Its cosmology — featuring a world tree connecting multiple realms, divine beings in complex relationships with natural forces, and a cyclical vision of cosmic destruction and renewal — provides points of contact with indigenous and polytheistic traditions worldwide. The poems' treatment of sacrifice, wisdom-seeking, the afterlife, and moral conduct invites comparison with the ethical and spiritual teachings of other religious traditions. Interfaith engagement with the Edda also raises important questions about the reconstruction and revival of pre-Christian traditions in the modern world.
