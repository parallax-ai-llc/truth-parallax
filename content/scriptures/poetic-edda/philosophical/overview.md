## Overview

The Poetic Edda engages profound philosophical questions through mythological narrative and wisdom poetry. Völuspá raises questions about determinism and the nature of time — whether the future is fixed or open, and what it means for even the gods to be subject to fate. Hávamál articulates a practical ethics of moderation, reciprocity, and social wisdom that resonates with Aristotelian virtue ethics and Stoic philosophy. The poems collectively explore the relationship between knowledge and power, the problem of mortality, and the meaning of existence in a cosmos destined for destruction.
