## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Poetic Edda connect the literary texts to material evidence from the Viking Age and earlier periods. Runestones, picture stones (such as those from Gotland), metalwork, and other artifacts provide visual and material parallels to scenes described in the poems. Archaeological findings from cult sites, burial practices, and settlement patterns help contextualize the religious and social world reflected in the Edda. The relationship between textual and material evidence is complex, as the poems were written down centuries after the Viking Age, but archaeological discoveries continue to illuminate the traditions behind the texts.
