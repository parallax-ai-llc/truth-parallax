---
title: "Sutta Nipata - Chapter 4"
scripture: "sutta-nipata"
chapter: 4
---

**1** "Whoever abides in loving-kindness, having gained insight through the teaching of the Buddhas, this one could reach that state of peace, the blissful cessation of conditioned existence.

**2** "One who is skilled in doing good, who wishes to attain that state of calm, should act thus: he should be able, upright, perfectly upright, of good speech, gentle, and humble.

**3** "Contented, easily supported, with few duties, of light livelihood, with senses calmed, prudent, not impudent, not greedily attached to families.

**4** "He should not do any slight wrong on account of which other wise men might censure him. May all beings be happy and secure; may their hearts be wholesome.

**5** "Whatever living beings there be — feeble or strong, tall, stout, or medium, short, small, or large, without exception — seen or unseen, those dwelling far or near, those who are born or those who are to be born, may all beings be happy.

**6** "Let none deceive another, nor despise any person whatsoever in any place. Let him not wish any harm to another out of anger or ill-will.

**7** "Just as a mother would protect her only child at the risk of her own life, even so let one cultivate a boundless heart towards all beings.

**8** "Let thoughts of infinite love pervade the whole world — above, below, and across — without any obstruction, without any hatred, without any enmity.

**9** "Whether one stands, walks, sits, or lies down, as long as one is awake, one should develop this mindfulness. This, they say, is the noblest living here.

**10** "Not falling into wrong views, being virtuous and endowed with insight, by discarding attachment to sense desires, one never again comes to lie in a womb.

**11** He who has risen above gain and loss, above praise and blame, is indeed a sage established in peace.

**12** As a solid rock is not shaken by the wind, even so the wise are not moved by praise or blame.

**13** As a deep lake, calm and clear, even so on hearing the teachings the wise become serene.

**14** The good renounce everything. The saintly do not prattle with a yearning for pleasures. The wise show no elation or depression when affected by happiness or sorrow.

**15** He who is without attachment, who has understood things as they truly are, who has seen the state of sorrowless freedom from birth — such a great being, I declare, is the highest of men.

**16** The sage who, remaining in seclusion, delights in right contemplation, should deter himself from those things that cause suffering, for in this world there is nothing that does not cause suffering.

**17** Calmness of body, calmness of speech, calmness of mind, composure, the shedding of bait of the world — such a monk is called one who is at peace.

**18** Arouse yourself! Sit up! What is the use of sleeping? For the sick, pierced by an arrow and suffering, what sleep is there?

**19** Arouse yourself! Sit up! Train yourself firmly to attain peace. Let not the king of death, perceiving you are careless, deceive you and lead you into his domain.

**20** Cross over this attachment to which gods and men cling and which they do not go beyond. Let not the moment pass, for those who miss the moment grieve when consigned to hell.
