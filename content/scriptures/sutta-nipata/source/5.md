---
title: "Sutta Nipata - Chapter 5"
scripture: "sutta-nipata"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion, the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in the Jeta Grove, in Anathapindika's park. Then the Blessed One addressed the monks thus:

**2** "Monks, there are these four kinds of persons found existing in the world. What four? The one who practises neither for his own benefit nor for the benefit of others. The one who practises for the benefit of others but not for his own benefit. The one who practises for his own benefit but not for the benefit of others. And the one who practises for his own benefit and for the benefit of others.

**3** "Of these four persons, monks, the one who practises for his own benefit and for the benefit of others is the chief, the best, the foremost, the highest, and the most excellent."

**4** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the monks were glad and approved of his words.

**5** "Monks, be islands unto yourselves, be a refuge to yourselves, seek no external refuge. Hold fast to the Dhamma as an island, hold fast to the Dhamma as a refuge, seek no other refuge.

**6** "And how does a monk become an island unto himself? Here, a monk abides contemplating the body as body, ardent, clearly aware, and mindful, having put away covetousness and grief for the world.

**7** "He abides contemplating feelings as feelings, contemplating mind as mind, contemplating mind-objects as mind-objects, ardent, clearly aware, and mindful, having put away covetousness and grief for the world.

**8** "This is how a monk is an island unto himself, a refuge to himself, seeking no external refuge, with the Dhamma as his island, the Dhamma as his refuge, seeking no other refuge.

**9** "Monks, all conditioned things are impermanent. All conditioned things are suffering. All phenomena are not-self. When one sees this with wisdom, one turns away from suffering. This is the path to purification.

**10** "As a mother, even at the risk of her own life, protects her son, her only son, so should one cultivate boundless loving-kindness toward all beings.

**11** "Let one's thoughts of boundless loving-kindness pervade the whole world: above, below, and across, without any obstruction, free from hatred and ill-will.

**12** "Whether standing, walking, seated, or lying down, as long as one is free from drowsiness, one should practise this mindfulness. This is called the sublime abiding.

**13** "Monks, there is an unborn, unoriginated, uncreated, unformed. Were there not this unborn, unoriginated, uncreated, unformed, there would be no escape from the world of the born, originated, created, formed.

**14** "Since there is an unborn, unoriginated, uncreated, unformed, therefore is there an escape from the born, originated, created, formed.

**15** "The wise ones, ever meditative, strenuously exerting themselves, attain Nibbana, the highest freedom and happiness.

**16** "The energetic, mindful, pure in deed, considerate, self-controlled, who live according to the Dhamma — their glory increases.

**17** "Through endeavour, vigilance, discipline, and self-control, let the wise man make for himself an island which no flood can overwhelm.

**18** "Fools, men of little intelligence, are the greatest enemies of themselves, for they do evil deeds which bear bitter fruits.

**19** "That deed is not well done which, having been done, one repents, and the fruit of which one reaps crying with a tearful face.

**20** "That deed is well done which, having been done, one does not repent, and the fruit of which one reaps with delight and happiness."
