---
title: "Sutta Nipata - Chapter 3"
scripture: "sutta-nipata"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in the Jeta Grove. Then the Brahmin Aggikabharadvaja saw the Blessed One begging for alms from house to house in Savatthi.

**2** The Brahmin spoke thus: "Stay there, you shaveling! Stay there, you wretched monk! Stay there, you outcast!"

**3** The Blessed One said: "Do you know, Brahmin, who an outcast is, and what the conditions are that make an outcast?"

**4** "No indeed, Master Gotama. It would be good if Master Gotama would teach me the Dhamma, so that I might know who an outcast is."

**5** "Listen then, Brahmin, and attend carefully, I will speak." "Yes, sir," the Brahmin replied. The Blessed One spoke:

**6** "The man who is angry and bears hatred, who is wicked and hypocritical, who has embraced wrong views and is deceitful — know him as an outcast.

**7** "Whoever in this world harms living beings, whether once-born or twice-born, whoever has no sympathy for living beings — know him as an outcast.

**8** "Whoever destroys and besieges villages and towns, and is known as an oppressor — know him as an outcast.

**9** "Whether in village or forest, whoever steals what belongs to others, what is not given — know him as an outcast.

**10** "Whoever, having really taken on a debt, when asked to pay denies it, saying, 'I owe you nothing' — know him as an outcast.

**11** "Whoever, desiring some trifling thing, kills a person going along the road and seizes that trifling thing — know him as an outcast.

**12** "The man who, for his own sake, or for the sake of others, or for the sake of wealth, speaks lies when asked as a witness — know him as an outcast.

**13** "Whoever is seen consorting with the wives of relatives or of friends, either by force or with their consent — know him as an outcast.

**14** "Whoever, being rich, does not support his aged mother and father who have passed their youth — know him as an outcast.

**15** "Whoever strikes or annoys by speech his mother, father, brother, sister, or mother-in-law — know him as an outcast.

**16** "Whoever, when asked about what is good, teaches what is evil and advises in a concealing way — know him as an outcast.

**17** "Whoever, having done an evil deed, wishes that it may not be known to others and who acts in concealment — know him as an outcast.

**18** "Whoever, having gone to another's house and having been entertained with good food, does not honour that host by inviting him in return — know him as an outcast.

**19** "Whoever deceives by lies a Brahmin, an ascetic, or any other mendicant — know him as an outcast.

**20** "Not by birth is one an outcast; not by birth is one a Brahmin. By deed one becomes an outcast, by deed one becomes a Brahmin."
