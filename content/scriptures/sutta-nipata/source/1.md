---
title: "Sutta Nipata - Chapter 1"
scripture: "sutta-nipata"
chapter: 1
---

**1** As a snake sheds its worn-out skin, so should one shed one's past.

**2** As a snake sheds its old skin, let the monk shed lust, hatred, and delusion — let him shed them entirely.

**3** He who has cut off lust entirely, as one plucks an autumn lily from a pond, cultivates the path to peace — Nibbana, as taught by the Blessed One.

**4** He who has cut off lust entirely, like a flood that carries away a sleeping village, goes to Nibbana, leaving behind the cycle of rebirth.

**5** As a flood carries away a sleeping village, so death takes away a man of distracted mind, even before his desires are satisfied.

**6** Just as a bee collects nectar and leaves without harming the flower, its colour, or its scent, so too should a sage move through a village.

**7** Not the faults of others, not what others have done or left undone, but one's own deeds done and undone should one consider.

**8** Like a beautiful flower, full of colour but without fragrance, are the fine but fruitless words of one who does not practise them.

**9** Like a beautiful flower, full of colour and also of fragrance, are the fine and fruitful words of one who practises them.

**10** Just as one would make many a garland from a heap of flowers, so a mortal, having come into being, should do many good deeds.

**11** The scent of flowers does not blow against the wind, nor does the fragrance of sandalwood, tagara, or jasmine. But the fragrance of the virtuous blows against the wind. A virtuous person pervades all directions.

**12** Sandalwood, tagara, lotus, and jasmine — of these the fragrance of virtue is by far the best.

**13** Mean is the fragrance of tagara or sandalwood. But the fragrance of the virtuous rises up to the gods as the highest.

**14** Mara never finds the path of those who are virtuous, careful in living, and freed by right knowledge.

**15** Just as upon a heap of rubbish thrown on the highway, a sweet-smelling, lovely lotus may grow, so among the rubbish of beings, a disciple of the Fully Enlightened One outshines the blind worldlings in wisdom.

**16** This body is like a city made of bones, plastered with flesh and blood, where old age and death, pride and deceit are deposited.

**17** Even the gorgeous chariots of kings wear out; the body too falls into decay. But the Dhamma of the good does not decay. Thus the good teach one another.

**18** The man of little learning grows old like an ox — his flesh increases, but his wisdom does not.

**19** Through many births I have wandered in samsara, seeking but not finding the builder of the house. Sorrowful is repeated birth.

**20** O house-builder, you have been seen! You shall build no house again. All your rafters are broken, your ridgepole is destroyed. My mind has attained the unconditioned. Achieved is the end of craving.
