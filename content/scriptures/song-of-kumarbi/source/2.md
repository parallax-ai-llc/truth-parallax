---
title: "Song of Kumarbi - Chapter 2"
scripture: "song-of-kumarbi"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Kumarbi bit the loins of Anu. And Anu's manhood united with Kumarbi's insides like bronze.

**2** When Kumarbi had swallowed the manhood of Anu, he rejoiced and laughed. Anu turned to him and spoke:

**3** "Thou rejoicest over thine insides, because thou hast swallowed my manhood. Rejoice not over thine insides!

**4** "In thine insides I have planted a heavy burden. First, I have impregnated thee with the noble Storm God.

**5** "Second, I have impregnated thee with the irresistible River Aranzah. Third, I have impregnated thee with the noble Tasmisu.

**6** "Three terrible gods have I planted as seed in thy belly. Thou shalt end by striking the rocks of thine own mountain with thy head!"

**7** When Anu had finished speaking, he went up to heaven and hid himself. But Kumarbi, the wise king, spat upon the earth.

**8** From his mouth he spat out that which was in his insides. That which Kumarbi spat out fell upon Mount Kanzura.

**9** And the months passed: one month, two months, three months, four months passed. The hero Kumarbi grew heavy with the children in his belly.

**10** When the appointed time came, the gods within him sought to be born. They sought a way out of the body of Kumarbi.
