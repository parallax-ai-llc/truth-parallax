---
title: "Song of Kumarbi - Chapter 3"
scripture: "song-of-kumarbi"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Storm God, the noble god, spoke from within Kumarbi: "Where shall I come forth? Through what part of the body?"

**2** He considered the ear, but it was not fitting. He considered the right side, but it was not fitting.

**3** Then he came forth from the good place, and the Storm God was born.

**4** Kumarbi, when he saw the Storm God, was seized with rage. He cried out: "What shall I do with this child? Shall I swallow him back, or shall I cast him down?"

**5** But Anu, from heaven, heard the cry of Kumarbi and laughed. He said: "The Storm God is born, and he shall rule over heaven and earth."

**6** The Storm God grew quickly, like a mighty cedar. His strength was the strength of a hundred bulls, and his voice was the thunder.

**7** He mounted his chariot drawn by the sacred bulls Sheri and Hurri, and he rode forth to do battle with the enemies of the gods.

**8** The mountains trembled at his passing, and the rivers stood still. The clouds gathered at his command, and the rain fell upon the earth.

**9** And Kumarbi saw the power of the Storm God and was afraid. For the child he had carried in his belly had become mightier than he.

**10** The old order was passing. A new generation of gods was rising to power. And the Storm God would rule in heaven.
