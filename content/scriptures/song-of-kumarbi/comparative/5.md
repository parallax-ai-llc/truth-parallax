Teshub's establishment as divine king parallels the conclusions of other succession narratives:
- **Greek**: Zeus defeats the Titans and establishes Olympian rule
- **Babylonian**: Marduk defeats Tiamat and is proclaimed king of the gods
- **Ugaritic**: Baal defeats Yamm and Mot to establish his sovereignty
- **Norse**: Odin and his brothers defeat Ymir and establish the current world order

In each tradition, the storm god or his equivalent takes power as the final ruler in a series of successions, establishing the divine order under which humanity currently lives. The universality of this pattern suggests a deep structural affinity across Indo-European and Semitic mythological traditions.
