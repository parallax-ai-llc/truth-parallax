Kumarbi's male pregnancy connects to a broader mythological pattern of extraordinary births:
- Zeus gives birth to Athena from his head after swallowing Metis
- Dionysus is born from Zeus's thigh
- In Egyptian myth, Atum creates Shu and Tefnut from his own body
- In Norse myth, Loki gives birth to Sleipnir after transforming into a mare

These traditions share the concept that divine reproduction operates outside the norms of biological generation — gods can conceive and give birth in ways that transcend gender. The Hurro-Hittite tradition provides the most explicit example of male divine pregnancy.
