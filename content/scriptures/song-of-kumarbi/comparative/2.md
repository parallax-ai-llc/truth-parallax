The castration and swallowing scene provides the strongest evidence for literary transmission between the ancient Near East and Greece. The parallels with Hesiod are remarkably specific:

- Kumarbi bites off Anu's genitals / Kronos cuts off Ouranos's genitals with a sickle
- Kumarbi becomes pregnant with gods / Kronos swallows his children (inverse but structurally parallel)
- The storm god (Teshub) is born from this act / The storm god (Zeus) emerges as king

Walter Burkert and M.L. West have argued for direct transmission, likely through Phoenician or Cilician intermediaries during the 8th-7th centuries BCE. The specificity of the parallels makes independent development unlikely.
