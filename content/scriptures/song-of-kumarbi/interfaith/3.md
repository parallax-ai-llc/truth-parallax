The image of a god pregnant with gods speaks to the interfaith concept of divine creativity taking unexpected forms. The divine creative act, across traditions, often involves a paradoxical self-transformation:
- **Christianity**: God becomes incarnate in a human womb (the Incarnation)
- **Hinduism**: Vishnu takes on multiple avatars, each born into the world
- **Kabbalah**: The divine *sefirot* emerge from the hidden *Ein Sof* through a process of divine self-limitation
- **Taoism**: The Tao produces all things by 'giving birth' without possessing

Kumarbi's pregnancy, while vastly different in its specifics, participates in this universal intuition that the divine creates through self-transformation.
