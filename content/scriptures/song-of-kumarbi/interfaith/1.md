The idea that the current divine order was preceded by earlier orders — that the gods themselves have a history of succession — raises provocative interfaith questions. Monotheistic traditions affirm that God is eternal and without predecessor. Hindu tradition includes cycles of creation and dissolution but maintains the eternity of Brahman. Buddhist cosmology describes successive world-systems without a first cause. The Song of Kumarbi's opening tablet contributes a strikingly different perspective: divine order is not given but achieved, through conflict and overthrow.
