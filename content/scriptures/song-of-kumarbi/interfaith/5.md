The establishment of divine kingship — the arrival of a final, legitimate ruler — resonates with messianic and eschatological hopes across traditions:
- **Judaism**: The coming of the Messiah to establish God's kingdom
- **Christianity**: Christ's return and the establishment of the Kingdom of God
- **Islam**: The coming of the Mahdi and the final establishment of justice
- **Buddhism**: The coming of Maitreya, the future Buddha
- **Zoroastrianism**: The Saoshyant and the final renovation (*Frashokereti*)

The Song of Kumarbi presents the mythological prototype of this hope: after ages of conflict and succession, a just ruler finally takes the throne. The theological question — is this order truly final? — is one that every tradition must answer in its own way.
