The first tablet introduces the primordial divine order: Alalu sits as king in heaven, served by Anu. After nine years, Anu rebels and overthrows Alalu, who flees to the 'dark earth.' Guterbock's analysis identified this section as establishing the pattern of succession that structures the entire myth. The figure of Alalu is not well attested outside this text, though some scholars have connected him to the Hurrian deity Alalus known from theophoric names. The nine-year reign suggests a symbolic rather than literal time frame, possibly connected to cultic cycles.
