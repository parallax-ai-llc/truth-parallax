The fifth tablet, the most fragmentary of the composition, appears to describe Teshub's assumption of divine kingship and the establishment of a new cosmic order. The text is too damaged for a continuous narrative reconstruction, but the surviving fragments indicate that Teshub's rule is presented as legitimate and stable — in contrast to the contested reigns of his predecessors. The succession narrative thus ends with the establishment of the current divine order.

Scholarship has debated whether the Song of Kumarbi originally formed a single composition or was the first in a series of related texts (the Kumarbi Cycle), with subsequent conflicts (Song of Ullikummi, Song of Hedammu) testing but ultimately confirming Teshub's sovereignty.
