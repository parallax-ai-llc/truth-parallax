The third tablet describes Kumarbi's pregnancy with the gods born from Anu's swallowed seed. The text describes Kumarbi's distress as the gods grow within him and his attempts to deal with his condition. Anu, from his place in the sky, warns Kumarbi about the burden he carries. Scholarly reconstruction of this section is complicated by significant damage to the tablet; the precise number and identity of the gestating gods remain debated, though Teshub (the storm god) is clearly among them.

Beckman and Hoffner have analyzed the medical and obstetric language used to describe Kumarbi's pregnancy, noting parallels with Mesopotamian medical texts that describe pregnancy complications.
