The fourth tablet narrates the birth of Teshub and the other gods from Kumarbi's body. The precise mechanism of birth is debated due to textual damage — some reconstructions suggest birth through the skull (paralleling Zeus's birth of Athena), while others propose a more conventional emergence. The birth of Teshub is the narrative's pivotal event, establishing the figure who will ultimately take kingship in heaven.

Scholarship has also identified references to the birth of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers from Kumarbi's body, connecting the theogonic narrative to the geographic and cosmological framework of Mesopotamian tradition.
