The third tablet is among the most damaged of the Song of Kumarbi fragments. Reconstruction depends on parallel passages within the broader Kumarbi Cycle and on comparative evidence from Hurrian texts found at other sites. The Hurrian language texts from Hattusa — including bilingual Hurrian-Hittite texts — have been essential for understanding the theological vocabulary employed in this section.

The concept of divine pregnancy may be reflected in cultic practice: Hurrian and Hittite ritual texts describe ceremonies associated with divine birth, including birth incantations and rituals for the safe delivery of divine and royal children.
