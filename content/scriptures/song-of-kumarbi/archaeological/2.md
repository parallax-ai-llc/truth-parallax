The tablet preserving the castration scene is one of the better-preserved fragments of the Song of Kumarbi, though significant portions remain missing. The text was found in the temple archive area of Hattusa, alongside other mythological, ritual, and administrative texts. The Hurrian origin of the myth is confirmed by the divine names and by parallels with other Hurrian mythological texts found at Hattusa and at Ortakoy (ancient Sapinuwa).

The rock sanctuary of Yazilikaya, near Hattusa, preserves a relief procession of Hurrian deities that includes figures from the Kumarbi cycle, providing visual evidence for the myth's cultic significance.
