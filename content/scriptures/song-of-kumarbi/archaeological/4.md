The birth of Teshub (Hittite Tarhunt, Hurrian Tessob) is the central event of Hurro-Hittite theology. Archaeological evidence for Teshub's worship is extensive: he is depicted on the rock reliefs at Yazilikaya (near Hattusa) as the leading male deity, standing on the backs of mountain gods and accompanied by bulls. Teshub's cult is attested at Aleppo, where the great temple of the storm god has been excavated, and at numerous other sites across Anatolia and northern Syria.

The bull iconography associated with Teshub connects him to the broader ancient Near Eastern tradition of the storm god as bull (compare Baal's epithet 'Bull' and the biblical polemics against golden calf worship).
