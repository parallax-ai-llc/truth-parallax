The first tablet was found among the cuneiform archives at Hattusa (Bogazkoy), in fragmentary condition. The text is written in Hittite cuneiform but preserves Hurrian divine names and theological concepts, reflecting the multilingual, multicultural character of the Hittite scribal establishment. The figure of Alalu may be connected to cultic practices at Hurrian religious centers such as Urkesh (Tell Mozan), where Kumarbi worship is attested in temple inscriptions.
