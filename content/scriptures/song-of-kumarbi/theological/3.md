Kumarbi's pregnancy represents one of ancient mythology's most profound theological paradoxes: the god who seized power through violence now carries within him the god who will overthrow him. The storm god Teshub gestates within the body of his father/antagonist, making Kumarbi simultaneously Teshub's origin and his rival.

This theology of paradoxical containment — the new order growing within the old — resonates with theological concepts of transformation: the seed that must die to produce fruit (John 12:24), the womb of darkness from which light emerges, the concept that every established order contains the seeds of its own transformation.
