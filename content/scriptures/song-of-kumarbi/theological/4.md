The birth of Teshub represents the theological concept of new order emerging from old conflict. Teshub is born from the body of his adversary, carrying within him the power of Anu (swallowed by Kumarbi) and the form given by Kumarbi's body. The new king of the gods is thus a synthesis of previous orders — not a complete rupture but a transformation.

This theology of emergent sovereignty — the new arising from within the old — provides an alternative to both the eternalism of monotheistic traditions (God always was) and the nihilism of purely cyclical views (nothing ever really changes). In the Song of Kumarbi, change is real, but it carries the past within it.
