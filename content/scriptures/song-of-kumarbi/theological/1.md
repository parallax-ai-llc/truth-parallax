The first tablet presents a startling theological premise: there was a time before the current divine order. Alalu's reign — and his overthrow — establishes that divine kingship is not eternal but historical. The gods themselves have a political history marked by conflict, ambition, and regime change. This theology of divine temporality stands in sharp contrast to monotheistic traditions that insist on God's eternal and unchanging sovereignty.

The flight of Alalu to the 'dark earth' after his overthrow introduces the theme of the defeated god who does not die but is banished — a motif that resonates with the fallen angels tradition and the Greek Titans imprisoned in Tartarus.
