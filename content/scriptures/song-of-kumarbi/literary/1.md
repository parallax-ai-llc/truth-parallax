The first tablet establishes the narrative's characteristic economy: events are narrated in compressed, formulaic language. 'Formerly, in the olden days, Alalu was king in heaven. Alalu was seated on the throne, and mighty Anu, foremost of the gods, was standing before him.' The opening creates a scene of ordered hierarchy that the narrative will immediately disrupt. The literary technique of establishing order only to shatter it is fundamental to the poem's structure.
