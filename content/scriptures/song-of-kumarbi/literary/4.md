The fourth tablet brings the pregnancy narrative to its climax. The birth scene — however it was described in the damaged text — represents the moment of resolution toward which the poem has been building. The literary structure of the whole narrative is revealed as a gestation plot: the central action (the castration and swallowing) initiated a process that unfolds through the remaining tablets until the birth of the new divine order.

The birth of gods from a male body inverts the biological order in a way that emphasizes the mythological text's freedom from naturalistic constraints. In mythological narrative, anything is possible — and this freedom is part of the genre's literary power.
