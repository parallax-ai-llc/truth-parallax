---
title: "Letter of Peter to Philip - Chapter 2"
scripture: "letter-peter-to-philip"
chapter: 2
---

**1** "Concerning the pleroma: I am the one who was sent down in the body because of the seed which had fallen away. And I came down into their mortal mold. But they did not recognize me; they were thinking of me that I was a mortal man."

**2** "And I spoke with him who belongs to me, and he listened to me, just as you too who listened to me today. And I gave him authority in order that he might enter into the inheritance of his fatherhood."

**3** "And I took them, namely those who had received form from me. I gave them form from the seeds of my fullness. And I filled them with my spirit."

**4** "Concerning the matter of how you are detained: it is because you are mine. When you strip off from yourselves what is corrupted, then you will become illuminators in the midst of mortal men."

**5** "Concerning the matter of why the powers fight against you: it is because they do not want you to become established, for they fight against the inner man. And you must fight against them in this way: come together and teach in the world the salvation with a promise."

**6** "And you, gird yourselves with the power of our Father, and let your prayer be known. And he, the Father, will help you, as he has helped you by sending me."

**7** "Be not afraid. I am with you forever, as I previously said to you when I was in the body." Then there came lightning and thunder from heaven, and what appeared to them in that place was carried up to heaven.

**8** Then the apostles gave thanks to the Lord with every blessing. And they returned to Jerusalem. And while coming up they spoke with each other on the road concerning the light which had come.

**9** And a remark was made concerning the Lord. It was said: "If he, our Lord, suffered, then how much must we suffer?"

**10** Peter answered and said: "He suffered for our sake, and it is necessary for us also to suffer for the sake of our smallness."

**11** Then a voice came to them, saying: "I have told you many times: it is necessary for you to suffer. It is necessary that you be brought to the church through the seven stewards of this age."

**12** "And whoever does not suffer, it is impossible for him to find the Father. As also James the just suffered and saw the Lord and knew him."

**13** Then the apostles rejoiced greatly, and they came up to Jerusalem. And they went up to the temple and gave instruction in salvation in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ. And they healed a multitude.

**14** And Peter opened his mouth, he said to his fellow disciples: "Did our Lord Jesus, when he was in the body, show us everything? For he came down. My brothers, listen to my voice." And he was filled with a holy spirit. He spoke thus:

**15** "Our illuminator, Jesus, came down and was crucified. And he bore a crown of thorns. And he put on a purple garment. And he was crucified on a tree and he was buried in a tomb. And he rose from the dead."

**16** "My brothers, Jesus is a stranger to this suffering. But we are the ones who have suffered through the transgression of the mother. And because of this, he did everything like us. For the Lord Jesus, the Son of the immeasurable glory of the Father, he is the author of our life."
