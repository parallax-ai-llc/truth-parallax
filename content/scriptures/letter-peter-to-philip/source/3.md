---
title: "Letter of Peter to Philip - Chapter 3"
scripture: "letter-peter-to-philip"
chapter: 3
---

**1** "My brothers, let us therefore not obey these lawless ones, and walk in that which is without grief, without care, and without fear."

**2** "Let us not fear death. For we know that the rulers of this world will be judged. And they will be ashamed, and they will be troubled."

**3** "Now I exhort you, my brothers: remember what the Lord told us. He said: 'If you do not make yourselves like the child, you will not enter the kingdom of heaven.' The child is without malice and without suspicion."

**4** While Peter was saying these things, the Lord appeared to them again. He said to them: "Peace be to you all and to everyone who believes in my name."

**5** "And when you depart, joy be to you, and grace, and power. And be not afraid. Behold, I am with you forever."

**6** Then the apostles parted from each other into four words in order to preach. And they went in the power of Jesus, in peace.

**7** Peter went to the regions of the west and preached boldly. And Philip went to the cities of the east and proclaimed the gospel of the Lord.

**8** And each of the apostles went forth to the places that the Lord had appointed for them. And they healed the sick and raised the dead and cast out demons.

**9** And they preached the good news of the Lord Jesus Christ, with signs and wonders following them. And many believed and were baptized.

**10** And the churches were established, and the believers grew in number day by day. And the word of the Lord spread throughout all the regions.

**11** And the apostles remembered the words of the Lord, how he had said: "You will be persecuted for my name's sake. But be of good courage, for I have overcome the world."

**12** And they endured persecution and suffering, but they did not cease from preaching. For the love of Christ constrained them, and the power of the Holy Spirit sustained them.

**13** And Peter, remembering the instruction of the Lord, taught the brethren, saying: "We must strip off the old man with his deeds, and put on the new man, who is renewed in knowledge after the image of him who created him."

**14** "For the Lord said: 'He who loses his life for my sake shall find it. But he who saves his life shall lose it.' Let us therefore not cling to the things of this world, but set our minds on things above."

**15** "For our citizenship is in heaven, from whence also we look for the Savior, the Lord Jesus Christ, who shall change our vile body, that it may be fashioned like unto his glorious body."

**16** And these are the words and the preachings and the ministry which the apostles received from the Lord. And they went forth in peace, preaching and teaching, in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit. Amen.
