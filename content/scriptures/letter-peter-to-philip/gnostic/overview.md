## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Letter of Peter to Philip integrates two concerns central to Gnostic Christianity: the authority of apostolic revelation and the meaning of suffering in a world ruled by archons. Peter's letter calling Philip back to the community emphasizes the importance of apostolic unity as the foundation of authentic teaching. The subsequent revelation on the Mount of Olives provides the Gnostic cosmological framework — the Pleroma, the Mother's error, the archons — and explains the apostles' suffering as an inevitable consequence of being spiritual beings trapped in a material world created by ignorant powers.
