## Overview

For Christian readers, the Letter of Peter to Philip engages with themes central to Christian apostolic theology: the unity of the apostles, the meaning of Christ's post-resurrection appearances, and the significance of apostolic suffering. Peter's role as the one who calls the community together resonates with his canonical portrayal as the leader of the apostles. The text's Gnostic cosmological revelation, however, transforms the canonical framework: the apostles suffer not because of persecution for Christ's sake but because they are spiritual beings trapped in a world made by ignorant archons.
