## Overview

The Letter of Peter to Philip addresses a universal concern of religious communities: the relationship between spiritual insight and worldly suffering. The text's question — why do those who possess divine knowledge still suffer? — echoes the Buddhist recognition that enlightenment does not eliminate physical pain, the Hindu concept of prarabdha karma (karma that must still be experienced even after liberation), and the Christian tradition of the suffering saint. Every tradition must explain why spiritual advancement does not automatically protect against worldly affliction.
