## Overview

Jewish readers find in the Letter of Peter to Philip a transformation of Jewish concepts of prophetic suffering and communal solidarity. The motif of the prophet who suffers because of his message — attested throughout the Hebrew prophetic tradition (Jeremiah, Isaiah's Suffering Servant) — is reinterpreted within a Gnostic framework: the apostles suffer not because they speak God's word to a resistant people but because they are pneumatic beings trapped in a world created by the demiurge. The concept of deficiency caused by the 'Mother's disobedience' transforms the Jewish Sophia/Hokhmah tradition in a direction alien to rabbinic theology.
