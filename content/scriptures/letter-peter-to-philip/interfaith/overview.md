## Overview

The Letter of Peter to Philip addresses a question that every spiritual community must face: why do those who have received divine truth still suffer? The answers offered by different traditions — karma (Hinduism/Buddhism), testing (Islam), sanctification through suffering (Christianity), exile (Judaism) — all attempt to give meaning to what might otherwise seem meaningless. The text's answer — that suffering is inherent in the condition of spiritual beings existing in a material world — represents the Gnostic contribution to this universal conversation.
