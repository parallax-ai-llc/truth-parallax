## Overview

The Letter of Peter to Philip is studied for its composite genre (combining an epistolary opening with a revelation dialogue), its Christology, and its existence in two copies (NHC VIII,2 and Codex Tchacos). The text's concern with apostolic unity and the meaning of suffering suggests a community that understood itself as continuing the apostolic mission under conditions of persecution or marginalization. Scholars have debated whether the text reflects an actual schism (Philip's departure) or uses the epistolary convention to address broader questions of community cohesion.
