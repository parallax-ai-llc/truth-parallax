## Overview

The interfaith reading of the Acharanga Sutra engages the text as a profound resource for contemporary dialogue on non-violence, environmental ethics, animal welfare, and the spiritual foundations of peace. Mahavira's teaching on ahimsa, as preserved in the Acharanga, represents one of the most radical and comprehensive statements of non-violent ethics in world religious literature.

Interfaith interpreters find in the Acharanga Sutra principles that speak to urgent contemporary concerns: its extension of moral consideration to all forms of life anticipates modern environmental ethics, its analysis of the roots of violence in attachment and ignorance resonates with peace studies, and its model of voluntary simplicity offers an alternative to consumer culture.

## Key Interpretive Principles

Interfaith readers seek common ground between the Acharanga's teachings and the ethical insights of other traditions while respecting the distinctive Jain framework in which these teachings are embedded. The text's emphasis on personal responsibility, mindful action, and compassion for all beings provides a natural foundation for inter-religious dialogue on shared ethical commitments.
