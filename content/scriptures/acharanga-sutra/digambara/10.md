## Overview

The Digambara reading of Lecture 10, "Carefulness in Walking," interprets this teaching through the lens of complete renunciation and strict ascetic discipline. While Digambara scholars do not accept the Shvetambara Agamas as canonical scripture in the strict sense, they recognize that the Acharanga Sutra preserves authentic elements of Mahavira's teaching, and they read this lecture with particular attention to passages that support the Digambara understanding of monastic life and spiritual practice.

This lecture addresses themes central to the Jain understanding of carefulness in walking, presenting teachings that have shaped Jain thought and practice for over two millennia.

## Key Themes

### Strict Ascetic Reading

Digambara interpreters emphasize the most rigorous dimensions of this lecture's teaching, reading its prescriptions through the lens of complete renunciation. Where the text describes ascetic practices, Digambara readers understand these as pointing toward the Digambara ideal of total dispossession — including the abandonment of clothing — as the authentic expression of Mahavira's intent.

### Karmic Implications

The Digambara reading pays close attention to this lecture's treatment of karmic bondage and liberation, interpreting the text through the sophisticated Digambara theory of karma developed by thinkers such as Kundakunda and Nemichandra. Every action described in the lecture is evaluated for its karmic consequences, with the Digambara emphasis on the absolute necessity of complete non-attachment.

Lecture 10 contains several passages of particular importance for the digambara interpretive tradition. The teaching on carefulness in walking reveals fundamental aspects of Mahavira's vision of the spiritual path, as understood and elaborated within digambara interpretation scholarship and practice.

The digambara tradition has engaged extensively with this lecture through its commentarial literature. Key commentators have elaborated the lecture's compressed teachings into detailed expositions that address philosophical questions, resolve apparent difficulties, and apply the text's principles to the circumstances of their own times.
