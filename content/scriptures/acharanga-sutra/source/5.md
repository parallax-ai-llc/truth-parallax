---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 5"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Mahavira said: Truthfulness is the second great vow. The monk should speak only what is true, what is pleasant, and what is beneficial.

**2** He should not speak what is false, even if it be pleasant or beneficial. He should not speak what is true if it causes harm, for such speech partakes of violence.

**3** Speech should be measured, gentle, and purposeful. Idle talk, gossip, and careless words bind the soul with karma as surely as lies.

**4** The monk who has mastered truthfulness speaks rarely and deliberately. His words carry the weight of truth and the fragrance of kindness.

**5** Non-stealing is the third great vow. The monk should not take anything that is not freely given to him. Even to use something beyond the time or manner permitted is a form of stealing.

**6** The monk should accept only what is offered, only what he needs, and only what does not deprive another. He should not desire what belongs to others.

**7** Celibacy is the fourth great vow. The monk should abstain from all sensual pleasures, for sensual pleasures are the strongest chains that bind the soul.

**8** The celibate monk should not look at, think about, or speak about matters that arouse desire. He should guard his senses at all times.

**9** Non-possessiveness is the fifth great vow. The monk should possess nothing — no property, no wealth, no comforts. He should be free from all attachment to material things.

**10** The monk who practises these five great vows with sincerity and completeness burns away the accumulated karma of countless lives and advances rapidly toward liberation.
