---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 15"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Mahavira said: I shall now describe the nature of liberation, the supreme goal of all spiritual practice.

**2** Liberation is the state in which the soul is completely free from all karma. It has shed every particle of karmic matter and shines in its inherent perfection.

**3** The liberated soul possesses infinite knowledge — it knows all things, past, present, and future, simultaneously and perfectly.

**4** It possesses infinite perception — it perceives all things, in all places, at all times, without obstruction.

**5** It possesses infinite bliss — a bliss that is beyond all worldly pleasures, beyond all heavenly pleasures, beyond all description.

**6** It possesses infinite energy — the power to do all things, though it chooses to do nothing, for it has no desire and no need.

**7** The liberated soul rises to the summit of the universe, to Siddha Loka, where it abides forever. It does not return to the cycle of birth and death.

**8** In Siddha Loka, the liberated souls exist without bodies, without senses, without minds. They exist as pure consciousness, pure knowledge, pure bliss.

**9** All liberated souls are equal. There is no hierarchy among them, no difference in their perfection. Each is complete in itself, infinite and eternal.

**10** This is the goal toward which all Jain practice is directed. Every act of non-violence, every moment of meditation, every practice of austerity brings the soul closer to this supreme state.
