---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 9"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of karma, for karma is the substance that binds the soul and causes its transmigration.

**2** Karma is a subtle form of matter that adheres to the soul through the activity of the passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed.

**3** There are eight kinds of karma: knowledge-obscuring karma, perception-obscuring karma, feeling-producing karma, deluding karma, life-determining karma, body-determining karma, status-determining karma, and obstructing karma.

**4** Knowledge-obscuring karma prevents the soul from knowing. Perception-obscuring karma prevents the soul from perceiving. When these two are destroyed, the soul attains omniscience.

**5** Feeling-producing karma causes the soul to experience pleasure and pain. Deluding karma causes the soul to identify with what is not the soul and to experience the passions.

**6** Life-determining karma determines the length of life in a particular birth. Body-determining karma determines the type of body. Status-determining karma determines the social standing.

**7** Obstructing karma prevents the soul from exercising its inherent powers of giving, gaining, enjoying, and exerting its will.

**8** The influx of karma is caused by the activities of body, speech, and mind, especially when motivated by the passions. The monk should therefore control his activities and conquer his passions.

**9** The stopping of karmic influx is achieved through the practice of the three guards (of body, speech, and mind), the five types of carefulness, and the ten forms of dharma.

**10** The shedding of karma already accumulated is achieved through the practice of austerities — both external and internal. When all karma is shed, the soul attains liberation.
