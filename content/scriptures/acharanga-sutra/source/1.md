---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 1"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard: the venerable Mahavira, the great hero, the Tirthankara, who had overcome all attachment, who had conquered all passions, who had attained omniscience, spoke thus to his disciples.

**2** The wise who have learned the truth know that all beings desire to live. No being wishes to die. Therefore, let no one kill any creature, nor cause any creature to be killed, nor consent to the killing of any creature.

**3** All beings are fond of life; they like pleasure and hate pain; they shun destruction and like to live; they long to live. To all beings life is dear.

**4** He who knows the nature of the soul knows that the soul is bound by karma through the actions of the body, speech, and mind. These actions, when performed with passion and attachment, bind the soul.

**5** The ignorant, by harming others, harm themselves. He who harms another being harms his own soul. He who kills a creature kills his own nature.

**6** All beings with two, three, four, or five senses — all tremble at punishment, all fear death. Comparing oneself with others, let one not kill nor cause to kill.

**7** The mendicant who comprehends this and who practises the discipline of non-violence in thought, word, and deed — that mendicant is on the path of liberation.

**8** The wise man should not act sinfully toward earth, nor cause others to act so, nor allow others to act so. He who knows the nature of sin against earth knows all sins against all beings.

**9** A monk who injures no creature, who does not take what is not given, who is chaste, who possesses nothing — such a monk is free from the bonds of karma.

**10** This is the first great lesson taught by the Jinas: non-violence is the supreme religion. Non-violence is the supreme self-control. Non-violence is the supreme gift. Non-violence is the supreme penance.
