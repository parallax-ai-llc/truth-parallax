---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 11"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should understand the nature of the soul, for the soul is the central reality of the Jain teaching.

**2** The soul is eternal, uncreated, and indestructible. It has existed since beginningless time and will exist for all eternity. It is never born and never dies.

**3** The soul is characterized by consciousness. Consciousness is not a quality that the soul possesses; it is the very nature of the soul. Where there is a soul, there is consciousness.

**4** In its pure state, the soul possesses infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. These are its inherent perfections, obscured by karma.

**5** The soul is not all-pervading like the Brahman of the Vedantins. It is of the same size as the body it occupies, expanding and contracting as it moves from body to body.

**6** Every living being, from the smallest one-sensed organism to the most developed five-sensed being, possesses a soul. All souls are equal in their essential nature.

**7** The differences among beings — in knowledge, in perception, in power — are not inherent in the soul but are caused by the varying degrees of karmic obstruction.

**8** When all karmic obstruction is removed, every soul attains the same infinite perfection. The liberated soul of an ant is equal to the liberated soul of a Tirthankara.

**9** This teaching of the equality of all souls is the foundation of Jain non-violence. Since all souls are equal, no soul has the right to harm another.

**10** He who truly understands the nature of the soul treats all beings with equal respect and compassion, for he sees in every creature a soul striving for the same liberation that he seeks.
