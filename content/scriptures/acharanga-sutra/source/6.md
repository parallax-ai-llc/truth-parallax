---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 6"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should practise the six essential duties daily, for they are the foundation of spiritual progress.

**2** The first duty is Samayika — equanimity. The monk should practice being equal toward all beings, free from attachment and aversion, for forty-eight minutes.

**3** The second duty is Chaturvimsati-stava — the praise of the twenty-four Tirthankaras. By praising the Jinas, the monk strengthens his faith and his aspiration.

**4** The third duty is Vandana — paying respect to the teachers. The monk should bow before his guru with humility and gratitude, recognizing the guru as the guide on the path.

**5** The fourth duty is Pratikramana — repentance. The monk should reflect upon his faults and transgressions and sincerely repent for them, resolving not to repeat them.

**6** The fifth duty is Kayotsarga — the abandonment of the body. The monk should stand or sit in complete stillness, transcending all bodily awareness, absorbed in the contemplation of the soul.

**7** The sixth duty is Pratyakhyana — renunciation. The monk should renounce specific attachments and activities, gradually loosening the bonds of karma.

**8** These six duties purify the soul, reduce the influx of karma, and shed the karma already accumulated. They are the practical means of spiritual progress.

**9** The monk who performs these duties daily, with sincerity and devotion, progresses steadily on the path of liberation.

**10** Even the householder can practise modified forms of these duties and thereby make progress on the spiritual path, preparing for the complete renunciation in future lives.
