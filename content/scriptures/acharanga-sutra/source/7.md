---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 7"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should practise careful movement, for the earth beneath his feet teems with life.

**2** He should walk during daylight only, watching the path before him, placing his feet with care, lest he crush the tiny creatures of the earth.

**3** He should not walk in the rain, for the rain brings forth countless minute beings. He should not walk in darkness, for he cannot see what he might harm.

**4** He should not stamp his feet, not run, not jump. He should walk slowly, deliberately, with full awareness of every step.

**5** The careful monk examines his food before eating, his water before drinking, his seat before sitting, his bed before lying down.

**6** He does not eat after sunset, for in the darkness, insects may fall into his food unseen. He does not eat raw food that contains seeds, for each seed is a potential life.

**7** He does not kindle fire, for fire destroys countless beings. He does not fan fire, for the same reason. He does not extinguish fire with water, for the water beings are also harmed.

**8** These precautions may seem extreme to the worldly, but the monk who understands the sanctity of all life observes them with joy, knowing that he is treading the path of the Jinas.

**9** The careful life is not a burden but a liberation. By restraining himself from harming others, the monk frees himself from the karma of violence.

**10** This teaching of carefulness is unique to the Jain tradition. It expresses the profound conviction that all life is sacred and that every creature, however small, possesses a soul.
