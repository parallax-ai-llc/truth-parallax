---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 8"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Mahavira said: The monk should practise meditation as the direct means of purifying the soul and attaining liberation.

**2** Meditation is of four kinds: sorrowful meditation, cruel meditation, righteous meditation, and pure meditation. The first two bind the soul; the last two liberate it.

**3** Sorrowful meditation arises from the loss of what is pleasant and the encounter with what is unpleasant. It binds the soul with the karma of suffering.

**4** Cruel meditation arises from the desire to harm others, to protect one's possessions with violence, and to deceive others. It binds the soul with the heaviest karma.

**5** Righteous meditation consists of the contemplation of the teachings of the Jinas, the contemplation of the nature of the universe, the contemplation of the results of karma, and the contemplation of the form of the universe.

**6** Pure meditation is the contemplation of the soul in its pure nature — infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. This meditation destroys all remaining karma.

**7** In pure meditation, the soul withdraws from all external objects and rests in its own nature. All mental modifications cease. The soul shines with its inherent light.

**8** The practitioner of pure meditation should fix his consciousness upon the soul alone, excluding all other objects. He should maintain this contemplation with unwavering concentration.

**9** When pure meditation reaches its perfection, the soul is completely freed from karma. It rises to the summit of the universe, to Siddha Loka, the abode of the liberated souls.

**10** There the soul abides forever in its infinite perfection, never again to be born, never again to suffer, never again to die. This is Moksha, the supreme goal of all spiritual practice.
