---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 14"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Mahavira said: The path of the householder is also honourable, for not all can renounce the world at once.

**2** The householder should observe the five minor vows: limited non-violence, limited truthfulness, limited non-stealing, limited celibacy, and limited non-possessiveness.

**3** He should limit the area of his activities, not ranging beyond a fixed boundary. He should limit the quantity of his possessions, not accumulating beyond a fixed amount.

**4** He should observe periods of meditation and religious practice daily, setting aside time for the contemplation of the soul and the study of the scriptures.

**5** He should observe fasts on holy days, give charity to monks and the poor, and participate in the worship at the Jain temple.

**6** The householder should earn his livelihood by honest means. He should not engage in trades that involve violence — such as the sale of weapons, poisons, or living beings.

**7** He should not eat meat, for the killing of animals for food is a grave sin. He should not eat after sunset, for the same reasons as the monk.

**8** He should treat his wife, children, parents, and dependents with kindness and fairness. He should be a good neighbour and a responsible member of society.

**9** The householder who lives according to these principles accumulates merit and prepares himself for the full renunciation in this life or in a future life.

**10** The Lord said: "Do not despise the householder's path. It is the foundation upon which the monk's path is built. Without the householder's support, the monks could not survive."
