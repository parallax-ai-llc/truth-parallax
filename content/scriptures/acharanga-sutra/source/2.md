---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Mahavira said: The soul is bound by karma, and karma is the cause of its wandering through the cycle of births and deaths in the various states of existence.

**2** There are four states of existence: as a celestial being, as a human, as an animal, and as a hellish being. The soul transmigrates through these states according to its karma.

**3** The cause of karma is the passionate activity of the soul — desire, hatred, and delusion. When these passions are conquered, the soul is freed from karma.

**4** He who understands this truth should renounce all attachment and aversion. He should cultivate equanimity toward all beings and all conditions of life.

**5** The monk should endure with equanimity the twenty-two hardships: hunger, thirst, cold, heat, insect bites, nakedness, tedium, women, wandering, sitting, resting, abuse, beating, begging, failure to obtain alms, disease, thorns, dirt, respect, knowledge, ignorance, and lack of faith.

**6** He who endures these hardships without complaint, without anger, without attachment — such a monk is truly practising austerity.

**7** Austerity is not merely the mortification of the body. True austerity is the purification of the soul through the conquest of the passions.

**8** External austerities are: fasting, eating less than one's fill, restricting the kinds of food, giving up delicacies, sleeping in lonely places, and mortification of the body.

**9** Internal austerities are: expiation of sins, reverence, service to the elders, study of the scriptures, meditation, and giving up attachment to the body.

**10** He who practises both external and internal austerities with sincerity and devotion progresses rapidly on the path of liberation.
