---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 10"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Mahavira said: The ten forms of dharma are the supreme virtues that every seeker should cultivate on the path of liberation.

**2** The first is forgiveness. The monk should forgive all beings who have harmed him, without any trace of anger or resentment. Forgiveness liberates the soul from the karma of hatred.

**3** The second is humility. The monk should be free from pride and arrogance. He should regard himself as the servant of all beings and the student of all teachers.

**4** The third is straightforwardness. The monk should be free from deceit and hypocrisy. His thoughts, words, and deeds should be in harmony.

**5** The fourth is contentment. The monk should be satisfied with whatever comes to him, without desire for more and without complaint about less.

**6** The fifth is truthfulness. The monk should speak only the truth, and should speak it with gentleness and compassion.

**7** The sixth is self-restraint. The monk should control his senses and his passions, maintaining constant vigilance over his thoughts, words, and deeds.

**8** The seventh is austerity. The monk should practise penance for the purification of the soul, enduring hardships with equanimity.

**9** The eighth is renunciation. The monk should give up all possessions and all attachments, living without any sense of "mine."

**10** The ninth is non-attachment, and the tenth is celibacy. He who cultivates these ten virtues progresses rapidly on the path of liberation and approaches the state of the Siddhas.
