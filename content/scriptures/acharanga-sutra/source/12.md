---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 12"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Mahavira said: The universe is eternal. It was never created and will never be destroyed. It exists of its own nature, without the need of a creator.

**2** The universe consists of six eternal substances: soul, matter, the medium of motion, the medium of rest, space, and time. These six substances have always existed and will always exist.

**3** Souls are infinite in number. They fill the universe in every part. Some are liberated, dwelling at the summit of the universe in eternal perfection. Others are bound, wandering through the cycle of births and deaths.

**4** Matter is the substance that constitutes the physical world. It is also the substance of karma, which adheres to the soul and obscures its inherent qualities.

**5** The medium of motion enables souls and matter to move. The medium of rest enables them to be still. Without these two, neither motion nor rest would be possible.

**6** Space provides the room in which all substances exist. Occupied space is finite; it is surrounded by empty space, which is infinite.

**7** Time is the substance that enables change and duration. Without time, nothing could change, nothing could endure, nothing could be measured.

**8** The universe has no creator. It operates according to its own eternal laws. Souls are bound by their own karma and liberated by their own efforts. No god can bind them or free them.

**9** The Tirthankaras are not gods who created the world. They are souls who have attained liberation through their own efforts and who teach the path to others out of compassion.

**10** This view of the universe is the foundation of the Jain philosophy. It is based on the principle of non-absolutism — that truth is many-sided and that no single perspective can capture the whole truth.
