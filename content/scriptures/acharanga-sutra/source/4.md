---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 4"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Mahavira said: The practice of non-violence requires the utmost vigilance, for violence can be committed not only by the body but also by speech and mind.

**2** Violence of the body includes killing, injuring, binding, mutilating, and overloading living creatures. The monk should abstain from all such acts.

**3** Violence of speech includes harsh words, false accusations, slander, ridicule, and all speech that causes pain to others. The monk should guard his speech.

**4** Violence of the mind includes harboring thoughts of hatred, ill-will, cruelty, and indifference to the suffering of others. The monk should purify his thoughts.

**5** Non-violence extends to all categories of living beings: those with one sense, such as earth-bodies, water-bodies, fire-bodies, air-bodies, and plant-bodies; and those with two, three, four, or five senses.

**6** The wise monk walks carefully on the path, sweeping it before him, lest he crush a living creature. He strains his water, lest he swallow a tiny being. He checks his seat before sitting.

**7** He speaks gentle words that cause no pain. He thinks thoughts of compassion for all beings. He considers all life sacred.

**8** Non-violence is not merely the absence of physical harm. It is the positive cultivation of love, compassion, and goodwill toward all living beings.

**9** He who practises perfect non-violence in thought, word, and deed has conquered the greatest enemy — his own passions. Such a person radiates peace and goodwill.

**10** This is the supreme teaching of the Jinas: Ahimsa Paramo Dharma — Non-violence is the highest religion. Let all beings be happy. Let all beings be without disease. Let all beings see what is auspicious. Let no being suffer.
