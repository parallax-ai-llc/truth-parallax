---
title: "Acharanga Sutra - Chapter 3"
scripture: "acharanga-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Mahavira said: The world is full of suffering. Birth is suffering, old age is suffering, disease is suffering, death is suffering, and being united with the unpleasant is suffering.

**2** The cause of suffering is attachment. From attachment arises desire, from desire arises action, from action arises karma, and from karma arises rebirth and suffering.

**3** The cessation of suffering comes through the cessation of attachment. When attachment is destroyed, karma is destroyed, and the soul attains its natural state of infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy.

**4** The path to the cessation of suffering is the threefold path of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct. These three together constitute the path of liberation.

**5** Right faith is the belief in the true nature of reality as taught by the Jinas. It is the foundation upon which right knowledge and right conduct are built.

**6** Right knowledge is the understanding of the seven categories of reality: soul, non-soul, the influx of karma, the bondage of karma, the stopping of karmic influx, the shedding of karma, and liberation.

**7** Right conduct is the practice of the five great vows: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness.

**8** These five vows, when practised completely by monks, are called the great vows. When practised partially by householders, they are called the minor vows.

**9** The householder should also observe the three supplementary vows: limiting the area of one's activities, limiting the quantity of one's possessions, and devoting time to religious practices.

**10** He who walks this threefold path with sincerity and perseverance attains liberation from the cycle of birth and death, and his soul shines forth in its inherent perfection.
