## Overview

The Shvetambara tradition venerates Lecture 16, "The Final Lectures," as the direct teaching of Lord Mahavira transmitted through the canonical lineage. This lecture is studied, memorized, and expounded by Shvetambara scholars and monks as an authoritative guide to the understanding and practice of the Jain path. The Shvetambara reading situates this lecture within the larger canonical framework and interprets it in light of the commentarial traditions of Shilanka, Haribhadra, and other great Shvetambara acharyas.

This lecture addresses themes central to the Jain understanding of the final lectures, presenting teachings that have shaped Jain thought and practice for over two millennia.

## Key Themes

### Canonical Authority

Shvetambara interpreters approach this lecture as authoritative scripture whose teachings are binding on all who follow the Jain path. The lecture's prescriptions are interpreted within the broader canonical context, with attention to how they relate to parallel passages in other Agamas and to the commentarial elaborations of the great Shvetambara acharyas.

### Practical Application

The Shvetambara reading emphasizes the practical applicability of this lecture's teachings to the daily life of monks, nuns, and laypeople. While the most demanding prescriptions apply fully only to monastics, Shvetambara interpreters extract principles from this lecture that can guide the ethical conduct of the entire Jain community.

## Key Verses/Passages

Lecture 16 contains several passages of particular importance for the shvetambara interpretive tradition. The teaching on the final lectures reveals fundamental aspects of Mahavira's vision of the spiritual path, as understood and elaborated within shvetambara interpretation scholarship and practice.

## Commentary Tradition

The shvetambara tradition has engaged extensively with this lecture through its commentarial literature. Key commentators have elaborated the lecture's compressed teachings into detailed expositions that address philosophical questions, resolve apparent difficulties, and apply the text's principles to the circumstances of their own times.
