Shia interpretation of Ihya Ulum al-Din book 6, "The Mysteries of Fasting," engages with Ghazali's spiritual and ethical teachings while adapting the legal framework to Shia jurisprudence. The spiritual psychology presented in this book — particularly its analysis of the heart's states, the soul's diseases, and the path of purification — resonates with teachings attributed to the Imams of the Ahl al-Bayt.

Fayd Kashani's adaptation of this book in his al-Mahajja al-Bayda demonstrates how the core spiritual insights can be transposed into a Shia key. The fundamental concerns — sincerity of intention, purification of the soul, and knowledge of God through self-knowledge — transcend sectarian boundaries and represent a shared Islamic spiritual heritage that both Sunni and Shia traditions claim.
