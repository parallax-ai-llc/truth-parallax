Sunni reading of Ihya Ulum al-Din book 32, "On Patience and Gratitude," appreciates Ghazali's integration of legal obligation with spiritual depth. This book demonstrates Ghazali's characteristic method: beginning with the external requirements of Islamic practice as established by the jurists, then revealing the inner states and intentions that transform mere compliance into genuine worship. The Sunni reader finds here both reliable guidance on religious duty and aspiration toward spiritual excellence.

Ghazali's treatment draws on hadith, Quranic commentary, jurisprudence, and the sayings of early Muslim scholars and saints. While some hadith citations in this book have been questioned by specialists in hadith criticism, the overall teaching has been accepted by the Sunni scholarly consensus as faithfully representing the spirit of prophetic guidance. The book serves as a model for how legal and spiritual learning can be unified in practice.
