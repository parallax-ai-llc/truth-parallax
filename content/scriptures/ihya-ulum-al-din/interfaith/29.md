Interfaith reading of Ihya Ulum al-Din book 29, "On the Condemnation of Pride and Self-Admiration," explores how Ghazali's teaching addresses spiritual concerns shared across religious traditions. The themes of this book — the cultivation of inner virtue, the discipline of desire, the pursuit of sincerity, and the relationship between the human heart and the divine — resonate with the spiritual teachings of Judaism, Christianity, Hinduism, and Buddhism.

Interfaith readers find in this book a model for how deep commitment to a particular tradition can illuminate universal dimensions of the human spiritual condition. Ghazali's practical wisdom about self-knowledge, the dangers of spiritual pride, and the transformative power of sincere devotion speaks across religious boundaries while remaining firmly rooted in Islamic faith and practice.
