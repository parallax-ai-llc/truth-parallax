## Overview

Interfaith engagement with the Ihya Ulum al-Din has been drawn to Ghazali's emphasis on themes that transcend religious boundaries: the primacy of sincerity, the dangers of spiritual pride, the transformative power of self-knowledge, and the centrality of love in the relationship between the human and the divine.

## Interpretive Approach

Interfaith readers find in the Ihya a sophisticated articulation of concerns shared across religious traditions: How does one move from external compliance to genuine spiritual life? How does one purify intention and overcome self-deception? What is the relationship between moral action and spiritual transformation? Ghazali's answers, while rooted in Islamic theology and practice, address universal dimensions of the human spiritual condition.

## Significance for Dialogue

The Ihya offers interfaith dialogue a model of how a tradition can maintain its distinctive identity while addressing universal human concerns. Ghazali's insistence that religious life must be animated by inner sincerity rather than mere outward conformity resonates with reform movements in every tradition. His vision of knowledge of self as the path to knowledge of God echoes the Delphic oracle, the Upanishadic tradition, and the Christian mystics' teaching that the soul is the image of God.
