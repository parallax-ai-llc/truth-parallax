Academic analysis of Ihya Ulum al-Din book 11, "On the Etiquette of Eating," examines Ghazali's sources, argumentative strategies, and the book's place within the larger structure of the work. Scholars trace the material to earlier authors — particularly al-Muhasibi, al-Makki, and al-Qushayri — while analyzing Ghazali's distinctive contributions and innovations. The book's hadith citations have been evaluated by specialists, with some found to be weak or fabricated, though the overall ethical and spiritual teaching remains respected.

Recent scholarship has explored this book's contributions to moral psychology, comparing Ghazali's analysis with Aristotelian virtue ethics, Buddhist psychology, and contemporary cognitive science. The sophistication of Ghazali's phenomenological descriptions of spiritual and psychological states has attracted attention from scholars working at the intersection of philosophy, psychology, and religious studies.
