## On Intention, Sincerity, and Truthfulness

**1.** The Prophet said: "Actions are judged by intentions, and every person will receive what he intended." This is the key to all worship and the measure of all deeds.

**2.** Intention is the soul of the act. The act without intention is a body without a soul—a dead thing that benefits no one.

**3.** Two people may perform the same act: one with sincerity and one with ostentation. The first earns the pleasure of God; the second earns nothing but the fleeting admiration of people.

**4.** Sincerity is the purification of the intention from all admixtures. The sincere act is the act done for God alone, with no thought of reward, praise, or recognition.

**5.** The Prophet said: "God does not accept any act unless it is done sincerely for His sake." Sincerity is the condition of acceptance.

**6.** Truthfulness is the alignment of the outward with the inward. The truthful man is the same in private as he is in public, the same in secret as he is in the open.

**7.** The truthful man does not say one thing and do another. His tongue, his heart, and his limbs all speak the same language.

**8.** Sincerity is difficult because the ego is subtle. It hides in the corners of the intention and whispers: "Do this so that people will praise you." The sincere man hunts the ego out of every corner.

**9.** A saint was asked: "What is the hardest thing?" He replied: "Sincerity. I have struggled with my sincerity for thirty years, and I am still not sure of it."

**10.** This is because the ego can even corrupt the desire for sincerity—the man who is proud of his sincerity is no longer sincere.

**11.** The levels of intention are three: the intention of the beginner, which is to act for the sake of reward; the intention of the intermediate, which is to act for the sake of God; and the intention of the advanced, which is to act because God has willed it, without any awareness of self.

**12.** At the highest level, the servant does not think: "I am being sincere." He simply acts, and the sincerity is natural, like breathing.

**13.** Purify your intention before every act. Ask yourself: "For whom am I doing this?" If the answer is "For God," proceed. If the answer is anything else, stop and purify.

**14.** The intention is more important than the act, for a small act done with sincerity outweighs a great act done with ostentation.

**15.** And know that the ultimate sincerity is to be unaware of your sincerity, for the awareness of sincerity is itself a veil between you and God.
