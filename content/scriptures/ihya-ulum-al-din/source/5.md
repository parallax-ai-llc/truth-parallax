## The Mysteries of Almsgiving

**1.** Almsgiving purifies wealth as prayer purifies the soul. The one who gives his wealth in the way of God has taken a step toward liberation from the prison of possessions.

**2.** Wealth is a test, not a blessing, unless it is used in the service of God. The miser who hoards is more enslaved than the slave in chains.

**3.** The Prophet said: "Protect yourselves from the Fire, even with half a date." This means that no gift is too small if it is given with sincerity.

**4.** The mystery of almsgiving is that what you give does not diminish your wealth—it increases it. This is a spiritual law that contradicts the logic of the marketplace.

**5.** God says: "Whatever you spend, He replaces it." The hand that gives is the hand that receives. The one who withholds withholds from himself.

**6.** There are three levels of almsgiving: the almsgiving of the common people, which is to give from surplus; the almsgiving of the elect, which is to give what is needed; and the almsgiving of the elect of the elect, which is to give everything and trust in God.

**7.** Abu Bakr brought all his wealth to the Prophet and was asked: "What have you left for your family?" He replied: "God and His Messenger." This is the highest degree.

**8.** Do not give to be seen by others, for the almsgiving that is done for show is not almsgiving—it is commerce, buying the admiration of people with the currency of God.

**9.** The best charity is that which is given in secret, so that the left hand does not know what the right hand has given.

**10.** Do not follow your charity with reminders of your generosity, for the reminder destroys the charity as fire destroys wood.

**11.** The poor man who receives your charity is doing you a favor, not the other way around. He is giving you the opportunity to purify your soul.

**12.** Give to the poor not because they need your wealth but because you need to give it. Your need to give is greater than their need to receive.

**13.** The truly generous man gives without being asked, and the truly wise man gives before the need arises.

**14.** Do not wait until you are wealthy to be generous, for the habit of generosity must be cultivated in poverty if it is to flourish in wealth.

**15.** The mystery of almsgiving is the mystery of love: the more you give, the more you have. This is the mathematics of the spirit, and it has no equation.
