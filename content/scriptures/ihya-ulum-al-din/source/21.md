## On the Wonders of the Heart

**1.** The heart is the king of the body, and the limbs are its soldiers. When the king is righteous, the soldiers are righteous. When the king is corrupt, the soldiers are corrupt.

**2.** The Prophet said: "There is in the body a piece of flesh—if it is sound, the whole body is sound; if it is corrupt, the whole body is corrupt. That is the heart."

**3.** The heart is not the physical organ that pumps blood—it is the subtle, spiritual center of the human being that is capable of knowing God.

**4.** The heart is the mirror of the divine. When it is polished, it reflects the light of God. When it is tarnished, it reflects only the darkness of the world.

**5.** The polishing of the heart is the work of a lifetime. Each act of worship, each act of kindness, each moment of remembrance is a stroke of the polishing cloth.

**6.** The heart has states: expansion and contraction, joy and sorrow, openness and closure. These states are messages from God. The wise man reads them as a sailor reads the wind.

**7.** The heart is the battlefield between the angel and the devil. The angel whispers from the right, calling to good. The devil whispers from the left, calling to evil. The heart chooses.

**8.** The wonder of the heart is that it can contain what the heavens and the earth cannot contain: the knowledge of God.

**9.** The heart is the seed of the human being. The body is the husk. When the seed dies, the husk is worthless. When the seed lives, the husk is honored.

**10.** The healthy heart is the heart that loves God more than anything else, fears God more than anything else, and hopes in God more than anything else.

**11.** The sick heart is the heart that loves the world more than God, fears creation more than God, and hopes in people more than in God.

**12.** The dead heart is the heart that has forgotten God entirely and has become absorbed in the world. This is the greatest death, for the body may be alive while the heart is dead.

**13.** The revival of the heart is the purpose of all religion, all worship, and all striving. It is not the body that needs to be saved—it is the heart.

**14.** The heart speaks a language that the mind cannot understand. It is the language of intuition, of spiritual taste, of direct perception. Trust this language, for it comes from a place deeper than thought.

**15.** O seeker, guard your heart as you guard your most precious treasure, for it is the vessel of faith, the seat of knowledge, and the throne of God in the human being.
