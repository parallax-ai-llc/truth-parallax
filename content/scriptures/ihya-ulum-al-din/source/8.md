## The Etiquette of Reciting the Quran

**1.** The Quran is the speech of God that descended from the unseen world to the seen world. Reciting it is not merely reading words—it is listening to God speak.

**2.** The proper etiquette of recitation begins with the state of the heart. The heart must be present, attentive, and humble before the Majesty of the Speaker.

**3.** When you open the Quran, intend that you are opening a letter from the King of all kings, addressed specifically to you.

**4.** The Prophet said: "The best of you is he who learns the Quran and teaches it." Learning here means not memorization alone but understanding and practice.

**5.** Recite with deliberation, not in haste. The Quran was not revealed to be raced through but to be contemplated. Each verse is a garden; do not run past it without stopping to inhale its fragrance.

**6.** When you come to a verse of mercy, pause and ask for mercy. When you come to a verse of punishment, pause and seek refuge. When you come to a verse of glorification, glorify.

**7.** The reciter should weep, and if tears do not come, he should make himself weep, for the Quran was sent down with sorrow and should be received with sorrow.

**8.** This sorrow is not despair—it is the sorrow of the lover who reads a letter from the Beloved and is overwhelmed by the beauty of the words and the distance of the Writer.

**9.** The Quran has an outward meaning and an inward meaning, and the inward has an inward, to seven depths. The scholar reads the first level; the saint reads the seventh.

**10.** Do not think that you have understood a verse because you have read its commentary. The commentary is the shell; the meaning is the pearl, and the pearl is found only by diving.

**11.** The Quran is like a sea: the child plays on its shore, the swimmer ventures into the shallows, and the diver descends into the depths. Each finds treasures according to his capacity.

**12.** Recite the Quran at dawn, for dawn is the time of opening, and the heart is most receptive at that hour.

**13.** Let the Quran be your companion in solitude and your guide in confusion. When you do not know which way to turn, open the Book, and the way will become clear.

**14.** The one who recites the Quran and acts upon it is clothed by God in garments of honor on the Day of Judgment. The one who recites it and ignores it has no excuse.

**15.** The mystery of the Quran is that it is inexhaustible. A verse that you read a thousand times reveals a new meaning on the thousand-and-first reading. This is because the words are finite but their source is Infinite.
