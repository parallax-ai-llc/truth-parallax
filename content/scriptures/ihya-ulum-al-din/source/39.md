## On the Remembrance of Death

**1.** The remembrance of death is the most powerful medicine for the heedless heart. The one who remembers death often is freed from the delusions of the world.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Remember often the destroyer of pleasures"—that is, death. The remembrance of death does not destroy pleasure—it purifies it.

**3.** Death is not an ending—it is a transition. The caterpillar does not end when it enters the cocoon; it is transformed. So the soul does not end at death; it is released.

**4.** But the state in which the soul is released depends on the state in which it lived. The soul that lived in remembrance of God returns to God with joy. The soul that lived in heedlessness returns with regret.

**5.** Prepare for death as a traveler prepares for a journey. The wise traveler packs his provisions before the journey begins. The fool waits until the caravan has left.

**6.** The provisions for the journey of death are good deeds, and good deeds are the currency of the next world. In this world, gold is the currency. In the next world, deeds are the currency.

**7.** Visit the sick and attend funerals, for these are reminders of the fragility of life. The one who attends many funerals does not forget that his own funeral is approaching.

**8.** The Prophet used to visit graves and say: "Peace be upon you, O inhabitants of the graves. You have preceded us, and we shall follow."

**9.** When you lie down to sleep, imagine that it is your final sleep. When you wake up, imagine that it is your final morning. This practice will sharpen your awareness and purify your actions.

**10.** Do not postpone repentance, for death does not send a warning. Many have gone to sleep intending to pray in the morning and have not woken up.

**11.** The deathbed is not the place to begin your preparation—it is the place where your preparation is tested. What you sowed in life, you reap at death.

**12.** The one who remembers death is not morbid—he is the most alive of all people, for he wastes no time, hoards no grudges, and withholds no love.

**13.** Death strips away all pretenses. The titles, the wealth, the status—all are left behind. Only the soul and its deeds continue.

**14.** The grave is the first station of the next world. It is either a garden from the gardens of Paradise or a pit from the pits of the Fire. What makes the difference is what you carried with you.

**15.** O seeker, let the remembrance of death be your constant companion. It will not darken your life—it will illuminate it, as a lamp illuminates a dark room by making visible what was hidden.
