## On Vigilance and Self-Examination

**1.** Vigilance is the state of constant awareness of God's presence. The vigilant man knows that he is always being watched, and this knowledge shapes his every action.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Worship God as if you see Him, for though you do not see Him, He sees you." This is the definition of vigilance.

**3.** The vigilant man does not sin in private, for he knows that there is no private place. The one who sins in secret because he thinks no one can see has forgotten the One who sees everything.

**4.** Self-examination is the partner of vigilance. Vigilance watches over the present moment; self-examination reviews the past.

**5.** Umar ibn al-Khattab said: "Call yourselves to account before you are called to account, and weigh your deeds before they are weighed for you."

**6.** The wise man examines himself at the end of each day. He reviews his actions, his words, and his thoughts. What was good, he gives thanks for. What was bad, he repents of.

**7.** This daily accounting is like the merchant's inventory. The merchant who does not check his stock will find himself bankrupt. The believer who does not check his soul will find himself spiritually bankrupt.

**8.** There are two types of self-examination: before the act and after the act. Before the act, examine your intention. After the act, examine your execution.

**9.** Before the act, ask: "Is this act lawful? Is my intention pure? Am I doing this for God or for my ego?" If the answers are satisfactory, proceed. If not, stop.

**10.** After the act, ask: "Did I perform this act well? Was my intention pure throughout? Did any ostentation creep in?" If the act was good, give thanks. If it was flawed, repent.

**11.** The vigilant man is like a sentinel on a watchtower. He does not sleep, he does not rest, he does not look away, for the enemy can approach from any direction at any time.

**12.** The enemy is the ego, and its approach is subtle. It does not attack with a sword—it whispers with a honeyed tongue.

**13.** The Prophet said: "The intelligent man is the one who takes account of himself and works for what comes after death."

**14.** Be strict with yourself and lenient with others, for you know your own faults from within, and you know the faults of others only from the surface.

**15.** The perfection of vigilance is to reach the point where you are always in God's presence, not because you are trying but because you have forgotten how to be anywhere else.
