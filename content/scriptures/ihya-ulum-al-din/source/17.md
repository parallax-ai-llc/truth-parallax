## On the Etiquette of Travel

**1.** Travel is a teacher, and the road is a school. The one who has never left his home knows only one page of the book of the world.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Travel, and you will be healthy." Travel strengthens the body, broadens the mind, and humbles the spirit.

**3.** When you set out on a journey, begin with a prayer and commend yourself to God. The traveler is under the protection of God, and his supplication is answered.

**4.** Travel with good companions, for the companion determines the quality of the journey. A good companion is a mercy; a bad companion is a trial.

**5.** Be gentle with your mount and your vehicle, for the Prophet forbade overloading beasts and said: "Fear God in your treatment of these dumb animals."

**6.** The etiquette of travel includes carrying provisions for yourself and others, being patient with delays and hardships, and remembering that every difficulty on the road is a purification.

**7.** When you arrive in a new place, pray two units of prayer in gratitude for your safe arrival. And when you see a new sight, remember the One who created it.

**8.** The traveler learns humility, for on the road, he is a stranger, and the stranger has no status. This is good for the soul, which needs regular reminders of its dependence on God.

**9.** Travel to seek knowledge, for the Prophet said: "Seek knowledge, even unto China." The scholars of the past traveled for months to hear a single tradition.

**10.** Do not travel solely for pleasure, for the purpose of travel is benefit—benefit to the mind, benefit to the body, and benefit to the spirit.

**11.** The greatest journey is the journey from the self to God, and this journey requires no mount, no provision, and no companion—only sincerity.

**12.** But the outward journey is a metaphor for the inward journey, and the hardships of the road mirror the hardships of the spiritual path.

**13.** The one who has traveled knows that the world is both vast and small—vast in its diversity and small in its impermanence.

**14.** Return from your travels with gratitude for your home, for the purpose of travel is to teach you the value of what you have left behind.

**15.** And know that this life itself is a journey, and the grave is the destination. Travel light, for the one who carries too much baggage will arrive exhausted.
