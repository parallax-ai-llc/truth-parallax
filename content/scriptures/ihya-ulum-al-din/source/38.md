## On Meditation and Reflection

**1.** Meditation is the lamp of the heart, and reflection is its fuel. Without meditation, the heart is dark, and without reflection, the lamp has no flame.

**2.** God says: "Do they not reflect upon themselves? God did not create the heavens and the earth and what is between them except in truth and for a specified term."

**3.** The Prophet said: "An hour of reflection is better than a year of worship." This is because worship without reflection is routine, but reflection without worship is still a form of worship.

**4.** The subjects of reflection are four: the signs of God in creation, the attributes of God, the condition of the self, and the reality of death.

**5.** Reflect upon creation: the sun, the moon, the stars, the mountains, the seas, the animals, the plants. Each is a sign pointing to its Creator. The one who sees the signs without seeing the Signifier has missed the point.

**6.** Reflect upon the attributes of God: His mercy, His justice, His power, His knowledge. Each attribute reveals an aspect of the Infinite, and each aspect invites the heart to a deeper understanding.

**7.** Reflect upon yourself: your body, with its intricate design; your soul, with its capacity for good and evil; your life, with its brevity and its purpose. Each reflection brings you closer to the knowledge of your Creator.

**8.** Reflect upon death: its certainty, its nearness, its finality. The contemplation of death is the most powerful corrective for the heedless heart.

**9.** The quality of reflection matters more than the quantity. A few minutes of deep, sincere reflection are worth more than hours of shallow thinking.

**10.** Reflection should lead to action. The one who reflects and does not act is like the one who diagnoses his illness and does not take the medicine.

**11.** The heart that reflects regularly grows in wisdom as the tree that is watered regularly grows in height.

**12.** Set aside time each day for reflection. Sit in a quiet place, close your eyes, and contemplate the signs of God. Let your heart wander through the garden of creation and pick the flowers of understanding.

**13.** Meditation on the Quran is the highest form of reflection, for the Quran is the speech of God, and the contemplation of God's speech is the contemplation of God Himself.

**14.** Do not rush through the Quran. Stop at each verse and ask: "What does this mean? What does it demand of me? How does it change my understanding?"

**15.** The fruit of reflection is certainty, and certainty is the highest station of faith. The one who has reflected deeply on the signs of God does not merely believe—he knows.
