## On the Condemnation of the World

**1.** The world is a prison for the believer and a paradise for the disbeliever. This does not mean that the believer suffers more, but that the believer sees the world for what it is—a temporary dwelling.

**2.** The world is like a shadow: it lengthens and then it shrinks, it appears and then it vanishes. The one who chases the shadow chases what can never be caught.

**3.** God says: "The life of this world is nothing but play and diversion." Play is what occupies the child, and diversion is what distracts the traveler from his destination.

**4.** The condemnation of the world is not the condemnation of the earth, the sky, the rivers, and the trees—these are the signs of God. It is the condemnation of attachment to them.

**5.** The world itself is innocent. It is the heart's attachment that is guilty. The world is a bridge; cross it but do not build your house upon it.

**6.** A wise man said: "The world is like sea-water: the more you drink, the thirstier you become." This is the nature of worldly desire—it creates more desire, never satisfaction.

**7.** The Prophet said: "If the son of Adam had a valley full of gold, he would wish for a second valley. Nothing fills his eyes except dust."

**8.** The remedy for love of the world is the remembrance of death. He who remembers death often sees the world in its true light—a way-station, not a destination.

**9.** Visit the graveyard and consider: every tomb contains one who once walked as you walk, ate as you eat, and planned as you plan. Now they are dust.

**10.** The world deceives with three things: its beauty, its promise of permanence, and its illusion of control. All three are lies.

**11.** Its beauty fades. Its permanence is a mirage. And control is the greatest illusion of all, for no one controls even his next breath.

**12.** The wise man uses the world as a tool and the foolish man is used by it as a tool. The wise man rides the world; the foolish man is ridden by it.

**13.** Take from the world what you need, and leave the rest. The traveler carries provisions, not the entire market.

**14.** The best of people is the one who lives in the world but is not of it—who walks through the marketplace with his heart in the mosque.

**15.** The condemnation of the world is the liberation of the heart, for the heart that is free from the world is free for God.
