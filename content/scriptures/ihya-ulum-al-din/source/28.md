## On the Condemnation of Status and Ostentation

**1.** The love of status is a subtle poison that corrupts even the most devoted worshipper. Many a prayer has been ruined by the desire to be seen praying.

**2.** Ostentation is the showing of good deeds to gain the admiration of people. The Prophet called it "the lesser idolatry," for the ostentatious man worships the opinion of people alongside God.

**3.** The one who prays to be seen by people has made people his audience and forgotten that God is his only audience.

**4.** The Prophet said: "Shall I not tell you of what I fear for you more than the False Messiah? It is the hidden idolatry: that a man stands up to pray and beautifies his prayer because he sees another person looking at him."

**5.** The remedy for ostentation is to remember that God sees the intention behind the act. The act may be beautiful, but if the intention is corrupt, the act is worthless.

**6.** The love of status makes the scholar preach for applause rather than truth, the leader rule for admiration rather than justice, and the worshipper pray for reputation rather than God.

**7.** Status in the eyes of people is like the shadow: the more you chase it, the faster it runs. But if you turn your back on it and walk toward the sun, the shadow follows you.

**8.** The truly great are unknown, for they hide their virtues as carefully as others hide their vices.

**9.** A saint was asked: "Who is the best of men?" He replied: "The one whose goodness is hidden from others and whose faults are known only to himself."

**10.** The test of sincerity is this: would you perform the same act if no one were watching? If the answer is yes, the act is sincere. If the answer is no, there is ostentation.

**11.** The love of praise is natural, but the wise man does not act for the sake of praise. He acts for the sake of God, and if praise comes, he does not rejoice, and if blame comes, he does not despair.

**12.** The Prophet said: "The one who seeks knowledge to compete with the scholars, to argue with the foolish, or to attract the attention of people—God will cast him into the Fire."

**13.** Seek obscurity, for obscurity is the friend of sincerity. The flower that blooms in the wilderness is purer than the flower that blooms in the marketplace.

**14.** If you find yourself pleased by the praise of others, be alarmed, for the pleasure of praise is the first step toward the worship of people.

**15.** The cure for the love of status is the contemplation of death. In the grave, there is no status, no rank, no reputation. Only the deeds remain, stripped of all pretense.
