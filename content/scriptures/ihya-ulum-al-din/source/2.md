## The Foundations of Belief

**1.** The foundation of belief is the testimony that there is no god but God and that Muhammad is the Messenger of God. This is not merely a statement of the tongue but a condition of the heart.

**2.** True belief is like a tree whose roots are in the heart, whose trunk is the declaration of the tongue, and whose branches are the actions of the limbs.

**3.** If the root is firm, the tree stands. If the root is rotten, no amount of watering will save it.

**4.** The scholars have disputed concerning the nature of belief: is it knowledge, or action, or both? Know that belief is a light that God casts into the heart, and when the light is present, both knowledge and action follow naturally.

**5.** The heart that truly believes is like a lamp that has been lit—it does not need to be told to shine. It shines because its nature is light.

**6.** Doubt is not the enemy of belief—it is the testing ground of belief. The belief that has not been tested by doubt is untried and fragile.

**7.** The Prophet said: "Renew your faith." They asked: "How do we renew our faith?" He said: "By frequently saying: There is no god but God."

**8.** This renewal is necessary because the heart rusts as iron rusts, and the remembrance of God is the polish that removes the rust.

**9.** The foundation of belief is built upon three things: knowledge of God's existence, knowledge of His attributes, and knowledge of His acts.

**10.** As for knowledge of His existence, it is the knowledge that is imprinted upon every soul, for every soul was asked: "Am I not your Lord?" And every soul answered: "Yes."

**11.** The forgetting of this primordial testimony is the source of all disbelief, and the remembering of it is the source of all faith.

**12.** Belief is not a possession that, once acquired, can never be lost. It must be guarded, nourished, and renewed, like a fire that needs fuel.

**13.** The greatest threat to belief is not doubt from without but heedlessness from within. The heart that forgets God gradually hardens, and the hardened heart is the dead heart.

**14.** Guard your heart, O seeker, for it is the vessel of belief, and a cracked vessel cannot hold water.

**15.** The perfection of belief is to worship God as if you see Him, for though you do not see Him, He sees you. This is the station of excellence, and it is the fruit of the tree of belief.
