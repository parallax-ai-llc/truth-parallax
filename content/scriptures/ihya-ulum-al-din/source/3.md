## The Mysteries of Purity

**1.** Purity is the key to prayer, and prayer is the key to Paradise. Without purity, no prayer is accepted, and without prayer, no servant draws near to God.

**2.** There are four degrees of purity: the purification of the body from filth and impurities, the purification of the limbs from sins and transgressions, the purification of the heart from blameworthy traits, and the purification of the innermost self from everything other than God.

**3.** The first degree is the purity of the common people. The second is the purity of the elect. The third is the purity of the elect of the elect. The fourth is the purity of the prophets and the saints.

**4.** Most people stop at the first degree and think they have achieved purity. But washing the body while the heart is filthy is like painting the outside of a cup while the inside is full of poison.

**5.** The Prophet said: "Purity is half of faith." This means that removing what is harmful is as important as acquiring what is beneficial.

**6.** The water that purifies the body flows from the earth. The water that purifies the heart flows from heaven. The first is found in rivers and wells. The second is found in remembrance and reflection.

**7.** A man may wash himself a hundred times a day and still be impure if his heart harbors envy, pride, or malice. And a man may be outwardly dusty from travel and yet be purer than the moon.

**8.** The mystery of purity is that the outward act is a mirror of the inward state. When you wash your hands, intend to wash them of wrongdoing. When you wash your face, intend to turn it toward God.

**9.** The ablution before prayer is not mere ritual—it is the preparation of the body to stand in the presence of the King. Would you enter the court of a king without washing?

**10.** The purest of all purifications is the purification from the illusion of self. When the self is cleansed of its claim to independent existence, the servant stands before God in absolute purity.

**11.** This is the purity of the prophets: to be so empty of self that only God remains.

**12.** Strive, O seeker, for the inner purity as you strive for the outer. For God looks not at your outward form but at your heart.

**13.** The Prophet said: "God does not look at your forms or your wealth, but He looks at your hearts and your deeds."

**14.** Let your purity be layers, like the layers of an onion: peel away the outer layer of bodily impurity, then the layer of sinful actions, then the layer of blameworthy character, until you reach the innermost kernel, which is purity itself.

**15.** And know that purity is not a destination but a path—a continuous cleansing that ends only when the servant meets his Lord in perfect transparency.
