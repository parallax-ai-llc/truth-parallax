## On the Unity of God and Trust in Him

**1.** The Unity of God is the foundation upon which all else is built. There is no god but God—this is not merely a creed to be recited but a reality to be lived.

**2.** To live in the Unity of God means to see no other power, no other provider, no other protector. Whatever comes to you comes from Him. Whatever is taken from you is taken by Him.

**3.** Trust in God is the fruit of the knowledge of His Unity. The one who truly knows that there is no power but God's power has no reason to fear, no reason to worry, no reason to despair.

**4.** Trust is not the abandonment of effort—it is the abandonment of anxiety. The trusting man works hard but does not worry, for he knows that the outcome is in God's hands.

**5.** The Prophet said: "If you trusted God with a true trust, He would provide for you as He provides for the birds: they go out hungry in the morning and return full in the evening."

**6.** The birds do not sit in the nest and wait for food to come to them. They go out and search. But they do not worry, and they do not hoard. This is trust.

**7.** There are three degrees of trust: the trust of the common believer, which is to believe that God will provide; the trust of the elect, which is to be indifferent to provision; and the trust of the elect of the elect, which is to have no awareness of anything other than God.

**8.** The third degree is the station of the prophets and the greatest saints. At this station, the servant does not even think about provision, for his entire being is occupied with God.

**9.** Trust does not mean that you will not be tested. The birds are sometimes hungry, and the servants of God sometimes suffer. But the trusting soul knows that the suffering has a purpose.

**10.** God says: "Whoever trusts in God, He is sufficient for him." Sufficient—not that He will give you everything you want, but that He will give you everything you need.

**11.** The Unity of God means that there is only one Actor in the universe. The causes that we see are veils over the true Cause. Behind every event is the hand of God.

**12.** When you see causes and effects, see through them to the One who causes all causes. The wind blows, but it is God who makes it blow. The rain falls, but it is God who sends it down.

**13.** This seeing is the eye of Unity, and it transforms the world from a chaos of competing forces into a harmony of divine will.

**14.** Trust is the practical application of Unity. If there is truly only one God, then there is truly only one to trust, one to fear, one to love, and one to rely upon.

**15.** Rest in this knowledge, and your heart will find peace, for the heart that rests in God rests in the only resting place that does not shift beneath it.
