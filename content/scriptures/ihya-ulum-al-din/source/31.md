## On Patience and Gratitude

**1.** Patience and gratitude are the two wings of the believer. With patience he endures affliction, and with gratitude he receives blessing. Without either wing, the bird of faith cannot fly.

**2.** Patience is of three kinds: patience in obedience to God, patience in refraining from sin, and patience in enduring affliction. The highest is patience in obeying God when obedience is difficult.

**3.** The Prophet said: "How wonderful is the affair of the believer, for it is all good. If he is blessed with prosperity, he is grateful, and that is good for him. If he is afflicted with adversity, he is patient, and that is good for him."

**4.** Patience is not passive resignation—it is active trust. The patient man does not say: "I have given up." He says: "I trust that God has a purpose."

**5.** Gratitude is of three kinds: gratitude of the tongue, which is to praise God; gratitude of the heart, which is to recognize the blessing; and gratitude of the limbs, which is to use the blessing in obedience to God.

**6.** The truly grateful person is the one who is grateful not only for blessings but also for trials, for he sees that trials are blessings in disguise.

**7.** God says: "If you are grateful, I will increase you." Gratitude is the key to increase, and ingratitude is the key to decrease.

**8.** The patient man is tested by affliction. The grateful man is tested by blessing. Both tests are difficult, but the test of blessing is more subtle, for many fail to be grateful for what they have.

**9.** A sage was asked: "Which is harder—patience or gratitude?" He replied: "Gratitude, for the patient man knows he is being tested, but the grateful man often forgets that blessing is also a test."

**10.** Combine patience and gratitude, and you have the perfection of faith. Be patient when you lose, and grateful when you gain, and at peace in both states.

**11.** The Prophet was the most patient and the most grateful of men. In adversity, he said: "Praise be to God in every condition." In prosperity, he prostrated in gratitude.

**12.** Patience without gratitude becomes bitterness. Gratitude without patience becomes entitlement. The two must be balanced.

**13.** The patient man endures the present with trust. The grateful man receives the present with joy. Together they transform every moment into worship.

**14.** Do not complain, for complaint is the opposite of patience. And do not take blessings for granted, for heedlessness is the opposite of gratitude.

**15.** Patience is the armor of the soul, and gratitude is its wings. With armor and wings, the soul is invincible—protected from below and lifted from above.
