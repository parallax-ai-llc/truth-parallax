## On the Lawful and the Forbidden

**1.** The lawful is clear and the forbidden is clear, and between them are doubtful matters that many people do not know. He who guards against the doubtful has protected his religion and his honor.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Leave that which makes you doubt for that which does not make you doubt." When the heart is uneasy about a thing, it is a sign that the thing is doubtful.

**3.** The lawful nourishes the soul and brightens the heart. The forbidden corrupts the soul and darkens the heart. The effects may be invisible, but they are as real as the effects of medicine and poison on the body.

**4.** A morsel of forbidden food darkens the heart for forty days. This darkness is not visible to the eye, but it dims the light of spiritual perception.

**5.** The sources of the forbidden are three: taking the property of others without right, consuming what is harmful to the body or soul, and acquiring wealth through deception.

**6.** Do not think that the forbidden is limited to food and drink. Every transaction, every word, every glance has its lawful and its forbidden aspect.

**7.** The scrupulous man examines the source of his income as carefully as he examines the food he eats. For the money earned through injustice will be spent in injustice, and the cycle of harm continues.

**8.** When you do not know whether a thing is lawful or forbidden, consult your heart. The Prophet said: "Consult your heart, even if the scholars give you a ruling."

**9.** The heart of the believer is a compass that points toward the truth, provided it has not been corrupted by sin and heedlessness.

**10.** The scholars may disagree, but the heart, when it is pure, does not disagree. The pure heart knows the lawful by the expansion it feels and the forbidden by the constriction it feels.

**11.** Do not seek loopholes in the law, for the one who seeks loopholes has already decided to disobey and is looking for permission.

**12.** The purpose of the law is not to restrict you but to protect you. The fence around the garden protects the flowers; the law around the soul protects the spirit.

**13.** Be scrupulous in the small things, for the one who is careless in small things will be careless in great things.

**14.** The Prophet was the most scrupulous of men. A single date that fell from the public treasury he would not eat, lest it belong to the poor.

**15.** The perfection of scrupulousness is to reach the point where the heart recoils from the doubtful as naturally as the hand recoils from fire.
