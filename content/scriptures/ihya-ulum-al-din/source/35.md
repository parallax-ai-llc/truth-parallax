## On Love, Longing, and Contentment

**1.** Love is the highest station of the seeker and the ultimate goal of the spiritual journey. All other stations are preparations for love, and all other virtues are fruits of love.

**2.** The Prophet said: "None of you truly believes until God and His Messenger are more beloved to him than anything else."

**3.** Love of God is not an emotion—it is a state of the heart in which the Beloved occupies every chamber and every corner, leaving no room for anything else.

**4.** The sign of love is that the lover prefers the will of the Beloved over his own will, the pleasure of the Beloved over his own pleasure, and the presence of the Beloved over his own existence.

**5.** Longing is the burning desire of the lover for the Beloved in the Beloved's absence. The one who does not long has not loved, for longing is the proof of love.

**6.** The Prophet said: "O God, I ask You for the longing to meet You." Longing for God is itself a gift from God, for God does not give longing to those He does not intend to satisfy.

**7.** Contentment is the acceptance of whatever comes from the Beloved—joy or sorrow, ease or hardship—with equal serenity, knowing that whatever the Beloved does is good.

**8.** The lover who is content says: "If You give, I am grateful. If You withhold, I am patient. If You are present, I am joyful. If You are absent, I am longing. In every state, I am Yours."

**9.** The deepest love is the love that has no cause. The one who loves God for His blessings loves the blessings, not God. The one who loves God for Himself loves God.

**10.** A woman saint was asked: "Do you love God?" She said: "Yes." "Do you hate the devil?" She said: "My love of God leaves no room for hatred of anyone."

**11.** She was asked: "Do you love the Prophet?" She said: "Who does not love the Prophet? But my love of God has so consumed me that I have no love left for anyone else—not even for hating."

**12.** This is the pinnacle of love: when love has consumed the lover entirely, leaving nothing—no hatred, no desire, no self.

**13.** The stages of love are: inclination, attachment, devotion, intimacy, and annihilation. At each stage, the lover loses more of himself and gains more of the Beloved.

**14.** At the stage of annihilation, the lover no longer exists as a separate being. He is absorbed in the Beloved as the drop is absorbed in the ocean.

**15.** And yet, the paradox of divine love is that in losing everything, the lover finds everything. In dying to self, the lover is born to God. And in the union of lover and Beloved, all separation ends and all longing is fulfilled.
