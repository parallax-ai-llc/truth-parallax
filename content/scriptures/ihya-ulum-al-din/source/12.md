## On the Etiquette of Marriage

**1.** Marriage is half of religion, for the Prophet said: "When a servant of God marries, he has completed half of his religion; let him fear God in the remaining half."

**2.** The purpose of marriage is not merely the satisfaction of desire but the completion of the soul. Each spouse is a mirror for the other.

**3.** Choose a spouse for piety above all else, for beauty fades, wealth diminishes, and lineage is the relic of ancestors. But piety endures.

**4.** The best of you is the best to his family, and the Prophet was the best to his family. He helped with household tasks, mended his own garments, and treated his wives with gentleness.

**5.** Marriage is a covenant, and the terms of that covenant are kindness, patience, and mutual respect. The one who breaks this covenant has broken a promise to God.

**6.** Do not make your home a battlefield. The home should be a sanctuary, not a prison. When the husband and wife are at war, the children are the casualties.

**7.** The husband has duties to his wife, and the wife has duties to her husband, but the foundation of all duties is mercy. Without mercy, duty becomes tyranny.

**8.** The Prophet said: "The most complete of believers in faith are those who are best in character, and the best of you are those who are best to their wives."

**9.** Overlook the faults of your spouse, for you have faults of your own. The one who demands perfection from others while excusing himself is a hypocrite.

**10.** Do not reveal the secrets of your marriage to others, for the marriage bed is a trust, and the betrayal of trust is among the worst of sins.

**11.** The companionship of marriage is a form of worship. When the husband and wife sit together in remembrance of God, the angels surround them.

**12.** Be patient with your spouse, for patience in marriage is a greater act of devotion than patience in solitary worship.

**13.** The rights of the wife include provision, kind treatment, and the protection of her dignity. The rights of the husband include respect, cooperation, and faithfulness.

**14.** Marriage is a garden that must be tended daily. The garden that is neglected produces only weeds. The garden that is watered with love produces fruit in every season.

**15.** If difficulties arise in marriage, seek counsel from the wise, not from the foolish. Many a marriage has been saved by wisdom and destroyed by gossip.
