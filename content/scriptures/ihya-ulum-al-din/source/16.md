## On the Etiquette of Seclusion

**1.** Seclusion is the retreat of the heart from the distractions of the world so that it may attend to the business of the spirit.

**2.** The benefit of seclusion is that it frees the seeker from the demands of social life, which consume the hours and scatter the attention.

**3.** But seclusion has its dangers. The one who retreats without preparation may find himself alone with an enemy worse than any he has left behind—his own ego.

**4.** The Prophet withdrew to the cave of Hira before the revelation came to him. This teaches that seclusion is a preparation for illumination.

**5.** In seclusion, the faults of the self become visible, for there is no one to blame and no one to impress.

**6.** The seclusion of the body is valuable only if it is accompanied by the seclusion of the heart. The one who retreats to a cave but carries the marketplace in his heart has not truly secluded himself.

**7.** Set aside a portion of each day for seclusion with God. Even a few minutes of true withdrawal can refresh the soul as rain refreshes the earth.

**8.** In seclusion, speak to God as you would speak to a friend. Tell Him your sorrows, your fears, your hopes, and your failings. He already knows them, but the speaking itself is a form of healing.

**9.** The etiquette of seclusion includes choosing a clean and quiet place, facing the direction of prayer, and closing the eyes so that the outward sight gives way to the inward sight.

**10.** Do not extend your seclusion beyond your capacity, for excessive seclusion can lead to delusion. The balanced path alternates between seclusion and engagement with the world.

**11.** The best seclusion is internal seclusion—the ability to be alone with God even in the midst of a crowd.

**12.** The saint is the one who is in seclusion at all times, for his heart is always turned toward God, regardless of his outward circumstances.

**13.** The Prophet said: "The best of people is one who lives long and does good." This teaches that seclusion is not an end in itself but a means to better engagement with the world.

**14.** Return from your seclusion renewed and strengthened, and carry the light of your retreat into the darkness of the world.

**15.** Seclusion without service is selfishness. Service without seclusion is burnout. The wise man balances the two, drawing from the well of solitude to water the garden of community.
