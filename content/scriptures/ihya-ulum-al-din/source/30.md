## On the Condemnation of Self-Deception

**1.** Self-deception is the most dangerous of all deceptions, for it is invisible to the one who suffers from it. The liar knows he is lying; the self-deceived does not know he is deceiving himself.

**2.** The signs of self-deception are many: the man who commits sins while counting on God's mercy, the scholar who teaches but does not practice, the worshipper who prays for show.

**3.** The greatest self-deception is to think that God owes you something because of your worship. God owes nothing to anyone—His gifts are grace, not payment.

**4.** The self-deceived man looks at his own virtues and says: "I am saved." The sincere man looks at his own faults and says: "I am in danger."

**5.** The Prophet said: "The wise man is the one who takes account of himself and works for what comes after death. The fool is the one who follows his desires and then places his hopes in God."

**6.** Placing hopes in God without effort is not faith—it is laziness disguised as faith. And effort without placing hopes in God is not strength—it is arrogance disguised as self-reliance.

**7.** The self-deceived man compares himself to those who are worse than him and says: "I am better." The sincere man compares himself to those who are better and says: "I have far to go."

**8.** Self-deception makes the vice appear as virtue. The miser calls his miserliness "prudence." The coward calls his cowardice "caution." The lazy man calls his laziness "trust in God."

**9.** The remedy for self-deception is honest self-examination, carried out in the light of God's revelation, not in the dark of one's own opinions.

**10.** Ask a trusted friend to tell you your faults, for the friend can see what the mirror of self-regard cannot show.

**11.** The self-deceived man is like a sick man who thinks he is healthy—he does not seek the physician, and his disease worsens.

**12.** The first step toward healing is the admission of sickness. Say: "I may be deceiving myself," and you have already taken the first step.

**13.** The scholars said: "Beware of three things: the scholar who does not practice, the worshipper who is not sincere, and the ascetic who is not humble. These are the most self-deceived of people."

**14.** God does not look at your opinion of yourself—He looks at the reality of yourself. And the reality is often very different from the opinion.

**15.** Strip away the layers of self-deception, and what remains is the naked soul before God—trembling, humbled, and finally honest. This honesty is the beginning of salvation.
