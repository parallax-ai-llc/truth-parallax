## On Music and Ecstasy

**1.** The question of music and ecstasy has divided the scholars, but the truth lies in the state of the heart, not in the outward form of the melody.

**2.** Music is a mirror. If the heart is pure, music reflects purity. If the heart is corrupt, music reflects corruption. The same music can elevate one soul and degrade another.

**3.** The remembrance assemblies of the Sufis, where the Name of God is chanted with melody and rhythm, can produce states of ecstasy that open doors to the unseen world.

**4.** But ecstasy that is manufactured is not the same as ecstasy that descends. The one who forces ecstasy upon himself is like a man who forces tears—the tears are real, but the sorrow is not.

**5.** True ecstasy is a gift from God that arrives uninvited and overwhelms the heart with a joy that has no cause in the visible world.

**6.** David sang the Psalms, and the mountains sang with him. This is the original music—the praise of God that moves the entire creation to dance.

**7.** The whirling of the Sufis is the whirling of the planets around the sun, the whirling of the electrons around the nucleus, the whirling of the heart around its Lord. All creation is in a state of whirl.

**8.** The one who whirls with awareness is a worshipper. The one who whirls without awareness is a top. The difference is in the intention, not in the movement.

**9.** Listening to beautiful recitation of the Quran is the highest form of musical worship, for it combines the beauty of melody with the truth of divine speech.

**10.** The heart has ears that the body does not have. There are sounds that only the heart can hear—the sound of the Name of God reverberating through creation.

**11.** When the heart hears this sound, it enters a state beyond description. The body may tremble, the eyes may weep, the limbs may move—but these are effects, not the cause.

**12.** Do not judge the one who is in a state of ecstasy, for you do not know what he hears. The sober man who has never been drunk cannot judge the drunkard.

**13.** But beware of false ecstasy, which is the imitation of a state one has not attained. The false ecstatic is a liar in the guise of a lover.

**14.** The criterion is this: does the music draw you closer to God or further from Him? If closer, it is lawful for you. If further, it is forbidden.

**15.** The melody that awakens the sleeping heart is a mercy. The melody that puts the wakeful heart to sleep is a trial. Know which is which, and choose accordingly.
