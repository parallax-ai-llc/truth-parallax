## On the Evils of the Tongue

**1.** The tongue is the smallest organ in the body, but it is the source of the greatest sins. It is easier to control a wild horse than to control the tongue.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Most of what takes people to the Fire is the tongue." For the tongue can commit sins that the hand cannot reach.

**3.** The evils of the tongue are many: lying, backbiting, slander, gossip, flattery, arguing, cursing, jesting excessively, and revealing secrets.

**4.** Lying is the root of all evils of the tongue. The liar weaves a web that eventually traps him. The Prophet said: "Truthfulness leads to righteousness, and righteousness leads to Paradise."

**5.** Backbiting is to speak of your brother in a way that he would dislike, even if what you say is true. If it is false, it is slander, which is worse.

**6.** God says: "Would any of you like to eat the flesh of his dead brother?" Backbiting is compared to eating the flesh of the dead because the absent person cannot defend himself.

**7.** Gossip is the carrying of tales from one person to another with the intent to cause discord. The gossip-monger is the arsonist of human relationships.

**8.** Flattery corrupts both the flatterer and the flattered. The flatterer lies to gain favor, and the flattered believes the lie and is ruined by his own vanity.

**9.** Arguing for the sake of argument is a disease of the ego. The one who argues to prove himself right has already lost, for he has placed his ego above the truth.

**10.** The remedy for the evils of the tongue is silence. The one who is silent is safe from the majority of sins.

**11.** The Prophet said: "Whoever believes in God and the Last Day, let him speak good or remain silent." This is the criterion: if what you are about to say is good, say it. If not, be silent.

**12.** Before speaking, ask yourself three questions: Is it true? Is it necessary? Is it kind? If the answer to any of these is no, do not speak.

**13.** The tongue is a key: it opens the doors of Paradise and the doors of the Fire. Choose carefully which door you wish to open.

**14.** The wise man speaks little and means much. The fool speaks much and means little. Be among those whose words are few and whose meaning is vast.

**15.** Guard your tongue as you guard a loaded weapon, for the damage it can do is as swift, as irreversible, and as devastating.
