## The Mysteries of Fasting

**1.** Fasting is the shield of the believer and the gate to the garden of self-discipline. Through fasting, the servant breaks the tyranny of the body over the spirit.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Fasting is for Me, and I shall reward it Myself." This means that the reward of fasting is too great to be measured by the scales of ordinary reckoning.

**3.** There are three degrees of fasting: the fasting of the common people, which is to abstain from food and drink; the fasting of the elect, which is to abstain from sin; and the fasting of the elect of the elect, which is to abstain from everything other than God.

**4.** The one who abstains from food but fills his eyes with forbidden sights, his ears with gossip, and his tongue with backbiting has not truly fasted. He has merely gone hungry.

**5.** The mystery of fasting is that it weakens the animal soul and strengthens the spiritual soul. When the belly is empty, the heart is full. When the belly is full, the heart is empty.

**6.** Fasting teaches the rich what the poor feel every day. It is a school of empathy that words cannot provide.

**7.** The Prophet said: "When one of you is fasting, let him not behave indecently or make a commotion. If someone insults him, let him say: I am fasting."

**8.** Fasting is training for patience, and patience is the foundation of all virtues. The one who can restrain his appetite can restrain his anger, his lust, and his greed.

**9.** The breaking of the fast is itself a spiritual act, for it teaches gratitude. The one who has been hungry knows the value of bread. The one who has been thirsty knows the value of water.

**10.** Do not overeat when breaking the fast, for that defeats the purpose. The belly that is emptied in the morning and stuffed in the evening has learned nothing.

**11.** The true fast does not end at sunset—it continues in the heart. The heart that fasts from envy, malice, and worldly desire is fasting even when the body eats.

**12.** God does not need your hunger. He needs your awareness. Fasting without awareness is a diet, not a devotion.

**13.** The fasting person is in the state of the angels, for the angels do not eat or drink. For a few hours each day, the human being rises to the angelic station through self-restraint.

**14.** Fasting in Ramadan is the collective fast of the community, and in this collective practice lies a power that individual fasting cannot achieve—the power of shared sacrifice.

**15.** The perfection of fasting is to reach the point where you no longer desire what you have given up, and this desirelessness is itself the reward.
