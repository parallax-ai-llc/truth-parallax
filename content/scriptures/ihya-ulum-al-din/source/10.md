## On the Arrangement of Daily Devotions

**1.** The arrangement of daily devotions is the architecture of the spiritual life. As the body needs regular meals, the soul needs regular nourishment.

**2.** The day should be divided into portions: a portion for the obligatory prayers, a portion for the recitation of the Quran, a portion for reflection, a portion for earning a livelihood, and a portion for rest.

**3.** The night should not be wasted entirely in sleep. The latter part of the night is the most blessed time, when God descends to the lowest heaven and asks: "Is there anyone who calls upon Me, that I may answer him?"

**4.** The Prophet used to stand in prayer in the latter part of the night until his feet swelled. He divided the night into thirds: one third for prayer, one third for sleep, and one third for the needs of his community.

**5.** Begin the day with the remembrance of God, for the first words upon waking set the tone for the entire day. Let the first thing upon your tongue be "In the Name of God."

**6.** End the day with an accounting of the self. Before sleep, review your actions: what did you do well? What did you do badly? What must you repent of? What must you continue?

**7.** This nightly accounting is like the merchant's ledger. The merchant who does not balance his books daily will find himself bankrupt. So too the servant who does not examine his soul daily.

**8.** Set aside time for solitude, for the soul that is always in company has no time for reflection, and the soul that does not reflect does not grow.

**9.** But do not make solitude an excuse for laziness. The devotion of solitude must be balanced by the devotion of service. The hermit who serves no one serves only his own ego.

**10.** The daily devotions should not be performed mechanically, for mechanical devotion is the shell without the kernel. Each act of worship should be performed as if it were the first time—with freshness, with wonder, with the awareness that you are standing before the Infinite.

**11.** Do not overwhelm yourself with devotions beyond your capacity, for the Prophet said: "Take on only as much as you can sustain." The best devotion is that which is consistent, even if it is small.

**12.** A small act performed daily with sincerity is better than a grand act performed once with show.

**13.** The rhythm of daily devotion is the heartbeat of the spiritual life. When the heartbeat stops, the life stops. When the devotion stops, the spirit grows cold.

**14.** Arrange your devotions as you arrange your garden: with variety, with beauty, with attention to the seasons of the soul.

**15.** And know that the purpose of all devotion is not to earn God's favor—you already have it—but to make yourself aware of the favor you have always possessed.
