## On the Conduct of Life and the Character of Prophethood

**1.** The character of the Prophet, peace be upon him, is the model for all humanity. God said: "You are upon a magnificent character."

**2.** He was gentle without being weak, firm without being harsh, generous without being wasteful, and humble without being servile.

**3.** He never struck anyone with his hand—not a woman, not a servant, not a child. His strength was in his restraint, not in his force.

**4.** When wronged, he did not seek revenge. When insulted, he prayed for the insulter. When harmed, he forgave.

**5.** He said: "I was sent only to perfect good character." This means that the purpose of religion is not ritual alone but the refinement of the human being.

**6.** His humor was gentle and truthful. He joked but never lied. He smiled often and laughed rarely—but when he laughed, his face shone like the moon.

**7.** He sat with the poor and ate with the slaves. He mended his own shoes and patched his own garments. No task was beneath him.

**8.** When he entered a gathering, he sat wherever there was space, without seeking the place of honor. He was the most honored man in the room, but he behaved as if he were the least.

**9.** He listened to everyone who spoke to him and gave each person his full attention. No one who spoke to him ever felt ignored.

**10.** His generosity knew no bounds. He never said "no" to a request. If he had nothing to give, he would promise, and his promise was as good as gold.

**11.** He said: "The strong man is not the one who can wrestle others to the ground. The strong man is the one who controls himself when angry."

**12.** His anger was reserved for injustice, never for personal insult. When God's law was violated, he stood firm. When his own person was insulted, he was silent.

**13.** He was the bravest of men in battle and the most compassionate in victory. He forgave the people of Mecca who had driven him out and persecuted his followers.

**14.** To follow the Prophet is not merely to imitate his outward actions but to cultivate his inward character: his patience, his mercy, his humility, and his trust in God.

**15.** The conduct of life is the application of character to circumstance. The man of good character responds to every situation with wisdom, patience, and mercy—and this is the Prophetic way.
