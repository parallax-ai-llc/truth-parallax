## On the Last Things

**1.** The Last Things are the matters that await every soul after death: the questioning in the grave, the resurrection, the gathering, the reckoning, the bridge, and the final abode.

**2.** The questioning in the grave is the first trial. The angels ask: "Who is your Lord? What is your religion? Who is your Prophet?" The one who lived upon these answers will answer with ease. The one who merely memorized them will stammer.

**3.** The resurrection is the re-gathering of the scattered parts of every human being who ever lived. God says: "Does man think that We cannot assemble his bones? Indeed, We are able to put together in perfect order the very tips of his fingers."

**4.** On the Day of Gathering, every soul will stand before God, naked, barefoot, and uncircumcised, as on the day of birth. No one will have an advocate except his deeds.

**5.** The reckoning is the weighing of deeds on the divine scale. The scale is real, and the deeds have weight. A single act of sincerity may outweigh a mountain of ritual.

**6.** The bridge extends over the abyss, and every soul must cross it. For the righteous, it is wide and easy. For the sinful, it is narrow as a razor and sharp as a sword.

**7.** Paradise is the abode of peace, where the servants of God dwell in the presence of their Lord. No eye has seen, no ear has heard, and no heart has imagined what God has prepared for those who love Him.

**8.** The greatest joy of Paradise is not the gardens, the rivers, or the palaces—it is the vision of God's Face. The Prophet said: "You will see your Lord as you see the full moon on a clear night."

**9.** The Fire is the abode of regret, where the heedless soul faces the consequences of its heedlessness. But even the Fire is a manifestation of God's justice, and justice is an attribute of perfection.

**10.** Do not be so afraid of the Fire that you forget to love Paradise. And do not be so enamored of Paradise that you forget the One who created it.

**11.** The wise man does not worship God for the sake of Paradise or out of fear of the Fire—he worships God because God is worthy of worship. This is the worship of the free, not the worship of the slave.

**12.** A woman saint said: "O God, if I worship You for fear of the Fire, burn me in the Fire. If I worship You in hope of Paradise, deny me Paradise. But if I worship You for Your own sake, do not deny me Your eternal Beauty."

**13.** This is the perfection of the spiritual life: to worship God for God, to love God for God, and to seek God for God—not for what He gives but for who He is.

**14.** The Ihya—the Revival of the Religious Sciences—is written for this purpose: to revive the dead heart, to awaken the sleeping soul, and to guide the wandering seeker back to the path.

**15.** And the path ends where it began: with the testimony that there is no god but God. This is the first word and the last word, the beginning and the end, the alpha and the omega of all knowledge.

**16.** May God have mercy upon the author and the reader, the teacher and the student, and all who seek Him with sincere hearts. And praise be to God, the Lord of all the worlds.
