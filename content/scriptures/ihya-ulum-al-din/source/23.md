## On Breaking the Two Desires: Appetite and Lust

**1.** The two desires—appetite for food and appetite for physical pleasure—are the strongest chains that bind the soul to the earth.

**2.** These desires are not evil in themselves, for God created them for the preservation of the individual and the species. But when they are uncontrolled, they become the tyrants of the soul.

**3.** The one who is enslaved by his appetite eats without necessity, drinks without thirst, and indulges without limit. His belly is his god, and his god is the most unworthy of gods.

**4.** The Prophet said: "The son of Adam fills no vessel worse than his stomach." The full belly is the enemy of the wakeful heart.

**5.** The remedy for the appetite is fasting—not the fasting that merely delays eating until sunset, but the fasting that teaches the soul to be content with less.

**6.** Eat to sustain your worship, not to satisfy your greed. The body needs far less than the soul imagines.

**7.** As for the second desire, it is lawful within the bounds of marriage and forbidden outside it. The one who channels this desire through lawful means has sanctified it; the one who follows it without restraint has been destroyed by it.

**8.** The remedy for illicit desire is to lower the gaze, to guard the heart, to fast, and to occupy oneself with worship and useful work. The idle soul is the playground of temptation.

**9.** Know that every indulgence of the lower desires weakens the will, and every act of restraint strengthens it. The will is a muscle that grows with exercise and atrophies with neglect.

**10.** The saints who have conquered these two desires have freed themselves from the heaviest chains. They walk the earth lightly, unburdened by the weight of craving.

**11.** Do not think that the breaking of desire means the destruction of pleasure. On the contrary, the one who has broken the tyranny of desire discovers a higher pleasure—the pleasure of the spirit in the presence of God.

**12.** The physical pleasures are shadows of the spiritual pleasures, as the reflection of the moon in water is a shadow of the real moon.

**13.** The one who is content with the reflection will never look up to see the real moon. But the one who looks up discovers that the real moon is infinitely more beautiful.

**14.** Break the chains of appetite, and you will fly. Keep them, and you will crawl. The choice is yours, but the consequences are certain.

**15.** The Prophet said: "Paradise is surrounded by hardships, and the Fire is surrounded by desires." This means that the path to the highest is through the discipline of the lowest.
