## On Commanding the Good and Forbidding the Evil

**1.** Commanding the good and forbidding the evil is the pillar of the religion and the mission of every prophet.

**2.** God says: "You are the best nation raised up for mankind—you command the good and forbid the evil and believe in God."

**3.** The one who sees an evil and does not oppose it—with his hand, with his tongue, or at least with his heart—has the weakest of faith.

**4.** But commanding the good requires wisdom. The one who commands without wisdom does more harm than good, for he turns people away from the truth rather than toward it.

**5.** The first duty is to command yourself before commanding others. The one who does not practice what he preaches is a hypocrite, and the hypocrite's preaching is poison.

**6.** The Prophet said: "A man will be brought on the Day of Judgment and thrown into the Fire, and his intestines will spill out, and he will go round them as a donkey goes round a millstone. The inhabitants of the Fire will say: 'What is wrong with you?' He will reply: 'I used to command the good and not do it, and forbid evil and do it.'"

**7.** Be gentle in your commanding, for gentleness achieves what harshness cannot. The Prophet said: "God is gentle and loves gentleness, and He gives for gentleness what He does not give for harshness."

**8.** Do not be harsh with the sinner, for harshness drives people away. Be firm in the truth but soft in the delivery.

**9.** The one who commands the good out of arrogance—believing himself better than the sinner—has fallen into a sin worse than the sin he is opposing.

**10.** Know the right time and the right place. The wise physician administers the medicine when the patient can receive it, not when the physician feels like giving it.

**11.** Do not expose the faults of others in public. Advise privately, and if private advice fails, then increase in prayer for the person.

**12.** The purpose of commanding the good is reform, not humiliation. If your method produces humiliation without reform, your method is wrong.

**13.** Be patient with those who reject your counsel, for the prophets themselves were rejected, and they did not abandon their mission.

**14.** The greatest form of commanding the good is your own example. The life that embodies the truth speaks louder than a thousand sermons.

**15.** And remember: you are not responsible for the outcome—only for the effort. The guidance belongs to God, and the effort belongs to you.
