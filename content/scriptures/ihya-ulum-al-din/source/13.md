## On the Etiquette of Earning a Livelihood

**1.** Earning a livelihood is an obligation upon the believer, for the Prophet said: "The truthful and trustworthy merchant will be with the prophets and the righteous on the Day of Judgment."

**2.** Do not think that the pursuit of livelihood is separate from the pursuit of God. The one who earns his bread honestly is engaged in worship.

**3.** The hand that labors honestly is more beloved to God than the hand that begs. The Prophet kissed the hand of a laborer and said: "This is a hand that God and His Messenger love."

**4.** Honesty in trade is the foundation of livelihood. The merchant who cheats has poisoned his own sustenance, for the wealth acquired by fraud nourishes the body but poisons the soul.

**5.** Give full measure and full weight. Do not diminish the rights of people, and do not spread corruption in the land.

**6.** The greedy man earns much and enjoys little. The contented man earns little and enjoys much. It is not the amount but the blessing that matters.

**7.** Trust in God does not mean abandoning effort. The Prophet said: "Tie your camel and then trust in God." Effort is the outward form; trust is the inward reality.

**8.** Do not be so absorbed in your livelihood that you forget your prayers. The livelihood is for the body, and prayer is for the soul. Do not feed the servant and starve the master.

**9.** Trade with integrity, for your reputation is worth more than your profit. The merchant who is trusted earns more in the long run than the merchant who cheats.

**10.** Do not hoard wealth beyond your needs. Wealth that is hoarded is wealth that is wasted, for it benefits neither the owner nor the poor.

**11.** The best livelihood is that which suffices for your needs without excess, leaving you free for worship and service.

**12.** Be generous in your dealings. The seller who is easy-going and the buyer who is accommodating are beloved by God.

**13.** Do not envy the success of others, for your sustenance has already been decreed. What is yours will come to you even if the world opposes it.

**14.** Work with your hands, for the Prophet said: "No one has ever eaten food better than that earned by the work of his own hands."

**15.** And remember that all sustenance comes from God. You are the means, not the source. The farmer plants, but God grows. The merchant trades, but God provides.
