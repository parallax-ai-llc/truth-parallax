## On Invocations and Supplications

**1.** Invocation is the remembrance of God with the tongue, and supplication is the calling upon God with the heart. Both are forms of prayer, and both draw the servant near to his Lord.

**2.** God says: "Remember Me, and I will remember you." The servant's remembrance of God is a response to God's remembrance of the servant, for God remembered us before we remembered Him.

**3.** The best invocation is "There is no god but God," for it negates all false gods and affirms the One True God in a single breath.

**4.** The invocation of the tongue without the invocation of the heart is like the movement of the millstone without grain—it turns but produces nothing.

**5.** But do not abandon the invocation of the tongue simply because the heart is absent, for the tongue's invocation may eventually awaken the heart, as a knock at the door may eventually rouse the sleeper.

**6.** The Prophet said: "Shall I not tell you of the best of your deeds, the purest before your Lord, the highest in your degrees, and better for you than spending gold and silver?" They said: "Yes." He said: "The remembrance of God."

**7.** Make your invocation constant—in walking, sitting, lying down, and rising. Let the tongue never be idle when it can be remembering God.

**8.** The stages of invocation are three: the invocation of the tongue, the invocation of the heart, and the invocation of the spirit. In the first, the tongue moves and the heart may or may not attend. In the second, the heart remembers without the tongue. In the third, the spirit is absorbed in the Remembered, and both tongue and heart are silenced.

**9.** Supplication is the weapon of the believer. The one who supplicates has acknowledged his weakness and God's strength, and this acknowledgment is itself the answer to the supplication.

**10.** God says: "Call upon Me, and I will answer you." The answer may not come in the form you expect, but it always comes. Sometimes the answer is the thing you asked for; sometimes it is something better; sometimes it is the prevention of something worse.

**11.** Do not be discouraged if the answer is delayed, for the delay itself may be the answer. God's timing is not your timing.

**12.** The etiquette of supplication includes beginning with praise of God, sending blessings upon the Prophet, confessing one's sins, and then presenting one's need with humility.

**13.** The supplication of the oppressed is answered, even if the oppressed is a disbeliever, for injustice is hateful to God regardless of who suffers it.

**14.** Supplicate for others before yourself, for the angels say: "And the same for you." The one who prays for his brother in his absence is himself prayed for.

**15.** The highest invocation is silence—the silence of the heart that has been so filled with God that words are no longer necessary.
