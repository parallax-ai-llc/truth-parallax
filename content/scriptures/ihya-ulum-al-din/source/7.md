## The Mysteries of Pilgrimage

**1.** The pilgrimage to the House of God is an obligation upon every Muslim who has the means. But the outward journey to Mecca is a mirror of the inward journey to the heart.

**2.** When you leave your home, intend that you are leaving the home of the ego. When you travel toward Mecca, intend that you are traveling toward God.

**3.** The donning of the white garment of ihram signifies the stripping away of all worldly distinctions. In ihram, the king and the beggar are equal, for both wear the same cloth.

**4.** This is the teaching of the pilgrimage: that before God, all are equal. The only distinction is piety, and piety is invisible to the eye.

**5.** The circumambulation of the Kaaba is the orbit of the heart around its Lord. The planets orbit the sun, and the hearts orbit God—and the Kaaba is the visible center of this invisible orbit.

**6.** The running between Safa and Marwa is the running of the seeker between hope and fear. Hagar ran between the two hills seeking water for her son, and in her desperation, God opened the well of Zamzam.

**7.** The standing at Arafat is the standing before God on the Day of Judgment. It is a rehearsal for the final accounting, performed in this life so that the servant may prepare for the next.

**8.** The stoning of the pillars is the casting away of the whispers of the devil—the inner voices that tempt the soul away from God.

**9.** The sacrifice of the animal is the sacrifice of the animal nature within the self. Abraham was willing to sacrifice his son, and God accepted a ram in his place. The outward sacrifice is a symbol of the inward surrender.

**10.** The mystery of the pilgrimage is that it is a death before death. The pilgrim leaves behind his name, his rank, his possessions, and his identity. He becomes a number in a sea of white-clad figures.

**11.** In this dissolution of identity, the pilgrim finds something greater than identity—he finds unity with the community of believers and, through them, unity with God.

**12.** Many go to Mecca and return unchanged. These have performed the outward rites but have missed the inward meanings.

**13.** The true pilgrim returns as a newborn, purified of sin, humble in spirit, and grateful for every breath.

**14.** The Prophet said: "The accepted pilgrimage has no reward except Paradise." This is because the accepted pilgrimage transforms the pilgrim entirely.

**15.** Know that the Kaaba is a house of stone, and the heart is the true house of God. If you have purified the house of your heart, you have performed the truest pilgrimage, even if you have never set foot in Mecca.
