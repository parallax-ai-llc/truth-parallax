## On Poverty and Renunciation

**1.** Poverty in the spiritual sense is not the absence of wealth—it is the absence of attachment to wealth. The truly poor man before God is the one who needs nothing but God.

**2.** The Prophet said: "Poverty is my pride." This is not a glorification of material destitution but a declaration of spiritual freedom.

**3.** The rich man who is attached to his wealth is poorer than the beggar who has nothing, for the rich man is enslaved by what he possesses, while the beggar is free.

**4.** Renunciation is not the rejection of the world—it is the freeing of the heart from the world. The renunciant may possess wealth, but the wealth does not possess him.

**5.** The Prophet said: "Renunciation of the world does not mean forbidding what is lawful or wasting wealth. Renunciation is that you do not trust what is in your hand more than what is in God's hand."

**6.** The heart of the renunciant is like an open hand: things come into it and leave it freely. The heart of the attached is like a closed fist: it grips whatever it holds and will not let go.

**7.** The test of renunciation is loss. When something is taken from you, does your heart constrict or remain at peace? If it constricts, there was attachment. If it remains at peace, there was freedom.

**8.** Poverty before God means to stand before Him empty-handed, acknowledging that everything you have is His and everything you need is from Him.

**9.** This is the most dignified of all stations, for the one who is poor before God is rich in God. And the one who is rich in God needs nothing from creation.

**10.** The great saints chose poverty not because they could not earn but because they did not want earning to distract them from the Earner.

**11.** Renunciation is the first step on the journey. The heart that is heavy with the world cannot ascend. The balloon rises only when the ballast is thrown overboard.

**12.** Do not boast of your poverty, for that is a form of pride. And do not boast of your renunciation, for that is a form of attachment—attachment to the image of oneself as a renunciant.

**13.** The true renunciant is hidden. He does not announce his poverty or display his detachment. He lives in the world as everyone else does, but his heart is elsewhere.

**14.** The Prophet said: "Be in this world as if you were a stranger or a traveler." The traveler takes what he needs and moves on. He does not build a mansion at the roadside inn.

**15.** The perfection of poverty is to need nothing, to want nothing, and to be nothing—and in that nothingness, to find the Everything that is God.
