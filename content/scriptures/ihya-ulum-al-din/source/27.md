## On the Condemnation of Miserliness and Greed

**1.** Miserliness is the hoarding of what should be given, and greed is the pursuit of what should be left. Both are diseases of the soul that close the hand and harden the heart.

**2.** The miser is the enemy of himself, for he deprives himself of the joy of giving, which is greater than the joy of receiving.

**3.** The Prophet said: "Every day two angels descend: one says, 'O God, give the generous man a replacement,' and the other says, 'O God, give the miser destruction.'"

**4.** The miser saves for tomorrow, but tomorrow may never come. And even if it comes, the miser will save for the next tomorrow, and so on until death arrives and the savings pass to the heirs.

**5.** The heirs spend in a day what the miser hoarded in a lifetime. And the miser is called to account for what he withheld, while the heirs enjoy what they did not earn.

**6.** Greed is the insatiable hunger for more. It is the child of fear—the fear that what you have is not enough, the fear that the world will run out, the fear that God will not provide.

**7.** The greedy man is like the one who drinks salt water: the more he drinks, the thirstier he becomes, until the thirst kills him.

**8.** The remedy for miserliness is to give, even when it is difficult. Start small: give a morsel of food, a kind word, a smile. Each act of giving loosens the grip of miserliness.

**9.** The remedy for greed is contentment. Be satisfied with what God has given you, and the fire of greed will die down.

**10.** The Prophet said: "Richness is not in abundance of possessions—richness is the contentment of the soul."

**11.** The generous man sleeps with peace, for he has nothing to guard. The miser sleeps with anxiety, for he fears the thief.

**12.** Consider: you brought nothing into this world and you will take nothing out. Whatever you have is a loan from God. Use it well, and it will be credited to your account.

**13.** The miser thinks he is saving, but he is losing. The generous man thinks he is losing, but he is saving. The economy of God is the inverse of the economy of the world.

**14.** Do not wait until you are wealthy to be generous. The poor man who gives from his little gives more than the rich man who gives from his surplus.

**15.** And know that the ultimate miserliness is the withholding of the self from God—the hoarding of the heart, the guarding of the ego, the refusal to surrender. Give yourself to God, and all other giving becomes easy.
