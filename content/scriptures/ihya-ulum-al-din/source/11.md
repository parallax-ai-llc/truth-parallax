## On the Etiquette of Eating

**1.** Eating is not merely the filling of the belly—it is the sustaining of the body for the worship of God. The one who eats mindlessly eats like the beast; the one who eats mindfully eats like the saint.

**2.** Begin every meal in the name of God and end it with praise. Between the beginning and the end, eat with moderation.

**3.** The Prophet said: "The son of Adam fills no vessel worse than his belly. A few morsels are enough to keep his back straight. If he must eat more, then a third for food, a third for drink, and a third for air."

**4.** Eat to live; do not live to eat. The belly is a servant, not a master. When the belly becomes the master, the spirit becomes the slave.

**5.** Eat together, not alone, for the blessing of food is multiplied by companionship and diminished by solitude.

**6.** Do not criticize food that is placed before you. If you like it, eat it. If you do not, leave it without comment. The Prophet never criticized any food.

**7.** The etiquette of eating is the etiquette of gratitude. Every morsel is a gift from God, processed by a thousand hands before it reaches your plate.

**8.** Consider the journey of the bread: from the seed in the earth, to the rain from the sky, to the farmer's labor, to the baker's oven, to your table. In every morsel is a universe of effort and grace.

**9.** Do not waste food, for waste is a form of ingratitude. The one who wastes bread has not understood what bread is.

**10.** Share your food with the hungry before satisfying yourself. The meal that is shared is blessed; the meal that is hoarded is cursed.

**11.** The Prophet ate sitting on the ground, on a simple mat, and he never ate reclining. This posture of humility while eating teaches that the servant is never above the gift.

**12.** Fast regularly, for fasting teaches you the true value of food. The one who has never been hungry does not know what eating means.

**13.** The food of the saints is little, and their gratitude is much. The food of the heedless is much, and their gratitude is little.

**14.** When you are full, stop. The desire to eat beyond fullness is the desire of the ego, not the body. Learn to distinguish between the two.

**15.** The final etiquette of eating is to rise from the table with the intention to use the energy you have received in the service of God and His creatures.
