## On the Condemnation of Pride and Vanity

**1.** Pride is the mother of all vices and the veil that separates the soul from God. The proud man says "I" when he should say "He."

**2.** The Prophet said: "No one who has an atom's weight of pride in his heart will enter Paradise." An atom's weight—that is how destructive pride is.

**3.** Pride is of two kinds: the pride that rejects the truth and the pride that looks down upon people. Both are forms of blindness.

**4.** The one who rejects the truth out of pride has chosen darkness over light because the light came from a source he considers beneath him.

**5.** The one who looks down upon people has forgotten that all people are made from the same clay and will return to the same earth.

**6.** Iblis was the first to sin through pride. He said: "I am better than he—You created me from fire and created him from clay." This single act of pride led to eternal exile.

**7.** The remedy for pride is humility, and humility is the recognition of one's true condition before God: that you are a creature, dependent, needy, and mortal.

**8.** When pride arises, remember your origin—a drop of fluid that you would wash off your hand in disgust. From this, you claim superiority?

**9.** And remember your end—a body in the earth, consumed by worms, returning to dust. From this, you claim permanence?

**10.** Vanity is the mirror image of pride: it is the admiration of oneself, the preening of the ego. The vain man is in love with himself, and this is the most barren of all loves.

**11.** The Prophet was the greatest of men, yet he said: "Do not elevate me above what I am." He forbade excessive praise and seated himself among the poor.

**12.** The humble man is like water: it flows to the lowest place and gives life to everything it touches. The proud man is like fire: it rises upward and consumes everything in its path.

**13.** Consider: what do you have that you did not receive? Your intelligence, your beauty, your strength, your wealth—all are gifts from God. How can you be proud of what was given to you?

**14.** The truly humble man does not think less of himself—he thinks of himself less. His attention is on God and on the service of God's creation, not on the inflation of his own importance.

**15.** O seeker, crush the idol of pride, for as long as it stands in the heart, the light of God cannot enter. The door of heaven is low, and only those who bow their heads may pass through.
