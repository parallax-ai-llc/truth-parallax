## The Book of Knowledge

**1.** In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. Praise be to God whose glory silences the eloquent, whose blessings are beyond numbering by the counters, and whose reality defeats the most determined seekers.

**2.** Know that the pursuit of knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim, male and female. But the knowledge that is obligatory is not the knowledge of words—it is the knowledge that leads to the purification of the heart.

**3.** The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Seek knowledge, even unto China." This means that no distance is too great in the pursuit of that which illuminates the soul.

**4.** Knowledge is of two kinds: the knowledge of the tongue, which is God's evidence against the children of Adam, and the knowledge of the heart, which is the beneficial knowledge.

**5.** The knowledge of the heart is like a lamp that shines in the darkness of the breast, and by its light the servant distinguishes truth from falsehood.

**6.** Many a scholar carries knowledge as a mule carries books—burdened by the weight but nourished by none of it.

**7.** The scholar who does not act upon his knowledge is like a candle that gives light to others but burns itself away.

**8.** The first obligation of the seeker of knowledge is sincerity. If the intention is corrupt, the knowledge gained will be a curse, not a blessing.

**9.** Knowledge sought for the sake of debate and disputation leads to the hardening of the heart. Knowledge sought for the sake of God leads to its softening.

**10.** The sign of beneficial knowledge is that it increases humility, not pride. If your knowledge makes you arrogant, it is a poison disguised as a remedy.

**11.** The Prophet said: "The worst of scholars is he who visits princes, and the best of princes is he who visits scholars."

**12.** Beware of knowledge that occupies the tongue but does not reach the heart, for such knowledge is a deception of the self.

**13.** The scholar who practices what he knows is like a farmer who sows his field and reaps the harvest. The scholar who does not practice is like a farmer who counts the seeds and stores them away.

**14.** The door of knowledge is the acknowledgment of ignorance. He who thinks he knows has closed the door; he who knows he does not know has opened it.

**15.** Know, O seeker, that the knowledge of God is the highest knowledge, and all other knowledge is valuable only insofar as it leads to Him.
