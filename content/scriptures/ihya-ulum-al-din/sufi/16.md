Sufi interpretation of Ihya Ulum al-Din book 16, "On the Etiquette of Seclusion," reads Ghazali's teaching as a guide to the inner dimensions of religious life. For the Sufi reader, this book reveals the spiritual reality (haqiqa) that underlies the external form (shari'a) of Islamic practice. Ghazali's genius lies in showing that the outer and inner are not opposed but complementary — that the full realization of religious life requires attention to both the body's actions and the heart's states.

The spiritual psychology presented in this book draws on centuries of Sufi experience and teaching. Ghazali synthesizes the insights of earlier masters into a systematic framework that maps the diseases of the soul, the stages of purification, and the virtues that emerge when the heart is polished to reflect divine light. The Sufi reader finds here both practical guidance for the spiritual journey and theoretical understanding of the soul's transformation.
