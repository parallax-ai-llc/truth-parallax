## Overview

Marsanes is one of the most damaged and difficult texts in the Nag Hammadi library, with much of its content lost or illegible. What survives reveals a unique combination of Sethian Gnostic theology, Platonic metaphysics, and Greek grammatical theory. The text's interest in the mystical properties of vowels and consonants has been compared to the Pythagorean tradition of sacred numbers, to the Egyptian and Greek traditions of vocal magic (the use of vowel strings in magical papyri), and to the Sefer Yetzirah's theory of creation through Hebrew letters.
