## Overview

Marsanes addresses one of the most fascinating questions in the philosophy of religion: is language merely a human tool, or does it participate in the structure of reality? The widespread cross-cultural practices of mantra recitation (Hinduism, Buddhism), sacred name invocation (Judaism, Islam, Christianity), and vocal magic (shamanic traditions) all suggest that human beings across cultures have experienced language as something more than conventional — as a medium through which the divine structure of reality can be accessed and manipulated.
