## Overview

For Christian readers, Marsanes's concern with the mystical power of names and letters resonates with the Christian theology of the Word (Logos). The Gospel of John's assertion that 'In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God' (John 1:1) affirms that language — specifically the divine Word — is not merely human but cosmic and creative. While Marsanes develops this intuition in a very different direction (through Greek grammatical theory rather than incarnational theology), the underlying conviction that language participates in divine reality is shared.
