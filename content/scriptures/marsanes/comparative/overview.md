## Overview

Marsanes's theory of the mystical power of letters connects to one of the most widespread cross-cultural intuitions: that language and sound participate in the structure of reality itself. The Hindu concept of sacred sound (shabda-brahman) and the power of Sanskrit mantras, the Jewish Kabbalistic tradition of creation through the Hebrew letters (Sefer Yetzirah), the Islamic science of letters ('ilm al-huruf), the Pythagorean tradition of the music of the spheres, and the Tibetan Buddhist practice of mantra recitation all affirm that sound, language, and naming are not arbitrary human conventions but reflections of cosmic order.
