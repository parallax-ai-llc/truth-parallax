## Overview

Jewish readers find in Marsanes the closest Gnostic parallel to the Jewish mystical tradition of the power of Hebrew letters. The Sefer Yetzirah (Book of Formation) describes God creating the world through the twenty-two Hebrew letters and the ten sefirot — a theory that combines linguistics with cosmogony in a way strikingly similar to Marsanes's own approach. The Merkabah tradition's use of divine names as instruments of ascent and power also parallels Marsanes's interest in the relationship between language and divine reality.
