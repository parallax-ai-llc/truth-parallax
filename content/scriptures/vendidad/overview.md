## Overview

The Vendidad (Avestan: *vī-daēvō-dāta*, "the law rejecting the demons"), also known as the Vidēvdād, is a Zoroastrian legal and ritual text consisting of 22 chapters (*fargards*). It is the only complete *nask* (book) surviving from the original Sasanian-era Avestan canon. The Vendidad contains purity laws, cosmogonic narratives, ritual prescriptions for dealing with contamination (especially from death), and mythological accounts, all framed as a dialogue between Ahura Mazda and Zarathustra.

## Structure

The Vendidad's 22 fargards fall into several thematic sections:

1. **Mythological Prologue** (Fargards 1-2): The creation of the sixteen lands by Ahura Mazda and the counter-creations of Angra Mainyu; the reign of Yima and the vara (enclosure) protecting creation
2. **Purity and Agricultural Law** (Fargards 3-4): The sanctity of the earth, agricultural duties, and contractual law
3. **Death and Purification** (Fargards 5-12): Extensive regulations regarding corpse pollution (*nasu*), funerary practices, the dakhma (tower of silence), and purification ceremonies including the Barashnûm
4. **Sacred Animals and Gender Purity** (Fargards 13-16): Laws regarding the dog, the otter, and purity regulations for men and women
5. **Miscellaneous Laws and Mythology** (Fargards 17-22): Rules for hair and nails, the cock and dawn, Zarathustra's temptation by Angra Mainyu, healing arts, and demonology

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Zoroastrian**: The foundational legal code governing purity, funerary practice, and the priestly combat against demonic contamination
- **Academic**: A rich but complex source for Zoroastrian social history, ritual practice, and mythological traditions, though debated regarding dating and layers
- **Comparative**: Parallels with Levitical purity laws, Hindu dharmashastra, and other ancient Near Eastern legal-ritual codes
- **Protestant**: Of interest for understanding purity-law religion and the contrast with grace-based theology, as well as demonology parallels
- **Jewish**: Striking parallels with Leviticus and Numbers regarding corpse contamination, purification rituals, and the sacred-profane boundary
- **Islamic**: Context for understanding Zoroastrian ritual culture encountered during the Islamic conquest of Persia and the *tahara* (purity) discourse

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Purity and Pollution | Detailed laws governing contamination, especially from death, and the rituals of purification |
| Cosmogony | The creation of the world by Ahura Mazda and the counter-creations of Angra Mainyu |
| Demonology | The identification and combat of demons (*daevas*) through ritual, prayer, and sacred formulas |
| Funerary Law | Regulations for the disposal of the dead and the dakhma system |
| Sacred Animals | The sanctity of the dog and other creatures as allies against evil |
| Temptation Narrative | Zarathustra's confrontation with Angra Mainyu (Fargard 19) |
| Healing | The origins of medicine and the role of the healer |
