---
title: "Vendidad Chapter 13"
scripture: "vendidad"
chapter: 13
---

**1** O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! Which is the dog that belongs to the Righteous Creation?

**2** Ahura Mazda answered: It is the dog called Vanghapara, which evil-speaking people call Zairimyanura, the yellow-eared. It goes along the roads watching for the thief and the wolf.

**3** O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! Which is the dog that receives the protection of the good law?

**4** Ahura Mazda answered: It is the dog Vohunazga, the house-dog. No house could subsist on the earth made by Ahura, but for the house-dog and the shepherd's dog.

**5** O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! If a man shall strike a shepherd's dog, what is the penalty?

**6** Ahura Mazda answered: Eight hundred stripes with the Aspahe-astra, eight hundred stripes with the Sraosho-charana.

**7** O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! If a man shall strike a house-dog, what is the penalty?

**8** Ahura Mazda answered: Seven hundred stripes with the Aspahe-astra, seven hundred stripes with the Sraosho-charana.

**9** If a man gives bad food to a shepherd's dog, so that it becomes ill, he is guilty of the same crime as if he had given bad food to a righteous man.

**10** The dog is the lord of the night. He watches over the house by night. He drives the thief and the wolf from the fold. No one shall harm the dog, for the dog is the creation of Ahura Mazda.

**11** Whoever kills a shepherd's dog or a house-dog, his soul when passing to the other world shall howl louder and more sorely than the sheep does in the forest when the wolf ranges.

**12** No soul will come and meet his departing soul and help it through the accountings in the other world; nor will the dogs that guard the Chinvat bridge help him.

**13** He who gives food to a shepherd's dog has done as much good as if he had given food to a righteous man. He who gives bones to a dog has done as much good as if he had given a meal to a priest.
