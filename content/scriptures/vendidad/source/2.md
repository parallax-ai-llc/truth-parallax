---
title: "Vendidad Chapter 2 - The Legend of Yima"
scripture: "vendidad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Zarathushtra asked Ahura Mazda: O Ahura Mazda, most beneficent Spirit, Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! Who was the first mortal, before myself, Zarathushtra, with whom thou, Ahura Mazda, didst converse, whom thou didst teach the Religion of Ahura, the Religion of Zarathushtra?

**2** Ahura Mazda answered: The fair Yima, the good shepherd, O holy Zarathushtra! he was the first mortal, before thee, Zarathushtra, with whom I, Ahura Mazda, did converse, whom I taught the Religion of Ahura, the Religion of Zarathushtra.

**3** Unto him, O Zarathushtra, I, Ahura Mazda, spake, saying: "Well, fair Yima, son of Vivanghat, be thou the preacher and the bearer of my Religion!" And the fair Yima, O Zarathushtra, answered me, saying: "I was not born, I was not taught to be the preacher and the bearer of thy Religion."

**4** Then I, Ahura Mazda, said thus unto him, O Zarathushtra: "Since thou dost not consent to be the preacher and the bearer of my Religion, then make thou my world increase, make my world grow: consent thou to nourish, to rule, and to watch over my world."

**5** And the fair Yima, O Zarathushtra, answered me saying: "Yes! I will make thy world increase, I will make thy world grow. Yes! I will nourish, and rule, and watch over thy world. There shall be, while I am king, neither cold wind nor hot wind, neither disease nor death."

**6** And I, Ahura Mazda, brought to him two implements: a golden seal and a poniard inlaid with gold. Behold, here Yima bears the royal sway!

**7** Thus, under the sway of Yima, three hundred winters passed away, and the earth was replenished with flocks and herds, with men and dogs and birds and with red blazing fires, and there was room no more for flocks, herds, and men.

**8** Then I warned the fair Yima, saying: "O fair Yima, son of Vivanghat! The earth has become full of flocks and herds, of men and dogs and birds and of red blazing fires, and there is room no more for flocks, herds, and men."

**9** Then Yima stepped forward, in light, southwards, on the way of the sun, and pressed the earth with the golden seal, and bored it with the poniard, speaking thus: "O Spenta Armaiti, kindly open asunder and stretch thyself afar, to bear flocks and herds and men."

**10** And Yima made the earth grow larger by one-third than it was before, and there came flocks and herds and men, at his will and wish, as many as he wished.

**11** Thus, under the sway of Yima, six hundred winters passed away, and the earth was replenished with flocks and herds, with men and dogs and birds. And Yima again made the earth grow larger by one-third.

**12** Thus, under the sway of Yima, nine hundred winters passed away, and the earth was replenished. And Yima again made the earth grow larger by one-third.

**13** Then the Maker, Ahura Mazda, called together a meeting of the celestial Yazads in the Airyana Vaejah of good creation; the fair Yima, the good shepherd, called together a meeting of the best of the mortals, in the Airyana Vaejah of good creation.

**14** To that meeting came Ahura Mazda, in the Airyana Vaejah of good creation; to that meeting came the fair Yima, the good shepherd, in the Airyana Vaejah of good creation.

**15** And Ahura Mazda spake unto Yima, saying: "O fair Yima, son of Vivanghat! Upon the material world the fatal winters are going to fall, that shall bring the fierce, foul frost; upon the material world the fatal winters are going to fall, that shall make snow-flakes fall thick, even an aredvi deep on the highest tops of mountains.

**16** "And all the three sorts of beasts shall perish, those that live in the wilderness, and those that live on the tops of the mountains, and those that live in the bosom of the dale, under the shelter of stables.

**17** "Therefore make thee a Vara, long as a riding-ground on every side of the square, and thither bring the seeds of sheep and oxen, of men, of dogs, of birds, and of red blazing fires.

**18** "Therefore make thee a Vara, long as a riding-ground on every side of the square, for a dwelling-place for men; a Vara, long as a riding-ground on every side of the square, for oxen and sheep.

**19** "There thou shalt make waters flow in a bed a hathra long; there thou shalt settle birds, by the ever-green banks that bear never-failing food. There thou shalt establish dwelling-places, consisting of a house with a balcony, a courtyard, and a gallery.

**20** "Thither thou shalt bring the seeds of men and women, of the greatest, best, and finest kinds on this earth; thither thou shalt bring the seeds of every kind of cattle, of the greatest, best, and finest kinds on this earth.

**21** "Thither thou shalt bring the seeds of every kind of tree, of the greatest, best, and finest kinds on this earth; thither thou shalt bring the seeds of every kind of fruit, the fullest of food and sweetest of odour.

**22** "All those seeds shalt thou bring, two of every kind, to be kept inexhaustible there, so long as those men shall stay in the Vara.

**23** "There shall be no humpbacked, none bulged forward there; no impotent, no lunatic, no poverty, no lying, no meanness, no jealousy, no decayed tooth, no leprous to be confined, nor any of the brands wherewith Angra Mainyu stamps the bodies of mortals."

**24** And Yima did as Ahura Mazda wished; he made the Vara, and brought thither the seeds of all beings, and there these beings live the happiest of lives.
