## Overview

From a comparative perspective, Vendidad Fargard 15 (Purity of Men and Women) offers significant material for cross-traditional analysis. This chapter, situated within the Vendidad's laws regarding sacred animals and gender purity, contains themes and motifs that resonate across religious boundaries — from the Vedic tradition with which Zoroastrianism shares Indo-Iranian roots, to the Abrahamic traditions that developed in historical proximity to Persian religion.

The themes of gender and purity addressed in this chapter find parallels in multiple traditions, raising questions about common human religious experience, shared cultural heritage, and possible historical transmission of ideas.

## Key Themes

- **Indo-Iranian parallels**: Cognate concepts, rituals, and theological structures shared with the Vedic tradition
- **Abrahamic resonances**: Thematic parallels with Jewish, Christian, and Islamic texts and practices
- **Universal patterns**: Motifs of gender that appear across diverse religious traditions

## Key Verses

The chapter's approximately 54 verses contain material particularly suited to comparative analysis, especially passages addressing the relationship between the divine and the human, the nature of gender, and the ritual or ethical response demanded of the faithful.
