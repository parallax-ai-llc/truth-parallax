## Overview

Comparative study of the Vendidad yields some of the most striking parallels between Zoroastrianism and other religious traditions. Its purity laws parallel Levitical legislation in remarkable detail; its cosmogonic narratives echo Near Eastern and Indo-European creation myths; its demonology parallels systems across cultures; and its eschatological passages (particularly Fargard 19) have been studied as possible sources for Abrahamic apocalypticism.

## Key Comparative Themes

### Levitical Parallels
The Vendidad's laws regarding corpse contamination, purification through water and sacred substances, prescribed periods of impurity, and the sacred-profane boundary parallel Leviticus and Numbers with striking specificity. Whether this reflects common Near Eastern heritage, direct influence, or parallel development remains debated.

### Creation Narratives
The creation of the sixteen lands (Fargard 1) and the Yima narrative (Fargard 2) parallel Genesis, Mesopotamian creation myths, and Vedic cosmogony. Yima's vara (enclosure) has been compared to Noah's ark.

### Demonology
The Vendidad's systematic demonology — Angra Mainyu, the daevas, and demons like Nasu (putrefaction) and Bushyasta (sloth) — parallels the developing demonology of Second Temple Judaism, early Christianity, and Islamic jinn traditions.

### Sacred Animals
The Vendidad's reverence for the dog parallels sacred animal traditions across cultures and stands in contrast to attitudes in some other Near Eastern traditions.
