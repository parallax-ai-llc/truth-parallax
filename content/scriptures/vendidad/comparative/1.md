## Overview

From a comparative perspective, Vendidad Fargard 1 (The Creation of the Lands) offers significant material for cross-traditional analysis. This chapter, situated within the mythological prologue of the Vendidad, containing cosmogonic narratives, contains themes and motifs that resonate across religious boundaries — from the Vedic tradition with which Zoroastrianism shares Indo-Iranian roots, to the Abrahamic traditions that developed in historical proximity to Persian religion.

The themes of creation and lands addressed in this chapter find parallels in multiple traditions, raising questions about common human religious experience, shared cultural heritage, and possible historical transmission of ideas.

## Key Themes

- **Indo-Iranian parallels**: Cognate concepts, rituals, and theological structures shared with the Vedic tradition
- **Abrahamic resonances**: Thematic parallels with Jewish, Christian, and Islamic texts and practices
- **Universal patterns**: Motifs of creation that appear across diverse religious traditions

## Key Verses

The chapter's approximately 20 verses contain material particularly suited to comparative analysis, especially passages addressing the relationship between the divine and the human, the nature of creation, and the ritual or ethical response demanded of the faithful.

## Commentary Tradition

Comparative scholarship on this chapter draws on the work of scholars like **Mircea Eliade**, **Mary Boyce**, and **Michael Stausberg**, who have situated Zoroastrian texts within the broader history of religions. The Indo-Iranian comparisons pioneered by **Georges Dumézil** and his school remain influential.
