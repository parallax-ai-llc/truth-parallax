## Overview

Jewish scholarly engagement with the Vendidad is of particular significance because of the extensive parallels between Zoroastrian and Levitical purity laws. The Vendidad's regulations regarding corpse contamination, purification rituals, and the sacred-profane boundary have been compared to Leviticus and Numbers since the 19th century, raising fundamental questions about shared heritage, parallel development, or possible Persian influence on Jewish law during the Second Temple period.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### Corpse Contamination Parallels
The Vendidad's treatment of corpse pollution (*nasu*) parallels Numbers 19's red heifer purification and Levitical impurity laws with remarkable specificity. Both traditions prescribe graded periods of impurity, purification through sacred substances (haoma/cow urine vs. ashes of the red heifer), and priestly oversight.

### The Yima-Noah Parallel
Yima's vara (Fargard 2) — an enclosed structure built to preserve the seed of all living things from a catastrophic winter — parallels the Noah narrative. Whether this reflects common Near Eastern tradition or specific literary influence has been debated.

### Demonology and Angelology
The Vendidad's systematic demonology parallels the developing demonology of Second Temple Judaism, including texts like 1 Enoch and the Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs, composed during the period of Persian cultural influence.

### Eschatological Framework
Fargard 19's eschatological passages — including the resurrection of the dead and the final defeat of evil — parallel the developing Jewish eschatology of Daniel and the later apocalyptic literature.
