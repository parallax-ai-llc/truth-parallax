## Overview

Academic study of the Vendidad presents both extraordinary opportunities and significant challenges. As the only complete *nask* surviving from the original 21-nask Avestan canon, it provides unparalleled evidence for Zoroastrian law, ritual, social structure, and mythology. However, scholars have long recognized that the Vendidad is a composite text containing material from widely different periods, from possibly very ancient mythological traditions to late Sasanian-era legal rulings.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Compositional History
The Vendidad's fargards vary dramatically in style, language, and content. Scholars distinguish between the archaic mythological chapters (1-2, 19), the detailed purity legislation (5-12), and miscellaneous legal-ritual material. **Jean Kellens** and **Prods Oktor Skjærvø** have analyzed the linguistic layers.

### Legal and Social History
The Vendidad provides rich evidence for ancient Iranian social structure, including references to contracts, penalties, priestly hierarchies, gender roles, and the treatment of animals. **Albert de Jong** has studied these in the context of Iranian religious history.

### Comparative Legal Studies
The Vendidad's purity laws invite comparison with Levitical legislation, Hindu dharmashastra, and Mesopotamian legal codes. Such comparisons illuminate both shared ancient Near Eastern/Indo-European patterns and distinctive Zoroastrian features.

### Mythological Analysis
The Yima narrative (Fargard 2) and the temptation of Zarathustra (Fargard 19) are studied as major mythological texts with parallels across Indo-European traditions.
