## Overview

Islamic engagement with the Vendidad is shaped by the historical encounter between Islam and Zoroastrian ritual culture following the conquest of Persia. The Vendidad's purity laws, demonology, and funerary practices were among the most visible aspects of Zoroastrian life that Muslim jurists, theologians, and historians confronted. The text provides essential context for understanding both the Islamic critique of Zoroastrian practice and the areas of convergence between the two traditions.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### Purity Law Comparison (Tahara)
The Vendidad's purity system invites comparison with Islamic *tahara* (ritual purity). Both traditions require purification after contact with specific pollutants, prescribe ablution (*wudu*/padyab), and understand purity as a prerequisite for worship. However, the Vendidad's system is far more elaborate regarding death pollution.

### Funerary Practice
The Vendidad's prescription of exposure in the dakhma (tower of silence) — avoiding burial, cremation, or water disposal — contrasts sharply with Islamic burial practice. Muslim jurists criticized the dakhma system, and it became one of the most contentious aspects of Zoroastrian-Muslim interaction.

### Demonology and Jinn
The Vendidad's demonology (daevas, drug, nasu) has been compared to Islamic jinn and shayatin traditions. Some scholars have suggested that certain Islamic demonological concepts may have been influenced by Persian Zoroastrian traditions.

### Al-Biruni's Documentation
The great Muslim scholar al-Biruni (973-1048 CE) documented Zoroastrian purity practices derived from the Vendidad with remarkable accuracy and scholarly objectivity, providing an invaluable external witness to the text's practical application.
