## Overview

Within the living Zoroastrian tradition, the Vendidad is the foundational legal and ritual code governing purity, funerary practice, and the priestly combat against demonic contamination. Its 22 fargards provide the authoritative framework for understanding pollution (*rimani*), purification (*barashnûm*), and the proper treatment of the dead. The Vendidad is the only Avestan text recited in its entirety during a specific ritual — the Vendidad ceremony, performed overnight as a powerful act of spiritual cleansing.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Purity System
The Vendidad's elaborate purity laws govern every aspect of contact with dead matter (*nasu* — the demon of putrefaction). Traditional Zoroastrian interpretation understands pollution not merely as physical but as spiritual contamination requiring ritual remedy.

### The Dakhma System
The Vendidad prescribes exposure of the dead in the dakhma (tower of silence) rather than burial or cremation, to avoid contaminating the sacred elements of earth, fire, and water. This practice, central to traditional Zoroastrianism, is understood as a cosmic duty.

### Mythological Authority
The Vendidad's cosmogonic and mythological sections (the creation of the lands, the reign of Yima, Zarathustra's temptation) provide the traditional narrative framework for understanding sacred history.
