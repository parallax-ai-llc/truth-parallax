## Overview

Protestant engagement with the Vendidad has historically focused on several key areas: the contrast between purity-law religion and grace-based theology, the Vendidad's demonology in relation to biblical spiritual warfare, and the striking parallels between the temptation of Zarathustra (Fargard 19) and the temptation of Christ. Protestant scholars have also examined the Vendidad as evidence for the religious context in which Cyrus the Great's decree liberated the Jews from Babylon.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### Law and Grace
The Vendidad's detailed purity legislation — with its prescribed penalties, purification rituals, and ritual taboos — invites comparison with Protestant theology's understanding of the law-gospel distinction. Just as the Reformation critiqued the legalism of late medieval Catholicism, Protestant scholars have examined the Vendidad as an example of religious legalism, while also recognizing the theological depth of the purity concept.

### The Temptation Narrative
Fargard 19's account of Angra Mainyu tempting Zarathustra with worldly dominion in exchange for abandoning the good religion presents a remarkable parallel to the temptation of Christ (Matthew 4, Luke 4). Protestant scholars have debated whether this reflects common mythological patterns, Zoroastrian influence on the Gospels, or independent prophetic experience.

### Demonology and Spiritual Warfare
The Vendidad's systematic identification and combat of demons through prayer and ritual has been compared to Ephesians 6's "armor of God" and the broader Protestant understanding of spiritual warfare.
