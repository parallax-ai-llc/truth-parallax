**Hexagram 15: Qian — Modesty**

☷☶

**The Judgment:** Qian indicates progress and success. The superior man, being modest, carries things through to completion.

**The Image:** Within the earth, a mountain. Thus the superior man reduces that which is too much, and augments that which is too little. He weighs things and makes them equal.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** A superior man modest about his modesty may cross the great water. Good fortune.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** Modesty that comes to expression. Perseverance brings good fortune.

**Line 3 (Nine in the Third Place):** A superior man of modesty and merit carries things to conclusion. Good fortune.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** Nothing that would not further modesty in movement.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** No boasting of wealth before one's neighbour. It is favourable to attack with force. Nothing that would not further.

**Line 6 (Top Six):** Modesty that comes to expression. It is favourable to set armies marching to chastise one's own city and one's own country.
