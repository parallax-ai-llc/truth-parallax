**Hexagram 38: Kui — Opposition**

☲☱

**The Judgment:** Kui indicates that, notwithstanding the condition of things which it denotes, in small matters there will still be good success.

**The Image:** Above, fire; below, the lake. Thus amid all fellowship the superior man retains his individuality.

**Line 1 (Initial Nine):** Remorse disappears. If you lose your horse, do not run after it; it will come back of its own accord. When you see evil people, guard yourself against mistakes.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** One meets his lord in a narrow street. No blame.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** One sees the wagon dragged back, the oxen halted, a man's hair and nose cut off. Not a good beginning, but a good end.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** Isolated through opposition, one meets a like-minded man with whom one can associate in good faith. Despite the danger, no blame.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** Remorse disappears. The companion bites his way through the wrappings. If one goes to him, how could it be a mistake?

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** Isolated through opposition, one sees one's companion as a pig covered with dirt, as a wagon full of devils. First one draws a bow against him, then one lays the bow aside. He is not a robber; he will woo at the proper time. As one goes, rain falls; then good fortune comes.
