**Hexagram 26: Da Chu — The Taming Power of the Great**

☶☰

**The Judgment:** Da Chu indicates that, under the conditions which it supposes, it will be advantageous to be firm and correct. If its subject do not seek to enjoy his revenues in his own family without taking service at court, there will be good fortune. It will be advantageous to cross the great stream.

**The Image:** Heaven within the mountain. Thus the superior man acquaints himself with many sayings of antiquity and many deeds of the past, in order to strengthen his character thereby.

**Line 1 (Initial Nine):** Danger is at hand. It furthers one to desist.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** The axletrees are taken from the wagon.

**Line 3 (Nine in the Third Place):** A good horse that follows others. Awareness of danger, with perseverance, furthers. Practise chariot driving and armed defence daily. It furthers one to have somewhere to go.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** The headboard of a young bull. Great good fortune.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** The tusk of a gelded boar. Good fortune.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** One attains the way of heaven. Success.
