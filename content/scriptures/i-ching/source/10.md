**Hexagram 10: Lu — Treading/Conduct**

☰☱

**The Judgment:** Lu suggests the idea of one treading on the tail of a tiger, which does not bite him. There will be progress and success.

**The Image:** Heaven above, the lake below. Thus the superior man discriminates between high and low, and thereby fortifies the thinking of the people.

**Line 1 (Initial Nine):** Simple conduct. Progress without blame.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** Treading a smooth, level course. The perseverance of a dark man brings good fortune.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** A one-eyed man is able to see, a lame man is able to tread. He treads on the tail of the tiger. The tiger bites the man. Misfortune. Thus does a warrior act on behalf of his great prince.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** He treads on the tail of the tiger. Caution and circumspection lead ultimately to good fortune.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** Resolute conduct. Perseverance with awareness of danger.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** Look to your conduct and weigh the favourable signs. When everything is fulfilled, supreme good fortune comes.
