**Hexagram 7: Shi — The Army**

☷☵

**The Judgment:** Shi indicates how, in the case which it supposes, with firmness and correctness, and a leader of age and experience, there will be good fortune and no error.

**The Image:** In the middle of the earth is water. Thus the superior man increases his masses by generosity toward the people.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** An army must set forth in proper order. If the order is not good, misfortune threatens.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** He is in the midst of the army. Good fortune. No blame. The king bestows a triple decoration.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** Perchance the army carries corpses in the wagon. Misfortune.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** The army retreats. No blame.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** There is game in the field. It furthers one to catch it. Without blame. Let the eldest lead the army. The younger transports corpses; then perseverance brings misfortune.

**Line 6 (Top Six):** The great prince issues commands, founds states, vests families with fiefs. Inferior people should not be employed.
