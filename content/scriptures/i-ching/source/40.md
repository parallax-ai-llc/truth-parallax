**Hexagram 40: Xie — Deliverance**

☳☵

**The Judgment:** Xie intimates that the south-west will be advantageous. If no further undertaking is necessary, returning brings good fortune. If there is something yet to be done, hastening brings good fortune.

**The Image:** Thunder and rain set in. Thus the superior man pardons mistakes and forgives misdeeds.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** Without blame.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** One kills three foxes in the field and receives a yellow arrow. Perseverance brings good fortune.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** If a man carries a burden on his back and nonetheless rides in a carriage, he thereby encourages robbers to draw near. Perseverance leads to humiliation.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** Deliver yourself from your great toe. Then the companion comes, and him you can trust.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** If only the superior man can deliver himself, it brings good fortune. Thus he proves to inferior men that he is in earnest.

**Line 6 (Top Six):** The prince shoots at a hawk on a high wall. He kills it. Everything serves to further.
