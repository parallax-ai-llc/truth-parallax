**Hexagram 56: Lu — The Wanderer**

☲☶

**The Judgment:** Lu intimates that in the condition which it denotes there may be some little attainment and progress. If the stranger or traveller be firm and correct as he ought to be, there will be good fortune.

**The Image:** Fire on the mountain. Thus the superior man is clear-minded and cautious in imposing penalties, and protracts no lawsuits.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** If the wanderer busies himself with trivial things, he draws down misfortune upon himself.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** The wanderer comes to an inn. He has his property with him. He wins the steadfastness of a young servant.

**Line 3 (Nine in the Third Place):** The wanderer's inn burns down. He loses the steadfastness of his young servant. Danger.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** The wanderer rests in a shelter. He obtains his property and an axe. My heart is not glad.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** He shoots a pheasant. It drops with the first arrow. In the end this brings both praise and office.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** The bird's nest burns up. The wanderer laughs at first, then must needs lament and weep. Through carelessness he loses his cow. Misfortune.
