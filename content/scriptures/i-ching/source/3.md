**Hexagram 3: Zhun — Difficulty at the Beginning**

☵☳

**The Judgment:** Zhun intimates that, in the case which it presupposes, there will be great progress and success, and the advantage will come from being correct and firm. Any movement in advance should not be lightly undertaken. There will be advantage in appointing feudal princes.

**The Image:** Clouds and thunder represent Zhun. Thus the superior man, in accordance with this, adjusts his measures of government as in sorting the threads of the warp and woof.

**Line 1 (Initial Nine):** Hesitation and hindrance. It will be advantageous to abide firm and correct; advantageous also to be made a feudal prince.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** Difficulty piles on difficulty. Horse and wagon part. He is not a robber; he will woo at the proper time. The maiden is chaste, she does not pledge herself. Ten years, then she pledges herself.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** Whoever hunts deer without the forester only loses his way in the forest. The superior man understands the signs of the time and prefers to desist. To go on brings humiliation.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** Horse and wagon part. Strive for union. To go brings good fortune. Everything acts to further.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** Difficulties in blessing. A little perseverance brings good fortune. Great perseverance brings misfortune.

**Line 6 (Top Six):** Horse and wagon part. Bloody tears flow.
