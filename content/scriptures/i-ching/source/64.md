**Hexagram 64: Wei Ji — Before Completion**

☲☵

**The Judgment:** Wei Ji intimates progress and success in the circumstances which it denotes. We see a young fox that has nearly crossed the stream, when its tail gets immersed. There will be no advantage in any way.

**The Image:** Fire over water: the image of the condition before transition. Thus the superior man is careful in the differentiation of things, so that each finds its place.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** He gets his tail in the water. Humiliating.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** He brakes his wheels. Perseverance brings good fortune.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** Before completion, attack brings misfortune. It furthers one to cross the great water.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** Perseverance brings good fortune. Remorse disappears. Shock, thus to discipline the Devil's Country. For three years, great realms are awarded.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** Perseverance brings good fortune. No remorse. The light of the superior man is true. Good fortune.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** There is drinking of wine in genuine confidence. No blame. But if one wets his head, he loses it, in truth.
