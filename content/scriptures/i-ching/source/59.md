**Hexagram 59: Huan — Dispersion/Dissolution**

☴☵

**The Judgment:** Huan intimates that, under its conditions, there will be progress and success. The king goes to his ancestral temple; and it will be advantageous to cross the great stream. It will be advantageous to be firm and correct.

**The Image:** The wind drives over the water. Thus the kings of old sacrificed to the Lord and built temples.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** He brings help with the strength of a horse. Good fortune.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** At the dissolution he hurries to that which supports him. Remorse disappears.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** He dissolves his self. No remorse.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** He dissolves his bond with his group. Supreme good fortune. Dispersion leads in turn to accumulation. This is something that ordinary men do not think of.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** His loud cries are as dissolving as sweat. Dissolution! A king abides without blame.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** He dissolves his blood. Departing, keeping at a distance, going out, is without blame.
