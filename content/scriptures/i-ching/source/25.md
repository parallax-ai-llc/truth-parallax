**Hexagram 25: Wu Wang — Innocence/The Unexpected**

☰☳

**The Judgment:** Wu Wang indicates great progress and success, while there will be advantage in being firm and correct. If its subject and his action be not correct, he will fall into errors, and it will not be advantageous for him to move in any direction.

**The Image:** Under heaven, thunder rolls. All things attain their natural state. Thus the kings of old, rich in virtue and in harmony with the time, fostered and nourished all beings.

**Line 1 (Initial Nine):** Innocent behaviour brings good fortune.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** If one does not count on the harvest while ploughing, nor on the use of the ground while clearing it, it furthers one to undertake something.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** Undeserved misfortune. The cow that was tethered by someone is the wanderer's gain, the citizen's loss.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** He who can be persevering remains without blame.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** Use no medicine in an illness incurred through no fault of your own. It will pass of itself.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** Innocent action brings misfortune. Nothing furthers.
