**Hexagram 4: Meng — Youthful Folly**

☶☵

**The Judgment:** Meng intimates that, in the case which it presupposes, there will be progress and success. It is not I who seek the young fool; the young fool seeks me. At the first oracle I inform him. If he ask a second and third time, it is importunity. Being importunate, I do not instruct him. There will be advantage in being firm and correct.

**The Image:** A spring wells up at the foot of the mountain. Thus the superior man fosters his character by thoroughness in all that he does.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** To make a fool develop, it is well to apply discipline. The fetters should be removed. To go on in this way brings humiliation.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** To bear with fools in kindliness brings good fortune. To know how to take women brings good fortune. The son is capable of taking charge of the household.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** Take not a maiden who, when she sees a man of bronze, loses possession of herself. Nothing furthers.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** Entangled folly brings humiliation.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** Childlike folly brings good fortune.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** In punishing folly, it does not further one to commit transgressions. The only thing that furthers is to prevent transgressions.
