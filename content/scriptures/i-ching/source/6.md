**Hexagram 6: Song — Conflict**

☰☵

**The Judgment:** Song intimates that, though there is sincerity in one's contention, he will yet meet with obstruction and apprehension. A cautious halt halfway brings good fortune. Going through to the end brings misfortune. It will be advantageous to see the great man. It will not be advantageous to cross the great stream.

**The Image:** Heaven and water go their opposite ways. Thus in all his transactions the superior man carefully considers the beginning.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** If one does not perpetuate the affair, there is a little gossip. In the end, good fortune comes.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** One cannot engage in the conflict. He returns home, gives way. The people of his town, three hundred households, remain free of guilt.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** To nourish oneself on ancient virtue induces perseverance. Danger, but in the end good fortune comes. If by chance you are in the service of a king, seek not works.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** One cannot engage in the conflict. One turns back and submits to fate, changes one's attitude, and finds peace in perseverance. Good fortune.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** To contend before him brings supreme good fortune.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** Even if by chance a leather belt is bestowed on one, by the end of a morning it will have been snatched away three times.
