**Hexagram 53: Jian — Development/Gradual Progress**

☴☶

**The Judgment:** Jian suggests to us the marriage of a young lady, and the good fortune attending it. There will be advantage in being firm and correct.

**The Image:** On the mountain, a tree. Thus the superior man abides in dignity and virtue, in order to improve the mores.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** The wild goose gradually draws near the shore. The young son is in danger. There is talk. No blame.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** The wild goose gradually draws near the cliff. Eating and drinking in peace and concord. Good fortune.

**Line 3 (Nine in the Third Place):** The wild goose gradually draws near the plateau. The man goes forth and does not return. The woman carries a child but does not bring it forth. Misfortune. It furthers one to fight off robbers.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** The wild goose gradually draws near the tree. Perhaps it will find a flat branch. No blame.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** The wild goose gradually draws near the summit. For three years the woman has no child. In the end nothing can hinder her. Good fortune.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** The wild goose gradually draws near the cloud heights. Its feathers can be used for the sacred dance. Good fortune.
