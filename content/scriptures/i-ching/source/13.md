**Hexagram 13: Tong Ren — Fellowship with Men**

☰☲

**The Judgment:** Tong Ren, or union of men, appears here as we find it in the open country. There will be progress and success. It will be advantageous to cross the great stream. It will be advantageous to maintain the firm correctness of the superior man.

**The Image:** Heaven together with fire. Thus the superior man organises the clans and makes distinctions between things.

**Line 1 (Initial Nine):** Fellowship with men at the gate. No blame.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** Fellowship with men in the clan. Humiliation.

**Line 3 (Nine in the Third Place):** He hides weapons in the thicket; he climbs the high hill in front of it. For three years he does not rise.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** He climbs up on his wall; he cannot attack. Good fortune.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** Men bound in fellowship first weep and lament, but afterward they laugh. After great struggles they succeed in meeting.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** Fellowship with men in the meadow. No remorse.
