**Hexagram 28: Da Guo — Preponderance of the Great**

☱☴

**The Judgment:** Da Guo suggests to us a beam that is weak. There will be advantage in moving under its conditions in any direction whatever. There will be success.

**The Image:** The lake rises above the trees. Thus the superior man, when he stands alone, is unconcerned; and if he has to renounce the world, he is undaunted.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** To spread white rushes underneath. No blame.

**Line 2 (Nine in the Second Place):** A dry poplar sprouts at the root. An older man takes a young wife. Everything furthers.

**Line 3 (Nine in the Third Place):** The ridgepole sags to the breaking point. Misfortune.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** The ridgepole is braced. Good fortune. If there are ulterior motives, it is humiliating.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** A withered poplar puts forth flowers. An older woman takes a husband. No blame. No praise.

**Line 6 (Top Six):** One must go through the water. It goes over one's head. Misfortune. No blame.
