**Hexagram 2: Kun — The Receptive/Earth**

☷☷

**The Judgment:** Kun intimates that, under the conditions which it denotes, there will be great progress and success. It will be advantageous for the superior man, having an object to pursue, at first to lose his way, and then to find his lord. It will be advantageous to find friends in the south and west, and to forego friends in the north and east. Quiet perseverance will bring good fortune.

**The Image:** The earth's condition is receptive devotion. Thus the superior man, being possessed of ample virtue, supports all things.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** When there is hoarfrost underfoot, solid ice is not far off.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** Straight, square, great. Without purpose, yet nothing remains unfurthered. The earth's nature is to bring forth; it carries and sustains all things.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** Hidden lines. One is able to remain persevering. If one should follow a king, seek not works, but bring to completion.

**Line 4 (Six in the Fourth Place):** A tied-up sack. No blame, no praise.

**Line 5 (Six in the Fifth Place):** A yellow lower garment brings supreme good fortune.

**Line 6 (Top Six):** Dragons fight in the meadow. Their blood is black and yellow.
