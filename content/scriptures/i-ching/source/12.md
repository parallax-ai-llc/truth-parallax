**Hexagram 12: Pi — Standstill/Stagnation**

☰☷

**The Judgment:** Pi denotes a time when the great departs and the small approaches. It will not be advantageous for the superior man to be firm and correct. There is no intercourse between heaven and earth, and all things do not communicate.

**The Image:** Heaven and earth do not unite: the image of Standstill. Thus the superior man falls back upon his inner worth in order to escape the difficulties. He does not permit himself to be honoured with revenue.

**Line 1 (Initial Six):** When ribbon grass is pulled up, the sod comes with it. Each according to his kind. Perseverance brings good fortune and success.

**Line 2 (Six in the Second Place):** They bear and endure; this means good fortune for inferior people. The standstill serves to help the great man to attain success.

**Line 3 (Six in the Third Place):** They bear shame.

**Line 4 (Nine in the Fourth Place):** He who acts at the command of the highest remains without blame. Those of like mind partake of the blessing.

**Line 5 (Nine in the Fifth Place):** Standstill is giving way. Good fortune for the great man. What if it should fail, what if it should fail? In this way he ties it to a cluster of mulberry shoots.

**Line 6 (Top Nine):** The standstill comes to an end. First standstill, then good fortune.
