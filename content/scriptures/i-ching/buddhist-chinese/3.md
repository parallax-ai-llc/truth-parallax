The Buddhist reading of Hexagram 24, Fu (Return), interprets the return of the yang line as the return to original mind (ben xin) — the Buddha-nature that is always present but obscured by delusion. The moment of return, when the first glimmer of yang appears in the midst of complete yin, corresponds to the moment of initial awakening when the practitioner first glimpses the true nature of mind beneath the layers of habitual confusion.

Hexagram 18, Gu (Work on What Has Been Spoiled), is read through the Buddhist lens of purification practice. The decay that has accumulated through past neglect corresponds to the karmic obstructions that the practitioner must address through repentance, meditation, and ethical reform. The hexagram's teaching that three days of preparation should precede and follow the work of repair resonates with the Buddhist emphasis on mindful intention and careful integration of spiritual practice.
