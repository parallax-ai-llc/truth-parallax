Buddhist interpretation of the second group reads Hexagram 11, Tai (Peace), as an image of the Buddha-land — the harmonious interpenetration of all phenomena that characterizes enlightened perception. The movement of heaven downward and earth upward, creating communion, mirrors the Huayan teaching of the mutual penetration of the phenomenal and the absolute (li and shi).

Hexagram 16, Yu (Enthusiasm), with its image of thunder arising from the earth, is read by Chinese Buddhist interpreters as depicting the arising of bodhicitta — the aspiration for enlightenment that emerges from the ground of ordinary experience. This spontaneous arising of spiritual motivation, like thunder, cannot be forced or manufactured; it occurs naturally when conditions are right, a principle that Chan Buddhism emphasizes in its teaching on sudden awakening.
