Chinese Buddhist interpretation of Hexagram 49, Ge (Revolution), reads transformation through the lens of the Buddhist teaching on the potential for radical change in consciousness. Just as the hexagram describes the transformation of one state into another, the Buddhist practitioner undergoes the revolution of turning away from delusion toward enlightenment. The Yogācāra concept of āśraya-parāvṛtti (turning about in the deepest seat of consciousness) provides the doctrinal framework for this reading.

Hexagram 50, Ding (The Cauldron), is interpreted as a symbol of the transformation of raw experience into refined wisdom through the heat of meditative practice. Just as the cauldron transforms raw ingredients into nourishment through fire, meditation transforms the practitioner's confused perceptions into the clear awareness that nourishes the path to awakening. The ritual significance of the cauldron — used for offerings — also connects to the Buddhist practice of offering one's practice for the benefit of all sentient beings.
