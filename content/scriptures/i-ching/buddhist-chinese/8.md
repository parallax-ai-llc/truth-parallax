The Buddhist reading of the final hexagrams finds particular significance in the closing pair. Hexagram 63, Ji Ji (After Completion), with every line in its proper place, represents the moment of full realization — all phenomena seen in their true nature. Yet the judgment warns that initial success leads to later disorder, echoing the Buddhist teaching that even enlightenment is not a static achievement but must be continuously actualized.

Hexagram 64, Wei Ji (Before Completion), as the I Ching's final word, resonates powerfully with the Buddhist teaching that the path of awakening is never finished. The bodhisattva vow to liberate all sentient beings before entering final nirvāṇa means that the work of compassion is, in a profound sense, always before completion. The I Ching's refusal to end with completion mirrors the Mahāyāna refusal to treat nirvāṇa as a final resting place.
