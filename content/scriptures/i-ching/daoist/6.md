The Daoist reading of the sixth group focuses on Hexagram 48, Jing (The Well), as an image of the Dao itself — the inexhaustible source that nourishes all beings without discrimination. The well does not seek out those who need water; it simply provides, endlessly and without effort. This is the model for the Daoist sage's relationship to the world: being available, giving without attachment, nourishing without controlling.

Hexagram 41, Sun (Decrease), and Hexagram 42, Yi (Increase), illustrate the Daoist principle that loss and gain are interdependent. The Dao De Jing teaches that the Dao decreases the excess and supplements the insufficient, and these hexagrams demonstrate this principle in action. The Daoist practitioner learns to welcome decrease as the natural complement of increase, knowing that both are necessary movements in the Dao's eternal rhythm.
