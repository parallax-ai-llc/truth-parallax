The Daoist reading of the fourth group focuses on Hexagram 25, Wu Wang (Innocence or Without Falseness), which represents the natural, uncontrived state that Daoism values above all. The innocence described here is not the innocence of ignorance but the spontaneous authenticity of one who has returned to alignment with the Dao — acting without calculation, responding without premeditation, living without artifice.

Hexagram 27, Yi (Nourishment), with its image of the open mouth, teaches the Daoist practitioner about the proper taking in and giving out of energy. Daoist dietary practices, breathing exercises, and the circulation of qi all connect to this hexagram's teaching about how the sage nourishes life. The parallel between physical and spiritual nourishment reflects the Daoist understanding that body and spirit are not separate but continuous expressions of the same vital energy.
