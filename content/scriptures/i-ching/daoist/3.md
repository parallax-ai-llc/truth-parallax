Daoist interpretation of the third group gives central importance to Hexagram 24, Fu (Return). The return of the single yang line at the nadir of yin represents the Dao's inexhaustible capacity for renewal — the still point at which reversal occurs naturally, without effort or intention. The Daoist practitioner studies this hexagram to understand the principle of wu wei: the return happens of itself when conditions are ripe; forcing it would be counterproductive.

Hexagram 23, Bo (Splitting Apart), which precedes Return, illustrates the Daoist understanding that decline is not a catastrophe but a natural phase in the cycle of change. When yang energy is exhausted, the wise response is not to fight the decline but to conserve energy and wait for the natural return. This patient acceptance of the cycle of growth and decay is central to Daoist spiritual practice.
