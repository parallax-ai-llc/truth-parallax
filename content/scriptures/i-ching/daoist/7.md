Hexagram 49, Ge (Revolution), in Daoist reading, represents the sudden, transformative change that occurs when conditions reach their extreme. Unlike the Confucian emphasis on the moral justification for revolution, the Daoist reading treats transformation as a natural event — like the shedding of a snake's skin or the turning of the seasons. Revolution happens when the old form can no longer contain the new energy; the sage recognizes this and aligns with the transformation.

Hexagram 52, Gen (Keeping Still, Mountain), holds special significance for Daoist meditation practice. The teaching to keep the back so still that one does not feel the body, to walk in the courtyard without seeing the people — this describes the state of deep stillness (jing) that Daoist meditators cultivate. From this stillness, the practitioner perceives the subtle movements of the Dao that are invisible to the agitated mind.
