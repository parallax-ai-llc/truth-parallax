Daoist reading of the second group highlights the natural cycles of expansion and contraction, fullness and emptiness that the hexagrams reveal. Hexagram 11, Tai (Peace), with heaven below and earth above, represents the harmonious mingling of yin and yang — not a social ideal but a natural state in which energies flow freely. Its reversal in Hexagram 12, Pi (Standstill), shows how blockage occurs when energies separate and cease to circulate.

The Daoist reader finds in Hexagram 15, Qian (Modesty), an expression of the Daoist principle that the valley (the low, the receptive, the empty) is the source of all streams. The mountain contained within the earth — the trigram image of this hexagram — illustrates the Daoist teaching that true power is hidden, that the soft overcomes the hard, and that the sage who appears to have nothing in fact contains everything.
