Daoist interpretation of the fifth group gives particular attention to Hexagram 33, Dun (Retreat), which expresses the Daoist sage's characteristic response to unfavorable conditions. Unlike the Confucian reading, which treats retreat as a strategic calculation, the Daoist reading sees retreat as the natural response of one who recognizes that the time for action has passed. The sage withdraws not to preserve virtue for future use but because withdrawal is simply what the situation calls for.

Hexagram 40, Jie (Deliverance), represents the release of tension that comes when a difficult period ends. The Daoist reading emphasizes that deliverance comes not through heroic effort but through the natural resolution of forces — thunder and rain clearing the air. The sage's role is to recognize the moment of release and to act swiftly and simply, without complicating the situation with unnecessary plans or ambitions.
