The Daoist reading of the final hexagrams emphasizes the profound teaching of Wei Ji (Before Completion) as the text's ultimate statement. The universe is never finished; the Dao never arrives at a final state. This perpetual incompletion is not a deficiency but the very nature of the living Dao — always in process, always becoming, never fixed in a final form.

Hexagram 61, Zhong Fu (Inner Truth), which appears in this final group, expresses the Daoist understanding that genuine spiritual attainment manifests as a quality of inner emptiness and openness. The image of wind over lake — the wind's influence penetrating the water's surface — represents the way that inner sincerity naturally communicates itself without words or deliberate action. The sage's influence, like the wind's, is invisible, impersonal, and irresistible.
