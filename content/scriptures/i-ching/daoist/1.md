The Daoist reading of the opening hexagrams Qian and Kun emphasizes their cosmological rather than moral significance. Qian represents the pure yang energy of heaven — creative, initiating, expansive — while Kun represents the pure yin energy of earth — receptive, nurturing, containing. Together they manifest the Dao's fundamental polarity, the dynamic tension from which all phenomena arise and to which all phenomena return.

Daoist interpretation notes that Qian and Kun never appear in isolation in the natural world; they always interact, producing the sixty-two hexagrams that represent the mixed conditions of actual experience. The sage who understands this recognizes that all situations are temporary configurations of yin and yang, and responds with the spontaneous flexibility of water rather than the rigid correctness of moral principle.
