## Overview

Religious interpretation of the I Ching approaches the text as a sacred instrument for discerning the will of Heaven and aligning human action with cosmic patterns. In both Daoist and folk religious contexts, the I Ching functions as a living oracle through which practitioners communicate with the spiritual dimensions of reality.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Divination Practice

The most ancient and enduring religious use of the I Ching is divination -- consulting the oracle through yarrow stalk or coin methods to receive guidance for specific situations. Religious interpreters regard the hexagrams not as random results but as meaningful communications from the cosmic order.

### Daoist Integration

Daoist interpreters read the I Ching as a complement to the Dao De Jing and the Zhuangzi, finding in its hexagram system a detailed map of the Dao's self-expression through yin and yang. The text becomes a guide to *wu wei* (effortless action) -- acting in harmony with the natural patterns of change.

### Spiritual Cultivation

In the internal alchemy (*neidan*) tradition, the I Ching's symbols are mapped onto the body's energy system. The trigrams correspond to specific organs and energy centers, and the transformations between hexagrams describe the stages of spiritual cultivation.
