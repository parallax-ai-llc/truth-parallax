## Spiritual Significance of Hexagram 47

Hexagram 47, Kun (Oppression, Exhaustion), carries profound spiritual significance within the Daoist and folk religious traditions that have used the I Ching as a sacred oracle for millennia. When this hexagram appears in divination, it is understood as a communication from the cosmic order, offering guidance for aligning one's actions with the Dao.

### Oracular Meaning

In traditional divination practice, receiving Hexagram 47 indicates a situation characterized by exhaustion. The practitioner is counseled to embody adversity and cultivate endurance. The hexagram's imagery draws from the natural world to convey spiritual truths that transcend purely rational understanding.

### Daoist Reading

Daoist interpreters read Kun as an expression of the Dao's ceaseless self-transformation. The hexagram reveals how Oppression, Exhaustion manifests as a particular pattern of yin and yang energies, teaching the practitioner to flow with these energies rather than resist them. The principle of *wu wei* (effortless action) finds specific application in the guidance this hexagram provides.

### Internal Alchemy

In the *neidan* (internal alchemy) tradition, the trigrams composing Hexagram 47 correspond to specific energetic processes within the practitioner's body-mind system. The hexagram maps a stage in the alchemical transformation of vital essence (*jing*) into spirit (*shen*), providing a symbolic language for describing subtle interior experiences.

### Ritual and Seasonal Associations

Traditional practice associates this hexagram with specific seasonal energies, ritual observances, and temporal qualities. Receiving this hexagram at a particular time of year or phase of life carries additional layers of meaning that enrich the basic oracular interpretation.
