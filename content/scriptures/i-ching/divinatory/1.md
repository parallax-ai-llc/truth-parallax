The divinatory tradition reads the first group of hexagrams as establishing the foundational oracular vocabulary. Hexagram 1, Qian (The Creative), when received in consultation, indicates a time of powerful creative energy in which bold action is favored. The six yang lines represent pure initiative, and the oracle counsels the questioner to act with confidence and vision. Hexagram 2, Kun (The Receptive), counsels the opposite: yield, follow, support rather than initiate.

In practical divination, the transition from Qian to the subsequent hexagrams — Zhun (Difficulty at the Beginning), Meng (Youthful Folly) — mirrors the common experience of the questioner who receives a powerful initial inspiration but must then navigate the practical difficulties of bringing it into reality. The oracle's guidance becomes increasingly specific and situational, teaching the diviner that cosmic principles manifest differently depending on context and timing.
