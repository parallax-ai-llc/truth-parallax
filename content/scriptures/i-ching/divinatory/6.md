Divinatory interpretation of the sixth group addresses the querent's relationship to fundamental resources and values. Hexagram 48, Jing (The Well), in consultation, indicates that the questioner has access to a deep source of wisdom or sustenance that is not being fully utilized. The oracle counsels drawing from this source rather than seeking new resources — the answer to the current situation lies in what is already available but perhaps overlooked.

Hexagram 43, Guai (Breakthrough), received in divination, signals a moment of decisive resolution in which the questioner must confront a difficult truth or situation that can no longer be avoided. The oracle counsels courageous but non-violent action — making the truth known publicly rather than engaging in secret struggle. The hexagram warns that the one remaining yin line, though seemingly weak, can still cause harm if not addressed openly and honestly.
