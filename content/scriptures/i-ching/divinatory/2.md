Divinatory interpretation of the second group focuses on the practical guidance these hexagrams offer for social and political situations. Hexagram 11, Tai (Peace), received in consultation, indicates a favorable period in which cooperation between different parties is possible and fruitful. The oracle advises the questioner to take advantage of harmonious conditions while they last, knowing that the reversal of Hexagram 12, Pi (Standstill), is inevitable.

Hexagram 13, Tong Ren (Fellowship with Men), is one of the most frequently consulted hexagrams in the divinatory tradition for questions about relationships, partnerships, and community. The oracle counsels openness, sincerity, and the willingness to work with others toward common goals. The changing lines within this hexagram provide nuanced guidance about the specific challenges and opportunities the questioner faces in their particular social situation.
