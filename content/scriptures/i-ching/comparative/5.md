The fifth group's treatment of retreat and advance connects the I Ching with the universal strategic wisdom found in military and political texts across cultures. Hexagram 33, Dun (Retreat), can be compared with Sun Tzu's Art of War, Kautilya's Arthashastra, Machiavelli's Prince, and the Bhagavad Gita's treatment of strategic action. Each tradition recognizes that withdrawal can be the wisest form of engagement.

Hexagram 36, Ming Yi (Darkening of the Light), invites comparison with traditions that address the problem of preserving wisdom or virtue during times of oppression. The Marrano tradition in Judaism, the taqiyya principle in Shia Islam, and the Confucian concept of the hidden worthy (yinshi) all address the situation of the wise person living under an unjust regime — a perennial human situation that receives nuanced treatment in the I Ching.
