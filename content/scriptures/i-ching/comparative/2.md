Comparative study of the second group's themes of peace and stagnation connects the I Ching with political philosophies across cultures that address the rise and fall of social order. The cyclical alternation between Tai (Peace) and Pi (Standstill) parallels the Greek concept of anacyclosis (the cycle of political constitutions), the Islamic concept of dawla (the rise and fall of dynasties), and the Hindu concept of yugas (world ages of declining virtue).

Hexagram 15, Qian (Modesty), invites comparison with virtue ethics traditions worldwide that identify humility or modesty as a central virtue. The Daoist praise of the valley, the Christian virtue of humility, the Buddhist emphasis on egolessness, and the Islamic concept of tawadu all point toward a cross-cultural recognition that authentic virtue involves the diminishment rather than the aggrandizement of the self.
