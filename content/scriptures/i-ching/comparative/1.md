Comparative analysis of the I Ching's opening hexagrams examines the yin-yang binary alongside dualistic cosmological systems in other traditions. The heaven-earth pairing of Qian and Kun can be compared with the Zoroastrian Ahura Mazda-Angra Mainyu duality, the Hindu Purusha-Prakriti distinction, and the Kabbalistic Ein Sof and Shekinah. In each case, the cosmos is understood as arising from the interaction of complementary principles, though the I Ching is distinctive in treating its primal pair as equal and mutually necessary rather than hierarchically ordered.

The hexagram system as a whole — sixty-four figures generated by the systematic combination of two elements — invites comparison with other binary classification systems. The West African Ifá divination system, which also generates 256 figures from binary combinations, presents a striking structural parallel that has generated scholarly debate about possible historical connections, independent invention, or shared cognitive foundations for binary thinking.
