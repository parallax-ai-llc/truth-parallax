The theme of return in Hexagram 24 connects the I Ching with cyclical cosmologies worldwide. The eternal return of yang at the moment of its greatest absence parallels the Stoic concept of eternal recurrence, the Hindu concept of cosmic cycles (kalpa), and the Mesoamerican calendrical cycles that structure the Aztec and Maya understanding of time. In each tradition, the cosmos is understood as moving through recurring patterns rather than progressing toward a final end.

Hexagram 17, Sui (Following), raises comparative questions about the nature of leadership and authority across cultures. The I Ching's teaching that the leader must sometimes follow, and that following requires its own form of wisdom, connects with servant-leadership concepts in Christian ethics, the Buddhist emphasis on the teacher who adapts to the student's capacity, and the democratic principle of government responsive to the governed.
