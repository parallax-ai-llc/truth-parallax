The fifth group of hexagrams addresses the Confucian themes of strategic retreat, the exercise of power, gradual progress, and liberation from difficulty. Hexagram 33, Dun (Retreat), teaches that withdrawal from an unfavorable situation is not cowardice but wisdom — the junzi knows when to advance and when to retreat, preserving moral integrity for future engagement.

Hexagram 35, Jin (Progress), and Hexagram 36, Ming Yi (Darkening of the Light), form a complementary pair that Confucian readers find deeply instructive. Progress occurs when virtue is recognized and employed by the ruler; the darkening of the light occurs when the worthy are suppressed by an unworthy ruler. The junzi must be prepared for both situations, advancing confidently when conditions are favorable and preserving inner brilliance during times of darkness.
