Confucian reading of the final group emphasizes completion, incompletion, and the cyclical nature of the moral life. Hexagram 63, Ji Ji (After Completion), and Hexagram 64, Wei Ji (Before Completion), form the text's concluding pair, teaching that every completion contains the seeds of new challenges and every state of incompletion contains the seeds of fulfillment.

The placement of Wei Ji (Before Completion) as the final hexagram is deeply significant for Confucian interpretation. The moral life is never finished; the junzi never reaches a state of final perfection but continues to cultivate virtue in response to ever-changing circumstances. This open ending reflects the Confucian conviction that moral cultivation is a lifelong process — and the I Ching itself, having been studied to its final hexagram, invites the reader to begin again from the beginning with deeper understanding.
