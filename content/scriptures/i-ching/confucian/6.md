Confucian interpretation of the sixth group focuses on the dialectic of decrease and increase, the courage required for breakthrough, and the deep wisdom symbolized by the well. Hexagram 41, Sun (Decrease), and Hexagram 42, Yi (Increase), teach the junzi about the proper circulation of resources and virtue in society — the ruler decreases personal excess to increase the welfare of the people.

Hexagram 48, Jing (The Well), receives special attention in Confucian reading as a symbol of the inexhaustible source of moral wisdom available to all who draw from it. The well provides water regardless of who comes to it — an image of the universality of moral principle and the Confucian commitment to education as a means of moral transformation accessible to all, regardless of social station.
