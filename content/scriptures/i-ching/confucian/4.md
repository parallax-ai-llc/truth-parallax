Confucian reading of the fourth group emphasizes the themes of innocence, great accumulation, nourishment, and endurance that define the mature moral life. Hexagram 25, Wu Wang (Innocence), teaches the junzi to act from genuine sincerity rather than calculated self-interest — a core Confucian value that connects to the teaching on cheng (sincerity) in the Doctrine of the Mean.

Hexagram 32, Heng (Duration), closes this group with a meditation on constancy in the midst of change. The Confucian interpretation reads this as teaching that the junzi maintains moral constancy not by rigidity but by continuously adapting outward behavior to changing circumstances while holding firm to inner principles. This dynamic constancy — changing in order to remain the same — epitomizes the Confucian understanding of virtue as a living, responsive engagement with the world.
