The seventh group's hexagrams address themes of revolutionary change, institutional renewal, and the challenges of transition and travel. Hexagram 49, Ge (Revolution), presents the Confucian teaching on legitimate political change — revolution is justified only when the existing order has exhausted its moral mandate, and only when the revolutionaries act from genuine moral conviction rather than personal ambition.

Hexagram 50, Ding (The Cauldron), immediately follows Revolution, teaching that transformation must be followed by the establishment of proper institutions. The cauldron, used for ritual offerings, symbolizes the Confucian conviction that political order requires a foundation in ritual propriety (li) and cultural refinement (wen). The junzi transforms society not through force alone but through the establishment of institutions that embody and transmit moral values.
