The third group of hexagrams, in Confucian reading, addresses themes of following, cultivation of inner beauty, decline, and the crucial principle of return. Hexagram 17, Sui (Following), teaches the junzi when to lead and when to follow — a key concern of Confucian political ethics. Hexagram 22, Bi (Grace), explores the relationship between substance and adornment, reflecting the Confucian concern with the balance between inner virtue (zhi) and outward refinement (wen).

Hexagram 24, Fu (Return), is one of the most philosophically significant hexagrams in the Confucian tradition. The single yang line appearing at the bottom of five yin lines represents the return of moral vitality after a period of decline. Cheng Yi and Zhu Xi read this hexagram as revealing the indestructibility of moral principle (li) — however dark the times, the seed of renewal is always present, and the junzi's task is to recognize and nurture this returning goodness.
