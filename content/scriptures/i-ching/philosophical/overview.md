## Overview

Philosophical interpretation of the I Ching reads the text as a systematic account of the patterns of change that govern all phenomena. Rather than approaching the hexagrams as oracular pronouncements, philosophical interpreters extract from them a coherent worldview centered on process, polarity, and the dynamic interplay of complementary forces.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Process Philosophy

The I Ching's central insight -- that change is the fundamental reality -- aligns with process philosophical traditions from Heraclitus to Whitehead. Each hexagram represents not a static state but a dynamic pattern of transformation, always in the process of becoming something else.

### Dialectical Thinking

The yin-yang polarity that structures every hexagram embodies a dialectical mode of thought in which apparent opposites are understood as complementary aspects of a single reality. Light and dark, firm and yielding, active and receptive are not adversaries but partners in the cosmic dance of change.

### Situational Ethics

The I Ching offers a philosophical ethics grounded not in abstract rules but in sensitive attunement to the demands of each unique situation. The "superior person" of the I Ching is one who reads the signs of the times correctly and responds with appropriate action.
