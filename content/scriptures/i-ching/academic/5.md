Academic analysis of the fifth group examines the textual evidence for the composition date and authorship of the hexagram judgments and line statements. The traditional attribution of the judgments to King Wen and the line statements to the Duke of Zhou has been largely abandoned by modern scholars, who instead identify multiple anonymous authors working within the Western Zhou court tradition over a period of perhaps two centuries.

The Mawangdui manuscript version of this section reveals variant readings that illuminate the history of textual transmission. Differences in hexagram order, character usage, and supplementary material between the Mawangdui and received versions demonstrate that the I Ching continued to evolve as a text well into the Han dynasty, with standardization occurring only gradually through the influence of officially sanctioned editions.
