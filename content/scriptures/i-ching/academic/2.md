Academic study of the second group examines the social and political context reflected in the hexagram texts. Hexagram 11, Tai (Peace), and Hexagram 12, Pi (Standstill), with their themes of communion and separation between ruler and people, likely reflect the political concerns of the Western Zhou court, where the legitimacy of royal authority and the maintenance of feudal relationships were pressing issues.

The line statements in this group contain references to specific historical events and social practices — marriage customs, military expeditions, ritual offerings — that provide valuable evidence for historians of the Western Zhou period. Scholars analyze these references alongside bronze inscription evidence and the limited textual sources for this period to reconstruct the social world in which the I Ching's earliest layers were composed.
