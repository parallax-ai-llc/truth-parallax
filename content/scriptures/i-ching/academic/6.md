Scholarly study of the sixth group has focused on Hexagram 48, Jing (The Well), as a case study in the archaeology and sociology of the I Ching. The well was a central feature of Zhou dynasty settlement patterns, and the hexagram's detailed attention to the well's construction, maintenance, and social significance reflects the material realities of an agricultural society dependent on reliable water sources.

The image system of the sixth group — lake, mountain, wind, thunder, water — has been analyzed by scholars studying the I Ching's relationship to early Chinese natural philosophy. The correlative thinking that connects natural phenomena to human situations, and that would later develop into the elaborate correspondence systems of Han dynasty cosmology, is already present in rudimentary form in these hexagram texts.
