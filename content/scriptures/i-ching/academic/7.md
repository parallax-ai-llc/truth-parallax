Academic examination of the seventh group focuses on Hexagram 49, Ge (Revolution), and its role in Chinese political philosophy. The character ge literally means the skin of an animal, and the hexagram's imagery of shedding and transformation has been connected to seasonal molting rituals documented in Zhou dynasty texts. The political meaning of revolution as legitimate change of dynasty was a later interpretive development.

Hexagram 56, Lü (The Wanderer), provides evidence for the social status and experience of travelers in Western Zhou society. The line statements describe the dangers and social marginality of travel in a feudal society where identity was tied to place and kinship. This hexagram's imagery has been compared with similar depictions of the stranger's vulnerability in other ancient Near Eastern and Mediterranean literatures.
