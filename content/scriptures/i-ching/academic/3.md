The third group of hexagrams has received particular academic attention for the theme of return (Hexagram 24) and its philosophical development in later Chinese thought. The original oracle text likely referred simply to the return of a person or army, but the philosophical elaboration in the Ten Wings and by later commentators transformed this into a cosmological principle of cyclical renewal that became central to Chinese philosophy.

Academic analysis of Hexagram 23, Bo (Splitting Apart), examines its line statements as evidence of Western Zhou anxieties about social and political disintegration. The image of a bed being split apart from its legs upward has been interpreted by scholars as reflecting the feudal hierarchy's vulnerability to erosion from below — a political concern that would have been immediately relevant to the Zhou aristocracy.
