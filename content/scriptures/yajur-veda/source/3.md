---
title: "Yajur Veda - Chapter 3"
scripture: "yajur-veda"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The sacrificial post is raised. The altar is prepared. The fire is kindled. The sacred waters flow. Let the offering proceed according to the eternal law.

**2** O Agni, lead us along the right path to prosperity. O god, thou knowest all the ways. Keep far from us crooked evil. We shall offer thee the fullest praise.

**3** May the Vishvedevas, the universal gods, protect the sacrifice. May they accept the oblation. May they grant us their favour.

**4** The Vishvedevas have come to our sacrifice. They sit upon the sacred grass. They drink the Soma. They grant blessings to the worshipper.

**5** O Indra, most mighty, come to us. The Soma is pressed, the offerings are ready. Drink and be satisfied. Give us strength and prosperity.

**6** O Indra, thou who art the lord of horses, the lord of cattle, the lord of strength — accept our worship. May thy thunderbolt protect us.

**7** With the divine Soma, with the sacred butter, with the holy hymn, we worship thee, O Indra. Come, mighty one, come to our feast.

**8** The three great fires are kindled: Garhapatya, the fire of the householder; Ahavaniya, the fire of offering; and Dakshinagni, the southern fire. May they carry our oblations to the gods.

**9** The Hotri chants the hymn, the Adhvaryu prepares the offering, the Udgatri sings the Saman, the Brahman guards the whole. Thus the fourfold sacrifice proceeds.

**10** Let the four priests perform their duties well. Let the sacred fires blaze brightly. Let the hymns ascend to heaven. Let the oblations reach the gods.

**11** We offer to thee, O Agni, the first oblation. We offer to thee, O Soma, the second. We offer to thee, O Indra, the third. May the gods accept our offering.

**12** The sacrifice is complete. The fire has consumed the offering. The hymns have been chanted. The gods are pleased. May they grant us health, wealth, and long life.
