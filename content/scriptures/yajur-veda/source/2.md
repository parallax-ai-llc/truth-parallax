---
title: "Yajur Veda - Chapter 2"
scripture: "yajur-veda"
chapter: 2
---

**1** That god Savitar, our protector, has risen, the creator who has measured out the earthly regions. He has illumined the world with his golden hands. The far-seeing one has mounted the heights of heaven.

**2** He has yoked the seven bright horses to his chariot; the divine Savitar, the golden-handed, proceeds on his journey through the heavens and the earth.

**3** May Savitar, whose hands are golden, whose tongue is sweet, whose laws are true, protect us from every quarter. May the divine Savitar bestow upon us blessing.

**4** O Agni, thou art the light. Thou art the protector of the body. Thou dost increase. Grant us food. Keep away from us the evil spirit that comes by night.

**5** O waters, ye are wholesome. Further us to strength, to see great joy. The best flavour that is yours, accord to us here, like loving mothers.

**6** For you we gladly go to him in whose dwelling you make us rejoice. O waters, give us offspring.

**7** Ishana of all that is and all that shall be, of gods and of men — may he, the Overlord, grant us splendid treasure.

**8** He who is the sovereign of the gods, in whom the worlds find their rest, who rules over two-footed and four-footed creatures — to what god shall we offer our oblation?

**9** By whom the heaven is mighty and the earth firm, by whom the sun has its foundation, by whom the firmament and the ether were established — who is the god to whom we shall offer our worship?

**10** To Prajapati, lord of all that is born, we offer this our oblation. May he grant us what we desire. May we become the lords of riches.

**11** Do not harm us, O Rudra, in our children and descendants, in our life, in our cattle, in our horses. Do not, O Rudra, slay our heroes in thy wrath. We call on thee with oblations continually.

**12** May Rudra's missile turn aside and spare us, may the great anger of the fierce god pass us by. Unstring thy bow for our wealthy patrons. O bounteous god, be merciful to our children and their children.
