---
title: "Yajur Veda - Chapter 4"
scripture: "yajur-veda"
chapter: 4
---

**1** That supreme Brahman is infinite, and this conditioned Brahman is infinite. The infinite proceeds from the infinite. Taking the infinitude of the infinite, it remains as the infinite alone. Om, peace, peace, peace.

**2** Om. May my speech be based on mind; may my mind be based on speech. O self-luminous one, reveal thyself to me. May both of them bring the Veda to me. May not all that I have heard depart from me.

**3** I shall unite day and night through this study. I shall speak the right; I shall speak the true. May that protect me; may that protect the speaker. May it protect me, may it protect the speaker. Om, peace, peace, peace.

**4** He who dwells in the earth, and within the earth, whom the earth does not know, whose body the earth is, and who controls the earth from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**5** He who dwells in the water, and within the water, whom the water does not know, whose body the water is, and who controls the water from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**6** He who dwells in the fire, and within the fire, whom the fire does not know, whose body the fire is, and who controls the fire from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**7** He who dwells in the sky, and within the sky, whom the sky does not know, whose body the sky is, and who controls the sky from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**8** He who dwells in the wind, and within the wind, whom the wind does not know, whose body the wind is, and who controls the wind from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**9** He who dwells in the sun, and within the sun, whom the sun does not know, whose body the sun is, and who controls the sun from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**10** He who dwells in all beings, and within all beings, whom all beings do not know, whose body all beings are, and who controls all beings from within — He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal.

**11** He is the unseen Seer, the unheard Hearer, the unthought Thinker, the unknown Knower. There is no other seer but He, no other hearer but He, no other thinker but He, no other knower but He. He is thy Self, the Inner Controller, the Immortal. Everything other than Him is perishable.

**12** The face of truth is covered with a golden disc. Unveil it, O Pushan, so that I who love the truth may see it. O Pushan, the sole seer, O controller, O sun, offspring of Prajapati, spread forth thy rays and gather thy radiance, that I may behold that nature of thine which is the fairest. He, the Person that dwells there — I am he.
