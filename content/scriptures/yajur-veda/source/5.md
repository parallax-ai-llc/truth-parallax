---
title: "Yajur Veda - Chapter 5"
scripture: "yajur-veda"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Om. That is full; this is full. From fullness, fullness comes. When fullness is taken from fullness, fullness still remains. Om, peace, peace, peace.

**2** Into blind darkness enter they who worship ignorance; into darkness greater than that, as it were, they that delight in knowledge.

**3** One thing, they say, is obtained from knowledge; another, they say, from ignorance. Thus we have heard from the wise who taught us this.

**4** He who knows at the same time both knowledge and ignorance, overcomes death through ignorance and obtains immortality through knowledge.

**5** Into blind darkness enter they who worship the unmanifest; into darkness greater than that, as it were, they who delight in the manifest.

**6** One thing, they say, is obtained from the manifest; another, they say, from the unmanifest. Thus we have heard from the wise who taught us this.

**7** He who knows at the same time both the manifest and the unmanifest, overcomes death through the unmanifest and obtains immortality through the manifest.

**8** The door of truth is covered with a golden disc. Open it, O Pushan, for me who worship truth. Open it so that I may see the nature of the Supreme.

**9** O Pushan, the sole seer, O Yama, O Sun, O offspring of Prajapati — remove thy rays, gather up thy brilliance, that I may behold thy most blessed form. The Person who dwells there — I am He.

**10** Now may my breath return to the all-pervading, immortal Prana. May this body be reduced to ashes. Om. O mind, remember, remember thy deeds. O mind, remember, remember thy deeds.

**11** O Agni, lead us by the right path to prosperity. Thou, O god, knowest all the ways. Remove the deceitful sin far from us. We shall offer to thee the fullest praise.

**12** May there be peace in the heavens, peace in the atmosphere, peace on the earth. May the waters flow peacefully. May all the divine herbs and plants grow in peace. May all the beneficent beings bring us peace. May the Vedic Law propagate peace throughout the world. May all things be a source of peace to us. And may that peace also bring peace to us. Om, peace, peace, peace.
