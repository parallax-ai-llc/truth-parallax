## Overview

The Yajur Veda ("Veda of Sacrificial Formulas") is the primary liturgical handbook of the Adhvaryu priest, who performs the physical actions of the Vedic sacrifice. It contains the prose mantras (*yajus*) and ritual formulas recited during the preparation and execution of the yajna (fire sacrifice). The Yajur Veda exists in two major recensions: the Krishna (Black) Yajur Veda, which interweaves mantras with ritual commentary, and the Shukla (White) Yajur Veda, which separates the mantras into the Samhita and the commentary into the Shatapatha Brahmana.

## Structure

The Yajur Veda's ritual content can be organized into five thematic sections covering the major sacrificial rites:

1. **Darsha-Purnamasa**: New and full moon rites, the foundational model for all Vedic sacrifice
2. **Soma Sacrifice Formulas**: Mantras for the elaborate multi-day Soma pressing rituals
3. **Agnichayana**: Instructions for the construction of the great fire altar from 10,800 bricks
4. **Royal Rituals**: Formulas for the Rajasuya (royal consecration) and Ashvamedha (horse sacrifice)
5. **Pravargya and Supplementary Rites**: Ancillary ceremonies and their mantras

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Traditional**: The Yajur Veda is the indispensable manual for conducting Vedic sacrifice and maintaining cosmic order
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Shankara drew extensively on the Yajur Veda's Isha Upanishad and Brihadaranyaka Upanishad
- **Vaishnavism**: The Purusha Sukta (also in the Yajur Veda recension) is central to Vaishnava temple liturgy
- **Shaivism**: The Rudram and Chamakam from the Krishna Yajur Veda are the most important Shaiva liturgical texts
- **Academic**: Essential for understanding ritual theory and the sociology of the priestly class
- **Comparative**: Compared with Zoroastrian *yasna* liturgy, Roman *carmina*, and other ancient ritual traditions
