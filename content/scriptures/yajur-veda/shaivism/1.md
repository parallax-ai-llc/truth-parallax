Shaiva interpretation reads the Darsha-Purnamasa rites through the lens of Shiva as the lord of time (*Mahakala*) who governs the cosmic rhythms of creation and dissolution. The new and full moon rites mark the temporal pulse of the cosmos, and the Yajurvedic formulas recited during these ceremonies align the sacrificer with Shiva's temporal governance, participating in the divine rhythm that sustains the universe.

The fire of the Darsha-Purnamasa is understood as Shiva's creative energy (*tapas*), and the offerings are acts of surrender to the lord of sacrifice. The twice-monthly rhythm of these rites models the Shaiva understanding of the continuous oscillation between creation (*srshti*) and dissolution (*samhara*) that characterizes Shiva's cosmic dance.
