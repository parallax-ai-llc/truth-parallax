The Agnichayana fire altar is of supreme importance to Shaivism because it represents the most elaborate ritual invocation of the sacred fire that is Shiva's essence. The construction of the altar from 10,800 bricks is understood as a ritual reconstruction of Shiva's cosmic body, and the five layers correspond to the five faces (*Panchavaktra*) or five activities (*Panchakritya*) of Shiva: creation, preservation, destruction, concealment, and grace.

The geometric precision of the fire altar construction connects to the Shaiva tradition of sacred geometry (*yantra*), where geometric patterns serve as material supports for meditation on Shiva's nature. The Agnichayana's mathematical sophistication — its use of the Pythagorean theorem and precise spatial calculations — reflects the Shaiva insight that the divine expresses itself through the order and beauty of mathematical form.
