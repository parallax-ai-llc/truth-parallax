Shaiva reading of the Soma sacrifice formulas connects the Soma ritual to Shiva's identity as the bearer of the crescent moon. In Shaiva iconography, the moon on Shiva's head represents Soma, and the divine nectar that flows from it symbolizes the ambrosia of consciousness that Shiva bestows upon his devotees. The elaborate Soma sacrifice is thus read as a ritual enactment of Shiva's grace.

The transformative process of the Soma ritual — the violent pressing of the plant, the filtering and purification, the emergence of the golden divine juice — parallels the Shaiva understanding of spiritual transformation through Shiva's grace: the raw material of ordinary consciousness is subjected to the discipline (*tapas*) of practice, filtered through the guru's teaching, and transformed into the nectar of liberation.
