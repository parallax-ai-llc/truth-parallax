The Pravargya rite holds special significance in Shaiva tradition because its central symbol — the blazing clay pot (*mahavira*) heated until it becomes a miniature sun — resonates with Shiva's identity as the lord of light and fire. The restoration of the sun's head, the mythological background of the Pravargya, connects to Shaiva narratives of cosmic restoration and Shiva's role as the healer and renewer of the universe.

Shaiva reading of the supplementary rites emphasizes their role in addressing the full range of human spiritual needs, just as Shiva's five activities encompass the totality of cosmic function. The rites of purification invoke Shiva as the purifier, the rites of protection invoke him as the destroyer of evil, and the rites of blessing invoke him as the bestower of grace — revealing the comprehensive nature of Shiva's care for the world.
