Shaiva interpretation reads the Rajasuya and Ashvamedha as rituals that derive their power from Shiva, the king of kings (*Rajarajeshvara*). The earthly king's consecration is understood as his initiation into Shiva's sovereignty, and the royal authority established through the ritual is a delegated expression of Shiva's cosmic lordship. The Ashvamedha horse, roaming freely, symbolizes Nandi, Shiva's bull, who moves freely through all the worlds.

The Sri Rudram, found in the Krishna Yajur Veda's fourth chapter, assigns to Rudra-Shiva every role and social function, including that of the king. The royal rituals are thus encompassed within Shiva's all-embracing nature: he is simultaneously the king and the kingmaker, the sovereign and the power behind sovereignty, the lord of all who transcends all roles while inhabiting them.
