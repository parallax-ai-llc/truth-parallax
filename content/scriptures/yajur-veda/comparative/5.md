The Pravargya rite's solar mythology — the restoration of the sun's severed head — compares with solar renewal myths across cultures: the Egyptian myth of Ra's nightly journey through the underworld, the Aztec myth of the Fifth Sun, and the Japanese myth of Amaterasu's emergence from the cave. All these narratives address the universal anxiety about the sun's disappearance and the ritual means of ensuring its return.

The supplementary rites' function of error correction and atonement compares with confession and penance traditions across religions: the Catholic sacrament of reconciliation, the Jewish practice of *teshuvah* (repentance), the Islamic *tawba*, and the Buddhist confession ceremony. The Yajur Veda's systematic approach to ritual expiation reveals a universal human concern for addressing imperfection and maintaining right relationship with the sacred.
