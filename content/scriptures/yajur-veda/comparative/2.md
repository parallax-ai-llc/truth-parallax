The Soma sacrifice's elaborate multi-day format compares with extended ritual ceremonies in other traditions: the Catholic Easter Triduum, the Jewish Sukkot week, the Muslim Hajj, and the Hopi Snake Dance ceremony all involve multi-day ritual sequences with specific liturgical content for each phase. The Yajur Veda's Soma formulas demonstrate how complex societies develop extended ritual forms to address the full range of cosmic and social needs.

The Soma sacrifice's requirement for multiple specialized priestly orders compares with the division of liturgical labor in other traditions. The medieval Catholic Mass required celebrant, deacon, subdeacon, and choir; the Jewish Temple required kohanim, Levites, and the ma'amad; the Chinese imperial sacrifice required elaborate bureaucratic coordination. These parallels illuminate the universal tendency of complex ritual systems to develop specialized roles.
