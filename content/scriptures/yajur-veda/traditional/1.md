The Darsha-Purnamasa (new and full moon) rites represent the foundational model of all Vedic sacrifice. The Yajur Veda provides the precise formulas that the Adhvaryu priest recites while performing the physical actions of these twice-monthly rituals: the preparation of the sacrificial ground, the kindling of the fires, the offering of grain cakes (*purodasha*) and milk oblations, and the formal dismissal of the ritual. These rites are the minimum sacrificial obligation of the Vedic householder.

Traditional commentary emphasizes that the Darsha-Purnamasa is not merely a routine observance but a microcosm of the entire sacrificial system. Every element of the more elaborate Soma sacrifice and seasonal rituals is present in embryonic form in these basic rites. The Adhvaryu's mastery of the Darsha-Purnamasa formulas is therefore the essential foundation upon which all further ritual competence is built.
