The Pravargya and supplementary rites section of the Yajur Veda contains formulas for ancillary ceremonies that accompany the major sacrifices. The Pravargya rite — the heating of a special clay pot (*mahavira*) until it glows red and the pouring of boiling milk into it — is one of the most esoteric Vedic rituals, associated with the restoration of the sun's head and the attainment of immortality.

Traditional interpretation emphasizes that these supplementary rites, though seemingly minor, are essential to the completeness of the sacrificial system. Each ancillary ceremony addresses a specific spiritual need — purification, protection, atonement, or blessing — and the Yajurvedic formulas that accompany them carry the full authority of Vedic revelation. The mastery of these supplementary rites distinguishes the accomplished Adhvaryu from the novice.
