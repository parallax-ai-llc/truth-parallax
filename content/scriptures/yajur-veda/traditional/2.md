The Soma sacrifice formulas constitute the liturgical core of the Yajur Veda, providing the mantras for the most elaborate and prestigious of all Vedic rituals. The Adhvaryu's role in the Soma sacrifice is immensely complex: he must coordinate the pressing of the Soma stalks, the filtering of the juice, the offering to the gods, and the ritual consumption by the priests, all while reciting the appropriate Yajurvedic formulas with exact timing.

The traditional understanding holds that the Soma sacrifice is the supreme ritual act, capable of securing the greatest blessings — progeny, cattle, prosperity, heaven, and even immortality. The Yajur Veda's Soma formulas are therefore among the most sacred utterances in the entire Vedic corpus, transmitted with the utmost care through the oral tradition and performed only by those who have undergone years of rigorous training.
