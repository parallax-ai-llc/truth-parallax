The Agnichayana chapter describes the mantras and procedures for the construction of the great fire altar (*mahavedi*), one of the most extraordinary rituals in human religious history. The altar, built in the shape of a falcon with outstretched wings, requires 10,800 bricks arranged in five layers according to precise geometric specifications. The Yajur Veda provides the formula for the consecration of each brick and each layer.

Traditional understanding sees the fire altar as a symbolic reconstruction of the cosmos. The first layer represents the earth, the subsequent layers the atmosphere and heavens, and the completed altar becomes a microcosmic image of the universe itself. The Adhvaryu's recitation of the Yajurvedic formulas during construction transforms inert bricks into a living cosmic structure through which the sacrificer may ascend to the heavenly world.
