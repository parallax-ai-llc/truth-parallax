The Rajasuya and Ashvamedha formulas provide scholars with the most detailed evidence for the political theology of the Vedic period. The ritual construction of kingship through consecration, symbolic combat, and territorial assertion reflects a society in which political authority was inseparable from ritual legitimation. Scholars compare these rites with coronation ceremonies in other ancient cultures to understand the universal and particular features of sacred kingship.

Jan Heesterman's analysis of the Rajasuya identifies in the ritual a 'pre-classical' layer where the king was a sacrificial victim who gained power through ordeal, gradually transformed into the classical model where the king is the sacrificer who gains cosmic sovereignty through offering. This evolutionary analysis illuminates the relationship between violence, sovereignty, and sacrifice in Indo-Aryan political thought.
