The Soma sacrifice formulas are studied for their evidence of the most elaborate ritual system of the ancient world. Scholars have identified multiple forms of the Soma sacrifice — from the one-day *agnishtoma* to the twelve-day *dvadashaha* and the year-long *sattras* — each requiring specific Yajurvedic formulas. The coordination of four priestly orders in these rituals represents a level of liturgical organization unmatched in other ancient traditions.

The linguistic analysis of the Soma formulas provides crucial data for the chronological stratification of the Yajur Veda. Scholars note that the prose *yajus* formulas exhibit linguistic features intermediate between the archaic Sanskrit of the Rig Veda and the classical Sanskrit of later literature, helping to date the Yajur Veda's composition to approximately 1200-800 BCE.
