Scholars study the Darsha-Purnamasa formulas as the most basic unit of the Vedic sacrificial system, from which all more complex rituals are derived. The detailed prescriptions in the Yajur Veda and their elaboration in the Brahmana literature provide a complete picture of the ritual procedure, enabling scholars to reconstruct the actual performance of these rites and understand their social and religious function in Vedic society.

The Darsha-Purnamasa rites have been central to ritual theory since Marcel Mauss and Henri Hubert's classic *Essai sur la nature et la fonction du sacrifice* (1899). Jan Heesterman's analysis of the 'pre-classical' sacrifice, Frits Staal's formalist approach, and Brian K. Smith's interpretation through the lens of resemblance and classification all draw extensively on these foundational rites as documented in the Yajur Veda.
