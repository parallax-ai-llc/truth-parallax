## Overview

Vaishnavas regard the Yajur Veda as containing essential revelations of Vishnu-Narayana's supreme nature, particularly through the Purusha Sukta (which appears in both the Rig and Yajur Vedic recensions) and the Narayana Sukta. The Yajur Veda's detailed sacrificial formulas are understood as worship directed ultimately to Vishnu, and many of its mantras are incorporated into daily Vaishnava temple liturgy and personal worship (*sandhyavandana*).

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Purusha Sukta
The Purusha Sukta describes the cosmic sacrifice of the Supreme Person (Purusha) from whose body the entire universe emanates. Sri Vaishnavas identify this Purusha with Narayana and read the hymn as revealing that the universe is the body of God — a central doctrine of Ramanuja's Vishishtadvaita.

### Temple Liturgy
The Yajur Veda's ritual formulas form the backbone of daily temple worship (*arcana*) in Vaishnava temples. The Sri Vaishnava tradition, in particular, preserves Yajurvedic mantras in its *agama*-based worship alongside the Tamil Prabandham.

## Theological Emphases

- **Narayana as Yajna-Purusha**: The Lord of sacrifice is Vishnu himself; all offerings reach him
- **Ashvamedha and Rajasuya**: Royal rituals prefigure Vishnu's cosmic sovereignty
- **Sarira-Sariri**: Ramanuja's body-soul analogy draws on the Yajur Veda's vision of cosmic interconnection
- **Liturgical Continuity**: Living Vaishnava worship preserves Yajurvedic ritual in adapted form
