Vaishnavas understand the Pravargya and supplementary rites as expressions of the comprehensive nature of Vishnu's grace, which addresses every dimension of human need. The Pravargya's symbolism of the blazing sun restored to wholeness is read as the Lord's power to restore and renew creation, while the supplementary rites' attention to purification, protection, and blessing reflects the many aspects of Narayana's care for his devotees.

The completeness of the supplementary rites — covering every possible contingency and need — mirrors the Vaishnava understanding of the Lord's complete (*purna*) nature. Nothing falls outside his concern; no human need is too small for his attention. The Yajurvedic formulas for these ancillary ceremonies thus embody the Vaishnava teaching that the Lord's grace encompasses every dimension of existence.
