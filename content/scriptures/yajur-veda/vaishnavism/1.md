Vaishnavas understand the Darsha-Purnamasa rites as worship offered to Vishnu-Narayana through the medium of fire. The grain cakes and milk oblations prescribed in the Yajur Veda's formulas are offerings to the Lord who is the ultimate recipient of all sacrifice, as Krishna declares in the Gita: 'I am the ritual, I am the sacrifice, I am the offering' (9.16). The twice-monthly rhythm of these rites establishes a pattern of regular divine service.

The Vaishnava reading emphasizes that the efficacy of the Darsha-Purnamasa depends not only on ritual precision but on the devotional intention (*bhava*) of the sacrificer. The formulas are not magical incantations but prayers addressed to a personal God who responds to the sincerity of worship. This devotional interpretation of Vedic sacrifice bridges the gap between the Yajur Veda's ritual formalism and the bhakti tradition.
