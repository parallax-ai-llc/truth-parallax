Vaishnavas interpret the Agnichayana fire altar as a material representation of Vishnu's cosmic body. The 10,800 bricks arranged in the shape of a falcon evoke Garuda, Vishnu's divine eagle mount, and the five layers of the altar correspond to the five manifestations of the Lord in the Pancharatra theological system. The construction of the altar becomes an act of creating a dwelling for the Lord in the sacrificial space.

The Purusha Sukta's vision of the cosmic person from whose body the universe emanates provides the theological framework for understanding the Agnichayana: each brick, each measurement, each geometric relation is an element of the Lord's cosmic body being reassembled in the ritual space. The Adhvaryu's Yajurvedic formulas sanctify this construction as an act of loving devotion that makes the Lord present among his worshippers.
