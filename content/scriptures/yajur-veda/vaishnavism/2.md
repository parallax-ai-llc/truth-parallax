Vaishnavas read the Soma sacrifice formulas as the most elaborate expression of Vedic worship directed to Vishnu-Narayana. The identification of Soma with Vishnu is ancient: several Rig Vedic hymns describe Vishnu and Soma in parallel terms, and the Sri Vaishnava tradition identifies the Soma offering as an act of supreme devotion to the Lord who is himself the essence of the sacred plant's transformative power.

The Soma sacrifice's complexity and grandeur reflect the cosmic scale of Vishnu's sovereignty. Just as the ritual requires the coordinated effort of multiple priests performing their roles with perfect precision, the cosmos itself functions through the Lord's orchestration of innumerable beings and forces. The Yajurvedic formulas thus celebrate Narayana's cosmic governance through the medium of ritual performance.
