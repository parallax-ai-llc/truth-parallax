The Agnichayana fire altar construction is interpreted by Advaitins as the supreme symbolic act of the ritual section of the Veda. The building of the cosmos in microcosm through precisely arranged bricks represents the Advaitic insight that the universe is a structured appearance (*nama-rupa*) superimposed on the formless Brahman. The construction reveals the order inherent in reality, while the Upanishadic teaching reveals that this order is ultimately illusory (*maya*).

The Shatapatha Brahmana's extensive commentary on the Agnichayana — identifying each brick with a cosmic element, each layer with a world — demonstrates the Vedic mind's capacity for systematic symbolic thought. Advaitins see this capacity as the very faculty that, when turned inward, leads to the recognition that the entire cosmic structure exists within and as consciousness.
