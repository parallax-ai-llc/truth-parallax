The Advaita reading of the Soma sacrifice formulas emphasizes the Chandogya and Brihadaranyaka Upanishads' reinterpretation of the Soma ritual as a metaphor for the soul's journey toward liberation. The pressing of Soma represents the discipline of self-inquiry, the filtering represents the removal of ignorance, and the offering represents the final dissolution of the individual ego into Brahman.

Shankara's commentary on the Brihadaranyaka identifies the 'honey' (*madhu*) of the Soma offering with the bliss (*ananda*) of Brahman-realization. The elaborate Soma sacrifice thus serves a pedagogical purpose in the Advaitic framework: it trains the mind in concentration, precision, and the understanding of symbolic correspondence — skills that are later redirected toward the contemplation of non-dual truth.
