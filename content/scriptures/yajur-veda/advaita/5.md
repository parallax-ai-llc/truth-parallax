Advaitins read the Pravargya and supplementary rites as the final refinements of ritual practice before the aspirant is ready for the leap to pure knowledge. The Pravargya's symbolism — the restoration of the sun's head, the attainment of the blazing light — is interpreted as pointing to the dawn of self-knowledge that illuminates the darkness of ignorance and reveals the self's true nature as luminous awareness.

The supplementary rites' attention to completeness and correction reflects the Advaitic emphasis on thorough preparation: just as no sacrifice is complete without its ancillary ceremonies, no aspirant is ready for *jnana* without the complete purification that comes from disciplined ritual practice, ethical living, and the cultivation of the six treasures (*shat-sampatti*) of tranquility, restraint, withdrawal, endurance, faith, and concentration.
