The Advaita interpretation of the royal rituals finds in the Rajasuya and Ashvamedha not merely political ceremonies but allegories of the soul's sovereignty over the inner kingdom. The true Rajasuya is the self-knowledge that establishes the *atman* as the sovereign lord of all experience, while the true Ashvamedha is the sacrifice of the ego-self that roams freely through the world of desire.

The Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (1.1) opens with a meditation on the Ashvamedha that transforms the horse sacrifice into a cosmic vision: the horse's head is the dawn, its eye is the sun, its breath is the wind. This Upanishadic allegorization exemplifies the Advaitic method of using the ritual as a gateway to the recognition that the sacrifice, the sacrificer, and the cosmos are all manifestations of one Brahman.
