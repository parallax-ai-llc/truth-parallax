## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reader approaches the Right Ginza as a cosmogonic text that speaks to universal questions: the origin of the world, the nature of good and evil, and the purpose of human life. The Mandaean creation narrative, with its vision of a luminous source from which all being emanates, resonates with mystical traditions across religions — from Kabbalistic *Ein Sof* to the Neoplatonic One to the Buddhist concept of *dharmakaya*.

The ethical teachings of the Right Ginza — compassion, truthfulness, ritual purity, and care for the natural world — offer common ground for interreligious dialogue, even as their cosmological grounding in light-darkness dualism represents a distinctive theological perspective. Interfaith practitioners find in the Right Ginza an invitation to consider how different religious traditions have addressed the fundamental human experience of alienation from the divine and the longing for spiritual homecoming.
