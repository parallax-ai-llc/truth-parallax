## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reader finds in the Left Ginza a profound meditation on death, the soul's destiny, and the community's responsibility to its departed members. The Mandaean conviction that the living can assist the dead through prayer and ritual resonates across religious boundaries — from the Catholic Mass for the dead to Buddhist memorial rites to Hindu ancestral offerings.

The Left Ginza's eschatology raises universal questions that animate interfaith dialogue: What happens after death? Can the living help the dead? Is death a liberation or a loss? The Mandaean answer — that death is the soul's homecoming to the World of Light, assisted by the prayers and rituals of the community — offers a distinctive voice in the global conversation about mortality, hope, and the bonds that connect the living and the dead.
