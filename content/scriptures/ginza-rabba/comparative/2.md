## Comparative Reading

Comparative readers of the Left Ginza discover a rich eschatological tradition that invites cross-cultural analysis. The soul's post-mortem journey through heavenly stations finds parallels in the Tibetan *Bardo Thodol*, the Egyptian *Book of the Dead*, the Islamic *mi'raj* literature, and the Gnostic ascent narratives of the Nag Hammadi texts.

The Left Ginza's prayers for the dead resonate with Catholic prayers for souls in purgatory, Buddhist transfer-of-merit practices, and Hindu *shraddha* rituals — all traditions in which the living actively assist the departed in their post-mortem journey. The Mandaean insistence that proper ritual observance is necessary for the soul's successful ascent raises comparative questions about the relationship between ritual, community, and individual salvation across religious traditions.
