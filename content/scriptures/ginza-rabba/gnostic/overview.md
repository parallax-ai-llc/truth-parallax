## Overview

The Gnostic reading of the Ginza Rabba emphasizes its elaborate cosmogonic mythology, its dualistic theology, and its soteriology of knowledge (*gnosis* or, in Mandaic, *manda*). For scholars of Gnosticism, the Ginza Rabba is one of the most important primary sources available — a text that has been continuously transmitted within a living Gnostic community, unlike the buried libraries of Nag Hammadi or the fragmentary Manichaean texts.

The Right Ginza presents a cosmogony in which the World of Light, presided over by the Great Life, is opposed by the forces of darkness led by the demiurgic figure Ptahil and the dark mother Ruha. Human souls are sparks of light trapped in material bodies, and salvation consists in awakening to one's true luminous nature through knowledge, ritual, and moral conduct. This framework aligns closely with the classic Gnostic pattern identified by Hans Jonas and other scholars, while also displaying unique Mandaean elaborations.
