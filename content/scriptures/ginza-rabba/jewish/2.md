## Jewish Reading

Jewish scholars approach the Left Ginza with interest in its eschatological vision and its relationship to Jewish afterlife beliefs. The soul-ascent tradition preserved in the Left Ginza shares features with the *hekhalot* (heavenly palace) literature of rabbinic Judaism, in which mystics ascend through celestial chambers, encountering angelic guardians who demand passwords and liturgical knowledge.

The Mandaean prayers for the dead invite comparison with Jewish mourning practices, particularly the recitation of *Kaddish* and the belief in the soul's post-mortem purification. While mainstream rabbinic Judaism developed its eschatological teachings in different directions — emphasizing resurrection of the dead and the World to Come — the Left Ginza preserves a vision of the afterlife that may reflect currents of Second Temple Jewish thought not fully represented in the rabbinic canon.
