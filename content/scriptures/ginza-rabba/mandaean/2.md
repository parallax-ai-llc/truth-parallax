## Mandaean Reading

The Left Ginza holds special sacred significance for the Mandaean community as the liturgical text that accompanies the soul on its journey after death. When a Mandaean dies, the priest performs the *masiqta* — the elaborate death ceremony that may last three days or more — reciting passages from the Left Ginza to guide the departed soul through the heavenly checkpoints (*matartas*) where it must answer to celestial guardians.

The Left Ginza's prayers and hymns express the Mandaean belief that death is not an end but a liberation of the soul from the prison of the body. The *nišimta* ascends through the celestial spheres, shedding the impurities accumulated during earthly life, until it reaches the World of Light and is reunited with its divine source. The community's recitation of these prayers is understood as a collective act of spiritual assistance — the living helping the dead complete their final journey. The Left Ginza thus functions as both eschatological teaching and pastoral care.
