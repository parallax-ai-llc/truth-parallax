## Overview

The Mandaean reading of the Ginza Rabba is the living heart of Mandaean religious practice. For the Mandaean community, this text is not merely literature but divine revelation from the World of Light, transmitted through celestial messengers (*uthras*) and preserved by an unbroken chain of priestly transmission. Every major Mandaean ritual — from the weekly *masbuta* (baptism) to the elaborate *masiqta* (death mass) — draws its liturgical language and theological authority from the Ginza Rabba.

The Mandaean priest (*tarmida* or *ganzibra*) is the custodian of this text, trained through years of apprenticeship to recite its passages correctly during ceremonies. The Right Ginza provides the cosmological framework within which all ritual action makes sense: creation proceeds from the Great Life (*Hiia Rbia*) through emanations of light, and human beings are called to align themselves with this light through moral purity, ritual observance, and knowledge (*manda*). The Left Ginza accompanies the soul on its post-mortem journey, with prayers designed to ease its passage through the heavenly checkpoints (*matartas*) and into the World of Light.
