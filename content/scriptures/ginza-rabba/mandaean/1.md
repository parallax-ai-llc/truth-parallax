## Mandaean Reading

The Mandaean community receives the Right Ginza as the foundational revelation of the World of Light. This section establishes the cosmological order that undergirds all Mandaean ritual and ethical life. The creation narrative begins with the Great Life (*Hiia Rbia*), the supreme, unknowable source of all light and goodness, from whom emanate the *uthras* — celestial beings of light who serve as intermediaries between the divine realm and the material world.

The Right Ginza describes how the material world came into being through the actions of the demiurgic figure Ptahil, acting under the influence of the dark powers, particularly Ruha (the spirit of chaos and deception). Human beings, though created in material form, carry within them a spark of the divine light — the *nišimta* (soul) — which longs to return to its source in the World of Light. The ethical teachings of the Right Ginza flow from this cosmology: to live rightly is to nurture the light within, to practice ritual purity through *masbuta*, and to resist the temptations of the material world and the forces of darkness. Mandaean priests recite passages from this section during baptismal ceremonies, weddings, and community gatherings.
