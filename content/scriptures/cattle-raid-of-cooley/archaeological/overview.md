## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Táin connect the epic's narrative to the material culture of Iron Age Ireland. The text's descriptions of weapons (swords, spears, chariots), fortifications (the royal site of Emain Macha/Navan Fort), social organization (the warrior aristocracy, the institution of fosterage), and ritual practices provide data that can be compared with archaeological evidence. Sites mentioned in the Táin — including Emain Macha, Cruachan, and various ford-crossing points — have been identified and excavated, providing material context for the literary narrative.
