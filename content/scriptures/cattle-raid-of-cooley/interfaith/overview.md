## Overview

The Táin preserves elements of pre-Christian Celtic warrior religion within its heroic narrative. Cú Chulainn's supernatural transformation (ríastrad), his relationship with the war goddess Morrígan, the prophetic role of druids and seers, and the sacral significance of cattle all reflect religious concepts predating the Christianization of Ireland. Interfaith analysis explores these pre-Christian elements in dialogue with warrior traditions in other cultures, considers the spiritual dimensions of heroism and sacrifice, and examines how the medieval Christian scribes who recorded the tale negotiated with its pagan content.
