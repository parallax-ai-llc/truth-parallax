---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 2"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Their least valuable possessions were brought first, their pails and their cauldrons, their tubs and their iron vessels, their jugs and their wash-pails. Then were brought their finger-rings and their clasps, their brooches and their golden ornaments, their apparel of every hue.

**2** In all these things, they were found to be equal, each of them in regard to the other. Their flocks of sheep were driven from the fields and the green on every side, and these were found to be equal and alike.

**3** Their great horses and steeds were brought from pasture, and these were equal. Their herds of swine were driven from woods and glens and waste places, and these were equal. Their droves of cattle and their oxen were brought, and these too were equal.

**4** But among the kine of Medb there was a great bull, and he had been a calf of one of Medb's cows, and his name was Finnbennach, the White-Horned. But he, deeming it unworthy of him to be in a woman's possession, had gone over to the herds of Ailill.

**5** And Medb could find in all her herds no bull to equal him. And it was as if she possessed not a penny's worth, for she held it to be nothing if she had not a bull as great as Ailill's.

**6** Then Medb summoned her chief messenger, Mac Roth, and bade him find where in all Ireland there might be a bull to equal or surpass Finnbennach. Mac Roth said: "I know where such a bull is: in the province of Ulster, in the territory of Cooley, in the house of Daire mac Fiachna. The bull is called the Donn Cuailnge, the Brown Bull of Cooley."
