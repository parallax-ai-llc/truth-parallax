---
title: "Táin Bó Cúailnge - Chapter 17"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 17
---

**1** Once upon a time it befell Ailill and Medb that, when their royal bed had been prepared for them in Rath Cruachan in Connacht, they spoke together as they lay on their pillow.

**2** "In truth, O woman," spoke Ailill, "she is a well-off woman that is the wife of a man of substance."

**3** "True, indeed," spoke the woman; "but wherefore speakest thou so?"

**4** "For this reason," answered Ailill, "that thou art better off today than the day I took thee to wife."

**5** "I was well-off before my union with thee," spoke Medb.

**6** "Thy wealth was such as I had not heard nor known of," answered Ailill; "but a woman's wealth was all thou hadst, and the foes of neighbouring provinces were wont to carry off the spoil and the booty that they took from thee."

**7** "Not so was it," spoke Medb; "for my father was in the high-kingship of Ireland, and I was the noblest and the most distinguished of his six daughters."

**8** Then were their lowliest of chattels brought to them that they might know which of them had the more goods and riches and treasures. Their pails and their cauldrons and their iron-wrought vessels were fetched to them, and their rings and bracelets and thumb-rings and their golden treasures.
