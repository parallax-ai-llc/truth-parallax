---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 3"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Medb sent Mac Roth with nine messengers to Daire mac Fiachna, to ask for the loan of the Donn Cuailnge for a year, and in return she would give fifty heifers, and a portion of the fine Plain of Ai, and a chariot worth thrice seven bondmaids, and her own friendly thighs on top of that.

**2** Daire was pleased with the offer and agreed to lend the bull. But that night, Daire's servants overheard the messengers of Medb boasting that if Daire had not given the bull willingly, they would have taken it by force.

**3** When Daire heard this, he was enraged. He said: "I swear by the gods my people swear by, unless they carry it off by force, they shall not take it by fair means!"

**4** The messengers returned to Cruachan and told Medb of Daire's refusal. Medb said: "There is no need to polish knobs and smooth edges about it. It was known it would be taken by force if it were not given willingly. And taken it shall be."

**5** Then Medb and Ailill mustered the men of Connacht and their allies: the men of Leinster, the men of Munster, and the exiles of Ulster led by Fergus mac Roich, who had left Ulster in the time of the treachery against the sons of Usnech.

**6** Thus began the great hosting of Connacht against the province of Ulster, for the sake of the Brown Bull of Cooley.
