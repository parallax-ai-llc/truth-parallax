---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 1"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Once upon a time, it befell Ailill and Medb that, when their royal bed had been prepared for them in Rath Cruachan in Connacht, they spoke together as they lay upon their pillow.

**2** "It is true what they say, O woman," said Ailill. "She is a well-off woman who is the wife of a wealthy man."

**3** "She is indeed," said Medb. "But why do you say so?"

**4** "I say it," said Ailill, "because you are better off today than the day I married you."

**5** "I was well off before I ever saw you," said Medb.

**6** "It was a wealth we had not heard of and did not know about," said Ailill. "You were but a woman of property, and enemies from neighboring lands were carrying off spoils and plunder from you."

**7** "Not so was I," said Medb. "My father was the High King of Ireland, Eochaid Feidlech mac Finn. I was the noblest and worthiest of his six daughters. I surpassed them all in grace and giving, in battle and combat."

**8** Then Ailill and Medb had their possessions brought before them, so that each might know the other's wealth, their household goods, their jewels and ornaments, their flocks and herds.
