---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 5"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Cuchulainn began his defense of Ulster at the ford. He cut a forked branch of oak with a single stroke of his sword, and drove it into the middle of the stream with one thrust of his hand, so that no chariot could pass it.

**2** On the fork he set the heads of four warriors whom he had slain, one head upon each prong, and this was a warning to the hosts of Connacht.

**3** Fergus mac Roich, who knew Cuchulainn well, warned the armies: "Beware of this youth. He is Cuchulainn, the Hound of Culann, and there is no warrior in Ireland who can stand against him."

**4** Medb sent warriors one by one to the ford to fight Cuchulainn in single combat, for that was the custom of the ford: one man against one man. And one by one, Cuchulainn slew them all.

**5** He slew Fraech mac Fidaig, who came with his bright sword. He slew Orlam, son of Ailill and Medb, and set his head on a stake.

**6** Each day new champions came, and each day Cuchulainn met them at the ford. Each night he slept little, for the Morrigan came to him in dreams, and the cold of the winter bit hard.

**7** And the hosts of Connacht began to fear the young warrior of Ulster, who held the ford alone against them all.
