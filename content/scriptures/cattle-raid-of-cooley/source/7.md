title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 7"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Then Medb sent for Ferdiad mac Daman, who was Cuchulainn's foster-brother. They had trained together under the warrior-woman Scathach in Alba, and they had been the closest of companions.

**2** Ferdiad did not wish to fight Cuchulainn, for he loved him. But Medb promised him great rewards: the hand of her daughter Finnabair, and much land, and freedom from tribute. And her satirists threatened to satirize him if he refused.

**3** When Cuchulainn saw Ferdiad at the ford, his heart was heavy, and he said: "Welcome would your coming be, Ferdiad, were it not under these terms. But now I do not bid you welcome. Why have you come to fight your foster-brother?"

**4** Ferdiad answered: "I have come because I must. It is not fair that I should fall because of you, O Cuchulainn, Hound of the beautiful feats."

**5** They fought at the ford for three days. Each day they fought with different weapons: on the first day, with casting weapons; on the second, with broad spears and stabbing weapons; on the third, with heavy, smiting weapons.

**6** Each evening they ceased their combat and shared food and herbs. Their horses were in the same paddock and their charioteers at the same fire, for their love endured even in battle.
