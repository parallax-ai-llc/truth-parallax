---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 4"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Now at this time, the men of Ulster lay in their pangs, because of the curse of Macha. For Macha, who was with child, had been forced to race against the king's horses, and she had won the race but had cried out in her birth-pangs:

**2** "A curse upon the men of Ulster! In the hour of your greatest need, the pangs of a woman in childbirth shall seize upon you, and for five days and four nights you shall be as weak as a woman in labor."

**3** And this curse fell upon all the men of Ulster except Cuchulainn, for he was not of Ulster blood but was the son of Lugh of the Long Arm, of the Tuatha De Danann.

**4** Cuchulainn alone stood between the armies of Connacht and the province of Ulster. He was then but seventeen years of age, yet he was the greatest warrior in all Ireland.

**5** When word came to Cuchulainn that the hosting of Connacht was coming, he went forth with his charioteer Laeg and stationed himself at the ford of the river, which was the entrance to Ulster.

**6** And Cuchulainn said: "While I have life, no enemy shall pass this ford into the province of Ulster."
