---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 9"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 9
---

**1** After the death of Ferdiad, Cuchulainn lay wounded and exhausted. His body was covered with wounds from head to foot, and there was not a part of him without a wound.

**2** Then Lugh of the Long Arm, his father from the Tuatha De Danann, came to him in the night. And Lugh said: "Sleep now, Cuchulainn, by the grave-mound of Lerga, and I will watch over the ford for three days and three nights."

**3** And Lugh put herbs of healing upon every wound, and he sang songs of power over him, so that Cuchulainn slept and was healed.

**4** Meanwhile, the boy-troop of Emain Macha heard of Cuchulainn's plight. One hundred and fifty boys marched out from Emain to aid him. Three times they attacked the hosts of Connacht, and three times their number fell.

**5** And all the boy-troop was slain, and not one of them escaped. When Cuchulainn awoke and learned what had happened, he was seized with the battle-fury, the riastrad.

**6** He mounted his chariot, and the first distortion came upon him. His body shook and trembled, and every joint and limb and organ trembled within him. His features became a red bowl of fury.

**7** One eye sank into his head so that a crane could scarcely reach it; the other protruded onto his cheek. His mouth was twisted to his ear. The hero-light rose from his brow. And in this terrible form, he drove into the hosts of Connacht.
