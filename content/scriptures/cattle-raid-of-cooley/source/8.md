---
title: "Cattle Raid of Cooley Section 8"
scripture: "cattle-raid-of-cooley"
chapter: 8
---

**1** On the fourth day, Cuchulainn and Ferdiad fought in the ford itself, in the deepest water. They fought with every feat of arms they knew.

**2** The combat was fierce and long, and both warriors were torn and bloodied. But at last Cuchulainn called for the Gae Bolga, the terrible barbed spear that Scathach had given him alone.

**3** Laeg, his charioteer, sent the Gae Bolga down the stream to Cuchulainn, and Cuchulainn cast it with his foot at Ferdiad. The spear entered Ferdiad's body and filled every joint and every limb with its barbs.

**4** Ferdiad fell, and Cuchulainn caught him and carried him to the north side of the ford, that Ferdiad might die on the Ulster side rather than on the Connacht side.

**5** And Cuchulainn lamented over Ferdiad: "Alas, O golden brooch, O Ferdiad! O strong striker, thy death weighs on me. Our last meeting has been sorrowful. You are dead, and I must remain, living and lamenting."

**6** "Yesterday he was greater than a mountain; today there is nothing of him but his shadow. O Ferdiad, it was sad for you that you did not take the counsel of any of those who knew me well."

**7** Then Cuchulainn fell beside Ferdiad in a swoon of grief, and Laeg said: "Rise up, Cuchulainn, for the hosts of Connacht will not wait for you to recover." But Cuchulainn could not rise for a time, so great was his sorrow.
