## Overview

The Táin invites comparison with the great heroic epics of other Indo-European traditions. Cú Chulainn parallels Achilles (the young, preternaturally gifted warrior who fights alone while his comrades are incapacitated), Arjuna (the supreme warrior facing a moral crisis), and Siegfried (the dragon-slaying hero of Germanic tradition). The cattle raid as a narrative framework has parallels in Vedic mythology (the recovery of stolen cattle being a core Indo-European mythological motif). The text's treatment of heroic fury, single combat, and the tragic costs of war resonates across epic traditions worldwide.
