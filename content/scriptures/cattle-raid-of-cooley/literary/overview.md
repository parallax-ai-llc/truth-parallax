## Overview

The Táin is the supreme achievement of medieval Irish prose narrative. Its literary power derives from the combination of epic scope with intimate psychological portraiture, hyperbolically heroic action with moments of tender humanity, mythological wonder with earthy humor. The text employs a distinctive narrative technique — prose interspersed with verse passages, flashbacks embedded within the main narrative, catalogs and set-pieces alternating with dramatic dialogue. Literary analysis examines the Táin's narrative structure, characterization, prose style, and its place in the tradition of European epic.
