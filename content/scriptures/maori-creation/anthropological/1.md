The first cycle describes Te Kore — the Void, the Nothingness, the realm of potential being that precedes all creation. In Maori cosmology, Te Kore is not simply emptiness but a pregnant nothingness, a realm of limitless potential from which all existence will eventually emerge. Different tribal traditions enumerate various stages or levels of Te Kore (sometimes up to twelve), each representing a progressive intensification of potential, from the void-of-nothing to the void-of-seeking to the void-of-becoming.

Anthropological study of Te Kore: The Void and Potential examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
