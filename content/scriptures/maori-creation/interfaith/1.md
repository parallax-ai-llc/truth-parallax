The first cycle describes Te Kore — the Void, the Nothingness, the realm of potential being that precedes all creation. In Maori cosmology, Te Kore is not simply emptiness but a pregnant nothingness, a realm of limitless potential from which all existence will eventually emerge. Different tribal traditions enumerate various stages or levels of Te Kore (sometimes up to twelve), each representing a progressive intensification of potential, from the void-of-nothing to the void-of-seeking to the void-of-becoming.

Interfaith engagement with Te Kore: The Void and Potential offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
