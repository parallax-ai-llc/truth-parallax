The fifth cycle recounts Tane's creation of the first woman, Hineahuone, from the red earth of Kurawaka. Tane breathes life into her and she becomes his wife, bearing a daughter, Hinetitama (Dawn Maiden). When Hinetitama discovers that Tane is both her father and husband, she flees in shame to the underworld and transforms into Hine-nui-te-po, the goddess of death. She declares that she will pull their descendants into the darkness, establishing the reality of human mortality.

Interfaith engagement with The Creation of Humanity offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
