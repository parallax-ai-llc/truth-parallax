## Overview

Interfaith engagement with Maori creation narratives offers rich opportunities for dialogue on creation, the nature of the divine, the origin of mortality, and the relationship between human beings and the natural world. The Maori concept of whakapapa — the genealogical interconnection of all things — provides a distinctive framework for understanding the kinship of all creation that resonates with ecological and relational theologies in many traditions.

The progression from Te Kore to Te Ao Marama invites comparison with other traditions' accounts of creation from nothing (creatio ex nihilo) while offering a distinctively Polynesian understanding of creation as the actualization of potential rather than the imposition of form on formless matter. The figure of Maui as a trickster hero who challenges divine order invites dialogue about the role of human agency, creativity, and even transgression in the divine plan.
