The sixth cycle tells the exploits of Maui-tikitiki-a-Taranga, the great culture hero and trickster of Polynesian tradition. Born prematurely and cast into the sea, Maui is raised by his ancestor Tama-nui-ki-te-rangi. His exploits include fishing up the North Island of New Zealand (Te Ika a Maui), slowing the sun to lengthen the day, obtaining fire from the underworld goddess Mahuika, and his final, fatal attempt to conquer death by entering the body of Hine-nui-te-po — confirming human mortality established in the previous cycle.

Interfaith engagement with Maui and the Shaping of the World offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
