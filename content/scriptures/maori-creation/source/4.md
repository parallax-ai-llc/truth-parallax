---
title: "Maori Creation - Chapter 4"
scripture: "maori-creation"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Then Tane-mahuta, having separated his parents and filled the world with light, desired to create a being in the likeness of the gods.

**2** He went to the sacred red earth at Kurawaka, and there he shaped with his hands the form of a woman from the earth.

**3** He breathed life into her nostrils, and she sneezed, and breathed, and opened her eyes. This was Hine-ahu-one, the Earth-formed Maid.

**4** And Tane took Hine-ahu-one as his wife, and she bore a daughter, Hine-titama, the Dawn Maid, who was exceedingly beautiful.

**5** And Tane also took Hine-titama as his wife, for she did not know that Tane was her father, and she bore children to him.

**6** But one day Hine-titama asked: "Who is my father?" And when she was told that Tane himself was both her father and her husband, she was overcome with shame and grief.

**7** She cried out: "The threads of my life are woven in darkness!" And she fled from Tane, fled to the underworld, to the realm of Rarohenga.

**8** Tane pursued her to the entrance of the underworld, calling to her to return. But she turned and said unto him:

**9** "Go back, Tane. Go back to the world of light, and raise our children there. Let me remain here in the darkness."

**10** "I shall stay below to gather in our children. Go you back, and push them toward me. I shall remain here in Te Po, the night, the darkness."

**11** And so Hine-titama became Hine-nui-te-po, the Great Woman of the Night, the goddess of death. She dwells in the underworld and receives the spirits of the dead.

**12** And from that day, the children of Tane — all mortal beings — are born into the world of light, but in death they descend to Hine-nui-te-po, who gathers them into the darkness.

**13** Thus was death brought into the world, and thus the cycle of life and death was established.
