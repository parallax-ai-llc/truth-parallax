---
title: "Maori Creation - Chapter 2"
scripture: "maori-creation"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Rangi the Sky Father and Papa the Earth Mother lay close together, and between them were their children confined in darkness.

**2** And the children of Rangi and Papa were many: Tane-mahuta, god of forests and birds; Tu-matauenga, god of war and man; Tangaroa, god of the sea and fish; Rongo-ma-tane, god of cultivated plants; Haumia-tiketike, god of uncultivated food; and Tawhirimatea, god of storms.

**3** These children dwelt in darkness between their parents, for Rangi lay upon Papa, and there was no light.

**4** And the children conspired together, saying: "Let us determine what shall be done with Rangi and Papa. Shall we slay them, or shall we separate them?"

**5** Then Tu-matauenga, the fiercest of the children, said: "Let us slay them."

**6** But Tane-mahuta spoke: "Nay, not so. It is better to rend them apart, and to let the heaven stand far above us, and the earth lie under our feet. Let the sky become as a stranger to us, but the earth remain close to us as our nursing mother."

**7** And the brothers agreed, save Tawhirimatea, god of storms, who grieved at the thought of his parents' separation.

**8** Then Rongo-ma-tane arose and strove to separate the heaven from the earth; but he was unable to rend them apart. And Tangaroa arose and strove; but he could not. Then Haumia-tiketike arose and strove; but in vain.

**9** Then Tu-matauenga arose and strove; but he, too, failed.

**10** Then at last, slowly, Tane-mahuta, the god of the forests, arose. He paused, and his head was pressed firmly against his mother the earth, and his feet were raised up against his father the sky.

**11** He strained his back and his limbs with a mighty effort. Far, far beneath him he pressed the earth down. Far, far above him he thrust the sky.

**12** And Rangi and Papa cried out in their pain: "Wherefore do you wish to slay your parents? Why do you wish to separate us?"

**13** But Tane heeded not their cries. He pressed and pressed, and the sky was rent from the earth. And the light rushed in, and creatures were made visible who had been hidden between the bodies of Rangi and Papa.

**14** Then Tawhirimatea, god of storms, was angry that his parents had been torn apart, and he followed his father Rangi to the sky.
