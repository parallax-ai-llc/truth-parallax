---
title: "Maori Creation - Chapter 3"
scripture: "maori-creation"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Tawhirimatea from his dwelling in the sky waged war upon his brothers. He sent forth his children: Winds of the West, Winds of the South, Winds of the East, and Winds of the North; fierce squalls, whirlwinds, dense clouds, massive clouds, dark clouds, gloomy thick clouds, fiery clouds, clouds which precede hurricanes, clouds of thunder-storms.

**2** And these fell upon the forests of Tane-mahuta. They snapped the trunks of the trees, tore them to pieces, and left them on the ground, food for insects and rot.

**3** Then Tawhirimatea turned upon Tangaroa, god of the sea. And the sea rose in mighty waves, and the waters were tossed about, great and terrible.

**4** And Tangaroa's children were frightened. Ika-tere, the father of fish, fled into the sea. Tu-te-wehiwehi, the father of reptiles, fled to the forests of Tane.

**5** And Tangaroa was wroth with Tane because Tane sheltered the deserter Tu-te-wehiwehi; and since that day Tangaroa has ever waged war on Tane, overwhelming his forests with the sea in floods.

**6** But Tane supplies men with canoes and with nets to capture the children of Tangaroa; and thus the gods remain at enmity.

**7** Then Tawhirimatea turned upon his brothers Rongo-ma-tane and Haumia-tiketike. But Papa, the Earth Mother, snatched them away and hid them within herself, so that Tawhirimatea sought them in vain.

**8** Then Tawhirimatea attacked Tu-matauenga, the god of fierce human beings. But Tu stood firm. He was the only one of the brothers who had not fled before the god of storms.

**9** Tu stood upright and unshaken upon the breast of his mother Earth. At last the heart of the god of storms grew calm, and his power was spent.

**10** Then Tu-matauenga, the god of war and of man, became angry with his brothers, because they had not aided him in the battle with Tawhirimatea.

**11** So Tu turned upon each of his brothers in turn: he snared the birds of Tane; he caught the fish of Tangaroa; he dug up the roots of Rongo and the fern-roots of Haumia.

**12** Thus Tu-matauenga devoured them all, as payment for their cowardice, and they became his food. And man, the descendant of Tu, has from that day been the consumer of all things upon the earth.

**13** Only Tawhirimatea was not vanquished by Tu; and so it is that storms and winds and hurricanes still assail man, destroying his canoes and his houses, and man is ever at the mercy of the tempest.
