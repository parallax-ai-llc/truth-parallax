The first cycle describes Te Kore — the Void, the Nothingness, the realm of potential being that precedes all creation. In Maori cosmology, Te Kore is not simply emptiness but a pregnant nothingness, a realm of limitless potential from which all existence will eventually emerge. Different tribal traditions enumerate various stages or levels of Te Kore (sometimes up to twelve), each representing a progressive intensification of potential, from the void-of-nothing to the void-of-seeking to the void-of-becoming.

From an Indigenous perspective, Te Kore: The Void and Potential carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
