## Overview

The Maori creation narratives are not historical curiosities but living foundations of Maori identity, spirituality, and social practice. Whakapapa (genealogy) remains the fundamental organizing principle of Maori society, and the creation narratives provide the ultimate genealogical framework from which all whakapapa descends. Every Maori person can trace their descent through specific lines back to the Ancestral canoes, through the gods, and ultimately to the cosmic origins described in the creation narratives.

Indigenous engagement with these narratives emphasizes their continuing role in tikanga Maori (Maori customary practice), including the protocols of the marae (meeting ground), the karakia (prayers and incantations) that invoke Ancestral power, and the whaikorero (oratory) that situates every significant occasion within the genealogical and cosmological framework established by the creation narratives.
