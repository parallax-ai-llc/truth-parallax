The fifth cycle recounts Tane's creation of the first woman, Hineahuone, from the red earth of Kurawaka. Tane breathes life into her and she becomes his wife, bearing a daughter, Hinetitama (Dawn Maiden). When Hinetitama discovers that Tane is both her father and husband, she flees in shame to the underworld and transforms into Hine-nui-te-po, the goddess of death. She declares that she will pull their descendants into the darkness, establishing the reality of human mortality.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Creation of Humanity carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
