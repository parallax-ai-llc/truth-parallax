## Overview

Academic study of Maori creation narratives has a long history, beginning with early European ethnographers (Grey, Best, Smith) and continuing through contemporary Maori and non-Maori scholars. Key areas of scholarly inquiry include the reconstruction of pre-contact cosmological traditions from post-contact sources, the relationship between different tribal versions of the creation narratives, the debate over the antiquity and authenticity of the Io tradition (the supreme being), and the literary and philosophical analysis of the narratives.

The Io debate is particularly significant: some scholars argue that Io-matua-kore (the parentless supreme being) represents an ancient esoteric tradition, while others contend that the Io concept was influenced by Christian monotheism introduced by missionaries. This debate has implications for understanding the relationship between Indigenous and colonial knowledge systems.
