The first cycle describes Te Kore — the Void, the Nothingness, the realm of potential being that precedes all creation. In Maori cosmology, Te Kore is not simply emptiness but a pregnant nothingness, a realm of limitless potential from which all existence will eventually emerge. Different tribal traditions enumerate various stages or levels of Te Kore (sometimes up to twelve), each representing a progressive intensification of potential, from the void-of-nothing to the void-of-seeking to the void-of-becoming.

Academic analysis of Te Kore: The Void and Potential draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
