## Overview

Comparative analysis of Maori creation narratives reveals deep connections with other Polynesian traditions — the separation of Sky Father and Earth Mother is found across Polynesia, as are the figures of Tane, Tangaroa, and Maui. These shared elements reflect the common Austronesian heritage of Polynesian peoples and allow reconstruction of ancestral narratives predating the settlement of New Zealand.

Beyond the Polynesian context, the separation of heaven and earth finds parallels in many traditions: the Egyptian separation of Nut (sky) and Geb (earth), the Mesopotamian splitting of Tiamat, and similar narratives in African, East Asian, and other traditions. The Maui trickster cycle connects to trickster traditions worldwide, while the origin of death through Hine-nui-te-po raises universal questions about mortality that are addressed by creation narratives across cultures.
