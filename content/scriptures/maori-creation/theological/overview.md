## Overview

Theological engagement with Maori creation narratives raises profound questions about the nature of creation from potentiality (Te Kore), the relationship between separation and generation, the theological meaning of mortality, and the nature of divine-human kinship. The progression from Te Kore (Void) through Te Po (Darkness) to Te Ao Marama (World of Light) presents a distinctive theology of creation as the actualization of potential through progressive stages of becoming.

The separation of Rangi and Papa raises theological questions about the relationship between unity and diversity, between the primordial wholeness and the differentiated world of experience. The origin of death through Hinetitama's transformation into Hine-nui-te-po presents a theology of mortality rooted in the consequences of transgressed boundaries, inviting comparison with other traditions' explanations of death's origin.
