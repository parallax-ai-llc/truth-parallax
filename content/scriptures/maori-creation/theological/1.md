The first cycle describes Te Kore — the Void, the Nothingness, the realm of potential being that precedes all creation. In Maori cosmology, Te Kore is not simply emptiness but a pregnant nothingness, a realm of limitless potential from which all existence will eventually emerge. Different tribal traditions enumerate various stages or levels of Te Kore (sometimes up to twelve), each representing a progressive intensification of potential, from the void-of-nothing to the void-of-seeking to the void-of-becoming.

Theological engagement with Te Kore: The Void and Potential raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order. This section's themes invite reflection on fundamental theological questions that transcend any single tradition while being rooted in the specific spiritual vision of this particular culture.

The theological concepts embedded in this section — whether explicit or implicit — offer distinctive perspectives on questions that occupy theologians across traditions: the nature of creation, the relationship between the human and the divine, the meaning of suffering and transformation, and the foundations of moral order.
