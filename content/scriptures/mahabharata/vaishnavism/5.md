The Udyoga Parva chronicles the final diplomatic efforts to prevent the Kurukshetra War, centering on Krishna's embassy to the Kaurava court. For Vaishnavas, this parva is profoundly significant because it reveals Krishna acting simultaneously as compassionate peacemaker and sovereign Lord who already knows the war is inevitable. Krishna's peace mission demonstrates that God exhausts every avenue of mercy before permitting destruction — yet His omniscience ensures that dharma will ultimately prevail.

Krishna's appearance before the Kaurava assembly is a theological watershed. When Duryodhana foolishly attempts to bind Krishna, the Lord reveals His cosmic form (vishvarupa), filling the assembly hall with the blazing effulgence of innumerable universes. This pre-Gita theophany establishes Krishna's divine sovereignty before the war even begins. Vaishnava commentators note that this revelation offers the Kauravas a final chance for repentance — a chance they reject, sealing their doom through their own free choice.

The Udyoga Parva also contains Krishna's famous declaration to Karna, revealing Karna's true identity as Kunti's eldest son. Vaishnava interpreters see Krishna's offer to make Karna king of the Pandavas as an expression of divine impartiality — the Lord offers grace even to those aligned against His devotees. Karna's refusal, motivated by loyalty to Duryodhana, illustrates how attachment to the wrong cause, however noble in human terms, leads to destruction when it opposes divine will. The parva's conclusion — armies marshaled, alliances formed — confirms that when adharma refuses correction, the Lord sanctions righteous war.
