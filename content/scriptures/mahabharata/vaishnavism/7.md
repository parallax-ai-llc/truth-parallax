The Drona Parva covers days eleven through fifteen of the Kurukshetra War, with Drona assuming command of the Kaurava forces after Bhishma's fall. For Vaishnavas, this parva intensifies the epic's central theological tension: how does divine righteousness operate within the brutal realities of war? The death of Abhimanyu — Arjuna's young son trapped and killed in the Chakravyuha formation — is one of the epic's most heartrending episodes, and Vaishnava commentators approach it as a test of faith in Krishna's ultimate justice.

Abhimanyu's heroic death, surrounded and killed by six maharathis violating all rules of fair combat, challenges the devotee's understanding of divine protection. Vaishnava interpreters explain that Abhimanyu, as a partial incarnation of the moon god, fulfilled his cosmic purpose through his sacrifice. His death also serves Krishna's larger plan by igniting Arjuna's resolve to destroy Jayadratha, removing a key Kaurava commander. The Lord's plan operates on a scale invisible to human perception — what appears as tragedy is part of a larger divine tapestry.

The parva's most controversial episode is the killing of Drona through Yudhishthira's half-truth — stating "Ashwatthama is dead" (referring to an elephant, not Drona's son). Krishna directly orchestrates this deception, and Vaishnava commentators defend it by arguing that the Lord's commands transcend conventional morality. Madhva emphasizes that Drona, despite his personal righteousness, chose the side of adharma and thus fell under the jurisdiction of divine justice. Krishna's willingness to employ unconventional means reveals that protecting dharma sometimes requires actions that shock ordinary moral sensibilities but serve a higher transcendent purpose.
