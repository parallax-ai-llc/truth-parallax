The Drona Parva's intensifying warfare is read by Dvaita Vedanta as the arena in which Vishnu's cosmic plan unfolds with mathematical precision. Madhva's Tatparya Nirnaya provides detailed accounts of battles that the standard text only summarizes, revealing hidden theological significance in combat encounters. For Madhva, every duel, every stratagem, and every death on the Kurukshetra battlefield serves Vishnu's predetermined plan for the destruction of demonic forces and the restoration of dharmic order.

Abhimanyu's death, while deeply grievous, is explained in Dvaita theology through the doctrine of divine sovereignty. Vishnu permits suffering even among His devotees' families because His governance of the cosmos operates according to principles that transcend individual human welfare. Abhimanyu's sacrifice generates the karmic force that empowers Arjuna's subsequent destruction of Jayadratha and contributes to the Pandavas' eventual victory. Madhva insists that Abhimanyu, as a righteous soul, attains an exalted celestial status after death — his suffering is temporary while his reward is eternal.

The killing of Drona through Yudhishthira's half-truth is defended by Madhva with a distinctive Dvaita argument: Krishna, as the Supreme Lord, possesses absolute authority over truth and dharma. His command supersedes all conventional moral rules because He alone comprehends the total moral structure of the cosmos. Madhva goes further than other commentators in defending every controversial action sanctioned by Krishna, arguing that questioning God's decisions reflects the arrogance of finite minds attempting to judge infinite wisdom. Drona's death, commanded by Krishna, is therefore not merely excusable but positively righteous — a direct expression of divine justice.
