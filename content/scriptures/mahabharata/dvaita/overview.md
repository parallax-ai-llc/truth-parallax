## Overview

Madhvacharya's Dvaita (dualist) Vedanta provides one of the most systematic and distinctive readings of the Mahabharata. In his *Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya* ("Determination of the True Import of the Mahabharata"), Madhva argues that the epic's primary purpose is to establish the supremacy of Vishnu (identified with Krishna and Narayana) and to reveal a hierarchical ordering of reality (*taratamya*) in which God, souls, and matter remain eternally distinct.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya
Madhva's magnum opus on the epic presents a complete retelling that highlights hidden theological meanings. He identifies Bhima as the incarnation of Vayu (the wind god) and the foremost devotee, argues that the epic encodes a celestial hierarchy, and resolves apparent contradictions through a sophisticated hermeneutic that distinguishes primary and secondary meanings.

### The Hierarchy of Beings
Madhva reads the epic's characters as instantiating a cosmic hierarchy: Vishnu is supreme, followed by Lakshmi, Brahma, Vayu (Bhima), and other deities in descending order. The Pandavas' ultimate victory confirms the triumph of those aligned with Vishnu's will.

## Theological Emphases

- **Vishnu's Absolute Supremacy**: Krishna's actions throughout the epic confirm His position as the one supreme God
- **Bhima as Ideal Devotee**: Madhva elevates Bhima above Arjuna as the epic's central devotee-hero
- **Eternal Real Distinctions**: The soul's individuality persists eternally — liberation is not merger but eternal service
- **Encoded Meaning**: The epic contains multiple layers of meaning accessible only through proper interpretation
