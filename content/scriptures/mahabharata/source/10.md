---
title: "Mahabharata Sauptika Parva (Book 10)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Ashvatthama, Kripa, and Kritavarma — the only three surviving warriors of the Kaurava army — came upon Duryodhana lying broken on the battlefield. Ashvatthama was consumed with rage and grief.

**2** Ashvatthama swore a terrible oath: "O Duryodhana, before the night is over, I shall slay the Pandavas and all their allies. I shall bring you their heads as an offering. This I swear by all that is sacred."

**3** In the darkness of night, as the Pandava camp slept, Ashvatthama beheld an owl attacking sleeping crows in a great tree. Thus inspired by this act of nocturnal slaughter, he resolved to attack the sleeping camp.

**4** At the gate of the camp, Ashvatthama encountered a terrible being of gigantic form, blazing with fire. It was a manifestation of Lord Shiva, guarding the camp. Ashvatthama could not pass until he worshipped Shiva and offered himself as a sacrifice.

**5** Shiva, pleased with his devotion, entered into Ashvatthama and granted him supernatural power. With this divine fury upon him, Ashvatthama entered the sleeping camp of the Pandavas.

**6** Ashvatthama first slew Dhrishtadyumna, crushing him to death with his bare hands and feet. Dhrishtadyumna cried out: "Let me die with weapons in my hands, like a warrior!" But Ashvatthama showed no mercy.

**7** Then Ashvatthama slew the five sons of Draupadi as they slept, mistaking them for the five Pandavas. He also slew Shikhandi and many other warriors. Kripa and Kritavarma set fire to the camp from all sides, and those who fled the tents were cut down.

**8** In a single night, the entire Pandava camp was destroyed. All the warriors who survived eighteen days of battle perished in their sleep. Only the five Pandavas, Krishna, Satyaki, and Yuyutsu survived, for they had been sleeping elsewhere.

**9** When Draupadi learned that all five of her sons had been murdered in their sleep, her grief was beyond measure. She demanded that Arjuna bring her the jewel from Ashvatthama's forehead, without which he could not live.

**10** Arjuna pursued Ashvatthama and found him hiding among the sages near the banks of the Ganga. In desperation, Ashvatthama released the Brahmastra, the most terrible of all weapons, directing it at the womb of Uttara to destroy the last heir of the Pandava line.

**11** Arjuna countered with his own Brahmastra, and Krishna commanded both to withdraw their weapons. Arjuna obeyed, but Ashvatthama, not knowing how to recall the weapon, redirected it at Uttara's unborn child.

**12** Krishna declared: "The child in Uttara's womb shall be born dead, but I shall revive him with my own power. And you, Ashvatthama, for this most heinous of crimes — attacking the unborn — shall wander the earth for three thousand years, alone, afflicted with diseases, with no companion and no shelter." The jewel was torn from his forehead, and he was cast out, cursed to immortal suffering.
