---
title: "Mahabharata Bhishma Parva (Book 6)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 6
---

**1** On the field of Kurukshetra, the two great armies stood arrayed against each other. Conch shells were blown, drums thundered, and the earth trembled beneath the weight of warriors, horses, chariots, and elephants.

**2** Arjuna, seeing his grandsires, teachers, uncles, brothers, sons, and friends standing in both armies, was overcome with grief. He said to Krishna: "O Govinda, my limbs fail me, my mouth is parched, my body trembles, and Gandiva slips from my hand."

**3** Arjuna said: "I do not desire victory, O Krishna, nor kingdom, nor pleasures. What is the use of a kingdom obtained by the slaughter of our own kinsmen? I shall not fight." So saying, he cast aside his bow and arrows and sat down in his chariot, his heart overwhelmed with sorrow.

**4** The Blessed Lord said: "Whence has come upon thee this dejection in this hour of crisis? It is disgraceful, contrary to honor, and an obstacle to the attaining of heaven. Yield not to unmanliness, O Arjuna. It does not become thee."

**5** The Blessed Lord said: "The wise grieve neither for the living nor for the dead. Never was there a time when I did not exist, nor you, nor these rulers of men. Nor shall we ever cease to be hereafter."

**6** "As the embodied soul continuously passes, in this body, from childhood to youth to old age, so too does the soul pass into another body at death. The wise are not deluded by this."

**7** "The contacts of the senses with their objects, O son of Kunti, give rise to cold and heat, pleasure and pain. They come and go and are impermanent. Endure them bravely, O Bharata."

**8** "That which pervades the entire body, know it to be indestructible. No one is able to destroy the imperishable soul. These bodies of the embodied soul are said to have an end, but the soul itself is eternal, indestructible, and immeasurable."

**9** "Just as a man casts off worn-out garments and puts on new ones, so the embodied soul casts off worn-out bodies and enters into others that are new."

**10** "Weapons cannot cleave the soul, fire cannot burn it, water cannot wet it, and wind cannot dry it. This soul is uncleavable, incombustible, insoluble, and cannot be dried. It is eternal, all-pervading, stable, immovable, and ancient."

**11** "Considering also your own dharma, you should not waver. For a Kshatriya, there is nothing more meritorious than a righteous war. Happy are the Kshatriyas who obtain such a war, unsought, as an open gate to heaven."

**12** "You have a right to action alone, never to its fruits. Let not the fruits of action be your motive, nor let your attachment be to inaction. Steadfast in yoga, O Arjuna, perform actions, abandoning attachment, remaining the same in success and failure. This evenness of mind is called yoga."

**13** "When a man abandons all the desires of his heart and is satisfied in the Self by the Self alone, then he is said to be one of steady wisdom. He whose mind is not shaken by adversity, who does not long for pleasures, and who is free from attachment, fear, and anger — he is called a sage of steady wisdom."

**14** Bhishma was appointed the commander-in-chief of the Kaurava army. For ten days he fought with matchless valor, slaying thousands upon thousands of Pandava warriors. None could withstand the might of his arrows.

**15** On the tenth day, following Krishna's counsel, the Pandavas placed Shikhandi before Arjuna's chariot. Bhishma, recognizing Shikhandi as having been born a woman, lowered his weapons. Arjuna then pierced Bhishma with countless arrows until the grandsire fell from his chariot onto a bed of arrows.

**16** Bhishma, lying upon his bed of arrows, said: "O Pandavas, I do not blame you. This is the working of Time. Let hostilities cease for now. Bring me water and a pillow." Arjuna shot three arrows into the earth, creating a rest for his head, and water sprang forth to cool his burning body.
