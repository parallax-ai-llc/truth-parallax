---
title: "Mahabharata Mahaprasthanika Parva (Book 17)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 17
---

**1** After the departure of Krishna, the Pandavas lost all desire for the world. Yudhishthira installed Parikshit, the grandson of Arjuna, upon the throne of Hastinapura and prepared for the great journey.

**2** The five Pandavas, accompanied by Draupadi and a dog, set out on their final journey toward the north, toward the great mountain Meru, renouncing everything they possessed. They walked in single file, never looking back.

**3** As they journeyed across the earth, Draupadi was the first to fall. Bhima asked: "O Yudhishthira, why has the princess of Panchala, who never departed from virtue, fallen upon the road?"

**4** Yudhishthira replied without turning back: "She was partial to Arjuna among her five husbands, though she should have loved all equally. For this partiality, she has fallen." And he walked on.

**5** Sahadeva fell next. Yudhishthira said: "He was proud of his wisdom and believed none could equal him in learning. For this pride, he has fallen." And they walked on.

**6** Nakula fell. Yudhishthira said: "He believed there was none equal to him in beauty and was vain of his appearance. For this vanity, he has fallen."

**7** The mighty Arjuna fell. Bhima cried out in anguish. Yudhishthira said: "He boasted that he could consume all his enemies in a single day and was proud of his skill. He did not always fulfill his promises. For this, he has fallen."

**8** Then Bhima himself fell. He called out: "O king, tell me why I, who served you faithfully, have fallen." Yudhishthira said: "You were a glutton and boasted of your strength without regard for others. For this, you have fallen."

**9** Yudhishthira walked on alone, with only the dog following him. He did not weep, he did not look back. Step by step, he climbed toward the summit of Mount Meru, toward the gates of heaven.

**10** Indra appeared with his celestial chariot and said: "O Yudhishthira, ascend. You have earned the right to enter heaven in your mortal body. Of all the Pandavas, you alone have walked the entire path without falling."

**11** Yudhishthira said: "This dog has been my faithful companion. It has followed me when all others fell. I will not enter heaven without it." Indra replied: "There is no place for dogs in heaven. Abandon it and enter."

**12** Yudhishthira refused: "I cannot abandon one who is devoted to me, who has followed me in my darkest hour. To abandon the faithful is a sin equal to slaying a Brahmin. I will not enter heaven without this dog."

**13** The dog then revealed itself as Dharma, the god of righteousness and Yudhishthira's celestial father. He said: "O my son, you have been tested and found true. Your compassion for this humble creature is the final proof of your virtue. Enter heaven."
