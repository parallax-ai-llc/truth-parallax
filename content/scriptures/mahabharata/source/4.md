---
title: "Mahabharata Virata Parva (Book 4)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The twelve years of forest exile having passed, the Pandavas entered upon their thirteenth year, which was to be spent in concealment. If discovered, they would be bound to repeat the exile for another twelve years.

**2** Yudhishthira said: "We shall go to the kingdom of Virata, the king of Matsya. There we shall assume disguises and live among his servants for one year."

**3** Yudhishthira assumed the disguise of a Brahmin named Kanka, skilled in the game of dice. He offered his services to King Virata as a courtier and companion. Thus the king of dharma lived as a humble attendant.

**4** Bhima disguised himself as a cook named Ballava. He entered the royal kitchen and prepared food for King Virata. None suspected that beneath the cook's attire was the mightiest warrior among men.

**5** Arjuna, by a curse of the Apsara Urvashi, had been transformed to live as a eunuch for one year. He took the name Brihannala and became a teacher of music and dance to Princess Uttara in the inner chambers of the palace.

**6** Nakula served as the keeper of the king's horses, taking the name Granthika. Sahadeva became the keeper of the king's cattle, calling himself Tantipala. Both brothers concealed their royal bearing beneath humble service.

**7** Draupadi disguised herself as a maid named Sairandhri, a hairdresser, and entered the service of Queen Sudeshna. She declared that she was protected by five Gandharva husbands who would slay any man who wronged her.

**8** Kichaka, the commander of Virata's armies and the queen's brother, became infatuated with Draupadi. He pursued her relentlessly. Draupadi, unable to reveal her identity, sought the help of Bhima.

**9** Bhima, in the darkness of the dancing hall at night, seized Kichaka and slew him with his bare hands, crushing his body into a shapeless mass. The death of Kichaka caused terror throughout the kingdom, for he had been considered invincible.

**10** When the year of concealment was nearly complete, Duryodhana's spies reported that Kichaka had been slain by extraordinary strength. Suspecting the Pandavas were hiding in Virata's kingdom, the Kauravas invaded Matsya and seized the royal cattle.

**11** Young Prince Uttara boasted that he would single-handedly repel the Kaurava army. But when he saw the vast host arrayed with Bhishma, Drona, and Karna, his courage failed. He turned to flee from the chariot.

**12** Brihannala, who was driving Uttara's chariot, revealed himself as Arjuna. He retrieved his weapons from where they had been hidden in a Shami tree and said: "Fear not, O prince. Today you shall witness the might of Arjuna's bow, Gandiva."

**13** Arjuna single-handedly routed the entire Kaurava army. He used the Sammohana weapon to put the warriors to sleep, and he defeated Bhishma, Drona, Karna, and Ashvatthama in succession. He stripped their outer garments as trophies for Uttara.

**14** When King Virata learned the true identity of his servants, he was filled with wonder and joy. He offered his daughter Uttara in marriage to Arjuna, but Arjuna, having been her teacher, suggested she wed his son Abhimanyu instead. And so it was agreed.
