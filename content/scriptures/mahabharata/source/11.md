---
title: "Mahabharata Stri Parva (Book 11)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 11
---

**1** After the war, the Pandavas entered Hastinapura. The city was filled with mourning. The women of the Kuru household — widows, mothers, and daughters — came forth wailing, their hair unbound, their ornaments cast aside.

**2** Gandhari, the mother of the hundred slain Kauravas, came before Krishna with eyes blazing with grief and fury. She said: "O Krishna, you had the power to prevent this war. You could have stopped this slaughter. Why did you allow my sons to perish?"

**3** Gandhari cursed Krishna: "As I have watched my own race destroyed before my eyes, so shall your race, the Yadavas, destroy themselves in a fratricidal conflict. And you, Krishna, shall die alone in the wilderness, slain by a common hunter."

**4** Krishna accepted the curse calmly. He said: "O queen, I know this to be true. The Yadavas are already destined for destruction. Your curse shall hasten what fate has already decreed. I accept it."

**5** Dhritarashtra, blind and grief-stricken, summoned the Pandavas. He embraced Yudhishthira and Bhima. When Bhima stepped forward, Krishna, knowing that Dhritarashtra harbored murderous rage, pushed forward an iron statue of Bhima instead. Dhritarashtra crushed the statue to powder with his embrace.

**6** Dhritarashtra, realizing what he had done, wept with remorse. Krishna said: "O king, do not grieve. It is not Bhima who was crushed, but an iron image. Forgive your nephews, for the war was not of their choosing."

**7** Kunti revealed to her sons the secret of Karna's birth. Yudhishthira, learning that he had unknowingly caused the death of his eldest brother, was overcome with grief. He said: "The greatest sorrow of this war is not the loss of a kingdom but the death of Karna, my own brother, by the hands of his own brothers."

**8** The women of the Kaurava household went to the battlefield of Kurukshetra. There they found the bodies of their husbands, sons, fathers, and brothers. Gandhari walked among the fallen, recognizing each of her hundred sons.

**9** Gandhari, seeing the mangled body of Duryodhana, cried out: "O my son, you who commanded eleven armies, you lie here on the bare earth, covered with blood and dust. Where are your royal robes? Where is your golden crown?"

**10** Draupadi mourned over the bodies of her five sons, slain by Ashvatthama in the night. She cried: "O my children, you who were princes, you who should have ruled kingdoms, you lie here cut down in your youth. What mother can bear such grief?"

**11** Yudhishthira performed the funeral rites for all the fallen warriors — both Pandava and Kaurava. He offered water to Karna, honoring him as his eldest brother. The pyres burned for many days, and the smoke darkened the sky over Kurukshetra.

**12** Vidura spoke to Dhritarashtra: "O king, do not drown in grief. All beings who are born must die. This is the eternal law. The wise do not mourn for what cannot be changed. Fix your mind on dharma and seek liberation."
