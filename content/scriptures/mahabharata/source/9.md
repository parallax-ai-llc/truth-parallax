---
title: "Mahabharata Shalya Parva (Book 9)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 9
---

**1** After the fall of Karna, Shalya, the king of Madra, was appointed the last commander of the Kaurava army. Though he served the Kauravas by obligation, he was a warrior of immense strength and skill.

**2** Shalya arranged the remnants of the Kaurava army in the formation known as the Sarvatobhadra, the array that is auspicious on all sides. On the eighteenth day, he led his forces with desperate valor.

**3** Yudhishthira himself engaged Shalya in combat. Though Shalya was his uncle by marriage, dharma demanded that they fight. The duel was fierce, and both warriors displayed extraordinary skill with weapons.

**4** Yudhishthira hurled a mighty javelin at Shalya with all his strength. The weapon, blazing like the fire at the end of the world, pierced Shalya through the chest. The king of Madra fell from his chariot like a great tree uprooted by a storm.

**5** With the fall of Shalya, the Kaurava army lost all hope. The surviving warriors fled in every direction, and Duryodhana, seeing his last commander slain and his army destroyed, fled from the battlefield alone.

**6** Duryodhana, weary and wounded, entered a lake and by his magical powers lay concealed beneath its waters. He declared: "I have lost everything — my brothers, my friends, my army. Let me rest here. The earth holds no more joy for me."

**7** The Pandavas tracked Duryodhana to the lake. Yudhishthira called out: "O Duryodhana, you who commanded eleven great divisions, how can you hide in a lake like a coward? Come out and fight like the Kshatriya you claim to be."

**8** Stung by these words, Duryodhana emerged from the lake. He said: "I shall fight all five of you, one at a time. I choose the mace as my weapon, for in mace combat I have no equal."

**9** Bhima stepped forward to face Duryodhana. Krishna said: "O Bhima, remember your oath. Remember Draupadi's humiliation. Remember the wax house, the poisoned food, the exile. Let your mace drink the blood of this sinful wretch."

**10** The mace fight between Bhima and Duryodhana raged with terrible fury. Both warriors were disciples of Balarama and equally matched in skill. They circled each other like two great bulls, their iron maces crashing together with the sound of thunderbolts.

**11** As the combat continued without resolution, Krishna hinted to Arjuna, who signaled to Bhima by slapping his own thigh. Understanding the message, Bhima struck Duryodhana upon the thighs, shattering them with his mace.

**12** Duryodhana fell to the ground. Balarama, who had arrived to witness the combat, was enraged at the foul blow, for striking below the waist was forbidden in mace combat. He raised his plough weapon to attack Bhima, but Krishna restrained him.

**13** Duryodhana, lying broken on the ground, cried out: "I have lived as a king, I have been generous, I have fought bravely. You have won by trickery and unfair means. But I die a hero's death and shall go to heaven, while you shall live in a kingdom of ashes."
