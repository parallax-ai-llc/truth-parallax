---
title: "Mahabharata Anushasana Parva (Book 13)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 13
---

**1** Bhishma continued his instructions to Yudhishthira on the bed of arrows. He said: "O king, I shall now teach you the duties of charity, the rules of conduct, and the mysteries of dharma as revealed by the ancient sages."

**2** "The gift of food is the greatest of all gifts. Food is life, food is strength, food is the foundation of all existence. He who feeds the hungry attains the highest merit. There is no charity equal to the gift of food."

**3** "The gift of knowledge is superior even to the gift of food, for knowledge liberates the soul from the cycle of birth and death. He who teaches the Vedas, who imparts wisdom to the ignorant, attains a merit that is inexhaustible."

**4** "The cow is the most sacred of all animals. She is the mother of the world, for from her flows the milk that nourishes all creatures. He who protects cows, who serves them and honors them, attains the highest worlds."

**5** Bhishma taught the Vishnu Sahasranama, the thousand names of Lord Vishnu. He said: "He who recites these thousand names with devotion is freed from all sins, attains all desires, and at death reaches the supreme abode of Vishnu."

**6** "Vasudeva is the Supreme Being, the creator, the preserver, and the destroyer. He is the beginning, the middle, and the end of all things. He is the soul of all souls, the light of all lights."

**7** "Among the names of Vishnu: He is Vishvam, the universe itself. He is Vishnu, the all-pervading. He is Vashatkara, the sacred syllable. He is Bhuta-Bhavya-Bhavat-Prabhu, the lord of past, present, and future."

**8** On the duties of women, Bhishma said: "A virtuous wife is the greatest blessing a man can possess. She is the light of the home, the foundation of the family, and the pillar of dharma. By serving her husband with devotion, she attains the highest heaven."

**9** On the rules of conduct, Bhishma taught: "One should never injure others by deeds, words, or thoughts. One should always speak the truth. One should never covet the wealth of others. One should practice self-restraint and compassion."

**10** "The five great sins are: the killing of a Brahmin, the drinking of intoxicating liquor, theft, adultery with the wife of one's teacher, and association with those who commit these sins. These five sins lead to the lowest hells."

**11** "But even the greatest sinner can be redeemed through repentance, austerity, and devotion. The grace of the Lord purifies all sins, as fire purifies gold. No one is beyond redemption who sincerely seeks the divine."

**12** Bhishma, having completed his vast instructions spanning the Shanti and Anushasana Parvas, prepared for death. He said: "O Yudhishthira, I have taught you all that I know. Now the sun turns northward, and the auspicious hour has come. I shall depart from this body."
