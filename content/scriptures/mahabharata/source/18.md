---
title: "Mahabharata Svargarohana Parva (Book 18)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 18
---

**1** Yudhishthira ascended to heaven in Indra's chariot. But when he arrived, he found Duryodhana seated on a celestial throne, blazing with glory. The sight filled Yudhishthira with indignation.

**2** Yudhishthira said: "I will not remain in a heaven where Duryodhana dwells! Where are my brothers? Where is Draupadi? Where is Karna? I desire to be where they are, not where this wicked king holds court."

**3** The celestial messenger said: "O king, Duryodhana attained heaven through his death on the battlefield as a Kshatriya. All enmity ends at death. Here there is no hatred, no envy, only the reward of merit."

**4** Yudhishthira demanded to see his brothers. He was led through dark and terrible regions filled with suffering. He heard the voices of Bhima, Arjuna, Nakula, Sahadeva, Draupadi, and Karna crying out from the darkness: "O Yudhishthira, stay with us! Your presence brings us comfort."

**5** Yudhishthira, filled with fury, said to the messenger: "Go tell the gods that I shall remain here with my brothers and my wife. I reject a heaven where Duryodhana sits in splendor while my righteous brothers suffer in hell."

**6** At that moment, the illusion vanished. The darkness dissolved, the cries of suffering ceased, and a fragrant wind blew. Indra and Dharma appeared before Yudhishthira, and all was light.

**7** Dharma said: "O my son, this was your final test. Every king must see hell, even if only for a moment. You chose to remain with your brothers in suffering rather than dwell in heaven alone. This is the mark of the truly righteous."

**8** Indra said: "All your brothers and Draupadi are here in heaven. What you witnessed was maya, illusion. Duryodhana's time in heaven is limited, earned by his warrior's death. But your brothers dwell here eternally, by the merit of their lives."

**9** Yudhishthira then beheld his brothers in their divine forms — Bhima radiant with the splendor of Vayu, Arjuna shining with the glory of Indra, Nakula and Sahadeva in the beauty of the Ashvins, and Draupadi adorned with celestial garments.

**10** He saw Karna blazing like the sun, united at last with his brothers. He saw Bhishma in the realm of the Vasus. He saw Drona merged with Brihaspati. He saw Abhimanyu among the stars. Every warrior who fell at Kurukshetra had attained the world appointed by his deeds.

**11** Yudhishthira, bathing in the celestial Ganga, shed his mortal body and all its suffering. Freed from anger, grief, and every earthly attachment, he assumed his divine form and took his place among the gods.

**12** Thus ends the Mahabharata, the great epic composed by Vyasa. He declared: "What is found here may be found elsewhere. What is not found here is found nowhere. This history contains the essence of dharma, artha, kama, and moksha — righteousness, wealth, desire, and liberation."

**13** Vyasa said: "With uplifted arms I cry, but none heeds me. From dharma come wealth and pleasure. Why then is dharma not pursued? One should not abandon dharma for the sake of pleasure, nor for the sake of fear, nor even for the sake of one's life. Dharma is eternal. Pleasure and pain are not eternal."
