---
title: "Mahabharata Ashramvasika Parva (Book 15)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Fifteen years after the great war, Dhritarashtra, Gandhari, and Kunti resolved to retire to the forest. Dhritarashtra said: "I have lived long enough in this palace of sorrow. My sons are dead, my kingdom belongs to another. Let me spend my remaining days in austerity and prayer."

**2** Yudhishthira grieved at their departure. He said: "O uncle, this kingdom is yours. Command me as you will. Do not leave us." But Dhritarashtra was firm in his resolve, and Vidura accompanied them to the forest.

**3** In the forest hermitage, the aged king and queens practiced severe austerities. They lived on roots, fruits, and water, sleeping on the bare ground, and spending their days in meditation and worship.

**4** Vidura, the wisest of the Kurus, practiced the most severe penances. His body wasted away until he was nothing but skin and bones. One day, as Yudhishthira visited the hermitage, Vidura walked into the forest and merged his life force into Yudhishthira.

**5** Vyasa, through his yogic power, summoned the spirits of all the fallen warriors for one night. From the waters of the Ganga emerged Bhishma, Drona, Karna, Abhimanyu, the hundred sons of Dhritarashtra, Draupadi's five sons, and all the others who had perished.

**6** That night, the living and the dead were reunited. Dhritarashtra embraced his sons. Kunti spoke with Karna. Draupadi saw her children once more. All enmity was dissolved, and there was only love and peace among them.

**7** When dawn came, the spirits returned to the river and disappeared. Vyasa said: "O king, these warriors have all attained the heavenly realms according to their merit. Do not grieve for them. They are beyond suffering."

**8** Two years after their retirement, a great forest fire swept through the hermitage. Dhritarashtra, Gandhari, and Kunti, too weak to flee and unwilling to abandon their austerities, sat in meditation and allowed the flames to consume their bodies. They attained heaven through this final sacrifice.

**9** Narada brought the news to Yudhishthira. He said: "O king, your uncle, your aunt, and your mother have left their bodies in the fire of the forest. But grieve not, for they have attained the highest realms through their austerities and devotion."

**10** Yudhishthira performed the funeral rites for the three departed elders with great ceremony. The entire kingdom mourned, for Dhritarashtra, despite his weaknesses, had been the patriarch of the family, and Kunti had been the mother of the Pandavas.
