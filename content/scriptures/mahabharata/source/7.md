---
title: "Mahabharata Drona Parva (Book 7)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 7
---

**1** After the fall of Bhishma, Dronacharya was appointed commander of the Kaurava forces. Duryodhana said: "O preceptor, you are the greatest of warriors. Lead our army and bring us victory over the Pandavas."

**2** Drona arranged his forces in the formation called the Chakravyuha, the discus array, a labyrinthine battle formation that only he and Arjuna among the living knew how to penetrate and escape.

**3** Abhimanyu, the son of Arjuna and Subhadra, knew how to enter the Chakravyuha, for he had learned it while still in his mother's womb. But he knew not how to come out. When Arjuna was drawn away to a distant part of the battlefield, Abhimanyu resolved to enter alone.

**4** Abhimanyu said: "I shall break through this array. Let the other warriors follow close behind me." He charged into the formation with the fury of a young lion, slaying warriors by the thousands with his blazing arrows.

**5** But Jayadratha, by the boon of Lord Shiva, held back the other Pandava warriors at the entrance. Abhimanyu was left alone inside the array, surrounded by the greatest warriors of the Kaurava army: Drona, Karna, Ashvatthama, Kripa, and Duryodhana.

**6** Alone against many, Abhimanyu fought with unmatched valor. When his bow was broken, he fought with his sword. When his sword was shattered, he fought with his chariot wheel. The six great warriors attacked him simultaneously, violating every code of righteous warfare.

**7** Abhimanyu, the noble youth, was slain by the combined assault of those six warriors. His fall caused the heavens to weep and the gods to turn away in sorrow. Even Drona shed tears at the death of so valiant a boy.

**8** When Arjuna learned of his son's death, his grief turned to terrible wrath. He swore an oath: "Before the sun sets tomorrow, I shall slay Jayadratha, who prevented the others from protecting my son. If I fail, I shall enter the fire."

**9** The next day, Krishna guided Arjuna's chariot through the Kaurava army toward Jayadratha. When sunset approached and Arjuna had not reached his target, Krishna created the illusion of darkness by concealing the sun behind his Sudarshana disc.

**10** The Kauravas rejoiced, believing the sun had set and Arjuna had failed his vow. Jayadratha came forward to gloat. At that moment, Krishna revealed the sun once more. Arjuna, with a single shaft, severed Jayadratha's head and sent it flying into the lap of his father, who was meditating nearby.

**11** Drona fought for five days as commander, employing every divine weapon in his arsenal. He slaughtered the Pandava forces without mercy, and none could stand against him in battle.

**12** Krishna devised a stratagem: Bhima slew an elephant named Ashvatthama and Yudhishthira declared, "Ashvatthama is dead" — adding softly "the elephant," which Drona could not hear over the noise of battle. Believing his son was dead, Drona laid down his arms in grief.

**13** Dhrishtadyumna, who had been born from the sacrificial fire for the purpose of slaying Drona, seized his opportunity. He struck off the head of the unarmed Drona as the preceptor sat in meditation, preparing to leave his body. This act, though fulfilling destiny, was condemned by many as contrary to dharma.
