---
title: "Mahabharata Vana Parva (Book 3)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Pandavas, having lost the second game of dice, departed from Hastinapura and entered the great forest. Accompanied by Draupadi and a number of Brahmins, they wandered through the wilderness of Kamyaka.

**2** Yudhishthira lamented: "By my own fault, through the vice of gambling, I have brought ruin upon my brothers, upon Draupadi, and upon myself. The wise say that gambling is the root of all evil, and now I have proved them right."

**3** Draupadi spoke to Yudhishthira with great anger: "O king, it is not through patience and forgiveness that our kingdom will be recovered. The Kauravas understand only force. Your forbearance only emboldens the wicked."

**4** Bhima said: "O Yudhishthira, Draupadi speaks the truth. What is the use of virtue if it cannot protect the virtuous? Let us cast aside this exile and march upon Hastinapura with weapons in our hands."

**5** Yudhishthira replied: "O Bhima, I have given my word, and a promise made must be kept. Truth is the highest dharma. When the thirteen years have passed, if Duryodhana refuses to return our kingdom, then we shall fight."

**6** During their exile, Arjuna undertook a journey to the Himalayas to obtain divine weapons. He performed fierce austerities and pleased Lord Shiva, who appeared to him in the guise of a Kirata, a forest hunter.

**7** Shiva, pleased with Arjuna's valor and devotion, granted him the Pashupata weapon, the most powerful of all arms. Thereafter Indra took Arjuna to heaven, where he obtained weapons from all the gods and learned the arts of celestial music and dance.

**8** In the forest, the sage Brihadasva narrated to Yudhishthira the story of Nala and Damayanti, to show that even the greatest kings have suffered through dice and have recovered their fortunes through patience and virtue.

**9** The sage Markandeya narrated many histories to the Pandavas, including the story of Savitri, who by her devotion and wisdom rescued her husband Satyavan from the grasp of Yama, the lord of death.

**10** Markandeya said: "O Yudhishthira, Savitri followed Yama and through her eloquent speech and steadfast devotion won back the life of her husband. Such is the power of a virtuous wife."

**11** Jayadratha, the king of Sindhu, attempted to abduct Draupadi during the exile. The Pandavas pursued him, defeated his army, and Bhima dragged him before Draupadi. But Yudhishthira spared his life, having him shaved and released in humiliation.

**12** Krishna visited the Pandavas in the forest and consoled them. He said: "O Yudhishthira, do not grieve. Dharma will prevail. The wicked Kauravas shall reap the fruit of their actions. When the time comes, I shall be by your side."
