---
title: "Mahabharata Shanti Parva (Book 12)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Yudhishthira, having won the kingdom, was consumed by remorse for the terrible slaughter. He said: "What use is this kingdom purchased with the blood of my kinsmen? I shall renounce the throne and retire to the forest."

**2** Bhishma, lying upon his bed of arrows, awaiting the auspicious hour of death, summoned Yudhishthira. He said: "O king, it is your duty to rule. A king who abandons his kingdom out of grief sins against all his subjects. Come to me, and I shall instruct you in dharma."

**3** Bhishma taught: "Dharma is subtle and difficult to comprehend. What appears to be dharma may be adharma, and what appears to be adharma may be dharma. One must use discernment and follow the guidance of the wise."

**4** On the duties of a king, Bhishma said: "The king is the foundation of the state. When the king is righteous, the age is Krita. When the king is negligent, the age is Kali. The character of the ruler determines the character of the age."

**5** "A king should be like the sun, drawing up wealth through taxation and returning it as rain through public works and charity. He should never tax his subjects beyond their capacity, nor should he allow his treasury to remain empty."

**6** "Danda, the rod of punishment, is the foundation of all order. Without punishment, the strong would devour the weak, as large fish devour the small. It is the fear of punishment that keeps the world in order."

**7** "But the rod of punishment must be wielded with justice and compassion. A king who punishes too harshly destroys his subjects. A king who punishes too lightly invites contempt. The wise king finds the middle path."

**8** On the science of moksha, Bhishma taught: "The self is eternal, unborn, and undying. It is not slain when the body is slain. It passes from body to body, driven by its karma, until it attains liberation through knowledge."

**9** "The three gunas — sattva, rajas, and tamas — bind the soul to the material world. Sattva binds through attachment to happiness and knowledge, rajas through attachment to action, and tamas through negligence and delusion."

**10** "Liberation comes through the renunciation of the fruits of action, through devotion to the Supreme, and through the knowledge that the individual self and the universal Self are one. This is the highest teaching."

**11** "Non-violence is the highest dharma. Non-violence is the greatest gift. Non-violence is the highest austerity. Non-violence is the greatest sacrifice. All beings desire life and fear death. Knowing this, the wise practice compassion toward all creatures."

**12** "Truth is the eternal Brahman. Truth is the highest austerity. Truth is the highest sacrifice. All things are established in truth. There is nothing higher than truth."

**13** Yudhishthira, having received these teachings from Bhishma, was restored in spirit. He accepted the burden of kingship as his sacred duty, resolved to rule with justice and compassion according to the principles of dharma.
