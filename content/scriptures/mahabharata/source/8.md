---
title: "Mahabharata Karna Parva (Book 8)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 8
---

**1** After the fall of Drona, Karna was appointed the commander of the Kaurava forces. Duryodhana said: "O Karna, you are my dearest friend and the mightiest of archers. Now lead our army and fulfill your promise to slay Arjuna."

**2** Karna said: "O Duryodhana, I shall fight to my last breath for your cause. Today either Arjuna shall fall or I shall fall. The earth shall drink the blood of one of us before the sun sets."

**3** Karna fought with extraordinary prowess, routing the Pandava forces. He defeated Yudhishthira in combat and could have slain him, but remembering his promise to Kunti that he would spare all Pandavas save Arjuna, he let him go with mocking words.

**4** Yudhishthira, humiliated by Karna, quarreled bitterly with Arjuna in the camp. In his anger he cursed Arjuna's bow Gandiva. Krishna intervened and restored peace between the brothers, reminding them of their true enemy.

**5** Shalya, the king of Madra and uncle of the Pandavas, was compelled by circumstance to serve as Karna's charioteer. But following Krishna's secret counsel, he constantly disparaged Karna's abilities and undermined his confidence.

**6** Shalya said to Karna: "O son of a charioteer, you boast much but your deeds are small. Arjuna's skill is born of divine training. You cannot equal him any more than a jackal can equal a lion."

**7** Karna replied: "O Shalya, drive the chariot forward and be silent. The world shall see today whether Karna or Arjuna is the greater warrior. Even the gods themselves shall bear witness to my valor."

**8** The final duel between Karna and Arjuna was the greatest single combat of the entire war. The sky was darkened by their arrows, the earth trembled beneath their chariots, and the gods gathered in the heavens to watch.

**9** During the combat, the wheel of Karna's chariot sank into the earth, fulfilling an ancient curse. Karna descended from his chariot to free the wheel. He called out to Arjuna: "O Arjuna, wait! The codes of warfare forbid you to strike an unarmed and helpless foe."

**10** Krishna said to Arjuna: "O Partha, where was Karna's righteousness when Abhimanyu was slain by six warriors together? Where was his dharma when Draupadi was dragged into the assembly? Strike now, Arjuna, and do not hesitate."

**11** Arjuna, following Krishna's command, released the Anjalika weapon. The great arrow blazed through the air like the sun at the end of an age and struck off Karna's head. Thus fell the greatest of the Kaurava warriors, and the earth mourned his passing.

**12** After Karna's death, Kunti revealed to the Pandavas that Karna had been their eldest brother. Yudhishthira was overcome with grief and cursed all women, saying that henceforth no woman would be able to keep a secret. The Pandavas mourned their brother even as they celebrated their victory.
