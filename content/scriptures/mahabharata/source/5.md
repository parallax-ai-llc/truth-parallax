---
title: "Mahabharata Udyoga Parva (Book 5)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The period of exile having ended, the Pandavas demanded the return of their kingdom. Yudhishthira sent envoys to Dhritarashtra, saying: "We have fulfilled every condition. Let the sons of Dhritarashtra now honor their word and restore Indraprastha."

**2** Duryodhana declared: "I shall not give the Pandavas even as much land as can be covered by the point of a needle. If they desire their kingdom, let them fight for it."

**3** Krishna, seeking to avert war, himself went as an envoy to Hastinapura. He spoke to the assembled Kurus: "O king Dhritarashtra, make peace with the Pandavas. Give them but five villages, one for each brother, and this terrible war may be averted."

**4** Duryodhana refused even five villages. He conspired to capture Krishna in the assembly. But Krishna revealed his divine universal form, blazing with the light of a thousand suns, and the entire court trembled with terror.

**5** Krishna said: "O foolish Duryodhana, you cannot bind me. I am the Supreme Lord, from whom all creation proceeds. You rush toward your own destruction, dragging your kinsmen with you."

**6** Kunti sent a message to Karna through Krishna, revealing that Karna was her firstborn son, born of the Sun god before her marriage to Pandu. She implored him to join his brothers, the Pandavas.

**7** Karna replied: "O mother, I know this truth, but I cannot abandon Duryodhana, who gave me honor when the whole world scorned me. I shall fight against my brothers, but I promise you this: I shall not slay any Pandava save Arjuna. Whether I slay Arjuna or he slays me, you shall still have five sons."

**8** Before the war, both Arjuna and Duryodhana went to Krishna seeking his aid. Duryodhana asked for Krishna's army; Arjuna chose Krishna himself, even unarmed. Krishna agreed to serve as Arjuna's charioteer.

**9** Vidura warned Dhritarashtra: "O king, abandon this wicked son of yours. One family may be sacrificed for the sake of a village; one village for the sake of a country. Duryodhana will bring ruin upon the entire Kuru race."

**10** Dhritarashtra lamented: "I know that where dharma is, there victory will be. The Pandavas are righteous and Krishna is on their side. Yet I cannot control my son. Fate drives us to destruction."

**11** Sanjaya said to Dhritarashtra: "O king, I have seen the army of the Pandavas. It is vast and powerful, led by warriors of matchless valor. And Krishna, the Lord of all, stands with them. What hope have we against the divine will?"

**12** Both sides marshalled their forces. Eleven Akshauhinis assembled under Duryodhana, and seven under Yudhishthira. The great warriors chose their sides, and the stage was set for the most devastating war the world had ever known.
