---
title: "Mahabharata Sabha Parva (Book 2)"
scripture: "mahabharata"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Yudhishthira, having established his rule in Indraprastha, performed the Rajasuya sacrifice, by which he was consecrated as emperor. Kings from all directions came bearing tribute, and the glory of the Pandavas spread across the earth.

**2** Narada said to Yudhishthira: "O king, the hall built by the demon architect Maya is beyond comparison. Neither Indra in heaven nor Yama in his domain possesses such a hall as this."

**3** Maya Danava, the great architect of the Asuras, having been saved by Arjuna from the fire of the Khandava forest, built for Yudhishthira a magnificent assembly hall, adorned with gems, filled with celestial fragrance, extending over ten thousand cubits.

**4** When Duryodhana visited the great assembly hall of Yudhishthira, he was confounded by the illusions of its construction. He mistook solid ground for water and water for solid ground. Falling into a pool, he was humiliated, and Draupadi and the Pandavas laughed.

**5** Duryodhana, burning with shame and jealousy, returned to Hastinapura. He said to Shakuni: "Uncle, I have witnessed the prosperity of the Pandavas and I cannot bear it. Either I shall destroy them or I shall give up my life."

**6** Shakuni, the master of dice, replied: "O Duryodhana, do not grieve. I know the science of dice. No one can defeat me at the game. Invite Yudhishthira to play, and through dice we shall win everything that is his."

**7** Dhritarashtra, though troubled in heart, gave his consent to the game of dice. He sent Vidura to invite the Pandavas. Vidura protested, saying: "O king, I do not approve of this. The game of dice will cause the destruction of the Kuru race."

**8** Yudhishthira, though knowing the dangers, accepted the invitation out of his duty as a Kshatriya. He said: "A challenge cannot be refused by one who follows dharma. I will come to play, though I foresee evil in this."

**9** In the game, Shakuni won throw after throw through deception. Yudhishthira staked and lost his wealth, his kingdom, his brothers, himself, and finally Draupadi. The assembly sat in stunned silence as each wager was lost.

**10** Draupadi was dragged into the assembly hall by Duhshasana. She cried out: "O elders of the Kuru race! If Yudhishthira had already lost himself, how could he stake me? I ask the assembly to judge this question of dharma!"

**11** Duhshasana seized Draupadi by her hair and attempted to disrobe her before the entire court. But by the grace of Lord Krishna, her garment became endless, and Duhshasana could not succeed in his vile attempt.

**12** Draupadi, with tears of rage, pronounced: "These Kauravas who have insulted me in this assembly shall pay with their blood. My hair shall remain unbound until Bhima washes it with the blood of Duhshasana." And Bhima swore a terrible oath to fulfill her vow.

**13** Dhritarashtra, fearing the consequences of the insult to Draupadi, granted her boons and restored the Pandavas' freedom. But a second game of dice was proposed, and the Pandavas were exiled for thirteen years, the last year to be spent in concealment.
