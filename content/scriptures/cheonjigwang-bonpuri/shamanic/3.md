In shamanic performance, Section 3, 'Cheonjiwang's Descent,' is recited as a living ritual act that re-enacts a primordial event at the foundation of the cosmos. The mudang's voice becomes the vehicle through which the original creative acts are made present again in the sacred space of the gut ceremony. Each performance renews the cosmic order that this section describes, maintaining the boundary structures — between heaven and earth, living and dead, human and spirit — that make orderly existence possible.

The dramatic and musical dimensions of this section's performance are inseparable from its meaning. The rhythmic patterns of the janggu drum, the melodic contours of the mudang's chant, and the choreographic movements that accompany the narration all contribute to the ritual re-creation of the primordial events described in the text. Shamanic practitioners understand that the power of the bonpuri lies not in its words alone but in the total performance through which those words become embodied reality.
