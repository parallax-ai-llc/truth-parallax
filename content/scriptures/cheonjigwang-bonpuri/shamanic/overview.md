## Overview

Within the shamanic tradition, the Cheonjigwang Bonpuri is not merely a creation myth but a performative act that re-enacts the original ordering of the cosmos. When a mudang recites this bonpuri during a gut ceremony, she is understood to be re-establishing the fundamental structures of reality — the separation of heaven and earth, the distinction between the living and the dead, the boundary between the human and spirit worlds. The narrative is thus both a story about the past and a ritual act in the present.

## Ritual Function

The Cheonjigwang Bonpuri typically opens the most important gut ceremonies, particularly the Keun Gut (큰굿, "great gut"), establishing the cosmological framework within which all subsequent ritual acts will take place. By narrating the creation of the world, the mudang creates a sacred space in which the boundaries between human and spirit worlds become permeable, enabling the communication and healing that are the gut's central purposes.

## Living Narrative

The shamanic tradition emphasizes that each performance of the Cheonjigwang Bonpuri is unique. While the narrative's essential structure remains stable, individual mudang bring their own vocal artistry, dramatic interpretation, and spiritual power to each recitation. The bonpuri lives not on the page but in the voice of the mudang, the rhythm of the drums, and the presence of the spirits who are summoned by its telling.
