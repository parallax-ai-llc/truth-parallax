Buddhist-Korean interpretation of Section 7, 'The Ordering of the World,' reads this portion of the creation myth through the lens of Buddhist cosmological and soteriological concepts. While the Cheonjigwang Bonpuri predates Buddhism's arrival on the Korean peninsula, centuries of mutual influence have produced versions of the narrative that incorporate Buddhist elements — cosmological frameworks, karmic logic, and devotional references — into its fundamentally shamanic structure.

Buddhist readers find in this section's cosmogonic themes resonances with Buddhist teachings on the arising and dissolution of world-systems (lokadhatu), the role of collective karma in shaping the conditions of existence, and the relationship between cosmic order and the moral quality of beings. These parallels suggest not direct borrowing but a shared human impulse to understand the relationship between moral and cosmic order.
