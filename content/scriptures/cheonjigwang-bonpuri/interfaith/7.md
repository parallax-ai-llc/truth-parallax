Interfaith interpretation of Section 7, 'The Ordering of the World,' values this portion of the Korean creation myth as a unique contribution to humanity's diverse treasury of cosmogonic narratives. The themes addressed here — the processes of cosmic origin and ordering — represent universal human concerns expressed through the distinctive cultural and spiritual vocabulary of Korean shamanism. Rather than ranking this account against the creation narratives of other traditions, the interfaith approach receives it as an equally valid expression of humanity's drive to understand origins.

This section offers particular value for interfaith dialogue because it arises from an oral, performative tradition rather than a literate, textual one. Its survival as a living ritual narrative — still performed in gut ceremonies — challenges the assumption that authentic religious expression requires fixed scriptural texts, opening interfaith conversation to the vast heritage of oral and embodied spiritual traditions.
