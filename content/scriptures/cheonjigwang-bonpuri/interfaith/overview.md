## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Cheonjigwang Bonpuri values the creation myth as a unique Korean contribution to the world's treasury of cosmogonic narratives. Rather than measuring it against the creation accounts of textual religions, the interfaith tradition receives it as an independent and equally valid expression of humanity's universal impulse to narrate the origins of the cosmos and account for the human condition.

## The Problem of Evil

The Cheonjigwang Bonpuri's distinctive treatment of the problem of evil — the unworthy twin's trickery wins him rule over the human world — offers a perspective that enriches interfaith dialogue. Unlike traditions that attribute evil to a fallen angel, original sin, or ignorance, the Korean myth locates the source of worldly imperfection in a primordial act of deception that subverts the intended cosmic order. This narrative theology invites comparison with theodicies across traditions.

## Indigenous Cosmology

The interfaith tradition highlights the Cheonjigwang Bonpuri's value as an indigenous cosmological text that predates and exists independently of the world's major textual religions. Its survival within the living practice of Korean shamanism demonstrates the resilience of oral religious traditions and their capacity to maintain sophisticated cosmological thought outside the frameworks of literacy and institutional religion.
