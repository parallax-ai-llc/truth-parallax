Confucian readers of Section 2, 'Separation of Heaven and Earth,' find in this portion of the creation myth themes that resonate with and challenge Confucian philosophical commitments. The narrative's treatment of cosmic order, authority, and moral structure raises questions that are central to Confucian political and ethical philosophy: How is legitimate authority established? What is the relationship between cosmic order and human governance? How do primordial events shape the moral landscape of the present?

While Confucian scholars historically regarded shamanic narratives as unworthy of serious intellectual attention, contemporary Confucian thinkers recognize in the Cheonjigwang Bonpuri a sophisticated cosmological vision that, while expressed in mythological rather than philosophical language, addresses the same fundamental questions about order, authority, and moral structure that occupy Confucian philosophy.
