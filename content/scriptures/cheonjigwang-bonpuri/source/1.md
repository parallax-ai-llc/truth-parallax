---
title: "Cheonjigwang Bonpuri Section 1"
scripture: "cheonjigwang-bonpuri"
chapter: 1
---

**1** In the beginning, heaven and earth were not yet separated. All was darkness, and chaos prevailed. There was no sun, no moon, no stars.

**2** Heaven and earth were joined together like the two halves of a clam shell. There was no distinction between above and below, between light and darkness.

**3** Then the Great Ancestor stirred, and with a mighty effort, he separated heaven from earth. Heaven rose upward and earth sank downward.

**4** But the separation was not complete. The sky hung low, almost touching the earth. The earth was uneven, with mountains jutting up and valleys sinking down.

**5** And so the Great Ancestor called upon the heavenly smiths to raise the sky higher. They placed copper pillars at the four corners of the earth and lifted the sky upon them.

**6** When the sky was lifted, light entered the world for the first time. But there was still no sun and no moon, and the light was dim and gray.

**7** And the Great Ancestor said: The world needs light. I shall create the sun and the moon to illuminate heaven and earth.
