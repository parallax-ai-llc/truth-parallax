---
title: "Cheonjigwang Bonpuri Section 2"
scripture: "cheonjigwang-bonpuri"
chapter: 2
---

**1** In those days, two suns and two moons appeared in the sky. The heat of the two suns scorched the earth by day, and the brightness of the two moons kept the people awake by night.

**2** The people cried out in distress. The crops withered in the fields. The rivers dried up. The animals hid in their burrows.

**3** And at night, the two moons shone so brightly that no one could sleep. The people grew weary and sick.

**4** Then the Cheonjigwang, the King of Heaven and Earth, heard the cries of the people. He said: This cannot continue. I must restore order to the sky.

**5** The Cheonjigwang took his great bow and shot down one of the suns and one of the moons. The sun that was struck fell to earth and became a great fireball.

**6** Where it fell, a mountain rose up, and the mountain still smokes to this day. The moon that was struck shattered into a thousand pieces, and they became the stars.

**7** Now there was one sun for the day and one moon for the night, and the people rejoiced, for the heat was bearable and they could sleep at night.
