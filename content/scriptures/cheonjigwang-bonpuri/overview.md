## Overview

The Cheonjigwang Bonpuri (천지왕본풀이, "Origin Narrative of the King of Heaven and Earth") is the foundational creation myth of Korean shamanism, most fully preserved in the Jeju Island tradition. As a bonpuri (본풀이, "unraveling of origins"), it belongs to a genre of sacred narratives recited during gut (shamanic rituals) to explain the origins of deities, the cosmos, and the fundamental ordering of the world. It is among the most important texts in Korean Musok, establishing the cosmological framework within which all other shamanic narratives and rituals operate.

## Structure

The Cheonjigwang Bonpuri unfolds in seven sections:

1. **The Primordial Chaos** (혼돈/混沌): The state before creation, when heaven and earth were undifferentiated
2. **Separation of Heaven and Earth** (천지개벽/天地開闢): The cosmic act of differentiation that creates the ordered universe
3. **Cheonjiwang's Descent** (천지왕 하강): The sky god's journey to the earthly realm
4. **The Marriage of Sky and Earth** (천지혼인): The union of celestial and terrestrial principles
5. **The Twin Brothers** (쌍둥이 형제): The birth of Daebyeol-wang and Sobyeol-wang
6. **The Contest of the Twins** (형제 겨루기): The competition that determines the governance of the world
7. **The Ordering of the World** (세상 정리): The establishment of the present cosmic and moral order

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Cheonjigwang Bonpuri has been interpreted across multiple traditions:

- **Shamanic**: Performed as a foundational ritual narrative establishing the cosmic order that the gut ceremony maintains
- **Buddhist-Korean**: Examined for its parallels with Buddhist cosmological narratives and its syncretic incorporation of Buddhist elements
- **Confucian**: Read for its implicit theories of governance, moral order, and the proper relationship between rulers
- **Academic**: Studied as Korea's most important indigenous creation myth and compared with regional cosmogonies
- **Interfaith**: Valued as a unique expression of Korean cosmological thought with universal resonances

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Cosmogony | The creation of the ordered universe from primordial chaos |
| Duality | The interplay of opposing but complementary cosmic principles |
| Sovereignty | The contest for rulership as a model for legitimate authority |
| Justice | The imperfect ordering of the world explaining the existence of evil |
| Mediation | The shaman's role in maintaining the cosmic order established at creation |
| Oral Performance | The narrative as a living ritual act rather than a fixed literary text |
