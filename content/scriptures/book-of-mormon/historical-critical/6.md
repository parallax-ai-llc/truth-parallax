## Mosiah

This section of the Book of Mormon covers the reign of King Benjamin and his transformative covenant speech, the mission of the sons of Mosiah to the Lamanites, and the transition from monarchy to a system of judges.

Historical-critical analysis of this section examines its claims against available evidence from archaeology, linguistics, genetics, and comparative history. The historical-critical method neither assumes nor denies divine inspiration but asks what can be established through empirical investigation.

For the narrative sections, scholars note the absence of corroborating archaeological or inscriptional evidence for the civilizations, cities, battles, and populations described. LDS scholars propose limited geographical models and argue that absence of evidence is not evidence of absence, while critics regard the cumulative lack of confirmation as significant.

Textual-critical analysis examines the section's language, identifying both features consistent with ancient Near Eastern literary forms and features suggesting nineteenth-century American composition. The relationship to the King James Bible — including its reproduction of translation errors unique to the KJV — is a key area of investigation.
