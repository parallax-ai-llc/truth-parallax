## Overview

Academic scholarship on the Book of Mormon spans multiple disciplines — literary criticism, religious studies, history, archaeology, linguistics, and sociology. The field is characterized by a productive tension between scholars who work within LDS faith commitments and those who approach the text from secular or non-LDS perspectives.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Literary Analysis
Grant Hardy's *Understanding the Book of Mormon* (2010) demonstrated that the text sustains sophisticated literary analysis regardless of one's views on its origins. The narrators — Nephi, Mormon, Moroni — have distinct voices, agendas, and literary strategies.

### Historical Contextualization
Scholars place the Book of Mormon within the religious ferment of early nineteenth-century America — the Second Great Awakening, anti-Masonic movements, debates over Native American origins, and the "burned-over district" of upstate New York. Fawn Brodie and Dan Vogel represent this approach.

### Textual Criticism
Royal Skousen's critical text project has painstakingly reconstructed the original and printer's manuscripts, providing the scholarly foundation for all textual study of the Book of Mormon.
