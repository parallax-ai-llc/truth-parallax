## 2 Nephi

This section of the Book of Mormon covers Nephi's teachings on the Atonement, Isaiah's prophecies applied to the latter days, Lehi's final blessings, and the foundational declaration that 'men are that they might have joy'.

For Latter-day Saints, 2 Nephi contains some of the most doctrinally significant passages in all of scripture. Lehi's final blessings to his children, particularly 2 Nephi 2, constitute a foundational text on the Fall of Adam, the Atonement, and moral agency. The declaration that "men are, that they might have joy" (2 Nephi 2:25) is one of the most beloved doctrinal statements in the LDS tradition, encapsulating the purpose of the plan of salvation and the optimistic theology that distinguishes Latter-day Saint belief from much of traditional Christianity.

The extensive Isaiah quotations (2 Nephi 12-24) are read by LDS interpreters as prophetically relevant to the latter-day gathering of Israel. Daniel Peterson and other LDS scholars have argued that Nephi's use of Isaiah demonstrates an ancient literary sophistication consistent with Book of Mormon historicity. Nephi's commentary on Isaiah in 2 Nephi 25-30 provides what Latter-day Saints consider inspired exegesis, interpreting the ancient prophet's words in light of the Restoration — including explicit prophecies about the coming forth of the Book of Mormon itself and the restoration of the gospel in the last days.

The "doctrine of Christ" articulated in 2 Nephi 31-32 — faith, repentance, baptism, the gift of the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end — is the doctrinal backbone of LDS missionary teaching and temple recommend interviews. These chapters are cross-referenced with 3 Nephi 11 and Doctrine and Covenants 20 as establishing the essential ordinances of salvation. Elder Bruce R. McConkie described 2 Nephi 9 (Jacob's sermon on the Atonement) as "the most comprehensive and inspired discussion of the atonement in all scripture," a view widely shared in LDS doctrinal commentary.
