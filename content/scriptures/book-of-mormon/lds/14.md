## Title Page/Testimony

This section of the Book of Mormon covers the title page (attributed to Moroni as a summary of the book's purpose), the testimony of the Three Witnesses and the Eight Witnesses who reported seeing the golden plates.

The title page of the Book of Mormon holds a unique position in LDS understanding: Joseph Smith declared it was "a literal translation, taken from the very last leaf, on the left hand side of the collection or book of plates" — meaning it is not Joseph Smith's composition but Moroni's ancient summary of the book's purpose. The stated purpose — "to show unto the remnant of the House of Israel what great things the Lord hath done for their fathers; and that they may know the covenants of the Lord" and "to the convincing of the Jew and Gentile that Jesus is the Christ" — frames the entire Book of Mormon as a covenant document and a witness of Christ, the two themes Latter-day Saints consider most central to the text.

The testimonies of the Three Witnesses (Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer, Martin Harris) and the Eight Witnesses are printed in every copy of the Book of Mormon and constitute a foundational element of LDS apologetics. The Three Witnesses testified that an angel showed them the golden plates and that the voice of God declared the translation correct. The Eight Witnesses testified to physically handling and examining the plates. LDS scholars, particularly Richard Lloyd Anderson in *Investigating the Book of Mormon Witnesses* (1981), have extensively documented that none of the eleven witnesses ever denied their testimony, even those who later left the church and had personal conflicts with Joseph Smith. Daniel Peterson and other apologists argue that the consistency and durability of these testimonies under adversity constitute strong evidence for the Book of Mormon's authenticity.

For Latter-day Saints, the witness testimonies fulfill the biblical law of witnesses ("in the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established," 2 Corinthians 13:1) and establish the Book of Mormon's truth claims on the same evidentiary foundation as biblical scripture. Doctrine and Covenants 17 records the revelation in which God commanded that the Three Witnesses be shown the plates, linking the witness experience directly to divine initiative rather than human arrangement. The testimonies also model the LDS understanding of how spiritual knowledge is obtained: through a combination of physical evidence and spiritual confirmation.
