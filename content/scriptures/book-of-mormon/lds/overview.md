## Overview

For members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, the Book of Mormon is "the keystone of our religion" (Joseph Smith) — a divinely translated ancient record that serves as a second witness of Jesus Christ alongside the Bible. Its historicity is affirmed as a matter of faith, and its teachings are considered scripture equal in authority to the Bible.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Testimony and the Spirit
LDS interpretation emphasizes that the truth of the Book of Mormon is confirmed through the Holy Ghost (Moroni 10:3-5). While intellectual study is valued, spiritual witness is considered the primary mode of knowing.

### Prophetic Commentary
Living prophets and apostles provide authoritative commentary on the Book of Mormon. General Conference addresses, official curriculum materials, and prophetic statements guide LDS interpretation within institutional boundaries.

### Typology and Application
LDS readers find in the Book of Mormon types and patterns applicable to contemporary life. The cycle of pride, prosperity, wickedness, and destruction (*pride cycle*) is a central interpretive framework.

## Theological Emphases

- **Christ-centeredness**: The Book of Mormon's primary purpose is to convince "Jew and Gentile that Jesus is the Christ" (Title Page)
- **Covenant theology**: God's promises to Lehi's descendants and the gathering of Israel
- **Plan of Salvation**: The Book of Mormon articulates LDS soteriology with particular clarity
- **Priesthood authority**: The text demonstrates the necessity of divine authorization for ordinances
- **Agency**: Moral choice as the foundation of the human experience
