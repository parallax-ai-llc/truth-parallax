## Moroni

This section of the Book of Mormon covers Moroni's final writings, including instructions on sacrament prayers, ordination, the gifts of the Spirit, and the famous invitation to readers to pray for a spiritual confirmation of the book's truth (Moroni 10:3-5).

The book of Moroni serves as both the conclusion of the Book of Mormon and its most directly practical section for Latter-day Saint worship. Moroni's recorded sacrament prayers (Moroni 4-5) are the exact prayers used in every LDS sacrament meeting worldwide — they are recited verbatim, and a priest must repeat the prayer if even a single word is altered. This direct liturgical continuity between an ancient text and modern practice is, for Latter-day Saints, powerful evidence that the restored church operates under the same priesthood authority as Christ's ancient church.

Moroni's instructions on ordination (Moroni 3), baptism (Moroni 6), and the conduct of church meetings reflect what LDS readers understand as the organizational pattern Christ established among the Nephites. The simplicity and specificity of these instructions — elders and priests ordained by the laying on of hands, members meeting together often to partake of the sacrament, meetings conducted by the power of the Holy Ghost — are seen as consistent with the revelations Joseph Smith received about church organization in Doctrine and Covenants 20.

Moroni 7, containing Mormon's sermon on faith, hope, and charity, is one of the great doctrinal discourses in LDS scripture. Its teaching that "charity is the pure love of Christ, and it endureth forever" (Moroni 7:47) is one of the most memorized verses in the church and provides the theological foundation for the Relief Society motto. The sermon's systematic treatment of how to distinguish good from evil — "every thing which inviteth to do good, and to persuade to believe in Christ, is sent forth by the power and gift of Christ" (Moroni 7:16) — offers Latter-day Saints a practical test for discernment in daily life.

Moroni's promise (Moroni 10:3-5) is the capstone of the entire Book of Mormon and the foundation of the LDS conversion process. Every missionary invitation to investigators includes this passage: read the Book of Mormon, ponder it, and ask God "with a sincere heart, with real intent, having faith in Christ" whether it is true. The promise that the Holy Ghost will "manifest the truth of it unto you" has been the reported spiritual experience of millions of Latter-day Saints and constitutes the epistemological basis of LDS testimony.
