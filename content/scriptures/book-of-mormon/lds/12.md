## Ether

This section of the Book of Mormon covers Moroni's abridgment of the Jaredite record — an earlier civilization that crossed the ocean to the Americas at the time of the Tower of Babel and eventually destroyed itself through civil war.

The book of Ether, Moroni's abridgment of the Jaredite record, presents Latter-day Saints with a second witness of the pattern that dominates the Book of Mormon: a people led by God to the Americas, granted extraordinary blessings, and ultimately destroyed through their own wickedness. The Jaredite journey in sealed barges across the ocean is read as a literal historical migration, and Hugh Nibley devoted extensive research in *There Were Jaredites* to demonstrating that the Jaredite narrative contains details consistent with ancient Near Eastern culture at the time of the Tower of Babel.

Ether 12, Moroni's discourse on faith, contains passages that are central to LDS devotional life. The teaching that God "giveth unto men weakness that they may be humble" and that through grace "weak things become strong unto them" (Ether 12:27) is among the most frequently quoted verses in General Conference and is foundational to the LDS understanding of how divine grace interacts with human effort. Elder David A. Bednar has taught extensively on this passage, connecting it to the doctrine that the Atonement of Jesus Christ is not merely a remedy for sin but an enabling power that helps individuals transcend natural limitations.

The brother of Jared's vision (Ether 3), in which he sees the premortal, spirit-body of Jesus Christ, is one of the most theologically significant passages in the Book of Mormon for LDS readers. It affirms the LDS doctrine — unique among Christian traditions — that Christ possessed a spirit body in the premortal existence that was in the form of his eventual physical body, a teaching reinforced in Doctrine and Covenants 130:22. The sealed portion of the plates, described in Ether as containing a full account of this vision and of the history of the world from beginning to end, remains a subject of anticipation among Latter-day Saints who believe it will be revealed when the world is ready to receive it.

Moroni's warning against "secret combinations" (Ether 8:22-25), drawn from the Jaredite experience, is treated by LDS readers as a direct prophetic address to modern readers, warning against conspiratorial organizations that seek to overthrow the freedom of all lands.
