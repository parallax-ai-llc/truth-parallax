## Mormon

This section of the Book of Mormon covers Mormon's own record of the final wars between Nephites and Lamanites, the destruction of the Nephite nation, and Mormon's reflections on the tragedy he witnessed.

The book of Mormon (the book within the Book of Mormon named for its compiler) is read by Latter-day Saints as both a historical tragedy and a prophetic warning. Mormon, the prophet-general who abridged the entire record, writes here in his own voice about the final destruction of the Nephite nation. His lamentation over a people who had rejected the gospel despite having received every spiritual advantage is understood by LDS readers as directly applicable to latter-day covenant people who may similarly squander their blessings. President Ezra Taft Benson's repeated teaching that the Book of Mormon was written for "our day" finds its most explicit confirmation in Mormon's own statement that he has "seen your day" and writes to those who will live in the time of the book's coming forth (Mormon 8:35).

Mormon's character — a righteous man who served faithfully despite leading a people who refused to repent — is held up in LDS teaching as a model of discipleship under impossible circumstances. His willingness to continue compiling sacred records even as his civilization collapsed around him illustrates the LDS principle that individual faithfulness to God's purposes matters regardless of whether the broader culture accepts the gospel. This resonates with the Latter-day Saint teaching that covenant members must remain faithful even when surrounded by worldly opposition.

Mormon's epistle on infant baptism (Moroni 8, included as part of Mormon's writings) is one of the clearest doctrinal statements in the Book of Mormon and has directly shaped LDS practice. The teaching that "little children are alive in Christ" and that baptizing infants is a "solemn mockery before God" (Moroni 8:8-9) provides the scriptural foundation for the LDS practice of baptizing children at age eight rather than in infancy — a practice codified in Doctrine and Covenants 68:27. This teaching also connects to the Pearl of Great Price's account of the doctrine revealed to Adam that "children are not accountable before me until they are eight years old" (JST Genesis / Moses 6:54).
