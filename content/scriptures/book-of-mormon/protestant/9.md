## 3 Nephi

This section of the Book of Mormon covers the climactic appearance of the resurrected Jesus Christ to the Nephites, his teaching of the Sermon on the Mount, the establishment of his church, and the period of peace that followed.

Protestant evaluation of this section focuses on its theological claims measured against biblical orthodoxy. Points of convergence — the centrality of Christ's Atonement, the necessity of faith and repentance, the reality of divine judgment — are acknowledged, while points of divergence are carefully noted.

The fundamental Protestant concern remains the question of *sola scriptura*: if the Bible is sufficient for salvation and godly living, what is the theological necessity of additional scripture? Protestant scholars also examine the relationship between Book of Mormon soteriology and the Protestant understanding of justification by grace through faith alone.

Some Protestant scholars, such as Craig Blomberg, have engaged in constructive dialogue with LDS counterparts, seeking to identify genuine theological common ground while maintaining honest disagreement on fundamental issues.
