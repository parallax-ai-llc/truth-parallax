title: "Book of Mormon Section 9: Third Nephi"

chapter: 9
---

Third Nephi records the most sacred events in the Book of Mormon: the signs of Christ's birth, the destruction at His death, and His personal ministry among the Nephites. In the thirty-fourth year since the sign of His coming, there arose a great storm, a terrible tempest, and dreadful thunderings and lightnings such as had never been known in all the land.

The city of Zarahemla was burned with fire. The city of Moroni sank into the depths of the sea. The earth was carried up upon the city of Moronihah. Highways were broken up, plains were wrecked, and many smooth places became rough. Three days of thick darkness covered the land, and the inhabitants heard a voice piercing through the darkness, declaring that these destructions had come because of wickedness and abomination.

After the days of darkness, the people gathered at the temple in the land Bountiful. They heard a voice from heaven, and the third time they opened their ears to hear it, and it said: Behold my Beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased, in whom I have glorified my name. Hear ye him.

And they saw a Man descending out of heaven, clothed in a white robe, and He came down and stood in the midst of them. He stretched forth His hand and spake unto the people, saying: Behold, I am Jesus Christ, whom the prophets testified shall come into the world. I am the light and the life of the world. I have drunk out of that bitter cup which the Father hath given me, and have glorified the Father in taking upon me the sins of the world.

He invited them to come forth and thrust their hands into His side and feel the prints of the nails in His hands and in His feet. And this they did, going forth one by one, and did see with their eyes and did feel with their hands, and did know of a surety and did bear record that it was He of whom it was written by the prophets that should come.
