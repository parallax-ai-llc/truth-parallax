---
title: "Book of Mormon Section 5: Words of Mormon and Mosiah"
scripture: "book-of-mormon"
chapter: 5
---

Mormon, the compiler of these records, inserts a brief explanation of how he abridged the large plates of Nephi and preserved the small plates of Nephi because of the prophecies and revelations they contained. He testifies that he knows the records are true, and that the Lord shall bring them forth in His own due time.

The book of Mosiah records the reign of King Benjamin, a righteous ruler who labored with his own hands that he might serve his people and not burden them with taxes. In his old age, he gathered his people to the temple and delivered a great sermon from a tower, speaking words given him by an angel of God.

King Benjamin taught that the natural man is an enemy to God, and has been from the fall of Adam, and will be forever and ever unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit and putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord and becometh as a child, submissive, meek, humble, patient, full of love, willing to submit to all things which the Lord seeth fit to inflict upon him, even as a child doth submit to his father.

The people, hearing these words, fell to the earth, for the fear of the Lord had come upon them. They cried aloud with one voice, saying: O have mercy, and apply the atoning blood of Christ that we may receive forgiveness of our sins and our hearts may be purified. And the Spirit of the Lord came upon them, and they were filled with joy, having received a remission of their sins and having peace of conscience because of their exceeding faith in Jesus Christ who should come.

Mosiah also records the account of the people of Zeniff, who returned to the land of Nephi and fell under the bondage of the Lamanites, and the account of Alma the Elder, who believed the words of the prophet Abinadi and established the church among the believers at the waters of Mormon.
