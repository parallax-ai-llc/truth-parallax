---
title: "Book of Mormon Section 14: Moroni, Part One"
scripture: "book-of-mormon"
chapter: 14
---

And now I, Moroni, after having made an end of abridging the account of the people of Jared, write a few things that I may be of worth unto my brethren the Lamanites in some future day, according to the will of the Lord. I wander whithersoever I can for the safety of mine own life, for the Lamanites hunt every Nephite who will not deny the Christ.

Moroni records the words of his father Mormon concerning faith, hope, and charity. Mormon taught that faith is things which are hoped for and not seen, and urged men not to dispute because they do not see, for ye receive no witness until after the trial of your faith. By faith all things are fulfilled, and without faith there cannot be any hope, and without hope ye cannot be partakers of charity.

Charity is the pure love of Christ, and it endureth forever, and whoso is found possessed of it at the last day, it shall be well with him. Wherefore, my beloved brethren, pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart that ye may be filled with this love which He hath bestowed upon all who are true followers of His Son Jesus Christ, that ye may become the sons of God, that when He shall appear we shall be like Him, for we shall see Him as He is, that we may have this hope, that we may be purified even as He is pure.

Moroni also records the instructions given by Christ for the administration of the ordinances of the gospel: the manner of baptism, the blessing of the sacramental bread and wine, and the ordination of priests and teachers. These are recorded with exactness, that future generations may know the proper forms of worship.

He records that the church met together oft to fast and to pray and to speak one with another concerning the welfare of their souls. And the meetings were conducted by the church after the manner of the workings of the Spirit, and by the power of the Holy Ghost, for as the power of the Holy Ghost led them whether to preach, or to exhort, or to pray, or to supplicate, or to sing, even so it was done.
