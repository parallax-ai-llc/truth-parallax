---
title: "Book of Mormon Section 1: First Nephi"
scripture: "book-of-mormon"
chapter: 1
---

I, Nephi, having been born of goodly parents, therefore I was taught somewhat in all the learning of my father. And I make a record of my proceedings in my days, having been highly favored of the Lord in all my days. I, Nephi, begin with the account of my father Lehi, a prophet who dwelt at Jerusalem in the first year of the reign of Zedekiah, king of Judah.

Lehi, having prayed unto the Lord with all his heart on behalf of his people, received a vision in which he saw a pillar of fire dwelling upon a rock before him. He saw and heard much, and because of the things which he saw and heard he did quake and tremble exceedingly. He returned to his own house and cast himself upon his bed, being overcome with the Spirit, and was carried away in a vision, seeing the heavens opened and God sitting upon His throne surrounded by numberless concourses of angels.

And it came to pass that the Lord commanded my father, even in a dream, that he should take his family and depart into the wilderness. And he left his house, and his land of inheritance, and his gold, and his silver, and his precious things, and took nothing with him save his family, and provisions, and tents, and departed into the wilderness.

The Lord commanded my father to send his sons back to Jerusalem to obtain the brass plates of Laban, which contained the five books of Moses, a record of the Jews from the beginning, and also the prophecies of the holy prophets. After many tribulations, during which my brothers Laman and Lemuel murmured against our father, I, Nephi, went forth and was led by the Spirit, not knowing beforehand the things which I should do, and obtained the plates.

And we also obtained the record of our genealogy from the plates. And it came to pass that the Lord also commanded my father that he should send again to Jerusalem to bring Ishmael and his family, that his sons might take wives. The Lord guides those who exercise faith, and the records of the scriptures are preserved for the instruction of future generations.
