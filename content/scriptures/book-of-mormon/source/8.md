---
title: "Book of Mormon Section 8: Helaman"
scripture: "book-of-mormon"
chapter: 8
---

Helaman, the son of Alma, received the sacred records and continued the history of the Nephite people through a period of increasing wickedness and internal strife. The Gadianton robbers, a secret combination bound by oaths of murder and plunder, gained power among the Nephites and became one of the greatest threats to the civilization.

Helaman's sons Nephi and Lehi went forth to preach among the Lamanites and were cast into prison. They were encircled about as if by fire, and the earth shook, and a cloud of darkness overshadowed those who held them captive. A voice came unto them saying: Repent ye, repent ye, and seek no more to destroy my servants. The three hundred souls who witnessed this event were converted and went forth among the Lamanites, preaching with great power.

The prophet Nephi, son of Helaman, stood upon a tower in his garden, which was by the highway that led to the chief market, and prophesied unto the people of their wickedness. He revealed to them by the power of God that their chief judge had been murdered, and he identified the murderer, all of which was confirmed, leading some to recognize him as a prophet.

The Lord spoke unto Nephi, saying: Blessed art thou, Nephi, for those things which thou hast done, for thou hast not feared them and hast not sought thine own life but hast sought my will and to keep my commandments. And because thou hast done this with such unwearyingness, behold, I will bless thee forever, and I will make thee mighty in word and in deed, in faith and in works.

Helaman also preserves the teaching that the church must be built upon the rock of the Redeemer, who is Christ the Son of God, a foundation upon which, if men build, they cannot fall. This is the message that echoes throughout the book: that Christ is the sure foundation upon which all must build to withstand the storms of life.
