---
title: "Book of Mormon Section 4: Enos, Jarom, and Omni"
scripture: "book-of-mormon"
chapter: 4
---

Enos, the son of Jacob, recounts his wrestle before God. While hunting beasts in the forests, the words of his father concerning eternal life and the joy of the saints sunk deep into his heart. His soul hungered, and he kneeled down before his Maker and cried unto Him in mighty prayer and supplication for his own soul, all the day long and into the night.

And there came a voice unto him saying: Enos, thy sins are forgiven thee, and thou shalt be blessed. And Enos knew that God could not lie, wherefore his guilt was swept away. He asked how it was done, and the Lord said unto him: Because of thy faith in Christ, whom thou hast never before heard nor seen. And many years shall pass away before He shall manifest Himself in the flesh.

After receiving forgiveness for himself, Enos's faith began to be unshaken in the Lord, and he prayed with many long strugglings for his brethren, the Nephites. And then his heart turned to the Lamanites, and he poured out his whole soul unto God for them. The Lord answered that He would visit the Lamanites according to their diligence in keeping His commandments.

The book of Jarom continues the record briefly, noting that the Nephites observed the law of Moses and looked forward to the coming of the Messiah. They fortified their cities and prepared for war against the Lamanites, who came against them many times but were driven back.

The book of Omni passes the plates through several hands in rapid succession. During this period, the Nephites under King Mosiah discovered the people of Zarahemla, who had come from Jerusalem at the time of the Babylonian captivity. The two peoples united, and Mosiah was appointed their king. They also discovered a large stone with engravings that told of the Jaredites, a people who had come from the great tower at the time of the confusion of tongues.
