---
title: "Book of Mormon Section 12: Mormon"
scripture: "book-of-mormon"
chapter: 12
---

And now I, Mormon, make a record of the things which I have both seen and heard. When I was about ten years of age, Ammaron, the keeper of the records, told me that when I was about twenty-four years old, I should go to the hill Shim and retrieve the plates of Nephi and engrave upon them all the things that I had observed concerning my people.

I was large in stature and the Nephites appointed me, being but fifteen years of age, to be their leader in battle against the Lamanites. The wickedness of the Nephites was so great that the Lord refused to preserve them by His miraculous power, and they began to be swept off by the Lamanites as a dew before the sun.

I, Mormon, did utterly refuse from that time forth to be a commander and a leader of this people because of their wickedness and abomination. I had led them notwithstanding their wickedness, I had loved them according to the love of God which was in me with all my heart, and my soul had been poured out in prayer unto my God all the day long for them. But it was without faith, because of the hardness of their hearts.

Yet Mormon could not entirely refrain from record-keeping and leadership. He agreed to lead the Nephite armies once more, though without hope of victory. He gathered his people to the land of Cumorah, by a hill called Cumorah, where he hid all the records that had been entrusted to him in the hill, save it were the few plates which he gave unto his son Moroni.

The final battle at Cumorah destroyed the Nephite nation. Of the hundreds of thousands who fell, only Mormon, his son Moroni, and a few others survived. Mormon, wounded by the Lamanites, wrote his last testament, a lament and an exhortation to the future readers of the record: Know ye that ye must come unto repentance, or ye cannot be saved. Know ye that ye must lay down your weapons of war and delight no more in the shedding of blood. Know ye that ye must come to the knowledge of your fathers and repent of all your sins and iniquities.
