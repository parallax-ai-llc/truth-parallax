## 4 Nephi

This section of the Book of Mormon covers the brief record of nearly two hundred years of peace following Christ's visit, followed by the gradual return to wickedness and division.

Community of Christ approaches this section with an emphasis on its theological and devotional value. While some members affirm the historical nature of the narrative, the denomination officially allows diverse views on historicity, focusing instead on the text's spiritual message.

The Community of Christ reading emphasizes themes of peace, justice, and the worth of all persons — values central to the denomination's contemporary identity. Passages that speak of reconciliation, community-building, and divine grace receive particular attention, while passages involving warfare or ethnic conflict are read critically in light of the church's commitment to nonviolence.

Recent Community of Christ scholarship has encouraged reading the Book of Mormon alongside other scriptures and in dialogue with contemporary theological scholarship, maintaining an open and inquiring approach.
