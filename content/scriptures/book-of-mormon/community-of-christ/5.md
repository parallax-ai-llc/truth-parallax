## Words of Mormon

This section of the Book of Mormon covers Mormon's editorial bridge connecting the small plates of Nephi to his abridgment of the large plates, explaining his purposes as editor and compiler.

Community of Christ reads the Words of Mormon as a fascinating editorial moment that raises important questions about how scripture comes into being. Mormon's explanation that he felt "moved by the Spirit" to include the small plates alongside his abridgment invites reflection on the role of human discernment in the production of sacred texts. For Community of Christ, which understands continuing revelation as a process that involves the community's collective discernment as well as individual inspiration, this passage illustrates that scripture is never simply dictated — it is always mediated through human judgment, cultural context, and editorial choices.

This perspective aligns with Community of Christ's approach to its own expanding canon. The denomination has continued to add sections to its Doctrine and Covenants — most recently in 2016 — through a deliberative process involving prayer, study, and the common consent of World Conference delegates. Mormon's editorial decision-making in the Words of Mormon can be seen as an ancient parallel to this modern practice: a faithful individual discerning what to preserve and how to organize it, trusting that the Spirit is at work in the process even when the full picture is not yet clear.

Community of Christ scholars have also noted that the Words of Mormon highlight the contingency and vulnerability of sacred records. The fact that portions of the record could be lost (as happened with the 116 pages in the Joseph Smith story) and that backup records needed to be preserved reminds readers that scripture exists in a material world subject to accident, loss, and human failure. This awareness encourages humility about textual certainty and supports Community of Christ's non-literalist reading posture, which values scripture without treating it as an inerrant or self-interpreting document.
