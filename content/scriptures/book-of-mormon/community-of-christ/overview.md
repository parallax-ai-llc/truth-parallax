## Overview

The Community of Christ (formerly the Reorganized Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints) holds the Book of Mormon as scripture but has developed a distinctive interpretive approach that emphasizes its theological and devotional value without requiring a specific position on historicity. Members are free to understand the text as an ancient historical record, an inspired nineteenth-century composition, or something in between.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Responsible Interpretation
Community of Christ encourages the use of historical-critical and literary methods alongside devotional reading. The denomination's statement on scripture affirms that all texts must be interpreted "responsibly" in light of ongoing revelation and the church's current understanding.

### Christological Focus
The Book of Mormon is valued primarily for its witness to Jesus Christ. Its theological content — particularly its teachings on grace, repentance, and the nature of God — is considered more important than questions of historicity or geography.

### Peace and Justice
Under the leadership of recent prophets-presidents, Community of Christ has emphasized the Book of Mormon's relevance to contemporary issues of peace, justice, and the worth of all persons.

## Theological Emphases

- **Grace**: The Book of Mormon's teachings on divine grace resonate with the denomination's Christ-centered theology
- **Community**: The text's emphasis on Zion-building aligns with Community of Christ's communal values
- **Continuing revelation**: The Book of Mormon is one witness among many, including ongoing prophetic revelation
- **Inclusivity**: Recent interpretation emphasizes passages that affirm human dignity and divine love for all
