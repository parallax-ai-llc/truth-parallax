## Overview

The Baal Cycle provides the most detailed example of the Chaoskampf — the cosmic battle between the storm god and the forces of chaos (Sea, Death) — a mythological pattern found across the ancient Near East and beyond. Its comparative significance extends from direct illumination of biblical language to structural parallels with Greek, Mesopotamian, and Hittite mythology.

## Key Comparative Connections

### Biblical Parallels
The Baal Cycle has transformed understanding of biblical poetry and theology:
- **YHWH vs. Sea**: Psalm 74:13–14, Isaiah 27:1, and Job 26:12–13 use language directly paralleled in the Baal-Yamm conflict
- **Leviathan/Lotan**: The seven-headed sea monster Lotan (*ltn*) in the Baal Cycle is the direct cognate of biblical Leviathan
- **Storm god imagery**: Psalm 29's description of YHWH's voice as thunder over the waters echoes Baal's characterization
- **Anti-Baal polemic**: 1 Kings 18 (Elijah on Mount Carmel) directly engages the claims made for Baal in the cycle

### Greek Parallels
- **Zeus vs. Typhon**: The storm god's battle with a chaos monster parallels Baal vs. Yamm
- **The palace of the gods**: Baal's palace on Mount Sapan parallels Zeus's establishment on Olympus
- **Seasonal absence**: Baal's sojourn in the underworld parallels Persephone/Adonis myths

### Mesopotamian Parallels
The Enuma Elish presents Marduk defeating Tiamat (Sea) and building his palace — a pattern closely paralleling Baal's victory over Yamm and construction of his palace.
