Yamm's challenge to the divine assembly parallels the chaos-monster's challenge to cosmic order in other traditions. In the Enuma Elish, Tiamat threatens the younger gods; in the Hebrew Bible, the sea (*yam*) is the primordial chaos that God subdues (Psalm 74:13-14, Job 26:12). The name Yamm itself is cognate with Hebrew *yam* (sea), and the mythological pattern of the storm god defeating Sea directly underlies biblical poetry about YHWH's sovereignty over the waters.

The motif of a cosmic adversary demanding tribute from the divine assembly also parallels the Satan's challenge in Job 1-2.
