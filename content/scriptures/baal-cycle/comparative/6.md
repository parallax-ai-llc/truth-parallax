Anat's defeat of Mot using agricultural processes (splitting, winnowing, burning, grinding, sowing) has been compared to agricultural rituals across the ancient Mediterranean:
- **Greek**: The Eleusinian Mysteries and the grain-death-rebirth cycle of Demeter/Persephone
- **Egyptian**: Osiris as the grain god whose death and dismemberment parallels the agricultural cycle
- **Phrygian**: Attis and the harvest rituals

The Baal Cycle's treatment is distinctive in that it is the warrior goddess, not the dying god himself, who accomplishes the defeat of Death. This active female role in the restoration of cosmic order is a significant feature of Ugaritic mythology.
