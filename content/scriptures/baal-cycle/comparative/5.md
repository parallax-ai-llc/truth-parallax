Baal's death at the hands of Mot parallels the dying god traditions found across the ancient Near East and Mediterranean:
- **Sumerian**: Dumuzi's descent to the underworld
- **Egyptian**: Osiris killed by Set
- **Greek**: The deaths of Adonis, Attis, and Dionysus
- **Phrygian**: Attis's self-castration and death

James Frazer's *Golden Bough* grouped these traditions as examples of the 'dying and rising god' pattern, though modern scholarship (particularly Jonathan Z. Smith) has questioned whether 'rising' is an accurate description for all these figures. The Baal Cycle's treatment is distinctive: Baal's death is definitive and his return requires external intervention.
