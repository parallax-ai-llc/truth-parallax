The building of Baal's palace is the theological center of the cycle. In Ugaritic theology, a god without a palace is a god without full sovereignty — the palace is not merely a residence but the cosmic sign of legitimate rule. Baal's long struggle to obtain permission for his palace (requiring the advocacy of both Anat and Athirat before El) reveals a theology in which even divine sovereignty is subject to process and authorization.

The window debate is theologically significant: the window allows Baal's voice (thunder) to reach the earth, enabling rain and fertility, but also creates a vulnerability through which Mot (Death) can enter. This theology of necessary vulnerability — that exercising power requires opening oneself to risk — is a profound insight found across traditions.
