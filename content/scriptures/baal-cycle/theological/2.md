Baal's victory over Yamm establishes the theological basis for his claim to kingship. In Ugaritic theology, divine kingship is not a birthright but an achievement — Baal earns his throne through combat. This theology of earned sovereignty contrasts with traditions that present divine rule as eternal and unchallenged.

The role of Kothar-wa-Khasis as weapon-maker introduces a theology of divine cooperation: Baal cannot defeat Yamm alone but requires the skill of the craftsman god. Sovereignty is not achieved in isolation but through the combined efforts of the divine community.
