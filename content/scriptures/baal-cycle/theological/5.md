Baal's death introduces the most profound theological crisis in the cycle. If the storm god — the giver of rain, the defeater of Sea — can be swallowed by Death, then no power is absolute. Mot's realm, like Ereshkigal's in the Descent of Inanna, has its own sovereignty that even the king of the gods must acknowledge.

The cosmic consequences of Baal's death — agricultural failure, the cessation of rain — reveal a theology in which divine vitality and earthly fertility are directly linked. Without Baal, the world dies. This theology of divine necessity — the world needs its gods as much as the gods need their world — stands in contrast to traditions that emphasize divine self-sufficiency.
