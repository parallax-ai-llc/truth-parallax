The sixth tablet resolves the cycle's theological crisis: Death is defeated, and the storm god returns. But the resolution is ambiguous — Mot is not permanently destroyed but will return. This theology of cyclical conflict, in which the forces of life and death are in perpetual tension, differs from eschatological traditions that envision a final, permanent victory over death (1 Corinthians 15:26, 'The last enemy to be destroyed is death').

The agricultural imagery of Anat's treatment of Mot — grinding, winnowing, sowing — suggests a theology in which death is not merely defeated but transformed: death becomes the means by which new life emerges, just as the grain must be cut and buried to produce a new harvest.
