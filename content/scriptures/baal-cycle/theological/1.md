The first tablet introduces a central theological tension: El, the supreme god, appears willing to surrender Baal to Yamm. This raises the question of El's character — is he weak, indifferent, or playing a deeper game? The relationship between El (transcendent, passive, authoritative) and Baal (active, dynamic, combative) reflects a theology in which transcendence and immanence are distributed across different deities.

Yamm's demand for Baal as tribute also raises the theological question of chaos's claims on order: does the sea have a legitimate right to challenge the storm god? The text suggests that Yamm's authority was granted by El himself, making the conflict an internal divine dispute rather than a simple battle between good and evil.
