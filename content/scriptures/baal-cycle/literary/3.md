The third tablet juxtaposes two contrasting scenes: Baal's orderly banquet and Anat's chaotic battle. This literary contrast — celebration and slaughter, feast and fury — creates a complex portrait of the divine world as simultaneously civilized and savage. The banquet scene employs the conventions of ancient Near Eastern royal feast descriptions; the battle scene shatters those conventions with imagery of overwhelming, joyful violence.

Anat's battle scene is one of the most vivid passages in Ugaritic literature, employing kinetic imagery, bodily detail, and emotional description that immerse the audience in the goddess's experience of combat.
