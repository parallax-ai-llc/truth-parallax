The battle scene is the Baal Cycle's first great action sequence. The literary technique is cinematic: the crafting of the weapons is described in detail, the first blow is narrated with suspense (Yamm staggers but does not fall), and the second blow brings decisive victory. The naming of the two maces — Yagarrish and Ayyamari — personalizes the weapons, giving them agency in the narrative.

Athtart's rebuke of Baal for excessive violence introduces a note of moral complexity: even a justified victory can be conducted unjustly. This literary nuance prevents the battle from being a simple triumph narrative.
