The fourth tablet is the literary masterpiece of the Baal Cycle. The negotiations for the palace — Anat's threats, Athirat's diplomacy, El's eventual consent — create a rich political drama within the divine world. The construction scene employs vivid imagery: 'Fire was set in the house, flame in the palace; for a day and a second, fire fed on the house, flame on the palace; a third, a fourth day, fire fed on the house, flame on the palace.' The transformation of the palace through fire suggests purification and consecration.

The window debate is a masterful literary device, creating suspense and foreshadowing within a construction narrative. Baal's eventual decision to open the window — and his challenge to Mot that follows — sets up the tragedy of the fifth tablet.
