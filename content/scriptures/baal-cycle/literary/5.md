The fifth tablet achieves its literary power through a dramatic reversal: the god who defeated Sea is now defeated by Death. The cycle's narrative arc — from Baal's initial insecurity through his triumph over Yamm and the building of his palace to his catastrophic fall — creates a tragic structure in which success contains the seeds of its own destruction (Baal's opened window invites Mot's power).

The announcement of Baal's death is one of the most powerful passages in Ugaritic literature. El's mourning — descending from his throne, pouring dust on his head, gashing his flesh — is described with ritual precision that suggests actual mourning practices.
