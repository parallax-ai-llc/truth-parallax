The defeat of chaos by the divine warrior resonates across traditions: YHWH's victory over Leviathan (Isaiah 27:1), Krishna's defeat of the serpent Kaliya (Bhagavata Purana), St. George and the dragon, and the Buddha's defeat of Mara. In each case, the cosmic battle represents the triumph of order over chaos, good over evil, consciousness over delusion.

The Baal Cycle's contribution to this universal pattern is its insistence that the victory is not easy — it requires preparation, specialized weapons, and a second attempt after the first blow fails. Divine victory over chaos is real but costly.
