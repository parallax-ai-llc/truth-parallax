The defeat of Death and the return of life is perhaps the most universal theme in world religion:
- **Christianity**: The resurrection of Christ as the defeat of death and the promise of new life
- **Judaism**: The vision of dry bones restored to life (Ezekiel 37) and the eventual hope of resurrection
- **Islam**: The Day of Resurrection (*Yawm al-Qiyamah*) when the dead are raised
- **Hinduism**: The cyclical renewal of the cosmos through creation and dissolution
- **Buddhism**: The cycle of death and rebirth (*samsara*) and the possibility of liberation

The Baal Cycle's distinctive contribution is its agricultural framework: death is not merely defeated but processed — ground, winnowed, and sown — so that it becomes the source of new life. This insight, that death and life are not opposites but stages in a single process, resonates across traditions that understand suffering as transformative rather than merely destructive.
