The building of a divine palace/temple as the sign of established sovereignty resonates across traditions:
- **Judaism**: The Temple in Jerusalem as the dwelling place of God's name (1 Kings 8:29)
- **Christianity**: 'In my Father's house are many rooms' (John 14:2)
- **Islam**: The Ka'aba as the house of God on earth
- **Hinduism**: The temple (*mandir*) as the body of God made manifest in stone

The Baal Cycle's insistence that a god needs a house to be truly king speaks to the universal human conviction that the sacred must be housed — that divine presence requires a place in the material world.
