Baal's death confronts every religious tradition with the question: can the divine die? Different traditions answer differently:
- **Christianity**: God dies on the cross — and this death is the center of salvation history
- **Buddhism**: The Buddha dies (*parinirvana*), demonstrating that even enlightened beings are subject to impermanence
- **Islam**: 'Every soul shall taste death' (Quran 3:185) — but Allah is eternal and cannot die
- **Hinduism**: The gods are reborn in each cosmic cycle but are not eternal in the absolute sense

The Baal Cycle's treatment of divine death is neither resignation nor triumph but crisis — a disruption of the cosmic order that demands resolution. Baal's death is not the end of the story, but it is real.
