The figure of the warrior goddess — fierce, independent, and delighting in battle — challenges the gendering of the divine common in many traditions. While Abrahamic traditions generally use masculine language for the divine warrior (YHWH as man of war, Exodus 15:3), Hindu tradition preserves the feminine warrior divine in Durga and Kali. The Baal Cycle's Anat provides the ancient Canaanite witness to this tradition of feminine divine power.

Interfaith dialogue benefits from engaging with aspects of the divine that challenge comfortable assumptions — including the reality that many traditions include a divine dimension of fierce, protective violence.
