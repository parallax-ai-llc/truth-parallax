**Section VI: The Return of Baal**

**1** And the Kindly One, El the Benign,
**2** had a dream.
**3** In a vision the Creator of Creatures saw:

**4** The heavens rained oil,
**5** the wadis ran with honey.
**6** The Kindly One, El the Benign, rejoiced.

**7** He set his feet on the footstool.
**8** He opened his mouth and laughed.
**9** He raised his voice and cried:

**10** "Let me sit and rest!
**11** Let my soul repose in my breast!
**12** For Mighty Baal is alive!
**13** For the Prince, Lord of the Earth, exists!"

**14** El called to the virgin Anat:
**15** "Listen, O virgin Anat!
**16** Say to Shapsh, the Torch of the Gods:

**17** 'The furrows of the fields are cracked, O Shapsh!
**18** The furrows of El's fields are cracked!
**19** Baal is neglecting the furrows of the ploughland.
**20** Where is Mighty Baal?
**21** Where is the Prince, Lord of the Earth?'"

**22** The virgin Anat departed.
**23** She set face toward Shapsh, the Torch of the Gods.
**24** She raised her voice and cried:

**25** "Message of Bull El, your father,
**26** word of the Kindly One, your begetter:
**27** The furrows of the fields are cracked, O Shapsh!
**28** The furrows of El's fields are cracked!
**29** Baal is neglecting the furrows of the ploughland.
**30** Where is Mighty Baal?
**31** Where is the Prince, Lord of the Earth?"

**32** Shapsh, the Torch of the Gods, replied:
**33** "I will seek Mighty Baal."

**34** [Gap in text]

**35** And Mighty Baal returned.

**36** [Gap in text—Baal returns to his throne on Mount Zaphon. But Mot also revives and confronts Baal.]

**37** Mot, the beloved son of El, spoke:
**38** "Because of you, O Baal, I have experienced shame.
**39** Because of you I have experienced winnowing with the sword,
**40** because of you I have experienced burning with fire,

**41** because of you I have experienced grinding with millstones,
**42** because of you I have experienced sowing in the fields,
**43** because of you I have experienced sifting in the sea."

**44** [Gap in text]

**45** "Give me one of your brothers and I will eat;
**46** the anger which I feel will be turned away.
**47** If you do not give me one of your brothers,
**48** then I will indeed—

**49** I will consume mankind,
**50** I will consume the multitudes of the earth."

**51** [Gap in text]

**52** They eyed each other like glowing coals.
**53** Mot was strong; Baal was strong.
**54** They gored each other like wild bulls.
**55** Mot was strong; Baal was strong.

**56** They bit each other like serpents.
**57** Mot was strong; Baal was strong.
**58** They kicked each other like stallions.
**59** Mot fell; Baal fell.

**60** Shapsh called out to Mot:
**61** "Listen, O Mot, son of El!
**62** How can you fight with Mighty Baal?
**63** How will Bull El, your father, not hear you?

**64** He will surely remove the supports of your throne!
**65** He will surely overturn the seat of your kingship!
**66** He will surely break the sceptre of your rule!"

**67** Mot, the beloved son of El, was afraid.
**68** He was terrified, the beloved of El.
**69** Mot departed from his attack.

**70** [Gap in text]

**71** Mighty Baal sat on his royal throne,
**72** upon his seat of dominion.

**73** Days became months,
**74** months became years.

**75** And in the seventh year,
**76** Mot, son of El, spoke to Mighty Baal.
**77** He raised his voice and said:

**78** "Because of you, Baal, I have experienced shame.
**79** Because of you I have experienced splitting with the sword.
**80** Because of you I have experienced burning with fire.
**81** Because of you I have experienced grinding with millstones.
**82** Because of you I have experienced winnowing with the sieve.
**83** Because of you I have experienced scattering in the fields.
**84** Because of you I have experienced sowing in the sea.

**85** Give me your brothers that I may eat!"

**86** [Gap in text]

**87** But Baal enthroned his daughters:
**88** Pidray, Girl of Light,
**89** Tallay, Girl of Rain.

**90** Shapsh, the Torch of the Gods, spoke:
**91** "Rule, O Rider of the Clouds!
**92** Behold, your enemy you have struck!
**93** Behold, your foe you have vanquished!
**94** Take your eternal kingdom,
**95** your dominion everlasting!"

**96** [Colophon: The Scribe: Ilimilku of Shubbanu. The text was dictated by Attanu-Purlianni, chief priest of the temple.]
