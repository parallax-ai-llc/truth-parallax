**Section V: The Search for Baal and Anat's Vengeance**

**1** Gapn and Ugar went to El,
**2** at the source of the rivers,
**3** amid the channels of the two deeps.

**4** They penetrated the domain of El and entered
**5** the pavilion of the King, Father of Years.

**6** At El's feet they bowed and fell,
**7** prostrated and honoured him.
**8** They raised their voices and cried:

**9** "Mighty Baal is dead!
**10** The Prince, Lord of the Earth, has perished!"

**11** Thereupon the Kindly One, El the Benign,
**12** descended from his throne; he sat on the footstool.
**13** From the footstool he sat on the ground.

**14** He poured straw of mourning on his head,
**15** dust of wallowing on his crown.
**16** For clothing he covered himself with sackcloth.

**17** He gashed his skin with a stone,
**18** with a razor he cut his side-whiskers and beard.
**19** He harrowed his arms,
**20** he ploughed his chest like a garden,
**21** he harrowed his back like a valley.

**22** He raised his voice and cried:
**23** "Baal is dead! What becomes of the people?
**24** The son of Dagon! What becomes of the multitudes?
**25** After Baal I shall descend into the earth."

**26** Anat also went wandering,
**27** searching every mountain to the heart of the earth,
**28** every hill to the heart of the fields.

**29** She came to the pleasant land of the pasture,
**30** to the beautiful field of Shlmmt.
**31** She came upon Baal fallen on the ground:

**32** Mighty Baal was dead,
**33** the Prince, Lord of the Earth, had perished.

**34** The virgin Anat wept.
**35** She poured straw of mourning on her head,
**36** dust of wallowing on her crown.
**37** For clothing she covered herself with sackcloth.

**38** She gashed her skin with a stone,
**39** with a razor she cut her side-whiskers.
**40** She harrowed her arms,
**41** she ploughed her chest like a garden,
**42** she harrowed her back like a valley.

**43** "Baal is dead! What becomes of the people?
**44** The son of Dagon! What becomes of the multitudes?
**45** After Baal let us descend into the earth."

**46** With her, Shapsh, the Torch of the Gods, descended.
**47** Until she was sated with weeping,
**48** she drank tears like wine.

**49** Aloud she cried to the Torch of the Gods, to Shapsh:
**50** "Load me, pray, with Mighty Baal."

**51** Shapsh, the Torch of the Gods, obeyed.
**52** She lifted Mighty Baal onto the shoulders of Anat.
**53** She took him up to the heights of Zaphon.
**54** She wept for him and buried him.
**55** She placed him in the hollows of the earth-ghosts.

**56** She slaughtered seventy wild oxen
**57** as a tribute to Mighty Baal.
**58** She slaughtered seventy cattle,
**59** seventy sheep, seventy deer,
**60** seventy mountain-goats, seventy asses.

**61** [Gap in text]

**62** Then Anat set face toward El,
**63** at the source of the rivers,
**64** amid the channels of the two deeps.

**65** She penetrated the domain of El and entered
**66** the pavilion of the King, Father of Years.

**67** El called to Lady Athirat of the Sea:
**68** "Listen, O Lady Athirat of the Sea:
**69** give one of your sons that I make him king."

**70** Lady Athirat of the Sea replied:
**71** "Shall we not make king one who knows how to reign?"

**72** The Kindly One, El the Benign, replied:
**73** "One small in strength cannot race with Baal,
**74** cannot wield the lance alongside the son of Dagon."

**75** Lady Athirat of the Sea replied:
**76** "Let us make Athtar the Terrible king!
**77** Let Athtar the Terrible reign!"

**78** Then Athtar the Terrible went up to the heights of Zaphon.
**79** He sat on the throne of Mighty Baal.
**80** His feet did not reach the footstool,
**81** his head did not reach the top.

**82** Athtar the Terrible spoke:
**83** "I cannot reign on the heights of Zaphon."

**84** Athtar the Terrible descended from the throne of Mighty Baal.
**85** He descended and ruled over the earth, god of it all.

**86** [Gap in text]

**87** A day, days passed;
**88** months became years.
**89** The virgin Anat sought him.

**90** Like the heart of a cow for her calf,
**91** like the heart of a ewe for her lamb,
**92** so was the heart of Anat after Baal.

**93** She seized Mot, the son of El.
**94** With a sword she split him.
**95** With a sieve she winnowed him.
**96** With fire she burnt him.

**97** With millstones she ground him.
**98** In the fields she sowed him.
**99** The birds ate his flesh,
**100** the sparrows consumed his parts.

**101** Flesh cried out to flesh.
