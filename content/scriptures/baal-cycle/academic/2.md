The second tablet narrates Baal's decisive battle against Yamm. The divine craftsman Kothar-wa-Khasis (Skillful-and-Wise) fashions two maces for Baal, naming them Yagarrish ('Driver') and Ayyamari ('Expeller'). The first blow merely staggers Yamm; the second strikes him between the eyes and brings him crashing down. Baal's victory is celebrated, though Athtart (Astarte) rebukes Baal for mutilating the fallen Yamm.

Smith's commentary analyzes the battle scene as drawing on royal military iconography: the storm god's victory over Sea reflects the Ugaritic king's idealized triumph over external enemies.
