The third tablet presents two major scenes: Baal's victory banquet and Anat's independent military exploits. Anat, Baal's sister and ally, is depicted in a scene of extraordinary violence — she wades through blood and gore, fighting human warriors in her palace. The scene has been analyzed as either a mythological battle, a ritual enactment, or a literary expression of the goddess's warrior nature.

Scholarship has debated Anat's relationship to Baal: sister, consort, or both? The text uses the term 'maiden' (*btlt*) for Anat, which Neal Walls has interpreted as indicating her perpetual virginity and warrior status rather than a sexual relationship with Baal.
