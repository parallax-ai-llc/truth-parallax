The fifth tablet narrates Baal's confrontation with Mot (Death) and his descent to the underworld. After Baal opens the window in his palace and sends a challenge to Mot, Death responds with a terrifying message: 'My appetite is that of the lion in the wasteland... My throat swallows mightily.' Baal must descend to Mot's realm, and the text states starkly: 'Baal has fallen to the earth; dead is Almighty Baal; perished is the Prince, Lord of the Earth.'

Smith analyzes Baal's death as the theological crisis of the cycle: if the king of the gods can die, what does divine kingship mean? The text presents Baal's death as a genuine catastrophe, not a voluntary descent.
