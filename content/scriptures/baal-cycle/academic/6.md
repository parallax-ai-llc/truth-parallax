The sixth tablet narrates the resolution of the cycle: Anat finds Mot, attacks him with furious violence ('she seized Mot, son of El; with a sword she split him, with a sieve she winnowed him, with fire she burned him, with millstones she ground him'), and Baal is eventually restored to his throne. The agricultural imagery of Anat's treatment of Mot — winnowing, grinding, sowing — connects the mythological narrative to the agricultural cycle.

Smith and Pitard analyze the conclusion as establishing a pattern of cyclical conflict: Mot is not permanently destroyed but returns to challenge Baal, suggesting a seasonal or agricultural interpretation. The text concludes with the sun goddess Shapsh mediating between the two, establishing a cosmic balance.
