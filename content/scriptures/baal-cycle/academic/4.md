The fourth tablet, the best preserved of the six, narrates the building of Baal's palace on Mount Sapan (Jebel Aqra, on the Syrian-Turkish border). After negotiations involving Anat, Athirat (Asherah), and El, permission is granted. Kothar-wa-Khasis builds the palace, and a notable debate occurs over whether to include a window — Baal initially refuses, then relents. The palace's completion is celebrated with a great feast.

Smith interprets the palace-building as the central event of the cycle: Baal's palace is the visible sign of his kingship, the cosmic house that establishes his sovereignty. The window debate has been analyzed as foreshadowing Baal's vulnerability to Mot (Death), who may enter through the window.
