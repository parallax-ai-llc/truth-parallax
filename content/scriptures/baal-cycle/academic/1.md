The first tablet (KTU 1.1) introduces the conflict between Baal and Yamm (Sea/River). Yamm sends messengers to El's divine assembly demanding that Baal be surrendered as tribute. El, presiding over the assembly on Mount Lel, appears to capitulate. The tablet is heavily damaged, and scholarly reconstruction of the sequence of events relies heavily on Mark S. Smith's comprehensive commentary.

The tablet establishes the political structure of the Ugaritic divine world: El is the supreme authority but exercises his power through the assembly; Yamm has been granted royal authority by El; and Baal's position is contested and insecure.
