Mot (Death) is not well attested as a cult figure at Ugarit — there is no known temple of Mot, and he does not appear in offering lists. This absence from the cultic record is itself significant: Mot is a theological concept (the inevitability of death) rather than a deity who received worship. The archaeological evidence thus confirms the text's portrayal of Mot as the adversary of the divine order rather than a member of it.

The mourning rituals described in the text — gashing the flesh, pouring dust, donning sackcloth — are well attested in the archaeological and textual record of the Levant and correspond to mourning practices documented in both Ugaritic ritual texts and biblical descriptions.
