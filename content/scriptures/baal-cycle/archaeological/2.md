The battle between Baal and Yamm is depicted in Ugaritic art, including cylinder seals showing a figure (identified as Baal) striking a serpentine creature with a weapon. These seals provide visual confirmation of the mythology preserved in the text. The mace, Baal's characteristic weapon, appears frequently in Syrian and Levantine iconography of the Late Bronze Age.

The divine craftsman Kothar-wa-Khasis has been connected to the Greek Hephaestus and the Egyptian Ptah — all divine artisans who create weapons and objects of power. Metallurgical workshops at Ugarit and nearby sites provide the material context for the mythology of divine craftsmanship.
