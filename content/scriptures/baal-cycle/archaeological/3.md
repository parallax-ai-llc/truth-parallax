The warrior goddess Anat is well attested in the archaeological record of the Levant. Her name appears in place names (Beth-Anath, Anathoth), personal names, and inscriptions across the region. Terracotta figurines depicting armed female figures have been found at Ugarit and other Levantine sites, possibly representing Anat or related goddesses.

The banquet scene described in the tablet corresponds to archaeological evidence of feasting at Ugarit, including the *marzihu* institution — a religious feast association that combined dining, drinking, and ritual mourning. Large quantities of fine pottery, wine jars, and animal bones found in contexts associated with temples confirm the importance of ritual feasting.
