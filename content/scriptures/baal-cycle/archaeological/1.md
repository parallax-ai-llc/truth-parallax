KTU 1.1 was found in the house of the high priest at Ras Shamra, inscribed on a large clay tablet in Ugaritic alphabetic cuneiform. The tablet's discovery in 1930 was part of the initial wave of finds that revolutionized understanding of pre-Israelite Canaan. The context — between the temples of Baal and Dagan — confirms the text's cultic significance.

The divine assembly described in the text has been connected to the architectural layout of Ugarit's sacred precinct, where multiple temples dedicated to different deities suggest a structured pantheon reflected in both built space and literary text.
