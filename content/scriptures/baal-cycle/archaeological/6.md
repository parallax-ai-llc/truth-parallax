The agricultural practices described in Anat's treatment of Mot — threshing, winnowing, grinding, sowing — correspond precisely to grain-processing techniques documented in the archaeological record of Late Bronze Age Syria. Basalt grinding stones, flint sickle blades, and threshing floors have been excavated at Ugarit and surrounding sites, confirming that the mythological imagery draws directly on material agricultural practice.

The seasonal interpretation of the Baal-Mot conflict is supported by Ugaritic ritual texts that prescribe offerings and ceremonies at key agricultural moments — sowing, harvest, and the period of summer drought when Baal (rain) is absent and Mot (death/dryness) prevails.
