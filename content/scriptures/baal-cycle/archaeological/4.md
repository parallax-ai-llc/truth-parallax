Mount Sapan, identified with Jebel Aqra (ancient Mount Hazzi/Casius) on the Syrian-Turkish border, is a prominent peak visible from Ugarit. The mountain reaches 1,717 meters and is notable for its dramatic weather — it attracts lightning storms, confirming its association with the storm god. Archaeological survey has identified cult installations on the mountain's slopes, though the summit remains unexplored.

The palace-building motif connects to the material reality of temple construction at Ugarit. The temple of Baal on the acropolis of Ras Shamra has been partially excavated, revealing a large stone structure with a courtyard and main sanctuary that may have been understood as the earthly counterpart of Baal's celestial palace on Mount Sapan.
