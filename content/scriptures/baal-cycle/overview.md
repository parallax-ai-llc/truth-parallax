## Overview

The Baal Cycle (KTU 1.1–1.6) is the most extensive mythological composition recovered from ancient Ugarit (modern Ras Shamra, Syria), inscribed on six clay tablets in the Ugaritic alphabetic cuneiform script and dating to approximately 1400–1200 BCE. The cycle narrates the storm god Baal's struggle for kingship among the gods, his battles against Yamm (Sea) and Mot (Death), and his ultimate establishment as divine king with a palace on Mount Sapan (Jebel Aqra). The tablets were discovered in the house of the high priest during French excavations beginning in 1929.

## Structure

The six tablets of the Baal Cycle narrate an interconnected sequence of conflicts and triumphs:

1. **Tablet 1 (KTU 1.1)**: The challenge of Yamm — Sea demands Baal as tribute from El's divine assembly
2. **Tablet 2 (KTU 1.2)**: Baal's battle with Yamm — defeat of Sea with the weapons crafted by Kothar-wa-Khasis
3. **Tablet 3 (KTU 1.3)**: Baal's banquet and Anat's battles — the warrior goddess's ferocity
4. **Tablet 4 (KTU 1.4)**: The building of Baal's palace on Mount Sapan
5. **Tablet 5 (KTU 1.5)**: Baal's descent to the underworld — Mot swallows Baal
6. **Tablet 6 (KTU 1.6)**: Baal's return — Anat defeats Mot, and Baal is restored to his throne

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Baal Cycle is indispensable for understanding Canaanite religion and its relationship to biblical tradition:

- **Academic**: The foundational text of Ugaritic studies, essential for Semitic linguistics and ancient Near Eastern religion
- **Comparative**: Illuminates biblical polemics against Baal worship, parallels with the Chaoskampf motif, and connections to Greek mythology (Zeus vs. Typhon)
- **Theological**: Explores divine kingship, the problem of death, and the cyclical renewal of cosmic order
- **Literary**: A masterwork of ancient Semitic poetry, employing parallelism, epithets, and formulaic language
- **Archaeological**: The tablets' discovery at Ras Shamra revolutionized understanding of pre-Israelite Canaan
- **Interfaith**: Provides essential context for biblical language about God's sovereignty over sea and death

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Chaoskampf | The cosmic battle against Sea (Yamm) as primordial chaos |
| Divine Kingship | Baal's palace as the visible sign of his sovereignty |
| Death and Resurrection | Baal's descent to Mot's realm and his eventual return |
| The Divine Assembly | El presides over the council of gods on Mount Lel |
| Fertility and Storm | Baal as provider of rain and agricultural abundance |
