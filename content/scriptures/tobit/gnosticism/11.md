Gnostic reading of Tobit 11 would interpret the restoration of Tobit's sight as the central soteriological event: the reception of gnosis that opens the spiritual eyes and allows the soul to perceive the truth hidden behind the material world's deceptive appearances. The fish gall applied to Tobit's eyes represents the revealed knowledge that removes the scales of ignorance, a powerful symbol of the Gnostic conversion experience.

The reunion of the family, with each member now participating in the blessings brought by the divine messenger, represents the gathering of the community of the enlightened. Tobit's praise upon seeing mirrors the Gnostic hymns of thanksgiving found in texts like the Gospel of Truth, where the soul that receives gnosis breaks into joyful recognition of its true nature and origin. The homecoming is not merely a return to a house but a return to awareness of the divine homeland.
