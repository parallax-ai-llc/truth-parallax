Gnostic interpretation of Tobit 7 would read the marriage of Tobias and Sarah as a hieros gamos, a sacred marriage that restores the original unity of paired divine beings (syzygies). In Valentinian Gnosticism, the Pleroma consists of paired Aeons, and the tragedy of the material world began with the separation of Sophia from her consort. Every earthly marriage that is truly blessed represents a recovery of this lost unity.

Sarah's liberation from Asmodeus through her union with Tobias, guided by the divine messenger, would symbolize the soul's liberation from archonic bondage through reunion with its divine counterpart. The fact that previous husbands died while Tobias survives indicates that only the pneumatic, the one guided by gnosis, can achieve this sacred union. Raguel's preparation of a grave that goes unused foreshadows the defeat of death itself through the power of revealed knowledge.
