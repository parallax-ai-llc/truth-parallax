A Gnostic reading of Tobit 1 would interpret Tobit's exile in Nineveh as a metaphor for the soul's imprisonment in the material world. The Gnostic myth of the divine spark trapped in matter finds a narrative parallel in Tobit's situation: a righteous soul living among pagans, maintaining fidelity to a higher truth that the surrounding world cannot perceive or appreciate. His acts of burying the dead, which bring him into conflict with earthly authorities, symbolize the Gnostic's countercultural commitment to spiritual reality over material power.

Tobit's piety in exile would be read as the faint memory of the Pleroma that the divine spark retains even in the material prison. His observance of spiritual practices amid a hostile environment mirrors the Gnostic pneumatic who preserves secret knowledge while living in a world ruled by archons. The chapter sets up a narrative of alienation and hidden faithfulness that resonates deeply with the Gnostic experience.
