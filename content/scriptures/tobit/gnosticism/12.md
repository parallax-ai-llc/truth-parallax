Gnostic interpretation of Tobit 12 would read Raphael's self-revelation as the climactic unveiling of the divine messenger's true identity, the moment when the disguised emissary from the Pleroma reveals himself and delivers his final teaching before ascending back to the transcendent realm. This pattern of descent, disguised presence, revelation, and ascent is the fundamental structure of Gnostic soteriology, found in the Hymn of the Pearl and the Apocryphon of John.

Raphael's identification as one of the seven who stand before God places him in the highest angelic hierarchy, but a Gnostic reading would distinguish between the true God of the Pleroma and the Demiurge. Raphael serves the transcendent God, not the creator of the material world. His ascension from the family's sight symbolizes the return of the divine messenger to the Pleroma after completing his salvific mission, leaving behind the knowledge (gnosis) that the recipients must now preserve and transmit.
