A Gnostic reading of Tobit 9 would note that the retrieval of money, the ostensible purpose of the journey, is accomplished almost as an afterthought by the divine messenger. This relegation of material concerns to secondary status reflects the Gnostic hierarchy of values, in which spiritual liberation far outweighs material prosperity.

Raphael's ability to accomplish the material task effortlessly while orchestrating the far more important spiritual liberation illustrates the Gnostic teaching that the divine emissary operates on multiple levels simultaneously. The material world's concerns, while not ignored, are subordinate to the work of salvation. Gabael's invitation to the wedding feast extends the circle of those who witness the liberating event, reflecting the Gnostic community's gathering around the revealed knowledge.
