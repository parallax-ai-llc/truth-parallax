## Overview

No surviving Gnostic text provides a direct commentary on Tobit, but the book's themes invite Gnostic-style interpretation. The narrative's concern with hidden angelic identity, demonic oppression, spiritual blindness, and the revelation of secret knowledge aligns with motifs central to Gnostic thought. A Gnostic reading of Tobit would likely focus on Raphael as a revealer figure and Tobit's blindness as a symbol of ignorance in the material world.

## Gnostic-Resonant Themes

The angel Raphael travels in disguise, revealing his true identity only at the end, a pattern that mirrors the Gnostic motif of the divine messenger who descends into the material world incognito to deliver saving knowledge. Tobit's physical blindness would be read as a metaphor for the spiritual blindness of souls trapped in matter, and his healing as an awakening to gnosis. The demon Asmodeus, who afflicts Sarah, could be interpreted as an archon who claims possession over souls in the material realm.

## Speculative Reading

In a Gnostic framework, the fish whose organs provide healing might be seen as a symbol from the natural world that, properly understood through revealed knowledge, becomes an instrument of liberation. The entire journey of Tobias, guided by a hidden angel toward truth and healing, would resemble the Gnostic narrative of the soul's journey out of ignorance, aided by a divine emissary from the Pleroma.
