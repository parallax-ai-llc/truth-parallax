Gnostic interpretation of Tobit 2 would read Tobit's blindness as the quintessential symbol of the human condition in the material world. All souls in matter are blind, unable to perceive the true light of the Pleroma that exists beyond the visible cosmos. Tobit's physical affliction becomes a metaphor for the universal spiritual blindness that gnosis alone can cure.

The cause of blindness, bird droppings falling into his eyes while he performs a righteous act, would be read as illustrating the arbitrary cruelty of the material world, where the Demiurge's creation operates without regard for justice or merit. This is not punishment but simply the nature of existence in a flawed cosmos. The Gnostic reader would see in Tobit's suffering the condition of all pneumatics before they receive the saving knowledge that restores their spiritual sight.
