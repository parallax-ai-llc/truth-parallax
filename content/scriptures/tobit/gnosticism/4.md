Gnostic interpretation of Tobit 4 might read Tobit's instructions to Tobias as preparatory teaching before the soul's journey toward liberation. In Gnostic texts, the soul must be prepared with specific knowledge before undertaking the ascent through the archonic spheres. Tobit's moral instruction would be understood as the exoteric (outer) teaching that precedes the esoteric (inner) revelation that Raphael will later provide.

The emphasis on righteous conduct and charitable giving, while affirmed on the surface, would be understood by Gnostic readers as belonging to the realm of the Demiurge's law, necessary but insufficient for true salvation. The deeper teaching, the gnosis that liberates, comes not from human parents but from the divine messenger. This distinction between conventional morality and salvific knowledge is fundamental to Gnostic soteriology.
