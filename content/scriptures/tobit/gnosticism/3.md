A Gnostic reading of Tobit 3 would interpret Asmodeus as an archon who claims dominion over Sarah, killing any mortal who attempts to unite with her. In Gnostic mythology, archons are jealous rulers who seek to prevent the divine spark from escaping their control. Sarah's seven dead husbands represent the soul's repeated failed attempts to achieve liberation through ordinary means, without the aid of revealed knowledge.

The dispatch of Raphael in response to the prayers of Tobit and Sarah mirrors the Gnostic narrative of a divine emissary sent from the Pleroma to liberate trapped souls. The key Gnostic insight is that the material world's problems cannot be solved by material means alone; a messenger from beyond the cosmos must intervene. The simultaneous prayers reaching God together suggest that the scattered divine sparks are connected by their common origin in the transcendent realm.
