Gnostic reading of Tobit 5 would identify Raphael as the central Gnostic figure of the narrative: the divine emissary from the Pleroma who descends into the material world in disguise. The pattern of a heavenly being concealing his true identity and traveling incognito among mortals is a defining motif of Gnostic revelation literature, found in the Apocryphon of John, the Hymn of the Pearl, and other Gnostic texts.

Raphael's assumption of the name Azariah ('God has helped') and a false genealogy represents the necessity for the divine messenger to adopt the forms and conventions of the material world in order to operate within it. The Demiurge and his archons cannot recognize the emissary in disguise. Tobias, who does not yet know the truth, represents the soul before gnosis, who follows the divine guide without yet understanding the full nature of the reality being revealed.
