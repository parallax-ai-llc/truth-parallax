Gnostic reading of Tobit 8 would interpret the wedding night as the climactic moment of liberation. The burning of the fish organs to expel Asmodeus represents the application of revealed knowledge to defeat the archonic power that has imprisoned the soul. The demon's flight to Egypt, where Raphael binds him, symbolizes the final confinement of the archonic force, removed from its ability to oppress the liberated pneumatics.

The prayer of Tobias and Sarah, invoking God's creation of Adam and Eve, would be reinterpreted in Gnostic fashion. The true creation is not the Demiurge's fashioning of material bodies but the original emanation of paired Aeons in the Pleroma. The couple's prayer reaches beyond the material creator to the transcendent God who is the source of all genuine union. The unused grave represents the defeat of material death through spiritual awakening, a central Gnostic teaching.
