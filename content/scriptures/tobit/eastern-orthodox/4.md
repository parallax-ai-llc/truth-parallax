Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 4 reads Tobit's instructions as a transmission of holy tradition from parent to child, reflecting the Orthodox emphasis on the family as the primary site of spiritual formation. The detailed ethical teaching on almsgiving, marriage, and just dealings mirrors the moral instruction found in Orthodox catechesis and the writings of the Church Fathers.

The chapter's emphasis on almsgiving resonates particularly with Orthodox social theology. The Fathers of the Church, especially Basil the Great and John Chrysostom, taught extensively on the obligation of charitable giving, and Tobit's instructions align with their vision of wealth as a stewardship to be used for the benefit of the poor. The advice to marry within the community of faith reflects the Orthodox canonical expectation that marriages be blessed within the Church.
