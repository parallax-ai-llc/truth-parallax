Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 10 draws on the theme of patient waiting, which resonates deeply with the Orthodox liturgical experience. The long periods of waiting in Orthodox worship, the extended vigils and services, train the faithful in the kind of patient endurance that Tobit and Hannah must exercise in this chapter. Their anxiety and prayer model the honest struggle of faith that the Orthodox tradition does not suppress but sanctifies.

The chapter's portrayal of family bonds under strain illustrates the Orthodox understanding that the domestic church is the arena where faith is most intimately tested. Hannah's maternal grief and Tobit's faltering reassurances are read with pastoral sympathy by Orthodox commentators, who recognize that the path to holiness passes through, not around, the ordinary sufferings of family life.
