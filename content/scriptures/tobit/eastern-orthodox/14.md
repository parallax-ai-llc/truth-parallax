Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 14 reads Tobit's peaceful death as a model of the Christian repose. In the Orthodox funeral service, the departed is commended to God with the prayer that they may rest 'where the righteous repose.' Tobit's death at peace, surrounded by his family, with his faith vindicated and his legacy secured, illustrates this ideal.

The chapter's prophetic dimension, with Tobit foreseeing the destruction of Nineveh and the restoration of Israel, reflects the Orthodox understanding that the righteous are granted prophetic insight as a gift of the Holy Spirit. His final instructions to maintain righteousness and almsgiving serve as a spiritual testament, a genre familiar in Orthodox hagiography, where many saints deliver final teachings to their disciples before their departure to the Lord.
