Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 5 finds in Raphael's companionship the scriptural basis for the Orthodox devotion to guardian angels. The Orthodox Church teaches that every baptized Christian is accompanied by a guardian angel, and Raphael's concealed presence alongside Tobias illustrates this belief in vivid narrative form. The troparion for the Guardian Angel in Orthodox hymnography echoes the themes of this chapter.

The journey motif in this chapter is read by Orthodox exegetes as an image of the Christian life itself, a pilgrimage through the world with an unseen angelic companion guiding the faithful toward their divine destination. The fact that Raphael's true identity remains hidden underscores the Orthodox teaching that the spiritual world is present and active even when imperceptible to the senses, a principle central to Orthodox sacramental theology and iconographic tradition.
