Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 11 reads the restoration of Tobit's sight as a type of spiritual illumination, the movement from darkness to light that characterizes the baptismal experience. In the Orthodox baptismal liturgy, the newly baptized are called the 'newly illumined,' and Tobit's recovery of sight prefigures this sacramental illumination.

The reunion of the family and Tobit's spontaneous praise connect to the Orthodox theology of doxology, the proper response of the creature to divine grace. Orthodox worship is fundamentally an act of thanksgiving, and Tobit's response models the liturgical posture of gratitude. The chapter's emphasis on physical healing also reflects the Orthodox understanding of salvation as encompassing the whole person, body and soul, not merely a spiritual abstraction.
