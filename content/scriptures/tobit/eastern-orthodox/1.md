Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 1 emphasizes Tobit's righteousness as an expression of covenant faithfulness maintained in exile. His commitment to burying the dead, even at the risk of persecution, resonates with the Orthodox reverence for the departed and the Church's funeral traditions. The Orthodox understanding of the communion between the living and the dead finds a narrative precursor in Tobit's insistence that the bodies of the deceased deserve honor.

The chapter presents Tobit as a model of ascetic virtue amid a hostile environment. His fasting, almsgiving, and adherence to the Law despite the surrounding pagan culture reflect the Orthodox ideal of living as a pilgrim in the world. Orthodox patristic commentary reads Tobit's exile as an image of the Christian's sojourning in a fallen world, maintaining faithfulness while awaiting the heavenly homeland.
