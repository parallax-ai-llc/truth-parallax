Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 2 reads Tobit's blindness as a trial permitted by God for the purification and perfection of the righteous man's soul. In the Orthodox tradition of theosis, suffering is understood not as punishment but as a means through which God refines the believer, burning away attachment to earthly comfort and drawing the soul closer to divine likeness.

The exchange between Tobit and Anna reflects the Orthodox understanding that even the saints experience moments of testing in their relationships. Tobit's patience, though imperfect, illustrates the gradual process of sanctification that Orthodox theology describes. The physical blindness is read by the Fathers as a symbol of the spiritual darkness that precedes illumination, a pattern found in the lives of many Orthodox saints who passed through periods of desolation before receiving deeper communion with God.
