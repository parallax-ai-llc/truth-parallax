Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 12 regards Raphael's self-revelation as a key text for Orthodox angelology. The seven angels who stand before God's glory (12:15) are associated in Orthodox tradition with the seven archangels venerated in the Church's liturgical calendar. The Synaxis of the Archangels, celebrated on November 8, honors Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, and others in the angelic hierarchy.

Raphael's teaching on prayer, fasting, and almsgiving (12:8-10) aligns with the Orthodox triad of ascetic practices that form the core of spiritual discipline. The angel's statement that he presented their prayers before God reinforces the Orthodox understanding of angels as liturgical beings who participate in the heavenly worship mirrored in the Divine Liturgy. His ascension from their sight prefigures the Christ's Ascension and the Orthodox theology of divine hiddenness, in which God's presence is real but veiled from mortal eyes.
