Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 13 reads the hymn as a prophetic canticle that anticipates the eschatological glorification of the New Jerusalem. The vision of a city rebuilt with precious stones resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the heavenly kingdom, where the transfigured creation shines with uncreated divine light. This imagery connects to the Orthodox theology of theosis, in which all creation is destined to be transfigured.

The hymn is used in Orthodox worship as a canticle, incorporated into the cycle of prayer that structures the liturgical day. Its movement from lamentation through confession to triumphant praise mirrors the structure of the Orthodox liturgical year, which moves from the penitential seasons through Pascha to the joy of Pentecost. The hymn's call for all nations to praise God reflects the Orthodox Church's understanding of its universal mission.
