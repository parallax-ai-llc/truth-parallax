Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 9 reads Raphael's retrieval of the money as an illustration of the divine economy, in which God's plan encompasses and fulfills all human needs, both material and spiritual. The Orthodox concept of divine oikonomia, God's providential management of creation for the salvation of all, finds a small but telling illustration in this chapter.

The bringing of Gabael to the wedding feast extends the circle of blessing and communion, reflecting the Orthodox understanding that God's grace is never private but communal. The chapter, though brief, demonstrates the Orthodox conviction that no detail of life falls outside God's providential care, a teaching central to Orthodox spiritual theology and expressed in the common Orthodox prayer 'Lord, have mercy.'
