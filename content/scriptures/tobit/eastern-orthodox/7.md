Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 7 reads the marriage of Tobias and Sarah as a scriptural model for the Orthodox Mystery (Sacrament) of Marriage. The exchange of consent, the father's blessing, and the writing of the contract reflect elements preserved in the Orthodox marriage service, which includes crowning, the common cup, and the blessing of the priest. The Orthodox Rite of Marriage draws explicitly on Tobit's wedding imagery.

Raguel's faith, trusting God despite the apparent danger, exemplifies the Orthodox understanding that the sacraments require faith even when circumstances seem impossible. The Orthodox theology of marriage as a path to holiness and theosis finds narrative support in this chapter, where marriage is presented not merely as a social contract but as a divinely ordained union blessed by God through angelic mediation.
