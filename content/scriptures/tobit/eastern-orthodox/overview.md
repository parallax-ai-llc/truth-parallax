## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox Church receives Tobit as canonical scripture, following the Septuagint tradition that has always included it. Orthodox interpretation emphasizes the book's witness to the reality of the angelic world, the power of prayer, and the sanctification of marriage as central themes consistent with Orthodox theology and spiritual life.

## Theological Emphases

Orthodox readers find in Tobit a vivid illustration of the communion between the visible and invisible worlds. Raphael's presence alongside Tobias exemplifies the Orthodox belief in guardian angels and angelic intercession. The book's emphasis on burying the dead connects to Orthodox funeral theology and the reverence shown to the departed. Tobit's faithful endurance through suffering reflects the Orthodox understanding of purification and the path toward theosis.

## Liturgical and Devotional Use

Tobit is read in Orthodox lectionary cycles, particularly in connection with marriage services. The prayer of Tobias and Sarah on their wedding night (8:5-7) is among the most beloved marriage texts in Orthodox tradition. The book also features in patristic homilies and iconographic traditions, where Raphael is depicted as one of the three named archangels venerated in Orthodox worship.
