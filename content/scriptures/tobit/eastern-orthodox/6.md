Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 6 reads the encounter with the fish and Raphael's instructions within the Orthodox understanding of creation as charged with spiritual significance. Material creation, in Orthodox theology, is not inert matter but participates in God's purposes. The fish organs becoming instruments of healing and deliverance illustrates the principle that all creation can serve as a vehicle of divine grace.

This understanding connects to Orthodox sacramental theology, in which water, oil, bread, and wine become bearers of God's transforming power. The chapter's narrative of natural elements deployed against demonic forces prefigures the Orthodox practice of blessing water, oil, and other elements for the healing and protection of the faithful. Orthodox commentary reads this not as magic but as the proper ordering of creation under God's authority, mediated through angelic instruction.
