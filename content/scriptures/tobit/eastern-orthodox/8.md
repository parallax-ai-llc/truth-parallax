Orthodox exegesis of Tobit 8 centers on the wedding night prayer of Tobias and Sarah (8:5-7), which is incorporated into the Orthodox marriage service. The prayer's invocation of God as Creator of Adam and Eve grounds marriage in the theology of creation and reflects the Orthodox understanding of marriage as a restoration of the original unity between man and woman intended by God.

The expulsion of the demon Asmodeus through the burning of the fish organs and prayer illustrates the Orthodox theology of spiritual warfare, in which demonic powers are defeated through the combination of prayer and blessed material elements. The chapter's resolution, in which death gives way to life and the prepared grave goes unused, is read as a Paschal image: the victory of life over death through divine intervention, foreshadowing the empty tomb of Christ.
