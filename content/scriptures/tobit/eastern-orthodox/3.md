Orthodox interpretation of Tobit 3 highlights the liturgical dimension of prayer. The simultaneous prayers of Tobit and Sarah, ascending to God and being heard together, illustrate the Orthodox theology of corporate prayer and the communion of the faithful. In the Divine Liturgy, the prayers of all the faithful are offered together, and this chapter provides a narrative example of how God hears the interconnected prayers of his people.

The dispatch of Raphael in response to prayer demonstrates the Orthodox belief in the active ministry of angels. Orthodox worship includes frequent invocations of angelic protection and intercession. The affliction of Sarah by the demon Asmodeus is read within the Orthodox framework of spiritual warfare, in which demonic powers are real but ultimately subject to God's authority. The chapter teaches that God's response to suffering may not come immediately or in the form expected, but it comes through the ministry of his heavenly servants.
