Tobit 10 portrays the anxiety of Tobit and Hannah as they wait for Tobias's delayed return. Hannah weeps daily and fears her son is dead, while Tobit tries to reassure her. The chapter offers a poignant portrait of parental love and the tension between faith and fear.

Protestant readers find this chapter deeply human and relatable. The honest depiction of a mother's grief and a father's wavering reassurance resonates with the canonical Psalms of lament, where faith and distress coexist. The chapter demonstrates that the book of Tobit, whatever its canonical status, understands the emotional texture of family life with genuine insight. Protestant pastoral theology can draw on such narratives as illustrations of the struggle to trust God when circumstances are dark.
