Tobit 7 describes Tobias's arrival at the home of Raguel in Ecbatana and the arrangement of his marriage to Sarah. Raguel, aware that Sarah's previous seven husbands have all died, agrees to the marriage with trepidation and quietly prepares a grave. Protestant readers note the chapter's strong emphasis on marriage within the kinship community, a practice rooted in the Torah's laws of inheritance and tribal identity.

While Protestant theology does not require endogamous marriage in the same way, the principle of marrying within the community of faith resonates with New Testament teaching. The chapter's portrayal of Raguel's faith amid fear, agreeing to the marriage while preparing for the worst, illustrates a realistic and sympathetic picture of trust in God that does not eliminate human anxiety. Protestant commentators value this as honest and pastoral narrative.
