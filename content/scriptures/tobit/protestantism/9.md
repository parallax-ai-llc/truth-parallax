Tobit 9 is the briefest chapter, in which Raphael travels to Rages to collect the money Tobit had deposited with Gabael, bringing Gabael back to the wedding feast. Protestant readers note the chapter's efficient resolution of the financial subplot, which was the original stated purpose of Tobias's journey.

The chapter illustrates a pattern common in Providence narratives: the ostensible reason for a journey turns out to be secondary to God's larger purposes. Tobias went to collect money but found a wife, healing, and deliverance. Protestant interpreters draw a parallel with canonical stories like Joseph's journey to Egypt, where human plans serve as vehicles for divine purposes that far exceed the original intention.
