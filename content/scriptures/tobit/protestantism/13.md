Tobit 13 contains Tobit's hymn of praise, a psalm celebrating God's mercy, calling for repentance, and envisioning the future glory of Jerusalem rebuilt with precious stones. The hymn's structure and language closely parallel the canonical Psalms and the prophetic visions of Isaiah and Ezekiel.

Protestant readers recognize this chapter as one of the most theologically rich portions of the book. The hymn's vision of a restored and glorified Jerusalem anticipates the eschatological vision found in Revelation 21, where the New Jerusalem descends from heaven adorned with precious stones. While Protestants do not grant this hymn the same authority as the canonical Psalms, its theological vision and devotional quality are widely acknowledged. The chapter demonstrates the continuity between Tobit's piety and the canonical worship tradition.
