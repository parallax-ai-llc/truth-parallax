Tobit 14 concludes the book with Tobit's final instructions, his death at a great age, and Tobias's subsequent life and death in Media. Tobit prophesies the destruction of Nineveh and the rebuilding of the Temple, exhorting his family to maintain righteousness and almsgiving.

Protestant readers note the chapter's historical perspective: the prophecy of Nineveh's destruction aligns with the canonical book of Nahum, and the reference to the Temple's rebuilding places the narrative within the broader scope of Israel's history. The chapter serves as an epilogue that vindicates Tobit's lifelong faithfulness, demonstrating that God ultimately rewards the righteous. Protestant commentators appreciate this conclusion as consistent with the canonical wisdom tradition, even while maintaining that the book's authority remains in the category of edifying literature rather than inspired scripture.
