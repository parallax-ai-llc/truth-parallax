Tobit 12 is the pivotal revelation scene in which Raphael discloses his true identity as one of the seven angels who stand before God. He delivers a summary teaching on the value of prayer, fasting, and almsgiving, then ascends, leaving the family in awe. Protestant readers note that this chapter raises significant theological questions about angelology.

The concept of seven archangels who stand before God's throne appears in Revelation 8:2, providing a canonical point of contact. However, Protestant theology is generally cautious about developing an elaborate doctrine of angels beyond what Scripture clearly teaches. The chapter's moral instruction on almsgiving, while consistent with canonical ethics, is framed with an authority that Protestants reserve for canonical scripture alone. Nonetheless, the scene's dramatic power and Raphael's exhortation to praise God are widely appreciated.
