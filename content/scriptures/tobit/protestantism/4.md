Tobit 4 contains Tobit's ethical instructions to his son Tobias before sending him on a journey to retrieve money deposited in Media. The teachings cover almsgiving, honoring parents, endogamous marriage, fair wages, and avoiding excess. Protestant readers recognize these instructions as consistent with Old Testament wisdom literature, sharing themes with Proverbs and Sirach.

While Protestants would not derive doctrine from this chapter, its practical wisdom, particularly the injunction to give alms and to treat workers fairly, aligns with canonical ethical teaching. The instruction to marry within the community of faith has parallels in Paul's teaching (2 Corinthians 6:14). Protestant commentators note that Tobit's advice reflects the concerns of a faithful parent preparing his child for life in a challenging world, a universal theme that transcends questions of canonicity.
