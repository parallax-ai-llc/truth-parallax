Tobit 11 describes the homecoming of Tobias and the healing of Tobit's blindness through the application of the fish's gall to his eyes. Upon seeing his son and daughter-in-law, Tobit praises God and blesses Tobias. The chapter brings the narrative's central crisis to resolution.

Protestant interpreters read the healing as the narrative climax of a story about divine faithfulness. While the mechanism of healing through fish gall is unusual, the theological point is clear: God answers the prayers of the righteous in God's own time and manner. Protestant readers connect this to the canonical theme of God restoring sight, both physical and spiritual (Psalm 146:8, Isaiah 35:5), which ultimately finds its fullest expression in the healing miracles of Jesus.
