Tobit 8 describes the wedding night of Tobias and Sarah, during which Tobias burns the fish's heart and liver to drive away the demon Asmodeus, and the couple prays together before consummating their marriage. Raphael pursues and binds Asmodeus in Egypt. The prayer of Tobias and Sarah (8:5-7), invoking God's creation of Adam and Eve, is one of the most admired passages in the book.

Protestant commentators, while cautious about the exorcistic elements, appreciate the chapter's theology of marriage grounded in creation. The prayer's appeal to Genesis 2, asking God to bless their union as God blessed the first human pair, reflects a theology of marriage that Protestants affirm from the canonical text. The contrast between a marriage entered in prayer and the demon's destructive power over previous unions illustrates the Protestant conviction that God's blessing is essential for the flourishing of marriage.
