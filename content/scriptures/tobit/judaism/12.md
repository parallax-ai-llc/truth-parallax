Jewish scholarly analysis of Tobit 12 examines Raphael's self-identification as one of the seven angels before God's throne within the context of Second Temple Jewish angelology. The concept of seven archangels appears in 1 Enoch (20:1-8) and reflects a developed angelic hierarchy that was influential in Jewish thought before the rabbinic period. This hierarchy would later be moderated in rabbinic theology, which was cautious about excessive angelological speculation.

Raphael's teaching on prayer, fasting, and almsgiving as superior to wealth (12:8) reflects values central to Jewish ethics. The concept that acts of tzedakah deliver from death is found in Proverbs 10:2 and 11:4, providing a canonical foundation for the teaching. The chapter's angelological framework, while not adopted into the rabbinic canon, contributed to the development of Jewish mystical traditions, including the angelic liturgy of the Hekhalot literature.
