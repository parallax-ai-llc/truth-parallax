Jewish scholars read the healing of Tobit's blindness in chapter 11 within the broader context of healing narratives in Jewish literature. The restoration of sight carries symbolic weight in Jewish tradition, where seeing is associated with understanding and divine revelation. The prophetic hope that God will 'open the eyes of the blind' (Isaiah 35:5) provides a canonical frame for interpreting Tobit's healing.

The family reunion and Tobit's praise of God reflect the Jewish practice of reciting blessings (berakhot) for extraordinary events. The tradition of blessing God for restoring sight, attested in the morning blessings of the Jewish liturgy (Birkhot HaShachar), finds a narrative precursor in Tobit's grateful response. The chapter illustrates the Jewish conviction that healing is a gift from God deserving of formal acknowledgment and praise.
