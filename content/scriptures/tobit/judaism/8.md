Jewish scholarly interest in Tobit 8 focuses on the wedding night prayer (8:5-7), which invokes the creation of Adam and Eve as the theological foundation for marriage. This appeal to Genesis 2 reflects a tradition of marriage theology that would continue in rabbinic literature, where the creation narrative is central to the understanding of marriage as a divine institution.

The defeat of Asmodeus connects to the broader Jewish tradition of demonic beings who can be overcome through righteous prayer and divinely appointed means. The Talmudic narrative of Solomon and Ashmedai (Gittin 68a-b) reflects a later development of the same tradition. The chapter's depiction of prayer as the essential preparation for marriage anticipates the centrality of prayer in the Jewish wedding ceremony, including the Sheva Berakhot (Seven Blessings) that invoke God's creation of humanity.
