## Overview

Tobit is a deuterocanonical book set during the Assyrian exile, telling the intertwined stories of Tobit, a pious Israelite living in Nineveh, and Sarah, a young woman in Ecbatana plagued by a demon. The angel Raphael, sent by God in disguise, guides Tobit's son Tobias on a journey that brings healing to both households.

## Structure

The book weaves together two parallel narratives that converge through divine providence:

1. **Tobit's Story** (Chapters 1–3): A righteous man who buries the dead, is blinded, and prays for death
2. **Sarah's Story** (Chapter 3): A woman whose seven husbands have been killed by the demon Asmodeus
3. **Tobias's Journey** (Chapters 4–12): Guided by Raphael, Tobias heals his father and marries Sarah
4. **Tobit's Hymn and Death** (Chapters 13–14): A psalm of praise and final prophecy

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Tobit occupies a distinctive place across religious traditions:

- **Catholicism / Eastern Orthodoxy**: Canonical scripture, included in the Old Testament
- **Protestantism**: Classified as apocryphal; valued for edification but not doctrine
- **Judaism**: Not part of the Tanakh, but fragments found among the Dead Sea Scrolls attest to its early Jewish circulation
- **Islam**: No direct parallel, though themes of angelic guidance and piety resonate with Quranic narratives
