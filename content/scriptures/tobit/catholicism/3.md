Catholic interpretation of Tobit 3 highlights the theology of prayer and God's providential response. The simultaneous prayers of Tobit and Sarah, heard together by God and answered through the mission of Raphael, illustrate Catholic teaching on the communion of saints and the interconnectedness of the faithful before God. The Catechism teaches that the prayers of the righteous are powerful and that God responds through his angels and saints.

The figure of Asmodeus introduces Catholic demonology, which affirms the reality of demonic beings who can afflict individuals. The Church's teaching on spiritual warfare, including the ministry of exorcism, finds scriptural support in narratives like this. Catholic interpretation also sees in Sarah a figure of patient endurance and hope, her prayer expressing the kind of honest lament that the Psalms model. God's response through Raphael demonstrates that no prayer goes unheard, even when the answer comes through unexpected channels.
