Catholic exegesis of Tobit 7 reads the marriage arrangement between Tobias and Sarah as a precursor to the Church's sacramental understanding of marriage. The exchange of consent, the father's blessing, and the writing of a marriage contract reflect elements that would later be incorporated into the Catholic Rite of Marriage. The Council of Trent pointed to the book of Tobit as supporting the sacramental nature of matrimony.

Raguel's faith in allowing the marriage despite the deaths of Sarah's previous husbands illustrates the Catholic teaching on trust in divine providence even in the face of apparently insurmountable obstacles. His quiet preparation of a grave alongside the wedding preparations is read not as lack of faith but as the honest realism of a parent who must act while still trusting God. Catholic pastoral theology draws on this chapter's portrayal of marriage as a union blessed by God, entered with prayer, and sustained by divine grace.
