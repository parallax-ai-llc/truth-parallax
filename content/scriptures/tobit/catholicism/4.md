Catholic exegesis of Tobit 4 reads Tobit's instructions as a compendium of moral teaching consistent with the Law and the prophets. The emphasis on almsgiving, which Tobit calls a treasure stored up against the day of need (4:9), is foundational for Catholic social teaching and the theology of charitable works. The Council of Trent cited Tobit alongside canonical texts in its defense of the meritorious nature of good works.

The instructions on marriage within the community of faith, fair treatment of workers, and avoidance of excess reflect the natural law tradition that Catholic moral theology develops. Tobit's advice anticipates the social encyclicals of the modern papacy, particularly Rerum Novarum's teaching on just wages. Catholic catechesis frequently draws on this chapter as a model of parental responsibility in transmitting the faith, a theme emphasized in the Catechism's treatment of the domestic church.
