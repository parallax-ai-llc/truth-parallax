Catholic exegesis of Tobit 10 draws attention to the theme of parental love and the domestic dimension of faith. The anxiety of Tobit and Hannah as they await Tobias's return portrays the ordinary suffering of parents who must trust God with their children's welfare. Catholic pastoral theology recognizes this as a universal experience and finds in the chapter a model for the prayer of worried parents.

The chapter's honest portrayal of faith coexisting with fear reflects the Catholic understanding that holiness does not eliminate human emotion but sanctifies it. The Church's teaching on the family as the domestic church finds illustration in this scene of parents praying, hoping, and struggling to trust. Catholic devotional tradition connects the waiting of Tobit and Hannah to the broader theme of patient hope, which finds its liturgical expression in the season of Advent.
