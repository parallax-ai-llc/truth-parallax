Catholic interpretation of Tobit 9 notes the efficient resolution of the financial subplot as an illustration of how God's providential plan accomplishes multiple purposes simultaneously. Raphael retrieves the money and brings Gabael to the wedding feast, tying together the practical and spiritual dimensions of the narrative.

Catholic exegesis sees in this chapter a reflection of the principle that God's economy wastes nothing. What began as a practical errand becomes the occasion for healing, marriage, and the revelation of angelic ministry. This integrative vision, in which material and spiritual concerns are not separated but united under divine providence, reflects the Catholic sacramental imagination that sees all of life as potentially charged with divine purpose.
