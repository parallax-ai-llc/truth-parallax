Catholic interpretation of Tobit 11 reads the restoration of Tobit's sight as both a literal healing and a spiritual metaphor. The application of the fish gall to Tobit's eyes, removing the white films, is understood within the Catholic sacramental framework as God working through material means. The healing prefigures Christ's healing of the blind, particularly the man born blind in John 9, who also receives sight through the application of physical matter (mud and saliva).

Tobit's hymn of praise upon seeing again is read as a model of thanksgiving after receiving God's grace. Catholic liturgy echoes this pattern in the prayers of thanksgiving after the sacraments. The reunion of the family, with Tobit blessing his daughter-in-law Sarah, illustrates the Catholic understanding of the family as a community of grace where God's blessings are shared and celebrated together.
