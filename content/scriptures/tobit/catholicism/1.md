Catholic interpretation of Tobit 1 reads the chapter as inspired scripture that reveals the character of a righteous Israelite living faithfully under foreign rule. Tobit's commitment to tithing, dietary observance, burial of the dead, and almsgiving establishes him as a model of covenant fidelity. The Catholic Lectionary draws on this chapter to illustrate the spiritual works of mercy, particularly the burial of the dead, which is listed among the corporal works of mercy in Catholic catechesis.

The Church Fathers, especially Ambrose and Augustine, cited Tobit's almsgiving as scriptural support for the meritorious nature of charitable works. The Catechism of the Catholic Church references the broader theology of Tobit in its treatment of prayer and divine providence. Catholic exegesis reads Tobit's sufferings not as punishment but as a trial of faith through which God's greater purposes are revealed, consistent with the Catholic understanding of redemptive suffering.
