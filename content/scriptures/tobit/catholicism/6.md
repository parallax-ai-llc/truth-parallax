Catholic interpretation of Tobit 6 reads the fish and its healing properties within a sacramental framework. While not claiming the fish organs are sacraments in the technical sense, Catholic exegesis sees in them an illustration of the principle that God works through material means to accomplish spiritual purposes. This is consistent with the Catholic understanding of sacramentality, in which physical elements become vehicles of divine grace.

The use of the fish's heart and liver to drive away the demon Asmodeus anticipates the Church's practice of exorcism, in which prayers and blessed objects are used as instruments of divine power against evil spirits. Catholic theology maintains that the power in such cases belongs to God alone, with the material elements serving as signs and instruments of divine will. Raphael's instruction to Tobias models the proper relationship between faith, obedience, and the use of God-appointed means for spiritual protection.
