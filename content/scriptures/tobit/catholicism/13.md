Catholic exegesis of Tobit 13 reads Tobit's hymn as a prophetic psalm that celebrates God's mercy, calls for universal repentance, and envisions the eschatological glory of Jerusalem. The hymn's vision of a Jerusalem rebuilt with precious stones (13:16-17) is connected by Catholic interpreters to the New Jerusalem of Revelation 21, making it a significant text for Catholic eschatology.

The hymn is used in the Catholic Liturgy of the Hours, incorporated into the prayer of the Church as an inspired canticle alongside the canonical Psalms. Its structure of lamentation, confession, and hope mirrors the movement of the Lenten liturgy from penitence to Easter joy. Catholic exegesis also notes the hymn's universal scope, calling on all nations to praise God, which anticipates the Catholic Church's missionary understanding of its universal vocation.
