Catholic interpretation of Tobit 12 regards Raphael's self-revelation as one of the key scriptural foundations for Catholic angelology. Raphael identifies himself as one of the seven angels who stand before the glory of God (12:15), a tradition that finds resonance in Revelation 8:2. Catholic theology names three archangels (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael), and the feast of the Archangels on September 29 celebrates all three.

Raphael's teaching on prayer, fasting, and almsgiving (12:8-10) is frequently cited in Catholic catechesis, particularly during Lent. The triad of prayer, fasting, and almsgiving forms the backbone of Lenten discipline, and Catholic tradition traces this threefold practice in part to Tobit. The angel's declaration that he has been testing and recording their deeds supports the Catholic understanding of angelic ministry as encompassing both protection and moral witness. His ascension from their sight prefigures the Ascension of Christ and models the appropriate response of worship and praise.
