Catholic interpretation of Tobit 14 reads the final chapter as a testament that vindicates the life of faith. Tobit's prophecy of Nineveh's destruction and the Temple's rebuilding demonstrates the reliability of divine promises and the accuracy of prophetic vision. His final instructions to his family to maintain almsgiving and righteousness serve as a last will that transmits not wealth but wisdom.

Catholic exegesis draws from this chapter the teaching on the good death: Tobit dies at peace, surrounded by family, having passed on his faith to the next generation. This exemplifies the Catholic ideal of the ars moriendi (art of dying), in which preparation for death through a righteous life, final instructions, and trust in God's promises constitutes the culmination of the spiritual journey. The chapter's mention of Tobias burying his parents with honor reinforces the Catholic teaching on filial piety as a sacred obligation.
