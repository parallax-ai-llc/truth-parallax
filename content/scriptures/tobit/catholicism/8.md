Catholic interpretation of Tobit 8 centers on the wedding night prayer of Tobias and Sarah (8:5-7), one of the most frequently cited passages in Catholic marriage liturgy. The prayer's appeal to God as the Creator of Adam and Eve grounds marriage in the theology of creation and is read during the Rite of Marriage. The prayer's emphasis on companionship and mutual help rather than lust alone reflects the Catholic understanding of the unitive and procreative purposes of marriage.

The defeat of the demon Asmodeus through the burning of the fish organs and prayer demonstrates the Catholic teaching that spiritual evil is overcome through both material sacramental signs and faithful prayer. The chapter's resolution, in which Raguel fills in the grave he had prepared, symbolizes the triumph of life and blessing over death and curse. Catholic typological reading sees in this wedding a foreshadowing of Christ's union with the Church, the ultimate marriage to which all earthly marriages point.
