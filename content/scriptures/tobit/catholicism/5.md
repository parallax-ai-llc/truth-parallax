Catholic interpretation of Tobit 5 finds in Raphael's disguised companionship a scriptural foundation for the doctrine of guardian angels. The Catechism of the Catholic Church (CCC 336) teaches that from birth to death, every human life is surrounded by the watchful care and intercession of angels. Raphael's hidden presence alongside Tobias illustrates this teaching in narrative form.

The chapter also demonstrates the Catholic understanding of divine providence working through secondary causes. God does not intervene directly in a miraculous display but sends an angel who operates within the natural order of human encounter and companionship. This mediatorial structure is characteristic of Catholic sacramental theology, in which grace operates through created means. The dramatic irony of the scene, where the characters do not recognize the angel, reflects the Catholic conviction that God's grace is often at work in ways we do not immediately perceive.
