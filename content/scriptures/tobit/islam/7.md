Islamic reading of Tobit 7 finds parallels in the Islamic institution of marriage (nikah). The formal arrangement between families, the involvement of the bride's father (wali), and the writing of a marriage contract (mahr agreement) reflect practices that have counterparts in Islamic marriage law. The chapter's presentation of marriage as a sacred institution requiring divine blessing aligns with the Islamic understanding of nikah as an act of worship.

Raguel's trust in God despite his fear for Tobias's life illustrates the Islamic virtue of tawakkul, placing one's affairs in God's hands while taking reasonable precautions. The Quranic principle 'tie your camel, then trust in God' (Tirmidhi) is exemplified in Raguel's dual action of consenting to the marriage while preparing for the worst. Muslim readers would appreciate the chapter's portrayal of marriage as embedded in a community of faith and family.
