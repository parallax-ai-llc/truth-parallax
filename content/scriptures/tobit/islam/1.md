From an Islamic perspective, Tobit 1 presents a narrative of pious monotheistic life under foreign rule that resonates with Quranic themes. Tobit's commitment to charity, prayer, and religious observance despite living in a pagan environment parallels the Quranic descriptions of righteous believers who maintain their faith amid hostile surroundings. The emphasis on giving alms to the poor aligns with the Islamic pillar of zakat (obligatory charity).

Islamic engagement with this chapter falls under the category of Isra'iliyyat. Muslim scholars would affirm the monotheistic framework and the moral teachings while withholding judgment on historical details not confirmed by the Quran. The chapter's portrayal of faithful living in exile resonates with the early Muslim community's experience of persecution in Mecca and migration to Medina.
