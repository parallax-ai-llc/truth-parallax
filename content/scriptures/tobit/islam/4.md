Islamic engagement with Tobit 4 finds strong resonance in the chapter's ethical teachings. Tobit's instructions on charity, honoring parents, fair dealing, and marriage closely parallel Islamic moral teaching derived from the Quran and Sunnah. The emphasis on sadaqah (voluntary charity) as a source of spiritual merit aligns with numerous Quranic verses and hadith that praise charitable giving.

The chapter resembles the Islamic tradition of wasiyyah (testament or bequest), in which a parent transmits moral and spiritual guidance to the next generation. The Prophet Muhammad's farewell sermon similarly combined practical and spiritual instruction. Muslim readers would recognize in Tobit's advice a reflection of universal moral principles that Islam affirms as part of the common prophetic heritage shared across the Abrahamic traditions.
