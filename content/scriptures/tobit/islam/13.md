Islamic engagement with Tobit 13 finds strong resonance in the hymn's call to repentance and praise. The themes of tawbah (repentance) and divine mercy that pervade this chapter are central to Quranic theology. The hymn's insistence that God punishes and then shows mercy, scatters and then gathers, reflects the Quranic pattern of divine discipline followed by compassion toward those who return to God.

The vision of a glorified Jerusalem holds different significance in Islam, where al-Quds (Jerusalem) is the third holiest city and the site of the Prophet's Night Journey and Ascension (Isra and Mi'raj). While the specific eschatological vision differs from Islamic expectations, the broader theme of God's ultimate restoration of justice and worship resonates with the Quranic promise of a Day of Judgment when all wrongs will be set right.
