Islamic interpretation of Tobit 10 emphasizes the virtue of parental love and the obligation of children to ease their parents' anxiety. The Quran places enormous emphasis on honoring parents (Quran 17:23-24), and the chapter's portrayal of Tobit and Hannah's suffering during their son's absence illustrates the depth of parental attachment that Islam affirms and protects.

The chapter's depiction of faith tested by delay resonates with the Islamic understanding of sabr, which encompasses not only patience in suffering but patience in waiting for God's timing. The Quranic promise that 'with hardship comes ease' (Quran 94:5-6) provides the theological framework through which Muslim readers would understand the parents' ordeal, trusting that God's plan unfolds according to a wisdom that transcends human impatience.
