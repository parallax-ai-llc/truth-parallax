Islamic reading of Tobit 12 engages with the theme of angelic revelation while noting differences from Islamic angelology. The concept of angels presenting human prayers before God is consistent with the Islamic belief in recording angels (kiraman katibin) who document human deeds (Quran 82:10-12). However, Islam identifies four chief angels (Jibril, Mika'il, Israfil, Azra'il) and does not include Raphael among them.

Raphael's teaching that prayer with charity is better than accumulated wealth strongly parallels Quranic and prophetic teaching. The Quran repeatedly emphasizes that wealth without charitable giving is spiritually worthless (Quran 104:1-4), and the hadith literature praises the combination of prayer and sadaqah as the marks of true faith. Muslim readers would find the ethical teaching of this chapter fully compatible with Islamic values, even while the angelological framework differs from Islamic doctrine.
