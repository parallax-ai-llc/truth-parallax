Islamic interpretation of Tobit 2 would read Tobit's affliction through the lens of divine testing (ibtila). The Quran teaches that God tests the faithful through hardship (Quran 2:155-157), and Tobit's blindness after performing a righteous deed exemplifies this pattern. The concept of sabr (patient endurance) in the face of suffering, one of the most valued virtues in Islam, finds illustration in Tobit's response to his affliction.

The parallel with the Quranic narrative of Ayyub (Job), who was tested with illness and loss yet remained faithful to God, is particularly strong in this chapter. Muslim readers would recognize the pattern of a righteous servant afflicted not as punishment but as a means of spiritual elevation. The chapter's honesty about the strain that suffering places on family relationships also resonates with the Quranic acknowledgment that trials test the bonds between people.
