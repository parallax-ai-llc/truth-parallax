Islamic reading of Tobit 3 would highlight the power of sincere prayer (du'a) to reach God, a central teaching of the Quran. The simultaneous prayers of Tobit and Sarah, heard and answered together by God, illustrate the Quranic promise that God is near and responds to the caller (Quran 2:186). The dispatch of an angel in response to prayer reflects the Islamic belief in angels as agents of God's will.

The figure of the demon Asmodeus would be understood within the Islamic framework of jinn, beings of smokeless fire who can interact with and afflict humans. The Quran acknowledges the existence of jinn and their capacity for both good and evil (Quran 72:1-15). However, Muslim scholars would emphasize that only God has power over such beings and that deliverance comes through prayer and divine decree, not through material remedies.
