Islamic reading of Tobit 14 would interpret Tobit's peaceful death and moral testament within the framework of prophetic legacy. The Quran presents the deaths of righteous persons as a peaceful return to God, and Tobit's serene departure after fulfilling his moral and spiritual obligations models the Islamic ideal of husn al-khatima (a good ending). The Prophet Muhammad taught that the way a person dies reflects the quality of their life.

Tobit's prophecy of Nineveh's destruction connects to the Quranic story of the people of Yunus (Jonah), whose city was spared after repentance (Quran 10:98). The eventual destruction of Nineveh, after it returned to its sinful ways, illustrates the Quranic warning that God's reprieve is temporary for those who do not truly reform. Muslim readers would recognize in this chapter the universal prophetic principle that nations rise and fall according to their obedience or disobedience to God.
