Islamic reading of Tobit 5 focuses on the theme of tawakkul (trust in God) and divine provision. The sending of an angel to guide and protect Tobias on his journey illustrates the Quranic teaching that God provides for and guides those who place their trust in Him (Quran 65:3). While Islam does not name Raphael among its principal angels, the concept of angelic protection and guidance is well established in Islamic theology.

The angel's concealment of his true identity would be understood within the Islamic tradition of angels appearing in human form, as in the famous hadith of Jibril (Gabriel), who appeared to the Prophet Muhammad as a man in white garments to teach the fundamentals of Islam. The chapter's emphasis on setting out on a journey with trust in God's provision resonates with the Islamic practice of reciting travel prayers (du'a al-safar) and commending oneself to God's protection.
