Islamic engagement with Tobit 11 would read the healing of Tobit's blindness as an act of divine mercy (rahma), consistent with the Quranic description of God as al-Shafi (the Healer). The restoration of sight carries particular significance in Islamic tradition, where spiritual insight and physical healing are both understood as gifts from God. The Quranic healing of Ayyub (Job) from his affliction provides a direct parallel.

Tobit's response of praise and gratitude upon receiving his sight reflects the Islamic virtue of shukr (gratitude to God). The Quran teaches that those who are grateful will receive more (Quran 14:7), and Tobit's praise models the proper response to divine blessing. Muslim readers would note the consistency of this narrative with the broader prophetic pattern of affliction, patience, and divine restoration found throughout the Quran.
