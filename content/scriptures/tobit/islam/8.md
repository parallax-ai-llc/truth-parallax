Islamic engagement with Tobit 8 would affirm the chapter's emphasis on prayer before the consummation of marriage. The Islamic tradition includes specific prayers (du'a) to be recited on the wedding night, asking God for blessing and protection. Tobias and Sarah's prayer before their union reflects a practice that Islam endorses, grounding the marital relationship in divine worship from its inception.

The defeat of the demon through prayer aligns with the Islamic teaching that sincere supplication to God is the believer's most powerful weapon against evil. However, the role of the fish organs in the exorcism would be treated with the caution noted in chapter 6. The chapter's appeal to the creation of Adam and Hawwa (Eve) as the foundation of marriage resonates with the Quranic teaching that God created humanity in pairs for mutual comfort and mercy (Quran 30:21).
