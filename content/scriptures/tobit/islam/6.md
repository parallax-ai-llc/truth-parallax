Islamic engagement with Tobit 6 would exercise caution regarding the use of fish organs as a remedy against demons. While the Quran acknowledges the existence of jinn and their potential to harm humans, Islamic theology insists that protection from evil comes through prayer, recitation of the Quran (particularly Surat al-Falaq and Surat an-Nas), and trust in God alone. The attribution of spiritual power to material objects without Quranic or prophetic authorization would be viewed skeptically.

Muslim scholars would distinguish between the chapter's underlying theological principle, that God provides means of healing and protection, and the specific mechanism described. The Islamic practice of ruqyah (spiritual healing through Quranic recitation) affirms that healing comes from God through revealed words, not through natural substances. The chapter would be classified among the Isra'iliyyat narratives that are neither confirmed nor denied by Islamic sources.
