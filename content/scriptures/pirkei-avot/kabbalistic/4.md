## The Four Questions and the Four Worlds

Ben Zoma's four questions (4:1) are mapped by kabbalistic interpreters onto the four worlds of emanation. "Who is wise?" corresponds to Atzilut (Emanation, the world of pure wisdom). "Who is mighty?" corresponds to Beriah (Creation, where the soul conquers the forces of separation). "Who is rich?" corresponds to Yetzirah (Formation, contentment with the divine portion assigned to one's soul). "Who is honored?" corresponds to Asiyah (Action, the world of deeds where honor is given, not taken).

## The Vestibule and the Banquet Hall

The teaching "This world is like a vestibule before the World to Come" (4:16) is understood mystically as describing the soul's journey through the lower worlds toward its ultimate destination in the divine light. The vestibule (prozdor) represents the world of Asiyah, where the soul is clothed in a physical body and given the opportunity to perform mitzvot that generate spiritual garments for its entry into the upper worlds.

## Humility as Bittul

The chapter's emphasis on humility is understood as pointing toward bittul — the nullification of the ego that allows the divine light to flow through the individual. The Hasidic masters taught that pride (gaavah) is the root of all spiritual obstacles because it creates a false sense of self-sufficiency that blocks the soul's connection to its Source.
