## The Spiritual Chain

Kabbalistic interpretation reads the chain of tradition in Chapter 1 as describing not merely the transmission of legal teachings but the flow of divine light (ohr) through successive generations. Each link in the chain — Moses, Joshua, the Elders, the Prophets — represents a spiritual vessel (keli) through which the supernal wisdom descended into the world. The Zohar teaches that the Torah Moses received at Sinai contained all four levels of meaning: peshat, remez, derash, and sod.

## The Three Pillars and the Sefirot

The Men of the Great Assembly's three directives — deliberation in judgment (din), raising students (chesed through teaching), and making a fence around Torah (gevurah as protective boundary) — correspond to the three pillars of the sefirotic tree: right (chesed), left (gevurah), and center (tiferet as the balanced judgment). Each ethical teaching thus maps onto the divine structure, revealing that human moral action reflects and affects the supernal realms.

## Hillel and Shammai as Cosmic Polarity

The divergence between Hillel (lenient, compassionate, emphasizing chesed) and Shammai (strict, rigorous, emphasizing gevurah) reflects the fundamental polarity within the Godhead. The Ari (Rabbi Isaac Luria) taught that in the messianic era, the halakhah will follow Shammai, because the world will be able to sustain the higher intensity of gevurah. Until then, Hillel's chesed prevails as the world requires mercy.
