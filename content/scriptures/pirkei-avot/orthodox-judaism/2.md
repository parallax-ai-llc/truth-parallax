## Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai and His Students

Chapter 2 centers on Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai, who preserved Torah study after the Temple's destruction, and his five outstanding students. The chapter models how a master teacher identifies and cultivates the unique strengths of each student. Orthodox commentators see in this the principle that Torah education must be tailored to the individual — each student possesses a distinct spiritual capacity.

## The Good Path and the Evil Path

Rabban Yochanan's famous question to his students — "What is the good path to which a person should cling?" (2:9) — elicits five different answers: a good eye, a good friend, a good neighbor, foresight, and a good heart. Rabbi Elazar ben Arakh's answer, "a good heart," is declared the best because it encompasses all the others. Orthodox interpretation sees this as teaching that inner character (the heart's orientation) determines all external conduct.

## Rabbi's Teaching

The chapter also preserves the teaching of Rabbi Yehudah HaNasi himself: "Which is the right path that a person should choose? That which is honorable for the one who follows it and honorable in the eyes of others" (2:1). This balance between inner integrity and social responsibility is central to the Orthodox understanding of proper Torah living.
