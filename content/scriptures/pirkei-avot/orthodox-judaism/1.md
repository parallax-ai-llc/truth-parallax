## The Chain of Transmission

Chapter 1 of Pirkei Avot establishes the foundational principle of mesorah — the unbroken chain of tradition from Moses at Sinai through Joshua, the Elders, the Prophets, and the Men of the Great Assembly to the named rabbinic sages. Orthodox interpretation reads this as a historical-theological claim: the Oral Torah is not a human invention but a divine revelation faithfully transmitted across generations. Each link in the chain received and passed on authentic teaching.

## Key Teachings

The chapter opens with the Men of the Great Assembly's three maxims: "Be deliberate in judgment, raise up many students, and make a fence around the Torah" (1:1). These three principles encapsulate the Orthodox approach to Torah: careful legal reasoning, widespread education, and protective legislation (gezeirot) to prevent transgression. The teachings of the zugot (pairs) — Yose ben Yoezer and Yose ben Yochanan through Hillel and Shammai — demonstrate that legitimate diversity of emphasis exists within the unified tradition.

## Hillel and Shammai

The chapter culminates with the foundational teachings of Hillel and Shammai (1:12-15). Hillel's "Be of the disciples of Aaron, loving peace and pursuing peace, loving people and bringing them close to Torah" represents the inclusive, compassionate pole of Orthodox teaching, while Shammai's "Make your Torah study fixed" emphasizes discipline and commitment. Together they model the breadth of authentic Torah personality.
