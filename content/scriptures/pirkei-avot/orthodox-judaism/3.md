## Contemplating One's Origin and Destiny

Chapter 3 opens with Akavya ben Mahalalel's teaching: "Contemplate three things and you will not come to sin — know from where you came, to where you are going, and before Whom you will give an accounting" (3:1). Orthodox commentators read this as establishing the theological framework for ethical conduct: awareness of human mortality and divine judgment provides the motivation for righteous living.

## Torah Study and Fear of Sin

A central theme is the relationship between Torah study and fear of sin. Rabbi Chanina ben Dosa teaches: "Anyone whose fear of sin precedes their wisdom, their wisdom endures; but anyone whose wisdom precedes their fear of sin, their wisdom does not endure" (3:9). Orthodox interpretation understands this as establishing that intellectual Torah knowledge without yirat shamayim (fear of Heaven) is unstable and potentially dangerous.

## The Sanctity of Torah Study

The chapter includes the powerful teaching that when even two people sit together and exchange words of Torah, the Divine Presence (Shekhinah) rests between them (3:2). Orthodox tradition takes this literally as describing a metaphysical reality — Torah study creates a space for divine indwelling, making even the most modest study session a sacred encounter.
