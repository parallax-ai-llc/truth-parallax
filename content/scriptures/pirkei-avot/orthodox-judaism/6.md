## The Crown of Torah

Chapter 6, known as Kinyan Torah ("Acquisition of Torah"), is a later addition to Pirkei Avot (technically a baraita rather than Mishnah), appended to complete the six-week Shabbat study cycle between Pesach and Shavuot. It opens with Rabbi Meir's teaching that whoever engages in Torah study for its own sake (lishmah) merits many things and "the whole world is worthwhile for their sake" (6:1).

## The 48 Qualities

The chapter's centerpiece is the enumeration of 48 qualities through which Torah is acquired (6:6), including study, attentive listening, verbal articulation, understanding of the heart, awe, humility, joy, serving the sages, and knowing one's place. Orthodox tradition treats this list as a practical curriculum for the aspiring Torah scholar, emphasizing that Torah knowledge requires not merely intellectual effort but comprehensive character refinement.

## Torah as Life's Purpose

The chapter concludes with teachings praising Torah as God's most precious possession, given to Israel as an act of love. "Turn it over and turn it over, for everything is in it" (5:22, often associated with Chapter 6) encapsulates the Orthodox conviction that Torah contains all wisdom and that its study is the highest human activity, the very purpose for which the world was created.
