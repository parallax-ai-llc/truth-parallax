---
title: "Pirkei Avot - Chapter 6"
scripture: "pirkei-avot"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The sages taught in the language of the Mishnah. Blessed be He who chose them and their teaching. Rabbi Meir said: Whoever engages in Torah study for its own sake merits many things; and not only that, but the entire world is worthwhile for his sake alone. He is called friend, beloved, lover of God, lover of humanity. It clothes him with humility and reverence; it enables him to become righteous, pious, upright and faithful.

**2** Rabbi Joshua ben Levi said: Every day a Divine voice goes forth from Mount Horeb and proclaims: "Woe to the creatures for their contempt of the Torah!" For whoever does not occupy himself with the Torah is called "rebuked."

**3** He who learns from his fellow a single chapter, a single law, a single verse, a single word, or even a single letter, must treat him with respect.

**4** This is the way of Torah: Bread with salt you shall eat, water by measure you shall drink, upon the ground you shall sleep, a life of deprivation you shall live, and in Torah you shall labor. If you do this, "happy shall you be, and it shall be well with you" — happy shall you be in this world, and it shall be well with you in the world to come.

**5** Do not seek greatness for yourself, and do not covet honor. More than you study, do. Do not yearn for the table of kings, for your table is greater than theirs and your crown is greater than theirs, and your Employer can be relied upon to pay you the reward of your labor.

**6** Torah is greater than the priesthood and than royalty, for royalty is acquired with thirty qualities, the priesthood with twenty-four, and the Torah is acquired with forty-eight qualities.
