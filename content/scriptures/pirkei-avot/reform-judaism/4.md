## Redefining Greatness

Ben Zoma's four questions (4:1) — Who is wise? Who is mighty? Who is rich? Who is honored? — and their counterintuitive answers resonate deeply with Reform values. True wealth is contentment, true strength is self-mastery, true honor comes from honoring others. These teachings challenge materialistic and hierarchical notions of success and replace them with an ethic of inner character and mutual respect.

## The Dignity of Every Person

The chapter's teaching "Do not despise any person and do not dismiss anything" (4:3) aligns with Reform's commitment to human dignity and equality. Reform interpretation reads this as a mandate against prejudice in all its forms, requiring openness to learning from every human being regardless of status, background, or identity.

## Engaging This World

While the chapter includes teachings about the World to Come, Reform interpretation tends to focus on its this-worldly ethics. The emphasis falls on making this world a place of justice, compassion, and beauty rather than treating it merely as a preparation for an afterlife.
