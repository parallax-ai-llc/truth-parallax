Section 6 of the Kebra Nagast — The Transfer of the Ark of the Covenant — occupies a unique position in interfaith dialogue as a text that draws on and reinterprets narratives shared by Judaism, Christianity, and Islam while centering an African perspective. The section's engagement with the Solomon-Sheba tradition — one of the great shared narratives of the Abrahamic faiths — makes it a natural point of contact for interreligious conversation.

Interfaith engagement with this section illuminates how shared sacred narratives function differently in different religious communities, how concepts of divine election and covenant are understood across traditions, and how African Christianity offers distinctive perspectives that enrich global interreligious dialogue. The section challenges the assumption that the Abrahamic traditions are primarily Western or Middle Eastern phenomena, demonstrating the depth and sophistication of African theological reflection.
