## Overview

The Kebra Nagast occupies a unique position in interfaith dialogue as a text that draws on and reinterprets narratives shared by Judaism, Christianity, and Islam while centering an African perspective. The figure of the Queen of Sheba appears in all three Abrahamic scriptures, and the Kebra Nagast's elaborate development of her story provides a distinctively Ethiopian and African contribution to this shared heritage. The text's claim that the Ark of the Covenant resides in Ethiopia connects Jewish, Christian, and Ethiopian sacred geography in provocative ways.

Interfaith engagement with the Kebra Nagast illuminates how shared narratives are received and transformed in different religious and cultural contexts. The Solomon-Sheba story serves different functions in Jewish, Islamic, and Ethiopian Christian tradition — from a tale of diplomatic exchange to a romance to a foundational national myth. The text also raises important questions for interfaith dialogue about the relationship between religion and ethnicity, the concept of divine election, and the possibility of multiple chosen peoples within a single theological framework.
