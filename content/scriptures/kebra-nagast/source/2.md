---
title: "The Queen of Sheba"
scripture: "kebra-nagast"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Makeda, Queen of Sheba, ruled over a great kingdom in the south. Her land was rich with gold, spices, and precious stones.

**2** She heard of the wisdom of Solomon, king of Israel, and resolved to visit him. She gathered a great caravan: seven hundred and ninety-seven camels laden with gifts, donkeys without number, and a great retinue.

**3** She journeyed to Jerusalem, and when she arrived, Solomon received her with great honor. He showed her the Temple and the wonders of his kingdom.

**4** They spoke of many things: of wisdom and of governance, of God and of righteousness. And Makeda was amazed at Solomon's wisdom, for it surpassed all that she had heard.

**5** And she said: Blessed are your servants who stand before you always and hear your wisdom. Blessed is the Lord your God, who delights in you and has set you on the throne of Israel.

**6** Before she departed, Solomon lay with Makeda, and she conceived a son.
