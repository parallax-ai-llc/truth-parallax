## Overview

Academic study of the Kebra Nagast examines the text from historical, literary, theological, and political perspectives. Scholars analyze its composition history (drawing on Arabic, Coptic, and Ge'ez sources), its literary structure and rhetorical strategies, its relationship to biblical and post-biblical Jewish and Christian traditions, and its political function as a legitimating charter for the Solomonic dynasty that ruled Ethiopia from 1270 to 1974.

Key scholarly questions include the text's sources and compositional history, the historical reality (if any) behind the Queen of Sheba narrative, the relationship between the Kebra Nagast and the broader tradition of Solomonic literature in Jewish, Christian, and Islamic traditions, and the text's role in shaping Ethiopian national identity and political theology. The Kebra Nagast is increasingly studied as a major work of medieval African literature deserving of the same scholarly attention given to comparable texts from European and Asian traditions.
