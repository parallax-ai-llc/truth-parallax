Section 4 of the Kebra Nagast — The Birth and Youth of Menelik I — raises important questions for Protestant theological engagement concerning the boundaries of sacred history, the authority of extra-biblical tradition, and the relationship between covenant theology and national identity. Protestant readers encounter in this section a confident extension of biblical narrative beyond canonical bounds, raising fundamental questions about how the biblical story continues in post-biblical communities.

Protestant engagement with this section involves both appreciation and critique. On one hand, the section testifies to the deep roots of Christianity in Africa and the creative appropriation of biblical narrative in a non-Western context. On the other hand, its conflation of sacred and political authority, its particularistic claims of national election, and its reliance on extra-biblical tradition challenge Protestant commitments to *sola scriptura* and the universality of the gospel.
