Section 10 of the Kebra Nagast — The Prophecies Concerning Ethiopia — provides rich material for comparative analysis across multiple literary, theological, and political traditions. The themes of this section connect to one of the most widely distributed story complexes in world literature — the traditions surrounding Solomon, the Queen of Sheba, and the Ark of the Covenant — with versions in the Hebrew Bible, the Quran, Jewish midrash, Islamic legends of the prophets, and various Ethiopian and Arabian traditions.

Comparative analysis of this section reveals how shared narrative elements are transformed in different cultural and religious contexts. The Kebra Nagast's distinctive elaboration of these themes — centering Ethiopia as the heir of Israel's covenant, emphasizing the transfer of the Ark, and legitimating a specific dynasty — invites comparison with similar national-religious narratives from other cultures that claim divine election and sacred origins for their ruling houses.
