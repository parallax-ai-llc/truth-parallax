## Overview

Within the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, the Kebra Nagast holds a position of unique reverence as a text that confirms Ethiopia's special role in sacred history. The narrative of the Ark of the Covenant's transfer to Ethiopia — where it is believed to reside to this day in the Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion in Axum — is not regarded as legend but as historical truth, foundational to Ethiopian Christian identity. The text articulates the theological conviction that God transferred the divine covenant from Israel to Ethiopia through the lineage of Solomon and Makeda.

Ethiopian Orthodox engagement with the Kebra Nagast is inseparable from the broader liturgical, theological, and institutional life of the Church. The text's Christological and Marian theology reflects the distinctive emphases of Tewahedo (meaning "unity," referring to the unity of divine and human natures in Christ) Christology. Its vision of sacred kingship shaped the relationship between church and state in Ethiopia for centuries, with the emperor understood as the anointed successor of Solomon and the guardian of the Ark.
