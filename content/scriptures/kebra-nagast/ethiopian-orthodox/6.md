Section 6 of the Kebra Nagast — The Transfer of the Ark of the Covenant — holds deep significance within the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church as sacred historical narrative confirming Ethiopia's unique role in salvation history. Ethiopian Orthodox engagement with this section reads it as authoritative testimony to the divine providence that brought the Ark of the Covenant to Ethiopia and established the Solomonic dynasty as God's chosen rulers.

Within the liturgical and theological life of the Ethiopian Church, this section contributes to the overarching narrative of Ethiopia's divine election — a narrative that shapes Ethiopian Christian identity, informs liturgical practice, and sustains the conviction that Ethiopia occupies a unique place in God's purposes for humanity. The section's theological content reflects the distinctive emphases of Tewahedo Christianity: its Christological commitments, its Marian devotion, its integration of Old Testament and New Testament themes, and its vision of sacred kingship.
