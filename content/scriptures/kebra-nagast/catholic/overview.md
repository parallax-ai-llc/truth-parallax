## Overview

Catholic engagement with the Kebra Nagast connects to several important theological themes: the relationship between Scripture and Tradition, the theology of sacred kingship, Marian devotion, and the ecumenical relationship between the Catholic Church and the Oriental Orthodox churches. The text's emphasis on sacred tradition as a vehicle of divine truth — extending and interpreting biblical narrative through post-biblical sources — resonates with the Catholic understanding of Tradition as a legitimate and necessary complement to Scripture.

The Kebra Nagast's extensive Marian theology — celebrating the Virgin Mary as the Ark of the New Covenant, the fulfillment of the typological significance of the Ark of the Covenant — connects to Catholic Mariological tradition and its use of Old Testament typology. The text's vision of sacred kingship, while specific to the Ethiopian context, engages theological questions about the proper relationship between spiritual and temporal authority that have been central to Catholic political theology since Augustine's *City of God*.
