Section 12 of the Kebra Nagast — The Queen of the South — engages themes of significant interest to Catholic theological reflection: the relationship between Scripture and Sacred Tradition, the theology of sacred kingship, the role of the Virgin Mary in salvation history, and the ecumenical relationship between the Catholic Church and the Oriental Orthodox churches. Catholic engagement with this section finds resonance with the Catholic understanding of Tradition as a living vehicle of divine truth.

The section's theological content — its integration of biblical narrative with post-biblical tradition, its typological interpretation of the Old Testament, and its vision of the Church's relationship to political authority — connects to centuries of Catholic theological reflection on similar questions. The Kebra Nagast's Ethiopian perspective enriches Catholic understanding of the diversity of Christian theological expression and the multiple cultural forms in which the Gospel has been received and proclaimed.
