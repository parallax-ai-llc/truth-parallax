## Overview

The Yoga Tattva Upanishad ("Upanishad of Yoga Principles") belongs to the Krishna Yajur Veda and is one of the most comprehensive Yoga Upanishads. It presents a systematic teaching on the four types of yoga (Mantra, Laya, Hatha, and Raja), the awakening of kundalini energy, the practice of pranayama (breath control), and the stages of samadhi (meditative absorption). The text provides detailed practical instructions that bridge the Upanishadic philosophical tradition with the tantric yoga practices that would later be elaborated in the Hatha Yoga tradition.

## Structure

The Yoga Tattva Upanishad consists of four sections:

1. **Section 1 — The Four Types of Yoga**: Mantra Yoga, Laya Yoga, Hatha Yoga, and Raja Yoga
2. **Section 2 — Pranayama and Purification**: Breath control techniques and the purification of nadis (energy channels)
3. **Section 3 — Kundalini and the Chakras**: The awakening of kundalini and its ascent through the chakras
4. **Section 4 — Samadhi and Liberation**: The stages of meditative absorption leading to liberation

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: Yoga practices directed toward communion with Vishnu
- **Shaivism**: Kundalini yoga as the awakening of Shiva-Shakti within
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Yoga as preparatory purification for non-dual realization
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Yogic discipline as devotional practice under God's guidance
- **Academic**: Key source for the history of yoga and tantric practice
- **Comparative**: Energy-body practices paralleled in Chinese, Tibetan, and other traditions

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Four Types of Yoga | Mantra, Laya, Hatha, and Raja Yoga as progressive paths |
| Kundalini Awakening | The arousal and ascent of the serpent energy |
| Pranayama | Systematic breath control for purification and energy management |
| Chakras and Nadis | The subtle energy centers and channels of the yogic body |
| Samadhi | Progressive stages of meditative absorption leading to liberation |
