## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Yoga Tattva Upanishad a comprehensive teaching on the awakening of Shakti (kundalini) and her reunion with Shiva at the crown of the head. The four types of yoga correspond to progressively subtle methods of facilitating this reunion — from the gross (Hatha) through the intermediate (Mantra and Laya) to the supreme (Raja).

## Theological Emphases

The kundalini's ascent through the chakras is the central narrative of Shaiva tantra: Shakti rising from her dormant state at the base of the spine to reunite with Shiva at the *sahasrara* (crown chakra). This reunion is the inner meaning of the Shiva-Shakti marriage and represents the restoration of the unity of consciousness that was fragmented through creation.
