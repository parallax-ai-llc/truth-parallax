---
title: "Yoga Tattva Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "yoga-tattva-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Vishnu said: I shall now describe the stages of Samadhi and the final liberation that is the goal of all Yoga.

**2** When the Yogi, through the practice of Pranayama, Mudras, and meditation, has caused the Kundalini to rise to the Sahasrara, he enters the state of Samadhi.

**3** In the first stage, Savitarka Samadhi, the Yogi meditates upon a gross object and becomes absorbed in it. The distinction between the meditator and the object begins to dissolve.

**4** In the second stage, Savichara Samadhi, the Yogi meditates upon a subtle object — a tanmatra, a subtle element — and becomes absorbed in its essence.

**5** In the third stage, Sananda Samadhi, the Yogi experiences pure bliss, beyond all objects, beyond all thought. Only bliss remains, and the consciousness of bliss.

**6** In the fourth stage, Sasmita Samadhi, even the bliss is transcended. Only the pure sense of "I am" remains — the awareness of existence itself, without any object.

**7** Beyond these four stages is Nirbija Samadhi, the seedless absorption, in which even the sense of "I am" is dissolved. The Yogi's consciousness merges with the infinite consciousness of Brahman.

**8** In Nirbija Samadhi, there is no meditator, no meditation, and no object of meditation. There is only the one undivided consciousness, the Absolute, the Brahman.

**9** The Yogi who has attained Nirbija Samadhi is liberated forever. His karma is destroyed, his ignorance is dissolved, his bondage is broken. He is free.

**10** This is the supreme teaching of the Yoga Tattva Upanishad. He who practises it with devotion, under the guidance of a realized master, attains the highest goal of human life — liberation from the cycle of birth and death. Om, peace, peace, peace.
