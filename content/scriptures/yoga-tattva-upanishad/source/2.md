---
title: "Yoga Tattva Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "yoga-tattva-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Vishnu said: I shall now describe the practice of Pranayama, which is the foundation of all yogic practice.

**2** The Yogi should sit in Padmasana, the lotus posture, facing north or east, in a clean and quiet place. He should close his eyes and fix his mind upon the Self within.

**3** He should inhale through the left nostril while mentally repeating the seed mantra sixteen times. He should retain the breath while repeating the mantra sixty-four times. He should exhale through the right nostril while repeating the mantra thirty-two times.

**4** This ratio of 1:4:2 in inhalation, retention, and exhalation constitutes the perfect Pranayama. The Yogi should practise this gradually, beginning with a few rounds and increasing over time.

**5** The three stages of Pranayama are: Adhama, the lowest, in which the body perspires; Madhyama, the middle, in which the body trembles; and Uttama, the highest, in which the body levitates and the mind enters Samadhi.

**6** By the practice of Pranayama, the nadis are purified, the Kundalini is awakened, and the vital forces enter the Sushumna, the central channel of the spine.

**7** When the Prana enters the Sushumna, the mind becomes steady, free from all disturbances. This is called the Unmani state, the state beyond mind.

**8** The signs of progress in Pranayama are: lightness of the body, lustre of the skin, increase in the digestive fire, perception of the inner sound, and freedom from disease.

**9** The Yogi should observe moderation in food, sleep, and activity. He should eat sattvic food, avoid extremes, and maintain a disciplined daily routine.

**10** He who practises Pranayama with devotion and regularity, under the guidance of a competent teacher, attains mastery over the vital forces and prepares the way for the higher stages of Yoga.
