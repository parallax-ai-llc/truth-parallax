---
title: "Yoga Tattva Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "yoga-tattva-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Vishnu, the great lord, the supreme being, of his own accord explained to Brahma the Yoga Tattva, the truth of Yoga, for the liberation of all beings.

**2** Vishnu said: I shall describe the Yoga Tattva for the benefit of the Yogins who are devoted to the practice of Yoga and who seek liberation from the cycle of birth and death.

**3** Yoga is of four kinds: Mantra Yoga, Laya Yoga, Hatha Yoga, and Raja Yoga. Each leads to the same goal, but by different means.

**4** Mantra Yoga is the yoga of sacred sounds. By the repetition of mantras with devotion and concentration, the mind is purified and the deity invoked within the mantra manifests in the heart of the practitioner.

**5** Laya Yoga is the yoga of dissolution. By fixing the mind upon the inner self and dissolving all mental modifications into the consciousness of the Absolute, the Yogi attains the state beyond mind.

**6** Hatha Yoga is the yoga of force. By the practice of asanas, pranayama, mudras, and bandhas, the Yogi gains control over the body and the vital forces, preparing the way for Raja Yoga.

**7** Raja Yoga is the royal yoga, the yoga of meditation. It is the direct path to Samadhi, in which the mind is completely stilled and the Self is realized.

**8** These four yogas are not separate paths but progressive stages of the one science of Yoga. Each prepares the practitioner for the next, and all culminate in the realization of the Self.

**9** The Yogi should begin with the purification of the body and the mind through Hatha Yoga, then proceed to the concentration and meditation of Raja Yoga.

**10** The foundation of all Yoga is the grace of the guru, the divine teacher. Without the guru's guidance, the Yogi cannot navigate the subtle inner realms and may be led astray.
