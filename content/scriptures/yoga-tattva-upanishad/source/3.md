---
title: "Yoga Tattva Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "yoga-tattva-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Vishnu said: I shall now describe the Mudras, the sacred gestures and locks that are essential to the practice of Yoga.

**2** Maha Mudra: The Yogi should press the perineum with the left heel, extend the right leg, grasp the toe with both hands, and perform Jalandhara Bandha. He should inhale through the left nostril and retain the breath.

**3** This Maha Mudra destroys the seeds of all diseases. It awakens the Kundalini and causes the Prana to flow into the Sushumna.

**4** Maha Bandha: Pressing the perineum with the left heel, placing the right foot upon the left thigh, contracting the throat, and drawing the apana upward — this is Maha Bandha, the great lock.

**5** Maha Vedha: In the posture of Maha Bandha, the Yogi should strike the ground with his buttocks gently. The Prana enters the Sushumna and the Yogi attains steadiness of mind.

**6** Khechari Mudra: The Yogi should turn the tongue backward into the cavity of the skull and fix the gaze between the eyebrows. This is Khechari, which conquers disease, death, and old age.

**7** Jalandhara Bandha: Contracting the throat and pressing the chin against the chest, this lock prevents the nectar from falling into the digestive fire and preserves it.

**8** Uddiyana Bandha: Drawing the abdomen back toward the spine, the Yogi causes the Prana to fly upward through the Sushumna. This is called the flying-up lock.

**9** Mula Bandha: Contracting the perineum and drawing the apana upward, the Yogi unites the apana with the prana. This root lock is the key to awakening the Kundalini.

**10** These Mudras and Bandhas should be practised under the guidance of a qualified teacher. They are powerful techniques that can cause harm if practised incorrectly, but when practised rightly they lead to liberation.
