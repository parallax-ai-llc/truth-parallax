## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Yoga Tattva Upanishad's detailed yoga practices serve as preparatory disciplines (*sadhana*) that purify the mind for the direct recognition of non-dual Brahman. The four types of yoga and the kundalini practices are not themselves liberation but means of removing the mental impurities that obstruct self-knowledge.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara's tradition values yoga practices for their mind-purifying effects while insisting that liberation comes through knowledge alone. The samadhi described as the culmination of yoga is read as *savikalpa samadhi* (absorption with conceptual content) that must give way to *nirvikalpa samadhi* (absorption without conceptual content) — the direct, unmediated awareness of Brahman that constitutes liberation.
