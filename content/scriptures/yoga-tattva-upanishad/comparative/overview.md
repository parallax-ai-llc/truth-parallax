## Overview

The Yoga Tattva Upanishad's comprehensive system of yogic practice invites comparison with systematic spiritual paths across traditions. Its detailed mapping of the subtle energy body — nadis, chakras, kundalini — parallels similar systems in other Asian traditions.

## Comparative Themes

The kundalini/chakra system compares with the Chinese *dantian* (energy centers) and *meridian* (energy channel) system, the Tibetan Buddhist *tsa-lung* (channel-wind) practices, and the Korean *dan-jeon* tradition. The four types of yoga parallel the Christian fourfold contemplative path (lectio, meditatio, oratio, contemplatio), the Sufi stages of the path (*maqamat*), and the Buddhist graduated path (*lamrim*). The samadhi described as the culmination of practice compares with Buddhist *jhana* (absorption), Christian *unio mystica*, and the Sufi state of *fana* (annihilation in God).
