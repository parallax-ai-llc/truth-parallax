## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Yoga Tattva Upanishad's four types of yoga as disciplines that cultivate the physical, mental, and spiritual fitness required for receiving God's grace. The yogic path is not self-sufficient — it prepares the practitioner, but liberation ultimately depends on Vishnu's gracious intervention.

## Theological Emphases

The kundalini energy is understood as a real force within the God-created energy body, and its mastery through yoga is a form of devotional discipline. The samadhi that crowns the path is the soul's deep absorption in awareness of God — not the dissolution of individuality but its perfection in divine communion. The four types of yoga provide options suited to different temperaments, all leading to the same goal of devotion.
