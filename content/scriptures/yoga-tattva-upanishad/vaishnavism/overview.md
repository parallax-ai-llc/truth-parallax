## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Yoga Tattva Upanishad's fourfold yoga system as a progressive path toward union with Vishnu. Mantra yoga (repetition of divine names), Laya yoga (absorption in the inner sound), Hatha yoga (mastery of the body), and Raja yoga (royal meditation) are understood as complementary practices that prepare the devotee for the ultimate experience of God's presence.

## Theological Emphases

The kundalini energy described in the text is read by Vaishnavas as the soul's latent spiritual power, which is activated through devotion and directed upward toward the Lord dwelling at the crown. The samadhi that crowns the yogic path is understood not as the dissolution of individual consciousness but as the devotee's ecstatic absorption in God's infinite beauty and love.
