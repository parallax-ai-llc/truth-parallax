## Overview

The Yoga Tattva Upanishad is dated to approximately the early centuries CE and represents an important transitional text between Upanishadic meditation and the fully developed Hatha Yoga tradition. Its fourfold classification of yoga — Mantra, Laya, Hatha, and Raja — provides evidence for the diversity of yogic practices in pre-classical India.

## Research Themes

Scholars debate the text's relationship to Patanjali's Yoga Sutras: the Yoga Tattva's system is both broader (four types of yoga versus one) and more physiologically detailed (kundalini, chakras, nadis). James Mallinson notes that the text's Hatha Yoga teachings predate the major Hatha Yoga texts (Hatha Yoga Pradipika, Gheranda Samhita) and may represent an earlier stratum of practice. The kundalini and chakra teachings connect the text to tantric traditions.
