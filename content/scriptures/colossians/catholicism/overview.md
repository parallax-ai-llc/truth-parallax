## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Colossians emphasizes the fullness of Christ present in and through the church, the role of tradition and apostolic teaching, and the household codes as a model for domestic spirituality. The letter's vision of Christ as the head of the body (1:18) grounds Catholic ecclesiology, while its warnings against false teaching affirm the need for authoritative doctrine.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Christ and the Church
The body-head metaphor (1:18) is read as establishing the organic relationship between Christ and the church. The church participates in Christ's fullness and mediates his presence to the world through sacrament and proclamation.

### Tradition and Teaching
Paul's exhortation to "continue in the faith, stable and steadfast" (1:23) and to hold to "the tradition" reflects the Catholic emphasis on apostolic tradition as a source of authority alongside Scripture. The false teaching Paul combats illustrates the danger of private interpretation.

### Baptismal Participation
"Having been buried with him in baptism, in which you were also raised with him" (2:12) is understood sacramentally: baptism effects real participation in Christ's death and resurrection, not merely symbolic identification.

## Theological Emphases

- **Fullness**: The church as the locus of Christ's fullness on earth
- **Baptism**: Sacramental death and resurrection with Christ
- **Domestic church**: The household codes (3:18–4:1) as the foundation of the *ecclesia domestica*
