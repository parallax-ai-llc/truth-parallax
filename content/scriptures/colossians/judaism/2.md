## Fullness in Christ Against False Teaching

Jewish scholarly engagement with Colossians 2 examines fullness against false teaching within the context of Second Temple Judaism and its relationship to emerging Christianity. The key passages — hidden treasures of wisdom (2:3), fullness of deity bodily (2:9), circumcision made without hands (2:11), nailing the record of debt (2:14), shadow and substance (2:17) — raise important questions about Paul's use of Jewish Scripture, his relationship to Pharisaic tradition, and the parting of ways between Judaism and Christianity.

Rabbinic parallels to Paul's arguments illuminate the Jewish context from which his thought emerged. The theological concepts in this chapter — covenant, righteousness, community — have roots in the Hebrew Bible and in the interpretive traditions of the sages, even when Paul develops them in new directions.

Modern Jewish scholars engage this chapter as part of a broader effort to understand Paul within Judaism rather than against it, recognizing the complex relationship between early Christ-followers and the wider Jewish community of the first century.
