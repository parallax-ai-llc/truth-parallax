## Overview

Eastern Orthodox reading of Colossians focuses on the cosmic Christ hymn (1:15-20), the doctrine of the divine image, and the renewal of the human person in Christ. The letter's declaration that Christ is "the image of the invisible God" (1:15) is foundational for Orthodox icon theology and for the understanding of humanity's creation and restoration in God's image.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Christ as the Image of God
Colossians 1:15 is a cornerstone of Orthodox theology: Christ perfectly images the invisible God, and through him humanity recovers the divine likeness lost in the fall. This theology underpins the veneration of icons — because God has made himself visible in Christ, the invisible can be depicted.

### Cosmic Liturgy
The Christ hymn's vision of all things created "through him and for him" (1:16) is read liturgically: creation itself is a cosmic liturgy, and the church's worship participates in this cosmic praise.

### Theosis and Renewal
"You have put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator" (3:10) describes the ongoing process of theosis — the restoration of the divine image through knowledge of God and participation in Christ.
