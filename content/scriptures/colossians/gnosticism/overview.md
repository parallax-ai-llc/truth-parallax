## Overview

Colossians was a crucially important text for Gnostic interpreters, primarily because of its use of the term *pleroma* ("fullness"), its references to cosmic "thrones, dominions, rulers, and authorities" (1:16), and its language of hidden mystery and secret wisdom. The Valentinian Gnostics in particular built their cosmological systems partly on Colossian terminology.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### The Pleroma
The declaration that "in him all the fullness (*pleroma*) of God was pleased to dwell" (1:19) was reinterpreted: the *Pleroma* is the divine realm of the aeons, and Christ descended from this fullness into the deficient material world to redeem the fallen sparks of light.

### Thrones, Dominions, and Archons
The cosmic hierarchy of 1:16 — "thrones, dominions, rulers, authorities" — was mapped onto the archonic hierarchy of Gnostic cosmology. These powers were identified with the malevolent rulers of the material cosmos, created by or subordinate to the Demiurge.

### The Hidden Mystery
The "mystery hidden for ages and generations but now revealed" (1:26) was read as *gnosis* — the secret knowledge of humanity's divine origin and the path of return to the *Pleroma*. Only the pneumatic (spiritual) elite receive this revelation.
