## Overview

Protestant interpretation of Colossians centers on the sufficiency of Christ against all rival claims to spiritual authority. The letter's declaration that "in him the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily" (2:9) and that believers "have been filled in him" (2:10) grounds the Protestant insistence that no supplementary mediator, ritual, or philosophical system is needed beyond Christ.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Sufficiency of Christ
The Christ hymn (1:15-20) establishes Christ's supremacy over all creation and all powers. Protestant interpreters apply this against any system — whether medieval Catholicism, modern syncretism, or prosperity theology — that adds requirements beyond faith in Christ.

### Freedom from Legalism
Paul's warning against those who judge believers "in questions of food and drink, or with regard to a festival or a new moon or a Sabbath" (2:16) has been central to Protestant arguments against ceremonial law and ritualism.

### The New Self
The ethical instruction to "put off the old self" and "put on the new self" (3:9-10) shapes Protestant sanctification theology: moral transformation flows from union with Christ, not from external observance.
