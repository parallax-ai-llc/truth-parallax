---
title: "Kiteba Cilwe - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kiteba-cilwe"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Melek Taus descended to the earth and blessed it. He walked upon the land in four directions, and wherever he walked, the earth brought forth abundance.

**2** He said to the people: "I am your lord and your protector. Worship God, the supreme creator, and honor me as his representative upon the earth."

**3** He taught the people the ways of righteousness: to speak truth, to deal justly with one another, and to keep the sacred observances that God had ordained.

**4** The first observance is the Wednesday, which is the sacred day, for on Wednesday Melek Taus first descended to the earth. On this day the faithful shall offer prayers and give thanks.

**5** The second observance is the New Year, which falls in the month of Nisan, in the spring, when the earth is renewed. On this day the faithful shall paint eggs of many colors, and shall gather flowers, and shall celebrate with joy.

**6** The third observance is the pilgrimage to the sacred shrine of Lalish, in the valley between the mountains. There the tomb of Sheikh Adi is found, and there the faithful shall go to pray and to receive blessings.

**7** Sheikh Adi was the holy man who came in ancient times to teach the Yazidis the true path. He dwelt in the valley of Lalish and established the sacred shrine. He was a man of great wisdom and holiness.

**8** Melek Taus revealed himself to Sheikh Adi and said: "Thou art my chosen servant. Through thee I shall teach my people the way of light."

**9** Sheikh Adi taught the people many things: the proper prayers, the sacred hymns, the laws governing marriage and family, the rituals of birth and death, and the order of the priestly castes.

**10** There are three priestly castes among the Yazidis: the Sheikhs, the Pirs, and the Murids. Each has its own duties and responsibilities. The Sheikhs are the teachers, the Pirs are the spiritual guides, and the Murids are the common believers.

**11** No one may marry outside their caste, for the order established by Sheikh Adi must be maintained. This is the will of Melek Taus, and it shall not be changed.

**12** The faithful shall not eat lettuce, for it is forbidden. They shall not wear the color blue next to their skin, for this is a sacred color reserved for Melek Taus. They shall not speak the name that is forbidden, for it brings calamity.

**13** Every Yazidi must have a brother or sister of the hereafter, a spiritual companion chosen from another caste, who shall pray for them and guide them in matters of the spirit.

**14** These are the teachings that Melek Taus gave through Sheikh Adi, and they have been preserved from generation to generation, spoken in secret and guarded by the faithful.
