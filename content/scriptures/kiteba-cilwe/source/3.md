---
title: "Kiteba Cilwe - Chapter 3"
scripture: "kiteba-cilwe"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Melek Taus spoke further to the faithful, saying: "Know that the soul is eternal. It does not perish with the body, but passes from one form to another."

**2** "When a man dies, his soul enters another body, according to his deeds in life. If he has been righteous, his soul ascends to a higher state. If he has been wicked, his soul descends."

**3** "This is the law of transmigration, which God has ordained. Through many lives the soul is purified, until at last it returns to the divine light from which it came."

**4** "Therefore, live righteously, O my people. Do not steal, do not lie, do not bear false witness. Honor thy father and thy mother. Be generous to the poor and the stranger."

**5** "The greatest sin is to betray the faith. He who abandons the Yazidi religion shall be cut off from his people, for he has turned his back on Melek Taus."

**6** "Do not reveal the secrets of the faith to outsiders. The mysteries of Melek Taus are for the faithful alone. Guard them as thou wouldst guard thy life."

**7** Then Melek Taus taught the faithful about the seven angels who govern the world. Each angel has dominion over a day of the week, and each has authority over a portion of the earth.

**8** The first is Azazil, who is Melek Taus himself, the lord of all. The second is Dardail, the lord of Monday. The third is Israfil, the lord of Tuesday. The fourth is Mikail, the lord of Thursday. The fifth is Jibrail, the lord of Friday. The sixth is Shamsail, the lord of Saturday. The seventh is Nurail, the lord of Sunday.

**9** Each of these angels descended to the earth and took a portion of it under his care. They brought forth the different peoples and nations, each according to the will of God.

**10** But Melek Taus is above them all, for he is the representative of God upon the earth. He alone has the authority to forgive sins and to grant blessings.

**11** The faithful shall honor the sanjak, the sacred image of Melek Taus in the form of a peacock, which is carried throughout the Yazidi lands. When the sanjak comes, the people shall gather and offer their prayers and their gifts.

**12** For Melek Taus watches over his people always. He sees their deeds and knows their hearts. Nothing is hidden from him, for he is the eye of God upon the earth.

**13** And in the fullness of time, Melek Taus shall return in glory, and the faithful shall be gathered to him. The righteous shall dwell in the garden of light, and the wicked shall be purified in the fires of the hereafter.

**14** This is the promise of Melek Taus to his people, and it shall not fail. For God is faithful, and his angels are true, and the light shall overcome the darkness in the end.
