---
title: "Kiteba Cilwe - Chapter 1"
scripture: "kiteba-cilwe"
chapter: 1
---

**1** In the name of the most compassionate and merciful God. Praise be to God, the Lord of all worlds, the merciful, the compassionate, who created the world and all that is therein.

**2** In the beginning was the great God, the creator of all. He existed before all things, and from his essence came forth the light of the world.

**3** He created the pearl of great beauty, and from it he fashioned the heavens and the earth. For forty thousand years the pearl rested upon nothing, and then God created the great bird, Anfar, and placed the pearl upon its back.

**4** Then God created the seven angels from his own illumination. The first among them was Melek Taus, the Peacock Angel, who was chief among all.

**5** God said to the seven angels: "I have created you from my light, and I have given you dominion over the earth and all that dwells therein."

**6** Melek Taus was the most devoted of all the angels. He wept for seven thousand years before God, and his tears formed the springs and rivers of the earth.

**7** And God said to Melek Taus: "Thou art the greatest of my angels. I have placed thee above all others. Thou shalt be the lord of this world, and the guardian of my people."

**8** Then God created the form of man from earth, water, fire, and air. He breathed into him the breath of life, and man became a living soul.

**9** God said to the angels: "Bow down before Adam, whom I have created." And all the angels bowed down, for such was the command of God. And Melek Taus was the first to worship God alone and then to honor Adam.

**10** And God was pleased with Melek Taus and said: "Because thou hast honored me above all, and because thou hast understood my will, I have made thee ruler over all the earth."

**11** Then God gave to Adam a companion, and from them descended all the peoples of the earth.

**12** God placed the Yazidis in a special place of honor, for they were the chosen people, descended from Adam alone. Their seed was placed in a jar, and from that jar came forth the ancestors of the Yazidi people.

**13** And God sent Melek Taus to watch over the Yazidis and to guide them in the path of righteousness. He said: "These are my people, and thou art their guardian."

**14** The Yazidis shall not speak the name of Melek Taus in vain, nor shall they utter words of disrespect toward any of the angels. For the angels are the servants of God, and God has placed them in authority over the earth.

**15** This is the first teaching of the Book of Illumination: that God is one, that his angels serve him, and that Melek Taus is the chief among them, the guardian of the earth and the protector of the faithful.
