## Overview

The Kiteba Cilwe (Book of Revelation or Book of Illumination) is the second of the two principal sacred texts of the Yazidi tradition. Cast as a first-person revelation by Melek Taus (the Peacock Angel), the text presents the divine self-disclosure of the chief angelic figure in Yazidi theology, articulating his nature, power, and relationship to the Yazidi people.

In this remarkable text, Melek Taus speaks directly to the reader, asserting his divine authority, describing his governance of earthly affairs, and establishing the covenant between himself and the Yazidi community. The text addresses the nature of divine power, the reasons for Yazidi distinctiveness, and eschatological promises of vindication. Like the Meshaf Resh, the Kiteba Cilwe has a complex textual history involving oral transmission, multiple recensions, and scholarly debate about authenticity and provenance.
