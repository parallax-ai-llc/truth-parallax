A comparative reading of Section 2 of the Kiteba Cilwe situates its treatment of divine power within the broader context of world religious literature. The themes of omnipotence, sovereignty find parallels and contrasts in multiple traditions, offering rich material for cross-cultural analysis.

Comparative scholars have identified resonances between this section's teachings and similar discussions in other scriptures and philosophical texts. These parallels illuminate both universal patterns in human religious thought and the distinctive contributions of Yazidi Faith to the world's understanding of divine power.
