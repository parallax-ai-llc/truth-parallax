## Overview

Jewish scholarly engagement with the Visperad focuses on the parallels between the Zoroastrian Gahambar festivals and the Jewish liturgical calendar, as well as the broader question of Persian influence on Second Temple Jewish worship. The Visperad's emphasis on seasonal sanctification, communal assembly, and priestly ritual finds striking echoes in Jewish practice developed during and after the Persian period.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### Festival Parallels
The six Gahambars, with their combination of worship, communal feasting, and agricultural thanksgiving, parallel Jewish festivals such as Sukkot (Tabernacles), Shavuot (Weeks/Pentecost), and Pesach (Passover). The structure of multi-day festivals combining sacrifice and communal meals is shared.

### Priestly Calendar Traditions
The Visperad's concern with the proper liturgical calendar parallels intense Second Temple debates about the calendar (evident in the Dead Sea Scrolls), suggesting shared Persian-period concerns about sacred time.

### Communal Worship Assembly
The Visperad's invocation of the gathered faithful (*hamazor*) parallels the Jewish concept of the *minyan* and the gathered congregation as the proper context for solemn worship.
