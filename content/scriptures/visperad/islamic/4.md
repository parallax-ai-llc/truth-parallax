## Overview

From an Islamic scholarly perspective, Visperad Karda 4 (The Ritual Offerings) is examined within the framework of the Quran's reference to the Majus (Zoroastrians) and the broader Islamic engagement with pre-Islamic Persian religious culture. This chapter, part of the Visperad, the liturgical supplement recited during the Gahambar festivals, addresses themes that Muslim scholars have engaged with critically and comparatively — assessing Zoroastrian teachings against the standard of *tawhid* and Islamic law.

The historical encounter between Islam and Zoroastrianism following the conquest of Persia produced a rich body of Muslim writing about Zoroastrian beliefs and practices, ranging from polemical critique to scholarly documentation by figures like al-Biruni and al-Shahrastani.

## Key Themes

- **Tawhid and dualism**: Islamic critique of Zoroastrian theological categories in light of the absolute oneness of God
- **Legal comparison**: Parallels and contrasts between Zoroastrian law as reflected in this chapter and Islamic *shari'ah* and *fiqh*
- **Cultural encounter**: The historical experience of Muslim-Zoroastrian interaction and its intellectual legacy

## Key Verses

The chapter's approximately 7 verses address themes of offering and ritual that intersect with Islamic theological and legal concerns. Muslim scholars have examined such passages both critically and with scholarly interest.

## Commentary Tradition

Islamic commentary on Zoroastrian texts draws on the works of **al-Shahrastani** (*Kitab al-Milal wa al-Nihal*), **al-Biruni** (*al-Athar al-Baqiyah*), and the broader *milal wa nihal* (sects and creeds) genre. Contemporary Islamic scholars continue to engage with Zoroastrian texts in the context of interfaith dialogue and the study of pre-Islamic Persian civilization.
