## Overview

The Amitabha Sutra (阿彌陀經), also known as the Smaller Sukhavativyuha Sutra, is the briefest of the Three Pure Land Sutras and the most widely recited text in East Asian Pure Land Buddhism. Unlike the longer sutras, the Buddha delivers this teaching unsolicited, spontaneously describing the wonders of Amitabha's Western Pure Land to his disciple Shariputra and urging all beings to aspire to rebirth there through the practice of mindful recitation.

## Structure

The sutra follows a single, flowing discourse:

1. **Description of Sukhavati**: The jeweled trees, pools, celestial music, and radiant light of the Pure Land
2. **Six Directions Buddhas**: Buddhas of every direction attest to the truth of this teaching
3. **Call to Practice**: The exhortation to hold Amitabha's name with single-minded concentration

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Amitabha Sutra is chanted daily in monasteries and homes throughout East Asia:

- **Pure Land**: The daily liturgical text par excellence in Chinese, Korean, and Vietnamese Pure Land practice
- **Mahayana**: Integral to evening services in nearly all Chinese Buddhist monasteries
- **Zen**: Frequently recited alongside meditation practice in dual-practice lineages
- **Tibetan**: Amitabha devotion exists in Tibetan Buddhism through Phowa and Sukhavati practices

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Spontaneous Teaching | The Buddha teaches without being asked, indicating the teaching's supreme importance |
| Adornments of Sukhavati | Vivid descriptions conveying the perfection of Amitabha's Buddha-land |
| Mindful Recitation | Holding the name of Amitabha with undistracted concentration for one to seven days |
| Universal Witness | Buddhas of six directions confirm the truth of this sutra |
| Faith | Emphasis on trusting the Buddha's words about what cannot be seen directly |
