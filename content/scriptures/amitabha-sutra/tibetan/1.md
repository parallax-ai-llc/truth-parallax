The Amitabha Sutra's vivid description of Sukhavati provides the imagery that Tibetan practitioners use in their visualization of Dewachen. The jeweled landscape, lotus pools, and radiant light described in the sutra are visualized in detail during aspiration prayers and phowa practice. In the Vajrayana framework, this visualization is not mere imagination but a tantric practice that purifies ordinary perception and aligns the practitioner's mind with the pure perception of enlightened beings. The sutra's promise that Amitabha will appear at the moment of death to guide the faithful resonates with the Tibetan emphasis on consciousness transference at death, where the practitioner directs awareness toward Amitabha's Pure Land through the crown of the head.
