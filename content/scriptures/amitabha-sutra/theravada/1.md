The Amitabha Sutra's description of Sukhavati and its call for devoted recitation lie outside the Theravada canonical framework, yet the text's underlying dynamics are not entirely foreign to the Pali tradition. The practice of buddhanussati — sustained recollection of the Buddha's qualities — is one of the forty canonical meditation subjects listed in the Visuddhimagga. When a Theravada practitioner contemplates the Buddha's virtues with concentrated attention, the resulting mental unification shares structural features with the single-minded recitation the Amitabha Sutra prescribes. The key difference lies in soteriology: Theravada buddhanussati is a concentration practice that supports insight, while the Amitabha Sutra's recitation is understood as securing rebirth in a transcendent realm through the Buddha's vow-power.
