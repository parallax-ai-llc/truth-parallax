## Overview

The Amitabha Sutra lies outside the Pali canon and has no formal role in Theravada practice. However, the Theravada tradition's own devotional practices — particularly buddhanussati (recollection of the Buddha's qualities) — share certain structural features with the recitative practice the Amitabha Sutra prescribes.

## Comparison with Pali Devotion

Theravada practitioners recite the qualities of the Buddha (Buddho, Araham, Samma-sambuddho) as a concentration practice. While the doctrinal framework differs from Pure Land recitation, both practices use verbal repetition focused on the Buddha as a means of cultivating mental unification. The Amitabha Sutra's call for single-minded recitation resonates with the Theravada understanding of ekaggata (one-pointedness) as a factor of concentration.

## Modern Encounters

In Southeast Asian countries with significant Chinese Buddhist populations, Theravada practitioners encounter the Amitabha Sutra through cultural contact. This has fostered mutual understanding while highlighting the distinctive soteriological emphases of each tradition.
