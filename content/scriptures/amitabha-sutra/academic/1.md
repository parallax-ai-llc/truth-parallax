Academic study of the Amitabha Sutra has focused on its textual history, translation dynamics, and liturgical function. Kumarajiva's 402 CE Chinese translation, renowned for its literary elegance, became the standard version chanted throughout East Asia. Comparison with Xuanzang's later, more literal translation and the surviving Sanskrit text reveals how translation choices shaped religious practice — Kumarajiva's accessible style contributed directly to the sutra's popularity as a liturgical text. Scholars such as Daniel Stevenson have examined the sutra's central role in Chinese Buddhist evening services, showing how a brief text became the most frequently recited scripture in East Asian Buddhism through its integration into monastic daily ritual.
