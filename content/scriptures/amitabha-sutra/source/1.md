---
title: "Amitabha Sutra Chapter 1"
scripture: "amitabha-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was dwelling in Shravasti, in Jeta's Grove, in the garden of Anathapindada, together with a great assembly of monks, twelve hundred and fifty in number, all great Arhats well known to the assembly.

**2** At that time the Buddha said to the Elder Shariputra: "West of here, past a hundred thousand million Buddha-lands, there is a world called Ultimate Bliss. In that land there is a Buddha called Amitabha, who is now teaching the Dharma."

**3** "Shariputra, why is that land called Ultimate Bliss? Because the beings in that land experience no suffering whatsoever, but only every kind of joy. Therefore it is called Ultimate Bliss."

**4** "Moreover, Shariputra, the Land of Ultimate Bliss is surrounded by seven tiers of railings, seven layers of netting, and seven rows of trees, all adorned with the four kinds of precious gems. For this reason, that land is called Ultimate Bliss."

**5** "Moreover, Shariputra, in the Land of Ultimate Bliss there are pools of the seven gems, filled with the water of eight meritorious qualities. The bottom of each pool is covered with golden sand. On the four sides are stairs of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and crystal. Above are towers adorned with gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, mother-of-pearl, red pearls, and carnelian."

**6** "In the pools there are lotuses as large as cartwheels: blue ones radiating blue light, yellow ones radiating yellow light, red ones radiating red light, white ones radiating white light — subtle, wonderful, fragrant, and pure."

**7** "Moreover, Shariputra, in that Buddha-land heavenly music is constantly playing, and the ground is made of gold. Six times during the day and night a heavenly rain of mandarava flowers falls. In the early morning, the beings of that land fill their robes with these wonderful flowers and make offerings to hundreds of thousands of millions of Buddhas in other lands. At mealtime they return and take their meal and walk about."

**8** "Moreover, Shariputra, in that land there are always rare and wonderful birds of many kinds and colors: white cranes, peacocks, parrots, sharikas, kalavinkas, and birds of shared destiny. Six times during the day and night, these birds sing with harmonious and elegant sounds, proclaiming the five roots, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, and the Eightfold Noble Path. When the beings of that land hear these sounds, they all recollect the Buddha, recollect the Dharma, and recollect the Sangha."

**9** "Shariputra, do not think that these birds were born as the result of evil karma. Why? Because in that Buddha-land the three evil destinies do not exist. In that land even the names of the three evil destinies do not exist, how much less the realities! These birds have been manifested by Amitabha Buddha so that the Dharma-sounds may be proclaimed."

**10** "Shariputra, if there are good men and good women who hear the name of Amitabha Buddha and hold fast to it — whether for one day, two days, three, four, five, six, or seven days — single-mindedly and without confusion, then at the end of life, Amitabha Buddha, together with his holy assembly, will appear before those persons. When those persons die, their minds will not be confused, and they will immediately attain rebirth in Amitabha Buddha's Land of Ultimate Bliss."

**11** "Shariputra, because I see these benefits, I speak these words: if there are sentient beings who hear this teaching, they should vow to be born in that land. All the Buddhas of the six directions — east, south, west, north, below, and above — with their long, broad tongues, covering the three-thousand-great-thousand worlds, speak truthful words: 'Sentient beings, you should believe this sutra, praised by all Buddhas, which speaks of the inconceivable merits and virtues of Amitabha.'"

**12** When the Buddha finished speaking this sutra, Shariputra and all the monks and nuns, all the laymen and laywomen, and all the devas, humans, and asuras of the entire world, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully received it, made obeisance, and departed.
