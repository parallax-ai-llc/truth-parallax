The Amitabha Sutra's vision of a celestial paradise accessible through devoted recitation invites comparison with devotional traditions across world religions. The practice of holding a sacred name with single-minded concentration parallels the Hindu practice of nama-japa, the Sikh practice of naam simran, the Orthodox Christian Jesus Prayer, and the Sufi practice of dhikr. All these traditions share the conviction that sustained invocation of a sacred name transforms consciousness and brings the practitioner into the presence of the divine or transcendent. The Amitabha Sutra's distinctive contribution is its cosmological specificity — the detailed description of the destination — combined with the cosmic attestation of Buddhas in six directions, which grounds the practice in a Buddhist understanding of multiple world-systems and multiple Buddhas.
