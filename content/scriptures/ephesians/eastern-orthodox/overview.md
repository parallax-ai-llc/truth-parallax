## Overview

Eastern Orthodox theology finds in Ephesians a comprehensive vision of God's cosmic plan (*oikonomia*) to unite all things in Christ (1:10). The letter's themes of recapitulation, mystery, and the church as the fullness of Christ are foundational for Orthodox theology, liturgy, and spirituality.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Cosmic Recapitulation
Ephesians 1:10 — "to unite all things in him, things in heaven and things on earth" — is read through the lens of Irenaeus' theology of *anakephalaiosis* (recapitulation). Christ gathers up and restores all creation, reversing Adam's fall.

### The Mystery of the Church
The church as the "fullness" (*pleroma*) of Christ (1:23) is understood not as an institution but as the mystery of divine-human communion. The liturgy makes this reality present in each celebration.

### Theosis
"That you may be filled with all the fullness of God" (3:19) is a key text for the Orthodox doctrine of deification. The goal of Christian life is not merely moral improvement but participation in the divine nature.
