## Overview

Ephesians has been central to Protestant theology from the Reformation onward. The declaration that "by grace you have been saved through faith, and this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works" (2:8-9) is one of the most frequently cited passages in Protestant soteriology, encapsulating the doctrine of *sola gratia* (grace alone).

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Sola Gratia
Ephesians 2:8-9 is the locus classicus for the Protestant insistence that salvation is entirely God's gift. Calvin built his doctrine of election partly on Ephesians 1's language of predestination and the believer being "chosen before the foundation of the world" (1:4).

### The Church as Body of Christ
Protestant ecclesiology draws on Ephesians' vision of the church as a living organism with Christ as head (1:22-23; 4:15-16). This image has been used both to critique institutional hierarchies and to affirm the diversity of spiritual gifts.

### Spiritual Warfare
The armor of God passage (6:10-20) has been enormously influential in Protestant piety, shaping prayer practices, spiritual formation, and the understanding of the Christian life as a battle against cosmic powers.
