## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Ephesians emphasizes the letter's ecclesiology, its sacramental theology, and its vision of marriage as a reflection of Christ's relationship to the church. The declaration that the church is "built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets" (2:20) grounds Catholic teaching on apostolic succession and tradition.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Ecclesiology
Ephesians' vision of the church as the "body of Christ" and the "fullness of him who fills all in all" (1:23) is foundational for Catholic ecclesiology. The church is not merely a human institution but a divinely constituted reality participating in Christ's own fullness.

### Marriage as Sacrament
The analogy between Christ and the church and husband and wife (5:21-33) is the primary New Testament basis for Catholic teaching that marriage is a sacrament. The "great mystery" (*mysterion mega*, 5:32) is interpreted as pointing to the sacramental nature of the marital bond.

### Grace and Good Works
Catholic interpretation of 2:8-10 emphasizes that while salvation is by grace through faith, believers are "created in Christ Jesus for good works" (2:10). Grace and works are not opposed but integrated.

## Theological Emphases

- **Unity**: The church as the instrument of cosmic unity (1:10)
- **Sacramental life**: Baptism, Eucharist, and marriage as participation in Christ
- **Social teaching**: The "new humanity" (2:15) as a model for reconciliation
