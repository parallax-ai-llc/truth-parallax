## Spiritual Blessings in Christ

Catholic interpretation of Ephesians 1 reads spiritual blessings within the broader framework of the church's sacramental and moral theology. The key passages — chosen before the foundation (1:4), predestination (1:5), sealed with the Spirit (1:13), Christ above all (1:20-23) — illuminate the relationship between grace, faith, and the life of virtue within the community of believers.

The Church Fathers, particularly John Chrysostom and Augustine, provided foundational readings of this chapter that continue to shape Catholic exegesis. The emphasis falls on how Paul's teaching integrates personal transformation with communal responsibility and sacramental participation.

Catholic moral theology draws on this chapter's instruction to inform the church's social teaching and pastoral practice. The text is read not in isolation but within the living tradition of the church, where Scripture, tradition, and the magisterium work together to illumine God's revelation.
