## Overview

Islamic engagement with Ephesians centers on its themes of divine sovereignty, cosmic order, and the moral obligations of community life. The letter's emphasis on God's supreme will governing all things resonates with the Islamic understanding of *qadr* (divine decree) and *tawhid* (divine unity).

## Quranic Resonances

### Divine Sovereignty
Ephesians' repeated emphasis on God's will and purpose (1:5, 9, 11) aligns with the Quranic teaching that God is sovereign over all affairs: "To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth" (Quran 2:255).

### Cosmic Order
The vision of all things united under one head (1:10) resonates with Islam's understanding of *tawhid* — the absolute oneness of God ordering all creation.

### Community and Ethics
The ethical instructions of chapters 4–6 — truthfulness, honest labor, generosity, familial responsibility — parallel Quranic moral teaching closely.

## Points of Divergence

| Ephesians Theme | Islamic Perspective |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Church as body of Christ | The *ummah* is the community of believers, not a mystical body |
| Salvation by grace through faith | Salvation through faith, righteous deeds, and divine mercy |
| Christ as head of all things | God alone is sovereign; Christ (*Isa*) is a prophet |
