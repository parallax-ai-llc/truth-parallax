## Overview

Ephesians was one of the most important Pauline letters for Gnostic interpreters. Its language of "heavenly places" (1:3, 20; 2:6), "principalities and powers" (3:10; 6:12), "mystery" (1:9; 3:3-4; 5:32), and "fullness" (*pleroma*, 1:23; 3:19) mapped directly onto Gnostic cosmology and soteriology, making the letter a primary source for Valentinian and other Gnostic systems.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### Heavenly Places and the Pleroma
The repeated references to "heavenly places" (*en tois epouraniois*) were identified with the *Pleroma* — the divine fullness above the material cosmos. Being "seated with Christ in the heavenly places" (2:6) described the Gnostic's true spiritual citizenship in the realm of light.

### Principalities and Powers
The "rulers and authorities in the heavenly places" (3:10) and the "cosmic powers over this present darkness" (6:12) were read as the archons — demonic rulers of the material world who must be overcome through *gnosis*.

### The Great Mystery
The "great mystery" of 5:32 — ostensibly about marriage — was reinterpreted in Valentinian theology as the mystery of the *syzygy* (paired emanation) and the reunion of the fallen Sophia with her divine counterpart in the Pleroma.
