## Overview

Academic study of the Yasna applies the tools of modern scholarship — Indo-Iranian comparative linguistics, historical criticism, literary analysis, and archaeological context — to understand the text within its ancient Iranian setting. The Yasna is of particular importance to scholars because it contains the Gathas, the oldest compositions in the Avestan language and the primary evidence for the historical Zarathustra and his teachings. Academic approaches rigorously distinguish between the Old Avestan Gathas (likely composed c. 1200-1000 BCE, though dates remain debated) and the Younger Avestan liturgical framework.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Avestan Linguistics
The Gathas present formidable linguistic challenges due to the archaic nature of Old Avestan, its small corpus, and numerous *hapax legomena*. Scholars like **Helmut Humbach**, **Stanley Insler**, and **Prods Oktor Skjærvø** have produced competing translations that differ significantly, reflecting genuine ambiguity in the text.

### Comparative Indo-Iranian Studies
The Yasna is studied alongside the Vedic *Rigveda* and the *yajna* ritual, as the two traditions share a common Indo-Iranian heritage. Cognates between Avestan *yasna* and Sanskrit *yajña* extend to ritual vocabulary, divine names, and liturgical structure.

### Redaction and Composition History
Scholars analyze the layers of the Yasna — Old Avestan Gathas, the archaic Yasna Haptanghaiti, and the Younger Avestan framework — to reconstruct the text's compositional history and the development of Zoroastrian liturgy over centuries.

## Key Debates

| Debate | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Dating Zarathustra | Proposals range from 1700 BCE to 600 BCE; linguistic evidence favors the earlier range |
| Gathic Interpretation | Fundamental disagreements persist about the meaning of key Gathic passages |
| Ritual vs. Ethical Religion | Whether the Gathas represent a "reform" rejecting ritual or a transformation of existing practice |
| Haoma Question | Whether Zarathustra rejected or accepted haoma — Yasna 32:14 vs. Yasna 9-11 |
