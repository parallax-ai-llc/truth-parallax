---
title: "Yasna 9 – The Hom Yasht (Part 1)"
chapter: 9
---

**1.** At the hour of Hawan, Haoma came to Zarathushtra, as he served the fire and chanted the Gathas. Zarathushtra asked him: "Who art thou, O man, the most beautiful of all beings in the material world, whom I have seen to be of immortal frame?"

**2.** Thereupon Haoma, the holy, the bountiful, answered: "I am Haoma, the righteous, the furtherer of the world. Come and press me, O Zarathushtra, for the drink of life! Praise me as the other Saoshyants have praised me before thee."

**3.** Then Zarathushtra asked: "Homage unto Haoma! Who was the first of mortals who pressed thee for the corporeal world? What blessings did he obtain?"

**4.** Haoma answered: "Vivanghvant was the first of mortals who pressed me. This was the blessings he obtained thereby: to him was born a son, Yima the splendid, who ruled over all the seven regions of the earth, who made death and disease depart from them."

**5.** "Who was the second, O Haoma?" Haoma answered: "Athwya was the second. This was the blessing he obtained: to him was born a son, Thraetaona of the heroic line, who smote Azhi Dahaka, the three-mouthed, the three-headed, the six-eyed."

**6.** "Who was the third, O Haoma?" Haoma answered: "Thrita of the family of the Samas was the third. This was the blessing he obtained: to him were born two sons, Urvakhshaya and Keresaspa, mighty warriors and champions of the Right."

**7.** "Who was the fourth, O Haoma?" Haoma answered: "Pourushaspa was the fourth. This was the blessing he obtained: to him wast thou born, O Zarathushtra, the righteous, the enemy of the Daevas, the friend of Ahura Mazda!"

**8.** "Thou, O Zarathushtra, wast born in the house of Pourushaspa, the enemy of the Daevas, the friend of Ahura Mazda, the famed in the land of Iran."

**9.** "Thou, Zarathushtra, wast the first who recited the Ahuna Vairya. Thou wast the first who recited the doctrine of the Right. The Daevas hid in the earth when thou wast born."

**10.** Zarathushtra asked: "O fair Haoma, golden-hued! What are the gifts for him who shall press thee in the corporeal world?" Haoma answered: "He shall obtain righteousness and a good name. His children shall be healthy and strong. He shall live long and prosper."
