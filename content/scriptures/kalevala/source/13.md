---
title: "Kalevala Canto 13"
scripture: "kalevala"
chapter: 13
---

Ilmarinen, smith eternal,
Hammered out the magic Sampo,
Forged it from a swan's quill feather,
From the milk of barren heifer,
From a single grain of barley,
From the wool of summer lambkin.

First he forged a bow of gold,
Then a boat with red-gold trimming,
Then a heifer, golden-horned,
Then a plough of gold and silver.
None of these the right creation.

But he broke them, threw them backward,
Back into the flaming furnace.
Then the winds blew, wild winds blowing,
Eastern wind and western wind,
Southern wind and northern wind.

Blew one day and then a second,
Blew a third day without ceasing.
Then the Sampo rose to being,
Lid of many colors gleaming.
