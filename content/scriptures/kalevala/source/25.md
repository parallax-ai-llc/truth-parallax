---
title: "Kalevala Canto 25"
scripture: "kalevala"
chapter: 25
---

Ilmarinen, smith eternal,
Mourned his lost and murdered wife.
From gold he forged another maiden,
From silver fashioned her a bride.

But the golden maiden answered not,
Her silver lips they could not speak,
Her side was cold against his body,
No warmth came from her gleaming form.

Then Ilmarinen spoke in sadness:
No man should forge himself a wife,
No man should fashion bride of metal.
Gold gives neither love nor comfort,
Silver offers no embrace.

He journeyed back to dark Pohjola,
Sought a second maid from Louhi.
But Louhi scorned and mocked the smith,
Would not give a second daughter.
