---
title: "Kalevala Canto 31"
scripture: "kalevala"
chapter: 31
---

Vainamoinen, old and steadfast,
Mourned the kantele, his treasure,
Lost beneath the ocean waters.

From the jawbone of a great pike,
From its teeth he made the pegs,
From the hair of Hiisi's gelding,
He fashioned strings of five.

Then he sat upon the hillock,
Played the kantele of fishbone.
All of nature came to listen,
Forest creatures gathered round him.

Birds descended from the tree-tops,
Fish arose from out the water,
Even Tapio the forest-god
Climbed a birch to hear the playing.

Tears of joy ran down their faces,
Tears like pearls of ocean water.
