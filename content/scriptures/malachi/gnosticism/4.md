The "Sun of Righteousness" with "healing in its wings" (4:2) provides one of the richest symbols for Gnostic light theology. In Gnostic cosmology, the true God is identified with Light — the *Autogenes* (Self-Generated) Light of the Pleroma that is radically distinct from the physical sun created by the Demiurge. The Sun of Righteousness, in a Gnostic reading, represents the breaking of the Pleromatic light through the shell of the material cosmos, illuminating those who have eyes to see. The "healing" it brings is the healing of the cosmic wound — the separation of the divine sparks from their source — through the restoration of knowledge.

The conjunction of Moses and Elijah (4:4-5) is given a typological reading in which Moses represents the law of the Demiurge (useful for governing the psychic class but not salvific in itself) and Elijah represents the prophetic pneumatic tradition that points beyond the law to direct knowledge of the divine. The Gnostic teacher fulfills and surpasses both: offering a knowledge that transcends both legal obedience and prophetic ecstasy, leading directly to union with the Pleroma.

The book's final threat — "lest I come and strike the land with a decree of utter destruction" (4:6) — is read as the Demiurge's characteristic mode of governance: threats, punishments, and coercion. The true God, by contrast, does not threaten destruction but offers liberation through knowledge. The Gnostic reading of Malachi thus ends with a sharp distinction between two deities: the wrathful, threatening god who closes the Old Testament and the hidden, merciful Father who will be revealed in the Gnostic gospels. The silence between the Testaments is, for the Gnostic, the silence before the true revelation breaks through.
