Gnostic reading of Malachi 1 seizes upon the critique of the Levitical priesthood as confirmation that the Demiurge's cultic apparatus has been corrupted from within. The priests who offer blind and lame animals are not merely negligent but are acting consistently with the nature of demiurgic worship: a system based on ignorance cannot produce genuine holiness. The Gnostic response to Malachi's call for better sacrifices would be that no material sacrifice, however perfect, can satisfy the true God who exists beyond the material realm entirely.

The election of Jacob over Esau (1:2-5) is read in the Valentinian system as reflecting the three types of humanity: the *pneumatic* (spiritual), the *psychic* (soul-ish), and the *hylic* (material). Jacob represents the psychic type who, while not fully awakened to *gnosis*, is capable of receiving it; Esau represents the hylic type, bound entirely to the material realm. The true pneumatics, however, stand above both — they are the hidden remnant who do not appear in the text but for whom the text's deeper meaning is intended.

The vision of universal worship (1:11) is given a distinctive Gnostic twist: the "pure offering" among all nations represents not a ritual act but the offering of *gnosis* itself — the knowledge of the true God that transcends all material worship systems. When the smoke of incense rises "from the rising of the sun to its setting," it symbolizes the ascent of the pneumatic soul through the archonic spheres to the Pleroma above, leaving behind the deficient worship of the Demiurge's Temple.
