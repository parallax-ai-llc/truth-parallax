The messenger of the covenant (3:1) is a figure of great interest for Gnostic Christology. In Gnostic systems, the revealer who descends from the Pleroma into the Demiurge's realm to awaken the pneumatic elect fulfills exactly this role: a messenger who comes "suddenly to his temple" — the temple being the human body that houses the divine spark. The Christ of Gnostic theology does not come to reform the Demiurge's Temple worship but to reveal its inadequacy and point the way beyond it to the true, spiritual worship of the Father of All.

The refining fire (3:2-3) is reinterpreted as the purifying effect of *gnosis* on the soul. Knowledge of one's true divine origin burns away the accumulated layers of material attachment, emotional bondage, and demiurgic conditioning that keep the pneumatic trapped in ignorance. The "sons of Levi" who are purified represent the psychic class that, through the intervention of the Gnostic revealer, can be elevated toward pneumatic awareness — though they will never fully achieve the gnosis that the pneumatics possess by nature.

The critique of those who say "it is vain to serve God" (3:14) presents an interesting case for Gnostic interpreters. On one level, this complaint is correct from a Gnostic perspective: serving the Demiurge is indeed vain, since the material rewards and punishments he dispenses are part of the prison system rather than true liberation. On another level, the Gnostic might agree with the prophet that those who possess genuine spiritual knowledge will be vindicated — not by the Demiurge but by the true God who sees beyond the material realm into the hidden depths of the pneumatic soul.
