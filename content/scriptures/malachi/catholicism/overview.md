## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Malachi gives special prominence to 1:11 — "From the rising of the sun to its setting my name is great among the nations, and in every place incense is offered to my name, and a pure offering" — which has been read since the earliest Church Fathers as a prophecy of the Eucharist. This verse is cited in the Catechism of the Catholic Church (CCC 1350) and in the documents of the Council of Trent as scriptural warrant for the sacrificial character of the Mass.

## Theological Emphases

The indictment of the priesthood (1:6-2:9) is read as both a warning to the Levitical priesthood and, by extension, to the Catholic ministerial priesthood. The Council of Trent invoked Malachi's vision of a "pure offering" among all nations as evidence that the Eucharistic sacrifice fulfills and surpasses the Temple sacrifices. The prophet's teaching on marriage (2:14-16) — "the LORD was witness between you and the wife of your youth" — supports Catholic teaching on the indissolubility of sacramental marriage.

## Liturgical Use

Malachi 3:1-4 is read during Advent and at the Feast of the Presentation of the Lord (February 2), connecting the prophet's vision of the Lord suddenly coming to His temple with the infant Jesus being presented in the Temple. The "Sun of Righteousness" (4:2) appears in Advent hymnody and the liturgy of Christmas.
