## Overview

The Tejobindu Upanishad ("Drop of Radiance" Upanishad) belongs to the Krishna Yajur Veda and is classified among the Samnyasa (renunciation) Upanishads. It presents an extraordinarily rigorous vision of non-dual realization, teaching a fifteen-limbed yoga that goes far beyond Patanjali's eight limbs. The text describes Brahman as the luminous point (*tejobindu*) — the radiant essence of consciousness that underlies all existence. Its uncompromising teaching on the nature of absolute reality makes it one of the most philosophically intense texts in the Upanishadic corpus.

## Structure

The Tejobindu Upanishad consists of six sections:

1. **Section 1 — The Fifteen-Limbed Yoga**: A systematic path of meditation beyond the traditional eight limbs
2. **Section 2 — The Nature of the Mind**: The mind's limitations and their transcendence
3. **Section 3 — The Radiant Point**: Brahman as the luminous essence of consciousness
4. **Section 4 — Negation of the World**: The systematic denial of phenomenal reality
5. **Section 5 — The State of the Liberated**: The jivanmukta's experience
6. **Section 6 — The Supreme Teaching**: The final declaration of non-dual truth

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: The radiant point as the Lord's luminous presence in the heart
- **Shaivism**: Tejobindu as Shiva's luminous consciousness at the center of being
- **Advaita Vedanta**: One of the most radical statements of non-dual truth in the Upanishads
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: The luminous Brahman as the personal God radiating infinite perfections
- **Academic**: Important for the study of yoga systems beyond Patanjali's framework
- **Comparative**: Parallels with mystical light traditions across world religions
