Section 1 presents the Tejobindu's distinctive fifteen-limbed yoga, extending Patanjali's eight limbs with additional contemplative stages. Vaishnava commentators read these as an intensified devotional discipline: the eight standard limbs are supplemented by *tarka* (reasoning), *dharana* (concentration on God), *pratyahara* (withdrawal into divine presence), *dhyana* (meditation on Vishnu), *samadhi* (absorption in God), *manasa* (mental worship), and *aishvarya* (divine power). The extended path reflects the depth of commitment required for complete communion with the Lord.

Each additional limb represents a deeper level of surrender and devotion, culminating in the recognition that only God's radiant presence exists. The fifteen-limbed path is not about self-effort alone but about progressively opening to the Lord's transforming grace.
