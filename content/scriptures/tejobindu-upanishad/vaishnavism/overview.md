## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Tejobindu Upanishad's teaching on the luminous point (*tejobindu*) as referring to Vishnu's radiant presence dwelling in the heart of every being. The fifteen-limbed yoga described in the text is understood as an extended devotional discipline that progressively purifies the mind for receiving the Lord's self-revelation.

## Theological Emphases

The Upanishad's rigorous demands — transcending not only the senses and mind but also the ego and all mental constructs — are read as the requirements for complete surrender (*prapatti*) to the Lord. The radiant point that the meditator eventually perceives is the light of Vishnu's presence, which transforms the devotee's consciousness and grants liberation. The text's radical non-duality is interpreted as describing the devotee's complete absorption in God's glory.
