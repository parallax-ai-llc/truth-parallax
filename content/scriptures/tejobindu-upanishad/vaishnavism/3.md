Section 3 reveals the *tejobindu* — the radiant point of luminous Brahman that is the essence of all existence. Vaishnava commentators identify this with Vishnu's radiant form dwelling in the heart, the self-luminous presence of God that is perceived when all mental obscurations are removed. The radiant point is not something created through meditation but the eternal light of the Lord that is always present.

The experience of the tejobindu is the devotee's direct vision (*darshana*) of God — the moment when the purified heart perceives the Lord's infinite radiance that was always there, hidden only by the mind's restless activity.
