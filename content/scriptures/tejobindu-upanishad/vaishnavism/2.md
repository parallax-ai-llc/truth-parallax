Section 2 examines the mind's nature and its role in spiritual bondage. The Tejobindu teaches that the mind is a product of ignorance — a superimposition upon the radiant self. Vaishnava commentators read this as describing the mind's natural tendency to turn away from God and toward material objects, creating the illusion of independence from the divine.

The teaching that the mind must be transcended through sustained meditation aligns with the Bhagavad Gita's instruction (6.25-26) that the mind should be gradually withdrawn from all objects and fixed on God alone. The mind's dissolution is not nihilistic but liberating — it reveals the divine light that the mind's activity had obscured.
