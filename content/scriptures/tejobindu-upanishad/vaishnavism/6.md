Section 6 presents the Tejobindu's final teaching: the absolute reality is the radiant Brahman, and nothing else truly exists. Vaishnava commentators read this as the culmination of devotion — the devotee so absorbed in God's glory that nothing else registers. The supreme teaching is not philosophical negation but devotional ecstasy: God is so overwhelming that the entire universe appears as His radiance.

The final declaration of non-duality is understood by Vaishnavas as expressing the devotee's complete absorption in God, not the metaphysical dissolution of the world.
