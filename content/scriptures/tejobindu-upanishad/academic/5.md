Section 5's description of the jivanmukta (one liberated while alive) contributes to the extensive Indian literature on the characteristics of the ideal sage. Scholars note that the jivanmukta concept raises important philosophical questions: how can a body continue to function after the dissolution of the ego and its motivations? The various schools answer this differently, reflecting deeper disagreements about the nature of liberation.

The jivanmukta ideal also has sociological significance, as it provides a model of spiritual authority that does not depend on institutional or ritual status but on the direct realization of truth.
