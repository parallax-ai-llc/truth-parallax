## Overview

The Tejobindu Upanishad is classified among the Samnyasa Upanishads and is generally dated to the early centuries CE. Its fifteen-limbed yoga system — extending Patanjali's eight limbs with additional contemplative stages — provides important evidence for the diversity of yoga systems in ancient India.

## Research Themes

Scholars note the text's philosophical extremism: its negation of all phenomenal categories is more radical than most other Upanishads, approaching the absolute negation found in Gaudapada's Karika and Nagarjuna's Madhyamaka. This raises questions about possible Buddhist influence or parallel development. The bindu (point) as a meditation object connects the text to tantric traditions that use point-concentration as a gateway to non-dual awareness.
