Section 4's systematic negation of phenomenal categories represents the most philosophically radical passage in the Tejobindu Upanishad. Scholars compare it with the *neti neti* ("not this, not this") method of the Brihadaranyaka, Gaudapada's *ajativada* (non-origination doctrine), and Nagarjuna's *sunyavada* (emptiness teaching). The extent of negation — encompassing not only the material world but also the gods, the Vedas, and even liberation itself — goes beyond most other Upanishadic texts.

The philosophical question of whether this represents a genuinely nihilistic position or a strategic negation aimed at pointing beyond all categories to an ineffable reality is debated among scholars.
