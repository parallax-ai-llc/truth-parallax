Section 1's fifteen-limbed yoga system is one of the most distinctive features of the Tejobindu Upanishad. Scholars note that this extended system — going beyond Patanjali's well-known eight limbs — provides evidence for the diversity of yoga classifications in ancient India. The additional limbs may represent practices that were part of oral teaching traditions not captured in the more standardized texts.

The fifteen-limbed system suggests a meditation-intensive approach that emphasizes internal transformation over the external observances more prominent in earlier yoga texts. The progressive refinement of practice from gross to subtle levels reflects the influence of tantric meditation methodology.
