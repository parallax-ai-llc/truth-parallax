Section 3's teaching on the *tejobindu* connects the Upanishad to the broader Indian tradition of *bindu* (point) meditation, which appears in both Vedantic and tantric contexts. The bindu as a meditation object represents the attempt to concentrate awareness to its most refined point, from which expansion into unlimited consciousness becomes possible.

Scholars note the phenomenological precision of the description — the radiant point perceived in meditation is a widely reported experience across contemplative traditions, suggesting a genuine experiential basis for the teaching. The Tejobindu Upanishad's distinctive contribution is the identification of this meditation experience with the ultimate metaphysical reality (Brahman).
