Section 6's supreme teaching represents the Tejobindu Upanishad's metaphysical climax — the identification of the radiant point (*tejobindu*) with the infinite Brahman. Scholars note the text's philosophical achievement in connecting the phenomenology of meditation (the experience of a radiant point in contemplation) with the metaphysics of non-duality (Brahman alone is real).

The section's uncompromising tone and absolute claims place it among the most radical expressions of monistic idealism in Indian philosophy, comparable to Gaudapada's *ajativada* and the later Advaita tradition's most extreme formulations.
