Section 5's description of the liberated sage resonates deeply with the Shaiva concept of *jivanmukti*. The sage who has recognized the tejobindu — the radiant essence of consciousness — lives in the world as Shiva Himself, free from all identification with limited forms. Every experience is recognized as a manifestation of Shiva's consciousness.

The jivanmukta's freedom is not a state achieved but a recognition realized: consciousness was never bound, and the sage's life becomes the spontaneous expression of this recognition.
