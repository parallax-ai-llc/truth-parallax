The fifteen-limbed yoga of Section 1 extends traditional yogic practice into the subtlest domains of consciousness. Shaiva interpreters recognize in these additional limbs the progressive dissolution of mental constructs (*vikalpas*) that veil Shiva's radiant nature. Beyond the standard eight limbs, the practitioner enters increasingly refined states of awareness where the distinction between meditator and object of meditation dissolves.

The extended yoga system reflects the Shaiva understanding that liberation requires going beyond conventional practice into the direct recognition of consciousness itself. Each additional limb is a further letting-go of limitation, a deeper penetration into the luminous core of awareness that is Shiva.
