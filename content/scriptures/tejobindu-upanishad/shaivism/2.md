Section 2's analysis of the mind as a product of ignorance resonates with the Kashmir Shaiva understanding of *manas* (mind) as one of the limiting *tattvas* (principles) that contract infinite consciousness. The mind's constant activity — creating thoughts, desires, and identifications — is the mechanism through which Shiva appears to be limited.

The Shaiva practitioner recognizes that the mind is not an enemy to be destroyed but a contracted form of consciousness to be expanded. The Tejobindu's teaching on transcending the mind points to the Shaiva goal of recognizing that awareness itself — prior to and beyond all mental activity — is unlimited Shiva.
