Madhva reads the fifteen-limbed yoga as an intensified devotional discipline designed to prepare the soul for God's grace. Each limb represents a deeper level of purification and focus, progressively removing the obstacles that prevent the soul from receiving Vishnu's self-revelation. The extended path reflects the genuine difficulty of spiritual transformation — it is not a simple matter but a life-long undertaking requiring extraordinary dedication.

For Dvaita, the fifteen limbs demonstrate that liberation requires serious effort — not because effort itself liberates, but because only a thoroughly purified and focused soul is ready to receive the grace that does.
