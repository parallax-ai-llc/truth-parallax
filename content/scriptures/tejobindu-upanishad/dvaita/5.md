Madhva reads the description of the liberated sage as the portrait of the perfect devotee — one who has been freed by God's grace from all material bondage and lives in constant awareness of the Lord's presence. The jivanmukta for Dvaita is not someone who has realized identity with Brahman but someone whose devotion has become so complete that it transforms every moment of life.

The liberated soul's equanimity and freedom from desire reflect not the transcendence of individuality but its perfection through love of God.
