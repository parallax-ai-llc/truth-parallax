## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Tejobindu Upanishad's teaching on the luminous point as describing God's infinite radiance — the glory of Vishnu that the purified soul perceives in deep meditation. The rigorous yoga described in the text is understood as the discipline required to prepare the soul for this overwhelming vision.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the text's negations — denying the ultimate reality of the world, the body, and the mind — are not ontological statements about the non-existence of creation but expressions of the soul's overwhelming focus on God. When the devotee is absorbed in God's radiance, everything else fades into insignificance, not because it is unreal but because God's glory eclipses all finite beauty.
