Madhva reads Section 6's supreme teaching as the revelation of God's infinite glory — a glory so overwhelming that it encompasses everything. The declaration that nothing exists apart from Brahman is understood not as a denial of creation's reality but as an affirmation of God's omnipresence. Nothing exists apart from God because God pervades everything, not because everything except God is non-existent.

The Dvaita reading preserves both God's transcendence (He is the radiant point beyond all) and His immanence (He pervades all as the radiant essence of every being).
