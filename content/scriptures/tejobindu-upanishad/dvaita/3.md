Madhva reads the *tejobindu* as the concentrated radiance of God — Vishnu's luminous essence perceived by the purified soul in deep meditation. The radiant point is not the soul's own light but God's light, and perceiving it is an experience of divine grace, not self-realization in the Advaita sense.

For Dvaita, the tejobindu confirms God's infinite perfection — His radiance is so intense that it can be concentrated into a single point and yet remain infinite. The devotee's vision of this radiance is the beginning of the eternal bliss of communion with God.
