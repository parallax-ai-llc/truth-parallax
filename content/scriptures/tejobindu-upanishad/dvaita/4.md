Madhva reads Section 4's negations with caution, interpreting them not as absolute denials of the world's reality but as expressions of the world's utter insignificance compared to God's infinite glory. The world is real but finite; God is real and infinite. When the devotee is absorbed in God's radiance, the world naturally recedes from attention — not because it is unreal but because God's beauty eclipses everything else.

For Dvaita, nihilistic interpretations of these negations are mistaken. The world is God's real creation, and denying its reality implicitly denies God's creative power.
