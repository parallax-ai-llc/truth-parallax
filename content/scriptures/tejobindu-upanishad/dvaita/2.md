Madhva reads Section 2's teaching on the mind's limitations as describing a real obstacle to spiritual progress. The mind's tendency toward material attachment is a genuine condition that must be overcome through discipline and divine grace. Unlike Advaita, which denies the mind's ultimate reality, Dvaita affirms the mind as a real instrument that can be either an obstacle or a vehicle, depending on its orientation.

The teaching that the mind must be transcended means, for Dvaita, that the mind must be purified and redirected rather than annihilated. A mind focused on God becomes transparent to the divine, while a mind focused on material objects becomes opaque.
