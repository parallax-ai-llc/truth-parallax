---
title: "Tejobindu Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "tejobindu-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. The point of light that is the supreme Brahman — on that we shall meditate. The Tejobindu is the supreme Brahman.

**2** The Param-Dhyana, or the highest meditation, should be upon Tejobindu, which is the Atman of the universe. That meditation should always be practised by the wise.

**3** He who has the supreme meditation using the Pranava or the syllable Om, that is the threefold Brahman — the three Vedas, the three worlds, the three fires, the three matras — and is a point of effulgence.

**4** From that effulgent point springs the universe of consciousness. He who constantly meditates on it is a Yogi, a knower of Brahman.

**5** The meditation should be practised with closed eyes in the lotus posture. One should contemplate the supreme Self as being in the middle of the lotus of the heart.

**6** Just as a lamp placed inside a vessel illumines it, so the Atman seated in the heart illumines the whole body.

**7** One should meditate on the Self as being pure consciousness, as being the witness of all, as self-luminous, as having the nature of pure bliss.

**8** The Atman is without parts, without action, tranquil, without fault, without taint. It is the supreme bridge to immortality.

**9** It is like a fire that has consumed all its fuel. He who knows this Atman — he becomes Brahman.

**10** The Yogi should ever think: "I am Brahman." There is no doubt about this. He who thinks thus attains liberation. He attains liberation indeed.
