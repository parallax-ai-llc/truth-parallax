---
title: "Tejobindu Upanishad - Chapter 6"
scripture: "tejobindu-upanishad"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Now the state of Videhamukti, liberation after the fall of the body, is described.

**2** When the Jivanmukta leaves the body, he does not return to any other body. He merges in Brahman as a river merges in the sea.

**3** Just as the space in a pot merges with the universal space when the pot is broken, so the liberated soul merges with the supreme Brahman.

**4** There is no return for the liberated one. There is no rebirth, no suffering, no bondage.

**5** He becomes one with the infinite, eternal, all-pervading Brahman. He becomes the supreme bliss itself.

**6** This is the teaching of the Tejobindu Upanishad. He who studies this Upanishad with devotion is freed from all sins.

**7** He who meditates on its meaning attains the state of Brahman. He is not born again in this world of sorrow.

**8** This Upanishad should be taught only to one who is devoted, who is calm, who has controlled the senses, and who is fit for the highest knowledge.

**9** Om. This is the truth. The knower of this truth becomes immortal. The knower of this truth becomes Brahman.

**10** Om. Peace, peace, peace.
