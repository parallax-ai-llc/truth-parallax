---
title: "Tejobindu Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "tejobindu-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Now the nature of the Jivanmukta, the one liberated while living, is described.

**2** He who has realized Brahman, who sees the Self in all beings and all beings in the Self — he is the Jivanmukta.

**3** He who is free from the pairs of opposites — pleasure and pain, heat and cold, praise and blame — he is the Jivanmukta.

**4** He who is the same in honour and dishonour, the same towards friend and foe, who has given up all undertakings — he is the Jivanmukta.

**5** He who has no attachment to the body, to the senses, to the mind, to the intellect — he is the Jivanmukta.

**6** He who sees Brahman in the waking state, in the dream state, and in deep sleep — he is the Jivanmukta.

**7** He who is free from 'I' and 'mine,' who is without desire, who is ever content — he is the Jivanmukta.

**8** He who has transcended the three gunas — sattva, rajas, and tamas — and who rests in the pure Self — he is the Jivanmukta.

**9** He who neither desires what is pleasant nor shuns what is unpleasant, who is beyond karma and beyond rebirth — he is the Jivanmukta.

**10** He who has attained the supreme knowledge, who has realized the identity of the Self with Brahman — he is the Jivanmukta. He is liberated while still in the body.
