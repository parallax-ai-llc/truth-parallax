---
title: "Tejobindu Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "tejobindu-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Brahman alone is real, the world is appearance. The individual self is none other than Brahman.

**2** Just as a rope is mistaken for a snake in the dark, so is Brahman mistaken for the world. When light comes, the snake is known to be a rope. When knowledge comes, the world is known to be Brahman.

**3** Just as in a mirage water is seen where there is no water, so in Brahman the world is seen where there is no world.

**4** Just as the blueness of the sky is not real, so the diversity of the world is not real. The one Brahman appears as many through the power of Maya.

**5** This Maya is neither real nor unreal, neither existent nor non-existent. It is indescribable. Yet by the knowledge of Brahman it is destroyed.

**6** When Maya is destroyed, the individual soul knows itself to be Brahman. There is no difference between the individual soul and Brahman.

**7** As the space enclosed in a pot is not different from the universal space, so the individual soul is not different from the supreme Self.

**8** When the pot is broken, the space in the pot becomes one with the universal space. When ignorance is destroyed, the individual soul becomes one with Brahman.

**9** This is the supreme truth. This is the highest knowledge. He who knows this is freed from all bondage.

**10** Om. This is the truth. This is the Upanishad. Om. Peace, peace, peace.
