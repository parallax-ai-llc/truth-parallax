---
title: "Tejobindu Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "tejobindu-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Now we shall describe the fifteen limbs of Yoga as taught in this Upanishad. They are: Yama, Niyama, Tyaga, Mouna, Desha, Kala, Asana, Mulabandha, Dehasamya, Drik-sthiti, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, and Samadhi.

**2** Yama has ten aspects: non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, compassion, straightforwardness, patience, firmness of mind, moderate diet, and cleanliness.

**3** Niyama also has ten aspects: penance, contentment, faith, charity, worship of God, hearing of scriptures, modesty, intellect, recitation, and sacrifice.

**4** Tyaga, or renunciation, is of two kinds: the renunciation of the pleasures of the senses and the renunciation of the ego.

**5** Mouna, or silence, is also of two kinds: the silence of speech and the silence of the mind. The real silence is the silence of the mind.

**6** The best Desha, or place, is one that is solitary, free from insects, fire, and water, with a smooth surface, neither too high nor too low.

**7** The best Kala, or time, is when one is neither too cold nor too warm, when the mind is calm and steady.

**8** Asana is that posture which is firm and pleasant. The lotus posture is the best.

**9** The fifteen limbs of Yoga, when practised with faith and devotion, lead to the highest state of consciousness.

**10** He who practises all fifteen limbs attains the state of Brahman. He becomes one with the supreme Self.
