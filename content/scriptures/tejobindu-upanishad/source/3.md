---
title: "Tejobindu Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "tejobindu-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The nature of the supreme Self is now described. It is without beginning and without end. It is unchanging and immovable.

**2** It is neither gross nor subtle. It is neither short nor long. It is neither red nor white. It is neither shadow nor darkness.

**3** It is not air, not ether, not fire, not water, not earth. It is not speech, not mind, not prana.

**4** It is without eyes, without ears, without hands, without feet. It is not the Vedas, not the sacrifices, not the pilgrimages.

**5** It is neither the enjoyer nor the enjoyment nor the enjoyed. It is neither the knower nor the knowledge nor the known.

**6** It is the reality behind all appearances. It is self-luminous. It is the source of all light. It is the supreme Brahman.

**7** As the sun illumines the whole world, so does the Atman illumine the whole universe of consciousness.

**8** As a lamp needs no other lamp to illumine itself, being in its own nature luminous, so the Self needs no other knowledge to know itself.

**9** The Self is its own proof. It shines by its own light. It is self-revealed and self-known.

**10** He who realizes this Self becomes free from all bondage. He becomes Brahman. He becomes Brahman indeed.
