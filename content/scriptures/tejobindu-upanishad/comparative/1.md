The Tejobindu's fifteen-limbed yoga invites comparison with other systematic spiritual paths that progressively refine the practitioner's awareness. The Christian mystical ladder (from purgation through illumination to union), the Sufi *maqamat* (stations) of the spiritual journey, the Buddhist *jhana* (absorption) stages, and the Kabbalistic ascent through the *Sefirot* all describe progressive stages of spiritual deepening.

The extension beyond eight to fifteen limbs reflects a universal pattern in contemplative traditions: as practice deepens, new subtleties are discovered that require new categories. The Christian contemplatives' distinction between acquired and infused contemplation, and the Buddhist distinction between *samatha* and *vipassana* at increasingly refined levels, parallel this developmental logic.
