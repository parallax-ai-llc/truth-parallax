The *tejobindu* — the radiant point of consciousness — finds parallels in light-mysticism across traditions. The Kabbalistic *nekudah* (primordial point) from which creation emanates, the Sufi *nuqta* (point of divine knowledge), the Tibetan Buddhist *tigle* (luminous drop), and the Christian mystics' experience of the divine spark (*scintilla animae* in Meister Eckhart) all describe a concentrated point of divine light at the center of consciousness.

The experience of inner light as a point or drop is reported across cultural contexts, suggesting that it may reflect a universal feature of deep contemplative experience. The Tejobindu's identification of this point with ultimate reality provides a metaphysical framework for an experience that other traditions describe in different theological terms.
