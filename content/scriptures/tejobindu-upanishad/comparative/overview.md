## Overview

The Tejobindu Upanishad's vision of ultimate reality as a point of radiance beyond all mental constructs invites comparison with light mysticism across traditions. The experience of divine light in meditation — from the initial spark to the infinite radiance of Brahman — parallels luminous experiences reported in Christian, Islamic, Jewish, and Buddhist contemplative traditions.

## Comparative Themes

The Tejobindu's radical negation of all phenomenal categories parallels the apophatic theology of Pseudo-Dionysius (God as "beyond all affirmation and negation"), Nagarjuna's Buddhist *sunyata* (emptiness of all categories), and Meister Eckhart's concept of the Godhead beyond God. The luminous point (*bindu*) as the gateway to transcendence compares with the Tibetan Buddhist *tigle* (luminous drop), the Kabbalistic *nekudah* (primordial point), and the Sufi *nuqta* (point of divine knowledge).
