The Tejobindu's portrait of the liberated sage — free from all attachment, dwelling in unbroken awareness of ultimate reality — parallels ideals of spiritual perfection across traditions. The Buddhist arhat (liberated one), the Christian saint in beatific vision, the Sufi *wali* (friend of God), and the Daoist sage who moves with the Tao all describe human beings who have achieved such complete transformation that their ordinary lives become transparent to the divine.

The concept of liberation while still alive — not waiting for death to be free — compares with the Buddhist *nirvana with remainder*, the Christian concept of realized eschatology (the kingdom of God already present), and the Zen master's enlightenment in everyday activity.
