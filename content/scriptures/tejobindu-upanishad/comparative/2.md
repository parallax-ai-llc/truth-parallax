The Tejobindu's radical treatment of the mind — denying its ultimate reality and seeking its transcendence — parallels contemplative traditions that aim to go beyond ordinary mental activity. The Zen concept of *mu-shin* (no-mind), the Christian Cloud of Unknowing's teaching on abandoning all thought to encounter God, and the Sufi ideal of *fana* (annihilation of the ego-mind in God) all describe the same fundamental movement beyond mental constructs to a deeper reality.

The philosophical question raised by Section 2 — whether the mind is ultimately real or a superimposition — echoes debates in Western philosophy from Descartes (can the mind doubt itself?) through Hume (is there a self beyond the stream of mental events?) to contemporary consciousness studies (is consciousness produced by the brain or more fundamental than it?).
