Section 4's systematic negation of all phenomenal reality parallels the most radical forms of apophatic theology and negative philosophy across traditions. Nagarjuna's emptying of all categories in the *Mulamadhyamakakarika*, Pseudo-Dionysius's denial that God is any of the things that can be said about Him, Meister Eckhart's concept of the Godhead beyond God, and the *via negativa* of John of the Cross all follow a similar trajectory of radical negation leading to ineffable reality.

The universal appearance of this strategy — denying everything that can be thought or said in order to point to what lies beyond thought and speech — suggests a fundamental structure of mystical cognition that transcends cultural boundaries.
