The Tejobindu's supreme teaching — that the radiant point of consciousness is the sole reality — encapsulates the core insight of non-dual mysticism across traditions. The Sufi teaching of *wahdat al-wujud* (unity of being), the Kabbalistic *Ein Sof* as the reality behind all manifestation, the Buddhist *tathagatagarbha* (Buddha-nature) as the luminous essence of all beings, and the Christian mystical concept of the divine ground (*Grunt* in Meister Eckhart) all point to the same recognition: the radiant core of consciousness is the ultimate reality, and everything else is its expression.

The Tejobindu's identification of the infinitesimal (point) with the infinite (Brahman) parallels the Zen koan tradition's paradoxical insights, the Christian mystics' coincidence of opposites, and the quantum physicist's wave-particle complementarity — all suggesting that reality at its deepest level transcends ordinary logical categories.
