For Advaita, the *tejobindu* — the radiant point — represents Brahman as self-luminous consciousness. This is the Advaita's core teaching: Brahman is *svayam-jyotis* (self-luminous), shining by its own light without needing any external illumination. The radiant point perceived in deep meditation is not a vision of something other than the self but the self's recognition of its own luminous nature.

The Tejobindu Upanishad's radical position is that this luminous point is the only reality — everything else is superimposition upon it. The meditator does not create the light through practice but removes the darkness of ignorance that concealed it.
