## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Tejobindu Upanishad is one of the most radical expressions of non-dual truth. Its systematic negation of all phenomenal categories — "not this, not this" taken to its extreme — and its description of Brahman as the luminous point beyond all mental constructs make it a favorite text among advanced Advaita practitioners.

## Theological Emphases

The fifteen-limbed yoga is read as a comprehensive program for dismantling the layers of ignorance that veil the non-dual self. The text's uncompromising position — that the world, the body, the mind, and even the gods are ultimately unreal compared to the luminous Brahman — represents the Advaita teaching at its most intense. The *tejobindu* is the self-luminous Brahman that remains when every superimposition has been removed.
