Section 1's fifteen-limbed yoga provides a comprehensive systematic path for the Advaita practitioner. Beyond the standard eight limbs, the additional stages involve progressively subtler forms of self-inquiry and contemplation. The extended system reflects the Advaita recognition that many seekers need a gradual, structured path to prepare for the final, instantaneous recognition of non-dual Brahman.

The fifteen limbs systematically remove layer after layer of false identification until only the self-luminous Brahman remains. The progression from gross to subtle to transcendent practice mirrors the Advaita understanding that the truth is always already present — practice simply removes what obscures it.
