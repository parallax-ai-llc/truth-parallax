For Advaita, Section 2's analysis of the mind is central: the mind is the instrument of *avidya* (ignorance), projecting the apparent world of multiplicity upon the non-dual Brahman. The teaching that the mind is itself unreal — a superimposition upon the self — represents the Advaita position at its most radical: not only is the world the mind's projection, but the mind itself is a projection upon the witnessing consciousness.

The dissolution of the mind described here is not the destruction of anything real but the recognition that the mind was never ultimately real. When the mind is recognized as a superimposition, the self-luminous Brahman that was always present stands revealed.
