For Advaita, Section 5 describes the state of *jivanmukti* — liberation while still living in the body. The jivanmukta has recognized the self as Brahman and lives in unbroken non-dual awareness. The body continues to function due to *prarabdha karma* (karma already set in motion), but the sage no longer identifies with it. There is no sense of doership, no attachment, no desire — only the radiance of Brahman shining through a transparent instrument.

This is the pinnacle of Advaita realization: not an experience to be achieved but the natural state that remains when all false identifications have been removed.
