Section 6 presents the Tejobindu's ultimate declaration: Brahman alone is real; the world is unreal; the individual self is Brahman. This encapsulates the three core propositions of Advaita Vedanta with extraordinary force and clarity. The radiant point is revealed as not other than the infinite Brahman — the appearance of finitude was the last veil, and with its removal, infinite consciousness stands revealed.

For Advaita, this section represents the moment of *aparoksha-anubhuti* — direct, unmediated realization — the goal of all Vedantic practice and the end of all seeking.
