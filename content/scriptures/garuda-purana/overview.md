## Overview

The Garuda Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, narrated by Vishnu to his divine mount Garuda, the celestial eagle. It is one of the most widely known Puranas due to its detailed treatment of death, the afterlife, funeral rites, and the journey of the soul — subjects that make it a key text in Hindu funeral and memorial rituals.

The Garuda Purana is generally dated to the 9th-12th centuries CE in its present form. Beyond its famous eschatological content, the text contains encyclopedic material on gemology, medicine, grammar, dharma, and devotional practices. In traditional Hindu practice, portions of the Garuda Purana are recited during the mourning period following a death, making it one of the most ritually active Puranas in contemporary Hindu life.

## Structure

The text is divided into three main parts:

1. **Purva Khanda — Earlier Section**: Cosmology, dharma, gemology, medicine, grammar, and the worship of Vishnu
2. **Preta Khanda — Section on the Dead**: The journey of the soul after death, the torments of hell, funeral rites, and memorial ceremonies
3. **Brahma Khanda — Brahman Section**: Philosophical teachings, Vedantic knowledge, yoga, and the nature of liberation

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Garuda Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: A key devotional text affirming that worship of Vishnu ensures a favorable afterlife and ultimate liberation
- **Shaivism**: Engaged primarily for its eschatological material, which is considered compatible with Shaiva afterlife theology
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for the Brahma Khanda's teachings on the identity of atman and Brahman beyond death
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Valued for its clear depiction of the soul's dependence on Vishnu for liberation from samsara
- **Academic**: A crucial source for understanding Hindu attitudes toward death, funeral rituals, and eschatological beliefs

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Death and Afterlife | Detailed accounts of what happens to the soul after death |
| Funeral Rites | The proper procedures for cremation, mourning, and memorial ceremonies |
| Heavens and Hells | Descriptions of post-mortem rewards and punishments |
| Gemology | The properties, classification, and spiritual significance of precious stones |
| Liberation | The ultimate release from the cycle of death and rebirth through Vishnu's grace |
