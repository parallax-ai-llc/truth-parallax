---
title: "Garuda Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "garuda-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Lord said: I shall now describe the nature of liberation and the means by which the soul attains freedom from the cycle of birth and death.

**2** Liberation is of four kinds: Salokya, dwelling in the same world as the Lord; Samipya, dwelling near the Lord; Sarupya, having the same form as the Lord; and Sayujya, complete union with the Lord.

**3** Salokya is attained through worship and devotion. The devotee dwells in the same world as the Lord and enjoys His divine presence.

**4** Samipya is attained through deeper devotion and service. The devotee abides near the Lord, ever in His company.

**5** Sarupya is attained through profound meditation on the form of the Lord. The devotee attains a divine form similar to that of the Lord.

**6** Sayujya is the highest liberation. It is attained through the knowledge of the identity of the self with Brahman. The devotee merges completely with the Supreme and becomes one with Him.

**7** The Lord said: The path to liberation is open to all, regardless of birth, caste, or station. He who turns to Me with a sincere heart, I accept him and grant him liberation.

**8** In this age of Kali, the easiest and most effective means of liberation is the chanting of the divine name. The name of the Lord is more powerful than all sacrifices, all austerities, and all pilgrimages combined.

**9** He who chants My name with love and devotion, whether he be learned or ignorant, rich or poor, high-born or low-born — I carry him across the ocean of worldly existence.

**10** Thus I have told you, O Garuda, the essence of all knowledge. He who hears this with faith, who remembers it, who lives by it — he attains liberation. He is freed from all sins, all fears, and all sorrows. He attains My supreme abode, from which there is no return. Thus ends the Garuda Purana.
