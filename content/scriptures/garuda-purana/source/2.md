---
title: "Garuda Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "garuda-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Lord said: I shall now describe the duties that the living should perform for the benefit of the departed soul.

**2** When a person dies, the body should be bathed, anointed with sacred substances, clothed in clean garments, and placed upon the funeral pyre with the head pointing south.

**3** The eldest son or the nearest male relative should light the funeral pyre with the sacred fire. As the body burns, the sacred mantras should be recited.

**4** For ten days after death, offerings of water and sesame seeds should be made to the departed soul. These offerings nourish the subtle body of the departed as it journeys through the afterlife.

**5** On the eleventh day, the shraddha ceremony should be performed. Food should be offered to the Brahmanas, and gifts of charity should be given in the name of the departed.

**6** The annual shraddha ceremony should be performed on the anniversary of death, during the fortnight of the ancestors in the month of Ashvina, and at other appointed times.

**7** The offerings made at the sacred city of Gaya are especially powerful. He who performs the shraddha at Gaya liberates his ancestors for seven generations past and seven generations to come.

**8** The Lord said: The departed soul receives the merit of the offerings made by the living. Therefore, the living should never neglect the duties owed to the departed.

**9** But the greatest offering one can make for the departed is the chanting of the Lord's name. The divine name penetrates all the worlds and reaches even those souls in the darkest regions.

**10** He who lives a righteous life, who worships the Lord, and who performs his duties faithfully — that person benefits not only himself but all his ancestors and descendants. Such is the power of dharma.
