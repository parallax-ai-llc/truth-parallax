## Qualifications for Overseers and Deacons

The qualifications for overseers and deacons are read by Gnostic interpreters as describing the characteristics of authentic pneumatic leadership rather than mere institutional office. An overseer "above reproach" (3:2) is one who has attained sufficient gnosis to see through the illusions of the material world and is no longer susceptible to the archons' snares of greed, drunkenness, or violence. These are not simply moral failings but points of attachment through which the cosmic rulers maintain their grip on the soul. The requirement to be "hospitable" and "able to teach" (3:2) reflects the pneumatic teacher's essential mission: to provide a safe harbor for awakening souls and to transmit the hidden knowledge that liberates.

The instruction that deacons must "hold the mystery of the faith with a clear conscience" (3:9) explicitly invokes the language of mystery, which Gnostic readers take as confirmation that the early community possessed esoteric teachings not reducible to public proclamation. The "mystery of the faith" is gnosis itself, the secret knowledge of humanity's divine origin and the Demiurge's fraudulent claim to supreme deity. A "clear conscience" means having fully internalized this knowledge so that it is no longer intellectual assent but lived reality, the divine spark burning cleanly without the smoke of material attachment.

The chapter's climactic hymn, "the mystery of godliness" (3:16), is the most explicitly Gnostic passage in the letter. "He was manifested in the flesh" describes the descent of the divine aeon into material form. "Vindicated by the Spirit" marks the pneumatic Christ's triumph over the archons who attempted to destroy him. "Seen by angels" refers to the archontic rulers who witnessed the Savior's passage through their spheres with astonishment. "Proclaimed among the nations" is the spreading of gnosis through the material world. "Believed on in the world" is the awakening of divine sparks. "Taken up in glory" is the return of the Redeemer to the Pleroma, blazing the trail that all pneumatic souls will eventually follow.
