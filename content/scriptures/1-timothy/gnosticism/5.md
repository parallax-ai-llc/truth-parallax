## Widows, Elders, and Slaves

Gnostic interpretation of 1 Timothy 5 centers on esoteric knowledge and the liberation of the divine spark. The key passages — honor widows who are truly widows (5:3), elders who rule well (5:17), laying on of hands (5:22) — have been read through the lens of spiritual awakening, the duality of matter and spirit, and hidden meanings, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Ancient Gnostic teachers and modern scholars of Nag Hammadi texts gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 1 Timothy. The chapter's teaching on widows, elders, and slaves has shaped Gnostic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Gnostic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 1 Timothy 5 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
