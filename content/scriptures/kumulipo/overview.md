## Overview

The Kumulipo is a Hawaiian creation chant of over 2,000 lines, traditionally attributed to the eighteenth century and composed for the high chief Ka-I-i-mamao (later associated with Lono-i-ka-makahiki). Its name, which translates roughly as "Beginning in Deep Darkness" (from kumu, "source/origin," and lipo, "deep darkness"), encapsulates its central narrative arc: the emergence of all life from the primordial darkness (Po) into the light (Ao). The Kumulipo is simultaneously a cosmogonic poem, a comprehensive ecological catalog, and a royal genealogy that traces the descent of the Hawaiian ali'i (chiefs) from the first stirring of life in the cosmic darkness.

The chant is structured in sixteen sections (wā or "eras"), divided into two major parts. The first eight chants describe the era of Po (darkness, night, the unseen realm), during which all forms of plant and animal life come into being in paired sequences — each marine creature matched with a terrestrial counterpart. The final eight chants describe the era of Ao (light, day, the seen realm), which begins with the appearance of the first humans and gods and continues through an extensive genealogical record linking the cosmic origins to the historical ruling chiefs. Queen Lili'uokalani translated the Kumulipo into English in 1897 during her imprisonment after the overthrow of the Hawaiian monarchy.

## Structure

| Chant | Title | Topic |
|---------|-------|-------|
| 1 | The Birth of the Coral Polyp and Sea Creatures | The birth of coral and first sea creatures in the era of Po... |
| 2 | The Birth of Fish and Land Plants | Paired births of fish and plants, establishing land-sea connections... |
| 3 | The Birth of Flying Creatures | The emergence of birds, insects, and aerial creatures... |
| 4 | The Birth of Creeping Things | Crawling creatures and insects filling ecological niches... |
| 5 | The Birth of Pigs and Other Animals | Larger animals including the culturally significant pig... |
| 6 | The Birth of Mice and Other Small Creatures | Small creatures and the diversity of animal creation... |
| 7 | The Birth of the Dog and Night Creatures | Night creatures approaching the boundary of darkness and light... |
| 8 | The Transition from Po to Ao | The pivotal shift from darkness to light, from nature to humanity... |
| 9 | La'ila'i and the First Humans | The first woman La'ila'i and the origins of humanity... |
| 10 | The Genealogy of Wakea and Papa | Sky Father and Earth Mother, the origin of islands and chiefs... |
| 11 | The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 1) | Beginning the record of chiefly descent from divine ancestors... |
| 12 | The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 2) | Continuing genealogies through voyaging and settlement periods... |
| 13 | The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 3) | Expansion and consolidation of chiefly power across islands... |
| 14 | The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 4) | Approaching the historical period in genealogical record... |
| 15 | The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 5) | Recent historical lineages approaching the present generation... |
| 16 | Ka-I-i-mamao and the Living Chiefs | The culmination linking cosmic origins to the living chief... |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Indigenous**: A sacred genealogical chant central to Hawaiian cultural identity and sovereignty claims
- **Academic**: A major text in Pacific studies, oral literature, and ethnobiological classification
- **Comparative**: Parallels with other Polynesian cosmogonies and genealogical traditions worldwide
- **Theological**: Raises questions about the relationship between cosmogony, genealogy, and political theology
- **Anthropological**: Essential for understanding Hawaiian social hierarchy, ecology, and knowledge systems
- **Interfaith**: An Indigenous Pacific creation tradition offering ecological and genealogical perspectives on origins

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Po and Ao | The fundamental duality of darkness/night and light/day as cosmological principle |
| Paired Creation | The systematic pairing of marine and terrestrial life forms reflecting ecological interconnection |
| Genealogical Cosmology | The understanding that all life forms part of a single genealogical continuum |
| Ecological Knowledge | The detailed classification of species reflecting generations of environmental observation |
| Political Theology | The legitimation of chiefly authority through genealogical connection to cosmic origins |
| Mana and Descent | Spiritual power (mana) as inherited through genealogical lines from primordial sources |
