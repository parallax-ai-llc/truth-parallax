## Overview

Anthropological study of the Kumulipo examines its role in structuring Hawaiian social hierarchy, political authority, land tenure, and ceremonial practice. The chant's genealogical framework reflects and legitimates the Hawaiian ali'i (chiefly) system, in which political authority, land rights, and spiritual power are determined by genealogical rank — the closeness of one's descent from the divine and cosmic ancestors.

Key anthropological themes include the relationship between genealogical knowledge and political power, the role of priestly specialists (kahuna) in maintaining and performing genealogical chants, the ecological knowledge embedded in the species classifications, and the chant's function in Hawaiian ceremonies of investiture and political transition.
