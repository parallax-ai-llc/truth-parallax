The fifteenth chant brings the genealogical record into the more recent historical period, approaching the generation of the ali'i for whom the Kumulipo was composed. The density of genealogical information increases as the chant nears its culmination, with each connection carefully documented to establish the unbroken chain of descent from the cosmic origins through the divine ancestors to the present ruling line.

Anthropological study of The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 5) examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
