---
title: "Fifth Era - The Birds"
scripture: "kumulipo"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Born was the bird, the feathered one,
Born was the hawk that circles high,
Born was the owl that hunts by night,
Born was the plover on the shore.

**2** Born was the albatross, the far-flying one,
Born was the frigate bird, born was the tern,
Born was the petrel that skims the wave,
Born was the heron that stands in the marsh.

**3** The forests filled with birdsong,
The cliffs echoed with their cries,
The marshes rang with their calls,
The sky was alive with wings.

**4** Born was the chicken, the domestic fowl,
Born was the crow, the clever one,
Born was the honey-creeper, bright of feather,
Born of the darkness, children of night.

**5** And the birds nested in the trees,
And the birds fished in the streams,
And the birds sang at the edge of dawn,
Though dawn had not yet come.
