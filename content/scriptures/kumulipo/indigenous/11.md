The eleventh chant begins the detailed genealogical record of the ali'i (chiefly) lineages, tracing the descent from the cosmic and divine ancestors through the generations of human rulers. Each name in the genealogy carries significance, often encoding historical events, migrations, and alliances. This section reflects the Hawaiian understanding that political authority derives from genealogical connection to the gods and the creative forces of the cosmos.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 1) carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
