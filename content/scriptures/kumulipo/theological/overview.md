## Overview

Theological engagement with the Kumulipo raises questions about the relationship between cosmogony and political theology, the sacred dimensions of genealogy, and the theological implications of understanding all of creation as a single genealogical continuum. The Kumulipo presents a vision of creation in which the divine, the natural, and the human are not separate categories but continuous aspects of a single genealogical unfolding.

The concept of mana (spiritual power) as something inherited through genealogical descent — from the primordial darkness through the gods to the human chiefs — presents a distinctive theology of divine-human relationship. The chant's comprehensive inclusion of all species in the genealogical framework suggests a theology of kinship with all creation that has resonance with contemporary ecological theology.
