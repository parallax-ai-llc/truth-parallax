The fourth chant catalogs the birth of creeping and crawling creatures — caterpillars, rats, and various insects — paired with marine counterparts. These creatures of the underbrush and darkness reflect the continuing era of Po and the gradual filling of ecological niches. The chant demonstrates the comprehensiveness of the Hawaiian creation vision, which includes all forms of life, not just those considered noble or beautiful.

Interfaith engagement with The Birth of Creeping Things offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
