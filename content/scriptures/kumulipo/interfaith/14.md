The fourteenth chant continues the genealogical sequence, moving closer to the historical period. The names become more recognizable as historical figures, and the genealogical record increasingly intersects with documented Hawaiian history. This chant demonstrates the Kumulipo's function as a living document that connects cosmogony to contemporary political reality.

Interfaith engagement with The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 4) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
