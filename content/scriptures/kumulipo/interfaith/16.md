The sixteenth and final chant brings the genealogical record to its culmination in the person of the ali'i for whom the Kumulipo was composed — traditionally identified as Lono-i-ka-makahiki or Ka-I-i-mamao. This chant completes the cosmic genealogy, demonstrating that the living chief stands at the end of an unbroken chain of descent that begins with the first stirring of life in the primordial darkness. The chief's authority is thus rooted in the very structure of creation itself.

Interfaith engagement with Ka-I-i-mamao and the Living Chiefs offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
