The fifteenth chant brings the genealogical record into the more recent historical period, approaching the generation of the ali'i for whom the Kumulipo was composed. The density of genealogical information increases as the chant nears its culmination, with each connection carefully documented to establish the unbroken chain of descent from the cosmic origins through the divine ancestors to the present ruling line.

Interfaith engagement with The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 5) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
