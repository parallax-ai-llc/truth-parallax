The twelfth chant continues the chiefly genealogies, extending through further generations and incorporating references to historical events, voyaging traditions, and the settlement of the Hawaiian archipelago. The genealogical record serves simultaneously as history, political charter, and spiritual connection, linking the ruling chiefs of each generation to the primordial creative forces described in the opening chants.

Comparative analysis of The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 2) reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions. The motifs and structures found in this section connect to widespread patterns in human religious and mythological expression while retaining features that are specific to this particular cultural and spiritual context.

Cross-cultural comparison illuminates the universal dimensions of the human religious imagination while also highlighting the unique contributions of this tradition to the global tapestry of spiritual thought.
