## Overview

Comparative analysis of the Kumulipo reveals significant connections with other Polynesian cosmogonic traditions — Maori, Samoan, Tongan, and Tahitian creation narratives share structural and thematic elements reflecting their common Austronesian heritage. The progression from darkness to light, the genealogical structure, and the connection between cosmogony and political authority are pan-Polynesian themes.

Beyond the Polynesian context, the Kumulipo invites comparison with other genealogical cosmogonies (such as the biblical Genesis genealogies and Hesiod's Theogony), other creation-through-darkness narratives, and other traditions that use ecological classification as a framework for understanding cosmic origins. The Kumulipo's systematic pairing of species has also drawn comparison with modern scientific taxonomy and ecological thinking.
