The sixth chant continues the cataloging of animal life, focusing on mice and various small creatures. The emphasis on diversity and proliferation reflects the Hawaiian understanding of creation as an expansive, generous process that fills every possible ecological niche. Each creature, no matter how small, has its place in the cosmic genealogy and contributes to the wholeness of creation.

Comparative analysis of The Birth of Mice and Other Small Creatures reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions. The motifs and structures found in this section connect to widespread patterns in human religious and mythological expression while retaining features that are specific to this particular cultural and spiritual context.
