The fourth chant catalogs the birth of creeping and crawling creatures — caterpillars, rats, and various insects — paired with marine counterparts. These creatures of the underbrush and darkness reflect the continuing era of Po and the gradual filling of ecological niches. The chant demonstrates the comprehensiveness of the Hawaiian creation vision, which includes all forms of life, not just those considered noble or beautiful.

Comparative analysis of The Birth of Creeping Things reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions.
