The fourteenth chant continues the genealogical sequence, moving closer to the historical period. The names become more recognizable as historical figures, and the genealogical record increasingly intersects with documented Hawaiian history. This chant demonstrates the Kumulipo's function as a living document that connects cosmogony to contemporary political reality.

Academic analysis of The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 4) draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
