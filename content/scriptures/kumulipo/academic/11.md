The eleventh chant begins the detailed genealogical record of the ali'i (chiefly) lineages, tracing the descent from the cosmic and divine ancestors through the generations of human rulers. Each name in the genealogy carries significance, often encoding historical events, migrations, and alliances. This section reflects the Hawaiian understanding that political authority derives from genealogical connection to the gods and the creative forces of the cosmos.

Academic analysis of The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 1) draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
