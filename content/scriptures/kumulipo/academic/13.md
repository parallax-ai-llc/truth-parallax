The thirteenth chant extends the genealogical record further, documenting the expansion and consolidation of chiefly power across the Hawaiian Islands. The names and relationships encoded in this chant reflect specific historical periods and the complex political dynamics of Hawaiian society. The genealogy serves as a legitimating narrative, demonstrating the unbroken connection between the present rulers and the cosmic origins of creation.

Academic analysis of The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 3) draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
