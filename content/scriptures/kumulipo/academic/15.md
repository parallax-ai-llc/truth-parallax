The fifteenth chant brings the genealogical record into the more recent historical period, approaching the generation of the ali'i for whom the Kumulipo was composed. The density of genealogical information increases as the chant nears its culmination, with each connection carefully documented to establish the unbroken chain of descent from the cosmic origins through the divine ancestors to the present ruling line.

Academic analysis of The Lineage of Chiefs (Part 5) draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.
