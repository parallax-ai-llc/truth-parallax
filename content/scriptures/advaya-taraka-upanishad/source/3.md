---
title: "Advaya Taraka Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "advaya-taraka-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Now is described the Amanaska Yoga — the Yoga without mind. When the mind is dissolved, there remains only the Atman, the pure consciousness, self-luminous and eternal.

**2** As salt dissolved in water becomes one with water, so the mind dissolved in the Atman becomes one with Brahman. This is called the state of Unmani.

**3** As camphor merges in fire without leaving any residue, so the mind merges in the Atman without any trace of duality remaining.

**4** The Yogi who has attained this state perceives no difference between the individual soul and the Supreme Soul. For him there is neither day nor night, neither waking nor sleeping.

**5** He who perceives the non-dual Taraka becomes Brahman itself. He is freed from the bondage of birth and death forever.

**6** This supreme state is beyond speech, beyond mind, beyond all description. It is the state of pure being, pure consciousness, and pure bliss — Sat-Chit-Ananda.

**7** That which is not reached by speech, but by which speech is illumined — know that alone as Brahman, not this which they worship here.

**8** The knower of this Advaya Taraka, the non-dual deliverer, crosses over sorrow. He attains immortality. He becomes Brahman. Thus ends the Advaya Taraka Upanishad.
