---
title: "Advaya Taraka Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "advaya-taraka-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The syllable Om is the Taraka — that which causes one to cross this ocean of worldly existence. The Yogi who contemplates upon this Taraka gains salvation.

**2** That alone is Taraka which is the non-dual Brahman. It is termed Taraka because it enables one to cross the great ocean of births and deaths.

**3** The wise man should always contemplate upon the Taraka Brahman, which is visible between the two eyebrows, which is the bridge of liberation.

**4** Between the eyebrows, above the root of the nose, there shines the great light. That is the great bridge which leads to liberation. One who knows this crosses over the darkness of ignorance.

**5** The Taraka is twofold: it is murti (with form) and amurti (without form). That which ends in dissolution is murti. That which exists beyond dissolution is amurti, the imperishable Taraka.

**6** The internal Taraka is the light perceived within, between the eyebrows, when the eyes are closed in meditation. The external Taraka is the light perceived without, in space, through the practice of Shambhavi Mudra.

**7** The Yogi perceives first the light of the inner sun, then the light of the inner moon, then the light of the inner fire, then the light of lightning, then the light resembling crystal, and finally that which is infinite and all-pervading.

**8** When the Yogi perceives these lights, he is freed from all sins and attains liberation. The wise man who thus meditates attains the supreme state.
