## Overview

Indigenous engagement with the Book of Breathings approaches the text through the lens of indigenous cultural practitioners, spiritual leaders, and community members. This interpretive tradition emphasizes living tradition, cultural identity, spiritual continuity, community practice, and resistance to reductive outside interpretations.

For indigenous communities connected to the Book of Breathings, the text is not merely a historical artifact but a living expression of spiritual knowledge, cultural identity, and communal wisdom. Indigenous readings insist on the text's continuing authority and relevance, resisting its reduction to "mythology" in the dismissive sense and affirming its status as sacred knowledge that guides contemporary life, ceremony, and understanding.

Indigenous engagement with the Book of Breathings also raises critical questions about cultural sovereignty, intellectual property, the ethics of scholarly access, and the right of indigenous communities to control the interpretation and dissemination of their sacred traditions.
