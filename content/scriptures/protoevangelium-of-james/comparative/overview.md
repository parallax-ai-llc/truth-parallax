## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Protoevangelium of James places its miraculous birth narrative alongside similar traditions worldwide — from the births of the Buddha, Krishna, and Zoroaster to the Qur'anic account of Mary (Surah 19). The motif of the barren woman who conceives through divine intervention resonates across cultures, as does the theme of the sacred child whose birth transforms the cosmos.
