---
title: "Protoevangelium of James - Chapter 15"
scripture: "protoevangelium-of-james"
chapter: 15
---

**1** And there was an order from the Emperor Augustus to register how many were in Bethlehem of Judea. And Joseph said: I shall register my sons; but this child -- what shall I do with her? How shall I register her? As my wife? I am ashamed. As my daughter then? But all the sons of Israel know that she is not my daughter.

**2** This day of the Lord shall do as the Lord wills.

**3** And he saddled the ass, and set her upon it, and his son led it, and Joseph followed after. And they drew near to three miles, and Joseph turned and saw her sorrowful, and said within himself: Perhaps that which is within her distresses her.

**4** And again Joseph turned and saw her laughing, and said to her: Mary, what is it with thee that I see thy face at one time laughing, at another sorrowful?

**5** And Mary said unto Joseph: It is because I see with my eyes two peoples, one weeping and lamenting, and one rejoicing and exulting.

**6** And they came to the middle of the journey, and Mary said to him: Take me down from the ass, for that which is within me presses to come forth.

**7** And he took her down from the ass, and said to her: Where shall I take thee, and shelter thy shame? For the place is desert.
