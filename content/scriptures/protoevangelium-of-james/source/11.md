---
title: "Protoevangelium of James - Chapter 11"
scripture: "protoevangelium-of-james"
chapter: 11
---

**1** And she was in her sixth month, and behold, Joseph came from his building, and entering into his house, found her great with child.

**2** And he smote his face, and cast himself down upon the ground on sackcloth, and wept bitterly, saying: With what countenance shall I look unto the Lord my God? And what prayer shall I make concerning this maiden?

**3** For I received her a virgin out of the temple of the Lord my God, and have not kept her safely. Who is it that has hunted me? Who has done this evil in my house, and has defiled the virgin?

**4** Is not the story of Adam repeated in me? For as at the hour of his giving thanks the serpent came and found Eve alone and deceived her, so hath it happened unto me also.

**5** And Joseph arose from the sackcloth, and called Mary, and said unto her: O thou who hast been cared for by God, why hast thou done this? Thou hast forgotten the Lord thy God. Why hast thou humbled thy soul, thou that wast nourished up in the Holy of Holies, and didst receive food from the hand of an angel?

**6** And she wept bitterly, saying: I am pure, and I know not a man. And Joseph said to her: Whence then is that which is in thy womb?

**7** And she said: As the Lord my God liveth, I know not whence it is to me.
