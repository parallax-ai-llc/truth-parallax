---
title: "Protoevangelium of James - Chapter 24"
scripture: "protoevangelium-of-james"
chapter: 24
---

**1** Now I, James, who have written this history, at the time when a tumult arose in Jerusalem on the death of Herod, withdrew to the desert place until the tumult in Jerusalem ceased.

**2** And I glorified the Master, God, who gave me the wisdom to write this account.

**3** And the record of these things was preserved among the faithful in Jerusalem, and copies were kept by those who had been eyewitnesses of these events.

**4** And they were handed down through the generations, that the truth might be known concerning the birth of the Saviour and the events surrounding his coming into the world.

**5** For these things were not done in secret, but many bore witness to them in the holy city.

**6** And the testimony of the midwife and of Salome was recorded for the ages, that all might know the manner of his birth.

**7** And blessed are they who read these words and keep them in their hearts, for they shall know the truth of the things which were spoken by the prophets of old.
