## Overview

The Catholic reading of the Protoevangelium of James recognizes the text as the source of many cherished Marian traditions. The names of Mary's parents (Joachim and Anna, honored as saints), the Feast of the Presentation of Mary, and the tradition of Mary's perpetual virginity all trace to this text. While not canonical, the Protoevangelium is valued as a witness to the early Church's devotion to Mary that found dogmatic expression in Catholic teaching.
