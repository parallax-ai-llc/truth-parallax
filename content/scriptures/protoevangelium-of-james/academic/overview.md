## Overview

The Academic study of the Protoevangelium of James examines the text's mid-second-century composition, its literary relationship to the canonical infancy narratives (Matthew 1-2, Luke 1-2), its manuscript tradition (over 150 Greek manuscripts), and its enormous influence on the development of Mariology. Scholars analyze the text's theological agenda — particularly its emphasis on Mary's purity — and its evidence for early Christian attitudes toward virginity, gender, and sanctity.
