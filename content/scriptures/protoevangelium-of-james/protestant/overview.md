## Overview

The Protestant engagement with the Protoevangelium of James is shaped by the Reformation's rejection of much Marian devotion. The text is studied critically as the source of traditions — Mary's perpetual virginity, her Temple presentation, the names Joachim and Anna — that Protestants regard as pious legends rather than historical facts. The work illustrates the development of extra-biblical tradition that Protestants consider a departure from scriptural sufficiency.
