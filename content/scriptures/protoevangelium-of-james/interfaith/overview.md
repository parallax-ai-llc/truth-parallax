## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Protoevangelium of James draws on the shared reverence for Mary across Christianity and Islam. The Qur'an's account of Mary's childhood in the Temple (Surah 3:35-37) closely parallels the Protoevangelium's narrative, suggesting either common tradition or literary dependence. The text facilitates Christian-Muslim dialogue on Mary's sanctity and provides a basis for exploring the universal theme of sacred motherhood across religious traditions.
