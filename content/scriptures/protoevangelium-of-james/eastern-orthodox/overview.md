## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox encounter with the Protoevangelium of James is particularly intimate, as the text has profoundly shaped Orthodox liturgy, iconography, and theology. The feasts of the Nativity of the Theotokos, her Presentation in the Temple, and the Conception by Anna all derive from this text. Orthodox icons of the Nativity, including the cave, the midwife, and the doubting Salome, follow the Protoevangelium's narrative rather than the canonical accounts.
