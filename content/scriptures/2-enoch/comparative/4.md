## Comparative Reading

The third heaven as the location of paradise in 2 Enoch 4 brings together two of the most globally attested religious motifs: the celestial garden and the tree of life. The relocation of Eden from earth to the third heaven represents a development that Paul references in 2 Corinthians 12:2-4, and that becomes standard in later rabbinic cosmology. Structurally, this heavenly garden corresponds to what Mircea Eliade terms the "paradisiac archetype," a primordial sacred space characterized by abundance, harmony, and divine proximity that appears across virtually every religious tradition.

The tree of life at the center of this paradise finds direct parallels in the Mesopotamian *kiskanu* tree described in Sumerian texts, the Norse Yggdrasil connecting nine worlds, the Buddhist *Bodhi tree* under which Siddhartha achieved enlightenment, and the Quranic *Sidrat al-Muntaha* (Lote Tree of the Uttermost Boundary) encountered by Muhammad during the *Mi'raj*. Joseph Campbell treated these as manifestations of the "World Tree" archetype in *The Mythic Image*, arguing that the vertical tree connecting cosmic realms expresses the universal human intuition of an *axis mundi*. What distinguishes 2 Enoch's version is the explicit identification of the tree with the place prepared for the righteous, linking cosmology to eschatological ethics.

The rivers flowing from paradise in 2 Enoch echo the four rivers of Genesis 2:10-14, but the pattern of paradisiac rivers appears independently in the Iranian *Bundahishn* (the river Ardvi Sura flowing from Mount Hara), in Hindu cosmology (the Ganges descending from heaven through Shiva's hair), and in Aztec mythology (the paradisiac Tlalocan with its abundant waters). Wendy Doniger's comparative method highlights that while the structural motif of the irrigated garden-paradise is remarkably stable across cultures, the *function* of these rivers shifts significantly: in 2 Enoch they carry fragrance and mark eschatological reward, while in Mesopotamian parallels they primarily sustain cosmic fertility.
