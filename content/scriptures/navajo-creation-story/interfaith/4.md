The Fourth World, the White World or Ni'hodisxos, is where First Man (Altse Hastiin) and First Woman (Altse Asdzaa) emerged as central figures. This world saw the development of more complex social and spiritual structures, but also faced a great flood that forced the beings to seek passage to the next world. The reed through which they climbed upward became a central symbol of emergence and transformation.

Interfaith engagement with The White World (Ni'hodisxos) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
