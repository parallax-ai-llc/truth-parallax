The First World, known as the Black World or Ni'hodilqil, represents the initial stage of creation in the Diné Bahaneʼ. In this primordial realm of darkness, the first beings — insect people and air-spirit beings — came into existence. The Black World was a place of conflict and spiritual immaturity, where the inhabitants struggled with basic moral and social challenges.

Interfaith engagement with The Black World (Ni'hodilqil) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
