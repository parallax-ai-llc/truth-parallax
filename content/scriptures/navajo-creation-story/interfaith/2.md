The Second World, the Blue World or Ni'hodootl'izh, represents the next stage of emergence. After being expelled from the Black World due to conflict and moral failings, the beings entered a realm populated by blue-feathered beings including swallow people. This world introduced new forms of social interaction and conflict, as the newcomers had to negotiate their place among existing inhabitants.

Interfaith engagement with The Blue World (Ni'hodootl'izh) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
