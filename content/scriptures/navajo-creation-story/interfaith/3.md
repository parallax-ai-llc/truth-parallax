The Third World, the Yellow World or Ni'haltsidoh, marks a significant development in the emergence narrative. This world contained rivers, mountains, and more complex landscapes. The beings encountered grasshopper people and other creatures. It was in this world that many important social structures began to take shape, and the beings started developing more sophisticated relationships with the natural world.

Interfaith engagement with The Yellow World (Ni'haltsidoh) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
