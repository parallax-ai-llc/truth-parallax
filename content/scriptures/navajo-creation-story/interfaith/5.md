The Fifth World, the Glittering World or Ni'hodiszin, is the present world where the Diné people live. This is the world of Changing Woman (Asdzaa Nadleehe), the most revered figure in Navajo tradition, who gave birth to the Hero Twins and established many of the ceremonies and practices that define Diné life. The four sacred mountains were placed at the cardinal directions to define the Diné homeland (Dinétah).

Interfaith engagement with The Glittering World (Ni'hodiszin) offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
