The Fourth World, the White World or Ni'hodisxos, is where First Man (Altse Hastiin) and First Woman (Altse Asdzaa) emerged as central figures. This world saw the development of more complex social and spiritual structures, but also faced a great flood that forced the beings to seek passage to the next world. The reed through which they climbed upward became a central symbol of emergence and transformation.

Comparative analysis of The White World (Ni'hodisxos) reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions. The motifs and structures found in this section connect to widespread patterns in human religious and mythological expression while retaining features that are specific to this particular cultural and spiritual context.

Cross-cultural comparison illuminates the universal dimensions of the human religious imagination while also highlighting the unique contributions of this tradition to the global tapestry of spiritual thought.
