The First World, known as the Black World or Ni'hodilqil, represents the initial stage of creation in the Diné Bahaneʼ. In this primordial realm of darkness, the first beings — insect people and air-spirit beings — came into existence. The Black World was a place of conflict and spiritual immaturity, where the inhabitants struggled with basic moral and social challenges.

Comparative analysis of The Black World (Ni'hodilqil) reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions. The motifs and structures found in this section connect to widespread patterns in human religious and mythological expression while retaining features that are specific to this particular cultural and spiritual context.
