The Third World, the Yellow World or Ni'haltsidoh, marks a significant development in the emergence narrative. This world contained rivers, mountains, and more complex landscapes. The beings encountered grasshopper people and other creatures. It was in this world that many important social structures began to take shape, and the beings started developing more sophisticated relationships with the natural world.

Comparative analysis of The Yellow World (Ni'haltsidoh) reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions. The motifs and structures found in this section connect to widespread patterns in human religious and mythological expression while retaining features that are specific to this particular cultural and spiritual context.

Cross-cultural comparison illuminates the universal dimensions of the human religious imagination while also highlighting the unique contributions of this tradition to the global tapestry of spiritual thought.
