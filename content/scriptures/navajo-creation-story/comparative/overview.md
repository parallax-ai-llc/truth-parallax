## Overview

Comparative analysis of the Navajo Creation Story reveals both striking parallels with and significant differences from creation narratives found in other traditions worldwide. The emergence motif — the progressive journey upward through multiple worlds — connects the Diné narrative to other Southwestern Pueblo emergence traditions (Hopi, Zuni, Acoma) and, more broadly, to multi-world cosmologies found in Mesoamerican, Hindu, and Buddhist traditions.

The figure of Changing Woman finds parallels in earth goddess traditions worldwide, while the Hero Twins' monster-slaying exploits connect to hero-twin narratives found across the Americas and beyond. However, comparative study must also attend to what is distinctive about the Navajo narrative: its emphasis on hózhó (beauty/harmony), its integration of narrative and ceremony, and its specific relationship to the Diné homeland and sacred geography.
