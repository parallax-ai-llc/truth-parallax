---
title: "Navajo Creation Story - Chapter 3"
scripture: "navajo-creation-story"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Fourth World was called the White World, or the Glittering World. When the people emerged, they found it dark and damp, for there was no sun yet.

**2** The people gathered together and held a great council. They said: "We must make this world right. We must create order from chaos."

**3** First Man and First Woman took the sacred medicine bundle and from it they brought forth the materials for the sun and the moon.

**4** They fashioned the sun from a great disc of turquoise, bordered with red rain, lightning, and serpents. They set it in the sky to the east, and it rose, giving light and warmth to the world.

**5** They fashioned the moon from a disc of white shell, and they set it in the sky to follow the sun. And the people rejoiced, for there was light.

**6** Then they placed the stars in the sky. First Woman began to lay them out carefully in patterns, each constellation with its proper meaning and its proper place.

**7** But Coyote grew impatient. He seized the blanket on which the remaining stars were laid and flung them into the sky. And that is why some stars are in beautiful patterns, but most are scattered at random across the heavens.

**8** First Man and First Woman then created the four sacred mountains that mark the boundaries of the Navajo homeland.

**9** To the east they placed the mountain of white shell — Sisnaajiní, Dawn Mountain. To the south they placed the mountain of turquoise — Tsoodził, Blue Bead Mountain. To the west they placed the mountain of abalone — Dook'o'oosłííd, Abalone Shell Mountain. To the north they placed the mountain of jet — Dibé Nitsaa, Big Sheep Mountain.

**10** Upon each mountain they placed a guardian spirit, and they fastened each mountain to the earth with a bolt of lightning, a ray of sunlight, a rainbow, and a sheet of rain.

**11** Then they created the plants: the corn, the beans, the squash, and the tobacco — the four sacred plants. And they created the animals and assigned each its place.

**12** And First Man said: "Now this world is prepared. But the people must learn to live in harmony, or the troubles of the lower worlds will follow them here."
