---
title: "Navajo Creation Story - Chapter 5"
scripture: "navajo-creation-story"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Armed with the lightning weapons of their father the Sun, the Hero Twins returned to the earth to slay the monsters.

**2** First they sought Ye'iitsoh, the Big Giant, the greatest of all the monsters. They found him at a lake, drinking the waters. He was enormous, and his armor was of flint.

**3** Monster Slayer hurled the lightning bolt, and it struck the giant. The earth shook and the sky thundered. The giant fell, and his blood flowed in a great stream that hardened into the black lava fields that can still be seen today.

**4** Then the twins went forth and slew the other monsters, one by one. They destroyed the Horned Monsters, the Bird Monsters, the Tracking Bear, and the Eyes That Kill.

**5** But four beings came before the twins and pleaded for their lives: Old Age, Cold, Poverty, and Hunger.

**6** Old Age said: "If you slay me, people will never die, and the earth will become too crowded." And the twins spared Old Age.

**7** Cold said: "If you slay me, the earth will become too hot, and the rivers will dry up, and the crops will wither." And the twins spared Cold.

**8** Poverty said: "If you slay me, people will have no reason to work, and they will become lazy and idle." And the twins spared Poverty.

**9** Hunger said: "If you slay me, people will have no desire for food, and they will not plant or hunt, and life will lose its savor." And the twins spared Hunger.

**10** So it is that Old Age, Cold, Poverty, and Hunger remain in the world, for they teach the people the value of life.

**11** After the monsters were slain, Changing Woman went to the western ocean and built her home there. She created the four original clans of the Navajo from the skin of her own body: the Towering House Clan, the Bitter Water Clan, the Big Water Clan, and the One-Who-Walks-Around Clan.

**12** And she taught the people the ceremonies and the songs, the prayers and the sandpaintings, by which they might live in harmony — in hozho — walking in beauty.

**13** "In beauty I walk. With beauty before me, I walk. With beauty behind me, I walk. With beauty above me, I walk. With beauty below me, I walk. With beauty all around me, I walk. It is finished in beauty. It is finished in beauty."

**14** Thus was the world made ready for the Navajo people, the Diné, who walk between the four sacred mountains in the Glittering World, following the path of beauty that Changing Woman taught them.
