---
title: "Navajo Creation Story - Chapter 2"
scripture: "navajo-creation-story"
chapter: 2
---

**1** In the Yellow World, the Insect People met the Grasshopper People, the Snake People, and the Lizard People. They also found there the great rivers and the sacred mountains.

**2** The people discovered Coyote, the trickster, who would become both helper and troublemaker throughout the ages.

**3** The people began to plant crops and build homes. They discovered fire and learned to cook their food. They made tools and weapons.

**4** But once again, the people quarreled. They committed adultery and spoke lies. The men and women argued with one another over who was more important.

**5** The men said: "We are more important, for we hunt and fight and provide." The women said: "We are more important, for we plant and weave and bear children."

**6** And so the men and women separated. The men went to one side of the river and the women went to the other. For four years they lived apart.

**7** During the separation, both men and women suffered greatly. The men could not cook or make clothing. The women could not hunt or make tools. Both sides grew thin and weak.

**8** At last they realized that they needed each other, and they came together again. But from their separation came strange and monstrous beings — the monsters that would later plague the people.

**9** Then Coyote did a terrible thing. He stole the Water Monster's children from the great river. And the Water Monster became enraged.

**10** The waters began to rise. From all four directions the flood came — white water from the east, blue water from the south, yellow water from the west, and black water from the north.

**11** The people fled to the tops of the mountains, but the water continued to rise. They planted a great reed, and it grew tall, piercing the sky of the Yellow World.

**12** The people climbed inside the reed, one by one, taking with them the seeds of all the plants and the young of all the animals. And through the reed they ascended to the Fourth World.
