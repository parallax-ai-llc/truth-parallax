## Overview

Theological engagement with the Navajo Creation Story raises profound questions about the nature of creation, the relationship between the sacred and the natural world, and the role of narrative in mediating divine reality. The emergence model of creation — a progressive journey through worlds rather than a single act of divine fiat — offers a distinctive theology of creation as process, development, and moral maturation.

The figure of Changing Woman presents a theology of the divine feminine that is central rather than peripheral, embodying principles of renewal, fertility, and the cyclical nature of life. The concept of hózhó (beauty, balance, harmony) functions as both an aesthetic and a theological principle, suggesting that the proper state of existence is one of integrated beauty and that illness, conflict, and disorder represent deviations from this fundamental harmony that can be restored through ceremony.
