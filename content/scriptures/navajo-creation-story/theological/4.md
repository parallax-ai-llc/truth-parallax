The Fourth World, the White World or Ni'hodisxos, is where First Man (Altse Hastiin) and First Woman (Altse Asdzaa) emerged as central figures. This world saw the development of more complex social and spiritual structures, but also faced a great flood that forced the beings to seek passage to the next world. The reed through which they climbed upward became a central symbol of emergence and transformation.

Theological engagement with The White World (Ni'hodisxos) raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order. This section's themes invite reflection on fundamental theological questions that transcend any single tradition while being rooted in the specific spiritual vision of this particular culture.
