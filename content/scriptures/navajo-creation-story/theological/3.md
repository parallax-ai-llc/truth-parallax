The Third World, the Yellow World or Ni'haltsidoh, marks a significant development in the emergence narrative. This world contained rivers, mountains, and more complex landscapes. The beings encountered grasshopper people and other creatures. It was in this world that many important social structures began to take shape, and the beings started developing more sophisticated relationships with the natural world.

Theological engagement with The Yellow World (Ni'haltsidoh) raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order.
