The Fifth World, the Glittering World or Ni'hodiszin, is the present world where the Diné people live. This is the world of Changing Woman (Asdzaa Nadleehe), the most revered figure in Navajo tradition, who gave birth to the Hero Twins and established many of the ceremonies and practices that define Diné life. The four sacred mountains were placed at the cardinal directions to define the Diné homeland (Dinétah).

Anthropological study of The Glittering World (Ni'hodiszin) examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
