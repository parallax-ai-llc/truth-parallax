## Overview

Anthropological study of the Navajo Creation Story situates the narrative within the broader context of Diné social organization, kinship, ceremonial practice, and relationship with the environment. The Bahaneʼ serves as a charter for Navajo society, providing the origin and rationale for clan organization, gender roles, ceremonial practice, and the relationship between the Diné and their homeland.

Key anthropological themes include the relationship between the creation narrative and the Navajo clan system (with different clans tracing their origins to different moments in the emergence narrative), the integration of the creation story with the elaborate system of healing ceremonies (the chantway system), and the role of the narrative in defining Diné identity and territorial claims. The creation story also encodes extensive ecological knowledge about the relationship between the Diné and their environment.
