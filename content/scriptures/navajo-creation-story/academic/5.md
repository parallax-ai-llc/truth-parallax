The Fifth World, the Glittering World or Ni'hodiszin, is the present world where the Diné people live. This is the world of Changing Woman (Asdzaa Nadleehe), the most revered figure in Navajo tradition, who gave birth to the Hero Twins and established many of the ceremonies and practices that define Diné life. The four sacred mountains were placed at the cardinal directions to define the Diné homeland (Dinétah).

Academic analysis of The Glittering World (Ni'hodiszin) draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
