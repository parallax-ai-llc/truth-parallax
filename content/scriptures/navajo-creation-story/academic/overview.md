## Overview

Academic study of the Navajo Creation Story spans multiple disciplines including anthropology, religious studies, linguistics, oral literature, and Indigenous studies. Scholarly engagement has progressed from early ethnographic documentation (Washington Matthews' foundational work in the 1880s-1890s) through structural and comparative analyses to contemporary approaches that emphasize Diné perspectives, intellectual sovereignty, and the limitations of Western analytical frameworks when applied to Indigenous knowledge systems.

Key areas of academic inquiry include the relationship between different versions of the Bahaneʼ as recorded by different narrators and from different clan perspectives, the literary and poetic structure of the oral narratives, the cosmological and philosophical concepts embedded in the creation story (particularly hózhó, the concept of beauty and harmony), and the relationship between narrative, ceremony, and healing in Navajo tradition.
