Chapter 6, the culmination of the Svetasvatara, declares God to be the cause of all causes, the supreme being who is known through devotion and grace. The teaching that "by knowing God one is released from all bonds" (6.13) is the essence of Vaishnava soteriology. The chapter's enumeration of divine attributes — omniscience, omnipotence, self-sufficiency, independence — describes Vishnu-Narayana as the supreme personal God whose grace alone can liberate the soul.

The climactic verse on grace — "Only by the grace (*prasada*) of God does one attain God" (6.21) — is one of the most important verses in Vaishnava theology. It establishes that liberation is not achievable through human effort alone but requires the Lord's favor, freely given to the devoted soul. This teaching became the foundation of the *prapatti* (surrender) doctrine in Shri Vaishnavism and the concept of divine grace in all Vaishnava traditions. The chapter ends with a vision of God as "the bridge to immortality" (6.20) — Vishnu as the means and the destination of the spiritual journey.
