Chapter 4 introduces the concept of *maya* — God's creative power — and the parable of the two birds on one tree. The teaching that *prakriti* (nature) is *maya* and the great Lord (*mahesvara*) is the wielder of *maya* (4.10) is read by Vaishnavas as describing Vishnu's relationship to His material energy. Maya is not illusion but Vishnu's real creative power — the energy through which He projects, sustains, and withdraws the cosmos while remaining untouched by its processes.

The two-birds parable (4.6-7) — two birds on one tree, one eating the fruit, the other watching — is central to Vaishnava theology. The eating bird is the individual soul (*jivatman*), experiencing the fruits of karma. The watching bird is Vishnu as the inner controller (*antaryamin*), witnessing but not bound by the soul's karmic experiences. When the eating bird "sees the Lord and His glory" and recognizes its eternal relationship with the watching bird, it is freed from sorrow — this is the Vaishnava understanding of liberation through recognition of one's dependence on God.
