Chapter 5 explores the relationship between the individual self (*kshetrajna*, the knower of the field) and the supreme self, using Samkhya-like terminology while maintaining a theistic framework. The teaching that the individual self is "of the size of a thumb" and dwells within the heart (5.8-9) is read by Vaishnavas as describing the *jivatman* (individual soul) in its subtle form, residing in the heart alongside Vishnu as the inner controller. The two are intimately related but never identical.

The chapter's teaching on the three *gunas* (qualities of nature — *sattva*, *rajas*, *tamas*) and their binding effect on the soul (5.7) is read through the Vaishnava lens as describing the soul's entanglement in Vishnu's material energy (*prakriti*). Liberation comes not through the soul's own effort to transcend the *gunas* but through Vishnu's grace, which frees the devoted soul from material bondage. The chapter's emphasis on the soul's smallness relative to God reinforces the Vaishnava understanding of the soul as eternally subordinate to the Lord.
