Chapter 2 teaches the practice of yoga as the means to the vision of God. The detailed instructions — controlling the breath, withdrawing the senses, concentrating the mind — are read by Vaishnavas as the preparatory disciplines that make the heart fit to receive Vishnu's self-revelation. The teaching that God is seen "as oil in sesame seeds, as butter in cream, as water in riverbeds" (2.15) reveals that Vishnu is always present but requires the refined perception of the disciplined practitioner to be recognized.

The vision of God described in 2.14-17 — seeing the Lord as a brilliant light, as the source of all illumination — is identified with the *darshana* (divine vision) that is the goal of Vaishnava devotion. The chapter emphasizes that this vision comes not from self-effort alone but from God's willingness to be seen. The practitioner prepares through yoga, but the actual vision is a gift of divine grace — a theme that will be developed fully in Chapter 6.
