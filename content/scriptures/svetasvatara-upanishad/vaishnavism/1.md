Chapter 1 opens with the fundamental philosophical question: What is the cause of existence? Is it time, nature, necessity, chance, the elements, or a person (*purusha*)? The sages gathered around Svetasvatara consider and reject each candidate, arriving at the conclusion that the cause must be a supreme being who rules over all these principles. Vaishnavas identify this supreme being with Vishnu-Narayana, the personal God who transcends and governs all cosmic principles.

The chapter's teaching that God is "hidden in all beings" and is "the one who rules over all sources of creation" (1.3) directly supports the Vaishnava doctrine of Vishnu as the inner controller (*antaryamin*). The rejection of impersonal principles — time, nature, necessity — as ultimate explanations demonstrates that only a personal, conscious God can account for the ordered purposefulness of creation. The instruction to discover this God through meditation (*dhyana*) and yoga (1.3) establishes the devotional path as the means to knowledge of the Lord.
