Chapter 6's climactic declaration of God's supremacy is the cornerstone of Dvaita theology. The teaching that God is "the cause of all causes" (6.9), possessing no superior and no equal, establishes the absolute hierarchy with Vishnu at the summit. The detailed enumeration of divine attributes — He alone is self-existent, all-knowing, all-powerful, the source of time and nature and souls — describes the infinite God of Dvaita philosophy, who possesses every perfection in unlimited degree.

The verse on grace (6.21) is Madhva's strongest proof-text against the Advaita position: liberation requires God's *prasada* (grace), which is the personal, willful act of a conscious being toward His devotee. Grace is not the impersonal self-revelation of an attributeless Absolute but the deliberate choice of a loving God to free His beloved servant from bondage. The chapter's final teaching that God is known "through devotion" (*bhakti*, 6.23) — and specifically through devotion to God *and* to one's guru — establishes the Dvaita path of loving devotion as the supreme means to liberation.
