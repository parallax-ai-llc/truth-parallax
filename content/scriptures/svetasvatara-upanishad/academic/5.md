Chapter 5 represents the Svetasvatara's most sustained engagement with Samkhya terminology and concepts. The discussion of *purusha* (self), *prakriti* (nature), and the three *gunas* closely parallels proto-Samkhya formulations, yet the chapter's consistent subordination of these principles to a supreme Lord distinguishes it from the classical Samkhya system, which is non-theistic. Scholars have used this chapter as evidence for the existence of an early theistic Samkhya (*Sesvara Samkhya*) that preceded the atheistic formulation of Ishvarakrishna's *Samkhya Karika*.

The description of the individual self as "of the size of a thumb" (*angushtha-matra*) appears also in the Katha Upanishad (2.1.12) and became a standard motif in later Hindu literature. Whether this reflects an early physiological understanding (the heart as the seat of consciousness, approximately thumb-sized) or a symbolic teaching about the self's subtlety is debated. The chapter's synthesis of Samkhya analysis with Upanishadic theology represents a distinctive philosophical achievement.
