The Svetasvatara's yoga instructions — sit in a quiet place, control the breath, withdraw the senses, focus the mind — describe a universal contemplative technology found across traditions. The Christian hesychast tradition (Mount Athos), the Sufi practice of *muraqaba* (meditation), Jewish *hitbodedut* (contemplative solitude), and Buddhist *samatha* (calm abiding) all prescribe remarkably similar physical and mental disciplines for achieving inner stillness and spiritual vision.

The meditative visions described (smoke, fire, lightning, crystal) parallel the *nimitta* (meditation signs) described in the Theravada Buddhist *Visuddhimagga*, the "interior castle" visions of Teresa of Avila, and the "lights" experienced in Sufi meditation. The convergence across traditions that had no historical contact suggests that these phenomena arise from the structure of human consciousness itself when it is systematically stilled and focused — a hypothesis that modern contemplative science is beginning to investigate.
