Chapter 6 is the theological culmination of the Svetasvatara and the most important chapter for Shaiva theology. The declaration that God is "the cause of all causes" (*karanam karanajnapah*, 6.9), possessing no master and no creator above Himself, establishes Shiva's absolute sovereignty. The chapter's systematic enumeration of divine attributes — self-existent, self-knowing, beyond time, beyond the qualities of nature — presents Shiva not as one deity among many but as the ultimate reality itself.

The teaching on grace (*prasada*, 6.21) is foundational for all Shaiva traditions. Liberation comes not through the soul's independent effort but through Shiva's self-revelation to the devotee. In Shaiva Siddhanta, this is the descent of *shaktipata* — Shiva's grace-bestowing power. In Kashmir Shaivism, it is the removal of *anava mala* — the fundamental impurity that conceals the soul's true nature as Shiva. The chapter's description of God as "the bridge to immortality" (6.20) identifies Shiva as both the goal and the path — consciousness itself is the bridge from ignorance to recognition.
