Chapter 3 is the most important chapter for Shaiva theology. The declaration "There is one Rudra only" (*eko hi rudro*, 3.2) establishes Shiva's unique supremacy with Vedic authority. The chapter's detailed description of Rudra — who creates all beings, maintains them, and withdraws them at the end of time — provides the scriptural foundation for Shaiva Siddhanta, Kashmir Shaivism, and all major Shaiva traditions.

The description of Rudra as having "faces, heads, and necks in all directions" (3.3) and as "the great seer, self-luminous" (3.3-4) presents Shiva as both the transcendent Lord and the immanent consciousness pervading all creation. The chapter's identification of Rudra with the cosmic *purusha* of the Purusha Sukta (3.14-16) claims the authority of the oldest Vedic hymns for Shaiva theology. The teaching that Rudra is "the inner self of all beings" (3.11) establishes the doctrine that Shiva is not only the cosmic creator but the innermost consciousness of every living being.
