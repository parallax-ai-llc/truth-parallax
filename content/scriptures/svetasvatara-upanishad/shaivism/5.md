Chapter 5's exploration of the individual and supreme selves resonates with the Shaiva distinction between *pashu* (the bound soul) and *pati* (the Lord). The individual self, described as "of the size of a thumb" within the heart, is Shiva-consciousness in its most contracted form — limited by the three *malas* (impurities of individuality, karma, and illusion). The supreme self is the same consciousness in its unlimited, universal mode.

The chapter's Samkhya-like language of *gunas* and *prakriti* is reinterpreted through the Shaiva framework: the three *gunas* are three modes of Shiva's creative power (*shakti*), and the soul's bondage by the *gunas* is the result of *maya mala* — the impurity of mistaking Shiva's creative play for an independent reality. Liberation is the recognition that what appeared as binding material nature is actually the dynamic self-expression of consciousness. The bound soul (*pashu*) discovers itself as the Lord (*pati*) through Shiva's grace.
