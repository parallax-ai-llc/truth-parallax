---
title: "Svetasvatara Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "svetasvatara-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** He who is one without any colour, by the manifold application of his power distributes many colours in his hidden purpose, and into whom in the beginning and at the end the universe is gathered, He is the God — may He endow us with good thoughts.

**2** That itself is the fire, that is the sun, that is the air, that is the moon; that itself is the starry firmament, that is Brahman, that is the waters, that is Prajapati.

**3** Thou art woman, thou art man; thou art youth and maiden too. Thou as an old man totterest along on thy staff; being born thou becomest facing in every direction.

**4** Thou art the dark-blue bird and the green parrot with red eyes, thou hast the lightning as thy child. Thou art the seasons and the seas. Having no beginning, thou dost abide with all-pervadingness, from whom all worlds are born.

**5** There is one unborn being — red, white, and black — who produces many creatures like herself. There is one unborn being who loves her and lies by her side; there is another who leaves her, while she is eating what has to be eaten.

**6** Two birds, inseparable friends, cling to the same tree. One of them eats the sweet fruit; the other looks on without eating.

**7** On the same tree man sits grieving, immersed, bewildered by his own impotence. But when he sees the other lord contented, and knows his glory, then his grief passes away.

**8** When the seer sees the brilliant maker and lord as the Person who has his source in Brahman, then he is wise; and shaking off good and evil, he reaches the highest oneness, free from stain.

**9** For he is the Breath shining forth in all beings, and he who understands this truly is the knower, there is no talker of too much. He revels in the Self, he delights in the Self, and having performed works he is the greatest of the knowers of Brahman.

**10** The Self, which is to be realized by the truth of penance, of continence, of knowledge, and of constant discipline — this Self within the body, full of light, pure — the ascetics behold when their faults have been removed.

**11** Truth alone conquers, not falsehood. By truth the path is laid out, the Way of the Gods, on which the sages who have their desires fulfilled travel to where is that supreme abode of truth.

**12** That vast, divine, unthinkable form — it shines forth subtler than the subtlest. It is further than the far, yet here it is near at hand, set down in the secret place of the heart, even here among those who behold it.

**13** He is not to be grasped by the eye, nor by speech, nor by the other senses, nor by penance, nor by good works. A man becomes pure through serenity of intellect; thereupon, in meditation, he beholds Him who is without parts.

**14** That subtle Self is to be known by thought in which the senses in five different forms have centered. The whole of men's thought is pervaded by the senses. When it is purified, the Self shines forth.

**15** Whatever form he wishes, that form the man of purified nature obtains by thought, and whatever objects he desires, he obtains those objects and that form. Therefore let him who desires prosperity worship the knower of the Self.

**16** He knows that supreme home of Brahman, in which all is contained and shines brightly. The wise who, without desiring happiness, worship that Person, transcend this seed of birth.

**17** He who with the truth of the Self, united in meditation, realizes the truth of Brahman as omniscient — there is no mystery for him to penetrate. This is the teaching.

**18** He who in the beginning creates Brahma and delivers to him the Vedas — that God who is the light of his own intellect — to Him, desiring liberation, I go for refuge.

**19** To Him who is without parts, without activity, tranquil, irreproachable, without blemish, the highest bridge to immortality — like a fire that has consumed its fuel — to Him I go for refuge.

**20** Only when men shall roll up the sky like a hide, will there be an end of misery, unless God has first been known.

**21** Through the power of his penance and through the grace of God, the wise Svetasvatara has truly proclaimed Brahman to the best of ascetics, as approved by the company of seers.
