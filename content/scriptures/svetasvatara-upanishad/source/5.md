---
title: "Svetasvatara Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "svetasvatara-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** In the imperishable, infinite, highest Brahman, wherein the two — knowledge and ignorance — are hidden, ignorance is perishable but knowledge is immortal. And He who rules both knowledge and ignorance is another — He is Brahman.

**2** It is He who, being one, rules over every source, over all forms and over all sources. It is He who, in the beginning, bears in His thoughts the wise son, the fiery one, whom He wishes to look on while he is born.

**3** In that field in which the God, after spreading out one net after another in various ways, draws it together again, the Lord, having thus created the lords, exercises His lordship over all.

**4** As the sun shines, lighting up all regions, above, below, and across, so does that glorious adorable God, who is one, rule over all that has the nature of the born.

**5** He who is the source and origin of the gods, the ruler of all, Rudra, the great seer, who of old gave birth to the golden germ — may He endow us with good thoughts.

**6** O Rudra, your form which is kindly and not terrible, and reveals what is holy — with that most blessed form, O dweller among the mountains, look upon us!

**7** O lord of the mountains, make auspicious the arrow which you hold in your hand to throw. O protector of the mountain, injure not man or beast!

**8** Higher than this is Brahman, the supreme, the great, hidden in all beings according to their bodies, the one embracer of the universe — by knowing Him as the Lord, men become immortal.

**9** I know this mighty Person, of the colour of the sun, beyond darkness. Only by knowing Him does one pass over death. There is no other path to go.

**10** There is nothing higher than He, nothing different from Him, nothing greater or smaller than He. Like a tree, established in heaven, He, the one, fills this whole universe.

**11** That which is beyond this world is without form and without suffering. Those who know it become immortal, but others suffer misery indeed.

**12** He, the Lord of all, the bestower of blessings, the adorable God — by realizing Him who resides within the self, one attains peace at last.

**13** He, the creator of the gods and the bestower of their powers, the support of the universe, Rudra the omniscient, who at the beginning gave birth to the golden germ — may He endow us with good thoughts.

**14** He who is the sovereign of the gods, He in whom all the worlds rest, He who rules over all two-footed and four-footed beings — to that God let us sacrifice an oblation.
