---
title: "Svetasvatara Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "svetasvatara-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Savitri, having first collected his mind and expanded his thoughts to the enlightening truth, discovered the light of fire and brought it out of the earth.

**2** With minds collected, we are in the service of the divine Savitri, with strength unto blessedness.

**3** Savitri, having reached with his mind the gods who rise up to the light of heaven, with great light has shone forth.

**4** The sages of the great sage collect their mind and collect their thoughts. He who alone knows the law has ordered the invocations. Great is the praise of the divine Savitri.

**5** I join myself with your ancient prayer with praises. Let my verse go forth like the sun on its path. All the sons of the Immortal listen, they who have reached their heavenly homes.

**6** Where the fire is kindled, where the wind is directed, where the Soma flows over — there the mind is born.

**7** Let us love the ancient Brahman by the grace of Savitri. If you make your dwelling there, the path will not hurt you.

**8** If a wise man holds his body with its three erect parts even, and turns his senses with the mind towards the heart, he will then in the boat of Brahman cross over all the streams which cause fear.

**9** Compressing his breathings let him, who has subdued all motions, breathe forth through the nose with gentle breath. Let the wise man restrain his mind vigilantly, as he would a chariot yoked with vicious horses.

**10** Let him practice his exercises in a place level, pure, free from pebbles, fire, and dust, delightful by its sounds, its water, and its bowers, not painful to the eye, and full of shelters and caves.

**11** When Yoga is being performed, the forms which come first, producing apparitions in Brahman, are those of misty smoke, sun, fire, wind, fireflies, lightnings, and a crystal moon.

**12** When, as earth, water, fire, air, and ether arise, the fivefold quality of Yoga takes place, then there is no longer illness, old age, or pain for him who has obtained a body produced by the fire of Yoga.

**13** The first results of Yoga they call lightness, healthiness, steadiness, a good complexion, an easy pronunciation, a sweet odour, and slight excretions.

**14** As a metal disc, tarnished by dust, shines bright again after it has been cleaned, so is the one incarnate self satisfied and free from grief, after it has seen the real nature of the Self.

**15** And when by means of the real nature of his self he sees, as by a lamp, the real nature of Brahman, then having known the unborn, eternal God, who is beyond all natures, he is freed from all fetters.

**16** He indeed is the God who pervades all regions; He is the firstborn, and He is within the womb. He has been born, and He will be born. He stands behind all persons, looking everywhere.

**17** The God who is in the fire, who is in the water, who has entered into the whole world, who is in plants, who is in trees — to that God be adoration! Yea, to that God be adoration!
