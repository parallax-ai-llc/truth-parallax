## The Righteous Shall Live by Gnosis

Gnostic interpretation reinterprets Habakkuk 2:4 as "the righteous shall live by his *gnosis*." The "faith" or "faithfulness" by which the righteous survive the injustice of the material world is, for the Gnostic, the saving knowledge of one's divine origin and destiny. This knowledge is not mere information but a transformative apprehension that liberates the soul from the archontic prison.

## The Watchtower of Knowledge

Habakkuk's position on the watchtower is read as the Gnostic's elevated perspective — the vantage point of one who has awakened to the truth about the material world. From this height, the prophet can see what those trapped in the everyday experience of the archontic order cannot: the vision of the world's true nature and its coming dissolution.

## The Five Woes Against the Cosmos

The five woe oracles are read as indictments of the material world itself. Greed, violence, exploitation, intoxication, and idolatry are not aberrations but the fundamental characteristics of the Demiurge's creation. The closing verse — "The LORD is in his holy temple; let all the earth keep silence" — is reinterpreted: the true God is in his transcendent temple (the Pleroma), and the material earth must fall silent before a reality it cannot comprehend.
