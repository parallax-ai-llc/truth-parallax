## The Vision of the True God

Gnostic interpretation reads Habakkuk 3 as a theophanic vision of the true God — the God beyond the Demiurge — whose power shatters the material cosmos. The imagery of mountains scattering, the deep giving voice, and the sun and moon standing still in their place describes not merely historical divine acts but the ultimate dissolution of the material order when the true God reveals himself in full power.

## The Cosmic Scope of the Vision

The prayer-hymn's cosmic scope — covering heavens, earth, mountains, rivers, sea, sun, and moon — suggests a vision that encompasses the entire created order. The Gnostic reads this as a vision of the moment when the true God's light penetrates every corner of the Demiurge's creation, dissolving its structures and liberating the divine sparks trapped within them.

## Joy Beyond the Material World (3:17-19)

Habakkuk's declaration of joy in the absence of all material provision is read in Gnostic interpretation as the ultimate expression of liberation from material attachment. When the fig tree does not blossom and the fields produce no food, the spiritual person finds joy in God alone — that is, in the transcendent reality beyond the material cosmos. This is the joy of *gnosis*: the recognition that the soul's true sustenance comes not from the Demiurge's world but from the Pleroma.
