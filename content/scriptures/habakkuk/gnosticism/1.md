## The Complaint Against the Created Order

Gnostic interpretation reads Habakkuk's complaints as a prophetic articulation of the fundamental Gnostic question: why is the world structured for suffering? Habakkuk's cry — "Why do you make me see iniquity, and why do you idly look at wrong?" (1:3) — is the cry of the divine spark trapped in a cosmos that seems designed by an indifferent or malicious power. The Gnostic takes Habakkuk's question further: the injustice the prophet sees is not a flaw in an otherwise good creation but the essential character of the Demiurge's world.

## The Babylonians as Archontic Instruments

God's declaration that he is raising up the Babylonians is read with suspicion: is this the true God speaking, or the Demiurge using one set of archontic powers against another? The Gnostic reading sees in the Babylonian instrument not divine justice but the Demiurge's characteristic method — maintaining control through violence and counter-violence, never achieving genuine liberation.

## The Beginning of Awakening

Habakkuk's refusal to accept God's answer at face value — his second complaint, his deeper questioning — is read as the beginning of spiritual awakening. The prophet who questions the justice of the created order is already on the path to *gnosis*, for the first step toward liberation is the recognition that something is fundamentally wrong with the world as it is.
