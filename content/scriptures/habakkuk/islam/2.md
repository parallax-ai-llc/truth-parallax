## Living by Faith

Islamic interpretation of Habakkuk 2 resonates with the Quranic emphasis on *iman* (faith) and *sabr* (patience) as the twin pillars of the righteous life. The verse "the righteous shall live by his faithfulness" echoes the Quran's repeated pairing of faith and patience: "Indeed, mankind is in loss, except for those who have believed and done righteous deeds and advised each other to truth and advised each other to patience" (103:2-3). The believer endures injustice not with resignation but with active trust in God's ultimate justice.

## The Five Woes and Islamic Ethics

The five woe oracles align closely with Islamic moral teaching. The condemnation of greed echoes the Quran's warnings against hoarding wealth. The woe against violence mirrors the Islamic prohibition of *zulm* (oppression). The condemnation of drunkenness aligns with the Islamic prohibition of intoxicants. The woe against idolatry reflects Islam's most fundamental commitment: *tawhid*, the absolute oneness of God.

## "The LORD Is in His Holy Temple" (2:20)

The call for silence before God resonates with the Islamic practice of *khushu'* — the deep reverence and stillness of the heart in the presence of God, particularly during *salah* (prayer). The verse invites the entire earth into the posture of worship — an invitation the Quran extends: "To Him belongs whoever is in the heavens and the earth. All are devoutly obedient to Him" (30:26).
