## The Prophet's Prayer of Submission

Islamic interpretation of Habakkuk 3 reads the prayer-hymn as an expression of *tawakkul* — complete trust in God's plan despite incomprehensible circumstances. The theophanic vision of God's power reassures the prophet that the God who commands the heavens and the earth is fully capable of executing justice, even when his timing and methods exceed human understanding. The Quran echoes this confidence: "And to Allah belongs the unseen of the heavens and the earth, and to Him will be returned the matter, all of it" (11:123).

## The Theophany and God's Majesty

The vision of God's glory covering the heavens (3:3) resonates with the Quranic descriptions of divine majesty: "Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth" (24:35). Muslim scholars see in Habakkuk's theophany a prophetic witness to the same God whose glory and power the Quran celebrates. The shaking of mountains and the splitting of rivers are familiar themes in Quranic eschatology.

## Faith Beyond Circumstances (3:17-19)

The prophet's declaration of joy in God despite total deprivation aligns with the highest Islamic ideal of *rida* — contentment with God's decree. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Wonderful is the affair of the believer, for his affairs are all good. If something good happens to him, he is thankful, and that is good for him. If something bad happens to him, he is patient, and that is good for him" (Muslim). Habakkuk's closing words embody this principle perfectly.
