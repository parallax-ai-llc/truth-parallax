## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Habakkuk engages with the same central verse — "the righteous shall live by his faithfulness" (2:4) — but reads it within the broader Catholic understanding of faith as *fides formata*, faith formed by love and expressed in works. The Council of Trent affirmed that justification involves both faith and the transformative grace that produces righteous living. Habakkuk's canticle in chapter 3 has been incorporated into the Liturgy of the Hours and is prayed as part of the Friday morning office during Lent.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

The Church Fathers read Habakkuk's dialogue with God as a model of prayerful trust in divine providence. Jerome's commentary emphasized that the prophet's questions are not expressions of doubt but of faith seeking understanding. The five woe oracles are read as a comprehensive moral catalogue applicable in every age. The canticle of chapter 3 is valued as one of the great prayer-hymns of the Old Testament.

## Theological Emphases

- **Faith Formed by Love**: The righteous live by a faith that expresses itself in charitable works
- **Trust in Providence**: Habakkuk models the believer's trust in God's governance of history
- **Prophetic Prayer**: The book legitimizes bringing difficult questions to God in prayer
- **The Canticle**: Chapter 3 is a liturgical treasure, prayed in the Church's daily office
