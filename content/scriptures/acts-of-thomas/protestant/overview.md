## Overview

The Protestant engagement with the Acts of Thomas studies the text for its witness to the diversity of early Christianity and the development of traditions not preserved in the canonical texts. The work raises important questions about the relationship between apostolic authority, ascetic practice, and orthodox theology. Protestant scholars have been particularly interested in the Hymn of the Pearl and its implications for understanding early Christian soteriology.
