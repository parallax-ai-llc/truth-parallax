---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 2: The King's Palace"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 2
---

**1** And leaving the ship they entered into the city, and lo, there were noises of flutes and water-organs, and trumpets sounded about them; and the apostle inquired, saying: What is this festival that is in this city?

**2** And they that were there said to him: Thee also have the gods brought to make merry in this city. For the king's daughter is about to be married, and this is the rejoicing and assembly for the wedding.

**3** And the king had sent forth heralds to proclaim everywhere that all should come to the marriage, rich and poor, bond and free, strangers and citizens: and if any refuse and come not to the marriage he shall answer for it unto the king.

**4** And Abban having heard that, said to the apostle: Let us also go, lest we offend the king, especially since we are strangers. And he said: Let us go.

**5** And after they had put up in the inn and rested a little space they went to the marriage; and the apostle seeing them all set down, laid himself in the midst, and all looked at him, as at a stranger and one come from a foreign land.

**6** But Abban the merchant, being the lord's master, laid himself in another place. And as they dined and drank, the apostle tasted nothing; so they that were about him said unto him: Wherefore art thou come here, neither eating nor drinking?

**7** But he answered them, saying: I am come here for something greater than food or drink, and to fulfil the king's will. For the heralds proclaim the king's messages, and whoso hearkeneth not to the heralds shall be subject to the king's judgement.

**8** Now when the marriage feast was done, the bridegroom lifted up the curtain of the bride-chamber that he might bring the bride to himself. And he saw the Lord Jesus bearing the likeness of Judas Thomas and speaking with the bride.

**9** And the bridegroom said unto him: Wentest thou not out in the sight of all? How then art thou found here? But the Lord said to him: I am not Judas who is also called Thomas: I am his brother.

**10** And the Lord sat down upon the bed and bade them also sit upon chairs, and began to say unto them: Remember, my children, what my brother spake unto you and what he committed before you: and know this, that if ye abstain from this foul intercourse ye become holy temples, pure, being quit of afflictions and troubles known and unknown, and ye will not be girt about with cares for life and for children, whose end is destruction.

**11** And the bride and bridegroom received the Lord's words and gave themselves wholly unto him and refrained from foul desire, and so continued, dwelling in purity.
