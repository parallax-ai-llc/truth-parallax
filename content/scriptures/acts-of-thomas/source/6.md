---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 6: The Youth and the Maiden"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 6
---

**1** And the apostle departed thence and went unto another city, and a young man came to meet him, and fell at his feet and besought him, saying: I have committed a great sin.

**2** And the apostle said: Tell me thy sin, and the Lord shall forgive thee. And the youth said: There was a maiden whom I loved, and she loved me, and we had agreed together. But when I went to her to take her as my wife, I found another man with her, and I slew them both.

**3** And the apostle hearing this was grieved and said unto him: Come, my son, let us go and see this deed. And they went and came to the place, and the apostle saw the bodies lying there.

**4** And Thomas lifted up his voice and said: My Lord and my God, who didst not withhold thy grace from the apostles, and didst send them to preach in the world, and now by thy grace call all men unto thyself — raise up these dead, that they may live and glorify thee.

**5** And the apostle touched the bodies and said: Rise in the name of Jesus Christ. And the young man and the maiden arose as from sleep.

**6** And the apostle said to them: Tell us what ye have seen and where ye have been. And the young man said: We were taken to a place of darkness and stench, wherein we heard the groaning and weeping of many.

**7** And the maiden said: We were brought to the edge of a great pit from which arose a foul smell, and in it we saw the souls of those who were tormented in various ways.

**8** And the apostle said: Ye have seen the punishments and torments that await sinners. Repent now and believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and he shall forgive your sins.

**9** And the youth who had slain them fell down at the apostle's feet and said: I repent me of that which I have done. And the apostle baptized them all three in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit.

**10** And the apostle said: The Lord is merciful and long-suffering and will forgive every man that turneth unto him. Go in peace and sin no more.
