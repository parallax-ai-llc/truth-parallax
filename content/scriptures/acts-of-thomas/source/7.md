---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 7: The Captain's Wife"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 7
---

**1** And the apostle went on further, teaching and healing. And there met him a woman, the wife of the captain of King Misdaeus, whose name was Siphor, and she fell at his feet and besought him, saying: Man of God, my daughter is grievously vexed with a devil.

**2** And the apostle said: Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and thy daughter shall be healed. And she said: I do believe, Lord; help thou mine unbelief.

**3** And they came unto the captain's house, and the apostle entered in and saw the maiden lying on the ground tearing herself.

**4** And the apostle stood over her and said: Evil spirit, I command thee in the name of Jesus Christ the Son of the living God, come out of her and torment her not.

**5** And the spirit cried out with a loud voice: What have I to do with thee, apostle of the Most High? Why art thou come hither to destroy us before the time?

**6** And the apostle said: I command thee in the name of Jesus, come out and harm no one hereafter. And the evil spirit came out of her, and the maiden arose and was healed.

**7** And the captain Siphor and his wife and all his household believed in the Lord Jesus Christ and were baptized. And the apostle broke bread and gave them the eucharist, and they continued in the teaching of the apostle.

**8** And Thomas said unto them: Keep yourselves from all uncleanness and covetousness and gluttony and slander; for these are the works of the adversary. But put on charity and gentleness and faithfulness and purity, for these are the garments of the kingdom.

**9** And the captain said: We will keep all these things, O apostle of the Most High, for thou hast shown us the way of truth. And the apostle went on his way rejoicing.
