---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 9: Mygdonia, Wife of Charisius"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 9
---

**1** And the apostle came unto the city of King Misdaeus, and he lodged in the house of one of the chief men of the city.

**2** And the fame of the apostle spread throughout all the city, and many came unto him to hear the word and to be healed of their diseases. And among them came Mygdonia, the wife of Charisius, who was a kinsman and friend of King Misdaeus.

**3** And when she heard the apostle speaking of the Lord Jesus Christ, and of the kingdom of heaven, and of the life eternal, her heart was pierced and she believed in the Lord.

**4** And she returned to her house and said unto her husband Charisius: A great gift hath been given me this day, for I have heard the words of life from the mouth of a stranger, a man of God, who preacheth a new doctrine.

**5** And Charisius said unto her: What is this doctrine of which thou speakest? And she answered: He preacheth purity and holiness and the love of one God who created all things, and he saith that we must forsake the things of this world and seek those things which are above.

**6** And Charisius was troubled at these words, for he perceived that the heart of his wife was changed. And he went to see the apostle, and found him teaching in the midst of a great company.

**7** And the apostle said unto all that heard: Blessed are the pure in heart, for they shall see God. Blessed are they that have kept their flesh pure, for they shall become the temple of God. Blessed are the continent, for unto them will God speak.

**8** And Mygdonia returned again and again to hear the apostle, and she gave herself wholly to prayer and fasting, and she would no longer share the bed of Charisius.

**9** And Charisius went unto King Misdaeus and said: That sorcerer whom they call Thomas hath bewitched my wife, and she will no more come to my bed but hath forsaken me.

**10** And King Misdaeus sent soldiers to seize the apostle and bring him before the judgement seat. And when Thomas was brought before the king, Misdaeus said unto him: Who art thou, and by whose power doest thou these things?

**11** And the apostle answered: I am the servant of the Lord Jesus Christ, the Son of the living God, and by his power I heal the sick and preach the truth.
