---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 10: The Imprisoned Mygdonia"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 10
---

**1** And King Misdaeus commanded that Thomas be imprisoned, and Mygdonia hearing it went to the prison to visit the apostle.

**2** And Charisius also went and found his wife sitting at the apostle's feet, listening to his words. And he was exceedingly wroth and said unto the king: Send this sorcerer out of our country, or else let him be put to death, for he hath destroyed my house.

**3** And Thomas was brought again before King Misdaeus, who said unto him: I have shown thee kindness and thou hast repaid me with evil, turning the hearts of my people against their own households.

**4** And the apostle said: I have done no evil, O king, but have preached the truth. For there is one God, the Lord of heaven and earth, and one Lord Jesus Christ, the Son of the Most High, through whom all things were made.

**5** And the king said: Persuade this woman to return to her husband as is fitting, and I will release thee. And Thomas said: This I cannot do, for she hath chosen the better part, which shall not be taken from her.

**6** Then the king commanded Mygdonia to be shut up in her own house, and soldiers were set to guard her, that she should not go forth to hear the apostle.

**7** But Mygdonia ceased not from prayer and fasting, and she said: Though they shut me up in this body, yet my soul is free and cannot be imprisoned, for it belongeth unto God.

**8** And in the night the Lord appeared unto her in a vision and said: Fear not, Mygdonia, for I am with thee. And she was comforted and gave thanks unto God.

**9** And the nurse of Mygdonia, whose name was Marcia, also believed, having seen the constancy of her mistress. And she said: The God whom thou worshippest is the true God, for no human power could give thee such strength.

**10** And Mygdonia said: Blessed art thou, Marcia, for thou hast perceived the truth. The Lord shall be thy shepherd, and thou shalt not want.
