---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 13: The Martyrdom of Thomas"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 13
---

**1** And King Misdaeus commanded his soldiers, saying: Take this man to the mountain that is without the city, and there pierce him with spears, and put an end to him.

**2** And the soldiers took the apostle and led him forth. And there went with him Vazan the king's son, and Siphor the captain, and Mygdonia, and Tertia, and Marcia. And they wept and lamented, for they loved the apostle exceedingly.

**3** And the apostle said unto them: Weep not for me, my children, but weep for yourselves and for your sins. For I go unto my Lord, and I shall be with him for evermore. But see that ye hold fast the faith which I have delivered unto you, and forsake it not.

**4** And when they came unto the mountain, the apostle said unto the soldiers: Do that which ye have been commanded to do. And he knelt down and prayed, saying: My Lord and my God, I give thee thanks that thou hast counted me worthy to suffer for thy name's sake.

**5** And he said: Come, Lord Jesus, and receive my spirit. And he said unto Vazan: My son, when I am departed, let the brethren assemble together for the breaking of bread and for prayer. And be thou a leader unto them, and feed the flock of the Lord with diligence.

**6** And having said these things, the apostle stretched forth his hands and prayed: Lord Jesus Christ, let not the evil one have power over me. Receive thou my soul and keep it, until the time appointed. And the soldiers pierced him with their spears.

**7** And thus was fulfilled the martyrdom of the apostle Thomas, which he accomplished by the will of his Lord in the land of the Indians.

**8** And Vazan and Siphor took the body of the apostle and wrapped it in fine linen and laid it in the sepulchre wherein the kings of old were wont to be buried.

**9** And Mygdonia and Tertia ceased not from the faith but ministered unto the poor and the sick, and they taught many women concerning the Lord Jesus.

**10** And Vazan also, who had been made a presbyter by the apostle, ceased not from preaching the word, and he built churches and ordained ministers, and the faith grew exceedingly in the land of the Indians.

**11** And after a long time, King Misdaeus himself fell grievously sick, and no physician could heal him. And he remembered the apostle Thomas and repented of what he had done.

**12** And he sent to the sepulchre of the apostle and took dust from thence and laid it upon his body, saying: I believe now that thou art the servant of the true God. And he was healed, and he also received the faith.

**13** And the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ was with them all, and they lived in peace and in the love of Christ. And the bones of the apostle Thomas were later carried away unto the West, but the faith which he planted in India endured. To the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ, to whom be praise and honour for ever and ever. Amen.
