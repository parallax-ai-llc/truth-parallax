---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 4: The Colt"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 4
---

**1** And the apostle continued on his journey with Abban the merchant, and they came unto the land of India, and Abban went to salute the king Gundaphorus and told him of the carpenter whom he had brought with him.

**2** And the king was glad, and commanded Thomas to come before him, and when he came in the king said unto him: What craft canst thou perform for me? And Thomas said unto him: I can build palaces and houses and temples and court-houses and everything that is needed.

**3** And the king took him and went forth out of the gates of the city, and as they went along the way the king began to discuss with him the building of a court-house in the place where he desired to build one. And Thomas said: Yea, this is a place fit for the building.

**4** And the king said: Begin then and build. And Thomas said: I cannot begin to build now at this season. And the king said: When canst thou begin? And he said: I will begin in November and finish in April.

**5** And the king marvelled and said: Every building is builded in the summer, but thou canst thou build and construct in the winter? And Thomas replied: Thus it must be done, and there is no other way.

**6** And the king said: If then thou hast determined so, draw me a plan how the work shall be, since I shall come after some time. And the apostle took a reed and drew, measuring the place; and the doors he set toward the east to look toward the light, and the windows toward the west toward the winds, and the bakehouse he made to be toward the south, and the aqueduct for the service toward the north.

**7** And the king saw it and said to the apostle: Truly thou art a craftsman, and it beseemeth thee to serve kings. And he left much money with him and departed from him.

**8** And from time to time the king sent money and provision unto Thomas, and the apostle distributed it all to the poor and the afflicted, saying: The king knoweth that he shall receive his recompense, but the poor must for the present be refreshed.

**9** And after a long time the king sent a messenger unto Thomas, saying: Show me what thou hast built, or what I shall pay thee. And the apostle sent to him saying: The palace is built and only the roof remaineth.

**10** And the king hearing it, sent him again gold and silver, saying: Let the palace be roofed. And the apostle gave thanks unto the Lord and said: I thank thee, Lord, in every way, that thou didst die for a short time that I might live in thee for ever, and that thou hast sold me to find many that through me should be bought.

**11** And he ceased not to teach and refresh the afflicted, saying: The Lord hath dispensed this unto you and he giveth unto every one his food: for he is the nourisher of orphans and steward of the widows, and unto all that are afflicted he is rest and repose.

**12** And when the king came to the city he inquired of his friends concerning the palace which Thomas had built for him, and they told him: Neither hath he built a palace, nor hath he done aught else of that which he promised to do, but he goeth about the towns and villages, and if he have somewhat he giveth it unto the poor, and teacheth of a new God, and healeth the sick, and driveth out devils.

**13** And the king striking his face with his hands, sent for Thomas the apostle, and said to him: Hast thou built me the palace? And he said: Yea, I have built it. And the king said: When then shall we go and see it? And the apostle answered and said: Thou canst not see it now, but when thou departest this life, then thou shalt see it.
