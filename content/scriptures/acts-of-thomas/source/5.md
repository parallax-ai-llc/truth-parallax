---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 5: The Baptism of Gundaphorus"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 5
---

**1** And the king was exceedingly wroth, and commanded Thomas to be cast into prison until he should inquire and learn who should destroy him. And Thomas was led away to prison, and the king considered by what death he should destroy him.

**2** But Gad the brother of the king fell sick, and by reason of his vexation and the disappointment which the king had suffered, he was grievously troubled; and he sent for the king his brother and said unto him: O king my brother, I commend unto thee my house and my children. For I am vexed by reason of the provocation that hath befallen thee, and lo I am dying.

**3** And when the soul of Gad the king's brother departed, angels received it and brought it up into heaven, showing him the places and dwellings there, and asked him: In what place wouldest thou dwell?

**4** And when they drew near the building of the apostle Thomas, which he had built for the king, Gad beholding it said to the angels: I beseech you, my lords, let me dwell in one of the lower chambers of this palace.

**5** And they said to him: Thou canst not dwell in this building, for it is the palace which that Christian hath built for thy brother. And he said: I beseech you, my lords, suffer me to go to my brother, that I may buy this palace of him; for my brother knoweth not what it is like, and he will sell it to me.

**6** Then the angels let the soul of Gad go; and as they were putting on his grave clothes, his soul entered into him. And he said to them that stood about him: Call my brother to me, that I may beg of him one request.

**7** Straightway they told the king, saying: Thy brother is revived! And the king arose and with a great company went to his brother and came in and stood by his bed as one stunned, unable to speak to him.

**8** And his brother said: I know and am persuaded, brother, that if any had asked of thee the half of thy kingdom, thou wouldest give it for my sake. Wherefore I entreat thee to grant me one favour which I beg of thee: that thou sell me that which I desire.

**9** And the king answered and said: And what is it that thou wouldest have me sell thee? And he said: Assure me by an oath that thou wilt not deny me. And the king sware unto him: Whatsoever of my possessions thou desirest, I will give thee.

**10** And he said to him: Sell me that palace which thou hast in the heavens. And the king said: A palace in the heavens — whence should I have that? And he said: That which that Christian built for thee, who is now in the prison, the man whom the merchant bought and brought unto thee; I mean that Hebrew slave whom thou desiredst to punish, having suffered some deception at his hand — whereat I also was vexed: but now I have seen and behold I dwell in one of its lower chambers.

**11** And the king hearing these things and perceiving concerning those things which were eternal, was amazed. And he said: That palace I cannot sell thee, but I pray to enter into it and to dwell therein and to be accounted worthy of the inhabitants of it. And if thou truly desirest to buy such a palace, behold, the man liveth and will build thee one better than that.

**12** And forthwith he sent and brought the apostle out of the prison, and said unto him: I entreat thee, as a man entreating the servant of God, that thou pray for me and beseech him whose servant thou art to forgive me, and overlook that which I have done unto thee or thought to do.

**13** And the apostle said: I give thee thanks, Lord Jesus, that thou hast revealed thy works in these men; for thou alone art the God of truth, and there is none other, and thou art he that knoweth all things unknown to the most.

**14** And the apostle began to pray and to say: Come, O gift of the Most High; come, O perfect mercy; come, Holy Spirit; come, thou that dost make known the mysteries of the chosen; come, thou that art partaker in all the combats of the noble athlete; come, O peace.

**15** And when he had prayed, the apostle said to the king: Where are the builders? And the king said: They are here. And the apostle said: Gundaphorus, thy palace hath been built, and the foundations thereof are prayers.

**16** And the king Gundaphorus and Gad his brother were baptized and believed in the Lord Jesus Christ.
