---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 3: The Serpent"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 3
---

**1** And the apostle went forth to depart on his way, and when he had gone forth, a wild ass's colt came and stood before him, and Thomas said: By Jesus, who hath given speech unto dumb things, do thou also speak and tell us what thou wilt. The colt opened its mouth and said: Twin of Christ, apostle of the Most High, privy to the hidden word of the life-giver, receiver of the secret of the holy mysteries, fellow worker with the Son of God.

**2** And the apostle journeyed along the way, and there was a great serpent lying by the wayside, and when it saw the apostle draw near it hissed and puffed and made itself ready.

**3** And the apostle said unto it: Tell us, what is thy boast and thy seed? For there is none more vile than thou. And the serpent answered and said: I am a reptile of the reptile nature, the baleful son of the baleful father; I am son of him that hurt and smote the four brethren which stood upright.

**4** I am son of him that sitteth on a throne over all the earth, that receiveth back his own from them that borrow. I am son of him that girdeth about the sphere. I am kinsman to him that is outside the ocean, whose tail lieth in his own mouth.

**5** I am he that entered through the fence into paradise and spake with Eve the things which my father bade me speak unto her. I am he that kindled and inflamed Cain to kill his own brother.

**6** I am he that sent the angels down from above and entangled them in desires of women, that children born of earth might come of them and I might work my will in them.

**7** And the apostle said unto it: Tell me of what seed and of what race thou art. And the serpent said unto him: I am a creeping thing that crept from the beginning, and my dwelling is the earth. And the apostle said: Look upon thy nature, that thou art brought to nought.

**8** And immediately that serpent swelled and burst and died, and its venom was shed forth, and in the place where its venom was shed there was a great chasm, and that serpent was swallowed up therein.

**9** And the apostle said unto the people: Follow ye this God whom I preach unto you, the Lord Jesus, the living God, for he alone shall destroy these serpents, and alone is he that shall make clean your souls from their venom.
