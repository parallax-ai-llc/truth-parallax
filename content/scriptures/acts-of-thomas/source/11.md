---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 11: Tertia, Wife of Misdaeus"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 11
---

**1** And it came to pass that Tertia, the wife of King Misdaeus himself, heard concerning the apostle and the doctrine which he preached, and she came secretly to the prison to hear his words.

**2** And the apostle said unto her: O woman, the Lord Jesus Christ, whom I preach, is the physician of the souls and bodies of men. He healeth the blind and the lame, and he raiseth the dead; and above all these things, he cleanseth the soul from all defilement.

**3** And Tertia heard his words and her heart was opened, and she wept and said: I am the most wretched of women, for I am the wife of the king who persecuteth the servants of God.

**4** And the apostle said: The Lord looketh not upon the outward estate of a person, whether they be king or beggar, but he looketh upon the heart. If thy heart be turned unto him, he receiveth thee.

**5** And Tertia said: Pray for me, O servant of God, that I may receive the seal of thy God, and become a handmaid of the Most High. And the apostle prayed over her and anointed her with holy oil.

**6** And when Misdaeus the king heard that his own wife also had gone to the apostle, he was filled with fury and said: This sorcerer hath bewitched my whole household.

**7** And he commanded Thomas to be brought before him, and said: Sorcerer and deceiver, how long wilt thou trouble my kingdom? Thou hast turned the heart of my friend's wife, and now my own wife also doth follow after thy enchantments.

**8** And the apostle said: These things which thou callest sorcery are the works of the living God. I have not bewitched anyone, but I have opened their eyes to see the truth and their ears to hear the word of God.

**9** And Tertia came before the king and said: O king, be not wrathful, but hearken to the words of this man, for he speaketh the truth. The gods which we worship are idols made with hands, but the God whom he preacheth is the maker of heaven and earth.

**10** But the king would not hear her, and he sent her away to her chambers with a guard, and he determined in his heart to destroy the apostle.
