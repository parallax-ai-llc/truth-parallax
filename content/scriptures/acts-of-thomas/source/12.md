---
title: "Acts of Thomas — Act 12: Vazan, Son of Misdaeus"
scripture: "acts-of-thomas"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Now Vazan, the son of King Misdaeus, heard all that had come to pass concerning the apostle, and concerning Mygdonia and Tertia, and he came to the prison to see Thomas.

**2** And when he came in, the apostle said unto him: Who art thou, young man, and what seekest thou? And Vazan said: I am the son of King Misdaeus, and I have come to learn of the God whom thou preachest.

**3** And the apostle said: Blessed art thou, Vazan, that thou comest seeking God while thou art yet young. For the Lord saith, Suffer the young to come unto me, for of such is the kingdom of heaven.

**4** And Vazan said: My father is determined to destroy thee, but I beseech thee, pray for him that his eyes may be opened. And the apostle said: The Lord is long-suffering and of great mercy, and he desireth not the destruction of any, but that all should come to repentance.

**5** And the apostle taught Vazan concerning the Lord Jesus Christ, and concerning the resurrection of the dead, and the life eternal, and the kingdom that hath no end.

**6** And Vazan believed and said: Baptize me, O apostle, that I may receive the grace of the Lord. And the apostle baptized him in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit.

**7** And when King Misdaeus heard that his own son also had been baptized, he rent his garments and said: This man hath left me no one; he hath taken my friend's wife, and my wife, and now my son.

**8** And the king sent for his son and said: Hast thou also lost thy senses, that thou hast been deceived by this impostor? And Vazan said: I have not been deceived, O father, but I have found the truth. The God whom Thomas preacheth is the only true God.

**9** And the king was exceedingly grieved but could not persuade his son to turn away from the faith. And he determined to make an end of the apostle swiftly.
