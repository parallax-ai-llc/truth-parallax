## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Acts of Thomas places the text at the intersection of multiple cultural and religious worlds. The Indian setting invites comparison with Hindu and Buddhist hagiography and ascetic traditions. The Hymn of the Pearl resonates with Gnostic myths of the soul's descent, Platonic philosophy, and universal narratives of spiritual exile and return. The text's asceticism finds parallels in Jain renunciation and Buddhist monasticism.
