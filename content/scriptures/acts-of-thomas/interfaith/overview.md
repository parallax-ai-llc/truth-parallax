## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Acts of Thomas draws on its unique position as a Christian text set in India. The narrative provides one of the earliest witnesses to the encounter between Christianity and South Asian cultures, raising questions about inculturation, translation of religious concepts, and the universal appeal of ascetic ideals. The text facilitates Hindu-Christian and Buddhist-Christian dialogue on renunciation, spiritual liberation, and the nature of the divine.
