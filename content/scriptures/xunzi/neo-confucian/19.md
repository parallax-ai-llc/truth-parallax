Neo-Confucian reading of Xunzi chapter 19, "A Discussion of Ritual," typically proceeds with critical caution. While acknowledging the intellectual power of Xunzi's arguments, Neo-Confucian interpreters measure his claims against the Mencian orthodoxy that human nature is originally good. Where Xunzi sees the need for external transformation, the Neo-Confucian tradition emphasizes the recovery of an innate moral nature obscured by selfish desires.

Nevertheless, Qing dynasty scholars who sought to move beyond Song-Ming metaphysical debates found in this chapter valuable insights about the practical mechanisms of moral cultivation. Xunzi's attention to institutional design, educational method, and the social conditions necessary for moral development offered resources that purely introspective approaches to self-cultivation could not provide.
