## Tian Lun (Discourse on Heaven)

Heaven's ways are constant. It does not exist for the sake of Yao, nor does it cease to exist because of Jie. If you respond to it with good government, there will be good fortune. If you respond to it with misgovernment, there will be misfortune.

If you strengthen the fundamental pursuits and are frugal in expenditure, then Heaven cannot make you poor. If you attend to nourishment and act in accordance with the seasons, then Heaven cannot make you ill. If you follow the Way and are not of two minds, then Heaven cannot bring you disaster.

Therefore floods and droughts cannot cause famine, cold and heat cannot cause illness, and strange portents cannot cause misfortune.

But if you neglect the fundamental pursuits and are extravagant in spending, then Heaven cannot make you rich. If you neglect nourishment and act contrary to the seasons, then Heaven cannot make you whole.

The stars falling, the trees groaning -- the people of the state are all terrified. They ask: "Why is this?" I answer: There is no special reason. It is simply the operation of heaven and earth, the transformation of yin and yang. It is rare, but it is natural. To marvel at it is permissible; to fear it is wrong.

Rather than exalting Heaven and hoping for its gifts, would it not be better to accumulate resources and use them wisely? Rather than following Heaven and praising it, would it not be better to control what Heaven has mandated and use it?
