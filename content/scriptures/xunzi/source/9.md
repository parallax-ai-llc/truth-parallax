## Wang Zhi (The Rule of a True King)

The true king makes impartial use of the worthy. He appoints men to office according to their abilities. He does not leave a single man of talent unused, nor does he allow a single unworthy man to hold office.

He makes the people rich and then teaches them. For the people cannot be taught unless they have enough to eat. They cannot be taught propriety if they are cold and hungry.

Therefore, the true king first enriches the people and then instructs them. He secures their livelihood and then demands their service. He provides for their needs and then establishes moral standards.

The principles of good government: Divide the land equitably. Regulate the markets fairly. Reduce taxes to what is necessary. Employ the people in season, so that agriculture is not disrupted.

When the king is humane, the people love him. When the king is righteous, the people respect him. When the king makes wise decisions, the people trust him. When the king is generous, the people serve him willingly.

The distinction between the true king and the tyrant: The king enriches the people; the tyrant enriches himself. The king seeks the advice of the worthy; the tyrant surrounds himself with flatterers. The king governs by moral influence; the tyrant governs by fear.

A state may be small, but if it follows the Way, it will be strong. A state may be large, but if it departs from the Way, it will fall.
