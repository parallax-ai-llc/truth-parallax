## Fa Xing (The Proper Model of Conduct)

What should be taken as the proper model of conduct? I say: Ritual. Why ritual? Because without ritual, desires have no limit. Without limits, there is contention. Without an end to contention, there is disorder.

The ancient kings hated disorder and established ritual to give it form. They divided the noble from the base, the old from the young, the wise from the foolish. They made each receive what was appropriate to him, and each bear his proper burden. This is the great foundation of government.

When farmers devote themselves to farming, when merchants devote themselves to trade, when craftsmen devote themselves to their craft, when scholars devote themselves to learning -- each one doing what he does best -- then the state is well ordered and resources are abundant.

The model of conduct is not arbitrary. It is rooted in the nature of things and the needs of society. Those who depart from this model in pursuit of personal advantage bring harm to themselves and others.

Consider: The compass and square are the models for circles and squares. Ritual is the model for human conduct. Without the compass, how would circles be drawn? Without ritual, how would conduct be regulated?

The superior man follows the model and thereby achieves virtue. The petty man departs from the model and thereby falls into disorder. The distinction between the gentleman and the petty man lies precisely here: in whether one follows or abandons the proper model.
