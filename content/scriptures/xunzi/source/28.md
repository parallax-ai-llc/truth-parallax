## You Zuo (The Right-Hand Vessel)

Confucius was visiting the temple of Duke Huan of Lu. There he saw a vessel that tilted to one side. Confucius asked the temple keeper: "What vessel is this?"

The keeper replied: "This is the vessel that sits at the right hand of the seat."

Confucius said: "I have heard of such a vessel. When it is empty, it tilts. When it is half full, it stands upright. When it is full to overflowing, it topples over."

Confucius turned to his disciples and said: "Pour water into it." They did so. When it was half full, it stood upright. When they continued to pour, it toppled over and the water spilled out.

Confucius sighed and said: "Alas! What is there that does not topple when it is full to overflowing?"

Zi Lu asked: "Is there a way to maintain fullness?"

Confucius said: "Yes. Those who are intelligent should guard it with simplicity. Those who have great merit should guard it with humility. Those who are brave and strong should guard it with caution. Those who are rich should guard it with frugality. This is the way of reducing and diminishing."

This parable teaches the principle of moderation: Excess leads to collapse. The man who is at the height of success is closest to failure. The vessel that is perfectly balanced is the one that is neither empty nor full.
