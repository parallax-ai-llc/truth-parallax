## Yi Bing (A Debate on Military Affairs)

The essential principle of military affairs: The army of the true king does not kill. It liberates. It does not invade territory -- it brings order.

The strength of an army lies not in its weapons but in the unity of its people. When the people are united, the army is strong. When the people are divided, the army is weak.

A city wall ten miles around and defended by a hundred thousand men -- if the people have no will to fight, the wall is useless. An army of a million men -- if the soldiers have no loyalty, the army is useless.

Therefore the ruler who wishes to strengthen his military must first win the hearts of the people. He wins their hearts through good government, fair taxation, and just administration.

The art of war: Choose generals wisely. Train soldiers rigorously. Maintain discipline strictly. But above all, ensure that the cause is just. An army that fights for a just cause is invincible.

The benevolent man uses force only as a last resort. He exhausts all peaceful means before resorting to arms. When he does fight, he fights with restraint. He does not slaughter the surrendered. He does not destroy the crops. He does not harm civilians.

The difference between a righteous army and a predatory army: The righteous army is welcomed by the people of the conquered land. The predatory army is resisted. This difference determines victory and defeat more surely than any strategy or weapon.
