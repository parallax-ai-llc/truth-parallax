## Li Lun (Discourse on Ritual)

From what did ritual arise? I say: Man is born with desires. When desires are not satisfied, he cannot help but seek to satisfy them. When this seeking knows no measure or limit, there cannot help but be contention. When there is contention, there is disorder. When there is disorder, there is exhaustion.

The ancient kings hated this disorder, and so they established rites and righteous principles to divide and regulate men's desires, to provide for their satisfaction, and to ensure that desire did not exhaust its object, nor the object sink beneath desire. The two were sustained in balance. This is the origin of rites.

Rites have three roots: Heaven and Earth are the root of life. Ancestors are the root of the family. Rulers and teachers are the root of order. Without Heaven and Earth, how would we live? Without ancestors, where would we come from? Without rulers and teachers, how would there be order?

Therefore, rites serve Heaven above and Earth below, honor ancestors, and exalt rulers and teachers. These are the three roots of rites.

Rites trim what is too long, stretch what is too short, eliminate excess, repair deficiency. They express the beauty of love and reverence, and step by step bring the individual to the perfection of courtesy and refinement.

Music combines with rites: rites distinguish, music unites. Rites order the outer, music orders the inner. The combination of rites and music governs the complete man.
