## Rong Ru (Of Honor and Disgrace)

There is the honor that comes from virtue and the honor that comes from power. There is the disgrace that comes from conduct and the disgrace that comes from circumstance. The honor of virtue is what you bring about by your own efforts. The honor of power is what others confer upon you.

The honor that comes from virtue -- this is what you should seek. The honor that comes from power -- this should not be sought. Therefore the gentleman is attentive to his own conduct.

Those who are proud and extravagant will meet with ruin. Those who are modest and frugal will meet with success. It has been so from ancient times.

The farmer plows well and cannot thereby be guaranteed a harvest. The merchant trades well and cannot thereby be guaranteed wealth. The craftsman works well and cannot thereby be guaranteed riches. But the gentleman cultivates virtue and can thereby be guaranteed honor.

Therefore, what depends on heaven -- this one should accept. What depends on oneself -- this one should make effort in.

If one sees profit and does not think of harm, if one sees advantage and does not think of disaster, then even if the world is his domain, it will not be sufficient for him. Therefore the gentleman considers what is right; the petty man considers what is profitable.

When the gentleman encounters difficulties, he maintains his virtue. When the petty man encounters difficulties, he abandons his principles.
