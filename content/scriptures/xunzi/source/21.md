## Jie Bi (Dispelling Blindness)

The thing that all men should fear is being blinded by a small point of truth and thereby being prevented from seeing the great pattern.

Men are blinded by desire, so they cannot see the harm. They are blinded by hatred, so they cannot see the good. They are blinded by the near, so they cannot see the far. They are blinded by the far, so they cannot see the near. They are blinded by the shallow, so they cannot see the deep. They are blinded by the ancient, so they cannot see the present.

When things are divided and examined one by one, each division blinds. This is the common sickness of all theories.

How does one cure this blindness? Through the Way. The mind must be guided by the Way. If the mind follows the Way, it can perceive truth clearly. If the mind departs from the Way, it falls into partiality.

The sage avoids blindness by not allowing himself to become attached to any single thing. He examines all sides. He weighs the evidence. He suspends judgment until all the facts are known.

The mind is the ruler of the body and the master of spiritual intelligence. It gives commands but receives none from outside itself. It controls itself. It forbids itself. It makes its own decisions.

Therefore the gentleman disciplines his mind. He keeps it empty, unified, and still. Empty so that it may receive new knowledge. Unified so that it is not divided. Still so that it is not agitated by things.
