## Bu Gou (Nothing Improper)

The gentleman is respectful in conduct, humble in manner, loyal and trustworthy, and does nothing improper. Even if he is slandered and defamed, he does not fear. He is firm in his virtue and self-controlled in his actions.

When you are young, submit to your elders. When you are poor, submit to the wealthy. When you are lowly, submit to the honored. When you are ignorant, submit to the learned. When you are wrong, submit to what is right.

Even if a man has the power of an ox, he should not compete on the basis of strength. Even if he runs as fast as a horse, he should not compete on the basis of speed. Even if his knowledge is as broad as heaven and earth, he should not compete on the basis of knowledge. One should compete on the basis of virtue.

The gentleman does not boast of what he has accomplished. He does not denigrate others for what they have not accomplished. He is cautious in speech and diligent in action.

The petty man is the opposite: He exaggerates his accomplishments and conceals his faults. He claims credit when things go well and assigns blame when things go poorly.

The gentleman takes care to be correct in the smallest matters, knowing that the great depends upon the small. He does not neglect a single rule of propriety, knowing that the whole depends upon each part.
