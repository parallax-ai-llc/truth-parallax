## Qiang Guo (On Strengthening the State)

The means by which a state is strengthened: Propriety and righteousness. Not military force, not territory, not wealth.

When the state is governed by propriety and righteousness, the people are orderly without the need for harsh punishments. The officials are dutiful without the need for constant supervision. The army is brave without the need for threats.

When the state is governed by force and trickery, the people are restless even under heavy punishments. The officials are corrupt even under close surveillance. The army is cowardly even under threat of execution.

A state that relies on virtue is like a tree with deep roots. Storms cannot uproot it. A state that relies on force is like a wall without a foundation. A single shock will topple it.

The way to strengthen the state is simple: Appoint the worthy to office. Dismiss the unworthy. Reward merit. Punish wrongdoing. Promote agriculture. Regulate commerce. Educate the people.

If the ruler does these things consistently, within a generation the state will be strong. If he neglects them, within a generation the state will be weak.

The state does not need natural defenses to be strong. It does not need vast territory to be secure. What it needs is a ruler who follows the Way, ministers who are loyal and able, and people who are contented and well-governed.
