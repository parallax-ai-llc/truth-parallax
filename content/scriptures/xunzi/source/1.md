## Quan Xue (Encouragement of Learning)

The gentleman says: Learning should never cease. Blue comes from the indigo plant, yet it is bluer than the plant itself. Ice is made from water, yet it is colder than water. A piece of wood as straight as a plumb line may be bent into a wheel that is as round as any circle drawn with a compass; even after the wood has dried, it will not straighten again, because the bending process has made it permanently so.

Thus it is that by broad learning and daily examining himself, the gentleman makes his knowledge clear and his conduct without fault.

I once spent a whole day in thought, but it was not as good as a moment of study. I once stood on tiptoe to gaze into the distance, but it was not as good as climbing to a high place for a broader view.

If you do not climb a high mountain, you will not know the height of heaven. If you do not look down into a deep valley, you will not know the depth of the earth. If you do not hear the words left by the ancient kings, you will not know the greatness of learning.

Accumulate enough earth, and you will make a hill from which rain and wind arise. Accumulate enough water, and you will make an abyss in which dragons are born. Accumulate enough goodness, and spiritual clarity will naturally develop, and the mind of the sage will be fully realized.

Therefore, there is no better way to learn than to be near a great teacher.
