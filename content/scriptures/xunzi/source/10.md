## Fu Guo (Enriching the State)

The fundamental method of enriching the state: Regulate expenditures, promote agriculture, and conserve resources. Tax the people lightly in times of prosperity; assist them in times of hardship.

When the granaries are full, the people know propriety and moderation. When clothing and food are sufficient, the people know honor and shame.

Therefore, the sage ruler does not exhaust the strength of the people. He employs them in season and allows them to rest in the proper time. He does not seize more than what is needed and does not hoard what is not used.

The ten-thousand things each have their proper place. Agriculture and commerce each have their proper function. If you employ the people in farming during the planting season and do not call them away for military service, the crops will be abundant.

If the ruler is frugal, the wealth of the state will be sufficient. If the ruler is extravagant, even the wealth of the whole world will be insufficient.

The true king enriches the common people. The hegemon enriches the officers. A state that is barely surviving enriches its high officials. A state that is doomed enriches only the ruler's personal treasury.

When the ruler's treasury is full and the people are impoverished, this is what is called "the vessel overflowing at the top and dry at the bottom." Such a state cannot defend itself against external attack nor maintain internal stability.
