## Xiu Shen (Cultivating the Person)

To see what is good and search within oneself for it; to see what is bad and examine oneself for it -- such is the way to cultivate the person.

When you do good, do not seek to be recognized. When you have faults, do not be afraid to change. If a man speaks well of you and it accords with reality, be happy. If a man speaks ill of you and it accords with reality, be worried.

Therefore the gentleman is broad and calm, the petty man is narrow and anxious. The gentleman is at ease yet alert, the petty man is agitated yet sluggish.

One who is firm, enduring, simple, and cautious is close to humanity. One who is respectful, modest, deferential, and yielding is close to propriety. One who is suspicious, quarrelsome, grasping, and demanding is close to disgrace.

If you treat the worthy with respect, the unworthy will retreat. If you put the upright into positions of power, the crooked will be straightened.

The gentleman cultivates himself and waits; that is all. He does not try to gain honor through improper means. He does not try to avoid shame through improper means.

When the gentleman is wealthy, he can act with generosity. When he is poor, he can discipline himself. When alive, he can serve. When dead, he is mourned. How can such a man be blocked or frustrated?
