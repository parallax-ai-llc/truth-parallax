## Yue Lun (Discourse on Music)

Music is joy. It is an essential part of human feeling that cannot be eliminated. When a man is joyful, he must give expression to it in voice and movement. This expression, guided by the principles of the Way, becomes music.

Therefore music is the means of guiding joy. Metal, stone, silk, and bamboo are the instruments of music. When music is performed, the people look upward with bright and clear expressions. When drums and shields are employed in military dances, the people look warlike and determined.

Music enters deeply into men and transforms them rapidly. Therefore the ancient kings were careful to give it the proper form. When music is moderate and tranquil, the people are contented and not licentious. When music is stern and majestic, the people are unified and not quarrelsome.

When the people are contented and unified, the army is strong and the city walls secure. Enemy states dare not attack.

Mo Di condemned music. But if music were eliminated, how would joy be expressed? Without proper expression, joy would become licentious. Licentiousness leads to disorder.

The ancient kings did not create music merely for the pleasure of the ear. They used it to guide the hearts of men toward goodness. The tones of a well-ordered age are calm and express contentment. The tones of a disordered age are bitter and express anger. The tones of a dying state are mournful and express sorrow.

Music and government are directly connected. Understanding music is close to understanding how to govern.
