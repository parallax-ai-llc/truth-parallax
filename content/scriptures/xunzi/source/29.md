## Zi Dao (The Way of the Son)

The Way of the son: To serve one's parents with the utmost devotion. To anticipate their needs before they are expressed. To follow their wishes unless doing so would lead to unrighteousness.

When a parent is about to commit an error, the son should admonish gently. If the parent does not listen, the son should admonish again with increased respect and earnestness. If the parent still does not listen, the son should weep and follow.

There are three degrees of filial piety: The highest is to bring honor to one's parents. The next is not to bring disgrace upon them. The lowest is merely to support them.

The filial son nourishes the will of his parents, not merely their bodies. He provides food and clothing, but more importantly, he gives them joy, respect, and peace of mind.

When the filial son serves his parents, he is completely attentive in his daily care. He makes them comfortable in their living arrangements. He is careful in matters of illness. He is sorrowful in mourning them. He is solemn in sacrificing to them. If he fulfills these five duties, he may be called a filial son.

The Way of the son extends to the Way of the minister: He who can serve his parents well can also serve his ruler well. The virtues of loyalty and devotion are the same, whether directed toward parent or prince.
