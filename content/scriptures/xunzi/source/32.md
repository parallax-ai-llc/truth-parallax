## Yao Wen (Yao Asked)

Yao asked Shun: "What is the most important thing in governing the world?"

Shun replied: "To make the people the foundation. The ruler exists for the people, not the people for the ruler."

Shun asked Yu: "How do you maintain the Way amid floods and disasters?"

Yu replied: "By ceaseless effort and by putting the welfare of the people above my own comfort. I passed my own door three times without entering, because the work of saving the people could not wait."

The meaning of these exchanges: The ancient sage kings understood that governance is service. The ruler serves the people as the physician serves the patient -- his skill exists for their benefit, not his own.

The principles of the ancient sage kings may be summarized: Love the people. Employ the worthy. Be diligent. Be sincere. Be moderate. These five principles, consistently applied, are sufficient to bring order to the world.

No clever schemes are needed. No intricate strategies. No subtle manipulations. Simply love the people with sincerity, employ the worthy with discernment, be diligent in public duties, be sincere in all dealings, and be moderate in all things.

The Way of the sage kings is simple to understand but difficult to practice. It is difficult not because it is complex, but because it requires the suppression of selfishness. He who can suppress his selfishness and act for the good of the people -- he is a sage king, whether he wears the crown or not.
