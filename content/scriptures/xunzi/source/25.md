## Cheng Xiang (Working Songs)

The people of the world toil greatly. They plow and plant, they weave and spin. They have no leisure, yet the age grows ever more disordered. Why? Because those above them do not follow the Way.

A working song says: "We plow in spring, we weed in summer, we harvest in autumn, we store in winter -- yet we have not enough to eat. Whose fault is this?"

Another says: "When the ruler is wise, the people are at ease. When the ruler is foolish, the people suffer. When rites and righteousness prevail, the people are orderly. When they are neglected, the people are in turmoil."

The gentleman hears the songs of the people and knows the state of government. When the songs are joyful, the government is good. When the songs are bitter, the government is bad.

A song of longing: "We long for a wise ruler as the dry field longs for rain. If a sage were to arise, the world would be transformed as earth is transformed by spring."

A song of instruction: "Do not be like the river that overflows its banks -- rushing forward without measure. Do not be like the mountain that stands alone -- proud and isolated. Be like the valley that receives all streams and nourishes all things."

These songs embody the wisdom of the common people. The philosopher must listen to them, for in them lies the truth about government.
