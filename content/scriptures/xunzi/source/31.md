## Ai Gong (Duke Ai)

Duke Ai of Lu asked Confucius: "I wish to select among my people and appoint those who are worthy so that the state may be well governed. How should I go about making my selection?"

Confucius replied: "In the present age, many men speak of propriety and righteousness but when they return home, they do not practice what they preach. If you wish to know whether a man is worthy, do not listen to his words alone. Observe his conduct."

"Watch what he does when he is at ease. Observe what he takes pleasure in. Examine what he recommends when he is in a position of influence."

"If he is poor, observe whether he refrains from dishonesty. If he is rich, observe whether he refrains from arrogance. If he is in a position of power, observe whether he refrains from oppression."

"Put him under pressure and observe whether he maintains his principles. Give him leisure and observe whether he remains disciplined. Test him with joy and observe whether he remains moderate. Test him with anger and observe whether he maintains self-control."

"These are the means by which the worthy may be known. For the worthy man is consistent in all circumstances. His virtue does not change with his fortune."

Duke Ai said: "Excellent! If I follow this advice, I will be able to distinguish the worthy from the unworthy, and my state will be well governed."
