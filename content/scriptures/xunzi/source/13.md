## Chen Dao (The Way of the Minister)

The Way of the minister lies in loyalty -- but not blind obedience. The true minister is loyal to the Way, not merely to the ruler's person.

There are ministers who serve by following orders. There are ministers who serve by offering counsel. There are ministers who serve by admonishing. The highest form of ministerial service is admonition.

When the ruler is right, the minister follows. When the ruler is wrong, the minister remonstrates. If the ruler will not listen after repeated remonstrance, the minister may withdraw from service. This is the Way of the loyal minister.

The minister who flatters the ruler and agrees with everything is not loyal but traitorous. He appears to serve the ruler but in fact he harms him. He appears to love the ruler but in fact he leads him to ruin.

There are three types of ministers to guard against: The sycophant, who tells the ruler what he wants to hear. The self-seeker, who uses his office for personal gain. The factional partisan, who serves his clique rather than the state.

The true minister is upright and outspoken. He risks the ruler's displeasure to speak the truth. He values the welfare of the state above his own safety. He serves the public interest, not private gain.

If the ruler follows the Way, the minister serves with joy. If the ruler departs from the Way, the minister serves with sorrow but does not abandon his post until all recourse is exhausted.
