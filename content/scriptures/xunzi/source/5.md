## Fei Xiang (Against Physiognomy)

Physiognomy is not as important as evaluating a man's heart. Evaluating the heart is not as important as choosing the right Way. A man's appearance does not determine his heart. His heart determines his Way.

If his Way is right, even if his appearance is ugly, it does not harm his being a gentleman. If his Way is wrong, even if his appearance is handsome, it does not prevent his being a petty man.

The ancient sage kings did not judge men by their appearance. They judged them by their conduct and their virtue.

Consider: The Duke of Zhou was not handsome, and Confucius was of unusual appearance. Yet they were the greatest sages. Consider: King Jie and King Zhou were both men of impressive stature, yet they were the worst tyrants.

Therefore the wise ruler does not trust physiognomy. He examines a man's words and observes his actions. He determines whether the man's conduct accords with propriety and righteousness. He sees whether the man is consistent in his behavior over time.

The appearance of a man is given by heaven and cannot be changed. But his heart and his conduct are within his own power. Therefore the gentleman focuses his effort on what he can control -- his virtue, his learning, his conduct -- and does not waste his attention on what he cannot.

Physiognomy is the art of the ignorant. The gentleman does not practice it.
