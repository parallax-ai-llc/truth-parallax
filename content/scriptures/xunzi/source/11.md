## Wang Ba (Kings and Hegemons)

There are three types of rulers: the true king, the hegemon, and the one who merely survives.

The true king wins the hearts of the people through virtue. The hegemon wins the cooperation of the feudal lords through good faith. The mere survivor clings to power through cleverness and force.

The true king does not need to fight. When he raises an army, it is to punish wrongdoing, and the people of the enemy state welcome him. His soldiers do not need to bloody their blades, because the opposing forces lay down their arms.

The hegemon must use force, but he uses it with restraint and in accordance with covenant agreements. He maintains alliances through the faithful observance of treaties. He is feared but also trusted.

The mere survivor uses deception and brute force. He breaks treaties when it suits him. He is feared but not trusted. His state may prosper for a time, but ultimately it will fall.

The secret of the true king: He treats the people as his children. He loves them with sincerity. Therefore the people serve him as they would a parent. When the people serve the ruler as they serve a parent, what enemy can stand against them?

The Way of the true king is this: Use virtue to win hearts. Use propriety to order society. Use righteousness to guide policy. Use humanity to treat the people. This is the Way that has never failed.
