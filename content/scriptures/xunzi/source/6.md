## Fei Shi Er Zi (Against the Twelve Masters)

In our age there are men who dress up false teachings and fraudulent doctrines, presenting them as the true Way. They confuse the distinctions between right and wrong, so that the people are led astray.

Zisi and Mencius followed the Way partially but without completeness. They perceived a corner of the great principle and thought it was the whole. With some small knowledge and no great insights, they were nevertheless self-satisfied and self-assured.

Mo Di understood utility but not culture. Song Xing understood desire but not reverence. Shen Dao understood following but not leading. Hui Shi and Deng Xi understood language but not substance.

Zhuangzi was blinded by the idea of Heaven and did not understand the importance of man. If you know only of Heaven and do not understand man, you cannot govern the world.

Only Confucius and the Duke of Zhou perceived the whole. They combined culture with substance, propriety with humanity, order with freedom. Their Way encompassed all things without partiality.

The error of the twelve masters was this: each grasped one aspect of the truth and mistook it for the whole. The complete Way must encompass all aspects -- heaven and man, culture and substance, the individual and society, the inner and the outer.

The gentleman learns broadly and examines each teaching carefully. He does not follow any teacher blindly, but seeks the complete truth.
