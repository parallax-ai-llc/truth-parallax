## Zheng Ming (The Rectification of Names)

The reason why names were instituted: It was to denote actualities, so that on the one hand, the noble and the base might be made clear, and on the other, similarities and differences might be distinguished.

When the noble and base are clear, and similarities and differences are distinguished, there is no danger that men's intentions will be misunderstood, and no danger that affairs will suffer from ambiguity or neglect.

The ancient kings chose names that directly corresponded to actualities. But now that the sage kings are no more, names are confused and actualities are obscured. Standards of right and wrong are unclear, and even officials who apply the law and scholars who recite the texts are all in a muddle.

There are three fallacies in the use of names: Using names to confuse names. Using actualities to confuse names. Using names to confuse actualities.

The test of correctness in naming: When the name is heard, the actuality should be understood. This is the usefulness of names. When names and actualities correspond, the people can communicate without misunderstanding.

General names refer to categories: "Horse" refers to all horses. Specific names refer to individuals. The relationship between general and specific names must be clearly maintained.

The rectification of names is the foundation of all government. When names are correct, discourse is clear. When discourse is clear, affairs can be properly managed. When affairs are properly managed, the people live in peace.
