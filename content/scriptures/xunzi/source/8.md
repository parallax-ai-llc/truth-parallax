## Ru Xiao (The Achievements of the Confucians)

There are vulgar Confucians, refined Confucians, and great Confucians. The vulgar Confucian imitates the outward forms but does not understand the inner substance. The refined Confucian understands the principles but cannot fully realize them. The great Confucian both understands and realizes.

The great Confucian takes the ancient kings as his model, takes propriety and righteousness as his standards, and takes the way of the former kings as his guide. He regulates all things within the four seas.

If he holds a small office, he brings order to a small domain. If he holds a great office, he brings order to the whole world. He has never been known to be diminished by poverty, nor corrupted by wealth.

The vulgar Confucian memorizes texts without understanding them. He observes rites without knowing their meaning. He speaks of the ancients but cannot apply their teachings to the present.

The great Confucian adapts the ancient principles to the present circumstances. He understands that the Way is not rigid but responds to changing conditions while maintaining its essential character.

When the great Confucian holds power, laws are just and penalties are fair. When he retires from office, his personal conduct remains a model for others. In prosperity he shares with others; in adversity he endures alone. This is the mark of the great Confucian.
