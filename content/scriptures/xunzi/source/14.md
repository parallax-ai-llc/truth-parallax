## Zhi Shi (On Attracting Men of Worth)

The wise ruler attracts worthy men to his service. The foolish ruler drives them away.

How does the ruler attract the worthy? By honoring virtue, rewarding merit, being generous in his treatment of officials, and being sincere in his dealings.

When worthy men hear that a ruler honors virtue, they come from distant places to serve him. When they hear that he rewards merit, they exert themselves to the utmost. When they hear that he is generous, they remain loyal. When they hear that he is sincere, they trust him.

How does the ruler drive away the worthy? By honoring flattery, rewarding trickery, being stingy in his treatment of officials, and being deceitful in his dealings.

The worthy man is like water -- he flows toward the low ground. If the ruler humbles himself, the worthy will come to him. If the ruler is arrogant, the worthy will avoid him.

The ruler who wishes to attract worthy men must first make himself worthy. He must cultivate his own virtue before he can recognize virtue in others.

Five things that attract worthy men to serve: Clear distinction between right and wrong. Fair system of rewards and punishments. Generous provision for officials. Sincere respect for the learned. Willingness to listen to criticism.

Five things that repel worthy men: Confusion of right and wrong. Arbitrary rewards and punishments. Stinginess toward officials. Contempt for learning. Intolerance of criticism.
