Confucian interpretation of Xunzi chapter 1, "Encouraging Learning," reads this text as a contribution to the ongoing Confucian project of moral transformation through ritual and education. Xunzi's arguments here develop the conviction that human beings are not born virtuous but must be shaped through accumulated effort, disciplined practice, and submission to the guidance of worthy teachers and the ritual traditions of the sage kings.

The chapter demonstrates Xunzi's characteristic method of rigorous argumentation combined with vivid analogy. His insistence that moral achievement requires conscious effort (wei) does not diminish its value but rather enhances it — for the Confucian reader, the fact that virtue must be won through sustained struggle makes it all the more admirable. The exemplary person (junzi) is not one who was born good but one who has labored to become good through the transformative power of ritual and learning.
