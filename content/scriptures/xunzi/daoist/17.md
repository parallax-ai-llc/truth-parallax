Daoist engagement with Xunzi chapter 17, "A Discussion of Heaven," highlights the fundamental tension between Xunzi's confidence in deliberate human effort and the Daoist ideal of effortless harmony with the natural Way. Where Xunzi argues that human nature must be actively reshaped through ritual discipline, Daoist readers counter that such artificial intervention creates the very disharmony it seeks to remedy.

Yet this chapter also reveals moments where Xunzi's naturalism approaches Daoist sensibilities. His recognition that heaven (tian) operates according to its own patterns, indifferent to human moral concerns, echoes the Daoist understanding of the Way as beyond human categories of good and evil. The Daoist reader finds here an interlocutor who shares certain premises but draws radically different conclusions about how humans should respond to the natural order.
