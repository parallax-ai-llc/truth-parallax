Section 3 describes the self beyond the mind — pure consciousness that remains when all mental activity has ceased. Shaiva interpreters recognize this as Shiva in His absolute nature (*Paramashiva*) — the awareness that is the ground of all experience but is never itself an object of experience. The meditation on Om has led the practitioner to the silence beyond all sound, which is the Shaiva *anuttara* (the unsurpassable).

The teaching that Brahman is beyond the mind confirms the Shaiva principle that consciousness transcends its own products. The mind is a creation of consciousness, not the other way around. When consciousness withdraws from its mental creations, it recognizes itself as unlimited.
