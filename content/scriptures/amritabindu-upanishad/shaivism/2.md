Section 2's meditation on Om is read by Shaiva interpreters as a practice of dissolving thought-constructs through the sacred sound. Om in its fullness contains all sounds and therefore all experiences — it is the complete expression of consciousness in sonic form. Meditation on Om is the process of allowing awareness to expand beyond its limited identifications until it recognizes its own infinite nature.

The three sounds of Om (A-U-M) and the silence after them correspond to the four states of consciousness taught in the Mandukya Upanishad. The silence — the fourth state (*turiya*) — is Shiva's own nature: pure awareness beyond all vibration.
