Section 1's teaching on the two states of mind is read through the Shaiva lens as describing consciousness in its contracted and expanded modes. The mind attached to objects is awareness identified with its content — the fundamental error that Kashmir Shaivism calls *anavamala* (the impurity of finiteness). The mind freed from objects is awareness resting in its own nature — the natural state of Shiva.

The section establishes that the difference between bondage and liberation is not a difference in substance but in orientation. The same consciousness that appears as a limited mind becomes unlimited awareness when its identification with objects dissolves.
