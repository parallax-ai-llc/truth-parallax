Section 1 declares the Amritabindu's central teaching: the mind attached to sense objects leads to bondage, while the mind freed from objects leads to liberation. Vaishnava commentators read this as affirming the importance of redirecting the mind from material enjoyment to divine contemplation. The mind is like a river — if it flows toward sense objects, it leads to the ocean of samsara; if it flows toward God, it leads to the ocean of bliss.

The teaching that the mind is the cause of both bondage and liberation supports the Vaishnava emphasis on constant remembrance of God (*smaranam*) as the primary spiritual practice. A mind filled with God has no room for the attachments that cause suffering.
