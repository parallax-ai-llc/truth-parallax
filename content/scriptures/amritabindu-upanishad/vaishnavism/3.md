Section 3 culminates in the realization of Brahman beyond the mind — the supreme reality that the purified mind finally apprehends. Vaishnava commentators read this as the devotee's direct vision (*darshana*) of the Lord, which transcends mental conception. The mind, purified through meditation on Om, becomes transparent to the divine presence that was always there but hidden by attachment.

The description of Brahman as beyond the senses, beyond thought, and beyond change is read as describing Vishnu in His transcendent aspect — the Lord who exceeds all human comprehension yet graciously reveals Himself to the devoted heart.
