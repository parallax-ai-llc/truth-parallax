Section 2 provides practical instructions for meditation on Om as the means to free the mind from attachment. Vaishnava commentators read Om as the sonic form of Vishnu — the sacred syllable that contains the Lord's name and nature. Meditation on Om is thus meditation on God, and the progressive refinement of attention described here is the devotee's journey from external worship to internal communion.

The teaching that Om has three constituent sounds (A-U-M) corresponding to three states of consciousness (waking, dreaming, deep sleep) is understood as pointing to Vishnu's presence in all states of the devotee's experience. God is not only the object of meditation but the ground of all awareness.
