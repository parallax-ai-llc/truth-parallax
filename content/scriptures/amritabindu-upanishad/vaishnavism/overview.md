## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Amritabindu Upanishad's teaching on the mind as confirming the necessity of directing the mind toward God. A mind scattered among sense objects is in bondage; a mind focused on Vishnu attains the nectar of liberation. The Upanishad's meditation on Om is understood as meditation on the Lord, since Om is Vishnu's primary sound-form.

## Theological Emphases

The teaching that the mind is both the cause of bondage and liberation supports the Vaishnava emphasis on *bhakti* (devotion) as mind-purification. When the mind is filled with thoughts of God — His names, forms, qualities, and pastimes — it naturally detaches from material objects and becomes the vehicle for divine communion. The "drop of nectar" (*amritabindu*) is the taste of God's presence that the purified mind experiences.
