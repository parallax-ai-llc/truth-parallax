---
title: "Amritabindu Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "amritabindu-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Self, smaller than the small, greater than the great, is hidden in the heart of each creature. A man who has left all grief behind sees the majesty of the Self, by the grace of the Creator.

**2** Though sitting still, He walks far; though lying down, He goes everywhere. Who save myself can know that God who rejoices and rejoices not?

**3** Knowing the Self, the bodiless in bodies, the firm among the infirm, the great and all-pervading, the wise man never grieves.

**4** This Self is not to be obtained by one who is destitute of fortitude, nor through heedlessness, nor without distinct concentration. But if a wise man strives after it by those means, his self enters the abode of Brahman.

**5** Having attained this, the seers become satisfied with knowledge, their purpose accomplished, free from passion, tranquil. Having attained the all-pervading on every side, calm, with integrated selves, they enter into the All itself.

**6** Those who have ascertained the meaning of the Vedanta knowledge, who are Sannyasins, purified in heart through the Yoga of renunciation — at the time of the great end, they are all liberated in the worlds of Brahman, beyond death.

**7** The fifteen parts return to their foundations, and all the senses to the corresponding elements. The actions and the Self with knowledge become all one in the highest Imperishable.

**8** As the flowing rivers disappear in the ocean, casting off name and form, even so the knower, being freed from name and form, reaches the divine Person, higher than the high. He who knows that highest Brahman becomes Brahman himself. Thus ends the Amritabindu Upanishad.
