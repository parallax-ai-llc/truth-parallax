---
title: "Amritabindu Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "amritabindu-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The mind alone is the cause of bondage and the mind alone is the cause of liberation. When attached to objects, it leads to bondage; when freed from objects, it leads to liberation.

**2** As milk, when once turned to curds, never becomes milk again, so the mind, when once it has attained Yoga, never returns to its former state of bondage.

**3** The syllable Om is the bow, the Self is the arrow, and Brahman is called its mark. It is to be hit by a man who is not thoughtless; and then, as the arrow becomes one with the target, the Self becomes one with Brahman.

**4** In whom the sky, the earth, and the atmosphere are interwoven, and also the mind, together with all the life-breaths — know Him alone as the one Self. Dismiss other words; this is the bridge to immortality.

**5** Where the arteries of the body are brought together, like the spokes in the centre of a wheel — there He moves about, becoming manifold. Meditate on the Self as Om.

**6** He who knows this, which is hidden in the cave of the heart, he cuts the knot of ignorance here in this world. The Self-existent pierced the openings of the senses so that they turn outward; therefore man looks outward, not inward at himself. Some wise man, however, with his eyes closed and wishing for immortality, saw the Self behind.

**7** The wise who know the Self, which is bodiless among bodies, unchanging among changing things, great and all-pervading — they never grieve.

**8** This Self cannot be obtained by instruction, nor by intellectual power, nor through much hearing. He is obtained only by the one whom the Self chooses; to such a one the Self reveals Its own nature.
