---
title: "Amritabindu Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "amritabindu-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The mind is said to be twofold: the pure and also the impure. It is impure by union with desire; it is pure when from desire completely free.

**2** When the mind, having been made firm by steady practice, becomes motionless, then the mind attains the state of Unmani, the supreme goal.

**3** The mind should be restrained in the heart till it reaches its dissolution; this is Jnana, this is Dhyana — all else is mere extension of ties.

**4** It is not to be thought of; it is not to be thought of not; it is not to be thought of as both; it is not to be thought of as neither. By this the Yogi attains emancipation.

**5** Yoga is the going and the coming of the mind. When it neither comes nor goes, and the mind has become steady and motionless, that is the highest state.

**6** The mind should be restrained so long until it is dissolved in the heart. This is knowledge and liberation; all the rest is mere bondage.

**7** That happiness which belongs to the mind which by deep meditation has been washed clean of all impurity and has entered within the Self, cannot be described by words; it can be felt by the inward power only.

**8** Water in water, fire in fire, ether in ether — no one can distinguish them. Similarly, the soul who has entered the Supreme Spirit, none can perceive as separate.
