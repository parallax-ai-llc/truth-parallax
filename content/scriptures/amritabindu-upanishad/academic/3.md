Section 3's description of Brahman beyond the mind reflects the broader Upanishadic tendency toward apophatic theology — defining the ultimate reality by what it is not. The teaching that Brahman transcends sensory perception, mental conception, and verbal description anticipates the *neti neti* ("not this, not this") approach of the Brihadaranyaka and the systematic negation of later Advaita.

Scholars note the text's logical progression: bondage is diagnosed as mental attachment (Section 1), a cure is prescribed through Om meditation (Section 2), and the result is described as the realization of a reality beyond the mind (Section 3). This clear structure suggests a pedagogical text designed for systematic instruction.
