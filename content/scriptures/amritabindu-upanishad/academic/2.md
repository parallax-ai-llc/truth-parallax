Section 2's Om meditation instructions connect the Amritabindu to the broader tradition of Om-based meditation in Indian spirituality. The analysis of Om into three constituent sounds (A-U-M) and their correlation with states of consciousness follows the pattern established in the Mandukya Upanishad and elaborated in Gaudapada's Karika.

Scholars note that the Amritabindu's meditation instructions are more practical and less philosophical than the Mandukya's analysis, suggesting a text designed for practitioners rather than theorists. The combination of theoretical framework (the mind causes bondage and liberation) with practical technique (meditate on Om) is characteristic of the Yoga Upanishads as a genre.
