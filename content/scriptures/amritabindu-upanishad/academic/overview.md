## Overview

The Amritabindu Upanishad is classified among the Yoga Upanishads, a group of minor Upanishads that focus on meditation techniques. Scholars date it to approximately the 2nd-3rd century CE. Its teaching on the mind as the source of both bondage and liberation anticipates the central concern of Patanjali's Yoga Sutras (*yogash chitta-vritti-nirodhah* — "yoga is the cessation of mental fluctuations").

## Research Themes

The text's psychological insight — that the mind's relationship to its objects determines the quality of consciousness — represents an important development in Indian philosophy of mind. The Amritabindu's concise teaching may have served as a practical manual for meditators, providing both theoretical framework and practical technique in a memorizable format. Its influence can be traced through later Yoga and Vedanta texts.
