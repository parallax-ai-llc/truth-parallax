The meditation on Om as a sacred sound finds parallels across traditions. The Christian mystical tradition of the "prayer of the heart" (a single phrase repeated until it merges with consciousness), the Sufi *dhikr* (repetition of a divine name until the rememberer disappears into the remembered), the Jewish practice of *hitbodedut* (meditative prayer), and the Pure Land Buddhist *nembutsu* (invocation of Amitabha's name) all use repeated sacred sounds to transform consciousness.

The Om meditation's progression from gross sound to subtle resonance to silence parallels the Christian contemplative progression from vocal prayer to meditation to contemplation, and the Sufi stages from the *dhikr* of the tongue to the *dhikr* of the heart to the *dhikr* of the soul.
