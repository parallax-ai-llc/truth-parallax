Section 1's teaching that the mind is both the problem and the solution finds echoes across contemplative traditions. The Buddhist *Dhammapada* opens with: "Mind is the forerunner of all actions. All deeds are led by mind, created by mind." The Christian mystic Meister Eckhart taught that the same soul that falls into sin can rise to God through *Gelassenheit* (letting go). The Sufi tradition teaches that the *nafs* (ego-self) that leads to sin is also the *nafs* that, when purified, becomes transparent to God.

The image of the mind as a double-edged instrument — capable of causing both suffering and liberation — parallels the Buddhist concept of the "sword of prajna" (wisdom) that cuts through delusion, and the Christian concept of the Word of God as a "two-edged sword" (Hebrews 4:12).
