Madhva reads Section 3 as describing the God who transcends all mental conception — Vishnu in His supreme, ineffable nature. The mind purified through meditation can receive a glimpse of God's glory, but God Himself always exceeds what the mind can contain. This is not a limitation but a testimony to God's infinite greatness.

For Dvaita, the realization described here is not the dissolution of the self into Brahman but the soul's overwhelming encounter with the divine — the experience of God's infinite perfection that fills the purified mind with wonder and devotion.
