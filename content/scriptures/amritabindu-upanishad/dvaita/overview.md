## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Amritabindu's teaching on mind as affirming the necessity of mental purification for receiving God's grace. The mind attached to sense objects is unfit to receive divine knowledge; the mind purified through meditation and devotion becomes the vessel for Vishnu's self-revelation.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the mind is the cause of bondage because its attachment to material objects prevents the soul from recognizing its dependence on God. It is the cause of liberation because, when properly directed through devotion and meditation, it becomes the instrument through which God communicates His grace. The meditation on Om is valued as a means of concentrating the mind on God, not as a technique for dissolving the mind into an impersonal absolute.
