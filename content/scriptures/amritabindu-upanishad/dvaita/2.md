Madhva reads the Om meditation as a devotional practice that concentrates the mind on God. The sacred syllable is not an impersonal vibration but the sound-form of Vishnu's name. Repeating and meditating on Om with devotion draws the Lord's grace, which purifies the mind and prepares it for receiving divine knowledge.

For Dvaita, the three sounds of Om represent not three states of consciousness but three aspects of God's nature: A for Vishnu as creator, U for Vishnu as sustainer, and M for Vishnu as the guide of all souls. The meditation leads not to the dissolution of the mind into an impersonal silence but to the clarification of the mind's relationship with the personal God.
