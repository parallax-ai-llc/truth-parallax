## Overview

The Amritabindu Upanishad ("Drop of Nectar" Upanishad) belongs to the Krishna Yajur Veda and is classified among the Yoga Upanishads. This concise and powerful text teaches that the mind is the cause of both bondage and liberation: a mind attached to sense objects leads to bondage, while a mind freed from objects attains the nectar (*amrita*) of immortality. The Upanishad provides meditation instructions focused on Om as the means to purify and transcend the mind.

## Structure

The Amritabindu Upanishad consists of three sections comprising approximately 22 verses:

1. **Section 1 — The Two States of Mind**: The mind as the cause of bondage and liberation
2. **Section 2 — Meditation on Om**: Practical instructions for using Om to transcend the mind
3. **Section 3 — The Nature of Brahman**: The realization of the self as beyond the mind

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: The mind purified through devotion becomes the vehicle of divine communion
- **Shaivism**: Mind as contracted consciousness that expands through recognition
- **Advaita Vedanta**: The mind transcended reveals the non-dual self beyond all thought
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: The purified mind as the prerequisite for receiving God's grace
- **Academic**: Important source for early Indian psychology and the philosophy of mind
- **Comparative**: Mind-purification parallels across contemplative traditions worldwide

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Mind as Bondage and Liberation | The same mind that binds can also free |
| Meditation on Om | Using the sacred syllable to transcend mental fluctuations |
| The Drop of Nectar | The experience of immortality through mental stillness |
| Beyond the Mind | The self as the witness prior to all mental activity |
| Sense Control | Withdrawing the mind from sense objects |
