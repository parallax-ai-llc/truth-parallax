## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Amritabindu Upanishad provides a concise statement of the relationship between mind, bondage, and liberation. The famous verse "The mind alone is the cause of bondage and liberation" (*mana eva manushyanam karanam bandha-mokshayoh*) is frequently cited in Advaita literature. Bondage is the mind's projection of duality where none exists; liberation is the mind's cessation of projection, revealing the non-dual Brahman.

## Theological Emphases

The Advaita reading emphasizes that the mind does not create Brahman through meditation — it merely removes the obstacles to recognizing what is already the case. The meditation on Om is a means of stilling the mental fluctuations (*vrittis*) that obscure the witness-consciousness. When the mind becomes perfectly still, the self shines by its own light. The "drop of nectar" is not a new acquisition but the revelation of what was always present.
