For Advaita, Section 2's meditation on Om is the premier technique for transcending the mind. Om is the symbol of Brahman — not in the sense of representing something absent but in the sense of being the direct sonic expression of the absolute. Meditation on Om progressively refines attention from the gross sound through its subtle resonance to the silence beyond it, which is Brahman.

The three constituents of Om (A-U-M) corresponding to the three states of consciousness (waking, dreaming, deep sleep) point to the fourth state (*turiya*) — the witness of all three states, which is the self. The meditation on Om thus becomes a journey from the manifest to the unmanifest, from sound to silence, from the mind to Brahman.
