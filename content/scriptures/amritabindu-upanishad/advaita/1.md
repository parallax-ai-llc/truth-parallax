Section 1 contains the Amritabindu's most famous teaching and one of Advaita's most cited verses: the mind is the cause of both bondage and liberation. For Shankara's tradition, this means that bondage is nothing but the mind's projection of duality onto the non-dual Brahman, and liberation is the cessation of this projection. The self is never actually bound — only the mind creates the appearance of bondage.

The teaching implies that liberation does not require any change in the self (which is already Brahman) but only a change in the mind's activity — specifically, its cessation of projecting separateness and multiplicity onto the undivided reality.
