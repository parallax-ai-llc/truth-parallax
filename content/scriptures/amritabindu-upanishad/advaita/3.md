Section 3 presents the Amritabindu's highest teaching: the self realized when the mind is transcended is Brahman — infinite, undivided, beyond all qualities. For Advaita, this is the culmination of the meditation process: the mind, purified and stilled through meditation on Om, dissolves into the pure awareness that is its source. What remains is not nothing but everything — the fullness of Brahman.

The teaching that Brahman is beyond the mind is crucial for Advaita: it means that Brahman cannot be known as an object of thought but can be *realized* as the subject — the knower behind all knowing. This realization is liberation.
