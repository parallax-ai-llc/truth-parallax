The vision of an entire sacred realm displayed within ordinary space resonates with mystical experiences reported across traditions. The Christian tradition of Hildegard of Bingen includes visions of the cosmos contained within the body of Christ. The Hindu tradition describes Yashoda seeing the entire universe within the mouth of the infant Krishna. William Blake's vision of "a world in a grain of sand" expresses the same intuition in Romantic poetry.

The concept of multiple sacred realms coexisting with the ordinary world parallels the Kabbalistic teaching on the four worlds (Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, Assiah), the Islamic cosmology of multiple heavens, and the Hindu understanding of multiple lokas (worlds) accessible through different states of consciousness. Each tradition recognizes that the visible world is only one dimension of a multi-layered reality.

Akshobhya's realm as a vision of perfected community resonates with utopian visions across traditions: the Christian New Jerusalem, the Islamic garden of paradise, the Confucian vision of the Great Unity (Da Tong), and Plato's ideal Republic. Each represents the aspiration for a world where spiritual principles are fully embodied in social reality.
