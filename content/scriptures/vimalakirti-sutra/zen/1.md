The opening chapter's revelation that the buddha land's purity depends on the mind's purity strikes at the heart of Zen teaching. When Shakyamuni touches the earth and reveals the hidden splendor of the world, this is the original face before your parents were born. The impure world and the pure land are not two. This is not a philosophical proposition but a direct pointing.

Zen master Zhaozhou was asked, "Does a dog have buddha-nature?" He answered "Wu!" (No!/Nothing!). The Buddha Land chapter of the Vimalakirti Sutra teaches the same thing from another angle: this very world, with all its dust and confusion, is itself the pure land when seen with clear eyes. Nothing needs to be added or removed.

The chapter challenges the practitioner to look at their immediate experience right now and ask: is purity something to be attained in the future, or is it already present in this very moment? Zen practice begins and ends with this question.
