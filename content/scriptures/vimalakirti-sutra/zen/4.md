Even the bodhisattvas are reluctant. This is the sutra's most radical teaching from a Zen perspective: not even exalted spiritual attainment is safe from Vimalakirti's dharma combat. Maitreya, the future Buddha himself, was told that his prediction of future buddhahood is based on illusion. Past mind cannot be grasped, present mind cannot be grasped, future mind cannot be grasped.

This teaching cuts through spiritual ambition at its root. The Zen practitioner who sits with the expectation of future enlightenment is in the same position as Maitreya clinging to his prediction. Zhaozhou said, "If you have a staff, I will give you a staff. If you have no staff, I will take it away." The future buddha who still holds onto "future buddhahood" needs it taken away.

The chapter demolishes the entire framework of spiritual progress. There is no attainment and no non-attainment. The only thing to do is to meet this moment completely, without reference to where one has been or where one is going.
