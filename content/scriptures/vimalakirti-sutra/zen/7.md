The goddess who transforms Shariputra into a woman cuts through all views about identity with the directness of a Zen master. When Shariputra protests, she does not argue. She acts. This is the Zen method: do not explain, demonstrate. When words fail, a shout or a slap will do. The goddess uses transformation.

The teaching that gender is empty of inherent nature extends to all categories of identity. Male, female, monk, layperson, teacher, student, enlightened, deluded: all of these are empty designations painted onto the seamless fabric of reality. The goddess does not teach this through philosophy but through direct experience, which is always more convincing than any argument.

Zen Master Zhaozhou was asked, "Does a newborn baby have consciousness?" This question, like the goddess's transformation of Shariputra, probes beneath the layers of assumed identity to the original nature that precedes all categories. What were you before your parents were born? What are you right now, stripped of every label?
