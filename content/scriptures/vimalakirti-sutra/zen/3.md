The disciples' reluctance to face Vimalakirti is a delicious Zen comedy. These are the Buddha's greatest students, the cream of the sangha, and they are all afraid of one layman. Each tells a story of being thoroughly defeated in a dharma exchange. From a Zen perspective, their defeats are their greatest teachings.

Shariputra was sitting quietly in meditation when Vimalakirti challenged him: "Meditation is not necessarily sitting, and sitting is not necessarily meditation." This single sentence demolishes the common misunderstanding that Zen practice means sitting on a cushion with a blank mind. True samadhi is not disturbed by action, and true action does not disturb samadhi.

Each disciple's story functions as an independent koan. The practitioner can take any of these encounters and work with it: What is meditation without sitting? What is giving without discrimination? What is the dharma that has no fixed form? These questions, when held with the whole body and mind, become gateways to direct realization.
