Vimalakirti lifts Akshobhya's entire buddha realm on the palm of his hand. This is not a feat of supernatural power but a demonstration of what Zen calls "the identity of the absolute and the relative." The whole universe sits on a single palm. A sesame seed contains Mount Sumeru. This very body is the body of Buddha.

The name Akshobhya means "Immovable" or "Unshakable." In Zen, the immovable mind is not a rigid mind but a mind that moves freely without being moved. It responds to everything without being disturbed by anything. This is the samadhi that Vimalakirti demonstrates throughout the sutra: perfect stillness in perfect activity.

Zen Master Dongshan was asked, "What is Buddha?" He answered, "Three pounds of flax." Vimalakirti answers the same question by holding up an entire buddha realm. Both responses point beyond the question to direct experience. The vision of Akshobhya's realm is not something to look at but something to look from.
