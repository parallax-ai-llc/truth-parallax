The emanation bodhisattva's journey to a distant buddha land for fragrant rice is a Zen koan in narrative form. Where is this distant land? How far must one travel? In Zen, the answer is always: it is right here. The entire journey across countless buddha fields happens in the space between two breaths. It happens in the space of no-space.

The Fragrant Land, where the buddha teaches through fragrance rather than words, points to the Zen understanding that dharma is not limited to verbal instruction. The scent of incense in the meditation hall, the taste of tea in the tearoom, the sound of a bell at dawn: each is a complete teaching, lacking nothing. The six sense gates are all dharma gates.

The meal that satisfies all present without being exhausted resembles the master's teaching that never runs dry. A single word from a realized teacher can nourish a lifetime of practice. The fragrant rice is the dharma itself, offered freely and received completely by those with the capacity to taste it.
