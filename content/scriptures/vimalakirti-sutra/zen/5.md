When Manjushri arrives at Vimalakirti's sickroom, two lions meet face to face. The dialogue that follows is a Zen dharma combat of the highest order, with each exchange peeling away another layer of conceptual understanding. Vimalakirti's teaching that his body is empty, like foam, like a flame, like a reflection, is not philosophy. It is an invitation to look directly.

The question "Where does the bodhisattva's illness come from?" and the answer "From great compassion" reveal the inseparability of wisdom and compassion in Zen. The bodhisattva does not fall ill despite having wisdom but because of having compassion. This is the meaning of the Zen vow: "Sentient beings are numberless; I vow to save them."

Zen practitioners may recognize in this dialogue the pattern of a mondo (question and answer) exchange that characterizes the encounter literature (yulu) of the Chan tradition. Each question is not seeking information but testing realization, and each answer arises from the depths of practice.
