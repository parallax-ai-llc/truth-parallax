The bodhisattvas' reluctance to visit Vimalakirti extends the sutra's teaching beyond the Sravaka-Mahayana distinction to suggest that even conventional bodhisattva understanding falls short of Vimalakirti's realization. Maitreya recounts being told that his prediction of future buddhahood is based on a mistaken understanding of time and identity.

This chapter deepens the Mahayana teaching on emptiness by showing that even exalted bodhisattva concepts such as prediction, stages of practice, and future attainment are empty of inherent existence. Vimalakirti challenges each bodhisattva to recognize that their understanding, while advanced, still contains subtle reifications.

The progressive structure, from disciples to bodhisattvas, mirrors the Mahayana understanding of deepening levels of realization. Just as the Heart Sutra negates even the categories of the path itself, this chapter demonstrates that non-dual wisdom requires relinquishing attachment to every spiritual concept, including the concept of the bodhisattva path.
