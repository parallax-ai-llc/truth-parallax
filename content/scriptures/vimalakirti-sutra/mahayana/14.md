The final chapter records the Buddha's formal entrustment of the sutra's teaching to Maitreya, the future Buddha, and to Ananda, the keeper of the dharma. This entrustment establishes the text's authority within the Mahayana canon and ensures its transmission to future generations of practitioners.

The Buddha teaches that bodhisattvas should recognize two types of practitioners: those who delight in elaborate literary expressions of the dharma and those who are not intimidated by profound and difficult teachings. Both types are to be supported, but the sutra emphasizes that those who can penetrate its deep meaning are rare and precious.

The entrustment also serves as a teaching on the impermanence of the dharma itself. The Buddha acknowledges that authentic teachings may be distorted or lost over time, and therefore charges the assembly with the responsibility of preserving the sutra's meaning. This awareness of dharma decline reflects the Mahayana understanding that the teaching must be actively maintained against the forces of degeneration.
