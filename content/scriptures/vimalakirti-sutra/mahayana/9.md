The emanation sent to the buddha land of Sarvagandhasugandha to obtain fragrant rice demonstrates the Mahayana cosmology of infinite buddha lands, each expressing the unique qualities of its presiding buddha. In the Fragrant Land, the buddha teaches through fragrance rather than words, revealing that the dharma can be communicated through any sense faculty.

The emanation bodhisattva's journey across countless buddha fields illustrates the Mahayana vision of a cosmos pervaded by awakened activity. Each buddha land reflects different skillful means appropriate to the beings who dwell there. This diversity of methods is itself a teaching on upaya, showing that liberation is not bound to any single form of instruction.

The fragrant rice nourishes the assembly both physically and spiritually, blurring the distinction between material sustenance and dharma teaching. This reflects the Mahayana understanding that the bodhisattva's every action, including eating, can become a vehicle for awakening when pervaded by wisdom and compassion.
