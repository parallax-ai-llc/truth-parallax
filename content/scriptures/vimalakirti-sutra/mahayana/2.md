Vimalakirti's skillful means represent the Mahayana ideal of upaya-kausalya at its most creative and compelling. As a wealthy householder who frequents wine shops, gambling houses, and courts of power, Vimalakirti demonstrates that the bodhisattva's sphere of activity encompasses all of samsara. His apparent illness is itself a supreme skillful means, creating the occasion for the teachings that follow.

The Mahayana doctrine of skillful means teaches that awakened beings may employ any method, even those that appear to violate conventional morality, when motivated by genuine compassion and guided by wisdom. Vimalakirti's deception about his illness is justified because it serves the liberation of beings who come to console him.

This chapter establishes Vimalakirti as the model lay bodhisattva, demonstrating that full awakening and the exercise of skillful means are not limited to monastics or even to beings who appear to be practicing Buddhism. The bodhisattva's work of liberation pervades every dimension of society and culture.
