The encounter between Manjushri and Vimalakirti is the heart of the sutra, presenting a dialogue between the bodhisattva of wisdom and a lay practitioner who embodies the integration of wisdom and skillful means. Vimalakirti teaches that his illness is empty, arising from the compassionate identification with suffering beings.

The teaching on the emptiness of illness is a practical application of the Prajnaparamita doctrine: just as form is emptiness, illness is emptiness, and the body that suffers is without inherent existence. Yet this recognition does not lead to indifference but to deepened compassion, because the bodhisattva understands that beings suffer precisely through their attachment to the reality of what is empty.

Manjushri's questions draw out the Mahayana understanding that the bodhisattva's body is not an individual possession but a manifestation of the dharma-body, arising from compassion and wisdom for the benefit of all beings. This teaching transforms the experience of illness from personal suffering into an expression of the bodhisattva's liberating activity.
