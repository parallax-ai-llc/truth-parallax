The Dharma Gate of Non-duality is the philosophical and dramatic climax of the sutra. Thirty-two bodhisattvas each articulate their understanding of non-duality by transcending a specific pair of opposites: self and other, samsara and nirvana, purity and defilement, form and emptiness. Each response represents a genuine insight, progressively deepening the teaching.

Manjushri then offers a higher formulation: non-duality is the absence of words, speech, and conceptual designation regarding any dharma. His answer is superior because it does not merely transcend a single duality but points to the non-dual nature of all language and conceptualization.

Vimalakirti's thunderous silence surpasses even Manjushri's formulation. By saying nothing, Vimalakirti avoids the contradiction of using words to point beyond words. His silence is the direct manifestation of non-duality itself, not a teaching about non-duality but its very presence. This moment has been celebrated across Mahayana tradition as the ultimate expression of prajna-paramita.
