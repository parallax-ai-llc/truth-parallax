The senior disciples' refusal to visit Vimalakirti serves as a vehicle for the Mahayana teaching that the Sravaka path, while valid, does not represent the fullness of Buddhist realization. Each disciple recounts an encounter where Vimalakirti demonstrated the limitations of their understanding, exposing the gap between arhat realization and the bodhisattva's non-dual wisdom.

Shariputra's encounter highlights the distinction between quietistic meditation and the Mahayana understanding of true samadhi as being undisturbed amid all activities. Subhuti's encounter challenges the distinction between worthy and unworthy recipients of alms. Mahakashyapa's encounter questions the practice of seeking alms only from the poor.

Each episode reveals a pattern: the Sravaka disciples maintain dualistic distinctions that the bodhisattva path transcends. The chapter does not dismiss the disciples' achievements but situates them within a larger framework where non-dual wisdom and universal compassion represent a more complete expression of the Buddha's teaching.
