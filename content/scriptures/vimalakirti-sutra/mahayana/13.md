The chapter on the offering of the Dharma teaches that the supreme offering is not material gifts but the practice, preservation, and transmission of the teaching itself. This reflects the Mahayana emphasis on prajna (wisdom) as the highest perfection, surpassing even the perfection of generosity (dana) in its capacity to liberate beings.

The sutra enumerates what constitutes a genuine dharma offering: following the deep sutras, practicing in accord with dependent origination, transcending the duality of being and non-being, and upholding the bodhisattva's commitment to universal liberation. Each element of the offering corresponds to an aspect of the bodhisattva path.

The teaching that dharma offering surpasses material offering does not diminish the value of generosity but contextualizes it within the larger framework of the six perfections. Material offerings create merit, but the offering of dharma creates the conditions for complete liberation. The bodhisattva's ultimate gift is the wisdom that frees all beings from the cycle of suffering.
