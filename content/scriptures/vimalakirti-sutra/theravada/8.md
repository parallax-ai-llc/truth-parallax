The eighth chapter presents the famous Dharma Gate of Non-duality, where thirty-two bodhisattvas each offer their understanding of non-duality, Manjushri then offers a higher formulation, and finally Vimalakirti responds with silence. This progression from conceptual articulation to transcendent silence is the climactic teaching of the sutra.

From a Theravada perspective, the Buddha's own noble silence (ariya-tunhi-bhava) on certain metaphysical questions provides a parallel. The fourteen unanswered questions (avyakata) in the Pali canon represent the Buddha's refusal to engage with questions that do not conduce to liberation, which shares something of Vimalakirti's wordless response.

However, the Theravada understanding of silence differs: the Buddha remains silent because certain questions are wrongly framed, while Vimalakirti's silence is presented as the direct expression of non-dual truth. The distinction reflects different epistemological frameworks regarding the relationship between language and ultimate reality.
