Even the great bodhisattvas, including Maitreya, decline to visit Vimalakirti, each recounting a previous encounter where the layman's understanding surpassed theirs. From a Theravada perspective, the figure of Maitreya is recognized as the future Buddha currently dwelling in Tusita heaven, but the concept of multiple advanced bodhisattvas active in the present is distinctly Mahayana.

The chapter reveals that Vimalakirti's realization transcends not only the Sravaka path but also conventional bodhisattva understandings. This escalating pattern of reluctance builds dramatic tension while making the theological point that non-dual realization cannot be contained within any established framework.

Theravada practitioners may observe that the Pali Jataka tales also present the Bodhisatta (future Buddha) as sometimes learning from unexpected teachers, suggesting that the motif of spiritual humbling is not entirely absent from the earlier tradition.
