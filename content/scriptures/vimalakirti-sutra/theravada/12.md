Vimalakirti displays the entire buddha realm of Akshobhya, bringing it into the assembly without shrinking the realm or enlarging the gathering place. The Theravada tradition recognizes Akkhobhya as one of the past Buddhas mentioned in the Buddhavamsa but does not develop the concept of simultaneously accessible buddha lands in the way the Mahayana traditions do.

This chapter demonstrates the interpenetration of reality and the non-obstruction of phenomena, a teaching central to later Huayan philosophy. From a Theravada perspective, the closest parallel is the Buddha's ability to see all world-systems through his divine eye (dibbacakkhu), though this is understood as a perceptual ability rather than an ontological demonstration.

The vision of Akshobhya's realm serves to validate Vimalakirti's realization by showing that he has the power to manipulate entire cosmic dimensions. Theravada tradition would classify such abilities among the higher psychic powers (abhinna) while maintaining its characteristic restraint about their spiritual significance.
