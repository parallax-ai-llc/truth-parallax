This chapter contains the famous exchange between Shariputra and a goddess who has been dwelling in Vimalakirti's room. When Shariputra expresses discomfort at her female form, the goddess magically swaps their bodies, demonstrating that gender is empty of inherent existence. The Theravada tradition includes accounts of spontaneous sex change in the Vinaya and commentary literature.

The Pali canon's Soma Sutta records the nun Soma's response to Mara's suggestion that women cannot attain enlightenment: "What does being a woman matter when the mind is well-concentrated?" This shares the Vimalakirti Sutra's conclusion that gender is irrelevant to realization, though the rhetorical strategies differ dramatically.

The goddess's transformation of Shariputra goes further than Theravada formulations by making the fluidity of gender a direct teaching on the emptiness of all dharmas. From a Theravada perspective, this episode illustrates the Mahayana tendency to extend the implications of anatta into every dimension of experience.
