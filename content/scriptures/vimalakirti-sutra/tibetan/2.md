Vimalakirti's skillful means are understood in Tibetan Buddhism through the framework of the four enlightened activities (phrin las bzhi): pacifying, enriching, magnetizing, and wrathful subjugation. A realized master may employ any of these methods depending on the needs of the student, and Vimalakirti's feigned illness is an example of the enriching activity that draws beings into the sphere of dharma teaching.

The Tibetan tradition of the mahasiddhas provides rich parallels to Vimalakirti's unconventional behavior. Masters like Tilopa, Naropa, and Drukpa Kunley displayed behavior that appeared bizarre or transgressive to conventional observers but served as powerful skillful means for breaking through the conceptual habits of their students.

Vimalakirti's existence as a wealthy householder who has attained supreme realization mirrors the Tibetan tradition of recognized lay practitioners (ngakpas) who maintain families and engage in worldly activities while practicing the highest tantric methods. The sutra validates the spiritual authority of the non-monastic path.
