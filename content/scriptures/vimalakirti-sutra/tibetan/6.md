The inconceivable liberation demonstrated in this chapter parallels the Vajrayana understanding of siddhi (supernatural accomplishment), which arises naturally from the realization of emptiness and the practice of deity yoga. In Tibetan Buddhism, the ability to manipulate the appearances of the phenomenal world is understood as a natural consequence of realizing their empty nature.

The demonstration of fitting vast objects into tiny spaces resonates with the Dzogchen teaching on the nature of space (dbyings), in which the spacious nature of awareness is unlimited and can contain all phenomena without obstruction. Similarly, the Mahamudra teaching on the inseparability of appearance and emptiness is directly illustrated by Vimalakirti's feats.

Tibetan commentators classify these demonstrations within the framework of the eight great siddhis and the supreme siddhi of buddhahood. While ordinary practitioners may develop limited supernatural abilities through concentration practice, the inconceivable liberation described here arises only from the complete realization of emptiness combined with the bodhisattva's compassionate motivation.
