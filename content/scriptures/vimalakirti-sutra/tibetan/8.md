The Dharma Gate of Non-duality is studied in Tibetan Buddhist monastic universities as a key teaching on the nature of ultimate truth (don dam bden pa). The progressive sequence of the bodhisattvas' responses is used pedagogically to illustrate increasingly subtle understandings of non-duality, culminating in the recognition that non-duality itself cannot be expressed.

Tibetan commentators have drawn parallels between the sequence of responses and the progressive stages of Madhyamaka reasoning. Each bodhisattva's transcendence of a particular duality corresponds to the analysis of a specific conceptual pair, while Manjushri's response represents the Prasangika insight that all conceptual elaboration (prapanca) must be abandoned. Vimalakirti's silence represents direct realization beyond all philosophical positions.

The Dzogchen tradition particularly values Vimalakirti's silence as pointing to the nature of rigpa (pure awareness), which cannot be captured in any conceptual framework. The silence is not mere absence of speech but the natural state (gnas lugs) itself, beyond affirmation and negation, beyond even the subtle concepts of Madhyamaka analysis.
