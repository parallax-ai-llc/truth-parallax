The bodhisattvas' reluctance adds another dimension to the Tibetan understanding of the stages (bhumi) of the bodhisattva path. Even advanced bodhisattvas may have subtle conceptual obscurations (shes bya'i sgrib pa) that prevent them from fully realizing non-duality. Vimalakirti's challenges expose these residual obscurations.

Maitreya's encounter is particularly significant for Tibetan Buddhism, which reveres Maitreya as the author (through Asanga) of the five treatises (byams chos sde lnga) that form the foundation of Yogacara study in Tibet. Vimalakirti's challenge to Maitreya's concept of future buddhahood introduces the teaching that awakening transcends temporal categories.

The Tibetan tradition reads this chapter through the lens of the Madhyamaka critique of inherent existence. Each bodhisattva's subtle reification of some aspect of the path is dismantled through Vimalakirti's questioning, mirroring the dialectical method employed by Nagarjuna and later Tibetan Madhyamaka philosophers such as Tsongkhapa and Gorampa.
