The vision of Akshobhya's realm is understood in Tibetan Buddhism through the practice of pure land visualization and the mandala principle. In the Vajrayana tradition, the practitioner regularly visualizes pure realms as part of deity yoga practice, and the ability to perceive the mandala within ordinary appearances marks the development of pure perception.

Akshobhya (Mi bskyod pa in Tibetan) is associated with the mirror-like wisdom (me long ye shes) that reflects all phenomena without distortion. The vision of his realm represents the practitioner's capacity to see the world as the spontaneous display of the five wisdoms, transforming ordinary perception into enlightened vision.

The display of an entire buddha realm on the palm of a hand illustrates the Dzogchen teaching on the universe arising within the vast expanse of awareness. The realm is not brought from outside but recognized as always present within the nature of mind. This chapter demonstrates how the Vimalakirti Sutra prefigures themes that would become central to Vajrayana practice.
