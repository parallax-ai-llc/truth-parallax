The teaching on dharma offering connects with the Tibetan practice of the seven-branch offering, which includes prostration, offering, confession, rejoicing, requesting the turning of the dharma wheel, requesting teachers to remain, and dedicating merit. The dharma offering is understood as the most meritorious element of this practice, surpassing material offerings in its capacity to benefit beings.

In Tibetan monastic tradition, the printing and distribution of dharma texts is considered among the highest forms of generosity. The woodblock printing culture of Tibet, which produced millions of pages of Buddhist texts, was motivated by the understanding expressed in this chapter: that preserving and sharing the dharma is the supreme offering.

The chapter's teaching also connects with the Tibetan practice of dharma debate (rtsod pa), where monks engage in rigorous philosophical exchange as a form of dharma offering to each other and to the tradition. The sharp questioning and precise reasoning of Tibetan debate are themselves offerings of wisdom, sharpening understanding and preserving the authentic meaning of the teaching.
