The journey to the Fragrant Land is understood in Tibetan Buddhism through the practice of visiting pure lands through meditation and emanation. The Tibetan tradition teaches that advanced practitioners can project their consciousness to other buddha fields during meditation, receiving teachings and bringing back spiritual nourishment. This practice is particularly developed in the terma (treasure) tradition.

The Fragrant Land, where the buddha teaches exclusively through fragrance, illustrates the Tibetan understanding that the dharma manifests through all six sense faculties. In tantric practice, the transformation of sensory experience through the mandala principle means that all sensations become aspects of enlightened awareness. The fragrant rice embodies this principle of transformed perception.

The emanation bodhisattva's ability to travel across countless buddha fields connects with the Tibetan teaching on the nirmanakaya (emanation body), one of the three kayas through which a buddha's activity manifests. The entire episode demonstrates how the nirmanakaya functions to connect different realms of existence and bring benefit across cosmic dimensions.
