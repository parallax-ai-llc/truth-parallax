## Overview

The Vimalakirti Sutra is preserved in the Tibetan Kangyur and has been studied within Tibetan Buddhist monastic universities as an important Mahayana sutra. Tibetan commentators have read it through the lens of Madhyamaka philosophy, finding in Vimalakirti's teachings a dramatic illustration of Nagarjuna's doctrine of emptiness.

## Doctrinal Significance

Tibetan scholars emphasize the sutra's demonstration that emptiness is not a mere negation but the basis for compassionate engagement with the world. Vimalakirti's inconceivable displays of liberation are understood through the framework of the two truths, showing how a realized being operates simultaneously at the conventional and ultimate levels.

## Tantric Connections

The figure of Vimalakirti as a lay householder who surpasses monastics in realization resonates with the Tibetan tradition of realized lay tantric practitioners (ngakpas and mahasiddhas). His ability to transform environments and manifest miraculous powers prefigures the siddhi traditions of Vajrayana Buddhism.
