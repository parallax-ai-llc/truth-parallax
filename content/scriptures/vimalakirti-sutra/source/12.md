---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 12"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 12
---

**1.** At that time the Buddha said to Vimalakirti: "When you wish to see the Tathagata, how do you view him?"

**2.** Vimalakirti said: "As I view the true nature of my own body, so I view the Buddha. I view the Tathagata as not coming from anywhere in the past and not going to anywhere in the future."

**3.** "I view him as not existing in the present time. I view him as neither in form, nor in the nature of form. He is neither in sensation, perception, volition, nor consciousness."

**4.** "He does not arise from the four elements. He is not in space. He transcends all spheres of the senses."

**5.** "He is without defilement, in accordance with the nature of non-action. He is without thought, like empty space. He is beyond all language and expression."

**6.** "He cannot be designated, for he transcends designation. He is neither strength nor weakness. He is neither this shore nor the other shore, nor the stream between. Yet he can be perceived by the wise."

**7.** "He is like space, which does not rest on anything. The Tathagata, likewise, rests on nothing — neither on the conditioned nor on the unconditioned."

**8.** "He is beyond all marks, yet he is not without marks. He is beyond all arising, yet he is not divorced from causes and conditions."

**9.** "He is neither one thing nor a different thing. He is neither this nor that. He is neither departure nor arrival. All who try to see him through categories fail."

**10.** "The Tathagata's body is the Dharma-body. It is born of immeasurable merit, of immeasurable wisdom, of morality, meditation, liberation, and the insight of liberation."

**11.** "It is born of benevolence, compassion, joy, and equanimity. It is born of charity, discipline, and patience. It is born of all the paramitas."

**12.** "It is born of expedient means. It is born of the six supernatural powers. It is born of the three clear insights."

**13.** "It is born of the thirty-seven aids to enlightenment. It is born of Samatha and Vipasyana. It is born of the ten powers, the four fearlessnesses, and the eighteen uncommon qualities."

**14.** "It is born of the cutting off of all unwholesome dharmas and the gathering of all wholesome dharmas. It is born of the truth. It is born of non-slackening."

**15.** "From such immeasurable pure dharmas is the body of the Tathagata born. If you wish to attain this body, you should cut off all the afflictions of beings. This is how I view the Tathagata."
