---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 8"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 8
---

**1.** At that time Vimalakirti said to the great assembly of Bodhisattvas: "Good sirs, tell me: how does a Bodhisattva enter the Dharma-gate of non-duality?"

**2.** The Bodhisattva Dharmavikurvana said: "Good sirs, birth and extinction are two. But since dharmas are originally not born, they now have no extinction. The attainment of the acceptance that dharmas are not born — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**3.** The Bodhisattva Srigandha said: "Self and selflessness are two. Since the self cannot be apprehended, how can there be selflessness? One who sees the true nature of the self does not set up duality — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**4.** The Bodhisattva Srikuta said: "Defilement and purity are two. When one perceives the true nature of defilement, there is no aspect of purity — one accords with the mark of extinction. This is the entrance into non-duality."

**5.** The Bodhisattva Bhadrajyotis said: "Distraction and attention are two. When there is no distraction, there is no attention and no mind to attend. One who is equal in these — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**6.** The Bodhisattva Sunetra said: "The Sravaka-vehicle and the Buddha-vehicle are two. When one sees that the nature of all dharmas is like the nature of Nirvana, there is neither Sravaka-vehicle nor Buddha-vehicle — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**7.** The Bodhisattva Tisya said: "Good and not-good are two. If one does not seize upon good or not-good, and enters the boundless with the mind, one enters the gate of non-duality."

**8.** The Bodhisattva Simha said: "Sin and blessing are two. If one understands the nature of sin, it does not differ from blessing. To use the diamond wisdom to determine this matter, being neither bound nor freed — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**9.** The Bodhisattva Suddhadhimukti said: "The conditioned and the unconditioned are two. But if one leaves the mind in a state like space, with no thought and no action, this is the entrance into non-duality."

**10.** The Bodhisattva Narayana said: "The worldly and the superworldly are two. The worldly nature is empty, which is the superworldly. Within this there is no entering, no leaving, no overflowing, no scattering — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**11.** The Bodhisattva Priyadarsana said: "Form and emptiness are two. Form itself is emptiness; it is not that form is extinguished and then there is emptiness. The nature of form is emptiness — this is the entrance into non-duality."

**12.** In this way thirty-one Bodhisattvas each explained the meaning of non-duality according to their understanding.

**13.** Then they asked Manjusri: "What is the Bodhisattva's entrance into the Dharma-gate of non-duality?"

**14.** Manjusri said: "In my opinion, in regard to all dharmas, being without words, without speech, without demonstration, without perception, and transcending all questions and answers — this is the entrance into the Dharma-gate of non-duality."

**15.** Then Manjusri asked Vimalakirti: "We have each given an explanation. Good sir, now it is your turn. What is the Bodhisattva's entrance into the Dharma-gate of non-duality?" At this, Vimalakirti remained silent, saying nothing at all. Manjusri praised him, saying: "Excellent! Excellent! To have neither words nor speech — this is the true entrance into the Dharma-gate of non-duality."
