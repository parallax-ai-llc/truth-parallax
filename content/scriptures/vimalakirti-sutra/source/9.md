---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 9"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1.** At that time Sariputra, seeing that there were no seats in the room, thought: "Where will all these Bodhisattvas and great disciples sit?"

**2.** Vimalakirti, knowing his thought, said: "Sariputra, did you come for the Dharma or did you come for a seat?"

**3.** Sariputra said: "I came for the Dharma, not for a seat."

**4.** Vimalakirti said: "Sariputra, one who seeks the Dharma does not concern himself with body or life, how much less with a seat."

**5.** "One who seeks the Dharma seeks nothing among form, sensation, perception, volition, or consciousness. One who seeks the Dharma does not seek to grasp the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Sangha."

**6.** "One who seeks the Dharma does not seek to perceive suffering, or to abandon its accumulation, or to realise its cessation, or to practise the path. Why? Because the Dharma is beyond arising and ceasing."

**7.** "Sariputra, if you can seek the Dharma in this manner, you are truly a seeker of the Dharma."

**8.** Then Vimalakirti asked Manjusri: "In all the Buddha-lands you have visited, which has the finest lion-seats?"

**9.** Manjusri said: "In the east, beyond countries as numerous as the sands of thirty-six Ganges rivers, there is a world called Merudhvaja. Its Buddha is called Merupradiparaja. The lion-seats of that world are eighty-four thousand yojanas high."

**10.** Vimalakirti, exercising his supernatural power, caused that Buddha Merupradiparaja to send thirty-two thousand lion-seats, lofty and magnificent, which entered Vimalakirti's room — and the room was not enlarged.

**11.** Neither Vaisali nor the Saha world was crowded. All remained as before.

**12.** Vimalakirti said: "Manjusri, please be seated in a lion-seat. Transform your body to match the size of the seat." Those Bodhisattvas who had attained supernatural powers transformed themselves into bodies forty-two thousand yojanas tall and sat in the seats.

**13.** But the new Bodhisattvas and the great disciples were unable to ascend the seats. Vimalakirti said: "Sariputra, sit in the lion-seat."

**14.** Sariputra said: "Householder, the seat is too large and too high. I cannot climb up. May I ask you to reduce its size?"

**15.** Vimalakirti said: "Sariputra, bow to the Tathagata Merupradiparaja, and then you will be able to sit." Then all the new Bodhisattvas and great disciples bowed to that Buddha and were immediately able to sit in the lion-seats. This is the inconceivable liberation of the Bodhisattva — to place eighty-four thousand lion-seats in a small room without any crowding or obstruction.
