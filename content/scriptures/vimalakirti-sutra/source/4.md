---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 4"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1.** The Buddha then said to Subhuti: "Go to Vimalakirti and inquire about his illness."

**2.** Subhuti said: "World-Honoured One, I am not worthy to go. Once I went to Vimalakirti's house to beg for food. He took my bowl and filled it, then said:"

**3.** "'Subhuti, if you can regard all foods as equal without making distinctions, and thereby regard all dharmas as equal, and if you can beg for food in this manner without discrimination, then you may take the food.'"

**4.** "'If without cutting off desire, anger, and stupidity you are yet not a part of them; if without destroying the body you are in accord with the one-and-only appearance; if without rooting out ignorance and craving you can find liberation — then you are worthy to receive this food.'"

**5.** "'If you can see the Buddha without leaving the defilements behind, if you enter the heterodox views without going the way of the Buddha, and yet can reach the other shore — then you may take this food.'"

**6.** "World-Honoured One, when I heard these words I was bewildered. I did not know what to say, what to answer. I put down the bowl and was about to leave."

**7.** "But Vimalakirti said: 'Subhuti, take your bowl. Do not be afraid. If the Tathagata were to create an illusion-person, and that illusion-person were asked such questions, do you think he would be frightened?'"

**8.** "I said: 'No.' And Vimalakirti said: 'All things are like illusions and apparitions. You should not fear them. The wise are not frightened by words.'"

**9.** "'Why? Because all words and speech are by nature empty. Only those who are attached to words are frightened. Liberation does not mean liberation from things, but liberation from all ideas.'"

**10.** The Buddha then asked Purnamaitrayaniputra, Maha-Katyayana, Aniruddha, Upali, and Rahula to go, but each declined, recounting how Vimalakirti had silenced them.

**11.** Upali said: "Once two monks who had violated the precepts came to me in shame. Vimalakirti came and said: 'Upali, do not aggravate their sins. Wipe them out at once, without tormenting these monks' minds.'"

**12.** "'Why? Because the nature of those sins does not exist internally, nor externally, nor in between. As the Buddha has said, when the mind is defiled, the living being is defiled; when the mind is purified, the living being is purified.'"

**13.** "'The mind is found neither internally nor externally, nor in between. Sin is the same as the mind — it has no fixed nature. All dharmas are the same — they do not go beyond Suchness.'"

**14.** "'Upali, is your mind defiled when it achieves liberation?' I said: 'No.' He said: 'In the same way, the minds of all beings are without defilement. Delusions and wrong views — that is defilement. To be without delusions and wrong views — that is purity.'"

**15.** "'Clinging to self — that is defilement. Not clinging to self — that is purity. Upali, all appearances are false. If you can understand that nothing is produced, nothing is destroyed — this is the meaning of Upali's discipline.' When I heard this, I was unable to reply."
