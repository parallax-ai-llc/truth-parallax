---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 3"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1.** At that time the elder Vimalakirti thought to himself: "I lie here on my sickbed, yet the World-Honoured One, the greatly compassionate one, does not take pity on me and send someone to inquire about my illness."

**2.** The Buddha, knowing his thoughts, said to Sariputra: "Go to Vimalakirti and inquire about his illness."

**3.** Sariputra said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, I am not worthy to go and inquire about his illness. Why?"

**4.** "Because I recall that once in the past I was sitting in quiet meditation under a tree in the forest. Vimalakirti came and said to me:"

**5.** "'Sariputra, you should not assume that this sort of sitting is true quiet sitting. Quiet sitting means that in the three realms of existence you manifest neither body nor mind — this is quiet sitting.'"

**6.** "'Not arising from your Samadhi of extinction and yet showing yourself in the ceremonies of daily life — this is quiet sitting. Not abandoning the principles of the Way and yet showing yourself in the activities of a common person — this is quiet sitting.'"

**7.** "'The mind not dwelling internally and not engaged externally — this is quiet sitting. Practising the thirty-seven aids to enlightenment without being moved by any of the wrong views — this is quiet sitting.'"

**8.** "'Entering Nirvana without cutting off the afflictions — this is quiet sitting.' If you can sit in this manner, you will merit the seal of the Buddha."

**9.** "World-Honoured One, when I heard these words I was dumbfounded and could make no reply. Therefore I am not worthy to go and inquire about his illness."

**10.** Then the Buddha said to Maha-Maudgalyayana: "Go to Vimalakirti and inquire about his illness."

**11.** Maudgalyayana said: "World-Honoured One, I am not worthy to go. Once I was preaching the Dharma to a group of laypeople. Vimalakirti came and said:"

**12.** "'Maudgalyayana, when you preach the Dharma to white-robed laypeople, you should not preach the way you do. The Dharma is without living beings, because it is free from the defilement of living beings.'"

**13.** "'The Dharma is without self, because it is free from the defilement of self. The Dharma is without life, because it is free from birth and death. The Dharma is without persons, because it cuts off all continuity between past and future.'"

**14.** "'The Dharma is eternally serene, because it puts an end to all phenomena. The Dharma is without form, because it is free from conditions. The Dharma has no name, because words and speech are cut off.'"

**15.** "'The Dharma cannot be preached, for any explanation is beyond the mark. The Dharma is beyond sight, hearing, and perception, like empty space. This, Maudgalyayana, is how the Dharma should be preached.' When I heard these words I was struck speechless. Therefore I am not worthy to go."
