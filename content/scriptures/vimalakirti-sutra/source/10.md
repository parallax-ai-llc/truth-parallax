---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 10"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1.** At that time Sariputra thought: "It is approaching mealtime. Where will all these Bodhisattvas and great disciples eat?"

**2.** Vimalakirti, knowing his thought, said: "Sariputra, the Buddha has preached the eight liberations. You should practise them. How can you attend to the Dharma with a mind occupied by thoughts of eating?"

**3.** "If you wish to eat, wait a moment. I will give you a meal such as you have never had before."

**4.** Then Vimalakirti entered into Samadhi and, using his supernatural powers, showed the assembly a land called Sarvagandhasugandha — Accumulated Fragrances — in the upper region, far beyond as many Buddha-lands as there are sands in forty-two Ganges rivers.

**5.** The Buddha of that land was called Gandhottamakuta — Fragrance Accumulated. In that world, the fragrance of the trees surpassed all the fragrant substances of the divine and human worlds of the ten directions.

**6.** That land had no Sravakas or Pratyekabuddhas — only Bodhisattvas. The Buddha there did not teach with words but used fragrance to convert beings. The Bodhisattvas sat under fragrant trees, and from the fragrance they attained all the samadhis.

**7.** Vimalakirti then created a phantom Bodhisattva and sent him to that world to bring back food. The phantom Bodhisattva went, bowed to the Buddha Gandhottamakuta, and requested food.

**8.** The Bodhisattvas of that world marvelled: "Where does this being come from? Where is the Saha world? What are its 'words' by which its Dharma is preached?"

**9.** The Buddha Gandhottamakuta said: "In the lower region there is a world called Saha. Its Buddha is called Sakyamuni. There the Bodhisattva Vimalakirti displays inconceivable liberation and preaches the Dharma to the Bodhisattvas."

**10.** "He has sent this phantom Bodhisattva to praise my land and cause the Bodhisattvas here to aspire beyond their present achievements."

**11.** The Bodhisattvas of that world said: "How great must this man be, that his supernatural powers are so vast and his wisdom so deep!" The Buddha replied: "His greatness is immeasurable. He sends phantom Bodhisattvas to all the lands in the ten directions, always benefiting beings."

**12.** Then the Buddha Gandhottamakuta placed the fragrant rice in a vessel and gave it to the phantom Bodhisattva. And nine million Bodhisattvas of that world said: "We wish to go and see the Saha world, pay homage to the Buddha Sakyamuni, and meet Vimalakirti."

**13.** The phantom Bodhisattva brought the fragrant rice back to Vimalakirti's room. The fragrance of that rice spread throughout the city of Vaisali, and then throughout the three-thousand great thousandfold world.

**14.** When the Bodhisattvas, Sravakas, gods, and men in that assembly ate the rice, they experienced a bliss they had never known. All their pores released fragrance like the fragrance of the trees in the land of Accumulated Fragrances.

**15.** Vimalakirti said: "Sariputra, in that Buddha-land there are no words, no speech. All the Bodhisattvas there are taught by fragrance alone. Under each fragrant tree they sit, and from the fragrance they enter into all the samadhis. This is the inconceivable work of the Buddhas."
