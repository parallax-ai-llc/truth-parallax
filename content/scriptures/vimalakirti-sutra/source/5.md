---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 5"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1.** Then the Buddha said to the Bodhisattva Maitreya: "Go to Vimalakirti and inquire about his illness."

**2.** Maitreya said: "World-Honoured One, I am not worthy to go. Once in the Tushita heaven I was preaching to the king of the Tushita gods about the stage of non-regression."

**3.** "Vimalakirti came and said: 'Maitreya, the World-Honoured One has given you a prophecy that in one more lifetime you will attain supreme, perfect enlightenment. But in which lifetime will you receive this prophecy — the past, the future, or the present?'"

**4.** "'If in the past life, that life is already gone. If in a future life, that life has not yet come. If in the present life, the present does not abide.'"

**5.** "'As the Buddha has said, at this very moment you are born, grow old, and die. If your prophecy is given in the state of no-birth, then no-birth is the same as the stage of right assurance, and in that stage there is no receiving of prophecies and no attaining of supreme enlightenment.'"

**6.** "'How then, Maitreya, have you received your prophecy — through the aspect of Suchness as it is born, or through the aspect of Suchness as it is extinguished?'"

**7.** "'If through the Suchness of birth — Suchness has no birth. If through the Suchness of extinction — Suchness has no extinction.'"

**8.** "'All living beings have the nature of Suchness. All dharmas have the nature of Suchness. All the sages and worthies have the nature of Suchness. Even Maitreya has the nature of Suchness.'"

**9.** "'If Maitreya receives a prophecy of attaining Buddhahood, then all living beings should likewise receive such a prophecy. Why? Because Suchness is neither dual nor divided.'"

**10.** "'If Maitreya attains supreme enlightenment, then all beings likewise attain it. Why? Because all beings are the manifestation of Suchness.'"

**11.** The Buddha then asked the Bodhisattva Prabhavyuha, and the Bodhisattva Jagatimdhara, and the prince Sudatta to go, but each likewise declined.

**12.** The Bodhisattva Jagatimdhara said: "Once Vimalakirti came to me and said: 'Jagatimdhara, do not teach the Dharma of likes and dislikes. The true Dharma has neither likes nor dislikes.'"

**13.** "'If you preach with a mind that likes some things and dislikes others, you are still in the realm of give and take. The Dharma is beyond all transactions.'"

**14.** "'Jagatimdhara, the Dharma is not an object. One who pursues objects is not pursuing the Dharma. The Dharma is not a this or a that. One who dwells in this or that is not dwelling in the Dharma.'"

**15.** "'The Dharma is without being grasped or relinquished. One who grasps or relinquishes is not grasping the Dharma. The Dharma has no abode. One who dwells in an abode is not dwelling in the Dharma.' When I heard this I could not reply."
