---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 7"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1.** Manjusri asked Vimalakirti: "How should a Bodhisattva regard all living beings?"

**2.** Vimalakirti said: "A Bodhisattva should regard all living beings as a wise man regards the reflection of the moon in water — he sees it but knows it is not real."

**3.** "As a magician regards the illusion he creates. As a wise man regards the echo of a voice. As a wise man regards clouds forming in the sky."

**4.** "As a wise man regards the bubble on water. As a wise man regards the plantain tree — having no heartwood. As a wise man regards a flash of lightning."

**5.** "As a wise man regards the fifth element — it does not exist. As a wise man regards empty space — without substance."

**6.** Manjusri asked: "If a Bodhisattva regards beings in this way, how can he practise great compassion toward them?"

**7.** Vimalakirti said: "A Bodhisattva who regards beings in this way should think: 'I must preach the Dharma for them, the Dharma that accords with this truth.' This is true compassion."

**8.** "He should practise the compassion of cessation — for all dharmas finally cease. He should practise the compassion of non-production — for there is no arising in the three realms."

**9.** "He should practise the compassion of no-self — for he saves beings without seeing beings to be saved."

**10.** "He should practise the compassion of equanimity — levelling all the three periods of time. He should practise the compassion that is without passion — for it is free from desire."

**11.** "He should practise the compassion of non-duality — for it transcends internal and external. He should practise the compassion that is firm and unshakeable — for its determination is ultimate."

**12.** Manjusri then asked: "What is the root of the Bodhisattva's practice?"

**13.** Vimalakirti said: "The afflictions of beings are the root of the Bodhisattva's practice. If there were no afflictions, the Bodhisattva would have nothing to do."

**14.** "It is like the lotus which does not grow on dry land but in the mud and water. In the same way, the qualities of Buddhahood do not grow in beings who have attained the certain truth of the Unconditioned."

**15.** "It is in the mud of the afflictions that beings plant the seed of Buddhahood. As a seed planted in the sky will never grow, but planted in manured and moist soil it will flourish — so the seed of Buddhahood will not grow in those who have already attained the Unconditioned, but it will grow in the mountain of wrong views and afflictions."
