---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 2"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1.** At that time the elder son of the Licchavi, Ratnakara, along with five hundred sons of the Licchavis, each holding a jewelled parasol, came to where the Buddha was and offered their parasols to the Lord.

**2.** Then the Buddha, through his supernatural power, caused all the jewelled parasols to be transformed into one single parasol, so large that it covered the entire three-thousand great thousandfold world.

**3.** The whole expanse of this world was reflected within it — the mountains, rivers, the sun and moon, all the continents, all the beings and their cities.

**4.** Then the Buddha said to Ratnakara: "The Buddha-land is pure in accordance with the purity of the mind of the Bodhisattva."

**5.** "When the mind of the Bodhisattva is pure, then the Buddha-land will be pure. Therefore, Ratnakara, if the Bodhisattva wishes to attain a pure land, he should purify his mind."

**6.** "In accordance with the purity of the mind, the Buddha-land is pure. In accordance with the depth of the Bodhisattva's thought, the Buddha-land is deep."

**7.** "The Buddha-land of the Bodhisattva is the land of all living beings. Why? Because the Bodhisattva takes living beings as his Buddha-land."

**8.** "The straight mind is the Bodhisattva's Pure Land. When he attains Buddhahood, beings who are without flattery will be born in his land."

**9.** "The deep mind is the Bodhisattva's Pure Land. When he attains Buddhahood, beings who have accumulated merit will be born there."

**10.** "The mind of bodhi is the Bodhisattva's Pure Land. When he attains Buddhahood, beings who practise the Great Vehicle will be born there."

**11.** "Generosity is the Bodhisattva's Pure Land. When he attains Buddhahood, beings who can give all will be born there. Morality, patience, vigour, meditation, and wisdom are each the Bodhisattva's Pure Land."

**12.** "The four immeasurable minds — benevolence, compassion, joy, and equanimity — are the Bodhisattva's Pure Land."

**13.** "The six supernatural penetrations are the Bodhisattva's Pure Land. When he attains Buddhahood, beings who have achieved the six supernatural penetrations will be born there."

**14.** At that time Sariputra, receiving the Buddha's supernatural power, thought: "If the Buddha-land is pure in accordance with the mind of the Bodhisattva, then when the World-Honoured One first set forth on the Bodhisattva path, his mind must have been impure. How else can this Buddha-land be so impure?"

**15.** The Buddha, knowing his thought, said: "Sariputra, is the sun and moon impure because a blind man cannot see its light? The fault lies with the blind man, not with the sun and moon. The Tathagata's land is always pure. It is you who cannot see it." At that moment the Buddha pressed his toe on the ground, and the three-thousand great thousandfold world was adorned with hundreds of thousands of jewels. The entire assembly saw the land transformed into one of immeasurable purity.
