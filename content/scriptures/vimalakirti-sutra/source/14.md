---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 14"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 14
---

**1.** At that time the Lord said to Maitreya the Bodhisattva: "Maitreya, I now entrust to you this supreme, perfect enlightenment. After the Buddha's extinction, you and the great Bodhisattvas should spread this Sutra widely, so that it will not be cut off."

**2.** "In the future there will be Bodhisattvas who, hearing this Sutra, will feel doubt and suspicion. You should know that they are newly come to the Bodhisattva-path."

**3.** "And those who hear this Sutra and do not doubt, who joyfully accept it — you should know that they are not newly come. They have long planted good roots."

**4.** "Maitreya, let me state this clearly: among the Bodhisattvas there are two kinds. What are these two? The first kind delights in ornate phrases and literary elegance. The second kind is not afraid of profound meaning and is able to enter the truth."

**5.** "Those who delight in ornate phrases should be known as beginners in the Bodhisattva-path. Those who can enter the profound, unafraid of its depth, should be known as those who have long practised the Way."

**6.** "Maitreya, there are two further kinds among the beginners that cause them to be unable to determine the meaning of the profound Dharma. What are these two? The first is that they have never heard this Sutra before and so are startled and doubtful."

**7.** "The second is that they do not wish to protect and accept the profound Sutra — they look down on those who study it. Those who have these two faults will not be able to benefit from the profound Dharma."

**8.** "There are also two kinds among those who have long practised the Way that cause them to fail to attain the acceptance of the truth. What are they? The first is that they revere the Sravakas but lack reverence for the Bodhisattvas."

**9.** "The second is that though they have heard the teaching, they do not practise it. Those who have these two faults, even though they have long practised, will not attain the benefit of the profound Dharma."

**10.** The Bodhisattva Maitreya said: "World-Honoured One, what you have spoken is rare and wonderful. I will avoid these faults and will uphold this Dharma of supreme, perfect enlightenment which the Tathagata has accumulated over countless kalpas."

**11.** "If in the future there are good men and good women who seek the Great Vehicle, I will see to it that this Sutra is placed in their hands, that the power of memory is given them, and that they receive and keep it, read and recite it, and expound it to others."

**12.** "World-Honoured One, in the latter age, if anyone can receive, keep, read, recite, and expound this Sutra, know that it is Maitreya's supernatural power that enables them to do so."

**13.** The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent, Maitreya! As you have said, I rejoice in your words."

**14.** Then all the Bodhisattvas said with one voice: "After the World-Honoured One's extinction, we will broadly expound this Dharma in the lands of the ten directions. And we will guide those who preach the Dharma, causing them to obtain this Sutra."

**15.** Then the four heavenly kings said: "World-Honoured One, in all places where this Sutra is expounded, read, recited, and practised, we will go there to hear the Dharma and will protect the preacher. We will guard him within a hundred yojanas, so that no trouble can reach him." When the Buddha had finished preaching this Sutra, all the Bodhisattvas, the venerable Ananda, and the four classes of the assembly, together with the gods, men, asuras, and others of all the worlds, heard the Buddha's teaching, and with great joy, believed, received, and practised it.
