---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 6"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 6
---

**1.** Then the Buddha said to Manjusri: "You go to Vimalakirti and inquire about his illness."

**2.** Manjusri said: "World-Honoured One, this man is difficult to approach. His understanding is profound. His eloquence is unimpeded. His wisdom embraces all. He knows the practices of all Bodhisattvas."

**3.** "He has entered the secret storehouses of all the Buddhas. He has conquered all the demons. He exercises all the supernatural powers. He has attained perfect wisdom and skill in expedient means."

**4.** "Nevertheless, I will go as the Buddha commands, and inquire about his illness."

**5.** Then all the Bodhisattvas, the great disciples, the Indras, the Brahmas, the four guardian kings, and others thought: "Now Manjusri and Vimalakirti will discourse together. Surely they will speak the wonderful Dharma." And eight thousand Bodhisattvas, five hundred Sravakas, and hundreds of thousands of gods all wished to follow Manjusri.

**6.** Manjusri, with his great retinue, entered the city of Vaisali and went to the house of Vimalakirti. The room was empty, with not a single piece of furniture. Vimalakirti was alone, lying on his sickbed.

**7.** Manjusri said: "Householder, is your illness bearable? Is it decreasing? The World-Honoured One sends me to inquire about you."

**8.** Vimalakirti said: "Manjusri, my illness has lasted from remote ages down to the present. As long as beings are ill, I am ill. When all beings are cured, then I will be cured."

**9.** "Why? The Bodhisattva, for the sake of all beings, enters the realm of birth and death. And because he is in the realm of birth and death, he is subject to illness."

**10.** "If all beings can be freed from illness, then the Bodhisattva will no longer be ill. It is like a wealthy man whose only son falls ill — the parents are ill as long as the son is ill; when the son recovers, the parents also recover."

**11.** "The Bodhisattva is the same way. He loves all beings as a parent loves an only child. When beings are ill, the Bodhisattva is ill. When beings recover, the Bodhisattva recovers."

**12.** Manjusri asked: "Householder, what is the root of this illness?"

**13.** Vimalakirti said: "The illness of the Bodhisattva arises from great compassion."

**14.** Manjusri asked: "Householder, your room is empty. Why is there no furniture, no attendants?"

**15.** Vimalakirti said: "Manjusri, all the Buddha-lands are also empty. All things are empty. Emptiness does not need furniture. My room is empty because it reflects the teaching of inconceivable liberation. In this one small room, all the Buddha-lands of the ten directions can be contained."
