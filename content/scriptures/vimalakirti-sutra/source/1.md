---
title: "Vimalakirti Sutra Chapter 1"
scripture: "vimalakirti-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1.** Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in Vaisali, in the garden of Amrapali, with a great gathering of monks numbering eight thousand, and thirty-two thousand Bodhisattvas.

**2.** All were well known and had achieved great supernatural penetrations. They were entrusted with the task by all the Buddhas. They guarded the city of the Dharma.

**3.** They knew how to preach the Dharma. Their fame resounded in the ten directions. They had mastered the dharanis and were unimpeded in their eloquence.

**4.** At that time in the city of Vaisali there lived a wealthy householder named Vimalakirti. He had already served innumerable Buddhas in the past, deeply planting good roots.

**5.** He had attained the acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. His eloquence was unimpeded. He exercised supernatural powers freely. He had attained all the dharanis.

**6.** He had arrived at the stage of fearlessness. He had conquered all the obstructions of evil. He had entered the gate of deep Dharma.

**7.** He was accomplished in wisdom and skilled in the practice of expedient means. He had fulfilled all the great vows. He understood the minds and actions of all beings.

**8.** Though he was but a layman dressed in white, he observed the purity of a monk's discipline. Though he lived at home, he was not attached to the three realms.

**9.** Though he appeared to have a wife and children, he was always pure in his practice of brahmacharya. Though he appeared to have attendants, he delighted in solitude.

**10.** Though he wore jewels and ornaments, his real adornment was the marks and signs of excellence. Though he ate and drank, what he truly relished was the joy of meditation.

**11.** Though he frequented the gambling halls and places of amusement, his purpose was always to convert those who were attached to such things. He associated with heretics without ever forsaking the true Dharma.

**12.** Though he studied secular texts, he always took delight in the Dharma. Among all people he was honoured as the most worthy of respect.

**13.** He upheld the correct laws and regulated both young and old. Though he engaged in all kinds of business and trade, he took no pleasure in profit or gain.

**14.** He would appear at crossroads and on highways to benefit living beings. He entered the government courts to protect those in need.

**15.** He entered the lecture halls to guide beings in the correct teaching. He entered the schools and houses of learning to inspire the students. He entered the places of ill repute to show the folly of desire. He entered the drinking houses to steady the wills of those who were addicted. Among the honourable he was the most honourable. Among the elders he was deemed the foremost.
