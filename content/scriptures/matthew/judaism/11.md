## Jesus and John in Jewish Prophetic Tradition

Jesus' evaluation of John the Baptist (11:7-15) places both figures within the Jewish prophetic tradition. The identification of John with Elijah (11:14) reflects the expectation of Elijah's return (Malachi 4:5-6) that was widespread in Second Temple Judaism. Jewish scholars note that the question "Are you the one who is to come?" (11:3) presupposes a shared Jewish vocabulary of Messianic expectation.

## Wisdom Theology

Jesus' thanksgiving prayer (11:25-27) employs the language of Jewish Wisdom theology. The theme of divine wisdom hidden from the wise and revealed to the simple echoes Proverbs, Sirach, and the Wisdom of Solomon. Jewish scholars have compared Jesus' self-presentation as the revealer of the Father's will with the personification of Wisdom (*Hokhmah*) in Jewish literature, who invites the simple to her table (Proverbs 9:1-6).

## The Easy Yoke and Torah

Jesus' invitation to take his "yoke" (11:29-30) uses a metaphor commonly applied to Torah in rabbinic literature. The "yoke of the kingdom of heaven" (*ol malkhut shamayim*) and the "yoke of the commandments" (*ol mitzvot*) are standard rabbinic terms for accepting God's sovereignty and the obligation of Torah observance. Jewish scholars debate whether Jesus offers an alternative to Torah or a particular interpretation of it.
