## The Confession at Caesarea Philippi and Petrine Primacy

Matthew 16:13-20 is the most important ecclesiological passage in the Catholic tradition. Peter's confession, "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" (16:16), and Jesus' response — "You are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church" (16:18) — are the scriptural foundation for the doctrine of the papacy. Catholic teaching holds that Jesus conferred upon Peter a unique, personal authority that is transmitted through apostolic succession to each Bishop of Rome.

## The Keys of the Kingdom

The "keys of the kingdom" (16:19) signify supreme jurisdictional authority. Drawing on Isaiah 22:22 (the key of the house of David given to Eliakim), Catholic theology interprets the keys as the authority to govern the universal Church, to define doctrine, and to bind and loose with binding effect. The First Vatican Council (1870) cited this passage as the scriptural basis for papal infallibility when the Pope speaks *ex cathedra*.

## The First Passion Prediction

Jesus' prediction of his suffering, death, and resurrection (16:21-23) and Peter's rebuke reveal the tension between human and divine perspectives. Catholic spiritual theology reads Peter's failure here as a reminder that even those holding the highest office in the Church remain subject to error in personal judgment — a distinction Catholic theology maintains between the charism of infallibility in official teaching and the fallibility of personal opinion.
