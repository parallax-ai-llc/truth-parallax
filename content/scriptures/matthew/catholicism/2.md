## The Epiphany

The visit of the Magi (2:1-12) is the basis for the Feast of the Epiphany, one of the oldest Christian celebrations. Catholic tradition reads the Magi as representatives of the Gentile nations recognizing Christ's kingship. Their three gifts are interpreted as recognizing Jesus as king (gold), God (frankincense), and mortal destined for death (myrrh). The Epiphany liturgy emphasizes the universal scope of Christ's manifestation.

## The Holy Innocents

The massacre of the children of Bethlehem (2:16-18) is commemorated as the Feast of the Holy Innocents (December 28). Catholic tradition regards these children as the first martyrs, dying not by their own confession but by God's providence in place of Christ. This feast has become associated with the Church's advocacy for the protection of innocent life.

## The Flight to Egypt and the Return

The Holy Family's flight to Egypt (2:13-15) and return to Nazareth are read typologically: Jesus recapitulates Israel's journey, fulfilling Hosea 11:1 ("Out of Egypt I called my son"). Catholic devotion sees the Holy Family's refugee experience as grounds for the Church's social teaching on the dignity of migrants and refugees.
