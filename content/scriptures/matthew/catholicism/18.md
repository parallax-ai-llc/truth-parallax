## The Community Discourse and Ecclesiology

Matthew 18 is foundational for Catholic understanding of the Church as a community of forgiveness and reconciliation. The chapter addresses governance, scandal, correction, and mercy — the essential dynamics of ecclesial life. Catholic canon law draws on 18:15-17 for the principles of fraternal correction and the process of ecclesiastical discipline.

## Humility, Scandal, and the Lost Sheep

Jesus' teaching on becoming like children (18:1-5), the warning against causing scandal (18:6-9), and the Parable of the Lost Sheep (18:10-14) establish the Church's fundamental posture: care for the vulnerable and pursuit of the lost. Catholic social teaching's "preferential option for the poor" resonates with Jesus' concern for "the least of these."

## Binding and Loosing, and the Unforgiving Servant

The authority to "bind and loose" (18:18) extends the Petrine authority of 16:19 to the community of disciples. Catholic theology sees here the foundation of the Sacrament of Reconciliation — the Church's power to forgive or retain sins in Christ's name. The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant (18:21-35) — the man forgiven an enormous debt who refuses to forgive a small one — grounds the Catholic teaching that receiving God's mercy obliges extending mercy to others. The prayer "forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us" is not merely aspirational but sacramentally binding.
