## The Martyrdom of John the Baptist

The death of John the Baptist (14:1-12) is commemorated on August 29 as the Feast of the Passion (Beheading) of St. John the Baptist. Catholic tradition sees John's death as a prototype of Christian martyrdom — the ultimate witness to truth in the face of corrupt political power. John's courage in denouncing Herod's unlawful marriage has been invoked throughout Catholic history as a model for the Church's prophetic engagement with secular authority.

## The Feeding of the Five Thousand and the Eucharist

The feeding miracle (14:13-21) is read by Catholic theology as a direct prefiguring of the Eucharist. Jesus' actions — taking, blessing, breaking, and giving bread — mirror the fourfold Eucharistic action of the Mass. The superabundance of twelve baskets left over reflects the inexhaustible grace of the Eucharistic sacrifice. The *Catechism* (CCC 1335) explicitly connects this miracle to the institution of the Eucharist.

## Walking on Water

Peter's walk on water (14:28-31) illustrates the Catholic understanding of faith as gift and struggle. Peter's initial response in faith, his faltering, and Jesus' rescuing hand model the dynamic of grace: God calls, the believer responds, weakness intervenes, and Christ sustains. The disciples' confession, "Truly you are the Son of God" (14:33), anticipates Peter's fuller confession at Caesarea Philippi.
