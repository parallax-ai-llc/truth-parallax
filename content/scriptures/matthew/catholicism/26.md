## The Institution of the Eucharist

The Last Supper narrative (26:26-29) is the institution of the Eucharist, the "source and summit of the Christian life" (*Lumen Gentium* 11). Jesus' words — "This is my body... this is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins" — are repeated at every Catholic Mass. Catholic doctrine teaches that the bread and wine become, by transubstantiation, the true Body and Blood of Christ, while retaining the appearances (*species*) of bread and wine.

## Gethsemane and the Agony

Jesus' prayer in Gethsemane (26:36-46) — "My Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me; nevertheless, not as I will, but as you will" — is the model of obedient prayer in Catholic spirituality. The Agony in the Garden is the first of the Sorrowful Mysteries of the Rosary. Catholic theology emphasizes that Jesus' human will, while naturally shrinking from suffering, freely submits to the Father's will — a submission that is the essence of redemptive sacrifice.

## The Arrest, Trial, and Peter's Denial

Judas' betrayal (26:47-56) and Peter's denial (26:69-75) frame the Passion as a moment of comprehensive human failure. Catholic tradition sees in Peter's tears of repentance the possibility of restoration — a theme that grounds the Sacrament of Reconciliation. Even the greatest apostle falls; even the greatest fall can be redeemed through genuine contrition.
