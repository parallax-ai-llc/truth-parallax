## Tradition and Authority

Jesus' debate with the Pharisees over handwashing and *corban* (15:1-20) is a sensitive passage in Catholic-Protestant dialogue. Catholic interpreters note that Jesus condemns not tradition *per se* but human traditions that contradict divine commandment. The Catholic Church distinguishes between Sacred Tradition (the apostolic deposit of faith) and merely human traditions, arguing that Jesus' critique targets the latter, not the former.

## The Canaanite Woman and Universal Grace

The encounter with the Canaanite woman (15:21-28) is a powerful passage in Catholic missiology. Her persistent faith despite apparent rejection demonstrates that divine grace extends beyond ethnic and religious boundaries. Catholic social teaching draws on passages like this to affirm the dignity of all persons and the universal scope of God's salvific will (*Lumen Gentium* 16).

## The Feeding of the Four Thousand

The second feeding miracle (15:32-39) reinforces the Eucharistic typology of the first. Catholic commentators note its setting in Gentile territory as evidence that the Eucharistic table is open to all peoples. The seven baskets (compared to twelve in the first miracle) have been read symbolically: seven representing the Gentile nations, twelve representing the tribes of Israel.
