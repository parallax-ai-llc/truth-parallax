## The Resurrection

The resurrection of Jesus (28:1-10) is the foundation of Catholic faith. As Paul wrote, "If Christ has not been raised, your faith is futile" (1 Corinthians 15:17). Catholic theology insists on the bodily nature of the resurrection — not a metaphor or spiritual experience but a real, physical event that transformed the body of Jesus. The Easter Vigil, the most solemn liturgy of the Catholic year, celebrates this event with the lighting of the Paschal candle, the renewal of baptismal vows, and the Eucharist.

## The Great Commission and the Church's Mission

The Great Commission (28:18-20) is the charter of the Church's missionary activity. Catholic missiology draws from this passage the mandate to "make disciples of all nations." The Trinitarian baptismal formula — "in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit" — is the only valid form for Catholic baptism. The command to teach "all that I have commanded" grounds the Church's claim to a comprehensive teaching authority (Magisterium).

## "I Am with You Always"

Jesus' final promise — "I am with you always, to the end of the age" (28:20) — forms an *inclusio* with the name Immanuel ("God with us") in 1:23. Catholic theology sees this promise fulfilled in the Eucharistic presence, in the sacraments, and in the Church as the Body of Christ. The Gospel does not end with a farewell but with an enduring presence — the foundation of Catholic confidence that the Church, despite its human failings, remains the dwelling place of God among his people.
