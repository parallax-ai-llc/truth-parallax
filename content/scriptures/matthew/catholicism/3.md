## John the Baptist as Precursor

Catholic theology assigns John the Baptist a unique role as the last and greatest of the Old Testament prophets, the precursor (*prodromos*) who prepares the way for Christ. His call to repentance and his baptism of water are understood as preparatory rites that point toward the sacrament of baptism instituted by Christ.

## The Baptism of Jesus

The baptism of Jesus (3:13-17) is a Trinitarian theophany: the Son is baptized, the Spirit descends, and the Father speaks. Catholic theology holds that Jesus' baptism sanctifies the waters, prefiguring the sacrament of baptism. The *Catechism* (CCC 1224) teaches that Jesus' submission to John's baptism is an act of solidarity with sinful humanity, anticipating his "baptism" of death on the cross (cf. Luke 12:50).

## Sacramental Implications

Catholic sacramental theology finds in this passage the institution narrative for baptism. The descent of the Holy Spirit upon Jesus prefigures the outpouring of the Spirit in the sacrament. The Feast of the Baptism of the Lord, celebrated on the Sunday after Epiphany, closes the Christmas season and marks the beginning of Jesus' public ministry in the liturgical calendar.
