## The Apostolic Mission

Catholic theology reads the Missionary Discourse as the foundation of apostolic ministry and, by extension, of the ordained priesthood. Jesus' selection of the twelve (10:1-4) parallels the later institution of the apostolic college. The authority to heal, cast out demons, and proclaim the kingdom is understood as transmitted through apostolic succession to the bishops and, through them, to priests and deacons.

## Martyrdom and Witness

Jesus' warnings about persecution (10:16-25) have shaped Catholic hagiography profoundly. The promise that the Holy Spirit will speak through believers in times of trial (10:19-20) grounds the Catholic veneration of martyrs who witnessed courageously before hostile authorities. The history of the Church is read as the ongoing fulfillment of these predictions.

## Confessing Christ Before Others

The call to confess Christ publicly (10:32-33) is foundational for Catholic understanding of the relationship between interior faith and exterior witness. The sacraments of initiation — Baptism, Confirmation, and Eucharist — are the Church's structured way of forming believers who will acknowledge Christ "before others" at the cost of comfort or life itself.
