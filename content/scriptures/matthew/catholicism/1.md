## The Genealogy of Jesus Christ

Catholic interpretation of Matthew's genealogy emphasizes its theological architecture. The three sets of fourteen generations (1:17) are understood as a deliberate structuring of salvation history into epochs: from Abraham to David (promise), from David to the exile (kingdom and failure), and from the exile to Christ (restoration). The inclusion of four women — Tamar, Rahab, Ruth, and Bathsheba — is read by Catholic commentators as evidence of God's providence working through irregular and even scandalous circumstances, prefiguring God's choice of Mary.

## The Virgin Birth and Marian Theology

The virginal conception (1:18-25) is a foundational Marian dogma. Catholic teaching holds that Mary was a virgin before, during, and after the birth of Christ (*semper virgo*). The angel's message to Joseph and the fulfillment of Isaiah 7:14 establish Jesus as "God with us" (*Immanuel*), a theme the *Catechism of the Catholic Church* connects to the Incarnation as the supreme act of divine condescension.

## Joseph's Role

Catholic tradition venerates Joseph as the patron of the universal Church. His righteousness (1:19), his obedience to the angel, and his protective role in the Holy Family are celebrated liturgically on the Solemnity of St. Joseph (March 19). Pope Francis' apostolic letter *Patris Corde* (2020) drew extensively on Matthew's portrait of Joseph as a model of quiet faithfulness.
