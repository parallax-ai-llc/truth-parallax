## Palm Sunday and the Liturgical Year

The triumphal entry into Jerusalem (21:1-11) is the basis for the Palm Sunday liturgy, which opens Holy Week. Catholic parishes reenact the procession with blessed palms, and the Gospel of the Passion is read in its entirety. The crowd's acclamation, "Hosanna to the Son of David!" (21:9), is incorporated into the *Sanctus* of the Mass: "Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord. Hosanna in the highest."

## The Cleansing of the Temple

Jesus' cleansing of the temple (21:12-17) is read in Catholic theology as a prophetic act asserting God's holiness over corrupt practice. Catholic social teaching has drawn on this passage to critique economic injustice, particularly when it invades sacred or communal spaces. The temple, in Catholic typology, prefigures the Church — and the Church, like the temple, must be purified.

## Parables of Judgment

The Parable of the Two Sons (21:28-32) and the Parable of the Tenants (21:33-46) warn against presuming upon covenantal privilege. Catholic interpreters read these parables as calls to genuine faithfulness within the Church rather than as supersessionist claims against Judaism. The Second Vatican Council's *Nostra Aetate* (1965) explicitly repudiated the interpretation that the Jewish people bear collective guilt.
