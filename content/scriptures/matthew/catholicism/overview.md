## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Matthew is guided by Sacred Tradition, the Magisterium, and the patristic consensus. Matthew has held a privileged position in Catholic theology because of its ecclesiological content — it is the only Gospel that uses the word *ekklesia* ("church") — and its central role in establishing the Petrine primacy (16:17-19).

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Petrine Primacy
Matthew 16:18-19 ("You are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church") is the cornerstone of the Catholic doctrine of papal authority. Catholic tradition reads this as Jesus conferring supreme jurisdictional authority on Peter and, by succession, on the Bishop of Rome.

### The Sermon on the Mount
Catholic moral theology draws extensively on the Sermon on the Mount. The Beatitudes (5:3-12) are understood as the charter of Christian perfection, applicable to all believers. The *Catechism of the Catholic Church* treats the Sermon as the definitive expression of the New Law.

### Sacramental Reading
Catholic interpreters find sacramental imagery throughout Matthew: baptism (3:13-17; 28:19), Eucharist (26:26-29), penance and reconciliation (18:15-18), and holy orders (10:1-4; 28:18-20).

## Theological Emphases

- **Ecclesiology**: The Church as the visible community founded by Christ on the apostles
- **Merit and Grace**: The ethical demands of the Sermon as the fruit of grace, not opposed to it
- **Marian Devotion**: The infancy narrative (Chapters 1-2) as a foundation for Mariology
- **Social Teaching**: The works of mercy (25:31-46) as the basis for Catholic social doctrine
