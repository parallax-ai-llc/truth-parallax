## Judgment, Discernment, and the Golden Rule

Catholic moral theology reads Jesus' warning against judging (7:1-5) as a call to charity in judgment, not the abolition of moral discernment. The Church distinguishes between judging actions (necessary and proper) and judging persons' eternal destiny (reserved to God alone). The Golden Rule (7:12) is identified as a formulation of the natural law accessible to reason, confirming the universality of God's moral order.

## The Narrow Gate and the Way of Perfection

The narrow gate (7:13-14) resonates with the Catholic tradition of ascetical theology. The way of perfection described by St. John of the Cross and St. Teresa of Avila echoes Jesus' teaching that the path to life is demanding. The warning against false prophets (7:15-23) grounds the Catholic emphasis on the Magisterium as the authentic interpreter of doctrine.

## Building on Rock

The parable of the wise and foolish builders (7:24-27) concludes the Sermon. Catholic interpretation identifies the "rock" with Christ and, by extension, with the Church built on Peter (cf. 16:18). Hearing and doing Jesus' words is the foundation of the moral life. The crowds' astonishment at Jesus' authority (7:28-29) reflects the Catholic conviction that Jesus teaches not as a private interpreter but as the definitive revelation of God.
