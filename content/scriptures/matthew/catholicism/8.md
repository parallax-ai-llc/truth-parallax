## Healing and the Sacrament of the Sick

The healing narratives of Matthew 8 are read in Catholic theology as prefiguring the sacramental ministry of the Church. The healing of the leper (8:1-4) and Jesus' command to show himself to the priest connect physical healing with liturgical rite. The *Catechism* (CCC 1503-1505) identifies Jesus' healings as signs of the kingdom and foundations for the Sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick.

## The Centurion's Faith

The centurion's words, "Lord, I am not worthy to have you come under my roof" (8:8), are prayed at every Catholic Mass before Communion. This liturgical usage reflects the Catholic conviction that approaching Christ in the Eucharist requires both humility and faith. The centurion's faith, as a Gentile, anticipates the universality of the Church.

## Discipleship and the Storm

The cost of discipleship (8:18-22) and the calming of the storm (8:23-27) are read together as a catechesis on following Christ through suffering. The boat on the sea has been a Catholic symbol of the Church (*navis ecclesiae*) since the patristic era — tossed by waves but never capsized, because Christ is aboard.
