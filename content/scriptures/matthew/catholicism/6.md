## The Three Pillars of Piety

Catholic tradition identifies almsgiving, prayer, and fasting (6:1-18) as the three essential practices of the spiritual life. These form the structure of Lenten observance. Jesus' emphasis on practicing piety "in secret" does not prohibit communal worship but warns against performing devotion for human approval rather than divine encounter.

## The Lord's Prayer

The Lord's Prayer (6:9-13) holds a central place in Catholic liturgy and catechesis. It is prayed at every Mass, in the Liturgy of the Hours, and in the Rosary. The *Catechism* devotes an entire section (CCC 2759-2865) to its exposition. Catholic interpretation distinguishes seven petitions and understands the prayer as the summary of the entire Gospel. St. Teresa of Avila called it the perfect contemplative prayer.

## Trust in Providence

Jesus' teaching on anxiety and trust (6:25-34) — "Do not be anxious about your life" — is foundational for Catholic spirituality of abandonment to divine providence. St. Francis de Sales, St. Therese of Lisieux, and Jean-Pierre de Caussade developed rich spiritual traditions around this passage. The command to "seek first the kingdom of God" (6:33) orients all human striving toward the ultimate good.
