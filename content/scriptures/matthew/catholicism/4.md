## The Temptation and Lenten Spirituality

The temptation narrative (4:1-11) provides the theological foundation for the season of Lent. Jesus' forty days of fasting in the desert are the model for the Church's forty days of penitential preparation before Easter. Catholic Lenten disciplines of fasting, prayer, and almsgiving correspond to the three temptations Jesus overcomes. The desert fathers and the monastic tradition drew inspiration from Jesus' victory over Satan through self-denial and reliance on God's word.

## The Beginning of Ministry in Galilee

Jesus' move to Capernaum and the beginning of his Galilean ministry (4:12-17) fulfill Isaiah 9:1-2, bringing "great light" to those in darkness. Catholic lectionary readings assign this passage to the Third Sunday of Ordinary Time, emphasizing the call to conversion: "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand" (4:17).

## The Calling of the First Disciples

The calling of Peter, Andrew, James, and John (4:18-22) is foundational for Catholic understanding of apostolic vocation. Their immediate response — leaving nets and father — models the radical self-gift that Catholic theology sees in religious life and priestly vocation. The promise to make them "fishers of men" grounds the apostolic mission of the Church.
