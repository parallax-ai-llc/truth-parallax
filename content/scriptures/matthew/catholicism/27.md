## The Trial Before Pilate

The trial before Pilate (27:1-26) raises questions about the nature of justice and complicity in evil. Catholic teaching firmly rejects the charge of collective Jewish guilt for Jesus' death. The *Catechism* (CCC 597-598) states: "All sinners were the authors of Christ's Passion... the Church does not hesitate to impute to Christians the gravest responsibility for the torments inflicted upon Jesus." Pilate's handwashing is read as moral cowardice, not innocence.

## The Crucifixion and the Sacrifice of the Mass

The crucifixion (27:33-56) is the central event of Catholic soteriology. Catholic theology teaches that Jesus' death is a true sacrifice — the offering of himself to the Father for the redemption of the world. The Mass is understood not as a new sacrifice but as the sacramental re-presentation of Calvary, making Christ's once-for-all sacrifice present in every time and place. The tearing of the temple veil (27:51) signifies that through Christ's sacrifice, access to God is opened to all.

## Death and Burial

The reality of Jesus' death is essential to Catholic faith in the resurrection. Joseph of Arimathea's burial of Jesus in a new tomb (27:57-61) confirms this reality and fulfills Isaiah 53:9. Catholic Holy Saturday liturgy reflects on Christ's descent among the dead — the *Harrowing of Hell* — in which Christ liberates the righteous who died before his coming.
