## Jesus' Testimony About John

Catholic liturgy commemorates John the Baptist with two feasts — his Nativity (June 24) and his Martyrdom (August 29). Jesus' praise of John as "more than a prophet" (11:9) and as the Elijah who was to come (11:14) reflects the Catholic understanding of John's unique role in salvation history as the bridge between the Old and New Covenants.

## The Hidden Wisdom of God

Jesus' thanksgiving prayer (11:25-27) — "You have hidden these things from the wise and understanding and revealed them to little children" — is central to Catholic mystical theology. The passage affirms that divine wisdom is received not through intellectual mastery but through humility and openness. St. Therese of Lisieux's "Little Way" draws profoundly on this theme.

## Come to Me

The invitation "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden" (11:28-30) is read in Catholic devotion as Christ's perpetual invitation extended through the Eucharist and the sacraments. The Sacred Heart devotion, formally promoted since the 17th century, centers on this image of Christ's compassionate, self-giving love. The "easy yoke" is understood as the grace-filled life of discipleship within the Church.
