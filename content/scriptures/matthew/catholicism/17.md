## The Transfiguration

The Transfiguration (17:1-8) is celebrated as a major feast in the Catholic calendar (August 6). Catholic theology sees the event as a revelation of Christ's divine glory, confirming the faith of the disciples before the scandal of the cross. The presence of Moses and Elijah represents the witness of the Law and the Prophets to Christ. The Father's voice, "This is my beloved Son... listen to him," establishes Christ as the definitive revelation surpassing all prior prophets.

## Tabor Light and Eschatological Hope

Catholic mystical theology has drawn on the Transfiguration as a foretaste of the beatific vision — the glorified state of the redeemed in heaven. The radiance of Jesus' face and garments anticipates the resurrection body and the transformation of all creation. Pope Benedict XVI, in *Jesus of Nazareth*, described the Transfiguration as "a glimpse of what is to come."

## The Epileptic Boy and Temple Tax

The healing of the epileptic boy (17:14-21) after the disciples' failure illustrates the necessity of prayer and faith. Catholic tradition connects the episode to the sacramental life: where human effort fails, divine power mediated through prayer and the Church's ministry succeeds. The temple tax episode (17:24-27) reflects Jesus' freedom as Son while demonstrating his voluntary submission to legitimate authority — a principle applied in Catholic social teaching to the relationship between Church and state.
