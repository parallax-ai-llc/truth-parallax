## Marriage and Divorce

Jesus' teaching on marriage and divorce (19:1-12) is one of the most practically consequential passages in Protestant ethics. Jesus appeals to the creation ordinance of Genesis 2:24, declaring that God's original intention was lifelong, exclusive union. Protestant traditions have varied on the permissibility of divorce: most allow it for adultery (following the Matthean "exception clause," 19:9), and many also for desertion (following Paul in 1 Corinthians 7:15).

## Jesus and the Children

The blessing of the children (19:13-15) has been used in the infant baptism debate. Paedobaptist Protestants (Reformed, Lutheran, Methodist) see Jesus' welcome of children as supporting the inclusion of covenant children in baptism. Baptists and other credobaptist traditions read it as affirming children's value without implying baptismal practice.

## The Rich Young Man

The encounter with the rich young man (19:16-30) exposes the limits of self-achieved righteousness. The young man's claim to have kept all the commandments meets Jesus' demand: "Sell what you possess and give to the poor." Protestant interpreters see this not as a universal command to voluntary poverty but as a diagnostic revealing this man's idol. Jesus' saying about the camel and the eye of a needle (19:24) underscores that salvation is humanly impossible — "but with God all things are possible" (19:26).
