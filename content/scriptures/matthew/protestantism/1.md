## The Genealogy and Birth of the Messiah

Protestant interpreters regard Matthew's opening genealogy as a deliberate theological statement. By tracing Jesus' lineage through Abraham and David, Matthew establishes Jesus' legal right to the Messianic throne. The inclusion of four women — Tamar, Rahab, Ruth, and Bathsheba — has drawn significant attention; many Protestant commentators see in them a foreshadowing of God's inclusion of Gentiles and sinners in the plan of redemption.

## The Virgin Birth

The virginal conception of Jesus (1:18-25) is a non-negotiable doctrine for virtually all Protestant traditions. Matthew's citation of Isaiah 7:14 ("the virgin shall conceive") is read as direct prophetic fulfillment. Protestant interpreters debate whether *parthenos* represents a precise translation of the Hebrew *almah*, but affirm that the miraculous conception establishes Jesus' unique divine-human identity. The name "Immanuel" ("God with us") frames the entire Gospel, finding its counterpart in Jesus' closing promise: "I am with you always" (28:20).

## Joseph's Righteousness

Joseph's decision to divorce Mary quietly (1:19) and his subsequent obedience to the angel illustrate the Protestant emphasis on faith responding to God's word. Luther saw Joseph as a model of trust in divine promise against all human evidence.
