## The Community Discourse

Matthew 18 is the fourth of Jesus' five major discourses and addresses life within the community of disciples. Protestant ecclesiology draws heavily on this chapter for principles of church governance, discipline, and mutual care.

## Greatness and Humility

Jesus' response to the disciples' question "Who is the greatest in the kingdom?" (18:1-5) — placing a child in their midst — overturns all hierarchical conceptions of authority. Protestant interpreters, particularly in the Free Church tradition, see this as foundational for egalitarian church structures. The warning about causing "little ones" to stumble (18:6-9) establishes the community's responsibility to protect the vulnerable.

## The Lost Sheep and Church Discipline

The Parable of the Lost Sheep (18:10-14) in this context addresses not evangelism but pastoral care within the community. The procedure for church discipline (18:15-20) — private confrontation, witnesses, then the whole church — is the Protestant template for ecclesiastical discipline. The promise "where two or three are gathered in my name, there am I among them" (18:20) grounds church authority in Christ's presence rather than institutional structures. The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant (18:21-35) sets the outer boundary: the community that has received limitless forgiveness must extend limitless forgiveness.
