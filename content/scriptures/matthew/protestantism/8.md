## Healing Miracles and Faith

Matthew 8 collects a series of healing narratives that Protestant interpreters read as demonstrations of Jesus' Messianic authority. The healing of the leper (8:1-4) shows Jesus' willingness to touch the untouchable. The centurion's faith (8:5-13) is highlighted in Protestant exegesis as a model of faith — Jesus marvels at a Gentile's trust, foreshadowing the inclusion of all nations.

## The Cost of Discipleship

The two would-be followers (8:18-22) prompt Jesus' famous sayings about foxes having holes and letting the dead bury the dead. Protestant interpreters, particularly Bonhoeffer in *The Cost of Discipleship*, use these passages to challenge "cheap grace" — the presumption that following Jesus requires no sacrifice.

## Calming the Storm

The stilling of the storm (8:23-27) functions as both a nature miracle and a discipleship lesson. The disciples' cry, "Lord, save us!" (8:25), has been read in Protestant devotion as the fundamental prayer of faith. Jesus' rebuke — "Why are you afraid, O you of little faith?" — frames the Christian life as a journey from fear to trust in Christ's sovereign power over all creation.
