## The Missionary Discourse

Matthew 10 contains Jesus' instructions to the twelve apostles as he sends them on their first mission. Protestant interpreters debate the extent to which these instructions apply to the contemporary Church. Dispensationalists tend to limit them to the immediate mission to Israel; Reformed and evangelical interpreters see enduring principles for all Christian witness.

## Persecution and Courage

Jesus' warnings about persecution (10:16-25) have shaped Protestant theology of suffering. The promise that the Spirit will provide words in times of trial (10:19-20) has been claimed by martyrs from the Reformation onward. The instruction to "fear not those who kill the body but cannot kill the soul" (10:28) is foundational to Protestant courage under persecution.

## The Sword and Division

Jesus' declaration, "I have not come to bring peace, but a sword" (10:34), has generated extensive Protestant discussion. Most interpreters read this not as advocacy of violence but as a realistic prediction that faithful discipleship will divide families and communities. The closing promise — "whoever loses his life for my sake will find it" (10:39) — frames discipleship as radical self-surrender.
