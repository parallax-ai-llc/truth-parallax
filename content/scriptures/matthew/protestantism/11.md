## Jesus and John the Baptist

John's question from prison — "Are you the one who is to come, or shall we look for another?" (11:3) — is treated with pastoral sensitivity in Protestant commentary. Calvin and Luther both affirmed that even the greatest believers can experience seasons of doubt. Jesus' response points to his works as evidence, fulfilling Isaiah 35 and 61.

## Judgment on Unrepentant Cities

Jesus' woes against Chorazin, Bethsaida, and Capernaum (11:20-24) raise the Protestant doctrine of accountability. Greater privilege entails greater judgment. Protestant missions theology has drawn on this passage to emphasize the urgency of evangelism.

## Rest for the Weary

The invitation "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest" (11:28-30) is among the most beloved verses in Protestant devotion. Luther read it as the heart of the gospel — Christ offering relief from the burden of works-righteousness. The "easy yoke" is understood not as the abolition of all demands but as the replacement of legalistic burden with the gracious lordship of Christ.
