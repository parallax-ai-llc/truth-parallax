## Peter's Confession at Caesarea Philippi

Peter's declaration, "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" (16:16), is the climactic christological moment of Matthew's Gospel. Protestant interpreters unanimously affirm this as the definitive confession of faith. The critical question is Jesus' response: "On this rock I will build my church" (16:18). Protestants generally identify "the rock" as Peter's confession of faith rather than Peter's person, distinguishing their reading from the Catholic doctrine of Petrine primacy.

## The Keys of the Kingdom

The "keys of the kingdom" (16:19) and the authority to "bind and loose" are understood in Protestant theology as the authority to proclaim the gospel and exercise church discipline — an authority later extended to the whole community (18:18), not reserved for a single office. Reformed and Lutheran traditions connect this passage to the ministry of Word and sacrament.

## Take Up Your Cross

Jesus' first passion prediction (16:21-23) and his call to self-denial (16:24-28) define discipleship in terms of the cross. Peter's rebuke of Jesus and Jesus' sharp reply — "Get behind me, Satan!" — illustrate the radical incompatibility between human expectations and God's redemptive plan. Bonhoeffer's famous dictum, "When Christ calls a man, he bids him come and die," draws directly from this passage.
