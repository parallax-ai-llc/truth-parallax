## The Transfiguration

The Transfiguration (17:1-8) is a pivotal event in Protestant Christology. Jesus is revealed in divine glory alongside Moses (the Law) and Elijah (the Prophets), confirming that he is the fulfillment of both. The Father's voice — "This is my beloved Son... listen to him" — echoes the baptism (3:17) and reinforces the Protestant emphasis on Christ as the final and supreme authority, superseding the Law and the Prophets.

## The Epileptic Boy

The healing of the epileptic boy (17:14-21) after the disciples' failure becomes a teaching moment on faith. Jesus' statement, "If you have faith like a grain of mustard seed, you will say to this mountain, 'Move,' and it will move" (17:20), has been central to Protestant discussions of faith — not its quantity but its object (Christ) that matters.

## The Temple Tax

The episode of the temple tax (17:24-27) raises questions about Jesus' relationship to Jewish institutional obligations. Protestant interpreters note that Jesus pays the tax voluntarily, "so as not to give offense," while asserting his freedom as a "son" of the king. This passage has informed Protestant discussions of Christian freedom and the relationship between the church and civil obligations.
