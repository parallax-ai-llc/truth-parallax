## Sabbath Controversies

The two Sabbath controversies (12:1-14) — plucking grain and healing on the Sabbath — are central to Protestant understanding of Jesus' authority over the Law. Jesus' declaration, "The Son of Man is lord of the Sabbath" (12:8), is read as a Christological claim that recalibrates the entire legal system around his person.

## The Beelzebul Controversy

The Pharisees' accusation that Jesus casts out demons by Beelzebul (12:22-32) prompts Jesus' teaching on the unforgivable sin — blasphemy against the Holy Spirit. Protestant interpreters generally define this as the persistent, willful attribution of the Spirit's work to Satan, distinguishing it from ordinary sins that are freely forgiven through repentance and faith.

## The Sign of Jonah

Jesus' refusal to give a sign except "the sign of Jonah" (12:38-42) — three days and three nights in the belly of the earth — prefigures the crucifixion and resurrection. Protestant interpreters read this as a definitive rejection of sign-seeking faith in favor of trust in the proclaimed word. The comparison with Nineveh and the Queen of the South underscores that even Gentiles responded to lesser revelations.
