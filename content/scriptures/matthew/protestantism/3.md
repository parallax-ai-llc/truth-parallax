## John the Baptist's Ministry

Protestant interpreters see John the Baptist as the last of the Old Testament prophets, the forerunner promised in Malachi 3:1 and Isaiah 40:3. His call to repentance ("Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand," 3:2) echoes the prophetic tradition and establishes repentance as the prerequisite for entering the kingdom.

## The Baptism of Jesus

Jesus' baptism (3:13-17) is a pivotal moment in Protestant theology. The descent of the Spirit and the Father's voice ("This is my beloved Son") provide a Trinitarian revelation. Protestant interpreters generally understand Jesus' baptism not as indicating personal sinfulness but as his identification with sinful humanity — a vicarious act anticipating the cross.

## Baptismal Theology

For Protestants, Jesus' baptism raises important questions about the nature of Christian baptism. Baptists and other believer-baptism traditions see this passage as a model of conscious, voluntary baptism. Reformed and Lutheran traditions, while practicing infant baptism, emphasize the divine initiative displayed in the Father's declaration over the Son.
