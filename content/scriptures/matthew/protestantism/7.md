## Judging Others and the Golden Rule

Protestant interpreters read Jesus' warning against judgment (7:1-5) not as prohibiting all moral discernment but as condemning self-righteous hypocrisy. The Golden Rule (7:12) — "whatever you wish that others would do to you, do also to them" — is identified by many Protestant ethicists as the summary of the Law and the Prophets, a principle of natural law accessible to all.

## The Narrow Gate and False Prophets

The narrow gate (7:13-14) and the warning against false prophets (7:15-23) have been central to Protestant soteriology. The image of the narrow way reinforces the doctrine of salvation through genuine faith, not nominal affiliation. The test of "fruits" (7:16-20) provides the Protestant criterion for discerning true from false teaching — a principle foundational to the Reformation itself.

## The Wise and Foolish Builders (7:24-27)

The parable that concludes the Sermon — building on rock versus sand — summarizes the Protestant emphasis on obedient faith. Hearing Jesus' words is insufficient; one must act on them. Yet Protestant interpreters are careful to distinguish this from works-righteousness: the "doing" flows from a transformed heart, not from merit-seeking effort. The crowds' astonishment at Jesus' authority (7:28-29) underscores that he teaches not as one citing tradition but as the source of authority himself.
