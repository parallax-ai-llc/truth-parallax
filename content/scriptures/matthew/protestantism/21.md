## The Triumphal Entry

Jesus' entry into Jerusalem (21:1-11) fulfills Zechariah 9:9, presenting the Messiah as humble, riding on a donkey. Protestant interpreters emphasize that Jesus deliberately stages this entry to make a prophetic claim — he is the promised king, but his kingdom operates through humility and peace, not military conquest. The crowds' acclamation, "Hosanna to the Son of David!" (21:9), recognizes his Messianic identity.

## The Cleansing of the Temple

The temple cleansing (21:12-17) is read in Protestant theology as Jesus asserting authority over corrupt religious institutions. Luther drew a direct parallel between the money changers and the sale of indulgences. Jesus' citation of Isaiah 56:7 ("My house shall be called a house of prayer") and Jeremiah 7:11 ("den of robbers") frames the action as prophetic judgment.

## The Cursing of the Fig Tree and Parables of Judgment

The fig tree (21:18-22) symbolizes Israel's fruitlessness despite outward appearance. The Parable of the Two Sons (21:28-32) and the Parable of the Tenants (21:33-46) extend this theme, warning that the kingdom will be taken from those who bear no fruit and given to those who do. Protestant interpreters read these parables as applicable to any religious community that rests on privilege rather than faithfulness.
