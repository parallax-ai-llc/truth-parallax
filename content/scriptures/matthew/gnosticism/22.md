## The Wedding Garment and the Garment of Light

The Parable of the Wedding Banquet (22:1-14) is read in Gnostic terms as the invitation to the Pleromatic feast. The wedding garment is the "garment of light" — the spiritual body that the pneumatic person recovers through *gnosis*. Those without the garment are the *hylikoi* and *psychikoi* who lack the divine spark or have not awakened it. The outer darkness into which the ungarmented guest is thrown represents continued imprisonment in the material world.

## Render unto Caesar and the Two Realms

Jesus' teaching on Caesar and God (22:15-22) is reinterpreted in Gnostic thought as a statement about the two cosmic realms: Caesar represents the archonic powers that govern the material world; God represents the transcendent Father. "Render to Caesar what is Caesar's" acknowledges that the body and its material obligations belong to the Demiurge's domain. "Render to God what is God's" — the soul, the divine spark — belongs to the Pleroma and must be returned to its source.

## The Sadducees and Resurrection

The Sadducees' question about resurrection (22:23-33) and Jesus' response are read through the lens of Gnostic anthropology. The Gnostic understanding of resurrection is not the reconstitution of the physical body (as in orthodox Christianity) but the liberation of the divine spark from the material body — a spiritual resurrection that occurs through *gnosis* in this life, not through physical resuscitation at the end of time.
