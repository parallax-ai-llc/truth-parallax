## Inner Purity and the Rejection of Material Law

Jesus' teaching that defilement comes from the heart, not from external sources (15:10-20), is a key Gnostic text. It supports the Gnostic rejection of the Demiurge's purity laws as irrelevant to spiritual reality. The true impurity is ignorance (*agnoia*) — the failure to recognize one's divine origin. External observances, dietary laws, and ritual purification belong to the Demiurge's system and have no power over the soul's spiritual state.

## The Canaanite Woman and the Outsider's Gnosis

The Canaanite woman's encounter with Jesus (15:21-28) is read by Gnostic interpreters as illustrating that *gnosis* is available to those outside the Demiurge's chosen people. Her persistence in seeking healing despite Jesus' apparent rebuff shows the determination of the divine spark to find the light, regardless of ethnic or religious boundaries. The "crumbs from the table" may represent the fragments of gnosis that penetrate even the Demiurge's barriers.

## The Second Feeding

The feeding of the four thousand (15:32-39), in a Gentile context, extends the distribution of spiritual nourishment beyond Israel. In Gnostic reading, this confirms that the divine sparks are scattered throughout all nations, not confined to the Demiurge's elect people. The seven baskets (compared to twelve in the first feeding) may represent the seven archonic spheres through which *gnosis* must penetrate to reach those trapped in the lower realms.
