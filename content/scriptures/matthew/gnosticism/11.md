## Hidden Wisdom and the Elect

Jesus' prayer of thanksgiving (11:25-27) — "You have hidden these things from the wise and understanding and revealed them to little children" — is one of the most important passages in Gnostic exegesis. It confirms the Gnostic conviction that divine wisdom is not accessible through conventional learning or institutional religion but is revealed to those whom the Father chooses. The "wise and understanding" are the religious authorities of the Demiurge's system; the "little children" are the simple-hearted *pneumatikoi*.

## "No One Knows the Father Except the Son"

The exclusive claim of 11:27 — "No one knows the Father except the Son" — is read by Gnostics as proof that the God of the Old Testament (the Demiurge) is not the true Father. The Jewish scriptures, the Pharisees, and the entire religious establishment do not know the Father because they worship the Demiurge. Only the Son — the Christ from the Pleroma — has knowledge of the true, hidden God. This verse is the Gnostic charter for the radical distinction between the God of the Old Testament and the Father of Jesus.

## Rest for the Weary

The invitation "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest" (11:28) is the Gnostic gospel in miniature. The "labor" is the soul's enslavement to the material world and the Demiurge's law. The "rest" (*anapausis*) is a technical term in Gnostic theology for the return of the divine spark to the Pleroma — the cessation of the cosmic exile. The Gospel of Thomas (saying 90) preserves a parallel: "Come unto me, for my yoke is easy and my lordship is gentle, and you will find rest for yourselves.'
