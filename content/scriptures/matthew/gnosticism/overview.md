## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Matthew selectively appropriates the Gospel's sayings of Jesus, reading them as encoded transmissions of salvific knowledge (*gnosis*). While Matthew's narrative framework — rooted in Jewish Messianic fulfillment — is largely incompatible with Gnostic cosmology, individual sayings, parables, and episodes have been extracted and reinterpreted within Gnostic mythological systems. The Gospel of Thomas, a collection of sayings with significant Matthean overlap, provides a window into how Gnostic communities may have received and transformed Matthean material.

## Key Interpretive Strategies

### Selective Appropriation
Gnostic readers did not accept Matthew as a unified narrative. Instead, they mined it for sayings that could support their theology:
- **"The kingdom of heaven is within you"** (cf. Luke 17:21, Thomas 3): Evidence that salvation is interior knowledge, not external observance
- **"Do not cast your pearls before swine"** (7:6): Justification for keeping *gnosis* secret from the uninitiated
- **"Many are called, but few are chosen"** (22:14): Only the spiritual elite (*pneumatikoi*) receive true knowledge

### Anti-Demiurgic Reading
The Old Testament God referenced in Matthew's fulfillment quotations is, for Gnostics, the Demiurge. Jesus' relationship to Hebrew prophecy is thus reframed: he fulfills not the Demiurge's plan but the hidden purpose of the transcendent Father.

## Theological Emphases

- **Secret Teaching**: Matthew's parables as esoteric instruction for initiates (cf. 13:11, "To you it has been given to know the secrets of the kingdom")
- **Rejection of Material Atonement**: The Passion narrative is allegorized or rejected; the true Christ does not suffer physically
- **Baptism as Awakening**: Matthew 3 reinterpreted as the descent of the divine Christ upon the human Jesus
- **Resurrection as Spiritual**: The risen Christ of Matthew 28 understood as a spiritual, not bodily, appearance

## Key Gnostic Texts with Matthean Parallels

| Text | Relationship to Matthew |
|------|------------------------|
| Gospel of Thomas | ~50% overlap with Matthean sayings |
| Pistis Sophia | Extended commentary on Matthean sayings |
| Gospel of Philip | Sacramental reinterpretation of Matthean themes |
| Apocryphon of James | Secret post-resurrection teaching (cf. Matthew 28) |
