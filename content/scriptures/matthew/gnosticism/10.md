## The Mission of the Pneumatikoi

The sending of the twelve (10:1-4) is read in Gnostic tradition as the commissioning of those who have received *gnosis* to transmit it to others who are capable of receiving it. The authority to heal and cast out demons represents the pneumatic person's power over the archonic forces. However, unlike the orthodox reading, the Gnostic interpretation limits the audience: the mission is not to all people but to the "lost sheep" — the divine sparks scattered throughout the material world.

## Persecution by the Archons

Jesus' warnings about persecution (10:16-25) are read as predictions of the archons' hostility toward those who bear the light of *gnosis*. The "councils" and "synagogues" that persecute the disciples represent the institutional structures of the Demiurge's religion. The promise that the Spirit will speak through the persecuted (10:19-20) is understood as the Pleromatic *Pneuma* breaking through the archons' oppression.

## The Hidden Revealed

The saying "What I tell you in the dark, say in the light" (10:27) is pivotal for Gnostic communities. It acknowledges that Jesus' deepest teaching was given in private — "in the dark" — and authorizes its eventual proclamation. This verse justifies the Gnostic claim to possess secret teachings of Jesus not found in the public Gospels, transmitted through a hidden chain of initiation.
