## Pearls Before Swine

Jesus' saying, "Do not give dogs what is holy, and do not throw your pearls before pigs" (7:6), is one of the most frequently cited Matthean passages in Gnostic literature. It provides direct justification for the Gnostic practice of concealing esoteric teaching from the uninitiated. The "pearls" are the truths of *gnosis*; the "pigs" are the *hylikoi* (material people) or *psychikoi* (soul-people) who are incapable of receiving spiritual knowledge. Only the *pneumatikoi* (spiritual people) can appreciate the pearl.

## The Narrow Gate and the Few

The narrow gate (7:13-14) confirms the Gnostic conviction that salvation through *gnosis* is available only to the few. The "many" who take the wide path are those trapped in the Demiurge's religion of law and obedience; the "few" who find the narrow way are those who receive the secret knowledge transmitted by the Christ. The Valentinian division of humanity into three classes — material, psychic, and spiritual — maps onto this passage.

## The Wise Builder and the Foundation of Gnosis

The parable of the wise and foolish builders (7:24-27) is read as contrasting those who build on the foundation of *gnosis* (the rock) with those who build on the unstable ground of the Demiurge's law and prophets (the sand). The "storms" that test the foundation represent the archons' attacks on the awakened soul. Only knowledge of one's true divine origin provides a foundation that cannot be shaken.
