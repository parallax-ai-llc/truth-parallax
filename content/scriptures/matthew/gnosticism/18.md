## The Spiritual Community

Gnostic interpreters read Matthew 18 as addressing the inner life of the pneumatic community — the circle of initiates who have received *gnosis*. The emphasis on humility, mutual care, and forgiveness describes the social dynamics of Gnostic conventicles: small, intimate groups bound by shared esoteric knowledge rather than institutional structures.

## Becoming Like Children

Jesus' teaching on becoming like children (18:1-5) is read not as moral instruction but as a call to recover the original simplicity of the divine spark before it was encrusted with archonic conditioning. The "child" represents the pre-lapsarian state of the pneumatic soul — uncorrupted by the Demiurge's systems of knowledge, hierarchy, and law. To "become like a child" is to strip away the false self constructed by the archons.

## "Where Two or Three Are Gathered"

The promise of Christ's presence "where two or three are gathered in my name" (18:20) is the charter of Gnostic worship. It validates small, informal gatherings — the typical format of Gnostic conventicles — over against the institutional structures of the proto-orthodox church. The Christ is not found in cathedrals and hierarchies but in intimate circles where *gnosis* is shared. The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant (18:23-35) warns that even within the pneumatic community, the failure to extend the freedom one has received leads to spiritual imprisonment.
