## Prayer in Secret and the Hidden God

Jesus' instruction to pray "in secret" (6:6) — "your Father who sees in secret will reward you" — is foundational for Gnostic devotion. The "secret" place of prayer is the inner chamber of the soul, the point of contact between the divine spark and the transcendent Father. The Demiurge, who governs the visible world, cannot penetrate this interior space. True prayer bypasses the cosmic hierarchy and reaches the unknown God directly.

## The Lord's Prayer Reinterpreted

The Lord's Prayer receives a radical reinterpretation in Gnostic exegesis. "Our Father who art in heaven" addresses not the sky-god of the Old Testament but the Father beyond all heavens. "Thy kingdom come" invokes the restoration of the Pleroma and the dissolution of the material cosmos. "Deliver us from evil" is a plea for liberation from the Demiurge's domain. The Valentinian teacher Marcus reportedly offered an expanded version of the prayer that made these Gnostic meanings explicit.

## Treasure in Heaven

The teaching on storing treasures in heaven rather than on earth (6:19-21) is read as a direct instruction to invest in spiritual knowledge rather than material accumulation. The "moth and rust" that destroy earthly treasure represent the corrosive nature of the material world itself — a world destined for dissolution. True treasure is *gnosis*, which belongs to the imperishable realm of the Pleroma.
