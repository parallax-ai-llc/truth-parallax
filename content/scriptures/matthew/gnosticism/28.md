## The Resurrection as Spiritual Awakening

Gnostic interpreters read the resurrection (28:1-10) not as the resuscitation of a physical body but as the definitive emergence of the spiritual Christ from the material husk. The "earthquake" (28:2) signals the material world's shuddering response to the Pleroma's power. The angel rolling back the stone represents the removal of the archonic barrier between the material and spiritual realms. The empty tomb is proof not that the body was physically transformed but that the divine has departed its material prison.

## Post-Resurrection Teaching

The Gnostic tradition placed enormous emphasis on the period between the resurrection and the ascension as the time when Jesus delivered his most esoteric teachings. Texts like the *Apocryphon of James*, the *Pistis Sophia*, and the *Books of Jeu* present extended post-resurrection dialogues in which the risen Christ reveals the secrets of the Pleroma, the structure of the archonic heavens, and the passwords needed for the soul's ascent. Matthew 28's brief account is, for Gnostics, merely the public surface of a vast, hidden teaching.

## The Great Commission Reinterpreted

The Great Commission (28:18-20) is given an esoteric reading: "make disciples" means transmit *gnosis* to those capable of receiving it; "baptizing" is the Gnostic rite of illumination, not water baptism; "teaching them to observe all that I have commanded" refers to the secret teachings, not the public moral instruction. The promise "I am with you always" (28:20) assures the pneumatic community that the Pleromatic Christ remains present in the world even after the visible Jesus has departed — present not in institutional structures but in the living transmission of *gnosis* from teacher to student.
