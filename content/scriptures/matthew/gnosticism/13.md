## Parables as Encoded Gnosis

Matthew 13 is the richest chapter for Gnostic interpretation. Jesus' statement that parables conceal the "secrets of the kingdom of heaven" (13:11) from the crowds while revealing them to the disciples is direct evidence, in Gnostic reading, that Jesus taught on two levels: an exoteric public teaching and an esoteric private teaching reserved for the initiated. The parables are not simple illustrations but encoded transmissions of *gnosis*.

## The Sower and the Three Classes

The Parable of the Sower (13:1-23) maps directly onto the Valentinian tripartite anthropology. The seed on the path represents the *hylikoi* (material people), who cannot receive the word at all. The rocky ground and thorns represent the *psychikoi* (soul-people), who receive it partially but are overcome by the Demiurge's world. The good soil represents the *pneumatikoi* (spiritual people), who receive the gnosis and bear fruit. Only the last class is capable of salvation.

## The Hidden Treasure and the Pearl

The parables of the Hidden Treasure (13:44) and the Pearl of Great Price (13:45-46) describe the discovery of *gnosis* — the supreme value that demands the renunciation of everything else. In the Gospel of Thomas (saying 76), the pearl is explicitly connected to the kingdom of the Father. The merchant who sells everything for the pearl is the pneumatic person who, upon discovering their true divine origin, abandons all material attachments and pursues the way of return to the Pleroma.
