## The Sermon as Esoteric Teaching

Gnostic interpreters read the Sermon on the Mount as Jesus' public teaching that conceals deeper esoteric truths. The Gospel of Thomas, which shares many sayings with the Sermon, preserves these teachings in a form stripped of Matthew's narrative framework — suggesting, for Gnostic scholars, that the original sayings were transmitted as secret wisdom rather than public discourse.

## The Beatitudes as Stages of Awakening

The Beatitudes are reinterpreted as descriptions of the pneumatic person's journey toward *gnosis*: "poor in spirit" describes the recognition of the material world's poverty; "mourning" is grief over the soul's exile from the Pleroma; "hungering and thirsting for righteousness" is the desire for salvific knowledge. Each Beatitude marks a stage in the awakening of the divine spark trapped within the material body.

## "Be Perfect" — The Call to Gnosis

The command "Be perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect" (5:48) is the climactic call of the Sermon in Gnostic reading. The "heavenly Father" is not the Demiurge but the true, transcendent God. "Perfection" (*teleios*) is the attainment of complete *gnosis* — the full awakening of the divine spark to its origin in the Pleroma. This is not moral perfection in the orthodox sense but ontological transformation: becoming what one truly is.
