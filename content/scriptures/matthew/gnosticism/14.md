## The Death of the Baptist and Prophetic Limits

The execution of John the Baptist (14:1-12) is read in Gnostic thought as illustrating the fate of the Demiurge's prophets within the Demiurge's own system. John, the greatest figure the old dispensation could produce, is destroyed by the worldly power that the Demiurge permits. This confirms the Gnostic view that the Demiurge cannot protect even his own servants from the chaos of his creation.

## Spiritual Nourishment

The feeding of the five thousand (14:13-21) is allegorized in Gnostic exegesis as the distribution of spiritual nourishment — *gnosis* itself. The loaves represent the teachings of the Christ; the fish, in some Gnostic systems, symbolize the souls drawn from the sea of matter. The twelve baskets of leftovers represent the superabundance of divine knowledge, which exceeds what the immediate audience can absorb.

## Walking on Water and Transcendence

Jesus walking on water (14:22-33) demonstrates the pneumatic Christ's transcendence over the material elements. Water, as primordial matter, is the substance of the Demiurge's creation. The Christ walks above it, untouched by it. Peter's attempt to walk and his sinking represent the *psychikos* who aspires to pneumatic transcendence but, being only partially awakened, falls back into the material when fear (the archons' weapon) overwhelms faith.
