## The Dissolution of the Material Cosmos

The Olivet Discourse (Matthew 24) is read in Gnostic eschatology as the prophecy of the material world's dissolution. The destruction of the temple (24:2) symbolizes the end of the Demiurge's institutional power. The cosmic signs — the sun darkened, the moon failing, the stars falling (24:29) — describe the collapse of the archonic celestial system. This is not a disaster but a liberation: the material prison is dismantled, and the divine sparks are freed.

## False Christs and Archonic Deception

Jesus' warnings about false messiahs (24:4-5, 23-26) are read as cautions against the archons' attempts to prevent the pneumatic elect from receiving true *gnosis*. The Demiurge and his agents will produce counterfeit revelations designed to keep the divine sparks in bondage. True discernment — the ability to distinguish Pleromatic truth from archonic imitation — is the essential pneumatic skill.

## The Coming of the Son of Man

The coming of the Son of Man "on the clouds of heaven with power and great glory" (24:30) is interpreted not as a future physical event but as the eschatological revelation of the Pleromatic Christ in his full glory — the final tearing of the veil between the Demiurge's cosmos and the Pleroma. When this occurs, all illusions dissolve, all archonic structures collapse, and the divine sparks return to their source.
