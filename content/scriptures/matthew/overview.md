## Overview

The Gospel of Matthew is the first book of the New Testament and one of the three Synoptic Gospels. Traditionally attributed to Matthew (Levi), a tax collector called by Jesus to be one of the twelve apostles, this Gospel presents Jesus as the long-awaited Jewish Messiah who fulfills the prophecies and Law of the Hebrew Bible. Its Hebrew-flavored name, *Mattityahu* ("Gift of YHWH"), reflects the deeply Jewish orientation of the text.

## Structure

Matthew is organized into five major discourse sections, each ending with a formulaic transition ("When Jesus had finished saying these things..."), widely seen as paralleling the five books of the Torah:

1. **The Sermon on the Mount** (Chapters 5–7): The ethical teaching of the kingdom
2. **The Missionary Discourse** (Chapter 10): Instructions for the twelve apostles
3. **The Parables Discourse** (Chapter 13): Parables of the kingdom of heaven
4. **The Community Discourse** (Chapter 18): Life within the community of disciples
5. **The Olivet Discourse** (Chapters 24–25): Eschatological teaching on the end times

These discourses are framed by narrative sections covering Jesus' birth and genealogy (Chapters 1–2), his ministry in Galilee (Chapters 3–18), his journey to and ministry in Jerusalem (Chapters 19–23), and his passion, death, and resurrection (Chapters 26–28).

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Matthew holds a distinctive position across multiple religious traditions:

- **Christianity**: The most-cited Gospel in the early Church; foundational for ecclesiology and ethics
- **Judaism**: A primary text for understanding the Jewish-Christian dialogue and the parting of the ways
- **Islam**: Jesus (*Isa*) is a revered prophet; Matthean teachings on charity, prayer, and humility resonate with Quranic values
- **Gnosticism**: Certain sayings and the emphasis on hidden knowledge attracted Gnostic interpreters

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Fulfillment | Jesus as the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecy ("This was to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet...") |
| Kingdom of Heaven | Matthew's preferred term (used over 30 times) for the reign of God |
| Righteousness | A surpassing righteousness demanded of Jesus' followers (5:20) |
| Ecclesiology | The only Gospel to use the word *ekklesia* ("church," 16:18, 18:17) |
| Judgment | Vivid parables of final judgment (sheep and goats, wise and foolish virgins) |
| Discipleship | The cost and character of following Jesus |
