## Tradition: Human and Divine

Jesus' critique of the Pharisees' human traditions (15:1-9) is understood in Orthodoxy not as a rejection of tradition itself but as a distinction between authentic Sacred Tradition (the life of the Holy Spirit in the Church) and merely human customs that contradict divine commandment. Orthodox theology holds that Holy Tradition — encompassing Scripture, the Councils, the Fathers, the liturgy, and iconography — is the living memory of the Church, not a human invention.

## The Canaanite Woman's Faith

The Canaanite woman's encounter with Jesus (15:21-28) is celebrated in Orthodox preaching as one of the great examples of persistent, humble faith. Her willingness to accept even "crumbs from the table" demonstrates the Orthodox understanding that faith is not passive acceptance but active, even bold, engagement with God. Jesus' commendation — "Great is your faith!" — shows that the boundaries of grace extend far beyond expected limits.

## The Second Feeding Miracle

The feeding of the four thousand (15:32-39) in Gentile territory extends the Eucharistic type to the nations. Orthodox liturgical theology sees in the two feeding miracles a foreshadowing of the Eucharist offered to all peoples — Jew and Gentile alike — in the one Body of Christ.
