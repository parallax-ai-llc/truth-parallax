## The Incarnation of the Word

Orthodox interpretation of Matthew 1 centers on the mystery of the Incarnation — God the Word taking flesh from the Virgin Mary, the Theotokos ("God-bearer"). The genealogy is not merely a historical record but a theological statement: the eternal Son of God enters fully into human history, assuming a real human lineage with all its complexities, including the four women whose presence signals God's surprising providence.

## The Theotokos and the Virgin Birth

The virginal conception (1:18-25) is confessed in the Orthodox Creed and celebrated in numerous hymns and icons. Orthodox theology affirms Mary's perpetual virginity and venerates her as the supreme example of human cooperation with divine grace (*synergeia*). The name Immanuel ("God with us") expresses the central Orthodox conviction: in Christ, God does not merely act upon the world from outside but enters it from within.

## Joseph the Righteous

The Orthodox Church commemorates St. Joseph on the Sunday after the Nativity. His righteousness, his obedience to the angelic revelation, and his protective role in the Holy Family are celebrated as virtues of faith and humility. Orthodox hymnography praises Joseph as the guardian of the mystery of the Incarnation.
