## The Beatitudes in Orthodox Worship

The Beatitudes (5:3-12) are sung at every Divine Liturgy in the Orthodox Church during the Third Antiphon. This liturgical placement makes them not merely ethical instruction but a living prayer: the congregation sings itself into the kingdom described by Christ. Each Beatitude describes a stage on the path of *theosis* — the progressive transformation of the human person into the likeness of God.

## The Sermon as the Path of Theosis

Orthodox interpretation reads the Sermon on the Mount as the charter of deification. "Be perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect" (5:48) is not an impossible demand but the genuine vocation of every baptized person, achievable through the synergy of divine grace and human effort. The Fathers — particularly St. Gregory of Nyssa in *On the Beatitudes* — taught that perfection is not a static achievement but an infinite progress (*epektasis*) into the inexhaustible life of God.

## The Antitheses and the Interior Life

The antitheses (5:21-48) are read in Orthodox ascetical theology as addressing the inner dispositions of the heart — the *logismoi* (thoughts) that precede sinful action. The commandment against anger (5:21-26), lust (5:27-30), and hatred of enemies (5:43-48) targets the roots of passion. The Orthodox tradition of *nepsis* (watchfulness over thoughts) draws directly on this interior ethic.
