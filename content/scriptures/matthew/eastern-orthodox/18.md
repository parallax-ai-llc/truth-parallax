## The Community of Forgiveness

Matthew 18 addresses the life of the Christian community. Orthodox ecclesiology reads this chapter as a blueprint for the parish and monastic community alike — a community defined by humility, mutual care, and unlimited forgiveness. The chapter's teaching is lived out in the Orthodox practice of mutual forgiveness on Forgiveness Sunday, the eve of Great Lent, when all members of the community ask and grant forgiveness of one another.

## Becoming Like Children

Jesus' teaching on becoming like children (18:1-5) is understood in Orthodox spirituality not as endorsing naivety but as describing the qualities of humility, trust, and openness that characterize the spiritual life. The patristic tradition, particularly St. John Climacus, connects childlike simplicity with the virtue of *apatheia* — freedom from the domination of disordered passions.

## The Lost Sheep and Forgiveness Without Limit

The Parable of the Lost Sheep (18:10-14) and Peter's question about forgiveness (18:21-22) establish the Orthodox conviction that God's mercy is infinite and that the Christian community must mirror this mercy. The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant (18:23-35) warns that receiving God's forgiveness while withholding it from others is a spiritual catastrophe. Orthodox confession always includes the penitent's commitment to forgive those who have wronged them.
