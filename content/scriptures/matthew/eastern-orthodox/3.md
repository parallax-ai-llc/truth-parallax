## The Feast of Theophany

The baptism of Jesus (3:13-17) is celebrated in the Orthodox Church as the Feast of Theophany (January 6/19), one of the twelve Great Feasts. The term "Theophany" ("manifestation of God") reflects the Trinitarian revelation at the Jordan: the Father's voice, the Son's baptism, and the Spirit's descent. The Great Blessing of Waters — in which rivers, lakes, and seas are blessed — liturgically reenacts Christ's sanctification of the waters of creation.

## The Forerunner

St. John the Baptist is known in Orthodoxy as the "Forerunner" (*Prodromos*), the greatest of the prophets. His role is to prepare the way for Christ, and he is depicted in Orthodox iconography with wings (symbolizing his angelic mission) and often pointing toward Christ. His call to repentance establishes the fundamental posture of the spiritual life.

## Baptismal Theology

Orthodox baptism is performed by triple immersion in the name of the Trinity, directly following the Matthean pattern. The baptismal liturgy explicitly invokes the Jordan event, and the newly baptized person is understood to die and rise with Christ. Chrismation (anointing with holy oil) follows immediately, corresponding to the descent of the Spirit upon Jesus.
