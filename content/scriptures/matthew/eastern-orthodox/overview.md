## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Matthew is anchored in the consensus of the Church Fathers and the liturgical life of the Church. Matthew is the most frequently read Gospel in Orthodox worship, with its passages woven throughout the liturgical calendar. The Orthodox reading emphasizes *theosis* (deification) — humanity's calling to participate in the divine nature — as the overarching lens through which Jesus' teaching and ministry are understood.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Patristic Exegesis
The Greek Fathers provide the authoritative framework:
- **St. John Chrysostom**: His 90 *Homilies on Matthew* remain the most influential Orthodox commentary
- **St. Maximus the Confessor**: Reads Matthew's ethical teaching through the lens of cosmic restoration
- **St. Gregory Palamas**: The Transfiguration (Matthew 17) as revelation of God's uncreated energies

### Liturgical Context
Matthew is read systematically throughout the Orthodox liturgical year. The Beatitudes are sung at every Divine Liturgy. The Transfiguration, Passion, and Resurrection narratives from Matthew shape the Church's most important feasts.

## Theological Emphases

- **Theosis**: The Sermon on the Mount as the pathway to deification ("Be perfect as your heavenly Father is perfect," 5:48)
- **Transfiguration**: Matthew 17 as the paradigmatic revelation of Christ's divine glory and humanity's destiny
- **The Beatitudes**: Sung liturgically as the map of the spiritual life
- **Ancestral Sin**: Matthew's account of the human condition understood through the lens of ancestral rather than inherited guilt
