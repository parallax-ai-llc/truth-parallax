## The Three Pillars of Lenten Practice

Orthodox Lenten discipline is structured around the three practices of Matthew 6: almsgiving (6:1-4), prayer (6:5-15), and fasting (6:16-18). These are not isolated acts of piety but interconnected dimensions of repentance. Jesus' insistence on practicing them "in secret" resonates with the Orthodox emphasis on the interior life — the hidden struggle of the heart that only God sees.

## The Lord's Prayer in Orthodox Worship

The Lord's Prayer is prayed at every Divine Liturgy and in the daily offices. Orthodox commentary, following St. Maximus the Confessor, reads the seven petitions as a complete summary of the spiritual life: from the hallowing of God's name to deliverance from evil, the prayer traces the soul's ascent from earthly need to divine communion. The doxology ("For thine is the kingdom...") is included in the Orthodox liturgical tradition.

## Providence and the Freedom from Anxiety

Jesus' teaching on anxiety (6:25-34) is central to Orthodox spirituality of trust in divine providence. The command "Do not be anxious about tomorrow" has shaped the tradition of monastic simplicity and the rejection of material accumulation. St. John Climacus, in *The Ladder of Divine Ascent*, identifies anxiety as a failure of faith — the opposite of the peaceful trust (*hesychia*) that characterizes the mature spiritual life.
