## The Martyrdom of the Forerunner

The beheading of St. John the Baptist (14:1-12) is commemorated on August 29 as one of the most solemn days in the Orthodox calendar, observed with strict fasting. Orthodox hymnography celebrates John's courage in speaking truth to power and sees his death as the seal of his prophetic ministry. His head, venerated as a relic in multiple locations, symbolizes the cost of bearing witness to divine truth.

## The Feeding Miracle and the Eucharist

The feeding of the five thousand (14:13-21) is read in Orthodox theology as a direct prefiguring of the Divine Liturgy. Jesus' actions — taking, blessing, breaking, giving — mirror the Eucharistic rite. The superabundance of twelve baskets reflects the inexhaustible nature of divine grace communicated through the Holy Mysteries. The entire creation participates in this abundance when consecrated by God's blessing.

## Walking on Water

Peter's walk on the water (14:28-31) is a paradigmatic moment of faith and faltering. Orthodox spiritual directors use this episode to teach that the spiritual life requires keeping one's gaze fixed on Christ. When attention shifts to the waves — circumstances, fears, passions — the believer begins to sink. The remedy is always the same: the cry "Lord, save me!" and the hand of Christ extended in rescue.
