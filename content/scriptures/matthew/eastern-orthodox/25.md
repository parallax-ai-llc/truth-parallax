## The Ten Virgins and Spiritual Readiness

The Parable of the Ten Virgins (25:1-13) is chanted on Holy Tuesday in the Orthodox liturgical calendar. The oil in the lamps is understood by the Fathers as the grace of the Holy Spirit, maintained through prayer, fasting, and the practice of virtue. St. Seraphim of Sarov famously taught that "the true aim of the Christian life is the acquisition of the Holy Spirit" — a formulation that draws directly on this parable.

## The Talents and Accountability

The Parable of the Talents (25:14-30) teaches that every person is entrusted with gifts for the service of the kingdom. Orthodox spiritual theology understands these "talents" broadly — encompassing natural abilities, spiritual gifts, material resources, and even suffering. The buried talent represents the refusal to engage with the world in love and service, a withdrawal born of fear rather than faith.

## The Last Judgment

The judgment of the sheep and goats (25:31-46) is depicted in Orthodox iconography on the western wall of churches — the last image the faithful see as they leave the nave. This placement is deliberate: the works of mercy are not an abstract ideal but the concrete criterion of the Christian life. Orthodox tradition lists corporal and spiritual works of mercy as the practical expression of love for the Christ who is encountered in the suffering neighbor.
