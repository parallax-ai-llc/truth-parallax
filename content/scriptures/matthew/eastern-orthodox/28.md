## Pascha: The Resurrection

The resurrection of Jesus (28:1-10) is celebrated in the Orthodox Church as Pascha — the Feast of Feasts, the most joyous day of the liturgical year. The Paschal liturgy begins at midnight with the proclamation: "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life!" This troparion, repeated hundreds of times during the Paschal season, encapsulates the Orthodox understanding of the resurrection as the definitive victory over death, sin, and the powers of darkness.

## The Great Commission

The Great Commission (28:18-20) establishes the Church's mission to "make disciples of all nations." Orthodox missiology reads this passage as the charter for the Church's apostolic expansion — from Jerusalem to Antioch, Constantinople, Alexandria, and eventually to every continent. The Trinitarian baptismal formula is the sacramental heart of Orthodox initiation, followed immediately by Chrismation and first Communion.

## "I Am with You Always"

The Gospel's final words — "I am with you always, to the end of the age" (28:20) — form an *inclusio* with "Immanuel" (1:23) and express the deepest conviction of Orthodox faith: God is not distant but intimately present in his Church through the Holy Spirit, the sacraments, and the communion of saints. This promise is not merely a comfort but a reality experienced in every Divine Liturgy, where heaven and earth meet and Christ is truly present among his people.
