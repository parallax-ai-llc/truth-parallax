## The Apostolic Mission

Orthodox ecclesiology reads the sending of the twelve (10:1-4) as the foundation of the apostolic ministry that continues in the episcopate. The authority given to the apostles — to heal, cast out demons, and proclaim the kingdom — is transmitted through the unbroken succession of bishops from the apostles to the present. The Orthodox Church understands itself as the continuation of this apostolic community.

## Persecution and Martyrdom

Jesus' warnings about persecution (10:16-25) have been profoundly actualized in Orthodox history — from the Roman persecutions to the Ottoman *yoke* to the Soviet destruction of churches and monasteries. The promise of the Spirit's guidance in times of trial (10:19-20) is celebrated in the lives of the Neo-Martyrs and Confessors of the 20th century, who are among the most venerated saints in contemporary Orthodoxy.

## Confession and the Cross

The call to confess Christ before others (10:32-33) and to take up the cross (10:38) defines the Orthodox understanding of witness (*martyria*). Every Christian is called to be a witness, and the ultimate form of witness is martyrdom. The white garment given at baptism symbolizes this readiness to bear witness even unto death.
