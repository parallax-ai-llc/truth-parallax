## The Entry into Jerusalem and Palm Sunday

The triumphal entry (21:1-11) is celebrated on Palm Sunday, the beginning of Holy Week in the Orthodox calendar. Parishes distribute blessed palm or willow branches, and children figure prominently in the celebration — echoing the children who cried "Hosanna" in the temple (21:15). The hymn of the day declares: "Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord!" — words incorporated into the Divine Liturgy's Sanctus.

## The Cleansing of the Temple

Jesus' cleansing of the temple (21:12-17) is read as a prophetic act of purification. Orthodox theology sees the temple as a type of the Church and of the human person: both must be cleansed of everything that obstructs communion with God. The monastic tradition of *nepsis* (watchfulness) extends this principle to the interior temple of the heart — driving out the "money changers" of disordered thoughts and passions.

## Parables of Fruitfulness

The cursing of the fig tree (21:18-22) and the parables that follow (21:28-46) warn against the appearance of piety without the substance of faithfulness. Orthodox spiritual theology emphasizes that the spiritual life must bear visible fruit — not merely in external observance but in the transformation of character, the practice of mercy, and the cultivation of the virtues.
