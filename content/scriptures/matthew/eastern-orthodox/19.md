## Marriage as Sacred Mystery

Orthodox theology treats marriage as one of the seven Holy Mysteries (sacraments). Jesus' teaching on the indissolubility of marriage (19:1-9) is foundational, though the Orthodox Church, following the principle of *oikonomia* (pastoral economy), permits divorce and remarriage in limited circumstances — recognizing that the ideal of lifelong union may be broken by human sin while offering a path of healing and restoration.

## Monasticism and the Kingdom

Jesus' saying about those who have become "eunuchs for the sake of the kingdom of heaven" (19:12) is the primary scriptural foundation for Orthodox monasticism. The monastic life — lived on Mount Athos, in the deserts of Egypt, and in communities worldwide — represents the most radical response to this call. Monasticism is regarded not as an escape from the world but as the heart of the Church's prayer.

## The Rich Young Man

The encounter with the rich young man (19:16-22) and Jesus' words about the difficulty of the rich entering the kingdom are read in Orthodox ethics as a warning against attachment to material possessions. The ideal of evangelical poverty, embodied in the monastic vow, is not a rejection of creation's goodness but a liberation from the passions that distort humanity's relationship to created things.
