## The Feast of Epiphany and the Magi

The visit of the Magi is associated in the Orthodox tradition with the Feast of the Nativity (December 25/January 7). The Magi represent the nations of the earth recognizing Christ, and their gifts are read symbolically: gold for his kingship, frankincense for his divinity, myrrh for his mortality. Orthodox iconography depicts the Magi in varied ethnic dress, emphasizing the universality of Christ's epiphany.

## The Massacre of the Innocents

The Holy Innocents are commemorated on December 29 in the Orthodox calendar. They are venerated as the first martyrs — those who died not by their own will but by God's providence in testimony to Christ. Their innocent suffering prefigures the Passion and anticipates the reality that the kingdom of God is built on sacrifice.

## The Flight into Egypt

The Holy Family's flight to Egypt is a beloved theme in Orthodox iconography and hymnography. Jesus' entry into Egypt is read as the sanctification of the Gentile world: the idols of Egypt fall before the presence of the true God (cf. Isaiah 19:1). The return from Egypt recapitulates Israel's exodus, establishing Jesus as the true Israel who fulfills the covenant journey.
