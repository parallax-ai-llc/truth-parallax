## Purity of the Heart

Jesus' teaching that defilement comes from the heart, not from external sources (15:10-20), resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *niyyah* (intention) as the foundation of all action. A famous hadith states: "Actions are judged by intentions" (*innama al-a'mal bi-al-niyyat*, Sahih al-Bukhari). However, Islam maintains external purity laws (*taharah*) alongside the emphasis on interior purity, seeing them as complementary rather than contradictory.

## The Canaanite Woman

The encounter with the Canaanite woman (15:21-28) raises the question of the scope of prophetic mission. Jesus' initial statement that he was "sent only to the lost sheep of the house of Israel" (15:24) is read by Muslim scholars as confirming the Quranic teaching that Jesus was sent specifically to the Children of Israel (3:49, 61:6), not to all humanity. The universal prophetic mission, in Islamic theology, belongs uniquely to Muhammad, the "seal of the prophets" (33:40).

## Divine Provision

The second feeding miracle (15:32-39) reinforces the theme of God providing for his servants through prophetic agency. The Quran repeatedly emphasizes that sustenance (*rizq*) comes from God alone: "And there is no creature on earth but that upon Allah is its provision" (11:6). The feeding miracles demonstrate this principle in dramatic fashion.
