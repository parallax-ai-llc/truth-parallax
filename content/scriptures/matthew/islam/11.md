## Yahya and Isa

The relationship between John (Yahya) and Jesus (Isa) in Matthew 11 is understood in Islam as the mutual confirmation of prophets. The Quran records that Yahya was given the mission to "confirm a Word from Allah" — identified as Jesus (3:39). John's question from prison, "Are you the one who is to come?" (11:3), is read as a prophet seeking confirmation, not expressing doubt. Both Yahya and Isa are honored prophets in the Islamic chain of prophethood.

## Divine Wisdom and Humility

Jesus' prayer of thanksgiving (11:25-27) — praising God for revealing truth to the humble rather than the learned — aligns with the Quranic emphasis on humility before God. The Quran states: "And the servants of the Most Merciful are those who walk upon the earth humbly" (25:63). However, the exclusive claim "No one knows the Father except the Son" (11:27) is rejected by Islam as implying a unique divine-human relationship that contradicts God's accessibility to all believers through prayer and submission.

## The Easy Burden

Jesus' invitation to come to him for rest (11:28-30) is read by Muslim scholars as a prophetic call — inviting people to the path of God, which, though demanding, is lighter than the burden of sin and ignorance. The Quran describes Islam itself in similar terms: "Allah intends for you ease and does not intend for you hardship" (2:185). Sufi scholars have particularly appreciated Jesus' theme of spiritual rest and have incorporated it into Islamic mystical literature.
