## The Prophetic Mission

Jesus' sending of the twelve (10:1-4) is understood in Islam as a prophet delegating authority to his followers, a pattern repeated in Muhammad's sending of companions to various tribes and regions. The authority to heal and cast out demons is understood as granted by God for the purpose of authenticating the prophetic message, not as evidence of the disciples' own spiritual power.

## Persecution of Prophets

Jesus' warnings about persecution (10:16-25) reflect the Quranic theme of prophets suffering rejection and violence. The Quran states: "Every time a messenger came to them with what their souls did not desire, a party of them they denied, and another party they killed" (5:70). Muslim scholars see in the persecution of Jesus' followers a confirmation of the universal pattern of prophetic suffering.

## Trust in God Alone

Jesus' instruction to "fear not those who kill the body" (10:28) and his teaching about divine providence over sparrows (10:29-31) align closely with the Islamic concept of *tawakkul* (reliance on God). The Quran teaches: "Say, 'Never will we be struck except by what Allah has decreed for us'" (9:51). Muslim scholars find in these passages evidence of Jesus' authentic prophetic teaching, consistent with the message of all God's messengers.
