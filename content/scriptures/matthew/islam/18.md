## Humility and Islamic Ethics

Jesus' teaching on humility (18:1-5) — placing a child in the disciples' midst — resonates with the Quranic virtue of *tawadu'* (humility). The Prophet Muhammad is reported to have said: "He who has in his heart the weight of a mustard seed of pride will not enter Paradise" (Sahih Muslim). Muslim scholars see in Jesus' teaching on humility a prophetic instruction consistent with the message brought by all of God's messengers.

## Forgiveness in Islam

The teaching on forgiveness (18:21-35) and the Parable of the Unforgiving Servant resonate with the Quranic emphasis on *'afw* (pardon) and *maghfirah* (forgiveness). The Quran states: "Let them pardon and overlook. Would you not like that Allah should forgive you?" (24:22). The Islamic teaching on forgiveness parallels Jesus' instruction: the believer who has received God's forgiveness is obligated to extend forgiveness to others.

## Community Life

Jesus' instructions for community discipline (18:15-17) are read by Muslim scholars as prophetic guidance for managing disputes — a function served in Islamic jurisprudence by the concepts of *nasiha* (sincere counsel) and *hisbah* (accountability). The Islamic tradition of resolving disputes through graduated steps of private counsel, mediation, and communal judgment parallels the Matthean procedure.
