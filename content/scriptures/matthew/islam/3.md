## Yahya (John the Baptist) in Islam

John the Baptist is known in Islam as Yahya ibn Zakariyya, a prophet mentioned by name in the Quran (3:39, 6:85, 19:7-15, 21:90). He is praised as "noble, chaste, and a prophet from among the righteous" (3:39). His role as a precursor to Jesus is affirmed, though Islam understands this as one prophet confirming another within the same prophetic chain, not as preparation for a divine incarnation.

## Purification and Islamic Ritual

John's baptism as described in Matthew 3 resonates with Islamic purification rituals. *Wudu* (ablution before prayer) and *ghusl* (full ritual bath) are essential Islamic practices that emphasize spiritual and physical purity. Muslim scholars see in John's baptism a form of purification consistent with the prophetic emphasis on ritual cleanness, though Islam does not adopt baptism as a sacrament.

## The Voice from Heaven

The heavenly declaration "This is my beloved Son" (3:17) is rejected by Islamic theology as a textual corruption (*tahrif*) or a misinterpretation of the original revelation. Islam holds that God confirmed Jesus' prophethood, but the language of divine sonship contradicts *tawhid*. The Quran explicitly states: "It is not befitting for Allah to take a son" (19:35). Muslim scholars suggest that the original *Injil* may have contained a confirmation of Jesus' prophethood without the filial language.
