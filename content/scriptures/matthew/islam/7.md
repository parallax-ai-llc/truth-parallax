## The Golden Rule and Islamic Ethics

The Golden Rule (7:12) — "whatever you wish that others would do to you, do also to them" — finds a close parallel in a hadith attributed to the Prophet Muhammad: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself" (Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim). Muslim scholars see this convergence as evidence that all prophets — including Jesus and Muhammad — taught the same fundamental ethical principles rooted in divine revelation.

## Discernment and False Prophets

Jesus' warning against false prophets (7:15-20) resonates with the Quranic concern about distinguishing true from false revelation. The Quran warns against those who "fabricate lies against Allah" (3:94) and emphasizes the criterion of consistency with divine truth. Muslim scholars apply this test retrospectively: the authentic teachings of Jesus are those consistent with the Quran, while teachings that contradict *tawhid* represent later distortion.

## The Wise and Foolish Builders

The parable of building on rock versus sand (7:24-27) parallels Quranic imagery of foundations: "Is then one who laid the foundation of his building on piety to Allah and His pleasure better, or one who laid the foundation of his building on the brink of a crumbling precipice?" (9:109). Muslim scholars read the parable as affirming the universal prophetic principle that faith must be grounded in God's revelation and expressed in righteous action.
