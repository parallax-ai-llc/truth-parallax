## Miracles of Isa

The healing miracles in Matthew 8 are understood in Islam as *mu'jizat* — miraculous signs granted by God to authenticate his prophets. The Quran explicitly affirms that Jesus healed the blind and the leper and raised the dead "by permission of Allah" (3:49, 5:110). The crucial qualification — "by permission of Allah" — distinguishes the Islamic understanding from the Christian claim that Jesus performed miracles by his own divine power.

## The Centurion's Faith

The centurion's recognition of Jesus' authority (8:5-13) is read in Islamic context as a Gentile recognizing a true prophet. The centurion's humility and faith are admired, though Islamic theology would interpret the "authority" Jesus possesses as delegated prophetic authority, not inherent divine power. The episode illustrates the Quranic principle that God guides whom He wills (2:272).

## Power Over Nature

The calming of the storm (8:23-27) is understood as another miracle granted by divine permission. The disciples' question, "What sort of man is this?" (8:27), is answered by Islam very differently than by Christianity: he is a prophet endowed with extraordinary miraculous power, not a divine being. The Quran emphasizes that no miracle occurs except by God's leave, preventing any inference of independent divine nature from miraculous acts.
