## The Messianic Confession

Peter's confession, "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" (16:16), is interpreted very differently in Islam. The title *al-Masih* (the Messiah) is accepted — the Quran itself calls Jesus *al-Masih Isa ibn Maryam* (3:45, 4:171). However, "Son of the living God" is categorically rejected. The Quran states unambiguously: "He neither begets nor is born" (112:3). Muslim scholars read Peter's confession as a later Christian elaboration of what was originally a recognition of Jesus' prophetic and Messianic status without divine sonship.

## The Rock and the Church

Jesus' words about building his "church" on "this rock" (16:18) are read by Muslim scholars as reflecting later ecclesiastical concerns projected back onto Jesus. Islam does not recognize a church hierarchy established by Jesus. The Muslim community (*ummah*) is constituted by shared faith and practice, not by apostolic succession or institutional authority. The concept of "binding and loosing" (16:19) is understood as prophetic authority to teach God's law, not as institutional jurisdiction.

## The Passion Prediction

Jesus' prediction of his suffering and death (16:21) is one of the passages most directly challenged by Islamic theology. The Quran states: "They did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but it was made to appear so to them" (4:157). Muslim scholars interpret Jesus' passion predictions as either later Christian insertions or as references to a trial that God would ultimately rescue him from.
