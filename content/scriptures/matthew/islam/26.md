## The Last Meal and Passover

The Last Supper (26:17-30) takes place during the Jewish Passover, which Islam acknowledges as commemorating God's deliverance of the Israelites from Egypt. The Quran references this deliverance (2:49-50, 26:60-66). However, Islam does not accept the Christian theological interpretation of the Last Supper as the institution of the Eucharist. The bread and wine are understood as elements of a meal, not as the body and blood of Christ. The concept of sacramental consumption of a divine person contradicts *tawhid*.

## The Agony in the Garden

Jesus' prayer in Gethsemane (26:36-46) is admired by Muslim scholars as a model of prophetic submission to God's will. The prayer "Not as I will, but as you will" (26:39) is the essence of *islam* — submission to God's decree. However, Muslim theology holds that God ultimately answered this prayer by rescuing Jesus from crucifixion, consistent with the Quranic statement that Jesus was not killed (4:157).

## The Arrest

The arrest scene (26:47-56) is read in light of the Islamic belief that God would not allow his prophet to be humiliated and killed on a cross. Muslim scholars propose various theories: a substitute was crucified in Jesus' place (*shubbiha lahum*, "it was made to appear so to them," 4:157), or Jesus was taken up to heaven before the crucifixion could occur. The arrest narrative is read as historically approximate but leading to an outcome different from what Christians believe.
