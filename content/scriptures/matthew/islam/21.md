## The Entry into Jerusalem

Jesus' humble entry into Jerusalem on a donkey (21:1-11) is admired in Islamic tradition as exemplifying prophetic humility. The Prophet Muhammad entered Mecca during the conquest of 630 CE with similar humility, bowing on his camel in gratitude to God rather than in triumphant arrogance. Muslim scholars see in both entries the prophetic rejection of worldly pomp and the affirmation that true authority comes from God alone.

## The Temple Action

Jesus' cleansing of the temple (21:12-17) is understood in Islam as a prophetic act of purification — removing corruption from a sacred space. The Quran commands: "We charged Ibrahim and Ismail: 'Purify My House for those who walk around it, and those who stay there for devotion, and those who bow and prostrate'" (2:125). The principle that sacred spaces must be preserved from commercial exploitation resonates with Islamic stewardship of mosques.

## Prophetic Authority

Jesus' assertion of authority over the temple and his conflict with the religious establishment (21:23-27) are read as consistent with the prophetic pattern. Prophets challenge corrupt religious leadership — Moses opposed Pharaoh, Muhammad confronted the Quraysh. Jesus' parables of judgment (21:28-46) warn religious leaders who fail to practice what they preach, a theme echoed in the Quran's warnings to hypocrites (*munafiqun*).
