## The Transfiguration and Divine Glory

The Transfiguration (17:1-8) is read in Islamic theology as a theophanic event — God manifesting his light through his prophet. The Quran describes God as "the Light of the heavens and the earth" (24:35) and records that when Moses asked to see God on Mount Sinai, the mountain crumbled and Moses fell unconscious (7:143). Muslim scholars see the Transfiguration as comparable: a moment of divine manifestation mediated through a prophet, not evidence of the prophet's own divinity.

## Moses, Elijah, and Prophetic Unity

The appearance of Moses and Elijah alongside Jesus confirms, from an Islamic perspective, the unity of the prophetic message. All three are honored prophets in Islam. Their gathering on the mountain illustrates the Quranic principle that all prophets proclaim the same essential truth: the oneness of God and the obligation of righteous living. The Father's command, "Listen to him," is read as God endorsing Jesus' prophetic message, not as establishing Jesus' unique divine status.

## The Epileptic Boy

The healing of the epileptic boy (17:14-21) is another example of prophetic miracle. The emphasis on faith ("If you have faith like a grain of mustard seed," 17:20) aligns with the Islamic emphasis on *iman* (faith) as the precondition for experiencing God's power. The Quran states: "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you" (14:7) — divine blessing responds to human faith and gratitude.
