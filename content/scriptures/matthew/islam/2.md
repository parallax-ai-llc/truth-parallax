## Divine Protection of Prophets

The flight to Egypt (2:13-15) is read in Islamic tradition as an example of God's protection of his prophets. The Quran affirms that God safeguards his messengers until their mission is complete (Quran 5:67). The Magi's visit, while not directly paralleled in the Quran, is understood as consistent with the pattern of non-Israelites recognizing divine truth — similar to the Queen of Sheba's recognition of Solomon's wisdom (Quran 27:22-44).

## Herod's Tyranny

The massacre of the innocents (2:16-18) illustrates the Quranic theme of tyrannical rulers opposing God's prophets. Pharaoh's slaughter of Israelite children (Quran 28:4) provides a direct parallel. Muslim commentators see in Herod's actions the recurring pattern of worldly power attempting to destroy divine messengers — a pattern that God always frustrates through miraculous intervention.

## Egypt in Islamic Geography

Egypt holds a honored place in Islamic sacred geography as the land where the prophets Yusuf (Joseph) and Musa (Moses) carried out their missions. Jesus' sojourn in Egypt connects him to this prophetic lineage. Muslim scholars such as al-Tha'labi included the flight to Egypt in their *Qisas al-Anbiya'* (Stories of the Prophets) as part of the divinely orchestrated protection of the infant prophet.
