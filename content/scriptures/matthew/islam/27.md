## The Crucifixion: The Central Divergence

Matthew 27's account of Jesus' trial and crucifixion represents the single most fundamental point of disagreement between Christianity and Islam. The Quran states categorically: "They did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but it was made to appear so to them" (4:157). Islam holds that God rescued Jesus from the cross, consistent with his pattern of protecting his prophets from ultimate defeat by their enemies.

## Theories of Substitution

Muslim scholars have proposed several theories to explain the apparent crucifixion. The most common is the *substitution theory*: God caused someone else to take on Jesus' appearance and be crucified in his place. Candidates include Judas Iscariot, Simon of Cyrene, or an unnamed volunteer. Other scholars interpret the Quranic verse more broadly: the crucifixion appeared to happen but was a divine illusion (*shubbiha lahum*), with Jesus being taken directly to heaven.

## The Death of Jesus in Islamic Theology

While Islam denies the crucifixion, it does affirm that Jesus will eventually die — either having died naturally after being taken up or destined to die after his return at the end of days. The Quran states: "Peace is on me the day I was born, and the day I die, and the day I am raised alive" (19:33). The theological point is not that Jesus never dies but that his death is not a humiliating execution on a Roman cross — God's prophets are ultimately vindicated, not defeated.
