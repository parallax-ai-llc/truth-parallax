## Overview

The interfaith reading of the Kalpa Sutra engages the text as a resource for understanding how sacred biography functions across religious traditions and for finding common ground between Jain spirituality and the values of other faith communities. Mahavira's life story — his renunciation of royal privilege, his embrace of voluntary poverty, his teaching of non-violence, and his attainment of liberation — resonates with the life narratives of spiritual leaders in many traditions.

Interfaith interpreters find in the Kalpa Sutra themes of universal significance: the search for meaning beyond material success, the transformative power of compassion and non-violence, the possibility of spiritual liberation, and the importance of communal ritual in sustaining faith. The text's emphasis on Mahavira's equal treatment of all beings regardless of caste, gender, or species provides a powerful message for contemporary interfaith dialogue on justice and equality.

## Key Interpretive Principles

Interfaith readers approach the Kalpa Sutra with respect for its specifically Jain theological framework while identifying themes and values that bridge religious boundaries. The text's biographical narratives are read as expressions of universal human spiritual aspirations, while its distinctive Jain elements — the Tirthankara concept, the emphasis on non-violence, the theory of karma — are presented as unique contributions to humanity's collective spiritual wisdom.
