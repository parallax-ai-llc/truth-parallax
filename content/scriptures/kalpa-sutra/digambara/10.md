## Overview

The Digambara reading of Section 10, "Rules for the Rainy Season Retreat," engages this portion of the Kalpa Sutra from the perspective of the sky-clad tradition's own biographical and doctrinal sources. While Digambara scholars respect the Kalpa Sutra's narratives, they evaluate them against their own textual traditions (such as the Adipurana) and interpret the text through the Digambara emphasis on complete renunciation and strict asceticism.

This section addresses rules for the rainy season retreat, a subject of enduring significance for Jain tradition and practice.

## Key Themes

### Digambara Biographical Traditions

Digambara interpreters compare this section's narratives with their own biographical traditions, noting points of agreement and divergence. Where the Kalpa Sutra's accounts differ from Digambara sources, scholars evaluate the competing claims through doctrinal reasoning and textual analysis, generally preferring their own tradition's versions while acknowledging the antiquity and value of the Kalpa Sutra's accounts.

### Ascetic Ideals

The Digambara reading pays particular attention to this section's treatment of renunciation and ascetic practice, interpreting the text through the lens of the Digambara insistence on complete dispossession as the only authentic expression of the Tirthankara's teaching.
