## Overview

Academic study of the Kalpa Sutra examines the text through the lenses of historical criticism, literary analysis, art history, and comparative religion. Scholars investigate the text's date of composition, its relationship to other Jain biographical traditions, its literary structure and rhetorical strategies, and its role in shaping Jain communal identity through ritual recitation.

The Kalpa Sutra's illuminated manuscripts, produced primarily in western India from the 12th to the 16th centuries, are among the most important works of Indian miniature painting and have attracted significant art-historical attention. Academic study also examines the text's role in the annual Paryushana festival as a case study in the social functions of sacred biography.

## Key Interpretive Principles

Academic readers situate the Kalpa Sutra within the broader context of Indian hagiographic literature, comparing its biographical conventions with those of Buddhist and Hindu sacred biography. Critical questions include the historical reliability of the text's accounts, the relationship between biography and doctrine in the narratives, and the ways in which the text constructs religious authority through genealogical and biographical claims.
