## Overview

The Shvetambara tradition regards the Kalpa Sutra as one of its most sacred texts, attributed to the great Acharya Bhadrabahu I and occupying a central place in the annual Paryushana festival. The public recitation of the Kalpa Sutra over the eight days of Paryushana is the liturgical high point of the Shvetambara religious year, with the third day's recitation of Mahavira's birth narrative (janma kalyanak) celebrated with particular devotion and joy.

The Shvetambara reading treats the Kalpa Sutra as authoritative sacred biography, accepting its accounts of the Tirthankaras' lives as both historically reliable and spiritually exemplary. The text's monastic regulations are regarded as binding prescriptions for the conduct of monks and nuns, particularly during the rainy season retreat.

## Key Interpretive Principles

Shvetambara interpreters approach the Kalpa Sutra with devotional reverence, reading the Tirthankaras' biographies as models for spiritual aspiration and the monastic rules as authoritative guides for contemporary practice. The text's ceremonial recitation during Paryushana is understood not merely as a commemorative act but as a spiritually meritorious practice that strengthens the entire community's commitment to the Jain path.
