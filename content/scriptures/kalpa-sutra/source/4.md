---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 4"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** After attaining omniscience, the venerable Mahavira established the fourfold order of His followers: monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen.

**2** He preached His first sermon in the assembly hall called Samavasarana, where gods, men, and animals gathered to hear the truth.

**3** He taught: "The soul exists. It is eternal. It is the doer of its own actions. It is the experiencer of the consequences of its actions. It can be liberated. It can attain liberation."

**4** He taught the path of non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness. He taught the threefold path of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct.

**5** His eleven chief disciples, the Ganadharas, received His teaching and organized it into the twelve Angas, the canonical scriptures of the Jain tradition.

**6** Indrabhuti Gautama was the foremost among the Ganadharas. He was a Brahmana scholar who came to debate with Mahavira but was converted by the power of His teaching.

**7** For thirty years after His enlightenment, Mahavira wandered throughout the land, teaching the path of liberation to all who would listen.

**8** He taught kings and commoners, Brahmanas and outcasts, men and women, with equal compassion. He made no distinction of birth, caste, or gender.

**9** He established rules for the conduct of monks and nuns, for the observances of laymen and laywomen, for the administration of the community.

**10** His teaching spread throughout the land, and thousands took the vow of renunciation under His guidance, while millions adopted the householder's vows.
