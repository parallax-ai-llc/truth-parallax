---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The prince Vardhamana grew up in the royal palace, endowed with all the marks of a great being — supreme beauty, supreme strength, and supreme wisdom.

**2** Even as a child, He showed extraordinary courage. When His companions were frightened by a snake, He seized it fearlessly and cast it aside, thus earning the name Mahavira — the great hero.

**3** He excelled in all the arts and sciences — in archery, in horsemanship, in wrestling, in music, in painting, and in all the branches of knowledge.

**4** Yet His mind was not attached to worldly pleasures. From childhood, He contemplated the nature of the soul, the bondage of karma, and the path of liberation.

**5** When He reached the age of thirty, having fulfilled His duties to His parents and family, He resolved to renounce the world and seek the supreme truth.

**6** He stood beneath an Ashoka tree, and there, in the presence of a multitude of gods and men, He plucked out His hair in five handfuls and took the vow of renunciation.

**7** He said: "I renounce all killing, all lying, all stealing, all sensual pleasures, and all possessions. Henceforth I shall live as a homeless monk, devoted solely to the liberation of my soul."

**8** The gods provided Him with a divine garment, which He wore for thirteen months. After that, He went completely naked, for He had renounced even clothing.

**9** For twelve years He wandered as an ascetic, enduring the most severe hardships — cold, heat, hunger, thirst, insect bites, beatings, and abuse.

**10** He endured all with perfect equanimity, without anger, without complaint, without attachment. His mind was fixed upon the soul, and His body was merely a garment that He would one day discard.
