---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 5"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The venerable Mahavira attained final liberation, Nirvana, at the age of seventy-two, in the city of Pava, on the night of the new moon in the month of Kartika.

**2** On that night, He was seated in deep meditation, His legs crossed, His hands in His lap, His mind absorbed in the infinite Self.

**3** He shed the last particle of karma. His soul, freed from all bondage, rose to the summit of the universe, to Siddha Loka, the abode of the liberated souls.

**4** At that moment, the gods illuminated the city of Pava with divine light, for the light of the world had gone out. They said: "The light of knowledge that illumined the world has set; we shall now illuminate the night with material light."

**5** This night is celebrated as Diwali, the festival of lights, by the Jain community, in memory of the Nirvana of Lord Mahavira.

**6** The kings and princes who were present at Pava took a vow: "Since the light of knowledge has gone out, we shall henceforth illuminate the night with earthly lights."

**7** Thus the tradition of lighting lamps on the night of Mahavira's Nirvana was established and continues to this day.

**8** The venerable Mahavira was the last of the twenty-four Tirthankaras of this cosmic age. Before Him came Parshvanatha, Neminatha, and twenty-one others, stretching back to Rishabhadeva, the first Tirthankara.

**9** Each Tirthankara taught the same eternal truth: the soul is bound by karma and can be freed by the practice of non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation.

**10** Though the Tirthankaras have passed, their teaching remains, preserved in the scriptures, practised by the faithful, and available to all who seek the path of liberation.
