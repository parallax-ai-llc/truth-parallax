---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 6"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Now I shall describe the life of Parshvanatha, the twenty-third Tirthankara, who preceded Mahavira by two hundred and fifty years.

**2** Parshvanatha was born in the city of Varanasi, the son of King Ashvasena and Queen Vama. He was named Parshva because the serpent Dharanendra had protected him while he was in the womb.

**3** Even as a young prince, Parshva showed extraordinary compassion for all living beings. When he saw a Brahmana about to burn a log containing a pair of serpents, he rescued them.

**4** At the age of thirty, Parshva renounced the world and became an ascetic. He practised severe penance for eighty-three days, and on the eighty-fourth day, he attained omniscience.

**5** The demon Meghamalin, harboring an ancient grudge, assailed Parshva with terrible storms, floods, and darkness. But the serpent king Dharanendra spread his hood over the meditating sage and protected him.

**6** Parshva taught four great vows: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, and non-possessiveness. Mahavira later added a fifth vow — celibacy — making the vows five.

**7** Parshva's teaching attracted many followers, and his fourfold order — monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen — flourished for centuries.

**8** He attained Nirvana at the age of one hundred, on the summit of Mount Sammeta, where his followers built a great shrine in his memory.

**9** The followers of Parshva and the followers of Mahavira recognized each other as members of the same tradition, for both taught the same fundamental truths.

**10** He who remembers Parshvanatha, who worships his image, who follows his teaching — he is protected from all dangers and progresses on the path of liberation.
