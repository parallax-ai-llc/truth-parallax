---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 7"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Now I shall describe the life of Rishabhadeva, also known as Adinatha, the first Tirthankara, the first teacher, the father of civilization.

**2** Rishabhadeva was born in the first age of the present cycle, when human beings lived in a state of natural abundance and had no need of organized society.

**3** As the age declined and the wish-fulfilling trees ceased to provide, human beings needed instruction in the arts of civilization. Rishabhadeva taught them.

**4** He taught them agriculture, the art of cooking, the building of houses, the making of pottery, the weaving of cloth, and all the crafts necessary for civilized life.

**5** He established the institution of marriage, the system of governance, the organization of society, and the rule of law. He was the first king, the first teacher, the first law-giver.

**6** He taught the seventy-two arts to men and the sixty-four arts to women. He established the system of writing and the science of numbers.

**7** Having established civilization, Rishabhadeva recognized the impermanence of worldly life. He renounced his kingdom and became an ascetic.

**8** For one thousand years He practised penance, enduring the most severe austerities. He was the supreme example of renunciation and self-conquest.

**9** He attained omniscience and taught the path of liberation to all beings. His teaching was the same as that of all the Tirthankaras who followed — non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation.

**10** Rishabhadeva, the first Tirthankara, is the father of Jain tradition. He showed that the path of worldly activity and the path of spiritual liberation are not opposed, for one who has mastered the world is best qualified to renounce it.
