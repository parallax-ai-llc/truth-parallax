---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 9"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Now I shall describe the succession of teachers who have preserved and transmitted the teaching of the Jinas from generation to generation.

**2** After the Nirvana of Lord Mahavira, His chief disciple, Indrabhuti Gautama, attained omniscience and led the community for twelve years.

**3** After Gautama, the leadership passed to Sudharman, the last of the direct disciples of Mahavira. He led the community for twelve years.

**4** After Sudharman, the leadership passed to Jambu, who was the last person in this age to attain omniscience. After Jambu, the age of omniscient teachers came to an end.

**5** The knowledge was then transmitted through a succession of learned teachers — the Acharyas — who, though not omniscient, preserved the teaching faithfully.

**6** The great Acharyas — Bhadrabahu, Sthulabhadra, Umasvati, Kundakunda, and others — organized the scriptures, wrote commentaries, and guided the community through the ages.

**7** When disputes arose among the followers, leading to the division into the Shvetambara and Digambara traditions, the essential teaching remained the same: non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation.

**8** Both traditions honour the same twenty-four Tirthankaras, teach the same fundamental doctrines, and follow the same path of liberation. Their differences are in matters of discipline and practice, not in matters of truth.

**9** The Jain community has maintained its tradition for over two and a half millennia, through periods of royal patronage and periods of persecution, through times of prosperity and times of hardship.

**10** He who honours the succession of teachers, who respects the tradition they have preserved, who follows the path they have illuminated — he is a true Jain, a true follower of the Jinas.
