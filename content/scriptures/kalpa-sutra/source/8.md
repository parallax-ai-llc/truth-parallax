---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 8"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Now I shall describe the rules of conduct for the monks during the rainy season retreat, the Paryushana, which is the holiest period of the Jain calendar.

**2** During the four months of the rainy season, the monks should remain in one place and not wander, for the rains bring forth countless tiny creatures that might be harmed by walking.

**3** This period of retreat is devoted to intensive study, meditation, fasting, and self-examination. It is a time of spiritual renewal.

**4** During Paryushana, the monks recite the Kalpa Sutra, narrating the lives of the Tirthankaras, for the inspiration and edification of the community.

**5** The laypeople also observe special vows during this period. They fast, meditate, study the scriptures, and confess their transgressions.

**6** On the last day of Paryushana, all members of the community — monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen — ask forgiveness of one another for any harm they may have caused during the year.

**7** They say: "If I have offended you in any way — by thought, word, or deed — I ask your forgiveness. And I forgive all who have offended me."

**8** This practice of universal forgiveness, Kshama-vani, is the crown of the Paryushana festival. It purifies all relationships and heals all wounds.

**9** The community that practises universal forgiveness is freed from the karma of enmity and hatred. It becomes a community of peace, harmony, and mutual respect.

**10** He who sincerely asks forgiveness and sincerely grants forgiveness takes a great step on the path of liberation. For forgiveness is the practical expression of non-violence.
