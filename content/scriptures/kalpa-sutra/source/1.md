---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 1"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Obeisance to the Arhats, obeisance to the liberated souls, obeisance to the religious leaders, obeisance to the religious teachers, obeisance to all monks in the world.

**2** This fivefold obeisance, which destroys all sin, is the first and foremost of all auspicious recitations.

**3** Thus have I heard: the venerable Mahavira, the last of the twenty-four Tirthankaras, descended from heaven and took his last birth for the welfare of all beings.

**4** In the continent of Jambudvipa, in the country of Bharatavarsha, in the city of Kundagrama, the venerable Mahavira was conceived in the womb of the Brahmani Devananda.

**5** The embryo was then transferred by the god Indra to the womb of the Kshatriya queen Trishala, the wife of King Siddhartha, for it is the rule that Tirthankaras are born only in royal families.

**6** During the pregnancy, the mother had sixteen auspicious dreams: an elephant, a bull, a lion, the goddess Lakshmi, a garland, the moon, the sun, a flag, a golden vase, a lotus lake, the ocean of milk, a celestial palace, a heap of jewels, a smokeless fire, and other signs.

**7** These dreams foretold the birth of a great being — one who would become either a universal emperor or a Tirthankara, a ford-maker who would show the way across the ocean of worldly existence.

**8** The venerable Mahavira was born on the thirteenth day of the bright half of the month of Chaitra, when the moon was in the constellation of Uttaraphalguni.

**9** At the moment of His birth, the gods descended from heaven and performed the birth ceremonies, bathing the newborn with sacred waters from Mount Meru.

**10** The child was named Vardhamana, meaning "the one who brings prosperity," for during the time of His mother's pregnancy, the family's wealth and glory had increased greatly.
