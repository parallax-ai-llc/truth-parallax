---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 3"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** During His twelve years of penance, Mahavira practised the most rigorous austerities known to the spiritual tradition.

**2** He fasted for days, weeks, and months at a time. He stood in meditation under the blazing sun. He sat motionless while insects crawled over His body and bit Him.

**3** He was beaten by villagers who mistook Him for a thief. He was bound and whipped. Dogs were set upon Him. Yet He harmed no one and bore no ill will toward any creature.

**4** He did not seek shelter in houses or monasteries. He slept under trees, in abandoned shrines, in cremation grounds, and in the open fields.

**5** He ate only the simplest food, and only what was offered to him without his asking. He often went without food for days, and he broke his fast only when the food was offered in the proper manner.

**6** Throughout these years, He practised the deepest meditation. He withdrew His consciousness from the body and the senses and fixed it upon the innermost Self.

**7** He conquered all four passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — one by one, until not a trace of passion remained in His soul.

**8** On the bank of the river Rijuvalika, under a Sal tree, in the field of the householder Samaga, the venerable Mahavira attained omniscience — Kevala Jnana.

**9** In that supreme moment, all the veils of karma were destroyed. His soul shone forth with infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy.

**10** He saw all things — past, present, and future — simultaneously and perfectly. He knew all the souls in the universe, their states, their karma, and their destinies. He had become a Jina, a conqueror, a Tirthankara.
