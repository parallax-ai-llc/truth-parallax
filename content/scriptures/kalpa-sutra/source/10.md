---
title: "Kalpa Sutra - Chapter 10"
scripture: "kalpa-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Thus ends the Kalpa Sutra, the sacred text that narrates the lives of the Tirthankaras and the rules of monastic conduct.

**2** He who hears this Sutra with faith and devotion acquires great merit. He who recites it during the Paryushana festival brings blessings upon the entire community.

**3** The teaching of the Jinas is eternal. It was not invented by any person but was discovered by the Tirthankaras through their own spiritual realization.

**4** The truth that the Jinas teach is the same in every age: the soul exists, karma binds it, and the path of non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation liberates it.

**5** This truth is available to all — to monks and householders, to men and women, to the learned and the simple. No one is excluded from the path of liberation.

**6** The practice of non-violence is the foundation of all spiritual practice. Without non-violence, there can be no truthfulness. Without truthfulness, there can be no renunciation. Without renunciation, there can be no liberation.

**7** Let all beings be happy. Let all beings be peaceful. Let all beings be free from suffering. Let all beings attain liberation.

**8** May the teaching of the Jinas endure for all time. May it bring peace to the world and liberation to all who follow it.

**9** Obeisance to the Arhats, obeisance to the liberated souls, obeisance to the religious leaders, obeisance to the religious teachers, obeisance to all monks in the world.

**10** This fivefold obeisance, which destroys all sin, is the most auspicious of all auspicious things. Thus ends the Kalpa Sutra. Parasparopagraho Jivanam — souls render service to one another.
