Orthodox interpretation of chapter 12 gives particular attention to Judas's prayer and sacrifice for the dead soldiers (12:39-45), which provides scriptural foundation for the Orthodox practice of commemorating the departed through memorial services (panikhida), the offering of kollyva, and the inclusion of the names of the deceased in the proskomedia preparation of the Eucharistic gifts.

The Orthodox understanding of prayer for the dead differs somewhat from the Catholic doctrine of purgatory, emphasizing instead the ongoing relationship between the living and the departed within the communion of saints. The prayers of the Church benefit the departed not through a juridical mechanism of satisfaction but through the solidarity of love that unites all members of the Body of Christ, whether living or reposed. This chapter provides the Old Testament foundation for this deeply Orthodox practice.
