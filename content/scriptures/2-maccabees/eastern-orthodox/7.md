The martyrdom of the seven brothers and their mother holds a place of special honor in Orthodox theology and worship. The feast of the Holy Maccabean Martyrs (August 1) celebrates these figures as forerunners of Christian martyrdom, and Orthodox hymnography praises the mother — known in tradition as Solomonia — as a model of faith surpassing even that of Abraham, who was asked to sacrifice only one son.

The brothers' explicit statements of resurrection faith (7:9, 7:14, 7:23) are read by Orthodox interpreters as anticipations of the Paschal proclamation: "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death." The theology of bodily resurrection expressed in this chapter aligns perfectly with Orthodox teaching on the redemption of the whole person — body and soul — and the glorification of the material creation through the incarnation, death, and resurrection of Christ.
