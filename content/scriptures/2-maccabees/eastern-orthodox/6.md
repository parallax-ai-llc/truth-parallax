The martyrdom of Eleazar is venerated in the Orthodox liturgical tradition as a model of steadfast witness to the faith. Orthodox hymnography on the feast of the Holy Maccabees celebrates Eleazar's refusal to compromise his conscience, even at the cost of his life. His rejection of the suggestion to merely pretend to eat forbidden food illustrates the Orthodox teaching that outward conformity to falsehood defiles the soul, regardless of inner intentions.

Orthodox moral theology draws from Eleazar's example the principle that the truth must be embodied in action, not merely held in the mind. The elder's concern for the example he sets for the young reflects the Orthodox emphasis on the community's shared responsibility for the spiritual formation of its members and the teaching that scandal — leading others into sin through one's example — is among the gravest of offenses.
