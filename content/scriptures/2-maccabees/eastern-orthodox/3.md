Orthodox interpreters read the Heliodorus episode as a powerful demonstration of the angelic protection that surrounds holy places and sacred objects. The appearance of a horseman in golden armor and two young men of surpassing strength illustrates the Orthodox teaching on the active ministry of angels in the material world — a belief expressed in Orthodox iconography and liturgical prayer.

The healing of Heliodorus through the intercession of the high priest Onias demonstrates the Orthodox understanding of priestly mediation and the power of intercessory prayer. The episode prefigures the sacrament of Holy Unction and the Orthodox practice of prayer for the sick, in which the healing power of God is channeled through the ministry of the Church's ordained clergy.
