Orthodox interpretation reads the opening letter of 2 Maccabees as a liturgical document that demonstrates the unity of God's people through shared worship and festival observance. The letter's emphasis on the sacred fire of the Temple altar resonates with Orthodox theology of the Holy Fire — the miraculous light that the Orthodox faithful believe descends at the Church of the Holy Sepulchre each Pascha.

The concern for maintaining liturgical continuity across the Diaspora reflects the Orthodox principle that the Church's unity is constituted and expressed through shared eucharistic worship. The letter's exhortation to observe the festival of the Temple's purification prefigures the Orthodox practice of celebrating the Feast of the Exaltation of the Cross and other feasts that commemorate the sanctification of holy places.
