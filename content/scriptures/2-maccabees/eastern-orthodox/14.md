Orthodox interpreters read Nicanor's threat to destroy the Temple in chapter 14 as one of the most extreme expressions of anti-religious persecution in Scripture. The intention to replace the Temple of God with a shrine to Dionysus represents the ultimate archontic assault on sacred space — the attempt to substitute the worship of the true God with the worship of demons.

The case of Razis is discussed in Orthodox moral theology with recognition of the extreme circumstances that drove his action. While Orthodox teaching generally prohibits suicide, the patristic tradition — including comments by some Church Fathers on similar cases — acknowledges the complexity of situations in which believers face a choice between death at their own hands and capture that may lead to forced apostasy. The Orthodox approach emphasizes compassion and leaves the final judgment of such cases to God's mercy.
