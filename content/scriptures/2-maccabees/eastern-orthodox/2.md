Orthodox exegesis reads chapter 2's account of Jeremiah hiding the sacred objects with interest, as the Orthodox tradition maintains a deep reverence for holy relics and sacred objects as bearers of divine grace. The concealment of the ark, the tent, and the altar of incense illustrates the Orthodox understanding that sacred things are preserved by divine providence even when hidden from human sight.

The chapter's reference to the fire from heaven at the Temple's dedication connects to the Orthodox theology of divine fire as a manifestation of God's presence and approval. The descent of the Holy Spirit as tongues of fire at Pentecost, the Orthodox understanding of the uncreated light of Tabor, and the Holy Fire ceremony all participate in this theological trajectory that 2 Maccabees illuminates from its Old Testament perspective.
