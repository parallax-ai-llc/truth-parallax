Orthodox exegesis gives particular emphasis to Judas's vision of Onias and Jeremiah praying for the people (15:12-16), which provides direct scriptural support for the Orthodox doctrine of the intercession of the saints. The vision of the departed righteous actively praying for the living confirms the Orthodox understanding of the Church as a single body encompassing both the living and the reposed, united in prayer and mutual love.

The golden sword presented by Jeremiah to Judas symbolizes the heavenly assistance that the saints provide to the Church Militant — a theme expressed in Orthodox iconography through the depiction of warrior-saints bearing divinely given weapons. The book's conclusion reinforces the Orthodox conviction that sacred history is simultaneously human and divine, temporal and eternal, historical and eschatological.
