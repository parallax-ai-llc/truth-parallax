Orthodox interpretation of chapter 10's Temple purification emphasizes its typological connection to the consecration of Christian churches, one of the most solemn rites in the Orthodox liturgical tradition. The eight-day celebration with its processions, hymns, and offerings prefigures the Orthodox practice of celebrating the dedication of a new church over multiple days, with the sealing of the altar, the deposition of relics, and the first celebration of the Divine Liturgy.

The heavenly horsemen who fight alongside the Maccabean forces (10:29-30) are understood within the Orthodox theological framework of the communion of saints and the angelic hierarchies. Orthodox iconography regularly depicts warrior-saints such as George and Demetrius on horseback, and the Maccabean visions are read as Old Testament anticipations of this heavenly military assistance.
