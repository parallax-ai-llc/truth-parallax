Orthodox exegesis reads chapter 5's account of Antiochus's assault on Jerusalem through the lens of the many sieges and destructions that Orthodox communities have endured throughout history. The massacre of the inhabitants and the desecration of the Temple resonate with the Orthodox experience of the fall of Constantinople in 1453 and other catastrophic events that have shaped Orthodox consciousness.

The withdrawal of Judas to the wilderness (5:27) is understood as an anticipation of the monastic withdrawal that has been central to Orthodox spirituality since the Desert Fathers. The wilderness becomes a place of spiritual preparation and divine encounter, where the faithful are strengthened for the struggle against the powers of the world.
