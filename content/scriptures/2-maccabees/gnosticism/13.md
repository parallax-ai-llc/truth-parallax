Gnostic readers interpret chapter 13's military narrative as another episode in the ongoing struggle between pneumatic consciousness and archontic power. The execution of Menelaus, the corrupt high priest, symbolizes the destruction of the false spiritual authority that collaborates with the Demiurge's regime. In Gnostic terms, Menelaus represents the psychic religious leader who maintains the outward forms of religion while serving the interests of material power.

The chapter's emphasis on prayer and fasting before battle illustrates the Gnostic principle that spiritual practice must precede and accompany all action in the material world. The pneumatic individual does not engage with worldly affairs from a position of attachment but from a stance of inner detachment that allows effective action without spiritual compromise.
