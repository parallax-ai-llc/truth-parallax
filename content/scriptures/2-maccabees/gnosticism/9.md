Gnostic interpreters read the death of Antiochus IV in chapter 9 as the dissolution of an archontic power whose role in the cosmic drama has been exhausted. The detailed description of his physical decomposition symbolizes the disintegration of material power when it is no longer sustained by the archontic forces that created it. The Demiurge's agents, like all material beings, are subject to decay and dissolution.

Antiochus's deathbed repentance is read with Gnostic skepticism — the archon may recognize the existence of a higher power but cannot achieve genuine gnosis. His acknowledgment of divine justice remains at the psychic level, unable to penetrate to the pneumatic reality that transcends all material categories of justice and retribution.
