Gnostic readers find in chapter 2's account of hidden sacred objects a powerful symbol of the esoteric knowledge concealed beneath the surface of exoteric religion. The ark of the covenant, hidden by Jeremiah in a secret cave, represents the gnosis that is veiled within the outer narrative of Scripture — accessible only to those who possess the key of spiritual interpretation.

Jeremiah's statement that the hiding place will remain unknown "until God gathers his people together again" (2:7) resonates with the Gnostic hope for the eschatological reunion of the scattered divine sparks in the Pleroma. The concealment of sacred knowledge is a necessary condition of existence in the archontic realm, but the day of revelation will come when all that is hidden is made manifest.
