The martyrdom of the seven brothers is of particular interest to Gnostic interpreters, who read it as an allegory of the progressive liberation of the divine spark from the seven planetary spheres governed by the archons. Each brother's death represents the soul's passage through one of the archontic realms, and the mother who encourages them represents Sophia — the divine Wisdom whose fall gave rise to the material world and whose grief at her children's suffering drives the process of cosmic redemption.

The brothers' declarations of resurrection faith (7:9, 7:14, 7:23) are reinterpreted in Gnostic terms as confidence not in the physical resurrection of the body but in the return of the divine spark to the Pleroma. The statement about creation from nothing (7:28) is read through the Gnostic lens as referring to the Demiurge's creation of the material world from the formless void — an act that, in Gnostic cosmology, represents not divine generosity but the tragic dispersal of divine light into material darkness.
