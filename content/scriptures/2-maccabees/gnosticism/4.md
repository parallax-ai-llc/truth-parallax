Gnostic readers interpret the Hellenization crisis in chapter 4 as an allegory of the contamination of authentic gnosis by the counterfeit knowledge of the material world. The replacement of legitimate spiritual authority (Onias) with worldly pretenders (Jason, Menelaus) symbolizes the displacement of genuine pneumatic leadership by psychic or hylic figures who serve the interests of the archontic powers.

The gymnasium and its associated Greek cultural practices represent the seductions of material wisdom — philosophy and culture divorced from authentic spiritual knowledge. The Gnostic critique of worldly wisdom, expressed in texts like the Gospel of Thomas and the Apocryphon of John, finds a narrative parallel in this chapter's depiction of the devastating consequences that follow when the community of the faithful substitutes cultural sophistication for genuine knowledge of the divine.
