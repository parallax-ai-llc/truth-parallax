Gnostic interpreters read the opening letter of 2 Maccabees as an expression of the exoteric religious community's concern for ritual continuity — a concern that, while understandable at the psychic level, reflects an attachment to material forms of worship that the pneumatic consciousness transcends. The emphasis on sacred fire and festival observance represents the outward religion of the soul-bound (psychic) believer who has not yet awakened to the inner gnosis.

Nevertheless, the fire imagery resonates with Gnostic symbolism of the divine spark imprisoned in matter. The sacred fire that was hidden and later restored can be read as an allegory of the divine light concealed within the material world, preserved by the secret tradition of gnosis until the time of its full revelation.
