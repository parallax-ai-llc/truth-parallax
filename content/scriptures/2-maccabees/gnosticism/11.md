Gnostic interpreters approach chapter 11's diplomatic narratives as an allegory of the provisional accommodations that the pneumatic soul must make with the archontic powers while still imprisoned in the material world. The peace agreements and letters represent the temporary truces between the spirit and matter that allow the work of gnosis to continue without open conflict.

The heavenly rider who appears before Lysias's forces symbolizes the aeon or spiritual guardian who demonstrates to the archontic powers that the pneumatic community is under divine protection. This supernatural intervention does not resolve the underlying conflict between spirit and matter but creates the conditions within which the awakened soul can continue its journey toward liberation.
