Gnostic readers interpret the Temple purification in chapter 10 as a symbol of the interior cleansing that occurs when the divine spark is liberated from the contamination of archontic influence. The removal of pagan objects and the restoration of authentic worship represent the stripping away of false beliefs and attachments that obscure the pneumatic consciousness.

The eight-day celebration resonates with Gnostic numerology, in which the Ogdoad (the number eight) represents the realm above the seven archontic spheres — the threshold of the Pleroma. The rededicated Temple, illuminated by renewed fire, symbolizes the purified soul that has passed through the seven planetary gates and stands at the boundary of the transcendent divine.
