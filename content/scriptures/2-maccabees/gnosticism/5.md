Gnostic interpreters read chapter 5's account of the assault on Jerusalem as a dramatization of the archontic invasion of the inner sanctuary of the soul. The massacre of the inhabitants represents the suppression of the spiritual faculties by the forces of ignorance, while the plundering of the Temple symbolizes the theft of gnosis by the Demiurge's agents.

Judas's withdrawal to the wilderness (5:27) resonates with the Gnostic theme of the pneumatic individual's retreat from the world of archontic domination into the interior desert where authentic gnosis can be preserved and cultivated. The wilderness becomes a place of spiritual resistance — not physical but contemplative — where the divine spark is protected from the assaults of material power.
