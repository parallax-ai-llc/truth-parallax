Gnostic readers interpret Judas's military victories in chapter 8 as symbolic of the pneumatic soul's triumph over the archontic forces that seek to maintain its imprisonment in matter. The heavenly appearances that accompany the Maccabean forces represent the intervention of aeons from the Pleroma who assist the awakened soul in its struggle for liberation.

The distribution of spoils to the persecuted and vulnerable (8:28-30) symbolizes the sharing of gnosis with those who are ready to receive it — the widows, orphans, and afflicted representing the spiritually impoverished who have not yet awakened to their divine origin. The Gnostic mission of sharing salvific knowledge with the elect mirrors Judas's care for the most vulnerable members of his community.
