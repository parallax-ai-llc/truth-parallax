Gnostic interpreters read the Heliodorus episode as an allegory of the archontic powers' attempt to plunder the inner treasury of the soul — the divine spark of gnosis that the Demiurge's agents seek to capture and suppress. The angelic warriors who punish Heliodorus represent the spiritual guardians who protect the pneumatic consciousness from archontic interference.

The healing of Heliodorus through the intercession of the high priest illustrates the Gnostic understanding that even the agents of the Demiurge can be temporarily enlightened by exposure to genuine spiritual power. However, the healing does not constitute full gnosis — Heliodorus departs impressed but not transformed, returning to the service of his earthly master rather than seeking the transcendent divine.
