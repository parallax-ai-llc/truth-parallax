Gnostic interpreters read Nicanor's threat to destroy the Temple in chapter 14 as the archontic assault on the last refuge of gnosis in the material world. The intention to replace the Temple of God with a shrine to Dionysus represents the substitution of authentic spiritual knowledge with the ecstatic but ultimately material religion of the Demiurge's world.

The self-destruction of Razis is read by Gnostic interpreters as an act of liberation — the pneumatic soul choosing to leave the material body rather than allow it to be captured and used by the archontic powers. In Gnostic thought, the body is ultimately a prison, and its deliberate destruction can be understood as the soul's assertion of its sovereignty over matter, though this interpretation remains controversial even within Gnostic circles.
