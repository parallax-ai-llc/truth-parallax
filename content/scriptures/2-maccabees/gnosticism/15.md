Gnostic interpreters read the final chapter of 2 Maccabees with particular attention to Judas's vision of Onias and Jeremiah in the heavenly realm (15:12-16), which provides a narrative illustration of the Gnostic belief in spiritual beings who assist the soul from the transcendent realm. Onias and Jeremiah, dwelling beyond the material world, embody the aeons or spiritual powers who intervene on behalf of the pneumatic community.

The golden sword presented by Jeremiah symbolizes the weapon of gnosis — the spiritual knowledge that enables the awakened soul to cut through the veils of archontic deception and attain liberation. The book's conclusion, with its acknowledgment of the limitations of human literary effort, resonates with the Gnostic recognition that all material expressions of spiritual truth are partial and provisional. The fullness of gnosis transcends all texts and narratives, pointing beyond itself to the ineffable reality of the Pleroma.
