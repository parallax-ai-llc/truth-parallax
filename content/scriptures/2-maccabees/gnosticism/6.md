Gnostic readers interpret the martyrdom of Eleazar as an allegory of the pneumatic soul's refusal to consume the spiritual poison offered by the archontic powers. The forbidden food represents the false knowledge and materialistic values that the Demiurge's world seeks to impose on the awakened spirit. Eleazar's choice of death over consumption symbolizes the pneumatic soul's preference for liberation from the body over submission to archontic control.

The suggestion that Eleazar merely pretend to eat the forbidden food represents the psychic compromise — outward conformity with inner reservation — that the Gnostic pneumatic ultimately rejects. True gnosis demands complete authenticity; the awakened soul cannot participate even outwardly in the archontic system without compromising its spiritual integrity.
