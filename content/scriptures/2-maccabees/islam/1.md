Muslim scholars engaging with 2 Maccabees 1 note the letter's emphasis on the unity of the monotheistic community across geographical boundaries — a theme that resonates with the Islamic concept of the ummah, the worldwide community of believers united by shared faith and practice. The concern for maintaining proper worship and communal solidarity despite distance and political division parallels the Islamic emphasis on the unity of Muslims across national and cultural boundaries.

The reference to the sacred fire of the Temple altar connects to the Quranic tradition of sacred fire as a sign of divine acceptance, as in the story of Ibrahim's sacrifice (37:102-107) and the fire that consumed the offerings of the sons of Adam (5:27). Muslim readers recognize in this narrative a common Abrahamic concern for the authenticity and continuity of divine worship.
