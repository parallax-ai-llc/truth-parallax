Muslim readers interpret chapter 5's account of Antiochus's assault on Jerusalem through the lens of the Quranic narratives of cities and communities destroyed for their rejection of God's messengers or their submission to tyranny. The massacre of the inhabitants and the plundering of the Temple illustrate the devastation that results when oppressive power is wielded against a community of faith.

The withdrawal of Judas Maccabeus to the wilderness (5:27) resonates with the hijra (migration) tradition in Islam — the strategic retreat that enables the faithful to regroup, prepare, and eventually return to establish justice. The Prophet Muhammad's own hijra from Mecca to Medina provides the paradigmatic Islamic model for this pattern of withdrawal and return.
