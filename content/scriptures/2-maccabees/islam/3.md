Muslim readers find in the Heliodorus episode a powerful illustration of the Quranic principle that God protects his holy places from desecration. The angelic intervention that punishes the would-be plunderer resonates with the Quranic account of the army of Abraha, which attempted to destroy the Kaaba and was destroyed by divine intervention (Surah al-Fil, 105:1-5).

The high priest Onias's prayer for Heliodorus's healing after his punishment illustrates the Islamic virtue of showing mercy to one's enemies, a quality the Quran attributes to the Prophet Muhammad and commends to all believers (3:159). The episode demonstrates that divine protection of sacred spaces is accompanied by the obligation of the faithful to show compassion even to those who have transgressed.
