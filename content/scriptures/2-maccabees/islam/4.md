Islamic interpreters read chapter 4's account of the corruption of the Jewish priesthood as illustrative of the Quranic warning against religious leaders who betray their trust. The Quran condemns rabbis and monks who "devour the wealth of the people unjustly" (9:34), and the simony and treachery of Jason and Menelaus exemplify the kind of religious corruption that Islam identifies as a recurring pattern in the history of earlier communities.

The Hellenization of Jerusalem raises questions about cultural assimilation and the preservation of religious identity that are highly relevant to contemporary Islamic discourse. The tension between engagement with Western culture and the preservation of Islamic practice mirrors the dilemma faced by Hellenistic Jews, and Muslim intellectuals have drawn on this parallel to illuminate their own community's challenges.
