Islamic interpreters read chapter 10's account of the Temple purification as an act of restoring monotheistic worship after pagan contamination — a theme that resonates with the Prophet Muhammad's purification of the Kaaba after the conquest of Mecca. The removal of pagan objects and the restoration of authentic worship illustrate the Islamic principle that sacred spaces must be dedicated exclusively to the worship of the one God.

The heavenly horsemen who appear in battle (10:29-30) resonate with the Islamic tradition of angelic warriors assisting the faithful, as described in the Quranic accounts of the battles of Badr and the Trench. Muslim readers see in these supernatural interventions confirmation of the principle that God supports those who struggle for the preservation and restoration of true worship.
