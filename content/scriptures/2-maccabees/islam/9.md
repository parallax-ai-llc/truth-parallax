Muslim scholars read the death of Antiochus IV in chapter 9 as an illustration of the Quranic principle of divine retribution against tyrannical rulers. The Quran's accounts of the destruction of Pharaoh, the people of 'Ad, the people of Thamud, and other communities that rejected God's guidance provide a framework within which Antiochus's fate is understood as part of a universal pattern of divine justice.

Antiochus's deathbed repentance, like Pharaoh's last-moment declaration of faith as the sea closed over him (10:90-91), illustrates the Quranic teaching that there comes a point at which repentance is no longer accepted — when the signs of death become apparent and the opportunity for genuine turning has passed. Muslim readers draw from this narrative the urgent moral lesson that repentance must be sincere and timely.
