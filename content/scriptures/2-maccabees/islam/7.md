The martyrdom of the seven brothers and their mother is one of the narratives from the biblical tradition most frequently referenced by Muslim scholars, as it embodies the Islamic values of faith, patience (sabr), and trust in the resurrection (al-ba'th). The mother's exhortation to her sons to accept death rather than apostatize resonates with the Quranic praise of those who remain faithful under persecution.

The brothers' explicit statements of resurrection faith parallel the Quranic teaching that God will raise the dead on the Day of Judgment and that the martyrs are alive with their Lord (3:169). The mother's reference to creation from nothing (7:28) accords with the Quranic doctrine of God's absolute creative sovereignty — creating by divine command (kun fa-yakun). Muslim scholars have recognized in this chapter a powerful expression of monotheistic conviction that transcends the boundaries of particular traditions.
