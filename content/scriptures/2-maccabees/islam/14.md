Muslim scholars read chapter 14 with particular attention to Nicanor's blasphemous threat to destroy the Temple, which resonates with the Quranic condemnation of those who seek to destroy places of worship (2:114). The sanctity of mosques, churches, and synagogues is affirmed in the Quran (22:40), and Muslim readers see in Nicanor's threat a violation of the divine law that protects all houses of worship.

The case of Razis raises questions that Islamic jurisprudence has also addressed — the permissibility of self-destruction when faced with capture by enemies who will force apostasy. Islamic law generally prohibits suicide but recognizes the complexity of situations in which believers face extreme duress. The concept of istishhad (seeking martyrdom) in battle is distinguished from suicide, though the boundary between them has been debated throughout Islamic history.
