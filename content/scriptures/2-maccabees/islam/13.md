Islamic interpreters read chapter 13 as another episode in the ongoing struggle between the community of faith and the forces of persecution. The execution of Menelaus, the corrupt high priest, illustrates the Islamic principle that betrayal of religious trust is among the most serious of sins — the Quran warns that those who conceal what God has revealed in exchange for worldly gain will face severe punishment (2:174).

The chapter's account of communal prayer and supplication before military engagement reflects the Islamic practice of seeking divine guidance and assistance through istikhara (seeking God's decision) and collective du'a before undertaking significant actions. Muslim readers recognize in the Maccabean integration of prayer and action a pattern consistent with the Prophetic example.
