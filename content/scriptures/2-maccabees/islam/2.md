Islamic interpreters find in chapter 2's account of Jeremiah hiding the sacred objects a reflection of the Quranic theme of God preserving his signs and instruments for future generations. The Quran describes God as the guardian of his revelation (15:9), and the concealment of the ark and other sacred objects on Mount Nebo illustrates the broader Abrahamic conviction that God ensures the preservation of what is needed for the continuity of true worship.

The chapter's emphasis on the connection between prophetic authority and the preservation of sacred tradition resonates with the Islamic understanding of the prophetic chain (silsilat al-anbiya) through which God's revelation has been transmitted across the generations. Muslim readers see in the Maccabean concern for sacred continuity a parallel to the Islamic concern for the preservation of the Quran and the Sunnah.
