The martyrdom of the seven brothers and their mother in chapter 7 is one of the most powerful narratives in biblical literature, and Protestant interpreters engage it with deep respect even while denying the canonical status of 2 Maccabees. The explicit statements of belief in bodily resurrection (7:9, 7:14, 7:23) are recognized as crucial evidence for the development of this doctrine in Second Temple Judaism, providing important background for the New Testament teaching on resurrection.

The mother's exhortation to her sons, encouraging them to accept death in confidence that God will raise them, has been compared by Protestant writers to the faith of Christian martyrs throughout the ages. The passage's assertion of creation ex nihilo (7:28) is noted as one of the earliest explicit formulations of this doctrine, though Protestant interpreters insist that such theological truths must be confirmed by canonical Scripture to serve as doctrinal authority.
