Protestant readers recognize chapter 10's account of the Temple purification as the historical foundation of Hanukkah, providing important background for the Gospel of John's reference to Jesus at the Feast of Dedication (John 10:22). The celebration's parallels with the Feast of Tabernacles noted in the text (10:6-8) illustrate the interconnected nature of Jewish liturgical tradition.

The chapter's accounts of military victories attributed to divine intervention — including the appearance of heavenly horsemen — are read by Protestant scholars as reflecting the theological perspective of the author rather than strict historical reporting. Nevertheless, the underlying conviction that God fights on behalf of the faithful resonates with Protestant theology of providence.
