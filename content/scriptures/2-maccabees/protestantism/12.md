Chapter 12 is one of the most contested passages in 2 Maccabees from a Protestant perspective, as it contains the passage on prayer for the dead (12:43-46) that Catholic theology uses to support the doctrine of purgatory. Protestant interpreters reject the doctrinal conclusions drawn from this passage, arguing that the text is not canonical Scripture and that the practice it describes reflects a developing Jewish tradition rather than divinely revealed truth.

Nevertheless, Protestant scholars acknowledge the passage's importance for understanding the diversity of beliefs about the afterlife in Second Temple Judaism. The text reveals that at least some Jews in this period believed in an intermediate state in which the dead could benefit from the prayers and offerings of the living — a belief that would later be incorporated into Catholic and Orthodox theology but rejected by the Reformers.
