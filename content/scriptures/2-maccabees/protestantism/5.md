Protestant commentators read chapter 5's account of Antiochus's assault on Jerusalem as a horrifying example of the violence that state power can unleash against religious communities. The massacre of Jerusalem's inhabitants and the plundering of the Temple illustrate the biblical principle that earthly rulers who set themselves against God's people invite divine judgment.

The chapter's brief mention of Judas Maccabeus withdrawing to the wilderness (5:27) is noted by Protestant interpreters as evidence of the spiritual preparation that precedes effective action. The pattern of withdrawal, prayer, and return to battle reflects the Reformation understanding that authentic spiritual leadership is forged in solitude with God before being exercised in the public arena.
