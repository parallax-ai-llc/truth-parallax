Protestant interpreters read the final chapter of 2 Maccabees with appreciation for its dramatic account of Nicanor's defeat and Judas's vision of the heavenly intercession of Onias and Jeremiah. While Protestants reject the Catholic use of 15:12-16 to support the doctrine of the intercession of saints, they recognize the passage as evidence of developing beliefs about the afterlife in Second Temple Judaism.

The book's conclusion, with the author's modest apology for his literary efforts, provides a human touch that Protestant readers appreciate. The final words — acknowledging that his account may be imperfect but was offered in good faith — resonate with the Protestant emphasis on human limitation and the sufficiency of sincere effort before God.
