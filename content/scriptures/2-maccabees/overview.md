## Overview

2 Maccabees is not a sequel to 1 Maccabees but rather a parallel and independent account of many of the same events, covering the period from approximately 180 to 161 BCE. It presents itself as an abridgment of a five-volume work by Jason of Cyrene and was originally composed in Greek. The book is notably more theological in tone than 1 Maccabees, emphasizing divine intervention, martyrdom, and the resurrection of the dead.

## Structure

2 Maccabees is organized thematically rather than strictly chronologically:

1. **Prefatory Letters** (Chapters 1–2): Two letters to the Jews in Egypt concerning the festival of Hanukkah
2. **Heliodorus and the Temple** (Chapter 3): Divine protection of the Temple treasury
3. **Crisis under Antiochus IV** (Chapters 4–7): Hellenization, persecution, and the martyrdoms of Eleazar and the seven brothers
4. **Judas Maccabeus's Campaigns** (Chapters 8–15): Military victories attributed to divine assistance

## Cross-Traditional Significance

2 Maccabees holds particular doctrinal importance across traditions:

- **Catholicism**: Canonical; key prooftexts for purgatory (12:43-46), prayer for the dead, and the intercession of saints
- **Eastern Orthodoxy**: Canonical; supports the practice of commemorating the faithful departed
- **Protestantism**: Apocryphal; historically informative but rejected as doctrinal authority, partly due to its support for prayer for the dead
- **Judaism**: Not canonical, but the martyrdom narratives deeply influenced rabbinic theology

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Martyrdom | Suffering and death for faithfulness to Torah |
| Resurrection | Explicit belief that God will raise the righteous dead |
| Prayer for the Dead | Offering sacrifices on behalf of fallen soldiers |
| Divine Intervention | Angelic appearances and heavenly warriors |
| Temple Theology | The Jerusalem Temple as God's chosen dwelling |
