Catholic interpretation of 2 Maccabees 15 places particular emphasis on Judas's dream-vision of the deceased high priest Onias and the prophet Jeremiah praying for the Jewish people (15:12-16). This passage provides direct scriptural support for two central Catholic doctrines: the intercession of the saints in heaven on behalf of the living, and the efficacy of prayer offered by the righteous departed.

The Catechism of the Catholic Church cites this passage in its teaching on the communion of saints (CCC 956) and the practice of invoking the intercession of the saints. The vision of Jeremiah presenting Judas with a golden sword symbolizes the heavenly assistance that the saints provide to the Church Militant. The book's conclusion, with its emphasis on the mingling of history and interpretation, reflects the Catholic hermeneutical principle that sacred history is always more than mere chronicle — it is the revelation of God's saving purposes.
