Catholic commentators read chapter 4 as a cautionary narrative about the corruption that follows when religious leaders seek worldly power and accommodation with pagan culture. The successive usurpations of the high priesthood by Jason and Menelaus — purchased through bribery from a pagan king — illustrate the sin of simony that the Church has condemned throughout its history.

The murder of the righteous Onias III is understood by Catholic interpreters as a type of the passion of Christ — the innocent high priest killed through the machinations of corrupt religious authorities. The Catholic doctrine of the indefectibility of the Church finds an anticipatory expression in the conviction that God's purposes survive even the most egregious failures of human leadership.
