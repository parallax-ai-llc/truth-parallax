Chapter 12 contains one of the most doctrinally significant passages in 2 Maccabees for Catholic theology: Judas's offering of a sin sacrifice and prayers for fallen soldiers found wearing idolatrous amulets (12:39-45). The Catechism of the Catholic Church (CCC 1032) explicitly cites this passage as scriptural evidence for the practice of praying for the dead and, by implication, for the existence of purgatory.

Catholic interpretation reads Judas's action as demonstrating several key doctrinal points: that the dead can benefit from the prayers and offerings of the living; that there exists an intermediate state between death and final judgment in which purification is possible; and that the living and the dead remain united in the communion of saints. The text's explicit statement that it is "a holy and wholesome thought to pray for the dead" (12:46, Douay-Rheims) has been cited in Catholic teaching from the Church Fathers through the Council of Trent to the present day.
