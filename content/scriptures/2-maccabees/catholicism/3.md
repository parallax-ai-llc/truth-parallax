Catholic interpretation reads the Heliodorus episode in chapter 3 as a dramatic demonstration of God's protection of sacred spaces and their contents. The angelic warriors who punish Heliodorus for attempting to seize the Temple treasury illustrate the Catholic teaching that holy places are under divine guardianship and that sacrilege invites supernatural retribution.

The high priest Onias's prayer for Heliodorus's healing after his punishment demonstrates the Catholic principle of charity toward enemies and the priestly ministry of intercession. The episode is read as prefiguring the Church's sacramental authority to mediate divine healing and reconciliation, and it supports the Catholic teaching that consecrated persons and places possess a real, not merely symbolic, sacred character.
