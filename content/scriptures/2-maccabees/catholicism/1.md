Catholic interpretation reads the opening letter of 2 Maccabees as an inspired communication between the Jerusalem community and the Egyptian Diaspora, expressing the unity of God's people across geographical boundaries. The letter's emphasis on the proper observance of the festival of Hanukkah and the sacred fire reflects the Catholic understanding that liturgical practice is not merely human custom but divinely guided tradition.

The reference to the sacred fire of the altar, miraculously preserved and restored, resonates with Catholic theology of the Eucharistic sacrifice, in which the fire of divine love continually renews the offering. The letter's concern for communal unity and shared liturgical observance prefigures the Catholic Church's emphasis on universal communion through shared sacramental worship.
