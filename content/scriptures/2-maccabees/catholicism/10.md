Catholic interpretation of chapter 10's Temple purification emphasizes its typological significance for the Church's liturgical life. The eight-day celebration, with its branches, palm fronds, and hymns of thanksgiving, prefigures the Catholic practice of celebrating the dedication of churches and altars with solemn liturgical rites. The reference to the Feast of Tabernacles (10:6) connects the Maccabean rededication to the broader biblical theology of God's dwelling among his people.

The heavenly horsemen who appear in battle (10:29-30) illustrate the Catholic teaching on the communion of saints, in which the Church Militant on earth is assisted by the Church Triumphant in heaven. The intervention of celestial warriors confirms the Catholic conviction that the spiritual and material realms are not separate but interconnected dimensions of a single reality ordered by divine providence.
