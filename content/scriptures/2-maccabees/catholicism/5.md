Catholic exegesis reads chapter 5's account of Antiochus's assault on Jerusalem as a narrative of sacrilegious violence against God's holy city and Temple. The plundering of the Temple's sacred vessels and the massacre of the inhabitants illustrate the Catholic understanding that persecution, while permitted by divine providence, ultimately serves to purify and strengthen the faith of God's people.

The brief mention of Judas Maccabeus withdrawing to the wilderness (5:27) is read by Catholic interpreters as a model of strategic retreat and spiritual preparation — akin to Jesus's withdrawal to the desert before his public ministry and the monastic tradition of withdrawal from the world for the sake of deeper engagement with God.
