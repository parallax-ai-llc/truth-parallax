Catholic exegesis reads chapter 2's account of Jeremiah hiding the ark of the covenant with particular interest, as the tradition of the hidden ark connects to broader Catholic teaching about the continuity of God's presence with his people even when visible signs of that presence are withdrawn. The Catholic tradition has seen in the hidden ark a type of the Blessed Virgin Mary, the "Ark of the New Covenant" who bore Christ within her.

The chapter's reference to the fire from heaven consuming the sacrifice at the Temple's dedication (2:10) is understood typologically as prefiguring the descent of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost. Catholic interpreters note the emphasis on the indissoluble connection between sacred history, prophetic authority, and liturgical practice — a connection that the Catholic Magisterium identifies as foundational to the Church's sacramental life.
