Catholic exegesis reads the death of Antiochus IV in chapter 9 as a dramatic demonstration of the principle articulated in the Magnificat: God "has put down the mighty from their thrones" (Luke 1:52). The detailed description of the tyrant's physical suffering and moral anguish illustrates the Catholic teaching that sin carries intrinsic consequences and that God's justice, while patient, is ultimately inexorable.

Antiochus's deathbed letter, expressing remorse and promising restoration to the Jews, is read by some Catholic commentators as evidence of the possibility of deathbed conversion — a theme important in Catholic pastoral theology. However, the text leaves ambiguous whether his repentance was genuine or merely self-serving, illustrating the Catholic teaching that the sincerity of conversion is known fully only to God.
