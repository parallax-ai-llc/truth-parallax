Jewish historians read chapter 8's account of Judas's military victories as evidence of the effectiveness of the Maccabean resistance movement and the religious motivation that sustained it. The chapter's emphasis on prayer and fasting before battle reflects the integration of military action and religious devotion that characterized the early phase of the revolt.

Modern Jewish interpreters have debated the historical reliability of 2 Maccabees' account, noting its tendency toward exaggeration and theological interpretation compared to the more restrained narrative of 1 Maccabees. Nevertheless, the underlying historical reality of significant Maccabean military success against Seleucid forces is well established, and the chapter provides valuable evidence for the tactics and motivations of the Jewish resistance.
