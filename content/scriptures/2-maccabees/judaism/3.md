Jewish readers appreciate chapter 3's account of Heliodorus's supernatural punishment as a narrative of divine protection for the Temple treasury. The episode illustrates a theme central to Jewish theology: God's active protection of the Temple as his chosen dwelling place. The miraculous intervention — angelic warriors punishing the would-be plunderer — reflects a tradition of divine guardianship that appears in various forms throughout Jewish literature.

Modern Jewish historians have debated the historicity of the Heliodorus episode, noting that the combination of accurate historical detail (Heliodorus is known from other sources) and supernatural narrative is characteristic of Jewish historiography in this period. The story functions both as historical record and theological statement, affirming God's sovereignty over his holy place.
