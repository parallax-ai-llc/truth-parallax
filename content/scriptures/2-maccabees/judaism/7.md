The martyrdom of the seven brothers and their mother is perhaps the most influential narrative in 2 Maccabees for the development of Jewish religious thought. The story became a foundational text for Jewish martyrology, retold and elaborated in rabbinic literature (Gittin 57b, Lamentations Rabbah), and invoked by Jewish communities facing persecution from the Roman period through the Middle Ages to the Shoah.

The brothers' explicit declarations of resurrection faith (7:9, 7:14, 7:23) represent one of the earliest clear statements of this doctrine in Jewish literature and contributed to the Pharisaic teaching that became normative in rabbinic Judaism. The mother's exhortation in 7:28, which includes the earliest explicit statement of creation ex nihilo in Jewish literature, reflects the theological sophistication of Hellenistic Judaism and its ability to articulate fundamental beliefs in philosophical terms.
