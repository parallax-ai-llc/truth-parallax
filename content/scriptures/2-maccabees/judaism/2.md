Jewish interpreters approach chapter 2's account of Jeremiah hiding the ark of the covenant with interest, as it preserves a tradition about one of the great mysteries of Jewish history — the fate of the ark after the Babylonian destruction of the First Temple. While rabbinic tradition offers various accounts of the ark's disposition (including its concealment beneath the Temple Mount), 2 Maccabees' version places it on Mount Nebo, connecting it to the Mosaic tradition.

The chapter's reference to Jason of Cyrene and the method of historical composition provides valuable evidence for the literary culture of Hellenistic Judaism. Jewish historians have studied this preface as an example of the sophisticated historiographical practices developed by Diaspora Jewish writers who were engaged with Greek literary conventions while remaining committed to their ancestral tradition.
