Jewish scholars read the opening letter of 2 Maccabees as an important document of Diaspora Judaism, revealing the relationship between the Jerusalem community and the large Jewish population in Egypt. The letter's emphasis on the shared observance of Hanukkah illustrates the role of festivals in maintaining Jewish unity across geographical distance — a function that festivals continue to serve in modern Jewish life.

The reference to the sacred fire of the altar, miraculously preserved and restored, represents a tradition not found in other Jewish sources and reflects the theological creativity of Hellenistic Judaism. Jewish scholars note the tension between this supernatural account and the more restrained narrative of 1 Maccabees, seeing in the contrast evidence of different theological perspectives within Second Temple Judaism.
