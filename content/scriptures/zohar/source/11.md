---
title: "The Idra Zuta - The Small Assembly"
scripture: "zohar"
chapter: 11
---

**1** On the day that Rabbi Shimon was to depart from this world, he gathered his companions for the last time. He said: Now I shall reveal what I have never revealed before.

**2** He spoke of the mysteries of the divine countenance, of the hair of the Ancient One, of the forehead, the eyes, the nose, the mouth, and the beard.

**3** Each feature represents a channel of divine energy flowing into the world. The eyes represent providence. The ears represent the hearing of prayer. The mouth represents the spoken word of creation.

**4** As Rabbi Shimon spoke, a fire surrounded the house, and no one could approach. The companions wept, for they knew their master was departing.

**5** Rabbi Shimon said his final words: "I am for my Beloved, and my Beloved is for me." He lay down and died, and a voice from heaven proclaimed: "Happy are you, Rabbi Shimon, for the portion of the World to Come is yours.
