---
title: "The Hidden Light"
scripture: "zohar"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai opened his discourse: "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth." The word "created" implies a hidden mystery, for creation emerges from the concealed to the revealed.

**2** Before the Holy One created the world, He was alone with His Name. He conceived the idea of creating the world and traced the designs of the world before Him, but they would not endure.

**3** This may be compared to an architect who conceives the plan of a building. He draws and redraws until the design is perfect. Only then does he begin to build.

**4** The Holy One, blessed be He, created worlds and destroyed them, until He created this world and said: "This pleases Me; those did not please Me."

**5** In the beginning, before shape and form were created, He was alone, without form and without resemblance to anything. Who can comprehend Him as He was before creation? It is forbidden to represent Him by any form or shape.
