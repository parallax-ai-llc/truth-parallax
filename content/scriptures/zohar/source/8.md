---
title: "The Night of the Bride"
scripture: "zohar"
chapter: 8
---

**1** On the eve of Shabbat, the Community of Israel is adorned as a bride before the King. The Shekhinah descends, and all harsh judgments are removed from the world.

**2** Rabbi Shimon said: When Shabbat enters, the Shekhinah rests upon all the holy people. The souls receive an additional soul, a Neshamah Yeterah, which departs when Shabbat ends.

**3** On the night of Shabbat, the table should be set with joy. Bread and wine should be placed upon it, for it is the table of the King.

**4** Songs should be sung, and Torah should be studied. For on this night, the gates of heaven are open, and the prayers of the righteous ascend without hindrance.

**5** The Shabbat is a taste of the World to Come. It is a day when the soul is free from the burdens of the body, and the divine light shines with special radiance.
