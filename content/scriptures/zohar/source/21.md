---
title: "Good and Evil"
scripture: "zohar"
chapter: 21
---

**1** Rabbi Shimon said: There is no absolute evil in creation. What appears as evil is actually good that has been distorted or misdirected.

**2** The evil inclination, the yetzer hara, was created by God as a necessary component of human existence. Without it, no one would build a house, marry, have children, or engage in business.

**3** The task of the righteous person is not to destroy the evil inclination but to harness it in the service of God. The energy of desire, properly directed, becomes the energy of holiness.

**4** When good and evil are separated in the final purification, what was called evil will be revealed as the hidden face of good. The husk will fall away, and the fruit will remain.

**5** The struggle between good and evil is the crucible in which the soul is refined. Through this struggle, the soul achieves its perfection and returns to its source in God.
