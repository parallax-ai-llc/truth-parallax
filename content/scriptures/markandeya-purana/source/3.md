---
title: "Markandeya Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "markandeya-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Markandeya spoke: Now I shall tell you the story of the Devi, the Supreme Goddess, who is the source of all power and the protectress of the universe.

**2** In ancient times, when the demon Mahishasura obtained a boon that he could not be slain by any god or man, he conquered the three worlds and drove the gods from their celestial abodes.

**3** The gods, defeated and humiliated, gathered together and directed their combined energies toward a single point. From that concentration of divine energy there arose a great radiance.

**4** That radiance took the form of a woman of surpassing beauty and terrible power. She was the Devi, the Great Goddess, the embodiment of the Shakti of all the gods.

**5** Shiva gave her his trident, Vishnu gave her his discus, Vayu gave her his bow, Indra gave her his thunderbolt. Each god contributed a weapon or ornament to the Goddess.

**6** Mounted upon a great lion, blazing with divine splendour, the Devi rode forth to battle against Mahishasura and his demon army.

**7** The battle raged for nine days and nine nights. The Devi slew the demon generals one by one, and her lion devoured the demon soldiers.

**8** On the final day, Mahishasura himself came forth to battle, changing his form from buffalo to lion to elephant to man.

**9** But the Devi, laughing with divine joy, pierced him with her trident and cut off his head with her sword. Thus was the demon Mahishasura slain.

**10** The gods sang hymns of praise to the Devi, and she bestowed boons upon them, saying: "Whenever you are afflicted by the demons, remember me, and I shall come to your aid."

**11** This is the Devi Mahatmya, the Glory of the Goddess, which is recited during the nine nights of Navaratri. Whoever hears it with devotion is freed from all suffering and attains the highest bliss.
