---
title: "Markandeya Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "markandeya-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Markandeya spoke: Now hear the account of the hymns of praise to the Devi and her many forms.

**2** The sage said: In this world, beings are bound by the three gunas -- sattva, rajas, and tamas. These qualities of nature pervade all creation and determine the character and destiny of every being.

**3** When sattva predominates, a being is inclined toward knowledge, purity, and devotion. When rajas predominates, a being is driven by desire, ambition, and restless activity. When tamas predominates, a being is sunk in ignorance, sloth, and delusion.

**4** The Supreme Goddess, who is beyond all the gunas, manifests herself through them for the creation, preservation, and dissolution of the universe.

**5** As Mahalakshmi, she preserves the world through the quality of sattva. As Mahasaraswati, she creates through the quality of rajas. As Mahakali, she dissolves through the quality of tamas.

**6** Yet she herself is beyond all these qualities, eternal, unchanging, the supreme consciousness from which all things arise and to which all things return.

**7** The devotee who worships the Goddess with a pure heart, free from selfish desire, attains liberation from the cycle of birth and death.

**8** The devotee who worships her for worldly ends receives wealth, power, and the fulfilment of desires, but remains bound to the wheel of samsara.

**9** Therefore the wise seek the Goddess not for worldly gain but for the supreme knowledge that liberates the soul from all bondage.

**10** Markandeya said: This teaching was given by the Goddess herself to King Suratha and the merchant Samadhi, and through them it has been transmitted to all humanity.

**11** Whoever hears this teaching with faith and devotion, whoever recites it with a pure heart, shall be freed from all sins and shall attain the grace of the Divine Mother.
