## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Proverbs draws on the Fathers, the Scholastics, and the Magisterium to read the book as a guide to virtue, a source for moral theology, and a text with rich typological significance — particularly in the identification of Lady Wisdom with both Christ and, in devotional tradition, the Blessed Virgin Mary.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Sapiential Theology
The Catholic intellectual tradition places Proverbs within the broader framework of *sapientia* (wisdom), connecting biblical wisdom to the natural law tradition developed by Thomas Aquinas.

### Marian Typology
The personification of Wisdom in Proverbs 8 — "The Lord possessed me at the beginning of his way" — has been applied to Mary in Catholic liturgy and devotion, particularly in the Feast of the Immaculate Conception readings.

### Virtue Ethics
Proverbs' practical teachings map naturally onto the Catholic framework of cardinal and theological virtues: prudence, justice, temperance, and fortitude.

## Theological Emphases

- **Natural Law**: Proverbs' appeal to observation and experience supports the Catholic natural law tradition
- **Prudence**: The virtue of prudence (*prudentia*) is extensively illustrated in Proverbs
- **Social Teaching**: Proverbs' concern for the poor and just governance informs Catholic social doctrine
- **Domestic Church**: The household instructions contribute to the theology of family life
