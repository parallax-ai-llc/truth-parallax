## Eshet Chayil: The Woman of Valor

Proverbs 31:10-31, the *Eshet Chayil* ("Woman of Valor"), holds a unique place in Jewish domestic liturgy. It is recited or sung by the husband every Friday evening at the Shabbat table, honoring the woman of the household.

## Key Verses and Rabbinic Interpretation

### The Acrostic Poem (31:10-31)

The *Eshet Chayil* is an alphabetical acrostic — each verse begins with a successive letter of the Hebrew alphabet, from *aleph* to *tav*. This literary form suggests completeness: the woman of valor embodies the totality of virtue.

### Allegorical Readings

Jewish tradition offers multiple allegorical layers:

| Reading | Proponent | Meaning |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Torah | Rashi, Vilna Gaon | The woman = Torah |
| The Soul | Kabbalistic | The woman = the *neshamah* (divine soul) |
| Shabbat | Midrashic | The woman = the Shabbat Queen |
| Sarah | Midrash Mishlei | Each verse refers to a righteous woman in Tanakh |

### "She fears the Lord" (31:30)

This verse provides the key to the entire poem: external beauty fades, but *yirat Hashem* endures. The Vilna Gaon writes that this verse summarizes not only the poem but the entire book of Proverbs.

## Liturgical Practice

The recitation of *Eshet Chayil* on Friday evening is attested from at least the 16th century, when the kabbalists of Safed incorporated it into the Shabbat *Kabbalat Shabbat* service. Today it is a universal Jewish practice.

## Theological Significance

The *Eshet Chayil* embodies the Jewish ideal that wisdom is not abstract philosophy but lived practice — expressed in generosity, industry, teaching, and reverence for God.
