## Words of the Wise

Jewish interpretation of Proverbs 22 draws on the rich tradition of rabbinic commentary — Talmud, Midrash, and the great medieval exegetes — to read these proverbs as practical expressions of Torah wisdom.

## Key Themes

### Reputation

Rashi frequently identifies the themes of Proverbs with Torah study and observance. The reputation discussed in this chapter is understood as an aspect of *hokhmah* (wisdom) rooted in *yirat Hashem* (fear of God).

### Training Children and Wealth

The Malbim's systematic commentary distinguishes carefully between related concepts, showing how training children and wealth are distinct but complementary aspects of the wise life.

### Poverty

The Vilna Gaon's mystical-ethical commentary on Proverbs finds deep spiritual significance in every word. The theme of poverty is connected to the soul's journey toward perfection through Torah and mitzvot.

## Mussar Application

The *mussar* (ethical instruction) tradition draws extensively on Proverbs for character development. Rabbi Israel Salanter's 19th-century movement used verses like those in this chapter as daily meditations for *tikkun middot* (character refinement).

## Theological Significance

For Jewish readers, Proverbs 22 is not merely practical advice but Torah-grounded wisdom that shapes the character of one who walks in God's ways. Every proverb reflects the divine moral order embedded in creation.
