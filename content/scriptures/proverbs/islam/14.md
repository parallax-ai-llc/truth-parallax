## Proverbs of Solomon V

Islamic engagement with Proverbs 14 evaluates its wisdom teachings against the standard of the Quran and Sunnah. While many practical and ethical observations in this chapter find parallels in Islamic *hikmah* (wisdom) and *adab* (ethical conduct) literature, the text is read with the awareness of *tahrif*.

## Quranic and Hadith Parallels

### Wisdom

The Quran addresses themes of wisdom in multiple passages. The Islamic tradition of *hikmah* literature, from Luqman's wisdom (Quran 31:12-19) to al-Ghazali's ethical writings, shares Proverbs' concern for practical moral guidance.

### Folly and Fear Of The Lord

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) addressed folly and fear of the Lord in numerous hadith, demonstrating that Islamic ethical teaching covers the same ground as biblical wisdom literature.

| Proverbs Theme | Islamic Parallel |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Wisdom | Related Quranic and hadith teachings |
| Folly | *Adab* (ethical conduct) literature |
| Fear Of The Lord | Prophetic example (*sunnah*) |
| Prudence | Islamic moral theology (*akhlaq*) |

## Wisdom of Sulayman

Islam honors Solomon (*Sulayman*) as a prophet who received divinely granted wisdom. Insofar as Proverbs preserves authentic Solomonic wisdom, it may retain remnants of prophetic teaching, though filtered through the process of *tahrif*.

## Theological Significance

The ethical content of Proverbs 14 is evaluated positively where it aligns with Quranic principles of justice, truthfulness, generosity, and God-consciousness. Where it diverges, the Quran serves as the corrective standard.
