## Overview

Islam honors Solomon (*Sulayman*) as a prophet endowed with extraordinary wisdom (*hikmah*) by God. While the Book of Proverbs is not directly cited in the Quran, its wisdom themes resonate with the Quranic portrayal of Sulayman and the broader Islamic *adab* (ethical-literary) tradition. Muslim scholars engage with Proverbs through the lens of *tahrif* — the belief that biblical texts have undergone alteration — accepting wisdom that aligns with Quranic teaching while questioning attributions and contexts.

## Quranic Parallels

The Quran praises Solomon's wisdom in several passages:

- **Quran 27:15**: "And We gave knowledge to David and Solomon, and they said: 'Praise be to Allah.'"
- **Quran 21:79**: "And We made Solomon to understand the matter, and to each We gave judgment and knowledge."
- **Quran 38:30**: "And We gave to David, Solomon — an excellent servant. Indeed, he was one who repeatedly turned back [to Allah]."

## Key Themes Shared with Islamic Tradition

- **Fear of God (*taqwa*)**: Parallels Proverbs' "fear of the Lord" as the beginning of wisdom
- **Just Speech**: The Quran and hadith extensively address truthful and measured speech
- **Avoiding Excess**: Temperance in wealth, food, and pleasure
- **Honoring Parents**: A theme shared between Proverbs and Quranic ethics

## Islamic Reception

| Aspect | Islamic View |
|--------|-------------|
| Solomonic authorship | Accepted in principle; Sulayman was a wise prophet |
| Textual reliability | Subject to *tahrif*; individual proverbs evaluated against Quranic principles |
| Wisdom content | Much is compatible with Islamic *hikmah* and *adab* literature |
| Lady Wisdom (Proverbs 8) | Not recognized as a theological concept; wisdom is an attribute of God |
