## Warning Against Adultery

Islamic engagement with Proverbs 5 evaluates its wisdom teachings against the standard of the Quran and Sunnah. While many practical and ethical observations in this chapter find parallels in Islamic *hikmah* (wisdom) and *adab* (ethical conduct) literature, the text is read with the awareness of *tahrif*.

## Quranic and Hadith Parallels

### Adultery

The Quran addresses themes of adultery in multiple passages. The Islamic tradition of *hikmah* literature, from Luqman's wisdom (Quran 31:12-19) to al-Ghazali's ethical writings, shares Proverbs' concern for practical moral guidance.

### Marriage and Faithfulness

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) addressed marriage and faithfulness in numerous hadith, demonstrating that Islamic ethical teaching covers the same ground as biblical wisdom literature.

| Adultery | Related Quranic and hadith teachings |
| Marriage | *Adab* (ethical conduct) literature |

| Consequences | Islamic moral theology (*akhlaq*) |

## Wisdom of Sulayman

Islam honors Solomon (*Sulayman*) as a prophet who received divinely granted wisdom. Insofar as Proverbs preserves authentic Solomonic wisdom, it may retain remnants of prophetic teaching, though filtered through the process of *tahrif*.

## Theological Significance

The ethical content of Proverbs 5 is evaluated positively where it aligns with Quranic principles of justice, truthfulness, generosity, and God-consciousness. Where it diverges, the Quran serves as the corrective standard.
