## Overview

Proverbs holds unique importance for Gnostic traditions because of its personification of Wisdom (*Hokhmah* in Hebrew, *Sophia* in Greek). The Gnostic myth of Sophia — a divine being whose fall from the Pleroma results in the creation of the material world — draws directly on the imagery of Proverbs 1-9, while radically reinterpreting it.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Sophia as Divine Aeon
Gnostic systems, particularly Valentinianism, identified Lady Wisdom of Proverbs with Sophia, one of the Aeons in the divine Pleroma. Proverbs 8:22-31, where Wisdom describes her origins "before the world began," was read as Sophia's testimony of her pre-cosmic existence.

### The Two Wisdoms
Some Gnostic systems distinguished between a higher Sophia (who remains in the Pleroma) and a lower Sophia (Achamoth or Sophia Prunikos), whose fall generates the material world. This mirrors Proverbs' own contrast between Lady Wisdom and the "strange woman" (Dame Folly).

### Inversion of Fear
While Proverbs teaches "the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom," Gnostic interpreters questioned *which* Lord is feared. If the Demiurge, then fear is bondage; true wisdom (*gnosis*) comes through knowledge of the transcendent God, not fear of the creator.

## Nag Hammadi Connections

- **The Thunder, Perfect Mind**: A text featuring a divine feminine speaker whose paradoxical self-descriptions echo Proverbs' Wisdom
- **On the Origin of the World**: References Sophia's role in creation
- **The Sophia of Jesus Christ**: Presents a Gnostic Sophia teaching that reframes biblical wisdom

## Theological Significance

| Proverbs Concept | Gnostic Reinterpretation |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Fear of the Lord | Fear of the Demiurge — a lesser, limiting emotion |
| Lady Wisdom | Sophia, the fallen and redeemed Aeon |
| The "strange woman" | Lower Sophia or the material world's seduction |
| Tree of Life (3:18) | Access to true gnosis, hidden by the archons |
| Wisdom's house (9:1) | The Pleroma, with its seven pillars as Aeons |
