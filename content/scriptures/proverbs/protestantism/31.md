## The Words of King Lemuel and the Woman of Valor

Proverbs 31 divides into two sections: the oracle of King Lemuel's mother (31:1-9) and the acrostic poem celebrating the *Eshet Chayil*, the "Woman of Valor" (31:10-31).

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Queen Mother's Instruction (31:1-9)

A mother warns her royal son against wine and women, urging him instead to defend the rights of the poor. Protestant interpreters note the remarkable authority of a woman's voice in shaping a king's character.

### The Woman of Valor (31:10-31)

This acrostic poem (each verse begins with a successive letter of the Hebrew alphabet) has been interpreted in multiple ways within Protestantism:

- **Literal**: A celebration of the godly wife and mother
- **Christological**: Some Puritan interpreters saw the woman as a figure of the Church
- **Wisdom**: The Woman of Valor as Lady Wisdom from chapters 1-9, bringing the book full circle

| Characteristic | Verse | Significance |
|---------------|-------|-------------|
| Industry | 31:13-19 | The dignity and value of work |
| Generosity | 31:20 | Concern for the poor |
| Fearlessness | 31:25 | Strength and confidence |
| Wisdom | 31:26 | Teaching with kindness |
| Fear of the Lord | 31:30 | The foundation returns |

### "Charm is deceitful, and beauty is vain" (31:30)

This verse echoes the book's opening theme: the fear of the Lord is what truly matters. The inclusio (literary bookend) between 1:7 and 31:30 frames the entire book.

## Theological Significance

The chapter demonstrates that wisdom is embodied in daily life — in work, family, generosity, and speech. The Protestant emphasis on vocation finds rich expression here: every calling, including domestic labor, is dignified before God.
