## Sayings of the Wise

Proverbs 23 belongs to the latter collections of the book, which include diverse voices and literary forms beyond the standard two-line proverb. Protestant interpretation values these sections for their practical wisdom and their contributions to the book's overall theological vision.

## Key Themes

### Temperance

The chapter's emphasis on temperance reflects the comprehensive scope of biblical wisdom — encompassing governance, social ethics, and personal conduct.

### Discipline and Wine

The interplay between discipline and wine in this chapter provides material for Protestant ethical reflection on the Christian's responsibilities in society.

### Greed

The theme of greed connects to the broader canonical witness about the moral order God has established in creation.

## Interpretive Approaches

| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Canonical | Reading within the context of the whole Bible |
| Wisdom theology | Connecting to the broader sapiential tradition |
| Practical ethics | Direct application to daily life |
| Christological | Seeing Christ as the fulfillment of wisdom |

## Theological Significance

Protestant theology finds in these chapters confirmation that the fear of the Lord extends to every dimension of life. The diverse literary forms — numerical sayings, longer instructions, and biographical observations — demonstrate that wisdom takes many shapes.
