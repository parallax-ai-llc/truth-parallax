---
title: "Asclepius Section 3"
scripture: "asclepius"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Man is a great wonder, O Asclepius, an animal worthy of reverence and honor. For he passes into the nature of a god as though he himself were a god.

**2** He is familiar with the race of demons, knowing that he shares the same origin with them. He despises that part of his nature which is only human, for he has put his hope in the divinity of the other part.

**3** How much more fortunate is the blend of human nature! Joined to the gods by a kindred divinity, he inwardly despises that part of him by which he is bound to the earth. All other living things he binds to himself in a bond of love, feeling himself related to them by the divine plan.

**4** He raises his eyes to heaven, while he takes care of the earth. And so he is in the fortunate middle position: he loves those things that are below him and is beloved by those above.

**5** He cultivates the earth; he mingles with the elements by the swiftness of thought. He descends to the depths of the sea by the keenness of his mind. Everything is permitted to him: heaven itself does not seem too high, for he measures it by the sagacity of his mind as though it were nearby.

**6** No dimness of the air obstructs the concentration of his thought. No denseness of the earth impedes his work. No depth of water blocks his downward gaze. He is everything, and he is everywhere.

**7** Of all these living things, some have received the use of fire, others the use of air, others the use of water, and others the use of earth. But man uses all of these—fire, water, air, and earth. He sees heaven too, and touches it with his perception.

**8** But God—God is above heaven, and exists everywhere, and pervades all things. For he is both energy and power, and there is nothing difficult in conceiving God.

**9** If you wish to contemplate God, look at the ordering of the world, and the beautiful arrangement of that order; look at the necessity of what is made manifest, and the providence of what has been and what is coming to be.

**10** Look at matter filled wholly with life; see the great God in motion, with all the good and beautiful within him—gods and demons and men.
