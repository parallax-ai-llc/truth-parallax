---
title: "Asclepius Section 4"
scripture: "asclepius"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Are you saying, Trismegistus, that God contains all things within himself? And where, then, are these things? For material space is not empty.

**2** Asclepius, where will you place them? What place will you assign to these things apart from God? For God is not space, nor is he some material thing. But do not suppose that there is something apart from him: rather, all things exist in God.

**3** Energy is not something that exists in space; rather, space exists in energy. And all things are contained in energy. There is nothing among all existing things which is not God.

**4** Neither size nor place nor quality nor form nor time surrounds God; for he is all. And as the All, he pervades all things and embraces all things. Worship and adore this word of mine, O Asclepius.

**5** For there is one religion of God alone, and that is: not to be evil. To seek after these things with the faculty of mind—this is the peculiar property of divine knowledge and divine understanding.

**6** For to know God through the mind alone, to gaze upon God with the eyes of the mind alone—that is the beautiful and the good, and it shall profit the man who does so above all things.

**7** It can profit no one to worship the one God of single-minded devotion. For in the course of worship, one must not let the excess of any thing cause evil.

**8** For the gifts of God are unlike those of men. They cannot be corrupted nor diminished nor withdrawn. For the worship of God is one thing: the knowledge of the beings who exist.

**9** To know the beings who exist, and to give thanks to God in accordance with this knowledge—this is the only incense offering that is worthy of God.

**10** And when you have understood these things, Asclepius, you will find that the human mind shares the nature of the great God, provided that it is trained, and you will see that it surpasses even the nature of the gods in heaven.

**11** For none of the heavenly gods will leave the borders of heaven and descend to earth; but man ascends into heaven and measures it, and knows what is high and what is low, and understands all things with precision.
