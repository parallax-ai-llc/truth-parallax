---
title: "Asclepius Section 1"
scripture: "asclepius"
chapter: 1
---

**1** This is the discourse which Hermes Trismegistus spoke to Asclepius. O Asclepius, are you not aware that Egypt is the image of heaven? Or, more truly, the place below which is the transference and descent of all things that are governed and exercised in heaven?

**2** And to speak the truth, our land is the temple of the whole world. And yet, since it is fitting that the wise should know all things in advance, it is not right that you should be ignorant of this: there will come a time when it will appear that the Egyptians have in vain honored the divine with heartfelt piety.

**3** All their holy worship will become without effect and fruitless. For the gods, leaving the earth, will return to heaven; they will abandon Egypt, and the land which was once the home of religion will be bereft of the presence of the deities.

**4** Foreigners will fill this country and this land. Not only will there be neglect of religion, but, what is more grievous, it will be enacted under the forms of law, with penalties prescribed, that men shall not practice piety or worship God.

**5** Then this most holy land, the home of sanctuaries and temples, will be filled with tombs and the dead. O Egypt, Egypt! Of your religion only fables will remain, and they will be incredible to your children.

**6** Only words cut in stone will remain to tell of your pious deeds. The Scythian or the Indian or some other such neighboring barbarian will inhabit Egypt.

**7** For the divine will return to heaven, and all the people will die, and thus Egypt will be made a desert, bereft of God and man.

**8** And I speak to you, O most holy river, and I tell you what will come: you will overflow with blood, even to your banks. And the divine blood will not merely pollute your divine waters, but will cause them to overflow entirely, and the count of the dead will far exceed the living.

**9** And whoever does survive will be recognized as an Egyptian only by his language; in his actions he will seem to be of another race.

**10** Why do you weep, Asclepius? Egypt will be set upon an even more grievous path, and far worse things than these shall come to pass. The land which once was holy, the land most loving of the divine, dwelling place of the gods upon the earth—this land, teacher of religion and piety—shall exceed all others in cruel deeds.
