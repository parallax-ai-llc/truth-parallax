---
title: "Asclepius — Section 6: Restoration and Final Teaching"
scripture: "asclepius"
chapter: 6
---

**1** "But when all these things have come to pass, Asclepius, then the Lord, the Father and god of the world, will look on that which has come to pass, and will stay the disorder by the counterforce of his will, which is the good."

**2** "He will call back to the right path those who have gone astray; he will cleanse the world of evil, washing it away with floods, burning it out with the fiercest fire, and expelling it with war and pestilence."

**3** "And thus he will bring back his world to its former aspect, so that the cosmos will once more be deemed worthy of worship and wondering reverence."

**4** "Such is the new birth of the cosmos; it is a making again of all things good, a holy and awe-inspiring restoration of all nature; and it is wrought inside the process of time by the eternal will of the Creator."

**5** "For the will of God has no beginning; it is ever the same, and as it now is, even so it has ever been, without beginning. For it is the very being of God to purpose good."

**6** Asclepius asked: "Is purpose, then, the same as will, O Trismegistus?"

**7** "Will, O Asclepius, issues from purpose; and from will issues each several act of will."

**8** "For all things are in God, and God is in all things. The two are joined together, as the one depends upon the other."

**9** "For God both is all that now exists and all that is not. He has brought forth all things that exist, and keeps within himself all things that do not exist."

**10** "This is the whole of knowledge, Asclepius, these are the things which we have ventured to recount. We have set forth things divine and have revealed things sacred."

**11** The Perfect Discourse of Asclepius.
