## Overview

The Asclepius addresses themes of universal significance: the dignity of the human being, the sacred character of the cosmos, and the cyclical pattern of spiritual decline and renewal. The concept of humanity as a 'great miracle' parallels the Islamic concept of al-insan al-kamil (the perfect human), the Hindu concept of the microcosm (the human body as a miniature universe), and the Jewish concept of the image of God (tzelem Elohim). The prophecy of Egypt's decline and the promise of restoration mirror the Hindu concept of pralaya (cosmic dissolution) followed by srishti (recreation) and the Buddhist understanding of the periodic arising and passing of world-systems.
