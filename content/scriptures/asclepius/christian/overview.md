## Overview

For Christian readers, the Asclepius offers both resonances and challenges. The celebration of humanity's dignity anticipates the Christian humanist tradition (Pico della Mirandola explicitly drew on this text). The concept of the living cosmos resonates with the sacramental theology that sees creation as suffused with divine presence. However, the text's polytheistic framework — including gods made by human beings — stands in tension with Christian monotheism, and the 'Lament for Egypt' has been read as a lament for the passing of paganism in the face of Christian triumph.
