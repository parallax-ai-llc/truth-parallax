## Overview

Jewish readers find in the Asclepius echoes of the biblical celebration of human dignity — 'You have made him a little lower than the angels and crowned him with glory and honor' (Psalm 8:5) — and the prophetic tradition of national decline and restoration. The concept of the living cosmos resonates with the Jewish blessing tradition that sees God's presence throughout creation, while the notion of human-made gods represents a sharp departure from the Jewish prohibition of idolatry. The cyclical framework of decline and restoration can be compared to the Jewish concept of galut (exile) and geulah (redemption).
