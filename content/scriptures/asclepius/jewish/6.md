## The Restoration of All Things — Jewish Perspectives

Jewish readers encounter in this section of Asclepius 21-29 evidence of how Jewish traditions were appropriated and transformed in the Gnostic context. Jewish readers find in the Asclepius echoes of the biblical celebration of human dignity — 'You have made him a little lower than the angels and crowned him with glory and honor' (Psalm 8:5) — and the prophetic tradition of national decline and restoration.

The section "The Restoration of All Things" draws on Jewish sources — Genesis narratives, prophetic imagery, wisdom traditions, and possibly early mystical speculation — but deploys them within a framework that diverges from rabbinic Judaism. This transformation is not simple distortion but a creative engagement with traditions that remained fluid and contested in the second and third centuries.

Reading this passage against rabbinic parallels illuminates both shared concerns and fundamental divergences — revealing what was at stake theologically when Jewish concepts entered Gnostic systems.
