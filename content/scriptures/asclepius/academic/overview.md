## Overview

The Asclepius (NHC VI,8) is a Coptic translation of sections 21-29 of the Latin Hermetic dialogue Asclepius, allowing scholars to compare the Coptic and Latin versions for evidence of textual transmission and translation practice. The 'Lament for Egypt' (sections 24-26) has been studied as a possible response to the Christianization of Egypt and the decline of traditional Egyptian religion. The text's celebration of humanity as a 'great miracle' (magnum miraculum) had enormous influence on Renaissance humanism — Pico della Mirandola's Oration on the Dignity of Man (1486) draws directly on this passage.
