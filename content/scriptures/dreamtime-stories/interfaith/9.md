The Dreaming narratives encode a comprehensive system of law and morality that governs every aspect of Aboriginal life. These laws — covering kinship obligations, marriage rules, territorial boundaries, resource management, ceremonial responsibilities, and social behavior — are not human inventions but were established by the Ancestral Beings during the Dreaming. Transgression of these laws in the narratives invariably results in consequences (transformation into stone, exile, death), establishing precedents for the maintenance of social order.

Interfaith engagement with Law and Morality in the Dreaming offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
