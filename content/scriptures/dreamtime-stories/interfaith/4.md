Aboriginal Australian astronomical knowledge is among the oldest in the world, with the night sky serving as a vast map of Dreaming narratives. The stars, constellations, planets, and celestial phenomena are understood as Ancestral Beings or as the marks of their actions. The Pleiades are often associated with a group of women (the Seven Sisters, known as Kungkarungkara in Pitjantjatjara tradition) pursued across the sky. The dark spaces between stars (dark constellations, such as the Emu in the Sky) are as significant as the bright stars themselves.

Interfaith engagement with The Sky Ancestors and Celestial Dreaming offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
