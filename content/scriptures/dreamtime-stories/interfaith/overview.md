## Overview

Interfaith engagement with Aboriginal Australian Dreaming traditions is both urgent and delicate — urgent because the Dreaming offers the perspective of the world's oldest continuous spiritual tradition on questions of ecological stewardship, time, and belonging, and delicate because Aboriginal protocols strictly govern the sharing of sacred knowledge. Any interfaith engagement must respect these protocols and the authority of Aboriginal custodians over their own knowledge.

When appropriately shared, the Dreaming offers interfaith conversations a radically different understanding of the relationship between time and eternity, between land and spirituality, and between human beings and other species. The concept of custodianship — caring for country, knowledge, and ceremony not as owners but as temporary guardians — resonates with environmental and spiritual concerns across many faith traditions in the context of contemporary ecological crisis.
