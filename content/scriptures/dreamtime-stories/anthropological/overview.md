## Overview

Anthropological study of Aboriginal Australian Dreaming traditions encompasses social organization, kinship, land management, art, ceremony, and knowledge systems. The Dreaming provides the framework for Aboriginal social life: totemic affiliations determine marriage rules, ceremonial obligations, and rights to country; Songlines define territorial boundaries and trade networks; and the laws established by the Ancestors govern every aspect of interpersonal and intergroup relations.

Key anthropological themes include the relationship between Dreaming narratives and land management (including fire-stick farming and other ecological practices), the structure of knowledge transmission through initiation, the gendered dimensions of ceremonial knowledge (men's business and women's business), the role of art (rock art, body painting, ground designs, bark painting) in maintaining and transmitting Dreaming knowledge, and the impact of colonization on the continuity of Dreaming traditions.
