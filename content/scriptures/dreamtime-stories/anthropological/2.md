The Songlines (also called Dreaming Tracks or Yiri in some languages) are the paths across the landscape that the Ancestral Beings traveled during the Dreaming, singing the world into existence as they went. Each song corresponds to a specific stretch of country, and by singing the appropriate songs in sequence, one can navigate across vast distances. The Songlines form a continental network connecting sacred sites and different language groups, creating a system of trade, ceremony, and knowledge exchange that spans the entire Australian continent.

Anthropological study of The Songlines and Dreaming Tracks examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
