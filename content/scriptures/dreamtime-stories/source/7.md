---
title: "The Tiddalik Flood"
scripture: "dreamtime-stories"
chapter: 7
---

**1** In the Dreaming, Tiddalik the giant frog woke up with a great thirst. He began to drink. He drank the rivers, he drank the lakes, he drank the billabongs.

**2** He drank until every drop of water in the land was in his belly. The rivers were dry, the lakes were empty, the land was parched. The animals and the people had nothing to drink.

**3** The animals gathered together and tried to think of a way to make Tiddalik release the water. They decided they must make him laugh, for if he laughed, the water would pour from his mouth.

**4** The kookaburra told jokes. The kangaroo did tricks. The wombat pulled funny faces. But Tiddalik did not laugh.

**5** Then the eel, Nabunum, began to dance. He twisted and wriggled and tied himself in knots. Tiddalik watched, and his mouth began to twitch. He tried to hold it in, but the eel was too funny.

**6** Tiddalik burst out laughing, and the water poured from his mouth in a great flood. The rivers filled again, the lakes were restored, and the land was green once more.

**7** And the people learned: do not take more than you need. If one person hoards everything, all will suffer. Share the water, share the food, share the land.
