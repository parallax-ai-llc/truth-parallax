---
title: "The Songlines"
scripture: "dreamtime-stories"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The paths that the Ancestor Spirits walked across the land are called songlines. Each songline is a route marked by songs, and each song describes the landscape and the events that created it.

**2** A person who knows the song can navigate across the country, even through land they have never seen before. The song tells them where the waterholes are, where the food grows, where the sacred sites lie.

**3** The songlines cross the whole of the continent, connecting the sacred sites of different peoples. A songline may pass through the territories of many different language groups.

**4** When a person walks a songline and sings the appropriate songs, they are re-enacting the journey of the Ancestor Spirits. They are keeping the Dreaming alive.

**5** If the songs are not sung, the land will lose its power. The waterholes will dry up, the animals will leave, the plants will die. The songs sustain the land, and the land sustains the people.

**6** This is why the ceremonies must be performed, why the songs must be sung, why the sacred sites must be protected. For the land and the people and the Dreaming are one.
