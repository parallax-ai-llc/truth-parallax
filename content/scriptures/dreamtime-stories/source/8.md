---
title: "How Death Came to the World"
scripture: "dreamtime-stories"
chapter: 8
---

**1** In the Dreaming, there was no death. The people lived forever, and the land was crowded with the living.

**2** The Moon said to the people: When I die, I am gone for three days, but I always come back. You too shall die and come back, like me.

**3** But the Native Cat was jealous of the people. He said: No, when the people die, they shall not come back. They shall die forever, like me.

**4** The Moon argued with the Native Cat, but the Native Cat was stubborn. He insisted that death should be permanent.

**5** And so it was decided: death would be permanent for the people. They would not come back like the moon. The Native Cat had his way.

**6** And the people were angry with the Native Cat, for he had brought permanent death into the world. But it could not be undone. What has been decided in the Dreaming cannot be changed.

**7** This is why the people perform the ceremonies for the dead, to help their spirits find their way back to the Dreaming, to the place of the Ancestor Spirits, where they will dwell forever.
