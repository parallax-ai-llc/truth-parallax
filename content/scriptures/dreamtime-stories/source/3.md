---
title: "The First Men"
scripture: "dreamtime-stories"
chapter: 3
---

**1** In the Dreaming, the Ancestor Spirits rose from beneath the earth. They had been sleeping for a long time, and when they came to the surface, the world was new and empty.

**2** The Ancestor Spirits took many forms. Some took the form of kangaroos, some of emus, some of lizards, some of snakes. They traveled across the land, singing the world into existence.

**3** As they walked, they sang. And their songs created everything they sang about: the hills, the rivers, the waterholes, the trees, the animals. The world was sung into being.

**4** Then the Ancestor Spirits created the first men and women. They made them from clay, from bark, from feathers, from the elements of the earth.

**5** They taught the first people the law: how to hunt, how to gather, how to find water, how to read the signs of the land. They taught them the ceremonies and the songs.

**6** When their work was done, the Ancestor Spirits returned to the earth. Some became rocks, some became trees, some became waterholes. They are still here, in the land, watching over their descendants.
