---
title: "The Eagle and the Crow"
scripture: "dreamtime-stories"
chapter: 6
---

**1** In the Dreaming, the Eagle and the Crow were men. They were rivals, and they were always competing with each other.

**2** The Eagle was proud and strong. He hunted alone and kept the best food for himself. The Crow was cunning and resourceful. He shared his food with others and made many friends.

**3** One day a great fire swept across the land. The Eagle flew high above the flames, saving only himself. The Crow gathered the people together and led them to safety by the river.

**4** After the fire, the people were hungry. The Eagle had food but would not share. The Crow had little but shared everything he had.

**5** The people said: The Crow is our friend. The Eagle thinks only of himself. And from that time, the Crow was honored as a keeper of the law, for the law says: share what you have, care for your people, put the community before yourself.

**6** The Eagle and the Crow were changed into the birds we see today. The Eagle soars alone in the sky, proud but solitary. The Crow stays close to the people, noisy but loyal.
