Aboriginal Australian astronomical knowledge is among the oldest in the world, with the night sky serving as a vast map of Dreaming narratives. The stars, constellations, planets, and celestial phenomena are understood as Ancestral Beings or as the marks of their actions. The Pleiades are often associated with a group of women (the Seven Sisters, known as Kungkarungkara in Pitjantjatjara tradition) pursued across the sky. The dark spaces between stars (dark constellations, such as the Emu in the Sky) are as significant as the bright stars themselves.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Sky Ancestors and Celestial Dreaming carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
