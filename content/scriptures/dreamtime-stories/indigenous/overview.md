## Overview

The Dreaming is not a subject of study for Aboriginal Australians but the living reality within which all existence takes place. Each Aboriginal person is connected to specific Dreaming narratives through their birth site, their totemic affiliations, and their family and language group. These connections carry obligations — the responsibility to maintain sacred sites, perform ceremonies, pass on knowledge, and care for country according to the laws established by the Ancestral Beings.

Indigenous engagement with the Dreaming emphasizes its inseparability from country (land), language, and ceremony. The Dreaming is not a set of stories to be read or analyzed but a living spiritual reality that is maintained through the ongoing practice of ceremony, art, song, and the proper custodianship of sacred places. The appropriation and misrepresentation of Dreaming narratives by non-Indigenous people is a significant concern for Aboriginal communities.
