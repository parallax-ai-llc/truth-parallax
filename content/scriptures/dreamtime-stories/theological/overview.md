## Overview

Theological engagement with Aboriginal Australian Dreaming traditions raises fundamental questions about the nature of time, creation, and the relationship between the sacred and the material world. The Dreaming's non-linear temporality — in which the creative era is simultaneously past, present, and future — challenges linear conceptions of sacred history found in many other traditions and offers a distinctive theology of immanence in which the divine is permanently present within the landscape.

The concept that law and morality are embedded in the landscape itself — created by the Ancestors and maintained through ceremony — presents a theology of natural law quite different from both Western natural law theory and divine command ethics. The totemic system's assertion of kinship between humans and other species offers a theology of creation in which all beings share in the sacred nature of existence.
