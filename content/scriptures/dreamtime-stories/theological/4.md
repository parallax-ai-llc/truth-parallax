Aboriginal Australian astronomical knowledge is among the oldest in the world, with the night sky serving as a vast map of Dreaming narratives. The stars, constellations, planets, and celestial phenomena are understood as Ancestral Beings or as the marks of their actions. The Pleiades are often associated with a group of women (the Seven Sisters, known as Kungkarungkara in Pitjantjatjara tradition) pursued across the sky. The dark spaces between stars (dark constellations, such as the Emu in the Sky) are as significant as the bright stars themselves.

Theological engagement with The Sky Ancestors and Celestial Dreaming raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order.
