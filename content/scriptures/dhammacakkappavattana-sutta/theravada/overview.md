## Overview

The Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta is the doctrinal cornerstone of Theravada Buddhism. As the Buddha's first discourse, it establishes the fundamental framework — the Middle Way and the Four Noble Truths — upon which the entire Theravada system is built. It is chanted annually on Asalha Puja, the holiday commemorating the first sermon.

## Doctrinal Foundation

Every aspect of Theravada teaching and practice can be traced back to this sutta. The Four Noble Truths provide the diagnostic framework: suffering is identified, its cause is diagnosed, its cessation is affirmed, and the path to cessation is prescribed. The Noble Eightfold Path elaborated here becomes the comprehensive guide to ethical conduct, mental discipline, and wisdom.

## The Three Turnings

The sutta's presentation of each truth through three turnings — knowledge (sacca-nana), task (kicca-nana), and accomplishment (kata-nana) — is particularly emphasized in Theravada exegesis. This structure shows that intellectual understanding alone is insufficient; each truth must be practiced and realized through direct experience.
