In Tibetan Buddhist education, the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta provides the foundation upon which the entire graduated path (lam rim) is built. The Four Noble Truths are studied in detail through the lens of the sixteen aspects — four aspects for each truth — which provide a systematic framework for analytical meditation. For the truth of suffering: impermanence, suffering, emptiness, and selflessness. For the truth of origin: cause, origin, production, and condition. For the truth of cessation: cessation, pacification, excellence, and definite emergence. For the truth of the path: path, suitability, achievement, and deliverance. This sixteen-fold analysis, drawn from the Abhidharmakosha and elaborated by Tibetan commentators, transforms the sutta's concise teaching into an extensive curriculum of contemplation. The Tibetan approach exemplifies its characteristic method of taking a foundational teaching and developing it into a comprehensive system of study and practice.
