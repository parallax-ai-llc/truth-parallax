The historicity and textual development of the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta have been debated by scholars for over a century. The question of whether the Four Noble Truths represent the Buddha's actual first teaching or a later systematization remains unresolved. K.R. Norman argued that the sutta's formulaic structure suggests later composition, while Tilmann Vetter proposed that the earliest teaching emphasized meditation practice rather than doctrinal formulations. More recently, Rupert Gethin and Alexander Wynne have defended the essential historicity of the Four Noble Truths as an early teaching. Comparative study of the Pali, Sanskrit, and Chinese versions reveals a remarkably stable core text with variations in framing, suggesting careful oral transmission from an early period. The sutta's placement at Sarnath (Isipatana) has been confirmed by archaeological evidence of an early Buddhist site, though the relationship between the textual account and historical events remains a matter of scholarly interpretation.
