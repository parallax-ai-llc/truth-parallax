## Overview

Mahayana Buddhism honors the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta as the First Turning of the Dharma Wheel while situating it within a broader hermeneutical framework. According to the Mahayana schema of three turnings, this sutta represents the Buddha's initial, foundational teaching — true and essential, but later supplemented by the deeper teachings of emptiness (Second Turning) and Buddha-nature (Third Turning).

## The Three Turnings Framework

In this framework, the Four Noble Truths are not superseded but deepened. The Second Turning (Prajnaparamita literature) reveals that the truths themselves are empty of inherent existence. The Third Turning (Yogacara and tathagatagarbha texts) reveals the luminous nature of mind underlying all experience. Each turning builds upon rather than negating the previous one.

## Skillful Means

Mahayana interpreters understand the First Turning as the Buddha's skillful adaptation of his teaching to the capacity of his initial audience — the five ascetics who had practiced severe austerity. The Middle Way teaching was precisely what they needed to hear, demonstrating the Buddha's upaya (skillful means) from the very beginning of his teaching career.
