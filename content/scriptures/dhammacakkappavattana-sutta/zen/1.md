Zen's engagement with the first sermon focuses not on its doctrinal content but on the moment of transmission. When Kondanna 'saw' the truth — 'Whatever is subject to arising is subject to cessation' — Zen recognizes this as the first instance of direct, mind-to-mind realization that defines the Zen lineage. A Zen master might ask: 'Before the Buddha opened his mouth at Deer Park, what was the first sermon?' This question points beyond words to the living reality that the sermon indicates. The Middle Way, in Zen understanding, is not a philosophical position between two extremes but the living reality of each moment, which is neither this nor that, neither existing nor not-existing. The Four Noble Truths are not doctrines to be believed but realities to be directly experienced — and in Zen, that experience is available right now, not at the end of a long path of study.
