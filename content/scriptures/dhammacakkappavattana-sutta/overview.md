## Overview

The Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta ("Setting in Motion the Wheel of the Dhamma") is the Buddha's first discourse, delivered at the Deer Park in Isipatana (modern Sarnath) to the five ascetics who had been his former companions. Found in the Samyutta Nikaya (SN 56.11), this sutta establishes the foundational framework of Buddhist teaching: the Middle Way between the extremes of self-indulgence and self-mortification, and the Four Noble Truths.

## Structure

The sutta presents a single, tightly structured discourse:

1. **The Middle Way**: Rejection of the two extremes and establishment of the Noble Eightfold Path
2. **The Four Noble Truths**: Suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path to cessation
3. **Three Turnings and Twelve Aspects**: Each truth understood through knowledge, practice, and realization
4. **Kondanna's Attainment**: The elder Kondanna realizes the Dhamma — "Whatever is subject to arising is subject to cessation"

## Cross-Traditional Significance

As the Buddha's inaugural teaching, this sutta is honored across every Buddhist tradition:

- **Theravada**: The doctrinal cornerstone, chanted on Asalha Puja day commemorating the first sermon
- **Mahayana**: Recognized as the "First Turning of the Wheel," later supplemented by the Second and Third Turnings
- **Zen**: The moment of first transmission from Buddha to disciple, the origin of the lineage
- **Tibetan**: The foundation upon which the progressive turnings of the wheel are built

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Middle Way | The path between sensual indulgence and severe asceticism |
| Four Noble Truths | The diagnostic framework of suffering, origin, cessation, and path |
| Noble Eightfold Path | Right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration |
| Three Turnings | Each truth approached through knowledge, task, and accomplishment |
| First Transmission | The inaugurating moment of the Buddhist teaching lineage |
