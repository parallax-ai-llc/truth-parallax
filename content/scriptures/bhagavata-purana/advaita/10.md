## Overview

The tenth Skandha presents the greatest challenge and opportunity for Advaita interpretation. The intensely personal, embodied, emotional devotion described here — particularly in the rasa lila — seems to resist non-dual reading. Yet Sridhara Swami and later Advaitins like Madhusudana Sarasvati developed powerful interpretations that embrace the devotional intensity while pointing it toward non-dual realization.

Sridhara Swami reads the rasa lila as the supreme illustration of the identity of the individual self with Brahman. The gopis' absorption in Krishna represents the aspirant's complete dissolution of individual identity in Brahman-consciousness. The multiplication of Krishna to dance with each gopi simultaneously illustrates Brahman's capacity to be fully present as each individual self without being divided or diminished.

Madhusudana Sarasvati went further, arguing that the bhakti described in the tenth Skandha — particularly the gopis' prema — is not merely a means to non-dual knowledge but is itself the highest expression of non-dual realization. When the devotee is so completely absorbed in God that no sense of separation remains, this is functionally identical with the non-dual state.

## Key Themes

- **Absorption as Non-duality**: The gopis' complete absorption in Krishna as functional non-dual realization
- **Multiplication as Unity**: Krishna's simultaneous presence with all gopis illustrating Brahman's non-dual omnipresence
- **Prema as Jnana**: The highest love as identical with the highest knowledge
- **Madhusudana's Synthesis**: Bhakti-jnana identity at the highest level
- **Lila as Maya**: Krishna's play as the creative activity of Brahman through maya

## Key Verses and Passages

### The Rasa Lila — Advaita Reading

Sridhara Swami: Krishna multiplying himself without being divided illustrates Brahman's nature — the one reality fully present as the many without ceasing to be one. The gopis' loss of individual identity in the dance is the Advaitic dissolution of the ego.

### Madhusudana's Bhakti-Jnana Identity

Madhusudana Sarasvati's radical position: at the highest level, prema and jnana are identical. The gopis' total absorption in Krishna IS non-dual realization, expressed in devotional rather than philosophical language.

## Commentary Tradition

The tenth Skandha generated one of the most creative developments in Advaita history: Madhusudana Sarasvati's synthesis of supreme bhakti and non-dual knowledge, which influenced all subsequent Advaita-bhakti discussions.
