## Overview

Advaita Vedanta reads the Prahlada narrative as a dramatic illustration of key Advaitic principles. Prahlada's declaration that Vishnu is omnipresent — present in the pillar, in every atom, everywhere — is essentially the Advaitic teaching that Brahman pervades all reality. When Narasimha emerges from the pillar, it confirms what Prahlada already knows: the divine is not limited to any location but is the very substance of all existence.

Sridhara Swami reads Prahlada as a model Advaitin in devotional guise. Prahlada's equanimity in the face of persecution reflects the sthitaprajna (one of steady wisdom) described in the Bhagavad Gita, and his insistence on Vishnu's omnipresence is a devotional expression of the Advaitic truth that everything is Brahman.

The household dharma teachings are read by Advaitins as valid conventional duties that purify the mind and prepare the householder for the higher realization of non-dual Brahman.

## Key Themes

- **Omnipresence as Advaita**: Prahlada's teaching of divine omnipresence as expressing non-dual truth
- **Equanimity**: Prahlada's composure as the sthitaprajna — established in wisdom
- **God in the Pillar**: Brahman present in all things, including inanimate matter
- **Devotional Advaita**: Prahlada as a model of non-dual realization expressed through devotion
- **Conventional Ethics**: Grihastha dharma as preparatory practice for higher realization

## Key Verses and Passages

### Omnipresence and Non-duality

Sridhara Swami reads Prahlada's insistence that Vishnu is everywhere as equivalent to the Upanishadic teaching 'sarvam khalv idam brahma' — everything is indeed Brahman. Prahlada's devotional language expresses the same truth that Advaita states philosophically.

### The Pillar as Brahman

The fact that God emerges from a pillar demonstrates that Brahman is not separate from matter but is the very reality of all material existence — a point central to Advaita ontology.

## Commentary Tradition

Advaitin commentary on the Prahlada narrative emphasizes its philosophical content while appreciating its devotional power. Sridhara Swami's treatment is considered a masterpiece of Advaita-bhakti synthesis.
