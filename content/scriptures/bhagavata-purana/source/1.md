---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. I meditate upon the Supreme Truth, from whom the creation, sustenance, and dissolution of this universe proceed, who is ever conscious and eternal, and who is the cause of all causes.

**2** That Supreme Being, by whom this entire universe was created and in whom it exists, who is beyond the three modes of material nature and yet is the source of all — to Him I bow.

**3** In the beginning, the Supreme Lord alone existed. There was nothing else, neither gross nor subtle, neither manifest nor unmanifest. That which existed then is He who is the seer, the seen, and the act of seeing.

**4** He, the infallible Lord, projected the material universe through His external energy, and He enters into it as the Supersoul. Though appearing to be affected by the qualities of matter, He remains unchangeable.

**5** The sages, having satisfied the Lord by their devotion, received from Him this knowledge, which is the ripe fruit of the Vedic tree, full of nectar and without husk.

**6** This Bhagavata is as brilliant as the sun. It has arisen just after the departure of Lord Krishna to His own abode, accompanied by religion, knowledge, and all that is auspicious. Those who have lost their vision in the dense darkness of this age of Kali shall get light from this Purana.

**7** Let me offer my respectful obeisances unto the sage Vyasa, the incarnation of Vasudeva, who compiled this Purana for the welfare of all people.

**8** By hearing this Purana, devotion to the Supreme Lord arises in the heart, and by that devotion, detachment from worldly things and true knowledge manifest themselves.

**9** This beautiful Bhagavatam, compiled by the great sage Vyasa, is sufficient in itself for the realization of God. What need is there of any other scripture?

**10** As soon as one attentively and submissively hears the message of the Bhagavatam, the Supreme Lord becomes established within the heart.
