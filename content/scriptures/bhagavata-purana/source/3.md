---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Shukadeva said: In the beginning of creation, the Lord first expanded Himself in the universal form of the Purusha incarnation, and all the elements of material creation were manifested from Him.

**2** A part of the Purusha lies down within the water of the universe, from the navel lake of whose body sprouts a lotus stem, and from the lotus, Brahma, the master of all engineers in the universe, becomes manifest.

**3** It is believed that all the universal planetary systems are situated on the extensive body of the Purusha, but He has nothing to do with the created material ingredients.

**4** The devotees, with their perfect eyes, see the transcendental form of the Purusha, who has thousands of legs, thighs, arms, and faces — all extraordinary. In that body there are thousands of heads, ears, eyes, and noses, all decorated with thousands of helmets and glowing earrings.

**5** From that Purusha, the universal form of the Lord, all the demigods, along with their diverse energies, manifest. They manifest the material ingredients, and the material experience of the living entity appears through the interaction of the three modes of nature.

**6** Thus the Lord of the universe, having expanded Himself into the universal form, created the beings, and the beings so created worship Him by means of sacrifices, with a desire for heavenly pleasures.

**7** In every millennium, the Supreme Lord assumes His incarnation of different forms and activities for the protection of the faithful and the annihilation of the unfaithful.

**8** In the first incarnation, the Lord assumed the form of the four Kumaras, and in the practice of penance they illuminated the path of realization from the very beginning.

**9** In the second incarnation, the Lord assumed the form of the boar, and for the welfare of the earth He lifted the earth from the nether regions of the universe.

**10** In the third incarnation, the Lord as the divine sage Narada gave the Satvata-tantra, which shows the path of worship by which one may directly perceive the Supreme Lord.

**11** In the fourth incarnation, the Lord became the twin-born sons Nara and Narayana, and by severe penance they practised control of the senses.

**12** In the fifth incarnation, the Lord appeared as Kapila, the foremost among perfected beings, and gave an exposition of the elements of creation to the sage Asuri.
