---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 12"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Suta Goswami said: Thus I have recounted to you the essence of all the Puranas, the beautiful Bhagavatam, which is the ripe fruit of the Vedic wish-fulfilling tree.

**2** In the age of Kali, when the good qualities of men diminish day by day, when truthfulness, cleanliness, mercy, and austerity are almost entirely destroyed, there is one great remedy — the chanting of the holy names of the Lord.

**3** Whatever result was obtained in the age of Satya by meditating on Vishnu, in the age of Treta by performing sacrifices, and in the age of Dvapara by serving the Lord's lotus feet — that same result is obtained in the age of Kali simply by chanting the names of the Lord.

**4** This age of Kali is an ocean of faults, but it has one great virtue: simply by chanting the names of Krishna, one is freed from material bondage and attains the supreme destination.

**5** The Bhagavatam is the essence of all Vedanta philosophy. He who has tasted its sweetness has no attraction for anything else.

**6** This Bhagavatam is the very cream of the Vedas, churned from the ocean of Vedic knowledge. He who drinks this nectar of the Bhagavatam through the ear never thirsts for anything else.

**7** The Supreme Lord is the protector of the surrendered souls. He is the supreme controller. He is the supreme friend. He is the source of all incarnations. He is the Lord of all lords.

**8** This most auspicious narrative of the Bhagavatam, when regularly heard and chanted, purifies the heart and bestows devotion to the Supreme Lord, which is the highest goal of life.

**9** He who recites this Bhagavatam with devotion at the time of the conjunction of the sun, or at the time of the eclipse, or at the time of the full moon, or on the eleventh day of the lunar fortnight, becomes free from all sins.

**10** Let me offer my obeisances unto the Supreme Lord, unto the sages who have transmitted this knowledge, unto the devotees who have preserved it, and unto the Bhagavatam itself, which is the literary incarnation of God. By its mercy, may all beings attain liberation.
