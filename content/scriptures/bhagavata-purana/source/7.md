---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 7"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Prahlada, the great devotee of the Lord, though born in a family of demons, from his very childhood devoted himself wholeheartedly to the Supreme Lord Vishnu.

**2** Prahlada said: One can learn the supreme art of devotional service when one is not too much attached to material things, is not too much afflicted by material miseries, and when one associates with great devotees of the Lord.

**3** Hearing and chanting about the transcendental name, form, qualities, paraphernalia, and pastimes of Lord Vishnu, remembering them, serving the lotus feet of the Lord, offering worship with sixteen types of articles, offering prayers, becoming a servant, considering the Lord one's best friend, and surrendering everything unto Him — these nine processes are the purest forms of devotional service.

**4** One who has dedicated his life to the service of the Lord through these nine methods of devotional service should be understood to be the most learned person, for he has acquired complete knowledge.

**5** My dear friends, O sons of the demons, the Supreme Lord is equally disposed to every living entity. No one is His enemy, and no one is His dear friend. But He acts as friend to those who approach Him with devotion, as fire manifests its warmth to those who approach near.

**6** Persons who are strongly entrapped by the consciousness of enjoying material life, and who have therefore accepted as their leader or guru a similar blind man attached to external sense objects, cannot understand that the goal of life is the Supreme Lord.

**7** For this reason, O best of the asuras, one should from the very beginning of life, from the tender age of childhood, engage in devotional service, for human life is most rarely obtained and though temporary, gives the opportunity to reach the highest perfection.

**8** The human form of life affords one a chance to return home, back to Godhead. Therefore, every living entity, especially one in the human form of life, must engage in devotional service.

**9** A pure devotee of the Lord does not pray to the Lord for liberation from this material bondage, for he is automatically liberated by the Lord's mercy. He does not pray for material profit, for he knows that the Lord provides all necessities.

**10** A devotee prays only for the Lord's service and the welfare of all living entities. Such a devotee is truly dear to the Lord.
