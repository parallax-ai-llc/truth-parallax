---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 11"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 11
---

**1** The Supreme Lord said: O Uddhava, neither Yoga, nor Sankhya philosophy, nor righteousness, nor study of the Vedas, nor penance, nor renunciation can win Me as devotion alone can.

**2** I am attained only by unflinching devotional service. Devotion to Me purifies even the lowliest of beings, for devotion is beyond all considerations of birth or station.

**3** The characteristics of pure devotion: it is free from all material desires, free from the contamination of speculative knowledge and fruitive activity, and it is favourably disposed toward the Lord.

**4** Through devotion alone, the devotee comes to know Me — what and who I am in truth. Then, knowing Me in truth, he immediately enters into My nature.

**5** O Uddhava, from this world I have accepted twenty-four teachers. From each I have learned a lesson that has deepened My understanding.

**6** From the earth I learned patience and steadfastness; from the air I learned detachment; from the sky I learned that the Self is all-pervading and untouched by material contact.

**7** From water I learned the quality of purity; from fire I learned that the wise man, though accepting objects of the senses, is not contaminated, as fire is not contaminated by what it burns.

**8** From the moon I learned that the soul is always complete, though the body waxes and wanes like the lunar phases. From the sun I learned that the Self, though reflected in various objects, remains one.

**9** From the pigeon I learned that excessive attachment leads to misery. From the python I learned to be content with whatever food comes by its own accord.

**10** From the ocean I learned to be deep, grave, and unshakeable. From the moth I learned that the uncontrolled senses bring about one's destruction when they become attracted to the fire of sense objects.

**11** From the bee I learned to take the essence from different sources. From the elephant I learned the danger of being captivated by the touch of sense objects.

**12** Thus, from the example of each of these teachers, a wise person should learn discrimination and detachment, and should devote himself wholly to the Supreme Lord.
