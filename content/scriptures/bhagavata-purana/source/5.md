---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Vyasa said: Thus being instructed by the great sage Narada, I sat down in meditation upon the Supreme Lord at my hermitage on the banks of the Sarasvati.

**2** As I was thus deeply absorbed in meditation with a mind that was perfectly fixed, the Supreme Lord appeared before my inner vision.

**3** I saw the Lord in His full personal feature, along with His external energy, which stood before Him like a shadow.

**4** I also saw the conditioned souls suffering threefold miseries due to their being forgetful of the relationship with the Lord, and the remedy lay only in devotional service.

**5** Thus I began to compose the great narration of the Bhagavatam for the benefit of the people at large, as instructed by my spiritual master.

**6** Simply by giving aural reception to this Vedic literature, the feeling of devotion to Lord Krishna sprouts up at once to extinguish the fire of lamentation, illusion, and fearfulness.

**7** When the Lord in the form of a child assumed the charge of creation from Brahma, He breathed life into the dead body of the material creation.

**8** It is the Lord who maintains the universe by His potencies. He creates them, maintains them, and annihilates them at will.

**9** He who has thus heard this narration of the Bhagavatam from the very beginning, from the student of Vyasa, shall certainly attain the lotus feet of the Lord.

**10** This narration of the Bhagavatam, first spoken by the great sage Shukadeva, was heard by the royal sage Parikshit, as he sat fasting unto death on the bank of the Ganges, surrounded by all the sages of the world.
