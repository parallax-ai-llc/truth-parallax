---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 10"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 10
---

**1** The Supreme Lord Krishna, the son of Vasudeva, appeared in Mathura at midnight during the eighth day of the dark fortnight, when the stars were auspiciously aligned and the heavens rejoiced.

**2** At the time of His birth, the quarters of the horizon became clear and auspicious, the stars shone with lustre, and on the earth all was peace and beauty.

**3** The rivers flowed with clear waters, the lakes were filled with lotuses, the forests were full of beautiful birds and flowers, and a gentle breeze blew carrying the fragrance of sanctity.

**4** The child Krishna grew in Vrindavana among the cowherds, delighting all with His divine play, stealing butter, playing His flute, and enchanting the hearts of all who beheld Him.

**5** When Krishna played upon His flute, the rivers ceased to flow, the clouds provided shade, the deer stood motionless, and the gopis left all their duties to come to Him.

**6** The gopis said: O Krishna, the sound of Thy flute draws us irresistibly to Thee. Our hearts have become Thy dwelling place. What woman in all the three worlds could resist the melody of Thy flute?

**7** Krishna said: Those who worship Me with exclusive devotion, meditating on My transcendental form — to them I carry what they lack and preserve what they have.

**8** Even the Vedas cannot fully describe the glories of the Lord's pastimes in Vrindavana, where He played as a child among the cowherds, where He danced with the gopis in the moonlit nights.

**9** The rasa dance of the Lord with the gopis is the supreme expression of divine love, wherein each gopi felt that the Lord was dancing with her alone, and the Lord multiplied Himself to be with each one.

**10** This transcendental pastime of the Lord, when heard with faith and devotion, purifies the heart of the hearer and awakens divine love for the Supreme Lord.
