---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 9"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The glories of the Supreme Lord are sung throughout the universe by those who have realized the truth of His divine nature through devotion, penance, and surrender.

**2** The Supreme Lord, in His incarnation as the divine Fish, preserved the Vedas at the time of the great deluge, and thus the eternal knowledge was saved for the benefit of future creations.

**3** In His incarnation as the Tortoise, the Lord sustained the great mountain Mandara upon His back during the churning of the ocean of milk, through which the nectar of immortality was obtained.

**4** As the great Boar, He lifted the earth from the depths of the cosmic waters where she had been cast by the demon Hiranyaksha, and He restored her to her proper place.

**5** As Nrisimha, the Man-Lion, the Lord appeared from the pillar to protect His devoted child Prahlada and to vanquish the demon Hiranyakashipu, who could be slain neither by man nor beast, neither by day nor by night.

**6** As the Dwarf Brahmana, Vamana, the Lord approached the mighty King Bali and, with three steps, covered the entire universe, reclaiming it for the gods.

**7** As Parashurama, the Lord rid the earth of tyrannical warriors twenty-one times, restoring righteousness to the world.

**8** As Rama, the ideal king, the Lord demonstrated the path of dharma through His perfect conduct, His devotion to truth, and His unwavering commitment to righteous rule.

**9** As Krishna, the Lord appeared in the fullness of His divine nature, enchanting all beings with His beauty, wisdom, and love, and He spoke the song celestial for the liberation of all.

**10** As Buddha, the Lord appeared to teach compassion toward all living beings and to guide those who had strayed from the path of virtue.

**11** At the end of the age of Kali, the Lord shall appear as Kalki, riding a white horse, blazing like a comet, to destroy the wicked and to restore the age of truth.

**12** He who hears these accounts of the Lord's incarnations with faith is freed from all sins and attains the supreme abode.
