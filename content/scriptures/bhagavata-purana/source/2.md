---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Shukadeva Goswami said: Any person who desires to be free from all miseries must hear about, glorify, and remember the Supreme Lord, who is the Supersoul, the controller, and the saviour from all miseries.

**2** Those who are serious about liberation should render constant service to the Supreme Lord by hearing and chanting about His transcendental name, form, qualities, and pastimes.

**3** The highest perfection of human life, achieved either by complete knowledge of matter and spirit, by practice of mystic powers, or by perfect discharge of duty, is to remember the Supreme Lord at the end of life.

**4** A person who has broader intelligence, whether he be full of material desires, free from material desires, or desiring liberation, must by all means worship the supreme whole, the Supreme Lord.

**5** The great transcendentalists who have realized the Self describe Him as being beyond the three modes of material nature. Such liberated persons qualify themselves to enter the kingdom of God.

**6** The Supreme Lord is the original person. He is the cause of creation, sustenance, and dissolution. He knows everything past, present, and future. He is the master of all energies.

**7** One who has not listened to the messages about the prowess and marvellous acts of the Supreme Lord, which are themselves all-purifying — that person's ears are like serpent-holes, and his tongue is like that of a frog.

**8** The upper portion of the body, though decorated with a silk turban, is only a heavy burden if not bowed down before the Lord. And the hands, though decorated with glittering bangles, are like those of a dead man if not engaged in the service of the Lord.

**9** The eyes which do not look at the symbols of the Supreme Lord are like the eyes on the plumes of a peacock; they are beautiful but cannot see.

**10** One who has not tasted the dust of the feet of the Lord is as good as dead; one who has not experienced the fragrance of the tulasi leaves from the lotus feet of the Lord is as good as a dead body, breathing though he be.
