---
title: "Bhagavata Purana - Chapter 8"
scripture: "bhagavata-purana"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The Supreme Lord said: O Uddhava, a saintly person is merciful and never injures others. Even if others are aggressive, he is tolerant and forgiving toward all living entities.

**2** His strength and meaning of life come from the truth itself, and he is free from all envy and jealousy. His mind is equal in happiness and distress, and he is dedicated to working for the welfare of all living beings.

**3** His intelligence is never bewildered by material desires, and he has controlled his senses. His behaviour is always gentle, clean, and without possessions. He makes no effort for his own livelihood.

**4** He is always satisfied, moderate in eating, sober, grave, and merciful. He is friendly, poetic, expert, and silent.

**5** Taking shelter of Me, such a person does not see anything as separate from Me, for he sees all beings established in Me and Me established in all beings.

**6** One who thus sees Me in all and all in Me — I am never lost to him, nor is he ever lost to Me.

**7** The yogi who, established in oneness, worships Me as dwelling in all beings — however he may live, that yogi lives in Me.

**8** He who sees the equal presence of the Supreme Lord in all beings, the imperishable in the perishable — he truly sees.

**9** Seeing the Lord equally present everywhere, he does not degrade himself by his own mind, and thus he reaches the supreme goal.

**10** Through devotion alone can I be known as I truly am. And knowing Me in truth, he forthwith enters into My nature.
