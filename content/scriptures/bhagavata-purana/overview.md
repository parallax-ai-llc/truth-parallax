## Overview

The Bhagavata Purana (also known as Srimad Bhagavatam) is widely regarded as the most beloved and influential of the eighteen Mahapuranas. Traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa, it is structured as a narration by the sage Shuka to King Parikshit during the last seven days of the king's life. Scholars generally date its composition to between the 6th and 10th centuries CE, with South India as a likely place of origin.

The Bhagavata Purana is distinguished by its literary beauty, philosophical depth, and devotional intensity. It elevates bhakti (devotion) to the status of the supreme spiritual path and presents Krishna not merely as an avatar of Vishnu but as the source of all avatars — Svayam Bhagavan, the Supreme Person himself. Its tenth Skandha, describing Krishna's life and pastimes, has inspired centuries of devotional art, music, poetry, and theology.

## Structure

The text is divided into twelve Skandhas (cantos):

1. **Skandha 1 — Creation and Framework**: The setting, Vyasa's dissatisfaction, and the framework narrative
2. **Skandha 2 — Cosmic Manifestation**: The universal form, cosmic creation, and Bhagavan's nature
3. **Skandha 3 — Status of Devotion**: The dialogue of Vidura and Maitreya, creation accounts, and Kapila's teachings
4. **Skandha 4 — Primary Creation**: Stories of Dhruva, Prithu, and the Pracetas
5. **Skandha 5 — Creative Process**: Rishabha, Bharata, cosmography, and the structure of the universe
6. **Skandha 6 — Prescribed Duties**: Ajamila, the Daksha-Narada dispute, and the battle of Indra and Vritra
7. **Skandha 7 — Science of God**: Prahlada and Narasimha, the ideal householder
8. **Skandha 8 — Withdrawal of Cosmic Creation**: Gajendra, the churning of the ocean, Vamana avatar
9. **Skandha 9 — Liberation**: The dynasties of kings, Rama's story
10. **Skandha 10 — The Summum Bonum**: The complete life of Krishna — birth, childhood, youth, and kingship
11. **Skandha 11 — General History**: The Uddhava Gita and Krishna's final teachings
12. **Skandha 12 — Age of Deterioration**: Kali Yuga, the future, and the Purana's summary

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Bhagavata Purana holds a unique position across Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: The supreme scriptural authority for many Vaishnava schools, especially the Gaudiya and Vallabha traditions
- **Shaivism**: Respected as a Mahapurana, though its Vaishnava theology is reinterpreted
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited by Advaitins for its philosophical passages, with important Advaita commentaries
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Considered the highest Puranic authority, confirming Vishnu's supremacy
- **Academic**: One of the most studied texts in Indian literary and religious studies

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Bhakti | Devotion to Krishna/Vishnu as the supreme spiritual path and ultimate goal |
| Krishna Lila | The divine play of Krishna as the complete revelation of the Absolute |
| Avatara | The descents of the divine, with Krishna as the source of all avatars |
| Cosmology | Elaborate descriptions of cosmic creation, structure, and dissolution |
| Vairagya | Renunciation and detachment as companions to devotion |
