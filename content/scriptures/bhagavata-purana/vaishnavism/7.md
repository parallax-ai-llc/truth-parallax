## Overview

The seventh Skandha centers on the narrative of Prahlada and the Narasimha avatar — one of the most powerful stories in Vaishnava literature. The young Prahlada's unwavering devotion to Vishnu despite his demonic father Hiranyakashipu's persecution demonstrates the absolute supremacy of bhakti over all opposing forces.

For Vaishnavas, the Prahlada narrative encapsulates the entire theology of the Bhagavata in miniature: the devotee who remains faithful to God regardless of circumstances, the futility of opposing the divine will, and God's dramatic intervention to protect his devotee. The emergence of Narasimha (the half-man, half-lion avatar) from a pillar demonstrates Vishnu's omnipresence — he is present even in the most unlikely place.

The Skandha also contains important teachings on householder dharma (grihastha-dharma), providing a practical ethical framework alongside its dramatic devotional narrative. The ideal householder, according to this canto, integrates worldly responsibilities with constant devotion to Vishnu.

## Key Themes

- **Unwavering Devotion**: Prahlada as the supreme model of steadfast bhakti under persecution
- **Narasimha Avatara**: God's creative intervention to fulfill a seemingly impossible promise
- **Omnipresence**: Vishnu present in every atom, including the pillar from which Narasimha emerges
- **Devotion vs. Power**: Bhakti triumphing over material strength and demonic might
- **Grihastha Dharma**: Integrating worldly duties with devotional life

## Key Verses and Passages

### Prahlada's Nine Forms of Bhakti (7.5.23)

Prahlada's enumeration of the nine forms of devotion (shravanam, kirtanam, smaranam...) is the locus classicus for the navavidha bhakti (nine modes of devotion) teaching that structures Vaishnava practice.

### The Emergence of Narasimha (7.8)

Vishnu emerges as Narasimha from a pillar, fulfilling the conditions of Hiranyakashipu's boon (neither inside nor outside, neither day nor night, neither man nor animal) while protecting his devotee. This demonstrates both Vishnu's omnipresence and his creative problem-solving on behalf of devotees.

## Commentary Tradition

The Prahlada-Narasimha narrative has generated an enormous body of Vaishnava commentary, iconography, temple tradition, and devotional literature. Every major Vaishnava commentary tradition gives this canto extensive treatment.
