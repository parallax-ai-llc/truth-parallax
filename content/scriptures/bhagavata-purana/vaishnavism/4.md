## Overview

The fourth Skandha contains some of the most beloved Vaishnava devotional narratives, including the stories of Dhruva and King Prithu. For Vaishnavas, these narratives demonstrate the transformative power of devotion to Vishnu and the principles of dharmic governance under divine sovereignty.

The story of Dhruva — the young prince who undertakes severe austerities to gain Vishnu's favor after being rejected by his father's other wife — is foundational for Vaishnava devotional theology. Dhruva's initial motivation is material (desire for his father's kingdom), but through the process of devotion, his desires are transformed, and he ultimately achieves a vision of Vishnu that transcends all worldly ambition.

King Prithu's story introduces the concept of the ideal Vaishnava ruler — a king who governs as Vishnu's representative, extracting prosperity from the earth through righteous governance rather than exploitation. This narrative establishes important Vaishnava political theology about the relationship between divine authority and earthly kingship.

## Key Themes

- **Transformed Desire**: Dhruva's journey from material ambition to pure devotion
- **Child Devotee**: The child as an exemplary devotee — sincerity overcoming limitations
- **Divine Kingship**: Prithu as the model of governance under Vishnu's sovereignty
- **Devotion and Austerity**: The integration of bhakti and tapas in spiritual practice
- **Grace Transforming Motivation**: Vishnu's grace converting material desire into spiritual aspiration

## Key Verses and Passages

### Dhruva's Vision of Vishnu (4.9)

Vaishnava commentators read Dhruva's divine vision as demonstrating that sincere devotion, even when initially mixed with material motives, leads to the highest spiritual attainment. Dhruva's famous statement — 'I sought broken glass and found a diamond' — encapsulates the Vaishnava teaching that Vishnu gives far more than the devotee seeks.

### Prithu's Governance (4.17-22)

King Prithu's righteous rule is read as establishing the model of Vaishnava political theology: the king rules as Vishnu's representative, and his authority derives from his devotion and dharmic conduct.

## Commentary Tradition

Vaishnava commentators give extensive attention to the Dhruva narrative, which has inspired countless devotional poems, songs, and artistic representations. The story is frequently used in devotional preaching as an accessible example of bhakti's transformative power.
