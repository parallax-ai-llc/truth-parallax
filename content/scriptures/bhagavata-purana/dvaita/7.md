## Overview

The seventh Skandha is of supreme importance for Dvaita Vedanta. Prahlada occupies an exalted position in the Dvaita hierarchy of devotees, and the Narasimha narrative provides powerful evidence for the reality of devotion, the personality of God, and Vishnu's active intervention in the world.

Madhvacharya reads Prahlada as one of the greatest souls in the taratamya — a jiva whose inherent nature (svarupa) inclines him toward Vishnu with absolute certainty, even in the face of demonic opposition. Prahlada's devotion is not merely a choice but an expression of his essential being.

The Narasimha avatar is read as proof of Vishnu's creative sovereignty — he can assume any form to protect his devotees and fulfill his purposes. The creative solution to Hiranyakashipu's boon (appearing as neither man nor animal, neither inside nor outside, etc.) demonstrates that no condition or limitation can constrain Vishnu's will.

## Key Themes

- **Taratamya**: Prahlada's exalted position in the hierarchy of souls
- **Svarupa-yogyata**: Prahlada's inherent devotional nature manifesting despite circumstances
- **Creative Sovereignty**: Narasimha demonstrating Vishnu's limitless creative power
- **Real Protection**: Vishnu's genuine, active intervention to protect his devotees
- **Anti-Advaita Evidence**: The narrative presupposing real devotion, a real God, and real evil

## Key Verses and Passages

### Prahlada's Essential Nature

Madhvacharya reads Prahlada's unshakeable devotion as reflecting his svarupa — his essential nature as an uttama-jiva predisposed to the highest devotion. This is not a product of environment (Prahlada was raised by demons) but of the soul's inherent constitution.

### Real Evil, Real Intervention

The Dvaita reading insists on the reality of Hiranyakashipu's evil, Prahlada's suffering, and Narasimha's intervention. None of these are illusory — the narrative presupposes a real cosmic drama between genuine forces of good and evil.

## Commentary Tradition

The Dvaita tradition's engagement with the Prahlada-Narasimha narrative is among its most extensive and passionate. Madhvacharya's works treat Prahlada as a model for all devotees.
