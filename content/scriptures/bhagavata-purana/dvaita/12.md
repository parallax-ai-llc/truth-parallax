## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta reads the twelfth Skandha as confirming the reality of cosmic time, the genuine decline of dharma in the Kali Yuga, and the sufficiency of nama-sankirtana as the divinely prescribed path for the present age. The Kali Yuga's darkness is real, not symbolic — a genuine deterioration in moral and spiritual conditions caused by the real operation of cosmic time under Vishnu's governance.

Madhvacharya reads the text's identification of itself with Krishna as confirming the Dvaita position that the Bhagavata is the highest scriptural authority — the living literary presence of the Supreme Person. The text's authority derives not from human composition but from its divine origin as Vishnu's self-expression through Vyasa.

The Markandeya narrative's demonstration that the cosmos is periodically dissolved and recreated confirms the Dvaita position on the reality of cosmic processes — real creation, real sustenance, and real dissolution, all under Vishnu's sovereign control.

## Key Themes

- **Real Kali Yuga**: Genuine cosmic decline, not merely symbolic darkness
- **Sufficient Practice**: Nama-sankirtana as truly sufficient for liberation in this age
- **Supreme Scripture**: The Bhagavata as the highest scriptural authority, Krishna in text form
- **Real Cosmic Cycles**: Markandeya witnessing genuine creation and dissolution
- **Vishnu's Governance**: All cosmic events including the Kali Yuga under Vishnu's control

## Key Verses and Passages

### Nama-sankirtana as Sufficient

Madhvacharya and his followers take the Bhagavata's prescription of nama-sankirtana for the Kali Yuga at face value: in this age of diminished capacity, the simple practice of chanting Vishnu's names is genuinely sufficient for liberation — not a lesser practice but the one perfectly suited to present conditions.

### The Bhagavata's Authority

The text's concluding self-identification is read by Dvaitins as establishing its supreme authority among all scriptures — it is not one Purana among eighteen but the literary incarnation of the Supreme Person.

## Commentary Tradition

Dvaita commentary on this final Skandha establishes the practical and theological conclusions of the entire Dvaita reading of the Bhagavata.
