## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta reads the eleventh Skandha's Uddhava Gita as Krishna's comprehensive statement of Dvaita theology — confirming the eternal distinction between God and souls, the reality of devotion, and the supremacy of bhakti as the path to liberation. Where Advaitins read jnana-centered passages as supporting non-dualism, Dvaitins read them as describing knowledge of God's distinct nature and the soul's eternal relationship with him.

Madhvacharya reads the Uddhava Gita as complementary to the Bhagavad Gita, both providing systematic Dvaita teaching from Krishna himself. The fact that Krishna — the Supreme Person — teaches a comprehensive path that culminates in devotion (not in the dissolution of individuality) is read as the strongest possible evidence for the Dvaita position.

The destruction of the Yadavas and Krishna's departure are read as real cosmic events demonstrating that even the highest worldly attainment — living alongside God himself — does not constitute eternal liberation. Only the soul's individual, conscious, eternal devotion to Vishnu constitutes moksha.

## Key Themes

- **Systematic Dvaita Teaching**: The Uddhava Gita as Krishna's comprehensive Dvaita statement
- **Bhakti as Culmination**: All paths reaching their goal in eternal devotion
- **Real Departure**: Krishna's leaving as a genuine cosmic event, not an illusion
- **Eternal Relationship**: Liberation as conscious, personal, permanent devotion
- **Against Dissolution**: The Uddhava Gita confirming that individuality persists in liberation

## Key Verses and Passages

### Bhakti as the Highest Path (11.14)

Madhvacharya reads Krishna's declaration of bhakti's supremacy as the definitive refutation of the Advaita position. If non-dual knowledge were the highest attainment, Krishna would not culminate his teaching with bhakti — the practice presupposes the permanent duality of lover and beloved.

### Knowledge of God's Nature

Dvaita interpreters read the jnana passages not as teaching identity with Brahman but as teaching knowledge about Brahman — understanding Vishnu's nature, qualities, and relationship with the soul.

## Commentary Tradition

Dvaita commentary on the Uddhava Gita is among the most extensive in the Madhva tradition, as this section provides the textual basis for the Dvaita position on the relationship between jnana and bhakti.
