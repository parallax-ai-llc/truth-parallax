Mandala 6, attributed to the Bharadvaja family, contains one of the largest collections of hymns in the Family Books. The Bharadvajas were among the most prominent priestly clans, and their hymns demonstrate a masterful command of Vedic poetic technique. Traditional commentary highlights the Mandala's extensive Agni cycle as a comprehensive theology of the sacrificial fire.

The Mandala also contains important hymns to Pushan, the pastoral deity who guides cattle and protects travelers — a reminder that the Vedic religion was intimately connected to the pastoral economy of the Indo-Aryan people. The Bharadvaja hymns to Indra are among the most vigorous battle hymns in the collection.
