Mandala 9 is unique in the Rig Veda: every hymn is addressed to Soma Pavamana — the sacred Soma juice in the process of being purified through woolen filters for the ritual offering. Traditional understanding treats this Mandala as the liturgical heart of the Soma sacrifice, with each hymn accompanying a specific stage of the pressing and filtering process.

The traditional commentators explain that Soma is simultaneously a plant, a juice, a deity, and a cosmic principle. The hymns describe Soma flowing like a river, bellowing like a bull, and shining like the sun, using a rich tapestry of metaphors to convey the transformative power of the ritual substance. The Pavamana hymns are among the most frequently chanted in Vedic ceremonies today.
