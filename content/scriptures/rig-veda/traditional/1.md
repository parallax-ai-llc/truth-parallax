Mandala 1 opens the Rig Veda with the invocation of Agni, the sacrificial fire, as the foremost deity and priest of the gods. The traditional commentator Sayana explains that Agni is addressed first because he is the mouth of the gods through whom all oblations reach their intended recipients. The hymns of this Mandala, attributed to various rishis including Madhucchandas and Dirghatamas, establish the fundamental themes of the entire collection: praise, petition, and the maintenance of cosmic order through sacrifice.

The later hymns of Mandala 1 include the celebrated Dirghatamas cycle (1.140-164), which contains some of the most enigmatic and philosophically suggestive verses in the Rig Veda, including the declaration 'Ekam sat vipra bahudha vadanti' (1.164.46). Traditional exegesis treats these riddle hymns as encoding deep truths about the sacrificial system and the nature of speech (*vac*) itself.
