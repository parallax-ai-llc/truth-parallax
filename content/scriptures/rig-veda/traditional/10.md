Mandala 10, the final and most philosophically significant book of the Rig Veda, contains the great cosmogonic hymns that have shaped Indian thought for three millennia. The Nasadiya Sukta (10.129) speculates on the origin of existence with radical agnosticism, the Purusha Sukta (10.90) describes creation through the sacrifice of the cosmic person, and the Hiranyagarbha Sukta (10.121) asks 'Who is the god whom we should worship?'

Traditional interpretation treats Mandala 10 as the culmination of Vedic revelation, where the seers' vision reaches beyond the individual deities to contemplate the ultimate source of all existence. The Mandala also contains the marriage hymn of Surya (10.85), the funeral hymns (10.14-18), and the celebrated Vac Sukta (10.125) in which the goddess of Speech declares her own supremacy — all texts of continuing ritual and philosophical importance.
