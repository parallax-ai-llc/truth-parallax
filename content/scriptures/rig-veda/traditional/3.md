Mandala 3 is attributed to the great sage Vishvamitra and contains the most sacred verse in all of Hinduism: the Gayatri Mantra (3.62.10), addressed to Savitr (the solar deity). Traditional understanding holds that this single verse encapsulates the essence of the entire Veda. It is recited daily by millions during the *sandhyavandana* (twilight prayer) and is considered the mother of all mantras.

Beyond the Gayatri, Vishvamitra's Mandala contains important hymns to Agni, Indra, and the Vishvedevas (all-gods). The traditional narrative surrounding Vishvamitra — his rivalry with Vasishtha, his transformation from warrior-king to Brahmin sage — enriches the reading of these hymns as the utterances of a seer who attained his vision through fierce austerity.
