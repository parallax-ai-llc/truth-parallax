Mandala 5, attributed to the Atri family, is characterized by its rich devotional hymns to Agni, Indra, the Maruts, and the twin Ashvins. The Atri seers are traditionally renowned for their piety and their close relationship with the Ashvins, the divine physicians who rescued Atri from a burning pit according to Vedic legend.

Traditional interpretation values this Mandala for its elaborate Agni hymns, which develop the theology of fire as the foundation of all sacrifice. The hymns to the Ashvins are particularly notable for their descriptions of the divine twins' healing miracles and their willingness to aid devotees in distress, themes that resonate with the later devotional emphasis on divine grace.
