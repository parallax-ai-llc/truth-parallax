Mandala 8, primarily attributed to the Kanva family, is distinguished by its stylistic affinities with the Avesta and by its use of unique meters. Traditional interpretation notes the Mandala's extensive Indra hymns and its invocations of the Ashvins, as well as its reference to the mysterious Ashtaka festivals that may reflect seasonal agricultural rites.

The traditional reading emphasizes the Kanvas' reputation as accomplished poets and ritual specialists. This Mandala contains some of the most vivid descriptions of Soma preparation and drinking, portraying the exhilaration of the sacrificial experience and the gods' eager reception of the sacred offering. The hymns to Indra celebrate his strength, generosity, and protection of his worshippers.
