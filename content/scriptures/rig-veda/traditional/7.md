Mandala 7, attributed to the sage Vasishtha, is traditionally regarded as containing some of the most spiritually elevated hymns in the Rig Veda. Vasishtha's hymns to Varuna (7.86-89) explore the relationship between the individual and the divine with a moral depth and emotional intensity unmatched elsewhere in the collection, expressing anguish over sin and longing for divine forgiveness.

The Mandala also contains the Mahamrityunjaya Mantra (7.59.12), addressed to Tryambaka (Rudra-Shiva), which is one of the most widely chanted mantras in contemporary Hinduism. Traditional understanding holds that this verse has the power to conquer death and bestow immortality, and it is recited at funerals, healing ceremonies, and daily worship.
