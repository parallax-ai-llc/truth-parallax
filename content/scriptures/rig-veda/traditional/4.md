Mandala 4 is attributed to the seer Vamadeva, renowned in traditional accounts for his extraordinary spiritual realization while still in the womb. The hymns reflect a deep intimacy with the divine, particularly in the famous Vamadeva hymn (4.26-27) where the sage declares his identity with Manu, Surya, and the gods — a passage later cited in Upanishadic discussions of self-realization.

The Mandala's hymns to Agni emphasize the fire god's role as the intermediary between humans and gods, while its Indra hymns celebrate the god's cosmic victories. Traditional commentators note the theological sophistication of Vamadeva's compositions, which anticipate the Upanishadic insight that the individual self participates in the divine nature.
