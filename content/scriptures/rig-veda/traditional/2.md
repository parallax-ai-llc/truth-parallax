Mandala 2, the shortest of the Family Books, is attributed to the sage Gritsamada and his lineage. Traditional interpretation emphasizes its focused devotion to Agni and Indra, with particularly important hymns to Brihaspati (the lord of sacred speech) that establish the theological connection between ritual utterance and divine power.

The traditional reading highlights Gritsamada's hymns as exemplary models of priestly craft, demonstrating how the skilled poet-priest uses meter, imagery, and invocation to summon the deity's presence and secure the blessings of the sacrifice. The Mandala's hymns to the Maruts (storm gods) are also valued for their vivid poetic imagery.
