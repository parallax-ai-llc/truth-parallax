Mandala 7's Vasishtha hymns to Varuna (7.86-89) are valued in Advaita for their exploration of moral consciousness and the longing for liberation from bondage (*papman*). While Advaitins interpret this bondage primarily as ignorance rather than moral sin, the emotional depth of these hymns — the anguished awareness of separation from the divine — resonates with the Advaitic description of the suffering caused by *avidya*.

The Mandala's theological sophistication, particularly its treatment of *rta* (cosmic order) and the interplay of divine transcendence and immanence, represents a stage of Vedic thought that is already approaching the Upanishadic vision. Advaitins read Vasishtha's quest for Varuna's grace as the seeker's yearning for the direct knowledge of Brahman.
