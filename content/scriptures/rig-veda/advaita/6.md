Advaitins find in Mandala 6's Bharadvaja hymns a developed theology of Agni as the universal principle present in all beings and all natural phenomena. The identification of fire with consciousness, warmth, and life itself supports the Advaitic reading that the Rig Veda's many divine names are aspects of one undifferentiated awareness.

The Mandala's hymns to Pushan, who guides the departed to the realm of the fathers, are read metaphorically as pointing to the inner guide — the *antaryamin* (inner controller) who leads the soul from ignorance to knowledge. The external deity becomes a symbol of the guru principle that is ultimately the self's own higher nature.
