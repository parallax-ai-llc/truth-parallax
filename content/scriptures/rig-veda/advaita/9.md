Mandala 9's Soma Pavamana hymns hold special significance for Advaita Vedanta because the process of Soma purification — pressing, filtering, and clarifying — serves as a powerful metaphor for the purification of consciousness through spiritual practice. The raw Soma plant represents the unrefined mind; the filtered juice represents the purified awareness capable of realizing Brahman.

The hymns' recurrent imagery of Soma as light, as a flowing stream, and as the source of immortality parallels the Advaitic description of Brahman as *sat-chit-ananda* (existence-consciousness-bliss). The Pavamana process — removing impurities to reveal the essence — mirrors the Advaitic method of negating the false (*neti neti*) to reveal the ever-present truth.
