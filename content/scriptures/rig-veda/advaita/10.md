Mandala 10 is the most important section of the Rig Veda for Advaita Vedanta. The Nasadiya Sukta (10.129), with its declaration that before creation 'there was neither existence nor non-existence,' is read as pointing to the non-dual Brahman that transcends all categories. The hymn's concluding agnosticism — 'perhaps even He does not know' — is interpreted not as skepticism but as the recognition that Brahman is beyond the subject-object duality required for conventional knowledge.

The Purusha Sukta (10.90) is read as teaching that the entire universe is the manifestation of one cosmic consciousness, while the Hiranyagarbha Sukta (10.121) and Vac Sukta (10.125) are understood as approaching the non-dual truth from different angles. Advaitins regard Mandala 10 as the ripening of Vedic thought into the philosophical maturity that will flower fully in the Upanishads.
