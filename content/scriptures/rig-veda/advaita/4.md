Vamadeva's declaration of cosmic identity in hymn 4.26 — 'I was Manu, I was Surya' — is one of the most important Rig Vedic passages for Advaita Vedanta. The Aitareya Aranyaka and later Advaitic commentaries cite this as a Vedic precedent for the Upanishadic realization 'Aham Brahmasmi' ('I am Brahman'). Vamadeva's experience represents the direct non-dual realization that the individual self is identical with the cosmic self.

Advaitins read the entire Mandala in light of this peak experience, seeing Vamadeva's hymns to Agni and Indra as the utterances of a liberated sage who perceives all deities as expressions of the one Brahman. The Mandala thus serves as a bridge between the ritual context of Vedic hymns and the knowledge context of the Upanishads.
