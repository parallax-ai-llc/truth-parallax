The Advaita reading of Mandala 2 focuses on the hymns to Brihaspati and Agni as pointing toward the non-dual principle underlying all divine manifestation. Brihaspati, as the lord of sacred speech, represents the power of *shabda Brahman* (Brahman as word) that the Advaita tradition will later develop through its exegesis of the Upanishadic mahavakyas.

Advaitins note that Gritsamada's hymns, while ostensibly praising distinct deities, repeatedly emphasize the unity underlying divine plurality. The imagery of fire pervading all things and the identification of Agni with the cosmic principle prefigure the Upanishadic teaching that Brahman is the inner self of all beings.
