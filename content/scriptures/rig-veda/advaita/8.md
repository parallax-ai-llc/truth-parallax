The Advaita interpretation of Mandala 8 focuses on the Kanva hymns' treatment of Soma as a symbol of the bliss (*ananda*) that characterizes Brahman. The ecstatic descriptions of Soma drinking — the sense of expansion, immortality, and union with the divine — are read as intimations of the joy that accompanies non-dual realization.

The Mandala's extensive Indra hymns are interpreted through the Advaitic lens as descriptions of consciousness conquering the darkness of ignorance. Indra's slaying of Vritra (the cosmic serpent who withholds the waters) symbolizes the breakthrough of knowledge that releases the 'waters' of awareness from the grip of *avidya*.
