The Advaita reading of Mandala 5 highlights passages where the Atri seers point beyond the multiplicity of gods toward an underlying unity. The Mandala's hymns to Agni as the 'face of all the gods' and the inner fire of consciousness support the Advaitic interpretation that the Vedic pantheon is a pedagogical device for approaching the one Brahman through multiple aspects.

The Atri hymns' emphasis on divine light and illumination resonates with the Advaitic concept of Brahman as self-luminous consciousness (*svayam-prakasha*). The seers' petition for 'the light that shines beyond darkness' anticipates the Upanishadic teaching that knowledge of Brahman transcends both ignorance and its opposite.
