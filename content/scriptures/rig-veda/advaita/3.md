For Advaita Vedanta, Mandala 3's supreme contribution is the Gayatri Mantra (3.62.10), which Shankara interprets as a meditation on the supreme light of consciousness (Brahman) manifesting as the solar deity Savitr. The mantra's prayer — 'May that divine light illuminate our intellects' — is read as a petition for the dawn of self-knowledge that dispels the darkness of ignorance (*avidya*).

The Advaitic interpretation transforms the Gayatri from a solar hymn into a meditation on non-dual awareness. The 'light' (*bharga*) of Savitr is ultimately the light of pure consciousness by which all objects are known, and the 'illumination' (*dhiyo yo nah prachodayat*) sought is the liberating insight that the knower, the known, and the process of knowing are one.
