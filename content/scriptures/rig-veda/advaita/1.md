Advaitins approach Mandala 1 as the opening of the Vedic revelation whose ultimate purport is the non-dual Brahman. While the hymns praise multiple deities — Agni, Indra, Varuna — the Advaita reading sees these as provisional names for the one reality. The celebrated verse 1.164.46, 'Ekam sat vipra bahudha vadanti' ('Truth is one; the wise call it by many names'), is the hermeneutical key that unlocks the entire Mandala.

Shankara and later Advaitins interpret the Dirghatamas hymns (1.140-164) as containing encrypted teachings about the nature of consciousness and the limitations of speech. The riddle-like quality of these hymns suggests that ultimate truth cannot be directly stated but only pointed toward — a principle that the Upanishads will develop into the method of *neti neti* ('not this, not this').
