Vaishnavas find in Mandala 4 significant passages that support the theology of divine immanence and grace. Vamadeva's declaration of cosmic identity (4.26-27) is read not as a claim of monistic identity with an impersonal absolute but as the soul's recognition of its eternal relationship with the Supreme Person who indwells all beings.

The Mandala's hymns to Indra are interpreted through the Vaishnava understanding that Indra acts under Vishnu's direction and authority. Indra's heroic deeds are made possible by Vishnu's empowering presence, and the praise offered to Indra ultimately reaches Vishnu as the Supreme Lord whom all the gods serve.
