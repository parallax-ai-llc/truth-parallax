Vaishnavas interpret Mandala 9's Soma Pavamana hymns as celebrations of the Lord's purifying grace that flows into the devotee's heart. The imagery of Soma as a golden stream, a purifier, and the bestower of immortality is read as a description of Vishnu-Narayana's grace (*prasada*) that cleanses the soul and grants liberation.

The elaborate process of Soma purification — pressing, filtering, and offering — symbolizes the devotional process of surrender, purification, and divine acceptance. Just as the raw Soma becomes fit for the gods through the ritual process, the soul becomes fit for Vaikuntha through the purifying discipline of bhakti under the Lord's guidance.
