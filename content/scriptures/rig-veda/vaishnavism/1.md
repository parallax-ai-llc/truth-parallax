Vaishnava interpretation of Mandala 1 highlights the hymns to Vishnu and the theological framework that establishes Vishnu's unique position among the Vedic deities. While Agni and Indra receive more hymns numerically, Vaishnavas point to the qualitative supremacy of Vishnu's role — his three cosmic strides that encompass all of reality and his partnership with Indra that makes the latter's victories possible.

The Mandala's hymn to Vishnu (1.154) is a cornerstone of Vaishnava theology: Vishnu's three strides (*trivikrama*) measure out the earth, atmosphere, and the highest heaven, establishing him as the all-pervading Lord. The 'highest step of Vishnu' (*paramam padam*) is identified with Vaikuntha, the eternal spiritual abode, which 'the wise always behold like an eye extended in heaven.'
