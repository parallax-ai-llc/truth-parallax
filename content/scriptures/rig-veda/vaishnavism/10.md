Mandala 10's Purusha Sukta (10.90) is one of the most important texts in Vaishnava theology. The cosmic Purusha from whose sacrifice the universe emanates is identified with Vishnu-Narayana, and the hymn's declaration that 'three-fourths of the Purusha is in heaven' is read as revealing that the material cosmos is only a fraction of Narayana's infinite reality, the greater portion being his transcendent spiritual realm.

The Hiranyagarbha Sukta (10.121), with its repeated refrain 'Who is the god whom we should worship?', is answered by Vaishnavas with the declaration that it is Narayana, the golden embryo (*hiranyagarbha*) from whom all creation springs. Mandala 10's cosmogonic hymns are thus read as Vedic testimony to the supremacy of Vishnu-Narayana as the one source and sustainer of all existence.
