The Atri hymns of Mandala 5 are read by Vaishnavas as expressions of devotional surrender that prefigure the bhakti tradition. The seers' intimate petitions to the Ashvins, Agni, and Indra — pleading for protection, healing, and blessing — reflect the devotional attitude (*bhava*) that Vaishnavism considers the essence of the soul's relationship with God.

The Mandala's references to divine grace, rescue from danger, and the gods' responsiveness to sincere prayer are seen as early expressions of the theological principle that the Lord responds to genuine devotion. The Atri seers' legendary rescue by the Ashvins models the paradigm of divine intervention on behalf of the faithful.
