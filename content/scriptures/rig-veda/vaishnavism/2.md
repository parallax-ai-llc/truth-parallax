Vaishnavas read Mandala 2's hymns to Agni and Indra as ultimately pointing to Vishnu, the inner controller (*antaryamin*) of all deities. The theological principle that Vishnu empowers the other gods from within allows Vaishnavas to interpret praise of any deity as indirect worship of the Supreme Lord who is their indwelling essence.

The Mandala's hymns to Brihaspati, the lord of sacred speech, are connected to the Vaishnava understanding that the Veda itself is the breath of Narayana. The creative power of the divine word reflects Vishnu's creative sovereignty, and the priestly hymns are ultimately petitions to the Lord who receives all worship through the agency of the subordinate gods.
