Vaishnavas read Vasishtha's hymns to Varuna in Mandala 7 as anticipating the devotional relationship between the soul and the Supreme Lord. Varuna's moral sovereignty, his all-seeing eye, and his power to bind and release are attributes that later Vaishnava theology will fully attribute to Vishnu-Narayana. The anguished longing for divine forgiveness expresses the soul's dependence on grace.

The Mandala's depth of devotional feeling — the sense of personal relationship with a moral, responsive deity — resonates strongly with Vaishnava theology. The seeker's confession of sin, plea for mercy, and trust in divine compassion model the attitude of *prapatti* (self-surrender) that becomes central to Sri Vaishnava soteriology.
