Vaishnava tradition reverences the Gayatri Mantra (3.62.10) while interpreting the 'divine light' of Savitr as the effulgence of Vishnu-Narayana. In the Sri Vaishnava tradition, the Gayatri is recited with the understanding that the supreme light illuminating the intellect is the radiance of the Lord's divine form. Some Vaishnava traditions also employ the Vishnu Gayatri alongside the traditional Savitr Gayatri.

Vishvamitra's hymns in Mandala 3 are read within the broader narrative of Vishnu's cosmic activity. The references to the gods' battles and victories are understood as divine play (*lila*) orchestrated by Vishnu, who enables the devas to triumph over the forces of darkness while himself remaining the supreme, transcendent witness.
