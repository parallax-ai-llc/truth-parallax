Mandala 8 contains several hymns that Vaishnavas cite as evidence of Vishnu's supremacy in the earliest Vedic layer. The Kanva hymns' descriptions of Vishnu's cosmic deeds and his friendship with Indra reinforce the theological point that Vishnu is the supreme power behind all divine activity, willingly supporting and enabling the other gods' functions.

The Mandala's Soma hymns are interpreted through the Vaishnava lens as celebrations of the divine joy (*ananda*) that flows from communion with the Lord. The imagery of Soma as the source of immortality and bliss prefigures the Vaishnava teaching that eternal life is found through devotion to Vishnu, who is himself the ambrosia of immortality (*amrita*).
