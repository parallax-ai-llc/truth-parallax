Mandala 6's Bharadvaja hymns include a celebrated hymn to Vishnu and Indra together (6.69), which Vaishnavas read as demonstrating Vishnu's primacy: while Indra is the warrior, Vishnu is the cosmic sovereign whose power makes Indra's victories possible. The hymn's description of their joint creation and governance of the cosmos is interpreted as Vishnu's supreme lordship exercised through Indra's subordinate agency.

The Bharadvaja family's hymns to various deities are understood within the Vaishnava framework as praise ultimately directed to Narayana, the inner self of all gods. The Mandala's rich devotional language — praise, petition, thanksgiving — establishes the liturgical patterns that will develop into the elaborate worship of Vishnu in temple and home.
