Shaiva interpretation of Mandala 8 finds significance in the Kanva hymns' descriptions of divine power that transcends the conventional pantheon. The hymns' references to a deity who exists beyond and above the other gods are read as intimations of Shiva's transcendent nature as Paramashiva — the consciousness that is the source and ground of all divine manifestation.

The Mandala's vivid Soma hymns are interpreted through the Shaiva lens as descriptions of the inner nectar (*amrita*) that flows when consciousness recognizes its own nature. The ecstatic states described in Soma hymns parallel the *ananda* (bliss) of Shaiva meditation, where the practitioner experiences the intoxicating joy of union with Shiva-consciousness.
