Shaiva interpretation of Mandala 5 draws attention to the Atri hymns' treatment of Rudra and the Maruts. The Maruts, as Rudra's sons and the embodiment of cosmic energy, are understood as manifestations of Shiva's dynamic power (*shakti*) that pervades and animates the natural world. The storm imagery — wind, lightning, rain — symbolizes the activity of consciousness.

The Mandala's devotional tone, with its intimate appeals to the gods for protection and healing, resonates with the Shaiva understanding of the devotee's relationship with the Lord. The Atri seers' acknowledgment of human vulnerability before divine power models the Shaiva virtue of surrender (*sharanagati*) to Shiva's will and grace.
