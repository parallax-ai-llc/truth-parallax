Shaiva tradition reads Vamadeva's cosmic identity declaration (4.26-27) as a statement of Shaiva non-dual recognition: the individual self realizes its identity with Paramashiva, the universal consciousness. Unlike the Advaitic reading that emphasizes impersonal Brahman, the Shaiva interpretation maintains that this realization is of a dynamic, creative consciousness that is Shiva himself.

Kashmir Shaivism finds in Vamadeva's experience the prototype of *pratyabhijna* (recognition) — the sudden awakening to one's identity as the supreme consciousness that has always been present but was obscured by ignorance. The Mandala's hymns to the gods are then read as the liberated sage's celebration of consciousness manifesting as the entire divine pantheon.
