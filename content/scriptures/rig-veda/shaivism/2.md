Mandala 2 contains the celebrated Rudra hymn 2.33, which Shaivas regard as one of the most important theological statements in the Rig Veda. The hymn praises Rudra as the 'strongest of the strong,' the physician who possesses a thousand remedies, and the father of the Maruts. Its request that Rudra's wrath be directed elsewhere while his healing grace be bestowed on the worshipper captures the essential Shaiva experience of the divine.

Shaiva interpretation emphasizes the uniqueness of Rudra among the Vedic deities: unlike Indra or Agni, Rudra stands apart from the other gods, dwelling in the wild and the margins. This liminal quality — Rudra as the outsider god — prefigures Shiva's later identity as the supreme ascetic who dwells beyond the boundaries of conventional religion and society.
