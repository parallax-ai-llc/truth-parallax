Mandala 10's cosmogonic hymns are of great importance to Shaiva philosophy. The Nasadiya Sukta's description of the pre-creation state — 'darkness was hidden by darkness' — is read by Kashmir Shaivism as pointing to the state of Paramashiva before the manifestation of the universe: pure, undifferentiated consciousness, the absolute that precedes and transcends all categories of existence and non-existence.

The Purusha Sukta's description of cosmic creation through sacrifice is interpreted as Shiva's free creative act (*svatantrya*) — the universe emanates from Shiva not through external compulsion but through the overflow of his creative bliss. The Hiranyagarbha Sukta's question 'Who is the god?' is answered by Shaivas with the recognition that it is Shiva-Parameshvara, the golden embryo of consciousness from which all reality unfolds.
