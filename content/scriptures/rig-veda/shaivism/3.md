Shaiva reading of Mandala 3 finds significance in the Vishvamitra hymns' treatment of cosmic creation and the divine fire. Kashmir Shaivism interprets Agni not merely as the sacrificial fire but as the fire of consciousness (*chit-agni*) that is Shiva's own luminous awareness. The Gayatri Mantra's invocation of divine light is read as a meditation on the self-luminous consciousness that is Paramashiva.

The Mandala's references to cosmic battles between gods and demons are interpreted by Shaivas as the dynamic activity (*kriya*) of consciousness overcoming the limitations (*mala*) that obscure the self's true nature. Vishvamitra's own transformation from warrior to seer models the Shaiva path of recognizing one's innate divinity through intense practice and divine grace.
