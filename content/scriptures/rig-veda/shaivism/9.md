Mandala 9's Soma Pavamana hymns are read by Shaivas as describing the purification of consciousness that leads to the recognition of one's identity with Shiva. The elaborate filtering process through which Soma is purified parallels the Shaiva understanding of spiritual practice (*sadhana*) as the removal of the three impurities (*malas*) — anava (individuality), mayiya (differentiation), and karma (action) — that veil the soul's true Shiva-nature.

The imagery of Soma as a golden stream flowing toward the divine resonates with the Shaiva concept of *shaktipat* — the descent of grace that sweeps the aspirant toward liberation. The hymns' ecstatic tone and their vision of Soma as the source of immortality reflect the Shaiva experience of *samavesha* (absorption into Shiva-consciousness).
