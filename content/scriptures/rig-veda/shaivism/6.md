Shaiva reading of Mandala 6 focuses on the Bharadvaja hymns' theology of fire as the universal presence pervading all things. Kashmir Shaivism identifies this universal fire with the *chiti-shakti* (power of consciousness) of Shiva, which manifests as the inner warmth of awareness in every sentient being. Agni's role as intermediary between humans and gods parallels the guru's function in Shaiva tradition.

The Mandala's hymns celebrating divine power in nature — storms, rivers, the dawn — are read as praise of Shiva's cosmic dance (*tandava*) that continuously creates, sustains, and dissolves the universe. The Bharadvaja seers' sensitivity to the sacred in natural phenomena reflects the Shaiva vision of the world as Shiva's self-expression.
