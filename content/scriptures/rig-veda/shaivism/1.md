Shaiva interpretation of Mandala 1 focuses on the hymns to Rudra (especially 1.114), which present the fierce deity who is both destroyer and healer, the lord of storms who is also the bestower of medicines. This dual nature — simultaneously terrifying (*ghora*) and benevolent (*shiva*) — is the defining characteristic of the deity who will develop into the Shiva of later tradition.

The hymn to Rudra at 1.114 establishes the fundamental Shaiva theology: Rudra is the father of the Maruts (storm gods), the lord of healing herbs, the fierce protector whose anger must be averted through praise and offering. Shaivas read this as the earliest revelation of Shiva's nature as the supreme lord who encompasses all opposites within his cosmic personality.
