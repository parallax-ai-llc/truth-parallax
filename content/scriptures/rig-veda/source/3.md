---
title: "Rig Veda - Chapter 3"
scripture: "rig-veda"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Agni I praise, the household priest, the divine minister of sacrifice, the invoker, the greatest bestower of treasure.

**2** The wise have set thee, O Agni, on the head of the earth, resplendent, the giver of food, the master of all.

**3** Come to us, O Agni, thou art the protector of our sacrifice. Seated on the sacred grass, accept our offerings.

**4** O Agni, thou art great and mighty. Through thee we obtain offspring, through thee cattle. Thou art the nearest kinsman of our bodies.

**5** Vishvamitra has composed this hymn for thee, O Agni, the friend of the family. May we, endowed with heroes, kindle thee.

**6** Agni has been kindled as the bright leader of the people, the resplendent one, the strong, who drives away the darkness.

**7** May Agni, the invoker, wisest of all, grant us his worship, for he is the true priest at the sacrificial feasts.

**8** O Agni, thou art the protector of our bodies. As a father protects his children, so protect us. Grant us life, grant us offspring.

**9** We have set our minds upon the fire of Agni. May that Agni, well kindled, help us to cross over all troubles.

**10** O Indra, mightiest of all, thou dost possess strength. Thou art the slayer of Vritra. Thou didst set free the waters to flow, thou didst shatter the cloud.

**11** Thou, Indra, art the destroyer of fortresses. Thou didst slay the dragon that lay upon the mountain. Thou didst release the seven rivers to flow.

**12** Heaven and Earth trembled at the birth of Indra. All the gods feared him. He fills both heaven and earth with his greatness.
