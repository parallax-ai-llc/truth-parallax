---
title: "Rig Veda - Chapter 7"
scripture: "rig-veda"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Varuna, true to holy law, sits down among his people; he, most wise, sits there to govern all. From thence perceiving all wondrous things, he sees what has been done and what remains to be done.

**2** That Varuna, the righteous one, has set the sun in the heavens; he has appointed the path of the rivers. The rivers flow by his command. They never tire, they never cease.

**3** If we have sinned against the man who loves us, have ever wronged a brother, friend, or comrade, the neighbour ever with us, or a stranger, O Varuna, remove from us the trespass.

**4** If we, as gamesters cheat at play, have cheated, done wrong unknowingly or sinned of purpose, cast all these sins away like loosened fetters, and, Varuna, let us be thine own beloved.

**5** King Varuna, whatever sin thy worshipper may have committed, whatever law of thine we as human beings may violate — do not, O God, subject us to thy fatal weapon.

**6** O Varuna, whatever sin we have committed against a heavenly host, whatever friend we have injured by our thoughtlessness — release us from that sin.

**7** Soma, the divine, flows for Indra, for Varuna, for the Maruts, for Vishnu. Drink, O gods, of this pressed Soma.

**8** Vishnu strode wide across the earth. With three great steps he measured out the universe. For the protection of all, Vishnu made three strides.

**9** Of Vishnu now I declare the mighty works, who measured out the earthly regions. Who propped the highest place of congregation, thrice setting down his footstep, widely striding.

**10** For this his mighty deed is Vishnu lauded, like some wild beast, dread, prowling, mountain-roaming. He within whose three wide-extended paces all living creatures have their habitation.

**11** The three great strides of Vishnu — with them he strode over this universe. In these his strides all beings dwell. May Vishnu, the wide-striding, be gracious to us.

**12** May Vishnu, who upholds the three worlds, who made the three strides over heaven and earth and all that lives, grant us his protection and his blessing.
