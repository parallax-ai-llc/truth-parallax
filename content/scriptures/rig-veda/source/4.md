---
title: "Rig Veda - Chapter 4"
scripture: "rig-veda"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Agni, the divine minister, the invoker of the gods, with his red flames, I worship him.

**2** When Agni is kindled, when the oblation is poured, he stretches out his arms. He is the bright one, the purifier.

**3** O Agni, accept this fuel from us. Blaze forth brightly, thou whose flames are bright. Burn up the forces of the godless.

**4** With the offerings of the worshipper, O Agni, accept this prayer. Thou art the carrier of offerings, the best of sacrificers.

**5** With thy resplendent flames, O Agni, and with thy blessed tongue, bring hither the gods to our sacrifice.

**6** The sun rises in the east, the dispeller of darkness, illumining all the quarters of the world with his golden rays.

**7** He moves upward, the mighty, the all-seeing, whose rays support the worlds. The sun fills heaven and earth and the atmosphere with his light.

**8** Seven bay horses yoke the car of Surya. He stretches out his arms of light to illumine all beings.

**9** Surya the god has mounted the bright regions of the sky. Like a warrior, he repels the darkness with his light.

**10** The Dawn comes shining like a lady of light, stirring to pleasant motion every living creature. The fire has been lit for the offering of the morning.

**11** She comes bringing all blessings, lighting all creatures. The Dawn, the daughter of Heaven, has been seen, coming with light to every house.

**12** With the dawn the darkness is driven away. The sun has risen, ascending the great realm of light. Night gives way to the blazing one. We have arrived where men prolong their lives.
