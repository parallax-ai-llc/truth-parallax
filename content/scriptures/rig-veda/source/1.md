---
title: "Rig Veda - Chapter 1"
scripture: "rig-veda"
chapter: 1
---

**1** I magnify Agni, the purohita, the divine ministrant of the sacrifice, the hotri priest, the greatest bestower of treasures.

**2** Agni, worthy to be magnified by the ancient rishis and by the present ones — he will bring the gods hither.

**3** Through Agni may one gain day by day wealth and welfare which is glorious and replete with heroic sons.

**4** O Agni, the sacrifice and ritual which thou encompassest on every side — that indeed goes to the gods.

**5** May Agni, the invoker, the sage, the true, the most glorious, a god, come hither with the gods.

**6** Whatever good thou wilt do for thy worshipper, O Agni — that, Angiras, is indeed thy truth.

**7** To thee, O Agni, we approach day by day, O illuminer of darkness, with our prayer, bringing homage to thee.

**8** The ruler of sacrifices, the guardian of Rita, the shining one, growing in his own abode.

**9** O Agni, be easy of access to us, as a father is to his son. Be with us for our well-being.

**10** We invoke Mitra, of holy strength, and Varuna, who destroys the foe; they who by their power make the rain to fall.

**11** By Rita, Mitra and Varuna, sustainers of Rita, having obtained the power of Rita, have taken their seat.

**12** Great is Indra, who by his power is like a bull. He truly drives all the people onward by his might. He alone rules the wealth of the world.
