---
title: "Rig Veda - Chapter 9"
scripture: "rig-veda"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Flow forth, O Soma, in thy most sweet and exhilarating stream. For Indra, for the Thunderer.

**2** Flow onward, Soma, with thy stream, the ordainer of the world, the guardian of the people.

**3** Purified by the holy men, thou flowest, O Soma, through the fleece. Stream forth to us in a thousand paths.

**4** O Soma, thou art the king. Thou art the lord of strength. Thou art the upholder of the sky.

**5** The Soma juices are pressed for Indra, for Vayu, for Varuna, for the Maruts, for Vishnu. Flow, most divine, to the wooden vessel.

**6** Soma, the divine juice, flows through the filter, golden-hued, beloved of the gods. He settles in the vessels like a hawk upon the tree.

**7** The tawny-coloured flows along the path of Rita, the cosmic order. Soma bestows upon the worshippers cattle, horses, and wealth.

**8** O purifying Soma, bring us wealth. Flow forth, the finder of the light.

**9** This Soma has been pressed for thee, O Indra. Come and drink. Sit upon the sacred grass and accept our offering.

**10** Make us better than we are; make us prosperous. O Soma, inviolable one, be gracious unto us, be kind.

**11** Soma, the divine, the immortal, purified by the sacred stone, poured through the woollen sieve — let him bring us blessings, let him bring us strength.

**12** We have drunk the Soma; we have become immortal; we have gone to the light; we have found the gods. What can hostility do to us now, and what the malice of mortal man, O immortal one?
