Vasishtha's penitential hymns to Varuna (7.86-89) represent one of the earliest expressions of individual moral consciousness before the divine, a theme that resonates across religious traditions. The anguished awareness of sin, the plea for forgiveness, and the trust in divine mercy parallel the Penitential Psalms of the Hebrew Bible (especially Psalm 51), the confessional prayers of Mesopotamian religion, and the Christian tradition of confession and absolution.

The Mahamrityunjaya Mantra (7.59.12), a prayer for deliverance from death, participates in the universal human confrontation with mortality that drives religious expression worldwide. Comparable prayers include the Egyptian Book of the Dead's formulas for safe passage, the Tibetan Book of the Dead's instructions for the dying, and the Christian commendation of the dying (*commendatio animae*).
