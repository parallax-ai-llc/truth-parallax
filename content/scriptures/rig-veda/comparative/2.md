Mandala 2's focused devotion to a small number of deities, particularly the divine priest Brihaspati, invites comparison with priestly hymn traditions in other cultures. The concept of a deity who is himself a priest — mediating between the human and divine realms through sacred speech — parallels the figure of Christ as both priest and sacrifice in Christianity, the role of angels as intermediaries in Islamic tradition, and the divine scribe Thoth in Egyptian religion.

The Mandala's hymns to the Maruts (storm gods) compare with storm deity traditions worldwide: Zeus and his thunderbolts in Greek religion, Thor in Norse mythology, Tlaloc in Aztec religion, and the storm god Hadad in ancient Near Eastern religion. These parallels illuminate the universal human tendency to experience the divine through the awesome power of storms.
