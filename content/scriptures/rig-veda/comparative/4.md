Vamadeva's cosmic identity declaration (4.26-27) — 'I was Manu, I was Surya' — invites comparison with mystical identity claims in other traditions. Al-Hallaj's 'Ana'l-Haqq' ('I am the Truth') in Sufism, Meister Eckhart's declaration of union with the Godhead in Christian mysticism, and the Mahayana Buddhist concept of Buddha-nature in all beings represent parallel experiences of the individual self recognizing its identity with the ultimate reality.

These comparative parallels raise profound questions about the nature of mystical experience: is the claim of divine identity a universal human possibility, or does each tradition's specific context give it a fundamentally different meaning? The cross-cultural pattern suggests a common structure of consciousness, while the theological frameworks differ radically.
