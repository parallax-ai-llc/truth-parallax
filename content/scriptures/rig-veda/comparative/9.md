Mandala 9's Soma hymns participate in a widespread ancient tradition of sacred intoxication and its relationship to divine experience. The Greek cult of Dionysus, the Mesoamerican use of peyote and psilocybin mushrooms, the Amazonian ayahuasca tradition, and the Sufi metaphor of divine wine all reflect the cross-cultural intuition that certain substances or experiences can dissolve the boundary between human and divine consciousness.

The scholarly debate over Soma's botanical identity connects to broader anthropological questions about entheogens (psychoactive substances used in religious contexts). R. Gordon Wasson's work on Soma and subsequent debates have influenced the broader study of psychoactive plants in religion, from Aldous Huxley's mescaline experiments to contemporary research on psilocybin and religious experience.
