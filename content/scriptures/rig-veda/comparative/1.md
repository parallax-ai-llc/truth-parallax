Mandala 1's theological diversity — spanning simple fire invocations, heroic battle hymns, and profound philosophical riddles — invites comparison with the literary range of other ancient scriptural collections. The Dirghatamas hymns' riddling style has parallels in the Havamal of the Norse Edda, the riddle traditions of Anglo-Saxon poetry, and the enigmatic utterances of the Delphic oracle, suggesting a cross-cultural pattern of encoding sacred knowledge in puzzling language.

The declaration 'Truth is one; the wise call it by many names' (1.164.46) has become one of the most widely cited verses in interfaith dialogue. Its theological pluralism compares with similar statements in the Quran ('To each among you We have prescribed a law and a clear way,' 5:48), the Buddhist teaching of *upaya* (skillful means), and the Baha'i principle of progressive revelation.
