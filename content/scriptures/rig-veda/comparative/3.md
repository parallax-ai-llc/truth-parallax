The Gayatri Mantra (3.62.10) — a prayer for divine illumination of the intellect — invites comparison with prayers of enlightenment across traditions. Its focus on inner illumination through divine light parallels the Christian prayer 'Lighten our darkness, Lord' from the Book of Common Prayer, the Buddhist refuge in the 'light of dharma,' and the Quranic description of God as 'the Light of the heavens and the earth' (24:35).

The concept of a single supreme mantra that encapsulates an entire scriptural tradition has parallels in the Islamic *shahada*, the Jewish *Shema*, the Buddhist *Om Mani Padme Hum*, and the Christian Lord's Prayer. Each tradition identifies one formula as containing the essence of its teaching, a practice that the Gayatri Mantra exemplifies in the Hindu context.
