Mandala 5's hymns to the Ashvins, the divine twin healers, participate in a widespread Indo-European tradition of divine twins associated with horses, healing, and dawn. The Greek Dioscuri (Castor and Pollux), the Lithuanian Dieva deli, and the Anglo-Saxon Hengest and Horsa all share structural features with the Ashvins, suggesting a common mythological inheritance from Proto-Indo-European religion.

The Atri hymns' emphasis on divine rescue and healing compares with healing traditions in other scriptures: the psalms of healing in the Hebrew Bible, the healing miracles of Jesus in the Gospels, and the healing sutras in Mahayana Buddhism. The universal human appeal to divine power in times of illness and distress is one of the most cross-culturally consistent features of religious practice.
