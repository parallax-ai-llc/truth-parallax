Mandala 4, attributed to Vamadeva, is studied for its theological sophistication, particularly the self-referential hymns (4.26-27) in which the poet identifies himself with divine and cosmic beings. Scholars debate whether these represent genuine mystical experience, a conventional poetic device, or a ritualized claim to the authority of the ancient seers.

The Mandala's hymns to Agni have been analyzed for their development of fire theology, which scholars trace from simple invocation of the cooking and hearth fire to elaborate identification of Agni with cosmic processes. This theological development parallels and illuminates similar processes in Zoroastrian fire worship, providing evidence for the shared Indo-Iranian religious heritage.
