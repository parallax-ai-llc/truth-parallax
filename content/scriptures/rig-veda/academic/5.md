Mandala 5's Atri hymns are studied for their descriptions of the Ashvins and the medical practices they represent. Scholars have identified references to prosthetic limbs, cataract surgery, and herbal remedies in the Ashvin hymns, providing evidence for the state of medical knowledge in the Vedic period. The Atri family's special connection with the Ashvins may reflect a historical association between this priestly lineage and healing practices.

The Mandala's linguistic features and metrical patterns have been analyzed to establish its relative chronology within the Rig Veda. Scholars place it within the core Family Book stratum, contemporary with Mandalas 2-4 and 6-7, and note its use of archaic grammatical forms that distinguish it from the later Mandalas 1, 8, and 10.
