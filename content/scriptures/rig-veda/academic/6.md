Mandala 6, attributed to the Bharadvaja family, is one of the largest Family Books and provides extensive data for the study of Vedic ritual, society, and poetics. Scholars note the Mandala's detailed references to pastoral life — cattle, horses, chariots — as evidence for the material culture of the Indo-Aryan pastoral-nomadic economy in the Punjab region.

The Bharadvaja hymns' treatment of Pushan, the pastoral deity, has been compared with similar divine shepherd figures in other Indo-European traditions (Hermes, Pan) and with pastoral deities in ancient Near Eastern religion. The Mandala also contains important geographical references that scholars use to reconstruct the Vedic settlement area.
