Mandala 9 is structurally unique in the Rig Veda: it collects all hymns to Soma Pavamana from across the various priestly families into a single book organized by meter. Scholars have debated whether this arrangement is original or the result of a secondary editorial process that extracted Soma hymns from the Family Books. The latter view is now generally accepted.

The identification of the Soma plant remains one of the most debated questions in Vedic studies. R. Gordon Wasson's theory that Soma was the fly agaric mushroom (*Amanita muscaria*) sparked decades of scholarly discussion. Alternative candidates include ephedra, cannabis, and various other plants. The hymns' vivid descriptions of Soma's effects — exhilaration, visions, a sense of immortality — remain central evidence in this ongoing debate.
