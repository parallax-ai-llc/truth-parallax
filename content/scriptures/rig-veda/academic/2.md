Mandala 2 is the shortest of the Family Books and is attributed to the Gritsamada lineage. Scholars note its relatively homogeneous style and its focus on a limited range of deities, primarily Agni, Indra, and Brihaspati. The attribution to a single family supports the theory that the Family Books originated as the liturgical repertoires of specific priestly clans.

Comparative linguists have noted similarities between Mandala 2's Brihaspati hymns and Iranian parallels, suggesting that the cult of the divine priest-poet may have pre-Indo-Iranian roots. The Mandala's hymns provide valuable evidence for the social organization of the priestly class and the competitive dynamics between rival ritual specialists.
