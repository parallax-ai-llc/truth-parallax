Scholars identify Mandala 1 as a later compilation that frames the older Family Books (Mandalas 2-7). Its 191 hymns are attributed to multiple seers, suggesting an editorial process that gathered diverse material into an opening book. The Mandala's range — from simple invocations to the complex riddle hymns of Dirghatamas — reflects the full chronological and stylistic spectrum of Rig Vedic composition.

The Dirghatamas hymns (1.140-164) have attracted particular scholarly attention for their enigmatic, riddling style that may encode astronomical, ritual, or philosophical knowledge. Verse 1.164.46, with its theological pluralism, has been analyzed by scholars such as Max Muller, who used it to support his theory of henotheism — the worship of one god at a time without denying the existence of others.
