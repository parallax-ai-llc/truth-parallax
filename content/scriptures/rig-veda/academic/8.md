Mandala 8 presents distinctive features that have drawn scholarly attention: its use of meters not found in the other Family Books, its stylistic similarities to the Avesta, and its association with the Kanva family, who may have had connections to the western periphery of the Vedic world. Some scholars have argued that parts of Mandala 8 may represent a separate collection that was integrated into the Rig Veda at a relatively late stage.

The Mandala's Valakhilya hymns (8.49-59), an appendix of eleven short hymns, are generally considered a late addition. Linguistic and metrical analysis supports the view that Mandala 8 occupies an intermediate chronological position between the Family Books (2-7) and the clearly later Mandalas 1 and 10.
