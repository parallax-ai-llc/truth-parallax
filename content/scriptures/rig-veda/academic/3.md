Mandala 3 is attributed to Vishvamitra and contains the Gayatri Mantra (3.62.10), which scholars identify as one of the most historically significant verses in Indian civilization due to its continuing daily recitation by millions. Scholarly analysis of the mantra focuses on its meter (the *gayatri* meter is named after this verse), its theology of solar worship, and its function in the *sandhyavandana* ritual.

Scholars have extensively studied the Vishvamitra-Vasishtha rivalry reflected in Mandalas 3 and 7, reading it as evidence of real historical competition between priestly families for royal patronage. The hymns' references to battles (particularly the Battle of the Ten Kings in the related tradition) provide some of the earliest historical data for reconstructing Indo-Aryan political history.
