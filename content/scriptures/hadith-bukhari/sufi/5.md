The Sufi reading of Sahih al-Bukhari, Chapter 5 (Marriage and Family) engages with the prophetic teachings on marriage, family relations, and domestic life. Through the lens of mystical and devotional reading, this section's 22 traditions are examined with attention to the inner dimensions of prophetic guidance and the purification of the heart. These traditions cover marital rights and obligations, the treatment of spouses and children, inheritance, and the ethical framework governing intimate and familial relationships.

Sufi masters have drawn on these traditions to illuminate the stations of the spiritual path and the transformative power of prophetic example. In this chapter, the interplay between specific prophetic directives and broader ethical principles provides rich material for Sufi spiritual hermeneutics. Each hadith is situated within the larger architecture of Sahih al-Bukhari's thematic organization, where its placement illuminates connections to related traditions elsewhere in the collection.

For the spiritual seeker, these traditions are not merely historical reports but living springs of guidance. Sufi practitioners approach each hadith as an invitation to deeper self-knowledge and divine intimacy, finding in the Prophet's words and actions a map of the interior journey. The outward rulings contained in these traditions are honored as the necessary vessel for the inward spiritual reality they contain.
