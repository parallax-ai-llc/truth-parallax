## Overview

Sufi interpreters approach Sahih al-Bukhari with a particular attentiveness to the inner, spiritual dimensions (*batin*) of the prophetic traditions. While accepting the outward legal rulings derived from these hadiths, Sufis seek deeper layers of meaning that illuminate the states of the heart, the stations of spiritual wayfaring, and the transformative power of prophetic love.

## Interpretive Approach

The Sufi reading of al-Bukhari emphasizes hadiths that speak to spiritual realities: the famous Hadith of Gabriel (Jibril), which defines *ihsan* (spiritual excellence) as "worshipping God as though you see Him," is considered foundational to the entire Sufi enterprise. Similarly, hadiths about sincerity of intention (*niyyah*), the remembrance of God (*dhikr*), and the purification of the heart are given special prominence. Sufi commentators like Shah Waliullah al-Dihlawi have drawn on Bukhari's collection to articulate the relationship between outward Sharia compliance and inward spiritual realization.

## Spiritual Themes

Sufi engagement with al-Bukhari highlights several recurring motifs: the Prophet as the perfect human being (*al-insan al-kamil*), whose every action reveals divine wisdom; the importance of love (*mahabbah*) for God and the Prophet as the highest motivation for obedience; and the eschatological hadiths as descriptions of spiritual states accessible in this life through mystical experience, not merely future events.
