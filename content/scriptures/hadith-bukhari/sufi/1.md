For Sufi interpreters, the opening of Sahih al-Bukhari with the hadith on intention is profoundly significant, as it places the inner life of the heart at the very foundation of religious practice. The Sufi reading emphasizes that *niyyah* is not merely a mental formulation before worship but a continuous orientation of the soul toward God. This understanding transforms every act described in the collection — from prayer to commerce — into a potential vehicle for spiritual transformation.

The Hadith of Gabriel, with its culminating definition of *ihsan* as "worshipping God as though you see Him," is regarded by Sufis as the charter for the entire mystical path. This tradition establishes that Islam encompasses not only outward submission and doctrinal belief but a third, innermost dimension of direct spiritual awareness. Sufi masters across the centuries have used this hadith to legitimate the pursuit of experiential knowledge of God alongside the study of law and theology.
