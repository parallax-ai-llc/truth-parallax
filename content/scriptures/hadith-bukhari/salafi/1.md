Salafi scholars approach the Book of Revelation and Faith as establishing the foundational principles of authentic Islamic practice. The hadith on intention is understood as mandating sincerity in all worship, with particular emphasis on ensuring that religious acts conform to the Sunnah rather than to innovated practices. For Salafi methodology, this opening tradition sets the criterion by which all subsequent hadiths — and all Muslim behavior — must be evaluated.

The traditions on the nature of revelation are read as affirming the direct, unmediated character of prophetic communication from God. Salafi interpretation resists allegorical or mystical readings of these accounts, instead emphasizing their historical factuality and their implications for the absolute authority of the prophetic Sunnah. The section's hadiths on the articles of faith are used to establish the Salafi creed, particularly regarding the six pillars of belief and the relationship between faith and works.
