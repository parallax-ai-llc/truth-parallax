The Salafi reading of Sahih al-Bukhari, Chapter 6 (Jihad and Military Expeditions) engages with the ethics of conflict, the conditions for legitimate struggle, and the conduct of military campaigns during the Prophet's lifetime. Through the lens of scriptural purism and manhaj, this section's 30 traditions are examined with attention to strict adherence to the apparent meaning and rejection of innovation. The hadiths in this section address both the spiritual meaning of *jihad* as inner striving and the historical accounts of the Prophet's military expeditions, treaties, and rules of engagement.

Salafi scholars prioritize direct application of these hadiths to practice, seeking to replicate the way of the earliest Muslim generations. In this chapter, the interplay between specific prophetic directives and broader ethical principles provides rich material for Salafi textual literalism. Each hadith is situated within the larger architecture of Sahih al-Bukhari's thematic organization, where its placement illuminates connections to related traditions elsewhere in the collection.

The traditions in this chapter continue to shape living Muslim practice, providing authoritative guidance that believers consult for both personal devotion and communal regulation. Commentators across the centuries have elaborated on these hadiths, producing a layered tradition of interpretation that enriches the contemporary reader's engagement with the prophetic Sunnah.
