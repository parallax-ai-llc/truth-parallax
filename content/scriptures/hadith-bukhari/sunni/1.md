The Book of Revelation and Faith opens Sahih al-Bukhari with the foundational hadith on intention (*niyyah*): "Actions are judged by intentions." Sunni scholars regard this as the most important single hadith in Islam, establishing that the validity of every act of worship depends on the sincerity of the worshipper's purpose. Al-Bukhari's decision to begin his collection with this tradition is itself considered a masterful editorial choice, signaling that the entire enterprise of hadith study must be grounded in pure intention.

This section also contains the Hadith of Gabriel, which defines the three dimensions of the religion: Islam (submission), Iman (faith), and Ihsan (spiritual excellence). For Sunni theology, these traditions establish the basic framework within which all other hadiths are to be understood. The Book of Revelation further includes traditions about how the Prophet received divine communication, providing the epistemic foundation for the entire prophetic Sunnah.
