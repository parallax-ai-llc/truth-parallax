The Sunni reading of Sahih al-Bukhari, Chapter 6 (Jihad and Military Expeditions) engages with the ethics of conflict, the conditions for legitimate struggle, and the conduct of military campaigns during the Prophet's lifetime. Through the lens of legal and theological authority, this section's 30 traditions are examined with attention to jurisprudential derivation and creedal affirmation. The hadiths in this section address both the spiritual meaning of *jihad* as inner striving and the historical accounts of the Prophet's military expeditions, treaties, and rules of engagement.

The four Sunni legal schools have drawn extensively on these traditions to establish normative rulings. In this chapter, the interplay between specific prophetic directives and broader ethical principles provides rich material for orthodox Sunni scholarship. Each hadith is situated within the larger architecture of Sahih al-Bukhari's thematic organization, where its placement illuminates connections to related traditions elsewhere in the collection.

The traditions in this chapter continue to shape living Muslim practice, providing authoritative guidance that believers consult for both personal devotion and communal regulation. Commentators across the centuries have elaborated on these hadiths, producing a layered tradition of interpretation that enriches the contemporary reader's engagement with the prophetic Sunnah.
