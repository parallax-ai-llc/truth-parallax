Shia scholars engage with al-Bukhari's Book of Revelation and Faith with particular attention to traditions that bear on the question of legitimate religious authority after the Prophet. While accepting the hadith on intention as universally applicable, Shia interpreters note that the concept of *niyyah* extends to political and communal matters — including the question of who rightfully leads the Muslim community.

The hadiths on revelation are examined for what they reveal about the nature of prophetic knowledge and its transmission. Shia theology emphasizes that the Imams inherited a special knowledge (*ilm*) from the Prophet, and traditions in this section are read in light of that doctrine. Hadiths about the beginning of revelation to the Prophet Muhammad are also compared with Shia accounts preserved in works such as al-Kafi to identify areas of convergence and divergence.
