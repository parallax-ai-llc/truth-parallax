## Overview

Shia scholarship engages with Sahih al-Bukhari selectively and critically. While acknowledging its significance in the broader Islamic scholarly tradition, Shia scholars apply their own criteria of narrator evaluation, particularly scrutinizing narrators who are considered hostile to Ahl al-Bayt (the Prophet's household) or who participated in political events that Shia tradition views negatively.

## Interpretive Approach

The Shia approach to al-Bukhari's collection involves several distinctive features. First, narrators are assessed not only for memory and integrity but also for their stance toward Ali ibn Abi Talib and the Imams. Second, hadiths that align with Shia theological positions — particularly those affirming the virtues of Ahl al-Bayt — are embraced and often cited to support Shia positions from Sunni sources themselves. Third, hadiths perceived as politically motivated or contradicting Quranic principles are subjected to rigorous critique.

## Points of Engagement

Shia scholars have historically engaged with specific categories of Bukhari's hadiths. Traditions relating to the virtues of the Prophet's companions are examined for possible bias, while hadiths about the Prophet's family — such as the Hadith of the Cloak or the Hadith of Ghadir — are highlighted as evidence supporting the Shia understanding of legitimate succession. This critical engagement has produced a rich scholarly literature of comparative hadith analysis.
