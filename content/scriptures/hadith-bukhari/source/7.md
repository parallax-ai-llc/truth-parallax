---
title: "Sahih al-Bukhari - Chapter 7"
scripture: "hadith-bukhari"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Narrated Aisha: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever introduces something in this matter of ours that is not from it, it will be rejected."

**2** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Leave me as I leave you, for the people who were before you were ruined because of their questioning and their disagreeing with their prophets. If I forbid you to do something, then keep away from it. And if I command you to do something, then do as much of it as you can."

**3** Narrated Jabir: The Prophet, peace be upon him, used to teach his companions to perform the prayer for guidance in all matters, as he used to teach them a chapter from the Quran. He said: "When any of you intends to do something, let him pray two units of voluntary prayer, then say: O Allah, I consult You through Your knowledge, and I seek strength through Your power, and I ask of Your great bounty."

**4** Narrated Anas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Make things easy and do not make them difficult. Give glad tidings and do not drive people away."

**5** Narrated Abu Hurairah: A Bedouin urinated in the mosque. The people rushed to beat him, but the Prophet said: "Leave him alone and pour a bucket of water over it. You have been sent to make things easy and not to make them difficult."

**6** Narrated Aisha: Whenever the Prophet was given a choice between two things, he would choose the easier of the two, as long as it was not sinful. But if it was sinful, he would be the farthest of all people from it.

**7** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Religion is easy. No one overburdens himself in his religion without being overwhelmed by it. So aim for what is right, be moderate, and receive the glad tidings."

**8** Narrated Abu Musa: When the Prophet, peace be upon him, sent any of his companions on a mission, he would say: "Give glad tidings and do not drive people away. Make things easy and do not make things difficult."
