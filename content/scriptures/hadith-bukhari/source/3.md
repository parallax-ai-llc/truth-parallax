---
title: "Sahih al-Bukhari - Chapter 3"
scripture: "hadith-bukhari"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Narrated Anas ibn Malik: While we were sitting with the Prophet in the mosque, a man came riding on a camel. He made his camel kneel down in the mosque, tied its foreleg and then said: "Which of you is Muhammad?" The Prophet was sitting amongst us leaning on his arm. We replied: "This white man reclining on his arm."

**2** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Knowledge will be taken away, ignorance and afflictions will appear, and harj will increase." They asked: "O Messenger of Allah, what is harj?" He said: "Killing, killing."

**3** Narrated Abdullah ibn Mas'ud: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Do not wish to be like anyone except in two cases: a person whom Allah has given wealth and he spends it righteously, and a person whom Allah has given wisdom and he acts according to it and teaches it to others."

**4** Narrated Ibn Umar: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "While I was sleeping, I was given a bowl of milk. I drank from it until I saw the moisture coming out of my nails, and then I gave the rest to Umar ibn al-Khattab." They asked: "What do you interpret this as, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Knowledge."

**5** Narrated Anas: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "From among the signs of the Hour are the following: knowledge will diminish, ignorance will prevail, drinking of intoxicants will be common, and adultery will be widespread."

**6** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever follows a path seeking knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise."

**7** Narrated Muawiyah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When Allah wishes good for someone, He bestows upon him understanding of the religion."

**8** Narrated Abu Shuraih: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "By Allah, he does not believe! By Allah, he does not believe! By Allah, he does not believe!" It was asked: "Who, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "The one whose neighbor does not feel safe from his evil."
