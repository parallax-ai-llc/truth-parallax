---
title: "Sahih al-Bukhari - Chapter 4"
scripture: "hadith-bukhari"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When the call to prayer is made, Satan takes to his heels and passes wind with noise during his flight so that he may not hear the call. When the call is finished, he comes back. And when the iqamah is pronounced, he again takes to his heels."

**2** Narrated Anas ibn Malik: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Perform the ablution properly. Whoever performs ablution well, his sins will come out from his body, even from beneath his nails."

**3** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The prayer of a person who does hadath is not accepted till he performs ablution." A person from Hadramawt asked Abu Hurairah: "What is hadath?" He replied: "Breaking wind silently or audibly."

**4** Narrated Ibn Abbas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, passed by two graves and said: "They are being punished, but not for a great sin. One of them did not guard himself from being soiled with urine, while the other used to go about spreading calumny."

**5** Narrated Aisha: The Prophet, peace be upon him, used to like starting from the right side in performing ablution, combing his hair, and putting on his shoes.

**6** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "If a dog drinks from the vessel of any one of you, let him wash it seven times."

**7** Narrated Anas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, used to perform ablution for every prayer. He was asked: "What did you used to do?" Anas replied: "We used to pray all the prayers with a single ablution unless we broke it."

**8** Narrated Ibn Abbas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, performed ablution by washing his face, then his arms, then he wiped his head, then he washed his feet.
