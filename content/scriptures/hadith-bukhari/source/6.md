---
title: "Sahih al-Bukhari - Chapter 6"
scripture: "hadith-bukhari"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Narrated Anas ibn Malik: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "When you go to relieve yourself, do not face the qiblah nor turn your back to it."

**2** Narrated Abu Ayyub al-Ansari: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When any of you goes to the privy, let him not face the qiblah nor turn his back toward it. Rather, let him face east or west."

**3** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Beware of the two things that bring curses." They asked: "What are the two things that bring curses, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "The one who relieves himself in the pathway of people, or in their shade."

**4** Narrated Anas: Whenever the Prophet went to relieve himself, he used to say: "O Allah, I seek refuge with You from all offensive and wicked things."

**5** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever performs ablution, let him clean his nose well, and whoever uses stones to clean himself, let him use an odd number."

**6** Narrated Aisha: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, used to use his right hand for eating and performing ablution, and his left hand for cleaning himself and other such things.

**7** Narrated Abu Qatadah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When any one of you drinks, let him not breathe into the vessel. And when he goes to relieve himself, let him not touch his private parts with his right hand, and let him not clean himself with his right hand."

**8** Narrated Abdullah ibn Abu Qatadah: His father said: "The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, forbade breathing into the vessel or blowing into it."
