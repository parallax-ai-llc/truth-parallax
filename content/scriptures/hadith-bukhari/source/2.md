---
title: "Sahih al-Bukhari - Chapter 2"
scripture: "hadith-bukhari"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Faith has over sixty branches, and modesty is a branch of faith."

**2** Narrated Abdullah ibn Amr: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "A Muslim is one from whose tongue and hand other Muslims are safe. And an emigrant is one who abandons what Allah has forbidden."

**3** Narrated Anas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself."

**4** Narrated Anas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "There are three qualities, whoever has them will taste the sweetness of faith: that Allah and His Messenger are more beloved to him than anything else; that he loves a person only for the sake of Allah; and that he would hate to return to disbelief just as he would hate to be thrown into the Fire."

**5** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, was asked: "Which deed is best?" He said: "Faith in Allah and His Messenger." It was asked: "Then what?" He said: "Striving in the cause of Allah." It was asked: "Then what?" He said: "An accepted pilgrimage."

**6** Narrated Abdullah ibn Amr: A man asked the Prophet, peace be upon him: "Which aspect of Islam is best?" He said: "That you feed the hungry and extend greetings of peace to those you know and those you do not know."

**7** Narrated Abu Musa: They asked: "O Messenger of Allah, whose Islam is best?" He said: "The one from whose tongue and hand the Muslims are safe."

**8** Narrated Anas: The Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever possesses these three qualities shall taste the sweetness of faith: that Allah and His Messenger are dearer to him than all else; that he loves a person for no reason except for the sake of Allah; and that he would abhor returning to disbelief as he would abhor being cast into the Fire."
