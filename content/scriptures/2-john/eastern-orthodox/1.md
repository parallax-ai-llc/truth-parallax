## The Elder's Witness to Truth

2 John opens with the elder writing "in truth" to the elect lady, establishing truth as the letter's governing theme. Orthodox theology understands truth not as abstract proposition but as a living reality — Christ himself is "the truth" (John 14:6). Walking in truth means living in union with Christ through the Church's faith, worship, and sacramental life.

## Love as Obedience

Verses 5–6 define love as walking according to God's commandments. Orthodox moral theology affirms that love and obedience are inseparable: the commandments are not legalistic requirements but descriptions of the life of love. The person who truly loves God naturally fulfills the commandments, and the person who keeps the commandments grows in love.

## Guarding the Incarnation

Verses 7–11 warn against those who deny Christ's coming in the flesh and instruct the community to refuse them hospitality. The Orthodox tradition, shaped by the Christological controversies of the early centuries, regards the confession of the incarnation as the foundation of all Christian faith. The ecumenical councils exist precisely to define and protect this confession, and 2 John provides the scriptural mandate for such vigilance.
