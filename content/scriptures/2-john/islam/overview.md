## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 John appreciates the letter's insistence that love must be grounded in truth and that false teaching must be resisted even at the cost of social norms like hospitality. The integration of truth and love resonates with the Islamic understanding that genuine piety requires both sincerity of belief and moral integrity in practice. The letter's brevity and practical focus make it accessible for comparative ethical reflection.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Truth as Foundation
2 John's emphasis on "walking in truth" resonates with the Islamic concept of *haqq* (truth, right). The Quran describes God as *Al-Haqq* (The Truth) and demands truthfulness from believers: "O you who have believed, fear Allah and be with those who are true" (9:119). Muslim readers appreciate John's conviction that truth is the necessary foundation of genuine love and community.

### Guarding Against False Teaching
The letter's warning against welcoming those who corrupt the faith parallels the Islamic concern with protecting the community from *bid'ah* (religious innovation) and false teaching. The Prophet Muhammad warned: "Every newly-invented thing is an innovation, and every innovation is going astray" (Muslim). Both traditions recognize the responsibility to maintain the integrity of received teaching.

## Theological Emphases

- **Truth and Love**: The inseparability of truthfulness and genuine care
- **False Teaching**: The community's responsibility to resist doctrinal corruption
- **Hospitality and Discernment**: The balance between welcome and vigilance
- **Moral Integrity**: Living consistently with the truth one professes
