## Truth and Love Together

2 John's integration of truth and love resonates with the Islamic conviction that genuine piety requires both doctrinal integrity (*haqq*) and compassionate conduct (*ihsan*). The Prophet Muhammad embodied this integration: firm in truth yet gentle in manner. Muslim readers appreciate the letter's refusal to separate love from truthfulness, recognizing in this a prophetic principle shared across the Abrahamic traditions.

## Guarding the Community

2 John 7–11, instructing the community to refuse hospitality to false teachers, resonates with the Islamic concern for protecting the *ummah* (community) from corrupting influences. The Prophet Muhammad warned against innovators who introduce falsehood into the religion, and Islamic scholars have always maintained the responsibility to distinguish authentic teaching from deviation (*bid'ah*). Both traditions affirm that the community has a right and duty to protect its doctrinal integrity.

## The Command to Love

Verses 5–6, reaffirming the command to love one another, align with the Islamic emphasis on brotherhood and mutual care within the community of faith. The Prophet Muhammad said: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself" (Bukhari and Muslim). The integration of love and obedience — "this is love, that we walk according to his commandments" — mirrors the Islamic understanding that love for God is expressed through submission to God's will.
