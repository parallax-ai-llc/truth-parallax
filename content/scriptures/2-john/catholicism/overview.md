## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 John emphasizes the letter's insistence that truth and love belong together and its implicit support for doctrinal authority within the Church. The elder's role in defining the boundaries of acceptable teaching and fellowship anticipates the later development of the Magisterium. The letter's warning against welcoming false teachers supports the Catholic conviction that the Church has the responsibility and authority to identify and exclude heretical teaching.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Elder's Authority
The elder writes with an authority that presupposes his responsibility for the community's doctrinal integrity. Catholic interpreters see in this a precedent for the episcopal and magisterial oversight of the faith. The bishop, as successor of the apostles, exercises the same kind of pastoral authority — guarding the flock against false teaching while nurturing it in truth and love.

### Truth and Love in Catholic Theology
Benedict XVI frequently emphasized the inseparability of truth and love, drawing on 2 John's integration of the two. *Caritas in Veritate* (2009) develops this theme: charity without truth degenerates into sentimentality, and truth without charity becomes cold ideology. 2 John provides the scriptural foundation for this synthesis.

## Theological Emphases

- **Doctrinal Authority**: The elder's role as a model of episcopal oversight
- **Truth in Love**: The integration of doctrinal fidelity and genuine charity
- **The Incarnation**: Christ's coming in the flesh as the non-negotiable confession
- **Guarding the Faith**: The community's responsibility to protect the deposit of faith
