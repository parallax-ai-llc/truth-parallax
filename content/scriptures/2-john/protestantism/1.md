## Truth and Love United

2 John opens with the elder expressing love "in truth" for the elect lady and her children. Protestant interpreters emphasize that this is not a mere greeting formula but a theological statement: genuine Christian love is always grounded in truth. The elder rejoices that some of the community's members are "walking in truth" (v. 4), and this observation sets the stage for the letter's practical instruction.

## The Love Command Renewed

Verses 5–6 reaffirm the command to "love one another" — a command that is "not as though I were writing you a new commandment, but the one we have had from the beginning." Protestant interpreters read this as a restatement of the Johannine love ethic, rooted in Christ's own command (John 13:34). Love is defined not by feeling but by obedience: "this is love, that we walk according to his commandments" (v. 6).

## Refusing False Teachers

Verses 7–11 contain the letter's most distinctive teaching: believers must not welcome or even greet those who deny that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh. Protestant interpreters have debated the application of this instruction. Most agree that it applies to formal hospitality that would provide a platform for false teaching, not to basic human courtesy. The principle underscores that doctrinal fidelity is not optional but essential to the health of the church.
