## Overview

The Linga Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, centered on the worship of Shiva in his iconic form as the linga (the abstract, aniconic symbol representing Shiva's cosmic creative power). It is one of the principal Shaiva Puranas and provides extensive material on Shaiva theology, cosmology, ritual, and yoga.

The text is generally dated to the 5th-10th centuries CE and represents a significant source for understanding the development of linga worship and Shaiva devotional theology. The Linga Purana describes the origin and significance of the linga, the mythology of Shiva, and the practices associated with Shaiva worship.

## Structure

The text is divided into two parts:

1. **Purva Bhaga — Earlier Section**: The origin and significance of the linga, Shaiva cosmology, the mythology of Shiva, and the creation narrative
2. **Uttara Bhaga — Later Section**: Shaiva yoga, meditation practices, festivals, pilgrimage, and the paths to liberation through Shiva's grace

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Linga Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: Approached as a sectarian Shaiva text while acknowledging its importance in the broader Puranic corpus
- **Shaivism**: A central text for understanding linga worship, Shaiva cosmology, and Shiva's supremacy
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for passages on Shiva as the formless absolute and the nature of consciousness
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Engaged critically as a Shaiva text, with Dvaita scholars reinterpreting Shiva references as subordinate to Vishnu
- **Academic**: An essential source for the history of linga worship and the development of Shaiva Puranic literature

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Linga Worship | The theological significance and ritual practice of worshipping Shiva through the linga |
| Shiva's Supremacy | Shiva as the supreme reality from whom all creation emerges |
| Shaiva Cosmology | The creation, preservation, and dissolution of the universe through Shiva's five acts |
| Yoga and Liberation | Shaiva yogic practices leading to union with Shiva |
| Mythology of Shiva | The narratives of Shiva's cosmic deeds and divine play |
