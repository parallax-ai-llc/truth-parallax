---
title: "Linga Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "linga-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Suta said: "Now hear, O sages, the further teaching of the Linga Purana, concerning the creation of the universe and the glory of Mahadeva."

**2** From the supreme Shiva arose Shakti, the divine energy. From their union arose the cosmic egg, resplendent with the light of a thousand suns.

**3** From that cosmic egg arose Brahma, the creator. He looked in the four directions and thus arose his four faces. He began the work of creation as ordained by the great Lord.

**4** Brahma created the mind-born sons: Marichi, Atri, Angiras, Pulastya, Pulaha, Kratu, and Vasishtha. These seven became the progenitors of all living beings.

**5** Then Brahma created the eleven Rudras, the eight Vasus, the twelve Adityas, the two Ashvins, and the forty-nine Maruts. All these arose from portions of the great Lord Shiva.

**6** The Lord Shiva, pleased with Brahma's devotion, taught him the path of dharma, saying: "He who follows dharma attains the highest good. He who abandons dharma falls into darkness."

**7** "There are four stages of life: the student, the householder, the forest-dweller, and the renunciant. Each has its own duties, and by fulfilling them one progresses toward liberation."

**8** "The householder should perform the five great sacrifices daily: the sacrifice to Brahman through the study of the Vedas, the sacrifice to the ancestors through offerings of water, the sacrifice to the gods through fire oblations, the sacrifice to all beings through the offering of food, and the sacrifice to guests through hospitality."

**9** "Among all forms of worship, the worship of the linga is supreme. He who worships the linga with pure heart and mind attains the fruit of all sacrifices."

**10** "The linga should be worshipped with bilva leaves, which are most dear to me. With flowers of the dhatura, with sacred ash, and with water from holy rivers."

**11** The sages asked: "O Suta, tell us of the sacred places where the linga is worshipped, and what are the fruits of pilgrimage to those places?"

**12** Suta replied: "There are twelve jyotirlingas, self-manifested lingas of light, in different parts of the land. He who visits these sacred places and worships with devotion attains the supreme abode."

**13** "At Somanatha, the Lord manifests as the lord of the moon. At Mallikarjuna, he dwells on the sacred mountain. At Mahakaleshvara, he presides over time itself."

**14** "At Omkareshvara, he manifests as the sacred syllable. At Kedarnatha, he dwells in the high mountains. At Bhimashankar, he appeared to destroy the demon."

**15** "At Vishwanatha in Kashi, the Lord grants liberation to all who die there. At Tryambakeshvara, he manifests as the three-eyed one."

**16** "At Vaidyanatha, he is the lord of physicians. At Nageshvara, he is the lord of serpents. At Rameshvara, he was worshipped by Rama. At Grishneshvara, he bestows his grace upon all."

**17** "He who visits these twelve sacred places and worships the Lord with devotion, chanting His names and meditating upon His form, is freed from the cycle of birth and death."

**18** Suta continued: "The Lord Shiva then taught the path of yoga, saying: 'By the practice of yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, and samadhi, the yogi attains union with the supreme Self.'"

**19** "He who controls his senses, who is established in meditation, who sees the Self in all beings and all beings in the Self, he is the true yogi. He attains the supreme peace."

**20** "The wise man should abandon attachment to the fruits of action. He should perform his duties without desire, offering all actions to the great Lord. Thus he is freed from bondage."

**21** "At the end of time, when the great dissolution comes, all beings are absorbed into the Lord. The universe dissolves into the five elements, the elements into the subtle principles, and the subtle principles into Prakriti. Prakriti is absorbed into the great Lord."

**22** "Then the Lord alone remains, without a second, the eternal witness, the supreme consciousness, the bliss absolute. He is Shiva, the auspicious one, the lord of all."

**23** "After the dissolution, when the time comes for creation again, the Lord wills it, and the universe comes forth again, like a seed sprouting from the earth."

**24** "Thus the cycle of creation and dissolution continues eternally, under the will of the supreme Lord Shiva. He who knows this is freed from all fear."

**25** Suta said: "Thus have I narrated to you the essence of the Linga Purana. He who hears this with faith and devotion, who recites it and contemplates its meaning, shall attain the highest state. He shall dwell in the abode of Shiva for all eternity. Om Namah Shivaya."
