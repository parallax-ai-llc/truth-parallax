---
title: "Linga Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "linga-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Salutations to the great Lord Shiva, who is the supreme Brahman, the cause of creation, preservation, and dissolution, who is adorned with the crescent moon, and who is the lord of all beings.

**2** The sages, headed by Shaunaka, were performing a great sacrifice in the forest of Naimisha. Having completed the rites, they approached the venerable Suta and said:

**3** "O Suta, thou art learned in all the Puranas and in all the sacred lore. We desire to hear from thee the Linga Purana, which reveals the glory of the great Lord Shiva."

**4** Suta said: "Hear, O sages, I shall narrate unto you the Linga Purana, as it was told by Brahma to the sages of old. This Purana is sacred and destroys all sins."

**5** In the beginning, there was neither existence nor non-existence. There was neither the sky nor the heavens above. What covered all? Where was it? In whose protection? Was there water, the deep unfathomable?

**6** There was neither death nor immortality then. There was no sign of night or day. That One breathed, without breath, by its own power. Other than that, there was nothing beyond.

**7** Darkness was hidden by darkness in the beginning. All this was an indistinguishable sea. That which was covered by the void, that One, was born through the power of austerity.

**8** From that supreme being arose the great linga, the mark of Shiva, self-luminous, eternal, without beginning and without end, the cause of all causes, the seed of all that exists.

**9** The sages asked: "O Suta, what is the linga? How did it manifest? What is its nature and what is its significance? Explain this to us in full."

**10** Suta replied: "The linga is the primal form of Mahadeva. It is the mark of the formless Brahman. It is self-luminous and self-existent. All the worlds arise from it and dissolve back into it."

**11** In the beginning, when all was water, a great pillar of fire arose, blazing with immeasurable radiance. Brahma and Vishnu beheld it and were filled with wonder.

**12** Vishnu assumed the form of a boar and dived downward to find the base of that pillar. Brahma assumed the form of a swan and flew upward to find its summit. But neither could find the end of it.

**13** Then the great Lord Shiva manifested from that pillar and said: "I am the beginning, the middle, and the end. I am Brahman, the supreme reality. This linga is my form, the form of the formless."

**14** Brahma and Vishnu bowed down before the great Lord and praised Him, saying: "Thou art the supreme being. Thou art the lord of all. From thee all this has come forth, and unto thee all shall return."

**15** The Lord said: "He who worships this linga with devotion, with flowers, incense, and sacred offerings, shall attain liberation. He shall be freed from the bonds of birth and death."

**16** Thus the worship of the linga was established. The sages, the gods, and all beings worship the sacred linga, which is the mark of the eternal Shiva, the lord of the universe.

**17** He who hears this account of the origin of the linga with faith and devotion shall be freed from all sins. He shall attain the supreme abode of Shiva.

**18** The linga is worshipped in three forms: the earthen linga, the linga of precious stone, and the self-manifested linga. Each bestows upon the devotee the fruits of righteousness, prosperity, desire, and liberation.

**19** This Purana, known as the Linga Purana, consists of the teachings of the great Lord Shiva regarding dharma, knowledge, detachment, and supreme devotion. It is the essence of all the Vedas.

**20** Suta said: "Thus have I narrated to you the beginning of the Linga Purana. Now hear further the glories of the great Lord, as told by Brahma to the sages."
