## Overview

Religious Taoism receives the Taiping Jing as one of the foundational revealed scriptures of the organized Taoist religion. The text's vision of a coming age of Great Peace, its detailed instructions for ritual practice, healing, and community organization, and its framework of divine communication between heaven and the human world have profoundly shaped Taoist liturgy, social organization, and eschatological expectation from the second century CE to the present.

## Historical Impact

The Taiping Jing's influence on Chinese religious history is immense. Its millenarian vision of Great Peace inspired the Yellow Turban Rebellion of 184 CE, one of the largest religious uprisings in world history. While the rebellion failed, the religious movements it spawned — particularly the Celestial Masters tradition — evolved into the institutional forms of Taoism that endure to this day.

## Liturgical Foundation

The religious tradition draws on the Taiping Jing's teachings on "guarding the one" (shouyi), a meditative practice of maintaining awareness of the unity underlying all apparent multiplicity. This practice, along with the text's healing rites, exorcistic techniques, and protocols for divine communication, continues to inform Taoist ritual and contemplative practice.
