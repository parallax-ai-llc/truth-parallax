Zen interpretation of Section 4, 'Inherited Burden and Merit,' focuses on the contemplative dimensions of the Taiping Jing's teaching, particularly the practice of 'guarding the one' (shouyi) and the cultivation of inner peace as the foundation for all transformation — personal, social, and cosmic. While the text's millenarian and political dimensions may seem remote from Zen's emphasis on individual awakening, this section reveals a contemplative core that resonates with Zen practice.

Zen readers find in this section the insight that all external transformation depends upon inner cultivation — that Great Peace in the world begins with great peace in the mind. The Taiping Jing's teaching that unified awareness is the foundation of harmonious action mirrors the Zen conviction that awakened consciousness naturally expresses itself in compassionate and effective engagement with the world.
