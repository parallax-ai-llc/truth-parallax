## Overview

The Book of Thomas the Contender is studied for its place within the Thomas tradition, its distinctive literary genre (combining revelation dialogue with prophetic woe oracles), and its radical ascetic theology. Scholars have debated the text's relationship to the Gospel of Thomas: while both are attributed to Judas Thomas and share an emphasis on self-knowledge, the Book of Thomas's fierce anti-somatic rhetoric goes well beyond anything in the sayings Gospel. The 'twin' motif connects to Syrian Christian traditions that identified Thomas as Jesus's literal twin brother (cf. the Acts of Thomas).
