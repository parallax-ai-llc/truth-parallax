## Overview

The Book of Thomas the Contender's radical asceticism places it in conversation with the world's most rigorous renunciation traditions. The Jain practice of sallekhana (fasting to death), the Hindu sannyasa (complete renunciation of worldly life), the Theravada Buddhist vinaya rules restricting bodily pleasures, and the Christian desert fathers' extreme austerities all reflect the same conviction that bodily desire is a fundamental obstacle to spiritual liberation. The fire imagery — passion as a consuming fire, and fire as the punishment for those consumed by passion — connects to Buddhist descriptions of the three fires (greed, hatred, delusion) and to Zoroastrian fire symbolism.
