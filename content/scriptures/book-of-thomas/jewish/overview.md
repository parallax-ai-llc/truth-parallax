## Overview

Jewish readers will find the Book of Thomas the Contender deeply alien to the mainstream Jewish affirmation of bodily existence. The rabbinic tradition celebrates the body as God's creation, affirms the sanctity of marriage and procreation, and regards bodily pleasures enjoyed within proper boundaries as gifts to be appreciated (the Talmud states that one will be called to account for legitimate pleasures refused, Jerusalem Talmud Kiddushin 4:12). The text's radical rejection of the body represents a trajectory of Jewish-influenced thought that departed sharply from the values of rabbinic Judaism.
