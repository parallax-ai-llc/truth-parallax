## Overview

The Book of Thomas the Contender raises a question that every religious tradition must answer: what is the proper relationship between the body and the spirit? The text's extreme asceticism represents one end of a spectrum that includes traditions celebrating the body (indigenous religions, Tantra, Judaism) and traditions seeking a middle way (Buddhism's rejection of both indulgence and extreme austerity). The universal insight embedded in the text — that unchecked desire can be destructive — is shared across traditions, even by those that reject the extreme conclusion that the body itself is the enemy.
