## Jonah's Prayer as Psalm Compilation

Jewish interpreters have long noted that Jonah's prayer is composed almost entirely of quotations and allusions from the Psalms (Psalm 18, 42, 69, 120, 142, among others). This observation raises the question: is Jonah composing original prayer or reciting known liturgical texts? The Midrash suggests that Jonah, in his extremity, drew on the treasury of Israel's prayer — a model for all Jews who turn to the Psalms in times of distress.

## The Fish as Instrument of Mercy

Rabbinic tradition distinguishes between two fish: according to one midrash, Jonah was first swallowed by a male fish with a spacious interior, then transferred to a female fish crowded with embryos, where the discomfort prompted his prayer. Whether taken literally or as homiletical elaboration, the point is clear: God calibrates his instruments of correction to produce repentance.

## "Salvation Belongs to the LORD" (2:9)

This declaration is the hinge of the book. Rashi and Radak emphasize that Jonah here acknowledges what he had tried to deny: God's right to save whomever he chooses — including Nineveh. The vow to offer sacrifice indicates that Jonah's heart has begun to turn, though the full lesson will not come until chapter 4.
