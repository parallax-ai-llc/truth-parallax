## The Soul's Descent into Matter

Gnostic interpretation of Jonah 1 reads the prophet's flight as an allegory of the divine spark's descent into the material world. Jonah's journey "down" — to Joppa, into the ship, into the hold, into sleep — mirrors the soul's progressive entrapment in the layers of the material cosmos. The ship represents the body; the sea represents the chaotic material realm created and governed by the Demiurge.

## The Sleep of Ignorance

Jonah's sleep in the hold while the storm rages is read as the sleep of *agnoia* — spiritual ignorance. The soul, trapped in matter, forgets its divine origin and falls into unconsciousness. The captain's command to "arise and call upon your God" (1:6) is the first call to awakening — the initial stirring of the soul toward *gnosis*.

## The Descent into the Deep

Being cast into the sea represents the soul's full immersion in the material world. Yet this descent is also the precondition for the encounter with the great fish — the moment of deepest entrapment that paradoxically becomes the occasion for awakening. The Gnostic reading finds hope in the very depth of the fall: it is precisely in the darkest place that the light of knowledge breaks through.
