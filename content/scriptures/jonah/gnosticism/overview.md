## Overview

Gnostic interpreters read the Book of Jonah as an allegory of the soul's descent into the material world and its eventual liberation through divine knowledge (*gnosis*). The sea represents the chaos of the material cosmos; the great fish represents the body or the realm of the Demiurge that swallows and imprisons the divine spark. Jonah's prayer from within the fish is the soul's cry of recognition — the moment of awakening to its true origin and destiny beyond the material order.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

While no complete Gnostic commentary on Jonah survives, the book's imagery permeates Gnostic literature. The *Pistis Sophia* and various Nag Hammadi texts employ the motif of descent into watery depths and re-emergence as a symbol of spiritual death and rebirth. Jonah's reluctance to go to Nineveh is read as the soul's resistance to its mission within the material world — a mission it did not choose but must fulfill to achieve liberation.

## Theological Emphases

- **The Cosmic Sea**: The ocean represents the chaotic material world created by the Demiurge
- **The Fish as Prison**: The great fish symbolizes the body or the archontic powers that trap the soul
- **Awakening and Gnosis**: Jonah's prayer is the moment of spiritual awakening within material entrapment
- **Ascent and Liberation**: Jonah's emergence from the fish represents the soul's return to the Pleroma
