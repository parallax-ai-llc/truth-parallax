## The Soul's Cry from the Prison of Matter

Gnostic interpretation reads Jonah's prayer from the fish as the archetypal cry of the divine spark trapped in the material world. The "belly of Sheol" is the deepest level of material entrapment — the soul has descended as far as possible into the Demiurge's domain. Yet it is precisely in this darkest place that awakening occurs. The prayer represents the moment of *anamnesis* — the soul's remembrance of its true divine origin.

## Gnosis in the Depths

The prayer's language of descent and ascent maps onto the Gnostic drama of the soul's fall and return. "The waters closed in over me; the deep surrounded me" (2:5) describes the soul's immersion in the material elements. But "you brought up my life from the pit" (2:6) expresses the Gnostic hope: the true God, beyond the Demiurge, reaches down to rescue the trapped spark.

## "Salvation Belongs to the LORD"

In Gnostic reading, this declaration distinguishes the true God — the source of genuine salvation — from the Demiurge, who offers only the illusion of order within a fundamentally imprisoning cosmos. True salvation is liberation from the material world itself, achieved through knowledge of one's divine origin.
