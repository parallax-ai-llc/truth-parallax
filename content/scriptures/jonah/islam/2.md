## The Supplication of Yunus

The prayer of Yunus from within the fish is one of the most celebrated supplications in Islam. The Quran records: "And he called out within the darknesses, 'There is no deity except You; exalted are You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers'" (21:87). This prayer — known as the *du'a of Yunus* or *du'a of Dhun-Nun* ("the one of the fish") — is recommended by the Prophet Muhammad for anyone in distress. A hadith states: "No Muslim makes supplication with this prayer regarding any matter except that God answers him."

## Divine Response

The Quran continues: "So We responded to him and saved him from the distress. And thus do We save the believers" (21:88). Islamic commentators emphasize the universality of this promise — the rescue is not for Yunus alone but for all believers who turn to God with sincerity. The key elements are *tasbih* (glorification of God), *tawhid* (affirmation of God's oneness), and *istighfar* (acknowledgment of one's own wrongdoing).

## Lessons for the Believer

Muslim scholars draw from Yunus's prayer the principle that sincere repentance is always accepted, regardless of the extremity of the circumstances. The "darknesses" — of the night, the sea, and the fish's belly — represent the multiple layers of trial that the believer may face, yet God's mercy penetrates every darkness.
