## Overview

Jonah is known as Yunus in Islam and holds a distinguished place among the prophets. Surah 10 of the Quran is named after him (*Surah Yunus*), and his story is referenced in multiple passages (Quran 21:87-88, 37:139-148, 68:48-50). The Quran recounts that Yunus left his people in anger before receiving God's permission, was swallowed by a great fish, and cried out from within its belly — "There is no deity except You; exalted are You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers" (21:87). God accepted his repentance and delivered him, and his people, uniquely among Quranic nations, repented and were spared punishment.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

Classical tafsir scholars such as al-Tabari and Ibn Kathir elaborate on the Quranic narrative, drawing on prophetic hadith and earlier Israelite traditions. The key lesson is *sabr* (patience) and *tawbah* (repentance): Yunus's error was impatience, not disbelief, and his repentance from within the fish is held up as a model supplication for all believers. The Prophet Muhammad instructed Muslims to recite the prayer of Yunus in times of distress.

## Theological Emphases

- **Tawbah**: Yunus's prayer from the fish is a model of sincere repentance accepted by God
- **Divine Mercy**: Nineveh is the only Quranic community that repented and was spared after being warned
- **Patience and Submission**: Yunus's lesson is that prophets must not abandon their mission out of frustration
- **Prophetic Fallibility**: Islam affirms that prophets are human and may err, but God guides them back to the right path
