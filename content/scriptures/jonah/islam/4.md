## God's Lesson to His Prophet

Islamic interpretation of the final chapter of Jonah's story draws on Quranic and hadith material that emphasizes the lesson God taught his prophet about patience and mercy. The Quran records: "And [mention] the man of the fish, when he went off in anger and thought that We would not decree [anything] upon him" (21:87). Muslim scholars see in the plant episode — though it is not detailed in the Quran — an illustration of the Quranic principle that God's mercy encompasses all things: "My mercy encompasses all things" (7:156).

## Prophetic Fallibility and Growth

Islamic theology affirms that prophets are human beings who grow through their experiences with God. Yunus's anger and God's gentle correction illustrate the Quranic teaching that God guides his prophets through trials. The Prophet Muhammad specifically instructed his followers: "Do not say that I am better than Yunus ibn Matta," indicating a special regard for Yunus's humility and the lesson he learned.

## The Universality of Divine Mercy

The chapter's central teaching — that God cares for all people, not only for the prophet's own community — aligns perfectly with the Islamic understanding of God as *Rabb al-'alamin* (Lord of all the worlds). Divine mercy is not tribal or national but universal, extending to all who turn to God in sincerity.
