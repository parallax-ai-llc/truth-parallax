## Yunus's Departure

The Quran recounts that Yunus (Jonah) "went off in anger" (37:140) and "fled to the laden ship" (37:140). Islamic interpretation emphasizes that Yunus's error was not disbelief but impatience — he left his people before receiving God's explicit permission. Al-Tabari and Ibn Kathir note that prophets, while protected from major sin (*ismah*), may exercise flawed human judgment, and God uses these moments to teach both the prophet and the community.

## The Drawing of Lots

The Quran mentions the drawing of lots: "He drew lots and was among the losers" (37:141). Islamic commentators see this as God's way of exposing Yunus's flight — the lot did not fall by chance but by divine decree. The episode illustrates the Quranic principle that nothing happens outside God's knowledge and will.

## Lessons for the Believer

Muslim scholars draw from this chapter the lesson that believers must not abandon their responsibilities out of frustration. The duty of *da'wah* (calling people to God) cannot be set aside because the messenger finds the task unpleasant or fears failure. God's plan unfolds according to his wisdom, not the prophet's preferences.
