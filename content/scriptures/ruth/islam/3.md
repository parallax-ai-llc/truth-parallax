## Modesty, Trust, and Honorable Conduct

Islamic reading of Ruth 3 approaches the threshing-floor episode with particular attention to the values of modesty (*haya'*), honorable conduct (*adab*), and trust between men and women. While the scene's cultural context differs from Islamic norms, Muslim scholars find in it principles consistent with Quranic ethics.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Naomi's Guidance and Family Authority

Naomi's role as guide and counselor reflects the Islamic value of family wisdom and elder guidance. The Quran instructs believers to honor and consult their elders, and the family unit (*usrah*) is the foundation of social order. Naomi's strategic thinking on Ruth's behalf demonstrates the protective role of family — seeking a secure and honorable future for her daughter-in-law.

### Modesty (Haya')

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "Modesty (*haya'*) is a branch of faith" (Sahih al-Bukhari). Islamic reading of this chapter focuses on the restraint displayed by both Ruth and Boaz:

- Ruth approaches Boaz respectfully and with a specific legal purpose
- Boaz responds with honor, praising Ruth's character rather than exploiting the situation
- Both parties maintain propriety despite the intimacy of the setting

Muslim scholars note that the narrative emphasizes the integrity of both characters, presenting their conduct as exemplary rather than scandalous.

### Trust in God's Plan (Tawakkul)

Ruth's act requires enormous trust — in Naomi's guidance, in Boaz's character, and ultimately in God's plan. The Quran teaches: "And whoever relies upon Allah — then He is sufficient for him" (Surah 65:3). Ruth's willingness to place herself in a vulnerable position reflects deep trust in divine guidance working through human relationships.

### The Legal Dimension

Ruth's request for Boaz to act as redeemer is a legal petition, not a romantic proposition. Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) values proper legal channels for marriage and property transactions. Ruth's approach, guided by Naomi, operates within the accepted legal framework of her society.

## Theological Themes

- **Haya' (Modesty)**: Restraint and propriety as expressions of faith
- **Adab (Proper Conduct)**: Both Ruth and Boaz model honorable behavior
- **Tawakkul**: Trust in God even in vulnerable and uncertain situations
- **Family Guidance**: The role of elder wisdom in navigating life's critical decisions

## Reflection

Muslim readers find in this chapter a narrative that affirms the compatibility of human initiative and divine guidance. Ruth acts with courage but within proper channels; Boaz responds with generosity but within moral boundaries. The threshing floor, despite its potential for moral compromise, becomes a place where honor and faith prevail — a testament to the power of character formed by trust in God.
