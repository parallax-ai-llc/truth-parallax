## Overview

The Book of Ruth is not part of the Quran, and Islam does not treat it as revealed scripture. However, Islamic scholarship engages with it as part of the *Isra'iliyyat* — traditions drawn from Jewish and Christian sources that may illuminate Quranic themes. Muslim scholars approach Ruth with interest where its themes align with Quranic values: trust in God (*tawakkul*), divine providence (*qadar*), loyalty to family, and care for the vulnerable.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Isra'iliyyat Framework

Classical Muslim historians and exegetes such as al-Tabari and Ibn Kathir referenced Israelite narratives to provide context for Quranic accounts of the prophets. The story of Ruth, as an ancestor of David (Dawud), is relevant to the Islamic understanding of prophetic lineage.

### Thematic Resonance with the Quran

Muslim scholars note that Ruth's themes — divine provision for the poor, the importance of family bonds, and trust in God's plan — echo Quranic teachings. The Quran repeatedly emphasizes God's care for orphans, widows, and the vulnerable (e.g., Surah 93:6-10).

### Comparative Theological Analysis

Modern Muslim scholars engage in comparative study, examining how the Ruth narrative illustrates ethical principles shared across Abrahamic traditions: generosity, hospitality, and social responsibility.

## Theological Emphases

- **Tawakkul**: Ruth's journey embodies trust in God despite uncertainty — a core Islamic virtue
- **Qadar (Divine Decree)**: God's unseen hand guiding events toward his purpose mirrors the Quranic concept of divine decree
- **Family Loyalty**: Ruth's devotion to Naomi reflects the Quranic emphasis on honoring family bonds (*silat al-rahim*)
- **Social Justice**: The gleaning laws parallel the Islamic institutions of *zakat* and *sadaqah*
- **Prophetic Lineage**: Ruth as an ancestor of Dawud (David) connects to the Islamic theology of prophethood

## Notable Islamic Scholars

- **al-Tabari**: Referenced Israelite traditions about David's ancestry in his *Tarikh*
- **Ibn Kathir**: Discussed the lineage of Dawud in *al-Bidaya wa'l-Nihaya*
- **al-Tha'labi**: Included stories of Ruth's context in *Qisas al-Anbiya*
