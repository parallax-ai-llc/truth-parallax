## Overview

Protestant interpretation of Ruth emphasizes the sovereignty of God working through ordinary human faithfulness. The book is read as a narrative of divine providence, covenant loyalty (*hesed*), and redemption — themes that resonate deeply with Reformation theology. Boaz as the kinsman-redeemer (*go'el*) is understood as a typological foreshadowing of Christ, who redeems his people not by obligation but by grace.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Typological-Christological

Many Protestant commentators read Boaz as a type of Christ. Just as Boaz redeemed Ruth — a foreigner with no claim — so Christ redeems sinners who have no merit of their own. The emphasis falls on grace rather than law.

### Redemptive-Historical

Reformed Protestants situate Ruth within the larger arc of salvation history. The genealogy in chapter 4, linking Ruth to David, is read as a critical link in the chain from Abraham to Christ (Matthew 1:5).

### Literal-Narrative

Evangelical interpreters value the book's historical setting "in the days when the judges ruled," seeing it as a contrast to the moral chaos of Judges. Ruth and Boaz exemplify covenant faithfulness in a time of widespread unfaithfulness.

## Theological Emphases

- **Providence**: God is never quoted as speaking in Ruth, yet his hand is visible in every event — a model of ordinary providence
- **Grace and Inclusion**: Ruth the Moabite is welcomed into Israel, prefiguring the Gentile inclusion in the church
- **Hesed**: Covenant loyalty as the defining virtue, exemplified by Ruth toward Naomi and by Boaz toward Ruth
- **The Kinsman-Redeemer**: The *go'el* institution as a picture of substitutionary redemption

## Notable Protestant Commentators

- **Matthew Henry**: Emphasized Ruth's piety and Boaz's godly character as moral examples
- **Charles Spurgeon**: Preached on Boaz as a picture of Christ welcoming sinners
- **Robert Hubbard**: Provided a detailed evangelical exegesis in the NICOT series
- **Katharine Sakenfeld**: Highlighted themes of women's agency and divine faithfulness
