---
title: "Vamana Purana Section 1"
scripture: "vamana-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. Salutation to Lord Vishnu in his form as Vamana, the divine dwarf, who measured the three worlds with three steps. This Purana reveals the glory of Vishnu and the path of dharma.

**2** Pulastya said to Narada: "O divine sage, I shall narrate to you the Vamana Purana, which describes the incarnation of Vishnu as the dwarf Brahmin and reveals the nature of creation, the duties of men, and the worship of the gods."

**3** The story begins with the demon king Bali, the grandson of Prahlada. Through his austerities and righteous conduct, Bali conquered the three worlds and drove Indra and the gods from heaven. Yet Bali was a virtuous ruler, just and generous.

**4** The gods, dispossessed of their realm, sought refuge with Vishnu. Aditi, the mother of the gods, performed severe austerities to obtain Vishnu as her son. The Lord was pleased and was born as Vamana, the divine dwarf.

**5** Vamana was small in stature but radiant as the sun. He wore the sacred thread, carried an umbrella and a water pot, and approached the sacrificial ground where Bali was performing a great ceremony, giving gifts to all who asked.

**6** Vamana said: "O great king, I ask of you only three steps of land — as much as I can cover in three strides. This is all I desire." Bali smiled at the modest request of the small Brahmin.

**7** Shukra, the guru of the demons, warned Bali: "O king, this is not an ordinary Brahmin. This is Vishnu in disguise. Do not grant his request, for he will take everything from you." But Bali replied: "If the Lord himself asks something of me, how can I refuse? I shall not break my word."

**8** Bali poured the ceremonial water over Vamana's hands, sealing the gift. At that moment, Vamana grew to cosmic size. With his first step he covered the earth. With his second step he covered the heavens. There was nowhere left for the third step.

**9** Vamana said: "O Bali, you promised three steps, but two have covered all that you possess. Where shall I place my third step?" Bali bowed his head and said: "Place it upon my head, O Lord. I offer myself."

**10** Vishnu, pleased by Bali's devotion and generosity, pressed him down to the nether world of Sutala with his foot. But he granted Bali a great boon: "You shall rule Sutala, which I shall make more beautiful than heaven itself. And I myself shall stand as the guardian of your gate."

**11** The Purana teaches: "Bali's story shows that true devotion lies in the willingness to give everything to the Lord — not just possessions, but one's very self. He who offers his head to the Lord's foot attains the highest liberation."

**12** The sage concluded: "The Vamana incarnation teaches that the Lord humbles the proud and exalts the humble. He who is small in ego is great in spirit. He who surrenders all, receives all."
