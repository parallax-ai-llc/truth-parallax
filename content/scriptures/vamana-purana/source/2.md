---
title: "Vamana Purana Section 2"
scripture: "vamana-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Vamana Purana narrates the stories of Shiva and Parvati, their marriage, and the birth of their sons Karttikeya and Ganesha.

**2** Parvati, the daughter of the mountain king Himavan, was born as the reincarnation of Sati, Shiva's first wife, who had immolated herself at Daksha's sacrifice. From childhood, Parvati was devoted to Shiva and resolved to win him as her husband.

**3** Parvati performed severe austerities in the Himalayas. She fasted, she endured heat and cold, she meditated upon Shiva. Kama, the god of love, shot his flower arrows at Shiva to awaken desire, but Shiva burned him to ashes with his third eye.

**4** Shiva tested Parvati by appearing as an old Brahmin and disparaging himself: "Shiva is a vagabond, smeared with ashes, dwelling in cremation grounds, wearing serpents as ornaments. Why do you desire such a husband?" But Parvati's devotion was unshaken.

**5** Pleased by her devotion, Shiva accepted Parvati as his wife. The wedding was celebrated with great splendor. All the gods attended, and Brahma performed the sacred rites. The mountains trembled with joy, and the rivers ran with nectar.

**6** From the union of Shiva and Parvati was born Karttikeya, the god of war. He was born to destroy the demon Taraka, who could only be slain by a son of Shiva. Karttikeya, at the age of seven days, led the divine army against Taraka and slew him.

**7** The Purana narrates the story of Andhaka, the blind demon, who was the son of Shiva and Parvati by an unusual birth. Andhaka, corrupted by his demon nature, desired his own mother Parvati. Shiva fought him in a terrible battle and impaled him on his trident.

**8** But from Andhaka's blood, which fell upon the earth, new demons arose. The goddess Shakti drank the blood before it could touch the ground. Andhaka, purified by Shiva's trident, repented and became a devoted attendant of Shiva, named Bhringi.

**9** The Purana describes the sacred sites associated with Shiva: "Wherever Shiva performed austerity, wherever his linga manifested, wherever his divine sports occurred — these places are sanctified for all time. He who visits them with devotion attains the world of Shiva."

**10** The twelve Jyotirlingas are enumerated and their stories told. Each is a place where Shiva manifested as a self-born pillar of light, and each carries the power to liberate the devotee from the cycle of rebirth.

**11** The Purana teaches the worship of the Shiva Linga: "The Linga is the supreme symbol. It is the union of form and formless, the meeting point of the manifest and the unmanifest. He who worships the Linga with water, bilva leaves, and the Panchakshara mantra 'Om Namah Shivaya' attains liberation."

**12** The sage concluded: "Shiva and Parvati are the divine parents of the universe. Their love is the model for all human love. Their union sustains creation. Their worship liberates the soul."
