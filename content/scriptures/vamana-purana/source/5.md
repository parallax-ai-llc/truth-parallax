---
title: "Vamana Purana Section 5"
scripture: "vamana-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The Vamana Purana describes the worship of the various forms of the divine and the mantras and practices associated with each.

**2** The worship of Vishnu in his various avatars is prescribed. The ten principal avatars are: Matsya (fish), Kurma (tortoise), Varaha (boar), Narasimha (man-lion), Vamana (dwarf), Parashurama (warrior sage), Rama (prince of Ayodhya), Krishna (lord of Vrindavan), Buddha (the enlightened one), and Kalki (the future warrior).

**3** Each avatar has its own mantra, its own form of worship, and its own festivals. The Purana declares: "He who worships all the avatars with equal devotion attains the complete grace of Vishnu, for each avatar reveals a different aspect of the divine nature."

**4** The worship of Shiva is also described: "The five-syllabled mantra, Om Namah Shivaya, is the essence of all the Vedas. He who chants this mantra with devotion is freed from all sins and attains the world of Shiva at death."

**5** The eight forms of Shiva are enumerated: Sharva, Bhava, Rudra, Ugra, Bhima, Pashupati, Ishana, and Mahadeva. Each form presides over one of the eight elements: earth, water, fire, air, space, the sun, the moon, and the soul.

**6** The worship of Ganesha is prescribed: "Before any undertaking, Ganesha must be worshipped with modakas, durva grass, and red flowers. The mantra 'Om Gam Ganapataye Namah' removes all obstacles and ensures success."

**7** The worship of the sun is described: "The Aditya Hridayam, the hymn to the sun, was taught by the sage Agastya to Rama on the battlefield of Lanka. He who recites it is freed from disease, sorrow, and sin."

**8** The Purana describes the practice of mantra japa: "The efficacy of a mantra depends on three factors: the initiation by a qualified guru, the correct pronunciation, and the sincerity of the devotee. A mantra received without proper initiation is like a seed planted in barren soil."

**9** The science of Yantra is described: "A Yantra is a geometric diagram that embodies the power of a deity. When consecrated with the proper mantras, it becomes a dwelling place of the divine. The Sri Yantra is the supreme Yantra, representing the union of Shiva and Shakti."

**10** The Purana teaches the practice of Nyasa, the ritual placement of mantras on the body: "By touching the various parts of the body while chanting mantras, the devotee transforms his body into a temple of the deity. Each limb becomes charged with divine energy."

**11** The Purana describes the power of sacred music: "The singing of bhajans and kirtans is a powerful means of worship. When the name of the Lord is sung with devotion, the atmosphere is purified, the hearts of the listeners are softened, and the divine presence descends."

**12** The sage concluded: "All forms of worship — mantra, yantra, puja, kirtan, meditation — are different streams flowing into the ocean of divine love. The sincere devotee need not master all of them. Even one practice, performed with wholehearted devotion, is sufficient for liberation."
