---
title: "Vamana Purana Section 4"
scripture: "vamana-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The Vamana Purana describes the sacred geography, the holy places, and the pilgrimages that purify the soul.

**2** The sage said: "The earth is dotted with sacred sites like the sky is dotted with stars. Each site is a doorway to the divine. The pilgrim who visits these sites with devotion steps through these doorways and approaches the Lord."

**3** The glory of Kurukshetra is described: "The field of Kurukshetra is the most sacred of all fields. It is the field of dharma, where the great war was fought and where Krishna delivered the Bhagavad Gita. A bath in the lakes of Kurukshetra during a solar eclipse yields infinite merit."

**4** The glory of Prayaga is described: "At the confluence of the Ganga, the Yamuna, and the Sarasvati, the merit of a single bath equals the merit of all the sacrifices described in the Vedas. Brahma himself performed the first sacrifice at Prayaga."

**5** The Purana describes the seasonal pilgrimages: "In Chaitra, visit Chitrakuta. In Vaishakha, visit Badrinath. In Kartika, visit Vrindavan. In Magha, visit Prayaga. Each season has its appointed holy place, and the merit obtained is multiplied by the auspiciousness of the time."

**6** The rules for pilgrims are prescribed: "The pilgrim should travel with humility, eat simple food, sleep on the ground, and devote every moment to prayer and meditation. He should give charity to the poor and feed the hungry wherever he goes."

**7** The Purana warns: "The merit of pilgrimage is lost if the pilgrim indulges in gossip, criticism, anger, or deceit during the journey. The external pilgrimage must be accompanied by an internal purification. Otherwise, it is a mere tour, not a spiritual practice."

**8** The Purana describes the merit of bathing in sacred rivers: "He who bathes in the Ganga is freed from all sins accumulated over a hundred lifetimes. He who bathes in the Yamuna obtains the love of Krishna. He who bathes in the Sarasvati obtains wisdom."

**9** The glory of the Narmada river is described: "The Narmada is unique among rivers. Her very stones are Shiva Lingas. Merely walking along her banks yields the merit of performing the Ashvamedha sacrifice. She flows westward while other rivers flow east, symbolizing the path of liberation."

**10** The Purana describes the merit of building temples, digging wells, and planting gardens along pilgrimage routes: "He who provides shelter for pilgrims, water for the thirsty, and shade for the weary earns merit that endures as long as the sun and moon."

**11** The Purana teaches: "The ultimate pilgrimage is inward. The body is the temple, the soul is the deity, and the mind is the pilgrim. He who knows this truth performs the greatest pilgrimage without taking a single step."

**12** The sage concluded: "Whether one travels to distant sacred sites or worships the divine within one's own heart, the purpose is the same: to purify the soul and draw near to the Lord. All paths lead to the same destination."
