---
title: "Vamana Purana Section 3"
scripture: "vamana-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Vamana Purana describes the stories of the great devotees and the power of devotion to transform the soul.

**2** The story of Dhruva is narrated. The young prince, rejected by his stepmother Suruchi who declared that only her own son could sit on the king's lap, went to the forest at the age of five to find God.

**3** Narada met Dhruva in the forest and taught him the mantra: "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya." Dhruva practiced such severe meditation that the gods themselves were alarmed. He stood on one foot, ate nothing, breathed not, and fixed his mind entirely on Vishnu.

**4** Vishnu appeared before Dhruva and asked: "What do you desire, O child?" Dhruva replied: "I sought a place higher than my father's throne. But now that I have seen you, I desire nothing but your grace." Vishnu granted him an eternal position as the Pole Star, the fixed point of the universe.

**5** The story of the sage Markandeya is told. Destined to die at the age of sixteen, Markandeya embraced the Shiva Linga as Yama's noose came for him. Shiva appeared and kicked Yama, declaring: "This devotee is under my protection. Death has no power over him."

**6** Markandeya thus became an immortal sage. The Purana teaches: "Devotion conquers death. The devotee who clings to the Lord with unshakeable faith transcends the power of time and achieves immortality."

**7** The story of Prahlada is retold with emphasis on his teachings. When asked what he had learned, young Prahlada said: "I have learned that the worship of Vishnu is the highest wisdom, and that the material world, with all its pleasures and powers, is transient and unreal."

**8** Prahlada taught the demon children: "Do not seek wealth, power, or pleasure. Seek the Lord alone. This human life is rare and precious. Use it for the worship of the divine, not for the accumulation of things that perish."

**9** The story of Ambarisha is narrated in detail. The king was so devoted to Vishnu that even the curse of the sage Durvasa could not harm him. The Sudarshana disc of Vishnu pursued Durvasa across the three worlds until the sage fell at Ambarisha's feet and begged forgiveness.

**10** The Purana teaches: "The protection of the Lord is absolute. He who surrenders to Vishnu need fear nothing — not the wrath of sages, not the power of demons, not the terrors of death. The Lord is the shield that no weapon can pierce."

**11** The Purana describes the rewards of devotion: "The devotee of Vishnu attains a spiritual body of pure consciousness, adorned with all divine qualities. He enters Vaikuntha, the eternal abode, and dwells there in bliss, serving the Lord forever."

**12** The sage concluded: "The stories of the great devotees are not mere tales. They are maps of the spiritual journey. Each devotee's story illuminates a different aspect of the path — faith, surrender, love, perseverance, and the ultimate triumph of the soul."
