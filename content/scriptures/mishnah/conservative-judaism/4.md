## Legal Reasoning as Religious Practice

Conservative Judaism finds in Seder Nezikin a model for the halakhic process itself. The careful argumentation of Bava Kamma, the nuanced case analysis of Bava Metzia, and the procedural rigor of Sanhedrin exemplify the kind of reasoned legal discourse that the Conservative movement aspires to in its own halakhic deliberations.

## Between Jewish and Civil Law

Conservative Jews live under both Jewish and civil legal systems. Nezikin's comprehensive civil and criminal code raises questions about the relationship between these two systems. Conservative halakhah generally applies the principle of dina d'malkhuta dina (the law of the land is the law) while maintaining that Jewish ethical principles should inform Jews' engagement with civil society and public policy.

## Procedural Justice

The procedural protections elaborated in Sanhedrin — careful judicial deliberation, protection of minority opinions, preference for mercy in cases of doubt — are values that Conservative Judaism brings to contemporary discussions of criminal justice. The movement has drawn on Nezikin in its advocacy for fair courts, opposition to the death penalty, and support for restorative justice approaches.
