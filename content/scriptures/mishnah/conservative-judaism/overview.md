## Overview

Conservative Judaism regards the Mishnah as both a sacred text and a historical document. Following the "positive-historical" approach of Zacharias Frankel, Conservative scholars study the Mishnah with deep reverence for its authority while also employing critical methods to understand its development. The Mishnah is seen as the product of a divinely inspired but humanly mediated process of legal reasoning.

## Interpretive Approach

The Conservative approach integrates traditional Talmudic study with modern academic scholarship. Scholars like Louis Ginzberg and Saul Lieberman exemplified this dual methodology, bringing philological rigor and historical awareness to their Mishnah commentary while maintaining commitment to halakhic observance. The Mishnah's legal debates are studied both for their practical rulings and for the underlying principles of legal reasoning they embody.

## Theological Emphases

Conservative interpretation emphasizes the Mishnah as a model for the halakhic process — the ongoing, communal enterprise of applying Torah principles to new situations. The Committee on Jewish Law and Standards sees itself as continuing the Mishnah's own methodology: gathering scholarly opinions, debating vigorously, and reaching decisions that balance tradition with contemporary needs.
