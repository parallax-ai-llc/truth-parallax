## Gender and Halakhic Evolution

Conservative Judaism's engagement with Seder Nashim reflects its most transformative halakhic work. The movement has used the halakhic process itself to address gender inequities, arguing that the sages' own principles of human dignity (kevod ha-beriyot) and the avoidance of injustice authorize — indeed require — modifications to laws that produce unjust outcomes in changed social circumstances.

## The Agunah Problem

The Conservative movement has been at the forefront of addressing the agunah crisis — the situation of women unable to obtain a religious divorce (get) from recalcitrant husbands. The Lieberman clause, inserted into the Conservative ketubah, provides a halakhic mechanism for addressing this problem. This approach exemplifies the Conservative method: using internal halakhic tools to solve problems created by changed social conditions.

## Egalitarian Marriage

Conservative Judaism now performs fully egalitarian weddings, with both partners participating equally in the ceremony. This development emerged through careful halakhic reasoning, drawing on precedents within Nashim itself and on broader principles of human dignity and equality. The Conservative approach insists that egalitarianism is not imposed from outside but emerges from within the halakhic tradition's own deepest commitments.
