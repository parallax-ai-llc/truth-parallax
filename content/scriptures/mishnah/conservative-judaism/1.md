## Agricultural Law and Ethical Obligation

Conservative Judaism engages Seder Zeraim as both operative halakhah (where applicable) and as a source of enduring ethical principles. The order's agricultural laws are studied through the dual lens of traditional commentary and historical-critical scholarship, recognizing their rootedness in the agricultural economy of ancient Israel while extracting principles of social justice and environmental responsibility for contemporary application.

## Berakhot as Living Practice

Tractate Berakhot is fully operative in Conservative practice. The laws of the Shema, the Amidah, blessings over food, and other daily prayers structure the Conservative Jew's daily spiritual life. Conservative liturgical committees have worked to balance fidelity to the traditional prayer texts with sensitivity to contemporary theological concerns, including gender-inclusive language and the theology of chosenness.

## Social Justice in Agricultural Law

Conservative scholars highlight how the Zeraim laws create a systematic framework for caring for the poor within the agricultural economy. The multiple layers of obligation — pe'ah, leket, shikchah, ma'aser ani — ensure that the economically vulnerable have reliable access to sustenance. The Conservative movement draws on these precedents in its social justice advocacy, arguing that Jewish law has always demanded structural responses to poverty.
