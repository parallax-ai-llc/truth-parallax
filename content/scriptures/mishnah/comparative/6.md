## Purity Systems Worldwide

Seder Tohorot's elaborate purity system finds parallels across world religions. Hindu concepts of shuddhi (purity) and ashuddhi (impurity), Islamic tahara (purity) and najasa (impurity), Shinto concepts of kegare (impurity) and harae (purification), and Zoroastrian purity laws all classify persons, objects, and states according to categories of ritual fitness. Each system links physical states to spiritual or sacred conditions.

## Purification Through Water

The Mishnah's laws of mikveh (ritual immersion bath) parallel water purification rituals across traditions. Islamic wudu (ablution) and ghusl (full immersion), Hindu snana (bathing in sacred rivers), Christian baptism, and Shinto misogi (purification under a waterfall) all employ water as a medium of ritual transformation. The universal association of water with purification reflects deep symbolic connections between water, life, cleansing, and renewal.

## Death, Birth, and Purity Boundaries

The Mishnah's association of impurity with death, childbirth, and certain bodily emissions parallels similar associations in Hindu dharmashastra, Shinto purity laws, and many indigenous religious systems. Anthropologists suggest these cross-cultural patterns reflect universal human experiences of the boundaries between life and death, the body's openings and emissions, and the need to ritually mark transitions between sacred and ordinary states.
