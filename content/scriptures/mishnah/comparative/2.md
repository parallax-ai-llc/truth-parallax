## Sacred Calendars

Seder Moed's system of sacred time parallels the ritual calendars of other traditions. The Islamic lunar calendar with its five daily prayers, Ramadan fast, and Hajj pilgrimage; the Hindu panchanga with its festivals and vrata (observances); the Buddhist uposatha days; and the Christian liturgical year with its seasons and feast days all structure time according to sacred rhythms that connect communities to their foundational narratives.

## The Sabbath Concept

The Jewish Shabbat finds partial parallels in other traditions' days of rest and worship: the Christian Sunday, the Islamic Jumu'ah (Friday), the Buddhist uposatha days, and the Roman dies fasti and nefasti (days on which certain activities were permitted or forbidden). However, the totality of the rabbinic Sabbath — encompassing complete cessation from creative labor — is distinctive in its comprehensiveness and rigor.

## Pilgrimage Festivals

The three pilgrimage festivals (shalosh regalim) described in Moed parallel pilgrimage traditions worldwide: the Islamic Hajj to Mecca, the Hindu tirtha-yatra to sacred rivers and temples, the Buddhist pilgrimage to sites of the Buddha's life, and the medieval Christian pilgrimage to Jerusalem, Rome, and Santiago de Compostela. Each tradition sanctifies physical journeying as a form of spiritual practice.
