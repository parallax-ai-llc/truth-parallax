## The Order of Holy Things

Seder Kodashim (Order of Holy Things) contains 14 tractates governing the Temple sacrificial system, ritual slaughter of animals, firstborn animals, and consecrated property. Key tractates include Zevachim (animal sacrifices), Menachot (meal offerings), Chullin (non-sacred slaughter), Bekhorot (firstborn), and Middot (measurements of the Temple). Though the Temple no longer stands, Orthodox Judaism continues to study these laws in anticipation of its future rebuilding.

## Study as Substitute for Sacrifice

The Talmud teaches that one who studies the laws of the burnt offering is considered as if they had offered one. Orthodox tradition therefore treats the study of Kodashim not merely as academic exercise but as a form of spiritual service that partially fulfills the function of the sacrifices during the Temple's absence. The daily recitation of the korbanot (sacrificial passages) in the morning service reflects this principle.

## Preparation for the Future

Orthodox authorities who anticipate the rebuilding of the Third Temple regard the study of Kodashim as practical preparation. The detailed descriptions of Temple procedures, measurements, and sacrificial rites preserved in the Mishnah constitute the blueprint for the restored Temple service, making their mastery a religious obligation for the generation that will witness the redemption.
