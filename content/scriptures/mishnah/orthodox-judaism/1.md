## The Order of Seeds

Seder Zeraim (Order of Seeds) encompasses the agricultural laws of the Torah as elaborated by the Oral Tradition. Its 14 tractates address blessings (Berakhot), the corners of fields left for the poor (Pe'ah), tithes (Ma'aserot), the sabbatical year (Shevi'it), and other laws governing the produce of the Land of Israel. Orthodox Judaism treats these laws as divinely ordained, applicable in their full scope whenever Jews possess sovereignty over the Land of Israel.

## Berakhot and Daily Life

Though categorized under agriculture, Seder Zeraim begins with Tractate Berakhot (Blessings), which governs the Shema, the Amidah, and blessings over food and other occasions. Orthodox commentators explain that blessings precede agricultural law because recognizing God's sovereignty — expressed through blessings — is the prerequisite for properly relating to the material world. One must first acknowledge the Creator before engaging with creation.

## Contemporary Relevance

With the reestablishment of Jewish sovereignty in the Land of Israel, many Zeraim laws have returned to practical halakhic relevance. Orthodox authorities in Israel issue rulings on terumah, ma'aser, shemittah (sabbatical year), and orlah (fruit of young trees). The Chief Rabbinate oversees the heter mekhirah (sale of land) and other mechanisms for observing these ancient agricultural laws under modern conditions.
