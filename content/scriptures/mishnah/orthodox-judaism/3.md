## The Order of Women

Seder Nashim (Order of Women) comprises 16 tractates governing the legal dimensions of marriage, divorce, vows, and family relations. Its central tractates include Kiddushin (betrothal), Ketubot (marriage contracts), Gittin (divorce), Sotah (the suspected adulteress), Yevamot (levirate marriage), and Nedarim (vows). Orthodox Judaism treats these laws as the divinely mandated framework for family life.

## The Sanctity of Marriage

Orthodox interpretation reads the laws of Nashim as reflecting the sanctity (kedushah) inherent in the marriage relationship. The term kiddushin (betrothal) derives from the root k-d-sh (holy), indicating that marriage is not merely a social contract but a sacred bond governed by divine law. The ketubah (marriage contract) protects the wife's rights and establishes mutual obligations rooted in Torah values.

## Halakhic Sensitivity

While acknowledging that many of Seder Nashim's laws reflect patriarchal social structures, Orthodox authorities emphasize that the rabbinic elaboration consistently moved toward protecting women's interests within the existing legal framework. The ketubah's financial protections, the limitations on a husband's power to divorce, and the careful procedures of Gittin all demonstrate the sages' concern for justice and dignity within the institution of marriage.
