## The Order of Damages

Seder Nezikin (Order of Damages) is the largest order of the Mishnah, with 20 tractates covering civil and criminal law, court procedure, torts, property law, commercial transactions, and ethical conduct. Its tractates include Bava Kamma (first gate — torts), Bava Metzia (middle gate — lost property and labor), Bava Batra (last gate — property and commerce), Sanhedrin (courts and capital punishment), and Makkot (punishments). This order also contains Pirkei Avot.

## The Torah's Legal System

Orthodox Judaism understands Seder Nezikin as presenting a complete, divinely ordained legal system that addresses every dimension of human interaction and dispute. The four categories of damages (ox, pit, crop-destroying animal, and fire) established in Bava Kamma provide the foundational principles from which all tort law is derived, demonstrating the Torah's comprehensive scope.

## Justice and Mercy

The legal proceedings described in Sanhedrin reveal the rabbinic commitment to justice tempered by mercy. The elaborate procedural safeguards in capital cases — requirements for warning, witnesses, judicial deliberation, and the principle that a unanimous guilty verdict results in acquittal — demonstrate that the Torah's justice system is designed to protect the accused while maintaining social order.
