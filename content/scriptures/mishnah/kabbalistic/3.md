## Marriage as Cosmic Union

Kabbalistic reading of Seder Nashim understands earthly marriage as a reflection of the supernal union between the masculine and feminine aspects of the divine — between the Holy One, Blessed Be He (centered on Tiferet) and the Shekhinah (Malkhut). The laws of kiddushin (betrothal) and nissuin (marriage) thus encode the dynamics of cosmic union and separation, making every wedding a participation in the divine mystery.

## The Shekhinah in Exile

The laws of divorce (Gittin) are read mystically as reflecting the exile of the Shekhinah from Her consort. Just as sin causes a separation between the upper and lower dimensions of the divine, so human estrangement reflects and intensifies this cosmic rift. Conversely, harmonious marriage — achieved through the meticulous observance of family law — contributes to the reunion of the divided aspects of God.

## Vows and the Power of Speech

The laws of vows (Nedarim and Nazir) are understood in terms of the kabbalistic teaching about the creative power of speech. Human words, like divine utterances, have the power to create and bind realities. The Zohar teaches that vows ascend to the heavenly court and generate spiritual consequences, explaining the extreme seriousness with which the Mishnah treats the making and annulment of vows.
