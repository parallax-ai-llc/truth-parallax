## Seeds and Spiritual Sustenance

Kabbalistic interpretation reads Seder Zeraim as addressing the flow of divine sustenance (shefa) from the upper worlds into the material realm. Just as seeds planted in the earth draw upon hidden forces to produce visible growth, so the soul draws upon the concealed light of the Ein Sof to nourish its spiritual development. The agricultural laws encode the mechanics of this spiritual economy.

## Blessings as Channels of Light

The blessings prescribed in Tractate Berakhot are understood by the kabbalists as theurgic acts — not merely expressions of gratitude but active channels that draw divine light downward. The Zohar teaches that when a person recites a blessing with proper kavvanah (intention), they create a connection (tzinor) between the upper and lower worlds, facilitating the flow of blessing (berakhah from the sefirah of yesod) into material reality.

## Tithes and the Rectification of Sparks

The various tithes and offerings prescribed in Zeraim are read as mechanisms for elevating the holy sparks (nitzotzot) trapped in the material world since the breaking of the vessels (shevirat ha-kelim). By separating terumah and ma'aser with proper intention, the farmer participates in the cosmic work of tikkun, extracting holiness from the mundane and returning it to its divine source.
