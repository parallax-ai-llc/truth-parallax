## The Temple as Cosmic Axis

Kabbalistic reading of Seder Kodashim understands the Temple as the physical manifestation of the sefirotic tree — the point where heaven and earth meet. The Holy of Holies corresponds to keter, the highest sefirah; the various courts and chambers map onto the lower sefirot; and the altar represents the sefirah of malkhut, where divine and human worlds intersect. The sacrificial service was the mechanism by which the connection between worlds was maintained.

## Sacrifice as Elevation of Sparks

The word korban (sacrifice) derives from the root k-r-b, meaning "to bring near." Kabbalistic interpretation understands sacrifice as bringing the material world near to the divine, elevating the holy sparks trapped in animal and vegetable matter. The burning of the sacrifice releases these sparks upward, effecting a tikkun (rectification) in the supernal worlds. The pleasant aroma ascending to God symbolizes the return of sparks to their source.

## The Red Heifer and the Mystery of Purification

Tractate Parah (Red Heifer) describes what the tradition itself calls a chok — a law beyond rational comprehension. The kabbalists read the red heifer ritual as encoding the deepest mysteries of death, purification, and the transformation of impurity into purity. The paradox that the heifer purifies the impure while rendering the pure handler impure reflects the inscrutable dynamics of the highest levels of divine will (ratzon ha-elyon).
