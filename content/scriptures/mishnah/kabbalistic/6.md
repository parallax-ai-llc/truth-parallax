## Tumah and the Concealment of Light

Kabbalistic interpretation understands tumah (ritual impurity) as the result of the concealment or withdrawal of divine light. Death, the primary source of impurity, represents the ultimate withdrawal of the divine life-force from the physical vessel. The various categories of impurity in Seder Tohorot map the gradations of this concealment, from the most intense (a human corpse) to lesser degrees associated with certain emissions and states.

## The Mikveh and the Waters of Creation

The mikveh (ritual bath) is understood as a return to the primordial waters (mayim) that existed before creation — the undifferentiated divine potential from which all form emerges. Immersion in the mikveh is thus a symbolic death and rebirth: the person descends into the formless waters and emerges renewed, their connection to the source of life restored. The Ari (Rabbi Isaac Luria) developed elaborate kavvanot (meditative intentions) for mikveh immersion.

## Niddah and the Lunar Cycle

The laws of niddah (menstrual purity) are connected by the kabbalists to the cosmic rhythms of the moon and the sefirah of malkhut (which is symbolized by the moon). The monthly cycle of withdrawal and renewal in the woman's body mirrors the waxing and waning of the moon and the ebb and flow of divine light in the world. The mikveh immersion at the cycle's conclusion represents the restoration of full divine illumination — the new moon's emergence into fullness.
