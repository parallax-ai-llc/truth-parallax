---
title: "Mishnah - Chapter 4"
scripture: "mishnah"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Ben Zoma says: Who is wise? He who learns from every person, as it is said: "From all my teachers I have gained understanding."

**2** Who is strong? He who conquers his inclination, as it is said: "He who is slow to anger is better than the mighty, and he who rules his spirit than he who captures a city."

**3** Who is rich? He who is happy with his lot, as it is said: "When you eat of the labor of your hands, you shall be happy and it shall be well with you." "You shall be happy" — in this world. "And it shall be well with you" — in the World to Come.

**4** Who is honored? He who honors others, as it is said: "For those who honor Me I will honor, and those who despise Me shall be lightly esteemed."

**5** Ben Azzai says: Run to perform even a minor commandment, and flee from transgression. For one commandment leads to another commandment, and one transgression leads to another transgression.

**6** For the reward of a commandment is a commandment, and the reward of a transgression is a transgression.

**7** He used to say: Do not despise any person and do not dismiss any thing. For there is no person who does not have his hour, and there is no thing that does not have its place.

**8** Rabbi Levitas of Yavneh says: Be exceedingly humble in spirit, for the hope of mortal man is the worm.

**9** Rabbi Yochanan ben Beroka says: Whoever profanes the Name of Heaven in secret will be punished in public. It is all one whether the profanation is committed unintentionally or intentionally.

**10** Rabbi Yishmael his son says: He who studies Torah in order to teach — it is granted to him to study and to teach. He who studies in order to practice — it is granted to him to study and to teach, to observe and to practice.

**11** Rabbi Tzadok says: Do not make the Torah a crown with which to aggrandize yourself, nor a spade with which to dig. And thus Hillel used to say: He who makes use of the crown of the Torah shall perish. Hence you may infer that whoever derives a worldly benefit from the words of Torah removes his life from the world.

**12** Rabbi Yose says: Whoever honors the Torah is himself honored by others. Whoever dishonors the Torah is himself dishonored by others.

**13** Rabbi Yishmael says: He who refrains from judging removes from himself enmity, theft, and false swearing. He who is arrogant in rendering legal decisions is foolish, wicked, and of a haughty spirit.

**14** He used to say: Do not judge alone, for there is none who may judge alone save One. And do not say: "Accept my opinion," for the choice is theirs and not yours.

**15** Rabbi Yonatan says: Whoever fulfills the Torah in poverty shall in the end fulfill it in wealth. And whoever neglects the Torah in wealth shall in the end neglect it in poverty.

**16** Rabbi Meir says: Engage less in business and engage more in Torah. Be of humble spirit before every person. If you neglect the Torah, you will have many more neglectors against you. But if you labor in the Torah, He has great reward to give you.
