---
title: "Mishnah - Chapter 1"
scripture: "mishnah"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Moses received the Torah from Sinai and transmitted it to Joshua, and Joshua to the elders, and the elders to the prophets, and the prophets transmitted it to the men of the Great Assembly.

**2** They said three things: Be deliberate in judgment; raise up many disciples; and make a fence around the Torah.

**3** Shimon the Righteous was of the last of the men of the Great Assembly. He used to say: Upon three things the world stands — upon the Torah, upon the Temple service, and upon acts of loving-kindness.

**4** Antigonus of Sokho received the tradition from Shimon the Righteous. He used to say: Be not like servants who serve the master on condition of receiving a reward; rather, be like servants who serve the master without the condition of receiving a reward. And let the fear of Heaven be upon you.

**5** Yose ben Yo'ezer of Tzeredah and Yose ben Yochanan of Jerusalem received the tradition from them. Yose ben Yo'ezer says: Let your house be a meeting-house for the sages; sit in the dust of their feet; and drink in their words with thirst.

**6** Yose ben Yochanan of Jerusalem says: Let your house be open wide; let the poor be members of your household; and do not engage in excessive conversation with women. They said this even regarding one's own wife — how much more so regarding another man's wife.

**7** From this the sages said: Anyone who engages in excessive conversation with women causes evil to himself, neglects the study of Torah, and in the end will inherit Gehenna.

**8** Yehoshua ben Perachyah and Nittai the Arbelite received the tradition from them. Yehoshua ben Perachyah says: Appoint for yourself a teacher; acquire for yourself a companion; and judge every person favorably.

**9** Nittai the Arbelite says: Distance yourself from a bad neighbor; do not associate with the wicked; and do not despair of retribution.

**10** Yehudah ben Tabbai and Shimon ben Shetach received the tradition from them. Yehudah ben Tabbai says: Do not act as a counselor to judges. When the litigants stand before you, regard them both as guilty; but when they leave your presence, regard them both as innocent, once they have accepted the judgment.

**11** Shimon ben Shetach says: Interrogate the witnesses thoroughly, and be careful with your words, lest from them they learn to lie.

**12** Shemayah and Avtalyon received the tradition from them. Shemayah says: Love work; hate lordship; and do not make yourself known to the government.

**13** Avtalyon says: Sages, be careful with your words. For you may be exiled to a place of evil waters, and the disciples who come after you may drink and die, and the name of Heaven will be profaned.

**14** Hillel and Shammai received the tradition from them. Hillel says: Be of the disciples of Aaron — loving peace and pursuing peace, loving mankind and drawing them close to the Torah.

**15** He used to say: A name made great is a name destroyed. He who does not increase shall decrease. He who does not study deserves to die. And he who makes use of the crown of the Torah shall perish.

**16** He used to say: If I am not for myself, who will be for me? And if I am only for myself, what am I? And if not now, when?

**17** Shammai says: Make your Torah study a fixed practice. Say little and do much. And receive every person with a cheerful countenance.

**18** Rabban Gamaliel says: Appoint for yourself a teacher and free yourself from doubt, and do not accustom yourself to tithe by estimation.
