---
title: "Mishnah - Chapter 2"
scripture: "mishnah"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Rabbi Yehudah HaNasi says: Which is the right path that a person should choose for himself? Whatever is an honor to the one who does it and an honor in the eyes of others.

**2** Be as careful with a minor commandment as with a major one, for you do not know the reward for the fulfillment of the commandments. And reckon the loss incurred in fulfilling a commandment against the reward gained, and the gain of a transgression against the loss it entails.

**3** Contemplate three things and you will not come into the grip of sin: Know what is above you — a seeing eye, a hearing ear, and all your deeds are written in a book.

**4** Rabban Gamaliel the son of Rabbi Yehudah HaNasi says: Torah study is excellent together with a worldly occupation, for the effort of both makes sin forgotten. All Torah study that is not combined with work will ultimately cease and lead to sin.

**5** All who occupy themselves with the community should do so for the sake of Heaven, for the merit of their ancestors sustains them, and their righteousness endures forever.

**6** And as for you, God will say: I credit you with a great reward as if you had done it all.

**7** Be cautious with the ruling authority, for they do not befriend a person except for their own needs. They appear as friends when it is to their advantage, but they do not stand by a man in the hour of his distress.

**8** He used to say: Do His will as if it were your own will, so that He will do your will as if it were His. Nullify your will before His will, so that He will nullify the will of others before your will.

**9** Hillel says: Do not separate yourself from the community. Do not trust in yourself until the day of your death. Do not judge your fellow until you have reached his place. Do not say something that cannot be heard, for in the end it will be heard.

**10** And do not say: "When I have leisure I will study," for perhaps you will never have leisure.

**11** He used to say: A boor cannot fear sin; an ignorant person cannot be pious; a bashful person cannot learn; an impatient person cannot teach; and not all who engage in commerce grow wise.

**12** In a place where there are no men, strive to be a man.

**13** He also saw a skull floating on the water. He said to it: Because you drowned others, you were drowned. And those who drowned you shall in the end themselves be drowned.

**14** He used to say: The more flesh, the more worms. The more property, the more anxiety. The more wives, the more witchcraft. The more maidservants, the more lewdness. The more slaves, the more thievery. The more Torah, the more life. The more study, the more wisdom. The more counsel, the more understanding. The more charity, the more peace.

**15** He who has acquired a good name has acquired it for himself. He who has acquired for himself words of Torah has acquired for himself the life of the World to Come.

**16** Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai received the tradition from Hillel and Shammai. He used to say: If you have studied much Torah, do not claim credit for yourself, for this is the purpose for which you were created.
