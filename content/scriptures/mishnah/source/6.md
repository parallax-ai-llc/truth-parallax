---
title: "Mishnah - Chapter 6"
scripture: "mishnah"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The sages taught in the language of the Mishnah. Blessed be He who chose them and their learning.

**2** Rabbi Meir says: Whoever engages in Torah for its own sake merits many things. Not only that, but the entire world is worthwhile for his sake alone. He is called friend, beloved, one who loves God, one who loves mankind.

**3** It clothes him in humility and fear, and prepares him to be righteous, pious, upright, and faithful. It distances him from sin and draws him near to merit.

**4** Others benefit from his counsel and wisdom, understanding and strength, as it is said: "Counsel is mine and wisdom; I am understanding, strength is mine."

**5** It gives him sovereignty and dominion and analytical judgment. The secrets of Torah are revealed to him, and he becomes like a spring that ever gathers force and like a river that flows on with ever-sustained vigor.

**6** He becomes modest, patient, and forgiving of insult. And it magnifies him and exalts him above all things.

**7** Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi says: Every day a heavenly voice goes forth from Mount Horeb and proclaims: "Woe to mankind for their contempt of the Torah!"

**8** For whoever does not occupy himself with the Torah is called "rebuked," as it is said: "Like a gold ring in the snout of a swine is a beautiful woman who lacks discretion."

**9** And it says: "The tablets were the work of God, and the writing was the writing of God, engraved upon the tablets." Read not "engraved" (charut) but "freedom" (cherut), for there is no free man except one who occupies himself with the study of Torah.

**10** And whoever occupies himself with the study of Torah is exalted, as it is said: "From Mattanah to Nahaliel, and from Nahaliel to Bamoth" — from gift to the inheritance of God, and from the inheritance of God to the heights.

**11** Torah is acquired through forty-eight qualities: study, attentive listening, ordered speech, understanding of the heart, discernment of the heart, awe, reverence, humility, joy, purity, serving the sages, companionship with peers, debate among students, composure, knowledge of Scripture, knowledge of Mishnah, moderation in business, moderation in worldly affairs, moderation in pleasure, moderation in sleep, moderation in conversation, moderation in laughter, patience, a good heart, faith in the sages, acceptance of suffering.

**12** One who knows his place, who is happy with his lot, who makes a fence around his words, who does not claim credit for himself, who is beloved, who loves God, who loves mankind, who loves righteousness, who loves justice, who loves reproof, who distances himself from honor, who does not let his heart become swelled by his learning, who does not delight in rendering legal decisions, who bears the yoke with his fellow, who judges him favorably, who sets him on the path of truth, who sets him on the path of peace, who thinks deliberately in his study, who asks and answers, who listens and adds to his learning, who learns in order to teach and who learns in order to practice, who makes his teacher wiser, who is exact in his teaching, and who says a thing in the name of the one who said it.

**13** Thus you have learned: whoever says a thing in the name of the one who said it brings redemption to the world, as it is said: "And Esther told the king in the name of Mordecai."

**14** Great is Torah, for it gives life to those who practice it, in this world and in the World to Come.
