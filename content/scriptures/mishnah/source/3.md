---
title: "Mishnah - Chapter 3"
scripture: "mishnah"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Akavya ben Mahalalel says: Contemplate three things and you will not come to sin. Know from where you came, and to where you are going, and before whom you are destined to give an account and reckoning.

**2** From where did you come? From a putrid drop. To where are you going? To a place of dust, worms, and maggots. Before whom are you destined to give an account and reckoning? Before the King of kings, the Holy One, blessed be He.

**3** Rabbi Chanina, the deputy of the priests, says: Pray for the welfare of the government, for were it not for the fear of it, a man would swallow his neighbor alive.

**4** Rabbi Chananya ben Teradyon says: When two sit together and there are no words of Torah between them, this is a session of scoffers, as it is said: "He does not sit in the seat of scoffers." But when two sit together and there are words of Torah between them, the Divine Presence rests between them.

**5** Even one who sits and studies Torah alone — the Holy One, blessed be He, fixes a reward for him, as it is said: "He sits alone and in stillness, yet he takes it upon himself."

**6** Rabbi Shimon says: Three who ate at one table and did not speak words of Torah there — it is as if they ate of the sacrifices of the dead. But three who ate at one table and spoke words of Torah there — it is as if they ate from the table of God.

**7** Rabbi Chananya ben Chakinai says: He who keeps awake at night and he who walks alone on the road, and he who turns his heart to idleness — behold, this person forfeits his life.

**8** Rabbi Nechunyah ben Hakaneh says: Whoever takes upon himself the yoke of Torah — the yoke of government and the yoke of worldly cares are removed from him. But whoever throws off the yoke of Torah — the yoke of government and the yoke of worldly cares are placed upon him.

**9** Rabbi Chalafta ben Dosa of Kfar Chananya says: When ten sit together and study Torah, the Divine Presence rests among them. And from where do we know this applies even to five? Even to three? Even to two? Even to one?

**10** Rabbi Elazar of Bartota says: Give Him of what is His, for you and what is yours are His. As David said: "For all things come from You, and from Your own hand we have given You."

**11** Rabbi Yaakov says: He who walks on the road and studies, and interrupts his study and says: "How beautiful is this tree! How beautiful is this ploughed field!" — Scripture considers it as if he has forfeited his life.

**12** Rabbi Dostai bar Yannai in the name of Rabbi Meir says: Whoever forgets even a single thing from his study — Scripture considers it as if he has forfeited his life, as it is said: "Only take heed and guard your soul diligently, lest you forget the things which your eyes saw."

**13** Rabbi Chanina ben Dosa says: Anyone whose fear of sin precedes his wisdom — his wisdom endures. But anyone whose wisdom precedes his fear of sin — his wisdom does not endure.

**14** He used to say: Anyone whose deeds exceed his wisdom — his wisdom endures. Anyone whose wisdom exceeds his deeds — his wisdom does not endure.

**15** He used to say: Anyone in whom the spirit of one's fellows finds delight — the spirit of God finds delight in him. Anyone in whom the spirit of one's fellows does not find delight — the spirit of God does not find delight in him.

**16** Rabbi Dosa ben Harkinas says: Morning sleep, midday wine, children's chatter, and sitting in the assembly houses of the ignorant drive a person from the world.
