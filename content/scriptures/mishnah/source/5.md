---
title: "Mishnah - Chapter 5"
scripture: "mishnah"
chapter: 5
---

**1** By ten utterances the world was created. What does this teach? Could it not have been created by one utterance? Rather, this is in order to punish the wicked who destroy the world that was created by ten utterances, and to give good reward to the righteous who sustain the world that was created by ten utterances.

**2** There were ten generations from Adam to Noah — to make known how great is His patience, for all those generations kept provoking Him, until He brought upon them the waters of the flood.

**3** There were ten generations from Noah to Abraham — to make known how great is His patience, for all those generations kept provoking Him, until Abraham our father came and received the reward of them all.

**4** With ten trials our father Abraham was tried, and he withstood them all — to make known how great was the love of Abraham our father.

**5** Ten miracles were wrought for our fathers in Egypt, and ten at the sea. Ten plagues the Holy One brought upon the Egyptians in Egypt, and ten at the sea.

**6** With ten trials our ancestors tried the Holy One in the wilderness, as it is said: "They have tested me these ten times and have not heeded my voice."

**7** Ten miracles were wrought for our ancestors in the Temple: no woman miscarried from the scent of the holy meat; the holy meat never became putrid; no fly was ever seen in the slaughterhouse; the high priest never suffered a seminal emission on the Day of Atonement; the rains never extinguished the fire on the altar; the wind never prevailed over the column of smoke; no disqualifying defect was ever found in the Omer or in the Two Loaves or in the Showbread; the people stood pressed together yet bowed down with ample room; never did a serpent or scorpion injure anyone in Jerusalem; and no person ever said to his fellow: "The place is too narrow for me to lodge in Jerusalem."

**8** Ten things were created on the eve of the Sabbath at twilight: the mouth of the earth, the mouth of the well, the mouth of the donkey, the rainbow, the manna, the staff, the Shamir worm, the script, the writing, and the tablets.

**9** Some say: also the destructive spirits, and the burial place of Moses, and the ram of Abraham our father. And some say: also the first pair of tongs, made with tongs.

**10** There are seven characteristics of an unformed person, and seven of a wise person. A wise person does not speak before one who is greater than him in wisdom; does not interrupt the speech of his fellow; is not hasty to answer; asks what is relevant and answers to the point; speaks of the first point first and of the last point last; regarding that which he has not heard, he says: "I have not heard"; and he acknowledges the truth.

**11** The opposite of these characterize the unformed person.

**12** Seven kinds of punishment come to the world for seven categories of transgression. When some tithe and others do not, a famine from drought comes. When all resolve not to tithe, a famine from tumult and drought comes.

**13** There are four types of character among people: One who says "Mine is mine and yours is yours" — this is the average type; some say this is the type of Sodom. "Mine is yours and yours is mine" — this is an ignorant person. "Mine is yours and yours is yours" — this is a pious person. "Yours is mine and mine is mine" — this is a wicked person.

**14** There are four types of temperament: Easy to anger and easy to pacify — his gain is canceled by his loss. Hard to anger and hard to pacify — his loss is canceled by his gain. Hard to anger and easy to pacify — this is a pious person. Easy to anger and hard to pacify — this is a wicked person.
