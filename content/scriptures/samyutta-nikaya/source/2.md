---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 2"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 2
---

**1** At Savatthi. "Monks, I will teach you about the all. Listen carefully.

**2** "What is the all? The eye and forms, the ear and sounds, the nose and smells, the tongue and tastes, the body and tangible objects, the mind and mental objects.

**3** "This, monks, is called the all.

**4** "If anyone were to say: 'Having rejected this all, I shall make known another all' — that would be a mere empty boast on his part. If he were questioned, he would not be able to reply, and he would meet with vexation. For what reason? Because it is beyond his domain."

**5** "Monks, the eye is burning, forms are burning, eye-consciousness is burning, eye-contact is burning. Whatever feeling arises with eye-contact as condition — whether pleasant or painful or neither — that too is burning.

**6** "Burning with what? Burning with the fire of lust, with the fire of hatred, with the fire of delusion. Burning with birth, aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair, I say.

**7** "The ear is burning, sounds are burning. The nose is burning, smells are burning. The tongue is burning, tastes are burning. The body is burning, tangible objects are burning. The mind is burning, mental objects are burning.

**8** "Monks, seeing thus, the well-instructed noble disciple becomes disenchanted with the eye, disenchanted with forms, disenchanted with eye-consciousness, disenchanted with eye-contact.

**9** "He becomes disenchanted with whatever feeling arises with eye-contact as condition. He becomes disenchanted with the ear, with the nose, with the tongue, with the body, with the mind.

**10** "Being disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate. Through dispassion, he is liberated. When liberated, there comes the knowledge: 'It is liberated.'

**11** "He understands: 'Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this state of being.' "

**12** Thus spoke the Blessed One. The monks were delighted and approved of his words. And while this discourse was being delivered, the minds of all the monks were liberated from the taints by non-clinging.
