---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 7"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 7
---

**1** At Savatthi. The Blessed One said: "Monks, I will teach you about the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned. Listen to that.

**2** "And what, monks, is the unconditioned? The destruction of lust, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion — this is called the unconditioned.

**3** "And what, monks, is the path leading to the unconditioned? Mindfulness directed to the body — this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.

**4** "Serenity and insight — this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.

**5** "Concentration with thought and examination, concentration without thought but with examination, concentration without thought and without examination — this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.

**6** "The four foundations of mindfulness, the four right strivings, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, the Noble Eightfold Path — each of these is called the path leading to the unconditioned."

**7** "Monks, there is a not-born, a not-brought-to-being, a not-made, a not-conditioned. If there were no not-born, no not-brought-to-being, no not-made, no not-conditioned, no escape would be discerned from what is born, brought-to-being, made, conditioned.

**8** "But since there is a not-born, a not-brought-to-being, a not-made, a not-conditioned, therefore an escape is discerned from what is born, brought-to-being, made, conditioned."

**9** "Monks, there are these two elements of Nibbana. What two? The Nibbana element with residue remaining and the Nibbana element with no residue remaining.

**10** "The Nibbana element with residue remaining: herein a monk is an arahant, one whose taints are destroyed, who has lived the holy life, done what had to be done. His five sense faculties remain, and owing to their being intact, he still experiences what is agreeable and disagreeable.

**11** "His destruction of lust, hatred, and delusion is called the Nibbana element with residue remaining.

**12** "The Nibbana element with no residue remaining: herein a monk is an arahant. For him, all that is felt, not being delighted in, will become cool right here. This is called the Nibbana element with no residue remaining."

**13** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the monks were delighted.
