---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 9"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 9
---

**1** At Savatthi. "Monks, just as a hen who has eight or ten or twelve eggs, which she has properly covered, properly incubated, and properly maintained — even though this wish may not occur to her: 'Oh, that my chicks might pierce through the egg-shell with their claws and beaks and hatch safely!' — yet the chicks are capable of piercing through and hatching safely,

**2** "So too, when a monk has developed and cultivated the Noble Eightfold Path, even though this wish may not occur to him: 'Oh, that my mind might be liberated from the taints by non-clinging!' — yet his mind is liberated from the taints by non-clinging."

**3** "Monks, just as the river Ganges inclines towards the east, slopes towards the east, tends towards the east,

**4** "So too a monk who develops the seven factors of enlightenment inclines towards Nibbana, slopes towards Nibbana, tends towards Nibbana.

**5** "And what are the seven factors of enlightenment? The mindfulness factor of enlightenment, the investigation-of-states factor, the energy factor, the rapture factor, the tranquillity factor, the concentration factor, and the equanimity factor of enlightenment.

**6** "When the mindfulness factor of enlightenment is developed, it leads to the investigation of states. When states are investigated with wisdom, energy is aroused.

**7** "When energy is aroused, rapture arises. When the mind is uplifted by rapture, the body and mind become tranquil.

**8** "One who is tranquil and happy — his mind becomes concentrated. One whose mind is concentrated sees things with equanimity.

**9** "These seven factors of enlightenment, when developed and cultivated, lead to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, and to Nibbana."

**10** "Monks, just as dawn is the forerunner and precursor of the rising of the sun, so right view is the forerunner and precursor of wholesome states.

**11** "For a monk with right view, right intention arises. For one with right intention, right speech arises. For one with right speech, right action arises. For one with right action, right livelihood arises. For one with right livelihood, right effort arises. For one with right effort, right mindfulness arises. For one with right mindfulness, right concentration arises."

**12** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the monks were delighted.
