---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 3"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 3
---

**1** At Savatthi. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached the Blessed One and said:

**2** "Venerable sir, is there any one thing that, when maintained, secures both kinds of good — the good pertaining to this life and the good pertaining to the future life?"

**3** "There is one such thing, great king," the Blessed One replied. "It is diligence. Just as the footprints of all living beings that walk fit inside the footprint of the elephant, which is the greatest of footprints — so diligence is the one thing that secures both kinds of good."

**4** On another occasion King Pasenadi said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, today, while I was sitting in the judgement hall, I saw that even wealthy nobles, wealthy brahmins, and wealthy householders, who are rich, with great wealth and property, engage in speaking falsehood for the sake of sense pleasures."

**5** Then the Blessed One said: "So it is, great king. Those wealthy ones, rich, with great wealth, speak falsehood motivated by sensual desire, overcome by sensual desire, with minds obsessed by sensual desire. They will reap suffering for a long time."

**6** On another occasion the Blessed One said: "Great king, there are these four things that are dear and agreeable to a ruler. What four?

**7** "A kingdom is dear to a ruler. Life is dear to a ruler. His queen is dear to a ruler. His ministers are dear to a ruler.

**8** "A ruler who acts unjustly loses all four. A ruler who acts justly preserves all four.

**9** "Therefore, great king, you should train yourself thus: 'I will rule with righteousness. I will rule with justice. I will not let injustice prevail.'

**10** "The ruler who rules with righteousness attains happiness in this world and the next."

**11** Then King Pasenadi said: "Wonderful, venerable sir! It is as if what was overturned has been set upright, as if what was hidden has been revealed. I go for refuge to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha."

**12** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and King Pasenadi was delighted.
