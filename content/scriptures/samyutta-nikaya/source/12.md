---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 12"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 12
---

**1** At Savatthi. "Monks, I will teach you the Four Noble Truths. Listen carefully.

**2** "And what are the Four Noble Truths? The noble truth of suffering, the noble truth of the origin of suffering, the noble truth of the cessation of suffering, and the noble truth of the way leading to the cessation of suffering.

**3** "And what, monks, is the noble truth of suffering? Birth is suffering, aging is suffering, illness is suffering, death is suffering. Union with what is displeasing is suffering. Separation from what is pleasing is suffering. Not to get what one wants is suffering. In brief, the five aggregates subject to clinging are suffering.

**4** "And what is the noble truth of the origin of suffering? It is this craving which leads to renewed existence, accompanied by delight and lust, seeking delight here and there — that is, craving for sensual pleasures, craving for existence, craving for extermination.

**5** "And what is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering? It is the remainderless fading away and cessation of that same craving, the giving up and relinquishing of it, freedom from it, non-reliance on it.

**6** "And what is the noble truth of the way leading to the cessation of suffering? It is this Noble Eightfold Path — that is, right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration.

**7** " 'This is the noble truth of suffering' — thus, monks, in regard to things unheard before, there arose in me vision, knowledge, wisdom, true knowledge, and light.

**8** " 'This noble truth of suffering is to be fully understood' — thus there arose in me vision, knowledge, wisdom, true knowledge, and light.

**9** " 'This noble truth of suffering has been fully understood' — thus there arose in me vision, knowledge, wisdom, true knowledge, and light.

**10** "As long as my knowledge and vision of these Four Noble Truths, in their three phases and twelve aspects, was not thoroughly purified, I did not claim to have awakened to the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.

**11** "But when my knowledge and vision of these Four Noble Truths was thoroughly purified, then I claimed to have awakened to the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.

**12** "The knowledge and vision arose in me: 'Unshakable is the liberation of my mind. This is my last birth. There is now no further becoming.' "

**13** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the monks were delighted. And while this discourse was being spoken, there arose in the venerable Kondanna the dust-free, stainless vision of the Dhamma: "Whatever is subject to origination is all subject to cessation."

**14** And the devas of the earth raised a cry: "At Varanasi, in the Deer Park at Isipatana, the Blessed One has set in motion the unsurpassed Wheel of the Dhamma, which cannot be stopped by any recluse or brahmin or deva or Mara or Brahma or anyone in the world."

**15** Thus, on that occasion, the Blessed One uttered this inspired utterance: "Kondanna has indeed understood! Kondanna has indeed understood!" That is how the venerable Kondanna acquired the name Annata-Kondanna — "Kondanna Who Has Understood."
