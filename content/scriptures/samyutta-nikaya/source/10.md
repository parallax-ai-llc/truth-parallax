---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 10"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 10
---

**1** At Savatthi. "Monks, whatever beings there are — whether those born from eggs, born from a womb, born from moisture, or of spontaneous birth; with form or without form; percipient, non-percipient, or neither-percipient-nor-non-percipient — the Tathagata, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, is declared the best of all.

**2** "Monks, just as all the streams and rivers that flow into the great ocean lose their former names and identities and are reckoned simply as 'the great ocean,'

**3** "So too, monks, the members of the four castes — nobles, brahmins, merchants, and workers — who go forth from home to homelessness in this Dhamma and Discipline proclaimed by the Tathagata, abandon their former names and clans and are reckoned simply as 'recluses who are sons of the Sakyan.'

**4** "Monks, the great ocean has one taste — the taste of salt. In the same way, this Dhamma and Discipline has one taste — the taste of liberation."

**5** "Monks, dependent on the eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises. The meeting of the three is contact. With contact as condition, there is feeling. What one feels, one perceives. What one perceives, one thinks about. What one thinks about, one mentally proliferates.

**6** "With what one has mentally proliferated as the source, concepts characterized by mental proliferation beset a man with respect to past, future, and present forms cognizable through the eye. So too with ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind.

**7** "But when a monk abides contemplating rise and fall in these six bases of contact, he does not delight in them. Not delighting, he does not cling. Not clinging, he is liberated.

**8** "He understands: 'Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming back to any state of being.' "

**9** "Monks, all is burning. And what is the all that is burning? The eye is burning, forms are burning, eye-consciousness is burning.

**10** "Burning with what? Burning with the fire of lust, with the fire of hatred, with the fire of delusion; burning with birth, aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair.

**11** "Seeing thus, monks, the instructed noble disciple becomes disenchanted. Through disenchantment, he becomes dispassionate. Through dispassion, he is liberated."

**12** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the monks were delighted and liberated.
