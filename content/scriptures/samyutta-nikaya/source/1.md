---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 1"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in the Jeta Grove, in Anathapindika's Park.

**2** Then, when the night had advanced, a certain devatā of stunning beauty, illuminating the entire Jeta Grove, approached the Blessed One.

**3** Having approached, she paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the devatā addressed the Blessed One in verse:

**4** "Many devas and humans have pondered about blessings, desiring what is good. Tell me the highest blessing."

**5** The Blessed One replied: "Not to associate with fools, to associate with the wise, to honour those worthy of honour — this is the highest blessing.

**6** "To reside in a suitable locality, to have done meritorious actions in the past, and to set oneself in the right direction — this is the highest blessing.

**7** "Much learning, skill in handicraft, a highly trained discipline, and pleasant speech — this is the highest blessing.

**8** "Supporting one's father and mother, cherishing wife and children, and peaceful occupations — this is the highest blessing.

**9** "Generosity, righteous conduct, assistance to relatives, and blameless actions — this is the highest blessing.

**10** "To cease and abstain from evil, abstinence from intoxicating drink, and diligence in virtue — this is the highest blessing.

**11** "Reverence, humility, contentment, gratitude, and the timely hearing of the Dhamma — this is the highest blessing.

**12** "Patience, obedience, meeting with monks, and timely discussions on the Dhamma — this is the highest blessing.

**13** "Self-restraint, the holy life, insight into the Noble Truths, and the realization of Nibbana — this is the highest blessing.

**14** "A mind unshaken when touched by the worldly conditions, sorrowless, stainless, and secure — this is the highest blessing.

**15** "Those who have fulfilled all these are victorious everywhere, and they attain happiness everywhere — for them these are the highest blessings."

**16** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the devatā was delighted and departed.
