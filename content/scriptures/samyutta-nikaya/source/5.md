---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 5"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 5
---

**1** At Savatthi. "Monks, there was once a time when the river Ganges slanted, inclined, and tended towards the east.

**2** "Then a great crowd of people came with shovels and baskets, thinking: 'We will make this river Ganges slant, incline, and tend towards the west.'

**3** "What do you think, monks? Would that great crowd of people be able to make the river Ganges slant, incline, and tend towards the west?"

**4** "No, venerable sir."

**5** "And why not? The river Ganges naturally slants, inclines, and tends towards the east. It is not easy to make it tend towards the west. That crowd would only reap fatigue and vexation."

**6** "In the same way, monks, when a monk develops and cultivates the Noble Eightfold Path, he naturally slants, inclines, and tends towards Nibbana.

**7** "And how does a monk develop the Noble Eightfold Path? Here a monk develops right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration.

**8** "Each of these depends upon seclusion, depends upon dispassion, depends upon cessation, and ripens in release.

**9** "When a monk develops the Noble Eightfold Path, he naturally slants, inclines, and tends towards Nibbana, just as the Ganges naturally tends towards the east."

**10** At Savatthi. "Monks, just as the river Ganges slants towards the east, so too a monk who develops the four foundations of mindfulness slants towards Nibbana.

**11** "What are the four? Here, a monk dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and grief for the world.

**12** "He dwells contemplating feelings in feelings, mind in mind, and mind-objects in mind-objects, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and grief for the world.

**13** "When a monk develops these four foundations of mindfulness, he naturally slants, inclines, and tends towards Nibbana."

**14** Thus spoke the Blessed One, and the monks were delighted.
