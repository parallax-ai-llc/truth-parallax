---
title: "Samyutta Nikaya - Chapter 4"
scripture: "samyutta-nikaya"
chapter: 4
---

**1** At Savatthi. Then Mara the Evil One approached the Blessed One and addressed him in verse:

**2** "Why now do you live alone in the forest, in this remote dwelling? Have you lost something? Are you seeking friendship with someone?"

**3** The Blessed One replied: "Having cut through all attachments, having pulled out craving with its root, I am one who has attained peace. It is you who are defeated, End-maker."

**4** Then Mara the Evil One, realizing "The Blessed One knows me, the Fortunate One knows me," sad and disappointed, vanished right there.

**5** On another occasion, Mara approached the Blessed One and spoke: "It is a time for sorrow, not for meditation."

**6** The Blessed One replied: "There is no sorrow for one who has no expectations, who has nothing, who is free from hindrances, and who is mindful."

**7** Then Mara approached the Blessed One in another guise and said: "You have children, delight in your children! You have cattle, delight in your cattle! Possessions are delightful to a man."

**8** The Blessed One replied: "He who has children sorrows over children. He who has cattle sorrows over cattle. Attachments are truly a man's sorrows. He who has no attachments has no sorrow."

**9** Then Mara sent his three daughters — Craving, Discontent, and Lust — to distract the Blessed One.

**10** They approached the Blessed One in various beautiful forms, but the Blessed One sat unmoved, like a great rock unshaken by the wind.

**11** The daughters returned to Mara and said: "Father, the recluse Gotama cannot be tempted. He has transcended all craving. His mind is free, like the flight of a bird through the sky."

**12** Then Mara, defeated, vanished with his host, and the Blessed One remained in the bliss of liberation.
