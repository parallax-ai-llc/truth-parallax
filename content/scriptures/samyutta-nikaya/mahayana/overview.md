## Overview

Mahayana Buddhism recognizes the Samyutta Nikaya, through its Chinese parallel the Samyukta Agama, as preserving the doctrinal foundations upon which Mahayana philosophy rests. Nagarjuna's Madhyamaka philosophy explicitly draws on the dependent origination teachings of the Connected Discourses.

## Key Themes

Mahayana scholars find in the Samyutta Nikaya the seeds of the emptiness (sunyata) teaching. The dependent origination discourses demonstrate that all phenomena arise in dependence on conditions, lacking independent existence -- a principle that Nagarjuna develops into the full Madhyamaka analysis.

## Relevance to Mahayana

The Samyutta Nikaya's analysis of the aggregates, sense bases, and conditional relations provides the philosophical vocabulary and analytical framework upon which all subsequent Buddhist philosophy builds.
