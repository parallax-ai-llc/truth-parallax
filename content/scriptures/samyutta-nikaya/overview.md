## Overview

The Samyutta Nikaya ("Connected Discourses") is the third of the five nikayas of the Sutta Pitaka in the Pali Canon. It contains over 2,900 suttas organized into 56 samyuttas (connected groups) arranged thematically by subject matter. The collection is particularly important for its systematic treatment of core Buddhist doctrines including dependent origination, the five aggregates, the sense bases, and the factors of the Noble Eightfold Path.

## Structure

The 56 samyuttas are organized into 12 section groups across five vaggas:

1. **Sagatha Vagga (I)**: Verses with devas, Mara, and Brahma
2. **Sagatha Vagga (II)**: Verses with nuns, Brahmins, and kings
3. **Nidana Vagga (I)**: Dependent origination and its analysis
4. **Nidana Vagga (II)**: Elements, feeling, and the chain of causation
5. **Khandha Vagga (I)**: The five aggregates and their impermanence
6. **Khandha Vagga (II)**: Non-self and the clinging aggregates
7. **Salayatana Vagga (I)**: The six sense bases and sense experience
8. **Salayatana Vagga (II)**: Contact, feeling, and craving through the senses
9. **Maha Vagga (I)**: The Noble Eightfold Path and the factors of awakening
10. **Maha Vagga (II)**: The four foundations of mindfulness and right effort
11. **Maha Vagga (III)**: The powers, faculties, and factors of enlightenment
12. **Maha Vagga (IV)**: Stream-entry, the truths, and final discourses

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Samyutta Nikaya has a partial parallel in the Chinese Samyukta Agama. Its doctrinal content is foundational for all Buddhist scholastic traditions, providing the textual basis for Abhidhamma and Abhidharma analysis across Theravada and Mahayana schools.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Dependent Origination | The twelve-link chain of conditioned arising |
| Five Aggregates | Form, feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness |
| Six Sense Bases | How sense experience conditions craving and suffering |
| Noble Eightfold Path | The complete path from right view to right concentration |
| Impermanence | The transient nature of all conditioned phenomena |
