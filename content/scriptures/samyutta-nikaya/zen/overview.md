## Overview

Zen Buddhism values the Samyutta Nikaya's emphasis on direct insight into the nature of conditioned existence. The collection's analysis of how attachment arises through the senses and how liberation comes through seeing the impermanent, unsatisfactory, and non-self nature of all phenomena aligns with Zen's emphasis on direct realization.

## Zen Reading

Zen practitioners find in the Samyutta Nikaya's direct, analytical approach a complement to koan practice. The Buddha's systematic dismantling of self-view and his pointing to the unconditioned resonate with Zen's aim of breaking through conceptual thinking to direct experience.

## Relevance to Practice

The collection's teachings on the sense bases and the arising of craving through contact inform the Zen practice of moment-to-moment awareness and non-attachment.
