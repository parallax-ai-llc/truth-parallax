## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Samyutta Nikaya's doctrinal content provides fascinating material for cross-traditional philosophical analysis. The collection's treatment of causation, selfhood, and the path to liberation engages with questions addressed by every major philosophical and religious tradition.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Causation Compared

The Samyutta Nikaya's dependent origination can be compared with Aristotelian causation, Humean regularity theory, process philosophy, and Jain karma theory, revealing distinctive Buddhist contributions to the philosophy of causation.

### Self and Non-Self

The collection's non-self analysis invites comparison with Hindu atman theory, Jain jiva doctrine, Christian concepts of the soul, and contemporary discussions of personal identity in Western philosophy.

## Comparative Framework

| Concept | Buddhism (SN) | Hinduism | Western Philosophy |
|---------|--------------|----------|--------------------|
| Causation | Dependent origination | Divine will/karma | Efficient causation |
| Self | Non-self (anatta) | Atman/Brahman | Substance/bundle theory |
| Liberation | Nibbana | Moksha | Eudaimonia |
