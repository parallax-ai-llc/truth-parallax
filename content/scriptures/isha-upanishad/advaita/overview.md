## Overview

Shankara's commentary on the Isha Upanishad is among his most important works, establishing the text as a foundational source for Advaita Vedanta. He reads the Upanishad as teaching the sole reality of Brahman and the illusory nature of the phenomenal world. The opening verse — "All this is pervaded by the Lord" — means that Brahman alone is real, and the world of names and forms is a superimposition (*adhyasa*) upon it.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara's reading of verse 1 — "Enjoy through renunciation; do not covet" — establishes the Advaita principle that liberation comes through knowledge-born detachment, not through action. The famous paradoxes of verses 4-5 (the self moves and does not move) describe Brahman from the empirical and absolute standpoints respectively. The teaching on *vidya* and *avidya* (verses 9-14) is interpreted as distinguishing between higher knowledge of Brahman and lower ritual knowledge, both of which must be transcended.
