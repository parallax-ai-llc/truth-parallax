## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Isha Upanishad as a revelation of Vishnu's all-pervading lordship. The opening declaration — "All this is pervaded by the Lord (*Isha*)" — is understood as referring to Vishnu or Narayana, who is both the immanent sustainer and the transcendent controller of all creation. The Upanishad's call to enjoy the world through renunciation is read as an invitation to live in awareness of God's ownership of all things.

## Theological Emphases

Baladeva Vidyabhushana and other Gaudiya Vaishnava commentators emphasize that the Isha Upanishad teaches neither world-negation nor world-affirmation but God-centered engagement. The world belongs to the Lord (verse 1), and true renunciation means recognizing this divine ownership rather than claiming anything as one's own. The prayers to Surya and Agni in the closing verses (15-18) are read as prayers to Vishnu in His solar and fiery manifestations.
