Kashmir Shaiva interpreters read the Isha Upanishad's 18 verses as a compact expression of non-dual Shaiva philosophy. The opening verse — "All this is pervaded by the Lord" — declares that Paramashiva, the supreme consciousness, is the substance and reality of everything that exists. Unlike dualistic readings that separate God from the world, the Shaiva interpretation sees the world as Shiva's own body — His creative self-expression through the power of Shakti.

The paradoxes of verses 4-8 are particularly resonant with Kashmir Shaivism's understanding of consciousness as simultaneously static (*prakasha*, luminosity) and dynamic (*vimarsha*, self-awareness). The self that "moves and does not move" is consciousness in its dual aspect of transcendent stillness and immanent activity. The reconciliation of knowledge and ignorance in verses 9-14 aligns with the Shaiva teaching that liberation is not the rejection of the world but the recognition of its divine ground — seeing *samsara* and *nirvana* as one.
