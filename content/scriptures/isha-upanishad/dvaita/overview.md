## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita reading of the Isha Upanishad emphasizes God's sovereign lordship over creation as the text's central teaching. The "Lord" (*Isha*) is Vishnu, who pervades all things as their controller and sustainer while remaining eternally distinct from them. The Upanishad's opening verse is not a statement of identity between God and the world but of God's complete ownership and governance.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the Isha Upanishad's teaching to "enjoy through renunciation" means recognizing that all things belong to God and should be used in His service. The paradoxes of verses 4-8 describe God's simultaneous transcendence and immanence — He is far (transcendent) and near (immanent as the inner controller). The *vidya/avidya* teaching (verses 9-14) is read as distinguishing between knowledge of God's nature and knowledge of the material world, both of which are necessary for the devotee.
