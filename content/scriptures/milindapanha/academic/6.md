Book 6 of the Milindapanha addresses the similes — extended analogies illustrating the qualities of the ideal Buddhist practitioner, which has attracted significant scholarly attention from multiple disciplinary perspectives. Academic study of this section examines its literary structure, philosophical arguments, historical context, and relationship to the broader Pali literary tradition. Scholars also investigate the text's evidence for Hellenistic-Buddhist cultural exchange, looking for traces of Greek philosophical methods in Nagasena's argumentation.

Key academic questions concerning the use of vivid similes to describe the virtues and practices of the accomplished Buddhist monk include the text's compositional date and history, the historical reliability of its portrayal of King Milinda (Menander I), and the extent to which Nagasena's philosophical arguments reflect genuine engagement with Greek logical and rhetorical traditions. The text's sophisticated use of analogy and thought experiment has attracted particular attention from philosophers, who find in it a rigorous argumentative method that deserves serious philosophical engagement.
