## Overview

Tibetan Buddhism engages with the Milindapanha primarily through its philosophical arguments and dialectical method, which resonate with the Tibetan tradition's own emphasis on structured philosophical debate (*rtsod pa*). While the text does not hold the same canonical status in the Tibetan tradition as it does in Theravada countries, its arguments on non-self, rebirth, and dependent origination are studied as exemplary instances of Buddhist philosophical reasoning.

The Tibetan scholastic tradition is particularly interested in the Milindapanha's treatment of rebirth without a transmigrating self — a topic that occupies a central place in Tibetan philosophical education and is addressed in the *tenets systems* (*grub mtha'*) curriculum. Nagasena's analogies (the flame passing from candle to candle, milk transforming into curds) are studied alongside the more technically elaborate treatments found in Vasubandhu's Abhidharmakosa and Nagarjuna's Mulamadhyamakakarika as complementary approaches to this fundamental Buddhist teaching.
