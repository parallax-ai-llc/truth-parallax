Book 3 of the Milindapanha addresses the cutting off of puzzles — the resolution of philosophical dilemmas about Buddhist doctrine, which the Tibetan tradition engages with through its own rich dialectical heritage. Tibetan monastic education places great emphasis on philosophical debate (*rtsod pa*), and the Milindapanha's dialogue format provides an early model of the kind of rigorous, adversarial philosophical exchange that characterizes Tibetan Buddhist scholarship.

The Tibetan engagement with Nagasena's resolution of apparent paradoxes and difficult questions in Buddhist teaching situates Nagasena's arguments within the broader framework of the *grub mtha'* (tenets systems) — the hierarchical presentation of Buddhist philosophical schools. Nagasena's positions are typically classified as representing the Sautrantika or Vaibhashika perspective, providing a foundation that is then refined and transcended by the Madhyamaka and Yogacara analyses. The Milindapanha thus serves as an introduction to philosophical reasoning in the Tibetan curriculum, demonstrating the power of clear analysis to dispel confusion about basic Buddhist teachings.
