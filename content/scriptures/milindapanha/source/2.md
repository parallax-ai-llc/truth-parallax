---
title: "Milindapanha - Chapter 2"
scripture: "milindapanha"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The king said: "Venerable Nagasena, will you converse with me again?" Nagasena replied: "If your Majesty will converse as a wise man converses, I will; but if your Majesty converses as a king converses, I will not."

**2** "How is it then that wise men converse?" asked the king. "When wise men talk together, O king, there is a winding up and an unwinding; one or the other is convicted of error, and he admits his mistake, and yet thereby they are not angered."

**3** "And how is it that kings converse?" "When a king talks, he maintains one position, and whoever contradicts him, the king has him punished." The king said: "Very well, then. I shall converse as a scholar, not as a king."

**4** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, what is the purpose of your going forth? What is the final goal?" Nagasena replied: "Our going forth is for the purpose that this suffering may cease and that no further suffering may arise. The final goal is Nibbana without remainder."

**5** "Is it the case that everyone who goes forth attains Nibbana?" "No, not everyone. But he who walks rightly, who knows what should be known, who gives up what should be given up, who develops what should be developed, who realizes what should be realized — he attains Nibbana."

**6** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, is there anyone who is reborn after death?" Nagasena replied: "He who has defilements is reborn; he who has no defilements is not reborn."

**7** "Is it the same being that is reborn?" "Neither the same nor another."

**8** "Give me an illustration." "What do you think, O king? Are you the same Milinda who existed as a child, a tender little baby?" "No, venerable sir. The little baby was one, and I am another."

**9** "If that is so, then you have had no mother, no father, no teacher! Is the student one person and the graduate another? Is the one who commits a crime one person and the one who is punished another?"

**10** "No, venerable sir. But what would you say to that?" Nagasena replied: "I was a tender baby, and now I am grown up. All these are united in one body by means of this body."

**11** "Give me an illustration." "It is as if a man were to light a lamp — would it burn all night?" "Yes." "Is the flame of the first watch the same as the flame of the middle watch?" "No." "Is the flame of the middle watch the same as that of the last watch?" "No."

**12** "Then is there one lamp in the first watch, another in the middle watch, and yet another in the last watch?" "No, it is through connection with one lamp that the light shines all through the night."

**13** "In exactly the same way, O king, the elements of being are connected in succession, one arising and another ceasing, proceeding as it were without intermission. Therefore neither as the same nor as another does a man arrive at the last stage of consciousness."

**14** "Wonderful, Nagasena! Give me another illustration." "It is like milk, O king. Fresh milk in due time turns to curds, from curds to butter, from butter to ghee. It would not be right to say that the ghee was the same as the milk, but they have proceeded from it."

**15** The king said: "You are clever, venerable Nagasena."
