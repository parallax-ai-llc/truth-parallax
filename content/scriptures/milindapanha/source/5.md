---
title: "Milindapanha - Chapter 5"
scripture: "milindapanha"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, what is the root of past karma? What is the root of future karma? What is the root of present karma?"

**2** Nagasena replied: "Ignorance is the root of past karma, of future karma, and of present karma. Through ignorance arise the activities which produce rebirth."

**3** "Venerable Nagasena, you say that fire in hell is much more fierce than ordinary fire. You also say that a small stone cast into hell-fire would be consumed in a moment, but that beings born in hell are not consumed though they burn for ages. This I do not believe."

**4** Nagasena replied: "What do you think, O king? Do not the female sea-monsters and crocodiles and turtles and peacocks and pigeons eat stones and pebbles?" "Yes, they do."

**5** "But are those stones and pebbles consumed in the bellies of those animals?" "Yes, they are." "But is the embryo in the womb also consumed?" "No, venerable sir." "Why not?" "Because of the power of karma, O king."

**6** "In the same way, beings born in hell are not consumed even though they burn for ages — because of the power of karma. They die only when their evil karma is exhausted."

**7** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, how far is it from here to the Brahma world?" "Very far, O king. If a rock the size of a tower were to fall from the Brahma world, it would take four months to reach the earth, falling forty-eight thousand leagues a day."

**8** "But you say that a monk who has developed the power of mind can travel there in an instant, even as quickly as a strong man might stretch out his arm. This I find hard to believe."

**9** "What is your native city, O king?" "Alasanda." "How far is it from here?" "About eight hundred leagues." "Do you recall some deed you did there?" "Yes, I do." "How quickly did you recall it?" "In an instant, venerable sir."

**10** "So you have traveled eight hundred leagues in an instant! In the same way, a monk who has developed the power of mind can travel to the Brahma world in an instant."

**11** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, by what is it that people are able to recollect what was done long ago?" "By memory, O king."

**12** "Is it not rather by the mind that we recollect?" "Do you not sometimes forget things and then recall them later?" "Yes." "Were you then without mind when you forgot?" "No, but the memory failed me."

**13** "Then it is memory, not mind, by which we recollect." "You are right, venerable Nagasena."
