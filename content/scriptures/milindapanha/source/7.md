---
title: "Milindapanha - Chapter 7"
scripture: "milindapanha"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, does the Buddha still exist?" Nagasena replied: "Yes, O king, the Blessed One still exists."

**2** "Can you point out the Buddha?" "The Blessed One has passed into Nibbana by the Nibbana element in which no residual aggregates remain. It is not possible to point out the Blessed One."

**3** "Give me an illustration." "What do you think, O king? When a great fire is burning, is it possible to point out a flame that has gone out, saying 'that flame is here or there'?" "No, that flame has ceased; it cannot be pointed out." "In the same way, the Blessed One has passed away. He cannot be pointed out."

**4** "But the Blessed One can be pointed out by means of the body of the Dhamma. For the Dhamma was taught by the Blessed One."

**5** The king asked: "Is the practice of the holy life possible without having met the Buddha in person?" "Yes, O king. The practice is possible because the teaching remains."

**6** "How so?" "What do you think, O king? Is it possible for the students of a master who has died to practice his art, even though they never met him personally?" "Yes, if the tradition of his teaching has been preserved."

**7** "In the same way, though the Blessed One has passed away, his teaching remains. And those who practice according to his teaching can attain the highest goal."

**8** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, is the Buddha matchless?" "Yes, O king, the Blessed One is matchless."

**9** "But how can you know he is matchless if you have never met him?" "What do you think, O king? Could those who have never seen the ocean know that the ocean is great and vast and deep, full of water, and that its waters do not overflow?"

**10** "Yes, they could know it by the rivers that flow into it." "In the same way, seeing the excellence of the Dhamma, I know: the Blessed One is matchless, supreme, unequalled."

**11** The king said: "Venerable Nagasena, you have answered all my questions with wisdom and clarity. I am convinced. I take refuge in the Buddha, in the Dhamma, and in the Sangha."

**12** "From today let the venerable Nagasena accept me as a lay follower who has taken refuge, from this day forth, for as long as life endures."

**13** Then King Milinda, having found the truth, having crossed beyond doubt, having been freed from uncertainty, having gained confidence in the teaching of the Blessed One, said:

**14** "Well spoken, venerable Nagasena! The knots of my perplexity have been untied. The darkness of my doubt has been dispelled. There is no other teacher like unto you, save the Blessed One himself."

**15** Thus concludes the Questions of Milinda, the dialogue between King Milinda and the Elder Nagasena, for the illumination of the Dhamma.
