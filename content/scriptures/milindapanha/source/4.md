---
title: "Milindapanha - Chapter 4"
scripture: "milindapanha"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, is the body dear to those who have gone forth?" Nagasena replied: "No, O king, the body is not dear to them."

**2** "Then why do they nourish the body and care for it?" "Has your Majesty ever been wounded in battle?" "Yes, venerable sir." "Was the wound treated?" "Yes, it was treated." "Was the wound dear to you?" "No, it was not dear to me."

**3** "If the wound was not dear, why did you have it treated?" "Not because it was dear, but so that the flesh might grow up again."

**4** "In the same way, O king, the body is not dear to those who have gone forth. But without clinging to the body, they take care of it for the purpose of maintaining the holy life. The body is like a wound, and one cares for it without attachment."

**5** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, is there such a thing as the soul?" Nagasena replied: "What is the soul in your conception, O king?"

**6** "The living principle within, that sees, hears, experiences, and knows — the being that acts and directs."

**7** "If the soul sees through the eye, could it not also see through the ear? Could it hear through the eye? Could it smell through the tongue?" "No, venerable sir."

**8** "Then, O king, the living principle within does not experience freely through any sense. If a man were to make an opening in the wall of a granary, would the grain come out through every opening?"

**9** "Yes, through whichever opening was made." "But the soul does not see through every door. It sees through the eye, it hears through the ear. Therefore, O king, the conscious being within is not the same as the 'soul' of which you speak."

**10** The king asked: "Is there such a thing as fate?" "No, O king. Some things are produced by karma, some by causes, some by the seasons, and some arise of themselves."

**11** "Give me an illustration." "Why are all beings not alike? Some are short-lived, some long-lived. Some are sickly, some healthy. Some are ugly, some beautiful." "Because of the difference in their karma, O king."

**12** "But the heat of a fire, the flowing of water, the blowing of wind — are these produced by karma?" "No, O king. These arise by reason of causes and conditions." "So not everything is determined by karma." "No, not everything."

**13** The king said: "Excellent, Nagasena. You have made it clear."
