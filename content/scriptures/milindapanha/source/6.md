---
title: "Milindapanha - Chapter 6"
scripture: "milindapanha"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, what is Nibbana?" Nagasena replied: "The cessation of suffering, O king. Where there is no more arising, no more passing away, no more birth, no more death — that is Nibbana."

**2** "But is there such a place, venerable Nagasena?" "Nibbana exists, O king, but it is not a place. It cannot be pointed to in terms of location or direction."

**3** "Give me an illustration." "Does wind exist, O king?" "Yes, it exists." "Please show me the wind — its color, shape, whether it is thick or thin, long or short." "It cannot be shown in those terms. Yet the wind exists."

**4** "In the same way, O king, Nibbana exists. But it cannot be shown by color or shape."

**5** "But can you describe some quality of Nibbana by which it can be recognized?" "Yes, O king. As a lotus is not soiled by the water in which it grows, Nibbana is not soiled by any defilement. This is one quality of the lotus found in Nibbana."

**6** "As water cools the fever of burning, Nibbana cools the fever of all defilements. This is a quality of water found in Nibbana."

**7** "As medicine is the refuge of beings afflicted by poison, Nibbana is the refuge of beings afflicted by the poison of defilements. This is a quality of medicine found in Nibbana."

**8** "As the ocean is empty of corpses, Nibbana is empty of the corpses of defilements. As the ocean is vast and limitless and cannot be filled by all the rivers, Nibbana is vast and limitless and cannot be filled by all beings."

**9** "As space is not born and does not grow old, does not die and does not arise, does not pass from one state to another — so Nibbana is not born, does not grow old, does not die, does not arise, and does not pass from one state to another."

**10** "As the peak of a mountain cannot be shaken by the wind, Nibbana cannot be shaken by any circumstance."

**11** "As food sustains the life of all beings, Nibbana, when realized, sustains the life of the noble ones. And as Nibbana puts an end to all suffering, this is a quality that it shares with nothing else."

**12** The king said: "Wonderful, venerable Nagasena! The nature of Nibbana has been made clear by these beautiful illustrations. I am satisfied."
