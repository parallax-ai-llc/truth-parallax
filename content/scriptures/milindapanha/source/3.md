---
title: "Milindapanha - Chapter 3"
scripture: "milindapanha"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The king said: "Venerable Nagasena, what is it that is reborn?" "Name-and-form is reborn, O king."

**2** "Is it this same name-and-form that is reborn?" "No, it is not this same name-and-form that is reborn, but with this name-and-form one does a deed — good or evil — and by reason of that deed another name-and-form is reborn."

**3** "If that is so, would not one be freed from one's evil deeds?" Nagasena replied: "Yes, if one were not reborn. But since one is reborn, one is not freed."

**4** "Give me an illustration." "Suppose a man were to steal a mango from another man's tree. Would the owner of the mangoes have a case against him? If the thief were to say: 'The mangoes I took were not the same mangoes that you planted; I am not liable' — would that plea be valid?"

**5** "Certainly not, because the mangoes he took were the growth of those that had been planted." "In the same way, O king, with this name-and-form one does a deed, and by reason of that deed, another name-and-form is reborn. Therefore one is not freed from one's evil deeds."

**6** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, what is the distinguishing mark of attention?" "Apperception, O king, and selection." "Give me an illustration."

**7** "It is as the storekeeper of the king's granary shows the king the contents: 'So much rice, so much millet, so much barley, so much pulse.' In the same way, attention apperceives and selects among mental objects."

**8** "What is the mark of consciousness?" "Knowing, O king." "Give me an illustration." "It is as the watchman of a city, sitting at the crossroads in the middle of the city, would see a man coming from the east, another from the west, one from the north, one from the south. In the same way consciousness knows what enters through any of the six doors of the senses."

**9** "What is the mark of wisdom?" "Cutting off, O king, and illumination." "How does it cut off?" "It cuts off the defilements." "How does it illuminate?" "It produces the light of knowledge."

**10** "Give me an illustration." "It is as if a man were to bring a lamp into a dark house. The lamp would dispel the darkness and produce light, making the objects visible. In the same way, wisdom dispels the darkness of ignorance and produces the light of knowledge."

**11** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, where does wisdom dwell?" "Nowhere, O king." "Then there is no such thing as wisdom." "Where does the wind dwell, O king?" "Nowhere, venerable sir." "Then there is no such thing as wind."

**12** "You are clever, venerable Nagasena."
