Shaiva interpreters read Chapter 7's distinction between higher and lower natures as corresponding to the Shaiva categories of Shiva (pure consciousness) and Shakti (dynamic creative power). The "lower nature" of eight material elements (7.4) corresponds to Shakti's manifest forms, while the "higher nature" (7.5) is consciousness itself — the life force animating all beings.

The chapter's teaching that the deluded do not recognize the divine because of maya's three gunas (7.13-14) aligns with the Kashmir Shaiva concept of *mahamaya* — the great power of concealment that makes the infinite appear finite. However, unlike Advaita which treats maya as ultimately unreal, Shaivism regards it as a genuine power (*shakti*) of consciousness, integral to its creative self-expression.
