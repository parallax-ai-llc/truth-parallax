The Shaiva reading of Chapter 17 on three types of faith sees *shraddha* not merely as belief but as the fundamental orientation of consciousness. Sattvic faith represents consciousness oriented toward self-recognition, rajasic faith toward the pursuit of power and experience, and tamasic faith toward inertia and delusion.

The chapter's analysis of food, sacrifice, austerity, and charity in their threefold aspects provides a practical framework for recognizing the gunas' operation in daily life. Shaiva interpreters note that even these categories must ultimately be transcended. The sacred syllables "Om Tat Sat" (17.23) are read as pointing to the three aspects of Paramashiva: Om as the vibrating source of manifestation, Tat as transcendence, and Sat as the reality that encompasses both.
