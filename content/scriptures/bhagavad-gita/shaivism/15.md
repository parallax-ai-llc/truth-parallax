The cosmic *ashvattha* tree described in Chapter 15 receives a distinctive interpretation in Shaiva thought. Rather than seeing it merely as an illustration of samsara to be cut down, Shaivas understand it as a representation of Shakti's creative unfolding — the tree of manifestation rooted in supreme consciousness (Shiva) with branches extending into the diverse phenomenal world.

The teaching on the Supreme Person (*Purushottama*) who is beyond both the perishable and imperishable (15.16-18) aligns with the Kashmir Shaiva concept of Paramashiva — the absolute that transcends the distinction between Shiva (transcendent consciousness) and Shakti (immanent power). Abhinavagupta reads this chapter as the culmination of the Gita's metaphysical teaching, establishing the ultimate non-dual reality that includes yet exceeds all dualities.
