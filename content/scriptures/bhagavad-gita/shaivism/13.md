Chapter 13's distinction between the field (*kshetra*) and the knower of the field (*kshetrajna*) maps precisely onto Shaiva categories. The field is *Prakriti* (manifest nature), and the knower is consciousness (*Purusha/Shiva*). However, Kashmir Shaivism adds a crucial element: the knower is not merely a passive witness but the active source and sustainer of the field through its power (*Shakti*).

The chapter's teaching that one Knower pervades all fields (13.3) is read by Shaivas as affirming the non-dual nature of consciousness — there is only one experiencer appearing as many. The list of qualities constituting "knowledge" (13.8-12) describes the progressive purification of awareness that allows the aspirant to recognize what has always been true: that they are the universal Knower, Shiva Himself.
