The Shaiva interpretation of Karma Yoga in Chapter 3 emphasizes that action performed with full awareness of one's divine nature is itself a form of liberation, not merely a preparation for it. Kashmir Shaivism teaches that the universe is Shiva's creative expression (*abhasa*), and therefore engaging in worldly action can be a direct participation in divine creativity.

Krishna's teaching that the entire cosmos operates through the interplay of the three gunas (3.27-28) aligns with the Shaiva understanding of Shakti's dynamic manifestation. The wise person, recognizing this, acts without ego — not because action is inferior to knowledge, but because enlightened action is identical with the freedom of consciousness. Desire (*kama*) and anger (*krodha*), identified as enemies in 3.37, are understood as contracted forms of divine will (*iccha shakti*).
