Chapter 4's teaching on divine incarnation is reinterpreted in Shaiva thought as the perpetual self-revelation of consciousness. While Vaishnavas understand avatara as the descent of a personal God, Shaivas read it as the universal principle of consciousness manifesting whenever recognition is needed. Shiva, as absolute consciousness, is always already present — the "descent" is the removal of obscuring veils.

The chapter's teaching that ancient sages attained liberation through knowledge (4.15) and that the fire of knowledge burns all karma (4.37) strongly resonates with Kashmir Shaivism's emphasis on *jnana* as recognition (*pratyabhijna*). The various sacrifices described (4.24-33) are read as progressive modes of offering limited identity into the fire of universal awareness.
