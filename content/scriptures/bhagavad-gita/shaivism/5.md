The Shaiva reading of Chapter 5 finds in it a perfect expression of the non-dual relation between action and renunciation. For Kashmir Shaivism, true renunciation is not the abandonment of action but the abandonment of the false sense of limited agency. The enlightened being described in 5.8-9 — who knows "I do nothing at all" while seeing, hearing, and acting — embodies the Shaiva ideal of *karmamukti* (liberation within action).

Verse 5.18, describing the sage who sees equally a learned brahmin, a cow, an elephant, a dog, and an outcaste, is read as expressing the Shaiva vision of consciousness (*chit*) as the one reality underlying all differentiated forms. This equal vision is not an ethical prescription but a natural consequence of recognizing Paramashiva in all manifestation.
