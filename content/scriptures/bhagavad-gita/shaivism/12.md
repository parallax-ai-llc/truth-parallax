The Shaiva approach to Chapter 12's teaching on bhakti reads devotion not as the worship of an external deity but as the heart's natural orientation toward its own deeper nature. The question of whether to worship the formed or formless (12.1) is resolved in Shaiva thought by recognizing that form and formlessness are two aspects of the same reality — Shiva is both *sakala* (with form) and *nishkala* (formless).

The qualities of the ideal devotee described in 12.13-20 — free from enmity, compassionate, without ego or possessiveness — are read as natural expressions of the state of recognition (*pratyabhijna*). When one recognizes oneself as universal consciousness, these qualities arise spontaneously rather than through effort. The devotee is "dear to Me" because there is, ultimately, no separation between the devotee and the Divine.
