Abhinavagupta's reading of Chapter 18 synthesizes all the Gita's teachings into the Shaiva principle of recognition (*pratyabhijna*). The chapter's comprehensive treatment of renunciation, duty, the gunas, and knowledge is understood as describing the progressive stages by which consciousness recognizes its own unlimited nature through engagement with — not withdrawal from — the world.

The climactic verse 18.66 ("Abandon all dharmas and surrender to Me") is read by Shaiva interpreters not as submission to an external deity but as the dissolution of all limited self-definitions into the infinite freedom of consciousness. "Me" is Paramashiva — one's own deepest nature. Krishna's final injunction to Arjuna to act from this recognition (18.73) represents the Shaiva ideal: enlightened engagement with the world as divine play (*lila*), rooted in the unshakeable recognition of one's identity with the absolute.
