The Shaiva reading of Chapter 8 emphasizes consciousness as the imperishable (*akshara*) Brahman that underlies all states and transitions, including death. The teaching on remembering the Divine at the time of death (8.5-8) is connected to the Shaiva practice of maintaining awareness through all states — waking, dreaming, deep sleep, and the transition of death.

Kashmir Shaivism's understanding of *turiya* (the fourth state) and *turiyatita* (beyond the fourth) provides a framework for understanding 8.20-21: the unmanifest beyond the unmanifest. This supreme abode is not a distant heaven but the ever-present ground of consciousness, accessible through recognition (*pratyabhijna*) rather than physical departure from the world.
