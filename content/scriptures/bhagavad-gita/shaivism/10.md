The divine glories (*vibhutis*) enumerated in Chapter 10 are read by Shaiva interpreters as illustrations of how supreme consciousness (*chit*) manifests as every form of excellence in the phenomenal world. Each vibhuti — from Vishnu among the Adityas to the Himalayas among mountains — reveals Shiva's creative power expressing itself through particular forms.

Abhinavagupta emphasizes that these vibhutis are not external possessions of God but modes of His self-expression. Recognizing divine glory in the world's beauty, power, and splendor is itself a spiritual practice — a form of *shambhavopaya* (Shiva's method) where awareness spontaneously recognizes its own creative manifestation. The chapter culminates in Krishna's statement that He sustains the entire universe with a single fragment of Himself (10.42), which Shaivas read as expressing Shiva's inexhaustible creative power.
