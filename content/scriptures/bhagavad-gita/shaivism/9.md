Chapter 9's teaching that God pervades the entire universe while remaining transcendent (9.4-6) expresses what Kashmir Shaivism calls *vishvottirna-vishvamaya* — the paradox of being both the universe and beyond it simultaneously. Shiva is the totality of manifestation yet exceeds it infinitely.

The Shaiva reading of 9.26 — the offering of a leaf, flower, fruit, or water — interprets this not merely as devotional worship but as the recognition that every act of offering is consciousness offering itself to itself. The chapter's universalism (9.29: "I am equal to all beings") is read as the foundational Shaiva principle that consciousness does not discriminate — it is equally present in all things as their very essence.
