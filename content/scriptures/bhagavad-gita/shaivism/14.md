The three gunas described in Chapter 14 are understood in Shaiva thought as expressions of Shakti's creative power operating at the material level. Sattva, rajas, and tamas are not alien forces but modes of consciousness's own self-limitation. They create the appearance of bondage by veiling (*tamas*), projecting (*rajas*), and revealing (*sattva*) aspects of the one reality.

The Shaiva reading of 14.19-20 — transcending the gunas and attaining the state beyond birth, death, and suffering — connects directly to the concept of *turiya*, the fourth state of consciousness that underlies and transcends waking, dreaming, and deep sleep. For the Shaiva, transcending the gunas is not escaping nature but recognizing that one was never truly bound by it.
