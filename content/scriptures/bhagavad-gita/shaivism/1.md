In the Shaiva reading, particularly through Abhinavagupta's *Gitarthasangraha*, Arjuna's despair represents the universal human condition of spiritual ignorance (*ajnana*). The battlefield is understood not merely as a historical setting but as the inner field of consciousness where conflicting tendencies must be confronted and transcended. Arjuna's paralysis mirrors the aspirant's confusion when facing the demands of self-transformation.

Abhinavagupta interprets the Kaurava and Pandava armies as symbolic of binding and liberating forces within consciousness. Arjuna's attachment to family and teachers represents the deep-seated impressions (*samskaras*) that bind the individual to limited identity. The chapter establishes the dramatic necessity for divine instruction — the awakening of supreme consciousness that will unfold through Krishna's teaching.
