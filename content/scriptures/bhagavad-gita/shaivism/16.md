Shaiva interpreters read Chapter 16's contrast between divine and demonic natures not as a moral dualism but as a description of expanded versus contracted consciousness. The divine nature (*daivi sampat*) represents consciousness aware of its infinite nature, while the demonic (*asuri sampat*) represents consciousness identified with limitation, craving, and control.

The three gates to hell — lust, anger, and greed (16.21) — are understood as extreme contractions of consciousness's own natural powers: desire (*iccha*), knowledge (*jnana*), and action (*kriya*). In their pure, uncontracted form, these are Shiva's own powers. Liberation does not require destroying desire but recognizing its divine source and allowing it to function freely as creative expression.
