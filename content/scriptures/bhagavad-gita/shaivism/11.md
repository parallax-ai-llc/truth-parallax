The vision of the universal form (*vishvarupa*) in Chapter 11 is among the most significant passages for Shaiva interpretation. Abhinavagupta reads this not as a unique historical event but as a representation of the aspirant's direct experience of universal consciousness — seeing the entire cosmos as the body of the Divine. The terror and wonder Arjuna experiences reflect the overwhelming nature of non-dual recognition.

The Shaiva reading notes that Arjuna's subsequent preference for Krishna's personal form (11.45-46) represents the human need for a relatable form of the absolute. However, Shaivas emphasize that the universal and personal forms are equally valid expressions of consciousness — neither superior nor inferior. The ultimate recognition is that both forms, and indeed all forms, are manifestations of the one Paramashiva.
