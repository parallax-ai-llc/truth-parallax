Chapter 17's analysis of three types of faith maps onto a comparative study of religious sincerity across traditions. The distinction between genuine faith (sattvic), self-serving faith (rajasic), and degraded faith (tamasic) parallels discussions in Christian theology about true versus false faith, the Islamic distinction between *iman* (genuine faith) and *nifaq* (hypocrisy), and the Buddhist analysis of *saddha* (faith) versus blind attachment.

The threefold analysis of sacrifice, austerity, and charity provides a framework for comparing religious practices across traditions. Every major religion distinguishes between worship performed with genuine devotion and worship performed out of habit, social pressure, or selfish motivation. The Gita's systematic correlation of practice quality with inner disposition anticipated modern psychological studies of intrinsic versus extrinsic religiosity.
