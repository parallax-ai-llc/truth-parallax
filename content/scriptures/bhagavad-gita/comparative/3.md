The Gita's Karma Yoga — action performed without attachment — finds significant parallels in other traditions. The Taoist concept of *wu wei* (non-action or effortless action) shares the Gita's insight that the most effective action arises from freedom rather than desire. However, where Taoism generally counsels withdrawal from social structures, the Gita insists on engagement with worldly duties.

Christian monastic traditions developed a similar integration of work and spiritual practice. Saint Benedict's "Ora et Labora" (pray and work) parallels the Gita's teaching that all action can be transformed into spiritual practice when offered to God. The Japanese Zen concept of *samu* (work practice) and the Islamic concept of *niyyah* (intention) similarly emphasize that the inner orientation of the actor transforms the spiritual value of the action.
