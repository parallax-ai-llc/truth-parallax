Chapter 7's teaching on God's relationship to the material world resonates with philosophical theology across traditions. The distinction between God's higher and lower natures (7.4-5) parallels the Christian distinction between God's transcendence and immanence, the Kabbalistic concept of *Ein Sof* (the Infinite) and the *sefirot* (emanations), and the Islamic distinction between God's essence (*dhat*) and attributes (*sifat*).

The Gita's teaching that maya conceals divine reality (7.14-15) finds parallels in the Sufi concept of the world as a "veil" (*hijab*) over the divine face, in Plato's cave allegory, and in the Buddhist concept of *avidya* (ignorance). In each case, ordinary human consciousness is understood as a state of concealment from which one must be liberated through grace, knowledge, or practice.
