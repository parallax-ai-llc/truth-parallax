Chapter 18's comprehensive conclusion invites comparison with other "summit teachings" in world scripture. As the Gita concludes with total surrender to Krishna, the Gospel of John concludes with the command to love (John 15:12-17), the Diamond Sutra concludes with letting go of all dharmas, and the Quran's final sura (Al-Nas) seeks refuge in God alone. Each tradition distills its message to a single essential act of spiritual orientation.

The verse "Abandon all dharmas and surrender to Me" (18.66) has been compared with Luther's *sola fide* (faith alone), Shinran's *tariki* (other-power), and the Quran's concept of *tawakkul* (total trust in God). All represent a radical simplification of the religious life to a single gesture of trust and submission, transcending the multiplicity of rules, practices, and philosophical systems. The convergence suggests a deep structural pattern in human religious experience: the recognition that ultimate transformation requires a surrender that exceeds any technique.
