Chapter 16 contrasts divine (*daivi*) and demonic (*asuri*) natures, which Vaishnavas understand as the fundamental moral-spiritual divide conditioning the soul's relationship with Krishna. The twenty-six divine qualities listed in 16.1-3 describe the character of a devotee, while the demonic qualities (16.4) describe those who turn away from God.

Vaishnavas note that the demonic nature is characterized above all by the denial of God's existence and the assertion that the world arises from desire alone (16.8). Krishna declares that lust, anger, and greed are the three gates to hell (16.21) and that one should follow scriptural injunctions to determine right action (16.23-24). The chapter reinforces the Vaishnava emphasis on *shastra* (scripture) as the guide for moral and spiritual life.
