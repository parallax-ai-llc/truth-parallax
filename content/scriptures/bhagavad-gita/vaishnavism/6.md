Chapter 6's teaching on meditation (*dhyana yoga*) is interpreted by Vaishnavas as describing a practice that culminates in meditation on Krishna's personal form. While the chapter details the postures, practices, and mental disciplines of classical yoga, Vaishnavas point to the concluding verse (6.47) as the key: "Of all yogis, the one who worships Me with faith, with the mind focused on Me, is the most united in yoga."

Arjuna's practical objection that the mind is as difficult to control as the wind (6.34) receives Krishna's compassionate response that practice and detachment can tame it (6.35). The Vaishnava tradition notes that Krishna also promises that no effort on the spiritual path is ever lost (6.40-45), reassuring aspirants that devotional practice carries forward across lifetimes.
