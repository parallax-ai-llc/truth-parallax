Chapter 17 analyzes the three types of faith (*shraddha*) corresponding to the three gunas. Vaishnavas read this chapter as a taxonomy of spiritual orientations: sattvic faith directed toward the gods (and ultimately Krishna), rajasic faith toward power-seeking entities, and tamasic faith toward ghosts and spirits.

The chapter's treatment of food, sacrifice, austerity, and charity in their three modes provides practical guidance for Vaishnava daily life. The closing teaching on "Om Tat Sat" (17.23-27) is especially significant: these three designations of Brahman sanctify all acts of sacrifice and penance. Vaishnavas understand this as affirming that all genuine spiritual practice is ultimately connected to the Supreme and that acts performed without faith are *asat* (unreal) and bear no fruit.
