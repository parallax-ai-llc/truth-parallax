Chapter 14 teaches the three *gunas* (qualities of material nature) — *sattva* (goodness), *rajas* (passion), and *tamas* (ignorance). Vaishnavas interpret this teaching as revealing the mechanism of material bondage. The soul, though transcendental, becomes conditioned by identifying with these material qualities and their effects on body and mind.

The chapter's conclusion is especially significant for Vaishnavas: when Arjuna asks how one can transcend the three gunas, Krishna answers that unalloyed devotional service (*avyabhicharini bhakti*) is the means (14.26). This verse directly connects the metaphysical teaching on gunas with the practice of bhakti, confirming the Vaishnava position that devotion is the ultimate solution to material bondage, transcending even the "good" quality of sattva.
