Chapter 4 introduces the doctrine of divine descent (*avatara*), which is absolutely central to Vaishnavism. Krishna's declaration "Whenever dharma declines, I manifest Myself" (4.7-8) is the theological foundation for the Vaishnava understanding of God's personal involvement in creation. Unlike Advaita's reading of avatara as illusory, Vaishnavas insist that Krishna's descent is fully real.

The chapter also teaches *jnana yoga* (the yoga of knowledge), but Vaishnavas read this knowledge as ultimately being knowledge of Krishna Himself. Verse 4.9, declaring that one who knows the divine nature of Krishna's birth and activities is liberated, is especially treasured. The various forms of sacrifice described in 4.24-33 are understood as progressive steps toward the supreme sacrifice of total devotion.
