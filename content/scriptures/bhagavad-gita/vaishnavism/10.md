Chapter 10 reveals Krishna's *vibhutis* (divine glories), demonstrating that every form of excellence, beauty, and power in the universe is a fragment of His infinite splendor. Vaishnavas read this chapter as an invitation to see Krishna everywhere — in the best among all categories of beings and phenomena.

The chapter's opening teaching, that Krishna is the origin of everything and that the wise worship Him with love knowing this (10.8), is the epistemological foundation of Vaishnava theology. Arjuna's response — acknowledging Krishna as the Supreme Brahman, Supreme Abode, and Supreme Purifier (10.12-13) — is taken as the model confession of faith. The list of vibhutis (10.20-42) is used in Vaishnava meditation to cultivate awareness of Krishna's presence in all things.
