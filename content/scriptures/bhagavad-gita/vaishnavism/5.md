In Vaishnava interpretation, Chapter 5 resolves the apparent tension between renunciation (*sannyasa*) and selfless action (*karma yoga*) by showing that both lead to the same goal when performed in devotion to Krishna. Krishna declares the yoga of action superior to mere renunciation of action (5.2), a teaching Vaishnavas cite to support active devotional service over passive withdrawal.

The chapter's description of the liberated sage who sees Krishna everywhere (5.18) and who offers all actions to Brahman (5.10) is read by Vaishnavas as describing the devotee who has realized Krishna's all-pervasiveness. The closing verses (5.27-29), identifying Krishna as the enjoyer of all sacrifices and the friend of all beings, anticipate the fuller revelation of bhakti in later chapters.
