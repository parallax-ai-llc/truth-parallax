Chapter 8 discusses the imperishable Brahman and the science of dying, both critical topics in Vaishnava practice. Krishna's instruction that one who remembers Him at the moment of death attains His abode (8.5-8) is the foundation of the Vaishnava emphasis on constant remembrance (*smarana*) of Krishna throughout life. The deathbed consciousness is seen as the culmination of lifelong devotion.

The chapter contrasts the path of light (*shukla gati*) leading to liberation with the path of darkness (*krishna gati*) leading to rebirth (8.24-26). Vaishnavas emphasize that devotees who attain Krishna's supreme abode (8.21) never return to the material world, distinguishing this from the temporary heavens of the Vedic rituals. This teaching of the eternal spiritual world is central to Vaishnava cosmology.
