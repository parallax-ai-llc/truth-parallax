Chapter 7 marks a turning point in the Gita as Krishna begins to reveal His divine nature directly. Vaishnavas consider this the beginning of the "middle six" chapters focused on bhakti. Krishna distinguishes His lower material nature (*apara prakriti*) from His higher spiritual nature (*para prakriti*), which is the life force sustaining all beings (7.4-5).

The classification of four types of devotees (7.16) — the distressed, the seeker of knowledge, the seeker of wealth, and the wise — is important in Vaishnava theology. Krishna declares the wise devotee most dear (7.17-18). The chapter's teaching that the deluded do not surrender to Krishna because of the covering of maya (7.14-15) underscores the Vaishnava emphasis on divine grace as necessary for liberation.
