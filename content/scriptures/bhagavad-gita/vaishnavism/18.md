Chapter 18, the Gita's culmination, holds supreme importance in Vaishnava tradition. It recapitulates all major teachings and builds toward what Vaishnavas consider the ultimate revelation. The analysis of action, knowledge, and the performer according to the three gunas (18.18-40) provides a comprehensive framework, while the teaching on *svadharma* (one's own duty, 18.41-48) affirms the Vaishnava acceptance of varnashrama as divinely ordained.

The chapter's crescendo begins at 18.55, where Krishna declares that through devotion one truly knows Him, and culminates in the "most secret of all" teaching (18.64-66): "Abandon all varieties of dharma and surrender unto Me alone. I shall deliver you from all sin; do not grieve." Vaishnavas regard this verse (*charama shloka*) as the absolute pinnacle of the Gita, containing the entire teaching in essence — total, unconditional surrender (*sharanagati*) to Krishna's grace.
