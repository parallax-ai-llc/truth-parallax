Chapter 13's analysis of the *kshetra* (field/body) and *kshetrajna* (knower of the field/soul) is interpreted by Vaishnavas as establishing the ontological distinction between matter, soul, and God. While Advaita reads the "knower" as ultimately identical with Brahman, Vaishnavas insist on three distinct realities: the material field, the individual soul who knows it, and the Supreme Soul (Paramatma/Krishna) who knows all fields.

The chapter's teaching on the *Supersoul* (*Paramatma*) present in every heart (13.18, 13.23) is central to Vaishnava theology. This indwelling divine presence guides, sanctions, and witnesses every action. The Vaishnava reading sees this chapter as establishing the philosophical framework for devotion: because God is both transcendent and intimately present, loving relationship with Him is the natural function of the soul.
