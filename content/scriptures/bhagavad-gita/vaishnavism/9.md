Chapter 9, titled "Raja Vidya" (Royal Knowledge), is among the most treasured chapters in Vaishnava tradition. Krishna calls this the most confidential knowledge (9.1-2) and reveals His simultaneous immanence and transcendence: all beings exist in Him, yet He is not in them (9.4-5). This paradox is resolved in Vaishnava theology through the doctrine of *acintya bhedabheda* (inconceivable simultaneous oneness and difference).

The Vaishnava reading highlights 9.22 — Krishna's promise to personally carry the necessities of those who worship Him exclusively — as expressing the intimate personal relationship between God and devotee. Verse 9.26, in which Krishna accepts a leaf, flower, fruit, or water offered with devotion, is foundational to Vaishnava temple worship (*puja*). The chapter's universalism (9.32-33) declaring that anyone can attain Krishna through devotion, regardless of birth, is also emphasized.
