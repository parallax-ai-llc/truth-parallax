Chapter 15, "The Yoga of the Supreme Person," is revered in Vaishnava tradition for its systematic presentation of Krishna's supremacy. The metaphor of the cosmic *ashvattha* tree (15.1-4), rooted in Brahman with branches extending downward into the material world, illustrates the relationship between the eternal and temporal realms.

The chapter's climactic declaration (15.18-19) — that Krishna is the Supreme Person (*Purushottama*), beyond both the perishable material nature and the imperishable individual soul — is the definitive Vaishnava theological statement in the Gita. Krishna stands above both the material cosmos and the individual souls, being the source and sustainer of both. One who knows Him as Purushottama "worships Me wholeheartedly," and this knowledge constitutes the essence of all scripture.
