Chapter 12 on Bhakti Yoga is the heart of the Vaishnava reading of the Gita. Arjuna's question — whether to worship the personal God or the formless Brahman — receives Krishna's unequivocal answer: devotion to the personal form is superior (12.2). Those who worship the unmanifest face greater difficulty because the path of formlessness is arduous for embodied beings (12.5).

The chapter outlines a progressive hierarchy of devotional practice (12.8-12): meditation on Krishna, devoted practice, action for Krishna's sake, and finally renunciation of the fruits of action. Vaishnavas treasure the closing verses (12.13-20), which describe the qualities of a devotee dear to Krishna — compassionate, free from ego, equal in pain and pleasure, and ever content in devotion. This portrait defines the Vaishnava spiritual ideal.
