Chapter 17's taxonomy of faith according to the three gunas provides scholars with evidence for the diversity of religious practice in the Gita's world. The description of different objects of worship — gods, yakshas, rakshasas, ghosts (17.4) — maps the popular religious landscape, while the analysis of food, sacrifice, and austerity reflects concern with the ritualization of daily life.

Scholars note that the chapter's systematic application of the guna framework to faith, food, sacrifice, austerity, and charity demonstrates the Gita's method of using a single analytical category to organize diverse phenomena. The concluding section on "Om Tat Sat" (17.23-28) has been analyzed as an attempt to integrate the Gita's devotional theology with traditional Vedic mantra practice, bridging the gap between innovative bhakti and established ritual authority.
