Chapter 6's detailed description of meditation practice — including posture, location, diet, and mental technique (6.10-15) — provides valuable evidence for the history of yoga. Scholars note both continuities and differences with Patanjali's Yoga Sutras (c. 2nd century CE), suggesting either a common tradition or parallel development. The Gita's yoga is distinctly theistic, unlike the more philosophical yoga of Patanjali's early sutras.

The passage on the failed yogi (6.37-45) — who falls from the path before achieving liberation — addresses a practical concern that suggests a real audience of practitioners. Krishna's reassurance that spiritual progress carries over between lifetimes reflects the developed doctrine of karma and rebirth that was well established by the Gita's composition period. Scholars note this passage may address concerns raised by Buddhist critiques of Brahmanical spiritual practices.
