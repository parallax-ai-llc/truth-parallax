Scholars note that Chapter 1 serves a literary function within the Mahabharata by establishing the dramatic context for the philosophical dialogue that follows. The detailed enumeration of warriors on both sides (1.3-11) anchors the Gita in the broader epic narrative, and some scholars argue this catalog reflects an earlier martial poem that was later expanded with philosophical content.

The psychological realism of Arjuna's crisis — trembling limbs, dry mouth, the dropping of his bow (1.28-30) — has drawn comparison with battlefield trauma descriptions in other ancient literatures, including the Iliad. Scholars debate whether Arjuna's arguments against fighting (1.36-44) reflect historical concerns about Brahmanical social order or are literary devices to motivate Krishna's systematic teaching.
