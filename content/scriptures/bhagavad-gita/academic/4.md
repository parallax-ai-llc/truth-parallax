The doctrine of avatara (divine descent) presented in Chapter 4 has been a major focus of academic study. Scholars observe that 4.6-8 represents one of the earliest systematic statements of incarnation theology in Indian literature. The concept has no clear Vedic precedent and may represent a theological innovation of the Bhagavata or Pancharatra tradition incorporated into the Gita.

Academic attention has also focused on 4.13, where Krishna claims to have created the four-varna system. Scholars debate whether this represents an original social ideology or a later interpolation designed to give divine sanction to the caste system. The chapter's taxonomy of sacrifices (4.24-33) is analyzed as reflecting the historical transition from Vedic ritual sacrifice to interiorized spiritual practice.
