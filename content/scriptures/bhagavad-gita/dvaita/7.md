Chapter 7's revelation of Krishna's divine nature is read by Dvaita as the systematic exposition of Vishnu's relationship to the cosmos. The lower nature (7.4) is real material substance (*prakriti*), and the higher nature (7.5) is the community of individual souls (*jivas*). Both are permanently real and permanently distinct from God, who controls and sustains them.

Madhva's reading of 7.14 — that divine maya is difficult to cross — understands maya not as cosmic illusion (as in Advaita) but as God's inscrutable power of governance and concealment. Maya is real and operates under God's control to regulate the souls' experience according to their karma and devotion. Surrender to God alone enables the soul to cross beyond maya and attain liberation (7.14b).
