Chapter 4's teaching on divine incarnation (4.6-8) is foundational to Dvaita theology. Madhva affirms that Vishnu's avatars are fully real divine descents — God voluntarily assumes a form made of pure, spiritual matter (*shuddha sattva*) to protect the righteous, destroy evil, and re-establish dharma. These are not appearances or illusions but genuine acts of divine grace.

The Dvaita reading strongly emphasizes 4.11: "In whatever way people approach Me, I reward them accordingly." Madhva interprets this not as universalism but as a statement about the hierarchy of devotion — those who know Vishnu's true nature receive the highest reward (liberation), while those with lesser understanding receive proportionally lesser results. This connects to the Dvaita doctrine of *taratamya* (gradation among souls).
