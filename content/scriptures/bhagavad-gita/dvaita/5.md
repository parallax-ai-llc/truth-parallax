Madhva's reading of Chapter 5 maintains the Dvaita distinction between renunciation and action while affirming that both must be performed in devotion to Vishnu. True renunciation is not the cessation of action but the abandonment of selfish motivation, and true action is service offered to God. Both paths are valid because both recognize the soul's dependence on God.

The Dvaita reading of 5.15 — "The Lord does not take on anyone's sin or merit" — emphasizes God's transcendence and justice. God does not arbitrarily distribute suffering or reward; each soul receives the consequences of its own actions. However, God's grace can mitigate karmic consequences for devoted souls. This balance between karma and grace is a distinctive feature of Madhva's theology.
