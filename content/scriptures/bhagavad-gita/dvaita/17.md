Dvaita reads Chapter 17's analysis of three types of faith as describing the real correlation between a soul's innate nature and its spiritual orientation. Sattvic souls naturally gravitate toward worship of Vishnu, rajasic souls toward powerful beings, and tamasic souls toward degraded practices. This is not merely a psychological observation but a metaphysical truth about the gradation of souls.

The chapter's treatment of proper sacrifice, austerity, and charity in the sattvic mode provides practical guidance for Dvaita devotees. Acts performed according to scriptural injunction, without desire for results, and offered to Vishnu constitute the highest form of religious practice. The triple designation "Om Tat Sat" (17.23) is read by Madhva as three names of Vishnu, affirming that the Supreme Person is the ultimate referent of all sacred utterances.
