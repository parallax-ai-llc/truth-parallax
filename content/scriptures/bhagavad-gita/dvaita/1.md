Madhva's Dvaita reading of Chapter 1 sees Arjuna's despair as a genuine moral crisis rather than a merely symbolic spiritual event. The battlefield is real, the characters are historical, and Arjuna's dilemma reflects the authentic difficulty of discerning dharma when duties conflict. Madhva insists on the literal reality of the Mahabharata narrative as a divinely revealed record (*itihasa*).

In the Dvaita reading, Arjuna's emotional collapse demonstrates the insufficiency of human reason alone to resolve complex ethical situations. His turning to Krishna for guidance (1.46-47) illustrates the Dvaita principle that the individual soul (*jiva*) is inherently dependent on God (*Vishnu/Krishna*) for knowledge, guidance, and ultimate liberation. Without divine grace, even the most capable human being falls into confusion.
