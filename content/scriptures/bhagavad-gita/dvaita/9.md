Madhva's reading of Chapter 9 highlights Krishna's declaration "All beings are in Me; I am not in them" (9.4) as expressing the asymmetric dependence between God and the world. The universe depends entirely on Vishnu, but Vishnu is not dependent on or limited by the universe. This is the fundamental Dvaita ontological insight: God is independently real (*svatantra*), while everything else is dependently real (*paratantra*).

The promise of 9.22 — that Krishna personally ensures the welfare of exclusive devotees — is treasured in Dvaita as expressing the intimate personal relationship between God and soul. The chapter's universalism (9.32-33) is read as affirming that anyone, regardless of birth or social status, can attain Vishnu through devotion, but the Dvaita tradition adds that innate capacity (*yogyata*) determines the degree of liberation each soul can achieve.
