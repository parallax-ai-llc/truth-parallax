Dvaita interprets Chapter 3 on Karma Yoga as teaching that all action should be performed as service to Vishnu. Krishna's declaration that actions performed as sacrifice to Vishnu free one from bondage (3.9) is taken literally: the purpose of all human activity is to please and serve God. This is not metaphorical but ontological — the soul's essential nature (*svarupa*) is to serve God.

Madhva emphasizes 3.22-24, where Krishna describes His own tireless action as a model. Unlike Advaita's reading that the self is a non-doer, Dvaita affirms that the individual soul genuinely acts, but its action is always dependent on and sanctioned by God. The soul's agency is real but derivative — God is the independent (*svatantra*) agent who enables all action. This theology of dependent agency is central to Madhva's ethics.
