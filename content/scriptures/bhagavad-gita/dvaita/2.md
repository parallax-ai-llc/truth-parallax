Madhva's commentary on Chapter 2 affirms the immortality of the individual soul while carefully distinguishing the Dvaita position from Advaita's identification of atman with Brahman. When Krishna teaches that the self is never born and never dies (2.20), Dvaita understands this as the eternal existence of the individual soul — real, distinct, and permanently different from God.

The teaching on equanimity (2.14-15, 2.38) is read as prescribing the devotee's attitude toward worldly experience, not as pointing toward non-dual awareness. The *sthitaprajna* (person of steady wisdom, 2.54-72) is one who has firm devotion to Vishnu and complete detachment from material results, not one who has realized identity with Brahman. For Madhva, wisdom is knowledge of the five fundamental differences (*pancha-bheda*) and of Vishnu's absolute supremacy.
