## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta, founded by Madhvacharya (13th century), interprets the Bhagavad Gita as teaching the eternal and real distinction between God (Vishnu/Krishna), individual souls, and the material world. Madhva's *Gitabhashya* and *Gita Tatparya Nirnaya* offer a rigorous theistic reading that directly challenges Shankara's non-dual interpretation.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Madhva's Five Differences
Madhva's interpretation is grounded in *pancha-bheda* (five real differences): between God and soul, God and matter, soul and matter, one soul and another, and one material thing and another. The Gita's teachings, even passages that appear to teach identity (e.g., "You are That"), are read as affirming dependence rather than identity.

### Jayatirtha's Elaboration
Jayatirtha's sub-commentary on Madhva's *Gitabhashya* provides detailed philosophical arguments defending the realist reading against Advaita objections.

## Theological Emphases

- **Vishnu's Supremacy**: Krishna is Vishnu incarnate, the independently real (*svatantra*) Supreme Being
- **Hierarchy of Souls**: Souls are graded by their innate capacity (*yogyata*) for liberation
- **Grace and Merit**: Liberation requires both divine grace and individual devotion
- **Real World**: The material world is genuinely real, not illusion — God is its efficient and material cause
