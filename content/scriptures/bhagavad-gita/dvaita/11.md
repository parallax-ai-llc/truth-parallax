The Dvaita reading of Chapter 11 treats the universal form as a literal theophany — God revealing His cosmic body to the devotee. Unlike the Advaita interpretation that relativizes this vision, Madhva takes it as demonstrating Vishnu's absolute sovereignty over time, creation, and destruction. The vision is genuinely terrifying because God's power is genuinely infinite.

Madhva notes that the universal form includes the destruction of the Kaurava warriors (11.26-30), demonstrating that events on the battlefield are part of God's cosmic plan. Arjuna is not the true agent of their destruction — God has already determined the outcome (11.33). However, this does not negate Arjuna's genuine agency; rather, God's will and the individual's action work in concert through the Dvaita principle of *sahakari karana* (cooperative causation).
