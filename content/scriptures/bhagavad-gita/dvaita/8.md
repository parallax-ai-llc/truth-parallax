The Dvaita reading of Chapter 8 emphasizes the literal reality of the spiritual world (*Vaikuntha*) to which liberated souls ascend. When Krishna speaks of the "supreme abode" from which no one returns (8.21), Madhva understands this as a genuinely existing transcendent realm where liberated souls enjoy eternal proximity to Vishnu — not identity with Him, but blissful relationship.

The practice of remembering God at death (8.5-8) is taken as a serious practical instruction in Dvaita. The soul's destination at death is determined by its consciousness, which is the cumulative result of lifelong devotion and practice. Madhva notes that the different paths described (8.24-26) indicate a genuine cosmological geography through which the departing soul travels, emphasizing the realistic ontology that characterizes Dvaita thought.
