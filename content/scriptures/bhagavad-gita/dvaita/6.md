Madhva reads Chapter 6's teaching on meditation as describing the devotional practice of meditating on Vishnu's form, qualities, and glories. Unlike Advaita's interpretation of meditation as preparation for formless self-knowledge, Dvaita understands *dhyana* as an end in itself — sustained contemplation of the beloved personal God is both the practice and the goal.

The Dvaita reading gives special emphasis to 6.47: "The greatest yogi is one who worships Me with faith." This is not a secondary concession to devotion but the climactic statement of the chapter. Madhva notes that Arjuna's objection about the mind's restlessness (6.34) is answered by Krishna's promise that practice and detachment succeed — but the Dvaita tradition adds that God's enabling grace is the ultimate factor that makes practice effective.
