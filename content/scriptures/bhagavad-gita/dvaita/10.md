Dvaita reads Chapter 10's divine glories as real manifestations of Vishnu's infinite power and sovereignty. Each vibhuti demonstrates that God is the supreme entity in every category of existence — not symbolically, but as the actual controlling power behind every excellent manifestation. The vibhutis are evidence of God's *sarva-gunapurnatva* (possession of all positive qualities to an infinite degree).

Madhva emphasizes 10.12-13, where Arjuna confesses Krishna as the Supreme Brahman and Supreme Person, as the model theological declaration. This is not a poetic expression but a truth-claim about reality: Krishna/Vishnu is the independently real Supreme Being. The teaching that Vishnu sustains the universe with a "single fragment" (10.42) demonstrates His infinite transcendence — the entire cosmos is a negligible fraction of His glory.
