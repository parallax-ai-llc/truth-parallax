Madhva's commentary on Chapter 16 treats the divine and demonic natures as reflecting the inherent tendencies (*svabhava*) of different categories of souls. In Dvaita theology, souls are eternally graded in their capacity for liberation: some are inherently inclined toward God (*sattvika jivas*), some are mixed (*rajasika*), and some are inherently turned away (*tamasika*). This doctrine of innate spiritual capacity is distinctive to Madhva.

The three gates to hell — lust, anger, and greed (16.21) — are genuine spiritual dangers that the aspirant must overcome through devotion to God and adherence to scripture. Madhva emphasizes 16.23-24, which establishes *shastra* (scripture) as the authority for determining right and wrong action. Human reason alone is insufficient; divine revelation through the Vedas is necessary because God's will exceeds human comprehension.
