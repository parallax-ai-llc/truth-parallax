The Dvaita reading of Chapter 14's teaching on the three gunas affirms the gunas as real properties of real matter (prakriti), not as illusory appearances on a non-dual consciousness. The soul is genuinely affected by the gunas through its association with the material body, and liberation requires God's grace to free the soul from this binding influence.

Madhva interprets the question of how to transcend the gunas (14.21) and the answer pointing to bhakti (14.26) as confirming the Dvaita position that devotion to God — not self-knowledge in the Advaita sense — is the means of liberation. The soul cannot liberate itself by its own effort alone; it requires the active intervention of God's grace, which is bestowed in response to sincere and sustained devotion.
