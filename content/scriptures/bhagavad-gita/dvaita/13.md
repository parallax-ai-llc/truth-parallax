Madhva's reading of Chapter 13 insists on the ontological reality of three distinct categories: the field (*kshetra* — matter), the knower of the field (*kshetrajna* — the individual soul), and the Supreme Knower (God) who knows all fields. Against Advaita's reading that the individual and supreme knowers are one, Dvaita maintains that the individual soul's knowledge is limited while God's knowledge is infinite.

Verse 13.3 — "Know Me as the Knower of the field in all fields" — is not read as affirming identity between the individual and God but as establishing God's omniscient presence within every living being as the inner controller (*antaryamin*). The soul knows its own field; God knows all fields. This distinction is permanent and unbridgeable, constituting one of the five fundamental differences that define Dvaita metaphysics.
