Dvaita interprets Chapter 12's teaching on bhakti as the supreme path, fully consistent with Madhva's system. Krishna's declaration that worshipping the personal form is superior to meditation on the impersonal absolute (12.2-5) is the clearest scriptural support for Dvaita's rejection of Advaita's impersonalism. God is eternally personal, and devotion to His personal form is the highest spiritual practice.

The qualities of the ideal devotee (12.13-20) describe the soul that has achieved maximal devotion to Vishnu. Madhva reads these not merely as moral ideals but as the natural characteristics of a soul that has been graced by God's favor. Compassion, equanimity, and freedom from ego arise when the soul recognizes its complete dependence on God and surrenders its false claim to independence.
