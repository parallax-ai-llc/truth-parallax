Madhva's reading of Chapter 18 culminates in the affirmation of total surrender (*prapatti*) to Vishnu as the supreme teaching of the Gita. The chapter's comprehensive treatment of duty, knowledge, and action according to the three gunas (18.18-40) provides the ethical framework, but the ultimate message is found in 18.66: "Abandon all dharmas and surrender to Me alone."

For Dvaita, this verse does not mean abandoning moral duty but recognizing that all dharma is ultimately sustained by and directed toward Vishnu. Surrender means acknowledging the soul's eternal dependence on God and offering all actions, thoughts, and devotion to Him. Madhva emphasizes that Krishna's promise "I shall deliver you from all sin" (18.66) is a genuine divine guarantee — God's grace, not human effort, is the ultimate cause of liberation. However, this grace is not arbitrary but responds to the soul's sincere devotion and innate capacity.
