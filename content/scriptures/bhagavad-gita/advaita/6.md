For Advaita, Chapter 6's teaching on meditation is a description of the penultimate stage of spiritual practice. *Dhyana* (meditation) stills the mind and prepares it for the arising of self-knowledge. However, Shankara is clear that meditation itself does not produce liberation — only knowledge (*jnana*) does. Meditation creates the conditions of mental clarity in which the truth of non-duality can be recognized.

The Advaita reading of 6.29 — "seeing the self in all beings and all beings in the self" — is interpreted as the direct experience of non-duality, not as a devotional sentiment. When the meditator realizes the self pervading everything, the boundary between subject and object dissolves. This is *nirvikalpa samadhi* — objectless awareness in which the self knows itself as infinite, undivided consciousness.
