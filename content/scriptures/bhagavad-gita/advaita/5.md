Chapter 5's resolution of the tension between renunciation and action is, for Advaita, resolved at a deeper level than most readings suggest. Shankara argues that true *sannyasa* (renunciation) is the renunciation of the sense of doership, which is synonymous with self-knowledge. Karma yoga, being action without attachment, is a means to this knowledge but not identical with it.

The Advaita reading highlights 5.15-16: "The Lord does not create agency or actions, nor the union of action with its fruit. It is nature (*svabhava*) that operates. Knowledge is covered by ignorance, and thereby beings are deluded." This verse is crucial because it establishes that the self neither acts nor causes action — all activity belongs to prakriti. Liberation is uncovering this ever-present truth, not attaining a new state.
