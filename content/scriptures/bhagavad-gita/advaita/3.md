Shankara reads Chapter 3 on Karma Yoga as addressed primarily to seekers not yet qualified for pure jnana. Krishna's exhortation to act without attachment (3.19) is a concession to human nature, not the highest teaching. The Advaita position is that for the enlightened sage (*jnani*), action and inaction are equally irrelevant, since the self is the non-doer (*akarta*).

The chapter's teaching that "all actions are performed by the gunas of prakriti; the self-deluded person, misidentifying with the ego, thinks 'I am the doer'" (3.27) is central to Advaita. This verse establishes that agency belongs to prakriti, not to the self. The self is the witness (*sakshi*), pure consciousness that illuminates all activity without participating in it. Karma yoga is thus the progressive realization that one has never truly acted.
