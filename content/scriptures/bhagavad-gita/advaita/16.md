Shankara reads Chapter 16's enumeration of divine and demonic qualities primarily as a guide for aspirants who have not yet attained self-knowledge. The divine qualities (16.1-3) create conditions favorable to knowledge, while the demonic qualities (16.4-20) reinforce ignorance and bondage. From the standpoint of Brahman, neither divine nor demonic natures have ultimate reality — they are modifications of prakriti.

The Advaita emphasis falls on 16.8: the demonic view that the universe has "no truth, no basis, no God" and arises from desire alone. This materialistic worldview is the antithesis of Vedantic understanding. Shankara argues that the recognition of a real, conscious ground of being is the essential prerequisite for liberation — without it, the inquiry "Who am I?" cannot even begin.
