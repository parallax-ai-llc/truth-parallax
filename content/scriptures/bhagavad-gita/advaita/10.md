For Advaita, Chapter 10's enumeration of divine glories (*vibhutis*) serves a meditative purpose: training the mind to see Brahman as the reality behind all excellent forms. Shankara interprets Krishna's statement "I am the self seated in the hearts of all beings" (10.20) as the definitive teaching of this chapter — the vibhutis are particular illustrations, but the essential point is that Brahman is the self of everything.

The Advaita reading notes that the vibhutis are concessions to the limitations of the human mind, which cannot grasp the infinite directly. By meditating on excellence wherever it appears, the seeker gradually develops the capacity to recognize Brahman as the substratum of all experience. The culminating verse (10.42) — "I sustain the entire universe with a single fragment of Myself" — is read not as God's boast but as pointing to how a fraction of Brahman's reality is sufficient to manifest the entire phenomenal world.
