Chapter 2 is the cornerstone of the Advaita reading of the Gita. Shankara identifies the core teaching in 2.16: "The unreal has no being; the real has no non-being." This establishes the fundamental Advaita distinction between the unchanging reality (*sat*, Brahman/Atman) and the changing phenomena (*asat*, the empirical world). The self described in 2.17-25 is not the individual soul but Brahman itself — infinite, partless, and beyond all modification.

The teaching on *nishkama karma* (2.47-48) is, for Shankara, a preparatory discipline rather than the ultimate teaching. Selfless action purifies the mind (*chitta shuddhi*) and makes it fit for the dawn of self-knowledge. The *sthitaprajna* (person of steady wisdom, 2.54-72) is one who has directly realized "I am Brahman" and for whom all action has ceased to be binding. This is not a psychological state but an ontological recognition.
