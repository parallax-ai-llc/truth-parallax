The Advaita reading of Chapter 14 treats the three gunas as properties of prakriti (nature), not of the self (*atman*). The self, being pure consciousness, is ever free of the gunas; it is only through false identification (*tadatmya adhyasa*) with the body-mind that the self appears to be bound by goodness, passion, or ignorance.

Shankara interprets the question "What are the marks of one who has transcended the gunas?" (14.21) and Krishna's answer (14.22-25) as describing the *jivanmukta* — one liberated while living. Such a person is not affected by the gunas' play because they know themselves as the witness (*sakshi*) of all mental states. Verse 14.26, which names *bhakti* as the means of transcending the gunas, is read by Shankara as referring to *para bhakti* (supreme devotion), which is identical with knowledge of Brahman.
