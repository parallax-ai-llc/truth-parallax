Chapter 9's declaration that God pervades the universe while remaining unaffected (9.4-5) is read by Advaita as describing Brahman's relationship to the phenomenal world. Just as space is everywhere but untouched by what occurs within it, Brahman supports all manifestation without being modified by it. The world exists in Brahman; Brahman does not exist in the world.

Shankara notes the verse "beings do not dwell in Me" (9.5) as a corrective to crude pantheism — the world is not literally inside God. The relationship is one of *vivartavada* (apparent transformation): the world appears within Brahman through maya but does not alter Brahman's nature. The devotional language of 9.26-34 is read as *upasana* (meditative worship) suited to seekers not yet ready for direct knowledge of the impersonal absolute.
