Shankara reads Chapter 7's distinction between the higher and lower natures (7.4-5) as the distinction between *para* Brahman (the unconditioned absolute) and *apara* prakriti (conditioned nature). The individual soul (*jiva*) is Brahman appearing as limited by ignorance; the "higher nature" is Brahman's intrinsic being as pure consciousness.

The chapter's teaching that four types of people worship God (7.16) is interpreted by Shankara as describing progressive levels of understanding. The *jnani* (person of knowledge, 7.17-18) is the highest because such a person realizes the identity of self and God. Krishna's declaration "the jnani is My very self" (7.18) is, for Advaita, the clearest statement in the Gita of non-duality: the knower of Brahman is not different from Brahman.
