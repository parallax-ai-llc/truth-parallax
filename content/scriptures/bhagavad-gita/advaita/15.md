Chapter 15 occupies a special place in the Advaita tradition. Shankara calls it the chapter that summarizes the entire teaching of the Gita and the Vedas. The cosmic *ashvattha* tree (15.1-4) represents samsara — the beginningless cycle of existence rooted in Brahman but extending through maya into the material world. The instruction to "cut down this tree with the axe of non-attachment" (15.3) is the call to liberation through knowledge.

The three *purushas* described in 15.16-18 — the perishable (all beings), the imperishable (the *kutastha* or unchanging witness), and the Supreme Person (*Purushottama*) — are read by Shankara as Brahman viewed from different perspectives. From the empirical standpoint, there are three; from the ultimate standpoint, there is only Brahman. The "Supreme Person" is not a fourth entity above the other two but Brahman itself, known when ignorance is removed.
