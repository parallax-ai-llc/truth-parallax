Chapter 13 on the field and its knower is among the most important chapters for the Advaita reading. Shankara identifies the "knower of the field" (*kshetrajna*) as the universal self, Brahman, which appears to be individual through its association with specific bodies (*kshetras*). The crucial verse is 13.3: "Know Me as the Knower of the field in all fields" — for Shankara, this means there is only one Knower, one Consciousness, appearing as many.

The twenty qualities listed as constituting "knowledge" (13.8-12) are ethical and spiritual prerequisites for direct self-realization. They purify the mind and remove obstacles to recognizing what is always already the case: that the self is infinite, partless Brahman. The chapter's conclusion — "one who sees that all actions are performed by prakriti alone and that the self is the non-doer sees truly" (13.30) — is the essence of Advaita.
