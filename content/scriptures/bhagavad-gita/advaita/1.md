Shankara's commentary on Chapter 1 situates Arjuna's despair within the broader Advaita framework of *avidya* (ignorance). Arjuna's sorrow arises from superimposing (*adhyasa*) reality upon the body and its relations, mistaking the non-self for the self. His grief over potentially killing Bhishma, Drona, and his kinsmen reveals the fundamental error that Advaita seeks to correct: identification with the body-mind complex.

The Advaita reading sees this chapter as presenting the human predicament in its starkest form. Without self-knowledge, even the noblest person is subject to confusion, grief, and moral paralysis. Arjuna represents the qualified seeker (*adhikari*) whose very suffering becomes the gateway to liberation. His surrender to Krishna as teacher (1.46-47) models the prerequisite humility for Vedantic inquiry.
