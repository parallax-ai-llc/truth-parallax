The Advaita reading of Chapter 4 focuses on the liberating power of knowledge (*jnana*). While the chapter discusses divine incarnation (4.6-8), Shankara treats this as a secondary teaching. The primary message is 4.37-38: "As a blazing fire reduces wood to ashes, so does the fire of knowledge reduce all karma to ashes. There is nothing in this world as purifying as knowledge."

For Advaita, the "knowledge" that destroys karma is not intellectual understanding but the direct, non-conceptual realization of non-duality. Verse 4.35 describes its effect: "Knowing which, you will not again fall into delusion, for you will see all beings in the self and then in Me." Shankara reads "in Me" as "in Brahman" rather than in a personal deity, interpreting Krishna's teaching as pointing beyond his personal form to the impersonal absolute.
