The Advaita reading of Chapter 17 on three types of faith understands *shraddha* as the mental orientation that determines the quality of one's spiritual practice and, consequently, one's readiness for self-knowledge. Sattvic faith, directed toward genuine spiritual teaching, creates the inner conditions for Vedantic inquiry.

Shankara notes that the threefold analysis of sacrifice, austerity, and charity (17.11-22) describes the progressive refinement of the practitioner's mind. Sattvic practice performed without desire for results purifies the intellect and makes it fit for the direct apprehension of Brahman. The sacred syllable "Om" (17.23-24) is Brahman's sound-symbol (*shabda Brahman*), and its recitation during spiritual acts connects the practitioner with the ultimate reality that pervades all genuine spiritual endeavor.
