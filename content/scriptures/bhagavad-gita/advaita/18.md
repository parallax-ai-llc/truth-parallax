Chapter 18 is, for Shankara, the comprehensive synthesis of the Gita's teaching, which he reads as establishing two paths: the path of action (*pravritti marga*) for those still in ignorance, and the path of renunciation (*nivritti marga*) for those ready for self-knowledge. The chapter's treatment of renunciation versus abandonment (18.2-12) clarifies that true renunciation is the giving up of desire and ego, not of action per se.

The Advaita reading reaches its culmination at 18.50-55, where Krishna describes the state of Brahman-realization: serene, desireless, equal to all beings, and established in supreme devotion. Shankara interprets the final instruction (18.66) — "Surrender all dharmas unto Me" — as the dissolution of all superimposed identities, duties, and limitations in the recognition of one's identity with the attributeless Brahman. The "Me" to whom one surrenders is not a personal deity but the absolute self that one always already is.
