Shankara's commentary on Chapter 12 addresses the question of personal versus impersonal worship with characteristic subtlety. While the text declares worship of the personal God easier (12.5), Shankara argues that the worship of the imperishable, indefinable, unmanifest absolute (12.3-4) is ultimately superior — it leads directly to liberation through knowledge.

The difficulty of the impersonal path (12.5) is acknowledged but attributed to the limitations of the embodied mind, not to any deficiency in the goal. Personal devotion is a legitimate and easier means, but it must eventually lead to the recognition that the personal God is a manifestation of impersonal Brahman. The devotee's qualities described in 12.13-19 are, for Shankara, naturally present in one who has realized non-duality — they are not moral achievements but spontaneous expressions of self-knowledge.
