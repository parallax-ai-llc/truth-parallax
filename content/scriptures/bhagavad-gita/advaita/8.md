The Advaita reading of Chapter 8 on the Imperishable Brahman focuses on verses 8.20-21 that describe the unmanifest (*avyakta*) beyond the unmanifest — the supreme reality that is not destroyed when all beings perish. Shankara identifies this as *nirguna Brahman*, the attributeless absolute that transcends both the manifest and unmanifest realms of prakriti.

The chapter's practical teachings on remembering God at death (8.5-8) are read by Advaita as applicable to the unenlightened seeker who still experiences a sense of individuality. For the *jnani* who has realized non-duality, there is no "death" to prepare for — the self was never born and never dies. The paths of light and darkness (8.24-26) describe the fate of the individual soul, which is, from the ultimate standpoint, a superimposition on changeless Brahman.
