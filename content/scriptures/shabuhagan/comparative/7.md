A comparative reading of Chapter 7 of the Shabuhagan situates its treatment of great fire within the broader context of world religious literature. The themes of eschatology, cosmic conflagration find parallels and contrasts in multiple traditions, offering rich material for cross-cultural analysis.

Comparative scholars have identified resonances between this chapter's teachings and similar discussions in other scriptures and philosophical texts. These parallels illuminate both universal patterns in human religious thought and the distinctive contributions of Manichaeism to the world's understanding of great fire.
