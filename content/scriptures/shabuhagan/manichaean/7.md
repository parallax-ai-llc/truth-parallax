Chapter 7 of the Shabuhagan, addressing great fire, holds particular significance within the Manichaean tradition. The themes of eschatology, cosmic conflagration are central to Manichaean theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Manichaean tradition, this chapter's treatment of great fire is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on eschatology, cosmic conflagration inform Manichaean self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
