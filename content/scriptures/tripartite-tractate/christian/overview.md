## Overview

For Christian readers, the Tripartite Tractate is the most ambitious attempt in Gnostic literature to develop a comprehensive systematic theology — comparable in scope to Irenaeus's Adversus Haereses or Origen's De Principiis. The text engages with virtually every major Christian doctrine — God, creation, fall, incarnation, salvation, church, eschatology — while developing each in a distinctly Valentinian direction. The text's generous eschatology, which extends salvation to psychics (possibly including non-Gnostic Christians), suggests a Valentinianism open to dialogue with the broader church.
