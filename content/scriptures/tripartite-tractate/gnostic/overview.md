## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Tripartite Tractate presents the most comprehensive Valentinian theological system in the Nag Hammadi library — a complete account of the divine realm, the fall, the creation of the material world, the formation of three classes of humanity, and the eschatological restoration of all things. The text's distinctive replacement of Sophia with the Logos as the fallen aeon may represent a later, more philosophically refined stage of Valentinian thought, and its generous eschatology — which extends the possibility of salvation even to psychic (ensouled but not fully spiritual) humanity — reflects a more inclusive soteriology than some other Valentinian texts.
