---
title: "Tripartite Tractate Section 3"
scripture: "tripartite-tractate"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Now one of the aeons, the Logos, conceived a thought of his own, apart from the will of the Father. He wished to comprehend the incomprehensible Father on his own, without the mediation of the Son.

**2** And in this presumption the Logos overreached himself. He brought forth something imperfect, because he acted without the consent of the Totality.

**3** For the intention of the Logos was good—he wished to honor the Father—but his deed was beyond his capacity. And so he produced something that was not in the likeness of the fullness.

**4** And that which he produced was formless and without beauty, for it came forth from a thought that was divided. It was a deficiency, a likeness of the Totality but without truth.

**5** And the Logos was grieved at what he had produced. He repented of his presumption, and he turned back toward the Father, seeking restoration.

**6** But the things he had produced could not be taken back. They had come into existence, and they persisted in their deficiency. And from the deficiency, the material world came into being.

**7** And the Logos was divided: his higher part, the thought that was in harmony with the Father, ascended back toward the fullness. But his lower part, the part that had produced the deficiency, remained with his creation.

**8** And from the division of the Logos, three orders came into being: the spiritual, the psychic, and the material. The spiritual is from the higher part of the Logos, the psychic from his turning back, and the material from his presumption.

**9** And the Logos labored to bring order to the chaos he had produced. He fashioned the material world and gave it structure, and he placed within it the seeds of the spiritual and the psychic.

**10** And the Father, in his mercy, did not abandon the Logos, but sent the Son to assist him in the restoration of all things. For the will of the Father is that nothing should be lost.
