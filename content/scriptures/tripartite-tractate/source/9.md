---
title: "Tripartite Tractate Section 9"
scripture: "tripartite-tractate"
chapter: 9
---

**1** This, then, is the teaching concerning the Father, the Son, and the Church. The Father is the root, the Son is the emanation, and the Church is the fruit.

**2** And the three are one, for the fruit contains the seed of the root, and the emanation proceeds from the root and produces the fruit. And in the fullness, there is no separation between them.

**3** The Father is unknowable in his essence, but he is known through the Son. The Son is the revelation of the Father, the word through which the silence speaks, the light through which the invisible becomes visible.

**4** And the Church is the response to the revelation, the praise that ascends to the Father, the love that returns to its source. For the Church is the gathering of all who have recognized the Father through the Son.

**5** And the human being who knows these things participates in the life of the fullness even while dwelling in the world of the Demiurge. For gnosis is not a matter of place but of nature.

**6** He who knows the Father dwells in the Father, even while his body dwells in the material world. And the material world cannot separate him from the Father, for the bond of gnosis is stronger than any material bond.

**7** And we who have received this teaching give thanks to the Father, through the Son, in the unity of the Church. For we have been shown the truth, and the truth has set us free from the ignorance that bound us.

**8** For it was not our fault that we were born into the world of the Demiurge. But it is our privilege that we have been awakened by the Son and gathered into the Church.

**9** And we look forward to the consummation, when the deficiency shall be abolished and the fullness restored. For we know that the will of the Father shall be accomplished, and nothing shall prevent it.

**10** This is the Tripartite Tractate, concerning the Father, the Son, and the Church, and concerning the origin and the restoration of all things. May the grace of the Father be with all who read and understand these words.
