---
title: "Tripartite Tractate Section 5"
scripture: "tripartite-tractate"
chapter: 5
---

**1** And humanity was placed in the garden of the Demiurge, and the Demiurge commanded them according to his law. But the spiritual seed within them was restless, for it remembered, even in its sleep, the fullness from which it had come.

**2** And the human beings lived under the rule of the Demiurge, and they served him in ignorance. They worshipped him as the only God, not knowing the Father of the Totalities.

**3** And the powers and the archons, who are the servants of the Demiurge, kept the human beings in bondage through fear and desire. They bound them to the material world through the passions of the flesh.

**4** But there were some among the human beings in whom the spiritual seed was strong. And these ones felt within themselves a longing for something beyond the material world, a memory of the light.

**5** And they searched for the truth, but the archons placed before them many deceptions: false teachings, false prophets, and false gods. And many were led astray.

**6** But those who carried the true seed could not be permanently deceived. For the Logos continued to work within them, guiding them toward the truth.

**7** And the Logos sent forth prophets and teachers into the world, to speak the word of truth. But the archons corrupted the words of the prophets, and mixed error with truth, so that humanity could not easily discern the one from the other.

**8** And the human race was divided into three classes, according to the three orders: the spiritual, the psychic, and the material. Each class perceives the truth according to its nature.

**9** The material class cannot perceive the truth at all, for they have no capacity for it. The psychic class perceives the truth partially, as through a veil. But the spiritual class perceives the truth directly, for the seed within them is of the same nature as the truth.

**10** And the Father, seeing the suffering of the spiritual seeds in the world of the Demiurge, determined to send the Son into the world, to awaken the seeds and gather them back into the fullness.
