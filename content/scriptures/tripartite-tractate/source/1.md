---
title: "Tripartite Tractate Section 1"
scripture: "tripartite-tractate"
chapter: 1
---

**1** As for what we can say about the things which are exalted, what is fitting is that we begin with the Father, who is the root of the Totality, the one from whom we have received grace to speak about him.

**2** He existed before anything other than himself came into being. The Father is a single one, like a number, for he is the first one and the one who is only himself.

**3** Yet he is not like a solitary individual. Otherwise, how could he be a Father? For whenever there is a father, the name son follows. But the single one, who alone is the Father, is like a root, with tree and branches and fruit.

**4** It is said of him that he is a Father in the proper sense, since he is inimitable and immutable. Because of this, he is single in the proper sense, and is a God, because no one is a god for him, nor is anyone a father to him.

**5** For he is unbegotten, and there is no other who begot him, nor another who created him. For whoever is someone's father or someone's creator, he too has a father and creator.

**6** It is certainly possible for him to be father and creator of the one who came into being from him and the one he created, for he is not a father in the proper sense, nor a god, because he has someone who begot him and who created him.

**7** It is the Father alone in the proper sense, the root of the Totality, who alone is God, whose nature no one else can comprehend.

**8** He is the one who is without beginning and without end. Not only is he without end—he is immortal for this reason, that he is unbegotten—but he is also unwavering in his eternal existence, in his identity, in that by which he is established, and in that by which he is great.

**9** Neither he himself nor any other can remove himself from that in which he exists, nor can anyone force him to produce an end which he himself has not desired.

**10** He has not had anyone who initiated his own existence. Thus, he is himself unchanged, and no one else can remove him from his existence and his identity and his stability.
