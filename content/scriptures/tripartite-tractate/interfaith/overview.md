## Overview

The Tripartite Tractate addresses some of the deepest questions in religious thought: Is all of humanity equally capable of salvation? Is the material world fundamentally good or fundamentally flawed? Will everything ultimately be restored to its original perfection? These questions arise in every major tradition: Buddhism's teaching on universal Buddha-nature, Hinduism's debate between inclusive and exclusive paths to liberation, Christianity's tension between universal salvation and particular election, Islam's understanding of the relationship between divine mercy and human response. The text's comprehensive system offers one deeply thought-through set of answers to these perennial questions.
