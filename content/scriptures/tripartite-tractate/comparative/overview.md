## Overview

The Tripartite Tractate's three-race anthropology — dividing humanity into pneumatic (spiritual), psychic (ensouled), and hylic (material) types — resonates with hierarchical anthropologies across cultures. The Hindu varnashrama system classifies humanity into four varnas based on spiritual quality; Plato's Republic divides the city into three classes corresponding to three parts of the soul (reason, spirit, appetite); and traditional Chinese cosmology classifies human beings according to the proportion of yin and yang in their constitution. The question of whether these systems reflect genuine spiritual diversity or merely justify social hierarchy is pressing in all traditions.
