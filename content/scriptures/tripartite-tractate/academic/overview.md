## Overview

The Tripartite Tractate is the longest Valentinian text in the Nag Hammadi library and represents one of the most sophisticated Gnostic theological compositions. Scholars have noted its departure from the standard Valentinian system: the replacement of Sophia with the Logos as the fallen aeon, the more positive valuation of the Demiurge, and the inclusive eschatology that extends salvation to psychics as well as pneumatics. Harold Attridge and Elaine Pagels have argued that these modifications reflect a later stage of Valentinian theology that was moving toward rapprochement with proto-orthodox Christianity.
