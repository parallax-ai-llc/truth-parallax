## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Tripartite Tractate a massive transformation of Jewish theology. The text appropriates the concept of a single God but surrounds him with an elaborate hierarchy of divine beings. The creation of the material world is attributed not to God's sovereign act but to the fallen Logos's failed attempt. The three-race anthropology replaces the Jewish concept of the covenant people with a cosmological hierarchy of human types. The eschatological vision replaces the Jewish hope for national restoration with a cosmic drama of spiritual return to the Pleroma.
