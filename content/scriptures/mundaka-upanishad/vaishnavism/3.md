Chapter 3 contains the celebrated two birds parable (3.1.1-2): "Two birds, inseparable companions, cling to the same tree. One eats the sweet fruit; the other looks on without eating." Vaishnavas identify the eating bird as the individual soul (*jiva*) experiencing the fruits of karma, and the watching bird as the Supersoul (*Paramatma*) — Vishnu's localized presence witnessing without attachment.

The chapter's teaching that when the soul, grieving from its sense of helplessness, turns to the Lord and recognizes His glory, its sorrow ends (3.1.2) is the Vaishnava message in its purest form. Liberation comes not through self-effort but through turning to God in humble devotion. The concluding declaration *Satyameva Jayate* — "Truth alone triumphs" (3.1.6) — is read as affirming the ultimate triumph of divine truth over all forms of ignorance and illusion.
