Chapter 2 presents the magnificent bow-and-arrow metaphor: "Om is the bow, the self is the arrow, and Brahman is the target. It should be hit by one who is concentrated" (2.2.3-4). Vaishnavas read this as describing the focused practice of devotion — the aspirant fixes the mind on Vishnu through the sacred syllable Om and, with unwavering concentration, achieves union with the Lord. The "hitting" of the target is not merger but loving connection.

The chapter's cosmogonic passage (2.1.1-3) describes the emanation of the universe from Brahman: "From Him arise life, mind, all the senses, space, wind, fire, water, and earth, the support of all." Vaishnavas understand this as affirming Vishnu's role as the efficient and material cause of creation. The universe is His real creation, not an illusion, and its beauty and order reflect His divine attributes.
