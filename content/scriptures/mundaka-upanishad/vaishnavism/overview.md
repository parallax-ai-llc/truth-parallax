## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Mundaka Upanishad as revealing the supremacy of knowledge of Vishnu over all other forms of learning. The distinction between higher and lower knowledge (1.1.4-5) establishes that even mastery of the Vedas, grammar, and astronomy constitutes "lower knowledge" compared to direct realization of the Supreme Lord.

## Theological Emphases

The two birds parable (3.1.1-2) is central to the Vaishnava reading: the individual soul (*jiva*) and the Supreme Soul (*Paramatma*) share the same tree (body) but have fundamentally different natures. The jiva eats the fruit of karma while the Lord watches, ready to liberate the soul when it turns to Him in devotion. The bow-and-arrow metaphor (2.2.3-4) is read as describing the focused practice of devotion aimed at the Supreme Person.
