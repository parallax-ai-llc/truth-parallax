The bow-and-arrow metaphor (2.2.3-4) has attracted scholarly attention for its use of martial imagery in a contemplative context. This yoking of warrior and meditator imagery is characteristic of the Upanishadic period, when the kshatriya (warrior) class was increasingly associated with philosophical innovation. Scholars note parallels with the Zen arts tradition, where martial skills become vehicles for spiritual practice.

The chapter's cosmogonic hymn (2.1.1-3) has been analyzed as a sophisticated synthesis of earlier Vedic cosmogonic models. The enumation of elements — from Brahman through consciousness, breath, and the physical elements — suggests a proto-evolutionary framework that would later be systematized in Samkhya philosophy. The verse "Brahman expands through tapas" (2.1.1) connects the Upanishadic worldview with the older Vedic concept of *tapas* (creative heat) as the force generating the cosmos.
