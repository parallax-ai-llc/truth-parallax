The two birds parable (3.1.1-2) has been traced to Rig Veda 1.164.20, making it one of the oldest philosophical images in the Indian tradition. Scholars debate whether the Vedic version carries the same philosophical weight or was originally a naturalistic image (perhaps referring to actual birds) later invested with metaphysical significance by the Upanishadic authors.

The verse *Satyameva Jayate* (3.1.6) has been studied for its transformation from a philosophical statement about the nature of truth into a political motto. Adopted as the national motto of the Republic of India in 1947, it illustrates how Upanishadic texts have been mobilized for modern national identity. Scholars note the irony that a text teaching withdrawal from worldly concerns has become a rallying cry for political engagement.
