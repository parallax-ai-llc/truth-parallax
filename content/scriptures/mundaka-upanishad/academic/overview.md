## Overview

Scholars place the Mundaka Upanishad among the middle-period Upanishads, probably composed between the 5th and 3rd centuries BCE. Its explicit critique of Vedic ritual (1.2.7-10) — dismissing ritual practitioners as "fools who regard sacrifice as supreme" — marks a significant development in Indian religious thought, representing the ascendancy of contemplative knowledge over ritual performance.

## Research Themes

The *para/apara vidya* distinction has been analyzed as reflecting an institutional tension between Brahmanical priestly functions (lower knowledge) and emerging contemplative movements (higher knowledge). The two birds parable, shared with the Rig Veda (1.164.20) and Katha Upanishad, has been studied for its textual history. The Indian national motto *Satyameva Jayate* derives from 3.1.6, connecting this ancient text directly to modern Indian political identity.
