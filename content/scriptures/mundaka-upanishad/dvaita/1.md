Madhva reads Chapter 1's knowledge distinction as differentiating between knowledge of the created world (lower) and knowledge of God's nature and attributes (higher). Both forms of knowledge are real and valuable, but knowledge of Vishnu's infinite perfections is supremely important because it alone leads to liberation. Madhva rejects Advaita's dismissal of all conceptual knowledge as "lower" — genuine theological knowledge of God's attributes is *para vidya*.

The critique of ritual in 1.2.7-10 is read by Madhva as targeting ritualism divorced from devotion, not ritual per se. Sacrifices performed with knowledge of and devotion to Vishnu are valid and meritorious. The sparks-from-fire metaphor is read as affirming real creation: individual souls genuinely emanate from God, just as real sparks fly from a real fire. The sparks are real entities distinct from the fire, though dependent on it.
