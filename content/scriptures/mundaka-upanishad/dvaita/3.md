Madhva's reading of the two birds parable is the linchpin of Dvaita's critique of Advaita. The two birds are two — the individual soul and the Supreme Lord — and they remain two eternally. The eating bird (soul) genuinely experiences karma; the watching bird (God) genuinely witnesses without involvement. Their co-presence on the same tree (body) does not imply their identity.

The verse "the knower of Brahman becomes Brahman" (3.2.9) is read by Madhva not as identity but as the soul attaining Brahman-like qualities in liberation — bliss, knowledge, freedom from suffering. The liberated soul resembles God in many respects but never becomes God. Madhva reads *Satyameva Jayate* (3.1.6) as affirming that the truth of God's eternal distinction from souls will ultimately triumph over the false teaching of identity.
