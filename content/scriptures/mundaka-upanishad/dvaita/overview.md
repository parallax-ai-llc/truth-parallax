## Overview

Madhva's reading of the Mundaka Upanishad emphasizes the eternal distinction between the individual soul and the Supreme Lord, against Advaita's identification of the two. The two birds parable (3.1.1) is the key text: Madhva insists the two birds are genuinely two — the dependent soul and the independent God — sharing the same body but differing in nature.

## Theological Emphases

The *para/apara vidya* distinction is read by Dvaita as differentiating between knowledge of God's nature (higher) and knowledge of the created world (lower). Both are real and valuable, but knowledge of Vishnu's supreme attributes is infinitely more important. The bow-and-arrow metaphor is read as the practice of focused devotion directed toward Vishnu, with the soul achieving proximity to God (not identity with Him) upon reaching the target.
