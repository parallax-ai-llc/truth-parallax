## Overview

The Mundaka Upanishad's themes resonate powerfully across religious traditions. Its distinction between higher and lower knowledge parallels the Sufi distinction between *zahir* (exoteric) and *batin* (esoteric) knowledge, the Kabbalistic separation of *nigleh* (revealed) and *nistar* (hidden) Torah, and the Christian distinction between *scientia* (knowledge) and *sapientia* (wisdom).

## Comparative Themes

The bow-and-arrow metaphor (2.2.3-4) — where the self is the arrow, Om is the bow, and Brahman is the target — resonates with Zen archery's integration of self and target, the Sufi metaphor of the heart as an arrow aimed at God, and the Christian concept of spiritual "intentionality." The two birds parable has been compared with the Augustinian distinction between the outer and inner person, and with the Buddhist teaching on the relationship between the deluded mind and Buddha-nature.
