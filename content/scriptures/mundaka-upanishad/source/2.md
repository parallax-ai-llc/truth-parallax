---
title: "Mundaka Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "mundaka-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** This is the truth: As from a blazing fire, sparks essentially akin to it fly forth by the thousands, so also, O beloved, do various beings come forth from the imperishable Brahman and return thither.

**2** He is self-luminous, formless, uncreated, existing both without and within. He is beyond Prana, beyond mind, pure, and higher than the high Imperishable.

**3** From Him are born Prana, mind, all the sense organs, Akasa, Vayu, fire, water, and earth, which supports all.

**4** Fire is His head, His eyes are the sun and moon, the quarters His ears, His speech the revealed Vedas, air is His Prana, the universe is His heart. From His feet came the earth. He is indeed the inner Atman of all beings.

**5** From Him comes Agni, of which the fuel is the sun. From the moon comes rain, and from rain the plants that grow on the earth. The male pours the seed into the female. Thus are created the many beings from the Purusha.

**6** From Him come the Rig, the Sama, the Yajus, the Diksha, all sacrifices, ceremonies, and gifts, the year, the sacrificer, and the worlds in which the moon shines and the sun.

**7** From Him also are born the gods of diverse orders, the Sadhyas, men, cattle, birds, Prana and Apana, rice and barley, tapas, faith, truth, continence, and law.

**8** The seven senses are produced from Him; the seven flames, the seven kinds of fuel, the seven oblations, and these seven seats in which move the Pranas set in the secret place of the heart in groups of seven.

**9** From Him come all the seas and mountains, from Him flow rivers of every kind; from Him come all plants and the essences by which the inner Atman dwells in the elements.

**10** The Purusha alone is all this — karma, tapas, Brahman, the supreme immortal. He who knows this hidden in the cave of the heart, O beloved, destroys even here the knot of ignorance.
