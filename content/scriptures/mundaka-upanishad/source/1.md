---
title: "Mundaka Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "mundaka-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om! Brahma, the creator of the universe and the preserver of the world, was the first among the gods to manifest himself. He taught the knowledge of Brahman, the foundation of all knowledge, to his eldest son Atharva.

**2** That knowledge of Brahman which Brahma taught to Atharva, Atharva taught to Angir in ancient times. He taught it to Satyavaha, the son of Bharadvaja, and Bharadvaja taught it in succession to Angiras.

**3** Shaunaka, the great householder, approached Angiras in the proper manner and asked: "Revered sir, what is that by the knowing of which all this becomes known?"

**4** To him he said: "Two kinds of knowledge must be known — that is what the knowers of Brahman tell us. They are the Higher Knowledge and the Lower Knowledge."

**5** The Lower Knowledge is the Rig Veda, the Yajur Veda, the Sama Veda, the Atharva Veda, Shiksha, Kalpa, Vyakarana, Nirukta, Chhandas, and Jyotisha. And the Higher Knowledge is that by which the Imperishable is attained.

**6** That which cannot be seen, nor seized, which has no origin, which has no properties, which has neither eyes nor ears, neither hands nor feet, which is eternal, all-pervading, omnipresent, and exceedingly subtle — that is the Imperishable, which the wise perceive as the source of all beings.

**7** As the spider sends forth and draws in its thread, as plants grow on the earth, as hair grows on the head and body of a living man — so from the Imperishable arises here the universe.

**8** By tapas Brahman increases in size, and from it food is produced; from food are produced Prana, mind, the elements, the worlds, and karma, and with it, its fruits.

**9** From Him who is omniscient in general and all-knowing in detail, whose tapas consists of knowledge — from Him are produced this Brahma, name, form, and food.
