---
title: "Mundaka Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "mundaka-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Two birds, inseparable companions, dwell upon the same tree. One of them eats the sweet fruit; the other looks on without eating.

**2** Sitting on the same tree, the individual soul is deluded and grieves on account of its helplessness. But when it sees the other — the Lord — whom it worships, and His glory, then it becomes free from grief.

**3** When the seer sees the self-luminous Creator, the Lord, the Purusha, the Brahman source, then he, the knower, shaking off good and evil, stainless, attains supreme identity with the Lord.

**4** The Prana, verily, shines through all beings. The wise man who knows this does not talk too much. He sports in the Atman, delights in the Atman, and performing actions, he is the best among the knowers of Brahman.

**5** The Atman is attainable by truth, by tapas, by right knowledge, and by the constant practice of chastity. He whom the sinless seekers behold is resplendent, pure, and within the body — made of light.

**6** Truth alone prevails, not untruth. By truth is laid out the path leading to the gods, by which the sages who have their desires fulfilled travel to where is that supreme abode of Truth.

**7** Vast, divine, of inconceivable form, subtler than the subtle — it shines forth, farther than the far, yet near at hand, set down in the cave of the heart, even here, among those who see.

**8** It is not grasped by the eye, nor by speech, nor by the other senses, nor by tapas, nor by karma. When one's nature is purified by the light of knowledge, then alone one sees the indivisible Brahman through meditation.

**9** The subtle Atman is to be known by the mind in which the Pranas have entered fivefold. The entire mind of creatures is pervaded by the Pranas. When it is purified, the Atman manifests itself.

**10** Whatever world the man of purified mind desires, and whatever objects he desires — that world he wins, those objects he wins. Therefore he who desires prosperity should worship the knower of the Atman.
