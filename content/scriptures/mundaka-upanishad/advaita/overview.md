## Overview

The Mundaka Upanishad is a cornerstone text for Advaita Vedanta. Shankara's commentary treats it as a systematic exposition of the difference between *apara vidya* (lower knowledge of rituals and Vedic sciences) and *para vidya* (higher knowledge of Brahman). The Indian national motto *Satyameva Jayate* ("Truth alone triumphs") comes from Mundaka 3.1.6.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara reads the sparks-from-fire metaphor (2.1.1) as expressing Brahman's relationship to the phenomenal world: just as sparks emerge from and return to fire without altering its nature, the world appears within and dissolves back into Brahman. The two birds parable (3.1.1) illustrates the superimposition of the empirical self onto the witnessing consciousness. When the eating bird recognizes the watching bird as its own true nature, liberation occurs.
