The two birds parable receives its definitive Advaita interpretation in Shankara's commentary. The eating bird is the *jiva* — the self mistakenly identified with the body-mind and therefore subject to the pleasures and pains of karma. The watching bird is the *atman/Brahman* — the uninvolved witness that never acts, enjoys, or suffers. Liberation is the recognition that the eating bird is an appearance within the watching bird — that one was always the witness, never the experiencer.

The Mundaka's climactic declaration — "the knower of Brahman becomes Brahman" (3.2.9) — is Advaita's central proof-text for the identity of the self with the absolute. Shankara reads this not as a transformation or achievement but as a recognition: one does not become Brahman through effort but realizes that one was always Brahman. The becoming is the dissolution of the false appearance of being something other than Brahman.
