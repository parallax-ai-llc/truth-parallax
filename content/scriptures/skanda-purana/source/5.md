---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the worship of the Divine Mother as taught in the Skanda Purana.

**2** The Divine Mother is the supreme Shakti, the cosmic energy that creates, sustains, and dissolves the universe. She is the power behind all powers, the force behind all forces.

**3** She is Durga, the invincible one, who destroys the demons of ignorance and evil. She is Lakshmi, the goddess of prosperity, who bestows wealth and auspiciousness.

**4** She is Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge, who inspires learning, art, and sacred speech. She is Parvati, the consort of Shiva, the goddess of devotion and love.

**5** She is Kali, the dark goddess, who devours time itself. She is Annapurna, the goddess of food, who nourishes all beings. She is all goddesses in one.

**6** The nine nights of worship — Navaratri — are dedicated to the Divine Mother. On each night, a different aspect of the Goddess is worshipped with special rites.

**7** The first three nights honour Durga, who destroys the impurities of the soul. The next three honour Lakshmi, who bestows the wealth of virtue. The final three honour Sarasvati, who grants the light of knowledge.

**8** The tenth day, Vijayadashami, celebrates the victory of the Goddess over the demon, the triumph of light over darkness, of knowledge over ignorance.

**9** He who observes the Navaratri with fasting, prayer, and devotion obtains the grace of the Divine Mother. She removes all obstacles and grants all desires.

**10** The Divine Mother says: "I am the Mother of all beings. I am the power that moves the universe. He who takes refuge in Me has nothing to fear. I protect My children as a mother hen protects her chicks."
