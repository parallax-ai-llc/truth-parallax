---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 7"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now give the concluding teaching of the Skanda Purana, the essence of all sacred wisdom.

**2** The Supreme Lord is one, though worshipped in many forms. Shiva, Vishnu, Brahma, Shakti, Ganesha, Skanda — all are manifestations of the one Absolute.

**3** He who quarrels over the superiority of one deity over another is deluded by ignorance. The wise see the same Lord in all forms and worship Him with equal devotion.

**4** The purpose of all worship, all pilgrimage, all ritual, all austerity is the purification of the mind. A pure mind is the doorway to liberation.

**5** When the mind is pure, the Self is realized. When the Self is realized, all bondage is destroyed. When bondage is destroyed, the soul attains eternal freedom and bliss.

**6** The three paths — karma, bhakti, and jnana — are not opposed to one another. They are three aspects of the one spiritual life. Action purifies, devotion transforms, knowledge liberates.

**7** In this age of Kali, the chanting of the divine name is the sovereign remedy for all spiritual ills. The name of the Lord is more powerful than all mantras, all rituals, all pilgrimages combined.

**8** The Lord dwells in the heart of every being. He is not far away but is nearer than one's own breath. To find Him, one need only turn within with sincerity and love.

**9** He who sees the Lord in all beings and all beings in the Lord — he never turns away from the Lord, and the Lord never turns away from him. This is the supreme realization.

**10** Thus ends the Skanda Purana, the largest and most comprehensive of all the Puranas. May Lord Skanda, the divine commander, grant victory to all seekers of truth. Om, peace, peace, peace.
