---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The sages asked Suta: "Relate to us the Skanda Purana, the largest of all the Puranas, which describes the glory of Lord Skanda, the son of Shiva."

**2** Suta said: Lord Skanda, also known as Kartikeya, Kumara, and Murugan, is the commander of the divine armies, the vanquisher of the demon Tarakasura, the embodiment of divine youth and valour.

**3** When the demon Tarakasura, having obtained the boon that only a son of Shiva could slay him, terrorized the three worlds, the gods sought a way to unite Shiva with Parvati.

**4** From the union of Shiva and Parvati arose a seed of divine fire, so powerful that neither heaven nor earth could contain it. The fire-god Agni carried it to the Ganga, and the Ganga deposited it in a bed of reeds.

**5** From that divine seed, the child Skanda was born, brilliant as a thousand suns, with six heads and twelve arms. The six Krittikas, the Pleiades, nursed him, and thus he is called Kartikeya.

**6** The child grew with supernatural speed. On the seventh day, he was anointed the commander of the divine armies. The gods gave him their weapons, and he set forth to battle Tarakasura.

**7** The battle shook the three worlds. Skanda's spear, the Vel, blazed with divine fire as he hurled it at the demon. Tarakasura was split asunder, and the worlds were freed.

**8** The gods rejoiced and showered flowers upon the victorious Skanda. Shiva and Parvati embraced their son, and all creation celebrated.

**9** Lord Skanda represents the power of divine knowledge that destroys the demon of ignorance. His six heads represent the six directions, seeing all truth from every angle.

**10** He who worships Lord Skanda with devotion obtains valour, wisdom, and protection. The devotee of Skanda conquers all enemies, both external and internal.
