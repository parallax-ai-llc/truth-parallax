---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Suta said: The Skanda Purana describes the sacred places of the earth in greater detail than any other Purana. I shall relate the glory of the foremost among them.

**2** The sacred city of Kashi, Varanasi, is the eternal abode of Lord Shiva. It is said that Shiva carries this city upon His trident, never allowing it to be destroyed even during cosmic dissolution.

**3** He who dies in Kashi, whether he be righteous or sinful, attains liberation, for Lord Shiva himself whispers the Taraka mantra into the ear of every being that dies there.

**4** The five sacred zones of Kashi — from the Manikarnika Ghat to the Panchakroshi road — encompass the entire spiritual universe. To circumambulate Kashi is to circumambulate the cosmos.

**5** The Lord said: "Kashi is My eternal dwelling. I never leave it. Even when the worlds are dissolved, Kashi remains, shining with My light upon the prongs of My trident."

**6** Prayaga, the king of sacred places, where the Ganga, Yamuna, and invisible Sarasvati meet, is the place of the greatest merit. He who bathes there during the Magh Mela is freed from the sins of countless lifetimes.

**7** Rameswaram, where Lord Rama worshipped Shiva before crossing to Lanka, is the place where the worship of Shiva and Vishnu are united. It teaches that there is no difference between the two.

**8** Badrinath in the Himalayas, Dwarka on the western shore, Puri on the eastern shore, and Rameswaram in the south — these four dhams encompass the four directions and the totality of the sacred.

**9** But the wise know that the truest sacred place is the heart purified by devotion. Where the mind is pure and the heart is full of love for the Lord, there is the holiest of all places.

**10** He who visits sacred places with a pure heart and a devoted mind obtains the full fruit of pilgrimage. He who visits them with a corrupt heart obtains nothing.
