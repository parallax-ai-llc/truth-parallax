---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the nature of Dharma as taught in the Skanda Purana, the eternal law that sustains the universe.

**2** Dharma is that which upholds, sustains, and protects. It is the cosmic order, the moral law, the foundation of all well-being. Without dharma, the world would collapse into chaos.

**3** The roots of dharma are the Vedas, the Smritis, the conduct of the virtuous, and the conscience of the pure-hearted. When in doubt, follow the example of the saints.

**4** Non-violence is the highest dharma. He who refrains from harming any creature in thought, word, and deed practises the supreme virtue.

**5** Truth is the foundation of dharma. The world rests upon truth. The gods are sustained by truth. By truth the sun shines and the wind blows. Truth is the eternal refuge.

**6** Compassion is the essence of dharma. He who feels the suffering of others as his own, who acts to relieve that suffering, who extends kindness to all beings — he is the true practitioner of dharma.

**7** Forgiveness is the ornament of dharma. He who forgives those who have wronged him, who returns good for evil, who bears no malice — he is stronger than the strong.

**8** Self-control is the discipline of dharma. He who has conquered his senses, who has mastered his anger and desire, who is even-minded in pleasure and pain — he is established in dharma.

**9** Charity is the practice of dharma. He who gives to the needy, who shares his wealth with the world, who holds nothing for himself alone — he is the true upholder of dharma.

**10** He who follows dharma prospers in this world and attains the highest world in the next. He who abandons dharma is abandoned by all that is good. Therefore, let a man never forsake dharma under any circumstance.
