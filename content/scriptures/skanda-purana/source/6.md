---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 6"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the nature of sacred vows and observances as taught in the Skanda Purana.

**2** A vow, or Vrata, is a sacred undertaking in which the devotee dedicates himself to a specific spiritual practice for a fixed period of time.

**3** The Ekadashi vow — fasting on the eleventh day of each lunar fortnight — is the most important of all vows for the devotees of Vishnu. By observing Ekadashi, one is freed from the sins of the present life.

**4** The Pradosha vow — worshipping Shiva during the twilight hour on the thirteenth day of each lunar fortnight — is most dear to Lord Shiva. He who observes it attains Shiva's grace.

**5** The Chaturthi vow — worshipping Ganesha on the fourth day of each lunar fortnight — removes all obstacles and grants success in all undertakings.

**6** The Somavara vow — fasting on Mondays in honour of Shiva — is practised especially by women seeking virtuous husbands and by men seeking spiritual merit.

**7** The Satyanarayan vow — the worship of Lord Vishnu as the embodiment of truth — is performed on full moon days and brings prosperity, health, and happiness to the family.

**8** All vows should be observed with sincerity, purity, and devotion. A vow observed without faith is like a seed planted in barren soil — it bears no fruit.

**9** The highest vow is the vow of truth. He who has resolved to speak the truth at all times, to live by truth, and to seek truth above all — he has taken the supreme vow.

**10** He who observes sacred vows with devotion and breaks them not attains the favour of the gods and the fulfilment of all his desires. This is the teaching of the Skanda Purana.
