---
title: "Skanda Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "skanda-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the glory of the river Ganga, the most sacred of all rivers, the purifier of the three worlds.

**2** The Ganga descends from the heavens, flowing from the toe of Lord Vishnu. She was brought down to earth by the penance of King Bhagiratha, who sought to liberate the souls of his ancestors.

**3** When the Ganga descended from heaven, her force was so great that she would have shattered the earth. Lord Shiva received her in His matted locks and released her gently upon the earth.

**4** The Ganga flows through the three worlds — in heaven as the Mandakini, on earth as the Ganga, and in the nether world as the Bhogavati. She is the purifier of all three realms.

**5** He who bathes in the Ganga is freed from all sins. He who drinks her water with faith is purified in body and soul. He who dies on her banks attains liberation.

**6** Even the utterance of the name "Ganga" destroys accumulated sins. He who remembers the Ganga at the time of death attains the supreme abode.

**7** The Ganga is the mother of all rivers. She carries in her waters the blessings of Vishnu, the grace of Shiva, and the power to liberate all who touch her.

**8** The ashes of the dead, when immersed in the Ganga, ensure the liberation of the departed soul. This is why the banks of the Ganga are the most sacred cremation grounds.

**9** The Lord said: "The Ganga is My own form, flowing upon the earth for the liberation of all beings. He who dishonours her dishonours Me."

**10** He who hears the glory of the Ganga, who worships her with devotion, who bathes in her waters — he is freed from all bondage and attains the lotus feet of the Lord.
