---
title: "Narada Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "narada-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Narada continued: "I shall now describe the nature of Dharma in its four aspects: truth, compassion, austerity, and purity."

**2** "In the Satya Yuga, Dharma stands on four legs. In the Treta Yuga, on three. In the Dvapara Yuga, on two. In the Kali Yuga, on one leg alone."

**3** "In the Kali Yuga, truth is diminished, compassion is rare, austerity is neglected, and purity is scarcely to be found. Men become short-lived, weak in body and mind."

**4** "But even in the Kali Yuga, there is one great advantage: that which in other ages required long periods of meditation and sacrifice can be attained simply by chanting the name of the Lord."

**5** "The names of Vishnu are: Narayana, Vasudeva, Hari, Krishna, Govinda, Madhava, Keshava, and Achyuta. He who chants these names with devotion is freed from all sins."

**6** "The twelve months of the year are sacred to Vishnu in His various forms. In each month, a particular form of the Lord is worshipped with appropriate rituals and offerings."

**7** "In the month of Chaitra, Vishnu is worshipped as Vasudeva. In Vaishakha, as Sankarshana. In Jyeshtha, as Pradyumna. In Ashadha, as Aniruddha."

**8** "The Ekadashi vrata — fasting on the eleventh day of each fortnight — is the most sacred of all vratas. It is dear to Vishnu above all other observances."

**9** "He who observes the Ekadashi fast with devotion, abstaining from food and drink, keeping vigil through the night, and meditating on the Lord — he attains the merit of a thousand sacrifices."

**10** "The sacred rivers — Ganga, Yamuna, Sarasvati, Godavari, Narmada, Sindhu, and Kaveri — are the forms of the goddess who purifies all who bathe in them."

**11** "But greater than the merit of bathing in all sacred rivers is the merit of a moment's association with a devotee of the Lord. For the devotee carries the Lord in his heart."

**12** "Thus has been described the second section of the Narada Purana, concerning Dharma, vratas, and sacred places."
