---
title: "Narada Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "narada-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Narada said: "I shall now describe the path of liberation and the supreme knowledge by which the cycle of birth and death is destroyed."

**2** "The Atman is eternal, unchanging, and beyond all modification. It is not born, nor does it die. It is not the body, nor the mind, nor the senses."

**3** "The Atman is Sat-Chit-Ananda — existence, consciousness, and bliss. It is the witness of all states — waking, dreaming, and deep sleep — yet it is beyond all three."

**4** "The cause of bondage is ignorance — the false identification of the Atman with the body, the mind, and the senses. The cause of liberation is knowledge — the direct realization that the Atman is Brahman."

**5** "The wise man should practice discrimination between the real and the unreal, between the eternal and the transient. The Atman alone is real; all else is the play of Maya."

**6** "Through devotion to the Lord, through the study of the scriptures, through the company of the holy ones, and through meditation — the veil of ignorance is gradually removed."

**7** "When the veil is completely removed, the Atman shines forth in its own nature, self-luminous, all-pervading, and blissful."

**8** "He who has realized the Atman sees the Lord in all beings and all beings in the Lord. For him there is no more sorrow, no more delusion, no more death."

**9** "The liberated one dwells in the world but is not of the world. Like the lotus in water, he is untouched by the impurities of worldly life."

**10** "He performs his duties without attachment, offers all actions to the Lord, and remains ever absorbed in the contemplation of the supreme truth."

**11** "This is the essence of the Narada Purana: that devotion to Vishnu is the supreme path, that knowledge of the Atman is the supreme goal, and that the grace of the Lord is the supreme means."

**12** Suta said: "He who hears this Purana with devotion, he who recites it, he who studies it — he shall be freed from all sins and shall attain the supreme abode of Vishnu. Om Tat Sat."
