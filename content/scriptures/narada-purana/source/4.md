---
title: "Narada Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "narada-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Narada said: "I shall now describe the nature of creation and the genealogy of the great dynasties."

**2** "From Brahma were born the mind-born sons: Marichi, Atri, Angiras, Pulastya, Pulaha, Kratu, Bhrigu, Vasishtha, Daksha, and Narada — I myself."

**3** "From these Prajapatis sprang the races of gods, demons, humans, and all living beings."

**4** "Daksha had sixty daughters, of whom thirteen were given to Kashyapa. From these thirteen wives of Kashyapa arose the gods, the demons, the Nagas, the Gandharvas, the birds, and the animals."

**5** "Aditi bore the twelve Adityas, the gods of the sun. Diti bore the Daityas, the great demons. Danu bore the Danavas. Vinata bore Garuda, the lord of birds, and Aruna, the charioteer of the sun."

**6** "Kadru bore the thousand-headed serpents, the Nagas, of whom Shesha, Vasuki, and Takshaka are the greatest."

**7** "From Manu Svayambhuva arose the race of men. His sons and daughters populated the earth with the four varnas — Brahmana, Kshatriya, Vaishya, and Shudra."

**8** "The Solar Dynasty descended from Surya through Manu, and from this line arose Rama, the seventh incarnation of Vishnu."

**9** "The Lunar Dynasty descended from Soma through Budha, and from this line arose Krishna, the eighth incarnation of Vishnu."

**10** "These two great dynasties are the pillars of human history, and from them have sprung the kings and heroes who have shaped the world."

**11** "The wise man who studies the genealogies of the gods and kings gains merit equal to performing the great sacrifices."

**12** "Thus has been described the fourth section of the Narada Purana, concerning creation and the royal dynasties."
