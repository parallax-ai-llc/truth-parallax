---
title: "Narada Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "narada-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Narada said: "I shall now describe the incarnations of Vishnu, by which He descends into the world to restore Dharma and to protect the righteous."

**2** "When the world is overrun by evil, when Dharma declines and Adharma prevails, the Lord takes birth among men for the protection of the good and the destruction of the wicked."

**3** "The first incarnation was Matsya, the Fish, by which the Lord saved the Vedas from the deluge and preserved the seeds of all creation."

**4** "The second was Kurma, the Tortoise, who supported Mount Mandara on His back when the gods and demons churned the ocean of milk."

**5** "The third was Varaha, the Boar, who lifted the earth from the depths of the cosmic ocean where the demon Hiranyaksha had hidden it."

**6** "The fourth was Narasimha, the Man-Lion, who appeared from a pillar to slay the demon Hiranyakashipu, who had obtained a boon that he could not be killed by man or beast, by day or by night, inside or outside."

**7** "The fifth was Vamana, the Dwarf, who measured the three worlds in three steps and restored the sovereignty of the gods from the demon king Bali."

**8** "The sixth was Parashurama, who destroyed the warrior kings who had become oppressors of the earth, and restored the Brahmanas to their rightful position."

**9** "The seventh was Rama, the prince of Ayodhya, who slew the demon king Ravana and established the reign of righteousness upon the earth."

**10** "The eighth was Krishna, the dark one, who spoke the Bhagavad Gita on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, revealing the supreme knowledge to Arjuna."

**11** "The ninth was Buddha, who taught the path of compassion and non-violence, leading beings away from the slaughter of animals in sacrifice."

**12** "The tenth shall be Kalki, who will appear at the end of the Kali Yuga, riding a white horse, to destroy the wicked and to restore the Satya Yuga upon the earth."

**13** "He who meditates upon these incarnations with devotion is freed from the cycle of birth and death and attains the eternal abode of Vishnu."
