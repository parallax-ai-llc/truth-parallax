---
title: "Narada Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "narada-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om! Salutations to the blessed Narayana. Suta spoke to the sages assembled at the forest of Naimisha: "I shall narrate the ancient Purana as told by Narada to Sanatkumara."

**2** Sanatkumara said: "O divine sage Narada, thou who roamest the three worlds, tell me of the supreme Dharma, the path that leads to liberation."

**3** Narada replied: "O great one, I shall tell thee the Narada Purana, which contains the essence of all the Vedas and Shastras. Listen with devotion."

**4** "In the beginning, Narayana alone existed. There was no Brahma, no Vishnu, no Shiva, no sun, no moon, no stars. Only the supreme Purusha existed, without a second."

**5** "From Narayana arose the cosmic waters. In those waters He placed His seed, and from that seed arose the golden egg of Brahma."

**6** "Brahma, the creator, was born within the egg. He created the heavens and the earth, the gods and the demons, the humans and the animals."

**7** "He established the four Vedas — Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva — as the foundation of all knowledge and Dharma."

**8** "The supreme Dharma is the worship of Vishnu, the preserver of all worlds. Through devotion to Vishnu, one attains liberation from the cycle of birth and death."

**9** "The wise man should perform his prescribed duties according to his station in life, offering all actions to the Lord, without attachment to the fruits."

**10** "The path of devotion is the easiest and most direct path to the Lord. Even the lowliest of beings, if devoted to the Lord, attains the highest goal."

**11** "I shall now describe the sacred places of pilgrimage, the festivals, the vratas, and the rituals by which one may purify the mind and attain the grace of the Lord."

**12** "He who hears this Purana with faith and devotion is freed from all sins and attains the supreme abode of Vishnu."
