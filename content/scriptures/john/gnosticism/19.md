John 19's crucifixion is the passage most thoroughly reinterpreted by Gnostic Christology. Docetic Gnostics held that the divine Christ departed from the body of Jesus before or during the crucifixion — the material shell suffered and died, but the heavenly being remained impassible. The Basilidean tradition held that Simon of Cyrene was crucified in Jesus' place through a divine substitution. The Valentinian approach was more subtle: the psychic and material elements of Jesus suffered, but the pneumatic Christ observed from above, untouched by pain.

The cry 'It is finished' (*tetelestai*, 19:30) is the Savior's declaration that the mission in the material world is complete and the return to the Pleroma is imminent. The blood and water from the pierced side (19:34) are interpreted symbolically: water represents the purification of gnosis, blood represents the psychic element left behind. The tearing of the temple veil — though narrated in the Synoptics, implied in John's theological framework — represents the dissolution of the barrier between the material world and the Pleroma. The Savior's passage through death opens the way for all pneumatic souls to follow.
