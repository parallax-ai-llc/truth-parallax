John 5's healing at Bethesda and the subsequent discourse are read through the Gnostic lens of the Savior's authority over the Demiurge's systems. The Sabbath healing (5:1-18) demonstrates that the heavenly Christ operates outside the Demiurge's temporal framework — the Sabbath is the Demiurge's ordinance, and the Savior is not bound by it. The declaration 'My Father is working until now' (5:17) distinguishes the true Father (the God of the Pleroma) from the Demiurge who rested on the seventh day.

The discourse on the Son's relationship to the Father (5:19-30) maps the Gnostic relationship between the Savior and the transcendent God. The Son does 'only what he sees the Father doing' (5:19) — this describes the Aeon's perfect reflection of the divine source. The authority to judge (5:22, 27) is the Savior's prerogative, not the Demiurge's — the final judgment will reveal the Demiurge's ignorance and liberate the divine sparks. The four witnesses (5:33-47) — John, the works, the Father, and the Scriptures — constitute a multilayered testimony that the pneumatic reader can decode.
