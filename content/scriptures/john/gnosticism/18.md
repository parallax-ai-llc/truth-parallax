John 18's arrest narrative reveals the Savior's sovereign power even in the moment of apparent capture. The 'I AM' that causes the soldiers to fall (18:5-6) demonstrates that the archonic powers cannot seize the heavenly Christ without his consent. Gnostic interpreters read the arrest as a voluntary submission — the Savior allows himself to be taken because his mission requires passage through the archonic realm of death.

The trial before Pilate (18:28-40) is a confrontation between the Savior and the archonic political power. Jesus' statement 'My kingdom is not of this world' (18:36) is literally true in Gnostic cosmology: the Savior's kingdom is the Pleroma, entirely beyond the material cosmos. Pilate's question 'What is truth?' (18:38) embodies the archonic condition — unable to recognize truth even when it stands incarnate before them. The representative of Roman power, the greatest earthly authority, is revealed as merely an agent of the Demiurge.
