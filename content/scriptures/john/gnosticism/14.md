John 14 is the most important chapter in the Farewell Discourse for Gnostic theology. The 'many mansions' (*monai*) in the Father's house (14:2) are identified with the Aeons of the Pleroma — the divine dwelling places to which the pneumatic souls will return. Each Aeon represents a level of divine reality, and the Savior prepares a place for each type of pneumatic soul according to its spiritual capacity.

The declaration 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life' (14:6) is the Gnostic charter: the Savior is the exclusive path from the material world to the Pleroma. The Paraclete promise (14:16-17, 26) describes the ongoing presence of divine gnosis in the community after the Savior's departure. The 'Spirit of truth whom the world cannot receive' (14:17) confirms the exclusivity of gnosis — it is available only to the pneumatic elect, not to the hylic world or the psychic masses. The promise 'we will come to him and make our home with him' (14:23) describes the divine indwelling that constitutes the Gnostic experience.
