The Bread of Life discourse (6:22-71) is reinterpreted in Gnostic thought as a teaching about spiritual, not material, nourishment. The bread of life is gnosis itself — the knowledge of the true God that sustains the pneumatic soul. 'Unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood' (6:53) refers not to a physical sacrament but to the internalization of the divine knowledge that the Savior brings from the Pleroma.

The departure of many disciples (6:66) illustrates the fundamental Gnostic principle of selection: the masses cannot receive gnosis and fall away, leaving only the elect pneumatics. Peter's confession — 'You have the words of eternal life' (6:68) — identifies the pneumatic remnant who remain faithful to the Revealer. Heracleon's commentary distinguished between the feeding miracle (addressed to the psychic masses) and the discourse (addressed to the pneumatic elite), reflecting the Valentinian tripartite anthropology of hylic, psychic, and pneumatic types.
