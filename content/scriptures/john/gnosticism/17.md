The High Priestly Prayer (17:1-26) is the Savior's prayer of return to the Pleroma, and Gnostic interpreters read it as a revelation of the divine hierarchy. The mutual glorification of Father and Son (17:1-5) describes the relationship between the transcendent God and the Aeon who descended as Savior. 'Glorify me in your own presence with the glory that I had with you before the world existed' (17:5) explicitly affirms the Savior's pre-cosmic existence in the Pleroma.

The prayer for the disciples (17:6-19) distinguishes the elect from the world: 'I am not praying for the world but for those whom you have given me' (17:9). This exclusivity is precisely what Gnostic theology teaches — salvation is not universal but limited to the pneumatic elect. The prayer for unity — 'that they may all be one, even as we are one' (17:22) — describes the reintegration of the divine sparks into the Pleroma, achieving the original unity disrupted by Sophia's error and the Demiurge's creation.
