John 21 is read by Gnostic interpreters as an epilogue containing hidden teachings about the community's esoteric tradition. The miraculous catch of 153 fish (21:1-14) is subjected to elaborate numerical interpretation — Pythagorean and Gnostic numerology assigned deep significance to numbers, and 153 was calculated as the triangular number of 17, itself the sum of various symbolically significant numbers.

Peter's restoration (21:15-17) and the contrast with the Beloved Disciple (21:20-23) is particularly significant. Gnostic communities typically identified with the Beloved Disciple rather than Peter, seeing in this unnamed figure the founder of the esoteric tradition distinct from Petrine institutional Christianity. The Beloved Disciple leans on Jesus' breast (13:23) — the position of intimate knowledge — and remains while Peter follows at a distance. The statement 'If it is my will that he remain until I come' (21:22) was read as conferring a special, permanent authority on the esoteric tradition represented by the Beloved Disciple. The final verse — 'the world itself could not contain the books' (21:25) — confirms that the written Gospel is only a fragment of the full revelation, the rest transmitted through the secret oral tradition that Gnostic teachers claimed to possess.
