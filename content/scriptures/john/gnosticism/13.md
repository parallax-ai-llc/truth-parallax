John 13's foot washing is interpreted by Gnostic readers as a purification ritual — the Savior cleansing the pneumatic disciples of the material contamination accumulated through existence in the Demiurge's world. The act of washing symbolizes the removal of the archonic influence that clings to even the most spiritual souls. Peter's initial refusal (13:8) represents the psychic's resistance to the radical transformation that gnosis requires.

The departure of Judas (13:21-30) is charged with cosmic significance: Judas embodies the hylic (material) type who serves the archonic purpose. 'It was night' (13:30) — the departure into darkness is the return to the realm of ignorance. The new commandment to love (13:34) defines the pneumatic community — love among the elect is the earthly reflection of the divine love within the Pleroma. This love is exclusive: it binds the pneumatic insiders against the hostile cosmos.
