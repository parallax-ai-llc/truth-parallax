John 12 marks the transition from the Book of Signs to the Book of Glory, and Gnostic interpreters read this transition as the beginning of the Savior's return to the Pleroma. The anointing at Bethany (12:1-8) is a ritual preparation for the departure — the fragrant oil symbolizes the divine substance that will soon be released from its material vessel. The triumphal entry (12:12-19) is the pneumatic king entering the Demiurge's capital to pronounce judgment.

The grain of wheat metaphor (12:24) — 'unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit' — describes the descent of the divine spark into matter and its eventual liberation through gnosis. The voice from heaven (12:28) is the voice of the true Father, breaking through the cosmic barrier. The judgment of 'the ruler of this world' (12:31) is the judgment of the Demiurge and his archons — the cross is the moment when the archonic powers are unmasked and overthrown.
