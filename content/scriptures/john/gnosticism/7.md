John 7's Feast of Tabernacles setting provides the backdrop for Jesus' offer of 'rivers of living water' (7:37-38). Gnostic interpreters read the water ceremony of Sukkot as a shadow of the true divine outpouring — the Spirit that flows from the Pleroma through the Savior to the pneumatic elect. The Demiurge's festival, with its material rituals, can only point toward the spiritual reality that gnosis reveals.

The division among the people (7:40-44) reflects the Gnostic understanding that humanity is divided into types: some are drawn to the light, others remain in darkness. The Pharisees' dismissal of the crowd (7:49) ironically illustrates the archons' contempt for those they rule. Nicodemus' cautious defense (7:50-52) shows the psychic type at work — advocating for reason and procedure but unable to make the leap to full gnosis.
