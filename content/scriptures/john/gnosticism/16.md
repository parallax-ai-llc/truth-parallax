John 16's Paraclete teaching continues the Gnostic understanding of divine gnosis persisting after the Savior's departure. The Spirit of truth will 'convict the world concerning sin and righteousness and judgment' (16:8-11) — this describes the Gnostic critique of the archonic moral and religious order. The 'sin' is the world's failure to recognize the Savior; the 'righteousness' is the Savior's return to the Father (the Pleroma); the 'judgment' is the condemnation of the archon of this world.

The promise to 'guide you into all the truth' (16:13) describes the progressive revelation of gnosis that the pneumatic community receives through inspired teachers and secret traditions. 'He will declare to you the things that are to come' (16:13) refers to the apocalyptic revelations about the dissolution of the material world and the return of all divine sparks to the Pleroma. The declaration 'I have overcome the world' (16:33) is the Savior's triumphant announcement of victory over the archonic cosmos.
