John 15's vine imagery is reinterpreted in Gnostic theology as describing the relationship between the Pleroma (the vine) and the divine sparks (the branches). The abiding of the branches in the vine (15:4) represents the pneumatic soul's connection to its divine source — a connection that the archons seek to sever. The pruning (15:2) is the removal of material attachments and psychic delusions that prevent the soul from bearing the fruit of gnosis.

The contrast between the community of the elect and the hostile world (15:18-25) perfectly reflects the Gnostic self-understanding: the pneumatic community is hated by the world because it possesses knowledge that threatens the archonic order. 'If you were of the world, the world would love you as its own' (15:19) — the archons love those who serve their system and persecute those who see through it. The witness of the Spirit (15:26) against the world is the ongoing activity of gnosis exposing the Demiurge's lie.
