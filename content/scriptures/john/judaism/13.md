John 13's foot washing takes place before or during a Passover meal (the precise chronology differs from the Synoptics). Jewish scholars note the significance of the Passover setting: the festival of liberation from slavery becomes the context for Jesus' act of voluntary servitude. The foot washing would have been understood as a slave's task, and its performance by the master reverses the social hierarchy in a way that would have been shocking.

The departure of Judas after receiving the morsel (13:26-30) is set against the Passover context, where sharing bread carries deep covenantal significance. Jewish scholars note that betrayal at a shared meal violates one of the most basic norms of ancient Near Eastern hospitality. The 'new commandment' to love one another (13:34) echoes Leviticus 19:18, though Jewish scholars debate whether the qualifier 'as I have loved you' genuinely adds something new or simply intensifies the existing commandment.
