Jewish scholars engage the Johannine Prologue through the lens of Jewish Wisdom literature and the Aramaic concept of *Memra* (Word). The Logos of John 1:1 has roots in the personified Wisdom of Proverbs 8, Sirach 24, and the Wisdom of Solomon, where Wisdom is present at creation, dwells with God, and comes to dwell among Israel. The Targumic *Memra* — God's creative and revelatory Word — provides another Jewish background for the Logos concept. Daniel Boyarin has argued that the Johannine Prologue fits within the spectrum of first-century Jewish theology about divine mediator figures.

However, the claim that 'the Word was God' (1:1) and 'became flesh' (1:14) moves beyond what Jewish theology can accommodate. The incarnation of a divine being in human flesh crosses a boundary that Jewish monotheism, in all its diversity, ultimately does not permit. Jewish scholars note that the Prologue represents a moment in the trajectory of Christological development where the parting of the ways between Judaism and the Jesus movement becomes theologically inevitable.
