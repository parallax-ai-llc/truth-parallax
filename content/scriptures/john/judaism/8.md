John 8 contains the most intense confrontation between Jesus and 'the Jews' in the Gospel, making it one of the most problematic chapters in Jewish-Christian relations. The dialogue about Abraham (8:31-59) escalates from a discussion of spiritual freedom to mutual accusations of illegitimacy and demonic influence. Jesus' statement 'You are of your father the devil' (8:44), directed at Jews who had believed in him, has been used throughout Christian history to demonize the Jewish people — a reading that Jewish scholars emphatically reject as a distortion of an intra-communal polemic.

The 'I AM' declaration — 'Before Abraham was, I AM' (8:58) — represents the point of no return in the dialogue. The claim to pre-existence and the use of the divine name (Exodus 3:14) constitutes, from a Jewish perspective, blasphemy — hence the attempt to stone Jesus (8:59). Jewish scholars argue that this passage reflects not the historical Jesus but the Johannine community's developed Christology, projected back onto the narrative as part of an increasingly bitter dialogue with the synagogue.
