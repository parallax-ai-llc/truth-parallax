John 5's healing at Bethesda and the Sabbath controversy engage with Jewish halakhic issues. The pool at Bethesda has been archaeologically confirmed, supporting the narrative's topographical accuracy. The command to carry a mat on the Sabbath (5:8-10) violates the rabbinic prohibition against carrying objects in the public domain on Shabbat (m. Shabbat 7:2). Jewish scholars debate whether Jesus was challenging the oral law (later codified in the Mishnah) or asserting a different interpretation of Sabbath rest.

The discourse on the Son's relationship to the Father (5:17-47) represents the theological claims that most sharply divide Judaism and Christianity. The assertion that Jesus is 'making himself equal with God' (5:18) is precisely the charge that Jewish theology levels against Johannine Christology. The appeal to Moses as a witness to Jesus (5:46) employs a christological reading of the Torah that Jewish interpretation rejects. Jewish scholars note that the passage reflects a community in bitter conflict with the synagogue, projecting its theological claims back onto the historical Jesus.
