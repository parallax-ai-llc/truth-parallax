John 21's epilogue narrates the appearance at the Sea of Tiberias and Peter's restoration. The fishing scene (21:1-14) is set in Galilee, and Jewish scholars note the return to the ordinary world of work after the extraordinary events of the passion and resurrection. The miraculous catch of 153 fish has generated extensive numerical speculation; Jewish scholars familiar with gematria traditions note that the number may have symbolic significance, though no consensus exists on its meaning.

Peter's threefold restoration (21:15-17) is examined by Jewish scholars as a narrative resolution to the threefold denial. The distinction in the Johannine community between Peter (the leader of the broader apostolic movement) and the Beloved Disciple (the founder of the Johannine community) reflects the plurality of early Christian communities and their sometimes competing claims to authority. The final verse — 'the world itself could not contain the books' (21:25) — is a literary hyperbole familiar from rabbinic rhetoric, where similar claims are made about the breadth of Torah.
