John 15's vine imagery is read by Muslim scholars as describing the relationship between a prophet and his faithful community. The call to 'abide in me' (15:4) is understood as a call to follow Jesus' teaching and example — remaining faithful to monotheistic worship and moral righteousness. The pruning (15:2) represents the purification that comes through trials, a concept well established in Islamic spirituality (*tasawwuf*).

The commandment to love (15:12-17) aligns with the Islamic emphasis on love and brotherhood among believers. However, the warning about 'the world's' hatred (15:18-25) is read by Muslim scholars in a different light: the opposition Jesus faced came not from 'the world' in a cosmic sense but from specific groups who rejected his prophetic message — the same pattern repeated with every prophet, including Muhammad. The persecution of Jesus' followers (15:20) prefigures the persecution of the early Muslim community in Mecca.
