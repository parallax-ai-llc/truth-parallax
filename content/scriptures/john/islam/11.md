The raising of Lazarus (11:1-44) is of particular significance in Islamic interpretation because the Quran explicitly affirms that Jesus raised the dead by God's permission (3:49, 5:110). This miracle is therefore one of the most Quranically validated events in the Gospel narrative. Muslim scholars emphasize the crucial qualifier: 'by God's permission' (*bi-idhni Allah*). Jesus does not raise Lazarus by his own inherent power but as an instrument of God's will.

Jesus' declaration 'I am the resurrection and the life' (11:25) is reinterpreted: Jesus may be a means through which God grants resurrection and life, but he is not himself the source. The weeping of Jesus (11:35) powerfully confirms his humanity — a prophet deeply moved by human suffering. The political consequences of the miracle (11:47-53) — the plot to kill Jesus — fit the Quranic pattern of prophets facing persecution from those who reject their message. However, the plot's success is denied: God would not allow his prophet to be killed (Quran 4:157).
