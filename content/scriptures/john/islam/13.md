John 13's foot washing is appreciated by Muslim scholars as a beautiful example of prophetic humility and service. The hadith literature records numerous instances of Muhammad's humility — mending his own shoes, serving his household, sitting with the poor. Jesus' act of washing his disciples' feet fits this prophetic pattern of servant leadership.

The departure of Judas (13:21-30) and his betrayal are acknowledged in Islamic tradition, though the theological implications differ. The new commandment — 'love one another as I have loved you' (13:34) — resonates with Islamic values of brotherhood (*ukhuwwah*) and compassion (*rahmah*). The prediction of Peter's denial (13:36-38) illustrates the frailty of human commitment, a theme present throughout the Quran in its accounts of those who initially follow prophets but later waver.
