The wedding at Cana (2:1-11) is understood by Muslim scholars as a prophetic miracle (*mu'jiza*) — a sign granted by God to authenticate Jesus' mission. The transformation of water into wine presents a secondary difficulty, since wine consumption is prohibited (*haram*) in Islam (Quran 5:90-91). Some Muslim commentators suggest that the original miracle may have been altered in transmission, while others note that wine was not prohibited in the Mosaic law and that Jesus was operating under a different dispensation.

The temple cleansing (2:13-22) is appreciated as a prophetic protest against corruption in worship, consistent with the role of prophets throughout Islamic salvation history. Muhammad himself purified the Kaaba of idols upon the conquest of Mecca, establishing a parallel. Jesus' zeal for the purity of worship resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *tawhid* — keeping worship directed solely and purely toward God.
