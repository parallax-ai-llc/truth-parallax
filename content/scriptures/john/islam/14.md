John 14 contains the passages most hotly contested between Christian and Islamic interpreters. Jesus' statement 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life' (14:6) is rejected in its exclusivist interpretation — Islam affirms that all authentic prophets, from Adam to Muhammad, showed the way to God. The claim that 'no one comes to the Father except through me' is understood as applicable only to Jesus' immediate audience and time, not as a universal, permanent exclusion of other prophetic paths.

The Paraclete passages (14:16-17, 26) are central to the Islamic reading of John. Muslim scholars identify the Paraclete (*parakletos*) with Muhammad, arguing that the original word was *periklytos* ('praised one'), which corresponds exactly to the meaning of the names Ahmad and Muhammad. The Quran records Jesus saying: 'I bring good tidings of a messenger who will come after me, whose name is Ahmad' (61:6). The description of the Paraclete as one who will 'teach you all things' (14:26) and 'guide you into all the truth' (16:13) is read as describing Muhammad's comprehensive and final revelation.
