John 8 presents claims about Jesus that Islamic theology systematically rejects. The declaration 'I am the light of the world' (8:12) is reinterpreted: Jesus may be a light of guidance, as all prophets are, but God alone is the ultimate Light — 'Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth' (Quran 24:35). The teaching on truth and freedom (8:31-36) resonates with Islamic values, but the exclusivist framing — freedom through Jesus alone — is contested.

The climactic 'Before Abraham was, I AM' (8:58) is categorically rejected. This claim of pre-existence and identification with the divine name is incompatible with the Islamic understanding of Jesus as a created being, born miraculously of the Virgin Mary but having no pre-existence. Muslim scholars interpret this either as a later theological addition to the text or as a figurative statement about Jesus' spiritual significance — not a literal claim of divine identity. The hostile reaction (8:59) is noted as evidence that even Jesus' contemporaries recognized the claim as extraordinary and problematic.
