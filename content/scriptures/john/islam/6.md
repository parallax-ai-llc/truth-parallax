John 6 contains the feeding of five thousand, which Islam accepts as a prophetic miracle with Quranic parallels. Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:112-115) records the disciples asking Jesus for a table spread from heaven — a miracle God grants as a sign. The multiplied bread in John 6 resonates with this Quranic tradition. Jesus' walking on water (6:16-21) is another sign of prophetic authority, analogous to Moses parting the sea.

The Bread of Life discourse (6:22-71), however, is emphatically rejected. The statement 'unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood' (6:53) is understood by Muslim scholars as either a later interpolation or a grotesque misunderstanding of Jesus' original teaching. The consumption of blood is prohibited in Islam (Quran 2:173, 5:3), making this passage doubly problematic. Muslim interpreters who engage with the text charitably may read it as a metaphor for spiritual nourishment through following Jesus' teaching, stripped of any sacramental or incarnational theology.
