John 17's prayer is appreciated by Muslim scholars as an example of prophetic intercession (*shafa'ah*) — a concept central to Islamic theology. Muhammad is understood as the supreme intercessor on the Day of Judgment, and Jesus' prayer for his followers models this intercessory role. The prayer for unity (17:21) resonates with the Islamic emphasis on the unity of the *ummah* (the global Muslim community).

The prayer for sanctification 'in the truth' (17:17) and the mission to the world (17:18) describe the universal prophetic mandate that Islam sees as culminating in Muhammad's mission. The prayer that the disciples would be protected from 'the evil one' (17:15) aligns with the Islamic practice of seeking God's protection from Satan (*Isti'adhah*). The intimate relationship between the Father and Son described in this prayer (17:21-23) is, however, reinterpreted: the unity is one of purpose and mission, not of divine nature.
