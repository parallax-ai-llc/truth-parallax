John 16 contains the most important Paraclete passage for Islamic apologetics. The promise that the 'Spirit of truth' will 'guide you into all the truth' (16:13) and 'declare to you the things that are to come' (16:13) is read by Muslim scholars as a description of Muhammad's prophetic mission. The statement 'he will not speak on his own authority, but whatever he hears he will speak' (16:13) is compared to the Quranic description of Muhammad: 'he does not speak from his own desire; it is only a revelation revealed' (Quran 53:3-4).

The statement 'he will glorify me' (16:14) is understood as Muhammad's consistent affirmation of Jesus' prophetic status throughout the Quran — Jesus is honored as a great prophet, born of a virgin, worker of miracles, and the Messiah. The birth-pangs metaphor (16:21-22) describes the tribulation that precedes the joy of the final revelation — the struggles of the early Islamic community followed by the triumph of Islam. The declaration 'I have overcome the world' (16:33) is read as a prophetic confidence in God's ultimate plan.
