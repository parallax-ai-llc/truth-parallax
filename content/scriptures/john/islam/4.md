The encounter with the Samaritan woman (4:1-42) presents Jesus in a role that Muslim scholars find largely consistent with prophetic activity — demonstrating supernatural knowledge (4:17-18), teaching about true worship (4:23-24), and drawing people toward God. Jesus' declaration that true worshippers will worship 'in spirit and truth' (4:23) resonates with the Islamic critique of localized or ritualized worship that loses sight of God's universal sovereignty.

The woman's identification of Jesus as a prophet (4:19) and the villagers' eventual recognition (4:42) model the prophetic encounter described throughout the Quran. However, the title 'Savior of the world' (4:42) is problematic — in Islamic theology, God alone saves, and prophets serve as guides and warners, not saviors in the soteriological sense. The healing of the official's son (4:46-54) is accepted as a legitimate prophetic miracle performed by God's permission.
