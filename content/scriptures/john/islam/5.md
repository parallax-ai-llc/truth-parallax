John 5's Sabbath healing and discourse raise questions about Jesus' relationship to divine authority. Muslim scholars appreciate the healing at Bethesda (5:1-9) as a prophetic miracle, consistent with the Quran's affirmation that Jesus healed the sick by God's permission (3:49). The controversy over Sabbath observance reflects the Quranic critique that excessive legalism was imposed upon the Israelites (4:160).

The discourse on the Son's authority (5:19-30) is read critically by Muslim scholars. Statements like 'the Son can do nothing of his own accord' (5:19) and 'I can do nothing on my own' (5:30) are cited as evidence that Jesus acknowledged his dependence on God — consistent with the Islamic view of Jesus as a prophet, not a divine being. However, the claims of equality with the Father (5:18) and authority to raise the dead and judge (5:21-22) are attributed to later theological development by the Johannine community.
