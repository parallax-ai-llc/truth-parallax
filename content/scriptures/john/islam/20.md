John 20's resurrection narrative is reinterpreted through the lens of Quran 4:158: 'Rather, Allah raised him to Himself.' Islam affirms that Jesus is alive — not dead and risen, but taken up to heaven without dying. The empty tomb (20:1-10) is consistent with this view: the tomb is empty because God removed Jesus, not because he died and was resurrected.

The appearances to Mary Magdalene (20:11-18) and the disciples (20:19-23) are more challenging for the Islamic reading. If Jesus was taken up to heaven, the post-resurrection appearances must be explained differently — perhaps as brief returns from heaven before the final ascension, or as later fabrications by the Johannine community. Thomas' confession 'My Lord and my God!' (20:28) is rejected as attributing divinity to a human prophet. The purpose statement (20:30-31) — 'that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ' — is partially acceptable (Jesus is indeed the *Masih*), but the Christological content that John pours into this title exceeds what Islam can affirm.
