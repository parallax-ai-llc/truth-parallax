John 12's events preceding the passion are read by Muslim scholars with attention to prophetic themes. The anointing at Bethany (12:1-8) illustrates devotion to a prophet, and the triumphal entry (12:12-19) demonstrates popular recognition of Jesus' prophetic and messianic status. The arrival of Greeks seeking Jesus (12:20-22) signals the universality of his prophetic message.

The grain of wheat metaphor (12:24) — 'unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies' — is interpreted by Muslim scholars not as a theology of salvific death but as a general principle of sacrifice and dedication in God's service. The voice from heaven (12:28) confirms divine support for Jesus' mission, consistent with the Islamic understanding that God communicates with and supports his prophets. The judgment of 'the ruler of this world' (12:31) aligns with Islamic belief in Satan's (Iblis') opposition to God's prophets and the ultimate divine victory over evil.
