John 21's epilogue narrates a post-resurrection appearance that Islamic scholars interpret within their theological framework. The fishing miracle (21:1-14) is understood, if accepted, as a final prophetic sign demonstrating God's power through Jesus before his ascension to heaven. The shared meal of bread and fish (21:12-13) illustrates prophetic fellowship with disciples.

Peter's restoration and commission (21:15-17) is read by Muslim scholars as consistent with the prophetic pattern of entrusting the community to faithful leaders after the prophet's departure. The prediction of Peter's death (21:18-19) and the saying about the Beloved Disciple (21:20-23) are noted without extensive commentary. From the Islamic perspective, the most important point about John 21 is what it does not contain: any clear, unambiguous statement that this prophet was divine. The Gospel's efforts to establish Jesus' divinity are, in the Muslim reading, the work of a community that progressively elevated a human prophet to divine status — a process the Quran describes and corrects.
