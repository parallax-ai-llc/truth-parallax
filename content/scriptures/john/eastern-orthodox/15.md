John 15's vine imagery establishes the Orthodox understanding of the organic relationship between Christ and the Church. 'Abide in me, and I in you' (15:4) describes the mutual indwelling (*perichoresis*) that is the goal of theosis — the faithful participate in the divine life through the sacraments, prayer, and ascetical struggle. The pruning imagery (15:2) is connected to the spiritual disciplines of fasting, repentance, and confession that purify the faithful for greater fruitfulness.

The commandment to love (15:12-17) and the warning about the world's hatred (15:18-25) have been read throughout Orthodox history in the context of persecution — from the Roman Empire through the Ottoman period to the Soviet era. Martyrdom is understood as the supreme expression of abiding in Christ's love. The statement 'Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends' (15:13) is the theology of martyrdom in a single sentence.
