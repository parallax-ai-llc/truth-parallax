John 21 narrates the final appearance of the risen Christ and Peter's restoration. The miraculous catch of 153 fish (21:1-14) is read as a sign of the Church's universal mission. Various patristic interpretations assigned symbolic meaning to the number 153, but the essential point is the abundance of the catch and the unbroken net — the Church's capacity to embrace all nations without division.

Peter's threefold restoration — 'Feed my sheep' (21:15-17) — is understood in Orthodox tradition as a personal restoration after the threefold denial, not as a conferral of universal jurisdiction. Orthodox ecclesiology recognizes Peter's primacy of honor among the apostles but rejects the Roman claim of universal jurisdiction. Jesus' prediction of Peter's martyrdom (21:18-19) — 'by what death he was to glorify God' — connects pastoral care to sacrificial witness. The Beloved Disciple's witness (21:24) establishes the apostolic testimony that the Church receives and guards through the ages.
