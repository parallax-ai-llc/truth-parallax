John 5 presents the healing at Bethesda and the discourse on the Son's authority. The healed man is told to 'take up your bed and walk' (5:8) on the Sabbath, and Jesus' explanation — 'My Father is working until now, and I am working' (5:17) — reveals the continuous divine activity that sustains creation. Orthodox theology affirms that God's creative and sustaining energies are ever at work, not limited to a single act in the past.

The discourse on the Son's relationship to the Father (5:19-30) articulates the theological foundation of the Nicene dogma. The Son does whatever the Father does (5:19), shares the Father's life-giving power (5:21), and receives all judgment (5:22). Orthodox Christology holds these statements together without subordinating the Son: the distinction is one of hypostatic relation (the Son is begotten of the Father), not of nature or dignity. The witnesses to Jesus (5:33-47) establish the convergence of testimony that the Orthodox tradition sees continued in Scripture, Tradition, and the Fathers.
