John 8's declaration 'I am the light of the world' (8:12) is intimately connected to the Orthodox theology of uncreated light. St. Gregory Palamas taught that the light Christ reveals is not a created symbol but the uncreated divine energy — the same light that shone on Mount Tabor. The entire chapter moves between light and darkness, knowledge and ignorance, freedom and slavery, mapping the spiritual terrain that the Orthodox ascetical tradition navigates through prayer, fasting, and sacramental participation.

The climactic 'I AM' declaration — 'Before Abraham was, I AM' (8:58) — is the supreme self-revelation of Christ's divine identity. Orthodox theology reads this as Christ speaking the divine name revealed to Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3:14), claiming identity with the God of Israel. The hostile response confirms the magnitude of the claim. The icon of Christ Pantocrator, which dominates the dome of every Orthodox church, depicts this very reality: the pre-existent Logos who holds all creation in his hand.
