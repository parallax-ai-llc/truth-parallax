John 20 is the preeminent Paschal text. The Paschal Liturgy proclaims 'Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life.' Mary Magdalene's encounter with the risen Christ (20:11-18) is celebrated in Orthodox tradition: she is honored as 'equal to the apostles' for being the first witness of the resurrection. The bestowal of the Spirit (20:22) and the authority to forgive sins (20:23) ground the Orthodox sacrament of Confession.

Thomas' confession 'My Lord and my God!' (20:28) is celebrated on Thomas Sunday (the Sunday after Pascha), also known as Antipascha. Orthodox theology does not condemn Thomas for doubting but celebrates his eventual confession as the fullest articulation of faith in the Gospels. The purpose statement (20:30-31) — 'that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ' — encapsulates the evangelical purpose that the Orthodox Church continues to fulfill through its liturgical proclamation and sacramental life.
