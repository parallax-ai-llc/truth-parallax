John 18 narrates the arrest and trial with details that reveal Christ's sovereign dignity. The 'I AM' declaration (18:5-6) that causes the soldiers to fall back reveals the divine power concealed within the apparent weakness of the arrest. Orthodox theology reads the entire passion narrative through the lens of Christ's voluntary self-offering: 'No one takes my life from me; I lay it down of my own accord' (10:18).

The trial before Pilate engages questions of kingship and truth that are central to Orthodox political theology. 'My kingdom is not of this world' (18:36) has been interpreted not as a retreat from the public sphere but as a redefinition of power: Christ's kingdom operates through love, truth, and sacrifice, not through coercion. Pilate's question 'What is truth?' (18:38) stands answered by the presence of Christ himself — 'I am the truth' — though Pilate cannot perceive what stands before him.
