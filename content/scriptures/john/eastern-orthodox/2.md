The wedding at Cana (2:1-11) is read in Orthodox tradition as revealing the Theotokos' intercessory role and Christ's power to transform creation. Mary's request and Jesus' response — often translated 'What is that to me and to you?' — is interpreted by the Fathers not as a rebuke but as indicating that the hour of full revelation has not yet come. Nevertheless, Mary's intercession is effective, and her instruction 'Do whatever he tells you' (2:5) is understood as the Theotokos directing all creation toward obedience to Christ.

The transformation of water into wine manifests Christ's glory and prefigures the greater transformation wrought in the Eucharist. Orthodox hymnography for the Theophany feast connects the Cana miracle to the baptismal sanctification of water. The temple cleansing (2:13-22) reveals Christ's authority over the worship of Israel and anticipates the establishment of a new mode of worship centered on his risen Body — the Church and its Eucharistic liturgy.
