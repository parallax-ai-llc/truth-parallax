The encounter with the Samaritan woman is commemorated on the fifth Sunday after Pascha in the Orthodox liturgical calendar. Orthodox tradition identifies her by name — Photini ('the illumined one') — and venerates her as a saint and 'equal to the apostles.' Her journey from ignorance to faith to evangelism models the catechumenal process and the universal call to bear witness.

Jesus' teaching on worship 'in spirit and truth' (4:23-24) does not, in Orthodox understanding, abolish sacred space or liturgical form. Rather, it announces the new covenant worship centered on Christ, which the Divine Liturgy embodies. Spirit and truth are not opposed to ritual but are the inner reality that liturgy makes present. The healing of the official's son (4:46-54) demonstrates faith in Christ's word as the essential disposition for receiving divine grace — the same disposition required of those who approach the sacraments.
