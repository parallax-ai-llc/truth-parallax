John 10's Good Shepherd discourse is central to Orthodox pastoral theology and the theology of ordained ministry. The bishop as shepherd of the flock is understood as exercising Christ's own pastoral care. 'I know my own and my own know me' (10:14) describes the intimate relationship between Christ and the faithful that is mediated through the liturgical and sacramental life of the Church.

The statement 'I and the Father are one' (10:30) is the bedrock of Nicene Christology, affirmed at the First Ecumenical Council (325) against Arius. The unity is one of nature (*ousia*), not merely of will or purpose. The discourse at the Feast of Dedication (Hanukkah) connects Jesus' self-revelation to the rededication and illumination of the temple — in Orthodox theology, Christ himself is the true temple, and the faithful who are united to him through the sacraments share in his light and glory.
