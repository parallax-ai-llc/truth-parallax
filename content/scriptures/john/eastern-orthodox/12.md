John 12 marks the transition to the hour of glorification, beginning with Mary's anointing (12:1-8) and the triumphal entry (12:12-19), commemorated on Palm Sunday. The entry into Jerusalem is celebrated with great solemnity in Orthodox churches, with the faithful carrying branches and singing 'Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord.' The children's hymns connect to the praise offered in the Divine Liturgy.

The arrival of Greeks seeking Jesus (12:20-22) signals the universality of salvation, and Jesus' response — the grain of wheat must die to bear fruit (12:24) — establishes the paschal pattern of death-and-resurrection that defines Orthodox spiritual life. The voice from heaven (12:28) provides divine confirmation, and the judgment of 'the ruler of this world' (12:31) announces the cosmic victory of the cross. Orthodox theology understands the cross not primarily as punishment borne but as the defeat of death and the powers of evil.
