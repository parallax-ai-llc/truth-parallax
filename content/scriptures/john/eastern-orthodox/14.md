John 14 contains the Farewell Discourse, which Orthodox theology reads as Jesus' final teaching before the passion. The promise of 'many mansions' (*monai*) in the Father's house (14:2) is understood eschatologically — the diverse dwelling places represent the varied degrees of union with God that the saints enjoy. The declaration 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life' (14:6) is central to Orthodox soteriology: Christ is not one way among many but the unique path to the Father.

The Paraclete promise (14:16-17, 26) is crucial for Orthodox pneumatology, particularly regarding the *Filioque* controversy. Orthodox theology insists that the Spirit proceeds from the Father alone (John 15:26 — 'who proceeds from the Father'), not 'from the Father and the Son' as the Western *Filioque* addition states. This distinction, which contributed to the Great Schism of 1054, reflects a deep theological commitment to preserving the Father's unique role as the source (*arche*) of the Trinity.
