The High Priestly Prayer (17:1-26) is the supreme biblical text for the Orthodox theology of divine unity and sanctification. Jesus prays that the Father would glorify the Son — and glory in Orthodox theology is the uncreated divine energy that the faithful are called to share through theosis. The prayer that the disciples may be 'one, even as we are one' (17:22) describes the goal of the spiritual life: participation in the inner life of the Trinity.

The prayer for sanctification 'in the truth' (17:17) and the identification of the Father's word as truth ground the Orthodox understanding of truth as a Person — Christ himself — not merely a set of propositions. The prayer for all future believers (17:20-26) establishes the universal scope of Christ's intercession and the ecclesial dimension of sanctification: holiness is not achieved in isolation but within the Body of Christ, the Church.
