The raising of Lazarus is commemorated on Lazarus Saturday, the day before Palm Sunday, in the Orthodox liturgical calendar. This unique liturgical placement connects the miracle directly to the Paschal events: the raising of Lazarus is the prelude to Christ's own death and resurrection. The troparion of the feast proclaims: 'By raising Lazarus from the dead before your passion, you confirmed the universal resurrection, O Christ God.'

Jesus' declaration 'I am the resurrection and the life' (11:25) is not a promise about the distant future but a statement about the present reality of divine life communicated through Christ. Orthodox theology holds that Christ's life-giving power is operative now through the sacraments, particularly the Eucharist, which is 'the medicine of immortality' (Ignatius of Antioch). Jesus' weeping (11:35) reveals his full humanity and the divine compassion that embraces human suffering without being diminished by it.
