John 17 — the High Priestly Prayer — is one of the most theologically rich chapters in the New Testament. Jesus prays for his own glorification (17:1-5), for his disciples (17:6-19), and for all future believers (17:20-26). Protestant interpreters find in this prayer the definitive model for intercessory prayer and the assurance that Christ continues to intercede for his people (cf. Romans 8:34, Hebrews 7:25).

The prayer for unity — 'that they may all be one, even as you, Father, are in me, and I in you' (17:21) — has been invoked in both ecumenical and anti-ecumenical Protestant arguments. Some read it as a call for institutional church unity; others insist the unity is spiritual, already realized among all true believers regardless of denomination. The prayer for sanctification 'in the truth' (17:17) and the identification of God's word as truth ground the Protestant emphasis on Scripture as the instrument of holiness.
