John 20 presents the resurrection appearances that ground Protestant faith. Mary Magdalene's encounter with the risen Jesus (20:11-18) — 'Do not cling to me, for I have not yet ascended' (20:17) — reveals a new mode of relationship with the risen Christ. The bestowal of the Spirit on the disciples (20:22) and the commission 'As the Father has sent me, even so I am sending you' (20:21) establish the church's mission.

Thomas' doubt and confession (20:24-29) is central to Protestant faith. His declaration 'My Lord and my God!' (20:28) is the highest Christological confession in the Gospels. Jesus' response — 'Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed' (20:29) — speaks directly to every subsequent generation of believers. The purpose statement (20:30-31) — 'these are written so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in his name' — encapsulates the Protestant conviction that the Gospel's purpose is to generate saving faith.
