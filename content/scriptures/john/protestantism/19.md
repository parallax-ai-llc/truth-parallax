John 19 narrates the crucifixion with distinctive Johannine details that have shaped Protestant theology. Jesus carries his own cross (no Simon of Cyrene), emphasizing his sovereign agency. The inscription 'Jesus of Nazareth, the King of the Jews' (19:19) in three languages declares his universal kingship. The seamless garment (19:23-24) fulfills Psalm 22:18 and has been read allegorically as representing the unity of the Church.

The cry 'It is finished' (*tetelestai*, 19:30) is one of the most theologically significant words in Protestant soteriology. It declares the work of atonement complete — nothing remains to be added by human effort. The piercing of Jesus' side, producing blood and water (19:34), is read sacramentally by some Protestant interpreters (baptism and Eucharist) and literally by others (confirming the reality of death). The burial by Joseph and Nicodemus (19:38-42) — both secret disciples who now act publicly — demonstrates that the cross creates courage.
