John 10 presents the Good Shepherd discourse, containing two 'I AM' sayings: 'I am the gate for the sheep' (10:7) and 'I am the good shepherd' (10:11). Protestant interpreters emphasize the exclusivity of the gate — 'whoever enters by me will be saved' (10:9) — as supporting the *solus Christus* principle. The good shepherd who 'lays down his life for the sheep' (10:11) is the definitive image of substitutionary atonement in Johannine theology.

The statement 'I and the Father are one' (10:30) is, alongside 8:58 and 1:1, a cornerstone text for the doctrine of Christ's deity. The hostile response (10:31-33) confirms that the claim was understood as divine self-identification. The Dedication (Hanukkah) setting (10:22) provides a festival of light and rededication as the backdrop for Jesus' self-revelation. The sheep imagery — 'My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me' (10:27) — has been central to Reformed discussions of election, perseverance, and the security of the believer.
