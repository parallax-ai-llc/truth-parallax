John 16 continues the Farewell Discourse with teaching on the Spirit's work and the disciples' future. The Paraclete will 'convict the world concerning sin and righteousness and judgment' (16:8-11) — Protestant preaching theology draws on this passage to understand the Spirit's role in evangelism and conversion. The promise that the Spirit will 'guide you into all the truth' (16:13) has been invoked in Protestant debates about continuing revelation versus the sufficiency of Scripture.

The metaphor of birth pangs (16:21-22) transforms present suffering into the anticipation of joy, and the promise 'your sorrow will turn into joy' (16:20) has sustained Protestant communities through persecution. The climactic declaration 'I have overcome the world' (16:33) grounds the Protestant confidence that the victory has already been won, even as believers continue to experience tribulation in the present age.
