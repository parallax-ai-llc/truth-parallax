John 12 marks the transition from the Book of Signs to the Book of Glory. Mary's anointing of Jesus' feet (12:1-8) is contrasted with Judas' objection, establishing the pattern of extravagant devotion versus calculating self-interest. The triumphal entry (12:12-19) fulfills Zechariah 9:9 and generates the opposition that will culminate in the cross.

The arrival of Greeks seeking Jesus (12:20-22) triggers the declaration that 'the hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified' (12:23). Protestant interpreters note the paradox: glorification comes through death, like a grain of wheat falling into the ground (12:24). The voice from heaven (12:28-30) provides divine confirmation, and Jesus' statement 'Now is the judgment of this world; now will the ruler of this world be cast out' (12:31) defines the cross as the decisive cosmic victory. The chapter closes with a summary of the theme of belief and unbelief (12:37-50), citing Isaiah 6:10 to explain the mystery of rejection.
