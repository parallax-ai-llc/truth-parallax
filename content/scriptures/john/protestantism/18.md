John 18 narrates the arrest in the garden (18:1-11), the interrogation before Annas and Caiaphas (18:12-27), and the trial before Pilate (18:28-40). Protestant interpreters note John's distinctive details: Jesus' majestic self-identification 'I AM' causes the soldiers to fall back (18:6), revealing that the arrest occurs only because Jesus permits it. Peter's use of the sword (18:10-11) is firmly rejected — 'Put your sword into its sheath' — a passage invoked in Protestant debates about pacifism and just war.

The interrogation before Pilate generates the exchange about truth — 'What is truth?' (18:38) — that has haunted Western philosophy and theology. Protestant interpreters read Pilate's question as the epitome of worldly cynicism confronted by the incarnate Truth. Jesus' statement 'My kingdom is not of this world' (18:36) has shaped Protestant political theology, particularly the distinction between the spiritual and temporal realms.
