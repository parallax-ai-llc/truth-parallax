John 14 contains some of the most comforting words in Protestant devotional life. 'In my Father's house are many rooms... I go to prepare a place for you' (14:2) shapes Protestant eschatological hope. 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me' (14:6) — the sixth 'I AM' saying — is the definitive Protestant text for the exclusivity of salvation through Christ.

The promise of the Paraclete — 'another Helper, to be with you forever, even the Spirit of truth' (14:16-17) — is foundational for Protestant pneumatology. The Spirit's indwelling of every believer, not just clergy or saints, grounds the Protestant doctrine of the priesthood of all believers. The promise of peace — 'not as the world gives' (14:27) — and the exhortation 'let not your hearts be troubled' (14:1, 27) make this chapter central to Protestant pastoral care, preaching at funerals, and seasons of trial.
