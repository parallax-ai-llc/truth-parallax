John 21, the Epilogue, narrates the appearance at the Sea of Tiberias and the restoration of Peter. The miraculous catch of 153 fish (21:1-14) has generated extensive symbolic speculation, but Protestant interpreters typically emphasize its function as a resurrection sign confirming Jesus' identity: 'It is the Lord!' (21:7). The shared meal of bread and fish echoes both the feeding miracles and the Emmaus road recognition scene (Luke 24).

Peter's threefold restoration (21:15-17) — matching his threefold denial — is read as a paradigm of grace and recommissioning after failure. The distinction between *agapao* and *phileo* in Jesus' questions has been debated, but most modern Protestant scholars doubt a significant semantic difference. Jesus' prediction of Peter's death (21:18-19) and the enigmatic saying about the Beloved Disciple (21:20-23) round out the Gospel. The final verse — 'there are also many other things that Jesus did' (21:25) — reminds readers that the written Gospel is a selection, sufficient for faith but not exhaustive.
