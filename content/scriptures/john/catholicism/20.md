John 20 narrates the resurrection events that are central to Catholic faith. Mary Magdalene's encounter with the risen Jesus (20:11-18) is celebrated in Catholic tradition — she is honored as 'apostle to the apostles.' Jesus' bestowal of the Spirit (20:22) and the commission 'If you forgive the sins of any, they are forgiven them' (20:23) is the primary scriptural basis for the Catholic sacrament of Reconciliation (Confession). The power to forgive sins is transmitted from Christ to the apostles and through them to priests in apostolic succession.

Thomas' confession 'My Lord and my God!' (20:28) is the climactic Christological statement of the Gospel. Catholic theology sees in Thomas' journey from doubt to faith a model of honest questioning that leads to deeper belief. The purpose statement (20:30-31) establishes that the Gospel is written to generate and sustain faith — the same purpose served by the Church's proclamation and sacramental life.
