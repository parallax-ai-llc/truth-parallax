The wedding at Cana (2:1-11) holds immense significance in Catholic theology. Mary's intercession — 'They have no wine' (2:3) — and Jesus' response illustrate the Catholic doctrine of Mary as mediatrix and intercessor. Her instruction to the servants, 'Do whatever he tells you' (2:5), is understood as Mary's perpetual counsel to the Church. The transformation of water into wine is read as the first sacramental sign, prefiguring the Eucharistic transformation of wine into the Blood of Christ.

The temple cleansing (2:13-22) reveals Jesus' zeal for his Father's house. The statement about destroying and raising the temple in three days (2:19) is understood christologically: Jesus' body is the true temple, destroyed on the cross and raised on the third day. Catholic ecclesiology extends this — the Church is the Body of Christ, the temple of the Holy Spirit in the world.
