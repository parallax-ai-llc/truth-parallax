John 21 narrates the appearance at the Sea of Tiberias and Peter's restoration. The miraculous catch of fish (21:1-14) is read by Catholic interpreters as a symbol of the Church's universal mission — the net holds 153 fish 'and although there were so many, the net was not torn' (21:11), representing the Church's capacity to embrace all peoples without division.

Peter's threefold commission — 'Feed my lambs... Tend my sheep... Feed my sheep' (21:15-17) — is the Johannine parallel to the Matthean Petrine commission and the scriptural basis for papal primacy. The risen Christ entrusts the entire flock to Peter's pastoral care, and Catholic theology understands this commission as continuing in Peter's successors, the Bishops of Rome. The enigmatic saying about the Beloved Disciple (21:20-23) and his role as witness (21:24) establish the apostolic testimony that the Church guards and transmits.
