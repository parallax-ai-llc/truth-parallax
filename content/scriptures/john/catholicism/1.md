Catholic interpretation of the Johannine Prologue (1:1-18) is foundational for Trinitarian dogma. The identification of the Logos with God ('the Word was God') and yet as distinct from the Father ('the Word was with God') provides the scriptural basis for the Nicene-Constantinopolitan Creed's affirmation that the Son is 'God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God.' The incarnation ('the Word became flesh') is the central mystery of Catholic faith, celebrated at Christmas and professed at every recitation of the Creed.

John the Baptist's witness to Jesus as 'the Lamb of God' (1:29) connects to the Catholic understanding of the Eucharist, where the priest proclaims 'Behold the Lamb of God' before communion. The calling of the first disciples (1:35-51) establishes the apostolic foundation of the Church. Andrew's bringing of Peter to Jesus (1:41-42) and Jesus' renaming of Simon as Cephas (Peter/Rock) anticipates the Petrine commission of Matthew 16.
