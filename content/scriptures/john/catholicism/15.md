John 15's vine imagery — 'I am the true vine' (15:1) — establishes the organic ecclesiology central to Catholic self-understanding. The Church is the branches united to Christ the vine, and the sacraments are the means through which the life-giving sap flows. 'Apart from me you can do nothing' (15:5) grounds the Catholic insistence on the necessity of grace. The pruning imagery (15:2) is connected to the purifying work of suffering and the sacrament of Reconciliation.

The commandment to love (15:12-17) — 'Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends' (15:13) — inspires the Catholic theology of martyrdom and sacrificial service. The transition from servants to friends (15:15) describes the intimacy with Christ that the sacramental life makes possible. The world's hatred (15:18-25) has been read throughout Catholic history as explaining persecution and martyrdom.
