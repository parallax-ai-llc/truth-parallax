John 5's healing at Bethesda (5:1-18) and the subsequent discourse illuminate the Catholic understanding of Christ's divine authority transmitted to the Church. The healing on the Sabbath and Jesus' claim that 'my Father is working until now, and I am working' (5:17) assert a divine prerogative that the Catholic Church sees as exercised through the sacramental ministry — particularly the anointing of the sick and the power to forgive sins.

The discourse on the Son's relationship to the Father (5:19-30) provides scriptural foundation for Nicene Christology. The four witnesses (5:33-47) — John the Baptist, the works, the Father, and the Scriptures — establish the principle that faith rests on multiple convergent testimonies, a principle the Catholic Church applies to its understanding of Scripture, Tradition, and the Magisterium as complementary sources of authority.
