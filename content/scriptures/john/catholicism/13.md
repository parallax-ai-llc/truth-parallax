John 13 opens the Book of Glory on Holy Thursday, the day the Catholic Church commemorates the institution of the Eucharist and the priesthood. The foot washing (13:1-20) is reenacted in the Holy Thursday liturgy, when the priest washes the feet of twelve members of the congregation. Catholic interpretation sees this act as both a model of humble service and a symbol of the purifying grace of the sacraments.

The departure of Judas into the night (13:30) and the new commandment of love (13:34-35) establish the contrast between betrayal and fidelity that structures the passion narrative. Catholic moral theology identifies the 'new commandment' as the summary of Christian ethics — love modeled on Christ's self-sacrificial love. The prediction of Peter's denial (13:36-38) introduces the theme of apostolic weakness and divine mercy that runs through the passion.
