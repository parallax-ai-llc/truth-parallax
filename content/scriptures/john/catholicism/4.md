The encounter with the Samaritan woman (4:1-42) is read in Catholic tradition as a model of evangelization and catechesis. The woman's progressive discovery of Jesus' identity mirrors the catechumenal process: from ignorance to curiosity to confession of faith. Her return to the village to share her encounter (4:28-30, 39) represents the apostolate of every baptized person.

Jesus' teaching on worship 'in spirit and truth' (4:23-24) does not, in Catholic interpretation, negate the importance of sacred places, liturgical forms, or sacraments. Rather, it teaches that authentic worship requires interior disposition — the spirit — together with objective truth as mediated through the Church's liturgy. The healing of the official's son (4:46-54) demonstrates that faith in Christ's word is the foundation of sacramental efficacy — the sacraments work *ex opere operato* (by the work performed) when received with faith.
