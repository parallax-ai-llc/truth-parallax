John 12 marks the transition to the hour of glorification. Mary's anointing of Jesus' feet (12:1-8) is a model of devotional love that inspires Catholic worship — the use of precious incense, beautiful vessels, and costly adornments in the liturgy reflects the conviction that nothing is too extravagant for the worship of Christ. The triumphal entry (12:12-19) is commemorated on Palm Sunday, opening Holy Week.

The arrival of the Greeks (12:20-22) signals the universal scope of Jesus' mission, and his response — 'unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone' (12:24) — provides the definitive Catholic theology of redemptive suffering and the fruitfulness of sacrifice. The voice from heaven (12:28) constitutes a divine confirmation that Catholic theology reads as Trinitarian. The judgment of 'the ruler of this world' (12:31) announces the cosmic dimension of the cross.
