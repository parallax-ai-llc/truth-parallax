John 8's declaration 'I am the light of the world' (8:12) resonates throughout Catholic liturgy, from the Easter Vigil's Lucernarium (light service) to the baptismal candle given to the newly christened. The teaching on freedom through truth (8:31-36) shapes Catholic moral theology: true freedom is not license but liberation from sin through Christ and his Church.

The dialogue about Abraham (8:33-58) culminates in the supreme 'I AM' declaration (8:58), which Catholic Christology identifies as a claim to share in the divine name and nature. The Catechism of the Catholic Church (n. 590) cites this passage as evidence that Jesus claimed divine identity. The hostility this provoked — the attempt to stone him — confirms that his contemporaries understood the claim. Catholic apologetics draws on this passage to argue that Jesus did not present himself merely as a moral teacher but as God incarnate.
