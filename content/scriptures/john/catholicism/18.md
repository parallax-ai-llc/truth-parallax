John 18 narrates the arrest and trial with distinctively Johannine details. Jesus' sovereign 'I AM' (18:5-6), causing the soldiers to fall back, reveals his divine authority even in the moment of apparent defeat. The transfer to Annas and Caiaphas (18:12-27) is interwoven with Peter's denial, creating a dramatic contrast between Jesus' faithful testimony and Peter's failure.

The trial before Pilate (18:28-40) is the most developed in any Gospel. Jesus' declaration 'My kingdom is not of this world' (18:36) has shaped Catholic political theology from Augustine's *City of God* through the modern social encyclicals. Pilate's question 'What is truth?' (18:38) stands as the perennial question that Catholic philosophy and theology seek to answer. The Catholic intellectual tradition affirms that truth is accessible through both faith and reason, and that Christ himself is the Truth incarnate.
