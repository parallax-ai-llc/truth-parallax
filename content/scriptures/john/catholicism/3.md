John 3 presents the dialogue with Nicodemus that is foundational for Catholic sacramental theology. The statement 'unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God' (3:5) is the primary biblical basis for the Catholic doctrine of baptismal regeneration. The Council of Trent cited this verse in defining that baptism is necessary for salvation. The 'water and Spirit' are understood as referring to the sacrament of baptism, not merely a metaphor for spiritual renewal.

John 3:16-17 expresses God's salvific will for the whole world — a universal offer of grace that Catholic theology affirms while maintaining that the ordinary means of receiving this grace are the sacraments of the Church. The discussion of light and darkness (3:19-21) shapes Catholic moral theology: sin is a turning away from the light, and the sacrament of Reconciliation restores the penitent to light. John the Baptist's final testimony (3:27-36) models the disposition of humility before Christ.
