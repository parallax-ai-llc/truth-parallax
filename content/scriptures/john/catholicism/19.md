John 19's crucifixion narrative contains details of supreme importance for Catholic theology. The seamless garment (19:23-24) is read as a symbol of the Church's unity, which should not be torn. Mary standing at the foot of the cross (19:25-27) and Jesus' entrustment of her to the Beloved Disciple — 'Woman, behold, your son... Behold, your mother' (19:26-27) — is the scriptural foundation for the Catholic doctrine of Mary as Mother of the Church. This passage is invoked to explain Mary's spiritual motherhood of all believers.

The cry 'It is finished' (19:30) declares the completion of the work of redemption. The blood and water from Jesus' pierced side (19:34) have been interpreted since the Fathers as symbols of the Eucharist (blood) and baptism (water) — the sacraments flowing from Christ's sacrifice. The careful burial by Joseph and Nicodemus (19:38-42) establishes the reality of Jesus' death and the dignity of the human body, even in death.
