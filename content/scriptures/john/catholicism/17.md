John 17 — the High Priestly Prayer — is the supreme biblical text for Catholic ecclesiology and ecumenism. Jesus prays as the true high priest, interceding for his disciples and for all who will believe through their word. The prayer for unity — 'that they may all be one' (17:21) — is the leitmotif of the Catholic ecumenical movement, cited extensively in Vatican II's *Unitatis Redintegratio* and in subsequent papal documents on Christian unity.

The prayer for sanctification 'in the truth' (17:17) and Jesus' self-consecration 'that they also may be sanctified in truth' (17:19) ground the Catholic theology of holiness and the universal call to sanctity articulated at Vatican II. The mutual indwelling of Father, Son, and believers (17:21-23) describes the goal of the spiritual life — union with God through Christ in the Spirit, realized most fully through the Eucharist.
