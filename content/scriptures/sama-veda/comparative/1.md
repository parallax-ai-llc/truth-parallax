The Agneya fire hymns of the Sama Veda invite comparison with sacred fire traditions across world religions. The Zoroastrian fire temple (*atash behram*), the Eternal Flame in Judaism (the *ner tamid*), the Easter fire in Christianity, and the sacred hearth of Vesta in Roman religion all reflect the cross-cultural association of fire with divine presence, purification, and the maintenance of cosmic order.

The musical setting of fire hymns in the Sama Veda compares with the role of music in fire rituals across traditions. The Zoroastrian *yasna* ceremony includes chanted liturgy alongside fire tending, while Christian hymnody accompanies the lighting of candles and the Easter Vigil fire. The universal pairing of fire and song in worship suggests a deep connection between these two sensory experiences of the sacred.
