The Pavamana Soma purification chants compare with sacred beverage traditions across cultures: the Christian Eucharistic wine, the Jewish Kiddush, the Zoroastrian haoma ritual, and the Amazonian ayahuasca ceremony all involve the ritual transformation of a substance into a vehicle of divine communion. The Sama Veda's musical accompaniment to this process highlights the multisensory nature of transformative ritual.

The concept of purification through ritual process — central to the Pavamana chants — resonates with purification rites in many traditions: the Christian baptism, the Muslim wudu, the Jewish mikveh, and the Shinto misogi all involve ritual transformation from impure to pure state. The Sama Veda uniquely adds the dimension of musical accompaniment, suggesting that sound itself participates in the purification.
