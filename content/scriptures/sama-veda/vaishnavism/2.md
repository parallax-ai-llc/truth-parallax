Vaishnavas read the Aindra hymns of the Sama Veda through the theological principle that Indra's power derives from Vishnu. The heroic samans celebrating Indra's victories are ultimately celebrations of Vishnu's cosmic sovereignty, exercised through Indra as an empowered agent. The musical grandeur of these chants reflects the majesty of the Lord whose power enables all divine activity.

The Aindra section's martial vigor connects to the Vaishnava understanding of the Lord as the protector of the righteous and the destroyer of evil. Just as Vishnu descends as avatara to restore dharma, Indra — empowered by Vishnu — battles the forces of cosmic disorder. The samans' powerful melodies embody this divine protective energy.
