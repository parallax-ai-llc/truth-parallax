Vaishnava interpretation of the Agneya fire hymns emphasizes that all sacrificial fire ultimately carries oblations to Vishnu-Narayana, the supreme recipient of all worship. The samans to Agni are understood as praise directed through the fire to the Lord who dwells in the heart of all beings. Krishna's self-identification with the Sama Veda elevates these fire chants to expressions of direct divine connection.

The melodic quality of the fire hymns — their beauty, order, and harmony — reflects the aesthetic perfection of the Lord's divine nature. Vaishnavas understand the Sama Veda's transformation of text into music as revealing a dimension of worship that goes beyond intellectual understanding, engaging the devotee's heart in the same way that Krishna's flute enchants all beings through the power of divine sound.
