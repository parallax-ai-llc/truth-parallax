The Pavamana Soma hymns are interpreted by Vaishnavas as celebrating the purifying power of divine grace (*prasada*) that flows from the Lord into the devotee's heart. The imagery of Soma being filtered and purified parallels the process of bhakti, through which the devotee's consciousness is cleansed of material attachments and made fit for divine communion.

Krishna's declaration that he is the Sama Veda gives these Soma purification chants a special devotional significance: the very process of purification is an act of the Lord working within the devotee. The Soma's transformation from raw plant to divine offering mirrors the soul's journey from material bondage to spiritual liberation through the Lord's transforming grace.
