The Aindra section provides scholars with data on how hymns to a specific deity were selected and arranged for musical performance. The choice of which Rig Vedic Indra hymns to include in the Sama Veda appears to have been guided by both liturgical function (their place in the Soma sacrifice sequence) and musical suitability (the phonological properties of the text for melodic adaptation).

Ethnomusicological fieldwork with living Samavedic chanters has revealed that the Aindra samans employ distinct musical characteristics — stronger rhythmic patterns, higher registers, and more forceful vocal production — compared with the more meditative fire and Soma hymns. These performance differences reflect the content of the texts and provide evidence for an ancient system of word-painting in Vedic chanting.
