Scholars study the Agneya section of the Sama Veda for its transformation of Rig Vedic fire hymns into musical compositions, providing evidence for the development of Indian musical theory and liturgical practice. The musical notation systems preserved in Samavedic manuscripts — using numerical symbols for tonal values — represent one of the earliest attempts to encode musical performance in written form.

Analysis of the Agneya chants reveals the process by which the Udgatr priest adapted Rig Vedic verses: words were extended through vowel prolongation, stobha syllables were inserted, and the text was restructured to fit musical patterns. This process of musical elaboration has been compared by scholars with similar practices in Gregorian chant, where biblical texts were set to increasingly complex melodies over centuries.
