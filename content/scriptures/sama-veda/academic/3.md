The Pavamana section is of central scholarly interest because it accompanies the most important ritual of the Vedic sacrificial system: the Soma pressing. Scholars analyze these chants for evidence of the actual Soma ritual's performance sequence, as each saman corresponds to a specific stage of the pressing and filtering process. The chants thus serve as a kind of liturgical commentary on the ritual action.

The Pavamana hymns have also been examined for their linguistic features, which include archaic grammatical forms and specialized ritual vocabulary that may preserve very early strata of Indo-Aryan religious language. Comparative study with the Avestan yasna (Soma/Haoma ritual) reveals shared terminology and ritual structures that point to a common Indo-Iranian sacrificial tradition.
