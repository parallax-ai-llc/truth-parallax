Shaiva reading of the Aindra hymns recognizes in Indra's cosmic battles a reflection of Shiva's role as the destroyer of the three cities (*Tripurantaka*) and the conqueror of death. The samans' dynamic melodies embody the *tandava* energy of Shiva's cosmic dance, which simultaneously creates and destroys, binding and liberating the soul through divine activity.

The Aindra section's celebration of heroic power is interpreted through the Shaiva understanding that true strength lies not in physical force but in the power of consciousness to overcome limitation. Indra's victories symbolize the practitioner's triumph over the *malas* (impurities) that veil consciousness, achieved through the grace (*anugraha*) of Shiva.
