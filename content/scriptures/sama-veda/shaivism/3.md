Shaiva tradition reads the Pavamana Soma hymns as describing the purification of consciousness that occurs through Shiva's grace. The filtering of Soma parallels the removal of the three *malas* — anava (ego), mayiya (illusion), and karma (accumulated action) — that bind the soul in the cycle of rebirth. The saman's melody carries the practitioner through this purification process.

The ecstatic quality of the Soma hymns resonates with the Shaiva experience of *samavesha* — the blissful absorption into Shiva-consciousness that occurs when the impurities are removed. The 'intoxication' of Soma is read metaphorically as the overwhelming bliss (*ananda*) of recognizing one's identity with Shiva, the supreme consciousness from which all experience arises.
