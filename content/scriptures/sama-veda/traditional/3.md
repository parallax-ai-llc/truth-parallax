The Pavamana section contains the Soma purification hymns — chants that accompany the pressing, filtering, and clarifying of the sacred Soma juice. These are among the most liturgically important samans, as they are performed during the climactic moments of the Soma sacrifice when the divine beverage is being prepared for offering to the gods and consumption by the priests.

Traditional Samavedic training places particular emphasis on the Pavamana chants, as their correct performance is believed to directly influence the efficacy of the Soma ritual. The melodies are designed to mirror the flowing, filtering process of the Soma preparation, with liquid, cascading musical patterns that traditional practitioners describe as themselves being a form of purification.
