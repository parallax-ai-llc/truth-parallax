The Agneya section of the Sama Veda contains the fire hymns set to melody for the Soma sacrifice. These samans transform the Rig Vedic hymns to Agni into elaborate musical compositions chanted by the Udgatr priest. Traditional understanding holds that the melodic rendering amplifies the mantras' spiritual power, as the saman adds the dimension of sacred music to the spoken word's inherent efficacy.

The fire hymns of the Sama Veda accompany the kindling and maintenance of the sacrificial fires, creating a sonic environment that sustains the ritual space. The traditional system prescribes specific melodies for each stage of the fire ceremony, and the precision of the Udgatr's chanting is considered as essential to the sacrifice's success as the Adhvaryu's physical actions or the Hotr's recitations.
