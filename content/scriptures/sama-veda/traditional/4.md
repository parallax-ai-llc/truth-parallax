The Aranyaka section of the Sama Veda contains the 'forest chants' — samans of such esoteric power that they were traditionally taught and performed only in the wilderness, away from human habitation. These chants represent the most secret and sacred portion of the Samavedic tradition, reserved for advanced practitioners who had completed their study of the other sections.

The Aranyaka chants mark the transition from external ritual to internal meditation. Traditional understanding holds that these melodies are too powerful for the village setting and must be practiced in the solitude of the forest, where their transformative energy can unfold without danger. This section bridges the gap between the Samhita's ritual content and the Upanishadic wisdom that culminates the Vedic tradition.
