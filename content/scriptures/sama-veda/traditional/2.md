The Aindra section gathers the Sama Veda's hymns to Indra, the king of the gods, set to some of the most vigorous and dynamic melodies in the collection. These samans celebrate Indra's heroic deeds — his slaying of the cosmic serpent Vritra, his release of the imprisoned waters, and his sovereignty over the three worlds — through musical patterns that embody the energy and power of the deity.

Traditional performance of the Aindra hymns requires the Udgatr to convey Indra's martial vigor through vocal technique, employing powerful crescendos and rhythmic patterns that mirror the deity's thunderbolt strikes. The coordination between the Udgatr, Prastotr, Pratiharttr, and Subrahmanya in these chants represents one of the most demanding forms of Vedic liturgical performance.
