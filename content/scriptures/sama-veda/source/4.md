---
title: "Sama Veda - Chapter 4"
scripture: "sama-veda"
chapter: 4
---

**1** That whence the sun rises, and that where it sets — that the gods established as the foundation of Law. That is the truth.

**2** Brihaspati, when first he had his being from mighty splendour in the highest heaven, born in many forms, seven-mouthed, the holy, dispelled the darkness with his radiant brightness.

**3** With hymns and songs of praise we magnify thee, O Brihaspati, the strong. We offer our oblations unto thee, the lord of prayer, the lord of sacred speech.

**4** Brihaspati, the finder of the way, has shown the path of truth. He is the guardian of the sacred word, and through his might the gods have conquered the demons.

**5** The singer approaches thee, O lord of sacred chant, with songs. May the divine Brihaspati hear our prayer and grant us his protection.

**6** May Sarasvati, the divine, the flowing, the rich in streams, the goddess of sacred speech, accept our sacrifice.

**7** Sarasvati, who perfects our devotion, who inspires the singer, who gives wisdom to the sage — may she be gracious unto us.

**8** On Sarasvati's banks the sages sing. Through her the sacred chants are born. She is the mother of the Vedas, the inspirer of all knowledge.

**9** These chants are dedicated to the gods. Through sacred song the worshipper attains the highest. The Sama is the nectar of the Vedas.

**10** The singer who knows the Sama in its truth ascends beyond the worlds. He reaches the realm of light, the abode of the immortals.

**11** Through chanting, through the power of sacred sound, the wise cross over the darkness. The Sama Veda is the bridge to immortality.

**12** Thus the sacred chants have been revealed for the benefit of gods and men. He who sings them with devotion and understanding attains the supreme goal. Om, peace, peace, peace.
