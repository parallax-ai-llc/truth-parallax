---
title: "Sama Veda - Chapter 1"
scripture: "sama-veda"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Agni I first invoke for our prosperity; I call on Mitra, Varuna, to aid us here. I call on Night who gives rest to all moving life; I call on God Savitar to lend us his aid.

**2** Throughout the dusky firmament advancing, laying to rest the immortal and the mortal, borne on his golden chariot, he comes, Savitar, god who looks on every creature.

**3** The god moves by the upward path, the downward; with two bright bays, adorable, he journeys. Savitar comes, the god from the far distance, and chases from us all distress and sorrow.

**4** Come, Agni, praised with song to feast and sacrificial offering. Sit as hotar on the sacred grass.

**5** O thou to whom they sing the Sama chants, let thy resplendent flames shine high. Thou art the giver of horses, giver of cattle, Agni, the giver of all.

**6** The juice that is pressed, the Soma that is filtered, flows for thee, O Indra. Come, drink of the sweet juice.

**7** These songs of praise have been sung for thee, O Indra. These Soma juices have been pressed. Come to us, come quickly.

**8** Indra, Lord of the Soma feast, accept this offering. Thou art the conqueror, the mighty one. Come to our sacrifice.

**9** We sing to thee, O Soma, pressed for the worship of the gods. Flow forth in thy most sweet and exhilarating stream.

**10** Flow, Soma, in thy sweetest and most gladdening stream, pressed for Indra, for his drink. Settle in the vessels, O purified one.

**11** For thee, O Soma, for thy juice, the pious ones have raised their voice in song. Flow onward to the filter, to the sieve.

**12** The chants arise for thee, O Indra, the hymns are sung, the prayers are said. Come to our sacrifice and drink of the pressed Soma.
