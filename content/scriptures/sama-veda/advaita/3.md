The Pavamana Soma hymns hold deep significance for Advaita Vedanta as metaphors for the purification of the mind (*chitta-shuddhi*) that prepares the aspirant for self-knowledge. The process of pressing, filtering, and clarifying the Soma juice precisely parallels the Advaitic discipline of removing the impurities of attachment, aversion, and delusion that obscure the ever-present Brahman.

The Chandogya Upanishad's meditations on the Udgitha emerge from this Pavamana context, and Advaitins trace a direct line from the ritual purification of Soma to the philosophical purification of consciousness. The saman's melody serves as a vehicle for this inner purification, its beauty and precision gradually dissolving the mental agitation that prevents the recognition of non-dual awareness.
