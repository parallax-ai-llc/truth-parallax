The Advaita interpretation of the Aindra hymns reads Indra not as a separate deity but as a symbolic representation of pure consciousness overcoming the forces of ignorance. Indra's slaying of Vritra — the serpent who withholds the cosmic waters — represents the breakthrough of self-knowledge (*atma-jnana*) that releases the 'waters' of awareness from the bondage of *avidya*.

The Chandogya Upanishad's teaching that Indra sought knowledge of the self from Prajapati (Chandogya 8.7-12) reinforces the Advaitic reading that even the king of the gods must ultimately seek liberation through knowledge of Brahman. The Aindra samans thus celebrate not merely divine heroism but the universal quest for self-realization.
