Advaitins interpret the Agneya fire hymns of the Sama Veda as pointing to the inner fire of consciousness (*chit-agni*) that illuminates all experience. While the ritual context involves the physical sacrificial fire, the Advaitic reading sees the saman's melody as awakening the aspirant to the self-luminous awareness that is Brahman. The Chandogya Upanishad's identification of the Udgitha with Om connects these fire chants to the ultimate non-dual reality.

The transformation of Rig Vedic text into Samavedic melody illustrates a key Advaitic principle: the same truth can be apprehended at different levels. The spoken word operates at the level of intellect, while the sung melody engages a deeper stratum of consciousness, moving the aspirant from conceptual understanding toward direct experience of the non-dual awareness that underlies all perception.
