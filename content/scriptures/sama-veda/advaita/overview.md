## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Sama Veda holds special significance because it is the parent text of the Chandogya Upanishad, one of the most important sources of non-dual teaching. The Chandogya's celebrated mahavakya "Tat tvam asi" ("You are That") and its extensive meditations on the Udgitha (*Om*) connect the Sama Veda's musical liturgy directly to the highest Advaitic insight: the identity of the individual self with Brahman.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Udgitha as Om
The Chandogya Upanishad (1.1) identifies the Udgitha — the central chant of the Sama Veda — with the syllable Om, and through Om with Brahman itself. Shankara's commentary traces how the ritual chanting leads the aspirant from sonic experience to the direct realization of non-dual awareness.

### From Ritual to Knowledge
Advaitins see the Sama Veda's liturgical function as preparatory: the beauty and precision of samavedic chanting purifies the mind and makes it fit for the higher knowledge (*jnana*) taught in the Upanishads. The progression from Samhita to Upanishad mirrors the aspirant's journey from action to knowledge.

## Theological Emphases

- **Nada Brahman**: The Sama Veda embodies the principle that ultimate reality can be apprehended through sacred sound
- **Chandogya Connection**: The Sama Veda's authority in Advaita rests largely on its Upanishadic teaching
- **Om as Essence**: The syllable Om distills the entire Sama Veda into a single vibration of non-dual truth
- **Transcendence of Ritual**: The melodies point beyond themselves to the silence of pure awareness
