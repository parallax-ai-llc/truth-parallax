## Overview

The Protestant reading of 3 Enoch examines the text for its illumination of New Testament angelology and Christology. The exalted Metatron — enthroned, crowned, bearing divine names — provides a crucial context for understanding how early Christians could attribute divine prerogatives to the exalted Christ within a monotheistic framework. The 'two powers in heaven' controversy reflected in chapter 7 (the demotion of Metatron) directly illuminates the theological tensions underlying early Christological claims.
