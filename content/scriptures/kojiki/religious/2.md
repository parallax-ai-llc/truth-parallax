## The Divine Commission and Sacred Lineage

Volume II narrates events of profound religious significance for Shinto: the divine commission of the imperial line, the first emperor's sacred journey, and the establishment of proper worship throughout the land.

### Tenson Korin: The Heavenly Descent

The descent of Ninigi-no-Mikoto, grandson of Amaterasu, to rule the Central Land of Reed Plains is the foundational event of imperial Shinto theology. Amaterasu bestows three sacred treasures -- the mirror, the jewel, and the sword -- which become the imperial regalia and objects of deep religious veneration.

### Emperor Jimmu's Eastern Expedition

Jimmu's campaign to establish his capital in Yamato is understood religiously as a sacred mission guided by divine providence. The appearance of the Yatagarasu (three-legged crow) as divine guide, the bestowal of the sacred sword Futsu-no-mitama, and the assistance of various kami demonstrate that human sovereignty rests on divine authorization.

### Kami of Land and Sea

Throughout Volume II, kami of mountains, rivers, and seas play active roles in the narrative. These accounts provide the mythological charter for the shrines dedicated to these kami across Japan. Each local shrine's identity is rooted in the sacred narratives of the Kojiki and its companion text, the Nihon Shoki.
