---
title: "Kojiki - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kojiki"
chapter: 2
---

**1** At this time the Male-Who-Invites said: "The child which we have borne last is not good." So he drew the ten-grasp sabre that was augustly girded on him, and cut off the head of his child the Fire-Deity. Hereupon the blood that stuck to the point of the sword and bespattered the multitudinous rock-masses was the Deity Rock-Splitter, next the Deity Root-Splitter, next the Rock-Possessing-Male-Deity.

**2** The blood that stuck to the upper part of the sword and bespattered the multitudinous rock-masses was the Awfully-Swift-Deity, next the Fire-Swift-Deity, next the Brave-Awful-Possessing-Male-Deity, another name for whom is the Brave-Dashing-Male-Deity, and another name for whom is the Luxuriant-Swift-Deity.

**3** The blood that collected on the hilt of the august sword and leaked out between his fingers was the Deity Kura-Okami and next the Deity Kura-Mitsuha.

**4** The Deity that was born from the head of the Fire-Deity who was slain was the Deity-of-the-Large-Mountain-Possessor. The Deity that was next born from his chest was the Deity-of-the-Middle-Mountain-Possessor. The Deity that was next born from his belly was the Deity-of-the-Dark-Mountain-Possessor. The Deity that was next born from his genitals was the Deity-of-the-Deep-Valley. The Deity that was next born from his left hand was the Deity-of-the-Innermost-Mountains. The Deity that was next born from his right hand was the Deity-of-the-Outer-Mountains. The Deity that was next born from his left foot was the Deity-of-the-Mountain-Foot. The Deity that was next born from his right foot was the Deity-of-the-Flat-Mountains.

**5** Then the Male-Who-Invites, wishing to meet his younger sister the Female-Who-Invites, followed after her to the Land of Hades. So when from the palace she raised the door and came out to meet him, the Male-Who-Invites spoke, saying: "Thine Augustness my lovely younger sister, the lands that I and thou made are not yet finished making; so come back."

**6** Then the Female-Who-Invites answered, saying: "Lamentable indeed that thou camest not sooner. I have eaten of the hearth of Hades. Nevertheless, as I am honored by thine entry here, I wish to return. Moreover, I will discuss it with the Deities of Hades. Look not at me."

**7** Having thus spoken, she went back inside the palace, and the time of her absence was very long, so that he could not wait. So having taken and broken off one of the end-teeth of the comb stuck in the august left bunch of his hair, he lit one fire and went in and looked.

**8** Maggots were swarming, and she was rotting, and in her head dwelt the Great-Thunder, in her breast dwelt the Fire-Thunder, in her belly dwelt the Black-Thunder, in her genitals dwelt the Crack-Thunder, in her left hand dwelt the Young-Thunder, in her right hand dwelt the Earth-Thunder, in her left foot dwelt the Rumbling-Thunder, in her right foot dwelt the Couchant-Thunder. Altogether eight Thunder-Deities had been born and dwelt there.

**9** Hereupon the Male-Who-Invites, startled at the sight, fled back, whereupon his younger sister the Female-Who-Invites said: "Thou hast put me to shame," and at once sent the Ugly-Female-of-Hades to pursue him.

**10** Then the Male-Who-Invites took his black august head-dress and cast it down, and it instantly turned into grapes. While she picked them up and ate them, he fled on; but as she still pursued him, he took and broke the multitudinous and close-toothed comb in the right bunch of his hair and cast it down, and it instantly turned into bamboo-sprouts. While she pulled them up and ate them, he fled on.

**11** And afterward the Female-Who-Invites sent the eight Thunder-Deities with a thousand and five hundred warriors of Hades to pursue him. So he, drawing the ten-grasp sabre that was augustly girded on him, fled forward brandishing it behind him, and the Thunder-Deities all pursued to the Even-Pass-of-Hades.

**12** So he took three peaches that were growing at the base of the Even-Pass-of-Hades, and waited and smote his pursuers therewith, so that they all fled back. Then the Male-Who-Invites announced to the peaches: "Like as ye have helped me, so must ye help all living people in the Central Land of Reed-Plains when they shall fall into troublous circumstances and be harassed and suffer."

**13** Last of all the Female-Who-Invites came herself in pursuit. So he drew a thousand-draught rock, and blocked up the Even-Pass-of-Hades, and placed the rock in the middle; and they stood opposite to one another, and exchanged leave-takings.

**14** Then the Female-Who-Invites said: "My lovely elder brother, thine Augustness, if thou do this, I will in one day strangle to death a thousand of the folk of thy land." Then the Male-Who-Invites replied: "My lovely younger sister, thine Augustness, if thou do this, I will in one day set up a thousand and five hundred parturition-houses. In this manner each day a thousand people would surely be born."

**15** So the Female-Who-Invites is called the Great-Deity-of-Hades. Again she is called the Road-Reaching-Great-Deity. Again the rock with which the Even-Pass-of-Hades was blocked is called the Great-Deity-of-the-Road-Turning-back, and again it is called the Blocking-Great-Deity-of-the-Door-of-Hades.

**16** So the Even-Pass-of-Hades is now called the Ifuya-Pass in the Land of Idzumo.

**17** Therefore the Male-Who-Invites said: "I have been to a most unpleasant land, a horrible, unclean land. Therefore I will perform the purification of mine august person." So he went to the plain of Ahagi at Tachibana in Himuka in the island of Tsukushi, and purified and cleansed himself.

**18** When he washed his august left eye, the Deity that was born was called the Heaven-Shining-Great-August-Deity, who is also called Amaterasu. When he washed his august right eye, the Deity that was born was called the Moon-Night-Possessor, who is also called Tsukuyomi. When he washed his august nose, the Deity that was born was called the Brave-Swift-Impetuous-Male-Augustness, who is also called Susanoo.

**19** The Male-Who-Invites was greatly delighted, saying: "I, begetting child after child, have at my final begetting gotten three illustrious children." At once he took off his august necklace of jewels and, shaking the jewels till they jingled, bestowed it upon Amaterasu, the Heaven-Shining-Great-August-Deity, saying: "Do thou rule the Plain of High Heaven."

**20** Next he said to the Moon-Night-Possessor: "Do thou rule the Dominion of the Night." Next he said to the Brave-Swift-Impetuous-Male-Augustness: "Do thou rule the Sea-Plain."
