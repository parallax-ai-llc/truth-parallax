Japanese Buddhist readings of the Kamiyo section interpret the spontaneous appearance of the primordial kami as an expression of the self-manifesting nature of Buddha-nature (busshō). The creative acts of Izanagi and Izanami are read through the lens of dependent origination — each kami, each island arising in dependence upon prior conditions rather than from nothing. Amaterasu, the sun goddess, was widely identified with Dainichi Nyorai (Mahāvairocana), the cosmic Buddha of Shingon Buddhism.

The descent of Izanagi to the underworld (Yomi) to retrieve Izanami, and his horrified flight from her decaying form, was interpreted by Buddhist commentators as an illustration of attachment and the suffering it produces. Izanagi's subsequent purification in the river became a model for Buddhist lustration practices and the removal of spiritual defilements. The entire creation narrative was thus read as a drama of enlightenment and delusion played out in mythological terms.
