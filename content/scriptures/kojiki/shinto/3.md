The third volume of the Kojiki, tracing the imperial lineage from Emperor Jimmu to Empress Suiko, is read in Shinto tradition as the record of how divine authority was transmitted from the heavenly realm to the earthly throne. The concept of tennō (heavenly sovereign) is grounded in this narrative, which presents each emperor as a descendant of Amaterasu carrying forward the mandate established when Ninigi descended with the three imperial regalia.

Shinto ritual surrounding the imperial household — enthronement ceremonies, the Daijōsai (Great Thanksgiving Festival), and the ongoing rites performed by the emperor — draws directly upon the Kojiki's account of this sacred lineage. The text establishes the theological framework within which the emperor functions not as a political ruler alone but as the chief priest of the Shinto tradition, mediating between the kami and the people.
