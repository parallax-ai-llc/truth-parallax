## Overview

Shinto tradition receives the Kojiki as its most authoritative written source for understanding the kami, the creation of the world, and the sacred foundations of Japanese life. The text is not merely a literary or historical document but a revelation of the divine order that underlies all reality, from the generation of the cosmos to the establishment of the imperial line.

## Theological Emphases

The myths of Izanagi and Izanami, the birth of Amaterasu from the left eye of Izanagi during his purification, and the descent of Ninigi to rule the earthly realm form the core narrative that Shinto theology draws upon. These stories establish the principles of musubi (creative interconnection), harae (purification), and the intimate relationship between the kami, the land, and the people of Japan.

## Interpretive Context

In shrine practice, the Kojiki provides the mythological context for festivals, rituals, and the veneration of specific kami enshrined throughout Japan. The text is read not as ancient history but as a living account of realities that continue to shape the spiritual landscape.
