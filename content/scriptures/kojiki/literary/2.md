The Izumo Cycle represents the Kojiki at its most narratively engaging. The stories of Ōkuninushi — his mistreatment by his eighty brothers, his rescue by a princess from the underworld, his cleverness in overcoming Susanoo's trials — follow the pattern of the folktale hero with the warmth and humor characteristic of oral storytelling. The romance between Ōkuninushi and Suseribime, including their exchange of love poems, introduces the theme of passionate attachment that would become central to Japanese literary tradition.

Susanoo's transformation from the destructive, weeping god expelled from heaven to the dragon-slaying hero who rescues Kushinadahime is one of the great character arcs in world mythology. The famous poem he composes upon building his wedding palace — 'Many clouds rise / the clouds form a fence / a many-layered fence / for the newlyweds to retire within / what a fine many-layered fence!' — is traditionally considered the first Japanese waka poem, making Susanoo both a mythological and literary founder figure.
