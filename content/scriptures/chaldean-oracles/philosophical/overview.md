## Overview

The Philosophical reading of the Chaldean Oracles engages with the text's radical claim that theurgy — sacred ritual action — can accomplish what pure intellectual contemplation cannot: actual union with the transcendent divine. This claim posed a fundamental challenge to the Platonic tradition, generating the great debate between Porphyry (who favored intellectual ascent) and Iamblichus (who defended theurgy) that shaped the entire subsequent history of Neoplatonism.
