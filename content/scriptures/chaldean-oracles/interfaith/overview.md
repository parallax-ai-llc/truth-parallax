## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Chaldean Oracles explores how this pagan theurgical text has influenced and found resonance across multiple religious traditions. The Oracles' doctrines of divine emanation, ritual ascent, and the soul's purification shaped not only Neoplatonism but also Christian mystical theology (through Pseudo-Dionysius), Islamic philosophy, and Renaissance esotericism. This reading examines the Oracles as a site of convergence for diverse traditions of mystical practice and theological reflection.
