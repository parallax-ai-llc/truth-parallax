## Overview

The Academic study of the Chaldean Oracles situates these fragmentary hexameter verses within the religious and intellectual landscape of the second-century Roman Empire and their subsequent reception by the Neoplatonic philosophers. Scholars examine the Oracles' complex cosmology, their relationship to Middle Platonic and Gnostic thought, and the pivotal role they played in the development of theurgy as a philosophical and religious practice from Iamblichus through Proclus.
