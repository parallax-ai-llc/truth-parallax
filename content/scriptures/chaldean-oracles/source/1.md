---
title: "Chaldean Oracles — Fragment 1: The First Fire"
scripture: "chaldean-oracles"
chapter: 1
---

**1** There exists a certain Intelligible which you must perceive by the flower of mind. For if you incline your mind toward it and perceive it as perceiving a specific thing, you will not perceive it.

**2** For it is the power of a radiant sharpness, shining with intellectual flashes. You must not perceive that Intelligible violently, but with the extended flame of the extended mind, which measures all things except that Intelligible.

**3** The Father perfected all things and handed them over to the Second Mind, which the whole race of men calls First.

**4** For the Paternal Mind sowed symbols through the cosmos, the Mind which thinks the Intelligibles. The Intelligibles are called unspeakable beauties.

**5** The Father did not instill fear, but rather poured forth persuasion.
