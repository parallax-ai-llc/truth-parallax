## Overview

The Literary reading of the Chaldean Oracles attends to the text's distinctive poetic form: hexameter verses delivered in the voice of divine revelation. The Oracles draw on the conventions of Greek oracular poetry — the Delphic tradition, Homeric hymns, and Orphic verse — while deploying a highly technical theological vocabulary. The fragmentary state of the text itself becomes a literary feature, as the surviving quotations present a mosaic of cryptic utterances that invite interpretation.
