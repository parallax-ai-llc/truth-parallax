## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 1 Samuel draws on Scripture, Tradition, and the Magisterium to read the book as a narrative of sacred anointing, providential governance, and the costly demands of obedience. Hannah's prayer (chapter 2) is treasured as a prefigurement of Mary's Magnificat, and the anointing of kings is understood as a sacramental foreshadowing of the Church's own rites of consecration.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Patristic and Medieval Allegorical Reading

The Church Fathers read David's anointing as a type of Christ's messianic consecration and Saul's rejection as a warning against pride and disobedience. Medieval commentators developed rich allegorical readings of the Ark narrative and David's combat with Goliath.

### Marian Typology

Hannah's song of praise (2:1–10) is recognized as the literary and theological precursor to Mary's Magnificat (Luke 1:46–55). Catholic tradition sees in Hannah a model of faithful prayer, maternal sacrifice, and cooperation with divine providence.

### Sacramental-Liturgical

The anointing of Saul and David with oil prefigures the sacraments of Baptism, Confirmation, and Holy Orders. The sacred character of anointing — setting apart for God's service — runs through Catholic sacramental theology.

## Theological Emphases

- **Sacred Anointing**: The *mashiach* concept as the foundation for Christian understanding of the Anointed One
- **Obedience and Authority**: Saul's failure illustrates the danger of placing personal judgment above divine command
- **Cooperation with Grace**: David's courage before Goliath exemplifies human action empowered by divine grace
- **The Communion of Saints**: The interconnection of Hannah, Samuel, David, and the Messiah across generations

## Notable Catholic Commentators

- **St. Gregory the Great**: Drew moral lessons from Saul's rise and fall in the *Moralia*
- **St. Thomas Aquinas**: Referenced 1 Samuel in discussions of kingship, obedience, and natural law
- **The Venerable Bede**: Provided allegorical commentary connecting David to Christ
- **Pope Benedict XVI**: Cited David's faith as a model of trust in God's providence
