## Obedience as the True Sacrifice

Catholic interpretation of 1 Samuel 15 places Samuel's oracle — "To obey is better than sacrifice" (15:22) — at the heart of the chapter's meaning. This principle shapes Catholic understanding of the relationship between liturgical worship and moral obedience.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Command Against Amalek (15:1-3)

Catholic moral theology addresses the difficulty of the herem command through the lens of progressive revelation: God's self-disclosure unfolds across salvation history, and the Church reads the Old Testament in light of Christ, who commands love of enemies.

### "To obey is better than sacrifice" (15:22-23)

Catholic liturgical theology affirms that the external rites of worship must be animated by interior obedience. The Mass is not a magical ritual but the offering of the whole self in union with Christ's sacrifice. Without obedience, liturgical celebration becomes empty form.

### The Tearing of the Kingdom (15:27-28)

The torn robe as prophetic sign carries sacramental significance in Catholic reading: visible, material signs convey spiritual realities. The torn garment makes visible the invisible reality of God's judgment.

## Theological Themes

- **The Primacy of Obedience**: Liturgical worship without moral obedience is null
- **Progressive Revelation**: The Old Testament is read in light of Christ's fuller revelation
- **Prophetic Signs**: Material signs that embody and communicate spiritual truths
- **The Tragedy of Self-Deception**: Saul's inability to see his own disobedience

## Reflection

Catholic theology joins Protestant theology in affirming the primacy of obedience over ritual. The Mass itself, the Church teaches, requires that worshipers bring not merely their physical presence but their obedient hearts. Samuel's oracle remains a permanent challenge to every form of worship that substitutes ceremony for conversion.
