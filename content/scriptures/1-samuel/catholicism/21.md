## Sacred Bread and Human Need

Catholic interpretation of 1 Samuel 21 is shaped decisively by Jesus' own citation of this episode in the Synoptic Gospels. The showbread given to David becomes a key text in Catholic sacramental theology, illustrating the relationship between ritual law and pastoral necessity.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David Receives the Showbread (21:1-6)

Jesus' reference to this passage establishes a christological hermeneutic: the sacred bread, normally reserved for priests, may be given to the hungry in cases of genuine need. Catholic moral theology has drawn from this the principle of *epikeia* — the prudent application of law that considers the legislator's intent rather than rigid literalism.

### The Bread as Eucharistic Type

Catholic exegesis reads the showbread — the bread of the Presence — as a type of the Eucharist. Just as this sacred bread sustained David in his hour of need, so the Eucharist sustains the pilgrim Church.

## Theological Themes

- **Epikeia**: The pastoral application of law in cases of genuine necessity
- **Eucharistic Typology**: The showbread as a prefigurement of the Blessed Sacrament
- **Christ's Authority**: Jesus' citation of this passage establishes his authority over the law
- **Mercy and Law**: The harmony between compassion and sacred order

## Reflection

Catholic theology finds in this chapter a principle that Pope Francis has emphasized: the law exists to serve human flourishing, not to burden it. The sacred bread given to the hungry David anticipates the Eucharist given to the hungry Church — bread that nourishes precisely because it is holy.
