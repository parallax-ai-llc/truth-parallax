## Overview

While 1 Samuel is not part of the Quran, the narrative of Saul (Talut) and David (Dawud) is directly referenced in Surah al-Baqarah (2:246–251). The Quran presents Talut as a king chosen by God despite the people's objections, and Dawud as the young warrior who slays Jalut (Goliath) by God's permission. Islamic scholarship engages with the broader 1 Samuel narrative through the *Isra'iliyyat* tradition, finding resonance with Quranic principles of divine sovereignty, trust in God, and righteous leadership.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Quranic Parallel

The Quran's account of Talut and Dawud (Surah 2:246–251) provides the authoritative Islamic lens for reading the 1 Samuel narrative. God appoints Talut as king, tests the army at the river, and grants Dawud victory over Jalut — a narrative of divine selection and faith under trial.

### Isra'iliyyat Framework

Classical Muslim historians such as al-Tabari, Ibn Kathir, and al-Tha'labi supplemented the Quranic account with details from Israelite traditions, providing fuller narratives of Samuel, Saul, and David.

### Thematic Resonance

Muslim scholars identify themes in 1 Samuel that align with Quranic values: the primacy of God's choice over human preference, the testing of faith through hardship, and the responsibility of just governance.

## Theological Emphases

- **Divine Selection of Leaders**: God chooses Talut despite the people's desire for a wealthy king — sovereignty belongs to God alone
- **Tawakkul**: Trust in God in the face of overwhelming odds, exemplified by Dawud before Jalut
- **The Test of the River**: The Quranic test at the river (2:249) illustrates the separation of true believers from the wavering
- **Prophetic Lineage**: Dawud as both king and prophet in Islamic tradition, recipient of the Zabur (Psalms)
- **Just Governance**: The Quranic ideal of the righteous ruler who governs by God's command

## Notable Islamic Scholars

- **al-Tabari**: Provided extensive narratives of Israelite kings in *Tarikh al-Rusul wa'l-Muluk*
- **Ibn Kathir**: Discussed Talut and Dawud in *al-Bidaya wa'l-Nihaya* and *Qisas al-Anbiya*
- **al-Tha'labi**: Collected prophetic narratives including the Saul-David story in *Qisas al-Anbiya*
- **Fakhr al-Din al-Razi**: Provided detailed tafsir on the Talut-Dawud passage in Surah 2
