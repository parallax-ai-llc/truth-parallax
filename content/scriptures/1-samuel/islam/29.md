## Rescue by Divine Decree

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 29 reads the Philistine dismissal as qadar -- divine decree protecting David through pagan decisions. "They plan, and Allah plans. Allah is the best of planners" (Surah 8:30).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### God as Best of Planners

David is rescued from an impossible dilemma by God's intervention through pagan calculations.

### Lutf -- God's Subtle Kindness

Divine protection operates without visible intervention.

## Theological Themes

- **Qadar**: Divine decree through ordinary decisions
- **God as Best of Planners**: Human schemes serve God unknowingly
- **Moral Rescue**: God prevents situations compromising integrity
- **Lutf**: God's subtle kindness behind the scenes

## Reflection

No angel appears, no miracle occurs -- yet God's purposes are accomplished through pagan self-interest. This is lutf.
