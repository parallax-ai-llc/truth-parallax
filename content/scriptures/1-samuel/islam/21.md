## Provision in Extremity

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 21 reads the showbread given to David through darurah (necessity). The Quran allows prohibited foods in extreme need: "Whoever is forced by severe hunger -- Allah is Forgiving" (Surah 5:3).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### The Principle of Necessity

David receives sacred bread because his need is genuine. Islamic jurisprudence establishes that darurah can override ordinary prohibitions.

### Divine Provision

"There is no creature on earth but that upon Allah is its provision" (Surah 11:6).

## Theological Themes

- **Darurah**: Necessity permits otherwise prohibited actions
- **Divine Provision**: God provides through unexpected means
- **Sidq and Its Limits**: Truthfulness in tension with preservation of life
- **God's Care**: The righteous are sustained even in exile

## Reflection

God sustains his servant David through irregular but providential means.
