## Repentance and Divine Deliverance

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 7 reads Samuel's call to repentance through tawbah -- turning away from sin and returning to God.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### The Call to Repentance

Samuel's demand to remove foreign gods parallels the Quran: "Turn in repentance to your Lord and submit to Him" (Surah 39:54). Islamic tawbah requires concrete action.

### Intercessory Prayer

Samuel's intercession resonates with the concept of the righteous who pray for their communities.

### The Ebenezer Stone

The memorial parallels Islamic practice of shukr (thanksgiving) and dhikr (remembrance).

## Theological Themes

- **Tawbah**: Repentance as concrete action
- **Shafa'ah**: Intercession by the righteous
- **Divine Deliverance**: God saves those who sincerely repent
- **Dhikr**: Remembrance as foundation for trust

## Reflection

Muslim readers find a universal pattern: sincere repentance brings divine deliverance. "When My servants ask about Me, I am near. I respond to the call of the caller" (Surah 2:186).
