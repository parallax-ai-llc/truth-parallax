## A Mother's Trust in God

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 1 approaches Hannah's prayer through Quranic values: the desperate supplication (du'a) of a faithful servant, trust in God's timing (tawakkul), and the principle that God answers the sincere. Hannah's story resonates deeply with Islamic theology of prayer.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### The Power of Du'a

Hannah's persistent, silent prayer parallels the Quranic teaching: "Call upon Me; I will respond to you" (Surah 40:60). Her wordless petition embodies the Islamic ideal of sincerity (ikhlas) in prayer.

### Barrenness and Divine Decree

Hannah's barrenness and eventual conception illustrate the Quranic principle that God alone grants children: "He gives to whom He wills female children, and He gives to whom He wills males" (Surah 42:49). The parallels to the Quranic accounts of Zakariyya and Maryam are striking.

### The Vow of Dedication

Hannah's dedication of her son parallels Maryam's mother's vow in Surah 3:35: "My Lord, I have pledged to You what is in my womb, consecrated for Your service."

## Theological Themes

- **Du'a and Ikhlas**: Sincere supplication as the essence of the believer's relationship with God
- **Qadar**: God's decree over fertility and family
- **Nadhr (Vow)**: The Islamic framework for making and fulfilling vows to God
- **Sabr**: Hannah's patience under trial models steadfast endurance

## Reflection

Muslim readers find in Hannah's prayer a universal pattern: the sincere heart that turns to God in desperation is never turned away. Her story illustrates principles at the heart of Islamic devotion -- trust, patience, and the conviction that God hears every whispered prayer.
