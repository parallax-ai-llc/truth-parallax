## Victory by God's Permission

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 14 reads Jonathan's raid through tawakkul -- trust in God's power regardless of numbers.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### The Faithful Few

Jonathan's victory with one companion directly parallels: "How many a small company has overcome a large company by permission of Allah" (Surah 2:249). This verse from the Talut-Dawud narrative applies here.

### Rash Oaths

Saul's oath parallels Quranic teaching on oaths. Islam provides mechanisms for expiation (kaffarat al-yamin).

## Theological Themes

- **Tawakkul**: Absolute trust in God's power
- **The Faithful Few**: God's power is not limited by numbers
- **Oaths**: The framework for making, keeping, and expiating oaths
- **Courage Born of Faith**: True bravery flows from trust in God

## Reflection

"How many a small company has overcome a large company by permission of Allah" (Surah 2:249) is the Islamic commentary on every act of faith-driven courage.
