## The Voice of Divine Revelation

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 3 reads Samuel's calling through the framework of wahy (divine revelation). His experience resonates with the Islamic understanding of how God communicates with the righteous.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Divine Communication

Samuel's nighttime calling parallels Quranic descriptions: "It is not granted to any mortal that Allah should speak to him except by revelation or from behind a veil" (Surah 42:51). The gradual recognition reflects learning to discern God's voice.

### The Rarity of God's Word

"The word of the LORD was rare in those days" parallels the concept of jahiliyyah -- periods of spiritual ignorance between prophetic missions.

### Obedient Response

Samuel's response -- "Speak, LORD, for your servant is listening" -- embodies the concept of islam itself: submission to God's will.

## Theological Themes

- **Wahy**: Divine revelation and its modes of transmission
- **The Servant's Posture**: Submission and readiness to obey as the essence of faith
- **Periods of Spiritual Darkness**: God's word becomes rare when the faithful are corrupt
- **Prophetic Integrity**: The obligation to deliver God's message faithfully

## Reflection

Muslim readers find in Samuel's calling a familiar pattern: God breaks into human silence with a message that demands obedient response.
