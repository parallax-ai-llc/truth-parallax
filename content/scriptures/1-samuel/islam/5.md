## The Fall of the Idol

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 5 celebrates the humiliation of Dagon as a demonstration of tawhid. The Quran's central commitment is the rejection of idolatry, and Dagon's collapse provides a powerful illustration.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### The Impotence of Idols

Dagon's fall parallels the Quranic critique: "Do you worship that which you yourselves carve, while Allah created you and what you make?" (Surah 37:95-96). Ibrahim's destruction of the idols parallels Dagon's collapse.

### God's Universal Sovereignty

The plagues demonstrate that God's authority extends to all nations: "To Allah belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth" (Surah 3:189).

## Theological Themes

- **Tawhid**: The absolute oneness of God and the impotence of all rivals
- **The Destruction of Idols**: Ibrahim's example as the model of idol-breaking
- **Universal Lordship**: God is Lord of all creation
- **Divine Justice**: God's judgment reaches all who violate his sovereignty

## Reflection

Muslim readers find in Dagon's fall an echo of Ibrahim's destruction of the idols and Muhammad's cleansing of the Ka'bah. Every idol must fall before the one true God.
