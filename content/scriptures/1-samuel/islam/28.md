## The Forbidden Path

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 28 reads Saul's consultation as sihr (sorcery) -- one of the seven destructive sins. The Quran condemns sorcery explicitly (Surah 2:102).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### The Prohibition of Sihr

Sorcery, divination, and consulting the dead are forbidden. Saul's resort illustrates the danger of rejecting legitimate guidance.

### Divine Silence

"Whoever turns away from My remembrance -- he will have a depressed existence" (Surah 20:124). God withdraws guidance from the persistently disobedient.

## Theological Themes

- **The Prohibition of Sihr**: Among the gravest sins
- **Divine Silence as Judgment**: God withdraws guidance from the disobedient
- **Desperation**: When faith fails, forbidden paths become tempting
- **The Seven Destructive Sins**: Sorcery alongside shirk, murder, usury

## Reflection

The cure for spiritual darkness is not forbidden knowledge but renewed tawbah -- repentance and return to the straight path.
