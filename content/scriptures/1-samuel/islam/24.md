## The Restraint of the Righteous

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 24 reads David's refusal to kill Saul as hilm -- forbearance. The Quran praises "those who restrain anger and pardon the people" (Surah 3:134).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Hilm

David's restraint embodies a virtue the Quran celebrates as divine. God is al-Halim (the Forbearing).

### Leaving Vengeance to God

"Is not Allah the most just of judges?" (Surah 95:8). The believer entrusts judgment to God.

## Theological Themes

- **Hilm**: Forbearance as divine attribute and human virtue
- **Leaving Vengeance to God**: Ultimate justice belongs to the Creator
- **Patience Under Persecution**: Enduring injustice as a mark of faith
- **Restraint of Power**: Withholding force as supreme virtue

## Reflection

The ability to refrain from exercising legitimate power is, in Islamic ethics, a greater display of strength than any act of force.
