## Dawud Defeats Jalut

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 17 is anchored in the Quranic account (Surah 2:249-251). The Quran emphasizes divine permission as the source of victory.

## The Quranic Account

### Surah 2:249-251

"So they defeated them by permission of Allah, and Dawud killed Jalut, and Allah gave him the kingship and wisdom." The emphasis on divine permission (bi-idhn Allah) is the key lens.

### The Test of the River

The Quran adds a detail: Talut tests his army at a river. Only the faithful few who restrain themselves continue to battle.

## Key Themes

### Tawakkul in Action

Dawud embodies perfect tawakkul: "Whoever relies upon Allah -- then He is sufficient for him" (Surah 65:3).

### The Faithful Minority

Truth is determined by faithfulness, not majority.

## Theological Themes

- **Bi-Idhn Allah**: Victory comes only by God's permission
- **Tawakkul**: Complete reliance on divine power
- **The Faithful Few**: Victories won by the committed minority
- **Dawud's Kingship**: The beginning of divinely appointed rule

## Reflection

For Muslim readers, this is a Quranic revelation: victory belongs to God, is granted by his permission, and comes to those who trust completely.
