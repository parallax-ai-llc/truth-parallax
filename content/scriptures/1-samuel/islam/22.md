## Tyranny Unmasked

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 22 reads the massacre as zulm (oppression) -- among the most condemned sins. "Do not think that Allah is unaware of what the wrongdoers do" (Surah 14:42).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Zulm

Saul's willingness to kill priests reveals total moral corruption. The Quran reserves its harshest condemnation for oppressors.

### The Community of the Oppressed

David's gathering of the distressed resonates with the early Muslim community: refugees who gathered around the Prophet in Medina.

## Theological Themes

- **Zulm**: Oppression as among the gravest sins
- **The Gathering of the Oppressed**: Community formed from refugees
- **Divine Accountability**: Every act of oppression will be accounted for
- **The Prophetic Community**: Righteous community formed in wilderness

## Reflection

Muslim readers see in David's outcasts a parallel to the early Muslim community -- refugees who formed a society based on justice and faith.
