## The End Decreed by God

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 31 reads Saul's death through qadar. "Every soul will taste death, and you will only be given your full compensation on the Day of Resurrection" (Surah 3:185).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Qadar and Death

Death comes to all at God's appointed time. The men of Jabesh-gilead demonstrate the importance of honoring the dead, a principle Islam emphasizes through ghusl and janazah.

### The Honor of the Dead

Proper burial is a sacred obligation reflecting human dignity.

## Theological Themes

- **Qadar**: Every soul has an appointed time
- **Honor of the Dead**: Sacred obligation of dignified burial
- **Divine Justice**: Saul's end as consequence of disobedience
- **Innocent Suffering**: Jonathan's death as part of divine mystery

## Reflection

God's qadar encompasses all things. The book ends in tragedy, but Dawud -- prophet, king, servant of God -- will receive the kingdom God decreed from the beginning. To God belongs the sovereignty, and to God all things return.
