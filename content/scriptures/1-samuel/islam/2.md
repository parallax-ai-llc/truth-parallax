## God's Sovereignty and Human Corruption

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 2 finds resonance between Hannah's song of divine reversal and Quranic teaching on God's absolute sovereignty. The corruption of Eli's sons illustrates the Islamic warning against exploiting sacred trust.

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Divine Reversal

Hannah's proclamation that God humbles the proud and exalts the lowly echoes the Quran: "He elevates whom He wills and abases whom He wills" (Surah 6:83). All power and status are temporary grants from God.

### The Corruption of Religious Leaders

The sins of Hophni and Phinehas illustrate the Quranic warning against those who "consume the wealth of people unjustly" (Surah 9:34). Islamic tradition holds religious leaders to the highest standard of integrity.

## Theological Themes

- **Tawhid**: God's absolute sovereignty over all human affairs
- **Amanah**: Sacred trust -- the obligation of religious leaders to serve with integrity
- **Divine Justice**: God's pattern of reversing human hierarchies
- **Accountability**: Those given religious authority face the strictest accounting

## Reflection

Muslim readers recognize in Hannah's theology a kindred voice: the God who lifts the poor and brings down the mighty is the same God worshiped in every mosque.
