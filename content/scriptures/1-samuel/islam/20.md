## The Parting of the Faithful

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 20 reads the farewell as a supreme act of self-sacrificing loyalty, a virtue the Quran celebrates: "They prefer others over themselves, even though they are in privation" (Surah 59:9).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### Ithar -- Selfless Preference

Jonathan sacrifices his royal inheritance for David, exemplifying ithar -- preferring others over oneself.

### The Cost of Faithfulness

Jonathan endures his father's rage rather than betray his friend.

## Theological Themes

- **Ithar**: Preferring others -- one of the highest Islamic virtues
- **Covenant Under God**: Human bonds strengthened by divine witness
- **The Cost of Discipleship**: Faithfulness may cost cherished relationships
- **Tawakkul**: Trusting God with the outcome

## Reflection

Muslim readers find in Jonathan's self-sacrifice the essence of ihsan -- excellence in faith.
