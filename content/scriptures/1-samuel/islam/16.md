## God Chooses Dawud

Islamic engagement with 1 Samuel 16 reads David's anointing through divine selection. Dawud is honored as prophet, king, and recipient of the Zabur (Psalms).

## Key Themes and Islamic Parallels

### God Looks on the Heart

The principle aligns with the hadith: "Allah does not look at your bodies or forms, but He looks at your hearts and deeds" (Sahih Muslim).

### The Selection of the Unlikely

David the youngest, overlooked shepherd, resonates with God choosing the unexpected: Muhammad was an orphan from a minor clan.

### Dawud as Prophet-King

The Quran affirms: "We gave Dawud the Zabur" (Surah 4:163) and "We strengthened his kingdom and gave him wisdom" (Surah 38:20).

## Theological Themes

- **Divine Selection**: God chooses by invisible criteria
- **Taqwa**: Inner piety as God's standard
- **The Prophet-King**: Dawud's unique role
- **The Zabur**: The Psalms as revealed scripture

## Reflection

Muslim readers honor Dawud as one of God's greatest servants -- a prophet combining political authority with spiritual depth.
