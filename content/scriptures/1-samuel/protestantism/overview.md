## Overview

Protestant interpretation of 1 Samuel centers on the sovereignty of God in raising up and deposing leaders, the priority of obedience over ritual, and the typological reading of David as a foreshadowing of Christ. The book's famous declaration — "The LORD looks not on outward appearance, but on the heart" (16:7) — resonates deeply with Reformation theology's emphasis on inner faith over external works.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Redemptive-Historical

Reformed Protestants read 1 Samuel within the unfolding arc of salvation history. Samuel is the last judge, Saul the failed king, and David the man after God's own heart whose line will produce the Messiah. Each transition advances God's redemptive plan.

### Typological-Christological

David's anointing, his suffering as a fugitive, and his eventual enthronement are read as prefigurations of Christ: rejected by the powers of this world, yet chosen by God and destined for an eternal throne.

### Moral-Theological

Protestant preachers have drawn extensively from 1 Samuel for lessons on leadership, obedience, pride, and the dangers of compromise. Saul's disobedience in chapters 13 and 15 serves as a cautionary paradigm.

## Theological Emphases

- **Sovereignty of God**: God raises up Samuel, anoints Saul, rejects Saul, and chooses David — all according to his sovereign will
- **Obedience over Sacrifice**: Samuel's rebuke in 15:22 is a cornerstone text for Protestant ethics
- **The Heart**: God's criterion of judgment (16:7) aligns with the Reformation emphasis on justification by faith
- **The Spirit of God**: The Spirit's coming and departing illustrates the relationship between divine empowerment and human faithfulness

## Notable Protestant Commentators

- **John Calvin**: Preached extensively on 1 Samuel, emphasizing divine providence and the dangers of kingship
- **Matthew Henry**: Provided devotional commentary on every chapter, drawing moral and spiritual lessons
- **Dale Ralph Davis**: Offered accessible Reformed exposition in *1 Samuel: Looking on the Heart*
- **Robert Bergen**: Provided evangelical exegesis in the New American Commentary series
