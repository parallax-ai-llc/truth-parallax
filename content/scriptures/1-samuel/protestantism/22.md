## Blood at the Sanctuary

1 Samuel 22 contains one of the most horrifying episodes in the Hebrew Bible: Doeg the Edomite, on Saul's orders, massacres the priests of Nob — eighty-five men wearing the linen ephod — along with their families, livestock, and entire city. Protestant interpretation reads this as the darkest revelation of what Saul has become.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David Gathers His Band (22:1-5)

David becomes the leader of a band of outcasts — "everyone who was in distress, and everyone who was in debt, and everyone who was bitter in soul" (22:2). Protestant preachers have seen in this a picture of Christ gathering sinners and the broken, forming a kingdom from those the world rejects.

### Doeg's Report and the Massacre (22:9-19)

When Saul's own servants refuse to kill the LORD's priests, Doeg the Edomite obeys. Protestant theology identifies Doeg as the embodiment of moral corruption in the service of power. He does what no Israelite would do — and Saul rewards him for it.

### Abiathar Escapes (22:20-23)

The sole survivor of the priestly family flees to David, who accepts responsibility: "I have occasioned the death of all the persons of your father's house" (22:22). Protestant commentators value David's honest admission of guilt — a contrast with Saul, who never truly acknowledges his own failures.

## Theological Themes

- **Tyranny Unmasked**: Saul's willingness to slaughter priests reveals the full depth of his corruption
- **David's Guilt**: David's deception in chapter 21 has terrible consequences — actions have repercussions
- **The Gathering of the Outcast**: David's band of misfits as a type of the church
- **Moral Courage**: The Israelite servants who refuse Saul's command model righteous civil disobedience

## Reflection

Protestant theology confronts in this chapter the devastating logic of sin. Saul's initial disobedience has escalated into mass murder of priests. David's well-intentioned deception has contributed to innocent deaths. The chapter offers no easy resolution — only the sobering truth that sin, once unleashed, leaves destruction far beyond what its initiators intended.
