## The Sacred Bread and the Fugitive

Orthodox interpretation of 1 Samuel 21 reads the giving of the showbread to David through the christological lens provided by Jesus himself. The sacred bread — the bread of the Presence — becomes a type of the Eucharist, given to sustain God's anointed in his hour of greatest need.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David Receives the Showbread (21:1-6)

Christ's citation of this episode establishes its christological significance: the Lord of the Sabbath is greater than the Sabbath itself. Orthodox theology reads this not as an abolition of sacred law but as its fulfillment — the sacred bread finds its ultimate meaning in the Eucharist, given freely to all who hunger.

### The Bread of the Presence as Eucharistic Type

Orthodox eucharistic theology sees in the showbread a type of the Holy Gifts. Just as the bread of the Presence sustained David in the wilderness, so the Eucharist sustains the pilgrim Church on its journey through this world.

## Theological Themes

- **Eucharistic Typology**: The showbread as a prefigurement of the Holy Gifts
- **Christ's Authority**: Jesus' citation establishes the christological reading
- **Pastoral Economy**: The Orthodox principle of *oikonomia* in applying sacred law
- **Sacred Sustenance**: The bread of the Presence as divine provision for the journey

## Reflection

Orthodox theology reads this chapter through the lens of the Eucharist: the sacred bread, given to the anointed one in his time of need, anticipates the Holy Gifts that sustain the Church in every generation. David's hunger is the hunger of the soul for God — a hunger that only the true Bread of the Presence can satisfy.
