## The Covenant at the New Moon

Jewish interpretation of 1 Samuel 20 reads the farewell of David and Jonathan against the background of the Rosh Chodesh (New Moon) festival, at which David's absence from Saul's table precipitates the final crisis. The chapter models the deepest form of covenant loyalty.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Rosh Chodesh Setting (20:5, 18, 24)

The New Moon feast provides the narrative's framework. Jewish tradition notes the significance: Rosh Chodesh celebrates renewal — and at this feast of renewal, the old relationship between Saul and David comes to its irreversible end. Rashi sees in the timing a divine commentary on the transition of kingship.

### Jonathan's Covenant (20:14-17, 42)

Jonathan's covenant with David includes a request: "Show me the *hesed* of the LORD, that I may not die... and do not cut off your *hesed* from my house forever." This covenant is fulfilled in 2 Samuel 9, when David shows kindness to Jonathan's son Mephibosheth. The rabbis cite this as a model of covenant faithfulness across generations.

### The Farewell (20:41-42)

The parting of the two friends moves the Midrash to comment: "Greater was the love of Jonathan for David than the love of all the women of Israel for their husbands." The rabbis see in this friendship the purest expression of *hesed* — loyalty that survives every test.

## Theological Themes

- **Hesed Across Generations**: Covenant faithfulness extending to descendants
- **Rosh Chodesh**: The New Moon as a setting of transition and renewal
- **Covenant Loyalty**: Bonds of *hesed* that transcend personal interest and even death
- **The Farewell**: The human cost of faithfulness to God's purposes

## Reflection

Jewish tradition reads this farewell as one of the most beautiful and painful moments in the Hebrew Bible. Jonathan, who loves David more than his own life, sends his friend into exile to save him from his father's hand. The covenant they seal — "The LORD shall be between me and you forever" — becomes the foundation of the *hesed* that David will show to Jonathan's son and that God shows to Israel.
