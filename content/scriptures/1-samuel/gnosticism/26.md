## The Sleep of the Archon's Servants

Gnostic reading of 1 Samuel 26 interprets the 'deep sleep' that falls on Saul's camp as the spiritual torpor of those trapped in the material world. The archon's servants sleep while the pneumatic moves freely among them.

## Key Themes and Gnostic Interpretation

### The Deep Sleep

The tardemah (deep sleep) represents the ignorance that characterizes the hylic and psychic -- they are spiritually asleep, unaware of the pneumatic's presence. Gnostic texts frequently use sleep as a metaphor for spiritual ignorance.

### David's Repeated Restraint

The second refusal to kill Saul confirms the pneumatic's settled character: non-violence is not a momentary impulse but a fundamental orientation that comes from knowing a reality beyond the archon's world.

## Theological Themes

- **Spiritual Sleep**: The ignorance of the archon's servants
- **Pneumatic Freedom**: The awakened soul moves freely among the sleeping
- **Settled Virtue**: Non-violence as a stable disposition, not a momentary choice
- **The Archon's Vulnerability**: Even the Demiurge's champions are subject to spiritual sleep

## Reflection

Gnostic reading delights in the image of the pneumatic moving freely through the camp of the sleeping archon's soldiers. This is the condition of the gnostic in the material world: awake among the sleeping, seeing among the blind, free among the enslaved.
