## The Archon's Massacre

Gnostic reading of 1 Samuel 22 interprets the massacre at Nob as a revelation of the Demiurge's system at its most violent: the archon's representative (Saul) uses the archon's enforcer (Doeg) to destroy the archon's own priests -- the system consuming itself.

## Key Themes and Gnostic Interpretation

### The Self-Devouring System

The Demiurge's system destroys its own servants. The priests who maintained his worship are slaughtered by his anointed king. Gnostic reading sees in this the inevitable logic of the archontic order: power without gnosis eventually destroys everything, including its own institutions.

### David's Band of Outcasts

The gathering of the distressed, indebted, and discontented around David represents the formation of a pneumatic community -- those who have been expelled from or damaged by the Demiurge's system gathering around the hidden king.

## Theological Themes

- **The System Consuming Itself**: Archontic power eventually destroys its own institutions
- **The Pneumatic Community**: Outcasts gathering around the hidden king
- **Violence as Self-Revelation**: The massacre reveals the Demiurge's true character
- **The Remnant**: Abiathar's survival as the preservation of the pneumatic seed

## Reflection

Gnostic reading sees in the massacre at Nob the ultimate self-revelation of the archontic system: it is not merely unjust but self-destructive. The God who anointed Saul now watches his anointed king slaughter his own priests. The pneumatic community, gathered in the wilderness, watches from outside the collapsing structure.
