An interfaith reading of Chapter 9 of the Kephalaia seeks to build bridges of understanding around the themes of three paths. The chapter's teachings on elect, hearers offer points of contact with other traditions' reflections on similar questions.

Interfaith engagement with this chapter emphasizes shared human concerns and complementary insights, while respecting the distinctive theological context of Manichaeism. The themes addressed here — elect, hearers, sinners — resonate across religious boundaries and invite collaborative reflection on questions of ultimate significance.
