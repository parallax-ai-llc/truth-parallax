Chapter 7 of the Kephalaia, addressing formation of Adam, holds particular significance within the Manichaean tradition. The themes of anthropogony, light-soul are central to Manichaean theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Manichaean tradition, this chapter's treatment of formation of Adam is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on anthropogony, light-soul inform Manichaean self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
