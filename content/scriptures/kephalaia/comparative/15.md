A comparative reading of Chapter 15 of the Kephalaia situates its treatment of final teachings within the broader context of world religious literature. The themes of testament, legacy find parallels and contrasts in multiple traditions, offering rich material for cross-cultural analysis.

These parallels illuminate both universal patterns in human religious thought and the distinctive contributions of Manichaeism to the world's understanding of final teachings.
