## Overview

A comparative approach to the Kephalaia situates this text within the broader context of world religious literature, identifying parallels, contrasts, and mutual influences across traditions. The dialogical format invites comparison with Buddhist sutras and Platonic dialogues. This comparative perspective enriches understanding both of the Kephalaia itself and of the religious traditions with which it is brought into dialogue.

Key areas of comparative inquiry include cosmological narratives, theological concepts, ethical frameworks, ritual practices, and soteriological visions. The comparative study of the Kephalaia contributes to a deeper appreciation of both the universal themes in human religious experience and the distinctive contributions of Manichaeism to the world's spiritual heritage.
