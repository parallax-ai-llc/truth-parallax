---
title: "Varaha Purana Section 6"
scripture: "varaha-purana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The concluding section of the Varaha Purana describes the dissolution of the universe, the nature of the Supreme, and the final teaching of the Lord.

**2** Varaha said: "At the end of the Kalpa, when the time of dissolution has come, I shall withdraw all creation into myself. As a tortoise withdraws its limbs, so shall I withdraw the worlds."

**3** "The elements will dissolve in reverse order. Earth will merge into water, water into fire, fire into air, air into space. Space will merge into ego, ego into intelligence, and intelligence into the unmanifest Prakriti."

**4** "Only I shall remain — without form, without quality, without name. I am the witness of all creation and all dissolution. I am the eternal ground upon which the drama of the universe is enacted."

**5** "After the night of dissolution, I shall create again. The worlds will emerge from me, the beings will take form, the laws of dharma will be re-established. The cycle is without beginning and without end."

**6** The Purana teaches the ultimate nature of the Self: "The individual soul and the Supreme Soul are not different. The wave is not different from the ocean. The spark is not different from the fire. The ray is not different from the sun."

**7** "The apparent difference is caused by Maya, the power of illusion. When Maya is removed through knowledge and devotion, the individual soul recognizes itself as the Supreme. This recognition is liberation."

**8** "The liberated soul does not go anywhere. It does not achieve anything new. It simply recognizes what it has always been — the eternal, infinite, blissful Brahman. Liberation is not a becoming; it is a remembering."

**9** The Purana describes the marks of the liberated soul: "He is free from desire, from anger, from fear. He is equal to all beings. He sees the Lord in the ant and in the elephant. He neither seeks praise nor fears blame."

**10** "He acts in the world as an actor on a stage. He plays his role with skill and devotion, but he never forgets that he is not the character. When the play is over, he removes his costume and resumes his true identity."

**11** The Lord said to the earth: "I have taught you this Purana, which contains the essence of all sacred knowledge. Share it with the sages, and through them with all mankind. It is the lamp that dispels the darkness of ignorance."

**12** The sage concluded: "Thus ends the Varaha Purana, narrated by the Lord in his boar incarnation to the goddess Earth. He who hears this Purana with faith attains liberation. He who recites it obtains all desires. He who meditates upon it dwells in the eternal presence of the Lord. Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya."
