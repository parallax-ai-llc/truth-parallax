---
title: "Varaha Purana Section 4"
scripture: "varaha-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The Varaha Purana describes the sacred places of pilgrimage and the merit that is obtained by visiting them.

**2** The Lord said: "I dwell in all sacred places, but there are certain sites where my presence is especially manifest. He who visits these sites with devotion obtains my grace in abundance."

**3** The glory of Mathura is described: "Mathura, the birthplace of Krishna, is the most sacred city of the Vaishnavas. He who circumambulates Mathura obtains the merit of visiting all the Tirthas of the earth."

**4** The twelve forests of Vrindavan are described: Madhuvana, Talavana, Kumudavana, Bahulavana, Kamyavana, Khadiravana, Vrindavana, Bhadravana, Bhandiravana, Beluvana, Lohavana, and Mahavana. Each forest is sanctified by the childhood sports of Krishna.

**5** The glory of Dwarka is described: "Dwarka, the golden city built by Krishna in the western sea, is the gateway to Vaikuntha. He who dies in Dwarka attains liberation, for Krishna himself is the presiding deity."

**6** The Purana describes the merit of circumambulating the Govardhana hill: "Lord Krishna lifted Govardhana on his little finger to shelter the people of Vraja from Indra's wrath. This hill is non-different from Krishna himself. He who walks around it obtains the merit of millions of sacrifices."

**7** The Purana also describes the Naimisha forest, where the Puranas were first recited by Suta to the sages. "Naimisha is the hub of the wheel of the universe. In this forest, time moves differently, and the merit of worship is multiplied infinitely."

**8** The merit of river bathing is described: "A bath in the Ganga destroys all sins. A bath in the Yamuna grants the love of Krishna. A bath in the Godavari grants wisdom. A bath in the Kaveri grants devotion. Each sacred river carries a specific blessing."

**9** The Purana teaches the concept of internal pilgrimage: "The body is the sacred city. The seven chakras are the seven sacred rivers. The heart is the inner sanctum where the Lord resides. He who realizes this need not travel to any external Tirtha."

**10** The Purana warns against the commercialization of pilgrimage: "He who visits sacred sites for the purpose of trade, entertainment, or social display gains no merit. The true pilgrim approaches the sacred with humility, awe, and an open heart."

**11** The Purana describes the merit of dying in a sacred place: "He who dies in a Tirtha, with the name of the Lord on his lips, attains liberation instantly. But even he who merely thinks of a sacred place at the time of death receives great merit."

**12** The Lord concluded: "All places are sacred, for I pervade all places. But certain places are gates through which the divine energy enters the world more abundantly. The wise seek these gates and pass through them to reach me."
