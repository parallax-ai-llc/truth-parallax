---
title: "Varaha Purana Section 2"
scripture: "varaha-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Varaha Purana describes the worship of Vishnu and the forms of devotion that lead to liberation.

**2** Varaha said to the earth: "I shall now describe the means by which mortals can attain my grace. The path is simple: worship me with devotion, chant my name, remember me at all times, and offer every action to me."

**3** "He who builds a temple to me, even a small one, even of clay and straw, obtains the merit of a thousand sacrifices. He who maintains a temple and provides for its worship attains my eternal abode."

**4** "He who offers me a single leaf, a single flower, a single fruit, or a single drop of water with devotion — I accept it with joy. I do not look at the gift; I look at the heart of the giver."

**5** The Purana describes the worship of Vishnu in the form of the Shalagrama: "The Shalagrama stone is my most sacred emblem. I dwell within it by my own will. He who worships the Shalagrama daily in his home transforms his house into Vaikuntha."

**6** The rules for daily worship are prescribed: "Rise before dawn. Bathe in cold water. Wear clean garments. Sit facing east. Light a lamp. Offer flowers, incense, and food to the Lord. Chant the Vishnu Sahasranama. Meditate upon the form of the Lord."

**7** The Purana describes the efficacy of the divine names: "The name of the Lord is more powerful than the Lord himself. For the Lord is bound by the rules of dharma, but his name is free from all restrictions. The name liberates the sinner, the ignorant, and the fallen."

**8** The story of Gajendra, the elephant king, is narrated. Caught in the jaws of a crocodile in a lake, Gajendra, after exhausting all his strength, offered a lotus to Vishnu with his trunk and cried out: "O Adi Purusha, I take refuge in you!"

**9** Vishnu immediately flew on Garuda and struck the crocodile with his Sudarshana disc, freeing Gajendra. The Lord said: "He who remembers this story of Gajendra's liberation at the time of any danger shall be freed from that danger."

**10** The Purana teaches: "The grace of the Lord is not earned by merit. It is freely given to all who ask. The only requirement is sincerity. The Lord does not ask: 'Are you worthy?' He asks only: 'Do you call upon me?'"

**11** The Purana describes the merit of fasting on Ekadashi: "Of all the Vratas, the Ekadashi fast is most dear to me. He who abstains from food on Ekadashi and spends the day in worship and meditation obtains my eternal grace."

**12** The Lord concluded: "All the Vedas, all the sacrifices, all the austerities, all the pilgrimages are contained in devotion to me. The devotee who surrenders to me completely need not practice anything else. I am his protection, his salvation, and his eternal home."
