---
title: "Varaha Purana Section 5"
scripture: "varaha-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The Varaha Purana describes the nature of dharma and adharma, the laws of karma, and the paths through which the soul journeys between lives.

**2** The Lord said: "Dharma is that which sustains and supports all beings. It is the law that upholds the universe. When beings follow dharma, the world prospers. When they abandon it, the world decays."

**3** "The foundation of dharma is truth. Upon truth stands justice. Upon justice stands peace. Upon peace stands happiness. He who speaks truth, who acts justly, who lives peacefully — he is the upholder of dharma."

**4** "Adharma is the opposite of dharma. It is falsehood, injustice, violence, and greed. It brings suffering to the doer and to all who are touched by it. The fruit of adharma is hell, followed by rebirth in lower forms of life."

**5** The Purana describes the journey of the soul after death. The righteous soul, departing from the body, is met by the messengers of Vishnu, who escort it along the path of light to the heavenly realms.

**6** The sinful soul is seized by the messengers of Yama and dragged along the path of darkness to the city of Yama. There it is judged according to its deeds and assigned to the appropriate hell for punishment.

**7** The Purana describes the various hells in detail. Each hell corresponds to a specific sin: Tamisra for theft, Raurava for bearing false witness, Kumbhipaka for killing animals for pleasure, Asipatravana for destroying forests and nature.

**8** But the Purana also teaches the means of avoiding these hells: "He who repents his sins, who confesses them, who performs penance, and who takes refuge in the Lord is freed from the consequences of his evil deeds."

**9** The Purana teaches the doctrine of grace: "Even the worst sinner can be redeemed by the grace of the Lord. The grace of the Lord is not earned by merit. It is freely given to all who call upon him with sincerity."

**10** The story of the sinful Brahmin Ajamila is retold. Though he had abandoned all dharma, his final cry of "Narayana" — calling his son's name — summoned the messengers of Vishnu, who rescued him from Yama's servants.

**11** The Purana teaches: "The name of the Lord is the greatest purifier. Greater than all rivers, greater than all sacrifices, greater than all austerities is the single utterance of the Lord's name with faith and devotion."

**12** The Lord concluded: "I have described to you the paths of dharma and adharma, the consequences of good and evil deeds, and the means of liberation. He who follows this teaching shall never fall into darkness."
