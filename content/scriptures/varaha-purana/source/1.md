---
title: "Varaha Purana Section 1"
scripture: "varaha-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. Salutation to Lord Vishnu in his form as Varaha, the great boar, who lifted the earth from the cosmic waters and restored her to her rightful place.

**2** The earth, submerged in the primordial ocean by the demon Hiranyaksha, cried out to the Lord for protection. Vishnu, hearing her cry, assumed the form of a mighty boar and plunged into the waters.

**3** The boar was of immense size, dark as a rain cloud, with eyes blazing like the sun. The Vedas formed his legs, the sacrificial altar his snout, the sacred fire his tongue, and the sacred grass his body hair.

**4** Varaha dove to the bottom of the cosmic ocean, found the earth resting upon the hood of the serpent Shesha, and lifted her upon his tusks. The gods showered flowers from heaven and chanted hymns of praise.

**5** Hiranyaksha challenged Varaha to battle. The two fought for a thousand divine years. At last Varaha struck the demon with his mace and slew him, liberating the earth from her captor.

**6** The earth, placed upon the waters, addressed Varaha with devotion: "O Lord, you have rescued me from the depths of the ocean. Teach me now the dharma by which beings can attain liberation."

**7** Varaha said: "O earth, I shall teach you the Varaha Purana, which contains the knowledge of dharma, the science of liberation, and the secrets of creation and dissolution. Listen with attention."

**8** The Lord described the creation of the universe: "From my body arose Brahma, who created the worlds, the beings, and the laws of dharma. From my anger arose Rudra, who destroys. From my compassion arose Vishnu, who preserves."

**9** The Lord described the nature of the soul: "The soul is eternal, unborn, and undying. It is encased in five sheaths: the sheath of food, the sheath of breath, the sheath of mind, the sheath of knowledge, and the sheath of bliss. Behind all sheaths is the self-luminous Atman."

**10** "The soul transmigrates through eighty-four hundred thousand species of life — from plants to insects, from insects to animals, from animals to humans, from humans to gods — driven by karma. Only in the human form can liberation be sought and attained."

**11** The Lord taught the law of karma: "As a man sows, so shall he reap. Good deeds lead to good births; evil deeds lead to evil births. But beyond both good and evil is the knowledge of Brahman, which liberates the soul from all karma."

**12** The Lord concluded: "I have lifted the earth from the waters. So too do I lift the devotee from the ocean of worldly existence. He who takes refuge in me shall never sink into the darkness of ignorance."
