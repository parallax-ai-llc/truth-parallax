## Overview

The Varaha Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, named after Varaha, the boar avatar of Vishnu who rescued the Earth from the cosmic ocean. The text is presented as a dialogue between Varaha (Vishnu in boar form) and the Earth goddess (Bhudevi), making it unique in its narrative framing among the Puranas.

The Varaha Purana is generally dated to the 10th-12th centuries CE in its present form, though it incorporates older material. It is Vaishnava in orientation but includes significant material on Shaiva and Shakta worship, reflecting the syncretic tendencies of medieval Hindu religious culture. The text contains extensive treatment of vratas, tirthas, and dharma.

## Structure

The text contains approximately 218 chapters that can be organized into six thematic sections:

1. **Section 1 — The Varaha Avatar**: The boar incarnation, the rescue of the Earth, and Vishnu's cosmic deed
2. **Section 2 — Cosmology and Creation**: The structure of the universe, creation narratives, and cosmological geography
3. **Section 3 — Dharma and Duties**: Social and religious obligations, varnashrama dharma, and ethical conduct
4. **Section 4 — Vratas and Festivals**: Votive observances, fasts, and their spiritual rewards
5. **Section 5 — Pilgrimage and Sacred Sites**: Holy places, their legends, and the merit of pilgrimage
6. **Section 6 — Worship and Liberation**: Devotional practices, temple worship, and the paths to moksha

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Varaha Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: A key text for Varaha theology and the relationship between Vishnu and the Earth goddess
- **Shaivism**: Noted for its inclusion of Shaiva material within a broadly Vaishnava framework
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for passages on the nature of the divine beyond sectarian distinctions
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Valued for its Vaishnava orientation and its demonstration of Vishnu's protective cosmic role
- **Academic**: Studied for its syncretic character and its treatment of the Varaha myth cycle

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Varaha Avatar | Vishnu as the cosmic boar rescuing the Earth from the primordial waters |
| Earth as Sacred | The personification and sanctity of the Earth as Bhudevi |
| Syncretic Worship | Integration of Vaishnava, Shaiva, and Shakta elements |
| Pilgrimage | The merit and practice of visiting sacred sites |
| Dharma | Ethical and social duties in the context of cosmic order |
