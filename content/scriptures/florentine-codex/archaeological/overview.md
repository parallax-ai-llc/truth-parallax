## Overview

Archaeological engagement with the Florentine Codex approaches the text through the lens of archaeological and material evidence that contextualizes, corroborates, or illuminates the text. This interpretive tradition emphasizes material culture, site interpretation, iconographic analysis, and the relationship between text and artifact.

Archaeological and material analysis of the Florentine Codex examines the relationship between the text's narratives and the physical evidence recovered through excavation, survey, and scientific analysis. This approach seeks to corroborate, contextualize, and enrich understanding of the text by connecting its descriptions to actual sites, artifacts, architectural remains, and iconographic programs.

Archaeological engagement with the Florentine Codex also addresses questions of dating, geographic distribution, cultural interaction, and the material conditions under which the text and its associated traditions were produced, maintained, and transformed over time.
