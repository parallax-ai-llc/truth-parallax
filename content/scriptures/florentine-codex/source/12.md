---
title: "Book XII - The Conquest"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 12
---

**1** This is the account of the coming of the Spaniards, as told by the elders of Mexico who witnessed it.

**2** Motecuhzoma heard reports of strange beings on the coast: men with white skin and beards, riding great animals that looked like deer, wearing garments of metal, carrying weapons that thundered and flashed.

**3** He sent gifts to them: the treasures of Quetzalcoatl, gold and turquoise and feathers. He thought perhaps the god Quetzalcoatl had returned from the east, as the prophecies foretold.

**4** But the strangers were not gods. They melted down the gold and demanded more. They made alliances with the peoples who hated the Mexica: the Tlaxcalans, who had long been enemies.

**5** The strangers entered Tenochtitlan and were received as guests. But they seized Motecuhzoma and held him captive. Then they attacked the people during the festival of Toxcatl, killing the dancers and the musicians.

**6** The people rose in fury and drove the strangers from the city on the night called the Noche Triste, the Night of Sorrow. But the strangers returned with their allies, and after a siege of many months, Tenochtitlan fell.

**7** Cuauhtemoc, the last tlatoani, was captured. The temples were destroyed and the idols cast down. The old ways were forbidden. And the people wept, for the Fifth Sun had set, and a new and terrible age had begun.
