title: "Book I - The Gods"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 1
---

**1** This is the account of the gods worshipped by the people of New Spain, according to the ancient traditions passed down from the elders.

**2** The first and greatest was Huitzilopochtli, the god of war, the sun god. He was the patron of the Mexica, who guided them on their long migration to the Valley of Mexico.

**3** Huitzilopochtli was born on Coatepec, the Serpent Mountain. His mother, Coatlicue, was sweeping the temple when a ball of feathers fell from the sky and she became pregnant.

**4** Her other children, the Centzon Huitznahua, four hundred brothers, and Coyolxauhqui their sister, were enraged. They marched upon Coatepec to kill their mother. But Huitzilopochtli burst fully armed from his mother's womb.

**5** He wielded the Xiuhcoatl, the fire serpent, and struck down Coyolxauhqui. Her body fell to the base of the mountain, broken into pieces. Then he scattered the four hundred brothers across the sky, where they became the stars.

**6** This is why the sun must fight each day to rise, defeating the moon and the stars. And this is why the Mexica offered sacrifice to Huitzilopochtli, to give him strength for the daily battle.
