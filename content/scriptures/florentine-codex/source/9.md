---
title: "Book IX - The Merchants"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The pochteca, the merchants, were honored among the people of Mexico. They traveled to distant lands across mountains and through jungles, carrying their goods on their backs.

**2** Before setting out on a journey, the merchants consulted the tonalpouhque to find an auspicious day. They made offerings to Yacatecuhtli, the god of merchants, the lord of the vanguard.

**3** They carried cacao, gold, precious feathers, fine cloths, and jade to trade in distant markets. They brought back rare goods: quetzal feathers from the south, turquoise from the north, amber from the coast.

**4** The merchants traveled in companies for protection, for the roads were dangerous. Bandits and hostile peoples threatened them at every turn.

**5** When a merchant returned from a successful journey, he gave a great feast. But he did so quietly, without boasting, for the merchants knew that envy could destroy them. They hid their wealth and lived modestly.

**6** The old merchants counseled the young: Be humble on the road and humble at home. Do not flaunt your wealth. The tall tree catches the wind, and the rich man catches the thief.
