---
title: "Book VII - The Sun, Moon, and Stars"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 7
---

**1** At Teotihuacan the gods gathered in the darkness, for the Fourth Sun had been destroyed and there was no light.

**2** They said: Who will carry the burden of being the sun? Who will cast himself into the fire so that the world may have light?

**3** Tecuciztecatl, the rich and proud one, volunteered. But when he approached the fire, he drew back in fear. Four times he tried, and four times he retreated.

**4** Then Nanahuatzin, the poor and humble one, covered with sores and boils, stepped forward. Without hesitation, he threw himself into the flames. The fire consumed him, and from the flames he rose as the sun.

**5** Ashamed, Tecuciztecatl then threw himself into the embers. He rose as the moon, shining as brightly as the sun. But one of the gods threw a rabbit at the moon's face, dimming its light. This is why the moon bears the mark of a rabbit.

**6** But the sun did not move. It hung on the horizon, burning. The sun demanded sacrifice. The gods sacrificed themselves, their hearts cut out to feed the sun. And from that time, the sun required blood to continue its daily journey across the sky.
