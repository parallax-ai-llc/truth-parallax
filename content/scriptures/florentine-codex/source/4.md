---
title: "Book IV - The Calendar and Divination"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The tonalpohualli, the count of days, consists of two hundred and sixty days. It is formed by the combination of thirteen numbers with twenty day-signs.

**2** Each day has its character. Some days are fortunate, some unfortunate. The day on which a child is born determines its destiny and its nature.

**3** Ce Cipactli, One Crocodile, is a fortunate day. Those born on this day will prosper. Ce Miquiztli, One Death, is an unfortunate day. Those born on it must make offerings to avoid their fate.

**4** The tonalpouhque, the readers of the days, were consulted at every important occasion: at births, at marriages, at the beginning of journeys, at the planting of crops.

**5** They said: The day on which you are born is the day that governs your life. But through offerings and penance, an unfortunate destiny can be mitigated. The gods are not inflexible.

**6** The xiuhpohualli, the count of years, consists of three hundred and sixty-five days, divided into eighteen months of twenty days, plus five unlucky days called nemontemi.
