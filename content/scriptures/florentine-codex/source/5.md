---
title: "Book V - Omens and Superstitions"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The people of Mexico observed many omens and signs. An owl hooting near a house foretold death. A rabbit entering the house foretold misfortune.

**2** If a person heard a weasel cross their path at night, they believed they would soon face obstacles. If a skunk sprayed near a house, it was a sign of discord in the family.

**3** There were ten great omens that foretold the coming of the Spaniards, years before they arrived.

**4** The first omen was a pillar of fire that appeared in the night sky for a year. The second was the temple of Huitzilopochtli catching fire spontaneously. The third was the temple of Xiuhtecuhtli being struck by lightning without thunder.

**5** The fourth omen was a comet visible in the daytime. The fifth was the boiling of the lake, which rose and flooded houses. The sixth was a weeping woman heard at night, crying out for her children.

**6** The seventh was a strange bird with a mirror on its head, in which Motecuhzoma saw warriors riding deer. The eighth was monstrous beings that appeared and vanished. These omens terrified the people and their king.
