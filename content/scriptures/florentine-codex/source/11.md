---
title: "Book XI - Earthly Things"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 11
---

**1** These are the animals, plants, and minerals of this land, as the elders knew them and described them.

**2** The quetzal bird is the most precious of all birds. Its feathers are long and green, shining like emeralds. It dwells in the cloud forests and cannot live in captivity. Those who captured it plucked its feathers and released it, for to kill it was forbidden.

**3** The jaguar, ocelotl, is the lord of the animals. It is brave and cunning, a hunter by night. The warriors who captured the most prisoners in battle were granted the right to wear jaguar skins and were called the ocelotl warriors.

**4** The cacao tree bears its fruit in pods. The beans are dried and roasted and ground into a paste. Mixed with water and spices, they make the drink called chocolate, which was reserved for the nobles and the warriors.

**5** The maguey plant provides everything: from its sap comes pulque, the fermented drink; from its fibers come thread and cloth; from its thorns come needles; from its leaves come roofing and paper.

**6** The earth provides gold and silver, jade and turquoise, obsidian and flint. From obsidian the craftsmen make knives sharper than any metal, and mirrors that reflect the face of the one who gazes into them.
