---
title: "Book VIII - Kings and Lords"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 8
---

**1** These are the customs and governance of the kings and lords of Mexico. The tlatoani, the Great Speaker, was chosen from the royal lineage by a council of elders and warriors.

**2** The tlatoani was the supreme ruler, the judge, the war commander, the representative of the gods on earth. He lived in a great palace with many rooms and gardens and pools.

**3** He ate alone, behind a screen, and his food was prepared by many cooks. His dishes were of fine pottery, painted with designs. He drank chocolate prepared with vanilla and honey.

**4** The laws of Mexico were strict. Theft was punished by slavery or death. Drunkenness in public was punished severely, except for the elderly, who were permitted to drink. Adultery was punished by death.

**5** The marketplace, the tianquiztli, was the center of economic life. Judges sat in the marketplace to settle disputes. Fixed prices were maintained, and dishonest merchants were punished.

**6** The pochteca, the long-distance merchants, were a powerful class. They traveled to distant lands, trading luxury goods. They also served as spies for the tlatoani, reporting on the strength and intentions of foreign peoples.
