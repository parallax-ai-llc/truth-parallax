---
title: "Book VI - Rhetoric and Moral Philosophy"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 6
---

**1** These are the huehuetlatolli, the words of the elders, the speeches and counsels passed down from the ancient times.

**2** The father speaks to his son: O my son, my precious one, you have come into the world. The Lord of the Near and the Nigh has given you life. You have been sent into a place of hardship, a place of weariness.

**3** Listen, my son: the earth is not a place of contentment, not a place of joy. It is said that the earth is a place of weeping, a place of sorrow, a place where one becomes acquainted with suffering.

**4** A wind blows, sharp as obsidian. It is said that the wind is hot and cold at the same time. It is the place of hunger and thirst, of weariness and sleepiness.

**5** Therefore be humble. Walk quietly. Do not raise your face arrogantly. Do not go about looking from side to side. On the road, do not look to every face.

**6** Speak slowly. Do not speak hastily. Do not disturb others. When given something, receive it gratefully. If it is good, do not boast of it. If it is meager, do not despise it. Be moderate in all things.
