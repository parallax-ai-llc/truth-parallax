---
title: "Book II - The Ceremonies"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 2
---

**1** These are the feasts and ceremonies observed throughout the year by the people of Mexico, arranged according to the eighteen months of twenty days each.

**2** In the month of Atlcahualo, the first month, ceremonies were held to bring rain. Children were sacrificed to the rain gods, the Tlaloque, upon the mountaintops.

**3** The people said: If the children weep, their tears will bring the rain. The more they weep, the more it will rain. Therefore they were made to weep before the sacrifice.

**4** In the month of Toxcatl, the feast of Tezcatlipoca was held. For one year, a young man lived as the image of Tezcatlipoca. He was given four beautiful wives and lived in luxury.

**5** At the end of the year, he climbed the steps of the temple, playing his flute. At each step he broke one of his flutes. At the top, he was sacrificed. His heart was offered to the sun.

**6** In the month of Panquetzaliztli, the great feast of Huitzilopochtli was held. A procession circled the great temple, and captives were sacrificed at the summit.
