---
title: "Book III - The Origin of the Gods"
scripture: "florentine-codex"
chapter: 3
---

**1** In the beginning, there was only Ometeotl, the dual god, who was both male and female, the Lord and Lady of Duality. They dwelt in the thirteenth heaven.

**2** From Ometeotl came four sons: Tezcatlipoca the Black, Tezcatlipoca the Red (Xipe Totec), Quetzalcoatl the White, and Huitzilopochtli the Blue.

**3** These four gods created the world, but the world had to be created five times, for each creation ended in destruction. These are the Five Suns.

**4** The First Sun was the Sun of Earth, presided over by Tezcatlipoca. It ended when jaguars devoured the people. The Second Sun was the Sun of Wind, presided over by Quetzalcoatl. It ended when a great wind destroyed everything.

**5** The Third Sun was the Sun of Rain, presided over by Tlaloc. It ended in a rain of fire. The Fourth Sun was the Sun of Water, presided over by Chalchiuhtlicue. It ended in a great flood.

**6** The Fifth Sun is our present sun. It was created at Teotihuacan, where the gods gathered and one of them threw himself into a great fire, becoming the sun. This is the Sun of Movement, and it will end in earthquakes.
