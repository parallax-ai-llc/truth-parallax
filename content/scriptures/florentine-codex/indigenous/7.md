Book 7 of the Florentine Codex — The Sun, Moon, and Stars — holds deep significance for contemporary indigenous communities connected to this tradition. Indigenous engagement with this book emphasizes its continuing relevance as living spiritual knowledge, its role in sustaining cultural identity and ceremonial practice, and its authority as a sacred text that communicates essential truths about the relationship between humans, the natural world, and the divine.

Indigenous readers and practitioners approach this book not as an artifact of the past but as a continuing source of guidance, identity, and spiritual power. The narrative patterns and theological principles it articulates remain relevant to contemporary understanding and practice. Indigenous readings resist the reduction of the Florentine Codex to mere historical curiosity, insisting on its living authority and its capacity to speak to present conditions and future aspirations.
