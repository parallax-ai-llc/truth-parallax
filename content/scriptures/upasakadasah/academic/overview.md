## Overview

Academic scholars value the Upasakadasah as a primary source for understanding lay Jain practice and its relationship to monastic life. The text provides evidence for the social and economic world of ancient Jain householders and the institutional structures that supported lay practice.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Social History

The text's descriptions of the ten laypersons -- their occupations, families, social positions, and daily lives -- provide valuable evidence for the social history of ancient India, particularly the merchant and artisan communities that historically supported Jainism.

### Lay-Monastic Relations

Scholars examine the text for evidence of how lay and monastic communities interacted, including patterns of patronage, instruction, and mutual obligation.

### Comparative Lay Practice

The Upasakadasah's system of lay vows is compared with Buddhist upasaka practices, Hindu grihasthadharma, and lay religious commitments in other traditions.
