## Overview

The Digambara tradition reads the Upasakadasah with particular attention to the relationship between lay practice and monastic life. While affirming the value of the householder path, Digambara interpreters emphasize that ultimate liberation requires progressing beyond lay life to full monastic renunciation.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Lay Practice as Preparation

Digambara scholars view the twelve vows of the householder as preparatory stages on the path to full renunciation. The exemplary laypersons in the text are admired for their devotion, but their stories point toward the necessity of eventual monastic commitment.

### Strict Vow Interpretation

The Digambara tradition interprets the lay vows with characteristic strictness, emphasizing the need for sincere and rigorous practice even within the limitations of householder life.

### Progressive Renunciation

The text is read as illustrating a progressive path from limited lay vows toward the complete renunciation of monastic life, ultimately culminating in the sky-clad asceticism that Digambaras regard as the highest expression of spiritual commitment.
