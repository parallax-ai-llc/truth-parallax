---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 3: The Story of Chandanbala"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 3
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling near the city of Vaishali. In that city there lived a devout layman named Chandanbala, a merchant of great repute.

**2** Chandanbala had heard the teachings of the Jina from his youth. His parents had been followers of the path, and he had been raised in an atmosphere of reverence for all living beings.

**3** Yet Chandanbala faced a great trial. His business required him to travel widely, and in his travels he encountered many situations that tested his vows.

**4** On one journey, Chandanbala's caravan was attacked by robbers. His companions urged him to fight, but Chandanbala remembered his vow of non-violence.

**5** He said to his companions: "Let them take our goods. Our goods can be replaced, but a life once taken cannot be restored. I will not break my vow, even to save my wealth."

**6** The robbers, astonished by his composure, asked: "What manner of man are you, who yields his wealth without resistance?" Chandanbala replied: "I am a follower of the Jina. I have vowed not to cause harm to any living being. My wealth is less precious to me than my vow."

**7** The chief of the robbers was moved by these words. He said: "I have robbed many men, but never have I met one who values his word above his wealth. Tell me more of this path you follow."

**8** Chandanbala spoke to the robbers of the teachings of Mahavira: of the sanctity of all life, of the law of karma, and of the path to liberation through non-violence, truth, and self-restraint.

**9** Several of the robbers, moved by his words, laid down their weapons and renounced their former way of life. They became followers of the Jina and took upon themselves the vows of the layman.

**10** When Chandanbala returned to Vaishali and told the venerable Mahavira what had happened, the Blessed One said: "O Chandanbala, by your steadfastness in the vow of non-violence, you have accomplished more than any warrior with his sword."

**11** "For the warrior may conquer a city, but he who conquers himself conquers the world. And he who, by his example, leads others to the path of righteousness—he is the greatest of all conquerors."

**12** Chandanbala continued to practise the twelve vows of the layman throughout his life, and by his example he led many others to the path of the Jina.
