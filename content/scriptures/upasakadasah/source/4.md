---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 4: The Story of Suradeva"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 4
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling in the city of Rajagriha, in the garden of the merchant Dhanya. In that city there lived a householder named Suradeva, who was a worker in precious metals.

**2** Suradeva was skilled in his craft, and his ornaments were prized by kings and nobles. Yet despite his success, he felt an emptiness within him that no amount of gold could fill.

**3** Hearing that the venerable Mahavira was teaching nearby, Suradeva went to listen. The Blessed One was speaking about the nature of true wealth.

**4** The Blessed One said: "There are two kinds of wealth: external wealth, which consists of gold, silver, land, and possessions; and internal wealth, which consists of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct."

**5** "External wealth is unstable—it can be stolen by thieves, consumed by fire, seized by kings, or lost through misfortune. Internal wealth is stable—no thief can steal it, no fire can burn it, no king can seize it."

**6** "He who possesses only external wealth and lacks internal wealth is poor indeed, though he may own all the gold in the world. He who possesses internal wealth is truly rich, even if he owns nothing."

**7** Suradeva was profoundly moved. He approached the Blessed One and said: "Lord, I have spent my life fashioning ornaments of gold and silver for the bodies of others. Now I wish to fashion the ornament of virtue for my own soul."

**8** The Blessed One said: "O Suradeva, the ornament of virtue surpasses all others. It never tarnishes, it never breaks, and it adorns the soul for all eternity. Begin by practising the vow of truthfulness."

**9** "A craftsman deals daily with questions of value and worth. Let your speech be as pure as the finest gold. Do not misrepresent the quality of your work. Do not deceive your customers. Let your word be your bond."

**10** "Extend this truthfulness beyond your trade. Speak the truth in all dealings. Avoid harsh speech that wounds others. Avoid idle gossip that serves no purpose. Let every word that leaves your lips be measured and beneficial."

**11** Suradeva accepted the vow of truthfulness along with the other vows of the layman. From that day forward, his reputation for honesty became even greater than his reputation for craftsmanship.

**12** The Blessed One said: "The layman who practises truthfulness builds a foundation upon which all the other virtues can stand. Truthfulness is the root; the other vows are the branches and the flowers."
