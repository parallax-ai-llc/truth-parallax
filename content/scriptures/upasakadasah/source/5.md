---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 5: The Story of Chulani Pita"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 5
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling near the city of Mithila. In that city there lived a householder named Chulani Pita, who was a farmer of modest means.

**2** Chulani Pita tilled the earth and grew grain to feed his family. He was a simple man, not learned in the scriptures, but honest and hardworking.

**3** When Chulani Pita heard the teaching of the venerable Mahavira, a doubt arose in his mind. He approached the Blessed One and said: "Lord, I am a farmer. I plough the earth, and in ploughing I harm the creatures that dwell within it. How can I practise non-violence?"

**4** The Blessed One said: "O Chulani Pita, it is true that the farmer's work involves harm to living beings. The earth is teeming with one-sensed creatures—earth-bodied souls that dwell within the soil."

**5** "Yet the layman is not expected to observe non-violence in the same degree as the ascetic. The layman's vow is one of limited non-violence. He refrains from intentional harm to mobile beings—beings with two or more senses."

**6** "In your work, be mindful. Plough only when necessary. Do not plough the earth needlessly. Do not use more land than you need. Do not burn the fields unnecessarily. In these ways, even a farmer can practise the spirit of non-violence."

**7** "Moreover, share the fruits of your labour. Feed the hungry. Support the ascetics. Give alms to the poor. The merit gained from these acts of generosity will outweigh the unavoidable harm done in your occupation."

**8** Chulani Pita said: "Lord, your words bring me comfort. I was afraid that the path of the Jina was closed to a man like me. Now I see that even a humble farmer can walk this path."

**9** The Blessed One said: "The path is open to all—to the king and to the farmer, to the merchant and to the servant. What matters is not the station of one's birth but the sincerity of one's practice."

**10** "He who observes even one vow with perfect sincerity earns greater merit than he who observes all the vows with a distracted mind. Begin where you are, with what you can do, and gradually increase your practice."

**11** Chulani Pita took the vows of the layman according to his capacity. He continued to farm, but with greater mindfulness. On the holy days he fasted and sat in meditation. He gave generously to ascetics and to the poor.

**12** The venerable Mahavira said: "O Chulani Pita, the seed of dharma has been planted in the field of your heart. Water it with practice, guard it with vigilance, and in time it will bear the fruit of liberation."
