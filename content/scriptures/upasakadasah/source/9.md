---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 9: The Story of Nandini Pita"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 9
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling in the city of Kaushambi. In that city there lived a householder named Nandini Pita, who was a keeper of cattle.

**2** Nandini Pita owned a great herd of cattle, and he took pride in the health and beauty of his animals. He cared for them with devotion, knowing each by name, and his dairy prospered.

**3** When Nandini Pita heard the teachings of the venerable Mahavira regarding the sanctity of all life, he was troubled. He asked: "Lord, I profit from the labour of my cattle. I take their milk, which is meant for their calves. Is this a form of stealing?"

**4** The Blessed One said: "O Nandini Pita, the householder who tends cattle with care, who does not overwork them, who feeds them well, and who does not send them to slaughter—he acts within the bounds of the lesser vow."

**5** "Yet the wise householder should reflect: all beings desire happiness and fear suffering. The cow loves her calf as the human mother loves her child. Take only what is needed, and leave enough for the calf."

**6** "Do not keep cattle merely for profit. Do not sell them to those who will slaughter them. Do not brand them with hot irons when gentler means of marking will serve. In all your dealings with them, let compassion be your guide."

**7** Nandini Pita said: "Lord, some of my neighbours raise cattle only to sell them for slaughter. They say it is their livelihood and that the animals were born to serve man. What should I say to them?"

**8** The Blessed One said: "No being is born to serve as food for another. Each soul, whether in a human body or an animal body, seeks its own happiness and fears its own death. He who kills for profit sows the seeds of his own future suffering."

**9** "You cannot force others to follow this path. But you can set an example. Show your neighbours that it is possible to prosper without cruelty. Let your well-cared-for cattle and your prosperous dairy speak for themselves."

**10** "And when the opportunity arises, speak gently of the law of karma. Tell them that the suffering we inflict on others returns to us, and the kindness we show to others returns to us also."

**11** Nandini Pita accepted the vows of the layman with special attention to the principle of compassion toward animals. He resolved never to sell his cattle for slaughter and to care for them even in old age when they could no longer produce milk.

**12** The Blessed One said: "O Nandini Pita, he who shows mercy to those who are in his power—to servants, to children, and to animals—he practises the highest form of non-violence. For it is easy to be gentle to one's equals; the true test of virtue is how one treats those who are weaker."
