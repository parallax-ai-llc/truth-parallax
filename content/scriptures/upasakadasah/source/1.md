---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 1: The Story of Ananda"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard: At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling in the Pushpottara Hall in the city of Champa, in the garden called Purnabhadra.

**2** At that time and at that period there lived in the city of Champa a householder named Ananda, who was rich, prosperous, and of high birth. He was a merchant by profession and was known throughout the land for his honesty and generosity.

**3** One day Ananda heard that the venerable Mahavira, the Jina, the omniscient one, was staying nearby. His heart was moved with devotion, and he resolved to visit the great teacher.

**4** Having adorned himself with clean garments, Ananda went to the place where the venerable Mahavira was seated. He bowed down before him, circumambulated him three times, and sat down at a respectful distance.

**5** The venerable Mahavira delivered a discourse on dharma, speaking of the nature of the soul, the bondage of karma, the path of liberation, and the duty of the householder.

**6** Having heard this discourse, Ananda was deeply moved. He said: "Lord, I wish to take upon myself the vows of a layman. I wish to follow the path of righteousness while remaining in the world."

**7** The venerable Mahavira said: "O Ananda, well done! A householder who follows the lesser vows with sincerity and diligence earns great merit. Listen carefully to the duties of a layman."

**8** "A layman should observe five lesser vows: the vow of limited non-violence, in which he refrains from intentional harm to mobile beings; the vow of limited truthfulness, in which he avoids gross lies; the vow of limited non-stealing, in which he takes nothing not given; the vow of limited celibacy, in the form of fidelity to one's spouse; and the vow of limited non-attachment, in which he limits his possessions."

**9** "In addition to these five vows, a layman should observe three supplementary vows: limiting the area of his activities to reduce unintentional harm; limiting the number and kind of things he uses; and avoiding purposeless harmful activities."

**10** "He should also observe four instructional vows: practising equanimity through regular meditation; observing special periods of austerity; living as an ascetic on certain days; and contributing to the support of ascetics."

**11** Ananda accepted these twelve vows with a joyful heart. He said: "Lord, I shall observe these vows as long as I live. I shall strive to be a worthy layman, following the path of righteousness."

**12** The venerable Mahavira blessed Ananda, saying: "O Ananda, by faithfully observing these vows, you shall accumulate great merit. You shall be reborn in a celestial realm and shall eventually attain liberation."
