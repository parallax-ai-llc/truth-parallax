---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 8: The Story of Mahashatabala"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 8
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling in the city of Varanasi, in the garden of a wealthy patron. In that city there lived a householder named Mahashatabala, who was a commander in the king's army.

**2** Mahashatabala was a warrior by birth and by training. He had fought in many battles and was honoured for his courage and skill. Yet as he grew older, the memory of the men he had slain weighed upon his conscience.

**3** He came to the venerable Mahavira and said: "Lord, I am a soldier. My duty is to protect the kingdom by force of arms. But your teaching says that all violence is sinful. How am I to reconcile my duty with my conscience?"

**4** The Blessed One said: "O Mahashatabala, the warrior who fights to protect the innocent and to uphold justice creates different karma from the warrior who fights for conquest and plunder. Yet all violence produces karmic consequences."

**5** "The householder who cannot renounce his duties in the world should perform them with the minimum possible violence and with a mind free from hatred. Fight if you must, but without anger. Protect if you must, but without cruelty."

**6** "Yet know this: the greatest battle is not fought on the field with swords and arrows. The greatest battle is fought within the heart, against one's own passions. He who conquers his anger, his pride, his greed, and his delusion—he is the true conqueror."

**7** Mahashatabala said: "Lord, I have conquered many enemies on the battlefield, but I have never conquered the enemies within. My anger still controls me. My pride still blinds me. Teach me how to fight this inner battle."

**8** The Blessed One said: "The weapons of the inner battle are different from the weapons of the outer battle. The weapon against anger is forgiveness. The weapon against pride is humility. The weapon against greed is contentment. The weapon against delusion is knowledge."

**9** "Practise these weapons daily. When anger arises, remember the suffering it has caused you and others. When pride arises, remember that you too are mortal and that your achievements are the result of karma, not of your own power alone."

**10** "When greed arises, reflect on how much you truly need. When delusion arises, study the teachings of the Jina and seek the company of the wise."

**11** Mahashatabala accepted the vows of the layman. He resolved to retire from active military service and to devote the remainder of his life to the practice of self-conquest.

**12** The Blessed One said: "O Mahashatabala, you have fought bravely on the field of battle. Now fight bravely on the field of the soul. The victory that awaits you there is far greater than any worldly triumph, for it is the victory that leads to eternal liberation."
