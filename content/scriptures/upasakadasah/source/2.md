---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 2: The Story of Kamadeva"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 2
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling in the city of Champa, in the garden called Purnabhadra. In that city there lived a householder named Kamadeva.

**2** Kamadeva was a man of great wealth and worldly pleasures. He delighted in fine food, beautiful garments, music, and dance. His house was filled with every luxury that the world could offer.

**3** One day, hearing of the venerable Mahavira's presence, Kamadeva went to hear his discourse. The Blessed One spoke of the impermanence of worldly pleasures and the bondage they create.

**4** The Blessed One said: "Pleasures of the senses are like honey on the edge of a sword. The taste is sweet, but the cut is inevitable. He who pursues sensory pleasures without restraint binds himself ever more tightly in the chains of karma."

**5** "The body is the vehicle of the soul, not its master. He who serves the body and neglects the soul is like a man who polishes the cage while the bird within starves."

**6** Kamadeva was deeply stirred by these words. He reflected: "I have spent my life in the pursuit of pleasures that fade. I have adorned this body that will one day be dust. What have I done for the soul that is eternal?"

**7** He approached the venerable Mahavira and said: "Lord, I have been a slave to my senses. Now I wish to become a master of myself. Teach me the way."

**8** The Blessed One said: "O Kamadeva, the way begins with restraint. You need not renounce the world entirely, but you must learn to master your desires. Begin with the vow of limited non-attachment. Fix a limit to your possessions and give away the surplus."

**9** "Then practise fasting on the holy days. Sit in meditation, reflecting on the nature of the soul. Gradually, the grip of the senses will weaken, and the light of the soul will shine forth."

**10** Kamadeva said: "Lord, I accept. From this day forward I shall limit my possessions. I shall fast on the eighth and fourteenth days of each fortnight. I shall sit in meditation each day, reflecting on the teachings of the Jina."

**11** The venerable Mahavira said: "Well done, O Kamadeva. He who restrains himself while living in the world is like a lotus in muddy water—rooted in the mud, yet its blossom is unstained."

**12** Kamadeva faithfully observed his vows. In time, his attachment to worldly pleasures diminished. He became known for his generosity, his equanimity, and his devotion to the path of the Jina.
