---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 10: The Story of Salihipita"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 10
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling in the city of Rajagriha, in the garden called Gunashilaka. In that city there lived a householder named Salihipita, who was an elder of the merchant community.

**2** Salihipita had lived a long life and had seen much of the world. He had known prosperity and adversity, joy and sorrow. Now, in his old age, he sought the deeper meaning of life.

**3** He came to the venerable Mahavira and said: "Lord, I have lived many years. I have earned wealth, raised children, and served my community. But as death approaches, I wonder: what endures? What will I take with me when I leave this body?"

**4** The Blessed One said: "O Salihipita, of all that you have accumulated in this life—your wealth, your possessions, your titles, your reputation—none of these will follow you beyond death. Only your karma follows you."

**5** "The good deeds you have done, the vows you have observed, the compassion you have shown—these create merit that accompanies the soul into the next life. The harm you have caused, the lies you have told, the greed you have indulged—these create demerit that also accompanies the soul."

**6** "Therefore, the wise man, knowing that death may come at any time, does not postpone the practice of dharma. He does not say: 'I will begin tomorrow.' He begins today, this very moment."

**7** Salihipita said: "Lord, I have not been a wicked man, but neither have I been a particularly virtuous one. I have lived an ordinary life, concerned mainly with my business and my family. Is there still time for me to accumulate merit?"

**8** The Blessed One said: "It is never too late. Even a single moment of sincere devotion to the truth can transform a lifetime of mediocrity. The thief who, at the moment of death, sincerely repents and takes refuge in the dharma, earns merit that alters his destiny."

**9** "For you, Salihipita, I recommend the practice of Samayika—equanimity meditation. Sit in a quiet place, withdraw your senses from external objects, and reflect on the equality of all beings."

**10** "Reflect: every being fears death, just as I do. Every being desires happiness, just as I do. In this fundamental respect, the king and the beggar, the human and the animal, are equal. Let this reflection fill your heart with compassion for all beings."

**11** "Further, practise the giving of four kinds: food to the hungry, medicine to the sick, knowledge to the ignorant, and fearlessness to those who are afraid. These four kinds of giving destroy vast quantities of negative karma."

**12** Salihipita accepted the vows of the layman and spent his remaining years in the practice of meditation and generosity. The Blessed One said: "O Salihipita, the evening of life can be its most beautiful part, for it is then that the soul, freed from the distractions of youth and the ambitions of middle age, can turn wholly toward the light. Make this evening radiant with dharma, and the night that follows shall give way to the dawn of liberation."
