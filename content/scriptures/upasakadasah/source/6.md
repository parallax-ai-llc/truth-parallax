---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 6: The Story of Kundakoliya"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 6
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling near the city of Shravasti. In that city there lived a householder named Kundakoliya, who was known for his fierce temper and quarrelsome nature.

**2** Kundakoliya was wealthy and powerful, and few dared to cross him. He was quick to anger and slow to forgive. His servants feared him, his neighbours avoided him, and his family lived in anxiety.

**3** One day, a dispute arose between Kundakoliya and a neighbouring merchant. In his rage, Kundakoliya struck the man and was brought before the town council. The council ordered him to pay compensation and warned him to restrain his temper.

**4** Humiliated and troubled, Kundakoliya wandered into the garden where the venerable Mahavira was teaching. He sat at the back of the assembly, not intending to stay, but the words of the Blessed One arrested him.

**5** The Blessed One was speaking about anger: "Anger is a fire that burns the one who harbours it more than the one against whom it is directed. The angry man destroys his own peace, his own health, his own relationships."

**6** "Anger arises from the delusion that others are responsible for our suffering. But the wise one knows that all suffering arises from one's own karma. No one can cause you suffering unless your karma permits it."

**7** "Therefore, when someone harms you, reflect: 'This is the fruit of my own past actions. By enduring it with equanimity, I exhaust my karma. By retaliating with anger, I create new karma and prolong my suffering.'"

**8** "The antidote to anger is forgiveness. Forgiveness is not weakness; it is the strength of the soul. The weak cannot forgive; forgiveness is the attribute of the strong."

**9** These words pierced Kundakoliya's heart. He saw himself clearly for the first time—a man enslaved by his own anger, creating suffering for himself and for all around him.

**10** He approached the venerable Mahavira and fell at his feet: "Lord, I have been a slave to anger all my life. I have harmed many and brought misery to those I love. Show me how to free myself from this bondage."

**11** The Blessed One said: "O Kundakoliya, practise forbearance. When anger arises, do not act upon it. Pause. Breathe. Reflect. Remember that the being before you is also a soul, suffering under the weight of its own karma, just as you are."

**12** Kundakoliya took the vows of the layman and devoted himself especially to the practice of forbearance. In time, his fierce nature softened. Those who had feared him came to respect him. His household knew peace. And Kundakoliya himself found a happiness he had never known.
