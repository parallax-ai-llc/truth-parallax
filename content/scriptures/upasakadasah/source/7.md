---
title: "Upasakadasah Section 7: The Story of Saddala Putta"
scripture: "upasakadasah"
chapter: 7
---

**1** At that time the venerable Mahavira was dwelling near the city of Polasapura. In that city there lived a householder named Saddala Putta, who was a physician.

**2** Saddala Putta was skilled in the healing arts. He knew the properties of herbs and minerals, and he treated the ailments of both rich and poor. But he was troubled by a question that his medical knowledge could not resolve.

**3** He went to the venerable Mahavira and asked: "Lord, I heal the bodies of men, but their bodies grow sick again. I cure one disease, and another takes its place. Is there a cure for all suffering, a medicine for the soul?"

**4** The Blessed One said: "O Saddala Putta, the diseases of the body are caused by the imbalance of the bodily elements. But the disease of the soul is caused by karma. And as long as the soul is bound by karma, suffering will arise again and again."

**5** "The medicine for the soul is the threefold path: right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct. Right faith is the acceptance of the truth as taught by the omniscient ones. Right knowledge is the understanding of the nature of reality—the soul, karma, and liberation."

**6** "Right conduct is the practice of the vows—non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-attachment. This threefold medicine, taken together, cures the disease of the soul permanently."

**7** Saddala Putta said: "Lord, in my practice of medicine, I sometimes must cause pain in order to heal—I must cut, I must cauterize, I must administer bitter medicines. Is this a violation of non-violence?"

**8** The Blessed One said: "The intention behind the action determines its karmic effect. He who causes pain with the intention of healing creates very different karma from he who causes pain with the intention of harming."

**9** "Yet even in healing, be mindful. Use the least harmful method. Do not cause unnecessary pain. And remember: you heal the body, which is temporary. The greater healing is to lead the soul toward liberation, which is permanent."

**10** "In your practice, observe these principles: do not use medicines derived from the killing of animals when plant-based remedies are available. Do not charge excessive fees from the poor. Do not withhold treatment from any being in need."

**11** Saddala Putta accepted the vows of the layman and continued his medical practice with even greater compassion. He treated the poor without charge and used his skill to relieve suffering wherever he found it.

**12** The Blessed One said: "The physician who heals the body serves this life. The teacher who heals the soul serves all lives. But the layman who combines both—who heals the body with compassion and nourishes the soul with dharma—he serves both this world and the next."
