## Overview

The Upasakadasah (Upāsakadaśāḥ, "Ten Chapters on Lay Followers") is the seventh of the twelve Angas of the Shvetambara Jain canon. It narrates the stories of ten exemplary householders (upasakas) who embraced the Jain path while continuing to live in the world. Each chapter describes how a layperson came to Lord Mahavira, took the twelve vows of a householder, and lived a life of dharma despite worldly responsibilities.

## Structure

The text is organized into 10 chapters, each centered on one exemplary layperson:

1. **Chapter 1 — Ananda**: The ideal householder and his twelve vows
2. **Chapter 2 — Kamadeva**: Renunciation within worldly life
3. **Chapter 3 — Chulanipiya**: Balancing family duties and dharma
4. **Chapter 4 — Suraadeva**: Generosity and right conduct
5. **Chapter 5 — Chulanisaddha**: Faithfulness and spiritual progress
6. **Chapter 6 — Kundalika**: Patience in the face of adversity
7. **Chapter 7 — Sadhana**: The practice of non-violence in daily life
8. **Chapter 8 — Mahachula**: Mastery of the partial vows
9. **Chapter 9 — Nandini**: Devotion and spiritual aspiration
10. **Chapter 10 — Salihipiya**: The culmination of the lay path

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Digambara**: Maintain their own understanding of lay practice with stricter interpretations
- **Shvetambara**: Consider this text the definitive guide to householder dharma
- **Academic**: Important for understanding Jain social history and lay-monastic relations
- **Comparative**: Parallels with Buddhist upasaka literature and Hindu grihasthadharma

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Lay Dharma | The spiritual path available to householders |
| Twelve Vows | The formal commitments of a Jain layperson |
| Exemplary Lives | Real individuals as models for lay practice |
| Partial Renunciation | Practicing discipline within worldly life |
| Non-Violence | Ahimsa applied to household and commercial life |
