## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Divine Messages places them alongside other scriptures produced through claimed divine communication: the Quran as recited by Muhammad, the Book of Mormon as translated by Joseph Smith, and the Baha'i writings as revealed through Baha'u'llah. Each tradition claims a distinctive mode of divine communication, and the Cao Dai *cơ bút* method — mediumistic writing through a specialized instrument — represents a unique variant.

The theological content of the Divine Messages — particularly the claim that all religions are expressions of a single divine truth — invites comparison with Baha'i progressive revelation, Theosophical perennialism, and the inclusivist theologies found in Hinduism's concept of *Istadevata* (chosen deity). The Cao Dai approach is distinctive in its combination of explicit syncretism with institutional formality.
