## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of Cao Dai Prayers and Rituals reveals a liturgical tradition that consciously incorporates elements from multiple world religions. The four daily prayer times echo Islamic *salat*, the incense offerings recall Buddhist and Catholic practice, and the moral invocations reflect Confucian devotional recitation.

Comparative liturgists note that the Cao Dai ritual system represents an unusually deliberate attempt to create a universal liturgy — a form of worship that honors the devotional practices of multiple traditions while subordinating them to a unified theological vision. This approach raises comparative questions about the relationship between ritual form and religious identity.
