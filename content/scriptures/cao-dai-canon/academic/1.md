## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the Divine Messages examines the *cơ bút* séances as a distinctive form of scriptural production. The spirit-writing sessions involved elaborate ritual protocols — specific mediums, ritual implements (the *ngọc cơ* or corbeille à bec), and witnessed transcription — that scholars compare with similar practices in Chinese *fuji* divination, European Spiritualist automatic writing, and Brazilian Spiritist *psychography*.

The content of the Divine Messages reveals a sophisticated theological program: the systematic integration of Buddhist, Taoist, Confucian, and Christian concepts within a framework of progressive revelation. Scholars note the influence of French Spiritism (particularly Allan Kardec's writings) on the Cao Dai understanding of spirit communication, reflecting the colonial encounter between Vietnamese and European religious cultures.
