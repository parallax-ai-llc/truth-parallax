## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the New Canonical Codes examines them as a legal system that synthesizes Vietnamese customary law, Confucian ethics, Buddhist precepts, and colonial-era legal concepts. The codes provide detailed regulations for personal conduct, family relationships, and community governance that reflect the social concerns of early twentieth-century Vietnamese society.

Scholars note that the codes represent a modernizing impulse within the Cao Dai movement — an attempt to create a rational, codified legal system that replaces the informal, tradition-based social regulation of Vietnamese village life. The prohibition of opium use, for example, reflects the anti-colonial dimension of Cao Dai social teaching, while the detailed family law provisions demonstrate engagement with both traditional Vietnamese and modern Western legal concepts.
