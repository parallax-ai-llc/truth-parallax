## Overview

The Cao Dai Canon encompasses the sacred texts of Cao Dai (Đại Đạo Tam Kỳ Phổ Độ — "Great Way of the Third Period of Salvation"), a syncretic religion founded in southern Vietnam in 1926. Cao Dai's scriptures are distinctive in that many were received through *cơ bút* (spirit writing or séance), in which the Supreme Being (*Cao Đài*) and various spiritual entities communicated with the faithful through mediumistic practice. The canon draws upon Buddhist, Taoist, Confucian, Christian, and indigenous Vietnamese religious traditions.

## Structure

The Cao Dai Canon comprises four major divisions:

1. **Divine Messages (Thánh Ngôn)**: Revelations received from the Supreme Being and spiritual teachers through *cơ bút* séances
2. **Religious Constitution (Pháp Chánh Truyền)**: The organizational structure, hierarchy, and governance of the Cao Dai church
3. **Prayers and Rituals (Kinh Thiên Đạo và Thế Đạo)**: Daily prayers, ceremonial liturgy, and ritual prescriptions
4. **New Canonical Codes (Tân Luật)**: Updated regulations governing religious practice, ethics, and community life

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Cao Dai**: The definitive revelation of the Supreme Being for the Third Era of salvation
- **Academic**: A key text for studying Vietnamese religious innovation, syncretism, and colonial-era new religions
- **Comparative**: A paradigmatic example of deliberate religious synthesis and inter-traditional theology
- **Buddhist-Vietnamese**: Examined for its creative integration of Mahayana Buddhist concepts within a syncretic framework
- **Confucian**: Studied for its adoption and adaptation of Confucian ethical principles and social hierarchy
- **Interfaith**: Valued as one of the most explicitly ecumenical scriptures in world religion

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Supreme Being | Cao Đài as the one God who has revealed through all religions |
| Third Era | The current age of universal salvation, following the eras of creation and struggle |
| Syncretism | The deliberate unification of Buddhist, Taoist, Confucian, and Christian teachings |
| Spiritism | Communication with the divine realm through séance and spirit writing |
| Universal Salvation | God's plan to save all beings through the Great Way |
| Hierarchy | An elaborate ecclesiastical structure modeled on the Catholic Church |
