## Confucian Reading

The Confucian reading of the New Canonical Codes identifies their deep roots in Confucian moral philosophy and social ethics. The codes' detailed attention to family relationships, filial piety, social propriety, and the moral obligations of leadership reflects the Confucian conviction that society's health depends on the moral cultivation of its members and the proper ordering of relationships.

Confucian scholars note that the codes' treatment of the five relationships — ruler-subject, parent-child, husband-wife, elder-younger, friend-friend — closely follows Confucian models, adapted to the Cao Dai context. The integration of Confucian social ethics into a religious legal code demonstrates the continuing vitality of Confucian moral thought in Vietnamese culture and its compatibility with theistic religious frameworks.
