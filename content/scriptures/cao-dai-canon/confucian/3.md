## Confucian Reading

The Confucian reading of the Prayers and Rituals section identifies the deep influence of Confucian ritual theory on Cao Dai worship. The emphasis on propriety, order, and moral intention in worship reflects the Confucian conviction that ritual (*li*) is both the expression and the cultivation of virtue. The carefully prescribed gestures, vestments, and procedures of Cao Dai ceremony embody the Confucian principle that correct form shapes correct character.

Confucian scholars note that the Cao Dai prayer texts include moral self-examination and ethical resolve — elements that recall the Confucian practice of daily self-reflection described in the Analects. The integration of moral cultivation into liturgical practice creates a worship system in which spiritual devotion and ethical development are inseparable.
