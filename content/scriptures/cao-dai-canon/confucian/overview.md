## Overview

The Confucian reading of the Cao Dai Canon examines the text's substantial incorporation of Confucian ethical and social principles. The canon's emphasis on filial piety, social harmony, hierarchical order, and moral self-cultivation reflects the deep influence of Confucian thought on Vietnamese culture and the Cao Dai founders' conviction that Confucian ethics provide the moral foundation for the universal religion.

Confucian scholars note that the Cao Dai Canon adopts the Confucian virtues — *ren* (benevolence), *yi* (righteousness), *li* (propriety), *zhi* (wisdom), and *xin* (trustworthiness) — as binding moral obligations for adherents. The canon's elaborate ecclesiastical hierarchy mirrors Confucian social organization, and its emphasis on ritual propriety in worship reflects the Confucian conviction that correct ritual practice both expresses and cultivates moral virtue.
