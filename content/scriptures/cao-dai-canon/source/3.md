---
title: "Cao Dai Canon Section 3"
scripture: "cao-dai-canon"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Supreme Being revealed the structure of the divine hierarchy: "The spiritual government mirrors the earthly government, for as above, so below.

**2** At the summit is Myself, the Supreme Being, Cao Dai. Below Me are the three ancient saints: the Buddha, Lao Tzu, and Confucius. They represent the three aspects of the divine teaching.

**3** Below them are the celestial beings, the Immortals, the Saints, and the Sages, who guide and protect humanity.

**4** The earthly hierarchy of the Cao Dai Church consists of the Pope, the Cardinals, the Archbishops, the Bishops, the Priests, and the Student Priests.

**5** The Legislative Body consists of the three principal branches: the Confucian branch governs human relations and social ethics, the Taoist branch governs esoteric practices and spiritual cultivation, and the Buddhist branch governs monastic life and meditation.

**6** All three branches work together in harmony, for they are three aspects of one truth. Just as the three teachings of Confucius, Lao Tzu, and the Buddha are ultimately one teaching, so the three branches of the Church serve one divine purpose."
