## Cao Dai Reading

The Prayers and Rituals section of the Cao Dai Canon prescribes the daily devotional practices, ceremonial liturgy, and sacramental rites that structure the spiritual life of every Cao Dai adherent. The daily prayer cycle includes morning, noon, evening, and midnight prayers, each facing the altar of the Supreme Being and reciting texts drawn from Buddhist, Taoist, Confucian, and Christian sources — a liturgical synthesis unique in world religion.

The Cao Dai community understands these prayers and rituals as the divinely ordained means of maintaining connection with the Supreme Being and the spirit world. The elaborate ceremonial life of the Cao Dai temple — with its colorful vestments, incense offerings, and orchestrated processions — expresses in sensory form the theological vision of universal harmony that animates the entire canon.
