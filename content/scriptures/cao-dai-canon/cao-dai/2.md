## Cao Dai Reading

The Religious Constitution (*Pháp Chánh Truyền*) establishes the organizational framework of the Cao Dai church, which the community understands as divinely ordained. The constitution prescribes an elaborate ecclesiastical hierarchy modeled in part on the Catholic Church — with a pope (*Giáo Tông*), cardinals, archbishops, bishops, and priests — while incorporating elements of Buddhist monastic organization and Confucian administrative structure.

The Cao Dai community regards the Religious Constitution not as a human invention but as a divine blueprint for the institutional expression of the Great Way. The detailed prescriptions for offices, ranks, vestments, and procedures are understood as the Supreme Being's provision for the orderly propagation of the faith. The elaborate hierarchy ensures that the teaching is transmitted faithfully and that the community's spiritual life is guided by qualified, properly authorized leaders.
