## Overview

For Cao Dai adherents, the canon represents the definitive revelation of the Supreme Being (*Cao Đài*) for the Third Era of salvation. The Cao Dai community believes that God has revealed divine truth in three successive periods: the first through figures such as Moses, Dipankara Buddha, and the Jade Emperor; the second through Jesus, Shakyamuni Buddha, Laozi, Confucius, and Muhammad; and the third — the present era — directly through the Cao Dai religion, which unifies all previous revelations into a single Great Way.

The *cơ bút* (spirit writing) through which much of the canon was received is not regarded as mere mediumship but as the Supreme Being's chosen method of communication for the Third Era. The séances through which divine messages were transmitted involved trained mediums operating under strict ritual conditions, and the resulting texts are treated with the same reverence accorded to scripture in other traditions. The Tây Ninh Holy See — Cao Dai's central temple — serves as the institutional guardian of the canon and the center of liturgical life.
