## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Prayers and Rituals section highlights the Cao Dai liturgy as one of the most ambitious experiments in interreligious worship in modern history. The daily prayer cycle, which draws upon Buddhist, Taoist, Confucian, and Christian devotional forms, represents a practical attempt to create a universal liturgy that honors the spiritual contributions of multiple traditions.

Interfaith practitioners find in Cao Dai worship both inspiration and cautionary lessons. The liturgy demonstrates that elements from different traditions can be meaningfully combined in communal worship, while also raising questions about cultural appropriation, decontextualization, and the loss of tradition-specific meaning that can occur when ritual forms are transplanted from their original settings.
