## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the New Canonical Codes highlights their attempt to create a universal ethical framework by synthesizing moral principles from multiple religious traditions. The codes' combination of Buddhist compassion, Confucian social ethics, and Christian charity represents a practical experiment in interreligious moral theology — an attempt to identify and codify the ethical common ground shared by the world's major traditions.

Interfaith practitioners find in the New Canonical Codes evidence that genuine moral convergence across traditions is possible, even as the codes' specific prescriptions reflect the cultural context of early twentieth-century Vietnam. The codes challenge the assumption that moral systems are tradition-specific and incommensurable, suggesting instead that a shared ethical foundation can be constructed from the moral wisdom of diverse religious sources.
