## Overview

The interfaith reading of the Cao Dai Canon highlights its unprecedented attempt to create a scripture that explicitly honors and integrates multiple religious traditions. The canon's teaching that all religions are branches of a single divine tree — different expressions of the one Supreme Being's truth — represents one of the most radically ecumenical positions in the history of world religion.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Cao Dai Canon a concrete model for interreligious theology — not merely tolerance or dialogue but genuine synthesis. The canon's saints include Jesus, the Buddha, Laozi, Confucius, Muhammad, and others, all honored as divine teachers who revealed authentic aspects of the Supreme Being's truth in their respective eras and cultures. This inclusive canon challenges exclusivist claims in all traditions while raising questions about the limits and possibilities of religious synthesis.
