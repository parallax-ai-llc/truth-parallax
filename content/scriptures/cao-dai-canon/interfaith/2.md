## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Religious Constitution examines how a syncretic religion creates institutional structures that honor multiple traditions simultaneously. The Cao Dai constitution's combination of Catholic, Buddhist, and Confucian organizational elements represents a practical experiment in interreligious integration — not merely at the level of theology or spirituality but at the concrete level of institutional governance.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Religious Constitution a model — however imperfect — for creating religious institutions that draw on the organizational wisdom of multiple traditions. The constitution raises important questions about whether genuine institutional synthesis is possible or whether the attempt inevitably privileges one tradition's forms over others.
