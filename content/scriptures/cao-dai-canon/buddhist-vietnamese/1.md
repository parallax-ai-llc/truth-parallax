## Buddhist-Vietnamese Reading

The Buddhist-Vietnamese reading of the Divine Messages examines how the Supreme Being's revelations integrate Mahayana Buddhist concepts into their syncretic framework. The messages reference karma, reincarnation, the bodhisattva ideal, and the aspiration for universal liberation — all core Mahayana teachings — while reframing them within a theistic context that attributes ultimate agency to the Supreme Being rather than to the impersonal workings of dharma.

Vietnamese Buddhist scholars note that the Divine Messages draw particularly on Pure Land Buddhism's emphasis on divine grace and the Thiền (Chan/Zen) tradition's teaching on direct spiritual experience. The Supreme Being's promise to save all beings through the Great Way resonates with Amitabha Buddha's universal vow, while the emphasis on direct communication through *cơ bút* echoes Zen's claim to transmission beyond words and scriptures.
