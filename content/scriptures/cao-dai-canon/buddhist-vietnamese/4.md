## Buddhist-Vietnamese Reading

The Buddhist-Vietnamese reading of the New Canonical Codes identifies their substantial indebtedness to Buddhist ethical precepts. The codes' prescriptions for vegetarianism, abstinence from intoxicants, and non-violence echo the five precepts (*ngũ giới*) of Buddhist lay practice, while the more demanding requirements for senior clergy parallel the monastic *vinaya* rules.

Buddhist-Vietnamese scholars note that the codes adapt Buddhist ethical ideals to the circumstances of lay householders in Vietnamese society — a practical moral theology that reflects the influence of engaged Buddhism and the Vietnamese Buddhist tradition of integrating monastic and lay practice. The codes' emphasis on compassion, self-discipline, and service to others resonates with the bodhisattva ideal of Mahayana Buddhism.
