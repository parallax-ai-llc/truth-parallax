## Buddhist-Vietnamese Reading

The Buddhist-Vietnamese reading of the Religious Constitution notes its incorporation of elements from Vietnamese Buddhist monastic organization. The constitution prescribes vegetarian discipline for senior clergy, meditation retreats, and a monastic option that reflects the influence of Vietnamese Buddhist practice on the Cao Dai institutional framework.

Buddhist-Vietnamese scholars observe that the constitution adapts Buddhist monastic ideals to a new context — creating a religious hierarchy that combines the discipline of the *sangha* with the administrative efficiency of Catholic governance and the social respectability of Confucian officialdom. This synthesis reflects the religious environment of southern Vietnam, where Buddhist, Catholic, and Confucian institutions coexisted and mutually influenced one another.
