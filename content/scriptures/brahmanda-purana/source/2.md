---
title: "Brahmanda Purana Anushanga Pada (Book 2)"
scripture: "brahmanda-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Anushanga Pada describes the dynasties of kings, the histories of the sages, and the stories of the gods in detail.

**2** The lineage of the solar dynasty is traced from Vivasvan, the sun god, through Manu to Ikshvaku and his descendants. Each king is described with his virtues, his deeds, and his contribution to the maintenance of dharma.

**3** The story of Parashurama is narrated. Born as the son of the sage Jamadagni and Renuka, he was a Brahmin by birth but a warrior by nature. When the Kshatriya king Kartavirya Arjuna stole his father's divine cow and later killed Jamadagni, Parashurama took a terrible oath.

**4** Parashurama said: "I shall rid the earth of the Kshatriya race twenty-one times." With his battle-axe, given by Shiva, he fulfilled this vow, filling five lakes with Kshatriya blood. Then he retired to perform austerities on Mount Mahendra.

**5** The lunar dynasty is also described. From Soma, the moon god, descended Budha, who married Ila, the daughter of Manu. From their union descended Pururavas, whose love for the celestial Apsara Urvashi is one of the most celebrated stories of the Puranas.

**6** The Purana narrates the birth and exploits of Karttikeya, the war god, son of Shiva and Parvati. Born to destroy the demon Taraka, Karttikeya was raised by the six Krittikas and hence is called Shadanana, the six-faced one.

**7** The story of the Tripura — the three flying cities of the demons — is narrated. The demon architect Maya built three cities of gold, silver, and iron that flew through the sky. Shiva destroyed all three with a single arrow, the Pashupata, and the gods rejoiced.

**8** The Purana contains the Lalita Sahasranama, the thousand names of the goddess Lalita Tripurasundari. This hymn is one of the most sacred texts of Shakta worship.

**9** The goddess Lalita is described: "She is red as the rising sun, adorned with all ornaments, seated upon a lotus. She holds the noose, the goad, the bow of sugarcane, and arrows of flowers. She is the supreme Shakti, the power behind all creation."

**10** The Purana teaches: "The worship of the goddess is the worship of the creative power of the universe. She is Shakti, without whom Shiva cannot act. She is Maya, who veils the truth. She is also Vidya, who reveals it."

**11** The Purana narrates the destruction of the demon Bhandasura by the goddess Lalita. When the gods were oppressed by Bhandasura, who had been created from the ashes of Kama (the god of love), Lalita arose from the fire of cosmic consciousness and destroyed him with her armies.

**12** The sage concluded: "The Anushanga Pada teaches that the universe is sustained by the dynamic interaction of the masculine and feminine principles. Shiva and Shakti, Vishnu and Lakshmi, Brahma and Sarasvati — the divine pairs govern all aspects of existence."
