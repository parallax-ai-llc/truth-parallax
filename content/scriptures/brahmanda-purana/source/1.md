---
title: "Brahmanda Purana Prakriya Pada (Book 1)"
scripture: "brahmanda-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. Salutation to the Supreme Brahman, who manifested as the cosmic egg, the Brahmanda, from which all creation emerged. This Purana reveals the mysteries of the universe.

**2** The sages at Naimisharanya asked Suta: "O learned one, tell us of the creation of the Brahmanda, the cosmic egg, and of the beings that dwell within it. How did this vast universe come into being?"

**3** Suta said: "In the beginning, there was only Brahman, without attributes, without form, beyond the reach of mind and speech. From Brahman arose Prakriti, the primal nature, and Purusha, the cosmic spirit."

**4** From the union of Prakriti and Purusha arose Mahat, the great intelligence. From Mahat arose Ahankara, the sense of self. From Ahankara arose the five subtle elements: sound, touch, form, taste, and smell.

**5** These elements combined to form the cosmic egg, the Brahmanda, which floated upon the waters of dissolution. The egg was covered by seven sheaths: water, fire, air, space, ego, intelligence, and primal nature, each ten times larger than the one it enclosed.

**6** Within the egg, Brahma was born. He looked in every direction and saw nothing. Then, through the power of meditation, he perceived the entire plan of creation within himself and began to manifest the worlds.

**7** Brahma created the four Vedas from his four mouths. He created the gods, the demons, the humans, and all other beings. He established the laws of dharma and the cycle of the four ages.

**8** The Purana describes the geography of the Brahmanda: seven continents surrounded by seven oceans, seven nether regions below, and seven celestial regions above. At the center stands Mount Meru, the golden mountain.

**9** The movements of the celestial bodies are described: the sun revolves around Meru, creating day and night. The moon waxes and wanes. The planets move in their appointed courses, governed by invisible cords of divine energy.

**10** The Purana narrates the story of the Manvantaras, the periods ruled by successive Manus. Each Manvantara has its own Manu, its own Indra, its own sages, and its own avatar of Vishnu. The present is the seventh Manvantara, ruled by Manu Vaivasvata.

**11** The sage teaches: "The Brahmanda is not merely a physical structure but a manifestation of consciousness. Every atom of the universe is pervaded by the divine intelligence. The macrocosm and the microcosm mirror each other."

**12** The Prakriya Pada concludes: "He who knows the structure of the Brahmanda knows the structure of his own being. He who understands creation understands himself. For the universe is within, as it is without."
