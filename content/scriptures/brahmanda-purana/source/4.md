---
title: "Brahmanda Purana Upasamhara Pada (Book 4)"
scripture: "brahmanda-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The Upasamhara Pada describes the dissolution of the universe and the ultimate teaching of the Brahmanda Purana.

**2** The sage said: "At the end of each Kalpa, the universe returns to its source. This is not destruction but transformation. Matter returns to energy, energy returns to consciousness, and consciousness rests in the Supreme."

**3** The process of dissolution mirrors the process of creation in reverse. Earth dissolves into water, water into fire, fire into air, air into space, space into ego, ego into intelligence, intelligence into unmanifest nature, and unmanifest nature into Brahman.

**4** The Purana teaches: "The cycles of creation and dissolution are without beginning and without end. They will continue as long as the Supreme wills it. The individual soul, caught in these cycles, revolves like a wheel until it attains liberation."

**5** The future dissolution is described: "When the time of dissolution comes, drought will parch the earth for a hundred years. Seven suns will appear. The oceans will boil away. The mountains will crumble. All living beings will perish."

**6** "Then twelve years of rain will deluge the earth. The cosmic wind will scatter the clouds. Brahma will reabsorb the universe into himself. He will sleep for a night equal in length to his day. Then he will awake, and creation will begin anew."

**7** The Purana reveals the highest teaching: "The universe is the body of Brahman. Creation is Brahman's exhalation; dissolution is Brahman's inhalation. The individual soul is a cell in the body of Brahman. When the cell realizes its identity with the body, liberation is attained."

**8** "Brahman is not far away. Brahman is not hidden. Brahman is here, now, in this very moment. It is the awareness that reads these words. It is the consciousness that hears these sounds. To seek Brahman outside oneself is to search for the sun with a torch."

**9** The Purana describes the fruit of studying the Brahmanda Purana: "He who hears this Purana with devotion attains the merit of visiting all the Tirthas, performing all the sacrifices, and giving all the gifts. He is freed from the cycle of birth and death."

**10** The sage teaches: "The Brahmanda, the cosmic egg, is within you. The seven worlds are within you. The sun, the moon, and the stars are within you. Know thyself, and you know the universe. Know the universe, and you know Brahman."

**11** "This is the final teaching: You are not the body. You are not the mind. You are not the ego. You are the witness, the eternal Self, identical with Brahman. This knowledge alone liberates. This knowledge alone is real. All else is passing shadow."

**12** The sage concluded: "Thus ends the Brahmanda Purana, the Purana of the cosmic egg. May all beings attain the knowledge of Brahman. May all beings be free from suffering. May all beings dwell in peace. Om Shanti, Shanti, Shanti."
