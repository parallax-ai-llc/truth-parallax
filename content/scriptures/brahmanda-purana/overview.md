## Overview

The Brahmanda Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, whose title means "the Purana of the Cosmic Egg (Brahmanda)." It is a text of considerable theological importance, particularly for the Shakta (goddess-worship) tradition, as it contains the Lalita Sahasranama (the Thousand Names of the Goddess Lalita), one of the most revered hymns in all of Hindu goddess worship.

The Brahmanda Purana is generally dated to the 4th-10th centuries CE and shares significant material with the Vayu Purana, suggesting common source traditions. The text is notable for its detailed cosmological accounts, its treatment of the Manvantara system (cosmic ages), and its inclusion of the Lalitopakhyana (the narrative of the goddess Lalita Tripurasundari), which has become foundational to Shakta and Srividya traditions.

## Structure

The text is organized into four Padas (sections):

1. **Prakriya Pada — Introductory Section**: Cosmogony, the origin of the cosmic egg, and the preliminary creation narrative
2. **Anushanga Pada — Continuation Section**: Genealogies, the Manvantara system, and the succession of cosmic ages
3. **Upodghata Pada — Supplementary Section**: The Lalitopakhyana, the Lalita Sahasranama, and goddess theology
4. **Upasamhara Pada — Concluding Section**: The dissolution of the universe, eschatology, and the paths to liberation

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Brahmanda Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: Engaged for its cosmological content while acknowledging its significance to Shakta traditions
- **Shaivism**: Valued for its connections to Shaiva-Shakta theology, especially the Lalitopakhyana
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for passages on the cosmic egg as a manifestation of the one non-dual Brahman
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Approached selectively, with focus on cosmological material compatible with Vaishnava theology
- **Academic**: A key source for studying the development of Shakta theology, Srividya traditions, and Puranic cosmology

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Cosmic Egg | The Brahmanda as the primordial structure from which the universe emerges |
| Lalita Sahasranama | The thousand names of the supreme goddess Lalita Tripurasundari |
| Goddess Theology | The supreme feminine divine as the ultimate reality |
| Manvantara System | The cyclical succession of cosmic ages and their ruling Manus |
| Cosmology | Detailed accounts of the structure and cycles of the universe |
