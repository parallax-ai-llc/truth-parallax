## Overview

Neo-Confucian interpreters elevated the Book of Documents to new philosophical significance by reading its political narratives through the lens of metaphysical cosmology. The famous sixteen-character formula attributed to the transmission from Yao to Shun — "The human mind is precarious, the mind of the Dao is subtle; be refined, be single-minded; hold fast the mean" — became the cornerstone of the Neo-Confucian understanding of the orthodox transmission of the Way (daotong) from sage-king to sage-king.

## Interpretive Approach

Zhu Xi and the Cheng brothers read the Shujing as demonstrating the identity of cosmic principle (tianli) with moral governance. The sage-kings are understood not merely as effective rulers but as individuals who had fully realized the principle inherent in their nature and applied it to statecraft. The Great Plan (Hong Fan) received particular attention as a cosmological treatise showing how the five phases, the five affairs, and the eight governmental functions all reflect a single underlying principle. Neo-Confucian commentators also engaged in rigorous textual criticism, with scholars such as Yan Ruoqu ultimately demonstrating that portions of the received text were later forgeries.

## Contemporary Relevance

The Neo-Confucian engagement with the Shujing continues to inform philosophical discussions about the relationship between metaphysics and politics, the nature of legitimate authority, and the intellectual foundations of Chinese constitutional thought.
