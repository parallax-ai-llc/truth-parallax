---
title: "Book of Documents — The Charge to the Duke of Shao"
scripture: "book-of-documents"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The Duke of Shao was displeased, and the King went on to say, Oh, Heaven gives long life or early death to man on no double principle.

**2** Heaven is not to be trusted. Our course is simply to seek the prolongation of virtue. It is not that Heaven cuts short the lives of men; they bring them to an end themselves.

**3** If people lose their virtue and proceed to do evil, Heaven will give its mandate to correct them and punish them.

**4** We should not repine against Heaven, but examine ourselves. The ways of Heaven are not invariable.

**5** Do you, O Duke, act as the assistant to the young king. Establish the government upon its basis, making the people tranquil and giving stability to the officers.

**6** With reverence display your virtue; be careful in the employment of officers; be anxious in your counsels; and act with benevolence toward the people.
