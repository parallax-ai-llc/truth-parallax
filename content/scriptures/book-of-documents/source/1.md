title: "Book of Documents — The Canon of Yao"
scripture: "book-of-documents"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Examining into antiquity, we find that the Emperor Yao was called Fang-hsun. He was reverential, intelligent, accomplished, and thoughtful, naturally and without effort.

**2** He was sincerely courteous, and capable of all complaisance. The display of these qualities reached to the four extremities of the empire, and extended from earth to heaven.

**3** He was able to make the able and virtuous distinguished, and thence proceeded to the love of the nine classes of his kindred, who all became harmonious.

**4** He also regulated and polished the people of his domain, who all became brightly intelligent. Finally, he united and harmonized the myriad states.

**5** He commanded Hsi and Ho, in reverent accordance with their observation of the wide heavens, to calculate and delineate the movements and appearances of the sun, the moon, the stars, and the zodiacal spaces.

**6** The day is of the medium length, and the star is in Niao; you may thus exactly determine mid-spring. The people are dispersed in the fields, and birds and beasts breed and copulate.
