---
title: "Book of Documents — The Great Plan"
scripture: "book-of-documents"
chapter: 3
---

**1** In the thirteenth year, the King went to inquire of the Count of Chi, and said to him, Oh Count of Chi, Heaven, working unseen, secures the tranquility of the lower people. I do not know the method by which the constant principles of its order should be set forth.

**2** The Count of Chi thereupon replied, I have heard that in old time Gun dammed up the inundating waters, and thereby threw into disorder the arrangement of the five elements.

**3** God was consequently roused to anger, and did not give him the Great Plan with its nine divisions, and thus the unvarying principles of the method were allowed to go to ruin.

**4** But Yu rose up and continued the work, and to him Heaven gave the Great Plan with its nine divisions.

**5** The first division relates to the five elements: water, fire, wood, metal, earth. The second relates to the reverent attention to the five personal matters: demeanor, speech, seeing, hearing, thinking.

**6** The third relates to the earnest devotion to the eight objects of government: food, goods, sacrifices, public works, education, justice, hospitality, military affairs.
