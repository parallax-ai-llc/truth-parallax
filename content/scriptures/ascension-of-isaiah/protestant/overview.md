## Overview

The Protestant engagement with the Ascension of Isaiah focuses on its significance for understanding early Christological development. The text's portrait of the pre-existent Beloved who descends through the heavens provides important parallels to Philippians 2:6-11 and the Christology of Hebrews. The allusion to Isaiah's martyrdom in Hebrews 11:37 ('sawn in two') connects the canonical text to this tradition.
