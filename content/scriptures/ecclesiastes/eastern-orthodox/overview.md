## Overview

The Orthodox Church reads Ecclesiastes through the patristic tradition, especially St. Gregory of Nyssa's eight homilies on the book. Gregory's reading is characteristically Orthodox in its emphasis on the soul's journey from attachment to worldly things toward union with God (*theosis*). The book's themes of impermanence and divine mystery resonate deeply with Orthodox spirituality.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Patristic Exegesis
St. Gregory of Nyssa's homilies interpret Ecclesiastes as a spiritual ascent: Qoheleth progresses from recognizing the world's vanity to discovering that God alone satisfies. This mirrors the Orthodox understanding of purification, illumination, and union.

### Liturgical Context
Ecclesiastes is not read as a continuous lectionary text, but its themes pervade Orthodox hymnography, especially during Great Lent and funeral services: "Vanity of vanities, all is vanity" appears in Orthodox funeral rites.

### Apophatic Theology
Qoheleth's declaration that God's works cannot be fully understood resonates with the Orthodox apophatic tradition — the knowledge that God transcends all human categories.

## Theological Emphases

- **Impermanence**: All earthly things pass; only God endures
- **Repentance**: The recognition of vanity as the first step toward *metanoia*
- **Divine Mystery**: God's ways are beyond human comprehension
- **Joy as Gift**: True joy comes from God, not from achievement
