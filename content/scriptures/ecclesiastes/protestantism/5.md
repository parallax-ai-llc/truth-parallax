## Reverence Before God

Protestant interpretation of Ecclesiastes 5 reads Qoheleth's observations within the broader canonical context, recognizing both the legitimacy of his complaints about life "under the sun" and the ultimate hope that transcends them.

## Key Themes

### Vows

The chapter's engagement with vows reflects Qoheleth's honest assessment of life's frustrations. Protestant theology values this honesty — it reflects the groaning of creation described in Romans 8:22, awaiting redemption.

### Reverence and Wealth

The interplay between reverence and wealth in this chapter demonstrates Qoheleth's method: rigorous observation leading to theological reflection. The Reformation's commitment to truth, even uncomfortable truth, finds an ally in Ecclesiastes.

### Sleep

The theme of sleep connects to the Protestant understanding of God's sovereignty over all aspects of life, including those that seem random or unjust from a human perspective.

## Interpretive Approaches

| Approach | Reading |
|----------|---------|
| Canonical | Reading within the context of the whole Bible |
| Pastoral | Addressing real human experiences of frustration and doubt |
| Christological | Pointing toward Christ as the answer to life's *hebel* |
| Existential | Engaging honestly with the human condition |

## Theological Significance

Ecclesiastes 5 contributes to the Protestant understanding that faith does not require pretending life is always good. Honest lament and questioning have a place within the life of faith — indeed, they drive the believer toward deeper trust in God's sovereign purposes.
