## Overview

Shaiva interpreters read the Atma Upanishad's three levels of self as describing consciousness at different degrees of contraction and expansion. The external self is consciousness fully contracted into identification with the body; the inner self is consciousness partially expanded through introspection; the supreme self is consciousness in its infinite, unlimited nature — Shiva.

## Theological Emphases

The progressive discovery of the self from external to inner to supreme mirrors the Shaiva path of recognition (*pratyabhijna*): the practitioner moves from the contracted awareness of everyday life through the expanding awareness of meditation to the unlimited awareness that is Shiva's own nature. The three selves are not three different entities but one consciousness at three levels of self-recognition.
