## Overview

The Atma Upanishad's threefold analysis of the self — external, inner, and supreme — invites comparison with tripartite anthropologies across traditions. The body-soul-spirit trichotomy appears in remarkably similar forms in Greek, Christian, Islamic, and other philosophical traditions.

## Comparative Themes

The three selves parallel Paul's distinction between body (*soma*), soul (*psyche*), and spirit (*pneuma*) in 1 Thessalonians 5:23. They also compare with Plato's tripartite soul (appetitive, spirited, rational), the Islamic *nafs* (ego-self), *ruh* (spirit), and *sirr* (secret/divine), and the Kabbalistic *nefesh* (vital soul), *ruach* (spirit), and *neshamah* (divine soul). The universal recurrence of this threefold pattern suggests a fundamental structure of human self-awareness.
