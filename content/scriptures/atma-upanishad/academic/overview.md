## Overview

The Atma Upanishad's threefold analysis of the self provides important evidence for the development of philosophical psychology in India. The text's systematic distinction between the external self (body-identified), the inner self (mind-identified), and the supreme self (transcendent) reflects a progressive sophistication in the Upanishadic understanding of consciousness.

## Research Themes

Scholars note the text's pedagogical clarity — its use of a simple threefold schema to convey the complex Upanishadic teaching on the self. The external/inner/supreme distinction anticipates the *pancha-kosha* (five sheaths) analysis of the Taittiriya Upanishad in simplified form. The text's dating is uncertain but is generally placed in the early centuries CE.
