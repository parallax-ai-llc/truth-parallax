---
title: "Atma Upanishad Section 1"
scripture: "atma-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The Purusha has three forms of experience: the external self, the inner self, and the supreme self. That which has skin, flesh, blood, sinew, fat, marrow, and bone—this is known as the external self.

**2** The external self is that which is perceived through the organs of sense: the eyes, the ears, the tongue, the nose, and the skin. It is the body composed of the five elements—earth, water, fire, air, and ether.

**3** He who identifies himself with this body, who says "I am this body," who considers the pleasures of the body as his own pleasures, and the pains of the body as his own pains—he is the Bahyatman, the external self.

**4** This external self is born and it dies. It is subject to hunger, thirst, heat, and cold. It experiences pleasure and pain through contact with the objects of the senses.

**5** The wise man, however, does not identify the Self with this body. He sees the body as a garment which is worn and then discarded. He knows that the body is impermanent, but the Self is eternal.

**6** The body is formed from the union of the five elements: from earth comes its solidity, from water its fluidity, from fire its warmth, from air its movement, and from ether the spaces within it.

**7** Through the organs of action—the hands, the feet, the tongue, and the organs of excretion and generation—the external self interacts with the world. Through the organs of knowledge—the eye, the ear, the nose, the tongue, and the skin—it gathers experience.

**8** But the wise one knows: "I am not this body. I am the witness of the body. I am that which remains when all the modifications of the body are removed."
