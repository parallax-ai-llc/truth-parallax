---
title: "Atma Upanishad Section 3"
scripture: "atma-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Now the Supreme Self, the Paramatman, is to be known. It is the syllable Om. It is the self that is beyond the three states, the self that is called Turiya—the fourth.

**2** The Paramatman is that which is pure consciousness, without beginning and without end. It is not born, it does not die, it does not grow, it does not decay. It is unchangeable, eternal, and immovable.

**3** It is not the gross body, nor the subtle body, nor the causal body. It is the witness of all three bodies. It is self-luminous, needing no other light to illumine it.

**4** As the sun illumines all things but is not affected by the defects of what it illumines, so the Supreme Self illumines all the bodies and all the minds, but is not affected by their defects.

**5** It is one and without a second. It pervades all things, as ether pervades all things. It is subtler than the subtlest. It is beyond all qualities and all attributes. It is the ground of all existence.

**6** The Supreme Self is neither the doer of actions nor the enjoyer of their fruits. It is the eternal witness, ever pure, ever free. Actions belong to the body and the mind, not to the Self.

**7** As the crystal appears to be coloured by the objects placed near it but is in itself colourless, so the Supreme Self appears to be affected by the conditions of the body and mind but is in itself unaffected.

**8** The knower of the Supreme Self goes beyond sorrow. He who sees all beings in the Self and the Self in all beings, hates none. For one who has become the Self of all beings, how can there be delusion or grief for him who sees oneness?

**9** The Supreme Self is the lord of all, the ruler of all, the protector of all. It does not become greater by good works nor lesser by bad works. It is the lord, the supreme bridge that holds these worlds apart so that they do not clash together.

**10** This Self is not attained by one who has no strength, nor by one who is careless, nor by austerity without purpose. But the Self of the wise one who strives with right means enters into the abode of Brahman.

**11** Having attained this knowledge, the sages, satisfied with their wisdom, with their souls rightly established, free from passions, and tranquil in mind, having found the all-pervading Self in all, enter into that All itself.

**12** He who knows the Atman as described herein—as the external self, the inner self, and the Supreme Self—is freed from all bonds and attains immortality. Thus declares this Upanishad.
