## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Atma Upanishad's threefold analysis of the self as revealing the progressive discovery of Vishnu's presence. The external self (*bahyatman*) is the embodied soul in its most limited identification; the inner self (*antaratman*) is the soul aware of its spiritual nature; the supreme self (*paramatman*) is Vishnu dwelling within as the inner controller.

## Theological Emphases

The three levels of self correspond to three levels of spiritual maturity in the Vaishnava path: the materialist who identifies with the body, the seeker who turns inward, and the devotee who recognizes God's presence as the supreme self within. The Paramatman is not an impersonal principle but the personal Lord who has graciously entered each being as guide and sustainer.
