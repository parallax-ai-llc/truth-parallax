## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Atma Upanishad provides a clear pedagogical framework for the progressive stripping away of false identifications until the true self — Brahman — is revealed. The external self is the body mistaken for the self; the inner self is the mind mistaken for the self; the supreme self is the self as it truly is — pure consciousness identical with Brahman.

## Theological Emphases

The three levels represent the Advaita path of discrimination (*viveka*): first, recognizing "I am not this body"; then, recognizing "I am not this mind"; finally, recognizing "I am Brahman." The Paramatman of the Atma Upanishad is not a deity dwelling within but the seeker's own true nature — the non-dual self that was always present but hidden by successive layers of false identification.
