## Overview

Madhva reads the Atma Upanishad's three levels of self as confirming the Dvaita hierarchy: the external self is the embodied individual soul; the inner self is the same soul in its reflective capacity; and the supreme self is Vishnu dwelling within as the inner controller (*antaryamin*), eternally distinct from the individual soul.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the three selves do not collapse into one — the Paramatman remains eternally distinct from the individual atman. The progression from external to inner to supreme represents the soul's growing awareness of God's presence, not the dissolution of the soul into God. The supreme self is the Lord who graciously accompanies each soul, guiding it toward liberation through devotion.
