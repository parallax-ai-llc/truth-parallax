## Overview

Advaita Vedanta reads the Vayaviya Samhita's eschatological and cosmological teachings through its non-dual lens. The cosmic dissolution described here mirrors the philosophical dissolution of the apparent world in the realization of non-dual Brahman. Just as the universe is periodically reabsorbed into its source in the cosmic pralaya, so the apparent duality of the world is dissolved in the aspirant's realization of Brahman.

The Pashupata teachings presented in this samhita, with their framework of Lord, soul, and bondage, are read by Advaitins as a valid preliminary teaching (adhikari-bheda) suited to aspirants at a particular stage of development. The ultimate teaching, for Advaita, transcends even this framework: there is no separate Lord, no separate soul, and no real bondage — only the one non-dual Brahman appearing in various guises.

The Vayu connection is philosophically significant for Advaitins because Vayu (air/breath) represents prana — the vital force that, in Upanishadic teaching, is the closest physical correlate to the universal Brahman. The samhita's association with Vayu thus points, for Advaitins, to the intimate connection between breath-awareness and Brahman-realization.

## Key Themes

- **Dissolution as Realization**: Cosmic pralaya mirroring the dissolution of duality in knowledge
- **Preliminary Teaching**: The Pashupata framework as a stepping stone to non-dual realization
- **Vayu-Prana-Brahman**: The Vayu connection pointing to breath as a path to Brahman
- **Cyclical Illusion**: The cosmic cycles as demonstrating the impermanence of all phenomenal reality
- **Beyond Lord and Soul**: Transcending even the pati-pashu distinction in non-dual knowledge

## Key Verses and Passages

### Dissolution and Knowledge

Advaitins read the cosmic dissolution as a cosmological analog of the philosophical dissolution of ignorance: just as the universe returns to its undifferentiated source in pralaya, so the mind returns to its source in Brahman through self-knowledge.

### Vayu and Prana

The samhita's Vayu association is connected by Advaitins to pranayama (breath control) practices and to the Upanishadic identification of prana as a primary manifestation of Brahman.

## Commentary Tradition

Advaitin engagement with eschatological material tends to emphasize its philosophical rather than cosmological significance. The dissolution of worlds is a reminder of the dissolution of ignorance that constitutes liberation.
