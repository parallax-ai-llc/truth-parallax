---
title: "Shiva Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "shiva-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om Namah Shivaya. The sages asked Suta: "Tell us about Lord Shiva, the auspicious one, the supreme lord of the universe, the destroyer of sorrow."

**2** Suta said: Lord Shiva is the Supreme Being, the Absolute, the one without a second. He is the beginning and the end, the creator and the destroyer, the eternal and the infinite.

**3** He is beyond all forms, yet He assumes forms for the benefit of His devotees. He is beyond all attributes, yet He is worshipped with attributes by those who seek His grace.

**4** He is Mahadeva, the great god. He is Maheshvara, the great lord. He is Pashupati, the lord of all creatures. He is Nataraja, the cosmic dancer.

**5** His abode is Mount Kailasa, the silver mountain, where He dwells in eternal bliss with His consort Parvati, His sons Ganesha and Kartikeya, and His devotees.

**6** He wears the crescent moon upon His brow, the sacred Ganga flows from His matted locks, serpents adorn His body, and the tiger skin is His garment.

**7** His third eye, when opened, can destroy the universe. Yet from that same eye flows the nectar of compassion for His devotees.

**8** He drank the poison Halahala that arose from the churning of the ocean, holding it in His throat to save the world. Thus His throat is blue, and He is called Neelakantha.

**9** He is the lord of yoga, the supreme ascetic, the master of meditation. All yogis seek His grace, for He is the source of all spiritual power.

**10** He who chants the five-syllabled mantra "Namah Shivaya" with devotion is freed from all bondage and attains the supreme state. This is the teaching of the Shiva Purana.
