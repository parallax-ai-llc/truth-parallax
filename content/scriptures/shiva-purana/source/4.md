---
title: "Shiva Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "shiva-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the glory of the Shiva Linga and the twelve Jyotirlingas, the self-manifested lingas of light.

**2** The Shiva Linga is the symbol of the Supreme Lord in His formless aspect. It represents the infinite pillar of light that has no beginning and no end.

**3** The twelve Jyotirlingas are the most sacred Shiva shrines in all of Bharatavarsha. They are self-manifested, not made by human hands, and they shine with the light of Shiva.

**4** Somnath is the first Jyotilinga, established on the western shore. It represents the moon's devotion to Shiva, for Soma is another name for the moon.

**5** Mallikarjuna on the hill of Shri Shaila, Mahakala at Ujjain, Omkareshvara on the island in the Narmada, Kedarnath in the Himalayas — each is a gateway to liberation.

**6** Bhimashankar, Vishwanath at Varanasi, Trimbakeshwar near the source of the Godavari, Vaidyanath, Nageshwar, Rameshwaram, and Ghrishneshwar — these complete the twelve.

**7** He who visits all twelve Jyotirlingas with devotion is freed from all sins and attains the supreme abode of Shiva. Even the remembrance of their names purifies the mind.

**8** The Linga worship is the most ancient and the most universal form of Shiva worship. It transcends all sectarian boundaries, for the Linga is the symbol of the formless Absolute.

**9** The devotee who worships the Linga with the sacred Bilva leaves, with water, milk, and honey, while chanting the Rudra mantras, attains the grace of Shiva.

**10** The Lord said: "I dwell wherever My Linga is worshipped with devotion. Whether it be in a grand temple or a humble shrine, whether it be made of stone or sand — wherever devotion is, there I am."
