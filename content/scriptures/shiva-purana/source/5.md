---
title: "Shiva Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "shiva-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the Rudra, the fierce form of Shiva, and His hymn of praise, the Rudram.

**2** Rudra is the howler, the terrible one, the destroyer of evil. He is the storm, the thunder, the lightning. He is the wrath of righteousness directed against unrighteousness.

**3** Yet Rudra is also the most compassionate. He is the healer, the bestower of medicine, the physician of the soul. He destroys disease and suffering.

**4** The Rudram, the great hymn to Rudra found in the Yajur Veda, is the most powerful of all Vedic chants. It praises Shiva in all His forms, in all His aspects, in all His manifestations.

**5** Rudra is in the fire and in the water, in the plants and in the trees, in the rocks and in the mountains. He is in the animals and in the birds. He is in all that exists.

**6** He is the lord of thieves and the lord of kings. He is the lord of the forest and the lord of the assembly. He is in the highest and the lowest, in the great and the small.

**7** He is the blue-necked one, the thousand-eyed one, the bearer of countless arrows. His wrath is terrible, but His mercy is infinite.

**8** The chanting of the Rudram brings peace, prosperity, and protection. It removes diseases, destroys enemies, and purifies the mind of all impurities.

**9** He who chants the Rudram with devotion and understanding attains the vision of Shiva. He sees Shiva in all things and all things in Shiva.

**10** The Rudram declares: "Namaste Rudra manyave" — "Salutations to Thee, O Rudra, of terrible wrath, and salutations to Thy arrow, and salutations to Thy bow." He who thus worships Rudra is freed from all fear.
