---
title: "Shiva Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "shiva-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the birth of Lord Ganesha, the son of Shiva and Parvati, the remover of obstacles, the lord of beginnings.

**2** Parvati, desiring a guardian for her private chambers, created from the sandalwood paste on her body a beautiful boy and breathed life into him.

**3** She said: "You are my son. Guard this entrance and let no one pass without my permission." The boy took up his post with devotion.

**4** When Shiva returned and sought to enter, the boy barred His way. Shiva, not recognizing the boy, sent His attendants to remove him, but the boy defeated them all.

**5** In anger, Shiva severed the boy's head with His trident. When Parvati learned what had happened, Her grief shook the universe.

**6** Shiva, to restore peace, commanded His attendants to bring the head of the first creature they encountered facing north. They brought the head of an elephant.

**7** Shiva placed the elephant head upon the boy's body and restored him to life. He embraced him as His own son and named him Ganesha, the lord of His hosts.

**8** Shiva declared: "Ganesha shall be worshipped first among all the gods. No ritual, no ceremony, no auspicious undertaking shall begin without first invoking Ganesha."

**9** Ganesha is the remover of obstacles, the patron of learning and wisdom, the god of beginnings. He rides the mouse, which symbolizes the ego that must be conquered.

**10** He who worships Ganesha before any undertaking removes all obstacles from his path. The devotee of Ganesha succeeds in all his endeavours and attains prosperity, wisdom, and liberation.
