---
title: "Shiva Purana - Chapter 6"
scripture: "shiva-purana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the cosmic dance of Shiva, the Tandava, which is the source and dissolution of all creation.

**2** Lord Shiva dances the Ananda Tandava, the dance of bliss, in the hall of consciousness. His dance is the rhythm of the universe, the pulse of creation and destruction.

**3** In His upper right hand He holds the drum, the Damaru, whose beat is the sound of creation — the primordial vibration from which all things arise.

**4** In His upper left hand He holds the flame, Agni, which is the fire of dissolution — the power that consumes the universe at the end of each cycle.

**5** His lower right hand is raised in the Abhaya mudra, the gesture of fearlessness, assuring His devotees that they need not fear.

**6** His lower left hand points downward to His raised foot, indicating the path of liberation — the refuge at the feet of the Lord.

**7** His right foot tramples upon the demon Apasmara, the dwarf of ignorance. He who is crushed by the feet of Shiva is freed from the darkness of delusion.

**8** The ring of fire that surrounds the dancing Shiva represents the cycle of samsara, the wheel of birth and death, within which all beings revolve.

**9** Yet the dancer Himself is unmoved, still, and blissful at the centre. He is the eye of the storm, the calm at the heart of all activity.

**10** He who meditates upon the cosmic dance of Shiva understands the mystery of creation and destruction. He sees that birth and death are but movements of the divine dance, and that the dancer Himself is eternal and free.
