---
title: "Shiva Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "shiva-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Suta said: I shall now describe the marriage of Shiva and Parvati, the most sacred union in all creation.

**2** When Shiva's first consort, Sati, abandoned her body, Shiva withdrew into deep meditation on Mount Kailasa, renouncing all involvement with the world.

**3** Sati was reborn as Parvati, the daughter of Himavan, the king of the mountains. From her earliest childhood, she was devoted to Shiva and resolved to win Him as her husband.

**4** Parvati performed the most severe austerities — standing in water in winter, sitting amidst five fires in summer, eating only fallen leaves, and finally giving up even leaves, subsisting on air alone.

**5** The gods sent Kama, the god of love, to disturb Shiva's meditation and awaken His desire for Parvati. But Shiva opened His third eye and reduced Kama to ashes.

**6** Undeterred, Parvati continued her austerities with even greater intensity. Shiva, moved by her devotion, tested her by appearing as an old Brahmana and speaking ill of Himself.

**7** Parvati's devotion remained unshaken. She said: "Even if Shiva be all that you say, my heart belongs to Him alone. I will worship none other."

**8** Pleased by her unwavering devotion, Shiva revealed His true form and accepted Parvati as His consort. The gods rejoiced, and the universe was filled with the light of their union.

**9** Their marriage was celebrated with the greatest splendour. All the gods, sages, celestial beings, and creatures of every realm attended the wedding of Shiva and Parvati.

**10** This marriage symbolizes the union of consciousness and energy, of the static and the dynamic, of the masculine and the feminine principles of the universe. Without their union, creation cannot proceed.
