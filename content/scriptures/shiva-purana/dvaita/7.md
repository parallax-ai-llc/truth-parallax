## Overview

The Vayaviya Samhita holds unique interest for Dvaita Vedanta because of its prominent role for Vayu. In Madhvacharya's theological system, Vayu (identified with Mukhyaprana and Hanuman) occupies the exalted position of the highest created being after Lakshmi — the primary executor of Vishnu's will and the intermediary between Vishnu and all other beings.

Dvaita interpreters read Vayu's prominence in this Shaiva samhita as confirmation of Vayu's cosmic importance, while arguing that the text's attribution of Vayu's relationship to Shiva is a sectarian misreading. In the Dvaita view, Vayu's primary allegiance is to Vishnu, and his appearance in a Shaiva context reflects the text's failure to recognize the correct cosmic hierarchy.

Madhvacharya himself claimed to be the third avatara of Vayu (after Hanuman and Bhima), giving the Dvaita tradition a personal stake in Vayu theology. The Vayaviya Samhita's treatment of Vayu's cosmic functions thus intersects directly with Dvaita sectarian identity.

## Key Themes

- **Vayu in Dvaita Theology**: The wind god as the highest created being after Lakshmi
- **Madhva as Vayu's Avatar**: The personal connection between the Dvaita founder and Vayu
- **Misattribution**: The samhita's Shaiva framing of Vayu as a sectarian error
- **Cosmic Hierarchy**: Vayu's functions properly understood within the Dvaita taratamya
- **Eschatological Sovereignty**: All dissolution and renewal as governed by Vishnu through his agents

## Key Verses and Passages

### Vayu's True Allegiance

Dvaita interpreters argue that Vayu's cosmic functions described in this samhita actually serve Vishnu's purposes. The samhita's attribution of these functions to Shiva's service represents a sectarian reassignment that Dvaita hermeneutics corrects.

### Madhvacharya's Vayu Identity

Madhvacharya's self-identification as Vayu's third avatara gives the Dvaita tradition a unique personal connection to this samhita's content, while also providing a theological framework for reinterpreting it.

## Commentary Tradition

Dvaita commentary on this samhita is found in the context of Madhvacharya's broader Vayu theology, particularly in his claims to be Vayu's incarnation and in his students' elaboration of Vayu's cosmic role within the taratamya framework.
