## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta engages with the Vidyeshvara Samhita primarily through its hermeneutical framework for handling texts that appear to contradict Vishnu's supremacy. Madhvacharya classified the Shiva Purana as a tamasic Purana, meaning its supremacy claims for Shiva must be reinterpreted in light of what Dvaitins regard as the clearer testimony of sattvic Puranas and the Vedas.

The Dvaita approach does not dismiss the Shiva Purana entirely but applies a principle of hierarchical reading: where different scriptural texts appear to conflict, the clearer and higher-authority texts (the Vedas, sattvic Puranas) take precedence. The Vidyeshvara Samhita's declarations of Shiva's supremacy are thus read as contextual praise (stuti) rather than ontological claims.

Dvaitins acknowledge Shiva's greatness within the hierarchy of beings (taratamya) while insisting that he remains categorically distinct from and subordinate to Vishnu. Shiva occupies the third position in the cosmic hierarchy, after Vishnu and Lakshmi.

## Key Themes

- **Tamasic Classification**: The Shiva Purana as a tamasic text with qualified authority
- **Hierarchical Hermeneutics**: Reading Shaiva claims in light of Vedic Vaishnava teaching
- **Contextual Praise**: Supremacy language as stuti rather than ontological statement
- **Taratamya**: Shiva's exalted but subordinate position in the cosmic hierarchy
- **Anti-Shaiva Polemic**: Systematic arguments against Shaiva supremacy claims

## Key Verses and Passages

### The Cosmic Linga Narrative — Dvaita Reading

Madhvacharya and his followers offer an alternative reading of the cosmic linga story. They argue that Vishnu's apparent failure to find the linga's bottom was voluntary — a deliberate act of respect — while Brahma's claim to have found the top was a lie, demonstrating the difference between truthful Vishnu and prideful Brahma.

### Shiva's Thousand Names

Dvaitins argue that many names attributed to Shiva in this samhita actually refer to Vishnu or describe attributes that properly belong to Vishnu alone.

## Commentary Tradition

Madhvacharya's hermeneutical approach to Shaiva Puranas is developed in his Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya and other works. His student Jayatirtha systematized these methods, while Vyasatirtha applied them in dialectical debate with Shaiva and Advaita opponents.
