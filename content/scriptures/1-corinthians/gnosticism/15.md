## Spiritual Resurrection

1 Corinthians 15 was the most contested chapter between Gnostics and the orthodox church. Paul's declaration that "flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God" (15:50) was the Gnostic proof text par excellence for rejecting physical resurrection. The "spiritual body" (soma pneumatikon, 15:44) is the pneumatic self, liberated from the material body at death.

## The Adam-Christ Typology

Paul's contrast between the "first Adam" (psychikos) and the "last Adam" (pneumatikos, 15:45-49) was mapped directly onto the Gnostic anthropology. The first Adam is the Demiurge's creation, the "man of dust" (15:47); the last Adam is the Pleromatic Christ, the "man of heaven." The pneumatic's destiny is to shed the dusty body and assume the heavenly form.

## The Mystery of Transformation

Paul's "mystery" — "we shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed" (15:51) — was read as describing the pneumatic's transformation at death or at the final consummation of the cosmic drama. The "change" is not the resurrection of the material body but the liberation of the divine spark from its material prison and its return to the Pleroma.

## Victory Through Gnosis

Paul's triumph over death (15:55-57) was read as the pneumatic's victory over the Demiurge and his archons. The "sting of death" is the archontic power that holds spirits captive in matter; the "victory" is the gnosis that breaks this power. Thanks be to God — the true God of the Pleroma — who gives this victory through gnosis.
