## Celibacy and Transcendence

Paul's preference for celibacy (7:7-8, 32-35) was embraced by many Gnostic groups as confirming the superiority of the pneumatic life over material entanglements. Marriage binds the spirit to the material world through sexual reproduction, perpetuating the archons' creation. Celibacy is a form of resistance against the Demiurge's design.

## Marriage as Concession

Paul's acknowledgment that marriage is permissible but not ideal (7:9, 28) was read as his accommodation to the psychic level. The psychics need marriage as a concession to bodily weakness; the pneumatics transcend the need for material union. The Gnostic Gospel of Philip, however, reimagined marriage as a spiritual sacrament (the "bridal chamber") symbolizing the reunion of the divine spark with its Pleromatic counterpart.

## Freedom from Material Conditions

Paul's teaching to remain in one's calling (7:17-24) was interpreted as the pneumatic's indifference to social circumstances. Whether enslaved or free, married or single, the pneumatic's true identity is unaffected by material conditions. These are temporary arrangements within the Demiurge's world that will pass away when the spirit returns to the Pleroma.
