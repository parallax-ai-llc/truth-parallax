## Prophecy as Pneumatic Speech

Paul's preference for prophecy over tongues (14:1-5) was read by Gnostics as affirming the superiority of intelligible gnosis (prophecy) over ecstatic but unintelligible experiences (tongues). The pneumatic teacher speaks clearly about the mysteries of the Pleroma; mere ecstasy without understanding remains at the psychic level.

## Speaking Mysteries in the Spirit

Paul's statement that "one who speaks in a tongue speaks not to men but to God; for no one understands him, but he utters mysteries in the Spirit" (14:2) was cited by Gnostics as evidence that glossolalia involves contact with the divine realm. The "mysteries" spoken are fragments of Pleromatic language, the original tongue of the divine beings.

## Order and the Pneumatic Assembly

Paul's instructions for orderly worship (14:26-40) were read as describing the pneumatic community's liturgical practice. The Gnostic assembly included hymns, teachings, revelations, and secret prayers conducted in a structured manner. The demand for order reflects the Pleromatic harmony that the community seeks to mirror on earth.
