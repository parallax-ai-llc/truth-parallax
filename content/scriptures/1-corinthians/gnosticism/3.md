## The Foundation Beyond the Demiurge

Paul's declaration that "no one can lay a foundation other than Jesus Christ" (3:11) was read by Gnostics as affirming that the true foundation of spiritual life is not the Demiurge's creation or law but the revelation brought by the Pleromatic Christ. The Demiurge built on sand; Christ builds on the eternal reality of the Pleroma.

## Tested by Fire

Paul's image of work tested by fire (3:13-15) was interpreted as the dissolution of material and psychic works at the end of the age, when only pneumatic reality will survive. The "gold, silver, and precious stones" (3:12) represent gnosis; the "wood, hay, and straw" represent the Demiurge's material constructions.

## The Temple of the True God

Paul's statement that "God's Spirit dwells in you" (3:16) was read as describing the pneumatic community in which the divine spark resides. The "temple" is not the Demiurge's Jerusalem temple but the pneumatic body/community that houses the Pleroma's presence. Destroying this temple invites cosmic consequences (3:17).
