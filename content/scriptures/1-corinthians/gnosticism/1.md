## The Wisdom the Archons Cannot Grasp

Paul's statement that "the wisdom of this world is foolishness with God" (cf. 1:20) was read by Gnostics as confirming that the Demiurge's wisdom is inferior. The "wise" and "scribes" and "debaters of this age" (1:20) are the Demiurge's servants, operating within the limited horizon of the material cosmos. The true wisdom belongs to the Pleroma.

## The Cross as Cosmic Event

For Gnostic interpreters, "Christ crucified" (1:23) was not the death of the spiritual Christ but the suffering of the material body that the Aeon Christ temporarily inhabited. The Treatise on the Resurrection (NHC I,4) teaches that the spiritual Christ did not truly suffer; the archons believed they had captured and killed him, but they grasped only his material semblance.

## The Calling of the Pneumatics

Paul's teaching that God chose the foolish and weak (1:27-28) was read as the calling of the pneumatic elect, who appear foolish and weak by worldly standards but possess the divine spark that surpasses all material wisdom and power.
