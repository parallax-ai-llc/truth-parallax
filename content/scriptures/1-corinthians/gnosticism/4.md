## Stewards of the Mysteries

Paul's description of apostles as "stewards of the mysteries of God" (4:1) was read as confirming the existence of secret teachings entrusted to a select few. The Valentinian succession claimed to be the authentic line of these stewards, transmitting gnosis from Paul through Theudas to Valentinus.

## The Spectacle of Suffering

Paul's description of apostles as a "spectacle to the world, to angels, and to men" (4:9) was interpreted cosmically: the pneumatic teachers are on display before the archons (angels) and the material world. Their suffering is the consequence of carrying the light of gnosis through the Demiurge's hostile domain.

## Fathers in Gnosis

Paul's claim to be the Corinthians' spiritual father (4:15) was appropriated by Gnostic teachers as the model for their own authority. The pneumatic teacher begets spiritual children through the transmission of gnosis, just as Paul begot the Corinthians through the gospel.
