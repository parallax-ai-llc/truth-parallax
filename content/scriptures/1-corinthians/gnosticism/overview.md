## Overview

1 Corinthians was arguably the most important Pauline letter for Gnostic interpreters. Paul's distinction between "the wisdom of this age" and "God's wisdom, secret and hidden" (2:6–7) seemed to confirm the Gnostic claim that a deeper, esoteric knowledge existed beyond the public teaching of the church. Valentinian Gnostics in particular treated 1 Corinthians as a key to their entire theological system.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Hidden Wisdom (Chapters 1–2)
Valentinians read Paul's contrast between worldly wisdom and divine wisdom as a distinction between the Demiurge's limited knowledge and the true gnosis of the Pleroma. When Paul says he speaks "wisdom among the mature" (2:6), Gnostics understood "the mature" (*teleioi*) as the pneumatic elect who alone can receive the secret teaching.

### The Spiritual Body (Chapter 15)
Paul's distinction between the "natural body" (*soma psychikon*) and the "spiritual body" (*soma pneumatikon*) in 15:44 was foundational for Gnostic anthropology. Many Gnostics rejected physical resurrection entirely, reading "flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God" (15:50) as proof that salvation involves the liberation of the spirit from the material body, not its reconstitution.

### Psychic vs. Pneumatic
Paul's tripartite language — "the natural person (*psychikos*) does not accept the things of the Spirit" (2:14) — was mapped onto the Valentinian division of humanity into three classes. The psychics (ordinary Christians) live by faith and moral effort; the pneumatics (Gnostics) possess direct spiritual knowledge; the hylics (materialists) are beyond salvation.

## Theological Emphases

- **Secret Wisdom**: 1 Corinthians 2:6–16 as Paul's acknowledgment of esoteric gnosis
- **Spiritual Resurrection Only**: 1 Corinthians 15:50 as rejection of bodily resurrection
- **The Demiurge's Rulers**: "The rulers of this age" (2:8) who crucified Christ as the archons
- **Gnosis over Pistis**: Knowledge (*gnosis*) surpasses mere faith (*pistis*) as the path to salvation
