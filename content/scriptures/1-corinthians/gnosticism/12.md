## Pneumatic Gifts

Paul's list of spiritual gifts (12:4-11) was mapped by Valentinians onto a hierarchy of pneumatic endowments. The "word of wisdom" (12:8) is the highest gift, corresponding to full gnosis; "faith" (12:9) belongs to the psychic level. The distribution of gifts by "one and the same Spirit" (12:11) confirms that the Pleroma's divine power operates diversely within the community.

## The Body and the Pneumatic Community

Paul's body metaphor (12:12-27) was reinterpreted by Gnostics as describing the Pleromatic body, not the institutional church. The true Body of Christ is the community of pneumatics, each carrying a fragment of the divine light. The "weaker members" (12:22) are the psychics who, though less spiritually advanced, play a role in the divine economy.

## The Higher Gifts

Paul's exhortation to "earnestly desire the higher gifts" (12:31) was read as an invitation to ascend from psychic faith to pneumatic gnosis. The "still more excellent way" that Paul promises (12:31b) is not merely love (as the orthodox reading holds) but the path of gnosis that chapter 13 will describe in veiled terms.
