## The Rulers of This Age

Paul's statement that "none of the rulers of this age understood" God's wisdom, "for if they had, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory" (2:8) was the foundational text for Gnostic cosmology. The "rulers" (archontes) are not Roman officials but the cosmic archons who govern the material world. Their crucifixion of Christ was an act of ignorance that paradoxically brought about their own defeat.

## Hidden Wisdom Among the Mature

Paul's claim to speak "wisdom among the mature" (2:6) was the primary Gnostic proof text for the existence of an esoteric teaching reserved for the pneumatic elite. The "mature" (teleioi) are the fully developed pneumatics who can receive the secret gnosis that the psychic Christians cannot comprehend.

## The Psychic and the Pneumatic

Paul's distinction between the "natural person" (psychikos, 2:14) who cannot receive spiritual things and the "spiritual person" (pneumatikos, 2:15) who judges all things was the cornerstone of Valentinian anthropology. This verse established the two-tier church: the psychic majority who live by faith and the pneumatic elite who possess direct gnosis.
