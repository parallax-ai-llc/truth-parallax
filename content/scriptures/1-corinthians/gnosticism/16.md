## The Collection as Pneumatic Solidarity

Paul's instructions about the collection (16:1-4) were read by Gnostics as describing the material support that pneumatic communities provided for one another. The network of Gnostic teachers and communities required financial support, and this passage was cited as apostolic precedent.

## The Chain of Teaching

Paul's commendations of co-workers (16:10-18) were read as identifying members of the pneumatic network through which secret teaching was transmitted. Timothy, Apollos, and the household of Stephanas were seen as links in the chain connecting Paul's gnosis to the Valentinian school.

## Maranatha and the Return to the Pleroma

The Aramaic cry "Maranatha" (16:22) was interpreted not as a prayer for Christ's second coming in the orthodox sense but as an invocation of the Pleromatic Christ's continued presence with the pneumatic community. The "coming" of the Lord is the ongoing descent of gnosis from the Pleroma to the elect, and the final gathering of all pneumatic sparks back to their divine source.
