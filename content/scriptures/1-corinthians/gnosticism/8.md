## Idol Food and Gnosis

Paul's statement that "we know that an idol has no real existence" (8:4) was enthusiastically adopted by Gnostic interpreters. The "knowledge" (gnosis) that idols are nothing confirms the pneumatic's liberation from the Demiurge's false gods. Valentinians, as reported by Irenaeus, freely ate food offered to idols, citing this passage as justification.

## Knowledge That Liberates

Paul's warning that "knowledge puffs up" (8:1) was read by Gnostics not as a critique of gnosis itself but as a warning against incomplete knowledge. True gnosis is accompanied by love because the pneumatic who has achieved full knowledge recognizes the interconnection of all divine sparks. Incomplete gnosis inflates the ego; complete gnosis dissolves it.

## The Strong and the Weak

Paul's concern for the "weak" who might be led astray (8:9-12) was mapped onto the Valentinian distinction between pneumatics and psychics. The pneumatics know that idols are nothing; the psychics are still bound by the Demiurge's prohibitions. Paul accommodates the psychics but does not share their limitations.
