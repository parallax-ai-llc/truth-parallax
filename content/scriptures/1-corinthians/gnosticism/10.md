## The Spiritual Rock

Paul's statement that "the Rock was Christ" (10:4) was read by Gnostics as evidence that the spiritual Christ was secretly present in the Old Testament, working against the Demiurge's intentions. Christ provided "spiritual food" and "spiritual drink" (10:3-4) to nourish the pneumatic seeds hidden among the Israelites, sustaining them until the full revelation of gnosis.

## The Archons' Idols

Paul's warning to "flee from idolatry" (10:14) was interpreted as a warning against worship of the archons, including the Demiurge himself. The "demons" behind the idols (10:20) are the archontic powers who demand worship and sacrifice. The pneumatic who has received gnosis sees through all forms of archontic religion.

## The Cup and the Table

Paul's eucharistic language (10:16-17) was appropriated by Gnostic sacramental practice. The Valentinian Eucharist was understood as a pneumatic rite that nourished the divine spark with spiritual sustenance from the Pleroma, distinct from the psychic church's merely symbolic or material communion.
