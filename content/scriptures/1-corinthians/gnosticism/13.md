## Love and Gnosis

Gnostic interpreters did not dismiss 1 Corinthians 13 but reread it through their system. "Love" is the Pleromatic quality that unites all divine beings. When Paul says that without love he is "nothing" (13:2), the Gnostic reads this as: without participation in the Pleroma's love, even gnosis is incomplete. Love is the medium through which gnosis operates.

## The Mirror and the Face

Paul's statement that "now we see in a mirror dimly, but then face to face" (13:12) was the definitive text for the Gnostic eschatological vision. The "mirror" is the material world, which reflects the Pleroma's reality in distorted form. The "face to face" encounter is the pneumatic's final reunion with the divine source, when the mirror of matter is dissolved and direct knowledge replaces mediated perception.

## The Passing Away of Partial Knowledge

Paul's teaching that "knowledge" will "pass away" (13:8) was interpreted not as the end of gnosis but as the end of partial, mediated knowledge. In the Pleroma, the pneumatic will know fully, even as they are fully known (13:12). The incomplete gnosis of this life will give way to the complete, unmediated knowledge that characterizes life in the divine fullness.
