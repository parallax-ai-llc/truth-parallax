## The Body and the Archons

Paul's statement that "the body is not meant for sexual immorality, but for the Lord" (6:13) was read by Valentinian Gnostics as distinguishing between the material body (which belongs to the archons) and the pneumatic self (which belongs to the true God). The body will be destroyed (6:13b); what matters is the spirit's liberation.

## Members of Christ

Paul's warning about joining "the members of Christ" to immorality (6:15) was interpreted as describing the pneumatic seed's potential contamination through material entanglements. The divine spark, which is Christ's own substance within the believer, must not be subjected to archontic domination through bodily excess.

## The Temple of the Spirit

Paul's declaration that the body is "a temple of the Holy Spirit" (6:19) was read as describing the pneumatic's unique status: the body temporarily houses the divine spark. Unlike the orthodox reading, which sees this as sanctifying the body, the Gnostic reading sees it as explaining why the spirit must eventually be liberated from its bodily temple.
