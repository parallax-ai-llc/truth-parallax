## Pneumatic Freedom and the Body

Some Gnostic groups interpreted Paul's discussion of sexual immorality (5:1-5) differently from the orthodox reading. If the body is merely the Demiurge's creation and the pneumatic self is untouched by material actions, then bodily behavior is spiritually irrelevant. This libertine interpretation was rejected by Valentinians but embraced by certain other Gnostic schools.

## The Leaven of the Demiurge

Paul's leaven metaphor (5:6-8) was read by Gnostics as describing the contaminating influence of the Demiurge's material religion. The "old leaven" is the psychic worship of the creator God; the "unleavened bread of sincerity and truth" is pure gnosis, free from the Demiurge's deceptions.

## Judging by Gnosis

Paul's instruction to judge insiders (5:12) was read as the pneumatic community's right to evaluate members based on their spiritual attainment. Those who persist in ignorance or material attachment may be excluded from the inner circle of gnosis, though their psychic faith might still offer a lesser form of salvation.
