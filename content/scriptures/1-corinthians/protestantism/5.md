## Church Discipline

Paul commands the Corinthian church to expel a man engaged in sexual immorality (5:1-2), delivering him "to Satan for the destruction of the flesh, so that his spirit may be saved" (5:5). Protestant churches have drawn on this passage to develop practices of church discipline, though the precise meaning of "delivering to Satan" remains debated.

## The Leaven of Sin

Paul's metaphor of leaven — "a little leaven leavens the whole lump" (5:6) — underscores the communal consequences of tolerating sin. Protestant ethics emphasizes that individual sin affects the entire body. The exhortation to "cleanse out the old leaven" (5:7) grounds the Reformed practice of church discipline as a mark of the true church.

## Judging Those Inside

Paul's distinction between judging those inside the church and leaving those outside to God's judgment (5:12-13) shapes Protestant ecclesiology's understanding of the church's jurisdiction. The church exercises discipline over its members but does not claim authority over the wider society in the same way.
