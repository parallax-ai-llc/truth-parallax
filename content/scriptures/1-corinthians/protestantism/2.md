## Hidden Wisdom

Paul speaks of "God's wisdom, secret and hidden, which God decreed before the ages" (2:7). Protestant interpreters generally identify this wisdom with the gospel of Christ crucified, not with a separate esoteric teaching. The "mystery" is now revealed through apostolic preaching, accessible to all who receive the Spirit.

## The Natural and Spiritual Person

Paul's distinction between the "natural person" (psychikos) who "does not accept the things of the Spirit of God" (2:14) and the "spiritual person" (pneumatikos) who "judges all things" (2:15) is central to Protestant pneumatology. The Holy Spirit illuminates Scripture for the believer, enabling understanding that unaided reason cannot achieve.

## The Mind of Christ

The chapter concludes with the remarkable claim: "we have the mind of Christ" (2:16). Reformed theology connects this to the internal testimony of the Holy Spirit (testimonium Spiritus Sancti internum), Calvin's doctrine that the Spirit confirms the truth of Scripture in the believer's heart.
