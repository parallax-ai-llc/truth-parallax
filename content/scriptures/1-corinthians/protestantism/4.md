## Servants and Stewards

Paul describes apostles as "servants of Christ and stewards of the mysteries of God" (4:1). Protestant ministry theology emphasizes that pastors are stewards, not masters, of God's word. The Reformation rejected clerical hierarchy in favor of servant leadership modeled on Paul's self-description.

## The Irony of Apostolic Suffering

Paul's biting irony in 4:8-13 — "you have become kings! And would that you did reign, so that we might share the rule with you!" — contrasts the Corinthians' triumphalism with the apostles' humiliation. Protestant theology draws on this to warn against prosperity theology and the confusion of worldly success with spiritual maturity.

## Spiritual Fatherhood

Paul claims to be the Corinthians' "father in Christ Jesus through the gospel" (4:15). Protestant interpreters balance this with the warning against calling anyone "father" (Matthew 23:9), seeing Paul's statement as describing his unique role as church planter rather than establishing a hierarchical office.
