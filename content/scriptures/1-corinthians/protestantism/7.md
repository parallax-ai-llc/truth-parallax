## Marriage and Singleness

Paul treats marriage and singleness as legitimate callings, preferring singleness for its freedom to serve the Lord without distraction (7:32-35) while affirming that marriage is good and that it is "better to marry than to burn with passion" (7:9). Protestant theology, following Luther, has generally elevated marriage over celibacy, in contrast to the medieval Catholic emphasis on virginity.

## The Pauline Privilege

Paul's permission for a believing spouse to allow an unbelieving partner to leave (7:15) — the "Pauline privilege" — has shaped Protestant divorce and remarriage ethics. Most Protestant traditions allow remarriage after abandonment by an unbelieving spouse on the basis of this passage.

## Remain in Your Calling

Paul's principle that "each person should remain in the situation that the Lord has assigned to him" (7:17) has been applied broadly in Protestant theology. Luther's doctrine of vocation (Beruf) — that all legitimate callings are sacred, not just religious ones — draws on this Pauline teaching that God's call meets people where they are.
