## Lawsuits Among Believers

Paul rebukes the Corinthians for taking disputes before secular courts (6:1-8), asking "Is there no one among you wise enough to settle a dispute between the brothers?" (6:5). Protestant churches have historically encouraged mediation and reconciliation within the congregation, though the application of this principle to complex modern legal disputes remains debated.

## The Body as Temple

Paul's declaration that "your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit within you" (6:19) — here addressed to individuals — is foundational for Protestant sexual ethics. The body belongs to God, purchased at the price of Christ's blood (6:20), and is therefore not available for sexual immorality.

## Union with Christ

Paul's argument that "he who is joined to the Lord becomes one spirit with him" (6:17) grounds the Protestant doctrine of union with Christ (unio cum Christo), which Calvin called "the central doctrine of the whole of theology." Sexual immorality is grievous precisely because it involves the members of Christ in a contradictory union.
