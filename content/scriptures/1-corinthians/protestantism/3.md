## The Church's One Foundation

Paul declares that "no one can lay a foundation other than that which is laid, which is Jesus Christ" (3:11). Protestant ecclesiology takes this as axiomatic: the church is built on Christ alone, not on Peter, tradition, or any human institution. This verse was central to Reformation debates about papal authority.

## Building with Gold or Straw

Paul's warning that each person's work will be tested by fire (3:12-15) and that some will be "saved, but only as through fire" (3:15) has been debated between Protestants and Catholics. Protestants generally reject the Catholic use of this passage to support purgatory, reading it instead as referring to the quality of ministry being tested at the final judgment.

## The Temple of the Holy Spirit

Paul's declaration that "you are God's temple and God's Spirit dwells in you" (3:16) uses a plural "you," referring to the community, not just individuals. Protestant ecclesiology draws on this to affirm the corporate indwelling of the Spirit in the gathered church.
