## Head Coverings

Paul's instructions about head coverings in worship (11:2-16) have generated extensive Protestant debate. Some interpreters read the passage as reflecting first-century cultural norms no longer binding; others see it as establishing a permanent principle of gender distinction in worship. The argument from creation order (11:8-9) and the appeal to "nature itself" (11:14) complicate purely cultural readings.

## The Lord's Supper

Paul's account of the institution of the Lord's Supper (11:23-26) — "This is my body... This cup is the new covenant in my blood" — is the earliest written account of the eucharistic words, predating the Gospels. Protestant interpretations divide: Luther held to the real presence of Christ "in, with, and under" the elements; Zwingli read "is" as "signifies"; Calvin taught a spiritual presence received by faith through the Spirit.

## Discerning the Body

Paul's warning about eating and drinking "without discerning the body" (11:29) and the consequence of divine judgment (11:30) underscores the seriousness of communion in Protestant theology. Most Protestant traditions practice some form of self-examination before communion, following Paul's instruction: "Let a person examine himself" (11:28).
