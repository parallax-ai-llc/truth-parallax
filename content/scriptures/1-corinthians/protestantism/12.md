## The Diversity of Gifts

Paul lists spiritual gifts — wisdom, knowledge, faith, healing, miracles, prophecy, discernment, tongues, interpretation (12:8-10) — and insists they come from "the same Spirit" (12:11). Protestant debate centers on whether all these gifts continue today (continuationism) or whether some ceased with the apostolic age (cessationism).

## One Body, Many Members

Paul's body metaphor (12:12-27) is foundational for Protestant ecclesiology. Each member is necessary, no gift is superior, and the body cannot function if any part is missing. This democratic vision of the church challenged medieval hierarchies and continues to shape Protestant congregational life.

## The Greater Gifts

Paul's exhortation to "earnestly desire the higher gifts" (12:31) and his pivot — "And I will show you a still more excellent way" — sets up chapter 13. Protestant interpreters debate whether Paul is ranking gifts (prophecy above tongues) or redirecting attention entirely from gifts to love as the supreme criterion of spiritual maturity.
