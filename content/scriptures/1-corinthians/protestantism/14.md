## Prophecy Over Tongues

Paul's preference for prophecy over tongues (14:1-5) — "the one who prophesies is greater than the one who speaks in tongues, unless someone interprets" — has shaped Protestant worship priorities. Prophecy edifies the whole congregation; uninterpreted tongues edify only the speaker. Most Protestant traditions prioritize intelligible preaching over ecstatic utterance.

## Order in Worship

Paul's instructions for orderly worship (14:26-33) — "all things should be done for building up" (14:26) and "God is not a God of confusion but of peace" (14:33) — have been foundational for Protestant liturgical order. Reformed worship, in particular, emphasizes simplicity, intelligibility, and edification as the criteria for all worship practices.

## Women and Silence

Paul's instruction that "women should keep silent in the churches" (14:34-35) is one of the most debated passages in Protestant exegesis. Egalitarian interpreters argue this is a situational instruction, a later interpolation, or a quotation Paul is refuting. Complementarian interpreters read it as a general principle consistent with 11:2-16 and 1 Timothy 2:11-12.
