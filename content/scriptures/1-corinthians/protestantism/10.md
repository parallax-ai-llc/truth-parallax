## Warnings from Israel's History

Paul recounts Israel's wilderness failures — idolatry, sexual immorality, testing God, grumbling (10:1-10) — as "examples for us" (10:6). Protestant typological reading of the Old Testament draws heavily on this passage, which demonstrates that Israel's history is not merely historical record but divinely intended instruction for the church.

## The Temptation Clause

"God is faithful, and he will not let you be tempted beyond your ability, but with the temptation he will also provide the way of escape" (10:13) is one of the most quoted verses in Protestant pastoral care. It affirms both divine sovereignty and human responsibility in the face of moral struggle.

## The Lord's Table and Idol Feasts

Paul's argument that participation in the Lord's Supper is incompatible with participation in idol feasts (10:14-22) — "you cannot drink the cup of the Lord and the cup of demons" (10:21) — establishes the principle of exclusive loyalty to Christ. Protestant worship theology draws on this to argue for the purity and distinctiveness of Christian worship.
