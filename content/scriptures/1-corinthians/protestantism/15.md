## The Resurrection Gospel

Paul defines the gospel as the death, burial, and resurrection of Christ "in accordance with the Scriptures" (15:3-4) and lists the resurrection appearances. This chapter is the most sustained argument for bodily resurrection in the New Testament and the cornerstone of Protestant apologetics: "if Christ has not been raised, your faith is in vain" (15:17).

## Adam and Christ

Paul's Adam-Christ typology (15:21-22, 45-49) — "as in Adam all die, so also in Christ shall all be made alive" — complements the argument of Romans 5. Protestant covenant theology sees this as establishing the principle of federal headship: Adam's death and Christ's life encompass their respective peoples.

## The Resurrection Body

Paul's distinction between the "natural body" and the "spiritual body" (15:44) and his declaration that "flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God" (15:50) have generated debate. Protestant interpreters generally affirm a transformed physical body, not a disembodied spirit, reading "spiritual" as "animated by the Spirit" rather than "made of spirit."

## Victory over Death

The chapter's triumphant conclusion — "O death, where is your victory? O death, where is your sting?" (15:55) — is among the most celebrated passages in Protestant hymnody and funeral liturgy. Death has been defeated through Christ's resurrection, and believers await the same victory.
