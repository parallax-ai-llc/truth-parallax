## Charity as the Form of Virtues

Catholic moral theology, following Aquinas, identifies the love described in 1 Corinthians 13 with the theological virtue of charity (caritas), which is the "form" of all other virtues. Without charity, even faith and hope are incomplete. This chapter describes not a human emotion but a divinely infused virtue that orders all other goods toward God.

## The Perfection of Love

Paul's description of love's qualities — patient, kind, not envious, not boastful (13:4-7) — provides the portrait of sanctity that the Catholic tradition holds up in the lives of the saints. Hagiography measures holiness against this standard, and the canonization process examines heroic virtue in precisely these terms.

## The Beatific Vision

Paul's statement that "now we see in a mirror dimly, but then face to face" (13:12) is read as anticipating the beatific vision, the direct sight of God that the blessed enjoy in heaven. Catholic eschatology teaches that this face-to-face knowledge of God is the ultimate fulfillment of human existence.
