## The Institution Narrative

Paul's account of the Last Supper (11:23-26) is the earliest written record of the eucharistic words and the foundation of Catholic Mass theology. "This is my body" and "this cup is the new covenant in my blood" are the words of consecration spoken by the priest at every Mass, effecting the transubstantiation of bread and wine into Christ's body and blood.

## Discerning the Body

Paul's warning about eating and drinking "without discerning the body" (11:29) has been interpreted by Catholic theology as requiring both recognition of Christ's real presence in the Eucharist and proper moral disposition. Those in a state of mortal sin must first receive the sacrament of reconciliation.

## Head Coverings and Tradition

Paul's appeal to "the traditions" he handed on (11:2) supports the Catholic understanding of Sacred Tradition as a source of authority alongside Scripture. The specific practice of head coverings has been interpreted as culturally conditioned, but the underlying principle of ordered worship reflecting divine truth remains.
