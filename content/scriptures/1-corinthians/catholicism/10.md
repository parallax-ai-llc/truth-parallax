## Typological Reading

Paul's reading of Israel's wilderness experience as "types" for the church (10:6, 11) supports the Catholic method of typological interpretation. The crossing of the Red Sea prefigures baptism (10:2); the manna and water from the rock prefigure the Eucharist (10:3-4). This sacramental typology is embedded in the Catholic liturgy.

## The Spiritual Rock

Paul's statement that "the Rock was Christ" (10:4) is read by Catholic interpreters as a classic instance of christological typology. Christ was present and active in Israel's history, providing spiritual sustenance that anticipated the sacraments of the New Covenant.

## The Cup of Blessing

Paul's eucharistic language — "The cup of blessing that we bless, is it not a participation in the blood of Christ?" (10:16) — is foundational for Catholic real-presence theology. The word "participation" (koinonia) indicates a real sharing in Christ's body and blood, not merely a memorial or symbol.
