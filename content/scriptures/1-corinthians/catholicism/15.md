## The Bodily Resurrection

Paul's argument for bodily resurrection (15:1-58) is the most important New Testament text for Catholic eschatology regarding the resurrection of the body. The Apostles' Creed's affirmation "I believe in the resurrection of the body" draws directly on Paul's teaching that Christ's resurrection is the "firstfruits" of a universal bodily resurrection (15:20).

## The Glorified Body

Paul's description of the resurrection body — imperishable, glorious, powerful, spiritual (15:42-44) — shapes Catholic teaching on the qualities of the glorified body: impassibility, clarity, agility, and subtlety (Aquinas, Summa Theologiae, Supplement, qq. 82-85).

## Victory Over Death

Paul's triumph — "O death, where is your victory?" (15:55) — is sung in the Catholic Easter Vigil liturgy. The resurrection is not merely an event in Christ's biography but the cosmic defeat of death itself, the foundation of Christian hope that the Catholic funeral liturgy proclaims.
