## The Spirit's Revelation

Paul's teaching that "God has revealed to us through the Spirit" (2:10) the things prepared for those who love God grounds Catholic pneumatology. The Holy Spirit is the agent of revelation, working through Scripture, Tradition, and the Magisterium to guide the Church into all truth (John 16:13).

## Spiritual Discernment

Paul's distinction between the "natural person" (psychikos) and the "spiritual person" (pneumatikos) is read by Catholic theology as describing the difference between unaided reason and reason illuminated by faith. Grace does not destroy nature but perfects it (Aquinas), enabling a depth of understanding inaccessible to philosophy alone.

## The Mind of Christ

The claim that "we have the mind of Christ" (2:16) is understood corporately in Catholic theology: the Church as a whole, guided by the Spirit, possesses the mind of Christ. This grounds the Catholic doctrine of the sensus fidei, the instinct of the faithful as a whole for the truths of faith.
