## Stewards of God's Mysteries

Paul's description of apostles as "stewards of the mysteries of God" (4:1) resonates with Catholic sacramental theology. The ordained minister is a steward of the sacraments, not their master. The "mysteries" (mysteria) are the sacramental rites through which God's grace is communicated.

## Apostolic Authority

Paul's assertion of paternal authority — "I became your father in Christ Jesus through the gospel" (4:15) — supports the Catholic understanding of apostolic authority transmitted through episcopal succession. The bishop stands in a paternal relationship to his flock, continuing the apostolic ministry.

## The Kingdom in Power

Paul's distinction between "talk" and "power" (4:20) echoes the Catholic emphasis on the Church not merely as a teaching institution but as a sacramental reality through which God's power operates. The kingdom of God is present in the sacraments, in the saints, and in the transforming power of grace.
