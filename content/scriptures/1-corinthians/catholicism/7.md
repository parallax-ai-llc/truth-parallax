## Marriage as Sacrament

Paul's teaching on marriage in 1 Corinthians 7 is read alongside Ephesians 5:32 in Catholic sacramental theology. Marriage is a sacrament that signifies the union of Christ and the Church. Paul's practical counsel — "it is better to marry than to burn" (7:9) — is situated within this sacramental framework.

## The Excellence of Virginity

Paul's personal preference for celibacy (7:7-8) and his counsel that "the unmarried woman or virgin is anxious about the things of the Lord" (7:34) has shaped the Catholic tradition of consecrated virginity and religious life. Celibacy is not a rejection of marriage but a prophetic sign of the kingdom where "they neither marry nor are given in marriage" (Matthew 22:30).

## The Indissolubility of Marriage

Paul's instruction that "the wife should not separate from her husband" and "the husband should not divorce his wife" (7:10-11) supports the Catholic teaching on the indissolubility of sacramental marriage. The Pauline privilege (7:15) allows dissolution only in the specific case of a marriage between a baptized and an unbaptized person where the unbeliever departs.
