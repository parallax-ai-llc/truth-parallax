## Prophecy and Edification

Paul's preference for prophecy over tongues (14:1-5) aligns with the Catholic emphasis on intelligible worship that builds up the community. The post-Vatican II liturgical reform, which emphasized the use of vernacular languages, reflects this Pauline principle that worship should be understood by all participants.

## Order in the Liturgy

Paul's insistence that "all things should be done decently and in order" (14:40) supports the Catholic liturgical tradition's emphasis on structured, rubrical worship. The Mass follows a prescribed order (the Roman Missal) that ensures coherence, dignity, and theological integrity.

## The Role of Women

Paul's instruction about women keeping silent (14:34-35) is interpreted in Catholic theology within the broader context of Paul's affirmation of women's active participation in worship (11:5). The Catholic Magisterium has distinguished between the question of women's ordained ministry (reserved to men) and women's full participation in the Church's life and mission.
