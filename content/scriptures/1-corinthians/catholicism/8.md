## Knowledge and Charity

Paul's warning that "knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" (8:1) is a foundational principle in Catholic moral theology. Intellectual understanding of doctrine must be accompanied by charity; otherwise it becomes a source of pride. The Catholic intellectual tradition, from Augustine through Aquinas, insists that theology's purpose is union with God, not merely conceptual mastery.

## The Weak Conscience

Paul's concern for those whose conscience is weak regarding idol food (8:7-13) informs Catholic teaching on the formation of conscience and the sin of scandal. Leading another into sin through one's exercise of freedom is a serious moral failure.

## Christ, Through Whom All Things Exist

Paul's Christological statement — "one Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom are all things and through whom we exist" (8:6) — is cited in Catholic theology as an affirmation of Christ's role in creation, connecting Pauline Christology to the Johannine prologue and the Nicene Creed.
