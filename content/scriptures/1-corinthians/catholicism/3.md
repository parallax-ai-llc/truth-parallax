## The One Foundation

Paul's declaration that "no one can lay a foundation other than Jesus Christ" (3:11) is affirmed by Catholic theology while also maintaining that Christ established Peter as the visible head of the Church (Matthew 16:18). The foundation is Christ; the structure built upon it includes the Petrine office and apostolic succession.

## Tested by Fire

Paul's statement that each person's work will be "revealed by fire" and that some will be "saved, but only as through fire" (3:13-15) is cited by Catholic theology in support of the doctrine of purgatory. The Council of Florence (1439) and the Council of Trent drew on this passage to teach that the souls of the just undergo purification after death.

## The Temple of God

Paul's affirmation that "you are God's temple" (3:16) is read in Catholic ecclesiology as referring to both the local community and the universal Church. The indwelling Spirit sanctifies the community gathered around the Eucharist, making the Church the living temple of God.
