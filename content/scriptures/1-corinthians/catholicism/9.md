## The Right to Support

Paul's argument that those who preach the gospel "should get their living by the gospel" (9:14) supports the Catholic practice of providing for the clergy. The analogy to the Temple priesthood (9:13) is read as establishing continuity between the Old Testament priesthood and Christian ordained ministry.

## Self-Denial for the Gospel

Paul's self-discipline — "I pommel my body and subdue it" (9:27) — resonates with the Catholic ascetical tradition. Fasting, abstinence, and voluntary mortification are understood as participation in Paul's apostolic discipline, training the body for the service of God.

## All Things to All People

Paul's missionary adaptability (9:19-23) grounds the Catholic principle of inculturation: the gospel can and must be expressed in the cultural forms of every people, without compromising its essential content. This principle guided Catholic missionary activity from the Jesuit missions to the Second Vatican Council.
