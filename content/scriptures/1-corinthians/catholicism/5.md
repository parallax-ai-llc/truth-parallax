## Excommunication and Healing

Paul's command to expel the immoral man (5:1-5) grounds the Catholic practice of excommunication as a medicinal penalty aimed at the sinner's repentance. The phrase "deliver this man to Satan for the destruction of the flesh, so that his spirit may be saved" (5:5) is read as combining severity with mercy.

## The Paschal Lamb

Paul's declaration that "Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed" (5:7) connects the Eucharist to the Jewish Passover and to Christ's death. Catholic liturgical theology sees every Mass as the re-presentation of this sacrifice, with Christ as the true Paschal lamb.

## Purity of the Community

Paul's exhortation to "celebrate the festival... with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth" (5:8) underscores the Catholic emphasis on the moral condition of the community gathered for worship. The requirement of examining one's conscience before communion (cf. 11:28) flows from this principle of communal purity.
