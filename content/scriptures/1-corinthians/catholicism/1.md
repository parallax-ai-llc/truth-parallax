## Unity in the Church

Paul's rebuke of factions in Corinth speaks directly to Catholic ecclesiology's emphasis on visible church unity. The question "Is Christ divided?" (1:13) undergirds the Catholic understanding that schism is a grave sin against the body of Christ. The Church's unity is not merely spiritual but institutional, sacramental, and visible.

## The Wisdom of the Cross

Paul's contrast between "the wisdom of the world" and "the foolishness of God" (1:25) is read by Catholic theologians through the lens of the cross as both scandal and salvation. The crucifixion is the supreme act of divine wisdom, revealing a God who saves through self-giving love rather than coercive power.

## Baptism and Belonging

Paul's references to baptism (1:13-17) establish that believers belong to Christ, not to the minister who baptized them. Catholic sacramental theology affirms that baptism's efficacy does not depend on the minister's holiness (ex opere operato), a principle already implicit in Paul's argument.
