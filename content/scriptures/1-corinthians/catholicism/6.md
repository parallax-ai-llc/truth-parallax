## The Body Belongs to the Lord

Paul's insistence that "the body is not meant for sexual immorality, but for the Lord" (6:13) is central to Catholic moral theology on chastity. The body has a theological dignity: it is destined for resurrection (6:14) and is a member of Christ (6:15).

## The Body as Temple

Paul's declaration that "your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit" (6:19) grounds the Catholic theology of the body elaborated by John Paul II in his Theology of the Body catecheses. The body is sacred because it is indwelt by the Spirit and destined for glory.

## Bought with a Price

Paul's reminder that "you were bought with a price" (6:20) connects bodily ethics to the redemption accomplished by Christ. Catholic moral theology teaches that sexual ethics are not arbitrary rules but flow from the dignity conferred on the body by creation and redemption.
