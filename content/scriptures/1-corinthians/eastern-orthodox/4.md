## Apostolic Humility

Paul's self-description as "the scum of the world, the refuse of all things" (4:13) embodies the kenotic humility that Orthodox theology prizes. The true apostle is marked not by power and prestige but by voluntary self-emptying in imitation of Christ's own kenosis (Philippians 2:7).

## Spiritual Fatherhood

Paul's claim to be the Corinthians' "father in Christ Jesus" (4:15) resonates with the Orthodox tradition of spiritual fatherhood (startsy/geronda). The spiritual father guides disciples through personal relationship, prayer, and shared life, not merely through institutional authority.

## The Kingdom in Power

Paul's statement that "the kingdom of God does not consist in talk but in power" (4:20) aligns with the Orthodox emphasis on the transformative power of the sacraments and the energies of the Holy Spirit. The kingdom is present reality, experienced in liturgy, prayer, and holy living.
