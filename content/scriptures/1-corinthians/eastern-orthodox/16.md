## The Collection and Koinonia

Paul's instructions about the collection for Jerusalem (16:1-4) embody the koinonia (communion) that binds local churches together. Orthodox ecclesiology understands each local church as fully the Church, yet also as existing in communion with all other local churches through shared faith, sacraments, and mutual support.

## The Kiss of Peace

Paul's instruction to "greet one another with a holy kiss" (16:20) is preserved in the Orthodox liturgy's exchange of the Kiss of Peace before the anaphora. This liturgical gesture expresses the reconciliation and mutual love that must precede the offering of the Eucharist.

## Maranatha

The Aramaic cry "Maranatha" — "Our Lord, come!" (16:22) — expresses the eschatological longing that pervades Orthodox worship. Every Divine Liturgy anticipates the second coming, and the Eucharist is understood as a foretaste of the messianic banquet in the age to come.
