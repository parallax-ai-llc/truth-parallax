## Love as the Path of Theosis

Orthodox theology reads 1 Corinthians 13 as describing the ascent of the soul toward God through love. The qualities of love — patience, kindness, humility, endurance (13:4-7) — are the virtues cultivated through the ascetic life, and they mark the progressive transformation of the person into the likeness of Christ.

## The Passing Away of Partial Knowledge

Paul's teaching that prophecy, tongues, and knowledge will "pass away" (13:8-10) is read in light of the eschatological vision: in the age to come, the partial and mediated knowledge of this life will give way to direct, face-to-face communion with God. This is the Orthodox understanding of the beatific vision.

## The Greatest Is Love

Paul's climactic declaration that "the greatest of these is love" (13:13) is the foundation of Orthodox ethical theology. Love is not an emotion but the very nature of God (1 John 4:8), and participation in divine love through theosis is the ultimate purpose of human existence.
