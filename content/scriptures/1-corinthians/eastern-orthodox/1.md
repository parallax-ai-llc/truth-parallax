## The Scandal of Division

Chrysostom's homilies on 1 Corinthians 1 treat the Corinthian divisions as a failure of humility and love. The factions around Paul, Apollos, and Cephas reflect the human tendency to elevate personalities over Christ. Orthodox ecclesiology, grounded in conciliarity (sobornost), insists that the Church's unity is rooted in Christ and expressed through the common faith, not through loyalty to particular leaders.

## The Wisdom of the Cross

Paul's theology of the cross (1:18-25) resonates with the Orthodox emphasis on kenosis, God's self-emptying love. The cross is not merely a legal mechanism for satisfying divine justice but the supreme revelation of divine love: God enters the depths of human suffering and death to overcome them from within.

## The Calling of the Lowly

Paul's observation that God chose the weak and foolish (1:27-29) aligns with the Orthodox tradition's celebration of holy fools (yurodivy), whose apparent foolishness reveals divine wisdom that confounds worldly calculation.
