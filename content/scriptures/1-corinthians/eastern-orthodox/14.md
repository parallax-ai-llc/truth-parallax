## Prophecy and Edification

Paul's preference for prophecy over tongues (14:1-5) aligns with the Orthodox liturgical tradition's emphasis on intelligible worship. The Divine Liturgy is conducted in the language of the people (a principle established by Cyril and Methodius in the Slavic mission), ensuring that worship is accessible and edifying.

## Order and Peace

Paul's insistence that "God is not a God of confusion but of peace" (14:33) grounds the Orthodox liturgical order (typikon). The detailed rubrics of Orthodox worship are not legalism but an expression of the divine harmony that worship should reflect.

## All Things for Building Up

Paul's summary principle — "Let all things be done for building up" (14:26) — governs the Orthodox approach to worship, preaching, and pastoral care. The criterion for all ecclesial activity is its capacity to build up the Body of Christ and draw people toward communion with God.
