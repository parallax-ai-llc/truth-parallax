## Marriage and Monasticism

Orthodox theology reads 1 Corinthians 7 as establishing two equally honored paths: marriage and monasticism. Paul's preference for celibacy (7:7-8) grounds the monastic tradition, while his affirmation of marriage (7:2-5) prevents any denigration of the married state. Both paths lead to theosis.

## The Sacrament of Marriage

Paul's instructions on marriage (7:10-16) are read in the context of the Orthodox sacrament of crowning (wedding). The crowns placed on the couple symbolize both royal dignity and martyrdom — the self-sacrifice that marriage demands. The indissolubility of marriage is affirmed, though Orthodox canon law permits remarriage in cases of pastoral necessity.

## Remaining in One's Calling

Paul's principle of remaining in the state where one was called (7:17-24) resonates with the Orthodox understanding that holiness is possible in every situation. The liturgical sanctification of daily life — through blessings, prayers, and the observance of fasts and feasts — transforms every condition into an opportunity for communion with God.
