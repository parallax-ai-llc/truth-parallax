## The Divine Liturgy's Foundation

Paul's institution narrative (11:23-26) is the earliest witness to the eucharistic tradition and the foundation of the Divine Liturgy. The anaphora (eucharistic prayer) of St. John Chrysostom incorporates Paul's words, and the entire liturgical action is understood as the community's participation in Christ's sacrifice.

## The Awesome Mystery

Paul's warning about unworthy communion (11:27-32) reflects the Orthodox understanding of the Eucharist as a phrikton mysterion (awesome mystery). The faithful prepare through fasting, confession, and prayer. The pre-communion prayers express both longing and trembling before the gift of Christ's body and blood.

## Tradition Received and Handed On

Paul's statement "I received from the Lord what I also delivered to you" (11:23) is foundational for the Orthodox understanding of Sacred Tradition (paradosis). The faith is not invented but received and transmitted, a living chain connecting the contemporary Church to the apostolic community.
