## The Body's Destiny

Paul's teaching that "God raised the Lord and will also raise us up by his power" (6:14) grounds the Orthodox reverence for the human body. The body is not a disposable shell but destined for resurrection and transfiguration. Orthodox veneration of relics flows from this conviction.

## The Temple of the Holy Spirit

Paul's declaration that "your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit" (6:19) aligns with the Orthodox emphasis on the sanctification of the whole person — body, soul, and spirit. Ascetic practices (fasting, prostrations, vigils) involve the body precisely because the body participates in holiness.

## Union with Christ

Paul's warning against joining "the members of Christ" to immorality (6:15) reflects the Orthodox understanding that through baptism and Eucharist, the believer is physically united with Christ. Sexual sin is not merely a moral failure but a violation of the sacramental union with Christ's body.
