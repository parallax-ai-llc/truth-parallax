## The Gifts of the Spirit

Paul's enumeration of charisms (12:4-11) is read in Orthodox theology as describing the Spirit's diverse energies (energeiai) operating within the Body. The gifts are not possessions of individuals but manifestations of the Spirit for the common good (12:7).

## The Body of Christ

Paul's body metaphor (12:12-27) is the foundation for the Orthodox theology of the Church as a living organism, animated by the Holy Spirit and structured by the liturgical and canonical tradition. No member is dispensable; the diversity of gifts serves the unity of the whole.

## Conciliarity

Paul's vision of the body where "God has so composed the body... that there may be no division" (12:24-25) grounds the Orthodox principle of conciliarity (sobornost). Decisions in the Church are made through conciliar processes — local synods, ecumenical councils — not through papal fiat or individual authority.
