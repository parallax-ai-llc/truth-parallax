## Baptismal and Eucharistic Typology

Paul's reading of the Exodus — Israel "baptized into Moses in the cloud and in the sea" (10:2) and eating "spiritual food" and drinking "spiritual drink" (10:3-4) — grounds the Orthodox sacramental reading of the Old Testament. The liturgical typology of the Divine Liturgy draws on these Pauline parallels.

## The Rock Was Christ

Paul's identification of the rock as Christ (10:4) is a foundational text for Orthodox Christological typology. Christ was present throughout Israel's history, providing sustenance that anticipated the Eucharist. This retroactive presence of Christ in the Old Testament is central to Orthodox biblical interpretation.

## Participation in the Body

Paul's eucharistic language — "the cup of blessing" as "participation in the blood of Christ" (10:16) — is the basis for Orthodox real-presence theology. The bread and wine are truly transformed into Christ's body and blood through the invocation of the Holy Spirit (epiclesis), and communicants truly participate in Christ's risen life.
