## Love Over Knowledge

Paul's principle that "knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" (8:1) is a cornerstone of Orthodox spiritual theology. The Fathers warn against intellectual pride (the most dangerous form of prelest or spiritual delusion) and insist that true theology is inseparable from prayer and love.

## The Weak Brother

Paul's concern for the weak conscience (8:7-13) aligns with the Orthodox emphasis on bearing one another's burdens. The stronger Christian's freedom must yield to love, just as the experienced monk adjusts his practice for the sake of a struggling brother.

## One God, One Lord

Paul's confessional statement — "one God, the Father... and one Lord, Jesus Christ" (8:6) — is foundational for the Trinitarian theology developed by the Cappadocian Fathers. This verse was crucial in the Arian controversy, affirming Christ's role in creation and his unity with the Father.
