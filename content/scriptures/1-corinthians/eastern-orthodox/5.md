## Discipline and Repentance

Paul's command to expel the immoral man (5:1-5) is understood in Orthodox canonical practice as aimed at repentance and restoration. The canonical penances prescribed by the Fathers — temporary exclusion from communion — serve a therapeutic purpose: healing the sinner and protecting the community.

## Christ Our Pascha

Paul's declaration that "Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed" (5:7) connects directly to the Orthodox celebration of Pascha, the feast of feasts. The Paschal Troparion — "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death" — expresses the victory that Paul's Passover language anticipates.

## Purity of the Community

The exhortation to "cleanse out the old leaven" (5:7) is enacted liturgically in the Orthodox preparation for Pascha, when Great Lent functions as a period of communal and individual purification. The community prepares itself to celebrate the resurrection with "the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth" (5:8).
