## Christ Is Risen

Romans 15 is the Paschal chapter par excellence. The declaration that "Christ has been raised from the dead" (15:20) is the foundation of Orthodoxy's central celebration: Pascha. Chrysostom's Paschal Homily, read at every Orthodox Easter service, draws directly on this chapter's triumph over death: "O death, where is your sting? O Hades, where is your victory?" (15:55).

## The Cosmic Scope of Resurrection

Paul's teaching that "as in Adam all die, so also in Christ shall all be made alive" (15:22) is read by Orthodox theology as describing a cosmic event. Christ's resurrection is not merely the resuscitation of one man but the defeat of death as a cosmic power. All creation participates in this victory.

## The Spiritual Body

Paul's description of the resurrection body (15:42-49) — imperishable, glorious, powerful, spiritual — describes what Orthodox theology calls the transfigured body. The resurrection is not escape from the body but its transformation into a mode of existence fully permeated by the Holy Spirit.
