## Charity and Zakah

Paul's instructions about the collection for Jerusalem (16:1-4) parallel the Islamic institution of zakah (obligatory charity), one of the five pillars of Islam. Both Paul and Islam emphasize systematic, organized giving for the benefit of those in need. The Quran specifies categories of recipients for zakah (9:60), demonstrating the same concern for structured charitable distribution.

## Community Solidarity

Paul's commendations and travel plans (16:5-18) reveal networks of mutual support similar to those in the early Muslim ummah. The Islamic principle of tawasul (mutual consultation and support) and the obligation of hospitality to travelers reflect the same communal bonds Paul describes.

## Final Greetings

Paul's closing — "If anyone has no love for the Lord, let him be accursed. Maranatha!" (16:22) — expresses the seriousness of commitment to God that Islam shares. Islam similarly warns of consequences for those who reject God's message: "Indeed, those who disbelieve after their belief and then increase in disbelief — never will their repentance be accepted" (3:90). Both traditions demand wholehearted devotion.
