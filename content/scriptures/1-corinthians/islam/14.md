## Intelligible Worship

Paul's preference for intelligible prophecy over unintelligible tongues (14:1-19) aligns with the Islamic emphasis on the clarity and comprehensibility of worship. The Quran describes itself as "a clear Arabic Quran" (12:2) and emphasizes that God's message is meant to be understood. Islamic worship prioritizes recitation of the Quran and comprehensible supplication.

## Order in Worship

Paul's insistence on orderly worship (14:33, 40) resonates with the structured nature of Islamic prayer (salah). The five daily prayers follow a precise sequence of movements and recitations, and the Friday congregational prayer (Jumu'ah) has a prescribed format. Both Paul and Islam insist that worship should be conducted with reverence and order.

## Community Guidance

Paul's instruction that "all things should be done for building up" (14:26) parallels the Islamic principle that religious practices serve the maslaha (public interest) of the community. Islamic jurisprudence evaluates practices by their benefit to the community, just as Paul evaluates worship practices by their capacity to edify.
