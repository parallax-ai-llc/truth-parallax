## The Cross and Islamic Theology

Paul's declaration that "we preach Christ crucified" (1:23) directly engages the Quranic denial of the crucifixion: "They did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but it was made to appear so to them" (4:157). The entire soteriological framework of 1 Corinthians — salvation through the cross — is rejected by Islam as built on a false premise. God's power is demonstrated not in weakness but in divine sovereignty and protection of His prophets.

## Divine Wisdom

Paul's contrast between divine and worldly wisdom (1:18-25) resonates with the Quranic emphasis on divine wisdom (hikmah) that surpasses human understanding. The Quran describes itself as "the Wise Book" (Kitab al-Hakim, 10:1). Islam shares the conviction that God's wisdom confounds human expectations, but locates that wisdom in the Quran's guidance, not in the paradox of a crucified messiah.

## Humility Before God

Paul's observation that God chose the weak and foolish (1:27-29) aligns with the Quranic teaching that worldly status means nothing before God: "Indeed, the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous" (49:13). Both traditions affirm that divine favor is not determined by social position.
