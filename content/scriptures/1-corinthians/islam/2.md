## Divine Revelation

Paul's teaching that God reveals hidden wisdom through the Spirit (2:10) parallels the Islamic understanding of wahy (divine revelation) communicated through the angel Jibril (Gabriel) to the prophets. Islam affirms that God reveals what is beyond human knowledge but through prophetic revelation, not through an indwelling Spirit in individual believers.

## The Limits of Human Reason

Paul's argument that the "natural person does not accept the things of the Spirit of God" (2:14) resonates with the Islamic recognition that human reason alone cannot attain full knowledge of God. The Quran teaches: "They do not encompass Him in knowledge" (20:110). Both traditions affirm the necessity of divine revelation to supplement human reason.

## Hidden Wisdom

Paul's reference to wisdom "hidden" and "decreed before the ages" (2:7) invites comparison with the Islamic concept of al-lawh al-mahfuz (the Preserved Tablet) on which God's decrees are written. Divine wisdom is eternally established and revealed to humanity according to God's will and timing.
