## The Sanctity of the Body

Paul's teaching that "your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit" (6:19) resonates with Islamic teaching on the body's dignity. Islam teaches that the human body is an amanah (trust) from God and must be treated with respect. Harming one's own body is prohibited, and physical purity is a prerequisite for prayer (wudu).

## Sexual Ethics

Paul's condemnation of sexual immorality (6:13-18) aligns closely with Islamic moral teaching. The prohibition of zina is one of the Quran's clearest moral commands, reinforced by extensive hadith literature. Both Paul and Islam treat the body as belonging to God and sexual misconduct as a violation of that divine ownership.

## Bought with a Price

Paul's statement that "you were bought with a price" (6:20), while grounded in Christological atonement that Islam rejects, expresses a principle Islam shares: human beings belong to their Creator and are accountable to Him for how they use their bodies and their lives.
