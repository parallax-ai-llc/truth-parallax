## Love and Rahma

Paul's hymn to love (13:1-13) resonates deeply with the Islamic concept of rahma (mercy, compassion), one of God's most frequently invoked attributes. The qualities Paul attributes to love — patience, kindness, absence of envy and arrogance (13:4-7) — overlap substantially with the Islamic virtues of sabr (patience), ihsan (excellence), and tawadu (humility).

## Love Over Knowledge

Paul's principle that "knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" (8:1, echoed in 13:2) aligns with the Sufi emphasis that ilm (knowledge) without mahabba (love) is spiritually barren. The great Sufi master Rumi taught that love is the bridge between the human and the divine, a sentiment Paul would recognize.

## The Endurance of Love

Paul's declaration that "love never ends" (13:8) resonates with the Quranic teaching about the permanence of God's mercy. The Quran's most repeated phrase — Bismillah al-Rahman al-Rahim — places mercy and compassion at the center of all divine action. Both traditions affirm that love and mercy are eternal qualities reflecting God's own nature.
