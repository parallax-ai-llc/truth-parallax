## Prophetic Suffering

Paul's description of apostolic suffering (4:9-13) resonates with the Quranic narrative of prophets who endured persecution. The Quran speaks of prophets being rejected, mocked, and killed (2:87, 3:183). Islam honors the patience (sabr) of the prophets in the face of opposition as a model for believers.

## Stewardship and Accountability

Paul's concept of apostles as "stewards of the mysteries of God" (4:1) parallels the Islamic understanding of prophets as trustees (umana) of God's message. Each prophet was entrusted with a revelation and held accountable for its faithful transmission. The concept of amanah (trust) is central to Islamic theology.

## Humility Over Boasting

Paul's rebuke of Corinthian boasting (4:7-8) aligns with the Quranic condemnation of pride (kibr) and self-glorification. The Prophet Muhammad taught: "No one who has the weight of a mustard seed of arrogance in his heart will enter Paradise" (Sahih Muslim). Both Paul and Islam see humility before God as essential to authentic faith.
