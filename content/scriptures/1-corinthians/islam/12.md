## Unity of the Ummah

Paul's body metaphor (12:12-27) parallels the Islamic concept of the ummah as a single body. The Prophet Muhammad said: "The believers are like one body; when one limb ails, the whole body responds with fever and sleeplessness" (Sahih Muslim). Both traditions envision the community of faith as an organic unity.

## Diversity of Gifts

Paul's teaching about diverse gifts serving one purpose (12:4-11) resonates with the Islamic understanding that God has given different abilities and callings to different people. The Quran states: "We have raised some of them above others in degrees so that some may take others in service" (43:32). Diversity serves the community's needs.

## Mutual Dependence

Paul's insistence that "the eye cannot say to the hand, 'I have no need of you'" (12:21) aligns with the Islamic ethic of mutual support and the prohibition of arrogance. The Quran teaches: "Cooperate in righteousness and piety" (5:2). Both traditions reject self-sufficiency and emphasize interdependence within the community.
