## Modesty in Worship

Paul's instructions about head coverings (11:2-16) resonate with the Islamic emphasis on modesty (haya) and hijab in worship and public life. The Quran instructs believing women to "draw their headcovers over their chests" (24:31) and to "bring down over themselves part of their outer garments" (33:59). Both Paul and Islam connect bodily modesty to worship and divine order.

## Communal Meals and Equality

Paul's critique of inequality at the Lord's Supper (11:17-22) echoes Islamic teachings about equality in worship and communal meals. The Prophet Muhammad emphasized that all believers are equal before God regardless of social status. The Islamic practice of communal meals during Ramadan (iftar) embodies this principle.

## Ritual and Remembrance

Paul's eucharistic instruction to "do this in remembrance of me" (11:24-25) employs a concept Islam understands differently. Islam has its own practices of remembrance (dhikr) — the remembrance of God through prayer, recitation of the Quran, and invocation of divine names — but does not practice a ritual meal centered on a human figure.
