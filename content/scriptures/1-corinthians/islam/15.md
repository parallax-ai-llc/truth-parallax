## Bodily Resurrection

Paul's argument for bodily resurrection (15:1-58) engages a doctrine Islam strongly affirms. Belief in the Day of Resurrection (Yawm al-Qiyamah) is one of the six articles of Islamic faith. The Quran is emphatic: "Does man think that We will not assemble his bones? Yes, We are able to proportion even his fingertips" (75:3-4). Islam and Paul agree that the dead will be raised bodily.

## Resurrection Without the Cross

While Islam affirms bodily resurrection, it rejects Paul's argument that human resurrection depends on Christ's resurrection (15:13-17). In Islamic theology, resurrection is an act of God's power (qudrah) on the Day of Judgment, not contingent on any prior event. God, who created humanity from nothing, has the power to raise the dead directly.

## Victory Over Death

Paul's triumphant declaration over death (15:55-57) resonates with the Quranic promise of Paradise (Jannah) for the righteous. Islam affirms that death is not the end and that the faithful will enjoy eternal life. However, this victory is achieved through God's mercy and the individual's faith and deeds, not through the mechanism of Christ's resurrection.
