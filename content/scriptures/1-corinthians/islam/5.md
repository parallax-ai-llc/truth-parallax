## Sexual Morality

Paul's condemnation of sexual immorality (5:1-2) aligns with Islamic moral teaching. Islam strictly prohibits zina (fornication and adultery), considering it among the major sins. The Quran commands: "Do not approach unlawful sexual intercourse" (17:32). Both Paul and the Quran treat sexual morality as a matter of communal, not merely individual, concern.

## Community Purity

Paul's insistence on removing the immoral person from the community (5:9-13) parallels Islamic principles of enjoining good and forbidding evil (al-amr bil-ma'ruf wan-nahy an al-munkar). The community has a collective responsibility to maintain moral standards, a principle fundamental to Islamic social ethics.

## The Leaven Metaphor

Paul's warning that "a little leaven leavens the whole lump" (5:6) expresses a principle Islam affirms: individual sin corrupts the community. The Prophet Muhammad compared the community to passengers on a ship: if one person drills a hole in the hull, all are endangered. Collective moral responsibility is a shared value.
