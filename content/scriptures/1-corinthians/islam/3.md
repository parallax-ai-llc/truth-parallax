## Building on God's Foundation

Paul's metaphor of building on a foundation (3:10-15) resonates with the Quranic teaching about the importance of building one's life on the foundation of taqwa (God-consciousness): "Which is better: one who founds his building on God-consciousness and His pleasure, or one who founds it on the brink of a crumbling cliff?" (9:109).

## The Community as Sacred Space

Paul's designation of the community as "God's temple" (3:16) parallels the Islamic understanding of the mosque and the community of believers as sacred spaces where God is worshipped. The Quran states: "The mosques are for Allah alone" (72:18). Both traditions see the gathered community as a place of divine presence.

## Accountability

Paul's warning that each person's work will be tested (3:13-15) aligns with the Islamic teaching of individual accountability on the Day of Judgment. The Quran states: "Every soul will be compensated for what it earned" (3:25). Both Paul and the Quran emphasize that actions have eternal consequences.
