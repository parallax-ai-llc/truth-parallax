## Idol Food and Tawhid

Paul's discussion of food offered to idols (8:1-13) engages directly with the Islamic prohibition of food dedicated to other than God. The Quran states: "Forbidden to you are dead animals, blood, the flesh of swine, and that which has been dedicated to other than Allah" (5:3). Islam's position is absolute: food offered to idols is haram (forbidden) regardless of the eater's knowledge or intention.

## The Oneness of God

Paul's declaration that "there is no God but one" (8:4) is affirmed enthusiastically by Islamic theology. The shahada — "There is no god but Allah" — is the foundation of Islam. However, Paul's addition of "one Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom are all things" (8:6) introduces a Christological element that Islam sees as compromising pure monotheism.

## Conscience and Community

Paul's concern for the impact of one's actions on others (8:9-13) resonates with the Islamic principle of communal responsibility. Islam teaches that believers are "mirrors for one another" and that leading another into sin is a serious offense. The principle of protecting the weaker member is shared.
