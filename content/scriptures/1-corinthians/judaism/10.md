## Exodus Typology

Paul's reading of Israel's wilderness experience as "types" for the church (10:1-11) employs a method familiar in Jewish interpretation. Midrashic literature regularly draws moral lessons from the Exodus narrative. However, Paul's specifically Christological reading — "the Rock was Christ" (10:4) — goes beyond Jewish typological practice by inserting Christ into Israel's sacred history.

## Idolatry Warnings

Paul's warnings against idolatry (10:7, 14) echo the centrality of the prohibition of avodah zarah in Jewish law. The Talmud treats idolatry as one of the three cardinal sins for which a Jew must die rather than transgress (Sanhedrin 74a). Jewish readers recognize Paul's anti-idolatry urgency as thoroughly Jewish.

## The Cup and the Table

Paul's eucharistic language (10:16-21) invites comparison with the Jewish kiddush (sanctification over wine) and the Passover seder meal. The cup of blessing (kos shel berakhah) is a Jewish liturgical term. Paul reinterprets Jewish table fellowship in Christological terms, but the ritual framework remains recognizably Jewish.
