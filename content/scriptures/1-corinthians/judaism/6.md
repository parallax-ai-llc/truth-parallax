## Lawsuits and Jewish Courts

Paul's rebuke of Christians taking disputes to secular courts (6:1-8) reflects Jewish practice. Jewish communities maintained their own courts (batei din) for settling disputes among members. The Talmud strongly discourages recourse to non-Jewish courts (arkaot shel goyim, Gittin 88b). Paul's instruction follows this established Jewish communal norm.

## The Body and Holiness

Paul's teaching about the body's sanctity (6:12-20) has parallels in Jewish thought about the body as God's creation deserving respect. The rabbinic concept of sh'mirat ha-guf (guarding the body) treats physical self-care as a religious obligation. The body belongs to its Creator and must be used in accordance with divine purposes.

## Sexual Ethics and Halakhah

Paul's condemnation of sexual immorality echoes the extensive halakhic traditions governing sexual conduct in Judaism. The arayot (forbidden sexual relationships) of Leviticus 18 form the basis for both Jewish and Pauline sexual ethics. Jewish scholars note substantial overlap while observing differences in theological framing.
