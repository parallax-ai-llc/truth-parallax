## The Temple Metaphor

Paul's designation of the community as "God's temple" (3:16) draws on Jewish theology of the Temple as God's dwelling place. In the post-Temple period, rabbinic Judaism similarly spiritualized Temple language: "When ten sit together and occupy themselves with Torah, the Shekhinah rests among them" (Avot 3:6). Both Paul and the rabbis transferred Temple theology to the community of the faithful.

## Builders and Workers

Paul's metaphor of building on a foundation (3:10-15) parallels rabbinic metaphors of Torah scholars as builders. The Talmud uses construction imagery for the work of interpretation: "Do not read bonayikh [your builders] but banayikh [your children]" (Berakhot 64a). Jewish scholars note that Paul uses familiar Jewish rhetorical patterns.

## The Fire of Judgment

Paul's image of work tested by fire (3:13-15) echoes the prophetic tradition of refining fire (Malachi 3:2-3, Zechariah 13:9). In Jewish thought, divine judgment purifies rather than merely punishes. The concept of post-mortem purification also appears in some Jewish sources, particularly in later kabbalistic literature.
