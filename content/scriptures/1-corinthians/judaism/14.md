## Prophecy and the Synagogue

Paul's instructions about prophetic speech in the assembly (14:1-5, 29-32) invite comparison with the synagogue setting. The synagogue allowed multiple speakers to expound Torah (the haftarah reading, public teaching), and the Mishnah discusses the order of service. Paul's concern for orderly prophetic speech reflects the communal worship practices of the synagogue.

## Intelligible Speech

Paul's insistence on intelligible speech in worship (14:6-19) resonates with the Jewish principle that prayer and Torah reading must be understood by the congregation. The Targumim (Aramaic translations) were introduced precisely because Hebrew was no longer the vernacular. The priority of comprehension in worship is a shared value.

## Order and Edification

Paul's summary principle that "all things should be done for building up" (14:26) parallels the rabbinic concern for tikkun (establishing order) in communal worship. The Mishnah and Talmud devote extensive attention to the proper ordering of prayer services, ensuring that worship is both reverent and edifying.
