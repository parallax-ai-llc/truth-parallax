## Marriage in Jewish Tradition

Paul's discussion of marriage (7:1-16) invites extensive comparison with rabbinic teaching. Judaism regards marriage as a mitzvah (commandment) and generally discourages prolonged celibacy. The Talmudic statement "any man who has no wife lives without joy, without blessing, and without goodness" (Yevamot 62b) contrasts with Paul's preference for singleness.

## Divorce

Paul's prohibition of divorce (7:10-11) is stricter than Jewish halakhah, which permits divorce through a get (bill of divorce) following Deuteronomy 24:1-4. The schools of Hillel and Shammai debated the grounds for divorce (Gittin 90a), but both accepted divorce as permissible. Paul's teaching here diverges from normative Jewish practice.

## Remaining in One's State

Paul's instruction to remain in one's calling (7:17-24) has a parallel in the rabbinic concept that holiness is achievable in every life situation. The Mishnah teaches that God cares not about the amount of one's offering but about the direction of one's heart (Menachot 13:11). Sanctification occurs within ordinary life, not only in separation from it.
