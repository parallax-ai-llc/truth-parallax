## Community Discipline and Herem

Paul's command to expel the immoral man (5:1-5) parallels Jewish practices of communal discipline, including niddui (temporary excommunication) and herem (permanent ban). The Dead Sea Scrolls community similarly practiced expulsion for serious transgressions. Jewish scholars note that Paul's disciplinary logic follows recognizably Jewish communal patterns.

## Passover and Purification

Paul's Passover typology — "Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed" (5:7) — reveals his Jewish liturgical consciousness. The metaphor of removing leaven before Passover (bedikat hametz) was a practice every Jewish household performed annually. Paul applies this familiar domestic ritual to the moral purification of the community.

## Judging Those Within

Paul's principle of judging insiders while leaving outsiders to God (5:12-13) mirrors Jewish communal jurisdiction. Rabbinic courts (batei din) had authority over Jewish community members but not over non-Jews. This jurisdictional distinction reflects the same logic Paul employs.
