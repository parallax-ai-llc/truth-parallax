## Overview

1 Corinthians is a letter written by the Apostle Paul to the church he founded in Corinth, a prosperous Roman port city in Greece. Composed around 53–55 CE from Ephesus, the letter addresses a series of practical and theological crises in the young congregation, ranging from internal divisions and sexual immorality to disputes about worship, spiritual gifts, and the resurrection of the dead.

## Structure

The letter responds to reports Paul received and questions the Corinthians sent him:

1. **Divisions in the Church** (1:1–4:21): Factions around leaders; true wisdom vs. worldly wisdom
2. **Moral Issues** (5:1–6:20): Sexual immorality, lawsuits among believers, the body as temple
3. **Marriage and Singleness** (7:1–40): Counsel on marriage, divorce, and celibacy
4. **Food Offered to Idols** (8:1–11:1): Freedom, conscience, and the rights of apostles
5. **Worship and Order** (11:2–14:40): Head coverings, the Lord's Supper, spiritual gifts, prophecy, and tongues
6. **The Resurrection** (15:1–58): The bodily resurrection of Christ and of believers
7. **Closing Matters** (16:1–24): The collection for Jerusalem, travel plans, final greetings

## Cross-Traditional Significance

1 Corinthians speaks to enduring questions across religious traditions:

- **Protestantism**: Key text for ecclesiology, the Lord's Supper, and charismatic theology
- **Catholicism**: Foundation for sacramental theology, especially the Eucharist (11:23–26)
- **Eastern Orthodoxy**: The resurrection chapter (15) is central to Paschal theology
- **Judaism**: Paul's relationship to Torah observance and Jewish identity is debated throughout
- **Islam**: The letter's ethical teachings invite comparison with Quranic moral instruction
- **Gnosticism**: The wisdom language of chapters 1–2 was appropriated by Valentinian interpreters

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Unity | The church as one body with many members |
| Wisdom | God's wisdom versus the world's wisdom |
| Love | The "more excellent way" of chapter 13 |
| Resurrection | The bodily resurrection as the heart of the gospel |
| Spiritual Gifts | Diversity of gifts for the common good |
| Freedom and Responsibility | Christian liberty exercised with care for others |
