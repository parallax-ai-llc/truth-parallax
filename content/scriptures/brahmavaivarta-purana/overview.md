## Overview

The Brahmavaivarta Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, devoted primarily to the worship of Krishna and Radha. Its title means "the Purana of the transformations of Brahma," though the text is overwhelmingly focused on Krishna theology rather than Brahma. It represents one of the most important Puranic sources for the theology of Radha-Krishna devotion.

The text is generally dated to the 15th-16th centuries CE in its present form, making it one of the later Puranas. It reflects the flourishing of Vaishnava devotional movements in Bengal and eastern India, and its theological emphasis on Krishna as the supreme deity and Radha as his eternal consort aligns with the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition.

## Structure

The text is divided into four Khandas (sections):

1. **Brahma Khanda — Brahma Section**: The creation of the universe, the nature of Brahma, and cosmological narratives
2. **Prakriti Khanda — Nature Section**: The manifestation of the divine feminine, the goddesses, and the role of Prakriti
3. **Ganapati Khanda — Ganesha Section**: The birth and exploits of Ganesha, his worship, and his cosmic role
4. **Sri Krishna Janma Khanda — Birth of Krishna Section**: The birth, childhood, and divine play of Krishna with Radha and the gopis

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Brahmavaivarta Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: A foundational text for Radha-Krishna theology, especially within Gaudiya and Pushti Marga traditions
- **Shaivism**: Engaged primarily for the Ganapati Khanda and its treatment of Ganesha worship
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for passages on the ultimate identity of Krishna with Brahman
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Referenced for its strong affirmation of Krishna's supremacy, though approached critically for its late date
- **Academic**: Studied for its role in the development of Radha-Krishna devotional theology and Bengali Vaishnavism

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Radha-Krishna Theology | The eternal love of Radha and Krishna as the highest spiritual reality |
| Divine Feminine | The role of Prakriti and the goddesses in creation and cosmic order |
| Ganesha | The worship and significance of Ganesha as remover of obstacles |
| Lila | The divine play of Krishna as the expression of ultimate reality |
| Bhakti | Devotional love as the supreme path to liberation |
