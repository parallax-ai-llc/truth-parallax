---
title: "Brahmavaivarta Purana Prakriti Khanda (Book 2)"
scripture: "brahmavaivarta-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Prakriti Khanda describes the nature of the divine feminine, the forms of the goddess, and the stories of the great women who are manifestations of Prakriti.

**2** The Purana teaches: "Prakriti, the divine feminine, manifests in five primary forms: Radha, the supreme consort of Krishna; Durga, the warrior goddess; Lakshmi, the goddess of fortune; Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge; and Savitri, the goddess of the Vedas."

**3** Radha is described in her supreme glory: "Her complexion is like molten gold. Her eyes are like lotus petals. She wears garments of blue silk and ornaments of divine gems. She is the source of all beauty, all grace, and all feminine power in the universe."

**4** The story of Durga's birth is narrated. When the demon Mahishasura conquered the three worlds, the combined energy of all the gods formed the goddess Durga. From Shiva came her face, from Vishnu her arms, from Brahma her feet, and from each god a weapon.

**5** Durga fought Mahishasura for nine days, finally slaying him on the tenth. The gods praised her: "O goddess, you are the power that sustains the universe. Without you, the gods are powerless. You are the mother, the protector, and the liberator."

**6** The story of Lakshmi is narrated. She emerged from the churning of the ocean, radiant as the full moon, and chose Vishnu as her eternal lord. She is the source of all wealth, all prosperity, and all beauty. Without her grace, no endeavor can succeed.

**7** Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge, is described: "She is white as snow, seated upon a white lotus, holding the Vina, the book, the rosary, and the pot of sacred water. She is the mother of the Vedas, the patroness of all arts, and the source of all eloquence."

**8** The Purana teaches the equality of the divine masculine and feminine: "Shiva without Shakti is a corpse. The masculine principle is consciousness; the feminine principle is energy. Neither can exist without the other. Together they are the complete Brahman."

**9** The story of Savitri is narrated. When her husband Satyavan was destined to die, Savitri followed Yama and through her wisdom and devotion won back his life. She is the model of the faithful wife whose love conquers even death.

**10** The Purana describes the worship of the goddess: "The Devi should be worshipped with flowers, incense, lamps, and the recitation of her thousand names. She is pleased by devotion, not by elaborate rituals. A sincere prayer from the heart reaches her instantly."

**11** The Purana teaches that women are to be honored: "Where women are honored, there the gods are pleased. Where women are dishonored, all sacred rites are fruitless. The prosperity of a family depends upon the happiness of its women."

**12** The sage concluded: "The Prakriti Khanda reveals that the divine feminine is not subordinate to the masculine but is its equal and complement. The universe is born from their union, sustained by their harmony, and dissolved into their embrace."
