---
title: "Brahmavaivarta Purana Brahma Khanda (Book 1)"
scripture: "brahmavaivarta-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. Salutation to Lord Krishna, the Supreme Being, who is the cause of all causes, the source of all creation, and the object of all devotion. This Brahmavaivarta Purana reveals his divine nature.

**2** Saunaka asked Suta: "O learned one, tell us of the creation that proceeds from Brahman, of the divine sports of Krishna, and of the worship of Radha and Krishna, which is the highest form of devotion."

**3** Suta said: "In the beginning, there was only Krishna, the Supreme Person. He is Sat-Chit-Ananda — existence, consciousness, and bliss. From his left side manifested Radha, his eternal consort, the supreme Shakti."

**4** From the union of Krishna and Radha, all creation proceeds. Radha is Prakriti, the primal nature. Krishna is Purusha, the cosmic spirit. Their eternal love-play is the cause of the creation, maintenance, and dissolution of the universe.

**5** From Krishna's body emanated the forms of Vishnu, Shiva, and Brahma. Vishnu preserves, Brahma creates, and Shiva destroys. But all three are manifestations of the one Supreme Krishna.

**6** The Purana narrates the creation of the material universe. From Krishna's glance upon Prakriti arose Mahat-tattva, the great principle. From Mahat arose Ahankara. From Ahankara arose the five elements, the senses, and the mind.

**7** The celestial world of Goloka is described: "Above Vaikuntha, above Kailash, above all the worlds, is Goloka, the eternal abode of Krishna. It is made of consciousness, not matter. There, Krishna tends his cows, plays his flute, and dances with the Gopis in eternal joy."

**8** The Purana teaches that Radha is the supreme goddess: "All other goddesses are portions of Radha. Lakshmi, Sarasvati, Durga, and all the Shaktis are rays of Radha's splendor. She is the original feminine principle from whom all feminine power flows."

**9** The creation of the demigods is described. Brahma created Daksha from his right thumb. From Daksha's daughters came the gods, demons, serpents, birds, and all species of life. Each species serves a purpose in the divine plan.

**10** The Purana teaches the supremacy of devotion: "Neither knowledge, nor austerity, nor sacrifice, nor yoga can equal devotion to Krishna. A single tear shed in love for Krishna is worth more than a thousand Ashvamedha sacrifices."

**11** The forms of Krishna are described: "Krishna appears as Govinda, the cowherd; as Gopala, the protector of cows; as Vasudeva, the son of Vasudeva; as Jagannath, the lord of the world; and in infinite other forms. Each form reveals a different aspect of his unlimited nature."

**12** The sage concluded: "The Brahma Khanda establishes the theological foundation: Krishna is the Supreme Being, Radha is his eternal consort, and devotion to them is the highest path. All other deities, all other paths, all other scriptures find their fulfillment in this truth."
