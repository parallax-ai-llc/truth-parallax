---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 4: Catukkanipata (The Book of the Fours)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 4
---

**1** "Monks, there are these four efforts. What four? The effort to prevent unarisen unwholesome states from arising, the effort to abandon arisen unwholesome states, the effort to arouse unarisen wholesome states, and the effort to maintain and perfect arisen wholesome states."

**2** "Monks, there are these four bases of power. What four? Here a monk develops the basis of power that possesses concentration due to desire and volitional formations of striving, concentration due to energy, concentration due to mind, and concentration due to investigation."

**3** "Monks, there are these four kinds of persons found existing in the world. What four? One who practises for his own benefit but not for the benefit of others. One who practises for the benefit of others but not for his own benefit. One who practises neither for his own benefit nor for the benefit of others. One who practises for both his own benefit and for the benefit of others. Of these four persons, the one who practises for both his own benefit and for the benefit of others is the chief."

**4** "Monks, there are these four nutriments for the sustenance of beings who have come into being or for the support of those seeking birth. What four? Edible food, gross or subtle; contact as the second; mental volition as the third; and consciousness as the fourth."

**5** "Monks, there are these four foundations of mindfulness. What four? Here a monk dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, and mindful, having removed covetousness and grief in regard to the world. He dwells contemplating feelings in feelings, mind in mind, and mental objects in mental objects, ardent, clearly comprehending, and mindful."

**6** "Monks, there are these four ways of answering questions. What four? There are questions that should be answered categorically, questions that should be answered with a counter-question, questions that should be answered analytically, and questions that should be set aside."

**7** "Monks, possessing four qualities, a person is deposited in hell as if brought there. What four? Without investigation, without consideration, he praises one who deserves dispraise, dispraises one who deserves praise, believes what should not be believed, and disbelieves what should be believed."

**8** "Monks, there are these four kinds of speech. What four? Speech that is true, beneficial, agreeable, and timely — this is the best speech. Speech that is true, beneficial, but disagreeable — this should be spoken at the right time. Speech that is true but neither beneficial nor agreeable — this should be avoided. Speech that is untrue — this should never be spoken."

**9** "Monks, there are these four qualities of a good friend. What four? He gives what is hard to give, does what is hard to do, endures what is hard to endure, and reveals his secrets to you while keeping your secrets."

**10** "Monks, there are these four things conducive to the long endurance of the true Dhamma. What four? Here monks learn the discourses correctly, they preserve the correct meaning, they respect the elders, and they do not alter the training rules. These are the four things conducive to the long endurance of the true Dhamma."
