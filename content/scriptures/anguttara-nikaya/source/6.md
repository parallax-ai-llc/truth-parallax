---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 6: Chakkanipata (The Book of the Sixes)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 6
---

**1** "Monks, there are these six things that are unsurpassed. What six? The unsurpassed sight, the unsurpassed hearing, the unsurpassed gain, the unsurpassed training, the unsurpassed service, and the unsurpassed recollection."

**2** "And what is the unsurpassed sight? Here, monks, a certain person goes to see an elephant treasure, a horse treasure, a jewel treasure, or a great treasure of gold and silver. But this is not the unsurpassed sight. The sight of the Tathagata, the Arahat, the Fully Enlightened One — this is the unsurpassed sight."

**3** "Monks, there are these six qualities of a worthy person. What six? Here a monk is accomplished in virtue, accomplished in concentration, accomplished in wisdom, and when he teaches the Dhamma, he teaches for dispassion, not for passion; for unfettering, not for fettering; for the cessation of clinging, not for taking up of clinging."

**4** "Monks, there are these six things conducive to communal living. What six? Here a monk maintains bodily acts of loving-kindness toward his companions in the holy life, both in public and in private. He maintains verbal acts of loving-kindness. He maintains mental acts of loving-kindness."

**5** "Whatever righteous gains he obtains, he shares them with his virtuous companions. Whatever virtue he possesses — unbroken, untorn, unblotched — he holds it in common with his companions. Whatever view he holds — the noble view leading to liberation — he holds it in common with his companions. These six things are conducive to communal living, creating love and respect, leading to cohesion, to non-dispute, to concord, and to unity."

**6** "Monks, there are these six sense-bases: the eye-base, the ear-base, the nose-base, the tongue-base, the body-base, and the mind-base. A noble disciple who has seen clearly with right wisdom the origin, the passing away, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in regard to these six sense-bases is liberated."

**7** "Monks, there are these six elements. What six? The earth element, the water element, the fire element, the air element, the space element, and the consciousness element. A monk who understands these six elements sees: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.'"

**8** "Monks, there are these six kinds of persons worthy of gifts. What six? The one freed both ways, the one freed by wisdom, the body-witness, the one attained to view, the one freed by faith, and the Dhamma-follower."

**9** "Monks, there are these six recollections. What six? Here a noble disciple recollects the Tathagata, recollects the Dhamma, recollects the Sangha, recollects his own virtues, recollects his own generosity, and recollects the devas. When a noble disciple recollects any of these, his mind is not obsessed by lust, hatred, or delusion."

**10** "Monks, there are these six rewards of stream-entry. What six? One is firm in the true Dhamma. One is unable to fall back. There is a limit to suffering. One is endowed with uncommon knowledge. One has clearly seen causes and conditions. One has clearly seen the phenomena arisen from those causes and conditions."
