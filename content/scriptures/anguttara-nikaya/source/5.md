---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 5: Pancakanipata (The Book of the Fives)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 5
---

**1** "Monks, there are these five hindrances, obstructions of the mind, corruptions of wisdom. What five? The hindrance of sensual desire, the hindrance of ill-will, the hindrance of sloth and torpor, the hindrance of restlessness and worry, and the hindrance of doubt."

**2** "Monks, there are these five faculties. What five? The faculty of faith, the faculty of energy, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of concentration, and the faculty of wisdom. These five faculties, monks, when developed and cultivated, merge in the deathless, have the deathless as their destination, the deathless as their final goal."

**3** "Monks, there are these five powers. What five? The power of faith, the power of energy, the power of mindfulness, the power of concentration, and the power of wisdom. What is the power of faith? Here a noble disciple has faith and is convinced of the Tathagata's enlightenment."

**4** "Monks, there are these five contemplations that should be often reflected upon by a woman or a man, by a householder or one gone forth. What five? 'I am subject to ageing, I have not gone beyond ageing.' 'I am subject to illness, I have not gone beyond illness.' 'I am subject to death, I have not gone beyond death.' 'I must be separated and parted from all that is dear and beloved to me.' 'I am the owner of my actions, the heir of my actions.'"

**5** "Monks, possessing five qualities, a monk is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverence, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. What five? He is accomplished in virtue, accomplished in concentration, accomplished in wisdom, accomplished in liberation, and accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation."

**6** "Monks, there are these five destinations. What five? Hell, the animal realm, the domain of ghosts, human beings, and devas. I know hell, monks, and the path and way leading to hell. I know the animal realm, the domain of ghosts, the human realm, and the deva realm, and the path and way leading to each."

**7** "Monks, there are these five aggregates of clinging. What five? The aggregate of form subject to clinging, the aggregate of feeling subject to clinging, the aggregate of perception subject to clinging, the aggregate of mental formations subject to clinging, and the aggregate of consciousness subject to clinging."

**8** "Monks, there are these five trades that should not be engaged in by a lay follower. What five? Trade in weapons, trade in living beings, trade in meat, trade in intoxicants, and trade in poisons."

**9** "Monks, possessing five qualities, a lay follower is a jewel of a lay follower. What five? He has faith, he is virtuous, he is not superstitious but believes in actions, he does not seek outside the teaching for those worthy of gifts, and he first supports the teaching here."

**10** "Monks, there are these five benefits of listening to the Dhamma. What five? One hears what one has not heard before. One clarifies what one has heard before. One removes doubt. One straightens one's views. One's mind becomes serene."
