---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 3: Tikanipata (The Book of the Threes)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 3
---

**1** "Monks, there are these three roots of unwholesomeness: greed, hatred, and delusion. These are the three roots of unwholesomeness."

**2** "Monks, there are these three roots of wholesomeness: non-greed, non-hatred, and non-delusion. These are the three roots of wholesomeness."

**3** "Monks, there are these three kinds of persons found existing in the world. What three? One who is like a drought, one who rains locally, and one who pours down everywhere. And how is a person like a drought? Here a certain person does not give to anyone — not to ascetics and brahmins, not to the wretched, the destitute, wayfarers, and beggars. This person is like a drought."

**4** "And how is a person one who rains locally? Here a certain person gives to some but not to others. This person is like one who rains locally. And how is a person one who pours down everywhere? Here a certain person gives to all — to ascetics and brahmins, to the wretched, the destitute, wayfarers, and beggars. This person is like one who pours down everywhere."

**5** "Monks, there are these three characteristics of a fool. What three? A fool is one who thinks bad thoughts, speaks bad words, and does bad deeds. If a fool were not so characterized, how would the wise know him as a fool?"

**6** "Monks, there are these three characteristics of a wise person. What three? A wise person thinks good thoughts, speaks good words, and does good deeds."

**7** "Monks, there are these three things that are conducted in secret, not openly. What three? Affairs with women, the mantras of the brahmins, and wrong view. But these three things shine in the open, not in secret: the moon, the sun, and the Dhamma-Vinaya proclaimed by the Tathagata."

**8** "Monks, there are these three fires. What three? The fire of lust, the fire of hatred, and the fire of delusion. These, monks, are the three fires."

**9** "Monks, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? One who has a mind like an open wound, one who has a mind like lightning, and one who has a mind like a diamond. The one with an open wound is easily angered; the one like lightning has quick insight; the one like a diamond is one who has destroyed the taints."

**10** "Monks, there are three things that are unsurpassable. What three? The unsurpassable vision, the unsurpassable practice, and the unsurpassable liberation. The vision of the Dhamma is the unsurpassable vision; the practice of the Noble Eightfold Path is the unsurpassable practice; the liberation of the Arahat is the unsurpassable liberation."
