---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 1: Ekanipata (The Book of the Ones)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 1
---

**1** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing that so leads to the arising of unwholesome states not yet arisen, or to the growth and increase of unwholesome states already arisen, as negligence. In one who is negligent, unwholesome states not yet arisen come to arise, and unwholesome states already arisen grow and increase."

**2** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing that so leads to the arising of wholesome states not yet arisen, or to the growth and increase of wholesome states already arisen, as earnestness. In one who is earnest, wholesome states not yet arisen come to arise, and wholesome states already arisen grow and increase."

**3** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing so obstructive as an untamed mind. An untamed mind is indeed obstructive."

**4** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing so productive as a tamed mind. A tamed mind is indeed productive."

**5** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing that brings such suffering as an undeveloped and uncultivated mind. An undeveloped and uncultivated mind brings suffering."

**6** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing that brings such happiness as a developed and cultivated mind. A developed and cultivated mind brings happiness."

**7** "Monks, I do not know of any single form that so enslaves the mind of a man as the form of a woman. The form of a woman enslaves the mind of a man."

**8** "Monks, I do not know of any single form that so enslaves the mind of a woman as the form of a man. The form of a man enslaves the mind of a woman."

**9** "Monks, I do not know of any single sound that so enslaves the mind of a man as the voice of a woman. Monks, I do not know of any single sound that so enslaves the mind of a woman as the voice of a man."

**10** "Monks, I do not know of any single thing that changes so quickly as the mind. It is not easy to give a simile for how quickly the mind changes. The mind changes so quickly that it is not easy to give a simile for it."

**11** "Monks, luminous is this mind, but it is defiled by adventitious defilements. The uninstructed worldling does not understand this as it really is; therefore for him there is no mental development."

**12** "Monks, luminous is this mind, and it is freed from adventitious defilements. The instructed noble disciple understands this as it really is; therefore for him there is mental development."

**13** "Monks, if beings knew, as I know, the result of giving and sharing, they would not eat without having given, nor would the stain of niggardliness overcome the mind. Even if it were their last bit, their last morsel of food, they would not eat without having shared, if there were someone to share it with."

**14** "Monks, there is one person whose arising in the world is for the welfare of many, for the happiness of many, out of compassion for the world, for the good, the welfare, the happiness of gods and men. Who is that one person? It is the Tathagata, the Arahat, the Fully Enlightened One."

**15** "Monks, hard to find in the world is a person of rare occurrence. What person? The Tathagata, the Arahat, the Fully Enlightened One — such a person is hard to find in the world."

**16** "Monks, the arising of one person is hard to find in the world. Of what person? Of the Tathagata. The arising of such a one is hard to find in the world."

**17** "Monks, there is one thing that, when developed and cultivated, leads to the abandoning of lust, to the abandoning of hatred, to the abandoning of delusion. What one thing? Mindfulness of the body. When mindfulness of the body is developed and cultivated, it leads to the abandoning of lust, hatred, and delusion."

**18** "Monks, whatever states there are that are wholesome, that belong to the wholesome, that are on the side of the wholesome — all of them are rooted in careful attention. They converge upon careful attention. Careful attention is declared the chief among them."

**19** "Monks, just as the dawn is the forerunner and harbinger of the rising of the sun, so right view is the forerunner and harbinger of wholesome states."

**20** "Monks, I do not see any single thing on account of which unarisen unwholesome states arise and arisen wholesome states decline, as wrong view. In one of wrong view, unarisen unwholesome states arise and arisen wholesome states decline."
