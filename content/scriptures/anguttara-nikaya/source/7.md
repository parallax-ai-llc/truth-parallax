---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 7: Sattakanipata (The Book of the Sevens)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 7
---

**1** "Monks, there are these seven factors of enlightenment. What seven? The enlightenment factor of mindfulness, the enlightenment factor of investigation of phenomena, the enlightenment factor of energy, the enlightenment factor of joy, the enlightenment factor of tranquility, the enlightenment factor of concentration, and the enlightenment factor of equanimity."

**2** "Monks, when the enlightenment factor of mindfulness is developed, it leads to the development of investigation of phenomena. Investigation of phenomena leads to the development of energy. Energy leads to the development of joy. Joy leads to the development of tranquility. Tranquility leads to the development of concentration. Concentration leads to the development of equanimity."

**3** "Monks, there are these seven treasures. What seven? The treasure of faith, the treasure of virtue, the treasure of conscience, the treasure of moral shame, the treasure of learning, the treasure of generosity, and the treasure of wisdom."

**4** "The treasure of faith: here a noble disciple has faith; he is convinced of the Tathagata's enlightenment. The treasure of virtue: he abstains from the destruction of life, from taking what is not given, from sexual misconduct, from false speech, and from intoxicants."

**5** "The treasure of conscience: he has a sense of conscience; he is conscientious about bodily misconduct, verbal misconduct, and mental misconduct. The treasure of moral shame: he has a sense of moral shame; he feels shame about bodily, verbal, and mental misconduct."

**6** "The treasure of learning: he has learned much, remembers what he has learned, and stores up what he has learned. Those teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end — such teachings he has learned, remembered, recited, investigated with the mind, and well penetrated by view."

**7** "The treasure of generosity: he dwells at home with a mind devoid of the stain of stinginess, freely generous, open-handed, delighting in relinquishment, devoted to charity, delighting in giving and sharing."

**8** "The treasure of wisdom: he is wise; he possesses the wisdom that discerns arising and passing away, that is noble and penetrative, leading to the complete destruction of suffering."

**9** "Monks, there are these seven qualities of a good person. What seven? He knows the Dhamma. He knows the meaning. He knows himself. He knows moderation. He knows the right time. He knows the assembly. He knows persons."

**10** "Monks, there are these seven kinds of persons existing in the world worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverent salutation, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. What seven? The both-ways-liberated, the wisdom-liberated, the body-witness, the view-attainer, the faith-liberated, the Dhamma-follower, and the faith-follower."
