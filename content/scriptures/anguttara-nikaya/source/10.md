---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 10: Dasakanipata (The Book of the Tens)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 10
---

**1** "Monks, there are these ten fetters. What ten? The five lower fetters and the five higher fetters. And what are the five lower fetters? Identity view, doubt, distorted grasp of rules and vows, sensual desire, and ill will. These are the five lower fetters."

**2** "And what are the five higher fetters? Desire for form, desire for the formless, conceit, restlessness, and ignorance. These are the five higher fetters. These are the ten fetters."

**3** "Monks, there are these ten courses of unwholesome action. What ten? Killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, divisive speech, harsh speech, idle chatter, covetousness, ill will, and wrong view."

**4** "Monks, there are these ten courses of wholesome action. What ten? Abstaining from killing, abstaining from stealing, abstaining from sexual misconduct, abstaining from false speech, abstaining from divisive speech, abstaining from harsh speech, abstaining from idle chatter, non-covetousness, non-ill will, and right view."

**5** "Monks, there are these ten perceptions which, when developed and cultivated, are of great fruit and great benefit, merging in the deathless, having the deathless as their consummation. What ten? The perception of unattractiveness, the perception of death, the perception of repulsiveness in food, the perception of non-delight in the entire world, the perception of impermanence, the perception of suffering in impermanence, the perception of not-self in suffering, the perception of abandoning, the perception of dispassion, and the perception of cessation."

**6** "Monks, there are these ten qualities of an Arahant. What ten? Here, a monk has abandoned five qualities and possesses five qualities. What five has he abandoned? Lust, hatred, delusion, conceit, and wrong view."

**7** "What five does he possess? He possesses the aggregate of virtue of one beyond training, the aggregate of concentration of one beyond training, the aggregate of wisdom of one beyond training, the aggregate of liberation of one beyond training, and the aggregate of the knowledge and vision of liberation of one beyond training."

**8** "Monks, whatever grounds there are for making merit productive of a future birth, all these do not equal a sixteenth part of the mind-release of loving-kindness. The mind-release of loving-kindness surpasses them and shines forth, bright and brilliant."

**9** "Just as whatever stars there are, their radiance does not equal a sixteenth part of the moon's radiance, and the moon's radiance surpasses them and shines forth, bright and brilliant, so too, whatever grounds there are for making merit, all these do not equal a sixteenth part of the mind-release of loving-kindness."

**10** "Monks, there are these ten grounds for praise. What ten? Here, a monk himself is accomplished in virtue and he encourages others in the accomplishment of virtue. He himself is accomplished in concentration and encourages others likewise. He himself is accomplished in wisdom and encourages others likewise. He himself is accomplished in liberation and encourages others likewise. He himself is accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation and encourages others in the knowledge and vision of liberation."

**11** "Monks, one who possesses ten qualities is an outcaste. What ten? Here, a certain person is a killer of living beings, a taker of what is not given, one who engages in sexual misconduct, a speaker of falsehood, a speaker of divisive speech, a speaker of harsh speech, a speaker of idle chatter, one who is covetous, one who has a mind of ill will, and one who holds wrong view."

**12** "Monks, one who possesses ten qualities is a true sage. What ten? Here, a certain person abstains from killing, abstains from stealing, abstains from sexual misconduct, abstains from false speech, from divisive speech, from harsh speech, from idle chatter; is not covetous, has a mind without ill will, and holds right view. This is a true sage."
