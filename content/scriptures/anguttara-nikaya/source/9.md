---
title: "Anguttara Nikaya - Chapter 9: Navakanipata (The Book of the Nines)"
scripture: "anguttara-nikaya"
chapter: 9
---

**1** "Monks, there are these nine things rooted in craving. What nine? Dependent on craving, seeking arises. Dependent on seeking, acquisition arises. Dependent on acquisition, ascertainment arises. Dependent on ascertainment, desire and lust arise. Dependent on desire and lust, attachment arises. Dependent on attachment, possessiveness arises. Dependent on possessiveness, miserliness arises. Dependent on miserliness, guarding arises. Because of guarding, various evil, unwholesome states arise — taking up of clubs and weapons, conflicts, quarrels, disputes, accusations, divisive speech, and false speech."

**2** "Monks, there are these nine successive cessations. What nine? For one who has attained the first jhana, the perception of sensual desire has ceased. For one who has attained the second jhana, applied and sustained thought have ceased. For one who has attained the third jhana, joy has ceased."

**3** "For one who has attained the fourth jhana, in-and-out breathing has ceased. For one who has attained the sphere of infinite space, the perception of form has ceased. For one who has attained the sphere of infinite consciousness, the perception of the sphere of infinite space has ceased."

**4** "For one who has attained the sphere of nothingness, the perception of infinite consciousness has ceased. For one who has attained the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, the perception of nothingness has ceased. For one who has attained the cessation of perception and feeling, perception and feeling have ceased. These are the nine successive cessations."

**5** "Monks, there are these nine ways in which a grudge-bearing person settles a grudge. What nine? He thinks: 'He has done me harm, he is doing me harm, he will do me harm.' He thinks: 'He has done harm to one dear to me, he is doing harm, he will do harm to one dear to me.' He thinks: 'He has done a favor for one who is odious to me, he is doing a favor, he will do a favor for one who is odious to me.' These are the nine ways."

**6** "And what are the nine ways of removing a grudge? He thinks: 'He has done me harm — but what will be gained by bearing a grudge?' He thinks: 'He is doing me harm — but what will be gained?' He thinks: 'He will do me harm — but what will be gained?' And likewise regarding harm to the dear and favors to the odious. In this way, the grudge is removed."

**7** "Monks, there are these nine abodes of beings. What nine? There are beings various in body and various in perception, such as humans and certain devas — this is the first abode. There are beings various in body but identical in perception, such as the Brahma-devas first reborn in that plane — this is the second abode."

**8** "There are beings identical in body but various in perception, such as the Abhassara devas — this is the third. Identical in body and identical in perception, such as the Subhakinna devas — the fourth. The sphere of infinite space — the fifth. The sphere of infinite consciousness — the sixth. The sphere of nothingness — the seventh. The sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception — the eighth. Beings without perception — the ninth."

**9** "Monks, I do not see even one other thing that changes so quickly as the mind. It is not easy to give a simile for how quickly the mind changes. The mind, monks, is luminous, but it is defiled by adventitious defilements. The uninstructed worldling does not understand this as it really is."

**10** "The mind, monks, is luminous, and it is freed from adventitious defilements. The instructed noble disciple understands this as it really is. Therefore I say that there is development of the mind for the instructed noble disciple."
