## Overview

The Anguttara Nikaya ("Numerical Discourses") is the fourth of the five nikayas of the Sutta Pitaka in the Pali Canon. It contains over 9,500 suttas organized by ascending numerical groups (nipatas) from ones to elevens. This organizational method makes it an encyclopedic reference for Buddhist teaching, covering ethics, psychology, meditation, philosophy, and the path to liberation in accessible, numbered formats.

## Structure

The collection is organized into 11 nipatas (chapters), each corresponding to a numerical group:

1. **Ekaka Nipata — Book of Ones**: Single topics and fundamental teachings
2. **Duka Nipata — Book of Twos**: Paired teachings and contrasts
3. **Tika Nipata — Book of Threes**: Triads including the three characteristics
4. **Catukka Nipata — Book of Fours**: The four noble truths, four bases of power
5. **Pancaka Nipata — Book of Fives**: Five aggregates, five hindrances, five faculties
6. **Chakka Nipata — Book of Sixes**: Six sense bases, six recollections
7. **Sattaka Nipata — Book of Sevens**: Seven factors of awakening
8. **Atthaka Nipata — Book of Eights**: The eightfold path, eight worldly conditions
9. **Navaka Nipata — Book of Nines**: Nine abodes of beings
10. **Dasaka Nipata — Book of Tens**: Ten fetters, ten perfections
11. **Ekadasaka Nipata — Book of Elevens**: Groups of eleven

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Anguttara Nikaya has partial parallels in the Chinese Ekottara Agama. Its numerical organization parallels the Jain Sthananga Sutra, suggesting shared pedagogical methods in ancient Indian religion. The collection is particularly valued for its practical ethical teachings accessible to both monastics and laypeople.
