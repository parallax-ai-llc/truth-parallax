## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Anguttara Nikaya highlights its remarkable parallels with numerical classification systems across religious and philosophical traditions. The collection's practical ethical teachings also invite comparison with moral teachings worldwide.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Numerical Systems Compared

The Anguttara Nikaya's method directly parallels the Jain Sthananga Sutra's numerical organization, and resonates with numbered teachings in other traditions -- the Ten Commandments, the Five Pillars, the Eightfold Path.

### Lay Ethics

The collection's extensive lay teachings invite comparison with householder ethics in Jainism, Hindu dharma literature, Confucian ethics, and Christian moral theology.

## Comparative Framework

| Feature | Buddhism (AN) | Jainism (Sthananga) | Confucianism |
|---------|--------------|--------------------|--------------|
| Method | Numbered 1-11 | Numbered 1-10 | Five Relations |
| Audience | Monks and laity | Primarily monastic | Universal |
| Focus | Ethics and meditation | Doctrinal categories | Social ethics |
