## The Goodness of the Law

Catholic interpretation affirms with Paul that "the law is holy, and the commandment is holy and righteous and good" (7:12). Unlike some Protestant readings emphasizing the Law's condemning function, Catholic theology maintains that the moral law, especially the Decalogue, remains a valid and binding guide for the Christian life. What the Law could not do was heal the wound of sin; that required grace.

## The Inner Conflict and Concupiscence

The struggle described in 7:14-25 is read by most Catholic interpreters as describing the experience of the unredeemed or the regenerate person insofar as concupiscence remains. The Council of Trent distinguished between concupiscence and sin proper: the baptized person retains disordered desires, but these are not themselves sinful unless consented to.

## Deliverance Through Christ

Paul's answer to the cry of wretchedness (7:25) points to the sacramental life as the ordinary means of ongoing deliverance. Confession addresses post-baptismal sin; the Eucharist strengthens against temptation. The moral struggle is real, but the means of grace are sufficient.
