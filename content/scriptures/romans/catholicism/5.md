## Original Sin

Romans 5:12-21 is the primary biblical foundation for the Catholic doctrine of original sin. The Council of Trent (Session 5, 1546) cited this passage extensively, defining that Adam's sin is transmitted to all descendants "by propagation, not by imitation." The Latin Vulgate's translation of 5:12 shaped Western theology's understanding that all humanity sinned "in Adam."

## Grace Superabounding

Paul's declaration that "where sin increased, grace abounded all the more" (5:20) is central to Catholic soteriology. The Easter Vigil liturgy celebrates the "happy fault" (felix culpa) that "earned so great a Redeemer," a direct theological appropriation of Paul's Adam-Christ typology.

## Christ and Adam

The parallel between Adam and Christ is read through the lens of Irenaeus's recapitulation theology: Christ recapitulates every stage of human life that Adam corrupted. Christ brings humanity to a higher state than Adam possessed before the Fall, and this greater grace is communicated through the sacraments, especially baptism.
