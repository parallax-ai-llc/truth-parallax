## The State and the Church

Romans 13:1-7 is foundational for Catholic social teaching on Church-State relations. Following Aquinas, Catholic interpretation holds that civil authority derives from God and serves the common good. However, this authority is not absolute: unjust laws do not bind in conscience. Leo XIII's Immortale Dei (1885) and Vatican II's Gaudium et Spes (1965) both engage with this passage.

## Love as the Fulfillment of the Law

Paul's teaching that "love is the fulfilling of the law" (13:10) resonates with Catholic moral theology's insistence that charity is the form of all virtues. Aquinas taught that the moral law is summed up in the double commandment of love.

## Eschatological Urgency

The call to "cast off the works of darkness" (13:12) carries eschatological significance. Augustine's conversion was catalyzed by this passage (Confessions VIII.12), and the liturgy reads Romans 13 during Advent, connecting Paul's urgency with the Church's expectation of Christ's return.
