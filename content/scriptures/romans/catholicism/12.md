## Spiritual Worship and the Liturgy

Paul's call to present one's body as "a living sacrifice" (12:1) is read by Catholic interpreters as connecting daily life to liturgical worship. The phrase "spiritual worship" links the offering of the self to the Eucharistic sacrifice, where Christ's offering is made present and the faithful unite their lives to it.

## The Body of Christ

Paul's body metaphor (12:4-8) is foundational for Catholic ecclesiology, elaborated in Pius XII's encyclical Mystici Corporis Christi (1943). Each member's gifts serve the common good and are discerned in relationship to the community.

## The Practice of Charity

The ethical instructions of 12:9-21 form the heart of Catholic moral teaching on charity. Genuine love, blessing persecutors, overcoming evil with good are the ordinary demands of the Christian life, empowered by grace and modeled by the saints.
