## The Mystery of Election

Catholic interpretation of Romans 9 resists both strict double predestination and the reduction of election to mere foreknowledge. Following Augustine and Aquinas, Catholic theology affirms that God's election is gratuitous while maintaining that God sincerely wills the salvation of all people (1 Timothy 2:4).

## Israel's Privileges

Paul's enumeration of Israel's privileges (9:4-5) affirms that God's gifts to Israel are real and enduring. Catholic teaching, especially since Nostra Aetate (1965), emphasizes that the Jewish people remain in a covenant relationship with God.

## Vessels of Mercy

The potter-and-clay metaphor (9:20-23) is read as illustrating God's patience and purpose, not arbitrary predetermination. The emphasis falls on God's mercy, not on a decree of reprobation. Aquinas argued that God permits but does not cause the damnation of the wicked.
