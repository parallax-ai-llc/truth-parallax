## Christ the Fulfillment of the Law

Paul's declaration that "Christ is the end of the law" (10:4) is read by Catholic interpreters as affirming that Christ fulfills the Law rather than abolishing it. The moral commandments remain binding; the ceremonial law finds its completion in Christ and the sacraments.

## Confession and Belief

The formula of 10:9 is understood in Catholic theology as describing the act of faith that initiates justification. This confession finds its sacramental expression in the baptismal creed and in the liturgical profession of faith at Mass. Faith is a public, ecclesial act.

## The Mission of the Church

Paul's chain of reasoning about preaching and sending (10:14-15) provides the theological foundation for the Church's missionary mandate. The apostolic succession ensures that authorized preachers continue to be "sent" in continuity with the original apostles.
