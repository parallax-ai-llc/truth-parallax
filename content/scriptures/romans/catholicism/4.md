## Abraham's Faith

Catholic interpretation of Romans 4 follows Augustine in reading Abraham's faith as genuine trust in God that was itself a gift of grace. The "counting" of faith as righteousness (4:3) does not mean Abraham received a merely legal status; rather, his faith was the beginning of a real interior righteousness perfected in obedience.

## Before Circumcision

Paul's observation that Abraham was justified before circumcision (4:10) is interpreted by Catholic scholars as demonstrating that the sacraments of the Old Law did not justify by their own power. However, Catholic theology sees circumcision as a genuine sacramental sign anticipating baptism, the New Covenant sacrament that truly confers grace.

## The Promise and Its Heirs

Paul's argument that the promise comes "by grace" and is "guaranteed to all his offspring" (4:16) is read as affirming the universal scope of God's saving will. The promise extends to all who share Abraham's faith, and the Church is the community in which that promise is sacramentally mediated.
