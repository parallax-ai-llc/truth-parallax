## Bearing with the Weak

Romans 15 opens with the principle that the strong ought to bear with the failings of the weak (15:1). Catholic social teaching extends this to structural justice: the strong have an obligation to support the vulnerable. The preferential option for the poor finds a Pauline warrant here.

## Christ the Servant of All

Paul's statement about Christ's service (15:8-9) undergirds the Catholic theology of Christ as both the fulfillment of Israel's hope and the savior of all nations. The liturgy's use of the Old Testament citations in 15:9-12 enacts this conviction in worship.

## The Collection and Church Unity

Paul's discussion of the collection for Jerusalem (15:25-27) illustrates the concrete, material dimension of ecclesial communion. Catholic social teaching sees in this a model for solidarity between local churches.
