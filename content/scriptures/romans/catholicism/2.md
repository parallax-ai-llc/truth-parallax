## The Impartiality of Divine Judgment

Romans 2 develops Paul's argument that God judges "without partiality" (2:11). Catholic interpreters emphasize that Paul affirms a real connection between judgment and deeds: "He will render to each one according to his works" (2:6). This reflects the Catholic understanding that genuine faith produces works that merit reward, not independently of grace, but as grace-enabled acts of cooperation.

## The Law Written on the Heart

Paul's statement that Gentiles "show the work of the law written on their hearts" (2:15) is the principal Pauline text for Catholic natural law theory. Aquinas cited it in the Summa Theologiae to demonstrate that all rational creatures participate in the eternal law through natural inclination. This doctrine undergirds Catholic social teaching and moral theology.

## Interior Transformation

Paul's teaching that true circumcision is "a matter of the heart, by the Spirit" (2:29) resonates with the Catholic emphasis on interior transformation through the sacraments. Baptism is understood as the New Covenant circumcision, effecting a real change in the soul.
