## Baptismal Regeneration

Romans 6 is the primary Pauline text for Catholic baptismal theology. Paul's statement that believers are "baptized into Christ Jesus" and "baptized into his death" (6:3) is read as referring to sacramental water baptism. The Council of Trent affirmed that baptism truly effects what it signifies: death to sin and rebirth in Christ. The baptismal font is understood as both tomb and womb.

## Freedom from Sin's Dominion

Paul's declaration that "sin will have no dominion over you" (6:14) describes the real transformation in the baptized person. Catholic theology teaches that baptism removes original sin and all personal sins, though concupiscence remains. The baptized person is genuinely freed from sin's reign, not merely declared free.

## Instruments of Righteousness

The exhortation to "present your members to God as instruments of righteousness" (6:13) grounds Catholic moral theology's emphasis on cooperation with grace. The justified person, empowered by habitual grace, actively participates in sanctification. Grace initiates, sustains, and completes, but God and the human will work together.
