## Universal Sinfulness

Romans 3:1-20 establishes that "all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God" (3:23). Catholic theology reads this in light of the doctrine of original sin, formally defined at the Council of Trent. Every human being, except the Blessed Virgin Mary by the privilege of the Immaculate Conception, inherits the wound of Adam's sin and stands in need of redemption.

## The Revelation of God's Righteousness

Romans 3:21-26 is the heart of the letter's soteriology. Catholic interpretation holds that God's righteousness is both revealed and communicated to the believer through infused grace. The Council of Trent taught that justification involves "not only the remission of sins but also the sanctification and renewal of the interior man" (Session 6, Chapter 7).

## Faith and the Law

Catholic teaching affirms that initial justification is received by faith and cannot be merited. However, Trent insisted that this faith must be "faith working through love" (Galatians 5:6), and that the "works of the law" Paul rejects are the ceremonial prescriptions of the Mosaic code, not all good works as such.
