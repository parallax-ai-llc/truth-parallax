## God Has Not Rejected Israel

Catholic interpretation of Romans 11 has undergone significant development since Vatican II. Nostra Aetate (1965) declared that the Jews remain "most dear to God" and that God's gifts and calling are "irrevocable," directly citing Romans 11:28-29. This reversed centuries of supersessionist readings.

## The Olive Tree and the Church

Paul's olive tree metaphor (11:17-24) is read as depicting the organic relationship between Israel and the Church. The Church is not a new tree but a continuation of the covenant people, with Gentile believers grafted onto the root of Israel.

## The Mystery of Universal Salvation

Paul's proclamation that "all Israel will be saved" (11:26) is read as expressing God's universal salvific will. The 2015 Vatican document The Gifts and the Calling of God Are Irrevocable explores how this applies to the Jewish people, affirming that while the Church does not conduct organized missionary efforts targeting Jews, salvation comes through Christ.
