## Conscience and Pastoral Sensitivity

Catholic interpretation draws on Paul's teaching about the "weak" and the "strong" to develop the theology of conscience. The principle of 14:5 is cited in support of the inviolability of conscience, elaborated by Vatican II in Dignitatis Humanae (1965). At the same time, conscience must be formed by truth.

## The Kingdom and Its Priorities

Paul's declaration about the kingdom (14:17) helps Catholic moral theology distinguish between essential truths and matters of legitimate pastoral adaptation. This principle informs the Church's approach to inculturation.

## Not Causing Scandal

The warning against causing a brother to stumble (14:13-21) is developed in Catholic moral theology as the sin of scandal. Aquinas treats scandal as a sin against charity, and the concept plays a significant role in pastoral practice.
