## The Rejection of Original Sin

Romans 5:12-21 presents the doctrine of inherited sin. Islam categorically rejects this. The Quran narrates Adam's sin and immediate repentance: "Then Adam received from his Lord words, and He accepted his repentance" (2:37). Adam's sin was personal and forgiven; it was not transmitted to descendants.

## Human Nature in Islam

The Islamic concept of fitrah directly contradicts inherited sin. Every child is born in natural goodness and orientation toward God. Sin is acquired through wrong choices, not biological inheritance. Humanity does not need cosmic rescue from an inherited condition.

## Grace and Repentance

While Paul emphasizes the "free gift" overcoming Adam's transgression, Islam teaches that God's mercy is available directly through repentance (tawbah): "Say, O My servants who have transgressed, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins" (39:53).
