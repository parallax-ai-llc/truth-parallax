## Christ as Servant and the Prophetic Model

Paul presents Christ as one who served others (15:3). Islam recognizes Jesus (Isa) as a prophet and servant of God who embodied devotion. The Quran describes Jesus as "a servant of Allah" (19:30). However, Islam rejects his elevation to a cosmic soteriological role.

## The Unity of Believers

Paul's prayer for unity (15:6) expresses a desire Islam shares. The Quran commands: "Hold firmly to the rope of Allah all together and do not become divided" (3:103). Both Paul and the Quran see division within the community as a serious spiritual malady.

## Paul's Missionary Journey and the Spread of Islam

Paul's missionary ambitions (15:19-24) reveal the expansive drive of early Christianity. Muslim scholars see a parallel in Islam's rapid spread. Both movements understood themselves as carrying a universal message, though Islam critiques Paul's message as departing from original prophetic teaching.
