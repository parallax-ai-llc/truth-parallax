## Baptism and Islamic Purification

Paul's theology of baptism as death and resurrection with Christ (6:3-4) has no parallel in Islamic practice. Islam has purification rituals (wudu and ghusl) but these are acts of ritual preparation, not sacramental participation in a salvific event.

## Freedom from Sin

Paul's teaching that believers are "freed from sin" (6:7) resonates with Islamic moral exhortation. The Quran calls believers to resist sinful inclinations through taqwa, prayer, fasting, and righteous deeds. However, Islam does not posit a prior bondage from which one must be mystically liberated.

## The Wages of Sin

Paul's declaration (6:23) expresses a stark binary. Islam acknowledges that persistent, unrepented sin leads to punishment, but emphasizes that God's mercy is vast and repentance is always possible. The path to eternal life runs through faith, righteous deeds, and God's mercy.
