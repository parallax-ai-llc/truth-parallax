## Israel in Islamic Theology

Paul's engagement with Israel's fate addresses a question Islam resolves differently. In Islamic theology, the Children of Israel received genuine divine favor, but their covenant was not the final one. The Quran acknowledges Israel's election (2:47) while teaching that this election carried obligations not always met.

## The Olive Tree and Religious History

Paul's olive tree metaphor can be read from an Islamic perspective as illustrating the succession of religious communities. Islam sees itself as the restoration of the original Abrahamic root religion. All true prophets taught islam (submission to God), and Muhammad's mission was to restore the primordial faith.

## God's Mercy Encompasses All

Paul's conclusion that God "has consigned all to disobedience, that he may have mercy on all" (11:32) expresses universality of mercy resonating with Islamic theology. The Quran states: "My mercy encompasses all things" (7:156). Islam affirms vast divine mercy but without requiring the Christological mechanism Paul describes.
