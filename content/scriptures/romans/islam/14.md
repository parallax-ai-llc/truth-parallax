## Dietary Laws and Halal

Romans 14's discussion of dietary scruples invites comparison with Islamic dietary laws. Islam has clear rules about halal (permissible) and haram (forbidden) food, particularly the prohibition of pork and alcohol. Unlike Paul, who relativizes dietary restrictions, Islam maintains that God's dietary laws are permanent and beneficial.

## Respect for Differences

Paul's principle of not judging on disputable matters (14:1-4) resonates with the Islamic concept of ikhtilaf (legitimate scholarly disagreement). The four Sunni schools hold differing positions on many questions, and mutual respect is encouraged, though this applies to matters of interpretation, not clearly established commands.

## The Kingdom of God

Paul's declaration about the kingdom (14:17) expresses a spiritual principle Islam affirms in part. The Quran emphasizes that piety is not merely external (2:177). Yet Islam insists external observances are inseparable from interior piety, not subordinate to it.
