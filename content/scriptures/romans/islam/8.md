## The Spirit in Islamic Theology

Paul's teaching on the Holy Spirit engages a concept understood differently in Islam. The Quran refers to the Ruh (Spirit) as God's agent but not as a distinct divine Person. Islam rejects the Trinitarian theology that reads Romans 8 as describing the Third Person of the Trinity.

## Creation Groaning for Liberation

Paul's vision of creation groaning (8:19-22) finds resonance in Islamic eschatology. The Quran describes the Day of Judgment as a cosmic transformation. Islam shares the conviction that the material world is not in its final state, but the transformation is an act of God's power on the Last Day, not outworking of Christ's resurrection.

## God's Mercy and Protection

The chapter's assurance that nothing can separate us from God's love (8:38-39) expresses confidence Islam shares in its own terms. The Quran's Bismillah affirms that God's mercy encompasses all things. However, Islam grounds this not in a Christological event but in God's own nature and his promise to the righteous.
