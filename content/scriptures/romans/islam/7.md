## Law as Guidance, Not Condemnation

Paul's argument that the Law became an instrument of sin (7:7-13) is alien to Islamic theology. In Islam, divine law (sharia) is guidance and mercy: "guidance and mercy for the people" (7:52). The idea that God's own law could provoke sin would be seen as impugning divine wisdom.

## The Moral Struggle

Paul's description of inner conflict (7:19) resonates with the Islamic understanding of the struggle against the nafs (lower self). The Quran speaks of three levels: the soul that incites to evil (12:53), the self-reproaching soul (75:2), and the soul at peace (89:27). The moral life is progressive purification toward peace.

## Deliverance Through Taqwa

Where Paul finds deliverance only through Christ (7:25), Islam teaches that deliverance comes through taqwa (God-consciousness), repentance, and obedience to divine guidance. Prayer, fasting, charity, and remembrance of God provide the practical means for overcoming the lower self.
