## Ibrahim, the First Hanif

Paul's appeal to Abraham invites Islamic engagement, since Ibrahim holds a central place in Quranic theology. The Quran describes Ibrahim as a hanif, a pure monotheist: "Ibrahim was neither a Jew nor a Christian, but he was a hanif, a Muslim" (3:67). Ibrahim's faith was inseparable from his acts of obedience.

## Faith as Submission

Where Paul emphasizes Abraham's trust as the ground of justification, Islam emphasizes Ibrahim's islam (submission). The Quran's Ibrahim submits his entire will to God's command. The Islamic reading rejects the Pauline dichotomy: Ibrahim's faith is his obedience, and his obedience is his faith.

## Circumcision and Islamic Practice

Paul's argument about Abraham being justified before circumcision is theologically significant for Christianity but less so for Islam. Islam practices circumcision (khitan) as a Sunnah connected to Ibrahim, but it is not a condition of salvation.
