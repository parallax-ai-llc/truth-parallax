## Universal Sinfulness

Paul's conclusion that "all have sinned" (3:23) represents a point of departure. Islam acknowledges human sinfulness but rejects total depravity or inherited sinful nature. Human beings are fallible but retain the capacity for righteousness through God's guidance.

## The Rejection of Vicarious Atonement

Paul's teaching that God put Christ forward "as a propitiation by his blood" (3:25) is directly rejected by Islamic theology. The Quran denies crucifixion (4:157) and teaches individual accountability: "no bearer of burdens shall bear the burden of another" (6:164).

## Faith and Works Together

Paul's declaration about justification "by faith apart from works of the law" (3:28) articulates a dichotomy foreign to Islam. In Islamic soteriology, faith (iman) and righteous deeds (amal salih) are inseparable. The Pauline separation is seen as a theological distortion.
