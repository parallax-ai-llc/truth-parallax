## Divine Sovereignty and Qadr

Romans 9's discussion of God's sovereign choice resonates with the Islamic doctrine of qadr (divine decree). Islam affirms God's absolute will: "He cannot be questioned for His acts" (21:23). The potter-and-clay metaphor finds echoes in the Quranic teaching that God guides whom He wills (14:4).

## The Question of Israel

Paul's anguish over Israel's unbelief (9:1-5) is a distinctly Christian problem that Islam frames differently. In Islamic theology, Jewish rejection of later prophets is a recurring theme. The Quran calls the Children of Israel to remember God's favor and fulfill their covenant (2:40).

## Mercy, Not Arbitrary Power

While both Paul and Islam affirm divine sovereignty, Islamic theology insists that God's decrees are always just and wise. The Quranic names Al-Adl (The Just) and Al-Hakim (The Wise) qualify the exercise of divine power.
