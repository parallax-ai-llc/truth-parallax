## Individual Accountability

Paul's teaching that God "will render to each one according to his works" (2:6) aligns with the Quranic principle of individual accountability: "Whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it" (99:7-8). Muslim scholars affirm this from Romans 2 while noting that Paul's subsequent argument seems to undermine it by declaring justification through faith apart from works.

## The Law Written on the Heart

Paul's reference to Gentiles who have "the work of the law written on their hearts" (2:15) resonates with the Islamic teaching that God has endowed every person with a moral conscience (damir). The Quran speaks of the soul God "inspired with discernment of its wickedness and its righteousness" (91:7-8).

## The Inadequacy of External Religion

Paul's critique of those who rely on religious identity while failing morally (2:17-24) echoes Quranic warnings against hypocrisy (nifaq). The Quran extensively condemns the munafiqun who profess faith outwardly but lack sincerity.
