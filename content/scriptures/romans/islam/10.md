## The Universality of the Call

Paul's declaration that "there is no distinction between Jew and Greek" (10:12) resonates with Islam's universal message. The Quran declares Muhammad "a mercy to the worlds" (21:107). Islam shares Paul's conviction that God's message is universal, though the content differs.

## Preaching and Dawah

Paul's chain of reasoning about faith, hearing, and preaching (10:14-15) parallels the Islamic concept of dawah (invitation to Islam). The Quran commands: "Invite to the way of your Lord with wisdom and good instruction" (16:125). Both traditions hold that God's message must be actively communicated.

## The Prophetic Succession

Paul's argument that Israel heard but did not obey echoes a pattern in Islamic theology: prophets sent and rejected. The Quran narrates this from Noah through Abraham, Moses, and Jesus. Islam sees Muhammad as the final prophet whose message corrects what came before.
