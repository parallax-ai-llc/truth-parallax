## Government and Divine Authority

Paul's instruction to submit to governing authorities (13:1) resonates with the Islamic principle that legitimate authority derives from God. However, Islam develops this into a comprehensive political theology where the ideal government implements sharia. Muslim scholars agree civil authority serves a divine function but add that it is legitimate only insofar as it upholds justice.

## Love of Neighbor

Paul's teaching that "love is the fulfilling of the law" (13:10) finds a parallel in the Prophet's saying: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself." Islam sees love of neighbor as central but operating within specific divine commands.

## Moral Urgency

Paul's call to "cast off the works of darkness" (13:12) expresses an eschatological urgency Islam shares. The Quran warns that the Hour is approaching and believers must prepare: "Let every soul look to what it has put forth for tomorrow" (59:18).
