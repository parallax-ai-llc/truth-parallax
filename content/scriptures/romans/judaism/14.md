## Dietary Laws in Context

Romans 14's discussion of dietary scruples invites comparison with Jewish kashrut. The "weak" who abstain from meat likely include Jews who could not be certain that available meat met halakhic standards. Rather than rejecting kashrut, Paul may be navigating a practical problem of mixed table fellowship.

## Holy Days

Paul's statement about esteeming days differently (14:5) touches directly on Sabbath and festival observance. Jewish scholars note that Paul does not explicitly command abandoning Sabbath but allows diversity. Nevertheless, the trajectory makes Shabbat optional, incompatible with halakhah which treats it as a binding covenant sign.

## The Priority of Community

Paul's concern that the strong should not cause the weak to stumble (14:13-21) reflects values shared by Jewish communal ethics. The halakhic principle of marit ayin and the injunction of lifnei iver address similar concerns about the impact of behavior on others.
