## The Advantage of Being Jewish

Paul's question "What advantage has the Jew?" (3:1) and his answer acknowledging Israel's entrustment with "the oracles of God" (3:2) is read by Jewish scholars as affirming that Torah and covenant retain value. Yet Paul's subsequent universal indictment (3:9) appears to undermine this advantage.

## Works of the Law

Paul's declaration about justification apart from "works of the law" (3:20) is among the most contested phrases in Jewish-Christian dialogue. Jewish scholars point out that Torah observance is not a means of "earning" salvation but a response to the covenant relationship. The New Perspective on Paul has moved closer to this Jewish position.

## Righteousness in Jewish Thought

The concept of tzedakah (righteousness) in Jewish tradition is relational and covenantal. God's righteousness is God's faithfulness to the covenant; human righteousness is faithful response to Torah. Jewish scholars suggest Paul's own understanding may be closer to this Jewish sense than later Protestant readings acknowledged.
