## Jewish and Gentile Names

The list of greetings provides a window into the early Roman Christian community's composition. Jewish scholars note the mix of Jewish names (Priscilla, Aquila, Andronicus, Herodion "my kinsman") and Gentile names, reflecting the mixed character of the congregations and supporting the picture of early Christianity as a movement within the Jewish community.

## Kinsmen and Community

Paul's repeated reference to "my kinsmen" (16:7, 11, 21) indicates that Jewish believers remained identifiable within the Roman churches. Jewish scholars use this to argue against the view that Paul's gospel was anti-Jewish: he maintained close relationships with fellow Jews in the movement.

## The Social World of Early Christianity

The house-church network parallels the structure of Diaspora Jewish communities, which also met in private homes and relied on patronage networks. The overlap suggests early Christianity emerged from within the institutional framework of Diaspora Judaism.
