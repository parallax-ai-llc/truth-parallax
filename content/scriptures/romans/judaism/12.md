## Ethical Parallels with Jewish Teaching

Romans 12 moves from theology to ethics, and Jewish scholars note extensive parallels with rabbinic moral teaching. Paul's call to "bless those who persecute you" (12:14) echoes the Talmudic teaching about those who hear insults without responding (Shabbat 88b). The instruction to live in harmony resonates with rabbinic emphasis on humility (anavah).

## Overcoming Evil with Good

Paul's instruction to "overcome evil with good" (12:21) has close parallels in Proverbs 25:21-22, which Paul explicitly cites. Jewish scholars observe that this ethic of non-retaliation is deeply rooted in the Hebrew Bible and rabbinic tradition, not a uniquely Christian innovation.

## The Body Metaphor

Paul's comparison of the community to a body (12:4-5) has parallels in rabbinic thought. The concept of kol Yisrael arevim zeh bazeh ("all Israel is responsible for one another," Shevuot 39a) expresses the same communal interconnectedness.
