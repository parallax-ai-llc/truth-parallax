## All Israel Will Be Saved

Romans 11:26 is the single most discussed verse in Jewish engagement with the New Testament. For Jewish readers, the question is what Paul means by "Israel" and "saved." Most modern Jewish scholars, and an increasing number of Christian ones, read Paul as referring to ethnic Israel.

## The Irrevocable Gifts and Calling

Paul's declaration that "the gifts and the calling of God are irrevocable" (11:29) has become the cornerstone of post-Holocaust Jewish-Christian dialogue. The 2015 Vatican document took its title from this verse. For Jewish readers, this vindicates what Judaism has always maintained: the covenant at Sinai was never revoked.

## The Olive Tree

The olive tree metaphor (11:17-24) is read by Jewish scholars as Paul's own acknowledgment that Christianity depends on Judaism, not the reverse. The warning against Gentile boasting (11:20-21) is seen as prophetic: the subsequent history of Christian anti-Judaism represents precisely the arrogance Paul warned against.
