## The Torah's Role

Romans 7 presents the most problematic Pauline treatment of Torah for Jewish readers. Paul's implication that the Law provokes sin (7:7) is rejected by Jewish theology. The Torah is "a tree of life to those who hold fast to it" (Proverbs 3:18); it reveals sin but also provides the means to overcome it through observance and repentance.

## The Yetzer Hara and Paul's Inner Conflict

The inner conflict described in 7:14-25 finds a partial parallel in the rabbinic concept of the yetzer hara. The Talmud acknowledges that the evil inclination is powerful (Kiddushin 30b). However, the rabbinic solution is Torah study and the commandments, which serve as the antidote. Paul's conclusion that only Christ can deliver bypasses Torah as remedy.

## Law as Holy

Jewish readers affirm Paul's acknowledgment that the law is "holy and righteous and good" (7:12) but question why he argues it is powerless to save. In Jewish understanding, Torah is a comprehensive guide for life, not merely a diagnostic tool.
