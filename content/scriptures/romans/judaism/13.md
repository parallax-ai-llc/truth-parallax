## Obedience to Government

Paul's instruction to submit to governing authorities (13:1-7) has a parallel in the rabbinic principle dina demalkhuta dina ("the law of the government is the law," Nedarim 28a). Both Paul and the rabbis recognized the legitimacy of civil authority while maintaining ultimate allegiance to God.

## Love as the Fulfillment of the Law

Paul's statement that "love is the fulfilling of the law" (13:10) echoes Hillel's dictum: "What is hateful to you, do not do to your fellow. That is the whole Torah; the rest is commentary. Go and study" (Shabbat 31a). Jewish readers note that Hillel adds "go and study," insisting on the irreducible particularity of the commandments.

## Eschatological Urgency

Paul's sense that "the night is far gone" (13:12) reflects an eschatological urgency common in Second Temple Judaism. The Dead Sea Scrolls community lived in similar expectation. The failure of this imminent expectation required both traditions to adjust their eschatological frameworks.
