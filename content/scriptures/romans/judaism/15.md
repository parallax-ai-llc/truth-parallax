## Scripture and Gentile Inclusion

Paul's Old Testament quotations in 15:9-12 demonstrate his claim that Gentile inclusion was anticipated in the Hebrew Bible. Jewish scholars note that these passages, in their original context, envision the nations acknowledging Israel's God without necessarily joining the covenant community. Paul's reading is creative and theologically motivated.

## The Collection for Jerusalem

Paul's discussion of the collection for Jerusalem (15:25-28) reveals material ties between Gentile and Jewish believers. Jewish scholars see this as an echo of the Temple tax and Jewish practice of supporting the community in the Land of Israel.

## Paul's Jewish Identity

Throughout Romans 15, Paul presents himself as a Jewish apostle to the Gentiles (15:16), using priestly language to describe his work. Jewish scholars emphasize that Paul never renounced his Jewish identity; his mission was conceived within a Jewish eschatological framework.
