## Christ as the End of the Law?

Paul's statement that "Christ is the end of the law" (10:4) is one of the most contested verses in Jewish-Christian relations. Jewish scholars note that telos can mean "goal" or "fulfillment" rather than "termination." If the Torah's purpose was to point toward the messianic age, and if Jesus is not the Messiah, then Torah remains fully operative.

## The Accessibility of Torah

Paul's citation of Deuteronomy 30:12-14 (10:8) is striking because in its original context, Moses speaks about the Torah, not about Christ. Jewish readers observe that Paul has reinterpreted a passage about Torah accessibility as a passage about faith in Christ, revealing the distance between his reading and the peshat.

## Hearing and Believing

Paul's assertion that "faith comes from hearing" (10:17) and his lament that Israel has heard but not believed raises questions. Jewish scholars respond that Israel has heard and remains faithful to the Torah. The disagreement is about which word constitutes God's definitive revelation.
