## Paul's Anguish for Israel

Paul's declaration that he could wish himself "accursed" for his kinsmen (9:3) reveals his deep identification with the Jewish people. Jewish scholars like Daniel Boyarin read this as evidence that Paul never ceased being a Jew, even as he developed a theology that would lead to the parting of the ways.

## Israel's Privileges

Paul's enumeration of Israel's prerogatives (9:4) is an extraordinary affirmation within the New Testament. Jewish readers note these privileges are listed in the present tense: they belong to Israel now. This verse has become important in Jewish-Christian dialogue as a counter to supersessionism.

## Election and Freedom

The examples of Jacob and Esau raise the question of divine election. Jewish theology affirms God's sovereign choice of Israel as a people but resists applying this to individual salvation. The rabbis taught that Esau's rejection was based on foreknown conduct. In the prophetic contexts Paul cites, the emphasis is on repentance and God's willingness to relent.
