## Abraham's Faith as Participation

Orthodox interpreters read Abraham's faith (4:3) not as mere intellectual assent but as a living, participatory relationship with God. Abraham's willingness to leave his homeland and to offer Isaac demonstrates faith inseparable from obedience and love. The "counting" of faith as righteousness describes a real transformation, not a forensic fiction.

## Before the Law

Paul's argument that Abraham was justified before circumcision (4:10) is understood by the Fathers as demonstrating that God's grace transcends all historical dispensations. Chrysostom emphasizes that Abraham's example demolishes the claim that external rites are sufficient for salvation.

## The God Who Gives Life to the Dead

Paul's description of Abraham's faith in "the God who gives life to the dead" (4:17) resonates with Orthodox paschal theology. The resurrection of Christ is the supreme instance of God giving life to the dead, and every act of faith participates in this same divine power.
