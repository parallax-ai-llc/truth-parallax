## God's Faithfulness to Israel

Orthodox theology reads Romans 11 as affirming the ongoing significance of Israel in God's plan. Paul's declaration that "the gifts and the calling of God are irrevocable" (11:29) means that God's covenant with the Jewish people has not been annulled.

## The Olive Tree and Ecclesial Humility

The olive tree metaphor (11:17-24) warns Gentile Christians against boasting over the "natural branches." Orthodox ecclesiology takes this warning seriously: the Church's identity is rooted in Israel's story.

## The Depth of God's Wisdom

The concluding doxology (11:33) is a liturgical high point. Orthodox theology responds to the mystery of election with worship rather than systematic resolution. The doxology acknowledges that God's ways surpass human comprehension, and the proper response is prayerful wonder.
