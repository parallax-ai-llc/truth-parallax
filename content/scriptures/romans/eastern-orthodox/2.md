## The Justice of God

Orthodox interpretation reads God's judgment not as juridical condemnation but as the revelation of truth. God's impartiality (2:11) reflects divine justice understood as fairness and healing rather than retribution. Chrysostom emphasizes that Paul's argument targets self-righteousness.

## The Natural Law and the Heart

Paul's teaching that Gentiles "show the work of the law written on their hearts" (2:15) aligns with the Orthodox understanding of the human person created in God's image. The nous (spiritual intellect) retains a natural capacity to perceive moral truth even apart from explicit revelation.

## Circumcision of the Heart

The concept of interior circumcision "by the Spirit" (2:29) anticipates the Orthodox theology of baptism as the sacrament that transforms the whole person. The Fathers read this as a call to the ascetic life, the ongoing purification of the heart that enables the vision of God.
