## Baptism as Paschal Participation

Romans 6 is read liturgically at the Orthodox baptismal service and Paschal Vigil. The immersion of the catechumen enacts Paul's theology: burial with Christ in death so that one might walk in newness of life (6:4). This is sacramental reality: the baptized person truly participates in Christ's death and resurrection.

## Freedom from the Tyranny of Death

Paul's declaration that "sin will have no dominion over you" (6:14) describes liberation from the reign of death and corruption. However, this freedom requires ongoing cooperation through the ascetic struggle to "present your members as instruments of righteousness" (6:13).

## The Wages of Sin and the Gift of God

The chapter's conclusion (6:23) encapsulates the Orthodox understanding: sin pays its wage naturally as corruption and death; God's gift is participation in eternal, divine life, theosis in its fullest expression.
