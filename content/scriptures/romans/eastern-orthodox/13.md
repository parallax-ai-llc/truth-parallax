## Church and State: The Principle of Symphony

Romans 13:1-7 is read through the lens of symphonia, the Byzantine ideal of cooperation between Church and State. Civil authority is divinely ordained but does not have authority over the Church in spiritual matters. When the State oversteps its bounds, obedience to God takes precedence.

## Love as Fulfillment

Paul's teaching that "love is the fulfilling of the law" (13:10) resonates with the Orthodox understanding that the commandments express the divine love constituting the life of the Trinity. Love reveals the inner meaning of the commandments.

## Putting on Christ

The call to "put on the Lord Jesus Christ" (13:14) carries baptismal and ascetic significance. In baptism, the newly illumined are clothed in white garments symbolizing Christ. The ongoing "putting on" of Christ is the daily work of prayer, fasting, repentance, and participation in the sacraments.
