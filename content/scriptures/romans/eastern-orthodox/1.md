## The Gospel as Divine Power

Chrysostom's homilies on Romans 1 emphasize that the gospel is not merely information but transformative power (dynamis). God's saving action is a healing of human nature corrupted by the passions. The patristic reading sees Paul as a physician diagnosing the human condition before prescribing the cure.

## The Knowledge of God Through Creation

Paul's assertion that God's attributes are "clearly perceived" through creation (1:19-20) resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the logoi, the divine principles embedded in all created things (Maximus the Confessor). Creation is a theophany, revealing God's wisdom and goodness. The failure described in 1:21-23 is a spiritual blindness caused by the passions.

## The Descent of the Passions

The catalogue of sins in 1:24-32 is read by Orthodox moral theology as a description of the passions (pathe) that enslave the soul when it turns from God. The threefold "God gave them up" is understood not as punitive wrath but as God respecting human freedom: when humanity rejects the divine light, God allows the natural consequences to unfold.
