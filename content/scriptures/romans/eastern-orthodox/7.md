## The Law and Spiritual Warfare

Orthodox interpreters read Romans 7 through ascetic theology. Paul's description of inner conflict maps onto the patristic understanding of the battle between the nous and the pathe (passions). The Law reveals the good but cannot empower its fulfillment; only the grace of the Holy Spirit can heal the divided will.

## The Goodness of the Law

Chrysostom insists that Paul exalts the Law: it is "holy and righteous and good" (7:12), and the fault lies with sin, not the commandment. Orthodox theology does not share the sharp Lutheran antithesis between Law and Gospel; the Torah is a stage in God's progressive pedagogy.

## The Cry for Deliverance

Paul's anguished question (7:24) is the cry of every person on the path of repentance. Orthodox spirituality recognizes this struggle as the normal experience of the Christian life. The answer through Christ (7:25) points to the sacramental and ascetic life as the arena in which deliverance is progressively realized.
