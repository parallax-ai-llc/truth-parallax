## The Universal Condition

Romans 3:9-20 establishes that all are "under sin." Orthodox theology reads this not primarily in juridical terms but in ontological terms: humanity is enslaved to death, corruption, and the passions. The problem is not merely guilt but the disintegration of human nature that occurred through the ancestral sin.

## The Righteousness of God

The revelation of God's righteousness "apart from the law" (3:21) is understood as God's own faithfulness and saving action. God's righteousness is participatory: believers are made righteous by sharing in God's own life through Christ and the Spirit. This is the beginning of theosis.

## Redemption and Healing

Paul's language of redemption and propitiation (3:24-25) is read by the Fathers as describing Christ's victory over death and the healing of human nature. Athanasius and Gregory of Nyssa emphasized that Christ assumed human nature to heal it from within. The cross is the defeat of death, sin, and the devil, the Christus Victor motif that predominates in Orthodox soteriology.
