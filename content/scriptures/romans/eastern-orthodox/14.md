## Fasting and Freedom

Orthodox interpretation of Romans 14 engages directly with the Church's extensive fasting tradition. Paul's discussion of dietary practices is read not as relativizing fasting disciplines but as establishing the priority of charity over judgment. The spirit in which the fast is kept matters more than the letter.

## Not Judging One Another

Paul's command not to pass judgment on one another (14:13) is central to Orthodox spiritual direction. The Fathers, especially John Climacus, treat judging others as one of the most dangerous spiritual vices. The Lenten prayer of St. Ephrem asks God to remove "the spirit of judging."

## Living and Dying to the Lord

Paul's statement that "whether we live or die, we belong to the Lord" (14:8) is read at Orthodox funeral services, affirming that death does not sever the bond between believer and Christ. The communion of saints unites the living and the departed.
