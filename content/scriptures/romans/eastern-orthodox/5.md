## Ancestral Sin, Not Original Guilt

Romans 5:12 is the most theologically consequential verse in the Orthodox reading. The Greek text is translated "because all sinned" rather than the Latin "in whom all sinned." Orthodox theology holds that humanity inherited mortality and a tendency toward sin from Adam, but not personal guilt. Death is the enemy, and it is death that leads all to sin.

## The Superabundance of Grace

Paul's Adam-Christ typology (5:12-21) is read through Irenaeus's recapitulation theology: Christ recapitulates every stage of human life that Adam corrupted. Grace does not merely restore the pre-Fall state but elevates humanity to participation in the divine nature (2 Peter 1:4).

## Peace and Hope

The opening declaration of peace with God (5:1) and hope of glory (5:2) describes the state of the baptized person on the path of theosis. Suffering produces endurance, character, and hope (5:3-4). Orthodox spirituality sees this as the ascetic path where trials become opportunities for growth in Christ.
