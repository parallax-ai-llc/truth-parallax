## The Universal Indictment

Romans 3:1-20 concludes Paul's argument that "all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God" (3:23). The chain of Old Testament quotations in 3:10-18 — "None is righteous, no, not one" — functions as a comprehensive biblical case for total depravity. Protestant theology, especially in its Reformed expression, takes this as foundational: humanity is incapable of self-rescue.

## The Righteousness of God Revealed

Romans 3:21-26 is widely considered the theological center of the letter. God's righteousness is manifested "apart from the law" through faith in Jesus Christ. The key term *hilasterion* (3:25) — variously translated as "propitiation" or "expiation" — has generated extensive Protestant debate. Those favoring "propitiation" (Leon Morris, John Piper) argue that Christ's death satisfies God's wrath; those favoring "expiation" (C.H. Dodd) emphasize the removal of sin.

## Faith Apart from Works of the Law

Paul's conclusion that "a person is justified by faith apart from works of the law" (3:28) became the Reformation's battle cry. Luther added the word *allein* ("alone") in his German translation, insisting it captured Paul's meaning even though it does not appear in the Greek text. This verse remains the primary proof text for *sola fide* in Protestant theology.
