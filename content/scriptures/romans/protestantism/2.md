## God's Impartial Judgment

Romans 2 turns the argument toward those who judge others while practicing the same sins. Protestant interpreters read this chapter as Paul's rhetorical strategy to level the playing field between Jew and Gentile before God's judgment. The principle that God "will render to each one according to his works" (2:6) does not contradict justification by faith but establishes the standard that no one can meet apart from grace.

## The Law Written on the Heart

Paul's statement that Gentiles "do by nature the things in the law" and show "the work of the law written on their hearts" (2:14-15) has been pivotal for Protestant natural law theory. Calvin saw this as evidence of a universal moral awareness, while Barth cautioned against building too much on natural theology, insisting that true knowledge of God comes only through Christ.

## True Circumcision

Paul's argument that "circumcision of the heart, by the Spirit" (2:29) surpasses physical circumcision reflects a prophetic tradition (Deuteronomy 30:6; Jeremiah 4:4). Protestant interpreters see this as anticipating the New Covenant reality where external religious markers give way to inward transformation by the Holy Spirit.
