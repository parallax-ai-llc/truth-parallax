## Christ, the End of the Law

Romans 10 contains Paul's declaration that "Christ is the end (*telos*) of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes" (10:4). Protestant interpreters debate whether *telos* means "termination" (Christ abolishes the Law's condemning function) or "goal" (Christ is what the Law always pointed toward). Both readings affirm that righteousness now comes through faith in Christ.

## The Word of Faith

Paul's quotation of Deuteronomy 30:12-14 to describe the accessibility of the gospel — "The word is near you, in your mouth and in your heart" (10:8) — demonstrates the Protestant conviction that salvation is not achieved through heroic effort but received through simple confession and belief. The formula "if you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved" (10:9) is one of the most frequently cited verses in Protestant evangelism.

## The Necessity of Preaching

Paul's chain of reasoning — "how are they to believe in him of whom they have never heard? And how are they to hear without someone preaching?" (10:14) — provides the theological foundation for the Protestant emphasis on preaching and mission. The Reformation's elevation of the sermon as the central act of worship is grounded in this passage's insistence that "faith comes from hearing, and hearing through the word of Christ" (10:17).
