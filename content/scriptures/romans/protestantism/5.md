## Adam and Christ: Two Representatives

Romans 5:12-21 establishes the Adam-Christ typology that is foundational to Protestant covenant theology. Adam's disobedience brought sin and death to all humanity; Christ's obedience brings righteousness and life. Reformed theology formalized this as "federal headship" — Adam and Christ each act as covenant representatives for their respective peoples.

## Peace with God

The chapter opens with the declaration that "since we have been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ" (5:1). Protestant interpreters stress that this peace is an objective reality — a changed legal status — not merely a subjective feeling. The believer stands in grace (5:2) and boasts in hope of the glory of God.

## Grace Abounding

Paul's climactic statement that "where sin increased, grace abounded all the more" (5:20) captures the Protestant emphasis on the superabundance of grace. Grace does not merely match sin; it overwhelms it. This verse became central to Reformation preaching and hymnody, assuring believers that no depth of sin can exceed the reach of God's mercy in Christ.
