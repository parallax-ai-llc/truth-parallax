## The Mystery Revealed

The closing doxology's reference to "the mystery that was kept secret for long ages but has now been disclosed" (16:25-26) was the capstone of Gnostic interpretation. Valentinians cited this as Paul's acknowledgment that his teaching had an exoteric level for the psychic church and an esoteric level for the pneumatic elect.

## The Chain of Transmission

The greetings in Romans 16 were read as evidence of the apostolic network through which esoteric teaching was transmitted. Valentinus claimed a connection to Paul through Theudas, and Paul's co-workers were seen as initiated pneumatics carrying the secret tradition.

## Glory to the Unknown God

The final doxology (16:27) was read as praise directed to the supreme God of the Pleroma, not the Demiurge. The "only wise God" is the Monad, the Forefather, from whom all emanation proceeds. Through Jesus Christ, the Pleromatic revealer, the pneumatic offers glory to the God the Demiurge does not know.
