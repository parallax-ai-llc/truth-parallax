## Pneumatic Election

Romans 9's discussion of divine election was the primary Gnostic proof text for fixed natures. The choice of Jacob over Esau "before they had done anything" (9:11) demonstrated that salvation depends on nature, not conduct. Pneumatics are chosen from before creation; hylics are destined for destruction; psychics occupy an intermediate position.

## The Potter and the Three Natures

The potter metaphor (9:20-23) was mapped onto Valentinian tripartite anthropology. "Vessels of mercy" (9:23) are pneumatics; "vessels of wrath" (9:22) are hylics. The psychics, central to Valentinian theology, are vessels whose fate depends on whether they receive pneumatic instruction.

## The Demiurge's Ignorance

Paul's citation about Pharaoh (9:17) was read as describing the Demiurge's manipulation of the material world. The Demiurge "hardens" and "shows mercy" within his domain, but does not know the true God above him. The supreme God's election operates at a level the Demiurge cannot perceive.
