## Universal Captivity

Paul's declaration that "all have sinned" (3:23) was read as describing souls trapped in the Demiurge's material creation. "Sin" is ontological captivity, the state of the divine spark entombed in matter. "Falling short of the glory of God" refers to the Pleroma's glory, from which all material beings are separated.

## Redemption Through Gnosis

The language of "redemption" (apolytrosis, 3:24) was claimed by Valentinians as a technical term for a sacramental rite of liberation. Irenaeus reports that certain Valentinians practiced a rite called apolytrosis involving anointing and secret formulas. Paul's use was seen as evidence he knew this tradition.

## The True God Revealed

The revelation of God's righteousness "apart from the law" (3:21) was interpreted as the supreme God breaking through the Demiurge's legal system. The "law" belongs to the Demiurge; "righteousness" comes from beyond. Faith in Christ (3:22) is the gnosis that recognizes this higher reality.
