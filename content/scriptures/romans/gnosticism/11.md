## The Olive Tree and the Two Seeds

Gnostic interpreters read the olive tree metaphor (11:17-24) through the doctrine of two seeds. The "natural branches" (Israel) represent the psychic remnant who received the Demiurge's law but lacked gnosis. The "wild branches" (Gentiles) represent pneumatic elect who possess the divine spark despite being outside the Demiurge's covenant.

## The Mystery Revealed

Paul's "mystery" (11:25) was read as classic esoteric teaching. Gnostics claimed Paul communicated mysteries openly to the psychic church but reserved deeper meaning for the pneumatic elite. The "fullness" (pleroma) of the Gentiles (11:25) deliberately employs the Gnostic technical term.

## The Doxology and Apophatic Theology

The concluding doxology (11:33) was embraced as affirming the incomprehensibility of the true God. The Demiurge's ways are scrutable; it is the supreme God whose wisdom exceeds understanding. This aligned with the Gnostic emphasis on the absolute transcendence of the true divine being.
