## Baptism as Mystery Initiation

Gnostic interpreters read Romans 6's baptismal theology as describing a mystery rite liberating the pneumatic from the Demiurge's dominion. Being "baptized into Christ's death" (6:3) meant dying to the material world. The "newness of life" (6:4) was the awakening of the pneumatic self to its true identity as a spark of the Pleroma.

## Dead to the Archons

Paul's declaration that "our old self was crucified" so that "the body of sin might be brought to nothing" (6:6) was read as the destruction of the archontic "body," the material layers imprisoning the spirit. The pneumatic who has received gnosis is "dead" to the material world's powers.

## Slaves of the True God

Paul's metaphor of slavery (6:18) was interpreted as the transfer of allegiance from the Demiurge to the true God. The pneumatic Gnostic no longer serves the creator of the material world but is devoted to the unknown God of the Pleroma. This transfer is accomplished through sacramental reception of gnosis.
