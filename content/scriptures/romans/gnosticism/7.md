## The Demiurge's Law

Paul's statement that the Law is "holy and righteous and good" (7:12) yet produces death was interpreted as a precise description of the Demiurge's character: just within his limited sphere but fundamentally ignorant of higher reality. His law functions within the material cosmos but cannot reach the pneumatic dimension.

## The Pneumatic Trapped in Matter

The conflict of 7:14-25 was read as the experience of the pneumatic spark imprisoned in a material body governed by the archons. The "law of sin in my members" (7:23) describes archontic forces operating through the body; "delight in the law of God" (7:22) describes the pneumatic self recognizing its true nature.

## Deliverance Through Gnosis

Paul's cry "Who will deliver me from this body of death?" (7:24) was the quintessential Gnostic question. The "body of death" is the Demiurge's creation; deliverance through Christ (7:25) is the escape of spirit from its material prison. This made Romans 7 one of the most important Gnostic texts.
