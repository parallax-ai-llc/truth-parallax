## The Pneumatic Offering

Paul's call to present one's body as "a living sacrifice" (12:1) was interpreted as the offering of the material self to be consumed by gnosis. "Spiritual worship" (logike latreia) refers to the pneumatic's rational service, the cultivation of gnosis rather than the Demiurge's ritual sacrifices.

## Transformation by Gnosis

The command to "be transformed by the renewal of your mind" (12:2) was the definitive Gnostic proof text for salvation through knowledge. The "mind" (nous) is the pneumatic faculty that perceives the Pleroma. "Conformity to this world" means acquiescence to the Demiurge's order; "transformation" is the gnosis that liberates.

## Gifts of the Spirit

The diversity of gifts (12:6-8) was mapped by Valentinians onto their hierarchy. Prophecy and teaching belong to pneumatic teachers; service and mercy are for psychic faithful. This reinforced the Gnostic claim to spiritual authority.
