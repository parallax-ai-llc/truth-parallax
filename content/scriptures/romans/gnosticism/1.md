## The Demiurge's Wrath

Gnostic interpreters read Romans 1:18 as describing not the supreme God's judgment but the Demiurge's anger. The true God is beyond wrath. Valentinians saw Paul as carefully distinguishing between the Demiurge's justice (which punishes) and the true God's grace (which saves). The "God" of 1:18 and the "God" of 1:16 are not the same being.

## The Exchange of Glory

Paul's description of humanity exchanging "the glory of the immortal God for images" (1:23) was read as describing the Demiurge's own error: he mistook his limited glory for ultimate reality and fashioned a world of copies in place of the true Pleroma.

## The Passions as Archontic Influence

The catalogue of vices (1:29-31) was interpreted as the influence of the archons governing the material world. These passions are cosmic forces imposed on humanity by lower powers. Liberation requires not moral effort but gnosis, the recognition that one's true self is a divine spark trapped in archontic matter.
