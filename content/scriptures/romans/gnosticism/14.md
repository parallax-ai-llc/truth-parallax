## The Weak and the Strong as Psychics and Pneumatics

Gnostic interpreters mapped Paul's distinction between "weak" and "strong" (14:1-2) onto tripartite anthropology. The "weak" who observe dietary rules are psychics bound by the Demiurge's regulations. The "strong" who eat freely are pneumatics liberated by gnosis from the Demiurge's law.

## Knowledge and Freedom

Paul's statement that "nothing is unclean in itself" (14:14) was embraced as evidence that Paul shared the Gnostic view of material irrelevance. If nothing material is inherently clean or unclean, the Demiurge's dietary laws are arbitrary impositions. Some Gnostic groups took this to libertine conclusions.

## Paul's Accommodation

The instruction not to offend the weak (14:13-21) was read as Paul's tactical accommodation to the psychic majority. Paul, himself a pneumatic, counseled restraint in the presence of those who could not bear the full truth. This became the model for Gnostic secrecy.
