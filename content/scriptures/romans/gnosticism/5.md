## The Fall into Matter

Paul's Adam-Christ typology (5:12-21) was central to Gnostic cosmology. Adam's "sin" was reinterpreted as the fall of the divine spark into the material world. "Death" spreading to all (5:12) describes the entombment of spirit in matter, not moral transgression.

## Christ as the Emissary of the Pleroma

Christ's role in bringing "the free gift" (5:15-17) was read as the descent of a Pleromatic Aeon to liberate imprisoned sparks. The "obedience" of Christ (5:19) is his mission of revelation, bringing gnosis that restores pneumatic beings to their origin.

## Grace Over Law

Paul's contrast between trespass and free gift (5:16) was mapped onto the distinction between the Demiurge's regime (condemnation through law) and the true God's gift (liberation through gnosis). Romans 5 became a proof text for the claim that salvation comes through knowledge, not obedience to the creator God.
