## The Rulers of This World

Gnostic interpreters read Romans 13:1-7 not as political teaching but as a coded discussion of the archons. The "governing authorities" (exousiai) are the cosmic powers ruling the material world. Paul's instruction to "be subject" is practical advice for pneumatics living in the archons' realm: do not provoke the powers unnecessarily while embodied.

## Putting on the Armor of Light

Paul's call to "put on the armor of light" (13:12) was read as the pneumatic's progressive liberation from archontic influence. The "works of darkness" are activities inspired by the archons; the "armor of light" is gnosis itself, the luminous garment the redeemed spirit will wear upon returning to the Pleroma.

## Putting on Christ

"Put on the Lord Jesus Christ" (13:14) was interpreted as the pneumatic's assumption of the spiritual Christ, the Pleromatic Aeon. This is mystical identification: the pneumatic "puts on" Christ as a garment of light, recovering the luminous nature lost when the divine spark fell into matter.
