## The Demiurge's Judgment

Gnostic interpreters read Romans 2's discussion of judgment as describing the Demiurge's characteristic mode of operation. The Demiurge renders to each according to works (2:6), but this is the justice of the lower world, not the unconditional grace of the true God. The psychic lives under this regime; the pneumatic has transcended it through gnosis.

## The Law Written on the Heart

Paul's reference to "the work of the law written on their hearts" (2:15) was read as evidence of the divine spark, the pneumatic seed planted by Sophia in certain human beings. This inner knowledge surpasses the Demiurge's external law.

## True Circumcision as Gnosis

Paul's contrast between external circumcision and "circumcision of the heart" (2:29) was appropriated as describing the difference between the Demiurge's external rites and the interior knowledge that liberates the spirit. True "circumcision" occurs through gnosis, not any physical ritual.
