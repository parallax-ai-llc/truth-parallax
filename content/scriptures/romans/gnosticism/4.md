## Abraham as Proto-Gnostic

Valentinian interpreters read Abraham's faith (4:3) as a form of gnosis, a direct perception of the true God beyond the Demiurge. Abraham's departure from his homeland (Genesis 12:1) was allegorized as the pneumatic soul's departure from the material world.

## Before the Law

Paul's emphasis that Abraham's justification preceded circumcision and the Law (4:10-13) was crucial for Gnostic exegesis. It demonstrated that the pneumatic relationship with the true God is prior to and independent of the Demiurge's legal dispensation.

## The God Who Creates from Nothing

Paul's description of God as one "who gives life to the dead and calls into existence the things that do not exist" (4:17) was read through the cosmogonic myth. The true God's creative power operates beyond the Demiurge's capacity. The Demiurge fashions matter; the true God creates being itself.
