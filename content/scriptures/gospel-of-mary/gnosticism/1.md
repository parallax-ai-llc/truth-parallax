For the Gnostic reader, chapter 1 of the Gospel of Mary presents a foundational teaching on the nature of matter and sin. The Savior's declaration that matter will be dissolved into its own root articulates the Gnostic conviction that the material world is temporary and destined for dissolution. Sin, in this framework, is not moral transgression against a divine law but the condition of being entangled with matter — a mixing of spiritual and material natures that was never meant to occur.

This teaching liberates the Gnostic from conventional notions of guilt and moral failure. If sin is an ontological condition rather than a moral choice, then salvation cannot come through obedience to laws or through sacrificial atonement. Instead, the cure for sin is gnosis — the recognition that one's true nature is spiritual, not material. The Savior's instruction to seek the Good within oneself directs the disciple toward interior knowledge as the path of liberation, away from external religious observance.
