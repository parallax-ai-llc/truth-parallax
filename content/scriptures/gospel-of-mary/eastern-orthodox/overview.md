## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox Church does not accept the Gospel of Mary as canonical or authoritative. The text's theology — particularly its view of matter as the product of illicit mixture and its soteriological framework centered on gnosis rather than theosis — is incompatible with Orthodox teaching. Nevertheless, the Orthodox tradition holds Mary Magdalene in the highest regard, venerating her as "Equal-to-the-Apostles" (Isapostolos) and honoring her as the first witness to the resurrection.

## Orthodox Perspective on Authority

The dispute between Peter and Mary in the Gospel of Mary touches on questions of authority that the Orthodox tradition resolves through conciliar and patristic consensus rather than individual visionary experience. While Orthodoxy has a rich tradition of mystical theology and values personal experience of God (theoria), it insists that such experience must be tested against the apostolic faith as preserved in Scripture, the Ecumenical Councils, and the writings of the Fathers. The text's implied critique of hierarchical authority is thus received critically.

## Historical Value

Orthodox scholars acknowledge the Gospel of Mary as valuable evidence for the diversity of early Christian communities. The text's portrayal of tensions over Mary Magdalene's authority may reflect genuine historical disputes within second-century Christianity. The Orthodox tradition's own high regard for Magdalene suggests that the memory of her prominent role was preserved even in communities that ultimately rejected the theological framework of texts like this Gospel.
