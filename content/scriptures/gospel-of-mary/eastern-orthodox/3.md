The Orthodox tradition venerates Mary Magdalene as "Equal-to-the-Apostles" (Isapostolos) and celebrates her as the first witness to the resurrection, giving her a place of honor in the liturgical calendar. The dispute in chapter 3 thus touches on a figure whom the Orthodox Church already holds in high esteem. However, the text's resolution of the dispute — validating Mary's authority on the basis of the Savior's personal preference rather than ecclesial discernment — conflicts with the Orthodox model of authority.

Orthodox ecclesiology is conciliar rather than individual: authority resides in the consensus of the Church gathered in council, guided by the Holy Spirit and tested against the apostolic tradition. The Gospel of Mary presents an alternative model in which individual spiritual experience and personal relationship with the Savior constitute the basis for authoritative teaching. While Orthodoxy values personal holiness and spiritual insight, it insists that these must be received and validated within the community of the Church rather than standing as autonomous sources of doctrinal authority.
