Mary's vision of the soul's ascent past hostile powers bears structural similarities to Jewish Merkabah and Hekhalot traditions, in which the mystic ascends through heavenly palaces guarded by angelic gatekeepers. In both traditions, knowledge — of divine names, passwords, or one's true identity — is essential for passing safely through each stage. Jewish scholars have noted these parallels as evidence for the common mystical heritage shared by early Jewish and Gnostic-Christian traditions.

However, the theological frameworks differ significantly. In Jewish Merkabah mysticism, the goal of the ascent is the vision of God seated on the heavenly throne — an experience of awe and worship before the Creator. In the Gospel of Mary, the soul ascends past powers that are obstacles to liberation, and the goal is escape from the material cosmos. The Jewish tradition celebrates the encounter with the divine within the framework of creation; the Gospel of Mary seeks escape from creation altogether.
