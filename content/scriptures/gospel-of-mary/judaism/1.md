The Gospel of Mary's teaching on sin and matter represents an intra-Christian discussion with little direct relevance to Jewish theology. However, Jewish scholars note that the text's redefinition of sin as material entanglement departs from the Jewish understanding of sin (chet) as missing the mark — a moral and relational category involving the violation of God's commandments and the disruption of covenant faithfulness.

The Jewish tradition does address the tension between material and spiritual existence through the concept of the yetzer ha-ra (evil inclination), but this is understood as a morally neutral drive that can be directed toward good through Torah observance, not as evidence that material existence itself is sinful. The Gospel of Mary's ontological understanding of sin as an inherent feature of material existence has no parallel in Jewish thought, which affirms the goodness of the created world and the possibility of righteous life within it.
