Chapter 2 also suffers from significant lacunae — four pages are missing from the middle of Mary's vision, leaving scholars to reconstruct the full sequence of powers the soul encounters. The surviving text names Desire, Ignorance, and Wrath (with its seven forms: Darkness, Desire, Ignorance, Zeal of Death, Kingdom of the Flesh, Foolish Wisdom of the Flesh, and Wrathful Wisdom). Scholars have debated whether this taxonomy reflects Stoic passion theory, Platonic psychology, or an independent Gnostic systematization of the obstacles to spiritual liberation.

The soul-ascent genre itself has been the subject of comparative study. Ion Couliano, April DeConick, and others have traced the motif across Gnostic, Jewish, Greco-Roman, and Iranian traditions. The Gospel of Mary's version is distinctive for its brevity and its emphasis on the soul's dialogue with the powers — the soul overcomes not through magical passwords or angelic assistance but through philosophical argumentation about its own nature. This has led some scholars to characterize the text as more philosophical than mythological in its soteriology.
