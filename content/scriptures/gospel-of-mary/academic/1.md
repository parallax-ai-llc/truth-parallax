The first six pages of the Gospel of Mary are missing from the Berlin Codex, meaning that chapter 1 as we have it begins mid-conversation. Scholars must work with a fragmentary text, and attempts to reconstruct the lost opening rely on the internal logic of the surviving material and comparison with the two Greek fragments (P.Rylands 463 and P.Oxyrhynchus 3525). The extent and content of the lost material remain subjects of scholarly speculation.

The Savior's teaching on matter and sin in the surviving portion has attracted attention for its possible engagement with Stoic physics, particularly the concept of mixture (krasis). The claim that sin results from improper mixing echoes Stoic discussions of the blending of elements, though deployed for entirely different philosophical ends. Scholars including Anne Pasquier and Karen King have debated whether the text represents a form of Christian Stoicism, a Gnostic appropriation of Stoic categories, or an independent development that coincidentally employs similar language.
