Protestant theology finds the Savior's teaching in chapter 1 deeply problematic. The claim that sin results from the improper mixing of spiritual and material natures contradicts the biblical understanding of sin as willful rebellion against God. Protestant hamartiology, rooted in Paul's letters and developed by the Reformers, insists that sin is a moral and relational category — the creature's refusal to trust and obey the Creator — not an ontological condition arising from material entanglement.

The teaching that matter will dissolve into its own root also conflicts with Protestant eschatology, which anticipates not the annihilation of the material world but its renewal and transformation. Romans 8:21 speaks of creation being "set free from its bondage to corruption," and Revelation 21 envisions "a new heaven and a new earth." The Gospel of Mary's cosmology, which treats matter as inherently problematic, stands at odds with the Protestant affirmation that the physical world is God's good creation, destined for redemption rather than dissolution.
