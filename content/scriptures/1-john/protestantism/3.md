## Children of God

1 John 3:1–3 marvels at the love God has bestowed: "that we should be called children of God, and so we are." Protestant interpreters emphasize the present reality of adoption — believers are God's children now — and the eschatological hope: "when he appears we shall be like him" (3:2). This grounds the Protestant doctrine of glorification as the final stage of salvation.

## Righteousness and Sin

1 John 3:4–10 draws a sharp line between the children of God and the children of the devil. The declaration that "no one born of God makes a practice of sinning" (3:9) has generated extensive Protestant debate. Most interpreters read this as describing the habitual pattern of life rather than absolute sinlessness: the genuine believer does not continue in persistent, unrepentant sin.

## Love in Action

1 John 3:16–24 defines love in terms of Christ's self-sacrifice: "He laid down his life for us, and we ought to lay down our lives for the brothers." Protestant interpreters emphasize that love is not sentiment but action — "Let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth" (3:18). This concrete love provides assurance before God (3:19–21) and confirms that we abide in him.
