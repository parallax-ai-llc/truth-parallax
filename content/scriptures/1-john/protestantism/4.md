## Testing the Spirits

1 John 4:1–6 instructs believers to "test the spirits" by the criterion of the incarnation: "Every spirit that confesses that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh is from God." Protestant interpreters read this as a call to doctrinal discernment within the church. The confession of the incarnation is the non-negotiable boundary separating Christian faith from its counterfeits.

## God Is Love

1 John 4:7–12 contains the letter's theological climax: "God is love" (4:8). Protestant interpreters understand this not merely as a description of God's activity but as a revelation of the divine nature. God's love is demonstrated supremely in the sending of his Son as "the propitiation for our sins" (4:10). The Reformed tradition emphasizes that this propitiatory love is the foundation of all genuine human love.

## Perfect Love Casts Out Fear

1 John 4:17–21 teaches that "perfect love casts out fear" (4:18). Protestant pastoral theology draws on this passage to address the anxiety of believers who struggle with assurance. The love of God, fully received and believed, removes the fear of judgment and produces confidence on the day of judgment. This is not earned confidence but the gift of divine love apprehended by faith.
