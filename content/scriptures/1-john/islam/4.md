## Testing Spiritual Claims

1 John 4:1–3, calling believers to test spiritual claims against the criterion of right belief, resonates with the Islamic emphasis on discerning authentic from false religious teaching. The Quran warns against false prophets and commands the verification of reports: "O you who have believed, if there comes to you a disobedient one with information, investigate" (49:6). Both traditions insist on critical evaluation of spiritual claims rather than uncritical acceptance.

## God Is Love and Divine Attributes

1 John 4:8 — "God is love" — engages the Islamic theology of God's attributes (*sifat*). One of God's ninety-nine names in Islam is *Al-Wadud* (The Loving), and the Quran affirms God's love for specific categories of people: "Indeed, Allah loves those who are constantly repentant and loves those who purify themselves" (2:222). Muslim readers engage with John's statement while insisting that God's love is one of many divine attributes and cannot define the divine essence exhaustively.

## Love and the Absence of Fear

1 John 4:18 — "perfect love casts out fear" — touches on the Islamic discussion of *khawf* (fear of God) and *rajaa* (hope in God's mercy). The ideal Islamic spiritual state balances fear and hope, and the Sufi tradition speaks of advancing from fear-based worship to love-based worship. Muslim mystics like Rabi'a al-Adawiyya taught a love of God that transcended both fear of hell and desire for paradise, resonating with John's vision of perfect love.
