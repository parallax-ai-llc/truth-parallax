## Knowing God Through Obedience

1 John 2:3–6, teaching that knowledge of God is demonstrated through keeping his commandments, aligns directly with Islamic theology. In Islam, knowledge of God (*ma'rifah*) is inseparable from obedience to God's commands (*taat*). The Quran teaches: "Say, 'If you love Allah, then follow me, and Allah will love you'" (3:31). Muslim readers find in John's teaching a familiar integration of knowledge, love, and obedience.

## Warning Against the World

1 John 2:15–17 warns against love of the world and its passing desires. This resonates with the Quranic teaching on the transience of worldly life: "Know that the life of this world is but amusement and diversion and adornment and boasting to one another" (57:20). The Islamic tradition of *zuhd* (ascetic detachment from the world) echoes John's warning, though Islam frames it within the broader context of balance between worldly engagement and spiritual aspiration.

## False Teachers and the Corruption of Truth

1 John 2:18–23, warning about those who deny the truth and "went out from us," resonates with the Islamic concern about *bid'ah* (religious innovation) and the corruption of authentic teaching. The Quran warns against those who "distort words from their usages" (5:13), and the Islamic tradition carefully distinguishes authentic prophetic teaching from later corruption.
