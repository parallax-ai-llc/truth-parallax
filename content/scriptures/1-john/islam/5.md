## Faith as Victory

1 John 5:4 — "this is the victory that has overcome the world — our faith" — resonates with the Islamic concept of faith (*iman*) as the believer's source of strength against worldly temptation. The Quran promises: "And Allah will surely support those who support Him" (22:40). Both traditions affirm that genuine faith provides the resources to resist the pressures of a world that pulls believers away from God.

## Testimony and Truth

1 John 5:6–12, appealing to divine testimony about the Son, engages the Islamic emphasis on the reliability of God's witness (*shahadah*). While Muslim theology does not accept the Johannine testimony about Jesus as the divine Son, the principle that God provides truthful testimony is shared. The Islamic *shahada* — the testimony that there is no god but God and Muhammad is his messenger — represents a parallel structure of divine testimony and human response.

## Against Idolatry

1 John 5:21 — "keep yourselves from idols" — closes the letter with a command that resonates powerfully with the central Islamic principle of *tawhid* (God's absolute oneness). The prohibition of *shirk* (associating anything with God) is the most fundamental command in Islam. Muslim readers appreciate that 1 John, despite its Christological claims, closes with a warning that echoes the prophetic call to pure monotheism.
