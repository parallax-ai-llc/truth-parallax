## Overview

1 John is an epistle attributed to the author of the Fourth Gospel, written to a community experiencing internal division over Christological and ethical issues. Likely composed in the late first century, the letter addresses a schism caused by those who denied that Jesus came "in the flesh" — a proto-Docetic or proto-Gnostic position. The letter responds by insisting on the inseparable connection between correct belief about Christ, love for fellow believers, and moral obedience.

## Structure

1 John does not follow a linear argument but spirals through interconnected themes:

1. **Prologue: The Word of Life** (1:1–4): Eyewitness testimony to the incarnate Christ
2. **Walking in the Light** (1:5–2:17): Sin, confession, obedience, and the love command
3. **The Antichrist and True Believers** (2:18–27): Warning about those who deny Christ
4. **Children of God** (2:28–3:24): Righteousness, love, and confidence before God
5. **Testing the Spirits** (4:1–6): Discerning true from false teaching
6. **God Is Love** (4:7–21): The nature and source of love
7. **Faith, Testimony, and Assurance** (5:1–21): Victory through faith and confidence in eternal life

## Cross-Traditional Significance

1 John engages multiple traditions:

- **Protestantism**: A central text for the doctrine of assurance and the tests of genuine faith
- **Catholicism**: Important for sacramental theology, mortal and venial sin, and the theology of love
- **Eastern Orthodoxy**: The declaration "God is love" as foundational for the theology of the divine nature
- **Judaism**: The letter's sectarian dynamics illuminate intra-Jewish divisions in the first century
- **Islam**: The insistence on moral conduct alongside faith resonates with Islamic soteriology
- **Gnosticism**: The letter appears to combat proto-Gnostic Christology directly

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Love | God's love as the source and model for human love |
| Light and Darkness | The moral and theological contrast between truth and falsehood |
| Incarnation | The reality of Christ coming in the flesh |
| Assurance | Confidence of salvation through faith and love |
| Sin and Confession | The reality of sin and the provision of forgiveness |
| Antichrist | Those who deny the incarnation as forerunners of the antichrist |
