## Faith and the World

1 John 5:4 — "This is the victory that has overcome the world — our faith" — could be appropriated by Gnostic readers as the pneumatic's transcendence over the Demiurge's cosmos. However, the "faith" John describes is specific: it is faith that "Jesus is the Son of God" (5:5), confessing the identity of the human Jesus with the divine Son. This incarnational faith is the opposite of the Gnostic separation of the divine from the human.

## Water, Blood, and Spirit

1 John 5:6–8 insists that Jesus came "not by water only but by the water and the blood." This enigmatic statement may target the Cerinthian teaching that the divine Christ descended on Jesus at baptism (water) but departed before the crucifixion (blood). John insists on the unity of Christ through both events — a direct counter to the Gnostic Christological separation.

## Eternal Life and Gnosis

1 John 5:20 declares that "the Son of God has come and has given us understanding, so that we may know him who is true." The language of "understanding" and "knowing" resonates with Gnostic terminology, but the content is fundamentally different. The "knowledge" John describes is relational — knowing the true God through the incarnate Son — not cosmological gnosis about the structure of the Pleroma. The closing warning against "idols" (5:21) may include the Gnostic fabrication of false divine beings as objects of spiritual fascination.
