## The Anointing and Gnostic Claims

1 John 2:20, 27 — the "anointing" (*chrisma*) that teaches all things — uses language that Gnostic groups would have claimed for themselves. The Gnostic initiation rites often included anointing, and the Valentinians believed they possessed a special spiritual anointing that gave them access to hidden truth. 1 John turns this language against the secessionists: the true anointing belongs to the community that confesses the incarnation, not to those who deny it.

## The Antichrist and Gnostic Schism

1 John 2:18–23 identifies the "antichrists" as those who "went out from us" and deny that Jesus is the Christ. This describes a schism within the Johannine community, and the denial in view — separating the divine Christ from the human Jesus — matches the Gnostic Christology attributed to Cerinthus and later Gnostic teachers. The letter treats this denial as the most dangerous heresy because it strikes at the heart of salvation.

## Love of the World

1 John 2:15–17 warns against love of "the world." While Gnostic readers might agree with the negative assessment of the material world, John's meaning is quite different. For John, "the world" refers to human society organized against God — its desires and pride — not to the material cosmos as such. The Gnostic rejection of matter goes far beyond John's moral warning.
