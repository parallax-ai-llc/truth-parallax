## Testing the Spirits: The Incarnation Test

1 John 4:1–3 provides the definitive anti-Gnostic test: "Every spirit that confesses that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh is from God, and every spirit that does not confess Jesus is not from God." This passage directly targets the Gnostic separation of the divine Christ from the human Jesus. The Gnostic claim to spiritual authority through pneumatic revelation is subjected to the concrete, historical criterion of the incarnation.

## God Is Love and the Gnostic God

1 John 4:8 — "God is love" — presents a vision of God fundamentally different from the Gnostic portrait. The Gnostic transcendent God is characterized by silence, depth, and unknowability. While some Gnostic texts speak of divine love within the Pleroma, the Gnostic God does not love the material world (which is the Demiurge's mistake). 1 John insists that God loved the world by sending his Son into it (4:9–10) — precisely the world the Gnostics sought to escape.

## Fear and the Gnostic Condition

1 John 4:18 — "perfect love casts out fear" — addresses an experience familiar to Gnostic thought. The Gnostic condition is one of fear and alienation: the divine spark trapped in the hostile material cosmos, surrounded by the archons. The Gnostic answer to this fear is gnosis — the knowledge that liberates. John's answer is love — the love of God that enters the material world through the incarnation and dispels fear through intimate communion.
