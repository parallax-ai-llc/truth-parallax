## The Children of God and the Divine Spark

1 John 3:1–2 speaks of believers as "children of God" who will be transformed at Christ's appearing. Gnostic interpreters could read "children of God" as a description of the pneumatic class — those who possess the divine spark by nature. However, John's understanding is fundamentally different: divine childhood is a gift of love (3:1), not an innate metaphysical status. It is received through faith in the incarnate Christ, not through cosmological knowledge.

## The Seed of God

1 John 3:9 — "God's seed abides in him" — uses language strikingly similar to the Valentinian concept of the "spiritual seed" (*sperma pneumatikon*) implanted in the pneumatic by the divine Sophia. The Valentinians taught that this seed was the guarantee of salvation regardless of moral conduct. 1 John, by contrast, connects the divine seed directly to moral transformation: "no one born of God makes a practice of sinning."

## Cain and the Archons

1 John 3:12's reference to Cain, "who was of the evil one," resonates with Gnostic interpretations of Genesis. Some Gnostic texts, including the *Hypostasis of the Archons*, present Cain as the offspring of the archons — the evil cosmic rulers — rather than of Adam. While John's meaning is simpler (Cain as a moral example of hatred), the shared demonization of Cain shows the overlap between Johannine and Gnostic appropriation of Genesis traditions.
