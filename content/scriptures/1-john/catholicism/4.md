## Discernment of Spirits

1 John 4:1–6 calls believers to "test the spirits" by the confession of the incarnation. Catholic theology has developed an extensive tradition of discernment of spirits, from Ignatius of Loyola's Spiritual Exercises to modern charismatic renewal. The criterion remains the incarnation: any spiritual experience or teaching that diminishes the reality of Christ's flesh is not from God.

## God Is Love

1 John 4:8, 16 — "God is love" — is the theological foundation of Benedict XVI's encyclical *Deus Caritas Est*. Catholic theology understands this not as reducing God to an emotion but as revealing the inner life of the Trinity: the Father's eternal love for the Son in the Spirit. This Trinitarian love overflows into creation and redemption, and the Church's mission is to make this love visible in the world.

## Love and Confidence in Judgment

1 John 4:17–18 teaches that "perfect love casts out fear." Catholic theology reads this in relation to the particular and general judgment: the believer who lives in God's love approaches judgment not with terror but with confident hope. This confidence is not presumption but the fruit of a life lived in grace, sustained by the sacraments, and animated by charity.
