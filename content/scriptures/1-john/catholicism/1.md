## The Word Made Manifest

The prologue of 1 John (1:1–4) is read by Catholic interpreters in continuity with the Johannine prologue (John 1:1–18). The incarnate Word is not merely proclaimed but encountered — "heard," "seen," "touched." Catholic theology emphasizes that this encounter continues in the sacramental life of the Church, where believers encounter the incarnate Christ in the Eucharist, the Word proclaimed, and the community gathered.

## Fellowship and the Church

1 John 1:3 — "our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ" — grounds the Catholic understanding of the Church as communion (*koinonia*). The Church is not merely a human institution but a participation in the divine communion of Father and Son through the Holy Spirit. This Trinitarian fellowship is the deepest reality of ecclesial life.

## Confession and Forgiveness

1 John 1:9 — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins" — is a key text for the Catholic Sacrament of Reconciliation. Catholic theology reads this as affirming both the necessity of confession and the certainty of divine forgiveness. The sacramental form of confession to a priest is understood as the normative ecclesial expression of the confession described by John.
