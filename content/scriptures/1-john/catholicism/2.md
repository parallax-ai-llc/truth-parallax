## Christ Our Advocate

1 John 2:1–2 presents Christ as the believer's "advocate with the Father" and "the propitiation for our sins." Catholic theology reads this within the framework of Christ's ongoing heavenly intercession. The risen Christ continues to mediate for the faithful, and this intercession is made present in the sacrifice of the Mass, where Christ's propitiatory offering is represented sacramentally.

## The Anointing of the Holy Spirit

1 John 2:20, 27 speaks of an "anointing" (*chrisma*) that teaches believers all things. Catholic theology connects this anointing to the sacrament of Confirmation, in which the baptized receive the fullness of the Holy Spirit for the strengthening of faith. The "anointing" also informs the Catholic understanding of the *sensus fidei* — the instinct of faith given to the whole body of believers.

## The World and Its Desires

1 John 2:15–17 warns against love of the world: "the desires of the flesh and the desires of the eyes and pride of life." Catholic moral theology has drawn on this passage in its treatment of concupiscence — the disordered desires that remain after baptism and incline toward sin. The remedy is not withdrawal from the world but the cultivation of virtue through grace, the sacraments, and the moral life.
