## Children of God and Theosis

1 John 3:1–3 — "See what kind of love the Father has given to us, that we should be called children of God" — is read by Orthodox theology as a description of the deifying grace bestowed in baptism. The promise that "we shall be like him" (3:2) is the eschatological fulfillment of theosis — the complete transformation of the human person into the likeness of God. This transformation begins in the present through the sacramental and ascetic life.

## The Seed of God

1 John 3:9 — "No one born of God makes a practice of sinning, for God's seed abides in him" — is read by some Orthodox Fathers as referring to the gift of the Holy Spirit received in baptism and Chrismation. This divine "seed" — the grace of the Spirit — empowers the believer to resist sin and grow in holiness. The presence of the Spirit does not eliminate the struggle but provides the power to persevere.

## Love as Self-Sacrifice

1 John 3:16 — "He laid down his life for us, and we ought to lay down our lives for the brothers" — defines love in terms of Christ's kenosis (self-emptying). Orthodox spirituality reads this as the call to sacrificial love that characterizes the saints and martyrs. The lives of the saints are the practical commentary on this verse, demonstrating what it means to love "in deed and in truth" (3:18).
