## The Anointing and the Holy Spirit

1 John 2:20, 27 speaks of the "anointing" (*chrisma*) that teaches believers. Orthodox theology connects this to the sacrament of Chrismation — the anointing with holy oil that follows baptism and confers the gift of the Holy Spirit. The Chrismation is the personal Pentecost of each believer, sealing them with the Spirit and granting the capacity for spiritual discernment.

## The Antichrist and Orthodox Eschatology

1 John 2:18–23 warns of the antichrist and identifies many antichrists who deny the Father and the Son. Orthodox eschatology takes the figure of the antichrist seriously as both a present spiritual reality (every denial of Christ) and a future personal figure who will appear before Christ's return. The patristic tradition, from Irenaeus to John of Damascus, developed detailed teaching on the antichrist based in part on this passage.

## Love of the World

1 John 2:15–17 warns against love of the world. Orthodox ascetic theology reads this not as a rejection of material creation (which is good) but as a warning against attachment to the passions — the disordered desires that draw the soul away from God. The monastic tradition develops this teaching into a comprehensive program of spiritual combat aimed at liberating the heart from worldly attachment.
