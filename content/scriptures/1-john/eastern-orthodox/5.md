## Faith Born of God

1 John 5:1–5 teaches that faith born of God overcomes the world. Orthodox theology reads this as the fruit of baptismal regeneration: the new birth in water and the Spirit produces a faith capable of resisting the pressures of the fallen world. This faith is not a solitary possession but is lived within the community of the Church, sustained by the sacraments and the prayers of the saints.

## The Threefold Witness

1 John 5:6–8 speaks of the Spirit, the water, and the blood as witnesses. Orthodox interpretation connects these to the sacramental life: the water of baptism, the blood of the Eucharist, and the Spirit who animates both. The threefold witness is not merely historical (pointing to events in Jesus's life) but ongoing in the Church's liturgical practice.

## Assurance and Eternal Life

1 John 5:13 — "that you may know that you have eternal life" — is read in Orthodox theology not as a doctrine of once-for-all assurance but as an invitation to the continuous experience of divine life. Eternal life is not merely a future hope but a present reality encountered in prayer, the sacraments, and love for one another. The closing injunction to "keep yourselves from idols" (5:21) reminds the faithful that anything that replaces God as the center of life is an impediment to this ongoing communion.
