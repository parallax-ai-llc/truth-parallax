## Testing Prophetic Claims

1 John 4:1 — "test the spirits to see whether they are from God" — resonates with the Jewish tradition of testing prophetic claims. Deuteronomy 18:21–22 provides criteria for distinguishing true from false prophets, and the rabbinic tradition developed additional principles for evaluating religious authority. Jewish readers appreciate the call to critical discernment even within a community of faith.

## God Is Love in Jewish Perspective

1 John 4:8 — "God is love" — makes a claim about the divine nature that Jewish theology approaches cautiously. While the Hebrew Bible affirms that God acts lovingly — "I have loved you with an everlasting love" (Jeremiah 31:3) — Jewish theology is generally reluctant to reduce the divine nature to a single attribute. God is also just, holy, and mysterious. Jewish readers engage the Johannine claim as a profound but partial articulation of divine reality.

## Love and the Absence of Fear

1 John 4:18 — "Perfect love casts out fear" — touches on the Jewish theological discussion of the relationship between love and fear of God. Deuteronomy commands both: "You shall love the Lord your God" (6:5) and "You shall fear the Lord your God" (6:13). The rabbinic tradition distinguishes between *yirat ha-onesh* (fear of punishment) and *yirat ha-romemut* (awe of God's majesty), understanding the latter as compatible with — even integral to — love.
