## Faith and Commandments

1 John 5:1–5, linking faith to obedience — "this is the love of God, that we keep his commandments" (5:3) — resonates with the Jewish understanding of faith as faithfulness expressed through Torah observance. Jewish readers appreciate the integration of belief and practice that characterizes 1 John, in contrast to what they perceive as the Pauline separation of faith from works.

## Testimony and Witness

1 John 5:6–12, appealing to the testimony of the Spirit, water, and blood, engages the Jewish legal principle that truth is established by two or three witnesses (Deuteronomy 19:15). The Jewish legal tradition requires multiple witnesses for the establishment of facts, and John's appeal to a threefold testimony follows this evidentiary logic, even as the content of the testimony is distinctly Christian.

## Keeping from Idols

1 John 5:21 — "Little children, keep yourselves from idols" — closes the letter with a command that resonates with the most fundamental of all Jewish prohibitions. The Second Commandment forbids idolatry (Exodus 20:4–6), and the prophetic tradition consistently identifies idolatry as Israel's greatest sin. Jewish readers hear in this final verse an echo of the prophetic call that defines Judaism itself: the worship of the one God alone.
