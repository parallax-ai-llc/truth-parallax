## Overview

Zephaniah is a prophetic book set during the reign of King Josiah of Judah (c. 640-609 BCE). The prophet, whose name means "the LORD has hidden," traces his lineage back four generations to Hezekiah, suggesting royal ancestry. His ministry likely preceded and helped inspire Josiah's sweeping religious reforms.

## Structure

The book follows a classic prophetic pattern of judgment and restoration:

1. **The Day of the LORD Against Judah** (Chapter 1): A devastating oracle announcing cosmic judgment beginning with Judah
2. **Call to Repentance and Oracles Against Nations** (Chapter 2): Exhortation to seek the LORD, followed by judgments against Philistia, Moab, Ammon, Cush, and Assyria
3. **Woe and Restoration** (Chapter 3): Indictment of Jerusalem's leaders, followed by a dramatic reversal promising restoration and joyful reunion

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Zephaniah's themes resonate across multiple religious traditions:

- **Judaism**: Central to understandings of Yom YHWH and the theology of the faithful remnant (anawim)
- **Christianity**: The Day of the LORD imagery profoundly influenced New Testament eschatology and the medieval hymn *Dies Irae*
- **Islam**: The prophetic warning of a coming day of judgment echoes Quranic descriptions of Yawm al-Qiyamah
- **Academic**: A key text for understanding pre-exilic prophetic eschatology and its development

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Day of the LORD | A comprehensive, cosmic day of divine reckoning |
| Judgment | God's punishment of idolatry, injustice, and complacency |
| Remnant | A humble, faithful community that survives judgment |
| Restoration | God's promise to gather and renew the faithful |
| Humility | The call to seek righteousness and meekness |
