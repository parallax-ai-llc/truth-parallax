## Overview

Zephaniah is not explicitly named in the Quran, and the book holds no canonical status in Islam. However, Islamic scholarship engages with its themes through the lens of *Isra'iliyyat* — traditions drawn from Jewish and Christian sources that entered Islamic commentary. The prophet's central message of an imminent Day of Judgment resonates profoundly with Quranic descriptions of Yawm al-Qiyamah (the Day of Resurrection) and Yawm al-Din (the Day of Judgment).

## Quranic Parallels

Zephaniah's vivid depiction of the Day of the LORD finds extensive parallels in the Quran. The cosmic upheaval described in Zephaniah 1:14-18 — darkness, trumpet blasts, fire consuming the earth — closely mirrors Quranic passages such as Surah Al-Qari'ah (101), Al-Infitar (82), and At-Takwir (81). The call to repentance before the day of reckoning (2:1-3) echoes the Quranic refrain urging humanity to turn to God before it is too late.

## Theological Emphases

- **Divine sovereignty over judgment**: God alone determines the day and manner of reckoning, consistent with Islamic theology
- **Prophetic warning**: Zephaniah's role as a warner (*nadhir*) parallels the Quranic description of all prophets as warners sent to their peoples
- **Rejection of idolatry**: The condemnation of Baal worship and syncretism (1:4-6) aligns with the Quranic emphasis on *tawhid*
- **Humility before God**: The call to seek humility (2:3) resonates with Islamic virtues of *tawadu'* and submission
