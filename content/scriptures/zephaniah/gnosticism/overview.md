## Overview

Gnostic engagement with Zephaniah, while limited compared to Genesis or the Gospel of John, is not entirely absent. The *Apocalypse of Zephaniah*, a pseudepigraphical text surviving in fragmentary Coptic and Greek, appropriates the prophet's name for a visionary journey through heavenly realms — a characteristically Gnostic reframing. The canonical book's themes of cosmic destruction, hidden remnants, and radical divine intervention lend themselves to Gnostic reinterpretation.

## The Apocalypse of Zephaniah

This text, distinct from the canonical book, describes the prophet's ascent through multiple heavens, encounters with angels and demons, and visions of the judgment of souls. While not strictly Gnostic in all manuscripts, its themes of heavenly ascent, esoteric knowledge, and the soul's journey through hostile cosmic powers resonate with Gnostic cosmology. The text survives in Sahidic Coptic and Akhmimic fragments.

## Gnostic Reinterpretation of Key Themes

- **Cosmic destruction**: The Day of the LORD's annihilation of creation (1:2-3) aligns with the Gnostic hope for the dissolution of the Demiurge's material cosmos
- **The hidden remnant**: The "hidden" ones who survive judgment parallel the pneumatic elect who possess secret knowledge
- **Wrath of the creator**: The Demiurge's rage against those who defy him, rather than the true God's righteous judgment
- **Joyful restoration**: The promise of gathering (3:19-20) reread as the return of divine sparks to the Pleroma
