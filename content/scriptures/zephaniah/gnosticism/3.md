Zephaniah 3 presents particular interpretive opportunities for Gnostic readers. The indictment of Jerusalem's leaders — princes, judges, prophets, and priests (3:3-4) — is read as a condemnation of the Demiurge's earthly representatives, the institutional authorities who maintain ignorance and enforce submission to the cosmic order. The Gnostic suspicion of institutional religion finds ready ammunition in the prophet's wholesale rejection of the religious establishment.

The transformative vision of 3:9, where God promises "pure speech" for all peoples, is reinterpreted as the revelation of the true, secret language of *gnosis* — the knowledge that liberates the divine spark from material entrapment. Valentinian Gnostics, in particular, emphasized the role of sacred words, names, and formulas in the process of spiritual liberation. The unity of speech is not merely linguistic but ontological: it represents the moment when all pneumatic beings recognize their common origin in the Pleroma.

The joyful conclusion (3:14-20), where God promises to be "in your midst" and to "rejoice over you with singing," is perhaps the most challenging passage for consistent Gnostic interpretation, as it affirms the positive presence of the divine within the material world. Some Gnostic readers resolve this by distinguishing between the Demiurge of judgment (chapters 1-2) and the true God whose voice breaks through in the conclusion — a moment of Pleromatic light piercing the darkness of the material cosmos, calling the scattered sparks home.
