## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox tradition reads Zephaniah through the lens of patristic exegesis, liturgical theology, and the eschatological hope of cosmic transfiguration. The Day of the LORD is understood not merely as punitive destruction but as the purifying fire of divine love that burns away corruption and prepares creation for renewal. The Fathers saw in Zephaniah's closing vision a prefiguration of the Kingdom of God.

## Patristic Interpretation

The Church Fathers approached Zephaniah with attention to both its historical context and its spiritual meaning. Theodoret of Cyrrhus commented extensively on the Minor Prophets, reading Zephaniah's judgment oracles as directed against spiritual idolatry and complacency. Cyril of Alexandria saw the remnant theology as pointing toward the faithful Church that endures through persecution and trial.

## Theological Emphases

- **Theophany**: The Day of the LORD as a terrifying yet transformative manifestation of God's presence
- **Purification through fire**: Divine wrath understood as the experience of God's love by those who resist it, consistent with the Orthodox understanding of hell
- **The humble remnant (anawim)**: A model for Orthodox monasticism and the spiritual life of humility and dependence on God
- **Eschatological joy**: Zephaniah 3:14-17 as an anticipation of the Paschal joy, God rejoicing over His people with singing
