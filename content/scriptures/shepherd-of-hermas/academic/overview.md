## Overview

The Academic study of the Shepherd of Hermas examines the text's composition in stages (possibly spanning 90-150 CE), its near-canonical status in the early Church, and its significance for understanding Roman Christianity's social structure and theological development. Scholars analyze the text's contribution to the emergence of penitential theology, its evidence for early Christian prophecy and ecclesial authority, and its literary relationship to Jewish apocalypticism.
