## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Shepherd of Hermas situates its ethical teaching within the broader context of ancient moral formation. The Two Spirits doctrine parallels the Qumran Community Rule, Zoroastrian dualism, and Stoic psychology. The Tower allegory resonates with architectural metaphors for spiritual community found in Buddhist, Hindu, and Islamic traditions. The text's approach to wealth and poverty invites comparison with Confucian, Buddhist, and philosophical ethics.
