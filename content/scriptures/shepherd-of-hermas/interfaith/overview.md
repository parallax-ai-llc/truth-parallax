## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Shepherd of Hermas draws on its universal themes of moral formation, the struggle between good and evil inclinations, and the possibility of spiritual renewal after failure. The text's ethical teaching — on truthfulness, self-control, generosity, and cheerfulness — speaks across religious boundaries. The Shepherd's pastoral approach to human weakness offers a model of compassionate moral guidance shared by many traditions.
