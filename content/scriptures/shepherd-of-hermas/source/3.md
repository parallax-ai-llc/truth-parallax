---
title: "Shepherd of Hermas - Chapter 3"
scripture: "shepherd-of-hermas"
chapter: 3
---

**1** After she had finished these words, she said to me: "Do you wish to hear me read?" I said: "Yes, lady." She said to me: "Listen then, and hear the glories of God."

**2** I heard great and wonderful things which I cannot remember; for all the words were frightful, such as a man cannot bear. The last words, however, I remembered, for they were suitable for us and gentle:

**3** "Behold, the God of hosts, who by His invisible and mighty power and by His great wisdom created the world, and by His glorious purpose clothed His creation with beauty, and by His strong word fixed the heavens, and founded the earth upon the waters, and by His own wisdom and providence formed His holy Church, which also He blessed —

**4** "Behold, He removes the heavens and the mountains and the hills and the seas, and all things become level for His elect, that He may fulfil to them the promise which He made with great glory and rejoicing, if they shall keep the statutes of God which they received with great faith."

**5** When she had finished reading and risen from the chair, there came four young men and took away the chair and went away towards the east.

**6** She called me and touched my breast and said to me: "Did my reading please you?" I said to her: "Lady, these last words please me, but the first were hard and difficult."

**7** She said to me: "These last words are for the righteous, but the former are for the heathen and the apostates."

**8** While she was speaking with me, two men appeared and took her by the arms and went away towards the east, where the chair had gone.

**9** She went away cheerfully, and as she went said to me: "Be of good courage, Hermas."

**10** After she had gone, I saw a vision, and in this vision a great tower was being built upon the waters with bright square stones.
