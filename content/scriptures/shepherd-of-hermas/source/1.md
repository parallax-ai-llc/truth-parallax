---
title: "Shepherd of Hermas - Chapter 1"
scripture: "shepherd-of-hermas"
chapter: 1
---

**1** He who had brought me up sold me to a certain Rhoda at Rome. After many years I met her again and began to love her as a sister.

**2** After a certain time I saw her bathing in the river Tiber, and I gave her my hand and led her out of the river.

**3** Seeing her beauty, I thought in my heart: "I should be happy if I had a wife of such beauty and character." This was my only thought, and no other.

**4** After a certain time, while I was journeying to Cumae and was glorifying the creatures of God, how great and beautiful and mighty they are, I fell asleep as I walked.

**5** And a spirit took me and carried me through a trackless region through which no man could journey, for it was a precipitous place and broken into clefts by the waters. When I had crossed that river, I came to level ground and knelt down and began to pray to the Lord and to confess my sins.

**6** While I was praying, the heaven opened and I saw that woman whom I had desired, greeting me from heaven and saying: "Hail, Hermas!"

**7** And looking at her, I said to her: "Lady, what are you doing here?" She answered me: "I was taken up to accuse you of your sins before the Lord."

**8** I said to her: "Are you now my accuser?" "No," she said, "but hear the words which I am going to say to you. God, who dwells in the heavens and created that which is out of that which is not, and increased and multiplied it for the sake of his holy Church, is angry with you because you sinned against me."

**9** I answered and said to her: "Did I sin against you? In what place? Or when did I speak an evil word to you? Did I not always regard you as a goddess? Did I not always respect you as a sister? Why do you charge me falsely, lady, with these wicked and unclean things?"

**10** She laughed and said to me: "The desire for wickedness came up in your heart. Do you not think that for a righteous man it is a wicked deed if the evil desire rises in his heart? It is indeed a sin, and a great one," she said.
