---
title: "Shepherd of Hermas - Chapter 5"
scripture: "shepherd-of-hermas"
chapter: 5
---

**1** While I was praying at home and sitting on my bed, there entered a man of glorious appearance, dressed like a shepherd, with a white skin wrapped about him, a wallet on his shoulders, and a staff in his hand.

**2** He greeted me, and I greeted him back. Immediately he sat down beside me and said to me: "I have been sent by the most reverend angel to dwell with you for the rest of the days of your life."

**3** I thought he had come to tempt me, and I said to him: "Who are you? For I know the one to whom I was entrusted." He said to me: "Do you not recognize me?" "No," I said.

**4** "I am the shepherd to whom you were entrusted." While he was still speaking, his appearance changed, and I recognized him, and I was terrified, and fear seized me, and I was altogether overwhelmed with sorrow that I had answered him so wickedly and foolishly.

**5** He answered and said to me: "Do not be troubled, but be strengthened in my commandments which I am about to give you. For I was sent," he said, "to show you again all the things which you saw before, for these are the main points which are helpful to you."

**6** "First of all, write my commandments and the parables. But the rest you shall write as I shall show you. This is the reason," he said, "that I command you to write first the commandments and parables, so that you may read them at once and be able to keep them."

**7** So I wrote the commandments and parables as he commanded me.

**8** If then you hear them and keep them, and walk in them, and practise them with a pure heart, you shall receive from the Lord whatever He has promised you.

**9** But if, when you have heard them, you do not repent, but continue to add to your sins, you shall receive the contrary from the Lord.

**10** All these things the shepherd, the angel of repentance, commanded me to write.
