## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Thessalonians highlights the letter's explicit commendation of traditions received from the apostles (2:15) as a key proof text for the authority of sacred tradition alongside Scripture. The letter's eschatological teaching is read within the broader framework of Catholic apocalyptic theology.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Sacred Tradition
The command to "hold to the traditions which you were taught, whether by word of mouth or by letter" (2:15) is one of the most important Catholic proof texts for the authority of apostolic tradition. Catholic exegesis insists that not all apostolic teaching was committed to writing.

### The Restrainer
Many Catholic interpreters, following patristic tradition, identify the restrainer (2:6-7) with the Roman Empire or, more broadly, with legitimate civil and ecclesiastical authority that holds back the forces of chaos and lawlessness.

### Work and Communal Responsibility
The injunctions against idleness (3:6-12) are read within Catholic social teaching's broader framework of the dignity of labor and communal obligation.
