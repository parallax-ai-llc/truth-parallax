## The Man of Lawlessness

The "man of lawlessness" who "takes his seat in the temple of God, proclaiming himself to be God" (2:3-4) is, for Gnostic readers, an unmistakable portrait of the Demiurge himself, or Yaldabaoth, the ignorant creator who declared "I am God, and there is no other beside me." This is the central Gnostic charge: the god worshipped in the temple is not the transcendent Father but an inferior, deluded being who mistakes his limited creation for the totality of existence. His "lawlessness" consists not in breaking divine law but in establishing a counterfeit law that traps souls in material bondage while claiming to offer salvation.

The mysterious "restrainer" (2:6-7) who holds back the full revelation of this lawless one is interpreted as the veil of ignorance that prevents most humans from perceiving the Demiurge's true nature. As long as the restrainer is in place, the cosmic deception continues unopposed. When the restrainer is removed, the Demiurge stands exposed for what he is, and "the Lord Jesus will kill him with the breath of his mouth" (2:8), that is, the Savior's revelation of gnosis will dissolve the Demiurge's authority like shadows before the dawn. The "breath of his mouth" is the living word of truth that unmasks the pretender god.

Paul's warning about the "strong delusion" sent so that people "believe what is false" (2:11) describes the archontic counterfeit of religion, the elaborate system of sacrifices, laws, and cosmic fear through which the Demiurge maintains control over the psychic and hylic classes. The call to "stand firm and hold to the traditions" (2:15) refers not to institutional orthodoxy but to the secret apostolic teaching, the gnosis transmitted orally from the Savior through Paul to the pneumatic elect, which alone can withstand the Demiurge's program of deception.
