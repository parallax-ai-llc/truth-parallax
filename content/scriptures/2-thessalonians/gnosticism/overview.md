## Overview

Gnostic readers found rich material in 2 Thessalonians' apocalyptic imagery. The figure of the "man of lawlessness" who sits in God's temple claiming to be God (2:3-4) was identified by some Gnostic teachers with the demiurge — the ignorant creator god who falsely claims supremacy.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Man of Lawlessness as Demiurge
Some Gnostic interpreters identified the "man of lawlessness" who "takes his seat in the temple of God, proclaiming himself to be God" (2:4) with the demiurge of Gnostic cosmology — the inferior creator deity (often identified with the God of the Hebrew Bible) who usurps the place of the true, transcendent God.

### The Mystery of Lawlessness
The "mystery of lawlessness" already at work (2:7) was understood as the pervasive deception of the material world, which keeps souls trapped in ignorance. Only the revelation of true *gnosis* can overcome this cosmic deception.

### The Restrainer as Cosmic Principle
Gnostic interpreters offered esoteric readings of the restrainer (2:6-7): perhaps a cosmic principle, an aeon, or a spiritual power that temporarily prevents the full manifestation of the demiurge's deception, until the appointed time of revelation.
