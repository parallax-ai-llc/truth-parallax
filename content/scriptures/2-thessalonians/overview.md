## Overview

2 Thessalonians is a significant epistle in the New Testament canon, addressing themes of man of lawlessness, perseverance, second coming that have generated extensive theological reflection across Christian traditions and beyond. The letter's teaching has shaped doctrine, ethics, and worship across two millennia of interpretation.

## Structure

The letter comprises 3 chapters:

1. **Chapter 1**: Thanksgiving and Judgment (12 verses)
2. **Chapter 2**: The Man of Lawlessness (17 verses)
3. **Chapter 3**: Warning Against Idleness (18 verses)

## Cross-Traditional Significance

2 Thessalonians generates rich cross-traditional engagement:

- **Protestantism**: Emphasizes man of lawlessness and perseverance as central theological themes
- **Catholicism**: Reads the letter through the lens of sacred tradition and sacramental theology
- **Eastern Orthodoxy**: Interprets the text within patristic commentary and liturgical practice
- **Judaism**: Examines the letter's Jewish roots and implications for Jewish-Christian relations
- **Islam**: Engages with ethical parallels while critically assessing Christological claims
- **Gnosticism**: Reads esoteric and spiritual meanings beneath the surface of the text
