## Thanksgiving and Judgment

Eastern Orthodoxy interpretation of 2 Thessalonians 1 centers on patristic commentary and liturgical theology. The key passages — worthy of the kingdom (1:5), relief for the afflicted (1:7), glorified in his saints (1:10) — have been read through the lens of theosis, the liturgical life of the Church, and patristic consensus, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

John chrysostom, theodoret of cyrus, and other eastern fathers gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 2 Thessalonians. The chapter's teaching on thanksgiving and judgment has shaped Eastern Orthodoxy understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern eastern orthodoxy scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 2 Thessalonians 1 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
