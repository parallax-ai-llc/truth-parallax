## Overview

Eastern Orthodox exegesis of 2 Thessalonians draws heavily on patristic commentary, particularly the interpretations of John Chrysostom and Theodoret of Cyrus. The letter's commendation of tradition (2:15) and its apocalyptic imagery are read within the living tradition of the Church.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Holy Tradition
The Orthodox Church reads 2:15 as a foundational text for the authority of sacred Tradition. The "traditions" delivered by Paul encompass not only written texts but liturgical practices, doctrinal formulations, and the entire life of the Church as guided by the Holy Spirit.

### The Restrainer and Cosmic Struggle
Orthodox fathers offered varied identifications of the restrainer (2:6-7): the Roman Empire, the grace of the Holy Spirit, or the preaching of the gospel. The Orthodox reading situates this within a broader cosmic struggle between the kingdom of God and the forces of antichrist.

### Communal Discipline
The instructions about idleness (3:6-15) are understood within the Orthodox monastic tradition, where the rule of work and prayer (*ora et labora*) reflects Paul's insistence that all must contribute to the common life.
