## Overview

2 Thessalonians is a critical text for Protestant eschatology, particularly regarding the identity of the "man of lawlessness" and the nature of the restrainer. The Reformers frequently identified the papal office with the man of lawlessness, a reading that shaped Protestant-Catholic polemics for centuries. Modern evangelical interpretation tends toward a future individual antichrist figure.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Man of Lawlessness
Protestant exegetes debate whether the figure described in 2:3-4 is a future individual, a recurring type, or a historical institution. Dispensationalists typically expect a future personal antichrist; Reformed interpreters often emphasize the principle of lawlessness already at work.

### The Protestant Work Ethic
Paul's command that "if anyone is not willing to work, let him not eat" (3:10) has profoundly influenced Protestant ethics and social teaching, from the Puritans to modern welfare debates.

### Standing Firm in Apostolic Teaching
The injunction to "hold to the traditions" (2:15) is carefully handled by Protestants, who distinguish apostolic teaching preserved in Scripture from later ecclesiastical traditions.
