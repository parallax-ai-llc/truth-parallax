## Unity of Being and the Light of Muhammad

Book Six's exploration of *wahdat al-wujud* (the unity of being) connects, for Shia readers, to the concept of the *Nur Muhammadi* (the Light of Muhammad) — the pre-existent divine light from which the Prophet and the Ahl al-Bayt were created, and which constitutes the spiritual essence of the cosmos. If all being is ultimately one — as the Masnavi's final book teaches — then the Light of Muhammad is the medium through which this unity manifests and through which the believer can return to the source.

The Masnavi's incomplete ending resonates with the Shia experience of waiting for the Hidden Imam's return. Just as the Masnavi gestures toward a completion it does not reach, the Shia community lives in a state of anticipation — knowing the direction of the journey but not yet having arrived at its destination. The incomplete text becomes a symbol of the incomplete *ummah* — a community awaiting the return of its guide, its physician, its beloved imam.

For Shia readers, the Masnavi's final teaching — that lover and beloved are one, that mirror and beauty are one — points toward the eschatological reunion that defines Shia hope: the return of the Hidden Imam, the establishment of justice, and the ultimate unification of the community with its rightful leader and, through him, with the divine Beloved whom Rumi's entire poem celebrates.
