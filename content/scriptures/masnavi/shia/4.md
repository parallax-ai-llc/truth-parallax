## Purification and the Ahl al-Bayt

Book Four's theme of the purification of desire connects to the Shia veneration of the Ahl al-Bayt (the Prophet's household) as models of spiritual purity. The Quran's "Verse of Purification" (33:33) — "God only desires to remove impurity from you, O Ahl al-Bayt, and to purify you completely" — is central to Shia theology, and the theme of purification that runs through Book Four resonates with this Quranic promise.

The Sufi's struggle with jealousy and attachment in Rumi's story mirrors the broader human struggle with the lower self (*nafs*) — a struggle that the Ahl al-Bayt exemplified and overcame. Ali's legendary detachment from worldly goods, Fatima's patience in suffering, Husayn's willingness to sacrifice everything for truth — these models of purified desire provide the Shia reader with concrete examples of the transformation Rumi describes.

For Shia readers, Book Four's teaching is thus not merely theoretical but hagiographic: the purification of desire has been achieved by the Ahl al-Bayt, and their example provides both inspiration and guidance for those who follow in their path.
