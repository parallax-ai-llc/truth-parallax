## Book Three: The Unveiling of Mysteries

**1.** In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. O Light of Truth, the Masnavi has grown with thy nurture, like a sapling in the garden of the spirit.

**2.** The lamps are different, but the Light is the same: it comes from Beyond.

**3.** If thou keep looking at the lamp, thou art lost, for thence arises the appearance of number and plurality.

**4.** Fix thy gaze upon the Light, and thou art delivered from the dualism inherent in the finite body.

**5.** O Thou who art the kernel of existence, the disagreement between Muslim, Zoroastrian, and Jew depends on the standpoint.

**6.** Some Hindus brought an elephant for exhibition and placed it in a dark house.

**7.** A number of people went into the dark in order to see it. Since seeing it with the eye was impossible, each one felt it in the dark with the palm of his hand.

**8.** The hand of one fell on its trunk: he said, "This creature is like a water-pipe."

**9.** The hand of another touched its ear: to him it appeared to be like a fan.

**10.** Another handled its leg and said, "I found the elephant's shape to be like a pillar."

**11.** Another laid his hand on its back and said, "Truly, this elephant is like a throne."

**12.** Similarly, whenever any one heard a description of the elephant, he understood it only in respect of the part that he had touched.

**13.** If there had been a candle in each one's hand, the difference would have gone out of their words.

**14.** The eye of sense-perception is only like the palm of the hand: the palm has not power to reach the whole of the elephant.

**15.** The eye of the Sea is one thing and the foam another: leave the foam and look with the eye of the Sea.

**16.** Day and night there is the movement of foam-flecks from the Sea: thou beholdest the foam but not the Sea. How strange!

**17.** We are dashing against each other like boats: our eyes are darkened although we are in the clear water.

**18.** O thou that hast gone to sleep in the body's boat, thou hast seen the water, but look at the Water of the water.

**19.** The water has a Water that is driving it; the spirit has a Spirit that is calling it.

**20.** Every one's death is of the same quality as himself, my lad: to the enemy of God an enemy, to the friend of God a friend.

**21.** Your fear of death in fleeing from it is really your fear of yourself. Take heed, O dear soul!

**22.** It is your own ugly face, not the face of Death: your spirit is like the tree, and death is like the leaf.

**23.** From you it has grown, whether it be good or evil: every hidden thing of yours is made manifest at the Resurrection.
