## Book Five: The Light Upon Light

**1.** In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. This is the fifth of the Masnavi books, and in it is the light that shows the way from darkness.

**2.** O Day, arise! The atoms are dancing. The souls are dancing, overcome with ecstasy.

**3.** I will whisper in your ear where it is that the dance leads. All the atoms in the air and in the desert are dancing—

**4.** Each atom, happy or miserable, is entranced by the Sun of the unseen world.

**5.** If the walls and doors of this house could speak, they would cry out: "There is no God but God!"

**6.** We are like a lute: no more, no less. If the sound-box is stuffed full of anything, no music can come forth.

**7.** If the brain and the belly are burning clean with fasting, every moment a new song comes out of the fire.

**8.** The tones of the lute come from beyond—do not cover your ear.

**9.** Whatever pearl you seek, look for the pearl within the pearl.

**10.** A man who does not weep at the beauty of existence is only a donkey carrying a load of books through the rain.

**11.** Love came and became like blood in my body. It rushed through my veins and encircled my heart.

**12.** Everywhere I look I see the Friend's face. He has taken over every atom of my being.

**13.** The name of that Friend has replaced my own: whatever name I call, it is His name I pronounce.

**14.** Patience is the key to joy. The seeker who has patience finds at last. The door is heavy, but it opens.

**15.** A chickpea in the pot cries out: "Why do you set the fire beneath me? Why do you boil me?"

**16.** The cook strikes the chickpea with a ladle and says: "Do not leap away from the fire. I do not boil you because I hate you, but so that you may acquire taste and savor."

**17.** "You were drinking water in the garden—that drinking was for the sake of this fire."

**18.** "God's mercy precedes His wrath: He buys you in order that He may give you a kingdom."

**19.** So it is with affliction in this world: it is the cook's ladle that prepares you for the table of the King.

**20.** Suffering is a treasure, for it conceals mercies. The kernel of a walnut is hidden beneath a hard shell.

**21.** When you go through a hardship, raise your hands and say: "This is the polishing of my mirror."

**22.** Where there is ruin, there is hope for treasure. Why do you not seek the treasure in the ruin?

**23.** In the destruction of all that you are lies the construction of all that you shall be.
