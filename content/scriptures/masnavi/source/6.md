## Book Six: The Return to the Source

**1.** In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. This is the sixth book of the spiritual Masnavi, the final lamp that illumines the way of truth.

**2.** O thou who hast gone to sleep, arise! The caravan has moved on and you are still dreaming.

**3.** The night has passed, and with it the darkness. Arise, for the dawn is breaking over the mountain.

**4.** How long will you worship the things of this world? How long will you build castles on the sand?

**5.** When the flood comes, all your castles will be swept away. Build upon the rock of the Spirit, not upon the sand of the senses.

**6.** A man planted a tree and watered it every day. A voice said: "The fruit of this tree will be bitter."

**7.** The man replied: "I plant it not for the fruit but for the shade." The voice said: "Even the shade will pass."

**8.** "Then I plant it for the planting itself," said the man. And the voice was silent, for this was the right answer.

**9.** Do everything for its own sake, and the fruit will come of itself.

**10.** This world is a mountain: whatever you say, good or evil, comes back to you as an echo.

**11.** If you speak well of others, well comes back. If you speak ill, ill returns. The mountain does not change—only the echo changes.

**12.** Come, return from the existence of thy name to that nameless existence, from which thou camest.

**13.** The soul is from an invisible world. What is born of the wind carries with it the marks of the wind.

**14.** A river that flows into the sea is no longer a river—it is the sea. So the soul, returning to its origin, is no longer separate.

**15.** I was a hidden treasure and I loved to be known: therefore I created the world that I might be known.

**16.** The whole of creation is a mirror. In every atom there is a mirror that reflects the face of the Beloved.

**17.** Come, come, whoever you are—wanderer, worshiper, lover of leaving—it does not matter.

**18.** Ours is not a caravan of despair. Come, even if you have broken your vows a thousand times.

**19.** Come, yet again, come, come.

**20.** When I am dead, seek not my tomb in the earth, but find it in the hearts of those who love.

**21.** Silence is the language of God; all else is poor translation.

**22.** Beyond the ideas of right-doing and wrong-doing there is a field. I will meet you there.

**23.** When the soul lies down in that grass, the world is too full to talk about. Ideas, language, even the phrase "each other" makes no sense.

**24.** This is the end of the Masnavi, though the Masnavi has no end. If the oceans were ink, they could not write its complete meaning.
