## Book Four: The Sea of the Soul

**1.** In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. The Light of God which is the guide of all that are on earth and in heaven—

**2.** O Life of the heart, O Husamu'ddin, the desire to write the Fourth Book has come upon me.

**3.** The spiritual man eats little, for he fills himself with Light. He who fills his belly full cannot find the Way.

**4.** In every head is a soul and a secret—do not judge by the turban what lies beneath.

**5.** The body is like the lantern, the soul is the candle: and spiritual wisdom is the light that shines from the candle.

**6.** If the lantern is gone, the light-giving candle is still there. The pearl-diver knows the depths though the surface is troubled.

**7.** A man went to a barber to have his beard dyed. The barber said: "Your beard has become entirely white."

**8.** The man replied: "Then dye half of it." The barber said: "It would be better to wait and dye the other half to match."

**9.** So it is with us: half our life is gone in vanity, and we think to mend the remainder with more vanity.

**10.** O brother, you are nothing but your thoughts. The rest of you is only sinew and bone.

**11.** If your thought is a rose, you are a rose-garden. If it is a thorn, you are fuel for the fire.

**12.** If you are a friend of God, water will be your servant. Have you not heard how the sea obeyed Moses?

**13.** And did not water become the foe of the people of Pharaoh? Did it not swallow their host in its depths?

**14.** The same water serves you or drowns you—it depends on what you carry in your heart.

**15.** A piece of wood and a piece of iron fell into the river: the wood floated and the iron sank.

**16.** The wood said to the iron: "Why do you sink?" The iron replied: "It is my nature that pulls me down, as it is your nature that keeps you afloat."

**17.** If you would float upon the sea of the spirit, make yourself light—cast off the iron of attachment.

**18.** When you are free of self, you float. When you are full of self, you sink.

**19.** This is the test of every man: set him upon the water. He who floats has found the Way; he who sinks has found himself.

**20.** The sea is generous: it accepts all rivers. Be like the sea—open and vast.

**21.** The human being is a guest-house. Every morning a new arrival: a joy, a depression, a meanness—some momentary awareness comes as an unexpected visitor.

**22.** Welcome and attend to them all! Even if they are a crowd of sorrows who violently sweep your house empty of its furniture—

**23.** Still, treat each guest honorably. He may be clearing you out for some new delight.
