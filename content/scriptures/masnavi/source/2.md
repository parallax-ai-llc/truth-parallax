## Book Two: The Light Within

**1.** In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. This is the second book of the Masnavi and the explanation of how the light of the spirit prevails over the darkness of the body.

**2.** O friend, you are the guest of the one who possesses all things—why do you stretch your hand toward every plate?

**3.** Why do you seek water when you are the water? Why do you seek bread when you are the wheat?

**4.** A certain man knocked at the door of a friend. The friend said: "Who is there?" He answered: "It is I."

**5.** The friend said: "Go away. There is no place here for the raw. Naught but the fire of absence and separation will cook the raw one."

**6.** The wretched man went away and traveled for a year, burning with grief and longing in separation from his friend.

**7.** He was cooked and burned and then returned, and again walked up and down near the house of his friend.

**8.** He knocked at the door with a hundred fears and courtesies, lest any disrespectful word should come from his lips.

**9.** His friend called: "Who is at the door?" He answered: "It is thou who art at the door, O charmer of hearts!"

**10.** The friend said: "Since thou art I, come in, O myself! There is no room in the house for two I's."

**11.** The thread is not for the needle's double: since thou art single, come into this needle.

**12.** It is the thread that is connected with the needle: the eye of the needle is not suitable for the camel.

**13.** How shall the camel's being become slender, save by the shears of ascetic exercises and works?

**14.** For that, O reader, the hand of God is needed, for it is the Be and it was of every impossibility.

**15.** By His hand every impossible thing is made possible; by His fear every unruly one is made quiet.

**16.** He who is the Lord of all cunning can at every moment produce a hundred worlds without materials.

**17.** Know that the world of created beings is like pure and limpid water in which the attributes of the Almighty are reflected.

**18.** Their knowledge and their justice and their mercy are like the stars of heaven reflected in running water.

**19.** Kings are the theatre for the manifestation of God's kingship; the learned are the mirrors for God's wisdom.

**20.** Generations have passed, and this is a new generation. The moon is the same moon, the water is different water.

**21.** The justice is the same justice, the learning is the same learning; but those generations and peoples have been changed.

**22.** Generations on generations have gone, O friend, but these meanings are eternal and everlasting.

**23.** The water in the stream has changed many times: the reflection of the moon and of the stars remains the same.
