Daoist reading of Mozi section 15, "Against Fatalism," recognizes both the sincerity of Mozi's project and its fundamental limitation. While Mozi rightly challenges the artificiality of Confucian ritual and the waste of aristocratic excess, his alternative program of utilitarian reform substitutes one form of purposive striving for another. The Daoist sage would ask: why replace the burden of ritual with the burden of calculation?

The Zhuangzi's critique applies to this section as to the whole: Mozi's vision of a well-ordered society achieved through relentless effort and self-denial misses the effortless harmony that arises when human beings align with the natural Way. True benefit comes not from calculated programs of social improvement but from the spontaneous generosity that flows from a mind at peace with itself and the world.
