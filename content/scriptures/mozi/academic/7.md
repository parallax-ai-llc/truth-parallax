Academic analysis of Mozi section 7, "On Economy of Expenditure," examines the text's argumentative structure, rhetorical strategies, and philosophical implications within the context of Warring States intellectual culture. Scholars note that this section belongs to one of the "triads" — groups of three parallel chapters that present similar arguments in varying forms — providing evidence for the evolution of Mohist thought across generations of the school.

The arguments presented here have been analyzed in terms of their logical validity, historical context, and philosophical significance. Comparative ethicists have noted parallels with utilitarian traditions in Western philosophy, while historians of Chinese thought have situated the section within the broader context of inter-school debate during the Hundred Schools period. Questions of textual authenticity and compositional layers continue to be actively debated.
