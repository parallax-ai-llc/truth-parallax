## Overview

Academic study of the Mozi has experienced a renaissance since the late twentieth century, driven by recognition that Mohism represents one of the earliest systematic philosophical programs in world history. Scholars have analyzed its contributions to ethical theory, logic, epistemology, philosophy of science, and political thought, finding in the Mozi a remarkably modern sensibility.

## Interpretive Approach

Scholars employ textual criticism to reconstruct the compositional history of the Mozi, which contains material from different periods and likely multiple authors within the Mohist school. The existence of three parallel versions of several core chapters (the "triads") provides unique evidence for understanding how philosophical texts evolved through oral transmission and scribal redaction. Philosophical analysis focuses on the logical structure of Mohist arguments, the coherence of their ethical consequentialism, and their contributions to the theory of knowledge.

## Key Contributions

The Mozi's contributions to formal logic — particularly the "Mohist Canons" (not included in the fifteen core sections) — represent the most sophisticated logical thinking in ancient China. Scholars have also highlighted the Mohist school's remarkable social organization as a disciplined community with elected leaders, shared resources, and a commitment to mutual defense — making it one of the earliest documented examples of a philosophical community organized around principles of social justice.
