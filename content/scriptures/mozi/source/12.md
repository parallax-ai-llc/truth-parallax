---
title: "Mozi - Chapter 12"
scripture: "mozi"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Master Mo said: The gentlemen of the world all wish to follow the way of righteousness, and they desire to do what is right concerning explaining the spirits and their rewards.

**2** Yet the gentlemen of the world differ in what they consider right, and this brings confusion to the world. How do we know this?

**3** When a father in one family says this is right and a father in another family says that is right, each criticizes the other. This is the situation among families.

**4** When a ruler of one state says this is right and the ruler of another state says that is right, each attacks the other. This is the situation among states.

**5** Now if each person in the world held his own standard of right and wrong, there would be ten thousand standards for ten thousand people. The world would fall into chaos.

**6** Therefore Master Mo said: The way of the ancient sage-kings was to establish unified standards of righteousness, beginning with the Son of Heaven and reaching down to the common people.

**7** When the standards are unified and the people follow what is right, the state is well governed. When the state is well governed, the world is at peace.

**8** The worthy are exalted to high positions, and the unworthy are kept from power. This is the foundation of good government.

**9** Master Mo said: In the matter of explaining the spirits and their rewards, the gentleman examines the origin of the practice, its application in government, and its benefit to the common people.

**10** If a practice does not originate from the deeds of the sage-kings, if it cannot be verified in the experience of the common people, and if it does not benefit the state and the people, then it should be abandoned.

**11** The three tests are: examining its origin, verifying its application, and measuring its benefit. A practice that fails all three tests is not righteous.

**12** Therefore the righteous man governs by promoting what benefits the world and eliminating what harms it. This is the principle of universal love and mutual benefit.
