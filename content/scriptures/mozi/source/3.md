---
title: "Mozi - Chapter 3"
scripture: "mozi"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Master Mo said: To accomplish anything whatsoever, one must have standards. None have yet accomplished anything without them.

**2** The officers serving as generals and councillors all have standards. The artisans making squares and circles all have standards.

**3** The artisan uses his square to make a square and his compass to make a circle. With these four standards, the artisan can complete his work.

**4** Now the rulers govern their states without standards. In this they fall below the artisan.

**5** What then shall be taken as the standard in government? Master Mo said: Nothing is better to take as a standard than Heaven.

**6** Heaven's actions are all-inclusive and not partial. Its blessings are substantial and ungrudging. Its revelations are lasting and unfading.

**7** It was by following the model of Heaven that the Sage-Kings Yao, Shun, Yu, Tang, Wen, and Wu governed the world.

**8** When the Sage-Kings took Heaven as their model, their actions were all-inclusive and not partial. Their blessings were substantial and ungrudging. Their revelations were lasting and unfading.

**9** And so they were able to govern the world and satisfy the people.

**10** Now, the will of Heaven abominates the large state which attacks small states, the large house which disturbs small houses, the strong who plunder the weak, the clever who deceive the stupid, the honoured who disdain the humble. This is what the will of Heaven abominates.

**11** On the other hand, Heaven desires people to help and benefit one another, that those who know the way shall teach those who do not, and that people shall share property with one another.

**12** This is what the will of Heaven desires.

**13** What Heaven desires is right, and what Heaven does not desire is not right.
