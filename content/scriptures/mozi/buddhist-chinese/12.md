Chinese Buddhist interpretation of Mozi section 12, "The Will of Heaven III," draws parallels between Mohist and Buddhist approaches to universal concern while noting fundamental differences in philosophical foundation. Mozi's arguments here for extending care beyond partial attachments resonate with the bodhisattva ideal of working for the welfare of all sentient beings without discrimination.

However, Buddhist readers observe that Mozi's framework remains grounded in utilitarian calculation of worldly benefit, whereas Buddhist compassion (karuna) arises from insight into interdependence and aims at the cessation of suffering at its root. This section thus illuminates how two traditions can arrive at similar ethical conclusions — impartial concern for all — from very different metaphysical premises and with very different ultimate aims.
