Comparative analysis reveals a spectrum ranging from purely theoretical engagement to full immersion in political action, with Confucius occupying a distinctive position as a thinker who sought practical implementation of his moral philosophy through institutional reform.

Comparative analysis identifies common patterns in the dynamics of reform and resistance, including the tendency of entrenched elites to co-opt or destroy reform movements that threaten their position.
