---
title: "Spring and Autumn Annals - Chapter 4"
scripture: "spring-autumn-annals"
chapter: 4
---

**1** In the first year of Duke Min, in the spring, the Duke came to the throne and received the mandate.

**2** In the second month, envoys were sent to the neighbouring states to announce the accession.

**3** In the summer, there were disturbances at the borders. The Duke sent forces to restore order.

**4** In the autumn, the harvest was gathered. In years of plenty, the wise ruler stores grain for the years of want.

**5** In the winter, the Duke held court and reviewed the administration of the state.

**6** The Annals record the events of this reign to distinguish the righteous from the unrighteous, the faithful from the faithless.

**7** When the ruler follows the Way, the ministers are loyal and the people are content. When the ruler abandons the Way, treachery and rebellion arise.

**8** The rites and ceremonies of the ancients were not empty forms but expressions of the moral order that governs heaven and earth.

**9** He who observes the rites with sincerity maintains harmony in the state. He who neglects the rites brings disorder upon the people.

**10** The Master said: "The Spring and Autumn Annals is my judgment upon the age. By it, men may know what is right and what is wrong."

**11** Let the rulers of future ages study these records and learn that the Way of Heaven cannot be deceived. Good is rewarded and evil is punished, though the mills of Heaven grind slowly.

**12** This is the chronicle of Duke Min's reign, faithfully recorded for the instruction of posterity.
