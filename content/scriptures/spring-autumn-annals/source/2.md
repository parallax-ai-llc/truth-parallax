---
title: "Spring and Autumn Annals - Chapter 2"
scripture: "spring-autumn-annals"
chapter: 2
---

**1** In the first year of Duke Huan, in the spring, in the king's first month, the Duke came to the throne.

**2** In the third month, the Duke held audience with the feudal lords at Ke.

**3** The people of Qi defeated the forces of Lu at a place called Chang Shao.

**4** In the summer, there was a great drought in the state of Lu. The Duke ordered prayers for rain.

**5** In the autumn, the army of Lu marched to attack the people of Zheng, but returned without success.

**6** In the winter, the Duke sent an envoy to Qi to make peace. The envoy returned with a treaty.

**7** The Annals record: when states contend for power, the people suffer. When states maintain harmony, the people prosper.

**8** Duke Huan of Qi became the first of the Five Hegemons, bringing order to the feudal states by upholding the authority of the Zhou king.

**9** He summoned the lords to conferences, made them pledge to keep faith, and restrained the strong from oppressing the weak.

**10** The principle is this: the ruler who gains the hearts of the people through virtue will endure; the ruler who holds power through force alone will perish.

**11** Confucius praised Duke Huan, saying: "He united the feudal lords not by force of arms but by moral authority."

**12** The gentleman learns from history that virtue is the foundation of lasting power.
