## Overview

The Mabinogion engages philosophical themes through narrative rather than exposition. The tales explore the nature of sovereignty and its relationship to justice and the land, the ethics of honor and obligation in a warrior society, the boundaries between human and otherworldly existence, and the transformative power of enchantment. The Four Branches in particular raise questions about identity and metamorphosis, punishment and redemption, and the relationship between human agency and supernatural forces.
