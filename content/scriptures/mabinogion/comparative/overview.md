## Overview

The Mabinogion offers rich material for comparative study across Celtic, Indo-European, and world mythological traditions. The Four Branches contain motifs — the sovereignty goddess, the otherworldly cauldron, the shape-shifting magician — that have clear parallels in Irish mythology and broader Indo-European tradition. The three Arthurian romances invite comparison with the works of Chrétien de Troyes and raise questions about the direction of literary influence between Welsh and French traditions.
