## Overview

The Mabinogion represents the highest achievement of medieval Welsh prose narrative. Its tales demonstrate sophisticated storytelling techniques, including interlocking narrative structures, dramatic dialogue, vivid characterization, and a distinctive blend of realism and fantasy. The Four Branches form a unified narrative cycle of remarkable literary artistry, while Culhwch and Olwen preserves the most archaic Arthurian tale in any language. Literary analysis examines the tales' narrative structure, prose style, characterization, and relationship to oral storytelling traditions.
