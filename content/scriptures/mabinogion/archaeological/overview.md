## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Mabinogion connect the tales to historical sites, landscape features, and material culture in Wales and the wider Celtic world. Many locations mentioned in the tales — such as Harlech, Aberffraw, and Dyfed — are real places with archaeological significance. Iron Age and Romano-British material culture provides context for the archaic elements preserved in the tales, while medieval archaeological evidence illuminates the world of the tales' composition and audience.
