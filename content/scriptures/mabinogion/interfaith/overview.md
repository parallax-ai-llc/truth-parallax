## Overview

The Mabinogion presents a fascinating case of pre-Christian Celtic religious material preserved and transformed within a medieval Christian literary culture. The tales contain what appear to be euhemerized Celtic deities — Rhiannon as a sovereignty goddess, Manawydan as a sea god — alongside elements of medieval Christian culture. Interfaith analysis explores how the tales negotiate between pagan and Christian worldviews, and what they reveal about Celtic spirituality, the sanctity of the natural world, and the relationship between the human and divine.
