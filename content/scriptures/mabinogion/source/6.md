---
title: "Lludd and Llefelys"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Beli the Great, son of Manogan, had three sons: Lludd, Caswallawn, and Nyniaw, and according to the story he had a fourth son, Llefelys.

**2** Lludd son of Beli took the kingdom of the Island of Britain in his hand, and he rebuilt the walls of London and surrounded it with countless towers.

**3** Three plagues came upon the Island of Britain. The first was a people called the Coraniaid, who could hear every word the wind carried, so that no plan could be made against them.

**4** The second plague was a shriek that came on every May Eve, over every hearth in the Island of Britain. It pierced people's hearts and terrified them so that men lost their color and their strength, and women lost their children.

**5** The third plague was that however much provision and food was prepared in the king's courts, none of it was ever found except what was consumed on the first night.

**6** Lludd sailed to France to seek counsel from his brother Llefelys, who was king there. They spoke through a bronze horn so the Coraniaid could not hear.

**7** Llefelys gave remedies for each plague: insects crushed in water to destroy the Coraniaid; measuring the island to find its center where the two dragons fought; and staying awake to catch the mighty man who stole the provisions.

**8** Lludd followed his brother's counsel. He destroyed the Coraniaid, captured the dragons and buried them at Dinas Emrys, and overcame the giant enchanter. Thus the three plagues were ended.
