---
title: "The Third Branch - Manawydan Son of Llyr"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 3
---

**1** When the seven men of whom we spoke returned from Ireland with Branwen, they buried the head of Bendigeidfran in the White Mount in London, with its face toward France.

**2** Manawydan looked upon Dyfed and upon Harlech. He was filled with sorrow and longing and said: Alas, Almighty God, woe is me! There is none save me without a place to go to this night.

**3** And Pryderi said to him: Be not so sorrowful. My mother Rhiannon is the best woman for conversation. Come with me to Dyfed.

**4** So Manawydan went to Dyfed with Pryderi, and there he married Rhiannon. And for a time they lived happily.

**5** But one evening, after a feast at Arberth, they went to sit on the mound. A peal of thunder sounded, and a fall of mist came, so that no one could see his companion.

**6** When the mist lifted, the land was empty. All the houses, all the animals, all the people had vanished. Only the four of them remained: Manawydan, Pryderi, Rhiannon, and Cigfa.

**7** They wandered the deserted land, hunting and gathering. Then they went to England and worked as saddlers and shoemakers, but were driven from town to town because their work was too fine.

**8** At last Manawydan discovered the enchantment had been placed by Llwyd son of Cil Coed, to avenge the wrong done to his friend Gwawl. Manawydan broke the spell by threatening to hang a mouse, who was Llwyd's wife in enchanted form. The enchantment was lifted and Dyfed was restored.
