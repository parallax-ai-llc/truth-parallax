---
title: "The Dream of Rhonabwy"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Madawg son of Maredudd possessed Powys within its boundaries, from Porffordd to Gwauan in the uplands of Arwystli.

**2** And at that time he had a brother, Iorwerth son of Maredudd, who was not as great as himself. And Iorwerth took to raiding and thievery.

**3** Madawg sent men to seek his brother, and among them was Rhonabwy. They came to the house of Heilyn Goch son of Cadwgan, a wretched lodging.

**4** Rhonabwy lay down upon a yellow ox-skin, and there he slept. And as soon as he fell asleep, a vision came to him.

**5** He dreamed of the time of Arthur, and he saw Arthur's host encamped by the ford of the Severn. The warriors of Arthur were the most splendid sight any man had seen.

**6** Arthur and Owain son of Urien played a game of gwyddbwyll, while their men fought. Three times messengers came to say that Arthur's squires were attacking Owain's ravens, and three times Arthur said only: Play on.

**7** Then Owain's ravens rose up in fury and began to slaughter Arthur's men. Three times messengers came to Arthur, and three times Owain said: Play on. At last Arthur crushed the golden pieces on the board, and peace was made.

**8** And then Rhonabwy awoke. He had slept three days and three nights, and no poet or storyteller could ever tell this dream without a book, because of the number of colors on the horses and arms and equipment.
