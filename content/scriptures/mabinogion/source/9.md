---
title: "Owain, or the Lady of the Fountain"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The Emperor Arthur was at Caerleon upon Usk. One day he sat in his chamber, and with him were Owain son of Urien and Cynon son of Clydno and Cai son of Cynyr.

**2** Cynon told a tale of how he had journeyed to the most beautiful valley in the world and found a fountain beneath a great tree. He threw water on the slab beside it, and a terrible storm arose.

**3** When the storm passed, the tree was stripped of its leaves, and birds sang upon it most sweetly. Then a black knight came and overthrew Cynon and took his horse.

**4** Owain resolved to find this fountain. He rode until he found the valley and the tree and the fountain. He cast water on the slab, and the storm came, and the knight came.

**5** Owain fought the knight and wounded him mortally. He pursued him to his castle, and the gate fell behind him, trapping him. A maiden named Luned gave him a ring of invisibility.

**6** Owain saw the lady of the castle mourning her husband, and he fell in love with her. Through Luned's counsel, the lady agreed to marry Owain, for she needed a champion to defend the fountain.

**7** Owain defended the fountain for three years, until Arthur came to seek him. He returned to Arthur's court, promising to return in three months. But he forgot his promise and stayed too long.

**8** The lady's messenger came and took back the ring, calling him faithless. Owain went mad with grief and wandered in the wilderness until he was healed by a noblewoman's ointment. At last he returned, made amends, and was reunited with his lady.
