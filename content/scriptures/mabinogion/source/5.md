---
title: "The Dream of Macsen Wledig"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Macsen Wledig was Emperor of Rome, and he was the handsomest man and the best fitted to be emperor of all who had gone before him.

**2** One day he went hunting and grew weary. His men raised their shields against the sun and he slept. And in his sleep he saw a dream.

**3** He journeyed in his dream along a river valley to the highest mountain in the world, and beyond it he saw a fair land, and at the mouth of a river he saw a great castle, the fairest ever seen.

**4** Within the castle he found a maiden seated in a chair of red gold. She was the fairest sight any man had ever seen. He sat beside her and put his arms around her.

**5** But just then the dogs began barking and the clash of shields against each other woke him from his sleep. And from that moment he could find no rest, for he loved the maiden of his dream.

**6** His wise men said: Lord, send messengers to seek the maiden. And they searched through all the lands until they found her: Elen, daughter of Eudaf, at Aber Seiont in the land of Arfon.

**7** Macsen journeyed from Rome to Britain, found Elen, and married her. As her maiden fee, she asked for the Island of Britain for her father, and three chief fortresses to be built for her.

**8** While Macsen was in Britain, a new emperor was chosen in Rome. But Macsen marched back with the men of Britain and retook Rome, aided by the cunning of Elen's brothers.
