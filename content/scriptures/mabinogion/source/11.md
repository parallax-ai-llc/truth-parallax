---
title: "Geraint Son of Erbin"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Arthur was accustomed to holding court at Caerleon upon Usk. One Easter he held court there, and after the feast he went to hunt the white stag.

**2** Geraint son of Erbin rode with Queen Gwenhwyfar and a maiden. A strange knight and a dwarf appeared, and the dwarf struck the maiden and Geraint with a whip.

**3** Geraint followed the knight to a town where a tournament was to be held. The prize was a sparrowhawk. Whoever wished to claim it must have a lady to champion.

**4** Geraint lodged with an aged earl and his daughter Enid, the most beautiful maiden in the world, though she was dressed in a faded shift. With old armor borrowed from the earl, Geraint fought the knight and defeated him.

**5** Geraint claimed the sparrowhawk for Enid and brought her to Arthur's court. Arthur and Gwenhwyfar received her with joy, and she was given fine garments.

**6** Geraint married Enid and took her to his father's lands. But he loved her so much that he neglected his duties as a lord, spending his days in her chamber.

**7** Enid wept over this, and Geraint, overhearing only part of her words, thought she was unfaithful. He commanded her to ride before him and not to speak, no matter what she saw.

**8** Through many trials and dangers on the road, Enid proved her faithfulness again and again, warning Geraint of danger despite his command. At last Geraint understood her love, and they were reconciled. He returned to his duties, ruling wisely for the rest of his days.
