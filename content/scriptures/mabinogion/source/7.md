---
title: "Culhwch and Olwen"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Cilydd son of Celyddon Wledig wished for a wife as well-born as himself, and he married Goleuddydd daughter of Anlawdd Wledig.

**2** The woman became pregnant, and she went mad and shunned all habitations. When her time came, her senses returned to her. She came to a place where a swineherd was tending his swine, and through terror of the swine she gave birth.

**3** The boy was taken to the court, and they called him Culhwch because he had been found in a pig run. But the boy was noble, and he was cousin to Arthur.

**4** His stepmother told him he would never have a wife until he won Olwen, daughter of Ysbaddaden Chief Giant. At the mention of her name, love for the maiden rushed upon him.

**5** His father said: Go to Arthur, thy cousin. Ask him to trim thy hair, and ask him for Olwen as a gift. Arthur agreed and sent his best warriors to help.

**6** They found the fortress of Ysbaddaden. The giant said Culhwch must accomplish forty tasks, each seemingly impossible, before he could wed Olwen.

**7** With the help of Arthur and his warriors, every task was accomplished. They hunted the great boar Twrch Trwyth across Ireland, Wales, and Cornwall to win the razor, comb, and shears from between its ears.

**8** At last all tasks were fulfilled. Ysbaddaden's head was cut off and placed on a stake. Culhwch married Olwen, and she was his wife as long as she lived.
