---
title: "Peredur Son of Efrawg"
scripture: "mabinogion"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Earl Efrawg held an earldom in the north. He had seven sons, and six of them were slain in battle. His wife took Peredur, the seventh, to the wilderness to raise him ignorant of arms and war.

**2** One day Peredur saw three of Arthur's knights riding through the forest. He thought they were angels and asked his mother about them. When he learned they were knights, he resolved to go to Arthur's court.

**3** His mother gave him counsel before he departed: be courteous, pray at every church, take food and drink if offered, and be gentle to women. Then she fell dead as he rode away.

**4** Peredur arrived at Arthur's court, uncouth and ignorant of manners. A knight named the Red Knight had insulted the queen. Peredur pursued him, slew him with a sharpened stick, and took his armor.

**5** He went to learn knightly arts from two uncles in succession. The first taught him swordsmanship. The second told him never to ask the meaning of what he saw.

**6** At a mysterious castle, Peredur saw a procession: a great spear dripping blood, and a severed head on a platter in a pool of blood. Because of his uncle's counsel, he asked no questions.

**7** He should have asked about the spear and the head, for they concerned his own kinsmen, and the asking would have healed the land. His silence brought suffering to the kingdom.

**8** After many adventures, slaying monsters, freeing prisoners, and winning the love of the Empress of Constantinople, Peredur learned the truth about the severed head and avenged his cousin. The enchantment was ended.
