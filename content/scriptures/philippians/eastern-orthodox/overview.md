## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Philippians focuses on the Christ hymn (2:6-11) as a revelation of divine kenosis and the paradigm for theosis. Christ's descent into human existence and his subsequent exaltation reveal the pattern by which humanity participates in the divine life: through humility, obedience, and transformation by the Spirit.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Kenosis and Theosis
The hymn's pattern of descent and ascent — from divine glory through human humiliation to cosmic exaltation — is the archetype of theosis. As Christ descended to take on human nature, so humanity ascends to participate in the divine nature. The exchange is real, not merely metaphorical.

### Liturgical Hymn
Orthodox tradition recognizes in Philippians 2:6-11 one of the earliest Christian hymns, and it has shaped the hymnography of the Orthodox liturgy. The pattern of kenosis and glorification structures the liturgical cycle from Nativity through Pascha.

### Ascetic Struggle
Paul's language of "pressing on toward the goal" (3:12-14) and "straining forward" is read as describing the ascetic life — the disciplined struggle toward holiness that characterizes Orthodox monasticism and lay piety alike.
