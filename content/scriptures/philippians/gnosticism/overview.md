## Overview

Gnostic interpreters read Philippians through the lens of the descending-ascending redeemer myth. The Christ hymn (2:6-11), with its pattern of descent from the divine realm into the material world and subsequent exaltation, was interpreted as describing the journey of the divine Redeemer through the realms of the archons to rescue the trapped sparks of light.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### The Descent of the Redeemer
Christ's "emptying himself" and "taking the form of a servant" (2:7) was read as the divine Redeemer disguising himself to pass unrecognized through the archonic realms. The "form of a servant" is the material body assumed as camouflage.

### Heavenly Citizenship
Paul's declaration that "our citizenship is in heaven" (3:20) confirmed the Gnostic belief that the pneumatic (spiritual) person's true home is the *Pleroma*, not the material world created by the Demiurge.

### Transformation of the Body
The expectation that Christ "will transform our lowly body to be like his glorious body" (3:21) was interpreted as the shedding of the material body and the restoration of the spiritual body to its original luminous state.
