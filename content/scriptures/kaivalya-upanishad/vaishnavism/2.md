Section 2 contains the Kaivalya Upanishad's climactic declarations of realization: the sage recognizes the supreme self as pervading all and declares "I am this entire universe." Vaishnava commentators read this not as the soul's literal identity with the cosmos but as the devotee's vision of God in everything — the realization that Vishnu pervades all creation and that seeing the Lord everywhere is the fruit of perfect devotion.

The identification of the supreme self with Brahma, Shiva, Indra, and all other deities is read as confirming Vishnu's position as the one God who assumes all divine forms. The liberated devotee sees Vishnu's unity behind the apparent diversity of the divine.
