## Overview

Vaishnava commentators approach the Kaivalya Upanishad by reading its references to the supreme reality as pointing to Vishnu-Narayana, the ultimate personal God. While acknowledging the text's Shaiva terminology, Vaishnavas argue that the names "Shiva" and "Maheshvara" are titles that can apply to the highest being, who is Vishnu. The teaching on *kaivalya* (absolute independence) is understood as the soul's liberation into eternal communion with the Lord.

## Theological Emphases

Ramanuja's tradition reads the Kaivalya Upanishad's non-dual declarations not as asserting the metaphysical identity of the soul with an impersonal Brahman but as describing the liberated soul's intimate unity with the personal God. *Kaivalya* is not the soul's dissolution but its perfection — the attainment of a state free from material bondage where the soul enjoys the Lord's presence eternally.
