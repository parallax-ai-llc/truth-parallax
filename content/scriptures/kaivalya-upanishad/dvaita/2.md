Madhva reads Section 2's declarations carefully, distinguishing between the Supreme Lord's self-description and the individual soul's experience. The statement "I am this entire universe" is attributed to God speaking through the liberated sage, not to the sage claiming identity with Brahman. The soul in liberation experiences the Lord's glory and sees His presence everywhere, but it does not become God.

The identification of the supreme self with Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva is read as confirming that the one Supreme Lord (Vishnu) operates through all divine functions — creation, preservation, and dissolution — and bears all divine names.
