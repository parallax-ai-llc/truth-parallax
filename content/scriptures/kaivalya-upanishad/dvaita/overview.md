## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Kaivalya Upanishad's teaching on liberation as describing the soul's attainment of absolute freedom from material bondage through Vishnu's grace. *Kaivalya* is not the dissolution of the soul into an impersonal absolute but the soul's achievement of its highest possible state — eternal, blissful awareness in the presence of God.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the declaration "I am this whole universe" is not to be taken literally as the soul's identity with everything but as an expression of the liberated soul's expanded consciousness, which perceives God's presence everywhere. The text's Shaiva terminology is read as applying to Vishnu, the supreme being who bears many names. *Kaivalya* means the soul's independence from material bondage — not independence from God, on whom the soul eternally depends.
