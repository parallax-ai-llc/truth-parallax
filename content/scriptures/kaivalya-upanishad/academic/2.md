Section 2's ecstatic declarations of cosmic identity represent one of the most intense expressions of the mystical experience in Upanishadic literature. Scholars note the section's literary power — the progressive expansion from personal realization to cosmic identification — and its influence on later devotional and philosophical poetry.

The explicit identification of the supreme reality with Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma simultaneously reflects a theological position that was either genuinely non-sectarian (all gods are one) or strategically inclusivist (claiming all gods for one tradition). The interpretation depends on whether one reads the text as primarily Shaiva (with the other names absorbed into Shiva) or as genuinely ecumenical.
