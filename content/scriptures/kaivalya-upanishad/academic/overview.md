## Overview

The Kaivalya Upanishad is classified among the Shaiva Upanishads — texts that identify the supreme Brahman specifically with Shiva. Scholars date it to approximately the early centuries CE, though the dating remains uncertain. Its combination of Vedantic non-duality with explicit Shaiva devotion reflects the complex interaction between philosophical monism and sectarian theism in Indian religious history.

## Research Themes

The text's explicit identification of Brahman with Shiva-Maheshvara raises important questions about sectarian competition and synthesis in the Upanishadic corpus. Some scholars argue that the Kaivalya represents a Shaiva appropriation of Vedantic categories; others see it as evidence of an originally non-sectarian Upanishadic tradition that was claimed by various schools. The concept of *kaivalya* itself has a complex history, appearing in Samkhya-Yoga, Jainism, and Vedanta with related but distinct meanings.
