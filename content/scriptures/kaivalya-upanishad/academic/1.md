Section 1 follows a common Upanishadic pattern: a student approaches a teacher (here, Ashvalayana approaches Brahma) and asks for the highest knowledge. The answer — that liberation comes through renunciation and knowledge, not ritual — represents the standard Upanishadic position that was later elaborated by all Vedantic schools.

The meditation instructions in Section 1 are notable for their specificity and their Shaiva coloring. The description of the supreme reality as dwelling in the heart, beyond darkness, and identified with Shiva-Maheshvara reflects the synthesis of Upanishadic monism with Shaiva devotion that characterizes the Shaiva Upanishads.
