Section 1 presents Brahma teaching the path to kaivalya through meditation on the supreme reality, which the text identifies with Shiva-Maheshvara. The instruction to meditate on the Lord "in the cave of the heart" as the supreme light resonates deeply with Shaiva contemplative practice. The heart-cave (*hridaya-guha*) is the innermost space of consciousness where Shiva dwells.

The teaching that liberation comes not through ritual but through knowledge and renunciation aligns with the Shaiva emphasis on *jnana* (knowledge) as the direct means to recognition. The path described here is essentially the Shaiva path: turning inward, concentrating on the divine presence, and recognizing it as one's own nature.
