Section 2 is the Kaivalya Upanishad's supreme teaching and a foundational text for Shaiva non-dualism. The realized sage declares: "I am Brahma, I am Vishnu, I am Shiva, I am this entire universe" — not as a claim of the ego but as the recognition of consciousness as the sole reality. For Kashmir Shaivism, this is the *pratyabhijna* (recognition) that all Shaiva practice aims at: the individual recognizes that their own awareness is Shiva's unlimited consciousness.

The section's identification of the sage with all deities — Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Indra — expresses the Shaiva understanding that Shiva is not one god among many but the absolute consciousness that appears as all gods, all beings, and all worlds.
