Section 2 presents the Advaita experience in its most direct form: the sage who has completed the path of meditation realizes "I am this entire universe" — not as an ego-expansion but as the dissolution of the ego into the infinite Brahman. This is the *mahavakya* in experiential form: not "Brahman exists" but "I am Brahman and there is nothing else."

For Shankara's tradition, the declaration "I am Brahma, I am Vishnu, I am Shiva" demonstrates that all divine names point to the same non-dual reality. The apparent plurality of gods is resolved in the unity of Brahman, just as the apparent plurality of selves is resolved in the recognition that there is only one self.
