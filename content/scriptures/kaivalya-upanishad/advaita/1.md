Section 1 establishes the Advaita framework: liberation comes through knowledge, not action. Brahma's teaching that neither children, wealth, nor ritual works lead to immortality — only renunciation — echoes the Mahanarayana Upanishad and provides scriptural support for Shankara's prioritization of *jnana* over *karma*.

The meditation instruction — focusing on the supreme self in the cave of the heart — describes the *nididhyasana* (deep contemplation) that is the final stage of Advaita practice. The self to be meditated upon is not different from the meditator — this recognition is the content of kaivalya.
