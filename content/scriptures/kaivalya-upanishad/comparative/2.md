The Kaivalya's declaration "I am this entire universe" places it in the universal tradition of mystical identity experiences. Mansur al-Hallaj's *ana al-Haqq* ("I am the Truth/God"), the Hasidic teaching of *devekut* (cleaving to God so completely that the boundary dissolves), and Meister Eckhart's teaching that "the eye with which I see God is the same eye with which God sees me" all express the same recognition.

The identification of the self with all deities — "I am Brahma, I am Vishnu, I am Shiva" — compares with the Sufi concept of the *insan al-kamil* (perfect human) who mirrors all divine attributes, and the Christian concept of *theosis* (divinization) in which the saint participates in the divine nature (2 Peter 1:4).
