## Overview

The Kaivalya Upanishad's teaching on absolute liberation — the self's realization of its identity with the supreme reality — invites comparison with concepts of spiritual freedom across traditions. The radical claim "I am this whole universe" places the Kaivalya within the universal mystical tradition of theosis, unio mystica, and fana.

## Comparative Themes

The Kaivalya's declaration of identity with the divine parallels Mansur al-Hallaj's *ana al-Haqq* ("I am the Truth") in Sufism, Meister Eckhart's teaching on the soul's identity with the Godhead, and the Zen insight into Buddha-nature as one's original face. The concept of *kaivalya* as absolute independence compares with the Stoic ideal of the sage's inner freedom (*eleutheria*), the Buddhist nirvana as freedom from all conditioning, and the existentialist concept of radical freedom.
