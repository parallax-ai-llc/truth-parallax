Brahma's teaching that liberation comes not through external means (wealth, offspring, ritual) but through inner knowledge and renunciation parallels foundational teachings across traditions. The Buddha's rejection of ritual as the means to liberation, Jesus's teaching that the kingdom of God is within (Luke 17:21), and the Sufi insistence that external practice must be accompanied by inner transformation all express the same insight.

The meditation instruction — turning inward to find the divine in the heart — echoes Augustine's famous discovery: "You were within and I was without" (*Confessions* 10.27). The universality of the inward turn suggests a fundamental structure of contemplative experience across cultures.
