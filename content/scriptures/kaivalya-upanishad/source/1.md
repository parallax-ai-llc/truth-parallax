---
title: "Kaivalya Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "kaivalya-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Then Ashvalayana approached the Lord Brahma, the creator, and said: "Teach me, O Lord, the knowledge of Brahman, the highest, which is ever cherished by the wise, which is secret, and by which the learned quickly wash away all sins and reach the Purusha who is higher than the highest."

**2** To him the Grandsire said: "Know this by means of faith, devotion, and meditation. Not by works, not by offspring, not by wealth, but by renunciation alone is immortality attained."

**3** Higher than heaven, seated in the cave of the heart, it shines, which those who practise self-discipline attain. The ascetics, who have well ascertained the meaning of the Vedanta knowledge, who have purified their natures by the yoga of renunciation, at the time of the great end become free in the world of Brahma.

**4** In a secluded place, sitting in an easy posture, pure, with neck, head, and body erect, having given up the duties of all the stages of life, with subdued senses, having saluted his teacher with reverence, let him meditate within the lotus of the heart, on Brahman, the pure, the infinite, the blissful.

**5** The unthinkable, the unmanifested, the one of endless forms, the ever-good, the peaceful, the immortal, the origin of the creator, the one without beginning, middle, or end, the only one, the all-pervading, the Knowledge-Bliss, the formless, the wonderful.

**6** Meditating on the highest Lord, allied to Uma, powerful, three-eyed, blue-necked, and tranquil, the holy man reaches Him who is the source of all, the witness of all, and who is beyond darkness.

**7** He is Brahma, He is Shiva, He is Indra, He is the Immutable, the Supreme, the Self-luminous; He alone is Vishnu, He is the Life-force, He is Time, He is the Fire, and He is the Moon.

**8** He alone is all that was, and all that will be, the Eternal; knowing Him, one crosses over death; there is no other path for liberation.

**9** Seeing the Atman in all beings, and all beings in the Atman, one attains the highest Brahman, and not by any other means.

**10** Making the Atman the lower piece of wood, and Om the upper piece, by the practice of the friction of meditation, the wise man burns up the bond of ignorance.

**11** He, being deluded by Maya, moves about in the body, and through him all works are done in manifold ways during waking and other states.

**12** The cause of these states is matter; and he is the Lord of matter. Of His creation, mind and the other products, all this world becomes absorbed in Him at the time of dissolution.
