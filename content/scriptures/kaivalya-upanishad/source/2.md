---
title: "Kaivalya Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kaivalya-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** He who is the source of Brahma, the creator of all beings, the preserver of the universe, the almighty Lord, who is hidden in all beings as ghee is hidden in milk, in whom the knowledge of the self and self-discipline are contained, to Him the highest Brahman, be adoration.

**2** I am that Brahman, the one Illuminator of all, the supreme light, the ever pure knowledge. I have neither birth nor death, neither pleasure nor pain.

**3** I have no mind, no senses, no body, no prana; I am beyond hunger and thirst, beyond grief, beyond delusion, beyond old age and death; for I have shaken off the mortal body. I am the Spirit, beyond nature.

**4** I am not the doer, not the enjoyer; I am the changeless, the actionless. I am pure intelligence, one, ever blissful. I am all-pervading, I am the eternal Atman.

**5** I am beyond all thought, without any form; yet I am all-pervading. I am beyond the senses, beyond the mind. I am the pure consciousness that is the Self of all.

**6** I am neither male, nor female, nor neuter. I am the peaceful and supreme Brahman. I am beyond the known and beyond the unknown. In truth I am Brahman itself.

**7** He who reads the Kaivalya Upanishad is purified from all sins. He is purified from the sin of slaying a Brahmin, from the sin of drinking wine, from the sin of theft, from sins committed by thought, word, or deed.

**8** Therefore let him take refuge in this solitary place and read this; by a single reading he attains the merit that comes of the morning prayers. By reading it at noon he destroys the sin committed during the day. By reading it in the evening he destroys the sin committed during the night.

**9** The collection of morning, noon, and evening prayers destroys all sin accumulated in many past lives. He who thus knows, attains the nature of Brahman.

**10** He attains the fruits of bathing in all the holy waters, the fruits of all sacrifices, the fruits of a hundred recitations of the Gayatri, the merits of pranava a hundred thousand times repeated.

**11** He who reads the Kaivalya Upanishad attains the state of Kaivalya, the state of absolute freedom. Yea, he attains the state of absolute freedom. Thus is the Upanishad.
