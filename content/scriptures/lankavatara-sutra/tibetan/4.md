The three natures (rang bzhin gsum) framework presented in this chapter is studied in Tibetan Buddhist monastic education as part of the Yogacara philosophical system. Tibetan scholars have developed sophisticated analyses of how the three natures relate to the Madhyamaka two-truths framework of conventional (kun rdzob) and ultimate (don dam) truth.

The Tibetan Yogacara-Madhyamaka synthesis, developed by scholars such as Shantarakshita and his student Kamalashila, attempted to integrate the three natures with the Madhyamaka analysis of emptiness. The imagined nature corresponds to the way things appear to the deluded mind, the dependent nature corresponds to conventional reality, and the perfected nature corresponds to ultimate truth understood as the emptiness of the dependent nature.

The chapter's treatment of dependent origination from the mind-only perspective has been analyzed by Tibetan scholars through the categories of Madhyamaka philosophy. Tsongkhapa's commentary tradition emphasizes that the ultimate understanding of dependent origination reveals that things arise dependently and are therefore empty of inherent existence, a reading that transforms the Yogacara framework from within.
