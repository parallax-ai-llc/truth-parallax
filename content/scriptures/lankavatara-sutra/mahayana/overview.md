## Overview

The Lankavatara Sutra is a foundational text for both the Yogacara (Mind-Only) and tathagatagarbha strands of Mahayana Buddhism. It presents a comprehensive philosophical vision in which all experienced phenomena are projections of consciousness, and the inherent buddha-nature (tathagatagarbha) awaits recognition beneath the obscurations of ignorance.

## Doctrinal Significance

The sutra's synthesis of Yogacara and tathagatagarbha thought is distinctive. It teaches the eight consciousnesses with the alaya-vijnana as their foundation, while simultaneously affirming that this storehouse consciousness, when purified, reveals the tathagatagarbha. This integration provided a bridge between two of the most important philosophical movements in Mahayana Buddhism.

## Transformative Philosophy

The teaching of the "turning about" (paravrtti) in the deepest seat of consciousness describes liberation as a fundamental shift in how the mind relates to its own projections. This is not the acquisition of something new but the recognition of what has always been present, a teaching that influenced all subsequent Mahayana understanding of awakening.
