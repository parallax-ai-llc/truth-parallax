The third collection introduces the three natures (trisvabhava), one of the most important philosophical frameworks in Yogacara Buddhism. The imagined nature (parikalpita-svabhava) refers to the conceptual constructions that beings project onto experience. The dependent nature (paratantra-svabhava) refers to the actual flow of conditioned phenomena. The perfected nature (parinishpanna-svabhava) refers to the dependent nature seen without the overlay of imaginary constructions.

This framework provides the Mahayana practitioner with a precise tool for understanding the process of awakening. Liberation does not involve the destruction of phenomena (the dependent nature) but the cessation of imaginary constructions projected onto those phenomena (the imagined nature). What remains when projection ceases is the perfected nature, reality as it actually is.

The chapter's discussion of dependent origination from the mind-only perspective reinterprets the twelve links not as a description of an objective process but as an account of how consciousness generates the appearance of causality. This radical rereading of dependent origination shows how the Yogacara tradition maintained continuity with fundamental Buddhist teaching while transforming its philosophical implications.
