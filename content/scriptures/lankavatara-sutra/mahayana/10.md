The Sagathakam (verse chapter) provides a poetic recapitulation of the entire sutra's teaching, condensing complex philosophical arguments into memorable verses. In the Mahayana literary tradition, verse summaries serve as aids to memorization, objects of contemplation, and liturgical texts for communal recitation.

The verses address the major themes of the sutra: the mind-only nature of reality, the tathagatagarbha as the ground of awakening, the transformation of consciousness, the inexpressibility of ultimate truth, and the compassionate engagement of the bodhisattva. Each verse distills a teaching that received extended philosophical treatment in the prose chapters into a concentrated form suitable for sustained reflection.

The Sagathakam has been studied as a potentially independent text that was incorporated into the sutra during its compositional history. Whether or not this is the case, the verse chapter serves as a comprehensive summary that allows practitioners to engage with the sutra's essential teachings through a more accessible literary form. The Mahayana tradition has long valued verse as a vehicle for the dharma, recognizing that poetic language can communicate dimensions of meaning that prose argument cannot reach.
