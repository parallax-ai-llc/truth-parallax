## Overview

The Lankavatara Sutra (Laṅkāvatārasūtra) is a foundational Mahayana text that presents the Buddha's teachings on the nature of consciousness, the doctrine of mind-only (cittamatra), and the tathagatagarbha (buddha-nature embryo). Set on the island of Lanka (Sri Lanka) at the invitation of the demon king Ravana, the sutra takes the form of a series of questions and answers between Mahamati Bodhisattva and the Buddha.

## Structure

The sutra is organized across 10 chapters:

1. **Ravana's Request** — The demon king invites the Buddha to Lanka
2. **Collection of All Dharmas I** — First discourse on consciousness and reality
3. **Collection II** — Continuation of the philosophical dialogue
4. **Collection III** — Deeper exploration of mind-only teaching
5. **Collection IV** — Further analysis of consciousness
6. **Collection V** — Final collection discourse
7. **Change** — The transformation of consciousness
8. **Meat-eating** — Arguments against consuming flesh
9. **Dharani** — Protective formulas and incantations
10. **Verses (Sagathakam)** — Poetic summary of the entire teaching

## Cross-Traditional Significance

According to tradition, Bodhidharma transmitted the Lankavatara Sutra to his successor Huike as the essential text of early Chan Buddhism. The sutra's influence extends across Yogacara philosophy, Zen practice, and Tibetan Buddhism's understanding of buddha-nature.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Mind-Only | All phenomena are projections of consciousness |
| Tathagatagarbha | The inherent potential for buddhahood within all beings |
| Eight Consciousnesses | The alaya-vijnana as storehouse of karmic seeds |
| Self-realization | The noble wisdom of inner realization beyond words |
| Non-duality | Transcendence of subject-object dichotomy |
