## Overview

The Lankavatara Sutra holds a special place in Zen history as the text Bodhidharma reportedly transmitted to his successor Huike, making it the foundational scripture of early Chan Buddhism. Before the Diamond Sutra gained prominence through Huineng, the Lankavatara was the defining text of the Chan lineage.

## Zen Reading

Zen tradition emphasizes the sutra's teaching on pratyatma-arya-jnana, the "noble wisdom of self-realization," as the essence of what is transmitted from master to disciple. The sutra's insistence that truth cannot be captured in words and concepts aligns with the Zen motto of "a special transmission outside the scriptures."

## Practice Implications

The Lankavatara's description of the "turning about" at the deepest level of consciousness corresponds to the Zen experience of kensho or satori. The sutra teaches that this transformation cannot be achieved through intellectual analysis alone but requires a direct seeing that transcends the duality of subject and object.
