---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 5: On the Two Kinds of Selflessness"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Mahamati asked: "Lord, what are the two kinds of selflessness?" The Blessed One said: "Mahamati, the two kinds of selflessness are: the selflessness of persons and the selflessness of dharmas."

**2** "The selflessness of persons means that in the collection of the aggregates, elements, and sense bases, there is no self, no soul, no person, no being, no life principle. What is called a 'person' is merely a conventional designation for the combination of these factors."

**3** "When one analyzes the person into aggregates—form, feeling, perception, formations, consciousness—no self is found. Form is not the self. Feeling is not the self. Perception is not the self. Formations are not the self. Consciousness is not the self."

**4** "The selflessness of dharmas means that the dharmas themselves—the aggregates, elements, and bases—are also empty of self-nature. They arise in dependence on conditions and have no independent existence."

**5** "Mahamati, the imagined self-nature of things is like a city of the Gandharvas—it appears but has no reality. Their dependent self-nature is like a magician's trick—it functions but has no substance. Their absolute self-nature is the truth that transcends all predication."

**6** "When both kinds of selflessness are realized, the Bodhisattva is freed from the obstacles to knowledge and the obstacles to defilement. He enters the stages of the Bodhisattva path and proceeds to the realization of full Buddhahood."

**7** "Mahamati, the realization of the twofold selflessness is not a mere intellectual understanding. It must be realized in one's own inner experience, by means of meditation, by the cessation of discriminating thought."

**8** "Words and concepts can point to the truth, but they cannot capture it. The finger pointing at the moon is not the moon. The wise should not cling to the words but should seek the reality to which they point."

**9** "When the Bodhisattva realizes the twofold selflessness, he understands that there is no birth and no death, no bondage and no liberation, no defilement and no purity. These are all discriminations of the deluded mind."

**10** "In the state of self-realization, there is neither existence nor non-existence, neither self nor not-self. There is only the peace of noble wisdom, the bliss of Nirvana, the reality of suchness. This is the goal of the Bodhisattva's practice."
