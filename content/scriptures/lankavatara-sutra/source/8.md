---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 8: On the Buddha's Teaching"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Mahamati asked: "Lord, what is the teaching of the Tathagata?" The Blessed One said: "Mahamati, the teaching of the Tathagata is not a teaching. If it were a teaching, it would be something that could be grasped. But the truth that the Tathagata has realized cannot be grasped."

**2** "Words are not the truth. Words are merely fingers pointing at the moon. The ignorant look at the finger and miss the moon. The wise look through the finger to the moon itself."

**3** "Mahamati, the Tathagata teaches in many ways, using many words, many similes, many arguments, many methods. But all these are expedient means. They are not the truth itself."

**4** "The truth is beyond words, beyond thought, beyond conceptual elaboration. It can only be realized by each one for himself, in his own inner experience. No one can give it to another."

**5** "Mahamati, the Tathagata's teaching is like a raft for crossing the river. Once the river is crossed, the raft should be left behind. Similarly, the teaching is for the purpose of crossing from the shore of ignorance to the shore of wisdom. Once wisdom is attained, the teaching should not be clung to."

**6** "Yet the teaching is indispensable, for without it, beings would have no way to cross. The raft is not the shore, but without the raft, the shore cannot be reached."

**7** "Mahamati, there is what is known as the teaching without words. This is the direct transmission from mind to mind, the pointing directly to the nature of mind. It is transmitted not through speech or scripture but through the power of the Tathagata's presence."

**8** "The Buddhas of the past, present, and future all teach in two ways: by words and by wordless transmission. The teaching by words is adapted to the capacities of the listeners. The wordless teaching is the same for all."

**9** "Mahamati, do not be attached to words and letters. The meaning that lies behind the words is what you should seek. But also do not despise words and letters, for they serve as the entrance to the truth."

**10** "When the Bodhisattva realizes that all teachings are empty of self-nature, that they are expedient means, he does not cling to any teaching, yet he does not reject any teaching. He uses all teachings as instruments for the liberation of beings."
