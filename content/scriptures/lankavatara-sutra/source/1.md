---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 1: Ravana's Invitation"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. The Blessed One once stayed on the peak of Mount Malaya on the great ocean, in the palace of the King of the Rakshasas, in the city of Lanka, which was adorned with flowers and jewels of every kind, with peaks and ramparts, with towers of various gems.

**2** The Blessed One, who had experienced the state of self-realization by noble wisdom, by his own inner experience, had entered upon the stage of Tathagatahood. He was accompanied by a large assembly of Bodhisattvas.

**3** Ravana, Lord of the Rakshasas, maker of ten chariots, with his retinue, came to the Blessed One and said: "I wish to ask the Blessed One about the self-realization of noble wisdom, which is beyond the reach of philosophers and their dualistic discriminations."

**4** The Blessed One, knowing the mental dispositions of all those in the assembly, said: "Ravana, the Tathagatas of the past have discoursed on the one hundred and eight statements. I will do the same."

**5** "Lord of Lanka, these things are born of discrimination. They are like flowers in the sky, like the child of a barren woman, like the tracks of birds in the sky. They have no self-nature."

**6** "What appears to be external does not exist in reality. It is the mind that, due to habit-energy, appears in the form of multiplicities of objects and beings. The body, property, and abode—all these are discriminations of the mind."

**7** "Lord of Lanka, in this world there are two kinds of those who seek knowledge: those who seek it by means of philosophical propositions and those who seek the true meaning that transcends words. The teaching of transcending words is that of the Tathagatas."

**8** "What is the characteristic of the inner realization of noble wisdom? It is the cessation of discrimination. When discrimination ceases, there is Nirvana."

**9** "Lord of Lanka, things are not as they appear, nor are they otherwise. To the unenlightened, the world appears real. To the enlightened, it is seen as it truly is—empty of self-nature, like a dream, an illusion, a reflection."

**10** Then Ravana, overjoyed, praised the Blessed One with these verses: "I will ask the Buddha, the World-honoured One, who is free from attachment, about the inner realization experienced by the Tathagata—that which is free from words, that which is beyond the reach of the mind, that which is gained by practising the way of self-realization."
