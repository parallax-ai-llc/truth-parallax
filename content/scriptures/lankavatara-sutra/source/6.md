---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 6: On Nirvana"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Mahamati asked: "Lord, what is Nirvana?" The Blessed One said: "Mahamati, Nirvana is the cessation of discrimination. When all discriminating thought ceases, when the mind no longer divides reality into subject and object, into self and other—that is Nirvana."

**2** "Nirvana is not a place. It is not a thing. It is not extinction. It is not non-existence. It is the turning-about in the deepest seat of consciousness, the inner realization of noble wisdom."

**3** "The philosophers imagine Nirvana to be the cessation of the elements, the annihilation of existence. But this is not the Nirvana taught by the Tathagata."

**4** "Others imagine Nirvana to be an eternal state of bliss, a permanent self freed from the body. But this too is not the Nirvana of the Tathagata."

**5** "The Nirvana of the Tathagata is the realization that samsara and Nirvana are not two different things. When the discrimination between samsara and Nirvana ceases, that itself is Nirvana."

**6** "Mahamati, the Bodhisattva does not enter Nirvana as a place apart from the world. Rather, by his vow of compassion, he remains in the world to save all beings, while abiding in the wisdom of Nirvana."

**7** "This is the Nirvana of the Bodhisattva: not to abide in samsara because of wisdom, and not to abide in Nirvana because of compassion. It is the non-abiding Nirvana, the Nirvana that neither clings to the world nor abandons it."

**8** "The Sravakas and Pratyekabuddhas, having realized the selflessness of persons, enter into a Nirvana of their own. But they have not realized the selflessness of dharmas. Therefore their Nirvana is not complete."

**9** "The complete Nirvana is that of the Tathagata, who has realized both kinds of selflessness, who has transcended all views, all discriminations, all habit-energy. His Nirvana is the Dharmakaya itself."

**10** "Mahamati, do not seek Nirvana outside of the world of appearances. And do not cling to the world of appearances as if it were real. See through the appearances to the reality beyond—this is Nirvana."
