---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 10: Verses of the Blessed One (Sagathakam)"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Things are not as they appear, nor are they otherwise. Those who see things as they appear are the worldly-minded. Those who see things as otherwise are also deluded. When all dualities are transcended, there is the truth.

**2** The mind is like a painter. It paints the world with its discriminations. The six consciousnesses are the canvas. The world is the painting. But the painter, the canvas, and the painting are all mind.

**3** Like waves upon the ocean, the seven consciousnesses arise from the alaya. The ocean is the alaya. The waves are the functioning consciousnesses. Calm the ocean, and the waves subside.

**4** The world is nothing but mind. Mind is nothing but the world. When this is understood, the duality of mind and world is dissolved.

**5** Samsara is not different from Nirvana. Nirvana is not different from samsara. When discrimination between them ceases, there is peace.

**6** All things are empty of self-nature. They arise from conditions. They are like clouds in the sky—appearing, changing, disappearing—having no permanent substance.

**7** The Tathagata does not teach any dharma. For there is no dharma to be taught. If anyone says, "The Tathagata has taught a dharma," that person misunderstands the Tathagata.

**8** Form is emptiness. Emptiness is form. This is not a statement of identity but a description of the way things are. What appears as form is empty of self-nature. What is empty of self-nature appears as form.

**9** Let the Bodhisattva cultivate compassion without attachment, wisdom without pride, and effort without self-will. Let him save all beings while knowing that there are no beings to be saved.

**10** The Lanka has been described. The city of the mind has been entered. The truth beyond words has been pointed to. Now let the practitioner realize it for himself, in his own inner experience, beyond all teaching, beyond all scripture, beyond all thought.
