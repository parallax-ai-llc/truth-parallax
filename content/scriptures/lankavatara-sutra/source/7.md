---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 7: On Food and Non-Killing"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The Blessed One said: "Mahamati, let me now speak about the merit of not eating meat. Eating meat is not approved by the Buddhas. In no Sutra is the eating of meat approved for any purpose."

**2** "Mahamati, in the long course of transmigration, there is no being that has not at some time been one's mother, father, brother, sister, son, or daughter. When one eats meat, one may be eating the flesh of one's own relatives."

**3** "The Bodhisattva who desires to practise great compassion should not eat meat. For how can one who eats the flesh of sentient beings have great compassion?"

**4** "Mahamati, I have not permitted meat-eating to anyone and I do not permit it now and I will not permit it in any future time. In no text has it been approved."

**5** "The eating of meat is rejected by me. The ignorant say that I approved of meat-eating, but this is not so. Even in the discipline, when meat is permitted under certain conditions, it is permitted only as an expedient and not as a permanent practice."

**6** "Mahamati, even those who kill animals for food do so driven by their cravings and defilements. If there were no one to eat meat, there would be no one to kill."

**7** "The Bodhisattva, considering all beings as his own relatives, considering all beings as deserving of compassion, should refrain from eating meat. He should eat simple food—grains, vegetables, fruits—sufficient to sustain the body."

**8** "The body that is nourished by meat becomes a body of violence. The body that is nourished by plants becomes a body of peace."

**9** "In the future, there will be those among my followers who will say, 'The Buddha permitted meat-eating.' But they do not understand the intention of the teaching. They cling to the letter and miss the spirit."

**10** "Therefore, Mahamati, the Bodhisattva who aspires to the highest goal should practise non-killing and non-eating of meat, and should encourage all beings to do the same. This is the practice of great compassion."
