---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 9: On the Stages of the Bodhisattva"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Mahamati asked: "Lord, what are the stages through which the Bodhisattva progresses?" The Blessed One said: "Mahamati, the stages are those of the ordinary person, the practitioner, the Tathagata."

**2** "At the stage of the ordinary person, one discriminates between existence and non-existence, between self and not-self, between samsara and Nirvana. One is bound by views."

**3** "At the stage of the practitioner, one begins to see through discriminations. One realizes the emptiness of self and of dharmas. One practises the paramitas. One enters the stages of the Bodhisattva path."

**4** "The first stage is the Stage of Joy, where the Bodhisattva, for the first time, sees the truth of selflessness. The second stage is the Stage of Purity, where moral conduct is perfected."

**5** "Through the successive stages, the Bodhisattva develops wisdom and compassion in increasing measure. At each stage, deeper layers of discrimination are abandoned, and deeper levels of reality are perceived."

**6** "At the eighth stage, the Stage of Immovability, the Bodhisattva attains the patient acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. He realizes that nothing has ever truly arisen, nothing has ever truly ceased. This is a profound turning-point."

**7** "At the stage of the Tathagata, the Bodhisattva has transcended all stages. He has realized the Dharmakaya. He abides in the samadhi of the Tathagata, from which he manifests in countless forms for the benefit of beings."

**8** "Mahamati, the stages are not to be understood as separate places or states that one passes through sequentially, as if climbing a ladder. They are deepening realizations of the same truth."

**9** "Just as the full moon is always the full moon, though it appears to wax and wane to the observer, so the Tathagata-nature is always perfect, though it appears to develop through stages to the practitioner."

**10** "When the Bodhisattva reaches the final stage, he realizes that there are no stages, that there has never been any movement, any progress, any attainment. This is the supreme paradox and the supreme truth."
