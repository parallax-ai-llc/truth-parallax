---
title: "Lankavatara Sutra - Chapter 3: On Impermanence and the Tathagata-garbha"
scripture: "lankavatara-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Bodhisattva Mahamati asked: "Lord, the Blessed One speaks of the Tathagata-garbha, the womb or embryo of the Tathagata, as present in all beings. How does this differ from the self as taught by the philosophers?"

**2** The Blessed One said: "Mahamati, the Tathagata-garbha taught by me is not the same as the self taught by the philosophers. The Tathagata-garbha is taught as a skilful means to attract those who are attached to the notion of a self."

**3** "The Tathagata-garbha is described as luminous in nature, pure from the beginning, endowed with the thirty-two marks of excellence. It is hidden within the body of every being, like a precious gem wrapped in a dirty cloth."

**4** "But this Tathagata-garbha is not a self. It is emptiness, reality-limit, Nirvana, the unborn, the unqualified, the devoid of will-effort. It is taught under the name Tathagata-garbha for the sake of beings who might be frightened by the teaching of complete selflessness."

**5** "Mahamati, in the future and in the present, there are those among my disciples who, clinging to the letter of the Tathagata-garbha teaching, will imagine it to be a self. This would be a mistake. The Tathagata-garbha is not a self; it is the disclosure of the nature of things as they really are."

**6** "Just as a potter makes various vessels from the same clay, so from the one Tathagata-garbha, various forms of sentient beings appear. But the clay is not the pot, and the Tathagata-garbha is not the individual self."

**7** "Mahamati, all things are impermanent. All composite things are subject to decay. What arises in dependence on conditions will also cease when those conditions cease."

**8** "The wise perceive that all that is seen of this world is like a dream, like a mirage, like a city of the Gandharvas. It is not to be taken as real, nor is it to be denied as utterly non-existent. It is to be seen as it is—a manifestation of mind."

**9** "When the mind is purified and the habit-energy of discriminating consciousness is exhausted, there arises the inner realization of noble wisdom. This is not the annihilation of the world but the seeing of the world as it truly is."

**10** "The Tathagata-garbha, when freed from the covering of defilements, is none other than the Dharmakaya, the Truth-body of the Tathagata. It is the same in all beings. It is the ground of all existence and the goal of all practice."
