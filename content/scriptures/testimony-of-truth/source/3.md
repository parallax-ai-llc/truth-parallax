---
title: "Testimony of Truth - Chapter 3"
scripture: "testimony-of-truth"
chapter: 3
---

**1** But the one whom the serpent in Genesis beguiled is the interpretation of the one who deceives: for the serpent is wiser than all the animals that were upon the earth.

**2** In what way did the serpent speak? The serpent said, "The gods know that on the day when you eat from the tree which is in the midst of paradise, the eyes of your mind will be opened."

**3** But the Lord, the one who made all of this, said to Adam, "From every tree you may eat, but from the tree which is in the midst of paradise do not eat, for on the day that you eat from it you will surely die."

**4** But the serpent was wiser than all the animals in paradise, and he persuaded Eve, saying, "On the day when you eat from the tree which is in the midst of paradise, the eyes of your mind will be opened."

**5** And Eve obeyed, and she stretched forth her hand; she took from the tree and ate; she also gave to her husband with her. And immediately they knew that they were naked, and they took some fig leaves and put them on as girdles.

**6** But God came at the time of evening, walking in the midst of paradise. When Adam saw him he hid himself. And he said, "Adam, where are you?" He answered and said, "I have come under the fig tree." And at that very moment, God knew that he had eaten from the tree of which he had commanded him, "Do not eat of it."

**7** And he said to him, "Who is it who has instructed you?" And Adam answered, "The woman whom you have given me." And the woman said, "It is the serpent who instructed me." And he cursed the serpent, and called him devil.

**8** And he said, "Behold, Adam has become like one of us, knowing evil and good." Then he said, "Let us cast him out of paradise, lest he take from the tree of life and eat and live forever."

**9** But what sort is this God? First he maliciously refused Adam from eating of the tree of knowledge. And secondly he said, "Adam, where are you?" God does not have foreknowledge; otherwise, would he not know from the beginning?

**10** And afterwards he said, "Let us cast him out of this place, lest he eat of the tree of life and live forever." Surely he has shown himself to be a malicious grudger.

**11** And what kind of God is this? For great is the blindness of those who read, and they did not know him. And he said, "I am the jealous God; I will bring the sins of the fathers upon the children until three and four generations."

**12** And he said, "I will make their heart thick, and I will cause their mind to become blind, that they might not know nor comprehend the things that are said." But these things he has said to those who believe in him and serve him.

**13** And in one place Moses writes, "He made the devil a serpent for those whom he has in his generation."

**14** Also, in the book which is called Exodus, it is written thus: "He contended against the magicians, when the place was full of serpents according to their wickedness; and the rod which was in the hand of Moses became a serpent, and it swallowed the serpents of the magicians."

**15** This God who opposed them is the one who made the serpent. And the truth of this matter is evident in the types and symbols which have been written.

**16** But the man of truth — his mouth proclaims and his heart loves the truth.

**17** The wise man feeds on the truth, and will not be ruled by the desire of his heart.

**18** He who knows the truth is a free man. And the free man does not sin; for he who sins is the slave of sin.

**19** The truth is the mother; knowledge is the father. Those who think that sinning does not apply to them are called free by the world.

**20** Knowledge of the truth merely makes such people arrogant, which is what the words "it makes them free" mean. It even gives them a sense of superiority over the whole world.

**21** But love builds up. In fact, he who is really free through knowledge is a slave because of love toward those who have not yet been able to attain to the freedom of knowledge.

**22** And knowledge makes them capable of becoming free.
