---
title: "Testimony of Truth - Chapter 2"
scripture: "testimony-of-truth"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The foolish, thinking in their heart that if they confess, "We are Christians," in word only but not with power, while giving themselves over to ignorance, to a human death, not knowing where they are going nor who Christ is, thinking that they will live, when they are really in error, hasten towards the principalities and the authorities.

**2** They fall into their clutches because of the ignorance that is in them.

**3** For if only words which bear testimony were effecting salvation, the whole world would endure this thing and be saved.

**4** But it is in this way that they drew error to themselves.

**5** They do not know that they will destroy themselves. If the Father were to desire a human sacrifice, he would become vainglorious.

**6** For the Son of Man clothed himself with their first-fruits; he went down to Hades and performed many mighty works.

**7** He raised the dead therein; and the world-rulers of darkness became envious of him, for they did not find sin in him.

**8** But he also destroyed their works from among men, so that the lame, the blind, the paralytic, the dumb, and the demon-possessed were granted healing.

**9** And he walked upon the waters of the sea.

**10** For this reason he destroyed his flesh from every sovereign authority and every ruling power.

**11** And he turned them all aside. And he opened a way that leads to the imperishable.

**12** For when he was about to go up, when he was found in the body of flesh, he spent forty days — his incorruptible body, resisting all the principalities.

**13** And the way which they entered is the gate of the truth and knowledge. The way which they took is faith in the unseen.

**14** The truth which they loved is the imitation of the true Christ; for this world is a place of suffering and tribulation.

**15** But this is the true testimony: When man comes to know himself and God who is over the truth, he will be saved, and he will crown himself with the crown of the unfading glory.
