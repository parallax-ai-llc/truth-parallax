## Overview

The Testimony of Truth exemplifies the pattern of internal reform and critique that appears in virtually every religious tradition. The text's rejection of external rituals (baptism) and physical suffering (martyrdom) in favor of interior knowledge parallels the Hindu distinction between karma-kanda (ritual action) and jnana-kanda (knowledge), the Buddhist critique of Brahmanical ritual in favor of meditation and insight, and the Quaker rejection of sacraments in favor of inner light. The serpent-as-revealer narrative resonates with traditions worldwide that invert established religious narratives to expose what they see as deeper truths.
