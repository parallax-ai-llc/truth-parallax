## Critique of Orthodox Practice — Christian Engagement

For Christian readers, this section of Testimony of Truth offers both challenge and opportunity for theological reflection. For Christian readers, the Testimony of Truth presents a sharp challenge on two fronts: it attacks the foundations of orthodox practice (baptism, martyrdom) and it retells the foundational narrative (Genesis) in a way that makes the serpent the hero and God the villain.

The section "Critique of Orthodox Practice" engages with themes that remain central to Christian theology, though it develops them in directions that diverge from creedal orthodoxy. The divergence itself is instructive: by encountering a coherent alternative theology that works with much of the same scriptural and theological raw material, Christian readers can deepen their understanding of their own tradition's distinctive commitments.

The patristic response to Gnostic teachings — from Irenaeus's systematic refutation to Clement of Alexandria's more nuanced engagement — demonstrates that the early church took the Gnostic challenge with profound seriousness.
