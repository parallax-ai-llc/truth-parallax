## Overview

For Christian readers, the Testimony of Truth presents a sharp challenge on two fronts: it attacks the foundations of orthodox practice (baptism, martyrdom) and it retells the foundational narrative (Genesis) in a way that makes the serpent the hero and God the villain. The critique of martyrdom challenges one of early Christianity's most distinctive and powerful claims — that dying for Christ was the supreme imitation of Christ's own sacrifice. The text forces Christian readers to articulate why they value martyrdom and baptism, and whether these practices are essential or accidental to the faith.
