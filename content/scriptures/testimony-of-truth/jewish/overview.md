## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Testimony of Truth one of the most extreme Gnostic inversions of the Genesis narrative. The retelling of the Eden story from the serpent's perspective — in which the serpent is the true revealer and the God who forbade eating from the tree of knowledge is exposed as envious, ignorant, and hostile to human enlightenment — represents a direct attack on the God of Israel. The text's anti-demiurgic reading of Genesis stands at the opposite pole from rabbinic exegesis, which consistently affirms God's wisdom and justice in the creation and governance of the world.
