## Overview

The Testimony of Truth is one of the most provocative texts in the Nag Hammadi library — a fiercely independent Gnostic voice that attacks both the proto-orthodox church and other Gnostic groups. For Gnostic readers, the text represents a radical purist position: true testimony to the truth comes not through water baptism (which the author considers useless), not through martyrdom (which the author regards as foolish), and not through the compromised teachings of other Gnostic schools, but solely through interior renunciation and gnosis. The retelling of Genesis from the serpent's perspective — in which the serpent is the true revealer and the creator God is exposed as envious — represents one of the most daring hermeneutical moves in Gnostic literature.
