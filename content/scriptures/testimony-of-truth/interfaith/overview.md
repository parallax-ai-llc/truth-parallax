## Overview

The Testimony of Truth raises questions about the relationship between external religious practice and interior spiritual transformation that every tradition must address. Does baptism save, or is interior transformation what matters? Is martyrdom noble, or is it a misunderstanding of what God wants? Is the established religious narrative always reliable, or might the 'villain' sometimes be the truth-teller? These questions — uncomfortable in any tradition — point toward the universal tension between institutional religion and prophetic critique.
