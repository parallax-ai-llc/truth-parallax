## Overview

The Testimony of Truth is studied for its evidence of intra-Gnostic polemic and the internal diversity of the Gnostic movement. The text's attacks on Valentinus, Basilides, the Simonians, and other named groups provide historians with a Gnostic insider's perspective on sectarian differences — a valuable complement to the outsider's view provided by the Church Fathers. The text's critique of martyrdom is particularly significant: while the proto-orthodox church was developing a theology of martyrdom as the supreme witness to faith, this text argues that God does not desire human suffering and that dying for one's beliefs is a mark of ignorance rather than courage.
