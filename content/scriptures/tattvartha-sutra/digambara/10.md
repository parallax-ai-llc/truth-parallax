## The Nature of the Liberated Soul

Digambara interpretation of Chapter 10, the shortest yet most profound chapter, describes the state of *moksha* -- the complete and permanent liberation of the soul from all karmic bondage. The liberated soul (*siddha*) manifests its innate infinite qualities without any obstruction.

### The Four Infinities

Upon liberation, the soul manifests four infinite qualities: *ananta jnana* (infinite knowledge), *ananta darshana* (infinite perception), *ananta sukha* (infinite bliss), and *ananta virya* (infinite energy). Digambara interpreters, following Kundakunda, emphasize that these qualities are not newly acquired but are the soul's own nature, now fully revealed.

### The Siddha's Location

According to Digambara cosmology, liberated souls rise to the apex of the universe (*siddha-shila*), where they exist eternally in a state of bodiless, omniscient perfection. Pujyapada explains that the upward movement is the soul's natural tendency once freed from the weight of karmic matter, just as a gourd naturally rises when released underwater.

### Liberation as Irreversible

Digambara tradition emphatically affirms that liberation, once attained, is permanent and irreversible. The liberated soul will never again be subject to rebirth or karmic bondage. This distinguishes the Jain concept of moksha from certain Hindu concepts of cyclical creation and dissolution that might imply a return to bondage.
