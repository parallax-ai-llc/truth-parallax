## The Realms of Suffering

Digambara interpretation of Chapter 3 presents a detailed and sobering account of the hell realms (*naraka*) that constitute the lowest regions of the Jain cosmos. These descriptions serve both a cosmological and a soteriological purpose: they map the structure of the universe while motivating practitioners to pursue the path of liberation with renewed urgency.

### Seven Hells

The Tattvartha Sutra describes seven progressively severe hell realms, each characterized by increasing darkness, suffering, and duration of existence. Digambara commentators, particularly Pujyapada, provide elaborate descriptions of the conditions in each realm, including the types of suffering inflicted by the hellish beings (*narakis*) upon one another.

### Karmic Causes of Hellish Birth

Digambara interpreters connect the chapter's cosmological descriptions to the doctrine of karma: beings are born in hell realms as a result of intense passions (*kashayas*), particularly anger, cruelty, and deceit. The severity of hellish birth corresponds precisely to the intensity and duration of harmful actions performed in previous lives.

### Motivational Function

While the descriptions of hell realms may appear merely cosmological, Digambara teachers use them as powerful motivational tools. The vivid depiction of suffering serves to generate *samvega* (spiritual urgency) -- the recognition that worldly existence, in all its forms, is ultimately characterized by suffering and that liberation alone offers permanent relief.
