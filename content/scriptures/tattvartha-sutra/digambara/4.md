## Divine Beings in the Jain Cosmos

Digambara interpretation of Chapter 4 treats the celestial realms and their inhabitants with characteristic precision. The *devas* (celestial beings) of Jain cosmology are not gods in the theistic sense but souls whose accumulated merit has produced a temporary sojourn in heavenly realms, from which they will eventually descend.

### Four Orders of Celestial Beings

The chapter describes four categories of divine beings: *Bhavanpati* (residential gods dwelling in the lower world), *Vyantara* (peripatetic gods inhabiting the middle world), *Jyotishka* (luminous gods including sun and moon deities), and *Vaimanika* (celestial gods dwelling in heavenly palaces). Digambara commentators elaborate the characteristics, powers, and lifespans of each category.

### Celestial Life as Impermanent

Digambara interpreters emphasize that even the most exalted celestial existence is impermanent and ultimately unsatisfying. The *devas* enjoy immense pleasures and powers but remain bound by karma. Their eventual fall from celestial status demonstrates the futility of seeking happiness through merit alone, without the pursuit of liberation.

### The Indra System

Digambara tradition elaborates the Tattvartha Sutra's account of the Indras -- the rulers of various celestial assemblies -- who serve as attendants and devotees of the Tirthankaras. Their role as worshippers of the omniscient teachers illustrates that even divine status is inferior to the spiritual attainment of a human being who has realized right faith.
