## The Mechanics of Karmic Influx

Digambara interpretation of Chapter 6 examines *asrava* -- the process by which karmic matter flows into and adheres to the soul. This chapter is crucial for understanding the Jain theory of karma as a physical, material process rather than a merely moral or metaphysical principle.

### Activities as Channels of Influx

The Tattvartha Sutra identifies three types of activity (*yoga*) through which karma enters the soul: mental, verbal, and physical. Digambara commentators, particularly Akalanka, analyze how each type of activity creates vibrations in the soul that attract karmic matter, much as a wet cloth attracts dust particles.

### The Role of Passions

The four passions (*kashayas*) -- anger, pride, deceit, and greed -- are identified as the primary causes of karmic bondage. Digambara interpreters distinguish between intense passions that prevent spiritual progress and mild passions that, while still binding, allow for advancement on the path. The eradication of passions, not merely the cessation of external activity, is the key to stopping karmic influx.

### Auspicious and Inauspicious Karma

While all karma binds the soul, Digambara interpreters distinguish between *punya* (merit-producing karma) and *papa* (demerit-producing karma). However, following Kundakunda's teaching, they emphasize that from the ultimate perspective, both types of karma are forms of bondage -- golden chains and iron chains are equally binding.
