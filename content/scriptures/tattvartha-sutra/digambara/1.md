## The Three Jewels as Foundation

The Digambara tradition reads the opening chapter of the Tattvartha Sutra as establishing the absolute foundation of the spiritual path: *samyag-darshana* (right faith), *samyag-jnana* (right knowledge), and *samyak-charitra* (right conduct). These three jewels (*ratnatraya*) are not merely preparatory stages but constitute the very essence of liberation itself.

### Right Faith in Digambara Understanding

For Digambara interpreters, right faith means unwavering conviction in the seven fundamental categories (*tattvas*) as taught by the omniscient Tirthankaras. This faith is not blind belief but an intuitive recognition of truth that arises when the soul's innate knowledge is partially freed from karmic obstruction. Pujyapada's *Sarvarthasiddhi* emphasizes that right faith must precede all other spiritual attainments.

### The Inseparability of the Three Jewels

Digambara commentators stress that the three jewels are inseparable aspects of a single spiritual reality. Right faith without right knowledge is mere sentiment; right knowledge without right conduct is mere intellectualism; right conduct without right faith and knowledge is mere ritualism. Only their simultaneous cultivation leads to liberation.

### Strict Interpretation of Right Conduct

The Digambara tradition interprets right conduct in its most rigorous form, holding that the complete practice of the five great vows (*mahavratas*) requires total renunciation, including the abandonment of clothing. This strict reading distinguishes the Digambara understanding from the Shvetambara position.
