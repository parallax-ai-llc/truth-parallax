## The Nature of Karmic Bondage

Digambara interpretation of Chapter 8 analyzes *bandha* -- the process by which karmic matter bonds to the soul -- with particular attention to the four aspects of bondage: nature (*prakriti*), duration (*sthiti*), intensity (*anubhava*), and quantity (*pradesha*).

### Eight Types of Karmic Matter

The Tattvartha Sutra classifies karma into eight fundamental types: knowledge-obscuring, perception-obscuring, feeling-producing, delusion-producing, lifespan-determining, body-determining, status-determining, and power-obstructing. Digambara commentators elaborate each type's subtypes, producing a detailed taxonomy of 148 distinct karmic forms.

### The Mechanism of Bondage

Digambara interpreters describe the bondage process using the analogy of heated iron and water: just as hot iron causes water to evaporate upon contact, the soul's passions cause karmic matter to transform and adhere upon contact with the soul's vibrations. The intensity of the passions determines the tightness and duration of the bond.

### Delusion-Producing Karma as Root Cause

Following Kundakunda's emphasis on the primacy of inner transformation, Digambara commentators identify *mohaniya karma* (delusion-producing karma) as the most fundamental type of bondage. It is delusion that sustains the false identification of the soul with the body and worldly conditions, and its destruction is the decisive moment in the path to liberation.
