## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Tattvartha Sutra reads the text as a resource for inter-religious dialogue and mutual understanding. Jainism's long history of coexistence with other Indian traditions, combined with its philosophical commitment to *anekantavada* (the many-sidedness of reality), makes the Tattvartha Sutra a natural starting point for interfaith engagement.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Non-Violence as Common Ground

The Tattvartha Sutra's articulation of *ahimsa* provides a meeting point with traditions worldwide that affirm the sacredness of life. Interfaith interpreters highlight connections with Buddhist compassion, Hindu dharma of non-injury, Christian love ethics, Islamic mercy, and indigenous traditions of ecological harmony.

### Epistemological Humility

The Jain principle that reality is multifaceted and that no single perspective can capture the whole truth offers a philosophical framework for interfaith dialogue. This is not relativism but a disciplined recognition that different traditions may apprehend different aspects of the same reality.

### Shared Soteriological Concerns

The Tattvartha Sutra's path of purification -- removing the accretions that obscure the soul's innate perfection -- resonates with mystical traditions across religions: Christian theosis, Sufi fana, Buddhist buddha-nature, and Hindu self-realization all describe a process of uncovering or returning to an original state of spiritual wholeness.
