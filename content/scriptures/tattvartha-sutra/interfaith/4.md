## The Divine and the Sacred

Interfaith interpretation of Chapter 4 explores how the Jain understanding of celestial beings relates to concepts of divinity, holiness, and spiritual hierarchy in other traditions. The Jain non-theistic framework offers a distinctive perspective that enriches inter-religious dialogue about the nature of the sacred.

### Beyond Theism and Atheism

The Jain treatment of celestial beings challenges the simple theism-atheism dichotomy that often structures Western religious discourse. Jainism acknowledges divine beings but denies a creator God -- a position that interfaith interpreters find helpful for expanding the categories available for inter-religious understanding. The Jain perspective invites dialogue about whether reverence and worship require a theistic framework.

### Devotion Without Dependence

In Jain tradition, the Tirthankaras are revered as perfect beings who have achieved liberation through their own effort, not as gods who created or sustain the world. This model of devotion-without-dependence offers an alternative to both theistic devotionalism and secular humanism, providing interfaith dialogue partners with a third option for understanding the relationship between the human and the sacred.

### Hierarchies of Being

The chapter's systematic classification of celestial beings invites comparison with hierarchical cosmologies in other traditions and raises interfaith questions about the structure of spiritual reality. Whether expressed through angelic orders, buddha-fields, or celestial assemblies, the intuition that reality has a vertical, hierarchically ordered dimension appears across traditions.
