## Common Ground in Spiritual Foundations

Interfaith interpretation of Chapter 1 finds in the Tattvartha Sutra's threefold path a basis for dialogue with practitioners of diverse religious traditions. The emphasis on right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct resonates with foundational teachings across the world's religions while maintaining a distinctively Jain character.

### Faith Across Traditions

The Jain concept of *samyag-darshana* (right faith) provides an entry point for dialogue with traditions that also emphasize faith as foundational: Christian *pistis*, Islamic *iman*, Hindu *shraddha*, and Buddhist *saddha*. Interfaith interpreters note that while the objects of faith differ, the structural role of faith as an enabling condition for spiritual progress is remarkably consistent across traditions.

### Knowledge and Wisdom

The chapter's emphasis on knowledge as essential to liberation connects with the wisdom traditions found in all major religions: Jewish *chokmah*, Christian *gnosis*, Islamic *ilm* and *ma'rifa*, Buddhist *prajna*, and Hindu *vidya*. The Jain commitment to the possibility of complete knowledge invites dialogue about the nature and limits of human understanding.

### Ethics as Universal Concern

The inclusion of right conduct as an inseparable component of the path reflects the universal religious conviction that spiritual transformation must be embodied in ethical behavior. This conviction provides common ground for interfaith cooperation on issues of social justice, environmental stewardship, and the promotion of peace.
