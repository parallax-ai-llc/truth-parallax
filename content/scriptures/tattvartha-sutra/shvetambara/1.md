## The Threefold Path

Shvetambara interpretation of Chapter 1 emphasizes the opening aphorism's declaration that right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct together constitute the path to liberation. Drawing on Umasvati's auto-commentary (*svopajnabhashya*), Shvetambara scholars elaborate the conditions, characteristics, and interrelationships of these three essential components.

### Right Faith and Its Characteristics

Shvetambara commentators, particularly Siddhasena Divakara, describe right faith as possessing several distinctive marks: tranquility (*prasama*), desire for liberation (*samvega*), compassion (*anukampa*), and faith in the teachings (*astikya*). Right faith can arise either spontaneously, through study, or through the instruction of a qualified teacher.

### Five Types of Knowledge

The chapter's classification of knowledge into five types -- *mati* (sensory-intellectual), *shruta* (scriptural), *avadhi* (clairvoyance), *manahparyaya* (telepathy), and *kevala* (omniscience) -- receives detailed treatment. Shvetambara interpreters emphasize that the first two types are available to ordinary beings, while the higher types emerge as karmic obstructions are progressively removed.

### The Agamic Foundation

Shvetambara readers locate the Tattvartha Sutra's teachings within the broader canonical framework of the Shvetambara Agamas, particularly the Sthananga Sutra and the Uttaradhyayana Sutra. The opening chapter is seen as a concise summary of extensive agamic discussions on the nature of faith, knowledge, and conduct.
