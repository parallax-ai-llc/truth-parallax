## The Goal of All Striving

Shvetambara interpretation of Chapter 10 presents *moksha* -- the final and irreversible liberation of the soul from all karmic bondage -- as the supreme goal of Jain religious life. This brief but momentous chapter describes the nature, cause, and characteristics of the liberated state.

### The Path to Liberation

Shvetambara commentators emphasize that liberation is achieved through the simultaneous operation of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct carried to their perfection. When all destructive karma (*ghati karma*) is annihilated, the soul attains *kevala jnana* (omniscience). When the remaining non-destructive karma is exhausted, the soul is completely liberated.

### Characteristics of the Liberated Soul

The *siddha* (liberated soul) possesses infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. Freed from all material encumbrance, the siddha exists in a state of pure consciousness at the summit of the universe. Shvetambara interpreters stress that this state represents the actualization of what was always the soul's true nature.

### Liberation for All

In keeping with the Shvetambara commitment to inclusive soteriology, commentators affirm that liberation is attainable by all souls regardless of gender, caste, or species of birth. The 19th Tirthankara Mallinatha's liberation as a woman (according to Shvetambara tradition) serves as scriptural warrant for this inclusive vision. The path is open to all who cultivate the three jewels with sincerity and perseverance.
