## How Karma Enters the Soul

Shvetambara interpretation of Chapter 6 analyzes *asrava* -- the channels through which karmic matter flows into and attaches itself to the soul. This chapter bridges the metaphysical analysis of the preceding chapters with the practical ethical teachings that follow.

### The Three Channels of Influx

Karmic matter enters the soul through three types of activity (*yoga*): physical, verbal, and mental. Shvetambara commentators explain that every action, word, and thought creates vibrations in the soul that attract compatible karmic particles from the surrounding environment. The soul in its bondage state is never free from these vibrations.

### Passions and Their Gradations

The four fundamental passions -- anger (*krodha*), pride (*mana*), deceit (*maya*), and greed (*lobha*) -- are the primary intensifiers of karmic influx. Shvetambara interpreters, following Haribhadra, classify each passion according to its intensity: the most intense passions completely obstruct spiritual progress, while milder forms allow for gradual advancement on the path.

### Virtuous and Non-Virtuous Influx

Shvetambara commentators distinguish between *punya-asrava* (influx of merit-producing karma) and *papa-asrava* (influx of demerit-producing karma). While both types of karma ultimately bind the soul, meritorious actions produce favorable conditions for spiritual practice in future births, making ethical conduct practically essential even from a transcendent perspective.
