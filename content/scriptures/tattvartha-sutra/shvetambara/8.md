## The Process of Karmic Bondage

Shvetambara interpretation of Chapter 8 examines the mechanics of *bandha* -- how karmic matter bonds to the soul and determines the conditions of future existence. The analysis reveals the precise causal relationships between actions, passions, and their consequences.

### Four Dimensions of Bondage

Shvetambara commentators analyze bondage according to four parameters: *prakriti* (the nature or type of karma bound), *sthiti* (the duration of the bond), *anubhava* (the intensity of the karmic fruit), and *pradesha* (the quantity of karmic matter bound). Each dimension is determined by different aspects of the soul's activities and passions.

### The Eight Fundamental Karma Types

The Tattvartha Sutra classifies karma into eight basic types, which Shvetambara commentators further subdivide into 148 subtypes. The eight main types are: knowledge-obscuring, perception-obscuring, feeling-producing, deluding, lifespan-determining, physique-determining, status-determining, and power-obstructing. Each type specifically affects a particular aspect of the soul's innate nature.

### The Role of Intention

Shvetambara interpreters emphasize that the nature and intensity of karmic bondage depend not only on external actions but critically on the intention (*bhava*) behind them. An action performed with intense passion and deliberation produces heavy, long-lasting karma, while the same action performed inadvertently or with minimal passion produces lighter, shorter-duration karma.
