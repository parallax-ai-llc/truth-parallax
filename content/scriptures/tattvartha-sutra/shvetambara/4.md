## The Celestial Orders

Shvetambara interpretation of Chapter 4 presents the four orders of celestial beings within the framework of Jain cosmology, emphasizing that divine status, however exalted, remains within the cycle of birth and death and is therefore inferior to the pursuit of liberation.

### Classification of Devas

Following the Shvetambara canonical sources, commentators elaborate four classes of divine beings. The *Bhavanpati* gods dwell in palatial residences within the lower world. The *Vyantara* gods roam freely through the middle world. The *Jyotishka* gods constitute the celestial luminaries. The *Vaimanika* gods inhabit heavenly vehicles or palaces in the upper world.

### Powers and Limitations

Shvetambara interpreters detail the extraordinary powers of celestial beings: transformation of form, clairvoyance, supernatural movement, and the enjoyment of exquisite pleasures. However, these powers are always limited and temporary. Even the highest gods cannot attain omniscience while in their celestial state; for this, a human birth is required.

### Celestial Devotion to the Tirthankaras

Shvetambara tradition emphasizes the role of celestial beings as devout worshippers of the Tirthankaras. The elaborate celestial celebrations at the five auspicious events (*kalyanakas*) in each Tirthankara's life -- conception, birth, renunciation, omniscience, and liberation -- are central to Shvetambara liturgical tradition.
