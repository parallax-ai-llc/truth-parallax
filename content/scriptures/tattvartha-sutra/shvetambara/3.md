## The Lower Realms

Shvetambara interpretation of Chapter 3 describes the seven hell realms of the Jain cosmos, using these vivid descriptions both to convey cosmological knowledge and to instill the spiritual urgency (*samvega*) that motivates religious practice.

### Structure of the Hell Realms

The seven hells are arranged vertically below the middle world, each progressively worse than the one above. Shvetambara commentators drawing on the Agamas provide detailed descriptions of the physical conditions -- increasing darkness, extreme temperatures (alternating intense heat and cold), and the absence of any pleasure whatsoever.

### Beings in the Hells

Hell beings (*narakis*) possess five senses and a mind, making their experience of suffering acute and inescapable. Shvetambara sources describe how these beings are tormented both by the environmental conditions and by mutual hostility -- they are driven by intense anger to attack one another continuously, and their bodies regenerate after each destruction.

### Duration of Hellish Existence

The lifespans in each hell realm vary enormously, from a minimum of ten thousand years in the first hell to vast periods measured in *sagaropamas* (ocean-measured time units) in the seventh. Shvetambara interpreters note that these immense durations reflect the intensity of the karmic causes that produce hellish birth, particularly violence committed with deliberation and cruelty.
