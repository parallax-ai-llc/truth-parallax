---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 7"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The shedding of karma is called Nirjara. It is of two kinds: natural shedding and deliberate shedding.

**2** Natural shedding occurs when karma comes to fruition and is exhausted by being experienced. This happens to all beings automatically.

**3** Deliberate shedding occurs when the monk, through the practice of austerities, causes karma to be shed before it comes to fruition. This is the accelerated path to liberation.

**4** External austerities are: fasting, eating less than one's fill, restricting the number of food items, giving up delicacies, sitting in lonely places, and mortifying the body.

**5** Internal austerities are: expiation of sins, reverence for teachers, service to monks, study of the scriptures, meditation, and giving up attachment to the body.

**6** Of all the austerities, meditation is the most powerful, for through meditation the soul directly confronts and destroys its karmic bondage.

**7** The four kinds of meditation are: sorrowful, cruel, righteous, and pure. The first two bind the soul; the last two liberate it.

**8** Righteous meditation consists of the contemplation of the Jina's teaching, the analysis of the nature of reality, the consideration of karmic processes, and the contemplation of the structure of the universe.

**9** Pure meditation consists of contemplating the soul in its pure nature, free from all karmic obstructions. In this meditation, the soul directly perceives itself.

**10** When pure meditation reaches its perfection, all remaining karma is destroyed. The soul attains Kevala Jnana, omniscience, and at the death of the body, it attains final liberation.
