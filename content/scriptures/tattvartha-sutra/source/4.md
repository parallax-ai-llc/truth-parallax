---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 4"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The influx of karma into the soul is caused by the activities of body, speech, and mind, especially when accompanied by the passions.

**2** The passions are of four kinds: anger, pride, deceit, and greed. These are the primary causes of karmic bondage.

**3** There are also quasi-passions: laughter, pleasure, displeasure, sorrow, fear, and disgust. These too cause the influx of karma, though to a lesser degree.

**4** The activities of body, speech, and mind, when performed with passion, attract karmic matter to the soul, as a wet cloth attracts dust.

**5** The intensity of the karmic influx depends on the intensity of the passion. Great passion attracts heavy karma; mild passion attracts light karma.

**6** The type of karma attracted depends on the type of activity. Violence attracts life-shortening karma. Truthfulness attracts knowledge-producing karma.

**7** Yoga — the vibration of the soul through body, speech, and mind — is the channel through which karma flows into the soul.

**8** Yoga is of three kinds: bodily yoga, verbal yoga, and mental yoga. All three cause the influx of karma when accompanied by passion.

**9** Even without passion, yoga causes the influx of karma, but this karma is of a light and easily shed variety.

**10** The understanding of the mechanism of karmic influx is essential for the practitioner who seeks to stop the influx and to free the soul from its accumulated burden.
