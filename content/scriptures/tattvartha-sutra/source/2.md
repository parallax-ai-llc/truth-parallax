---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The soul is the knower, the experiencer, the doer. It is the subject that knows, perceives, and acts.

**2** Souls are of two kinds: worldly and liberated. Worldly souls are bound by karma; liberated souls are free from karma.

**3** Worldly souls are further divided into mobile and immobile. Immobile souls have only one sense — the sense of touch. They include earth-bodied, water-bodied, fire-bodied, air-bodied, and plant-bodied beings.

**4** Mobile souls have two to five senses. Two-sensed beings, such as worms, have touch and taste. Three-sensed beings, such as ants, add the sense of smell.

**5** Four-sensed beings, such as bees, add the sense of sight. Five-sensed beings, such as animals and humans, add the sense of hearing.

**6** Among five-sensed beings, some possess the faculty of reason, and some do not. Humans and some higher animals possess reason.

**7** The soul occupies the same space as the body it inhabits. It expands and contracts as it moves from body to body through the cycle of transmigration.

**8** The essential qualities of the soul are: infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. These are obscured by karma in the worldly state.

**9** When all karma is destroyed, the soul manifests its inherent qualities in full. This is the state of liberation — Moksha.

**10** The liberated soul rises to the summit of the universe, where it abides forever in its infinite perfection, never again to be born, never again to die.
