---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 10"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Thus the seven principles of reality have been described: the soul, the non-soul, the influx of karma, the bondage of karma, the stopping of karmic influx, the shedding of karma, and liberation.

**2** These seven principles, when understood correctly, constitute right knowledge. When believed in sincerely, they constitute right faith. When practised diligently, they constitute right conduct.

**3** The threefold path of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct is the path to liberation. Each supports the others; none can stand alone.

**4** Right faith without right knowledge is blind. Right knowledge without right conduct is barren. Right conduct without right faith is rootless.

**5** When all three are combined and practised together, they form the diamond thunderbolt that shatters the chains of karma and frees the soul from its age-old bondage.

**6** The householder practises this threefold path in a modified form, suited to the conditions of worldly life. The monk practises it in its complete form, suited to the life of total renunciation.

**7** Both the householder and the monk are on the same path. The householder walks more slowly; the monk walks more swiftly. But both are moving toward the same goal.

**8** The goal is Moksha — liberation — the state of infinite perfection that is the natural birthright of every soul.

**9** Let every soul strive toward this goal. Let every soul practise non-violence, truthfulness, and renunciation. Let every soul purify itself of the passions and free itself from the bondage of karma.

**10** This is the essence of the Tattvartha Sutra, the Scripture of All Realities. He who studies it with faith and practises it with devotion attains the supreme goal. Parasparopagraho Jivanam — souls render service to one another. Om, peace to all beings.
