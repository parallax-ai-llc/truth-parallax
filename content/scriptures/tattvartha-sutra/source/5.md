---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 5"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The bondage of karma is determined by four factors: its nature, its duration, its intensity, and its quantity.

**2** The nature of karma determines what type of result it will produce — whether it will obscure knowledge, obstruct perception, produce feeling, or determine the type of body.

**3** The duration of karma determines how long it will remain attached to the soul before bearing fruit. Some karma bears fruit quickly; other karma may remain dormant for aeons.

**4** The intensity of karma determines the strength of its effect when it comes to fruition. Intense karma produces strong effects; mild karma produces gentle effects.

**5** The quantity of karma determines how many karmic particles are attracted at any given moment. The more intense the passion, the greater the quantity.

**6** The bondage of karma is strengthened by the four passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — and by the five senses and their attachment to their objects.

**7** He who understands the nature of karmic bondage and its causes is motivated to practise the restraints that stop the influx of karma and the austerities that shed the karma already accumulated.

**8** The stopping of karmic influx, Samvara, is achieved through the practice of the vows, carefulness, the ten forms of dharma, contemplation, and the conquering of hardships.

**9** The shedding of karma, Nirjara, is achieved through external and internal austerities practised with sincerity and devotion.

**10** When all karma has been stopped and shed, the soul attains liberation. This is the supreme goal of all Jain spiritual practice.
