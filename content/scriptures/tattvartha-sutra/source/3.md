---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 3"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The lower world, the middle world, and the upper world — these three together constitute the universe.

**2** The lower world consists of seven hells, each deeper and more terrible than the one above. The beings there suffer the consequences of their most severe negative karma.

**3** The middle world is the world of humans, animals, and plants. It is the only world in which liberation can be attained, for only here can the threefold path be practised.

**4** The upper world consists of the celestial realms, where the gods dwell. The gods enjoy great pleasures but are not liberated, for they too are bound by karma.

**5** Above the upper world, at the summit of the universe, is Siddha Loka, the abode of the liberated souls. It is not a world but the boundary of all worlds.

**6** The universe is surrounded by empty space, which is infinite. Beyond the universe, there are no souls, no matter, no motion, no rest — only empty space.

**7** The universe has the shape of a cosmic person standing with arms akimbo. The lower world is the lower part, the middle world is the waist, and the upper world is the chest and head.

**8** Time moves in cycles. Each cycle consists of an ascending half and a descending half, each comprising six periods of increasing and decreasing virtue.

**9** We are currently in the fifth period of the descending half — a period of decline in virtue and happiness. The next period will be the sixth and lowest.

**10** After the lowest point is reached, the ascending half begins, and virtue gradually increases again. Thus the cycle of time turns eternally.
