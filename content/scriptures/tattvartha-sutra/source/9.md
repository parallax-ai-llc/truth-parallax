---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 9"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The stopping and shedding of karma, combined with the practice of austerities, constitute the path to liberation.

**2** Liberation, Moksha, is the complete and permanent freedom of the soul from all karmic bondage. It is the natural state of the soul, revealed when all obstructions are removed.

**3** The liberated soul possesses four infinite attributes: infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy.

**4** Infinite knowledge means knowing all things — all objects, all events, all beings, in all times, simultaneously and perfectly, without any error or omission.

**5** Infinite perception means perceiving all things directly, without the intermediary of the senses or the mind. The liberated soul perceives everything as it truly is.

**6** Infinite bliss means experiencing a happiness that is beyond all worldly and celestial pleasures. It is a self-contained, self-sufficient, eternal bliss.

**7** Infinite energy means possessing the power to accomplish anything, though the liberated soul, having no desires, does not exercise this power.

**8** The liberated soul has no body, no senses, no mind, no passions. It exists as pure consciousness, pure knowledge, pure bliss.

**9** It occupies a space slightly smaller than the last body it inhabited. It resides at the summit of the universe, in Siddha Loka, for all eternity.

**10** All liberated souls are equal in their perfection. There is no hierarchy among them. Each is complete in itself, infinite, eternal, and perfect.
