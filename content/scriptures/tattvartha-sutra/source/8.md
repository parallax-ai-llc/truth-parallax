---
title: "Tattvartha Sutra - Chapter 8"
scripture: "tattvartha-sutra"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Bondage is caused by wrong faith, non-restraint, carelessness, the passions, and the activities of body, speech, and mind.

**2** Wrong faith is the lack of belief in the true nature of reality. It is the root cause of all bondage, for without right faith, neither right knowledge nor right conduct can be achieved.

**3** Wrong faith is of five kinds: one-sided belief, perverted belief, doubt, indifference to the teaching, and misidentification of the path.

**4** Right faith is attained by the study of the scriptures, by the guidance of a qualified teacher, by the practice of meditation, and by the grace of one's own spiritual development.

**5** Non-restraint is the failure to observe the vows. He who does not practise non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness is in a state of non-restraint.

**6** Carelessness is the lack of vigilance in one's activities. The careless person harms others unknowingly and attracts karma without awareness.

**7** The passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — are the primary forces that drive the soul into bondage. They are the glue that makes karma adhere to the soul.

**8** When the passions are conquered, karma can no longer bind the soul. The soul becomes like a dry surface that does not attract dust.

**9** The conquest of the passions is achieved gradually, through the practice of the vows, the austerities, and especially through meditation on the pure nature of the soul.

**10** He who has conquered all passions and shed all karma attains the state of the Siddha — the perfected soul — and dwells in eternal freedom and bliss.
