The sixth chapter's teaching on karmic influx is the theoretical foundation for the yogic practitioner's discipline of watchfulness. Every moment of unconscious or passionate activity opens the soul to new karmic influx, making constant mindfulness (apramāda) the essential yogic practice. The practitioner learns to observe the arising of passions — anger, pride, deceit, greed — and to interrupt the chain of reaction before karma can flow in.

The yogic tradition has developed specific practices for each type of karmic influx described in this chapter. Physical practices such as kayotsarga (standing or sitting in motionless meditation) still the body's activity; verbal disciplines including silence (mauna) still the speech; and meditation techniques still the mind. Together these practices systematically close the channels through which karma enters the soul.
