The yogic reading of the fifth chapter on non-living substances provides the framework for understanding the material dimension of spiritual practice. The practitioner's body is composed of pudgala (matter), and the karmic matter that binds the soul is also pudgala. Yogic practice works directly with the material body and the material karma, using physical disciplines — posture, fasting, breath control, and endurance of hardship — to transform the soul's relationship to matter.

The chapter's analysis of the properties of matter informs the yogic practitioner's understanding of how diet, environment, and physical activity affect spiritual progress. The subtle material particles that constitute karma interact with the soul through the channels of bodily, verbal, and mental activity, and yogic discipline aims to refine and ultimately still these channels to prevent further karmic influx.
