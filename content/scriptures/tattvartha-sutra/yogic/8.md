The yogic practitioner reads the eighth chapter's analysis of karmic bondage as a diagnostic tool for spiritual practice. By understanding which types of karma are responsible for specific forms of suffering and limitation, the practitioner can direct ascetic and meditative efforts toward the most critical areas. The identification of deluding karma (mohanīya) as the root of bondage focuses the practitioner's attention on the passions and false beliefs that sustain the karmic cycle.

The chapter's teaching that the intensity and duration of karmic bondage depend on the intensity of the passions accompanying the action provides the rationale for the yogic cultivation of equanimity (samatā). By reducing the force of emotional reactions through meditative training, the practitioner weakens the binding power of karma even before achieving its complete destruction.
