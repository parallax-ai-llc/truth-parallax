The yogic reading of the first chapter focuses on the practical implications of the threefold path for the practitioner's daily discipline. Right faith is understood not merely as intellectual assent to Jain doctrine but as a lived orientation of the whole person toward truth — a stance that manifests in the practitioner's posture, breathing, and mental focus during meditation. Right knowledge arises through the progressive refinement of awareness that yogic practice cultivates.

The chapter's epistemological framework provides the theoretical basis for Jain meditation practice. The five types of knowledge represent stages of consciousness that the yogic practitioner may experience: from ordinary sensory awareness through heightened perceptual clarity to the ultimate state of kevala-jñāna, in which all reality is known simultaneously and directly. Each stage corresponds to specific practices and attainments on the yogic path.
