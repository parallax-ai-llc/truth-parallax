The yogic reading of the tenth chapter understands moksha not as a distant theoretical goal but as the natural culmination of sustained practice. The liberated state described in these ten sūtras — the soul abiding in infinite knowledge, perception, bliss, and energy — is the same state that the yogic practitioner glimpses in moments of deep meditation, though its permanent realization requires the complete destruction of all karma.

The brevity of the chapter reminds the yogic practitioner that liberation, while requiring tremendous effort to achieve, is ultimately simple: the soul returns to its own nature. All the elaborate practices described in the preceding chapters serve only to remove what is foreign to the soul. When the last particle of karma falls away, what remains is not something new but something eternally present — the soul's own infinite consciousness, finally unobscured.
