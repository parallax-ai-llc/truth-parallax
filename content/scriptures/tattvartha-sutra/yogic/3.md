The yogic reading of the cosmographic chapters serves the practitioner's development of vivid awareness of the vastness of saṃsāra. Through meditation on the lower realms — the seven hells with their specific torments — the practitioner generates the revulsion toward worldly existence (saṃvega) that fuels intensive practice. Through meditation on the middle world's geography, the practitioner develops appreciation for the extraordinary rarity and preciousness of human birth in conditions favorable to spiritual practice.

Specific meditation practices (dhyāna) prescribed in the Jain yogic tradition involve systematic visualization of the cosmographic structures described in this chapter. The practitioner mentally traverses the realms of existence, developing both the concentration (dhāraṇā) necessary for advanced meditation and the emotional motivation necessary for sustained ascetic commitment.
