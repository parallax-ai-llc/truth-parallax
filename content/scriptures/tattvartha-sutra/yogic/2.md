The yogic interpretation of the second chapter's teaching on the soul emphasizes the experiential dimension of self-knowledge. The distinction between the soul's inherent nature (characterized by infinite knowledge and bliss) and its current bound condition is not merely a philosophical proposition but a reality to be directly experienced through meditative practice. The practitioner's task is to distinguish consciousness itself from the karmic modifications that obscure it.

The chapter's classification of souls by sensory capacity provides the basis for the yogic practitioner's development of sensitivity to all forms of life. Through practices of mindful movement, careful speech, and mental discipline, the yogic practitioner cultivates an awareness of the living beings present in every environment, extending compassion and care to creatures that ordinary awareness overlooks.
