The yogic interpretation of the seventh chapter reads the five great vows as yogic disciplines rather than merely moral rules. Ahimsa, for example, is practiced not only by avoiding harm to others but by cultivating an inner state of non-violence in which aggressive impulses cease to arise. Each vow has both an external behavioral dimension and an internal meditative dimension that transforms the practitioner's consciousness.

The chapter's detailed prescriptions for careful movement (samiti) — walking carefully to avoid crushing small creatures, speaking carefully to avoid harm through words, eating carefully to avoid unnecessary destruction of life — constitute a form of moving meditation that maintains yogic awareness throughout daily activities. The Jain yogic ideal is not restricted to formal meditation sessions but extends to every moment of embodied existence.
