The Sufi reading of Sahih Muslim, Chapter 8 (Paradise, Hell, and the Day of Judgment) engages with the signs of the end times, the events of the Day of Judgment, and the descriptions of Paradise and Hell. Through the lens of mystical and devotional reading, this section's 22 traditions are examined with attention to the inner dimensions of prophetic guidance and the purification of the heart. The hadiths provide vivid accounts of eschatological events — from the minor and major signs preceding the Hour to the reckoning, the Bridge, and the eternal abodes of reward and punishment.

Sufi masters have drawn on these traditions to illuminate the stations of the spiritual path and the transformative power of prophetic example. In this chapter, the interplay between specific prophetic directives and broader ethical principles provides rich material for Sufi spiritual hermeneutics. Each hadith is situated within the larger architecture of Sahih Muslim's thematic organization, where its placement illuminates connections to related traditions elsewhere in the collection.

For the spiritual seeker, these traditions are not merely historical reports but living springs of guidance. Sufi practitioners approach each hadith as an invitation to deeper self-knowledge and divine intimacy, finding in the Prophet's words and actions a map of the interior journey. The outward rulings contained in these traditions are honored as the necessary vessel for the inward spiritual reality they contain.
