The Sunni reading of Sahih Muslim, Chapter 5 (Marriage, Divorce, and Family Law) engages with the prophetic teachings on marriage, family relations, and domestic life. Through the lens of legal and theological authority, this section's 24 traditions are examined with attention to jurisprudential derivation and creedal affirmation. These traditions cover marital rights and obligations, the treatment of spouses and children, inheritance, and the ethical framework governing intimate and familial relationships.

The four Sunni legal schools have drawn extensively on these traditions to establish normative rulings. In this chapter, the interplay between specific prophetic directives and broader ethical principles provides rich material for orthodox Sunni scholarship. Each hadith is situated within the larger architecture of Sahih Muslim's thematic organization, where its placement illuminates connections to related traditions elsewhere in the collection.

The traditions in this chapter continue to shape living Muslim practice, providing authoritative guidance that believers consult for both personal devotion and communal regulation. Commentators across the centuries have elaborated on these hadiths, producing a layered tradition of interpretation that enriches the contemporary reader's engagement with the prophetic Sunnah.
