## Overview

Sunni Islam regards Sahih Muslim as the second most authentic hadith collection, and many scholars consider its methodology and arrangement superior to all other compilations. Imam Muslim's distinctive approach of gathering all variant chains (*turuq*) of a single hadith in one place has made his collection an indispensable reference for hadith scholars and jurists seeking comprehensive evidence for legal and theological positions.

## Interpretive Approach

Sunni engagement with Sahih Muslim benefits from its systematic organization, which facilitates the study of how a single prophetic tradition was transmitted through multiple channels. This arrangement is particularly valued by scholars of hadith science (*usul al-hadith*), as it allows direct comparison of narrators and wording variations. Imam Muslim's introduction, in which he lays out his methodology and criteria for accepting traditions, is itself regarded as a foundational text in hadith science. The four Sunni legal schools rely extensively on Muslim's collection, especially in areas where it provides traditions not found in al-Bukhari.

## Theological Significance

Sahih Muslim's opening *Kitab al-Iman* (Book of Faith) is especially influential in Sunni theology, providing key traditions that define the nature and branches of faith. For Sunni Muslims, this collection — alongside al-Bukhari — forms the bedrock of the prophetic Sunnah, shaping everything from individual worship to communal governance.
