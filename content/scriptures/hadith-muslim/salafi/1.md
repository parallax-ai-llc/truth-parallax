The Salafi reading of Sahih Muslim, Chapter 1 (Faith and Its Branches) engages with the foundations of belief, the nature of prophetic communication, and the relationship between faith and action. Through the lens of scriptural purism and manhaj, this section's 28 traditions are examined with attention to strict adherence to the apparent meaning and rejection of innovation. These traditions address the most fundamental questions of Islamic theology: What constitutes true faith? How was divine guidance transmitted to humanity? What are the obligations that flow from sincere belief?

Salafi scholars prioritize direct application of these hadiths to practice, seeking to replicate the way of the earliest Muslim generations. In this chapter, the interplay between specific prophetic directives and broader ethical principles provides rich material for Salafi textual literalism. Each hadith is situated within the larger architecture of Sahih Muslim's thematic organization, where its placement illuminates connections to related traditions elsewhere in the collection.

The traditions in this chapter continue to shape living Muslim practice, providing authoritative guidance that believers consult for both personal devotion and communal regulation. Commentators across the centuries have elaborated on these hadiths, producing a layered tradition of interpretation that enriches the contemporary reader's engagement with the prophetic Sunnah.
