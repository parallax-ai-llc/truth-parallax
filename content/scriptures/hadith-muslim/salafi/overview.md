## Overview

Salafi scholars hold Sahih Muslim in the highest esteem as one of the two foundational hadith collections for establishing authentic Islamic practice and belief. They value Muslim's methodological rigor and his clear articulation of selection criteria, which align with the Salafi emphasis on returning to the purest sources of prophetic guidance free from later scholarly accretions.

## Interpretive Approach

The Salafi reading of Sahih Muslim prioritizes the collection's creedal content, particularly the Book of Faith, which contains traditions defining the nature of *iman* (faith) and its relationship to action. Salafi scholars use these hadiths to articulate their theological position that faith comprises belief, speech, and action, and that it increases and decreases. In matters of divine attributes, hadiths in Muslim describing God's attributes are affirmed according to their apparent meaning, consistent with the Salafi principle of *ithbat* (affirmation without likening God to creation).

## Legal and Practical Application

For Salafi practice, Sahih Muslim provides authoritative guidance on worship, social conduct, and governance. The collection's detailed descriptions of the Prophet's prayer, fasting, and pilgrimage serve as the primary reference for establishing correct ritual practice. Salafi scholars also draw on Muslim's political hadiths — such as those on obedience to rulers and the conditions for legitimate authority — to articulate their positions on governance and social order.
