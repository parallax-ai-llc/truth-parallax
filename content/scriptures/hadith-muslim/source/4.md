---
title: "Sahih Muslim - Chapter 4"
scripture: "hadith-muslim"
chapter: 4
---

**1** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Charity does not decrease wealth. No one forgives another except that Allah increases his honor. No one humbles himself for the sake of Allah except that Allah raises him in status."

**2** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Every joint of a person must perform a charity each day the sun rises. To judge justly between two people is a charity. To help a man with his mount, lifting him onto it or hoisting up his belongings onto it, is a charity. A good word is a charity. Every step you take toward prayer is a charity. Removing a harmful thing from the road is a charity."

**3** It is narrated on the authority of Adi ibn Hatim that the Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Protect yourselves from the Fire, even if it is with half a date."

**4** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Dharr that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Your smiling in the face of your brother is charity. Commanding good and forbidding evil is charity. Guiding a person who is lost is charity. Removing stones, thorns, and bones from the road is charity. Pouring water from your bucket into the bucket of your brother is charity."

**5** It is narrated on the authority of Aisha that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When a woman gives in charity from the food of her household, without being extravagant, she will have the reward of what she spent, her husband will have the reward for what he earned, and the storekeeper will have a similar reward. The reward of each of them will not diminish the reward of the others in the least."

**6** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Allah the Exalted says: 'Spend, O son of Adam, and I shall spend on you.'"

**7** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever removes a worldly grief from a believer, Allah will remove from him one of the griefs of the Day of Resurrection. Whoever alleviates the difficulties of a needy person, Allah will alleviate his difficulties in this world and the Hereafter."

**8** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Mas'ud that the Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "When a Muslim spends on his family, seeking the reward of Allah, it is counted as charity for him."
