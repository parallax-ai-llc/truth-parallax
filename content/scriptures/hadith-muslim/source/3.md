---
title: "Sahih Muslim - Chapter 3"
scripture: "hadith-muslim"
chapter: 3
---

**1** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The five daily prayers, and Friday to Friday, and Ramadan to Ramadan, are expiation for the sins committed between them, provided one avoids the major sins."

**2** It is narrated on the authority of Uthman that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever performs the ablution as he has been commanded, and prays as he has been commanded, his previous sins will be forgiven."

**3** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "If there was a river at the door of any one of you, and he bathed in it five times a day, would any dirt remain on him?" They replied: "No dirt would remain on him." He said: "That is the likeness of the five prayers, by which Allah erases sins."

**4** It is narrated on the authority of Jabir that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Between a man and disbelief and paganism is the abandonment of prayer."

**5** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Qatadah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Allah the Exalted has said: 'I have made five times of prayer obligatory on your servants. And I have made a covenant that whoever is mindful of them, I will admit him to Paradise. Whoever is not mindful of them has no covenant from Me.'"

**6** It is narrated on the authority of Aisha that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The two units of prayer before the dawn prayer are better than the world and everything in it."

**7** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The most burdensome prayers for the hypocrites are the night prayer and the dawn prayer. If they knew what reward they contain, they would come to them even if they had to crawl."

**8** It is narrated on the authority of Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Angels come to you in succession by night and by day. They meet at the dawn prayer and the afternoon prayer. Those who spent the night with you ascend, and their Lord asks them, though He knows best: 'In what state did you leave My servants?' They say: 'We left them praying, and we came to them while they were praying.'"
