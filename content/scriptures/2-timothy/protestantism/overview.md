## Overview

2 Timothy is treasured in Protestant tradition as Paul's final testament and as the source of the definitive statement on Scripture's inspiration: "All Scripture is God-breathed" (3:16). This verse has served as a cornerstone for the Protestant doctrine of biblical authority and the principle of *sola Scriptura*.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Inspiration and Authority of Scripture
Protestant theology builds its doctrine of Scripture substantially on 3:16-17. The affirmation that Scripture is "God-breathed" (*theopneustos*) undergirds the Reformation principle that the Bible is the final authority for faith and practice.

### Endurance and Faithfulness
The martial imagery of fighting the good fight (4:7) and the athletic metaphor of finishing the race shape Protestant preaching on perseverance. The letter's summons to endure hardship as a good soldier (2:3) has inspired countless sermons on faithfulness in ministry.

### Succession of Sound Teaching
The charge to entrust the gospel to "faithful men who will be able to teach others also" (2:2) is read by Protestants not as apostolic succession of office but as succession of sound teaching through generations.
