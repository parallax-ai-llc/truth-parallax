## All Scripture Is God-Breathed

Protestant interpretation of 2 Timothy 3 centers on sola Scriptura and the Reformation heritage. The key passages — difficult times in the last days (3:1), all Scripture is God-breathed (3:16), equipped for every good work (3:17) — have been read through the lens of the authority of Scripture and justification by faith, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Luther, calvin, and subsequent reformed and evangelical scholars gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 2 Timothy. The chapter's teaching on all scripture is god-breathed has shaped Protestant understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Protestant scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 2 Timothy 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
