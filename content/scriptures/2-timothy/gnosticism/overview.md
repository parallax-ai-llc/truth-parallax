## Overview

Gnostic engagement with 2 Timothy centers on rival claims to Paul's legacy. The letter warns against those who say "the resurrection has already happened" (2:18) — a teaching attributed to Gnostic interpreters like Hymenaeus and Philetus who understood resurrection as a present spiritual reality rather than a future bodily event.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Resurrection Already Past
The explicit condemnation of those who teach that "the resurrection has already happened" (2:18) targets a view central to many Gnostic systems: that the true resurrection is spiritual awakening, not future bodily resuscitation. The Gnostic *Treatise on the Resurrection* (from the Nag Hammadi library) teaches precisely this view.

### Rival Claims to Paul
Gnostic teachers claimed to possess Paul's secret teachings, transmitted orally to trusted initiates. The Pastoral author's emphasis on guarding the "deposit" and transmitting it through authorized channels (2:2) represents the proto-orthodox response: authentic Pauline teaching is public, institutional, and verifiable.

### Esoteric vs. Exoteric Teaching
Gnostics read Paul's distinction between different levels of teaching (milk vs. solid food; cf. 1 Cor 3:1-2) as evidence that the apostle reserved his deepest insights for spiritual adepts. The Pastoral Epistles' insistence on "sound doctrine" represents the opposing view: truth is public and accessible.
