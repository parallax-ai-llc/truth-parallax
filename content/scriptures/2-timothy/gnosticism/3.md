## All Scripture Is God-Breathed

The catalogue of vices characterizing "the last days" (3:1-5), including lovers of self, lovers of money, proud, arrogant, and those "having the appearance of godliness, but denying its power" (3:5), describes the condition of humanity under the full sway of the archons. These are not moral failings that individuals choose but symptoms of the Demiurge's deficient creation pressing down on souls trapped in matter. The "last days" are not a future period but the entire duration of the material cosmos, which from the Gnostic perspective has always been in a state of crisis and decay since Sophia's fall generated the flawed world.

Those who are "always learning and never able to arrive at a knowledge of the truth" (3:7) represent the psychic class: sincere seekers who accumulate doctrinal information without ever experiencing the transformative flash of gnosis. They remain trapped in the Demiurge's intellectual labyrinth, studying his scriptures according to his rules, never suspecting that the truth lies beyond the entire system. Just as Jannes and Jambres opposed Moses (3:8), the psychic teachers oppose the pneumatic revelation, defending the Demiurge's domain with borrowed magic.

The declaration that "all Scripture is God-breathed" (3:16) is handled by Gnostic interpreters with characteristic subtlety. Scripture is indeed "breathed" by a god, but which god? The Gnostic distinguishes between passages inspired by the transcendent Father, transmitted through prophets who unknowingly channeled the Pleroma's light, and passages reflecting the Demiurge's self-serving agenda. The pneumatic reader uses gnosis as the interpretive key, extracting the hidden divine sparks embedded within texts whose surface meaning serves the archontic order. Scripture is thus "profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction" (3:16) precisely when read at the pneumatic level, decoded to reveal the secret drama of Sophia's fall and the Savior's liberating descent.
