## All Scripture Is God-Breathed

Eastern Orthodoxy interpretation of 2 Timothy 3 centers on patristic commentary and liturgical theology. The key passages — difficult times in the last days (3:1), all Scripture is God-breathed (3:16), equipped for every good work (3:17) — have been read through the lens of theosis, the liturgical life of the Church, and patristic consensus, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

John chrysostom, theodoret of cyrus, and other eastern fathers gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 2 Timothy. The chapter's teaching on all scripture is god-breathed has shaped Eastern Orthodoxy understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern eastern orthodoxy scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 2 Timothy 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
