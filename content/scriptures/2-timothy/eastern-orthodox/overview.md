## Overview

Orthodox interpretation of 2 Timothy emphasizes the transmission of the apostolic deposit through the living Tradition of the Church, the complementary authority of Scripture and Tradition, and Paul's witness as a model for martyrdom and ascetical struggle.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Deposit of Faith
Orthodox theology reads the "good deposit" (1:14) as encompassing the fullness of apostolic teaching — doctrinal, liturgical, and spiritual — entrusted to the Church and preserved through the guidance of the Holy Spirit in the councils and the consensus of the Fathers.

### Scripture Within the Living Tradition
While affirming that "all Scripture is God-breathed" (3:16), Orthodox exegesis insists that Scripture is properly read and interpreted within the Tradition of the Church. The Bible is the Church's book, born from the Church's life and understood through the Church's worship.

### Martyrdom and Ascetical Struggle
Paul's language of fighting, running the race, and receiving the crown (4:7-8) is deeply woven into Orthodox spirituality, shaping the veneration of martyrs and the ascetical theology of the monastic tradition.
