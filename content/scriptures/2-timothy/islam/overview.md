## Overview

Islamic scholarship engages 2 Timothy primarily through the lens of prophetic succession and the nature of divine revelation. The letter's concept of inspired Scripture (3:16) invites comparison with the Islamic doctrine of *wahy* (divine revelation), while its model of teacher-student transmission parallels the *isnad* system in hadith scholarship.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Inspiration and Revelation
Muslim scholars compare the claim that Scripture is "God-breathed" (3:16) with the Islamic understanding of *wahy* — direct divine revelation to prophets. Islam distinguishes between the uncreated, directly dictated Qur'an and the inspired but human-mediated nature of previous scriptures, which Muslims believe have been subject to *tahrif* (textual alteration).

### Chains of Transmission
The instruction to pass teaching through faithful witnesses (2:2) resonates with the Islamic *isnad* system — the chains of transmission through which hadith (prophetic sayings) are authenticated. Both traditions value verifiable transmission of sacred knowledge.

### Suffering for Truth
Paul's willingness to endure suffering for his message (1:8; 2:9; 3:12) aligns with the Islamic concept of *sabr* (patient endurance) and the stories of prophets who suffered rejection, a recurring theme in the Qur'an.
