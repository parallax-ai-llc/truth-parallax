## Overview

Jewish scholarship on 2 Timothy is particularly interested in the letter's understanding of "the sacred writings" (3:15) and their inspiration, since these writings are the Jewish Scriptures. The letter's concept of scriptural authority has implications for understanding how early Christians appropriated Jewish texts.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Sacred Writings
Jewish scholars note that the "sacred writings" Timothy knew from childhood (3:15) are the Jewish Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings. The claim that these are "God-breathed" (3:16) shares common ground with Jewish doctrines of divine inspiration but is deployed in service of a Christological hermeneutic foreign to Judaism.

### Chains of Transmission
The model of passing teaching from master to disciple (2:2) parallels the rabbinic concept of the chain of tradition (*shalshelet ha-kabbalah*) described in Mishnah Avot 1:1, where Torah is transmitted from Moses through an unbroken succession of sages.

### Suffering for Faith
Paul's willingness to suffer for his convictions (1:8, 12; 2:9) resonates with Jewish traditions of martyrdom developed in the Maccabean period and elaborated in rabbinic literature. The concept of sanctifying God's name (*kiddush ha-Shem*) provides a parallel framework.
