## All Scripture Is God-Breathed

Catholic interpretation of 2 Timothy 3 centers on sacred Tradition, the Magisterium, and sacramental theology. The key passages — difficult times in the last days (3:1), all Scripture is God-breathed (3:16), equipped for every good work (3:17) — have been read through the lens of the unity of Scripture and Tradition under the guidance of the Magisterium, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

The church fathers, thomas aquinas, and modern catholic exegetes gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 2 Timothy. The chapter's teaching on all scripture is god-breathed has shaped Catholic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Catholic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 2 Timothy 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
