## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Timothy emphasizes the continuity of apostolic ministry through succession, the complementary roles of Scripture and Tradition, and Paul's example of faithful suffering. The letter's charge to "guard the good deposit" (1:14) is read as referring to the entire deposit of faith — both written and oral.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Scripture and Tradition Together
Catholics read 3:16 alongside 2:2 and 1:13-14: Scripture is inspired and profitable, but it functions within the broader context of apostolic tradition. The "good deposit" entrusted to Timothy includes more than what was written down.

### Apostolic Succession
The chain of teaching from Paul to Timothy to "faithful men" (2:2) is understood as scriptural evidence for apostolic succession — the transmission of authority and teaching from one generation of ordained ministers to the next.

### Suffering and Martyrdom
Paul's willingness to suffer for the gospel and his confidence in the "crown of righteousness" (4:8) have inspired Catholic spirituality of martyrdom and sacrificial ministry throughout the centuries.
