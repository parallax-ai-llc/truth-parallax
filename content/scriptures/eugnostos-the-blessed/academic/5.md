## Humanity and the Divine Order

The final section's treatment of humanity has been examined for its anthropological implications. Unlike many Gnostic texts, Eugnostos does not describe the creation of the material world or the entrapment of divine sparks in matter. The text's cosmological focus — describing the divine realm without narrating a fall — is unusual and may reflect an early stage of Gnostic thought before the development of the full fall-and-redemption narrative.
