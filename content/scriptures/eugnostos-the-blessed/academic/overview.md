## Overview

Eugnostos the Blessed is one of the most important texts for scholarly debates about the origins of Gnosticism. Its complete absence of Christian content — no Christ, no apostles, no salvation history — has led scholars such as Douglas Parrott to argue that it represents a pre-Christian or non-Christian form of Gnostic speculation. The existence of a Christianized version (the Sophia of Jesus Christ) in the same codex allows scholars to study the process of Christianization directly, comparing the two texts passage by passage to identify precisely what was added, modified, or retained.
