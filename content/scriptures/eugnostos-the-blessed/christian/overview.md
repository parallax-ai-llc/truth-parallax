## Overview

For Christian readers, Eugnostos the Blessed raises provocative questions about the relationship between Gnostic and Christian thought. The text's complete absence of Christian content challenges the assumption that Gnosticism was merely a Christian heresy — a parasitic growth on the body of the church. If Eugnostos represents a form of religious speculation that existed independently of Christianity, then the relationship between Gnosticism and Christianity may need to be reconceived as a meeting of two distinct traditions rather than a deviation from one. The text's theology of divine transcendence and emanation also raises questions about Christian doctrines of creation ex nihilo and the incarnation.
