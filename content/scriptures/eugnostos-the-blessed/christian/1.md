## The Ineffable One and the First Father

The opening section's portrait of the Ineffable God invites comparison with Christian apophatic theology — the tradition from Pseudo-Dionysius to Meister Eckhart that insists God transcends all human categories. However, where Christian apophaticism typically affirms that the transcendent God has freely chosen to reveal himself through creation and incarnation, Eugnostos presents a God who is knowable only through the chain of emanations.
