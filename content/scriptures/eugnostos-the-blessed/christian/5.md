## Humanity and the Divine Order

The text's vision of humanity within the divine order resonates with the Christian doctrine of the imago Dei but develops it in a different direction. Where Christianity sees the image of God as a gift conferred on creatures, Eugnostos sees the human as a diminished reflection of the divine androgynous being — a fragment seeking to rejoin its source.
