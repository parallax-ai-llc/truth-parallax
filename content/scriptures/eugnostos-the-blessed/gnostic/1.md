## The Ineffable One and the First Father

The opening section establishes the Gnostic principle of divine ineffability. The highest God cannot be named, described, or comprehended by any created mind. This radical transcendence — going beyond even the categories of 'God' and 'being' — sets the stage for the emanative process through which the unknowable becomes partially knowable through its self-manifestations. The First Father is the first step down from absolute transcendence, the first knowable expression of the Ineffable.
