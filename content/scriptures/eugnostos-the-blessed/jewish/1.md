## The Ineffable One and the First Father

The concept of an ineffable highest God resonates with the Jewish reluctance to pronounce the divine Name (the Tetragrammaton) and with Philo's assertion that God is beyond all human categories. However, the Gnostic development pushes this ineffability further than most Jewish thinkers were willing to go, suggesting that even the title 'God' is inadequate for the highest reality.
