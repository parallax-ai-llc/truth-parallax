## Humanity and the Divine Order

The text's vision of humanity as a reflection of the divine order engages with the Jewish concept of the tzelem Elohim (image of God, Genesis 1:26-27). However, where rabbinic tradition understands the image of God as a gift that confers dignity and responsibility on all human beings, Eugnostos restricts the divine image to the spiritual beings of the Pleroma, of which humanity is a distant reflection.
