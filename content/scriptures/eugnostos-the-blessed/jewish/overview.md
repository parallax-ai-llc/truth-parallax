## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in Eugnostos the Blessed a cosmological system that draws on Jewish concepts of divine transcendence and intermediary figures while developing them in directions that depart from mainstream Jewish theology. The text's radical emphasis on God's ineffability parallels the growing tendency in Second Temple Judaism to describe God as increasingly remote and to interpose intermediary figures (Wisdom, Logos, Metatron) between God and creation. The text may reflect a trajectory within Hellenistic Jewish thought that eventually became distinct from rabbinic Judaism.
