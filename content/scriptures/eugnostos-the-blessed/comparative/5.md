## Humanity and the Divine Order

The text's treatment of humanity as a reflection of the divine order parallels the Hindu concept of the atman as a microcosm of Brahman, the Hermetic maxim 'as above, so below,' and the Jewish concept of humanity as the image of God. The structural correspondence between the human and the divine is one of the most widespread ideas in the history of religion.
