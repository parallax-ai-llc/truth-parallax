## Academic Reading

Academic study of Book 3 — the longest and most philosophically sophisticated section — examines its arguments in relation to the contemporary theological discourse of the late Sasanian and early Islamic periods. Scholars identify engagement with Islamic *kalam* (theology), Mu'tazilite rationalism, and Manichaean dualism in the Denkard's philosophical arguments. The text's emphasis on the compatibility of reason and religion is compared with similar claims in Islamic falsafa and Christian scholasticism.
