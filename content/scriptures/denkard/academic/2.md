## Academic Reading

Scholars examine Book 2's cosmological fragments in relation to other Near Eastern cosmological systems, including Babylonian, Jewish, and Islamic models. The Zoroastrian concept of the multi-layered cosmos — with its heaven of lights, star station, and earth surrounded by the cosmic ocean — is compared with comparable cosmographies in neighboring traditions. The text's Pahlavi vocabulary is analyzed for evidence of both pre-Islamic Zoroastrian concepts and possible post-conquest influences.
