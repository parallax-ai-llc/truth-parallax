## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of Book 7's Zoroaster biography examines it as a hagiographic text that follows conventions shared with other prophetic and saintly biographies in the ancient Near East. Scholars compare the Denkard's account with earlier Avestan references to Zoroaster, the Greek accounts (Pliny, Plutarch), and later Persian traditions, tracing the development of the Zoroaster legend across centuries. The text's historical value — what it may preserve of genuine historical memory — is assessed alongside its literary and theological functions.
