## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of Book 1's surviving fragments examines them for evidence of Zoroastrian creation theology in its late Sasanian form. Scholars compare these fragments with earlier Avestan creation accounts (particularly Yasna 44 and the Bundahishn) to trace the development of Zoroastrian cosmogony over time. The fragments' theological vocabulary is analyzed for evidence of interaction with Manichaean, Christian, and Islamic creation theology.
