## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze Book 4's apologetic arguments as evidence of interreligious polemics in early Islamic Iran. The text's claims for Zoroastrian superiority are examined in relation to Islamic arguments for the finality of Muhammad's revelation, Christian claims about Christ's divinity, and Manichaean assertions about Mani's universal mission. The rhetorical strategies used — appeals to antiquity, rational coherence, and ethical superiority — are compared with apologetic methods in other traditions.
