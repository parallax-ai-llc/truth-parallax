## Zoroastrian Reading

Book 3 is the theological heart of the Denkard — a massive collection of philosophical discourses that address virtually every major question of Zoroastrian theology. Topics include the nature of Ahura Mazda, the origin of evil, the relationship between free will and divine providence, the purpose of creation, the nature of the soul, and the role of reason (*xrad*) in religious understanding. The Zoroastrian reader finds in Book 3 the most systematic articulation of the faith's intellectual foundations — a demonstration that Zoroastrianism is not merely a collection of rituals but a profound philosophical tradition.
