The Zoroastrian reading of the Denkard receives it as the most comprehensive statement of the Good Religion (*Beh Din*) ever compiled — a monumental effort to preserve the theological, philosophical, and practical wisdom of Zoroastrianism at a time when the faith was under existential threat from the Islamic conquest of Iran. For the Zoroastrian community, the Denkard is not merely an academic encyclopedia but a lifeline to the vast body of Avestan knowledge that was largely destroyed during the Arab conquest.

The Denkard's compilers — working under enormous cultural pressure — sought to demonstrate that Zoroastrianism possessed a comprehensive intellectual tradition equal or superior to the theological systems of Islam, Christianity, and other competing faiths. Their work preserves summaries of lost Avestan texts, theological arguments, and practical wisdom that would otherwise have been entirely lost. The Zoroastrian reader approaches the Denkard with reverence for this act of cultural and spiritual preservation.
