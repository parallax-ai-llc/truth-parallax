## Zoroastrian Reading

Book 4's defense of Zoroastrianism's superiority over competing religions reflects the existential situation of the community under Islamic rule. The Zoroastrian reader approaches this book as an act of spiritual resistance — a refusal to accept the claim that the Arab conquest proved the superiority of Islam. The arguments presented — emphasizing Zoroastrianism's antiquity, its rational coherence, its ethical depth, and the prophetic authenticity of Zoroaster — serve as ammunition for the faith's defenders in an era of cultural pressure and forced conversion.
