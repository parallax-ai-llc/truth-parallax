## Jewish Reading

Book 9's interpretive commentary invites Jewish comparison with the midrashic and Talmudic traditions of scriptural interpretation. Both Zoroastrian and Jewish hermeneutical traditions developed methods for extracting meaning from ancient, sometimes obscure sacred texts — methods that include linguistic analysis, allegorical reading, and the derivation of practical law from prophetic revelation. The parallel development of these interpretive traditions reflects the shared challenge of maintaining the relevance of ancient scriptures in changing historical circumstances.
