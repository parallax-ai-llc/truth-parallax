## Jewish Reading

Book 4's apologetic arguments invite Jewish reflection on the shared experience of being a religious minority under Islamic rule. The Zoroastrian community's defense of its faith's antiquity, rationality, and ethical depth parallels Jewish apologetic strategies in similar circumstances. The Jewish reader recognizes in the Denkard's defensive posture a familiar experience of cultural pressure and the determination to preserve religious identity.
