## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader of Book 1's creation fragments encounters a cosmogony that may have influenced Jewish creation theology during the formative Persian period. The Zoroastrian concept of creation as a deliberate act of a good God — undertaken within a framework of cosmic struggle between good and evil — resonates with post-exilic Jewish theology's more developed concepts of divine sovereignty and the angelic-demonic conflict. The relationship between Zoroastrian and Jewish creation theology remains a subject of scholarly debate.
