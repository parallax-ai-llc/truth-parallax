## Jewish Reading

Book 7's biography of Zoroaster invites Jewish comparison with the biblical accounts of Moses — another prophetic figure who received divine revelation, faced opposition, and established a covenantal community. Both traditions describe their founder as a mediator between God and humanity, though the specific character of their revelations and the communities they founded differ significantly. The Jewish reader finds in these parallels evidence of shared Near Eastern prophetic patterns.
