## Jewish Reading

The Jewish reader of Book 3's philosophical theology discovers a tradition of theological reasoning that may have influenced the development of Jewish philosophical theology. The Denkard's emphasis on the compatibility of reason and revelation, on divine justice, and on the moral nature of cosmic order resonates with themes that would later characterize Jewish philosophy from Saadia Gaon to Maimonides — thinkers who, notably, worked within the same Islamic intellectual milieu where Zoroastrian ideas continued to circulate.
