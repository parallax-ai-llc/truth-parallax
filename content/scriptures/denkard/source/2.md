---
title: "Book 2 – On the Superiority of the Good Religion (Fragment)"
chapter: 2
---

**1.** Concerning the superiority of the Good Religion over all other faiths, it is thus declared: the Religion of Ohrmazd is superior because it alone teaches the truth about the nature of the Creator, the nature of creation, and the purpose of existence.

**2.** Other religions err in attributing evil to the Creator, or in denying the existence of the Creator, or in worshipping that which is created rather than the Creator. The Good Religion alone teaches that the Creator is wholly good and that evil comes from a separate source.

**3.** The Good Religion teaches that man has free will, and that through the exercise of his free will he may choose good or evil. This is the foundation of justice.

**4.** If man had no free will, there could be no merit in virtue and no blame in sin. But because man is free to choose, his choices have consequence, and his deeds determine his fate.

**5.** The Good Religion teaches that the soul is immortal and that after death it shall be judged according to its deeds. The righteous shall dwell in paradise, and the wicked shall suffer in hell, until the Renovation when all shall be purified.

**6.** The Good Religion teaches that the material world is good, for it was created by Ohrmazd. The body is not evil; it is the instrument through which the soul performs its duties in the world.

**7.** He who rejects the material world and lives in asceticism beyond what is needful serves the Destructive Spirit, for the Destructive Spirit wishes to diminish the good creation. The Religion teaches the middle way: to enjoy the goods of the world with moderation and to use them in the service of righteousness.

**8.** The Good Religion teaches that marriage and the begetting of children are sacred duties, for through them the army of Ohrmazd is increased and the fight against the Destructive Spirit is sustained.

**9.** The Good Religion teaches charity to the poor, justice to all, protection of the weak, and the nurture of the earth and its creatures. These are the duties of every righteous man.

**10.** In all these things the Good Religion excels, and therefore it is called the best of all religions, given by Ohrmazd himself through his Prophet Zarathushtra.
