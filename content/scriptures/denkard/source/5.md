---
title: "Book 5 – On the Sayings of the Sages"
chapter: 5
---

**1.** Adurbad son of Mahraspand said: "The best possession of man is wisdom. The greatest wealth is contentment. The truest friend is righteousness. The deepest ruin is the lie."

**2.** He said also: "Make the Creator your friend through righteousness, and fear no enemy. Keep your tongue from falsehood, your hands from theft, and your heart from envy."

**3.** "Let your word be trustworthy, your promise firm, your judgment fair. Do not delay in doing good, for the opportunity may not return."

**4.** "In choosing a wife, seek virtue before beauty. In choosing a friend, seek wisdom before wealth. In choosing a king, seek justice before power."

**5.** "The generous man is loved by all; the miser is loved by none. Give freely of what you have, for what you give returns to you tenfold."

**6.** "Do not speak ill of the dead, for they cannot defend themselves. Do not mock the unfortunate, for fortune is fickle. Do not despise the humble, for Ohrmazd loves the humble."

**7.** "He who controls his anger is stronger than he who conquers a city. He who overcomes his greed is richer than a king. He who masters his lust is freer than a bird."

**8.** "Eat with moderation, sleep with moderation, work with diligence, and pray with devotion. This is the way of the righteous life."

**9.** "Do not put off till tomorrow what you can do today. Do not leave to another what you can do yourself. Do not trust to fortune what you can secure by effort."

**10.** "The four best things in the world are: a wise king, a faithful wife, a dutiful son, and a just priest. The four worst things are: a foolish king, a faithless wife, a rebellious son, and a corrupt priest."

**11.** "Fear the judgment of the Creator more than the judgment of men. Men may be deceived, but Ohrmazd sees all and knows all."

**12.** "Keep the sacred fire burning, both the fire in the temple and the fire in your heart. The outward fire is a reminder of the inward fire. He whose inner fire is extinguished is dead, though his body still moves."

**13.** "This too I say: the man who brings happiness to others shall himself find happiness. The man who causes sorrow to others shall himself find sorrow. This is the law of Asha, and it cannot be evaded."
