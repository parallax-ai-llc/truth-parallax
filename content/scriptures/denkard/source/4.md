---
title: "Book 4 – On the Deeds of the Faith"
chapter: 4
---

**1.** On the deeds of the faith it is declared that the Religion was first given by Ohrmazd to Zarathushtra on the bank of the river Daiti, when the Prophet was thirty years old.

**2.** For ten years before this, Zarathushtra had sought the truth, wandering in solitude and praying to Ohrmazd. In his thirtieth year, the archangel Vahman appeared to him in a vision and led him to the presence of Ohrmazd.

**3.** There Ohrmazd revealed to Zarathushtra the whole of the Religion: the nature of the two spirits, the creation of the world, the assault of the Destructive Spirit, the duties of man, and the promise of the Renovation.

**4.** For ten years more, Zarathushtra taught the Religion, but no one accepted it. He was reviled by the Karapans and Kavis, who served the demons. He was driven from his homeland.

**5.** At last he came to the court of King Vishtaspa, and there, through the power of the holy word and through miracles wrought by Ohrmazd, Vishtaspa accepted the Religion.

**6.** After the acceptance of the Religion by Vishtaspa, war broke out with the Turanians, who served the Lie. The wars of the Religion were long and fierce, but in the end the righteous prevailed.

**7.** Zarathushtra lived for seventy-seven years. He was slain by a Turanian, Tur-i Bratarvakhsh, while performing the sacred rites. But the Religion endured, for it was planted in the hearts of the faithful.

**8.** After Zarathushtra, the Religion was upheld by the righteous kings and the faithful priests. Under the Kayanian kings, the Religion prospered and was spread throughout the lands.

**9.** The accursed Alexander, when he invaded Iran, destroyed many copies of the Avesta and burned the fire temples. A great darkness fell upon the Religion.

**10.** But the faithful preserved what they could, carrying the sacred texts in their memories and in hidden places. Under the Arsacids and the Sasanians, the Religion was gradually restored.

**11.** The Sasanian kings gathered the scattered texts and commissioned the priests to compile them into the Denkard and other works, so that the wisdom of the ancients might be preserved for future generations.

**12.** The Religion has survived persecution, invasion, and destruction, because it is the truth of Ohrmazd, and the truth cannot be permanently suppressed.
