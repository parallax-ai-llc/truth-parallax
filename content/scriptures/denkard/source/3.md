---
title: "Book 3 – On Wisdom and the Religion"
chapter: 3
---

**1.** On wisdom it is declared that wisdom is of two kinds: innate wisdom and acquired wisdom. Innate wisdom is that which the Creator placed in man at the beginning. Acquired wisdom is that which man gains through study and experience.

**2.** Innate wisdom is like the seed, and acquired wisdom is like the fruit that grows from the seed. Without innate wisdom, acquired wisdom cannot take root; without acquired wisdom, innate wisdom cannot bear fruit.

**3.** The greatest acquired wisdom is the knowledge of the Religion, for through it man learns the will of the Creator and the purpose of creation.

**4.** Wisdom is the chief of all virtues. From wisdom spring righteousness, courage, temperance, and all other virtues. Without wisdom, a man cannot distinguish good from evil, and all his actions will be in vain.

**5.** The enemies of wisdom are ignorance, pride, and the lie. Ignorance blinds a man to the truth; pride makes him think he knows when he does not; and the lie corrupts the knowledge he has.

**6.** The wise man is humble, for he knows how much he does not know. He is a lover of truth, for truth is the way to wisdom. He is diligent in study, for wisdom does not come to the idle.

**7.** Of acquired wisdom, four kinds are distinguished: that which comes from hearing, that which comes from seeing, that which comes from experience, and that which comes from reasoning. All four are necessary for the complete man.

**8.** The sage who masters the Religion, who understands the nature of the Creator and the creation, who knows the duties of man and the consequences of his deeds — such a sage is the greatest treasure of the community.

**9.** A king without wisdom is like a blind man on a battlefield. A priest without wisdom is like a lamp without oil. A farmer without wisdom is like a field without water.

**10.** Let every man seek wisdom above all other things. Let him study the Religion, consult the wise, observe the world, and reflect upon his experience. Through wisdom he shall walk the path of righteousness and attain to the Best Existence.

**11.** The purpose of philosophy, when it is sound, is to support the Religion. When philosophy contradicts the Religion, the philosophy is in error, for the Religion comes from Ohrmazd, who is the source of all truth.

**12.** Through wisdom the Amesha Spentas govern creation. Through wisdom Zarathushtra received the Religion. Through wisdom the righteous kings ruled with justice. And through wisdom shall the Saoshyants accomplish the Renovation.
