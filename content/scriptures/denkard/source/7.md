---
title: "Book 7 – The Life of Zarathushtra"
chapter: 7
---

**1.** On the life of the Prophet Zarathushtra, son of Pourushaspa, of the Spitama family, it is thus declared in the Religion:

**2.** Before his birth, the glory of Zarathushtra descended from the Endless Light. It passed through the fire, through the sun, through the moon, and through the stars, and it came to the house of his mother Dughdova.

**3.** When Zarathushtra was conceived, the demons trembled, for they knew that the one who would overthrow them had entered the world. They sent Durasarun, the chief of sorcerers, to destroy the child, but he could not prevail.

**4.** When Zarathushtra was born, he laughed. He did not cry as other children cry, but laughed aloud, and the whole creation rejoiced, and the demons fled.

**5.** The Karapans and the sorcerers tried many times to kill the child Zarathushtra. They placed him in a fire, but the fire did not burn him. They placed him among the cattle, but the cattle did not trample him. They placed him among the wolves, but a ewe came and suckled him.

**6.** As a youth, Zarathushtra showed signs of extraordinary wisdom. He debated with the priests and confounded them with his questions. He saw the injustice of the world and longed for the truth.

**7.** At the age of twenty, Zarathushtra left his father's house and wandered in solitude, seeking the truth. For ten years he fasted and prayed and searched.

**8.** In his thirtieth year, at the spring festival, as he went to draw water from the river Daiti, the archangel Vahman appeared to him. Vahman was nine times the height of a man, radiant with light.

**9.** Vahman said: "Who art thou, and what dost thou seek?" Zarathushtra answered: "I am Zarathushtra. I seek righteousness above all things."

**10.** Then Vahman led Zarathushtra to the assembly of the Amesha Spentas, and there, in the presence of Ohrmazd, the Religion was revealed to him in its fullness.

**11.** Zarathushtra received the Religion in seven conferences with Ohrmazd. In each conference, one portion of the Religion was revealed, and at the end of the seventh, the whole Religion was complete.

**12.** Then Zarathushtra returned to the world and began to teach. For ten years no one accepted his teaching. The Karapans opposed him, and the rulers drove him from their lands.

**13.** The demon Ahriman himself came to Zarathushtra and tempted him, saying: "Renounce the Religion of Mazda, and I will give thee the sovereignty of the whole world." But Zarathushtra replied: "I will not renounce the Religion, though thou give me all the world."

**14.** At last Zarathushtra came to the court of King Vishtaspa, and through the power of the sacred word and through the miracles of Ohrmazd — the healing of the king's horse, the overthrow of the sorcerers — Vishtaspa accepted the Religion.

**15.** With the support of Vishtaspa, the Religion was proclaimed throughout the land. Wars were fought against the Turanians who served the Lie, and the righteous prevailed.

**16.** Zarathushtra lived for seventy-seven years and was slain by Tur-i Bratarvakhsh while performing the sacred rites. But his soul ascended to paradise, and the Religion endured through his successors.

**17.** From the seed of Zarathushtra, preserved in the lake Sovbar, shall come the three Saoshyants, who shall accomplish the Renovation and make all things new.
