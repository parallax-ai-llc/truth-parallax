---
title: "Book 6 – On the Precepts of the Ancients"
chapter: 6
---

**1.** The ancients have said: "The noblest calling of man is the pursuit of righteousness. All other callings are subordinate to this one."

**2.** "Let a man practice his calling with diligence and honesty. The priest should teach the Religion faithfully. The warrior should defend the land bravely. The farmer should till the soil industriously. The artisan should practice his craft skillfully."

**3.** "Each calling is necessary, and each is honored by Ohrmazd. No man should despise another's calling, for all together they sustain the world."

**4.** "The duty of the king is to rule with justice, to protect the weak, to punish the wicked, and to promote the Religion. A just king is the glory of his land; an unjust king is its shame."

**5.** "The duty of the priest is to study the Religion, to perform the sacred rites, to teach the faithful, and to counsel the king. A learned priest is the light of his community; an ignorant priest is its darkness."

**6.** "The duty of the warrior is to defend the land against its enemies, to protect the cattle and the farmers, and to maintain order. A brave warrior is the shield of his people; a cowardly warrior is their ruin."

**7.** "The duty of the farmer is to till the earth, to plant the seed, to tend the cattle, and to provide food for all. A diligent farmer is the nourisher of the world; a lazy farmer is a burden upon it."

**8.** "Above all these duties stands the universal duty of every man: to think good thoughts, to speak good words, and to do good deeds. This duty belongs to every calling and every station."

**9.** "Charity is among the highest virtues. Give to the poor from what you have, and Ohrmazd shall repay you. He who withholds from the needy when he has abundance commits a great sin."

**10.** "Marriage is a sacred duty. He who does not marry and beget children fails in his duty to Ohrmazd, for through children the army of righteousness is increased."

**11.** "Keep the sacred fire burning in your home. Offer praise to Ohrmazd at the five watches of the day. Observe the festivals and the fasts. These are the duties of the faithful."

**12.** "In all things seek the counsel of the wise and the learned. Do not rely on your own understanding alone, for the mind of a single man is limited, but the accumulated wisdom of the ancients is vast."
