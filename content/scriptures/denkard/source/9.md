---
title: "Book 9 – On the Glorification of the Religion"
chapter: 9
---

**1.** On the glorification of the Good Religion it is declared that the Religion of Ohrmazd surpasses all other creeds in truth, in righteousness, and in the salvation it offers to mankind.

**2.** The Religion teaches three things above all: think good thoughts, speak good words, do good deeds. In these three commandments the whole of duty is contained.

**3.** He who practices these three things shall cross the Chinvat Bridge with ease, shall dwell in the Best Existence, and at the Renovation shall receive an immortal body.

**4.** The Religion teaches that every soul is judged individually. No man shall bear the sins of another, and no man shall profit from the merits of another. Each shall stand alone before the judgment.

**5.** The Religion teaches that repentance is possible. He who has sinned may repent and make amends. The door of Ohrmazd is never closed to the penitent.

**6.** But repentance must be sincere. He who repents with his mouth but continues in sin gains nothing. True repentance is a change of heart, followed by a change of conduct.

**7.** The Religion teaches the equality of men and women before Ohrmazd. Both have souls, both have free will, both are judged according to their deeds. The duties of women differ from those of men, but the merit of fulfilling those duties is equal.

**8.** The Religion teaches respect for the natural world. The earth, the water, the fire, the plants, and the animals are all creations of Ohrmazd. To pollute the water, to defile the earth, to extinguish the fire, or to harm the beneficent animals is a sin against the Creator.

**9.** The Religion teaches moderation in all things. Excess in eating, in drinking, in sleeping, in working — all these are harmful. The wise man follows the middle path.

**10.** The Religion teaches that the purpose of life is to advance the cause of Ohrmazd by increasing righteousness and diminishing wickedness. Every good deed, however small, contributes to the Renovation. Every evil deed, however small, delays it.

**11.** Therefore let every man and woman of the Good Religion strive each day to add to the sum of goodness in the world. Let them think good thoughts, speak good words, and do good deeds. And in the end, the Renovation shall come, and all things shall be made perfect.

**12.** This is the teaching of the Good Religion, revealed by Ohrmazd to Zarathushtra, preserved by the faithful through all adversity, and destined to endure until the end of time. Blessed is he who hears and obeys.
