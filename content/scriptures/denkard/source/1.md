---
title: "Book 1 – On the Nature of the Good Religion (Fragment)"
chapter: 1
---

**1.** In the name of the Creator Ohrmazd. The Denkard is the compilation of the acts of the Religion, gathered from the scattered texts that survived the destruction wrought by the accursed Alexander and the devastation of the Arabs.

**2.** The knowledge of the Religion is the greatest treasure of mankind. Through this knowledge man comes to understand the Creator, to distinguish good from evil, and to order his life according to righteousness.

**3.** The Religion teaches that Ohrmazd, the Lord of Wisdom, created all things good, and that the Destructive Spirit, Ahriman, created all things evil. Between these two the world is a battleground.

**4.** The wise man chooses the Right and rejects the Lie. Through good thoughts, good words, and good deeds, he advances the cause of Ohrmazd and hastens the Renovation.

**5.** The foolish man chooses the Lie, and through evil thoughts, evil words, and evil deeds, he serves the Destructive Spirit and delays the Renovation.

**6.** The Religion is the guide for all mankind. It teaches the origin of the world, the nature of the Creator, the duties of man, the reward of the righteous, and the punishment of the wicked.

**7.** He who studies the Religion with a pure heart and practices its precepts shall attain to paradise. He who rejects the Religion or corrupts its teachings shall dwell in darkness until the Renovation.

**8.** The compilation of these acts of the Religion was undertaken so that the faithful might have a sure guide, and so that the wisdom of the ancients might not be lost through the passage of time and the hostility of enemies.
