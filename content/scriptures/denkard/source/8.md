---
title: "Book 8 – Contents of the Nasks"
chapter: 8
---

**1.** On the contents of the Nasks, the twenty-one books of the Avesta as revealed by Ohrmazd to Zarathushtra, it is thus declared:

**2.** The twenty-one Nasks are divided into three groups of seven. The first seven are the Gathic Nasks, which contain the words of Zarathushtra himself. The second seven are the Hadha-Manthric Nasks, which contain the laws and rituals. The third seven are the Datic Nasks, which contain the legal and civil codes.

**3.** The first Nask is the Stot Yasht. It contains commentary on the Ahunavar prayer, the most sacred of all prayers, and explanations of the Gathas of Zarathushtra.

**4.** The second Nask is the Sudkar. It treats of the ceremonies and rituals of the Religion, the sacred calendar, the festivals, and the proper manner of worship.

**5.** The third Nask is the Varshtmansar. It explains the conversion of King Vishtaspa and the spread of the Religion, and contains the accounts of the conferences between Zarathushtra and Ohrmazd.

**6.** The fourth Nask is the Bagh. It treats of the spiritual beings, the Amesha Spentas, the Yazads, and the Fravashis, and their functions in the governance of creation.

**7.** The fifth Nask is the Dvazda-Hamast. It treats of the creation of the world, the assault of the Destructive Spirit, and the history of mankind from Gayomard to the Renovation.

**8.** The sixth Nask is the Nadar. It treats of matters of conduct, the duties of various classes, the laws of purity and impurity, and the consequences of sin.

**9.** The seventh Nask is the Pajan. It treats of eschatology — the fate of the soul after death, the Chinvat Bridge, paradise, hell, and the Renovation.

**10.** Of the remaining fourteen Nasks, many have been lost through the destruction wrought by Alexander and the Arab invasion. What survives are fragments and summaries preserved in the Denkard and other compilations.

**11.** The Vendidad alone survives complete among the legal Nasks. It contains the laws of purity, the penalties for sin, and the prescriptions for the ritual cleansing of impurity.

**12.** The Denkard preserves summaries of all twenty-one Nasks, so that even when the original texts are lost, their contents may be known to the faithful.
