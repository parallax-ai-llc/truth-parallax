## Comparative Reading

Comparative study of Book 9's hermeneutical methods places Zoroastrian scriptural interpretation alongside other traditions' exegetical practices: Jewish *pardes* (four levels of interpretation), Christian typological and allegorical reading, Islamic *tafsir* and *ta'wil*, and Hindu *mimamsa*. Each tradition develops sophisticated methods for extracting meaning from sacred texts, and the comparative study of these methods illuminates both shared hermeneutical principles and culturally specific interpretive strategies.
