## Comparative Reading

Comparative analysis of Book 4's apologetic arguments reveals patterns common to religious apologetics across traditions: claims of antiquity, appeals to rational coherence, demonstrations of ethical superiority, and critiques of rival religions' internal contradictions. The Denkard's apologetics are compared with similar works in the Christian (Justin Martyr, Augustine), Islamic (al-Ghazali), Jewish (Judah Halevi), and Hindu (Shankara) traditions.
