## Comparative Reading

Comparative analysis of Book 2's cosmology examines how different traditions map the structure of the universe. The Zoroastrian multi-layered cosmos is compared with the Hebrew *shamayim* (heavens), the Buddhist cosmology of Mount Meru, the Hindu concept of the *lokas* (worlds), and the medieval Christian and Islamic cosmographies. Each tradition's spatial imagination reflects and reinforces its theological convictions.
