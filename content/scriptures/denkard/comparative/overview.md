Comparative study of the Denkard reveals the deep interconnections between Zoroastrian theology and the Abrahamic traditions — connections that run in both directions. The Denkard's theological concepts of ethical monotheism, cosmic dualism, individual judgment, bodily resurrection, and a final cosmic renovation influenced the development of Jewish, Christian, and Islamic eschatology, while the Denkard itself shows evidence of engagement with Islamic and Christian theological arguments.

Comparative scholars examine the Denkard as a key document for understanding the process of interreligious influence and competition in the late antique and early medieval Near East. The text's apologetic arguments — defending Zoroastrian positions against perceived challenges from Islam and Christianity — illuminate the theological fault lines that defined religious discourse in the region.
