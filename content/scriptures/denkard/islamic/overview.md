The Islamic reading of the Denkard engages with Zoroastrian theology as part of the pre-Islamic Persian intellectual heritage that profoundly influenced Islamic civilization. The Denkard was compiled during the early centuries of Islamic rule in Iran, and its theological arguments are often directed against Islamic claims — making it a crucial document for understanding the Zoroastrian-Islamic intellectual encounter.

Islamic scholars recognize that Zoroastrian concepts influenced Islamic thought in areas including eschatology, angelology, the concept of the *Sirat* (bridge of judgment, from the Zoroastrian *Chinvat* bridge), and the organization of paradise and hell. The Denkard's emphasis on the compatibility of religion and reason (*xrad*) anticipates the Islamic philosophical tradition's engagement with the same question, suggesting intellectual continuity across the Sasanian-Islamic transition.
