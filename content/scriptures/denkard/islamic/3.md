## Islamic Reading

The Islamic reader of Book 3's philosophical theology encounters a tradition of rational theological argument that anticipates and may have influenced the Islamic *kalam* tradition. The Denkard's insistence that true religion is compatible with reason, its systematic arguments for God's existence and justice, and its engagement with the problem of evil all find parallels in Islamic theological thought — particularly in the Mu'tazilite school, which emphasized divine justice and human free will.
