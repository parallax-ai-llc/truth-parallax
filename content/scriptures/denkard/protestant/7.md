## Protestant Reading

Book 7's biography of Zoroaster invites Protestant comparison with the Gospel accounts of Jesus. Both narratives describe a divinely chosen figure who receives a revelation, faces opposition from established powers, gathers followers, and establishes a religious community. The Protestant reader approaches these parallels with both interest and discernment, noting genuine similarities while maintaining the theological distinctiveness of the Christian revelation.
