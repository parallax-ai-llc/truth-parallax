## Protestant Reading

Book 8's record of the lost Avesta evokes for the Protestant reader the general principle that God's revelation has been communicated through multiple channels across human history. The loss of the majority of Zoroastrian scripture — and the Denkard's heroic effort to preserve what remained — recalls the Protestant emphasis on *sola scriptura* (scripture alone) and the importance of textual preservation for the life of faith.
