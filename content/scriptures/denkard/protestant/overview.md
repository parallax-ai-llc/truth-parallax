The Protestant reading of the Denkard engages with Zoroastrian theology as a significant context for understanding the development of concepts central to Christian faith — particularly eschatology, angelology, demonology, and the cosmic struggle between good and evil. Protestant scholars since the 19th century have recognized the influence of Zoroastrian ideas on Second Temple Judaism and, through Judaism, on early Christianity.

The Denkard's articulation of concepts such as individual moral responsibility, bodily resurrection, final judgment, and the ultimate triumph of good over evil resonates with Protestant theological convictions while raising important questions about the historical relationship between these traditions. The Protestant reader finds in the Denkard evidence that God's truth was not confined to the biblical tradition alone but found expression in the prophetic vision of Zoroaster as well.
