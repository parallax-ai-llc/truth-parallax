## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader of Book 3's philosophical theology encounters sophisticated arguments for ethical monotheism that predate similar arguments in Christian theology by centuries. The Denkard's insistence on the compatibility of reason and faith, on the reality of free will within a providential framework, and on the moral nature of ultimate reality resonates with Protestant theological convictions while challenging the assumption that these ideas originated solely within the biblical tradition.
