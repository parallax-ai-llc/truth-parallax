## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader of Book 6's wisdom sayings finds moral teachings that resonate with biblical Proverbs and the ethical teaching of Jesus — emphasizing honesty, compassion, moderation, and productive labor while condemning pride, sloth, and injustice. The convergence of Zoroastrian and biblical ethics suggests to the Protestant reader that God's moral truth is accessible beyond the boundaries of special revelation — a principle that Protestant natural theology has long affirmed.
