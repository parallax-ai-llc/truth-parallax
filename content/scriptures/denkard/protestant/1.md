## Protestant Reading

The Protestant reader of Book 1's creation fragments discovers ancient parallels to the biblical doctrine of creation — parallels that predate or are roughly contemporary with the Genesis account. The Zoroastrian concept of creation as a deliberate act of a good God, undertaken in opposition to destructive forces, resonates with the biblical understanding of creation as an act of divine sovereignty and goodness. The question of whether Zoroastrian creation theology influenced the Genesis account — particularly during the Persian period — remains a subject of scholarly and theological interest.
