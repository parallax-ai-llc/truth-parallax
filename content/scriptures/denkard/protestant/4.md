## Protestant Reading

Book 4's apologetic defense of Zoroastrianism invites Protestant reflection on the nature of religious apologetics itself. The Protestant reader recognizes familiar argumentative strategies — appeals to historical priority, rational coherence, and ethical fruit — while noting the irony that these same arguments are deployed against Christianity. The encounter with a tradition that claims priority and superiority provokes deeper reflection on the grounds of Christian faith.
