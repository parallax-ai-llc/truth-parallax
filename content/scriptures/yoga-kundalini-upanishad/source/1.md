---
title: "Yoga Kundalini Upanishad Chapter 1"
scripture: "yoga-kundalini-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Chitta, the mind-stuff, has two causes for its existence: Vasana, the subtle desires, and Prana, the vital breath. When one of these is controlled, the other is also controlled.

**2** Of the two, Prana should be controlled first. Prana is controlled by moderate food, by the practice of Asana (postures), and by Shakti-Chalana, the rousing of the Kundalini.

**3** The wise aspirant should take Sattvic food in moderate quantities. Sattvic food is that which is pure, wholesome, and conducive to spiritual practice. He should eat only enough to fill half the stomach, leaving a quarter for water and a quarter for the movement of air.

**4** The aspirant should practice the Asanas taught by the tradition. Of all Asanas, Padmasana (lotus posture) and Siddhasana (perfect posture) are the most important for meditation and Pranayama.

**5** In Padmasana, the aspirant sits cross-legged, placing the right foot upon the left thigh and the left foot upon the right thigh, with the spine erect and the hands resting upon the knees. This posture steadies the body and calms the mind.

**6** Shakti-Chalana, the rousing of the Kundalini, is achieved through the practice of Pranayama. The Kundalini, the serpent power, lies coiled three and a half times at the base of the spine, in the Muladhara Chakra.

**7** The Kundalini blocks the entrance to the Sushumna Nadi, the central channel. Until the Kundalini is awakened and enters the Sushumna, the aspirant cannot attain the higher states of consciousness.

**8** Pranayama consists of three parts: Puraka, inhalation; Kumbhaka, retention; and Rechaka, exhalation. The ratio should be 1:4:2. If the inhalation is four counts, the retention is sixteen counts, and the exhalation is eight counts.

**9** The aspirant should practice Pranayama seated in a comfortable posture, facing east or north. He should inhale through the left nostril, retain the breath while meditating upon the syllable Om, and exhale through the right nostril.

**10** This practice should be performed four times daily: at dawn, at noon, at sunset, and at midnight. Beginning with a small number of rounds, the aspirant should gradually increase until he can perform eighty rounds at each sitting.

**11** Through the practice of Pranayama, the body becomes light, the appetite increases, the inner sounds are heard, and the health improves. These are the signs of progress in the early stages.

**12** The sage said: "Pranayama is the foundation of all Yoga. Without the control of Prana, no higher practice can succeed. He who masters Pranayama masters the mind, and he who masters the mind attains the state of Samadhi."
