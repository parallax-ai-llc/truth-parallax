---
title: "Yoga Kundalini Upanishad Chapter 2"
scripture: "yoga-kundalini-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Kundalini, when awakened by the practice of Pranayama and Shakti-Chalana, enters the Sushumna Nadi, the central channel of the subtle body, which runs from the base of the spine to the crown of the head.

**2** The Sushumna is the royal road of the Kundalini. Within the Sushumna lies the Vajra Nadi, and within the Vajra lies the Chitrini Nadi, and within the Chitrini lies the Brahma Nadi, fine as a spider's thread. Through this innermost channel, the Kundalini ascends.

**3** The Kundalini passes through six Chakras, the energy centers of the subtle body. The first is Muladhara, located at the base of the spine. It has four petals and is associated with the element of earth. Its seed mantra is Lam.

**4** The second Chakra is Svadhisthana, located at the genital region. It has six petals and is associated with the element of water. Its seed mantra is Vam. When the Kundalini passes through this Chakra, the aspirant conquers desire.

**5** The third Chakra is Manipura, located at the navel. It has ten petals and is associated with the element of fire. Its seed mantra is Ram. When the Kundalini passes through this Chakra, the aspirant conquers hunger, thirst, and fear.

**6** The fourth Chakra is Anahata, located at the heart. It has twelve petals and is associated with the element of air. Its seed mantra is Yam. When the Kundalini passes through this Chakra, the aspirant hears the Anahata Nada, the unstruck sound.

**7** The fifth Chakra is Vishuddha, located at the throat. It has sixteen petals and is associated with the element of space. Its seed mantra is Ham. When the Kundalini passes through this Chakra, the aspirant gains knowledge of the past, present, and future.

**8** The sixth Chakra is Ajna, located between the eyebrows, the seat of the mind. It has two petals. Its seed mantra is Om. When the Kundalini passes through this Chakra, the aspirant attains the state of Savikalpa Samadhi, meditation with form.

**9** Above the six Chakras is the Sahasrara, the thousand-petaled lotus at the crown of the head. This is not a Chakra but the abode of Shiva, pure consciousness. When the Kundalini reaches the Sahasrara, it unites with Shiva, and the aspirant attains Nirvikalpa Samadhi, the formless absorption.

**10** In Nirvikalpa Samadhi, all distinctions between subject and object, between knower and known, between self and God, dissolve. Only pure consciousness remains — infinite, eternal, and blissful. This is the state of liberation.

**11** The aspirant should practice the three Bandhas to facilitate the ascent of the Kundalini: Mula Bandha (the root lock), Uddiyana Bandha (the abdominal lock), and Jalandhara Bandha (the chin lock). These locks seal the Prana within the Sushumna.

**12** The sage said: "The awakening of the Kundalini is the central event of the spiritual life. All practices — asana, pranayama, mantra, meditation — are preparations for this awakening. When the Kundalini rises to the Sahasrara, the journey is complete."
