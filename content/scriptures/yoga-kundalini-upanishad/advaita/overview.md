## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Yoga Kundalini Upanishad contributes to the cumulative Upanishadic teaching that Brahman — the non-dual absolute — is the sole reality. The text's themes of kundalini, chakras, pranayama are read as various approaches to the single truth that the individual self and the universal Brahman are identical.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara's tradition values the Yoga Kundalini Upanishad for its contribution to the *sruti-pramana* (scriptural evidence) supporting non-duality. Its teachings serve as means of generating the discrimination (*viveka*) and detachment (*vairagya*) that prepare the seeker for the direct recognition of Brahman.
