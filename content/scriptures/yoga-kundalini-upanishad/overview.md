## Overview

The Yoga Kundalini Upanishad belongs to the Krishna Yajur Veda and provides one of the most detailed Upanishadic accounts of kundalini yoga. The text systematically describes the awakening of the *kundalini shakti* — the serpent energy coiled at the base of the spine — its ascent through the central channel (*sushumna*) and the six chakras, and the ultimate union of individual consciousness with the supreme Brahman. Its practical, step-by-step approach makes it an essential text for understanding the yogic path of energy transformation.

## Structure

The Yoga Kundalini Upanishad consists of three chapters:

1. **Chapter 1 — Pranayama and Kundalini Awakening**: The breath practices that arouse the dormant kundalini energy
2. **Chapter 2 — The Ascent Through the Chakras**: Kundalini's journey through the six chakras to the crown
3. **Chapter 3 — Samadhi and Liberation**: The union of kundalini with Shiva at the crown and the attainment of liberation

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: Kundalini as the soul's journey upward toward union with the Lord
- **Shaivism**: The union of Shakti (kundalini) with Shiva at the crown — the essence of Shaiva tantra
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Kundalini yoga as a means of purifying the mind for non-dual realization
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: The energy body as God's creation, its mastery as devotional discipline
- **Academic**: Important source for the history of kundalini and chakra systems
- **Comparative**: Subtle energy systems compared across Asian traditions
