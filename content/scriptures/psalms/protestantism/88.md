## The Darkest Prayer

Psalm 88 stands alone in the Psalter as the only psalm that ends without a single note of hope or resolution. From its opening cry — "O LORD, God of my salvation; I cry out day and night before you" (v. 1) — to its devastating final word — "darkness is my closest friend" (v. 18) — the psalm is unrelenting in its depiction of spiritual agony. Protestant interpreters have valued this psalm precisely because of its bleakness. Luther, who knew the depths of spiritual despair, insisted that Psalm 88 belongs in Scripture because it gives voice to experiences that other psalms cannot reach. It validates the suffering of believers who find no light, no answer, and no comfort.

The psalmist describes a living death: "I am counted among those who go down to the pit; I am a man who has no strength, like one set loose among the dead, like the slain that lie in the grave, like those whom you remember no more" (vv. 4-5). The theological tension is agonizing — the sufferer addresses God as "God of my salvation" (v. 1) while simultaneously accusing God of being the cause of his suffering: "Your wrath lies heavy upon me, and you overwhelm me with all your waves" (v. 7). Calvin noted that this paradox — clinging to God while crushed by God — is the essence of the most severe spiritual trials. Faith is not eliminated in such darkness; it is the very thing that makes the darkness so painful.

Protestant Christological interpretation has seen in Psalm 88 a prophetic portrait of Christ's passion, particularly the cry of dereliction on the cross. The psalmist's isolation — "You have caused my companions to shun me; you have made me a horror to them" (v. 8) — mirrors Christ's abandonment by his disciples. The unanswered questions of verses 10-12 — "Do you work wonders for the dead? Do the departed rise up to praise you?" — receive their answer not within the psalm but in the resurrection, where Christ demonstrates that God does indeed work wonders for the dead. The psalm's darkness is real, but it is not the final word of Scripture.
