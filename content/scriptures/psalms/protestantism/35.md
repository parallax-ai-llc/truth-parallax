Psalm 35 is an imprecatory psalm in which David calls upon God to fight against his adversaries. "Contend, LORD, with those who contend with me; fight against those who fight against me" (35:1). Protestant interpretation of imprecatory psalms emphasizes that vengeance belongs to God alone (Romans 12:19) — David surrenders his desire for personal revenge by placing the matter in God's hands.

The psalm reveals the treachery David faced: false witnesses rose against him, repaying his good with evil (35:11-12). When his enemies were sick, David had fasted and prayed for them, yet they showed no reciprocal compassion (35:13-14). Protestant moral theology sees in this contrast the cost of loving one's enemies and the pain of betrayal by those one has served.

Rather than taking justice into his own hands, David appeals to God as righteous judge: "Vindicate me in your righteousness, LORD my God" (35:24). Protestant theology insists this is the proper response to injustice — entrusting oneself to the one who judges justly, as Christ himself modeled (1 Peter 2:23). The psalm teaches that faith does not require passivity before evil but channels the cry for justice toward the only Judge who is perfectly righteous.
