## Confident Prayer for Victory

Psalm 108 is a composite psalm, drawing from Psalm 57:7-11 (vv. 1-5) and Psalm 60:5-12 (vv. 6-13). Its combination of earlier material creates a unified prayer that moves from praise to petition. The psalm opens with resolute confidence: "My heart is steadfast, O God! I will sing and make melody with all my being! Awake, O harp and lyre! I will awake the dawn!" (vv. 1-2). Protestant interpreters have noted the deliberate quality of this praise — the psalmist's heart is "steadfast" (established, firm) before worship begins, suggesting that confident worship flows from a settled trust in God's character rather than from favorable circumstances.

David's praise expands to universal scope: "I will give thanks to you, O LORD, among the peoples; I will sing praises to you among the nations. For your steadfast love is great above the heavens; your faithfulness reaches to the clouds" (vv. 3-4). Calvin emphasized that David's worship is not private but public and missional — he praises God "among the nations," anticipating the Gentile inclusion that the gospel accomplishes. The cosmic dimensions of God's love ("above the heavens") and faithfulness ("to the clouds") assure the worshiper that no human need can exhaust divine resources.

The psalm's petition acknowledges a hard truth: "Give us help against the foe, for vain is the salvation of man!" (v. 12). This confession — that human deliverance is "vain" (empty, worthless) — is the practical expression of sola gratia. Luther preached this verse as a corrective to all forms of self-reliance, whether military, political, or spiritual. The psalm's final declaration rings with confident faith: "With God we shall do valiantly; it is he who will tread down our foes" (v. 13). The paradox is characteristically Protestant: human effort is not eliminated ("we shall do valiantly") but is grounded entirely in divine power ("it is he who will tread down"). Genuine Christian action is always empowered action — vigorous human engagement sustained by sovereign divine strength.
