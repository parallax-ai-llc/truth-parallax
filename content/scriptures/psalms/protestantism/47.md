Psalm 47 is an enthronement psalm celebrating God's universal kingship: "For the LORD Most High is awesome, the great King over all the earth" (47:2). Protestant theology connects this psalm to the ascension of Christ, who was exalted to the right hand of the Father and given authority over all nations.

The psalm's exuberant worship — clapping, shouting, singing praises (47:1, 6-7) — expresses the Reformed conviction that worship should be joyful and whole-hearted. "God has ascended amid shouts of joy, the LORD amid the sounding of trumpets" (47:5). Protestant interpreters often associate this verse with Christ's ascension (Acts 1:9-11), reading the psalm as prophetically describing the exalted Christ taking his throne.

Verse 9 envisions "the nobles of the nations" assembling as "the people of the God of Abraham." Protestant missiology draws on this inclusive vision — God's kingdom transcends ethnic and national boundaries. The Abrahamic covenant's promise that "all peoples on earth will be blessed through you" (Genesis 12:3) finds expression in this psalm's picture of universal worship under the reign of the one true King.
