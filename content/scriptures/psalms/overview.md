## Overview

The Book of Psalms (Hebrew: *Tehillim*, "Praises") is the largest collection of poetry in the Hebrew Bible and the most frequently quoted Old Testament book in the New Testament. Comprising 150 psalms attributed to various authors — primarily David, but also Asaph, the Sons of Korah, Solomon, Moses, and others — the Psalter served as the hymnbook and prayer book of ancient Israel and continues to shape worship across religious traditions.

## Structure

The Psalter is divided into five books, traditionally seen as mirroring the five books of the Torah:

| Book | Psalms | Themes |
|------|--------|--------|
| Book I | 1–41 | Davidic prayers, personal lament and trust |
| Book II | 42–72 | Elohistic psalms, national concerns, Korah/Asaph collections |
| Book III | 73–89 | Asaph collection, crisis of exile, covenant theology |
| Book IV | 90–106 | YHWH reigns, Moses psalm, creation and exodus praise |
| Book V | 107–150 | Pilgrimage, Hallel, Torah psalms, final doxology |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Psalms hold a unique position as a shared devotional text across multiple traditions:

- **Judaism**: Recited daily in liturgy; the Tehillim are central to personal prayer, mourning, and communal worship
- **Christianity**: The backbone of monastic liturgy (Divine Office/Liturgy of the Hours); extensively quoted by Jesus and the apostles
- **Islam**: The Zabur given to Dawud (David) is referenced in the Quran (4:163, 17:55, 21:105)
- **Gnosticism**: Select psalms reinterpreted through the lens of divine spark and cosmic liberation

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Prayer | The full range of human address to God — petition, complaint, thanksgiving |
| Worship | Liturgical praise, processional hymns, and enthronement psalms |
| Lament | Cries of distress both personal and communal, constituting nearly one-third of the Psalter |
| Praise | Declarative and descriptive praise of God's character and deeds |
| Messiah | Royal and messianic psalms pointing to God's anointed king |
| Wisdom | Torah meditation, the two ways, and the problem of theodicy |

## Psalm Types (Form Criticism)

Following Hermann Gunkel's classification:

- **Hymns of Praise**: Psalms 8, 19, 29, 33, 100, 103, 104, 145–150
- **Individual Laments**: Psalms 3, 5, 13, 22, 42–43, 51, 69, 88
- **Communal Laments**: Psalms 44, 60, 74, 79, 80, 137
- **Royal Psalms**: Psalms 2, 18, 20, 21, 45, 72, 89, 110
- **Thanksgiving Psalms**: Psalms 30, 32, 34, 92, 116, 118, 138
- **Wisdom Psalms**: Psalms 1, 37, 49, 73, 112, 119, 127, 128
- **Pilgrimage/Songs of Ascent**: Psalms 120–134
