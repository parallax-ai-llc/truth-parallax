## Overview

Gnostic engagement with the Psalms is distinctive and selective. Rather than reading the Psalter as straightforward praise of the Creator God, Gnostic traditions reinterpret psalm language through their cosmological framework: cries to God become the soul's longing for the Pleroma, enemies are identified with the archons (cosmic rulers), and deliverance means liberation from the material prison. The *Pistis Sophia* — a major Gnostic text — quotes and reinterprets dozens of psalms as the cries of Sophia (Wisdom) trapped in the lower realms.

## Key Gnostic Texts Using the Psalms

### Pistis Sophia (3rd century)
The most extensive Gnostic commentary on the Psalms. Pistis Sophia (Faith-Wisdom) recites psalm verses as she struggles against the archons in the lower aeons. Each psalm quotation is reinterpreted as Sophia's plea for rescue from the Demiurge's realm.

### Odes of Solomon (2nd century)
A collection of 42 hymns with strong Gnostic affinities, modeled on the Psalms but expressing themes of mystical union, spiritual ascent, and divine knowledge. Some scholars classify them as Jewish-Christian; others see clear Gnostic theology.

### Nag Hammadi Psalms
Several Nag Hammadi texts contain psalm-like hymns:
- **The Gospel of Truth**: Lyrical passages echoing psalm themes of seeking and finding
- **The Thunder, Perfect Mind**: A revelation discourse with psalm-like structure
- **The Exegesis on the Soul**: Uses psalm quotations to describe the soul's fall and restoration

## The Gnostic Inversion

| Orthodox Psalm Reading | Gnostic Reinterpretation |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| God the Creator is praised | The true God is beyond the Creator (Demiurge) |
| Enemies are earthly foes | Enemies are the archons and cosmic powers |
| Deliverance is historical | Deliverance is escape from the material world |
| The righteous prosper | The spiritually awakened (*pneumatikoi*) are persecuted by ignorance |
| Zion/Temple is God's dwelling | The true temple is the Pleroma or the inner divine spark |

## Theological Emphases

- **The soul's exile**: Lament psalms express the divine spark's imprisonment in matter
- **Gnosis as salvation**: Wisdom psalms point to salvific knowledge, not moral obedience
- **Sophia's cry**: The feminine divine principle calling out from the depths of the material world
- **Cosmic ascent**: Pilgrimage psalms reinterpreted as the soul's journey through the archonic spheres

## Major Gnostic Interpreters

| Source | Era | Contribution |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| Valentinian school | 2nd century | Allegorical psalm interpretation within Pleroma cosmology |
| Basilides | 2nd century | Psalms as expressions of the soul's cosmic journey |
| *Pistis Sophia* | 3rd century | Systematic Gnostic exegesis of psalm verses |
| Manichaean Psalm-Book | 3rd-4th century | Original psalms modeled on biblical Psalter |
