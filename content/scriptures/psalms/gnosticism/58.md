## Judgment on the Archontic Powers

Psalm 58's confrontation — "Do you indeed decree what is right, you gods?" (v. 1) — is read in Gnostic exegesis as a direct challenge to the archons, the cosmic rulers who claim divine authority but exercise it unjustly. The term "gods" (*elim*) is taken literally: these are the lesser divine beings created by or emanating from the Demiurge, who govern the planetary spheres and impose *heimarmene* (fate) upon humanity. The psalm charges them with failure to administer true justice, because true justice is impossible within a cosmic system built on ignorance and deception.

The description of the wicked as "estranged from the womb, speaking lies from birth" (v. 3) applies, in Gnostic reading, to the archons themselves. The *Apocryphon of John* narrates that the archons were "born" from the Demiurge's ignorant act of creation and have been spreading falsehood since their origin. Their deafness to truth — "like the deaf adder that stops its ear, that will not hear the voice of charmers" (vv. 4-5) — describes their willful refusal to acknowledge the superior deity they dimly perceive but reject.

The psalm's vivid judgments — dissolution like snails, uprooting like thorns (vv. 7-9) — are read as prophecies of the archons' ultimate destruction. Gnostic eschatology teaches that at the consummation of the cosmic drama, the archontic powers will be dissolved and the material cosmos will be unmade, as all divine light returns to the Pleroma. The concluding affirmation — "Surely there is a God who judges on earth" (v. 11) — is heard as a proclamation that the true God, though hidden from the archons, will ultimately judge and dismantle the false order they have imposed.
