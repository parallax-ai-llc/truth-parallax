## The Hidden Light Shining on All Peoples

Psalm 67's prayer that God would "make his face to shine upon us" (v. 1) is read by Gnostic interpreters as a petition for the uncreated light of the Pleroma to penetrate the archontic veil. The "face" of the true God is not the face of the Demiurge, who shows himself through natural phenomena and cultic appearances, but the ineffable radiance of the Father of All, which the *Apocryphon of John* describes as "immeasurable light, pure, holy, immaculate." The prayer for this light to shine "upon us" is the pneumatic community's request for illumination — the saving *gnosis* that reveals their true identity.

The universal scope — "that your way may be known on earth, your saving power among all nations" (v. 2) — aligns with the universalist strand in Gnostic soteriology. While Gnostic systems typically distinguish between hylics, psychics, and pneumatics, the Valentinian tradition in particular held that the Savior's mission extends to all who are capable of receiving *gnosis*. The refrain "Let all the peoples praise you" (vv. 3, 5) envisions a universal awakening that transcends the ethnic boundaries of the Demiurge's tribal religion.

The harvest imagery (v. 6) is given spiritual rather than agricultural meaning: the "increase" of the earth represents the maturation of the divine seeds (*spermatika*) scattered throughout humanity. The Valentinian *parable of the sower* interprets the harvest as the gathering of the pneumatic elect at the consummation, when all the divine sparks that have ripened through *gnosis* are harvested and returned to the Pleroma. The earth's "increase" is thus not material abundance but the growth of spiritual awakening throughout the world.
