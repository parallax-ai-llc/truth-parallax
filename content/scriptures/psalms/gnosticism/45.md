Psalm 45 is a royal wedding psalm that Gnostic theology reads as an allegory of the sacred marriage (*hieros gamos*) — the reunification of the divine pairs (syzygies) within the Pleroma. The king adorned in splendor (45:2-8) represents the Christos or Logos, the divine emanation who descends to unite with his consort. The bride "all glorious within" (45:13) is Sophia — or, in another reading, the Church of pneumatic souls.

The verse "Your throne, O God, will last for ever and ever" (45:6) is understood as addressing the true divine King — not the Demiurge, whose reign is temporary and will end when the Pleroma is restored. The eternal throne belongs to the Christos who rules within the Pleroma, where time as the material world knows it does not exist. His "scepter of justice" (45:6) represents the divine order that the Demiurge's cosmos imperfectly imitates.

The psalm's vision of the bride "led in to the king" with "joy and gladness" (45:15) describes the eschatological moment that Valentinian theology calls the entry into the Bridal Chamber. When all pneumatic sparks have been gathered and united with their angelic counterparts, they will enter the Pleroma in a cosmic wedding celebration. This is the ultimate destiny of the pneumatic — not mere salvation from sin but participation in the divine marriage that restores the original unity of the Godhead.
