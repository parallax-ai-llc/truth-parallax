Psalm 35's plea for divine intervention against enemies — "Contend, LORD, with those who contend with me" (35:1) — is read in Gnostic tradition as the pneumatic soul's cry against the archons and cosmic powers that seek to prevent its ascent to the Pleroma. The "enemies" are not merely human adversaries but the spiritual rulers of the material cosmos who entrap souls in ignorance and subjection.

The psalmist's experience of false witnesses (35:11) and ingratitude (35:12) mirrors the Gnostic narrative of the divine Sophia, who brought forth creation through a misguided act and was then subjected to the hostility of her own offspring. The *Pistis Sophia* describes Sophia's persecution by the archons in terms that echo this psalm: surrounded by hostile powers, betrayed by those she had generated, crying out for deliverance to the Light above.

The petition "Vindicate me in your righteousness" (35:24) expresses the Gnostic confidence that the true God's justice will ultimately prevail over the archons' tyranny. The material cosmos, with its apparent injustices and cruelties, is not the final reality. The *Pleroma* — the fullness of divine truth, beauty, and justice — will be restored, and all that has been scattered and imprisoned will be gathered back into the original unity.
