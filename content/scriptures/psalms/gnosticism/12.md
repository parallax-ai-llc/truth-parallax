Psalm 12's contrast between corrupt speech and pure divine words is read by Gnostic interpreters as a commentary on the relationship between the Demiurge's false teaching and the true God's hidden revelation. The "flattering lips" and "double heart" (12:2) characterize the Demiurge's communications: he tells humanity it is dust and ashes when it is actually divine; he claims to be the only God when the Pleroma stretches infinitely above him; he promises rewards and threatens punishments to maintain control. Every word of the archontic system is a form of deception.

The declaration that God's words are "like silver refined in a furnace, purified seven times" (12:6) is read by Gnostic interpreters as describing the hidden teachings — the secret traditions passed from the risen Christ to select apostles, the esoteric wisdom encoded in texts like the *Gospel of Thomas*, the *Pistis Sophia*, and the various Nag Hammadi treatises. These teachings are "purified" in the sense that they have been stripped of the Demiurge's distortions and reveal the naked truth about the human condition: we are divine sparks trapped in matter, and knowledge alone can liberate us.

The psalm's lament that "the faithful have vanished" (12:1) resonates with the Gnostic self-understanding as a persecuted minority. The pneumatics, who possess the true interpretation of Scripture, are surrounded by psychics and hylics who accept the Demiurge's version of reality. The arrogance described in verse 4 — "With our tongue we will prevail; our lips are with us; who is master over us?" — is the voice of the ecclesiastical authorities who claim interpretive monopoly and suppress Gnostic teaching. Yet the psalm's assurance that God will "arise" (12:5) promises that truth will ultimately prevail over institutional power.
