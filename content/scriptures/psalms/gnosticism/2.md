Psalm 2's depiction of nations raging against the Lord and His Anointed is read by Gnostic interpreters as a drama of cosmic conflict between the archons (rulers of the material realm) and the emissaries of the true God. The "kings of the earth" (2:2) are the archons — the planetary powers that govern the material cosmos and keep the divine sparks imprisoned in flesh. Their conspiracy against the Lord is not rebellion against the Demiurge (who is himself an archon, the chief archon) but against the transcendent God of the Pleroma, whose light threatens to expose the archons' regime of ignorance.

The declaration "You are my son; today I have begotten you" (2:7) is read in some Gnostic systems as the descent of the divine Aeon (Christ or Sophia) into the material realm to awaken the sleeping sparks. The *Apocryphon of John* (NHC II,1) describes how the divine realm sends emissaries to break through the archontic barriers and reach the imprisoned pneumatics. The "begetting" is not a physical or even an eternal generation within the Godhead but the moment of emanation — the sending-forth of a salvific power from the Pleroma into the darkness of the lower world.

The psalm's warning to "kiss the Son" (2:12) and find blessing in refuge is inverted in Gnostic reading: true refuge is not submission to the Demiurge's authority (which the psalm's surface meaning demands) but escape from it. The archons want humanity to worship and serve them, mistaking them for the true God. The Gnostic "kiss" is recognition — the moment when the pneumatic soul recognizes the divine messenger and receives the awakening knowledge that liberates from archontic bondage. The *Gospel of Philip* (NHC II,3) speaks of the "kiss" as a means of spiritual conception, by which the perfect beget one another through knowledge.
