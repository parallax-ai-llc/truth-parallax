## The Divine Council and the Archons

Psalm 82 is among the most significant psalms for Gnostic interpretation. Its depiction of God standing in judgment over "gods" (*elohim*) who have failed in their duties directly parallels the Gnostic myth of the archons — inferior cosmic rulers who govern the material world in ignorance.

## Gnostic Reading

### "God stands in the divine assembly" (82:1)

Gnostic interpreters identified this "divine assembly" with the Pleroma — the fullness of the true spiritual realm — and the "gods" with the archons who were created or emanated in the cosmic fall. The *Apocryphon of John* describes the archons as rulers placed over different spheres of the material cosmos, each ignorant of the true God above them.

### "You are gods, but you shall die like mortals" (82:6-7)

This pronouncement confirmed the Gnostic view that the archons possess a derivative, limited form of divine power but are ultimately destined for dissolution. They are not the true God but pale imitations whose authority is temporary. The *Pistis Sophia* describes the eventual stripping of the archons' power.

### Justice and the Archons' Failure (82:2-4)

The accusation that the "gods" show partiality to the wicked and fail to protect the vulnerable mirrors the Gnostic conviction that the material world is governed by unjust powers. The suffering of the innocent is explained not by divine mystery but by the incompetence and malice of the cosmic rulers.

## Theological Significance

- **Hermeneutical inversion**: The psalm's meaning is reversed through the Gnostic lens
- **Pneumatic knowledge**: True understanding requires spiritual illumination
- **Archontic framework**: Material existence as imprisonment requiring liberation
- **Transcendent hope**: The true God beyond the Demiurge offers salvation through gnosis
