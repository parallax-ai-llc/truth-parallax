Psalm 31's declaration "Into your hands I commit my spirit (*pneuma*)" (31:5) is among the most significant verses for Gnostic Christology, as these are the words attributed to Christ on the cross. In Gnostic interpretation, the crucifixion represents the divine spirit's passage through the deepest point of material existence and its return to the Father. The *pneuma* committed to the Father's hands is the divine spark returning to the Pleroma from which it descended.

The psalm's vivid descriptions of suffering — social isolation, physical decay, being forgotten like the dead (31:10-12) — describe the condition of the pneumatic trapped in the material world. The Gnostic does not romanticize suffering but recognizes it as the inevitable consequence of the divine spark's imprisonment in flesh. The *Nag Hammadi* texts describe the soul's anguish in terms remarkably similar to this psalm.

The confession "My times are in your hands" (31:15) expresses the Gnostic's trust that the true God — not the Demiurge, not fate (*heimarmene*), not the archons — ultimately governs the soul's destiny. While the material world operates according to the deterministic laws imposed by the archons, the pneumatic soul belongs to a higher order. Its "times" — its experiences, its journey, its ultimate return — are in the hands of the transcendent Father.
