## Gnostic Reading of Psalm 106

Gnostic interpreters approached the Psalms as texts containing hidden wisdom (*gnosis*) accessible only to the pneumatic (spiritual) reader. Psalm 106, with its themes of israel's rebellion, was read through the fundamental Gnostic hermeneutical lens: the God of the Hebrew Bible is the Demiurge, and true spiritual meaning lies beneath the surface of the text.

## Key Themes

### The Demiurge and the True God

Where the psalm speaks of God's power, judgment, or sovereignty, Gnostic readers asked a critical question: which God? The Demiurge (Yaldabaoth) who rules the material cosmos, or the true, hidden God of the Pleroma? This hermeneutical suspicion colors every verse, producing readings that invert the psalm's apparent meaning.

### The Pneumatic Reader

The *Valentinian Exposition* (NHC XI,2) and other texts teach that Scripture contains multiple levels of meaning. The hylic (material) reader takes the psalm at face value; the psychic (soul-level) reader grasps its moral teaching; but only the pneumatic reader perceives the hidden Gnostic truth — the drama of the divine spark's descent into matter and its liberation through knowledge.

### The Archontic Context

The themes of confession, rebellion, mercy in this psalm are reframed within the Gnostic cosmic narrative. The material world is a prison created by the archons; human experience within it — whether of suffering, praise, or petition — is shaped by this fundamental captivity. True liberation comes not through the Demiurge's mercy but through *gnosis* of the transcendent God.

## Theological Significance

- **Hermeneutical inversion**: The psalm's meaning is reversed through the Gnostic lens
- **Pneumatic knowledge**: True understanding requires spiritual illumination
- **Archontic framework**: Material existence as imprisonment requiring liberation
- **Transcendent hope**: The true God beyond the Demiurge offers salvation through gnosis
