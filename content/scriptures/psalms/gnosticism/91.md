## Protection Against the Archons

Gnostic readers of Psalm 91 reinterpreted its promises of divine protection as referring to the true God's intervention against the archons and their demonic forces. The "terror of the night," the "pestilence that stalks in darkness," and the "destruction that wastes at noonday" were identified with the hostile spiritual powers that seek to keep the divine spark imprisoned in matter.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Shelter of the Most High (91:1)

The "Most High" is identified not with the Demiurge (who falsely claims supreme status) but with the true, hidden God beyond the material cosmos. To "dwell in the shelter of the Most High" is to possess *gnosis* — the saving knowledge that connects the pneumatic (spiritual) person to the transcendent source.

### Angelic Protection (91:11-12)

Gnostic texts describe luminous beings — aeons, powers of light — who aid the pneumatic soul in its ascent through the archontic spheres after death. The *Pistis Sophia* and the *Books of Jeu* describe passwords and seals that the ascending soul must present to pass each guardian. The angels of Psalm 91 are reread as these liberating powers.

### The Demonic Threat (91:5-6)

The psalm's catalog of dangers — terror, arrows, pestilence, destruction — maps onto the Gnostic conception of the material world as a hostile environment governed by the archons and their demonic servants. The pneumatic person's protection comes not from the Demiurge but from the true God's agents of liberation.

## Theological Significance

- **Hermeneutical inversion**: The psalm's meaning is reversed through the Gnostic lens
- **Pneumatic knowledge**: True understanding requires spiritual illumination
- **Archontic framework**: Material existence as imprisonment requiring liberation
- **Transcendent hope**: The true God beyond the Demiurge offers salvation through gnosis
