Psalm 26 presents the Gnostic with a picture of the pneumatic (spiritual) soul seeking to maintain its purity in a world of material contamination. The psalmist's declaration, "I have led a blameless life; I have trusted in the LORD and have not faltered" (26:1), is read in Gnostic tradition as the voice of the divine spark (*pneuma*) asserting its allegiance to the true God above, rather than the Demiurge who rules the material cosmos.

The ritual of washing hands in innocence (26:6) takes on deeper significance in Gnostic interpretation: it symbolizes the purification of the soul from the contamination of matter (*hyle*). The Gnostic does not merely cleanse the body but awakens to the recognition that the true self is not the body at all but the imprisoned spark of divine light that must be liberated from material entanglement through *gnosis* (knowledge).

The psalmist's refusal to "sit with the deceitful" or "go with hypocrites" (26:4) reflects the Gnostic distinction between those who possess spiritual knowledge and those who remain trapped in ignorance. The Valentinian system classified humanity into three categories — pneumatics (spiritual), psychics (soulish), and hylics (material) — and this psalm's language of separation resonates with the pneumatic's need to maintain spiritual integrity amid the confusion of the lower world.
