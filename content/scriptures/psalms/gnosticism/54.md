## The Hidden Name and Pneumatic Deliverance

Psalm 54's appeal — "O God, save me by your name" (v. 1) — takes on special significance in Gnostic systems where the divine name is a closely guarded mystery. The *Gospel of Truth* teaches that the Father's Name is not an ordinary word but the Son himself — the Name is the means through which the unknowable Father makes himself known. To invoke this Name is to access a power beyond the Demiurge's reach, a saving knowledge that pierces through the archontic veils separating the material world from the Pleroma.

The "strangers" and "ruthless men" who seek the psalmist's life (v. 3) are identified in Gnostic reading with the archons — the hostile cosmic powers who govern the material world and seek to prevent the pneumatic soul from awakening to its true origin. The description that they "do not set God before themselves" is literally true of the archons, who either do not know the true God (in Sethian systems) or actively oppose Him (in Manichaean-influenced systems). The spiritual warfare described in this psalm is the ongoing struggle between the divine spark and the archontic forces that seek to keep it imprisoned in ignorance.

The psalmist's confident declaration — "Behold, God is my helper" (v. 4) — is read as the awakened pneumatic's recognition that the true God acts on behalf of those who possess *gnosis*. The freewill offering promised in verse 6 is not an animal sacrifice (which Gnostics universally rejected as serving the Demiurge) but the offering of knowledge itself — the pneumatic's response to divine liberation is the transmission of *gnosis* to other divine sparks still trapped in ignorance.
