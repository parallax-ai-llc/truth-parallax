Psalm 9's celebration of divine judgment against the nations is reread by Gnostic interpreters as the anticipated overthrow of the archontic powers. The "wonderful deeds" of God (9:1) are not the historical deliverances of Israel but the cosmic acts by which the true God undermines the Demiurge's regime — the sending of Sophia, the descent of the Christ-Aeon, and the progressive awakening of the divine sparks trapped in matter. Each act of *gnosis* — each soul that awakens — is a "wonderful deed" that diminishes the archons' power.

The throne established for judgment (9:7) is read as belonging not to the Demiurge but to the true God, who will ultimately judge the archons themselves. The *Apocalypse of Adam* (NHC V,5) describes a final judgment in which the powers that have kept humanity in bondage are themselves called to account. The Demiurge, who presumptuously sat in judgment over souls, will discover that a higher court exists — one before which his own regime of ignorance and control will be weighed and found wanting.

The psalm's concern for the "oppressed" and "afflicted" (9:9, 12) takes on cosmic significance in Gnostic reading. The oppressed are the divine sparks imprisoned in matter, and the oppressors are the archons who maintain the prison. God as "stronghold for the oppressed" (9:9) is the Pleroma as refuge — the ultimate reality to which the awakened soul escapes when it breaks free of the material cosmos. The cry "Arise, O Lord! Let not man prevail" (9:19) is the pneumatic's prayer for the dissolution of the material order itself — not a prayer within history but a prayer for the end of history and the restoration of all light to its source.
