Psalm 44's communal lament — "You gave us up to be devoured like sheep" (44:11) — takes on cosmic significance in Gnostic reading. The "us" is not merely Israel but all pneumatic souls who have been delivered into the material world by the Demiurge's creative act. The divine sparks were "devoured" — consumed, scattered, and imprisoned in material bodies — through a process the Gnostic myths describe in varying detail.

The psalm's protest of innocence — "All this came upon us, though we had not forgotten you" (44:17) — is the cry of the pneumatic who suffers not because of personal transgression but because of a cosmic disaster: the fall of Sophia, the ignorance of the Demiurge, the scattering of divine light into matter. Gnostic theology insists that the pneumatic's suffering is undeserved — the result of cosmic error, not personal sin.

The demand "Awake, Lord! Why do you sleep?" (44:23) addresses the perceived silence of the transcendent God, who seems absent from the material cosmos. Gnostic theology explains this silence: the true God is not asleep but is genuinely beyond the material world, separated from the pneumatics by the cosmic spheres the archons patrol. The cry to "awake" is really a cry for intervention — for the sending of a Revealer who will penetrate the archonic barriers and bring *gnosis* to the imprisoned sparks.
