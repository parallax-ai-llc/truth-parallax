## The Savior's Descent and Ascent Through the Spheres

Psalm 68 is one of the most theologically significant psalms for Gnostic Christology, particularly verse 18: "You ascended on high, leading a host of captives in your train and receiving gifts among men." Gnostic interpreters read this as a description of the Savior's cosmic journey — first descending through the archontic spheres disguised as an angel of each level, then ascending back to the Pleroma, leading the liberated divine sparks ("captives" freed from matter) and distributing the "gifts" of *gnosis*. The *Ascension of Isaiah* narrates precisely this journey, as does the *Second Treatise of the Great Seth*.

The psalm's opening — "God shall arise, his enemies shall be scattered" (v. 1) — describes the Savior's emergence from the Pleroma, which scatters the archontic forces that have maintained the imprisonment of the divine sparks. The *Trimorphic Protennoia* narrates the Father's voice shattering the archontic heavens, and this psalm's imagery of scattered enemies and melting wax (v. 2) echoes that cosmic disruption. The archons' power, which seemed absolute within the material cosmos, dissolves like wax before the fire of divine truth.

The description of God as "father of the fatherless" (v. 5) takes on special meaning: the divine sparks in matter are "orphans" — separated from their true Father, raised by the false father (the Demiurge), and ignorant of their origin. The Savior's mission is to reveal the true Father to these orphans, reuniting them with the Pleroma from which they were torn. The psalm's procession imagery (vv. 24-25) is read as the triumphant return of the redeemed pneumatics to the Pleroma, a cosmic liturgical procession that surpasses any earthly worship.
