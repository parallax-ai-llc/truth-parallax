Psalm 33's declaration that "by the word of the LORD the heavens were made" (33:6) directly engages Gnostic cosmogony, though the interpretation depends on which "word" is intended. In mainstream Gnostic thought, the visible heavens were made by the Demiurge through his inferior word-power, while the true divine Word — the Logos of the Pleroma — generated the spiritual cosmos (the aeons). The Valentinian tradition distinguished between the Logos who creates within the Pleroma and the Demiurge who shapes material reality in ignorance.

The psalm's claim that "the LORD watches over those who fear him" (33:18) is read as describing the true God's care for the pneumatics — those souls who possess the divine spark and recognize their spiritual parentage. The Demiurge watches the psychics and hylics, who belong to his sphere; but the pneumatics belong to a higher Father whose watchful care ensures their eventual return to the Pleroma.

The conclusion — "no warrior escapes by his great strength" (33:16) — reinforces the Gnostic teaching that material power is ultimately impotent. The archons may wield tremendous force within the material cosmos, but their power cannot prevent the pneumatic soul's liberation. Only *gnosis* — not military might, not wealth, not political authority — can effect genuine salvation. This psalm's critique of earthly power aligns with the Gnostic critique of the entire material order.
