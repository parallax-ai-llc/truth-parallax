Psalm 49's wisdom teaching on death and wealth — "no one can redeem the life of another" (49:7) — is read through the Gnostic lens as exposing the futility of all material solutions to the soul's fundamental problem. Wealth cannot purchase *gnosis*; social status cannot unlock the archonic barriers; physical health cannot substitute for spiritual awakening. Only the direct knowledge of one's divine origin can effect genuine redemption.

The psalm's observation that "the wise die, the foolish and the senseless also perish" (49:10) is understood in Gnostic theology as describing the fate of the body and the psyche — but not the pneuma. The divine spark is not subject to death because it was never truly born into the material world. It descended from the Pleroma and will return there. The death described in this psalm is the death of the material shell, which the Gnostic regards not with fear but with anticipation of liberation.

The hope of verse 15 — "God will redeem me from the realm of the dead" — is the Gnostic hope par excellence: liberation from the realm of death (the material cosmos) into the realm of life (the Pleroma). This redemption is accomplished through *gnosis* — the knowledge that awakens the divine spark and empowers its ascent through the archonic spheres. The psalm's distinction between those who are "redeemed" and those who "perish like beasts" (49:20) corresponds to the Gnostic classification of pneumatics (who possess the spark) and hylics (who do not).
