Psalm 47's enthronement hymn — "God has ascended amid shouts of joy" (47:5) — is read in Gnostic theology as describing the ascent of the divine Revealer back to the Pleroma after completing his mission of awakening. The Christos who descended to bring *gnosis* now ascends through the archonic spheres, triumphantly passing through each barrier that separates the material cosmos from the divine fullness.

The declaration "God is the King of all the earth" (47:7) is interpreted with careful Gnostic nuance. If "God" refers to the Demiurge, then this is a statement about the material world's ruler — true but limited. If it refers to the transcendent God, it is an eschatological proclamation: ultimately, the true God's sovereignty will extend over "all the earth" when the material cosmos is reabsorbed into the Pleroma.

The psalm's universal vision — "the nobles of the nations" gathering (47:9) — anticipates the Gnostic hope that all pneumatic sparks, scattered among the nations and imprisoned in diverse bodies, will ultimately be gathered into one. The unity of the Pleroma, disrupted by the fall of Sophia and the creation of matter, will be restored. Every nation and every people contains pneumatic seeds that will be harvested and returned to the divine fullness.
