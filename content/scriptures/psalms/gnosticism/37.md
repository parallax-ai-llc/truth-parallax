Psalm 37's wisdom on the prosperity of the wicked takes on cosmic dimensions in Gnostic reading. "Do not fret because of those who are evil" (37:1) — in Gnostic terms, the "evil" who prosper are those who serve the Demiurge's system: the archons and their earthly agents who accumulate material power. Their success is real but temporary, confined to the inferior realm that will ultimately be dissolved when the Pleroma is restored.

The promise that "the meek will inherit the land" (37:11) is transformed in Gnostic interpretation: the "land" is not the material earth but the spiritual inheritance of the Pleroma. The "meek" are the pneumatics who do not grasp for worldly power but cultivate the inner *gnosis* that connects them to their divine origin. Their "inheritance" is restoration to the fullness from which they fell — a spiritual estate infinitely more valuable than any earthly territory.

The psalm's assurance that the righteous will "dwell in the land forever" (37:29) is read eschatologically as pointing to the final state of the liberated soul in the Pleroma. In Valentinian eschatology, the pneumatic seeds will eventually be gathered into the divine Bridal Chamber, where they will exist in eternal unity with their angelic counterparts. This is the "forever" that the psalm promises — not perpetuity within the material world but the timeless existence of the Pleroma.
