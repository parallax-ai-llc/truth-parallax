## Divine Protection in Islamic Perspective

Psalm 91's themes of divine refuge and angelic protection resonate with Islamic theology, particularly the concepts of *tawakkul* (trust in God) and the role of angels (*mala'ikah*) in protecting believers. However, Islamic theology reframes these themes within strict *tawhid*.

## Quranic Parallels

### Trust in God (*Tawakkul*)

The Quran commands: "And upon Allah let the believers rely" (Quran 3:122). The psalm's imagery of sheltering under God's wings, while metaphorical, aligns with the Islamic understanding that true safety comes only from Allah. The Muslim does not fear "the terror of the night" (Psalm 91:5) because all events occur by God's decree (*qadar*).

### Angelic Protection

Islam affirms that angels serve as guardians: "For each one are successive angels before and behind him who protect him by the decree of Allah" (Quran 13:11). The psalm's reference to angelic protection (91:11-12) accords with this teaching, though Islamic theology is more specific about the angels' nature and role.

### The Temptation Narrative

The Islamic account of Shaytan's (Satan's) deception differs from the Christian temptation narrative, as Islam does not accept Christ's divinity. However, the broader principle — that sacred texts can be misused to justify spiritual error — is affirmed in Islamic hermeneutics. Scholars warn against *ta'wil* (interpretation) that serves personal desires rather than divine truth.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All themes are read through the lens of God's absolute oneness
- **Prophetic continuity**: The psalm's context within the chain of prophets
- **Quranic authority**: The Quran serves as the criterion for evaluating biblical texts
- **Ethical guidance**: Moral teachings that accord with Islamic values
