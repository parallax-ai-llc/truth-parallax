## God's Sovereign Judgment at the Appointed Hour

Psalm 75's divine declaration — "At the set time that I appoint I will judge with equity" (v. 2) — resonates profoundly with the Quran's teaching on the Hour (*as-Sa'ah*). The exact timing of the Day of Judgment is known to Allah alone: "They ask you about the Hour — when will it occur? Say, 'Its knowledge is only with my Lord. None will reveal its time except Him'" (Surah al-A'raf 7:187). This psalm's insistence that judgment operates on God's timetable, not humanity's, aligns with the Islamic emphasis on *qadr* (divine decree) and the unknowability of the appointed hour.

The warning against pride — "Do not lift up your horn on high" (v. 5) — corresponds to the Quran's sustained critique of *kibr* (arrogance). The Prophet Muhammad said, "No one who has an atom's weight of arrogance in his heart will enter Paradise" (Sahih Muslim). The psalm's teaching that exaltation comes from God alone, "not from the east or from the west" (v. 6), perfectly expresses the Islamic conviction that *'izzah* (honor and power) belongs entirely to Allah: "If anyone seeks honor, all honor belongs to Allah" (Surah Fatir 35:10).

The cup of foaming wine (v. 8) that the wicked must drain to the dregs is a vivid judgment image that finds parallels in the Quran's descriptions of the punishment awaiting the arrogant in the Hereafter. The Quran describes the drink of the inhabitants of Hell: "They will be given to drink boiling water so that it will sever their intestines" (Surah Muhammad 47:15). While the specific imagery differs, the theological principle is identical: those who reject God and persist in arrogance will face inescapable consequences. The psalm's promise that "the horns of the righteous shall be exalted" (v. 10) mirrors the Quranic assurance that the righteous will be honored in the Hereafter with gardens, rivers, and the pleasure of their Lord.
