## Overview

In Islam, the Psalms are identified with the *Zabur* (Arabic: زبور), one of the four revealed scriptures mentioned in the Quran, given to the Prophet Dawud (David), peace be upon him. The Quran states: "And to Dawud We gave the Zabur" (Quran 4:163, 17:55). While Muslims believe the original Zabur was divine revelation, the present Book of Psalms, like the Torah, is considered to have undergone *tahrif* (alteration) and is therefore read critically through the lens of the Quran.

## Quranic References to the Zabur

| Verse | Content |
|-------|---------|

| Quran 17:55 | "And We gave Dawud the Zabur" — context of prophetic hierarchy |
| Quran 21:105 | "And We have already written in the Zabur, after the [previous] mention, that the earth will be inherited by My righteous servants" (cf. Psalm 37:29) |
| Quran 34:10 | God gave Dawud "bounty" — mountains and birds glorified God with him |
| Quran 38:17-20 | Dawud's prayer, his kingdom strengthened, wisdom and decisive speech |

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Tawhid as Criterion
Any psalm suggesting divine plurality, anthropomorphism, or attributes inconsistent with God's absolute transcendence is reinterpreted or considered a corruption of the original text.

### Prophetic Infallibility (*Ismah*)
Psalms attributed to David that express sin, guilt, or moral failure (e.g., Psalm 51's superscription linking it to the Bathsheba episode) are evaluated carefully. Islamic tradition presents Dawud as a righteous prophet who was tested but not as the sinful figure portrayed in 2 Samuel 11.

### Praise and *Dhikr*
The Psalms' emphasis on praising God (*tasbih*, *hamd*) resonates deeply with the Islamic practice of *dhikr* (remembrance of God). The Quran specifically mentions that mountains and birds joined Dawud in glorifying God (Quran 21:79, 34:10, 38:18-19).

## Theological Emphases

- **Dawud as prophet-king**: A model of pious governance who combined prophecy with just rule
- **Cosmic praise**: All creation glorifies God — a theme shared between the Psalms and the Quran
- **Inheritance of the righteous**: Quran 21:105 directly echoes Psalm 37:29
- **Justice and mercy**: God's attributes celebrated in both the Psalms and the Quran's divine names
