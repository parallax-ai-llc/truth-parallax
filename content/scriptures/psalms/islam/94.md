## Islamic Perspective on Psalm 94

Islamic engagement with the Psalms (*Zabur*) is shaped by two foundational principles: the original *Zabur* was genuine revelation given to Prophet Dawud (David), and the present text has undergone *tahrif* (alteration). Muslim scholars therefore approach individual psalms with discernment, affirming what accords with the Quran and Sunnah while noting divergences.

## Quranic Resonances

The themes of god of vengeance found in Psalm 94 echo several Quranic passages. The Quran addresses similar themes within its own theological framework, emphasizing *tawhid* (God's absolute oneness), prophetic guidance, and accountability before God on the Day of Judgment.

## Key Themes in Islamic Reading

### *Tawhid* and Divine Attributes

Where the psalm speaks of God's justice, Islamic theology affirms these attributes within the framework of God's ninety-nine names (*al-asma al-husna*). Every divine attribute — mercy, justice, power, wisdom — is understood as belonging to God alone, without partner or equal.

### Prophetic Context

Muslim scholars relate the psalm's content to the broader prophetic narrative. The Quran presents the prophets — from Adam to Muhammad — as a continuous chain proclaiming the same essential message. The psalm's concerns are thus placed within a wider salvation history culminating in the final revelation.

### Ethical Teaching

The moral teachings implicit in this psalm align with Islamic ethics (*akhlaq*) in many respects. The emphasis on vengeance, oppression resonates with Quranic injunctions and the prophetic Sunnah.

## Theological Significance

- **Tawhid**: All themes are read through the lens of God's absolute oneness
- **Prophetic continuity**: The psalm's context within the chain of prophets
- **Quranic authority**: The Quran serves as the criterion for evaluating biblical texts
- **Ethical guidance**: Moral teachings that accord with Islamic values
