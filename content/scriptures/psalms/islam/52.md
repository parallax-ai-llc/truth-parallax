## Warning Against Reliance on Falsehood

Psalm 52's condemnation of the "mighty man" who "boasts of evil" and trusts in "the abundance of his riches" (vv. 1, 7) aligns with the Quran's repeated warnings against arrogance and materialism. The Quranic figure of Qarun (Korah), who boasted of his wealth and was swallowed by the earth (Surah al-Qasas 28:76-82), exemplifies the same pattern this psalm describes: boastful reliance on worldly power leading to divinely ordained ruin. The psalm's declaration that God will "uproot you from the land of the living" (v. 5) echoes the Quranic principle that the arrogant will be brought low.

The psalm's critique of the destructive tongue — "Your tongue plots destruction, like a sharp razor" (v. 2) — resonates with Islamic ethics of speech. The Prophet Muhammad taught, "Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day, let him speak good or remain silent" (Sahih al-Bukhari). Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) classifies various sins of speech — *gheebah* (backbiting), *buhtaan* (slander), *nameemah* (tale-bearing) — and this psalm's vivid imagery of the tongue as a weapon supports the prophetic teaching that the tongue is among the most dangerous instruments given to human beings.

The contrast between the wicked who trust in riches and the righteous who trust in God's steadfast love (v. 8) expresses the Islamic virtue of *tawakkul* (reliance on Allah). The Quran teaches, "And whoever puts their trust in Allah, He will be sufficient for them" (Surah at-Talaq 65:3). The psalm's image of the righteous as "a green olive tree in the house of God" (v. 8) resonates with the Quranic parable of the blessed tree whose roots are firm and whose branches reach the sky (Surah Ibrahim 14:24-25) — an image of faith rooted in divine truth and bearing fruit through divine grace.
