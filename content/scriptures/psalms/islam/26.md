Psalm 26 presents Dawud (David, peace be upon him) appealing to Allah on the basis of his integrity, a theme that resonates with the Quranic portrait of Dawud as a righteous servant. The Quran describes Dawud as one to whom Allah gave "wisdom and decisive speech" (38:20), and this psalm demonstrates the moral clarity that characterized his prophethood. The appeal "Vindicate me, LORD, for I have led a blameless life" (26:1) reflects not self-righteousness but the *taqwa* (God-consciousness) that defines the truly devout.

The psalmist's declaration, "I wash my hands in innocence" (26:6), parallels the Islamic practice of *wudu* (ritual ablution) before *salah* (prayer). Islam teaches that physical purification symbolizes and facilitates spiritual purification. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said that ablution washes away sins as water washes away dirt (Sahih Muslim), connecting outer cleansing with inner transformation.

The psalm's emphasis on avoiding "the assembly of evildoers" (26:5) aligns with the Islamic principle of choosing righteous companions. The Prophet Muhammad said, "A person is upon the religion of his close friend, so let one of you look at whom he befriends" (Abu Dawud). Islam teaches that moral integrity requires not only personal virtue but careful selection of one's community and associations.
