Psalm 33 celebrates Allah as Creator, declaring "By the word of the LORD the heavens were made" (33:6). This corresponds directly to the Quranic concept of *kun fayakun* — "Be! And it is" (36:82). Allah creates by His command, and the universe exists as an expression of His will. Islamic theology (*kalam*) has extensively discussed the relationship between God's word and creation, affirming that divine speech is an eternal attribute of Allah, not a created phenomenon.

The psalm's affirmation that "the plans of the LORD stand firm forever" (33:11) expresses the Islamic doctrine of *qadr* — Allah's comprehensive knowledge and decree of all events. The Quran states: "Indeed, all things We created with predestination (*qadr*)" (54:49). This is not fatalism but the recognition that Allah's wisdom encompasses all possibilities and that His plan is perfect even when human understanding is limited.

The conclusion — "No king is saved by the size of his army; no warrior escapes by his great strength" (33:16) — powerfully echoes the Quranic account of the Battle of Badr, where a small band of believers defeated a vastly superior force: "It was not you who killed them, but it was Allah who killed them" (8:17). Islamic theology consistently teaches that victory comes from Allah alone, not from material superiority. This psalm reinforces the *tawhid* (monotheistic) principle that all power belongs to Allah.
