## Allah's Knowledge of Hidden Conspiracies

Psalm 64's description of enemies who plot in secret (v. 2) and say "Who can see us?" (v. 5) directly confronts a delusion the Quran addresses repeatedly. Allah is *al-'Alim* (the All-Knowing) and *al-Basir* (the All-Seeing): "He knows what is in every heart" (Surah al-Mulk 67:13). The conspirators' confidence in concealment is itself a form of *kufr* — denial of God's omniscience. The Quran warns: "Do they not know that Allah knows what they conceal and what they declare?" (Surah al-Baqarah 2:77).

The principle of divine reversal — "God shoots his arrow at them" and "their own tongues are turned against them" (vv. 7-8) — resonates with the Quranic concept of *makr* (plotting). The Quran declares, "And they plotted, and Allah plotted, and Allah is the best of plotters" (Surah Ali 'Imran 3:54). The reversal of the wicked's schemes upon themselves is a recurring Quranic theme, illustrated in the story of Pharaoh, whose plot to destroy the male children of Israel led to Moses being raised in his own household (Surah al-Qasas 28:8-9).

The psalm's conclusion — "all mankind fears; they tell what God has brought about" (v. 9) — expresses the Islamic teaching that God's interventions in history serve as *ayat* (signs) for humanity. The Quran constantly invites reflection on God's dealings with past nations as evidence of divine justice and sovereignty. The psalm's call for the righteous to "rejoice in the LORD and take refuge in him" (v. 10) mirrors the Islamic response to witnessing divine justice: *tawba* (turning to God), *shukr* (gratitude), and renewed *taqwa* (God-consciousness).
