## Mizmor le-Todah: A Psalm for the Thank Offering

Psalm 100, titled *Mizmor le-Todah*, is associated with the *todah* (thanksgiving offering) described in Leviticus 7:12-15. Jewish tradition holds that in the messianic era, all sacrifices will cease except the *todah*, making this psalm eternally relevant.

## Key Themes

### The Todah and the Messianic Age

The Midrash Tehillim on this psalm teaches that all forms of prayer will eventually be superseded except thanksgiving. Rabbi Pinchas and Rabbi Levi taught in the name of Rabbi Yochanan: "In the future, all sacrifices will be abolished, but the sacrifice of thanksgiving will never be abolished; all prayers will be abolished, but thanksgiving will never be abolished."

### "All the earth" (100:1)

The universal scope of this psalm — "all the earth" is called to worship — is noted by Jewish commentators as significant. Ibn Ezra observes that this psalm anticipates the eschatological vision of Zechariah 14:9: "The LORD shall be King over all the earth."

### "We are His people" (100:3)

The covenantal identity of Israel is affirmed: "we are His people and the sheep of His pasture." The ketiv-qere variant — the written text reads *lo* ("not") while the reading tradition has *lo* ("his") — is discussed in the Talmud (Kiddushin 30b) as both readings being true: "He made us and not we ourselves" and "we are His." 

## Theological Significance

- **Torah connection**: The psalm resonates with Torah narratives and commandments
- **Rabbinic interpretation**: Multiple layers of meaning in the tradition
- **Liturgical life**: The psalm shapes Jewish daily and festival worship
- **Ethical formation**: Practical guidance for righteous living
