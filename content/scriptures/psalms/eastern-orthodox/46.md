Psalm 46 proclaims God's unshakeable protection with the confidence that characterizes Orthodox theology of the Church. "God is within her, she will not fall" (46:5) — Orthodox ecclesiology reads this as a promise of the Church's indefectibility. Through schisms, persecutions, and the fall of empires, the Orthodox Church has endured because God dwells within her through the Holy Spirit and the Holy Mysteries.

The "river whose streams make glad the city of God" (46:4) is identified by the Church Fathers — Cyril of Jerusalem, Ambrose, and others — with the grace flowing from baptism and the Eucharist. In Orthodox understanding, the sacramental life of the Church is the river that sustains the faithful through every historical upheaval. The baptismal font and the Eucharistic chalice are the streams that make glad the people of God.

"Be still, and know that I am God" (46:10) is the foundational text of the hesychast tradition — the practice of interior silence and contemplative prayer that has defined Orthodox spirituality for a millennium. Hesychasm teaches that true knowledge of God comes not through intellectual speculation but through the quieting of the mind and the descent of awareness into the heart, where God's presence is directly encountered in the Uncreated Light.
