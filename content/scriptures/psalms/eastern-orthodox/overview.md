## Overview

In the Eastern Orthodox tradition, the Psalter (*Psalterion*) is the most frequently used book of the Bible. It pervades every service of the daily cycle, and devout Orthodox Christians are encouraged to read through the entire Psalter weekly — or even daily during Great Lent. The Orthodox Church follows the Septuagint (LXX) numbering and text, which differs from the Hebrew Masoretic numbering used in Protestant Bibles.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Patristic Consensus
Orthodox interpretation is anchored in the Church Fathers:
- **St. Athanasius**: His *Letter to Marcellinus* is the classic Orthodox guide to praying the Psalms, assigning specific psalms to specific spiritual conditions
- **St. Basil the Great**: Homilies on the Psalms emphasizing their cosmic and moral dimensions
- **St. John Chrysostom**: Pastoral-moral interpretation for congregational edification

### Liturgical Embeddedness
The Psalter is divided into twenty *kathismata* (sections), read systematically throughout the weekly cycle of services. Specific psalms are appointed for Vespers (Psalms 103, 140), Matins (Psalms 3, 37, 62, 87, 102, 142), and the Hours.

### Theosis and Prayer
The Psalms are understood as the primary school of prayer, forming the believer in the path toward *theosis* (deification). The hesychast tradition draws heavily on psalm verses for the Jesus Prayer and contemplative practice.

## Theological Emphases

- **The Psalter as icon of prayer**: The Psalms reveal the full range of the soul's communion with God
- **Prophetic of Christ**: Following the Fathers, the Orthodox read messianic psalms as direct prophecies fulfilled in Christ's incarnation, passion, and resurrection
- **Cosmic praise**: The creation psalms (8, 19, 104) reveal the world as theophany
- **Repentance and mercy**: Psalm 50 (LXX; = Psalm 51 MT) is the quintessential penitential prayer

|-------------|-----|-------------|
| St. Athanasius | 4th century | *Letter to Marcellinus* — guide to the Psalter |
| St. Basil the Great | 4th century | Homilies on select Psalms |
