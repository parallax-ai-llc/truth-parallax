## The Psalm of Protection

Psalm 91 holds a special place in Orthodox devotion and is read during the service of Great Compline, particularly during Great Lent. The psalm's promises of divine protection are understood within the framework of spiritual warfare — the ongoing struggle against the passions and demonic powers.

## Key Themes

### The Shelter of the Most High (91:1-4)

The image of dwelling in God's shelter is connected in Orthodox theology to the practice of *hesychasm* — interior stillness and prayer. Those who abide in unceasing prayer dwell in the shelter of the Most High, protected by divine grace from spiritual harm.

### Angelic Ministry (91:11-12)

The Orthodox Church's rich angelology draws on this psalm. Angels are understood as real spiritual beings who serve as guardians and intercessors. The liturgical calendar includes feasts of the Archangels and the Guardian Angels, and iconography depicts angelic protection.

### Christ's Temptation (91:11-12 in Matthew 4:6)

The Orthodox Fathers read the devil's citation of this psalm as a paradigm of *prelest* — spiritual deception. The enemy takes genuine truth and distorts it. Discernment (*diakrisis*), one of the gifts of the Spirit, is the antidote to such deception.

## Theological Significance

- **Christological reading**: The Fathers hear Christ's voice in this psalm
- **Liturgical life**: The psalm shapes daily Orthodox worship
- **Theosis**: The psalm illuminates the path toward divine participation
- **Patristic continuity**: Orthodox interpretation stands in unbroken tradition
