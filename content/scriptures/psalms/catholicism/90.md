## The Prayer of Moses

Catholic tradition reverences Psalm 90 as the prayer of Moses, the oldest psalm in the Psalter. It is prayed at Compline in the Liturgy of the Hours and is used in funeral liturgies, where its meditation on mortality prepares the soul for eternity.

## Key Themes

### Divine Eternity and Human Frailty (90:1-6)

The contrast between God's timelessness and human transience is central to Catholic ascetical theology. The Fathers taught that meditation on death (*memento mori*) is not morbid but liberating — it frees the soul from attachment to passing things and directs it toward eternal goods.

### Sin and Judgment (90:7-11)

Moses attributes human mortality to God's wrath against sin. Catholic theology, following the Council of Trent, affirms that death entered the world through original sin (Romans 5:12). The psalm's sober assessment of divine anger is balanced by its trust in God as "our dwelling place."

### Wisdom and Numbered Days (90:12)

"Teach us to number our days" has been a guiding text for Catholic spiritual directors. The Ignatian Exercises include meditation on mortality, and the Benedictine motto *memento mori* reflects this psalm's spirituality. The CCC (1007-1014) discusses the Christian meaning of death in light of such texts.

## Theological Significance

- **Eternal perspective**: Human life measured against God's timelessness
- **Original sin**: Mortality as a consequence of the fall
- **Spiritual wisdom**: Numbering our days as a path to holiness
- **Liturgical prayer**: Psalm 90 in the Liturgy of the Hours and funeral rites
