Psalm 31 holds an irreplaceable place in Catholic devotion because of Jesus's final words on the cross: "Into your hands I commit my spirit" (31:5; Luke 23:46). The Church prays these words daily at Compline (Night Prayer) in the Liturgy of the Hours, entrusting each night — that small daily death — to the Father's care. Every Catholic who prays Compline reenacts Christ's ultimate act of surrender.

The psalm's vivid description of suffering — "I am forgotten as though I were dead; I have become like broken pottery" (31:12) — has been embraced by Catholic mystical theology as describing the "dark night of the soul." John of the Cross taught that God sometimes withdraws the felt sense of his presence to purify the soul's love, stripping away all that is not God so that the soul may cling to God alone.

Catholic social teaching draws on verse 15 — "My times are in your hands" — to ground the Church's consistent ethic of life. Because human existence belongs to God from conception to natural death, no one may arrogate the power to determine when life begins or ends. The psalm's trust in divine sovereignty over human times underpins the Church's defense of human dignity at every stage of life.
