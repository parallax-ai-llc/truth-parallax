## Christ the Priest-King

Psalm 110 is the most important messianic psalm in Catholic theology. It is the scriptural foundation for the doctrine of Christ's eternal priesthood, elaborated in the Letter to the Hebrews and reflected in the theology of the Mass.

## Key Themes

### The Lordship of Christ (110:1)

"The LORD says to my Lord" — this verse, quoted by Jesus Himself (Matthew 22:44), establishes that the Messiah transcends the Davidic category. Catholic Christology affirms that Christ is both true God (David's Lord) and true man (David's son). The Nicene Creed's confession that Christ "is seated at the right hand of the Father" draws on this verse.

### The Eternal Priesthood (110:4)

"You are a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek." Catholic theology sees here the scriptural basis for the ordained priesthood. Christ's priesthood, unlike the Levitical order, is eternal and unrepeatable. The ministerial priesthood of the Church participates in Christ's priesthood, making His sacrifice present in the Eucharist (*Presbyterorum Ordinis* 2).

### Melchizedek and the Eucharist

The connection between Melchizedek (who offered bread and wine in Genesis 14:18) and Christ's priesthood is foundational to Catholic eucharistic theology. The Roman Canon (Eucharistic Prayer I) explicitly names Melchizedek's offering as a type of the Eucharist.

## Theological Significance

- **Christ's divine-human identity**: Lord and Son of David
- **Eternal priesthood**: The basis for Catholic sacramental theology
- **Eucharistic sacrifice**: Melchizedek's offering fulfilled in the Mass
- **Eschatological victory**: Christ's reign until all enemies are subdued
