## Catholic Reading of Psalm 87

Catholic interpretation of Psalm 87 is shaped by the Church Fathers, the Magisterium, and the psalm's place in the Liturgy of the Hours. The psalm's themes of zion, city of god are read within the broader context of salvation history and the Church's sacramental life.

## Key Themes

### Patristic Interpretation

The Church Fathers — particularly St. Augustine in his *Enarrationes in Psalmos* — read this psalm Christologically, seeing in the psalmist's words a prophetic voice that speaks of and to Christ. Augustine's commentary remains the most extensive patristic treatment and continues to shape Catholic exegesis.

### Liturgical Context

Psalm 87 is prayed within the cycle of the Liturgy of the Hours, where it shapes the Church's daily prayer. The Second Vatican Council's *Sacrosanctum Concilium* reaffirmed the centrality of the Psalter in Catholic worship, describing the Divine Office as the prayer of the whole Church, head and members.

### Moral and Spiritual Application

Catholic moral theology draws from this psalm principles for the faithful life. The themes present here — Zion, nations, city of God — inform the Church's teaching on virtue, prayer, and the pursuit of holiness in everyday life.

## Theological Significance

- **Christological reading**: The psalm points to Christ as its ultimate subject
- **Ecclesial prayer**: The Church prays this psalm as the Body of Christ
- **Sacramental life**: The psalm's themes illuminate the grace of the sacraments
- **Moral formation**: Guidance for the faithful in pursuing holiness
