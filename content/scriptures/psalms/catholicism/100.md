## A Psalm of Thanksgiving

Psalm 100 is prayed daily in the Liturgy of the Hours and occupies a central place in Catholic worship. Its five verses encapsulate the theology of praise: God is Creator, we are His people, His love endures forever.

## Key Themes

### Universal Call to Worship (100:1)

"Shout for joy to the Lord, all the earth" — the psalm's opening extends the invitation to worship beyond Israel to all peoples. Catholic missiology sees here an anticipation of the Church's universal mission, embracing all nations in the praise of God.

### "He made us, and we are his" (100:3)

Catholic anthropology is grounded in this confession. Human identity is not self-created but received from God. The *Catechism* (CCC 356) affirms that human dignity derives from being created by God and called to communion with Him.

### Entering the Gates (100:4)

The psalm's imagery of entering the temple gates with thanksgiving is enacted in Catholic liturgy — the procession into the church, the gathering of the faithful, the preparation for the Eucharist. Every Mass begins with this movement from the world into sacred space.

## Theological Significance

- **Liturgical centrality**: Psalm 100 in the Liturgy of the Hours
- **Universal worship**: All peoples called to praise the Creator
- **Divine faithfulness**: God's *hesed* endures through all generations
- **Eucharistic preparation**: Entering God's presence with thanksgiving
