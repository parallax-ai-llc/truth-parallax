## The Temple as Sacramental Presence

Catholic interpretation of Psalm 84 centers on the longing for sacramental encounter with God. The psalmist's desire for the temple courts is understood as an expression of the soul's natural orientation toward God — what Augustine called the *cor inquietum* ("restless heart") that finds rest only in the divine presence.

## Key Themes

### The Church as God's Dwelling (84:1-4)

Catholic theology sees the temple longing fulfilled in the Church, where Christ is present in the Eucharist, the Word, the sacraments, and the gathered assembly. The *Catechism* (CCC 1179-1186) teaches that the church building is a sacramental sign of the heavenly Jerusalem.

### Pilgrimage Spirituality (84:5-7)

The journey through the Valley of Baca reflects the Catholic understanding of life as pilgrimage — the *via* of the saints, marked by both suffering and transformation. The tradition of pilgrimage to holy sites (Jerusalem, Rome, Santiago de Compostela) draws on this psalm's spirituality.

### "A Day in Your Courts" (84:10)

The preference for God's courts over worldly comfort resonates with the Catholic emphasis on the Mass as the "source and summit" of Christian life (*Sacrosanctum Concilium* 10). The doorkeeper's humility anticipates the evangelical poverty praised by Christ.

## Theological Significance

- **Sacramental presence**: God dwells among His people in the liturgy
- **Pilgrimage**: The Christian life as a journey toward the heavenly dwelling
- **Eucharistic longing**: The soul's desire for communion with God
- **Blessed poverty**: Preferring God's service over worldly honor
