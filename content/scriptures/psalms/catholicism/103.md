## The Great Hymn of Mercy

Catholic tradition treasures Psalm 103 as the definitive hymn of divine mercy — a theme given renewed emphasis by St. John Paul II's encyclical *Dives in Misericordia* and Pope Francis's declaration of the Jubilee Year of Mercy.

## Key Themes

### Forgiveness and Healing (103:3-5)

The catalog of divine benefits — forgiveness, healing, redemption, crowning with love — is read in Catholic theology as encompassing both the spiritual and corporal works of mercy. The sacrament of Reconciliation (Confession) embodies the forgiveness celebrated here, while the Anointing of the Sick corresponds to the healing.

### "As far as the east is from the west" (103:12)

This verse is central to the Catholic theology of absolution. When sins are forgiven in the sacrament of Reconciliation, they are truly removed — not merely covered or overlooked. Pope Francis has repeatedly cited this verse in his teaching on God's inexhaustible mercy.

### Compassion and Human Frailty (103:13-16)

God's fatherly compassion, knowing that "we are dust," balances justice with tenderness. This informs the Catholic approach to moral theology — acknowledging human weakness while calling toward holiness, a pastoral balance emphasized in *Amoris Laetitia*.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine mercy**: God's fundamental disposition toward His creatures
- **Sacramental forgiveness**: Reconciliation as the channel of mercy
- **Fatherly compassion**: God's knowledge of human frailty
- **Cosmic praise**: All creation summoned to bless the Lord
