Psalm 47 is an enthronement psalm that Catholic liturgical tradition has associated with the Feast of the Ascension. "God has ascended amid shouts of joy, the LORD amid the sounding of trumpets" (47:5). Catholic theology reads this as prophetically describing Christ's ascension to the Father's right hand, where he reigns as King of kings. The Ascension is not Christ's departure but his enthronement.

The psalm's universal vision — "God reigns over the nations; God is seated on his holy throne" (47:8) — undergirds Catholic missiology and the Church's claim to universality (*catholicity*). The Second Vatican Council's decree *Ad Gentes* affirmed that the Church is "missionary by her very nature," called to proclaim the kingship of God to every nation. This psalm provides the theological basis for that mission.

The gathering of "the nobles of the nations" as "the people of the God of Abraham" (47:9) is read by Catholic theology as anticipating the eschatological gathering of all peoples into the one People of God. The Catechism teaches that the Church is the "sacrament of the unity of the human race" (CCC 775), the visible sign and instrument of God's plan to unite all humanity under Christ's reign. Psalm 47's vision of universal praise finds its earthly anticipation in the Church's worldwide liturgy.
