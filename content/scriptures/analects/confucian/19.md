Confucian interpretation of Analects 19 records the sayings of Confucius's leading disciples, particularly Zizhang, Zixia, and Ziyou. The chapter provides evidence that the Master's teachings generated vigorous debate among his followers, with different disciples emphasizing different aspects of the Confucian way. Zixia's focus on learning and cultural refinement contrasts with Zizhang's emphasis on broad-minded tolerance, reflecting the creative diversity within the early Confucian community.

The chapter concludes with Zigong's eloquent defense of Confucius's reputation (19.23-25), comparing the Master to the sun and moon that cannot be surpassed, and to a wall too high for ordinary people to peer over. These passages have shaped the Confucian tradition's veneration of its founder and its understanding of the incommensurability of true sagely virtue.
