Confucian reading of Analects 10 provides a detailed portrait of Confucius's daily conduct, including his dress, diet, posture, and behavior in various social contexts. This chapter is distinctive in the Analects for its descriptive rather than discursive character, and Confucian interpreters have valued it as evidence that moral cultivation pervades every aspect of life. The Master's attentiveness to propriety in seemingly minor matters demonstrates that li is not confined to formal ceremonies but shapes the entire texture of lived experience.

The chapter illustrates the Confucian principle that the exemplary person embodies virtue in bodily practice, not merely in speech or thought. Confucius's careful attention to the preparation and presentation of food, to appropriate clothing for different occasions, and to respectful comportment in the presence of others reveals a vision of the moral life as thoroughgoing and comprehensive.
