Confucian interpretation of Analects 7 presents Confucius's self-understanding as a transmitter rather than a creator (7.1), a characterization that defines the Confucian relationship to cultural tradition. This chapter reveals the Master's tireless devotion to learning and teaching, his love of ancient culture, and his modesty in disclaiming sage status while embodying the virtues he taught. Confucian readers find here the model of the dedicated teacher-scholar.

The chapter contains Confucius's declaration that he never tires of learning or of teaching others (7.2), and his expression of joy in learning that causes him to forget his worries and not notice the approach of old age (7.19). These passages establish the Confucian ideal of learning as an intrinsically fulfilling activity that sustains the moral life throughout its course.
