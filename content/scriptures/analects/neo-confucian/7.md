Neo-Confucian interpretation of Analects 7 reads Confucius's self-description as a "transmitter, not a creator" (7.1) as indicating his role in transmitting the Way of heavenly principle that the sage-kings had originally realized. For Zhu Xi, this modesty does not diminish Confucius's stature but reveals the nature of moral truth: it is not invented but discovered and transmitted. The sage's task is to make manifest the eternal principles that govern human life and the cosmos.

The chapter's description of Confucius's tireless devotion to learning is interpreted as the sustained effort of "investigating things and extending knowledge" (gewu zhizhi) that Zhu Xi regarded as the foundation of Neo-Confucian self-cultivation. The Master's joy in learning that causes him to forget food (7.19) represents the state in which the investigation of principle becomes the deepest source of human fulfillment.
