Neo-Confucian interpretation of Analects 3 examines ritual propriety (li) as the external expression of heavenly principle. Zhu Xi reads Confucius's distress at the Ji family's ritual usurpation as a response to the violation of the principle of hierarchical order that structures both the cosmos and human society. When ritual is performed by unauthorized persons, it is not merely a social transgression but a disruption of the metaphysical order.

The chapter's discussion of the essence of ritual is interpreted through the Neo-Confucian insistence that li must express genuine moral substance (zhi). Zhu Xi argues that ritual without the inner reality of principle is empty form, while principle without ritual expression remains unrealized. The proper relationship between inner substance and outer form is one of the central concerns of Neo-Confucian philosophy.
