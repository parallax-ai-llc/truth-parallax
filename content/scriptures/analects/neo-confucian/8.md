Neo-Confucian reading of Analects 8 interprets the sage-kings' legacy through the framework of the succession of the Way (daotong). Zhu Xi developed the concept of the daotong—the lineage of sages who transmitted the true understanding of principle—and reads this chapter as documenting the moral heritage that passed from the ancient sage-kings through Confucius. Tai Bo's yielding of the throne (8.1) represents the ultimate triumph of principle over selfish desire.

Zengzi's teaching on the gentleman's heavy burden and long road (8.7) is read as describing the Neo-Confucian project of realizing heavenly principle in all things and affairs. The weight of this responsibility reflects the comprehensiveness of principle, which must be investigated and actualized in every domain of human life, from personal conduct to governance of the empire.
