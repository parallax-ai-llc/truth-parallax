Neo-Confucian interpretation of Analects 1, guided by Zhu Xi's commentary, reads the opening chapter as establishing the connection between learning and the realization of heavenly principle (tianli) within the human heart-mind. The famous opening on the pleasure of learning is interpreted not merely as intellectual satisfaction but as the joy that arises when principle is grasped and actualized. Zhu Xi emphasizes that learning (xue) means to emulate the sages, aligning one's nature with its original endowment of principle.

The chapter's teachings on filial piety are read through the Neo-Confucian framework as expressions of the principle of humaneness (ren) operating within the most fundamental human relationship. For Zhu Xi, filial piety is not merely a social obligation but a manifestation of the heavenly principle that structures all relationships. The Neo-Confucian reading thus transforms practical moral teachings into windows onto the metaphysical order.
