Neo-Confucian reading of Analects 12 centers on the pivotal definition of humaneness as "restraining oneself and returning to ritual propriety" (12.1). Zhu Xi interprets this as the process of overcoming selfish desires (si yu) and returning the heart-mind to its original state of pure heavenly principle. The phrase "return to ritual" means allowing principle to express itself through proper conduct, which is the natural state of the uncorrupted heart-mind.

The chapter's teaching that governance requires the ruler's personal rectitude (12.17) is given metaphysical grounding in Neo-Confucian commentary. When the ruler's heart-mind is aligned with principle, his moral influence transforms the people naturally, just as the wind bends the grass (12.19). This is not mere metaphor but describes the actual operation of principle in human society, connecting personal cultivation to cosmic order.
