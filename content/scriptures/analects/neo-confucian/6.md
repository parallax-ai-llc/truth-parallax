Neo-Confucian reading of Analects 6 highlights Confucius's teaching on the balance of substance (zhi) and refinement (wen) as reflecting the proper relationship between principle and its cultural expression. Zhu Xi interprets the exemplary person's balance of zhi and wen (6.18) as the harmonious integration of heavenly principle with the forms of civilized life, neither raw and uncultivated nor artificially ornamental.

The chapter's portrait of Yan Hui living contentedly in poverty (6.11) exemplifies the Neo-Confucian ideal of finding joy in the apprehension of principle regardless of material circumstances. Zhu Xi and the Cheng brothers (Cheng Hao and Cheng Yi) devoted extensive commentary to understanding what Yan Hui's joy consisted in, concluding that it was the joy of realizing heavenly principle within the heart-mind.
