Neo-Confucian interpretation of Analects 9 examines the Master's restraint and humility as expressions of the sage's relationship to principle. Confucius's reluctance to speak directly of humaneness, fate, and profit is read by Zhu Xi as pedagogical wisdom: these subjects are too profound to be communicated through mere verbal instruction and must be grasped through the student's own effort of investigation and reflection.

The chapter's account of Confucius's grief at the failure of the Way to prevail (9.9) is interpreted within the Neo-Confucian understanding of the relationship between principle and historical circumstance. While principle is eternal and universal, its realization in the world depends upon the conjunction of human effort and historical conditions (shi). The sage perseveres in moral cultivation even when conditions are unfavorable, trusting in the ultimate reality of principle.
