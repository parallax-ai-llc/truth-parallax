Neo-Confucian reading of Analects 16 interprets the chapter's political teachings through the concept of hierarchical order as an expression of heavenly principle. The critique of the Ji family (16.1) illustrates how the violation of principled hierarchy leads to the progressive fragmentation of political authority. Zhu Xi reads the deterioration from the Son of Heaven to feudal lords to ministers to household servants as the successive obscuration of principle by selfish desire at each level of the political hierarchy.

The chapter's categorization of beneficial and harmful pleasures (16.5) is given metaphysical significance in Neo-Confucian commentary. Beneficial pleasures are those aligned with principle, such as the enjoyment of ritual music and the company of virtuous friends, while harmful pleasures represent the indulgence of selfish desires that obscure the heart-mind's original clarity.
