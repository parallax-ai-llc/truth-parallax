Neo-Confucian interpretation of Analects 17 examines the tension between engagement and withdrawal through the lens of the sage's responsiveness to principle in varying circumstances. Confucius's analogy of the uneaten gourd (17.7) is read by Zhu Xi as expressing the principle that moral substance demands actualization; the sage cannot remain in a state of unrealized potential but must engage with the world to bring principle to bear on human affairs.

The chapter's teaching on the Odes (17.9) receives Neo-Confucian attention as evidence that aesthetic cultivation serves moral development. Zhu Xi argues that the Odes stimulate the heart-mind's apprehension of principle through the emotional power of poetry, making them an essential complement to the more systematic investigation of things through study and reflection.
