Chinese Buddhist reading of Analects 7 draws parallels between Confucius's devotion to learning and the Buddhist commitment to the study of dharma as a lifelong practice. The Master's tireless pursuit of understanding, his forgetting to eat when absorbed in learning (7.19), recalls the stories of Buddhist monks and nuns who dedicated their entire lives to the study and practice of the dharma, finding in this pursuit a source of boundless energy and joy.

Confucius's modesty in denying sage status (7.34) resonates with the Buddhist teaching on the dangers of spiritual pride. The bodhisattva who has attained high levels of realization does not proclaim his attainments, and the Chan tradition particularly values the master who deflects praise and points students toward their own direct experience. Chinese Buddhist interpreters found in Confucius's humility evidence of a genuinely spiritual temperament.
