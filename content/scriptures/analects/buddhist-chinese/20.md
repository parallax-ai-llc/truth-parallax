Chinese Buddhist interpretation of Analects 20 reads the concluding chapter's invocation of the sage-kings through the Buddhist concept of the dharma-king lineage. Just as Buddhism traces the transmission of the dharma through a succession of buddhas and patriarchs, the Confucian tradition traces the transmission of the Way through a succession of sage-kings from Yao and Shun through Confucius. Both traditions invest their founding figures with a moral authority that transcends individual historical circumstances.

The final verse on understanding fate, ritual, and words (20.3) is interpreted by Chinese Buddhists as pointing toward the integration of wisdom, practice, and discernment that characterizes the fully developed spiritual person in any tradition. Understanding fate parallels the Buddhist acceptance of karma and impermanence; understanding ritual parallels the discipline of moral practice; understanding words parallels the discernment of right speech and the ability to perceive truth through and beyond language.
