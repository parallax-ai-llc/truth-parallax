Chinese Buddhist reading of Analects 19 observes the disciples' varying interpretations as analogous to the proliferation of Buddhist schools, each emphasizing different aspects of the Buddha's comprehensive teaching. Just as the Theravada, Madhyamaka, Yogacara, and Chan traditions each claim to transmit the authentic dharma while differing in emphasis and method, Confucius's disciples developed distinct approaches to the Master's teaching.

Zigong's comparison of Confucius to the sun and moon (19.24) resonates with Buddhist descriptions of the Buddha as the "light of the world" whose teaching illuminates the darkness of ignorance. Chinese Buddhist interpreters read such passages as evidence that the Confucian tradition, while operating within a different doctrinal framework, recognized in its founder a figure of universal spiritual significance comparable to the Buddha himself.
