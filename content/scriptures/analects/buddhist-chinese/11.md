Chinese Buddhist reading of Analects 11 finds in the portrait of Yan Hui and the other disciples a parallel to the sangha's diversity of spiritual gifts. Just as the Buddha's disciples excelled in different aspects of the practice—Sariputta in wisdom, Maudgalyayana in supernatural powers, Ananda in memory—Confucius's disciples excel in virtue, speech, governance, or culture (11.3). Both traditions celebrate the complementarity of diverse excellences within a community united by devotion to the teacher and the path.

The concluding passage (11.26) where Confucius endorses Zeng Xi's simple aspiration resonates with Chan Buddhism's emphasis on the extraordinary within the ordinary. The wish to simply enjoy the spring breeze with friends, without any agenda of moral or political improvement, approaches the Chan insight that awakening is found not in extraordinary experiences but in the full appreciation of each present moment.
