Chinese Buddhist interpretation of Analects 16 reads the chapter's concern with political order through the Buddhist understanding of interdependent arising applied to social phenomena. The cascade of disorder from the top of the political hierarchy downward illustrates the Buddhist insight that all phenomena arise in dependence upon conditions, and that the corruption of one element in a system affects all others. Both Confucian and Buddhist analysis emphasize the interconnectedness of individual and social well-being.

The categorization of beneficial and harmful pleasures (16.5) parallels the Buddhist distinction between wholesome (kusala) and unwholesome (akusala) mental states. Chinese Buddhists appreciated the Confucian recognition that not all pleasures are equal and that discernment is required to distinguish those that support moral development from those that undermine it, a discernment central to the Buddhist path of right effort.
