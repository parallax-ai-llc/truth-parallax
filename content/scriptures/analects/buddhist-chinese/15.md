Chinese Buddhist reading of Analects 15 gives special attention to the Golden Rule (15.24) as a parallel to the Buddhist practice of exchanging self for others (paratmasamata). The instruction not to impose on others what one does not desire oneself reflects the same empathic capacity that the Buddhist tradition cultivates through meditation on the equality of self and other. Both traditions ground ethical conduct in the ability to imaginatively enter the experience of another being.

The chapter's emphasis on perseverance in the face of adversity resonates with the Buddhist paramita of patience (kshanti). The exemplary person who maintains moral commitment despite lack of recognition embodies the patient endurance that the bodhisattva cultivates through countless lifetimes of service to beings who may not appreciate or understand the sacrifice being made on their behalf.
