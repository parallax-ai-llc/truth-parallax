Academic study of Analects 10 has generated extensive debate about the chapter's literary character, purpose, and dating. Unlike the rest of the Analects, which records sayings and dialogues, chapter 10 provides descriptive accounts of Confucius's behavior. Some scholars regard it as an early biographical fragment, while others view it as a later idealization composed to provide a model of proper conduct. The chapter's detailed attention to dress codes, dietary rules, and postural norms has made it a valuable source for historians of material culture and daily life in ancient China.

Ritual studies scholars have used this chapter to reconstruct the protocols of Zhou-dynasty court life and to analyze the relationship between bodily practice and moral cultivation in early Chinese thought. The chapter provides evidence for the Confucian understanding of the body as a site of moral formation, a theme that has attracted increasing attention from scholars working at the intersection of philosophy and anthropology.
