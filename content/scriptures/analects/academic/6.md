Academic study of Analects 6 examines the chapter's contributions to Confucian moral psychology, particularly the teaching on the balance of substance (zhi) and refinement (wen) (6.18). This passage has generated extensive scholarly debate about the relationship between natural moral dispositions and cultural formation in Confucian thought. Some scholars read it as asserting the equal importance of both, while others argue that Confucius ultimately privileges substance as the necessary foundation for refinement.

The portrait of Yan Hui (6.11) has been analyzed as evidence for the earliest Confucian understanding of the relationship between virtue and material conditions. Scholars note that Yan Hui's example complicates any reading of early Confucianism as primarily concerned with political power and social status, revealing a contemplative or ascetic strand that later Neo-Confucianism would develop more fully.
