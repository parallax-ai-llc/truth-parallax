Daoist reading of Analects 5 questions the Confucian practice of evaluating and ranking human beings according to moral criteria. From a Daoist perspective, Confucius's assessments of his contemporaries and disciples reveal an attachment to moral judgment that is itself an obstacle to the Way. The Zhuangzi's sage recognizes the equality of all perspectives and the relativity of moral categories, refusing to rank persons as superior or inferior based on humanly devised standards of virtue.

Daoist interpreters note, however, that Confucius's evaluations sometimes reveal an appreciation for unconventional figures who defy rigid moral categories. The complexity of his judgments suggests an awareness that true moral insight cannot be reduced to simple formulas, an insight that Daoists develop more radically in their critique of all systematic moral thinking.
