Daoist reading of Analects 7 engages with Confucius's self-characterization as a lover of antiquity who transmits rather than creates (7.1). Daoists read this reverence for the past as an attachment to cultural forms that may obstruct the direct apprehension of the Way, which is always present and does not require historical mediation. The Daodejing's sage looks directly at the Way rather than through the lens of ancient traditions.

However, Confucius's descriptions of the joy he finds in learning, forgetting to eat and not noticing the approach of old age (7.19), resonate with the Daoist experience of absorption in the Way. When the self is forgotten in complete engagement with what is, the distinction between Confucian learning and Daoist wu wei may be less absolute than either tradition typically acknowledges.
