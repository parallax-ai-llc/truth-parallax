Daoist interpretation of Analects 6 finds resonance in Confucius's praise of Yan Hui's contentment in poverty (6.11). The ability to find joy with a bamboo bowl of rice and a gourd of water, in a mean lane that others would find insupportable, echoes the Daoist ideal of the sage who is content with simplicity and free from the desire for material accumulation. This passage suggests that Confucius's deepest moral insight converges with the Daoist understanding of the sufficiency of natural simplicity.

The teaching on the balance of substance and refinement (6.18) is read by Daoists as evidence that Confucius recognized the danger of excessive cultural ornamentation. While Daoists would prefer substance without refinement to any balance of the two, they appreciate the Confucian acknowledgment that cultural artifice can obscure rather than enhance natural virtue.
