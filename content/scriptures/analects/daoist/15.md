Daoist reading of Analects 15 engages with the Golden Rule (15.24) as a sophisticated but limited moral principle. While Daoists appreciate the empathic sensibility underlying reciprocity, they note that it still operates within the framework of self-other duality that the Dao transcends. The Daoist sage does not refrain from imposing on others because of empathic projection but because the very distinction between self and other has dissolved in the experience of the Way's unity.

The chapter's emphasis on moral perseverance in adversity is viewed by Daoists as admirable but unnecessary if one has truly released attachment to outcomes. The exemplary person who is distressed by their own lack of ability remains caught in the cycle of moral self-evaluation that Daoist practice seeks to transcend through the acceptance of one's natural endowment and place in the transformations of things.
