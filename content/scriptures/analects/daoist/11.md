Daoist reading of Analects 11 gives special attention to the concluding passage (11.26), which Daoists regard as the most authentically Daoist moment in the entire Analects. When Zeng Xi expresses his wish to bathe in the Yi River and enjoy the spring breeze with companions, Confucius sighs and says "I am with Zeng Xi!" This endorsement of spontaneous enjoyment of nature over political ambition is read by Daoist interpreters as evidence that Confucius, in his most unguarded moments, recognized the superiority of natural harmony over moral striving.

Daoist commentary on this passage emphasizes that Zeng Xi's aspiration involves no moral agenda, no desire to reform society, no conscious cultivation of virtue. It is simply the expression of a person at ease with the natural world—precisely the state that Daoist practice aims to cultivate through the release of moral ambition and the return to spontaneous simplicity.
