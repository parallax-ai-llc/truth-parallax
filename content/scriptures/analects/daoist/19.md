Daoist reading of Analects 19 observes the disciples' debates as evidence that Confucian moral teaching inevitably generates disagreement and faction—precisely the social conflict that the Way, operating without moral categories, avoids. The competing emphases of Zizhang, Zixia, and Ziyou illustrate the Daoist critique that once the great Way is fragmented into specific virtues and methods, unity is lost and dispute arises.

Zigong's defense of Confucius's incomparable stature (19.23-25) is read by Daoists as a form of sage-worship that elevates one human being above the natural equality of all things. The Daoist sage does not tower above others like the sun and moon but moves among them as one wave moves among other waves in the ocean of the Dao, distinguished only by the effortlessness of his naturalness.
