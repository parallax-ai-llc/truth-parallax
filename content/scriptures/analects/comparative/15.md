Comparative reading of Analects 15 places the Golden Rule (15.24) at the center of cross-cultural ethical dialogue. This principle appears in virtually every major ethical and religious tradition: the Christian "do unto others" (Matthew 7:12), the Jewish teaching of Hillel, the Buddhist teaching on the equality of self and other, the Hindu concept of atmaupamya (seeing others as oneself), and the Ubuntu philosophy of mutual care. The near-universality of this principle has been cited as evidence for a common moral sense underlying the diversity of cultural and religious traditions.

The chapter's teachings on perseverance in adversity invite comparison with the Stoic concept of the sage who maintains inner tranquility regardless of external circumstances, the Christian virtue of hope sustained through suffering, and the Buddhist teaching on the paramita of patience. These parallels suggest that the capacity to maintain moral commitment in the face of adversity is recognized cross-culturally as a hallmark of mature moral character.
