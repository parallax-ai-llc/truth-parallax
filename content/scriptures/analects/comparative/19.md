Comparative reading of Analects 19 places the disciples' diverse interpretations of Confucius's teaching alongside similar patterns of interpretive diversity in other traditions. The divergences between Zizhang, Zixia, and Ziyou parallel the differences between Paul, James, and John in the early Christian community, between the Theravada and Mahayana Buddhist schools, and between the Sunni and Shia traditions in Islam. In each case, a founding teacher's death generates a creative but sometimes contentious diversity of interpretation that becomes constitutive of the tradition itself.

Zigong's comparison of Confucius to the sun and moon (19.24) invites comparison with similar claims of unique and unsurpassable authority made on behalf of founders in other traditions. The elevation of a human teacher to a position of cosmic significance appears cross-culturally and raises common questions about the relationship between historical persons and the timeless truths they are believed to embody.
