Amos 5 contains the prophetic tradition's most powerful statement on justice. The call "Seek me and live" (5:4) resonates with the Quranic invitation to follow God's guidance as the path to true life. The Quran declares: "O you who believe, respond to Allah and to the Messenger when he calls you to that which gives you life" (8:24).

The condemnation of worship divorced from justice (5:21-24) finds direct parallels in Islamic teaching. The Quran states that piety is not merely facing east or west in prayer but involves belief in God, spending wealth on the needy, keeping promises, and being patient in adversity (2:177). Both Amos and the Quran insist that ritual observance without ethical commitment is not merely inadequate but offensive to God.

The reinterpretation of the Day of the LORD as darkness (5:18-20) has parallels in the Quranic descriptions of the Day of Judgment, which will be a day of terror for the wicked: "The Day they see it, it will be as though they had not remained in the world except for an afternoon or a morning thereof" (79:46). Both texts warn against the presumption that divine reckoning will be favorable — a warning that applies to all who assume their religious identity guarantees divine approval.
