## Overview

Amos is not named in the Quran, but his prophetic profile — a humble man from outside the religious establishment, called by God to confront the powerful and demand justice for the oppressed — resonates powerfully with the Quranic pattern of prophethood. Islamic engagement with Amos focuses on the universality of his ethical message and its compatibility with Quranic teachings on justice, accountability, and divine sovereignty.

## Prophetic Parallels

Amos's identity as a shepherd called to prophesy mirrors the Quranic emphasis on prophets drawn from humble circumstances. The Prophet Muhammad himself was a shepherd and merchant before receiving revelation. Amos's confrontation with Amaziah the priest (7:10-17) parallels Quranic narratives where prophets face opposition from religious elites — Musa confronting Pharaoh's sorcerers, Ibrahim challenging his father's idolatry. The oracles against the nations (chapters 1-2) align with the Quranic concept that God holds all peoples accountable, not merely the covenant community.

## Justice and Islamic Ethics

Amos's central demand — that justice and righteousness must accompany worship — finds direct expression in Islamic ethics. The Quran declares, "O you who believe, stand firmly for justice" (4:135). Amos's denunciation of those who "sell the righteous for silver, and the needy for a pair of sandals" (2:6) mirrors the Quranic condemnation of economic exploitation and cheating in measures (83:1-3). The prophetic insistence that God rejects worship divorced from ethical conduct is a shared principle across both traditions.
