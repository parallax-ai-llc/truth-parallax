Amos 7 presents visions of judgment and the dramatic confrontation between prophet and priest. The prophet's successful intercession in the first two visions illustrates the Islamic concept of prophetic intercession (*shafa'ah*). The Quran affirms that certain chosen servants may intercede with God's permission: "Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission?" (2:255).

The plumb line vision introduces a symbol of divine measurement that Islamic theology recognizes in the concept of the *sirat al-mustaqim* (the straight path). The Quran's opening prayer — "Guide us to the straight path" — echoes Amos's image of a standard against which all crookedness is measured. Both the plumb line and the straight path represent the objective divine norm against which human behavior is evaluated.

The confrontation between Amos and Amaziah (7:10-17) illustrates the tension between prophetic truth and institutional power that appears throughout Islamic history. The Quran records similar confrontations: Musa against Pharaoh, Ibrahim against Nimrod, Muhammad against the Quraysh elite. In each case, the prophet represents divine authority while the institutional leader represents human power, and the prophetic message ultimately prevails despite initial resistance and persecution.
