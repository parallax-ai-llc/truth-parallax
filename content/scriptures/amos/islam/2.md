Amos 2 completes the oracles and delivers the devastating indictment of Israel. The social crimes described — selling the righteous for silver, trampling the poor, denying justice — are condemned with equal force in Islamic teaching. The Quran warns: "Woe to those who give less than due, who, when they take a measure from people, take in full, but if they give by measure or by weight to them, they cause loss" (83:1-3).

The recollection of divine favor (2:9-11) — the gift of the land, the provision of prophets — establishes that Israel's accountability is proportional to the blessings received. Islamic theology applies this principle broadly: communities that receive prophetic guidance (*hidayah*) bear greater responsibility than those who have not. The Quran states that the people of the Book (Jews and Christians) will be judged by the revelations they received.

The military imagery of the chapter's conclusion (2:14-16) — swift, strong, warrior, horseman, all unable to escape — echoes the Quranic teaching that no human power can resist divine decree: "And Allah is Predominant over His affair, but most of the people do not know" (12:21). Both Amos and the Quran insist that military might provides no refuge from divine justice.
