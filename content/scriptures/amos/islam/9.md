Amos 9 moves from comprehensive judgment to restoration, a pattern that mirrors the Quranic cycle of warning, destruction, and the possibility of renewal. The vision of inescapable divine pursuit (9:1-4) parallels the Quranic affirmation of God's omniscience and omnipresence: "And He is with you wherever you are. And Allah, of what you do, is Seeing" (57:4).

The "sifting" of Israel among the nations (9:9) resonates with the Quranic account of the children of Israel's dispersal as a consequence of covenant violation: "And We said after him to the Children of Israel, 'Dwell in the land, and when the promise of the Hereafter comes, We will bring you forth in a mixed multitude'" (17:104). Both texts present dispersion as divine discipline that preserves the essential community while separating the faithful from the faithless.

The restoration promise (9:11-15) — overflowing harvests, rebuilt cities, permanent settlement — describes the kind of earthly blessing that the Quran also promises to faithful communities: "If only the people of the cities had believed and feared Allah, We would have opened upon them blessings from the heaven and the earth" (7:96). The principle is shared: divine blessing flows from covenant faithfulness, and restoration is always possible for those who genuinely return to God.
