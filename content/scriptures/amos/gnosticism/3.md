Amos 3:2 — "You only have I known... therefore I will punish you" — reveals the core logic of the archontic system: special relationship means special subjection. In Gnostic reading, the Demiurge's "knowing" is not intimate love but possessive surveillance. He "knows" Israel in the sense that a warden knows his prisoners — intimately aware of their movements, quick to punish deviation, interpreting any independence as rebellion.

The rhetorical questions of 3:3-8 establish a deterministic chain of cause and effect that reflects the archontic world's mechanical operation. Everything happens for a reason; nothing is free or spontaneous. The prophet speaks because the Demiurge has spoken, the lion roars because it has found prey, the trap springs because something has triggered it. This vision of reality as a closed system of causes and effects is precisely what gnosis breaks open, introducing a dimension of freedom from outside the system.

The judgment on Samaria's wealth (3:9-15) exposes the futility of material accumulation within the archontic domain. No amount of ivory, no number of houses, can provide security in a world governed by a wrathful deity. The destruction of luxury is not justice but the Demiurge's reminder that everything within his creation belongs to him and can be reclaimed at will.
