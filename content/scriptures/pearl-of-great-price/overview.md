## Overview

The Pearl of Great Price is a selection of revelations, translations, and writings of Joseph Smith, published as a canonical scripture of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. First compiled by Franklin D. Richards in 1851 as a missionary pamphlet in England, it was canonized as scripture in 1880.

The collection contains five distinct texts: the Book of Moses (an inspired revision of the early chapters of Genesis), the Book of Abraham (presented as a translation of ancient Egyptian papyri), Joseph Smith—Matthew (an inspired revision of Matthew 24), Joseph Smith—History (an autobiographical account of the First Vision and early restoration events), and the Articles of Faith (thirteen statements summarizing key LDS beliefs).

The Pearl of Great Price is notable for its cosmological scope, extending the biblical narrative with accounts of pre-mortal existence, the council in heaven, and the astronomical teachings attributed to Abraham. It has been a focus of particular scholarly interest due to the controversy surrounding the Book of Abraham and the relationship between the Joseph Smith Papyri and the published text.

## Structure

The Pearl of Great Price contains five books:

1. **Book of Moses** (8 chapters): Joseph Smith's inspired revision of Genesis 1–6, including Enoch's vision
2. **Book of Abraham** (5 chapters): Translated from Egyptian papyri, covering Abraham's early life and cosmological visions
3. **Joseph Smith—Matthew**: An extract from Joseph Smith's inspired revision of Matthew 24
4. **Joseph Smith—History**: Account of the First Vision, Moroni's visits, and the coming forth of the Book of Mormon
5. **Articles of Faith**: Thirteen statements of belief written by Joseph Smith in 1842

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Pearl of Great Price holds significance across multiple interpretive traditions:

- **LDS**: Scripture providing unique doctrinal insights into pre-mortal existence, cosmology, and the restoration
- **Community of Christ**: The Book of Moses is included in their Inspired Version; other portions are valued differently
- **Academic**: Key texts for studying Joseph Smith's prophetic claims and LDS theological development
- **Protestant**: Examined critically against biblical theology and manuscript evidence
- **Comparative**: The Book of Abraham and cosmological teachings invite comparison with other ancient texts
- **Historical-Critical**: The Joseph Smith Papyri controversy makes this a major focus of textual criticism

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Pre-mortal Existence | The doctrine of souls existing before mortal birth |
| Cosmology | Abraham's vision of stars, Kolob, and the organization of the universe |
| Restoration Narrative | Joseph Smith's account of divine visitations and the translation process |
| Prophetic Authority | The claim of direct divine communication in the modern era |
| Doctrinal Summary | The Articles of Faith as a concise statement of LDS belief |
