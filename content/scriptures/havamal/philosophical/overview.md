## Overview

The Hávamál articulates a sophisticated philosophical ethics centered on practical wisdom, reciprocity, and self-knowledge. Its emphasis on moderation, the value of friendship, and the importance of reputation resonates with Aristotelian virtue ethics. The poem's treatment of wisdom as something gained through suffering and sacrifice raises epistemological questions about the relationship between experience and knowledge. Odin's self-sacrifice for runic wisdom presents a striking model of knowledge as transformative ordeal rather than passive reception.
