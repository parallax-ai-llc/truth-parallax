## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Hávamál connect the poem's references to runic knowledge, ritual practices, and social customs to material evidence from the Viking Age and earlier periods. Runic inscriptions on stones, weapons, and amulets provide context for the Rúnatal's account of runic discovery. Evidence of ritual hanging and spear-wounding at cult sites resonates with Odin's self-sacrifice. Archaeological study of hall culture, hospitality practices, and gift exchange illuminates the social world reflected in the Gestaþáttr.
