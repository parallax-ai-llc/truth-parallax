---
title: "Havamal - Chapter 3"
scripture: "havamal"
chapter: 3
---

**1** A wise-counselled man will be mild of mood and use his might in measure; among the bold he will find, when he comes among the valiant, that no one alone is bravest.

**2** A man should be watchful and wary as well, and shrewd when he makes a friend. Laughing with one man, speaking fairly, a fool he is who trusts all.

**3** A wise man shall be heedful and wary as well, slow to speak and swift to hear. Let no man glory in the goodness of his mind until he learns it leads to good.

**4** Of his knowledge a man should never boast, but rather keep watch with care; when the wise and silent come to a farmstead, harm seldom befalls the wary; for a firmer friend no man ever finds than shrewdness of sound mind.

**5** The man who stands at a strange threshold should be cautious before he crosses it, glancing about him, for he cannot know where foes may sit within the house.

**6** Cattle die, kindred die, every man is mortal. But the good name never dies of one who has done well.

**7** Cattle die, kindred die, every man is mortal. But I know one thing that never dies: the glory of the great dead.

**8** With half a loaf and an empty cup I found me many a friend; but generous men and valiant are best to live and look upon.

**9** Young and alone on a long road, once I lost my way. Rich I felt when I found a companion; man is the joy of man.

**10** The pine tree wastes which is perched on a hill, nor bark nor needles shelter it; such is the man whom none doth love; for what shall he longer live?

**11** Hotter than fire love between false friends burns for five days, but is quenched when the sixth day comes, and friendship is all forsworn.

**12** A little a man should give, not always must he lavish gifts; with half a loaf and a tilted cup I have won me a faithful friend.

**13** Of small sands and of small seas, small are the minds of men; for all men are not equally wise: the half-wise are everywhere.

**14** A measure of wisdom each man should have, but never too much let him know; for the wise man's heart is seldom glad, if he who owns it be all too wise.

**15** A measure of wisdom each man should have, but never too much let him know; let no man the fate before him see; his mind will be freest from sorrow.

**16** The lives of the brave and noble are best, sorrows they seldom feed; but the coward fears all things, and not gladly does the niggard give.

**17** Fire is best among sons of men, and the sight of the sun, good health if a man may get it, and a life not stained with sin.

**18** Not altogether a wretch is he, though he be ill in health; some are blessed in their sons, some in their friends, some in their wealth, some in their deeds well done.
