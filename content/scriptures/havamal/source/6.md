---
title: "Havamal - Chapter 6"
scripture: "havamal"
chapter: 6
---

**1** I know those spells which a ruler's wife knows not, nor any son of man. The first is called Help, and help it can in sickness and sorrow and every sort of grief.

**2** I know a second which the sons of men must need who would be leeches.

**3** I know a third: if my need be great in fetters to hold my foe, blunt do I make my enemy's blade, and nor sword nor wile may wound.

**4** I know a fourth: if men bind me fast with fetters wrought for my limbs, I sing a spell that sets me free; chains fall from my feet and bonds from my hands.

**5** I know a fifth: if I see from afar a dart fly dangerously, however swift it fly, I can stay it, if I but set my gaze upon it.

**6** I know a sixth: if a man would harm me with a young tree's roots, the man who wakens my hate shall meet the harm, and not I.

**7** I know a seventh: if I see a hall blaze o'er the comrades within, however fierce the flame, I can save them all. Such is the spell I sing.

**8** I know an eighth: full useful to all, wherever hatred grows among the sons of men, I can heal it swiftly.

**9** I know a ninth: if I need to save my ship in a storm at sea, I still the wind and calm the waves, soothing the sea to sleep.

**10** I know a tenth: if I see witches riding through the air, I can work it so they wander from their skins and wander from their minds.

**11** I know an eleventh: if to battle I lead my old companions, I sing beneath the shields, and with power they go safe to the fight, safe out of the fight, safe on every side.

**12** I know a twelfth: if I see on a tree a corpse swinging from a noose, I can carve and stain the runes so that the man walks and speaks with me.

**13** I know a thirteenth: if on a young man I pour water at his naming, he shall not fall, though he go to battle; no sword shall bring him low.

**14** I know a fourteenth: if before a host I shall speak of the gods, of gods and elves I can tell full well, not the ignorant thus.

**15** I know a fifteenth that Thjodrorir sang before Delling's doors; power he sang for the gods, prowess for the elves, and wisdom for Odin the all-seeing.

**16** I know a sixteenth: if I seek love of a crafty maid, I turn the thoughts of the white-armed woman and change her heart within.

**17** I know a seventeenth: that ne'er will forsake me the maiden fair. Long hast thou listened for these songs, for thy good I spoke them to thee. Thou hast them now, use them if thou learnest them; profit thou if thou hearest them.

**18** I know an eighteenth that I ne'er will tell to maiden or man's wife, save to her alone who clasps me in her arms, or to my sister perchance. The last is always best when known by no one, and thus the songs are ended.
