---
title: "Havamal - Chapter 5"
scripture: "havamal"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Hew wood in wind, row out to sea in fair weather, dally with maidens at dusk, for day's eyes are many. In a ship seek for speed, in a shield for shelter, in a sword for keenness, in a maid for a kiss.

**2** By the fire drink ale, over ice go on skates, buy a horse when 'tis lean, a sword when 'tis rusty; fatten the horse at home, and the hound in thy kennel.

**3** In the words of a maiden or the speech of a woman let no man place his trust; for their hearts were shaped on a whirling wheel and fickleness was fixed in their breasts.

**4** In a breaking bow or a burning flame, in a ravening wolf or a croaking raven, in a rooting swine or a rootless tree, in a rising wave or a boiling kettle:

**5** In a flying dart or a falling billow, in ice one night old or a coiled-up snake, in a bride's bed-talk or a broken sword, in the play of a bear or a prince's child:

**6** In a sick calf or a stubborn thrall, in a witch's flattery or a fresh-slain foe, in a brother's slayer, though seen on the highway, in a half-burned house or a horse too swift: let none his trust in these things place.

**7** Let none put faith in the first sown fruit, nor yet in his son too soon; for weather rules the fruit and wit the son, both of these are doubtful.

**8** The love of women who deceive in heart is like driving a horse unshod over slippery ice, a spirited steed of two winters old and ill-trained; or sailing a rudderless boat in a brisk wind.

**9** I know that I hung on a windy tree nine long nights, wounded with a spear, dedicated to Odin, myself to myself, on that tree of which no man knows from where its roots run.

**10** No bread did they give me nor a drink from a horn, downward I peered; I took up the runes, screaming I took them, then I fell back from there.

**11** Nine mighty spells I learned from the famous son of Bolthor, Bestla's father, and I got a drink of the precious mead, poured from Odhroerir.

**12** Then I began to thrive and wisdom to get, I grew and well I was; each word led me on to another word, each deed to another deed.

**13** Runes shalt thou find and fateful staves, staves very strong, staves very great, which a mighty sage stained and the great gods made, and Odin graved among the gods.

**14** Knowest thou how to write, knowest thou how to read, knowest thou how to stain, knowest thou how to prove, knowest thou how to pray, knowest thou how to offer, knowest thou how to send, knowest thou how to spend?

**15** Better ask for too little than offer too much, like the gift should be the boon; better not to send than to overspend.

**16** Thus Odin graved ere the world began; then he rose from the deep, and came again.
