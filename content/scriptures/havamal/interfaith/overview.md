## Overview

The Hávamál offers rich material for interfaith dialogue through its treatment of wisdom, sacrifice, and ethical conduct. Odin's self-hanging on the World Tree to gain the runes resonates with Christian theology of the Cross, shamanic traditions of death and rebirth, and mystical traditions of self-emptying as a path to knowledge. The poem's practical ethics of hospitality, generosity, and moderation find echoes in the ethical teachings of virtually all major religious traditions, inviting productive dialogue about shared moral values across cultural boundaries.
