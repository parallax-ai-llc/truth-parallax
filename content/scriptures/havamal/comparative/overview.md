## Overview

The Hávamál provides extraordinarily rich material for comparative study. Its gnomic wisdom — proverbs about friendship, moderation, and practical conduct — parallels wisdom literature from across the ancient world, including the Hebrew Book of Proverbs, the Egyptian Instruction of Amenemope, the Greek Works and Days, and the Indian Arthashastra. Odin's self-sacrifice on the World Tree to gain wisdom invites comparison with shamanic initiation traditions, Christ's crucifixion, and other myths of sacrificial knowledge-seeking.
