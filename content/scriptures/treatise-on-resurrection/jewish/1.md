## The Reality of the Resurrection

The opening section reflects the transformation of a distinctly Jewish concept in a Hellenistic Christian context. In the Hebrew Bible, resurrection is consistently bodily: Ezekiel's valley of dry bones (Ezekiel 37), Daniel 12:2, and 2 Maccabees 7. The Treatise preserves the term 'resurrection' but empties it of bodily content — a transformation the rabbis would have viewed as fundamental distortion. The Mishnah's insistence in Sanhedrin 10:1 reflects the centrality of bodily resurrection to normative Judaism.
