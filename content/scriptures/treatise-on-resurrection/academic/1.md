## The Reality of the Resurrection

Scholarly analysis of the opening section situates it within the documented early Christian debate about the nature of resurrection. Second-century sources (Irenaeus, Tertullian) attest that this was a major point of contention. The teacher's argument from Christ's example follows a Pauline pattern (1 Corinthians 15:20-23) but arrives at a non-Pauline conclusion. The text's casual reference to the Savior existing 'in flesh' has generated debate about whether this represents docetism or a more nuanced position.
