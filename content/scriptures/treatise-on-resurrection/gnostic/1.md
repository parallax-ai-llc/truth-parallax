## The Reality of the Resurrection

The opening section addresses Rheginos's doubts about resurrection. The Gnostic teacher begins with the Savior's example: he 'existed in flesh' and 'swallowed death,' demonstrating that the divine can inhabit and transcend material form. The affirmation that the Savior transformed into an imperishable aeon establishes the paradigm for every pneumatic person's awakening. For the Gnostic reader, Rheginos's doubt is itself a symptom of material deception — the illusion that physical death is final.
