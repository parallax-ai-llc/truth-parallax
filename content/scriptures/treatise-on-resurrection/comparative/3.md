## Exhortation and Practice

The concluding exhortation to live without fear of death resonates with the Stoic meditatio mortis, the Epicurean argument that death is 'nothing to us,' the Zen practice of 'great death' before biological death, and the Christian mystical tradition of dying to self. The universal insight: accepting death liberates one to live more fully.
