## The Reality of the Resurrection

The opening section places the reader at the heart of an argument that divided early Christianity. The teacher's affirmation that resurrection is real but not bodily represents a position that many early Christians held but that was eventually excluded from orthodoxy. The argument from Christ's resurrection follows a logic shared with orthodox theology — Christ as 'firstfruits' — but arrives at a different conclusion about what resurrection means.
