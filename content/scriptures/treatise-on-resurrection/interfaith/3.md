## Exhortation and Practice

The exhortation to live without fear of death carries weight across every tradition that takes mortality seriously: the Samurai's Bushido code, the Buddhist monk's daily contemplation of death, Julian of Norwich's 'all shall be well,' and the Stoic memento mori all converge on the insight that accepting death liberates one to live more fully.
