## Overview

The Treatise on the Resurrection addresses what is perhaps the most universal of all religious questions: what happens when we die? Every human culture has developed responses to this question, and the Treatise's answer — that the true self is already beyond death — resonates with contemplative traditions across the world.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Death as Illusion

The Treatise's claim that "the world is an illusion" inverts ordinary assumptions about what is real. This inversion is shared by traditions as diverse as Advaita Vedanta, Platonic philosophy, and certain strands of Sufi thought.

### Present Awakening

The insistence that resurrection is a present event parallels the Buddhist emphasis on awakening as available in every moment, the Sufi teaching that *fana* reveals the already-present divine reality, and the Hindu recognition that the atman is already Brahman.

### The Teacher-Student Relationship

The letter form — a teacher guiding a student through doubt — reflects the universal importance of spiritual mentorship, from the guru-shishya relationship in Hinduism to the pir-murid relationship in Sufism.
