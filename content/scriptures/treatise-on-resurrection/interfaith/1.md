## The Reality of the Resurrection

The portrayal of a student seeking assurance about life beyond death touches every human heart. Across traditions — from Aboriginal Dreamtime narratives to Egyptian afterlife preparations to Buddhist Pure Land traditions — the conviction that death is not final is one of humanity's most persistent beliefs. The Treatise's distinctive contribution is its reframing: 'Do I already have a life that death cannot touch?' — shifting from hope to knowledge, from future to present.
