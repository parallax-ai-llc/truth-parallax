## The Nature of the Spiritual Body

The exploration of body and spirit addresses a tension present in virtually every religious tradition. The Treatise's sharp distinction represents one end of a spectrum including traditions affirming the body as sacred (indigenous religions, Judaism), viewing the body as a vehicle (Yoga, Taoism), and regarding it as an obstacle (Jainism). The ray-of-light metaphor recurs across traditions: the Hindu atman, the Quaker 'that of God in every person,' the Islamic ruh.
