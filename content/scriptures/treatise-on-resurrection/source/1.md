---
title: "Treatise on Resurrection — Section 1: The Reality of Resurrection"
scripture: "treatise-on-resurrection"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Some there are, my son Rheginos, who want to learn many things. They have this goal when they are occupied with questions whose answer is lacking. If they succeed with these, they usually think very highly of themselves.

**2** But I do not think that they have stood within the Word of Truth. They seek rather their own rest, which we have received through our Savior, our Lord Christ.

**3** We received it when we came to know the truth and rested ourselves upon it.

**4** But since you ask us pleasantly about what is proper concerning the resurrection, I am writing you that it is necessary.

**5** Indeed many are lacking faith in it, but there are a few who find it. So then, let us discuss the matter.

**6** How did the Lord proclaim things while he existed in flesh and after he had revealed himself as Son of God? He lived in this place where you remain, speaking about the Law of Nature — but I call it Death.

**7** Now the Son of God, Rheginos, was Son of Man. He embraced them both, possessing the humanity and the divinity, so that on the one hand he might vanquish death through his being Son of God, and that on the other through the Son of Man the restoration to the Pleroma might occur.

**8** For originally he was from above, a seed of the Truth, before this structure of the cosmos had come into being.

**9** In this structure many dominions and divinities came into existence.

**10** I know that I am presenting the solution in difficult terms, but there is nothing difficult in the Word of Truth. But since the Solution appeared so as not to leave anything hidden, but to reveal all things openly concerning existence — the destruction of evil on the one hand, the revelation of the elect on the other.

**11** This is the emanation of Truth and Spirit; Grace is of the Truth.
