---
title: "Dhammapada Atthakatha - Chapter 4"
scripture: "dhammapada-atthakatha"
chapter: 4
---

**1** This is the commentary on the Puppha Vagga, the Chapter on Flowers. The verse "Who shall overcome this earth?" was spoken by the Buddha while dwelling at Savatthi, with reference to five hundred monks who received a meditation subject from the Teacher.

**2** These five hundred monks, having received their meditation objects, went into the forest to practise. But they could not determine which subject was suited to their temperaments. They returned to the Buddha, who perceived that a teaching using the simile of flowers would penetrate their minds. He then asked: "Who shall overcome this earth, and the world of Yama? Who shall find out the plainly shown path of virtue, as a clever man finds out the right flower?"

**3** The verse "He who knows that this body is like froth" was spoken with reference to a number of monks who, while crossing the Ganges, saw a great mass of foam upon the water. The Buddha, seeing this as an occasion for teaching, said: "Monks, this body is like that foam — insubstantial and unreal. He who understands this will break the flower-tipped arrows of Mara."

**4** The verse "Death carries off a man who is gathering flowers" was spoken with reference to the inhabitants of Savatthi. The Buddha, surveying the world at dawn, saw that many people were about to die who had spent their lives distracted by pleasures, never reflecting on the Dhamma. He taught that death comes upon such men like a flood upon a sleeping village.

**5** The verse on the bee collecting nectar was spoken with reference to the monks who went on alms round in the city. The Buddha taught them: "As the bee takes nectar without harming the flower, so should a monk move through the village, taking only what is offered, without attachment and without causing harm."

**6** The verses on the scent of virtue were spoken with reference to the Elder Ananda. Ananda asked the Buddha: "Lord, the scent of flowers travels only with the wind. Is there a scent that travels against the wind?" The Buddha replied: "Ananda, the fragrance of virtue is carried in every direction. The fame of one who is virtuous, who is generous, who practises the Dhamma — this travels even against the wind, pervading every quarter."

**7** The verses on the lily growing on a heap of rubbish were spoken with reference to Garahadinna, a treasurer who lived near a refuse heap. Despite his lowly surroundings, he heard the Dhamma from the Buddha and attained Stream-entry. The Buddha declared that just as a sweet lotus can grow from a heap of rubbish cast on the highway, so the disciple of the Buddha shines with wisdom among the spiritually blind.
