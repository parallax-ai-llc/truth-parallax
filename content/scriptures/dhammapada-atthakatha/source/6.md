---
title: "Dhammapada Atthakatha - Chapter 6"
scripture: "dhammapada-atthakatha"
chapter: 6
---

**1** This is the commentary on the Pandita Vagga, the Chapter on the Wise Man. The verse "If you see an intelligent man who tells you where true treasures are to be found" was spoken by the Buddha while dwelling at Savatthi, with reference to the Elder Radha.

**2** The Elder Radha was an old brahmin who desired ordination but was refused by the monks because of his advanced age. The Buddha, perceiving with his divine eye that Radha had great capacity for attainment, asked whether any monk recalled a favour received from Radha. The Elder Sariputta said that Radha had once given him a spoonful of rice when he was on alms round. The Buddha said: "Is it not fitting, Sariputta, to repay so great a kindness?" Sariputta then ordained Radha.

**3** Under the guidance of the Elder Sariputta, Radha practised with great diligence and quickly attained Arahatship. He was always obedient, always receptive to instruction, never resentful of correction. The Buddha, praising Radha's attitude, spoke the verse: "If you see an intelligent man who tells you where true treasures are to be found, who shows what is to be avoided, follow that wise man."

**4** The verse "Do not have evil-doers for friends" was spoken with reference to the Elder Channa, who was proud and quarrelsome, associating with monks of similar temperament. The Buddha counselled the monks to choose their companions wisely, for the company one keeps shapes the character as surely as water shapes the stone.

**5** The verse "As a solid rock is not shaken by the wind" was spoken with reference to the Elder Lakuntaka Bhaddiya, who was very small in stature. Young monks would tease him, pulling his nose and ears and calling him names. But Bhaddiya never became angry, never harboured resentment. The Buddha declared that Bhaddiya was like a solid rock, unmoved by blame or praise.

**6** The verse "Wise people, after they have listened to the laws, become serene, like a deep, smooth, and still lake" was spoken after a great assembly had heard the Dhamma. The Buddha observed that those who truly receive the teaching become calm and clear, as a deep lake becomes still and transparent.

**7** The verse "Few are there among men who arrive at the other shore" was spoken with reference to a group of monks who discussed the difficulty of the path. The Buddha confirmed that few indeed cross to the further shore; the many merely run up and down the near bank, never venturing the crossing.

**8** The final verses on leaving the dark state for the bright state were spoken with reference to monks who had given up worldly life and were practising in seclusion. The Buddha encouraged them, teaching that those whose minds are grounded in the seven factors of enlightenment, who cling to nothing, are free even in this world.
