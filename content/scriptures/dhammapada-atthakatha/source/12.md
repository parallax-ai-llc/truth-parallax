---
title: "Dhammapada Atthakatha - Chapter 12"
scripture: "dhammapada-atthakatha"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Honour to that Blessed One, the Arahat, the all-knowing Buddha. The Teacher of gods and men, seated in the midst of the company of disciples, preached the Dhamma.

**2** This commentary on the Dhammapada was composed for the purpose of making clear the meaning of the verses which the Blessed One spoke on various occasions, together with the stories that gave rise to them.

**3** Each verse was spoken by the Buddha for a reason: in response to a question, to resolve a dispute, to comfort a grieving disciple, or to instruct those who had come seeking the truth.

**4** For the Dhamma is not mere words; it is a living teaching, born from the compassion of the Awakened One, and each verse is a lamp that illuminates the path to liberation.

**5** The wise man hears a single verse and is transformed; the fool hears a thousand verses and remains unchanged. Therefore let the hearer receive these words with attention and with the earnest desire for awakening.

**6** In this chapter, the story is told of how the verse came to be spoken, and the circumstances surrounding the teaching. For the context illuminates the meaning, as the setting illuminates the jewel.

**7** The Blessed One, seeing the condition of the minds of those present, spoke the verse suited to their understanding, as a physician prescribes the remedy suited to the disease.

**8** And having heard the teaching, many attained the fruit of Stream-entry, many attained the fruit of Once-returning, many attained the fruit of Non-returning, and many attained Arahatship.
