Academic analysis of the commentary on the Violence (Dandavagga) chapter examines the narrative techniques, historical assumptions, and literary conventions that shape its treatment of the harm caused by violence and the blessing of non-harm toward all living beings. Scholars study how the commentary constructs narrative contexts for the Dhammapada's verses, often drawing on common story elements (type-scenes, stock characters, formulaic descriptions) that suggest the influence of oral tradition. The relationship between the commentary's stories and parallel narratives in other Buddhist collections provides evidence for reconstructing the development of Buddhist narrative literature.

Scholarly attention to this chapter includes textual analysis of the Pali prose style, comparison with the parallel narratives in Sanskrit and Chinese sources, and examination of the commentary's role in shaping Theravada hermeneutical practice. The commentary's blend of doctrinal instruction and popular storytelling reflects the dual function of Buddhist commentary as both scholarly exegesis and popular religious literature.
