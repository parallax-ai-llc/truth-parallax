## Overview

The Dhammapada Atthakatha (Dhammapada Commentary) is the traditional Theravada commentary on the Dhammapada, attributed to the great commentator Buddhaghosa (5th century CE) though likely drawing on earlier Sinhalese sources. For each of the Dhammapada's 423 verses arranged across 26 chapters, the commentary provides a narrative context — typically a story from the Buddha's life explaining the circumstances that prompted each verse. These stories make the Dhammapada Atthakatha one of the richest collections of Buddhist narrative literature and a primary source for understanding how early Buddhists interpreted and applied the Dhammapada's ethical teachings.

## Structure

The 26 chapters follow the structure of the Dhammapada itself:

| Chapters | Theme |
|----------|-------|
| 1-3 | Mind, heedfulness, and mental discipline |
| 4-9 | Ethics, wisdom, and the consequences of action |
| 10-13 | Violence, aging, self, and the world |
| 14-17 | The Buddha, happiness, attachment, and anger |
| 18-20 | Purity, justice, and the path |
| 21-26 | Miscellaneous, hell, endurance, craving, the monastic life, and true nobility |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Dhammapada Atthakatha has influenced Buddhist literature across traditions:

- **Theravada**: The definitive commentary on the Dhammapada, shaping its interpretation for centuries
- **Mahayana**: Its narrative material has been compared with Mahayana avadana literature
- **Zen**: Its stories provide accessible Buddhist teachings appreciated across traditions
- **Tibetan**: Its ethical narratives parallel the Tibetan tradition of illustrative stories
- **Academic**: A primary source for the study of early Buddhist narrative literature and ethics

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Narrative Context | Stories explaining the circumstances behind each Dhammapada verse |
| Karma | Vivid illustrations of the workings of karmic causation |
| Moral Exemplars | Portraits of monks, nuns, laypeople, and the Buddha as ethical models |
| Community Life | Glimpses into the daily life of the early Buddhist community |
| Conversion Stories | Accounts of how individuals came to the dharma |
| Moral Psychology | Detailed analysis of mental states through narrative illustration |
