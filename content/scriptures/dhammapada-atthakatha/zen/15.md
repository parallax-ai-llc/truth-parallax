Zen engagement with the commentary on the Happiness (Sukhavagga) chapter appreciates the directness and immediacy of the Buddha's teaching encounters depicted in the stories. The commentary's narratives on true happiness as arising from inner peace rather than external circumstances frequently depict moments of sudden realization prompted by the Buddha's precisely targeted words — a pattern that resonates with the Zen tradition of the master's spontaneous, situation-specific teaching. The stories' emphasis on personal transformation through direct encounter parallels the Zen understanding of dharma transmission.

Zen readers value these stories not as historical records but as living expressions of the dharma's power to awaken. The brevity and directness of the Dhammapada's verses, illuminated by the commentary's narrative contexts, reflect the economy of expression that Zen prizes.
