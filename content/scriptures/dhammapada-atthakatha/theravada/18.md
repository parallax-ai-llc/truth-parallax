The Theravada commentary on the Impurity (Malavagga) chapter provides detailed narrative contexts for each verse, transforming the Dhammapada's concise teachings on moral impurity and the necessity of continuous purification through mindful practice into vivid stories from the Buddha's ministry. Buddhaghosa's commentary typically begins each story by identifying the occasion — a dispute among monks, a layperson's question, or a moral crisis in the community — and then shows how the Buddha's verse addressed the specific situation while conveying a universal ethical principle.

For Theravada practitioners, these stories serve as a treasury of moral exemplars and cautionary tales. The commentary's detailed accounts of karmic consequences — showing how specific actions lead to specific results across lifetimes — reinforce the Theravada understanding of moral causation and provide motivation for ethical conduct. The stories are widely used in Theravada sermons and popular Buddhist education throughout Southeast Asia.
