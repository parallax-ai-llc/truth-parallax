## Overview

Mahayana engagement with the Dhammapada Atthakatha recognizes it as part of the broader pan-Buddhist tradition of narrative literature that includes the avadana, jataka, and apadana genres. While the commentary is a distinctively Theravada work, its stories of karma, moral transformation, and the Buddha's compassionate teaching resonate with Mahayana values and parallel narrative collections preserved in Chinese and Tibetan canons.

## Comparative Study

Mahayana scholars have compared the Dhammapada Atthakatha's stories with parallel narratives in the Divyavadana, the Avadanashataka, and other Mahayana narrative collections. These comparisons reveal a shared Buddhist narrative heritage while illuminating the distinctive emphases of each tradition.

## Appreciation

The commentary's vivid illustrations of karma and moral causation, its portraits of the Buddha's compassion and skillful teaching, and its emphasis on the transformative power of the dharma are fully appreciated in Mahayana contexts, even where the specific doctrinal framework differs.
