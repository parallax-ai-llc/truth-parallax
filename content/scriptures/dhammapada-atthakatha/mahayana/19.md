Mahayana engagement with the commentary on the The Just (Dhammatthavagga) chapter recognizes the shared Buddhist ethical heritage while noting the commentary's Theravada orientation. The stories illustrating true justice and righteousness as rooted in wisdom and impartiality resonate with Mahayana values of compassion and wisdom, even where the specific doctrinal framework differs. Parallel narratives in Mahayana avadana collections — the Divyavadana, Avadanashataka, and others — often tell similar stories with distinctive Mahayana emphases.

Mahayana readers appreciate the commentary's vivid illustrations of karma and moral transformation as expressions of foundational Buddhist ethics that all traditions share. The Buddha's compassionate engagement with each person's specific situation, as depicted in these stories, exemplifies the skillful means (upaya) that Mahayana tradition regards as a defining quality of Buddhahood.
