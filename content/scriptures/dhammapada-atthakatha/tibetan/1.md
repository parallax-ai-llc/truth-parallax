Tibetan engagement with the commentary on the Twin Verses (Yamakavagga) chapter draws parallels with the Tibetan tradition's own extensive use of ethical narratives in spiritual education. The stories illustrating the primacy of mind and the paired consequences of wholesome and unwholesome intention find counterparts in Tibetan lamrim texts, which similarly use narrative to make doctrinal points vivid and memorable. The commentary's detailed illustrations of karmic causation align with the Tibetan lamrim teaching on karma and its results.

Tibetan readers appreciate the commentary's comprehensive approach to moral education through narrative. The Tibetan tradition's own Udanavarga commentary and various avadana collections preserved in the Kangyur contain parallel stories, confirming the shared Buddhist narrative heritage while revealing distinctive traditions of storytelling and moral instruction.
