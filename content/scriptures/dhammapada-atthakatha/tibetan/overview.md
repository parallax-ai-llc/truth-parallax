## Overview

Tibetan Buddhist engagement with the Dhammapada Atthakatha finds resonance with the Tibetan tradition's own rich literature of ethical narratives. The Tibetan Kangyur and Tengyur contain extensive avadana and jataka collections that parallel the commentary's stories, and the lamrim (graduated path) tradition makes extensive use of illustrative narratives to make doctrinal points concrete and memorable.

## Comparative Study

Tibetan scholars have noted parallels between the commentary's stories and narratives preserved in the Tibetan tradition, including the Udanavarga commentary and various avadana collections. These parallels confirm the shared Buddhist narrative heritage while revealing how different traditions adapted and developed common story material.

## Pedagogical Function

The Dhammapada Atthakatha's use of stories to illustrate karma, ethical conduct, and spiritual transformation mirrors the Tibetan tradition's own extensive use of narrative in moral education. Both traditions recognize the power of story to communicate ethical truths more effectively than abstract instruction.
