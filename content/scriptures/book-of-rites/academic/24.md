Academic scholars approach Chapter 24, Ai Gong Wen (Duke Ai Asks I), with attention to questions of dating, authorship, and the chapter's relationship to other texts in the Liji compilation. Critical analysis of the vocabulary, grammar, and conceptual framework suggests composition during the late Warring States or early Han period, though some prescriptions may preserve genuinely archaic practices. The chapter's treatment of Duke Ai provides valuable evidence for reconstructing the social history and institutional practices of pre-imperial and early imperial China.

Scholars have noted that the chapter's prescriptions regarding Confucius dialogue can be compared with evidence from excavated texts and archaeological sites. These comparisons sometimes confirm and sometimes contradict the received text, revealing a complex process of idealization, systematization, and editorial revision. The academic reading seeks to recover the historical reality behind the text's normative prescriptions, distinguishing between descriptions of actual practice and projections of an idealized ritual order.
