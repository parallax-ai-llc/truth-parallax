The Da Xue has been the subject of intense academic scrutiny, particularly regarding its original form and meaning before Zhu Xi's influential reorganization. Modern scholars have noted that the text as it appears in the Liji differs significantly from Zhu Xi's rearranged version, which became the standard for centuries. The original order of the text, without Zhu Xi's supplementary chapter on gewu (investigating things), presents a somewhat different intellectual program — one focused more on political action and less on epistemological method.

Scholars have dated the Da Xue to the late Warring States or early Han period, identifying conceptual connections with the legalist-influenced statecraft of that era. The text's program of moving from personal cultivation to political order reflects the Warring States concern with the relationship between inner virtue and effective governance. The academic reading thus recovers a Da Xue that is less a purely philosophical text and more a practical guide for officials seeking to integrate moral self-cultivation with political responsibility.
