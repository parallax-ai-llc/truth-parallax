Historical-critical scholarship on Chapter 40, Da Zhuan II (Great Commentary), situates the text within the broader intellectual landscape of the Warring States and Han periods. The chapter's discussion of grand commentary reflects concerns and terminology that scholars associate with specific schools and periods of Chinese thought. Comparative analysis with parallel passages in other ritual texts — including the Yi Li and the Zhou Li — reveals both shared traditions and distinctive emphases that illuminate the editorial processes behind the Liji's compilation.

The chapter's treatment of cosmic order has been examined through the methods of social history, revealing assumptions about gender, class, and political structure that reflect the particular conditions of the text's composition. Academic interpreters note that the chapter's prescriptions, while presented as timeless truths, encode the specific values and interests of the literate elite who composed and transmitted these texts. This critical perspective does not diminish the text's significance but situates it within its proper historical context.
