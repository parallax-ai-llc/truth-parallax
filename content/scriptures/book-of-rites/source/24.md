## Ji Yi (The Meaning of Sacrifices)

Sacrifice is not something coming from without, but issues from within, being born in the heart. When the heart is deeply moved, it finds expression in ceremony.

Therefore, only men of ability and virtue can give full expression to the meaning of sacrifice. The sacrifices of such men have their own blessedness -- not what the world calls blessedness. What the world calls blessedness means the completeness of five things: long life, riches, peace, the love of virtue, and an end crowning the life. What is meant by the blessedness of sacrifice is completeness of reverence.

The sacrifice in the suburb and the temple had three important features: That Heaven and Earth were served in it showed the greatness of its object. That ancestors were served in it showed the feeling of intimacy in it. That rulers and teachers were served in it showed that those who have instructed us receive their due.

Confucius said: "In his sacrifice, the filial son wishes his parents to enjoy the spiritual essence. He sets out the vessels of bamboo and wood. He presents the offerings of silk and fine fabric. He adjusts the music of strings and singing to promote harmony."

The sacrificer approaches and treats the matter with reverence and gravity, as if he could see those to whom the sacrifice is offered -- such is the visible form of the invisible spirit.

When a man is sincere and true in sacrificing, the spirits of the departed come and enjoy his offerings.
