## Sang Fu Xiao Ji (Record of Smaller Matters in Mourning Dress)

For the first grade of mourning, the coarsest cloth is used, without being hemmed at the edges. The cap is also of the coarsest cloth, and is tied under the chin with cords of hemp.

The mourner eats only rice gruel in the morning and evening. He does not eat vegetables or fruit.

When a father dies, the son may not have his hair dressed for three months. He wears a headband of coarsest cloth. The girdle is of twisted hemp.

In the second grade of mourning, the cloth is less coarse, with edges hemmed. The wearer drinks water only and eats no vegetables. He wails day and night, and does not regulate the times of wailing.

After the burial, the mourner puts on the cap. The change of garments gradually becomes lighter as the period advances.

For a mother, the same mourning is worn as for a father. But when the father is still alive, the mourning for a mother is reduced in degree.

When a friend dies, if there is no one to take charge of the funeral arrangements, the mourner says: "I will see to his burial." He then takes charge of the interment in its various details.

The rules about mourning say that the grief in the first year expresses the utmost distress. The regulations for food and drink, for rest and dwelling, during the three years, provide a graduated series for the natural expression of grief.
