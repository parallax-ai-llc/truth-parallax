## Yue Ji (Record of Music)

All musical sounds arise from the mind of man. The feelings move inwardly, and are embodied in sounds. When the sounds take definite form, they are called musical notes. When the notes are combined according to rules, they become music.

Therefore, the music of a well-governed age is calm and cheerful, and so is its government orderly. The music of a restless age is excited and fierce, and its government perverted. The music of a decaying state is sorrowful and full of care, and its people are in distress.

The ancient kings, understanding this, founded the teaching of ceremonies and music so that men might be regulated. When music prevails and reaches its full effect, there is no feeling of dissatisfaction. When ceremonies prevail and have their full effect, there is no strife or contention.

Music rises from the heart, and ceremonies have their origin in what is external. Since music rises from within, it produces stillness of the heart. Since ceremonies come from without, they produce the beauty of form.

Great music shares the same principles with heaven and earth. Great rites share the same structure with heaven and earth. Through the principles of music, heaven and earth are in harmony; through the structure of rites, heaven and earth are in order.

Music expresses the harmony of the universe, while rites express its order. Through harmony, all things are influenced; through order, all things have a proper place. Music comes from heaven; rites are shaped by earthly designs.
