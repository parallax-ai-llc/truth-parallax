## Hun Yi (The Meaning of the Marriage Ceremony)

Marriage is a rite which unites two persons of different surnames in friendship and love, for the purpose of serving the ancestral temple on the one hand and of continuing the family line on the other.

The ceremonial usages connected with marriage: First, there is the proposal; second, the asking of the name; third, the divination for the auspiciousness of the match; fourth, the engagement; fifth, the fixing of the date; sixth, the meeting of the bride.

The six ceremonials constitute the completeness of the marriage rite.

When the bridegroom goes to welcome the bride, he wears the dark square-topped cap. He goes to the house of the bride's father, who comes out to receive him. The bridegroom presents the wild goose he has brought, and the bride's father conducts him in.

After the bride arrives at the bridegroom's house, they eat together and drink from the same gourd cup. This indicates the union of the two.

On the second day the wife presents herself to her parents-in-law. She takes her place at the base of the steps and performs a libation in their honor.

The significance of the marriage rites is this: They teach the value of the bond between husband and wife. From this bond springs the relation between father and son. From the relation between father and son springs that between ruler and minister. Thus marriage is the root of all human relations and the foundation of civilization itself.
