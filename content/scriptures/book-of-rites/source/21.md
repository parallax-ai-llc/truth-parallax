## Za Ji II (Miscellaneous Records, Part 2)

Confucius said: "When I look at the customs of the various states, their rules about mourning clothing show many corruptions of the ancient practice."

Zengzi said: "The grief expressed in mourning should accord with the feeling of affection. The deceased should be honored according to his rank. The mourning should be regulated according to the relationship."

When parents are alive, a son should not presume to make himself known beyond his own neighborhood. When parents die, the mourning period is the time for him to show the sincerity of his affection.

The ancient kings established the mourning rites: For a father, three years. For a mother, three years. For a husband, three years. For a ruler, three years. These formed the heaviest mourning.

For brothers, one year. For uncles and cousins, nine months or five months, according to the degree of relationship.

Confucius said: "In the mourning for a parent, when the occasion for wailing has passed, one who still wails is excessively demonstrative. When the occasion for weeping has passed, one who still weeps is not discerning. Therefore the rules of propriety define a limit for all things."

The ceremonial practices for the dead follow the principle: Treat the dead as if they were living, the departed as if they were still present. Serve them as you served them when alive.
