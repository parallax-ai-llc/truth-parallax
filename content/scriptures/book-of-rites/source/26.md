## Jing Jie (The Meaning of the Marriage Ceremony)

The ceremony of marriage was intended to be a bond of love between two families of different surnames, with a view, in its retrospective character, to secure the services in the ancestral temple, and, in its prospective character, to secure the continuance of the family line.

Therefore the superior men, the ancient rulers, set a great value upon it. Hence, in regard to the ceremony: The man goes in person to meet the bride. When she arrives, he gives her a cup of wine. They eat together of the same sacrificial animal. These are the great points in the ceremony.

The man goes himself to meet the bride. This shows the feeling of affection and the principle of following, and of putting the strong in a position of leading the weak. This is the first lesson of the rules of propriety.

When a son-in-law goes to meet his bride, her father receives him with ceremony outside the gate. The father gives his daughter's hand and says: "Be careful and attentive. Serve your father-in-law and mother-in-law with care."

The woman takes up her position inside the gate, and the man goes out of it. They thereby show the separation that should be maintained between husband and wife.

From the distinction between husband and wife comes the affection between father and son; from that affection, the rectitude between ruler and minister. Whence it is said: The ceremony of marriage is the root of the other ceremonies.
