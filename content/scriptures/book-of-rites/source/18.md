## Xue Ji (Record of Education)

When a jade is cut and polished, it becomes a thing of use. When a man is educated, he knows the Way. Therefore the ancient kings, when establishing states and governing the people, placed education and schools foremost.

The Record of Teaching says: "When a ruler wishes to transform the people, must he not begin with education?" And again: "If you wish to know the effect of education, look at it as compared with what it has wrought."

Although a man may have good food, if he does not eat, he does not know its taste. Although there may be the best Way, if one does not learn it, he does not know its goodness. Therefore, only after learning does one know one's deficiency, and only after teaching does one know the difficulty.

After knowing one's deficiency, one is able to examine oneself; after knowing the difficulty, one is able to strengthen oneself. Therefore it is said: Teaching and learning promote each other.

The way of learning may be compared to the growth of plants. It must be gradual and orderly. The good teacher leads but does not drag; he strengthens but does not discourage; he opens the way but does not take the student to the end.

Leading without dragging produces harmony. Strengthening without discouraging makes learning easy. Opening the way without taking the student to the end makes the student think. He who produces harmony, easy progress, and thoughtfulness may be called a skillful teacher.
