## Biao Ji (The Black Robes)

Confucius said: "The superior man acts before he speaks, and afterwards speaks according to his actions."

Confucius said: "The superior man, in his relation to the world, has neither inclinations nor aversions. He is simply on the side of what is right."

Confucius said: "Faithfulness is the foundation of all right government. Ceremonies are what give it form. Its practice lies in the hands of the scholars; its perfection in the hands of the ruler."

Confucius said: "The ruler should consider himself as the parent of the people. He should select the worthy and raise them to office; he should be gracious to the people and kind to them; he should encourage them in the cultivation of the ground and the rearing of animals; he should guide them in the exercise of the ceremonial usages."

Confucius said: "The heart of benevolent man lies in righteousness, and it is clothed in propriety. In this way he lives, in this way he feeds, in this way he moves."

Confucius said: "The Way does not travel far from men. If a man pursues a course that is far from the common indications of consciousness, it should not be considered as the Way."

Confucius said: "If the superior man be not grave, he will not call forth reverence, and his learning will not be solid."
