## Kong Zi Xian Ju (Confucius at Home at Leisure)

Confucius, being at home at leisure, had Zeng Shen sitting by his side. The Master said: "Shen, do you know what it was by which the ancient kings secured the good condition of all under heaven, so that the people lived in peace with one another, and there were no complaints from high or low against their superiors?"

Zeng Shen rose from his mat and said: "I am not clever enough to know this."

Confucius said: "It was propriety. Propriety determines the relations of the noble and the mean; it discriminates the marks of the old and the young; it sets out what is appropriate between the poor and the rich, the small and the great."

Confucius continued: "A ruler should not be without propriety, for without it he cannot govern. An officer should not be without it, for without it he cannot maintain order. A man should not be without it, for without it he cannot live in society."

Confucius said: "Let me explain the five supreme forms of propriety: they are those that govern the relations between ruler and minister; between father and son; between husband and wife; between elder and younger brother; and between friends."

"These five are the supreme forms of propriety under heaven. The maintenance of life, the nourishing of the living, the mourning for the dead, the sacrificing to the departed -- these five supreme forms of propriety complete the duty of man."

This chapter summarizes the entire teaching: that propriety is the foundation upon which civilized human life rests.
