## Tan Gong II (Discourses on Mourning, Part 2)

Confucius said: "The practice of not making the coffin thick, not making the grave mound large, and not planting trees around it, began with the ancients."

Duke Ai asked Zai Wo about the altars to the spirits of the land. Zai Wo replied: "The Xia sovereign planted the pine tree about them; the men of Yin planted the cypress; and the men of Zhou planted the chestnut tree, meaning thereby to cause the people to be in awe."

Zengzi said: "Carefully attend to the rites for the dead and pursue the sacrifices for the departed ancestors. Then the virtue of the people will resume its proper excellence."

Zi You asked: "Is mourning for three years not already too long? If the superior man abstains for three years from the observances of propriety, those observances will be quite lost. If he abstains for three years from music, music will be ruined." Confucius replied: "After a year the mourner can eat rice and wear embroidered silk. Would you feel at ease in doing so?" "I should," was the reply. Confucius said: "If you can feel at ease, do it. But a superior man, during the whole period of mourning, does not enjoy pleasant food nor find happiness in music."

When Zi You was governor of Wu Cheng, there was the mourning for one of his officers. Confucius said: "Well done! Zi You is generous in his conduct of mourning."
