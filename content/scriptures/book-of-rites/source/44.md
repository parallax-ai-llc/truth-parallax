## Yan Yi (The Meaning of the Feast)

The rites of the feast: The ruler faces south. The ministers are seated facing north. The guests take the eastern positions, and the host the western.

The feast embodies the meaning of the relationship between ruler and minister. The ruler fills the cup and offers it to the minister; the minister receives it with reverence. The minister drinks and returns the cup; the ruler accepts it. In this exchange, the mutual duties and mutual respect between ruler and minister find expression.

In the feast, the ruler does not descend from the hall to welcome anyone below the rank of minister. For a minister, he descends one step. For a great officer, he descends two steps. This illustrates the distinctions of rank.

The music of the feast is joyful but not licentious. The drinking is regulated but not excessive. When the guests are properly pleased and have drunk an appropriate amount, the feast comes to its conclusion.

The courtesy of the feast consists in the host refilling the cup of the guest. The guest, after drinking, rises and offers the cup back to the host. This back-and-forth exchange illustrates the principle of reciprocity.

When the feast is concluded, the host sees the guests to the door. He bows to them and they depart.

The purpose of the feast was to illustrate the proper relations between superiors and inferiors, to promote social harmony, and to express gratitude.
