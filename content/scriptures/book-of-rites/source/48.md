## Ai Gong Wen (The Questions of Duke Ai)

Duke Ai asked Confucius: "What is the great principle of rites?"

Confucius replied: "Of all things that affect the interests of the people, there is none greater than the rites."

Duke Ai said: "I beg to ask in what the great importance of rites consists."

Confucius said: "The rites are the means by which the ancient kings sought to represent the ways of Heaven, and to regulate the feelings of men. He who neglects or violates them may be spoken of as dead, and he who observes them as alive."

Duke Ai said: "I would venture to ask how the rites are to be carried out?"

Confucius said: "The observance of rites depends on the character of the ruler. When the ruler loves propriety, the people are easy to command. When the ruler himself is courteous, the people respond with courtesy."

Confucius continued: "Government is like wind, and the people are like grass. When the wind blows, the grass bends."

Duke Ai asked: "How should I govern?"

Confucius said: "A husband and wife should be distinguished from each other. A father and son should show affection to each other. A ruler and minister should show respect to each other. These three things being rightly maintained, all other things will follow."

Duke Ai said: "Though I am without much knowledge, I will try to carry out what you have said."
