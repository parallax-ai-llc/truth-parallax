## Ru Xing (The Conduct of the Scholar)

Duke Ai of Lu asked Confucius: "Is the dress you wear that of a scholar?"

Confucius replied: "When I was young, I lived in Lu, and I wore the wide-sleeved garment of Lu. When I grew up, I lived in Song, and I was capped with the zhang-fu cap. I have heard that the learning of the scholar is extensive, and his dress follows the customs of his state. I do not know what scholar's dress you mean."

Duke Ai said: "I venture to ask about the conduct of the scholar."

Confucius replied: "If I were to enumerate his points hastily, I could not finish them; if I tried to give them in detail, they would remain after I had spoken till day's end. Allow me to set forth some of the more important ones."

"The scholar has a precious gem within his bosom, and he waits to be asked about it. He is loyal and wishes to benefit others; he is kind and wishes to encourage them. He considers kindness to be his wealth; ceremonial practice to be his domain; he does not seek abundant possessions."

"He is mild yet firm; simple yet distinguished. He observes others to regulate himself. He eats to satisfy hunger and dresses to ward off cold. He has no desire for display. This is the conduct of the scholar."

"The scholar will not go where there is peril for the sake of gain. He values his life but values righteousness more."
