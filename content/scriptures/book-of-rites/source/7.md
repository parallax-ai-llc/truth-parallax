## Zeng Zi Wen (The Questions of Zengzi)

Zengzi asked: "Can you tell me whether in the rites of mourning it is the grief that is the more important, or the observance of the forms?"

Confucius said: "In the rites of mourning, it is better that there be deep grief than minute attention to observances."

Zengzi asked: "How should a man conduct himself so as to be in accordance with the practice of an ancient sage?"

Confucius said: "A man should be able, by his own reflection, to know what is right. When one is in a humble position and yet does not serve his superiors, he cannot be kept in a humble position. When one wishes to serve his superiors, and does not first apply himself to serve those below him, he cannot serve his superiors."

Zengzi said: "I venture to ask whether all the ceremonies in the mourning rites must be observed."

Confucius said: "Of all the methods for the good ordering of men, there is none more urgent than the use of ceremonies. Ceremonies are of five kinds, and there is none of them more important than sacrifices."

Zengzi asked about the duty of a minister. Confucius said: "You ask of a minister! The greatest duty is faithfulness to his ruler. If the ruler has faults, the minister should admonish him. If the admonition be not listened to, the minister should leave his service."
