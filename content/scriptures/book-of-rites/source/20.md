## Za Ji I (Miscellaneous Records, Part 1)

When Zhuge was mourning for his mother, Confucius asked Zi Gong to go and observe. Zi Gong reported: "He wails as if he had a child who was sick."

Confucius said: "This is excessive. In the rites of mourning, doing too much is as bad as doing too little."

It is said: When a man is mourning for a parent, after the placing of the body in the coffin, he should not go beyond the gate. After the burial, he does not go beyond the neighborhood.

In the mourning rites, there is what is called a light affliction and a heavy affliction. The light affliction pertains to distant relations; the heavy, to near ones.

When a ruler goes to condole with a mourner, on entering the gate he should dismount from his carriage. When he arrives at the coffin, he should weep and express his sympathy. He must not stay for the full ceremony unless the deceased was a close relation.

The rule for condolence visits: If the family be poor, one should assist with the expenses. If the mourner be in difficulty, one should help him with the arrangements. Among friends, the surviving friend should take care of the funeral.

When the Master heard of the death of Bo Niu, he went to the door and looked in. He said: "It is killing him! It is the appointment of heaven, alas! That such a man should have such a sickness!"
