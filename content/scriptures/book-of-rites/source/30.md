## Zi Yi (The Embroidered Robe)

Confucius said: "The great Way is not far from man. When men try to pursue a course which is far from the common indications of consciousness, this course cannot be considered the Way."

Confucius said: "In archery we have something like the way of the superior man. When the archer misses the center of the target, he turns round and seeks the cause of his failure within himself."

Confucius said: "The virtues displayed by the superior man are like wind; those displayed by the small man are like grass. The grass must bend when the wind blows upon it."

The Master said: "The services of rites and music should not be neglected for a single day. If one cultivates himself in learning on the inside and practices rites on the outside, then the people can be transformed."

Confucius said: "The sovereign may not neglect the cultivation of his own character. Wishing to cultivate his character, he may not neglect serving his parents. In order to serve his parents, he may not neglect acquiring the knowledge of men. In order to know men, he may not neglect a knowledge of Heaven."

Confucius said: "He who possesses sincerity is he who, without an effort, hits what is right, and apprehends without the exercise of thought. He is the sage who naturally and easily embodies the right way. He who attains to sincerity is he who chooses what is good and firmly holds it fast."
