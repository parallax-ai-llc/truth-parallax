## She Yi (The Meaning of the Archery Ceremony)

The ancient kings made use of archery not merely to test skill, but to observe the character and conduct of men.

In archery there must be a correct posture; and the archer, when he takes his position, must be composed. After he is composed, he may then take aim. After taking aim, he may then shoot. In shooting, the archer reveals his character.

Confucius said: "How shall the archer avoid missing the mark? In archery we have something like the way of the superior man. When the archer misses the center of the target, he turns round and seeks the cause of his failure in himself."

Therefore the sage kings selected officers through archery. Those who hit the mark were approved; those who missed were rejected. The arrow flying straight and true was the visible token of the archer's inner rectitude.

The ceremony of archery was performed with music. The archers shot in time with the music. If one shot before the beat, his eagerness betrayed him. If one shot after the beat, his slowness betrayed him. He who shot in exact time with the music was composed and correct.

The Son of Heaven used targets with bears and elks depicted upon them. The feudal princes used targets with deer. The great officers used targets with tigers and leopards.

Through the archery ceremony, the sages tested a man's composure under pressure, his capacity for concentration, and his respect for form. These were the qualities needed in governance.
