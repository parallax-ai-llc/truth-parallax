## Shao Yi (Smaller Rules of Demeanor)

When sitting in attendance on a man of rank, if the host yawn, stretch himself, ask for his slippers, look about the time of night, or begin to eat -- the visitor should ask to be allowed to retire.

When sitting in attendance on a man of rank, one should not occupy the mat to its full extent. If one occupies it fully, he appears to be neglectful.

If a visitor be of lower rank, he should withdraw with his face toward the host. If of the same rank, he may withdraw with his back turned.

In conversing with a ruler, one should point to the sun and say "the day is such and such"; in conversing with a great officer, one should refer to events for the purpose of fixing the date.

When one is sitting at meat with another, he should not bolt his food. When eating with the hand, he should not throw the rice into a ball. He should not eat largely of the dishes set before the guest. He should not suck his fingers.

To give a meal to a poor man, one should not fill his dish so full that the food is piled above the edge. One who gives must keep to the manner of a host and not demean the guest.

The superior man does not try to speak with cleverness. He should be slow to speak and earnest in conduct.
