## Fu Wen (Subjects for Questions about the Mourning Dress)

The subjects for questions about the mourning dress: What is the rule about mourning dress for a stepmother? The reply was: The mourning for a stepmother is the same as for a mother, in its degree and duration.

What about mourning for a divorced mother? The mourning is reduced in degree, because the family bond has been severed.

What about mourning for a concubine-mother? The mourning follows a set of specific rules that differ from those for the principal wife.

The principle behind all mourning dress is this: The nearer the relationship, the heavier the mourning. The coarser the material of the garment, the deeper the grief it signifies. The finer the material, the lighter the mourning.

For the heaviest mourning, the material is rough and unhemmed. The staff is of bamboo. One eats only gruel and drinks only water.

For lesser mourning, the material is somewhat refined. One eats vegetables and fruit. One wails at set times during the day.

For the lightest mourning, one wears hemmed garments of finer material. One returns to ordinary food. One weeps when thinking of the departed.

These graduated forms serve to express the natural hierarchy of affection: closest for parents, extending outward to siblings, cousins, and more distant relations. Each degree has its proper garment, its proper food, and its proper duration.
