## Jian Zhuan (Treatise on Intervals of Mourning)

The treatise says: The mourning of three years is the highest expression of grief. The sackcloth with the jagged edges, the staff, the eating of congee, the dwelling in the mourning hut -- these are the means by which the grief of three years finds its expression.

At the end of twenty-five months, the great sacrifice of repose is offered, and the mourner returns to his ordinary dress. This is the completion of the three years' mourning.

The mourning of one year: The period commences from the announcement and ends at the sacrifice offered at the close of the mourning year. The garments are of somewhat less coarse material.

The mourning of nine months: This mourning is worn for certain more distant relations. The time is counted from the announcement to the conclusion of the ninth month.

The mourning of five months: This is the mourning for still more distant relations. The garments are lighter.

The mourning of three months: This is the lightest period. It is worn for the most distant relations for whom any mourning is required.

The ancient kings, in establishing these rules, considered the relationships of men in society: the five relations of ruler and minister, father and son, husband and wife, elder and younger brother, friend and friend. Each of these relationships carries with it appropriate observances in times of loss.

The graduated periods of mourning correspond to the natural diminishing of grief over time.
