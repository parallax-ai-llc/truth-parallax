## San Nian Wen (Questions about the Three Years' Mourning)

Some ask: What is the principle underlying the three years' mourning? The reply is: This period is prescribed because the grief demands it.

Confucius said: "A child is not separated from the arms of its parents until it is three years old. Three years' mourning is therefore the universal rule throughout the world. Did not the child enjoy the three years' love of his parents?"

Zai Wo asked: "Is not the mourning of one year long enough? If the superior man abstains for three years from the observances of propriety, those observances will be quite lost. If he abstains for three years from music, music will be ruined."

The Master said: "After one year, could you eat rice, wear fine clothes, and feel at ease?"

"I could," was the reply.

The Master said: "If you can feel at ease, do it. But the superior man, during the whole period of mourning, does not enjoy pleasant food which he may eat, nor derive pleasure from music which he may hear, nor feel at ease in a comfortable dwelling. Therefore he does not do what you propose."

When Zai Wo went out, the Master said: "How lacking in virtue is Yu! It is not until a child is three years old that it leaves the arms of its parents. The three years' mourning is universally observed throughout the world. Did Yu enjoy the three years' love of his parents?"
