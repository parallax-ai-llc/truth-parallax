## Ping Yi (The Meaning of the Exchange of Missions)

The ceremonies of international visits served to maintain good relations between states and to express respect between rulers.

When one ruler sent an envoy to another, the envoy carried a jade token as proof of his mission. The jade symbolized the virtue and sincerity of the sending ruler.

On the arrival of the envoy, the receiving ruler sent a lesser official to meet him outside the boundary. This was to show respect. The envoy was escorted into the state and provided with lodging and provisions.

When the envoy presented himself at court, he offered the jade and delivered his lord's message. The receiving ruler accepted the jade with appropriate ceremony and entertained the envoy with a banquet.

Before the envoy departed, return gifts were presented. The envoy carried back to his lord the tokens of amity and good will.

The significance of these ceremonies was: They prevented conflicts between states by maintaining regular and respectful communication. They demonstrated that even between states of different power, the principles of propriety and mutual respect must prevail.

When these ceremonies were properly observed, the feudal states lived in harmony. When they were neglected or corrupted, strife and warfare followed.

The ancient kings understood that international relations, like personal relations, must be governed by rites -- by formal expressions of respect, sincerity, and good faith.
