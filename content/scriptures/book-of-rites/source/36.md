## Shen Yi (The Meaning of the Deep Garment)

The deep garment had its origin with the ancient rulers. It could be worn at court, in the army, or while mourning. It was complete and covered the whole body. Hence its name, the deep garment.

The rules for making it: The upper garment was made of six strips of cloth. The lower garment was made of twelve strips. The twelve strips represented the twelve months of the year. The upper garment was round at the sleeves -- round like a compass. The lower garment was straight at the seam -- straight like the carpenter's square.

The cord of the sash hung down -- like a plumb line. The back of the garment, when erect, was straight -- like the marking board. Therefore the deep garment symbolized the principles of Heaven in its roundness, Earth in its squareness, and Man in his uprightness.

The garment was bordered along the edge, to express the idea of exactness and correctness.

It might be worn by the ruler or the common man, by the old or the young. It was the garment for all occasions. When a man put on the deep garment, he was reminded by the compass-roundness at the sleeves to embrace all men; by the square-straightness at the seam to act with righteousness; by the plumb-line cord to maintain integrity; by the straight back to keep the heart upright.

Thus even clothing became a means of moral cultivation.
