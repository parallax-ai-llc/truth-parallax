## Yu Zao (The Jade-Bead Cap)

The son of Heaven, when sacrificing, wore the cap of the jade beads. At the morning audience he wore the dark-colored square-topped cap. He wore the leather cap when going to meet visitors outside the gate. When hunting he wore the white cap.

The ministers and great officers wore the dark square-topped cap when sacrificing. The cap of the dark-colored silk was worn at the regular audience at court.

The cap with its appendages gave its wearer an imposing appearance. The sash served to tuck in the lower garment. These were the two things for which the ancient kings showed the greatest concern.

In all the thirteen articles of dress there was not one which was not expressive of some meaning. The cap was round to represent heaven; the shoes were square to represent earth. The sash hung down to represent the principle of straightforwardness.

For the girdle there was no fixed rule as to its size. It might be large or small. Its function was to gather and hold in the garment.

In the matter of dress, the Son of Heaven wore the complete set of twelve ornamental symbols. The prince wore nine. The high minister seven. A lower officer five.

The cap strings, when idle, hung down five inches. This expressed the idea of government being close to those over whom it ruled.
