## Wen Wang Shi Zi (King Wen as Son and Heir)

King Wen, as son and heir, served his father King Ji. Every day at cockcrow he dressed himself and went to wait outside the door of the inner apartment. When the attendant reported that King Ji was up, King Wen went in with a pleasant expression to inquire about his health.

When King Ji was well, King Wen retired and saw to the preparation of the various delicacies for his father's meals. He examined what these would be, and when they were placed before his father, he would taste them first himself.

If King Ji ate well, he looked pleased; if King Ji did not eat well, he looked anxious. When the meal was over, he asked what King Ji would like, and then gave orders to the attendants to provide it.

The lessons for the heir-son were: He must be able to carry on the ancestral sacrifices, and be competent to act as ruler. He was required to learn all the usages of the court.

The education of the heir-son began with the right use of language. When he was able to eat by himself, he was taught to use his right hand. When he was able to speak, he was taught to respond boldly and clearly.

In the education of the heir-son, there was the teaching at the proper seasons. When he entered the Great College, he was placed below the national scholars, that he might learn to respect the wise.
