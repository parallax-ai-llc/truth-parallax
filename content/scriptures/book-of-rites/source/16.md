## Da Zhuan (The Great Treatise)

The rites of the ancestral temple are the means by which kinship is maintained in order. The arrangement of noble and mean shows the structure of the body politic. The ordering of offices shows what each man is to do. The feasting at the conclusion shows who are intimate and who distant.

In managing affairs, the first consideration is given to propriety and righteousness, and the second to profit. Therefore, the superior man is ashamed to seek profit without reference to righteousness.

When the Great Way prevails, the world is shared by all alike. The worthy and the able are promoted. Sincerity is practiced and harmony cultivated.

The sacrifices in the suburban temple were the greatest of all sacrificial rites. These are the means by which the virtue of the ruler reaches up to Heaven above and down to Earth below.

The succession of fathers and sons, of elder brothers and younger brothers, and the distinctions of near and distant kin, are all traced through the ancestral temple.

The rites of succession and inheritance were designed so that the world would know the bonds of kinship. Therefore the wise kings of antiquity emphasized genealogical records and maintained the ancestral temple with the utmost care.

Thus the ancestral temple is the root; the suburban sacrifices are the branches. These institutions together form the foundation of government.
