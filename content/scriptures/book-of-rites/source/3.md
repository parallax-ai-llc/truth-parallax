## Tan Gong I (Discourses on Mourning, Part 1)

When Confucius was young and fatherless, he did not know the grave of his father. He buried his mother by the road at the junction of the Five Fathers. Those who passed said, "This indeed is a grave." Its appearance suggested it was merely a mound. Then Confucius learned the place of his father's grave from the mother of Man Fu, and proceeded to bury his mother alongside his father.

Confucius said: "I have heard that the inner coffin should be four inches thick, and the outer one five inches."

When Yan Yuan died, his father Yan Lu begged the carriage of Confucius to furnish an outer shell for his son's coffin. Confucius said: "Whether a man be talented or otherwise, each speaks of his own son. When Li died, he had an inner coffin but no outer shell. I could not walk on foot to furnish him with one, because I follow in the rear of the great officers, and it is not proper for me to walk on foot."

Zengzi said: "When a man has not yet been buried, the friends should supply all that is needed for the mourning rites. After he is buried, the ruler should supply it."

Confucius said: "To treat the dead as dead would show want of affection, and should not be done; to treat the dead as living would show a want of wisdom, and should not be done."
