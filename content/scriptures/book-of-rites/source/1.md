## Qu Li I (Summary Rules of Propriety, Part 1)

The Rules of Propriety say: "Always show reverence; appear as if thinking deeply; speak quietly and gently."

The course of propriety leads the people; music brings about harmony among them; government directs their actions; punishments prevent their evil deeds. When propriety, music, punishments, and government achieve their aims without error or confusion, the principles of kingly rule are complete.

Pride should not be allowed to grow; the desires should not be indulged; the will should not be gratified to the full; pleasure should not be carried to excess.

Men of talents and virtue can be familiar with others and yet respect them; can stand in awe of others and yet love them. They love others and yet acknowledge the evil that is in them; they accumulate wealth and yet are able to part with it; they rest in what gives them satisfaction and yet can seek satisfaction elsewhere. When you find wealth within your reach, do not try to get it by improper means.

Do not be dissolute in your manner; do not be one-sided in discussion; do not be sharp in retort.

According to the rules of propriety for a feast, the guest should take his place on the left, and the host on the right. The guest holds the cup of liquor with his face turned toward the place where the vessels are kept.
