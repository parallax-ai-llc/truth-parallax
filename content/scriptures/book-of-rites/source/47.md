## Da Zhuan (The Great Summary)

The great summary of rites begins: Rites proceed from heaven. The movement of them reaches to earth. They are distributed through all the business of life.

The rites bring about order in the sacrifices; propriety and elegance in the court; harmony in the village community; honesty in the management of affairs.

Rites are rooted in heaven, patterned on the earth, and practiced by men. They imitate the movements of the seasons, the progression of sun and moon, the arrangement of the stars. They take the forms of mountains and rivers, of animals and plants.

The three hundred rules of ceremony and the three thousand rules of demeanor wait for the proper man, and then they are practiced.

Therefore it is said: Unless a man has reached the fullness of virtue and the highest principle of the Way, the perfect rules of ceremony will not be fully realized.

The Odes say: "The virtuous man -- what does he desire? The virtuous man -- he is the pattern of the people."

The Master said: "Respect without the rules of propriety becomes laborious bustle. Caution without the rules of propriety becomes timidity. Boldness without the rules of propriety becomes insubordination. Straightforwardness without the rules of propriety becomes rudeness."

Therefore the superior man respects and reveres, puts things in order, follows the principles -- and the people of the world are at peace.
