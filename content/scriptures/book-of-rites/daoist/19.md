The Record of Music presents a theory of music's power to harmonize human emotions with cosmic rhythms that resonates deeply with Daoist cosmology. Daoist readers find in this chapter a shared understanding that sound and silence, movement and stillness, participate in the fundamental patterns of yin and yang. The chapter's recognition that music arises from the human heart's response to external stimuli echoes the Daoist insight that authentic expression flows from natural resonance rather than calculated intention.

However, Daoist interpreters diverge from the Confucian reading when the chapter prescribes specific musical forms as morally superior to others. The Daoist perspective holds that all natural sounds participate equally in the Tao, and that the hierarchical ranking of music into proper and improper forms reflects the very kind of artificial discrimination that separates human beings from their original nature. The most authentic music, from a Daoist standpoint, is the music that requires no composition — the wind in the pines, the flowing of water, the silence between sounds.
