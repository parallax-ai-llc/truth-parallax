Daoist interpreters read Chapter 14, Ming Tang Wei (Hall of Distinction), as a document of the very condition that Daoism seeks to overcome. The elaborate attention to architecture reflects what Laozi called the proliferation of "beautiful words" and "clever devices" that accompanies the loss of the Tao. Where the Confucian tradition sees civilizing refinement, Daoism sees the accumulation of artifice that distances human beings from their original connection to the natural order.

The chapter's discussion of ritual space provides Daoist readers with a useful contrast to their own approach. Where the Liji prescribes specific behaviors for specific situations, Daoism counsels responsiveness to the unique demands of each moment. The Daoist sage does not consult a manual of proper conduct but acts from a deep attunement to the situation that no set of rules could capture. This chapter thus serves the Daoist tradition as a negative example — demonstrating what happens when a culture substitutes codification for wisdom.
