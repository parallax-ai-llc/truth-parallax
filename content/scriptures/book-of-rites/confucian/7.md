Zengzi's questions to Confucius in this chapter reveal the master-disciple dynamic at the heart of Confucian moral transmission. Confucian readers regard Zengzi as a model of filial piety and earnest inquiry, and his questions about mourning rites, parental devotion, and the proper conduct of a gentleman reflect the tradition's deepest concerns. The dialogue format allows the chapter to explore ritual principles through specific cases rather than abstract generalizations.

The Confucian interpretation emphasizes Zengzi's role as one of the principal transmitters of Confucius's teachings, particularly regarding filial piety. His questions are not those of a novice but of a mature disciple seeking to refine his understanding of complex ritual situations. The chapter demonstrates that Confucian learning is an ongoing process of inquiry and refinement, never a finished accomplishment, and that even the most devoted student must continually test his understanding against the master's deeper wisdom.
