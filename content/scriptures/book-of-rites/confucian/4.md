Tan Gong II continues the collection of mourning narratives, providing additional examples and prescriptions that deepen the Confucian understanding of ritual grief. This chapter includes some of the most famous anecdotes in the Confucian tradition, including stories of Confucius himself responding to the deaths of students and associates. Confucian readers mine these narratives for guidance on how to calibrate mourning behavior to different relationships and circumstances.

The chapter's treatment of burial rites reflects the Confucian emphasis on dignifying the deceased while maintaining practical wisdom. The debates recorded here about the appropriate level of funeral expenditure reveal a tradition grappling with the tension between honoring the dead lavishly and avoiding wasteful excess. Confucian interpreters find in these discussions a model of ritual reasoning — the careful weighing of competing values that characterizes mature moral judgment.
