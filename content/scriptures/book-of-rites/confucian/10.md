The Ritual Implements chapter describes the material objects used in ceremonial practice, from sacrificial vessels to musical instruments. Confucian interpreters read this chapter as demonstrating that ritual propriety extends to the physical world — that the objects employed in ceremony must themselves embody the principles of order, hierarchy, and appropriateness. Each implement carries symbolic meaning, and using the wrong vessel or instrument undermines the efficacy of the rite.

For the Confucian tradition, the careful attention to material culture reflects the conviction that inner virtue must be expressed through external forms. The vessels used in sacrifice are not mere containers but symbols of the worshipper's sincerity and the cosmic order being invoked. The chapter's detailed specifications for different types of implements corresponding to different ranks and occasions illustrate the Confucian principle that proper differentiation is essential to genuine harmony.
