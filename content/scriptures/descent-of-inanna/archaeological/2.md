Lapis lazuli was one of the most precious materials in ancient Mesopotamia, imported over vast distances from mines in northeastern Afghanistan through complex trade networks. Archaeological evidence of lapis lazuli objects at sites across Mesopotamia — from the Royal Tombs of Ur to Nippur and Uruk — confirms the material's association with divinity and royalty that the literary text reflects.

Measuring rods and lines are attested in Mesopotamian temple-building rituals, where the gods symbolically measure out the dimensions of sacred structures. A copper measuring rod was among the finds at Nippur, and representations of deities holding measuring implements appear on cylinder seals.
