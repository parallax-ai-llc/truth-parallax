Breastplates and pectoral armor are known from Late Bronze and Iron Age contexts across the ancient Near East, though examples from the Sumerian period are rare. The literary description may reflect either actual armor or the ornamental chest pieces found on divine statues. Temple inventories from the first millennium BCE describe elaborate pectoral ornaments placed on cult images of Ishtar.

Metal breastplates and scale armor have been found at sites including Nuzi, Alalakh, and Ugarit — contemporary with the Akkadian version of the Descent. These finds confirm that the concept of chest protection, whether practical or ceremonial, was well established in the ancient Near Eastern material world.
