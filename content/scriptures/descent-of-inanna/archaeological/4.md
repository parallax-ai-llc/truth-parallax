Breast ornaments and pectoral jewelry are well documented in Mesopotamian archaeology. Terracotta plaques from the Old Babylonian period frequently depict female figures — possibly representing Ishtar/Inanna — wearing prominent breast ornaments. Stone and metal breast pendants have been found at sites including Ur, Uruk, and Assur.

The term *nunuz* also connects to the egg-shaped beads found in abundance at Mesopotamian sites, sometimes in specifically ritual or funerary contexts. Their association with fertility and the feminine divine is supported by their frequent occurrence in female burials and in deposits associated with the Ishtar cult.
