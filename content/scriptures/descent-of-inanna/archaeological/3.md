Lapis lazuli necklaces are among the most common luxury items found in Mesopotamian archaeological contexts. The Royal Tombs of Ur yielded spectacular examples of gold and lapis lazuli necklaces, and similar items appear in temple inventories describing the adornment of divine statues. The text's description of Inanna's necklace likely reflects actual cultic practice in which statues of the goddess were adorned with precious jewelry.

The practice of adorning divine statues (*salmu*) with clothing, jewelry, and cosmetics is well attested in Mesopotamian ritual texts. The annual ritual of undressing and redressing divine images may provide a cultic context for the literary motif of Inanna's stripping.
