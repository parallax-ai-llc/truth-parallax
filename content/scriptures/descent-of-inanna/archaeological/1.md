The first gate of the Descent references the *shugurra* crown, one of Inanna's defining items of regalia. Archaeological parallels for elaborate headdresses have been found in the Royal Tombs of Ur (c. 2600–2400 BCE), where Queen Puabi's gold and lapis lazuli headdress represents the type of crown that the literary tradition may have had in mind. The association of crowns with divine and royal authority is well attested in Mesopotamian glyptic art, where deities are distinguished from mortals primarily by their horned crowns.

The tablets preserving this section derive primarily from Nippur's scribal quarter, where Old Babylonian student scribes copied literary texts as part of their training. Minor textual variants between copies suggest the process of transmission and the challenges of reconstructing a single authoritative text.
