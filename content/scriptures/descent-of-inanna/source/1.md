**Section I: Inanna's Decision to Descend**

**1** From the Great Above she opened her ear to the Great Below.
**2** From the Great Above the goddess opened her ear to the Great Below.
**3** From the Great Above Inanna opened her ear to the Great Below.

**4** My lady abandoned heaven and earth to descend to the underworld.
**5** Inanna abandoned heaven and earth to descend to the underworld.
**6** She abandoned her office of holy priestess to descend to the underworld.

**7** In Uruk she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.
**8** In Badtibira she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.
**9** In Zabalam she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.
**10** In Adab she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.
**11** In Nippur she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.
**12** In Kish she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.
**13** In Akkad she abandoned her temple to descend to the underworld.

**14** She gathered together the seven me.
**15** She took them into her hands.
**16** With the me in her possession, she prepared herself:

**17** She placed the shugurra, the crown of the steppe, on her head.
**18** She arranged the dark locks of hair across her forehead.
**19** She tied the small lapis beads around her neck.
**20** She let the double strand of beads fall to her breast.

**21** She wrapped the royal robe around her body.
**22** She daubed her eyes with ointment called "Let him come, let him come."
**23** She bound the breastplate called "Come, man, come!" around her chest.
**24** She slipped the gold ring over her wrist.

**25** She took the lapis measuring rod and line in her hand.

**26** Inanna set out for the underworld.
**27** Ninshubur, her faithful servant, went with her.

**28** Inanna spoke to Ninshubur, her faithful servant:
**29** "O Ninshubur, my constant support,
**30** my sukkal who gives me wise advice,
**31** my warrior who fights by my side,

**32** I am descending to the kur, to the underworld.
**33** If I do not return,
**34** set up a lament for me by the ruins.
**35** Beat the drum for me in the assembly places.

**36** Circle the houses of the gods.
**37** Tear at your eyes, at your mouth, at your thighs.
**38** Dress yourself in a single garment like a beggar.
**39** Go to Nippur, to the temple of Enlil.

**40** When you enter his holy shrine, cry out:
**41** 'O Father Enlil, do not let your daughter
**42** be put to death in the underworld.
**43** Do not let your good metal
**44** be covered with the dust of the underworld.

**45** Do not let your good lapis lazuli
**46** be broken up into the stone of the stoneworker.
**47** Do not let your boxwood
**48** be cut up into the wood of the woodworker.

**49** Do not let the holy priestess of heaven
**50** be put to death in the underworld.'

**51** If Enlil will not help you,
**52** go to Ur, to the temple of Nanna.
**53** Weep before Father Nanna.

**54** If Nanna will not help you,
**55** go to Eridu, to the temple of Enki.
**56** Weep before Father Enki.

**57** Father Enki, the Lord of Wisdom,
**58** knows the food of life, he knows the water of life;
**59** he will surely bring me back to life."
