**Section VI: Dumuzi is Chosen**

**1** In Uruk, by the big apple tree,
**2** Dumuzi, the husband of Inanna,
**3** was dressed in his shining me-garments.
**4** He sat on his magnificent throne; he did not move.

**5** The galla seized him by his thighs.
**6** They poured milk out of his seven churns.
**7** They broke the reed pipe which the shepherd was playing.

**8** Inanna fastened on Dumuzi the eye of death.
**9** She spoke against him the word of wrath.
**10** She uttered against him the cry of guilt:
**11** "Take him! Take Dumuzi away!"

**12** The galla, who know no food, who know no drink,
**13** who eat no offerings, who drink no libations,
**14** who accept no gifts, seized Dumuzi.

**15** They made him stand up; they made him sit down.
**16** They beat the husband of Inanna.
**17** They gashed him with axes.

**18** Dumuzi let out a wail.
**19** He raised his hands to the sky, to Utu, the God of Justice:
**20** "O Utu, you who are my brother-in-law,
**21** I am the husband of your sister.
**22** I brought cream to your mother's house,
**23** I brought milk to Ningal's house.

**24** Turn my hands into the hands of a snake.
**25** Turn my feet into the feet of a snake.
**26** Let me escape from my demons.
**27** Let them not hold me."

**28** Utu accepted his tears.
**29** Utu changed the hands of Dumuzi into snake-hands.
**30** Utu changed the feet of Dumuzi into snake-feet.
**31** Dumuzi escaped from his demons.

**32** [Dumuzi fled to the sheepfold of his sister Geshtinanna.]

**33** But the galla flew after him.
**34** The galla, the demons of the underworld, are relentless;
**35** they know no mercy.

**36** They followed the fleeing Dumuzi to the sheepfold.
**37** They searched for him among the goats and sheep.
**38** The small galla struck the large galla.
**39** The large galla struck the small galla.

**40** They searched for Dumuzi.
**41** "Look in the house! Look in the field!
**42** He has fled from us!"

**43** Dumuzi dreamt a dream.
**44** He wept and cried out to his sister Geshtinanna:
**45** "My dream! O my sister, my dream!

**46** In my dream, rushes rise all about me;
**47** rushes grow thick about me.
**48** A single-growing reed trembles for me.
**49** From a double-growing reed, one is removed.

**50** In the wooded grove, tall trees rise fearsome about me.
**51** Over my holy hearth, water is poured.
**52** My holy churn—its milk is carried off.
**53** The cup fallen from its peg,
**54** my shepherd's crook has vanished.

**55** An eagle seizes a lamb from the sheepfold.
**56** A falcon catches a sparrow on the reed fence.
**57** My sister, your goats drag their lapis lazuli beards in the dust.
**58** Your sheep scratch the earth with bent feet."

**59** Geshtinanna spoke:
**60** "My brother, your dream is not favourable.
**61** Dumuzi, your dream is not favourable.
**62** The rushes which rise all about you
**63** are the galla who will pursue you.

**64** The single-growing reed which trembles for you—
**65** it is your mother who bore you, trembling for you.
**66** The tall trees of the wooded grove—
**67** they are the galla who will seize you.

**68** When water is poured over your holy hearth,
**69** the sheepfold will become a house of desolation.
**70** The cup fallen from its peg—
**71** you will fall from your mother's lap.

**72** The eagle that seizes a lamb—
**73** the galla will seize you.
**74** The falcon that catches a sparrow—
**75** the galla will catch you.

**76** My brother, I will go with you to the underworld.
**77** I will be your surety.
**78** Go now, hide yourself among my friends."
