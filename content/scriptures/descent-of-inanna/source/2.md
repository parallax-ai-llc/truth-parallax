**Section II: The Seven Gates**

**1** Inanna walked toward the underworld.
**2** When she arrived at the outer gates of the underworld,
**3** she knocked loudly.

**4** She cried out in a fierce voice:
**5** "Open the door, gatekeeper!
**6** Open the door, Neti!
**7** I alone would enter!"

**8** Neti, the chief gatekeeper of the kur, asked:
**9** "Who are you?"

**10** "I am Inanna, Queen of Heaven,
**11** on my way to the East."

**12** Neti said:
**13** "If you are truly Inanna, Queen of Heaven,
**14** on your way to the East,
**15** why has your heart led you on the road
**16** from which no traveller returns?"

**17** Inanna answered:
**18** "Because of my older sister, Ereshkigal.
**19** Her husband, Gugalanna, the Bull of Heaven, has died.
**20** I have come to witness the funeral rites.
**21** Let the beer of his funeral rites be poured into the cup.
**22** Let it be done."

**23** Neti, the chief gatekeeper of the kur,
**24** spoke to the queen of the underworld:
**25** "My queen, a maid,
**26** tall as heaven, wide as the earth,

**27** who wears the shugurra, the crown of the steppe, on her head,
**28** who is clothed with the royal robe about her body,
**29** who wears the breastplate called 'Come, man, come!'
**30** at the outer gate—it is Inanna."

**31** Ereshkigal slapped her thigh and bit her lip.
**32** She took the matter into her heart and dwelt on it.
**33** Then she spoke:

**34** "Come, Neti, my chief gatekeeper of the kur.
**35** Heed my words:
**36** Bolt the seven gates of the underworld.
**37** Then, one by one, open each gate a crack.

**38** Let Inanna enter.
**39** As she enters, remove her royal garments.
**40** Let the holy priestess of heaven enter bowed low."

**41** Neti heeded the words of his queen.
**42** He bolted the seven gates of the underworld.
**43** Then, one by one, he opened each gate a crack.

**44** At the first gate he removed the shugurra, the crown of the steppe, from her head.
**45** "What is this?"
**46** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**47** They may not be questioned."

**48** At the second gate he removed the small lapis beads from around her neck.
**49** "What is this?"
**50** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**51** They may not be questioned."

**52** At the third gate he removed the double strand of beads from her breast.
**53** "What is this?"
**54** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**55** They may not be questioned."

**56** At the fourth gate he removed the breastplate called "Come, man, come!" from her chest.
**57** "What is this?"
**58** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**59** They may not be questioned."

**60** At the fifth gate he removed the gold ring from her wrist.
**61** "What is this?"
**62** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**63** They may not be questioned."

**64** At the sixth gate he removed the lapis measuring rod and line from her hand.
**65** "What is this?"
**66** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**67** They may not be questioned."

**68** At the seventh gate he removed the royal robe from her body.
**69** "What is this?"
**70** "Quiet, Inanna, the ways of the underworld are perfect.
**71** They may not be questioned."

**72** Naked and bowed low, Inanna entered the throne room.
