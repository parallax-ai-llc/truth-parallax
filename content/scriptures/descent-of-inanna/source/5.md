**Section V: The Ascent and the Demons**

**1** Inanna was about to ascend from the underworld
**2** when the Anunnaki seized her:
**3** "No one ascends from the underworld unmarked.
**4** If Inanna wishes to return from the underworld,
**5** she must provide someone in her place."

**6** As Inanna ascended from the underworld,
**7** the galla, the demons of the underworld, clung to her side.
**8** The galla were demons who know no food, who know no drink,
**9** who eat no offerings, who drink no libations,
**10** who accept no gifts.

**11** They enjoy no lovemaking.
**12** They have no sweet children to kiss.
**13** They tear the wife from the husband's arms,
**14** they tear the child from the father's knees,
**15** they steal the bride from her marriage home.

**16** The demons clung to Inanna.
**17** The small galla who accompanied Inanna
**18** were like reeds the size of low picket fences.
**19** The large galla who accompanied Inanna
**20** were like reeds the size of high picket fences.

**21** The one who walked in front of Inanna was not a minister,
**22** yet he carried a sceptre.
**23** The one who walked behind her was not a warrior,
**24** yet he carried a mace.

**25** Ninshubur, dressed in a soiled sackcloth,
**26** waited outside the palace gates.
**27** When she saw Inanna
**28** surrounded by the galla,
**29** she threw herself in the dust at Inanna's feet.

**30** The galla said:
**31** "Walk on, Inanna,
**32** we will take Ninshubur in your place."

**33** Inanna cried:
**34** "No! Ninshubur is my constant support.
**35** She is my sukkal who gives me wise advice.
**36** She is my warrior who fights by my side.

**37** She did not forget my words.
**38** She set up a lament for me by the ruins.
**39** She beat the drum for me at the assembly places.
**40** She circled the houses of the gods.

**41** She tore at her eyes, at her mouth, at her thighs.
**42** She dressed herself in a single garment like a beggar.

**43** Alone, she set out for Nippur, for Ur, for Eridu.
**44** Because of her, my life was saved.
**45** I will never give Ninshubur to you."

**46** The galla said:
**47** "Walk on, Inanna,
**48** we will go with you to Umma."

**49** In Umma, at the holy shrine,
**50** Shara, the son of Inanna,
**51** was dressed in a soiled sackcloth.
**52** When he saw Inanna
**53** surrounded by the galla,
**54** he threw himself in the dust at her feet.

**55** The galla said:
**56** "Walk on to your city, Inanna,
**57** we will take Shara in your place."

**58** Inanna cried:
**59** "No! Not Shara!
**60** He is my son who sings hymns to me.
**61** He is my son who cuts my nails and smooths my hair.
**62** I will never give Shara to you."

**63** The galla said:
**64** "Walk on, Inanna,
**65** we will go with you to Badtibira."

**66** In Badtibira, at the holy shrine,
**67** Lulal, the son of Inanna,
**68** was dressed in a soiled sackcloth.
**69** When he saw Inanna
**70** surrounded by the galla,
**71** he threw himself in the dust at her feet.

**72** The galla said:
**73** "Walk on to your city, Inanna,
**74** we will take Lulal in your place."

**75** Inanna cried:
**76** "No! Not Lulal!
**77** He is my son. He is a leader among men.
**78** He is my right arm. He is my left arm.
**79** I will never give Lulal to you."

**80** The galla said:
**81** "Walk on to your city, Inanna.
**82** We will go with you to the big apple tree in Uruk."
