The gold ring as a symbol of authority, identity, and covenant appears across ancient traditions. The biblical narrative of Joseph's investiture with Pharaoh's signet ring (Genesis 41:42) and the prodigal son's reception of a ring (Luke 15:22) use the ring as a symbol of restored authority. In Norse mythology, Odin's ring Draupnir multiplies itself, symbolizing generative divine power.

The removal of Inanna's ring at the fifth gate parallels the stripping of authority in political traditions: the deposition of a monarch often involves the removal of the crown, scepter, and ring — the same sequence the Descent enacts on a cosmic scale.
