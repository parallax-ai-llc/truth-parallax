The breast stones of Inanna connect to a broader tradition of pectoral adornments with divine significance across the ancient world. The Israelite high priest's breastplate (*hoshen*) bearing twelve stones (Exodus 28:15-30) served as an oracle of divine judgment. Egyptian deities wear pectoral ornaments as signs of divine protection. The removal of such an intimate ornament marks an escalation in vulnerability.

In the Akkadian Descent of Ishtar, the corresponding item is described as a breast ornament that embodies the goddess's feminine power — its removal strips Ishtar of her capacity to inspire desire, causing all sexual activity on earth to cease.
