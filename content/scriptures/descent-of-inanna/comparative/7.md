The seventh gate — Inanna naked before Ereshkigal — is one of the most powerful scenes in ancient literature. The stripping is complete; the goddess stands before death with nothing. This scene has been compared to:

- The Egyptian weighing of the heart, where the deceased stands before Osiris stripped of all earthly pretense
- The Greek myth of Persephone seized by Hades — though Persephone does not go willingly
- The Christian tradition of Christ stripped before crucifixion and the theological concept of kenosis at its most radical

The Akkadian version adds a striking detail absent from the Sumerian: during Ishtar's absence in the underworld, all sexual desire on earth ceases — bulls will not mount cows, men and women avoid each other. This cosmic consequence of the goddess's captivity is paralleled by the agricultural consequences of Persephone's descent in the Greek tradition.
