The lapis lazuli measuring rod and line connect to a widespread ancient Near Eastern association between divine authority and the power of measurement. In Egyptian tradition, Thoth records the weighing of the heart against Ma'at's feather; in the Hebrew Bible, God measures out the heavens with a span (Isaiah 40:12). The removal of these instruments at the second gate symbolizes the stripping of Inanna's judicial and cosmic authority.

The use of lapis lazuli as the material of divine instruments has cross-cultural significance. Sourced primarily from Badakhshan (modern Afghanistan), lapis lazuli was one of the most valued materials in the ancient Near East, associated with the heavens and the divine in Mesopotamian, Egyptian, and Indus Valley traditions.
