The lapis lazuli necklace as a symbol of divine beauty and identity connects to broader patterns in ancient mythology. In the Greek tradition, the necklace of Harmonia was a divine gift that brought both beauty and curse — a parallel to the ambivalent nature of Inanna's regalia. In Hindu tradition, Shiva's necklace of skulls (*mundamala*) signals his sovereignty over death.

The progressive removal of jewelry at each gate parallels initiation rituals across cultures in which adornment is stripped away to reveal an essential or transformed identity. This pattern is found in mystery religions from Eleusis to the Mithraic mysteries.
