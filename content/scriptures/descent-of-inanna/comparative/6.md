The breastplate as divine protection connects to the warrior aspect of Inanna/Ishtar. In the Akkadian tradition, Ishtar is explicitly a war goddess, and her armor is both literal and symbolic. The biblical concept of the 'breastplate of righteousness' (Ephesians 6:14, Isaiah 59:17) draws on the same symbolic tradition of divine armor protecting the vital organs.

The removal of protective armor before entering the underworld parallels the universal mythological recognition that the defenses of life are useless against death. Achilles' armor, divinely made, does not save him; nor does Inanna's divinely crafted breastplate protect her in Ereshkigal's realm.
