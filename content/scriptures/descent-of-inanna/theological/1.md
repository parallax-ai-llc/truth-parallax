The first gate of the Descent introduces a fundamental theological paradox: the Queen of Heaven voluntarily approaches the realm of death, and the first thing demanded of her is the symbol of her sovereignty. The removal of the *shugurra* crown raises the question of whether divine power is inherent or conferred. If it can be removed at a gate, it is not intrinsic — it belongs to the realm from which the wearer comes.

The gatekeeper's formula — 'the decrees of the underworld are perfect' (*me kur-ra*) — asserts that the underworld has its own legitimate order, not subordinate to the order of heaven. This theology of dual sovereignty, in which different realms operate under different but equally valid laws, stands in tension with monotheistic traditions that assert a single divine order.
