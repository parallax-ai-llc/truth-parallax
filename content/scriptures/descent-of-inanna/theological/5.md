The gold ring carries associations of binding, covenant, and identity that resonate across theological traditions. Its removal at the fifth gate strips Inanna of her capacity to bind — to make covenants, seal agreements, and exercise authority through the act of ratification.

In a theological framework where divine power is exercised through binding decrees (the *me*), the loss of the ring represents the loss of performative authority — the ability to make things happen through declaration and seal. This is power at its most fundamental: not the appearance of authority but its mechanism.
