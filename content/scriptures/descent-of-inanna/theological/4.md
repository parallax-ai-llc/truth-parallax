The sparkling stones at Inanna's breast represent the most intimate stripping so far. These ornaments lie close to the heart — the seat of emotion and will in ancient Near Eastern thought. Their removal symbolizes the stripping of Inanna's innermost divine nature: her role as goddess of love, desire, and fertility.

Theologically, the fourth gate marks the midpoint of the seven-gate journey and a crossing from external to internal stripping. The first three gates removed symbols visible to all; the breast stones are more intimate, more personal. The underworld demands not only the appearance of power but its substance.
