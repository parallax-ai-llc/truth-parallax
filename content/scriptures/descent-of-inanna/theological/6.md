The breastplate is the last piece of protective regalia before the final garment. Its removal leaves Inanna essentially defenseless — her body exposed, her vital organs unprotected. Theologically, this penultimate stripping raises the question of divine vulnerability in its starkest form: can the gods be harmed? Can they be killed?

The underworld's answer is unequivocal: yes. The decrees of the underworld apply even to the Queen of Heaven. This theology of divine vulnerability stands in sharp contrast to traditions that insist on divine impassibility — the doctrine that God cannot suffer or be harmed.
