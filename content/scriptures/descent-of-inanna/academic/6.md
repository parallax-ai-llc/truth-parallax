At the sixth gate, Inanna's breastplate (*tu-di-da*) is removed. This item is interpreted by most scholars as a pectoral covering or ornamental chest piece that served both decorative and protective functions. The term *tu-di-da* appears in Sumerian lexical lists associated with items of divine adornment, confirming its cultic significance.

The removal of the breastplate leaves Inanna with only her final garment — the royal robe that will be taken at the seventh gate. Scholars have noted the escalating intimacy of the stripping: from crown to instruments to jewelry to armor to clothing, the sequence mirrors the physical and symbolic layers of Inanna's identity.
