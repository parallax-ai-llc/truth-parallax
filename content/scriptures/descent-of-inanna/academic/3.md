At the third gate, Inanna's lapis lazuli necklace (*za-gin su-nigin*) is removed. This piece of jewelry connects to the broader Mesopotamian association between necklaces and divine or royal identity. Archaeological evidence from sites across Mesopotamia confirms that elaborate necklaces were standard elements of both divine statuary adornment and elite burials.

Scholarship on this passage has focused on the relationship between the items removed and the specific *me* (divine powers) they represent. Bendt Alster and others have argued that each item corresponds to a specific aspect of Inanna's divine portfolio — though the precise correspondences remain debated.
