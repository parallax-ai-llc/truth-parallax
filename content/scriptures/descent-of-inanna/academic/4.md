The fourth gate sees the removal of the sparkling stones (*na4 nunuz*) worn at Inanna's breast. These have been variously interpreted as pectoral ornaments, breast-shaped beads, or fertility amulets. The term *nunuz* (literally 'egg' or 'bead') connects to Inanna's associations with fertility and sexuality. Scholarly debate continues about whether the stones had specific apotropaic or ritual significance.

Jerrold Cooper and others have noted that breast ornaments are particularly associated with female deities in Mesopotamian iconography, suggesting that the removal of these items specifically targets Inanna's identity as a female divine being.
