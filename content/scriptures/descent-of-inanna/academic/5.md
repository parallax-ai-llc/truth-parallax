At the fifth gate, Inanna's gold ring (*har kug-sig17*) is removed. Rings in Mesopotamian culture served as both personal adornment and instruments of authority — seal rings were used to authenticate legal documents and assert ownership. The removal of Inanna's ring thus strips her of yet another dimension of agency and identity.

Scholars have debated whether this ring should be understood as a finger ring, an ankle ring, or a broader ornamental band. The Akkadian version specifies ankle bracelets at this stage, while the Sumerian text is less specific. The discrepancy highlights the challenges of reconstructing specific cultural practices from fragmentary poetic texts.
