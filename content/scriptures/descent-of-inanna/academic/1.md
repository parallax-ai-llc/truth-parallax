The opening section of the Descent of Inanna establishes the goddess's decision to descend to the *kur* (underworld) and her preparations. Scholarly attention has focused on Inanna's stated motivation — she claims to attend the funeral of Gugalanna, Ereshkigal's husband — and whether this represents a genuine pretext or a transparent excuse. Kramer and Sladek interpreted the descent as motivated by Inanna's desire to extend her power to the underworld, while Katz has argued for a more complex reading in which Inanna's journey reflects genuine funerary obligation.

The first gate introduces the gatekeeper Neti, who follows Ereshkigal's instructions to admit Inanna only after removing one item of regalia at each gate. At the first gate, the *shugurra* (great crown) is removed. Linguistically, the formulaic exchange at each gate — 'What, pray, is this?' / 'Be silent, Inanna, the decrees of the underworld are perfect' — has been analyzed as a ritual formula reflecting actual cultic practice.
