At the second gate, Inanna surrenders the lapis lazuli measuring rod and line (*gis-nu11 za-gin*), instruments of cosmic measurement and divine authority. Scholars have interpreted these objects as symbols of Inanna's role in maintaining cosmic order — the capacity to measure, judge, and apportion. W.R. Sladek connected them to the *me* (divine decrees) that govern civilization.

The formulaic exchange is repeated nearly verbatim from the first gate, with only the item changing. This repetitive structure has been analyzed by scholars of oral poetry as evidence of the text's performative character — the repetition serves both mnemonic and ritual functions. The Akkadian version preserves the same sequence, though some details of the items differ.
