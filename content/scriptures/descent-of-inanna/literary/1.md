The opening of the Descent of Inanna establishes the poem's governing literary technique: formulaic repetition with incremental variation. The first gate introduces the pattern — gatekeeper's challenge, Inanna's response, removal of regalia — that will be repeated six more times, each repetition stripping away another layer of divine identity.

The literary economy of the opening is remarkable: in a few lines, the poem establishes Inanna's determination ('From the great above she set her mind toward the great below'), her preparations (gathering the seven *me*), and her vulnerability (instructing Ninshubur what to do if she fails to return). The tension between ambition and precaution gives the opening its dramatic power.
