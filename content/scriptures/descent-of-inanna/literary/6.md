The sixth gate creates almost unbearable literary tension. With only one item of regalia remaining, the audience knows that the next gate will leave Inanna completely naked and utterly vulnerable. The penultimate position in the sequence is a masterful literary device — the pressure of knowing what comes next infuses the sixth repetition with an urgency the earlier gates did not carry.

The breastplate's removal also shifts the poem's register from the symbolic to the physical. The earlier items (crown, rod, necklace) operated primarily as symbols of authority; the breastplate is armor — its removal makes Inanna physically vulnerable, not just symbolically diminished.
