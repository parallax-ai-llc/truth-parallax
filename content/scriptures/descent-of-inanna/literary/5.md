The fifth gate continues the poem's descent from external to intimate. The gold ring is smaller than the previous items — the progressive diminishment of the objects mirrors Inanna's diminishing power and dignity. The literary effect is one of contraction: the world of divine authority shrinks with each gate until almost nothing remains.

The formulaic exchange, now heard for the fifth time, has taken on an inexorable quality. The audience no longer expects variation; the repetition itself has become the message — the relentless, implacable process of stripping that cannot be stopped, argued with, or evaded.
