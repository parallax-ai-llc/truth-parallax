At the poem's structural midpoint (Gate 4 of 7), the literary tone shifts. The items being removed are now intimate and bodily rather than ceremonial and public. The sparkling stones at the breast suggest vulnerability at the most personal level. The repetition of the gate formula, unvarying in its language, creates an increasingly powerful contrast between the ritual calm of the words and the violence of what they describe.

The poem's architecture is revealed as a chiastic structure: the first three gates strip external power, the middle gate strips personal identity, and the final three will strip the most fundamental coverings.
