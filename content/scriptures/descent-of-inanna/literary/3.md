The third gate maintains the poem's repetitive structure while subtly shifting the emotional register. The move from crown and rod to necklace — from public symbols of authority to personal adornment — marks a transition from the political to the personal. The stripping is becoming more intimate, more invasive.

The cumulative effect of three repetitions has now established the pattern firmly enough that the audience can feel the poem's trajectory: Inanna is moving toward total vulnerability. Each gate tightens the dramatic tension, even as the formulaic language creates a surface of ritual calm.
