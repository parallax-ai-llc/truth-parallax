The removal of the breast stones — ornaments that lie close to the heart — resonates with traditions that speak of the stripping of emotional attachment and the exposure of the heart:

- **Sufism**: The concept of *qalb* (heart) as the seat of spiritual knowledge, which must be purified through *dhikr* (remembrance of God)
- **Christianity**: The Sacred Heart tradition and the idea that the heart must be 'circumcised' (Romans 2:29) or opened
- **Buddhism**: The Heart Sutra's teaching that 'form is emptiness, emptiness is form'
- **Judaism**: Deuteronomy 10:16 — 'Circumcise the foreskin of your heart' — the call to inner transformation

At this midpoint, the descent becomes explicitly a journey of the heart.
