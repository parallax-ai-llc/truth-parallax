The removal of the necklace — a symbol of beauty and personal identity — at the third gate speaks to spiritual traditions that emphasize the stripping away of vanity and self-image:

- **Christianity**: The monastic traditions of simplicity and the stripping of worldly identity in religious vows
- **Islam**: The wearing of simple white *ihram* garments during Hajj, setting aside all marks of status and beauty
- **Buddhism**: The shaving of the head and wearing of simple robes upon ordination
- **Hinduism**: The *sannyasi* who renounces all possessions and adornment

In each case, the stripping of adornment marks a passage from worldly identity toward a deeper truth.
