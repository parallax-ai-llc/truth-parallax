The removal of the measuring rod — the instrument of judgment and discernment — at the second gate resonates with traditions that speak of surrendering the capacity to judge as part of spiritual transformation:

- **Christianity**: 'Judge not, that you be not judged' (Matthew 7:1) — the surrender of judgment as a spiritual discipline
- **Buddhism**: The practice of equanimity (*upekkha*) — releasing the habit of measuring and comparing
- **Hinduism**: The Gita's teaching on acting without attachment to results — surrendering the measuring of outcomes
- **Taoism**: Lao Tzu's teaching that 'the Tao that can be measured is not the eternal Tao'

In each tradition, the release of the measuring faculty marks a deepening of spiritual awareness.
