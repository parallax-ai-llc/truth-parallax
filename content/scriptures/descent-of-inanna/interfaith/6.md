The removal of the breastplate — the last piece of protection before complete exposure — speaks to the spiritual theme of radical vulnerability:

- **Christianity**: Christ's stripping before crucifixion, traditionally depicted as the removal of all garments and protection
- **Buddhism**: The Buddha's abandonment of his princely armor and weapons when he left the palace
- **Sufism**: Rumi's teaching that the heart must be 'broken open' to receive divine light
- **Judaism**: The Yom Kippur liturgy in which the High Priest removes his golden vestments and enters the Holy of Holies in simple white linen

The removal of protection is a prerequisite for encounter with the ultimate — whether that ultimate is death, God, or truth.
