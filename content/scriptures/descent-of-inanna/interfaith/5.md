The removal of the gold ring — symbol of covenant, commitment, and binding authority — resonates with traditions that address the nature of divine and human commitment:

- **Judaism**: The wedding ring under the chuppah as symbol of covenant; God's covenant with Israel as an unbreakable bond
- **Christianity**: The bishop's ring as sign of marriage to the church; the 'ring of the fisherman' as papal authority
- **Islam**: The signet ring of the Prophet Muhammad as a symbol of prophetic authority
- **Hinduism**: The ring (*anguthi*) given at marriage as a symbol of the binding of two souls

The stripping of the ring symbolizes the dissolution of all commitments and bonds — a radical aloneness before the absolute.
