The first gate of the Descent introduces a theme with deep interfaith resonance: the willingness to surrender power and status in pursuit of a greater purpose. This voluntary self-emptying finds echoes across traditions:

- **Christianity**: The kenosis of Christ (Philippians 2:5-8), who 'emptied himself' of divine prerogatives
- **Buddhism**: The bodhisattva's renunciation of personal nirvana to serve all beings
- **Sufism**: The concept of *fana* (annihilation of the ego) as the path to divine union
- **Judaism**: Moses's face-to-face encounter with God, which required removing his sandals — a symbolic stripping of worldly identity

The gate itself symbolizes a universal spiritual insight: the passage from one state of being to another requires leaving something behind.
