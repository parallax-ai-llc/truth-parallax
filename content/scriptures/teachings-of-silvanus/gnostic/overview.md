## Overview

The Teachings of Silvanus occupies a unique position among the Nag Hammadi texts: its relatively orthodox Christian theology and its extensive use of Hellenistic philosophy set it apart from the mythological and cosmological texts that dominate the library. For Gnostic readers, the text's inclusion suggests that the Gnostic community at Nag Hammadi valued practical ethical wisdom alongside speculative theology. The emphasis on guarding the soul, cultivating the mind, and resisting the passions reflects a practical spiritual discipline that complements the more theoretical concerns of other Nag Hammadi texts.
