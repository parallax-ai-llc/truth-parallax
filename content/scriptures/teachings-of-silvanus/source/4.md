---
title: "Teachings of Silvanus - Chapter 4"
scripture: "teachings-of-silvanus"
chapter: 4
---

**1** My faithful son, do not be led astray by your excess thoughts, nor by the lust of your eyes, nor by the wanton desire that envelops you.

**2** Do not say that the flesh is a chain, for the flesh is the temple of God. Make the flesh holy, so that it may be a proper dwelling place for the Lord.

**3** If you destroy the temple of God, God will also destroy you. For the body is a garden of God, planted with every kind of tree.

**4** Accept reason and knowledge, and from the first drink what is bitter, so that at the end you may drink what is sweet.

**5** For the one who drinks what is bitter will not be able to drink what is sweet. But the one who drinks first what is sweet will find the bitter to be undrinkable.

**6** Know yourself, and know where you come from and where you are going. And know the meaning of the suffering you will undergo, so that you may know how to endure it.

**7** Wisdom leads the way, and she shows you the way of truth. She goes before you, preparing a place for you.

**8** O my son, return to your divine nature. Cast from you these evil, deceiving friends! Accept the one who is a true friend, Christ.

**9** For Christ is the true king. It is he who rules over all things. He it is who brings sight to the blind and healing to the sick.

**10** Let Christ alone enter your world, and let him bring to naught all the powers which have come upon you.
