## Overview

The Teachings of Silvanus has generated scholarly debate about the classification of Nag Hammadi texts and the boundaries of 'Gnostic' literature. The text contains no Gnostic mythology — no demiurge, no aeons, no Sophia — and its Christology is compatible with proto-orthodox theology. Scholars have proposed that its inclusion in the library reflects either the eclecticism of the collection or the breadth of the 'Gnostic' label itself. The text's close parallels to Clement of Alexandria's thought have led some scholars to locate it in the Alexandrian intellectual milieu of the late second century.
