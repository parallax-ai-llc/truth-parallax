## Overview

Jewish readers will recognize in the Teachings of Silvanus extensive engagement with the Jewish wisdom tradition. The text's exhortations to guard the soul, resist the passions, and seek wisdom echo Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Wisdom of Solomon, and Sirach. The personification of divine Wisdom and her identification with Christ draws on the Jewish Hokhmah tradition (Proverbs 8, Wisdom 7) while Christianizing it — a transformation that the Jewish reader can both recognize and critique. The ethical content of the text — self-knowledge, self-control, proper speech — has extensive parallels in Pirke Avot and other rabbinic wisdom literature.
