## Overview

For Christian readers, the Teachings of Silvanus may be the most accessible text in the Nag Hammadi library. Its identification of Christ with divine Wisdom, its call to guard the soul and resist the passions, and its vision of the mind as the faculty through which the soul knows God all resonate with mainstream Christian theology. The text demonstrates that the Nag Hammadi collection was not uniformly 'heretical' but included material that an orthodox Christian could largely affirm. The text's wisdom-Christology (Christ as Sophia and Logos) anticipates developments in Alexandrian theology (Clement, Origen).
