## Overview

2 Peter is a letter attributed to the Apostle Peter, likely the last New Testament document composed, dated by most scholars to the early second century. The letter addresses the threat of false teachers who deny the second coming of Christ and promote moral license, and it defends the reliability of apostolic testimony and prophetic Scripture. Its inclusion of material from the Epistle of Jude and its unique theological vocabulary have made it one of the most debated books in the canon.

## Structure

2 Peter divides into three chapters with distinct themes:

1. **Knowledge and Virtue** (1:1–21): Growth in divine knowledge, the eyewitness testimony of the Transfiguration, and the reliability of prophecy
2. **False Teachers** (2:1–22): Warning against false teachers who bring destructive heresies and practice immorality
3. **The Day of the Lord** (3:1–18): Defense of the second coming against scoffers, the dissolution and renewal of creation

## Cross-Traditional Significance

2 Peter engages traditions in distinctive ways:

- **Protestantism**: A key text for the doctrine of Scripture's inspiration and the certainty of Christ's return
- **Catholicism**: Important for the theology of the divine nature and the authority of apostolic tradition
- **Eastern Orthodoxy**: 2 Peter 1:4 ("partakers of the divine nature") is the central proof-text for theosis
- **Judaism**: The letter's use of Jewish apocalyptic traditions and its engagement with false prophecy
- **Islam**: The themes of false prophets and divine judgment resonate with Quranic teaching
- **Gnosticism**: The letter's polemic against false knowledge stands in tension with Gnostic claims

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Divine Knowledge | Growth in the knowledge of God as the path to godliness |
| False Teachers | Warning against those who distort the truth for personal gain |
| Second Coming | Defense of Christ's return against skeptics |
| Scripture | Prophecy as inspired by the Holy Spirit, not human invention |
| Judgment | God's certain judgment on the wicked, illustrated by biblical examples |
| Cosmic Renewal | The destruction of the present heavens and earth and the coming new creation |
