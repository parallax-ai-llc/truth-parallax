## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Peter centers on its themes of false prophets, divine judgment, and eschatological expectation — all of which have strong parallels in Quranic teaching. The letter's warnings against teachers who deny future judgment and promote moral license resonate with the Quran's repeated condemnation of those who reject the Day of Judgment. While Muslim scholars do not accept the letter's Christological framework, its moral and eschatological concerns align closely with Islamic theology.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### False Prophets
2 Peter 2, with its extended denunciation of false teachers, resonates with the Quranic concern about false prophets and those who distort divine revelation for personal gain. The Quran warns: "Woe to those who write the Book with their own hands, then say, 'This is from Allah,' in order to exchange it for a small price" (2:79). Both traditions insist on the integrity of divine revelation against human corruption.

### The Day of Judgment
2 Peter 3's defense of the certainty of divine judgment — against those who scoff, "Where is the promise of his coming?" (3:4) — parallels the Quran's frequent response to those who deny the Day of Judgment. The Quran states: "The Striking Calamity — what is the Striking Calamity? And what can make you know what is the Striking Calamity?" (101:1–3). Both texts affirm that divine judgment is certain, regardless of human skepticism.

### Divine Patience
2 Peter 3:9, teaching that God delays judgment out of patience, resonates with the Islamic understanding of God as *As-Sabur* (The Patient) and *Al-Halim* (The Forbearing). The Quran states: "If Allah were to punish people for their wrongdoing, He would not leave upon the earth any creature, but He defers them for a specified term" (16:61).

## Theological Emphases

- **False Prophets**: The danger of those who corrupt divine teaching for personal gain
- **Certainty of Judgment**: The Day of Judgment as inevitable despite human denial
- **Divine Forbearance**: God's patience as a mercy allowing time for repentance
- **Cosmic Eschatology**: The transformation of the heavens and earth in the final age
