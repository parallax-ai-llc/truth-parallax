## The Certainty of Judgment

2 Peter 3:3–7 defends the certainty of divine judgment against skeptics. This resonates powerfully with the Quran's consistent affirmation of the Day of Judgment (*Yawm al-Qiyamah*) against those who deny it: "But they have denied the Hour, and We have prepared for those who deny the Hour a Blaze" (25:11). Both texts address the same human tendency to deny future accountability and respond with the same conviction: judgment is certain.

## Divine Patience and Repentance

2 Peter 3:9, explaining God's delay as patience allowing time for repentance, aligns with the Quranic teaching on *tawbah* (repentance). God's name *At-Tawwab* (The Accepter of Repentance) reflects the divine desire for humanity to return to righteousness. The Quran states: "Say, 'O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins'" (39:53).

## Cosmic Transformation

2 Peter 3:10–13, describing the dissolution of the heavens and earth and the coming of a new creation, resonates with Quranic eschatology: "On the Day when the earth will be replaced by another earth, and the heavens as well" (14:48). Both traditions envision a radical cosmic transformation at the end of history, resulting in a new order in which divine justice fully prevails.
