## Knowledge and Piety

2 Peter 1:3–8, teaching that knowledge of God produces virtuous character, resonates with the Islamic emphasis on *'ilm* (knowledge) as the foundation of piety. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim" (Ibn Majah). Muslim readers appreciate the letter's insistence that genuine knowledge of God transforms moral conduct — a principle central to Islamic education and spiritual formation.

## Prophetic Witness

2 Peter 1:16–18, with its appeal to eyewitness testimony of a divine revelation, resonates with the Islamic emphasis on the reliability of prophetic witness. The Quran is understood as the direct, unmediated word of God transmitted through the Prophet Muhammad, and the hadith literature preserves the prophetic testimony with meticulous chains of transmission (*isnad*). Both traditions take the integrity of prophetic witness seriously.

## Scripture's Divine Origin

2 Peter 1:20–21 affirms that Scripture originates from the Holy Spirit, not human invention. This resonates with the Islamic doctrine of *wahy* (revelation) — the Quran is not the product of Muhammad's own intellect but the direct word of God communicated through the angel Jibril (Gabriel). Muslim readers recognize in 2 Peter a shared conviction about the divine origin of sacred Scripture.
