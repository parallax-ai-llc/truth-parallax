## The Day of the Lord

2 Peter 3:10 describes the "day of the Lord" when "the heavens will pass away with a roar, and the heavenly bodies will be burned up and dissolved." Catholic eschatology interprets this as the cosmic transformation that accompanies Christ's return in glory. The Catechism teaches that the material universe will be renewed, not annihilated — a transformation analogous to the glorification of the resurrected body.

## God's Patience and Universal Salvation

2 Peter 3:9 — God "not wishing that any should perish" — is important for Catholic theology of salvation. While the Catholic Church affirms the reality of hell as a possibility for those who finally reject God, it also teaches that God's universal salvific will extends to all people and that divine patience provides every opportunity for repentance.

## Growing in Grace and Knowledge

2 Peter 3:18 — "Grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ" — serves as the letter's concluding exhortation and captures the Catholic understanding of the Christian life as progressive growth. Sanctification is not a one-time event but a lifelong process of deepening knowledge of Christ and increasing participation in divine grace through the sacramental and moral life of the Church.
