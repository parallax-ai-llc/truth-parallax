## False Teachers in the Church

Catholic interpretation of 2 Peter 2 reads the warning against false teachers as a mandate for the Church's doctrinal authority. The Magisterium exists precisely to protect the faithful from the "destructive heresies" that false teachers introduce. The historical examples of divine judgment (2:4–10) demonstrate that God takes doctrinal fidelity seriously and will hold false teachers accountable.

## The Angels Who Sinned

2 Peter 2:4, referring to "angels who sinned," draws on Jewish apocalyptic traditions about fallen angelic beings. Catholic theology incorporates this passage into its teaching on the fall of the angels and the reality of demonic powers. The Catechism affirms the existence of angels and the reality of the devil and his angels as created beings who freely chose to reject God.

## The Way of Balaam

2 Peter 2:15–16 describes false teachers who follow "the way of Balaam," using religious authority for financial gain. Catholic pastoral teaching has drawn on this passage to warn against simony and the corruption of religious office. The prophetic rebuke — a donkey rebuking the prophet's madness — serves as a humbling reminder that God can use the most unexpected means to correct those who abuse their spiritual authority.
