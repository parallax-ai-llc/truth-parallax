## The Divine Nature and Grace

Catholic theology reads 2 Peter 1:4 — "partakers of the divine nature" — as affirming the reality of grace as a participation in God's own life. The *Catechism of the Catholic Church* (§460) cites this verse alongside the patristic tradition of deification: "The Word became flesh to make us 'partakers of the divine nature.'" This participation is effected through the sacraments, especially baptism and the Eucharist.

## Virtue and Cooperation with Grace

2 Peter 1:5–7 instructs believers to "make every effort to supplement your faith with virtue." Catholic moral theology reads this as an affirmation of cooperation with grace: the believer is not passive in sanctification but actively cultivates the virtues through the empowerment of the Holy Spirit. This active response to grace is central to the Catholic understanding of merit.

## Scripture and Tradition

2 Peter 1:20 — "no prophecy of Scripture is a matter of one's own interpretation" — is a foundational text for the Catholic teaching that Scripture must be interpreted within the living tradition of the Church. The Second Vatican Council's *Dei Verbum* affirms that "the task of authentically interpreting the Word of God has been entrusted solely to the Magisterium of the Church."
