## Overview

2 Peter stands in a complex relationship with Gnostic thought. On one hand, the letter's language of "knowledge" (*epignōsis*) and "partaking in the divine nature" (1:4) uses terminology that Gnostic interpreters could have found attractive. On the other hand, the letter's polemic against false teachers who promote "cleverly devised myths" (1:16) and its insistence on the physical reality of Christ's return may represent an explicit anti-Gnostic stance — making 2 Peter one of the earliest Christian texts to combat Gnostic-type claims.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Anti-Gnostic Polemic
Many scholars read 2 Peter 2 as directed against proto-Gnostic teachers who promoted libertine behavior based on claims of spiritual knowledge that transcended moral categories. The false teachers who "promise freedom" while being "slaves of corruption" (2:19) may represent Gnostic-influenced Christians who believed that the pneumatic was above the moral law.

### Knowledge Redefined
2 Peter's repeated emphasis on "knowledge" (*epignōsis*) of Christ (1:2, 3, 8; 2:20; 3:18) appears to reclaim the concept of salvific knowledge from Gnostic appropriation. In 2 Peter, true knowledge is not esoteric cosmological insight but moral and relational knowledge of Christ that produces godliness. The letter's "knowledge" is inseparable from ethical transformation.

### The Divine Nature
2 Peter 1:4 — "partakers of the divine nature" — uses language that resonates with Gnostic claims about the pneumatic's inherent divinity. However, 2 Peter frames participation in the divine nature as a gift of grace that must be pursued through moral effort (1:5–7), not as an innate spiritual status requiring only awakening. This represents a direct counter to the Gnostic understanding of pneumatic election.

## Theological Emphases

- **True vs. False Knowledge**: Reclaiming "knowledge" from Gnostic distortion
- **Moral Accountability**: The inseparability of knowledge and ethical conduct
- **Physical Parousia**: The defense of Christ's bodily return against spiritualizing interpretations
- **Cosmic Judgment**: The physical dissolution of creation as a counter to Gnostic cosmology
