## Overview

The Concept of Our Great Power (NHC VI,4) is an apocalyptic text that divides cosmic history into three ages: the age of the flesh, the age of the soul (psychic), and the age of the spirit (pneumatic). Each age is characterized by its dominant principle and its relationship to the Great Power, an ambiguous divine figure who presides over the cosmic drama. The text describes the flood (ending the first age), the coming of the Savior (transitioning to the psychic age), and the final conflagration and judgment (inaugurating the pneumatic age). The text is eclectic, drawing on Jewish apocalyptic, Christian eschatology, and Gnostic cosmology without fitting neatly into any single category. It is generally dated to the third or fourth century CE.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | The Age of the Flesh | The first cosmic age, dominated by the material principle, ending with the flood |
| 2 | The Age of the Psychic | The second age, characterized by the soul principle and the coming of the Savior |
| 3 | The Coming of the Pneumatic Age | The transition to the spiritual age through apocalyptic events and the defeat of the archon |
| 4 | The Final Consummation | The ultimate transformation of the cosmos and the vindication of the spiritual elect |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: Read as a Gnostic salvation history mapping the progressive spiritualization of the cosmos
- **Academic**: Studied for its eclectic theology and its relationship to both Jewish apocalyptic and Gnostic eschatology
- **Comparative**: Offers parallels to Hindu yugas, Zoroastrian world ages, and Joachim of Fiore's three ages of history
- **Christian**: Engages with biblical apocalyptic (Daniel, Revelation) while developing it in a Gnostic direction
- **Jewish**: Transforms Jewish apocalyptic periodization of history into a Gnostic framework
- **Interfaith**: Addresses the widespread religious intuition that history is moving toward a spiritual transformation

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Three Cosmic Ages | History is divided into ages of flesh, soul, and spirit, each representing a stage of cosmic evolution |
| The Great Power | An ambiguous divine figure presiding over the unfolding of cosmic history |
| Apocalyptic Eschatology | The current age will end in fire and judgment, inaugurating the age of the spirit |
| Progressive Spiritualization | The cosmos moves from material density toward spiritual transparency |
