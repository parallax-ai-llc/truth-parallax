## Overview

The Concept of Our Great Power addresses a widespread religious intuition: that history is not random but moves toward a spiritual transformation. Whether expressed as the Hindu cycle of yugas, the Buddhist expectation of Maitreya, the Jewish hope for messianic redemption, the Christian anticipation of the kingdom of God, or the Islamic expectation of the Day of Judgment, the conviction that the current age will give way to something higher and more spiritual is one of the most common features of human religious thought. The text's specific scheme — three ages of progressive spiritualization — represents one articulation of this universal hope.
