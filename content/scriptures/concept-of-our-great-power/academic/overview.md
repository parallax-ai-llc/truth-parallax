## Overview

The Concept of Our Great Power has attracted scholarly attention for its eclectic theology, which draws on Jewish apocalyptic periodization, Christian salvation history, and Gnostic cosmology without fitting neatly into any single category. The figure of the 'Great Power' itself is ambiguous — scholars debate whether it refers to the highest God, a subordinate divine being, or an impersonal cosmic force. The text's treatment of three cosmic ages has been compared to Jewish apocalyptic schemes (the Apocalypse of Weeks in 1 Enoch 93), Christian dispensationalist thought, and Joachim of Fiore's three ages of the Father, Son, and Spirit.
