## Overview

The Concept of Our Great Power's division of history into three progressive ages resonates with cyclical and progressive cosmologies worldwide. The Hindu doctrine of the four yugas (Satya, Treta, Dvapara, Kali) describes a cosmic cycle of decline, while the Zoroastrian concept of world ages (the 12,000-year cosmic drama) maps the conflict between good and evil toward an ultimate resolution. Joachim of Fiore's medieval Christian theology of three ages (Father, Son, Spirit) closely parallels the Gnostic scheme, and the Buddhist concept of mappo (the declining age of the dharma) addresses similar concerns about the trajectory of cosmic history.
