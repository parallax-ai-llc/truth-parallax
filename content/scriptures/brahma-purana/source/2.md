---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall describe to you the nature of the Supreme Lord, Narayana, who is the cause of all causes, the origin and dissolution of the universe.

**2** He is the eternal, the infinite, the one without beginning or end. He is beyond the reach of speech and mind. He is the self-luminous one, the witness of all.

**3** He pervades all things, yet is not contained by any. He is smaller than the smallest and greater than the greatest. He dwells in the heart of every being.

**4** He is the source of all the gods, all the worlds, all creatures. From Him all things proceed and to Him all things return.

**5** He is the lord of Lakshmi, the preserver of the universe, the slayer of demons, the protector of the righteous. He sleeps upon the cosmic serpent on the ocean of milk.

**6** From His navel springs the lotus upon which Brahma sits. From His breath come the Vedas. His will is the law of the universe.

**7** He incarnates in every age to protect the good and destroy the wicked, to re-establish dharma when it has declined.

**8** He is worshipped by the gods, the sages, the saints, and all devoted souls. His name alone is sufficient to destroy all sins and grant liberation.

**9** He who meditates upon Narayana with a pure heart, who chants His name with devotion, who surrenders all actions to Him — that person crosses over the ocean of worldly existence.

**10** The lotus feet of Narayana are the supreme refuge. He who takes shelter there has nothing to fear from death, from suffering, or from the terrors of this world.
