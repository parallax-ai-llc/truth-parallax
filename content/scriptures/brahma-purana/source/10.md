---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 10"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the final truth, the essence of all that I have taught.

**2** There is only one Supreme Reality, called by the wise as Brahman, Paramatman, and Bhagavan. It is the source, sustenance, and dissolution of all that exists.

**3** The universe is the manifestation of that Supreme Reality. All things arise from It, exist in It, and return to It. Like waves arising from the ocean and subsiding into it again.

**4** The Supreme Lord is both with form and without form, both with qualities and without qualities. He is worshipped by the devotee in His personal form and known by the sage in His impersonal nature.

**5** Devotion and knowledge are not opposed but are two wings of the same bird. Through devotion, the heart is purified; through knowledge, the truth is realized. Together they lead to liberation.

**6** He who sees all beings in the Self and the Self in all beings — he never turns away from It. For the one who sees oneness everywhere, what delusion, what sorrow can there be?

**7** The Supreme Lord is nearer than the nearest, dearer than the dearest. He is the very Self of the seeker. To seek Him is to seek one's own true nature.

**8** All the scriptures, all the rituals, all the pilgrimages, all the austerities lead to this one truth: the identity of the individual self with the Supreme Self.

**9** Tat Tvam Asi — Thou art That. This is the great saying of the Vedas. When the seeker realizes this truth through direct experience, all bondage is destroyed.

**10** Thus I have related to you this Purana, the essence of all knowledge. He who hears it with faith and devotion, who contemplates its meaning and lives by its teachings, attains the supreme goal of liberation. Om, peace, peace, peace.
