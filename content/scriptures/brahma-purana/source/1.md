---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The sages, assembled at the holy place of Naimisha, approached the great Suta and said: "O blessed one, relate to us the ancient Purana that was first spoken by Brahma, the creator of the universe."

**2** Suta said: In the beginning, before creation, there existed only the one Brahman, without a second — eternal, infinite, pure consciousness, self-luminous, and unchanging.

**3** From that Supreme Brahman arose the desire to create: "Let me become many." And from that desire, the cosmic energy, Prakriti, was set in motion.

**4** The Lord, wishing to create the worlds, first produced the cosmic waters. Into those waters He cast His seed, and from that seed arose a golden egg, brilliant as a thousand suns.

**5** Within that egg the Lord Brahma was born, the grandfather of all beings. He is called the self-born, for he was born from the Self alone.

**6** Brahma divided the egg into two: from the upper half he made the heavens, from the lower half the earth, and between them he established the atmosphere.

**7** He created the ten directions, time with its divisions, the mind, speech, desire, anger, and joy. From his body arose the various elements of creation.

**8** From his mouth came the Brahmanas and the Vedas. From his arms came the Kshatriyas and strength. From his thighs came the Vaishyas and nourishment. From his feet came the Shudras and service.

**9** He created day and night, the seasons, the years, and the great cycles of time. He established the moral law and the sacred rituals.

**10** Thus Brahma created this entire universe of moving and unmoving things. And having created it, he entered into it as the inner self of all beings.
