---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the nature of the four Yugas, the great ages through which the world passes in its endless cycle.

**2** The first age is Satya Yuga, the age of truth. In this age, dharma stands on all four feet. Men are virtuous, long-lived, and devoted to truth and meditation.

**3** In Satya Yuga, there is no disease, no sorrow, no envy, no hatred. All beings are contented and at peace. The lifespan of men is one hundred thousand years.

**4** The second age is Treta Yuga. In this age, dharma stands on three feet. Virtue diminishes by one quarter. Men begin to perform sacrifices and rituals to maintain the cosmic order.

**5** In Treta Yuga, the lifespan of men is ten thousand years. Kings rule with justice, but the seeds of unrighteousness begin to sprout.

**6** The third age is Dvapara Yuga. In this age, dharma stands on two feet. Virtue and vice are equally balanced. Disease and calamity increase.

**7** In Dvapara Yuga, the lifespan of men is one thousand years. The Vedas are divided into four, for men can no longer comprehend them as a whole.

**8** The fourth age is Kali Yuga, the age of strife. In this age, dharma stands on one foot only. Vice prevails over virtue. Men are short-lived, quarrelsome, and afflicted by suffering.

**9** In Kali Yuga, the lifespan of men is one hundred years and decreasing. Righteousness is abandoned, and darkness covers the earth. But even in this age, the chanting of the Lord's name is sufficient for liberation.

**10** At the end of Kali Yuga, the Lord shall appear as Kalki to destroy the wicked and restore Satya Yuga. Thus the cycle of the four ages turns eternally, without beginning and without end.
