---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the sacred city of Purushottama Kshetra, the abode of Lord Jagannatha, which is the holiest of all places.

**2** At that place the Supreme Lord resides in the form of Purushottama, the highest Person. He who worships Him there with devotion attains liberation.

**3** The sacred image of the Lord at Purushottama Kshetra is not made by human hands. It is self-manifest, having appeared of its own accord for the salvation of all beings.

**4** He who beholds the Lord at Purushottama is freed from the cycle of birth and death. Even the mere thought of visiting that place destroys accumulated sins.

**5** The Lord said: "I dwell at Purushottama Kshetra eternally. He who comes to Me there with faith and devotion, I shall never forsake him."

**6** The ocean at Purushottama is itself sacred, for the Lord bathes in its waters. He who bathes in that ocean with faith is purified of all sins.

**7** The festivals celebrated at Purushottama Kshetra are attended by the gods themselves, though unseen by mortal eyes. The chanting of the Lord's name fills the heavens with joy.

**8** He who dies at Purushottama Kshetra, whether knowingly or unknowingly, attains the supreme abode. For the Lord himself carries the departing soul to liberation.

**9** Even the trees, the animals, the insects, and the birds that dwell at Purushottama Kshetra attain liberation by the mere proximity to the Lord's presence.

**10** Thus Purushottama Kshetra is the supreme pilgrimage, the abode of the Lord, the gateway to liberation. He who hears of its glories is freed from all bondage.
