---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 7"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the sacred rivers, whose waters purify all who bathe in them.

**2** The Ganga descends from the heavens, flowing from the toe of Vishnu. She passes through the matted locks of Shiva and falls upon the earth for the liberation of all beings.

**3** He who bathes in the Ganga with faith is freed from all sins. He who dies on her banks attains the supreme abode. Even the utterance of her name is purifying.

**4** The Yamuna, the daughter of the sun, flows with dark waters. She who was beloved of Krishna in Vrindavana purifies all who touch her waters.

**5** The Sarasvati, the river of knowledge, flows invisibly. She joins the Ganga and Yamuna at the sacred confluence of Prayaga. She is the mother of the Vedas.

**6** The Narmada, flowing westward, is auspicious merely by her sight. The stones found in her bed are natural Shiva Lingas, worshipped throughout the land.

**7** The Godavari, called the Ganga of the south, was brought down by the sage Gautama. Her banks are adorned with sacred groves and hermitages.

**8** The Kaveri, the Krishna, the Tungabhadra, and all the sacred rivers of the south — they all purify those who bathe in their waters with devotion.

**9** But the wise say that the greatest river is the river of knowledge, which flows from the teacher to the student, from the scriptures to the seeker. He who bathes in that river is truly purified.

**10** He who sees all rivers as forms of the one divine water, who sees all places as the abode of the Lord, who sees all beings as the Self — he has truly bathed in all the sacred rivers.
