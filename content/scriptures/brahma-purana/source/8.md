---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 8"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the duties of the devotee and the means of attaining the grace of the Supreme Lord.

**2** The devotee should rise before dawn and meditate upon the Lord in the lotus of the heart. He should chant the sacred names and perform his daily worship with sincerity.

**3** He should speak the truth, practise non-violence, be compassionate to all creatures, and serve the saints and sages with humility.

**4** He should study the scriptures, listen to the stories of the Lord's incarnations, sing His praises, and keep the company of the devoted.

**5** The nine forms of devotion are: hearing about the Lord, singing His praises, remembering Him, serving His feet, worshipping Him, saluting Him, being His servant, being His friend, and offering oneself completely to Him.

**6** Of these nine, the complete surrender of oneself to the Lord is the highest. He who surrenders everything — body, mind, and soul — to the Lord, is dearest to Him.

**7** The Lord says: "He who offers to Me with devotion a leaf, a flower, a fruit, or water — I accept that offering, made with love, from the pure-hearted one."

**8** The Lord does not look at the magnitude of the offering, but at the love with which it is offered. A poor man's offering, given with devotion, is greater in the Lord's eyes than a king's treasure given with pride.

**9** The devotee should see the Lord in all beings and all beings in the Lord. He should treat all creatures with kindness, for the Lord dwells in the heart of every being.

**10** He who thus lives in constant remembrance of the Lord, who performs all actions as worship, who sees the divine in all things — he attains the supreme goal even while living in this world.
