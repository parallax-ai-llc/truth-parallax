---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 9"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the nature of the individual soul and its relationship with the Supreme Soul.

**2** The individual soul, the Jiva, is a portion of the Supreme Soul, the Paramatman. As a spark is to the fire, so is the Jiva to the Supreme.

**3** The Jiva is by nature pure, conscious, and blissful. But through association with Prakriti, it becomes bound by ignorance and identifies itself with the body.

**4** Bound by ignorance, the Jiva takes upon itself the attributes of the body and the senses, and thinks: "I am this body, I am the doer, I am the enjoyer."

**5** Through this false identification, the Jiva becomes subject to desire and aversion, to pleasure and pain, to birth and death. This is the bondage of samsara.

**6** But the Jiva is in truth never bound, for it is the eternal Self, unchanging and free. The bondage is only apparent, caused by ignorance, like the rope mistaken for a snake.

**7** When the light of knowledge arises, ignorance is dispelled, and the Jiva recognizes its true nature as the Supreme Self. This is liberation.

**8** The Supreme Lord dwells in the heart of every being as the inner witness, the silent observer. He is the eternal companion of the Jiva, guiding it through countless births.

**9** Two birds, inseparable companions, perch upon the same tree. One eats the sweet fruit, the other looks on without eating. The first is the Jiva; the second is the Lord.

**10** When the Jiva turns away from the fruit and beholds the glory of the Lord, all its sorrow comes to an end. It realizes: "I am not separate from the Lord. I am the Lord." This is the supreme knowledge.
