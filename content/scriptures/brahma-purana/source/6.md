---
title: "Brahma Purana - Chapter 6"
scripture: "brahma-purana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Brahma said: I shall now describe the dissolution of the universe, which occurs at the end of each great cycle of creation.

**2** When the appointed time arrives, the Lord withdraws His creative energy. The sun scorches the earth with seven times its heat. The seas boil and evaporate. The rivers run dry.

**3** A great drought afflicts the world for one hundred years. All creatures perish. The earth becomes barren and lifeless.

**4** Then twelve suns appear simultaneously in the sky, and their combined fire consumes the earth, the atmosphere, and the heavens. All the worlds are reduced to ashes.

**5** After the fire comes the deluge. Great clouds arise and pour rain for one hundred years. The waters cover everything. The entire universe is submerged in the cosmic ocean.

**6** The Supreme Lord reclines upon the cosmic serpent Shesha, floating upon the waters. He sleeps, and within His sleep the entire universe rests in potentiality.

**7** This dissolution is called Pralaya. It occurs at the end of each day of Brahma, which lasts for 4,320,000,000 human years. At the end of Brahma's night, which is of equal duration, creation begins again.

**8** At the end of Brahma's lifespan of one hundred divine years, the great dissolution, Mahapralaya, occurs. All the worlds, all the elements, even Prakriti itself, dissolve into the Supreme Brahman.

**9** In the Mahapralaya, nothing remains but the Supreme Brahman, alone, without a second, self-luminous, infinite, and eternal. Then, in the fullness of time, creation begins again.

**10** Thus the cycle of creation and dissolution is without beginning and without end. The Supreme Lord alone is eternal and unchanging. He who knows this is freed from all fear.
