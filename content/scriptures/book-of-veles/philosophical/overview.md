## Overview

The Book of Veles raises profound philosophical questions about the nature of sacred texts and religious authority. What makes a text authentically sacred — its age, its content, its reception by a believing community, or some combination? Can a text composed in the modern period transmit genuine spiritual truth or ancient wisdom? The controversy surrounding the Book of Veles illuminates tensions between scholarly and devotional approaches to sacred texts, the role of authenticity in religious authority, and the philosophical problems of textual interpretation.
