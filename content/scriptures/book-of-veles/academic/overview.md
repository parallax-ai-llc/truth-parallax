## Overview

The Book of Veles presents a fascinating case study in academic textual criticism. The overwhelming scholarly consensus is that the text is a modern fabrication, likely created by Yuriy Mirolyubov in the mid-twentieth century. Academic analysis identifies numerous linguistic anachronisms (mixing features from different periods of Slavic language development), historical impossibilities, and suspicious parallels with modern nationalist narratives. The study of the Book of Veles also illuminates broader questions about how religious texts acquire authority, how forgeries are detected, and why communities may embrace texts that scholarship rejects.
