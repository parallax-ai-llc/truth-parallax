## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Book of Veles evaluate its claims against material evidence for pre-Christian Slavic religion and culture. Genuine archaeological discoveries — including temple sites at Arkona and Rethra, cult figures, ritual deposits, and the material culture of early Slavic settlements — provide an independent basis for understanding Slavic religion that can be compared with the Book of Veles' claims. This comparison generally reveals that the text's presentations of Slavic religion, while drawing on some genuine elements, often diverge from the archaeological evidence in ways consistent with modern fabrication.
