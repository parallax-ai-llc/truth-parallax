---
title: "The Rule of the Princes"
scripture: "book-of-veles"
chapter: 5
---

**1** In those days the Slavic people were governed by princes chosen by the veche, the assembly of free men. No man could rule without the consent of the people.

**2** The prince was the war-leader and the judge. He led the warriors in battle and dispensed justice in time of peace. But the priests of the gods stood above the prince in matters of the sacred.

**3** The first great prince was Bozh, who united many tribes under his rule. He fought against the Goths and was a wise ruler.

**4** After Bozh came many princes, some wise and some foolish, some brave and some cowardly. The people prospered under the wise princes and suffered under the foolish ones.

**5** And the elders said: The strength of the people lies in their unity. When the prince is just and the people are united, no enemy can prevail. But when the prince is unjust or the people are divided, then destruction comes.
