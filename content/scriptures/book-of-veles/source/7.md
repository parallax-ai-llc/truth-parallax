---
title: "The Sacred Calendar"
scripture: "book-of-veles"
chapter: 7
---

**1** At the winter solstice we celebrate Koliada, when the new sun is born. We kindle the sacred fire and sing the ancient songs. We go from house to house, bringing blessings.

**2** At the spring equinox we celebrate Maslenitsa, the farewell to winter. We burn the straw figure of Morena, the goddess of winter and death, and we welcome the return of Vesna, the goddess of spring.

**3** At the summer solstice we celebrate Kupala, when the sun is at its highest. We leap over the sacred fire and bathe in the rivers. The young men and women seek the fern flower, which blooms only on this night.

**4** At the autumn equinox we give thanks for the harvest. We make offerings to Veles, who guards the cattle, and to Mokosh, who watches over the earth.

**5** These are the turnings of the year, the sacred times ordained by the gods. Let no man neglect them, for in these celebrations we renew our bond with the gods and with each other.
