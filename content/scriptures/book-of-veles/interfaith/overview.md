## Overview

The Book of Veles holds a central place in contemporary Slavic neopagan (Rodnovery) movements, which seek to revive pre-Christian Slavic religion. For practitioners in these movements, the text functions as a sacred scripture regardless of academic assessments of its authenticity. Interfaith engagement with the Book of Veles requires sensitivity to both scholarly concerns about historicity and the genuine spiritual needs and experiences of communities that venerate the text. This situation parallels debates about contested scriptures in other traditions and raises important questions about the relationship between historical scholarship and living faith.
