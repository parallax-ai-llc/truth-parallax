## Overview

Regardless of questions of authenticity, the Book of Veles contains mythological motifs that invite comparative analysis. Its presentation of Svarog as a celestial creator-smith parallels similar divine smith figures in other Indo-European traditions (Hephaestus, Vulcan, the Vedic Tvastr). The Triglav concept resonates with trinitarian structures in other religions. The text's cosmology — with its three-tiered universe and emphasis on the balance between sky gods and chthonic forces — reflects patterns found across Indo-European and world mythologies, whether these reflect genuine ancient tradition or modern reconstruction.
