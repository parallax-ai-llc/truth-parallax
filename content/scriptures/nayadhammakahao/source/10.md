---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 10"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the final teaching, on the path to liberation and the nature of the liberated soul."

**2** "The path to liberation has three components, called the Three Jewels: right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct. These three are inseparable, like the three legs of a tripod."

**3** "Right faith is the conviction that the Tirthankaras speak the truth, that the soul is real, that karma is real, that liberation is possible."

**4** "Right knowledge is the understanding of the nature of the soul, the nature of matter, the nature of karma, and the path of liberation as taught by the Tirthankaras."

**5** "Right conduct is the practice of the five vows — non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness — with steadfastness and sincerity."

**6** "When these three are practiced together, the karmic coverings are gradually removed, layer by layer, like the cleaning of a mirror."

**7** "First the deluding karma is destroyed, and right faith becomes unshakeable. Then the knowledge-obscuring and perception-obscuring karmas are destroyed, and the soul attains omniscience."

**8** "Finally, the remaining karmas are destroyed, and the soul is liberated. It rises to the apex of the universe, to the Siddha-loka, where it dwells forever."

**9** "The liberated soul possesses infinite knowledge — it knows all things, past, present, and future. It possesses infinite perception — it perceives all things simultaneously. It possesses infinite bliss — it experiences joy that is beyond description. It possesses infinite energy — it is beyond all limitation."

**10** "The liberated soul is beyond birth and death, beyond pleasure and pain, beyond time and space. It is pure consciousness, self-luminous, eternal."

**11** Gautama said: "O Blessed One, your teaching is like a lamp in the darkness, like a raft on the ocean, like a bridge over the abyss. We bow to thee, the omniscient one."

**12** Mahavira said: "Go forth, O Gautama, and teach this Dharma to all beings. Let them hear the truth, let them practice the path, and let them attain the supreme liberation."

**13** Thus concludes the Nayadhammakahao, the Narratives of the Righteous Law, as spoken by the Blessed Mahavira to his disciple Gautama.
