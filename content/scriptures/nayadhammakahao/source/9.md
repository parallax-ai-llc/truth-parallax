---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 9"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the teaching on the nature of the universe and the cycle of existence."

**2** "The universe is without beginning and without end. It was not created by any god, nor will it be destroyed by any god. It exists by its own nature, governed by natural laws."

**3** "The universe is divided into the inhabited world — the Lokakasha — and the uninhabited void — the Alokakasha — which surrounds it on all sides."

**4** "The inhabited world has three divisions: the lower world, where the hells are found; the middle world, where humans, animals, and plants dwell; and the upper world, where the gods reside."

**5** "Above all three divisions is the Siddha-loka — the abode of the liberated souls. It is at the very apex of the universe, and there the Siddhas dwell in eternal bliss."

**6** "The soul passes through the cycle of rebirth — samsara — taking birth in the four conditions: as a hell-being, as an animal or plant, as a human, or as a god."

**7** "The human birth is the most precious, for only as a human can one hear the Dharma, take the vows, and attain liberation."

**8** "The time-cycle moves in ascending and descending phases. In the ascending phase, virtue, happiness, and lifespan increase. In the descending phase, they decrease."

**9** "We are now in the fifth spoke of the descending phase — a time of increasing suffering and diminishing virtue. But even in this age, liberation is possible for those who practice with diligence."

**10** "In each half-cycle, twenty-four Tirthankaras appear to teach the Dharma. Mahavira is the last Tirthankara of the present descending half-cycle."

**11** Mahavira said: "The universe is vast, and the cycle of existence is long. But the soul that practices right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct can escape the cycle and attain the eternal peace of liberation."
