---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 7"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the story of the monk Harikeshi, who endured the greatest suffering with perfect equanimity."

**2** "Harikeshi was born in a low caste, despised by the world. But hearing the teachings of the Jina, he renounced everything and became a monk."

**3** "The people of the town mocked him: 'How can a man of low birth become a monk? He pollutes the order!' They threw stones at him, spat upon him, and beat him with sticks."

**4** "But Harikeshi endured all this without anger, without resentment, without any thought of retaliation. He said to himself: 'This body is not mine. These insults are not directed at my soul. The soul is beyond caste, beyond birth, beyond death.'"

**5** "He continued his practice, begging for alms from door to door. Many refused him. Some gave him spoiled food. Some set dogs upon him."

**6** "Through all this, Harikeshi maintained the equanimity of one who has seen the truth. He said: 'In previous births, I have been a king, a beggar, an animal, a god. Caste is a garment the soul wears for a time and then discards.'"

**7** "His endurance became legendary. Even the gods marveled at his patience and his steadfastness on the path."

**8** "Indra, the king of the gods, came down from heaven to test him. He appeared as a Brahmin and heaped abuse upon Harikeshi. But the monk only smiled and said: 'May you be well. May you be happy. May all beings find peace.'"

**9** "Indra was astonished and revealed his true form. He bowed before Harikeshi and said: 'O great monk, you are the true Brahmin — not by birth but by conduct. You are greater than the gods.'"

**10** "Harikeshi practiced until the end of his life, destroying all karma, and attained liberation — the eternal state of infinite knowledge and bliss."

**11** Mahavira said: "The soul knows no caste. The soul knows no birth. The soul is pure consciousness. He who realizes this is truly free."
