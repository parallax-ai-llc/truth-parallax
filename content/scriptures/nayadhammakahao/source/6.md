---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 6"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the teaching on the nature of karma, which is the cause of bondage and suffering."

**2** "Karma is not fate. Karma is not punishment. Karma is a natural law, as certain as the law that fire burns and water flows downward."

**3** "Every action of body, speech, and mind produces karmic particles that adhere to the soul, coloring it and determining its future experiences."

**4** "There are eight types of karma: knowledge-obscuring, perception-obscuring, feeling-producing, deluding, lifespan-determining, body-determining, status-determining, and obstructive."

**5** "Of these, the first four are called destructive karmas, for they obscure the soul's natural qualities. The last four are called non-destructive karmas, for they determine the conditions of rebirth."

**6** "The influx of karma occurs through the activities of the mind, speech, and body, especially when these activities are accompanied by passion — anger, pride, deceit, and greed."

**7** "The binding of karma depends on five factors: the nature of the activity, the duration of the karmic bond, the intensity of the passion, the quality of the karma, and the quantity of karmic particles."

**8** "The stopping of karmic influx occurs through the practice of the five vows, the three guptis (restraints of mind, speech, and body), and the ten dharmas (forgiveness, humility, straightforwardness, contentment, truth, self-restraint, austerity, renunciation, non-possessiveness, and celibacy)."

**9** "The destruction of existing karma occurs through austerities, both external (fasting, reduced diet, sleeping on hard ground, solitude) and internal (repentance, humility, service, study, meditation, and renunciation of the body)."

**10** "When all four destructive karmas are destroyed, the soul attains omniscience. When all eight karmas are destroyed, the soul attains liberation."

**11** Mahavira said: "He who understands the nature of karma understands the cause of all suffering and the path to its end."
