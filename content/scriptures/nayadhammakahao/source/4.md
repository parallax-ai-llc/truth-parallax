---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 4"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the story of Megha Kumar, the prince who heard music and was awakened."

**2** "Prince Megha Kumar was the son of King Shrenika of Rajagriha. He lived in the palace, surrounded by every luxury, attended by beautiful women and musicians."

**3** "One day, a monk came to the palace and sang a hymn in praise of the Jina. The melody was sweet, but the words pierced Megha Kumar's heart like an arrow."

**4** "The hymn spoke of the impermanence of youth, the certainty of death, the suffering of rebirth, and the bliss of liberation."

**5** "Megha Kumar was so moved that tears flowed from his eyes. He said: 'What have I been doing with my life? I have been decorating a burning house, celebrating in a prison.'"

**6** "He went to his father and said: 'Father, give me permission to renounce the world.' King Shrenika was grieved but gave his consent, for he knew the power of true renunciation."

**7** "Megha Kumar took the vows of a monk. He practiced fierce austerities: standing in the sun without shade, sitting in the rain without shelter, eating only what was offered without asking."

**8** "The other monks marveled at his endurance. But Megha Kumar said: 'This is nothing compared to the suffering I endured in countless previous births — as an animal, as a hell-being, as a hungry ghost.'"

**9** "'Better one life of austerity that leads to freedom than countless lives of pleasure that lead nowhere.'"

**10** "Through his practice, Megha Kumar attained the highest meditation — the shukla dhyana, the pure white meditation — in which the mind becomes perfectly still and the karmas are destroyed."

**11** "He attained liberation and became a Siddha, dwelling forever in the state of infinite knowledge, infinite bliss."

**12** Mahavira said: "Music awakened Megha Kumar; but the music of the Dharma awakens the soul itself. Let all beings listen with the ear of the heart."
