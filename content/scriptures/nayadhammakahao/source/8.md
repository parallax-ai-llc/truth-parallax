---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 8"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the teaching on non-violence — ahimsa — which is the supreme Dharma."

**2** "All living beings desire to live. No being wishes to die. Therefore, the first and greatest of all duties is to cause no harm to any living being."

**3** "There are beings everywhere — in the earth, in the water, in the air, in the fire. Even the smallest atom of matter may harbor living souls."

**4** "The wise man walks carefully, lest he crush the insects beneath his feet. He strains his water, lest he swallow the tiny beings within it. He sweeps his path, lest he harm the creatures in his way."

**5** "Non-violence is not merely the absence of killing. It is the absence of all harm — physical, mental, and verbal. To speak harshly is violence. To think evil of another is violence."

**6** "The monk who practices perfect non-violence does not kill, does not cause others to kill, and does not approve of killing."

**7** "He feels for all beings as he feels for himself. The pain of an ant is to him as the pain of a king. The life of a worm is to him as precious as the life of a god."

**8** "This is the teaching that Mahavira proclaimed as the foundation of all Dharma: 'Ahimsa paramo dharma' — non-violence is the highest religion."

**9** "He who harms no being, who speaks no harsh word, who thinks no evil thought — he is the true saint, regardless of what garments he wears or what rituals he performs."

**10** "The practice of non-violence leads to the cessation of karmic influx, for violence is the primary cause of karmic bondage."

**11** Mahavira said: "Let all beings embrace non-violence. Let all beings see themselves in others. For the soul that inhabits every body is the same soul — pure, luminous, and eternal."
