---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 2"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the story of Mrigaputra, the son of Mriga, who was a merchant in the city of Shravasti."

**2** "Mrigaputra was wealthy and lived in luxury, surrounded by pleasures. But one day he saw a funeral procession, and the sight of the dead man struck his heart."

**3** "He thought: 'This body, now carried to the burning ground, was once young and strong. It ate and drank and loved. Now it is nothing but a corpse, food for the flames.'"

**4** "'What then is the use of wealth? What is the purpose of pleasure? All that we gather must be left behind at the moment of death.'"

**5** "Mrigaputra went to the assembly of the Jina and heard the teaching on the transience of all compounded things. His heart was pierced by the arrow of truth."

**6** "He said to his wife and family: 'I have lived in delusion. I have mistaken the prison for a palace, the chains for ornaments. Now I shall seek the truth.'"

**7** "He took the vows of a householder first: to limit his possessions, to limit his travels, to avoid unnecessary violence, to observe fasting days, and to give charity."

**8** "After practicing as a layman for twelve years, strengthening his resolve, he took the full vows of a monk and entered the path of renunciation."

**9** "As a monk, Mrigaputra practiced the austerities: fasting, meditation, study of the scriptures, confession of faults, and service to other monks."

**10** "He meditated on the twelve reflections: the transience of life, the helplessness of the soul, the vastness of the cycle of rebirth, the loneliness of the soul, the separateness of body and soul, the impurity of the body, the influx of karma, the stopping of karma, the destruction of karma, the nature of the universe, the difficulty of attaining right faith, and the nature of the Dharma."

**11** "Through these practices, Mrigaputra destroyed the four destructive karmas and attained omniscience. He became a Kevalin — one who knows all things."

**12** Mahavira said: "Thus did a merchant attain the highest knowledge. The path of liberation is open to all, regardless of birth or station."
