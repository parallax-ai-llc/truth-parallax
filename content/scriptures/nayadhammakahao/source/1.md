---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 1"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. The Blessed Mahavira, the Venerable One, the omniscient, the all-knowing, spoke these words to his chief disciple Gautama:

**2** "O Gautama, I shall now relate to thee the narratives of the righteous law, the Nayadhammakahao, which contains the stories of those who have attained liberation through right conduct."

**3** "In the city of Champa there lived a king named Nami, who was powerful and just, ruling his kingdom with righteousness."

**4** "King Nami, hearing the teaching of the Jina, was moved to renunciation. He reflected: 'What is this world of pleasures? It is transient, impermanent, full of suffering.'"

**5** "He thought: 'The palace, the wealth, the beautiful women, the armies — all these are like dew upon the morning grass, like clouds passing across the sky, like foam upon the water.'"

**6** "And King Nami resolved to renounce his kingdom. He called his ministers and said: 'I have seen the truth. The pleasures of the world are a net that binds. I shall take the vows of a monk.'"

**7** "The ministers and the queens wept and pleaded, but Nami was firm. He distributed his wealth, removed his royal garments, plucked out his hair, and took the five great vows."

**8** "The five great vows are: non-violence toward all living beings; truthfulness in all speech; non-stealing; celibacy; and non-possessiveness."

**9** "King Nami wandered as a monk, enduring heat and cold, hunger and thirst, insult and injury, without complaint, maintaining perfect equanimity."

**10** "After years of austere practice, he attained the supreme knowledge, destroyed all karma, and became a Siddha — a liberated soul, free from the cycle of birth and death."

**11** Mahavira said: "Thus, O Gautama, did King Nami walk the path of liberation. So should all beings who seek the highest good renounce attachment and practice right conduct."
