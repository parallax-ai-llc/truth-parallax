---
title: "Nayadhammakahao - Chapter 5"
scripture: "nayadhammakahao"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Mahavira spoke: "Now hear the story of the great queen Chelana, who renounced her royal life to follow the path of liberation."

**2** "Queen Chelana was the wife of King Shrenika. She was beautiful, intelligent, and devoted to the Dharma."

**3** "When she heard the teachings of the Jina, she understood that the pleasures of the world are fleeting and that only the practice of righteousness leads to lasting happiness."

**4** "She asked the king's permission to take the vows of a laywoman, and he consented. She observed strict fasting, gave generously to monks and the poor, and practiced meditation."

**5** "On the sacred days, she would eat only once, abstaining from all food seasoned with salt, oil, or spice. She sat on the bare ground and kept vigil through the night."

**6** "She reflected: 'This body is a vessel of impurity — bones, blood, flesh, and excrement. How foolish to be attached to it! How foolish to decorate it and pamper it!'"

**7** "'The soul within is pure, luminous, and eternal. It is the soul that I must care for, not this temporary shell.'"

**8** "In time, she took the full vows of a nun and entered the order of the Jina. She practiced even more strictly: enduring the cold without fire, the heat without shade, hunger without complaint."

**9** "Her fellow nuns were inspired by her example. She taught them with gentleness and patience, saying: 'Sisters, do not be discouraged by the hardships of the path. The suffering of practice is brief; the suffering of samsara is endless.'"

**10** "Through her practice, Queen Chelana destroyed the deluding karma and attained right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct in their fullest measure."

**11** Mahavira said: "Thus did a queen show that liberation is not reserved for kings and sages alone. Women and men, rich and poor — all can walk the path."
