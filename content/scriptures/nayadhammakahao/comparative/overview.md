## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Nayadhammakahao reveals striking parallels with narrative traditions across world religions. The use of stories to teach moral and spiritual truths is a universal phenomenon, and the text provides rich material for cross-traditional analysis.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Jataka Parallels

The Nayadhammakahao's stories of monks and laypeople bear strong resemblance to the Buddhist Jataka tales, both in narrative structure and in their use of past-life stories to explain present circumstances. Whether there was direct literary influence remains debated.

### Universal Moral Themes

The themes of renunciation, moral consequence, and spiritual transformation found in the Nayadhammakahao appear in religious literature worldwide -- from biblical parables to Sufi teaching stories to Hindu puranic narratives.

### Narrative as Theology

The text demonstrates how narrative functions as a form of theology -- doctrines about karma, liberation, and the nature of reality are communicated not through abstract argument but through compelling stories.
