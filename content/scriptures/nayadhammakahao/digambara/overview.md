## Overview

The Digambara tradition values the Nayadhammakahao's narrative teachings while interpreting its stories through their own doctrinal framework. The tales of monks who achieved liberation through rigorous renunciation resonate deeply with the Digambara emphasis on complete ascetic withdrawal from worldly life.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Ascetic Exemplars

Digambara interpreters read the stories as confirming the necessity of absolute renunciation for spiritual progress. The monks and laypeople who achieve the highest spiritual states in these narratives exemplify the Digambara ideal of total detachment.

### Karmic Justice

The narratives illustrate the Digambara understanding of karma as an exact, material process. The consequences depicted in the stories follow precise karmic laws, demonstrating that moral causation operates with the certainty of physical law.

### Narrative Authority

While Digambara scholars may work from different textual recensions, they respect the narrative tradition as an authentic vehicle for Mahavira's teachings.
