## Overview

For the Shvetambara tradition, the Nayadhammakahao is the sixth Anga, cherished as a record of Mahavira's own use of stories to teach dharma. The text demonstrates that even the most profound philosophical truths can be communicated through narrative, making them accessible to a wider audience.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Narrative as Teaching Method

Shvetambara scholars emphasize that Mahavira chose to teach through stories precisely because narrative engages the emotions as well as the intellect. The Nayadhammakahao shows that dharma is not merely abstract doctrine but lived reality.

### Canonical Storytelling

The text's position as the sixth Anga gives its narratives canonical authority. These are not merely entertaining tales but authoritative teachings that carry the weight of scripture.

### Community Formation

The stories serve as shared cultural resources for the Shvetambara community, providing models of virtue and warnings against moral failure that shape collective identity and values.
