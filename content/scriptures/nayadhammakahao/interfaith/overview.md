## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Nayadhammakahao finds in its stories universal themes that resonate across religious boundaries. The power of narrative to communicate spiritual truth is recognized in every major tradition, and the text's tales of renunciation, moral consequence, and spiritual aspiration speak to shared human concerns.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Stories as Bridges

Narrative is perhaps the most natural medium for interfaith dialogue. The Nayadhammakahao's stories can be shared, discussed, and appreciated by people of all faiths, creating connections that transcend doctrinal differences.

### Shared Moral Wisdom

The values celebrated in the text -- compassion, self-discipline, generosity, truthfulness -- are recognized as virtues across virtually all religious traditions, providing natural points of interfaith connection.

### Universal Human Themes

The stories deal with universal human experiences -- attachment and loss, temptation and resistance, suffering and liberation -- that resonate across cultural and religious boundaries.
