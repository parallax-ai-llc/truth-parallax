Comparative interpretation of Chapter 14, 'Inquiring Words,' examines this portion of the Huainanzi alongside parallel treatments in philosophical traditions worldwide. The Huainanzi's encyclopedic ambition — its attempt to integrate cosmology, natural philosophy, political theory, and ethics into a comprehensive system — invites comparison with similar synthetic projects in other traditions: Aristotle's comprehensive philosophy, the Indian Arthashastra, and the Islamic philosophical encyclopedias.

Comparative scholars analyze the specific themes of this chapter alongside their treatment in other traditions, identifying both universal patterns in human philosophical reflection and the distinctive approaches shaped by Chinese cosmological assumptions, particularly the correlative thinking that characterizes Han-dynasty intellectual culture.
