## Dao Ying Xun (Responses of the Way)

The Way responds to all things without deliberation. It is like an echo answering a sound, like a shadow following a form.

An old man living near the border lost his horse. His neighbors came to console him. The old man said: "How do you know this is not a blessing?" Some months later, the horse returned, bringing several fine wild horses with it. The neighbors came to congratulate him. The old man said: "How do you know this is not a disaster?" His son, riding one of the wild horses, fell and broke his leg. The neighbors came to console him. The old man said: "How do you know this is not a blessing?" A year later, war broke out and all the young men were conscripted. Most died in battle. But the old man's son, because of his broken leg, was spared.

This story illustrates the Way's responses: Fortune and misfortune are intertwined. What appears to be disaster may lead to blessing. What appears to be blessing may lead to disaster. The sage does not rejoice at good fortune or grieve at bad fortune, because he understands that all things are connected.

The Way responds to sincerity with sincerity, to force with yielding, to rigidity with flexibility. It does not fight against the current of events but flows with them, finding advantage in every circumstance.

The sage who understands these responses can turn disaster into opportunity and weakness into strength. He does not resist the flow of events but rides upon it, like a boatman riding the current of a great river.
