## Jing Shen Xun (The Quintessential Spirit)

The quintessential spirit is the wellspring of life. When it is preserved, vitality flourishes. When it is dissipated, vitality declines.

In the beginning, before form existed, there was only the formless essence. From this essence, spirit emerged. From spirit, vital energy emerged. From vital energy, form emerged. Form and spirit combined to produce life.

The body is the dwelling place of life. The vital energy is the fullness of life. The spirit is the controller of life. When any one of these loses its proper position, all three are harmed.

Therefore the sage nourishes his spirit, makes his vital energy harmonious, and keeps his body healthy. He does not exhaust his spirit with excessive thinking. He does not scatter his vital energy with excessive desire. He does not wear out his body with excessive labor.

The ears should not be overstimulated with sounds. The eyes should not be overstimulated with sights. The mind should not be overwhelmed with schemes. When the senses are calm and the mind is still, the spirit naturally becomes clear and bright.

The perfect man uses his mind as a mirror. It reflects but does not retain. It responds but does not store. Therefore he can deal with things without being harmed by them.

He who can preserve the One and respond to the many, who can maintain stillness and yet deal with motion -- this is the man of the quintessential spirit.
