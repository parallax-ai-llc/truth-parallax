## Shi Ze Xun (Seasonal Rules)

In the first month of spring, the east wind thaws the ice. Hibernating creatures begin to stir. Fish rise to the surface beneath the ice. Otters sacrifice fish. Wild geese return northward.

The Son of Heaven resides in the eastern chamber. He rides in a chariot with bells of luan. He dresses in green robes and wears green jade. He eats wheat and mutton.

In this month, the vital force of all living things is rising. One must not cut down trees or destroy nests. One must not kill the young, whether of animals or humans. One must not build walls or raise earthworks. One must not assemble large armies.

In the first month of summer, bitter herbs flourish. Grasses and trees grow rank. The wheat harvest arrives.

In this month, the Son of Heaven must nourish the growth of all things. He must not drain marshes or clear forests. He must encourage agriculture and reduce taxes.

In the first month of autumn, cool winds arrive. White dew descends. Cicadas sing. Hawks sacrifice birds.

In this month, the Son of Heaven resides in the western chamber. He practices military arts. He repairs walls and cities. He prepares the granaries.

In the first month of winter, water begins to freeze. The earth begins to harden. Pheasants enter the water and become clams.

The seasonal rules teach that governance must follow the rhythms of nature. To act against the season is to invite disaster.
