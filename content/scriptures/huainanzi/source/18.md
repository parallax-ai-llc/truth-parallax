## Ren Jian Xun (The Human World)

The human world is governed by two forces: intention and circumstance. The sage masters both.

Intention without understanding of circumstance leads to disaster. A man may intend to cross a river, but if he does not know the depth and current, he will drown. Circumstance without intention leads to drift. A leaf floating on the river goes wherever the current takes it.

The sage combines clear intention with deep understanding of circumstance. He knows what he wants to achieve and he knows the conditions under which he must achieve it.

In the human world, relationships are primary. The relationship between ruler and minister, between parent and child, between husband and wife, between elder and younger, between friend and friend -- these five relationships form the fabric of society.

When these relationships are healthy, society is healthy. When they are corrupted, society decays. No system of laws, however well designed, can substitute for healthy human relationships.

The human world is also governed by the principle of reciprocity. Treat others as you wish to be treated. This is not merely a moral principle but a practical one: People respond to how they are treated. Treat them well, and they will serve you well. Treat them badly, and they will resist you.

The sage in the human world is neither naive nor cynical. He understands both the nobility and the baseness of human nature. He designs institutions that bring out the best in people and restrain the worst. He hopes for virtue but plans for vice.
