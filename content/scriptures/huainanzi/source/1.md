## Yuan Dao Xun (The Original Way)

The Way covers Heaven and supports Earth. It extends to the four quarters and opens up the eight directions. Its height is immeasurable. Its depth is unfathomable. It enfolds Heaven and Earth and endows the formless.

From it springs the river that flows so swiftly that a boat cannot travel upstream. It is so vast that it cannot be contained, so deep that it cannot be plumbed. Stretching and contracting, spreading and stopping, it is soft and gentle but also firm and hard, yielding yet overcoming.

Before Heaven and Earth existed, there was something nebulous and complete. Silent and void, standing alone and unchanging, revolving without cease, it could be the mother of all things. I do not know its name, so I call it the Way.

The Way gives birth to the One. The One gives birth to the Two. The Two gives birth to the Three. The Three gives birth to the ten thousand things. The ten thousand things carry yin and embrace yang, and through the blending of qi they achieve harmony.

He who holds to the Way is flexible and yielding and does not compete with others. He humbles himself and stands below. He is empty and still, clear and calm. He accords with Heaven above and responds to Earth below. He follows the changes of the four seasons and moves with the flow of things.

The sage models himself upon Heaven. Heaven does not speak, yet the four seasons proceed. Heaven does not calculate, yet the sun and moon shine.
