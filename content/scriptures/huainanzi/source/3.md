## Tian Wen Xun (Patterns of Heaven)

Heaven has its constants: the sun and moon proceed in their courses, the stars occupy their positions, the seasons follow their order. These patterns are not the result of deliberate planning but of the natural operation of the Way.

In the beginning, before heaven and earth were separated, all was a shapeless mass of qi. The clear and light qi rose and became heaven. The heavy and turbid qi sank and became earth. The blended and harmonious qi became man.

Heaven is round and Earth is square. The round governs by emanation; the square governs by darkness and rest. Emanation spits forth qi; darkness and rest absorb it. This is why fire and the sun are on the outside, while water and the moon are on the inside.

The sun travels one degree per day and completes its circuit in 365 and one-quarter days. The moon travels thirteen degrees and a fraction per day. The sun and moon meet once every twenty-nine and a half days.

The five planets -- Jupiter, Mars, Saturn, Venus, and Mercury -- each have their own periods and correspondences. Jupiter corresponds to wood and spring. Mars corresponds to fire and summer. Saturn corresponds to earth and the center. Venus corresponds to metal and autumn. Mercury corresponds to water and winter.

Understanding the patterns of heaven allows the sage to govern in accordance with the seasons, to plant and harvest at the proper time, and to maintain the harmony between heaven, earth, and man.
