## Chu Zhen Xun (Activating the Genuine)

The genuine nature of all things is received from Heaven. It cannot be altered any more than the nature of water can be made dry or the nature of fire can be made cold.

The sage preserves his genuine nature. He does not allow external things to disturb his inner peace. He is like a mirror: reflecting everything clearly but retaining nothing.

When the mind is still and empty, it can respond to all things. When it is agitated and full, it can respond to nothing. The empty vessel can be filled; the full vessel overflows and is useless.

The genuine person does not seek fame. He does not pursue profit. He does not compete for position. He acts according to the natural course of things and rests in his own nature.

This does not mean idleness or indifference. The genuine person acts -- but his action arises spontaneously, like the growth of plants or the flowing of water. It is not forced or calculated.

The ancients who understood this principle could govern great states with ease. They did not need elaborate policies or detailed regulations. They simply allowed the natural order to operate, intervening only when necessary and always with the lightest possible touch.

When the ruler's mind is empty and still, the ministers perform their duties without distortion. When the ruler's mind is agitated and partial, the ministers compete for favor and neglect their work.

The foundation of good government is the cultivation of the ruler's genuine nature.
