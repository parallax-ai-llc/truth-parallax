## Qi Su Xun (Integrating Customs)

The customs of the world are diverse. In the south, people live in nests above the water. In the north, they dwell in caves beneath the earth. In the east, they eat fish and salt. In the west, they eat meat and drink milk.

The sage does not try to make all customs the same. He recognizes that different peoples in different places have developed different ways of living, each suited to their environment.

The people of the coast build boats; the people of the mountains make shoes for climbing. These are not matters of right or wrong but of adaptation to circumstance.

Therefore the wise ruler governs each region according to its own customs. He does not impose the ways of the capital upon the frontier. He does not demand that mountain people live like plains people.

The principle of integrating customs: Respect diversity in non-essential matters while maintaining unity in essential ones. The essential matters are: obedience to law, payment of taxes, and loyalty to the state. In all other matters, the people should be free to follow their own traditions.

The ruler who insists on uniformity breeds resentment. The ruler who allows diversity breeds loyalty. People love their own customs as they love their own parents. To attack a people's customs is to attack their identity.

The sage harmonizes without making identical. He unifies without making uniform. This is the Way applied to the governance of diverse peoples.
