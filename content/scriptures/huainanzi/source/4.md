## Di Xing Xun (Topography)

The earth has nine continents and eight winds, six harmonies and four seas. The mountains are the bones of the earth; the rivers are its blood vessels; the grasses and trees are its hair.

In the far east is the Valley of the Morning Sun, where the sun rises. In the far west is the Valley of the Evening Sun, where the sun sets. In the far south grows the fiery grain. In the far north lies the dark ice.

The five sacred mountains anchor the land: Mount Tai in the east, Mount Hua in the west, Mount Heng in the south, Mount Heng in the north, and Mount Song in the center. Each mountain has its own character, its own creatures, its own spiritual influence.

The qualities of water vary with the land through which it flows. Water that flows over rocks is strong. Water that flows through sand is clear. Water that flows through clay is turbid. The character of the people varies similarly with the terrain they inhabit.

Those who live in mountains are hardy and independent. Those who live in marshes are patient and adaptable. Those who live on the coast are adventurous and open-minded. Those who live on the plains are steady and conventional.

The sage who governs must understand the topography of his domain. He must adapt his governance to the character of the land and its people. What works on the plains may not work in the mountains. What succeeds on the coast may fail in the interior.

Geography shapes destiny. The wise ruler works with the land, not against it.
