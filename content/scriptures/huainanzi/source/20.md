## Tai Zu Xun (The Exalted Lineage)

The exalted lineage of wisdom descends from the ancient sage kings through the sages and teachers of each generation. It is not a lineage of blood but of understanding.

The sage kings of antiquity -- Yao, Shun, Yu, Tang, Wen, and Wu -- each governed according to the conditions of their time. Yao governed through virtue alone, for the people were simple and few. Yu governed through tireless labor, for the floods required physical effort. Tang and Wu governed through military force, for tyranny required overthrow. Each was right for his time.

The lineage of wisdom teaches that there is no single method that is right for all times. The sage adapts. He learns from the past but does not imitate it slavishly. He applies timeless principles through timely methods.

The exalted lineage also teaches humility. Each sage knew that he stood upon the shoulders of those who came before. Each knew that those who came after would surpass him. The Way is greater than any individual who practices it.

The purpose of tracing this lineage is not ancestor worship but practical instruction. By studying how the sages of the past responded to the challenges of their time, we learn how to respond to the challenges of our own.

The lineage continues. In every generation, there are those who perceive the Way and practice it. They may not wear crowns or hold office. They may be scholars, farmers, craftsmen, or merchants. But they carry the lineage forward through their understanding and their conduct.
