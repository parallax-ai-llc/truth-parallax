## Bing Lue Xun (An Overview of the Military)

The sage does not delight in war, but when war is necessary, he does not shrink from it. The purpose of military force is to punish the unjust and protect the weak.

The foundation of military strength is the unity of the people. An army whose soldiers are willing to die for their country is stronger than an army ten times its size whose soldiers fight under compulsion.

The unity of the people depends upon good government. When the government is just, the people are united. When the government is unjust, the people are divided. No amount of military training can compensate for a divided people.

The principles of military strategy: Know yourself and know your enemy. Choose your ground carefully. Move when the enemy is unprepared. Strike where the enemy is weak. Avoid what the enemy is strong.

But above all: The supreme excellence is to win without fighting. The general who can subdue the enemy without battle -- through diplomacy, through economic pressure, through the moral authority of a just cause -- is the greatest of all generals.

When fighting is unavoidable: Be swift and decisive. Do not prolong the campaign. Treat prisoners humanely. Do not destroy crops or harm civilians. Leave the conquered people with enough to rebuild.

The army that fights for justice is welcomed by the conquered. The army that fights for plunder is resisted. This difference in reception determines long-term success more surely than any battlefield victory.
