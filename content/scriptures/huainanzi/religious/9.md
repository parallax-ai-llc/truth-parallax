Religious Taoism draws upon Chapter 9, 'The Art of Rulership,' as part of the cosmological and spiritual framework that informs Taoist ritual practice and cultivation. The chapter's treatment of its subject provides religious practitioners with a systematic understanding of the cosmic structures within which Taoist cultivation takes place — the patterns of heaven and earth, the operations of yin and yang, and the circulation of qi that sustains all phenomena.

The religious tradition reads this chapter's cosmological descriptions not as abstract theory but as maps of the sacred universe through which the practitioner navigates in meditation, ritual, and daily cultivation. The Huainanzi's detailed accounts of cosmic processes inform the visualization practices, liturgical calendars, and ritual protocols of organized Taoist religion.
