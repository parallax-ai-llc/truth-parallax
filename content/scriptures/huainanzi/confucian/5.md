Confucian interpretation of Chapter 5, 'Seasonal Rules,' engages with the Huainanzi's effort to integrate Confucian ethical values within a Taoist cosmological framework. While the text's fundamental orientation is Taoist, this chapter incorporates Confucian concerns — moral governance, social harmony, the cultivation of virtue — in ways that Confucian readers find both compelling and problematic.

Confucian interpreters note that the Huainanzi does not reject Confucian values but subordinates them to Taoist principles, treating Confucian ethics as a necessary but insufficient component of the sage-ruler's comprehensive wisdom. This hierarchical relationship between Confucian and Taoist thought has generated ongoing debate among Confucian scholars about the proper relationship between moral cultivation and cosmological understanding.
