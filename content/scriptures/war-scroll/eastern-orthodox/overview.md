## Overview

Eastern Orthodox scholars read the War Scroll through the lens of the *Christus Victor* atonement theology, which understands Christ's death and resurrection as a cosmic battle that defeated the powers of evil. The scroll's vision of a final war between the forces of light and darkness, with angelic participation and divine victory, resonates with the Orthodox understanding of salvation as liberation from the dominion of Satan.

## Interpretive Approach

Orthodox interpretation situates the War Scroll within the broader narrative of cosmic redemption that runs from Genesis through Revelation. The text is read as a witness to the Jewish anticipation of God's ultimate triumph over evil — an anticipation fulfilled, in Orthodox theology, through the incarnation, crucifixion, and resurrection of Christ, and the harrowing of Hades.

## Key Emphases

Orthodox scholars note the War Scroll's elaborate angelic hierarchy, which resonates with the angelology developed by Pseudo-Dionysius and other Church Fathers. The scroll's insistence that the battle is ultimately won by God's power, not human effort, aligns with the Orthodox emphasis on divine grace. The liturgical character of the warfare — conducted through priestly prayer and blessing — anticipates the Orthodox understanding of the Divine Liturgy as a participation in the heavenly worship where Christ has already conquered evil.

## Significance

For Eastern Orthodoxy, the War Scroll testifies to the ancient Jewish understanding that the cosmos is a battleground between good and evil, and that God's people participate in a conflict that transcends the visible world. This vision finds its fulfillment in the Orthodox Paschal proclamation: "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death."
