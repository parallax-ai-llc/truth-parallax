## Christus Victor

Eastern Orthodox scholars read Chapter 1's vision of the final cosmic war through the lens of the *Christus Victor* atonement theology, which understands Christ's death and resurrection as the decisive battle against the powers of darkness. The War Scroll's expectation of a future divine victory finds its fulfillment, in Orthodox theology, in the Paschal event — the harrowing of Hades and the liberation of those held captive by death and the devil.

## The Cosmic Dimension

The chapter's insistence that the final war is not merely a human conflict but a cosmic struggle involving angelic and demonic forces resonates with the Orthodox understanding of reality as a spiritual battlefield. Orthodox theology, following the Church Fathers, affirms that the invisible world is as real as the visible and that the spiritual warfare described by the apostle Paul is not merely metaphorical but describes the actual condition of creation awaiting its liberation.

## Light Overcomes Darkness

The chapter's fundamental conviction that light will ultimately triumph over darkness finds its most powerful liturgical expression in the Orthodox Paschal service, where the faithful process from a darkened church into the blaze of paschal candles, proclaiming "Christ is risen!" The War Scroll's dualism of light and darkness, while more absolute than Orthodox theology allows, nevertheless witnesses to a spiritual reality that Orthodoxy affirms: darkness is real but cannot prevail against the light.
