## The Paschal Pattern

Eastern Orthodox scholars read the seven-battle sequence of Chapter 5 — victories followed by defeats, culminating in God's decisive intervention — as a prefiguration of the Paschal pattern of death and resurrection. The three defeats endured by the Sons of Light before God's triumphant action anticipate the three days of Christ's burial before the resurrection. Orthodox theology sees this pattern of apparent defeat followed by divine victory as the fundamental rhythm of salvation history.

## The Harrowing of Hades

The chapter's climactic divine intervention, in which God personally destroys the forces of Belial, finds its supreme fulfillment in the Orthodox doctrine of the Harrowing of Hades. In the Paschal icon, Christ descends to the realm of death, shatters its gates, and liberates Adam and Eve — and through them, all humanity — from the dominion of death and the devil. The War Scroll's vision of God's definitive victory over darkness anticipates this central Orthodox proclamation.

## Eternal Worship

The post-victory celebration described in the chapter — purification, thanksgiving, and consecrated worship — prefigures the eternal worship of the Kingdom of God. Orthodox eschatology envisions the age to come as an eternal Divine Liturgy, in which all creation joins the angelic hosts in unceasing praise of the Triune God. The War Scroll's transition from battle to worship anticipates this vision of conflict resolved in eternal peace and praise.
