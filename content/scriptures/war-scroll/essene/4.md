## The Priests at War

Chapter 4 describes the central role of the priests in directing the eschatological battle. For the Essene community, war was fundamentally a liturgical act — the priests did not merely accompany the army but commanded it through prayer, blessing, trumpet signals, and ritual actions. The chief priest delivered a pre-battle address, pronouncing God's commitment to fight on behalf of the Sons of Light and cursing the forces of Belial.

## Angelic Allies

The chapter reveals the Essene conviction that the archangel Michael and the hosts of heaven would fight alongside the human army. The angels were not merely spectators but active combatants, and their presence required the same purity standards that governed the Temple. This belief that the community shared its life with the angels — already expressed in the Community Rule's vision of angelic worship — found its most dramatic expression in the War Scroll's depiction of joint human-angelic combat.

## The Prayers of Battle

The prayers prescribed in this chapter combine military encouragement with theological proclamation. They recount God's past victories over the enemies of Israel — from Egypt through the conquest of Canaan — as assurance that God would act again in the final battle. These prayers functioned as both liturgy and psychological preparation, reminding the warriors that the outcome was already determined by divine decree.
