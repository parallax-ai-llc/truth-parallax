## Mustering the Army of God

Chapter 2 describes the organization and recruitment of the forces of light with military precision. The Essene community understood these instructions as divinely revealed specifications for the composition and structure of God's army. Every detail — age requirements for service, tribal organization, rotation of divisions, and support roles — was ordained by God and would be followed exactly when the time came.

## Age and Fitness Requirements

The chapter specifies age brackets for different types of military service, exempting the elderly and those with physical impairments from combat roles while assigning them support duties. For the Essenes, these requirements reflected both practical military considerations and theological principles drawn from Deuteronomy's holy war regulations. The army's fitness standards were inseparable from its purity standards: only the physically and spiritually whole could serve in God's host.

## The Tribal Structure

The organization of the army along tribal lines — echoing the arrangement of the Israelite camp in Numbers 2 — expressed the community's conviction that the eschatological war would restore the ideal Israel of the wilderness period. The twelve tribes, long scattered and assimilated, would be reconstituted under divine command. The Essene community saw itself as the nucleus of this restored Israel, the faithful remnant around which the tribes would reassemble.
