## Overview

For the Essene community, the War Scroll was a prophetic blueprint for the final battle they believed was imminent. The community understood itself as the Sons of Light, destined to fight alongside the archangel Michael and the hosts of heaven against the forces of Belial. The scroll was not speculative literature but an operational manual for the end of days.

## Interpretive Approach

The Essenes read the War Scroll as divinely revealed battle plans, given through prophetic inspiration to prepare the community for its eschatological role. Every detail — the inscriptions on the banners, the sequences of trumpet blasts, the rotation of battle divisions — was understood as divinely ordained and to be followed with precision when the appointed time arrived.

## Key Emphases

The Essene reading emphasizes the priestly character of the eschatological war. Unlike conventional military campaigns, this battle is directed by priests who sound trumpets, offer prayers, and pronounce blessings. The community's rigorous purity discipline finds its ultimate justification here: only the ritually pure could stand in the ranks alongside the angels. The scroll's description of seven battles — three won by each side before God's decisive intervention in the seventh — expresses the community's conviction that human faithfulness and divine power work together in the cosmic drama.

## Significance

The War Scroll reveals the Essene community's deepest self-understanding: a priestly army in waiting, separated from the world to maintain the purity required for participation in the final holy war. It demonstrates how apocalyptic expectation shaped every dimension of the community's daily life and discipline.
