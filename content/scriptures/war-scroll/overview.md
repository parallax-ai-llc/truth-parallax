## Overview

The War Scroll (1QM), known in Hebrew as *Milhamah*, is a major sectarian composition found among the Dead Sea Scrolls at Qumran. It describes in vivid detail the eschatological war between the "Sons of Light" and the "Sons of Darkness," a final cosmic battle in which God and his angels intervene to secure ultimate victory for the righteous. The scroll dates to the late first century BCE or early first century CE.

## Structure

The War Scroll is organized into five major sections:

1. **The Final War** (Chapter 1): An overview of the eschatological conflict between light and darkness
2. **The Army of Light** (Chapter 2): Detailed organization and recruitment of the righteous forces
3. **Banners and Formations** (Chapter 3): Military equipment, inscriptions, and tactical arrangements
4. **Priests and Angels** (Chapter 4): The liturgical and supernatural dimensions of the battle
5. **The Seven Battles** (Chapter 5): The sequence of engagements culminating in God's victory

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The War Scroll is significant across multiple traditions:

- **Essene**: Reflects the community's self-understanding as the army of God destined for the final battle
- **Judaism**: Illuminates apocalyptic currents within Second Temple Judaism
- **Christianity**: Provides crucial background for New Testament apocalyptic imagery, especially Revelation
- **Academic**: A key text for studying ancient Jewish warfare ideology and apocalypticism

| Theme | Description |
