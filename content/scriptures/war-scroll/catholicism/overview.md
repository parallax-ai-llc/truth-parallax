## Overview

Catholic scholarship approaches the War Scroll with interest in its fusion of liturgical worship and eschatological combat, a combination that resonates with the Catholic concept of the *Ecclesia Militans* — the Church Militant. The scroll's vision of priests leading the battle through prayer, blessing, and trumpet blasts anticipates the Catholic understanding of spiritual warfare as fundamentally sacramental and liturgical.

## Interpretive Approach

Catholic interpreters read the War Scroll within the context of Sacred Tradition, noting how the scroll's themes of angelic assistance, priestly mediation, and cosmic combat find development in the Church's liturgical and theological heritage. The text is valued as a witness to beliefs about angelic warfare and divine intervention that the Church Fathers would later incorporate into Christian angelology and eschatology.

## Key Emphases

Catholic scholarship highlights the War Scroll's emphasis on angelic participation in human affairs, which resonates with Catholic teaching on guardian angels and the intercession of saints. The priestly direction of battle through liturgical actions — blessings, trumpet calls, and prayers — parallels the Catholic understanding of the Mass as a participation in the heavenly liturgy and a spiritual weapon against evil. The scroll's structured approach to the final conflict also invites comparison with the development of just war theory in the Catholic moral tradition.

## Significance

The War Scroll demonstrates to Catholic scholarship that the integration of worship, spiritual combat, and community discipline has deep roots in the biblical world. It provides historical precedent for the Catholic conviction that the liturgy is the Church's primary weapon in the ongoing struggle against the powers of darkness.
