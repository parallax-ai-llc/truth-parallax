## The Sovereignty of God

Catholic scholars read Chapter 5's decisive seventh battle — in which God directly intervenes to secure victory — as an expression of the absolute sovereignty of God over history. Catholic theology affirms that while human cooperation with grace is genuinely significant, the ultimate defeat of evil depends on God's initiative. The War Scroll's pattern of human effort reaching its limit and divine power completing the work resonates with the Catholic understanding of the relationship between grace and free will.

## The Paschal Mystery

The alternating pattern of victory and defeat in the seven battles invites comparison with the Paschal Mystery — the pattern of death and resurrection that lies at the heart of Catholic faith. The Sons of Light must endure defeat before experiencing God's final triumph, just as Christ passed through death before rising to eternal glory. Catholic theology sees this pattern replicated in the life of every believer and in the history of the Church.

## The Te Deum of Victory

The post-battle hymns of thanksgiving described in this chapter anticipate the great hymns of Catholic liturgical tradition, particularly the *Te Deum*, sung in thanksgiving for God's mighty acts. Catholic worship has always understood praise as the proper response to divine victory, and the War Scroll's integration of military triumph with liturgical celebration reflects a conviction that Catholic tradition fully shares.
