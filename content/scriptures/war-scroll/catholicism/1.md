## The Church Militant

Catholic scholars read Chapter 1's description of the final war in light of the traditional distinction between the Church Militant (on earth), the Church Suffering (in purgatory), and the Church Triumphant (in heaven). The War Scroll's vision of the faithful engaged in cosmic combat resonates with the Catholic understanding that the earthly Church is engaged in an ongoing spiritual battle against the powers of evil, sustained by the sacraments and the intercession of the saints.

## The Identification of Evil

The chapter's mapping of cosmic enemies onto specific historical powers invites comparison with Catholic social teaching's analysis of "structures of sin" — systemic evils that require not merely individual conversion but collective action and divine grace to overcome. Catholic scholars note that the War Scroll, like Catholic tradition, recognizes that evil operates through both personal sin and institutional corruption.

## Eschatological Hope

The chapter's vision of a final divine victory sustains a hope that Catholic theology shares. While Catholic eschatology emphasizes the inaugurated character of the kingdom — already present in the sacraments and the life of the Church — it also looks forward to the consummation, when Christ will return in glory, evil will be definitively defeated, and "God will be all in all" (1 Corinthians 15:28).
