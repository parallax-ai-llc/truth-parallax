## The Search for God in the Orthodox Tradition

Logia 30-38 address seeking and finding in terms that Orthodox theology evaluates against its own rich tradition of spiritual search. The Orthodox understanding of the spiritual life, articulated by the Fathers from Evagrius to Gregory Palamas, is one of ascent through purification (*katharsis*), illumination (*photismos*), and union (*theosis*). This path is embedded in the sacramental and liturgical life of the Church and guided by a spiritual father or mother. Thomas's solitary, self-directed seeking lacks this ecclesial, guided dimension.

Logion 37's image of stripping naked and trampling garments is evaluated by Orthodox interpreters in light of the baptismal tradition. In the early Church, as described by Cyril of Jerusalem and other catechetical Fathers, the baptismal candidate stripped off garments before entering the baptismal waters, signifying the putting off of the old self. Orthodox baptism retains the threefold immersion as the sacrament of new birth. Thomas uses this imagery without the sacramental act that gives it meaning — a characteristic Gnostic appropriation of ecclesiastical symbolism.

Logion 33's injunction to proclaim from the housetops what one hears in one's ear (parallel to Matthew 10:27) sits awkwardly within Thomas's overall framework of secrecy. Orthodox interpreters note this inconsistency: if these are secret sayings, why does one logion command public proclamation? Such tensions may reflect different layers of tradition incorporated into the collection, some originating in contexts more compatible with the public, kerygmatic character of Orthodox preaching.
