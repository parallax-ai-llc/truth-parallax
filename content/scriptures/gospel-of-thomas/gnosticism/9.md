## The Light Within All Things

Logia 77-85 contain some of the most explicitly Gnostic material in Thomas. Logion 77 is the great declaration of divine immanence: "I am the light that is over all things. I am the All. From me the All came forth, and to me the All has reached. Split a piece of wood; I am there. Lift up a stone, and you will find me there." Gnostic readers understand this as Jesus speaking not as a historical individual but as the divine Logos or Light that permeates all reality — the same light that constitutes the inner essence of every Gnostic soul.

Logion 83 distinguishes between two kinds of images: "The images are visible to people, but the light within them is hidden in the image of the father's light." This is the Gnostic doctrine of the hidden divine image — the true, luminous self concealed behind the visible, material form. The task of gnosis is to see through the outer image to the inner light, recognizing the divine spark that animates all authentic existence.

Logion 84 extends this teaching: "When you see your likeness, you are happy. But when you see your images that came into being before you, that neither die nor become visible — how much you will be able to bear!" The pre-existent "images" are the heavenly archetypes of the soul, the divine forms that existed in the Pleroma before the fall into matter. Encountering one's heavenly double is the culminating moment of Gnostic self-knowledge — overwhelming but ultimately liberating.
