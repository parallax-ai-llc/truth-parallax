## Making the Two One

Logia 96-105 return to the theme of transcending duality, with particular attention to the categories of male and female, inner and outer. Logion 97, the parable of the woman carrying a jar of meal that empties unnoticed, is read by Gnostic interpreters as a warning about the gradual, imperceptible loss of spiritual substance that occurs when the soul is not vigilant. The leaking jar represents the material body through which the divine spark slowly dissipates into the world.

Logion 101 presents a striking saying about father and mother: "Whoever does not hate his father and his mother as I do cannot be my disciple. And whoever does not love his father and mother as I do cannot be my disciple." The Gnostic reading distinguishes between the biological parents (who gave the disciple a material body and thus a prison) and the divine Father and Mother (the true spiritual parents from whom the soul originates in the Pleroma). One must hate the former and love the latter.

Logion 105 declares: "Whoever knows the father and the mother will be called the child of a prostitute." The Gnostic interpretation understands this provocatively: those who have attained true knowledge of their divine parentage will be despised by the ignorant world, which can only see them as illegitimate — children of no recognized earthly lineage. The world's contempt is the mark of the Gnostic's transcendence.
