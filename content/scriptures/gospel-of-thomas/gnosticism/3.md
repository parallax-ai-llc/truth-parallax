## The Garment of Flesh

Logia 21-29 explore the relationship between body and spirit, a theme of paramount importance in Gnostic anthropology. Logion 21 compares the disciples to children dwelling in a field that does not belong to them — an image the Gnostic tradition reads as describing the divine sparks temporarily inhabiting material bodies in an alien world created by the demiurge. The instruction to "strip naked" and return the field to its owners signifies the shedding of the material body at death or through ascetic practice.

Logion 22 introduces the theme of making "the two into one" — male and female, inner and outer, above and below. This is the Gnostic concept of *syzygy* (pairing) and ultimate reunification: the divided self must be restored to its original wholeness. The specific mention of making "an image in place of an image" points to the Gnostic teaching that the earthly, material image of the human being must be replaced by its heavenly, spiritual archetype.

Logion 29 states: "If the flesh came into being because of spirit, that is a marvel. But if spirit came into being because of the body, that is a marvel of marvels." Gnostic readers understand this as affirming the priority of spirit over matter while expressing astonishment at the paradox of divine light becoming imprisoned in material form. The body is the tomb or prison of the spirit, and liberation comes through recognition of one's true, non-material identity.
