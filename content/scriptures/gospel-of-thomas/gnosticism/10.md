## Rejection of the Cosmos

Logia 86-95 intensify Thomas's critique of the material world and conventional social structures. Logion 87 states: "Wretched is the body that depends on a body, and wretched is the soul that depends on these two." The Gnostic reading sees a threefold hierarchy — spirit, soul, body — in which the body is the lowest element, the soul is intermediate and potentially salvageable, and the spirit (the divine spark) is the true self that must be freed from its double imprisonment in soul and body.

Logion 89 asks why people wash the outside of the cup but fail to recognize that the one who made the inside also made the outside. Gnostic interpreters read this not as a call to moral consistency (as in the Synoptic parallel) but as a statement about the fundamental unity of inner and outer creation — both proceed from the same divine source, even if the outer world has become corrupted through the demiurge's ignorance.

Logion 95 counsels against lending at interest, which Gnostic readers understand metaphorically: do not invest your spiritual resources in worldly transactions that yield only material returns. The Gnostic must direct all energy toward the single goal of awakening, refusing to be drawn into the economic and social systems that bind the soul to the material world.
