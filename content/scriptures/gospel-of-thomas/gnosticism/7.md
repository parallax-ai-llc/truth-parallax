## Blessed Are the Persecuted

Logia 58-67 address the themes of suffering, blessing, and the cost of spiritual knowledge. Logion 58 declares: "Blessed is the person who has labored and has found life." Gnostic interpreters understand this "labor" not as moral effort or good works but as the arduous interior work of self-knowledge — the painful process of awakening from the sleep of material existence and confronting the disturbing truth of one's cosmic situation.

Logion 61 presents a dialogue with Salome in which Jesus declares, "I am the one who comes from what is whole... I was given some of the things of my father." Salome's response — "I am your disciple" — and the subsequent teaching about light and darkness establish the Gnostic theme of the revealer's divine origin. Jesus's wholeness contrasts with the dividedness of ordinary human existence; his mission is to restore that wholeness to those capable of receiving his teaching.

Logion 67 warns: "One who knows the All but fails to know oneself lacks everything." This is perhaps the most concise statement of the Gnostic principle in Thomas: cosmological knowledge without self-knowledge is worthless. The All — the totality of reality — is already within the self, and it is only through interior discovery that the external All can be truly comprehended. Knowledge of the macrocosm proceeds from knowledge of the microcosm.
