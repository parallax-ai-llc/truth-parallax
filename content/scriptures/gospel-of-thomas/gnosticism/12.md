## The Final Transformation

Logia 106-114 bring the Gospel of Thomas to its culmination with teachings on union, transformation, and the ultimate destiny of the Gnostic soul. Logion 106 promises: "When you make the two into one, you will become children of humanity, and when you say, 'Mountain, move away,' it will move away." The reunification of all dualities — the great Gnostic project that has been the thread running through the entire gospel — confers supernatural power, demonstrating the soul's mastery over the material world.

Logion 108 identifies the one who drinks from Jesus's mouth with Jesus himself: "Whoever drinks from my mouth will become like me. I myself shall become that person." This is the Gnostic doctrine of identity between revealer and revealed — the teaching that gnosis dissolves the distinction between the divine teacher and the awakened disciple. The Gnostic who fully receives the secret sayings becomes one with the living Jesus.

Logion 114, the final and most controversial saying, addresses Mary Magdalene: "Simon Peter said to them, 'Let Mary leave us, for women are not worthy of life.' Jesus said, 'I myself shall lead her in order to make her male, so that she too may become a living spirit resembling you males.'" Gnostic interpretation reads this not as a statement about biological sex but as a teaching about the transcendence of duality itself. "Male" symbolizes the spiritual, unified state; "female" represents the divided, material condition. Making Mary "male" means restoring her to the undivided wholeness that is the birthright of every Gnostic soul.
