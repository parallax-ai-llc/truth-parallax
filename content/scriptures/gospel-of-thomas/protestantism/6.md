## Election and the Solitary Life

Logia 49-57 introduce the concept of the "solitary" (*monachos*) and the "elect," language that intersects with, but differs significantly from, Reformed Protestant theology of election. Logion 49 pronounces blessing on "the solitary and the elect, for you will find the kingdom." In Reformed theology, election is God's sovereign, gracious choice of sinners for salvation in Christ; in Thomas, election appears to describe an inherent spiritual capacity — the possession of a divine spark that distinguishes the spiritual elite from the ordinary mass of humanity.

Logion 53 addresses circumcision: "If it were useful, their father would produce children already circumcised from their mother. Rather, the true circumcision in spirit has become profitable in every respect." Protestant interpreters note the parallel to Paul's teaching about circumcision of the heart (Romans 2:29; Philippians 3:3), but observe that Paul's teaching is grounded in the work of the Holy Spirit in the context of the new covenant community, not in a Gnostic devaluation of the body.

Logion 57, the parable of the wheat and the weeds, has a close parallel in Matthew 13:24-30. In Matthew, the parable teaches patience with the mixed community of believers and unbelievers until the final judgment. Thomas presents the parable without this eschatological framework, raising for Protestant interpreters the question of whether Thomas has deliberately stripped away the future-oriented dimension of Jesus's kingdom teaching.
