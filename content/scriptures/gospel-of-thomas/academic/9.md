## Christology and Cosmology in Logia 77-85

Logia 77-85 contain material central to scholarly debates about Thomas's Christology and cosmology. Logion 77's "I am" saying — "I am the light that is over all things. I am the All" — has been compared to the Johannine "I am" sayings (John 8:12, 14:6) and to similar declarations in Gnostic texts (*Trimorphic Protennoia*, *Thunder: Perfect Mind*). Marvin Meyer and others have argued that Thomas's "I am" saying may derive from an early Jewish-Christian wisdom Christology in which Jesus speaks with the voice of personified Wisdom (cf. Proverbs 8, Sirach 24).

Logion 77b ("Split a piece of wood; I am there. Lift up a stone, and you will find me there") appears in a different location in the Greek P.Oxy. 1, where it is attached to a different saying. This displacement between the Greek and Coptic versions is significant for understanding the text's compositional fluidity. Scholars have also compared 77b to a saying attributed to Jesus by the Muslim scholar al-Ghazali and to pantheistic traditions in Stoic philosophy, raising questions about the cultural breadth of Thomas's sources.

Logion 82 — "Whoever is near me is near the fire" — is independently attested by Origen (*Homilies on Jeremiah* 20:3), giving it a strong claim to antiquity. It is one of the *agrapha* (unwritten sayings of Jesus not found in the canonical Gospels) that scholars have most frequently accepted as potentially authentic. Its dual attestation in Thomas and Origen — two independent sources — satisfies a key criterion of historical Jesus research: multiple attestation.
