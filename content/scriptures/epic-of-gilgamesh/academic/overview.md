## Overview

Academic study of the Epic of Gilgamesh is central to the field of Assyriology and ancient Near Eastern literary studies. Scholars work with cuneiform tablets in Sumerian and Akkadian to reconstruct, translate, and interpret what is widely regarded as the oldest surviving work of great literature.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Textual Reconstruction
The epic survives in multiple versions spanning over a millennium. Scholars must piece together fragmentary tablets from different periods and recensions to reconstruct the narrative, noting significant variations between the Old Babylonian, Middle Babylonian, and Standard Babylonian versions.

### Literary Analysis
Academic study examines the epic's sophisticated literary techniques including ring composition, parallelism, repetition with variation, and the use of dreams as narrative devices. The work of Andrew George has been particularly influential in demonstrating the epic's literary artistry.

### Historical Contextualization
Scholars situate the epic within its broader cultural context, examining connections to Sumerian kingship ideology, Mesopotamian religious practices, and the scribal traditions that preserved and transmitted the text.
