## Overview

Comparative study of the Epic of Gilgamesh places this ancient Mesopotamian masterpiece alongside other literary and religious traditions that grapple with mortality, friendship, heroism, and the human condition. The epic's flood narrative in Tablet XI has been particularly significant for comparative studies with biblical and other ancient flood traditions.

## Key Comparative Themes

### The Flood Narrative
The flood account told by Utnapishtim in Tablet XI has generated extensive comparative study:
- **Genesis 6-9**: The story of Noah and the Ark
- **Atrahasis Epic**: An earlier Mesopotamian flood narrative
- **Hindu Matsya Purana**: Manu and the great fish
- **Greek tradition**: Deucalion and Pyrrha

### The Hero's Quest
Gilgamesh's journey follows patterns found in hero narratives worldwide:
- **Odyssey**: Odysseus's wandering journey home
- **Mahabharata**: Arjuna's quests and trials
- **Beowulf**: The hero confronting mortality through combat

### Friendship and Loss
The Gilgamesh-Enkidu relationship parallels other great literary friendships:
- **Achilles and Patroclus** (Iliad)
- **David and Jonathan** (Hebrew Bible)
- **Krishna and Arjuna** (Bhagavad Gita)
