## Overview

An interfaith approach to the Epic of Gilgamesh discovers in this ancient Mesopotamian epic themes that resonate powerfully across the world's religious and philosophical traditions: the confrontation with mortality, the meaning of friendship, the search for wisdom, and the question of what constitutes a well-lived life.

## Key Interfaith Themes

### Confronting Mortality
Gilgamesh's crisis in the face of Enkidu's death resonates with:
- **Buddhism**: The Buddha's encounter with old age, sickness, and death
- **Christianity**: Christ's agony in Gethsemane
- **Hinduism**: Arjuna's crisis on the battlefield of Kurukshetra
- **Judaism**: Ecclesiastes' meditation on the vanity of human striving

### The Flood and Divine Judgment
The flood narrative in Tablet XI connects to:
- **Judaism and Christianity**: Noah's Ark (Genesis 6-9)
- **Islam**: The story of Nuh (Noah) in the Quran
- **Hinduism**: Manu and the great flood
- **Greek religion**: Deucalion and Pyrrha

### Friendship as Spiritual Bond
The Gilgamesh-Enkidu relationship resonates with:
- **Christianity**: Spiritual friendship (anam cara) in Celtic Christianity
- **Buddhism**: Kalyanamitra (spiritual friendship)
- **Sufism**: The bond between master and disciple
- **Judaism**: The covenant friendship of David and Jonathan
