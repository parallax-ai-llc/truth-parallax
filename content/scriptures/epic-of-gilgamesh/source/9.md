**Tablet IX**

**1** For his friend Enkidu Gilgamesh
**2** did bitterly weep as he wandered the wild:
**3** "I shall die, and shall I not then be as Enkidu?
**4** Sorrow has come into my belly.

**5** I fear death, and so I roam the wild,
**6** to find Utnapishtim, son of Ubar-Tutu.
**7** I have taken the road and travel swiftly.
**8** When I arrive at mountain passes at night,
**9** I see lions, I am afraid, I lift my head in prayer to Sin.

**10** To the warrior among the gods my prayers go:
**11** 'Keep me safe!'"

**12** He lay down at night and woke from a dream.
**13** He saw in the moonlight lions delighting in life.
**14** He drew his axe from his belt,
**15** he drew the sword from its scabbard,
**16** he fell upon them like an arrow.
**17** He struck them, scattered them, killed them.

**18** [Gap in text]

**19** He reached the mountains of Mashu,
**20** which every day guard the rising and setting of the sun.
**21** Their peaks reach as high as the banks of heaven,
**22** their udders reach as low as the Netherworld.

**23** Scorpion-beings guard its gate,
**24** whose terror is dread, whose glance is death.
**25** Their shimmering radiance covers the mountains.
**26** At sunrise and sunset they guard the sun.

**27** When Gilgamesh saw them, his face went dark
**28** with fear and dismay.
**29** He took hold of his wits
**30** and bowed before them.

**31** A Scorpion-being called out to his mate:
**32** "This one who has come to us—his body is the flesh of a god!"
**33** The Scorpion-being's mate answered him:
**34** "Two-thirds of him is divine, one-third is human."

**35** The Scorpion-being, the male, called out,
**36** he said to the offspring of the gods:
**37** "Why have you come so far a journey?
**38** Why have you come here before me,

**39** travelling a road so difficult,
**40** crossing seas whose crossing is treacherous?
**41** Let me learn the reason for your coming!"

**42** Gilgamesh spoke to the Scorpion-being:
**43** "I come on account of my ancestor Utnapishtim,
**44** who joined the Assembly of the Gods and was granted eternal life.
**45** About Death and Life I must ask him!"

**46** The Scorpion-being opened his mouth to speak,
**47** saying to Gilgamesh:
**48** "Never has there been, Gilgamesh, a mortal man who could do that.
**49** No one has crossed through the mountains,

**50** for twelve leagues it is darkness throughout—
**51** dense is the darkness, and light there is none.
**52** To the rising of the sun—
**53** to the setting of the sun—

**54** to the rising of the sun they sent him forth."

**55** [Gap in text]

**56** "Whether in sorrow or pain,
**57** in cold or heat,
**58** gasping and sighing—
**59** go, Gilgamesh, the gate of the mountain is open to you!"

**60** When Gilgamesh heard this,
**61** he heeded the Scorpion-being's words.
**62** Along the Road of the Sun he went.

**63** At one league the darkness became thick around him,
**64** for there was no light; he could see nothing ahead
**65** and nothing behind him.
**66** At two leagues the darkness became thick around him.

**67** At three leagues the darkness became thick.
**68** At four leagues the darkness became thick around him,
**69** for there was no light; he could see nothing ahead
**70** and nothing behind him.

**71** At five leagues the darkness became thick.
**72** At six leagues the darkness became thick around him.
**73** At seven leagues the darkness became thick.
**74** At eight leagues he hurried on, the darkness thick around him.

**75** At nine leagues he felt the north wind on his face,
**76** but the darkness was thick around him,
**77** for there was no light; he could see nothing ahead
**78** and nothing behind him.

**79** At ten leagues the distance to the end grew near.
**80** At eleven leagues dawn broke.
**81** At twelve leagues the sun streamed out.

**82** There was the garden of the gods; all round him stood
**83** bushes bearing gems.
**84** Seeing it he drew near.
**85** The carnelian tree was bearing fruit,

**86** hung with vines good to look at.
**87** The lapis lazuli tree was in leaf,
**88** the fruit it bore was fine to see.
**89** [Gap in text]

**90** There were thorns and briars, all of precious stone,
**91** agate and speckled chalcedony.
**92** The sea-stones shone like lapis lazuli.
**93** Like the new moon and the full moon was the lustre.

**94** Gilgamesh walked about in the garden of precious stone,
**95** and lifted his eyes and saw—
