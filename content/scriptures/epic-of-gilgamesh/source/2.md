**Tablet II**

**1** Enkidu sat before the harlot.
**2** [The account of the taming of Enkidu continues from Tablet I.]

**3** "Come, let me lead you to Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**4** to the pure house, the residence of Anu and Ishtar,
**5** where Gilgamesh is perfect in strength,
**6** and like a wild bull lords it over the folk."

**7** She spoke to him, and her speech found favour;
**8** he knew by instinct, he should seek a friend.
**9** Enkidu spoke to the woman:
**10** "Come, Shamhat, escort me
**11** to the pure sacred temple, the residence of Anu and Ishtar,
**12** where Gilgamesh lords it over the people.

**13** I will challenge him, for my strength is mighty.
**14** I will shout aloud in Uruk: 'I am the mightiest!
**15** I am the one who changes destinies,
**16** he who was born in the wilderness is mighty, strength is mine!'"

**17** Shamhat said: "Come, let us go that he may see your face.
**18** Let me show you Gilgamesh, a man of joy and woe!
**19** Look at him, gaze at his face,
**20** he is beautiful in manhood, dignified,
**21** his whole body is charged with seductive charm.
**22** He is more powerful in strength of arms than you!"

**23** She divided her garment, with one part she clothed him,
**24** with the other she clothed herself.
**25** She took his hand like a companion,
**26** and led him to the shepherds' huts, the place of the sheepfold.

**27** The shepherds gathered about him;
**28** they whispered among themselves:
**29** "This fellow—how like Gilgamesh in build,
**30** tall in stature, proud, and like a battlement!

**31** Surely he was born in the mountains,
**32** his strength is like the meteorite of Anu!"
**33** They set bread before him,
**34** they set beer before him.
**35** He did not eat the bread, he looked askance,
**36** stared and gaped.

**37** Enkidu had no knowledge of eating bread,
**38** nor of drinking beer had he ever been shown.
**39** The harlot opened her mouth and said to Enkidu:
**40** "Eat the bread, Enkidu, the staff of life.
**41** Drink the beer, the custom of the land."

**42** Enkidu ate the bread till he was full,
**43** drank the beer—seven goblets—
**44** his mood became free, he started to sing,
**45** his heart grew merry, his face glowed.

**46** The barber groomed his body, so matted with hair,
**47** he anointed himself with oil, and became a man.
**48** He put on clothing, and was like a bridegroom.
**49** He took up his weapon to do battle with lions.

**50** The shepherds could rest at night;
**51** he caught the wolves and lions,
**52** the chief cattlemen could lie down.
**53** Enkidu was their watchman, a wakeful man, a singular youth.

**54** He made merry with Shamhat.
**55** He raised his eyes and saw a man.
**56** He said to the harlot:
**57** "Shamhat, bring the man here. Why has he come?
**58** Let me learn his name."

**59** The harlot called out to the man,
**60** went over to him. "Young man, where are you off to?
**61** What is the purpose of your painful journey?"

**62** The young man answered Enkidu:
**63** "I was invited to a wedding feast.
**64** It is the custom of the people
**65** to select the bride—
**66** I heap up delicacies on the ceremonial platter.

**67** For the king of Uruk-the-Sheepfold
**68** the veil is opened for the people to choose.
**69** For Gilgamesh, king of Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**70** the veil is opened for the people to choose.

**71** He mates with the destined wife,
**72** he first, the groom after.
**73** By the counsel of the gods it is so ordained:
**74** from the cutting of his umbilical cord it has been destined for him."

**75** At the words of the young man
**76** his face flushed with anger.

**77** Enkidu walked in front, with Shamhat behind him.
**78** When he entered Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**79** the crowd gathered about him.
**80** He stood in the street of Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**81** the people congregated around and said of him:

**82** "He is like Gilgamesh to a hair!
**83** Though shorter in stature, he is stronger of bone!
**84** This is the man who ate grass with the gazelles,
**85** who was reared on the milk of wild animals!

**86** In all the land his strength is mightiest."
**87** The men rejoiced:
**88** "Now Gilgamesh has met his match.
**89** This great one, this hero whose beauty is like a god,
**90** he is a match even for Gilgamesh."

**91** For Ishara the bed was laid out.
**92** Gilgamesh met the bride in the night.
**93** Enkidu blocked his access at the door of the marital chamber.
**94** He thrust his foot in to block the door.

**95** They grappled with each other,
**96** snorting like bulls.
**97** They shattered the doorpost;
**98** the wall shook.
**99** Gilgamesh and Enkidu grappled with each other,
**100** snorting like bulls;
**101** they shattered the doorpost,
**102** the wall shook.

**103** Gilgamesh bent his knee,
**104** his foot on the ground,
**105** his fury abated;
**106** he broke off the fight.

**107** After he broke off the fight,
**108** Enkidu spoke to him, to Gilgamesh:
**109** "As one alone your mother bore you,
**110** the wild cow of the enclosure, Ninsun;
**111** she raised you above the warriors,
**112** Enlil has granted you the kingship over the people."

**113** [They embraced and their friendship was sealed.]

**114** Gilgamesh opened his mouth, and said to Enkidu:
**115** "Now let us go to the Forest of Cedar,
**116** the dwelling place of Humbaba.
**117** Let us make the land hear our thundering."

**118** Enkidu opened his mouth, and said to Gilgamesh:
**119** "I knew him, my friend, in the uplands,
**120** when I roamed with the wild beasts.
**121** The forest is sixty leagues in every direction.
**122** Who would go down into its interior?

**123** Humbaba—his roaring is the Deluge,
**124** his mouth is fire, his breath is death!
**125** Why do you desire to do this thing?
**126** An unwinnable battle is Humbaba's ambush!"

**127** Gilgamesh opened his mouth and said to Enkidu:
**128** "I will climb the mountain forest,
**129** I will cut down the Cedar.
**130** I will establish my name for eternity."

**131** "My friend, if we go to the forest together,
**132** the worry will be lighter."

**133** Enkidu opened his mouth to speak, saying to Gilgamesh:
**134** "My friend, I have known the land—
**135** with the wild beasts I roamed all over it.
**136** The forest is sixty double-leagues in every direction.

**137** Who is there who would go into the forest?
**138** Humbaba's roaring is the Flood Storm,
**139** his mouth is Fire, his breath means Death!

**140** At sixty leagues he can hear the wild cow of his forest;
**141** who would go down into his forest?
**142** Enlil has appointed him to guard it:
**143** to terrify the people Enlil appointed him."

**144** Gilgamesh spoke to Enkidu, saying:
**145** "Who, my friend, can go up to heaven?
**146** Only the gods dwell with Shamash forever.
**147** As for mankind, their days are numbered,
**148** whatever they achieve is but wind.

**149** Even you are afraid of death!
**150** What has become of your bold strength?
**151** I will go in front of you,
**152** and your mouth can cry out: 'Go closer, do not be afraid!'

**153** Should I fall, I will have established my fame.
**154** 'Gilgamesh has fallen in battle with ferocious Humbaba!'

**155** You were born and raised in the wilderness,
**156** a lion attacked you, and you experienced all.
**157** People will say: 'He is truly brave,
**158** who ventures where Humbaba dwells.'"

**159** The elders of Uruk-the-Sheepfold spoke to Gilgamesh:
**160** "You are young, Gilgamesh, your heart has carried you away.
**161** You do not know what you are attempting.
**162** Humbaba's roaring is the Deluge,
**163** his mouth is Fire, his breath is Death.

**164** Why do you want to do this?
**165** An irresistible onslaught is Humbaba's raid."

**166** Gilgamesh listened to the word of his counsellors.
**167** He looked at his friend and laughed.
**168** "How shall I answer them, shall I say
**169** 'I am afraid of him, let me stay behind'?"

**170** They brought his weapons to him:
**171** mighty swords, bows, and quivers.
**172** He took the axes in his hand,
**173** his quiver and the bow of Anshan.

**174** The elders of Uruk-the-Sheepfold blessed Gilgamesh
**175** and gave counsel for the journey:
**176** "Do not trust, Gilgamesh, in your own strength alone.
**177** Let your eyes see all, make your blow strike home.

**178** He who goes in front saves his companion,
**179** he who knows the path protects his friend.
**180** Let Enkidu go in front of you;
**181** he knows the way to the Cedar Forest.

**182** He has seen battle, experienced combat.
**183** Enkidu will protect his friend and safeguard his companion,
**184** and bring his body back over the wide earth.

**185** May Shamash grant you your desire,
**186** may he let your eyes see what your mouth has spoken.
**187** May he open for you the barred path,
**188** the road made straight for your footsteps.

**189** May he open for you the mountain passes at night,
**190** may Lugalbanda stand beside you in your need."

**191** Gilgamesh opened his mouth to speak, saying to Enkidu:
**192** "Come, my friend, let us go to the Egalmah temple,
**193** before Ninsun, the Great Queen.
**194** Ninsun the wise, who is versed in all knowledge,
**195** will send us on our way with good counsel."

**196** Taking each other by the hand,
**197** Gilgamesh and Enkidu went to the Egalmah temple.
**198** Before Ninsun the Great Queen
**199** Gilgamesh rose up and entered before her:
**200** "O Ninsun, I have to take a long journey
**201** to the Land of Humbaba,
**202** a road I have never travelled,
**203** a road I do not know!"
