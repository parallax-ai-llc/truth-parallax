**Tablet IV**

**1** At twenty leagues they broke for some food,
**2** at thirty leagues they stopped for the night.
**3** Fifty leagues they walked in one day,
**4** in three days a walk of a month and a half.
**5** They drew near to Mount Lebanon.

**6** Towards sunset they dug a well,
**7** they filled their waterskins,
**8** Gilgamesh went up onto the mountain,
**9** he poured out a flour-offering and said:

**10** "Mountain, bring me a dream, a favourable one!"

**11** Enkidu did for Gilgamesh what travellers do:
**12** he sought a propitious wind, seeking a dream.
**13** A squall passed and he made him lie down.
**14** He threw him down in a circle of flour.

**15** [The sleep that flows over mankind swept over him.]
**16** In the middle of the night he finished his sleep.
**17** He got up and told his dream to his friend:

**18** "My friend, did you not call me? Why did I wake?
**19** Did you not touch me? Why am I startled?
**20** Did a god not pass by? Why is my flesh numb?
**21** My friend, I have had a dream—

**22** and the dream that I had was very disturbing.
**23** At the foot of the mountain there lay a great cedar—
**24** its shade was beautiful, the deepest shade.
**25** We stood there in the deep shade of the cedar."

**26** [Gap in text]

**27** Enkidu spoke, interpreting the dream for his friend:
**28** "My friend, your dream is favourable!
**29** The dream is very precious as an omen.
**30** My friend, the mountain which you saw
**31** means that we shall capture Humbaba and slay him,
**32** and his corpse shall lie upon the plain."

**33** At twenty leagues they broke for some food,
**34** at thirty leagues they stopped for the night.
**35** Fifty leagues they walked in one day,
**36** in three days a walk of a month and a half.

**37** They dug a well facing the setting sun,
**38** Gilgamesh went up onto the mountain.
**39** He poured out his flour-offering and said:
**40** "Mountain, bring me a dream, a favourable one!"

**41** Enkidu did for Gilgamesh what travellers do:
**42** he sought a propitious wind, seeking a dream.
**43** A squall passed and he made him lie down.
**44** He threw him down in a circle of flour.

**45** [The sleep that flows over mankind swept over him.]
**46** In the middle of the night he finished his sleep.
**47** He got up and told his dream to his friend:

**48** "My friend, did you not call me? Why did I wake?
**49** Did you not touch me? Why am I startled?
**50** Did a god not pass by? Why is my flesh numb?
**51** My friend, I have had a second dream—

**52** In my dream, my friend, a mountain toppled.
**53** It struck me, caught me by my feet.
**54** The light became dazzling; a man appeared,
**55** the most handsome in the land, his grace—
**56** He pulled me out from under the mountain,
**57** he gave me water to drink and my heart grew calm."

**58** Enkidu spoke, interpreting the dream for his friend:
**59** "My friend, your dream is favourable!
**60** The dream is very precious as an omen.
**61** The mountain, my friend, which you saw—
**62** it is Humbaba.
**63** We shall capture Humbaba and slay him,
**64** and cast his body upon the plain."

**65** At twenty leagues they broke for some food,
**66** at thirty leagues they stopped for the night.
**67** Fifty leagues they walked in one day,
**68** in three days a walk of a month and a half.

**69** They dug a well facing Shamash,
**70** Gilgamesh went up onto the mountain.
**71** He poured out his flour-offering and said:
**72** "Mountain, bring me a dream, a favourable one!"

**73** Enkidu did for Gilgamesh what travellers do.
**74** A squall passed and he made him lie down.
**75** He threw him down in a circle of flour.
**76** [The sleep that flows over mankind swept over him.]

**77** In the middle of the night he finished his sleep.
**78** He got up and told his dream to his friend:
**79** "My friend, did you not call me? Why did I wake?
**80** Did a god not pass by? Why is my flesh numb?

**81** My friend, I have had a third dream,
**82** and the dream that I had was very disturbing.
**83** The sky cried out, the earth thundered.
**84** Daylight failed, darkness came.

**85** Lightning flashed, fire blazed.
**86** The clouds thickened, raining death.
**87** Then the brightness vanished, the fire went out,
**88** and everything that had been falling turned to ashes."

**89** Enkidu spoke, interpreting the dream for his friend:
**90** "My friend, your dream is favourable!"

**105** Then Gilgamesh was overcome with dread.
**106** Enkidu opened his mouth and said to Gilgamesh:
**107** "The journey has exhausted you,
**108** and now you stand here uncertain whether to go on or turn back."

**109** Gilgamesh spoke to Enkidu:
**110** "Why, my friend, do we speak like weaklings?
**111** Have we not crossed all the mountains?
**112** The task that stands before us—
**113** before we can go further, let us push on!

**114** Should I not now have trembled in every limb?
**115** My friend, we have arrived at the forest.
**116** Now let us deliver our attack.
**117** We will surely prevail over all."

**118** They stood there marvelling at the forest,
**119** gazing at the lofty cedars.
**120** At the face of the forest where Humbaba was wont to walk
**121** there was a path; straight were the tracks, a good path.

**122** They saw the Cedar Mountain, dwelling of the gods, throne-dais of Irnini.
**123** Before the mountain the cedars raised their luxuriance.
**124** Their shade was good, filling one with happiness.
**125** The undergrowth was tangled, the forest dense.
