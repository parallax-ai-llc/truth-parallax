**Tablet V**

**1** They stood there marvelling at the forest,
**2** gazing at the lofty cedars,
**3** gazing at the entrance to the forest.
**4** Where Humbaba was wont to walk there was a path;
**5** the tracks were well trodden, the way was good.

**6** They saw the Cedar Mountain, abode of the gods,
**7** the throne-dais of Irnini.
**8** The cedars raised their luxuriance on the mountain face,
**9** their shade was good, filling one with joy.

**10** The thorns were matted, the thicket dense,
**11** the forest of the cedars was their joy.
**12** [Gap in text]

**13** Humbaba came forth from his dwelling.
**14** He fixed his eye, the eye of death.
**15** He shook his head and beckoned at Gilgamesh:
**16** "You are a fool, Gilgamesh!

**17** You who have a place among the cattle,
**18** your mother who bore you a calf of a wild cow—
**19** Why have you come here?
**20** You have brought before me
**21** this hireling on your right hand
**22** and this vagabond on your left."

**23** [Gap in text]

**24** Enkidu opened his mouth and spoke, saying to Gilgamesh:
**25** "My friend, Humbaba's features have changed!
**26** Though boldly we have come up to his lair,
**27** my heart will not quickly—"

**28** Enkidu opened his mouth and spoke, saying to Gilgamesh:
**29** "One alone cannot—
**30** A slippery path is not feared by two people who help each other.
**31** Twice three times—
**32** a three-ply rope cannot be cut.

**33** The mighty lion—two cubs can roll him over."

**34** [Gap in text]

**35** Gilgamesh spoke to Enkidu:
**36** "My friend, why do you speak like a weakling?
**37** With your spineless words you make me despondent.
**38** My friend, the task is set; the tools lie at hand:
**39** shall we not now deliver our attack?"

**40** [Gap in text]

**41** Shamash stirred up great winds against Humbaba:
**42** the south wind, the north wind, the east wind, the west wind,
**43** the blasting wind, the counterwind, the typhoon,
**44** the whirlwind, the tempest, the gust, the dustdevil,
**45** the ice wind, the storm, the sandstorm:
**46** thirteen winds rose up against him
**47** and Humbaba's face darkened.

**48** He could not charge forward, he could not retreat.
**49** The weapons of Gilgamesh reached Humbaba.

**50** Humbaba spoke to Gilgamesh:
**51** "Let me go, Gilgamesh, you will be my master,
**52** and I will be your servant.
**53** Let me go, and I will cut for you the trees of my forest,
**54** I will build you a house from their timber."

**55** But Enkidu spoke to Gilgamesh:
**56** "My friend, do not listen to what Humbaba says!
**57** Do not heed his entreaties!"
**58** [Gap in text]

**59** "If the guardian goes back to the thick of the forest,
**60** if he goes back to put on his seven coats of armour—
**61** come on, do not hold back!
**62** Do not stand there speaking!"

**63** [Gap in text]

**64** Humbaba spoke to Enkidu:
**65** "You know the rules of my forest, the rules—
**66** and you understand all the things so ordered by Enlil.
**67** I should have carried you up, and killed you
**68** at the very entrance of the branches of my forest.

**69** I should have fed your flesh to the screeching vulture,
**70** the eagle, and the vulture.
**71** So now, Enkidu, clemency is up to you:
**72** speak to Gilgamesh that he spare my life!"

**73** Enkidu opened his mouth and spoke, saying to Gilgamesh:
**74** "My friend, the guardian of the Cedar Forest—
**75** finish him off, slay him, destroy his power,
**76** before the god Enlil, the foremost, hears what we do!

**77** The great gods will become angry with us—
**78** Enlil in Nippur, Shamash in Sippar.
**79** Establish your renown for ever:
**80** 'Gilgamesh who slew Humbaba!'"

**81** Humbaba heard and—
**82** [Gap in text]

**83** Gilgamesh heeded the word of his companion.
**84** He took the axe in his hand,
**85** he drew the sword from his belt.
**86** Gilgamesh struck him in the neck.

**87** Enkidu, his friend, struck the second blow.
**88** At the third blow Humbaba fell.

**89** Confusion—
**90** the watchman of the forest lay dead.
**91** For two leagues the cedars resounded.
**92** Enkidu made the kill.

**93** The forest trembled; the mountains of Lebanon trembled.
**94** The mountains quaked and the hillsides—
**95** He slew the ogre, the forest's guardian.
**96** The seven splendours—their radiance was extinguished.

**97** He struck the ground, the trees of the forest trembled.
**98** Then they cut down the cedar.
**99** Enkidu selected the timber, saying to Gilgamesh:
**100** "My friend, we have felled a tall cedar
**101** whose top scrapes the sky.

**102** I will make from it a door, six poles high, two poles wide,
**103** one cubit thick. Its pivot, ferrule, and doorframe shall be of one piece.
**104** Let me carry it to Nippur on the flood—
**105** the Euphrates shall bear it,
**106** Nippur will rejoice."

**107** They tied together a raft;
**108** they laid the cedar upon it.
**109** Enkidu steered the raft
**110** while Gilgamesh held the head of Humbaba.
