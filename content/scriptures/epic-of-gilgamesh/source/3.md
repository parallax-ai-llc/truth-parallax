**Tablet III**

**1** The elders spoke to Gilgamesh, saying:
**2** "Gilgamesh, do not put your trust in your vast strength alone.
**3** Let your eyes be sharp, guard yourself!
**4** Let Enkidu go ahead of you;
**5** he knows the way, he has travelled the road.

**6** Within the forest, all the passes
**7** of Humbaba, he has knowledge of them.
**8** He who goes first protects his companion,
**9** he who knows the route shelters his friend.

**10** Let Enkidu go before you,
**11** the ground he knows; he has travelled the way.
**12** Through all the passes of the forest,
**13** he knows the ways of Humbaba."

**14** The elders addressed the young men,
**15** giving Enkidu their counsel for the journey:
**16** "We entrust the king to you; bring him back safely.
**17** Enkidu, you shall guard him—
**18** bring your companion back to us."

**19** Enkidu opened his mouth to speak,
**20** saying to Gilgamesh:
**21** "My friend, turn back!
**22** A journey that is not—"
**23** [Gap in text]

**24** Gilgamesh made offering and spoke the prayer:
**25** "O Shamash, I am going; I lift up my hands in prayer.
**26** May it be well with my soul.
**27** Bring me back to the quay of Uruk-the-Sheepfold.
**28** Establish over me your protection!"

**29** Gilgamesh called out to his friend,
**30** had his oracle taken.
**31** [Gap in text]

**32** Tears streamed down the face of Gilgamesh.
**33** "A road I have never travelled,
**34** a way I do not know—
**35** if only I could be sure that the venture is favoured,
**36** I would carry it out with a happy heart.

**37** They placed the headdress on my head—
**38** I shall go to a land I do not know!"
**39** [Gap in text]

**40** Gilgamesh and Enkidu went to the Great Palace
**41** to take leave of Ninsun, the Great Queen.
**42** Ninsun entered her chamber,
**43** [she washed her body with tamarisk, she donned a robe of?]
**44** she put on a garment suitable for her body,
**45** she put on an ornament suitable for her breast,
**46** she placed a tiara on her head.

**47** She climbed the stairs, mounted to the parapet,
**48** ascended to the roof, and set up an incense offering to Shamash.
**49** She set out the offering before Shamash,
**50** and raised her arms:

**51** "O Shamash, why have you given my son Gilgamesh a restless heart?
**52** Now you have touched him and he goes
**53** on a long journey to the place of Humbaba.
**54** He faces a battle he does not understand,
**55** he travels a road he does not know.

**56** Until the day that he goes and returns,
**57** until he reaches the Cedar Forest,
**58** until he has slain fierce Humbaba,
**59** and destroyed from the land all the evil you despise—

**60** In the day, when you traverse the sky,
**61** may Aya the Bride not fear to remind you,
**62** 'Commend him to the watchmen of the night!'

**63** You have opened, O Shamash, the way for the wild animals,
**64** you cross over the earth and open the mountain passes.
**65** You illumine the darkness, the monsters of the deep see your light.
**66** The great teeming rivers carry your radiance."

**67** Ninsun the Great Queen made an end of her prayer,
**68** she extinguished the incense and came down from the parapet.
**69** She called Enkidu and announced her decision:

**70** "Mighty Enkidu, you are not of my womb,
**71** yet I herewith adopt you
**72** with the devotees of Gilgamesh,
**73** the priestesses, the votaries, and the servers of the temple."

**74** She placed a pendant around Enkidu's neck.
**75** "I have taken Enkidu; Enkidu to Gilgamesh I have given."
**76** [Gap in text]

**77** "Enkidu, I entrust my strong one to you,
**78** bring him back in safety to me."
**79** [Gap in text]

**80** The counsellors blessed Gilgamesh:
**81** "May you achieve your heart's desire.
**82** May the god Shamash let you see with your eyes
**83** what your mouth has spoken.

**84** May he open for you the barred path,
**85** the road for your footsteps.
**86** May he open for you the mountain passes,
**87** the nighttime bring you blessings.

**88** May Lugalbanda stand beside you.
**89** Achieve your desire and win the victory.
**90** As a young child returns to his mother's lap,
**91** may you return to Uruk!"

**92** Enkidu spoke to Gilgamesh:
**93** "Since you have decided, make the journey!
**94** Let your heart not fear, look straight ahead!
**95** To the place of the forest path, the way I have walked—
**96** trust me and we shall go together."

**97** [Gap in text—further preparations for the journey]
