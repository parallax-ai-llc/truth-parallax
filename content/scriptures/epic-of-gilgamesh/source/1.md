**Tablet I**

**1** He who saw the Deep, the foundation of the country,
**2** who knew the ways, was wise in all matters!
**3** Gilgamesh, who saw the Deep, the foundation of the country,
**4** who knew the ways, was wise in all matters!

**5** He explored everywhere the seats of power,
**6** and knew the totality of wisdom about all things.
**7** He saw what was secret, discovered what was hidden,
**8** he brought back a tale of before the Deluge.

**9** He came a far road, was weary, found peace,
**10** and set all his labours on a tablet of stone.
**11** He built the wall of Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**12** of holy Eanna, the sacred storehouse.

**13** See its wall like a strand of wool,
**14** view its parapet that none could copy!
**15** Take the stairway of a bygone era,
**16** draw near to Eanna, seat of Ishtar the goddess,
**17** that no later king could ever copy!

**18** Climb Uruk's wall and walk back and forth!
**19** Survey its foundations, examine the brickwork!
**20** Were its bricks not fired in an oven?
**21** Did the Seven Sages not lay its foundations?

**22** One square mile is city, one square mile is orchards,
**23** one square mile is claypits, as well as the open ground of Ishtar's temple.
**24** Three square miles and the open ground comprise Uruk.

**25** Find the copper tablet-box,
**26** undo its bronze lock,
**27** open the door to its secret,
**28** lift out the lapis lazuli tablet and read it,
**29** the story of that man, Gilgamesh, who went through all kinds of sufferings.

**30** Surpassing all kings, powerful and tall
**31** beyond all others, violent, splendid,
**32** a wild bull of a man, unvanquished leader,
**33** hero in the front line, beloved of his soldiers,
**34** fortress they called him, protector of the people,
**35** raging flood-wave who destroys even walls of stone.

**36** Two-thirds of him was divine, one-third of him was human.
**37** The Great Goddess Aruru designed the model for his body,
**38** she prepared his form—
**39** beautiful, handsomest of men,
**40** perfect.

**41** He walks around in the enclosure of Uruk,
**42** like a wild bull he makes himself mighty, head raised over others.
**43** There is no rival who can raise his weapon against him.
**44** His fellows stand at the alert, attentive to his orders.

**45** The men of Uruk become troubled in their chambers:
**46** "Gilgamesh sounds the tocsin for his amusement,
**47** his arrogance has no bounds by day or night.
**48** No son is left with his father, for Gilgamesh takes them all;
**49** and is this the shepherd of the city, wise, comely, and resolute?

**50** Gilgamesh does not leave a girl to her mother,
**51** the warrior's daughter, the young man's bride."
**52** The gods kept hearing their complaints,
**53** the gods of the sky cried out to the Lord of Uruk, to Anu:

**54** "You have indeed brought into being a mighty wild bull, head raised!
**55** There is no rival who can raise a weapon against him.
**56** His fellows stand at the alert, attentive to his orders.
**57** Gilgamesh does not leave a son to his father.
**58** Day and night his tyranny grows harsher.

**59** Yet he is the shepherd of Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**60** Gilgamesh, guide of the teeming people.
**61** Though he is their shepherd and their protector,
**62** powerful, pre-eminent, expert and mighty,
**63** Gilgamesh does not leave a girl to her betrothed!"

**64** The god Anu listened to their complaints,
**65** and the gods cried out to Aruru the great:
**66** "It was you, Aruru, who created mankind;
**67** now create a zikru to him,
**68** let him be equal to the stormy heart of Gilgamesh,
**69** and let them be a match for each other, so that Uruk may find peace!"

**70** When Aruru heard this she created within herself the zikru of Anu.
**71** Aruru washed her hands, she pinched off clay and threw it into the wilderness.
**72** In the wilderness she created valiant Enkidu,
**73** born of Silence, endowed with strength by Ninurta.

**74** His whole body was shaggy with hair,
**75** he had a full head of hair like a woman,
**76** his locks billowed in profusion like Ashnan.
**77** He knew neither people nor settled living,
**78** but wore a garment like Sumukan.

**79** He ate grasses with the gazelles,
**80** and jostled at the watering hole with the animals.
**81** As with animals, his thirst was slaked with mere water.

**82** A notorious trapper
**83** came face to face with him opposite the watering hole.
**84** A first, a second, and a third day
**85** he came face to face with him opposite the watering hole.

**86** On seeing him the trapper's face went stark with fear,
**87** and he and his animals drew back home.
**88** He was rigid with fear, though stock-still
**89** his heart pounded, his face drained of colour.

**90** He had woe in his belly,
**91** his face looked like that of one who has made a long journey.
**92** The trapper addressed his father saying:

**93** "Father, a certain fellow has come from the mountains.
**94** He is the mightiest in the land, his strength is as mighty as the meteorite of Anu!
**95** He continually goes over the mountains,
**96** he continually jostles at the watering place with the animals.

**97** He fills in the pits that I have dug,
**98** wrenches out my traps that I have spread,
**99** released from my grasp the wild animals.
**100** He does not let me make my rounds in the wilderness!"

**101** The trapper's father spoke to him saying:
**102** "My son, there lives in Uruk a certain Gilgamesh.
**103** There is no one stronger than he,
**104** he is as strong as the meteorite of Anu.

**105** Go, set off to Uruk,
**106** tell Gilgamesh of this man of might.
**107** He will give you the harlot Shamhat, take her with you.
**108** The woman shall overcome the fellow as if she were strong.

**109** When the animals are drinking at the watering place
**110** have her take off her robe and expose her sex.
**111** When he sees her he will draw near to her,
**112** and his animals, who grew up in his wilderness, will become alien to him."

**113** He heeded his father's advice.
**114** The trapper went off to Uruk.
**115** He made the journey, stood inside of Uruk,
**116** and declared to Gilgamesh:

**117** "There is a certain fellow who has come from the mountains,
**118** he is the mightiest in the land.
**119** His strength is as mighty as the meteorite of Anu!
**120** He continually goes over the mountains,
**121** he continually jostles at the watering place with the animals.

**122** He fills in the pits that I have dug,
**123** wrenches out my traps that I have spread,
**124** released from my grasp the wild animals.
**125** He does not let me make my rounds in the wilderness!"

**126** Gilgamesh said to the trapper:
**127** "Go, trapper, bring the harlot, Shamhat, with you.
**128** When the animals are drinking at the watering place
**129** have her take off her robe and expose her sex.

**130** When he sees her he will draw near to her,
**131** and his animals, who grew up in his wilderness, will become alien to him."

**132** The trapper went, bringing the harlot, Shamhat, with him.
**133** They set off on the journey, making direct way.
**134** On the third day they arrived at the appointed place,
**135** and the trapper and the harlot sat down at their posts.

**136** A first day and a second they sat opposite the watering hole.
**137** The animals arrived and drank at the watering hole,
**138** the wild beasts arrived and slaked their thirst with water.

**139** Then he, Enkidu, offspring of the mountains,
**140** who eats grasses with the gazelles,
**141** came to drink at the watering hole with the animals,
**142** with the wild beasts he slaked his thirst with water.

**143** Then Shamhat saw him—a primitive,
**144** a savage fellow from the depths of the wilderness!
**145** "That is he, Shamhat! Release your clenched arms,
**146** expose your sex so he can take in your voluptuousness.

**147** Do not be restrained—take his energy!
**148** When he sees you he will draw near to you.
**149** Spread out your robe so he can lie upon you,
**150** and perform for this primitive the task of womankind!"

**151** Shamhat unclutched her bosom, exposed her sex, and he took in her voluptuousness.
**152** She was not restrained, but took his energy.
**153** She spread out her robe and he lay upon her,
**154** she performed for the primitive the task of womankind.

**155** For six days and seven nights Enkidu stayed aroused,
**156** and had intercourse with the harlot
**157** until he was sated with her charms.

**158** But when he turned his attention to his animals,
**159** the gazelles saw Enkidu and darted off,
**160** the wild animals distanced themselves from his body.

**161** Enkidu tried to rise up, but his body pulled back.
**162** His knees that wanted to go off with his animals went rigid;
**163** Enkidu was diminished, his running was not as before.
**164** But then he drew himself up, for his understanding had broadened.

**165** Turning around, he sat down at the harlot's feet,
**166** gazing into her face, his ears attentive as the harlot spoke.
**167** The harlot said to Enkidu:
**168** "You are beautiful, Enkidu, you are become like a god.

**169** Why do you gallop around the wilderness with the wild beasts?
**170** Come, let me bring you into Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**171** to the holy temple, the residence of Anu and Ishtar,
**172** the place of Gilgamesh, who is wise to perfection,

**173** but who struts his power over the people like a wild bull."
**174** What she said found favour with him;
**175** becoming aware of himself, he sought a friend.

**176** Enkidu spoke to the harlot:
**177** "Come, Shamhat, take me away with you
**178** to the sacred holy temple, the residence of Anu and Ishtar,
**179** the place of Gilgamesh, who is wise to perfection,

**180** but who struts his power over the people like a wild bull.
**181** I will challenge him, let me shout out in Uruk:
**182** 'I am the mightiest! I have come to change the old order,
**183** I am he who was born in the wilderness, might is mine!'"

**184** Shamhat replied: "Come, let us go, so he may see your face.
**185** I will lead you to Gilgamesh—I know where he will be.
**186** Look about you, Enkidu, inside Uruk-the-Sheepfold,
**187** where the weights of the people's garments are dyed in colours,
**188** where every day is a day for some festival,
**189** where the lyre and drum play continually,
**190** where harlots stand about prettily,
**191** exuding voluptuousness, full of laughter,
**192** and on the couch of night the sheets are spread."

**193** She pulled off her clothing, and clothed him with one piece
**194** while she clothed herself with a second.
**195** She took him by the hand and led him like a child
**196** to the table of shepherds, the place of the sheepfold.

**197** The shepherds gathered all around about him,
**198** they marvelled to themselves:
**199** "How the youth resembles Gilgamesh—
**200** tall in stature, towering up to the battlements over the wall!

**201** Surely he was born in the mountains;
**202** his strength is as mighty as the meteorite of Anu!"
**203** They placed food in front of him,
**204** they placed beer in front of him;
**205** Enkidu knew nothing about eating bread for food,
**206** and of drinking beer he had not been taught.

**207** The harlot spoke to Enkidu, saying:
**208** "Eat the food, Enkidu, it is the way one lives.
**209** Drink the beer, as is the custom of the land."
**210** Enkidu ate the food until he was sated,
**211** he drank the beer—seven jugs!—and became expansive
**212** and sang with joy!

**213** He was elated and his face glowed.
**214** He splashed his shaggy body with water,
**215** and rubbed himself with oil, and turned into a human.
**216** He put on some clothing and became like a warrior.
**217** He took up his weapon and chased lions
**218** so that the shepherds could eat.

**219** He routed the wolves, and chased the lions.
**220** The herders could lie down in peace,
**221** for Enkidu was their watchman.

**222** A certain man was invited to a wedding;
**223** Enkidu heard of it, and said to the harlot:
**224** "Shamhat, I wish to go to Uruk."

**225** In Uruk at this very moment Gilgamesh
**226** was going about the city, saying to the people:
**227** "Assemble the counsellors, the craftsmen and the notables;
**228** I want to have the bride first."

**229** Then Enkidu stood in the street of Uruk-the-Sheepfold.
**230** He blocked the way of Gilgamesh.
**231** The land of Uruk stood around him,
**232** the whole land assembled about him,
**233** the populace was thronging around him,
**234** the men were clustered about him.

**235** "Look at him, just like Gilgamesh to a hair—
**236** though shorter in stature, and bigger of bone!
**237** This is the one who was reared on the milk of wild animals!
**238** His strength is the mightiest in the land!"

**239** They grappled with each other at the entry to the marital chamber,
**240** in the street they attacked each other, the public square of the land.
**241** The doorposts trembled and the wall shook.

**242** Gilgamesh bent his knees, with his other foot on the ground,
**243** his anger abated and he turned his chest away.
**244** After he turned his chest Enkidu said to Gilgamesh:
**245** "Your mother bore you ever unique,
**246** the Wild Cow of the Enclosure, Ninsun,
**247** your head is elevated over other men,
**248** Enlil has destined for you the kingship over the people."

**249** They kissed each other and became friends.
