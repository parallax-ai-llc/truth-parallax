**Tablet VIII**

**1** With the first light of dawn Gilgamesh
**2** lamented for his friend:
**3** "Enkidu, my friend, your mother a gazelle,
**4** your father a wild ass!

**5** You who were raised by creatures with tails,
**6** and by the wild beasts of the wilderness with all its pastures—
**7** The paths going to the Cedar Forest
**8** mourn you and do not fall silent day or night.

**9** The elders of the broad city, of Uruk-the-Sheepfold, mourn you.
**10** The crowd that blessed us on our departure mourns you.
**11** The heights of highland mourn you.
**12** The pastures of the lowland lament.

**13** The body that you touched, he mourns;
**14** the fields where we grew up mourn you.
**15** The forest where we cut down the cedar mourns you,
**16** the bear, hyena, panther, cheetah, stag, ibex,
**17** all the creatures of the plain mourn you.

**18** The holy river Ulaya along whose banks we once walked mourns you.
**19** The pure Euphrates, where we drew water for the waterskins, mourns you.
**20** The warriors of broad Uruk-the-Sheepfold mourn you.
**21** We who slew the Bull, who destroyed the demon Humbaba—

**22** Who is there in Uruk who can compare to you?

**23** The farmer who called your name in his sweet field mourns you.
**24** The shepherd who poured out beer for your drinking mourns you.
**25** The harlot who anointed you with fragrant oil mourns you.
**26** The women of the palace, who led brides to choose the moment for lovemaking, mourn you.

**27** Your young brother whom you taught to fight mourns you.
**28** Your friend weeps bitterly for you.

**29** Hear me, elders, hear me, young men!
**30** My beloved friend, Enkidu, whom I loved so dearly,
**31** who went with me through every danger—
**32** the fate of mankind has overtaken him.

**33** All day and all night I wept over him
**34** and would not have him buried—
**35** as if my friend might yet rise up at my lament—
**36** seven days and seven nights,
**37** until a maggot fell out of his nose.

**38** Since his passing I have not found life.
**39** I roamed like a hunter in the midst of the steppe.
**40** O Enkidu, my friend, swift mule, wild ass of the mountain,
**41** panther of the steppe—

**42** we who met and scaled the mountain,
**43** seized and slew the Bull of Heaven,
**44** overthrew Humbaba who dwelt in the Cedar Forest—
**45** what is this sleep that has seized you?
**46** You have turned dark and cannot hear me!"

**47** But he did not lift his head.
**48** He touched his heart, but it did not beat.
**49** Then he veiled his friend's face like a bride.
**50** He circled around him like an eagle.

**51** Like a lioness whose cubs are in the pit,
**52** he paced back and forth, this way and that.
**53** He tore out his hair in clumps and scattered it about,
**54** he ripped off his fine clothes and flung them down as if they were abominations.

**55** With the first light of dawn, Gilgamesh
**56** sent out a call throughout the land.
**57** The metalworker, the jeweller, the coppersmith, the goldsmith—
**58** "Fashion a statue of my friend!"

**59** A statue of my friend I will commission,
**60** like none ever made for any friend:
**61** "My friend, your limbs—they shall be of gold,
**62** your body of lapis lazuli.
**63** Your chest shall be of gold,
**64** your skin of—"

**65** [Gap in text]

**66** "I will lay you out on a great bed,
**67** I will lay you out on a bed of honour.
**68** I will place you on my left, on the seat of ease.
**69** The rulers of the earth will kiss your feet.

**70** I will have the people of Uruk mourn and lament for you,
**71** joyful people I will fill with woe for you.
**72** And when you have gone
**73** I will roam the steppe with matted hair,
**74** I will don the skin of a lion and wander the wilderness."

**75** With the first light of dawn
**76** Gilgamesh opened his treasury.
**77** He brought out a table of elammaku-wood,
**78** a great bowl of carnelian he filled with honey,
**79** a great bowl of lapis lazuli he filled with butter.
**80** He decorated them and set them out before Shamash.
