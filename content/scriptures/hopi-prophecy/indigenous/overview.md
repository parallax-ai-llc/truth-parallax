## Overview

Hopi prophetic tradition is maintained by specific clans and religious societies within the Hopi community, and its public sharing has always been a matter of careful deliberation. The decision of certain Hopi elders — particularly Thomas Banyacya and Dan Katchongva — to share aspects of the prophecies with the wider world in the mid-twentieth century was itself understood as fulfilling a prophetic mandate: that the time would come when the Hopi message of balance and warning would need to reach all people.

Indigenous engagement with Hopi Prophecy emphasizes its integration with ongoing Hopi ceremonial life, agricultural practice, and community governance. The prophecies are not abstract predictions but practical guides for living, closely connected to the ceremonial calendar, the agricultural cycle, and the maintenance of the Hopi villages that have been continuously inhabited for over a thousand years.
