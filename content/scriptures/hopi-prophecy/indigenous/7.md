This section describes the Great Purification (Day of Purification), when the world will be cleansed of corruption and imbalance. Central to this prophecy is the return of Pahana, the True White Brother, who departed at the time of emergence carrying a matching stone tablet. The Purification may take the form of natural disasters, social upheaval, or spiritual transformation, depending on the choices humanity makes. The Hopi role is to maintain their ceremonies and way of life as an anchor of balance during this time.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Great Purification carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
