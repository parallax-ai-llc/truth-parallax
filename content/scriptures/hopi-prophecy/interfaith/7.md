This section describes the Great Purification (Day of Purification), when the world will be cleansed of corruption and imbalance. Central to this prophecy is the return of Pahana, the True White Brother, who departed at the time of emergence carrying a matching stone tablet. The Purification may take the form of natural disasters, social upheaval, or spiritual transformation, depending on the choices humanity makes. The Hopi role is to maintain their ceremonies and way of life as an anchor of balance during this time.

Interfaith engagement with The Great Purification offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
