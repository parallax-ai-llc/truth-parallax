## Overview

Interfaith engagement with Hopi Prophecy has been significant since the mid-twentieth century, when Hopi elders began sharing aspects of their prophetic tradition with broader audiences. The Hopi message of ecological balance, spiritual integrity, and humanity's responsibility for the future resonates powerfully with contemporary interfaith concerns about environmental crisis, social justice, and the need for spiritual renewal.

The Hopi prophetic tradition offers a distinctive Indigenous voice in interfaith conversations about eschatology (the study of last things), environmental ethics, and the role of traditional wisdom in addressing modern crises. The concept of the two paths — materialism leading to destruction versus spiritual harmony leading to continuation — provides a framework for interfaith dialogue about the spiritual dimensions of contemporary global challenges.
