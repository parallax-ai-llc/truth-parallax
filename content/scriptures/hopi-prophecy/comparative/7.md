This section describes the Great Purification (Day of Purification), when the world will be cleansed of corruption and imbalance. Central to this prophecy is the return of Pahana, the True White Brother, who departed at the time of emergence carrying a matching stone tablet. The Purification may take the form of natural disasters, social upheaval, or spiritual transformation, depending on the choices humanity makes. The Hopi role is to maintain their ceremonies and way of life as an anchor of balance during this time.

Comparative analysis of The Great Purification reveals both parallels with and distinctive departures from similar themes in other world traditions. The motifs and structures found in this section connect to widespread patterns in human religious and mythological expression while retaining features that are specific to this particular cultural and spiritual context.

Cross-cultural comparison illuminates the universal dimensions of the human religious imagination while also highlighting the unique contributions of this tradition to the global tapestry of spiritual thought. However, comparative analysis must be conducted with care to avoid reducing distinctive traditions to mere instances of universal patterns, thereby obscuring what is most valuable and specific in each tradition.
