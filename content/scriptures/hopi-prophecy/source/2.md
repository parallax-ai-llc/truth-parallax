---
title: "Hopi Prophecy - Chapter 2"
scripture: "hopi-prophecy"
chapter: 2
---

**1** When the Hopi emerged into this fourth world, they met Massau'u, the Guardian of the Earth. He was humble and wore simple clothing, and he grew his food from the earth.

**2** Massau'u said to the people: "I am the first and I will be the last. I have been waiting for you. This land is my land, but I will share it with you, if you follow the way of life I show you."

**3** "You must walk softly upon the earth and take only what you need. You must respect all living things, for they are your brothers and sisters."

**4** "You must never take more than you need, for the earth provides enough for all. If you are greedy, the earth will withhold its gifts."

**5** He gave the Hopi a set of sacred stone tablets. On these tablets were written the instructions for living in this world and prophecies of what was to come.

**6** Massau'u said: "Keep these tablets safe. They contain the plan of life for this world. When the time comes, you will show them to the world, and then all will know the truth."

**7** The people were told to make four great migrations, to the north, south, east, and west, before settling in their final homeland. These migrations would teach them about the earth.

**8** During their migrations, the Hopi were to leave their marks upon the land — petroglyphs and ruins — so that future generations would know they had passed that way.

**9** The Creator told them: "Your homeland will be a place that appears barren and desolate to others, but to you it will be sacred. It is the spiritual center of the earth."

**10** And so the Hopi settled in the arid lands of the Southwest, where they planted their corn and lived according to the teachings of Massau'u.
