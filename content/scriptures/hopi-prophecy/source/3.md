---
title: "Hopi Prophecy - Chapter 3"
scripture: "hopi-prophecy"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The elders taught that the corn is sacred. It is the staff of life, given by the Creator. To plant corn is to pray. To tend corn is to worship. To harvest corn is to give thanks.

**2** The Hopi were told: "Your corn will be your strength. As long as you plant corn and live by the Creator's plan, you will survive. When you abandon corn, you will lose your way."

**3** The cycle of the seasons teaches the people about the cycle of life. Spring is birth, summer is growth, autumn is harvest, and winter is rest. All things follow this pattern.

**4** The kachinas are the spirit beings who bring the rain and help the crops to grow. They come from the spirit world to dwell among the people during the ceremonial season.

**5** The ceremonies must be performed at the proper times and in the proper way. If the ceremonies are neglected, the rain will not come, the crops will fail, and the people will suffer.

**6** The elders said: "Every ceremony is a prayer. Every dance is a prayer. Every song is a prayer. When we pray together, our prayers have great power."

**7** The land itself is sacred. Every mesa, every spring, every shrine has a purpose in the Creator's plan. To disturb the land is to disturb the balance of life.

**8** The Hopi were told: "You must be the caretakers of this land. If you take care of the land, the land will take care of you. This is the covenant between you and the Creator."

**9** Water is life. The springs are sacred, for they bring water from the heart of the earth. Without water, there is no life. The people must protect the water.

**10** The teachings of the elders are to be passed down from generation to generation, from mouth to ear, so that the knowledge will never be lost. This is the sacred responsibility of the Hopi.
