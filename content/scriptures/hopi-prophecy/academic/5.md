This section catalogs the prophetic signs that indicate the approach of the Great Purification (Day of Purification). These signs include the coming of white-skinned people, the appearance of 'cobwebs in the sky' (power lines), roads like rivers of stone (highways), the 'gourd of ashes' (nuclear weapons), and other specific predictions that Hopi elders have identified as having been fulfilled. The accumulation of these signs indicates humanity's proximity to a critical turning point.

Academic analysis of The Signs of Purification draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
