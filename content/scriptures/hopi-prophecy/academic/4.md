This section addresses the sacred stone tablets and petroglyphs that encode Hopi prophetic knowledge. The most well-known is Prophecy Rock near Oraibi, which depicts two paths for humanity — the path of materialism leading to destruction, and the path of spiritual balance leading to survival. The stone tablets given to the Hopi at the time of emergence serve as title deeds to the land and as prophetic roadmaps, with specific symbols representing future events and choices facing humanity.

Academic analysis of The Prophecy Stones and Tablets draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
