## Overview

The Hopi Prophecy encompasses a body of prophetic teachings and sacred narratives maintained by the Hopi people of northeastern Arizona. Rooted in the Hopi emergence narrative — the journey of the people through three previous worlds into the present Fourth World — these prophecies describe the signs, choices, and potential outcomes that will determine humanity's future. The prophecies are encoded in sacred stone tablets, petroglyphs (most notably Prophecy Rock near the ancient village of Oraibi), and oral traditions passed down through specific clans and religious societies.

Central to Hopi prophetic thought is the concept of balance (often expressed through the Hopi concept of koyaanisqatsi — "life out of balance") and the understanding that humanity faces a fundamental choice between two paths: one of materialism, environmental destruction, and spiritual disconnection leading to purification through catastrophe, and another of humility, ecological harmony, and spiritual integrity leading to peaceful continuation. The prophecies describe specific signs marking the approach of the Great Purification, many of which Hopi elders have identified as having been fulfilled in the modern era.

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Indigenous**: A living prophetic tradition maintained by Hopi clans and religious societies
- **Academic**: A significant body of study in Indigenous prophecy, millenarianism, and oral tradition
- **Comparative**: Parallels with apocalyptic and millenarian traditions across world religions
- **Theological**: Raises questions about prophecy, human agency, ecological ethics, and spiritual purification
- **Anthropological**: Central to understanding Hopi worldview, social organization, and ceremonial life
- **Interfaith**: An Indigenous prophetic voice on global environmental crisis and spiritual renewal

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|

| Balance (Koyaanisqatsi) | The concept of life in and out of balance as a central moral principle |
| Prophecy and Agency | Prophecies as warnings and calls to action rather than fixed predictions |
| Ecological Ethics | The relationship between spiritual integrity and environmental stewardship |
| Purification | Catastrophic renewal as a consequence of collective moral failure |
| Sacred Covenant | The enduring agreement between the Hopi and Maasaw to live as humble stewards |
