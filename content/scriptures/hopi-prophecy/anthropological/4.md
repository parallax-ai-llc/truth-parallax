This section addresses the sacred stone tablets and petroglyphs that encode Hopi prophetic knowledge. The most well-known is Prophecy Rock near Oraibi, which depicts two paths for humanity — the path of materialism leading to destruction, and the path of spiritual balance leading to survival. The stone tablets given to the Hopi at the time of emergence serve as title deeds to the land and as prophetic roadmaps, with specific symbols representing future events and choices facing humanity.

Anthropological study of The Prophecy Stones and Tablets examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
