This section describes the Great Purification (Day of Purification), when the world will be cleansed of corruption and imbalance. Central to this prophecy is the return of Pahana, the True White Brother, who departed at the time of emergence carrying a matching stone tablet. The Purification may take the form of natural disasters, social upheaval, or spiritual transformation, depending on the choices humanity makes. The Hopi role is to maintain their ceremonies and way of life as an anchor of balance during this time.

Anthropological study of The Great Purification examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
