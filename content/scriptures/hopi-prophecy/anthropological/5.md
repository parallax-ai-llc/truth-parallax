This section catalogs the prophetic signs that indicate the approach of the Great Purification (Day of Purification). These signs include the coming of white-skinned people, the appearance of 'cobwebs in the sky' (power lines), roads like rivers of stone (highways), the 'gourd of ashes' (nuclear weapons), and other specific predictions that Hopi elders have identified as having been fulfilled. The accumulation of these signs indicates humanity's proximity to a critical turning point.

Anthropological study of The Signs of Purification examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
