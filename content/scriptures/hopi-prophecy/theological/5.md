This section catalogs the prophetic signs that indicate the approach of the Great Purification (Day of Purification). These signs include the coming of white-skinned people, the appearance of 'cobwebs in the sky' (power lines), roads like rivers of stone (highways), the 'gourd of ashes' (nuclear weapons), and other specific predictions that Hopi elders have identified as having been fulfilled. The accumulation of these signs indicates humanity's proximity to a critical turning point.

Theological engagement with The Signs of Purification raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order. This section's themes invite reflection on fundamental theological questions that transcend any single tradition while being rooted in the specific spiritual vision of this particular culture.

The theological concepts embedded in this section — whether explicit or implicit — offer distinctive perspectives on questions that occupy theologians across traditions: the nature of creation, the relationship between the human and the divine, the meaning of suffering and transformation, and the foundations of moral order.
