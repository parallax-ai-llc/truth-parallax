## Overview

Theological engagement with Hopi Prophecy raises important questions about the nature of prophecy itself, the relationship between human agency and divine will, the theological dimensions of ecological crisis, and the meaning of purification and renewal. The Hopi prophetic tradition presents a distinctive theology in which prophecy functions not as predetermined fate but as a warning and a call to choice — humanity can determine which path to follow.

The concept of koyaanisqatsi (life out of balance) functions as a theological diagnostic, identifying the root cause of individual and collective suffering in the departure from the original instructions given by the Creator. The covenant with Maasaw establishes a theology of stewardship in which human beings are entrusted with the care of the earth and held accountable for their relationship with the natural world.
