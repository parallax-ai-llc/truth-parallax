This section addresses the sacred stone tablets and petroglyphs that encode Hopi prophetic knowledge. The most well-known is Prophecy Rock near Oraibi, which depicts two paths for humanity — the path of materialism leading to destruction, and the path of spiritual balance leading to survival. The stone tablets given to the Hopi at the time of emergence serve as title deeds to the land and as prophetic roadmaps, with specific symbols representing future events and choices facing humanity.

Theological engagement with The Prophecy Stones and Tablets raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order. This section's themes invite reflection on fundamental theological questions that transcend any single tradition while being rooted in the specific spiritual vision of this particular culture.

The theological concepts embedded in this section — whether explicit or implicit — offer distinctive perspectives on questions that occupy theologians across traditions: the nature of creation, the relationship between the human and the divine, the meaning of suffering and transformation, and the foundations of moral order. Engaging with these concepts enriches theological reflection by expanding the range of perspectives brought to bear on enduring questions.
