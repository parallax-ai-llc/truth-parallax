This section describes the Great Purification (Day of Purification), when the world will be cleansed of corruption and imbalance. Central to this prophecy is the return of Pahana, the True White Brother, who departed at the time of emergence carrying a matching stone tablet. The Purification may take the form of natural disasters, social upheaval, or spiritual transformation, depending on the choices humanity makes. The Hopi role is to maintain their ceremonies and way of life as an anchor of balance during this time.

Theological engagement with The Great Purification raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order. This section's themes invite reflection on fundamental theological questions that transcend any single tradition while being rooted in the specific spiritual vision of this particular culture.

The theological concepts embedded in this section — whether explicit or implicit — offer distinctive perspectives on questions that occupy theologians across traditions: the nature of creation, the relationship between the human and the divine, the meaning of suffering and transformation, and the foundations of moral order. Engaging with these concepts enriches theological reflection by expanding the range of perspectives brought to bear on enduring questions.
