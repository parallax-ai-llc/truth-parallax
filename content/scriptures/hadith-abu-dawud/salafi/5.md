Salafi scholars treat Abu Dawud's concluding sections on the Sunnah and manners as a comprehensive guide to prophetic practice in creed and social conduct. The creedal traditions — hadiths on God's attributes, predestination, and the authority of the Sunnah — are foundational to the Salafi theological project of affirming the beliefs of the earliest Muslim community without the modifications introduced by later theological schools.

The manners traditions provide practical guidance for implementing the Sunnah in daily social interactions. Salafi scholars emphasize that following the Prophet's example in greetings, dress, social visits, and interpersonal conduct is not merely a matter of personal preference but a religious obligation for those who seek to live according to authentic prophetic guidance. Where contemporary social customs conflict with established prophetic manners, Salafi teaching prioritizes the authenticated Sunnah over cultural convention, using Abu Dawud's traditions as the evidentiary basis for distinguishing between the two.
