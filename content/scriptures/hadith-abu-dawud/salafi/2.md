Salafi engagement with Abu Dawud's zakat and fasting chapters focuses on extracting clear, authenticated rulings that can be applied directly from prophetic precedent. The zakat traditions are used to establish precise thresholds, rates, and categories of wealth subject to obligatory charity. Salafi scholars insist on applying these rulings as comprehensively as possible, resisting attempts to modernize or reinterpret the zakat categories in ways that depart from the apparent meaning of the prophetic texts.

The fasting traditions are approached with similar rigor. Salafi scholars use Abu Dawud's narrations to determine which fasting practices are authentically established as Sunnah and which are innovations lacking prophetic support. The criteria for beginning and ending Ramadan (local moon sighting versus calculation), the rulings on what invalidates the fast, and the conditions for traveler's exemption are all derived directly from the hadith evidence. Abu Dawud's inclusion of some weaker traditions is addressed through al-Albani's re-evaluation, ensuring that Salafi practice rests exclusively on authenticated prophetic guidance.
