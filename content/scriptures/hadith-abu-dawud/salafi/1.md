Salafi scholars engage with Abu Dawud's purification and prayer traditions as the primary evidential basis for establishing the correct prophetic method of worship. Each hadith is assessed for its authenticity using the evaluations of scholars such as al-Albani, and only those meeting the standards of *sahih* or *hasan* are treated as binding for practice. This meticulous approach reflects the Salafi commitment to performing every act of worship exactly as the Prophet performed it, without additions or modifications.

The prayer traditions in Abu Dawud are particularly important for the Salafi emphasis on praying "as you have seen me pray." Salafi scholars use these hadiths to determine the precise form of prayer — from the opening supplication to the final greeting — cross-referencing Abu Dawud's narrations with those in al-Bukhari, Muslim, and other collections. Where Abu Dawud records a practice not supported by other authentic sources, Salafi scholars exercise caution, and where he preserves a practice that others omit, they evaluate it for possible addition to the recommended acts of worship.
