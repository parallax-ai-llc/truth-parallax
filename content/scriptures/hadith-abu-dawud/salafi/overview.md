## Overview

The Salafi movement values Sunan Abu Dawud as a primary source for recovering the authentic legal practice of the Prophet and his companions. Abu Dawud's focus on *ahkam* hadiths aligns perfectly with the Salafi project of grounding every religious practice in authenticated prophetic evidence. His collection provides the textual basis for distinguishing genuine Sunnah from later cultural accretions and innovations.

## Interpretive Approach

Salafi scholars engage with Abu Dawud through the lens of rigorous re-authentication, particularly relying on the evaluations of Shaykh al-Albani, who produced a comprehensive grading of the entire collection. Hadiths confirmed as authentic are treated as binding evidence for practice, while those identified as weak are set aside for legal purposes. This approach reflects the Salafi commitment to ensuring that every act of worship rests on firm evidential foundations.

## Methodological Emphasis

For Salafi practitioners, Abu Dawud's collection is a practical manual for living according to the Sunnah. Its detailed coverage of daily rituals, commercial dealings, and social interactions provides specific prophetic guidance that Salafi Muslims seek to implement directly, bypassing the intermediary of later legal school opinions where possible. Abu Dawud's own statement that he included the most important legal hadiths resonates with the Salafi emphasis on essential prophetic practice as the core of Muslim religious life.
