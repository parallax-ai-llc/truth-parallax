Salafi engagement with Abu Dawud's marriage traditions emphasizes deriving family law directly from authenticated prophetic evidence. The conditions of the marriage contract, the rights and obligations of spouses, and the procedures for divorce are all grounded in specific hadiths rather than in the elaborations of later legal commentators. Salafi scholars use Abu Dawud's narrations to address contemporary family law issues, insisting that prophetic precedent provides sufficient and binding guidance.

The food traditions are applied to distinguish between genuinely Islamic dietary practices and cultural customs that may have been mistakenly attributed to the Sunnah. Salafi scholars use Abu Dawud's authenticated hadiths to establish which foods are definitively prohibited, which are permissible, and which are merely matters of preference. The prophetic etiquette of eating — invoking God's name, using the right hand, expressing gratitude — is practiced as part of the comprehensive Sunnah that governs every aspect of the believer's life, not merely as cultural tradition but as authenticated prophetic guidance.
