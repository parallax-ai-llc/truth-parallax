## Overview

Sunan Abu Dawud is one of the six canonical hadith collections in Sunni Islam, compiled by Imam Abu Dawud Sulayman ibn al-Ash'ath al-Sijistani (817–889 CE). The collection is particularly renowned for its focus on legal (*fiqhi*) traditions, gathering hadiths that bear directly on matters of jurisprudence and daily religious practice. Abu Dawud reportedly selected approximately 4,800 hadiths from a pool of 500,000, prioritizing those relevant to legal rulings.

## Structure

The collection is organized into thematic books reflecting the practical concerns of Islamic law:

1. **Purification and Prayer** — Ritual cleanliness, ablution, and the regulations of daily and congregational prayer
2. **Zakat and Fasting** — Charitable obligations, alms distribution, and the rules of Ramadan
3. **Jihad and Rites** — Military ethics, pilgrimage, sacrifice, and oaths
4. **Marriage and Food** — Marital law, dietary regulations, dress, and personal grooming
5. **Sunnah and Manners** — Prophetic precedent in creed, social conduct, and literary style

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Sunan Abu Dawud holds a recognized place across Islamic interpretive traditions:

- **Sunni Islam**: A primary source for deriving legal rulings across all four schools of jurisprudence
- **Shia Islam**: Consulted critically, with particular attention to narrators and legal content
- **Sufi traditions**: Referenced for hadiths on inward sincerity behind outward legal observance
- **Salafi movement**: Valued as a direct link to prophetic legal guidance
- **Academic scholarship**: Examined for its contribution to the development of Islamic legal literature
