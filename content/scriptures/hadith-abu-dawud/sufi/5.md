Sufi interpreters treasure Abu Dawud's final sections on the Sunnah and manners as guidance for embodying the prophetic character in every aspect of life. The Sunnah is understood not merely as a body of legal precedents but as the living example of the Perfect Human (*al-Insan al-Kamil*), whose every action, word, and silence reflected divine wisdom. To follow the Sunnah, in the Sufi understanding, is to gradually become transformed by the prophetic light.

The manners traditions provide concrete guidance for the spiritual aspirant's social life. Every interaction — greeting a neighbor, visiting the sick, comforting the bereaved, resolving a dispute — becomes an opportunity to practice the prophetic virtues of compassion, patience, and gentleness. Sufi masters teach that true spiritual progress is measured not by extraordinary mystical experiences but by the quality of one's ordinary conduct with others. Abu Dawud's detailed recording of the Prophet's social habits thus becomes, for Sufi readers, a practical manual of spiritual transformation through everyday life.
