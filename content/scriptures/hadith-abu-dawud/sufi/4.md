Sufi engagement with Abu Dawud's marriage and food traditions reveals the spiritual significance embedded in domestic and bodily life. Marriage is understood in Sufi thought not merely as a legal contract but as a mirror of the soul's relationship with God — the intimacy, trust, vulnerability, and mutual devotion that characterize the marital bond reflect the mystical relationship between the lover and the Beloved. The prophetic traditions on spousal rights and kindness are read as lessons in divine love expressed through human relationships.

The food traditions are embraced as guidance for mindful consumption — eating and drinking with awareness of God's provision and gratitude for His sustenance. Sufi masters teach that the quality and quantity of food directly affect the soul's capacity for spiritual experience. Abu Dawud's hadiths on prophetic eating habits — moderation, the invocation of God's name, and the expression of thanks — are adopted as practices of spiritual discipline that transform the mundane act of eating into an occasion of remembrance and presence.
