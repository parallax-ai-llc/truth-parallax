Sufi readers approach Abu Dawud's purification and prayer traditions as a guide to the outer forms whose inner meanings the spiritual aspirant must gradually realize. Purification is understood on multiple levels: the physical washing of ablution, the ethical purification of conduct, and the spiritual cleansing of the heart from everything that veils it from God. Abu Dawud's detailed prescriptions for ritual cleanliness are embraced as the necessary foundation upon which the edifice of inner purification is built.

The prayer traditions are read with attention to the moments of greatest spiritual proximity. The Prophet's statement that "the closest a servant comes to his Lord is during prostration" transforms the legal prescriptions for prayer into a roadmap of spiritual intimacy. Sufi practitioners follow Abu Dawud's detailed guidance on the form of prayer while cultivating the inner presence (*hudur al-qalb*) that transforms obligatory worship into mystical communion. Each prescribed movement becomes a gesture of the soul's surrender, and each recitation becomes a conversation with the Divine.
