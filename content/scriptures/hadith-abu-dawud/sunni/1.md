Abu Dawud's opening section on purification and prayer provides Sunni jurists with an exhaustive collection of prophetic traditions governing the prerequisites and performance of worship. The purification chapters detail every aspect of ritual cleanliness — from the types of water permissible for ablution to the specific procedures for *ghusl* (full ritual bathing) after various states of impurity. Sunni scholars value Abu Dawud's thoroughness in recording variant practices, allowing the four legal schools to locate hadith evidence for their respective positions.

The prayer traditions cover the obligatory and voluntary prayers with meticulous attention to detail: the opening supplication, the recitations, the physical postures, the number of units, and the supplications between prostrations. Abu Dawud records hadiths that address disputed points among Sunni jurists — such as raising the hands during prayer transitions and the placement of the feet during sitting — providing the raw evidence that each school uses to construct its rulings. This section remains the primary hadith reference for Sunni manuals of prayer.
