---
title: "Sunan Abu Dawud - Chapter 2"
scripture: "hadith-abu-dawud"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The best day on which the sun has risen is Friday. On it Adam was created, on it he was admitted to Paradise, and on it he was expelled from it."

**2** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The five daily prayers, and Friday prayer to Friday prayer, are an expiation for whatever sins come between them, as long as major sins are avoided."

**3** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever performs ablution and does it well, then comes to the Friday prayer and listens attentively and remains silent, he will be forgiven what is between that Friday and the previous one, and three days more."

**4** Narrated Aws ibn Aws: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Among the most excellent of your days is Friday. On that day, invoke blessings on me abundantly, for your invocations are presented to me."

**5** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "There is an hour on Friday during which no Muslim servant stands in prayer and asks Allah for something, except that Allah will grant it to him." And he indicated with his hand that it is a very short time.

**6** Narrated Abu Dharr: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever takes a bath on Friday and washes himself thoroughly, then goes early to the mosque and sits close to the imam and listens attentively, for every step he takes he will have the reward of a year's fasting and prayer."

**7** Narrated Samurah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Attend the Friday prayer and sit close to the imam, for a man keeps drawing away until he is left behind, even though he enters Paradise."

**8** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "If anyone performs ablution well, then comes to the Friday prayer, and listens and keeps silent, his sins between that time and the next Friday will be forgiven for him, and three days more."
