## Overview

The Catholic reading of the Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs engages with the text's sophisticated ethical framework in light of the Catholic tradition of moral theology. The patriarchs' autobiographical confessions of sin and their exhortations to virtue anticipate the Catholic practices of examination of conscience and confession. The virtue-and-vice structure resonates with the Catholic tradition of the seven deadly sins and their opposing virtues.
