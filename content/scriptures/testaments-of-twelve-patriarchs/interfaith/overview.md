## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs explores the work's ethical teachings as a meeting point for diverse moral traditions. The universal virtues extolled in the Testaments — compassion, chastity, simplicity, forgiveness, integrity — are valued across all major religious traditions. The Two Spirits doctrine reflects a cross-cultural recognition of the moral struggle within the human soul, expressed in Zoroastrian, Jewish, Christian, Buddhist, and philosophical traditions.
