**1** The copy of the Testament of Asher, the tenth son of Jacob and Zilpah, concerning the two faces of vice and virtue.

**2** In the hundred and twenty-fifth year of his life he said to his children:

**3** Hearken, ye children of Asher, to your father, and I will declare to you all that is upright in the sight of God.

**4** Two ways hath God given to the sons of men, and two inclinations, and two kinds of action, and two modes of action, and two issues.

**5** Therefore all things are by twos, one over against the other.

**6** For there are two ways of good and evil, and with these are the two inclinations in our breasts discriminating them.

**7** Therefore if the soul take pleasure in the good inclination, all its actions are in righteousness; and if it sin it straightway repenteth.

**8** For, having its thoughts set upon righteousness, and casting away wickedness, it straightway overthroweth the evil, and uprooteth the sin.

**9** But if it incline to the evil inclination, all its actions are in wickedness, and it driveth away the good, and cleaveth to the evil, and is ruled by Beliar; even though it work what is good, he perverteth it to evil.

**10** For whenever it beginneth to do good, he forceth the issue of the action into evil for him, seeing that the treasure of the inclination is filled with an evil spirit.

**11** A person then may with words help the good for the sake of the evil, yet the issue of the action leadeth to mischief.

**12** There is a man who showeth no compassion upon him who serveth his turn in evil; and this thing hath two aspects, but the whole is evil.

**13** And there is a man that loveth him that worketh evil, because he would prefer even to die in evil for his sake; and concerning this it is clear that it hath two aspects, but the whole is an evil work.

**14** Though indeed he have love, yet is he wicked who concealeth what is evil for the sake of the good name, but the end of the action tendeth unto evil.

**15** Another stealeth, doeth unjustly, plundereth, defraudeth, and withal pitieth the poor: this too hath a twofold aspect, but the whole is evil.

**16** He who defraudeth his neighbour provoketh God, and sweareth falsely against the Most High, and yet pitieth the poor; the Lord who commandeth the law he setteth at nought and provoketh, and yet he refresheth the poor.

**17** He defileth the soul, and maketh gay the body; he killeth many, and pitieth a few: this, too, hath a twofold aspect, but the whole is evil.

**18** Another committeth adultery and fornication, and abstaineth from meats, and when he fasteth he doeth evil, and by the power of his wealth overwhelmeth many; and notwithstanding his excessive wickedness he doeth the commandments: this, too, hath a twofold aspect, but the whole is evil.

**19** Such men are hares; clean in respect of the divided hoof, but in very deed unclean.

**20** For God in the tables of the commandments hath thus declared.
