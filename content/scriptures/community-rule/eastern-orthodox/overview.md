## Overview

Eastern Orthodox scholars approach the Community Rule through the lens of the Church's ascetic and liturgical tradition. The Qumran community's rigorous spiritual discipline, its vision of human participation in the angelic liturgy, and its understanding of the community as a living temple resonate deeply with Orthodox theology and monastic practice.

## Interpretive Approach

Orthodox interpretation reads the Community Rule as a witness to the deep Jewish roots of Christian asceticism. The scroll's vision of purification, progressive sanctification, and communion with the angelic realm anticipates the Orthodox doctrine of theosis — the gradual transformation of the human person into the likeness of God. The text is read typologically, as a shadow of the fuller spiritual reality revealed in Christ and the Church.

## Key Emphases

Orthodox scholars highlight the Community Rule's description of the community at prayer as participating in the worship of heaven, a concept central to the Orthodox understanding of the Divine Liturgy. The scroll's emphasis on inner purification alongside external ritual resonates with the hesychast tradition of interior prayer. The hierarchical structure of the community, centered on priestly authority, finds parallels in Orthodox ecclesiology. The Two Spirits doctrine, while not adopted in its stark dualism, is seen as reflecting a genuine spiritual insight about the cosmic struggle between good and evil.

## Significance

For Eastern Orthodoxy, the Community Rule testifies to an ancient Jewish understanding of holiness as communal, liturgical, and transformative — themes that the Church Fathers would develop into the rich tradition of Orthodox spirituality. The scroll helps demonstrate the deep continuity between Second Temple Jewish piety and the ascetic-liturgical tradition of the Orthodox Church.
