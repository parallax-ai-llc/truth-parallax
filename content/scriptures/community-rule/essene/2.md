## The Doctrine of the Two Spirits

This chapter contains the celebrated "Treatise on the Two Spirits," which the Essene community regarded as a revealed explanation of the human condition. God created two spirits — the Spirit of Truth (also called the Angel of Light or the Prince of Light) and the Spirit of Deceit (the Angel of Darkness or Belial) — and assigned every human being a portion in each. The cosmic struggle between these spirits explains why even the righteous sometimes falter and why the wicked occasionally do good.

## The Ways of Light and Darkness

The treatise catalogs the fruits of each spirit in meticulous detail. The Spirit of Truth produces humility, patience, compassion, eternal goodness, understanding, wisdom, and zeal for righteous laws. The Spirit of Deceit produces greed, sloth, blasphemy, cruelty, falsehood, pride, and deception. The Essene community understood itself as aligned with the Spirit of Truth, and its discipline was designed to strengthen this alignment while rooting out the influence of the Spirit of Deceit.

## The Appointed End

The chapter concludes with the assurance that God has set an appointed time for the destruction of the Spirit of Deceit. At the final visitation, God will purify the righteous with the Spirit of Truth, cleansing them of all wickedness, and the dominion of darkness will be ended forever. This eschatological hope sustained the community through the rigors of its ascetic life.
