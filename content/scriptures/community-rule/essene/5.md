## The Council of the Community

Chapter 5 describes the governing structure of the Yahad, centered on the Council of the Community. This body consisted of twelve laymen and three priests, a number that symbolically represented the twelve tribes of Israel and the three clans of Levi. For the Essenes, this council was not merely an administrative body but the spiritual foundation of the community — and by extension, of the entire world.

## The Role of the Maskil

The chapter introduces the Maskil (the "Instructor" or "Wise Leader"), a figure of central importance in the community's governance. The Maskil was responsible for examining candidates for admission, teaching the community's doctrines, and guiding members in the ways of the Spirit of Truth. This figure combined the roles of teacher, spiritual director, and judge, embodying the community's ideal of wisdom-guided leadership.

## Membership Procedures

Detailed procedures for admission, probation, and advancement through the community's ranks are laid out. A candidate underwent a two-year probationary period during which their property was held in escrow, their conduct was scrutinized, and their understanding of the community's teaching was tested. Only after full admission was a member's property merged with the community's holdings and their place in the hierarchy established. These rigorous procedures reflect the Essene conviction that the purity of the community depended on the worthiness of every individual member.
