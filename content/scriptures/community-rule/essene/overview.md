## Overview

For the Essene community at Qumran, the Community Rule was not merely a text to be studied but the living constitution that governed every aspect of daily existence. It defined who they were as the "Sons of Light," set apart from the corruption of the Jerusalem Temple establishment and the broader society they regarded as walking in darkness.

## Interpretive Approach

The Essenes read the Community Rule as divinely revealed instruction mediated through the Teacher of Righteousness. The document's dualistic theology of two spirits — one of truth and one of deceit — was understood not as abstract philosophy but as a lived reality. Every member had to choose which spirit to follow, and the community's strict discipline was designed to cultivate the spirit of light within each individual.

## Key Emphases

The Essene reading emphasizes the radical nature of covenant entry, which required a complete break from the outside world, surrender of private property, and submission to communal authority. The annual covenant renewal ceremony described in the scroll was the central liturgical event, reaffirming each member's commitment. Purity was not merely ritual but ontological — the community believed that through their faithfulness, they participated in the angelic worship of heaven.

## Significance

The Community Rule represents the Essene community's most complete self-portrait, revealing a group convinced that they alone preserved the true covenant of Israel and that the end of days was imminent. Their intense communal life, rigorous purity practices, and dualistic worldview all find their charter in this foundational document.
