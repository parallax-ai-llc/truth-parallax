## The Hymn of the Maskil

The final chapter of the Community Rule contains a magnificent hymn attributed to the Maskil, the community's spiritual leader. This hymn functions as both a theological summary and a devotional climax to the entire document. In it, the Maskil declares his total dependence on God's grace, confesses the weakness of human flesh, and praises God's righteousness and mercy in choosing the community as vessels of divine truth.

## Themes of Grace and Humility

The hymn is remarkable for its emphasis on divine grace. Despite the Community Rule's demanding legal framework, the closing hymn insists that human righteousness is inadequate and that salvation depends entirely on God's mercy: "As for me, my justification belongs to God, and the perfection of my way belongs to the uprightness of His heart." This tension between rigorous obedience and radical dependence on grace is one of the most theologically rich features of the entire Community Rule.

## Cosmic Worship

The hymn concludes with the Maskil situating the community's worship within the cosmic order, praising God at the appointed times — the beginning of day and night, the seasons, the festivals — and aligning human prayer with the celestial rhythms established at creation. The community's liturgical life thus becomes a participation in the eternal worship of the angels, bridging heaven and earth through faithful observance of the divinely ordained calendar.
