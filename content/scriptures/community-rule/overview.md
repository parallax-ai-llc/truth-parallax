## Overview

The Community Rule (1QS), also known as *Serekh ha-Yahad*, is one of the most important sectarian documents discovered among the Dead Sea Scrolls at Qumran. Dating to approximately the second century BCE, it served as the constitutional charter of the Qumran community, laying out the requirements for membership, communal conduct, and theological beliefs that governed daily life in this ascetic Jewish sect.

## Structure

The Community Rule is organized into six major sections:

1. **Entrance into the Covenant** (Chapter 1): The oath and commitment required of new members
2. **The Two Spirits** (Chapter 2): The theological framework of cosmic dualism between light and darkness
3. **Rules of Conduct** (Chapter 3): Practical guidelines for community living
4. **Purity Laws** (Chapter 4): Ritual requirements for maintaining holiness
5. **The Council** (Chapter 5): Organizational structure and leadership roles
6. **The Hymn of the Maskil** (Chapter 6): A closing liturgical composition

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Community Rule holds distinct importance across multiple religious and scholarly traditions:

- **Essene**: The foundational document for understanding Essene identity, practice, and self-understanding
- **Judaism**: A witness to the diversity of Second Temple Judaism and the roots of rabbinic communal organization
- **Christianity**: The dualism of light and darkness and communal practices illuminate the background of the New Testament
- **Academic**: An indispensable primary source for reconstructing Second Temple Jewish sectarianism

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Covenant | Entry into a renewed covenant through oath and commitment |
| Dualism | The cosmic struggle between the Spirit of Light and the Spirit of Darkness |
| Purity | Rigorous standards of ritual and moral purity |
| Community | Shared property, communal meals, and collective discipline |
| Eschatology | Anticipation of a final divine judgment and the triumph of light |
