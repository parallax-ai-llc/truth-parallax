## Monastic Rule and Community Life

Catholic scholars read Chapter 3's rules of conduct with particular attention to their parallels with later Christian monastic rules, especially the Rule of St. Benedict. The chapter's prescriptions for communal meals, structured prayer, hierarchical ordering, and disciplinary procedures anticipate many of the features that would characterize Catholic monastic life for over fifteen centuries.

## The Sacred Meal

The communal meal described in this chapter, presided over by a priest who pronounces blessings over bread and wine, has drawn significant interest from Catholic sacramental theologians. While the Qumran meal is not identified as a sacrifice, its sacred character, its priestly presidency, and its communal nature invite comparison with the Eucharist. Catholic scholars see in this meal a preparation for the fuller sacramental reality that Christ would institute at the Last Supper.

## Fraternal Correction

The chapter's graduated system of fraternal correction — private admonition, formal complaint before witnesses, communal judgment — parallels both the Gospel instruction in Matthew 18 and the Catholic tradition of fraternal correction in religious communities. Catholic moral theology has developed this principle into a broader ethic of charitable correction that balances justice with mercy.
