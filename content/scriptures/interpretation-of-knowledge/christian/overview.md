## Overview

For Christian readers, the Interpretation of Knowledge is one of the most directly relevant Nag Hammadi texts because it addresses a problem familiar from Paul's letters: division over spiritual gifts (1 Corinthians 12-14). The text's solution — deeper knowledge of the body's unity in Christ — is structurally similar to Paul's own argument, though the theological framework is Valentinian rather than proto-orthodox. The text demonstrates that Valentinian Christians read Paul seriously, worshipped in communities, and experienced the same pastoral challenges as other early Christian groups.
