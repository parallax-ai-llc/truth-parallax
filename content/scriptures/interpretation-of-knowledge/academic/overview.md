## Overview

The Interpretation of Knowledge is critically important for understanding Valentinian Christianity as a social reality rather than merely a system of thought. The text's direct engagement with congregational conflict — jealousy over spiritual gifts, tension between those with different charismata — provides evidence that Valentinian communities had worship gatherings, recognized diverse spiritual gifts, and experienced the same kinds of social tensions attested in Paul's Corinthian correspondence. The text's use of 1 Corinthians 12 as its primary scriptural framework demonstrates the centrality of Paul to Valentinian theology and ecclesiology.
