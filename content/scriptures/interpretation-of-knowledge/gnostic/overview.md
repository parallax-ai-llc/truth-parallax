## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Interpretation of Knowledge is a rare window into the pastoral life of a Valentinian community. The text addresses a very practical problem — jealousy and division over spiritual gifts — and offers a very Valentinian solution: deeper knowledge (gnosis) of the unity established by the Savior's sacrifice. The body-of-Christ metaphor, borrowed from Paul (1 Corinthians 12), is interpreted through a Valentinian lens: all members of the spiritual body share a common origin in the Pleroma, and jealousy reflects ignorance of this shared divine nature. The text demonstrates that Valentinian Christianity was not merely an intellectual movement but a living community with real social challenges.
