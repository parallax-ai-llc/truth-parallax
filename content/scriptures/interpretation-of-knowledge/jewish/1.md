## The Savior's Sacrifice for Unity — Jewish Perspectives

Jewish readers encounter in this section of Interpretation of Knowledge evidence of how Jewish traditions were appropriated and transformed in the Gnostic context. Jewish readers find in the Interpretation of Knowledge an engagement with the concept of communal solidarity that resonates with the Jewish idea of kol Yisrael arevim zeh ba-zeh ('all Israel is responsible for one another,' Talmud Shevuot 39a).

The section "The Savior's Sacrifice for Unity" draws on Jewish sources — Genesis narratives, prophetic imagery, wisdom traditions, and possibly early mystical speculation — but deploys them within a framework that diverges from rabbinic Judaism. This transformation is not simple distortion but a creative engagement with traditions that remained fluid and contested in the second and third centuries.

Reading this passage against rabbinic parallels illuminates both shared concerns and fundamental divergences — revealing what was at stake theologically when Jewish concepts entered Gnostic systems.
