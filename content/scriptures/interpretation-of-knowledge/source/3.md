---
title: "Interpretation of Knowledge - Chapter 3"
scripture: "interpretation-of-knowledge"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Now concerning the faith that you have received: guard it carefully. Do not become negligent, but pursue the knowledge of truth with diligence.

**2** For the adversary seeks to divide the members from one another, so that the body may be weakened. But if the members remain united, the adversary cannot prevail.

**3** Remember that the Savior said, "Where two or three are gathered in my name, there I am in their midst." For unity is the power of the body.

**4** Do not be like those who received the word but then turned away because of persecution. Rather, be like those who received the word and bore fruit, some thirtyfold, some sixtyfold, some a hundredfold.

**5** The Savior planted his word in the hearts of men. Some hearts were like rocky ground, and the word withered. Some hearts were like thorny ground, and the cares of the world choked the word. But some hearts were like good soil, and the word grew and produced fruit.

**6** Be therefore good soil. Receive the word with joy, and let it take root in your heart. Water it with prayer, and tend it with good deeds, so that it may bring forth an abundant harvest.

**7** For the knowledge of truth is not given for you alone, but for the sake of the entire body. Share what you have received, and do not hoard it.

**8** The lamp is not placed under a basket, but on a stand, so that it gives light to all who are in the house. So let your light shine before others, that they may see the truth and glorify the Father.

**9** This then is the interpretation of knowledge: that the Savior came to unite the divided, to heal the wounded, and to enlighten the ignorant. And the members of his body are to continue his work in the world.

**10** May the grace of the great invisible spirit be with you all, now and always. The Interpretation of Knowledge.
