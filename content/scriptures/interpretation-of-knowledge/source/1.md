---
title: "Interpretation of Knowledge - Chapter 1"
scripture: "interpretation-of-knowledge"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Those who are ignorant do not seek after God. For the thought does not seek; it receives. And the thought does not become wise through knowledge of the truth, but through the grace of the one who has given.

**2** Since we knew him not, we did not understand his wisdom. For to understand what belongs to God requires that God give the understanding. Yet those who are ignorant turn away from the light.

**3** For the word of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved, it is the power of God. Not as a stumbling block, but as the foundation of the cosmos.

**4** Now we know that the Savior descended not for our sake alone, but for the sake of the entirety. He came for the members, some of whom had already been made known to themselves, and some who had not yet been.

**5** Yet the members of the body are differentiated: some are eyes, some are hands, some are feet, some are ears, some are mouths. But are the eyes jealous of the ears? Or is the hand envious of the foot?

**6** The Savior taught that those who have received a gift should not be envious of those who have received differently, but should share their gifts with others.

**7** For the head cannot say to the feet, "I have no need of you." All the members suffer when one member suffers, and when one member is glorified, all the members rejoice.

**8** Therefore do not be jealous of your brother when he prospers, and do not hate the one who has received more than you. Rather, be at peace within the body.

**9** What gift then have you received from the Savior? Is it not the knowledge of truth? And if you have received knowledge, share it freely, so that others may also know the truth.

**10** For the Savior came to heal the division within the body, and to unite the members in one spirit, so that they might know the Father and be saved.
