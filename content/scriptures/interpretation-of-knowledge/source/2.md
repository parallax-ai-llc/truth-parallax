---
title: "Interpretation of Knowledge - Chapter 2"
scripture: "interpretation-of-knowledge"
chapter: 2
---

**1** He who was despised, is he not the one who was honored? And he who was cast out, is he not the one who was received? He was humbled, that you might be exalted. He took upon himself poverty, that you might be enriched.

**2** For the Savior became all things to all, that he might save all. He who had no sin took upon himself the sins of the world, that the world might be reconciled to the Father.

**3** He was wounded that your wounds might be healed. He suffered that your suffering might come to an end. He was put to death that you might receive life.

**4** For this reason the word says: "He was reckoned among the transgressors." And again: "He was led as a sheep to the slaughter, and as a lamb before its shearer is silent."

**5** But who has believed our report? And to whom has the arm of the Lord been revealed? He came unto his own, and his own received him not.

**6** Yet those who received him, to them he gave the power to become children of the Father. Not by the will of the flesh, but by the will of the spirit.

**7** Therefore the members should not be envious of one another. Let the one who has the word share it. Let the one who has the knowledge teach it. Let the one who has the power use it for service.

**8** For the body is one and has many members, and all the members of the body, being many, are one body. The foot is not less because it is not the hand. The ear is not less because it is not the eye.

**9** Rather, each member fulfills its purpose within the body, and the whole body is built up in love. This is the mystery of the church, which is the body of the Savior.

**10** He is the head, and we are the members. And just as the head cannot be separated from the body, so the members cannot be separated from one another without bringing harm to the whole.
