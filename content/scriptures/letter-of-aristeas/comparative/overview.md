## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Letter of Aristeas places it within the global phenomenon of sacred translation. The question of whether divine texts can be rendered in another language without loss — addressed by the Letter's claim of miraculous agreement among the translators — resonates with Islamic debates about translating the Qur'an, Buddhist discussions of rendering sutras from Sanskrit, and Christian reflection on the Word made flesh as divine 'translation.'
