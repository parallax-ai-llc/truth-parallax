## Overview

The Protestant engagement with the Letter of Aristeas reflects the tradition's complex relationship with the Septuagint. While Protestants generally prefer the Hebrew Masoretic text, understanding the Septuagint's origins is essential for studying the New Testament, which predominantly quotes the Old Testament in Greek. The Letter illuminates the translation tradition that shaped the Bible of the early church.
