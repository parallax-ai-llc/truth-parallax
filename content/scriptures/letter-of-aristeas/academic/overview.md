## Overview

The Academic study of the Letter of Aristeas analyzes the text as a work of second-century BCE Jewish apologetics rather than historical reportage. Scholars examine its ideological program — the legitimization of the Septuagint, the presentation of Judaism to a Greek audience, and the implicit argument for the compatibility of Torah and philosophy. The text's evidence for Hellenistic Jewish social location and cultural strategy is of primary interest.
