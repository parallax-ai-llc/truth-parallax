---
title: "Letter of Aristeas — Section 1"
scripture: "letter-of-aristeas"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Since I have collected material for a memorable history of my visit to Eleazar the High Priest of the Jews, and because you, Philocrates, have thought it important to learn about the purpose and reasons for the embassy, I have attempted to set it clearly before you.

**2** It was my devotion to the pursuit of religious knowledge that led me to undertake the embassy to the man I have mentioned, who was held in the highest esteem by his own citizens and by others, both for his virtue and his position.

**3** He possessed documents of the highest value to the Jews in his own land and elsewhere for the interpretation of the divine law, since their laws are written on leather parchments in Jewish characters.

**4** This embassy was arranged by me with great care. I had found a favorable opportunity in my campaign for the emancipation of those who had been carried into captivity from the land of the Jews by the father of the king.

**5** I convinced the king to free more than one hundred thousand captives, paying their owners the appropriate market price.
