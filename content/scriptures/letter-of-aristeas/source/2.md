---
title: "Letter of Aristeas — Section 2"
scripture: "letter-of-aristeas"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Demetrius of Phalerum, the president of the royal library, received large sums of money for the purpose of collecting, if possible, all the books in the world.

**2** By purchase and transcription, he carried out the purpose of the king to the best of his ability. He was asked in my presence, How many thousand books are there in the library?

**3** He replied: More than two hundred thousand, O King, and I shall make endeavor in the immediate future to gather together the remainder, so as to make up a total of half a million.

**4** I am told that the laws of the Jews are worth transcribing and deserve a place in your library.

**5** What is to prevent you from doing this? replied the king. Everything that is necessary has been placed at your disposal.

**6** They need to be translated, answered Demetrius, for in the country of the Jews they use a peculiar alphabet, just as the Egyptians use their form of letters. They have their own particular dialect as well.
