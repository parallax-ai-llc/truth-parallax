## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox encounter with the Letter of Aristeas is particularly significant because the Septuagint remains the authoritative Old Testament of the Orthodox Church. The Letter's narrative of miraculous translation undergirds the Orthodox conviction that the Septuagint is not merely a translation but a divinely inspired text in its own right, a position articulated by Church Fathers including Augustine and affirmed in Orthodox liturgical practice.
