## Overview

The Khuddaka Nikaya ("Minor Collection") is the fifth and final nikaya of the Sutta Pitaka in the Pali Canon. Despite its name, it is the largest collection, containing 18 books of extraordinary literary, doctrinal, and devotional diversity. It includes some of the most beloved texts in Buddhist literature: the Dhammapada, the Jataka tales, the poetry of the elder monks and nuns (Theragatha and Therigatha), and the philosophical Patisambhidamagga.

## Structure

The 18 books of the Khuddaka Nikaya:

1. **Khuddakapatha**: Short readings for novices
2. **Dhammapada**: Verses on the path of truth
3. **Udana**: Inspired utterances of the Buddha
4. **Itivuttaka**: "Thus it was said" — short discourses
5. **Sutta Nipata**: Collection of early discourses in verse
6. **Vimanavatthu**: Stories of heavenly mansions
7. **Petavatthu**: Stories of hungry ghosts
8. **Theragatha**: Verses of the elder monks
9. **Therigatha**: Verses of the elder nuns
10. **Jataka**: Birth stories of the Buddha's previous lives
11. **Niddesa**: Commentary on the Sutta Nipata
12. **Patisambhidamagga**: Path of discrimination — analytical treatise
13. **Apadana**: Lives of saints
14. **Buddhavamsa**: Chronicle of the Buddhas
15. **Cariyapitaka**: Basket of conduct — Bodhisatta practices
16. **Nettippakarana**: Guide to interpretation
17. **Petakopadesa**: Pitaka disclosure
18. **Milindapanha**: Questions of King Milinda

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Many texts in the Khuddaka Nikaya have influenced all Buddhist traditions. The Jataka tales are known throughout Asia. The Therigatha represents one of the earliest collections of women's spiritual poetry in world literature. The Dhammapada is perhaps the most widely translated Buddhist text.
