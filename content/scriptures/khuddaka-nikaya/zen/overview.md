## Overview

Zen Buddhism finds in the Khuddaka Nikaya's verse collections a model for the direct, poetic expression of awakened understanding. The Theragatha and Therigatha's poems of sudden realization and the Dhammapada's pithy wisdom resonate deeply with the Zen aesthetic.

## Zen Reading

Zen practitioners appreciate the Udana's accounts of the Buddha's spontaneous utterances of awakened insight, which parallel the Zen tradition of spontaneous expression (jigo). The Sutta Nipata's early, unadorned verses are valued for their directness.

## Relevance to Practice

The Khuddaka Nikaya's verse literature demonstrates that awakening can be expressed in immediate, experiential language that bypasses conceptual elaboration -- a principle central to Zen.
