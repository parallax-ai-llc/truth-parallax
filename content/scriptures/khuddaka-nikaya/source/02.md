---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 2: Dhammapada (The Path of Dhamma)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Mind is the forerunner of all actions. All deeds are led by mind, created by mind. If one speaks or acts with a corrupt mind, suffering follows, as the wheel follows the hoof of the ox that draws the cart.

**2** Mind is the forerunner of all actions. All deeds are led by mind, created by mind. If one speaks or acts with a serene mind, happiness follows, as a shadow that never departs.

**3** "He abused me, he struck me, he overpowered me, he robbed me." Those who harbour such thoughts do not still their hatred.

**4** "He abused me, he struck me, he overpowered me, he robbed me." Those who do not harbour such thoughts still their hatred.

**5** Hatred is never appeased by hatred in this world. By non-hatred alone is hatred appeased. This is a law eternal.

**6** There are those who do not realize that one day we all must die. But those who do realize this settle their quarrels.

**7** Heedfulness is the path to the Deathless. Heedlessness is the path to death. The heedful do not die. The heedless are as if already dead.

**8** The wise who are heedful, contemplative, and energetic attain Nibbana, the supreme freedom and happiness.

**9** By effort, by heedfulness, by restraint, and by self-control, the wise person may make for himself an island which no flood can overwhelm.

**10** The foolish and the ignorant give themselves over to heedlessness. But the wise guard heedfulness as their greatest treasure.

**11** Hard it is to be born as a human being. Hard is the life of mortals. Hard is the hearing of the true Dhamma. Hard is the arising of a Buddha.

**12** Not to do any evil, to cultivate good, to purify one's mind—this is the teaching of the Buddhas.

**13** Patient endurance is the supreme austerity. Nibbana is supreme, say the Buddhas. One is not a monk who harms another. One is not an ascetic who oppresses others.

**14** One is not made pure by birth, nor by family. By truth one is pure, by truth one is a brahman.

**15** All conditioned things are impermanent. When one perceives this with wisdom, one becomes disenchanted with suffering. This is the path to purity. All conditioned things are suffering. When one perceives this with wisdom, one becomes disenchanted with suffering. All phenomena are not-self. When one perceives this with wisdom, one becomes disenchanted with suffering.
