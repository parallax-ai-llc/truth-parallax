---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 18: Milindapanha (The Questions of King Milinda)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 18
---

**1** King Milinda went to the Elder Nagasena, and having exchanged courteous greetings, sat to one side. King Milinda said: "How is Your Reverence known, and what is your name, venerable sir?"

**2** "I am known as Nagasena, O king. But although parents give such a name, it is only a designation, a conventional term, a mere name. For there is no permanent individual implied therein."

**3** King Milinda said: "If there is no permanent individual, then who is it that stands before me? Who wears robes? Who receives food? Who meditates? Who attains Nibbana? There would be neither merit nor demerit."

**4** The Elder said: "O king, is the chariot the axle?" "No, venerable sir." "Is it the wheels? The body? The pole? The yoke?" "No, venerable sir." "Is the chariot all of these things taken together?" "No." "Is the chariot something apart from all these things?" "No."

**5** "Then, O king, I can discover no chariot! 'Chariot' is merely a word, a name, a designation, a conventional term for the combination of axle, wheels, body, pole, and yoke."

**6** "Just so, O king, 'Nagasena' is merely a word, a name, a designation, a conventional term for the combination of the aggregates of form, feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness. In the ultimate sense, there is no person here to be found."

**7** "Excellent, Nagasena! You have answered well. If the Buddha himself were here, he would approve." The king was delighted.

**8** The king asked: "Venerable Nagasena, what is it that is reborn?" "Name-and-form is reborn, O king." "Is it this same name-and-form that is reborn?" "No. But by this name-and-form deeds are done, good or evil, and by these deeds another name-and-form is reborn."

**9** "Give me an illustration." "It is like a flame, O king. Is the flame that burns in the first watch the same as the flame that burns in the middle watch?" "No." "Or the same as the one that burns in the last watch?" "No." "Then are there three flames?" "No, the light burns all night dependent on one and the same flame."

**10** "Just so, O king, the continuity of phenomena is connected; one arises, another ceases, succeeding one another, as it were, without interval. Therefore, neither as the same nor as another does one arrive at the last stage of consciousness."

**11** The king asked: "What is Nibbana? Is it something that can be pointed to in shape, or form, or size, or location?" The Elder said: "Nibbana cannot be pointed to in form, shape, or size. But it exists. Just as the wind cannot be shown in colour or shape, yet the wind exists, so Nibbana exists, though it cannot be shown."

**12** "By the practice of the Noble Eightfold Path, Nibbana is attained. That is all that can be said. But the peace of Nibbana is supreme. The bliss of Nibbana is supreme. The freedom of Nibbana is supreme. There is nothing higher. There is nothing beyond."
