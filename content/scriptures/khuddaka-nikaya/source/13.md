---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 13: Apadana (Legends of the Saints)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 13
---

**1** The Apadana of the Elder Sariputta: In a past life, immeasurable kalpas ago, when the Buddha Anomadassi appeared in the world, I saw the Blessed One, and with a delighted mind, I offered a handful of flowers.

**2** By that single act of offering flowers to a Buddha, I was reborn in heavenly realms for countless aeons. Never did I take birth in a lower realm. This is the fruit of that single offering made with faith.

**3** In this final existence, under the Buddha Gotama, I attained Arahantship and became the Marshal of the Dhamma, foremost in wisdom among all the disciples.

**4** The Apadana of the Elder Moggallana: In a distant past, I offered a lamp to a stupa of a former Buddha. By the merit of that offering of light, I was born again and again in fortunate realms.

**5** In this final birth, I attained the six supernatural knowledges and became foremost in psychic power among the disciples of the Buddha Gotama.

**6** The Apadana of the Elder Ananda: Long ago, I served a former Buddha as his attendant with devoted service, carrying his bowl, washing his robes, sweeping his dwelling. By that merit of service, I became, in this life, the devoted attendant of the Buddha Gotama.

**7** I memorised every word of the Dhamma spoken by the Blessed One. After his passing, at the First Council, I recited the entire Sutta Pitaka. Thus was the teaching preserved.

**8** The Apadana of the Elder Nun Mahapajapati: I was the first woman to go forth in the dispensation of the Buddha Gotama. I was the Buddha's foster-mother, who raised him after the death of his mother Maya.

**9** In past lives I had made many offerings to former Buddhas and had vowed: "May I be foremost among the nuns." That vow was fulfilled in this life.

**10** The Apadana of the Elder Nun Khema: In a past life, under a former Buddha, I offered a golden image and made the aspiration: "May I be foremost in wisdom among the nuns." That aspiration was fulfilled.

**11** The Blessed One declared: "Khema is foremost among my female disciples in wisdom." Having cut through all fetters, having destroyed all defilements, I dwell in the peace of Nibbana.

**12** Thus the Apadana records the past deeds and present attainments of the great elders, showing how the seeds of virtue planted in past lives bear fruit in the present, how the aspiration made before former Buddhas is fulfilled in the dispensation of the present Buddha.
