---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 14: Buddhavamsa (Chronicle of Buddhas)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 14
---

**1** At the great miracle, at the foot of a pair of Sala trees, seated in the noble posture, the Conqueror was asked by Sariputta: "How many Buddhas have there been? May the Victor tell us the line of the Buddhas."

**2** The Blessed One said: "Incalculable aeons ago, there was a Buddha named Dipankara, a Sage of great glory, immeasurable and hard to approach, beyond the range of thought."

**3** "At that time, I was an ascetic named Sumedha. Seeing the Buddha Dipankara, I lay down in the mud before him and spread my matted hair, making a path for him to walk upon. And I made the aspiration: 'May I, too, in the future, become a Buddha like this.'"

**4** "The Buddha Dipankara, standing at my head, looked into the future and declared: 'This ascetic, in a future age, will become a Buddha named Gotama.' From that moment, through countless lives, I practised the ten perfections."

**5** "After Dipankara, there arose the Buddha Kondanna, then the Buddha Mangala, then the Buddha Sumana, then the Buddha Revata, then the Buddha Sobhita."

**6** "Then arose the Buddha Anomadassi, the Buddha Paduma, the Buddha Narada, the Buddha Padumuttara, the Buddha Sumedha, the Buddha Sujata."

**7** "Then arose the Buddha Piyadassi, the Buddha Atthadassi, the Buddha Dhammadassi, the Buddha Siddhattha, the Buddha Tissa, the Buddha Phussa."

**8** "Then arose the Buddha Vipassi, the Buddha Sikhi, the Buddha Vessabhu, the Buddha Kakusandha, the Buddha Konagamana, the Buddha Kassapa."

**9** "And now I have arisen, the Buddha Gotama, the twenty-fifth in the line. Under each of these Buddhas I performed some meritorious deed and received the assurance of future Buddhahood."

**10** "Under the Buddha Dipankara, I practised the perfection of giving. Under another, the perfection of virtue. Under another, renunciation. Under another, wisdom. Under another, energy. Under another, patience. Under another, truthfulness. Under another, determination. Under another, loving-kindness. Under another, equanimity."

**11** "Through four incalculable ages and a hundred thousand aeons, I perfected these ten paramitas, and then I attained the supreme perfect enlightenment under the Bodhi tree, becoming the Sammasambuddha."

**12** "In the future, the next Buddha to arise in this world will be Metteyya, the Compassionate One. He will arise when the human lifespan has again increased, when the Dhamma has been lost. He will re-establish the teaching and bring liberation to countless beings."
