---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 16: Nettippakarana (The Book of Guidance)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 16
---

**1** Homage to the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One. This is the Netti, the Book of Guidance, by means of which the meaning of the Suttas is disclosed. It was spoken by the Elder Mahakaccana.

**2** There are three roots of the teaching: the root of suffering, the root of its origin, and the root of its cessation. All the teaching of the Blessed One revolves around these three.

**3** There are sixteen modes of conveying the teaching. These are the sixteen methods by which the Blessed One conveyed his meaning in the Suttas. They are called hara, the modes of guidance.

**4** The first mode is desana-hara, the mode of teaching. The Blessed One teaches by way of two foundations: the conditioned and the unconditioned, the wholesome and the unwholesome, the dark and the bright.

**5** The second mode is vicaya-hara, the mode of investigation. This investigates by way of the aggregates, the elements, and the bases—analysing phenomena into their components.

**6** The third mode is yutti-hara, the mode of construing. This construes the meaning by way of the dependent origination: "When this exists, that exists; with the arising of this, that arises."

**7** The fourth mode is padatthana-hara, the mode of bases. This traces the foundation of phenomena: craving is the foundation of clinging; clinging is the foundation of becoming; becoming is the foundation of birth.

**8** The fifth mode is lakkana-hara, the mode of characteristics. This defines the characteristics of phenomena: the characteristic of suffering is oppression; the characteristic of impermanence is arising and passing; the characteristic of not-self is uncontrollability.

**9** The sixth mode is catuvyuha-hara, the mode of the fourfold array. The teaching can be arranged under four headings: desire, being, views, and ignorance. All suffering can be traced to one of these four.

**10** All the Suttas of the Blessed One can be understood through these methods. The entire Tipitaka—the Vinaya, the Suttas, and the Abhidhamma—can be analysed by these modes of guidance.

**11** The purpose of the Netti is to enable the student of the Dhamma to penetrate the meaning of the Suttas, to see beneath the surface of the words to the intention of the Teacher.

**12** For the Dhamma is deep, hard to see, hard to understand, peaceful, excellent, beyond the reach of mere reasoning, subtle, to be experienced by the wise. The Netti provides the key to unlock its meaning.
