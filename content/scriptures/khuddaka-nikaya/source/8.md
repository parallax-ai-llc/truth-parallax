---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 8: Theragatha (Verses of the Elder Monks)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Subhuti spoke: "My little hut is roofed and pleasant; sheltered from the wind—so rain, sky, if you wish! My mind is well concentrated, my mind is freed—so rain, sky, if you wish!"

**2** Mahakassapa spoke: "Those rocky heights with hue of dark-blue clouds, where lies embossed many a shining lake, where limpid waters lave the mountain side—these are the haunts that bring me my delight."

**3** "Like the many-crested clouds that hang upon the mountain slopes, in those rocky heights I dwell alone, like a bird upon a branch. No company is there for me; it is like a lonely elephant in the forest."

**4** Talaputa spoke: "When shall I dwell in mountain caves, alone, without a companion, having discerned all existence as impermanent? This is my prayer."

**5** "When shall I dwell content with robe from dust-heap gathered, clad in the garment no one envies, wearing the cast-off clothing of the dead?"

**6** "When, with body wasted, as a tree in the forest, on a couch of grass in a mountain cave, shall I be free from care?"

**7** Adhimutta spoke: "I have no fear of death, nor do I long for life. I shall lay down this body fully conscious, mindful, and self-possessed."

**8** "I have served the Teacher well. The Buddha's bidding has been done. The heavy burden has been laid down; the guide to becoming has been uprooted."

**9** Sundara-Samudda spoke: "As a tree thrown down upon a hill, on the fourteenth day I shall be liberated. Having cut away both name and form, free from the taints, the cankers all destroyed."

**10** Sappadasa spoke: "Twenty-five years since my going forth, and not for a finger-snap's duration have I gained calm of mind. Having gained no concentration, consumed by lust, I stretched out my arms and wept."

**11** "Then, taking a razor, I thought: 'What is the use of this life?' But as I grasped the razor, insight arose in me. The danger became apparent, disenchantment became established. And therewith my mind was freed."

**12** Angulimala spoke: "Who once did live in negligence and then is negligent no more, he illuminates the world like the moon freed from a cloud. Whose evil deeds are covered over by good, he illuminates the world like the moon freed from a cloud."
