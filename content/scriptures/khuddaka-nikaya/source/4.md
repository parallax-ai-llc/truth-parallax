---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 4: Itivuttaka (The Thus-saids)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 4
---

**1** This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Arahant, so I have heard: "Monks, abandon one thing and I guarantee you non-returning. What one thing? Abandon greed and I guarantee you non-returning."

**2** Greed must be abandoned for the sake of realizing Nibbana. When a monk has abandoned greed, there is nothing to cause him to return to this world.

**3** This was said by the Blessed One: "Monks, abandon one thing and I guarantee you non-returning. What one thing? Abandon hatred and I guarantee you non-returning."

**4** This was said by the Blessed One: "Monks, abandon one thing and I guarantee you non-returning. What one thing? Abandon delusion and I guarantee you non-returning."

**5** This was said by the Blessed One: "Monks, there are these three roots of the unwholesome. What three? Greed, hatred, and delusion."

**6** "Whatever unwholesome action is done by a greedy person, a hateful person, a deluded person, whether by body, speech, or mind—all that is unwholesome."

**7** This was said by the Blessed One: "Monks, there are these three elements of escape. What three? The escape from sensuality, that is, renunciation. The escape from form, that is, the formless. And the escape from whatever has come to be, from anything conditioned—that is cessation."

**8** This was said by the Blessed One: "Monks, there are these two forms of Nibbana-element. What two? The Nibbana-element with residue remaining and the Nibbana-element without residue remaining."

**9** "And what is the Nibbana-element with residue remaining? Here a monk is an Arahant, whose taints are destroyed, who has lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, reached the true goal, destroyed the fetter of becoming, and is completely liberated through final knowledge. His five sense faculties remain, and owing to their being intact, he still experiences what is agreeable and disagreeable. That his lust, hatred, and delusion are destroyed—this is the Nibbana-element with residue remaining."

**10** "And what is the Nibbana-element without residue remaining? Here a monk is an Arahant, whose taints are destroyed. For him, all that is felt, not being delighted in, will become cool right here. This is the Nibbana-element without residue remaining."

**11** This was said by the Blessed One: "Monks, even if a monk were to seize the hem of my garment and walk behind me step for step, yet if he were covetous in his desires, with fierce longing for sense-pleasures, with a mind of ill will, with corrupt intentions, confused and unmindful, unalert, unconcentrated, scatterbrained, with faculties uncontrolled, that monk would be far from me and I from him."

**12** "But if a monk were to dwell a hundred yojanas away, yet if he is not covetous, not fierce in his longing, with a mind without ill will, uncorrupted in his intentions, with mindfulness established, alert, concentrated, with unified mind, with faculties controlled—that monk would be near to me and I to him."
