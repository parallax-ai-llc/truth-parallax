---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 9: Therigatha (Verses of the Elder Nuns)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Mutta spoke: "I am well freed, well freed, thoroughly well freed from three crooked things: from the mortar, from the pestle, and from my crooked husband. I am freed from birth and death. That which led to further becoming has been rooted out."

**2** Punna spoke: "I used to fill water-pots. Even in the cold I went down to the water, frightened by the threat of blows, afraid of angry words and punishment. But now I am free."

**3** "O water-pot, be happy! I set you free today. The thought of washing you has left me. I go; I dwell beside the tree and contemplate, 'Ah, happiness!'"

**4** Sumangalamata spoke: "O woman well set free! How free am I, how thoroughly free from kitchen drudgery! My worthless husband made me grind his sesame. Free from all that, free from that degrading work."

**5** "My angry and my greedy heart I have calmed and cooled. Now free I go beneath the trees and contemplate with happiness."

**6** Patacara spoke: "With ploughshares ploughing up the field, with seed the earth is sown. By supporting wife and children, young men acquire wealth. Then why have I, who keep the moral precepts, who do the Teacher's bidding, not attained Nibbana? I am not lazy, not puffed up."

**7** "Washing my feet, I watched the water as it flowed down the slope. On that my heart was calmed, as one calms a noble horse. Then I took a lamp and entered my chamber. I examined the bed, and sat upon the couch."

**8** "Then I took a needle and drew out the wick. The liberation of my mind was like the quenching of that lamp."

**9** Ambapali spoke: "My hair was black, like the colour of bees, with curly ends. Now, because of old age, it is like bark-fibre of hemp. Not otherwise is the word of the Speaker of Truth."

**10** "Fragrant as a perfumed casket full of fragrant things was my hair. Now, because of old age, it smells like rabbit's fur. Not otherwise is the word of the Speaker of Truth."

**11** "Such was this body. Now it is decrepit and aged, the abode of many pains. An old house with its plaster falling off. Not otherwise is the word of the Speaker of Truth."

**12** Mahapajapati Gotami spoke: "The Buddha, the hero, I honour. Best of all beings, who has liberated me and many other people from pain. All suffering has been understood. The cause of suffering, craving, has been dried up. The Noble Eightfold Path has been developed. The cessation has been realized. I have been the mother, and the mother's sister, the nurse and the disciple. Now I have done what should be done."
