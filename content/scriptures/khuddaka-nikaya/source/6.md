---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 6: Vimanavatthu (Stories of Celestial Mansions)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The venerable Moggallana, having risen to the celestial realm by his psychic power, saw a deva dwelling in a magnificent celestial mansion. He asked: "Deva, you dwell here in great splendour, surrounded by celestial nymphs, shining in all directions. What meritorious deed did you perform?"

**2** The deva replied: "In my previous life, when I was a human being, I offered a single meal with a devout heart to a monk who was an Arahant. By that single act of generosity, performed with faith, I was reborn here in this celestial mansion."

**3** The venerable Moggallana saw another deva dwelling in a mansion made of gold, with walls of beryl, floors of crystal, and pillars of gems. He asked: "What deed did you perform?"

**4** "Venerable sir, when I was human, I was a poor woman. But I had faith in the Blessed One. One day I offered my only garment to a monk who needed robes. By that single act, I was reborn here among the devas."

**5** Another deva said: "I was a servant in a wealthy household. Though I had nothing of my own, I served the monks with water and swept the monastery grounds. With a joyful heart I did this service. By that merit, I now enjoy this celestial splendour."

**6** Another deva said: "I kept the five precepts faithfully throughout my life. I abstained from killing, from stealing, from sexual misconduct, from lying, and from intoxicants. By that moral conduct, I was reborn here."

**7** Another deva said: "I built a rest-house for weary travellers and monks. I provided water, shade, and food for those who passed. By that merit of building a dwelling for the benefit of others, I now dwell in this celestial mansion."

**8** Another deva said: "I offered flowers to a stupa of the Buddha. With a devout heart, I placed garlands at the foot of the sacred shrine. By that simple offering, made with faith, I now shine with celestial radiance."

**9** The venerable Moggallana said: "Small indeed is the effort required to generate great merit. A single meal offered with faith, a single garment given with devotion, a single act of service performed with a pure heart—these lead to great rewards."

**10** "Not by great wealth is merit measured, but by the purity of the heart that gives. The widow's mite given with faith exceeds the rich man's gift given with pride."

**11** "Therefore let the wise, even with small means, cultivate generosity, morality, and mental development. For these lead to the celestial realms and beyond."

**12** "But even the celestial realms are impermanent. The devas, having exhausted their merit, must pass away. Therefore the truly wise aspire not to heaven but to Nibbana, the deathless, where there is no more passing away."
