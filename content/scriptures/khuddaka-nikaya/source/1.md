---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 1: Khuddakapatha (The Short Passages)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 1
---

**1** I go for refuge to the Buddha. I go for refuge to the Dhamma. I go for refuge to the Sangha. For the second time I go for refuge to the Buddha. For the second time I go for refuge to the Dhamma. For the second time I go for refuge to the Sangha. For the third time I go for refuge to the Buddha. For the third time I go for refuge to the Dhamma. For the third time I go for refuge to the Sangha.

**2** I undertake the training rule of abstaining from the taking of life. I undertake the training rule of abstaining from taking what is not given. I undertake the training rule of abstaining from sexual misconduct. I undertake the training rule of abstaining from false speech. I undertake the training rule of abstaining from fermented and distilled beverages that cause heedlessness.

**3** The Thirty-two Parts of the Body: In this body there are: head-hairs, body-hairs, nails, teeth, skin, flesh, sinews, bones, bone-marrow, kidneys, heart, liver, pleura, spleen, lungs, large intestines, small intestines, gorge, faeces, bile, phlegm, pus, blood, sweat, fat, tears, grease, spittle, snot, oil of the joints, and urine.

**4** Questions to the Novice: What is one? All beings subsist on food. What are two? Name and form. What are three? Three kinds of feeling. What are four? The Four Noble Truths. What are five? The five aggregates of clinging.

**5** What are six? The six internal sense bases. What are seven? The seven factors of enlightenment. What are eight? The Noble Eightfold Path. What are nine? The nine abodes of beings. What are ten? One endowed with ten qualities is called an Arahant.

**6** The Metta Sutta: This is what should be done by one who is skilled in goodness, and who knows the path of peace: Let him be able and upright, straightforward and gentle in speech, humble and not conceited, contented and easily satisfied.

**7** Unburdened with duties and frugal in his ways, peaceful and calm, and wise and skilful, not proud and demanding in nature. Let him not do the slightest thing that the wise would later reprove.

**8** Let him think thus: "May all beings be happy and safe. May their hearts rejoice. Whatever living beings there may be—feeble or strong, tall, stout, or medium, short, small, or large, without exception—seen or unseen, those dwelling far or near, those who are born or who are to be born: may all beings be happy!"

**9** Let none deceive another, or despise any being in any state. Let none through anger or ill will wish harm upon another.

**10** Even as a mother protects with her life her child, her only child, so with a boundless heart should one cherish all living beings—radiating kindness over the entire world, spreading upwards to the skies, and downwards to the depths, outwards and unbounded.

**11** Freed from hatred and ill will, whether standing or walking, seated or lying down, free from drowsiness, one should sustain this mindfulness. This is said to be the sublime abiding.

**12** By not holding to fixed views, the pure-hearted one, having clarity of vision, being freed from all sense desires, is not born again into this world.

**13** The Mangala Sutta: Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi. A certain deity, in the far extreme of the night, illuminating the entire Jeta's Grove with her surpassing beauty, came to the Blessed One and asked: "Many devas and human beings have pondered on blessings. Tell me the highest blessings."

**14** The Buddha replied: "Not to associate with fools, to associate with the wise, and to honour those worthy of honour—this is the highest blessing. To reside in a suitable place, to have done meritorious deeds in the past, and to have set oneself on the right course—this is the highest blessing."

**15** "Vast learning, skill in handicraft, a highly trained discipline, and pleasant speech—this is the highest blessing. A mind that is not touched by the vicissitudes of the world, a mind that is sorrowless, stainless, and secure—this is the highest blessing."
