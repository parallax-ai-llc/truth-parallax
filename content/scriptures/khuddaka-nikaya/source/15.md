---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 15: Cariyapitaka (The Basket of Conduct)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Honouring the Fully Enlightened One, the Sage who has compassion for the world, I shall now describe the conduct that leads to the perfection of Buddhahood, as practised by the great being in his former lives.

**2** The Perfection of Giving: In times past, when I was the king Sivi, a brahman came to me and said: "O king, I beg for your eyes." Without hesitation, I plucked out both my eyes and gave them to him. By this act, the perfection of giving was fulfilled.

**3** In another life, when I was the prince Vessantara, I gave away everything I possessed—my wealth, my kingdom, my horses, my chariot, and finally my children and my wife. Not from hatred of them, but from the overwhelming force of the vow to perfect generosity.

**4** The Perfection of Virtue: When I was the naga king Campeyya, though captured by a snake-charmer and forced to perform before crowds, I did not break my precepts. I did not harm the charmer, though I had the power to destroy him. By this patience and moral restraint, the perfection of virtue was advanced.

**5** The Perfection of Renunciation: When I was the ascetic Lomahansa, I abandoned a comfortable life and took up the life of a wanderer, enduring heat and cold, hunger and thirst, insults and discomfort. I renounced all pleasures for the sake of the highest goal.

**6** The Perfection of Wisdom: When I was the wise Vidhura, I taught the Dhamma to the naga king with such skill that even the non-human beings were transformed. By this exercise of wisdom in the service of others, the perfection of wisdom was advanced.

**7** The Perfection of Energy: When I was the prince Mahosadha, I exerted tremendous energy in solving problems, in outwitting enemies, in protecting my people. Through tireless effort directed toward the welfare of all, the perfection of energy was advanced.

**8** The Perfection of Patience: When I was the ascetic Khantivadi, the Preacher of Patience, a drunken king sliced my body limb by limb. Even as my hands and feet were cut off, I bore no anger, no resentment. I said: "May the king be happy. May my patience be perfected."

**9** The Perfection of Truthfulness: When I was the ascetic Saccatapasa, I would not utter a single falsehood even to save my own life. Truth was dearer to me than life itself.

**10** The Perfection of Determination: When I was the prince Temiya, I maintained my vow of silence and immobility for sixteen years, enduring the contempt of the court, the threats of his father the king, even the threat of being buried alive—all to fulfil my determination to renounce the world.

**11** The Perfection of Loving-kindness: When I was the king Ekaraja, even when my kingdom was seized and I was bound in chains, I bore only loving-kindness toward my enemies. I wished them well and harboured no thought of revenge.

**12** Thus, through countless lives, I practised these ten perfections, enduring hardship upon hardship, sacrificing life after life, out of compassion for all beings and the aspiration to attain the supreme enlightenment. This is the Basket of Conduct of the Bodhisattva.
