---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 12: Patisambhidamagga (The Path of Discrimination)"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 12
---

**1** There are four discriminations: the discrimination of meaning, the discrimination of the Dhamma, the discrimination of language, and the discrimination of perspicacity. These are the four analytic knowledges.

**2** What is the discrimination of meaning? Knowledge of the fruit by way of the cause—this is the discrimination of meaning. When one knows: "Because of this condition, this result arises"—that is understanding of meaning.

**3** What is the discrimination of the Dhamma? Knowledge of the cause by way of the fruit—this is the discrimination of the Dhamma. When one knows: "This is the condition that produces this result"—that is understanding of the Dhamma.

**4** What is the discrimination of language? Knowledge of the speech that expresses the meaning and the Dhamma—this is the discrimination of language. Unconfused knowledge of expression, able to teach the Dhamma in the correct terms.

**5** What is the discrimination of perspicacity? Knowledge about the three knowledges already described—this is the discrimination of perspicacity. It is knowledge about knowledge, understanding about understanding, meta-awareness.

**6** By the discrimination of meaning, one analyses the results. By the discrimination of Dhamma, one analyses the conditions. By the discrimination of language, one analyses the expressions. By the discrimination of perspicacity, one analyses the knowledges.

**7** There are three kinds of wisdom: wisdom arising from learning, wisdom arising from reflection, and wisdom arising from mental development. Wisdom arising from learning is knowledge gained from study of the teachings. Wisdom arising from reflection is knowledge gained from personal investigation. Wisdom arising from development is knowledge gained from meditation.

**8** What is the knowledge of suffering? It is: "This is suffering." Suffering is the five aggregates of clinging. They are impermanent, they are suffering, they are a disease, they are an abscess, they are a dart, they are a calamity, they are an affliction, they are alien, they are disintegrating, they are empty, they are not self.

**9** What is the knowledge of the origin of suffering? It is: "This craving is the origin of suffering." It is the craving that leads to renewed being, accompanied by delight and lust, finding delight here and there: craving for sense-pleasures, craving for existence, craving for non-existence.

**10** What is the knowledge of the cessation of suffering? It is: "This is the cessation of suffering." It is the remainderless fading and cessation of that very craving, its forsaking and relinquishing, freedom from it, non-reliance on it.

**11** What is the knowledge of the path? It is: "This is the path." It is the Noble Eightfold Path: right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration.

**12** When one possesses the four discriminations fully, one can teach the Dhamma in all its aspects, answering every question, resolving every doubt. This is the attainment of the Arahant who is endowed with the analytic knowledges.
