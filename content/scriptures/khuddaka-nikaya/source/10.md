---
title: "Khuddaka Nikaya - Chapter 10: Jataka Tales (Birth Stories) - Selected Tales"
scripture: "khuddaka-nikaya"
chapter: 10
---

**1** The Apannaka Jataka: The Bodhisattva, in a former life, was a wise merchant. He taught his companions: "When travelling through the desert, if someone says, 'There is no need to carry water—there is water ahead,' do not believe them. Trust in what is certain, not in what is doubtful."

**2** "Those merchants who trusted the yakkha's words threw away their water and perished in the desert. But those who kept their water and followed the wise merchant reached their destination safely."

**3** "So too in this life, the wise follow what is certain—the path of virtue, the teaching of the Buddhas—and do not throw away the water of the Dhamma on account of false promises."

**4** The Nigrodhamiga Jataka: The Bodhisattva was born as a golden deer, king of a herd. A human king captured deer for food. The Bodhisattva arranged that each day one deer would offer itself, to spare the herd.

**5** One day it was the turn of a pregnant doe. She begged to be postponed until her fawn was born. The Bodhisattva took her place. When the king saw the deer-king offering himself, he was moved to compassion and released all the deer.

**6** "Thus, even in the animal realm, the great being practised the perfection of self-sacrifice. He who would become a Buddha must be willing to give even his own life for the sake of others."

**7** The Vessantara Jataka: In his last birth before becoming the Buddha, the Bodhisattva was Prince Vessantara, who practised the perfection of giving to the utmost degree.

**8** He gave away his white elephant, the treasure of the kingdom, because a brahman asked for it. The people were angry, and the king exiled him. He gave away his chariot, his horses, and finally, when asked, even his two children and his wife.

**9** "Not from attachment but from the power of his vow did he give. His giving was not born of indifference but of the overwhelming wish to perfect the paramita of generosity."

**10** The god Sakka, to test him, came disguised as a brahman and asked for his wife. Vessantara gave her. But Sakka immediately returned her, saying: "There is no one in this world equal to you in giving."

**11** "By this supreme act of giving, the Bodhisattva fulfilled the perfection of generosity. In his next life, he was born as Siddhattha Gotama and attained supreme enlightenment."

**12** "The Jataka tales teach that the path to Buddhahood is long and arduous. Through countless lives, the Bodhisattva practised the ten perfections: generosity, virtue, renunciation, wisdom, energy, patience, truthfulness, determination, loving-kindness, and equanimity. By perfecting these, he became the Buddha."
