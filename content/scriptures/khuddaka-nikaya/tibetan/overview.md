## Overview

Tibetan Buddhism draws extensively on the Khuddaka Nikaya's narrative traditions, particularly the Jataka tales, which are told throughout the Tibetan Buddhist world as examples of the bodhisattva's practice of the six perfections.

## Key Themes

Tibetan interpreters value the Jataka tales as practical illustrations of how the bodhisattva cultivates generosity, morality, patience, effort, concentration, and wisdom across countless lifetimes. The Buddhavamsa's chronicle of past Buddhas informs the Tibetan understanding of Buddha lineage.

## Relevance to Tibetan Study

The Khuddaka Nikaya's narrative traditions have deeply influenced Tibetan Buddhist culture, from monastic education to popular art and storytelling.
