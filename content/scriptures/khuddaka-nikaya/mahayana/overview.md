## Overview

Mahayana Buddhism has been significantly influenced by the Khuddaka Nikaya, particularly through the Jataka tales, which narrate the Buddha's previous lives as a bodhisattva. These narratives provided a crucial foundation for the development of the Mahayana bodhisattva ideal.

## Key Themes

Mahayana scholars see in the Jataka tales and the Cariyapitaka the seeds of the bodhisattva path -- the aspiration to achieve Buddhahood for the sake of all beings through the practice of the perfections (paramitas) across countless lifetimes.

## Relevance to Mahayana

The Khuddaka Nikaya's devotional literature, merit traditions, and narratives of self-sacrifice for others anticipate central Mahayana themes of compassion, dedication of merit, and the bodhisattva's vast aspiration.
