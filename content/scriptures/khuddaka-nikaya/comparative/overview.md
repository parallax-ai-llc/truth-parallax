## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Khuddaka Nikaya reveals rich parallels with literary and devotional traditions across world religions. The collection's genres -- birth stories, devotional poetry, women's testimony, philosophical analysis -- all have counterparts in other traditions.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Narrative Traditions

The Jataka tales can be compared with Jain narrative literature (Nayadhammakahao), Hindu Panchatantra, Aesop's fables, and other moral storytelling traditions.

### Women's Spiritual Literature

The Therigatha invites comparison with women's spiritual writing across traditions: Hildegard of Bingen, Rabi'a al-Adawiyya, Mirabai, and other women mystics.

## Comparative Framework

| Genre | Buddhism (KN) | Other Traditions |
|-------|--------------|------------------|
| Birth stories | Jatakas | Jain narratives, Hindu avatara tales |
| Women's poetry | Therigatha | Sufi poetry, Christian mystical writing |
| Moral verses | Dhammapada | Proverbs, Analects, Tirukkural |
