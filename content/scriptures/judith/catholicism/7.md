Catholic interpretation of Judith 7 reads the siege and water shortage as a test of faith that reveals the limitations of merely human resistance. The elders' decision to give God five days to act before surrendering represents a failure of theological understanding that Judith will correct. Catholic moral theology sees in this ultimatum a form of presumption, the sin of setting conditions on God's grace.

The suffering of the besieged city, with children fainting from thirst and people collapsing in the streets, provides the narrative's emotional intensity and illustrates the Catholic teaching on the purifying role of suffering. The crisis forces the community to confront the depth of its trust in God, a confrontation that is necessary before divine deliverance can come.
