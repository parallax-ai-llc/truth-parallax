Catholic exegesis of Judith 4 emphasizes the liturgical response of Israel to the threat. The combination of prayer, fasting, sackcloth, and ashes reflects the Catholic penitential tradition, which maintains these practices as expressions of communal repentance and supplication. The chapter appears in the Catholic Lectionary during penitential seasons, connecting Israel's response to the Church's ongoing practice of corporate prayer.

The involvement of the high priest Joakim in directing the national response reflects the Catholic understanding of ordained leadership guiding the community in prayer. The chapter demonstrates the Catholic principle that spiritual preparation, including liturgical worship and ascetic discipline, is the proper first response to crisis, preceding and grounding any practical action.
