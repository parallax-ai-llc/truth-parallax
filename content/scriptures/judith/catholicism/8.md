Catholic exegesis of Judith 8 reads Judith's rebuke of the elders as an exercise of the prophetic voice within the community of faith. Her argument that the elders have no right to 'put God to the test' (8:12) echoes Deuteronomy 6:16 and establishes the theological center of the book. Catholic moral theology finds in Judith's speech a model of the virtue of counsel, one of the gifts of the Holy Spirit.

Judith's character, a wealthy, beautiful, pious widow who has lived an ascetic life, reflects several Catholic ideals: the vocation of widowhood honored by Paul (1 Timothy 5:3-16), the integration of prayer and action, and the exercise of lay authority in service to the community. Catholic Marian typology reads Judith as a foreshadowing of Mary, who likewise exercised decisive faith at a critical moment in salvation history.
