Catholic interpretation of Judith 14 emphasizes Achior's conversion as the fruit of witnessing God's mighty act. His response of faith, culminating in circumcision and incorporation into Israel, illustrates the Catholic teaching that God's saving acts in history are meant to draw all people to faith. The conversion of the outsider through the witness of the community resonates with the Catholic understanding of evangelization.

The Assyrian army's panic upon discovering Holofernes' body demonstrates the Catholic teaching that the defeat of evil's champions leads to the collapse of evil's power structures. The chapter moves from the intimate scene of Achior's conversion to the large-scale military rout, illustrating how personal faith and communal salvation are interconnected in the divine plan.
