Catholic exegesis of Judith 15 reads the victory celebration as a liturgical event, a procession of thanksgiving that mirrors the Church's own liturgical celebrations of divine deliverance. The crowning of Judith with olive branches and the procession to Jerusalem connect to Catholic traditions of pilgrimage, procession, and the public celebration of holy days.

The involvement of the high priest and the elders from Jerusalem represents the integration of local and institutional dimensions of the Church's life. Catholic ecclesiology sees in this chapter a model of the proper relationship between heroic individual action and the community's corporate response of thanksgiving. The plunder of the Assyrian camp, with Judith receiving Holofernes' possessions, symbolizes the spoils of spiritual victory.
