Catholic interpretation of Judith 9 regards this prayer as one of the most significant prayers in the deuterocanonical literature. Judith's supplication draws on the theology of divine election, the power of the weak, and God's special concern for the vulnerable. The prayer's language resonates with Mary's Magnificat (Luke 1:46-55), strengthening the Catholic typological reading of Judith as a figure of the Blessed Virgin.

The prayer's theology of God using the weak to confound the strong (9:10) anticipates Paul's teaching in 1 Corinthians 1:27 and supports the Catholic understanding of God's preferential option for the poor and marginalized. Catholic liturgical use of Judith's prayer connects it to the Church's ongoing tradition of supplication, in which the faithful cry out to God in times of persecution and trial.
