Catholic exegesis of Judith 2 notes the ironic providence at work in Holofernes' campaign. The vast military apparatus being assembled will ultimately serve God's purposes by creating the crisis through which God demonstrates divine power. Catholic theology reads history as the arena of divine providence, where even the plans of the wicked are encompassed by God's larger design.

The chapter's detailed military descriptions establish Holofernes as a formidable antagonist, which heightens the miraculous nature of his eventual defeat. Catholic commentators connect this to the theology of the Magnificat: God 'has shown the strength of his arm, he has scattered the proud in the conceit of their heart' (Luke 1:51). The military preparations of this chapter are the pride that God will scatter through the hand of a faithful woman.
