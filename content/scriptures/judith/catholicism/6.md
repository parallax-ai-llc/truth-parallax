Catholic exegesis of Judith 6 reads Holofernes' rejection of Achior's warning as an illustration of the hardened heart that refuses divine truth. The powerful reject the witness that could save them, while the humble receive it. This pattern recurs throughout Scripture, from Pharaoh's rejection of Moses to the Sanhedrin's rejection of the apostles.

The people of Bethulia's welcome of Achior, offering him hospitality and hearing his testimony, reflects the Catholic teaching on the obligation to receive truth wherever it is found. Achior's trajectory from pagan observer to believing convert prefigures the universal call to faith that the Catholic Church proclaims, affirming that God's grace works beyond the visible boundaries of the covenant community.
