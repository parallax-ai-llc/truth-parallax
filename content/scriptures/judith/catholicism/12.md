Catholic exegesis of Judith 12 highlights the contrast between Judith's ascetic discipline and Holofernes' indulgence. Judith maintains her dietary observances and prayer routine even in the enemy camp, illustrating the Catholic teaching on the importance of spiritual disciplines maintained under all circumstances. Her nightly departures for prayer reflect the monastic practice of night prayer (Compline/Matins).

Holofernes' drunken excess at the banquet leads directly to his downfall, illustrating the Catholic moral teaching on temperance and the dangers of intemperance. The chapter's structure, building toward the climactic act in chapter 13, demonstrates the Catholic understanding that spiritual victory is prepared by sustained prayer and discipline. The banquet scene has been a favorite subject of Catholic art, from medieval manuscripts to Renaissance paintings.
