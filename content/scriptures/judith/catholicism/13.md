Catholic interpretation of Judith 13, the beheading of Holofernes, reads the act as the climactic expression of divine power working through human agency. Judith's prayer before striking, 'Give me strength today, O Lord God of Israel' (13:7), grounds the act in faith rather than personal courage. Catholic theology sees here the principle of cooperation with divine grace: God's power operates through Judith's willing action.

Catholic Marian typology reaches its fullest expression in this chapter. Judith crushing Holofernes' head is read as a prefiguration of Mary's role in the defeat of Satan, as prophesied in Genesis 3:15. The acclamation 'You are the glory of Jerusalem, you are the great pride of Israel, you are the great boast of our nation' (15:9) is applied to Mary in Catholic liturgy. The chapter has inspired some of the most powerful works of Catholic sacred art.
