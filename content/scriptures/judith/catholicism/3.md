Catholic interpretation of Judith 3 highlights the chapter's portrayal of idolatrous worship demanded by a human ruler. The destruction of sacred groves and the demand that Nebuchadnezzar be worshipped as the sole god reflects the perennial threat of idolatry, which the Catholic tradition condemns as a violation of the First Commandment. The nations' capitulation to this demand represents the failure of faith under pressure.

The Catholic reading draws a contrast between these nations, who surrender their worship to a false god, and Israel, which will maintain its worship of the true God. This contrast illustrates the Catholic teaching on the virtue of fortitude and the obligation to resist any human authority that demands what belongs to God alone, a principle that would become central in Catholic social teaching on the rights of conscience and religious liberty.
