Judith 15 describes Israel's victory over the demoralized Assyrian army and the celebration that follows. The women of Israel crown Judith with olive branches and lead a procession of thanksgiving. The high priest and elders of Jerusalem come to honor her. Protestant readers note the similarity to the celebrations after the crossing of the Red Sea (Exodus 15) and Deborah's victory (Judges 5).

The chapter emphasizes that the victory belongs to God, not to Judith personally, a theological point that Protestant interpreters strongly affirm. The communal celebration and thanksgiving, with the whole nation joining in praise, illustrates the proper response to divine deliverance. The chapter functions as the narrative bridge between the act of salvation and the hymn of praise that concludes the book.
