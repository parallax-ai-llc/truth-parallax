Judith 5 contains Achior the Ammonite's speech to Holofernes, in which he recounts Israel's history and warns that Israel's God protects them when they are faithful. The speech functions as a theological summary of Deuteronomistic history: when Israel obeys God, they are invincible; when they sin, they are vulnerable. Protestant readers recognize this theological framework from the canonical books of Judges and Kings.

Achior's role as a pagan who speaks truth to power anticipates the biblical theme of outsiders who recognize God's sovereignty, paralleling figures like Rahab (Joshua 2) and Naaman (2 Kings 5). Protestant interpreters appreciate this chapter's theological clarity, even while noting that the speech's function within the narrative is literary rather than historically documentary.
