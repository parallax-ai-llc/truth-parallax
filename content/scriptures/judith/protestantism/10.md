Judith 10 describes Judith's preparations to enter the Assyrian camp. She bathes, adorns herself with her finest clothing and jewelry, and packs provisions of ritually clean food. Protestant readers note the deliberate use of beauty as a weapon, which raises ethical questions that the narrative does not fully resolve.

The chapter's description of Judith's beauty stunning the Assyrian soldiers connects to the broader biblical theme of God using unexpected means for deliverance. The guards' amazement at her beauty parallels the narrative device used in Esther, another beautiful woman who enters a dangerous court to save her people. Protestant interpreters generally read Judith's deception as justified within the context of holy war, similar to Rahab's deception of the Jericho soldiers.
