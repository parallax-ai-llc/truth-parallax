Judith 9 contains Judith's prayer before she undertakes her mission. She appeals to God as the Lord of history who has power over all nations and who uses the weak to confound the strong. The prayer is a powerful expression of trust in divine sovereignty and is widely regarded, even by Protestant scholars, as one of the finest prayers in the deuterocanonical literature.

Protestant commentators note the prayer's theological sophistication: Judith does not ask for personal glory but for God's name to be vindicated before the nations. This aligns with the canonical prayers of Moses (Exodus 32:11-14), Daniel (Daniel 9), and Nehemiah (Nehemiah 1:5-11). While Protestants do not attribute canonical authority to this prayer, its theological content is fully consistent with the canonical tradition of faithful supplication.
