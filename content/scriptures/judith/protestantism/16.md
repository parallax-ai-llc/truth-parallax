Judith 16 contains Judith's hymn of praise, a victory song that celebrates God as the divine warrior who has delivered Israel through the hand of a woman. The hymn draws on the canonical tradition of victory songs, particularly the Song of Moses (Exodus 15), the Song of Deborah (Judges 5), and the Psalms of divine kingship. Protestant scholars recognize its literary and theological quality.

The chapter concludes with Judith's return to her estates, her refusal to remarry, and her death at the age of 105. Protestant interpreters note the idealized portrait of a woman who lived and died in faithfulness, wisdom, and independence. While the hymn and epilogue are not granted canonical authority, they are valued as expressions of a robust theology of divine deliverance that resonates with the canonical tradition of praise.
