Judith 13 describes the beheading of Holofernes. Alone with the unconscious general, Judith prays for strength, seizes his sword, and strikes twice to sever his head. She places the head in her food bag and returns to Bethulia with her maid. Protestant readers note the deliberate echoes of Jael's killing of Sisera (Judges 4-5) and David's beheading of Goliath (1 Samuel 17).

The chapter is the narrative climax and the theological center of the book: God delivers through the hand of a woman, confounding the wisdom and power of the world. Protestant interpreters, while not granting canonical status, acknowledge the power of this narrative as an illustration of 1 Corinthians 1:27, where God chooses the weak things of the world to shame the strong. Judith's prayer before the act emphasizes that the power for deliverance comes from God alone.
