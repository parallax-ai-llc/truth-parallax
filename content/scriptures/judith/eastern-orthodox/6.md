Orthodox exegesis of Judith 6 notes the contrast between Holofernes' violent rejection of truth and Bethulia's hospitable reception of the truth-teller. The Orthodox tradition of philoxenia (love of strangers) finds illustration in the people's welcome of Achior, and his eventual conversion represents the Orthodox understanding that genuine encounter with God's people can lead to faith.

The chapter's dramatic structure, with Achior cast out by one community and received by another, mirrors the Orthodox theology of baptism as a movement from the kingdom of darkness into the community of light. Orthodox commentary reads Achior's trajectory as a catechumenal journey, from hearing the truth to witnessing God's action to full incorporation through the sacramental rite.
