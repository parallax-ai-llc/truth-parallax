Orthodox exegesis of Judith 10 reads her preparation and departure as a spiritual warrior going forth to battle. The Orthodox tradition of blessing soldiers before battle finds a precedent in Judith's departure from the city, accompanied by the prayers of the community. Her beauty, adorned for the mission, is understood as a charism, a gift of the Holy Spirit employed for divine purposes.

The chapter's imagery of Judith leaving the safety of the walls and descending into the enemy camp resonates with the Orthodox theology of kenosis (self-emptying), in which divine power is revealed through voluntary descent into danger and weakness. The guards' astonishment at her beauty prefigures the wonder that greets every manifestation of divine grace in the material world.
