Orthodox exegesis of Judith 14 reads Achior's conversion and circumcision as a baptismal type: the outsider who has witnessed God's saving act is incorporated into the community of faith through a sacramental rite. The Orthodox understanding of baptism as death and resurrection, dying to the old life and rising to the new, finds a narrative parallel in Achior's passage from pagan observer to member of Israel.

The collapse of the Assyrian army upon discovering Holofernes' body illustrates the Orthodox teaching that the defeat of evil's champion leads to the disintegration of evil's power. Orthodox hymnography for Pascha celebrates precisely this pattern: Christ's defeat of death leads to the liberation of all who were held captive. The chapter thus participates in the Paschal typology that runs throughout the Orthodox reading of the Old Testament.
