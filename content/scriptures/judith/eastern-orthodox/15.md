Orthodox interpretation of Judith 15 reads the victory procession as a liturgical event that prefigures the Church's own celebrations of divine deliverance. The crowning of Judith, the processional singing, and the communal thanksgiving mirror the structure of Orthodox festal liturgies, particularly the Paschal procession and the Great Doxology.

The chapter demonstrates the Orthodox conviction that divine deliverance must be met with communal worship and thanksgiving. The involvement of the high priest and the entire nation in the celebration reflects the Orthodox ecclesiological principle that the whole Church celebrates together. Private piety, however heroic, finds its completion in the corporate worship of the gathered community.
