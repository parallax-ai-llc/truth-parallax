Orthodox exegesis of Judith 2 reads the military preparations as unknowing instruments of divine providence. Holofernes' campaign, intended to subjugate the world, will instead provide the occasion for God's glory to be revealed. Orthodox theology understands all of history as the arena of divine oikonomia, in which God's purposes are accomplished even through the actions of those who oppose him.

The chapter's emphasis on the vastness of the Assyrian army creates a deliberate contrast with the smallness of Israel and the apparent weakness of the instrument God will use. This pattern of the weak overcoming the strong through divine power is central to Orthodox theology, reflected in the hymnography of the feasts of martyrs and in the Orthodox conviction that the Church's survival against all odds is itself a continuous miracle.
