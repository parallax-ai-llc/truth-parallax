Orthodox exegesis of Judith 4 highlights the liturgical character of Israel's response to the threat. The combination of prayer, fasting, sackcloth, and ashes mirrors the Orthodox Church's penitential practices, particularly during Great Lent. The chapter is read within the Orthodox tradition as demonstrating the primacy of worship and repentance over military strategy.

The involvement of the entire community, including women, children, and even animals in sackcloth, reflects the Orthodox understanding of corporate repentance that encompasses all of creation. The Orthodox Church's practice of community-wide fasting and prayer in times of crisis finds scriptural precedent in this chapter, affirming the belief that spiritual preparation is the necessary foundation for divine deliverance.
