Orthodox interpretation of Judith 13 reads the beheading of Holofernes as the defining act of divine deliverance in the book. Judith's prayer before striking the blow, her reliance on God's strength rather than her own, and the decisive nature of the act illustrate the Orthodox understanding of synergy between divine grace and human cooperation. God acts, but God acts through willing human instruments.

Orthodox Marian typology sees in Judith's crushing of Holofernes' head a prefiguration of the Theotokos's role in the defeat of the spiritual enemy. Just as Judith entered the enemy camp alone and returned with the trophy of victory, Mary through her fiat entered the mystery of the Incarnation and brought forth the Victor over death. Orthodox iconography sometimes depicts Judith alongside Mary and other Old Testament women who prefigure the Mother of God.
