Orthodox interpretation of Judith 5 reads Achior's speech as a providential testimony to God's faithfulness delivered by an unexpected witness. The Orthodox tradition recognizes that truth can be spoken by those outside the visible Church, a principle reflected in the patristic concept of the logos spermatikos, the seeds of the Word scattered throughout creation.

Achior's recounting of salvation history parallels the Orthodox liturgical practice of reciting salvation history in the context of worship, particularly during the Great Blessing of Water at Theophany and the readings of the Easter Vigil. The Deuteronomistic theology of his speech, linking faithfulness to divine protection, is affirmed in Orthodox moral theology while being understood within the larger context of God's mysterious purposes in allowing suffering.
