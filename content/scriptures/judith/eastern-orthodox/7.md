Orthodox interpretation of Judith 7 reads the siege of Bethulia as a trial of faith that mirrors the spiritual combat described by the Desert Fathers. The physical thirst of the besieged city becomes a metaphor for the spiritual thirst for God that intensifies in times of trial. The Orthodox ascetic tradition teaches that periods of deprivation can become occasions for deeper dependence on God.

The elders' decision to give God a five-day deadline represents a failure of faith that Orthodox theology identifies as presumption. The Orthodox Fathers taught that the believer must submit to God's timing without setting conditions, a principle embodied in the Prayer of Optina: 'Grant me the strength to bear the fatigue of the coming day with all that it shall bring.' Judith's correction of this error in the next chapter restores the proper posture of unconditional trust.
