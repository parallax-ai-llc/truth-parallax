Orthodox exegesis of Judith 12 emphasizes the ascetic dimension of Judith's behavior in the enemy camp. Her maintenance of dietary purity, prayer, and spiritual discipline while surrounded by paganism reflects the Orthodox monastic ideal of maintaining the spiritual life even in the most hostile environments. The Desert Fathers' teaching on spiritual vigilance finds a narrative illustration in Judith's unwavering discipline.

The contrast between Judith's fasting and Holofernes' feasting is read as an illustration of the Orthodox teaching that victory in spiritual warfare requires ascetic preparation. The chapter builds toward the climactic act with the rhythm of prayer and discipline, demonstrating that Judith's strength comes not from herself but from the sustained practice of communion with God through prayer.
