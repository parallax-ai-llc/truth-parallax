Orthodox interpretation of Judith 1 reads the chapter as the opening of an inspired narrative about the conflict between worldly power and divine authority. Nebuchadnezzar's imperial ambitions represent the archetypal pattern of human pride that sets itself against God, a pattern that the Orthodox Fathers traced from the Tower of Babel through the Roman persecutions to every era of the Church's history.

The chapter establishes the overwhelming nature of the earthly threat, which serves the theological purpose of magnifying the divine deliverance to come. Orthodox homiletics has long used this pattern of impossible odds overcome by faith as the paradigm for understanding the Church's survival and triumph through centuries of persecution, from the Roman arena to the Ottoman occupation.
