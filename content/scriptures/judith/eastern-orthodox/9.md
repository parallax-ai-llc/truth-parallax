Orthodox interpretation of Judith 9 regards the prayer as a model of hesychastic supplication. Judith's prostration, fasting, and crying out to God reflect the Orthodox prayer tradition in which the whole body participates in worship. The prayer's theology, acknowledging God as the Lord of history who uses the humble to defeat the proud, resonates with the Orthodox hymns sung during the feasts of the Theotokos.

The parallels between Judith's prayer and the Magnificat of the Theotokos are particularly noted in Orthodox commentary. Both women acknowledge God's power to scatter the proud and exalt the lowly. Orthodox liturgical tradition has woven these connections into the hymnography of Marian feasts, where Judith is invoked as a type of the Theotokos who, through faith and prayer, became the instrument of cosmic deliverance.
