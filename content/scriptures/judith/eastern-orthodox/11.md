Orthodox interpretation of Judith 11 reads the speech as an exercise of divine wisdom operating through human words. The double meanings in Judith's address to Holofernes illustrate the Orthodox understanding that God's truth can be present even in situations of apparent deception. The Fathers distinguished between lies told for selfish gain and stratagems employed in spiritual warfare against the enemies of God.

The chapter's dramatic irony, in which Holofernes hears flattery while the reader perceives divine judgment, reflects the Orthodox theological principle that the proud are blinded by their own arrogance. Holofernes' inability to perceive Judith's true intent is itself a manifestation of divine justice, as God gives the proud over to the delusions of their own hearts (cf. Romans 1:28).
