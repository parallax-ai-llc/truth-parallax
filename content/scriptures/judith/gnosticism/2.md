Gnostic interpretation of Judith 2 would read Holofernes as an archon, a lieutenant of the Demiurge sent to enforce the material world's tyrannical order. The vast military apparatus represents the overwhelming power of the material cosmos arrayed against the isolated community of pneumatics. The archons command seemingly irresistible force, yet they are fatally ignorant of the transcendent power that will defeat them.

The chapter's emphasis on material power, armies, chariots, and weapons, underscores the Gnostic teaching that the archons' strength is entirely worldly and therefore ultimately impotent against spiritual knowledge. The Gnostic reader knows that this mighty army will be undone not by a greater material force but by wisdom and cunning, the weapons of gnosis against the blunt instruments of material power.
