Gnostic interpretation of Judith 12 would read the banquet as an illustration of archonic excess. The material world's rulers indulge in sensory pleasure, drowning their already limited consciousness in wine and food. Holofernes' drunkenness represents the deepening stupefaction of the archon, sinking further into material ignorance precisely when the divine agent is preparing to strike.

Judith's maintenance of spiritual discipline amid the banquet illustrates the Gnostic pneumatic's ability to exist within the material world without being consumed by it. Her ritual purity and nightly prayer preserve the connection to the transcendent that material indulgence would sever. The contrast between the two figures at the banquet, one sinking into matter and the other maintaining spiritual awareness, embodies the fundamental Gnostic distinction between the material and the spiritual.
