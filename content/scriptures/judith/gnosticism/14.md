A Gnostic reading of Judith 14 would interpret Achior's conversion as the awakening of a psychic soul to gnosis through witnessing the divine act. The collapse of the Assyrian army, triggered by the discovery of their headless leader, represents the disintegration of archonic power once its authority is exposed as hollow. The archons are powerful only so long as their subjects remain in ignorance; once the illusion is shattered, their power dissolves.

The chapter illustrates the Gnostic teaching that the liberation of a few can trigger the liberation of many. Judith's single act of gnosis-empowered courage breaks the archonic hold over the entire region. The Gnostic reader would see in this pattern the hope that the spread of saving knowledge can ultimately dissolve the structures of material oppression that keep humanity enslaved.
