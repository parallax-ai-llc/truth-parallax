Gnostic reading of Judith 13 would interpret the beheading of Holofernes as the decisive defeat of the archonic power. The severing of the head, the seat of consciousness and authority, symbolizes the destruction of archonic rule over the pneumatic community. Sophia's wisdom, operating through courage and divine empowerment, accomplishes what no material army could: the overthrow of the tyrant from within his own stronghold.

The carrying of the head back to Bethulia represents the return of the divine agent from the material realm bearing the proof of the archon's defeat. In Gnostic soteriology, the defeat of the archons is not accomplished through force but through knowledge and cunning. Judith did not match Holofernes in military power; she understood his weakness and exploited it. This is the victory of gnosis over ignorance, wisdom over brute force, spirit over matter.
