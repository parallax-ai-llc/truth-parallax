Gnostic reading of Judith 6 would interpret Holofernes' rejection of Achior's truth as the archon's characteristic inability to understand the transcendent. The archons, in Gnostic theology, are powerful but stupid: they can command material forces but cannot comprehend the spiritual realities that transcend their domain. Holofernes' angry dismissal of Achior's warning is the rage of ignorance confronting truth it cannot process.

Achior's reception by the pneumatic community of Bethulia represents the movement from the archonic realm to the community of knowledge. His eventual conversion (14:10) completes this trajectory. The Gnostic reader would see in Achior a figure undergoing the process of awakening, moving from partial knowledge to full incorporation into the community that possesses gnosis.
