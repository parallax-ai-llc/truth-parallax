A Gnostic reading of Judith 9 would interpret the prayer as Sophia's appeal to the transcendent God beyond the Demiurge, the true divinity who alone can empower the defeat of archonic forces. The prayer's insistence that God is the 'Lord of the lowly' and the opponent of the proud expresses the Gnostic reversal in which the material world's powerful are spiritually impotent and the materially weak possess the true divine power.

The prayer's request for the power of deception against the enemy aligns with the Gnostic motif of the divine emissary who operates by stealth within the material world. The Gnostic revealer must deceive the archons precisely because the archons' world is built on deception. To overcome falsehood, the divine agent must work within the system of appearances while carrying the hidden truth of the Pleroma.
