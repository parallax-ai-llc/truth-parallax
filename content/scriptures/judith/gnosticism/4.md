Gnostic interpretation of Judith 4 would read Israel's prayer and fasting as the pneumatic community's spiritual preparation for combat with archonic forces. In Gnostic soteriology, the weapons against the archons are not material but spiritual: prayer, fasting, and above all knowledge. The community's turn to spiritual disciplines represents the awakening consciousness that precedes the decisive act of liberation.

The fortification of mountain passes, in a Gnostic reading, would symbolize the sealing of the spiritual boundaries that protect the pneumatic community from archonic intrusion. The Gnostic texts describe rituals and passwords that protect the soul from the archons during its ascent through the cosmic spheres, and Israel's practical preparations would be read as the narrative equivalent of these spiritual protections.
