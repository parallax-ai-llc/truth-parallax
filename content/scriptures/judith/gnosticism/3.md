A Gnostic reading of Judith 3 would interpret the nations' worship of Nebuchadnezzar as the capitulation of psychic and hylic souls to the Demiurge's false claim of divinity. These nations represent humanity asleep in matter, accepting the material world's authority without question. Their surrender of their own sacred sites to worship the archonic ruler illustrates the condition of souls who have forgotten their true origin in the Pleroma.

The contrast with Israel, which refuses to submit, represents the pneumatic community that retains the memory of the true God beyond the material cosmos. The Gnostic reader would see in this chapter the fundamental division of humanity that Valentinian theology describes: those who are content with the material world's false gods and those who, through the spark of divine light within them, recognize these gods as fraudulent imposters.
