A Gnostic reading of Judith 5 would find in Achior's speech a rare moment of truth spoken within the archonic camp. Achior, though an outsider, possesses a partial knowledge of the true God's relationship with the chosen people. In Gnostic categories, Achior would be a psychic soul, positioned between the pneumatic and the hylic, capable of recognizing truth but not yet fully illuminated.

His recounting of Israel's history, showing that divine protection follows faithfulness, reveals a pattern that the archons cannot understand. Holofernes' rejection of this truth in the next chapter demonstrates the archonic inability to comprehend the workings of the transcendent God. The Gnostic reader would see in this exchange the fundamental drama of gnosis: truth is spoken, but those in power are constitutionally incapable of receiving it.
