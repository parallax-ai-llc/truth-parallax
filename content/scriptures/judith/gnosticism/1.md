A Gnostic reading of Judith 1 would identify Nebuchadnezzar with the Demiurge or the chief archon, a tyrannical cosmic ruler who demands worship and submission from all beings within his domain. His claim to divine status and his rage against those who refuse allegiance mirror the Demiurge's declaration in Gnostic texts: 'I am God and there is no other beside me,' spoken in blasphemous ignorance of the true transcendent God.

The military campaign described in this chapter would represent the archonic forces marshaling their power to suppress the divine sparks who refuse to submit to the material world's false authority. The Gnostic reader would see in Israel's forthcoming resistance not merely a national conflict but a cosmic struggle between the forces of ignorance and the pneumatic community that preserves the hidden knowledge of the true God.
