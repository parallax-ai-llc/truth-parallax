Gnostic interpretation of Judith 15 would read the victory celebration as a moment when the pneumatic community experiences a foretaste of the freedom that awaits in the Pleroma. The archonic army has been defeated, the tyrant destroyed, and the community liberated from the threat of material destruction. This temporary earthly liberation mirrors the eternal liberation that gnosis promises.

The crowning of Judith with olive branches and the procession of praise reflect the Gnostic understanding that the divine agent deserves honor for bringing saving knowledge to the community. The communal celebration represents the gathering of pneumatics who have been preserved through the crisis, their community intact and their knowledge confirmed by the defeat of the archonic power.
