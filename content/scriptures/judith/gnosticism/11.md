A Gnostic reading of Judith 11 would interpret the speech as the Gnostic revealer's use of the archons' own language against them. Judith speaks words that Holofernes interprets as flattery and submission but that carry a hidden meaning accessible only to those with knowledge. This double register of meaning is characteristic of Gnostic texts themselves, which embed esoteric teaching within exoteric narratives.

Holofernes' inability to perceive the true meaning of Judith's words illustrates the archonic blindness that pervades the material world. The archons are perpetually deceived because they lack the capacity for higher understanding. The Gnostic reader, like Judith, operates on a level of awareness that transcends the archonic comprehension. The chapter thus models the Gnostic practice of communicating truth through veiled language.
