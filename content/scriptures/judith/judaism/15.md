Jewish reading of Judith 15 notes the women's leading role in the victory celebration, a motif that connects to Miriam's song at the Sea (Exodus 15:20-21) and the women who greeted David after his victory over Goliath (1 Samuel 18:6-7). The prominence of women in celebrations of military victory reflects an established Israelite tradition that the book of Judith consciously invokes.

The chapter's description of plunder and thanksgiving follows the biblical pattern of holy war as described in Deuteronomy, where victory is attributed to God and celebrated with communal worship. Jewish scholars note that while the historical setting is fictional, the cultural practices described, including the procession to Jerusalem and the presentation of offerings at the Temple, reflect genuine Second Temple practices.
