Jewish scholarly reading of Judith 2 examines the narrative's literary construction of an overwhelming military threat. The detailed catalog of Holofernes' forces serves the story's theological purpose of establishing the impossibility of human resistance, which makes God's deliverance through Judith all the more dramatic. This narrative strategy parallels the biblical pattern found in the Exodus narrative and the book of Judges.

Historians note that the military details, while not historically precise, reflect genuine knowledge of ancient Near Eastern warfare. The chapter provides evidence for Second Temple Jewish literary conventions and the ways in which Jewish authors adapted historical narrative for theological purposes. The combination of realistic military detail with deliberate historical anachronism is characteristic of the genre.
