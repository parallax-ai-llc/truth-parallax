Islamic reading of Judith 9 would affirm the chapter's portrayal of sincere supplication (du'a) as the believer's most powerful weapon. The Quran teaches that God is near and responds to the prayer of the caller (Quran 2:186), and Judith's passionate prayer before her mission illustrates this principle. The prayer's acknowledgment that God is the sole source of strength and victory is fully consistent with Islamic theology.

Judith's prayer for deception to succeed against the enemy would be evaluated within the Islamic ethics of war, which permits stratagems (hila) in battle. The Prophet Muhammad stated that 'war is deception' (Bukhari), indicating that military stratagems are permissible when facing an unjust aggressor. Judith's prayer would thus be understood as asking God to bless a legitimate military stratagem against an enemy who has demanded idolatrous worship.
