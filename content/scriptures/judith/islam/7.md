Islamic interpretation of Judith 7 would read the siege through the lens of sabr (patient endurance), one of the most emphasized virtues in the Quran. The physical suffering of the besieged community and the temptation to surrender their faith for survival mirrors the trials faced by the early Muslim community during the siege of Medina (Battle of the Trench, Quran 33:10-13).

The elders' ultimatum to God, giving five days before surrender, would be regarded critically in Islamic theology as a failure to trust in divine timing. The Quran teaches that God's help comes at the appointed time: 'Indeed, the help of Allah is near' (Quran 2:214). Setting conditions on God's intervention represents a failure of tawakkul that the narrative will correct through Judith's faithful response.
