Islamic engagement with Judith 5 would read Achior's speech as a recognition of the pattern that runs throughout Quranic narrative: God protects those who are faithful and withdraws protection from those who disobey. This principle is expressed repeatedly in the Quran through the stories of past nations (umam) who were destroyed when they rejected their prophets and protected when they obeyed.

Muslim scholars would affirm the theological content of Achior's speech while noting that it represents a simplified version of the more nuanced Quranic theology of divine testing, in which God sometimes allows the faithful to suffer for purposes beyond immediate human understanding. The outsider's recognition of divine truth resonates with the Quranic concept of fitra, the innate human capacity to recognize God's sovereignty.
