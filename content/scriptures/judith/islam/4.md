Islamic reading of Judith 4 finds strong resonance in Israel's response of prayer and fasting. The combination of du'a (supplication) and sawm (fasting) as a communal response to crisis mirrors Islamic practice, particularly during times of calamity. The Prophet Muhammad instructed his followers to turn to prayer and fasting in times of distress, and the chapter illustrates this same spiritual instinct.

The balance between prayer and practical preparation (fortifying the mountain passes) reflects the Islamic principle expressed in the hadith: 'Trust in Allah, but tie your camel.' Muslim readers would recognize in Israel's response a model of tawakkul (reliance on God) that does not exclude prudent human action. The chapter demonstrates the universal prophetic teaching that spiritual preparation is the foundation of all effective resistance.
