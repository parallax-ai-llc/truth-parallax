Islamic engagement with Judith 14 would note Achior's conversion as an illustration of the principle that witnessing God's power should lead to faith. The Quran describes numerous instances where divine signs prompt recognition of God's sovereignty, from the conversion of Pharaoh's magicians (Quran 7:120-122) to the submission of the jinn who heard the Quran (Quran 72:1-2).

The collapse of the Assyrian army upon discovering their leader's death illustrates the Quranic teaching that disbelievers are ultimately without true strength: 'Their might among themselves is severe, but you think of them as united. Their hearts are diverse' (Quran 59:14). Muslim readers would see in the Assyrian rout a confirmation that armies built on arrogance and false worship lack the spiritual foundation to withstand divine intervention.
