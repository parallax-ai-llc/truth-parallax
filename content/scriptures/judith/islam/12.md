Islamic engagement with Judith 12 appreciates the chapter's portrayal of maintaining dietary observance in a hostile environment. Judith's refusal to eat from Holofernes' table and her insistence on consuming only her own provisions parallels the Islamic obligation to eat only halal food and to maintain religious discipline regardless of circumstances. The Prophet Muhammad's emphasis on the importance of halal sustenance resonates with Judith's practice.

Holofernes' drunkenness leading to his downfall illustrates the Islamic prohibition against alcohol (khamr). The Quran identifies intoxicants as the work of Satan (Quran 5:90-91), and the narrative consequences in Judith support this teaching. Muslim readers would see in the contrast between Judith's sobriety and Holofernes' drunkenness a confirmation of the Islamic teaching that temperance is a source of strength and indulgence a path to ruin.
