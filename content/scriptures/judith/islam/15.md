Islamic reading of Judith 15 emphasizes the communal thanksgiving that follows divine deliverance. The Quran repeatedly instructs believers to be grateful for God's blessings and to recognize divine assistance when it comes: 'If you are grateful, I will surely increase you in favor' (Quran 14:7). The victory celebration in this chapter models the appropriate response to divine aid.

The chapter's description of communal joy and praise resonates with Islamic practices of communal celebration after military victories, including the celebration of Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha. The gathering of the entire community to give thanks reflects the Islamic understanding that gratitude (shukr) is both an individual and communal obligation. Muslim readers would affirm the chapter's theology of victory as God's gift, not human achievement.
