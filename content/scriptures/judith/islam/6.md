Islamic reading of Judith 6 would interpret Holofernes' rejection of Achior's warning as an example of istikbar (arrogant rejection of truth), a concept the Quran applies to Pharaoh, Abu Lahab, and other opponents of divine guidance. The Quran warns that those who reject truth out of pride seal their own doom: 'In their hearts is a disease, and Allah has increased their disease' (Quran 2:10).

The hospitality shown to Achior by the people of Bethulia reflects the Islamic value of welcoming the stranger and honoring the truth-teller. Muslim readers would see in Achior's trajectory a movement from honest recognition to full commitment, a pattern that parallels the conversion narratives in the Sira (prophetic biography) of companions who came to Islam through witnessing God's signs.
