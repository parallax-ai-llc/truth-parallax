Islamic reading of Judith 13 would interpret the beheading of Holofernes as an act of divine justice carried out through a faithful human agent. The Quran teaches that God aids the believers in battle and strikes terror into the hearts of the disbelievers (Quran 8:12). Judith's prayer before striking the blow, attributing all strength to God, is fully consistent with the Islamic teaching that victory comes from God alone.

The theological significance of a single woman of faith defeating the mightiest general in the world illustrates the Quranic principle that God's power transcends all human calculation. Muslim readers would connect this to the Battle of Badr, where a small, poorly equipped Muslim force defeated a much larger army through divine assistance. The chapter affirms the universal prophetic teaching that God fights for those who maintain faith and worship.
