Islamic reading of Judith 11 would interpret her speech to Holofernes as a legitimate stratagem of war (khud'ah). The Prophet Muhammad's statement that 'war is deception' provides the ethical framework for understanding Judith's carefully ambiguous words. In Islamic military jurisprudence, stratagems that do not violate specific prohibitions (such as harming civilians or breaking treaties) are permissible.

Muslim scholars would note that Judith's words, while deceptive to Holofernes, contain a deeper truth: God will indeed deliver the enemies of Israel, though not in the way Holofernes imagines. This layered meaning reflects the Islamic understanding that God's plans operate on levels that human beings cannot fully perceive. The chapter illustrates the principle that God can use even human deception as an instrument of divine justice.
