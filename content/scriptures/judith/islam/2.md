Islamic reading of Judith 2 would interpret the vast military preparations as an illustration of worldly power that is ultimately subject to divine will. The Quran teaches that no army, however large, can prevail if God decrees otherwise: 'How many a small company has overcome a large company by permission of Allah' (Quran 2:249). Holofernes' confidence in his military superiority mirrors the overconfidence of enemies of God throughout Quranic narrative.

Muslim scholars would note that the outcome of the campaign, defeat at the hands of a single woman of faith, demonstrates the Quranic principle that God's ways confound human calculation. The chapter serves as preparation for a narrative that affirms God's absolute sovereignty over all earthly affairs, a principle central to Islamic theology.
