## Signs of Cosmic Imprisonment

Ezekiel 4 describes the prophet bound on his sides for extended periods, eating rationed and unclean food. Gnostic interpreters read these sign-acts not as symbols of divine judgment but as revelations of the human condition itself: every soul is bound, rationed, and fed impure sustenance in the material world.

The 390 and 40 days of immobility were interpreted allegorically as representing the eons of cosmic imprisonment — the vast stretches of time during which divine sparks remain trapped in material bodies, unable to ascend. The binding of the prophet mirrors the binding of the soul: both are immobilized by forces beyond their control.

The unclean bread baked over dung symbolized, for Gnostic readers, the nature of material sustenance itself. All physical nourishment is, in Gnostic perspective, inherently impure — derived from the corrupt material world created by the Demiurge. The prophet's revulsion at this food (4:14) represents the pneumatic soul's instinctive disgust at material existence, the inner recognition that this world's offerings are fundamentally inadequate for beings whose true home is the Pleroma.
