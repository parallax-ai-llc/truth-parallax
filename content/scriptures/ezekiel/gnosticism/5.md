## The Scattered Sparks

Ezekiel 5 records the division of the prophet's shorn hair into three portions — burned, struck, scattered — with a small remnant preserved. Gnostic cosmology read the threefold division as representing the three categories of humanity in Valentinian thought: the *hylics* (material, destroyed), the *psychics* (soulish, struck but potentially salvageable), and the *pneumatics* (spiritual, scattered through the cosmos but carrying the divine spark).

The small remnant bound in the prophet's garment represents the pneumatic elect — those who carry within them the seed of divine light from the Pleroma. Even among this remnant, some are cast into the fire (5:4), representing the pneumatic souls who, despite their divine origin, fail to awaken to *gnosis* and are lost.

The theological rationale given in the chapter — that Jerusalem sinned worse than the nations — was reinterpreted by Gnostic readers as illustrating the paradox of proximity to the Demiurge. Those closest to the false god's institutions are most deeply enmeshed in cosmic deception. Distance from religious institutions, in this reading, can actually be advantageous for spiritual awakening.
