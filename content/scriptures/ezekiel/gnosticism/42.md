## The Chambers of Separation

Ezekiel 42's priestly chambers and the enclosure wall separating holy from common represent, in Gnostic reading, the archonic principle of division. The Demiurge governs through separation: sacred from profane, clean from unclean, priest from layperson. These distinctions, which the archonic system presents as divinely ordained, are understood by the Gnostic as instruments of control.

The priestly changing of garments -- sacred vestments inside, ordinary clothes outside -- illustrates the archonic system's demand for conformity to its categories. The priest must literally put on the archon's identity to approach the divine presence, then remove it to function in the ordinary world. The Gnostic sees this as a performance of submission to the cosmic order.

The perfect square of the temple enclosure communicates the Demiurge's preference for geometric order over organic vitality. The wall separating holy from common is the fundamental archonic boundary -- the division that gnosis transcends by revealing that all reality originates in the same divine Fullness. There is no profane space in the Pleroma.
