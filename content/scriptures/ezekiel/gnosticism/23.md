## Sisters in Bondage

Ezekiel 23's allegory of Oholah and Oholibah — two sisters whose political alliances constitute spiritual adultery — was reinterpreted by Gnostic readers as illustrating the entanglements of the soul with competing archonic powers. The sisters do not choose between God and idols (all the options are archonic) but between different forms of cosmic bondage.

The increasingly graphic descriptions of the sisters' alliances were read as depicting the soul's progressive enmeshment in materiality. Each alliance draws the soul deeper into the web of archonic control. The political powers the sisters pursue — Assyria, Babylon, Egypt — represent different archonic domains, each offering a different form of seduction but all ultimately serving the same purpose: the continued imprisonment of the divine spark.

The judgment that falls through the very nations the sisters pursued illustrates the Gnostic principle that the material world is a self-consuming system. The archons compete with each other, and those who ally with one are eventually destroyed by another. The only escape is not to play the game at all — to withdraw into the interior sanctuary of *gnosis*, beyond the reach of any archonic power.
