## The Valley of Bones and Pneumatic Awakening

Ezekiel 37's vision of dry bones revivified by divine breath is read by Gnostic interpreters on two levels. On the surface, the Demiurge demonstrates his power to reanimate dead matter -- an impressive but ultimately limited act, since material resurrection merely returns the soul to its prison. True resurrection, in Gnostic understanding, is the awakening of the divine spark from its sleep in matter.

The breath (ruach) that animates the bones is identified by some Gnostic interpreters with the pneumatic element that the Demiurge unknowingly breathed into Adam at creation -- the stolen divine light from Sophia. The vision thus inadvertently reveals the Demiurge's dependence on a spiritual power he does not fully understand or control.

The joining of two sticks -- Judah and Israel becoming one -- is read as a symbol of the reunification of the fragmented divine light scattered throughout the material cosmos. The Gnostic myth of the fall of Sophia describes divine light shattered and dispersed; its gathering and reunification is the ultimate purpose of the pneumatic mission in the world.
