## Overview

Ezekiel was one of the most important prophetic texts for Gnostic movements. The elaborate chariot-throne vision of chapter 1, the imagery of God's glory departing a corrupt temple, and the visionary ascent motifs provided fertile ground for Gnostic cosmological and soteriological speculation. Gnostic interpreters read Ezekiel's visions not as revelations of the one true God, but as encounters with the Demiurge or his archons — or alternatively as glimpses of the Pleroma beyond the Demiurge's dominion.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### The Chariot-Throne Vision (Chapter 1)

The throne vision was central to Gnostic heavenly-ascent traditions. Sethian and Valentinian texts reinterpreted the four living creatures, the wheels within wheels, and the figure on the throne:

- **Sethian reading**: The living creatures are archons guarding the boundaries of the Demiurge's realm; the true God dwells far above
- **Valentinian reading**: The vision reveals the structure of the Pleroma, with the four creatures corresponding to the four luminaries
- **Mandaean reading**: The throne vision describes the realm of darkness that the soul must traverse in its ascent to the world of light

### The Glory Departing the Temple (Chapters 8-11)

The departure of God's glory from the corrupt temple was read by Gnostics as a cosmic parable: the divine spark withdrawing from the material world corrupted by the Demiurge, leaving it to its fate.

### The Dry Bones Vision (Chapter 37)

Gnostic interpreters read the resurrection of dry bones not as a physical restoration of Israel, but as the spiritual awakening of pneumatic souls who have forgotten their divine origin.

## Theological Significance

- **Subversive Reading**: Gnostic exegesis inverts the prophetic message — what Ezekiel presents as the glory of the true God, Gnostics read as the limited power of the Demiurge
- **Heavenly Ascent**: Ezekiel's visionary experience becomes a prototype for the Gnostic soul's ascent through archonic realms to the Pleroma
- **Temple Critique**: The departure of glory validates the Gnostic rejection of material worship and institutional religion
- **Knowledge over Obedience**: Ezekiel's call to repentance is reframed as a call to awakening and *gnosis*

## Major Gnostic Schools Engaging Ezekiel

| Mandaean | Visionary ascent traditions parallel and diverge from Ezekiel |
