## The Unraveling

Ezekiel 7 declares the end upon the land with the collapse of every earthly structure — economic, political, social. Gnostic cosmology saw in this chapter a description of the eventual dissolution of the Demiurge's realm. The material cosmos is inherently unstable because it was created by an ignorant being; its collapse is not tragedy but inevitability.

The declaration that silver and gold cannot save (7:19) confirmed the Gnostic conviction that material wealth is not merely insufficient but belongs to the very order that must be transcended. The Gnostic path does not involve accumulating material or even spiritual "merit" within the Demiurge's system, but rather recognizing the entire system as illusory.

The comprehensive social collapse described in the chapter — the king mourning, the prince in despair, the people's hands going slack — illustrates the Gnostic teaching that the structures of the material world, however imposing, are fundamentally fragile. When the Demiurge's power wanes, all that depends on it crumbles. The Gnostic response to this collapse is not despair but recognition: the dissolving of illusion reveals the reality that always lay beyond it.
