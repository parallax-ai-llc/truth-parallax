## The Temple of Deception

Ezekiel 8 reveals escalating abominations within the Jerusalem temple — idol worship, weeping for Tammuz, sun worship. Gnostic interpreters read this chapter as a revelation of the true nature of the Demiurge's temple: it was never a site of genuine divine worship but always a center of cosmic deception.

The progression from external to internal, from public to hidden corruption, mirrored the Gnostic understanding of how the Demiurge's control operates. The surface level presents a facade of holiness, but deeper investigation reveals layer upon layer of falsehood. The seventy elders worshiping in darkness (8:11-12) represent the religious establishment that maintains the Demiurge's authority while privately serving darker powers — the archons who govern the material world.

The weeping for Tammuz (8:14) was particularly significant for Gnostic readers familiar with Near Eastern dying-and-rising god mythologies. Some Gnostic systems incorporated these myths into their cosmology: the descent and ascent of divine figures through the archonic realms parallels the dying-and-rising pattern. The women's mourning could be read as an unconscious echo of Sophia's own grief over the fall of divine light into matter.
