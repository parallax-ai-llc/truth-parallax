## The Measured Land

Ezekiel 45's allocation of land for the sacred district and prescriptions for just measures represent the Demiurge's idealized governance. Gnostic interpreters note that even the Demiurge's vision of justice operates within a framework of measurement, allocation, and control -- the categories of material existence rather than spiritual freedom.

The demand for honest weights and measures (45:10-12) reveals an interesting tension: the Demiurge recognizes the possibility of injustice within his own system. Gnostic theology acknowledges that the Demiurge is not purely malicious but ignorant -- capable of seeking justice within his limited framework while remaining blind to the greater injustice of cosmic imprisonment itself.

The prince's obligation to provide offerings illustrates the archonic economy of exchange: resources flow upward through the hierarchy to sustain the sacrificial system that the Demiurge requires. The pneumatic recognizes that this entire economy is unnecessary -- the true God neither needs nor accepts material offerings.
