## The Mark of Gnosis

Ezekiel 9 describes the marking of the righteous and the execution of the unmarked. Gnostic interpreters reread the mark not as a sign of moral righteousness but as the seal of *gnosis* — the awakening to one's true divine identity that distinguishes the pneumatic from the hylic.

In Gnostic soteriology, the fundamental distinction is not between the morally good and the morally bad (categories that belong to the Demiurge's law) but between the awakened and the sleeping — those who have received *gnosis* and those who remain in ignorance. The mark on the foreheads of those who "sigh and groan" is reinterpreted: their grief is not over moral abominations but over the cosmic situation itself — the entrapment of divine sparks in a world of matter and ignorance.

The execution beginning at the sanctuary (9:6) confirmed the Gnostic conviction that institutional religion is the Demiurge's primary instrument of control. Those who serve within its structures are most deeply implicated in the system of cosmic deception and therefore face the most searching judgment when the system collapses.
