## The Archon's Throne

Ezekiel 1 records the prophet's vision of the divine chariot-throne — four living creatures, wheels within wheels, and a figure of glory above. Gnostic interpreters radically reread this vision as a revelation not of the supreme God but of the Demiurge — the ignorant creator deity who rules the material cosmos. The elaborate throne machinery reveals the structure of the archonic realm that the Gnostic soul must traverse in its ascent to the true Pleroma.

In Sethian texts, particularly the *Apocryphon of John*, the Demiurge Yaldabaoth creates archons and powers that govern the material world. The four living creatures of Ezekiel correspond to the chief archons, each ruling a domain of material existence. The wheels within wheels represent the celestial spheres — the concentric barriers separating the material cosmos from the Pleroma of light. The "eyes" covering the wheels signify the archons' surveillance of imprisoned souls, ensuring they remain trapped in ignorance.

The figure on the throne, which orthodox interpreters identify with God, is read by Gnostics as Yaldabaoth himself — the being who declares "I am God, and there is no other besides me" (a claim the Gnostics considered the ultimate expression of cosmic ignorance). The true God dwells far above this visible throne, beyond all description and perception. Ezekiel's vision, in Gnostic reading, is valuable precisely because it maps the architecture of captivity that the awakened soul must overcome.
