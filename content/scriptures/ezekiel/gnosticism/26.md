## Tyre and the Architecture of Captivity

Ezekiel 26 prophesies Tyre's destruction. Gnostic interpreters read the fall of Tyre not as righteous judgment but as one archonic power destroying another. The Demiurge's jealous rage against Tyre's wealth and independence reveals his possessive nature -- he cannot tolerate any power center that does not acknowledge his sovereignty.

The detailed destruction -- scraping to bare rock, casting into the sea -- illustrates the Demiurge's characteristic method: reducing complexity and beauty to emptiness. Gnostic cosmology understands the material world as a dim reflection of the Pleroma's fullness, and the Demiurge's destructive acts further diminish what little beauty exists in the material realm.

The descent of Tyre to the Pit represents the soul's further entrapment in the lower regions of the archonic cosmos. In the Pistis Sophia, the descent through successive layers of the underworld corresponds to increasing distance from the light. Tyre's fate maps the downward trajectory that the unredeemed soul follows when it lacks the saving gnosis.
