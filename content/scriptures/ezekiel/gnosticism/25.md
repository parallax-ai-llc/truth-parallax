## Judgment and the Archons

Ezekiel 25 pronounces oracles against Ammon, Moab, Edom, and Philistia. Gnostic interpreters read the judgment of nations through the lens of cosmic conflict between archonic powers. The four nations represent four archontic dominions that govern portions of the material realm, each exercising tyrannical authority over the souls trapped within their territories.

The Demiurge's pronouncement of judgment against these nations reveals the internal conflict within the archonic hierarchy. In Sethian and Valentinian systems, the archons frequently war among themselves, each claiming supreme authority. The judgment oracles expose this cosmic dysfunction -- the Demiurge punishing subordinate powers for acting according to their own nature.

The Gnostic reader finds in these oracles an inadvertent revelation of the material world's fundamental disorder. The God who judges nations for violence is the same God who employs violence as His instrument -- a contradiction that Gnostic theology resolves by identifying this deity as the ignorant Demiurge rather than the transcendent Father of Light.
