## Edom and the Cycle of Vengeance

Ezekiel 35's judgment on Mount Seir illustrates what Gnostic interpreters identify as the archonic cycle of violence. Edom is condemned for perpetual enmity and for seizing land that belongs to others -- but the Demiurge employs identical methods in His own governance. The judgment on Edom is the Demiurge projecting his own shadow onto a subordinate power.

The phrase "the LORD was there" (35:10) is read by Gnostic interpreters with deep irony. The Demiurge claims to be present everywhere, watching everything -- a claim that the Gnostic recognizes as a declaration of cosmic surveillance rather than loving presence. The all-seeing God of the Old Testament is, in Gnostic reading, the warden of the cosmic prison.

The destruction of Edom clears the way for the Demiurge's own territorial expansion, not for genuine liberation. The pneumatic reader understands that exchanging one archonic ruler for another changes nothing fundamental. Only the gnosis that reveals the true God beyond all archonic claims can break the cycle of possession, dispossession, and vengeance.
