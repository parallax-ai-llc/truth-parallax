## The Feast of Liberation

Ezekiel 39's depiction of Gog's defeat and the subsequent feast for birds and beasts is read by Gnostic interpreters as the dissolution of archonic power. The great sacrificial feast (39:17-20) inverts the normal sacrificial economy: instead of creatures being offered to the archonic God, the archonic forces themselves become the feast. The cosmic order is reversed.

The seven months of burial and seven years of burning weapons represent the prolonged process of purifying the cosmos from archonic contamination. In Valentinian eschatology, the final dissolution of the material world involves the progressive separation of spirit from matter, light from darkness -- a process that these temporal markers symbolize.

The promise that God will pour out His Spirit (39:29) is read by some Gnostic interpreters as the Demiurge's unwitting announcement of the Pleroma's own plan: the outpouring of genuine divine pneuma that will awaken all the scattered sparks and draw them home. The Demiurge speaks of "my Spirit" without realizing that the Spirit exceeds his authority and serves the transcendent God he does not know.
