## The Demiurge Revealed

Ezekiel 28 is among the most important chapters for Gnostic interpretation. The king of Tyre, described as having been "in Eden, the garden of God," adorned with precious stones, and as a guardian cherub on the holy mountain until iniquity was found in him -- this figure maps directly onto the Gnostic understanding of the Demiurge or chief archon.

The description of original perfection followed by a fall through pride precisely matches the Gnostic account of Yaldabaoth in the Apocryphon of John. The Demiurge was created with a measure of divine light stolen from Sophia, granting him power and beauty, but his ignorance of the true God above him led to the arrogant declaration of sole divinity. The "iniquity" found in him is not moral failing but ontological ignorance -- the refusal to acknowledge what is above.

The passage's declaration, "You were perfect in your ways from the day you were created, till iniquity was found in you" (28:15), confirms the Gnostic insistence that the Demiurge is a created being, not the ultimate God. His perfection was derivative and conditional; his fall was inevitable once he confused his delegated authority with ultimate sovereignty. For the Gnostic, this chapter is a key that unlocks the entire Old Testament.
