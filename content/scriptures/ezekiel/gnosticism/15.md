## The Vine and the Cosmos

Ezekiel 15 declares that vine wood is useless for any purpose except burning. Gnostic cosmology read this as a statement about the material world itself: created by the Demiurge from inferior materials, it serves no structural purpose in the divine economy. Its only destiny is dissolution — the consuming fire that will eventually release the divine sparks trapped within it.

The already-charred vine (15:4-5) represents a cosmos already in decline. Gnostic systems often described the material world as aging and weakening, its archonic rulers losing power as the divine sparks gradually awaken and escape. The vine that was worthless when whole is doubly worthless when partially burned — the Demiurge's creation, never adequate to begin with, deteriorates further with time.

The theological point that election without fruitfulness is meaningless was accepted by Gnostic readers but redirected: the "fruit" that matters is not moral obedience to the Demiurge's law but *gnosis* — the awakening that enables the divine spark to recognize itself and begin its ascent. Without this fruit, all religious activity within the material world is as useless as vine wood for construction.
