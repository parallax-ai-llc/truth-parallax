## The Glory Withdraws

Ezekiel 10 narrates the departure of the divine glory from the temple. Gnostic interpreters read this departure with a sense very different from orthodox lamentation. If the being on the throne is the Demiurge, then his departure from the temple is not a tragedy but a weakening of archonic control — a crack in the prison walls through which the divine sparks might escape.

Alternatively, some Gnostic readers understood the departing glory as a trace of genuine divine light (Sophia's remnant) that had been trapped within the Demiurge's temple structure. Its departure represents Sophia's withdrawal from the material sanctuary, leaving it as the empty shell it truly is. The glory ascends eastward — toward the light, toward the Pleroma — abandoning the compromised structure.

The coals of fire scattered over the city (10:2) were read as the dissemination of *gnosis* — tiny sparks of divine knowledge scattered throughout the world, waiting to be recognized by those with eyes to see. Destruction and illumination coincide: the burning of the old order reveals hidden truths previously concealed by the temple's institutional power.
