## The Glory Returns to Its Prison

Ezekiel 43's account of God's glory returning to the temple is read by Gnostic interpreters as the re-imprisonment of divine light within the archonic structure. The glory that departed in chapters 10-11 -- a moment the Gnostic reads as a partial liberation -- now returns to its containment within the measured, controlled space of the temple.

The Demiurge's declaration, "This is the place of my throne... where I will dwell forever" (43:7), expresses his desire for permanent sovereignty over the divine light. The Gnostic recognizes this as the fundamental archonic project: to capture and retain the pneumatic element that gives the material cosmos its semblance of life.

The altar consecration rituals, with their sin offerings and blood sacrifices, reveal the Demiurge's characteristic mode of relationship: transactional, violent, and mediated by death. The true God of the Pleroma requires no sacrifice, no blood, no priestly mediation -- only the recognition of what has always been true: that the divine spark within the human being is consubstantial with the Fullness above.
