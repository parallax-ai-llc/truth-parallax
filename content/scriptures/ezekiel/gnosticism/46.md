## The Liturgical Prison

Ezekiel 46's detailed regulations for sabbath worship, festival observances, and the prince's liturgical role represent, in Gnostic reading, the temporal architecture of archonic control. The Demiurge governs not only space (the temple) but time (the liturgical calendar), ensuring that every moment is structured by his system of worship and obligation.

The regulations governing the prince's inheritance and land transactions reveal the Demiurge's concern with perpetuating his system across generations. The archonic cosmos is designed to be self-reproducing -- each generation inherits and transmits the structures of captivity. The year of liberty (46:17) offers only temporary relief within the system, not genuine freedom from it.

The Gnostic reader recognizes that liberation from the liturgical calendar is itself an act of spiritual awakening. The Gospel of Philip declares that the pneumatic is free from the obligation of times and seasons. True worship is not bound to sabbaths and new moons but occurs in the timeless recognition of the divine spark's identity with the Pleroma.
