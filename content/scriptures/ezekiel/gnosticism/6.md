## The Mountains of Deception

Ezekiel 6 condemns the mountain shrines where Israel practiced idolatrous worship. Gnostic interpreters extended the critique far beyond these particular shrines: all material worship — including the temple itself — constitutes a form of idolatry, because it directs devotion toward the Demiurge rather than the true God.

The destruction of altars and incense stands (6:4-6) was read as a liberating act — the dismantling of the apparatus of false worship. Where orthodox interpretation mourns the destruction of legitimate worship sites corrupted by misuse, Gnostic reading celebrates the destruction of structures that were always instruments of the Demiurge's power, designed to channel devotion toward himself and away from the true God.

The promise that survivors will "remember" God among the nations (6:9) was interpreted by Gnostic readers as anamnesis — the recovery of pre-cosmic memory. The scattered divine sparks, far from the Demiurge's temple, begin to remember their true origin in the Pleroma. Exile from the Demiurge's sacred space becomes, paradoxically, the occasion for genuine spiritual awakening.
