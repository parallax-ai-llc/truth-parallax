## The Watchman and Silence

Ezekiel 3 establishes the prophet as a watchman and concludes with his enforced muteness. Gnostic interpretation found the theme of silence particularly resonant. In Valentinian cosmology, *Sige* (Silence) is one of the primal Aeons of the Pleroma — the consort of *Bythos* (Depth). The prophet's silencing could thus be read as a moment of genuine contact with the Pleroma, where the Demiurge's noisy commands give way to the true divine silence beyond all speech.

The watchman metaphor was also subject to Gnostic reinterpretation. The watchman who warns of approaching danger becomes, in Gnostic reading, a figure of the Gnostic teacher who alerts sleeping souls to their true situation — their imprisonment in matter and the urgency of awakening to *gnosis*. The "danger" is not divine punishment but continued ignorance and the failure to recognize one's divine origin.

The seven days of stunned silence at Tel-abib (3:15) before the commission resumes were read by some Gnostic commentators as a period of initiation — the necessary passage through bewilderment and disorientation that precedes genuine spiritual insight. The Gnostic initiate, like the prophet, must endure a crisis of understanding before receiving the capacity for authentic spiritual speech.
