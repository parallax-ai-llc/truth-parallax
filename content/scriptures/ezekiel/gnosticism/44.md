## The Gate and the Guard

Ezekiel 44's closed eastern gate -- shut because God entered through it -- is read by Gnostic interpreters as a seal placed on the archonic cosmos. The gate through which the divine light entered the material world is sealed to prevent its escape. The Demiurge locks the door behind himself, ensuring that the divine energy he has captured remains within his domain.

The distinction between faithful and unfaithful Levites represents the archonic hierarchy's internal ranking system. Those who served the Demiurge faithfully are rewarded with closer proximity to him; those who served other archons are demoted. The Gnostic recognizes this as the logic of every hierarchical system: loyalty is rewarded not because it is virtuous but because it serves the interests of the ruler.

The detailed regulations for priestly conduct -- diet, marriage, mourning, dress -- illustrate the comprehensive nature of archonic control. Every aspect of the priest's life is regulated, leaving no space for spontaneous spiritual experience. The Gnostic liberation consists precisely in recognizing that these regulations have no binding authority over the pneumatic self.
