## The Demiurge's Sword

Ezekiel 21 presents God's sword drawn for slaughter — polished, sharpened, and irresistible. Gnostic interpreters read the unsheathed sword as revealing the Demiurge's true nature: beneath the facade of justice and covenant love lies a violent deity who rules through fear and destruction. The sword that cuts without discrimination (21:3-4) exposes the arbitrary cruelty that Gnostics attributed to the creator god.

Nebuchadnezzar's divination at the crossroads (21:21-22) illustrated, for Gnostic readers, the interplay between archonic powers. The Babylonian king is himself an instrument of archonic forces; his divination practices, while condemned by the Demiurge's prophet, actually represent communication with the same order of beings. The cosmic system is a web of power struggles among archons, with human beings caught in the crossfire.

The suspension of the crown "until he comes to whom judgment belongs" (21:27) received Gnostic Christological interpretation in some traditions. The one to whom authority truly belongs is not a Davidic political messiah but the Gnostic Christ — the aeon sent from the Pleroma to reveal *gnosis* and lead the divine sparks home. His authority transcends and supersedes the Demiurge's entire system of kingship and law.
