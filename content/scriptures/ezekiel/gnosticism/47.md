## The River and the Counterfeit Life

Ezekiel 47's river flowing from the temple, bringing life to the Dead Sea and sustaining trees that bear fruit every month, is read by Gnostic interpreters as the Demiurge's imitation of the true Water of Life that flows within the Pleroma. The material river mimics the spiritual reality it cannot embody -- it brings biological life, not pneumatic awakening.

The progressive deepening of the river -- ankle to knee to waist to unpassable -- represents the increasing absorption of the soul into the material system. What the orthodox reader experiences as immersion in grace, the Gnostic interprets as deeper entanglement with the archonic cosmos. The river carries the soul not upward but further into matter.

Yet some Valentinian interpreters found a positive reading: the river could represent the pneumatic current that flows from the true Pleroma, passing through the Demiurge's temple without his knowledge or control. The healing of the Dead Sea would then symbolize the awakening of souls previously dead to gnosis -- a transformation that exceeds the Demiurge's intention and serves the purposes of the transcendent God.
