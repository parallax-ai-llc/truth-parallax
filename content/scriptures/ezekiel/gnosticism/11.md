## The Hidden Sanctuary

Ezekiel 11 promises the exiles that God has been "a sanctuary in small measure" to them and will give them a new heart. Gnostic readers found this passage surprisingly compatible with their theology.

The concept of God as a "sanctuary in small measure" outside the physical temple was read as a validation of interior spirituality over institutional religion. The true sanctuary is not the Demiurge's stone structure but the divine spark within each pneumatic soul. The exiles, removed from the temple's controlling influence, are paradoxically closer to genuine divine encounter.

The promise of a new heart — replacing stone with flesh — was interpreted as the awakening of the pneumatic element within the soul. The "heart of stone" is the hylic consciousness, dominated by material attachments and the Demiurge's law. The "heart of flesh" is not material softness but spiritual sensitivity — the capacity to perceive the Pleroma and respond to the call of the true God. This transformation is not moral reformation (a category of the Demiurge's law) but ontological awakening — the recognition of one's true divine nature that constitutes *gnosis*.
