## The Priestly Chambers

Ezekiel 42 describes the chambers for priestly functions and concludes with the measurement of the temple enclosure. Jewish commentators focused on the halakhic implications: the regulations for consuming sacred offerings (kodashim) and the changing of priestly garments informed ongoing rabbinic discussions about temple procedure.

The separation of holy from common, materialized in the enclosure wall, reinforces the concept of kedushah (holiness) as separation. This principle is fundamental to halakhah: Shabbat is separated from the weekdays, kosher from treif, Israel from the nations. The temple's architecture makes visible the ordering principle that pervades all of Jewish religious life.

Rashi noted that the priestly requirement to change garments before entering the common areas reflects the principle that what has been sanctified must not be treated casually. This principle informs Jewish practice regarding sacred objects -- Torah scrolls, tefillin, mezuzot -- which must be treated with appropriate reverence and may not be repurposed for common use.
