## The Sharpened Sword

Ezekiel 21 presents God's sword drawn and polished for slaughter, the divination of Nebuchadnezzar at the crossroads, and the oracle suspending the Davidic crown "until he comes to whom judgment belongs."

Jewish interpretation of 21:26-27 (*"ad bo asher lo hamishpat"*) has been a cornerstone of messianic expectation. The passage echoes Genesis 49:10 ("until Shiloh comes"), and rabbinic tradition consistently read both as referring to the Messiah. The Targum translates: "until the Messiah comes, whose is the kingdom." The removal of the crown is temporary — a suspension, not an abolition — and the messianic age will see its restoration.

Rashi noted that the sword is "polished to flash like lightning" and interpreted the elaborate sword song (21:8-17) as expressing the inescapable nature of divine judgment once decreed. The prophet's groaning and striking his thigh represent genuine prophetic anguish — the messenger who delivers the verdict of doom does not do so with satisfaction but with grief. The rabbinic tradition valued this prophetic empathy, seeing in it a model for communal leaders who must sometimes deliver difficult truths.
