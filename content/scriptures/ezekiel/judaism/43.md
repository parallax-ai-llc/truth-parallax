## The Glory Returns

Ezekiel 43 records the return of God's glory to the temple and provides instructions for the altar's consecration. For Jewish theology, this is the climactic moment of the entire vision: the Shekhinah, which departed in chapters 10-11, returns to dwell among Israel. The Midrash (Lamentations Rabbah, Introduction 25) records that when the Shekhinah departed, it did so in ten stages and with great reluctance; its return signals the reversal of exile.

The altar consecration rituals (43:18-27) generated extensive halakhic discussion. The Talmud compares these rituals with those prescribed in Leviticus and Exodus, noting both continuities and differences. These discrepancies were a subject of study and debate, with the sages recognizing that the future temple's procedures may differ from those of its predecessors.

Rashi and Radak read the return of the glory as a defining feature of the messianic era. The Talmud (Yoma 21b) lists the Shekhinah among the things present in the First Temple but absent from the Second, and the expectation of its return animates Jewish messianic hope. The declaration "I will dwell in the midst of the people of Israel forever" (43:7) promises a permanent divine indwelling that no future sin or exile can reverse.
