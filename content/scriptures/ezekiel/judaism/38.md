## The War of Gog and Magog

Ezekiel 38 introduces the figure of Gog from the land of Magog, who leads a vast coalition against restored Israel. Jewish eschatology developed this prophecy into the doctrine of the War of Gog and Magog -- the final conflict that precedes or accompanies the messianic era. The Talmud (Berakhot 7b, Sanhedrin 97b) and Midrash discuss this war extensively.

Rashi and Radak read the prophecy as referring to a future eschatological event. Radak emphasized that Gog attacks after Israel has been restored to its land and is dwelling in security -- a detail that connects the prophecy to the messianic age. Maimonides, in the Mishneh Torah (Laws of Kings 12:2), included the War of Gog and Magog in his description of the events surrounding the Messiah's coming.

The Targum Yonatan identified Gog with specific nations, but mainstream rabbinic tradition generally understood Gog as a representative figure embodying the final resistance of the nations to God's sovereignty and Israel's restoration. The liturgical tradition references Gog and Magog in the sukkah prayers and in piyyutim for the festivals, connecting the eschatological battle to the cyclical renewal celebrated in the liturgical year.
