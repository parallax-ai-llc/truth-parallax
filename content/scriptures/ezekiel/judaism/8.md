## Abominations in the Temple

Ezekiel 8 transports the prophet in vision to the Jerusalem temple, where he witnesses four scenes of escalating idolatry: the image of jealousy, elders worshiping images in secret chambers, women weeping for Tammuz, and men worshiping the sun.

The Talmud (*Yoma* 9b) identifies idolatry as one of three cardinal sins that caused the destruction of the First Temple (alongside sexual immorality and bloodshed). Ezekiel 8 provides the prophetic evidence for this rabbinic assessment. The progression from external to internal, from public to hidden idolatry, reveals that the corruption was not superficial but systemic.

Jewish commentators paid particular attention to the seventy elders worshiping in darkness (8:11-12). Rashi identified them as members of the Sanhedrin — the supreme judicial body — whose corruption signified the collapse of institutional integrity. When the guardians of Torah themselves become idolaters, the nation's spiritual infrastructure fails entirely. The number seventy connects to the seventy elders appointed by Moses (Numbers 11:16), creating a devastating contrast between the original appointment and its ultimate degradation.
