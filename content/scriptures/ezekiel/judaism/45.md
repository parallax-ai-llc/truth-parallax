## The Sacred Allotment

Ezekiel 45 prescribes the allocation of land for the temple district and establishes standards for just weights, measures, and offerings. Jewish commentators noted the chapter's integration of cultic and ethical concerns -- honest measures and proper worship are presented as equally important dimensions of the holy community.

The land allocation was compared with the original tribal allotments in Joshua. Radak noted that the new arrangement places the sacred district at the geographic center of the land, emphasizing the temple's centrality to national life. This arrangement reflects the theological principle that worship of God is not one activity among many but the orienting center of the entire community.

The prescriptions for the prince's offerings on Passover and Sukkot connect the eschatological temple to the existing festival calendar. Rashi noted that the continuation of the festivals in the messianic era affirms their permanent significance. The Talmud (Megillah 14a) discusses which aspects of Torah will persist in the messianic age, and the prophetic evidence suggests that the festival cycle will endure.
