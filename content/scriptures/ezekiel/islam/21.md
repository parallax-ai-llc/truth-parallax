## The Sword Unleashed

Ezekiel 21 presents God's sword drawn and polished for slaughter. Nebuchadnezzar stands at a crossroads, using divination to choose his target. The oracle suspends the Davidic crown "until he comes to whom judgment belongs."

Islamic theology affirms God's sovereignty over all events, including those mediated through the actions of unbelieving rulers. Nebuchadnezzar's divination yields the result God has determined — not because the divination itself has power, but because God's decree encompasses all contingencies. The Quran states: "And you do not will except that Allah wills" (81:29). Islamic commentators would read Nebuchadnezzar's crossroads decision as illustrating this principle: human choice operates within divine sovereignty.

The suspension of the monarchy "until he comes to whom judgment belongs" (21:27) is interpreted in Islamic tradition within the framework of prophetic succession. The legitimate authority that passed through the Davidic line is understood in broader prophetic terms. Islamic theology does not share the specifically Davidic messianic expectation but affirms the principle that legitimate authority belongs ultimately to God and to those He designates.
