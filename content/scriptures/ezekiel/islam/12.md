## Exile Enacted

Ezekiel 12 records the prophet's enactment of exile and addresses popular skepticism about prophetic fulfillment. The people dismiss prophecy with proverbs: "every vision fails" and "the vision is for many days hence."

The dismissal of prophetic warning is a central Quranic theme. The Quran documents how every prophet faced rejection and mockery from their people: "No messenger came to them but they used to mock him" (15:11). The specific form of dismissal in Ezekiel 12 — not outright denial but temporizing delay — is particularly insidious. The people do not explicitly call the prophet a liar; they simply defer the warning into irrelevance.

Islamic theology addresses this through the concept of *'ajal* — the divinely appointed term. Every nation and every individual has a fixed term (Quran 7:34: "For every nation there is an appointed term"). The delay of judgment is not evidence of its cancellation but of divine patience (*hilm*). When the term arrives, "they will not remain behind an hour, nor will they precede it" (7:34). God's timing is not subject to human calculation or dismissal.
