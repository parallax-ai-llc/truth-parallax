## The Day of Judgment upon Egypt

Ezekiel 30's invocation of the "Day of the LORD" against Egypt connects to the Islamic theology of Yawm al-Din (the Day of Judgment). While the prophetic "Day of the LORD" refers to historical judgments, Islamic theology sees in these historical days a pattern that culminates in the ultimate Day of Resurrection. The Quran warns, "The Hour is surely coming -- there is no doubt about it -- yet most people do not believe" (40:59).

The systematic destruction of Egyptian cities demonstrates what Islamic theology calls God's qadr (decree) operating in history. Each city is named, each judgment specified, reflecting the Quranic principle that nothing occurs without divine knowledge and permission. "Not a leaf falls but He knows it" (6:59).

The breaking of Pharaoh's arms (30:21-26) illustrates the progressive dismantling of tyrannical power. Islamic political theology recognizes that God removes unjust rulers according to His timetable -- sometimes gradually, sometimes suddenly. The Quran states, "We have destroyed nations before you when they did wrong" (10:13), and Ezekiel 30 provides a detailed case study of this principle in action.
