## The Call to Repentance

Ezekiel 33's teaching on repentance resonates powerfully with the Islamic doctrine of tawbah (repentance). The chapter's insistence that the wicked who repent will live and the righteous who sin will die corresponds to the Quranic principle: "Whoever does righteousness -- it is for his own soul; and whoever does evil -- it is against it. Then to your Lord you will be returned" (45:15).

God's declaration, "I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked" (33:11), parallels the Quranic attribute of God as al-Ghafur (the All-Forgiving) and al-Tawwab (the Acceptor of Repentance). The Quran states, "Say, O My servants who have transgressed against themselves: do not despair of the mercy of God. Indeed, God forgives all sins" (39:53). Both traditions affirm that divine mercy is always available to the sincerely repentant.

The watchman metaphor resonates with the Islamic understanding of prophetic responsibility. Every prophet (nabi) and messenger (rasul) bears the obligation to warn their community. The Quran describes this as tabligh (conveyance of the message): the prophet's duty is to deliver the warning clearly; the response is each individual's responsibility. This principle is foundational to Islamic da'wah (invitation to God).
