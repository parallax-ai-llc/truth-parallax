## The Reckoning of Edom

Ezekiel 35's judgment on Mount Seir for its perpetual enmity and territorial aggression illustrates the Quranic principle that enmity and injustice between peoples invite divine retribution. The Quran states, "God does not love the aggressors" (2:190), and Edom's exploitation of Israel's vulnerability constitutes precisely the kind of aggression that draws divine wrath.

The principle of reciprocal justice -- Edom receiving the treatment it inflicted -- corresponds to the Islamic concept of qisas (retributive justice). The Quran teaches, "The recompense for an injury is an injury equal thereto" (42:40). While forgiveness is encouraged, the prophetic tradition affirms that God's own justice operates on this principle of precise correspondence.

The declaration that "the LORD was there" (35:10) even in the land's desolation affirms God's unbroken sovereignty over all territories. Islamic theology's emphasis on God's absolute ownership of all creation (mulk) resonates with this prophetic claim: no human seizure of territory can override God's ultimate possession and disposition of the land.
