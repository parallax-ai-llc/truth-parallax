## The New Heart and Divine Purpose

Ezekiel 36's promise of inner transformation -- a new heart, a new spirit, cleansing from impurity -- resonates with the Islamic understanding of tazkiyat al-nafs (purification of the soul). The Quran describes this as a fundamental prophetic mission: "He it is who sent among the unlettered a messenger from among themselves, reciting to them His verses and purifying them" (62:2).

The sprinkling with clean water (36:25) connects to the Islamic emphasis on ritual purification (taharah) as both physical and spiritual. Wudu (ablution before prayer) is understood not merely as hygienic practice but as a symbolic and spiritual cleansing that prepares the soul for the encounter with God. Ezekiel's prophetic imagery anticipates this integration of outer washing and inner transformation.

God's declaration that He acts "for the sake of my holy name" (36:22) rather than for Israel's merit resonates with the Islamic emphasis on divine sovereignty and the utter gratuity of mercy. The Quran teaches that God's actions serve His own wise purposes, which humans may only partially comprehend. The Islamic concept of hikmah (divine wisdom) encompasses even those acts of mercy that appear unmerited, recognizing that God's generosity exceeds human calculation.
