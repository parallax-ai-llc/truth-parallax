## History as Warning

Ezekiel 20 recounts Israel's history of rebellion from Egypt onward, structured around the pattern of divine gift, human rebellion, and divine restraint. This chapter resonates deeply with the Quranic genre of *qisas al-anbiya'* (stories of the prophets), which recounts the histories of previous nations as warnings for contemporary audiences.

The Quran repeatedly references the story of Bani Isra'il (the Children of Israel) as both a model and a cautionary tale. Surah al-Baqarah (2:40-123) provides an extended recital of Israel's history — divine favors, rebellions, golden calf, ingratitude — with explicit application to the Muslim community: "O Children of Israel, remember My favor which I have bestowed upon you... and do not be the first to disbelieve in it" (2:40-41). Ezekiel 20 provides a prophetic source for this same didactic pattern.

The principle that God acts "for the sake of my name" (20:9, 14, 22) — restraining judgment to prevent the profanation of His name among the nations — finds a parallel in the Islamic concept of divine wisdom (*hikmah*). God's actions in history serve purposes that transcend the immediate context, and divine patience with rebellious communities reflects not weakness but strategic forbearance in service of a larger plan.
