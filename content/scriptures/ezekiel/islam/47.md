## The River of Mercy

Ezekiel 47's vision of the life-giving river flowing from the temple resonates with the Quranic description of paradise, where rivers of water, milk, wine, and honey flow beneath gardens of eternal delight (47:15). The progressive deepening of the river -- from ankle to knee to waist to unpassable -- suggests the ever-increasing blessings that God prepares for the faithful.

The healing of the Dead Sea, bringing life where there was none, corresponds to the Islamic understanding of God as al-Muhyi (the Giver of Life). The Quran describes how God revives dead land through rain: "And you see the earth barren, but when We send down water upon it, it stirs and swells and grows every kind of beautiful pair" (22:5). The prophetic river achieves supernaturally what rain achieves naturally -- the transformation of death into life.

The trees bearing monthly fruit with leaves for healing have been connected in Islamic commentary to the Quranic description of the Lote Tree (Sidrat al-Muntaha) at the boundary of the highest heaven, encountered by the Prophet during the Mi'raj (53:14-16). Both traditions envision trees of cosmic significance marking the boundary between earthly and heavenly realms and offering sustenance and healing to those who approach.
