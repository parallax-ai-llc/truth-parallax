## The City of God's Presence

Ezekiel 48's division of the land and the naming of the city as "The LORD Is There" provides the book's climactic theological statement. Islamic interpretation reads this in connection with the Quranic teaching that God is always near: "We are closer to him than his jugular vein" (50:16). The promise of divine presence is not limited to a single city but extends to every place where God is worshipped in sincerity.

The twelve gates of the city, each named for a tribe, resonate with the Islamic understanding of the diversity of paths that lead to the one God. The Quran declares, "For each among you We have appointed a law and a way. Had God willed, He would have made you a single community" (5:48). The multiple gates suggest that approach to the divine presence admits of diversity within the framework of divine unity.

The name "The LORD Is There" expresses the ultimate theological hope shared by all Abrahamic traditions: the permanent, unbroken communion of God with His creation. Islamic eschatology envisions this as the state of paradise, where the faithful enjoy proximity to God, freedom from suffering, and the fulfillment of every righteous desire. The Quran describes this as "the abode of peace" (dar al-salam, 6:127), where God's greeting is "Peace!" (36:58). Ezekiel's prophetic naming of the city anticipates this eschatological fulfillment -- the city where God is present, and therefore where peace, justice, and life eternally prevail.
