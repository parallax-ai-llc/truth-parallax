## The Temple Chambers

Ezekiel 42 describes the priestly chambers flanking the temple -- rooms designated for eating sacred offerings and storing priestly vestments. The chapter concludes with the measurement of the entire temple complex as a perfect square of five hundred cubits, with a wall separating the holy from the common.

Protestant interpreters find the concluding measurement theologically significant. The perfect square communicates completeness and order, while the enclosing wall materializes the fundamental distinction between holy and profane. Reformed theology connects this to the doctrine of sanctification -- God's people are called out and set apart.

The priestly chambers' dual function -- for consuming sacred food and for changing garments -- illustrates the principle that ministry in God's presence requires both spiritual nourishment and appropriate preparation.
