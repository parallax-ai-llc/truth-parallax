## Judgment on Mount Seir

Ezekiel 35 delivers an oracle of judgment against Mount Seir (Edom), expanding on the brief condemnation in chapter 25. Protestant interpreters note that Edom's sin is characterized as "perpetual enmity" rooted in the ancient rivalry between Esau and Jacob.

Reformed commentators have emphasized the theological principle at work: Edom's attempt to seize the land that belongs to God's covenant people constitutes a direct assault on God's purposes. The phrase "the LORD was there" (35:10) punctuates the point -- even in judgment, God has not abandoned His claim on the land.

The chapter's placement between the shepherd discourse (ch. 34) and the restoration of Israel's mountains (ch. 36) is structurally significant. Edom represents every force that would exploit God's disciplinary judgment of His people as an opportunity for permanent displacement.
