## The Prince's Worship

Ezekiel 46 details the prince's liturgical role in sabbath and new moon observances, regulations for his use of the eastern inner gate, and rules governing inheritance and land transactions. The chapter carefully limits the prince's power, preventing the exploitation that characterized Israel's historical monarchy.

Protestant interpreters find the inheritance regulations (46:16-18) particularly significant. The prince may give land to his sons as permanent inheritance, but gifts to servants revert in the year of liberty. The prince "shall not take any of the inheritance of the people" (46:18) -- a constitutional check on power that resonates with Protestant political philosophy's emphasis on limited government.

The detailed liturgical calendar has led some Protestant interpreters to question whether these regulations are meant literally or symbolically. Either way, the underlying principle is clear: the restored community is structured around regular, ordered worship of God.
