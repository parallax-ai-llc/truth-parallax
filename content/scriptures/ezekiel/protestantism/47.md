## The River of Life

Ezekiel 47 describes a river flowing from beneath the threshold of the temple, growing progressively deeper as it flows eastward -- from ankle-deep to knee-deep to waist-deep to a river that cannot be crossed. Wherever this river flows, it brings life: trees line its banks bearing fruit every month, and even the Dead Sea becomes fresh.

Protestant interpreters have consistently read this vision as one of the Bible's most powerful images of God's life-giving grace. The progressive deepening has been read as representing the increasing fullness of the Spirit's work. John's Gospel (7:38) and Revelation (22:1-2) both draw on this imagery.

The healing of the Dead Sea is an especially potent symbol -- the most lifeless body of water on earth becomes a thriving ecosystem, an image of the gospel's power to bring life to the most hopeless situations. The monthly fruit and the leaves "for healing" anticipate Revelation 22:2 and the consummation of all things.
