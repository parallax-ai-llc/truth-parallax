## The Division of the Land

Ezekiel 48 concludes the book with the allocation of the restored land among the twelve tribes, the dimensions of the sacred district and city, and the naming of the city's twelve gates. The final words -- "The LORD Is There" -- provide the theological climax of the entire prophecy.

Protestant interpreters note the idealized geometry: each tribe receives an equal east-west strip, with the sacred district at the center. This differs markedly from the historical allotment in Joshua, suggesting the vision is less a real-estate plan than a theological statement about equity, order, and the centrality of worship.

The name "The LORD Is There" (Yahweh Shammah) reverses the book's most devastating moment -- God's departure from the temple. Protestant theology reads this as the Bible's ultimate promise: God will dwell permanently with His people. Reformed and evangelical interpreters connect this directly to Revelation 21-22, where God declares, "Behold, I am making all things new."
