## The Sacred District

Ezekiel 45 establishes the allocation of land surrounding the temple -- a sacred district for priests, Levites, and the city, along with land for the prince. The chapter also addresses just weights and measures and prescribes the prince's role in providing offerings for festivals.

Protestant interpreters note the chapter's integration of worship, justice, and governance. The demand for honest weights and measures (45:10-12) sits alongside liturgical regulations, demonstrating that holiness encompasses economic ethics as well as ritual practice. Reformed commentators emphasize this against any tendency to separate worship from social justice.

The prince's role as provider of offerings positions leadership as fundamentally oriented toward facilitating the community's worship rather than extracting its wealth -- a model of governance that contrasts sharply with the exploitative rulers condemned in chapter 34.
