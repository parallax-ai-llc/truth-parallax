## The Vision of the New Temple

Ezekiel 40 begins the book's final major section: a detailed vision of a new temple, received in the twenty-fifth year of exile. A heavenly guide with a measuring rod leads the prophet through the temple complex, recording precise architectural dimensions.

Protestant interpretation of the temple vision has produced significant disagreement. Dispensationalist interpreters read the vision as a literal blueprint for a future millennial temple. Reformed interpreters generally read it symbolically -- as an idealized vision of God's dwelling with His people, fulfilled spiritually in the church and ultimately in the new creation.

What Protestant interpreters across traditions agree on is the vision's theological function: after the departure of God's glory from the old temple (chapters 8-11), this vision assures the exiles that God's presence will return and worship will be restored. The meticulous measurements communicate order, holiness, and divine intentionality.
