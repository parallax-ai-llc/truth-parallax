## The Closed Gate and the Priesthood

Ezekiel 44 describes the eastern gate permanently shut because "the LORD, the God of Israel, has entered by it" (44:2). Only the prince may sit in it to eat bread before the LORD. The chapter then distinguishes between faithful Levitical priests (Zadokites) and those who went astray.

Protestant interpreters have debated the identity of the prince extensively. Some identify him as a future Davidic ruler, others as a figure typologically pointing to Christ. The closed gate has generated its own interpretive tradition -- Protestant exegetes generally reject the Marian reading in favor of Christological or eschatological interpretations.

The distinction between faithful and unfaithful Levites establishes a principle of accountability in ministry. Those who led the people into idolatry are demoted, while those who remained faithful are elevated -- reinforcing the Protestant emphasis on the weight of pastoral responsibility.
