## The Defeat of Gog

Ezekiel 39 completes the Gog oracle with a dramatic depiction of the invader's total destruction. God intervenes with fire, brimstone, pestilence, and torrential rain. The aftermath requires seven months for burial and seven years to burn the abandoned weapons.

Protestant interpreters note the extravagant symbolism -- seven months and seven years both signifying completeness. The passage communicates that God's victory is total, requiring no human military contribution. The grotesque feast for birds and beasts (39:17-20) anticipates Revelation 19:17-18, further connecting the Gog narrative to New Testament eschatology.

The chapter concludes with restoration theology (39:25-29): God will restore the fortunes of Jacob and pour out His Spirit upon Israel. The final verse -- "I will never again hide my face from them" -- promises the permanent, unbroken communion between God and His people that Protestant theology identifies as the ultimate goal of redemptive history.
