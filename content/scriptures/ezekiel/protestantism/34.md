## The True Shepherd

Ezekiel 34 contains one of the Old Testament's most powerful passages on leadership, condemning Israel's shepherds for feeding themselves rather than their flocks, and promising that God Himself will shepherd His people. The chapter culminates in the promise of "my servant David" -- a messianic shepherd who will tend the flock with justice.

Protestant Christological interpretation reads this chapter as directly anticipating Jesus' declaration "I am the good shepherd" (John 10:11). Reformed commentators emphasize that God's response to failed human leadership is not merely better human leadership but divine intervention -- God Himself enters to seek the lost, bind the injured, and strengthen the weak.

The covenant of peace promised in 34:25-31, with its vision of security, abundance, and freedom from harm, expresses the Protestant understanding of the kingdom inaugurated by Christ and consummated at His return.
