## The Invasion of Gog

Ezekiel 38 introduces the enigmatic figure of Gog, from the land of Magog, chief prince of Meshech and Tubal, who leads a vast coalition against a restored and peaceful Israel. Protestant interpretation of this chapter has generated significant diversity of opinion, with views ranging from historicist to futurist to idealist readings.

Dispensationalist interpreters have identified Gog with modern nations and read the passage as predicting a future invasion of Israel. Reformed and amillennial interpreters read Gog as a symbolic figure representing the final assault of hostile powers against God's people, corresponding to Revelation 20:8.

What unites Protestant readings is the recognition that Gog does not act autonomously. God declares, "I will turn you around and put hooks in your jaws" (38:4). The purpose of Gog's defeat is stated repeatedly: "that the nations may know me, when through you, O Gog, I vindicate my holiness before their eyes" (38:16). The chapter presents history's final conflict as a demonstration of God's uncontested supremacy.
