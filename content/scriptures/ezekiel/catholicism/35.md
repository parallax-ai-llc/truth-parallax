## The Desolation of Edom

Ezekiel 35 pronounces judgment on Mount Seir for its perpetual hostility toward Israel and its attempt to seize the land during exile. Catholic interpretation reads this within the framework of divine justice: Edom's sin is not merely political aggression but a spiritual offense against God's providential plan.

The principle of reciprocal justice is prominent: "As you rejoiced over the inheritance of the house of Israel, because it was desolate, so I will deal with you" (35:15). Catholic moral theology recognizes this as an expression of divine justice foundational to Catholic teaching on the last judgment.

Patristic commentators read Edom typologically as representing the carnal disposition that opposes the spiritual life. Origen and Jerome both interpreted the Esau-Jacob typology as reflecting the struggle between flesh and spirit, following the Catholic interpretive principle of the fourfold sense of Scripture.
