## Promise within Judgment

Ezekiel 11 combines a judgment oracle against Jerusalem's corrupt leaders with a transformative promise to the exiles. God declares that He has been "a sanctuary in small measure" to the scattered, and promises to gather them, give them one heart and a new spirit, and replace their heart of stone with a heart of flesh. The glory then departs the city entirely.

Catholic theology finds in verses 16-20 a foundational text for sacramental and ecclesiological reflection. The concept of God as "a sanctuary in small measure" (*miqdash me'at*) has been read as anticipating the dispersed sacramental presence of God — wherever the faithful gather, even without the physical temple, God is present. This prefigures the Catholic understanding of the parish church as the locus of divine presence through the Eucharist.

The promise of a new heart and a new spirit is understood in Catholic soteriology as describing the interior transformation effected by sanctifying grace. Unlike Protestant interpretations that emphasize the instantaneous character of regeneration, Catholic theology reads this transformation as a process — the ongoing work of grace that begins in baptism and continues through the sacraments, prayer, and moral life throughout the believer's journey toward theosis.
