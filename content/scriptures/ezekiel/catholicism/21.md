## The Drawn Sword

Ezekiel 21 presents the image of God's sword unsheathed and polished for slaughter. Nebuchadnezzar stands at a crossroads using divination to choose between Jerusalem and Ammon — the lot falls on Jerusalem. The chapter includes a cryptic oracle about the crown being removed "until he comes to whom judgment belongs."

Catholic eschatological interpretation has connected the messianic oracle of 21:27 to the broader prophetic theme of the suspended kingship. The removal of the crown and turban — priestly and royal insignia — signals an interregnum that endures "until he comes to whom judgment belongs." Catholic Christology reads this as fulfilled in Christ, who unites kingship and priesthood in his person, as elaborated in the Epistle to the Hebrews.

The providential irony of God using Nebuchadnezzar's pagan divination to accomplish divine purposes has been noted by Catholic commentators from Jerome onward. God's sovereignty encompasses and transcends all human systems, including those that are themselves sinful. This principle informs the Catholic theology of history: God writes straight with crooked lines, accomplishing salvation through the most unlikely instruments.
