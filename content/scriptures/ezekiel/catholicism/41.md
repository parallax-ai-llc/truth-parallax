## The Inner Sanctuary

Ezekiel 41 continues the detailed measurement of the temple's interior. Catholic interpretation finds in the decorative program of cherubim and palm trees a rich symbolic vocabulary that has shaped the Church's architectural and liturgical tradition.

The alternating cherubim and palm trees recall both Eden and Solomon's temple. Catholic theology reads this as expressing the eschatological return to paradise that worship anticipates. The Church's tradition of sacred art -- mosaics, frescoes, icons -- stands in conscious continuity with this biblical precedent. Sacrosanctum Concilium affirmed that sacred art serves the liturgy by elevating the mind to God.

The wooden altar described as "the table that is before the LORD" (41:22) has been read eucharistically by Catholic commentators. The altar-table where bread is set before the LORD prefigures the altar of the Eucharist, where Christ offers Himself under the appearances of bread and wine.
