## Liturgical Order

Ezekiel 46 details the prince's role in sabbath and festival worship, daily offerings, and land regulations. Catholic liturgical theology finds here a confirmation that ordered worship reflects God's intention for communal life.

The detailed liturgical calendar resonates with the Catholic Church's own calendar, which structures time around the mysteries of Christ. The Liturgy of the Hours, the cycle of feasts, and the daily Eucharist all reflect the principle established here: worship is a disciplined, rhythmic participation in divine life.

The regulations limiting the prince's power over land express a principle dear to Catholic social teaching: the universal destination of goods. No ruler may unjustly expropriate the property of the people.
