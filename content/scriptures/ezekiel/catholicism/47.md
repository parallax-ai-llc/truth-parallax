## The River of Life

Ezekiel 47's vision of the river flowing from the temple -- deepening progressively and bringing life even to the Dead Sea -- is one of the most beloved images in Catholic sacramental theology. The river has been interpreted as a figure of baptismal grace, the Holy Spirit's outpouring, and the sacraments' life-giving power.

The Fathers, particularly Ambrose and Cyril of Jerusalem, connected this river to baptismal waters. The progressive deepening was read as stages of Christian initiation and spiritual growth. The trees with leaves "for healing" anticipate Revelation 22:1-2 and correspond to the sacramental economy through which the Church mediates Christ's healing grace.

The healing of the Dead Sea has served as a powerful image in Catholic mission theology. No person, no culture, no situation is beyond the reach of God's transforming grace. The Catholic missionary tradition embodies the conviction that the river from God's temple is meant to reach the ends of the earth.
