## The Return of the Glory

Ezekiel 43 records the climactic return of God's glory to the temple, followed by altar consecration instructions. Catholic theology regards this as one of the most significant Old Testament moments, prefiguring the Incarnation and the real presence of Christ in the Eucharist.

God declares, "This is the place of my throne... where I will dwell in the midst of the people of Israel forever" (43:7). Catholic Christology reads this as fulfilled in Jesus Christ, in whom "the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily" (Colossians 2:9). The Church continues this indwelling presence sacramentally in every tabernacle where the Blessed Sacrament is reserved.

The altar consecration rituals are read typologically as pointing to Christ's sacrifice and the daily offering of the Mass. The seven-day consecration echoes the creation week, suggesting that restoration of worship constitutes a new creation. Catholic liturgical theology understands every Eucharist as participation in this definitive act of consecration.
