## The Day of Egypt

Ezekiel 30 announces the "Day of the LORD" against Egypt and its allies, detailing the destruction of specific cities and the breaking of Pharaoh's power. Catholic interpretation situates this within the prophetic tradition of the Dies Irae -- the day of wrath -- which has profoundly shaped Catholic liturgical and theological imagination.

The naming of specific Egyptian cities demonstrates what Catholic exegesis calls the concreteness of prophetic speech. God's judgment is not abstract or impersonal but addresses particular communities. This specificity resonates with the Catholic sacramental principle that grace and judgment operate through concrete, material realities.

The image of Pharaoh's broken arms illustrates the progressive nature of divine judgment. Catholic moral theology recognizes that consequences unfold over time and opportunities for repentance may diminish. The passage serves as a sobering reminder that the time for conversion is always now.
