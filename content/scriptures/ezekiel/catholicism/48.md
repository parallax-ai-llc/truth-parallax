## The City of God's Presence

Ezekiel 48 concludes the book with the division of the land, the city's dimensions, and the climactic naming: "The LORD Is There." Catholic theology reads this as the prophetic anticipation of the heavenly Jerusalem described in Revelation 21-22.

The twelve gates, each named for a tribe, have been connected in Catholic tradition to the twelve apostles who form the Church's foundation. The city's perfect symmetry and the centrality of the sacred district express the order and holiness characterizing God's eternal dwelling. Catholic eschatology looks forward to this consummation when "God will be all in all."

The name "The LORD Is There" reverses the departure of glory in chapters 10-11 and provides the book's theological conclusion. Catholic theology sees this promise realized partially in the Incarnation, sacramentally in the Eucharist, and definitively in the eschatological communion of saints.
