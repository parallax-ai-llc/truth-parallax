## The Coming of Gog

Ezekiel 38 introduces the mysterious figure of Gog from the land of Magog, leading a vast coalition against restored Israel. Catholic interpretation, shaped by Augustine's influential reading in The City of God, has generally understood Gog symbolically as representing the final persecution of the Church before Christ's return.

The Catechism of the Catholic Church (par. 675-677) teaches that the Church must pass through a final trial. While it does not specifically cite Ezekiel 38, this eschatological framework resonates with the Gog prophecy: a final assault on God's people defeated by divine intervention rather than human effort.

The theological heart of the chapter is God's sovereignty: "I will turn you around and put hooks in your jaws" (38:4). Catholic theology of providence affirms that evil, while real, never escapes God's ultimate governance. The entire episode serves the manifestation of God's glory.
