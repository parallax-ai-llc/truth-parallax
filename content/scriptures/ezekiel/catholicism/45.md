## The Holy District and Just Governance

Ezekiel 45 allocates land for the sacred district and prescribes just weights, measures, and festival offerings. Catholic social teaching finds in this chapter a prophetic integration of worship, justice, and governance.

The demand for honest weights and measures alongside liturgical regulations embodies the Catholic principle that worship and social justice are inseparable. Pope Benedict XVI articulated this connection in Deus Caritas Est: the Church's charitable activity is an essential expression of its nature. Ezekiel 45 demonstrates that this integration has prophetic roots.

The prince's obligation to provide offerings positions leadership as service -- a principle that Catholic theology develops in its understanding of authority as ministerium rather than dominium.
