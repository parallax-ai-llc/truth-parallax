## The Good Shepherd

Ezekiel 34's indictment of false shepherds and promise of God's own shepherding has profoundly shaped Catholic ecclesiology and pastoral theology. The condemnation of leaders who "feed themselves instead of the flock" has been cited by popes from Gregory the Great to Francis as a warning against clerical self-interest.

The promise of "my servant David" as the messianic shepherd is read Christologically: Jesus is the Good Shepherd who fulfills this prophecy. The Catholic understanding of the papacy and episcopate as participations in Christ's shepherding office derives partly from this prophetic vision.

The covenant of peace (34:25-31) is understood eschatologically as pointing to the kingdom of God inaugurated by Christ and sacramentally present in the Church. The eucharistic overtones -- God feeding His flock, providing showers of blessing -- connect the prophetic vision to the Church's liturgical life.
