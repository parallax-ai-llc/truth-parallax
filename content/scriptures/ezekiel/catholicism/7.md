## The Day of Wrath

Ezekiel 7 announces the end upon the land with escalating intensity: the day of doom has come, wrath is poured out, violence has grown, and neither silver nor gold can deliver. Economic, social, and political structures collapse simultaneously.

Catholic liturgical tradition has drawn on this chapter and similar prophetic passages to shape the theology of the *Dies Irae* — the Day of Wrath — in the Requiem Mass. The sequence hymn *Dies irae, dies illa* echoes the prophetic urgency of Ezekiel 7 and Zephaniah 1, reminding the faithful that divine judgment is real and imminent, even if its timing is unknown.

The comprehensive scope of the judgment — affecting every sphere of life — illustrates the Catholic principle that grace and sin are never compartmentalized. The chapter addresses not only cultic unfaithfulness but economic injustice, political corruption, and social disorder as dimensions of a single reality. Catholic social teaching, from *Rerum Novarum* to *Laudato Si'*, reflects this prophetic insistence that spiritual and material life are inseparable.
