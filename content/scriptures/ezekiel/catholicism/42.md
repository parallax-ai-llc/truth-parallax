## The Priestly Chambers

Ezekiel 42 describes chambers designated for priestly functions and concludes with the temple enclosure measured as a perfect square, with a wall separating the holy from the common. Catholic theology finds here a confirmation of principles fundamental to its sacramental and liturgical life.

The distinction between sacred and profane space corresponds to the Catholic understanding of consecrated places. Churches, altars, vessels, and vestments are set apart for divine worship through rites of dedication -- an embodied recognition that certain spaces are ordered to the encounter with God.

The priestly changing of garments -- sacred vestments within the holy precincts and ordinary clothes outside -- illustrates the principle of liturgical vesture that Catholic tradition has maintained. Vestments are sacramental signs that the minister acts in persona Christi.
