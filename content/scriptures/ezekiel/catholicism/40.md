## The New Temple Vision

Ezekiel 40 initiates the final vision: an elaborate architectural tour of a new temple guided by an angelic figure. Catholic interpretation reads the vision primarily through its theological and typological significance. The temple represents God's definitive dwelling with His people, fulfilled in the Incarnation, continued in the Church, and consummated in the heavenly Jerusalem.

The patristic tradition, particularly Origen and Jerome, read the temple vision allegorically: the measurements represent spiritual realities, the gates symbolize virtues, and the graduated holiness represents stages of the spiritual life. Modern Catholic exegesis gives greater weight to historical context while maintaining the validity of spiritual reading.

The Pontifical Biblical Commission has affirmed that prophetic visions need not be read as literal blueprints. The temple vision's perfect symmetry and order express the holiness and intentionality of God's presence -- qualities that Catholic theology sees embodied in the Church's own architecture, liturgy, and sacramental order.
