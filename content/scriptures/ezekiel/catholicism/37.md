## The Valley of Dry Bones

Ezekiel 37's vision of the valley of dry bones is one of the most celebrated passages in the prophetic literature. Catholic interpretation reads the vision on multiple levels: as a prophecy of national restoration, as a prefiguration of bodily resurrection, and as an image of spiritual renewal through the sacraments.

The Easter Vigil liturgy draws on this passage, connecting the breath of God animating dry bones to the Holy Spirit's work in baptism. The Catechism cites Ezekiel 37 in its treatment of the resurrection of the body (par. 990). The physical nature of the restoration affirms the Catholic conviction that redemption encompasses the whole person, body and soul.

The joining of two sticks into one has been read ecclesiologically as prefiguring the unity of the Church. Catholic ecumenical theology has found in this passage a mandate for pursuing the visible unity of Christians -- one people under one shepherd, dwelling in one covenant with God.
