## The Closed Gate

Ezekiel 44's permanently closed eastern gate -- shut "because the LORD, the God of Israel, has entered by it" -- has held a unique place in Catholic theology. From at least the fourth century, the Fathers interpreted this gate as a symbol of the Blessed Virgin Mary: as the gate was sanctified by God's passage and remained closed, so Mary's virginity was consecrated by the Incarnation and preserved perpetually.

This Marian reading, attested in Ambrose, Jerome, and Augustine, became standard in Catholic exegesis and is reflected in liturgical art where the Porta Clausa appears as a Marian symbol. Modern Catholic exegesis also attends to the historical context while maintaining the theological validity of the Marian interpretation.

The distinction between faithful and unfaithful Levites resonates with Catholic theology of the ministerial priesthood. Catholic canon law and sacramental theology maintain that priestly ministry requires not only valid ordination but personal holiness and fidelity.
