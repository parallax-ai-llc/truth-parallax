## The New Heart

Ezekiel 36's promise of a new heart and a new spirit represents one of the Old Testament's most significant anticipations of the New Covenant. Catholic theology reads this passage in continuity with Jeremiah 31:31-34 and sees its fulfillment inaugurated at the Last Supper when Jesus declared, "This cup is the new covenant in my blood."

The sprinkling with clean water (36:25) has been connected to baptism from the earliest patristic period. The Catechism of the Catholic Church cites this passage in its treatment of baptismal regeneration, seeing in Ezekiel's prophecy a prefiguration of the sacrament that effects new birth by water and the Holy Spirit.

God's stated motivation -- acting "for the sake of my holy name" rather than for Israel's merit -- resonates with the Catholic doctrine of grace as entirely gratuitous. The Council of Trent affirmed that the beginning of justification proceeds from God's prevenient grace, and Ezekiel 36 provides prophetic warrant for this teaching.
