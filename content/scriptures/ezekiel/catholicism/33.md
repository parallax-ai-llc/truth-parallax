## The Watchman and Repentance

Ezekiel 33 reestablishes the prophet as a watchman and delivers the news of Jerusalem's fall. The chapter's teaching on individual moral responsibility holds particular importance for Catholic moral theology and the sacrament of reconciliation.

The watchman metaphor has been applied in Catholic tradition to the episcopal office. Gregory the Great devoted extensive reflection to this passage in his Pastoral Rule, arguing that the failure to warn constitutes a grave sin on the part of the shepherd. The Catholic understanding of apostolic responsibility is deeply shaped by this prophetic model.

God's protest, "I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked, but that the wicked turn from his way and live" (33:11), expresses God's universal salvific will, a principle that the Council of Trent affirmed. The sacrament of reconciliation institutionalizes this prophetic promise, offering a concrete means by which the turning envisioned by Ezekiel can be enacted.
