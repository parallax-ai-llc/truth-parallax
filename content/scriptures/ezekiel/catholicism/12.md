## Signs of Exile

Ezekiel 12 records the prophet's enactment of exile — digging through a wall, carrying his baggage with covered face — and addresses the people's skepticism about prophetic fulfillment. The proverb "every vision comes to nothing" is met with God's declaration that none of His words will be delayed further.

Catholic exegesis draws attention to the interplay between sign and reality. The prophet's enacted exile functions as what later sacramental theology would call an *efficacious sign* — an action that not only represents but participates in the reality it signifies. The sign-acts of the prophets provide a biblical foundation for the Catholic understanding of sacramental efficacy: outward signs that convey inward grace.

The people's dismissal of prophecy as unfulfilled or delayed reflects a perennial challenge that the Church addresses through its teaching on eschatological hope. The Catholic tradition, drawing on 2 Peter 3:8-9, insists that apparent delay in divine action is not failure but patience. The liturgical calendar, with its cyclical re-presentation of salvation history, trains the faithful to live in the tension between promise and fulfillment.
