## The Vine Wood

Ezekiel 15 compares Jerusalem to vine wood, which is useless for any purpose except burning. Orthodox commentary reads this brief parable as a meditation on the nature of election and its obligations. Israel's chosenness does not confer inherent superiority but rather a specific purpose — bearing fruit — without which election itself becomes a ground of judgment.

The Fathers connected this passage to Christ's teaching in John 15, where the vine imagery is transformed: Christ is the true vine, and the faithful are branches who must abide in Him to bear fruit. Orthodox theology understands this "abiding" as the eucharistic and ascetic life — the disciplines through which believers remain connected to Christ, the source of spiritual life.

The already-charred state of the vine wood (15:4-5) — partially burned and now entirely worthless — serves as a warning against presuming that surviving initial judgment guarantees future security. Orthodox ascetic teaching emphasizes the necessity of ongoing vigilance: the spiritual life admits no coasting on past achievements.
