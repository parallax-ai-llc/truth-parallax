## The Temple Interior

Ezekiel 41 describes the inner dimensions of the temple and the decorative program of cherubim and palm trees. Orthodox theology reads the decorative elements as embodying the principle that sacred art is integral to theological truth, not mere ornamentation.

The alternating cherubim and palm trees represent the interpenetration of heavenly and earthly realities. Orthodox iconography operates on the same principle: icons are windows into the heavenly realm. The Seventh Ecumenical Council (Nicaea II, 787) defended icon veneration, and Ezekiel's temple decorations provide Old Testament precedent for the Church's use of sacred imagery.

The wooden altar described as "the table that is before the LORD" (41:22) has been interpreted as a type of the holy table (hagia trapeza) at the center of every Orthodox sanctuary. The eucharistic table fulfills what Ezekiel's altar prefigures: the place where heaven and earth meet, where God feeds His people with His own life.
