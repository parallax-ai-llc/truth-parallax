## The Assault of Gog

Ezekiel 38 introduces Gog from the land of Magog leading a vast coalition against restored Israel. Orthodox interpretation, following the patristic consensus established by Irenaeus, Hippolytus, and Andrew of Caesarea, reads this eschatologically as the final assault on the Church before Christ's return.

The Byzantine liturgical tradition incorporated Gog imagery into its understanding of the Antichrist. Andrew of Caesarea's Commentary on the Apocalypse connected Ezekiel 38-39 with Revelation 20:8, understanding both as describing the same eschatological event -- the final gathering of hostile forces, followed by decisive divine intervention.

The theological heart is divine sovereignty: God Himself brings Gog against Israel to demonstrate His holiness. Orthodox theology affirms that divine providence encompasses even the most threatening events. The Akathist Hymn declares that nothing in creation can ultimately resist God's salvific will.
