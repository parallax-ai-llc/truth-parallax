## Enacted Judgment

Ezekiel 4 describes the prophet's sign-acts: the siege model, the extended lying on his sides, and the rationed provisions. Orthodox liturgical theology finds in these enacted parables a precursor to the sacramental principle — physical actions that embody and communicate spiritual realities.

The prophet's bearing of Israel's and Judah's iniquity on his sides has been read in Orthodox Christological interpretation as a type of Christ's bearing of human sin. The innocent prophet suffers vicariously, not for his own transgressions but for those of his people. This typological reading connects Ezekiel's sign-acts to the theology of the Cross and the icon of the *Epitaphios* (the burial shroud of Christ).

The meager rations and unclean bread symbolize the spiritual famine of exile — separation from the temple, the sacrifices, and the full liturgical life of Israel. Orthodox theology understands this deprivation as pointing to the catastrophe of separation from the Eucharistic community, the true bread of life.
