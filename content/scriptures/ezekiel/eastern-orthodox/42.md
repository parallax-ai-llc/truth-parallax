## The Sacred Chambers

Ezekiel 42 details priestly chambers and concludes with the temple enclosure measured as a perfect square separated from the common by a wall. Orthodox liturgical theology finds here a confirmation of principles shaping every aspect of Orthodox worship.

The distinction between holy and common space corresponds to the Orthodox understanding of the church building as consecrated space. The antimension -- the cloth bearing a bishop's signature that must be present on every altar -- marks the space as authorized for the Holy Mysteries.

The priestly changing of garments is directly reflected in Orthodox liturgical practice. The vesting prayers interpret garments as spiritual armor and signs of grace. The prohibition against carrying the holy into the common resonates with Orthodox discipline surrounding the Eucharist and everything sanctified by contact with divine presence.
