## The City Named for God

Ezekiel 48 concludes with the land division, the holy city's dimensions, and the climactic declaration: "The LORD Is There." Orthodox eschatology reads this as a prophetic icon of the heavenly Jerusalem, the ultimate destination of the Church's pilgrimage.

The twelve gates named for tribes have been connected to the apostolic foundation and the communion of saints encompassing all the faithful. The perfect symmetry reflects the divine order that Orthodox theology sees as creation's goal -- the restoration of cosmic harmony disrupted by sin.

The name "The LORD Is There" provides the book's theological climax. Orthodox theology understands this as the promise of permanent communion with God -- the very definition of theosis. The Paschal proclamation, "Christ is risen!" is the fulfillment: in the risen Christ, God is permanently and irrevocably present with His people, and all creation is destined to become the dwelling of God.
