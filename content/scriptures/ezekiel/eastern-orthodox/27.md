## The Ship of Tyre

Ezekiel 27 portrays Tyre as a magnificent ship wrecked in the heart of the seas. Orthodox theology reads this lament as a meditation on the transience of worldly glory -- a theme central to the ascetical tradition. The Lenten Triodion repeatedly invokes the contrast between earthly splendor and heavenly treasure.

The commercial catalogue reveals the reach of Tyre's economy. Orthodox social ethics, rooted in the Cappadocian Fathers' teaching on wealth and poverty, does not condemn commerce but insists that economic activity must serve the common good. Basil the Great's homilies on wealth find their prophetic foundation in passages like this.

The mourning of merchants at Tyre's fall carries genuine emotional weight. Orthodox theology recognizes the authentic grief involved in the destruction of beauty, even when that destruction is just. The iconographic tradition, with its depiction of the Last Judgment, captures this same tension.
