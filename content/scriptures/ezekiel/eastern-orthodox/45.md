## The Sacred District

Ezekiel 45 allocates land for the temple district and prescribes just measures and festival offerings. Orthodox social theology finds a prophetic integration of worship and justice that resonates with the Church's self-understanding.

The demand for honest weights alongside liturgical prescriptions embodies the conviction that worship and ethics are inseparable. The Divine Liturgy's dismissal sends the faithful to embody the justice they have encountered in worship. John Chrysostom insisted that the Eucharist demands social responsibility.

The prince's role as provider of offerings reflects the symphonia ideal -- harmonious cooperation of civil and ecclesiastical authority. While historically complex, the prophetic vision of governance oriented toward worship and justice remains normative for Orthodox political theology.
