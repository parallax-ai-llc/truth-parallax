## The Shepherd and the Flock

Ezekiel 34 condemns false shepherds and promises God's own shepherding through "my servant David." Orthodox Christology reads this as fulfilled in Christ, who is at once divine (God Himself shepherding) and human (the servant David). The Chalcedonian definition finds a prophetic anticipation in this dual identification.

The Orthodox episcopate as a shepherding office draws heavily on this chapter. Bishops are called "shepherds," and their pastoral responsibilities are understood as participations in Christ's own shepherding. The ordination rite for bishops invokes this prophetic and evangelical imagery.

The covenant of peace (34:25-31) is understood eschatologically as pointing to the kingdom experienced liturgically. Every Divine Liturgy is a foretaste of the messianic banquet. The promise that "I, the LORD their God, am with them" (34:30) is fulfilled sacramentally in the Eucharist, where Christ is truly present among His people.
