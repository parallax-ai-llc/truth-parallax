## The Promise of Renewal

Ezekiel 36 contains the great promise of a new heart, a new spirit, and cleansing from impurities. Orthodox theology reads this as one of the most important prophecies of what the Fathers call theosis -- participation of human nature in divine life through the Holy Spirit.

The sprinkling with clean water (36:25) is connected in Orthodox baptismal theology to the sacrament of illumination (photismos). The baptismal rite includes prayers explicitly invoking Ezekiel's imagery. The replacement of the heart of stone with a heart of flesh describes the transformation that baptism initiates and the entire sacramental life sustains.

God's motivation -- acting for His holy name, not Israel's merit -- resonates with the Orthodox theology of grace as divine energeia freely given. The distinction between God's essence and energies, articulated by Gregory Palamas, ensures that transformation is genuine participation in divine life without confusion of Creator and creature. The new heart is animated by uncreated divine energy -- the beginning of theosis.
