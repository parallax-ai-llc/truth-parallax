## The Victory over Gog

Ezekiel 39 completes the Gog narrative with total destruction, a gruesome feast for scavengers, and the promise that God will pour out His Spirit and never again hide His face. Orthodox theology reads this as a prefiguration of Christ's ultimate victory over evil and death.

The feast imagery has been read in relation to both judgment and the eschatological banquet. The invaders who came to devour become food themselves. Orthodox iconography of the Last Judgment depicts a similar inversion where forces of evil are consumed by divine fire.

The concluding promise of the Spirit's outpouring is read as a Pentecost prophecy. Orthodox pneumatology sees the fulfillment beginning at Pentecost and continuing through the sacraments. The permanent availability of God's presence grounds Orthodox confidence that the Church will endure until the end of the age.
