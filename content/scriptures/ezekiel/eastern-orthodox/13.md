## Prophets of Delusion

Ezekiel 13 condemns those who prophesy from their own imagination and those who practice divination. Their message of false peace is compared to whitewash on an unstable wall.

Orthodox theology has consistently emphasized the danger of unauthorized spiritual teaching. The tradition of *obedience* (submission to a spiritual father or mother) exists precisely to guard against the self-deception described in this chapter. Those who "follow their own spirit and have seen nothing" (13:3) represent the spiritual danger of private revelation untested by the community of faith.

The imagery of whitewashing a wall has been applied by Orthodox commentators to superficial spirituality that addresses symptoms rather than causes. The Orthodox emphasis on deep inner transformation through repentance, fasting, prayer, and the sacraments contrasts with approaches that offer emotional comfort without genuine spiritual surgery. The wall must be rebuilt from its foundation, not merely painted.
