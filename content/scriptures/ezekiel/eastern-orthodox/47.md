## The River of Life

Ezekiel 47's river flowing from the temple -- deepening progressively and bringing life even to the Dead Sea -- holds a central place in Orthodox sacramental and mystical theology. The river is read as a figure of the Holy Spirit, whose life-giving energies flow from the Church to sanctify all creation.

The progressive deepening has been interpreted in the ascetical tradition as stages of spiritual growth: purification (katharsis), illumination (photismos), and deification (theosis). The deeper one enters divine grace, the less one remains in control and the more one is carried by the Spirit -- a surrender the Fathers describe as the highest freedom.

The healing of the Dead Sea resonates with the theology of transfiguration: all material creation is destined for transformation. The trees with leaves "for healing" find their sacramental realization in the Eucharist, the medicine of immortality. The river flows outward from the sanctuary, as sacramental grace flows from the Church to heal the world.
