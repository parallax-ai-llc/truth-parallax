## The Razor and the Remnant

Ezekiel 5 records the symbolic shaving of the prophet's head and the threefold division of the hair, representing the fate of Jerusalem's population. A small remnant is preserved in the prophet's garment, though even some of these are cast into the fire.

Orthodox interpretation emphasizes the sobriety of remnant theology. The Church preserves the faithful through judgment, but preservation is not guaranteed to any individual apart from genuine faithfulness. The Orthodox understanding of salvation resists both presumption (automatic salvation through membership) and despair (the impossibility of being preserved). The narrow way remains open, but it requires ongoing repentance and vigilance.

The theological rationale in the chapter's second half — that Jerusalem was more rebellious than the surrounding nations despite receiving God's statutes — resonates with the Orthodox principle that greater illumination brings greater responsibility. The nations that have received the fullness of apostolic Christianity bear a heavier accountability than those who have not.
