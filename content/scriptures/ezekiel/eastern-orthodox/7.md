## The Day of Finality

Ezekiel 7 pronounces the irrevocable end upon the land. Silver and gold become worthless, the marketplace ceases, and all human institutions collapse. Orthodox eschatological theology draws on this chapter to articulate the reality of divine judgment that transcends all earthly securities.

The comprehensive collapse described — economic, political, social — resonates with Orthodox apocalyptic theology, which views the present world order as provisional and ultimately subject to divine transformation. The imagery of valuables thrown into streets anticipates the Orthodox teaching that worldly attachments must be relativized in light of eternal realities.

Orthodox liturgical hymnography echoes this prophetic urgency. The hymns of Great Lent repeatedly call the faithful to recognize the transience of earthly life and the certainty of divine judgment — not as cause for despair but as motivation for repentance and spiritual vigilance.
