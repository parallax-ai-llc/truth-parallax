## The Vision of the New Temple

Ezekiel 40 begins the final section with a detailed vision of a new temple. Orthodox interpretation reads this primarily through Christological and ecclesiological significance. The temple represents God's dwelling with humanity, fulfilled in the Incarnation and continued in the Church.

The Byzantine architectural tradition drew consciously on Ezekiel's vision. The graduated holiness of the precincts corresponds to the Orthodox church building's narthex, nave, and sanctuary. The iconostasis embodies the principle of sacred space that Ezekiel establishes. The faithful enter God's presence by degrees, reflecting the spiritual journey toward theosis.

The angelic guide with the measuring rod recalls the Orthodox tradition of angels as mediators of revelation. The meticulous measurements communicate that God's dwelling is precisely ordered, reflecting the divine Logos who structures all reality. Orthodox theology sees in the temple vision an anticipation of the cosmic temple that all creation is destined to become.
