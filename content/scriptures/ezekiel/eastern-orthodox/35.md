## The Judgment of Edom

Ezekiel 35 pronounces desolation on Mount Seir for its perpetual enmity. Orthodox interpretation reads this within the broader theology of divine justice. The Fathers, particularly Cyril of Alexandria, interpreted Edom's hostility typologically as representing forces that oppose Christ and His Church.

The principle of reciprocal judgment resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the Last Judgment as depicted in iconography. The Judgment icon shows Christ separating sheep from goats, with each person's deeds determining their placement. Ezekiel 35 anticipates this eschatological separation.

The chapter's position between the shepherd discourse and the restoration promise is significant. Orthodox patristic reading understands that before new life can emerge, threats must be decisively overcome. This pattern -- death before resurrection, crucifixion before glorification -- is the paschal logic that structures Orthodox soteriology.
