## The Unsheathed Sword

Ezekiel 21 depicts God's sword drawn for slaughter, Nebuchadnezzar at the crossroads, and the suspension of the Davidic crown "until he comes to whom judgment belongs." Orthodox messianic theology reads the concluding oracle as pointing directly to Christ.

The removal of the turban and crown (21:26) — priestly and royal insignia — signals the end of the old dispensation's institutions. Orthodox Christology understands Christ as the one who unites prophet, priest, and king in his own person, fulfilling what had been divided and corrupted in Israel's history. The liturgical exclamation "Christ is King and Lord" echoes Ezekiel's prophecy of the one to whom authority belongs.

The image of God's sword cutting to right and left has been connected in Orthodox hymnography to the "two-edged sword" of Hebrews 4:12 — the word of God that penetrates and judges the thoughts of the heart. The prophetic sword is not arbitrary violence but divine discernment that separates truth from falsehood.
