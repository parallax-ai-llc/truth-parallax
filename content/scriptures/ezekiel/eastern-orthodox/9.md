## The Seal of the Faithful

Ezekiel 9 describes the marking of those who grieve over Jerusalem's abominations and the execution of all who lack the mark. Orthodox tradition has consistently read the *tau* mark as a prefiguration of the sign of the Cross.

The sacramental signing with the Cross — in baptism, chrismation, and the personal sign of the Cross made throughout Orthodox worship — carries this prophetic resonance. The faithful are sealed and protected through divine judgment by the Cross of Christ. Orthodox baptismal theology specifically invokes this imagery: the newly baptized is sealed (*sphragis*) with the gift of the Holy Spirit through chrismation.

That those who are preserved are identified by their grief over abominations — not merely their abstention from them — is theologically significant. Orthodox spirituality values *penthos* (compunction, godly grief) as a mark of spiritual maturity. It is not enough to avoid sin; genuine holiness involves the capacity to grieve over the sin of others and the brokenness of the world.
