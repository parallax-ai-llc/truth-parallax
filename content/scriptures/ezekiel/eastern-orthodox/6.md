## The Mountains Condemned

Ezekiel 6 directs judgment against the mountains of Israel where idolatrous worship took place. The destruction of altars and the scattering of bones around abandoned shrines represent God's comprehensive dismantling of false worship.

Orthodox commentary reads this within the theology of sacred space. The mountains were not inherently evil — mountains serve as places of divine encounter throughout Scripture (Sinai, Tabor, Zion). What corrupted them was the worship conducted upon them. Orthodox theology distinguishes between the sanctification of space through true worship and its desecration through false worship. The consecration of churches and the blessing of places reflect the positive counterpart to Ezekiel's judgment.

The promise that the scattered remnant will "remember" God among the nations (6:9) anticipates the liturgical anamnesis central to Orthodox worship. The Divine Liturgy is fundamentally an act of remembrance — but a remembrance that makes present what it recalls. The exiles' remembering initiates their restoration.
