## The Nations Under Judgment

Ezekiel 25 pronounces judgment on Ammon, Moab, Edom, and Philistia. Orthodox theology reads these oracles within the patristic framework of God's universal oikonomia -- His providential management of all nations toward the fulfillment of His purposes. The nations are judged not by the Mosaic covenant but by the natural moral law inscribed in creation.

The liturgical tradition of the Orthodox Church incorporates prophetic texts in the cycle of readings for Great Lent and Holy Week. The judgment on neighboring peoples for their hostility toward Israel serves as a mirror for the faithful: if those outside the covenant are held accountable, how much more those who have received the fullness of revelation?

Orthodox patristic commentators, following the Antiochene school represented by Theodoret of Cyrrhus, preferred the historical and moral senses while acknowledging a spiritual dimension. The four nations represent abiding spiritual dispositions -- pride, contempt, vengeance, and enmity -- that threaten the soul's journey toward theosis.
