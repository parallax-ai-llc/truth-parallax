## Signs of the Coming Exile

Ezekiel 12 presents the prophet enacting the departure of the exiles and addresses the people's skepticism about prophetic fulfillment. God declares that His words will no longer be delayed.

Orthodox interpretation draws attention to the prophetic sign-act as embodied proclamation. The prophet does not merely speak about exile; he performs it. This resonates with the Orthodox understanding of liturgy as enacted theology — the Divine Liturgy does not merely describe salvation but performs it, drawing the worshiper into the reality it proclaims.

The people's proverbs dismissing prophecy — "the days grow long and every vision fails" — represent the perennial temptation of *akedia* (spiritual apathy or sloth), one of the eight principal passions in Orthodox ascetic theology. When divine action seems delayed, the spiritual response is not dismissal but patient endurance (*hypomonē*), a virtue cultivated through the liturgical rhythm of the Church year.
