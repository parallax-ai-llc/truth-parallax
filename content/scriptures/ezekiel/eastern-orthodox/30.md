## The Day of Wrath

Ezekiel 30 invokes the "Day of the LORD" against Egypt. Orthodox interpretation reads the Day of the LORD within the liturgical-eschatological framework that shapes the Church's understanding of time. Every Divine Liturgy anticipates the final Day, and the prophetic announcements serve to awaken the faithful to the ultimate seriousness of their choices.

The breaking of Pharaoh's arms illustrates the progressive nature of divine judgment. Orthodox theology, following the Fathers, understands judgment not primarily as external punishment but as the natural consequence of turning away from the source of life. Gregory of Nyssa taught that sin carries its own penalty.

The naming of specific Egyptian cities demonstrates what Orthodox theology calls the concreteness of salvation history. God addresses particular peoples in particular places -- the basis of the Orthodox theology of the local church, each community gathered around its bishop and altar as a complete manifestation of the universal Church.
