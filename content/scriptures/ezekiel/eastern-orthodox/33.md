## The Watchman Restored

Ezekiel 33 reestablishes the prophet as watchman and announces Jerusalem's fall. The chapter's teaching on repentance resonates deeply with Orthodox pastoral theology and the sacrament of confession (exomologesis). The insistence that the wicked who turn will live grounds the Orthodox practice of penance.

The watchman metaphor has been applied to the bishop's office. The bishop, standing in apostolic succession, bears responsibility for warning the faithful. The canons of the ecumenical councils concerning episcopal duties reflect this prophetic model. Orthodox pastoral authority is inherently prophetic.

God's declaration, "I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked" (33:11), is foundational for the Orthodox understanding of God's universal salvific will. The Divine Liturgy of St. Basil prays for "all mankind," and the Orthodox tradition insists that God genuinely desires the salvation of every person. The possibility of repentance is never foreclosed during earthly life.
