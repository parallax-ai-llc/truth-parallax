## Eagles, Cedars, and the Messiah

Ezekiel 17 presents the political allegory of two eagles and a vine, followed by a messianic oracle about a tender sprig planted on a high mountain. Orthodox Christological reading focuses on the concluding oracle (17:22-24), seeing in the planted sprig a prophecy of Christ's coming.

The high mountain where God plants the sprig has been identified in Orthodox tradition with Zion and, typologically, with Golgotha — the place where the Cross was planted and from which the kingdom of God spreads to encompass all nations. The birds nesting in the great cedar represent the nations gathered into the Church, finding shelter under the branches of Christ's kingdom.

The reversal motif — "I bring low the high tree, and make high the low tree" (17:24) — is central to Orthodox theology of the Cross and Resurrection. God's power is revealed in apparent weakness, and the exaltation of Christ comes through humiliation. The Paschal troparion echoes this: "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death."
