## A New Heart Promised

Ezekiel 11 combines judgment on Jerusalem's leaders with the promise of a new heart and spirit for the exiles. God declares Himself "a sanctuary in small measure" wherever the faithful are scattered. The glory departs the city entirely.

Orthodox theology reads the promise of a new heart (11:19-20) through the lens of theosis — the transformation of human nature through participation in the divine life. The removal of the stony heart and its replacement with a heart of flesh is not merely a moral renovation but an ontological transformation — the restoration of the image of God that sin has obscured. This transformation is initiated in baptism and deepened throughout the Christian life through the sacraments, prayer, and ascetic struggle.

The concept of God as "a sanctuary in small measure" among the scattered has been applied to the Orthodox understanding of the local parish and the domestic church (the family). Even far from the great centers of Orthodoxy, wherever the faithful gather around the Eucharist, God is present as a sanctuary.
