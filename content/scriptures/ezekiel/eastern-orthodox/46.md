## Worship and Governance

Ezekiel 46 details the prince's liturgical role and land regulations. Orthodox liturgical theology finds confirmation that the Church's established forms and rhythms reflect divine intention rather than mere convention.

The sabbath and festival calendar resonates with the Orthodox liturgical cycle: the weekly Resurrection on Sunday, the Great Feasts, and the daily services from Vespers through Matins. Time itself is sanctified through regular, ordered worship that draws the community into divine life.

The regulations limiting the prince's power express what Orthodox social teaching calls the dignity of the human person created in God's image. The tradition of monasteries serving as refuges from unjust rulers embodies this: sacred space provides protection against temporal power's abuse.
