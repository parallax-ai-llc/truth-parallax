## Oholah and Oholibah

Ezekiel 23 presents the allegory of two sisters whose political alliances with foreign nations constitute spiritual adultery. Orthodox interpretation reads the political dimension as integral to the spiritual: the pursuit of worldly power at the expense of trust in God is itself a form of idolatry.

Orthodox ecclesiology has applied this principle to the Church's relationship with political power. The *symphonia* model (harmonious cooperation between Church and state) was always understood as precarious — an ideal that could easily degenerate into the Church's co-optation by political interests. The prophetic warning of Ezekiel 23 stands as a permanent reminder that the Church's primary loyalty is to God, not to any political arrangement.

The principle of aggravated culpability — Oholibah sinning worse than Oholah because she witnessed her sister's judgment — resonates with Orthodox teaching on the relationship between knowledge and responsibility. Those who have received the fullness of apostolic faith bear greater accountability than those who have not.
