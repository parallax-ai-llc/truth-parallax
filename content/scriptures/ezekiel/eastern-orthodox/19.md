## The Lament of the Lioness

Ezekiel 19 is a *qinah* (funeral dirge) for the fallen Davidic kings, using the images of a lioness whose cubs are captured and a vine uprooted. Orthodox hymnography draws on the lament tradition to express both grief and hope.

The Orthodox services of Holy Week, particularly the Lamentations of Holy Saturday (*Epitaphios Threnos*), employ the lament genre to mourn the death of Christ — the true king from David's line. Yet within the lament lies the seed of resurrection hope. The genre of mourning is transformed: what appears to be a funeral becomes a passage to glory.

The vine torn up and consumed by fire from its own branch (19:14) — an image of self-destruction — has been applied by Orthodox commentators to the internal corruptions that threaten any community. The fire comes from within, not from external enemies. The Orthodox tradition of self-examination and corporate repentance (exemplified in the Forgiveness Sunday vespers) addresses this danger directly.
