## Sufi Reading

Sa'di's final chapter brings the Sufi reader full circle, demonstrating that authentic mysticism expresses itself not in withdrawal from the world but in the refined conduct (*adab*) of daily life. The maxims gathered here constitute a practical manual for the Sufi who must live among people while maintaining inner orientation toward God. Sa'di's counsel on friendship, speech, generosity, and social relations reflects the Sufi teaching that every human interaction is an opportunity for spiritual practice. The Gulistan closes as it began — in the garden of the heart, where the roses of divine love bloom amid the thorns of worldly existence.
