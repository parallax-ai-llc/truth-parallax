## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reading of this chapter finds in contentment (*qana'a*) a station on the spiritual path where the heart, freed from the endless chase of desire, discovers the sufficiency of the Divine Presence. Sa'di's anecdotes of those who found peace in simplicity illustrate the Sufi teaching that the heart's capacity for God is inversely proportional to its attachment to the world. The contented soul is not passive but profoundly active in its orientation toward the Real (*al-Haqq*). Each story serves as a reminder that the rose garden of the spirit blooms only when the thorns of acquisitiveness are pruned away.
