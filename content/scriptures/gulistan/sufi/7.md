## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reading transforms Sa'di's chapter on education into a treatise on spiritual formation (*tarbiya*). The teacher-student relationship described here mirrors the bond between the Sufi master (*murshid*) and disciple (*murid*), in which the master's role is to polish the mirror of the student's heart until it can reflect divine light. Sa'di's observation that education cannot fundamentally change a corrupt nature echoes the Sufi teaching that the path requires both divine grace and human effort. The stories of wasted education serve as warnings against the spiritual danger of knowledge without practice — the hypocrisy of the scholar who preaches what he does not live.
