## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reads Sa'di's meditations on aging and mortality as teachings on the soul's preparation for its return to the Divine. Old age, in this reading, is not decline but a stripping away of worldly attachments that reveals the essential poverty (*faqr*) of the human condition before God. Sa'di's wry humor about physical decline masks a serious spiritual message: the body's weakening is an invitation to strengthen the spirit. The Sufi masters teach that awareness of death (*muraqaba*) is the beginning of authentic spiritual life, and Sa'di's chapter functions as a sustained exercise in this awareness — a memento mori that is simultaneously a celebration of the eternal life awaiting the purified soul.
