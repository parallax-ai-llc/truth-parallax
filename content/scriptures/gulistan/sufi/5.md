## Sufi Reading

The Sufi tradition reads Sa'di's love poetry through the lens of *ishq* — passionate, all-consuming love that, whether directed at a human beloved or explicitly at the Divine, always points toward the ultimate Beloved. The beauty of youth in Sa'di's verses is understood as a reflection (*tajalli*) of divine beauty manifest in creation. The lover's suffering in separation mirrors the soul's exile from its divine source, and the ecstasy of union foreshadows the mystic's experience of *fana*. Sa'di navigates the characteristic Sufi ambiguity between earthly and heavenly love with consummate artistry, allowing each reader to find the level of meaning suited to their spiritual capacity.
