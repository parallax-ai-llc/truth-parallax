## Sufi Reading

For the Sufi, Sa'di's praise of silence points beyond social prudence to the mystical discipline of inner stillness (*sukun*). True silence is not merely the absence of speech but the quieting of the ego's ceaseless chatter so that the voice of the Beloved may be heard. The Sufi masters teach that the tongue is the last organ to be tamed on the path, and Sa'di's witty demonstrations of the disasters caused by unguarded speech serve as cautionary tales for the seeker. In the deepest Sufi reading, silence is the language of the heart's direct communion with God — the station where words dissolve into pure *dhikr*.
