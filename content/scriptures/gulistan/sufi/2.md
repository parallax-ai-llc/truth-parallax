## Sufi Reading

This chapter is the heart of the Gulistan for the Sufi reader, as Sa'di draws on decades of wandering among dervish communities to portray the spiritual life in all its complexity. The dervishes depicted here embody *faqr* (spiritual poverty) — not destitution but freedom from attachment to worldly things. Sa'di shows both genuine mystics and fraudulent pretenders, teaching the Sufi to distinguish authentic spiritual realization from mere performance. The tales illustrate key Sufi concepts: *tawakkul* (trust in God), *rida* (contentment with divine decree), and the paradox that true wealth lies in wanting nothing. The humor and humanity of these stories reflect the Sufi conviction that holiness need not be solemn.
