## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reads Sa'di's tales of kings as allegories of the soul's governance over itself. The just king mirrors the perfected soul (*insan al-kamil*) who rules the kingdom of the body with wisdom and compassion. When Sa'di describes tyrants overthrown, the Sufi sees the inevitable collapse of the ego (*nafs*) that refuses to submit to divine will. The dervish who advises the king represents the spiritual master (*murshid*) whose counsel can transform the inner kingdom. Sa'di's insistence that kings are servants of the people echoes the Sufi teaching that true authority comes only through *fana* — the annihilation of self-will in divine purpose.
