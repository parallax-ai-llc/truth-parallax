## Shia Reading

The Shia reader encounters Sa'di's meditations on mortality through the lens of Karbala — the supreme paradigm of confrontation with death in the Shia imagination. Imam Husayn's willing embrace of martyrdom transforms the experience of aging and death from passive decline into an active preparation for meeting the Divine. Sa'di's wry observations about physical weakness gain additional poignancy when read alongside the Shia *rawda* (mourning assembly) tradition, which cultivates constant awareness of mortality and the impermanence of worldly power.
