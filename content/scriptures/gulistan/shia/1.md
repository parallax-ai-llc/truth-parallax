## Shia Reading

The Shia reading of Sa'di's tales of kings finds powerful resonance with the Shia narrative of legitimate authority and resistance to tyranny. Stories of unjust rulers overthrown echo the Shia memory of the Umayyad and Abbasid caliphs' oppression of the Ahl al-Bayt, while tales of just kings recall the model of Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib, whose governance of Kufa set the standard for Islamic justice. Sa'di's insistence that kingship is service, not privilege, aligns with Imam Ali's famous statement in the Nahj al-Balagha that the ruler's position is merely a trust held on behalf of the people and God.
