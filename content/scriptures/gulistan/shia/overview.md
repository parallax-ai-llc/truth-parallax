## Overview

The Shia reading of the Gulistan is inseparable from the deep reverence for Persian literary heritage within Shia-majority Iran and its cultural sphere. Sa'di is regarded not only as a supreme poet but as a moral voice whose teachings resonate with the Shia emphasis on justice (*'adl*), compassion, and the defense of the oppressed against tyrannical rulers.

Shia readers find particular resonance in Sa'di's stories of unjust kings brought low and righteous individuals vindicated — themes that echo the Shia narrative of the Ahl al-Bayt's suffering at the hands of oppressive caliphs. The Gulistan's celebration of the dervish who speaks truth to power recalls the model of Imam Husayn's stand at Karbala. In modern Iran, Sa'di's verses are woven into daily speech, public ceremonies, and the educational curriculum, making the Gulistan a living scripture of Iranian Shia cultural identity.
