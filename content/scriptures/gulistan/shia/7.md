## Shia Reading

The Shia reading of Sa'di's chapter on education is informed by the central Shia doctrine that the Imam is the supreme teacher (*mu'allim*) of humanity, possessing divinely granted knowledge (*'ilm ladunni*) that surpasses ordinary learning. Sa'di's observations about the limits of education for corrupt natures find theological resonance in the Shia distinction between exoteric knowledge and the esoteric understanding (*batin*) accessible only through the guidance of the Imam. The teacher-student relationships depicted here mirror the chain of spiritual transmission from the Prophet through the Imams to their followers.
