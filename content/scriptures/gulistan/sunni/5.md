## Sunni Reading

The Sunni reading of Sa'di's love poetry navigates the tension between the ghazal tradition's celebration of beauty and love and the Sunni emphasis on modesty and moral boundaries. Sunni scholars have traditionally read Sa'di's love verses as either metaphorical expressions of divine love or as honest observations of human nature that serve moral instruction by showing both the beauty and the dangers of passion. Sa'di's sophisticated treatment allows Sunni readers to appreciate the literary artistry while drawing ethical lessons about the proper channeling of human desire within the bounds of *shari'a*.
