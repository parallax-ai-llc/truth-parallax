## Sunni Reading

Sunni scholars find in Sa'di's chapter on silence a literary elaboration of numerous Prophetic *hadith* on the dangers of the tongue. The Prophet's teaching that 'whoever believes in God and the Last Day should speak good or remain silent' provides the theological anchor for Sa'di's witty demonstrations of the disasters caused by careless speech. The Sunni reading emphasizes the social dimension of this virtue: silence prevents *fitna* (discord), preserves communal harmony, and reflects the inner discipline (*mujahadat al-nafs*) that Islam demands of every believer.
