## Overview

The Sunni tradition has long embraced the Gulistan as a masterpiece of *adab* literature — the genre of refined conduct, moral instruction, and literary elegance that occupies a central place in classical Islamic education. In Sunni madrasas from Anatolia to South Asia, the Gulistan served for centuries as a primary text for teaching Persian language, ethical reasoning, and the art of eloquent expression.

Sunni scholars value Sa'di's alignment of practical wisdom with Qur'anic ethics and Prophetic *sunnah*. His stories consistently reward virtue, patience, and justice while exposing the consequences of tyranny, pride, and hypocrisy — themes consonant with Sunni moral theology. The Gulistan's influence on Ottoman, Mughal, and Central Asian Sunni literary cultures has been immense, shaping the ethical imagination of generations of Muslim scholars, administrators, and poets.
