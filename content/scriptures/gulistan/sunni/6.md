## Sunni Reading

The Sunni reading of this chapter draws on the Qur'anic emphasis on mortality and the afterlife (*akhira*) as the ultimate horizon of human life. Sa'di's reflections on aging and death serve as reminders of the Prophetic teaching to 'remember often the destroyer of pleasures — death.' Sunni scholars use these stories to teach *sabr* (patience) in the face of physical decline and *shukr* (gratitude) for the blessings of health and youth. The chapter reinforces the Sunni theological conviction that every stage of life is a divine decree (*qadar*) to be received with faith and equanimity.
