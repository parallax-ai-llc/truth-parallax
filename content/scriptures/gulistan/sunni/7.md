## Sunni Reading

The Sunni tradition finds in Sa'di's chapter on education a complement to the Islamic emphasis on the pursuit of knowledge (*'ilm*) as a religious obligation. The Prophet's injunction to 'seek knowledge even unto China' establishes the framework within which Sa'di's stories of successful and failed education are read. Sunni pedagogical theory distinguishes between *ta'lim* (instruction in knowledge) and *tarbiya* (moral formation), and Sa'di's chapter engages both dimensions. His observation that corrupt natures resist education resonates with theological discussions about human *fitrah* (innate disposition) and the role of environment and effort in moral development.
