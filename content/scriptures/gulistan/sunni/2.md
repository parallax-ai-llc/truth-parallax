## Sunni Reading

The Sunni reading of this chapter appreciates Sa'di's portrayal of ascetics and dervishes within a framework compatible with mainstream Sunni piety. Sa'di's balanced presentation — celebrating genuine piety while satirizing false asceticism — aligns with the Sunni tradition's nuanced acceptance of Sufism as a dimension of Islamic practice when it remains grounded in *shari'a*. The dervishes praised here embody *zuhd* (world-renunciation) and *tawakkul* (trust in God), virtues affirmed by the Qur'an and *sunnah*. Sa'di's humor in exposing pretenders serves the Sunni concern for authenticity in religious practice.
