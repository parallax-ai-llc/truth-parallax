## Sunni Reading

The Sunni tradition reads Sa'di's chapter on kings through the lens of *siyasa shar'iyya* — governance in accordance with Islamic law and Prophetic example. Sa'di's stories of just and unjust rulers illustrate principles found in works of Islamic political theory from al-Mawardi to Ibn Khaldun: that legitimate authority requires justice, consultation (*shura*), and accountability before God. The Sunni reader finds in Sa'di's tales practical illustrations of hadith teachings on the responsibilities of leadership, including the Prophet's warning that every shepherd will be asked about his flock. These stories served as mirrors for princes (*nasihat al-muluk*) throughout the Ottoman and Mughal empires.
