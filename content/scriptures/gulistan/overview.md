## Overview

The Gulistan ("Rose Garden") is one of the most celebrated works of Persian literature, composed by the poet Sa'di Shirazi in 1258 CE. Written in a mixture of prose and verse (*masnavi* and *ghazal*), the Gulistan weaves together moral anecdotes, practical wisdom, and lyrical beauty into a tapestry that has been read, memorized, and quoted across the Islamic world and beyond for nearly eight centuries.

## Structure

The Gulistan is organized into eight chapters (*bab*), each devoted to a particular theme of ethical and practical life:

1. **On the Conduct of Kings** — Stories illustrating just and unjust rulership
2. **On the Morals of Dervishes** — Tales of Sufi ascetics and their spiritual qualities
3. **On the Excellence of Contentment** — Praise of simplicity and inner sufficiency
4. **On the Advantages of Silence** — Wisdom about the power of restraint in speech
5. **On Love and Youth** — Reflections on passion, beauty, and the trials of love
6. **On Weakness and Old Age** — Meditations on mortality and the passage of time
7. **On the Effects of Education** — The transformative power of learning and upbringing
8. **On Rules for Conduct in Life** — Practical maxims for navigating human society

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Sufi**: A masterwork of Sufi-inflected ethics blending mystical insight with practical wisdom
- **Sunni**: Widely studied in Sunni madrasas as a model of *adab* (refined conduct) and moral instruction
- **Shia**: Treasured in the Persian-speaking Shia world as a crown jewel of Iranian literary heritage
- **Academic**: A foundational text for the study of Persian prose, medieval Islamic ethics, and comparative literature
- **Comparative**: Offers rich parallels with wisdom literature across cultures, from Aesop to Confucius
- **Interfaith**: Its universal moral insights have made it one of the most translated works from the Islamic world

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Practical Ethics | Moral instruction through vivid anecdotes and memorable aphorisms |
| Just Rulership | The responsibilities and temptations of power |

| Universal Wisdom | Insights that transcend cultural and religious boundaries |
