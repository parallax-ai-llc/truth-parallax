## On Weakness and Old Age

**1.** I remember that in the days of my youth I used to rise in the night and practice devotion and obedience to God.

**2.** One night I was sitting with my father and keeping awake all night and holding the blessed Quran in my lap, while the people around us were asleep.

**3.** I said to my father: "Not one of these lifts his head to perform a prayer. They are so fast asleep that thou wouldst say they are dead."

**4.** My father replied: "Life of thy father! It were better if thou also wert asleep, rather than slandering the people."

**5.** The pretender sees nothing but himself, for he has a veil of conceit before his eyes. If he possessed the eye of God, he would see that no one is more feeble than himself.

**6.** An old man was asked: "Why do you not take a wife?" He replied: "I have no desire for an old woman." They said: "Then take a young woman, for you have the means." He answered: "Since I, who am old, have no desire for an old woman, how can a young woman desire me, who am old?"

**7.** When age arrives, the hair grows white, the back bends, the eyes dim, the ears grow deaf, the teeth fall, and the breath shortens.

**8.** But the wise man does not grieve at old age, for the soul grows young as the body grows old.

**9.** The candle that has burned all night is not diminished thereby, for its purpose was to give light, and it has fulfilled its purpose.

**10.** They asked a sage: "What is old age?" He replied: "The morning after the night of youth."

**11.** "Youth is like the spring: beautiful but brief. Old age is like the autumn: less beautiful but rich with harvest."

**12.** A man of ninety was asked: "What have you learned in all your years?" He replied: "That the world is not worth the trouble of ambition."

**13.** "In youth, I thought the world was a garden prepared for my delight. In old age, I see it is a bridge to be crossed."

**14.** Be not grieved at the approach of age, for each year that passes plants a seed of wisdom that youth could not sow.

**15.** The body is the husk, the soul is the grain. The husk withers, but the grain endures.
