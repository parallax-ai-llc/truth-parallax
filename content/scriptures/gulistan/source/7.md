## On the Effects of Education

**1.** A vizier had a foolish son. He sent the boy to a learned man and said: "Perhaps you can give this boy an education and make him wise."

**2.** The learned man took pains with the boy for a long time but to no effect. He sent a message to the father saying: "Your son will never become wise, and he has made me foolish."

**3.** When a nature is original, education works upon it. But no amount of polishing will improve iron to the quality of gold.

**4.** Take a dog to the sea of Oman: it will still be a dog. But rain from heaven that falls upon the oyster becomes a pearl.

**5.** An ass that has carried books to Baghdad is still an ass when it arrives.

**6.** Nevertheless, do not abandon education, for the seed that falls upon stony ground, though it perish, enriches the soil for the next seed.

**7.** A father said to his son: "Take care, my child, for I have not left you a fortune. But I have given you an education, which is better than gold."

**8.** "Gold will be spent and leave you poor. But education remains with you and increases with age."

**9.** "The learned man is like the tree that produces fruit even when the gardener is gone. The ignorant man is like the field that produces nothing without constant labor."

**10.** A youth was a constant source of annoyance to his father. One day the father cast him out of the house. The mother wept.

**11.** The father said: "When the gardener sees that a branch of the tree has become crooked, he straightens it while it is young, for once it has grown thick, it cannot be made straight even by fire."

**12.** A disciple asked his master: "What is the essence of good education?" The master replied: "To learn what you do not know, and to practice what you know."

**13.** "Knowledge without practice is like a bee without honey. Practice without knowledge is like a boat without a rudder."

**14.** Whoever acquires knowledge and does not practice it resembles him who ploughs his field but does not sow seed.

**15.** The essence of education is not the filling of a vessel but the kindling of a fire. And the fire, once kindled, gives light to all who approach it.
