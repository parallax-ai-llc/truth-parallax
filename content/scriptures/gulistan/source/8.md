## On Rules for Conduct in Life

**1.** A man of learning who does not act according to his knowledge is like a bee that gives no honey.

**2.** All the sons of Adam are members of one body, for in their creation they are of one essence.

**3.** When the conditions of life bring suffering to one member, the other members find no rest.

**4.** Thou who art unconcerned by the suffering of others dost not deserve to be called a human being.

**5.** A sage was asked: "With all the trees that God Most High has created, lofty and shady, none is called free except the cypress, which has no fruit. What is the wisdom in this?"

**6.** He replied: "Every tree has a fruit and a season. When the season comes it is fresh, and when the season goes it is withered. But the cypress is evergreen—and the truly free man is the same in every season."

**7.** Set not thy heart upon the goods of this transitory world, for the Tigris will continue to flow through Baghdad after the Caliphs have ceased to exist.

**8.** If thy hand has plenty, be generous as the date tree. If it affords nothing to give, be a free man like the cypress.

**9.** A king said to a sage: "What is better than prayer?" The sage said: "For you, to remain asleep until midday, that for this one interval you may not afflict mankind."

**10.** Be good to the people for the sake of God, or for the sake of your own soul. Do not be good for show, for that is the worst form of evil.

**11.** The world, O my brother, will not remain with anyone. Bind the heart to the Creator, for this is enough.

**12.** Rely not upon possessions and the things of this life, for many a one with silver has perished in the night. To the wise, a single loaf is worth more than a treasury guarded by fear.

**13.** Do not struggle excessively for worldly goods, for all that is assigned to you will come in due time. And do not be idle, for the harvest does not come to the man who does not sow.

**14.** It is a condition of wisdom that a man should not make himself uneasy about that which has not yet come to pass.

**15.** They asked Luqman: "From whom did you learn wisdom?" He replied: "From the blind, who do not advance a step until they have tested the ground before them."

**16.** Sow kindness and you shall reap friendship. Plant love and you shall gather joy.

**17.** If you desire that God be merciful to you, be merciful to His creatures. This is the final wisdom, and the whole of the law.
