## The Manners of Kings

**1.** I have heard that a king of former times gave orders to put an innocent man to death. The poor wretch began to abuse the king in his own tongue during the last moments of his life.

**2.** It is a common saying that a man at the point of death speaks truth. As the proverb goes: "What can a man say who has nothing left to lose?"

**3.** The king asked: "What is he saying?" One of the viziers, a well-meaning man, replied: "O my lord, he says: 'And those who curb their anger and forgive men; God loves those who do good.'"

**4.** The king was moved to pity and spared the man's life. Another vizier, the enemy of the first, said: "Men of our rank ought to speak nothing but the truth in the presence of kings. That man was cursing the king and speaking improperly."

**5.** The king frowned at this remark and said: "That falsehood which he spoke was more acceptable to me than this truth which thou hast told, for the former had a good intention and the latter was spoken with malice."

**6.** And the wise have said: A falsehood mixed with good intention is preferable to a truth intended to cause harm.

**7.** A king who permits himself to be unjust will destroy the foundations of the world. Be not unconcerned regarding the oppression of the people, for one day the wall will fall upon the head of the oppressor.

**8.** Ten dervishes may sleep beneath one blanket, but two kings cannot be accommodated in a single kingdom.

**9.** A devout man who eats half a loaf will give the other half to the hungry; a king who conquers one kingdom stretches his hand to seize another.

**10.** An unjust king asked a holy man: "What is more excellent than prayer?" The holy man replied: "For you to remain asleep until noon, so that for this half of the day at least you may not afflict the people."

**11.** A king who stretches forth his hand upon the possessions of his subjects will cut off the hands of his own fortune. The king is the shepherd; if he lets the wolf devour the sheep, how shall the flock flourish?

**12.** Whosoever oppresses the peasants and destroys their dwellings will be the cause of the decline of the state.

**13.** It is related that one of the kings of Persia had extended the hand of tyranny over the people and had begun to diminish their property.

**14.** They began to emigrate to foreign countries, the revenue of the kingdom decreased, the treasury grew empty, and the enemies took heart.

**15.** If thou desirest that God should bestow on thee His grace, be gracious to His creation, for they too are His family.

**16.** He is no king who oppresses the people; the shepherd is to defend the sheep, not devour them.
