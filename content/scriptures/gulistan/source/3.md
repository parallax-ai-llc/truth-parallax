## On the Excellence of Contentment

**1.** A wealthy merchant once lost a caravan of goods upon the sea. He was plunged into grief and told his sorrows to a sage.

**2.** The sage said: "Grieve not. Better to have lost the cargo than to have been drowned with it."

**3.** If man could be satisfied with his daily bread, no one would lord it over another. But the hawk submits to captivity for the sake of a morsel of meat.

**4.** I once saw a rich man who was as mean-spirited as a beggar. He had the wealth of Qarun but the heart of a pauper.

**5.** He kept his gold in a chest and his heart in a prison. He fed neither himself nor others. He lived like the dog guarding the butcher's door: too thin from hunger to run, too greedy to leave.

**6.** The contented man is rich without possessions, as the discontented man is poor with all the riches of the world.

**7.** He who desires much finds that nothing suffices. He who desires little finds that everything is abundance.

**8.** An ascetic once entered the court of a sultan. The sultan said: "Ask something of me." The ascetic replied: "I ask nothing of those who ask of their servants."

**9.** The sultan said: "Give me counsel." The ascetic replied: "Take heed that the treasury of the world has passed from hand to hand, and it will pass from yours too."

**10.** "This world is a carcass, and he who seeks it is a dog. The lion does not eat the leavings of the wolf."

**11.** "Be content with a little, for the belly holds only so much. The grave holds only so much. The eye of man is either satisfied by contentment or filled with the dust of the grave."

**12.** They said to a tortoise: "Why do you carry your house upon your back?" The tortoise replied: "Better my own poor house than the palace of another."

**13.** The bee gathers honey from a thousand flowers but is content to live in a small hole. The elephant eats a forest and is never satisfied.

**14.** The dervish who asks for nothing possesses everything, for he has freed himself from the slavery of want.

**15.** Make your wants few, and your life will be a perpetual feast.
