## The Morals of Dervishes

**1.** A dervish who had acquired inner peace through the practice of contentment was asked: "Why do you sit so tranquil while the world is in turmoil?"

**2.** He replied: "I have nothing and therefore I fear nothing. The man who possesses treasures must guard them day and night; I sleep soundly."

**3.** I have heard of a man of God who was in want but wore the garment of contentment and endured the fire of poverty with patience.

**4.** A certain man said to him by way of comforting him: "Is it not a pity that such a man as thou should be thus helpless, while others enjoy abundance?"

**5.** The dervish replied: "Be silent. It is better to die in content than to live in greed. I am in the prison of contentment, not in the dungeon of desire."

**6.** Two dervishes from Khorasan traveled together. One was so weak that he ate only every other night; the other was so robust that he ate three times a day.

**7.** At the gate of a certain town, they were arrested on suspicion of being spies. They were put in a cell with a wall between them.

**8.** After two weeks, the wall was broken down and it was found that the one who ate much had perished, and the one who ate little had survived.

**9.** They said: "How strange! The strong one died and the weak one lived." A sage replied: "It would have been strange had the opposite occurred. The one had too much belly, while the other had a store of patience."

**10.** When the pure-hearted dervish eats but little, what is left he gives to the poor. When the rich man hoards his wealth, even if he eats all day, he leaves nothing for others.

**11.** The wealth of the generous is like the well: the more water you draw from it, the more flows in; but the wealth of the miser is like a grave—it grows only darker.

**12.** A disciple said to his master: "What must I do? The people trouble me, and their frequent visits prevent my devotions."

**13.** The master replied: "Lend something to each of them, and ask something from each of them. After this they will not come near thee again."

**14.** When the hand is generous, the world is a friend. When the hand is closed, the world turns away.

**15.** Do not be proud of your riches in the night, for the morning may find you in poverty. Many have gone to sleep in silk and woken on the bare earth.
