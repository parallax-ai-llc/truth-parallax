## On the Advantages of Silence

**1.** A wise man was asked: "Of the many celebrated trees that the Almighty has created lofty and shady, none is called free except the cypress, which bears no fruit. What is the mystery of this?"

**2.** He replied: "Every tree has its appointed fruit and season. During the season it flourishes, and out of season it withers. But the cypress is always green, and this quality belongs to the truly free."

**3.** "Set not thy heart upon what passes away, for the Tigris will still flow through Baghdad after the race of Caliphs is extinct."

**4.** "If thy hand has plenty, be liberal as the date-tree; but if it affords nothing to give away, be a free man, like the cypress."

**5.** A learned man who does not speak is like a bee without honey, and a fool who speaks incessantly is like a drum full of noise and empty of substance.

**6.** Speak not every thought that comes to mind. It takes one moment to light a fire and a lifetime to put it out.

**7.** I have regretted my speech many times, but my silence—never.

**8.** A sage was asked: "Who taught you wisdom?" He replied: "The blind, who never put a foot upon the ground without testing the way with their staffs."

**9.** A man who considers his words before speaking will not be put to shame. The thread must be made fine before it can pass through the eye of the needle.

**10.** Whenever thou art in doubt whether to speak or remain silent, choose silence.

**11.** A talkative man came to a dervish and chattered away without ceasing. At last the dervish said: "O man, if what thou sayest is useful, speak a little that we may hear it. If it is useless, be silent, and we shall be spared."

**12.** The tongue is a lion crouching in its den. If you do not keep it chained, it will devour you.

**13.** Four things are not retrieved once they have gone: the spoken word, the spent arrow, the past life, and the neglected opportunity.

**14.** Speak with thought, and you will have no need to repent. Build your house with care, and you will not need to rebuild it.

**15.** The wound inflicted by a sword heals in time. The wound inflicted by a word festers forever.
