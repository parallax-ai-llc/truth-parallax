## On Love and Youth

**1.** They tell of a man of Baghdad who was deeply in love. He had given his heart entirely, and no room was left for reason.

**2.** His beloved was of uncommon beauty. She walked with the grace of the cypress and spoke with the sweetness of sugar.

**3.** One night, the lover stood beneath her window and whispered: "O moon-faced one, do you sleep while I stand in the rain and weep?"

**4.** She replied from within: "O foolish one, do not imagine that I am as infatuated with you as you are with me. Go to sleep."

**5.** A man once asked a lover: "What is the difference between a true friend and a flattering companion?" The lover replied: "The flatterer brings you flowers when the sun is shining. The true friend brings you a lamp when the night is dark."

**6.** The beauty of the beloved is not the cause of love—it is the eye of the lover that discovers beauty. To the beetle, no perfume is sweeter than dung.

**7.** He who is intoxicated with wine will be sober by morning; but he who is intoxicated with the beloved will not be sober until the grave.

**8.** Love is not a thing to be taught—it is a pearl formed in the ocean of the heart, and no one taught the ocean how to make pearls.

**9.** A youth said to his father: "No wise man has ever said such-and-such a thing." His father replied: "Be wise first, my son, and then say what you please."

**10.** The fire of love makes the young old before their time, and makes the old young again. It has no respect for years.

**11.** When the heart is taken, the eyes follow. Where the heart goes, the body goes after it, stumbling like a blind man.

**12.** I asked a wise man: "What is the sign of a true lover?" He said: "That he gives up everything and gains nothing, and is happy still."

**13.** They asked Majnun: "Why do you pour dust upon your head?" He replied: "So that perhaps the wind may carry the dust to the dwelling of Layla."

**14.** Love has nothing to do with the five senses and the six directions: its goal is only the Beloved. The heart which does not love is a stone.

**15.** Without love, the days are a burden; with love, even a moment is an eternity of joy.
