## Interfaith Reading

Sa'di's practical maxims reveal the extensive common moral ground that exists across religious traditions. The Golden Rule — treat others as you wish to be treated — appears in every major tradition, and Sa'di's specific applications of this principle in areas such as friendship, hospitality, truthfulness, and generosity find parallels in Jewish, Christian, Buddhist, Hindu, and Confucian ethical teaching. The Gulistan's closing chapter demonstrates that interfaith dialogue need not remain at the level of theological abstraction but can engage the practical moral questions that all human beings face in daily life.
