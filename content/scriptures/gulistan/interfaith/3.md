## Interfaith Reading

Interfaith dialogue finds in Sa'di's praise of contentment a value affirmed across religious traditions: the conviction that material sufficiency, rather than accumulation, opens the path to spiritual flourishing. Buddhist simplicity, Franciscan poverty, Jewish *histapkut*, Hindu *aparigraha* (non-possessiveness), and the Quaker testimony of simplicity all converge with Sa'di's teaching. In an age of environmental crisis driven by overconsumption, this shared wisdom acquires urgent contemporary relevance as a resource for interfaith ecological ethics.
