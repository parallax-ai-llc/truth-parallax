## Interfaith Reading

Sa'di's meditation on silence creates space for interfaith exploration of contemplative practices that transcend doctrinal differences. Silent prayer, meditation, and contemplation constitute a meeting point for practitioners of different faiths who discover that the experience of interior silence — however differently theorized — shares a common phenomenology. Interfaith retreat movements that bring together practitioners of different traditions in shared silence draw, whether consciously or not, on the same wisdom that Sa'di articulates in this chapter.
