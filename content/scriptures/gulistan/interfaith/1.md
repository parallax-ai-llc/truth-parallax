## Interfaith Reading

Interfaith reading of Sa'di's tales of kings reveals a universal ethical concern for just governance that transcends religious boundaries. The prophetic tradition of speaking truth to power — exemplified by Moses before Pharaoh, Nathan before David, Muhammad before the Quraysh, and the Buddhist concept of the *chakravartin* (righteous ruler) — finds eloquent expression in Sa'di's stories. Participants in interfaith dialogue discover that their traditions share remarkably similar convictions about the accountability of rulers, the rights of the governed, and the corrosive effects of unchecked power.
