## Interfaith Reading

Sa'di's portraits of genuine and fraudulent dervishes open interfaith conversations about the nature of authentic spirituality across traditions. Every major religion has developed traditions of voluntary simplicity and renunciation — Christian monasticism, Buddhist sangha life, Hindu sannyasa, Jewish Nazirite vows — and every tradition has also grappled with the problem of spiritual pretension. Sa'di's balanced treatment provides common ground for interfaith participants to discuss what constitutes genuine spiritual commitment and how communities can distinguish authentic practice from mere performance.
