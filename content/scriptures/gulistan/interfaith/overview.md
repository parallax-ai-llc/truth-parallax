## Overview

The Gulistan holds a unique place in interfaith dialogue, largely because of Sa'di's famous verse inscribed at the entrance of the United Nations: "Human beings are members of a whole / In creation of one essence and soul." This couplet encapsulates the Gulistan's spirit of universal moral concern that transcends religious boundaries.

Interfaith readers find in the Gulistan a model for ethical discourse that is rooted in a specific tradition (Islamic, Sufi, Persian) yet speaks to universal human experience. Sa'di's stories feature characters of many faiths and none, and his moral judgments rest on shared human values — honesty, compassion, justice, humility — rather than sectarian criteria. The Gulistan thus serves as a bridge text in interfaith education, demonstrating that deep rootedness in one tradition need not preclude genuine moral universalism.
