## Interfaith Reading

Sa'di's reflections on aging and mortality create space for interfaith conversation about how different traditions prepare their adherents for death and support them through the vulnerabilities of old age. All major religions offer teachings on mortality — the Christian *ars moriendi*, the Tibetan *Bardo Thodol*, the Jewish practice of *hesped* and *shiva*, and Islamic eschatology — and Sa'di's humanistic approach provides common ground for discussing these diverse perspectives. In an aging global society, this interfaith conversation about the dignity and meaning of the last stage of life acquires particular urgency.
