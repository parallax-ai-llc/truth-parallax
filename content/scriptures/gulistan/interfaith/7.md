## Interfaith Reading

Sa'di's chapter on education invites interfaith reflection on how different traditions transmit wisdom across generations. The *madrasa*, *yeshiva*, Christian seminary, Buddhist monastery, and Hindu *gurukul* all represent institutional responses to the universal human challenge of moral and intellectual formation. Sa'di's observations about the limits and possibilities of education resonate across traditions, opening conversations about the relationship between intellectual knowledge and spiritual transformation, and about how interfaith education itself can contribute to mutual understanding.
