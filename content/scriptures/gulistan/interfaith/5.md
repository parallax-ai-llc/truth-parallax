## Interfaith Reading

Sa'di's love poetry opens interfaith dialogue about the role of love — both human and divine — in the spiritual life. The mystical traditions of all major religions use the language of love to describe the soul's relationship with the Ultimate: Sufi *ishq*, Christian *agape*, Jewish *devekut*, Hindu *bhakti*, and Buddhist *karuna* each articulate distinctive but resonant visions of love as the fundamental spiritual force. Sa'di's poetry, with its characteristic ambiguity between human and divine love, provides a starting point for exploring how different traditions negotiate the relationship between earthly affection and transcendent aspiration.
