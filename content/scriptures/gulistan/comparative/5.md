## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's love poetry participates in a cross-cultural tradition of using erotic imagery to express spiritual aspiration. Plato's *Symposium*, the biblical Song of Songs, the Hindu *Gita Govinda*, the troubadour *fin'amors*, and Rumi's love lyrics all navigate the boundary between human and divine love with varying degrees of ambiguity. Comparative analysis examines how different traditions negotiate the relationship between earthly passion and spiritual aspiration — whether they are understood as analogous, continuous, or opposed — and how Sa'di's characteristically Persian approach differs from and resembles these parallel traditions.
