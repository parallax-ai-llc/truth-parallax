## Comparative Reading

The value Sa'di places on silence resonates across religious and philosophical traditions: Quaker worship in gathered silence, Buddhist *noble silence* during meditation retreats, the Pythagorean discipline of years-long silence for initiates, and the Jewish mystical tradition of *hitbodedut*. Comparative study reveals that while the specific motivations differ — mystical receptivity, social prudence, spiritual discipline, or philosophical insight — the recognition of silence as a spiritual practice and ethical virtue is remarkably consistent across cultures.
