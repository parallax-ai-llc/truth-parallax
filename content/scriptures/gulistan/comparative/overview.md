## Overview

Comparative study of the Gulistan reveals its place within a global tradition of wisdom literature that includes the Panchatantra, Aesop's Fables, the Analects of Confucius, and the Book of Proverbs. Sa'di's method of teaching through brief, memorable stories with embedded moral lessons follows a pattern found across civilizations, suggesting both direct literary influence and independent convergence on effective pedagogical forms.

Scholars trace the migration of specific tale-types between the Gulistan and European, Indian, and Chinese narrative traditions. The Gulistan was among the first Persian texts translated into European languages, influencing Enlightenment thinkers such as Voltaire and Goethe, who found in Sa'di a kindred spirit of universal humanism.
