## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's reflections on education find parallels in Confucian *xiu shen* (self-cultivation), Plato's educational program in the *Republic*, Buddhist monastic training, and the Jewish emphasis on *Torah* study as moral formation. The question of whether education can transform human nature — or merely polish what is already present — recurs across traditions, with Sa'di's pragmatic skepticism about the limits of education echoed by Confucius's distinction between those born with knowledge and those who acquire it through effort, and by the Buddhist teaching that different beings have different capacities for awakening.
