## Comparative Reading

This chapter invites comparison with ascetic traditions across world religions: the Desert Fathers and Mothers of Christianity, Buddhist *bhikkhu* communities, Hindu *sannyasa*, and Daoist hermits. Sa'di's balanced treatment of asceticism — praising genuine spiritual poverty while satirizing performative piety — finds parallels in the Buddhist distinction between authentic and mere formal renunciation, the Christian tradition's ambivalence about ostentatious asceticism, and the Daoist critique of artificial virtue. Across traditions, the authentic renunciant is characterized by inner freedom rather than external display.
