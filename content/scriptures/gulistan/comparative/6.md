## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's meditations on aging and mortality invite comparison with Ecclesiastes' 'vanity of vanities,' the Buddhist teaching of *anicca* (impermanence), the Stoic *memento mori* tradition, and the Hindu concept of *vairagya* (dispassion) arising from awareness of life's transience. Across traditions, confrontation with mortality serves as a catalyst for spiritual awakening and a corrective to the illusion of permanence. Sa'di's distinctive contribution is his combination of philosophical gravity with irreverent humor — a tonal balance rarely achieved in other traditions' mortality literature.
