## Comparative Reading

Sa'di's praise of contentment finds rich parallels across philosophical and religious traditions. Stoic *ataraxia*, Buddhist non-attachment, Daoist *wu-wei*, Epicurean simplicity, and Jewish *histapkut* all converge on the insight that true well-being requires liberation from excessive desire. Comparative analysis reveals both shared wisdom and important differences: while the Stoic seeks contentment through rational self-mastery and the Buddhist through the realization of *anatta*, Sa'di grounds contentment in trust in divine providence — a distinctively theistic framing that he shares with Jewish and Christian contemplative traditions.
