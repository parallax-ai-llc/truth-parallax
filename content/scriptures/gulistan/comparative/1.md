## Comparative Reading

Comparative analysis places Sa'di's tales of kings alongside other wisdom traditions' reflections on governance: Kautilya's Arthashastra, Confucius's Analects on virtuous rulership, Machiavelli's Prince, and the biblical Proverbs of Solomon. While each tradition emphasizes different aspects — dharmic duty in India, *ren* in China, *virtù* in Renaissance Italy — all share the fundamental insight that the quality of governance determines the well-being of the governed. Sa'di's distinctive contribution is his combination of moral idealism with unflinching realism about human nature and political power.
