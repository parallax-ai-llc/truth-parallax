## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze this chapter as a contribution to the medieval Islamic discourse on authentic versus fraudulent asceticism — a concern shared by jurists, Sufis, and literary authors alike. The chapter provides valuable social-historical evidence about dervish communities, mendicant practices, and the relationship between ascetics and urban society in 13th-century Persia. Literary critics examine Sa'di's narrative techniques, noting how he balances admiration and satire to create a nuanced portrait of the Sufi milieu that resists simple categorization as either hagiography or critique.
