## Academic Reading

Scholars note the productive paradox of a chapter that uses elaborate rhetoric to praise silence — a tension Sa'di exploits with characteristic wit. Linguistic and rhetorical analysis reveals how Sa'di constructs his arguments through exemplary anecdotes that function as speech acts, performing the very judgment they narrate. The chapter is studied in relation to the broader Islamic tradition of reflection on the ethics of speech, from Qur'anic injunctions to al-Ghazali's influential treatment in the Ihya 'Ulum al-Din.
