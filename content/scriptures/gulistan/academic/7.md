## Academic Reading

This chapter is studied as a contribution to the medieval Islamic debate on education, *fitrah* (innate nature), and the limits of moral formation. Scholars situate Sa'di's observations within the broader tradition of Islamic educational theory, from Ibn Sina's treatises on pedagogy to al-Ghazali's model of spiritual formation. The chapter's anecdotes are analyzed as literary embodiments of philosophical arguments about the relative weight of nature and nurture — a debate that crosses cultural boundaries and continues in contemporary educational psychology.
