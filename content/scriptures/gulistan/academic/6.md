## Academic Reading

Scholars read this chapter in the context of Sa'di's own advanced age at the time of the Gulistan's composition, examining how the text negotiates between autobiographical reflection and literary convention. The chapter is analyzed as a contribution to the philosophical and literary discourse on mortality that spans cultures, with particular attention to Sa'di's debts to Arabic *mawt* poetry and the Persian elegiac tradition. The humor that pervades even the most somber reflections is studied as a rhetorical strategy for engaging readers with uncomfortable truths.
