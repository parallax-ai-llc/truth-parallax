## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the final chapter examines its function as a gnomological collection — a gathering of maxims and aphorisms that serves as the Gulistan's capstone. Scholars study Sa'di's compilation techniques, noting how he arranges diverse materials into a coherent ethical vision through thematic clustering and rhetorical variation. The chapter is read in relation to the broader *adab* tradition of practical wisdom literature, from the Arabic *hikam* collections to the European aphoristic tradition, revealing both shared strategies and distinctive features of Sa'di's approach.
