## Overview

The Gnostic Apocalypse of Peter is studied for its distinctive contribution to early Christian docetism and its evidence of inter-Christian polemic. The double-vision scene — Peter seeing both a suffering figure and a laughing Savior — is unique in early Christian literature and has been analyzed as a literary device for communicating the docetic message. Scholars have debated the text's relationship to the Second Treatise of the Great Seth, which presents a similar docetic Christology but from Christ's rather than Peter's perspective. The anti-orthodox polemic in the final section provides evidence of the social tensions between Gnostic and proto-orthodox communities.
