## Overview

For Christian readers, the Apocalypse of Peter challenges the centrality of the cross in Christian theology by presenting Peter — the 'rock' of the church — as receiving a vision that contradicts the orthodox understanding of the crucifixion. The use of Peter as the visionary protagonist is theologically provocative: if Peter himself saw the laughing Christ, then the proto-orthodox tradition that claims Peter's authority is based on a misunderstanding. The text thus turns the orthodox appeal to apostolic authority against itself.
