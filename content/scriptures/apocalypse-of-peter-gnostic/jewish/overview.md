## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Apocalypse of Peter a Gnostic transformation of Jewish prophetic vision traditions. The genre of visionary revelation — a human figure granted supernatural sight to perceive hidden realities — is well attested in Jewish apocalyptic literature (Daniel, 1 Enoch, 4 Ezra). The text appropriates this genre but deploys it in service of a docetic Christology and an anti-demiurgic theology that stand outside Jewish theological boundaries.
