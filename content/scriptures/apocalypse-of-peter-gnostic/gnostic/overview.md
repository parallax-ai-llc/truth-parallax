## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Apocalypse of Peter provides a privileged vision of what truly happened at the crucifixion: Peter sees the double reality that ordinary perception misses — one figure suffering on the cross while the living Jesus stands apart, laughing and radiant. This double vision is the essence of gnosis itself: the ability to see through material appearances to the spiritual reality behind them. The text confirms that the spiritual Christ is utterly beyond the reach of the archons' violence, and that the proto-orthodox church's focus on the suffering Christ is a fundamental misunderstanding.
