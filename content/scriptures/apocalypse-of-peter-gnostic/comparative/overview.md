## Overview

The Apocalypse of Peter's distinction between the apparent suffering body and the real, untouched spiritual being parallels concepts in multiple traditions. The Hindu distinction between the perishable body and the imperishable atman, the Buddhist teaching on the three bodies (trikaya) of the Buddha, and the Sufi concept of the relationship between the outer form (zahir) and inner reality (batin) all address the same fundamental question: what is the relationship between the visible, suffering form and the invisible, transcendent reality?
