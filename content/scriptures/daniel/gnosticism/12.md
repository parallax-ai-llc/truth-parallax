## The Resurrection of the Sparks and the Dissolution of Matter

Gnostic interpretation of Daniel 12 reads the final chapter as a vision of the consummation — not the resurrection of material bodies (which Gnostic theology generally rejected) but the liberation of the divine sparks from their material prison and their return to the Pleroma. The "awakening" of those who "sleep in the dust" (12:2) is understood as the awakening of gnosis in those who have been spiritually unconscious — trapped in the material world without awareness of their true nature.

The image of the wise shining "like the brightness of the sky" (12:3) resonates powerfully with the Gnostic understanding of the pneumatic self as a spark of divine light. When liberated from matter, the spark recovers its original luminance and returns to the light-realm from which it came. This is not physical resurrection but spiritual illumination — the final awakening.

The sealed book (12:4) represents the hidden gnosis that is revealed only at the appointed time and only to those who are capable of receiving it. The Gnostic communities understood themselves as the recipients of this sealed knowledge — knowledge that the canonical churches had suppressed or distorted.

The closing instruction — "Go your way, Daniel... you shall rest and shall stand in your allotted place at the end of the days" (12:13) — is read as a promise to the pneumatic self: after the tribulations of material existence, the divine spark will find its place in the Pleroma.

## Theological Themes

- **Spiritual Awakening, Not Physical Resurrection**: The "rising" is the awakening of gnosis
- **The Luminous Spark**: The wise "shine" because they recover their divine light-nature
- **The Sealed Gnosis**: Hidden knowledge revealed only to the elect at the appointed time
- **Return to the Pleroma**: The final promise is not earthly restoration but cosmic homecoming
