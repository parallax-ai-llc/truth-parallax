## The Seventy Weeks and the Cosmic Timeline

Gnostic interpretation of Daniel 9 reads the seventy weeks as a cosmic timeline — not merely a historical calculation but a revelation of the duration of the soul's captivity in the material world. Daniel's prayer of confession is reinterpreted: the "sins" confessed are not moral failures but the entanglement of the divine sparks in matter — the original tragedy that gnosis seeks to reverse.

The six purposes listed in 9:24 — "to finish the transgression, to put an end to sin, to atone for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal both vision and prophet, and to anoint a most holy place" — are read as a description of the Gnostic salvation event: the liberation of the divine sparks, the end of material captivity, and the restoration of the Pleroma.

The "anointed one" who is "cut off" (9:26) is understood as the descending Redeemer — the Gnostic Christ who enters the material world and appears to be destroyed by the archons but who, through this apparent defeat, accomplishes the liberation of the sparks. The destruction of "the city and the sanctuary" represents the dissolution of the old material order — the archontic structures that the Redeemer's coming undermines.

## Theological Themes

- **The Cosmic Timeline**: The seventy weeks measure the duration of the soul's material captivity
- **Sin as Entanglement**: The confession is not about moral failure but about the tragedy of material existence
- **The Gnostic Salvation Event**: The six purposes describe the restoration of the Pleroma
- **The Apparent Defeat of the Redeemer**: The Anointed One is "cut off" but achieves liberation through apparent destruction
