## The Little Horn and the Assault on Hidden Knowledge

Gnostic reading of Daniel 8 interprets the "little horn" not merely as a historical persecutor but as a type of the archontic power that assaults the sanctuary of gnosis — the inner temple of the pneumatic self where hidden knowledge resides. The desecration of the sanctuary represents the material world's attempt to suppress, corrupt, or destroy the salvific knowledge that the divine sparks carry within them.

The ram and the goat — successive imperial powers — represent the escalating violence of the archontic order. Each empire is more aggressive than the last, culminating in the "little horn" that reaches toward heaven itself (8:10), attempting to tear down the stars — the divine sparks that belong to the heavenly realm.

The question "How long?" (8:13) is the cry of the pneumatic selves: How long must we endure captivity in the material world? The answer — a fixed period of time — reveals that the archontic order has a divinely appointed limit. The Pleroma has decreed the end of material captivity, and the sanctuary of gnosis will be restored.

## Theological Themes

- **The Assault on Gnosis**: The little horn attacks the inner sanctuary of hidden knowledge
- **Archontic Escalation**: Each successive empire is more violent in its suppression of the pneumatic
- **The Stars as Divine Sparks**: The horn reaching toward the stars is the material world's assault on the spiritual
- **The Limit of Captivity**: The archontic order has a divinely appointed end
