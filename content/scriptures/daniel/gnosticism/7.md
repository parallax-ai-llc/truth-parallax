## The Heavenly Redeemer and the End of Archontic Rule

Gnostic interpretation of Daniel 7 finds in this chapter the most congenial material in the entire book. The four beasts rising from the chaotic sea represent the archons — monstrous powers that emerge from the formless matter (*hyle*) of the abyss. Their rule is terrifying but temporary. The heavenly court scene reveals the true God of the Pleroma — the "Ancient of Days" — who sits in judgment over the archons and strips them of their power.

The "one like a son of man" who comes on the clouds and receives eternal dominion is the Gnostic Redeemer par excellence — the heavenly Christ who descends from the Pleroma to liberate the divine sparks from archontic captivity. His kingdom is "not of this world" — it does not replace the archontic empires with another material kingdom but transcends them entirely.

The "saints of the Most High" who receive the kingdom (7:18, 27) are the pneumatic elect — those who possess gnosis and are therefore destined for the Pleroma. Their kingdom is not earthly rule but spiritual liberation: the return of the divine sparks to their source.

## Theological Themes

- **The Archontic Beasts**: The four beasts are the monstrous powers that rule the material cosmos
- **The Ancient of Days**: The true God of the Pleroma who judges the archons
- **The Heavenly Redeemer**: The Son of Man as the descending Christ who liberates the sparks
- **The Pneumatic Kingdom**: The saints' kingdom is spiritual liberation, not earthly rule
