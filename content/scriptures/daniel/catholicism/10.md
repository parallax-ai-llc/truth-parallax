## The Vision of Glory and the Communion of Saints

Catholic interpretation of Daniel 10 reads the chapter as a revelation of the heavenly realm that operates behind and above earthly history. The glorious figure who appears to Daniel, the angelic conflict, and the revelation of spiritual forces at work behind earthly governments all inform Catholic angelology and the theology of the communion of saints.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Glorious Figure (10:5-6)

Catholic tradition reads this vision in connection with the risen Christ of Revelation 1 and with the angels of Catholic devotion. Whether the figure is Christ or an angel, the vision reveals the glory of the heavenly realm.

### The Prince of Persia (10:13)

Catholic angelology takes seriously the idea of angelic and demonic beings assigned to nations and peoples. The Catechism (CCC 336) teaches that angels guide and protect, while fallen angels seek to obstruct God's purposes.

### Daniel's Fasting and Prayer (10:2-3)

Catholic tradition values Daniel's three-week fast as a model of penitential discipline. The connection between fasting and spiritual vision is a consistent theme in Catholic ascetical theology.

## Theological Themes

- **Catholic Angelology**: The chapter reveals the activity of angels in world affairs
- **Spiritual Combat**: The faithful are engaged in a spiritual battle that requires prayer and fasting
- **The Communion of Saints**: Angels, saints, and the faithful on earth cooperate in God's purposes
- **Ascetical Discipline**: Fasting opens the eyes to spiritual realities
