## Confession, Intercession, and the Anointed One

Catholic interpretation of Daniel 9 treasures both the penitential prayer (9:4-19) and the prophecy of the seventy weeks (9:24-27). Daniel's prayer is a model of liturgical confession, and the seventy weeks are read as pointing to Christ's coming, his atoning death, and the establishment of the New Covenant.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Daniel's Penitential Prayer (9:4-19)

Catholic liturgical tradition values Daniel's prayer as a model of corporate confession. The prayer's structure — praising God's faithfulness, confessing communal sin, and appealing to divine mercy — mirrors the pattern of the Penitential Rite at Mass.

### "Seventy weeks are decreed" (9:24)

Catholic interpretation, following St. Jerome, reads the seventy weeks as a prophetic timeline pointing to the coming of Christ. The six purposes listed in 9:24 — ending transgression, atoning for iniquity, bringing in everlasting righteousness, sealing vision and prophecy, and anointing a most holy place — are fulfilled in Christ's redemptive work.

### "The anointed one shall be cut off" (9:26)

This is read as a prophecy of Christ's crucifixion. The subsequent destruction of "the city and the sanctuary" is identified with Rome's destruction of Jerusalem in 70 CE — a consequence, in Catholic reading, of the rejection of the Messiah.

## Theological Themes

- **Penitential Prayer**: Daniel models the Church's practice of corporate confession
- **Christological Fulfillment**: The seventy weeks point to Christ's coming and redemptive death
- **The New Covenant**: Christ's sacrifice establishes the everlasting righteousness promised in 9:24
- **The Consequences of Rejection**: The destruction of Jerusalem follows the rejection of the Anointed One
