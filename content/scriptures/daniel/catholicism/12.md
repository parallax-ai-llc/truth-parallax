## Resurrection, Judgment, and Eternal Life

Catholic interpretation of Daniel 12 reads the final chapter as the consummation of the book's theological vision: the promise of bodily resurrection, final judgment, and eternal life. Daniel 12:2 is foundational for Catholic eschatology, affirming that the body participates in the soul's eternal destiny — a truth the Church confesses in the Creed: "I believe in the resurrection of the body."

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake" (12:2)

Catholic doctrine of the resurrection of the body finds one of its earliest biblical roots here. The Catechism (CCC 997) teaches that the resurrection will involve the reunification of soul and body — a teaching anticipated in Daniel and fulfilled in Christ's bodily resurrection.

### "Those who are wise shall shine like the brightness of the sky" (12:3)

Catholic theology of the beatific vision and the glorification of the saints draws on this imagery. The "shining" of the wise is understood as participation in the divine light — an anticipation of *theosis* as understood in the broader Catholic and Orthodox traditions.

### "Seal up the book" (12:4)

The sealing of the book suggests that revelation unfolds progressively. Catholic theology affirms that public revelation is complete in Christ but that the Church's understanding of that revelation deepens over time — the development of doctrine.

## Theological Themes

- **Bodily Resurrection**: The body shares in the soul's eternal destiny
- **Final Judgment**: Every person will face judgment and receive either eternal life or condemnation
- **The Beatific Vision**: The wise "shine" with participation in divine glory
- **The Development of Doctrine**: The sealed book is gradually unsealed as the Church grows in understanding
