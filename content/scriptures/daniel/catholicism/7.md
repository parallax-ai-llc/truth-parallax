## The Son of Man and the Kingdom of the Saints

Catholic interpretation of Daniel 7 is central to the Church's Christology and ecclesiology. The vision of the four beasts, the heavenly court, the Ancient of Days, and "one like a son of man" receiving eternal dominion has been read by the Fathers and the Magisterium as a prophetic revelation of Christ's divine authority and the Church's participation in his reign.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "One like a son of man" (7:13-14)

Catholic Christology identifies this figure with Christ — the incarnate Son of God who receives "dominion and glory and a kingdom." Pope Benedict XVI's *Jesus of Nazareth* devoted extensive attention to this passage, arguing that Jesus' self-identification as "Son of Man" was a direct claim to the divine authority described in Daniel.

### The Ancient of Days (7:9)

Catholic theology identifies the Ancient of Days with God the Father. The heavenly court scene reveals the inner life of the Trinity: the Father entrusts all authority to the Son.

### "The saints of the Most High shall receive the kingdom" (7:18)

Catholic ecclesiology reads the "saints" as the Church — the body of Christ who shares in his reign. The kingdom is not merely future but is already present in the Church's sacramental life.

## Theological Themes

- **Christological Authority**: The Son of Man receives divine, eternal dominion
- **Trinitarian Revelation**: The interaction between the Ancient of Days and the Son of Man reveals the Father-Son relationship
- **The Church's Participation**: The saints share in Christ's kingdom through the Church
- **The Judgment of Empires**: All earthly powers are subject to divine judgment
