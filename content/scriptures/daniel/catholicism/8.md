## Persecution and the Purification of the Sanctuary

Catholic interpretation of Daniel 8 reads the vision of the ram and the goat within the broader framework of salvation history. The desecration of the sanctuary by the "little horn" (identified with Antiochus IV Epiphanes) is understood as a type of every assault on the Church throughout history — and as a reminder that God sets limits on the duration of persecution.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Little Horn's Assault on the Sanctuary (8:9-12)

Catholic interpreters read Antiochus' desecration of the Temple as a type of the persecutions the Church would face throughout history. The profanation of the sanctuary parallels attacks on the Eucharist, the desecration of churches, and the persecution of the faithful.

### "How long?" (8:13)

The cry "How long?" echoes through Catholic martyrology. The saints in Revelation ask the same question (6:10). Catholic theology affirms that every persecution has a divinely appointed limit and that the Church will endure until Christ's return.

### Gabriel's Interpretation (8:15-26)

The angel Gabriel's role as interpreter of divine mysteries connects to the Catholic understanding of angelic mediation. Gabriel appears again at the Annunciation (Luke 1:26), linking Daniel's prophecy to the incarnation.

## Theological Themes

- **The Church under Persecution**: Antiochus' assault typifies every attack on the Church
- **The Limit of Evil**: God sets boundaries on persecution's duration and severity
- **Angelic Mediation**: Gabriel bridges the divine and human realms
- **Eucharistic Concern**: The desecration of the sanctuary resonates with Catholic reverence for the Eucharist
