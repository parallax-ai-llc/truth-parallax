## Daniel's Prayer and the Mystery of the Seventy Weeks

Orthodox interpretation of Daniel 9 values both the penitential prayer and the prophecy of the seventy weeks. Daniel's prayer is a model of corporate repentance — the righteous individual praying on behalf of the sinful community. Orthodox liturgical prayer regularly includes this pattern: the priest prays for the forgiveness of the whole congregation, identifying with their sins.

The seventy weeks are read Christologically: the timeline points to the coming of Christ, the Anointed One (*Christos*), whose death inaugurates the new and eternal covenant. The Fathers, particularly St. Hippolytus and Theodoret of Cyrrhus, calculated the weeks as pointing to the time of Christ's incarnation and passion.

The destruction of "the city and the sanctuary" (9:26) is understood as the Roman destruction of Jerusalem in 70 CE — the definitive end of the Old Covenant sacrificial system, which has been superseded by the one sacrifice of Christ offered eternally in the Eucharist.

## Theological Themes

- **Corporate Repentance**: The righteous pray on behalf of the sinful — a model for Orthodox liturgical prayer
- **Christological Fulfillment**: The seventy weeks point to Christ's coming, death, and the new covenant
- **The End of the Old Covenant**: The Temple's destruction signals the transition to Eucharistic worship
- **The Priest's Intercession**: Daniel models the priestly ministry of intercession
