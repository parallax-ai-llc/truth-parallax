## The Ancient of Days and the Coming Kingdom

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Daniel 7 is foundational for both Christology and iconography. The vision of the Ancient of Days — white-haired, enthroned, surrounded by fire — is one of the most important sources for Orthodox icons of God. The "one like a son of man" who receives eternal dominion is identified with Christ, the incarnate Logos who shares the Father's throne.

St. Hippolytus, in one of the earliest Christian commentaries on Daniel, identified the four beasts with Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome, and the Son of Man with Christ. The heavenly court scene reveals the inner life of the Trinity: the Father (Ancient of Days) bestows authority on the Son (Son of Man) in the presence of the heavenly court.

The "saints of the Most High" who receive the kingdom (7:18, 27) are understood as the Church — the body of Christ who participates in his reign through the liturgy, the sacraments, and the life of holiness.

## Theological Themes

- **The Ancient of Days**: Source for Orthodox iconography of the divine
- **Son of Man Christology**: The eternal Son receives divine authority from the Father
- **Trinitarian Vision**: The heavenly court reveals the relationship of Father and Son
- **The Church as Kingdom**: The saints share in the Son of Man's eternal reign
