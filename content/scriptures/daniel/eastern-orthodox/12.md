## Resurrection and the Radiance of the Saints

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Daniel 12 reads the final chapter as the consummation of the book's eschatological vision and a profound witness to the resurrection of the body. Daniel 12:2 is one of the earliest biblical affirmations of bodily resurrection — a truth that stands at the center of Orthodox faith. The Paschal troparion — "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life" — is the fulfillment of Daniel's promise.

The image of the wise shining "like the brightness of the sky" (12:3) resonates with the Orthodox theology of *theosis* — the deification of the human person through participation in the divine light. The Transfiguration of Christ on Mount Tabor, where his face shone like the sun, is the archetype of this shining, and Daniel's vision anticipates it.

The sealed book (12:4) is opened in Christ — the Lamb who alone is worthy to open the sealed scroll (Revelation 5). Orthodox theology reads the progressive unsealing of divine truth as the work of the Holy Spirit in the Church, guiding the faithful "into all truth" (John 16:13).

## Theological Themes

- **Bodily Resurrection**: The body participates in the soul's eternal destiny
- **Theosis**: The wise "shine" with the uncreated light of God — the goal of human existence
- **The Paschal Mystery**: Daniel's promise of resurrection is fulfilled in Christ's rising
- **The Unsealing of Truth**: The sealed book is opened in Christ and through the Holy Spirit's guidance
