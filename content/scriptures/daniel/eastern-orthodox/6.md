## The Sealed Tomb and the Resurrection

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Daniel 6 reads the lions' den narrative primarily as a type of Christ's death and resurrection. The sealed den is the sealed tomb; Daniel's emergence alive is a prefiguration of Christ rising on the third day. This typological reading is reflected in early Christian art, where Daniel among the lions is one of the most common images in the catacombs.

Daniel's prayer discipline — three times daily, with windows open toward Jerusalem — models the Orthodox understanding of prayer as the soul's orientation toward God. The open windows represent the soul's refusal to close itself off from the divine, even when openness brings danger.

The jealousy of Daniel's rivals represents the assault of the passions and the demonic powers against the faithful soul. Orthodox ascetical theology recognizes that faithfulness provokes opposition — from within (the passions) and from without (hostile powers) — but that God delivers those who persevere.

## Theological Themes

- **Resurrection Typology**: The sealed den and Daniel's emergence prefigure the Resurrection
- **The Discipline of Prayer**: Daniel's thrice-daily prayer models the Church's prayer rule
- **Spiritual Warfare**: Faithfulness provokes opposition from the passions and hostile powers
- **Catacomb Art**: The lions' den is among the earliest Christian images of salvation
