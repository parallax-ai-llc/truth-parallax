## Persecution and the Purification of the Faithful

Orthodox interpretation of Daniel 8 reads the vision of the ram and the goat as a prophetic account of the Hellenistic persecution of God's people, with the "little horn" (Antiochus IV) representing the archetypal persecutor of the faithful. The desecration of the sanctuary is understood as a type of every assault on the Church's worship — a theme with deep resonance for Orthodox Christians who have endured persecution throughout history.

The angel Gabriel's interpretation of the vision demonstrates the principle that divine revelation requires authoritative interpretation — a conviction central to Orthodox theology, which insists that Scripture is properly understood only within the living Tradition of the Church.

The question "How long?" (8:13) echoes the cry of the martyrs throughout history. Orthodox theology of martyrdom affirms that every persecution has a divinely appointed limit and that the blood of the martyrs is the seed of the Church.

## Theological Themes

- **Persecution and Endurance**: The Church has always faced opposition, but God sets limits
- **Authoritative Interpretation**: Scripture requires the Church's Tradition for proper understanding
- **The Desecration of Worship**: Attacks on the liturgy strike at the heart of the Church's life
- **Martyrdom**: The faithful witness of those who suffer for Christ purifies and strengthens the Church
