## Resurrection and the Sealed Book

Protestant interpretation of Daniel 12 reads the final chapter as the climax of the book's eschatological vision — the promise of resurrection, final judgment, and eternal life. Daniel 12:2 is the clearest Old Testament statement of bodily resurrection, and Protestant theology has given it foundational importance.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake" (12:2)

This verse is the Old Testament's most explicit affirmation of bodily resurrection. Protestant theology reads it in light of the New Testament's fuller revelation: Christ's resurrection is the "firstfruits" (1 Corinthians 15:20) of the resurrection promised here.

### "Those who are wise shall shine like the brightness of the sky" (12:3)

The promise that the righteous will shine with glory resonates with Jesus' words: "The righteous will shine like the sun in the kingdom of their Father" (Matthew 13:43). Protestant theology of glorification finds one of its roots here.

### "Seal up the book until the time of the end" (12:4)

The command to seal the book implies that its full meaning will not be understood until the events it describes begin to unfold. Protestant interpreters debate whether "the time of the end" has arrived or remains future.

### "Go your way, Daniel, for the words are shut up and sealed until the time of the end" (12:9)

Daniel is told to live faithfully without full understanding — a model for the Protestant life of faith. Believers are called to trust God's plan even when the details remain sealed.

## Theological Themes

- **Bodily Resurrection**: The clearest Old Testament promise of life after death
- **Final Judgment**: The distinction between those who awake to "everlasting life" and "everlasting contempt"
- **The Sealed Book**: Some truths are reserved for God's appointed time
- **Faithful Living in Uncertainty**: Daniel models trust in God's plan without demanding complete understanding
