## The God Who Reveals Mysteries

Protestant interpretation of Daniel 2 centers on the revelation that God alone knows and controls the course of history. Nebuchadnezzar's dream of a great statue — with a head of gold, chest of silver, belly of bronze, legs of iron, and feet of iron mixed with clay — is read as a prophetic map of successive world empires. The stone "cut without hands" that shatters the statue and fills the earth is understood as the kingdom of God, inaugurated by Christ.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "There is a God in heaven who reveals mysteries" (2:28)

Daniel's answer to the king is a confession of faith: the wisdom does not belong to Daniel but to God. Protestant theology emphasizes that all true knowledge of the future belongs to God alone — a rebuke to human presumption.

### The Four Kingdoms (2:31-43)

Protestant interpreters have traditionally identified the four metals as Babylon (gold), Medo-Persia (silver), Greece (bronze), and Rome (iron). The iron-and-clay feet have been variously interpreted as the divided Roman Empire or a future confederation of nations.

### "The God of heaven will set up a kingdom that shall never be destroyed" (2:44)

This is one of the most important verses in Protestant eschatology. The eternal kingdom is identified with the reign of Christ — whether understood as the Church (amillennial), the millennial kingdom (premillennial), or the progressive triumph of the gospel (postmillennial).

## Theological Themes

- **Divine Revelation**: God reveals what is hidden; human wisdom cannot penetrate the future
- **The Sovereignty of God over History**: Empires rise and fall at God's command
- **The Kingdom of God**: Christ's kingdom will outlast and surpass every earthly empire
- **Humility before God**: Daniel credits God, not himself, for the interpretation
