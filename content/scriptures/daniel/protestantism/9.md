## Prayer, Confession, and the Seventy Weeks

Daniel 9 is one of the most studied and debated chapters in Protestant biblical interpretation. It contains Daniel's magnificent prayer of confession on behalf of his people and the angel Gabriel's revelation of the "seventy weeks" — a prophetic timeline that Protestant interpreters have connected to the coming of Christ, the destruction of Jerusalem, and (in some traditions) the events of the end times.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Daniel's Prayer (9:4-19)

Daniel's prayer is a model of intercessory confession — praying on behalf of the nation, acknowledging collective sin, and appealing to God's mercy. Protestant prayer traditions value this as a pattern for corporate repentance.

### The Seventy Weeks (9:24-27)

Protestant interpreters have offered three main readings of the seventy "weeks" (literally "sevens"): (1) Messianic — the prophecy points to the coming and death of Christ; (2) Dispensationalist — the seventieth week is a future seven-year tribulation period; (3) Critical-historical — the weeks map onto the period from the exile to Antiochus Epiphanes.

### "The anointed one shall be cut off" (9:26)

Most Protestant interpreters identify "the anointed one" with Christ, whose crucifixion is prophesied in this verse. The subsequent destruction of "the city and the sanctuary" is read as the Roman destruction of Jerusalem in 70 CE.

## Theological Themes

- **Intercessory Prayer**: Daniel models corporate confession and appeal to divine mercy
- **Prophetic Fulfillment in Christ**: The seventy weeks point to the Messiah's coming and death
- **The Destruction of Jerusalem**: Foretold as a consequence of rejecting the Anointed One
- **The Sovereignty of God's Timeline**: History unfolds according to God's predetermined plan
