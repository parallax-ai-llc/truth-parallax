## The Judgment of Belshazzar

Protestant reading of Daniel 5 — the feast of Belshazzar and the writing on the wall — is a narrative of swift, decisive divine judgment. While Nebuchadnezzar was given time to repent (chapter 4), Belshazzar is weighed and found wanting in a single night. Protestant interpreters emphasize the contrast: the grandfather was humbled and restored; the grandson, who knew the story but refused to learn from it, is destroyed.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "You have been weighed in the balances and found wanting" (5:27)

This verse has become proverbial in the English language, and Protestant preachers have applied it to individuals, churches, and nations alike. The image of divine scales represents God's perfect justice — every life, every institution, every kingdom is measured against his standard.

### The Desecration of the Temple Vessels (5:2-4)

Belshazzar's use of the sacred vessels from Jerusalem's Temple for his drunken feast represents the ultimate sacrilege. Protestant interpreters read this as a warning against treating holy things with contempt — a principle applied to the Word of God, the sacraments, and the worship of the Church.

### "That very night Belshazzar the Chaldean king was killed" (5:30)

The immediacy of judgment underscores the Protestant emphasis on the urgency of repentance. There is no guarantee of tomorrow; the time to turn to God is now.

## Theological Themes

- **Swift Judgment**: God's patience has limits; judgment can come suddenly
- **Accountability for Knowledge**: Belshazzar knew his grandfather's story but refused to learn
- **The Desecration of the Sacred**: Contempt for holy things invites divine wrath
- **The Urgency of Repentance**: The night of judgment may come without warning
