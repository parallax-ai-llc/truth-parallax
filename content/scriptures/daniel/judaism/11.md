## The Wars of the Kings and the Endurance of the Faithful

Jewish interpretation of Daniel 11 reads the chapter as a detailed prophecy of the wars between the Ptolemaic and Seleucid kingdoms, culminating in the persecution of Antiochus IV Epiphanes. Rashi and Ibn Ezra provided verse-by-verse identifications, linking the prophecy to known historical events. The chapter's extraordinary specificity has been used both to defend prophetic authenticity and to argue for a Maccabean-era date of composition.

The "wise who instruct many" (11:33) are identified in Jewish tradition with the *maskilim* — the faithful teachers and scribes who maintained Torah study and observance during the Hellenistic persecution. These are the predecessors of the rabbinic tradition: scholars who preserved the Torah when the Temple itself was under assault.

The reference to those who "fall by sword and flame, by captivity and plunder, for some days" (11:33) is read as a description of the Maccabean martyrs — those who chose death rather than violate the Torah. Their sacrifice is honored in Jewish tradition and commemorated in the liturgy of Hanukkah.

## Theological Themes

- **Prophetic Detail**: The specificity of the prophecy testifies to divine knowledge of history
- **The Maskilim**: The wise teachers who preserve Torah through persecution
- **Maccabean Martyrdom**: Those who die for Torah observance sanctify God's name
- **The Pattern of Persecution**: Israel's suffering under hostile empires recurs across history
