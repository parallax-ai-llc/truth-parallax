## Resurrection, Judgment, and the Sealed Book

Jewish interpretation of Daniel 12 reads the final chapter as the climax of the book's eschatological vision. Daniel 12:2 — "Many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake" — is one of the most important proof-texts for the rabbinic doctrine of *tehiyyat ha-metim* (the resurrection of the dead). The Mishnah (Sanhedrin 10:1) declares that one who denies the resurrection of the dead "has no share in the world to come," and Daniel 12:2 is a primary biblical support for this belief.

The image of the wise shining "like the brightness of the sky" (12:3) is understood as a description of the *olam ha-ba* (the world to come), where the righteous enjoy the radiance of the Shekhinah. The Talmud (Berakhot 17a) states that "in the world to come there is no eating, no drinking... but the righteous sit with their crowns on their heads, enjoying the radiance of the Shekhinah."

The command to "seal up the book until the time of the end" (12:4) has been read by the rabbis as a reminder that God's ultimate plan remains hidden. The Talmud (Sanhedrin 97b) cautions: "May the spirit expire of those who calculate the end." Daniel is told to "go your way" and live faithfully — the proper response to eschatological mystery is not calculation but faithfulness.

## Theological Themes

- **Tehiyyat Ha-Metim**: The resurrection of the dead is a foundational rabbinic belief
- **The World to Come**: The righteous enjoy the radiance of the Shekhinah
- **The Sealed Book**: God's ultimate plan remains hidden until the appointed time
- **Faithfulness over Calculation**: The proper response to mystery is faithful living, not speculation
