## God's Sovereignty over the Kingdoms of the Earth

Islamic reading of Daniel 2 finds in Nebuchadnezzar's dream a powerful illustration of the Quranic principle that God alone controls the rise and fall of empires. The Quran declares: "Say, 'O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will'" (Surah 3:26). The statue's succession of metals — each inferior to the last — demonstrates the transience of all human power.

Daniel's role as interpreter of dreams parallels the Quranic account of Yusuf (Joseph), who interpreted Pharaoh's dreams by divine gift (Surah 12:43-49). Both figures demonstrate that the interpretation of hidden meanings belongs to God alone, and that he reveals his secrets to his chosen servants.

The stone that shatters the statue and fills the earth resonates with the Islamic understanding of God's ultimate kingdom — the triumph of divine truth over every form of human arrogance and empire. Muslim scholars read this as consistent with the Quranic promise that God's way (*din*) will ultimately prevail: "It is He who sent His Messenger with guidance and the religion of truth to manifest it over all religion" (Surah 9:33).

## Theological Themes

- **Divine Sovereignty**: God alone grants and removes political power
- **The Transience of Empires**: Every human kingdom is temporary; only God's reign endures
- **Prophetic Interpretation**: Dream interpretation is a divine gift, paralleling Yusuf's story
- **The Triumph of God's Way**: The stone that fills the earth symbolizes the ultimate victory of divine truth
