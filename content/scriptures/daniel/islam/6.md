## The Supremacy of Prayer Over Political Power

Islamic reading of Daniel 6 finds in the lions' den narrative a powerful affirmation of the supremacy of *salah* (prayer) over every earthly authority. Daniel's refusal to stop praying — even when prayer is made illegal — resonates with the Islamic understanding that prayer is the most fundamental obligation of the believer, taking precedence over every other duty.

The edict forbidding prayer to any god except the king represents the totalitarian claim of the state over the human conscience. Islamic political theology rejects this claim absolutely: *la ilaha illa Allah* — there is no god but God — means that no human being, however powerful, can claim the allegiance that belongs to God alone.

Daniel's deliverance from the lions parallels the Quranic narratives of divine protection for the faithful: Ibrahim saved from the fire, Musa saved from Pharaoh, Muhammad protected during the Hijrah. The pattern is consistent: God delivers those who remain faithful to him under persecution.

## Theological Themes

- **The Primacy of Salah**: Prayer is the non-negotiable center of religious life
- **Tawhid Against Tyranny**: No human power can claim what belongs to God
- **Divine Deliverance**: God protects the faithful from the schemes of their enemies
- **The Pattern of Prophetic Deliverance**: Daniel joins Ibrahim, Musa, and Muhammad in the pattern of divine rescue
