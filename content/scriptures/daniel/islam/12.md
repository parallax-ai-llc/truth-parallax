## Resurrection, Judgment, and the End of Days

Islamic engagement with Daniel 12 finds in the chapter's promise of resurrection and final judgment a strong resonance with core Quranic teaching. The belief in the Day of Judgment (*Yawm al-Qiyamah*) and the resurrection of the body (*ba'th*) is one of the six articles of Islamic faith, and Daniel 12:2 — "Many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake" — aligns with the Quranic affirmation: "Then indeed you, after that, on the Day of Resurrection will be resurrected" (Surah 23:16).

The image of the wise shining "like the brightness of the sky" (12:3) resonates with the Quranic descriptions of Paradise, where the righteous enjoy God's light and favor: "Their Lord gives them good tidings of mercy from Him and approval and of gardens for them wherein is enduring pleasure" (Surah 9:21).

The sealing of the book "until the time of the end" (12:4) aligns with the Islamic teaching that knowledge of the Hour belongs exclusively to God: "They ask you about the Hour: when is its arrival? Say, 'Its knowledge is only with my Lord. None will reveal its time except Him'" (Surah 7:187). This principle of divine concealment of the end times is fundamental to Islamic eschatology.

## Theological Themes

- **Bodily Resurrection**: The Quran and Daniel agree that the dead will be raised
- **The Day of Judgment**: Every soul will be held accountable before God
- **The Reward of the Righteous**: The wise "shine" — a parallel to Quranic descriptions of Paradise
- **Hidden Knowledge of the End**: The timing of the final hour belongs to God alone
