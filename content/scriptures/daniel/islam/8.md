## The Little Horn and the Persecution of the Faithful

Islamic reading of Daniel 8 engages with the vision's depiction of the persecution of God's people by a tyrannical ruler — the "little horn" identified with Antiochus IV Epiphanes. The desecration of the sanctuary and the persecution of the faithful resonate with the Quranic narrative of oppressive rulers who wage war against God's servants.

The Quran describes the persecution of believers by the powerful: "Those who persecute the believing men and the believing women and then do not repent will have the punishment of Hell" (Surah 85:10). Antiochus' assault on the Temple and the faithful illustrates this pattern of tyranny against which the Quran warns repeatedly.

The question "How long?" (8:13) echoes the cry of oppressed believers in every age. Islamic eschatology affirms that God sets limits on the duration of every trial: "Indeed, with hardship comes ease" (Surah 94:5). The faithful are called to endure with patience (*sabr*), knowing that God's justice will prevail.

## Theological Themes

- **Tyranny Against the Faithful**: Oppressive rulers who persecute believers face divine judgment
- **The Limit of Persecution**: God sets boundaries on the duration of suffering
- **Sabr**: Patient endurance in the face of oppression
- **The Sanctity of Worship**: Attacks on sacred spaces are among the gravest offenses
