## Angels, Hidden Realities, and Divine Communication

Islamic reading of Daniel 10 engages with the chapter's revelation of angelic beings and the hidden spiritual realm. Islam affirms belief in angels (*mala'ikah*) as one of the six articles of faith, and Daniel's encounter with a heavenly messenger resonates with the Quranic accounts of angelic revelation — particularly Jibril's (Gabriel's) communication with the Prophet Muhammad.

The concept of angelic "princes" assigned to nations aligns with the Islamic understanding that angels carry out God's will in the governance of creation. The Quran describes angels as servants of God who "do not disobey Allah in what He commands them but do what they are commanded" (Surah 66:6).

Daniel's three-week fast and its connection to spiritual vision resonates with the Islamic practice of fasting (*sawm*), which is understood not merely as physical abstinence but as a means of spiritual purification and closeness to God. The Prophet Muhammad said that fasting is a "shield" — a means of spiritual protection and heightened awareness.

## Theological Themes

- **Belief in Angels**: Angelic beings are active agents in God's governance of the world
- **Jibril and Daniel's Messenger**: Angelic communication with prophets is a consistent divine pattern
- **Fasting and Spiritual Perception**: Physical discipline opens the way for spiritual awareness
- **The Hidden Realm**: Behind visible events lies an invisible spiritual reality governed by God
