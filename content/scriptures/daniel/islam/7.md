## The Rise and Fall of Empires Under God's Decree

Islamic engagement with Daniel 7 reads the vision of the four beasts as an illustration of the Quranic teaching that all earthly kingdoms are subject to God's will and decree (*qadar*). The succession of empires — each more terrible than the last — demonstrates that human power, however formidable, is ultimately transient and subordinate to God's authority.

The heavenly court scene, where God (the "Ancient of Days") sits in judgment over the beasts, resonates with the Quranic depiction of the Day of Judgment: "To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. His is the dominion, and to Him is the return of all matters" (Surah 57:5).

The figure "like a son of man" who receives eternal dominion is understood within the Islamic framework not as a divine figure but as a righteous servant of God — perhaps a prophet or the community of the faithful — who is granted authority by God's decree. Islamic theology firmly maintains God's absolute oneness and would not interpret this figure as sharing in divinity.

## Theological Themes

- **Qadar**: The rise and fall of empires is entirely under God's decree
- **The Day of Judgment**: The heavenly court scene anticipates the final reckoning
- **God's Absolute Authority**: All dominion belongs to God and is delegated at his will
- **The Transience of Human Power**: Every empire, however fearsome, passes away
