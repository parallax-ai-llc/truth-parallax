The golden lampstand (4:2) with its seven lamps is a powerful symbol in Gnostic cosmology, where the number seven is associated with the seven archons who rule the seven planetary spheres through which the soul must ascend to reach the Pleroma. The lamps represent the limited light of the archonic realms — not the true Pleromatic light but its diminished reflections, each presided over by a cosmic ruler. The ascending soul must pass through each sphere, overcoming the archon at each level through the possession of the correct passwords and knowledge.

"Not by might, nor by power, but by my Spirit" (4:6) is appropriated by Gnostic interpreters as a statement about the nature of true salvation. The Demiurge rules by might and power — through the laws of nature, social coercion, and the threat of punishment. The true God operates through Spirit — through the interior awakening of *gnosis* that cannot be compelled from outside. Salvation is not achieved through obedience to the Demiurge's commands but through the Spirit's revelation of the pneumatic's true identity.

The two olive trees (4:11-14) are susceptible to a Valentinian reading as the two aspects of the revealer's work: the provision of knowledge (*gnosis*) and the provision of life (*zoe*). In the Gospel of John — a text deeply valued by Valentinian Gnostics — Christ declares "I am the way, the truth, and the life" (14:6). The oil flowing from the trees into the lamps represents the transmission of divine knowledge from the Pleroma through the revealer into the dimly lit archonic cosmos.
