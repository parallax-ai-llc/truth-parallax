Zechariah 8's expansive promises of restoration present a challenge for consistent Gnostic interpretation, as the vision of material prosperity and physical security seems to affirm the goodness of the created order. Gnostic readers might resolve this by allegorizing the imagery: the "elderly men and women" represent the ancient Aeons of the Pleroma; the "children playing" represent the newly generated emanations; the city "filled with people" represents the Pleroma restored to its original fullness after the correction of Sophia's error.

The gathering from east and west (8:7-8) is reinterpreted as the collection of scattered divine sparks from throughout the material cosmos. The pneumatic elect, dispersed throughout the Demiurge's creation, will be gathered and returned to their origin in the Pleroma. This ingathering is the true purpose of the Gnostic mission — not the building of earthly communities but the restoration of the Pleroma's completeness.

The vision of nations grasping the robe of a Jew (8:23) is given an ironic twist in Gnostic reading: the nations will eventually recognize that the true knowledge came through the Jewish prophetic tradition, but in a form that the Jewish establishment itself rejected. The Gnostic claim to possess the hidden meaning of the Jewish scriptures — the secret *gnosis* that the surface narrative conceals — is a form of "grasping the robe" of the Jewish tradition while transcending its institutional expressions.
