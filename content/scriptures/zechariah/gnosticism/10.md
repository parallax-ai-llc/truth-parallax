Zechariah 10's critique of false diviners and empty idols (10:2) resonates with the Gnostic rejection of all material religious systems as ultimately fraudulent. The teraphim and diviners represent the various modes of demiurgic religion — priesthoods, oracles, sacrificial systems — that claim to mediate divine knowledge but actually keep humanity bound to the material realm. Only the direct revelation of *gnosis*, bypassing all institutional intermediaries, can provide authentic contact with the true God.

The shepherd motif (10:2-3) — the people wandering like sheep without a shepherd — describes the human condition from a Gnostic perspective. Without the Gnostic revealer, humanity is lost in the Demiurge's cosmos, guided by false shepherds (priests, rulers, philosophers) who cannot lead them to their true home. The promise that God will "attend to his sheep" represents the intervention of the true God who sends the revealer to gather and guide the pneumatics home.

The reunification of Ephraim and Judah (10:6-12) is allegorized as the reunification of the separated syzygies (divine pairs) in the Pleroma. The division of the kingdoms represents the cosmic fragmentation that resulted from Sophia's error; the reunification represents the restoration of the Pleroma's original harmony. The passage through the sea (10:11) becomes the pneumatic soul's passage through the hostile waters of material existence, guided by the revealer's knowledge.
