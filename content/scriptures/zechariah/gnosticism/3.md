The vision of Joshua's filthy garments and their replacement with pure vestments (3:1-5) is rich material for Gnostic reinterpretation. The filthy garments represent the material body — the "garments of skin" (cf. Genesis 3:21) that the archons imposed on the pneumatic soul to trap it in matter. The removal of these garments and the bestowal of clean robes symbolize the shedding of material entanglement through *gnosis* and the restoration of the soul's original luminous garment — a theme found extensively in the *Hymn of the Pearl* (Acts of Thomas) and other Gnostic texts.

The Satan figure accusing Joshua (3:1) is read in some Gnostic systems as the Demiurge himself — the cosmic accuser who keeps the pneumatic bound to the material realm through guilt, law, and judgment. The angelic rebuke of Satan represents the intervention of a higher power (the true God or His emissary) that overrides the Demiurge's accusatory system and liberates the pneumatic from the cycle of sin and punishment.

The Branch (3:8) is identified in Gnostic Christology with the revealer who descends from the Pleroma to awaken the sleeping pneumatics. The "stone with seven eyes" (3:9) is read as a symbol of the transcendent knowledge that perceives all things — the omniscient *gnosis* that the revealer brings, opening the eyes of the elect to their true divine origin and destiny.
