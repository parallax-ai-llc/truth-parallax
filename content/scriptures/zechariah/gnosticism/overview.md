## Overview

Zechariah's elaborate visionary sequences, angelic mediation, and cosmic symbolism made it fertile ground for Gnostic appropriation. The book's structure — a prophet receiving revelations through angelic interpreters about cosmic powers, hidden meanings, and eschatological transformation — closely parallels the form of Gnostic revelatory literature. While direct Gnostic commentaries on Zechariah are not extant, the book's themes and imagery appear throughout the Nag Hammadi literature and in the Church Fathers' accounts of Gnostic exegesis.

## Key Interpretive Themes

The angelic interpreter (*mal'akh*) who mediates Zechariah's visions corresponds to the Gnostic revealer figure who mediates *gnosis* to the elect. The cosmic powers represented by horses, chariots, and winds (chapters 1, 6) map onto the archonic hierarchy that governs the material cosmos. The Satan figure who accuses Joshua (chapter 3) is susceptible to multiple Gnostic readings — either as the Demiurge prosecuting the pneumatic, or as a lower archon acting within the Demiurge's system.

## The Branch and the Pierced One

The Branch (3:8, 6:12) who combines priestly and kingly authority could be identified with the Gnostic Christ who descends from the Pleroma to reunite the scattered divine sparks. The pierced one (12:10) resonates with Gnostic Christologies that debated whether the divine Christ truly suffered or merely appeared to do so (docetism).
