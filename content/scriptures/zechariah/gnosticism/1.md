Gnostic interpretation of Zechariah 1 finds the night visions particularly amenable to its cosmological framework. The angelic mediator who interprets the visions for the prophet parallels the Gnostic revealer figure — Christ, Seth, or another emissary from the Pleroma — who descends to impart *gnosis* to those capable of receiving it. The mediating angel is necessary because direct contact with the true God is impossible within the material cosmos; knowledge must be transmitted through a chain of spiritual intermediaries.

The horsemen patrolling the earth (1:8-11) are susceptible to a Gnostic reading as archons — the cosmic rulers who maintain the Demiurge's order over the material realm. Their report that "all the earth remains at rest" (1:11) is not reassuring but ominous from a Gnostic perspective: the material cosmos is quiescent because it is firmly under archonic control. The apparent peace is the peace of a well-managed prison.

The divine jealousy for Jerusalem (1:14) is read by Gnostic interpreters as the Demiurge's possessive attachment to his favored city — not the love of a supreme God but the territorial jealousy of a lesser deity who regards Jerusalem as his particular domain. The true God, in Gnostic theology, is beyond jealousy or passion, dwelling in the immeasurable silence of the Pleroma.
