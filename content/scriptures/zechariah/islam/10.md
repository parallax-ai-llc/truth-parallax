Zechariah 10's pastoral imagery — God as the true source of rain and provision, false shepherds who mislead the flock — connects to central Quranic themes. The polemic against teraphim and diviners (10:2) aligns with the strict Quranic prohibition of *sihr* (sorcery) and *kahanah* (soothsaying). The Prophet Muhammad said: "Whoever visits a fortune-teller and believes what he says has disbelieved in what was revealed to Muhammad" (Ahmad). Both prophetic traditions insist on exclusive reliance on God for guidance and provision.

The gathering of scattered peoples (10:6-12) resonates with the Islamic eschatological concept of *hashr* (gathering). On the Day of Judgment, all humanity will be gathered before God for reckoning: "The Day He will gather you for the Day of Assembly" (64:9). While Zechariah speaks of the historical regathering of Israel, the Islamic reader recognizes the pattern of scattering and gathering as a cosmic principle that will find its ultimate expression at the end of history.

The passage through the sea (10:11), echoing the Exodus, connects to the Quranic account of Musa (Moses) leading the Israelites through the divided sea. The Quran narrates this event in vivid detail (26:63-66) and treats it as a paradigmatic act of divine deliverance. The prophetic reuse of Exodus imagery in Zechariah confirms the Islamic conviction that God's pattern of saving the faithful and destroying the oppressor is consistent across all prophetic eras.
