The menorah vision (4:1-14) with its seven lamps and two olive trees connects to Islamic symbolism of light. The Quran contains the famous "Light Verse" (24:35): "Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth. The example of His light is like a niche within which is a lamp, the lamp is within glass, the glass as if it were a brilliant star." The architectural and symbolic resonance between Zechariah's golden lampstand and the Quranic *mishkat* (lamp niche) suggests a shared prophetic vocabulary of divine illumination.

The declaration "Not by might, nor by power, but by my Spirit" (4:6) aligns with the Islamic emphasis on *tawakkul* (reliance on God). The Quran teaches: "If Allah helps you, no one can overcome you; but if He forsakes you, who is there that can help you after Him? And upon Allah let the believers rely" (3:160). Both prophetic traditions insist that ultimate success comes from divine support, not human strength.

The two anointed ones (4:14) representing royal and priestly authority find a parallel in the Islamic distinction between political leadership (*khilafah*) and religious scholarship (*'ilm*). The ideal Islamic polity integrates both temporal governance and religious guidance, and the prophetic vision of two sources of "oil" sustaining the sacred light resonates with this dual structure of authority.
