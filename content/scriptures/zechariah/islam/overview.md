## Overview

The name Zechariah holds special significance in Islam, as Zakariyya is a named prophet in the Quran — the father of Yahya (John the Baptist) and a righteous priest who served in the Temple. However, Islamic tradition generally identifies the Quranic Zakariyya with a later figure than the author of this biblical book. Nevertheless, the thematic content of the Book of Zechariah — divine visions, messianic expectation, cosmic judgment, and the purification of worship — resonates with Quranic themes and has been discussed in Isra'iliyyat literature.

## Quranic Connections

The Quranic Zakariyya (Surah 3:37-41, 19:2-15, 21:89-90) is portrayed as an elderly priest who prays for an heir and is granted Yahya. While this narrative does not directly correspond to the Book of Zechariah, the prophetic concern for the Temple, the priestly vocation, and the divine response to faithful prayer are shared themes. The visionary elements of biblical Zechariah — angelic mediation, symbolic imagery, cosmic scope — also parallel the Quranic accounts of prophetic experience.

## Theological Parallels

The eschatological chapters (9-14) describe cosmic upheaval, divine intervention, and universal recognition of God's sovereignty — themes that mirror Quranic eschatology extensively. The vision of all nations worshiping in Jerusalem (14:16) parallels the Islamic vision of universal submission to God, though Islam centers this worship on Mecca rather than Jerusalem.
