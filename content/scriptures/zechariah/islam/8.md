Zechariah 8's promises of restoration — peace, prosperity, and the gathering of the dispersed — resonate with the Islamic vision of a just and prosperous society under divine guidance. The image of the elderly at peace and children playing (8:4-5) corresponds to the Quranic vision of paradise (*jannah*) as a place of complete security and joy: "They will not hear therein any ill speech — only greetings of peace" (56:25-26). While the prophetic text describes earthly Jerusalem, the Islamic reader recognizes the eschatological longing for a world perfected by divine justice.

The promise that God will be faithful and righteous (8:8) aligns with the Quranic attributes of God: *al-Sadiq* (the Truthful) and *al-'Adl* (the Just). Islamic theology insists that God's promises are absolutely reliable: "Who is more truthful than Allah in statement?" (4:87). The prophetic assurance of divine faithfulness provides common ground between the biblical and Quranic traditions.

The vision of nations seeking God through His people (8:23) resonates with the Islamic concept of *da'wah* (invitation to God). The Muslim community is called to be witnesses to God's truth among the nations: "Thus We have made you a just community that you will be witnesses over the people" (2:143). The prophetic pattern of nations drawn to God through the testimony of a faithful community operates in both the biblical and Islamic frameworks.
