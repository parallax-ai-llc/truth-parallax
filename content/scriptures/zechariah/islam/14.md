Zechariah 14's eschatological vision — cosmic upheaval, divine intervention, universal worship — has extensive parallels in Islamic eschatology. The LORD standing on the Mount of Olives (14:4) connects to Islamic traditions about the Last Day, where Jerusalem and its surroundings play a significant role. Hadith literature describes the Last Day's events unfolding in the region of greater Syria (*al-Sham*), and the Mount of Olives features in several eschatological traditions.

The living waters flowing from Jerusalem (14:8) find a parallel in the Quranic descriptions of rivers in paradise: "In it are rivers of water unaltered, rivers of milk the taste of which never changes, rivers of wine delicious to those who drink, and rivers of purified honey" (47:15). While the prophetic and Quranic contexts differ, the symbolic association of divine blessing with flowing water is shared across the Abrahamic traditions.

The vision of all nations worshiping God (14:16) corresponds to the Islamic ideal of universal submission to God. The Quran presents Islam as the primordial religion to which all humanity is called: "Indeed, the religion in the sight of Allah is Islam" (3:19). The prophetic vision of universal worship at the Feast of Booths is recontextualized in Islamic thought as the ultimate gathering of humanity in recognition of God's oneness — a vision that finds its annual anticipation in the Hajj pilgrimage, where Muslims from every nation gather to worship the one God.
