## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Zechariah highlights the book's extensive use in the Holy Week liturgy, particularly its Passion prophecies. The prophet's visions of a cleansed priesthood, a royal-priestly Messiah, and a purified Jerusalem are read as pointing toward the Church, the sacraments, and the eschatological fulfillment of God's promises. The Church Fathers gave extensive attention to Zechariah, and the book's imagery pervades Catholic art, hymnody, and theology.

## Liturgical Use

Zechariah 9:9 is read on Palm Sunday; 12:10 on Good Friday; 13:7 during the Passion narrative. The fountain "opened for the house of David for sin and uncleanness" (13:1) is connected to the blood and water flowing from Christ's pierced side (John 19:34), a foundational image for Catholic sacramental theology of Baptism and Eucharist.

## Theological Emphases

The Branch who builds the Temple and bears royal and priestly dignity (6:12-13) provides a prophetic foundation for the Catholic understanding of Christ as the eternal High Priest whose sacrifice the Eucharist makes present. The vision of universal pilgrimage to Jerusalem for the Feast of Booths (14:16-19) anticipates the Church's universal mission and the eschatological gathering of all nations.
