The flying scroll of judgment (5:1-4) entering the houses of the guilty resonates with the Orthodox understanding of God's omniscience and the inevitability of divine judgment. The Orthodox tradition teaches that judgment is not an external imposition but a revelation of the truth about each person's spiritual state. The scroll that "enters the house" symbolizes the penetrating character of divine truth, which exposes what is hidden and brings all things to light. The connection to the Last Judgment, depicted prominently in Orthodox iconography, is evident.

The woman named Wickedness (5:5-11) sealed in the ephah and removed to Shinar provides a dramatic vision of the banishment of evil. Orthodox theology of redemption includes the concept of Christ's *harrowing of hell* — His descent into Hades to liberate the captive souls and definitively defeat the power of death and sin. Zechariah's vision of wickedness being contained and exiled anticipates this christological triumph.

The two women with wings who carry the basket (5:9) are given various interpretations in patristic tradition. Some Fathers saw them as providential agents; others connected them to the angelic powers that execute God's will in creation. The overall movement of the vision — from the identification of sin, to its containment, to its removal — mirrors the Orthodox sacramental process of confession, absolution, and renewal.
