Zechariah 10 continues the restoration theme with a call to "ask rain from the LORD in the season of the spring rain" (10:1) — a reminder that God, not the Baal fertility cults, is the source of agricultural blessing. Protestant interpreters note the polemical edge: even in the post-exilic period, the temptation to seek blessing from false sources persists. The answer is always to return to the LORD who alone gives "showers of rain, to everyone the vegetation in the field" (10:1).

The indictment of "shepherds" and "male goats" (10:2-3) who lead the people astray introduces the shepherd motif that will dominate chapters 11-13. Protestant ecclesiology applies this text to church leadership: false shepherds who exploit the flock will face God's anger, while the LORD himself will attend to His sheep. The promise that from Judah will come "the cornerstone, the tent peg, the battle bow" (10:4) is read Christologically — Christ is the cornerstone of the Church, the anchor of faith, and the champion of God's people.

The chapter's vision of Ephraim and Judah reunited (10:6-12) points to a restoration that transcends the historical division of the kingdoms. Protestant interpreters read this as fulfilled in the Church, where all ethnic and national divisions are overcome in Christ: "There is neither Jew nor Greek... for you are all one in Christ Jesus" (Galatians 3:28).
