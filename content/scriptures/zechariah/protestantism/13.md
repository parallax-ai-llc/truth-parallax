Zechariah 13 opens with the promise of "a fountain opened for the house of David and the inhabitants of Jerusalem, to cleanse them from sin and uncleanness" (13:1). Protestant interpreters connect this directly to the atoning work of Christ on the cross, from whose pierced side flowed blood and water (John 19:34). The hymn "There Is a Fountain Filled with Blood" by William Cowper draws its imagery directly from this verse, and it has become one of the most enduring expressions of Protestant atonement theology.

The removal of idols and false prophets (13:2-6) describes a comprehensive purification of the land. The false prophet who denies his vocation — "I am no prophet, I am a worker of the soil" (13:5) — and bears mysterious wounds is an obscure passage that Protestant commentators interpret variously, but the overall thrust is clear: in the messianic age, false religion will be exposed and eliminated.

The smitten shepherd oracle (13:7) is one of the most directly quoted passages in the Passion narratives: "Strike the shepherd, and the sheep will be scattered." Jesus himself quotes this verse in predicting His disciples' abandonment (Matthew 26:31, Mark 14:27). The subsequent promise that a refined remnant — "one-third shall be left alive" (13:8-9) — will be purified as gold and silver through fire speaks to the Protestant understanding of the refining work of suffering in the life of the elect.
