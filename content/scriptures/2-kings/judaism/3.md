## The War Against Moab

Jewish interpretation of 2 Kings 3 focuses on the role of the prophet in military affairs, the conditions under which HaShem grants assistance, and the disturbing episode of Mesha's sacrifice.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### Elisha's Conditional Prophecy (3:14)

Elisha declares he would not even look at Jehoram were it not for Jehoshaphat's presence. **Rashi** explains that the *Shekhinah* (Divine Presence) does not rest upon one who is angry or sad, but only upon one who is in a state of joy and righteous disposition. Music helps Elisha achieve the proper state for receiving prophecy.

### Water Without Rain (3:16-20)

**Radak** reads this miracle as demonstrating that HaShem is not bound by natural causation. The water appears not from rain but from the direction of Edom — God can use any means to fulfill His word.

### Mesha's Sacrifice (3:27)

This verse has generated extensive rabbinic discussion. **Rashi** interprets "great wrath came upon Israel" as divine wrath prompted by the fact that Israel was also guilty of idolatry and had no moral standing to witness Mesha's sacrifice without self-examination. **Radak** suggests it was the wrath of Moab — the Moabites fought with desperate fury after witnessing their king's sacrifice.

## Theological Significance

- **Joy and prophecy**: The *Shekhinah* rests on the joyful and righteous
- **Divine freedom**: HaShem provides through unexpected channels
- **Moral standing**: One cannot condemn others' sins while practicing one's own
- **The limitations of alliance**: Military partnerships do not guarantee divine favor
