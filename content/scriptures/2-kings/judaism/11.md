## Athaliah and the Rescue of Joash

Jewish reading of 2 Kings 11 focuses on the providential preservation of the Davidic line, the priestly role in restoring legitimate governance, and the covenant renewal.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### The Survival of Joash (11:1-3)

**Rashi** explains that Jehosheba hid the infant Joash in the Kodesh HaKodashim (Holy of Holies) or in an adjacent chamber. The Talmud (Sotah 12a in parallel discussions) praises women who preserved future leaders of Israel — Yocheved saved Moses, Jehosheba saved Joash. The Midrash sees HaShem's hand in ensuring that the messianic line is never broken.

### Jehoiada the Priest (11:4-12)

**Radak** emphasizes that Jehoiada acted within the bounds of Torah law. The priest's role in coronation established the principle that legitimate authority derives from divine sanction, mediated through the religious leadership. This reflects the Torah's vision of Israel as a nation governed by both priestly and royal authority.

### Covenant Renewal (11:17)

The renewed covenant reaffirms Israel's foundational commitment. **Ralbag** notes that covenant renewal is necessary after periods of severe apostasy because the community must consciously recommit to principles that have been abandoned.

## Theological Significance

- **The indestructibility of the Davidic line**: HaShem preserves the messianic promise
- **Righteous women**: Women play decisive roles in salvation history
- **Legitimate authority**: Governance requires divine sanction
- **Covenant renewal**: Communities must periodically reaffirm their foundational commitments
