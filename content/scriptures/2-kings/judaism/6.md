## Miracles and the Siege of Samaria

Jewish interpretation of 2 Kings 6 reflects on the nature of prophetic perception, the ethics of warfare, and the severity of the siege as covenant punishment.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### The Floating Axe Head (6:1-7)

**Rashi** notes that the axe head was borrowed, making its loss a matter of financial obligation (*mammon*). Elisha's miracle demonstrates HaShem's concern for even mundane matters of justice. The Talmud (Bava Metzia) extensively discusses the obligations surrounding borrowed objects.

### Spiritual Sight (6:16-17)

**Ralbag** draws the lesson that the righteous perceive realities invisible to ordinary people. The chariots of fire represent the *malachei hasharet* (ministering angels) who constantly surround and protect those who serve HaShem. This connects to the daily liturgy: "The angel of the LORD encamps around those who fear Him and delivers them" (Psalm 34:8).

### Feeding the Enemies (6:21-23)

**Radak** explains that Elisha's command to feed the captured Arameans rather than kill them was both merciful and strategically wise — the raids ceased after this act of kindness. The Talmud (Sanhedrin 105b) teaches that one should overcome enemies through kindness when possible, as hatred breeds further conflict.

## Theological Significance

- **Justice in small things**: Even a borrowed axe head matters to HaShem
- **Angelic protection**: The righteous are surrounded by heavenly guardians
- **Kindness to enemies**: Mercy can accomplish what violence cannot
- **Prophetic perception**: The prophet sees what is hidden from ordinary sight
