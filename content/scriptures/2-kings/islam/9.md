## Jehu's Revolution: Divine Instrument and Human Agency

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 9 engages the theme of Allah using human agents to execute divine judgment upon wicked rulers.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Allah's Use of Instruments

The Islamic concept of *istidraj* (gradual leading to destruction) applies to the house of Ahab. Allah allowed them prosperity and power for a time, then brought about their destruction through Jehu. The Quran warns: "So when they forgot that by which they had been reminded, We opened to them the doors of every [good] thing until, when they rejoiced in that which they were given, We seized them suddenly" (Quran 6:44).

### Jezebel's Fate

The death of Jezebel, who promoted Baal worship in Israel, illustrates the Quranic principle that those who lead others astray face severe punishment: "That they may bear their own burdens in full on the Day of Resurrection and some of the burdens of those whom they misguide" (Quran 16:25). Jezebel's influence on Israel's religion multiplied the sin she must bear.

### Prophetic Fulfillment

The precise fulfillment of earlier prophetic words demonstrates the reliability of divine communication. Islamic theology affirms that Allah's word (*kalam Allah*) is absolutely truthful and will always be fulfilled.

## Theological Significance

- **Istidraj**: Allah gradually leads the wicked to destruction
- **Leadership accountability**: Those who mislead others bear compounded responsibility
- **Divine word fulfilled**: Allah's promises and warnings always come to pass
- **Human agency**: Allah uses human instruments without forcing their choices
