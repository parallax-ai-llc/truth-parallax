## Al-Yasa's Miracles: Signs of Prophetic Authority

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 4 treats al-Yasa's miracles as *mu'jizat* (prophetic miracles) — signs confirming his status as Allah's messenger.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Miracles as Divine Signs

In Islamic theology, miracles performed by prophets are called *mu'jizat* and serve as proof of their divine commission. They are distinguished from *karamat* (marvels granted to saints). Al-Yasa's miracles — multiplying oil, raising the dead, neutralizing poison, feeding many from little — are understood as demonstrations of Allah's power operating through His chosen prophet.

### The Raising of the Dead (4:32-37)

The raising of the Shunammite's son is significant because it demonstrates Allah's power over life and death. The Quran affirms this power: "He gives life and causes death" (Quran 2:258). Islamic theology holds that only Allah can give life; the prophet is merely the instrument through whom Allah acts.

### The Multiplication of Food (4:42-44)

The feeding of a hundred from twenty loaves parallels traditions about prophetic abundance. **Al-Tha'labi** narrates this episode as evidence of al-Yasa's prophetic rank, noting that similar miracles are attributed to other prophets in Islamic tradition.

### Compassion for the Poor (4:1-7)

Al-Yasa's concern for the widow illustrates the Islamic emphasis on social justice. The Quran commands care for orphans, widows, and the poor (Quran 4:36, 89:17-18). The prophet who neglects the vulnerable fails his mission.

## Theological Significance

- **Mu'jizat**: Prophetic miracles confirm divine commission
- **Allah's power over life and death**: Only God gives and takes life
- **Social justice**: Care for the poor is a prophetic duty
- **Prophetic abundance**: God's provision through His messenger exceeds natural limits
