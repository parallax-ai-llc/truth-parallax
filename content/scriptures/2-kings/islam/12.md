## Temple Repair and the Stewardship of Sacred Spaces

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 12 draws parallels between Joash's repair of the Temple and the Islamic obligation to maintain mosques and sacred spaces.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Care for Sacred Spaces

The Quran teaches: "The mosques of Allah are only to be maintained by those who believe in Allah and the Last Day and establish prayer and give *zakah*" (Quran 9:18). Joash's concern for the physical condition of the Temple aligns with the Islamic obligation to maintain *masajid* (mosques) in good repair. The *awqaf* (endowment) system in Islamic law was developed precisely to ensure the perpetual maintenance of religious buildings.

### Financial Integrity

Joash's system of transparent fund collection and disbursement mirrors the Islamic requirements for *zakah* and *sadaqah* administration. Islamic law requires strict accountability in the management of charitable funds, and the *mutawalli* (endowment administrator) bears legal responsibility for proper stewardship.

### The Fragility of Reform

Joash's later capitulation to Hazael — stripping the Temple of its treasures — demonstrates that external reform without internal *iman* (faith) is unstable. Islamic spiritual teaching emphasizes that outward compliance without inward conviction (*ikhlas* — sincerity) is ultimately fragile.

## Theological Significance

- **Maintenance of sacred spaces**: An obligation in Islamic law
- **Financial accountability**: Sacred funds require transparent management
- **Sincerity required**: Outward reform without inward faith is fragile
- **Awqaf system**: Islamic endowments parallel Joash's repair system
