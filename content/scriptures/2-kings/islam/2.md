## The Departure of Ilyas and the Mission of al-Yasa'

The departure of Ilyas (Elijah) and the succession of al-Yasa' (Elisha) are of particular interest in Islamic interpretation because both figures are named in the Quran as prophets.

## Quranic References

### Al-Yasa' in the Quran

Al-Yasa' is mentioned twice by name:
- **Quran 6:86**: "And Isma'il and al-Yasa' and Yunus and Lut — and all We preferred over the worlds."
- **Quran 38:48**: "And remember Isma'il and al-Yasa' and Dhul-Kifl — all are among the outstanding."

These references establish al-Yasa' as a recognized prophet in Islam, though the Quran provides no narrative details about his ministry. Muslim scholars supplement the Quranic references with Isra'iliyyat material from 2 Kings.

### Ilyas's Departure

Islamic tradition does not unanimously accept the bodily ascension of Ilyas. Some scholars, following Isra'iliyyat traditions, accept that Ilyas was taken alive to heaven. Others regard this as an unconfirmed detail. What is affirmed is that Ilyas completed his prophetic mission and that al-Yasa' succeeded him by divine appointment.

### Prophetic Succession

In Islamic understanding, prophets are appointed by Allah alone. The transfer of the prophetic mantle from Ilyas to al-Yasa' illustrates the principle that prophethood is not hereditary or humanly transmitted but is a divine gift (*wahb*) bestowed by Allah on whom He chooses (Quran 6:124).

## Theological Significance

- **Al-Yasa' as Quranic prophet**: Recognized and honored in the Quran
- **Prophetic appointment**: Allah alone chooses His messengers
- **Continuity of the prophetic message**: Every prophet teaches *tawhid*
- **Isra'iliyyat engagement**: Islamic scholars use biblical details cautiously
