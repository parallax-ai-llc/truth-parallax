## Pride and Divine Patience

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 14 focuses on the sin of pride (*kibr*), the divine attribute of patience (*sabr*), and the theological paradox of prosperity without righteousness.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Pride (*Kibr*)

Amaziah's arrogant challenge to Israel after his victory over Edom illustrates the Quranic warning against pride: "And do not walk upon the earth exultantly. Indeed, you will never tear the earth apart, and you will never reach the mountains in height" (Quran 17:37). The Prophet Muhammad stated: "He who has an atom's weight of pride in his heart will not enter Paradise." Amaziah's downfall is a direct consequence of his *kibr*.

### Divine Patience with Jeroboam II (14:26-27)

Allah's decision to save Israel through the wicked Jeroboam II demonstrates the attribute of *hilm* (forbearance). The Quran teaches that Allah gives respite (*umlah*) to the disobedient so they may repent: "And if Allah were to impose blame on the people for their wrongdoing, He would not have left upon it any creature" (Quran 16:61).

## Theological Significance

- **Kibr condemned**: Pride is among the most dangerous sins in Islam
- **Divine patience**: Allah's forbearance creates opportunity for repentance
- **Prosperity is not endorsement**: Material success does not indicate divine approval
- **Humility**: Self-knowledge and recognition of one's limitations
