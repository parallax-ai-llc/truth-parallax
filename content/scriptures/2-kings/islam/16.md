## Ahaz's Apostasy: The Corruption of Faith

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 16 condemns Ahaz's comprehensive apostasy, focusing on child sacrifice, the corruption of worship, and the subordination of faith to political power.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Child Sacrifice

The sacrifice of Ahaz's son is utterly condemned in Islamic theology. The Quran explicitly addresses this: "And do not kill your children for fear of poverty. We provide for them and for you. Indeed, their killing is ever a great sin" (Quran 17:31). The pre-Islamic practice of burying daughters alive (*wa'd*) was abolished by Islam, and the prohibition of child sacrifice is absolute.

### Corruption of Worship

Ahaz's replacement of the divinely prescribed altar with a pagan copy violates the Islamic principle that worship must follow divine instruction (*tawqifi*). In Islamic jurisprudence, acts of worship (*'ibadah*) must be performed exactly as prescribed by Allah and the Prophet; human innovation in worship (*bid'ah*) is condemned.

### Political Subordination

Ahaz's declaration of vassalage to Assyria rather than trusting in Allah mirrors the Islamic concept of *tawakkul* (reliance on Allah). The Quran teaches: "And upon Allah let the believers rely" (Quran 3:122). Placing trust in human powers rather than in Allah is a deficiency in *iman* (faith).

## Theological Significance

- **Child sacrifice prohibited**: The sanctity of life is absolute in Islam
- **Bid'ah in worship**: Innovation in prescribed worship is condemned
- **Tawakkul**: Believers must rely on Allah, not on human powers
- **Political pragmatism vs. faith**: Accommodating worldly power at the expense of faith is apostasy
