## The Disintegration of Israel

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 15 sees the rapid succession of assassinations in Israel as a demonstration of what happens when a society abandons divine guidance.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Political Instability Without Divine Guidance

The Quran teaches that stability comes from adherence to divine law: "And if only the people of the cities had believed and feared Allah, We would have opened upon them blessings from the heaven and the earth" (Quran 7:96). Israel's political chaos — five kings in rapid succession, most by murder — illustrates the inverse: abandoning Allah's guidance produces societal collapse.

### The Assyrian Advance (15:29)

Islamic theology reads the Assyrian conquest as an instrument of divine decree. The Quran states regarding the Children of Israel: "And We decreed for the Children of Israel in the Scripture: 'You will surely cause corruption on the earth twice, and you will surely reach great haughtiness'" (Quran 17:4). Muslim exegetes often connect this passage to the Assyrian and Babylonian destructions.

### Uzziah's Presumption (15:5)

The king's leprosy for overstepping priestly boundaries teaches the Islamic principle that each person has a designated role (*maqam*) in the divine order. Transgressing established boundaries invites divine correction.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine guidance produces stability**: Abandoning God's law leads to chaos
- **Empires as divine instruments**: Allah uses nations to accomplish His decrees
- **Surah al-Isra': the two corruptions**: The Quran references Israel's historical catastrophes
- **Proper boundaries**: Each role in the divine order must be respected
