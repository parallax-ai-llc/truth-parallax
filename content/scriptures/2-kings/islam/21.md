## Manasseh's Wickedness and the Question of Repentance

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 21 focuses on the depths of Manasseh's apostasy, the shedding of innocent blood, and the Islamic teaching on the vast scope of divine mercy.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Comprehensive Shirk (21:2-9)

Manasseh's sins — idolatry, sorcery, child sacrifice, astral worship — represent a systematic violation of *tawhid*. Islamic theology categorizes these as the gravest sins: *shirk* (associating partners with Allah), *sihr* (sorcery), and *qatl* (murder). The Quran lists these among the major sins (*kaba'ir*) that require sincere repentance.

### The Shedding of Innocent Blood (21:16)

The Quran declares: "Whoever kills a soul unless for a soul or for corruption in the land — it is as if he had slain mankind entirely" (Quran 5:32). Manasseh's massive shedding of innocent blood represents a crime of the highest magnitude.

### Tawbah: The Door of Repentance

While 2 Kings does not record Manasseh's repentance, Islamic theology affirms that the door of *tawbah* (repentance) is open to every sinner until death. The Quran declares: "Say: O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins" (Quran 39:53). No sin — including shirk — is beyond forgiveness if sincere repentance is offered before death.

## Theological Significance

- **The gravity of shirk**: Idolatry is the most serious sin
- **The sanctity of life**: Murder is equivalent to the destruction of all humanity
- **Tawbah**: The door of repentance is always open
- **Allah's mercy**: "He forgives all sins" — no exception for the sincerely repentant
