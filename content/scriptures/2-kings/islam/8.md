## Hazael's Rise and the Decline of Judah

Islamic interpretation of 2 Kings 8 reflects on prophetic foreknowledge, divine decree (*qadar*), and the consequences of allying with the ungodly.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Prophetic Foreknowledge (8:11-13)

Al-Yasa's tears over Hazael's future atrocities illustrate that prophets, by Allah's permission, can know future events. This is not independent knowledge but divinely granted insight (*ilm ladunni*). The Quran affirms: "He is the Knower of the unseen, and He does not disclose His unseen to anyone, except whom He has approved of His messengers" (Quran 72:26-27).

### Divine Decree

Hazael's rise, despite the evil he will commit, falls within Allah's *qadar* (divine decree). Islamic theology affirms that everything occurs by Allah's will, but this does not absolve human beings of moral responsibility. Hazael chooses his actions freely, even though Allah foreknows them.

### Alliance with the Wicked (8:18, 27)

The corruption of Judah through intermarriage with the house of Ahab illustrates the Islamic principle that association with the corrupt leads to corruption. The Prophet Muhammad taught: "A person is upon the religion of his close friend, so let each of you look to whom he befriends."

## Theological Significance

- **Prophetic foreknowledge**: Granted by Allah's permission
- **Qadar**: Divine decree and human responsibility coexist
- **The danger of bad company**: Association with the corrupt produces corruption
- **Prophetic compassion**: Even foreknowledge of evil evokes tears, not indifference
