## The Preservation of a Righteous Line

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 11 sees the preservation of the infant Joash as an act of divine providence, paralleling Quranic accounts of Allah preserving those He has chosen for special purposes.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Divine Protection

The hiding of the infant Joash parallels the Quranic narrative of Musa (Moses) being hidden from Pharaoh (Quran 28:7-13). In both cases, a woman acts as the instrument of divine preservation, and the child is protected within a hostile environment to fulfill a future divine purpose. Allah's *hifdh* (protection) extends to those He has chosen.

### Covenant and Governance

The covenant established by Jehoiada between God, king, and people illustrates the Islamic principle of *bay'ah* (oath of allegiance). In Islamic political theory, the ruler's authority derives from his commitment to justice and Islamic law (*shari'ah*). When a ruler violates this compact, the community has the right and duty to remove the ruler.

### The Restoration of Monotheism

The tearing down of the Baal temple after Athaliah's overthrow represents the restoration of pure monotheism — a central concern of Islamic theology. Every prophetic mission in the Quran aims at restoring *tawhid* after periods of deviation.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine preservation**: Allah protects those destined for His purposes
- **Bay'ah**: Legitimate authority requires mutual commitment between ruler and people
- **Restoration of tawhid**: The recurring prophetic mission is to restore monotheism
- **Women as instruments of God**: Women play decisive roles in preserving the righteous
