## The Destruction of Jerusalem: Divine Justice and the Remnant

2 Kings 25 records the destruction of Jerusalem and the Temple by Nebuchadnezzar. Islamic interpretation reads this event within the framework of Surah al-Isra' and the broader Quranic theology of divine justice.

## Quranic Framework

### The Second Corruption (Quran 17:4-8)

Many Muslim exegetes identify the Babylonian destruction of Jerusalem as one of the two corruptions mentioned in Surah al-Isra': "Then when the final promise came, [We sent your enemies] to sadden your faces and to enter the temple, as they entered it the first time, and to destroy what they had taken over with total destruction" (Quran 17:7). **Al-Tabari** and **Ibn Kathir** both connect this passage to the events of 2 Kings 25, though they differ on whether it refers to the Assyrian or Babylonian destruction.

### Divine Justice (*'Adl*)

The destruction of Jerusalem is read as an expression of Allah's perfect justice. The Quran teaches: "And your Lord does injustice to no one" (Quran 18:49). The people of Judah received centuries of prophetic warning; their destruction is the consequence of their own choices, not arbitrary divine cruelty.

### The Remnant (25:22-26, 27-30)

The survival of a remnant — both Gedaliah's community and the released Jehoiachin — illustrates the Quranic principle that Allah always preserves a remnant of believers: "And We saved those who believed and used to fear Allah" (Quran 41:18). Even in the most severe judgment, mercy operates.

### Jehoiachin Released (25:27-30)

The release and elevation of Jehoiachin in Babylon demonstrates that divine mercy accompanies divine judgment. The Quran promises: "Indeed, with hardship comes ease" (Quran 94:6). The arc of divine action includes both justice and mercy, punishment and restoration.

## Theological Significance

- **Surah al-Isra' fulfilled**: The Quranic prediction connects to historical events
- **Divine justice**: Judgment comes only after sustained warning and patience
- **A remnant preserved**: Allah always saves those who believe
- **Mercy within judgment**: Even in the harshest punishment, signs of mercy appear
- **Warning to all nations**: The pattern is universal, not limited to Israel
