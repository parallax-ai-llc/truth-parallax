## Prayer, Prophecy, and Divine Rescue

Islamic reading of 2 Kings 19 emphasizes the power of sincere supplication (*du'a*), the role of the prophet as intermediary, and Allah's destruction of the Assyrian army as a sign of His power.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Hezekiah's Du'a (19:14-19)

Hezekiah's prayer — spreading the letter before God and appealing to divine sovereignty — models sincere *du'a*. The Prophet Muhammad taught: "Du'a is the weapon of the believer." Islamic theology affirms that sincere supplication, offered with complete trust in Allah, is one of the most powerful spiritual acts available to the believer.

### The Angel's Destruction (19:35)

The destruction of 185,000 Assyrian soldiers by an angel demonstrates the power of the *mala'ikah* (angels). The Quran describes angelic intervention in battle: at Badr, Allah "reinforced you with a thousand of the angels, following one another" (Quran 8:9). The angelic destruction of the Assyrian army is consistent with the Islamic understanding of how Allah intervenes in history.

### Allah Mocks the Proud (19:21-28)

The oracle mocking Sennacherib's arrogance reflects the Quranic principle that Allah humbles the proud: "So today We will save you in body that you may be to those who succeed you a sign" (Quran 10:92). Sennacherib's death in his own god's temple completes the irony: the idol he trusted was powerless to protect even him.

## Theological Significance

- **Du'a**: Sincere supplication is the believer's most powerful weapon
- **Angelic intervention**: Angels act in the world by Allah's command
- **Allah humbles the proud**: No earthly power can resist the divine will
- **Prophetic mediation**: The prophet conveys Allah's response to the believer's prayer
