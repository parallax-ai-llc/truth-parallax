## The Discovery of the Book and the Call to Return

Islamic interpretation of 2 Kings 22 engages the theme of divine revelation lost and recovered, the authority of Scripture, and the prophetic voice calling to repentance.

## Islamic Interpretation

### The Lost Scripture

The discovery of the Book of the Law in the Temple raises the Islamic doctrine of *tahrif* (alteration of scriptures). If the Torah could be physically lost within the very temple built to house it, Islamic scholars argue, how much more could its text be altered over centuries of transmission? This narrative is sometimes cited as evidence supporting the Islamic position that the biblical text has not been perfectly preserved.

### Response to Divine Revelation

Josiah's response — tearing his garments and seeking prophetic counsel — models the proper response to encountering Allah's word. The Quran describes the faithful as those who "when the verses of the Most Merciful were recited to them, they fell in prostration and weeping" (Quran 19:58). Josiah's grief and humility align with this Quranic ideal.

### The Prophetess Huldah

Islam affirms that Allah can grant prophetic insight to women, though the majority scholarly position is that formal prophetic office (*nubuwwah*) was reserved for men. Huldah may be understood in Islamic terms as having received divine inspiration (*ilham*) rather than formal prophetic commission (*wahy*). This reflects an ongoing scholarly discussion within Islamic theology.

## Theological Significance

- **Tahrif**: The loss of the Book supports Islamic views on scriptural alteration
- **Proper response to revelation**: Humility, repentance, and obedience
- **Women and divine communication**: Allah communicates with whom He wills
- **The importance of preserving Scripture**: The Muslim emphasis on *hifz* (memorization) of the Quran
