## The Fall of Samaria: Divine Punishment for Persistent Disobedience

The fall of the northern kingdom in 2 Kings 17 is a central text for the Islamic understanding of how Allah deals with nations that persist in disobedience. Muslim exegetes connect this event to the Quranic account of the two corruptions of the Children of Israel.

## Quranic Connection

### Surah al-Isra' (17:4-8)

"And We decreed for the Children of Israel in the Scripture: 'You will surely cause corruption on the earth twice, and you will surely reach great haughtiness. So when the promise came for the first of them, We sent against you servants of Ours — those of great military might, and they probed even into the homes, and it was a promise fulfilled.'"

Muslim exegetes (including **Al-Tabari**, **Ibn Kathir**, and **Al-Qurtubi**) identify the fall of Samaria and/or the Babylonian destruction as the fulfillment of this divine decree. The "servants of great military might" are identified with the Assyrians or Babylonians.

### The Pattern of Divine Punishment

The Quran consistently teaches that nations are destroyed when they reject their prophets and persist in corruption. This pattern applies to 'Ad, Thamud, the people of Lut, Pharaoh, and the Children of Israel alike. The fall of Samaria fits this recurring Quranic framework.

### Syncretism as Shirk (17:33-41)

The mixed religion of the settlers — "fearing the LORD and serving their own gods" — is a form of *shirk*. Islam demands absolute *tawhid*: there is no room for mixing the worship of Allah with the worship of anything else.

## Theological Significance

- **Quranic fulfillment**: The fall of Samaria may fulfill Quran 17:4-8
- **The sunnah of Allah**: Nations that reject prophets face destruction
- **Shirk in any form**: Mixing worship is as bad as pure idolatry
- **Warning to all peoples**: The pattern applies universally, not only to Israel
