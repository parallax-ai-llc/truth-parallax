## Elisha's Miracles of Provision and Life

2 Kings 4 is a collection of four miracle narratives that demonstrate the breadth of Elisha's prophetic power and God's compassion for ordinary people. Protestant interpreters frequently draw typological connections between these miracles and the ministry of Christ.

## Key Episodes

### The Widow's Oil (4:1-7)

A prophet's widow faces the loss of her sons to a creditor. Elisha multiplies her single jar of oil to fill every vessel she can borrow. Protestant exegesis highlights the interplay of divine provision and human responsibility: the woman must actively gather vessels and pour. The oil stops only when no more vessels remain.

**Application**: Faith requires action. God's provision meets us in the act of obedience, not in passive waiting.

### The Shunammite Woman's Son (4:8-37)

A wealthy woman provides hospitality to Elisha and is promised a son. When the child dies, she seeks Elisha, who raises the boy from the dead. Protestant typology sees here a direct foreshadowing of Christ's raising of the dead, particularly the widow of Nain's son (Luke 7:11-17).

| Elisha | Christ |
|--------|--------|
| Stretches himself on the dead child | Touches the coffin/bier |
| The child sneezes seven times and lives | "Young man, I say to you, arise" |
| Gives the child back to his mother | Gives him back to his mother |

### Death in the Pot (4:38-41)

Poisonous gourds are inadvertently added to a stew. Elisha neutralizes the poison by adding flour. Protestant commentators see a picture of God's word purifying what is corrupted.

### Feeding the Hundred (4:42-44)

Twenty loaves of barley feed a hundred men with leftovers. This directly anticipates Christ's feeding of the five thousand (John 6:1-14). The pattern of multiplication and surplus is identical.

## Theological Significance

- **God's concern for the poor and vulnerable**: The widow and the Shunammite
- **Faith and persistence**: The Shunammite's refusal to accept her son's death
- **Typology of Christ**: Elisha's miracles prefigure Jesus's greater works
- **Provision beyond need**: Oil that fills every vessel; bread with surplus
