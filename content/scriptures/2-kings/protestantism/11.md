## Athaliah's Usurpation and Joash's Coronation

2 Kings 11 records the only interruption of the Davidic dynasty. When Athaliah, daughter of Ahab and Jezebel, sees her son Ahaziah is dead, she seizes the throne and massacres the royal family. But the infant Joash is hidden in the temple by Jehosheba, and after six years, the priest Jehoiada orchestrates a coup that restores the Davidic line.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Preservation of the Seed (11:1-3)

Athaliah's attempt to destroy the entire Davidic line represents, in Protestant theology, a direct assault on God's covenant promise to David (2 Samuel 7:12-16). The survival of Joash through Jehosheba's intervention demonstrates that no human scheme can thwart divine purpose. This is also understood as a broader spiritual principle: Satan's repeated attempts to destroy the messianic line (Pharaoh's infanticide, Herod's slaughter) all fail.

### Jehoiada's Covenant (11:17)

After Athaliah's execution, Jehoiada "made a covenant between the LORD and the king and people, that they should be the LORD's people, and also between the king and the people." This covenant renewal is a high point in Judah's history. Protestant interpreters see it as a model for national reformation rooted in covenantal commitment.

### The Destruction of Baal (11:18)

The people tear down the temple of Baal and kill the priest Mattan. The Baal cult that Athaliah had imported from her mother Jezebel is eradicated from Judah. Protestant Reformation theology drew parallels between Jehoiada's reforms and the Reformers' purging of what they considered idolatrous practices.

## Theological Significance

- **The indestructible covenant**: God's promise to David cannot be annulled
- **Faithful remnant theology**: One hidden child preserves the entire line
- **The priesthood's role in reform**: Jehoiada models spiritual leadership in political crisis
- **Covenant renewal**: National restoration begins with returning to the LORD
