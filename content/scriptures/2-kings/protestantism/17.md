## The Fall of Samaria

2 Kings 17 is the theological climax of the northern kingdom's story. Shalmaneser V of Assyria besieges Samaria, and in 722 BCE the city falls. The population is deported and replaced with foreign settlers who create the syncretic religion of the Samaritans. The narrator provides an extended theological commentary explaining why Israel fell.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Deportation (17:5-6)

The king of Assyria carries Israel away to Assyria, settling them in Halah, on the Habor, and in the cities of the Medes. The ten northern tribes effectively cease to exist as a political entity. Protestant interpreters emphasize that this is not random historical tragedy but the fulfillment of covenant curses (Deuteronomy 28:36, 64).

### The Theological Explanation (17:7-23)

The narrator provides the most extended theological commentary in Kings. The reasons for Israel's fall:

| Verse | Sin |
|-------|-----|
| 17:7 | Feared other gods |
| 17:8 | Walked in the customs of the nations |
| 17:9 | Built high places |
| 17:10 | Set up pillars and Asherim |
| 17:12 | Served idols |
| 17:16 | Made metal images, worshiped the host of heaven, served Baal |
| 17:17 | Burned sons and daughters, practiced divination |
| 17:19 | Judah also did not keep the commandments |

### "The LORD warned Israel and Judah by every prophet" (17:13)

Protestant theology emphasizes that judgment did not come without extensive warning. Every prophet from Samuel onward had called Israel to repentance. The fall is not God's caprice but the exhaustion of divine patience after centuries of warning.

### The Samaritan Syncretism (17:24-41)

The foreign settlers brought by Assyria create a mixed religion — fearing the LORD while serving their own gods. The narrator's verdict: "They do not fear the LORD" (17:34). Protestant interpreters see here the impossibility of serving God alongside other allegiances — anticipating Christ's teaching that no one can serve two masters.

## Theological Significance

- **Covenant faithfulness determines national destiny**: Israel fell because it violated the covenant
- **God's patience has limits**: Centuries of prophetic warning preceded judgment
- **Syncretism is not worship**: Mixing true and false religion is equivalent to false religion
- **Warning to the church**: The fate of Israel serves as a cautionary example (1 Corinthians 10:11)
