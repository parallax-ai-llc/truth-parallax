## Josiah and the Discovery of the Book of the Law

2 Kings 22 records the pivotal moment when the Book of the Law is discovered during temple repairs under King Josiah. The discovery triggers the most thorough reformation in Judah's history. Protestant interpreters see this chapter as a paradigm for spiritual renewal through the recovery of Scripture.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Josiah's Character (22:2)

"He did what was right in the eyes of the LORD and walked in all the way of David his father, and he did not turn aside to the right or to the left." This is the unqualified commendation given to no other king except David himself. Protestant theology values Josiah as the model of a ruler governed entirely by God's word.

### The Discovery of the Book (22:8-10)

Hilkiah the high priest finds "the Book of the Law" during temple renovations. Most Protestant scholars identify this as some form of Deuteronomy, though some argue for a larger portion of the Pentateuch. The significant point is that the written word of God had been lost — physically misplaced in the very temple built to house it. **Luther** saw in Josiah's experience a parallel to the Reformation's recovery of the Bible from neglect.

### Josiah Tears His Robes (22:11)

When the book is read to Josiah, he tears his garments in grief and horror. "Great is the wrath of the LORD that is kindled against us, because our fathers have not obeyed the words of this book" (22:13). Protestant preaching frequently returns to this response as a model of genuine conviction of sin: the word of God, properly heard, produces not self-justification but repentance.

### Huldah the Prophetess (22:14-20)

Josiah's officials consult Huldah the prophetess, who confirms that the curses in the book will indeed be fulfilled against Jerusalem — but Josiah himself will be gathered to his grave in peace "because your heart was penitent" (22:19). Protestant commentators note without embarrassment that God spoke through a woman when male prophets were also available, demonstrating that prophetic authority is God's to assign.

## Theological Significance

- **Scripture recovered, reformation follows**: The pattern of the Reformation itself
- **The word of God produces conviction**: Josiah's response models genuine repentance
- **The authority of Scripture**: A discovered book changes national policy
- **God speaks through whom He chooses**: Huldah's authority is not diminished by her gender
