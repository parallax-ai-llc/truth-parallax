## Isaiah's Prophecy and Divine Deliverance

2 Kings 19 records Hezekiah's prayer, Isaiah's prophecy against Sennacherib, and the miraculous destruction of 185,000 Assyrian soldiers by the angel of the LORD. The chapter is the climactic vindication of trust in God and the prophetic word.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Hezekiah's Prayer (19:14-19)

Hezekiah spreads Sennacherib's threatening letter before the LORD in the temple and prays. His prayer is a model of biblical petition: he acknowledges God's sovereignty ("You are the God, you alone, of all the kingdoms of the earth," 19:15), presents the specific threat, and asks for deliverance "that all the kingdoms of the earth may know that you, O LORD, are God alone" (19:19). The motive is God's glory, not mere survival.

### Isaiah's Oracle (19:21-34)

Isaiah's response, delivered in elevated poetry, mocks Sennacherib's arrogance: "The virgin daughter of Zion despises you, laughs you to scorn" (19:21). The theological center is verse 28: "I will put my hook in your nose and my bit in your mouth, and I will turn you back on the way by which you came." The mightiest empire on earth is led like a domesticated animal by God.

### The Angel of the LORD (19:35)

"That night the angel of the LORD went out and struck down 185,000 in the camp of the Assyrians." Protestant interpreters accept this as a historical event, often connecting it to Herodotus's account of mice (possibly carrying plague) destroying Sennacherib's army. The suddenness and scale confirm that deliverance came from God alone.

### Sennacherib's Assassination (19:36-37)

Sennacherib returns to Nineveh and is murdered by his own sons while worshiping in the temple of his god Nisroch. The irony is complete: the king who mocked Israel's God dies at the hands of his own children in his own god's temple.

## Theological Significance

- **Prayer as the weapon of faith**: Hezekiah's response to crisis is prayer, not panic
- **God mocks the proud**: Sennacherib's boasting is met with divine derision
- **Deliverance is from the LORD alone**: Human armies play no role
- **Historical vindication**: God's word through Isaiah is fulfilled exactly
