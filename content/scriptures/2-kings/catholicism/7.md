## Deliverance of Samaria and the Obligation to Proclaim

Catholic interpretation of 2 Kings 7 highlights the miraculous deliverance of Samaria, the lepers' discovery of the abandoned camp, and the theological obligation to share good news.

## Key Catholic Readings

### The Prophetic Word Fulfilled (7:1-2)

Elisha's prophecy of abundance in the midst of famine illustrates the Catholic principle that God's word accomplishes what it declares (cf. Isaiah 55:10-11). The skeptical officer who sees but does not eat is a warning against the sin of incredulity — a refusal to believe what God has promised.

### The Four Lepers as Evangelists (7:9)

"This is a day of good news. If we are silent and wait until morning light, punishment will overtake us." Catholic missionary theology has drawn extensively on this verse. Pope Paul VI's *Evangelii Nuntiandi* (1975) echoes this urgency: the Church exists to evangelize, and silence in the face of good news is itself a form of sin.

### Abundance Beyond Expectation (7:16)

The fulfillment exceeds Elisha's already remarkable prophecy. Catholic theology of grace sees here the principle of divine superabundance — God gives not merely what is sufficient but more than what is asked or imagined (cf. Ephesians 3:20).

## Theological Significance

- **The efficacy of God's word**: What God declares comes to pass
- **The obligation to evangelize**: Good news demands proclamation
- **Superabundant grace**: God's provision exceeds human expectation
- **Incredulity judged**: Seeing without believing brings exclusion from blessing
