## The Moabite Campaign and Prophetic Mediation

Catholic interpretation of 2 Kings 3 focuses on Elisha's role as mediator between God and the kings, the relationship between worship and divine guidance, and the disturbing episode of Mesha's sacrifice.

## Key Catholic Readings

### Elisha as Mediator (3:11-19)

Elisha's willingness to prophesy only for Jehoshaphat's sake, not for the idolatrous Jehoram, illustrates the Catholic principle that God's grace often flows through the intercession of the righteous on behalf of sinners. The Fathers see here a pattern that reaches its fullness in the saints' intercessory prayer.

### Music and Prophetic Inspiration (3:15)

The use of music to prepare for prophecy resonates with Catholic liturgical tradition. Sacred music is not mere decoration but a means of disposing the soul to receive divine communication. The Council of Trent and the Second Vatican Council both affirm the integral role of sacred music in worship.

### Mesha's Sacrifice (3:27)

The sacrifice of Mesha's firstborn son on the city wall raises profound questions about the relationship between true and false sacrifice. Catholic moral theology distinguishes between the abomination of child sacrifice and the legitimate offering of one's life to God. The Church Fathers contrast Mesha's desperate act with Abraham's willing obedience in Genesis 22 — and ultimately with God the Father's offering of His own Son.

## Theological Significance

- **Intercession of the righteous**: The holy mediate grace for sinners
- **Sacred music in worship**: Art serves the encounter with God
- **True vs. false sacrifice**: Only God determines what constitutes acceptable offering
- **Divine provision**: God provides water without rain, prefiguring sacramental grace
