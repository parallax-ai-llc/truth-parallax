## The Discovery of the Law and the Authority of Scripture

Catholic interpretation of 2 Kings 22 emphasizes the relationship between Scripture and Tradition, the role of the priesthood in preserving and transmitting the word of God, and the prophetic authority of Huldah.

## Key Catholic Readings

### The Book Found in the Temple (22:8)

The discovery of the Book of the Law in the temple illustrates a Catholic principle: Scripture belongs within the life of the worshiping community. The book was not found in a library or a private collection but in the temple — the heart of Israel's liturgical life. Catholic teaching holds that Scripture is properly read within the living tradition of the Church, not apart from it.

### Josiah's Repentance (22:11-13)

Josiah tears his robes in grief. Catholic moral theology sees this as an act of perfect contrition — sorrow for sin motivated by love of God rather than fear of punishment. Josiah's immediate response to the word of God models the disposition the Church calls for in every believer who encounters Scripture.

### Huldah the Prophetess (22:14-20)

The officials consult Huldah, a woman exercising prophetic authority in Israel. Catholic theology affirms that the charism of prophecy is not limited by sex. While the Catholic Church reserves ordained ministry to men, the prophetic and mystical gifts have been exercised by women throughout salvation history — from Miriam and Deborah through Catherine of Siena and Teresa of Avila.

## Theological Significance

- **Scripture within Tradition**: The word of God belongs in the worshiping community
- **Contrition and conversion**: The proper response to God's word is repentance
- **Women's prophetic gifts**: The Spirit bestows charisms without regard to sex
- **The priesthood's custodial role**: The priests preserve the written word
