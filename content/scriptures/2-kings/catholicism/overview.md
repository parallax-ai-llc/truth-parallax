## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Kings draws on both Sacred Scripture and Sacred Tradition, reading the book within the broader canon and the teaching authority of the Magisterium. The Church Fathers provided extensive commentary on the Elisha narratives, the reforms of faithful kings, and the theological meaning of exile. Catholic exegesis emphasizes typological connections to Christ and the Church, the role of prophetic office, and the moral lessons of kingship.

## Key Interpretive Emphases

### Patristic Typology
The Church Fathers read Elisha's miracles as types of Christ and the sacraments. Elisha's multiplication of oil and bread prefigure the Eucharist. The healing of Naaman in the Jordan prefigures baptism. Augustine, Ambrose, and Ephrem the Syrian all developed these connections.

### The Prophetic Office
Catholic theology sees the prophets as exercising a teaching office analogous to the Magisterium. The prophets' authority to correct kings mirrors the Church's authority to speak truth to secular power.

### Moral Theology of Kingship
The contrast between faithful kings (Hezekiah, Josiah) and wicked kings (Manasseh, Ahaz) provides case studies in Catholic moral theology. The king's responsibility to uphold true worship anticipates Catholic teaching on the duties of Christian rulers.
