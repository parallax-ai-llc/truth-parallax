## Elisha's Death and the Power Beyond the Body

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings 13 is particularly interested in the miracle at Elisha's tomb — a dead man revived by touching the prophet's bones — as it raises questions about the relationship between spirit and matter.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Pneumatic's Power Beyond Death (13:20-21)

The miracle at Elisha's tomb presents a paradox for Gnostic readers: if the material body is a prison, how can a dead body transmit life? One Gnostic resolution holds that the pneumatic teacher's body, even after death, retains a residual charge of the divine energy that animated it. The pneumatic essence has departed to the Pleroma, but its trace (*sphragis*) remains in the material vessel.

An alternative Gnostic reading rejects the miracle as a Demiurgic counterfeit — a false sign designed to bind people to material remains and prevent them from seeking the living *gnosis* that transcends all matter.

### The Arrows and Limited Knowledge (13:14-19)

Jehoash's half-hearted striking of the arrows represents the condition of the psychic person — one who has partial knowledge but lacks the fullness of *gnosis*. The pneumatic strikes with total commitment because they know the full truth; the psychic hesitates because their knowledge is incomplete.

### Covenant Compassion as Demiurgic Strategy (13:23)

God's compassion "because of his covenant with Abraham" is read by Gnostics as the Demiurge's calculated investment in his chosen lineage. The Demiurge shows "compassion" not out of genuine love but to maintain control over the people he has designated as his own.

## Theological Significance

- **Pneumatic trace**: The body may retain traces of divine energy after the spirit departs
- **Material remains**: Gnostic ambivalence about the body's significance
- **Partial knowledge**: The psychic person lacks the fullness of gnosis
- **Demiurgic compassion**: The creator-god's mercy serves his own purposes
