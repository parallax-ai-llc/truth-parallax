## The Hidden King and the Spark Preserved

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings 11 interprets the hiding of the infant Joash as an allegory of the divine spark preserved within the material world despite the archons' attempts to destroy it.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Hidden Spark

Athaliah's massacre of the royal family and the secret preservation of Joash parallels the Gnostic myth of the divine spark hidden within humanity. The archons (represented by Athaliah) attempt to destroy every trace of the divine presence in the material world, but one spark — hidden in the most sacred space — survives. This is the pneumatic seed that cannot be extinguished.

### The Temple as Refuge

Joash is hidden in the temple. In the Gnostic reading, the temple is an ambiguous space: it belongs to the Demiurge, but it also contains within it a hidden sacred dimension — the spark of the true God concealed within the Demiurge's own house. The pneumatic finds refuge even within the archontic system by recognizing the hidden divine presence.

### Covenant as Demiurgic Order

The covenant renewal led by Jehoiada is read as the reestablishment of Demiurgic order — the reassertion of the archontic system after a period of disruption. The people return to the Demiurge's service, not to genuine freedom. From the Gnostic perspective, covenant renewal is a re-enchaining, not a liberation.

## Theological Significance

- **The hidden spark**: The divine presence survives even the archons' attempts at destruction
- **Ambiguity of sacred space**: The temple contains both archontic authority and hidden divinity
- **Covenant as bondage**: Renewing the Demiurge's covenant is not liberation
- **Persistence of the pneumatic seed**: The spark cannot be fully extinguished
