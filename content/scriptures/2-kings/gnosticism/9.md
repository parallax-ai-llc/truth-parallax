## Revolution Within the Archontic Order

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings 9 views Jehu's violent revolution as a conflict between archontic powers — one faction of the Demiurge's servants overthrowing another. No genuine spiritual liberation is achieved.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Anointing as Archontic Commission

Jehu's anointing as king is, in the Gnostic reading, a commission from the Demiurge — one archontic agent replacing another. The oil of anointing belongs to the material ritual order, not to the Pleroma. The prophetic guild that anoints Jehu serves the Demiurge's purposes, rearranging the deck chairs on the sinking ship of material creation.

### Jezebel's Death

Jezebel, who promoted Baal worship, is killed by Jehu, who serves the Demiurge under the name of YHWH. From the Gnostic perspective, both Baal and YHWH are archontic powers. Jezebel's death is merely the triumph of one archon over another — the internal politics of the prison administration, not the liberation of the prisoners.

### Violence as the Demiurge's Method

The pervasive violence of 2 Kings 9 — Joram killed, Ahaziah killed, Jezebel killed — confirms the Gnostic assessment of the material world: it runs on violence. The Demiurge maintains his order through force, and his agents (kings, prophets, armies) perpetuate this pattern. The transcendent God does not act through violence but through the quiet illumination of *gnosis*.

## Theological Significance

- **Revolution as rearrangement**: Changing archontic rulers does not change the system
- **Rival archons**: Baal and YHWH are competing archontic powers
- **Violence as the Demiurge's tool**: The material order runs on force
- **True liberation is interior**: Gnosis, not political revolution, sets the soul free
