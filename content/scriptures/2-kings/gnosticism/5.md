## Naaman's Healing: Purification from Material Contamination

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings 5 reinterprets leprosy as a symbol of the material contamination that afflicts every soul trapped in the Demiurge's world. Naaman's healing represents the purification available through *gnosis*.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### Leprosy as Material Contamination

In Gnostic symbolism, physical disease represents the soul's corruption by matter. Naaman's leprosy is not merely a physical condition but a visible sign of his entrapment in the archontic world. His military power and social status are archontic attributes — they cannot heal the fundamental disease of material existence.

### The Jordan as Boundary Crossing

Naaman's sevenfold immersion in the Jordan is read as a passage through the seven archontic spheres. Each immersion corresponds to the shedding of one archontic influence. When he emerges, his flesh is "like the flesh of a little child" — he is reborn, freed from the accumulated contamination of material life. In Gnostic baptismal practice, the rite symbolized not forgiveness of sin but liberation from archontic bondage.

### Elisha Refuses Payment (5:16)

Gnosis cannot be bought or sold. The pneumatic teacher offers the knowledge of liberation freely, because *gnosis* belongs to the Pleroma, not to the Demiurge's economy. Gehazi's attempt to profit from the healing represents the corruption of *gnosis* by material motives — turning spiritual liberation into a commodity.

### Naaman's Earth (5:17)

Naaman's request for Israelite soil suggests a remnant of archontic thinking: the belief that the divine is localized in particular matter. True *gnosis* transcends all material attachments, including sacred geography.

## Theological Significance

- **Leprosy as material bondage**: Physical disease symbolizes spiritual contamination
- **Seven immersions**: Passage through the seven archontic spheres
- **Gnosis is free**: Spiritual knowledge cannot be commodified
- **Beyond sacred geography**: True divinity is not localized in matter
