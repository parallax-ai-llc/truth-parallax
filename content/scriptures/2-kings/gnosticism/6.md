## Seeing the Invisible: Gnosis as Spiritual Vision

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings 6 focuses on the theme of spiritual sight — the ability to perceive realities invisible to the hylic (material) person. Elisha's opening of his servant's eyes is the quintessential Gnostic moment in 2 Kings.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Opened Eyes (6:15-17)

When Elisha prays for his servant's eyes to be opened, the servant sees the hills filled with horses and chariots of fire. This is the Gnostic experience in miniature: the pneumatic perceives a reality invisible to the ordinary, material person. The "eyes" that are opened are not physical but spiritual — the *nous* (mind/intellect) that perceives the Pleroma behind the material veil.

The Valentinian tradition describes three types of humans: *hylic* (material), *psychic* (soul), and *pneumatic* (spiritual). Only the pneumatic can see the unseen. The servant's transformation from blind panic to calm perception mirrors the Gnostic conversion experience: the reception of *gnosis* transforms one's entire perception of reality.

### The Floating Axe Head (6:1-7)

Iron sinking in water represents the descent of the divine spark into matter. The wood (representing the Cross in orthodox reading, but in Gnostic terms, the tree of *gnosis*) reverses the descent, causing what was submerged to rise. This is the Gnostic drama: the pneumatic element, weighed down by matter, is restored to its proper state through the intervention of knowledge.

### Blindness and Sight (6:18-20)

Elisha strikes the Arameans with blindness and then restores their sight in Samaria. The Gnostic reads this as an allegory of the archons' condition: they think they see but are blind. True sight belongs only to those who possess *gnosis*.

## Theological Significance

- **Spiritual vision**: Gnosis opens the inner eye to perceive the Pleroma
- **Three types of humanity**: Hylic, psychic, and pneumatic
- **The spark restored**: What sinks into matter can be raised by knowledge
- **Archontic blindness**: The rulers of this world are blind to true reality
