## Elisha's Miracles: Gnosis Overcoming Material Limitation

Gnostic interpretation of 2 Kings 4 reads Elisha's miracles as demonstrations of how *gnosis* — knowledge of the true divine order — enables the pneumatic to overcome the limitations of the material world created by the Demiurge.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Multiplication of Oil (4:1-7)

The inexhaustible supply of oil from a single jar demonstrates the principle that the Pleroma's abundance transcends the material world's scarcity. In the Demiurge's realm, resources are limited; but for the one who possesses *gnosis*, the fullness of the divine overflows. Valentinian Gnostics connected oil to the anointing (*chrisma*) that seals the pneumatic for salvation.

### The Raising of the Dead (4:32-37)

Elisha's raising of the Shunammite's son is read as the awakening of the divine spark (*pneuma*) from its dormancy in matter. The child's death represents the soul's entrapment in the material body; Elisha's restoration represents the pneumatic teacher's ability to awaken the spark through *gnosis*. The seven sneezes may represent passage through the seven planetary spheres.

### Poison Neutralized (4:38-41)

The poisoned stew healed by adding flour is a metaphor for how *gnosis* neutralizes the toxic influence of the material world. The Demiurge's creation is inherently poisonous to the spirit; the addition of divine knowledge renders it harmless.

### Feeding Beyond Limitation (4:42-44)

The multiplication of food demonstrates that the pneumatic operates outside the Demiurge's economy of scarcity. Where the archontic world imposes limits, *gnosis* opens access to the Pleroma's superabundance.

## Theological Significance

- **Gnosis overcomes scarcity**: The Pleroma's fullness transcends material limitation
- **Awakening the divine spark**: The pneumatic teacher restores dormant souls
- **Gnosis as antidote**: Knowledge neutralizes the material world's toxicity
- **Archontic economy transcended**: The Demiurge's scarcity gives way to plerotic abundance
