## Temple Repair: Maintaining the Demiurge's House

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings 12 views the repair of the temple as the maintenance of the Demiurge's primary institution — the place where material worship keeps humanity focused on the archontic god rather than on the transcendent Pleroma.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### The Temple as Demiurgic Structure

The physical temple is the Demiurge's house — the center of the archontic religion that binds Israel to the material god. Repairing it is, in the Gnostic view, an act of reinforcing the prison. The Gnostic vision of true worship requires no building, no altar, no sacrifice — only the interior knowledge of one's divine origin.

### Financial Systems and Material Bondage

The elaborate system of collecting and managing temple funds illustrates how the archontic religion operates through material systems. Money, priesthood, institutional authority — these are the mechanisms by which the Demiurge maintains control. The Gnostic reads them as chains disguised as sacred duties.

### Stripping the Temple (12:17-18)

When Joash strips the temple to pay off Hazael, the Gnostic reader sees a moment of inadvertent truth: the temple's treasures are merely material objects, and their removal reveals the emptiness of the institution. The Demiurge's house, stripped of its gold, is just a building.

## Theological Significance

- **The temple as prison**: The physical house of worship reinforces archontic bondage
- **Institutional religion as control**: Priesthood, funds, and ritual maintain the system
- **Material worship is empty**: Stripped of its gold, the temple reveals its true nature
- **True worship is interior**: Gnosis requires no building or institution
