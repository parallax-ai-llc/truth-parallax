## The Purge of Baal: One Archon Defeats Another

Gnostic interpretation of 2 Kings 10 sees Jehu's destruction of Baal worship not as the victory of the true God over a false one but as one archontic power consolidating its dominance over a rival.

## Gnostic Interpretation

### YHWH vs. Baal: Archontic Rivalry

In the Gnostic cosmological framework, both YHWH and Baal are archons — rulers of the material world who compete for human allegiance. Jehu's destruction of the Baal temple is analogous to one territorial power absorbing another. The worshipers of YHWH are no freer than the worshipers of Baal; they have simply changed masters within the same prison.

### The Golden Calves Retained (10:29)

Jehu destroys Baal but keeps the golden calves. The Gnostic reader is not surprised: the Demiurge's system requires some form of material worship. Whether it takes the form of a Baal temple or golden calves is immaterial — the function is the same: keeping humanity focused on the material realm and ignorant of the Pleroma.

### Deception as the System's Nature

Jehu's ruse — pretending to worship Baal in order to destroy its adherents — reveals the inherently deceptive nature of the archontic order. In the Demiurge's world, deception is not an aberration but a structural feature. The archons deceive humanity about the nature of reality; human agents replicate this deception at every level.

## Theological Significance

- **Archontic rivalry**: YHWH and Baal compete within the same archontic system
- **Material worship persists**: Removing one form of idolatry installs another
- **Deception as structural**: The archontic order operates through deception
- **No political liberation**: Changing the form of worship does not free the soul
