## Overview

Gnostic reading of 2 Kings applies the characteristic Gnostic hermeneutical inversion to the historical narratives of Israel's monarchy. While no surviving Gnostic text provides a systematic commentary on 2 Kings, the principles established in Gnostic treatments of Genesis and the Prophets illuminate how these communities would have engaged the material. The key motifs — the Demiurge's flawed creation, the divine spark in elect individuals, and the escape from material bondage — all find resonance in 2 Kings.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### Elijah's Ascent (Chapter 2)
Elijah's departure in a chariot of fire is the single most important passage in 2 Kings for Gnostic interpretation. The ascent of a righteous figure beyond the material realm directly mirrors the Gnostic paradigm of the pneumatic soul escaping the archons' domain. In some Gnostic systems, Elijah is identified as a pneumatic figure who achieved *gnosis* and ascended past the planetary spheres ruled by the archons.

### Elisha as Pneumatic Teacher
Elisha's miracles — making poison harmless (4:38-41), multiplying food (4:42-44), healing Naaman (ch. 5) — can be read as acts of a pneumatic teacher who possesses knowledge that overcomes the material world's limitations. His ability to see the invisible heavenly army (6:17) aligns with the Gnostic emphasis on spiritual perception unavailable to the hylic (material) person.

### The Fall of Kingdoms
The destruction of Israel and Judah illustrates, in Gnostic terms, the ultimate failure of the Demiurge's political order. Kingdoms built on material power inevitably collapse. True sovereignty belongs to the Pleroma, not to earthly thrones.

## Relevant Gnostic Texts

- **The Apocalypse of Elijah**: A text influenced by Gnostic ideas, drawing on Elijah traditions
- **2 Jeu / Books of Jeu**: Describe the soul's ascent through archontic spheres, echoing Elijah's ascent
- **The Apocryphon of John**: Provides the cosmological framework for reading all Old Testament narrative
