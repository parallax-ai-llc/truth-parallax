## The Hidden King and the Restoration of Covenant

Orthodox interpretation of 2 Kings 11 celebrates the providential preservation of the Davidic line through the infant Joash and the priestly leadership of Jehoiada in restoring legitimate worship and governance.

## Orthodox Interpretation

### The Hidden King as Type of Christ

The hiding of the infant Joash in the temple for six years is read typologically as Christ hidden in the world before His manifestation. Just as Joash was preserved from Athaliah's murderous intent, so Christ was preserved from Herod. Orthodox iconography of the Nativity often includes the flight to Egypt, connecting these protective themes.

### Priestly Authority (11:4-12)

Jehoiada's leadership in crowning Joash illustrates the Orthodox understanding of the *symphonia* (harmony) between spiritual and temporal authority. The priest does not usurp the king's role but restores legitimate kingship. In the Byzantine tradition, the patriarch crowns the emperor, establishing the primacy of spiritual authority in legitimating temporal power.

### Covenant Renewal (11:17)

The covenant between God, king, and people mirrors the Orthodox understanding of the Eucharistic assembly: God, clergy, and laity are joined in a covenantal relationship renewed at every Divine Liturgy.

## Theological Significance

- **Providence**: God preserves His purposes through hidden means
- **Symphonia**: Spiritual and temporal authority work in harmony
- **Covenant renewal**: The community's relationship with God must be periodically reaffirmed
- **The indestructibility of the divine plan**: No human scheme can thwart God's economy
