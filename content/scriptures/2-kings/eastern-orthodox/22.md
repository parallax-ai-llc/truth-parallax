## The Recovery of Scripture and the Renewal of Worship

Orthodox interpretation of 2 Kings 22 emphasizes the relationship between the written word and the living tradition of worship, the king's humility before God's word, and the prophetic ministry of Huldah.

## Orthodox Interpretation

### Scripture Within Tradition (22:8)

The discovery of the Book of the Law in the temple illustrates the Orthodox principle that Scripture is not a free-standing document but lives within the worshiping community. The book was found *in the temple* — it belongs there. Orthodox theology holds that Scripture is properly interpreted within the liturgical and patristic tradition of the Church, not apart from it.

### Josiah's Compunction (22:11)

Josiah tears his garments upon hearing the Law. Orthodox ascetical theology calls this response *katanyxis* (compunction) — the piercing of the heart by divine truth. Compunction is a gift of the Holy Spirit, and it is the beginning of genuine *metanoia* (repentance). The liturgy of Great Lent is designed to produce exactly this response.

### Huldah's Prophetic Authority (22:14-20)

The consultation of the prophetess Huldah demonstrates that the Spirit distributes gifts without regard to sex. Orthodox hagiography celebrates numerous women as prophets, teachers, and equal-to-the-apostles (St. Mary Magdalene, St. Nina of Georgia, St. Thekla). The prophetic charism belongs to the Spirit, not to human categories.

## Theological Significance

- **Scripture in liturgical context**: The word of God belongs within worship
- **Compunction**: The proper response to God's word is a pierced heart
- **Women in the prophetic tradition**: The Spirit bestows gifts freely
- **The written word rediscovered**: When Scripture is recovered, renewal follows
