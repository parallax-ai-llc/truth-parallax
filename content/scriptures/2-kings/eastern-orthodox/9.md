## Divine Judgment and the Fall of Jezebel

Orthodox reading of 2 Kings 9 focuses on the fulfillment of prophetic words, the divine commission of Jehu, and the theological significance of Jezebel's death.

## Orthodox Interpretation

### Prophetic Fulfillment

The death of Joram in the field of Naboth (9:25-26) fulfills Elijah's prophecy from 1 Kings 21:19. Orthodox theology emphasizes the certainty of God's word: what the prophet speaks will come to pass, even if generations intervene. This certainty undergirds the Orthodox confidence in the fulfillment of all prophetic and eschatological promises.

### Jezebel's Death (9:30-37)

Jezebel's death marks the end of the most notorious promoter of Baal worship in Israel's history. Orthodox hymnography occasionally alludes to Jezebel as a type of heresy overthrown by orthodoxy. Her death demonstrates that no earthly power can ultimately resist God's purposes.

### The Complexity of Jehu

Orthodox moral theology, like Catholic, recognizes that divine commission does not sanctify every action of the commissioned agent. Jehu serves God's purposes but acts from mixed motives. The later condemnation in Hosea 1:4 confirms this. Orthodox spiritual discernment (*diakrisis*) involves distinguishing between the divine purpose and the human agent's imperfections.

## Theological Significance

- **Prophetic certainty**: God's word is fulfilled across generations
- **The overthrow of false worship**: Jezebel's end represents idolatry's defeat
- **Moral complexity**: Divine use of human agents does not approve all their actions
- **Discernment**: Distinguishing God's purpose from human imperfection
