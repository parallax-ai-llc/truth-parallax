## Overview

Academic study of the Great Law of Peace spans political science, legal theory, history, anthropology, and Indigenous studies. Scholarly interest has focused on several key areas: the dating of the Confederacy's founding (with estimates ranging from the twelfth to the sixteenth century), the relationship between the Great Law and the development of democratic thought in Europe and America, the structure and functioning of the Grand Council as a model of participatory governance, and the legal and philosophical principles embedded in the Great Law.

The debate over Haudenosaunee influence on the United States Constitution has generated significant scholarly attention, with researchers examining the documented interactions between Haudenosaunee leaders and the American founders and the structural parallels between the Great Law and the Constitution. The U.S. Congress acknowledged this influence in House Concurrent Resolution 331 (1988).
