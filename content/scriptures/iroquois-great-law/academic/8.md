This section details the procedures for deliberation and decision-making in the Grand Council. Issues are introduced by the Mohawk, passed across the fire to the Oneida and Cayuga for consideration, then to the Seneca, and finally confirmed by the Onondaga Firekeepers. This process ensures thorough deliberation and consensus rather than majority rule, reflecting the Great Law's commitment to unity and careful governance.

Academic analysis of The Process of Deliberation draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.
