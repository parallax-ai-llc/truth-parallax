This section describes the symbolic planting of the Great Tree of Peace (a white pine) at Onondaga, the central fire of the Confederacy. The tree's four white roots extend to the four directions, inviting all nations to follow them to shelter beneath its branches. An eagle sits atop the tree as a sentinel, watching for threats to the peace. Weapons of war were buried beneath the tree's roots.

Academic analysis of The Planting of the Tree of Peace draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.
