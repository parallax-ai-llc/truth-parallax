This section defines the central role of Clan Mothers in Haudenosaunee governance. Clan Mothers hold the authority to nominate and remove chiefs, making them the ultimate arbiters of political leadership. They are the keepers of the clan's lineage and hold significant authority over domestic and diplomatic affairs. This section reflects the matrilineal and matrilocal nature of Haudenosaunee society.

Academic analysis of The Role of Clan Mothers draws on multiple disciplines including religious studies, anthropology, linguistics, and comparative literature. Scholars have examined this section's historical context, its literary and narrative structure, its relationship to other versions and parallel traditions, and its philosophical implications. Textual analysis reveals complex layers of meaning that reward careful study while resisting reduction to any single interpretive framework.

Critical scholarship has increasingly emphasized the importance of engaging with Indigenous perspectives and interpretive traditions rather than imposing external analytical categories. This section's themes intersect with contemporary academic debates about Indigenous knowledge systems, oral tradition, and the limitations of Western scholarly methodologies when applied to non-Western sacred narratives.
