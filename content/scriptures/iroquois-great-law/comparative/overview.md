## Overview

Comparative analysis of the Great Law of Peace reveals significant parallels with other constitutional and governance traditions worldwide. The federal structure of the Confederacy — uniting sovereign nations under a common framework while preserving their autonomy — anticipates modern federal systems. The separation of peace chiefs and war chiefs parallels the civil-military distinction in other governance traditions. The consensus-based decision-making process offers a contrast to majoritarian democracy.

The role of Clan Mothers in selecting and removing chiefs finds parallels in other matrilineal governance traditions but is distinctive in the degree of formal political authority granted to women. The Great Law's integration of spiritual authority with political governance invites comparison with theocratic traditions, though the Great Law's emphasis on consensus and individual rights distinguishes it from authoritarian theocracies.
