The third section narrates the confrontation with Tadodaho (Atotarho), the powerful and twisted sorcerer of the Onondaga who opposed the peace. Through a combination of spiritual power, persuasion, and the offer of the position of chief firekeeper of the Confederacy, Tadodaho was transformed from an obstacle to peace into its guardian. This transformation represents the Great Law's principle that even the most resistant forces can be brought into harmony.

Theological engagement with The Defeat of Tadodaho raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order.
