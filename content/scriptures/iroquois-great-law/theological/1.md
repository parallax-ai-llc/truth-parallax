The opening section of the Great Law recounts the birth and mission of the Peacemaker (Deganawida), who was born among the Huron (Wendat) people and received a divine mandate to bring peace to the warring nations. His journey across the great lake in a stone canoe demonstrated his spiritual authority and set the stage for the transformation of the Five Nations from perpetual warfare to a confederacy of peace.

Theological engagement with The Peacemaker's Journey raises important questions about the nature of the sacred, the relationship between the divine and the world, and the meaning of human existence within a larger cosmic order. This section's themes invite reflection on fundamental theological questions that transcend any single tradition while being rooted in the specific spiritual vision of this particular culture.

The theological concepts embedded in this section — whether explicit or implicit — offer distinctive perspectives on questions that occupy theologians across traditions: the nature of creation, the relationship between the human and the divine, the meaning of suffering and transformation, and the foundations of moral order.
