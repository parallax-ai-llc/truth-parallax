This section describes the symbolic planting of the Great Tree of Peace (a white pine) at Onondaga, the central fire of the Confederacy. The tree's four white roots extend to the four directions, inviting all nations to follow them to shelter beneath its branches. An eagle sits atop the tree as a sentinel, watching for threats to the peace. Weapons of war were buried beneath the tree's roots.

Anthropological study of The Planting of the Tree of Peace examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.
