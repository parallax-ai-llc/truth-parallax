## Overview

Anthropological study of the Great Law of Peace examines its role in structuring Haudenosaunee social organization, kinship, political culture, and ceremonial life. The Great Law provides the framework for the clan system, the moiety structure, the organization of the longhouse, and the relationship between individual nations and the Confederacy as a whole.

Key anthropological themes include the matrilineal kinship system and its relationship to women's political authority, the role of wampum in diplomacy, record-keeping, and ceremonial practice, the Condolence Ceremony as a mechanism for political transition and grief processing, and the ways in which the Great Law adapts to contemporary challenges while maintaining its foundational principles.
