The final section affirms the eternal and unalterable nature of the Great Law. It establishes that the principles of peace, righteousness, and power (the three pillars of the Great Law) shall endure as long as the earth exists. The section includes provisions for the renewal and transmission of the law across generations, ensuring that the Confederacy's foundational principles remain a living guide for the Haudenosaunee people.

Anthropological study of The Eternal Nature of the Great Law examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
