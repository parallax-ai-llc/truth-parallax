This section establishes the organizational structure of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. The Five Nations (Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca) are arranged in a specific seating pattern at the Grand Council, with the Mohawk and Seneca as Elder Brothers, the Oneida and Cayuga as Younger Brothers, and the Onondaga as Firekeepers. The clan system and moiety structure provide the basis for representation and decision-making.

Anthropological study of The Structure of the Confederacy examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
