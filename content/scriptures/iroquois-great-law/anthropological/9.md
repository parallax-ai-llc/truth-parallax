This section establishes the protocols for war and peace. War chiefs are distinct from peace chiefs (sachems) and hold authority only in times of conflict. The Great Law prefers peace and diplomacy, establishing procedures for warning enemies before engaging in warfare and for offering peace terms. The section also addresses the adoption of individuals and nations into the Confederacy.

Anthropological study of War and Peace Protocols examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice.
