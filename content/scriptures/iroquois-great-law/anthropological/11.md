This section addresses the procedures for adopting individuals and entire nations into the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. Nations seeking shelter under the Tree of Peace may be admitted with full rights and representation. Individuals from other peoples may be adopted into clans. This section reflects the Confederacy's expansive vision of peace and its openness to incorporating others into its framework of governance.

Anthropological study of Adoption and Citizenship examines its role within the broader social, cultural, and ecological context of the community that maintains it. This section's themes are not merely narrative elements but reflect and reinforce specific patterns of social organization, kinship, territorial claims, ceremonial practice, and ecological knowledge.

Anthropological analysis reveals how this section functions within the community's lived experience — how it shapes and is shaped by social relationships, how it encodes practical knowledge about the environment, and how it provides the framework for ritual and ceremonial practice. Understanding these social dimensions is essential for appreciating the full significance of the narrative beyond its surface-level storyline.
