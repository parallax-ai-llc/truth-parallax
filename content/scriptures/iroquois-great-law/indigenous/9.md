This section establishes the protocols for war and peace. War chiefs are distinct from peace chiefs (sachems) and hold authority only in times of conflict. The Great Law prefers peace and diplomacy, establishing procedures for warning enemies before engaging in warfare and for offering peace terms. The section also addresses the adoption of individuals and nations into the Confederacy.

From an Indigenous perspective, War and Peace Protocols carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
