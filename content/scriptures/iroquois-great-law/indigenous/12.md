The final section affirms the eternal and unalterable nature of the Great Law. It establishes that the principles of peace, righteousness, and power (the three pillars of the Great Law) shall endure as long as the earth exists. The section includes provisions for the renewal and transmission of the law across generations, ensuring that the Confederacy's foundational principles remain a living guide for the Haudenosaunee people.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Eternal Nature of the Great Law carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
