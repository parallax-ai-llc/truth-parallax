This section articulates the rights and freedoms guaranteed to individuals within the Confederacy. These include freedom of religion and ceremony, the right to participate in governance through clan representation, and protections against arbitrary authority. The Great Law recognizes the inherent dignity and autonomy of each person while balancing individual rights with communal responsibilities.

From an Indigenous perspective, Individual Rights and Freedoms carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
