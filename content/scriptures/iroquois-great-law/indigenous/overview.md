## Overview

The Great Law of Peace is not a historical artifact but a living constitutional framework that continues to guide the governance of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. At the Six Nations territories in New York and Ontario, the Grand Council still meets according to the protocols established by the Peacemaker, with chiefs selected by Clan Mothers and decisions made through the consensus process described in the Great Law. The Condolence Ceremony continues to mark the installation of new chiefs and the mourning of departed leaders.

Indigenous engagement with the Great Law emphasizes its ongoing relevance as a model of governance, conflict resolution, and the integration of spiritual principles with political practice. The Great Law's principles of peace, righteousness, and power remain central to Haudenosaunee identity and political activism, informing contemporary Indigenous sovereignty movements and diplomatic engagements with settler governments.
