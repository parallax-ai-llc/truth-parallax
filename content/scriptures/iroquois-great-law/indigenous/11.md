This section addresses the procedures for adopting individuals and entire nations into the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. Nations seeking shelter under the Tree of Peace may be admitted with full rights and representation. Individuals from other peoples may be adopted into clans. This section reflects the Confederacy's expansive vision of peace and its openness to incorporating others into its framework of governance.

From an Indigenous perspective, Adoption and Citizenship carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
