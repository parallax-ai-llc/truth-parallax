This section establishes the organizational structure of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. The Five Nations (Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca) are arranged in a specific seating pattern at the Grand Council, with the Mohawk and Seneca as Elder Brothers, the Oneida and Cayuga as Younger Brothers, and the Onondaga as Firekeepers. The clan system and moiety structure provide the basis for representation and decision-making.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Structure of the Confederacy carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
