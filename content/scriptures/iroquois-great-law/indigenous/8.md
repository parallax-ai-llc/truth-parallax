This section details the procedures for deliberation and decision-making in the Grand Council. Issues are introduced by the Mohawk, passed across the fire to the Oneida and Cayuga for consideration, then to the Seneca, and finally confirmed by the Onondaga Firekeepers. This process ensures thorough deliberation and consensus rather than majority rule, reflecting the Great Law's commitment to unity and careful governance.

From an Indigenous perspective, The Process of Deliberation carries living significance for the community's ongoing spiritual and cultural practice. This is not a text to be analyzed from the outside but a sacred narrative that continues to inform daily life, ceremonial practice, and the community's relationship with the land and with each other. The knowledge contained in this section is maintained by specific custodians and transmitted through established protocols that ensure its integrity across generations.

Indigenous engagement emphasizes the inseparability of this narrative from the broader web of cultural practice, language, and identity. The themes addressed here are not abstract philosophical concepts but practical guides for living in right relationship with the community, the natural world, and the spiritual forces that sustain existence.
