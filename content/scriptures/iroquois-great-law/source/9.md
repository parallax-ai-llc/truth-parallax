---
title: "Protection of the House"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The hearth of every household is the center of the family. The smoke hole of every longhouse reaches to the sky. Through it the prayers of the people rise to the Creator.

**2** No lord of the Confederacy shall enter the dwelling of another uninvited. The sanctity of the home shall be respected by all.

**3** If a husband and wife find that they cannot live together in peace, they shall separate. The children shall remain with the mother, for the mother is the keeper of the house and the nurturer of the nation.

**4** The possessions of the household belong to the women. If a man leaves the household, he takes only his personal belongings.

**5** The welfare of children is the first duty of the nation. No child shall be without food, shelter, or care. The community shall provide for orphans and for those whose parents cannot provide.
