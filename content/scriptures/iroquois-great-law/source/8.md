---
title: "Symbols of the Confederacy"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Five arrows shall be bound together very strong, and each arrow shall represent one nation. As the five arrows are strongly bound, this shall symbolize the complete union of the nations.

**2** A bunch of wampum strings, each string representing one nation, bound together, shall symbolize the Confederacy and each nation within it.

**3** The Great White Pine, the Tree of Peace, is the symbol of the Confederacy. Its white roots extend in the four directions, inviting all who desire peace to follow them to the shade of the tree.

**4** The eagle atop the Tree of Peace represents vigilance. The nations must always be watchful for dangers that may threaten the Great Peace.

**5** The circle of fifty lords, the Council of the Confederacy, represents the unity of purpose and the equality of the nations within the Great Peace.
