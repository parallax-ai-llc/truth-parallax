---
title: "The Planting of the Great Tree of Peace"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 1
---

**1** I am Dekanawida, and with the Five Nations confederate lords I plant the Tree of the Great Peace.

**2** I name the tree the Tree of the Great Long Leaves. Under the shade of this Tree of the Great Peace, we spread the soft, white, feathery down of the globe thistle as seats for you, Adodarho and your cousin lords.

**3** We place you upon those seats, spread soft with the feathery down of the globe thistle, there beneath the shade of the spreading branches of the Tree of Peace.

**4** Roots have spread out from the Tree of the Great Peace, one to the north, one to the east, one to the south, and one to the west. These are the Great White Roots, and their nature is peace and strength.

**5** If any man or any nation outside the Five Nations shall obey the laws of the Great Peace and make known their disposition to the lords of the Confederacy, they may trace the roots to the tree.

**6** If their minds are clean, and if they are obedient and promise to obey the wishes of the Confederate Council, they shall be welcomed to take shelter beneath the Tree of the Long Leaves.

**7** We place at the top of the Tree of the Long Leaves an eagle who is able to see afar. If he sees in the distance any evil approaching, or any danger threatening, he will at once warn the people of the Confederacy.
