---
title: "Funeral Addresses and Mourning"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 7
---

**1** When a lord of the Confederacy dies, his colleague lords shall mourn him. The Council Fire shall be covered with ashes in token of grief.

**2** The Condolence Council shall be held to comfort the mourners and to raise a new lord in place of the departed. The ceremony shall begin with the Words of Condolence.

**3** We come to grieve with you. We wipe the tears from your eyes so that you may see clearly. We remove the obstruction from your ears so that you may hear. We remove the grief from your throat so that you may speak and breathe freely.

**4** In the fullness of time, when the mourning period has passed, the Noble Women of the clan shall nominate a new lord to fill the vacant seat.

**5** The new lord shall be invested with the title and the antlers of office. He shall be admonished to be patient, to be fair, and to always seek the welfare of his people.

**6** Thus the continuity of the Confederacy is maintained. As one lord falls, another rises. As one torch is extinguished, another is lit. The Great Peace endures.
