---
title: "Laws of War"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 6
---

**1** When the Confederate Council declares war, the War Chief shall be the leader of the warriors of the Five Nations. He shall carry the emblem of war.

**2** Before the Five Nations unite against a foreign nation, the Confederate Council shall try persuasion first. If the foreign nation refuses to accept the Great Peace, then the council may declare war.

**3** If an enemy nation refuses to accept the Great Peace after being asked three times, the War Chief and his warriors shall go forth with the weapons of war.

**4** When peace is made with a conquered nation, the terms shall be just. The conquered people shall be offered the Great Peace. If they accept, they shall be admitted to the protection of the Confederacy.

**5** No lord of the Confederacy shall take part in war. The lords are peace lords. Their duty is to deliberate and decide, not to fight. The War Chief is separate from the civil authority.

**6** When the war is over, the War Chief shall lay down his office and return to his status as an ordinary citizen. The authority of war shall not continue in time of peace.
