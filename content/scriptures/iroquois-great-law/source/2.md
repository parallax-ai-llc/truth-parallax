---
title: "The Constitution of the Council"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The lords of the Confederacy of the Five Nations shall be mentors of the people for all time. The thickness of their skin shall be seven spans, which is to say that they shall be proof against anger, offensive actions, and criticism.

**2** Their hearts shall be full of peace and good will, and their minds filled with a yearning for the welfare of the people of the Confederacy.

**3** With endless patience they shall carry out their duty. Their firmness shall be tempered with a tenderness for their people. Neither anger nor fury shall find lodgement in their minds.

**4** All their words and actions shall be marked by calm deliberation. In all of their deliberations in the Confederate Council, the lords shall be guided by the wishes of the people.

**5** If a lord of the Confederacy should seek to establish any authority independent of the jurisdiction of the Confederacy of the Great Peace, which is the Five Nations, he shall be warned three times. If the offending lord continues, he shall be removed by the War Chief.

**6** The lords of the Confederacy shall be the channel through which the will of the people is expressed. They shall not speak their own minds in council, but shall speak the will of those they represent.
