---
title: "Rights of the People"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The women heirs of the Confederacy lordship titles shall be called the Noble Women. They alone shall have the power to nominate lords.

**2** Whenever a lord falls ill, his colleague lords shall visit him and attend to his needs. If the illness is fatal, the Council shall gather to mourn and to raise a new lord in his place.

**3** The rights of the people of the Five Nations to free and unrestricted suffrage in all elections of lords of the Confederacy is hereby guaranteed.

**4** Any person of the Five Nations may express in open council their dissatisfaction with any matter before the council. The lords must hear them.

**5** The people shall have the right to voice their opinions through their chosen speakers. In matters that affect the welfare of all, every voice shall be heard.

**6** Whenever an especially important matter or a great emergency is presented before the Confederate Council, the lords shall consider it and if it is found that the question affects the welfare of the whole body of the Five Nations, the lords shall submit the matter to the people for discussion.
