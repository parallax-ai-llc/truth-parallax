---
title: "Religious Ceremonies"
scripture: "iroquois-great-law"
chapter: 10
---

**1** The rites and festivals of each nation shall be observed at the appointed times. The Midwinter Ceremony, the Maple Festival, the Planting Ceremony, the Strawberry Festival, the Green Corn Festival, and the Harvest Festival shall be held as our ancestors established them.

**2** The keepers of the faith shall conduct these ceremonies with reverence and proper form. They shall recite the Thanksgiving Address, giving thanks to all the elements of creation.

**3** We give thanks to our Mother the Earth, who sustains us. We give thanks to the rivers and streams that supply us with water. We give thanks to all the herbs and plants that furnish medicines for the cure of our diseases.

**4** We give thanks to the corn, beans, and squash, which give us life. We give thanks to the bushes and trees that provide us with fruit. We give thanks to the wind, which moves the air and banishes disease.

**5** We give thanks to the moon and stars, which give us light when the sun has gone to rest. We give thanks to our grandfather the Sun. We give thanks to the Creator who dwells in the sky-world, who gives us all things for our good.
