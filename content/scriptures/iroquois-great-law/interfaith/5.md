This section establishes the organizational structure of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. The Five Nations (Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca) are arranged in a specific seating pattern at the Grand Council, with the Mohawk and Seneca as Elder Brothers, the Oneida and Cayuga as Younger Brothers, and the Onondaga as Firekeepers. The clan system and moiety structure provide the basis for representation and decision-making.

Interfaith engagement with The Structure of the Confederacy offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
