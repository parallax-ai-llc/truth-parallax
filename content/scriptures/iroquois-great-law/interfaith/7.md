This section defines the central role of Clan Mothers in Haudenosaunee governance. Clan Mothers hold the authority to nominate and remove chiefs, making them the ultimate arbiters of political leadership. They are the keepers of the clan's lineage and hold significant authority over domestic and diplomatic affairs. This section reflects the matrilineal and matrilocal nature of Haudenosaunee society.

Interfaith engagement with The Role of Clan Mothers offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness.
