The final section affirms the eternal and unalterable nature of the Great Law. It establishes that the principles of peace, righteousness, and power (the three pillars of the Great Law) shall endure as long as the earth exists. The section includes provisions for the renewal and transmission of the law across generations, ensuring that the Confederacy's foundational principles remain a living guide for the Haudenosaunee people.

Interfaith engagement with The Eternal Nature of the Great Law offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
