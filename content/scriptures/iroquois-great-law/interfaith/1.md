The opening section of the Great Law recounts the birth and mission of the Peacemaker (Deganawida), who was born among the Huron (Wendat) people and received a divine mandate to bring peace to the warring nations. His journey across the great lake in a stone canoe demonstrated his spiritual authority and set the stage for the transformation of the Five Nations from perpetual warfare to a confederacy of peace.

Interfaith engagement with The Peacemaker's Journey offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
