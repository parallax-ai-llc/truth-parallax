This section articulates the rights and freedoms guaranteed to individuals within the Confederacy. These include freedom of religion and ceremony, the right to participate in governance through clan representation, and protections against arbitrary authority. The Great Law recognizes the inherent dignity and autonomy of each person while balancing individual rights with communal responsibilities.

Interfaith engagement with Individual Rights and Freedoms offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
