This section establishes the protocols for war and peace. War chiefs are distinct from peace chiefs (sachems) and hold authority only in times of conflict. The Great Law prefers peace and diplomacy, establishing procedures for warning enemies before engaging in warfare and for offering peace terms. The section also addresses the adoption of individuals and nations into the Confederacy.

Interfaith engagement with War and Peace Protocols offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
