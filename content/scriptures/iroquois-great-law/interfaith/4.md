This section describes the symbolic planting of the Great Tree of Peace (a white pine) at Onondaga, the central fire of the Confederacy. The tree's four white roots extend to the four directions, inviting all nations to follow them to shelter beneath its branches. An eagle sits atop the tree as a sentinel, watching for threats to the peace. Weapons of war were buried beneath the tree's roots.

Interfaith engagement with The Planting of the Tree of Peace offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
