This section tells the story of Hiawatha (Ayenwatha), a man consumed by grief after the loss of his family. The Peacemaker's encounter with Hiawatha and the development of the Condolence Ceremony — using strings of wampum to wipe away tears, open ears, and clear the throat — established one of the most important ritual and diplomatic protocols of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy.

Interfaith engagement with Hiawatha and the Consolation offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
