This section addresses the procedures for adopting individuals and entire nations into the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. Nations seeking shelter under the Tree of Peace may be admitted with full rights and representation. Individuals from other peoples may be adopted into clans. This section reflects the Confederacy's expansive vision of peace and its openness to incorporating others into its framework of governance.

Interfaith engagement with Adoption and Citizenship offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
