The third section narrates the confrontation with Tadodaho (Atotarho), the powerful and twisted sorcerer of the Onondaga who opposed the peace. Through a combination of spiritual power, persuasion, and the offer of the position of chief firekeeper of the Confederacy, Tadodaho was transformed from an obstacle to peace into its guardian. This transformation represents the Great Law's principle that even the most resistant forces can be brought into harmony.

Interfaith engagement with The Defeat of Tadodaho offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
