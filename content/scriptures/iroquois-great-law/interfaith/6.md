This section outlines the qualifications, rights, and responsibilities of the 50 royaneh (chiefs or sachems) who sit on the Grand Council. Chiefs must be of good character, patient, and selfless. They hold their titles through the clans and can be removed by the Clan Mothers if they fail in their duties. The section establishes that chiefs are servants of the people, not rulers over them.

Interfaith engagement with The Rights and Duties of Chiefs offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.
