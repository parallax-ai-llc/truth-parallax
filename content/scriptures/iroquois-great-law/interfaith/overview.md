## Overview

Interfaith engagement with the Great Law of Peace offers rich opportunities for dialogue on the spiritual foundations of governance, the pursuit of peace, the role of women in religious and political authority, and the integration of moral principles with practical governance. The Great Law's vision of peace as an active creation — requiring institutional structures, ongoing maintenance, and the transformation of adversaries into allies — resonates with peace-building traditions across many faiths.

The Peacemaker's story invites comparison with other prophetic figures who brought new social and spiritual orders: Moses, Muhammad, and the Buddha, among others. The Great Law's emphasis on consensus, inclusivity, and the protection of individual rights within a communal framework offers a distinctive Indigenous contribution to global conversations about justice, governance, and the common good.
