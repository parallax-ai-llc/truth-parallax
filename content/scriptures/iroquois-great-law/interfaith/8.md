This section details the procedures for deliberation and decision-making in the Grand Council. Issues are introduced by the Mohawk, passed across the fire to the Oneida and Cayuga for consideration, then to the Seneca, and finally confirmed by the Onondaga Firekeepers. This process ensures thorough deliberation and consensus rather than majority rule, reflecting the Great Law's commitment to unity and careful governance.

Interfaith engagement with The Process of Deliberation offers opportunities for cross-traditional dialogue on shared human concerns expressed through this tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The themes addressed in this section — creation, transformation, moral order, the relationship between humans and the natural world — are concerns that resonate across the world's spiritual traditions while being expressed here in uniquely powerful ways.

Respectful interfaith dialogue with this material requires acknowledging both the common ground it shares with other traditions and its irreducible distinctiveness. When approached with humility and genuine openness, this section offers resources for enriching one's own spiritual understanding while deepening appreciation for the breadth and depth of human spiritual experience.
