---
title: "Aitareya Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "aitareya-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** These deities, thus sent forth, fell into this great ocean. He subjected that person to hunger and thirst. They said to him: "Find out for us an abode wherein being established we may eat food."

**2** He brought them a cow. They said: "This is not enough." He brought them a horse. They said: "This is not enough."

**3** He brought them man. They said: "Well done, indeed." Therefore man is well done. He said to them: "Enter, each according to his place."

**4** Fire became speech, and entered the mouth. Air became scent, and entered the nostrils. The sun became sight, and entered the eyes. The quarters became hearing, and entered the ears.

**5** Plants and trees became hairs, and entered the skin. The moon became mind, and entered the heart. Death became the down-breathing, and entered the navel. Water became seed, and entered the generative organ.

**6** Hunger and thirst said to him: "Find an abode for us also." He said to them: "I assign you to these deities; I make you co-sharers with them." Therefore to whatever deity an oblation is offered, hunger and thirst are sharers in it.
