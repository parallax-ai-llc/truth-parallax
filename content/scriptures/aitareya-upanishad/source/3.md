---
title: "Aitareya Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "aitareya-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** He thought: "Here now are the worlds and the guardians of the worlds. Let me send forth food for them."

**2** He brooded over the water. From the water thus brooded over, a form was born. The form that was born is food.

**3** The food so made, wished to flee, turning away. He tried to seize it by speech. He could not seize it by speech. If he had seized it by speech, then simply by speaking one would be satisfied with food.

**4** He tried to seize it by scent. He could not seize it by scent. If he had seized it by scent, then simply by smelling one would be satisfied with food.

**5** He tried to seize it by the eye. He could not seize it by the eye. If he had seized it by the eye, then simply by seeing one would be satisfied with food.

**6** He tried to seize it by hearing. He could not seize it by hearing. If he had seized it by hearing, then simply by hearing one would be satisfied with food.

**7** He tried to seize it by the skin. He could not seize it by the skin. If he had seized it by the skin, then simply by touching one would be satisfied with food.

**8** He tried to seize it by the mind. He could not seize it by the mind. If he had seized it by the mind, then simply by thinking one would be satisfied with food.

**9** He tried to seize it by the generative organ. He could not seize it by the organ. If he had seized it by the organ, then simply by emission one would be satisfied with food.

**10** He tried to seize it by the down-breathing, and he seized it. Thus it is Vâyu, the air, who takes food, and it is Vâyu who lives on food.

**11** He thought: "How can this exist without me?" He thought: "By which way shall I enter?" He thought: "If speech speaks, if scent smells, if the eye sees, if the ear hears, if the skin feels, if the mind thinks, if the down-breathing draws in, if the organ emits, then who am I?"

**12** He opened the suture of the skull, and entered by that door. That door is called Vidrti, the cleft. That is the place of bliss.

**13** He, having entered in, considered: "What is it that would be here without me?" He perceived this person, the Brahman most far-reaching, and said: "I saw it."

**14** Therefore his name is Idandra, for Idandra is his name. Him who is Idandra they call Indra mysteriously, for the gods love mystery.
