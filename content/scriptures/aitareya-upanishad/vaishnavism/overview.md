## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Aitareya Upanishad as revealing Vishnu-Narayana as the supreme Atman from whom all creation proceeds. The opening declaration that "in the beginning, the Atman alone existed" is understood as referring to the Supreme Lord prior to creation. The cosmic person whose body becomes the universe is identified with the *Virat-purusha* of the Purusha Sukta, who is Vishnu Himself.

## Theological Emphases

The *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" (*prajnanam brahma*) is read by Vaishnavas not as asserting the identity of individual consciousness with an impersonal absolute but as affirming that supreme consciousness belongs to the personal God. Ramanuja interprets *prajnana* as the essential attribute of the Lord, who is the supreme knower and consciousness itself. The three births described in Chapter 2 are understood within the framework of the soul's journey through Vishnu's creation.
