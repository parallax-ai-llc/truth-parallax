Chapter 2 describes the three births of a person: conception in the womb, physical birth, and rebirth after death. Vaishnava interpreters understand these as stages in the soul's journey through the Lord's creation. The father's role in placing the seed in the mother is read as the soul's transmigration under Vishnu's guidance — the Lord as *antaryami* (inner controller) directs each soul to its appropriate birth according to *karma*.

The teaching that the father nurtures his son as "another self" reflects the Vaishnava understanding of familial duty (*dharma*) as service to God through the generations. The third birth — after death — points to the soul's continuation beyond the body, either toward liberation through devotion to Vishnu or toward rebirth in the material world.
