Chapter 1 describes how the Atman alone existed before creation and willed the universe into being. Vaishnava commentators identify this Atman as Vishnu-Narayana, the supreme personal God whose creative will (*sankalpa*) brings forth the cosmic waters, the cosmic person (*purusha*), and all the elements. The entry of fire, wind, sun, and other deities into the cosmic body reflects Vishnu's sovereignty — all gods serve as instruments of His creative activity.

The Upanishad's statement that the Atman "looked over" creation and resolved to enter it (1.1.3) is read as describing the Lord's immanent presence as Paramatma (Supersoul) within every being, a cornerstone of Vaishnava theology. Creation is not a mere cosmic accident but the purposeful act of a personal God who enters His own creation to sustain and guide it.
