Chapter 3 culminates in the *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" (*prajnanam brahma*), which Vaishnava commentators interpret as affirming that supreme consciousness belongs to the personal God Vishnu. Ramanuja argues that *prajnana* is the essential and defining attribute (*svarupa-nirupaka-dharma*) of the Lord, not that consciousness is an impersonal absolute. Brahman is supremely conscious — He is the knower, the seer, the thinker — and this consciousness is His very nature.

The chapter's enumeration of different types of awareness — perception, knowledge, wisdom, insight, determination — culminating in the declaration *prajnanam brahma* demonstrates for Vaishnavas that all consciousness derives from and depends upon the supreme consciousness of the Lord. Individual souls are conscious because they participate in God's consciousness, but they are never identical with it.
