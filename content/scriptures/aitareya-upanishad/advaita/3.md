Chapter 3 contains the Aitareya's supreme teaching and one of the four *mahavakyas* of Advaita Vedanta: *prajnanam brahma* — "consciousness is Brahman." Shankara's commentary emphasizes that this verse identifies pure consciousness — not as an attribute of Brahman but as Brahman's very essence. Brahman does not *have* consciousness; Brahman *is* consciousness.

The chapter lists various cognitive functions — perception, discernment, intelligence, wisdom — and then identifies them all as "names of consciousness" (*prajnanasya namadheya*). This radical reduction of all mental operations to a single principle of awareness, and then identification of that awareness with the absolute, is the Aitareya's unique contribution to Advaita. Liberation consists in recognizing that one's own consciousness — the witness of all experience — is Brahman itself.
