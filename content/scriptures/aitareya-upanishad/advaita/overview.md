## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Aitareya Upanishad is supremely important as the source of one of the four *mahavakyas*: *prajnanam brahma* ("consciousness is Brahman"). Shankara's commentary treats this text as a definitive proof that the individual self and the universal Brahman are identical in essence, and that consciousness — not matter, not action, not any quality — is the very nature of the absolute.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara reads the creation account not as a description of real origination but as an *adhyaropa* (provisional attribution) to be later negated. The Atman does not truly become many; the appearance of multiplicity is due to *maya*. The identification of consciousness with Brahman in Chapter 3 represents the Upanishad's ultimate teaching: once one recognizes pure awareness as one's own nature, the illusion of duality is destroyed and liberation is immediate.
