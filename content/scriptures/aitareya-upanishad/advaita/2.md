For Advaita, Chapter 2's three births operate at the empirical level of reality (*vyavaharika*). The self that appears to be born, to live, and to die is the individual soul (*jiva*) under the spell of ignorance (*avidya*). From the absolute standpoint (*paramarthika*), the Atman is unborn, unchanging, and deathless.

Shankara uses this chapter to establish the problem that the *mahavakya* of Chapter 3 will solve: how can the infinite, unborn Brahman appear as a finite being subject to birth and death? The answer is *adhyasa* — superimposition of birth and death upon the birthless self. Understanding the three births philosophically leads to the recognition that one was never truly born and thus can never truly die.
