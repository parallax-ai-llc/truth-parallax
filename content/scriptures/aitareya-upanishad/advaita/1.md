Shankara reads the creation account as a teaching device (*adhyaropa-apavada*): creation is provisionally described to lead the student toward the ultimate truth that Brahman alone is real. The Atman that exists alone before creation is non-dual Brahman. The statement that the Atman "created these worlds" (1.1.1) does not imply real transformation but apparent manifestation through *maya*.

The significance of Chapter 1 for Advaita lies in establishing that the sole reality prior to creation is conscious — the Atman "saw" and willed. This consciousness is not a quality of Brahman but Brahman's very nature, preparing the ground for the *mahavakya* of Chapter 3. The entry of the self into the created body introduces the problem of bondage that knowledge alone can resolve.
