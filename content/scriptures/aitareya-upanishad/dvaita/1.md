Madhva reads Chapter 1 as demonstrating Vishnu's absolute sovereignty as creator. The Atman who exists before creation and creates by His will is the personal God, eternally distinct from the souls and matter He creates. The cosmic person's body becoming the universe illustrates God's power (*shakti*) and His deliberate design of creation.

The entry of various deities into the cosmic body demonstrates the Dvaita hierarchy: all gods are subordinate to Vishnu, serving specific functions within His creation. The Upanishad's systematic account of creation — from the cosmic waters to the cosmic person to the individual body — reflects the orderly, purposeful nature of divine governance.
