## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita reading of the Aitareya Upanishad emphasizes that the Atman who creates the universe is the Supreme Lord Vishnu, eternally distinct from the individual souls and the material world. The creation account demonstrates God's absolute sovereignty — He alone exists prior to creation and brings forth all beings by His will.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, *prajnanam brahma* means that supreme consciousness is the defining attribute of the personal God Vishnu, not that impersonal consciousness is the absolute. The souls that enter created bodies remain eternally distinct from the Supreme Atman. The Upanishad's hierarchy of creation — with the Atman creating progressively — reflects the Dvaita understanding of God's orderly governance (*niyamana*) of the universe. Individual consciousness is real but dependent on and subordinate to divine consciousness.
