Madhva reads *prajnanam brahma* as a predication, not an identity statement: Brahman (Vishnu) is characterized by supreme consciousness, which is His essential nature and distinguishes Him from the limited consciousness of individual souls. The chapter's listing of various types of awareness demonstrates the range of consciousness but ultimately points to God as the supreme and unlimited knower.

For Dvaita, individual souls possess consciousness as a real attribute, but their consciousness is finite and dependent, whereas God's consciousness is infinite and independent. The identification of consciousness with Brahman does not erase the distinction between souls and God but highlights that consciousness in its fullness belongs to Vishnu alone. Liberation means attaining a state of bliss in the presence of this supremely conscious God.
