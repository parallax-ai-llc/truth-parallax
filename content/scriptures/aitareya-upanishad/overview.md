## Overview

The Aitareya Upanishad belongs to the Rig Veda and is one of the oldest Upanishads. It presents a profound cosmogony tracing the creation of the universe from the Atman alone, the entry of the self into the created body, and the nature of consciousness (*prajnanam*) as the essence of Brahman. Its famous declaration *prajnanam brahma* ("consciousness is Brahman") is one of the four *mahavakyas* (great sayings) of Vedanta.

## Structure

The Aitareya Upanishad consists of three chapters within the Aitareya Aranyaka:

1. **Chapter 1 — Creation**: The Atman creates the universe, the cosmic person, and the elements
2. **Chapter 2 — The Three Births**: The soul's entry into the body and the three births of a person
3. **Chapter 3 — Consciousness as Brahman**: The identification of consciousness with Brahman

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: The Atman who creates is Vishnu/Narayana, the supreme person
- **Shaivism**: Consciousness (*prajnana*) is Shiva's fundamental nature
- **Advaita Vedanta**: A primary proof-text for the identity of consciousness and Brahman
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: God as the supreme creator distinct from individual souls
- **Academic**: Important evidence for early Vedic cosmogonic thought
- **Comparative**: Creation from consciousness parallels idealist philosophies worldwide

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Creation from Self | The universe arises from the Atman alone |
| Consciousness as Brahman | Prajnanam Brahma — one of the four mahavakyas |
| The Three Births | Physical birth, spiritual birth, and birth after death |
| Entry of the Self | How the self enters the created body |
| Cosmic Person | The universe conceived as a divine person |
