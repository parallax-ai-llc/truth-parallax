## Overview

The Aitareya Upanishad forms chapters 4-6 of the Aitareya Aranyaka, itself part of the Rig Vedic tradition. Scholars date it to approximately the 6th-5th century BCE, placing it among the older prose Upanishads. Its cosmogonic narrative represents a transitional stage between the ritualistic cosmogonies of the Brahmanas and the more abstract philosophical speculation of later Upanishads.

## Research Themes

Patrick Olivelle notes that the Aitareya's creation account retains mythological elements (the cosmic person, the opening of bodily apertures for the gods) while moving toward the philosophical insight that consciousness is the unifying principle of reality. The *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" may represent one of the earliest systematic identifications of awareness itself as the ultimate principle, anticipating later philosophical idealism. The three births doctrine in Chapter 2 offers important evidence for early Vedic attitudes toward reproduction, education, and death.
