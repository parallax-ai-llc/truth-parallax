Chapter 1 presents one of the most detailed cosmogonic accounts in the Upanishadic literature. Scholars note its retention of mythological imagery (the cosmic waters, the cosmic egg, the opening of bodily apertures) alongside more abstract philosophical reflection. Keith identifies echoes of Rig Vedic creation hymns (especially RV 10.90, the Purusha Sukta) combined with new speculative elements.

The narrative structure — the Atman alone, then creation, then the problem of how the self relates to the body — may reflect an actual pedagogical sequence used in Vedic schools. Olivelle notes that the systematic mapping of cosmic elements onto bodily functions represents an early form of the *adhyatma* (self-referential) interpretation that becomes central to later Upanishadic thought.
