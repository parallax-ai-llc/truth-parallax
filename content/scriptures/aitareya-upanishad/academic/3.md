Chapter 3's *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" represents a watershed in Indian philosophical history. Scholars debate whether *prajnana* here denotes a subjective awareness (the witnessing consciousness of later Advaita), an objective intelligence pervading the cosmos (closer to Samkhya's *mahat*), or the cognitive capacity that distinguishes living beings from inert matter.

The chapter's enumeration of cognitive terms — *samjnana*, *ajnana*, *vijnana*, *prajnana*, *medha*, *drishti*, *dhriti*, *mati*, *manisha*, *smriti*, *sankalpa*, among others — provides an important early vocabulary of mind and consciousness in Indian philosophy. Joel Brereton notes that the Aitareya's identification of these diverse functions as aspects of a single *prajnana* anticipates the later philosophical project of reducing mental complexity to a unified principle of awareness.
