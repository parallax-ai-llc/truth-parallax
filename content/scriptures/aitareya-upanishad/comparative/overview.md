## Overview

The Aitareya Upanishad's teaching that consciousness is the ultimate reality and the source of creation invites comparison with idealist philosophies worldwide. Its cosmogony — the universe arising from a single conscious self — parallels creation accounts in which mind or spirit precedes and produces matter.

## Comparative Themes

The creation from the Atman alone echoes the Neoplatonic emanation from the One, the Kabbalistic *tzimtzum* (contraction of the divine to make room for creation), and the Christian *creatio ex nihilo* reinterpreted through idealist lenses. The *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" finds parallels in Bishop Berkeley's idealism, Hegel's Absolute Spirit, and the Buddhist Yogacara school's mind-only (*vijnapti-matra*) doctrine. The cosmic person (*purusha*) whose body becomes the world compares with the Norse Ymir, the Chinese Pangu, and the Mesopotamian Tiamat.
