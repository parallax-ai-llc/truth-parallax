The three births of the Aitareya invite comparison with birth symbolism across traditions. The notion of spiritual rebirth — a second or third birth beyond the physical — parallels the Christian concept of being "born again" (John 3:3-7), the Zoroastrian *naojote* (spiritual investiture), and initiation rituals in indigenous traditions worldwide.

The detailed interest in embryological development anticipates the Buddhist *Garbhavakranti Sutra* and comparable texts in Greek medicine (Hippocrates, Aristotle). The continuity of the self through death into a new birth compares with the Platonic doctrine of the soul's immortality and reincarnation as presented in the *Phaedo* and *Republic*.
