The Aitareya's creation from a solitary conscious self invites comparison with creation myths worldwide. The cosmic waters preceding creation parallel Genesis 1 (the spirit moving over the waters), the Egyptian Nun (primordial ocean), and the Babylonian Apsu. The cosmic person whose body becomes the universe echoes the Norse myth of Ymir, the Chinese Pangu, and the Babylonian Tiamat.

The deliberate, conscious act of creation — the Atman "seeing" and willing the world into existence — contrasts with creation through accident or conflict found in many mythologies, aligning more closely with the purposeful creation of monotheistic traditions. The mapping of cosmic elements onto the body anticipates microcosm-macrocosm thinking found in Hermeticism, Kabbalah, and Daoist internal alchemy.
