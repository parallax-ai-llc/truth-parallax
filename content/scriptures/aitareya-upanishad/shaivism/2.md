The Aitareya's three births doctrine receives a distinctive reading in Shaiva traditions. The first birth (conception) represents consciousness limiting itself by entering a particular form. The second birth (physical delivery) is the emergence of embodied awareness into the world. The third birth (death and beyond) points to consciousness transcending its bodily limitation.

From a Kashmir Shaiva perspective, these three births mirror the cosmic cycle of creation (*srishti*), sustenance (*sthiti*), and dissolution (*samhara*) — the rhythmic pulse of Shiva's creative consciousness. Recognition (*pratyabhijna*) that one's awareness has never truly been born or died dissolves the illusion of these three births and reveals the ever-free nature of consciousness.
