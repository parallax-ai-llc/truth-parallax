The Aitareya's opening creation narrative presents the Atman projecting the universe from Himself alone — a vision that Shaiva interpreters understand as Shiva's creative self-expression (*vimarsha*). The cosmic person whose apertures become the sense organs reflects the Shaiva tattva system, where consciousness progressively manifests as increasingly limited forms of awareness.

The statement that the Atman "saw" and then created emphasizes consciousness as prior to and constitutive of all reality. This aligns with Kashmir Shaivism's teaching that the universe is Shiva's own self-recognition taking form. The entry of deities into the body mirrors the involution of Shiva's infinite powers into finite capacities — universal fire becoming the power of speech, cosmic wind becoming breath.
