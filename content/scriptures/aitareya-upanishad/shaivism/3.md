The *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" is the Aitareya's supreme declaration and aligns perfectly with the Shaiva identification of Shiva with pure consciousness (*chit*). Chapter 3's systematic listing of the varieties of awareness — from simple perception to the highest wisdom — and their identification with Brahman mirrors Abhinavagupta's teaching that all modes of awareness, from the grossest to the subtlest, are expressions of Shiva's universal consciousness.

For Kashmir Shaivism, this chapter confirms that consciousness is not a property of Brahman but is Brahman itself. The recognition (*pratyabhijna*) that one's own awareness — in all its modes — is none other than Shiva is the essence of liberation. The Aitareya's progression from naming varieties of consciousness to identifying them all as Brahman mirrors the Shaiva journey from diversity to unity through recognition.
