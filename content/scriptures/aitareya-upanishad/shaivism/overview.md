## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Aitareya Upanishad a direct confirmation that consciousness (*prajnana*) is the ultimate reality — a teaching central to Kashmir Shaivism's identification of Shiva with pure awareness. The Upanishad's account of creation from the Atman alone mirrors the Shaiva understanding of the universe as Shiva's self-projection through His creative power (*Shakti*).

## Theological Emphases

The *mahavakya* "consciousness is Brahman" resonates deeply with the Pratyabhijna (Recognition) school's teaching that Shiva is unlimited consciousness (*chiti*) from which all reality manifests. The systematic account of the senses and their deities entering the cosmic body parallels the Shaiva mapping of *tattvas* (principles of reality). The Upanishad's conclusion that consciousness alone guides and sustains everything validates the Shaiva view that awareness, not matter, is the ground of existence.
