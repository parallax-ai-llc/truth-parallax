## Overview

The Russian Primary Chronicle is a major work of medieval Slavic literature. Its literary artistry encompasses diverse genres — annalistic entries, dramatic narratives, hagiographic passages, diplomatic documents, oral legends, and speeches. The testing of the faiths passage is a masterpiece of narrative construction, with its dramatic dialogue, vivid description, and rhetorical purpose. Literary analysis examines the Chronicle's narrative techniques, its integration of diverse source materials, its use of biblical typology, and its relationship to Byzantine literary models.
