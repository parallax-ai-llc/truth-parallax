## Overview

The Russian Primary Chronicle is one of the most important medieval documents of interfaith encounter. Its account of Vladimir's testing of the faiths presents a systematic (if obviously biased) comparison of Islam, Judaism, Western Christianity, and Eastern Orthodoxy from an Eastern Slavic perspective. The Chronicle also documents the encounter between Christianity and Slavic paganism — recording the destruction of pagan temples and idols alongside the establishment of Christian worship. These narratives raise important questions about religious freedom, the ethics of conversion, and the possibilities and limitations of interfaith dialogue in historical context.
