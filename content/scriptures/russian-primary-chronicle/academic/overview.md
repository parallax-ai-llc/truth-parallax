## Overview

The Russian Primary Chronicle is the most important primary source for academic study of early Eastern Slavic history and religion. Scholars analyze its complex textual history — including the relationship between the Laurentian, Hypatian, and other manuscript traditions — its sources (Byzantine chronicles, oral traditions, earlier Slavic annals), and its biases as a text written by Christian monks with particular theological and political agendas. Academic study of the Chronicle contributes to understanding of medieval historiography, the Christianization of Europe, and the reconstruction of pre-Christian Slavic religion.
