---
title: "The Baptism of Olga"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 5
---

**1** In the reign of Igor, the Drevlyanians revolted. Igor went to collect tribute from them a second time, and they killed him, saying: If a wolf comes among the sheep, he will take away the whole flock unless he be killed.

**2** Olga, Igor's wife, avenged his death upon the Drevlyanians with great cunning and severity. She destroyed their chief city, Iskorosten, by tying burning tinder to the legs of birds that returned to the nests under the roofs of the city.

**3** In the year 6463, Olga went to Constantinople. The reigning emperor was Constantine, son of Leo. Olga came before him, and he found her very intelligent.

**4** He said to her: You are fit to reign with us in this city. She was baptized and received the name Helen, after the ancient empress, mother of Constantine the Great.

**5** The patriarch instructed her in the faith and said: Blessed are you among Rus women, for you have loved light and left darkness. Your descendants shall bless you to the last generation.

**6** Olga returned to Kiev and tried to persuade her son Sviatoslav to be baptized, but he refused, saying his warriors would laugh at him. Yet Olga prayed for her son and for all the Rus people.
