---
title: "The Reign of Yaroslav the Wise"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Yaroslav was the mightiest of Vladimir's sons. He established himself in Kiev and built the great cathedral of Saint Sophia, after the cathedral in Constantinople.

**2** He loved books and read them constantly, by night and by day. He assembled many scribes and had them translate books from Greek into Slavic. He amassed many books.

**3** His father Vladimir had plowed and softened the soil, that is, he had enlightened the people through baptism. Yaroslav sowed the hearts of the faithful with the written word, and we reap the harvest, receiving the teaching of books.

**4** Great is the profit of book-learning. For from the words of books we are instructed and taught the way of repentance. Through the words of books we find wisdom and restraint.

**5** Yaroslav established the first written code of laws among the Rus, called the Russkaya Pravda. He built cities and churches throughout the land. He married his daughters to the kings of France, Hungary, and Norway.

**6** In his time the Rus land flourished, and Kiev was called the rival of Constantinople. Pilgrims came from all lands to see its golden domes and to marvel at its beauty.
