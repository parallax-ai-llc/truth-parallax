---
title: "Vladimir and the Choice of Faith"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Vladimir ruled in Kiev and worshipped idols. He set up idols on the hill outside the palace: Perun, with a head of silver and a mustache of gold, and Khors, Dazhbog, Stribog, Simargl, and Mokosh.

**2** Envoys came to Vladimir from the Bulgars of the Volga, professing the faith of Muhammad. They described their practices. Vladimir listened but said: Drinking is the joy of the Rus. We cannot exist without that pleasure.

**3** Then came the Germans from Rome, sent by the Pope. Vladimir said to them: Go back, for our fathers accepted no such principle.

**4** Then came Jewish Khazars, who said: We believe in one God, the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. Vladimir asked: Where is your land? They said: In Jerusalem, but God was angry with our forefathers and scattered us. Vladimir said: How can you teach others when God has rejected and scattered you?

**5** Then came a scholar from the Greeks, who told Vladimir the whole history of creation, the fall, the prophets, the coming of Christ, his death and resurrection, and the Last Judgment.

**6** Vladimir sent envoys to observe each faith. The envoys who visited Constantinople said: We knew not whether we were in heaven or on earth. For on earth there is no such splendor or such beauty. We only know that God dwells there among men. We cannot forget that beauty.
