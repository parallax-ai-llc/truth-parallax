---
title: "The Baptism of Rus"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 7
---

**1** In the year 6496, Vladimir marched against the Greek city of Kherson in Crimea. He besieged the city and took it.

**2** He sent envoys to the emperors Basil and Constantine, saying: I have taken your glorious city. I shall do the same to Constantinople unless you give me your sister in marriage. They replied: It is not fitting to give her in marriage to a pagan. Be baptized, and you shall have her.

**3** Vladimir agreed and was baptized in Kherson. He received the name Basil. And Anna, the emperor's sister, was given to him in marriage.

**4** When Vladimir returned to Kiev, he ordered the idols to be overthrown. Perun he ordered to be tied to a horse's tail and dragged to the river. He appointed twelve men to beat it with sticks, not because the wood could feel, but to dishonor the demon.

**5** Then Vladimir sent heralds throughout the city: If anyone, rich or poor, does not come to the river tomorrow, he will be my enemy. The people came to the Dnieper and were baptized in its waters.

**6** And Vladimir built a church on the hill where the idols had stood, and he established churches throughout the land. He sent for priests and teachers, and the children were taught to read books. And the mothers of these children wept for them, for they were not yet firm in the faith.
