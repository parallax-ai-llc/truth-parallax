---
title: "The Blinding of Vasilko"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 11
---

**1** In the year 6605, the Rus princes gathered at Lyubech and said to one another: Why do we ruin the Rus land with our feuding? Let each of us guard his own patrimony.

**2** And they kissed the cross to one another, saying: If any of us henceforth attack another, the cross and all the Rus land shall be against him.

**3** But scarcely had they parted when David Igorevich treacherously seized Prince Vasilko Rostislavich and put out his eyes, breaking the oath they had sworn.

**4** When Vladimir Monomakh heard of this crime, he wept and said: Such a thing has never happened in the Rus land, not from the time of our grandfathers, not from the time of our great-grandfathers.

**5** And the princes punished David and restored Vasilko's lands. The chronicler recorded this as a warning: Let no prince break the cross-kissing oath, for God's vengeance upon the oath-breaker is terrible.

**6** Vladimir Monomakh became the greatest prince after Yaroslav. He taught his children in his Testament: Fear God, help the poor, judge justly, and do not be idle, for idleness is the mother of all vices.
