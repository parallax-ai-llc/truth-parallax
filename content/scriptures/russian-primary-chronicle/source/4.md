---
title: "Oleg and the Shield on the Gates"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Oleg set out with a large army of Varangians, Chuds, Slavs, Merians, and Krivichians. He came to Smolensk and took the city. Then he came to Kiev.

**2** He saw that Askold and Dir were ruling in Kiev. He hid his warriors in the boats and sent word to them: We are merchants, and we are proceeding to Greece. They came out, and Oleg killed them.

**3** Oleg established himself in Kiev and said: Let this be the mother of Rus cities. He began to build stockaded towns, and he established tribute for the Slavs, the Krivichians, and the Merians.

**4** In the year 6415, Oleg attacked the Greeks. He came before Constantinople with two thousand vessels. The Greeks fortified the strait and closed up the city. Oleg disembarked and ordered his warriors to make wheels and put the ships on wheels.

**5** When the wind blew, they raised their sails and bore down upon the city from the open country. The Greeks were terrified and begged for peace.

**6** Oleg demanded tribute and concluded a treaty. He hung his shield upon the gate of Constantinople as a sign of victory. And the people called Oleg the Seer, because they were pagan and ignorant.
