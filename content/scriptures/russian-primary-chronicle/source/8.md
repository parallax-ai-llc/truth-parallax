---
title: "The Martyrdom of Boris and Gleb"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 8
---

**1** When Vladimir died, his son Sviatopolk seized power in Kiev. He plotted to kill his brothers so that he alone would rule.

**2** Boris, the most beloved of Vladimir's sons, was returning from a campaign against the Pechenegs. When he learned of his father's death, his warriors urged him to take Kiev by force. But Boris said: I will not raise my hand against my elder brother. Let him be to me as a father.

**3** Sviatopolk sent assassins. Boris was praying when they came. He said: Glory to thee, Lord, that thou hast deemed me worthy to suffer. And the assassins pierced him with their spears.

**4** Then Sviatopolk sent men to kill Gleb. Gleb was traveling by boat. When the assassins came, Gleb prayed: O God, receive my soul. And his own cook slit his throat.

**5** Boris and Gleb were counted among the first saints of the Rus church. They chose death over civil war, meekness over violence, and they became the passion-bearers of the Rus faith.

**6** Yaroslav, another son of Vladimir, rose against Sviatopolk and defeated him. Sviatopolk fled and died miserably in exile, and Yaroslav became prince of Kiev.
