---
title: "The Invitation to the Varangians"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 3
---

**1** In the year 6370 from the creation of the world, the tributaries of the Varangians drove them back beyond the sea and refused to pay them tribute. They set out to govern themselves, but there was no law among them, and tribe rose against tribe.

**2** There was discord among them, and they began to wage war one against another. They said to themselves: Let us seek a prince who may rule over us and judge us according to the law.

**3** They went across the sea to the Varangians, to the Rus. These particular Varangians were known as Rus, just as others are called Swedes, Normans, Angles, and Gotlanders.

**4** The Chuds, the Slavs, the Krivichians, and the Ves said to the Rus: Our land is great and rich, but there is no order in it. Come to rule and reign over us.

**5** Three brothers were chosen, with their kinfolk. They took with them all the Rus and came. The eldest, Rurik, settled in Novgorod; the second, Sineus, at Beloozero; and the third, Truvor, in Izborsk.

**6** On account of these Varangians, the district of Novgorod became known as the land of Rus. The present inhabitants of Novgorod are descended from the Varangian race, but formerly they were Slavs.
