---
title: "The Testament of Vladimir Monomakh"
scripture: "russian-primary-chronicle"
chapter: 12
---

**1** I, wretched one, named at baptism Basil by my grandfather Yaroslav, but commonly known by my Russian name Vladimir, and by my father named Monomakh.

**2** I write this for the sake of my children, or for anyone else who, on reading this, may accept my words to heart. First of all, for the sake of God and your own soul, retain the fear of God in your heart.

**3** Give generously to the orphan, defend the widow, and do not permit the mighty to destroy any man. Take not the life of the just or the unjust, nor order anyone to be killed.

**4** If you are worthy of death, do not destroy any Christian soul. Speaking and not speaking, swear not by God nor cross yourself, for there is no need.

**5** Do not neglect the poor; rather feed them. Do not bury your wealth in the ground, for that is a great sin. Visit the sick. Follow the dead, for we are all mortal. Pass no man without greeting him and give him a kind word.

**6** Love your wife, but grant her no power over you. Fear God, honor the old as a father, the young as brothers. In your house, do not be idle; do not rely on steward or servant.

**7** On campaign, do not rely on commanders. Set the sentries yourselves. Lie down by the warriors, rise early. Do not put aside your weapons hastily, for a man can perish suddenly through carelessness.

**8** Whatever good you are able to do, do not neglect it. I have made eighty-three campaigns, not counting the lesser ones. I concluded nineteen peace treaties with the Cumans. And through all this, God has preserved me to this old age.
