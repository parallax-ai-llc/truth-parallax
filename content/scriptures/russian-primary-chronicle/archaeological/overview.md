## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Russian Primary Chronicle evaluate and supplement its historical claims with material evidence. Excavations at sites mentioned in the Chronicle — Kiev, Novgorod, Staraya Ladoga, Gnezdovo — have confirmed, corrected, and enriched the textual record. Archaeological evidence of pre-Christian Slavic religious sites, the destruction of pagan temples, the construction of churches, and the material culture of the conversion period provides independent data for understanding the processes described in the Chronicle.
