## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Katha Upanishad a profound teaching on consciousness as the reality that transcends death. Nachiketa's encounter with Yama in the realm of death represents the aspirant's confrontation with the ultimate limit — and the discovery that pure awareness (*chit*) lies beyond even death's reach.

## Theological Emphases

The Katha Upanishad's famous declaration that the self is "not born, does not die" (1.2.18) is read through the Shaiva lens as describing Paramashiva — the absolute consciousness that is the true identity of every being. The chariot analogy (Chapter 3) is reinterpreted: the rider is not merely the individual soul but consciousness itself, which uses the body-mind complex as its vehicle of manifestation. Recognition of this truth — that one is the rider, not the chariot — constitutes liberation.
