The "thumbsized person" dwelling in the heart (2.1.12-13) is read by Shaiva interpreters as a poetic description of the concentrated point of awareness — the *bindu* — where individual and universal consciousness meet. This is not a literal entity of specific size but a reference to the focal point of self-awareness in the heart center, which serves as the gateway to recognition of one's identity with Shiva.

The chapter's teaching that from the self "the sun rises and sets" (2.1.9) is read as expressing the Shaiva doctrine that the external world of time and space is a projection of consciousness. The sun does not govern consciousness; consciousness projects the experience of the sun. This reversal of ordinary understanding — seeing the world as within consciousness rather than consciousness within the world — is the essence of the Shaiva transformation.
