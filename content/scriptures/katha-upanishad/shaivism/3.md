The razor's edge metaphor (1.3.14) is especially powerful in the Shaiva context, where it describes the extreme subtlety of the recognition required for liberation. The shift from contracted to expanded consciousness is not a journey from here to there but a razor-thin distinction in perspective — seeing with the eyes of Shiva rather than the eyes of the limited self.

The hierarchical ordering of realities (1.3.10-11) is read through the Kashmir Shaiva framework of the 36 *tattvas* (categories of reality). The progression from gross elements through senses, mind, and intellect to the Purusha maps onto the Shaiva ascent from *prithvi tattva* (earth) through progressively subtler categories to Shiva himself. The Purusha at the summit is Paramashiva — not one being among many but the consciousness that constitutes all beings.
