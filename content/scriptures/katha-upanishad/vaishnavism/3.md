Chapter 3 introduces the famous metaphor of the razor's edge — the spiritual path is as sharp and difficult to traverse as a razor's edge (1.3.14). Vaishnavas read this as emphasizing the need for disciplined devotional practice and the guidance of a genuine guru (*acharya*). Without proper guidance, even the sincere seeker can fall from the path.

The chapter also presents the hierarchical ordering of reality: senses are higher than their objects, mind is higher than senses, intellect higher than mind, the great self higher than intellect, the unmanifest higher than the great self, and the Purusha highest of all (1.3.10-11). Vaishnavas identify the Purusha at the summit as Vishnu — the Supreme Person who is the ultimate goal of all spiritual striving and the destination at which the razor-edged path terminates.
