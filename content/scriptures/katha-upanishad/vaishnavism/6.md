Chapter 6 concludes with the image of the eternal *ashvattha* tree whose roots are above and branches below — the cosmos rooted in Brahman (2.3.1). Vaishnavas read this as describing the material universe emanating from Vishnu, with the instruction to "cut down" the tree signifying liberation from material bondage through devotion to the Supreme Lord.

The Upanishad's concluding teaching that Brahman is "this, this" (*etad vai tat*) — present in all phenomena — directs the devotee to recognize Vishnu's presence everywhere. The final passage narrates Nachiketa's attainment of Brahman through the knowledge received from Yama (2.3.18), which Vaishnavas interpret as the triumph of devotion and divine grace over death itself. Nachiketa becomes the model devotee who, through unwavering dedication and the Lord's mercy, transcends mortality.
