## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Katha Upanishad as revealing the Supreme Lord Vishnu who dwells in the heart of every being as the Supersoul (*Paramatma*). Nachiketa's refusal of worldly pleasures in favor of knowledge about what lies beyond death exemplifies the Vaishnava ideal of *vairagya* (detachment from the material) combined with devotion to the divine.

## Theological Emphases

The Katha Upanishad's teaching on the "thumbsized person" (*angushthamatra purusha*) dwelling in the heart (2.1.12-13) is read by Vaishnavas as describing Vishnu's localized presence within every living being. Yama's revelation that the self cannot be attained by intellectual effort alone but chooses to reveal itself to the one it favors (1.2.23) underscores the Vaishnava emphasis on divine grace (*prasada*) as the ultimate means of liberation.
