Chapter 5 deepens the teaching on the inner self with the analogy of two birds on a tree (2.2.1) — one eating the fruit (the individual soul experiencing the world) and the other merely watching (the Supreme Soul, Vishnu). Vaishnavas treasure this image as perfectly expressing the eternal relationship between God and the soul: both are real, both dwell in the same body, but their natures are fundamentally different.

The chapter teaches that the self pervades all beings yet is subtler than the subtlest (2.2.8-9). The Vaishnava reading uses these passages to argue for Vishnu's simultaneous transcendence and immanence. The teaching that one who sees diversity rather than unity goes "from death to death" (2.2.10) is interpreted not as teaching the identity of all things (as in Advaita) but as counseling the devotee to see Vishnu's presence in all beings.
