Chapter 2 presents Yama's core teaching: the distinction between *shreyas* (the good) and *preyas* (the pleasant). Vaishnavas read this as the fundamental choice facing every soul — devotion to the Supreme Lord versus pursuit of material enjoyment. The wise choose the good, which leads to liberation; the foolish choose the pleasant, which binds them to the cycle of birth and death.

Yama reveals that the self (*atman*) cannot be known through ordinary means — "not by speech, not by mind, not by sight" (1.2.12). Vaishnavas interpret this as affirming the necessity of divine grace for God-realization. The critical verse 1.2.23 — "The self reveals itself to the one it chooses" — is foundational to Vaishnava theology, establishing that liberation depends on the Lord's gracious self-disclosure to the devoted soul rather than on human effort alone.
