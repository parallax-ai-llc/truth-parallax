Chapter 4 begins the second section of the Katha Upanishad with Yama's teaching on the self (*purusha*) that dwells in the cave of the heart. The "thumbsized person" (*angushthamatra purusha*) is identified by Vaishnavas as the Supersoul (*Paramatma*) — Vishnu's localized presence within every living being. This inner Lord is described as "lord of the past and the future" (2.1.5), confirming His sovereignty over time.

The Vaishnava reading emphasizes that realizing this inner divine presence — not through intellectual analysis but through devotion and grace — frees one from grief and fear (2.1.4). The teaching that the self is like a smokeless flame (2.1.13) is read as describing the pure, luminous nature of Vishnu's presence within the heart, undimmed by the material coverings of body and mind.
