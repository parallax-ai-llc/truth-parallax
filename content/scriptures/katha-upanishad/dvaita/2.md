Madhva's commentary on Chapter 2 insists that the self (*atman*) described by Yama is the individual soul — eternal, real, and distinct from God — not identical with Brahman as Advaita claims. The soul is "not born, does not die" (1.2.18) because it is an eternally existing entity, but its eternality is dependent on God's sustaining will, not intrinsic in the way God's is.

The verse that the self "reveals itself to the one it chooses" (1.2.23) is foundational to Madhva's theology of grace. God actively selects souls for liberation based on their innate capacity (*yogyata*) and demonstrated devotion. This is not arbitrary but reflects God's perfect justice and knowledge. The teaching is clear: human effort alone cannot achieve liberation. Without Vishnu's positive act of self-revelation, the soul remains bound.
