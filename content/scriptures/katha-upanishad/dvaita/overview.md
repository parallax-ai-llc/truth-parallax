## Overview

Madhva's reading of the Katha Upanishad emphasizes God's absolute transcendence over death and all material conditions. Nachiketa's dialogue with Yama demonstrates that the highest knowledge — knowledge of the Supreme Lord — transcends even the authority of the god of death, who must acknowledge a reality greater than himself.

## Theological Emphases

The Dvaita reading focuses on the verse "The self chooses whom it will reveal itself to" (1.2.23) as establishing the primacy of divine grace in liberation. For Madhva, this means that Vishnu actively selects souls for liberation based on their inherent spiritual capacity and devotion. The chariot analogy is read realistically: the soul is a genuine rider distinct from the chariot (body), horses (senses), and charioteer (intellect), and the road leads to the abode of Vishnu.
