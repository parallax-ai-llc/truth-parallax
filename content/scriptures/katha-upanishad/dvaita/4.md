The Dvaita reading of Chapter 4's "thumbsized person" in the heart identifies this figure as Vishnu in His *Paramatma* (Supersoul) form — the Lord who dwells within every being as inner controller and witness. This is a localized manifestation of the infinite God, not a diminishment of His nature. Vishnu's presence in the heart enables the soul's consciousness, sanctions its actions, and guides its spiritual development.

Madhva emphasizes the verse "From this self, fear arises and does not arise" (2.1.9) as describing God's sovereignty over all emotional and cosmic phenomena. God is the source of both creation and dissolution, comfort and terror. The devotee who recognizes the Lord in the heart is freed from fear — not because fear is unreal but because the Lord's protective presence is more powerful than any threat.
