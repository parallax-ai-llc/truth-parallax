Madhva reads the razor's edge metaphor as affirming the difficulty and seriousness of the spiritual path. Liberation is not easily attained — it requires sustained devotion, rigorous self-discipline, and divine grace. The image of the sharp edge warns against complacency and casual spiritual practice. Every step must be taken with full awareness and commitment.

The hierarchical ordering (1.3.10-11) is read by Dvaita as a genuine cosmological hierarchy with Vishnu at its apex. Each level represents a real category of being, not a conceptual abstraction. The Purusha beyond which there is nothing is Vishnu — the independently real Supreme Being who is the source, sustainer, and goal of all other realities. This hierarchy is permanent, not a ladder to be transcended through identity-realization.
