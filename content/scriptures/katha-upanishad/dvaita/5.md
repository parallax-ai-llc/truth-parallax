The two birds parable receives a characteristically Dvaita interpretation: the two birds are genuinely two — the individual soul (*jiva*) and the Supreme Soul (*Paramatma*). They share the same tree (body) but are ontologically distinct. The individual soul "eats" the fruits of karma, experiencing pleasure and pain; the Supreme Soul watches without being affected. This distinction is eternal and unbridgeable — the soul never becomes God.

Madhva reads the teaching that one who sees only diversity goes "from death to death" (2.2.10) not as counseling non-dual awareness but as warning against failure to see God's unifying presence in all things. The problem is not seeing many beings but failing to see the one God who sustains them all. Right vision sees both the many souls and the one God — unity and diversity are both real.
