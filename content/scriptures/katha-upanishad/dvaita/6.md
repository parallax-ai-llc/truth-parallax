The cosmic tree in Chapter 6 is read by Dvaita as a genuine cosmological structure rooted in Brahman (Vishnu) and extending through the hierarchical levels of material and spiritual existence. Cutting the tree means liberation from material bondage through devotion to the Lord who is its root and sustainer.

The Upanishad's conclusion — Nachiketa attains Brahman — is interpreted by Madhva as Nachiketa attaining the abode of Vishnu through Yama's teaching and his own extraordinary devotion. Liberation in Dvaita is not identity with Brahman but eternal proximity to Vishnu in His transcendent realm, enjoying blissful devotional relationship forever. Nachiketa's story demonstrates that even death — the ultimate worldly limit — is overcome by the power of divine knowledge and grace.
