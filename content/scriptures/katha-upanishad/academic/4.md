The "thumbsized person" in the heart (2.1.12-13) has been studied as evidence for early Hindu anthropological concepts. Some scholars connect it with archaic soul-beliefs found across cultures — the idea of a miniature inner person or homunculus — while others see it as a metaphorical description of concentrated awareness. The image appears in several Upanishads and in the Mahabharata, suggesting it was a well-established concept.

The chapter's statement that "from this self, the sun rises" (2.1.9) has been analyzed as a cosmogonic claim that places consciousness prior to the physical universe. Scholars note the contrast with Vedic cosmogony, where natural phenomena like the sun are often treated as primary. The Upanishadic reversal — consciousness as the source of nature rather than its product — represents a fundamental philosophical shift that scholars trace to the middle Upanishadic period.
