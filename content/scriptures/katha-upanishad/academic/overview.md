## Overview

Scholars generally date the Katha Upanishad to the 5th-3rd century BCE, making it one of the middle-period Upanishads. Its combination of narrative framework (the Nachiketa story) with systematic philosophical teaching makes it an important text for understanding the literary development of Upanishadic thought. The Nachiketa legend appears in earlier Vedic literature (Taittiriya Brahmana), but the Upanishad transforms a ritual narrative into a philosophical dialogue.

## Research Themes

The chariot analogy (1.3.3-9) has attracted considerable scholarly attention for its relationship to Platonic psychology (Phaedrus 246a-254e), where Plato also uses a chariot metaphor for the soul. Whether this reflects direct influence, common Indo-European heritage, or independent invention remains debated. The Katha Upanishad's hierarchical cosmology (senses-mind-intellect-self) has been analyzed as proto-Samkhya, possibly predating the classical Samkhya system.
