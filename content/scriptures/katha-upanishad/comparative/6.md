The cosmic tree of Chapter 6, rooted in the transcendent and branching into manifestation, belongs to a universal archetype. The Norse Yggdrasil connects nine worlds; the Kabbalistic Tree of Life maps the emanation of divine attributes; the shamanic world-tree provides the axis connecting upper, middle, and lower worlds. The Katha Upanishad's distinctive contribution is the call to cut this tree down — to recognize that the entire structure of manifestation, however beautiful, is not the ultimate reality.

The Upanishad's conclusion — that knowledge transcends death — resonates with the fundamental promise of every major religious tradition. The Christian resurrection, the Buddhist *amrita* (deathless state), the Taoist immortality of the sage, and the Islamic promise of eternal life all affirm that death does not have the final word. What distinguishes the Upanishadic teaching is its philosophical precision: death is transcended not through a future event but through present recognition that the self was never subject to death in the first place.
