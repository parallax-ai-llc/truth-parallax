Shankara reads Chapter 4's teaching on the inner self as pointing to the *pratyagatman* — the innermost self that is identical with Brahman. The "thumbsized person" in the heart is interpreted not as a miniature entity but as consciousness concentrated at the heart center, from which it illuminates the entire body-mind complex. The size reference is a concession to the limitations of language in describing the formless.

The teaching that "from this self, the sun rises" (2.1.9) is read by Shankara as expressing Brahman's causal relationship to the cosmos. Everything — including the sun, the cosmic order, and the gods themselves — depends on Brahman for its existence. This is not a theistic claim about a creator deity but a metaphysical statement about the ontological priority of consciousness over everything that appears within it.
