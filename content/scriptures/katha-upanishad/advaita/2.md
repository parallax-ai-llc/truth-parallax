Chapter 2 contains some of the most important verses for Advaita Vedanta. Yama's teaching on the self — "not born, does not die; it did not come into being and will not come into being" (1.2.18) — is read by Shankara as a direct statement of Brahman's unconditioned nature. The self is not a substance that undergoes changes but the changeless ground in which all change appears.

The verse "The self is not attained by the weak" (1.2.24) — *nayam atma balahinena labhyah* — is interpreted by Shankara as requiring not physical but spiritual strength: the strength of sustained inquiry, concentration, and renunciation. The verse on the self choosing to reveal itself (1.2.23) is read as expressing Brahman's self-luminous nature: it reveals itself when the obstacles of ignorance are removed, just as the sun "chooses" to shine when clouds disperse.
