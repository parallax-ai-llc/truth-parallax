## Overview

The Katha Upanishad is one of Shankara's most frequently cited sources. Its teaching that the self (*atman*) is identical with Brahman, and that this self is beyond birth and death, beyond cause and effect, forms a cornerstone of the Advaita system. Shankara wrote a detailed commentary (*bhashya*) on this Upanishad, treating it as a systematic exposition of non-dual knowledge.

## Theological Emphases

Shankara reads the Katha Upanishad's hierarchy of realities — from senses to mind to intellect to the "great self" to the unmanifest to Purusha (1.3.10-11) — as a progressive negation pointing to the attributeless Brahman beyond all categories. The famous verse "The self is not attained by teaching, nor by intellect, nor by much learning; it is attained by the one whom it chooses" (1.2.23) is interpreted as expressing the self-revealing nature of Brahman, which cannot be grasped as an object but is recognized when all objectification ceases.
