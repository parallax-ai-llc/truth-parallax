Shankara's commentary on Chapter 1 frames Nachiketa's story as illustrating the qualifications of a genuine seeker (*adhikari*). Nachiketa possesses *viveka* (discrimination between eternal and non-eternal), *vairagya* (detachment from enjoyments), the six virtues (including *shama* and *dama*), and *mumukshutva* (intense longing for liberation). His rejection of Yama's temptations demonstrates that only one who has turned away from the finite can receive knowledge of the infinite.

Shankara emphasizes that Nachiketa's question about what happens after death is ultimately a question about the nature of the self. Death is the dissolution of the body-mind complex, and the question "Does the self exist after death?" is really "What is the self's true nature?" The Advaita answer — that the self is Brahman, beyond birth and death — will be unfolded systematically through the remaining chapters.
