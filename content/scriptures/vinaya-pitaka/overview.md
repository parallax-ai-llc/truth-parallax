## Overview

The Vinaya Pitaka ("Basket of Discipline") is the first of the three pitakas (baskets) of the Pali Canon and contains the rules and procedures governing the Buddhist monastic community (Sangha). It provides the complete legal framework for the life of monks (bhikkhus) and nuns (bhikkhunis), including rules of conduct, procedures for communal decision-making, ordination ceremonies, and case histories that illustrate the application of monastic law.

## Structure

The Vinaya Pitaka is organized into 5 sections:

1. **Suttavibhanga (Bhikkhu)**: Analysis of the 227 rules for monks, with origin stories and case law
2. **Suttavibhanga (Bhikkhuni)**: Analysis of the 311 rules for nuns
3. **Mahavagga**: Great division — ordination, uposatha ceremony, rains retreat, and other procedures
4. **Cullavagga**: Lesser division — disciplinary procedures, schism, Council accounts
5. **Parivara**: Appendix — summaries, analyses, and classificatory guides

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Every Buddhist tradition preserves its own Vinaya. The Theravada Pali Vinaya, the Dharmaguptaka Vinaya (used in East Asian Buddhism), and the Mulasarvastivada Vinaya (used in Tibetan Buddhism) represent three major living lineages. Despite differences in detail, all share a common core structure and many identical rules.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Monastic Discipline | The 227/311 rules of the Patimokkha code |
| Community Governance | Procedures for communal decision-making and conflict resolution |
| Ordination | The ceremonies and requirements for entering monastic life |
| Origin Stories | Narratives explaining the circumstances that led to each rule |
| Legal Reasoning | Case-based analysis of how rules apply in specific circumstances |
