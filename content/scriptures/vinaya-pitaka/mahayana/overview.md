## Overview

East Asian Mahayana Buddhism follows the Dharmaguptaka Vinaya, a parallel Vinaya tradition preserved in Chinese translation. While Mahayana also introduced bodhisattva precepts (from the Brahmajala Sutra), the fundamental monastic discipline remains rooted in the Vinaya Pitaka tradition.

## Key Themes

Mahayana scholars recognize the Vinaya as the essential foundation for monastic practice, even as they supplement it with bodhisattva vows. The tension between the Vinaya's detailed rules and the bodhisattva ideal of compassionate flexibility has been a productive source of reflection in Mahayana monastic life.

## Relevance to Mahayana

The Vinaya's principles of communal governance, ethical discipline, and mindful conduct remain the foundation of Mahayana monastic life across East Asia.
