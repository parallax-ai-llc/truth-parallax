## Overview

The Vinaya Pitaka is the living constitution of the Theravada monastic community. The 227 rules of the Bhikkhu Patimokkha and the 311 rules of the Bhikkhuni Patimokkha govern every aspect of monastic life in Theravada countries from Sri Lanka to Myanmar to Thailand.

## Key Themes

Theravada monks and nuns study the Vinaya as the practical framework for their entire monastic life. The fortnightly recitation of the Patimokkha, the procedures for the rains retreat (vassa), and the ordination ceremonies all derive from the Vinaya Pitaka. The tradition holds that the Sasana (Buddhist dispensation) endures only as long as the Vinaya is maintained.

## Practice and Application

Every Theravada monastery follows the Vinaya's prescriptions for daily routine, communal governance, and the resolution of disputes. The Vinaya is not merely studied but lived as the foundational discipline of monastic life.
