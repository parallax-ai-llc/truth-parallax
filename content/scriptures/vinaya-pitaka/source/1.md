---
title: "Vinaya Pitaka Section 1: Parajika (Rules of Defeat)"
scripture: "vinaya-pitaka"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Reverence to the Blessed One, the Arahat, the Fully Enlightened One. At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesali, in the Great Grove, in the Hall with the Peaked Roof. And at that time the Blessed One addressed the monks concerning the rules of training.

**2** Whatever monk should engage in sexual intercourse, even with an animal, is one who is defeated, no longer in communion. As a man with his head cut off cannot become alive again with that body, even so a monk who has engaged in sexual intercourse is defeated, no longer in communion.

**3** Whatever monk should take, with the intention of stealing, what is not given to him, in such a manner as a king or a king's minister would seize a thief — that monk is defeated, no longer in communion.

**4** Whatever monk should intentionally deprive a human being of life, or should seek for him a weapon of death, or should praise the beauty of death, or should incite him to die — that monk is defeated, no longer in communion.

**5** Whatever monk should boast of a superhuman state, of a knowledge and insight worthy of the Noble Ones, saying: "Thus I know, thus I see," when he does not know and does not see — that monk is defeated, no longer in communion.

**6** These are the four rules of defeat. When a monk has committed any one of these offences, he is no longer a member of the Sangha. He is as one fallen, as a stone broken in two that cannot be put together again.

**7** The Blessed One said: "Monks, I shall recite the Patimokkha. Let all listen well, and pay attention, and I shall speak." The Patimokkha is recited on every Uposatha day, on the fourteenth or fifteenth of the half-month, when the monks assemble together.

**8** Before reciting the Patimokkha, the senior monk declares: "Let the Sangha hear me. Today is the Uposatha of the fifteenth. If the Sangha is ready, let the Sangha carry out the Uposatha and recite the Patimokkha. What is the preliminary duty of the Sangha? Let the venerable ones report their purity."

**9** If a monk knows of an offence but does not confess it during the Patimokkha recitation, this is a deliberate lie. The Blessed One has said that a deliberate lie is an obstacle to progress on the path.

**10** The Blessed One declared these rules not all at once, but one by one, as occasions arose. When a monk committed a particular offence for the first time, the Blessed One laid down a rule concerning that offence. Thus the discipline grew gradually, in response to the needs of the community.
