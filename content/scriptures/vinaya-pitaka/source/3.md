---
title: "Vinaya Pitaka Section 3: Khandhaka (The Great Chapter)"
scripture: "vinaya-pitaka"
chapter: 3
---

**1** At that time the Blessed One, having recently attained supreme enlightenment, was seated at the foot of the Bodhi tree, experiencing the bliss of liberation. For seven days the Blessed One sat in one posture, experiencing the bliss of emancipation.

**2** Then the Blessed One, at the end of seven days, arose from that state of concentration and contemplated dependent origination in forward order: "When this exists, that comes to be; with the arising of this, that arises. When this does not exist, that does not come to be; with the cessation of this, that ceases."

**3** Then the Blessed One thought: "This Dhamma that I have attained is deep, hard to see, hard to understand, peaceful, sublime, beyond the sphere of reasoning, subtle, to be experienced by the wise. But this generation delights in attachment. For such a generation, this truth would be hard to see."

**4** Then the Brahma Sahampati, knowing with his own mind the thought of the Blessed One, appeared before the Blessed One and said: "Lord, let the Blessed One teach the Dhamma. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who will be lost through not hearing the Dhamma."

**5** Then the Blessed One, having understood the request of the Brahma, out of compassion for beings, surveyed the world with the eye of a Buddha. He saw beings with little dust in their eyes and beings with much dust in their eyes, with keen faculties and dull faculties. And the Blessed One consented to teach.

**6** The ordination procedure is as follows: A candidate approaches the Sangha and requests ordination, saying three times: "Venerable sirs, I request the ordination. May the Sangha, out of compassion, raise me up." The motion is put and the ordination is conferred by a Sangha of at least ten monks.

**7** The Blessed One allowed the use of three robes and a bowl. The three robes are: the inner robe, the upper robe, and the outer cloak. A monk should possess only these three robes, and they should be made of allowable cloth, properly cut and sewn.

**8** Concerning the rainy season retreat: "Monks, I allow the observance of the rainy season retreat. There are two entries for the retreat: the earlier entry on the day after the full moon of Asalha, and the later entry one month later." During the retreat, monks should not travel but should remain in their lodgings, devoting themselves to practice.

**9** At the end of the rainy season, the ceremony of Kathina is held, in which lay supporters offer cloth to the Sangha. The Blessed One allowed the Kathina ceremony and laid down the procedures for its observance.

**10** The Blessed One said: "Monks, I allow a formal act of the Sangha for the settlement of disputes. Disputes should be settled face to face, by the decision of the majority, or by the finding of a committee. These are the seven methods for settling disputes, and by these the Sangha shall maintain harmony."
