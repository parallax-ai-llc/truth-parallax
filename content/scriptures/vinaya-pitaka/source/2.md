---
title: "Vinaya Pitaka Section 2: Sanghadisesa (Rules Entailing Initial and Subsequent Meetings)"
scripture: "vinaya-pitaka"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Whatever monk, overcome by lust, with altered mind, should engage in bodily contact with a woman — holding her hand, holding a braid of her hair, or caressing any part of her body — this is a Sanghadisesa offence.

**2** Whatever monk, overcome by lust, with altered mind, should address a woman with lewd words relating to sexual intercourse, as a young man might address a young woman — this is a Sanghadisesa offence.

**3** Whatever monk should act as a go-between for a man with a woman or for a woman with a man, whether for marriage or for temporary union — this is a Sanghadisesa offence.

**4** Whatever monk, angry and displeased, should make an unfounded charge of a Parajika offence against another monk, hoping thereby to deprive him of his status — this is a Sanghadisesa offence.

**5** Whatever monk should promote schism in a united Sangha, or should persist in taking up a cause that leads to schism — this is a Sanghadisesa offence. For the Blessed One has said: "Schism in the Sangha is one of the gravest misdeeds."

**6** A monk who has committed a Sanghadisesa offence must undergo a period of penance. First, he is placed on probation for as many days as the offence was concealed. Then he must undergo a six-day period of discipline called manatta.

**7** During the period of penance, the offending monk loses certain privileges: he may not ordain others, he may not give instructions, he may not exercise authority. He must announce his offence to every monk he meets and to every assembly he attends.

**8** After the period of manatta, the monk may be rehabilitated by a Sangha of at least twenty monks. The senior monk puts the motion, and if there is no objection, the monk is reinstated to full communion.

**9** These Sanghadisesa rules were laid down by the Blessed One to protect the purity of the monastic community. They serve as a middle way between the irrevocable expulsion of the Parajika offences and the lighter penances of lesser offences.

**10** The Blessed One said: "Monks, I allow the Sangha to impose penance upon a monk who has committed a Sanghadisesa offence. The purpose is not punishment but reformation, so that the monk may purify his conduct and return to the practice of the holy life."
