## Overview

Tibetan Buddhism follows the Mulasarvastivada Vinaya, the most extensive of the surviving Vinaya traditions. This version of the monastic code was transmitted to Tibet from India and remains the foundation of Tibetan monastic life, coexisting with tantric vows and bodhisattva precepts.

## Key Themes

Tibetan scholars study the Vinaya as one of the three trainings (sila, samadhi, prajna) that form the foundation of the Buddhist path. The Mulasarvastivada Vinaya's extensive narrative material provides rich teaching stories used in monastic education.

## Relevance to Tibetan Study

In Tibetan monastic education, Vinaya study is one of the five major topics (along with Madhyamaka, Pramana, Abhidharma, and the Ornament of Realization). Mastery of the Vinaya is required for the Geshe degree.
