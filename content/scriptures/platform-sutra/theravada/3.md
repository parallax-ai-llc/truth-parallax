The dialogue format of this chapter, where Huineng responds to questions from various practitioners, parallels the question-and-answer format found extensively in the Pali Nikayas. The Buddha frequently teaches in response to specific questions, adapting his instruction to the questioner's level of understanding and spiritual maturity.

Huineng's responses demonstrate a teaching style that is notably more direct and less analytical than the typical Theravada approach. Where the Buddha in the Pali suttas often employs detailed systematic analysis, graduated instruction, and logical argumentation, Huineng tends toward paradoxical statements and direct pointing that aim to bypass conceptual thinking entirely.

The Theravada tradition values clear, systematic exposition of the dharma. The Abhidhamma represents the most developed expression of this approach, categorizing all phenomena into precisely defined categories. Huineng's teaching style, which deliberately subverts categorical thinking, represents a fundamentally different pedagogical philosophy, though both aim at the same goal of liberating insight.
