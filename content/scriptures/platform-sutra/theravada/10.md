Huineng's final instructions and death scene can be compared with the Buddha's own final teachings as recorded in the Mahaparinibbana Sutta, one of the most important texts in the Theravada canon. Both figures offer last teachings, entrust the dharma to their students, and pass away surrounded by disciples. The parallels suggest that Huineng's death narrative was deliberately modeled on the Buddha's.

The Buddha's famous last words, "All conditioned things are impermanent. Work out your salvation with diligence," emphasize the practitioner's own effort and the impermanence of all phenomena. Huineng's final instructions similarly emphasize self-reliance and the inherent capacity of each being for awakening, though framed within the Mahayana language of buddha-nature.

The Theravada tradition places great importance on the manner of an arahat's death, viewing it as a final demonstration of the practitioner's realization. The state of mind at the moment of death is understood to have karmic significance. Huineng's calm and composed passing, accompanied by auspicious signs, follows the pattern established by the Buddha and honored throughout Theravada Buddhist tradition.
