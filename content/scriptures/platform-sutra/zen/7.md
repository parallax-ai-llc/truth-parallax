The teaching on adapting to different temperaments and circumstances is the Zen master's craft. A master who offers the same teaching to every student is like a doctor who prescribes the same medicine for every illness. Zhaozhou offered tea to every visitor, but the tea was different each time. Yunmen answered every question with a single word, but the word was always the right one.

Huineng teaches his students how to teach by showing them that true teaching arises from emptiness rather than from a fixed method. When the mind is free from predetermined responses, the appropriate teaching emerges naturally in response to the student's actual condition. This is what Zen calls "live words" (katsu) as opposed to "dead words" (shi).

The chapter reveals that Zen pedagogy is itself a form of meditation practice. The teacher who is fully present with the student, without preconception or plan, is practicing the same open awareness that characterizes zazen. Teaching and sitting are one, just as meditation and wisdom are one. The dokusan room is the meditation hall, and the meditation hall is the dokusan room.
