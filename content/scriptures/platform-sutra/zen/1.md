The autobiography of Huineng is the origin story of Zen. Every element of this narrative has been absorbed into the tradition's DNA: the illiterate woodcutter who surpasses the head monk, the midnight transmission of the robe and bowl, the flight south carrying the dharma in his body rather than in texts. This is not merely history but a statement of what Zen values.

The poetry contest between Shenxiu and Huineng is the koan at the heart of the Platform Sutra. Shenxiu writes: "The body is the bodhi tree, the mind a bright mirror's stand. At all times polish it diligently, let no dust alight." Huineng responds: "Bodhi originally has no tree, the mirror has no stand. Buddha-nature is always clean and pure; where could dust alight?" Every practitioner must resolve this koan: is there something to polish, or nothing?

The Fifth Patriarch's recognition of Huineng's realization demonstrates the transmission that occurs beyond words and outside scriptures. Hongren recognized Huineng's awakening not through examination or debate but through the direct perception of one awakened mind by another. This is what Zen means by "mind-to-mind transmission."
