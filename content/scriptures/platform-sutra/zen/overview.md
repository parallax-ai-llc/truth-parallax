## Overview

The Platform Sutra is the foundational text of Zen Buddhism, recording the life and teachings of the Sixth Patriarch Huineng. It is the only text by a Chinese author to receive the honorific title "sutra." For all branches of Chan/Zen, this text defines the essence of the tradition.

## Zen Reading

The story of Huineng's awakening encapsulates the Zen message: an illiterate layman surpasses learned monks through direct insight. His verse—"Originally there is not a single thing; where could dust alight?"—became the paradigmatic expression of sudden enlightenment in the Zen tradition, establishing the Southern School's dominance.

## Practice Implications

Huineng's teaching that meditation and wisdom are one, not two, fundamentally shaped Zen practice. Sitting meditation is not a technique for producing wisdom but the natural expression of one's already-awakened nature. This insight gave rise to the Zen emphasis on practice-realization, where practice itself is enlightenment, not a means to achieve it.
