Huineng's final teaching is a gift to all future practitioners. Before dying, he transmits the method of thirty-six pairs of opposites, teaching his students how to transcend any dualistic framework they encounter. When someone presents a position, respond with its opposite. When they present the opposite, respond with the original. In this way, all fixed views are dissolved and the living truth of non-duality is maintained.

The Sixth Patriarch's death is the final demonstration of his teaching. He sits in meditation, speaks his last words, and passes away in perfect composure. There is no drama, no regret, no unfinished business. The lamp goes out because there is no more oil, not because someone blew it out. This natural passing is the ultimate expression of the teaching that awakening is not something added to life but the very nature of life itself.

After Huineng, the tradition diversified into the five houses and seven schools that constitute the full flowering of Chan/Zen Buddhism. But every branch traces its root to this single figure, this illiterate woodcutter who heard the Diamond Sutra and saw his original face. The Platform Sutra ends, but the practice it transmits continues wherever a human being sits down, becomes quiet, and looks within.
