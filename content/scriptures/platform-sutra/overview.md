## Overview

The Platform Sutra of the Sixth Patriarch (Liuzu Tanjing) is the only Chinese Buddhist text honored with the title "sutra," a designation traditionally reserved for the words of the Buddha. It records the autobiography, teachings, and dialogues of Huineng (638–713), the Sixth Patriarch of Chan Buddhism, who is said to have attained awakening as an illiterate firewood seller upon hearing the Diamond Sutra.

## Structure

The sutra is organized into 10 chapters:

1. **Autobiography** — Huineng's life story and enlightenment
2. **Prajna** — Teaching on the perfection of wisdom
3. **Questions and Answers** — Dialogues with practitioners
4. **Meditation and Wisdom** — Their inseparable unity
5. **Sitting Meditation** — True meaning of meditation practice
6. **Repentance** — The formless repentance ceremony
7. **Temperaments and Circumstances** — Adapting teaching to students
8. **Sudden and Gradual** — The debate between northern and southern schools
9. **Royal Patronage** — Relations with the imperial court
10. **Final Instructions** — Huineng's last teachings and transmission

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Platform Sutra defined the identity of the Southern School of Chan and shaped all subsequent Zen traditions in China, Korea, Japan, and Vietnam. Its emphasis on sudden enlightenment and the inherent buddha-nature of all beings remains a cornerstone of Zen thought.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Sudden Enlightenment | Awakening as an instantaneous seeing of one's true nature |
| Buddha-nature | All beings inherently possess the awakened mind |
| Meditation and Wisdom | Samadhi and prajna as inseparable aspects of practice |
| Non-attachment | Freedom from clinging to concepts, including Buddhist ones |
| Lineage | The transmission of awakening from mind to mind |
