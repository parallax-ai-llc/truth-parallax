---
title: "Platform Sutra - Chapter 4"
scripture: "platform-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Hui Neng also composed a verse, and asked someone who could write to post it on the wall of the west corridor, so that his own original mind might be submitted. If you do not know your own original mind, studying the Dharma is of no benefit. If you know your own mind and see your own nature, you are called a great hero, a teacher of gods and humans, a Buddha.

**2** His verse said: "Bodhi originally has no tree, The mirror also has no stand. The Buddha-nature is always clean and pure; Where is there room for dust?"

**3** Another verse said: "The mind is the Bodhi tree, The body is the mirror stand. The mirror is originally clean and pure; Where can it be stained by dust?"

**4** The followers in the hall were all astonished when they heard this verse. They said to each other in amazement: "Strange indeed! One cannot judge a person by his appearance. How can it be that, after so short a time, he is a living Bodhisattva in the flesh?"

**5** The Fifth Patriarch saw the startled assembly, and fearing that they might harm Hui Neng, erased the verse with his shoe, and said: "This one has not yet seen his nature either." The assembly agreed.
