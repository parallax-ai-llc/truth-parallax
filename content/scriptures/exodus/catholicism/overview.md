## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Exodus draws on the Church Fathers, the Magisterium, and the liturgical tradition to read the book as a prefiguration of the Paschal mystery. The Passover, the Red Sea crossing, Sinai, and the Tabernacle are all understood sacramentally — pointing to the Eucharist, baptism, the moral law, and the Church.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### The Paschal Mystery
The Easter Vigil liturgy reads Exodus 14 (crossing of the Red Sea) as the central Old Testament prefiguration of Christ's death and resurrection. The Passover lamb is explicitly identified with Christ in the Roman Canon.

### The Decalogue
The Catechism devotes extensive treatment to the Ten Commandments (CCC 2052-2557), reading them not merely as ancient law but as the permanent expression of natural law and the foundation of Catholic moral teaching.

### Sacramental Typology
Catholic tradition sees the Exodus events as types of the sacraments:

| Exodus Event | Sacramental Antitype | CCC Reference |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Passover | Eucharist | CCC 1340 |
| Red Sea | Baptism | CCC 1221 |
| Sinai covenant | Confirmation / New Covenant | CCC 1964 |
| Manna | Eucharist | CCC 1094 |
| Tabernacle | The Church | CCC 1093 |
