## Overview

For the Eastern Orthodox Church, Exodus is the book of Pascha — the foundational narrative of divine deliverance that finds its ultimate fulfillment in Christ's resurrection. The Paschal liturgy, the most important celebration in Orthodoxy, is saturated with Exodus imagery: the Passover lamb, the crossing from death to life, and the journey from slavery to freedom.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### Paschal Typology
The Paschal Troparion declares: "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life." This is understood as the ultimate Exodus — liberation not from Pharaoh but from death itself.

### Theophany at Sinai
Orthodox theology reads the Sinai theophany through the Palamite distinction between God's essence (unknowable) and energies (experienceable). Moses saw the divine glory — the uncreated light — but not the divine essence.

### The Tabernacle and Divine Presence
The Tabernacle anticipates the Incarnation: God dwelling (*shekinah*) among His people prefigures Christ, who "tabernacled" among us (John 1:14).
