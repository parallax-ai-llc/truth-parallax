## The Furnishings Fashioned

Exodus 37 records Bezalel's construction of the five furnishings for the Tabernacle's interior: the Ark of the Covenant, the mercy seat with cherubim, the table of showbread, the golden lampstand, and the altar of incense, along with the holy anointing oil and incense. The chapter follows the instructions of chapters 25 and 30 with faithful precision. Protestant interpreters read this construction narrative as confirmation that God's design is not merely theoretical but realized in material form — a principle that points forward to the incarnation itself.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Bezalel Builds the Ark (37:1-9)

Bezalel personally constructs the Ark and mercy seat — the most sacred objects in all of Israel's worship. The Ark is acacia wood overlaid with gold, the mercy seat is pure beaten gold, and the cherubim are hammered from one piece with the mercy seat.

| Furnishing | Materials | Typological Significance |
|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| Ark | Acacia wood, gold | Christ's two natures — human and divine |
| Mercy seat | Pure gold, one piece | Propitiation — the place where wrath is turned aside |
| Cherubim | Gold, integral to mercy seat | Angelic witnesses, guardians of holiness |
| Poles for carrying | Acacia, gold-covered | The Ark moves with God's pilgrim people |

**Calvin** emphasized that the mercy seat was beaten from a single piece of gold, not assembled from parts. This unity speaks to the integrity of Christ's atoning work — His propitiation is not pieced together from multiple sources but is a single, complete, and perfect act. Romans 3:25 identifies Christ as the *hilasterion* (mercy seat/propitiation), the place where God's righteous wrath and His merciful love meet.

### The Table of Showbread (37:10-16)

The gold-overlaid table with its plates, dishes, bowls, and pitchers is constructed according to the pattern of 25:23-30. Twelve loaves will be placed on it continually, representing the twelve tribes in perpetual communion before the LORD.

Protestant interpreters connect the table to the Lord's Supper. Just as the bread was set before God as an ongoing expression of covenant fellowship, so the Lord's Table is the place where believers commune with Christ and with one another. The table is not merely memorial but a means of grace — an occasion of real spiritual nourishment.

### The Golden Lampstand (37:17-24)

The lampstand is hammered from a single talent of pure gold — one piece, with no joints or seams. Its six branches, almond-blossom ornaments, and seven lamps are all integral to the whole.

**Matthew Henry** observed that the lampstand's unity — hammered from a single piece — illustrates that all true light has a single source. Christ is the Light of the World (John 8:12), and the church's witness is derivative, not independent. The seven lamps correspond to the seven churches in Revelation 1-3, whose light depends entirely on Christ who walks among them.

The almond-blossom decoration is also significant. The Hebrew word for almond (*shaqed*) is related to the word for "watching" (*shoqed*). Jeremiah 1:11-12 plays on this connection. The lampstand's almond motif suggests that God is watchful, alert, and attentive — His light never sleeps.

### The Altar of Incense (37:25-28)

The golden altar, also made of acacia wood overlaid with gold, is constructed for the burning of incense morning and evening. Its position in the Holy Place, nearest the veil, signifies the intimate connection between prayer and God's presence.

### The Anointing Oil and Incense (37:29)

The chapter concludes with the preparation of the sacred anointing oil and the pure spice incense, compounded according to the pharmacist's art. These unique substances, reserved exclusively for sacred use, represent the Spirit's anointing and the prayers of the saints — both holy, both irreplaceable, both impossible to manufacture by merely human means.

## Theological Significance

- **The design realized**: What God commanded in chapters 25 and 30, Bezalel executes in chapter 37. The gap between divine instruction and human obedience is bridged by the Spirit's empowerment. Protestant theology affirms that obedience is both commanded and enabled — God gives what He requires.
- **Every furnishing points to Christ**: The Ark speaks of His presence, the mercy seat of His atonement, the table of His sustaining fellowship, the lampstand of His illuminating truth, and the incense altar of His perpetual intercession.
- **Unity and integrity**: The one-piece mercy seat and one-piece lampstand teach that Christ's person and work are undivided. His atonement cannot be fragmented, and His light cannot be separated from its source.
- **Material worship, spiritual reality**: Physical objects carry spiritual truth. Protestant theology, while rejecting the veneration of objects, affirms that God uses material means — bread, wine, water, word — to communicate spiritual realities.
