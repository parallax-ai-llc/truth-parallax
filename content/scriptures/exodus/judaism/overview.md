## Overview

Exodus (*Shemot*, "Names") is the foundational narrative of Jewish identity. The liberation from Egypt, the revelation at Sinai, and the giving of the Torah constitute the defining moment when Israel became a nation bound to God by covenant. The annual Passover Seder retells this story as a living experience: "In every generation, one must see oneself as though one personally went out of Egypt" (Mishnah Pesachim 10:5).

## Key Interpretive Themes

- **Redemption and memory**: The obligation to remember the Exodus is the most frequently repeated commandment
- **Halakhah from Exodus**: The Book of the Covenant (chs. 21-23) is the foundation of Jewish civil and criminal law
- **Sinai revelation**: The entire Torah — written and oral — was given at Sinai (Talmud Berakhot 5a)
- **The Golden Calf**: A paradigm for teshuvah (repentance) and God's mercy
