## The Earthly Copy

Exodus 25 begins the Tabernacle instructions with detailed specifications for the Ark of the Covenant, the table of showbread, and the golden lampstand. God commands Moses to build "according to the pattern which I showed you on the mountain" (25:40). Orthodox readers see the sacred architecture of divine presence. Gnostic interpreters see the Demiurge constructing a material copy of a heavenly reality he does not fully comprehend — an imitation Pleroma built of gold, acacia wood, and animal skins.

### The Orthodox Reading

God instructs Moses to receive offerings from the people and build a sanctuary "that I may dwell in their midst" (25:8). The Ark, overlaid with gold and topped with two cherubim, will house the tablets of the Law. The table will hold the bread of the Presence. The golden lampstand (menorah) with its seven branches will provide light. Everything follows a heavenly pattern revealed on the mountain.

### The Gnostic Reading

The phrase "according to the pattern" (25:40) is the key to Gnostic interpretation of the entire Tabernacle section. The Demiurge has a pattern — a heavenly model — that he instructs Moses to copy in material form. But where does this pattern come from? In Valentinian cosmology, the Demiurge fashioned the material cosmos as an imperfect copy of the Pleroma's structure, using forms he glimpsed but did not originate. The Tabernacle is a copy of a copy — the Demiurge's copy of the Pleroma, now copied again in gold and wood.

The *Tripartite Tractate* describes how the Demiurge creates "images" of the Pleromic aeons, but these images are always degraded, always material, always missing the essential quality of the originals — which is living consciousness. The Ark of the Covenant, however ornate, is a box. The cherubim, however finely crafted, are statues. The Demiurge cannot reproduce what he himself lacks: the living light of the Pleroma.

The cherubim atop the Ark present a particular puzzle. In Gnostic thought, certain angelic beings serve the true God beyond the Demiurge's realm. The cherubim in the Tabernacle are the Demiurge's domesticated version — crafted, positioned, and controlled. They face each other across the mercy seat, frozen in gold, a static imitation of the dynamic, living beings of the Pleroma.

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| The Tabernacle is God's dwelling among his people | The Demiurge builds a material prison for worship |
| The heavenly pattern guarantees sacred authenticity | The pattern is a copy of the Pleroma the Demiurge does not understand |
| The Ark contains the covenant | A golden box containing stone tablets — matter encasing matter |
| The lampstand gives light | Artificial light substituting for the Pleroma's radiance |

### Theological Significance

Exodus 25 initiates the Tabernacle construction that will dominate the remainder of Exodus. For Gnostic readers, every specification — every cubit of gold, every ring and pole — represents the Demiurge's attempt to capture the Pleroma's beauty in material form. The attempt is doomed to produce an elaborate, ornate prison for worship rather than a genuine dwelling of the divine.
