## The Name of the Demiurge

Exodus 6 presents God's reaffirmation of the covenant promises and the revelation that the patriarchs knew him as *El Shaddai* but not by the name *YHWH*. The chapter also includes a genealogy of Moses and Aaron. Orthodox readers see covenant continuity and divine faithfulness. Gnostic interpreters find evidence that the Demiurge's identity is unstable, shifting between names as he consolidates power over successive generations.

### The Orthodox Reading

God reassures Moses after the failed first encounter with Pharaoh. He reveals that while the patriarchs knew him as *El Shaddai* ("God Almighty"), the name *YHWH* — expressing his covenantal, redemptive character — was not fully disclosed until now. The genealogy grounds Moses and Aaron in the lineage of Levi, establishing their priestly authority. The chapter reaffirms that God will deliver Israel with "an outstretched arm and mighty acts of judgment."

### The Gnostic Reading

The admission that the patriarchs knew a different name for God is, for Gnostic readers, a crucial textual signal. The *Apocryphon of John* describes how the Demiurge operates under multiple names — Yaldabaoth, Saklas, Samael — each representing a different aspect of his ignorant rule. The shift from *El Shaddai* to *YHWH* suggests not progressive revelation but the Demiurge's evolving strategy of self-presentation. He adapts his identity to each generation's capacity for obedience.

The Valentinian teacher Ptolemy, in his *Letter to Flora*, argued that the Mosaic law (and by extension, the Mosaic God) represented an intermediate principle — neither the supreme good nor absolute evil, but a lesser deity of justice who lacks the transcendent goodness of the true Father. The shifting names in Exodus 6 fit this framework: a being who is not self-identical in the way the true Monad is, but who must constantly redefine himself in relation to his subjects.

The genealogy of Moses and Aaron (6:14–25) is read by Gnostics as the archonic chain of transmission — the establishment of a material lineage through which the Demiurge's authority is transmitted from generation to generation. Unlike the pneumatic spark, which has no earthly genealogy and belongs to the Pleroma, the Demiurge's power operates through bloodlines, tribal affiliations, and institutional succession.

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| God progressively reveals his name | The Demiurge shifts identity across generations |
| Covenant promises reaffirmed | The Demiurge reinforces claims of ownership |
| Genealogy establishes priestly authority | Archonic power transmitted through material lineage |
| "Outstretched arm" promises deliverance | The Demiurge promises violence as salvation |

### Theological Significance

Exodus 6 raises the central Gnostic question about the Hebrew Bible's deity: is progressive revelation the unfolding of a consistent divine nature, or is it the adaptive strategy of a lesser being who must constantly reinvent himself to maintain control? The instability of the divine name, for Gnostic readers, points toward the latter — and sets the stage for the escalating display of archonic power in the plagues to come.
