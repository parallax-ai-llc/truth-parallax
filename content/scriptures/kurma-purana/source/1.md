---
title: "Kurma Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "kurma-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The sages, assembled at the great sacrifice in the forest of Naimisha, spoke to Suta, the learned one: "O Suta, thou who art versed in all the Puranas, we desire to hear the Kurma Purana, which was spoken by the Lord Vishnu himself in the form of a tortoise."

**2** Suta said: "Hear, O sages, the sacred Kurma Purana, which was narrated by the blessed Lord Vishnu when he assumed the form of a great tortoise to support Mount Mandara during the churning of the ocean."

**3** In the beginning, there was only the supreme Brahman, without beginning and without end, self-luminous, eternal, unchanging. From that Brahman, by his own will, the universe came forth.

**4** The Lord Vishnu, the preserver of all worlds, is that supreme Brahman. He is the cause of creation, preservation, and dissolution. He pervades all things, yet is untouched by them.

**5** He who meditates upon the Lord Vishnu with a pure heart, who chants his sacred names, who offers devotion with sincerity, that person attains the highest state, from which there is no return.

**6** In ancient times, the gods and the demons resolved to churn the great ocean of milk to obtain the nectar of immortality. They took Mount Mandara as the churning rod and the serpent Vasuki as the rope.

**7** But the mountain, being exceedingly heavy, began to sink into the ocean. Then the Lord Vishnu, out of compassion for the gods, assumed the form of a great tortoise and placed the mountain upon his back.

**8** Thus supported, the churning proceeded. From the ocean arose many wondrous things: the wish-fulfilling cow Kamadhenu, the divine horse Uchchaihshravas, the celestial tree Parijata, and the goddess Lakshmi, resplendent with beauty.

**9** Then arose the deadly poison Halahala, which threatened to destroy all creation. The great Lord Shiva, out of compassion, drank the poison and held it in his throat, which turned blue. Therefore he is called Nilakantha, the blue-throated one.

**10** At last arose Dhanvantari, bearing the vessel of nectar. The demons seized it, but the Lord Vishnu, assuming the form of the enchantress Mohini, retrieved it and distributed it among the gods.

**11** Thus the gods obtained immortality, and the demons were defeated. And the Lord Vishnu, in the form of the tortoise, spoke to the assembled gods, teaching them the path of dharma.

**12** The Lord said: "I am the Self dwelling in all beings. I am the beginning, the middle, and the end of all that exists. He who sees me in all things, and all things in me, he never loses sight of me, nor do I lose sight of him."

**13** "Among the Vedas, I am the Sama Veda. Among the gods, I am Indra. Among the senses, I am the mind. Among living beings, I am consciousness."

**14** "Among the Rudras, I am Shankara. Among the Yakshas and Rakshasas, I am Kubera. Among the Vasus, I am Agni. Among the mountains, I am Meru."

**15** "Know me as the eternal seed of all beings. I am the intelligence of the intelligent, the splendor of the splendid. I am the strength of the strong, devoid of desire and attachment."

**16** "He who knows me as unborn and beginningless, as the great Lord of all worlds, he among mortals is undeluded, and he is freed from all sins."

**17** Thus spoke the Lord Vishnu in the form of the tortoise, and the gods, having heard his teaching, bowed down in reverence and returned to their celestial abodes, filled with knowledge and devotion.
