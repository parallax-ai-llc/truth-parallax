---
title: "Kurma Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "kurma-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Lord Vishnu said: "Now hear from me the account of creation, how the universe came into being from the unmanifest."

**2** "In the beginning, there was only darkness wrapped in darkness. All this was an indistinguishable sea. That which was hidden by the void, the One, was born through the power of austerity."

**3** "From the supreme Brahman arose Prakriti, the primal nature, consisting of the three gunas: sattva, rajas, and tamas. From Prakriti arose Mahat, the great principle of intelligence."

**4** "From Mahat arose Ahamkara, the principle of individuation. From Ahamkara arose the five subtle elements: sound, touch, form, taste, and smell."

**5** "From the five subtle elements arose the five gross elements: space, air, fire, water, and earth. From these five gross elements the entire visible universe was formed."

**6** "The cosmic egg came into being, floating upon the waters of dissolution. Within that egg, Brahma the creator was born. He looked in the four directions, and thus his four faces came into being."

**7** "Brahma created the mind-born sons: Marichi, Bhrigu, Angiras, Pulastya, Pulaha, Kratu, Daksha, Atri, and Vasishtha. These nine became the progenitors of all living beings."

**8** "From Daksha came the daughters who became the wives of the gods, the sages, and the ancestors of all creatures. Aditi, the mother of the gods, and Diti, the mother of the demons, were among his daughters."

**9** "From Aditi were born the twelve Adityas, the luminous gods of the heavens. Among them Vishnu was the greatest, the upholder of dharma, the protector of the worlds."

**10** "The universe is composed of fourteen worlds: seven above and seven below. Above are Bhur, Bhuvar, Svar, Mahar, Jana, Tapas, and Satya. Below are Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Rasatala, Talatala, Mahatala, and Patala."

**11** "In the center of the universe stands Mount Meru, made of gold, rising eighty-four thousand yojanas above the earth. Around it revolve the sun, the moon, and the stars."

**12** "The earth is divided into seven continents, each surrounded by a different ocean. The central continent is Jambudvipa, in which is found the land of Bharatavarsha, the land of action."

**13** "It is in Bharatavarsha alone that beings can perform actions that lead to liberation. The gods themselves desire to be born here, for here alone can the fruits of karma be reaped."

**14** "The time of the universe is measured in yugas. The four yugas are Satya, Treta, Dvapara, and Kali. In the Satya Yuga, dharma stands on four feet. In each succeeding yuga, one foot is lost."

**15** "At the end of the Kali Yuga, when dharma has all but disappeared, I shall incarnate as Kalki, riding a white horse, to destroy the wicked and to establish a new Satya Yuga."

**16** "Thus the cycle of creation and dissolution continues without end. A thousand such cycles make one day of Brahma, and another thousand make his night. At the end of Brahma's life, the entire universe is dissolved into the supreme Brahman."

**17** "But I, the supreme Lord, remain unchanged through all these cycles. I am the eternal witness, the unchanging reality behind the ever-changing universe."
