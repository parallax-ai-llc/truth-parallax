---
title: "Kurma Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "kurma-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The Lord Vishnu said: "Now hear from me the account of my incarnations, by which I descend into the world to protect the righteous and destroy the wicked."

**2** "Whenever dharma declines and adharma prevails, I manifest myself in the world. For the protection of the good, for the destruction of the wicked, and for the establishment of dharma, I come into being age after age."

**3** "In the first incarnation, I took the form of Matsya, the fish, to save Manu and the seven sages from the great deluge. I preserved the Vedas, which had been stolen by a demon, and restored them to Brahma."

**4** "In the second incarnation, I took the form of Kurma, the tortoise, to support Mount Mandara during the churning of the ocean. This is the form in which I now speak to you."

**5** "In the third incarnation, I took the form of Varaha, the boar, to rescue the earth from the demon Hiranyaksha, who had dragged her to the bottom of the cosmic ocean."

**6** "In the fourth incarnation, I took the form of Narasimha, half-man and half-lion, to destroy the demon Hiranyakashipu, who had obtained a boon that he could not be killed by man or beast, by day or by night, inside or outside, on earth or in the sky."

**7** "In the fifth incarnation, I took the form of Vamana, the dwarf, to reclaim the three worlds from the demon king Bali. With three strides I covered the earth, the sky, and the heavens."

**8** "In the sixth incarnation, I took the form of Parashurama, to destroy the arrogant Kshatriyas who had strayed from the path of dharma. Twenty-one times I swept the earth clean of their tyranny."

**9** "In the seventh incarnation, I took the form of Rama, the prince of Ayodhya, to destroy the demon king Ravana, who had abducted my consort Sita. The story of Rama is the eternal tale of dharma triumphant."

**10** "In the eighth incarnation, I descended as Krishna, the dark-hued one, the lord of Dwaraka, the charioteer of Arjuna. I spoke the Bhagavad Gita on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, revealing the path of selfless action, knowledge, and devotion."

**11** "In the ninth incarnation, I appeared as the Buddha, the awakened one, to teach compassion and non-violence to the world, and to lead the deluded away from the path of empty ritualism."

**12** "In the tenth incarnation, at the end of the Kali Yuga, I shall appear as Kalki, riding a white horse, bearing a flaming sword, to destroy the forces of darkness and to usher in a new age of truth."

**13** "These are my principal incarnations, but know that my incarnations are innumerable, like the waves of the sea. Whenever the need arises, I descend into the world in whatever form is necessary."

**14** "He who knows my divine incarnations in truth, when he leaves his body, he is not born again. He comes to me, O gods."

**15** "Therefore, fix your minds upon me. Worship me with devotion. Surrender all your actions to me. Take refuge in me alone. I shall deliver you from all sins. Do not grieve."

**16** "This is the supreme secret, the teaching of the Kurma Purana. He who hears it with faith, who recites it, who meditates upon it, shall attain the highest state. He shall dwell in the abode of Vishnu for all eternity."

**17** Thus concluded the Lord Vishnu his teaching in the form of the tortoise. The gods, having received this supreme wisdom, bowed down before the Lord with folded hands and returned to their celestial abodes, their hearts filled with devotion and knowledge.

**18** Suta said: "Thus have I narrated to you the essence of the Kurma Purana, as it was told by the Lord Vishnu himself. He who hears this sacred Purana is freed from all sins and attains the supreme goal."
