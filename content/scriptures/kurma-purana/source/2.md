---
title: "Kurma Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kurma-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Lord Vishnu, still in the form of the tortoise, continued to teach the gods, saying: "Now hear from me the nature of dharma, by which a man attains the highest good."

**2** "Dharma is the foundation of the universe. It upholds all beings. By dharma the sun rises, by dharma the rain falls, by dharma the seasons change. Without dharma, all would be chaos."

**3** "The duties of the four castes and the four stages of life have been ordained for the welfare of the world. He who performs his duties without attachment, offering the fruits of his actions to me, attains liberation."

**4** "The Brahmana shall study and teach the Vedas, perform and officiate at sacrifices, and give and receive alms. The Kshatriya shall protect the people, study the Vedas, and perform sacrifices. The Vaishya shall engage in agriculture, trade, and the tending of cattle. The Shudra shall serve the other three castes."

**5** "The student shall dwell with his teacher, study the sacred texts, practice celibacy, and serve his guru with devotion. The householder shall marry, support his family, perform the five daily sacrifices, and practice hospitality."

**6** "The forest-dweller shall retire to the forest, subsist on roots and fruits, practice austerities, and meditate upon the Self. The renunciant shall abandon all possessions, wander without a fixed abode, and devote himself entirely to the realization of Brahman."

**7** "Among all forms of worship, the worship of Vishnu is supreme. He who chants my thousand names with devotion, who meditates upon my form, who offers me even a leaf, a flower, a fruit, or water with love, I accept that offering."

**8** "The sacred places where I dwell are Badrinath in the high mountains, Dwaraka by the western sea, Jagannath Puri in the east, and Rameswaram in the south. He who visits these places with devotion is purified of all sins."

**9** "But know that the true pilgrimage is the pilgrimage of the mind. He whose mind is pure, who has conquered his senses, who sees the Self in all beings, he has visited all the sacred places."

**10** "The practice of yoga leads to the realization of the Self. By restraining the senses, by concentrating the mind, by meditating upon the supreme Brahman, the yogi attains samadhi, the state of perfect absorption."

**11** "In samadhi, the distinction between the knower, the known, and the act of knowing disappears. The yogi realizes his identity with the supreme Brahman. This is liberation, this is the highest state."

**12** "But for those who cannot practice yoga, the path of devotion is prescribed. By loving me with all their heart, by surrendering all their actions to me, by taking refuge in me alone, they attain the same supreme state."

**13** "I am equally disposed to all beings. None is hateful to me, none is dear to me. But those who worship me with devotion, they are in me, and I am in them."

**14** "Even if the most sinful person worships me with exclusive devotion, he must be regarded as righteous, for he has rightly resolved. Soon he becomes righteous and attains eternal peace. Know this for certain: my devotee never perishes."

**15** Thus spoke the Lord Vishnu, and the gods were filled with joy. They praised the Lord with hymns and songs, and the heavens resounded with their devotion.
