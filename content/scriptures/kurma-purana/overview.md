## Overview

The Kurma Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, named after Kurma, the tortoise avatar of Vishnu who supported Mount Mandara during the churning of the cosmic ocean. Despite being named after a Vaishnava avatar, the Kurma Purana is distinctly Shaiva in its theological orientation, with extensive material glorifying Shiva as the supreme deity.

The text is generally dated to the 6th-10th centuries CE and contains approximately 95 chapters in its present form. It presents a complex theological vision in which Shiva and Vishnu are ultimately understood as aspects of one supreme reality, though Shaiva perspectives predominate. The text includes important material on the Pashupata and Lakullsha Shaiva traditions.

## Structure

The text is organized into four major sections:

1. **Purva Vibhaga Part 1 — Cosmology and Avatars**: The Kurma avatar, the churning of the ocean, and the cosmological framework
2. **Purva Vibhaga Part 2 — Shaiva Theology**: The supremacy of Shiva, Pashupata doctrines, and Shaiva worship
3. **Uttara Vibhaga Part 1 — Dharma and Ethics**: Social duties, moral conduct, and the four stages of life
4. **Uttara Vibhaga Part 2 — Liberation and Yoga**: Yogic practices, Shaiva meditation, and the paths to moksha

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Kurma Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: Approached as a text with Vaishnava elements (the Kurma narrative) within a predominantly Shaiva framework
- **Shaivism**: A significant Shaiva Purana providing important material on Pashupata theology and Shiva worship
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for its passages on the unity of Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahman as the one non-dual reality
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Engaged critically, with Dvaita scholars reinterpreting Shaiva supremacy claims within their Vaishnava framework
- **Academic**: An important source for the history of Pashupata Shaivism and the interaction of Vaishnava and Shaiva traditions

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Kurma Avatar | Vishnu as the cosmic tortoise supporting the churning of the ocean of milk |
| Shaiva Supremacy | Shiva as the ultimate reality transcending all other deities |
| Pashupata Tradition | The theology and practices of the Pashupata Shaiva tradition |
| Shiva-Vishnu Unity | The theological reconciliation of Shaiva and Vaishnava claims |
| Yoga and Liberation | Shaiva yogic practices leading to union with Shiva |
