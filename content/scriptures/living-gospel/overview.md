## Overview

The Living Gospel (also known as the Great Gospel) is the first and most important of the seven canonical scriptures attributed to Mani (216-274/277 CE), the founder of Manichaeism. Though the original text has been largely lost, its contents can be substantially reconstructed from quotations, summaries, and references in both Manichaean and anti-Manichaean sources. The Living Gospel presented Mani's comprehensive vision of cosmic history as a drama of Light and Darkness.

The text articulated the foundational Manichaean cosmology: the existence of two eternal, co-equal principles (Light and Darkness), the cosmic catastrophe of their mixture, the creation of the material world as a mechanism for separating the trapped particles of light from darkness, and the ultimate eschatological restoration when all light would be liberated and the two realms permanently separated. Mani presented himself as the "Seal of the Prophets," the final messenger in a line that included Buddha, Zoroaster, and Jesus, sent to deliver the definitive revelation of saving knowledge (*gnosis*).

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Manichaean**: The foundational scripture of Mani's revelation, the first of the seven canonical texts
- **Academic**: Reconstructed from fragmentary evidence across multiple languages and source traditions
- **Comparative**: Rich material for comparison with Gnostic, Zoroastrian, Buddhist, and Christian cosmologies
- **Gnostic**: A major expression of gnostic soteriology with distinctive cosmological elaboration
- **Christian**: Examined in relation to early Christian heresiology and the figure of Jesus in Manichaean thought
- **Interfaith**: Illustrates the universalist aspirations of Mani's prophetic mission

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Cosmic Dualism | The eternal opposition of two co-equal principles, Light and Darkness |
| Trapped Light | The imprisonment of divine light particles in the material world |
| Gnosis | Saving knowledge as the key to liberation from the material prison |
| Prophetic Succession | Buddha, Zoroaster, Jesus, and Mani as successive messengers of light |
| Asceticism | The ethical and ascetic practices required for the liberation of light |
| Eschatology | The final cosmic separation and restoration of the original state |
