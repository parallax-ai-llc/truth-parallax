Chapter 14 of the Living Gospel, with its treatment of church, exemplifies key themes of the gnostic worldview. The motifs of elect, hearers are central to gnostic soteriology and cosmology, and this chapter develops them with characteristic depth and narrative power.

The gnostic dimensions of this chapter — particularly its engagement with church — connect to broader patterns in gnostic literature, from the Nag Hammadi texts to Mandaean writings. The emphasis on elect, hearers reflects the gnostic conviction that saving knowledge is the key to liberation from the material realm.
