Chapter 17 of the Living Gospel, with its treatment of prayer, exemplifies key themes of the gnostic worldview. The motifs of liturgy, hymns are central to gnostic soteriology and cosmology, and this chapter develops them with characteristic depth and narrative power.

The gnostic dimensions of this chapter — particularly its engagement with prayer — connect to broader patterns in gnostic literature, from the Nag Hammadi texts to Mandaean writings. The emphasis on liturgy, hymns reflects the gnostic conviction that saving knowledge is the key to liberation from the material realm.
