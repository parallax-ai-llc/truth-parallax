Chapter 22 of the Living Gospel, with its treatment of seal of prophets, exemplifies key themes of the gnostic worldview. The motifs of Mani, Paraclete are central to gnostic soteriology and cosmology, and this chapter develops them with characteristic depth and narrative power.

The gnostic dimensions of this chapter — particularly its engagement with seal of prophets — connect to broader patterns in gnostic literature, from the Nag Hammadi texts to Mandaean writings. The emphasis on Mani, Paraclete reflects the gnostic conviction that saving knowledge is the key to liberation from the material realm.
