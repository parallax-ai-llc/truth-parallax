Chapter 20 of the Living Gospel, addressing last judgment, holds particular significance within the Manichaean tradition. The themes of judgment, separation are central to Manichaean theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Manichaean tradition, this chapter's treatment of last judgment is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on judgment, separation inform Manichaean self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
