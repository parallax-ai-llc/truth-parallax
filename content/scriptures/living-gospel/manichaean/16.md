Chapter 16 of the Living Gospel, addressing three seals, holds particular significance within the Manichaean tradition. The themes of mouth, hand are central to Manichaean theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Manichaean tradition, this chapter's treatment of three seals is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on mouth, hand inform Manichaean self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
