Chapter 21 of the Living Gospel, addressing new aeon, holds particular significance within the Manichaean tradition. The themes of restoration, paradise regained are central to Manichaean theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Manichaean tradition, this chapter's treatment of new aeon is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on restoration, paradise regained inform Manichaean self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
