Chapter 11 of the Living Gospel, addressing Adam and Eve, holds particular significance within the Manichaean tradition. The themes of anthropogony, light soul are central to Manichaean theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Manichaean tradition, this chapter's treatment of Adam and Eve is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on anthropogony, light soul inform Manichaean self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
