## Overview

A comparative approach to the Living Gospel situates this text within the broader context of world religious literature, identifying parallels, contrasts, and mutual influences across traditions. Rich material for comparison with Gnostic, Zoroastrian, Buddhist, and Christian cosmologies. This comparative perspective enriches understanding both of the Living Gospel itself and of the religious traditions with which it is brought into dialogue.

Key areas of comparative inquiry include cosmological narratives, theological concepts, ethical frameworks, ritual practices, and soteriological visions. The comparative study of the Living Gospel contributes to a deeper appreciation of both the universal themes in human religious experience and the distinctive contributions of Manichaeism to the world's spiritual heritage.
