---
title: "Living Gospel Section 15"
scripture: "living-gospel"
chapter: 15
---

1. The Living Gospel teaches concerning prayer and worship: "Prayer is the breath of the Light Mind, the voice of the soul calling to its Father."

2. Four times each day the faithful shall pray: at the sixth hour, at the ninth hour, at sunset, and at the third hour of the night.

3. Before prayer, the faithful shall wash their hands and face with water, for water carries the Light, and through washing, the soul is reminded of purity.

4. The faithful shall face toward the Great Luminary—the Sun by day, the Moon by night—for these are the visible thrones of the divine power.

5. In prayer, the faithful shall make seven prostrations, bowing the body of Darkness before the throne of Light.

6. With the first prostration, pray: "Blessed is the Father of Greatness, the God of Truth, who dwells in the Paradise of Light."

7. With the second: "Blessed are the Aeons of Light and the angels who serve the Father eternally."

8. With the third: "Blessed are the Apostles of Light: Seth, Noah, Abraham, the Buddha, Zoroaster, Jesus, and Mani."

9. With the fourth: "Blessed are the Elect, who keep the Three Seals and liberate the Light from Darkness."

10. With the fifth: "Blessed are the Hearers, who serve the Elect and keep the Ten Commandments."

11. With the sixth: "I confess my sins and the sins of my former lives. May the Light Mind forgive me and strengthen me."

12. With the seventh: "May all the Light in the world be swiftly gathered, and may the separation come, and may the Darkness be sealed forever."

13. After the prostrations, the faithful shall sit in silence and meditate upon the Light within, and upon the Column of Glory, and upon the Paradise that awaits.
