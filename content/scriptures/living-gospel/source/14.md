---
title: "Living Gospel Section 14"
scripture: "living-gospel"
chapter: 14
---

1. The Gospel teaches concerning the daily meal of the Elect, which is the sacred act of liberation.

2. Each day, the Hearers bring food to the Elect: bread made from grain, fruits from the trees, and vegetables from the garden.

3. This food contains the Light that was released when the Third Messenger agitated the Archons. It is filled with the divine substance.

4. Before eating, the Elect prays over the food, saying: "I did not reap you, I did not grind you, I did not bake you. Another did these things and brought you to me. I eat you without guilt."

5. This prayer acknowledges that the preparation of food involves the suffering of the Light in the plants, which is borne by the Hearers as their sacred duty.

6. When the Elect consumes the food, the body of the Elect acts as a vessel of purification. The Light in the food is separated from the matter.

7. The purified Light rises through the body of the Elect, through prayer and holiness, and ascends to the Column of Glory.

8. The matter is expelled as waste, returned to the earth, and the Light is freed. This is the daily miracle of the Elect.

9. Therefore the Hearer who brings food to the Elect is not merely giving charity. They are participating in the cosmic work of salvation.

10. The alms of the Hearer are transformed into Light through the body of the Elect. Both the giver and the receiver share in the merit.

11. This is why the meal of the Elect is conducted in silence and reverence, for it is a sacred rite, not a mere satisfaction of hunger.

12. The Elect eats not for the body's sake but for the Light's sake, and every meal is an act of worship.
