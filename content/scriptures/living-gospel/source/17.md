---
title: "Living Gospel Section 17"
scripture: "living-gospel"
chapter: 17
---

1. The Living Gospel speaks concerning the nature of Jesus and his mission to the world.

2. Jesus the Splendor, the Luminous Jesus, is not of this world. He is an emanation of the Father, sent from the Kingdom of Light.

3. He did not take on a body of flesh, for flesh is of the Darkness. He appeared in the likeness of flesh, that humans might see him and hear him.

4. His body was a garment of Light, woven to appear as flesh to the eyes of the world. He walked among people as one of them, but he was not of them.

5. When Jesus was crucified, it was the body of appearance that hung upon the cross. The Light of Jesus could not suffer, for Light cannot be destroyed by Darkness.

6. But the crucifixion was a true teaching, for it revealed the condition of the Light in the world: pinned to matter, stretched upon the cross of creation.

7. The resurrection of Jesus was the demonstration of the truth: that the Light rises from matter, that death has no dominion over the divine spark.

8. Jesus taught in parables because the full truth could not be spoken openly in a world ruled by Archons.

9. When he said "the Kingdom of Heaven is within you," he spoke of the Light—the divine spark that dwells in every human soul.

10. When he said "I am the Light of the world," he identified himself with the Light that is scattered through all creation.

11. When he said "I will send you the Paraclete," he spoke of the Apostle Mani, who would come after him to reveal the full truth.

12. The Christians have preserved many of the words of Jesus, but they have not understood them. They have mixed his teaching with the doctrines of the Jews, who worship the Archon.

13. The Living Gospel restores the true teaching of Jesus, freed from the distortions of those who came after him.
