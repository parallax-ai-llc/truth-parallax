---
title: "Living Gospel Section 16"
scripture: "living-gospel"
chapter: 16
---

1. The Gospel teaches concerning the fasts and feasts of the Church of Light.

2. Every Monday, the faithful shall fast, taking no food until sunset, for Monday is the day on which the Apostle received his first revelation.

3. During the fast, the soul is freed from the burden of digestion, and the Light Mind grows stronger, and the Old Man grows weaker.

4. Fasting is not punishment but liberation. When the body is empty, the soul is light. When the body is heavy, the soul is weighed down.

5. Before the great Feast of the Bema, the faithful shall keep a fast of thirty days, eating only after sunset and taking only the simplest foods.

6. This fast commemorates the suffering of the Apostle, who endured chains and persecution for the sake of the Light.

7. The Feast of the Bema is the holiest day of the year. On this day, the community gathers around the empty throne of the Apostle.

8. The throne is set upon a platform of five steps, representing the five Greatnesses of the Father. It is covered with fine cloth but left empty.

9. For the Apostle is present in spirit, though his body has departed. His Light dwells with the Father, but his presence fills the assembly.

10. On the day of the Bema, the sacred books are read aloud. The community confesses its sins and renews its devotion.

11. Hymns are sung to the Father, to the Apostles of Light, and to the Church. The faithful embrace one another with the Right Hand of fellowship.

12. The Bema is a day of joy, for it celebrates the victory of Light over Darkness, the ascent of the Apostle, and the promise that all Light shall be gathered.

13. Other feasts are kept throughout the year, marking the descent of the Light Mind, the calling of the Apostle, and the sending forth of the missionaries.
