---
title: "Living Gospel Section 11"
scripture: "living-gospel"
chapter: 11
---

1. The Living Gospel speaks concerning the Light Mind and the Old Man who dwell in the human soul.

2. Within every person two powers contend: the Light Mind, which is from the Father, and the Old Man, which is from the Archons.

3. The Old Man was placed in the body at its creation. He is composed of greed, lust, anger, pride, and ignorance—the five attributes of Darkness.

4. The Old Man whispers to the soul: "This world is your home. The body is your self. Take what you desire. There is nothing beyond."

5. The Light Mind comes to the soul when it hears the call of truth and turns toward the Light. It is the gift of the Third Messenger.

6. The Light Mind says to the soul: "This world is a prison. The body is a garment. You are a stranger here. Your home is the Paradise of Light."

7. Between these two voices, every human being must choose. This is the great decision, the crisis of every life.

8. Those who follow the Old Man sink deeper into matter. Their Light grows dim. They are bound more tightly to the wheel of rebirth.

9. Those who follow the Light Mind begin to rise. Their Light grows bright. They loosen the bonds that tie them to the world.

10. The Light Mind brings five virtues: Love, Faith, Perfection, Patience, and Wisdom. These are the antidotes to the five poisons of the Old Man.

11. Love dissolves greed, for the one who loves gives freely. Faith dissolves doubt, for the one who trusts does not grasp. Perfection dissolves corruption, for the one who is whole needs nothing from the Darkness.

12. Patience dissolves anger, for the one who waits in peace cannot be provoked. Wisdom dissolves ignorance, for the one who knows cannot be deceived.

13. Cultivate these five virtues, and the Light Mind will grow strong within you, and the Old Man will be driven out, and your soul will be prepared for the ascent.
