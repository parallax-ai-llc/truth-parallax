## Overview

The Christian reading of the Living Gospel engages with this text from within the living tradition of Christian thought and practice. Examined in relation to early Christian heresiology and the figure of Jesus in Manichaean thought. This perspective brings the accumulated wisdom of Christian interpretation to bear on the text, illuminating dimensions that may not be apparent from external scholarly perspectives alone.

Within the Christian tradition, the Living Gospel is understood not merely as a historical document but as a living source of spiritual guidance and theological reflection. Christian interpreters bring distinctive hermeneutical principles, doctrinal frameworks, and experiential knowledge to their engagement with this text, producing readings that are both deeply informed and spiritually engaged.
