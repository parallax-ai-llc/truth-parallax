## Tibetan Buddhist Reading

This chapter presents the appearance of the wrathful deities — terrifying forms of the same enlightened energies that appeared previously as peaceful deities — and the judgment scene before Yama, the Lord of Death, who weighs the deceased's virtuous and sinful acts. Within the Tibetan Buddhist tradition, this material is understood as practical instruction of the highest importance, directly linked to the Dzogchen and Mahamudra teachings on the nature of mind.

The instructions in this chapter are intended to be read aloud to the dying or recently deceased person by a qualified lama or a dharma companion who has received the appropriate transmission. The reading is timed to correspond with the stages of the dissolution process: as the elements dissolve and the gross levels of consciousness withdraw, the practitioner (or the consciousness of the deceased) is guided to recognize each appearance as the natural display of rigpa — pure awareness — and to rest in that recognition without grasping or aversion.

In the Nyingma tradition, this chapter is closely connected to the Dzogchen practice of *thodgal* (direct crossing), in which the practitioner trains during life to recognize the spontaneous arising of light, sound, and form as the self-display of awareness. A practitioner who has mastered *thodgal* will, at the moment of death, recognize the luminosity and visions described in this chapter as familiar territory and attain liberation effortlessly. For those without such training, the reading of the text provides an external support for recognition.
