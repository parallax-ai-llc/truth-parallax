---
title: "Tibetan Book of the Dead - Chapter 1"
scripture: "tibetan-book-of-dead"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Herein lieth the setting-face-to-face to the reality in the intermediate state: the great deliverance by hearing while on the after-death plane, from the profound doctrine of the emancipation of the mind by meditation on the peaceful and wrathful deities.

**2** Obeisance to the Divine Body of Truth, the Incomprehensible, Boundless Light; obeisance to the Divine Body of Perfect Endowment, who are the Lotus and the Peaceful and the Wrathful Deities; obeisance to the Lotus-born Incarnation, Padma Sambhava, who is the Protector of all sentient beings; obeisance to the Gurus, the Three Bodies.

**3** This Great Doctrine of Liberation by Hearing shall now be presented for the benefit of all sentient beings.

**4** O nobly-born, listen well. There are those who have meditated much, yet have failed to recognize the signs of death and the signs of the intermediate state.

**5** The primary clear light is to be recognized at the moment of death. The secondary clear light is to be recognized during the intermediate state.

**6** When the expiration hath ceased, the vital-force will have sunk into the nerve-centre of Wisdom, and the Knower will be experiencing the Clear Light of the natural condition.

**7** Now the vital-force being thrown backwards and الأcoming along the right and left nerves, the intermediate state momentarily dawns.

**8** The above settingface-to-face is to be applied while the expiration is ceasing. The transference is best if applied when the breathing is about to cease.

**9** O nobly-born, that which is called death hath now come. Thou art departing from this world, but thou art not the only one; death cometh to all.

**10** Do not cling to this life out of fondness and weakness. Even though thou clingeth out of weakness, thou hast not the power to remain here.

**11** Thou wilt gain nothing more than wandering in this Sangsara. Be not attached; be not weak.

**12** Remember the Precious Trinity. O nobly-born, whatever fear and terror may come to thee in the intermediate state, forget not these words; and bearing their meaning at heart, go forwards: in them lieth the vital secret of recognition.

**13** Alas! when the Uncertain Experiencing of Reality is dawning upon me here, with every thought of fear or terror or awe for all visions set aside, may I recognize whatever visions appear as the reflections of mine own consciousness;

**14** May I know them to be of the nature of apparitions in the intermediate state; when at this all-important moment of achieving a great end, may I not fear the bands of peaceful and wrathful deities, mine own thought-forms.

**15** O nobly-born, when thy body and mind were separating, thou must have experienced a glimpse of the Pure Truth, subtle, sparkling, bright, dazzling, glorious, and radiantly awesome, in appearance like a mirage moving across a landscape in spring-time in one continuous stream of vibrations.

**16** Be not daunted thereby, nor terrified, nor awed. That is the radiance of thine own true nature. Recognize it.

**17** From the midst of that radiance, the natural sound of Reality, reverberating like a thousand thunders simultaneously sounding, will come. That is the natural sound of thine own real self.

**18** Be not daunted thereby, nor terrified, nor awed. The body which thou hast now is called the thought-body of propensities. Since thou hast not a material body of flesh and blood, whatever may come — sounds, lights, or rays — are all three unable to harm thee: thou art incapable of dying.

**19** It is quite sufficient for thee to know that these apparitions are thine own thought-forms. Recognize this to be the intermediate state.
