---
title: "Tibetan Book of the Dead - Chapter 3"
scripture: "tibetan-book-of-dead"
chapter: 3
---

**1** O nobly-born, if, notwithstanding the setting-face-to-face so many times, thou art still, through great obscurations of karma and evil propensities, unable to recognize, then the wrathful deities will come to shine upon thee.

**2** Thereupon the blood-drinking deities, called the Herukas, will dawn from within thine own brain and come to shine upon thee.

**3** From the eastern quarter of thy brain, the Buddha Heruka, dark brown of colour, with three heads, six hands, and four feet, the right face white, the left face red, the central face dark brown; the body emitting flames of radiance, will come to shine upon thee.

**4** O nobly-born, be not terrified; be not awed. Recognize it to be the embodiment of thine own intellect. As it is thine own tutelary deity, be not terrified.

**5** In reality, this is the Bhagavan Vairochana, the Father-Mother; therefore have no fear. Recognition and liberation will be simultaneous.

**6** From the southern quarter of thy brain, the Ratna Heruka, dark yellow in colour, with three heads, six hands, and four feet firmly postured, the right face white, the left face red, the central face dark yellow glowing, will come to shine upon thee.

**7** O nobly-born, be not terrified thereby. Recognize it as the embodiment of thine own intellect. It is thine own tutelary deity; therefore be not afraid.

**8** In reality, this is the Bhagavan Ratna-Sambhava, the Father-Mother; therefore have devotion.

**9** From the western quarter of thy brain, the Padma Heruka, dark red of colour, with three heads, six hands, and four feet firmly postured, the right face white, the left face blue, the central face dark red, will come to shine upon thee in a halo of flames.

**10** O nobly-born, be not terrified thereby. Recognize it as the embodiment of thine own intellect. As it is thine own tutelary deity, be not afraid.

**11** From the northern quarter of thy brain, the Karma Heruka, dark green of colour, with three heads, six hands, and four feet firmly postured, the right face white, the left face red, the central face dark green glowing, will come to shine upon thee.

**12** O nobly-born, be not terrified. Recognize it to be the embodiment of thine own intellect. As it is thine own tutelary deity, be not terrified. Be not afraid.

**13** O nobly-born, the outer ring of the mandala will be ringed about by the eight Htamenma goddesses, the eight Kerima goddesses, the four Female Door-Keepers, and twenty-eight powerful goddesses, having various heads — of lion, tiger, fox, wolf, vulture, and other beasts.

**14** Be not afraid of them. Recognize everything to be the thought-forms of thine own intellect. At this vitally important moment, remember the select teachings of the Guru.

**15** O nobly-born, he who meditateth upon the Great Perfection will recognize the clear radiances shining upon him to be his own thought-forms. They will be like meeting an old acquaintance. And trusting therein, liberation shall come.

**16** That which is called the Wrathful Deities will dawn from within thine own brain and come to shine upon thee. Be not afraid.

**17** Whatever apparitions of awe or terror may dawn upon thee, recognize them all to be the natural radiance of thine own intellect.

**18** O nobly-born, if thou recognizest not now, and if thou art frightened, all the peaceful deities will shine forth in the form of Mahakala, the Great Dark One. And all the wrathful deities will shine in the form of Dharma-Raja, the Lord of Death.

**19** And thine own thought-forms becoming Mara, thou wilt wander in the Sangsara.

**20** O nobly-born, if one recognizeth not one's own thought-forms, however learned one may be in the scriptures, one wandereth in the Sangsara. But if one recognizeth, by one important art, liberation will be obtained.
