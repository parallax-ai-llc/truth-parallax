---
title: "Tibetan Book of the Dead - Chapter 4"
scripture: "tibetan-book-of-dead"
chapter: 4
---

**1** O nobly-born, if thou hast still not been able to recognize, the Sidpa Bardo, the third stage of the intermediate state, will now dawn upon thee.

**2** At about the fourteenth day, the illusions of the Sidpa Bardo will come to shine. As previously, the setting-face-to-face will now be given.

**3** O nobly-born, in the Sidpa Bardo the body of the past life is called the shining illusory body. Thereupon the signs and the premonitions of the place of birth will come.

**4** At this time, a great wind shall arise. It shall be terrifying, fierce, icy, cutting. Fear it not. It is thine own illusory creation.

**5** A thick awesome darkness shall precede thee continually. Out of the midst thereof terrifying cries, "Strike! Slay!" will seem to reach thee. Fear these not.

**6** For those of much bad karma, many flesh-devouring demons will seem to pursue thee, uttering frightful yells. Terrifying phenomena, naturally arising, will come to shine upon thee.

**7** Some persons of bad karma will hear the sounds of weeping and wailing; and some will feel as though imprisoned in a dark region.

**8** Others will feel as though borne along by a whirlwind; and some will feel as though carried away in a flood; and some will hear the sound of fire, and some will hear the sound of wind.

**9** At that time, the grey light of the world of the pretas, the yellow light of the world of human beings, the green light of the world of the asuras, the blue light of the world of the devas, and the red light of the world of the animals will come to shine upon thee.

**10** O nobly-born, at this vitally important moment, the supreme art of the teaching is this: whatsoever the light-loss be, meditate upon the Great Compassionate One. Meditate upon thy tutelary deity, as though that deity were the reflection of the moon in water; apparent yet non-existent in itself.

**11** If thou knowest not how to meditate, then meditate upon the Compassionate One, and pray with intense earnestness. Thereby, through the grace of the Compassionate One, the womb-door will be closed.

**12** O nobly-born, through the power of karma thou wilt seem to be either going upwards, or going along on a level, or going downwards. Thereupon, meditate upon the Compassionate One. Remember.

**13** At this time, signs as to which continent thou art to be born into will come to thee. Recognize the signs.

**14** If thou art to be born in the Eastern Continent, thou wilt see a lake adorned with swans. Go not thither. Recall the revulsion. If thou goest there, into the continent which is pleasant and mild, even though thou mayest be reborn there, the dharma doth not prosper there. Enter not therein.

**15** If thou art to be born in the Southern Continent, thou wilt see grand and beautiful mansions. If thou art to enter therein, enter.

**16** If thou art to be born in the Western Continent, thou wilt see a lake adorned with mares. Go not thither.

**17** If thou art to be born in the Northern Continent, thou wilt see a lake adorned with cattle and trees. Although life endureth long there, the dharma doth not prosper. Enter not therein.

**18** O nobly-born, whatever visions come to thee, desire them not. Cling not to them; cling not to weakness. If thou clingest and if thou art weak, then thou wilt have to wander amidst the Six Lokas and suffer misery.

**19** The vital art lieth in the choosing of the womb-door. Pray earnestly to the Trinity. O nobly-born, even though thou desirest not to go onwards, and thou hast no power of thine own, willy-nilly thou must go.

**20** O nobly-born, there is a way of closing the womb-door: by meditating upon the Clear Light, or by meditating upon the tutelary deity, or by meditating upon Illusion.

**21** If, despite these instructions, the womb-door is not closed, and one is about to enter the womb, the teaching for the selection of a womb-door is as follows: listen well.

**22** Look upon the visions before thee. If choosing birth among men, choose a continent where the dharma prevaileth.

**23** O nobly-born, the vital art is in the selection. Pray that thou mayest be reborn where truth abideth, and where the dharma flourisheth, for the benefit of all sentient beings.

**24** The reading of this Great Doctrine of Liberation by Hearing shall bring liberation to all who hear it, even those of great sins and heavy karma. Therefore hold it well and read it in this manner.
