---
title: "Tibetan Book of the Dead - Chapter 2"
scripture: "tibetan-book-of-dead"
chapter: 2
---

**1** O nobly-born, if thou hast not recognized thine own true nature by now, from this time onward there will dawn upon thee the lights of the peaceful deities.

**2** On the first day, from the central Realm of the Spreading Forth of Seed, the Bhagavan Vairochana, white in colour, seated upon a lion-throne, bearing an eight-spoked wheel in his hand, embraced by the Mother of the Space of Heaven, will appear to thee.

**3** It is the aggregate of matter resolved into its primordial state which is the blue light. The Wisdom of the Dharma-Dhatu, blue in colour, shining, transparent, glorious, dazzling, from the heart of Vairochana as the Father-Mother, will shoot forth and strike against thee with a light so radiant that thou wilt scarcely be able to look at it.

**4** Along with it, a dull white light from the deva-loka will also come to shine upon thee simultaneously. Thereupon, through the power of bad karma, the glorious blue light of the Wisdom of the Dharma-Dhatu will produce in thee fear and terror, and thou wilt wish to flee from it. Thou wilt beget a fondness for the dull white light of the devas.

**5** At that moment, do not be frightened by the divine blue light which will appear shining, dazzling, and glorious; and be not overawed. That is the light of the Tathagata called the Light of the Wisdom of the Dharma-Dhatu.

**6** Be not fond of the dull white light of the devas. Be not attached to it; be not weak. If thou art attracted by it, thou wilt wander into the deva-loka and be caught in the whirl of the Six Lokas.

**7** On the second day, the purified form of the aggregate of water will shine as a white light. At that time, from the Eastern Realm of Pre-eminent Happiness, the Bhagavan Akshobhya as Vajra-Sattva, blue in colour, holding in his hand a five-pronged dorje, seated upon an elephant-throne, embraced by the Mother Mamaki, will appear to thee.

**8** The white light of the Mirror-like Wisdom, dazzlingly white, transparent and glorious, will come from the heart of Vajra-Sattva, the Father-Mother, and strike against thee with such dazzling brilliance that thou wilt scarcely be able to look upon it.

**9** Together with it, a dull smoke-coloured light from hell will also come to shine upon thee simultaneously. At that moment, through the power of anger, thou wilt be terrified at the dazzling white light and wilt wish to flee from it; thou wilt beget a fondness for the dull smoke-coloured light from hell.

**10** Act so at that moment as not to fear that bright, dazzling, transparent white light. Know it to be Wisdom. Place thine own mind therein in a state of resignation, in a state of non-action.

**11** On the third day, the purified form of the aggregate of feeling will shine as a yellow light. At that time from the Southern Realm of The Glorious, the Bhagavan Ratna-Sambhava, yellow in colour, bearing a jewel in his hand, seated upon a horse-throne, embraced by the Mother Sangyay-Chanma, will appear to thee.

**12** The yellow light of the Wisdom of Equality, gloriously yellow, adorned with orbs of rainbow light, transparent and beautiful, dazzling, will come from the heart of Ratna-Sambhava, the Father-Mother, and strike against thee.

**13** Simultaneously, together with the Wisdom Lights, the impure illusory lights of the Six Lokas will also come to shine. Do not be attracted towards any of them; but allow the mind to rest in the great equanimity.

**14** On the fourth day, the purified form of the aggregate of volition will shine as a red light. At that time, from the Western Realm of Happiness, the Bhagavan Amitabha, red in colour, bearing a lotus in his hand, seated upon a peacock-throne, embraced by the Mother Gokarmo, will appear to thee.

**15** The glorious red light of the Wisdom of Discriminating, dazzling red, adorned with orbs of rainbow light, glorious and dazzling, transparent and bright, will issue from the heart of Amitabha, the Father-Mother.

**16** O nobly-born, at that moment, through the power of passion, thou wilt be terrified by the dazzling red light, and wilt wish to flee. Thou wilt beget fondness for the dull red light of the pretas.

**17** At that moment, do not be afraid of the glorious, dazzling, transparent, bright red light. Recognizing it as Wisdom, keeping thine intellect in the state of resignation, thou wilt merge inseparably into it, and Buddhahood will be obtained.
