## Theravada Perspective

This chapter of the Tibetan Book of the Dead presents the moment of death and the arising of the clear light (*'od gsal*) — the fundamental luminosity of mind that appears when the gross levels of consciousness dissolve, offering the first and most direct opportunity for liberation. From a Theravada standpoint, this material is examined as a Tibetan Vajrayana elaboration of themes that have parallels — though substantial differences — in the Pali Canon's treatment of death, consciousness, and rebirth.

The Pali Abhidhamma describes the death process in terms of the dissolution of the *cuticitta* (death consciousness) and the arising of the *patisandhicitta* (relinking consciousness), a model that is significantly simpler than the Bardo Thodol's elaborate visionary sequence. Theravada teaching agrees that the quality of consciousness at the moment of death influences the subsequent rebirth, but it does not describe the kind of extended post-mortem journey through visionary states that the Bardo Thodol envisions.

Theravada practitioners may nonetheless find value in the chapter's underlying emphasis on mindful awareness in the face of death, which resonates with the Pali tradition's own teaching on *maranasati* (mindfulness of death) as a powerful stimulus to diligent practice.
