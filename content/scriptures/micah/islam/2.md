## Condemnation of Oppression

Islamic interpretation of Micah 2 finds deep resonance with the Quran's repeated condemnation of economic injustice. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Whoever seizes a hand-span of land unjustly, on the Day of Resurrection he will be encircled by it from the seven earths" (Bukhari). Micah's denunciation of land theft and the exploitation of the poor aligns perfectly with Islamic principles of economic justice and the prohibition of *zulm* (oppression).

## False Prophets and False Scholars

The false prophets who preach comfort for pay echo the Islamic concern about scholars who distort religion for worldly gain. The Quran warns: "Those who conceal what Allah has revealed of the Book and exchange it for a small price — they consume nothing into their bellies except fire" (2:174). Micah's critique reinforces the Islamic emphasis on the integrity of religious knowledge.

## The Promise of Gathering

The vision of God gathering the remnant has eschatological resonance in Islamic thought, connecting to the Quranic theme of God's ultimate gathering of all peoples for judgment and the restoration of justice.
