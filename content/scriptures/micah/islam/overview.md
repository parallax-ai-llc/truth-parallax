## Overview

While the Book of Micah is not part of the Quran, its themes resonate powerfully with Islamic teaching. Micah's denunciation of corrupt leaders who exploit the poor echoes the Quran's repeated warnings to those who cheat in scales, devour orphans' property, and pervert justice. The prophetic role as defined by Micah — speaking God's truth to powerful elites regardless of personal cost — aligns closely with the Islamic understanding of the prophets as warners (*nadhir*) and bearers of glad tidings (*bashir*).

## Key Interpretive Approaches

Muslim scholars engage with Micah through the *Isra'iliyyat* framework, accepting its moral teachings insofar as they are consistent with Quranic revelation. The book's emphasis on justice, mercy, and humility before God is seen as confirming the universal prophetic message that the Quran presents as the common heritage of all God's messengers.

## Theological Emphases

- **Social Justice**: God demands justice for the poor and will judge those who exploit them
- **Accountability of Leaders**: Rulers, judges, and religious authorities will answer to God for their stewardship
- **Prophetic Witness**: The prophet's duty is to speak truth regardless of opposition
- **Divine Mercy and Judgment**: God is both merciful and just — mercy is offered to those who repent, judgment falls on the impenitent
