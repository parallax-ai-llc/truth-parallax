## The Ruler from Humble Origins

Islamic interpretation of Micah 5 notes the chapter's emphasis on God's pattern of choosing the humble and insignificant as instruments of his purposes. This resonates with the Quranic principle that God exalts whom he wills and that true greatness comes not from worldly power but from submission to God. The Prophet Muhammad himself came from Mecca, which, while a sacred city, was peripheral to the great empires of his day — a pattern consistent with Micah's vision of a ruler from the small town of Bethlehem.

## The Eternal Origins

Muslim scholars, while not accepting the Christian interpretation of Micah 5:2 as a reference to divine pre-existence, acknowledge the prophetic significance of the passage within the Israelite tradition. Some Islamic commentators have read the prophecy in connection with Jesus (*Isa*), whom Islam honors as a prophet born in Bethlehem, while others apply it more broadly to God's pattern of raising leaders from unexpected places.

## Peace as God's Gift

The vision of universal peace (5:4-5) aligns with the Islamic ideal of *salam* — peace that comes from God and is established through righteous governance. The Quran describes the prophets as establishing justice and peace in the earth, and Micah's vision of a shepherd-ruler governing in God's strength resonates with the Islamic concept of the just ruler (*imam 'adil*).
