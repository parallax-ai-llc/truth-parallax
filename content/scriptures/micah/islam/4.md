## The Vision of Universal Worship

Islamic interpretation of Micah 4 finds deep resonance with the Quranic vision of a world united in worship of the one God. The image of all nations streaming to the mountain of the LORD to learn his ways echoes the Islamic understanding of *din al-fitrah* — the natural religion of monotheism to which all humanity is called. The Quran envisions a similar gathering: "On the Day We will gather from every nation a company of those who deny Our signs" (27:83) — and the righteous will be united in worship.

## Peace as Divine Gift

The vision of swords beaten into plowshares aligns with the Islamic understanding of *salam* (peace) as both a divine name and a divine gift. True peace comes not from human treaties alone but from submission to God's will. The Quran promises: "Allah invites to the Home of Peace and guides whom He wills to a straight path" (10:25).

## The Gathering of the Weak

God's promise to make the lame a remnant resonates with the Islamic emphasis on God's special care for the weak and marginalized. The Prophet Muhammad taught that believers should be sought among the poor and the humble, for they are closest to God.
