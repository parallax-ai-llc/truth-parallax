## God's Judgment on Idolatry

Islamic interpretation of Micah 1 finds strong resonance with the Quranic principle that idolatry (*shirk*) is the one sin that God does not forgive if maintained until death (Quran 4:48). Samaria's destruction because of its worship of idols and images aligns with the Quran's repeated accounts of nations destroyed for associating partners with God. The theophanic language — God descending, mountains melting — echoes Quranic descriptions of divine power and judgment.

## Warning to All Nations

Muslim scholars read the chapter through the *Isra'iliyyat* framework as a prophetic warning consistent with the universal message of all God's messengers: *tawhid* (monotheism) is the fundamental requirement, and its violation brings consequences that no military power or political alliance can avert. The fall of Samaria exemplifies the Quranic principle that God gives nations time to repent, but persistent idolatry leads to destruction.

## The Prophet's Role

Micah's role as warner aligns with the Islamic understanding of the prophetic office. Like the Quranic *nadhir* (warner), Micah announces consequences not out of vindictiveness but out of a divine commission to call people back to the straight path.
