## The Mountain Beyond the Cosmos

Gnostic interpretation reads the mountain of the LORD (4:1) not as a physical location but as a symbol of the transcendent realm — the Pleroma — which is "above all mountains," higher than any point within the Demiurge's material cosmos. The nations streaming to this mountain represent the divine sparks awakening to their true origin and ascending beyond the material world to their home in the fullness of the divine.

## Peace Beyond the Material Order

The vision of swords beaten into plowshares is read as a description of the state beyond the material cosmos, where the conflict inherent in the Demiurge's creation ceases entirely. True peace is impossible within the material world, which is structured by opposition and conflict. The peace Micah envisions can only be realized in the spiritual realm, where the archontic powers hold no sway.

## The True Torah

"Torah shall go forth from Zion" (4:2) is reinterpreted as the dissemination of *gnosis* — the true spiritual teaching that liberates the soul. The "Torah" of the material world, with its rules and regulations, is the Demiurge's law; the true Torah is the knowledge of the soul's divine origin and destiny.
