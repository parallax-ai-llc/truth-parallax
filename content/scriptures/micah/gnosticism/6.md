## The Lawsuit of the False God?

Gnostic interpretation of Micah 6 reads the covenant lawsuit with characteristic ambivalence. On one level, the demand for justice, mercy, and humility aligns with the Gnostic emphasis on spiritual transformation over material ritual. On another level, Gnostic readers ask: who is speaking? If the God of the covenant lawsuit is the Demiurge, then his complaints against Israel are the complaints of a possessive ruler angry that his subjects have not performed adequately. The question "What have I done to you?" could be read as the Demiurge's self-justification.

## "Walk Humbly with Your God"

Gnostic readers reinterpret this command as an invitation to walk with the *true* God — beyond the Demiurge, beyond ritual, beyond the material order. To "walk humbly" is to recognize one's status as a divine spark temporarily imprisoned in matter, and to seek the path of return through *gnosis* rather than through the sacrificial system the Demiurge demands.

## The Rejection of Sacrifice

The passage's dismissal of burnt offerings and firstborn sacrifice (6:6-7) is read approvingly: the Demiurge's demand for sacrifice is exposed as worthless. But the Gnostic takes this further — not only is sacrifice unnecessary, but the entire system of material worship is a distraction from the true spiritual path.
