## Overview

Gnostic readers find in the Book of Micah a prophetic critique of the material world's corrupt power structures, which they identify with the archons — the rulers of the lower cosmos. Micah's denunciation of judges who take bribes, prophets who prophesy for money, and rulers who build Zion with bloodshed resonates with the Gnostic conviction that the institutions of the material world are fundamentally corrupt, serving the Demiurge rather than the true God.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

The Gnostic reading of Micah focuses on the contrast between institutional religion — represented by the corrupt priests and false prophets — and the genuine knowledge of God that Micah represents. Micah 6:8's call to "walk humbly with your God" is reinterpreted as an invitation to inward, personal communion with the divine, bypassing the failed structures of organized worship. The remnant theology of chapters 4-5 is read as the preservation of a small group of spiritual knowers within a fallen world.

## Theological Emphases

- **Archontic Corruption**: The corrupt leaders Micah condemns represent the archons who rule the material world
- **Gnosis over Institution**: True religion is inner knowledge, not institutional observance
- **The Spiritual Remnant**: The faithful few who survive judgment represent the Gnostic elect
- **Liberation from False Authority**: Micah's critique anticipates the Gnostic rejection of worldly and religious power structures
