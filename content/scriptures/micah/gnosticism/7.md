## Lament Over the Fallen World

Gnostic interpretation of Micah 7 reads the prophet's lament as a description of the Demiurge's fallen creation. The faithlessness, betrayal, and corruption that Micah describes are not accidental features of human society but the inevitable characteristics of a cosmos governed by the archons. The material world is structured for conflict and betrayal — "a man's enemies are the members of his own household" (7:6) — because the Demiurge's creation lacks the harmony of the true divine realm.

## Turning to the True God

The prophet's declaration "I will look to the LORD" (7:7) is reinterpreted as a turning from the Demiurge to the true God beyond the material cosmos. This is the moment of *gnosis* — the recognition that the God of this world is not the ultimate God, and that liberation requires looking beyond the visible order to the hidden divine reality.

## "Who Is a God Like You?"

The Gnostic reads this question as a genuine inquiry: who is the true God, the one unlike any god known in the material world? The God who "pardons iniquity" and "casts sins into the sea" is not the Demiurge, who keeps accounts and demands payment, but the God of mercy beyond the archontic order — the God who liberates rather than imprisons.
