## The Vision of Cosmic Peace

Orthodox interpretation of Micah 4 reads the vision of universal peace not as a political program but as an eschatological icon — a glimpse of the transfigured cosmos in which all creation is restored to harmony under God's rule. The mountain of the LORD represents the Kingdom of God, which the Church anticipates in its liturgical life. Every Divine Liturgy is a participation in the reality that Micah envisions: the gathering of all peoples before God's presence in worship and peace.

## Nations Learning God's Ways

The vision of nations coming to learn God's Torah (4:2) resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the Church's missionary vocation: not cultural imperialism but the invitation to all peoples to enter the divine life. The nations do not lose their identity but find their true identity in relation to God.

## The Lame Made Strong

God's transformation of the lame and the outcast into a remnant (4:6-7) is read through the lens of *theosis*: God's power is made perfect in weakness, and salvation involves the transformation of human frailty into divine glory.
