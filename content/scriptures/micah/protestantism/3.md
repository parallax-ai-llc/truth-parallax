## The Indictment of Israel's Leaders

Protestant interpretation of Micah 3 reads the chapter as one of the most devastating critiques of corrupt leadership in Scripture. Micah indicts three groups: rulers who "tear the skin from my people" (3:2-3), prophets who "lead my people astray" (3:5), and priests who "teach for a price" (3:11). The imagery is deliberately gruesome — leaders who should shepherd the people instead devour them like butchers preparing meat.

## Micah's Prophetic Courage (3:8)

"But as for me, I am filled with power, with the Spirit of the LORD, and with justice and might, to declare to Jacob his transgression." This verse is a key text for Protestant understanding of prophetic ministry: true prophecy is empowered by the Spirit, distinguished by courage, and committed to truth regardless of consequences.

## "Zion Shall Be Plowed as a Field" (3:12)

This prophecy — that Jerusalem's corruption will result in its total destruction — is one of the most remarkable in the Hebrew Bible. It was remembered a century later when Jeremiah was threatened with death for a similar prophecy (Jeremiah 26:18), and Micah's words saved Jeremiah's life. Protestant commentators see this as evidence that Scripture preserves a tradition of prophetic dissent within Israel.
