## The Ruler from Bethlehem

Protestant interpretation of Micah 5 centers on the Bethlehem prophecy (5:2), one of the most important messianic predictions in the Old Testament. The verse is cited in Matthew 2:6 as the basis for the scribes' answer to Herod about where the Messiah would be born. Protestant commentators emphasize the theological significance of Bethlehem — a small, insignificant town chosen over mighty Jerusalem — as a demonstration of God's pattern of working through the humble and the unlikely.

## "Whose Coming Forth Is from of Old" (5:2)

The phrase "whose coming forth is from of old, from ancient days" is read by many Protestant interpreters as an affirmation of the pre-existence of Christ. The ruler from Bethlehem is not merely a future human king but one whose origins reach back to eternity, pointing to the divine nature of the Messiah.

## The Shepherd King (5:4)

"He shall stand and shepherd his flock in the strength of the LORD." Protestant interpretation connects this to Jesus' self-identification as the Good Shepherd (John 10). The messianic ruler governs not by coercion but by pastoral care, and his peace extends "to the ends of the earth" (5:4) — a vision fulfilled in the global spread of the gospel.
