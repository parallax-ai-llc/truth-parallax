## The Accountability of Leaders

Catholic interpretation of Micah 3 applies the chapter's stern indictment to all who hold authority in the Church and in society. The chapter's three-fold critique — of rulers, prophets, and priests — mirrors the Church's understanding of the three-fold office (prophet, priest, king) that belongs to Christ and is shared by all the baptized. When leaders abuse their authority, they betray the office entrusted to them. The chapter serves as a perpetual reminder that authority in the Church is for service, not exploitation.

## The Spirit-Filled Prophet

Micah's declaration of Spirit-empowerment (3:8) is read as a model of authentic prophetic ministry. Catholic theology distinguishes between the charism of prophecy and the institutional office — both are necessary, and when institutional leaders fail, the prophetic charism arises to call them to account. Micah exemplifies this dynamic.

## The Destruction of Zion

The prophecy that Zion will be "plowed as a field" (3:12) was understood by the Fathers as a warning that no institution is guaranteed divine protection if it betrays its vocation. The temple's destruction does not negate God's promises but reveals that God's purposes are not bound to any particular structure.
