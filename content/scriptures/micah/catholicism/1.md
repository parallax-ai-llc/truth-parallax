## The Lord Comes Down

Catholic interpretation of Micah 1 begins with the theophanic vision of God descending from his holy temple to tread upon the high places of the earth. The Church Fathers read this divine descent as a prefiguration of the Incarnation — the God who comes down to judge is the same God who will come down in Christ to save. Jerome noted that the judgment on Samaria's idols and the melting of mountains reveal the incompatibility between the living God and all human substitutes for him.

## Judgment on Idolatry

The condemnation of Samaria's images and "wages of a prostitute" (1:7) is read in Catholic moral theology as a warning against all forms of idolatry — the substitution of created goods for the Creator. The Catechism's teaching on the first commandment echoes Micah's concern: the human heart is prone to making idols of wealth, power, and pleasure.

## The Lament for Judah

The wordplay lament over Judah's cities (1:10-16) expresses the prophet's genuine grief at the suffering his people will endure. Catholic interpretation emphasizes that prophetic judgment is not cold denunciation but grieving love — the prophet mourns even as he speaks God's verdict.
