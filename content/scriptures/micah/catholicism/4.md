## The Mountain of the LORD

Catholic interpretation reads the vision of all nations streaming to the mountain of the LORD (4:1-3) as a prophecy fulfilled in the universal Church. The Second Vatican Council's *Lumen Gentium* echoes this imagery in describing the Church as a sign and instrument of unity among all peoples. The mountain represents the Church's visible presence in history, drawing all nations toward the God of Israel through the gospel.

## The Catholic Peace Tradition

The vision of swords beaten into plowshares has been foundational for Catholic peace teaching, from Augustine's *just war* theory (which takes the vision as a future ideal) to the modern popes' calls for disarmament. Pope John XXIII's *Pacem in Terris* and subsequent social encyclicals draw on this prophetic hope as a mandate for the Church's engagement in peacemaking.

## Zion in Labor (4:9-13)

The image of Zion as a woman in labor — suffering the pain of exile but destined for deliverance — is read in Catholic Marian interpretation as a type of the Church and of Mary. The Church, like Zion, endures suffering in history but trusts in God's promise of ultimate vindication.
