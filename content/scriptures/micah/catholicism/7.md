## From Darkness to Light

Catholic interpretation of Micah 7 reads the chapter as a journey from the prophet's lament over human sinfulness to a doxology of divine mercy. The movement mirrors the structure of the penitential liturgy: confession of sin, declaration of trust, and celebration of forgiveness. The chapter provides the theological grammar for the sacrament of reconciliation — the conviction that no human sin is beyond the reach of God's mercy.

## The Magnificat Connection

Mary's *Magnificat* (Luke 1:46-55) draws directly on Micah 7:20: "He has helped his servant Israel, in remembrance of his mercy, as he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and to his offspring forever." Catholic interpretation sees in this connection a profound theological continuity: the mercy Micah celebrated is the same mercy that finds its ultimate expression in the Incarnation. Mary is the faithful remnant who trusts in God's promises.

## "Who Is a God Like You?"

The concluding doxology (7:18-20) is treasured in Catholic theology as a celebration of God's essential nature: mercy. God "delights in steadfast love" (7:18) — mercy is not something God does reluctantly but something that defines who God is. This text underpins the Catholic confidence in divine mercy that runs from Augustine through Thomas Aquinas to Pope Francis.
