## The Tashlich Connection

Jewish interpretation of Micah 7 is inseparable from the liturgical practice of *Tashlich*, the Rosh Hashanah ceremony in which worshippers symbolically cast their sins into a body of flowing water. The ritual is based on Micah 7:19: "You will cast all their sins into the depths of the sea." This verse, recited at the water's edge, embodies the Jewish conviction that God's mercy is as deep as the sea and that genuine repentance results in the complete removal of sin.

## The Abrahamic Covenant (7:20)

The book closes by grounding God's mercy in his covenant with Abraham: "You will show faithfulness to Jacob and steadfast love to Abraham, as you have sworn to our fathers from the days of old." Jewish interpretation emphasizes that God's mercy is not arbitrary but covenantal — God forgives because he made promises, and God keeps his promises. This text is recited in the *Selichot* (penitential prayers) and in the Yom Kippur liturgy.

## "Who Is a God Like You?" (7:18)

This rhetorical question, a play on the prophet's own name (*Mi-khah* = "Who is like God?"), expresses the incomparability of God. No other god forgives like YHWH; no other god delights in mercy. The verse is a cornerstone of Jewish theological reflection on the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy.
