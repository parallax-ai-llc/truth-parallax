## The Davidic Messiah from Bethlehem

Jewish interpretation of Micah 5 reads the Bethlehem prophecy as a prediction of the future Davidic Messiah. Bethlehem is significant not in itself but as David's city — the prophecy announces that the future redeemer will come from David's line and his ancestral town. The Targum Jonathan translates: "From you shall come forth before me the Messiah, to exercise dominion over Israel." This remains a central text in Jewish messianic expectation.

## "From of Old, from Ancient Days"

Jewish commentators debate the meaning of this phrase. Rashi and Radak read it as referring to the ancient Davidic lineage — the Messiah's ancestry stretches back through David to antiquity. The Midrash and some kabbalistic interpretations see the Messiah's "name" as one of the things created before the world, giving the phrase a more cosmic significance.

## The Messianic Peace

The vision of the Messiah shepherding his people in peace and security (5:4-5) is understood in Jewish tradition as awaiting future fulfillment. The Messiah will bring not only spiritual renewal but concrete political peace — the end of foreign domination and the ingathering of the exiles. The Talmud and liturgy hold this hope as a living expectation.
