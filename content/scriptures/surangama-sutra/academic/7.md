The Shurangama Mantra has been the subject of academic study from multiple angles: its linguistic structure (combining Sanskrit, transliterated Chinese, and possibly non-standard phonetic elements), its ritual function in Chinese Buddhist monasticism, and its role in the authenticity debate surrounding the sutra. Scholars such as Robert Buswell have examined how dharani practice bridges the boundary between 'exoteric' and 'esoteric' Buddhism, and the Shurangama Mantra provides a key case study in this investigation.
