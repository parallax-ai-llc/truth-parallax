The analysis of the six elements represents a synthesis of multiple Buddhist philosophical traditions. Scholars have traced the sutra's treatment of elements to both the Sarvastivada Abhidharma's analysis of material phenomena and the Madhyamaka critique of inherent existence. The integration of these analytical traditions within a tathagatagarbha framework represents a distinctive philosophical achievement that has been the subject of detailed academic study, particularly by scholars working on the intersection of Yogacara and tathagatagarbha thought.
