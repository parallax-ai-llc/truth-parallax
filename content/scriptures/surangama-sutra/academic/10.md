The final skandha-demons, addressing the subtlest forms of meditative self-deception, have been studied as a contribution to the Buddhist understanding of how liberation can be obstructed even at the highest levels of practice. Scholars have analyzed the philosophical views attributed to these demon states — eternalism, nihilism, and various forms of partial insight — in relation to the heresiological traditions of Indian and Chinese Buddhism. The chapter's comprehensive warning against all forms of attachment to meditative experience has been compared with similar warnings in Christian mystical theology and Sufi literature.
