The seven locations of mind debate has attracted philosophical attention for its systematic method of elimination, which scholars have compared to both Indian dialectical traditions (the Nyaya school's approach to establishing the nature of the self) and Western philosophical methods. The chapter's conclusion — that the mind cannot be located in any spatial position — has been analyzed in relation to the Yogacara teaching of consciousness as a process rather than an entity, and in relation to contemporary philosophy of mind debates about the 'hard problem' of consciousness and the failure to locate subjective experience in the brain.
