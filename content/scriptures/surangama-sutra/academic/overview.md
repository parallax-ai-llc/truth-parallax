## Overview

The Surangama Sutra has been the subject of intense academic debate regarding its origins and authenticity. Unlike most Mahayana sutras whose Indian provenance is generally accepted, the Surangama Sutra has been questioned since at least the Song dynasty, with some scholars arguing it was composed in China rather than translated from Sanskrit.

## Authenticity Debate

No Sanskrit original has been found, and the sutra's literary style, philosophical synthesis, and apparent familiarity with Chinese Buddhist controversies have led scholars such as Lü Cheng to argue for Chinese composition. Others, including Ronald Epstein, have defended its Indian origins based on internal doctrinal evidence and the historical record of its translation by Paramiti in 705 CE.

## Philosophical Analysis

Regardless of its origins, the sutra represents a sophisticated synthesis of multiple Buddhist philosophical streams: Yogacara consciousness analysis, tathagatagarbha thought, Madhyamaka dialectics, and esoteric Buddhist practice. Academic study has illuminated how the text integrates these diverse strands into a coherent soteriology centered on the investigation and purification of mind.
