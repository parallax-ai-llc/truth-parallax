The first set of skandha-demons has attracted significant academic attention as a unique contribution to Buddhist phenomenology of contemplative experience. Scholars have noted that no other Buddhist text provides such a detailed, systematic taxonomy of the deceptive experiences that arise in meditation. Comparative studies have examined parallels with the Theravada Visuddhimagga's discussion of vipassanupakkilesa, the Tibetan treatment of nyams, and Christian discussions of spiritual consolations and desolations.
