The cosmogonic account in this chapter has been studied in relation to other Buddhist and Indian cosmological texts. Scholars note its distinctive combination of tathagatagarbha cosmogony — where the world arises from the deluded activity of the originally pure mind — with elements drawn from the twelve links of dependent origination. This synthesis has been compared with the cosmogonic accounts in the Lankavatara Sutra and the Awakening of Faith, texts that share the Surangama's concern with explaining the origin of delusion within an originally enlightened mind.
