The 25 Means of Perfect Penetration have attracted academic attention as a remarkable compilation of contemplative methods drawn from diverse Buddhist traditions. Scholars have identified sources in both Hinayana and Mahayana meditation literature, suggesting that the chapter represents a deliberate effort to create a comprehensive taxonomy of Buddhist practice. The literary structure — 25 practitioners each narrating their personal path to realization — has been analyzed as a rhetorical strategy for establishing the Surangama Sutra's authority as an encyclopedic guide to Buddhist practice.
