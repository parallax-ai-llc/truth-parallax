The skandha-demons of form and feeling parallel warnings about spiritual deception across traditions. The Christian tradition of 'discernment of spirits' (diakrisis), developed by Desert Fathers and systematized by Ignatius of Loyola, addresses similar concerns about mistaking consolations for genuine spiritual progress. The Sufi concept of spiritual intoxication (sukr) versus sobriety (sahw) addresses the danger of attachment to ecstatic states. The Surangama's detailed taxonomy provides Buddhist meditators with a level of specificity that complements these cross-traditional warnings.
