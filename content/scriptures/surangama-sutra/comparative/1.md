The investigation of where the mind resides invites comparison with the Western philosophical tradition of inquiry into the nature of consciousness. Descartes' cogito located the mind in a thinking substance; Hume's bundle theory dissolved the self into a stream of perceptions; and contemporary neuroscience seeks consciousness in neural correlates. The Surangama's systematic elimination of all spatial locations for the mind anticipates aspects of each approach while arriving at a distinctively Buddhist conclusion: the true mind is not located anywhere because it is the ground of all experience rather than an object within experience.
