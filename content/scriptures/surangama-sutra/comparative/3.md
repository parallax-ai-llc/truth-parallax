The analysis of the six elements can be compared with the pre-Socratic philosophers' search for the fundamental constituents of reality and Aristotle's theory of the four elements. While Greek philosophy sought to identify the basic building blocks of the physical world, the Surangama demonstrates that the elements cannot be established as independently existing realities. This anti-foundationalist conclusion anticipates aspects of process philosophy (Whitehead) and challenges the substance ontology that dominated Western thought from Aristotle through the modern period.
