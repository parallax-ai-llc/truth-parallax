The final skandha-demons — subtle attachments to philosophical views and refined states of consciousness — find parallels in the apophatic traditions' warnings against idolatry of concepts. Meister Eckhart's prayer to 'God to rid me of God' expresses the recognition that even the most refined theological concept can become an obstacle. The Cloud of Unknowing counsels abandoning all knowledge in approaching the divine. The Surangama's warning that attachment to the consciousness aggregate is the final obstacle resonates with these traditions' insistence that the ultimate cannot be grasped by any act of consciousness, no matter how refined.
