The Shurangama Mantra and dharani practice can be compared with sacred utterance traditions across religions: the Jewish recitation of Torah, Islamic recitation of Quran, Hindu japa of sacred mantras, and the Christian prayer of the name of Jesus. All these traditions hold that sacred sound possesses transformative power beyond its semantic content. The Surangama's integration of mantra with ethical precepts parallels the widespread requirement across traditions that sacred practices be grounded in moral purification.
