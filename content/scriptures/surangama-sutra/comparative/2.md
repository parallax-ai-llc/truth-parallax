The investigation of seeing nature parallels the phenomenological inquiries of Husserl, Merleau-Ponty, and the Advaita Vedanta tradition. The distinction between the act of seeing and the objects seen resonates with Husserl's distinction between noesis and noema, while the sutra's conclusion that the seeing nature is unchanging parallels the Advaita concept of the witness consciousness (saksin). However, the Buddhist refusal to reify this awareness as an eternal self (atman) distinguishes the Surangama's position from both Western substance metaphysics and Hindu Vedanta.
