The cosmogonic account of world formation through the stirring of ignorance within the pure mind invites comparison with creation narratives across traditions. Unlike the Genesis account of creation by divine command, the Surangama presents a self-originating process driven by delusion. This can be compared with the Neoplatonic emanation from the One, the Kabbalistic tzimtzum (divine contraction), and the Daoist emergence of the ten thousand things from the Dao. Each tradition must account for how multiplicity arises from unity, and the Surangama's answer — through a failure of self-recognition — is distinctive.
