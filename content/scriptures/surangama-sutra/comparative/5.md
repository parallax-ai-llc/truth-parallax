The 25 Means of Perfect Penetration can be compared with the diversity of contemplative practices across world religions: Christian lectio divina, Sufi whirling, Hindu yoga, Jewish Kabbalistic meditation, and Daoist internal alchemy. All these traditions recognize that different practitioners require different entry points into contemplative experience. The Surangama's explicit acknowledgment that any sense faculty or element can serve as a gateway to realization is a remarkably inclusive vision that finds parallels in the Bhagavad Gita's teaching of multiple yogas (karma, jnana, bhakti) and the Sufi concept of multiple stations on the path.
