The systematic mapping of the spiritual path in this chapter can be compared with stage models across contemplative traditions: Teresa of Avila's seven mansions, the Sufi stations and states (maqamat and ahwal), the Kabbalistic sefirot, and the Yogic stages described in Patanjali's Yoga Sutras. All these traditions recognize a progressive deepening of spiritual realization through identifiable stages. The Surangama's 57-level system is among the most elaborate such maps in world religious literature.
