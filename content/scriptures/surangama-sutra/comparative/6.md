Avalokitesvara's method of turning hearing inward parallels contemplative listening practices across traditions. The Quaker practice of 'listening for the still small voice,' the Sufi practice of sama (spiritual listening), and the Hindu practice of nada yoga (meditation on inner sound) all employ the faculty of hearing as a gateway to transcendent experience. The Surangama's detailed phenomenological description of the progressive dissolution of the subject-object structure of hearing provides an unusually precise account that enriches comparative understanding of auditory contemplative practices.
