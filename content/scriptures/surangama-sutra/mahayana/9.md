The first 25 of the 50 skandha-demons arise from the form aggregate (rupa-skandha) and the feeling aggregate (vedana-skandha). These include experiences of extraordinary light, physical transformation, unusual sounds, visions of Buddhas and bodhisattvas, and states of extreme bliss or equanimity that arise during deep samadhi. The Buddha warns that each of these experiences, while possibly indicating progress, becomes a demonic obstacle if the practitioner clings to it, identifies with it, or claims it as attainment. Mahayana teachers have used this chapter to counsel meditators that even genuine spiritual experiences must be released and not grasped.
