This chapter maps the complete bodhisattva path from initial aspiration to final Buddhahood, describing the three gradual stages of preparation and the 57 levels of attainment: the ten faiths, ten dwellings, ten practices, ten dedications, ten grounds, equal enlightenment, and marvelous enlightenment. This systematic presentation reflects the Mahayana understanding that the path to Buddhahood is vast and gradual, requiring countless lifetimes of practice. Each level is characterized by specific realizations and specific abandonments of defilement. Chinese Buddhist educators have used this chapter as a comprehensive map of the entire path, providing practitioners with clear landmarks for assessing their progress.
