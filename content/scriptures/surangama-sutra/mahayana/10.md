The final 25 skandha-demons arise from the perception aggregate (samjna-skandha), the formation aggregate (samskara-skandha), and the consciousness aggregate (vijnana-skandha). These represent increasingly subtle forms of delusion, including false insights into the nature of reality, distorted philosophical views, and subtle identification with refined states of consciousness. The chapter concludes with the warning that attachment to the consciousness aggregate is the most dangerous of all, because it is the most difficult to recognize. The practitioner who sees through even these final demons realizes the complete, perfect enlightenment of Buddhahood. This chapter serves as the sutra's final practical teaching, ensuring that practitioners have a detailed guide to the obstacles they will encounter at every stage of the path.
