The analysis of the six elements — earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness — demonstrates that none of these can be identified as the true mind, yet none are separate from it. Each element is shown to be neither internal nor external, neither arising nor ceasing. This teaching reflects the Mahayana understanding of the inseparability of samsara and nirvana: the very elements that constitute the conditioned world are, when properly understood, expressions of the unconditioned tathagatagarbha. Chinese Buddhist commentators read this chapter as establishing the philosophical foundation for the Tiantai teaching of the mutual interpenetration of all phenomena.
