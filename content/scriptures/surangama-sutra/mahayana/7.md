The Shurangama Mantra, presented in this chapter, is one of the longest and most revered mantras in East Asian Buddhism. Recited daily in Chinese Buddhist morning services, it is believed to purify negative karma, protect against demonic interference, and support the practitioner's progress on the path. The chapter also establishes the four clear and pure precepts (si zhong qing jing ming hui) — against killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech — as the indispensable foundation for all practice. Mahayana commentators emphasize that mantra and precepts work together: the mantra's power is only effective for those who maintain pure ethical conduct.
