The account of world formation in this chapter traces the origin of the universe to the initial stirring of ignorance within the originally pure mind. This cosmogony reflects the tathagatagarbha teaching that the world arises not from external causes but from the fundamental delusion that obscures the mind's true nature. The twelve categories of sentient beings are shown to arise through different combinations of this basic confusion. Mahayana commentators emphasize that understanding world formation in this way is not merely theoretical but transformative: seeing that the world arises from mind leads to the recognition that purifying the mind purifies the world.
