The seven locations of mind represent the sutra's foundational investigation, in which the Buddha guides Ananda through an exhaustive search for where the mind resides. Each proposed location — inside the body, outside, hidden in the sense organs, in darkness when eyes are closed, at the point of contact between sense and object, in the space between, and in no fixed place — is systematically refuted. This analysis establishes the Mahayana teaching that the true mind (zhen xin) transcends all spatial location. The true mind is not found within conditioned phenomena because it is the luminous ground from which all phenomena arise. Chinese commentators such as Hanshan Deqing emphasized that this investigation is not mere philosophy but a practice of directly realizing the mind's nature.
