The 25 Means of Perfect Penetration represent one of the sutra's most celebrated sections, in which 25 great beings each describe how they attained realization through a particular sense faculty, element, or aspect of consciousness. This chapter demonstrates the Mahayana principle that any phenomenon can serve as the gateway to awakening. The 25 methods include meditation on the six sense objects, the six sense faculties, the six consciousnesses, and the seven elements. The Buddha then asks Manjushri to evaluate these methods and recommend the one most suited for beings in this world — a request that leads to the selection of Avalokitesvara's method in Chapter 6.
