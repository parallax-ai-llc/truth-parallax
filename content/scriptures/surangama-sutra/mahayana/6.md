Avalokitesvara's method of perfect penetration through hearing is declared by Manjushri to be the supreme practice for beings in this world. By turning the hearing faculty inward — away from external sounds and toward the source of hearing itself — the practitioner progressively transcends the dualities of sound and silence, arising and ceasing, and ultimately realizes the unconditioned awareness that is the true mind. This method became enormously influential in Chinese Buddhism, forming the basis of the Chan practice of 'hearing the self-nature' and the devotional practice of calling upon Avalokitesvara's name. The chapter's detailed description of the stages of this practice provides a practical meditation manual.
