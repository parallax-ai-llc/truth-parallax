The investigation of seeing nature deepens the inquiry begun in Chapter 1. The Buddha demonstrates to Ananda that while objects of seeing change — light and darkness, near and far — the capacity for seeing itself does not change. This teaching points to the distinction between conditioned consciousness and the unconditioned awareness that underlies it. In Mahayana understanding, this unchanging seeing nature is identified with the tathagatagarbha — the womb of Buddhahood present in all beings. The chapter establishes that deluded beings mistake the changing objects of awareness for awareness itself, and that liberation comes through recognizing the true seeing nature that has never been born and will never die.
