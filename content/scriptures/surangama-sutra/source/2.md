---
title: "Surangama Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "surangama-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Buddha said to Ananda: "You and I are of the same clan. When you first gave rise to the mind of love for the Dharma, what excellent characteristic did you see that caused you to suddenly abandon the deep affection of the worldly life?"

**2** Ananda said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, I saw the thirty-two excellent characteristics of the Tathagata, which were supremely wonderful, incomparable, with a subtle, shining, pure radiance like crystal."

**3** "Since then I have constantly reflected and admired this, and my heart has longed for it. That is why I followed the Buddha and left the home life."

**4** The Buddha said: "Ananda, all beings from beginningless time have had all kinds of inversions. Their natures are like threads of a tangled vine."

**5** "Those who cultivate the Way do not obtain the true result because they do not understand the two fundamental roots. They cultivate in confusion, turning in circles for many kalpas without achieving the Way."

**6** "What are the two fundamentals? First, the root of beginningless birth and death, which is the mind of consciousness that you and all beings now make use of, taking it to be your own nature."

**7** "Second, the beginningless bodhi nirvana, the original bright substance of your nature, which can give rise to all conditions and which you now lose sight of."

**8** "Because you lose sight of this original brightness and use the false consciousness instead, you therefore undergo transmigration."

**9** "The mind with which you now listen to the Dharma — where is it? Point to the mind that listens."

**10** Ananda was silent, unable to answer. The Buddha said: "Ananda, this is precisely the nature of your error. You take the reflective awareness of conditions as your own mind, when in truth it is not your real mind."
