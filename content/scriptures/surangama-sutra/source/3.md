---
title: "Surangama Sutra - Chapter 3"
scripture: "surangama-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Ananda said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, if this aware and knowing mind is not my true mind, then am I without a mind?"

**2** The Buddha said: "Ananda, you have merely been following the false conditions of the objective world. Since time without beginning, you have mistaken this for your own mind."

**3** "It is like a man who sees his finger pointing at the moon, and takes the finger to be the moon. He has not only lost the moon, but he has lost the finger too."

**4** "Why? Because he takes that which points to the bright moon and calls it the bright moon. He thereby loses both the moon and the finger of brightness."

**5** "Ananda, the awareness that distinguishes between light and dark, discriminating between objective states — that is the finger, not the moon."

**6** "Your true mind, the original bright nature, is like the moon. It does not come or go, it does not arise or cease, it is not born and does not die."

**7** "It is eternally pure, eternally bright, eternally still, and eternally aware. It is your original face before your parents were born."

**8** "But because you pursue the phantom objects of the six sense faculties, you have covered over this bright nature."

**9** "When the objects are removed, the brightness of your original nature shines forth of itself, like the sun emerging from behind clouds."

**10** "This is the true mind, the Shurangama — the great, glorious concentration of all the Buddhas."
