---
title: "Surangama Sutra - Chapter 1"
scripture: "surangama-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the city of Shravasti, at the sublime abode in the Jeta Grove, with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty great bhikshus.

**2** They were all great Arhats who had ended all outflows, who had no further afflictions, who had attained self-mastery, whose minds were liberated, and whose wisdom was liberated.

**3** At that time, it being the day of the full moon, the Buddha, having received an invitation, prepared to enter the city for the begging of food.

**4** The Elder Ananda, not having yet received an invitation, was walking alone through the streets and lanes, begging for food in sequence.

**5** As he passed the house of a courtesan, he was drawn by a powerful spell and nearly fell into temptation. But the Buddha, knowing this, sent Manjushri with a dharani to rescue him.

**6** When Ananda returned to the Buddha's assembly, he wept bitterly and bowed at the Buddha's feet. He blamed himself for having learned much but having not yet attained the power of the Way.

**7** He earnestly requested the Buddha to teach him the wonderful concentration by which all the Tathagatas of the ten directions had attained supreme enlightenment.

**8** At that time the World-Honoured One, in the great assembly, reached out his golden arm, touched the crown of Ananda's head, and said:

**9** "There is a concentration called the Shurangama, the king of concentrations, which embraces all practices and leads to the gateway of the wonderful. It is the path by which all Tathagatas of the ten directions attained supreme enlightenment."

**10** "I shall now explain it for you. Listen well and with a calm mind."
