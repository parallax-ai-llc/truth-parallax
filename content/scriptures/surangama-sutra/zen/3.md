Zen reads the analysis of the six elements not as abstract philosophy but as direct pointing. When the Buddha demonstrates that earth is neither inside nor outside, neither arising nor ceasing, a Zen practitioner recognizes the same teaching that Dogen expressed: 'The entire earth is the true human body.' The elements are not separate from mind, and mind is not separate from the elements. This non-duality of mind and world is realized in zazen, where the boundary between self and environment dissolves. The chapter's analytical method serves Zen as a preparation for the direct experience that transcends analysis.
