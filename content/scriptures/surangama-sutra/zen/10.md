The subtlest skandha-demons — those of perception, formation, and consciousness — represent the most insidious obstacles because they masquerade as genuine insight. A practitioner may develop a philosophical system they believe is ultimate truth, or identify with a state of pure consciousness they believe is enlightenment. Zen addresses these dangers through the teacher-student relationship: a qualified master can recognize these subtle attachments and challenge them through dokusan (private interview) and the testing of the student's understanding. The Surangama's warning that attachment to consciousness itself is the final and most dangerous demon resonates with the Zen teaching that even the experience of 'emptiness' must be let go.
