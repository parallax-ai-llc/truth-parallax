Avalokitesvara's method — turning the hearing inward to realize the source of hearing — is particularly valued in Chan/Zen practice. The instruction to 'enter the stream of hearing and forget the object' describes a practice of withdrawing attention from external sounds and directing it toward the hearing faculty itself. At the deepest level, even the distinction between hearing and not-hearing dissolves, revealing the unconditioned awareness that Zen calls 'original face.' Some Chan teachers have used this practice as an alternative or complement to koan study, particularly for practitioners whose temperament is suited to auditory awareness.
