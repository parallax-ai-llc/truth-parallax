The skandha-demons of form and feeling are of supreme practical importance for Zen practitioners. Experiences of light, bliss, visions, and extraordinary bodily sensations regularly arise during intensive meditation retreats (sesshin). Without the guidance provided by this chapter, practitioners may become attached to these experiences, mistake them for enlightenment, or develop spiritual pride. Zen teachers routinely counsel students to 'let go' of such experiences — to neither grasp nor reject them but to continue sitting through them. The Surangama Sutra provides the detailed map that supports this counsel.
