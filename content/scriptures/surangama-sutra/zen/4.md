The account of world formation through the stirring of ignorance within the clear mind resonates with the Zen understanding that delusion and enlightenment are not two separate realities. The world arises from the same mind that realizes its emptiness. Chan Master Huangbo taught that 'the mind is the Buddha, and the Buddha is the mind' — there is no world separate from the mind that perceives it, and no mind separate from the world it manifests. Zen practitioners use this chapter as a reminder that the very world of apparent delusion is the field of awakening.
