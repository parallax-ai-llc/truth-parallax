Zen engages with the levels of practice described in this chapter through a characteristic tension. On one hand, Zen affirms the possibility of sudden awakening — realization that transcends all gradual stages. On the other hand, Zen acknowledges that awakening must be deepened, refined, and integrated into daily life through continued practice. The 57 levels can be read as a description of the process of maturation after initial kensho: the gradual embodiment of realization in every aspect of life. Dogen's teaching that practice and enlightenment are one (shusho ichinyo) resolves this tension by understanding every moment of practice as already the full expression of awakening.
