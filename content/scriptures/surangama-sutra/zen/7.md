Zen's relationship with mantra and dharani practice is ambivalent. While Zen emphasizes direct pointing beyond words, the Shurangama Mantra has been recited in Chan monasteries for centuries as part of the daily liturgy. Zen understands this recitation not as magical incantation but as a practice of total concentration — when the mantra is recited with complete attention, the boundary between reciter and recited dissolves, and the practice becomes a form of samadhi. The chapter's emphasis on precepts as the foundation of all practice is fully embraced by Zen, which regards ethical conduct as inseparable from meditation and wisdom.
