The opening investigation of the seven locations of mind is a natural Zen koan. Where is the mind? Ananda proposes seven answers; the Buddha rejects each one. A Zen practitioner sitting with this dialogue does not seek a correct eighth answer but enters the space opened by the exhaustion of all conceptual locations. When every location is eliminated, what remains is not nothing but the living reality of mind prior to conceptualization. Chan Master Xuanhua taught that this chapter should be studied not as philosophy but as a direct investigation — the same investigation that occurs when a Zen student sits with the question 'What is this?' and finds that no conceptual answer suffices.
