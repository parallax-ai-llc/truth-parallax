The investigation of seeing nature goes to the heart of Zen practice: What sees? The Buddha shows Ananda that seeing does not belong to the eyes, nor to the objects seen, nor to the consciousness that arises between them. In Zen terms, this chapter points to the 'host' — the unchanging awareness that witnesses all changing phenomena. Linji's teaching of the 'true person of no rank' who goes in and out through the doors of the face resonates directly with the Surangama's investigation. The chapter invites the practitioner to look directly at the nature of awareness itself, which is exactly what zazen accomplishes when practiced wholeheartedly.
