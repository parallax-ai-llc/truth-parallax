The Shurangama Mantra and dharani practices described in this chapter have no direct parallel in Theravada canonical literature, where mantra recitation plays a minimal role. However, the Theravada tradition does employ protective chanting (paritta), such as the Ratana Sutta and the Metta Sutta, which serves a function analogous to the Shurangama Mantra's protective power. The chapter's emphasis on moral precepts as the foundation for mantra practice resonates with the Theravada insistence that sila (ethical conduct) is the indispensable basis for all spiritual development.
