This chapter's account of world formation through the interplay of karma and consciousness can be compared with the Theravada understanding of dependent origination (paticca-samuppada) and cosmology as presented in texts like the Agganna Sutta. While the Surangama Sutra frames world formation in terms of the one true mind becoming deluded, the Theravada account emphasizes the twelve links of dependent origination as the mechanism by which beings generate their experienced worlds. Both traditions agree that the world as experienced is fundamentally shaped by consciousness and karma rather than existing independently of the mind.
