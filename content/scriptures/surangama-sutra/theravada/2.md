The investigation of the nature of seeing in this chapter parallels Theravada teachings on visual consciousness (cakkhu-vinnana). The Buddha demonstrates that seeing cannot be identified with the eye, the object, or the space between them. This analysis resonates with the Abhidhamma's treatment of the eye-door cognitive process (cakkhu-dvara vithi), where seeing arises through the convergence of multiple conditions. Theravada practitioners find in this chapter a sophisticated investigation of perception that, while expressed in Mahayana terminology, addresses the same fundamental question: what is the nature of conscious experience, and where does awareness truly reside?
