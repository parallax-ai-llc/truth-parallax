The second set of skandha-demons — arising from the perception, formation, and consciousness aggregates — addresses subtler forms of self-deception in advanced meditation. These include false experiences of cosmic consciousness, claims of special knowledge, and attachment to refined states of being. The Theravada tradition addresses similar dangers through the concept of manamatta (conceit), particularly the subtle residual conceit that persists even in advanced practitioners. Both traditions emphasize that the most dangerous obstacles are not gross defilements but subtle attachments to spiritual experience itself.
