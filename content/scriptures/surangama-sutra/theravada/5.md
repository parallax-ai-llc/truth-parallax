The 25 Means of Perfect Penetration, in which bodhisattvas and arhats each describe their individual path to realization through different sense faculties and elements, resonates with the Theravada recognition that different practitioners are suited to different meditation objects. The Visuddhimagga lists 40 meditation subjects (kammatthana), acknowledging that temperament and inclination shape practice. While the Theravada system is more systematically organized by temperament type, the Surangama's presentation shares the fundamental insight that there is no single correct entry point into meditative realization.
