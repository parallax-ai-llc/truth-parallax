The first set of skandha-demons — deceptive experiences arising from the form and feeling aggregates during deep meditation — resonates with the Theravada concept of vipassanupakkilesa, the ten corruptions of insight described in the Visuddhimagga. These include experiences of light, rapture, tranquility, and happiness that arise during vipassana and can be mistaken for attainment. The Surangama Sutra's more elaborate taxonomy of 50 states serves a similar practical purpose: warning meditators against attachment to extraordinary experiences that, however compelling, are not genuine realization.
