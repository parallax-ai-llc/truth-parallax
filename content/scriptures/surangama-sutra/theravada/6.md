Avalokitesvara's method of perfect penetration through hearing — turning the faculty of hearing inward to realize the nature of awareness itself — can be compared with the Theravada practice of mindfulness of sound and the contemplation of the ear-door cognitive process. While Avalokitesvara is a Mahayana bodhisattva figure not found in the Pali canon, the practice of investigating the nature of hearing resonates with the Theravada emphasis on examining the arising and passing of sensory experience. The chapter's teaching that awareness itself is unconditioned while sounds arise and pass finds a distant parallel in the Theravada distinction between consciousness and its objects.
