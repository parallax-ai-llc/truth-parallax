The Surangama Sutra's analysis of the six elements — earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness — parallels the Theravada treatment of the four great elements (mahabhuta) in the Abhidhamma. While the Pali tradition typically works with four material elements plus space and consciousness as separate categories, the sutra's integrated analysis of all six as aspects of the one true mind represents a characteristically Mahayana synthesis. Theravada readers can nonetheless appreciate the chapter's detailed phenomenological investigation of how each element is experienced, which complements the Abhidhamma's analytical approach to material reality.
