## Overview

The Surangama Sutra is less central to the Tibetan Buddhist tradition than to Chinese Buddhism, as it was not included in the standard Tibetan Kangyur. Nevertheless, Tibetan scholars have engaged with it through Chinese-Tibetan textual exchange, and several of its themes resonate deeply with Vajrayana practice.

## Points of Connection

The Shurangama Mantra's extensive dharani practice parallels the Tibetan emphasis on mantra recitation as a means of purification and protection. The sutra's 25 Means of Perfect Penetration, in which bodhisattvas and arhats describe their individual paths to realization, invite comparison with the Tibetan system of multiple valid paths suited to different practitioners' capacities and karmic dispositions.

## Meditation Obstacles

Tibetan Buddhism has its own extensive literature on obstacles in meditation, particularly in the Mahamudra and Dzogchen traditions. The Surangama Sutra's 50 skandha-demons can be read alongside Tibetan teachings on nyams (meditative experiences) that practitioners must not mistake for realization — a concern shared across contemplative Buddhist traditions.
