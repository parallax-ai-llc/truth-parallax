The 25 Means of Perfect Penetration parallel the Tibetan Buddhist understanding that different practitioners require different methods. The Tibetan system of classifying practitioners by their predominant defilement (desire, anger, or ignorance) and prescribing appropriate practices resonates with the sutra's presentation of 25 different entry points into realization. The Vajrayana tradition extends this principle even further, offering an enormous variety of practices — deity yoga, mantra, mandala, and more — to accommodate the diverse capacities and dispositions of practitioners.
