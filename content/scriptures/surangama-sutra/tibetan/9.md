The skandha-demons of form and feeling resonate with Tibetan teachings on nyams — meditative experiences of bliss, clarity, and non-thought that arise during shamatha and vipashyana practice. Tibetan meditation manuals consistently warn that these nyams, while signs of progress, must not be clung to or mistaken for realization. The Surangama Sutra's more elaborate taxonomy provides additional detail that complements the Tibetan understanding, offering a cross-traditional resource for practitioners navigating the challenges of deep meditation.
