The 57 levels of the bodhisattva path described in this chapter correspond to the Tibetan system of the five paths (lam lnga) and ten grounds (sa bcu), though the Surangama's enumeration is more detailed. Tibetan Buddhist education gives extensive attention to mapping the path, particularly in the Abhisamayalamkara literature studied in monastic universities. The parallel between the Surangama's systematic path description and the Tibetan graduated path (lam rim) framework reflects a shared Mahayana concern with providing practitioners clear landmarks for their journey to complete enlightenment.
