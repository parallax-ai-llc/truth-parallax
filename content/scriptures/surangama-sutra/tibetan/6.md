Avalokitesvara's method of perfect penetration through hearing holds special significance for Tibetan Buddhism, where Avalokiteshvara (Chenrezig) is the patron bodhisattva of Tibet. The practice of turning hearing inward can be compared with the Tibetan practice of listening to the 'sound of dharma' (chos kyi sgra) in Mahamudra meditation, where practitioners attend to the natural sound of awareness itself. The mantra OM MANI PADME HUM, associated with Chenrezig and recited millions of times by Tibetan practitioners, represents a devotional counterpart to the contemplative practice described in this chapter.
