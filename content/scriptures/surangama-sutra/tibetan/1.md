The investigation of the seven locations of mind can be compared with the Tibetan Buddhist analysis of mind in the Madhyamaka and Yogacara traditions. Tibetan philosophers such as Tsongkhapa analyzed the nature of mind with similar rigor, asking whether consciousness can be located in the brain, the heart, or anywhere else. The Prasangika Madhyamaka conclusion — that the mind is merely designated by convention and lacks inherent existence — resonates with the Surangama's finding that the mind cannot be located anywhere. Tibetan analytical meditation (dpyad sgom) employs a similar method of systematic investigation to undermine naive assumptions about the nature of self and mind.
