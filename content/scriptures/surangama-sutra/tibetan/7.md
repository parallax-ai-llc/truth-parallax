The Shurangama Mantra's protective and purifying functions parallel the extensive use of mantra in Tibetan Vajrayana practice. Tibetan Buddhism employs mantras for protection (raksha mantras), purification (Vajrasattva mantra), and realization (deity mantras), and the theoretical framework is similar: mantras work through the combination of sound, intention, and the blessings of enlightened beings. The chapter's insistence on pure ethical conduct as the foundation for mantra practice resonates with the Vajrayana emphasis on samaya (sacred vows) as the indispensable basis for tantric practice.
