The analysis of the six elements invites comparison with the Tibetan Vajrayana understanding of the five elements (earth, water, fire, wind, space) as the basis of both external phenomena and the internal subtle body. In Tibetan tantra, the elements are not merely physical constituents but expressions of the five Buddha families, and meditation on the elements is a means of purifying the subtle body and realizing the inseparability of appearance and emptiness. The Surangama's teaching that the elements are neither internal nor external resonates with the tantric understanding that the elements of the body and the elements of the world are fundamentally one.
