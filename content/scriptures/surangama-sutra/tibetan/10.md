The subtlest skandha-demons parallel the Dzogchen and Mahamudra teachings on the danger of mistaking meditative experiences for the nature of mind itself. In Mahamudra, practitioners are warned against confusing the alaya-vijnana (storehouse consciousness) with rigpa (pure awareness) — a mistake that corresponds to the Surangama's warning about attachment to the consciousness skandha. The Tibetan tradition's emphasis on the guru's role in cutting through these subtle self-deceptions mirrors the Surangama's insistence that only the Buddha's guidance can protect meditators from the most refined forms of delusion.
