The investigation of seeing nature in this chapter parallels Dzogchen teachings on the nature of rigpa (pure awareness). Dzogchen distinguishes between sem (ordinary dualistic mind) and rigpa (awareness itself), just as the Surangama distinguishes between conditioned consciousness and the unchanging seeing nature. The Tibetan teaching that rigpa is present in every moment of experience but obscured by the grasping of ordinary mind resonates with the sutra's demonstration that the true seeing nature is always present but unrecognized because beings mistake the objects of seeing for the seeing itself.
