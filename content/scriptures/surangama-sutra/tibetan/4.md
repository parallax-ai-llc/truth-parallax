The Surangama's account of world formation through the stirring of ignorance within the pure mind parallels the Tibetan concept of ma rig pa (fundamental ignorance) as described in Dzogchen. In Dzogchen teaching, the pure ground (gzhi) becomes obscured when awareness fails to recognize itself, giving rise to the three appearances of samsara, nirvana, and the path. This structural parallel suggests a shared understanding that the world of suffering arises not from external causes but from a fundamental misrecognition within awareness itself.
