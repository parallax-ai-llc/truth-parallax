## Overview

The Narayana Upanishad is of supreme importance for Vaishnavism as it provides direct Upanishadic authority for the identification of the ultimate Brahman with Narayana-Vishnu. The text's opening declaration — "Narayana alone existed; neither Brahma, nor Shiva, nor the earth, nor the heaven" — establishes Narayana as the sole reality prior to and underlying all creation.

## Theological Emphases

Ramanuja cites the Narayana Upanishad extensively as proof that the Upanishadic Brahman is not an impersonal absolute but the personal God Narayana. The text's teaching that all beings, including Brahma and Shiva, originate from Narayana confirms the Vaishnava hierarchy in which Vishnu is supreme and all other deities are His subordinates or manifestations. The meditation mantras provided serve as the foundation of Vaishnava devotional practice.
