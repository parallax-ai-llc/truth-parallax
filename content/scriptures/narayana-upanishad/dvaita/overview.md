## Overview

The Narayana Upanishad is one of the most important scriptural authorities for Madhva's Dvaita Vedanta. Its explicit declaration of Narayana's supremacy over all other beings — including Brahma and Shiva — directly supports the Dvaita hierarchy in which Vishnu-Narayana is the Supreme Being and all other entities are eternally dependent on Him.

## Theological Emphases

Madhva reads the Narayana Upanishad as a straightforward declaration of God's nature and sovereignty. Narayana's pre-existence before creation, His role as the source of all beings, and His position as the inner self of all creatures confirm the Dvaita understanding of God as the independent (*svatantra*) reality on whom everything else depends. The text's mantras for meditation on Narayana are valued as direct means of cultivating the devotion that leads to liberation.
