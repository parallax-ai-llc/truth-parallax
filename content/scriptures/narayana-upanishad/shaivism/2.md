Shaiva interpreters read this section's teachings on meditation on narayana as pointing to Shiva as the supreme consciousness underlying all reality. The Narayana Upanishad's insights are understood through the lens of Pratyabhijna (Recognition) philosophy — the progressive discovery that one's own awareness is identical with Shiva's unlimited consciousness.

The practical and philosophical content of this section serves the Shaiva goal of *pratyabhijna* — the direct recognition of one's identity with Shiva that constitutes liberation. The teachings remove the intellectual obstacles that prevent this recognition.
