## Overview

Shaiva interpreters approach the Narayana Upanishad by reading "Narayana" as a title applicable to the supreme reality under any name. The term *Narayana* etymologically means "the resting place of all beings" (*nara* = beings, *ayana* = abode), which Shaivas argue describes Shiva as the universal consciousness in which all beings reside.

## Theological Emphases

The Shaiva reading does not deny the Narayana Upanishad's teaching but recontextualizes it: the supreme being from whom all creation originates and who pervades all things is none other than Shiva under the name Narayana. Just as the Kaivalya Upanishad uses the name Shiva for the universal Brahman, this Upanishad uses the name Narayana — both point to the same non-dual consciousness.
