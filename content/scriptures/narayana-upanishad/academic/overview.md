## Overview

The Narayana Upanishad is classified among the Vaishnava Upanishads — texts that explicitly identify the supreme Brahman with Vishnu-Narayana. Scholars date it to approximately the early centuries CE, though some elements may be older. The text provides important evidence for the development of the Narayana cult and its integration into the Vedic/Upanishadic framework.

## Research Themes

The relationship between the Narayana Upanishad and the Mahanarayana Upanishad (attached to the Taittiriya Aranyaka) is debated. Some scholars see the shorter Narayana Upanishad as an extract or summary of the longer text; others view them as independent compositions from the same devotional milieu. The text's explicit subordination of Brahma and Shiva to Narayana reflects sectarian competition in the early centuries CE.
