---
title: "Narayana Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "narayana-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Om! Narayana is within and without. Narayana is above and below. Narayana is to the right and to the left. Narayana is before and behind. Narayana is in the east, in the west, in the north, and in the south.

**2** Narayana is all this — what has been and what will be. He alone is all this. Narayana alone is the supreme reality, pure and stainless.

**3** This universe, before creation, existed as Brahman alone. There was neither east nor west, neither above nor below. He alone existed, the one without a second.

**4** He projected the universe by His own power of Maya. In this universe He entered. Having entered, He became both the sentient and the insentient, the manifest and the unmanifest, the foundation and the non-foundation, knowledge and ignorance, truth and untruth.

**5** As truth, He became all that exists. That is what is called the real. Hence the scripture says: "In the beginning, all this was the real alone."

**6** He who knows this supreme Brahman becomes Brahman himself. In his lineage no one is born who does not know Brahman.

**7** He overcomes grief. He overcomes sin. Freed from the knots of the heart, he becomes immortal.

**8** This is declared by the Rig verse: "The knower of Brahman attains the highest." Brahman is truth, knowledge, and infinity. He who finds it hidden in the cave of the heart, in the highest heaven — he realizes all desires along with the all-knowing Brahman.

**9** Om. Thus ends the Narayana Upanishad, as contained in the Krishna Yajur Veda. Om Shanti, Shanti, Shanti.
