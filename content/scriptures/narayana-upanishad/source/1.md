---
title: "Narayana Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "narayana-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om! Then the Purusha Narayana desired: "I shall create offspring." From Narayana arose Prana, mind, all the senses, Akasa, air, fire, water, and earth — the supporter of all.

**2** From Narayana arose Brahma. From Narayana arose Rudra. From Narayana arose Indra. From Narayana arose the Prajapatis. From Narayana arose the twelve Adityas, the eleven Rudras, the eight Vasus — all the Vedic hymns.

**3** From Narayana all things originate. In Narayana all things exist. Into Narayana all things dissolve. Om, obeisance to Narayana.

**4** Narayana is the eternal reality. Narayana is the supreme truth. Narayana is the highest consciousness. Narayana is the supreme Self.

**5** Narayana is the supreme meditator. Narayana is the supreme meditation. Whatever there is in this world known through perception or through verbal testimony — all that is pervaded by Narayana within and without.

**6** He is without beginning, without end, the supreme Lord, the eternal, the blissful, the stainless, the pure, the one without a second — this is the Narayana Upanishad.

**7** Om! The Purusha of the measure of a thumb, the inner Atman, always dwelling in the hearts of men — He is perceived by the heart, by the mind, by the intellect. Those who know this become immortal.

**8** In the center of the heart there is a mass of fire; in that fire there is a small flame; in the middle of that flame dwells the supreme Self. He is Brahma, He is Shiva, He is Indra, He is the imperishable, the supreme, the self-luminous.

**9** He is Vishnu, He is Prana, He is time, He is fire, He is the moon. He is all that was and all that will be, the eternal. Knowing Him, one crosses death. There is no other path to liberation.

**10** Seeing the Atman as present in all beings, and all beings in the Atman — he attains the supreme Brahman, and not by any other means.
