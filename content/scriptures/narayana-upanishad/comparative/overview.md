## Overview

The Narayana Upanishad's declaration that a single personal God is the source of all creation and the inner self of all beings invites comparison with monotheistic theologies worldwide. Its uncompromising insistence on Narayana's supremacy over all other divine beings parallels the exclusive claims of the Abrahamic traditions.

## Comparative Themes

The Narayana Upanishad's opening — "Narayana alone existed; neither Brahma, nor Shiva" — echoes Genesis 1:1 ("In the beginning God created"), the Quran's *tawhid* ("There is no god but God"), and the Zoroastrian declaration of Ahura Mazda as the sole creator. The identification of God as both transcendent creator and immanent indweller parallels the Christian concept of the Holy Spirit, the Islamic understanding of God's nearness (Quran 50:16), and the Jewish concept of the *Shekhinah* (divine presence).
