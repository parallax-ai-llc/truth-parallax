## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Narayana Upanishad presents the supreme Brahman under the personal name Narayana — the *saguna* (qualified) aspect of the *nirguna* (unqualified) absolute. Shankara's tradition acknowledges the text as legitimate scripture while maintaining that the personal God Narayana is the conditioned Brahman, useful for meditation (*upasana*) but ultimately transcended in the recognition of the attributeless absolute.

## Theological Emphases

The Advaita reading does not diminish the Narayana Upanishad but places it within the framework of two levels of truth: at the empirical level (*vyavaharika*), Narayana is the supreme God and proper object of worship; at the absolute level (*paramarthika*), there is only the undifferentiated Brahman beyond all names and forms, including the name Narayana.
