## The Valley of Detachment: Trust in God Alone

The Valley of Detachment corresponds, for Sunni readers, to the Quranic ideal of *tawakkul* — absolute trust in God. The Quran commands: "Put your trust in God if you are truly believers" (5:23). Detachment is not indifference to the world but the liberation of the heart from dependence on anything other than God.

The prophetic model is instructive: Muhammad was engaged in the world — a husband, father, statesman, and military leader — yet his heart was attached to God alone. The Valley of Detachment does not require withdrawal from the world but rather the inner freedom that allows one to act in the world without being enslaved by it. The Sunni reader finds in this valley a call to spiritual maturity: the ability to hold worldly responsibilities with an open hand, knowing that all belongs to God and all returns to God.
