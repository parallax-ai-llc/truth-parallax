## Overview

Within Sunni Islam, the Conference of the Birds is received as a masterwork of devotional literature that dramatizes Quranic themes of seeking, submission, and divine encounter. Attar's poem is saturated with Quranic allusions and prophetic traditions, and its central message — that the soul must surrender its self-will to find God — aligns with the Islamic concept of *islam* (submission) at the deepest level. The seven valleys map a progressive deepening of the believer's relationship with God, from initial repentance (*tawba*) through various stages of purification to the ultimate annihilation of the ego in divine love.
