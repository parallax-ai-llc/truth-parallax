## The Valley of Poverty and Annihilation: Complete Submission

The final valley — poverty and annihilation — represents, for Sunni readers, the ultimate realization of *islam* (submission): the complete surrender of the ego to God. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Die before you die" — a hadith that the Sufi tradition interprets as the annihilation of the ego-self (*nafs*) in the divine will.

Sunni theology approaches *fana* cautiously, insisting that the distinction between Creator and creature is never abolished. The Sunni reading of this valley emphasizes *fana al-irada* (annihilation of self-will) rather than *fana al-wujud* (annihilation of being): the believer's will is so perfectly aligned with God's will that the distinction between personal desire and divine command disappears. The servant becomes a perfect instrument of divine purpose — not because the servant ceases to exist but because the servant ceases to will anything other than what God wills.
