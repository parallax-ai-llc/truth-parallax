## The Valley of Unity: Tawhid Realized

The Valley of Unity brings the seeker to the experiential realization of *tawhid* — the oneness of God that is the first principle of Islamic faith. For Sunni readers, the *shahada* ("There is no god but God") is not merely a creedal statement but a description of reality: everything that exists is a manifestation of God's creative will, and nothing has independent existence apart from God.

The Valley of Unity does not abolish the distinction between Creator and creation (which would be *hulul* or incarnationism, rejected by Sunni orthodoxy) but reveals the utter dependence of all creation on the one Creator. The Sunni reader understands this valley through the lens of the Ash'ari theological tradition: God alone is the true Agent (*fa'il haqiqi*); all creaturely agency is derivative and dependent. The experience of unity is not identification with God but the overwhelming perception of God's presence in all things.
