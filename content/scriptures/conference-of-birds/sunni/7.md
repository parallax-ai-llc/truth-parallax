## The Valley of Bewilderment: Humility Before the Divine

The Valley of Bewilderment humbles the intellect before the mystery of divine reality — a humbling that Sunni theology affirms through the concept of *tafwid* (entrusting matters to God) and the acknowledgment that God's ways exceed human comprehension. The Quran declares: "They encompass nothing of His knowledge except what He wills" (2:255).

For Sunni readers, bewilderment is not a crisis of faith but its deepening. The believer who has progressed through quest, love, knowledge, detachment, and unity discovers that God remains infinitely beyond all that has been experienced and understood. This recognition is not despair but *khushoo'* (humility and awe) — the state of the heart that the Quran identifies as the mark of true believers: "Those who humble themselves in their prayers" (23:2).
