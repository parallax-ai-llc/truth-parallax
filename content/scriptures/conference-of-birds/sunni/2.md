## The Valley of the Quest: Seeking God with Sincerity

The Valley of the Quest corresponds, for Sunni readers, to the Quranic command to seek God with sincerity (*ikhlas*) and effort (*jihad al-nafs*, the struggle against the lower self). The Quran declares: "Those who strive in Us, We will surely guide to Our paths" (29:69). The valley's trackless expanse represents the uncertainty that accompanies genuine faith — the willingness to set out without knowing the path, trusting in God's guidance.

The renunciation demanded in this valley aligns with the prophetic model of *zuhd* (asceticism) — the Prophet Muhammad's own simplicity of life and detachment from material wealth. For Sunni readers, the Valley of the Quest does not require monasticism but rather the reordering of priorities: worldly goods are to be used, not worshipped; earthly relationships are to be cherished, not idolized. The quest is not the abandonment of the world but the redirecting of the heart from the world to God.
