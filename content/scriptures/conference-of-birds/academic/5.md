## Detachment and Asceticism in Islamic Spiritual Literature

The Valley of Detachment draws on the Islamic ascetic tradition (*zuhd*) that preceded and shaped Sufi mysticism. Scholars have traced the concept of detachment from the early *zuhhad* (ascetics) of Basra (Hasan al-Basri, Rabi'a al-'Adawiyya) through the classical Sufi theorists (al-Qushayri, al-Hujwiri) to Attar's poetic synthesis. The valley represents a stage that virtually all Sufi systematizers identify as essential: the stripping away of attachment to both worldly and spiritual goods.
