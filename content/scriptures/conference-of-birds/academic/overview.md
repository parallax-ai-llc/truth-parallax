## Overview

The Conference of the Birds has been the subject of extensive scholarly analysis since its first European translation in the nineteenth century. Edward FitzGerald (translator of Omar Khayyam) produced a partial English version in 1889, and subsequent translations by C. S. Nott, Afkham Darbandi and Dick Davis, and Sholeh Wolpé have made the text widely accessible. Academic study has addressed the poem's allegorical structure, its relationship to other works in the *masnavi* tradition, its sources in earlier Sufi literature, and its reception across Persian, Turkish, and Urdu literary cultures.
