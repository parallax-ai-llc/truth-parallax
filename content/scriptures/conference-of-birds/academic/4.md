## Ma'rifa and Epistemology in Islamic Philosophy

The Valley of Knowledge engages with one of the central debates in Islamic epistemology: the nature and limits of human knowledge of God. Scholars have compared Attar's treatment to the positions of major Islamic thinkers: al-Ghazali's experiential epistemology, Ibn Arabi's doctrine of *kashf* (unveiling), and the Isma'ili concept of *ta'lim* (authoritative instruction). The valley's teaching that each bird sees the Simurgh according to its own capacity has been compared to Ibn Arabi's concept of the *tajalli* (divine self-disclosure) — the doctrine that God manifests in forms appropriate to the receptivity of the knower.
