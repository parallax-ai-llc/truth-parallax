## Love and Madness in Persian Literary Tradition

The Valley of Love draws on the rich Persian literary tradition of *ishq* (passionate love), particularly the Majnun-Layla romance cycle and the Sufi love poetry (*ghazal*) tradition established by Sana'i and perfected by Hafiz. Scholars have analyzed Attar's treatment of love as a bridge between the secular *ghazal* tradition and the mystical *masnavi* tradition, demonstrating how he deploys the conventions of profane love poetry (the cruel beloved, the mad lover, the consuming fire) in the service of mystical theology.
