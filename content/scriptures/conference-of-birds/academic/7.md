## Bewilderment and Apophatic Discourse

The Valley of Bewilderment has attracted the attention of scholars interested in the relationship between Sufi mysticism and apophatic theology. Michael Sells's analysis of "the language of unsaying" provides a framework for understanding the valley's paradoxical discourse: statements that simultaneously affirm and deny, narratives that subvert their own premises, and a progressive undermining of the conceptual categories that structured the earlier valleys.

Scholars have compared the Valley of Bewilderment to the apophatic traditions of Pseudo-Dionysius, Meister Eckhart, and Nicholas of Cusa in the Christian tradition, and to the Madhyamaka dialectic of Nagarjuna in the Buddhist tradition.
