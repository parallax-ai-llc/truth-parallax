## Fana in Sufi Theory and Practice

The Valley of Poverty and Annihilation (*faqr wa fana*) represents the culmination of the Sufi path as theorized by Abu Yazid al-Bistami, al-Hallaj, and al-Junayd. Scholars have distinguished between different models of *fana*: al-Bistami's ecstatic annihilation ("Glory be to me! How great is my majesty!"), al-Junayd's sober annihilation (the subsistence of the servant in the servant's proper place), and al-Hallaj's controversial identification of the human and divine ("Ana al-Haqq" — "I am the Truth/God"). Attar's presentation, scholars generally agree, is closest to al-Junayd's sober model, emphasizing the extinction of self-will rather than the extinction of personal being.
