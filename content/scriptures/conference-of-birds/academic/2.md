## The Seven Valleys: Structure and Sources

The seven-valley schema is one of Attar's most influential contributions to Sufi literature, later adapted by Baha'u'llah in the *Seven Valleys* and by numerous Sufi commentators. Scholars have traced its possible sources to al-Ghazali's stages of the path, Abu Nasr al-Sarraj's *Kitab al-Luma'* (which lists *maqamat* or spiritual stations), and the cosmological geography of the Isma'ili tradition.

The Valley of the Quest, as the first valley, establishes the narrative pattern: the hoopoe presents the valley's challenge, tells illustrative stories, and answers the birds' objections. Scholars have noted that the number of embedded stories varies significantly across valleys, with the earlier valleys receiving more elaborate narrative treatment than the later ones — a structure that mirrors the progressive simplification of the path itself.
