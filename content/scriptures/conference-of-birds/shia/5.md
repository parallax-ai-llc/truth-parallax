## The Valley of Detachment: Following the Imam's Example

The Valley of Detachment resonates with the Shia veneration of Imam Ali as the model of detachment (*zuhd*). Ali's legendary indifference to worldly power — his simplicity of life even while serving as caliph, his refusal to enrich himself from the public treasury — provides the Shia reader with a concrete example of the detachment Attar describes.

The valley also connects to the theme of *intizar* (expectation) — the Shia practice of awaiting the Hidden Imam's return. Living in expectation requires a specific form of detachment: not clinging to the present political or social order but maintaining an inner orientation toward a future justice that the Hidden Imam will establish.
