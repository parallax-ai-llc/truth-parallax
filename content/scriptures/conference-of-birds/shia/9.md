## The Simurgh: The Return of the Imam

The poem's climax — the thirty birds' recognition that they are the Simurgh — is read by Shia interpreters through the lens of eschatological hope. The Hidden Imam's return will reveal what the *ghaybah* concealed: that the divine presence was never absent, only veiled. Just as the birds discover that the Simurgh was always their own deepest reality, so the ummah will discover at the Imam's return that the guidance they sought was always present, hidden within the hearts of the faithful.

The number thirty (*si*) has resonated with some Shia readers as symbolic of the faithful remnant — those who maintained their allegiance to the Ahl al-Bayt when the majority followed other paths. The Simurgh's revelation is the vindication of the minority who persevered: the few who remained faithful discover that they carried the divine presence within them all along.
