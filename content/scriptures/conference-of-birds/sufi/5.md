## The Valley of Detachment: Letting Go of Everything

The fourth valley is the Valley of Detachment (*istighnah*). Here the seeker must release not only worldly attachments (already renounced in the Valley of the Quest) but spiritual attachments as well — attachment to visions, to spiritual states, to knowledge, even to the desire for God. True detachment is indifference to everything except the Beloved — and even the concept of "the Beloved" must eventually be released.

For the Sufi reader, this valley represents one of the most demanding stages of the path. The seeker who has experienced divine love and direct knowledge must now let go of these experiences themselves — recognizing that clinging to spiritual experiences is as much an obstacle as clinging to worldly pleasures. The Sufi masters warn against *shirk-e khafi* (hidden idolatry) — the subtle spiritual materialism that treasures divine gifts more than the Divine Giver.

The hoopoe's stories in this valley describe figures who have achieved everything and must now give it all away — kings who renounce not only their kingdoms but their very identities, lovers who renounce not only the world but their love itself.
