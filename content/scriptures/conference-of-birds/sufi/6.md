## The Valley of Unity: All Is One

The fifth valley is the Valley of Unity (*tawhid*). Here the seeker perceives that all apparent multiplicity — the countless forms of creation, the diversity of religious traditions, the distinction between self and other — is the manifestation of a single divine reality. The many birds discover that they are expressions of one Bird; the many seekers discover that they are expressions of one Seeker.

For the Sufi reader, this valley corresponds to the experience of *wahdat al-wujud* (the unity of being) — the realization that there is only God, and that everything that appears to exist independently is a reflection, shadow, or manifestation of the one divine Reality. This is not pantheism (God is everything) but rather the mystical perception that everything subsists in God and has no independent existence apart from God.

The Valley of Unity is a turning point in the journey: from here on, the categories that structured the earlier valleys — seeker and sought, lover and beloved, self and God — begin to dissolve. The bird that enters this valley as a separate creature begins to see itself as a feather on the wing of the Simurgh.
