## The Valley of Poverty and Annihilation: Fana

The seventh and final valley is the Valley of Poverty and Annihilation (*faqr wa fana*). Here the last trace of self — including the self that undertook the journey, the self that experienced love and knowledge and bewilderment — is extinguished. The drop falls into the ocean; the moth enters the flame; the bird ceases to be a bird and becomes... what? The valley offers no description, because there is no one left to describe.

For the Sufi reader, this valley is the culmination of the entire path: *fana fi-Allah* — annihilation in God. The seeker who has journeyed through quest, love, knowledge, detachment, unity, and bewilderment arrives at the point where the journey itself dissolves, because the one who was journeying dissolves. What remains is God — or, more precisely, what remains was always God; the illusion of a separate self that was seeking God was the only obstacle to the realization that God was always all there was.

The poverty (*faqr*) of this valley is not material poverty but ontological poverty — the recognition that the self has nothing of its own, is nothing of its own, and exists only as a reflection of the divine Reality. The hadith qudsi "My servant draws near to Me through nothing I love more than what I have made obligatory upon him" finds its ultimate expression here: the servant draws so near that the distinction between servant and Lord vanishes.
