## The Valley of Knowledge: Direct Experience

The third valley is the Valley of Knowledge (*ma'rifa*). Here the seeker moves beyond secondhand learning — books, doctrines, traditions — to direct experiential knowledge of the divine. Each bird sees the Simurgh differently, according to its own capacity, and each perception is valid at its own level but partial in relation to the whole.

For the Sufi reader, this valley marks the transition from *ilm* (theoretical knowledge) to *ma'rifa* (gnosis, direct knowing). The Sufi masters distinguish sharply between these two modes: one can study the properties of fire without ever having been burned. *Ma'rifa* is the burning — the direct, transformative encounter with the divine reality that no amount of study can produce or replace.

The valley's teaching that each seeker perceives the divine according to their own capacity reflects the Sufi doctrine of *tajalli* (divine self-disclosure): God reveals Godself in forms appropriate to the seeker's readiness. This is not relativism but recognition of the infinite richness of divine reality — a richness that no single perception, however exalted, can exhaust.
