## Love as Spiritual Path: Bhakti, Troubadours, and Sufi Ishq

The Valley of Love connects to the global tradition of love mysticism — the use of erotic and romantic imagery to describe the soul's relationship with the divine. The parallels with Hindu bhakti poetry (Mirabai, Andal, Jayadeva), the Christian Song of Songs tradition, the troubadour love poetry of medieval Provence, and the devotional poetry of Sikhism are extensive.

The cross-cultural comparison reveals that love mysticism tends to subvert social hierarchies: the lover is reduced to poverty, madness, and social disgrace by the power of love, dissolving the distinctions of caste, class, and religious identity that normally structure social life.
