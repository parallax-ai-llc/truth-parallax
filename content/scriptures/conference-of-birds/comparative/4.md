## Ways of Knowing: Epistemologies of the Sacred

The Valley of Knowledge's distinction between theoretical and experiential knowledge resonates with epistemological distinctions across traditions: the Buddhist *pariyatti* (study) vs. *pativedha* (realization), the Hindu *jnana* (knowledge) vs. *vijnana* (experiential wisdom), the Christian *scientia* (knowledge) vs. *sapientia* (wisdom), and the Confucian *zhi* (knowing) vs. *xing* (practice/realization).

In each tradition, the distinction serves a similar function: it protects against the intellectualist temptation to confuse knowing about the divine with knowing the divine.
