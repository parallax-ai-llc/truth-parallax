## Death of the Self: Ego-Dissolution Across Traditions

The Valley of Poverty and Annihilation connects to the universal theme of ego-dissolution as the prerequisite for spiritual liberation. The Christian mystic's "dark night of the soul" (John of the Cross), the Buddhist experience of *anatta* (no-self), the Hindu *jivanmukti* (liberation while alive), and the shamanic initiation through ritual death and rebirth all describe a process in which the ordinary sense of self is dissolved, making way for a more expansive or transparent mode of being.

The comparative question is whether these traditions describe the same experience differently or genuinely different experiences with superficial similarities. The answer likely depends on the level of analysis: phenomenologically similar, theologically distinct.
