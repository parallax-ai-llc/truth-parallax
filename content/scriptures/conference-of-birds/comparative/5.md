## Letting Go: Non-Attachment Across Traditions

The Valley of Detachment connects to the universal spiritual counsel of non-attachment. The Buddhist concept of *upekkha* (equanimity), the Stoic *apatheia* (freedom from destructive passions), the Bhagavad Gita's *nishkama karma* (action without attachment to results), the Christian concept of holy indifference (Ignatian *indiferencia*), and the Taoist *wu wei* (effortless action) all describe a condition in which the practitioner engages fully with the world without being enslaved by outcomes.

The comparative insight is that non-attachment is not passive withdrawal but active freedom — the capacity to act decisively without being driven by fear or desire.
