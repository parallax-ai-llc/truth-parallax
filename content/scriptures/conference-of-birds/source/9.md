## The Arrival at the Throne of the Simorgh

**1.** Of the thousands of birds that set forth, only thirty survived the journey. They were broken, exhausted, stripped of everything.

**2.** Their feathers were burned, their wings were torn, their bodies were battered by the trials of the seven valleys.

**3.** They arrived at the court of the Simorgh more dead than alive, with nothing left of themselves but a trembling hope.

**4.** At the threshold of the Kings presence, a great chamberlain appeared and said: "What business have you here? Turn back. The Simorgh has no need of you."

**5.** The birds cried out in anguish: "We have traveled through seven valleys, we have lost everything, we have given our lives for this journey. Do not turn us away!"

**6.** "We left our homes, our families, our comforts. We crossed the Valley of the Quest and burned in the Valley of Love."

**7.** "We were illumined in the Valley of Knowledge and emptied in the Valley of Detachment."

**8.** "We were dissolved in the Valley of Unity and driven mad in the Valley of Bewilderment."

**9.** "We died in the Valley of Poverty and Annihilation. We have nothing left. We are nothing."

**10.** Then the veil was lifted. The door was opened. And the thirty birds looked upon the Simorgh at last.

**11.** And what they saw was themselves. The Simorgh was a mirror—and in that mirror, thirty birds saw their own faces.

**12.** Si morgh—thirty birds. The name had contained the secret all along.

**13.** They looked at the Simorgh and saw themselves. They looked at themselves and saw the Simorgh. They looked again and saw that they and the Simorgh were one and the same.

**14.** They had sought the King outside themselves and found Him within. They had journeyed to the end of the world only to arrive at the beginning.

**15.** The sun of the Simorghs majesty shone upon them, and in that light, the shadows of their separate selves disappeared forever.

**16.** They were lost in the Simorgh, and the Simorgh was found in them. Neither the seeker nor the sought remained—only the light of pure Being.

**17.** And so the journey ended where it began, and the birds understood at last: the treasure was always within them, and the quest was the means by which they discovered it.

**18.** The world and all that is in it is the shadow of the Simorgh. If you seek the Simorgh, seek yourself. If you seek yourself, look beyond yourself.

**19.** This is the end of the tale, and the beginning of Truth.
