## The Excuses of the Birds

**1.** The nightingale was the first to speak. She said: "The mystery of love is my whole existence. I sing all night for the rose."

**2.** "The rose is enough for me—I want no other beauty. My love for the rose has made me famous throughout the world."

**3.** "How can I abandon the rose to seek the Simorgh? My love is here, in the garden, not behind some distant mountain."

**4.** The hoopoe replied: "O nightingale, you who are so lost in the form of the rose—the rose has no permanence. It blooms for a few days and then withers."

**5.** "Your love for the rose is only the love of appearances. Look beyond the rose to the One who created its beauty."

**6.** "Do not be content with the reflection when you can find the Source of all beauty."

**7.** Then the parrot stepped forward, resplendent in green and gold. She said: "I am content to seek the water of immortality."

**8.** "Why should I journey to the Simorgh when my only desire is to live forever?"

**9.** The hoopoe answered: "O parrot, the water of immortality is not to be found in a fountain. True immortality lies in dying to self."

**10.** "You seek to keep this cage of the body alive forever—but the soul that lives in this cage yearns for a greater freedom."

**11.** The peacock then spread his glorious tail. He said: "I once dwelt in Paradise and I desire only to return there."

**12.** "The Simorgh is not my concern. I want only to find again the garden of Eden."

**13.** The hoopoe said: "O peacock, that garden was only the threshold. You were turned out of Paradise for a reason."

**14.** "You befriended the serpent, and for that you fell. Now you are in love with the colors of your own tail—another form of the same vanity."

**15.** "If you seek only Paradise, you have set your heart on a palace and forgotten the King who dwells within it."

**16.** The duck said: "I cannot leave the water. My whole life is the water. I am content with my pond."

**17.** The hoopoe replied: "Your water is a prison, though you think it a palace. The ocean of the spirit is vaster than anything you have known."

**18.** "Every drop of your pond will one day dry up—but the ocean to which I call you is without end."

**19.** One by one the birds came forward with their excuses, and one by one the hoopoe answered them.

**20.** "Your attachments are chains," the hoopoe said. "Every excuse is a veil between you and the Truth."
