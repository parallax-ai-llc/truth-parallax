## The Hoopoe Presents the Quest

**1.** The birds of the world gathered together one day, those that were known and those that were hidden.

**2.** They assembled in great numbers—birds of every feather and every tongue—from every corner of the earth.

**3.** They came together with one purpose: to find a king, for without a king no realm can prosper.

**4.** The hoopoe, inspired and wise, stepped forward and spoke: "I have traveled far and know the way."

**5.** "I carry in my beak a message from the invisible world, and upon my head I wear the crown of truth."

**6.** "I have journeyed through valleys that no ordinary bird has seen, and I have knowledge of our true Sovereign."

**7.** "His name is the Simorgh, and He dwells behind the mountain of Qaf, the great mountain that encircles the world."

**8.** "He is close to us, but we are far from Him. The way to His dwelling is beset with peril."

**9.** "A hundred thousand veils of light and darkness stand between this assembly and His throne."

**10.** "It is not enough to wish for the journey—you must risk everything and give up your life."

**11.** "The path has seven valleys: the Valley of the Quest, the Valley of Love, the Valley of Knowledge, the Valley of Detachment, the Valley of Unity, the Valley of Bewilderment, and the Valley of Poverty and Annihilation."

**12.** "No one has traveled this road before who has not been utterly changed."

**13.** "If you desire this journey, you must leave behind both the good and the bad of the world."

**14.** "Cast away what you have, and choose to be nothing. When you are nothing, you will find everything."

**15.** "O birds, we have lived too long in ignorance of our King! Let us now set forth to find the Simorgh."

**16.** The hoopoe spoke with such fire that the whole assembly was moved. But then the excuses began, each bird rising with a reason not to go.
