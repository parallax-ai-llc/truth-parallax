## The Valley of Knowledge

**1.** The hoopoe said: "Beyond love lies the Valley of Knowledge. Here the traveler is illumined by a light that has no source."

**2.** "In this valley each bird finds a different path, for the ways of knowledge are as numerous as the creatures of the earth."

**3.** "One may discover the secret in a single atom, another may search the heavens and find nothing."

**4.** "Knowledge is not the same as learning. A man may read a thousand books and know nothing of the Way."

**5.** "The knowledge of this valley is the knowledge of the heart—a direct seeing, not a thing of words."

**6.** "When the sun of knowledge rises in this valley, each traveler sees according to his capacity. The owl sees one thing, the eagle another."

**7.** "An ant moving upon a piece of paper saw only the ink upon the page. It did not know the pen, the hand, or the scribe."

**8.** "So it is with ordinary knowledge—we see the ink but not the Hand that writes."

**9.** "In this valley, the seeker comes to understand that what he had called knowledge was only ignorance in disguise."

**10.** "A fish in the sea asked another fish: Tell me, where is the ocean that I have heard so much about? The other fish replied: You are swimming in it."

**11.** "The ignorant man searches for what he already possesses. The knowledgeable man sees that he has never been without it."

**12.** "When the dervish Shibli was asked what he had learned, he held up a white sheet of paper. When asked what was on the other side, he turned it over—it too was blank. This is what I know, he said."

**13.** "True knowledge is to stand at the edge of what you understand and to gaze into the infinite darkness beyond—and to call that darkness Light."

**14.** "The seeker in this valley loses all certainty. What he thought was solid ground turns to water, and what he thought was water becomes solid ground."

**15.** "But through this disorientation, a new kind of knowing is born—one that does not depend upon the mind."

**16.** "When you have traversed this valley, you will see with an eye that has no veil, and you will know with a knowing that requires no proof."
