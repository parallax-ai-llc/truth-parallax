## The Invocation

**1.** In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate, who gave the bird of the soul a place in the Way of spiritual knowledge.

**2.** He gave the parrot of the soul sugar from the invisible world, and taught the nightingale of the heart its melodious song.

**3.** His wisdom made the hoopoe a guide on the Way, and from the same source the falcon of the spirit found its quarry.

**4.** He who gives speech to the bird of the soul knows every word that such a bird will utter.

**5.** Before the bird of the soul can learn to speak, He has heard every note it will sing.

**6.** He gave to the birds their knowledge of flight, and to the fish their knowledge of the sea.

**7.** He poured into each creature a different measure of spirit, and set before each a different provision.

**8.** The gnat He made drunk with the cup of blood, and the bee He fed with a taste of honey.

**9.** If you seek Him, cast yourself upon the Way—throw yourself like a ball into the field of search.

**10.** The way is long, the sea is deep—do not think this journey is to be taken lightly.

**11.** You must have the heart of a lion and the soul of a moth drawn to the flame if you would succeed.

**12.** In the Name of the One who has no name, who appears by whatever name you call Him.

**13.** Creator and Sustainer of heaven and earth, whatever you have imagined, He is other than that.

**14.** The mind cannot reach the place where He is, the soul cannot travel to His country.

**15.** The prophets themselves are dumbfounded at this door; even the purest soul cannot fathom Him.

**16.** He is beyond all that you can conceive; above all that the sages have declared.

**17.** A hundred thousand veils of light and darkness are drawn across the way to His presence.

**18.** From the world of angels to the world of insects, there is not a single being that truly knows Him.

**19.** The nine spheres of heaven are but a bubble in the ocean of His power; the world of intelligence and the world of soul are but drops in that sea.

**20.** His Beauty is not diminished by the ugliness of the world, nor is His Perfection lessened by the defects of creation.
