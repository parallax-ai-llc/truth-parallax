## The Valley of Bewilderment and the Valley of Poverty and Annihilation

**1.** The hoopoe said: "The sixth valley is the Valley of Bewilderment. Here the traveler is lost in wonder and astonishment."

**2.** "All that he thought he knew is swept away. He is like a man struck by lightning who can neither speak nor move."

**3.** "In this valley, every step forward is a step backward, and every certainty dissolves into confusion."

**4.** "The traveler cries: I do not know whether I exist or do not exist, whether I am in love or not in love, whether I am sane or mad."

**5.** "I do not know whether my heart is full or empty, whether I am near or far. I know nothing of myself or of anyone else."

**6.** "A wise man was asked: Do you know yourself? He replied: If I say yes, I lie. If I say no, I also lie. I am bewildered."

**7.** "This is the valley where all contradictions meet and dissolve into a great silence."

**8.** "Joy and sorrow, hope and despair, knowledge and ignorance—all are poured into a single cup. The traveler drinks and is stunned."

**9.** "But in the very center of this bewilderment, a new clarity begins to dawn—the clarity of one who has given up all pretense of understanding."

**10.** The hoopoe said: "The seventh and final valley is the Valley of Poverty and Annihilation."

**11.** "Here the traveler ceases to exist. The drop falls into the ocean and is no more."

**12.** "This is not the poverty of the beggar—it is the poverty of one who has emptied himself of self."

**13.** "When the self is gone, what remains? Only the Beloved. Only the Ocean."

**14.** "A candle placed before the sun—where is its light? It still burns, but its flame is invisible in the greater radiance."

**15.** "So it is with the soul in the presence of God. It does not cease to be—it ceases to be separate."

**16.** "This is the goal of the journey. This is what the birds set forth to find. This is the secret of the Simorgh."
