## The Call That Comes to All: Beginning the Spiritual Journey

The Conference of the Birds opens with a call that resonates across all religious traditions: the call to leave the familiar and set out in search of the ultimate. Whether it takes the form of God's call to Abraham ("Go from your country and your kindred," Genesis 12:1), the Buddha's departure from the palace, or the hoopoe's summons to seek the Simurgh, the structure is the same: the spiritual journey begins with a disruption of the comfortable and a turning toward the unknown.

The birds' objections represent the universal human resistance to this call. Every tradition recognizes that the spiritual path demands sacrifice, and every tradition acknowledges the human tendency to prefer comfort over growth, the known over the unknown, the part over the whole. The hoopoe's patient, persistent responses model the quality that every spiritual tradition values: the willingness to meet doubt not with coercion but with story, wisdom, and love.
