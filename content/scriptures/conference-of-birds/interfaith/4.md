## Knowing Beyond Words: The Limits of Doctrine

The Valley of Knowledge reminds all traditions that their doctrines and theologies, however valuable, are fingers pointing at the moon — not the moon itself. The Jewish mystic who encounters the *Ein Sof* knows something that cannot be contained in halakhic categories. The Buddhist who experiences *satori* knows something that exceeds the sutras. The Muslim who tastes *dhawq* (direct spiritual experience) knows something that the *fiqh* (jurisprudence) cannot capture.

This is not anti-intellectualism but intellectual honesty: the recognition that the deepest truths are experiential rather than propositional, and that every formulation of the truth — however accurate — remains a formulation, not the truth itself.
