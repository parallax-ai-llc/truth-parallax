## Overview

The Conference of the Birds speaks across religious boundaries with extraordinary directness. Its central message — that the divine is not distant but intimately present, that the seeker and the sought are ultimately one, and that the spiritual journey requires courage, perseverance, and the willingness to lose oneself — resonates with contemplatives of every tradition. The poem's allegory is universal enough to be claimed by readers of many faiths while remaining firmly rooted in the Islamic and specifically Sufi tradition from which it springs.
