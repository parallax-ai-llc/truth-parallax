## Losing to Find: The Universal Paradox

The Valley of Poverty and Annihilation teaches the paradox that every contemplative tradition affirms: you must lose yourself to find yourself. Jesus declares: "Whoever loses their life for my sake will find it" (Matthew 16:25). The Buddha teaches that the illusion of a permanent self (*atman*) must be seen through for liberation to occur. The Sufi tradition speaks of *fana* — the annihilation of the ego in God.

The interfaith convergence on this paradox suggests that the ego — the sense of being a separate, independent, self-sufficient self — is the universal obstacle to spiritual realization, and that its dissolution is the universal path to freedom. The specific language varies; the direction is the same.
