## Holding Loosely: The Wisdom of Non-Attachment

The Valley of Detachment teaches a wisdom that every tradition, in its own way, affirms: the wisdom of holding the world with open hands. The Taoist sage flows like water. The Buddhist practitioner cultivates equanimity. The Christian saint practices holy indifference. The Jewish tzaddik trusts in divine providence.

The interfaith lesson is that detachment is not indifference to the world but freedom within it — the capacity to love without clinging, to act without being enslaved by outcomes, and to receive life's gifts with gratitude rather than grasping. This wisdom is easy to articulate and extraordinarily difficult to practice, which is why every tradition must teach it, and every practitioner must struggle with it, again and again.
