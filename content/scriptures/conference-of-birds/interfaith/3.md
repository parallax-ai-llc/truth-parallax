## The Fire of Love: When the Heart Takes Over

The Valley of Love addresses an experience that contemplatives across traditions recognize: the moment when the spiritual life ceases to be a deliberate practice and becomes an overwhelming passion. The Christian mystic Teresa of Avila describes being pierced by a golden arrow of divine love. The Hindu poet Mirabai declares that she has drunk the cup of divine poison and can never return to ordinary life. The Jewish Hasid dances with abandon before the Torah.

The interfaith convergence on love as the heart of the spiritual path suggests something fundamental about the nature of ultimate reality: whatever the divine is, it is experienced by those who draw near as a consuming, transformative fire that burns away everything that is not itself.
