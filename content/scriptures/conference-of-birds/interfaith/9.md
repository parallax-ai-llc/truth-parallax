## You Are What You Seek: The End of the Journey

The Conference of the Birds ends with one of the great revelations of world spiritual literature: the thirty birds discover that they are the Simurgh. The seeker and the sought were never separate. The journey was not toward a distant goal but into a present reality — a reality that could only be perceived after the veils of ego had been removed by the journey itself.

This insight — that the divine is not distant but intimately present, that the treasure we seek is hidden in the field of our own being, that the kingdom of God is within — is attested across every major religious tradition. The Conference of the Birds dramatizes it with unforgettable power: thousands of birds set out to find their king, and the thirty who arrive discover that the king is — and always was — themselves. The poem ends, but the song continues: in every tradition, in every seeker, in every moment of genuine longing and genuine love, the Simurgh is found.
