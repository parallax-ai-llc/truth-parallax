---
title: "Yasna 50 – Zarathushtra's Petition"
chapter: 15
---

**1.** Can my soul count on anyone for help? Who is there found for my herd, who for myself a protector, indeed, at my call other than the Right and Thyself, O Ahura Mazda, and Best Thought?

**2.** How, O Mazda, should one who desires for himself the blessing of the pastures seek it? Living in righteousness on the land, honest in his ways of life — he serves the community by deeds of Good Thought.

**3.** He who first devoted himself to the Ox and to the poor by his righteous action — he who, spurning the Karapans, chose not the company of the Liar — he is a member of thy household, O Mazda. Such is known as a son of Good Thought.

**4.** I will worship you with praise, O Mazda and Right, with the actions of Good Thought. Let the Dominion be firm, on its foundations founded in piety. Through Armaiti give to many a man the light by which they may see.

**5.** Come hither, O ye of Good Thought! Of thy Fire, O Mazda, the vigour and the force will I display. Through its glow the man of Right shall have blessing; but the man of the Lie shall have woe with the full tongue of his understanding.

**6.** Proclaim, O Mazda, what thou hast appointed me through the Right. With the words of Good Thought, O Lord, may the Wise One teach me, that my tongue may have a charm.

**7.** Arise for me, O Ahura! Through Armaiti give me strength. Through the best Right grant me power. Through Good Thought the mighty authority. And through the Dominion bestow the reward.

**8.** To thy support, O Mazda, wide as we desire it, to come now with Good Thought — grant through thy Spirit, O Ahura, that the desire of the faithful for the blessing may reach its fulfilment.

**9.** What prizes and rewards thou hast to give through Right and Good Thought, grant, O Mazda, and bestow them through Piety. For these do I pray, for the joys of thy blessedness.

**10.** Of Thee, O Mazda, and of Thy Right we are worshippers, now and for all time. And the prayer of devotion from our hearts — O Ahura, by thy sovereign Power give us the prize.

**11.** The mightiest of beings — the Lord of Good Thought, the Lord of the deeds of worship — he knows through the Right the secret things. By his tongue of the Spirit, through the Dominion, is bestowed the promised reward.
