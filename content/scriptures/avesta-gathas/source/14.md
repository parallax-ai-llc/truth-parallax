---
title: "Yasna 49 – The Woe of the Prophet"
chapter: 14
---

**1.** Ever has Bendva been to me an obstacle, the very worst, opposing the Right. I would call upon you, O Mazda and Right, for aid. Cast them out! Drive them from Good Thought! Drive them to the Lie!

**2.** The evil prince and the evil priest, through their evil dominion, shall deliver the creatures of God unto ruin, and the world of Right unto the Lie. But his own soul shall bring him repentance at the Bridge.

**3.** But when, O Mazda, men learn the Right, then shall the Lie be delivered into the hands of those who teach the Truth. When shall the way of the liars be cut off? When shall retribution come, and Good Thought arise?

**4.** The Karapans, who give the Ox into the power of Fury and Evil, with their teaching shall their own souls bring woe upon them when they reach the Bridge of the Separator. Their thoughts and doctrines are not according to the Right.

**5.** But Mazda himself hath promised, he the all-knowing Creator: "Welfare and Immortality are in the Dominion for them whose deeds through Good Thought are in accord with Right."

**6.** So, O Mazda, shall come to pass through the Right in the house of Thy praise the overcoming of the house of the Lie. I pray Thee, by the Blessings of Good Thought and the Right, to bestow the reward on the faithful.

**7.** Grant, O Mazda, through thy Grace and through the Right, that which Zarathushtra asks for the faithful — the rewards of Good Thought for those who practice the Religion. Give through thy Dominion the blessings of Piety.

**8.** The man who by Good Thought joins himself to the Right, and proclaims aloud the Religion — to him, O Ahura, shall come all things that thou hast set apart as the best reward.

**9.** May he who is the Lord of Wisdom guard us from the malice of the enemy! Cast down, O Armaiti, the Lie that rises against us! Cast down the hatred of the Liars, O thou mighty Right!

**10.** That which the Lord of Wisdom hath appointed, let every man receive, according to what he hath earned through his thoughts and words and deeds. From the Dominion of Good Thought comes the blessing of the Right.

**11.** O Ahura, teach me through the Right and through Good Thought to declare Thy Religion, that I may establish the world in the way of Truth. This is the most holy utterance; this is the best deed of worship.

**12.** O Mazda, reveal to us that which is revealed through the Right — the truths of Good Thought, and the grace of Piety, and the light of Thy Dominion, and the fire of ordeal. Through these shall the faithful receive their due reward.
