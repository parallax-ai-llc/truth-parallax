---
title: "Yasna 31 – The Prophet's Warnings"
chapter: 4
---

**1.** Mindful of your commands, we proclaim words unpleasant for those who by their commands destroy the beings of the Right, but best for those that are faithful to Mazda.

**2.** If the better path be not in sight for one who seeks it, then will I come to you all as judge of the parties twain whom Ahura Mazda knoweth, that we may live according to the Right.

**3.** What thou didst appoint to me as the better portion through thy Fire and through Righteousness, so long as I shall have power of action, so long will I teach men to seek after the Right.

**4.** Where is the righteous lord of life that will deprive Violence and the bloody-minded of their dominion? Where is the man to whom shall be given Good Thought of the understanding? As a Prophet he shall be regarded, even he, O Mazda Ahura, whose doctrine is for the support of Right.

**5.** That king or ruler is a wise man who comes to the help of the righteous, when the mighty lord of the province consults him. Thine, O Ahura Mazda, is the Dominion, whereby thou canst give to the right-living poor man the better portion.

**6.** Ahura Mazda, through his sovereign authority, hath given the Holy Word that teaches happiness. He, the holy one, through Good Thought gave the doctrine to those who through their righteousness bring the Lie within their hands.

**7.** He who first through Thought established the blessed realms as clothed in light; he by his wisdom created the Right. These realms Thou dost support through Thy Spirit, O Mazda, which is ever the same.

**8.** I knew Thee, O Mazda, in my thought, that Thou the First art also the Last — that Thou art Father of Good Thought, when I apprehended Thee in my eye — that Thou didst truly create Right, and art the Lord to judge the actions of life.

**9.** Thine was Armaiti, thine the Ox-Creator, the Spirit of Wisdom — O Ahura Mazda — because thou didst give her the choice of paths, to go either to a husbandman that cares for her or to one that does not.

**10.** So she chose the cattle-tending husbandman, the righteous lord of Good Thought, as her master. The godless one who turns from the tillage shall never share in the good tidings.

**11.** When thou, Mazda, in the beginning didst create the Individual and the Individuality, through thy Spirit, and powers of understanding — when thou didst make life clothed with the body, when thou madest actions and teachings, whereby one may exercise one's convictions at one's free will —

**12.** Then lifts up his voice the false speaker or the true speaker, the wise or the unwary, each according to his own heart and mind. Let Armaiti hand in hand with the Spirit try the heart.

**13.** Now is made known that which is done secretly and openly. Through thy Righteous Wisdom shall deeds of violence be requited. He who bestows by his hand on the righteous or wicked that which is good or ill, for him thy Fire of Ordeal shall decide all things.

**14.** I would ask thee what the punishment shall be for him who seeks to further the power of a wicked ruler, the evil-doer who cannot find his living except by injury to the husbandman's cattle and men.

**15.** I would ask whether the man of right who strives to improve the dwellings through Truth and Good Thought, and himself lives righteously — whether he, O Mazda, hath earned a share in Thy Dominion.

**16.** The blessing which Zarathushtra prays Ahura Mazda shall give him, and all those who practice and learn the words and actions of the Good Religion — the Dominion through Good Thought, according to the Right — this I too would strive after.

**17.** He who advances Righteousness with full dedication of himself, to him shall be given a home in the pastures of Right and Good Thought. This, O Mazda, hast thou appointed in thy Dominion.

**18.** Let the seeker earnestly seek what is best for himself: to that which is worst let the deceiver go. Let the wise discern between these things. Mazda Ahura through his Kingdom is the bestower of joy to the righteous.

**19.** He, Zarathushtra, who according to the law of the primeval Being, is a friend to Mazda Ahura, he, O Mazda, proclaims thee the mightiest of all beings, the Lord of the Right.

**20.** To this end shall the seeker strive, shall the man of righteousness strive, and the man that knows to the fullest; for unto the end was the creation made good, and unto the end was the Right made strong. Through the Dominion was Happiness appointed.

**21.** Be ye thus determined, O ye of Good Thought. Mazda Ahura's hand and Righteousness shall save you. Let every man and woman of you bestow on me Good Thought.

**22.** That which the souls of the righteous desire, that do Thou reveal, O Mazda: the power of the Good Mind, the truth, the beneficence, the holy Armaiti. These are all Thy gifts, O Ahura.
