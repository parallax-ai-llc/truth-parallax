---
title: "Yasna 43 – Ushtavaiti Gatha: Bliss to Him"
chapter: 8
---

**1.** Bliss be to him — bliss shall be to whomsoever Mazda Ahura, of his will, shall give! I pray for the mighty help of the Lord of Wide Pastures — the aid of Mazda through Right I have obtained. May he who is the most bountiful bestow on me the blessing of Good Thought.

**2.** And the best of all things for me: he who teaches me the Right, the noblest — even so have I acknowledged Mazda Ahura through Good Thought; and through Piety may He teach me the Best Way.

**3.** He that shall choose for me the Right, him I shall choose. For my part I choose your holy Armaiti. Let it be so, O Mazda, that thou give through Right the Dominion to those who strive for its increase.

**4.** As the holy one I recognized thee, O Mazda Ahura, when Good Thought came to me and asked me: "Who art thou? To whom dost thou belong? By what sign wilt thou appoint the days for questioning about thy possessions and thyself?"

**5.** Then I said to him: "To the first part, I am Zarathushtra, a true foe to the Liar, to the utmost of my power, but a powerful support would I be to the Righteous, that I may attain the future things of the infinite Dominion, according as I praise and sing Thee, O Mazda."

**6.** As the holy one I recognized thee, O Mazda Ahura, when Good Thought came to me. To his question, "For what wilt thou decide?" I made reply: "At the offering of praise to thy Fire, I will bethink me of Right so long as I have power."

**7.** "Then show me the Right, upon whom I call." "Through Armaiti united with himself — ask us what thou wouldst wish to ask; for thy asking is as that of one mighty, since He that is able should make thee as a mighty one possessed of thy desire."

**8.** As the holy one I recognized thee, O Mazda Ahura, when Good Thought came to me, when the still small voice taught me to proclaim what is best: "Let not a man seek again and again to please the Liars, for they make all the righteous enemies."

**9.** So at the offering of praise to thy Fire I will think upon Right with the highest thought that is mine. While I have power and strength, so will I teach to strive after the Right.

**10.** As the holy one I recognized thee, O Mazda Ahura, when Good Thought came to me, when first by your words I was instructed. Shall it bring me suffering among men, my devotion to that which Thou didst declare the best?

**11.** "Yes, truly it shall come to pass." And Thou didst tell me, O Mazda, of the making of the Best Life; and of the creation Thou saidst: "He that does not come to it, upon him shall be woes at the end."

**12.** As the holy one I recognized thee, O Mazda Ahura, when Good Thought came to me. Arise and come to me, that I may hear Thy precepts. Through the fullness of Right bestow on me the gifts of Thy Grace.

**13.** That reward which Thou hast appointed for the two parties, O Mazda, through Thy Fire by decree ordained — that sign which shall be given to the souls of men — of woe to the Liar, of a blessing to the Righteous —

**14.** As the holy one I recognized thee, O Mazda Ahura, when Good Thought came to me, to learn the state of desire of each: "That which the righteous wishes shall be granted; that which the wicked desires shall never be fulfilled."

**15.** Mazda Ahura, through the Dominion which is thine, give us endurance. Give through Right and Good Thought the reward for Zarathushtra and his followers, that they may teach the truth according to Thy Word.

**16.** Zarathushtra himself, O Ahura, chooses each one of thy holiest Spirit, O Mazda. May Right be embodied and strong! May Piety abide in the Dominion of the sun! Through Good Thought let him give the reward of their deeds to men!
