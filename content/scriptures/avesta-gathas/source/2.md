---
title: "Yasna 29 – The Cry of the Soul of the Earth"
chapter: 2
---

**1.** Unto you the Soul of the Ox complained: "For whom did ye create me? Who fashioned me? Violence and rapine oppress me, and cruelty and tyranny. I have no other pastor than you: then appear to me with good pasturage."

**2.** Then the Creator of the Ox asked of the Right: "Hast thou a judge for the Ox, that ye may be able to appoint him zealous tendance as well as fodder? Whom do ye will to be his lord, who may drive back violence together with the followers of the Lie?"

**3.** To him the Right replied: "No helper is there for the Ox that is free from cruelty. Those yonder have no knowledge how right-doing act towards the lowly." The strongest of beings is he to whose help I come at his call.

**4.** Mazda knoweth best the purposes that have been wrought already by demons and by mortals, and that shall be wrought hereafter. He, Ahura, is the decider. So shall it be as he shall will.

**5.** So we two, my soul and the soul of the mother Ox, made our petition unto Ahura, beseeching Mazda with hands outstretched, pressing the Faithful One with questions: "Shall the righteous be overcome by the wicked? Shall destruction be to those who truly work?"

**6.** Then spoke Ahura Mazda himself, who knows the law with wisdom: "There is found no lord or judge according to the Right. For the Creator has fashioned thee for the cattle-tender and the farmer."

**7.** This ordinance about the fat of increase did Ahura Mazda, one in will with Right, create for cattle, and the milk for them that crave nourishment, by his command, the holy one.

**8.** "Whom hast thou, O Good Thought, among men, who may care for us two?" "I know but this one, Zarathushtra Spitama, the only one that has heard our teaching; he will make known our thoughts, O Mazda, and those of the Right. So let us bestow on him charm of speech."

**9.** Then the Ox-Soul lamented: "That I should have to be content with the powerless word of an impotent man for my protector, when I wish for one that commands mightily! When ever shall there be one who shall give him help of hand?"

**10.** Do ye, O Ahura, grant them strength, and O Right, and O Good Thought, that dominion, through which he could produce for the world good dwelling and peace. I know thee, O Mazda, as the first to accomplish this.

**11.** Where are Right and Good Thought and Dominion? So acknowledge me, O ye men, for instruction, O Mazda, for the great fellowship at the powerful support. Come now, O Ahura, to give us aid.
