---
title: "Yasna 46 – Zarathushtra's Lament"
chapter: 11
---

**1.** To what land shall I flee? Where bend my steps? I am thrust out from family and tribe; I have no favor from the village to which I would belong, nor from the wicked rulers of the country. How am I to please thee, O Mazda Ahura?

**2.** I know, O Mazda, why I am powerless: my cattle are few, and I have but a few followers. I cry unto thee; see thou to it, O Ahura, granting me support as a friend gives to friend. Teach me through the Right the acquisition of Good Thought.

**3.** When, O Mazda, shall the sunrisings come forth for the world's upholding of the Right through the powerful teachings of the wisdom of the future Deliverers? Who are they to whose help Good Thought shall come? For my part I choose thy teaching, O Ahura.

**4.** The Liar stays the supporter of Right from prospering the cattle in district and province, infamous that he is, repellent in his actions. Whosoever, O Mazda, robs him of dominion or of life, he walks foremost in the ways of the good doctrine.

**5.** He who being lord would offer protection to the man of the right against the oppressor — that lord, a wise man, shall serve as ruler. Through Good Thought shall come to him the lordship that comes from the Right.

**6.** And whoso injures him shall be the offspring of the House of the Lie. To thee, O Good Thought, I turn in gratitude and in joy. Mazda Ahura makes all things grow at His own will through His Dominion.

**7.** Who will give me protection, O Mazda, when the Liar threatens me with violence? Who but Thy Fire and Thy Thought, through whose working, O Ahura, the Right shall grow? Declare this faith to my conscience!

**8.** He that would destroy my possessions, let not his burning come to me! But let the consequence of his acts come back to himself, O Mazda, to injure his own possessions — not those of a different man. Let his own ruin come to him in full measure.

**9.** Who is that devoted man, who first will teach me the precious thing — to adore Thee, O Ahura, the Righteous One, as the First and as the Lord? What Thou didst declare through the Right and through Good Thought, that instruction let Thy word declare to me.

**10.** Whoever, man or woman, shall give me what Thou, O Mazda Ahura, knowest to be best in this life — reward for truth, dominion through Good Thought — and all whom I shall bring to worship such as you, with all these I will cross the Bridge of the Separator.

**11.** The Karapans and the Kavis yoked mankind with their evil actions to destroy life. Their own soul and their own self made them quake when they reached the Bridge of the Separator, guests for all time in the House of the Lie.

**12.** But when kinship and alliances of the brotherhood shall come together, according to the Right of the expected Dominion, then shall Armaiti be present with Good Thought. And for them let Mazda appoint in His sovereign authority the end that is His.

**13.** He who satisfies Zarathushtra Spitama with his friendship, this man is renowned and worthy of the Good Religion. Him will Mazda Ahura endow with life. Through Good Thought he shall increase the pastures. Him we account as thy best friend, O Right.

**14.** O Zarathushtra, what righteous man is thy friend for the great covenant? Who wills to have good fame? It is Kavi Vishtaspa at the assembly. Those whom thou dost gather in the House of Good Thought, them will I call together with the words of Mazda.

**15.** O ye Haechat-aspas, Spitamas, I will declare unto you the word, whereby the wise shall distinguish between right and wrong, that through these means ye may keep the Right.

**16.** Frashaostra, the Hvogva, go thou thither with those faithful whom we both desire to reach the blessed dwelling. Where Armaiti is united with Right and where the Dominion of Good Thought is found — this Mazda Ahura's chosen abode.

**17.** Where I shall sing thee songs of praise, O Jamaspa Hvogva, not the songs of one that pleases the Liar, but hymns that embody the praise of Right — so shall the singer win Good Thought, and through Good Thought the imperishable Dominion.

**18.** He that as ruler gives to the righteous man the better portion — through Good Thought, by exercise of duty and authority — and he that holds fast to the realm of Truth — he enters into the dwelling of Mazda, the dwelling of Good Thought.

**19.** Let no man of you hearken unto the word or command of the Liar; for he brings house and community, district and province into misery and destruction. Resist them with weapon and with the word of the Right!
