---
title: "Yasna 44 – Questions to Ahura Mazda"
chapter: 9
---

**1.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who is by generation the Father of the Right, at the first? Who determined the path of sun and stars? Who is it by whom the moon waxes and wanes again? This, O Mazda, and yet more, I am fain to know.

**2.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who upholds the earth beneath and the firmament from falling? Who the waters and the plants? Who yoked swiftness to winds and clouds? Who is, O Mazda, Creator of Good Thought?

**3.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. What artist made light and darkness? What artist made sleep and waking? Who made morning, noon, and night, that call the understanding man to his duty?

**4.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who created together with Dominion the precious Piety? Who made by wisdom the son obedient to his father? I strive to recognize by these things thee, O Mazda, Creator of all things through the holy Spirit.

**5.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. What was the first best word that thou didst speak? What was the first declaration of Right? With what thought and what word didst Thou bring about creation? What was the beginning of the Best Life?

**6.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who created Piety, the blessed, together with Dominion? Who through wisdom made the son respectful in his listening to the father? With these questions, O Mazda, do I approach Thee, O bountiful Spirit, maker of all things.

**7.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. These things I would learn, to what region of Thy dominion shall I turn for refuge? Whither shall I go to serve Thee? Grant unto me the blessed way of the Right and the gift of Good Thought.

**8.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Shall I indeed, O Right, earn that reward, even ten mares with a stallion and a camel, which was promised to me, O Mazda, as well as through thee the future gift of Welfare and Immortality?

**9.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. He that will not give the reward to him that earned it, he that speaks the word of the Lie — what shall his penalty be even now? For I know his punishment at the last.

**10.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. How in the beginning, when the world was created, was the punishment ordained for the Daevas and those who followed them? For at the consummation they shall be handed over to the Lie.

**11.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Whether Piety will extend to those to whom thy Religion shall be proclaimed? I was ordained at the first by thee; all others I look upon with hatred of spirit.

**12.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who among those with whom I would speak is a righteous man, and who is a Liar? On which side is the enemy? Is it not the Liar that thwarts the efforts of the holy?

**13.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. How shall we drive the Lie from us, casting it down to those who, full of disobedience, will not strive after fellowship with Right, nor trouble themselves with the counsel of Good Thought?

**14.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. How shall I deliver the Lie into the hands of the Right, to destroy it according to the precepts of Thy teaching, to work a mighty destruction among the Liars, and to bring upon them torments and woe?

**15.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. If through the Right Thou hast the power — to protect him who appeals to Thee for aid — by what sign shall this come to pass? What is the token that shall be given? An avenger shall appear for me, at the time of the great trial.

**16.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who is victorious to protect by Thy doctrine all that are? By vision assure me how to set up the judge that heals the world. Then let him have Obedience coming with Good Thought unto every man whom thou dost please, O Mazda.

**17.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Shall I indeed, O Right, have the power to protect myself against my adversary, and thereby, through Thy teaching, to be mighty? Are the words well said that I have spoken? Give us then, O Mazda, through Thy Holy Spirit, the blessings of Good Thought.

**18.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Whether through Right I may win that mighty Dominion, through whose blessings one may hold the Two Liars fast. Let this mightiness come to me through Good Thought and through the doctrines of Truth.

**19.** This I ask Thee, tell me truly, Ahura. Who through righteousness gained the reward of Welfare and Immortality? Let Thy prophet declare the word that brings the blessing, O Mazda; may we through Right attain the bliss of the Best Life.

**20.** He that does not grant to the righteous man the reward of Good Thought, he that rejects the ordinances of the Right — him shall Mazda bring to justice at the Bridge. Of such the souls shall assuredly go down to the House of the Lie.
