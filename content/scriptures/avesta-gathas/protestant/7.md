## Protestant Christian Reading

Protestant Christian reading of Yasna 34 — Devotion to Ahura identifies theological motifs across its 15 verses that resonate with biblical revelation while maintaining awareness of fundamental differences. The hymn's emphasis on the sovereignty of a single righteous God, the moral accountability of human beings, and the eschatological triumph of good over evil invites comparison with the prophetic books of the Hebrew Bible and the apocalyptic literature of the New Testament.

From a Protestant perspective, the Gathic understanding of asha (truth/righteousness) as both a cosmic principle and an ethical imperative bears comparison with the biblical concept of *tsedaqah* (righteousness) and the Pauline doctrine of justification. Zarathustra's personal encounter with Ahura Mazda — his calling, his struggles against opposition, and his confidence in divine vindication — echoes the prophetic experience described in Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel.

Protestant theologians have variously interpreted these parallels as evidence of general revelation, common grace, or the universal human capacity to apprehend aspects of divine truth. The Gathas remain a valuable resource for Protestant engagement with questions of religious pluralism, the history of monotheism, and the relationship between special and general revelation.
