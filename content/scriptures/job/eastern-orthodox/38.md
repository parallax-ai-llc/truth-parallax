## God Speaks from the Whirlwind

The divine speeches from the whirlwind represent, in Orthodox theology, a supreme theophany — God's direct self-revelation to a human person. Orthodox tradition distinguishes between God's unknowable essence and His manifest energies; what Job encounters in the whirlwind is not the divine essence (which remains forever inaccessible) but the overwhelming presence of God's uncreated energies. "Where were you when I laid the earth's foundation?" (38:4).

The cascade of divine questions — about creation's foundations (38:4-7), the sea's boundaries (38:8-11), light and darkness (38:19-21), the constellations (38:31-33) — reveals what Orthodox theology calls the *logoi* of creation: the divine intentions embedded in every created thing. St. Maximus the Confessor taught that each creature contains a *logos* — a word of God — and that the contemplative who perceives these logoi is drawn into communion with the Logos Himself. God's questions invite Job into precisely this contemplative vision.

Orthodox liturgical theology reads Job 38 as a key text for the theology of creation celebrated in the Orthodox liturgical cycle. The Great Vespers of creation, the blessing of waters, and the sanctification of the cosmos through liturgical prayer all presuppose the vision of Job 38: a creation that is not autonomous but is sustained, governed, and permeated by divine wisdom and power at every moment.
