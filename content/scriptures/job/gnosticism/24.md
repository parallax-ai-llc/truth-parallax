## The Demiurge's Failed Governance

Job 24's catalogue of injustice — the wicked exploiting the poor, moving boundaries, seizing orphans' animals (24:2-4) — is, for the Gnostic reader, evidence that the material world is governed by an incompetent or malevolent deity. The question "Why does the Almighty not set times for judgment?" (24:1) receives a devastating Gnostic answer: because the ruler of this world is not the true God, and his justice is a sham.

The vivid descriptions of suffering — the hungry gleaning bare fields, the naked shivering without covering, the orphan snatched from the breast (24:6-9) — confirm the Gnostic diagnosis of material existence as fundamentally disordered. The *Testimony of Truth* (NHC IX,3) portrays the material world as a place where suffering is structural, not accidental. The Demiurge, who fashioned this world from ignorance and desire, cannot produce a just cosmos because justice is not within his nature.

For the Gnostic, Job's outrage is entirely justified but misdirected: he protests to the very deity who is responsible for the injustice. The true God — the Monad, the invisible Spirit, the Father of the Pleroma — does not govern the material world and is not implicated in its failures. Liberation comes not through reforming the Demiurge's system (which is irreformable) but through awakening to the knowledge that one's true home lies beyond this cosmos entirely.
