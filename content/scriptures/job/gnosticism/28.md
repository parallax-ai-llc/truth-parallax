## Sophia Hidden and Revealed

The Wisdom Poem of Job 28 holds extraordinary significance for Gnostic interpretation, given the central role of Sophia (Wisdom) in Gnostic cosmology. The poem's declaration that wisdom is hidden from all living things and "concealed from the birds of the sky" (28:21) mirrors the Gnostic myth of Sophia's fall and concealment. In Valentinian cosmology, Sophia fell from the Pleroma and became trapped in the lower world — hidden, yearning, and inaccessible to those who seek her through material means.

The mining metaphor (28:1-11) illustrates the futility of seeking Sophia through worldly knowledge. Humans can penetrate rock and discover gold, but they cannot discover wisdom — because wisdom is not a material substance to be extracted but a spiritual reality to be received. Gnostic epistemology insists that gnosis comes not through intellectual effort but through revelation: the Savior descends from the Pleroma to awaken the sleeping sparks and reunite them with Sophia.

The concluding verse — "The fear of the Lord, that is wisdom" (28:28) — is read by Gnostic interpreters as an orthodox interpolation that the deeper reading must transcend. Fear of the Lord (i.e., the Demiurge) is not wisdom but the beginning of bondage. True wisdom — gnosis — is the recognition that the "Lord" of this cosmos is not the true God, and that Sophia herself awaits liberation from her imprisonment in matter, a liberation that occurs through the pneumatic's awakening.
