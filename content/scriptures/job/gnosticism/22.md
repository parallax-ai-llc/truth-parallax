## Eliphaz as Archonic Voice

Gnostic interpretation reads Eliphaz's false accusations in Job 22 as the voice of the archons — the cosmic rulers who enforce the Demiurge's flawed system of retributive justice. His fabricated charges (22:6-9) represent the archonic strategy of guilt: keeping the divine spark trapped in material existence by convincing the pneumatic (spiritual) person that they deserve their suffering. The Gnostic recognizes this manipulation for what it is — a tool of cosmic control.

Eliphaz's exhortation to "agree with God and be at peace" (22:21) is, in Gnostic reading, an invitation to submit to the Demiurge's authority. The *Apocryphon of John* (NHC II,1) describes the archons as demanding worship and obedience from the human beings they have imprisoned. Eliphaz functions as a mouthpiece for this system — urging Job to accept the Demiurge's verdict, confess sins he did not commit, and submit to a cosmic order that is fundamentally unjust.

The Gnostic reader identifies with Job's refusal to comply. His rejection of Eliphaz's false accusations is an act of gnosis — the pneumatic recognition that the world's moral accounting is rigged by inferior powers. True innocence cannot be measured by the Demiurge's standards, and true liberation comes not through submission to the cosmic order but through knowledge of the Pleroma (divine fullness) beyond it.
