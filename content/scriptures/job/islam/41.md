## Leviathan

The description of Leviathan in Job 41 — fire-breathing, armored, fearless (41:18-21, 33) — demonstrates a principle central to Islamic theology: no creature, however powerful, stands outside Allah's sovereignty. The Quran declares: "To Allah belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth and whatever is within them, and He is over all things competent" (5:120). Leviathan, terrifying as it is, remains a creature — and the Creator governs all creatures.

Islamic eschatology provides a framework for reading the cosmic significance of Leviathan. The Quran and hadith describe cosmic events at the end of time — the sun rising in the west, the emergence of the Beast (*Dabbat al-Ard*, 27:82), the sounding of the trumpet — that reveal Allah's absolute power over all forces of creation and chaos. Leviathan, in this reading, belongs to the category of awesome creatures that demonstrate the gap between human power and divine sovereignty.

The theological point for the believer is *tawakkul* (trust): if Allah governs Leviathan, He can be trusted with every aspect of the believer's life. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) taught: "If the whole nation were to gather together to benefit you, they would not benefit you except with something that Allah has already written for you; and if they were to gather together to harm you, they would not harm you except with something that Allah has already written against you" (Tirmidhi). Leviathan, for all its terror, operates within Allah's decree.
