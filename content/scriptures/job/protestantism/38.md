## God Speaks from the Whirlwind

Job 38 is one of the most momentous passages in all of Scripture. God finally answers — not with an explanation of Job's suffering, but with a torrent of questions: "Where were you when I laid the earth's foundation?" (38:4). Protestant theology recognizes that God's response does not address the problem of suffering directly but transcends it by revealing the incomprehensible scope of His creative wisdom.

The divine questions cascade through the foundations of the earth (38:4-7), the sea (38:8-11), the dawn (38:12-15), the depths of Sheol (38:16-18), light and darkness (38:19-21), snow and hail (38:22-23), weather (38:24-30), and the constellations (38:31-33). Protestant interpreters note that every question is unanswerable — not because Job is ignorant but because these are things no human can know. The effect is not humiliation but reorientation.

The Reformation tradition reads Job 38 as the definitive statement on the limits of natural theology. Creation reveals God's power and wisdom (Romans 1:20), but it does not explain His particular purposes in any individual's suffering. Calvin found in this chapter confirmation that God's sovereignty is not arbitrary but operates through a wisdom so vast that human categories of justice and injustice cannot contain it. The God who speaks here is not defending Himself but inviting Job into wonder.
