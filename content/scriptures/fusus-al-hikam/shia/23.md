Shia interpretation of Fusus al-Hikam chapter 23, "The Bezel of Wisdom of Beneficence in the Word of Luqman," reads the prophetic wisdom through the lens of walaya (spiritual authority) and the esoteric dimensions of revelation. For Shia readers, the prophet discussed in this bezel embodies not only a particular divine wisdom but also a mode of spiritual authority that continues through the line of the Imams. The batin (inner meaning) of each prophetic mission is precisely what the Imams inherit and transmit.

The metaphysical framework of this bezel — particularly its treatment of divine self-disclosure (tajalli) and the Perfect Human (al-insan al-kamil) — resonates with the Shia understanding that in every age there must be a living proof (hujja) of God who serves as the mirror of divine attributes. Shia commentators such as Haydar Amuli and Mulla Sadra have read this chapter as confirming the metaphysical necessity of the Imamate.
