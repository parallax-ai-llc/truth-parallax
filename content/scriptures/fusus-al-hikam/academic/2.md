Academic analysis of Fusus al-Hikam chapter 2, "The Bezel of Wisdom of Breath in the Word of Seth," examines the bezel's metaphysical arguments, its place within Ibn Arabi's philosophical system, and its extensive commentary tradition. Scholars trace the chapter's sources in Quranic exegesis, Hadith, and earlier Sufi and philosophical literature, analyzing how Ibn Arabi transforms these materials into a distinctive metaphysical vision.

The chapter has been the subject of detailed study in terms of its logical structure, its relationship to parallel passages in the Futuhat al-Makkiyya, and its reception by commentators from al-Qashani and Jami to modern interpreters. Comparative philosophers have noted parallels with Neoplatonic, Vedantic, and Kabbalistic thought, while historians of religion have explored the chapter's implications for understanding the relationship between prophetic typology and metaphysical speculation.
