## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Intimacy in the Word of Elijah

**1.** Elijah is the one who was divided between the animal and the spiritual, between the body and the angel. He is the station of the isthmus.

**2.** His wisdom is the wisdom of intimacy—the closeness to God that comes from occupying the middle ground between heaven and earth.

**3.** Elijah was taken up and given the nature of the angels, yet he retained his human nature. He is both, simultaneously—a sign that the human can be angelic without ceasing to be human.

**4.** The intimate of God is one who is at home in both worlds. He walks on earth but his heart is in heaven. He eats and drinks but his sustenance is the remembrance of God.

**5.** Intimacy with God is not a reward for righteousness—it is the natural state of the soul. The soul was intimate with God before it entered the body, and it yearns to return to that intimacy.

**6.** The one who achieves intimacy in this life has remembered what the soul forgot when it descended into the world.

**7.** Elijah challenged the priests of Baal and called fire from heaven. The fire came because Elijah was intimate with the source of fire. The one who is intimate with the source can call upon the source.

**8.** But the greater fire is the fire of love in the heart, which burns without consuming, which illuminates without blinding, which warms without scorching.

**9.** The intimate of God sees God in the fire, in the wind, in the still small voice. He does not limit God to any single manifestation.

**10.** Elijah was fed by ravens in the wilderness. The intimate of God is sustained by means that the world considers impossible. His provision comes from the Unseen.

**11.** The wisdom of intimacy is that the closer you draw to God, the more God draws to you. "Draw near to Me a hand's span, and I will draw near to you an arm's length."

**12.** This drawing near has no end, for the Infinite can always be approached more closely. The journey into intimacy is infinite.

**13.** Elijah is said to still be alive, wandering the earth. This is because intimacy with God is life that does not end. The intimate does not die—he is translated from one mode of existence to another.

**14.** The body may dissolve, but the intimacy remains, for intimacy is a relationship of the spirit, not of the flesh.

**15.** The wisdom of Elijah is that the human being is the bridge between heaven and earth, and that he who walks this bridge with awareness walks in the company of God.
