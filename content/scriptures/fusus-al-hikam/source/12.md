## The Bezel of the Wisdom of the Heart in the Word of Shu'ayb

**1.** Shu'ayb's wisdom is the wisdom of the heart, for the heart is the seat of knowledge of God. Not the physical heart but the spiritual heart—the center of being.

**2.** Shu'ayb said to his people: "Give full measure and full weight, and do not diminish the things of the people." This outward teaching contains an inward meaning.

**3.** The measure is the measure of the heart. When you give full measure, you give with the fullness of the heart. When you diminish the measure, you diminish the heart.

**4.** The heart of the knower is vast enough to contain all the Names of God. The Throne of God, which encompasses the heavens and the earth, does not contain God—but the heart of the faithful servant contains Him.

**5.** This is the supreme paradox: that the Infinite, which the heavens and the earth cannot contain, is contained in the human heart.

**6.** The heart is the isthmus between the visible and the invisible, between the temporal and the eternal, between the creature and the Creator.

**7.** Shu'ayb warned against fraud because fraud is the corruption of the heart. When the external measure is false, it is because the internal measure—the heart—has been corrupted.

**8.** The heart has two eyes: one that sees the visible world and one that sees the invisible. Most people use only the first eye. The saints use both.

**9.** The heart expands and contracts. When it expands, it takes in the whole universe. When it contracts, it is narrower than the eye of a needle.

**10.** The cause of expansion is remembrance of God. The cause of contraction is forgetfulness of God. The heart that remembers is always expanding.

**11.** God is said to be in the heart, not because He is limited to it, but because the heart is the only locus that can receive His self-disclosure fully.

**12.** The angels receive the disclosure of particular Names. The heavens receive the disclosure of majesty. But the heart receives the disclosure of all Names simultaneously.

**13.** This is why the human being is the vicegerent of God on earth—not because of his body or his mind, but because of his heart.

**14.** The wisdom of Shu'ayb is to keep the heart pure and the measure true, for the one who cheats in the marketplace cheats his own heart.

**15.** Guard your heart above all things, for from it flow the springs of life. What enters the heart shapes the world, and what the heart contains, the world reflects.
