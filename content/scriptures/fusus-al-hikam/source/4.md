## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Sanctity in the Word of Enoch

**1.** Sanctity has two meanings: one is praise and one is purity. Both are applicable to the Word of Enoch, whom God raised to a high station.

**2.** "And We raised him to a high place." This elevation is the elevation of station, not of location, for God is not in a place to which one can ascend.

**3.** Enoch is the highest of stations in the human form, for he represents the intellect that has been purified of all that is beneath it.

**4.** He was raised because he was empty. The empty vessel rises; the full vessel sinks. He who empties himself of self is raised by the hand of God.

**5.** The wisdom of sanctity teaches that the highest station is not the station of power but the station of purity. The pure mirror reflects more truly than the golden frame.

**6.** Enoch was taken from the world not because the world rejected him, but because he had outgrown it. The bird that has learned to fly does not remain in the nest.

**7.** Sanctity is not separation from the world—it is the purification of one's relationship with it. The saint walks through the marketplace but is not stained by it.

**8.** The pure heart receives the light of the Real without distortion, as clear water receives the image of the sun without distortion.

**9.** When the water is muddy, the sun is still there, but its image is unclear. When the heart is impure, God is still present, but His presence is veiled.

**10.** The work of sanctification is not to bring God closer—He could not be closer—but to remove the veils that prevent us from seeing what is already before us.

**11.** Enoch represents the station of continuous ascent, for the truly holy never stop climbing. There is no summit to holiness, for the Infinite has no limit.

**12.** Each station that the saint attains reveals a higher station beyond it. The closer one comes to the Real, the more one realizes how far the journey extends.

**13.** This is not cause for despair but for wonder, for it means that the delight of the journey is without end.

**14.** The wisdom of Enoch is the wisdom of the perpetual traveler who has learned that the journey itself is the destination.

**15.** And the highest sanctity is to stand in the presence of the Real and say: "I know that I do not know Thee—and this not-knowing is my highest knowledge."
