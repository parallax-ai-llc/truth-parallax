## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Divinity in the Word of Adam

**1.** When the Real willed, in respect of His Beautiful Names—which are beyond enumeration—that their essences should be seen, or if you wish you may say that He willed to see His own Essence in a comprehensive being that would encompass the whole affair—

**2.** Being qualified by existence, through which His secret would be manifested to Himself—

**3.** For the seeing of a thing by itself is not the same as its seeing itself in another, as in a mirror, for there it manifests itself in a form bestowed by the place of beholding.

**4.** And this would not be without the existence of the place of beholding, and without his being manifested in it.

**5.** The Real gave existence to the entire world as a formless thing without spirit, and it was like an unpolished mirror.

**6.** It is in the nature of the Divine Decree that He does not prepare a locus without that locus receiving a divine spirit, which is expressed by the breathing into it.

**7.** This is nothing other than the coming forth of the hidden disposition to receive the outpouring of the perpetual Self-revelation, which has never ceased and never will cease.

**8.** Thus the receptacle is nothing other than the overflow itself, and the overflow has no end.

**9.** So Adam was the very eye of the world, and he is to the world what the pupil is to the eye.

**10.** Through the pupil comes the act of seeing, which is called sight. That is why he was called Man, for through him the Real looks upon His creation and bestows mercy upon it.

**11.** He is the human being, both originated in time and eternal without beginning. He is the perpetual and everlasting Word.

**12.** He is the dividing line between the world and the Real. He is the isthmus between the two seas.

**13.** Through him, God knows the world, for Adam is to the world what the bezel is to the ring—the place of the inscription and the sign.

**14.** God named him the vicegerent because he guards the creation, as a vicegerent guards the kingdom.

**15.** The angels could not see the rank of Adam, for they saw only their own perfection and did not recognize the perfection that encompasses all perfections.

**16.** They said: "Will You place upon it one who causes corruption therein and sheds blood, while we glorify Your praise and sanctify You?"

**17.** They did not know that through this being, the Real would manifest every Name, including Names unknown to the angels themselves.

**18.** So Adam was taught all the Names, and the angels were not given this knowledge, for they were limited to the Names they manifested, while Adam encompassed them all.
