## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Destiny in the Word of Ezra

**1.** The wisdom of destiny in the Word of Ezra concerns the knowledge of how God brings the dead to life and how the realities of things are restored after their dissolution.

**2.** Ezra passed by a village that was in ruins, its roofs caved in, and said: "How shall God give life to this after its death?" God caused him to die for a hundred years, then raised him.

**3.** He said: "How long have you tarried?" Ezra replied: "A day or part of a day." God said: "No, you have tarried a hundred years."

**4.** Look at your food and drink—they have not spoiled. But look at your donkey. This is so that We may make of you a sign for the people.

**5.** The food did not change because it was under the protection of the Divine Will. The donkey decayed because it was under the law of nature. Both laws are from God.

**6.** This teaches that God has two ways of acting: the way of custom, which is the law of nature, and the way of breaking custom, which is the miracle.

**7.** The wise man does not deny either way, for both are expressions of the same Power. The one who denies miracles has limited God; the one who denies natural law has denied God's wisdom.

**8.** Destiny is not blind fate—it is the expression of Divine Wisdom in the movement of time. Each moment is decreed, and each decree contains a wisdom.

**9.** Ezra's sleep of a hundred years was not a punishment—it was a teaching. He asked a question, and God answered him not with words but with experience.

**10.** The greatest teacher is direct experience. A thousand lectures on death cannot teach what one taste of death teaches.

**11.** God reassembled the bones of the donkey before Ezra's eyes—bone joining bone, flesh covering bone, skin covering flesh—until the donkey stood alive again.

**12.** This is the teaching: nothing that God has created is ever truly lost. The forms dissolve, but the realities are preserved in the knowledge of God, and He can reassemble them at will.

**13.** Do not grieve for what has passed, for it is stored in the treasury of God. And do not fear for what is to come, for it too is in His hand.

**14.** Destiny is the unfolding of what God already knows. To the servant, it appears as time. To God, it is a single moment.

**15.** The wisdom of Ezra is the wisdom of trust in the unfolding: that what appears as death is sleep, and what appears as destruction is preservation, and what appears as loss is gain in a form you do not yet recognize.
