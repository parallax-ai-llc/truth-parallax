## The Bezel of the Wisdom of the Spirit in the Word of Jacob

**1.** Jacob's wisdom is the wisdom of the spirit that sees beyond the veil of the senses. He knew Joseph was alive when all evidence said otherwise.

**2.** When his sons brought Joseph's shirt stained with false blood, Jacob said: "Your souls have tempted you to something. So patience is most fitting."

**3.** He did not say: "Joseph is dead." He said: "Patience." For the spirit within him knew what the senses did not.

**4.** The wisdom of the spirit is the knowledge that comes not through the eyes or ears but through the heart's direct perception of the Real.

**5.** Jacob wept until he was blind, and they said: "You will destroy yourself with grief." He replied: "I only complain of my grief and sorrow to God."

**6.** His blindness was not a punishment—it was the closing of the outward eye so that the inward eye could see more clearly.

**7.** When the shirt of Joseph was later cast upon his face, his sight returned. The same shirt that was once the instrument of deception became the instrument of revelation.

**8.** This teaches us that every trial contains within it the seed of its own resolution. The very thing that blinded Jacob restored his sight.

**9.** The spirit knows things that the mind cannot fathom. Jacob knew from the scent of the shirt that Joseph lived. This knowing was not rational—it was spiritual.

**10.** The fragrance of the Beloved carries across deserts and oceans. The spirit that loves deeply can detect this fragrance when all the senses have failed.

**11.** Jacob said: "I perceive the scent of Joseph, even if you think me senile." The people of reason laughed, but the people of the spirit understood.

**12.** There is a knowing that is beyond proof, beyond evidence, beyond the categories of the mind. It is the knowing of the lover, the knowing of the parent, the knowing of the prophet.

**13.** This knowing is never wrong, though it may appear wrong to the world. Jacob was right, and the world was wrong.

**14.** The spirit sees the whole when the senses see only the part. It sees the end when the mind sees only the beginning.

**15.** Trust the spirit over the senses, and the heart over the mind, for the spirit was with God before it entered the body, and it remembers what the mind has forgotten.
