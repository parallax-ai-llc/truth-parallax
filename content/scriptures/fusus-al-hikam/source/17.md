## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Existence in the Word of David

**1.** David was given the wisdom of existence, and his sign was that iron became soft in his hands. He made coats of mail, and God said: "Make wide the links."

**2.** The softening of iron is the softening of the hard heart. What is harder than iron, and what is harder than the human heart when it is turned away from God?

**3.** But in the hand of the prophet, even iron yields. In the hand of grace, even the hardest heart melts.

**4.** David was given a beautiful voice, and when he sang the Psalms, the mountains and the birds sang with him. This is because the whole of creation is engaged in the praise of God, and David's voice awakened what was already there.

**5.** The mountains did not learn a new song—they remembered the song they had always been singing. David's voice was the reminder that brought the silent praise into the realm of hearing.

**6.** Existence itself is praise. Every atom that exists praises God by the very fact of its existence. The stone praises, the tree praises, the river praises—but most people do not understand their praise.

**7.** David understood the praise of all things because he himself was pure praise. When the instrument is properly tuned, it hears the tuning of all other instruments.

**8.** God made David a vicegerent on earth and said: "Judge between people with truth." The vicegerent judges with the judgment of God, not with his own opinion.

**9.** David's sin with Bathsheba was a descent into the world of nature, and his repentance was an ascent back to the world of the spirit. The prophet sins differently from ordinary men, for even his sin contains a wisdom.

**10.** Through his repentance, David became the model of the repentant soul, and his Psalms became the voice of every soul that turns back to God.

**11.** The wisdom of existence is that everything that exists is a sign of the Existent, and every sign points to the One who placed it.

**12.** The iron that softens, the mountain that sings, the bird that joins in praise—all bear witness that existence is not random but intentional, not silent but eloquent.

**13.** David's coats of mail are a metaphor: each link connects to every other link, forming a whole that is stronger than any individual part. So it is with creation: each creature is linked to every other, forming a fabric of praise.

**14.** Make the links wide, said God—that is, let your praise encompass all things, exclude nothing, for everything that exists deserves to be included in the song.

**15.** The wisdom of David is the wisdom of praise, which is the highest form of knowledge, for to truly praise God one must truly know Him, and to truly know Him one must see Him in all things.
