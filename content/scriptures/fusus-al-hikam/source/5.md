## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Ecstatic Love in the Word of Abraham

**1.** Abraham was called the Friend of God, and the Friend is the one in whom the Beloved has permeated every atom, so that there is no part of the Friend that is not the Beloved.

**2.** Friendship in the language of the Real means that the dye of the Beloved has so thoroughly permeated the lover that the lover's own color has disappeared entirely.

**3.** It is said of a liquid that permeates a substance that it has become its friend, meaning it has penetrated every part of it. So the Real has permeated the essence of Abraham until nothing of Abraham remained that was not God.

**4.** Abraham was tested by fire, and the fire became cool and a place of safety. This is because the friend of God is not burned by the fire of the world—the fire recognizes the Beloved within him and withdraws.

**5.** When Nimrod cast Abraham into the fire, the fire said: "I cannot burn him, for there is nothing in him that is not my Lord."

**6.** The wisdom of ecstatic love is this: that the lover becomes so transparent that the Beloved shines through him without obstruction.

**7.** Abraham's love was tested again when he was commanded to sacrifice his son. He obeyed, not because he did not love his son, but because his love for God had permeated even his love for his son.

**8.** He was willing to give up everything—even the dearest thing—because the Friend had left no room in him for attachment to anything other than the Beloved.

**9.** The knife did not cut, for the Real will not allow His lover to suffer loss. What appears as sacrifice is in truth a gift: the ram appeared, and the son was returned.

**10.** This is the paradox of divine love: the more you give, the more you receive. The more you surrender, the more you are given.

**11.** Abraham built the Kaaba, and in building it, he built a house for the Beloved in the world. But the true Kaaba is the heart, and the true circumambulation is the turning of the heart around its Lord.

**12.** The physical Kaaba is a stone in the desert. The spiritual Kaaba is the heart of the knower. Both are houses of God, but the heart is the greater house.

**13.** Abraham's hospitality was legendary—he would not eat unless he had a guest. This is because the friend sees the Beloved in every face and serves every guest as if serving God.

**14.** The wisdom of Abraham teaches that love is not an emotion but a state of being. It is not something you feel—it is something you become.

**15.** When love has fully permeated you, you do not say "I love God"—you say nothing, for the one who would say it has been annihilated in the One he loves.
