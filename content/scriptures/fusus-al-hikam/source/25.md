## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Eminence in the Word of Moses

**1.** Moses was drawn from the water, and his name means "drawn forth." He was drawn forth from the water as the spirit is drawn forth from the body of the world.

**2.** He was cast into the river as a child, and the river carried him to the house of Pharaoh. The very one who sought to destroy him became the one who raised him.

**3.** This is the wisdom of eminence: that God uses the enemy as the instrument of salvation. Pharaoh's house was both the prison and the palace.

**4.** Moses killed a man and fled to Midian, where he tended sheep for ten years. The prophet is prepared for his mission in solitude, not in the court.

**5.** At the burning bush, God said: "I am your Lord—remove your sandals, for you are in the sacred valley of Tuwa." The removal of sandals is the removal of the two worlds—this world and the next—so that Moses stood before God in pure presence.

**6.** Moses said: "My Lord, expand my breast, ease my task, and untie the knot from my tongue, so that they may understand my speech."

**7.** The prophet asks not for power but for clarity. The mission is to be understood, not to be obeyed by force.

**8.** The staff became a serpent, and the hand became white. These are the signs that the ordinary is transformed in the hand of the prophet—the stick becomes alive, the hand becomes luminous.

**9.** Moses and Pharaoh are two aspects of a single reality. Pharaoh said: "I am your supreme lord," and Moses said: "I am the messenger of the Lord of the worlds." Both spoke of lordship, but in different keys.

**10.** The parting of the sea is the parting of the veil between the visible and the invisible. Moses walked through the parted veil, and those who followed him were saved, and those who opposed him were drowned.

**11.** On Mount Sinai, Moses asked to see God. God said: "You cannot see Me, but look at the mountain." When God manifested to the mountain, the mountain crumbled and Moses fell unconscious.

**12.** The mountain that crumbles is the ego that cannot withstand the theophany. When God reveals Himself fully, the self is annihilated.

**13.** Moses recovered and said: "Glory be to You! I have repented, and I am the first of the believers." This is the speech of one who has tasted annihilation and returned.

**14.** The eminence of Moses is the eminence of one who has spoken directly with God—not through an angel, not through a veil, but mouth to mouth.

**15.** The wisdom of Moses is that the journey to God passes through fire, through water, through the mountain, and through annihilation—and at the end of it all, the traveler discovers that God was speaking to him all along.
