## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Exaltation in the Word of Ishmael

**1.** Ishmael was true to his promise, and God exalted him because of this truthfulness. For truthfulness is the foundation upon which all spiritual stations are built.

**2.** He used to enjoin upon his family prayer and almsgiving, and he was acceptable in the sight of his Lord. His acceptability came from his sincerity.

**3.** Ishmael was a messenger and a prophet. The messenger carries a message from the Real to creation; the prophet carries a message from creation to the Real. Ishmael was both.

**4.** The wisdom of exaltation is that one is exalted not by one's own effort but by the grace of the Exalter. The one who exalts himself is lowered; the one who is exalted by God cannot be lowered.

**5.** Ishmael is the father of the Arabs, and from his line came the Seal of the Prophets. This is his exaltation in the world of form.

**6.** But his exaltation in the world of meaning is his truthfulness, for truthfulness is the mirror in which the Real sees Himself without distortion.

**7.** A lying mirror shows a false image. A truthful mirror shows things as they are. The truthful servant is the mirror of God.

**8.** Ishmael was placed in the desert, and from the barren sand sprang the well of Zamzam. This is the teaching: where there is truthfulness, even the desert gives water.

**9.** The dry land of the soul, when watered by truthfulness, brings forth springs that never run dry.

**10.** To be true to one's promise is to be true to one's own nature, for the soul's deepest nature is truth. The liar is at war with himself; the truthful man is at peace.

**11.** The exaltation of Ishmael is the exaltation of every soul that chooses truth over comfort, sincerity over show, and the Real over the apparent.

**12.** God says: "Be true, and I will exalt you." This is not a transaction—it is a law of the spirit, as certain as the law of gravity.

**13.** The stone that is thrown upward must come down. But the soul that is truthful rises and does not fall, for it has found its natural place.

**14.** Ishmael submitted to the knife of his father, and in that submission, he was exalted above all who resist. For resistance is the weight that keeps the soul earthbound.

**15.** The wisdom of exaltation is surrender. Not the surrender of the weak, but the surrender of the strong who knows that there is a will greater than his own.
