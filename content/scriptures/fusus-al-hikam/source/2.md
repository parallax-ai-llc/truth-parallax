## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Breath in the Word of Seth

**1.** Know that the gifts and favors that become manifest in the world of things, by the hand of servants or otherwise, are of two kinds in the estimation of the people of truth.

**2.** The first kind is the gift of the Essence, and the second is the gift of the Names. And their distinction is known to the people of taste.

**3.** An essential gift is that which comes without asking, without any preparedness on the part of the receiver. Indeed, his preparedness for receiving it is itself an essential gift.

**4.** Every gift that is obtained through asking is a gift of the Names, not of the Essence. For asking is itself a cause, and whatever results from a cause is attributed to that cause.

**5.** The gift of the Essence comes from nowhere, by nothing, through nothing. It is the gift of pure Grace.

**6.** Seth means "the gift of God," and he was called this because he was a pure gift, given to Adam without Adam's asking.

**7.** Indeed, Adam himself was an essential gift, for God created him without any prior request from creation. No one asked for Adam; he was the expression of the Divine Will manifesting without cause.

**8.** The breath that Seth represents is the breath of mercy. It flows through creation as the breath flows through the body, giving life to every limb.

**9.** Without this breath, the world would be a dead form—a body without a soul. It is the breath of the Real that animates all things.

**10.** And every person who breathes does so by this Divine Breath, whether he knows it or not. The breath of the saint and the breath of the sinner are both carried upon the same wind.

**11.** The distinction lies not in the breath itself but in the awareness of the one who breathes. He who breathes consciously breathes with God; he who breathes unconsciously breathes with forgetfulness.

**12.** The wisdom of breath teaches us that all gifts are ultimately from the Essence, for even the act of asking is itself a gift that was given before the asking.

**13.** The seeker did not choose to seek—the seeking was placed in him. The questioner did not create his question—the question was breathed into him.

**14.** When you understand this, you understand that there is nothing to ask for, because everything that is yours has already been given.

**15.** This is the secret of Seth: that grace precedes effort, that the gift precedes the request, that the answer precedes the question.
