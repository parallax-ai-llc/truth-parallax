## The Bezel of the Wisdom of the Opening in the Word of Salih

**1.** Salih was sent to the people of Thamud, and his sign was the she-camel that emerged from the rock. This is the wisdom of the opening: that from the hardest and most unlikely place, God brings forth life.

**2.** The rock is the material world in its densest form. The camel is the spirit that emerges from matter when God commands it.

**3.** God said to the rock: "Open." And the rock opened and the camel came forth. This is the same command that He gives to the heart: "Open." And when the heart opens, the spirit emerges.

**4.** The people of Thamud were told: "Leave her to graze upon God's earth and do her no harm." But they hamstrung her and were destroyed.

**5.** The she-camel represents the divine sign within creation. When you harm the sign, you harm yourself, for the sign is connected to its Source.

**6.** Every creature in the world is a she-camel of God—a sign emerging from the rock of existence. To harm any creature is to hamstring the sign of God.

**7.** The opening is not a single event—it is an ongoing revelation. At every moment, God is opening the rock of the world and bringing forth new signs.

**8.** The sunrise is an opening. The blooming of a flower is an opening. The birth of a child is an opening. Each is a camel emerging from the rock.

**9.** But the greatest opening is the opening of the heart. When the heart cracks open, the light of the Real floods in and transforms the entire being.

**10.** Do not fear the breaking of the heart, for it is through the crack that the light enters. The intact heart is a closed rock; the broken heart is an open door.

**11.** Salih wept for his people when they were destroyed, for the prophet weeps even for those who reject him. This is because the prophet sees with the eye of God, and God's mercy encompasses all things.

**12.** The punishment that fell upon Thamud was not vengeance—it was the natural consequence of harming the sign. When you cut the branch upon which you sit, you fall.

**13.** The wisdom of the opening teaches us to receive the signs of God with reverence and to protect them, for they are the means by which the hidden becomes manifest.

**14.** Every sign points beyond itself to the Signifier. The one who stops at the sign has missed the meaning; the one who sees through the sign arrives at the Real.

**15.** O seeker, look for the openings—in the rock, in the heart, in the world. Wherever something unexpected emerges, God is at work.
