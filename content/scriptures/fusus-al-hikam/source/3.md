## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Transcendence in the Word of Noah

**1.** Know that transcendence, with respect to God, is either the transcendence of limitation or the transcendence of belief.

**2.** The transcendence of limitation means to declare God free of all that limits the Real, and this is the transcendence of the knowers.

**3.** The transcendence of belief means to declare God free of what the intellect ascribes to Him through rational investigation, for the intellect cannot encompass the Infinite.

**4.** Noah said to his people: "Worship God, for you have no god other than Him." But his people placed their fingers in their ears and covered themselves with their garments.

**5.** Noah called them openly and called them secretly. He called them by night and by day. But the more he called them, the more they fled.

**6.** And what were they fleeing from? They were fleeing from the multiplicity of the Divine Names, which Noah invoked one after another, while they were attached to the unity of their idols.

**7.** For their idols—Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr—were each a face of the Divine, though the people of Noah did not know this in the way of the knowers.

**8.** The idol-worshiper worships God in a form without knowing it, for there is nothing in existence other than God. But the knower worships God in every form, knowing this.

**9.** The people of Noah worshipped specific forms and denied the rest. Noah invited them to worship the Formless through all forms.

**10.** This is why Noah said: "They have led many astray"—that is, the Names led many astray by their multiplicity, which veiled the seekers from the Unity.

**11.** And yet the astray and the guided are both within the embrace of the Real, for no one is outside the circle of His Being.

**12.** The wisdom of transcendence is to know that whatever you deny of God is also His, and whatever you affirm of God is also His. He is beyond affirmation and denial alike.

**13.** The flood of Noah is the flood of knowledge that overwhelms the mind and drowns all rational categories. Only the Ark of gnosis can save the traveler from this flood.

**14.** And the Ark is the heart—the comprehensive mirror that can contain what the mind cannot.

**15.** He who boards the Ark of the heart is saved. He who clings to the mountain of reason is drowned.
