## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Mercy in the Word of Solomon

**1.** Solomon was given a kingdom the like of which none after him would possess. His kingdom encompassed jinn, men, animals, and the wind—all creation served him.

**2.** But the wisdom of Solomon is not the wisdom of power—it is the wisdom of mercy. For true kingship is mercy, and mercy is the foundation of all divine governance.

**3.** God said: "My mercy encompasses all things." And Solomon's kingdom was the earthly mirror of this all-encompassing mercy.

**4.** Solomon understood the speech of birds, for the one who possesses mercy understands the language of all creatures.

**5.** He understood the speech of the ant who warned her companions: "Enter your dwellings lest Solomon and his armies crush you unknowingly." Solomon smiled and ordered his armies to be careful.

**6.** This is the mercy of the true king: that even the smallest creature is considered, even the ant beneath the foot is protected.

**7.** The Queen of Sheba came to Solomon and found her throne already in his court. She thought she was coming to test him, but she was the one being tested.

**8.** When she entered the palace with the glass floor, she thought it was water and raised her skirts. Solomon said: "It is a palace paved with glass." Appearances deceive; reality is other than what it seems.

**9.** Solomon's wisdom is to see through appearances to the reality beneath. The world is a glass floor that appears to be an ocean. The fool is deceived; the wise man walks upon it.

**10.** The mercy of Solomon extended to the jinn whom he commanded. He did not enslave them—he employed them, giving each a task suited to its nature.

**11.** This is the model of true governance: not to force creatures against their nature but to guide each according to its disposition. The wind serves differently from the bird, and the bird differently from the ant.

**12.** When Solomon died, his death was concealed—he remained leaning on his staff while the jinn continued their labor, not knowing he had passed.

**13.** Only when a worm ate through the staff and Solomon's body fell did the jinn realize their master had gone. This teaches that authority persists even after the one who holds it has departed, for it is God's authority, not man's.

**14.** The mercy that Solomon embodied did not die with him—it lives in every just ruler, every compassionate teacher, every merciful parent.

**15.** The wisdom of Solomon is that power without mercy is tyranny, and mercy without wisdom is weakness, but wisdom and mercy together are the throne of God on earth.
