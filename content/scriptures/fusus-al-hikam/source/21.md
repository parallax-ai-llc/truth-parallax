## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Sovereignty in the Word of Zachariah

**1.** Zachariah called upon his Lord in secret, saying: "My Lord, my bones have grown feeble and my head glistens with grey hair, and never have I been disappointed in my prayer to You."

**2.** He asked for an heir who would inherit the prophethood and carry on the work of God after him. This is the wisdom of sovereignty: the concern for continuity.

**3.** The true sovereign thinks not of himself but of what comes after him. His kingdom is not for his own pleasure but for the service of the Real.

**4.** God answered Zachariah and gave him John, though his wife was barren and he himself was old. This is because with God, no condition is permanent. The barren becomes fertile, and the old becomes young.

**5.** Zachariah's sign was that he could not speak for three days. The one who receives the greatest gift is often silenced by it, for words cannot contain what God has given.

**6.** Silence is the sign of the overwhelmed heart. When the gift is too great, the tongue falls silent and the hand trembles.

**7.** The sovereignty of Zachariah is not the sovereignty of a king who commands armies—it is the sovereignty of a servant who commands nothing but his own prayer.

**8.** And yet, this prayer moved the heavens. The prayer of the humble servant is more powerful than the decree of the mightiest king.

**9.** Zachariah prayed in the chamber of Mary, and he saw that she had provision that he had not provided. He asked: "Where does this come from?" She said: "It is from God."

**10.** And in that moment, Zachariah understood that God provides without means, without measure, without the intervention of human effort.

**11.** This inspired his own prayer for a child, for if God could provide for Mary without human agency, He could provide an heir for Zachariah without regard for age or barrenness.

**12.** The wisdom of sovereignty is the wisdom of trust in God's provision. The true sovereign is the one who trusts that God will provide what is needed for the continuation of His work.

**13.** Do not fear for the future of truth, for God has always raised a new voice when the old voice fell silent.

**14.** Zachariah passed the torch to John, and John passed it forward, and so it goes—each generation receives the light and passes it on.

**15.** The wisdom of Zachariah is that true sovereignty is stewardship, not ownership, and the greatest act of the sovereign is to prepare the way for those who come after.
