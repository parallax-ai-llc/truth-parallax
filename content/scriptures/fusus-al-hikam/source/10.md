## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Unity in the Word of Hud

**1.** Hud was sent to the people of 'Ad, and his message was the message of unity. He said: "Worship God—you have no other god."

**2.** The people of 'Ad were mighty in the land and said: "Who is mightier than us?" They did not see that the Might of God encompasses all might.

**3.** The wisdom of unity is to see that there is no power in existence other than the power of God. What you call your own power is His power manifesting through you.

**4.** "There is no might and no power except in God." This is not merely a phrase of piety—it is a statement of metaphysical truth.

**5.** The people of 'Ad were destroyed by wind, and wind is invisible. The invisible power destroyed the visible might, teaching that the unseen is more powerful than the seen.

**6.** Hud stood in the wind and was unharmed, for he had already surrendered to the One whose wind it was. The wind does not harm the one who has become one with its source.

**7.** Unity is not the denial of multiplicity—it is the perception of the One in the many. The colors of the rainbow are many, but the light is one.

**8.** He who sees only the colors quarrels over which is the true color. He who sees the light knows that all colors are refractions of a single white light.

**9.** The path of Hud is the straight path, and the straight path is the shortest distance between the servant and his Lord. And that distance is no distance at all.

**10.** The veil between you and God is not thick—it is thin. It is not a wall but a mist. One breath of sincerity disperses it.

**11.** God is closer to you than your jugular vein. He is nearer to you than you are to yourself. The unity that Hud proclaimed is not far—it is here.

**12.** Every creature walks upon the straight path, whether it knows it or not, for there is nowhere to go that is outside the embrace of God.

**13.** The sinner and the saint both walk upon the straight path. The difference is that the saint knows it, and the sinner does not.

**14.** When you see with the eye of unity, blame disappears, for you see that every creature is doing what it was created to do.

**15.** The wisdom of Hud is the wisdom of seeing the One in everything, and everything in the One, and resting in that vision.
