## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Uniqueness in the Word of Muhammad

**1.** The wisdom of Muhammad is the wisdom of uniqueness, for he is the Seal of the Prophets and the comprehensive being who gathered in himself all the wisdoms that were scattered among the prophets before him.

**2.** He was the first in the spiritual order and the last in the temporal order. He said: "I was a prophet when Adam was between water and clay."

**3.** His uniqueness is that he is the mirror in which God sees all His Names reflected simultaneously. Other prophets reflected particular Names; Muhammad reflected the Totality.

**4.** "We have not sent you except as a mercy to all the worlds." His mercy is not limited to one people or one time—it encompasses all creation.

**5.** The Night Journey and the Ascension are the supreme sign: from the earthly mosque to the heavenly mosque, and from the heavenly mosque to the presence of God Himself.

**6.** He traveled the distance that has no distance and arrived at the station that has no station. He was "two bow-lengths away or nearer."

**7.** At this station, all distinctions dissolved. There was no separation between the servant and the Lord, between the drop and the ocean, between the part and the whole.

**8.** Yet Muhammad returned. He came back to the world of multiplicity after tasting the wine of unity. This is his supreme achievement: not the ascent but the descent.

**9.** The one who ascends and does not return has been overwhelmed. The one who ascends and returns has been perfected. Muhammad returned to guide others to what he had seen.

**10.** He was given the Quran, which is the speech of God in the language of men. The Quran is the meeting point of the Infinite and the finite, the eternal and the temporal.

**11.** In Muhammad, all opposites are reconciled: the warrior and the merciful, the ruler and the servant, the human and the divine mirror.

**12.** He said: "I am the master of the children of Adam, and this is no boast." It is no boast because it is a statement of function, not of pride. The mirror does not boast of what it reflects.

**13.** The uniqueness of Muhammad is the uniqueness of the comprehensive: he contains all the wisdoms as the ocean contains all the rivers.

**14.** After him, no new prophet comes, for the circle is complete. But the saints who come after him inherit particular aspects of his wisdom, as the rivers draw from the ocean.

**15.** This is the seal and the completion. The Fusus al-Hikam ends as it began—with the recognition that the human being is the mirror of God, and that the most perfect mirror is the one that reflects all the Names without distortion.

**16.** And there is no god but God, and Muhammad is the Messenger of God. This is the first truth and the last truth, and all wisdoms are contained within it.
