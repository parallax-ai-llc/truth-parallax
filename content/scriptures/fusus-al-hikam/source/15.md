## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Prophecy in the Word of Jesus

**1.** Jesus was the Spirit of God and the Word of God cast into Mary. He was called the Spirit because he gave life to the dead, both the physically dead and the spiritually dead.

**2.** His creation was unique, for he was born of a mother without a father, as Adam was created from earth without father or mother.

**3.** The breath of Gabriel was the means by which the spirit was cast into Mary, and this breath carried the Word of God into the world of form.

**4.** Jesus said: "I am the servant of God." He did not say: "I am God." This is the wisdom of prophecy: the prophet knows his station and does not exceed it.

**5.** He healed the blind and the leper by the permission of God—not by his own power. The prophet is a channel, not a source.

**6.** The water that flows through a pipe comes from the spring, not from the pipe. To worship the pipe is to forget the spring.

**7.** Jesus breathed life into clay birds, and they flew. This is the power of the spirit that animates form. Without the breath of the spirit, the most perfect form is lifeless.

**8.** The miracle of Jesus is the miracle of the Word—that the spoken Word can bring dead matter to life, can heal the sick, and can open the eyes of the blind.

**9.** Every word spoken with the authority of truth carries something of this power. The word of the prophet heals; the word of the liar wounds.

**10.** Jesus was raised to heaven, and this raising is the raising of the spirit above the world of matter. The body of Jesus was the earthly form; the spirit of Jesus is the heavenly reality.

**11.** He who seeks Jesus in history seeks only the form. He who seeks the spirit of Jesus finds him here, now, in every act of mercy and every word of truth.

**12.** The wisdom of prophecy is the wisdom of the Word that creates, heals, and transforms. Every prophet is a Word of God, and every Word returns to its Speaker.

**13.** The asceticism of Jesus—his poverty, his wandering, his detachment—was not a rejection of the world but a demonstration that the spirit does not depend on the world for its sustenance.

**14.** Jesus said: "The world is a bridge—cross it but do not build upon it." This is the counsel of one who sees the world as a passage, not a destination.

**15.** The wisdom of Jesus is the wisdom of spirit over matter, of inner reality over outer form, of the eternal Word over the temporal world.
