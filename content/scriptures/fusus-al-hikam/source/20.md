## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Majesty in the Word of John the Baptist

**1.** John was given wisdom as a child, and compassion from God, and purity. He was devout from his youth.

**2.** God said: "O John, hold fast to the Book with strength." And We gave him wisdom while yet a boy.

**3.** The wisdom of majesty is the wisdom that comes not from experience but from the direct gift of God. John did not earn his wisdom—it was bestowed upon him before he could earn anything.

**4.** This teaches that wisdom is ultimately a grace, not an achievement. The wisest scholars cannot acquire what God gives to a child.

**5.** John was named by God before he was born. "We have not assigned this name to anyone before." He was unique, and his uniqueness was decreed before his birth.

**6.** The majesty of John is the majesty of purity. He was called devout, meaning he was protected from sin—not because he chose virtue over vice, but because God chose virtue for him.

**7.** John wept much and laughed little. His tears were not tears of sorrow but tears of awe before the majesty of God. When the heart is overwhelmed by majesty, tears are its only language.

**8.** The one who has tasted the majesty of God cannot look at the world the same way. Everything appears fragile, temporary, like a dream about to dissolve.

**9.** John was killed by a tyrant for speaking the truth. The prophet of majesty does not compromise, for he has seen the Majesty that makes all earthly power seem like a shadow.

**10.** His blood was spilled, and the earth cried out, and God sent armies to avenge him. For the blood of the truthful is sacred, and its violation shakes the foundations of the world.

**11.** The majesty of God is not distant and cold—it is near and overwhelming. It is the wave that crashes over the swimmer, not to drown him but to remind him of the power of the ocean.

**12.** John's strength was not the strength of the body—it was the strength of one who holds fast to the Book of God with everything he has.

**13.** To hold fast to the Book is to hold fast to Reality. The Book is the speech of God, and His speech is the foundation of the world.

**14.** When you hold fast to Reality, nothing can shake you. Kings may fall, empires may crumble, but the one who holds the Book stands firm.

**15.** The wisdom of John is the wisdom of unwavering devotion to the Majestic, which makes all other devotions seem like children's games.
