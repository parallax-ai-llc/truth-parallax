## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Truth in the Word of Isaac

**1.** The truth of the ransom in the story of Isaac—or Ishmael according to some—is that the reality offered for sacrifice was redeemed by the appearance of a lesser form.

**2.** The son was the truth, and the ram was the form. The form was accepted in place of the truth, because God is merciful and accepts the lesser in place of the greater.

**3.** This is the principle of substitution that governs the world: the outward form stands in for the inner reality, and the Real accepts both.

**4.** In every act of worship, the form is the body and the truth is the intention. God looks not at the form but at the truth within it.

**5.** The one who prays with his body but not with his heart has offered the ram. The one who prays with his heart has offered the son. Both are accepted, but the degrees are not equal.

**6.** Isaac went willingly to the place of sacrifice. This willingness is the essence of submission, and submission is the essence of Islam.

**7.** The child said: "O my father, do what you are commanded. You will find me, God willing, among the patient." This is the speech of one who has already surrendered.

**8.** The patience of Isaac is the patience of one who knows that what comes from God is good, even when it appears to the senses as destruction.

**9.** The knife was placed upon the throat, but the throat was not cut. This is because the truth cannot be destroyed—only the form can be altered.

**10.** The wisdom of truth teaches that every apparent destruction is in reality a transformation. Nothing perishes; it only changes form.

**11.** The seed that falls into the earth appears to die, but it is being transformed into a tree. The caterpillar that enters the cocoon appears to die, but it is being transformed into a butterfly.

**12.** So it is with the soul: what you call death is the soul's transformation from the form of this world to the truth of the next.

**13.** Isaac's willingness to die was not courage—it was knowledge. He knew that death was not an ending but a passage.

**14.** The truth of every being is indestructible. The forms come and go, but the truth remains.

**15.** This is the secret that Abraham and Isaac shared at the place of sacrifice: that what appears to be the ultimate loss is in truth the ultimate gain.
