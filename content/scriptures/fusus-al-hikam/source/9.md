## The Bezel of the Wisdom of Light in the Word of Joseph

**1.** The wisdom of light manifested in Joseph, whose beauty was a light that illuminated all who beheld him. His beauty was not merely physical—it was a theophany.

**2.** When the women of Egypt saw Joseph, they cut their hands and said: "This is not a man—this is a noble angel." They saw in him a light that transcended the human form.

**3.** Joseph's beauty was the beauty of the Real manifesting in creation. When beauty reaches a certain intensity, it is no longer the beauty of the creature but the Beauty of the Creator shining through.

**4.** Zuleikha loved Joseph with a love that consumed her entirely. The people blamed her, but when they saw Joseph, they understood.

**5.** This is because love is not a choice—it is a response to beauty, and beauty is a manifestation of the Divine.

**6.** Joseph was cast into the well, and from the well he was raised to the throne of Egypt. This is the wisdom of light: that it shines most brightly when it emerges from the deepest darkness.

**7.** The well is the place of concealment, the prison is the place of trial, and the throne is the place of manifestation. Every light passes through these three stations.

**8.** Joseph interpreted dreams because he could see the light of meaning behind the darkness of symbols. The dream is a veil, and the interpretation is the lifting of the veil.

**9.** The king's dream of seven fat cows devoured by seven lean cows was not about cows—it was about the alternation of abundance and scarcity that governs the world.

**10.** The one who sees the light behind the form sees the meaning behind the dream, the truth behind the parable, and the Real behind creation.

**11.** Joseph forgave his brothers, for he saw that their treachery was the means by which God brought him to his station. Without the well, there would have been no throne.

**12.** This is the deepest teaching of the wisdom of light: that everything serves the light, even darkness. Especially darkness.

**13.** The one who has this wisdom does not curse the darkness—he thanks it, for without darkness, light would have no meaning.

**14.** Joseph said: "No blame upon you today." This is the speech of one who sees that all things work together for the good of those who love God.

**15.** The beauty of Joseph is the beauty of forgiveness, which is the highest beauty of all, for it transforms the darkness of hatred into the light of love.
