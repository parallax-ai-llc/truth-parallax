## The Bezel of the Wisdom of the Singular in the Word of Khalid

**1.** The wisdom of the singular is the wisdom of the unique and unrepeatable moment—the moment in which the Real manifests in a form that has never appeared before and will never appear again.

**2.** Every moment of creation is singular. God does not repeat Himself. Each breath is a new creation, each instant a new theophany.

**3.** The people of heedlessness see sameness where the people of awakening see perpetual novelty. The sunrise of today is not the sunrise of yesterday, though the heedless say: "It is the same sun."

**4.** The wise man never steps into the same river twice, and the river never receives the same man twice.

**5.** The singular wisdom teaches that you are not the same person you were a moment ago. The Real is manifesting in you anew at every instant.

**6.** If you could see the constant renewal of creation, you would be in a state of perpetual wonder. But the veil of habit makes the miraculous seem ordinary.

**7.** Remove the veil of habit, and every breath becomes a miracle, every heartbeat a revelation, every glance a theophany.

**8.** The singular is the intersection of the eternal and the temporal—the point where the timeless enters time and creates a moment that partakes of both.

**9.** Every prophet is singular, every saint is singular, every human being is singular. No two faces are the same because no two theophanies are the same.

**10.** God manifests in each being according to the unique capacity of that being. No other being can receive what you receive, and no other being can manifest what you manifest.

**11.** This is your dignity and your responsibility: you are a unique face of the Infinite, and if you do not manifest your unique face, it will never be manifested.

**12.** The wisdom of the singular teaches that imitation is the death of the spirit. Do not try to be Moses or Jesus or Muhammad—be yourself, for God created you to manifest a Name that only you can manifest.

**13.** The universe is a mosaic, and you are a tile that cannot be replaced. Remove one tile, and the whole picture is diminished.

**14.** Honor your singularity, for it is the gift of God. And honor the singularity of every other being, for each is a face of the Beloved that you have not yet seen.

**15.** The wisdom of the singular is the wisdom of awe before the infinite creativity of God, who never creates the same thing twice and whose imagination has no end.
