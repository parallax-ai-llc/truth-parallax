Sunni reading of Fusus al-Hikam chapter 27, "The Bezel of Wisdom of Singularity in the Word of Muhammad," navigates between reverence for Ibn Arabi's spiritual authority and concern for theological orthodoxy. This bezel's treatment of prophetic wisdom must be reconciled with Sunni commitments to the absolute transcendence of God (tanzih) and the integrity of prophetic biography (sira). Scholars who accept the text read its more provocative formulations as expressions of mystical experience that, properly understood, remain within the bounds of orthodox tawhid.

The prophetic figure discussed in this bezel holds a specific place in Sunni sacred history, and the wisdom Ibn Arabi attributes to this prophet must be evaluated against the Quran, Hadith, and the consensus of the scholarly community. Sunni engagement with this chapter thus involves a careful balancing of mystical insight with theological responsibility.
