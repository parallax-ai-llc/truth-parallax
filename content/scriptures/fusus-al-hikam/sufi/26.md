Sufi interpretation of Fusus al-Hikam chapter 26, "The Bezel of Wisdom of Uniqueness in the Word of Khalid," meditates on the unique divine wisdom embodied in this prophetic figure. For the Sufi reader, each bezel reveals a distinct mode of divine self-disclosure (tajalli) that corresponds to a station on the path of spiritual realization. The prophet discussed here is not merely a historical figure but an eternal archetype — a permanent possibility of the relationship between the human and the divine.

The wisdom (hikma) associated with this prophet illuminates a particular aspect of the Unity of Being (wahdat al-wujud). The Sufi practitioner is invited to recognize this wisdom not as abstract doctrine but as a lived reality to be tasted (dhawq) through spiritual practice, contemplation, and the grace of divine unveiling. Each bezel thus serves as both a metaphysical teaching and a spiritual exercise.
