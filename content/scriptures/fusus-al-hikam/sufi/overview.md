## Overview

For the Sufi tradition, the Fusus al-Hikam is the definitive philosophical articulation of mystical experience — the text in which Ibn Arabi systematized the insights gained through direct unveiling (kashf) into a comprehensive metaphysical vision. Every major Sufi order has produced commentaries on the Fusus, and its study has been central to advanced Sufi education for eight centuries.

## Interpretive Approach

Sufi readers approach the Fusus as a text that can only be truly understood through spiritual realization, not merely intellectual analysis. Each bezel reveals a dimension of the divine self-disclosure that corresponds to a station on the mystical path. The reader is expected to move from intellectual understanding to experiential tasting (dhawq) — recognizing in the prophetic types the stages of their own spiritual journey.

## Key Emphases

The Sufi reading emphasizes the doctrine of wahdat al-wujud (Unity of Being) as the ultimate truth glimpsed in mystical experience: that there is only one reality, and all apparent multiplicity is the divine self-disclosure through infinite forms. The Perfect Human (al-insan al-kamil) who unites all divine names is the goal of the Sufi path — the realization that the mystic's true identity is the mirror in which God contemplates Himself.
