## Overview

Archaeological perspectives on the Book of Invasions connect the text's mythological geography to real archaeological sites and landscape features in Ireland. The Tuatha Dé Danann's association with the síd mounds links to the megalithic monuments of the Boyne Valley (Newgrange, Knowth, Dowth) and other prehistoric sites. The Battle of Moytura is located at real places in County Sligo and County Mayo. Archaeological evidence of Bronze and Iron Age settlements, ritual sites, and cultural change provides material context for the mythological narrative of successive invasions.
