---
title: "The Coming of the Fir Bolg"
scripture: "book-of-invasions"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The descendants of Nemed who had gone to Greece returned to Ireland. They were called the Fir Bolg, and with them came the Fir Domnann and the Gailioin.

**2** They divided Ireland into five provinces: Leinster, Munster, Connacht, Ulster, and Meath. This is the origin of the five-fold division of Ireland.

**3** The first king of the Fir Bolg was Slainge. Nine kings of the Fir Bolg ruled Ireland for thirty-seven years.

**4** The last king was Eochaid mac Eirc, the best king that ever ruled Ireland. In his time there was no rain, only dew; there was no year without harvest; falsehood was banished from Ireland, and justice was maintained.

**5** But the Tuatha De Danann were coming from the northern islands of the world, and with them they brought four treasures: the Stone of Fal, the Spear of Lugh, the Sword of Nuada, and the Cauldron of the Dagda.
