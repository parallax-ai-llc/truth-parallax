---
title: "The Coming of the Sons of Mil"
scripture: "book-of-invasions"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The sons of Mil Espaine, also called the Milesians or the Gaels, came from Spain. Ith son of Breogan saw Ireland from the top of a tower in Spain on a clear winter evening.

**2** Ith sailed to Ireland and was received by the three kings of the Tuatha De Danann: Mac Cuill, Mac Cecht, and Mac Greine. But they killed him, fearing he wished to take their land.

**3** The sons of Mil came with thirty-six ships to avenge Ith and to take Ireland. Among them were Eber Finn, Eremon, and Amergin the poet.

**4** As they approached the shore, the druids of the Tuatha De raised a wind of enchantment against them. Amergin spoke a great invocation:

**5** I am the wind that blows across the sea; I am a wave of the ocean; I am the roar of the sea; I am a bull of seven fights; I am a hawk on a cliff; I am a tear-drop of the sun; I am fair among flowers.

**6** By this poem the enchantment was broken, and the Milesians landed. They fought the Tuatha De Danann in two great battles and defeated them.

**7** The three queens of the Tuatha De asked that the island bear their names: Eriu, Banba, and Fodla. Amergin promised the name to Eriu, and so Ireland bears her name to this day.
