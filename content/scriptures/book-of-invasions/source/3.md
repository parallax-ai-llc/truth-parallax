---
title: "The Coming of Partholon"
scripture: "book-of-invasions"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Partholon son of Sera came to Ireland from the west, from the land of the Greeks. He came with his wife Dealgnaid, his three sons, and their people, one thousand in all.

**2** They came to Ireland three hundred years after the Flood. When they arrived, there was only one plain, three lakes, and nine rivers in Ireland.

**3** During Partholon's time, seven new lakes burst forth and four new plains were cleared. His people were the first to brew ale, the first to establish laws and customs, the first to make a guesting-house.

**4** Partholon fought the first battle in Ireland, against the Fomorians, the ancient race of darkness and chaos, who came from the sea. The battle was at Mag Itha, and Partholon was victorious.

**5** After three hundred years in Ireland, the people of Partholon perished of a plague. In one week, five thousand men and four thousand women died. They were buried on the plain that bears their name, Tallaght.

**6** Only one man survived: Tuan mac Cairill, who like Fintan lived through many ages in many forms, and preserved the memory of all that had passed.
