---
title: "The Division of Ireland"
scripture: "book-of-invasions"
chapter: 8
---

**1** After the defeat of the Tuatha De Danann, the sons of Mil divided Ireland between them. But first, the Tuatha De were given the underground, the sidhe-mounds, the fairy hills.

**2** The Dagda divided the sidhe-mounds among the Tuatha De Danann. To Lugh he gave Sidhe Rodrubain; to Ogma, Sidhe Airceltrai; to himself, Bru na Boinne, until it was taken from him by his son Oengus.

**3** Eber Finn took the south of Ireland, and Eremon took the north. But the two brothers quarreled, and war broke out between them.

**4** Eremon defeated and slew Eber Finn and became sole king of Ireland. From him descend the kings of Tara and the high kings of Ireland.

**5** From Eber Finn descend the kings of Munster and many great families of the south. The division between north and south would last for many centuries.

**6** And so Ireland was taken for the last time. The Gaels, the sons of Mil, held the land above ground, and the Tuatha De Danann held the land below, and both peoples shared the island, the seen and the unseen.

**7** This is the Lebor Gabala Erenn, the Book of the Taking of Ireland, which tells how the island was settled from the beginning of the world to the coming of the Gaels. It is the history and the memory of the Irish people.
