## Overview

The Book of Invasions is one of the most ambitious literary projects of medieval Ireland. Its grand narrative scope — from the creation of the world to the establishment of Gaelic Ireland — demonstrates the literary aspirations of medieval Irish scholars. Literary analysis examines the text's narrative structure, its integration of prose and verse, its use of genealogy and etymology as narrative devices, and its relationship to other genres of medieval Irish literature including saga, poetry, and historical writing.
