## Overview

The Book of Invasions offers rich material for comparative study. Its succession of mythological races parallels the Greek ages of gold, silver, bronze, and iron (Hesiod), the Hindu yugas, and other traditions of declining cosmic ages. The conflict between the Tuatha Dé Danann and the Fomorians resonates with cosmic battles between gods and titans found in Greek, Norse, and Vedic mythology. The text's integration of native mythology with biblical chronology parallels similar efforts in other medieval European cultures.
