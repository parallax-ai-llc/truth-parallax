## Overview

The Book of Invasions represents one of medieval Europe's most creative exercises in interfaith synthesis. Irish monks and scholars wove pre-Christian mythology — including the gods of the Tuatha Dé Danann — into the framework of biblical history, preserving native religious traditions while subordinating them to a Christian worldview. This synthesis raises important questions about cultural negotiation, the preservation of indigenous traditions within dominant religious frameworks, and the ways in which different religious worldviews can be brought into dialogue.
