## Overview

The Book of Invasions raises philosophical questions about the nature of history and myth, the relationship between divine and human sovereignty, and the meaning of conquest and settlement. The text's synthetic method — weaving pre-Christian mythology into Christian historical chronology — represents a philosophical engagement with the problem of reconciling different knowledge systems. The recurring theme of successive races gaining and losing sovereignty over Ireland invites reflection on the nature of political legitimacy and the relationship between a people and their land.
