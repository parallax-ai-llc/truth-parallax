---
title: "Brahma Sutras - Chapter 4"
scripture: "brahma-sutras"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Repetition of the contemplation of Brahman is necessary, for the scriptures instruct repeated meditation until knowledge becomes firm.

**2** The knowledge of Brahman, once firmly established, cannot be shaken by any amount of contrary reasoning or experience. It is like the knowledge of fire — once known, it cannot be doubted.

**3** At the time of death, the soul that has attained knowledge departs from the body. The pranas gather around the soul, and the soul, together with the pranas, departs.

**4** The soul of the knower of Brahman goes by the path of the gods, the Devayana. First it reaches the deity of fire, then the deity of the day, then the deity of the bright fortnight, then the deity of the six months of the northern solstice.

**5** From there it goes to the year, from the year to the sun, from the sun to the moon, from the moon to the lightning. There a being who is not human leads the soul to Brahmaloka.

**6** Having reached Brahmaloka, the soul never returns to the world of birth and death. It attains the state of Brahman and enjoys infinite bliss.

**7** The liberated soul possesses all the powers of Brahman except the power of creation, for creation belongs to the Lord alone.

**8** The liberated soul can assume any form at will, or be without any form. It can be one or many. It enjoys all the perfections of Brahman.

**9** The liberated soul is free from all suffering, all limitation, all bondage. It is established in pure being, pure consciousness, and pure bliss.

**10** There is no return for the liberated soul. The scriptures declare: "Those who have reached the world of Brahman through knowledge do not return." This is the final word.

**11** But those who attain liberation through the knowledge of Nirguna Brahman do not travel by any path, for they realize their identity with the all-pervading Brahman here and now. For them, there is no going and no returning.

**12** Thus the enquiry into Brahman is complete. He who knows Brahman becomes Brahman. There is no higher attainment than this. Om, peace, peace, peace.
