---
title: "Brahma Sutras - Chapter 1"
scripture: "brahma-sutras"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Now, therefore, the enquiry into Brahman.

**2** From which the origin, sustenance, and dissolution of this universe proceed — that is Brahman.

**3** The scriptures are the means of knowing Brahman, for Brahman is their main purport.

**4** But that Brahman is to be known from the Upanishads alone, for they are the authoritative source of the knowledge of Brahman.

**5** That from which the breath, mind, and all the senses proceed — know that to be Brahman.

**6** That which is not seen, but which causes seeing; that which is not heard, but which causes hearing; that which is not thought, but which causes thinking — know that to be Brahman.

**7** That bliss from which all beings are born, by which they live, and into which they enter at death — seek to know that; that is Brahman.

**8** The ether from which all these beings arise and into which they return — that ether is Brahman, for it is the source and support of all.

**9** The light that shines above the heavens, above all, above everything, in the highest world beyond which there is no other — that light is the same as the light within man.

**10** The being who is in the sun and the being who is in the eye — the knowers of Brahman know these to be one and the same.

**11** The being who consists of mind, whose body is breath, whose form is light, whose conception is truth, whose self is space — that is Brahman.

**12** Brahman is that which cannot be expressed by speech, but by which speech is expressed; that which cannot be apprehended by the mind, but by which the mind is apprehended. Know that alone as Brahman.
