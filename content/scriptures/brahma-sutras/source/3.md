---
title: "Brahma Sutras - Chapter 3"
scripture: "brahma-sutras"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The soul, in the state of deep sleep, becomes one with Brahman. This is known from the scriptural statement: "He becomes united with the True."

**2** But the soul returns from deep sleep to the waking state, retaining its individual identity. This shows that the soul, even in union with Brahman, does not entirely lose its individuality.

**3** The soul in the dream state creates its own world through the impressions of waking experience. The Lord is the creator of the dream world, for He controls the soul from within.

**4** The soul departs from the body at death through the arteries. The manner of its departure depends on its knowledge and its karma.

**5** He who has attained the knowledge of Brahman departs through the crown of the head, through the sushumna nadi. He goes by the path of light, the Devayana, and reaches Brahmaloka.

**6** He who has performed meritorious deeds but has not attained knowledge departs by the path of smoke, the Pitriyana. He reaches the world of the moon and returns to earthly life after exhausting his merit.

**7** He who has neither knowledge nor merit goes to the lower worlds and is reborn according to his karma.

**8** The meditation on the qualified Brahman, Saguna Brahman, leads to gradual liberation through Brahmaloka. The knowledge of the unqualified Brahman, Nirguna Brahman, leads to immediate liberation.

**9** The different Upanishadic meditations, though apparently different, are to be combined when they relate to the same subject. The qualities of Brahman described in different texts supplement one another.

**10** The knowledge of Brahman is not dependent on works. Works may purify the mind and prepare it for knowledge, but knowledge alone liberates.

**11** He who has attained the knowledge of Brahman is not reborn. His remaining karma is destroyed like seeds burnt by fire. He attains liberation.

**12** The liberated soul enjoys infinite bliss, infinite knowledge, and infinite freedom. It realizes its identity with Brahman and is never again subject to bondage.
