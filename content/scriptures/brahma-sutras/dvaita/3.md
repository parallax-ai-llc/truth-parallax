Madhva's commentary on Sadhana Adhyaya emphasizes that the means to liberation involve both knowledge and devotion, with divine grace as the indispensable enabling factor. The Upanishadic meditations described in this chapter are genuine devotional practices directed toward Vishnu, not preliminary exercises to be transcended. Knowledge of God's real nature and attributes is itself a form of devotion, and devotion without knowledge is blind.

The chapter's treatment of the soul's nature and qualifications introduces Madhva's distinctive doctrine of *taratamya* — the innate hierarchy among souls. Not all souls are equally capable of liberation; their spiritual capacities differ according to their eternal nature (*svarupa yogyata*). This does not mean unqualified souls are condemned but that different souls attain different degrees of proximity to God in liberation. The discussion of qualifications for Vedantic study is read as confirming that only those with genuine devotion and divine grace can attain liberating knowledge.
