Madhva's commentary on Samanvaya Adhyaya establishes that the Upanishads consistently teach Vishnu as the Supreme Being who is fundamentally different from all individual souls and all material entities. Where Shankara reads the *mahavakyas* as teaching identity between Atman and Brahman, Madhva reads them as expressing the soul's complete dependence on and difference from God. "Tat tvam asi" means "You belong to That," not "You are That."

Madhva introduces his distinctive hermeneutical principle that every Vedic text, properly understood, glorifies Vishnu's supremacy. The Upanishadic passages on Brahman as the source, sustainer, and dissolver of the universe (discussed in this chapter) describe Vishnu's real activities of creation and governance. Brahman's attributes — omniscience, bliss, lordship — are genuinely real qualities, not provisional descriptions to be negated by a higher insight. The first chapter establishes the realist metaphysical foundation upon which Madhva's entire system rests.
