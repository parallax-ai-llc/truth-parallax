Madhva's interpretation of Phala Adhyaya describes liberation as the soul's attainment of its own highest nature — eternal bliss proportional to its innate capacity — in the eternal presence of Vishnu. The liberated soul does not merge with God, lose its individuality, or attain equality with God. Instead, it enters Vishnu's supreme abode (*Vaikuntha*) and experiences unending joy in direct perception of and service to the Lord.

The hierarchy of liberation is a distinctively Dvaita contribution: even among liberated souls, there are differences in the degree of bliss experienced, corresponding to the soul's innate rank. Brahma occupies the highest position among liberated souls, followed by other deities, sages, and ordinary devotees. The chapter's description of the path of light is read as a literal journey, with the liberated soul guided by divine beings to Vaikuntha. Once there, the soul possesses all perfections except the power of cosmic creation, preservation, and destruction, which belong exclusively to Vishnu.
