## Overview

Madhvacharya's *Brahma Sutra Bhashya* (13th century) establishes Dvaita Vedanta as a rigorous theistic realism in direct opposition to Shankara's Advaita. Madhva reads the Brahma Sutras as teaching five eternal, real differences (*pancha-bheda*): between God and soul, God and matter, soul and matter, one soul and another, and one material entity and another. Brahman is Vishnu, the independently real Supreme Being who creates, sustains, and dissolves the genuinely real world.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Madhva's Direct Challenge
Madhva's commentary systematically reinterprets every sutra that Shankara read as supporting non-dualism. The *mahavakyas* ("Tat tvam asi") are read as expressing the soul's utter dependence on God, not identity with God. Madhva's *Anuvyakhyana* provides verse-by-verse alternative readings.

### Jayatirtha's Elaboration
Jayatirtha's *Nyaya Sudha* offers a massive sub-commentary that provides philosophical argumentation defending each of Madhva's interpretive moves against Advaita objections.

## Theological Emphases

- **Pancha-Bheda**: Five real, eternal differences constitute the structure of reality
- **Vishnu's Svatantrya**: God alone is independently real; everything else depends on Him
- **Taratamya**: A strict hierarchy among souls, with different innate capacities for liberation
- **Real World**: The material world is not illusion but a genuine transformation dependent on God's will
