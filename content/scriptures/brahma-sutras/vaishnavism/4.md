Ramanuja's interpretation of Phala Adhyaya presents liberation as the soul's attainment of Vaikuntha — the eternal spiritual realm where it exists in direct, loving relationship with Narayana. Unlike the Advaita reading, where moksha means the realization that the individual self was always identical with Brahman, the Vaishnava reading maintains that the liberated soul retains its individual identity while experiencing infinite bliss in God's presence.

The chapter's description of the soul's journey after death — through the *archiradi marga* (path of light) to the supreme abode — is read literally by Ramanuja as a genuine metaphysical journey. The liberated soul attains a spiritual body fit for eternal service and enjoyment in Vaikuntha. It shares in Brahman's qualities of knowledge and bliss while remaining ontologically distinct. Ramanuja emphasizes that the liberated soul does not acquire the power of cosmic creation, which belongs to God alone — a point that distinguishes Vaishnava liberation from Advaita's apparently unqualified identity.
