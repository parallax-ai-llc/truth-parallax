## Overview

Vaishnava tradition regards the Brahma Sutras as the systematic demonstration that the Upanishads consistently teach Brahman as the Supreme Personal God — Vishnu or Narayana. Ramanuja's *Sri Bhashya* (11th-12th century) is the foundational Vaishnava commentary, establishing *vishishtadvaita* (qualified non-dualism) as a rigorous philosophical system in which Brahman possesses infinite auspicious qualities and is inseparable from the souls and the world that constitute His body.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Ramanuja's Sri Bhashya
Ramanuja argues that the sutras teach a Brahman who is both the material and efficient cause of the universe, possessing the world and souls as His real attributes. Liberation is eternal, loving service to God in Vaikuntha, not dissolution of individuality.

### Vedanta Deshika
Vedanta Deshika's sub-commentaries and independent works elaborate Ramanuja's system with rigorous logical argumentation, defending Sri Vaishnavism against both Advaita and Dvaita objections.

## Theological Emphases

- **Saguna Brahman**: Brahman is essentially qualified (*savisesha*), possessing infinite auspicious attributes
- **Body-Soul Analogy**: The world and souls relate to Brahman as body to soul (*sharira-shariri*)
- **Prapatti**: Self-surrender to God as the supreme means of liberation
- **Real World**: The material world is genuinely real, a transformation (*parinama*) of Brahman's attributes
