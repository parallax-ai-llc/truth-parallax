The Vaishnava reading of Sadhana Adhyaya emphasizes that the means to liberation involve both knowledge and devotion, culminating in *prapatti* (self-surrender) to the Lord. Ramanuja interprets the various Upanishadic meditations (*vidyas*) discussed in this chapter as forms of *bhakti* — loving contemplation of Brahman's real attributes. Unlike the Advaita reading, which treats these meditations as preliminary practices superseded by *nirguna* knowledge, Ramanuja maintains that devotional meditation is itself the direct means of liberation.

The chapter's discussion of the soul's qualities and its relationship to Brahman is crucial for Sri Vaishnavism. The individual soul possesses real consciousness and bliss, but these are infinitesimal compared to Brahman's infinite attributes. The soul's nature is to be a servant (*shesha*) of God, and liberation is the recovery of this natural relationship. Ramanuja reads the qualifications for Vedantic knowledge — discrimination, dispassion, and the desire for liberation — as inseparable from devotion to Narayana.
