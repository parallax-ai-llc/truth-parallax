Ramanuja's commentary on Samanvaya Adhyaya demonstrates that all Upanishadic texts consistently teach Brahman as the Supreme Personal God — Narayana — who possesses infinite auspicious qualities. Against Shankara's reading that the Upanishads ultimately teach a qualityless (*nirguna*) Brahman, Ramanuja argues that every description of Brahman in the Upanishads refers to a real attribute of the Supreme Person. The opening sutra — "Then therefore the inquiry into Brahman" — establishes that knowledge of Brahman is the supreme human goal.

Ramanuja reads the key Upanishadic passages discussed in this chapter — the *Anandamaya* (bliss-self), the being from whom all creatures originate, the inner controller (*antaryamin*) — as consistently pointing to Vishnu. The *sharira-shariri* (body-soul) analogy is established here: the world and individual souls relate to Brahman as body to soul, genuinely real yet utterly dependent on and pervaded by the Supreme. This chapter lays the exegetical foundation for the entire Sri Vaishnava philosophical system.
