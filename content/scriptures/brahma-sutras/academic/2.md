Academic analysis of Avirodha Adhyaya focuses on the polemical sections where Badarayana refutes rival philosophical systems, as these provide invaluable evidence for the intellectual landscape of ancient India. The refutations of Samkhya (2.1-12), atomism (2.2.11-17), and Buddhism (2.2.18-32) help scholars date the text and identify which forms of these philosophies were current when the sutras were composed.

The anti-Buddhist sutras have been particularly important for dating debates. Scholars note that the Buddhist positions refuted seem to correspond to early Sautrantika or Yogachara views, suggesting a date no earlier than the 1st century BCE for at least portions of this chapter. However, the extreme brevity of the sutras makes precise identification of the targeted Buddhist school difficult, and the possibility that later commentators projected their own contemporary opponents onto the original text cannot be ruled out. The chapter thus serves as a mirror of India's philosophical diversity in its formative centuries.
