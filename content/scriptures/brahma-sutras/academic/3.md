Scholars examine Sadhana Adhyaya for its evidence of how Upanishadic meditation practices were understood and organized in the centuries surrounding the common era. The chapter's detailed discussion of various *vidyas* (meditative disciplines) — whether they can be combined, whether the same meditation taught in different Upanishads is identical or distinct — reveals a sophisticated hermeneutical tradition grappling with the practical implications of a growing scriptural corpus.

The chapter also provides evidence for the sociology of Vedantic knowledge. The discussion of who is qualified for Vedantic study, including sections that address the eligibility of different social groups, reflects the institutional and social contexts in which these philosophical traditions were transmitted. Academic scholars (e.g., Patrick Olivelle, Brian Black) have examined how these passages illuminate the relationship between philosophical inquiry and social hierarchy in ancient India, while noting that later commentators sometimes projected their own social assumptions onto the earlier text.
