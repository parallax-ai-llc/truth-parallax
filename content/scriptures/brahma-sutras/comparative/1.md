Samanvaya Adhyaya's project of demonstrating the internal harmony of scriptural teaching about ultimate reality invites comparison with similar enterprises in other traditions. Aquinas's Summa Theologica seeks to show the coherence of Christian revelation through systematic rational argumentation. Maimonides' *Guide for the Perplexed* harmonizes apparently contradictory biblical passages about God's nature. Islamic theologians in the *kalam* tradition similarly systematize Quranic teaching about divine attributes.

The chapter's fundamental question — What is the nature of ultimate reality? — is the starting point of metaphysics in every philosophical culture. Badarayana's answer, that Brahman is the source, sustainer, and ground of all existence, parallels Aristotle's concept of the Unmoved Mover, Plotinus's One, and the Neo-Confucian notion of *li* (principle) as the ground of all phenomena. The comparative question is whether these different traditions are describing the same reality through different conceptual frameworks or genuinely different realities altogether.
