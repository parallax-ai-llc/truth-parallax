Sadhana Adhyaya's discussion of the means to liberating knowledge invites comparison with the question of spiritual method across traditions. The Vedantic debate over whether liberation comes through knowledge alone (Shankara) or through a combination of knowledge, devotion, and practice (Ramanuja, Madhva) parallels the Christian debate between salvation by faith alone (*sola fide*) and faith combined with works — a dispute that divided Catholic and Protestant Christianity.

The chapter's discussion of prerequisites for spiritual knowledge — moral purity, dispassion, concentration, and desire for liberation — resonates with the Buddhist *paramitas* (perfections), the Sufi *maqamat* (stations), and the Christian mystical tradition's emphasis on purgation before illumination. The comparative insight is that traditions consistently recognize the need for both intellectual preparation and moral-emotional transformation before the deepest spiritual truths can be apprehended, though they differ sharply on whether human effort alone suffices or divine grace is indispensable.
