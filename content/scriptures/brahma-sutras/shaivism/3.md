The Shaiva interpretation of Sadhana Adhyaya connects the Upanishadic meditations to Shaiva spiritual practice. The various *vidyas* (meditative disciplines) discussed in this chapter are read as forms of Shiva contemplation, and the qualifications for Vedantic inquiry align with the Shaiva requirements of initiation (*diksha*), devotion (*bhakti*), and the grace of a qualified guru.

Srikantha's commentary integrates the Brahma Sutras' discussion of spiritual means with Shaiva Siddhanta's threefold path: *charya* (service), *kriya* (ritual worship), *yoga* (meditation), and *jnana* (knowledge). The individual soul (*pashu*) is bound by *pasha* (fetters of ignorance, karma, and maya), and the practices described in this chapter systematically loosen these bonds. Crucially, the Shaiva reading emphasizes that liberation ultimately depends on Shiva's grace (*anugraha*) — the soul's practice creates receptivity, but only Shiva's independent will can grant final liberation from the three *malas*.
