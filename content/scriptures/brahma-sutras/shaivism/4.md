The Shaiva reading of Phala Adhyaya presents liberation as *shivata* — becoming like Shiva, or realizing one's identity with Shiva, depending on the specific Shaiva school. In Kashmir Shaivism, liberation is the recognition (*pratyabhijna*) that one has always been Shiva. In Shaiva Siddhanta, the liberated soul attains Shiva's qualities of omniscience and bliss while remaining a distinct entity enjoying Shiva's eternal presence.

The chapter's discussion of the soul's journey after death is read through the Shaiva framework of grace and initiation. The soul that has received Shiva's grace and undergone proper initiation travels the path of light to Shiva's supreme abode (*Shivaloka* or *Kailasha*). There it exists in a state of perfect freedom, with all karmic bonds destroyed and the three *malas* permanently removed. The liberated soul possesses unlimited knowledge and bliss, lacking only the power of cosmic creation, which belongs to Shiva alone.
