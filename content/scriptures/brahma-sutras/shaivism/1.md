Srikantha's Shaiva commentary on Samanvaya Adhyaya establishes that the Brahman taught consistently throughout the Upanishads is Shiva — the supreme consciousness who creates, sustains, and dissolves the universe through His inherent Shakti. Every Upanishadic description of Brahman's nature — omniscience, bliss, luminosity, lordship — is read as pointing to Shiva as described in the Shaiva Agamas.

The key hermeneutical move is integrating Vedic and Agamic authority. Srikantha argues that the Shaiva Agamas are not heterodox additions but authentic elaborations of Vedic teaching. When the Upanishads describe Brahman as the source of all beings (Taittiriya 3.1), the inner controller (Brhadaranyaka 3.7), and the one God hidden in all creatures (Svetasvatara 6.11), they are describing Shiva. The Svetasvatara Upanishad, which explicitly names Shiva, is treated as the hermeneutical key to interpreting all other Upanishadic passages about Brahman.
