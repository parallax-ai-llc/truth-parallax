Philosophical analysis examines the six substances as a pluralist ontology. Unlike monistic systems (Advaita Vedanta) or nihilistic ones (some Buddhist readings), Jainism posits an irreducible plurality of eternal substances, raising fundamental questions about the nature of reality.
