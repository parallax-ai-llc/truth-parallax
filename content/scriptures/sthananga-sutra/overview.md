## Overview

The Sthananga Sutra (Sthānāṅga Sūtra) is the third of the twelve Angas of the Shvetambara Jain canon. It is a systematic enumeration text that classifies Jain doctrine according to numerical categories, proceeding from groups of one through groups of ten. This organizational method makes it an invaluable reference for the entire range of Jain philosophical and practical teachings.

## Structure

The text is organized into 10 sections (sthanas), each corresponding to a numerical grouping:

1. **Section 1 — Units of One**: Singular concepts — the soul, matter, liberation
2. **Section 2 — Groups of Two**: Paired categories — jiva/ajiva, bondage/liberation
3. **Section 3 — Groups of Three**: Triads — the three jewels, three types of knowledge
4. **Section 4 — Groups of Four**: Quaternaries — four destinies, four passions
5. **Section 5 — Groups of Five**: Pentads — five great vows, five types of beings
6. **Section 6 — Groups of Six**: Hexads — six substances, six types of living beings
7. **Section 7 — Groups of Seven**: Heptads — seven tattvas, seven types of fear
8. **Section 8 — Groups of Eight**: Octads — eight types of karma, eight prabhavanas
9. **Section 9 — Groups of Nine**: Nonads — nine categories, nine padarthas
10. **Section 10 — Groups of Ten**: Decads — ten virtues, ten types of dharma

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Digambara**: Value the numerical framework while maintaining their own doctrinal elaborations
- **Shvetambara**: Consider it essential for systematic study of Jain categories
- **Academic**: A key resource for reconstructing classical Jain taxonomies
- **Comparative**: Parallels with Buddhist Anguttara Nikaya's numerical organization

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Numerical Classification | Systematic ordering of all Jain categories by number |
| Doctrinal Completeness | Encyclopedic coverage of Jain teachings |

| Karma Theory | Detailed enumeration of karmic types and subtypes |
