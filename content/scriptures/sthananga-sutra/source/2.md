---
title: "Sthananga Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "sthananga-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** There are two categories of existence: the soul and the non-soul. All reality falls within these two.

**2** There are two kinds of actions: those that bind and those that liberate. Actions performed with attachment bind. Actions performed without attachment liberate.

**3** There are two aspects of every substance: the universal and the particular. The universal is what it shares with other substances. The particular is what distinguishes it.

**4** There are two kinds of penance: external penance, which involves the body, and internal penance, which involves the mind.

**5** There are two obstacles to spiritual progress: attachment to pleasure and aversion to pain. Both must be overcome.

**6** There are two sources of right knowledge: perception and inference. Through these, the wise discern the truth.

**7** There are two aspects of dharma: the teaching itself and the practice of the teaching. Both are essential.

**8** There are two kinds of beings: those who have attained self-control and those who have not. The former are on the path; the latter are not.

**9** There are two kinds of happiness: worldly happiness, which is temporary, and spiritual happiness, which is eternal.

**10** This is the teaching concerning pairs, as declared by the venerable Mahavira.
