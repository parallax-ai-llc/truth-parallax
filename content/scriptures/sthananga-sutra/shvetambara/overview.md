## Overview

For the Shvetambara tradition, the Sthananga Sutra is the third Anga of the canonical scriptures and serves as an indispensable reference for the systematic study of Jain doctrine. Its numerical method of organization makes it a foundational text for monastic education and scholarly research.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Canonical Reference

Shvetambara scholars treat the Sthananga Sutra as an authoritative encyclopedic reference, using its enumerations to verify and organize teachings found across the broader Agamic literature.

### Pedagogical Foundation

The text's progression from categories of one through categories of ten provides a natural curriculum for monastic students, building complexity gradually and ensuring comprehensive coverage of all doctrinal topics.

### Doctrinal Integration

The Sthananga Sutra's enumerations help Shvetambara scholars integrate diverse teachings from different parts of the canon into a unified systematic framework.
