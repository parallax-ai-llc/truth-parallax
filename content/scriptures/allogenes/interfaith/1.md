## The Call to Awakening: How Spiritual Journeys Begin

Allogenes' journey begins with a call — the arrival of Youel, who summons him from ordinary consciousness to a revelatory encounter with the divine. This pattern of the initiating call resonates across the world's spiritual traditions as one of the most universal structures of religious experience.

In the Hebrew Bible, God calls Abraham to leave his homeland (Genesis 12:1), Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3:4), and Samuel in the night (1 Samuel 3:4). In the Buddhist tradition, Siddhartha's encounter with the four sights (old age, sickness, death, and a renunciant) catalyzes his departure from the palace. In Sufism, the concept of *jadhba* (divine attraction) describes the moment when God "pulls" the soul toward the spiritual path. In indigenous traditions, the vision quest or initiatory dream marks the beginning of the shaman's calling.

The interfaith pattern suggests that spiritual transformation rarely begins with a deliberate decision but rather with an encounter that disrupts ordinary life — a call, a vision, a crisis, a question that will not be silenced. Allogenes does not choose to receive Youel's revelation; it arrives unbidden, shattering his previous understanding and setting him on a journey he could not have anticipated.
