## The Triple Power: Existence, Vitality, and Mentality

Youel reveals to Allogenes that the divine manifests as three interrelated aspects: Existence (*hyparxis*), Vitality (*zoe*), and Mentality (*nous*). These three constitute the "Triple-Powered One" — a being that is simultaneously one and three, undivided yet differentiated. For Gnostic readers versed in Platonic philosophy, this triad maps onto the Platonic categories of Being, Life, and Mind that structure the intelligible world.

The Triple Power represents the first stage of divine self-disclosure — the point at which the absolutely undifferentiated Unknowable One begins to express itself in knowable form. Existence is the sheer fact of divine being; Vitality is the dynamic energy by which the divine lives and moves; Mentality is the self-reflective awareness by which the divine knows itself. Together, they constitute the fullness of the divine as it can be apprehended by the ascending soul.

For Gnostic interpreters, the Triple Power provides the ontological foundation for the Barbelo aeon and, ultimately, for the entire cosmos. Every level of reality participates in Existence, Vitality, and Mentality — from the highest aeons to the material world — though in progressively diminished forms. The Gnostic path is the reversal of this diminishment: the ascent from fragmented, material existence to the unified fullness of the Triple-Powered One.
