## Levels of Reality: Cosmological Hierarchies

The Barbelo aeon's three-leveled structure — Kalyptos, Protophanes, Autogenes — exemplifies the cross-cultural tendency to organize reality hierarchically, with progressively more refined levels ascending toward the ultimate. This hierarchical impulse appears across contemplative traditions as a map of both the cosmos and the soul.

The Kabbalistic Tree of Life organizes ten sefirot into three pillars and four worlds, with each level representing a progressively more manifest expression of the divine *Ein Sof*. The Buddhist cosmology of the three realms (*triloka*) — desire, form, and formlessness — describes progressively subtler levels of existence. The Sufi schema of *shari'a* (law), *tariqa* (path), *haqiqa* (reality), and *ma'rifa* (gnosis) maps a progressive interiorization of religious experience.

In each case, the hierarchy is not merely descriptive but prescriptive: it provides a map for the spiritual journey, indicating where the seeker currently stands and what lies ahead. The Barbelo aeon functions similarly in Allogenes: it is both the structure of divine reality and the itinerary of the ascending soul.
