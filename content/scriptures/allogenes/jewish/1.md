## Youel and Jewish Angelic Mediation

The figure of Youel as Allogenes' primary revealer has significant connections to Jewish angelic traditions. The name Youel may derive from a Hebrew theophoric construction incorporating *El* ("God"), placing it within the broader pattern of Jewish angel names (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel) that encode divine attributes or functions.

The role of angelic mediators in Jewish tradition is well established. In the book of Daniel, Gabriel explains visions (Daniel 8:16, 9:21). In 1 Enoch, Uriel guides Enoch through the heavenly realms. In the Apocalypse of Abraham, the angel Yahoel (a name strikingly similar to Youel) serves as Abraham's celestial guide. The *hekhalot* literature describes angels who accompany the mystic through the heavenly palaces, providing the names and seals needed to pass each guardian.

Allogenes transforms this tradition by making the angelic mediator female — a departure from the predominantly male angelic figures of Jewish canonical and apocalyptic literature. However, the Wisdom tradition's personification of *Hokhmah* (Wisdom) as a feminine divine figure (Proverbs 8, Sirach 24, Wisdom of Solomon 7-8) provides a Jewish precedent for feminine divine mediation. The Sethian appropriation thus draws on and transforms an existing Jewish pattern.
