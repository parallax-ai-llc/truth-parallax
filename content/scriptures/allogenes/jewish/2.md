## The Triple Power and Jewish Divine Attributes

The Triple-Powered One's three aspects — Existence, Vitality, Mentality — can be productively compared with Jewish theological reflection on the divine attributes (*middot*). While rabbinic theology generally resisted systematic categorization of God's nature, certain patterns are discernible: the divine is understood as the source of being (Creator), the source of life (the living God, *El Hai*), and the source of wisdom (*Hokhmah*).

The Kabbalistic tradition later developed a more elaborate system of divine attributes through the ten sefirot, which describe distinct aspects of God's self-manifestation. The upper triad of the sefirot — Keter (Crown), Hokhmah (Wisdom), and Binah (Understanding) — bears a structural resemblance to the Sethian Triple Power, though the content and theological context differ significantly.

For Jewish readers, the comparison illustrates how Sethian Gnosticism and rabbinic/mystical Judaism drew on a shared pool of theological intuitions about the divine nature while developing them in dramatically different directions. The Sethian system locates the Triple Power above and beyond the created order; the rabbinic tradition locates God's attributes within the covenantal relationship with Israel.
