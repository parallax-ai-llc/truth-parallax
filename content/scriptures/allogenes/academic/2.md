## The Triple Power and Middle Platonic Metaphysics

The description of the Triple-Powered One as Existence (*hyparxis*), Vitality (*zoe*), and Mentality (*nous*) represents the most philosophically sophisticated passage in the text and has been central to debates about the relationship between Gnosticism and Neoplatonism. John Turner's landmark study demonstrated that this triad corresponds precisely to the Platonic categories of Being, Life, and Mind as developed in Middle Platonic commentary on Plato's *Sophist* and *Timaeus*.

The question of direction of influence remains debated. Did the Sethian authors draw on Middle Platonic philosophy to articulate their theological vision, or did Sethian theology develop independently and later come into contact with Platonism? Turner argues for a gradual "Platonization" of Sethian thought, with Allogenes representing a mature stage of this process. Kevin Corrigan has proposed a more reciprocal relationship, suggesting that Sethian texts may have influenced Plotinus's own development of the triad Being-Life-Mind in Ennead VI.

The philosophical vocabulary of the Triple Power section — *hyparxis*, *energeia*, *dynamis* — is technical and precise, suggesting an author (or authorial community) with formal philosophical training. This challenges the assumption, common since Hans Jonas, that Gnosticism was fundamentally anti-philosophical.
