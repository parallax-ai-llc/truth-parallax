## Overview

The Niralamba Upanishad ("Upanishad of Independence" or "Without Support") belongs to the Shukla Yajur Veda. The title *niralamba* means "without support" or "self-dependent," referring to the self that depends on nothing external for its existence or knowledge. The text proceeds through a systematic question-and-answer format, addressing fundamental philosophical questions about the nature of reality, liberation, knowledge, and the self. Its catechetical structure makes it an excellent summary of Vedantic teaching.

## Structure

The Niralamba Upanishad consists of three sections:

1. **Section 1 — Questions on Reality**: What is Brahman? What is the self? What is the world?
2. **Section 2 — Questions on Liberation**: What is bondage? What is liberation? Who is the knower?
3. **Section 3 — The Self-Dependent Truth**: The self as the unsupported support of all existence

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: The self-dependent being is Vishnu, the uncaused cause
- **Shaivism**: Independence (svatantrya) as Shiva's essential nature
- **Advaita Vedanta**: The self as self-luminous and self-dependent — the classic Advaita position
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Only God is truly self-dependent; souls depend on His grace
- **Academic**: A catechetical text summarizing Vedantic positions in Q&A format
- **Comparative**: Self-existence and aseity across philosophical and theological traditions

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Self-Dependence | The self as the reality that depends on nothing else |
| Catechetical Method | Systematic Q&A addressing fundamental philosophical questions |
| Nature of Brahman | The absolute reality beyond all categories |
| Bondage and Liberation | The nature of spiritual enslavement and freedom |
| Self-Knowledge | The recognition of the self as self-luminous awareness |
