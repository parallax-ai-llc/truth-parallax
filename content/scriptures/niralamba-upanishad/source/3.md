---
title: "Niralamba Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "niralamba-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** What is the supportless, the Niralamba? It is that Brahman which has no support other than itself, which is the support of all, yet depends on nothing.

**2** It is not the body, for the body is inert and perishable. It is not the Prana, for Prana is merely a function. It is not the mind, for the mind is fickle and subject to change. It is not the intellect, for the intellect is limited.

**3** It is that which remains when all adjuncts are negated — not this, not this — that pure consciousness which is the witness of all states — waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.

**4** As fire, though one, entering the world, takes a separate form according to whatever it burns, so the one inner Atman of all beings takes a form according to whatever form it enters, and yet exists outside all forms.

**5** As the sun, which is the eye of the whole world, is not defiled by the external faults seen by the eye, so the inner Atman of all beings is not defiled by the misery of the world, being external to it.

**6** The one supreme Self, the inner controller of all beings, who makes His one form manifold — the wise who perceive Him as abiding in the soul, to them belongs eternal bliss, and to no others.

**7** He who knows this supportless Brahman, in which the five groups of elements, the Pranas, and the mind are all woven — he, the wise one, becomes immortal.

**8** When all desires that dwell in the heart are destroyed, then the mortal becomes immortal, and he attains Brahman in this very life.

**9** When all the knots of the heart are cut asunder, then the mortal becomes immortal. This much is the teaching.

**10** Om! Thus ends the Niralamba Upanishad, as contained in the Shukla Yajur Veda. Om Shanti, Shanti, Shanti.
