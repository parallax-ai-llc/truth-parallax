---
title: "Niralamba Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "niralamba-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** What is Para? It is that supreme Brahman which is beyond all names and forms, beyond all qualities, beyond the reach of speech and mind, self-luminous, self-existent, infinite, and eternal.

**2** What is Apara? It is the lower knowledge — the knowledge of the Vedas, of rituals, of grammar, of the sciences — all that pertains to the world of names and forms.

**3** What is Bandha, bondage? It is the false identification of the Atman with the body, the senses, the mind, and the intellect. It is the delusion that says "I am the body, I am the doer, I am the enjoyer."

**4** What is Moksha, liberation? It is the direct realization that the Atman is Brahman, that there is no duality, that the individual self and the universal Self are one and the same. It is the removal of ignorance by knowledge.

**5** This bondage arises from ignorance alone and is destroyed by knowledge alone. When one knows "I am Brahman" — this knowledge alone is liberation.

**6** As the Akasa in the pot merges into the universal Akasa when the pot is broken, so the Jivatman merges into Paramatman when ignorance is destroyed.

**7** As a dream disappears upon waking, so the world of names and forms disappears upon the dawn of knowledge.

**8** As the rope-serpent disappears when the rope is known, so the world of multiplicity disappears when Brahman is known.

**9** There is no creation, no destruction, no bondage, no seeker, no aspirant, and no one liberated — this is the supreme truth.

**10** The Atman is one, eternal, pure, self-luminous, free, all-pervading, and non-dual. This is the teaching of the Niralamba.
