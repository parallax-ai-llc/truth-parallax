---
title: "Niralamba Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "niralamba-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om! I shall now describe the knowledge of the supportless — the Niralamba — which alone leads to the attainment of Kaivalya, which destroys the bonds of worldly existence, and which is the highest knowledge.

**2** What is Brahman? What is Atman? What is Jivatman? What is Prakriti? What is Paramatman? What is Brahma, Vishnu, and Rudra? What are Indra, Yama, Surya, Chandra, the Devas, Asuras, Pisachas, Manushyas, Stri, Purusha, and Napumsaka? What are Jati? What are Kriya?

**3** What is Para? What is Apara? What is Moksha? What is Bandha? What is the supportless? These are the questions; now hear the answers.

**4** Brahman is Sat-Chit-Ananda — Existence, Consciousness, Bliss — which is without parts, without qualities, without action, without fault, and without modification.

**5** Atman is that which shines as the consciousness in all beings, as one light manifests in many forms.

**6** Jivatman is Atman itself, limited by the adjuncts of the intellect and mind, seeming to be born, to grow, to decay, and to die; just as Akasa, though one, appears as the Akasa of the pot, the Akasa of the room.

**7** Prakriti is that which creates and manifests names and forms. She is the power of the Lord, the cause of the manifest and the unmanifest.

**8** Paramatman is the witness, the illuminator, the one who is the substratum of all, the infinite, the eternal, beyond the reach of speech and mind.

**9** Brahma is that aspect of the supreme Self which creates. Vishnu is that aspect which preserves. Rudra is that aspect which destroys.

**10** Indra is the lord of the senses. Yama is the restrainer. Surya is the illuminator. Chandra is the mind. The Devas are the sense organs. The Asuras are the evil tendencies.

**11** The Pisachas are impure desires. Humans are those beings possessed of the egoistic sense of "I am the doer." Women, men, and the neuter — these are distinctions pertaining to the body alone, not to the Atman.

**12** There is no caste for the Atman, for caste belongs to the body alone. There are no actions for the Atman, for actions pertain to the body and mind.
