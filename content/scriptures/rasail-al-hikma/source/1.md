## The Epistle of Unification

**1.** In the Name of the Compassionate, the Merciful, the Lord of ages. Praise to Him who is the First without a beginning and the Last without an end. He is the Manifest in all creation and the Hidden beyond all comprehension.

**2.** Know, O seeker of truth, that the foundation of wisdom is the knowledge of the One, who has no partner, no likeness, and no equal.

**3.** He is not a body, nor an attribute of a body. He does not occupy space, and time does not contain Him. He is before "before" and after "after."

**4.** Every attempt to define Him limits Him, and every attempt to describe Him fails, for He transcends all categories of thought.

**5.** He created the Universal Intellect, which is the first of His creations, and through the Universal Intellect He created the Universal Soul, and through the Universal Soul He created the world of matter.

**6.** The Universal Intellect is like the sun, and the Universal Soul is like the light that emanates from the sun, and the world of matter is like the shadows cast by that light.

**7.** The one who knows only the shadows lives in darkness. The one who sees the light lives in knowledge. The one who gazes upon the sun itself lives in truth.

**8.** The purpose of creation is the return of the soul to its origin. The soul descended from the world of light into the world of matter, and its journey is the journey of return.

**9.** This return is not a return in space or time—it is a return in consciousness. The soul that remembers its origin has already begun the return.

**10.** The veils between the soul and its origin are the veils of ignorance. Each veil removed brings the soul closer to the light of truth.

**11.** The first veil is the veil of the senses, which shows the world as it appears rather than as it is. The second veil is the veil of the intellect, which knows by categories and distinctions. The third veil is the veil of the self, which sees everything in relation to itself.

**12.** When all three veils are removed, the soul sees with the eye of truth, and the eye of truth sees only the One.

**13.** The One is not one among many—He is the One from whom the many arise, and to whom the many return. He is not a number—He is the source of all numbers.

**14.** The wise man speaks of the One in silence, for every word about the One creates a duality: the speaker and the spoken-of. In silence, the duality dissolves.

**15.** This is the epistle of unification: to know that there is no reality but the One Reality, and that all apparent realities are reflections of that One in the mirror of creation.
