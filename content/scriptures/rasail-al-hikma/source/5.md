## The Epistle of the Hidden and the Manifest

**1.** The One has two aspects: the Hidden and the Manifest. As the Hidden, He is beyond all knowledge and all description. As the Manifest, He is the source of all creation and all appearance.

**2.** The Hidden is called al-Batin—the Inner, the Concealed, the Essence that no mind can reach. The Manifest is called al-Zahir—the Outer, the Apparent, the Face that every creature beholds.

**3.** These two aspects are not two separate things—they are two faces of one Reality. The coin has two sides, but it is one coin.

**4.** Everything in creation has a hidden aspect and a manifest aspect. The body is the manifest; the soul is the hidden. The word is the manifest; the meaning is the hidden. The world is the manifest; God is the hidden.

**5.** The error of the materialist is to see only the manifest and to deny the hidden. The error of the extreme mystic is to see only the hidden and to deny the manifest. The wise man sees both.

**6.** The hidden and the manifest are in constant interplay. What is hidden becomes manifest, and what is manifest becomes hidden. The seed becomes the tree, and the tree produces the seed. The meaning becomes the word, and the word carries the meaning.

**7.** The purpose of the spiritual life is to perceive the hidden within the manifest—to see the soul within the body, the meaning within the word, God within the world.

**8.** The one who sees only the outer shell of things lives on the surface of existence. The one who penetrates to the inner reality lives at the depth.

**9.** The parable, the symbol, and the allegory are the bridges between the hidden and the manifest. They give form to the formless and voice to the silent.

**10.** Do not take the symbol for the reality it represents, for the map is not the territory, and the finger pointing at the moon is not the moon.

**11.** But do not discard the symbol, for without it, the mind has no means of approaching the hidden. The symbol is the boat that carries you across the river; once across, you leave the boat, but you honor it for the crossing.

**12.** The great teachers spoke in symbols because the truth they carried was too vast for literal language. The one who hears only the literal misses the ocean and catches only a shell.

**13.** The hidden God reveals Himself through the manifest world. Every sunset, every mountain, every face is a revelation. The one who has eyes to see reads these revelations as the scholar reads a book.

**14.** The manifest world conceals the hidden God. The very beauty that reveals Him also veils Him, for the eye that is captivated by the beauty stops looking for the Beautiful.

**15.** The interplay of the hidden and the manifest is the dance of existence. Learn to follow this dance, and you will find that the Hidden and the Manifest are partners, not opposites—and that their dance is the dance of the One.
