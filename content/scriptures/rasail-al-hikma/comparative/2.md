A comparative reading of Volume 2 of the Rasa'il al-Hikma situates its treatment of universal soul within the broader context of world religious literature. The themes of al-Nafs, soul find parallels and contrasts in multiple traditions, offering rich material for cross-cultural analysis.

Comparative scholars have identified resonances between this volume's teachings and similar discussions in other scriptures and philosophical texts. These parallels illuminate both universal patterns in human religious thought and the distinctive contributions of Druze Faith to the world's understanding of universal soul.
