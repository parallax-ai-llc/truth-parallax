The Prashna Upanishad's cosmogony through the pairing of matter and spirit resonates with dualistic creation accounts across traditions. The Chinese concept of Yin and Yang, the Zoroastrian pairing of *spenta mainyu* (creative spirit) and *angra mainyu* (destructive spirit), and the Kabbalistic interplay of *din* (judgment) and *chesed* (mercy) all envision creation as the product of complementary principles. The Prashna's Rayi-Prana pairing is distinctive in treating these as cooperative rather than oppositional.

The two paths after death — leading either to liberation or to rebirth — parallel afterlife doctrines in many traditions. The Egyptian weighing of the heart before Osiris, the Christian judgment leading to heaven or continued purification, and the Buddhist concept of rebirth determined by karma all share the Prashna's conviction that post-mortem destiny is shaped by the quality of one's life.
