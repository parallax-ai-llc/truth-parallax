The Prashna Upanishad's five-fold classification of Prana resonates with breath-related physiological systems in other traditions. Chinese medicine's *qi* circulates through the body via distinct channels and functions, similar to the five pranas. Tibetan medicine identifies five types of *lung* (wind) corresponding closely to the five pranas. Greek medicine's *pneuma* (breath/spirit) was also understood as distributed through the body with distinct functions, as described by Galen.

The king-and-governors analogy (3.4) for the relationship between the self and its bodily functions echoes Plato's analogy of the tripartite soul governed by reason (the charioteer) and the Confucian concept of the ruler's virtue radiating outward to order the state and the cosmos. Each tradition recognizes a governing principle within the person that must maintain order among subordinate functions — and identifies this principle with something higher than the body itself.
