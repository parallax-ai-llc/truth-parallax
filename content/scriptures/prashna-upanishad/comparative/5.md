The Prashna's teaching on AUM meditation resonates with sacred syllable practices across traditions. The Christian use of "Amen" (etymologically related to affirmation and truth), the Islamic *Bismillah* ("In the name of God"), the Buddhist *Om mani padme hum*, and the Sikh *Ik Onkar* all share the conviction that certain utterances serve as concentrations of divine reality — gateways through which the practitioner accesses the sacred.

The graduated results of AUM meditation — from worldly benefit to celestial attainment to liberation — parallel the Buddhist concept of the three vehicles (*yana*), the Sufi distinction between *shari'a* (law), *tariqa* (path), and *haqiqa* (truth), and the Christian mystical distinction between purgative, illuminative, and unitive stages. Each tradition recognizes that the same practice can be engaged at different levels of depth, yielding correspondingly different fruits.
