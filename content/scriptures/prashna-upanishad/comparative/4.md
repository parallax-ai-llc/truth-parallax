The Prashna's analysis of sleep and dreams anticipates and parallels explorations of consciousness states across traditions. The Tibetan Buddhist tradition of dream yoga (*milam*) practices lucid awareness within the dream state, similar to the Upanishadic teaching that the wise person recognizes the dream as self-created. Aristotle's *On Dreams* and *On Sleep and Waking* offer a naturalistic analysis of the same phenomena that the Prashna approaches from a spiritual perspective.

The teaching that the self experiences happiness in deep sleep — in the absence of all objects and all mental activity — resonates with contemplative traditions that distinguish between pleasures dependent on conditions and a deeper happiness inherent in awareness itself. The Buddhist concept of *sukha* (wellbeing) in deep meditation, the Christian mystical "peace that passes understanding," and the Daoist *wu wei* (effortless being) all point to a joy that arises when the mind's constant grasping ceases.
