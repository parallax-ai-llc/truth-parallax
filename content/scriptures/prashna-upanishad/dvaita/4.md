Madhva reads Chapter 4 as describing how Vishnu governs the soul through the three states of consciousness. In waking, the Lord directs the soul's engagement with the world through the senses. In dreaming, He presents karmic impressions to the mind for processing. In deep sleep, He holds the soul in His care as it rests from activity. The happiness of deep sleep is a gift of God's grace — a temporary respite from the soul's karmic burden.

The distinction between the three states demonstrates that the soul and God are always distinct: in each state, the soul has different experiences while God remains the unchanging controller and witness. If the soul were identical with God (as Advaita claims), there would be no transition between states and no variation in experience. The fact that the soul sleeps, dreams, and wakes proves that it is a dependent being whose states are governed by the Lord.
