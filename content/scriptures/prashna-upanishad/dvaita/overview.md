## Overview

Madhva reads the Prashna Upanishad as teaching the absolute supremacy of Vishnu through the metaphor of Prana. Just as Prana is supreme among the bodily faculties, so Vishnu is supreme among all beings and principles. The six questions structure a hierarchy of knowledge, with each answer pointing to a deeper level of Vishnu's sovereignty over creation, life, consciousness, and liberation.

## Theological Emphases

The Dvaita reading of the rivers-and-ocean simile (Chapter 6) differs sharply from Advaita: the rivers do not lose their existence when they reach the ocean but continue to exist within it. Similarly, liberated souls do not merge into Brahman but dwell within His abode, retaining their individuality while experiencing His bliss. The AUM meditation (Chapter 5) is understood as devotion to Vishnu through His sacred syllable, with the three components representing the Lord's roles as creator, sustainer, and liberator. The sixteen parts are real aspects of the person, not illusions to be dissolved.
