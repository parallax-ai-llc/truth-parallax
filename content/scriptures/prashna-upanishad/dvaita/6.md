Madhva reads the person with sixteen parts as Vishnu in His cosmic manifestation. The sixteen parts are real aspects of God's creative expression — genuine principles that emerge from and depend on the Lord. The river-ocean simile is interpreted differently from Advaita: the rivers do not cease to exist when they reach the ocean but continue to exist within it. Similarly, liberated souls do not lose their individual identity but dwell within Vishnu's infinite being, experiencing His bliss while remaining distinct persons.

The teaching that the liberated person becomes "free from parts" means freedom from the limiting conditions of embodied existence — from karma, from ignorance, from suffering — not freedom from individual selfhood. The soul retains its essential nature as a knower and enjoyer, but its knowing and enjoying are directed entirely toward Vishnu. This is liberation in the Dvaita sense: eternal, individual, blissful existence in the presence of the infinite God.
