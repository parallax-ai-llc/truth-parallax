Madhva reads Chapter 3 as a detailed account of how Vishnu governs the body through the five pranas. Each prana has its assigned function and region, demonstrating the ordered hierarchy that characterizes all of God's creation. The teaching that Prana originates from the Atman and distributes itself like a king appointing governors directly supports the Dvaita understanding of Vishnu as the supreme administrator who governs every aspect of existence through delegated authorities.

The five pranas function under Vishnu's direction, not independently. Their proper operation depends on God's sustaining will, and their dysfunction reflects either karmic consequences or divine testing. The chapter reinforces the Dvaita principle that no aspect of embodied life — not even breathing — operates independently of God's sovereign governance. Understanding this dependence is itself a form of devotion.
