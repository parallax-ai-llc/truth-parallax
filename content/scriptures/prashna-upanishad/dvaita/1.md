Madhva reads the creation through Rayi and Prana as describing Vishnu's real creative act through His material and spiritual energies. The world that emerges is real, not illusory, and its ordered structure reflects divine intelligence. The pairing of matter and spirit demonstrates that creation requires both a material cause and an efficient cause — and both ultimately derive from Vishnu, who is the source and controller of all principles.

The two paths after death are real post-mortem trajectories governed by Vishnu's sovereign will. The soul's destination is determined by a combination of accumulated karma and divine judgment. The *devayana* leads to gradual liberation through the world of Brahma, while the *pitriyana* leads to rebirth. The Dvaita emphasis is that neither path operates mechanically — Vishnu personally directs each soul's journey according to His justice and mercy.
