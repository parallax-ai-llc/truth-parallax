Madhva reads the AUM meditation as a devotional practice with real and graduated results. The three syllables A, U, and M correspond to the three aspects of Vishnu's cosmic governance — creation, preservation, and dissolution — and meditation on each aspect yields corresponding fruits. The complete AUM meditation directs the devotee's consciousness toward Vishnu in His fullness, leading to liberation in the form of eternal dwelling in Vishnu's presence.

The graduated results of AUM meditation demonstrate the Dvaita principle that spiritual attainment corresponds to the depth and sincerity of devotion. Partial devotion yields partial results; complete devotion yields the highest reward. The meditation on AUM is not a technique for merging with the Absolute but a practice of focusing the mind entirely on Vishnu, whose sacred syllable AUM encapsulates His infinite nature in a form accessible to the human devotee.
