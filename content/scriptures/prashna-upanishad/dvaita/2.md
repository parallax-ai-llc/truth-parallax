Madhva reads the contest of the faculties as a straightforward demonstration of Vishnu's supremacy, represented by Prana. When Prana rises to depart, all faculties must follow — just as when Vishnu withdraws His sustaining power, all beings and principles collapse. The hierarchy is real and permanent: Vishnu is supreme, and all other beings and powers exist in a graded order of dependence beneath Him.

The hymn of the faculties to Prana (2.5-13) is genuine devotion — the lesser beings recognizing and praising the greater. This models the proper relationship of all souls to God: recognition of dependence, gratitude for sustenance, and joyful submission to the Lord's supremacy. The chapter demonstrates that right knowledge begins with right relationship — understanding one's place in the divinely ordained hierarchy.
