Shankara's commentary on Chapter 3 explains the five pranas as functional aspects of the one Prana, which itself is a manifestation of Brahman within the empirical realm. The detailed account of prana, apana, samana, vyana, and udana describes the mechanism by which the subtle body operates — knowledge that belongs to the lower (*apara*) domain but serves to demonstrate Brahman's immanence within the body.

The analogy of the king and governors (3.4) illustrates a key Advaita principle: just as a king rules through deputies without personally performing their functions, so Brahman sustains the body through the pranas without being affected by bodily conditions. The self (Brahman) is the uninvolved witness that gives rise to Prana and its functions but remains untouched by their activity — just as the sun illuminates all activities without participating in them.
