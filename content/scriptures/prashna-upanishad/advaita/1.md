Shankara reads the first question's cosmogony as an account of creation from the empirical standpoint (*vyavaharika*). The pairing of Rayi and Prana represents the duality that characterizes the manifest world — matter and spirit, object and subject, the seen and the seer. From the absolute standpoint (*paramarthika*), there is no creation at all; Brahman alone exists, and the appearance of multiplicity is the product of *avidya* (ignorance).

The two paths after death — *devayana* and *pitriyana* — belong to the realm of action and meditation (*karma* and *upasana*). Neither path leads to final liberation, which comes only from the direct knowledge of Brahman. Shankara clarifies that the *jnani* (knower of Brahman) does not travel any path at death but is immediately liberated — the self recognizes its identity with Brahman, and the appearance of individual existence dissolves like a mirage.
