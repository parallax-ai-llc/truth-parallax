The contest of the faculties, resolved by Prana's supremacy, demonstrates the Shaiva principle that consciousness (*chit*) is the ground of all experience. Individual faculties — speech, sight, hearing, mind — are specific functions of the one consciousness, and when the underlying awareness withdraws, they all collapse. Prana represents not merely biological breath but the fundamental vibration (*spanda*) of Shiva's consciousness that animates all functions.

The hymn of praise to Prana echoes the Shaiva devotional tradition's celebration of Shiva as the life of all lives. The faculties' recognition that they owe their existence to Prana mirrors the Shaiva practitioner's recognition that all experience — including the sense of being an individual experiencer — is a gift of Shiva's consciousness. This is *pratyabhijna* in its most practical form: recognizing the divine source of every moment of awareness.
