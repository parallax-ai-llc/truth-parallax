The distribution of Prana into five functions within the body maps the Shaiva understanding of how consciousness differentiates itself into specific capacities. The five pranas are five modes of Shiva's creative vibration (*spanda*) operating within the body — prana moving outward (creation), apana moving downward (sustenance), samana equalizing (maintenance), vyana pervading (distribution), and udana ascending (transcendence). The five pranas correspond to five aspects of Shiva's cosmic activity: creation, maintenance, dissolution, concealment, and revelation of grace.

The teaching that Prana originates from the Atman (3.3) and enters the body "like a king appointing governors" (3.4) resonates with the Shaiva understanding of consciousness deploying itself through the *tattvas* (categories of manifestation). The king is Shiva-consciousness; the governors are the specific functions through which consciousness operates in the embodied state. Recognition means seeing through the governors to the king — perceiving the one consciousness behind all the diversified functions of life.
