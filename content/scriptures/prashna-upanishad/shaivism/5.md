AUM meditation is central to Shaiva practice, and Pippalada's teaching on its three components corresponds to the Shaiva analysis of the three states of consciousness. A represents waking consciousness (the gross dimension of Shiva's manifestation), U represents the dream state (the subtle dimension), and M represents deep sleep (the causal dimension). The complete syllable, including the silence that follows, represents *turiya* — the fourth state of pure Shiva-consciousness.

The graduated fruits of AUM meditation — from human rebirth through celestial attainment to liberation — demonstrate that the depth of realization depends on the completeness of practice. Partial awareness of Shiva's nature yields partial results. Only the complete recognition of Shiva in all states of consciousness — waking, dreaming, sleeping, and the transcendent fourth — yields complete liberation. The silence after AUM is the most important "syllable": it is the space of pure awareness in which Shiva recognizes Himself.
