Pippalada's cosmogony — creation through the union of Rayi (matter) and Prana (spirit) — resonates with the Shaiva understanding of creation as the interplay of Shiva and Shakti. Prana, the animating life-force, is Shiva's dynamic consciousness, while Rayi, the material substrate, is Shakti's creative power. Their union produces the manifest universe, just as Shiva and Shakti's union is the source of all existence in Shaiva theology.

The two paths after death — solar and lunar — correspond in Shaiva thought to liberation and continued bondage. The solar path represents the progressive dissolution of limiting conditions (*malas*) until the soul recognizes its identity with Shiva. The lunar path represents continued identification with limited selfhood, leading to rebirth. The chapter's cosmological framework establishes that all of manifestation is the play (*lila*) of consciousness — Shiva's self-expression through the medium of cosmic principles.
