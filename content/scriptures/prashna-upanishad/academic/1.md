The Prashna Upanishad opens with a cosmogonic teaching that pairs Rayi (matter/food) and Prana (life/spirit) as the twin principles from which Prajapati creates all beings. Scholars have noted the similarities between this dualistic cosmogony and the later Samkhya system, which also posits a fundamental duality of *purusha* (consciousness) and *prakriti* (matter). Whether the Prashna's teaching represents a proto-Samkhya position or an independent development is debated.

The doctrine of the two paths — solar and lunar, *devayana* and *pitriyana* — appears also in the Chandogya and Brihadaranyaka Upanishads. Comparative analysis suggests a common source text or oral tradition. The chapter's integration of cosmogony, ritual (the year as sacrifice), and eschatology reflects the Atharva Veda's characteristically synthetic approach, drawing together diverse strands of Vedic thought into a unified framework.
