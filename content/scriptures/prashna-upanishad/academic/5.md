Chapter 5's teaching on AUM meditation represents one of the earlier systematic treatments of the *pranava* (sacred syllable) in Upanishadic literature. The three-fold analysis of AUM into A, U, and M, with each component corresponding to different cosmological and soteriological levels, became foundational for later developments in the Mandukya Upanishad and its *karika* (verse commentary) by Gaudapada.

Scholars have noted that the graduated results of AUM meditation — human rebirth, celestial attainment, and liberation — may reflect a practical compromise between different levels of spiritual aspiration within the Vedic community. The teaching accommodates ritualists seeking worldly benefits alongside contemplatives seeking liberation, suggesting that the Prashna Upanishad's audience included practitioners at various stages of the spiritual path. The phonetic analysis of AUM also connects to the Shiksha (phonetics) tradition and its understanding of how sounds carry meaning.
