The "contest of the faculties" narrative in Chapter 2 belongs to a genre found across the Brahmanas and Upanishads — the *vada* (debate) in which different bodily functions compete for supremacy. Scholars have traced this motif from the Shatapatha Brahmana through the Chandogya, Brihadaranyaka, and Kaushitaki Upanishads. The consistent victory of Prana reflects the central importance of breath in Vedic thought and anticipates later yogic traditions centered on breath control (*pranayama*).

The hymn of praise to Prana (2.5-13) has been analyzed as an example of how Vedic hymnic literature was adapted to philosophical purposes. The language and structure echo earlier Rig Vedic hymns to natural deities, but the subject — the vital breath — marks a significant inward turn from cosmic to psycho-physical concerns. This development from external deity to internal principle is characteristic of the Upanishadic revolution in Vedic thought.
