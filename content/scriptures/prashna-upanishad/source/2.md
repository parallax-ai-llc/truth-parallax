---
title: "Prashna Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "prashna-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Then Bhargava Vaidarbhi asked him: Master, how many powers support a creature? Which of them illuminate this body? And which among them is greatest?

**2** To him he said: Space is indeed such a power, and so are air, fire, water, earth, speech, mind, eye, and ear. These, having illuminated the body, declared: We support this body and uphold it.

**3** Prana, the chief breath, said to them: Do not deceive yourselves. It is I who, dividing myself fivefold, support this body and uphold it. They did not believe him.

**4** He, out of pride as it were, rose upward. When he rose up, all the others rose up with him; and when he settled down, all the others settled down with him. As when a queen bee rises, all the bees rise with her, and when she settles down, all settle down with her, even so did speech, mind, eye, and ear. Being satisfied, they praised Prana.

**5** He is fire, he burns; he is the sun; he is rain; he is Indra; he is wind; he is the earth; he is Matter. He is what has form and what is formless. He is immortal. As spokes are fastened in the hub of a wheel, so is everything fastened in this Prana.
