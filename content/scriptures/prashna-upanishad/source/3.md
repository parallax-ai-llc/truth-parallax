---
title: "Prashna Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "prashna-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Then Kausalya Ashvalayana asked him: Master, whence is this Prana born? How does it come into this body? How does it distribute itself and establish itself? How does it go out? How does it support what is without, and how what is within?

**2** To him he said: You ask questions that are beyond the usual. Because you are a great seeker of Brahman, therefore I tell you.

**3** This Prana is born of the Self. As a shadow is cast by a person, so this Prana is spread by the Self. By the work of the mind it comes into this body.

**4** As a sovereign commands his officers, saying: Govern these villages or those villages, so does this Prana dispose the other vital breaths, each in its separate place.

**5** The Apana dwells in the organs of excretion and generation; the Prana itself abides in the eye and the ear, passing through the mouth and the nose. In the middle is the Samana; it distributes equally what has been offered as food. From this arise the seven flames.
