---
title: "Prashna Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "prashna-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Then Sauryayani Gargya asked him: Master, what are they that sleep in this person? What are they that are awake in him? Which is the god that sees dreams? Whose is the happiness? In whom do all these become established?

**2** To him he said: O Gargya, as all the rays of the setting sun become one in that circle of light, and as they spread forth when the sun rises again, even so does all this become one in the highest god, the Mind. Therefore in that state a person hears not, sees not, smells not, tastes not, touches not, speaks not, takes not, enjoys not, excretes not, moves not. They say: He sleeps.

**3** The fires of the Pranas alone are awake in this city of the body. The Apana is the householder's fire. The Vyana is the southern sacrificial fire. The Prana is the eastern fire, and because it is taken from the householder's fire, the Samana is so called.

**4** The Mind is the sacrificer. The fruit of the sacrifice is the Udana. It leads the sacrificer every day to Brahman.

**5** In deep sleep that god, the Mind, experiences greatness. Whatever has been seen, he sees again; whatever has been heard, he hears again; whatever has been enjoyed in various places and quarters, he enjoys again. The seen and the not-seen, the heard and the not-heard, the experienced and the not-experienced, the existent and the non-existent — he sees all, being All, he sees.
