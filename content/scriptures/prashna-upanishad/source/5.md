---
title: "Prashna Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "prashna-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Then Shaibya Satyakama asked him: Master, he among men who meditates on the syllable Om until the end of his life — what world does he win thereby?

**2** To him he said: O Satyakama, the syllable Om is indeed the higher and the lower Brahman. Therefore he who knows it attains, with its support, the one or the other.

**3** If he meditates on one letter alone, being enlightened thereby he quickly comes to earth. The hymns carry him to the world of men; and being endowed there with austerity, chastity, and faith, he experiences greatness.

**4** If he is united in his mind with two letters, he is carried by the Yajur hymns to the middle region, to the world of the moon. Having experienced greatness in the world of the moon, he returns again.

**5** Again, he who meditates on the Supreme Person through this syllable Om of three letters, he is united with the light, the sun. As a snake is freed from its skin, even so he is freed from sin. He is carried by the Sama hymns up to the world of Brahman, and from him, full of life, he beholds the Supreme Person who dwells in the body. On this there are two verses.
