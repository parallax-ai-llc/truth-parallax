The fourth question, from Gargya, asks about sleep — who sleeps, who wakes, who dreams, and who experiences happiness in deep sleep. Pippalada's answer explains that in sleep the mind absorbs the senses, and in deep sleep all faculties merge into Prana. Vaishnavas read the happiness experienced in deep sleep as a faint reflection of the soul's natural bliss in Vishnu's presence — temporarily freed from the disturbances of waking consciousness.

The teaching that all faculties rest in the mind during dream and in Prana during deep sleep demonstrates the hierarchy of subtle functions, with Prana (representing Vishnu's sustaining power) as the ground of all experience. The dreaming state, where the mind "re-sees what has been seen, re-hears what has been heard" (4.5), is read as the soul's experience of karmic impressions under Vishnu's supervision. Even in sleep, the Lord governs the soul's experience.
