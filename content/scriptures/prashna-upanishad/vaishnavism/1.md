The first question, posed by Kabandhi, asks the sage Pippalada about the origin of creatures. Pippalada's answer centers on Prajapati's creation through the pairing of Rayi (matter) and Prana (life-energy). Vaishnavas identify Prajapati with Brahma acting under Vishnu's direction, and the creative pairing of Rayi and Prana as the interaction of Vishnu's material energy (*prakriti*, identified with Lakshmi) and His spiritual energy. All creation proceeds from Vishnu's will, enacted through these twin principles.

The chapter's teaching on the two paths — the solar path (*uttarayana*) leading to the world of Brahma, and the lunar path (*dakshinayana*) leading to rebirth — establishes the cosmic framework within which the devotee's journey unfolds. The Vaishnava devotee aims for neither path in their ordinary sense but for the supreme destination beyond both: Vishnu's eternal abode, *Vaikuntha*, reached not by cosmic routes but by the Lord's grace.
