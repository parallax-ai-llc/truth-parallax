The fifth question, from Satyakama, asks about the fruits of meditating on AUM. Pippalada teaches that AUM has three components — A, U, and M — each yielding different results in meditation. Meditation on A alone leads to rebirth in the human world; A and U together lead to the lunar world; all three syllables together lead to Brahmaloka. Vaishnavas read this as a graduated practice of devotion to Vishnu through His sacred syllable, with the complete meditation leading to Vishnu's supreme abode.

The three components of AUM are identified with three aspects of Vishnu's nature: A with creation (Vishnu as originator), U with sustenance (Vishnu as preserver), and M with dissolution (Vishnu as the one who gathers all beings back to Himself). The complete syllable AUM, uttered as a whole, transcends these functions and points to Vishnu's transcendent nature beyond all cosmic activities. The devotee who meditates on the complete AUM with devotion attains the Lord Himself.
