The sixth and final question, from Sukesha, asks about the person (*purusha*) with sixteen parts (*kalas*). Pippalada's answer identifies this person with the supreme Brahman and explains that the sixteen parts — including Prana, faith, space, air, fire, water, earth, senses, mind, food, strength, austerity, mantras, action, the worlds, and name — emerge from this person and merge back into it, as rivers merge into the ocean. Vaishnavas identify this supreme Purusha with Vishnu-Narayana.

The simile of the rivers merging into the ocean (6.5) is read by Vaishnavas not as the dissolution of individual identity but as the soul's arrival at its ultimate destination — Vishnu's ocean of infinite bliss. The rivers (individual souls) continue to exist within the ocean (God) but are no longer separated from their source. The teaching that when the parts merge into the supreme Purusha, "the person becomes free from parts and immortal" (6.5) describes the liberated soul's eternal, blissful existence in Vishnu's presence.
