The third question, from Kausalya, asks how Prana originates and how it distributes itself in the body. Pippalada's answer describes the five functions of Prana — *prana* (outward breath), *apana* (downward breath), *samana* (equalizing breath), *vyana* (pervading breath), and *udana* (upward breath) — each governing different bodily regions and functions. Vaishnavas read this as describing five modes of Vishnu's sustaining activity within the embodied soul.

The teaching that Prana is born from the self (Atman, 3.3) is read as demonstrating that the vital force originates from Vishnu, the supreme Self who delegates specific functions to the five pranas. The image of the king appointing governors over different regions of his kingdom (3.4) directly illustrates the Vaishnava concept of Vishnu's cosmic governance — the Lord administers creation through appointed deputies, each responsible for a specific domain.
