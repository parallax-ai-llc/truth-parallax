The second question, from Bhargava, asks which deity (*deva*) is supreme among the faculties. Pippalada answers through the parable of the faculties' contest: when Prana prepares to depart, all other faculties — speech, sight, hearing, mind — are pulled along like bees following their queen. This demonstrates Prana's supremacy. Vaishnavas identify the supreme Prana with Vishnu, the sustaining Lord without whom no faculty can function.

The hymn that the grateful faculties sing to Prana (2.5-13) is read as a devotional hymn to Vishnu. The faculties recognize that they depend entirely on Prana for their existence and functioning, just as all beings depend on Vishnu. The praise of Prana as the one who "supports the three worlds" and "nourishes all beings" describes Vishnu's cosmic role as sustainer (*bhartr*) of the universe.
