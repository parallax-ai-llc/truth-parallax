## Overview

The Sentences of Sextus occupies a distinctive place in the Nag Hammadi library: it is not a Gnostic text in any doctrinal sense but a collection of Pythagorean-influenced ethical maxims that the Gnostic community at Nag Hammadi evidently valued for its practical wisdom. For Gnostic readers, the sentences complement speculative theology with practical ethics — the maxims about self-knowledge, self-control, and the care of the soul resonate with Gnostic anthropology even without Gnostic mythology. The text demonstrates that the Gnostic spiritual life involved not only cosmological knowledge but also moral discipline.
