---
title: "Sentences of Sextus - Chapter 5"
scripture: "sentences-of-sextus"
chapter: 5
---

**1** There is no one more blessed than the man who believes in God.

**2** It is the mark of the faithful not only to care for themselves but also for their neighbors.

**3** Use your possessions justly, and you will never lack.

**4** The man who is untroubled by worldly goods is rich in God.

**5** Do not consider yourself wise in the sight of God; but let God be the judge of your wisdom.

**6** Better is a humble man who serves God than a proud man who boasts of his wisdom.

**7** Teach what you know, to those who do not know. And learn what you do not know, from those who know.

**8** Do not consider it a mark of superiority to instruct others in the things of God.

**9** Revere that which is in you; for it is from God.

**10** What is in you that is from God is the mind. Therefore use the mind as God's gift.

**11** Consider every time spent without God as a loss.

**12** The man who worships God with his whole heart shall not be put to shame.

**13** God does not need anything; but the faithful man needs God.

**14** If you conduct yourself worthily of God, you will be worthy of God.

**15** Do not disturb a man who loves God, for you disturb God.

**16** Cast out from your soul every evil desire, and you will be pleasing to God.

**17** Let your piety convince others to be pious.

**18** The faithful man who spends his time among men should be like a light that shines.

**19** The mark of the wise man is his willingness to change his opinion when he finds the truth.

**20** These are the sentences of Sextus, given for the guidance of those who love wisdom and seek the path to God.
