---
title: "Sentences of Sextus - Chapter 3"
scripture: "sentences-of-sextus"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Use the possessions you have in a manner worthy of God.

**2** What God gives, no one can take away.

**3** A wise man does not delight in wealth, nor a sensible man in pleasure.

**4** Every part of life which is not devoted to God is wasted.

**5** If you love learning, you will attain much learning.

**6** Whatever you have learned, practice it. And you will know the truth.

**7** Gold is tested by fire, and the faithful man by temptation.

**8** Honor the wise man after God.

**9** On the other hand, never honor anyone who dishonors God.

**10** Be obedient to one greater than you in the things of God.

**11** To be wise is to know oneself, and to know oneself is to know God.

**12** If you know that you will die, let no thing of the world delight you excessively.

**13** If you fear death, it is because you love the world more than God.

**14** Do not fear death, for what you fear is the beginning of salvation.

**15** An unrighteous rich man is worse than a poor man who is dying of hunger.

**16** The faithful man fears God as a good father, not as a harsh master.

**17** Food makes the body strong; wisdom makes the soul strong.

**18** It is better to die of hunger than to serve with dishonor.

**19** Choose for yourself the life that is best; custom will make it pleasant.

**20** Whatever you do not want to be done to you, do not do to another.
