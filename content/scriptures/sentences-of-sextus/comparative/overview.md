## Overview

The Sentences of Sextus represents one of the clearest examples of cross-cultural ethical transmission in the ancient world. The maxims bridge Pythagorean, Stoic, Platonic, and Christian ethical traditions, demonstrating that practical moral wisdom often transcends philosophical and religious boundaries. The text's emphasis on self-knowledge, self-control, the power of speech, and the care of the soul parallels the ethical teachings of virtually every major philosophical and religious tradition: Confucian self-cultivation, Buddhist sila (ethical conduct), Islamic adab (spiritual courtesy), and Jewish musar (ethical discipline).
