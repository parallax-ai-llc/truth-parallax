## On Wealth and Poverty — Comparative Perspectives

This section of Sentences of Sextus addresses themes that resonate across the world's religious and philosophical traditions. The Sentences of Sextus represents one of the clearest examples of cross-cultural ethical transmission in the ancient world.

The section "On Wealth and Poverty" invites cross-cultural comparison while respecting the irreducible particularity of the Gnostic context. The questions addressed here — about the nature of ultimate reality, the human condition, and the possibility of liberation — are shared by traditions as diverse as Vedanta, Buddhism, Neoplatonism, Kabbalah, and Sufism.

Comparative method at its best illuminates without flattening: genuine parallels create space for mutual understanding, while differences remind us that each tradition develops its insights within a particular historical and cultural matrix.
