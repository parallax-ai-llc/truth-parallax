## Overview

For Christian readers, the Sentences of Sextus is a remarkable testament to the early church's willingness to appropriate pagan ethical wisdom. Origen knew the text and attributed it to Pope Xystus (Sixtus) II — a claim Jerome later rejected, recognizing the sentences as Pythagorean in origin. The text's emphasis on self-knowledge, self-control, and the care of the soul resonates with the Christian moral tradition from the Desert Fathers through Thomas Aquinas to modern Christian ethics. The sentences demonstrate that the boundary between 'Christian' and 'pagan' wisdom was permeable in the early centuries.
