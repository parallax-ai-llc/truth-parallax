## Overview

Jewish readers find in the Sentences of Sextus extensive parallels to Jewish wisdom literature. The maxims about self-knowledge, the power of speech, the care of the soul, and the proper attitude toward wealth echo themes found in Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Sirach, Wisdom of Solomon, and Pirke Avot. The form — short, memorable ethical sentences — is identical to the rabbinic mashal (aphorism) and the proverbial sayings collected in the biblical wisdom books. The parallels suggest a shared ancient Mediterranean tradition of ethical instruction that transcended ethnic and religious boundaries.
