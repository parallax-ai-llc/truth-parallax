## Overview

The Sentences of Sextus is one of the most widely attested texts from late antiquity, known in Greek, Latin, Syriac, Armenian, and Georgian versions in addition to the Coptic Nag Hammadi copy. Henry Chadwick's 1959 critical edition demonstrated that the sentences originated as a collection of Pythagorean ethical maxims that were subsequently Christianized. The text's inclusion in the Nag Hammadi library has been interpreted as evidence either of the eclecticism of the collection or of the broad appeal of practical ethical wisdom across sectarian boundaries.
