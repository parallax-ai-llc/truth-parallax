## Living for God's Will

1 Peter 4:1–6 calls believers to break with their former way of life and live according to God's will. Protestant interpreters read this as the practical outworking of the believer's union with Christ: having "suffered in the flesh," the Christian is to "live for the rest of the time in the flesh no longer for human passions but for the will of God" (4:2). This shapes Protestant teaching on progressive sanctification.

## Stewardship of Grace

1 Peter 4:7–11 instructs believers to use their gifts in service to one another as "good stewards of God's varied grace." Protestant ecclesiology has drawn on this passage to articulate the doctrine of spiritual gifts — every believer receives gifts for the common good of the community. The passage supports the Protestant emphasis on the active participation of all members in the life of the church.

## The Fiery Trial

1 Peter 4:12–19 encourages believers not to be surprised by suffering but to "rejoice insofar as you share Christ's sufferings." Protestant interpreters connect this to the broader biblical theology of suffering: present affliction is temporary, and those who share in Christ's sufferings will also share in his glory. The passage has sustained persecuted Protestant communities from the Huguenots to modern house churches.
