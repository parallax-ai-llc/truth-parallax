## Suffering for Doing Good

1 Peter 3:13–17 teaches that suffering for righteousness is "blessed" and calls believers to be ready to give a defense (*apologia*) for their hope. Protestant interpreters have drawn on this passage to encourage both personal apologetics and public witness. The instruction to give this defense "with gentleness and respect" (3:15) tempers Protestant zeal with pastoral sensitivity.

## Christ's Descent to the Spirits

1 Peter 3:18–20 describes Christ going to preach to "the spirits in prison." Protestant interpretation has been divided: some read this as Christ proclaiming victory over fallen angels, others as a reference to Christ's spirit preaching through Noah to the pre-flood generation. Most Protestant interpreters reject the Catholic and Orthodox reading of a descent into hell to liberate the dead, preferring interpretations that maintain the sufficiency of Christ's finished work on the cross.

## Baptism and Salvation

1 Peter 3:21 — "Baptism now saves you" — has generated extensive Protestant debate. Most Protestant interpreters emphasize Peter's own qualifier: baptism saves "not as a removal of dirt from the body but as an appeal to God for a good conscience." This is read as affirming that baptism's efficacy lies in the faith it expresses, not in the water itself.
