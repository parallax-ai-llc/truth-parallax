## A Living Hope

1 Peter 1:3–12 opens with a magnificent doxology praising God for the "living hope" granted through Christ's resurrection. Protestant interpreters emphasize the security of the believer's inheritance — "imperishable, undefiled, and unfading, kept in heaven for you" (1:4). Reformed theology reads this as assurance of perseverance: the inheritance is guarded by God's power, not human effort.

## Tested Faith

The metaphor of faith refined like gold through fire (1:6–7) has been central to Protestant pastoral theology. Suffering is not purposeless but produces proven, genuine faith. The Puritan tradition developed extensive pastoral literature on the uses of affliction, drawing on this passage to encourage believers under trial.

## The Call to Holiness

The chapter's second half (1:13–25) summons believers to holiness, quoting Leviticus: "Be holy, for I am holy" (1:16). Protestant interpreters read this as the call to sanctification — the progressive growth in holiness that follows justification. The redemption "with the precious blood of Christ" (1:18–19) grounds the ethical imperative in the finished work of the cross.
