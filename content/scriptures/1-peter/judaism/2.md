## Israel's Titles Transferred

1 Peter 2:9–10, which calls the Christian community "a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, God's own people," draws directly on Exodus 19:5–6 and Hosea 1:9–10. For Jewish readers, this is the most theologically sensitive passage in the letter. The transfer of Israel's covenant titles to a predominantly Gentile community raises the fundamental question of supersessionism: has the Church replaced Israel as God's people?

## The Cornerstone Tradition

1 Peter 2:6–8 draws on Isaiah 28:16, Psalm 118:22, and Isaiah 8:14 to construct a "stone" testimony about Jesus. Jewish readers note that these passages had their own interpretive history in Jewish tradition before Christian appropriation. Psalm 118 is part of the Hallel liturgy, and its "rejected stone" imagery was applied to Israel itself in rabbinic interpretation.

## Suffering Servanthood

1 Peter 2:22–25 draws extensively on Isaiah 53, the Suffering Servant passage. Jewish interpretation of Isaiah 53 has traditionally read the Servant as Israel, not as an individual Messiah. The application of this passage to Jesus represents one of the earliest and most consequential divergences between Jewish and Christian interpretation.
