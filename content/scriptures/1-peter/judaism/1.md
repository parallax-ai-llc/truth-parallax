## Exile and Diaspora Identity

Jewish scholars note that 1 Peter 1:1 addresses its audience as "exiles of the Dispersion" (*parepidēmois diasporas*), using the technical vocabulary of Jewish diaspora existence. The experience of living as a faithful minority amid a dominant culture is deeply familiar to Jewish communities throughout history. Jewish readers recognize in 1 Peter a strategy of identity maintenance that mirrors the Jewish experience of maintaining Torah observance in galut (exile).

## Tested Faith and the Refiner's Fire

The metaphor of faith tested like gold in fire (1:6–7) echoes the Hebrew Bible's imagery of divine testing, especially Malachi 3:2–3 and Zechariah 13:9. Jewish readers connect this to the broader biblical theme of God refining the faithful through adversity. The concept of suffering as divine pedagogy is deeply rooted in Jewish tradition, from the binding of Isaac to the prophetic understanding of the exile.

## Holiness and the Levitical Command

1 Peter 1:16 quotes Leviticus 11:44: "Be holy, for I am holy." Jewish readers note that this command originally addressed Israel within the context of Torah observance — dietary laws, purity regulations, and ethical conduct. The Christian appropriation of this command raises questions about whether holiness can be separated from the Torah's concrete prescriptions, or whether the ethical dimension alone suffices.
