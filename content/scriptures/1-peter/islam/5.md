## Shepherding and Leadership

1 Peter 5:1–4, instructing elders to shepherd the flock willingly and without selfish gain, resonates with the Islamic concept of pastoral leadership. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Each of you is a shepherd, and each of you is responsible for his flock" (Bukhari and Muslim). Islamic leadership is understood as a trust (*amanah*) and a responsibility, not a privilege, mirroring 1 Peter's emphasis on humble, servant-hearted leadership.

## Humility Before God

1 Peter 5:5–7, calling for humility and the casting of anxieties on God, aligns with the Islamic virtue of *tawadu'* (humility) and *tawakkul* (reliance on God). The Quran states: "And whoever relies upon Allah — then He is sufficient for him" (65:3). Both traditions teach that genuine humility before God liberates the believer from anxiety and self-reliance.

## Resistance to Evil

1 Peter 5:8–9, warning against the devil who seeks to devour, resonates with the Quranic teaching on Shaytan (Satan) as the enemy of humanity: "Indeed, Satan is an enemy to you; so take him as an enemy" (35:6). Both traditions teach vigilance against spiritual deception and the need for communal solidarity in resisting evil.
