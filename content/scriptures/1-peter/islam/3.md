## Gentleness in Defense of Faith

1 Peter 3:15–16, which calls believers to defend their hope "with gentleness and respect," resonates with the Quranic instruction: "Invite to the way of your Lord with wisdom and good instruction, and argue with them in a way that is best" (16:125). Both traditions teach that the defense of faith should be conducted with courtesy and intellectual integrity, not aggression or coercion.

## The Spirits in Prison

1 Peter 3:18–20, describing Christ preaching to "spirits in prison," engages with apocalyptic traditions that have parallels in Islamic eschatology. The Quran and hadith literature describe the experience of the soul in *barzakh* (the intermediate state between death and resurrection), and the concept of imprisoned spirits resonates with Islamic teaching about the state of the wicked dead. However, the Christological framework of the passage lies outside Islamic theology.

## Water and Salvation

1 Peter 3:20–21, connecting Noah's flood to baptism, draws on the story of Nuh (Noah), an important prophet in Islam. The Quran narrates Noah's story extensively (especially Surah 71), emphasizing his patience in preaching to his people and God's salvation of the faithful through the ark. Muslim readers appreciate the typological use of Noah while noting that Islam does not practice baptism as a salvific rite.
