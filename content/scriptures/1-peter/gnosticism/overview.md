## Overview

Gnostic interest in 1 Peter centers primarily on the enigmatic passage about Christ preaching to "the spirits in prison" (3:18–20) and the letter's references to hidden knowledge and future revelation. While 1 Peter was not a primary Gnostic text, its cosmological imagery — Christ's descent, the spirits, baptism as passage through water — offered material for Gnostic reinterpretation within their framework of cosmic drama and liberation from the material world.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Christ's Descent and the Liberation of Spirits
1 Peter 3:18–20, describing Christ going to preach to "the spirits in prison," was interpreted by some Gnostic readers as describing the Savior's descent through the archontic realms to liberate the divine sparks trapped by the Demiurge's cosmic prison. The "spirits" are the pneumatic seeds imprisoned in matter, and Christ's preaching is the delivery of salvific gnosis.

### Foreknowledge and Election
1 Peter 1:2, which speaks of election "according to the foreknowledge of God the Father," could be read in Valentinian terms as describing the predetermined salvation of the pneumatic class. The "inheritance imperishable, undefiled, and unfading" (1:3) is the Pleroma — the fullness of divine being from which the pneumatic seed originated and to which it returns.

### Baptism as Cosmic Passage
1 Peter 3:20–21 connects baptism to Noah's passage through the flood waters. Gnostic interpreters could read this typologically as the soul's passage through the material waters of the Demiurge's cosmos — a spiritual transit from the realm of ignorance to the realm of gnosis.

## Theological Emphases

- **Descent to the Spirits**: Christ's cosmic journey as the liberation of trapped divine sparks
- **Pneumatic Election**: The "chosen exiles" as the Gnostic elect
- **Baptism as Transition**: The flood and baptism as types of passage from matter to spirit
- **Suffering in Matter**: The letter's theme of suffering reframed as the pneumatic soul's experience in the Demiurge's world
