## Living Stones and the Pneumatic Temple

1 Peter 2:4–5, describing believers as "living stones" built into a "spiritual house," could be appropriated by Gnostic interpreters as a description of the pneumatic community — the gathering of divine sparks into a spiritual temple that transcends the material Demiurgic order. The "spiritual sacrifices" offered are not material rituals but the gnosis that rises to the true God beyond the Demiurge.

## The Cornerstone and the Stumbling Stone

1 Peter 2:6–8, with its imagery of a stone that causes some to stumble and others to build upon, could be read through the Gnostic distinction between pneumatics, psychics, and hylics. The pneumatics recognize the cornerstone (the Savior who brings gnosis), while the hylics stumble over it, trapped in their material blindness. The psychics may or may not respond, depending on their orientation.

## The Suffering Servant Reinterpreted

1 Peter 2:21–25, drawing on Isaiah 53, describes Christ bearing sins "in his body on the tree." Gnostic Christology typically denied that the true Christ suffered physically, maintaining that the divine Savior either inhabited a phantom body or withdrew before the crucifixion. Docetic readings of 1 Peter would distinguish between the material body that suffered on the cross and the spiritual Christ who remained impassible.
