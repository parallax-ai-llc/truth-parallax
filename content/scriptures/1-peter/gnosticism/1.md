## The Hidden Inheritance

1 Peter 1:3–5 describes an inheritance "imperishable, undefiled, and unfading, kept in heaven." Gnostic interpreters could read this as a description of the Pleroma — the fullness of divine being from which the pneumatic seed originated and to which it will return. The inheritance is "kept in heaven" because it belongs to the realm above the Demiurge's material creation, accessible only through gnosis.

## Tested by Fire

The metaphor of faith tested like gold (1:6–7) might be interpreted by Gnostic readers as the pneumatic soul's purification through its experience in the material world. The material realm, though hostile, serves as the crucible through which the divine spark is refined and prepared for its return to the Pleroma. This reading reframes suffering not as divine pedagogy but as the inevitable friction of spirit trapped in matter.

## Holiness and the Pneumatic Nature

The call to holiness (1:13–16) could be read in Gnostic terms as the recognition of the pneumatic's true nature. To "be holy" is to live in accordance with one's divine origin rather than conforming to the appetites of the material body. The Gnostic does not achieve holiness through moral effort but awakens to the holiness inherent in the divine spark.
