## Suffering in the Flesh

1 Peter 4:1 — "Since therefore Christ has suffered in the flesh, arm yourselves with the same way of thinking" — would be reinterpreted by Gnostic readers through their characteristic Christological dualism. The suffering "in the flesh" belongs to the material body, not to the divine Christ. The pneumatic "arms" themselves by recognizing the illusory nature of bodily suffering and identifying with the impassible divine spirit.

## Judgment of the Living and the Dead

1 Peter 4:5–6 refers to judgment of "the living and the dead" and the gospel preached "even to those who are dead." Gnostic interpreters could connect this to the theme of spiritual death and awakening: the "dead" are those trapped in material ignorance, and the gospel preached to them is the gnosis that awakens the divine spark. Judgment is not a future eschatological event but the ongoing discrimination between those who receive gnosis and those who do not.

## The Fiery Trial

1 Peter 4:12 — "Do not be surprised at the fiery trial" — could be read by Gnostic interpreters as a description of the pneumatic soul's necessary passage through the hostile material realm. The "fire" is the Demiurge's opposition to the awakening of the divine sparks within his domain. The pneumatic endures this trial not through moral fortitude but through the assured knowledge of ultimate return to the Pleroma.
