## The Spirits in Prison

1 Peter 3:18–20 is the passage of greatest Gnostic interest in the entire letter. Christ going to "preach to the spirits in prison" was interpreted by some Gnostic readers as the Savior's descent through the archontic realms to liberate the divine sparks imprisoned by the Demiurge and his archons. The "prison" is the material cosmos itself, and the "preaching" is the delivery of salvific gnosis to those trapped within it.

## Baptism as Cosmic Passage

1 Peter 3:20–21 connects baptism to Noah's passage through the flood. Gnostic interpreters could read the flood as the Demiurge's attempt to destroy the pneumatic seed, and the ark as the vessel of gnosis that preserves the elect through the material deluge. Baptism, in this reading, symbolizes the soul's passage through the watery chaos of the material world into the clarity of gnosis.

## Beyond Bodily Purification

Peter's clarification that baptism is "not the removal of dirt from the body" (3:21) might be seized upon by Gnostic interpreters as confirmation that the rite's significance is entirely spiritual. The material water is irrelevant; what matters is the interior awakening — the "appeal to God for a good conscience" understood as the soul's recognition of its pneumatic identity.
