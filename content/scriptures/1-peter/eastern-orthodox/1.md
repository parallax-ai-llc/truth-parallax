## The Living Hope and Paschal Joy

Orthodox liturgical theology reads 1 Peter 1:3–12 as a Paschal hymn. The "living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ" (1:3) is the foundation of the entire Orthodox liturgical year, culminating in the Paschal proclamation: "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death." The inheritance "kept in heaven" is understood not as a distant future but as a present reality already breaking into the world through the sacramental life.

## Faith Tested by Fire

1 Peter 1:6–7 on the testing of faith resonates with the Orthodox ascetic tradition. The Greek Fathers taught that spiritual trials (*peirasmoi*) serve as a furnace in which the soul is purified and prepared for deeper communion with God. This is not punitive but therapeutic — part of the divine pedagogy that leads the soul toward theosis.

## Be Holy as God Is Holy

The call to holiness in 1:13–25, grounding the ethical imperative in God's own holiness, is read by Orthodox theology as the call to theosis — participation in the divine nature. Holiness is not merely moral conformity but ontological transformation: the believer is called to become by grace what God is by nature. This reading is supported by 2 Peter 1:4, which speaks of becoming "partakers of the divine nature."
