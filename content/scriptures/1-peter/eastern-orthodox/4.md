## Suffering and the Paschal Mystery

1 Peter 4:1–2, calling believers to "arm yourselves" with the same intention as Christ who suffered, is read in Orthodox theology as an invitation to participate in the Paschal mystery. Suffering is not meaningless but a pathway through which the believer shares in Christ's death and resurrection. The ascetic tradition understands voluntary self-denial as a form of this participation.

## Mutual Love and the Eucharistic Community

1 Peter 4:7–11, with its call to "above all maintain constant love for one another" (4:8), shapes Orthodox understanding of the ecclesial community as a Eucharistic fellowship. Love is the essential characteristic of the Church, expressed most fully in the gathering around the Lord's table. The gifts described in 4:10–11 are exercised within and for this Eucharistic community.

## Sharing Christ's Sufferings with Joy

1 Peter 4:12–19 calls believers to rejoice in suffering for Christ's name. Orthodox theology reads this in light of the Paschal paradox: the cross is simultaneously the instrument of death and the source of life. The "fiery trial" (4:12) is not alien to the Christian life but its expected companion, and those who suffer with Christ will share in his glory at its revelation.
