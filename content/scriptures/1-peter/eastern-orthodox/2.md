## Living Stones and the Mystical Body

Orthodox ecclesiology reads 1 Peter 2:4–10 as a description of the Church as the mystical Body of Christ. Every baptized member is a "living stone" incorporated into the temple of the Holy Spirit. The "royal priesthood" is exercised through the liturgical life — especially the Divine Liturgy — where all the faithful offer spiritual sacrifices through Christ the Great High Priest.

## Christ the Cornerstone

The imagery of Christ as the "living stone" and "cornerstone" (2:4–8) is central to Orthodox Christology and ecclesiology. The Church is built on Christ, and those who reject him stumble. Orthodox interpretation emphasizes the corporate dimension: individual believers find their true identity only as they are incorporated into the living temple of the Church.

## The Suffering Servant and Theosis

1 Peter 2:21–25, drawing on Isaiah 53, presents Christ's suffering as the model for Christian life. Orthodox interpretation reads this not merely as moral example but as the paradigm of theosis: Christ took on human suffering and mortality to transform them from within, making possible the healing of human nature. "By his wounds you have been healed" (2:24) is understood as a statement about the ontological restoration of humanity.
