## Christ's Descent into Hades

1 Peter 3:18–20 is the most important text in the entire New Testament for Orthodox Paschal theology. Christ's journey to "preach to the spirits in prison" is understood as his descent into Hades after the crucifixion, where he proclaimed victory over death and liberated the righteous dead. The Paschal icon — the Anastasis — depicts this event as the central act of redemption. Christ shatters the gates of Hades and draws Adam and Eve (representing all humanity) out of the tombs.

## Baptism as Paschal Participation

1 Peter 3:20–21 connects baptism to Noah's passage through the flood, and Orthodox baptismal theology draws extensively on this typology. Baptism is a genuine participation in Christ's death and resurrection — the believer descends into the waters as into a tomb and rises to new life. The Orthodox practice of triple immersion reflects this understanding of baptism as a complete Paschal experience.

## A Good Conscience

Peter's clarification that baptism saves "not as a removal of dirt from the body but as an appeal to God for a good conscience" (3:21) is read by Orthodox interpreters as affirming the interior, spiritual dimension of the sacrament. Baptism is not magic but a genuine encounter with the risen Christ that transforms the whole person — body, soul, and spirit.
