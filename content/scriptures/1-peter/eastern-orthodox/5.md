## The Elders and Pastoral Care

1 Peter 5:1–4 addresses the elders of the church as fellow shepherds under the "Chief Shepherd." Orthodox ecclesiology understands the pastoral office as a continuation of the apostolic ministry, exercised within the liturgical and sacramental life of the Church. The bishop, as chief shepherd of the local church, embodies Christ's pastoral presence, and the priests share in this ministry.

## Humility and the Monastic Tradition

1 Peter 5:5–7, with its call to "clothe yourselves with humility," is a foundational text for Orthodox monasticism. Humility is considered the queen of the virtues in the monastic tradition, from the Desert Fathers to the Philokalia. The instruction to "cast all your anxieties on him" shapes the Orthodox practice of surrendering the will to God through prayer and obedience.

## Resistance to the Devil

1 Peter 5:8–9, warning against the devil who "prowls around like a roaring lion," informs Orthodox spiritual warfare theology. The ascetic tradition teaches that the Christian life is a continuous struggle against the demonic powers, waged through prayer, fasting, the sacraments, and the invocation of Christ's name. The promise of divine restoration after suffering (5:10) assures the faithful that God's grace ultimately prevails.
