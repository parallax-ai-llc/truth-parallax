## The Church as Spiritual Temple

1 Peter 2:4–10, with its imagery of living stones and a holy priesthood, is central to Catholic ecclesiology. The Church is understood as the new temple in which spiritual sacrifices are offered through Christ. Catholic interpretation distinguishes between the common priesthood of all the baptized (2:5, 9) and the ministerial priesthood of the ordained, both of which participate in Christ's one priesthood.

## Submission and Social Order

Catholic social teaching engages with 1 Peter 2:11–17 by affirming the general principle of respect for legitimate authority while insisting on the limits of obedience. The Catholic tradition of just war theory, civil disobedience, and social teaching recognizes that submission to authority is conditioned by the higher law of God. The Second Vatican Council's *Gaudium et Spes* develops this balance.

## Christ the Suffering Servant

1 Peter 2:21–25, which presents Christ's suffering as both redemptive sacrifice and moral example, is foundational for Catholic soteriology. Christ "bore our sins in his body on the tree" (2:24) — a text read in continuity with the Suffering Servant of Isaiah 53. Catholic theology holds together the substitutionary and exemplary dimensions of Christ's passion, understanding both as aspects of the single mystery of redemption.
