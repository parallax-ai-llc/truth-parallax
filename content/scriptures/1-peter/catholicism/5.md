## Pastoral Ministry

1 Peter 5:1–4 addresses the elders as shepherds of God's flock. Catholic interpretation reads this in continuity with Jesus's commission to Peter in John 21:15–17 ("Feed my sheep"). The pastoral office is understood as a participation in Christ's shepherding ministry, exercised in apostolic succession. The "Chief Shepherd" (5:4) is Christ, and the ordained minister serves as his representative.

## Humility and Grace

1 Peter 5:5–7 — "Clothe yourselves with humility toward one another" and "Cast all your anxieties on him" — shapes Catholic spiritual teaching. The tradition of spiritual humility, from Benedict's Rule to Ignatius of Loyola's Exercises, finds scriptural support in Peter's exhortation. Trust in God's providential care is the foundation of Catholic prayer and the interior life.

## The Church in Babylon

1 Peter 5:13's reference to "She who is at Babylon" is read by Catholic tradition as a reference to the church in Rome. This supports the historical claim that Peter exercised his apostolic ministry in Rome and was martyred there — the foundation of the Catholic doctrine of the Roman primacy and apostolic succession through the line of Roman bishops.
