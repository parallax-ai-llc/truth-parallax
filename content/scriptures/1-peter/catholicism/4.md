## Breaking with Sin

1 Peter 4:1–6, with its call to break with former sinful practices, is read in Catholic moral theology as a description of the baptismal transformation. The believer who has died with Christ in baptism is called to live a new life, turning away from the "passions of the Gentiles" (4:3). The sacramental life of the Church — especially the Eucharist and Reconciliation — sustains this ongoing conversion.

## Gifts for the Common Good

1 Peter 4:10–11 — "As each has received a gift, use it to serve one another" — informs Catholic teaching on charisms. The Second Vatican Council emphasized that the Holy Spirit distributes gifts among all the faithful for the building up of the Church. These charisms, both ordinary and extraordinary, are to be exercised under the discernment of the Church's pastors.

## Sharing Christ's Sufferings

1 Peter 4:12–19 teaches that suffering for Christ is a participation in his passion. Catholic theology of redemptive suffering, articulated in John Paul II's *Salvifici Doloris*, draws on this passage to affirm that human suffering, united with Christ's, contributes to the sanctification of the Church and the world.
