## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in this text a radical transformation of Second Temple messianic expectations. The substitution motif — someone else dying in place of the messiah — inverts Jewish traditions about the suffering of the righteous (the Suffering Servant of Isaiah 53, the martyrs of 2 Maccabees) while drawing on the Synoptic narrative's mention of Simon of Cyrene carrying the cross (Mark 15:21). The text's anti-demiurgic theology rejects the God of Israel as an ignorant archon, representing one of the sharpest Gnostic breaks with Jewish theology.
