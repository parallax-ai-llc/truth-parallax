## Overview

For Christian readers, the Second Treatise of the Great Seth presents the most direct possible challenge to the orthodox theology of the cross. The text does not merely question the significance of the crucifixion — it denies its reality, substituting a cosmic comedy (the archons' foolish attempt to kill God) for the cosmic tragedy that orthodox theology places at the center of salvation history. The laughing Christ inverts the image of the suffering Servant (Isaiah 53) that is foundational to orthodox Christology.
