## Overview

The Second Treatise of the Great Seth raises a question that surfaces across religious traditions: can the divine truly suffer? Christian orthodoxy affirms it (the crucified God); Islam denies it (Jesus was not crucified); Buddhism's concept of the teacher's three bodies (trikaya) distinguishes between the apparent physical form and the transcendent reality; Hindu theology distinguishes between the suffering of the avatara's material form and the unchanging nature of Brahman. The question touches the deepest nerve of religious thought about the relationship between the absolute and the world of suffering.
