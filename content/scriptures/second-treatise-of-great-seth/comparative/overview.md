## Overview

The Second Treatise of the Great Seth's docetic account of the crucifixion resonates with a striking parallel in Islam: the Quran states that Jesus was not crucified but that 'it was made to appear so to them' (4:157). While there is no direct literary dependence, both traditions share the conviction that the divine cannot truly suffer and that apparent suffering is an illusion or substitution. The laughing Christ motif connects to broader traditions of the 'divine trickster' who outwits cosmic powers — a theme found in Hermetic literature, in some Buddhist jataka tales, and in various mythological traditions worldwide.
