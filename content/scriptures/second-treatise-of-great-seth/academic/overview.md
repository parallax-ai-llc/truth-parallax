## Overview

The Second Treatise of the Great Seth is a central text for the study of early Christian docetism — the view that Christ only appeared to suffer and die on the cross. The substitution of Simon of Cyrene for Christ on the cross is attested in other early sources (Irenaeus attributes a similar teaching to Basilides, Adversus Haereses 1.24.4). The text's sharp anti-orthodox polemic provides evidence of the intensity of conflict between Gnostic and proto-orthodox Christians, and its first-person Christ-speech raises questions about the genre of Gnostic revelation literature.
