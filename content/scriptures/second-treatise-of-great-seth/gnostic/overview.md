## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Second Treatise of the Great Seth reveals the true meaning of the crucifixion: it was not the suffering of the divine Christ but the foolish act of ignorant archons who believed they could defeat the spiritual Savior through violence. Christ's laughter at the cross expresses the ultimate Gnostic insight — the material world's powers are impotent against the spirit. The text's sharp polemic against the proto-orthodox church identifies the institutional church as an extension of archontic power, worshipping the demiurge in the guise of the true God. Only those who possess gnosis see through this deception to the laughing, triumphant Christ.
