---
title: "Second Treatise of the Great Seth - Chapter 2"
scripture: "second-treatise-of-great-seth"
chapter: 2
---

**1** It was another, their father, who drank the gall and the vinegar; it was not I. They struck me with the reed; it was another, Simon, who bore the cross on his shoulder.

**2** It was another upon whom they placed the crown of thorns. But I was rejoicing in the height over all the wealth of the archons and the offspring of their error and their empty glory.

**3** And I was laughing at their ignorance. And I subjected all their powers. For as I came downward, no one saw me, for I was altering my shapes, changing from form to form.

**4** And therefore, when I was at their gates, I assumed their likeness. For I passed them by quietly, and I was viewing the places, and I was not afraid nor ashamed, for I was undefiled.

**5** I was speaking with those who belong to me, and was trampling on those who are harsh to them with zeal, and I was quenching the flame.

**6** And I was doing all these things because of my desire to accomplish what I desired by the will of the Father above.

**7** The Son of the Majesty, who was hidden in the region below, we brought to the height where I was in all these aeons with them, which height no one has seen nor known, where the wedding of the wedding robe is, the new one and not the old.

**8** For it is a new teaching that I give. I descended to their realm, I spoke the word, I reproved those who thought they were wise, and they were bewildered.

**9** All their glory and their arrogance and their pride — these are what I mocked. I have no need of their glory, for the Father of Truth exists.

**10** And he it is whom the world did not receive, because the world is ignorant of him. But I am he who has been glorified in the eternal realms.
