---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 4: Kevaddha Sutta (About Kevaddha)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Nalanda, in Pavarika's Mango Grove. Then the householder Kevaddha approached the Blessed One, paid homage, and sat to one side.

**2** He said: "Lord, Nalanda is a prosperous city. The people here have great faith in the Blessed One. It would be good if the Blessed One were to command some monk to perform a miracle of supernatural power. Thus the people would have even more faith."

**3** The Blessed One said: "Kevaddha, I do not teach the Dhamma to the monks in this way: 'Go, monks, perform miracles of supernatural power for the white-robed householders.'"

**4** "There are three kinds of miracles: the miracle of supernatural power, the miracle of telepathy, and the miracle of instruction."

**5** "As for the miracle of supernatural power, a monk exercises various supernatural powers: having been one, he becomes many; having been many, he becomes one. He appears and disappears. He passes through walls, through mountains, through water. He walks on water. He flies through the air. He touches the sun and the moon."

**6** "But a non-believer might say, 'There is a charm called Gandhara by which he does this.' Therefore I see danger in the miracle of supernatural power, and I am troubled and ashamed of it."

**7** "As for the miracle of telepathy, a monk reads the minds of other beings. He declares: 'This is your state of mind, this is what you are thinking, this is what is in your heart.' But a non-believer might attribute this to the Manika charm. Therefore I see danger in the miracle of telepathy."

**8** "But as for the miracle of instruction: a monk teaches thus: 'Consider in this way, not in that. Apply your mind in this way, not in that. Give up this, enter upon and abide in that.' This is the miracle of instruction, and of this I am not ashamed."

**9** "Kevaddha, once a certain monk had a question arise in his mind: 'Where do the four great elements—the earth element, the water element, the fire element, and the air element—cease without remainder?'"

**10** "He attained such concentration that the way to the devas was revealed. He asked the gods of the Four Great Kings, but they did not know. They sent him to the Thirty-three. They sent him higher, to the Yama gods, the Tusita gods, the Nimmanarati gods, the Paranimmitavasavatti gods, to the Brahma gods."

**11** "The Great Brahma said: 'I am Brahma, Great Brahma, the All-Seeing, the All-Powerful, the Lord.' But the monk pressed: 'I did not ask whether you are Brahma. I asked where the four great elements cease.' Then Brahma took the monk by the arm and led him aside and said: 'Monk, these gods believe I know everything. That is why I did not answer in front of them. I do not know. You should go back to the Blessed One.'"

**12** "Then the monk returned to me and asked the same question. And I answered: 'Where do the four great elements cease without remainder? Where water, earth, fire, and wind find no footing? Here: in the consciousness that is invisible, infinite, luminous all around—here water, earth, fire, and wind find no footing; here long and short, coarse and fine, fair and foul, name and form are brought to an end. With the cessation of consciousness, all this is brought to an end.'"
