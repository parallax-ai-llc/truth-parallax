---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 5: Kutadanta Sutta (About Kutadanta)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was touring in the Magadhan country and arrived at the brahman village of Khanumata. The brahman Kutadanta was living there, and he was preparing a great sacrifice of seven hundred bulls, seven hundred steers, seven hundred heifers, seven hundred he-goats, and seven hundred rams.

**2** Kutadanta heard of the Blessed One's arrival and thought: "I have heard that the ascetic Gotama knows the successful performance of a sacrifice with its three factors and sixteen requisites. I do not know this. Let me go and ask him."

**3** And Kutadanta went to the Blessed One and asked: "Master Gotama, I wish to hold a great sacrifice. Let the Master Gotama instruct me about the successful sacrifice."

**4** The Blessed One said: "Well then, brahman, listen. Long ago there was a king called Mahavijita who wished to perform a great sacrifice. He consulted his chaplain, who said:

**5** "'Your Majesty's country is beset by thieves. If the king were to tax the people, that would be the wrong thing to do. Suppose the king were to think: "I will root out this plague of thieves by executions, imprisonment, or fines." But the plague would not be properly ended.'"

**6** "'Instead, to those in the kingdom who are engaged in cultivating crops, let the king distribute grain and fodder. To those who are engaged in trade, let the king distribute capital. To those in government service, let the king assign proper living wages. Then the people, being intent on their own occupations, will not harm the kingdom. The king's revenues will be great. The country will be tranquil and not beset by thieves, and the people, happy in their lives, will play with their children and dwell in open houses.'"

**7** "And the king did so, and the country prospered. Then the king performed the great sacrifice with three factors: he was free from regret before, during, and after the sacrifice. And with sixteen requisites: the cooperation of his chaplain, queen, nobles, ministers, brahmans, householders, and so forth."

**8** "But, brahman, this sacrifice involved no slaughter of cattle, no felling of trees, no strewing of grass. The servants and workers did their jobs without being driven by punishment or fear, weeping and carrying out their work with tears on their faces. They worked willingly."

**9** "Yet there is, brahman, a sacrifice even less troublesome and of greater fruit: the building of residences for monks and ascetics. And better than that: going for refuge to the Buddha, the Dhamma, and the Sangha."

**10** "And better than that: undertaking the five precepts—abstaining from killing, from stealing, from sexual misconduct, from lying, and from intoxicants."

**11** "And better than that: a person goes forth from the household life, trains in morality, attains the jhanas, attains direct knowledge, and realizes the destruction of the taints."

**12** "There is no sacrifice higher or more sublime than this." When the Blessed One had finished speaking, Kutadanta said: "Magnificent, Master Gotama! I go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha. Let the Master Gotama accept me as a lay follower. And I release the seven hundred bulls, the seven hundred steers, the seven hundred heifers, the seven hundred he-goats, and the seven hundred rams. I give them their lives. Let them eat green grass and drink cool water, and let cool breezes blow upon them."
