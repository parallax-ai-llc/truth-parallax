---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 1: Brahmajala Sutta (The Supreme Net)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was travelling along the highway between Rajagaha and Nalanda with a large company of monks, about five hundred monks.

**2** And the wanderer Suppiya was also travelling along the highway, with his pupil, the youth Brahmadatta. And as they went, Suppiya spoke in many ways in disparagement of the Buddha, in disparagement of the Dhamma, in disparagement of the Sangha. But the youth Brahmadatta spoke in many ways in praise of the Buddha, in praise of the Dhamma, in praise of the Sangha.

**3** Then the Blessed One, knowing the minds of the monks, addressed them: "Monks, if anyone should speak in disparagement of me, of the Dhamma, or of the Sangha, you should not on that account be angry, resentful, or upset. If you were angry or displeased at such disparagement, that would be a hindrance to you."

**4** "If anyone should speak in disparagement of me, of the Dhamma, or of the Sangha, and you were angry and displeased, would you be able to recognise whether what was said was well spoken or ill spoken?" "No, Lord." "If anyone should speak in disparagement, you should explain what is incorrect as being incorrect: 'That is incorrect, that is not so, that is not our way, that is not found among us.'"

**5** "And if anyone should speak in praise of me, of the Dhamma, or of the Sangha, you should not on that account be pleased, happy, or elated. If you were, that would be a hindrance to you. You should acknowledge what is correct: 'That is correct, that is so, that is our way, that is found among us.'"

**6** "Monks, it is only to trifling matters, to matters of moral habit, that a worldling would refer when speaking in praise of the Tathagata."

**7** "And what are these trifling matters? 'The ascetic Gotama has given up the taking of life, has refrained from the taking of life. He has laid aside the rod, laid aside the sword. He is conscientious and kind, and dwells compassionate for the welfare of all living beings.' It is in such terms that a worldling would speak."

**8** "'The ascetic Gotama has given up taking what is not given, has refrained from taking what is not given. He takes only what is given, waits for what is given, and he dwells with a pure heart, not stealing.'"

**9** "'The ascetic Gotama has given up unchastity, living a life of chastity, remote, abstaining from the common practice of sexual intercourse.'"

**10** "'The ascetic Gotama has given up false speech, has refrained from false speech. He speaks the truth, adheres to the truth, is trustworthy and reliable, not a deceiver of the world.'"

**11** "There are, monks, other matters, profound, hard to see, hard to understand, peaceful, excellent, beyond the reach of mere reasoning, subtle, to be experienced by the wise—which the Tathagata, having realised them by his own super-knowledge, proclaims."

**12** "There are, monks, sixty-two grounds on which recluses and brahmans hold views about the past and the future. These are to be found in the Brahmajala Sutta, the Supreme Net, in which all views are caught as fish in a net. It is by knowing and seeing these as they really are that the Tathagata has gone beyond them all."
