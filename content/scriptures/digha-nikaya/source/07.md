---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 7: Sigalovada Sutta (The Layperson's Code of Discipline)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Rajagaha, in the Bamboo Grove. Now at that time, young Sigala, the householder's son, rising early, went out of Rajagaha with wet clothes and wet hair, and worshipped the different quarters—east, south, west, north, nadir, and zenith.

**2** The Blessed One, seeing him, asked: "Why do you worship the six quarters?" Sigala replied: "My father, on his deathbed, told me to do so. Out of respect for my father's word, I do this."

**3** The Blessed One said: "Young householder, that is not the way the six quarters should be worshipped in the Noble One's discipline. How should they be worshipped? One should abandon the four defilements of action, not do evil from the four causes, and not follow the six channels for dissipating wealth. Having thus avoided these fourteen evils, one covers the six quarters."

**4** "What are the four defilements of action? The destruction of life, taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, and false speech."

**5** "What are the four causes of evil action? Evil action is done because of desire, because of hatred, because of delusion, and because of fear."

**6** "What are the six channels for dissipating wealth? Addiction to intoxicants, roaming the streets at untimely hours, frequenting shows, addiction to gambling, association with bad companions, and addiction to idleness."

**7** "And how does one cover the six quarters? The east represents the parents. The south represents the teachers. The west represents the wife and children. The north represents friends and companions. The nadir represents servants and workers. The zenith represents ascetics and brahmans."

**8** "In five ways should parents be ministered to by a child: 'I will support them who supported me; I will perform their duties for them; I will maintain the family tradition; I will make myself worthy of my heritage; and I will offer alms on their behalf after they have died.'"

**9** "In five ways do parents thus ministered to show their love: they keep the child from evil, they encourage the child toward good, they have the child trained in a profession, they arrange a suitable marriage, and in due time they hand over the inheritance."

**10** "In five ways should teachers be ministered to by a pupil: by rising to greet them, by attending on them, by eagerness to learn, by personal service, and by respectful attention while receiving instruction."

**11** "In five ways should a wife be ministered to by a husband: by respect, by courtesy, by faithfulness, by handing over authority to her, and by providing her with adornments."

**12** "Young householder, these are the ways the six quarters should be covered. When one has thus honoured and revered the six quarters according to the Noble One's discipline, one has created harmony in one's household and in the world, and has laid the foundation for a life of peace."
