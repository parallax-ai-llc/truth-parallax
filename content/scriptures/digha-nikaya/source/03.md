---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 3: Ambattha Sutta (About Ambattha)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was touring in the Kosalan country with a large company of monks, about five hundred monks, and he arrived at a Kosalan brahman village called Icchhanankala.

**2** Now at that time the brahman Pokkharasati was living at Ukkattha on a royal domain granted to him by King Pasenadi of Kosala. And he heard: "The ascetic Gotama has arrived at Icchhanankala."

**3** He sent his pupil, the youth Ambattha, saying: "Go, young man, to the ascetic Gotama and find out whether his reputation is justified."

**4** The youth Ambattha went to the Blessed One. And when he arrived, instead of exchanging courteous greetings, he walked up and down while the Blessed One was sitting, and stood while the Blessed One was sitting.

**5** The Blessed One said: "Ambattha, if you were to converse with elder brahmans, with teachers and their teachers, would you walk up and down and stand while they were sitting?" "No, indeed."

**6** "Well, Ambattha, you came here to have a discussion, did you not? You should behave properly." Then Ambattha sat down, but he was full of pride on account of his birth.

**7** The Blessed One asked: "Ambattha, do you know the origin of the Kanha lineage, from which you claim descent?" And the Blessed One traced his ancestry to show that the Kanha lineage descended from a slave-girl of the Sakyans.

**8** Ambattha fell silent. Then the Blessed One said: "Ambattha, a person is not to be judged by birth, but by conduct. It is not birth that makes one a brahman or an outcaste—it is action."

**9** "There are four kinds of persons in this world: the dark person bound for darkness, the dark person bound for light, the bright person bound for darkness, and the bright person bound for light."

**10** "The dark person bound for darkness is one born in a low family who engages in wrong conduct. The dark person bound for light is one born in a low family who engages in right conduct. The bright person bound for darkness is one born in a high family who engages in wrong conduct. The bright person bound for light is one born in a high family who engages in right conduct."

**11** "Ambattha, the wise do not esteem a person on account of birth. It is wisdom and virtue that make a person worthy. Whoever is endowed with moral conduct, with concentration, with wisdom, with liberation, and with the knowledge and vision of liberation—that person is the best among humans."

**12** Then Ambattha, overcome by the Blessed One's teaching, reported everything to his teacher Pokkharasati. And Pokkharasati, filled with faith, went to the Blessed One, took refuge in the Buddha, the Dhamma, and the Sangha, and became a lay disciple.
