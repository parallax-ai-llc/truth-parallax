---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 10: Tevijja Sutta (On the Knowledge of the Three Vedas)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was touring in the Kosalan country with a large company of monks, about five hundred monks, and he arrived at the Kosalan brahman village of Manasakata, where he stayed in a mango grove on the bank of the river Aciravati.

**2** At that time many well-known brahmans were living in Manasakata. And two young brahmans, Vasettha and Bharadvaja, were walking and discussing: "What is the way to union with Brahma?"

**3** Vasettha said: "The way taught by the brahman Pokkharasati is the straight path to union with Brahma." Bharadvaja said: "The way taught by the brahman Tarukkha is the straight path." They could not convince each other.

**4** Then Vasettha said: "The ascetic Gotama is said to know the path to union with Brahma. Let us ask him." So they went to the Blessed One.

**5** The Blessed One asked: "Vasettha, is there a single brahman among the teachers of the Three Vedas who has seen Brahma face to face?" "No, Master Gotama." "Is there one among their teachers' teachers, going back seven generations?" "No, Master Gotama."

**6** "Then it is like a line of blind men, each holding on to the one in front—the first one does not see, the middle one does not see, and the last one does not see. The talk of these brahmans turns out to be laughable, mere words, empty and vain."

**7** "Vasettha, suppose a man said, 'I am in love with the most beautiful woman in the land.' And others asked, 'Do you know whether she is tall or short, dark or fair?' And he said, 'No.' They would say, 'You do not know or see the woman you are in love with.' Would not his words be foolish?" "Indeed, Master Gotama."

**8** "In the same way, these brahmans who are masters of the Three Vedas have not seen Brahma, yet they claim to teach the way to union with Brahma. This is not reasonable."

**9** "But, Master Gotama, is there a way to union with Brahma?" The Blessed One said: "What do you think, Vasettha? Is Brahma possessed of anger and ill will, or is he free from anger and ill will?" "He is free from anger and ill will." "Is he possessed of malice, or is he free from malice?" "Free from malice." "Is he pure or impure?" "He is pure."

**10** "Now, are the brahmans who are masters of the Three Vedas free from anger, ill will, and malice? Are they pure?" "No, Master Gotama, they are full of anger, ill will, and malice."

**11** "Then how could brahmans who are full of anger and ill will, who are impure, attain union with Brahma, who is free from anger and ill will and is pure? That is impossible."

**12** "But, Vasettha, a monk who cultivates a heart of loving-kindness toward all beings—pervading first one quarter of the world, then the second, then the third, then the fourth, above, below, and everywhere, the entire world—with a heart of loving-kindness, abundant, exalted, immeasurable, without hostility, without ill will; and who cultivates a heart of compassion, a heart of sympathetic joy, and a heart of equanimity in the same way—such a monk, at the breaking up of the body, is united with Brahma. This is the path to union with Brahma."
