---
title: "Digha Nikaya - Chapter 8: Agganna Sutta (On Knowledge of Beginnings)"
scripture: "digha-nikaya"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in the mansion of Migara's mother in the Eastern Park. At that time the monks Vasettha and Bharadvaja, who were training for the monkhood, were staying among the monks.

**2** The Blessed One, walking about in the evening, saw them and called to them: "Vasettha and Bharadvaja, you are brahmans by birth who have gone forth from the brahman caste. Do the brahmans not revile and abuse you?"

**3** "Indeed, Lord, they revile us thoroughly. They say: 'The brahman caste is the best, the fairest, the purest. Brahmans are the true sons of Brahma, born from his mouth. Why have you left the best caste and joined the inferior caste of the shaveling ascetic Gotama?'"

**4** The Blessed One said: "Vasettha, the brahmans have forgotten their ancient lore. Brahman women are seen to be fertile, to conceive, to bear children, and to nurse them, yet they claim to be born from Brahma's mouth. They misrepresent Brahma."

**5** "There comes a time, Vasettha, when sooner or later this world contracts. At a time of contraction, beings are mostly born in the Abhassara Brahma world. They dwell there, mind-made, feeding on delight, self-luminous, moving through the air, glorious."

**6** "Then sooner or later, after a very long period, this world begins to expand again. At a time of expansion, beings from the Abhassara world, having passed away there, are mostly reborn in this world. They are mind-made, feeding on delight, self-luminous, moving through the air, glorious. They exist thus for a very long time."

**7** "Then at some point, a savoury earth spread over the waters where they dwelt. It was like the skin that forms on hot milk as it cools. It was endowed with colour, smell, and taste. And some being of a greedy nature said, 'Well now, what can this be?' and tasted the savoury earth. And craving arose in that being."

**8** "And other beings, following that being's example, also tasted the savoury earth. And as they fed on it, their bodies became coarser and coarser, and their self-luminosity disappeared. Then the sun and moon appeared, and the stars. Then night and day, and months and seasons, and years."

**9** "As beings fed on the savoury earth, their bodies became more and more solid. Differences in appearance became manifest—some were better looking, some worse. And the better-looking ones despised the worse-looking ones. And because of their conceit, the savoury earth disappeared."

**10** "Then various other foods appeared—mushrooms, creeping plants, rice that grew without cultivation. And as beings fed on these, the differences between male and female became manifest. And with the arising of sexual desire, they built dwellings to conceal their behaviour."

**11** "Then a being of a lazy nature started storing rice instead of gathering it fresh. Others followed. Then they divided the land. Then stealing arose. Then they appointed a Great Chosen One to maintain order—this was the origin of the warrior caste. Others devoted themselves to meditation—this was the origin of the brahman caste. Others took up various trades—this was the origin of the merchant caste. Others took up hunting and similar pursuits—this was the origin of the worker caste."

**12** "So you see, Vasettha, it was by action and not by birth that these distinctions arose. Among all four castes, there are those who do evil and those who do good. Anyone from any caste, by going forth and living the holy life, by abandoning evil and cultivating good, becomes worthy of respect. It is not birth but practice of the Dhamma that makes a person noble."
