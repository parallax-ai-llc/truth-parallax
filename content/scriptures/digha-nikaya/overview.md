## Overview

The Digha Nikaya ("Collection of Long Discourses") is the first of the five nikayas (collections) of the Sutta Pitaka in the Pali Canon. It contains 34 of the longest suttas attributed to the historical Buddha, Siddhattha Gotama, organized into three vaggas (divisions). These discourses address a wide range of topics including cosmology, ethics, meditation, philosophy, and the Buddha's encounters with Brahmins and other religious teachers.

## Structure

The 34 suttas are grouped into 10 section groups for study:

1. **Sections 1-2 — Silakkhandha Vagga (I)**: The fruits of the ascetic life, the Brahmajala Sutta on views
2. **Sections 3-4 — Silakkhandha Vagga (II)**: Morality, meditation, and the gradual training
3. **Sections 5-6 — Maha Vagga (I)**: The Mahaparinibbana and Mahanidana Suttas
4. **Sections 7-8 — Maha Vagga (II)**: The Sakkapanha and Mahasatipatthana Suttas
5. **Sections 9-10 — Maha Vagga (III)**: Cosmology and the Agganna Sutta
6. **Sections 11-14 — Patika Vagga (I)**: The Patika and Udumbarika Suttas
7. **Sections 15-18 — Patika Vagga (II)**: The Cakkavatti and Sigalovada Suttas
8. **Sections 19-24 — Additional Discourses (I)**: Various longer dialogues
9. **Sections 25-30 — Additional Discourses (II)**: Debates with Brahmins
10. **Sections 31-34 — Additional Discourses (III)**: Final collection of long discourses

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Digha Nikaya holds importance across Buddhist traditions. Parallel versions exist in the Chinese Dirgha Agama and Tibetan canonical collections. The Mahaparinibbana Sutta and Brahmajala Sutta are studied across Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana Buddhism.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Gradual Training | The step-by-step path from morality through meditation to wisdom |
| Cosmology | Detailed descriptions of the Buddhist cosmos and its cycles |
| Dependent Origination | The chain of causation that perpetuates suffering |
| Debate and Dialogue | The Buddha's encounters with rival teachers and Brahmins |
| The Buddha's Final Days | The events surrounding the Buddha's passing |
