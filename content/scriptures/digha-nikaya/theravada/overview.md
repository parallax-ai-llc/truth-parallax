## Overview

The Digha Nikaya holds a position of supreme importance in the Theravada tradition as the collection of the Buddha's longest and most elaborate discourses. These suttas present the complete gradual training (anupubbi-katha) from morality through meditation to wisdom, and include some of the most revered texts in the Theravada canon.

## Key Themes

Theravada interpreters emphasize the Digha Nikaya's comprehensive presentation of the Buddhist path. The Brahmajala Sutta's analysis of 62 wrong views establishes the framework for right understanding. The Samannaphala Sutta presents the complete fruits of the contemplative life. The Mahaparinibbana Sutta, recording the Buddha's final days, is among the most sacred texts in the tradition.

## Practice and Study

In Theravada countries, the Digha Nikaya's discourses are regularly chanted, studied, and commented upon. The Mahasatipatthana Sutta (DN 22) provides the foundational text for vipassana meditation practice. The Sigalovada Sutta (DN 31) is known as the "layperson's vinaya" for its practical ethical guidance.
