## Overview

Mahayana Buddhism engages with the Digha Nikaya primarily through its Chinese parallel, the Dirgha Agama (Chang Ahan Jing), preserved in the Dharmaguptaka recension. While Mahayana traditions emphasize their own sutras, the long discourses provide important doctrinal foundations that the Mahayana tradition builds upon and extends.

## Key Themes

Mahayana scholars read the Digha Nikaya's cosmological discourses as prefiguring the vast cosmological vision of Mahayana sutras. The Buddha's encounters with devas and Brahmas anticipate the Mahayana understanding of multiple Buddha-fields and celestial bodhisattvas.

## Relevance to Mahayana

The Digha Nikaya's teachings on dependent origination, ethics, and meditation remain foundational for Mahayana practice. The discourses' emphasis on compassion and skillful teaching foreshadows the bodhisattva ideal.
