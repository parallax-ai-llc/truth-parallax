## Overview

Zen Buddhism, while emphasizing direct experience beyond textual study, recognizes the Digha Nikaya as preserving important early teachings. The collection's meditation discourses, particularly the Mahasatipatthana Sutta, provide foundations for the mindfulness and concentration practices that underlie Zen meditation.

## Zen Reading

Zen practitioners find in the Digha Nikaya's dialogues a model for the direct, questioning encounter between teacher and student. The Buddha's refusal to engage in metaphysical speculation in certain discourses resonates with Zen's emphasis on direct experience over conceptual elaboration.

## Relevance to Practice

The Digha Nikaya's practical meditation instructions and its emphasis on direct realization over mere intellectual understanding align with core Zen values.
