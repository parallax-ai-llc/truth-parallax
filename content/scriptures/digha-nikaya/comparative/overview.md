## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Digha Nikaya illuminates the Buddha's engagement with the religious diversity of his time and provides rich material for cross-traditional analysis. The collection's discourses on cosmology, ethics, and the nature of the self invite comparison with Hindu, Jain, and Western philosophical traditions.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Buddhist-Brahmanical Dialogue

The Digha Nikaya records extensive dialogues between the Buddha and Brahmin interlocutors, providing a model for respectful inter-religious engagement grounded in philosophical argument.

### Cosmological Comparison

The collection's cosmological discourses can be compared with Hindu puranic cosmology, Jain cosmography, and Western scientific cosmology.

## Comparative Framework

| Concept | Buddhism (DN) | Hinduism | Jainism |
|---------|--------------|----------|---------|
| Cosmos | Cyclical, multiple realms | Cyclical, divine creation | Eternal, uncreated |
| Self | Non-self (anatta) | Atman | Jiva |
| Path | Eightfold path | Various yogas | Three jewels |
