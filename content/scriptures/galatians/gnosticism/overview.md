## Overview

Gnostic interpreters seized upon Galatians' sharp contrast between flesh and spirit, law and freedom, slavery and liberation. Paul's language provided powerful support for the Gnostic narrative: the material world, governed by the law of the Demiurge, enslaves the divine spark within humanity, and only *gnosis* (spiritual knowledge) — not legal observance — can liberate it.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### The Elemental Spirits
Paul's reference to the *stoicheia tou kosmou* ("elemental spirits of the world," 4:3, 9) was read by Gnostics as the archons — cosmic rulers who enforce the Demiurge's law and keep humanity imprisoned in ignorance.

### Flesh Versus Spirit
The flesh/spirit contrast (5:16-26) was interpreted not as a moral struggle but as an ontological distinction between the material body (created by the Demiurge) and the divine pneumatic seed within the Gnostic believer.

### Freedom from the Law
Paul's rejection of Torah observance was read as confirming that all law originates from the inferior creator-god. True freedom comes through *gnosis* — knowledge of one's origin in the *Pleroma* (divine fullness).
