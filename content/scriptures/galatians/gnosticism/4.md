## Sons, Not Slaves

Gnostic interpreters read Galatians 4 through the lens of their distinctive cosmology, finding in adoption and freedom further evidence of the dualistic worldview that separates the true God from the material creator. The key passages — adoption as sons (4:4-7), return to weak elements (4:9), allegory of Hagar and Sarah (4:21-31) — were appropriated to support Gnostic teachings about the divine spark, the archonic powers, and the path of spiritual liberation through *gnosis*.

Valentinian and Sethian readers found in Paul's language a coded revelation intended for the pneumatic (spiritual) elite. The surface meaning, accessible to the psychic (ordinary) Christian, concealed a deeper teaching about the soul's origin in the divine *Pleroma* and its eventual return through knowledge of the true God beyond the Demiurge.

Elaine Pagels' groundbreaking study *The Gnostic Paul* demonstrated how systematically Gnostic communities read Paul's letters, including this chapter, as esoteric instruction. While mainstream Christianity rejected these readings, they reveal the extraordinary interpretive range that Paul's letters supported in the early centuries.
