## Overview

Eastern Orthodox reading of Galatians centers on baptismal transformation and life in the Spirit rather than the Western forensic categories of justification. Paul's declaration that "it is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me" (2:20) is foundational for the Orthodox understanding of theosis — the progressive deification of the believer through participation in Christ.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Baptismal Participation
"As many of you as were baptized into Christ have put on Christ" (3:27) is understood sacramentally: baptism effects a real ontological union with Christ, not merely a symbolic identification. This transforms the baptized person from within.

### Life in the Spirit
The fruit of the Spirit (5:22-23) represents the characteristics of the deified life. Orthodox interpreters read this not as moral self-improvement but as the natural outflowing of the Spirit's indwelling presence.

### Freedom as Communion
Christian freedom is understood not as individual autonomy but as communion — freedom from the passions and freedom for love within the body of Christ.
