## The Promise and the Law

Islamic engagement with Galatians 3 finds in the promise and the law resonances with Quranic teaching and the prophetic tradition. The key passages — Abraham's faith (3:6-9), curse of the law (3:10-14), the law as guardian (3:24-25), all one in Christ (3:28) — address themes that are also central to Islamic theology: God's sovereignty, moral accountability, community, and the proper orientation of human life toward the divine.

The Quran addresses many of the same fundamental questions Paul raises in this chapter, though it arrives at different conclusions regarding the nature of Jesus, the role of the law, and the means of salvation. Islamic scholars read Paul's arguments as reflecting the theological developments that led Christianity away from the pure monotheism (*tawhid*) that Islam seeks to restore.

Despite these theological differences, the ethical and spiritual themes of this chapter — patience, gratitude, generosity, and trust in God — find strong parallels in Islamic moral teaching and Sufi spirituality, demonstrating the shared Abrahamic heritage that underlies both traditions.
