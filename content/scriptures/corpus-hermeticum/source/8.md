---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 8"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Hermes to Tat: No one of the things that are is destroyed; men are mistaken when they call changes "destructions" and "deaths." For death is destruction, and nothing in the Kosmos is destroyed.

**2** If the Kosmos is a second god, a living being, it is impossible that any part of a living being should die. All things that are in the Kosmos are parts of the Kosmos. Death, therefore, is not the destruction of being but the dissolution of a compound.

**3** The dissolving of a compound is not death; it is a change. And what is change but the putting off of one form and the putting on of another?

**4** The sun sets not, though men call it setting; for the sun has no setting and no rising, but moves ceaselessly in its orbit. So all things change but are not destroyed.

**5** Consider the body of a man. It changes continually; now it is a child, now a youth, now a man, now an elder. Yet who would say that the child was destroyed when the youth appeared?

**6** Let go, then, of this error of destruction and death. There is no death. There is only transformation. And transformation is the movement of the Kosmos, the breath of God.

**7** If thou understand that all things are one — one Life, one Mind, one God — then thou wilt understand that nothing perishes, but all things change according to the eternal will.
