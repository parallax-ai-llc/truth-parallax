---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 1"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Once upon a time, when I had begun to think about the things that are, and my thoughts had soared high aloft, while my bodily senses had been restrained, as in deep sleep — I thought I beheld a Being of vast and boundless magnitude coming toward me, who called me by name and said: "What wouldst thou hear and see, and what hast thou in mind to learn and know?"

**2** I said: "Who art thou?" He answered: "I am Poimandres, the Mind of the Sovereignty. I know what thou desirest, and I am with thee everywhere."

**3** I said: "I wish to learn the things that are, and understand their nature, and know God. How greatly do I desire to hear!" He answered: "Hold fast in thy mind all that thou wouldst know, and I will teach thee."

**4** With these words, He changed His form, and straightway, in the twinkling of an eye, all things were opened to me, and I beheld a boundless view: all was Light, serene and joyous. And I was filled with love at the sight.

**5** And after a little while, there was a Darkness, coming down in a spiraling course, fearful and hateful, which I likened to a serpent. And then the Darkness changed into a sort of moist Nature, tossed about beyond all power of words, and pouring forth smoke as from fire, and sending forth a sound, an indescribable moan.

**6** Then there came forth from the Light a holy Word upon the moist Nature, and unmixed Fire leapt forth from the moist Nature and rose on high. The Fire was light, keen, and active. And the Air, being light, followed the Fire, rising from earth and water to the Fire, so that it seemed to hang from it.

**7** And earth and water remained in their place, mingled together, so that the earth could not be distinguished from the water; and they were kept in motion by reason of the breath of the Word which was borne upon them.
