---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 14"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Hermes: For that the Mind, O Tat, is of the very substance of God — if any substance of God there be — what kind of substance that may be, He alone can tell precisely.

**2** Mind is not of the substance of the things that are; it has no substance. For were it not without substance, it could not contain all things within itself. For every body occupies some place, and what occupies a place cannot contain another thing.

**3** Think of a place as something bodiless; then think of mind as bodiless, that fills no place, and yet doth need no place to hold it. Imagine mind apart from body, not limited, not bound, not set in measure; and yet imagine mind as free and pure.

**4** Mind is the first of all God's gifts to men; and from Mind follows Reason, and from Reason, Thought.

**5** And Thought is the servant of Reason, as Reason is the servant of Mind. And Mind is the minister of God.

**6** Now when the Mind doth enter the devout man's soul, it illumines it; and counteracts the dark tendencies of the lower nature.

**7** Happy is the soul that is filled with the Mind! And wretched is the soul that is devoid of it!

**8** For the soul devoid of Mind can neither speak nor act. Such is the nature of the Mind: it will have naught of dark or troublous things.
