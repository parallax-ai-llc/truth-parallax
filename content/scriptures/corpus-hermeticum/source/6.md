---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 6"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Hermes: The Good, O Tat, is in nothing else but in God alone; or rather, the Good is God Himself eternally.

**2** The Good is that which gives all and receives nothing. God gives all and receives nothing. Therefore God is the Good, and the Good is God.

**3** Another name of God is the Father, because He is the Maker of all things. For it is the part of a father to make.

**4** The greatest evil among men is not to know God. But to be able to know God, and to wish and hope to know Him, is the road which leads straight to the Good, and it is an easy road to travel.

**5** For on every side the Good will meet thee, in every place thou wilt see it, even when and where thou dost not look for it. Whether thou be waking or sleeping, whether sailing or on shore, by day or by night, speaking or silent — for there is nothing that the Good is not.

**6** Wouldst thou say that God is invisible? Speak not so! Who is more manifest than God? He has made all things for this purpose: that through them all thou shouldst see Him. This is the supreme Good of God: that He is made manifest.

**7** For the Mind sees God; and God sees the Mind. The Mind is in God, and God is in the Mind. Let us then worship and adore; let us give praise and render thanks to God; for this is the highest worship.
