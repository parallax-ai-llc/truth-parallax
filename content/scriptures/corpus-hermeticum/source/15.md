---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 15"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Hermes: Now I will speak to thee of Truth. Asclepius, thou must not think that God hath given Truth to any on the earth.

**2** Nay, none of all the things down here is true; for how could Truth be found down here in bodies so unstable?

**3** Such things alone are true which are conceived by mind, in concert with the soul. And even of these the bodies are not true, but only the concepts of the bodies.

**4** If, then, these concepts even are not true, how much more untrue are the bodies? For these are but shifting things and fleeting, that have no permanence.

**5** They are all image and appearance. They seem, but are not. At one time they appear and at another vanish. They are not even when they seem to be. And so they are in truth mere semblances.

**6** But Truth herself, Asclepius, abides ever, and is in God alone; or rather, Truth herself is God.

**7** In whom, then, doth Truth find her being? Not in the cosmos, Asclepius, for all things that come to be and have come to be in the cosmos are tinctured with deceit.

**8** For Truth abideth with the Father of all. If anything seems true on earth, it is a shadow of the Truth. For whatsoever is on earth is not the Truth, but seemeth so by image and reflection; but whatsoever is in Heaven, there abideth Truth.

**9** This much I say to thee: that Truth is something which is not to be attained on earth, as I have said. But if anyone hath power to apprehend it, let him apprehend; and if he see it, then let him delight in it.
