---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 4"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Hermes: The Maker made the whole world not with His hands but with His Word. Think of Him then as present and as ever being, as having made all things, as One and Only, as having of His own will fashioned all that is.

**2** For that is His body which is not tangible nor visible nor measurable nor extensible nor like any other body. It is not fire nor water nor air nor spirit, but all things come from Him.

**3** Being Good, He willed to consecrate this to Himself; He adorned the earth, and set the world in order, and brought Light out of Darkness.

**4** What, then, is God? He is the Good, the Beautiful, Happiness, Wisdom. He is Eternity, giving eternity. He is Life, giving life.

**5** Now the Mind, the Father of all, who is Life and Light, brought forth Man like unto Himself. And He loved Man as His own offspring; for Man, bearing the Father's image, was very fair.

**6** God did indeed love His own form; and He delivered over to Man all the things that had been made.

**7** And Man wished to create, and permission was given him by the Father. Entering into the Maker's sphere, where he was to have all authority, Man beheld the works of his brother the Maker, and the Seven Rulers loved him, and each gave Man a share of their own nature.
