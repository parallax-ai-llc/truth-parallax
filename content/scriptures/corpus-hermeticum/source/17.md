---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 17"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 17
---

**1** Tat: Speak to me, father, about the incorporeal.

**2** Hermes: Consider the incorporeal through its reflection. The eye of the mind, my son, perceives the invisible through the visible. What you see with bodily eyes is form and image only; the essence behind is invisible.

**3** For the cosmos itself is not visible in its essence, but in the effects and manifestations of its essence.

**4** And so it is with the soul, and so with Mind. They are invisible in their essence, but manifest in their effects.

**5** The soul manifests through the body, the Mind manifests through the soul. Thus the chain descends from the Invisible to the Visible.

**6** Consider the painter, my son. He conceives in his mind the image before he paints it. The painting is but the visible manifestation of what was once invisible thought.

**7** So it is with all creation: what you see is the outward expression of inward thought. And the supreme Thought is God.

**8** Now if you grasp this truth, that all visible things are but expressions of the Invisible, you will begin to understand the nature of God.

**9** For God is the Invisible made manifest through all things. He is everywhere present, yet nowhere seen. He is in all things, yet beyond all things.
