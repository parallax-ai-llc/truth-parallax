---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 7"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Hermes: Whither are ye borne, O men, ye that are drunken, having drunk the unmixed wine of ignorance? Ye cannot hold it, but are already beginning to spew it forth!

**2** Be sober; look up with the eyes of your heart, and if ye all cannot do so, at least those among you who can. For the evil of ignorance floods the whole earth, and corrupts the soul that is shut up in the body.

**3** Do not be carried along with the great stream; use a counter-current, ye who are able to gain the harbor of safety, and making port there, seek one who shall lead you by the hand and guide you to the door of knowledge.

**4** There shines the Light in which there is no darkness. There no man is drunken, but all are sober, gazing with the heart on Him who wishes to be seen.

**5** For He cannot be heard, nor can He be spoken of, nor can He be seen with the eyes, but only with the Mind and Heart. But first thou must tear off the garment that thou wearest, the garment of ignorance, the foundation of all evil.

**6** The garment of ignorance is the covering of the soul. So long as thou hatest thy body, thou canst not love thy true self. But when thou comest to know thy true self, which is immortal, thou wilt love it.

**7** Let all thy effort be directed to attaining knowledge of God. For knowledge of God is the goal of the soul.
