---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 12"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Hermes: The Mind, O Tat, is of the very essence of God. What type of essence God is, He alone precisely knows.

**2** The Mind is not cut off from God's essential being, but is spread abroad from it, as is the light of the sun.

**3** Now this Mind in men is God; and therefore are some of mankind gods, and their humanity is near to the divine.

**4** For the Good Daimon said: "Gods are immortal men, and men are mortal gods."

**5** Tat: But how is this, thrice-greatest one? Is not sense common to all men?

**6** Hermes: Not all, O son, have the True Mind. Some are deceived, and follow after the image of the thing; while the thing's self they leave behind, believing that the image is the truth.

**7** Tat: How then should I proceed, O father?

**8** Hermes: Labour not in vain, my son, but free thyself from attachments, and thou shalt find all things within thee.

**9** Let thy search be for truth alone. The wise man seeks truth as the drowning man seeks the shore.

**10** Tat: I hear and understand, father. You have opened the eyes of my mind.
