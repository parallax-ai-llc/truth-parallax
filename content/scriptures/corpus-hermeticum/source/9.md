---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 9"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Hermes to Asclepius: In yesterday's discourse, Asclepius, I dealt with thought; now it is fitting that I should discourse to thee on sense-perception and on the difference between sense and thought.

**2** Sense-perception belongs to the body, and thought belongs to the soul. Thought is an activity of the soul; sense-perception is a passivity of the body. Both are needed for the comprehension of truth.

**3** But the senses can deceive, while thought, when rightly directed, leads to truth. The senses perceive the visible world; thought perceives the invisible.

**4** The visible world is an image of the invisible. The things perceived by the senses are shadows of the things perceived by the mind. When thou seest a shadow, seek the substance that casts it.

**5** All bodies are in motion; only the bodiless is still. The soul moves within the body, the Mind moves within the soul, and God moves within the Mind. Thus all motion derives from the stillness of God.

**6** Knowledge is not the same as sense-perception. Knowledge comes from thought, and thought is a gift of God. Sense-perception is shared by all animals; knowledge belongs to man alone.

**7** But not all men have attained to knowledge. Knowledge is given to those who seek it with their whole heart, who purify themselves from the passions of the body and turn the eyes of the mind toward the Light.
