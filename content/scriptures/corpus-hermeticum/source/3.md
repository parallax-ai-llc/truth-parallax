---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 3"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The sacred discourse of Hermes Trismegistus: Glory be to God the Father of all; glory to God whose will is performed by His own Powers; glory to thee, O God, whose Word proceeding from thee is the planting of all Nature.

**2** The Light, the Mind, the first God, existed before the moist Nature which appeared out of the Darkness. The elements of Nature descended from the will of God, which received the Word and beheld the beautiful world of the Maker-Mind, and imitated it.

**3** And the elements of Nature brought forth living creatures. Air brought forth birds; water brought forth fish; and earth brought forth four-footed beasts and creeping things.

**4** But the Father of all, the Mind, being Life and Light, brought forth Man, equal to Himself, whom He loved as His own child. For Man was very fair, bearing the Father's image.

**5** Man, having received authority over all creation, stooped through the Harmony and broke through the vault of the heavens, and showed to downward-moving Nature the beautiful form of God.

**6** And Nature, seeing the beauty of the form of God in Man, smiled with love. And Man, seeing his own likeness reflected in the water of Nature, desired it and wished to dwell there. And at the moment he willed it, he took up his abode in the form devoid of reason.

**7** And that is why Man, unlike all other creatures upon the earth, is twofold: mortal by reason of the body, but immortal by reason of the essential Man.
