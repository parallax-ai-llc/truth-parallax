---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 18"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 18
---

**1** If thou wouldst praise God, O Tat, thou shouldst be silent; for in silence, God is praised.

**2** For God is not to be praised with lips, but with the mind. He cannot be set forth in words, for words define and limit. But God is limitless.

**3** What, then, is God? The Good, the Beautiful, Happiness, Wisdom, and all virtues, all powers, all forms, all things.

**4** What, then, is the praise of God? It is this: to know Him. For Knowledge alone is the praise of God. And the true offering is the return of the soul to its Source.

**5** Tat: But must I then, O father, offer nothing?

**6** Hermes: Offer no material thing, my son. The sacrifice that God requires is this: purity of heart, silence of the tongue, and the dedication of the mind to Truth.

**7** For the man who praises God in silence has found the eternal praise. The man who serves God in thought has found the eternal service.

**8** For all things visible shall pass away; but the worship of the heart endures forever.

**9** This, then, is the end of Knowledge: to worship God in silence, to know Him in the heart, and to return to Him who is the Source and End of all things.

**10** Thus hath the sermon been delivered unto thee, O Tat. Go forth and meditate upon these things, and thou shalt find the truth within thee; for the Kingdom of God is not without, but within.
