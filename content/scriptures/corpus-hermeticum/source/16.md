---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 16"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 16
---

**1** Asclepius: The sermon which thou preachedst yesterday, Thrice-greatest one, thou didst devote to me; 'tis only just, then, that today's should be devoted unto Tat, the more because it is a summary of all the General Sermons that have been preached to him.

**2** God, then, and Father, and the Good, O Tat, have the same nature, or more truly, the same energy.

**3** For nature is a name applied to things that grow, both to the things that move and to the things that do not move, that is, to things both active and at rest, of which the nature of each is shown forth by its own proper form.

**4** But Energy is something different; it is what is contained in something else, or rather, in all things, whether they be bodies or not — for movement itself is energy.

**5** Energy, then, is something bodiless — an image of the things that are. Now active Energy is different from passive; active Energy is that which is free and rules; passive Energy is that which is in bondage and is ruled.

**6** That which acts leads, and the passive is led. But Energy leads all things, and active Energy is free, and passive is enslaved.

**7** God, then, and Father, and the Good are names of the selfsame thing: the Mind, the Creator, the Ruler, and the Energy.
