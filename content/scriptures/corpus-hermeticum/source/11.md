---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum - Chapter 11"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Mind: Whither bear ye away, O men? Ye who are drunk, who have drained the wine of ignorance to the dregs — which ye cannot even carry, but are even now throwing up?

**2** Stay yourselves, be sober! Look upward with the eyes of the heart — if ye cannot all, at least ye who have the power.

**3** For the flood of ignorance surroundeth all the earth, and corrupteth the soul shut up in the body, and doth not suffer it to anchor in the Havens of Salvation.

**4** Sink not then in the great flood, but using the ebb, ye who are able, reach the haven of Salvation, and find a pilot; and let him guide you to the Gates of Knowledge,

**5** Where shines the Light, stripped clean of darkness; where none is drunk, but where all are sober, and in their hearts look up to Him who willeth to be seen.

**6** He is no ear can hear, no eye can see, nor tongue can speak, but only mind and heart.

**7** But first thou must tear away from thee the garment that thou wearest — the cloak of darkness, the web of ignorance, the prop of evil, the bond of corruption, the dark prison, the living death, the conscious corpse, the tomb thou carriest about with thee, the robber in thy house, the one who, through the things he loveth, hateth thee, and through the things he hateth, beareth thee malice.

**8** Such is the hateful garment thou hast put on; it strangles thee, and drags thee down to it, so that thou canst not gaze above, and see the Beauty of the Truth, the Good that doth abide above, and hate the evil of the garment.
