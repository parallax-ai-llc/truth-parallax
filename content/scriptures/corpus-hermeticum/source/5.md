---
title: "Corpus Hermeticum Section 5"
scripture: "corpus-hermeticum"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Hermes to his son Tat: God, the Father and the Good, has the same nature as the Kosmos, O Tat. Or rather, the Kosmos receives its nature from the Father.

**2** The Kosmos is not good, inasmuch as it is material, and for that reason it is subject to change and movement. But it is good inasmuch as it is a work of the Father, and in that sense it participates in the Good.

**3** For the Father made the Kosmos beautiful, yet the Kosmos is not good in itself. It is to be admired, but it is not to be worshipped; for it is not the Father but the image of the Father.

**4** God is invisible, but He makes Himself visible through His works. That is His peculiar quality. He is made visible through all things and in all things, and especially to those to whom He wishes to manifest Himself.

**5** If thou wouldst see Him, look at the sun, look at the course of the moon, look at the order of the stars. Who is it that maintains that order?

**6** Who is it that sets bounds to the sea? Who is it that set the earth in its place? There is One who is the Maker and Lord of all these things. And that One is God, the invisible God, who is made known through His visible works.

**7** It is impossible for the Maker to be invisible. He shows Himself through everything He has made. This is the grace of God: that He manifests Himself through all His works.
