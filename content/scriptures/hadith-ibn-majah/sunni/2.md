Ibn Majah's treatment of prayer and mosques provides Sunni jurisprudence with additional prophetic traditions governing the central act of Islamic worship. His prayer chapters cover the call to prayer (*adhan*), the conditions and pillars of valid prayer, the congregational and Friday prayers, and the various voluntary prayers that enrich the devotional life of the Muslim. Some of these traditions are unique to his collection, offering evidence not available in the other five canonical works.

The mosque traditions address the etiquette and sanctity of places of worship, the merits of congregational prayer, and the regulations governing the construction and maintenance of mosques. Sunni scholars draw upon these traditions to establish the normative framework for mosque life, from the proper conduct of worshippers to the responsibilities of the imam. Ibn Majah's unique narrations in this area contribute to a more complete picture of prophetic guidance on communal worship than would be available from the other canonical collections alone.
