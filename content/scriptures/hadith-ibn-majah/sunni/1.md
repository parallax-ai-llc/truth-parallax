Ibn Majah opens his collection with an important introduction (*Muqaddimah*) on the authority of the Sunnah — the prophetic practice as a binding source of religious guidance alongside the Quran. Sunni scholars value this introductory section for its clear articulation of the obligation to follow prophetic hadith and the condemnation of religious innovation (*bid'ah*). The traditions assembled here establish the epistemological framework within which the entire collection is to be understood.

The purification section that follows addresses the ritual prerequisites of worship with characteristic thoroughness. Ibn Majah records traditions on ablution, ritual bathing, the use of the tooth-stick (*siwak*), and the handling of impurities, providing the legal evidence base for Sunni jurisprudential rulings on ritual cleanliness. Sunni scholars appreciate that some of Ibn Majah's purification traditions are unique to his collection, filling gaps left by the other five canonical compilations and providing additional evidence for disputed legal questions.
