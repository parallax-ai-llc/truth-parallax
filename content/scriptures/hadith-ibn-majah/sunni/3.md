Ibn Majah's sections on fasting and pilgrimage address two of Islam's five pillars with the combination of legal precision and devotional warmth that characterizes his collection. The fasting traditions cover the Ramadan fast, voluntary fasting, the conditions for exemption, and the spiritual merits of fasting as a means of drawing closer to God. Some of his unique traditions on the virtues of fasting have become widely cited in Sunni devotional literature.

The Hajj chapters record prophetic traditions governing every aspect of the pilgrimage — from the intention to perform it to the rituals at each sacred site. Ibn Majah preserves traditions on the spiritual significance of the Hajj that complement the more legally focused treatments in other collections. Sunni scholars value his coverage of the Umrah (minor pilgrimage) and the merits of visiting the Prophet's mosque in Medina, providing a comprehensive prophetic framework for the pilgrim's devotional journey and its legal requirements.
