Academic study of Ibn Majah's commercial and inheritance traditions contributes to the economic and legal history of early Islamic society. The trade hadiths reveal the types of commercial practices in use, the ethical principles invoked to regulate the marketplace, and the tensions between prophetic ideals and commercial realities. Scholars analyze these traditions for evidence of the economic conditions in the Hijaz, the influence of pre-Islamic Arabian commercial custom, and the development of distinctive Islamic economic ethics.

The inheritance traditions are studied within the broader context of Near Eastern succession law. Academic researchers examine how the Islamic inheritance system — with its fixed shares, emphasis on female inheritance rights, and detailed mathematical framework — related to pre-Islamic Arabian practice and to the inheritance systems of neighboring Jewish and Christian communities. Ibn Majah's unique narrations provide additional data for reconstructing the development of this distinctive legal system and its relationship to the broader legal culture of the ancient Near East.
