Academic study of Ibn Majah's prayer and mosque traditions provides evidence for the development of Islamic liturgical practice and the social function of the mosque in early Muslim communities. The prayer traditions are analyzed for variant practices they preserve, the chains of transmission that carry them, and the relationship between Ibn Majah's unique narrations and the broader corpus of prayer hadith in the other canonical collections.

The mosque traditions are studied for their contribution to understanding the role of the mosque as a social institution. Scholars examine how the prophetic traditions on mosque etiquette, construction, and communal worship reflect the evolving character of the mosque from a simple prayer space in the Prophet's time to a complex institutional structure serving multiple community functions. Ibn Majah's unique narrations provide additional evidence for this developmental trajectory, filling gaps in the picture available from the other canonical collections.
