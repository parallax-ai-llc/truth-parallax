---
title: "Sunan Ibn Majah - Chapter 1"
scripture: "hadith-ibn-majah"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Narrated Umar ibn al-Khattab: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Actions are judged by intentions. Every person will be rewarded according to what he intended."

**2** Narrated Anas ibn Malik: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim."

**3** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever treads a path seeking knowledge, Allah will make easy for him the path to Paradise."

**4** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The best of you are those who learn the Quran and teach it."

**5** Narrated Kathir ibn Qais: I was sitting with Abu Darda in the mosque of Damascus. A man came and said: "O Abu Darda, I have come from the city of the Messenger for a tradition I have heard you relate from the Messenger of Allah." He said: "Did you not come for trade?" He said: "No." He said: "Did you not come for anything else?" He said: "No." Abu Darda said: I heard the Messenger of Allah say: "Whoever follows a path seeking knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise. The angels lower their wings in approval for the seeker of knowledge. All those in the heavens and on earth and the fish in the water seek forgiveness for the scholar. The superiority of the scholar over the worshipper is like the superiority of the moon over the stars. The scholars are the heirs of the prophets. The prophets did not leave behind dinars or dirhams; they left behind knowledge. Whoever takes it has taken a great share."

**6** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When a person dies, his deeds come to an end except for three: ongoing charity, knowledge from which others benefit, or a righteous child who prays for him."

**7** Narrated Anas: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever goes out seeking knowledge is in the path of Allah until he returns."

**8** Narrated Ibn Mas'ud: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Do not wish to be like anyone except in two cases: a person whom Allah has given wealth and he spends it in the right way, and a person whom Allah has given wisdom and he acts accordingly and teaches it to others."
