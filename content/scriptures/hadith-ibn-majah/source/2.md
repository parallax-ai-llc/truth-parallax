---
title: "Sunan Ibn Majah - Chapter 2"
scripture: "hadith-ibn-majah"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Islam began as something strange and will return to being strange as it began. So blessed are the strangers."

**2** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "My entire nation will enter Paradise except those who refuse." They said: "O Messenger of Allah, who will refuse?" He said: "Whoever obeys me will enter Paradise, and whoever disobeys me has refused."

**3** Narrated Irbad ibn Sariyah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, gave us a sermon which made our hearts tremble and our eyes flow with tears. We said: "O Messenger of Allah, this is as if it were a farewell sermon, so advise us." He said: "I advise you to fear Allah, and to hear and obey, even if a slave is appointed over you. Whoever among you lives after me will see great disagreement. You must follow my Sunnah and the sunnah of the rightly-guided caliphs. Hold fast to it and cling to it with your molar teeth."

**4** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever holds fast to my Sunnah when my nation becomes corrupt will have the reward of a hundred martyrs."

**5** Narrated Anas: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever revives a practice from my Sunnah that had died after me will have a reward similar to all those who act upon it, without that detracting from their rewards in the slightest."

**6** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Every innovation is misguidance, and every misguidance is in the Fire."

**7** Narrated Aisha: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever introduces something in this matter of ours which is not from it, it will be rejected."

**8** Narrated Jabir: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, used to say in his sermon: "The best speech is the Book of Allah, and the best guidance is the guidance of Muhammad. The worst of matters are newly invented ones, and every innovation is misguidance."
