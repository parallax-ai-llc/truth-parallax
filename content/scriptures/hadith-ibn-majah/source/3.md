---
title: "Sunan Ibn Majah - Chapter 3"
scripture: "hadith-ibn-majah"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Be in this world as if you were a stranger or a traveler."

**2** Narrated Ibn Umar: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, took me by the shoulder and said: "Be in this world as if you were a stranger or a traveler." And Ibn Umar used to say: "When evening comes, do not expect to see the morning, and when morning comes, do not expect to see the evening. Take from your health for your sickness, and from your life for your death."

**3** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Look at those who are beneath you, and do not look at those who are above you. That is more likely to prevent you from belittling the favors of Allah upon you."

**4** Narrated Sahl ibn Sa'd: A man came to the Prophet, peace be upon him, and said: "O Messenger of Allah, direct me to an act which, if I do it, Allah will love me and the people will love me." He said: "Be detached from the world and Allah will love you. Be detached from what people possess, and the people will love you."

**5** Narrated Anas: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "If the son of Adam had a valley full of gold, he would want to have two valleys. Nothing fills his mouth except dust, and Allah forgives whoever repents."

**6** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Richness is not having many possessions. Rather, true richness is the richness of the soul."

**7** Narrated Abdullah ibn Amr: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Successful is the one who has accepted Islam, has been provided with sufficient sustenance, and has been made content by Allah with what He has given him."

**8** Narrated Abu Hurairah: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The world is a prison for the believer and a paradise for the disbeliever."
