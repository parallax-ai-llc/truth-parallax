Sufi readers engage with Ibn Majah's prayer traditions as guides to the art of sacred communication. Each element of the prayer — standing, bowing, prostrating, reciting — is understood as a station in the soul's journey toward God. The call to prayer (*adhan*) is heard not merely as a summons to communal worship but as the divine invitation echoing through creation, calling every heart to return to its source.

The mosque traditions speak to the Sufi understanding of sacred space. While the physical mosque is a designated place of worship, the Sufi tradition teaches that the purified heart is the true mosque — the inner sanctuary where God is worshipped in spirit. Ibn Majah's traditions on the merits of mosque attendance and the etiquette of sacred space are embraced as prophetic guidance for cultivating the outer conditions that support inner devotion. The movement from the profane world into the mosque symbolizes the spiritual transition from scattered worldly consciousness to focused divine awareness.
