Salafi scholars engage with Ibn Majah's fasting and Hajj traditions through rigorous authentication, using al-Albani's comprehensive grading to identify which traditions provide reliable prophetic guidance and which are too weak to serve as evidence for practice. The fasting traditions are applied to determine the authentic Sunnah practices of Ramadan and voluntary fasting, distinguishing prophetic guidance from customs that may have become widespread without strong hadith support.

The Hajj traditions are used alongside the other canonical collections to reconstruct the Prophet's Farewell Pilgrimage as precisely as possible. Salafi scholars seek to perform the Hajj exactly as the Prophet performed it, and every authenticated tradition in Ibn Majah that adds detail to this reconstruction is valued. Where his unique narrations on the pilgrimage pass the test of authentication, they contribute to the Salafi project of restoring the original prophetic practice, purified of later additions and cultural innovations.
