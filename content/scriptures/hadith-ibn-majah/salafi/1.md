Salafi scholars accord particular importance to Ibn Majah's introductory section on the authority of the Sunnah, which articulates principles central to the Salafi theological project. His traditions on the obligation to follow the Prophet, the condemnation of innovation, and the authority of hadith alongside the Quran provide foundational textual support for the Salafi commitment to scripture-based religion. These introductory hadiths are frequently cited in Salafi literature as prophetic mandates for their methodology.

The purification traditions are engaged with careful attention to authentication, as the Salafi approach requires verified prophetic evidence for every act of worship. Ibn Majah's purification narrations are cross-referenced with al-Albani's grading, and only those confirmed as authentic are used as the basis for practice. The unique traditions in this section are evaluated with particular care, as their absence from the other canonical collections raises questions about their provenance that Salafi hadith methodology is designed to address.
