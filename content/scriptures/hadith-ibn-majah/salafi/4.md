Salafi scholars apply Ibn Majah's commercial and inheritance traditions to contemporary economic questions, insisting that prophetic precedent provides binding guidance for financial conduct. The trade traditions are used to evaluate modern business practices against authenticated prophetic standards, with particular attention to the prohibition of interest-based lending and the requirement of transparent transactions. Ibn Majah's unique commercial narrations are employed when they pass authentication, contributing additional evidence for the Salafi framework of Islamic economics.

The inheritance traditions are applied as prophetic legislation governing estate distribution. Salafi scholars use Ibn Majah's narrations alongside the Quranic inheritance verses and the hadith evidence from other collections to construct a comprehensive framework of Islamic succession law. The emphasis falls on following the prophetic and Quranic prescriptions exactly as stated, without the modifications or interpretive extensions that later legal commentators may have introduced. Where Ibn Majah preserves authenticated traditions on specific inheritance scenarios, these are added to the evidence base for legal rulings.
