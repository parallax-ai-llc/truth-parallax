Salafi engagement with Ibn Majah's prayer and mosque traditions emphasizes the need for authenticated prophetic evidence as the basis for liturgical practice. Each prayer tradition is evaluated through al-Albani's grading, with authenticated narrations added to the evidence base for determining the correct form of prayer and those failing authentication set aside. Ibn Majah's unique prayer traditions receive particular scrutiny, as Salafi methodology requires strong evidence before accepting practices not attested in the more reliable collections.

The mosque traditions are applied to contemporary questions about mosque practice, with Salafi scholars using Ibn Majah's authenticated narrations to distinguish between legitimate prophetic Sunnah and innovations that have entered mosque culture over the centuries. Traditions on the simplicity of early mosques, the proper conduct of worshippers, and the avoidance of decoration and distraction are cited as prophetic guidance for maintaining the mosque as a place of pure worship, free from the accretions of cultural custom and popular religion.
