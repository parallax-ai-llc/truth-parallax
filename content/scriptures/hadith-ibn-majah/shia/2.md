Shia engagement with Ibn Majah's prayer and mosque traditions follows the pattern of comparative evaluation characteristic of Shia hadith methodology. The prayer traditions are assessed for compatibility with Ja'fari liturgical practice, with attention to narrations that may support Shia positions on disputed points such as the combination of prayers and the form of the *qunut* supplication within prayer.

The mosque traditions are engaged with interest in any references to sacred sites associated with Ahl al-Bayt. While the Sunni canonical collections focus on the three holy mosques, Shia sacred geography includes the shrines and mosques of the Imams as centers of devotion. Ibn Majah's unique narrations are examined for any traditions that acknowledge the spiritual significance of sites connected to the Prophet's family, providing additional evidence for the Shia devotional landscape from within the Sunni canonical tradition.
