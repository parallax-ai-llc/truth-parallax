Shia engagement with Ibn Majah's commercial and inheritance traditions focuses on areas where Sunni and Shia jurisprudence diverge. The trade traditions are assessed for compatibility with Ja'fari commercial law, which shares fundamental principles — the prohibition of usury, the requirement of transparency — while differing on specific contract forms and conditions. Convergences are noted as evidence of shared prophetic foundations.

The inheritance chapters are of particular interest because Sunni and Shia inheritance law differ significantly on key questions — most notably the share of the Prophet's daughter Fatimah in her father's estate and the broader question of whether prophets leave inheritable property. Shia scholars examine Ibn Majah's inheritance narrations for evidence bearing on these disputes, evaluating each tradition through their own criteria of narrator reliability and textual coherence. The inheritance sections thus become a site of ongoing inter-traditional scholarly engagement.
