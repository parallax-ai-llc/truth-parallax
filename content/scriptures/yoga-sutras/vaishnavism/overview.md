## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Yoga Sutras through the lens of theistic devotion, emphasizing Patanjali's concept of *Ishvara pranidhana* (surrender to God) as the most direct path to *samadhi*. In this reading, Ishvara is identified with Vishnu or Narayana, and the entire yogic enterprise is understood as a means of deepening one's relationship with the personal God.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Bhagavata Yoga
The Bhagavata Purana's yoga teachings draw heavily on Patanjali's framework while redirecting all practice toward devotion to Krishna. The eight limbs become stages of purification that prepare the devotee for direct experience of the Lord.

### Ramanuja's Framework
Although Ramanuja did not write a commentary on the Yoga Sutras directly, his philosophical system incorporates Patanjali's categories within a devotional framework. Meditation (*dhyana*) is understood as sustained contemplation of Vishnu's divine attributes.

## Theological Emphases

- **Ishvara as Vishnu**: Sutra 1.24's definition of Ishvara as a special soul untouched by affliction is read as describing Vishnu specifically
- **Pranava as Divine Name**: The syllable Om (1.27-28) is understood as the sacred name of Narayana
- **Samadhi as Divine Vision**: The highest absorption is not impersonal but a direct experience of God's form
- **Grace**: While yogic effort is necessary, ultimate success depends on divine grace
