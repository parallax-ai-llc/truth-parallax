In the Vaishnava reading, Samadhi Pada establishes that the ultimate purpose of stilling the mind is to apprehend the Supreme Person. Patanjali's definition of yoga as *chitta vritti nirodha* (1.2) is understood not as an impersonal emptying of consciousness but as clearing away the mental obscurations that prevent direct vision of Vishnu. The mind becomes like a still lake that perfectly reflects the Lord's form.

The pivotal sutras on *Ishvara pranidhana* (1.23-29) are central to the Vaishnava interpretation. Ishvara is identified with Narayana, the special *purusha* untouched by karma and affliction. The *pranava* (Om) is His sacred name, and its repetition (*japa*) is not merely a concentration technique but an act of devotional remembrance that invites divine grace. For Vaishnavas, samadhi is ultimately a relational state — the soul resting in awareness of God.
