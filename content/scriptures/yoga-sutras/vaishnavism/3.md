The Vaishnava reading of Vibhuti Pada distinguishes carefully between supernormal powers (*siddhis*) and genuine spiritual attainment. While Patanjali catalogs extraordinary abilities arising from *samyama* — including knowledge of past lives, clairvoyance, and mastery over the elements — Vaishnava commentators stress that these powers are obstacles to devotion if pursued for their own sake. They are, at best, incidental signs of spiritual progress.

Patanjali's own warning that siddhis are "impediments to samadhi" (3.38) is read as confirming the Vaishnava principle that nothing should distract from loving service to God. The true *vibhuti* (glory) is not supernatural ability but the divine opulence of Vishnu Himself — as Krishna declares in Bhagavad Gita Chapter 10. The advanced practitioner, rather than reveling in powers, should surrender them at the Lord's feet, recognizing that all extraordinary capacity originates in God's own infinite potency.
