Vaishnavas read Sadhana Pada as outlining the practical disciplines through which the devotee purifies the heart for divine encounter. The *kriya yoga* triad — austerity (*tapas*), self-study (*svadhyaya*), and surrender to God (*Ishvara pranidhana*) — is interpreted as a devotional program. *Svadhyaya* means study of scripture, particularly texts that reveal Vishnu's nature, and *Ishvara pranidhana* is loving surrender to the Lord.

The five *kleshas* (2.3-9) — ignorance, egoism, attachment, aversion, and clinging to life — are understood as the root causes of the soul's forgetfulness of its eternal relationship with God. The eightfold path (*ashtanga yoga*) presented in this chapter provides the structure for disciplined devotional life. The ethical foundations of *yama* and *niyama* are read as expressions of dharma rooted in service to God, while the inner limbs prepare the devotee for the direct experience of the Lord described in Chapters 1 and 3.
