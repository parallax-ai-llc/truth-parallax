Vaishnavas reinterpret Patanjali's concept of *kaivalya* (isolation, liberation) through the lens of theistic devotion. While the classical Samkhya-Yoga reading understands kaivalya as the purusha's isolation from prakriti, the Vaishnava tradition reads this as the soul's liberation from material bondage into eternal loving relationship with God. Liberation is not solitary isolation but homecoming — the soul returning to its natural state of service to Vishnu.

The discussion of karma and its cessation (4.7-11) resonates with the Vaishnava teaching that accumulated karma is burned away by devotion and divine grace. Patanjali's final vision of *purusha* established in its own nature (4.34) is understood as the liberated soul resting in its eternal identity as a servant of God. True kaivalya is not separation from all relationship but freedom from all false relationships, enabling the one real relationship — with the Supreme — to shine forth.
