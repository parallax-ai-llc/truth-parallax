The Advaita perspective on Vibhuti Pada is one of qualified caution. While Patanjali's detailed catalog of supernormal powers (*siddhis*) is not denied, the Advaitin situates these abilities firmly within the realm of *maya* — the phenomenal world of appearances. Powers such as clairvoyance, levitation, and knowledge of past lives pertain to the mind-body complex, not to the Atman, which is ever free and beyond all attributes.

Shankara's tradition emphasizes that pursuit of siddhis represents a subtle form of worldly attachment (*vishaya raga*) that can actually impede liberation. The yogi who becomes fascinated with supernatural abilities has merely exchanged gross objects of desire for subtle ones. Patanjali himself acknowledges this danger (3.38), and the Advaita reading amplifies this warning: the only true *vibhuti* (power) worth pursuing is *atma jnana* — direct knowledge of the non-dual Self that instantly dissolves all ignorance and renders all phenomenal powers irrelevant.
