The Advaita reading of Samadhi Pada values Patanjali's analysis of mental states as a practical guide while reframing its metaphysical context. The definition of yoga as *chitta vritti nirodha* (1.2) is accepted as describing the stilling necessary for self-knowledge, but the Advaitin insists that what is revealed in that stillness is not an individual *purusha* but the non-dual Brahman — the one reality underlying all experience.

The distinction between *samprajnata* (cognitive) and *asamprajnata* (non-cognitive) samadhi maps, for the Advaitin, onto the distinction between preparatory meditation and the liberating insight of *nirvikalpa samadhi* — direct, unmediated awareness of the Self without any objective content. The sutras on *Ishvara* (1.24-26) are accepted at the *vyavaharika* (conventional) level, where God serves as a support for meditation, while at the *paramarthika* (ultimate) level, the distinction between meditator, meditation, and God dissolves.
