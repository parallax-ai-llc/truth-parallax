For Shaiva interpreters, Samadhi Pada describes the process by which individual consciousness recognizes its identity with Shiva, the universal consciousness. Patanjali's *chitta vritti nirodha* is not the suppression of awareness but the settling of mental agitation so that consciousness can rest in its own infinite nature. The *vrittis* are understood as contractions (*sankoca*) of Shiva's limitless awareness, and their cessation reveals the ever-present ground of pure consciousness.

The sutras on *Ishvara* (1.24-26) are read as describing Shiva — the supreme yogi, teacher of all teachers, untouched by time. The *pranava* (Om) is Shiva's primordial vibration (*nada*), and its contemplation leads to the *spanda* (creative pulsation) that is Shiva's dynamic self-expression. In the Kashmir Shaiva reading, the various types of samadhi cataloged in this chapter represent degrees of Shiva-recognition, culminating in the direct experience of oneself as Paramashiva.
