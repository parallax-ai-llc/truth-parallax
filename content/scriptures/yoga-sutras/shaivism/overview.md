## Overview

Shaiva tradition regards Shiva as the *Adiyogi* — the first yogi and originator of all yoga. The Yoga Sutras are read as describing practices that find their fullest expression within Shaiva systems, particularly Kashmir Shaivism and the Nath tradition. Patanjali's framework of mental cessation aligns with the Shaiva goal of recognizing one's identity with supreme consciousness (Paramashiva).

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Kashmir Shaivism
Abhinavagupta and his lineage view Patanjali's *nirodha* (cessation) not as suppression of consciousness but as the mind settling into its true nature as Shiva-consciousness. The *kleshas* (afflictions) are contractions (*malas*) of infinite awareness.

### Nath Tradition
The Nath yogis, beginning with Gorakshanath, developed Patanjali's physical and pranic practices into hatha yoga, understanding the body as a microcosm of Shiva-Shakti. Kundalini awakening is the experiential counterpart of *samadhi*.

## Theological Emphases

- **Shiva as Supreme Yogi**: Ishvara in the sutras is identified with Shiva, lord of yogis
- **Spanda (Vibration)**: The fluctuations of mind (*vrittis*) are vibrations of divine consciousness
- **Tantric Integration**: The Shaiva reading integrates Patanjali with tantric energy practices
- **Recognition over Cessation**: Liberation is not the cessation of awareness but its fullest recognition
