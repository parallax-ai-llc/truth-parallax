The Shaiva reading of Vibhuti Pada connects Patanjali's catalog of supernormal powers to the creative potency (*vimarsha shakti*) inherent in consciousness itself. Since the yogi's essential nature is Shiva — the source of all manifestation — the siddhis described in this chapter are natural expressions of consciousness recognizing its own creative power. They are not miracles but the normal capacities of unfettered awareness.

Kashmir Shaiva interpreters note that the *samyama* process (dharana + dhyana + samadhi applied to specific objects) parallels the tantric practice of *bhavana* — sustained creative contemplation that reveals the deeper reality within phenomena. However, Abhinavagupta's tradition emphasizes that attachment to siddhis creates a subtle form of *anava mala*, reinforcing the limited sense of individual agency. The mature yogi recognizes these powers as Shiva's play (*lila*) and does not claim personal ownership of them.
