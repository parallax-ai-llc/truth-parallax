The Shaiva interpretation of Kaivalya Pada diverges significantly from the classical Samkhya-Yoga reading. Rather than *kaivalya* meaning the isolation of *purusha* from *prakriti*, Shaiva commentators understand it as the full recognition of consciousness as the sole reality. Liberation is not withdrawal from the world but the recognition that the world is Shiva's self-expression and was never separate from pure consciousness.

Patanjali's discussion of the relationship between consciousness and its objects (4.15-24) receives a characteristically Shaiva reinterpretation: objects do not exist independently of consciousness but are its own manifestations, like waves in an ocean. The cessation of *gunas* described in the final sutras is not the collapse of the objective world but the dissolution of the illusion that the world stands apart from Shiva. The liberated yogi, established in *kaivalya*, experiences all phenomena as the luminous play of universal consciousness.
