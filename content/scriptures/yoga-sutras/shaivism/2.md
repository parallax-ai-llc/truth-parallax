Shaiva tradition reads the practical disciplines of Sadhana Pada as methods for removing the *malas* (impurities) that obscure the yogi's innate Shiva-nature. The three *malas* of Kashmir Shaivism — *anava mala* (sense of limitation), *mayiya mala* (sense of difference), and *karma mala* (sense of doership) — map onto Patanjali's *kleshas*. The root affliction of *avidya* (ignorance) is understood as the primal forgetting of one's identity as Shiva.

The eightfold path receives a distinctly Shaiva interpretation. *Asana* and *pranayama* are connected to the subtle body practices of kundalini yoga, where the awakening of Shakti through the central channel mirrors the ascent from gross to subtle awareness. The ethical observances (*yama* and *niyama*) are not mere social morality but expressions of one's growing recognition that all beings are manifestations of Shiva. *Ishvara pranidhana* is total absorption in Shiva-consciousness.
