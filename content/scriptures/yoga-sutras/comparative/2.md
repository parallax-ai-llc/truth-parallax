The eightfold path of Sadhana Pada invites direct comparison with the Buddhist Noble Eightfold Path and other systematic spiritual programs. Both traditions begin with ethical foundations (yama/sila), progress through mental discipline (dharana/samadhi), and culminate in transformative insight (samadhi/prajna). The structural parallels suggest either shared cultural origins in Indian shramanic traditions or independent convergence on similar contemplative principles.

The *kleshas* (afflictions) — particularly *avidya* (ignorance), *raga* (attachment), and *dvesha* (aversion) — have remarkably close parallels in the Buddhist *kilesas* and the Christian deadly sins tradition. Patanjali's analysis of how ignorance generates the entire chain of suffering anticipates the Buddhist *pratityasamutpada* (dependent origination). The Stoic concept of *pathos* (passion) as a disturbance of rational equanimity also resonates, suggesting a cross-cultural recognition that liberation requires confronting the root causes of psychological suffering.
