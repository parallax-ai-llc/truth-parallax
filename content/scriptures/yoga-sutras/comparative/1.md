Samadhi Pada's systematic analysis of meditative absorption invites comparison with contemplative taxonomies across world religions. The progressive stages of samadhi — from *savitarka* (with gross thought) through *nirvichara* (beyond subtle thought) to the seedless (*nirbija*) state — parallel the Buddhist jhana sequence, St. John of the Cross's stages of mystical ascent, and the Sufi *maqamat* (stations) described by al-Qushayri.

The concept of *Ishvara pranidhana* (surrender to God) as a path to samadhi creates a bridge between Patanjali's contemplative psychology and theistic mystical traditions. The practice of meditating on the divine through a sacred syllable (Om) has structural parallels in Christian *lectio divina*, the Sufi practice of *dhikr* (remembrance of God's names), and the Jewish mystical tradition of contemplating the divine names in Kabbalah. Each tradition recognizes that focused attention on the divine can catalyze states of absorption that transcend ordinary consciousness.
