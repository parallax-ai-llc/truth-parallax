The Dvaita reading of Sadhana Pada emphasizes that spiritual practice is the soul's active cooperation with divine grace. Patanjali's *kriya yoga* triad is interpreted as devotional discipline: *tapas* is austerity performed in service to God, *svadhyaya* is study of scriptures that reveal Vishnu's nature, and *Ishvara pranidhana* is wholehearted surrender to the Supreme.

The five *kleshas* reflect the soul's fundamental confusion about its dependent nature. *Avidya* (ignorance) is not merely misidentification with the body but the soul's forgetting that it exists entirely through and for God. The eightfold path provides the systematic means to overcome these afflictions, but the Dvaita tradition insists that no amount of practice succeeds without God's enabling grace. The soul's effort and God's grace work together — the soul cannot liberate itself, and God does not override the soul's free will.
