Dvaita Vedanta interprets *kaivalya* not as the soul's isolation in solitary existence but as its liberation into eternal, blissful relationship with God. The liberated soul is freed from material bondage and the cycle of rebirth, but it does not merge into or become God. Instead, it attains its own highest nature — one of knowledge, bliss, and eternal devotion — while remaining ontologically distinct from and dependent upon the Supreme.

Patanjali's analysis of karma cessation (4.7-11) supports the Dvaita teaching that accumulated karma is destroyed through a combination of God's grace and the soul's devoted practice. The final vision of *purusha* established in its own nature (4.34) is read as the soul resting in its true identity as an eternal servant of Vishnu, free from the distortions of material existence. This is not a diminished state but the soul's highest fulfillment — its innate capacity for loving service fully actualized in the presence of God.
