The Dvaita reading of Vibhuti Pada insists that all supernormal powers derive ultimately from God's sovereign will, not from the yogi's independent capacity. Whatever siddhis arise through *samyama* practice are granted by Vishnu and can be withdrawn at any time. This prevents the error of attributing divine powers to the finite soul, which would collapse the essential God-soul distinction that Dvaita rigorously maintains.

Madhva's followers distinguish between the genuine siddhis of liberated beings, which are permanent gifts of divine grace proportional to the soul's innate rank, and the temporary powers achieved through yogic technique, which remain within the sphere of *samsara*. Patanjali's warning that siddhis obstruct samadhi (3.38) is read as a reminder that the soul must not mistake the gifts for the Giver. The true glory (*vibhuti*) is God's own infinite majesty, of which all yogic powers are infinitesimal reflections.
