Dvaita Vedanta reads Samadhi Pada as confirming the eternal distinction between the individual soul and God. Patanjali's definition of Ishvara as a "special purusha" (*purusha vishesha*, 1.24) untouched by affliction, karma, and its results resonates powerfully with Madhva's teaching that God is independently real (*svatantra*) while all souls are eternally dependent (*paratantra*). The soul's yogic effort aims at drawing closer to God, never at becoming God.

The various levels of samadhi described in this chapter reflect, in the Dvaita reading, the hierarchical nature of spiritual attainment. Different souls, endowed with different innate capacities (*yogyata*), reach different depths of meditative absorption. The highest samadhi is not the dissolution of individual consciousness but its fullest flowering in direct, loving awareness of the Supreme Being's infinite attributes — His omniscience, omnipotence, and boundless compassion.
