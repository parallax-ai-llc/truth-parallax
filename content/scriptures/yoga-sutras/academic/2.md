Academic analysis of Sadhana Pada focuses on the relationship between Patanjali's practical system and the broader landscape of Indian ascetic and contemplative traditions. The *kriya yoga* of 2.1 (tapas, svadhyaya, Ishvara pranidhana) may represent an older, simpler formulation that Patanjali incorporated into his more elaborate eightfold system. Some scholars suggest the chapter contains layers of composition, with the eight limbs (2.29-55) possibly added to supplement the original *kriya yoga* framework.

The five *kleshas* (2.3-9) and the doctrine of *karma ashaya* (karmic repository) invite comparison with Buddhist concepts of *kilesa* and *alaya-vijnana*. Scholars debate the direction of influence, with some (e.g., Johannes Bronkhorst) arguing that yoga traditions influenced Buddhism, while others see Buddhist meditation theory feeding back into Brahmanical yoga. The eightfold structure itself has parallels in Buddhist, Jain, and other shramanic traditions, suggesting a shared contemplative culture in ancient India.
