Vibhuti Pada has attracted scholarly attention for its detailed enumeration of supernormal powers (*siddhis*), which some scholars regard as reflecting genuine contemplative traditions while others view as theoretical elaborations. The list of siddhis has close parallels in Buddhist texts (e.g., the *Visuddhimagga*'s *iddhis*) and Jain accounts of ascetic powers, suggesting a shared pan-Indian understanding of what advanced meditation could produce.

Scholars note a tension within the chapter between the systematic presentation of samyama as a reliable method for producing specific powers and Patanjali's own caveat that these powers impede samadhi (3.38). David Gordon White has argued that the siddhi tradition reflects an older "proto-tantric" layer in Indian religion, where the acquisition of power was itself the goal of practice. The academic consensus is that Patanjali incorporated this material while subordinating it to the soteriological goal of liberation.
