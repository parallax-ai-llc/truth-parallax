---
title: "To Nature (Physis)"
section: 10
---

O Nature, much-mechanic mother, great,
Whose ceaseless hand gives all their destined state,
Heavenly, abundant, venerable queen,
In every part of thy dominions seen.
Untamed, all-taming, ever-splendid light,
All-ruling, honored, and supremely bright.
Immortal, first-born, ever still the same,
Nocturnal, starry-girt, of matchless frame.
Thy feet's still traces in a circling course,
By thee the world, whose parts in swift commotion whirled,
Is ruled with never-wearied hand and curled.
All-parent Power, to thee each suppliant bends,
The universal queen whom all attends.
