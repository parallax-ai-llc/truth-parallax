---
title: "To Hecate"
section: 1
---

I call Hecate of the Crossroads, worshipped at the meeting of three paths,
O lovely one, of earthly, watery, and celestial frame,
Sepulchral, in a saffron veil arrayed,
Pleased with dark ghosts that wander through the shade;
Persian, unconquerable huntress hail!
The world's key-Loss you keep, and still decree
The fate and fortunes which attend on mortals.
O goddess, whether you dwell upon Olympus,
Or haunt the broad and boundless tracts of earth,
Or range through sacred Tartarus and dread,
Come to these sacred rites, with favouring mind.
O nurturer of youth, of lovely form, attend!
Bull-eater, queen of all, of manly form,
Hear me, O blessed one, and with kindly soul
Accept our sacred offering with grace.
