---
title: "To Prothyraea (Prothyraia)"
section: 2
---

O venerable goddess, hear my prayer,
For labour pains are thy peculiar care.
In thee, when stretched upon the bed of grief,
The sex, as in a mirror, views relief.
Guard of the race, endued with gentle mind,
To helpless youth benevolent and kind;
Benignant nourisher; great nature's key
Belongs to no divinity but thee.
Thou dwellest with all immanifest to sight,
And solemn festivals are thy delight.
Thine is the task to loose the virgin's zone,
And thou in every work art seen and known.
O Prothyraea, and target of respect,
Mild to thy votaries, with favour'd mind attend,
Propitious to these sacred rites incline,
Whether the heavens bright luminaries are thine,
Or whether to the light thy course is bent,
Celestial, much-named, from whom blessings are sent.
