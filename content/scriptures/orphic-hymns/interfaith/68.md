Orphic Hymn 68, addressed to Hygieia, offers entry points for interfaith reflection on the universal human practice of invoking the divine through prayer, hymn, and ritual. The hymn's celebration of Hygieia's attributes, its petition for blessing, and its use of incense offering connect to devotional practices found across the world's religious traditions.

From an interfaith perspective, the hymn illuminates shared patterns in how diverse religious traditions approach the divine: the use of multiple names and titles to express the complexity of divine nature, the offering of material gifts (incense) as a medium of communion, and the combination of praise and petition that characterizes prayer in virtually all traditions. The Orphic hymn thus serves as a bridge connecting ancient Greek spirituality with the living devotional practices of the world's religions.
