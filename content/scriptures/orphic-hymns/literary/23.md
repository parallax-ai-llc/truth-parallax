Orphic Hymn 23, addressed to Nereus, exemplifies the distinctive literary features of the Orphic hymnic collection: its hexameter verse, its rich accumulation of compound epithets, its compressed mythological allusions, and its consistent liturgical structure moving from invocation through celebration of attributes to closing petition. As a literary work, the hymn belongs to the long tradition of Greek hymnic poetry.

The hymn's literary artistry is evident in its careful selection and arrangement of epithets, each contributing to a cumulative portrait of Nereus's nature and powers. The movement from invocation to attributes to petition creates a rhetorical arc that is both aesthetically satisfying and ritually functional. The specification of a particular incense offering grounds the literary text in material religious practice, reminding us that these hymns were composed for performance, not merely for reading.
