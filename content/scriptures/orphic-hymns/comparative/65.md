Orphic Hymn 65, addressed to Ares, offers rich material for comparative religious analysis. The invocation of Ares in a liturgical context — celebrating the deity's attributes, recounting mythological associations, and petitioning for favor — connects to devotional practices across the world's religious traditions, from Vedic hymns to Islamic devotional poetry to Christian liturgical prayer.

The figure of Ares as presented in this hymn invites specific comparisons: the deity's cosmic functions and attributes resonate with analogous figures in other religious traditions, while the Orphic theological framework — with its emphasis on divine unity underlying multiplicity, the transmigration of souls, and the transformative power of ritual — connects to mystical and esoteric currents worldwide. The hymn's liturgical structure itself — invocation, praise, petition — represents a near-universal pattern in human religious expression.
