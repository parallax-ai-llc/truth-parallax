Orphic Hymn 17, addressed to Poseidon, has been the subject of significant scholarly analysis within the study of ancient Greek religion, Hellenistic theology, and the Orphic literary tradition. Academic engagement with this hymn examines its linguistic features, its theological content, its relationship to other Orphic texts, and its evidence for the cult practices of the community that produced the collection.

Scholars have examined the hymn's vocabulary and style in relation to other Greek hymnic literature, its theological content in the context of Orphic religious thought, and its prescribed incense offering as evidence for the ritual practices of the Orphic-Dionysian cult community. The treatment of Poseidon in this hymn contributes to our understanding of how the Orphic tradition received, transformed, and systematized traditional Greek religious concepts within its distinctive theological framework.
