Orphic Hymn 82, addressed to Notus, articulates Orphic theological convictions about the nature of the divine, the structure of the cosmos, and the proper relationship between worshipper and deity. The hymn's invocatory language, its accumulation of divine epithets, and its closing petition reveal a sophisticated theology of divine presence and liturgical efficacy.

The theological significance of this hymn lies in its presentation of Notus within the Orphic cosmological framework, where individual deities are understood as aspects or manifestations of a deeper divine unity. The Orphic hymns collectively articulate a theology in which proper ritual invocation — calling the god by the correct names and epithets, offering the prescribed incense, and making the appropriate petition — effects a real communion between the worshipper and the divine power invoked.
