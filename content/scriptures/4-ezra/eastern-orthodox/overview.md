## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox encounter with 4 Ezra comes primarily through Slavonic and Georgian translations. The text's vision of the heavenly city has resonated with Orthodox eschatological imagination, and its emphasis on the limits of rational theodicy aligns with the apophatic tradition. The patristic use of 4 Ezra by Clement of Alexandria and Ambrose attests to its early influence.
