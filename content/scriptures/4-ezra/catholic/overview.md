## Overview

The Catholic reception of 4 Ezra is shaped by its preservation in the Vulgate appendix as 2 Esdras. While not canonical, the text has been read by Catholic theologians as a witness to pre-Christian Jewish reflection on intermediate states, the fate of the soul after death, and the relationship between mercy and justice — themes that would later develop in Catholic eschatological theology.
