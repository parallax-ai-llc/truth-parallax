## Overview

The Comparative reading of 4 Ezra situates the text within a global matrix of theodicy traditions. The anguished questioning of divine justice finds parallels in the Babylonian Theodicy, the Book of Job, the Bhagavad Gita's reflection on dharmic action in the face of suffering, and Islamic kalām debates about God's justice. The eagle vision resonates with Daniel's four-kingdom scheme and Zoroastrian periodizations of world history.
