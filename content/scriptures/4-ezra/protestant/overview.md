## Overview

The Protestant engagement with 4 Ezra reflects the text's ambiguous canonical status. Luther included 2 Esdras in his Apocrypha section, and the text influenced Reformation-era eschatology, particularly through the eagle vision's anti-imperial imagery. Modern Protestant scholars value 4 Ezra for illuminating the theological crisis that shaped both rabbinic Judaism and early Christianity after the Temple's destruction.
