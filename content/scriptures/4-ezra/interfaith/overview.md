## Overview

The Interfaith reading of 4 Ezra draws on its power as a text that honestly confronts the problem of innocent suffering and the silence of God. Its dialogical structure — in which the human questioner is never fully satisfied by the angelic answers — provides common ground for Jewish, Christian, and Muslim reflection on theodicy. The text models a faith that includes protest and questioning.
