## Overview

The Gospel of the Egyptians presents a creation narrative that resonates with cosmogonic traditions across cultures while maintaining its distinctive Sethian character. The emergence of the divine world from a transcendent, invisible source parallels Hindu accounts of Brahman's self-manifestation, Buddhist cosmologies of world-formation, and Polynesian creation myths that begin with a primordial void. The Five Seals baptism connects to the universal practice of ritual purification as a means of spiritual transformation — from Jewish mikveh to Hindu sacred bathing to Christian baptism to Shinto misogi.
