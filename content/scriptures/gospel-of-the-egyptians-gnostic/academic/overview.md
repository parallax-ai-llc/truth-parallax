## Overview

The Gospel of the Egyptians is one of the most important texts for reconstructing Sethian Gnostic mythology and ritual practice. The existence of two Coptic copies (NHC III,2 and IV,2) that differ significantly from each other suggests a complex textual history and raises questions about the relationship between oral and written tradition in Gnostic communities. The Five Seals baptismal rite described in the text's climactic section is one of the few concrete pieces of evidence for Gnostic liturgical practice, allowing scholars to move beyond theological reconstruction to an understanding of how Gnostic communities actually worshipped.
