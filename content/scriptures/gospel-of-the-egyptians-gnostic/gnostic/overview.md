## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Gospel of the Egyptians (Holy Book of the Great Invisible Spirit) is one of the foundational texts of the Sethian tradition, providing the mythological basis for the Five Seals baptismal rite — the central sacrament by which the spiritual seed of Seth is sealed and restored to the divine realm. The text's elaborate cosmogony, tracing the emanation of the divine world from the Great Invisible Spirit through the divine triad and the four luminaries, maps the architecture of the Pleroma and provides the roadmap for the soul's return. The identification of Seth as both the ancestor of the spiritual race and the author of the sacred book claims the highest antiquity for Sethian revelation.
