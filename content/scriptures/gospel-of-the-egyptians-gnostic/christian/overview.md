## Overview

For Christian readers, the Gospel of the Egyptians raises important questions about the relationship between Gnostic and orthodox baptismal theology. Both traditions understood baptism as a transformative sacrament, but the Gnostic Five Seals rite operated within a fundamentally different cosmological framework. The text's identification of Seth — rather than Jesus — as the primary saving figure challenges the centrality of Christ in orthodox soteriology, while its complex theogony stands in sharp contrast to the relative simplicity of the biblical creation account.
