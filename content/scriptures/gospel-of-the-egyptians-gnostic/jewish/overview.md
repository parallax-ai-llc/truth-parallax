## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Gospel of the Egyptians a dramatic transformation of the Genesis figure Seth (Genesis 4:25-26) from the third son of Adam into a heavenly redeemer figure — the progenitor of a spiritual race and the agent of cosmic salvation. This transformation reflects the broader Gnostic tendency to elevate minor biblical figures into cosmic roles, often at the expense of figures central to Jewish tradition (Abraham, Moses, David). The text's baptismal theology, while foreign to rabbinic Judaism, may share roots with Jewish sectarian purification practices attested at Qumran.
