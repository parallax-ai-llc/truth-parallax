## Overview

The Gospel of the Egyptians (NHC III,2 and IV,2), more accurately titled the Holy Book of the Great Invisible Spirit, is a Sethian Gnostic text describing the origin of the divine world, the emanation of celestial beings from the Great Invisible Spirit, and the establishment of the Five Seals baptismal rite for the salvation of Seth's spiritual seed. The text survives in two Coptic copies that differ significantly, suggesting a complex textual history. Despite its conventional title, the text bears no relationship to the earlier, lost "Gospel of the Egyptians" known from patristic quotations. The colophon identifies the text as "the Holy Book of the Great Invisible Spirit" and attributes it to "the great Seth." It is generally dated to the late second or early third century CE and represents one of the most important witnesses to Sethian Gnostic mythology and ritual practice.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | The Great Invisible Spirit and the Triad | The highest God and the first emanations: the Father, the Mother (Barbelo), and the Son |
| 2 | The Emanation of the Celestial Beings | The generation of the divine aeons, the four luminaries (Harmozel, Oroiael, Daveithai, Eleleth), and the celestial Adam |
| 3 | Seth and the Seed of the Spirit | The role of the great Seth as protector of the spiritual race and the history of salvation |
| 4 | The Five Seals and Baptism | The baptismal rite of the Five Seals by which the spiritual seed is saved and restored to the divine realm |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: One of the foundational Sethian texts, providing the mythological basis for the Five Seals baptismal practice
- **Academic**: Critical for reconstructing Sethian Gnostic ritual, theology, and the history of Gnostic baptismal practice
- **Comparative**: Offers parallels to creation myths and ritual purification practices across religious traditions
- **Christian**: Challenges scholars to consider the relationship between Gnostic and orthodox baptismal theology
- **Jewish**: Transforms the Genesis figure of Seth into a heavenly redeemer and the ancestor of a spiritual race
- **Interfaith**: Addresses universal themes of divine origin, cosmic conflict, and ritual restoration to a primordial state of purity

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Great Invisible Spirit | The highest God is invisible, unknowable, and the ultimate source of all divine emanation |
| Sethian Cosmogony | The origin of the divine world through a series of emanations from the Great Invisible Spirit |
| Five Seals Baptism | The central ritual by which the spiritual seed of Seth is sealed and saved |
| Heavenly Seth | Seth is not merely Adam's son but a divine figure who descends to protect and redeem the spiritual race |
