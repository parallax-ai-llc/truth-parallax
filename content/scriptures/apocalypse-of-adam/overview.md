## Overview

The Apocalypse of Adam (NHC V,5) is a Sethian Gnostic text in which Adam, on his deathbed, reveals to his son Seth the hidden history of the spiritual race, the repeated attempts by the god of the material world to destroy the elect, and the coming of the Illuminator who will bring final salvation. The text is structured as a testament — a genre well attested in Jewish literature (Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs, Testament of Moses) — and its most distinctive feature is a passage describing thirteen kingdoms, each offering a different account of the Illuminator's origin. Notably, the text contains no explicitly Christian elements, leading some scholars (George MacRae, Charles Hedrick) to argue that it represents a pre-Christian or non-Christian form of Sethian Gnosticism.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Adam's Revelation to Seth | Adam recounts the primordial history: his creation, his original glory with Eve, their fall, and the loss of divine knowledge |
| 2 | The History of the Spiritual Race | The persecution of Seth's spiritual descendants by the creator god through the flood and fire |
| 3 | The Thirteen Kingdoms | Thirteen kingdoms each give a different account of the origin of the Illuminator |
| 4 | The Coming of the Illuminator | The "generation without a king" gives the true account of the Illuminator and his mission |
| 5 | The Final Salvation | The ultimate salvation of Seth's seed through baptism and knowledge |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: Read as a foundational Sethian salvation history tracing the spiritual race from Adam through the Illuminator
- **Academic**: Central to debates about the existence of pre-Christian Gnosticism and the Jewish roots of Sethian thought
- **Comparative**: The thirteen kingdoms passage offers parallels to traditions of multiple competing explanations found in other religious literatures
- **Christian**: Raises the question of whether Gnostic redeemer myths existed independently of Christianity or were derived from it
- **Jewish**: Transforms the Jewish testament genre and Genesis figures into vehicles for Gnostic revelation
- **Interfaith**: Addresses universal themes of primordial paradise, fall, persecution, and eschatological deliverance

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Adamic Revelation | Adam transmits hidden knowledge to Seth as a deathbed testament |
| The Illuminator | A messianic figure who comes to save the spiritual seed of Seth |
| Thirteen Kingdoms | Competing accounts of the Illuminator's origin from worldly kingdoms, versus the true "kingless generation" |
| Spiritual Persecution | The creator god repeatedly attempts to destroy the spiritual race through flood and fire |
