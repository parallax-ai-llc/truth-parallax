## Overview

The Apocalypse of Adam is one of the most debated texts in Nag Hammadi scholarship, primarily because of the question of whether it represents pre-Christian or non-Christian Gnosticism. The text contains no explicit references to Christ, the cross, or any recognizably Christian concepts, leading scholars such as George MacRae and Charles Hedrick to argue that it preserves a form of Gnostic thought that predates or exists independently of Christianity. The testament genre (a dying patriarch revealing hidden knowledge to his son) is well attested in Jewish literature, and the thirteen kingdoms passage has been compared to ancient near eastern and Jewish traditions of competing origin accounts.
