## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Apocalypse of Adam is a foundational salvation history that traces the spiritual race from its origins in Adam through successive crises to its ultimate deliverance by the Illuminator. Adam's deathbed revelation to Seth establishes the antiquity of Sethian gnosis — this knowledge was not invented but recovered, transmitted from the very beginning of human history. The thirteen kingdoms passage, in which worldly powers offer false accounts of the Illuminator's origin, dramatizes the Gnostic conviction that the truth about salvation is consistently obscured by the rulers of the material world, and that only the 'generation without a king' possesses the authentic teaching.
