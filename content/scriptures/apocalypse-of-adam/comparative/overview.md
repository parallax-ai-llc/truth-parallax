## Overview

The Apocalypse of Adam presents a salvation history that resonates with apocalyptic and messianic traditions across cultures. The pattern of a primordial revelation, repeated persecution, multiple false accounts of the savior's origin, and final deliverance parallels narratives found in Zoroastrianism (the successive ages and the coming Saoshyant), Hindu cyclical cosmology (the yugas and the coming of Kalki), and Buddhist eschatology (the coming of Maitreya). The thirteen kingdoms passage — offering competing accounts of the Illuminator — uniquely dramatizes the epistemological challenge of distinguishing true from false knowledge about salvation.
