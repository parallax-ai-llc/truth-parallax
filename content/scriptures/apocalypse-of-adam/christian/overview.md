## Overview

For Christian readers, the Apocalypse of Adam raises the provocative question of whether Gnostic redeemer myths existed before Christianity. If the Illuminator figure in this text is not derived from Jesus Christ, then the Gnostic redeemer concept may represent an independent religious development that Christianity later intersected with — rather than the reverse. This has implications for understanding the relationship between Gnostic and Christian Christology and for the broader question of religious originality and borrowing.
