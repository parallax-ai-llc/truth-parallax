## Guardians of the Demiurge's Domain

Gnostic interpretation reads the gatekeepers and treasurers as archontic functionaries — guardians who control access to the sacred and managers who accumulate material wealth in the Demiurge's name. These roles mirror the cosmic gatekeepers that the soul must pass on its ascent through the archontic spheres.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### Gatekeepers as Cosmic Guardians (26:1-19)

The temple gatekeepers, stationed at every entrance, mirror the archons who guard the gates between cosmic spheres. Gnostic ascent narratives describe the soul passing through guarded gates, requiring passwords or secret knowledge to proceed. The material gatekeepers are earthly reflections of cosmic reality.

### Treasuries as Material Accumulation (26:20-28)

The Demiurge accumulates material wealth — gold, silver, dedicated war spoils — in his temple. This accumulation reflects the archontic hoarding of cosmic energy, keeping resources within the material system rather than releasing them toward the Pleroma.

### Sacred-Civil Integration (26:29-32)

Levites serving as judges and administrators extend the Demiurge's control from the Temple into civil life. The comprehensive reach of archontic authority — governing both sacred and secular spheres — leaves no space free from the Demiurge's governance.

## Theological Significance

1. **Cosmic gatekeeping**: Temple guards mirror the archons at the cosmic gates
2. **Material hoarding**: The Demiurge accumulates wealth rather than releasing it
3. **Comprehensive control**: Sacred-civil integration extends archontic authority everywhere
4. **Passwords and gnosis**: Passing the gates requires knowledge the guardians cannot provide
