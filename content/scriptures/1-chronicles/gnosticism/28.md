## The Spirit-Given Plans and Solomon's Secret Knowledge

Gnostic interpretation reads David's charge to Solomon as the transmission of esoteric knowledge — the Spirit-given temple plans representing sacred architecture that, in Gnostic tradition, encodes cosmic secrets. The *Testament of Solomon* develops this theme extensively.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### Spirit-Given Plans: Whose Spirit? (28:11-19)

David receives temple plans from the Spirit. The critical Gnostic question is: which Spirit? If the plans come from the Demiurge's spirit, they create a material prison for the divine. If they originate from Sophia — the divine Wisdom who transcends the Demiurge — they may encode truths about the Pleroma within material architecture.

### Solomon's Gnosis (28:6-7)

The Gnostic tradition surrounding Solomon — the *Testament of Solomon*, the *Odes of Solomon*, the *Wisdom of Solomon* — presents him as a master of hidden knowledge. His power over demons (archons), his understanding of cosmic structures, and his possession of a magical ring all suggest that Solomon's wisdom transcends the Demiurge's intentions.

### Wholehearted Service (28:9)

"Serve him with wholehearted devotion and with a willing mind." Gnostic interpreters read this charge with irony: wholehearted service to the Demiurge is the deepest form of entrapment. Yet if the 'him' served is unknowingly the true God beyond the Demiurge, then the charge points beyond itself.

## Theological Significance

1. **Ambiguous Spirit**: The source of the temple plans determines their meaning
2. **Solomon's esoteric wisdom**: The Gnostic Solomon transcends his father's material piety
3. **Architecture as code**: The temple may encode cosmic secrets within its material structure
4. **Ironic devotion**: Wholehearted service to the Demiurge may inadvertently reach the true God
