## Preparing the Demiurge's Dwelling

Gnostic interpretation reads David's temple preparations as the construction of the Demiurge's earthly seat of power. The vast accumulation of gold, silver, and precious materials for a material building reveals the Demiurge's investment in physical structures — a god who needs a house, unlike the transcendent God who dwells beyond all creation.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### A God Who Needs a House (22:1-5)

The Demiurge's desire for a temple reveals his material nature. The true God of the Pleroma does not dwell in buildings made with hands — a principle even the biblical tradition occasionally acknowledges (1 Kings 8:27, Acts 7:48). The Demiurge's need for a dwelling place exposes his limitation.

### Solomon and Secret Knowledge (22:6-13)

The Gnostic tradition surrounding Solomon — reflected in the *Testament of Solomon* — attributes to him esoteric knowledge of cosmic powers. David's charge to Solomon may be read as the transmission of sacred knowledge that transcends the Demiurge's material purposes.

### War vs. Peace (22:8-10)

The distinction between David (blood) and Solomon (peace) resonates with the Gnostic preference for knowledge over violence. Solomon's association with wisdom (*chokmah*) connects him to Sophia — suggesting that the Temple's true purpose may exceed the Demiurge's intentions.

## Theological Significance

1. **Material god**: A deity who needs a house reveals his material limitation
2. **Solomon's gnosis**: The Gnostic Solomon possesses knowledge beyond the Demiurge's purposes
3. **Sophia connection**: Solomon's wisdom may connect him to the divine Sophia
4. **Transcendence**: The true God cannot be contained in material structures
