## Military Expansion as Archontic Domination

Gnostic interpretation reads David's military campaigns as the expansion of the Demiurge's domain through violence. The refrain "the LORD gave David victory" reveals the Demiurge as a war god who extends his territory through conquest and bloodshed.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### The War God (18:6, 13)

The Demiurge grants military victory — acting as patron deity of David's armies. Gnostic theology views war gods with deep suspicion: the true God of the Pleroma does not extend his realm through violence but through the quiet spread of gnosis.

### Justice as Control (18:14)

David's administration of "justice and righteousness" represents the Demiurge's moral order — a system of rules that maintains material stability but does not liberate the soul. Gnostic ethics questions whether the Demiurge's justice is truly just or merely expedient.

### Dedicated Spoils (18:11)

Consecrating war spoils to God completes the cycle of violence and worship that characterizes the Demiurge's system: kill in God's name, then offer the plunder to God. The true God neither commands violence nor accepts its proceeds.

## Theological Significance

1. **War and the Demiurge**: The cosmic ruler extends his domain through violence
2. **Justice as order**: Material justice maintains stability but does not liberate
3. **The violence-worship cycle**: War and worship are intertwined in the Demiurge's system
4. **True divinity and peace**: The Pleroma's God does not operate through conquest
