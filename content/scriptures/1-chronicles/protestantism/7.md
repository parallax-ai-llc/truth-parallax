## Genealogies of the Northern Tribes

Chapter 7 provides genealogies for six tribes: Issachar, Benjamin, Naphtali, Manasseh (west), Ephraim, and Asher. The lists vary in length and detail, reflecting the Chronicler's available sources and theological priorities.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Issachar's Warriors (7:1-5)

Issachar's genealogy emphasizes military readiness — 87,000 mighty warriors. Protestant interpreters note the Chronicler's interest in tribes that contributed to Israel's collective strength and security.

### Ephraim's Tragedy and Recovery (7:20-29)

The genealogy includes a narrative of Ephraim mourning sons killed by the men of Gath, followed by the birth of a new son and the rebuilding of towns. **Selman** reads this as a pattern of loss and restoration that typifies Israel's experience.

### The Absence of Dan and Zebulun

The omission of Dan and the minimal treatment of other tribes has generated much scholarly discussion. Some Protestant commentators suggest Dan's association with idolatry (Judges 18) led to its exclusion, while others attribute the gaps to manuscript damage.

## Theological Significance

1. **All Israel matters**: Even northern tribes are included in God's covenant purposes
2. **Loss and restoration**: Ephraim's narrative models the pattern of grief and renewal
3. **Selective theology**: The Chronicler's choices reveal theological priorities, not mere record-keeping
4. **Military readiness**: Faithfulness includes readiness to defend God's people
