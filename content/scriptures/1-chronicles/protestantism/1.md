## The Nations from Adam

1 Chronicles opens with a sweeping genealogy from Adam through Noah's sons to Abraham and his descendants, concluding with the lineage of Esau and the kings of Edom. Protestant interpreters read this universal scope as establishing that God's redemptive plan encompasses all humanity before narrowing to Israel.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### From Adam to Noah (1:1-4)

The genealogy begins with bare names — Adam, Seth, Enosh — echoing Genesis 5. Protestant scholars note the Chronicler's assumption that readers know the stories behind these names. The genealogy functions as a theological shorthand: God has been at work since the very beginning of human history.

### The Table of Nations (1:5-23)

Drawing on Genesis 10, the Chronicler maps the spread of humanity through Japheth, Ham, and Shem. Reformed interpreters see here a reminder that God is sovereign over all nations, not Israel alone.

### Abraham's Line and Esau's Descendants (1:24-54)

The chapter narrows from universal history to Abraham, then distinguishes between Isaac and Ishmael, Jacob and Esau. **Calvin** observed that God's electing purpose runs through the entire genealogy — choosing Isaac over Ishmael, Jacob over Esau — demonstrating sovereign grace.

## Theological Significance

1. **Universal scope**: God's purposes begin with all humanity before focusing on Israel
2. **Election**: The genealogy traces God's sovereign choosing through each generation
3. **Covenant continuity**: From Adam to Abraham, a single thread of divine purpose
4. **Historical rootedness**: The faith is grounded in real history, not myth
