## The Transjordan Tribes

Chapter 5 records the genealogies of the two-and-a-half tribes settled east of the Jordan — Reuben, Gad, and the half-tribe of Manasseh. The chapter concludes with a theological explanation of their exile: unfaithfulness to the God of their fathers.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Reuben's Lost Birthright (5:1-2)

Reuben forfeited the birthright through defiling his father's bed (Genesis 35:22). The birthright passed to Joseph's sons, but the ruler's line went to Judah. Protestant theology sees here the principle that privilege does not guarantee blessing — obedience does.

### Victory through Prayer (5:18-22)

The Transjordan tribes defeat the Hagrites because 'they cried out to God during the battle, and he answered their prayers because they trusted in him.' This is a paradigmatic text for Protestant understanding of prayer in spiritual warfare.

### The Exile Explained (5:25-26)

The chapter ends with theological causation: the tribes 'were unfaithful to the God of their ancestors,' so God stirred up the Assyrian kings to carry them into exile. The Chronicler's retribution theology is clear and unambiguous.

## Theological Significance

1. **Obedience over privilege**: Reuben's birthright loss teaches that status requires faithfulness
2. **Prayer in battle**: Military victory comes through trust in God, not human strength alone
3. **Retribution**: Unfaithfulness leads directly to exile — a warning for every generation
4. **Divine sovereignty**: God uses even pagan empires as instruments of judgment
