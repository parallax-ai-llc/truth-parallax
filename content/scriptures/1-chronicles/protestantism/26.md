## Guardians of the Sacred Space

Chapter 26 organizes the gatekeepers who guarded the Temple entrances and the treasurers who managed its resources. These roles, often overlooked, receive the Chronicler's careful attention as essential dimensions of faithful worship.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Gatekeepers' Assignments (26:1-19)

Gatekeepers are assigned to specific gates — north, south, east, and west — by lot. The roster includes descendants of Obed-Edom, whose household was blessed when the Ark rested with him (13:14). **Selman** observes that Obed-Edom's family has been drawn permanently into temple service through their faithful hosting of the Ark.

### The Temple Treasurers (26:20-28)

The treasurers manage both the 'treasuries of the house of God' and the 'treasuries for the dedicated things' — war spoils and voluntary offerings consecrated to the Temple. Protestant interpreters see here a model of faithful financial stewardship in the church.

### External Administration (26:29-32)

Some Levites serve as officials and judges 'in all the work of the LORD and in the service of the king' — extending sacred service beyond the Temple into civil administration. This integration of worship and governance reflects the Chronicler's vision of a society ordered under God.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sacred stewardship**: Managing God's resources is an act of worship, not mere administration
2. **Access and protection**: Gatekeepers model the balance between welcome and reverence
3. **Faithful legacy**: Obed-Edom's blessing leads to generational service
4. **Integration of sacred and civic**: God's order encompasses all of life, not just temple rituals
