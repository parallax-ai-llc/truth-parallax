## Supplementary Genealogies and the Prayer of Jabez

Chapter 4 continues the genealogy of Judah with supplementary lists, including the famous prayer of Jabez, and concludes with the genealogy of Simeon. The interweaving of genealogy with narrative asides reveals the Chronicler's theological purposes.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Prayer of Jabez (4:9-10)

Jabez — whose name means 'pain' — prays for divine blessing, enlarged territory, God's hand upon him, and protection from evil. God grants his request. Protestant interpreters have long treasured this passage as a model of bold, faith-filled prayer.

**Calvin** noted that Jabez's prayer demonstrates that blessing comes not from noble birth but from humble dependence on God. The brief narrative breaks the genealogical pattern to show that individual faith matters within the larger story.

### Judah's Clans (4:1-23)

The supplementary Judah genealogies include craftsmen, potters, and linen workers — ordinary people whose trades served the community. Protestant theology values the dignity of common labor as divine calling.

### Simeon's Genealogy (4:24-43)

Simeon's territory was absorbed into Judah, and the tribe's genealogy is brief. The note about Simeonites conquering Amalekite territory (4:42-43) demonstrates continued faithfulness to the conquest mandate.

## Theological Significance

1. **Prayer changes outcomes**: Jabez's prayer demonstrates individual agency within God's sovereign plan
2. **Dignity of vocation**: Craftsmen and workers are recorded alongside warriors and rulers
3. **Faith over birth**: Jabez's painful origins are overcome by faith
4. **Covenant faithfulness**: Even small tribes contribute to God's purposes
