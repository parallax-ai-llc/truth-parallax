## Worship Established Before the Ark

Chapter 16 records the celebration after the Ark's arrival in Jerusalem, including David's appointment of Levitical worship leaders and a composite psalm of praise drawn from Psalms 105, 96, and 106. This chapter represents the Chronicler's vision of ideal worship.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Offerings and Blessing (16:1-3)

David offers burnt offerings and fellowship offerings, then blesses the people and distributes food to every Israelite. Protestant interpreters see David exercising a priestly function that points forward to Christ, who is both King and Priest.

### The Psalm of Thanksgiving (16:8-36)

This composite psalm combines elements of three canonical psalms, creating a worship text that emphasizes God's covenant faithfulness, His deeds among the nations, and His sovereignty over creation. **Selman** identifies this as the Chronicler's model prayer: remembrance of God's acts, proclamation among the nations, and submission to His reign.

### Levitical Worship Orders Established (16:37-42)

David appoints Asaph and his associates to minister regularly before the Ark, while Zadok and his priestly brothers serve at the tabernacle in Gibeon. This dual arrangement continues until Solomon's Temple unites both centers of worship.

## Theological Significance

1. **Worship as response**: The psalm responds to God's covenant faithfulness with praise
2. **Proclamation**: Worship includes declaring God's deeds among the nations
3. **Ordered ministry**: Regular, appointed worship reflects God's desire for sustained devotion
4. **Covenant memory**: The psalm rehearses God's promises as the foundation of praise
