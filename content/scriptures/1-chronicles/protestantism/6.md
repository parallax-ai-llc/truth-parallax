## The Priestly Tribe and Its Service

Chapter 6, the longest genealogy in Chronicles, is devoted entirely to the tribe of Levi — priests, musicians, and temple servants. This chapter is the Chronicler's theological center of gravity for the genealogical section, reflecting the supreme importance of worship in Israel's life.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The High Priestly Line (6:1-15)

The genealogy traces the high priests from Aaron through Zadok to the exile. Protestant interpreters see this unbroken line as evidence of God's faithfulness to His covenant, even as it points beyond itself to Christ, the ultimate High Priest (Hebrews 7).

### The Temple Musicians (6:31-48)

David appointed Heman, Asaph, and Ethan (Jeduthun) as chief musicians — one from each of the three Levitical clans. Protestant worship theology draws on this text to affirm the centrality of music and song in congregational worship.

### Levitical Cities (6:54-81)

The forty-eight Levitical cities, including six cities of refuge, distributed the priestly presence throughout the land. **Matthew Henry** saw this as God's provision for spiritual instruction in every region — a pattern the church continues through local congregations.

## Theological Significance

1. **Worship as central**: The longest genealogy belongs to the worship leaders, not the kings
2. **Christ as High Priest**: The Aaronic line points to its fulfillment in Christ
3. **Music in worship**: The Chronicler's emphasis validates congregational singing and praise
4. **Distributed ministry**: Levitical cities model the spread of spiritual care throughout the land
