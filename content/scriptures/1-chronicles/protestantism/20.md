## The Conclusion of David's Wars

Chapter 20 concludes the Ammonite war with the capture of Rabbah and records three encounters with Philistine giants. Notably, the Chronicler omits the Bathsheba-Uriah episode that occupies 2 Samuel 11-12, passing directly from the spring campaign to the fall of Rabbah.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Capture of Rabbah (20:1-3)

Joab leads the army while David remains in Jerusalem. The Chronicler omits the entire Bathsheba narrative, moving directly to the city's capture and David's taking the Ammonite crown. Protestant scholars debate whether this omission reflects theological selectivity (focusing on David's public, covenantal role) or the Chronicler's assumption that readers know the Samuel account.

### Philistine Giants Defeated (20:4-8)

Three encounters with Philistine warriors of great stature — including a man with six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot — demonstrate God's continued protection. **Selman** notes that these victories echo David's own defeat of Goliath, showing that the spirit of giant-killing faith extends to David's warriors.

## Theological Significance

1. **Selective narrative**: The Chronicler focuses on David's public, covenantal significance
2. **Ongoing victory**: God continues to defeat Israel's enemies through David's warriors
3. **Community faith**: David's courage becomes a pattern for his followers
4. **Theological purpose**: The omission of Bathsheba is not denial but refocusing
