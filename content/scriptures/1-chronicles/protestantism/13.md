## The Ark's Perilous Journey

Chapter 13 begins the Ark narrative — the Chronicler's theological centerpiece. David's first attempt to bring the Ark to Jerusalem ends in tragedy when Uzzah touches the Ark and is struck dead. The chapter teaches that right intentions do not excuse wrong methods in approaching a holy God.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David Consults the Assembly (13:1-4)

David proposes bringing the Ark to Jerusalem by consulting 'the whole assembly of Israel.' Protestant interpreters note both the commendable impulse (restoring the Ark to centrality) and the procedural failure (using a cart instead of Levitical bearers as prescribed in Numbers 4).

### The Death of Uzzah (13:9-12)

When the oxen stumble and Uzzah reaches out to steady the Ark, God strikes him dead. David responds with anger and then fear. **Calvin** taught that this narrative demonstrates God's holiness cannot be domesticated by human good intentions — obedience to God's prescribed means of worship is essential.

### The Ark at Obed-Edom's House (13:13-14)

The Ark remains with Obed-Edom the Gittite for three months, and God blesses his household. The blessing demonstrates that the Ark is not inherently dangerous but requires proper reverence.

## Theological Significance

1. **Holiness demands reverence**: Good intentions do not excuse violation of God's commands
2. **Right worship, right way**: The regulative principle — worship must follow God's prescriptions
3. **Fear of God**: David's fear is the appropriate response to divine holiness
4. **Blessing through obedience**: Obed-Edom is blessed through proper relationship to the Ark
