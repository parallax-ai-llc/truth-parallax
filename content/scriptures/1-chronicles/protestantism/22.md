## David Prepares for the Temple

Chapter 22 begins the Chronicler's unique material on David's temple preparations — absent from Samuel-Kings. David gathers vast materials, charges Solomon to build the Temple, and explains why he himself cannot build it: he is a man of war, but Solomon will be a man of peace.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Explanation (22:7-10)

'I had it in my heart to build a house for the Name of the LORD my God. But this word of the LORD came to me: You have shed much blood and have fought many wars. You are not to build a house for my Name... A son will be born to you who will be a man of rest... He is the one who will build a house for my Name.' The contrast between David (war) and Solomon (peace/shalom) is central to Protestant understanding of the temple theology.

### The Charge to Solomon (22:11-16)

David charges Solomon with courage and obedience: 'Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid or discouraged.' The language echoes Joshua's commissioning (Joshua 1:6-9), drawing a typological parallel between entering the Promised Land and building the Temple.

### The Charge to Israel's Leaders (22:17-19)

David commands Israel's leaders to help Solomon, declaring: 'Is not the LORD your God with you? And has he not granted you rest on every side?' Protestant interpreters note that temple-building requires communal effort and communal peace.

## Theological Significance

1. **War and peace**: The Temple requires a man of peace, pointing to Christ the Prince of Peace
2. **Generational partnership**: David prepares what Solomon will build — faithfulness spans generations
3. **Courage in calling**: Solomon's charge echoes Joshua's, linking conquest and worship
4. **Community responsibility**: The Temple is the work of all God's people, not one leader alone
