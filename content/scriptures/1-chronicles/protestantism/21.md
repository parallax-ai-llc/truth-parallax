## The Census, the Plague, and the Temple Site

Chapter 21 records David's sinful census, the resulting plague, and his purchase of Araunah's threshing floor — the future site of the Temple. The Chronicler attributes the incitement to Satan (rather than God's anger as in 2 Samuel 24), reflecting a developing theology of evil agency.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Satan Incites the Census (21:1)

'Satan rose up against Israel and incited David to take a census.' The shift from 2 Samuel 24:1 ('the anger of the LORD') to Satan as instigator represents a significant theological development. Protestant scholars see this not as contradiction but as complementary perspectives — God's sovereign permission and Satan's malicious agency.

### Joab's Objection (21:3-4)

Joab protests the census as sinful, but David overrides him. **Calvin** noted the irony: the military commander shows more spiritual discernment than the king. Pride in military numbers replaces trust in God.

### The Plague and David's Repentance (21:7-17)

God sends a plague that kills 70,000 Israelites. David sees the destroying angel over Jerusalem and confesses: 'I am the one who sinned and done wrong. These are but sheep. What have they done? Let your hand fall on me and my family.' Protestant theology treasures this as a model of intercessory repentance.

### The Threshing Floor Purchase (21:18-30)

David buys the threshing floor and builds an altar. Fire from heaven consumes the offering, confirming God's acceptance. This site becomes the Temple mount — the Chronicler reveals that the Temple's location was born from judgment, repentance, and grace.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sin and consequence**: Even the faithful are not immune to pride and its devastating effects
2. **Intercessory repentance**: David stands between his people and God's judgment
3. **Grace in judgment**: The Temple site emerges from a moment of divine wrath and human repentance
4. **Satan and sovereignty**: Evil agency operates within God's sovereign purposes
