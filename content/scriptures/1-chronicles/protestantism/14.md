## Divine Confirmation of David's Reign

Chapter 14 recounts three signs of God's blessing on David: Hiram of Tyre builds David a palace, David's family grows, and David defeats the Philistines twice. The Chronicler places these confirmations between the two Ark narratives (chapters 13 and 15) to show that God's blessing accompanies faithful kingship.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Hiram's Embassy (14:1-2)

The king of Tyre sends materials and craftsmen for David's palace. David perceives that 'the LORD had established him as king over Israel and that his kingdom had been highly exalted for the sake of his people Israel.' Protestant interpreters emphasize the phrase 'for the sake of his people' — David's exaltation serves God's purposes for the community, not David's personal glory.

### Victory over the Philistines (14:8-17)

David inquires of God before each battle — in contrast to Saul, who failed to inquire (10:14). At Baal-perazim, God 'breaks out' against the enemy. In the second battle, God gives specific tactical instructions. **Selman** notes the Chronicler's emphasis on divine guidance: David's military success flows from spiritual obedience.

### David's Fame (14:17)

'So David's fame spread throughout every land, and the LORD made all the nations fear him.' The Chronicler presents David's international reputation as God's doing, not David's achievement.

## Theological Significance

1. **Inquiring of God**: David's practice of seeking divine guidance contrasts with Saul's failure
2. **Blessing for others**: David's exaltation serves God's people, not personal ambition
3. **Divine victory**: Military success belongs to God, who fights for His people
4. **International witness**: God's blessing on David makes the nations take notice
