## The Ark Arrives in Jerusalem — The Right Way

Chapter 15 describes David's successful second attempt to bring the Ark to Jerusalem, this time following the Levitical prescriptions. The contrast with chapter 13 is deliberate: obedience to God's ordained order transforms disaster into celebration.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Correction (15:1-2)

David prepares a tent for the Ark and declares: 'No one but the Levites may carry the ark of God, because the LORD chose them.' Protestant interpreters see David's learning from Uzzah's death as a model of repentance and reform — recognizing error and returning to Scripture's prescriptions.

### The Levitical Procession (15:3-15)

David assembles the Levites, who consecrate themselves and carry the Ark on poles as Moses had commanded. The Chronicler adds detail absent from 2 Samuel, emphasizing that 'the Levites carried the ark of God with the poles on their shoulders, as Moses had commanded according to the word of the LORD' (15:15).

### Music and Celebration (15:16-28)

David appoints Levitical musicians — Heman, Asaph, and Ethan — with cymbals, lyres, and harps. The procession becomes a worship service. **Calvin** noted that the Chronicler's extensive attention to musical arrangements demonstrates that worship is not incidental but central to the life of God's people.

### Michal's Contempt (15:29)

Michal despises David's exuberant dancing before the Ark. Protestant interpreters read her contempt as a warning against spiritual pride that disdains wholehearted worship.

## Theological Significance

1. **The regulative principle**: Worship must follow God's prescribed order
2. **Repentance and reform**: David learns from failure and obeys God's word
3. **Music in worship**: Levitical musicians model the centrality of praise
4. **Wholehearted worship**: David's dancing embodies unreserved devotion to God
