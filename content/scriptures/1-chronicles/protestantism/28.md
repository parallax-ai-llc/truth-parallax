## The Temple Plans and the Royal Commission

Chapter 28 records David's public charge to Solomon and all Israel, delivering the Spirit-inspired temple plans. This chapter parallels Moses' commissioning of Joshua, presenting Solomon's temple-building as a second entry into God's promised rest.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David Addresses the Assembly (28:1-8)

David acknowledges publicly that God chose him as king and chose Solomon to build the Temple. He charges Solomon and all Israel to observe God's commands so that they may possess the land and pass it on. **Hill** notes the Chronicler's concern for future generations — obedience today shapes the inheritance of tomorrow.

### The Charge to Solomon (28:9-10)

'And you, my son Solomon, acknowledge the God of your father, and serve him with wholehearted devotion and with a willing mind, for the LORD searches every heart and understands every desire and every thought.' Protestant interpreters treasure this verse as a summary of true religion: knowing God, serving wholeheartedly, and recognizing divine omniscience.

### The Spirit-Given Temple Plans (28:11-19)

David gives Solomon detailed plans for the Temple 'that the Spirit had put in his mind' — for the portico, the buildings, the treasuries, the priestly courses, and every article of service. The comparison to Moses receiving the tabernacle pattern on Sinai (Exodus 25:9) is unmistakable. **Calvin** saw this as evidence that true worship is prescribed by God, not invented by humans.

### The Final Encouragement (28:20-21)

'Be strong and courageous, and do the work. Do not be afraid or discouraged, for the LORD God, my God, is with you.' The echoes of Joshua 1:6-9 and Deuteronomy 31:6 are deliberate and powerful.

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine blueprint**: The temple plans are Spirit-given, not humanly devised
2. **Wholehearted service**: God requires inner devotion, not merely external conformity
3. **Generational faithfulness**: David's charge spans his lifetime and Solomon's calling
4. **Courage for the task**: God's presence is the ground of courage in His service
