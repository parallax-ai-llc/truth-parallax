## The Ark's Proper Transfer and Levitical Service

Jewish interpretation reads the successful transfer of the Ark as a narrative of *teshuvah* (repentance and correction). David, having learned from Uzzah's death, returns to the Torah's prescriptions and succeeds where he previously failed.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Correction (15:2, 13-15)

'No one but the Levites may carry the ark of God.' **Rashi** cites Numbers 7:9 as the halakhic basis: the Kohathites must carry the holy objects on their shoulders. David's explicit acknowledgment of prior error — 'we did not inquire of him about how to do it in the prescribed way' — models the rabbinic ideal of learning from mistakes (*chazarah bi-teshuvah*).

### The Musical Procession (15:16-24)

**Radak** notes that David's appointment of musicians formalizes Levitical musical service for the first time. The instruments specified — cymbals (*metziltayim*), lyres (*nevalim*), and harps (*kinnorot*) — become the standard temple orchestra.

### David's Dancing (15:29)

David's ecstatic dancing before the Ark is celebrated in rabbinic tradition as an expression of supreme joy in fulfilling a mitzvah. Michal's contempt is read as spiritual arrogance — looking down on genuine religious enthusiasm.

## Theological Significance

1. **Teshuvah through Torah study**: David corrects his error by returning to the Torah's prescriptions
2. **Halakhic precision**: The Levitical method of transport is not optional but divinely mandated
3. **Simchah shel mitzvah**: Joy in performing God's commandments is itself a religious value
4. **Musical formalization**: David establishes the Levitical musical tradition for all time
