## The Spirit-Led Gathering and the Joy of Unity

Jewish interpretation reads the rallying of warriors to David as evidence of divine orchestration. The Spirit's descent on Amasai and the joyful assembly at Hebron demonstrate that David's kingship is not merely political but divinely empowered.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Warriors from Benjamin (12:1-7)

**Rashi** notes the significance of Benjaminites — Saul's own tribesmen — defecting to David. This demonstrates that divine appointment overrides tribal loyalty when the two conflict.

### The Ruach on Amasai (12:18)

'Then the *ruach* (spirit) clothed Amasai...' **Radak** interprets this as prophetic inspiration — the Spirit moving Amasai to declare loyalty to David. The rabbinic tradition distinguishes between different levels of prophetic inspiration, and Amasai's declaration reflects genuine divine empowerment.

### The Feast at Hebron (12:38-40)

The three-day feast celebrates unity and divine favor. **Ralbag** observes that communal celebration (*simchah*) is essential to the covenant community's life. The Torah commands rejoicing at festivals (Deuteronomy 16:14), and David's coronation feast fulfills this principle.

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine orchestration**: God gathers diverse supporters around His chosen king
2. **Prophetic empowerment**: The Spirit inspires public declaration of loyalty
3. **Simchah**: Communal joy is a religious obligation, not merely a social custom
4. **Unity across tribes**: The ideal of kol Yisrael is realized at Hebron
