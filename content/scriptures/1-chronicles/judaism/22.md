## David's Temple Preparations and the Charge to Shlomo

Jewish interpretation reads David's temple preparations as a demonstration that the Torah's prohibition against David building the Temple did not prevent him from preparing everything for its construction. This distinction between direct action and preparatory service is halakhically significant.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### A Man of Blood (22:8)

**Rashi** explains that David's wars, though divinely mandated, rendered him unfit to build the Temple — a place of peace (*shalom*). The Midrash distinguishes between legitimate warfare and the shedding of blood that disqualifies one from certain sacred tasks.

### The Charge to Solomon (22:11-16)

"Be strong and courageous" echoes Moses' charge to Joshua. **Radak** notes the deliberate parallel: as Joshua entered the Promised Land, Solomon enters the work of building God's house. Each generation has its own divinely appointed task.

### Preparation as Service (22:2-5, 14-16)

**Ralbag** draws the practical lesson that those who cannot complete a task may still contribute decisively through preparation. David's gathering of materials — gold, silver, bronze, iron, timber, stone — demonstrates that service to God's purposes takes many forms.

## Theological Significance

1. **Preparation as legitimate service**: Those who cannot build may still prepare
2. **War and peace**: The Temple requires a context of peace, distinguishing David's role from Solomon's
3. **Generational partnership**: Each generation contributes to God's purposes in its own way
4. **Mosaic parallel**: Solomon's charge echoes Joshua's, linking the two great transitions
