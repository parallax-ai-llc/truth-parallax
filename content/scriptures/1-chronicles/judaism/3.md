## The Davidic Dynasty in Rabbinic Perspective

Jewish interpretation of the Davidic genealogy serves both historical and messianic purposes. The unbroken line from David through the exile to the post-exilic period preserves the possibility of a future Davidic king — the Mashiach.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Sons (3:1-9)

**Rashi** identifies each son and connects them to narratives in Samuel. The listing of Bathsheba as 'Bath-shua' raises textual questions that the commentators address. **Radak** notes the variant name as reflecting different dialectal traditions.

### The Kings of Judah (3:10-16)

The succession list serves as a historical framework for the kingdom's history. **Abarbanel** provides political analysis of each reign's strengths and failures, drawing lessons for governance.

### Post-Exilic Continuation (3:17-24)

The genealogy's extension through Zerubbabel and beyond demonstrates that the Davidic line survived the exile. Rabbinic messianic expectation builds on this biological continuity — the Mashiach ben David must descend from David's house through documented lineage.

## Theological Significance

1. **Messianic lineage**: The preserved Davidic line maintains the possibility of a future Mashiach
2. **Historical continuity**: The dynasty's survival testifies to God's covenant faithfulness
3. **Textual variants**: Differences between Chronicles and Samuel invite scholarly investigation
4. **Post-exilic identity**: The Davidic line anchors the returning community's hopes
