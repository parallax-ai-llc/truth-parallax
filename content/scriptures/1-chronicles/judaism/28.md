## The Spirit-Given Blueprint and the Charge to Build

Jewish interpretation reads David's charge to Solomon as a moment of supreme spiritual transmission — the plans for the Temple, given by the *ruach* (spirit), are entrusted to the next generation along with the exhortation to serve God with a whole heart.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Wholehearted Service (28:9)

"Serve him with a whole heart and a willing soul, for the LORD searches every heart." **Rashi** reads this as encompassing both intellectual knowledge (*da'at*) and emotional devotion (*avodah she-ba-lev* — service of the heart). The Talmud (Ta'anit 2a) identifies "service of the heart" as prayer.

### The Spirit-Given Plans (28:11-19)

"All this... the LORD made me understand in writing by His hand upon me." **Radak** compares this to Moses receiving the tabernacle pattern on Sinai (Exodus 25:9). The Temple's design is divine revelation, not human architecture — a principle that governs the halakhic discussions of Temple measurements in Tractate Middot.

### The Final Encouragement (28:20)

"Be strong and courageous and do the work." **Ralbag** draws the lesson that divine calling always requires human courage. The assurance of God's presence does not eliminate the need for effort — it sustains the effort.

## Theological Significance

1. **Avodah she-ba-lev**: Service of the heart encompasses prayer and inner devotion
2. **Divine architecture**: The Temple's design is revelation, comparable to the Sinai pattern
3. **Spiritual transmission**: Passing sacred knowledge to the next generation is a supreme duty
4. **Courage and calling**: Divine vocation requires human fortitude sustained by divine presence
