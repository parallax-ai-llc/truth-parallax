## The Satan, the Census, and the Birth of the Temple Site

Jewish interpretation reads chapter 21 as one of the most theologically dense passages in Chronicles. The Chronicler's attribution of the census to Satan (vs. God's anger in 2 Samuel 24), David's repentance, and the identification of the threshing floor as the future Temple site all generate extensive rabbinic discussion.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Satan's Incitement (21:1)

**Rashi** reads "Satan" (*satan*) as an adversarial figure — whether an angelic prosecutor (as in Job 1-2) or the evil inclination (*yetzer ha-ra*). The Talmud (Bava Batra 16a) identifies the Satan, the evil inclination, and the angel of death as one. The shift from 2 Samuel 24:1 reflects the Chronicler's developing angelology.

### The Prohibition of Counting (21:1-7)

The census violates the prohibition against counting Israel directly (Exodus 30:12). **Radak** explains that direct counting implies ownership — but Israel belongs to God alone. The halakhic practice of counting with a verse (*minyan*) rather than numbers derives from this prohibition.

### The Threshing Floor and the Temple (21:18-28)

David buys the threshing floor of Ornan (Araunah) and builds an altar. The Talmud (Zevachim 62a) identifies this site with Mount Moriah, where Abraham bound Isaac — making the Temple site the convergence of the two foundational acts of atonement in Jewish history.

## Theological Significance

1. **Satan in Jewish theology**: The adversary operates within God's sovereign permission
2. **Counting prohibition**: Israel belongs to God and cannot be numbered as human property
3. **Temple site as Moriah**: The convergence of Isaac's binding and David's altar on one site
4. **Teshuvah**: David's repentance transforms judgment into the foundation of worship
