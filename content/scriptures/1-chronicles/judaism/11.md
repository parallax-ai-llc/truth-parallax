## David's Anointing and the Capture of Yerushalayim

Jewish interpretation reads David's coronation and the conquest of Jerusalem as the establishment of the political and spiritual center that defines Jewish identity. The genealogical section has prepared for this moment: the chosen king takes the chosen city.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### All Israel at Hebron (11:1-3)

**Rashi** notes that the Chronicler presents a unified coronation, omitting the years of conflict between David and Ish-bosheth recorded in 2 Samuel. The Chronicler's interest is theological: David is king of *kol Yisrael* (all Israel) from the beginning.

### The Capture of the Jebusite City (11:4-9)

**Radak** discusses the strategic and theological significance of choosing Jerusalem — a city belonging to no single tribe — as the capital. The conquest fulfills the promise of a central sanctuary (Deuteronomy 12:5) and establishes the location that will define Jewish worship forever.

### The Mighty Warriors (11:10-47)

The warrior catalog demonstrates David's personal charisma and covenantal leadership. **Ralbag** draws the practical lesson that great leaders inspire great loyalty — not through coercion but through shared vision and mutual devotion.

## Theological Significance

1. **Kol Yisrael**: David's kingship encompasses all Israel, not just his own tribe
2. **Yerushalayim**: The chosen city becomes the eternal center of Jewish spiritual life
3. **Covenantal loyalty**: David's warriors model the bond between leader and community
4. **Divine appointment**: David's kingship fulfills prophetic promise, not mere political ambition
