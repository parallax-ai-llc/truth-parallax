## God-Given Victories and the Justice of David's Rule

Jewish interpretation reads David's military victories as divine confirmation of his kingship. The refrain "the LORD gave David victory" (*va-yosha Adonai et David*) attributes all success to God, not to David's military prowess.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Victory Refrain (18:6, 13)

**Rashi** emphasizes that the repeated refrain establishes divine causation. Military success is not earned but granted — a principle the rabbis extend to all areas of life: "everything is in the hands of Heaven except the fear of Heaven" (Berakhot 33b).

### Justice and Righteousness (18:14)

"David reigned over all Israel, doing what was just and right for all his people." **Ralbag** identifies justice (*mishpat*) and righteousness (*tzedakah*) as the twin pillars of legitimate governance. The rabbinic ideal of the righteous king combines judicial fairness with compassionate generosity.

### Dedicated Spoils (18:11)

David dedicates war spoils to God — consecrating material gain to sacred purpose. The rabbinic principle of *hekdesh* (consecration to the Temple) finds expression here.

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine victory**: All success belongs to God, received with gratitude
2. **Mishpat and tzedakah**: Justice and righteousness define legitimate governance
3. **Hekdesh**: Material blessings are consecrated to God's purposes
4. **Contrast with Saul**: David's victories confirm God's transfer of the kingdom
