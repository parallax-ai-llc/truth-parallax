## The Tribe of the First King and the Holy City

Orthodox interpretation reads Benjamin's genealogy as a bridge between Israel's failed first kingship (Saul) and the divinely appointed second (David). Benjamin's association with Jerusalem gives the tribe a sacred significance that outlasts Saul's rejection.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Benjamin and Jerusalem (8:1-28)

Benjamin's territory included Jerusalem, and after the schism the tribe remained with Judah. Orthodox ecclesiology values this loyalty to the center of worship as a model for maintaining communion with the Church's tradition even when political pressures favor schism.

### Saul's House (8:29-40)

Saul's genealogy is recorded with care and dignity. Orthodox theology holds that even those who fail in their calling are not erased from God's memory. The Church prays for all the departed, trusting that God's mercy extends beyond human judgment.

## Theological Significance

1. **Loyalty to tradition**: Benjamin's fidelity to Judah models faithfulness to the Church
2. **Dignity of the fallen**: Saul's genealogy is preserved, honoring even the failed king
3. **Sacred geography**: Benjamin's connection to Jerusalem underscores the tribe's liturgical importance
4. **Transition**: The chapter prepares for the narrative section's theological judgments
