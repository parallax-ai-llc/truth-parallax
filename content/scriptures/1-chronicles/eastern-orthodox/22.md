## The Man of War Prepares for the Prince of Peace

Orthodox interpretation reads David's temple preparations as a patristic allegory of the spiritual life: the warfare of asceticism prepares for the peace of contemplation. David (the ascetic warrior) prepares what Solomon (the contemplative peacemaker) will build.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### A Man of Blood vs. a Man of Peace (22:8-10)

The distinction between David (war) and Solomon (peace/*shalom*) resonates with Orthodox ascetical theology. **The Philokalia** teaches that the active life (*praxis*) of spiritual combat prepares the soul for the contemplative life (*theoria*) of divine vision.

### The Charge to Solomon (22:13)

'Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid or discouraged.' Orthodox spiritual direction echoes this charge to every Christian beginning the path of theosis — courage is required for the spiritual life, sustained by the assurance of God's presence.

### Community Preparation (22:17-19)

David calls all Israel's leaders to help Solomon. Orthodox ecclesiology emphasizes that the work of salvation is communal — the Church builds together what no individual can construct alone.

## Theological Significance

1. **Praxis and theoria**: Warfare prepares for peace; asceticism prepares for contemplation
2. **Generational faithfulness**: One generation prepares what the next will build
3. **Courage in calling**: The spiritual life requires fortitude sustained by divine presence
4. **Communal effort**: The Temple — like the Church — is built by the whole community
