## The Anointed King and the Holy City

Orthodox interpretation reads David's anointing and the capture of Jerusalem as the establishment of the sacred center of Israel's worship — a typological anticipation of Christ's entry into His glory and the founding of the Church.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### All Israel at Hebron (11:1-3)

The Chronicler presents all Israel anointing David — a vision of unity that Orthodox ecclesiology treasures. The whole people of God gather around God's anointed, just as the faithful gather around Christ in the Eucharistic assembly.

### The City of David (11:4-9)

Jerusalem's capture establishes the sacred geography of Israel's faith. Orthodox tradition venerates Jerusalem as the city of Christ's passion, death, and resurrection. The earthly Jerusalem is a type of the heavenly city described in Revelation 21.

### The Mighty Warriors (11:10-47)

The catalog of loyal warriors models the communion of saints — a community of faithful individuals bound by love and loyalty to their anointed king. Orthodox hagiography celebrates similar communities of faith surrounding holy leaders.

## Theological Significance

1. **Eucharistic unity**: All Israel gathered around God's anointed prefigures the Eucharistic assembly
2. **Sacred geography**: Jerusalem as the center of worship anticipates the heavenly city
3. **Communion of saints**: Loyal warriors model the bond of love within the Church
4. **Christological typology**: David's anointing and enthronement prefigure Christ's glory
