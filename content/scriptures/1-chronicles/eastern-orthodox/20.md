## The Chronicler's Selective Witness

Orthodox interpretation notes the Chronicler's omission of the Bathsheba narrative with characteristic nuance. The Chronicler does not deny David's sin — already well-known from Samuel — but selects material to emphasize David's covenantal and liturgical significance for the post-exilic community.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Omission of Bathsheba (20:1)

The Chronicler moves directly from the spring campaign to the fall of Rabbah, omitting 2 Samuel 11-12 entirely. Orthodox hermeneutics recognizes that sacred authors are theologians, not court reporters — they select material for theological purposes while maintaining truthfulness.

### The Giant-Slayers (20:4-8)

Three victories over Philistine giants extend the David-and-Goliath pattern. Orthodox hagiography similarly records the saints' victories over demonic powers — spiritual 'giants' overcome through faith and divine empowerment.

## Theological Significance

1. **Theological selection**: Sacred authors choose material for theological, not merely historical, purposes
2. **Canonical complementarity**: Chronicles and Samuel together provide the full picture
3. **Spiritual warfare**: Physical giants symbolize the spiritual enemies the faithful overcome
4. **Community courage**: David's warriors share in their leader's giant-killing faith
