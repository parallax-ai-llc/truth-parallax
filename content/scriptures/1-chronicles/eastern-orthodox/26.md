## Guardians of the Sacred and Stewards of the Holy

Orthodox interpretation reads the gatekeepers and treasurers as essential ministers whose service protects the sacred and manages the holy. These roles find their continuation in the Church's own custodians of sacred space and resources.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Gatekeepers (26:1-19)

Gatekeepers guard the sacred precincts — ensuring that worship space is protected and properly accessed. Orthodox churches maintain this ministry through the role of the sexton, the acolyte, and the tradition of the royal doors that separate the sanctuary from the nave.

### Temple Treasurers (26:20-28)

The faithful management of dedicated gifts and temple resources models Orthodox parish stewardship. The Church's resources — buildings, liturgical vessels, charitable funds — require the same careful oversight the Chronicler describes.

### Beyond the Temple (26:29-32)

Levites serving as judges and administrators extend sacred principles into civil life. Orthodox social thought affirms that the principles of justice, mercy, and stewardship apply to all dimensions of life, not only to liturgical worship.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sacred guardianship**: Protecting holy space is a sacred ministry
2. **Faithful stewardship**: Managing Church resources requires integrity and accountability
3. **Integration**: Sacred principles extend beyond the sanctuary into all of life
4. **Continuity**: The Church's custodians continue the Levitical gatekeeping ministry
