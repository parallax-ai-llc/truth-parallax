## The Royal Tribe and the Theotokos' Ancestry

Orthodox interpretation traces the Judahite genealogy with attention to its culmination in Christ through the Theotokos. Each generation from Judah to David is a step in God's preparation for the Incarnation — the central event of Orthodox theology.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Through Perez and Tamar (2:3-4)

The inclusion of Tamar demonstrates that God's purposes work through human brokenness. Orthodox theology acknowledges human sinfulness while emphasizing that God's grace transfigures every situation. The irregular circumstances of Tamar's story do not impede the divine plan.

### The Line to David (2:10-17)

From Ram through Boaz to Jesse to David — the royal lineage that leads to the Theotokos and her Son. **St. Theodoret** noted that God's patience with this imperfect lineage reveals the depth of divine condescension (*synkatabasis*).

### Bezalel the Craftsman (2:20)

Bezalel, gifted by the Spirit for sacred craftsmanship (Exodus 31:1-5), appears in Judah's genealogy. Orthodox iconographic tradition sees in Bezalel a prototype of the sacred artisan — one whose creativity serves the revelation of divine beauty.

## Theological Significance

1. **Incarnational genealogy**: Each generation prepares for God's entry into human flesh
2. **Divine condescension**: God works through imperfect human lineages
3. **Sacred art**: Bezalel models the Spirit-filled craftsman serving divine beauty
4. **Theotokos connection**: The Judahite line leads to Mary and through her to Christ
