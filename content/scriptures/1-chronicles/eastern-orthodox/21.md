## Sin, Repentance, and the Transfiguration of a Threshing Floor

Orthodox interpretation reads David's census, the plague, and the purchase of the threshing floor as a narrative of sin transfigured by repentance and grace. The future Temple site — born from the meeting of divine judgment and human penitence — models the paschal mystery.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Satan's Incitement (21:1)

The Chronicler attributes the census's instigation to Satan. Orthodox demonology takes the reality of Satan seriously — a fallen angel who tempts but cannot compel. The Christian's defense is prayer, fasting, and the sign of the cross.

### David's Repentance (21:8, 17)

'I have sinned greatly... Let your hand fall on me.' David's intercessory repentance — offering to bear punishment for his people — is a type of Christ, who bears the world's sin. Orthodox Lenten practice of prostrations and penitential prayer echoes David's posture.

### Fire from Heaven (21:26)

God's fire consuming David's offering confirms divine acceptance. Orthodox theology sees this as a theophany — God's manifest presence accepting the repentant sinner's offering. The Temple will rise on this very spot.

## Theological Significance

1. **Transfiguration of sin**: God transforms the site of judgment into the site of worship
2. **Intercessory repentance**: David's offer to suffer for his people prefigures Christ's sacrifice
3. **Spiritual warfare**: Satan's reality demands vigilance and prayer
4. **Theophanic fire**: God's manifest acceptance confirms the penitent's restoration
