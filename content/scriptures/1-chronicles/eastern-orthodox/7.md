## All Israel in God's Economy

Orthodox interpretation reads the northern tribal genealogies as testimony that God's providential care extends to all Israel, not merely to Judah and the Temple tribes. Even tribes that would later fall into schism remain within the scope of God's redemptive purpose.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Ephraim's Loss and Renewal (7:20-29)

Ephraim mourns sons killed in a raid, then experiences renewal through new birth and rebuilding. **St. Ephrem the Syrian** saw in such narratives the paschal mystery at work in ordinary life — death giving way to new life through God's grace.

### Military Readiness (7:1-5, 7, 9, 11, 40)

The repeated census of warriors in each tribe demonstrates readiness for communal defense. Orthodox social ethics values the defense of the innocent and the protection of the community as legitimate expressions of love for neighbor.

### The Whole People of God (7:1-40)

The inclusion of these tribes, however briefly, maintains the Chronicler's vision of a united Israel. Orthodox ecumenical theology sees in this vision a call to the unity of all Christ's people.

## Theological Significance

1. **Universal providence**: God's care extends to every tribe and people
2. **Paschal pattern**: Loss and renewal in Ephraim prefigure death and resurrection
3. **Communal defense**: Protecting the community is an expression of covenantal love
4. **Unity**: The whole people of God belong together in one covenant
