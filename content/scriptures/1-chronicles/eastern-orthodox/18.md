## God-Given Victory and Righteous Rule

Orthodox interpretation reads David's military victories as evidence of God's providential establishment of the kingdom. The refrain 'the LORD gave David victory' attributes all success to divine agency, modeling the Orthodox understanding that earthly accomplishments are gifts of grace.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Victory Refrain (18:6, 13)

'The LORD gave David victory wherever he went.' Orthodox theology affirms that all good things come from God. Military victory, like every blessing, is received with gratitude rather than claimed as personal achievement.

### Justice for All (18:14)

David's just rule — 'doing what was just and right for all his people' — reflects the Orthodox ideal of the Christian ruler as servant of justice. The Byzantine tradition of *symphonia* held that the emperor's primary duty was the administration of divine justice.

### Dedicated Spoils (18:11)

David consecrates war spoils to God. Orthodox stewardship theology teaches that all material blessings are to be offered back to God in thanksgiving — a principle expressed in the prosphora (offering bread) brought to the Liturgy.

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine attribution**: All victories belong to God, received with thanksgiving
2. **Righteous governance**: The ruler's primary duty is justice for all people
3. **Offering back**: Material blessings are consecrated to God's service
4. **Providential ordering**: God's hand guides the expansion of His covenant purposes
