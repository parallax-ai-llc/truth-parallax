## The King's Legacy: Preparing for What He Cannot Build

Catholic interpretation reads David's temple preparations as a model of generational faithfulness. David cannot build the Temple — he is a man of blood — but he prepares everything for his successor. The Church values this pattern of sacrificial preparation for future generations.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### A Man of Peace (22:8-10)

God tells David he has shed too much blood to build the Temple. His son Solomon — whose name echoes *shalom* (peace) — will build it. Catholic theology sees Christ as the true Prince of Peace who builds the eternal Temple of the Church.

### The Charge to Solomon (22:11-16)

David's charge echoes Joshua's commissioning: 'Be strong and courageous.' Catholic formation emphasizes that each generation must pass on both the faith and the courage to live it. The charge includes both spiritual exhortation and practical provision.

### Community Responsibility (22:17-19)

David commands the leaders to help Solomon, recognizing that the Temple is a communal project. Catholic social teaching affirms the principle of subsidiarity and solidarity: great works require the cooperation of the whole community.

## Theological Significance

1. **Generational stewardship**: Preparing for what one cannot complete is itself an act of faith
2. **Peace and worship**: The Temple requires a context of peace, not war
3. **Community effort**: Sacred work is accomplished through collective responsibility
4. **Courage and calling**: Each generation needs both faith and fortitude
