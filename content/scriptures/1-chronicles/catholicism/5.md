## The Transjordan Tribes: Faithfulness and Its Failure

Catholic interpretation reads the genealogies of the Transjordan tribes as a cautionary narrative about the consequences of spiritual infidelity. The chapter's concluding verdict — exile because of unfaithfulness — exemplifies the Chronicler's theology of divine justice.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Reuben's Forfeited Birthright (5:1-2)

Reuben lost the birthright through sin (Genesis 35:22), though his genealogy is still recorded. Catholic moral theology sees here the distinction between the irrevocability of God's gifts and the consequences of their misuse — grace is given freely but demands a faithful response.

### Victory through Crying Out to God (5:18-22)

The Transjordan tribes defeat the Hagrites 'because they cried out to him during the battle. He answered their prayers because they trusted in him.' Catholic theology of prayer emphasizes that God hears the cry of the faithful, especially in times of trial. This passage resonates with the tradition of invoking divine aid in spiritual combat.

### The Exile as Consequence (5:25-26)

The tribes 'were unfaithful to the God of their ancestors' and so God sent them into exile through Assyria. Catholic teaching on sin and its social consequences finds expression here: personal unfaithfulness produces communal disaster.

## Theological Significance

1. **Consequences of sin**: Spiritual infidelity leads to exile — separation from God's blessings
2. **Prayer in battle**: Crying out to God in distress is the model of authentic prayer
3. **Social dimension of sin**: Individual unfaithfulness affects the entire community
4. **Grace and responsibility**: God's gifts require faithful stewardship
