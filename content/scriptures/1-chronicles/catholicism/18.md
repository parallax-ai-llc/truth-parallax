## Just Rule and the Expansion of God's Kingdom

Catholic interpretation reads David's military victories as the expansion of a kingdom under God's blessing, where the refrain 'the LORD gave David victory wherever he went' attributes all success to divine providence rather than human might.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### God-Given Victory (18:6, 13)

The repeated refrain 'the LORD gave David victory' establishes the Catholic principle that legitimate temporal authority succeeds through God's providence, not merely through military power. Catholic just war theory traces its roots to this understanding of warfare under divine mandate.

### Justice and Righteousness (18:14)

'David reigned over all Israel, doing what was just and right for all his people.' Catholic social teaching identifies justice as the cardinal virtue of governance. David's just rule prefigures Christ's kingdom of justice and peace.

### Dedicated Treasures (18:11)

David dedicates the spoils of war to the LORD. Catholic theology of stewardship teaches that all material blessings — even those gained through legitimate effort — belong ultimately to God.

## Theological Significance

1. **Providence in victory**: God grants success to the faithful ruler
2. **Justice as governance**: The ideal king administers justice for all people
3. **Stewardship of wealth**: Material blessings are dedicated to God's purposes
4. **Typology**: David's just rule prefigures Christ's eternal kingdom
