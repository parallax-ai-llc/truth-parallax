## The Spirit-Led Gathering of God's People

Catholic interpretation reads the rallying of warriors to David as a Spirit-directed movement of the people of God toward their anointed king — a type of the Church gathering around Christ.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Warriors from Every Tribe (12:1-22)

Even Benjaminites from Saul's own tribe defect to David. Catholic theology sees the Holy Spirit at work in these defections — drawing individuals beyond tribal loyalty to the purposes of God's anointed.

### The Spirit upon Amasai (12:18)

'Then the Spirit came on Amasai... and he said: We are yours, David!' Catholic pneumatology recognizes this as a charismatic utterance — the Spirit inspiring public profession of loyalty to God's chosen. This pattern continues in the Church through confirmation and the gifts of the Spirit.

### The Festive Assembly (12:38-40)

The coronation feast — with food, drink, and joy — prefigures the Eucharistic banquet. Catholic liturgical theology sees in every sacred meal a participation in the heavenly feast. 'There was joy in Israel' — the proper response to being gathered by God.

## Theological Significance

1. **Spirit-led unity**: The Holy Spirit draws diverse people to God's anointed
2. **Charismatic profession**: Amasai's declaration models Spirit-inspired witness
3. **Eucharistic prefiguration**: The coronation feast anticipates the Church's sacred banquet
4. **Joy as theological virtue**: Joy accompanies the right ordering of God's people
