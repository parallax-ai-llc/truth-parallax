## The Liturgy of Thanksgiving Before the Ark

Catholic interpretation reads David's psalm of thanksgiving as a liturgical model — a structured act of worship combining praise, remembrance of God's mighty deeds, and call to worship. This composite psalm (drawn from Psalms 105, 96, and 106) anticipates the Eucharistic prayer's own structure of thanksgiving and anamnesis.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### David's Offerings (16:1-3)

David offers burnt offerings and fellowship offerings, then blesses the people in the LORD's name. Catholic theology sees David exercising a quasi-priestly function that anticipates Christ's unique priesthood and the ordained ministry's participation in it.

### The Psalm's Structure (16:8-36)

The psalm moves from call to praise (8-13), to remembrance of covenant deeds (14-22), to universal proclamation (23-33), to petition and doxology (34-36). This structure mirrors the Catholic Eucharistic prayer: thanksgiving, anamnesis (remembrance), and doxology.

### Continuous Worship Established (16:37-42)

David establishes regular, daily worship before the Ark. Catholic liturgical practice — the Liturgy of the Hours, daily Mass, perpetual adoration — continues this principle of unceasing worship.

## Theological Significance

1. **Eucharistic anticipation**: The thanksgiving psalm prefigures the Eucharistic prayer
2. **Anamnesis**: Remembrance of God's deeds is central to worship
3. **Unceasing worship**: Daily, regular liturgy reflects God's constant faithfulness
4. **Universal proclamation**: Worship includes declaring God's glory among all nations
