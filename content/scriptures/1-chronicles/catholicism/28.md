## The Spirit-Given Blueprint and the Royal Commission

Catholic interpretation reads David's charge to Solomon as a model of faithful leadership transmission. The Spirit-inspired temple plans parallel Moses' reception of the tabernacle pattern on Sinai — divine revelation providing the blueprint for sacred worship.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Wholehearted Service (28:9)

'Serve him with wholehearted devotion and with a willing mind, for the LORD searches every heart.' Catholic spiritual theology treasures this verse as a summary of interior religion. The Catechism teaches that prayer is 'the life of the new heart' (CCC 2697) — God seeks not mere external observance but inner devotion.

### Spirit-Given Plans (28:11-19)

David receives temple plans 'that the Spirit had put in his mind' — for every detail of the sanctuary. Catholic theology of sacred architecture affirms that church buildings are not merely functional spaces but theological statements inspired by faith and tradition.

### The Final Encouragement (28:20)

'Be strong and courageous, and do the work... for the LORD God, my God, is with you.' Catholic teaching on vocation emphasizes that God's call always comes with the grace sufficient for the task. The assurance of divine presence grounds the courage to act.

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine blueprint**: Sacred architecture is inspired by God, not invented by humans
2. **Interior devotion**: God seeks wholehearted service, not merely external conformity
3. **Vocation and grace**: God's call includes the grace to fulfill it
4. **Leadership succession**: Faithful transmission of responsibility is itself an act of faith
