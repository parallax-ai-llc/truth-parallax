## The Covenant as Foundation of the Church

Catholic interpretation reads the Davidic covenant as the Old Testament foundation of ecclesiology. God's promise to build David an eternal house finds its fulfillment in Christ and the Church He founded — the eternal kingdom promised to David's descendant.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### God's Reversal (17:4-14)

David proposes to build God a house; God responds by promising to build David a house (dynasty). Catholic theology reads this reversal as a principle of grace: God's gifts always exceed human offerings. The Temple David envisions becomes the Church Christ builds (Matthew 16:18).

### The Eternal Throne (17:12-14)

'I will set him over my house and my kingdom forever; his throne will be established forever.' Catholic Christology identifies this as a direct prophecy of Christ. The Nicene Creed affirms: 'His kingdom will have no end' — echoing the Chronicler's promise.

### David's Prayer (17:16-27)

David's prayer of humble gratitude models the proper response to God's promises. 'Who am I, LORD God?' — this posture of wonder echoes Mary's Magnificat (Luke 1:46-55), connecting David's response to the Annunciation.

## Theological Significance

1. **Grace precedes works**: God builds the house; human effort responds to divine initiative
2. **Christological fulfillment**: The eternal throne is fulfilled in Christ's kingship
3. **Ecclesiological foundation**: The promised 'house' becomes the Church
4. **Marian connection**: David's humility before grace anticipates Mary's fiat
