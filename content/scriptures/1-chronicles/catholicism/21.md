## Sin, Repentance, and the Birth of Sacred Space

Catholic interpretation reads David's census, the resulting plague, and the purchase of the threshing floor as a narrative of sin, repentance, and the emergence of sacred space. The future Temple site is born from the intersection of human sin and divine mercy.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Satan and the Census (21:1)

The Chronicler attributes the census's instigation to Satan — a development from 2 Samuel 24:1. Catholic demonology recognizes Satan as a real spiritual adversary who tempts the faithful. The Catechism teaches that Satan 'was at first a good angel, made by God' who fell through pride (CCC 391-395).

### David's Intercessory Repentance (21:17)

'Let your hand fall on me and my family, but do not let this plague remain on your people.' David's willingness to bear punishment for his people models intercessory prayer. Catholic theology sees a type of Christ, who bears the sins of the world.

### The Threshing Floor and Fire from Heaven (21:18-26)

David buys the threshing floor and offers sacrifice. Fire from heaven consumes the offering — divine acceptance. This site becomes the Temple mount. Catholic sacramental theology sees in this transformation a model of how grace consecrates ordinary spaces — the threshing floor becomes the altar of God.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sin and mercy**: The Temple site emerges from the meeting of human sin and divine grace
2. **Intercessory suffering**: David's offer to bear punishment prefigures Christ's atonement
3. **Consecration of space**: God transforms an ordinary threshing floor into sacred ground
4. **Spiritual warfare**: Satan's role reminds the faithful of the reality of spiritual combat
