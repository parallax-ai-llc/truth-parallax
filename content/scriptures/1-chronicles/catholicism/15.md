## Right Worship in Sacred Order

Catholic interpretation of the Ark's successful transfer emphasizes the necessity of proper liturgical order. David's correction — having the Levites carry the Ark as Moses prescribed — models the Church's commitment to worship according to sacred tradition.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Levitical Correction (15:1-2, 13-15)

David acknowledges: 'Because you, the Levites, did not bring it up the first time, the LORD our God broke out in anger against us. We did not inquire of him about how to do it in the prescribed way.' Catholic liturgical theology draws on this text to affirm that worship must follow the prescriptions of sacred tradition, not personal innovation.

### The Musical Procession (15:16-24)

The detailed organization of musicians with cymbals, lyres, harps, and trumpets establishes sacred music as an essential component of liturgical worship. **The Council of Trent** and **Vatican II** both affirmed the integral role of music in the liturgy, rooted in passages like this.

### David's Dancing (15:25-29)

David's exuberant dancing before the Ark expresses the joy of God's presence. Catholic spirituality embraces both ordered liturgy and heartfelt devotion — the two are not opposed but complementary. Michal's contempt represents the danger of reducing worship to mere propriety.

## Theological Significance

1. **Liturgical order**: Worship must follow divinely prescribed forms
2. **Sacred music**: Music is integral to liturgy, not an optional addition
3. **Joyful devotion**: Genuine worship combines order with heartfelt expression
4. **Priestly mediation**: Only authorized ministers handle the sacred properly
