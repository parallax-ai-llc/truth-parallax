## David's Anointing and the City of God

Catholic interpretation reads David's anointing and the capture of Jerusalem as the establishment of a sacred center that prefigures the Church. The convergence of kingship, holy city, and loyal community models the unity the Church seeks.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### All Israel at Hebron (11:1-3)

The Chronicler presents all Israel anointing David — a vision of unified covenant community. Catholic ecclesiology sees in this unity a type of the Church's catholicity: one people, one anointed leader, one covenantal bond.

### The Capture of Jerusalem (11:4-9)

Jerusalem becomes David's city and, by extension, God's city. Catholic tradition identifies Jerusalem as a type of the Church — the holy city where God dwells among His people. The earthly Jerusalem points to the heavenly Jerusalem (Revelation 21).

### The Mighty Warriors (11:10-47)

The catalog of warriors demonstrates that David's kingdom is built on personal loyalty and covenantal bond. Catholic teaching on the communion of saints resonates with this image — a community of individuals bound by devotion to Christ the King.

## Theological Significance

1. **Unity under anointing**: David's coronation models the Church's unity under Christ
2. **Sacred geography**: Jerusalem as God's city prefigures the Church and the heavenly city
3. **Communion of saints**: Loyal warriors model the devotion of the faithful to their King
4. **Sacramental kingship**: David's anointing carries sacred, not merely political, authority
