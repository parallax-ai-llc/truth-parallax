## Guardians and Stewards of Sacred Space

Catholic interpretation reads the gatekeepers and treasurers as exercising essential ministries of protection and stewardship. These roles — guarding access and managing resources — anticipate the Church's own concern for the reverent care of sacred spaces and goods.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Gatekeepers (26:1-19)

Gatekeepers guard each entrance to the sacred precinct. Catholic liturgical practice continues this ministry through sacristans, ushers, and those who maintain the dignity and order of worship spaces. The church building, like the Temple, requires guardians who balance welcome with reverence.

### Temple Treasurers (26:20-28)

Treasurers manage both regular temple finances and dedicated gifts. Catholic canon law and financial administration reflect this dual responsibility — ordinary operating funds and dedicated charitable resources require distinct and faithful management.

### Civil-Sacred Integration (26:29-32)

Levites serve as judges and administrators beyond the Temple. Catholic social teaching affirms that the principles of justice and stewardship extend from the sanctuary into all areas of public life.

## Theological Significance

1. **Sacred stewardship**: Managing God's house and resources is a sacred trust
2. **Gatekeeping**: Balancing access and reverence is an essential ministry
3. **Financial integrity**: Church finances require transparent, faithful administration
4. **Integration**: Sacred principles govern both liturgical and civil life
