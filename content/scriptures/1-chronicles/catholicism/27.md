## Ordered Governance Under God

Catholic interpretation reads David's military and civil organization as a model of temporal governance ordered under divine authority. The twelve monthly divisions, tribal leaders, and royal stewards demonstrate that good governance is a participation in God's providential ordering of creation.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Monthly Military Rotation (27:1-15)

The twelve divisions of 24,000 men each create a citizen army. Catholic social teaching values military service oriented toward the common good and the defense of justice, organized with prudence and proportionality.

### The Incomplete Census (27:23-24)

David does not count those under twenty because 'the LORD had promised to make Israel as numerous as the stars.' Catholic faith teaches that divine promise, not human calculation, is the ground of security. Trust in God's word takes precedence over statistical certainty.

### Royal Stewards (27:25-31)

Stewards of vineyards, olive groves, and herds manage the kingdom's agricultural wealth. Catholic social teaching on property affirms that material goods are entrusted to human stewardship for the common good, not for private accumulation.

## Theological Significance

1. **Ordered governance**: Good administration reflects God's providential ordering
2. **Trust over calculation**: Faith in God's promises supersedes reliance on numbers
3. **Stewardship**: Material resources are managed for the common good
4. **Temporal authority**: Civil governance, rightly ordered, serves God's purposes
