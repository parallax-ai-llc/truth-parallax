## The Universal Scope of Salvation History

Catholic interpretation of 1 Chronicles 1 reads the genealogy from Adam through Abraham to Esau as evidence that salvation history encompasses all humanity. The Chronicler's universal opening echoes the Church's own catholicity — a faith intended for every nation and people.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### From Adam to Noah (1:1-4)

The genealogy begins at the absolute beginning of human history. **St. Jerome**, who translated Chronicles into Latin for the Vulgate, noted that the Chronicler assumes the reader knows Genesis — these names are a theological shorthand for the entire drama of creation, fall, and covenant.

### The Table of Nations (1:5-23)

The spread of humanity through Noah's sons reflects Catholic teaching on the unity of the human family. The Second Vatican Council's *Nostra Aetate* affirms that all peoples share a common origin and a common destiny in God — a principle visible in this genealogical prologue.

### Abraham and the Election of Israel (1:24-54)

The narrowing from universal humanity to Abraham's line demonstrates the Catholic understanding of particular election within universal purpose. God chooses Israel not to exclude others but as a vehicle of blessing for all nations (Genesis 12:3).

## Theological Significance

1. **Universal salvation**: God's purposes begin with all humanity, not Israel alone
2. **Particular election**: God chooses a specific lineage as the instrument of His universal plan
3. **Canonical unity**: The genealogy connects Genesis to the Chronicler's post-exilic context
4. **Human solidarity**: All peoples share a common origin in God's creation
