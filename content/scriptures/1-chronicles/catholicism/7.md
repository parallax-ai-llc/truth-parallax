## The Northern Tribes in God's Plan

Catholic interpretation reads the northern tribal genealogies as evidence that God's covenant embraces all Israel, not just Judah and Benjamin. Even tribes that would later fall into schism and idolatry are recorded among God's people.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Issachar's Warriors (7:1-5)

The military census of Issachar's clans — 87,000 mighty warriors — reflects the tribe's contribution to Israel's defense. Catholic social teaching values military service when ordered to the common good and the defense of the innocent.

### Ephraim's Mourning and Renewal (7:20-29)

Ephraim mourns sons killed by the men of Gath, then fathers a new son and rebuilds. Catholic theology sees in this pattern the paschal mystery at work in ordinary life — death and resurrection, loss and renewal, repeated across generations.

### The Completeness of God's People (7:1-40)

The inclusion of these tribes, however briefly, demonstrates the Chronicler's concern for the wholeness of Israel. Catholic ecclesiology resonates with this vision: the Church is truly catholic only when it encompasses all peoples and conditions of life.

## Theological Significance

1. **Catholicity**: God's covenant encompasses all tribes, all peoples
2. **Paschal pattern**: Loss and renewal in Ephraim's story mirrors death and resurrection
3. **Common good**: Military service, rightly ordered, serves the community
4. **Incomplete records**: Even fragmentary witness testifies to God's purposes
