## The Israelite Lineage and the Path to Dawud

Islamic engagement with the Judahite genealogy focuses on the lineage leading to Dawud (David), whom Islam recognizes as a major prophet and recipient of the Zabur (Psalms). The specific genealogical details are evaluated against Islamic prophetic standards.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### The Tribe of Judah (2:1-8)

Islam does not assign the same theological significance to tribal divisions as Judaism and Christianity. The Israelite tribes are acknowledged as part of the Bani Isra'il (Children of Israel), but the tribal system is not central to Islamic theology.

### The Line to Dawud (2:10-17)

The genealogy from Ram to Jesse to David traces the human ancestry of Dawud — a prophet whom the Quran describes as one to whom God gave "the kingdom and wisdom and taught him whatever He willed" (Al-Baqarah 2:251). Islamic tradition honors this lineage while maintaining that Dawud's authority derived from divine appointment, not tribal inheritance.

### Boaz and Ruth (2:11-12)

The inclusion of Ruth the Moabite in the Davidic lineage is noted by Islamic scholars as evidence that God's prophetic selection transcends ethnic boundaries — a principle central to Islam's universal message.

## Theological Significance

1. **Prophetic lineage**: The genealogy traces the path to Dawud, a Quranic prophet
2. **Divine appointment**: Prophetic authority comes from God, not tribal inheritance
3. **Universalism**: The inclusion of non-Israelites affirms God's universal selection
4. **Zabur connection**: The Davidic line leads to the recipient of revealed scripture
