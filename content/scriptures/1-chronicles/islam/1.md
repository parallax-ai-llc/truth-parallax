## The Prophetic Lineage from Adam

Islamic engagement with 1 Chronicles 1 centers on the genealogy's connection to the Quranic understanding of prophetic history. Islam affirms a continuous chain of prophets from Adam through Ibrahim (Abraham), and the Chronicles genealogy — while not accepted as preserved revelation — touches on figures central to Islamic faith.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Adam to Nuh (Noah) (1:1-4)

Islam affirms Adam as the first prophet and Nuh as a major prophet (*rasul*) sent to warn his people. The Quran devotes an entire surah (Surah Nuh, 71) to Noah's mission. The biblical genealogy's brief listing of names contrasts with the Quran's emphasis on prophetic message rather than lineage.

### The Spread of Nations (1:5-23)

Islamic tradition affirms the diversification of humanity from Noah's descendants. **Al-Tabari** provides extensive narratives about the post-flood spread of nations, drawing on both biblical and Arabian traditions.

### Ibrahim's Line (1:24-34)

Ibrahim (Abraham) is a central figure in Islam — the friend of God (*khalil Allah*), the builder of the Kaaba, and the father of both Isma'il (Ishmael) and Ishaq (Isaac). Islamic tradition notes that the biblical genealogy follows the line of Isaac rather than Ishmael, whom Islam considers the forefather of the Arab prophets.

## Theological Significance

1. **Universal prophetic history**: Islam affirms prophets were sent to every nation (Quran 35:24)
2. **Ibrahim as pivot**: The Abrahamic line divides into the Ishmaelite and Israelite branches
3. **Prophetic chain**: The genealogy traces a chain of divinely guided figures
4. **Tahrif concern**: Discrepancies with Islamic tradition are attributed to textual alteration
