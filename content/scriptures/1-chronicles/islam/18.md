## Dawud's God-Given Victories

Islamic engagement with David's military campaigns emphasizes divine agency in all victories. The Quran affirms Dawud's military prowess — particularly his defeat of Jalut (Goliath) — as evidence of God's direct intervention on behalf of His faithful servants.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Victory from God (18:6, 13)

The refrain "the LORD gave David victory" aligns with the Quranic principle: "And there is no victory except from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Wise" (Ali Imran 3:126). Islamic tradition attributes every military success to divine grant, not human strength.

### Justice in Rule (18:14)

David's administration of justice for all his people reflects the Quranic portrait of Dawud as a righteous judge: "O Dawud, indeed We have made you a successor upon the earth, so judge between the people in truth" (Sad 38:26). Islamic political theory requires the ruler to administer justice (*adl*) to all subjects.

### Consecrated Spoils (18:11)

David's dedication of war spoils to God resonates with the Islamic concept of *khums* (the fifth) — the portion of war spoils set aside for God's purposes (Al-Anfal 8:41).

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine agency**: All victory comes from God alone
2. **Justice as duty**: The ruler's primary obligation is to administer justice
3. **Consecration of wealth**: Material gains are partly dedicated to God's purposes
4. **Prophetic governance**: Dawud models the integration of military leadership with divine justice
