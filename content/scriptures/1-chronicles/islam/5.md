## Divine Judgment on Unfaithfulness

Islamic engagement with chapter 5 focuses on the Chronicler's verdict that the Transjordan tribes were exiled for unfaithfulness — a theme deeply consonant with the Quranic understanding of God's dealings with the Bani Isra'il.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Reuben's Lost Birthright (5:1-2)

The loss of privilege through sin resonates with the Quranic principle that divine favor depends on righteousness, not birth: "Whoever does righteousness, whether male or female, while being a believer — those will enter Paradise" (An-Nisa 4:124). Birthright without faithfulness is meaningless.

### Victory through Prayer (5:20)

The tribes' victory through crying out to God in battle affirms the Islamic understanding of *du'a* in times of crisis. The Quran records multiple instances of prophets and believers crying out to God in distress and receiving divine aid.

### Exile for Unfaithfulness (5:25-26)

The Chronicler's verdict — exile because of unfaithfulness — aligns with the Quranic narrative of the Bani Isra'il's repeated cycles of favor and punishment. The Quran states: "So We sent upon them the flood and locusts and lice and frogs and blood as distinct signs, but they were arrogant and were a criminal people" (Al-A'raf 7:133).

## Theological Significance

1. **Divine justice**: Unfaithfulness leads to consequences, regardless of heritage
2. **Du'a in crisis**: Crying out to God in battle models sincere supplication
3. **Quranic parallel**: The exile confirms the Quran's account of Israel's cycles of disobedience
4. **Individual accountability**: Privilege without righteousness avails nothing before God
