## The Prophetic Commission and the Temple Blueprint

Islamic engagement with David's charge to Solomon reads the narrative as the transmission of prophetic authority from one prophet-king to another. The divinely inspired temple plans affirm that sacred architecture follows divine revelation, not human invention.

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Wholehearted Devotion (28:9)

"Serve him with wholehearted devotion and with a willing mind." This charge resonates with the Islamic concept of *ikhlas* (sincerity) — the foundation of all acceptable worship. The Quran commands: "So worship Allah, being sincere to Him in religion" (Az-Zumar 39:2).

### Divine Blueprint (28:11-19)

The Spirit-given temple plans affirm that sacred architecture follows divine guidance. Islamic tradition similarly holds that the Kaaba was built according to divine instruction — Ibrahim and Isma'il raised its foundations under God's direction (Al-Baqarah 2:127).

### The Commission to Sulayman (28:6-7, 20)

Dawud's charge to Sulayman models the prophetic transmission of sacred knowledge. In Islam, each prophet builds upon the message of his predecessor, culminating in the final revelation to Muhammad. The principle of prophetic succession is central to Islamic theology.

## Theological Significance

1. **Ikhlas**: Sincerity of heart is the foundation of all worship
2. **Divine architecture**: Sacred buildings follow divine instruction, not human design
3. **Prophetic succession**: Each prophet transmits and builds upon the message of his predecessor
4. **Courage and faith**: Divine commission requires both human courage and trust in God
