## Divine Guidance in Governance and Warfare

Islamic engagement with David's victories emphasizes his practice of seeking divine guidance before action — a principle central to Islamic decision-making through *istikharah* (seeking God's guidance) and *shura* (consultation).

## Key Points of Islamic Engagement

### Seeking God's Guidance (14:10, 14)

David inquires of God before each battle. This practice resonates with the Islamic tradition of *istikharah* — a prayer seeking God's guidance before important decisions. The Prophet Muhammad taught: "When one of you is considering a matter, let him pray two rak'ahs of prayer, then say: O Allah, I seek Your guidance..."

### God-Given Victory (14:10-16)

"The LORD gave David victory wherever he went." Islamic theology attributes all success to God: "And there is no victory except from Allah" (Al-Anfal 8:10). Military victory, like every blessing, comes from divine grant, not human power.

### International Recognition (14:17)

David's fame spreading to all nations reflects God's exaltation of His faithful servants. The Quran acknowledges Dawud's elevated status: "And We gave him wisdom and discernment in speech" (Sad 38:20).

## Theological Significance

1. **Istikharah**: Seeking God's guidance before major decisions is a prophetic practice
2. **Divine victory**: All success comes from God alone
3. **Prophetic exaltation**: God raises His prophets to international recognition
4. **Tawhid in warfare**: Victory is attributed to God, never to human military power
