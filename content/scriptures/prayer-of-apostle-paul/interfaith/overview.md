## Overview

The Prayer of the Apostle Paul demonstrates a truth recognized across all religious traditions: that the spiritual life is sustained not by theology alone but by prayer — the direct, personal address to the divine. The prayer's combination of invocation, petition, and trust reflects a universal structure found in the Muslim du'a, the Hindu stuti, the Buddhist refuge prayer, and the Jewish tefillah. The brevity and intensity of this prayer remind us that across all traditions, the heart's cry to the divine is often more powerful than the most elaborate theological system.
