## Overview

The Prayer of the Apostle Paul participates in the universal practice of invocatory prayer — the practice of calling upon the divine for aid, guidance, and transformation. The text's invocation of Christ as guide and redeemer parallels the Hindu devotee's invocation of the guru or the chosen deity (ishta devata), the Buddhist's taking refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and the Muslim's opening invocation (bismillah) at the beginning of every significant action. The placement of the prayer at the beginning of a codex mirrors the universal practice of beginning sacred activities with an invocation.
