This section — "The Request for Gnosis and Redemption" — raises questions about genre, literary composition, and theological classification. Its relationship to other Nag Hammadi texts, to the heresiological reports of the Church Fathers, and to the broader religious landscape of late antiquity illuminates both the text's distinctiveness and its participation in shared traditions of thought.
