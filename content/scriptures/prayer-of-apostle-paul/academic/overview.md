## Overview

The Prayer of the Apostle Paul is studied for its placement on the front flyleaf of Codex I (the Jung Codex), its Valentinian theological vocabulary, and its evidence for Valentinian devotional practice. The title 'Psychagogue' (guide of souls) is attested in both Hermetic and Gnostic sources and reflects a specific understanding of Christ's salvific function: not as sacrificial victim but as spiritual guide. The prayer's attribution to Paul connects it to the broader Valentinian tradition of claiming Paul as the apostle whose teachings most closely aligned with Gnostic theology.
