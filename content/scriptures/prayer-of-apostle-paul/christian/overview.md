## Overview

For Christian readers, the Prayer of the Apostle Paul demonstrates the continuity between Valentinian and orthodox Christian prayer practices. The prayer's structure — invocation, petition, and trust — mirrors the structure of orthodox Christian prayer. The title 'Redeemer' is shared with orthodox Christology, and the prayer's urgency and intimacy are indistinguishable from orthodox devotion. The differences are in the theological vocabulary: 'Psychagogue,' 'Pleroma,' 'First-born' carry specifically Valentinian meanings. The prayer challenges the assumption that Gnostic Christians were cold intellectuals rather than warm devotees.
