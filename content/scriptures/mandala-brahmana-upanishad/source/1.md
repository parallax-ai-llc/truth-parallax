---
title: "Mandala Brahmana Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "mandala-brahmana-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om! The great Muni Yajnavalkya went to Aditya-loka, and saluting him said: "O revered sir, describe to me the Atman-tattva."

**2** Aditya replied: "I shall describe the eightfold Yoga. Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, and Samadhi are the eight limbs."

**3** Yama is of ten kinds: non-injury, truth, non-stealing, continence, compassion, sincerity, patience, firmness, temperance in food, and purity.

**4** Niyama is of ten kinds: tapas, contentment, belief in the Vedas, charity, worship of Ishvara, study of doctrines, modesty, intellect, japa, and vrata.

**5** There are eight postures: Svastika, Gomukha, Padma, Vira, Simha, Bhadra, Mukta, and Mayura.

**6** Pranayama is the control of the vital breaths. Rechaka is the expiration. Puraka is the inspiration. Kumbhaka is the retention. In Rechaka, the time of expiration is twice that of inspiration; in Kumbhaka, it is four times; and in Puraka it is the ordinary time.

**7** Pratyahara is the subjugation of the senses by the mind, which withdraws from them their natural tendency to go outward toward their objects and directs them inward toward the Atman.

**8** Dharana is the fixing of the mind in a place suitable for meditation. Dhyana is the meditation on Atman as being Chaitanya in the midst of Akasa.

**9** Samadhi is the state in which the mind, abandoning the difference between the meditator and the object of meditation, becomes of the form of the object alone.

**10** Thus the eightfold Yoga has been described. There are five obstacles to Yoga: desire, anger, the out-going breath, fear, and sleep. To the Yogin these are to be overcome by moderation in food, exertion, and by steadfastness.
