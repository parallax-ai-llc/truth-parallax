---
title: "Mandala Brahmana Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "mandala-brahmana-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Then Yajnavalkya asked: "O Bhagavan, what is the nature of Kaivalya, the state of absolute independence?" Aditya replied:

**2** The state of Kaivalya is the condition in which the Atman alone shines, freed from all adjuncts and limitations, freed from the bonds of Maya, and established in its own supreme glory.

**3** As the Akasa in the pot, when the pot is destroyed, merges in the great Akasa and becomes one with it, so the Jivatman, when the body is destroyed through knowledge, merges in Paramatman.

**4** As the water in a mirage, the silver in an oyster shell, and the serpent in a rope — all these appear through ignorance and disappear through knowledge — so the world appears through Avidya and disappears through Vidya.

**5** As the lamp that is placed in a pot illumines the pot, even though the pot has many holes, so the Atman illumines the body.

**6** The wise man, having known that the Atman is bodiless in bodies, stable among the unstable, and great and all-pervading, does not grieve.

**7** This Atman cannot be attained by the study of the Vedas, nor by intelligence, nor by much learning. He whom the Atman chooses, by him alone is the Atman attained.

**8** When all the desires that dwell in the heart are destroyed, then the mortal becomes immortal. When all the knots of the heart are cut asunder, then the mortal becomes immortal. This is the teaching.

**9** Thus did Aditya describe to Yajnavalkya the nature of Atman-Vidya and Kaivalya, which is the supreme state of liberation, the highest goal of all beings.

**10** Om! Thus ends the Mandala Brahmana Upanishad, as contained in the Shukla Yajur Veda. Om Shanti, Shanti, Shanti.
