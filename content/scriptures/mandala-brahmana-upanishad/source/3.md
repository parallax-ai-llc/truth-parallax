---
title: "Mandala Brahmana Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "mandala-brahmana-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Then Yajnavalkya asked Aditya: "O Bhagavan, describe the nature of the five Akasas." Aditya replied: "There are five Akasas: Akasa, Para-Akasa, Maha-Akasa, Surya-Akasa, and Parama-Akasa."

**2** That which is of the nature of darkness, which is like the darkness of closed eyes, is called Akasa. That in which the Akasa merges through Atman-Yoga is called Para-Akasa.

**3** That which has the lustre of the sun, moon, and fire, and which is of the nature of supreme brilliance, is called Maha-Akasa.

**4** That which is of the nature of the supreme brilliance, the supreme light that is incomparable, and which pervades all space, is called Surya-Akasa.

**5** That which is the Akasa of Akasas, which is the highest of all, which is filled with the supreme glory, is called Parama-Akasa.

**6** By meditating upon these five Akasas in order, the Yogin perceives in the heart the five forms of light. The first is like a fire-fly, the second is like the light of gems, the third is like the light of the moon, the fourth is like the light of the sun, and the fifth is like the blaze of lightning.

**7** Beyond these there appear the forms of Atman — formless radiance, the supreme light beyond all lights, which is called the light of Brahman.

**8** The Yogin who perceives this light attains liberation. He sees the Atman in all things and all things in the Atman. He becomes one with Brahman, the eternal, the pure, the free.

**9** Thus has been told the Brahmana of the third section. He who knows this is freed from the bonds of death.
