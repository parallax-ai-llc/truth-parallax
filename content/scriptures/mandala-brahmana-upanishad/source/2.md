---
title: "Mandala Brahmana Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "mandala-brahmana-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Then Yajnavalkya asked: "What are the seats of the Atman?" Aditya replied: "The right eye is the seat of Agni; the left eye is the seat of Soma. Agni and Soma form the two eyes."

**2** The Atman in the right eye is known as Indha. The Atman in the left eye is known as Indrani. They have their abode in the middle of the heart, in the Akasa of the heart.

**3** There is a mass of red flesh in the heart. In that there is a small white lotus, which blossoms like a red lotus in many petals. In the middle of it is the ocean of the mind.

**4** In the middle of that ocean is the subtle Akasa. In that subtle space, everything is founded. The Purusha that is in the heart is as small as a grain of rice or barley. He is the Ruler of all, the Lord of all; he rules all that exists.

**5** The form that is in the right eye is Rig Veda, is Brahma, is Aditya. The form that is in the left eye is Yajur Veda, is Vishnu, is Soma.

**6** The Yogin should always practise concentration upon the orb of light between the two eyes. In this there appears the Sushumna passage.

**7** When the mind attains the region beyond the senses, it perceives in the Akasa the brilliant light called Jyotirmaya, which is Paramatman.

**8** All these forms are but reflections of the one Atman, which is the witness, the eternal, the pure, the enlightened, the free, the real, the supreme, and the blissful.

**9** Thus has been told the Brahmana of the second section. He who knows this becomes one with Brahman.
