---
title: "Mandala Brahmana Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "mandala-brahmana-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Then Yajnavalkya asked: "O Bhagavan, who is the Adhyatma?" Aditya replied: "The Purusha that is in the Mandala of the sun is the Adhyatma. He has a golden beard and golden hair. He is exceedingly brilliant, even to the tips of his nails."

**2** His eyes are like the red lotus. His body is luminous as the red lotus. The smoke-like form is Avyakta. And what produces the mirage of the body is Maya.

**3** The internal Purusha in the heart is of the size of a thumb, like the flame of a lamp. He is of the form of all. He is the inner light.

**4** That which is of the nature of pure consciousness, that which is in the midst of the Prana, in the midst of the heart, in the midst of the light, in the midst of the Akasa — that is the Atman.

**5** One should meditate on the Atman, who is of the size of a thumb, who is in the interior of the body, who is like a light without smoke, who is the maker of all, who is the Lord of all, and who illumines all.

**6** The Jivatman, which is in the body, is surrounded by the Nadis. The Nadis are subtle channels. Among these, the chief are Ida, Pingala, and Sushumna.

**7** Sushumna is the support of the universe and the path of salvation. It extends from the Muladhara to the Brahmarandhra, and it is in the interior of the Meru.

**8** In the Sushumna there is a shining thread-like passage. In that there is a region called the luminous region. Those who are Yogins see it.

**9** The Yogin who has attained perfection through the practice of Yoga perceives this brilliant thread and through it attains liberation.

**10** Thus has been told the Brahmana of the fourth section. He who knows this is freed from all bonds.
