## Overview

The Mandala Brahmana Upanishad belongs to the Shukla Yajur Veda and is classified among the Yoga Upanishads. It presents an elaborate teaching on internal yoga practices centered on the vision of the inner sun (*antah-surya*) — the luminous sphere of consciousness visible in deep meditation. The text describes five stages of yogic practice corresponding to five mandalas (circles or spheres) of inner light, culminating in the direct vision of Brahman as infinite radiance.

## Structure

The Mandala Brahmana Upanishad consists of five sections (brahmanas):

1. **Brahmana 1 — The First Mandala**: The initial vision of inner light through yogic practice
2. **Brahmana 2 — The Second Mandala**: The expansion of inner luminosity and its forms
3. **Brahmana 3 — The Third Mandala**: The deepening of meditative vision
4. **Brahmana 4 — The Fourth Mandala**: The subtle forms of light and consciousness
5. **Brahmana 5 — The Fifth Mandala**: The supreme vision of Brahman as infinite radiance

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: The inner light as the radiance of Vishnu dwelling in the heart
- **Shaivism**: The five mandalas as progressive revelations of Shiva's luminous nature
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Inner light as the self-luminosity of Brahman perceived in meditation
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: The vision of light as God's gracious self-revelation to the meditator
- **Academic**: Important source for the history of yogic light-vision practices
- **Comparative**: Inner light experiences across contemplative traditions worldwide
