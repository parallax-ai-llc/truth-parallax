## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Dialogue of the Savior a transformation of Jewish creation traditions and wisdom dialogue forms. The text's cosmological questions — about the origin of the world, the nature of the body, and the meaning of existence — echo the concerns of Jewish wisdom literature (Job, Ecclesiastes) while developing them in a distinctly Gnostic direction. The dissolution of the body as a spiritual goal stands in sharp tension with the Jewish affirmation of embodied existence.
