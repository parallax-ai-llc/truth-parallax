## Overview

The Dialogue of the Savior has attracted scholarly attention for its composite literary structure, which combines elements of a creation myth, a cosmological vision, a wisdom dialogue, and a sayings collection — some of which may overlap with traditions found in the Gospel of Thomas. The fragmentary state of the manuscript complicates analysis, but scholars have identified at least three distinct literary layers. The text's prominent role for Mary Magdalene has been examined in the context of early Christian debates about women's authority, particularly in relation to the Gospel of Mary and the Gospel of Philip.
