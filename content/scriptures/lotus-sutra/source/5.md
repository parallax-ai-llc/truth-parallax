---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 5: The Parable of the Medicinal Herbs"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 5
---
**1** At that time the World-Honoured One said to Maha-Kashyapa and the other senior disciples: "Excellent, excellent! Kashyapa, you have spoken well of the true merits of the Tathagata. They are indeed as you say."

**2** "The Tathagata has immeasurable, boundless, incalculable merits, and if you were to spend immeasurable millions of kalpas trying to describe them, you could never finish."

**3** "Kashyapa, you should know that the Tathagata is the king of the Dharma. Nothing that he says is false. With regard to all the teachings, he employs wisdom and expedient means to expound them."

**4** "It is like rain falling from a great cloud. The rain moistens all the grasses, trees, thickets, and forests. Each plant receives the moisture according to its nature—the small plants receive a small amount, the medium plants a medium amount, and the great trees receive a great amount."

**5** "The rain of the single cloud is the same throughout, but the plants and trees each receive it differently according to their nature. The small herbs, the medium herbs, the great herbs, the small trees, the great trees—each absorbs moisture according to its capacity."

**6** "In the same way, the Dharma preached by the Tathagata is of one flavour—the flavour of liberation. But living beings, hearing this Dharma, practise it differently according to their different capacities."

**7** "Whether they are Sravakas, Pratyekabuddhas, or Bodhisattvas, each benefits from the rain of the Dharma in accordance with his own nature and capacity, just as the different herbs and trees each benefit from the rain according to their own nature."

**8** "The Tathagata knows the Dharma that is of one mark, one flavour, namely: the mark of liberation, the mark of separation from objects, the mark of extinction, the mark of ultimate Nirvana, of constant calm and extinction, which in the end returns to emptiness."

**9** "The Buddha, knowing all this, observes the desires that are in the minds of the multitude and guides them accordingly. For this reason he does not immediately preach to them the all-embracing wisdom."

**10** "Kashyapa, it is a rare thing that you are all able to know that the Tathagata preaches the Dharma appropriately to each, that he uses one rain to nourish all."
