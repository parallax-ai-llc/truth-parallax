---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 28: Encouragements of the Bodhisattva Universal Worthy"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 28
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattva Universal Worthy, famed for his freely exercised supernatural powers, his dignity and virtue, accompanied by great Bodhisattvas in immeasurable, boundless, incalculable numbers, arrived from the east.

**2** The lands that he passed through trembled and shook, lotus flowers rained down, and immeasurable hundreds of thousands of ten thousands of millions of varieties of music sounded.

**3** Arriving at Mount Gridhrakuta, he bowed his head to Shakyamuni Buddha and said: "World-Honoured One, in the land of the Buddha Jeweled Awesome Virtue King, from far away I heard the Lotus Sutra being preached. I came with this assembly of Bodhisattvas to listen."

**4** "I beg the World-Honoured One to preach it to us. After the Tathagata's extinction, how can a good man or good woman obtain this Lotus Sutra?"

**5** The Buddha said: "Universal Worthy, if a good man or good woman fulfils four conditions, then after the Tathagata's extinction he will be able to obtain this Lotus Sutra."

**6** "First, he must be protected and kept in mind by the Buddhas. Second, he must plant the roots of virtue. Third, he must enter the stage of correct determination. Fourth, he must resolve to save all living beings."

**7** "If a good man or good woman fulfils these four conditions, then after the Tathagata's extinction he will be certain to obtain this sutra."

**8** Universal Worthy said: "World-Honoured One, in the last five hundred years of the corrupt and evil age, if a person embraces this sutra, I will guard and protect him, free him from decline and harm."

**9** "If he is walking and reciting this sutra, I will mount my six-tusked white elephant king and with a host of great Bodhisattvas will go to where he is. I will show myself, make offerings, guard, and comfort him."

**10** "If anyone troubles or vexes a person who embraces this sutra, I, Universal Worthy, will punish him. World-Honoured One, wherever this sutra is preached, read, recited, copied, or the scrolls are kept, one should build a stupa and make offerings. There is no need to enshrine relics within it, for this sutra already contains the whole body of the Tathagata."
