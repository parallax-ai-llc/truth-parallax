---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 10: The Teacher of the Law"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 10
---
**1** At that time the World-Honoured One, through the Bodhisattva Medicine King, addressed the eighty thousand great beings: "Medicine King, do you see in this great assembly the innumerable heavenly beings, dragon kings, yakshas, gandharvas, asuras, garudas, kinnaras, mahoragas, human and non-human beings, as well as monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen?"

**2** "Among them, those who in the Buddha's presence hear one verse or one phrase of the Lotus Sutra and feel even a single moment of joy—I hereby bestow on all of them a prediction that they shall attain supreme perfect enlightenment."

**3** "After the Tathagata has passed into extinction, if there are persons who hear the Lotus Sutra, even one verse or one phrase, and feel a moment of joy, I bestow on them too a prediction of supreme enlightenment."

**4** "If anyone shall receive and hold, read and recite, expound and copy even a single verse of this Lotus Sutra, and shall look upon this sutra with the same reverence as the Buddha, offering various flowers, incense, necklaces, powdered incense, paste incense, burning incense, silken canopies, streamers, and banners—such persons have already made offerings to ten ten thousand million Buddhas."

**5** "Medicine King, you should know that these persons are great Bodhisattvas who have achieved supreme perfect enlightenment. Out of pity for living beings, they have vowed to be born in this evil world so that they may broadly expound this Lotus Sutra."

**6** "If there is anyone who asks what sort of living beings will be able to attain Buddhahood in future ages, you should show them that these are the ones who in future ages shall attain Buddhahood."

**7** "If there is a good man or good woman who, after the Tathagata has passed into extinction, wishes to expound this Lotus Sutra for the four kinds of believers, how should that person expound it?"

**8** "That good man or good woman should enter the Tathagata's room, put on the Tathagata's robe, and sit in the Tathagata's seat."

**9** "The Tathagata's room is the state of great compassion toward all living beings. The Tathagata's robe is the mind of gentleness and patience. The Tathagata's seat is the emptiness of all phenomena."

**10** "Dwelling securely in these, and then with a mind never lazy or neglectful, one should broadly preach this Lotus Sutra for the Bodhisattvas and the four kinds of believers."
