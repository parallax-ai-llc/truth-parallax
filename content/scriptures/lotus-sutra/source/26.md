---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 26: Dharani"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 26
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattva Medicine King rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, pressed his palms together, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, if there is a good man or good woman who can embrace the Lotus Sutra, whether reading it, reciting it, understanding its meaning, or practising it as the sutra directs, what blessings will that person gain?"

**2** The Buddha said: "If a good man or good woman makes offerings to Buddhas equal in number to the sands of eight hundred ten thousand million nayutas of Ganges Rivers, what do you think—will that person's blessings be great?" "Very great, World-Honoured One."

**3** "But if a person is able to embrace, read, recite, and understand the meaning of even a single verse of the Lotus Sutra, that person's blessings will be even greater."

**4** Then the Bodhisattva Medicine King said to the Buddha: "I will now give to those who preach the Dharma dharani spells to guard and protect them." He then pronounced the dharani.

**5** The Bodhisattva Brave Donor then spoke his dharani for the protection of those who preach the sutra. The heavenly king Vaishravana spoke his. The heavenly king Upholder of the Nation spoke his.

**6** Ten rakshasi women came before the Buddha and spoke in unison: "World-Honoured One, we too wish to guard and protect those who read and recite and embrace the Lotus Sutra and to spare them from decline and harm."

**7** They then pronounced their dharani and said: "If anyone should trouble or vex the preachers of the Dharma, his head will split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

**8** The Buddha said to the rakshasi women: "Excellent! If you can guard and protect those who receive and hold the mere name of the Lotus Sutra, your blessings will be immeasurable."

**9** "How much more so if you guard and protect those who embrace the entire sutra, and who make offerings to the sutra of flowers, incense, necklaces, powdered incense, paste incense, burning incense, silken canopies, streamers, banners, music."

**10** "Those who guard and protect this sutra have already made offerings to me and to the Tathagata Many Treasures. They have also made offerings to all the Buddhas of the ten directions."
