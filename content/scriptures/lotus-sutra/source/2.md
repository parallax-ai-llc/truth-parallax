---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 2: Expedient Means"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 2
---
**1** At that time the World-Honoured One calmly arose from samadhi and addressed Shariputra: "The wisdom of the Buddhas is infinitely profound and immeasurable. The door to this wisdom is difficult to understand and difficult to enter. Not one of the voice-hearers or pratyekabuddhas is able to comprehend it."

**2** "The reason is that the Buddha has personally attended a hundred, a thousand, ten thousand, a million, a countless number of Buddhas and has fully carried out an immeasurable number of religious practices. He has exerted himself bravely and vigorously, and his name is universally known."

**3** "He has realized the Law that is profound and never known before, and preaches it in accordance with what is appropriate, yet his intention is difficult to understand."

**4** "Shariputra, ever since I attained Buddhahood I have through various causes and conditions, through various similes and parables, broadly expounded my teachings and through countless expedient means have guided living beings."

**5** "The reason is that the Tathagata is fully possessed of both expedient means and the paramita of wisdom."

**6** "Shariputra, the wisdom of the Tathagata is broad and great, profound and far-reaching. His mind is immeasurable, his eloquence is unobstructed. He has fully mastered the power, the state of fearlessness, the meditation, the emancipation, and the samadhis."

**7** "He has deeply entered the boundless and has awakened to the Law never before attained."

**8** "Shariputra, I know that living beings have various desires, various attachments deeply rooted in their minds. Taking cognizance of this basic nature of theirs, I therefore use various causes and conditions, similes and parables, and the power of expedient means to preach to them."

**9** "Shariputra, the Tathagata appears in the world only for the sake of the one great matter."

**10** "The Buddhas, the World-Honoured Ones, wish to open the door of Buddha-wisdom to all living beings, to allow them to attain purity. They wish to show the Buddha-wisdom to living beings. They wish to cause living beings to awaken to the Buddha-wisdom. They wish to induce living beings to enter the path of Buddha-wisdom."

**11** "Shariputra, this is the one great reason for which the Buddhas appear in the world."

**12** "There is only one Buddha vehicle. There are not two or three. The teachings of the Sravakas and Pratyekabuddhas are all expedient means to lead beings to the one Buddha vehicle."
