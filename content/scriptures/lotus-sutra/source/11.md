---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 11: The Appearance of the Treasure Tower"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 11
---
**1** At that time a tower of seven treasures, five hundred yojanas in height and two hundred and fifty yojanas in width and depth, emerged from the earth and hung suspended in the air before the Buddha.

**2** It was adorned with all manner of precious objects. It had five thousand railings and ten million chambers. Innumerable streamers and banners adorned it, hung with jewelled necklaces.

**3** From within the treasure tower came a loud voice praising the Buddha: "Excellent, excellent! Shakyamuni, World-Honoured One! You are able to preach to the great assembly the Lotus Sutra of the Wonderful Law, the teaching of the great vehicle that is for the instruction of Bodhisattvas and the guardian care of the Buddhas."

**4** "So it is, so it is. All that you, Shakyamuni, World-Honoured One, have said is truthful and correct."

**5** The Bodhisattva Great Joy of Preaching asked the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, for what reason has this treasure tower risen from the earth, and why does this voice issue from its midst?"

**6** The Buddha said: "Inside this treasure tower is the complete body of a Tathagata. Long ago, in the past, immeasurable thousands of ten thousands of millions of worlds to the east, in a land called Treasure Purity, there was a Buddha called Many Treasures."

**7** "When that Buddha was practising the Bodhisattva way, he made a great vow: 'After I have become a Buddha and passed into extinction, wherever in the ten-direction lands the Lotus Sutra is preached, my treasure tower shall appear so that I may hear the sutra and testify to it.'"

**8** Then the World-Honoured One, wishing to open the treasure tower, with his right finger unlatched the door. There was a great sound. And all the assembly saw the Tathagata Many Treasures seated on a lion throne within the tower, his body whole and unimpaired, sitting in meditation.

**9** The Buddha Many Treasures shared half of his seat with Shakyamuni Buddha, and the two Tathagatas sat side by side in the treasure tower.

**10** The Buddha then lifted the entire assembly up into the air by his supernatural powers, and spoke in a great voice: "Who is able to broadly preach the Lotus Sutra in this world? Now is the time to do so, for before long the Tathagata will enter Nirvana. He wishes to entrust this Lotus Sutra to someone so that it may be preserved."
