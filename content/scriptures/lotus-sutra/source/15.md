---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 15: Emerging from the Earth"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 15
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattvas who had come from other lands, more in number than the sands of eight Ganges Rivers, rose from their seats, pressed their palms together, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, permit us, after the Buddha's extinction, to diligently practise, embrace, read, recite, copy, and make offerings to this sutra in this world."

**2** The Buddha said: "Desist, good men! There is no need for you to guard and embrace this sutra. Why? Because in my world itself there are Bodhisattvas as numerous as the sands of sixty thousand Ganges Rivers, and each of those Bodhisattvas has a retinue also as numerous as the sands of sixty thousand Ganges Rivers."

**3** When the Buddha said this, the earth in the lands of the three-thousand-great-thousand world trembled and split open, and from it emerged immeasurable thousands of ten thousands of millions of Bodhisattvas simultaneously.

**4** Their bodies were golden-hued, with the thirty-two features and immeasurable radiance. Previously they had all been dwelling in the world of empty space underneath this world.

**5** The Bodhisattvas, having heard the voice of Shakyamuni Buddha, came up from below. Each one led his own great assembly, and they came to the Wonderful Stupa of Treasures, where the two World-Honoured Ones sat.

**6** When they arrived, they went before the two World-Honoured Ones and bowed at their feet. They also went to the Buddhas seated on the lion thrones under the jewelled trees and bowed to them.

**7** Four leaders headed all these Bodhisattvas: Superior Practices, Boundless Practices, Pure Practices, and Firmly Established Practices.

**8** The Bodhisattva Maitreya and the other Bodhisattvas were filled with doubt. They thought: "In the immeasurable, boundless hundreds, thousands, ten thousands, millions of kalpas, we have never seen these Bodhisattvas before."

**9** Maitreya asked the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, this great multitude of immeasurable Bodhisattvas—where did they come from? Who taught them? Under whose teachings did they first set their minds on enlightenment?"

**10** The Buddha said: "These Bodhisattvas are ones whom I converted and led since the time when I first attained Buddhahood. I trained their minds and caused them to aspire to the way."
