---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 4: Belief and Understanding"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 4
---
**1** At that time the venerable elders Subhuti, Maha-Katyayana, Maha-Kashyapa, and Maha-Maudgalyayana, hearing from the Buddha this Dharma which they had never heard before, the prediction that Shariputra would attain supreme perfect enlightenment, were struck with wonder and joy.

**2** They rose from their seats, adjusted their robes, bared their right shoulders, knelt on their right knees, pressed their palms together with single mind, and spoke to the Buddha: "We are the senior leaders of the monks, already worn out with age. We believed we had attained Nirvana and had no further task to perform. We made no effort to seek supreme perfect enlightenment."

**3** "But now, hearing this prediction of Buddhahood bestowed on Shariputra, our minds are dancing with joy. We have gained what we never had before. We never expected to hear such a wonderful Dharma today."

**4** "It is as if a young man left his father and went to another land, where he lived for many years in poverty. The father meanwhile became very wealthy. The father constantly thought of his son and grieved."

**5** "After many years, the son, wandering from place to place, by chance came to his father's city. The father recognized his son, but the son did not recognize his father."

**6** "The father sent messengers, but the son, seeing the wealthy household, was frightened and ran away, thinking they would imprison him. The father then used an expedient means."

**7** "He sent servants in shabby clothes to hire the son as a menial worker, sweeping dung. The son was glad to take the work. Gradually the father promoted him, giving him greater responsibilities, until the son managed the entire estate."

**8** "But still the son thought of himself as a poor worker and did not know he was the heir. Only at the end of his life did the father gather the assembly and declare: 'This is my son. Everything I have is his.'"

**9** "The son was overwhelmed with joy, thinking: 'Without any seeking on my part, these treasures have now come to me.'"

**10** "The great wealthy elder is the Tathagata. We are all the children of the Buddha. The Tathagata always said we were his children, but because of the three kinds of suffering, in the midst of births and deaths we experienced burning anguish."

**11** "The dung-sweeping work is the practice of the lesser vehicle. The treasures are the supreme perfect enlightenment. We practised the lesser vehicle, thinking that was sufficient, not knowing that we were heirs to the treasure of the Buddha."

**12** "Now we know: the Tathagata has always regarded us as his children and has been waiting patiently for us to be ready to receive our inheritance—the one Buddha-vehicle."
