---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 9: Prophecies Conferred on Learners and Adepts"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 9
---
**1** At that time Ananda and Rahula had this thought: "We have been thinking to ourselves, if only we could receive such predictions!" They rose from their seats, went before the Buddha, and bowed their heads to the ground.

**2** The Buddha said to Ananda: "In a future existence you will become a Buddha called King of Self-Control and Penetration with Wisdom Like the Mountains and Seas. You shall make offerings to sixty-two million Buddhas, guarding and upholding their correct Dharma."

**3** The Buddha said to Rahula: "In a future existence you will become a Buddha called Stepping on Seven-Jeweled Lotus Flowers."

**4** At that time the World-Honoured One, wishing to encourage the two thousand learners, spoke to them, saying: "Do you see Ananda and Rahula? They have received predictions. All of you, too, shall in future ages become Buddhas."

**5** The two thousand learners, hearing this, were filled with joy and spoke in verse, praising the Buddha.

**6** At that time the nun Mahaprajapati, together with the nun Yashodhara and their retinues, went before the Buddha. The Buddha said: "Why do you stand there with worried looks? Are you thinking that I have not mentioned your names in my predictions?"

**7** "Mahaprajapati, in a future age you shall become a Bodhisattva and then a Buddha called Gladly Seen by All Living Beings."

**8** "Yashodhara, in a future age you shall become a Bodhisattva and then a Buddha called Endowed with a Thousand Ten Thousand Glowing Marks."

**9** The nuns and their retinues, hearing their predictions, were filled with immeasurable joy. They spoke in verse: "World-Honoured One, leader and teacher, you bring tranquillity to heavenly and human beings. We have heard these predictions and our minds are at peace and satisfied."

**10** At that time the great assembly, having heard that all—monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, Sravakas, Pratyekabuddhas, and Bodhisattvas—would in the end attain Buddhahood, were filled with great joy, and their bodies were bathed in light.
