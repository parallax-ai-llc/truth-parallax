---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 27: The Former Affairs of King Wonderful Adornment"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 27
---
**1** At that time the Buddha said to the great assembly: "In the past, immeasurable kalpas ago, there was a Buddha called Cloud Thunder Sound Constellation King Flower Wisdom, and his land was called Adorned with Light."

**2** "In the reign of that Buddha there was a king named Wonderful Adornment. That king's wife was called Pure Virtue, and he had two sons named Pure Storehouse and Pure Eye."

**3** "These two sons possessed great supernatural powers, merit, virtue, and wisdom. They had long practised the way of the Bodhisattva—giving, morality, patience, energy, meditation, wisdom, and expedient means."

**4** "Their mother, Pure Virtue, said to the two sons: 'Your father believes in non-Buddhist teachings. You should go to him and persuade him to visit the Buddha Cloud Thunder Sound Constellation King Flower Wisdom.'"

**5** "The two sons displayed various supernatural transformations before their father. They rose into the air, walked on water, produced fire and water from their bodies, reduced their bodies to tiny size and enlarged them again."

**6** "The king, seeing these supernatural powers, was filled with great joy and said: 'Who is your teacher? Whose disciples are you?' The sons said: 'Our teacher is the Buddha Cloud Thunder Sound Constellation King Flower Wisdom.'"

**7** "The king said: 'I wish to go and see this Buddha.' The sons said: 'Father, you should go to him and make offerings.'"

**8** "The king went with his retinue and approached that Buddha. When he saw the Buddha, seated on the lion throne, his body marked with the thirty-two features, resplendent with light, the king was filled with great joy."

**9** "He bowed at the Buddha's feet and said: 'World-Honoured One, what roots of virtue has my queen planted that she has gained such sons who possess these supernatural powers?' The Buddha told him all their past causes and conditions."

**10** "The king then took off his necklace of pearls and offered it to the Buddha, saying: 'I take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha.' He entrusted the kingdom to his brother and, together with his wife, sons, and retinue, went forth to practise the way under that Buddha."
