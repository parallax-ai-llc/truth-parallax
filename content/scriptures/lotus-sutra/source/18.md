---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 18: The Benefits of Responding with Joy"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 18
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattva Maitreya said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, if a good man or good woman hears this Lotus Sutra and responds with joy, what fortune does that person gain?"

**2** The Buddha said to Maitreya: "After the Tathagata has passed into extinction, suppose there is a monk, a nun, a layman, a laywoman, or any other wise person, young or old, who, having heard this sutra, responds with joy."

**3** "After leaving the Dharma assembly, that person goes elsewhere—to a dwelling, a field, a town, a village, or a country—and tells what he has heard. A second person, hearing it, responds with joy and passes the teaching on. A third person hears it, and so on, until it reaches a fiftieth person."

**4** "Maitreya, the benefits gained by the fiftieth person who hears and responds with joy—I will now describe them."

**5** "Suppose a person gives alms to all the beings in four hundred ten thousand million asamkhya worlds, providing them with all the pleasures they desire, until they are all old. And then he teaches them to enter the stream of the Dharma."

**6** "Maitreya, what do you think? Are the benefits gained by this donor great or not?" Maitreya said: "Very great, World-Honoured One. Immeasurable and boundless."

**7** "But the benefits of that donor do not match one hundredth, one thousandth, one hundred thousand ten thousandth part of the benefits of the fiftieth person who hears a single verse of the Lotus Sutra and responds with joy."

**8** "How much more so for the first person who, in the assembly, hears and responds with joy! The benefits of such a person are greater still, immeasurable, boundless, beyond comparison."

**9** "Therefore, Maitreya, you should know that those who encourage others to sit down and listen to this sutra gain immeasurable benefits. Even giving away one's seat so that another may hear the sutra gains immeasurable merit."

**10** "How much more so for those who wholeheartedly listen, read, recite, and expound this sutra, and practise it as the sutra directs!"
