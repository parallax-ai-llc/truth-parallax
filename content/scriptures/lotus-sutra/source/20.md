---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 20: The Bodhisattva Never Disparaging"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 20
---
**1** At that time the Buddha said to the Bodhisattva Gaining Great Authority: "In the remote past, immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable asamkhya kalpas ago, there was a Buddha called Awesome Sound King."

**2** "After that Buddha had passed into extinction, in the age of the Counterfeit Law, monks of overbearing arrogance exercised great authority."

**3** "At that time there was a Bodhisattva monk called Never Disparaging. Why was he called Never Disparaging? Because whatever monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen he happened to meet, he would bow to all of them and speak words of praise."

**4** "He would say: 'I have profound reverence for you. I would never dare treat you with disparagement or arrogance. Why? Because you are all practising the Bodhisattva way and are certain to attain Buddhahood.'"

**5** "This monk did not devote his time to reading or reciting the sutras, but simply went around bowing to people. If he saw anyone in the distance, he would go to them and bow, saying: 'I would never dare disparage you, because you are all certain to become Buddhas.'"

**6** "Among the four kinds of believers there were those who grew angry and resentful and abused him, saying: 'Where does this senseless monk come from? He says, "I would never disparage you" and predicts that we will become Buddhas! We have no need of such vain and irresponsible predictions!'"

**7** "Some struck him with sticks and staves, pelted him with tiles and stones. But he would run to a safe distance and still call out in a loud voice: 'I would never dare disparage you! You are all certain to become Buddhas!'"

**8** "Because he always spoke these words, the overbearing monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen called him Never Disparaging."

**9** "When this monk was about to die, he heard in the sky the twenty thousand ten thousand million verses of the Lotus Sutra that had been preached by the Buddha Awesome Sound King, and he was able to accept and hold them all. He immediately gained purity of the six sense organs."

**10** "Gaining Great Authority, you should understand that that Bodhisattva Never Disparaging was none other than I myself. Those who in that era cursed and abused me—because they had slandered the sutra—for two hundred million kalpas they never encountered a Buddha. But because of the power of the Lotus Sutra, they were eventually led to supreme enlightenment."
