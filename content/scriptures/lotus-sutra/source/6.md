---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 6: Bestowal of Prophecy"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 6
---
**1** At that time the World-Honoured One, having finished speaking these verses, addressed the great assembly, saying words like these: "This disciple of mine, Maha-Kashyapa, in future existences will be able to enter the presence of three hundred ten thousand million Buddhas, World-Honoured Ones, and will make offerings to them."

**2** "In his last existence he will be able to become a Buddha called Light Bright Tathagata, his land will be called Light Virtue, and his kalpa will be called Great Adornment."

**3** At that time Maha-Kashyapa, overwhelmed with joy, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, and bowed to the ground with his face at the Buddha's feet.

**4** Then the World-Honoured One bestowed predictions of future Buddhahood upon Subhuti, Maha-Katyayana, and Maha-Maudgalyayana. He said: "Subhuti, in future ages, after he has made offerings to three hundred ten thousand million nayutas of Buddhas, he shall become a Buddha called Name Sign."

**5** "Maha-Katyayana, in future ages, shall become a Buddha called Jambunada Gold Light."

**6** "Maha-Maudgalyayana, in future ages, shall become a Buddha called Tamalapattra Sandalwood Fragrance."

**7** Each of these great Sravakas, upon hearing their predictions, was filled with immeasurable joy. Each rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and praised the Buddha's compassion.

**8** They said: "World-Honoured One, we now understand. It is as if a person were to come to a close friend's house and, being drunk with wine, fall asleep. The friend, having to go out on official business, sewed a priceless jewel inside the man's garment and left."

**9** "The man, not knowing about the jewel, wandered in poverty and hardship for many years, until one day his friend found him and told him about the jewel."

**10** "Just so, World-Honoured One, in the past you taught us the way of the Bodhisattva, but we forgot and did not realize it. We practised the lesser vehicle, thinking ourselves content with a small portion. But now the Tathagata awakens us, saying: 'This is not true extinction. Strive for the supreme wisdom of the Buddha!'"
