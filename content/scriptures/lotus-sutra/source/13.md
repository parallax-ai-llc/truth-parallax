---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 13: Encouraging Devotion"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 13
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattva Medicine King and the Bodhisattva Great Joy of Preaching, together with a retinue of twenty thousand Bodhisattvas, all in the presence of the Buddha, took this vow:

**2** "We beg the World-Honoured One not to worry. After the Buddha has passed into extinction, we will honour, embrace, read, recite, and preach this sutra."

**3** "In the evil ages to come, living beings will be fewer in good qualities and many will be overbearing and proud, greedy for offerings, increasing their roots of unwholesomeness, moving far away from liberation."

**4** "Although it will be difficult to teach and convert them, we will summon up the power of great patience and will read and recite this sutra, embrace, preach, and copy it, offering it various kinds of alms, never begrudging our bodies or our lives."

**5** Then five hundred Arhats in the assembly who had received predictions spoke to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, we too will make a vow. In other lands, we will broadly preach this sutra."

**6** Then eight thousand learners and adepts who had received predictions rose from their seats and made this vow: "World-Honoured One, we too will broadly preach this sutra in other lands. Why? Because in this evil world people are corrupt, filled with evil, lacking in good, overbearing and proud."

**7** The Buddha's aunt, the nun Mahaprajapati, and the nun Yashodhara, together with their retinues, also rose and made this vow, speaking in a single voice.

**8** The Buddha gazed at the immeasurable Bodhisattvas and said: "These Bodhisattvas have already in the presence of immeasurable hundreds of thousands of ten thousands of millions of Buddhas practised the Bodhisattva way. They will be able to guard and uphold and read and recite and broadly preach this sutra."

**9** "In the evil age to come, if there is anyone who embraces this sutra, that person should know that he is an emissary of the Buddha, sent by the Buddha to carry out the Buddha's work."

**10** "Those who are able to uphold this sutra in the evil age to come, the Buddha extends his hand to pat their heads, just as a father pats the head of his child."
