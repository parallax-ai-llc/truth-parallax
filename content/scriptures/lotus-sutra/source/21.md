---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 21: Supernatural Powers of the Tathagata"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 21
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattvas who had emerged from the earth, as numerous as the sands of a thousand worlds, all in the Buddha's presence with single mind pressed their palms together, gazed at the World-Honoured One's face, and said: "World-Honoured One, after the Buddha's extinction, we will broadly preach this sutra."

**2** At that time the World-Honoured One, in the presence of Manjushri and the others, displayed his great supernatural powers.

**3** He put out his broad and long tongue until it reached upward to the Brahma-heaven. And from all his pores he emitted immeasurable, countless beams of light, illuminating all the worlds in the ten directions.

**4** All the Buddhas seated on lion thrones under the jewelled trees likewise put out their tongues and emitted beams of light.

**5** After Shakyamuni Buddha and the other Buddhas had displayed these supernatural powers for a hundred thousand years, they drew in their tongues, coughed in unison, and in unison snapped their fingers. These two sounds filled all the Buddha-worlds in the ten directions.

**6** The earth trembled and shook in six ways. The living beings in those worlds—heavenly beings, dragons, yakshas, gandharvas, asuras, garudas, kinnaras, mahoragas, human and non-human beings—all saw in this world Shakyamuni Buddha and the Tathagata Many Treasures seated together in the treasure tower.

**7** They also saw the immeasurable Bodhisattvas and the four kinds of believers reverently surrounding Shakyamuni Buddha.

**8** The Buddha said to the Bodhisattva Superior Practices: "The supernatural powers of the Buddhas are as immeasurable as this. Yet if I were to use these supernatural powers for immeasurable kalpas to describe the benefits of this sutra, I could not exhaust them."

**9** "To put it briefly: all the doctrines of the Tathagata, all the supernatural powers of the Tathagata, all the treasure houses of the Tathagata, all the profound affairs of the Tathagata—all are proclaimed, revealed, and clearly expounded in this sutra."

**10** "Therefore, after the Tathagata has passed into extinction, you should wholeheartedly embrace, read, recite, explain, and copy it, and practise it as directed. In any land, wherever a person accepts, holds, reads, recites, explains, copies, or practises it as directed, that place is the place of the Buddha's enlightenment."
