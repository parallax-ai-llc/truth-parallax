---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 12: Devadatta"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 12
---
**1** At that time the Buddha addressed the Bodhisattvas and the heavenly and human beings of the four kinds: "In the past I sought the Lotus Sutra without ever being lazy or negligent. For many kalpas I was a king who vowed to seek the unsurpassed Bodhi."

**2** "At that time there was a seer who came and said to the king: 'I have a teaching called the Lotus Sutra of the Wonderful Law. If you will not go against me, I will expound it to you.'"

**3** "The king, hearing the words of the seer, danced for joy. He immediately accompanied the seer, providing for his needs, gathering fruit, drawing water, collecting firewood, preparing food, even offering his own body as a seat and a bed for the seer."

**4** "He served the seer in this way for a thousand years, all for the sake of the Dharma, never allowing himself to feel weariness."

**5** "Monks, the king at that time was I myself. And the seer was the person who is now Devadatta. Because Devadatta was a good friend to me, he enabled me to become fully endowed with the six paramitas. For this reason, Devadatta shall in a future age become a Buddha."

**6** At that time the World-Honoured One said: "Devadatta, after immeasurable kalpas have passed, will become a Buddha called Heavenly King."

**7** At that time the Bodhisattva Manjushri was seated on a thousand-petaled lotus flower, and he rose from the great ocean. He said to the assembled Bodhisattvas: "In the ocean I have constantly preached the Lotus Sutra."

**8** The Bodhisattva Wisdom Accumulated asked: "Is there any being in that ocean who, through this sutra, has swiftly attained Buddhahood?"

**9** Manjushri said: "There is the daughter of the Dragon King Sagara. She is only eight years old, yet she has wisdom and sharp faculties. She has mastered dharanis. She is able in an instant to attain Buddhahood."

**10** Then the dragon girl appeared before the assembly, took a precious jewel, and offered it to the Buddha. The Buddha accepted it. The dragon girl then in an instant transformed herself into a male, carried out all the Bodhisattva practices, and in the southern world called Spotless became a Buddha, preaching the Dharma to all beings."
