---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 14: Peaceful Practices"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 14
---
**1** At that time the Bodhisattva Manjushri said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, how should a Bodhisattva who has just set his mind on enlightenment preach this sutra in the evil ages to come?"

**2** The Buddha said to Manjushri: "If a Bodhisattva wishes to preach this sutra in the evil age, he should abide securely in four things. First, he should abide in the practices and associations of the Bodhisattva."

**3** "What are the Bodhisattva's practices? The Bodhisattva should be patient, gentle, and agreeable, never violent or alarmed. He should observe all things with equanimity, seeing them as they truly are, not acting on the basis of false views."

**4** "He should not hold discussions with the followers of the lesser vehicle, or with those who hold wrong views. He should not debate with them or compete with them."

**5** "He should teach the Dharma calmly and without discrimination. He should not disparage other teachers of the Dharma, nor point out the shortcomings of other people."

**6** "He should not refer to other monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen by name when criticizing them. He should not praise them by name when flattering them."

**7** "Second, the Bodhisattva should observe all dharmas as empty, as being in their true nature, as not arising, not going forward, not retreating, not turning. This is the second thing in which the Bodhisattva should abide."

**8** "Third, the Bodhisattva should not harbour jealousy or be deceitful. He should not belittle those who study the Buddha way. He should not search out their shortcomings."

**9** "Fourth, the Bodhisattva should give rise to a mind of great compassion toward all beings. He should think of the Tathagata as a compassionate father and of the Bodhisattvas as great teachers."

**10** "A Bodhisattva who abides securely in these four things will not fail in his preaching. The heavenly beings, dragons, and others will constantly guard and protect him."
