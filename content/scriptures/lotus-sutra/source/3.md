---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 3: Simile and Parable"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 3
---
**1** At that time Shariputra, his mind dancing with joy, immediately stood up, pressed his palms together, gazed at the face of the World-Honoured One and said: "Now I have heard from the World-Honoured One this voice of the Dharma, and my heart is filled with joy, having gained what I never had before."

**2** "Why do I say this? Because in the past, when I heard a Dharma of this kind from the Buddha and saw the Bodhisattvas receive predictions of future Buddhahood, I and the others felt that we had no part in this and were deeply grieved at losing the immeasurable knowledge and insight of the Tathagata."

**3** The Buddha said to Shariputra: "I will now use a parable to make this meaning clearer. Suppose there was a wealthy elder whose house was very large. The house had only one gate, and many people lived inside—a hundred, two hundred, even five hundred."

**4** "The halls and rooms were old and decaying, the walls crumbling, the pillars rotted. Then suddenly fire broke out on all sides, and the house was engulfed in flames."

**5** "The elder's sons, ten, twenty, or even thirty, were inside the house. The elder, seeing the fire raging on all sides, was greatly alarmed and thought: 'I am capable of escaping safely through the burning gate, but my sons are inside enjoying themselves and are not aware of the fire. They do not know that the house is on fire and feel no urgency.'"

**6** "He called to them, but the boys, absorbed in their games, did not believe him and were not frightened. They had no intention of leaving. The elder then thought of an expedient means."

**7** "He said to the boys: 'Outside the gate there are the playthings you have always wanted—goat-carts, deer-carts, and ox-carts. Come out quickly and I will give them to you!'"

**8** "The boys, hearing of these carts, rushed out of the burning house. The elder, seeing them safely out, sat at the crossroads and said: 'I am now the happiest of persons!'"

**9** "Then the boys said: 'Father, give us the carts you promised.' And the elder gave each of them not a goat-cart or deer-cart or ox-cart, but a great white ox-cart, adorned with jewels, far surpassing what they had asked for."

**10** "Shariputra, that elder is the Tathagata. The burning house is the triple world. The sons absorbed in play are the beings lost in the pleasures of the world. The three kinds of carts are the three vehicles—of the Sravakas, Pratyekabuddhas, and Bodhisattvas. The great white ox-cart is the one Buddha-vehicle."

**11** "There is no falsehood in the elder's expedient means, for he used the three carts to get the sons out of the burning house, and then gave them something far greater than what was promised."

**12** "Just so, the Tathagata uses the three vehicles as expedient means to draw beings out of the burning house of the triple world, and then gives them the one, supreme Buddha-vehicle."
