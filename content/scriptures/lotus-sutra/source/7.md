---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 7: The Parable of the Phantom City"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 7
---
**1** The Buddha said to the monks: "Suppose there is a dangerous and bad road, five hundred yojanas long, steep and difficult, a wild and deserted area inhabited by many evil beasts. A large group of people wish to travel this road to reach a place where there is a great treasure."

**2** "Among them there is a guide, wise and experienced, who knows the road well. He leads the people along. But midway, the travellers become exhausted, weary, and frightened. They say, 'We cannot go on. We wish to turn back.'"

**3** "The guide, a man of many expedient means, thinks: 'How pitiful! How can they want to turn back and miss the great treasure?' He uses his supernatural powers to conjure up a city in the midst of the dangerous road."

**4** "He tells the travellers: 'Do not be afraid. This great city has appeared before you. You may enter it and rest and do as you please. When you have rested, if you wish to proceed to the treasure, you may do so.'"

**5** "The travellers enter the phantom city and are greatly relieved, thinking they have escaped the dangerous road. When they have rested and their weariness has been dispelled, the guide causes the phantom city to disappear."

**6** "He says: 'Come, all of you. The treasure is nearby. I created this city merely as a resting place. It was not the destination.'"

**7** "Monks, the Tathagata is like that guide. The long dangerous road is the cycle of birth and death. The weary travellers are the sentient beings. The phantom city is the Nirvana of the Sravakas and Pratyekabuddhas."

**8** "The Tathagata sees that beings are exhausted by the sufferings of birth, aging, sickness, and death, and so he teaches the expedient Nirvana, saying: 'You have escaped suffering. You have done what needed to be done.'"

**9** "But when they have rested, the Tathagata reveals to them that this Nirvana is not the final destination. He says: 'Your task is not yet complete. You are near the Buddha-wisdom. Observe and consider—the Nirvana you have attained is not the true one.'"

**10** "It is merely an expedient means of the Tathagata, who divides the one Buddha-vehicle and teaches three. You must press on to the true destination—the supreme perfect enlightenment of a Buddha."
