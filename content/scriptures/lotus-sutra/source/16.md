---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 16: The Life Span of the Tathagata"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 16
---
**1** At that time the Buddha addressed the Bodhisattvas and all the great assembly: "Good men, you must believe and understand the truthful words of the Tathagata." Again he said to the assembly: "You must believe and understand the truthful words of the Tathagata." Once more he said to the assembly: "You must believe and understand the truthful words of the Tathagata."

**2** At that time the Bodhisattvas, with Maitreya as their leader, pressed their palms together and said: "World-Honoured One, we beg you to speak. We will believe and accept the Buddha's words."

**3** The Buddha said: "Listen well, all of you, to the secret and supernatural powers of the Tathagata. All the beings of the world—heavenly beings, humans, and asuras—believe that the present Shakyamuni Buddha left the palace of the Shakyas, went to the place of practice, and there attained supreme enlightenment."

**4** "But, good men, it has been immeasurable, boundless hundreds, thousands, ten thousands, millions of nayutas of kalpas since I in fact attained Buddhahood."

**5** "Suppose a person were to take five hundred thousand ten thousand million nayuta asamkhya three-thousand-great-thousand worlds, and grind them to dust. He passes through five hundred thousand ten thousand million nayuta asamkhya lands to the east and then drops one grain of dust. He continues on eastward and drops another. In this way he exhausts all the grains of dust."

**6** "What do you think? Can the number of those worlds be imagined or calculated?" Maitreya said: "No, World-Honoured One."

**7** "Good men, the number of kalpas since I attained Buddhahood surpasses even that number by hundreds, thousands, ten thousands, millions of nayutas of asamkhya kalpas. From that time I have been constantly in this world, preaching the Dharma, teaching and converting."

**8** "But when beings have little virtue, I say: 'Monks, the Tathagata is hard to encounter.' Beings who hear these words will surely realize how rare is the chance to meet a Buddha and will take serious thought and engrave the Buddha's teaching in their minds."

**9** "Therefore the Tathagata, though he does not in fact pass into extinction, says that he does. This is an expedient means used by the Tathagata to teach and convert."

**10** "It is like a physician who is wise and skilful, whose sons drink poison in his absence. When he returns, he offers them medicine. Some take it and are cured. Those who do not take it—he goes away so that they will think he is dead and, in grief, finally take the medicine and be cured."
