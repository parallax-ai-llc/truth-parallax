---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 1: Introduction"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 1
---
**1** Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was staying at Rajagriha, on the mountain called Gridhrakuta, together with a great assembly of twelve thousand monks. All were Arhats whose outflows had ended.

**2** There were also two thousand monks, both those who had more to learn and those who had completed their learning. The nun Mahaprajapati was there with a retinue of six thousand. And Rahula's mother, the nun Yashodhara, was also present with her retinue.

**3** There were eighty thousand Bodhisattva-mahasattvas present, all of whom had attained dharanis and the power of eloquence and turned the irreversible wheel of the Dharma.

**4** At that time the World-Honoured One, surrounded by the four kinds of believers, received offerings and was treated with reverence. For the sake of the Bodhisattvas he preached the Great Vehicle sutra called Innumerable Meanings, a teaching for the instruction of Bodhisattvas that is guarded and kept in mind by the Buddhas.

**5** When the Buddha had finished preaching this sutra, he sat with legs crossed in the lotus position and entered into the samadhi called the Station of Innumerable Meanings. His body and mind were motionless.

**6** At that time heaven rained down mandarava flowers and great mandarava flowers, manjushaka flowers and great manjushaka flowers, scattering them over the Buddha and the great assembly.

**7** At that time the Buddha emitted a ray of light from the tuft of white hair between his eyebrows, one of his special characteristics, lighting up eighteen thousand worlds in the eastern direction.

**8** In these worlds there was no place that the light did not reach, from the Avici hell below to the Akanishtha heaven above. And in these worlds the six paths of beings could all be seen.

**9** The Bodhisattva Maitreya had this thought: "Now the World-Honoured One has manifested this miraculous sign. For what reason has he done so?"

**10** The Bodhisattva Manjushri said: "I believe the Buddha is about to expound a great Dharma, to rain down a great Dharma rain, to sound the great Dharma conch, to beat the great Dharma drum, to set forth the meaning of the great Dharma."

**11** "Good men, in the past, in the presence of twenty thousand million Buddhas, I have seen this auspicious sign before. After those Buddhas emitted this light, they all preached a great Dharma."

**12** "Therefore you should know that the Buddha today, in emitting this light, is about to make it so that all living beings may hear and understand the Dharma that is difficult for all the world to believe. This sutra is the king of sutras, the Lotus of the Wonderful Law."
