---
title: "Lotus Sutra - Chapter 8: Prophecy of Enlightenment for Five Hundred Disciples"
scripture: "lotus-sutra"
chapter: 8
---
**1** At that time the venerable Purna, son of Maitrayani, hearing from the Buddha this teaching of wisdom, this teaching of expedient means adapted to what is appropriate, and hearing the predictions of supreme enlightenment bestowed on the great Sravakas, hearing the accounts of past causes and conditions—his mind danced with joy.

**2** He rose from his seat, went before the Buddha, bowed his head to the ground, then withdrew to one side, gazing at the Buddha's face without taking his eyes from it.

**3** The World-Honoured One said: "Monks, do you see this Purna? Among those who preach the Dharma, I have always called him the foremost. He has also been praised for his various merits."

**4** "In future ages, Purna will become a Buddha called Truth-shining. His land will be called Excellent Purity and his kalpa will be called Jewel Bright."

**5** At that time the twelve hundred Arhats who were free in their minds thought: "We rejoice, having gained what we never had before! If the World-Honoured One would bestow predictions of future Buddhahood upon us, just as he has on the other great disciples, how happy we would be!"

**6** The Buddha, knowing what was in their minds, said to Maha-Kashyapa: "These five hundred Arhats—I will now bestow predictions on all of them. Among them, the elder Kaundinya shall become a Buddha called Universal Brightness."

**7** "All five hundred Arhats, one by one, shall become Buddhas, all bearing the name Universal Brightness."

**8** The five hundred Arhats, having received their predictions, were filled with great joy. They said to the Buddha: "World-Honoured One, we have always been thinking that we had attained the final Nirvana. Now we know that we were like ignorant men."

**9** "It is like a man who went to a friend's house and fell asleep after drinking wine. His friend tied a priceless jewel inside his robe. Later the man, not knowing about the jewel, wandered and suffered in poverty."

**10** "His friend later found him and showed him the jewel. Just so, the Buddha long ago taught us the Bodhisattva way, but we forgot. Now the World-Honoured One awakens us, and we understand that we are not without the jewel of supreme enlightenment."
