## Mahayana Interpretation

Chapter 22 presents the solemn entrustment of the Lotus Sutra to the assembled bodhisattvas, charging them with its propagation after the Buddha's parinirvana. This chapter is central to Mahayana understanding because it advances the sutra's overarching teaching that all beings are destined for supreme Buddhahood through the One Vehicle (*ekayana*).

The Tiantai master Zhiyi analyzed this chapter extensively in his *Fahua xuanyi* (Profound Meaning of the Lotus Sutra) and *Fahua wenju* (Words and Phrases of the Lotus Sutra), identifying multiple layers of meaning that correspond to the four modes of interpretation: literal, moral, meditative, and ultimate. In the Tiantai reading, every element of the chapter — narrative, parable, prophecy, and devotional practice — is a manifestation of the one reality (*shixiang*) that the sutra reveals.

For Nichiren Buddhism, this chapter is read in light of *Namu Myoho Renge Kyo* — the title of the Lotus Sutra as the essence of the Buddha's enlightenment. Nichiren taught that the entire Lotus Sutra is contained in its title, and that each chapter unfolds a particular aspect of the single truth to which the title points.
