## Overview

The Lotus Sutra stands at the very heart of Mahayana Buddhism, revered as the supreme expression of the Buddha's teaching across the Tiantai, Tendai, and Nichiren traditions. Its declaration that all beings will attain Buddhahood through the One Vehicle (*ekayana*) is among the most consequential doctrinal claims in Buddhist history.

## The One Vehicle Doctrine

The sutra's revolutionary teaching is that the three vehicles — sravakayana, pratyekabuddhayana, and bodhisattvayana — are all skillful means (*upaya*) employed by the Buddha to guide beings of different capacities. In truth, there is only one vehicle: the Buddha Vehicle, which leads all beings without exception to supreme, perfect enlightenment (*anuttara samyak sambodhi*).

This teaching transforms the entire landscape of Buddhist soteriology. No being is excluded from the possibility of Buddhahood. The arhats, once thought to have reached the final goal, are revealed to be on a longer journey toward complete Buddhahood. The Mahayana reading of this teaching has shaped the devotional practices, philosophical systems, and institutional structures of East Asian Buddhism for nearly two thousand years.

## Tiantai Classification

The Tiantai patriarch Zhiyi (538–597) placed the Lotus Sutra at the pinnacle of his five-period, eight-teaching classification system (*panjiao*). According to this scheme, the Lotus Sutra represents the Buddha's final and complete teaching, in which he sets aside all earlier provisional methods and reveals the ultimate truth directly. This classification gave the Lotus Sutra its supreme authority in the Tiantai tradition and, through Tendai, in Japanese Buddhism as well.
