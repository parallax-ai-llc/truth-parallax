## Zen Reading

Chapter 2 of the Lotus Sutra presents the Buddha's revelation of skillful means (*upaya*), declaring that the three vehicles are in fact one vehicle — the Buddha Vehicle — adapted to the varying capacities of beings. Zen interpreters approach this chapter not as doctrinal exposition to be intellectually grasped but as a pointing toward direct experience — the same finger-pointing-at-the-moon that characterizes all authentic Zen teaching.

Dogen Zenji engaged deeply with the Lotus Sutra in his *Shobogenzo*, particularly the fascicles *Hokke-ten-Hokke* ("The Lotus Sutra Turns the Lotus Sutra") and *Kannon* (on Avalokiteshvara). For Dogen, reading the Lotus Sutra is itself practice-realization: the sutra is not an object of study but an activity of awakening. When the sutra "turns" the reader — that is, when the reader surrenders conceptual grasping — the truth of the chapter is realized.

The narrative and parabolic elements of this chapter function in Zen reading as extended koans. They present situations that cannot be resolved by logic alone, inviting the practitioner to break through conceptual thinking into direct knowing.
