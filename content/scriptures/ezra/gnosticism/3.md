## Restoring the Demiurge's Altar

Gnostic interpretation of Ezra 3 reads the rebuilding of the altar and the restoration of sacrifices as the re-establishment of the Demiurge's bloodthirsty cult. The *Testimony of Truth* explicitly critiques animal sacrifice as the work of a jealous, material god who demands the death of living creatures.

## Key Themes

### Sacrifice and the Demiurge

The Gnostic text *Testimony of Truth* (NHC IX,3) asks pointedly whether a true God would demand the slaughter of animals. The answer is no: only a lesser deity, the Demiurge, requires blood sacrifice because he feeds on the material world's energy. The rebuilding of the altar in Ezra 3 is thus the restoration of a system of spiritual exploitation.

### The Joy and Weeping (3:10-13)

The elders weep because they remember a grander version of the same prison. The younger generation rejoices at a lesser copy. From a Gnostic perspective, both responses miss the point: the temple, whether Solomon's or Zerubbabel's, is a structure of the material realm that keeps worshippers focused on the Demiurge.

### The True Temple

Gnostic texts identify the true temple not with any physical structure but with the inner person. The *Gospel of Philip* teaches that the true holy of holies is within, accessible only through *gnosis* (knowledge). The external temple is a counterfeit of this interior reality.

## Theological Significance

- **Sacrifice critiqued**: Animal sacrifice serves a lesser, material deity
- **False devotion**: Temple worship directs attention to the Demiurge
- **Interior temple**: The true holy place is within the pneumatic person
- **Counterfeit restoration**: Rebuilding the temple restores a system of bondage
