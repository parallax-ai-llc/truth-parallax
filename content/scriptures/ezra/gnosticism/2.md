## The Register of the Demiurge's Servants

Gnostic interpretation of the genealogical list in Ezra 2 sees it as a catalogue of those bound to the Demiurge's worship system. The careful enumeration of families, priests, and temple servants represents the organization of archontic religion — a hierarchy designed to maintain the material cult.

## Key Themes

### Genealogy as Material Identity

The obsessive concern with genealogical purity reflects attachment to material identity — precisely the kind of worldly distinction that Gnostic theology seeks to transcend. The *Gospel of Philip* teaches that true identity is not determined by bloodline but by the presence of the divine spark.

### The Priestly Hierarchy

The prominence of priests and Levites in the list represents the institutional structure of the Demiurge's religion. In Gnostic thought, such hierarchies serve to mediate between humanity and the false god, preventing direct encounter with the transcendent.

### Those Without Genealogy (2:59-63)

Ironically, those who cannot prove their lineage — the "unregistered" — may be closer to Gnostic truth. Their exclusion from the priestly system frees them from the Demiurge's cult, even if unintentionally.

## Theological Significance

- **Material identity**: Genealogical concern reflects attachment to the material world
- **Priestly hierarchy**: Institutional religion mediates access to a false god
- **Ironic exclusion**: Those excluded from the cult may be closer to truth
- **True identity**: Spiritual identity transcends bloodline and social status
