## Overview

Ezra is a book of the Hebrew Bible and the Christian Old Testament that narrates the return of the Jewish exiles from Babylon and the rebuilding of the Temple in Jerusalem. The book takes its name from Ezra the scribe, a priest and expert in the Torah who leads a second wave of returnees and initiates religious reforms.

## Structure

Ezra is traditionally divided into two main sections:

1. **The First Return and Temple Rebuilding** (Chapters 1–6): Cyrus's decree, the return under Zerubbabel, and the completion of the Second Temple
2. **Ezra's Mission** (Chapters 7–10): Ezra's arrival in Jerusalem and the crisis of intermarriage

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Ezra holds significance across multiple religious traditions:

- **Judaism**: Part of the Ketuvim (Writings); Ezra is regarded as a second Moses who restored the Torah
- **Christianity**: A historical bridge between exile and restoration, prefiguring themes of spiritual renewal
- **Islam**: The figure of Uzayr (Ezra) appears in the Quran (9:30) in a polemical context
- **Academic**: A key text for understanding Persian-period Judaism and the formation of Jewish identity

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Return from Exile | God's faithfulness in restoring His people to the land |
| Temple Rebuilding | The centrality of worship in communal restoration |
| Torah Observance | Ezra's emphasis on strict adherence to the Law |
| Separation | The controversial policy of dissolving foreign marriages |
| Providence | God working through pagan kings (Cyrus, Darius, Artaxerxes) |
