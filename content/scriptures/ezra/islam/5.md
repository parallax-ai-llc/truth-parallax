## Prophetic Encouragement

Islamic interpretation of Ezra 5 recognizes the role of prophets as God's messengers who guide communities through times of trial. The prophets Haggai and Zechariah fulfill the Quranic model of prophets who call their people back to obedience.

## Key Themes

### The Prophetic Mission

The Quran teaches that God sent prophets to every community (Quran 16:36) to guide them toward truth and warn them against error. Haggai and Zechariah function within this prophetic paradigm, calling the people to resume their divinely appointed task.

### "The Eye of Their God" (5:5)

God's watchful protection over the Jewish leaders resonates with the Quranic attribute of *al-Basir* (the All-Seeing). God sees and protects those who serve Him faithfully, even when they are vulnerable to worldly powers.

### Honest Administration

Tattenai's fair investigation illustrates the Islamic virtue of *adl* (justice). The Quran commands: "O you who believe, be persistently standing firm in justice" (Quran 4:135). Even a non-Israelite administrator can serve divine purposes by acting justly.

## Theological Significance

- **Prophetic guidance**: God sends messengers to renew communal commitment
- **Divine watchfulness**: God sees and protects His servants
- **Justice**: Fair administration serves divine purposes
- **Resumption**: God's work cannot be permanently halted
