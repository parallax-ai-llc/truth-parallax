## Overview

The Islamic engagement with Ezra centers on the Quranic mention of Uzayr (identified by most commentators with Ezra) in Surah al-Tawbah 9:30: "The Jews say, 'Uzayr is the son of Allah,' and the Christians say, 'The Messiah is the son of Allah.'" This verse has generated extensive discussion among Muslim scholars about who Uzayr was and whether any Jewish group actually held this belief. The broader narrative of Ezra — the return from exile, temple rebuilding, and Torah restoration — is read through the lens of *tahrif* (textual corruption), with Muslim scholars questioning whether the Torah restored by Ezra was identical to the original revelation given to Moses.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Classical Tafsir
Commentators like al-Tabari and Ibn Kathir discuss Uzayr in connection with Quran 9:30. Al-Tabari preserves multiple traditions, some identifying Uzayr with Ezra the scribe, others treating the verse as addressing a specific Jewish sect rather than all Jews. Ibn Kathir connects the verse to the high veneration of Ezra in Jewish tradition.

### Historical-Theological
The narrative of Torah restoration raises the question of textual integrity. Some Muslim scholars argue that Ezra reconstructed the Torah from memory after the Babylonian exile, introducing alterations that constitute *tahrif* (corruption). Others hold that *tahrif* is interpretive rather than textual.

### Quranic Parallels
Quran 2:259 describes a man whom God caused to die for a hundred years and then resurrected — some commentators identify this figure with Uzayr, linking it to Ezra's role in the post-exilic restoration.

## Theological Emphases

- **Tawhid**: The Quranic critique of calling Uzayr "son of Allah" reinforces absolute divine oneness
- **Tahrif**: The question of whether the Torah was faithfully preserved or corrupted during the exile
- **Ahl al-Kitab**: Ezra's story as part of the broader Islamic understanding of People of the Book
- **Divine Sovereignty**: God's power to restore communities and revive the dead (Quran 2:259)
