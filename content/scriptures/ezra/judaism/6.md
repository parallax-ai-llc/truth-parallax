## The Second Temple Dedicated

Ezra 6 narrates the completion and dedication of the Second Temple, followed by the celebration of Passover. Jewish interpretation reads this as a moment of profound *simchah* (joy) tempered by awareness of what has been lost.

## Key Verses and Rabbinic Interpretation

### Darius's Decree (6:1-12)

The Talmud (Rosh Hashanah 3a-4a) discusses the chronology of the Persian kings and their decrees regarding the temple. Darius's confirmation of Cyrus's original edict demonstrates that God's purposes cannot be permanently frustrated by political opposition.

### The Temple Completed (6:14-15)

The completion date — the third of Adar in the sixth year of Darius — is preserved with precision. Jewish tradition values exact dating because it anchors sacred events in historical time and enables the calculation of chronological relationships between biblical events.

### The Dedication Sacrifices (6:16-18)

The twelve male goats "for all Israel" is significant: even though only Judah and Benjamin physically returned, the sacrificial representation encompasses all twelve tribes. Rabbinic theology holds that the unity of Israel (*klal Yisrael*) transcends physical dispersion.

### The Passover (6:19-22)

The celebration of Passover connects the return from Babylon to the original Exodus from Egypt. Jewish liturgy explicitly draws this parallel: the Passover Haggadah declares, "In every generation, a person is obligated to see himself as if he personally went out of Egypt."

## Theological Significance

- **Simchah**: Joy in restoration, even amid awareness of loss
- **Klal Yisrael**: The unity of all Israel transcends physical separation
- **Passover as paradigm**: Every redemption echoes the original Exodus
- **Historical precision**: Sacred events are anchored in exact time
