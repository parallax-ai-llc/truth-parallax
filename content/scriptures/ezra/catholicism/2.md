## The Census of the Community

Catholic interpretation reads the genealogical list of Ezra 2 through an ecclesiological lens. The careful enumeration of families, priests, Levites, and temple servants reflects the structured nature of the covenant community.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### Ordered Community

The list reveals a community organized around worship: priests, Levites, singers, gatekeepers, and temple servants each have defined roles. Catholic ecclesiology sees this as prefiguring the hierarchical structure of the Church, where diverse ministries serve the common good under apostolic authority.

### Continuity of Identity

The returning exiles establish their identity through genealogical records. Catholic tradition values apostolic succession as a similar chain of continuity, maintaining the Church's identity through an unbroken line of authorized ministry stretching back to the apostles.

### Those Excluded (2:59-63)

Those who could not prove their priestly descent were barred from serving. Catholic canon law similarly requires proper ordination and authorization for sacramental ministry.

### The Whole Community (2:64-67)

The total number of 42,360 represents the entire assembly. Catholic theology emphasizes the communal nature of salvation: we are saved not as isolated individuals but as members of the People of God.

## Theological Significance

- **Ecclesial structure**: The community is ordered around worship and ministry
- **Apostolic continuity**: Identity requires legitimate lineage and authorization
- **Communal salvation**: God saves a people, not merely individuals
- **Sacred ministry**: Priestly service requires proper credentials
