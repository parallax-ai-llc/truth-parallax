## The Dedication and Passover

Ezra 6 culminates in the completion and dedication of the Second Temple, followed by Passover celebration. Catholic interpretation reads this as a type of the Church's sacramental life.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Darius Confirms the Decree (6:1-12)

Darius's support illustrates the Catholic principle of legitimate cooperation between Church and state. Catholic social teaching affirms that temporal authorities can rightly support the common good, which includes the flourishing of religious life.

### The Temple Completed (6:14-15)

The completion "by the command of the God of Israel and by the decree of Cyrus, Darius, and Artaxerxes" juxtaposes divine and human causality. Catholic theology holds that divine sovereignty and human agency are complementary: grace perfects nature.

### The Dedication Sacrifices (6:16-18)

The sin offering of twelve male goats "for all Israel" reflects Catholic universalism: the sacrifice encompasses the entire people, not just those physically present. The Eucharistic sacrifice is similarly offered for the whole Church, living and dead.

### The Passover (6:19-22)

Catholic liturgical theology sees the Passover as the direct antecedent of the Eucharist, the sacrifice of the Lamb that liberates God's people. "The LORD had made them joyful" (6:22) anticipates the joy of the Eucharistic feast.

## Theological Significance

- **Temple as divine presence**: God dwells among His people through sacred space
- **Eucharistic prefiguration**: Passover points to the sacrifice of the Mass
- **Grace and nature**: Divine command and human decree work in harmony
- **Universal offering**: The sacrifice encompasses the whole people of God
