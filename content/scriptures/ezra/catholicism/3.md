## The Restoration of Worship

Ezra 3 records the rebuilding of the altar and the laying of the temple foundation. Catholic interpretation highlights the priority of liturgical worship and sees in these events a prefiguration of the Church's sacramental life.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Altar Before the Temple (3:1-3)

The community rebuilds the altar before the temple walls rise. Catholic liturgical theology sees this as affirming the centrality of sacrifice, specifically prefiguring the Eucharist. The altar is the essential element; the building serves the altar, not the reverse.

### The Restored Sacrificial Calendar (3:4-6)

The immediate observance of the Feast of Tabernacles and daily offerings reflects the Catholic principle that liturgical worship follows a sacred calendar. The Church's liturgical year similarly structures communal life around the mysteries of Christ.

### The Foundation Ceremony (3:10-13)

The mingling of joy and weeping at the foundation's laying reflects the Catholic understanding of the Church as always in need of renewal, yet always continuous with its past. The tears of the elders honor what was lost; the joy of the young celebrates what is being born.

### Levitical Music and Praise (3:10-11)

The antiphonal refrain "For he is good, for his steadfast love endures forever" anticipates Catholic liturgical music, psalmody, antiphons, and hymns as essential to corporate worship.

## Theological Significance

- **Altar as center**: Sacrifice precedes and defines the worshipping community
- **Liturgical calendar**: Sacred time structures the community's life
- **Continuity and renewal**: The Church is both ancient and ever-new
- **Sacred music**: Liturgical song is integral to worship
