## Ezra: Priest, Scribe, and Teacher

Orthodox interpretation of Ezra 7 highlights the integration of priestly, scholarly, and pastoral authority in Ezra's person. The Orthodox Church sees in Ezra a model of the bishop-theologian who unites liturgical presidency, doctrinal teaching, and pastoral care.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Ezra's Lineage (7:1-5)

Ezra's priestly genealogy stretching back to Aaron establishes his authority in the community. Orthodox ecclesiology similarly grounds episcopal authority in apostolic succession — a chain of ordination that connects every bishop to the apostles.

### "The good hand of his God was on him" (7:6, 9)

This phrase, repeated throughout the chapter, expresses the Orthodox understanding of divine grace (*charis*) as the enabling power behind all authentic ministry. The grace of ordination empowers the priest for service beyond his natural capabilities.

### Study, Practice, Teach (7:10)

Ezra's three-fold commitment — to study the Torah, to practice it, and to teach it — reflects the Orthodox understanding of *theoria* (contemplation), *praxis* (practice), and *didaskalia* (teaching) as the integrated dimensions of spiritual authority.

### The Royal Commission (7:11-26)

Artaxerxes' letter grants Ezra sweeping authority. Orthodox Christianity has historically operated within imperial frameworks while maintaining its spiritual independence — the principle that the Church accepts worldly support without surrendering its divine mandate.

## Theological Significance

- **Integrated authority**: Priestly, scholarly, and pastoral roles are unified
- **Apostolic succession**: Authority flows through legitimate ordination
- **Grace of ministry**: God's hand empowers effective service
- **Contemplation and action**: Study, practice, and teaching are inseparable
