## The Catalogue of the Faithful

Orthodox interpretation of Ezra 2 reads the genealogical list as a register of the faithful remnant. The careful enumeration of families and religious personnel reflects the structured, hierarchical nature of the worshipping community that the Orthodox Church sees as continuous with its own ecclesial life.

## Key Interpretive Themes

### The Structured Community

The list organizes the returning community around its worship function: priests, Levites, singers, gatekeepers, and temple servants. Orthodox ecclesiology similarly understands the Church as a liturgical community with distinct orders of ministry — bishops, priests, deacons, and laity — each serving the common worship.

### Continuity Through Memory

The genealogical records establish that the returning community is the legitimate continuation of pre-exilic Israel. Orthodox tradition values apostolic succession as the analogous chain of continuity: the Church's identity is maintained through an unbroken line of episcopal consecration from the apostles to the present.

### The Excluded (2:59-63)

Those who could not prove their ancestry were excluded from priestly service. Orthodox canon law maintains strict requirements for ordination, ensuring that those who serve at the altar are properly authorized by the Church.

## Theological Significance

- **Liturgical community**: The Church is organized around worship
- **Apostolic continuity**: Identity requires legitimate succession
- **Sacred ordering**: Different ministries serve the one liturgy
- **Faithful remnant**: God preserves His people through every trial
