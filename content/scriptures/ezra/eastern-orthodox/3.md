## The Altar and the Foundation

Ezra 3 records the rebuilding of the altar and the laying of the temple foundation. Orthodox interpretation sees these events as the restoration of the cosmos's liturgical center — the point where heaven and earth meet in sacrificial worship.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Altar First (3:1-3)

The altar precedes the temple because sacrifice is the essential act of worship. Orthodox liturgical theology understands the altar (the Holy Table) as the center of the cosmos — the place where creation is offered back to God. Every Orthodox church orients its altar toward the east, anticipating Christ's return.

### The Feast of Tabernacles (3:4-6)

The immediate celebration of Sukkot reflects the Orthodox conviction that liturgical worship cannot be postponed. The Church's liturgical calendar structures all of life around the mysteries of salvation, and the faithful are called to worship regardless of external circumstances.

### The Foundation and the Tears (3:10-13)

The elders' weeping is understood not as faithlessness but as holy remembrance. Orthodox spirituality values the "gift of tears" (*penthos*) as a sign of spiritual depth. The mingling of joy and sorrow at the foundation reflects the Orthodox understanding that all earthly restoration remains incomplete until the eschaton.

## Theological Significance

- **Altar as cosmic center**: The place where creation meets its Creator
- **Liturgical priority**: Worship comes before all other communal activities
- **Gift of tears**: Holy grief is a mark of spiritual maturity
- **Eschatological tension**: Earthly restoration anticipates heavenly fulfillment
