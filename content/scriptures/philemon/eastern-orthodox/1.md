## Paul's Appeal for Onesimus

Eastern Orthodoxy interpretation of Philemon 1 centers on patristic commentary and liturgical theology. The key passages — I appeal to you for my child Onesimus (v.10), no longer as a slave but as a brother (v.16), charge that to my account (v.18) — have been read through the lens of theosis, the liturgical life of the Church, and patristic consensus, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

John chrysostom, theodoret of cyrus, and other eastern fathers gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Philemon. The chapter's teaching on paul's appeal for onesimus has shaped Eastern Orthodoxy understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern eastern orthodoxy scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Philemon 1 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
