## Overview

Catholic social teaching draws significantly from Philemon, seeing in Paul's appeal a model of Christian charity that transcends social structures while working to transform them from within. The letter illustrates the Catholic principle that the gospel does not primarily overthrow institutions by force but leavens them through love.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Dignity of Every Person
Catholic reading emphasizes that Paul's appeal rests on the fundamental dignity of Onesimus as a person created in God's image and reborn in Christ. This dignity is not conferred by social status but by baptism and creation.

### Intercession and Mediation
Paul's role as mediator between Onesimus and Philemon is read as an image of the intercessory role that saints play in Catholic theology. Just as Paul appeals on behalf of another, the saints intercede before God.

### Subsidiarity and Social Transformation
The letter models the Catholic principle of addressing social injustice through personal conversion and communal charity rather than through violent revolution — a theme developed in modern Catholic social encyclicals.
