## Paul's Appeal for Onesimus

Catholic interpretation of Philemon 1 centers on sacred Tradition, the Magisterium, and sacramental theology. The key passages — I appeal to you for my child Onesimus (v.10), no longer as a slave but as a brother (v.16), charge that to my account (v.18) — have been read through the lens of the unity of Scripture and Tradition under the guidance of the Magisterium, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

The church fathers, thomas aquinas, and modern catholic exegetes gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Philemon. The chapter's teaching on paul's appeal for onesimus has shaped Catholic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Catholic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Philemon 1 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
