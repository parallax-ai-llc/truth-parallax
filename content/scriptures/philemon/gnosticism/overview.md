## Overview

Gnostic interpreters read Philemon as an allegory of the soul's bondage and liberation. Onesimus, the runaway slave, becomes a symbol of the divine spark trapped in the material world, and Paul's appeal for his freedom represents the call of the divine messenger who seeks to liberate imprisoned spirits.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Enslaved Soul
In Gnostic reading, Onesimus represents the pneumatic soul enslaved to the material world (symbolized by Philemon's household). His flight and encounter with Paul allegorize the soul's journey from bondage under the archons to encounter with the divine revealer who recognizes its true nature.

### From Slave to Brother
The transformation from "slave" to "beloved brother" (v.16) was read as the transformation from ignorant psychic to awakened pneumatic. True brotherhood is not social but spiritual — the recognition of shared divine origin among those who possess the spark of the Pleroma.

### Paul as Divine Messenger
Paul's role as mediator and advocate was understood in Gnostic framework as the function of the revealer figure — the emissary from the Pleroma who intervenes in the material realm to liberate trapped divine sparks and reconcile them to their true origin.
