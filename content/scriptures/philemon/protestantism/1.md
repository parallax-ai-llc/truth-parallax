## Paul's Appeal for Onesimus

Protestant interpretation of Philemon 1 centers on sola Scriptura and the Reformation heritage. The key passages — I appeal to you for my child Onesimus (v.10), no longer as a slave but as a brother (v.16), charge that to my account (v.18) — have been read through the lens of the authority of Scripture and justification by faith, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Luther, calvin, and subsequent reformed and evangelical scholars gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Philemon. The chapter's teaching on paul's appeal for onesimus has shaped Protestant understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Protestant scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Philemon 1 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
