## Sufi Reading

The Sufi finds in the Sukhmani's opening emphasis on *Nam Simran* a direct parallel to the Sufi practice of *dhikr* — the continuous remembrance of God through the repetition of the divine names. Both traditions teach that the divine Name is not merely a label but a portal to the divine presence, and that its constant repetition transforms the consciousness of the invoker. The Sufi concept of *dhikr qalbi* (remembrance of the heart) resonates with Guru Arjan's teaching that true remembrance transcends verbal recitation to become a continuous inner awareness.
