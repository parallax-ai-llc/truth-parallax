The Sufi reading of Sukhmani Sahib discovers profound resonances between Guru Arjan's spiritual vision and the Sufi path of divine love and remembrance. The centrality of *Nam Simran* in the Sukhmani parallels the Sufi practice of *dhikr* — the continuous remembrance of God through the repetition of divine names and attributes. Both traditions teach that the divine Name is not merely a label but a living spiritual reality that transforms the one who invokes it.

The Sukhmani's teaching on *haumai* (ego) as the fundamental obstacle to spiritual realization resonates deeply with the Sufi concept of *nafs* (the ego-self) and the necessity of its annihilation (*fana*) in the Divine. The *Brahmgiani* of the Sukhmani shares key qualities with the Sufi *wali* (friend of God): both are characterized by constant awareness of the divine presence, detachment from worldly concerns, and the capacity to transmit spiritual blessing to others.
