## Sufi Reading

Guru Arjan's critique of empty ritual finds a strong echo in the Sufi tradition's emphasis on *ikhlas* (sincerity) and its critique of merely external religiosity. The great Sufi masters, from al-Ghazali to Rumi, have consistently taught that religious practices without inner presence and love are spiritually worthless — a teaching that Guru Arjan would heartily endorse.
