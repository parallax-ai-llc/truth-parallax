The Sikh reading of Sukhmani Sahib is the devotional heartbeat of the community's spiritual life. For the practicing Sikh, reciting Sukhmani Sahib — a practice that takes approximately 90 minutes — is an act of deep meditation and surrender to Waheguru (God). The composition is recited during times of joy and sorrow, at *akhand path* (continuous reading of the Guru Granth Sahib), at bedsides of the ill, and in personal daily devotion.

Guru Arjan Dev Ji composed Sukhmani Sahib as a comprehensive guide to the spiritual life centered on *Nam Simran* — the constant, loving remembrance of the divine Name. The Sikh reader understands each astpadi as a progressive deepening of the soul's relationship with Waheguru, from the initial recognition of the Name's importance through the stages of purification, surrender, and ultimate realization of *sahaj* (natural equipoise in divine awareness).
