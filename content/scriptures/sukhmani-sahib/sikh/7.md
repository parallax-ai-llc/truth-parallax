## Sikh Reading

This astpadi challenges the notion that elaborate rituals, pilgrimages, fasts, and ascetic practices can substitute for genuine inner devotion. Guru Arjan, consistent with the Sikh tradition's critique of empty formalism, teaches that God values the sincere devotion of the heart over all external performances. The Sikh reader understands this not as a rejection of religious practice but as an insistence that practice without inner love is spiritually barren.
