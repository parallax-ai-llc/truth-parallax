## Sikh Reading

The second astpadi on the Brahmgiani deepens the portrait, emphasizing the God-realized person's compassion, equanimity, and freedom from judgment. The Brahmgiani sees God in all beings, feels no enmity toward anyone, and remains undisturbed by praise or blame. For the Sikh, this teaching provides a practical standard against which to measure one's own spiritual progress — not through theological knowledge but through the quality of one's relationships and responses to life's challenges.
