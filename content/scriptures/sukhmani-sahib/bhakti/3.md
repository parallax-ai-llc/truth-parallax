## Bhakti Reading

Guru Arjan's teaching on divine immanence echoes the Bhakti conviction that God dwells within every heart, in every creature, in every grain of sand. The radical egalitarianism that flows from this insight — if God is in all, then none can be treated as inferior — connects the Sukhmani with the social revolutionary dimension of the Bhakti movement, which challenged Brahmanical caste hierarchy in the name of divine omnipresence.
