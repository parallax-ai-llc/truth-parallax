The Bhakti reading of Sukhmani Sahib situates Guru Arjan's composition within the broader Sant-Bhakti tradition of North India — the movement of devotional poets including Kabir, Ravidas, Namdev, and Mirabai who challenged caste hierarchy, ritual formalism, and sectarian exclusivism in the name of direct, loving devotion to the One God. The Guru Granth Sahib itself includes compositions by several Bhakti poets alongside those of the Sikh Gurus, testifying to the deep connections between these traditions.

The Sukhmani's emphasis on *Nam* as the supreme spiritual practice echoes the Bhakti teaching of *nam japa* — the repetition of God's name as the most accessible and effective path to liberation. Its critique of empty ritual and caste pride resonates with the radical social vision of the Sant poets, who declared that devotion, not birth, determines spiritual worth.
