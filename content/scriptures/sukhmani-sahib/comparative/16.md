## Comparative Reading

The ethics of speech — the teaching that words carry spiritual consequences — is emphasized across traditions: the Buddhist concept of Right Speech, the Jewish teaching on *lashon hara* (evil speech), the Islamic prohibition of *ghiba* (backbiting), and the Confucian emphasis on the rectification of names. Guru Arjan's teaching adds the distinctive Sikh perspective that speech directed against others is ultimately directed against the God who dwells in them.
