## Comparative Reading

Comparative study of the Sukhmani's conclusion alongside the closing passages of other sacred texts reveals how different traditions achieve literary and theological closure. The circular return to the opening theme mirrors structures found in the Qur'an's ring compositions, the Torah's narrative arc, and the Buddhist practice of dedicating merit at the end of meditation — suggesting a universal recognition that spiritual completion requires a return to origins.
