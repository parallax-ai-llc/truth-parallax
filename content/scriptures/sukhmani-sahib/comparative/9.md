## Comparative Reading

The ideal of the spiritually perfected person takes different forms across traditions: the Sikh *Brahmgiani*, the Buddhist *arhat* or *bodhisattva*, the Christian saint, the Confucian *junzi* (exemplary person), the Sufi *wali*, and the Hindu *jivanmukta*. Comparative analysis reveals both shared qualities (compassion, equanimity, wisdom) and distinctive emphases that reflect each tradition's particular values.
