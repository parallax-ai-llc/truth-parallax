Comparative study of Sukhmani Sahib reveals rich parallels with contemplative and devotional traditions across world religions. The central practice of *Nam Simran* (divine remembrance) finds counterparts in the Christian *Jesus Prayer*, Islamic *dhikr*, Jewish *hitbodedut*, Hindu *japa*, and Buddhist *nembutsu*. Each tradition recognizes repetitive invocation of the divine name or a sacred formula as a powerful means of spiritual transformation.

The Sukhmani's teaching on ego-transcendence (*haumai*) resonates with Buddhist *anatta*, Christian *kenosis*, Sufi *fana*, and Daoist *wu-wei*. Its vision of the God-realized person (*Brahmgiani*) invites comparison with the Buddhist *arhat*, the Christian saint, the Sufi *wali*, and the Hindu *jivanmukta*. These cross-traditional parallels illuminate both the universal dimensions of contemplative experience and the distinctive features of the Sikh spiritual path.
