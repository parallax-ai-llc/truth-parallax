## Comparative Reading

The Brahmgiani's equanimity invites comparison with the Stoic sage's *apatheia*, the Buddhist practitioner's *upekkha* (equanimity), the Daoist sage's *wu-wei* (non-action), and the Christian mystic's detachment. Each tradition describes a state of inner freedom in which the realized person responds to all circumstances with equal composure — though the theological frameworks that explain this state differ significantly.
