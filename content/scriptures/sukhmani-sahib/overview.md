## Overview

Sukhmani Sahib (ਸੁਖਮਨੀ ਸਾਹਿਬ, "Psalm of Peace" or "Jewel of Bliss") is one of the most beloved and frequently recited compositions in the Sikh scripture, authored by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the fifth Sikh Guru, in 1602–04 CE. It is included in the Guru Granth Sahib, the central religious scripture of Sikhism, at pages 262–296. The composition is a sustained meditation on the divine Name (*Nam*), the nature of God, and the path to inner peace through devotion, ethical living, and surrender to the divine will (*Hukam*).

## Structure

Sukhmani Sahib consists of 24 sections called *astpadi* (eight-stanza hymns), each preceded by a *salok* (introductory couplet). The 24 astpadis systematically explore the themes of divine remembrance, spiritual liberation, and the qualities of the God-centered person (*Gurmukh*):

1. **Astpadi 1–4**: The glory and necessity of *Nam Simran* (meditation on the divine Name)
2. **Astpadi 5–8**: The nature of God and the illusion of *maya* (worldly attachment)
3. **Astpadi 9–12**: The qualities of the *Brahmgiani* (God-realized person)
4. **Astpadi 13–16**: The futility of ego (*haumai*) and the necessity of humility
5. **Astpadi 17–20**: The universality of divine grace and the equality of all beings
6. **Astpadi 21–24**: Surrender to *Hukam* (divine will) and the attainment of *sahaj* (equipoise)

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Sikh**: The most popular devotional text after the daily *Nitnem* prayers, recited for healing, peace, and spiritual growth
- **Academic**: A key text for the study of Sikh theology, Punjab's devotional culture, and Sant-Bhakti literature
- **Comparative**: Rich parallels with contemplative traditions across religions
- **Sufi**: Resonances with Sufi *dhikr* and the concept of *fana* in the divine Name
- **Bhakti**: Shares deep roots with the North Indian *Sant* and *Bhakti* traditions
- **Interfaith**: A powerful resource for interfaith dialogue on meditation, peace, and universal spirituality

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Nam Simran | Meditation on and remembrance of the divine Name as the highest spiritual practice |
| Hukam | Surrender to the divine will as the foundation of inner peace |
| Haumai | The ego as the fundamental obstacle to spiritual realization |
| Brahmgiani | The qualities of the God-realized person who lives in constant divine awareness |
| Sahaj | The state of natural equipoise achieved through devotion and surrender |
