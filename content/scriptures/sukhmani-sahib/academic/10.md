## Academic Reading

The tenth astpadi's elaboration of the Brahmgiani ideal is studied for its ethical content, with scholars analyzing how Guru Arjan constructs a model of moral perfection that is simultaneously deeply Indian and distinctively Sikh. The Brahmgiani's qualities — compassion, equanimity, non-judgment — are compared with virtue lists in other Indian and non-Indian ethical traditions.
