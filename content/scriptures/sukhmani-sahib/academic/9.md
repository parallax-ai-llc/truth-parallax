## Academic Reading

Scholars examine the *Brahmgiani* concept in this astpadi in relation to similar ideals across South Asian traditions: the Hindu *jivanmukta* (liberated while living), the Buddhist *arhat*, and the Jain *kevalin*. The Brahmgiani's distinctive feature — full engagement with worldly life rather than ascetic withdrawal — is analyzed as a defining characteristic of Sikh spirituality that distinguishes it from some other Indian liberation models.
