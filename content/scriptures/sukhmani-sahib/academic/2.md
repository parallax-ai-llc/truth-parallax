## Academic Reading

Scholars examine the second astpadi's promises of spiritual benefit in relation to the broader South Asian discourse on the fruits (*phala*) of religious practice. The text's assurances of divine protection and inner peace are analyzed both theologically — as expressions of Sikh *kirpa* (grace) theology — and sociologically — as appeals to potential adherents in the competitive religious marketplace of early modern Punjab.
