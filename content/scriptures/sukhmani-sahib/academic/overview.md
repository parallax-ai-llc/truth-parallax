Academic study of Sukhmani Sahib situates the composition within the broader context of the North Indian *Sant* tradition, Sikh theological development, and the literary history of Punjabi devotional poetry. Scholars analyze the text's sophisticated theological arguments, its prosodic structure, and its relationship to earlier devotional compositions in the Guru Granth Sahib.

The 24-astpadi structure has been examined for its systematic theological organization, with scholars noting how Guru Arjan builds a comprehensive spiritual theology through progressive thematic development. Linguistic analysis reveals the text's multilingual vocabulary — drawing on Punjabi, Sanskrit, Hindi, Persian, and Arabic — as evidence of the cosmopolitan intellectual environment in which Sikh theology developed.
