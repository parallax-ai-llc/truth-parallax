## Academic Reading

Academic study of the fifteenth astpadi examines its universalist theology in relation to the competitive religious environment of Mughal India. Scholars analyze how Guru Arjan's affirmation of universal divine grace functioned as both a theological statement and a social strategy, positioning Sikhism as a non-sectarian tradition open to all — while also asserting the distinctive authority of the Sikh Guru as the supreme mediator of this universal grace.
