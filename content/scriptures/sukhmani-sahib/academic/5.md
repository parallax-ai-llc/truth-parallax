## Academic Reading

Scholars analyze the fifth astpadi's theological vocabulary, noting how Guru Arjan draws on Sanskrit, Persian, and Arabic terminology to articulate a concept of God that synthesizes elements from multiple religious traditions. This multilingual theological vocabulary is examined as evidence of the cosmopolitan intellectual culture of the Mughal period and the creative theological synthesis that characterizes Sikh thought.
