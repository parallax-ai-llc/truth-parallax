Sukhmani Sahib holds exceptional value for interfaith dialogue because its central teachings — the transformative power of divine remembrance, the necessity of ego-transcendence, the universality of divine grace, and the equality of all human beings before God — are affirmed across the world's major religious traditions. The composition's very title, 'Psalm of Peace,' signals its relevance to the universal human aspiration for inner and outer peace.

Interfaith groups have found Sukhmani Sahib to be a powerful resource for shared contemplative practice. Its meditative recitation creates a space of spiritual depth accessible to practitioners of all faiths, while its theological content — affirming one God, one humanity, and one path of loving devotion — provides a natural framework for interfaith encounter.
