The Buddha's exhortation to seek rebirth in Sukhavati through faith, vow, and practice crystallizes the Pure Land path. Mahayana commentators identify three essential conditions for rebirth: faith (xin) in Amitabha's vows, the vow (yuan) to be reborn in Sukhavati, and practice (xing), primarily the recitation of Amitabha's name. The chapter's warning that this teaching is 'difficult to believe' acknowledges the counter-intuitive nature of a path that emphasizes receptivity to the Buddha's grace rather than self-powered meditation. Shandao's commentary established that even ordinary beings burdened with heavy karma can attain rebirth through sincere recitation, democratizing access to liberation in a way that profoundly shaped East Asian Buddhist culture.
