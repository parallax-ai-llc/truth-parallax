## Overview

The Infinite Life Sutra stands as the foundational text of Pure Land Buddhism, the most widely practiced form of Mahayana Buddhism in East Asia. Its narrative of Dharmakara Bodhisattva's 48 vows and their fulfillment as Amitabha Buddha provides the doctrinal basis for the Pure Land path — a path of faith, vow, and practice that promises rebirth in a realm ideally suited for the attainment of final enlightenment.

## The 48 Vows

Dharmakara's vows are the heart of Pure Land soteriology. The 18th Vow — that all beings who sincerely desire rebirth in his land and call upon his name will be reborn there — became the doctrinal foundation for nembutsu/nianfo practice. Different Pure Land schools emphasize different vows, with Jodo Shinshu focusing almost exclusively on the 18th Vow as expressing Amitabha's unconditional compassion.

## Relationship to Other Mahayana Teachings

The Infinite Life Sutra integrates several major Mahayana themes: the bodhisattva's cosmic compassion, the power of vows (pranidhana), the creation of Buddha-fields through accumulated merit, and the accessibility of the dharma to beings of all capacities. It represents the devotional and soteriological dimension of Mahayana Buddhism alongside the philosophical traditions of Madhyamaka and Yogacara.
