The description of Sukhavati presents a Buddha-land of inconceivable beauty and perfection — jeweled trees that produce celestial music, lotus pools of eight excellent qualities, ground of gold, and beings who are all non-retrogressing bodhisattvas. Mahayana commentators understand this description not merely as cosmological information but as a vision of what becomes possible when a bodhisattva's compassion and wisdom are fully perfected. Sukhavati is the expression in spatial form of Amitabha's accumulated merit and wisdom. The land's perfection reflects the perfection of the Buddha who created it, and its beauty serves a soteriological function: beings born there are surrounded by conditions that naturally lead to awakening.
