Dharmakara's 48 vows constitute the doctrinal heart of Pure Land Buddhism and one of the most consequential passages in all of Mahayana literature. Each vow specifies a condition that must be met for Dharmakara to accept Buddhahood — ensuring that his Buddha-land will possess every quality needed for beings to attain liberation. The 18th Vow, in which Dharmakara pledges that all beings who sincerely desire rebirth and call upon his name will be reborn in his land, became the foundation of East Asian Pure Land practice. Shinran interpreted this vow as expressing Amitabha's unconditional compassion: it is not the practitioner's effort but the Buddha's vow-power that accomplishes salvation. This interpretation represents one of the most radical soteriological positions in Buddhist history.
