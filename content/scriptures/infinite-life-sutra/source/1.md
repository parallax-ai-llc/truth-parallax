---
title: "Infinite Life Sutra - Chapter 1"
scripture: "infinite-life-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the city of Rajagriha, on Vulture Peak, together with a great assembly of twelve thousand monks. They were all great arhats whom everyone knew.

**2** Among them were the venerable elders Shariputra, Mahamaudgalyayana, Mahakashyapa, Mahakatyayana, Mahakausthila, Revata, Shuddhipanthaka, Nanda, Ananda, Rahula, Gavampati, Pindola Bharadvaja, Kalodayin, Mahakapphina, Vakkula, and Aniruddha.

**3** There were also present bodhisattvas and mahasattvas: Manjushri the Dharma Prince, the Bodhisattva Ajita, the Bodhisattva Gandhahastin, the Bodhisattva Nityodyukta, and other great bodhisattvas.

**4** At that time the World-Honored One was serene in countenance, and his features were joyful. The venerable Ananda, having observed this, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, knelt on his right knee, and with palms together, addressed the Buddha.

**5** "World-Honored One, today your sense faculties are radiant, your body is serene, and your august countenance is as majestic as a clear mirror, whose brightness radiates outward and inward. Your magnificent appearance is superb and unsurpassed. For I have never seen you look so splendid and majestic as you do today."

**6** The Buddha said to Ananda, "Tell me, Ananda, whether some god urged you to come and ask me this question, or whether you asked out of your own wisdom, having observed my extraordinary appearance?"

**7** Ananda replied, "No god urged me to ask this question. I asked it of my own accord, because I saw that the World-Honored One's countenance was radiant and extraordinary."

**8** The Buddha said, "Well said, Ananda, well said! You have asked this question out of profound wisdom. With great compassion you are concerned about all sentient beings. The Tathagata, with immeasurable compassion, has appeared in the world for the sake of expounding the teaching of the Way. He wishes to save the multitudes of beings by endowing them with true and real benefit."

**9** "Such an occasion as this is difficult to encounter even in countless millions of ages. The reason I have appeared in this world is to reveal the teaching of the Way and to rescue sentient beings. Now I shall expound for you the teaching of the Buddha of Infinite Life."

**10** "Listen carefully and ponder well, for I shall explain." Ananda replied, "Yes, I will. I am delighted and eager to hear."
