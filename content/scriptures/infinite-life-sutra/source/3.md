---
title: "Infinite Life Sutra - Chapter 3"
scripture: "infinite-life-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Buddha said to Ananda, "The Bhikkhu Dharmakara, having made these great vows, practiced the bodhisattva path for immeasurable, boundless kalpas. In every life he accumulated immeasurable merit and virtue."

**2** "He never gave rise to greed, anger, or delusion. He never harbored thoughts of craving, hatred, or harmfulness. He was not attached to forms, sounds, smells, tastes, objects of touch, or mental objects."

**3** "He practiced patience and was not quarrelsome. He was never greedy, angry, or foolish. Inwardly, he maintained tranquility and serenity. Outwardly, he practiced kindness and compassion."

**4** "Having perfected his practices over countless kalpas, the Bhikkhu Dharmakara finally attained supreme enlightenment and became the Buddha of Infinite Life, Amitayus. He now dwells in the Western Pure Land, called Sukhavati, the Land of Ultimate Bliss, which lies beyond ten trillion Buddha lands from here."

**5** "In that land there is no suffering, only the enjoyment of bliss. That is why it is called the Land of Ultimate Bliss. In that land there are seven rows of railings, seven rows of netting, and seven rows of trees, all adorned with four kinds of precious gems."

**6** "In the Land of Ultimate Bliss there are pools of the seven jewels, filled with the water of eight excellent qualities. The bottom of each pool is covered with golden sand. The lotus flowers in those pools are as large as chariot wheels, blue colored with blue radiance, yellow colored with yellow radiance, red colored with red radiance, and white colored with white radiance, exquisitely fragrant."

**7** "The land called Ultimate Bliss is constantly filled with heavenly music. The ground is made of gold. Day and night, flowers of the mandara rain down from heaven. The beings of that land, in the calm of morning, each take flower baskets and make offerings to the ten trillion Buddhas of the other regions."

**8** "If there are sentient beings who hear of that land, they should aspire to be born there. Why? Because those who are born there are all beings of superior goodness, gathered together in one place."

**9** The Buddha said to Ananda and the assembly, "If good men and good women hear of the Buddha Amitayus and hold fast to his name, whether for one day, two days, three days, four days, five days, six days, or seven days, with concentrated and undistracted mind, then when the end of life comes, the Buddha Amitayus, together with his holy assembly, will appear before them."

**10** "At the moment of death, their minds will not be confused, and they will at once gain rebirth in the Land of Ultimate Bliss of the Buddha Amitayus. This is why I encourage all sentient beings to aspire to be born in that land."
