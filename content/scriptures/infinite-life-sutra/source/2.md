---
title: "Infinite Life Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "infinite-life-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Buddha said to Ananda, "In the distant past, inconceivable and innumerable kalpas ago, a Tathagata named Lokeshvararaja appeared in the world. He taught the Dharma for the benefit of sentient beings."

**2** "At that time there was a king who, hearing the Buddha's teaching, was filled with joy. He awakened the aspiration for supreme enlightenment. Having renounced his kingdom, he became a monk and was named Dharmakara."

**3** "His wisdom was outstanding and his resolve was supreme. In all the world, none could compare with him. He went to the Tathagata Lokeshvararaja and said: 'I have awakened the unsurpassed aspiration for enlightenment. I beseech you to teach me the Dharma, so that I may establish a pure Buddha land and attain supreme enlightenment.'"

**4** "The Buddha Lokeshvararaja taught him broadly about the qualities of two hundred and ten million Buddha lands. In accordance with his wish, the Buddha revealed to him all those lands."

**5** "Upon hearing this teaching and seeing all those lands, the Bhikkhu Dharmakara was inspired and spent five full kalpas contemplating the qualities and adornments of those pure lands. He then selected the finest features and established forty-eight great vows."

**6** "He said: 'If, when I attain Buddhahood, sentient beings in the lands of the ten directions who sincerely and joyfully entrust themselves to me, desire to be born in my land, and recite my name even ten times, should not be born there, may I not attain perfect enlightenment.'"

**7** "'If, when I attain Buddhahood, the sentient beings born in my land should not all possess the thirty-two marks of a great being, may I not attain perfect enlightenment.'"

**8** "'If, when I attain Buddhahood, the sentient beings born in my land should not all be of one appearance, may I not attain perfect enlightenment. If they should not all possess supernatural powers, may I not attain perfect enlightenment.'"

**9** "'If, when I attain Buddhahood, the light emanating from my body should be limited, not illuminating at least a hundred thousand million nayutas of Buddha lands, may I not attain perfect enlightenment.'"

**10** "'If, when I attain Buddhahood, my life span should be limited, not lasting at least a hundred thousand million nayutas of kalpas, may I not attain perfect enlightenment.' Thus he established his great vows."
