Dharmakara's 48 vows, in which a bodhisattva pledges to create an ideal world for the liberation of all beings, have no direct parallel in the Pali canon. The Theravada tradition does recognize the concept of a bodhisatta (one aspiring to Buddhahood) who makes great resolves (abhinihara), as described in the Jataka tales and the Buddhavamsa. However, the Theravada bodhisatta's resolve is typically focused on personal attainment of Buddhahood rather than the creation of a Buddha-field. The contrast illuminates a fundamental difference between Theravada and Mahayana soteriology: the scope and nature of the bodhisattva's aspiration. Theravada readers may nonetheless appreciate the depth of compassion expressed in vows that seek to ensure no being is left behind.
