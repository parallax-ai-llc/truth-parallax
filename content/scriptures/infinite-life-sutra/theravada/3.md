The exhortation to seek rebirth in Sukhavati through faith and recitation of Amitabha's name represents a soteriology fundamentally different from the Theravada emphasis on personal insight and meditative attainment. In Theravada teaching, liberation comes through the practitioner's own effort in developing morality, concentration, and wisdom. The Infinite Life Sutra's teaching that sincere calling upon Amitabha's name can secure rebirth in a realm where enlightenment is guaranteed challenges this self-power orientation. Modern Theravada-Pure Land dialogue has explored whether these represent genuinely incompatible approaches or complementary emphases within the broader Buddhist tradition.
