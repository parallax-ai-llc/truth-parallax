The elaborate description of Sukhavati — its jeweled trees, fragrant lotus pools, celestial music, and luminous radiance — contrasts with the Theravada understanding of liberation as the cessation of suffering rather than entry into a perfected realm. The Pali canon does describe heavenly realms (deva-loka) with great beauty and pleasure, but these are understood as impermanent destinations within samsara, not as environments conducive to final liberation. The Infinite Life Sutra's innovation lies in describing a realm that combines the beauty of a heaven with the soteriological function of a place of practice — a concept foreign to classical Theravada thought but comprehensible within its cosmological framework.
