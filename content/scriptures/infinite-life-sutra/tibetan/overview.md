## Overview

While Pure Land Buddhism as a distinct school did not develop in Tibet, devotion to Amitabha Buddha and aspiration for rebirth in Sukhavati (Tibetan: Dewachen) is deeply woven into Tibetan Buddhist practice. The Infinite Life Sutra's themes resonate through aspiration prayers, phowa (consciousness transference) practice, and the widely recited "Prayer of Sukhavati" composed by various Tibetan masters.

## Phowa Practice

The Tibetan practice of phowa — transferring consciousness to Amitabha's Pure Land at the moment of death — represents a tantric engagement with the Infinite Life Sutra's promise of rebirth in Sukhavati. This practice integrates visualization, mantra, and yogic technique in a distinctively Vajrayana approach to the Pure Land teaching.

## Aspiration Prayers

Tibetan masters including Karma Chagme and the First Panchen Lama composed widely recited prayers for rebirth in Dewachen. These prayers blend Pure Land aspiration with Vajrayana visualization and dedication of merit, demonstrating how the Infinite Life Sutra's vision has been adapted within the Tibetan Buddhist framework.
