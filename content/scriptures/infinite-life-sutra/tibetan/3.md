The exhortation to practice finds expression in Tibetan Buddhism through the widespread recitation of Amitabha's name and mantra (OM AMI DEWA HRIH), aspiration prayers for rebirth in Dewachen, and the practice of phowa. While the Tibetan approach adds Vajrayana elements — visualization, mantra, and yogic technique — the underlying aspiration is the same as in East Asian Pure Land practice: to secure rebirth in a realm where complete enlightenment is assured. The Tibetan tradition emphasizes that this practice is especially important at the moment of death, when the direction of consciousness can be decisively influenced.
