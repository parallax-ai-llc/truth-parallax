The description of Sukhavati corresponds to the Tibetan understanding of Dewachen, which is the subject of extensive visualization practice. Tibetan practitioners visualize Amitabha's Pure Land in great detail — its topography, inhabitants, and qualities — as part of aspiration prayers and phowa practice. The sutra's vivid imagery provides the foundation for these visualizations. In the Tibetan framework, the visualization is not mere imagination but a practice that purifies perception and aligns the practitioner's mind with the qualities of the Pure Land, making rebirth there more likely through the power of familiarization (goms pa).
