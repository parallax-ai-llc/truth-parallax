Dharmakara's 48 vows resonate with the Tibetan Buddhist emphasis on the bodhisattva vow (smon lam) as the driving force of the path to Buddhahood. The Tibetan tradition's own aspiration prayers — such as Samantabhadra's Prayer of Good Actions and the Prayer for Rebirth in Sukhavati — share the same structure of detailed aspirations for the welfare of all beings. The scale of Dharmakara's vision, spanning countless eons of practice to create a perfect Buddha-field, exemplifies the Mahayana understanding of the bodhisattva's commitment that Tibetan practitioners are trained to emulate through the practice of bodhicitta.
