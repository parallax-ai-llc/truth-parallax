Dharmakara's 48 vows invite comparison with covenant and promise traditions across religions. The structure of conditional commitment — 'If this condition is not met, may I not attain Buddhahood' — parallels the biblical covenant formula in which God binds himself to specific promises. However, Dharmakara is not a creator God but a bodhisattva whose vows create a realm through accumulated merit and wisdom. The vows can also be compared with the Bodhisattva's Fourfold Vow in Mahayana Buddhism and with the Christian concept of Christ's redemptive work — in each case, a being of supreme spiritual capacity acts for the benefit of all others.
