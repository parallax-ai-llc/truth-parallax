The exhortation to practice through recitation and faith invites comparison with devotional practices across religions: the Hindu practice of japa (repetitive recitation of a divine name), the Christian Jesus Prayer, the Islamic dhikr (remembrance of God), and the Jewish practice of hitbodedut (personal prayer). All these traditions recognize the transformative power of sustained, focused invocation of the sacred. The Pure Land teaching that faith and recitation can accomplish what meditation and ascetic practice cannot parallels the bhakti traditions of Hinduism, where devotion to a personal deity is held to be more accessible and effective than philosophical knowledge or yogic discipline.
