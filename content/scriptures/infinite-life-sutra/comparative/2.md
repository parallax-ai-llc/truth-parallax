The description of Sukhavati invites comparison with paradise traditions across world religions: the Garden of Eden, the Islamic Jannah with its rivers and gardens, the Hindu Vaikuntha, and the Celtic Otherworld. All share imagery of abundant beauty, harmony, and freedom from suffering. However, Sukhavati differs from most paradise concepts in being explicitly a means to an end — a realm designed to facilitate further spiritual progress rather than a final destination. This instrumental understanding of paradise is distinctive to Buddhism and reflects the tradition's overriding concern with liberation from the cycle of rebirth rather than with eternal enjoyment.
