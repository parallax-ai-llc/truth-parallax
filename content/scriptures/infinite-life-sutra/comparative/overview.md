## Overview

The Infinite Life Sutra's teaching on salvation through Amitabha's compassionate power invites comparison with grace-based soteriology in Christianity, bhakti devotion in Hinduism, and paradise traditions across world religions. The structural parallels between Pure Land Buddhism and Protestant Christianity have been noted since the earliest encounters between these traditions.

## Grace and Other-Power

The Pure Land concept of tariki (other-power) bears striking resemblance to the Christian doctrine of salvation by grace through faith. Shinran's radical formulation — that the nembutsu is not the practitioner's act but Amitabha's gift — parallels Luther's insistence on sola gratia. Karl Barth and D.T. Suzuki explored these parallels in a famous exchange.

## Paradise Traditions

Sukhavati can be compared with the Christian heaven, the Islamic Jannah, and the Hindu Vaikuntha. However, Sukhavati differs fundamentally in being a way-station for enlightenment rather than a final destination, and in being the product of a bodhisattva's vows rather than a pre-existing divine realm.
