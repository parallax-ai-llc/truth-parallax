The 48 vows have been the subject of extensive academic study, particularly regarding their textual history and doctrinal development. Comparison of the various Chinese translations reveals that the number and content of the vows varied — from 24 in some versions to 48 in the standard version — suggesting a process of expansion over time. Scholars such as Luis Gomez have analyzed the literary structure of the vows, noting their systematic coverage of every aspect of the ideal Buddha-land. The 18th Vow's centrality in later Pure Land theology, particularly in Honen's and Shinran's interpretations, has been studied as a case of hermeneutical selectivity that transformed the sutra's comprehensive vision into a focused devotional practice.
