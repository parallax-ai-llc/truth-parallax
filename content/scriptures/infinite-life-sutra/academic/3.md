The exhortation to practice has been studied in relation to the development of Pure Land devotion as a mass movement. Scholars have examined how the sutra's relatively open-ended prescription — faith, aspiration, and mindfulness of Amitabha — was progressively narrowed by later commentators into specific practices, particularly vocal recitation of the name. Daniel Stevenson's work on Chinese Pure Land liturgy and James Dobbins' study of Jodo Shinshu have shown how the sutra's exhortation was institutionalized in different ways across East Asian Buddhist cultures, reflecting local social conditions and existing religious practices.
