The exhortation to practice nembutsu (nianfo) has been integrated into Chan practice in several ways. The most characteristic Chan approach is to use the nembutsu as a form of concentration practice or koan inquiry. The practitioner recites 'Namo Amituofo' with full attention, or investigates 'Who is reciting the Buddha's name?' — transforming devotional practice into a method of self-inquiry. This dual practice tradition (chan-jing shuangxiu) became the dominant form of Chinese Buddhism from the Ming dynasty onward, demonstrating that Zen meditation and Pure Land recitation can function as complementary rather than competing methods.
