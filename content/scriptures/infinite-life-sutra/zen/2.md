When Zen reads the description of Sukhavati, it asks: Where is this Pure Land? The Chan tradition answers: it is the pure nature of your own mind. The jeweled trees are the virtues of the awakened mind; the lotus pools are the clarity of meditation; the celestial music is the dharma heard in every sound. This internalization of the Pure Land does not dismiss the sutra's description but deepens it. As Huineng reportedly taught, 'When the mind is pure, the Buddha-land is pure.' The description of Sukhavati thus becomes a mirror showing practitioners what their own mind looks like when freed from defilement — a vision that motivates and guides practice.
