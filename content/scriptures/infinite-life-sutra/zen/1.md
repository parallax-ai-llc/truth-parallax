Zen's engagement with Dharmakara's vows focuses on their inner meaning rather than their cosmological content. The question 'Who makes these vows?' becomes a point of investigation. If all beings possess Buddha-nature, then Dharmakara's vows are not merely the aspiration of an ancient bodhisattva but an expression of the vow-nature inherent in every mind. Chan Master Yongming Yanshou taught that the true meaning of Dharmakara's vows is realized when the practitioner awakens to their own mind's capacity for infinite compassion and wisdom. The vows are not received from outside but discovered within — the 'self-nature Amitabha' making vows to liberate all aspects of the 'mind-only Pure Land.'
