## Overview

An interfaith approach to the Coffin Texts finds in the democratization of the afterlife a theme of profound relevance to many religious traditions: the extension of spiritual hope and salvific knowledge beyond a privileged elite to all people.

## Key Interfaith Themes

### Universalization of Salvation
The Coffin Texts' extension of afterlife privileges from royalty to commoners parallels:
- **Buddhism**: The Buddha's rejection of caste-based spiritual hierarchy
- **Christianity**: "In Christ there is neither Jew nor Greek, slave nor free" (Galatians 3:28)
- **Islam**: All believers equal before God regardless of social status
- **Sikhism**: Rejection of caste and assertion of universal spiritual equality

### Transformation and Spiritual Rebirth
The transformation spells resonate with:
- **Christianity**: Being "born again" and transformation in Christ
- **Buddhism**: Transformation through enlightenment
- **Hinduism**: Liberation (moksha) through spiritual realization
- **Sufism**: Fana — annihilation of the ego self in God

### Navigating the Spiritual Journey
The Book of Two Ways as a map for the afterlife resonates with:
- **Buddhism**: Detailed maps of the stages of meditation and realization
- **Christianity**: Spiritual progress through stages of prayer (Teresa of Avila)
- **Sufism**: The stations (maqamat) of the spiritual path
