## Overview

Theological study of the Coffin Texts examines the significant religious developments of the Middle Kingdom, particularly the democratization of the afterlife and the evolution of Osirian theology. These texts mark a pivotal shift in Egyptian theological thinking about who has access to eternal life and on what basis.

## Key Theological Themes

### Democratization of Salvation
The most significant theological development in the Coffin Texts is the extension of resurrection hope beyond the pharaoh to non-royal individuals. This represents a fundamental shift in Egyptian soteriology — the theology of salvation.

### Osirian and Solar Synthesis
The Coffin Texts increasingly synthesize Osirian and solar theological traditions, allowing the deceased to identify with both Osiris (lord of the underworld) and Ra (lord of the heavens), participating in both the nocturnal and diurnal aspects of the cosmic cycle.

### Moral Accountability
While the full judgment scene of the Book of the Dead has not yet fully developed, the Coffin Texts show an increasing emphasis on moral character as a prerequisite for afterlife blessings.

### The Nature of the Divine
The Coffin Texts present a rich and complex theology in which the boundaries between divine and human are permeable — the deceased can become a god through proper ritual and moral preparation.
