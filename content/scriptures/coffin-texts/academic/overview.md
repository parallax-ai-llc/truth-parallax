## Overview

Academic study of the Coffin Texts focuses on their role as a transitional corpus between the royal Pyramid Texts and the more widely available Book of the Dead. Scholars examine the linguistic, theological, and social dimensions of these Middle Kingdom funerary compositions.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Textual Transmission
Scholars trace how spells from the Pyramid Texts were adapted and transformed in the Coffin Texts, analyzing changes in language, theology, and intended audience. This process of textual transmission reveals the mechanisms of religious change in ancient Egypt.

### Social History
The Coffin Texts provide crucial evidence for the democratization of the afterlife during the First Intermediate Period and Middle Kingdom. Academic analysis examines who had access to these texts and what this reveals about social stratification.

### The Book of Two Ways
The Book of Two Ways (CT spells 1029-1185) is studied as perhaps the earliest known map of the afterlife, providing invaluable evidence for Egyptian cosmography.
