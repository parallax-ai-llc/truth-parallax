## Overview

Comparative study of the Coffin Texts focuses on the process of religious democratization — the extension of afterlife privileges from elites to common people — which parallels similar developments in other ancient civilizations and religious traditions.

## Key Comparative Themes

### Democratization of the Afterlife
The spread of funerary privileges from royalty to commoners in Egypt parallels:
- **Greek religion**: The democratization of mystery cult participation
- **Hinduism**: The extension of Vedic ritual rights beyond the Brahmin caste
- **Buddhism**: The universalization of the path to liberation

### Transformation and Metamorphosis
The Coffin Texts' transformation spells find parallels in:
- **Ovid's Metamorphoses**: Transformation as divine power and punishment
- **Shamanic traditions**: Shape-shifting and spiritual transformation
- **Hindu avatars**: Divine manifestation in multiple forms

### Mapping the Afterlife
The Book of Two Ways as an early afterlife map parallels:
- **Dante's Divine Comedy**: Detailed mapping of afterlife realms
- **Tibetan Buddhist cosmography**: Detailed descriptions of bardo realms
- **Islamic descriptions**: The stages of Barzakh and the afterlife
