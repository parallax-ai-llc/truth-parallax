## Overview

The Coffin Texts are a collection of ancient Egyptian funerary spells written on coffins during the Middle Kingdom period (approximately 2055-1650 BCE). They represent a crucial transitional phase in Egyptian funerary literature, bridging the earlier Pyramid Texts — which were reserved exclusively for royalty — and the later Book of the Dead, which became available to anyone who could afford a papyrus scroll.

The democratization of the afterlife is the defining feature of the Coffin Texts. For the first time, non-royal individuals — nobles, officials, and wealthy commoners — could access spells and rituals previously restricted to the pharaoh. This shift reflects broader social and political changes during the First Intermediate Period and Middle Kingdom.

## Structure

The Coffin Texts contain over 1,100 individual spells, organized here into 20 thematic sections:

1. **Sections 1-7**: Protection, provisioning, and initial journey to the afterlife
2. **Sections 8-14**: Transformation spells, divine identification, and celestial navigation
3. **Sections 15-20**: The Book of Two Ways, judgment, and eternal existence

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Coffin Texts hold significance across multiple fields:

- **Academic**: Key source for Middle Kingdom religion, language, and social history
- **Comparative**: Evidence for the democratization of religious ideas across cultures
- **Hermetic**: Links between Middle Kingdom theology and later esoteric traditions
- **Theological**: The extension of resurrection hope beyond royalty to all people
- **Archaeological**: Intimate connection with coffin art, burial customs, and material culture
- **Interfaith**: Universal themes of equality before death and hope for transformation

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Democratization | The extension of afterlife privileges from royalty to commoners |
| Transformation | Spells enabling the deceased to become various divine beings |
| Cosmic Geography | The Book of Two Ways and the mapping of the underworld |
| Divine Identification | The deceased becoming one with Osiris and other gods |
| Protective Magic | Warding off demons and dangers in the Duat |
