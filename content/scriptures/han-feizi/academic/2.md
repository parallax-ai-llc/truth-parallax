Academic analysis of Han Feizi section 2, "On Having Standards," examines the text's argumentative strategies, historical context, and philosophical implications. Scholars have noted the sophistication of Han Fei's reasoning about institutional design, information asymmetry, and incentive structures — analyses that anticipate modern insights from rational choice theory and institutional economics.

The section has been compared with parallel passages in Western political realism, particularly Machiavelli's Prince and Hobbes's Leviathan. Scholars debate whether Han Fei's prescriptions were intended as universal principles of governance or as emergency measures for the specific crisis of the Warring States period. Questions of textual authenticity — whether this section was composed by Han Fei himself or by later members of the Legalist school — remain actively discussed.
