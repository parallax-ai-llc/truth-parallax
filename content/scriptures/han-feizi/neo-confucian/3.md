Neo-Confucian reading of Han Feizi section 3, "The Two Handles," measures Legalist claims against the standard of heavenly principle (tian li). The Neo-Confucian tradition holds that all legitimate governance must be grounded in moral reality, not merely in the effective exercise of power. Han Fei's arguments in this section, however politically astute, represent for Neo-Confucians a systematic inversion of the proper relationship between virtue and authority.

Qing dynasty scholars, more empirically minded than their Song predecessors, occasionally found in sections like this useful observations about administrative practice and institutional design. The challenge for the Neo-Confucian tradition has been to appropriate Han Fei's practical insights without accepting his amoral premises — to build institutions that serve moral ends rather than merely perpetuating power.
