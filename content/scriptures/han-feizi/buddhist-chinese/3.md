Chinese Buddhist interpretation of Han Feizi section 3, "The Two Handles," reads Legalist political analysis as an unwitting description of samsaric existence. Han Fei's observations about self-interest, manipulation, and the struggle for power accurately describe the condition of beings trapped in the cycle of craving and aversion. The Buddhist reader does not dispute the accuracy of these observations but rejects the conclusion that the appropriate response is to design more efficient systems of exploitation.

The Buddhist alternative to Han Fei's program is the cultivation of wisdom (prajna) and compassion (karuna) — qualities that transform the very motivational structure that Legalism takes as fixed. Where Han Fei sees self-interest as an immutable fact to be harnessed, Buddhism sees it as a form of ignorance to be overcome through practice and realization.
