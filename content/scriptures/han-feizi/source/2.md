## Cun Han (Preserving Han)

The state of Han is small and weak, surrounded by powerful enemies. How can it be preserved? Not through virtue alone, not through diplomacy alone, but through a clear-eyed assessment of the balance of power.

The small state must be clever where the large state can afford to be generous. The small state must be vigilant where the large state can afford to be careless.

A small state preserves itself by making itself useful to its powerful neighbors. It must offer something that the powerful state needs -- strategic position, skilled diplomacy, reliable alliance. If it offers nothing, it will be swallowed.

But usefulness alone is not sufficient. The small state must also maintain its internal strength. Its laws must be clear. Its officials must be capable. Its people must be united. A small state with good governance can survive; a large state with bad governance will perish.

The history of the states demonstrates this truth: Small states with wise rulers have outlasted large states with foolish ones. It is not size that determines survival, but the quality of governance.

Therefore, the ruler of a small state must be especially diligent. He cannot afford the mistakes that a powerful ruler might survive. Every policy must be calculated, every appointment must be justified, every resource must be used wisely.

The first step in preserving the state is knowing its true condition -- neither overestimating its strength nor underestimating its danger.
