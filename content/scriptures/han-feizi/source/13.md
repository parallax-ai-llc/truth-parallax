## He Shi (The Jade Disc of He)

A man of Chu named He found a piece of jade-bearing stone in the mountains. He presented it to King Li. The king ordered his jeweler to examine it. The jeweler said: "It is just a stone." The king, considering He a fraud, had his left foot cut off as punishment.

When King Li died and King Wu ascended the throne, He again presented the stone. King Wu ordered his jeweler to examine it. Again the jeweler said: "It is just a stone." King Wu had He's right foot cut off.

When King Wu died and King Wen ascended the throne, He went to the foot of the mountain and wept for three days and three nights until his tears were exhausted and he wept blood.

King Wen heard of this and sent a messenger to ask: "Many men have had their feet cut off. Why do you weep so bitterly?" He replied: "I do not weep because my feet were cut off. I weep because a precious jade is called a mere stone, and an honest man is called a fraud. This is why I weep."

King Wen ordered the stone cut open. Inside was found a treasure of incomparable beauty.

The lesson: True worth is often unrecognized by those who rely on superficial experts. The honest man who presents the truth may suffer, while charlatans with worthless stones may prosper -- until the truth is finally revealed.
