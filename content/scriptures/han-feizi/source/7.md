## Er Bing (The Two Handles)

The two handles of government are punishment and favor. What are called punishment and favor? To inflict death and suffering is called punishment. To bestow honor and reward is called favor.

Those who act as ministers fear the penalties and hope for the rewards. Therefore, if the ruler wields his punishments and favors, the ministers will fear his severity and rally to his bounty.

But if the ruler does not exercise the handles of punishment and favor himself, but instead entrusts them to his ministers, then the people will fear the ministers and despise the ruler. The ruler will lose his authority, and the ministers will gain it.

The ruler of the state of Han entrusted punishment to his prime minister and rewards to his favorite consort. As a result, the ministers served the prime minister and the consort rather than the ruler. The ruler sat on the throne but held no real power.

Therefore, the ruler must never relinquish the two handles. When the ruler holds the handles himself, order prevails. When he loses them, disorder follows.

If the ruler rewards those whom the minister wants rewarded, then the minister's power grows and the ruler's shrinks. If the ruler punishes those whom the minister wants punished, then the minister is feared and the ruler is ignored.

The enlightened ruler keeps the two handles in his own hands. He rewards and punishes based on his own judgment of merit and fault, not based on the recommendations of interested parties.
