## Jian Jie Shi Chen (The Difficulties of the Loyal Minister)

The loyal minister faces difficulties unknown to the treacherous. He must speak the truth when the truth is unwelcome. He must oppose the ruler's wishes when those wishes are harmful. He must risk his life to serve the state.

The treacherous minister has no such burdens. He tells the ruler what he wants to hear. He supports the ruler's every whim. He risks nothing.

Therefore the path of loyalty is steep and narrow, while the path of treachery is broad and smooth. It is no wonder that there are many treacherous ministers and few loyal ones.

The loyal minister is like the bitter medicine that cures disease. The treacherous minister is like the sweet poison that kills. The sick man prefers the sweet poison to the bitter medicine. The foolish ruler prefers the treacherous minister to the loyal one.

Consider the case of Wu Zixu: He admonished the King of Wu not to trust the state of Yue. The king refused to listen. Wu Zixu persisted in his warnings. The king grew angry and ordered Wu Zixu to kill himself. After Wu Zixu's death, the state of Yue attacked and destroyed Wu -- exactly as Wu Zixu had predicted.

The loyal minister's reward is often death. Yet still there are men who choose loyalty over treachery. This is because they value righteousness above life.

The enlightened ruler recognizes this sacrifice and protects his loyal ministers. He rewards courage in speaking the truth and punishes flattery. Only then will loyal ministers dare to serve.
