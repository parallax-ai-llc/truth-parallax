## Ai Chen (Loving the Minister)

The ruler who truly loves his ministers does not shower them with unearned rewards. The ruler who truly cares for his people does not exempt them from the law.

When the ruler gives rewards without merit, the ministers become lazy. When the ruler exempts people from law, the people become lawless. Indulgence is not love -- it is the destruction of those one claims to love.

The physician who cuts away diseased flesh causes pain, but he saves the patient's life. The ruler who enforces the law causes hardship, but he saves the state.

The truly loving parent is not the one who gives the child everything it wants, but the one who teaches the child discipline and prepares it for the world. So too with the ruler: True love for the ministers and people is shown through fair laws and consistent enforcement.

When rewards are given for merit, even the lazy become diligent. When punishments are applied for wrongdoing, even the wicked become cautious. This is the way to create a good society -- not through moral exhortation, but through proper incentives.

The ancient saying goes: "The loving mother has the spoiled child." When the mother is too indulgent, the child grows up undisciplined and eventually comes to harm. When the ruler is too indulgent, the ministers grow corrupt and the state falls into disorder.

Therefore: Strict fairness is the highest form of love in governance.
