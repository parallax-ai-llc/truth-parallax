## Ba Jian (Eight Villanies)

There are eight villanies by which ministers endanger their ruler:

The first is called "sharing the bed" -- when the ruler's wives and concubines are used by the minister to influence the ruler's decisions.

The second is called "being in the inner circle" -- when the ruler's attendants and favorites are used by the minister to distort the ruler's perception.

The third is called "father and brother" -- when the ruler's own relatives are used by the minister to circumvent the ruler's authority.

The fourth is called "nurturing the people's trust" -- when the minister uses his own wealth to buy the loyalty of the people, transferring their allegiance from the ruler to himself.

The fifth is called "making the people dependent" -- when the minister controls the distribution of benefits so that the people look to him rather than to the ruler.

The sixth is called "controlling speech" -- when the minister uses eloquent speakers and persuaders to shape public opinion in his own favor.

The seventh is called "wielding authority" -- when the minister builds his own military force or personal following.

The eighth is called "using external powers" -- when the minister makes alliances with foreign states to strengthen his own position against the ruler.

The enlightened ruler must be vigilant against all eight villanies. He must examine the conduct of his ministers closely and never allow any single minister to accumulate too much power. Divided authority is safe authority.
