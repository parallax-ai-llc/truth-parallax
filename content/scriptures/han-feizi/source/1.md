## Chu Jian Qin Nan (The First Interview with the King of Qin)

In the present age, the territory of the feudal lords stretches a thousand li, yet they cannot even bring order to their own states. Why? Because they rely on virtue and benevolence instead of law and statecraft.

The world has changed since the age of the sage kings. The population has grown. Resources are scarce. Men compete fiercely for advantage. In such an age, the methods of Yao and Shun are inadequate. What is needed is a system of clear laws, strict enforcement, and proper incentives.

If you wish to make the state strong, you must attend to agriculture and warfare. Enrich the people through farming; strengthen the army through training. These are the two pillars of national power.

The wise ruler does not rely on men being good. He creates a system in which even bad men are compelled to act well. He rewards those who serve the state and punishes those who harm it. Under such a system, the people naturally turn toward agriculture and warfare.

The ruler who follows the Way of the ancients without adaptation is like a man who waits by a stump for a rabbit to appear. One rabbit ran into a stump and died. The man waited by the stump forever after, hoping for another. He was the laughingstock of the whole state of Song.

Those who wish to govern with the methods of the former kings are all waiting by the stump.
