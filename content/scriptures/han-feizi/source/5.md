## Zhu Dao (The Way of the Ruler)

The Way of the ruler lies in emptiness and stillness. He does not show his likes, lest his ministers shape themselves to please him. He does not show his dislikes, lest his ministers conceal their true nature.

When the ruler shows his likes, the ministers will display those qualities whether they possess them or not. When the ruler shows his dislikes, the ministers will conceal those traits whether they possess them or not. Thus the ruler who reveals his mind makes himself blind.

The enlightened ruler is empty and still above, waiting without preconceptions. He lets his ministers present their proposals and then examines whether the results match the words. He does not originate projects himself but judges the projects his ministers propose.

Hold fast to the handle of law. Make your standards clear and firm. When words and deeds match, reward. When words and deeds do not match, punish. This is the method by which the ruler controls his ministers.

The ruler need not be wise in all things. He need not be skilled in all tasks. He need only know how to employ the wise and the skilled. The ruler who tries to do everything himself will exhaust himself and accomplish nothing.

The ruler is like the hub of a wheel. He remains at the center, still and empty, while the spokes of government radiate outward. If the hub moves, the wheel wobbles. If the hub is fixed, the wheel turns smoothly.

This is the paradox of power: The ruler who grasps at everything controls nothing. The ruler who remains still controls everything.
