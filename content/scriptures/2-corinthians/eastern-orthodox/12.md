## Visions, Thorns, and Power in Weakness

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Corinthians 12 approaches visions and weakness through the lens of theosis and the liturgical life of the church. The key passages — third heaven (12:2-4), thorn in the flesh (12:7), power in weakness (12:9-10) — are read as revealing the mystery of divine-human communion that is the goal of all Christian existence.

The Greek Fathers — Chrysostom, the Cappadocians, and Maximus the Confessor — shaped the Orthodox reading of this chapter, emphasizing the transformative encounter with God that Paul describes. Their exegesis remains normative within the Orthodox tradition, interpreted through the living experience of worship, asceticism, and prayer.

Orthodox liturgical theology finds in this passage echoes of the Divine Liturgy's movement from proclamation to transformation. The text is not merely studied but prayed, chanted, and lived within the worshipping community.
