## The God of All Comfort

Eastern Orthodox reading of the opening chapter finds in Paul's theology of suffering and comfort a paradigm for the Christian life understood as participation in the paschal mystery. The "sufferings of Christ" that abound in Paul (1:5) are not metaphorical but a real sharing in the divine life through kenosis.

The blessing formula (1:3-4) is liturgical in character and echoes the *berakah* tradition of Jewish prayer, which Orthodox worship has preserved and transformed. The God who comforts is the same God worshipped in the Divine Liturgy, where suffering and glory are held together in the mystery of the Eucharist.

Paul's near-death experience in Asia (1:8-11) exemplifies the Orthodox understanding of martyrdom and witness: the believer's life is held in the hands of "the God who raises the dead" (1:9), and this trust is the foundation of all Christian hope. The Fathers read this passage as an anticipation of the resurrection faith proclaimed at Pascha.
