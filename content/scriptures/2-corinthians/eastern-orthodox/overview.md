## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 2 Corinthians focuses on the themes of divine glory, transfiguration, and theosis (deification). Paul's statement that believers "are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another" (3:18) is a key text for the Orthodox doctrine of theosis — humanity's progressive participation in the divine nature.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Theosis and Transfiguration
The unveiling of glory in chapter 3 is read through the lens of the Transfiguration. Just as Moses' face shone, believers behold God's glory in Christ and are themselves transformed — not merely forensically declared righteous, but ontologically changed.

### Kenotic Ministry
Paul's weakness and suffering (4:7-12; 6:4-10) model the kenotic pattern of Orthodox pastoral ministry. The "treasure in jars of clay" (4:7) shows that divine power flows through human frailty, a principle central to Orthodox monasticism and priesthood.

### Liturgical Reconciliation
The "ministry of reconciliation" (5:18-20) is understood within the liturgical and sacramental life of the church, particularly the Mystery of Confession.
