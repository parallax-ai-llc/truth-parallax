## The God of Comfort and Hidden Suffering

Gnostic readers found in 2 Corinthians 1 a confirmation of the pneumatic believer's experience: the "affliction" Paul describes (1:4-8) reflects the suffering of the divine spark trapped in the material world, while the "comfort" comes from the true God above — not the Demiurge who created this world of suffering.

The "sentence of death" Paul received in Asia (1:9) was read as an encounter with the archonic powers that rule the material realm. Paul's survival through "the God who raises the dead" demonstrated that the true God intervenes to rescue the pneumatic elect from the Demiurge's domain.

Paul's distinction between the God who comforts and the world that afflicts maps onto the fundamental Gnostic dualism: the world is the creation of an inferior deity, but the true God — the Father of mercies — reaches into this broken creation to comfort and ultimately rescue those who possess the divine spark.
