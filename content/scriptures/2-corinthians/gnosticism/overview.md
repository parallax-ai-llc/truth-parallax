## Overview

Gnostic interpreters found in 2 Corinthians a rich source of imagery supporting their dualistic worldview. Paul's language of "inner" and "outer" persons (4:16), "treasure in jars of clay" (4:7), the contrast between the "seen" and the "unseen" (4:18), and the "veil" over Moses' face (3:13-16) all resonated with Gnostic themes of the divine spark trapped in material existence.

## Key Gnostic Readings

### The Inner Person and the Outer Shell
Paul's distinction between the "outer person" that is wasting away and the "inner person" being renewed (4:16) was read by Valentinian Gnostics as confirming the distinction between the pneumatic (spiritual) self and the material body — the true self is the divine spark imprisoned in flesh.

### Treasure in Earthen Vessels
The "treasure in jars of clay" (4:7) became a Gnostic metaphor for the divine *pneuma* (spirit) trapped within the material body fashioned by the Demiurge. Liberation comes through *gnosis* — knowledge of one's true divine origin.

### The Veil and Revelation
The veil over Moses' face (3:13-16) was interpreted as the ignorance imposed by the Demiurge, preventing humanity from seeing the true God. Christ removes the veil, revealing the hidden *pleroma* (fullness) beyond the material creator.
