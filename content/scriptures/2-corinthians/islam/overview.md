## Overview

Islamic engagement with 2 Corinthians draws on shared themes of patience in suffering (*sabr*), divine comfort, and the accountability of prophetic ministry. While Islam does not recognize Paul as a prophet, the letter's theological motifs resonate with Quranic teachings on the trials faced by God's messengers and the transience of worldly life.

## Quranic Resonances

### Suffering and Patience
Paul's catalogues of suffering (6:4-10; 11:23-28) parallel the Quran's accounts of prophets who endured persecution. The concept of *sabr* (patient endurance) is central: "Indeed, God is with the patient" (Quran 2:153).

### Comfort from God
The opening thanksgiving (1:3-7) — "the God of all comfort, who comforts us in all our affliction" — resonates with the Quranic assurance that "with hardship comes ease" (Quran 94:5-6).

### Generosity and Charity
Chapters 8–9's theology of giving aligns with the Islamic obligation of *zakat* (almsgiving) and *sadaqah* (voluntary charity). The principle that God loves a "cheerful giver" (9:7) parallels the Quranic teaching that charity purifies the soul (Quran 9:103).

## Points of Divergence

| 2 Corinthians Theme | Islamic Perspective |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| New covenant replacing old | Islam affirms continuous divine guidance, not supersession |
| Ministry of reconciliation through Christ | Reconciliation comes through submission to God directly |
| Paul's apostolic authority | Paul is not recognized as a prophet in Islam |
