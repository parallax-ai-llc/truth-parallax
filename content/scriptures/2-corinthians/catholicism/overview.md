## Overview

Catholic interpretation of 2 Corinthians emphasizes Paul's theology of apostolic ministry, sacramental reconciliation, and the redemptive value of suffering. The letter's depiction of Paul as a minister of the new covenant (3:6) has been central to Catholic understandings of ordained ministry and apostolic succession.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Apostolic Ministry and Succession
Paul's defense of his apostleship (chs. 10–13) is read as establishing the pattern for ordained ministry. The "ministry of reconciliation" (5:18-20) grounds the sacrament of penance and the priest's role as ambassador of Christ.

### Redemptive Suffering
Paul's catalogue of sufferings (6:4-10; 11:23-28) illustrates the Catholic teaching that suffering, united with Christ's passion, has redemptive value. The "thorn in the flesh" (12:7) models patient endurance.

### The Collection and Social Teaching
Chapters 8–9's theology of generosity aligns with Catholic social teaching on solidarity, the universal destination of goods, and preferential option for the poor.

## Theological Emphases

- **Reconciliation**: The ministry of reconciliation (5:18-20) as sacramental foundation
- **Suffering**: Participation in the sufferings of Christ (1:5) as sanctifying
- **Generosity**: Christ's poverty making many rich (8:9) as model for the church
