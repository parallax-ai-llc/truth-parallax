## Paul's Apostolic Authority

Protestant interpretation of 2 Corinthians 10 focuses on apostolic authority. The key passages — weapons not of the flesh (10:3-5), commending ourselves (10:12-18) — have been central to Reformed and evangelical theology, shaping understandings of grace, faith, and the Christian life.

Luther and Calvin both gave significant attention to this chapter, reading its themes through the lens of the law-gospel distinction and the sovereignty of God. The Reformation's emphasis on Scripture alone (*sola Scriptura*) made careful exegesis of Pauline texts like this one foundational for Protestant identity.

Modern Protestant scholarship engages this chapter through both traditional and New Perspective lenses, exploring how Paul's arguments functioned within their original context while remaining authoritative for the church today. The passage continues to shape Protestant preaching, devotional life, and theological reflection.
