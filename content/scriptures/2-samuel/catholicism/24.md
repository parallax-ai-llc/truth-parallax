## The Census and the Plague

The final chapter raises profound questions about divine justice, human sin, and the theology of sacrifice. Catholic tradition reads the threshing floor of Araunah as the site of the future temple — and thus a pivotal link in the chain from Abraham's sacrifice on Moriah to the Eucharistic sacrifice of Christ.

## Catholic Interpretive Tradition

### Divine Justice and Mercy

The tension between God's anger and David's choice to "fall into the hand of the LORD" illustrates the Catholic understanding that even God's justice is oriented toward mercy. **Pope Benedict XVI** in *Spe Salvi* reflected on the relationship between divine justice and divine love.

### The Theology of Sacrifice

David's insistence — "I will not offer burnt offerings to the LORD my God that cost me nothing" — is a foundational principle in Catholic spirituality and stewardship. True sacrifice requires personal cost. This principle extends from liturgical offering to the theology of the cross.

### The Temple Site

Catholic tradition identifies the threshing floor of Araunah with Mount Moriah (2 Chronicles 3:1), linking:

1. Abraham's near-sacrifice of Isaac (Genesis 22)
2. David's sacrifice to stop the plague (2 Samuel 24)
3. Solomon's temple (1 Kings 6)
4. Christ's sacrifice on Calvary — the definitive temple

### Liturgical Connection

The offering of sacrifice that averts divine wrath prefigures the Mass, in which Christ's sacrifice is re-presented to the Father for the salvation of the world.
