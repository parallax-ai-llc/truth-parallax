## The Ark Comes to Jerusalem

The transfer of the Ark of the Covenant to Jerusalem is a text of extraordinary liturgical and typological significance in Catholic tradition. The Ark prefigures both the Blessed Virgin Mary (who bore the divine presence within her) and the tabernacle of the Eucharist.

## Catholic Interpretive Tradition

### Mary as the New Ark

Catholic theology draws extensive parallels between the Ark narrative and the Visitation (Luke 1:39-56):

| 2 Samuel 6 | Luke 1 |
|------------|--------|
| David goes to the hill country of Judah | Mary goes to the hill country of Judah |
| "How can the ark of the LORD come to me?" (v. 9) | "Why is this granted to me?" (Luke 1:43) |
| David dances before the Ark | John leaps in Elizabeth's womb |
| The Ark stays three months at Obed-edom's house | Mary stays three months with Elizabeth |

**St. Ambrose**, **St. Athanasius**, and **Pope Benedict XVI** have all developed this typology. It is foundational to the Catholic doctrine of Mary as *Theotokos* and Ark of the New Covenant.

### The Death of Uzzah (6:6-8)

Catholic moral theology reads Uzzah's death as a lesson about approaching sacred things with proper reverence. The Council of Trent's teaching on worthy reception of the Eucharist echoes this theme: the sacred demands appropriate disposition.

### David's Liturgical Dance (6:14-15)

David's dancing before the Ark wearing a linen ephod is understood as a priestly act. Catholic liturgical theology sees here a precedent for the bodily dimension of worship — processions, genuflections, and liturgical gesture.

## Theological Significance

- **Marian Typology**: The Ark as prefiguration of Mary bearing Christ
- **Eucharistic Theology**: The Ark's presence bringing blessing as the Eucharistic presence sanctifies
- **Liturgical Worship**: David's procession as model for Catholic liturgical practice
- **Sacred and Profane**: The distinction between holy and common as permanent theological category
