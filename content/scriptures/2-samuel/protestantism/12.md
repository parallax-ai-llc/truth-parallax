## Nathan's Rebuke and Its Aftermath

Nathan the prophet confronts David with a parable about a rich man who steals a poor man's only lamb. David's furious response — "the man who did this deserves to die!" — becomes the trap: "You are the man!" This chapter contains David's repentance, the death of Bathsheba's first child, and the birth of Solomon.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Nathan's Parable (12:1-6)

The parable is a masterpiece of prophetic rhetoric. By disguising the accusation as a case requiring royal judgment, Nathan secures David's own condemnation before revealing the truth. **Calvin** saw this as a model of how the Word of God convicts the conscience through indirect means.

### "You Are the Man!" (12:7-12)

Nathan's declaration is among the most dramatic moments in Scripture. The oracle recounts God's generosity to David and pronounces judgment: the sword shall not depart from David's house, and what he did in secret will be done to him openly. Protestant theology reads this as illustrating that forgiveness does not eliminate temporal consequences.

### David's Repentance (12:13-23)

David's immediate confession — "I have sinned against the LORD" — is met with immediate forgiveness: "The LORD also has put away your sin; you shall not die." Yet the child dies. David's response (fasting while the child lives, then rising and worshiping after the death) puzzles his servants but reveals mature faith.

### Solomon's Birth (12:24-25)

The name Jedidiah ("beloved of the LORD") given by Nathan signals God's continuing favor despite judgment. Solomon's birth within this context of sin and grace is theologically rich.

## Theological Significance

- **Prophetic Courage**: Nathan models speaking truth to power
- **Repentance**: David as the paradigm of genuine repentance (cf. Psalm 51)
- **Grace and Judgment**: Forgiveness is real but consequences remain
- **God's Sovereignty**: Even from sin, God brings Solomon, the covenant heir
