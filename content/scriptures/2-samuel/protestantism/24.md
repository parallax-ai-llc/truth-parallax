## The Census and the Plague

The final chapter of 2 Samuel recounts David's census of Israel, God's wrath, a devastating plague, and David's purchase of Araunah's threshing floor for a sacrifice that stops the plague. This site, tradition holds, became the location of Solomon's temple.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### God's Anger and the Census (24:1-9)

The text states that "the anger of the LORD was kindled against Israel, and he incited David" to take a census. The parallel in 1 Chronicles 21:1 attributes the incitement to Satan. Protestant interpreters reconcile these accounts by invoking secondary causation: God permitted Satan to incite David, using David's sin to accomplish his purposes.

### The Choice of Punishment (24:10-14)

David is offered three punishments: three years of famine, three months fleeing enemies, or three days of plague. David chooses to "fall into the hand of the LORD, for his mercy is great." **Davis** notes the profound theology: David trusts God's character even in judgment.

### The Threshing Floor (24:15-25)

David insists on paying full price — "I will not offer burnt offerings to the LORD my God that cost me nothing." This becomes a foundational principle in Protestant stewardship theology. The site, identified with Mount Moriah (2 Chronicles 3:1), connects Abraham's near-sacrifice of Isaac, David's altar, and Solomon's temple.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine Sovereignty**: God's purposes are served even through sinful human actions
- **Costly Worship**: True devotion involves sacrifice, not convenience
- **Temple Theology**: The threshing floor connects sacrifice, temple, and ultimately Christ
- **Mercy in Judgment**: God relents and stops the plague — grace within wrath
