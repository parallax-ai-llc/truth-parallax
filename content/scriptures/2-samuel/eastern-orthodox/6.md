## The Ark Brought to Jerusalem

The procession of the Ark into Jerusalem is one of the most liturgically resonant passages in 2 Samuel for Orthodox Christianity. The entire episode — from Uzzah's death to David's dancing — is read as a revelation of the proper approach to the divine presence and a prefiguration of the Incarnation.

## Patristic Interpretation

### The Ark and the Theotokos

Orthodox theology draws a profound typological connection between the Ark of the Covenant and the Blessed Virgin Mary (Theotokos). Just as the Ark contained the presence of God, so Mary bore the incarnate Word within her womb. The hymns of the Dormition feast explicitly invoke this typology:

- The Ark overshadowed by divine glory / Mary overshadowed by the Holy Spirit
- David dancing before the Ark / John the Baptist leaping in Elizabeth's womb
- The Ark bringing blessing to Obed-edom's house / Mary bringing blessing to Elizabeth's house

### The Death of Uzzah

**Theodoret of Cyrus** interpreted Uzzah's death as a lesson about approaching the divine mysteries with proper fear and preparation. This connects to the Orthodox emphasis on preparatory fasting and confession before receiving Holy Communion.

### David's Dancing

St. Ambrose, honored in both East and West, interpreted David's dance as the soul's response to the divine presence — ecstatic worship that transcends social convention. In Orthodox liturgical practice, the body participates fully in worship through prostrations, processions, and gestures of veneration.

## Liturgical Significance

The Ark procession prefigures the Great Entrance in the Divine Liturgy, when the gifts of bread and wine are carried through the nave to the altar. The reverence, the procession, the hymns — all echo David's bringing of the Ark to Zion.
