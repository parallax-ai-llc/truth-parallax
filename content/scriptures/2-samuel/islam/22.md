## David's Song of Deliverance

David's psalm of thanksgiving is of particular interest to Islamic scholarship because of its connection to the Zabur (Psalms), which the Quran identifies as divine revelation given to Dawud. The themes of praise, divine power, and deliverance align with the Quranic portrayal of Dawud as the prophet of praise.

## Islamic Interpretation

### The Zabur Tradition

The Quran affirms that God gave Dawud the Zabur (Psalms):
- **Quran 4:163**: "And to Dawud We gave the Zabur"
- **Quran 17:55**: "And We have made some of the prophets exceed others, and to Dawud We gave the Zabur"

Islamic scholars understand the Zabur as a book of praise, prayer, and wisdom. While the current Book of Psalms is not considered a perfectly preserved version of the original Zabur, passages like 2 Samuel 22 that focus on praising God for deliverance are considered closest to the spirit of the original revelation.

### Divine Praise in Nature

The theophanic imagery of this psalm — mountains trembling, divine fire, cosmic upheaval — resonates with the Quranic theme of all creation praising God alongside Dawud. Surah Saba (34:10) describes mountains and birds joining in praise, and Surah Sad (38:18) speaks of mountains glorifying God with Dawud at evening and sunrise.

### Tawhid and the Psalm

Islamic readers approach the psalm's theology through the lens of strict *tawhid* (monotheism). The imagery of God as warrior, rock, and fortress is acceptable as metaphorical language, but any suggestion of divine embodiment or plurality is rejected.

## Theological Themes

- **Praise as Prophetic Vocation**: Dawud's primary role is as the prophet of praise
- **Nature Praising God**: Mountains and birds join Dawud in glorifying the Creator
- **The Zabur**: This psalm reflects the spirit of the original divine revelation
- **Divine Protection**: God safeguards his prophets and grants them victory
