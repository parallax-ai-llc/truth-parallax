## Summary

2 Samuel 10 — War with Ammon and Aram. Islamic interpretation approaches this chapter through the Quran and Hadith as corrective standards, recognizing David (Dawud) as a prophet and king while evaluating the biblical narrative for consistency with Islamic theological principles.

## Islamic Interpretation

### Quranic Framework

The Quran presents Dawud as a prophet-king endowed with wisdom, strength, and the Zabur (Psalms). He is described as one who glorified God constantly, with mountains and birds joining his praise (Quran 34:10, 38:18-19). All narratives about Dawud are evaluated against this Quranic portrait.

### Assessment of the Biblical Account

Islamic scholarship applies several criteria when evaluating biblical narratives:

- **Consistency with the Quran**: Passages aligned with Quranic teaching may be accepted
- **Prophetic dignity**: Narratives showing prophets in morally compromising positions are attributed to *tahrif* (textual corruption)
- **Isra'iliyyat classification**: Stories from Israelite sources are categorized as confirmed, neutral, or rejected

### The Events Described

The events in this chapter are treated within the Islamic framework of prophetic history. Where the narrative aligns with Islamic values of justice, mercy, wisdom, and divine sovereignty, Muslim scholars find common ground. Where it diverges — particularly regarding prophetic behavior or divine attributes — the Quranic account takes precedence.

## Theological Themes

- **Divine Sovereignty**: God grants authority and guides events according to his wisdom
- **Just Kingship**: Dawud as a model of the ruler who judges with equity
- **Prophetic Character**: The dignity and moral excellence of God's chosen messengers
- **Scripture and Preservation**: The importance of the Quran as the final, preserved revelation
