## David's Song of Deliverance

This great psalm (paralleling Psalm 18) is David's thanksgiving for deliverance from all his enemies. Jewish tradition reads it as both a personal poem and a prophetic text with eschatological dimensions.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### Placement and Significance

**Radak** notes that the placement of this psalm near the end of 2 Samuel is deliberate: it summarizes David's entire career of divine deliverance. The parallels with Psalm 18 are extensive, with minor textual variations that the commentators discuss at length. **Malbim** uses the differences between the two versions to draw out nuances of meaning.

### Theophanic Imagery (22:8-16)

The description of God descending with earthquake, smoke, fire, and darkness draws on the Sinai theophany tradition. **Rashi** interprets this language as poetic rather than literal, describing God's intervention in David's battles through figurative cosmic imagery. **Radak** agrees but adds that such language also has eschatological significance, pointing to the ultimate divine intervention in the messianic age.

### David's Righteousness Claims (22:21-28)

The claim "The LORD rewarded me according to my righteousness" raises obvious questions given chapters 11-12. **Rashi** explains that David is speaking of his consistent refusal to harm Saul despite having opportunities — a specific righteousness, not a claim of sinless perfection. **Metzudat David** similarly limits the claim to David's behavior during his years of persecution by Saul.

### Messianic Conclusion (22:50-51)

"He gives great salvation to his king and shows steadfast love to his anointed, to David and to his offspring forever" — this concluding verse reinforces the Davidic covenant (ch. 7) and Jewish messianic hope. The word "forever" (*ad olam*) points toward the eternal kingship of the Mashiach.

## Theological Themes

- **Gratitude**: The obligation to praise God for deliverance
- **Poetic Theology**: How figurative language conveys theological truth
- **Messianic Hope**: The psalm's conclusion pointing toward the Mashiach
- **Righteousness and Humility**: David's claims understood within their proper context
