## Summary

2 Samuel 18 — Absalom's Death. In Jewish tradition, this chapter belongs to the Nevi'im Rishonim (Former Prophets) and is read not merely as historical narrative but as prophetic literature conveying divine instruction through the events of David's reign.

## Rabbinic Interpretation

### The Classical Commentators

**Rashi** (Rabbi Shlomo Yitzchaki) provides close textual commentary, resolving difficulties by reference to Talmudic discussions and midrashic traditions. His peshat-oriented approach clarifies the plain meaning while incorporating relevant aggadic material.

**Radak** (Rabbi David Kimchi) offers grammatically precise interpretation, attending to Hebrew syntax and vocabulary while drawing theological conclusions from the narrative details.

**Ralbag** (Gersonides) extracts moral lessons (*to'alot*) from the events described, viewing the narrative as a source of practical wisdom for individual and communal life.

**Abarbanel** provides extended political and philosophical analysis, often comparing biblical kingship with the political thought of his own era.

### Midrashic Traditions

The Midrash elaborates on the narrative, filling gaps and drawing connections to other parts of the Tanakh. These traditions often highlight moral and theological dimensions that are implicit but not explicit in the biblical text.

## Theological Themes

- **Kingship and Torah**: The Davidic monarchy operates under Torah law, not above it
- **Divine Providence**: God guides the events of Israel's history toward redemptive ends
- **Messianic Hope**: The successes and failures of David's reign point toward the future Mashiach
- **Human Responsibility**: The narrative holds characters accountable for their moral choices
