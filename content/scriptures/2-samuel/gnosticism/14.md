## Summary

2 Samuel 14 — Absalom's Return. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Wise Woman of Tekoa

Joab's use of a wise woman to manipulate David through a fictional parable echoes Nathan's earlier strategy (chapter 12) — indirect narrative as a tool of persuasion. In Gnostic terms, the wise woman functions as a bearer of hidden knowledge, using allegory to penetrate the king's defenses. The technique itself — revealing truth through fiction — resonates with the Gnostic hermeneutical method of reading deeper meaning beneath surface narrative.

### Absalom's Incomplete Reconciliation

Absalom's return to Jerusalem without permission to see the king's face (14:24) creates a paradoxical situation: the exile is ended but the relationship remains broken. In Gnostic terms, this half-restoration mirrors the soul's condition in the material world — returned to the vicinity of the divine but denied direct encounter. Proximity without intimacy is the archontic condition.

### Absalom's Beauty

The chapter's detailed description of Absalom's physical beauty — his hair, his appearance, his perfection — presents a thoroughly material figure. In Gnostic reading, Absalom's celebrated beauty is the beauty of the material world itself: dazzling, seductive, and ultimately deceptive. The hair that is praised here will be his instrument of death in chapter 18.
