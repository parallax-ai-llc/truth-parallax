## Summary

2 Samuel 2 — David Anointed King of Judah. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Anointing at Hebron

David's anointing as king of Judah represents the Demiurge's transfer of archontic authority. The act of anointing — pouring oil on the head — is a material ritual that confers material power. In Gnostic terms, this is the Demiurge selecting his next agent, not the true God commissioning a spiritual leader. David asks the Demiurge where to go (2:1), and is directed to Hebron — obedient to the archontic voice.

### The Civil War Begins

The conflict between David's forces and Ish-bosheth's house represents the archetypal Gnostic scenario: archontic powers in competition. Both sides claim divine sanction; both employ violence; both seek material dominion. The battle at the pool of Gibeon, where twelve warriors from each side kill each other simultaneously, illustrates the perfectly symmetrical futility of archontic warfare.

### Abner and Joab

The personal enmity between Abner and Joab — commanders who serve rival material powers — drives events more than any divine purpose. The killing of Joab's brother Asahel initiates a cycle of blood vengeance that will persist throughout David's reign. Gnostic reading sees in this the self-perpetuating violence of the material order, where each act of aggression generates its inevitable response.
