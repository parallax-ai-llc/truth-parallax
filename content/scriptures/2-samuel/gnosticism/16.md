## Summary

2 Samuel 16 — David's Flight. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### Ziba's Deception

Ziba's gift of provisions to David — while falsely claiming that Mephibosheth has betrayed him — represents the archontic world's characteristic currency: deception in exchange for material advantage. David, without investigation, grants Ziba all of Mephibosheth's property. The material ruler, under stress, makes decisions based on who appears useful rather than who speaks truth. In the Demiurge's world, loyalty is purchased and truth is the first casualty.

### Shimei's Cursing

Shimei's public cursing of David — throwing stones and dust, calling him a "worthless man" and a "man of blood" — represents a truth-telling that emerges from the margins. In Gnostic terms, Shimei speaks what the archontic system normally suppresses: the king is indeed a man of blood, his power built on violence. David's remarkable tolerance of Shimei's abuse ("Perhaps the Lord will look upon my misery") suggests a moment of genuine humility — the archontic ruler briefly acknowledging that the system's critics may be right.

### Ahithophel's Counsel

Ahithophel's advice that Absalom publicly take David's concubines represents the deliberate humiliation of the fallen ruler through the sexual appropriation of his women. In Gnostic terms, this is the material world at its most degraded: women used as instruments of political symbolism, their bodies serving as the medium through which rival archons demonstrate dominance.
