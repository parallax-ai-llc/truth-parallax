## Summary

2 Samuel 3 — Abner Defects to David. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### Abner's Defection

Abner's shift of allegiance from Ish-bosheth to David is pure political calculation — triggered not by spiritual conviction but by a dispute over a concubine (Rizpah). In Gnostic terms, the transfer of archontic power is driven by desire, wounded pride, and material self-interest. The inner workings of the Demiurge's political system are revealed as thoroughly carnal.

### Joab's Murder of Abner

Joab's treacherous murder of Abner — luring him back under pretense of peace and stabbing him — reveals the violence at the heart of the archontic system. The Demiurge's agents destroy each other in pursuit of power. David's public lamentation for Abner and his protest of innocence function, in Gnostic reading, as political performance — the material ruler managing perception while benefiting from the murder.

### Women as Pawns

The chapter's treatment of women — Rizpah as the occasion of Abner's quarrel, Michal demanded back from her husband as a political token — illustrates the archontic system's reduction of the feminine to an instrument of male power. Gnostic traditions that honor *Sophia* would note how the material order systematically degrades the feminine principle.

## Relevant Gnostic Texts

- **The Gospel of Philip**: The degradation of the feminine in the material world
