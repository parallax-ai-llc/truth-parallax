## Summary

2 Samuel 20 — Sheba's Revolt. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### Sheba's Rebellion

Sheba's cry — "We have no share in David, no part in Jesse's son!" — echoes the northern tribes' earlier rejection of the Davidic house. In Gnostic terms, this is a rebellion within the archontic system, not against it. Sheba offers no alternative vision — no *gnosis*, no spiritual liberation — only a different political alignment. The material world's revolts merely rearrange the furniture of the Demiurge's house.

### Joab's Murder of Amasa

Joab's treacherous killing of his rival commander Amasa — greeting him with a kiss while disemboweling him with a concealed sword — is the chapter's most striking event. In Gnostic reading, Joab embodies the archontic principle in its purest form: ruthless pragmatism in service of material power. The Demiurge's most effective agents operate without moral constraint, eliminating competitors through deception and violence.

### The Wise Woman of Abel

The unnamed wise woman of Abel Beth-maacah who negotiates with Joab — persuading the city to throw Sheba's head over the wall — represents practical wisdom in service of survival. In Gnostic terms, she demonstrates the intelligence that operates within the material world's constraints: not *gnosis* but *sophia* in its reduced, pragmatic form. Her wisdom saves the city but requires participating in the system's violence.

### The Concubines

The chapter opens with the disturbing note that David's ten concubines — violated by Absalom in chapter 16 — are now confined "until the day of their death, living as widows" (20:3). The material system disposes of its damaged property. The women, victimized by the archontic power struggle, are imprisoned for life. The Gnostic reader sees in their fate the material world's treatment of those who bear the cost of power's games.
