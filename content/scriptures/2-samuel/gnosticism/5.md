## Summary

2 Samuel 5 — David King over All Israel. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Capture of Jerusalem

David's conquest of Jerusalem — the Jebusite stronghold — represents the Demiurge's agent establishing his capital, the center of archontic power in the material world. The Jebusites' taunt that "even the blind and the lame" could defend their city, and David's subsequent conquest, illustrates the material world's logic: power determines truth, and military superiority validates divine favor.

### The City of David

The renaming of Jerusalem as the "City of David" is, in Gnostic terms, the archontic appropriation of space. A place is claimed, named, and sacralized for the Demiurge's purposes. The material world is organized around centers of power, and the Demiurge requires a capital from which to govern his realm.

### Victory over the Philistines

David's victories over the Philistines, guided by the Demiurge's tactical instructions ("Go, for I will surely deliver the Philistines into your hands"), reveal the Demiurge as a military strategist. The "sound of marching in the tops of the balsam trees" (5:24) suggests archontic forces operating in the spiritual realm to achieve material victory — the invisible machinery of material power.
