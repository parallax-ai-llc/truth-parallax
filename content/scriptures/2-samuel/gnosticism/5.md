## Summary

2 Samuel 5 — David King over All Israel. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Archontic Framework

Gnostic cosmology recasts the Davidic narrative as a story about the Demiurge's governance of the material world through human agents. Kings, prophets, and institutions serve as instruments of archontic control — maintaining the structures that keep humanity bound to the material realm and ignorant of their true divine origin in the Pleroma.

### Power and Illusion

The events described in this chapter — whether political maneuvering, military action, or religious practice — are read as manifestations of the illusory power that characterizes the material world. The Demiurge's agents rise and fall, but the system of material bondage persists. Only *gnosis* — direct knowledge of the transcendent divine reality — can break the cycle.

### The Hidden Spark

Gnostic anthropology holds that within every human being lies a spark of the true divine light, trapped in matter by the archons. The human dramas of 2 Samuel are significant not for their political or moral content but as occasions when the spark might be recognized — moments of crisis, failure, or insight that crack open the archontic illusion.

### The Alternative Path

Where orthodox traditions draw moral and theological lessons from the Davidic narrative, Gnostic thought points toward a fundamentally different path: not better governance, not moral reform, not institutional religion, but awakening to the truth that lies beyond all material structures.

## Relevant Gnostic Texts

- **The Apocryphon of John**: The cosmological framework for understanding material power
- **The Gospel of Thomas**: Sayings that subvert conventional religious and political authority
- **The Gospel of Philip**: The sacramental dimension of *gnosis*
