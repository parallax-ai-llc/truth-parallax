## Summary

2 Samuel 4 — Murder of Ish-bosheth. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### The Assassination

Rechab and Baanah's murder of Ish-bosheth in his bed — decapitating him and carrying his head to David — represents the archontic system at its most brutal. The assassins expect reward, believing they serve David's material interests. In Gnostic terms, they are hylic individuals who understand only the logic of material power: eliminate rivals, curry favor with the stronger party.

### David's Justice

David's execution of the assassins is presented as righteous justice, but Gnostic reading detects a deeper irony. David eliminates the men who removed his last rival while claiming moral superiority. The material system's justice serves the system's power — every act of "righteousness" consolidates the archontic ruler's position.

### Mephibosheth's Introduction

The brief mention of Mephibosheth (4:4) — Jonathan's son, crippled in the chaos following Saul's death — introduces a figure of Gnostic significance. The lame heir of the fallen house, rendered powerless by the material world's violence, represents the divine spark damaged but not destroyed by archontic conflict. His story will unfold as a persistent reminder that the Demiurge's system leaves casualties.
