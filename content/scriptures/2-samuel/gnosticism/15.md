## Summary

2 Samuel 15 — Absalom's Conspiracy. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### Absalom's Seduction of the People

Absalom's four-year campaign of standing at the gate and offering sympathetic justice to all comers represents the archontic method of seizing power through manufactured consent. He "steals the hearts of the people of Israel" (15:6) not through *gnosis* but through the promise of better material governance — a rival archon offering a more attractive version of the same system.

### David's Flight

David's departure from Jerusalem — barefoot, weeping, ascending the Mount of Olives — presents a rare image of the material ruler stripped of power. In Gnostic terms, the loss of earthly authority creates the conditions for potential spiritual insight. David's willingness to leave the Ark behind ("If I find favor in the Lord's eyes, he will bring me back") suggests a moment when the king loosens his grip on the Demiurge's sacred objects.

### The Espionage Network

David's establishment of a spy network — Hushai, Zadok, Abiathar, and their sons as informants — reveals the material ruler's reliance on deception and intelligence. Even in exile, the archontic mind operates through manipulation and strategic calculation. The system of material power does not pause even in crisis.

### Ittai the Gittite

The loyalty of Ittai — a foreign mercenary who chooses to follow David into exile — presents an interesting Gnostic figure. An outsider to the Demiurge's covenantal system, Ittai chooses loyalty not from obligation but from personal devotion. His freedom from the system's structures makes his commitment more authentic, if still bound to a material ruler.
