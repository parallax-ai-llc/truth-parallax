## Summary

2 Samuel 9 — David's Kindness to Mephibosheth. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### Kindness as Covenant Obligation

David's search for surviving members of Saul's house — "for Jonathan's sake" — is presented as *hesed* (covenant loyalty). Gnostic interpretation reads this differently: David's kindness operates within the Demiurge's covenantal framework, where obligation and reciprocity bind individuals to the material order. Even generous acts serve the system's logic of debt and repayment.

### Mephibosheth at the King's Table

The lame son of Jonathan, brought from obscurity to eat at the king's table, represents a complex Gnostic figure. His physical disability marks him as damaged by the material world's violence, yet his elevation to the royal table places him under the most direct archontic surveillance. Kindness and control operate simultaneously — Mephibosheth is honored and contained.

### Ziba the Servant

The assignment of Ziba and his household as servants to Mephibosheth reveals the material order's hierarchy: the powerful distribute resources and assign roles, creating networks of dependency. In Gnostic terms, the Demiurge's system operates through cascading layers of obligation — king to noble, noble to servant, servant to land.
