## Summary

2 Samuel 17 — Ahithophel and Hushai's Counsel. Gnostic hermeneutics provide a radically alternative framework for reading this chapter, viewing the events of David's reign through the lens of Demiurgic power, archontic control, and the imprisonment of the divine spark in material existence.

## Gnostic Reading

### Competing Wisdom

Ahithophel's counsel ("like the oracle of God," 16:23) versus Hushai's counter-counsel represents a battle of material wisdom. Both advisors employ strategic intelligence in service of rival archontic powers. The text notes that the Lord ordained Hushai's inferior counsel to prevail — the Demiurge manipulating the outcome by undermining genuine wisdom. In Gnostic terms, the true God operates beyond such strategic games, while the Demiurge actively confounds human wisdom for his own purposes.

### Ahithophel's Suicide

Ahithophel's suicide upon learning that his counsel was rejected is the Gnostic chapter's most significant moment. The wisest counselor in Israel, whose advice was "like the oracle of God," recognizes that the rejection of his counsel means certain defeat. He puts his affairs in order and hangs himself — a calculated, rational response to an impossible material situation. In Gnostic terms, Ahithophel's death represents the failure of material wisdom to save even its greatest practitioner.

### The Spy Network in Action

The elaborate chain of intelligence — Jonathan and Ahimaaz hiding in a well, a woman covering the well with grain, the messengers reaching David — reveals the material world's dependence on deception, concealment, and clandestine communication. Even the servants of the "righteous" king operate through hiding and misdirection. The Demiurge's system functions through obscured truth at every level.

## Relevant Gnostic Texts

- **The Gospel of Thomas**: Saying 17 — what the eye has not seen and the ear has not heard — wisdom beyond material calculation
