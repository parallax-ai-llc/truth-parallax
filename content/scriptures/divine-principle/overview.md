## Overview

The Divine Principle (원리강론, Wŏlli Kangnon) is the foundational theological text of the Unification Church, formally known as the Family Federation for World Peace and Unification. Compiled from the teachings of Sun Myung Moon and systematized by Hyo Won Eu, the text was first published in Korean in 1966. It presents a comprehensive reinterpretation of biblical history through the lens of the Principle of Creation, the Fall, and the Providence of Restoration, culminating in the expectation of the Lord of the Second Advent.

## Structure

The Divine Principle is organized into thirteen chapters spanning two major parts:

1. **Part I — The Principle** (Chapters 1–7): Establishes the theological framework, including the Principle of Creation, the Fall of Man, eschatology, the mission of the Messiah, resurrection, predestination, and Christology
2. **Part II — The Providence of Restoration** (Chapters 8–13): Traces God's providential work through biblical and post-biblical history, from the foundations of restoration through Moses and Jesus, through the period of preparation, to the Second Advent and the ideal of world peace through true family

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Unificationist**: The authoritative doctrinal text, studied systematically by all members as the basis of faith and practice
- **Academic**: A key primary source for the study of new religious movements, Korean Christianity, and post-war millenarianism
- **Comparative**: Offers a distinctive synthesis of Christian, Confucian, and shamanistic motifs within a single systematic theology
- **Protestant**: Raises critical questions about Christology, biblical interpretation, and the boundaries of Christian orthodoxy
- **Interfaith**: Presents a theology explicitly oriented toward the unification of religions and the establishment of world peace
- **Theological**: Provides a comprehensive systematic theology that engages questions of creation, theodicy, soteriology, and eschatology

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Dual Characteristics | God's nature as the harmonious union of internal and external, masculine and feminine attributes |
| The Fall | The misuse of love by the first human ancestors, resulting in the corruption of the lineage |
| Indemnity | The principle by which humans must make conditions of restitution to reverse the effects of the Fall |
| Providence of Restoration | God's historical work to restore humanity to the original ideal of creation |
| True Family | The culmination of God's purpose: families centered on God's love as the building blocks of world peace |
