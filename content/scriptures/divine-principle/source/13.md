---
title: "Divine Principle - Chapter 13"
scripture: "divine-principle"
chapter: 13
---

**1** Everyone, without exception, is struggling to gain happiness and to avoid misfortune. From the commonplace affairs of individuals to the great events that shape the course of history, each is an expression of the human aspiration for ever greater happiness.

**2** How, then, does happiness arise? People feel happy when their desires are fulfilled. The word desire, however, is often understood to be base, and there is a tendency to think it should be repressed or even denied.

**3** This is because our desires, depraved by the human fall, frequently lead us toward wrongdoing. The original desire, however, which God bestowed on human beings at the creation, is the desire to fulfill the purpose of creation.

**4** Happiness is achieved when this original desire is satisfied. Therefore, it is only natural that God would want to see human beings achieve happiness.

**5** The purpose of God's creation of the universe was to feel happiness when He saw the purpose of creation fulfilled. Every being embodies the dual characteristics of yang and yin, and sungsang and hyungsang.

**6** Had human beings attained perfection, forming one body with God through the fulfillment of the purpose of creation, they would have possessed the divine nature. Then they would never have fallen.

**7** We must understand the Principle by which God created the universe, the root cause of the human fall, and the course of the providence of restoration, in order to accomplish the purpose of creation.

**8** This textbook elucidates these fundamental truths, which we call the Divine Principle. It is the new expression of truth for which the world has long been waiting.
