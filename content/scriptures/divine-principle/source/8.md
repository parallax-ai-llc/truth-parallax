---
title: "Divine Principle Section 8"
scripture: "divine-principle"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The history of restoration can be understood through parallel periods. God's providence follows a pattern, and the same types of events recur in different ages.

**2** The two-thousand-year period from Adam to Abraham parallels the two-thousand-year period from Abraham to Jesus. And the two-thousand-year period from Jesus to the present parallels the period from Abraham to Jesus.

**3** The four-hundred-year period of slavery in Egypt parallels the four-hundred-year period of persecution under the Roman Empire.

**4** The four-hundred-year period of the Judges parallels the period of the regional church leaders in the early Christian era.

**5** The one-hundred-and-twenty-year period of the United Kingdom under Saul, David, and Solomon parallels the period of the Christian Empire under Charlemagne and his successors.

**6** The four-hundred-year period of the Divided Kingdoms of Israel parallels the period of the Divided Kingdoms of East and West in medieval Christendom.

**7** These parallels demonstrate that history is not random but follows a providential pattern directed by God toward the goal of restoration.
