---
title: "Divine Principle Section 7"
scripture: "divine-principle"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Jesus came as the Messiah, the Second Adam, to restore what the first Adam had lost through the Fall. He came to establish the Kingdom of Heaven on earth.

**2** John the Baptist was in the position of Elijah, preparing the way for the Messiah. John's mission was to testify to Jesus and lead the people to believe in him.

**3** Although John the Baptist initially testified to Jesus at the Jordan, he later doubted, sending his disciples to ask: "Are you the one who is to come, or shall we look for another?"

**4** John's failure to fully support Jesus was a critical factor in the disbelief of the Jewish people. If John had followed Jesus, the religious leaders and the nation would have been more likely to accept him.

**5** Because the chosen people did not receive Jesus, he was forced to take the path of the cross. The crucifixion was not God's original will but a secondary course of restoration.

**6** Through the cross, Jesus accomplished spiritual salvation. By his resurrection, he opened the way for believers to be spiritually reborn. But the physical salvation — the establishment of the Kingdom of Heaven on earth — was left unfulfilled.

**7** The Lord of the Second Advent must come to complete the mission that Jesus left unfinished: the physical restoration of the Kingdom of Heaven on earth.
