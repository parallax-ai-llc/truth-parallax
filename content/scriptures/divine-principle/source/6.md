---
title: "Divine Principle Section 6"
scripture: "divine-principle"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Moses was called by God to lead the Israelites out of Egypt and into Canaan. The course of Moses represents a model for the course of Jesus and the course of the Second Coming.

**2** Moses established the foundation of faith through his forty years in Pharaoh's palace and forty years in the wilderness of Midian.

**3** The first national course to restore Canaan failed because the Israelites did not trust Moses after he killed the Egyptian.

**4** The second national course began with the Exodus from Egypt. Moses performed signs and miracles, and God led the people with a pillar of cloud and fire.

**5** At Mount Sinai, God gave the Ten Commandments and the Tabernacle as the center of faith. But while Moses was on the mountain, the people made the golden calf, and the foundation was lost.

**6** Because of the faithlessness of the first generation, the Israelites wandered forty years in the wilderness. The second generation, led by Joshua, entered Canaan.

**7** The course of Moses teaches that God's providence is prolonged by human failure. When central figures and the chosen people fail, the providence is extended, and the conditions of indemnity become heavier.
