---
title: "Divine Principle Section 4"
scripture: "divine-principle"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The purpose of the Messiah is to restore what was lost through the Fall. The Messiah comes as the True Parent to engraft fallen humanity back into God's lineage.

**2** God's providence of restoration is carried out through conditions of indemnity. Indemnity conditions reverse the process of the Fall by paying restitution for the original failure.

**3** The foundation of faith is established when a central figure makes an acceptable offering to God during a specified time period. Abel's offering was accepted because he made it with the correct heart and attitude.

**4** The foundation of substance is established when the person in Cain's position overcomes the fallen nature by submitting to the person in Abel's position. This reverses the process by which fallen nature was inherited.

**5** When both the foundation of faith and the foundation of substance are established, the foundation for the Messiah is laid, and the Messiah can come.

**6** Throughout history, God has worked through central figures and nations to establish these foundations. The failures and successes of biblical figures — from Adam's family to Abraham, Moses, and Jesus — represent stages in this providence.

**7** Jesus came as the Messiah but was unable to fully complete his mission because of the disbelief of the chosen people. He accomplished spiritual salvation through the cross, but the physical restoration remains to be completed.
