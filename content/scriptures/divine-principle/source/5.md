---
title: "Divine Principle Section 5"
scripture: "divine-principle"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The providence of restoration in Adam's family began with Cain and Abel. God could not work directly with Adam because Adam was the one who had fallen. Instead, God divided Adam's family into two positions.

**2** Abel represented the good side and was placed in the position to receive God's love. Cain represented the evil side and was in the position to make a condition to overcome the fallen nature.

**3** Cain was supposed to submit to Abel, love him, and offer through him. Had Cain done so, the fallen nature would have been reversed and the foundation of substance would have been established.

**4** Instead, Cain killed Abel, repeating the pattern of the Fall. The archangel had dominated Adam; now Cain dominated Abel. The providence in Adam's family was thus prolonged.

**5** The providence was extended to Noah's family. Noah established the foundation of faith through his obedience in building the ark. But Ham's failure after the Flood caused the foundation of substance to fail.

**6** The providence was further extended to Abraham's family. Abraham's offering on Mount Moriah established a foundation of faith, and the relationship between Esau and Jacob established a foundation of substance.

**7** Jacob's victory over Esau through love and humility became the model for overcoming the fallen nature and laid the foundation for the nation of Israel.
