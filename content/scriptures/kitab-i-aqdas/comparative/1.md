## Laws of Prayer and Fasting

This section of the Kitab-i-Aqdas addresses the obligatory prayers, their forms and times, the annual fasting period, and devotional ordinances that structure the spiritual life of the individual believer.

Comparative study of this section places its provisions alongside parallel teachings in other scriptural traditions. The universal themes of divine law, human obligation, and social order appear across traditions, but each scripture configures them differently. The Aqdas's explicit positioning as a successor text — acknowledging and then modifying prior revelations — makes it an especially productive subject for comparative analysis.

The relationship between individual spiritual practice and communal legal order, the tension between universal principles and particular applications, and the question of whether sacred law evolves or is eternally fixed are all illuminated through cross-traditional comparison.

The provisions of this section continue to shape Baha'i community life and provoke scholarly reflection on the nature of sacred law, the evolution of religious practice, and the possibility of universal ethical principles grounded in revelation.
