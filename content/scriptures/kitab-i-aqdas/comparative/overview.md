## Overview

Comparative study of the Kitab-i-Aqdas places it alongside other law-giving scriptures — the Torah, the Quran, the Vinaya Pitaka, the Manusmriti — to examine how different traditions understand the relationship between divine revelation and legal obligation. The Aqdas offers a particularly rich case study because it explicitly positions itself within a succession of prior scriptures.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Comparative Sacred Law
The Aqdas shares with the Torah and Quran the interweaving of narrative, law, and exhortation within a single revelatory text. However, it differs in its explicit self-awareness as a successor text that abrogates previous dispensations while honoring their divine origin.

### Universal Ethics
Compared with other ethical scriptures, the Aqdas is notable for its explicit universalism — its laws are intended for all humanity, not a particular ethnic or national community. This invites comparison with Enlightenment universalism and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

### Institutional Design
The Aqdas is unique among scriptures in establishing a detailed institutional framework (the Universal House of Justice) for ongoing legislation, creating a living legislative tradition rather than a fixed legal code.

## Key Comparisons

|---------|-------|-------|-------|
| Prayer | Shema/Amidah | Five daily prayers | Three obligatory prayers |
| Fasting | Yom Kippur + others | Ramadan | Nineteen days (Ala) |
| Holy War | Conditional | Jihad (debated) | Abolished |
| Inheritance | Patrilineal | Fixed shares | Detailed system |
| Music | Various views | Debated | Permitted |
