## Admonitions and Exhortations

This section of the Kitab-i-Aqdas addresses moral admonitions and spiritual exhortations addressed to kings, rulers, and the peoples of the world, calling for justice, unity, and transformation.

From an Islamic perspective, this section invites close comparison with the corresponding provisions of Islamic Sharia. Scholars of Islamic law note both the familiar framework (obligatory prayer, fasting, laws of personal status) and the significant departures (changes in prayer forms, modification of inheritance shares, abolition of certain traditional categories).

The fundamental question for Islamic theology remains whether post-Quranic legislation is possible. The Baha'i claim that Baha'u'llah's authority supersedes the Quran's legal provisions challenges the Islamic principle of the Quran's eternal validity. However, some scholars note that the concept of *naskh* (abrogation) within Islamic jurisprudence itself establishes a precedent for the supersession of earlier divine commands by later ones.

The provisions of this section continue to shape Baha'i community life and provoke scholarly reflection on the nature of sacred law, the evolution of religious practice, and the possibility of universal ethical principles grounded in revelation.
