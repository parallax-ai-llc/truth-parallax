## Overview

For Baha'is, the Kitab-i-Aqdas is the Most Holy Book — the supreme repository of divine law for the current dispensation, revealed by Baha'u'llah as the Manifestation of God for this age. Its laws are binding on Baha'is worldwide, though their application is gradual, governed by the Universal House of Justice, the supreme legislative body Baha'u'llah ordained in this very text.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Authoritative Interpretation
Baha'u'llah authorized his son Abdu'l-Baha and then Shoghi Effendi as authoritative interpreters. Their elucidations of the Aqdas are considered binding. This creates a clear chain of interpretive authority unique among world scriptures.

### Gradual Application
Not all laws of the Aqdas are currently applicable. The Universal House of Justice determines the timing and manner of implementation, recognizing that humanity must be prepared for certain provisions.

### Spirit and Letter
Baha'is understand the laws not merely as external obligations but as instruments of spiritual transformation. Obedience to divine law is an expression of love for God, not legalistic compliance.

## Theological Emphases

- **Covenant**: The Aqdas establishes the Baha'i Covenant — the system of succession and authority that preserves unity
- **World Order**: The text envisions a global commonwealth governed by principles of justice, consultation, and unity
- **Spiritual Transformation**: Laws serve the development of individual character and collective civilization
- **Progressive Revelation**: The Aqdas fulfills and supersedes previous scriptures while anticipating future revelation
