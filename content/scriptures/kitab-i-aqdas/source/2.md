---
title: "Kitab-i-Aqdas - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kitab-i-aqdas"
chapter: 2
---

**1** We have enjoined obligatory prayer upon you, with nine prostrations unto God, the Revealer of Verses, at noon and in the morning and the evening.

**2** We have relieved you of a greater number, as a command in the Book of God. He, verily, is the Ordainer, the Omnipotent, the Unrestrained.

**3** When ye desire to perform this prayer, turn ye towards the Most Holy Land, that spot which God hath made the Point to which all must turn in adoration.

**4** And when the Sun of Truth and Utterance shall set, turn your faces towards the Spot that We have ordained for you. He, verily, is Almighty and Omniscient.

**5** Everything that is hath come to be through His irresistible decree. Whenever My laws appear like the sun in the heaven of Mine utterance, they must be faithfully obeyed by all, though My decree be such as to cause the heaven of every religion to be cleft asunder.

**6** He doeth what He pleaseth. He chooseth, and none may question His choice. Whatsoever He, the Well-Beloved, ordaineth, the same is, verily, beloved. To this He Who is the Lord of all creation beareth Me witness.

**7** Whoso hath inhaled the sweet fragrance of the All-Merciful, and recognized the Source of this utterance, will welcome with his own eyes the shafts of the enemy, that he may establish the truth of the laws of God amongst men.

**8** Well is it with him that hath turned thereunto, and apprehended the meaning of His decisive decree.

**9** We have set forth the details of obligatory prayer in another Tablet. Blessed is he who observeth that whereunto he hath been bidden by Him Who ruleth over all mankind.

**10** God hath exempted women who are in their courses from obligatory prayer and fasting. Let them, instead, after performing their ablutions, give praise unto God, repeating ninety-five times between the noon of one day and the next: "Glorified be God, the Lord of Splendour and Beauty."

**11** When travelling, if ye should stop and rest in some safe spot, perform ye a single prostration in place of each unsaid obligatory prayer, and around the site of each prostration recite: "Glorified be God, the Lord of Might and Majesty, of Grace and Bounty."

**12** Whoso is not able to do this, let him say: "Glorified be God." And this shall assuredly suffice him. He is, of a truth, the all-sufficing, the ever-abiding, the forgiving, compassionate God.

**13** Upon completing your prostrations, seat yourselves cross-legged and repeat eighteen times: "Glorified be God, the Lord of the kingdoms of earth and heaven."

**14** Thus doth the Lord make plain the ways of truth and guidance, ways that lead to one way, which is this Straight Path. Render thanks unto God for this most gracious favour; offer praise unto Him for this bounty that hath encompassed the heavens and the earth.

**15** Recite ye the verses of God every morn and eventide. Whoso faileth to recite them hath not been faithful to the Covenant of God and His Testament, and whoso turneth away from these holy verses in this Day is of those who throughout eternity have turned away from God.

**16** Fear ye God, O My servants, one and all. Pride not yourselves on much reading of the verses or on a multitude of pious acts by night and day; for were a man to read a single verse with joy and radiance it would be better for him than to read with lassitude all the Holy Books of God.

**17** Read ye the sacred verses in such measure that ye be not overcome by languor and despondency. Lay not upon your souls that which will weary them and weigh them down, but rather what will lighten and uplift them.
