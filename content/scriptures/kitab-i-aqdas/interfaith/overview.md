## Overview

The Kitab-i-Aqdas holds particular significance for interfaith dialogue because the Baha'i Faith explicitly affirms the divine origin of all major religions and positions itself as the latest — though not the last — in a continuous series of divine revelations. The Aqdas articulates a vision of religious unity that challenges both exclusivist and relativist approaches.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Progressive Revelation as Framework
The Baha'i doctrine of progressive revelation — that God sends successive messengers to guide humanity through evolving stages of collective development — offers a distinctive framework for interfaith engagement. Each religion is understood as true for its time while anticipating the next stage.

### Shared Ethical Principles
Interfaith readers find in the Aqdas ethical principles resonant across traditions: justice, truthfulness, compassion, education, elimination of extremes of wealth and poverty, equality of women and men, and the harmony of science and religion.

### Challenges to Dialogue
The Aqdas's claim to supersede all previous dispensations creates tension with traditions that regard their own scriptures as final. Productive interfaith engagement requires acknowledging this tension while finding common ground.
