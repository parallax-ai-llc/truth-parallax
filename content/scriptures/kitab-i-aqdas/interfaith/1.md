## Laws of Prayer and Fasting

This section of the Kitab-i-Aqdas addresses the obligatory prayers, their forms and times, the annual fasting period, and devotional ordinances that structure the spiritual life of the individual believer.

Interfaith readers find in this section an opportunity to compare how different traditions legislate the relationship between the human and the divine. Prayer, fasting, family law, and social ethics appear across traditions, and the specific forms they take in the Aqdas reveal both the universal human need for spiritual discipline and the particular ways each tradition addresses that need.

The Aqdas's explicit universalism — its laws are intended for all humanity, not a particular ethnic or national group — distinguishes it from scriptures whose legal provisions were originally addressed to a specific community. This raises productive questions about the universalizability of religious law and the relationship between particular revelation and global ethics.

The provisions of this section continue to shape Baha'i community life and provoke scholarly reflection on the nature of sacred law, the evolution of religious practice, and the possibility of universal ethical principles grounded in revelation.
