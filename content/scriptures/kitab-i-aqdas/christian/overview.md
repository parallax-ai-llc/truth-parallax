## Overview

Christian engagement with the Kitab-i-Aqdas raises questions about the sufficiency of biblical revelation, the relationship between law and grace, and the interpretation of eschatological prophecy. The Baha'i claim that Baha'u'llah is the return of Christ challenges fundamental Christian theological categories.

## Key Interpretive Issues

### Law and Grace
Christian theology, particularly in its Pauline and Protestant expressions, understands the coming of Christ as inaugurating a new relationship with God based on grace rather than legal observance. The Aqdas's reintroduction of a detailed legal code invites comparison with the Mosaic Law and raises questions about the theological trajectory from law to grace.

### Eschatological Fulfillment
Baha'is interpret biblical prophecies of Christ's return (Matthew 24, Revelation) as fulfilled symbolically in Baha'u'llah. Most Christian traditions expect a literal, visible, and unmistakable Second Coming, creating a fundamental hermeneutical divergence.

### Ethical Convergence
Despite theological disagreements, Christian readers may find points of convergence in the Aqdas's emphasis on justice, compassion, universal brotherhood, education, and the elimination of prejudice — themes with deep biblical resonance.

## Theological Emphases

- **Sufficiency of Christ**: Whether additional revelation beyond the New Testament is necessary or possible
- **Kingdom of God**: The Aqdas's vision of world order compared with Christian eschatology
- **Moral law**: Points of convergence and divergence with biblical ethics
- **Christology**: The Baha'i understanding of Jesus versus traditional Christian doctrine
