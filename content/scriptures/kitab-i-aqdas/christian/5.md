## Prophecies and Promises

This section of the Kitab-i-Aqdas addresses prophecies concerning the future of humanity, promises of divine assistance, and declarations about the destiny of the Baha'i Faith and world civilization.

Christian readers encounter in this section a return to detailed divine legislation that raises theological questions about the relationship between law and gospel. The Pauline tradition's emphasis on freedom from law through grace stands in tension with the Aqdas's reintroduction of specific legal obligations.

However, the Catholic and Orthodox traditions have always maintained a role for canon law alongside grace, and the Reformed tradition's concept of the 'third use of the law' (as a guide for Christian living) suggests that divine legislation and spiritual freedom need not be opposites. The Aqdas's integration of legal provision with spiritual exhortation may be read as a synthesis of the Hebraic legal and the Christian spiritual emphases.

The provisions of this section continue to shape Baha'i community life and provoke scholarly reflection on the nature of sacred law, the evolution of religious practice, and the possibility of universal ethical principles grounded in revelation.
