## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of Part 7 places the Ofudesaki's teaching on causality and return alongside similar themes in other prophetic and scriptural traditions. The text's treatment of this theme — expressed through Japanese poetic forms yet claiming universal applicability — illustrates the tension between cultural particularity and religious universalism found in scriptures worldwide.

Comparative scholars note parallels with prophetic urgency in the Hebrew Bible, the Quran's divine address to humanity, and the visionary literature of other new religious movements. The Ofudesaki's distinctive contribution lies in its combination of intimate parental metaphor, poetic compression, and practical ethical instruction.
