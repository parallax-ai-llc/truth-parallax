## Buddhist-Japanese Reading

The Buddhist-Japanese reading of Part 4 explores resonances between the Ofudesaki's teaching on the parental heart and Buddhist concepts that pervaded Japanese religious consciousness. The text's treatment of suffering, causality, and the purification of the mind echoes Buddhist psychological analysis while reframing these concerns within a theistic framework centered on God the Parent.

Japanese Buddhist scholars note that the Ofudesaki's audience — ordinary people in late Tokugawa and early Meiji Japan — would have understood many of its concepts through a Buddhist lens. The text both draws upon this shared vocabulary and deliberately redirects it toward Tenrikyo's distinctive theological vision, creating a creative tension between Buddhist and Tenrikyo understandings.
