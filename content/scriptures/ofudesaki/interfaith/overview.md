## Overview

The interfaith reading of the Ofudesaki highlights its vision of universal divine parenthood and human brotherhood as resources for interreligious dialogue. The Ofudesaki's central teaching — that all human beings are children of the same God the Parent, regardless of nationality, race, or religion — represents one of the most explicitly universalist theologies in modern religious scripture.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Ofudesaki a prophetic voice that speaks to contemporary concerns about peace, justice, and environmental stewardship. The text's insistence that God desires the joyous life for all people — not merely for followers of one religion — challenges exclusivist claims in all traditions and invites dialogue about the nature of divine love, the purpose of human existence, and the possibility of a world in which all people live in mutual care and harmony.
