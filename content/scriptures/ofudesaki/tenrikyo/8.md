## Tenrikyo Reading

Part VIII addresses the truth of the world — the social and moral conditions that God the Parent observes and desires to transform. These verses contain implicit critiques of social inequality, exploitation, and the misuse of power, reflecting the historical context of Meiji-era Japan while speaking to universal human concerns.

Tenrikyo practitioners read Part VIII as a call to social responsibility rooted in the recognition that all human beings are equally children of God. The Joyous Life is not merely an individual spiritual attainment but a social reality in which all people live in mutual care and support. The divine Parent's vision extends beyond the individual heart to encompass the transformation of society itself.
