## Tenrikyo Reading

Part XIV addresses the establishment of the Grand Shrine at the Jiba — the place of human creation and the spiritual center of the world. God the Parent expresses the divine desire to see the construction of the Kanrodai (the Stand of Heavenly Dew) at the Jiba, which will serve as the focal point of the Service and the instrument of universal salvation.

Tenrikyo practitioners understand Part XIV as connecting spiritual truth to physical place. The Jiba is not merely a symbol but the actual site of human creation, and the Grand Shrine built upon it is the physical manifestation of the divine-human relationship. Pilgrimage to the Jiba (*ojiba-gaeri*, returning to the Jiba) is one of the most important practices in Tenrikyo life.
