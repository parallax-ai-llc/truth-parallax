## Tenrikyo Reading

Part XII, the longest section of the Ofudesaki, presents the teaching of single-hearted salvation — the divine promise that God the Parent will save all human beings without exception. The concept of *ichiretsu tasuke* (universal salvation) is the culmination of the Ofudesaki's theological vision: a God who will not rest until every child is brought home to the Joyous Life.

Tenrikyo practitioners find in Part XII the fullest expression of divine love and the most demanding call to faith. Single-hearted salvation requires single-hearted devotion — a total trust in God the Parent that transcends doubt, fear, and self-reliance. The verses promise that those who entrust themselves completely to the divine will experience the free and unlimited workings of God.
