## Tenrikyo Reading

Part IX conveys God's sense of urgency — the divine impatience with humanity's slow response to the revealed truth. The verses express God the Parent's desire to hasten the work of salvation, urging followers to deepen their commitment and spread the teaching more vigorously.

The Tenrikyo community receives Part IX as a call to renewed missionary effort and spiritual intensity. God's urgency is understood as parental concern — the Parent cannot rest while children suffer, and the hastening of the path is God's response to the depth of human need. Followers are reminded that time is precious and that the work of salvation requires active, energetic participation.
