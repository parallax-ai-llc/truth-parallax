## Tenrikyo Reading

Part VI develops the Ofudesaki's teaching on the 'dusts of the mind' — the eight mental attitudes (miserliness, covetousness, hatred, self-love, grudge-bearing, anger, greed, and arrogance) that obscure the divine nature within human beings and cause suffering. God the Parent urges humanity to sweep away these dusts through self-reflection and the practice of the Service.

Tenrikyo practitioners study Part VI as a practical manual for spiritual purification. The metaphor of dust is central to Tenrikyo psychology: just as dust accumulates gradually and imperceptibly, so the mind collects attitudes and habits that distort perception and generate conflict. The remedy is not ascetic denial but the daily sweeping of the mind — a process of self-examination, repentance, and reorientation toward the Joyous Life.
