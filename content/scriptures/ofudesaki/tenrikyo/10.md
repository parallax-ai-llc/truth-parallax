## Tenrikyo Reading

Part X returns to the metaphor of sweeping — the purification of the mind from the accumulated dusts that cause suffering and block the realization of the Joyous Life. God the Parent describes the sweeping as both an individual and a cosmic process: as each person cleanses their own mind, the world itself is gradually purified.

Tenrikyo practitioners find in Part X a vision of spiritual ecology — the interconnection between inner transformation and outer change. The sweeping of dust is not merely personal therapy but participation in God's ongoing work of world salvation. The verses encourage persistence and patience, reminding followers that the accumulated dust of generations cannot be swept away in a single effort.
