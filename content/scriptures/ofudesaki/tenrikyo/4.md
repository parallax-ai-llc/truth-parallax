## Tenrikyo Reading

Part IV elaborates on the nature of God's parental heart — the boundless compassion and concern that Oyagami feels for all human beings. These verses express God's sorrow at human suffering and divine eagerness to guide humanity back to the path of joy. The parental metaphor is not merely illustrative; Tenrikyo theology holds that God literally created human beings as children and relates to them with the love, patience, and discipline of a parent.

The Tenrikyo community finds in Part IV both comfort and challenge: comfort in the assurance of God's unwavering love, and challenge in the recognition that children must respond to parental guidance with trust and obedience. The verses describe a God who is not distant or indifferent but intimately involved in human affairs.
