## Overview

For Tenrikyo followers, the Ofudesaki is the direct word of God the Parent (*Oyagami*), communicated through the hand of the foundress Nakayama Miki. The text is approached with profound reverence — not as human literature or philosophical reflection but as the unmediated voice of the Creator addressing humanity with urgent parental love. Every verse is studied, memorized, and applied to daily life as a guide to achieving the *Yokigurashi* (Joyous Life) that God intends for all human beings.

The Ofudesaki occupies the highest position in Tenrikyo's scriptural hierarchy, followed by the Mikagura-uta (Songs for the Service) and the Osashizu (Divine Directions). Tenrikyo communities worldwide gather regularly to study and discuss the Ofudesaki, understanding each part as addressing both the specific historical circumstances of Miki's time and the universal human condition. The text's poetic form — traditional *waka* verse — is understood as divinely chosen to convey truths accessible to all people regardless of education or social status.
