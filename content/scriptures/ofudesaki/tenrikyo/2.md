## Tenrikyo Reading

Part II reveals the Tenrikyo teaching on the origin of humanity. God the Parent describes the creation of human beings from primordial mud at the original place, Jiba, which Tenrikyo identifies with the site of the Main Sanctuary in Tenri City, Nara Prefecture. This creation narrative is central to Tenrikyo theology — it establishes that all human beings share a common origin, created by the same divine Parent, and therefore constitute a single family.

The Tenrikyo community understands the creation account in Part II not as myth or metaphor but as revealed truth about humanity's actual origin. The teaching that God created humans to live the Joyous Life — and that suffering arises from deviation from this original intent — provides the foundation for Tenrikyo's soteriology and its optimistic vision of human potential.
