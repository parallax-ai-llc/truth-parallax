## Tenrikyo Reading

Part V introduces the central Tenrikyo practice of the Service (*Teodori*) — the sacred dance and prayer performed at the Jiba and in Tenrikyo churches worldwide. God the Parent reveals that the Service is the divinely appointed means through which salvation will be accomplished, not through individual effort alone but through the collective performance of the sacred dance.

The Tenrikyo community understands Part V as establishing the liturgical heart of the faith. The Service is not merely worship but an active participation in God's work of salvation — a means by which human beings cooperate with the divine will to bring about the Joyous Life for all. The specific movements and songs of the Teodori are understood as divinely prescribed, each gesture embodying an aspect of the divine truth.
