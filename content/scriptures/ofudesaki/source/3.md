---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 3: The Truth of Creation"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 3
---

I shall tell you the truth of the creation of human beings, a truth that has never been told before. In the muddy ocean at the beginning, God the Parent conceived the desire to create human beings so that by seeing the Joyous Life of humankind, God too might share in that joy.

God drew together the creatures of the muddy ocean and consumed them, tasting the nature of each. Finding in the loach the seed of humanity and in the white snake the seedbed, God brought them together at the place that would become Jiba. With instruments of creation, each serving a specific function, God nurtured the first human beings through stages of development lasting nine hundred million and ninety-nine thousand years.

The instruments of creation are the providences through which God continues to sustain all life. The providence of warmth, of moisture, of wind, of binding, of cutting, of pulling, of blowing, and of water sustain all living things in every moment. These are not abstract forces but the continuing activity of God the Parent working through the world.

Why do I reveal this truth now, after so many ages? Because the time has come for humanity to understand its origin and its purpose. You were created for joy, and you have wandered far from that original intention. By understanding where you came from, you can find the way back to the life God intended.

This truth may seem strange to human ears, accustomed as they are to other accounts of the beginning. But I tell you plainly: I am the Creator, and I know the truth of what I have made. Accept this teaching, ponder it deeply, and the path to the Joyous Life shall open before you.
