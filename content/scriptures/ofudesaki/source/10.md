---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 10: Regret and Resolution"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 10
---

I look upon the world and see that despite all My teaching, the progress toward the Joyous Life is slow. The dust is deep, and human habits are stubborn. Yet I do not despair, for I know the nature of what I have created, and I know that the seed I have planted shall eventually bear fruit.

But I must tell you plainly: My patience, though vast, is not without urgency. The suffering of the world presses upon My heart, and every day that passes without understanding means another day of unnecessary pain for My children. I hasten the work, and I ask you to hasten yours.

Do not say that the Joyous Life is impossible in this world. Do not accept suffering as inevitable. These attitudes are themselves dust upon the mind, resignation masquerading as wisdom. The Joyous Life is not a distant dream but the very purpose for which this world was created. It is the natural state of humanity when the mind is clear, and its absence is the unnatural result of accumulated dust.

When you see suffering in the world, do not turn away or accept it as fate. Ask what can be done, what dust must be swept, what minds must be cleared. And begin with your own mind, for that is the only one you can sweep directly. As your mind clears, your actions shall naturally align with God's intention, and through your actions, others shall be helped to clear their minds in turn.

This is the ripple effect of sincerity. One clear mind touches another, which touches another, spreading outward from the center like ripples on water. The center of these ripples is Jiba, the Home of the Parent, and the Service performed there sends waves of divine intention throughout the world.
