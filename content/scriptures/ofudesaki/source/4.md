---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 4: The Dusty Mind"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 4
---

I wish to explain the eight dusts of the mind more fully, for understanding these is essential to sweeping the mind clean. Each dust represents a way of using the mind that contradicts the intention of God the Parent for human happiness.

Miserliness is the dust of hoarding what should be shared. God provides abundantly for all, yet some grasp tightly to what they have, refusing to let it flow to those in need. This creates suffering both for those who lack and for those who hoard, for a clenched fist cannot receive new blessings.

Covetousness looks upon what others possess and desires it, unable to find contentment in what God has already provided. Self-love places one's own desires above the needs of others, forgetting that all are equally children of God. Greed reaches endlessly for more, never satisfied, like a fire that consumes everything yet is never filled.

Arrogance elevates oneself above others, forgetting that every human being was created from the same origin by the same Parent. Grudge-bearing holds onto past offenses, allowing old wounds to fester rather than healing them through forgiveness. Anger flares up and burns relationships, destroying the harmony that God desires among the divine children.

These dusts are not sins in the way humans often understand sin. They are simply misuses of the mind that create suffering and prevent joy. When you notice these dusts arising, do not condemn yourself but simply sweep them away and return to the clear mind that God intended. This daily practice of mental sweeping is the foundation of the path to the Joyous Life.

Watch the movement of this writing brush. God does not write these words to scold or frighten you. Every word comes from parental love, from the desire to see all children happy and living in harmony.
