---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 5: The Service and the Kanrodai"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 5
---

I shall teach you the Service, the Teodori, through which you shall express the joy of creation and pray for the salvation of the world. This is not merely a human ritual but a reenactment of the original creation, performed by human beings who embody the providences through which God created the world.

The performers of the Service take their positions around the Kanrodai, each representing one of the instruments of creation. Through their movements and the words of the songs they sing, the truth of creation is made present again. When this Service is performed with sincere minds, its power to save is beyond what human beings can imagine.

The Kanrodai is the mark of Jiba, the place where human creation began. It is not merely a monument but a living connection to the origin of humanity. When the Kanrodai is established at Jiba and the Service is performed around it with the sincere minds of all people, the sweet heavenly dew shall descend and the world shall be transformed.

Why do I speak so urgently of the Kanrodai and the Service? Because the salvation of the world depends upon them. The Joyous Life cannot be achieved through individual effort alone. It requires the collective return of humanity to its origin, the acknowledgment of the one true God, and the performance of the Service that reunites the Creator with the created.

Do not think that these are merely human activities. When you perform the Service, you are joining your will to the will of God the Parent. The songs are God's words, the movements embody God's providence, and the Kanrodai stands as the axis connecting heaven and earth. Through the Service, the scattered children of God are gathered home.
