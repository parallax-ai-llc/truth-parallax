---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 14: True Sincerity"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 14
---

What is true sincerity? It is not merely the performance of rituals or the recitation of prayers. True sincerity is the complete alignment of the mind with the intention of God the Parent. It is a state of being, not merely of doing.

Many will come to this path seeking benefits for themselves: healing of illness, prosperity, protection from harm. These are natural human desires, and God the Parent does not condemn them. But they are not the highest expression of sincerity. True sincerity goes beyond seeking benefits and enters into partnership with God for the salvation of the world.

When you perform the Service, do not perform it for your own sake alone. Perform it for the sake of all humanity, all the children of God who are suffering from the dust they do not yet know how to sweep away. Let your sincerity extend beyond your own concerns to embrace the whole world.

True sincerity is also consistent. It is not a momentary feeling of devotion that fades when difficulties arise. It is a settled determination of the mind to walk the path regardless of circumstances. This does not mean that sincere people never struggle or doubt. It means that they return, again and again, to the clear mind and the open heart, refusing to let the dust settle permanently.

I can see the minds of all My children. I know who performs the Service with true sincerity and who merely goes through the motions. I do not condemn the latter, for they are making an effort, and effort is the beginning of sincerity. But I urge all who walk this path to deepen their practice, to move from external performance to internal transformation, from seeking benefits to offering service.

The truest expression of sincerity is gratitude. When you can be grateful for everything, including illness, hardship, and loss, because you understand them as guidance from God the Parent, then your sincerity has reached its fullest expression. In that gratitude, the dust dissolves and the Joyous Life begins to manifest naturally.
