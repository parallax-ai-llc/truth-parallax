---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 8: The Metaphor of Water"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 8
---

Consider the nature of water. It flows downward, seeking the lowest place. It nourishes all things without discrimination. It adapts its form to whatever vessel contains it. In these qualities, water teaches the mind how to accord with the will of God the Parent.

The mind that seeks to rise above others, that distinguishes between worthy and unworthy recipients of kindness, that insists on maintaining its own rigid shape regardless of circumstances, has strayed from the natural way. Like water, the ideal mind is humble, generous, and flexible.

Yet water also has great power. It carves canyons through stone, sustains all living things, and in its vast oceanic form contains the origins of life itself. The humble mind, aligned with God's intention, possesses a similar quiet power that can transform the world without force or violence.

I use many metaphors in this writing because the truth I wish to convey is deeper than what words alone can express. The intellectual understanding of these teachings is important, but it is not sufficient. The truth must penetrate to the depths of the heart, transforming not just thought but feeling, not just belief but action.

When you read these words, do not simply agree or disagree with them. Hold them in your mind as you go about your daily life. Watch how the dusts arise and how they can be swept away. Observe how illness corresponds to mental states. Notice the difference between a mind full of dust and one that has been swept clean. Through this practice, the truth shall become not just something you believe but something you know from your own experience.

The path to the Joyous Life is not a path of belief alone but of practice, of daily sweeping, of constant return to the clear mind that God intended for every human being.
