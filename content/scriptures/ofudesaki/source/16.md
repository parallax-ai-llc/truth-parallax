---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 16: The Sazuke and Healing"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 16
---

I have granted the Sazuke, the divine grant, as a means through which God's providence can be channeled for the healing of illness. Those who receive the Sazuke bear a sacred responsibility, for through their hands and their prayers, the healing power of God the Parent reaches those in need.

The Sazuke is not magic, and those who administer it are not magicians. The healing comes from God, not from the human instrument. The role of the person administering the Sazuke is simply to serve as a clear channel through which divine providence can flow. This requires a mind swept clean of dust, for a dusty mind blocks the flow as surely as a clogged pipe blocks water.

When administering the Sazuke, the performer must set aside all self-concern, all doubt, and all sense of personal power. The prayer that accompanies the Sazuke is not a request for a favor but an invitation for God's healing providence to flow through the performer to the person in need. The effectiveness of the Sazuke depends not on the performer's skill but on the sincerity of both the giver and the receiver.

Healing through the Sazuke works in conjunction with the clearing of mental dust. The physical healing that results from the Sazuke is a sign and a support for the inner healing that must also take place. Without the clearing of the dust that caused the illness, the physical symptoms may return, for the root cause has not been addressed.

I say to those who have received the Sazuke: use it freely and without hesitation. Do not hoard it or restrict it to those you deem worthy. Every child of God is worthy of God's healing, and your role is simply to serve as the channel through which it reaches them. The more you use the Sazuke with sincerity, the clearer the channel becomes, and the more effectively God's providence can flow through you.
