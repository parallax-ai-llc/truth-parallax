---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 11: Free Will and Divine Will"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 11
---

The mind alone is yours. This is the fundamental gift and the fundamental challenge of human existence. God the Parent lends you the body, sustains you with divine providence in every breath and heartbeat, yet leaves the mind free to choose its own direction. This freedom is not an oversight but the very condition that makes the Joyous Life possible.

Joy that is compelled is not joy. A puppet dancing on strings may appear joyful, but its movements have no meaning. God desires not obedience born of fear or habit but the free, willing alignment of the human mind with the divine intention. When you choose to sweep away the dust, when you choose to live in accord with God's heart, that choice is the seed of genuine joy.

Yet this freedom also means that you can choose otherwise. You can accumulate dust, cause suffering to yourself and others, and live in opposition to the purpose for which you were created. God will not prevent this, though God will send signs and guidance to help you find your way back.

The relationship between divine will and human freedom is not a contradiction but a harmony. God's will is the Joyous Life for all humanity. Human freedom is the means by which that joy is realized. When the human mind freely chooses to align with God's intention, the result is not the loss of freedom but its fulfillment, for the mind was created for joy, and in choosing joy, it finds its truest nature.

Do not be confused by the difficulties of the path. The freedom that makes wrong choices possible is the same freedom that makes right choices meaningful. The dust that clouds the mind is not permanent. It can be swept away at any moment by an act of sincere will. And in that moment of clearing, the original brightness of the mind shines forth as God intended from the beginning.
