---
title: "Ofudesaki Section 12: The World as One Family"
scripture: "ofudesaki"
chapter: 12
---

I say to you again: all human beings in the world are brothers and sisters, children of a single Parent. This is not a metaphor but the literal truth of creation. You all came from the same origin, and you all carry within you the same divine nature, the same capacity for joy, and the same fundamental worth.

The divisions among you, the nations, races, classes, and factions, have no reality in the eyes of God the Parent. They are constructions of the dusty mind, ways of separating what God has united. As long as these divisions persist in your hearts, the Joyous Life cannot be realized, for the Joyous Life is a life lived together, not in isolation.

I do not say that all people must become the same. The diversity of humanity is beautiful, a reflection of the diversity of divine providence. Different people, different gifts, different perspectives, all contributing to the richness of the whole. But this diversity must be experienced as harmony, not as conflict. The instruments of creation differ from one another, yet they work together to sustain life. So must the diverse peoples of the world work together to build the Joyous Life.

Begin where you are. In your family, in your community, in your daily interactions, practice seeing others as brothers and sisters. When conflict arises, remember that the person before you is a child of the same Parent who created you. This does not mean that all behavior must be accepted without discernment, but it means that even correction and disagreement should be grounded in the recognition of shared divine origin.

The world is not yet ready to live as one family. I know this. But the seed must be planted before the harvest can come. Plant this seed in your own mind, tend it with daily practice, and trust that God the Parent is working in countless ways to bring the harvest in due time.
