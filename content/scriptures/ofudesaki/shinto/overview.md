## Overview

The Shinto reading of the Ofudesaki examines the text's relationship to Japanese *kami* theology and indigenous religious concepts. While Tenrikyo is an independent religion, its vocabulary and cosmological framework draw heavily on the Shinto tradition. The Ofudesaki's concept of *Oyagami* (God the Parent) engages with the Shinto notion of *kami* while transforming it into a monotheistic creator theology that differs from the polytheistic *kami* pantheon of traditional Shinto.

The Ofudesaki's creation narrative, in which God the Parent creates human beings from primordial mud at the original place (*Jiba*), echoes Japanese creation myths recorded in the Kojiki and Nihon Shoki while offering a distinctive alternative. Shinto scholars note both the continuities — the emphasis on sacred place, ritual purity, and the relationship between humanity and the divine — and the departures from Shinto orthodoxy, particularly the Ofudesaki's insistence on a single creator God and its universal soteriology.
