## Shinto Reading

The Shinto reading of Part 10 examines how the Ofudesaki's teaching on the sweeping of dust engages with traditional Japanese religious concepts. The text employs vocabulary and imagery familiar from Shinto tradition while transforming their meaning within a monotheistic theological framework that differs significantly from Shinto's polytheistic *kami* worship.

Shinto scholars note both continuity and rupture: the Ofudesaki's emphasis on purification, sincerity, and the sacred quality of the natural world resonates with Shinto sensibilities, while its insistence on a single creator God and its universal soteriology depart from the particularistic orientation of traditional Shinto practice.
