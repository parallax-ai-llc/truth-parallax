## Overview

Hebrews is deeply embedded in Orthodox liturgical theology and Christology. The letter's vision of Christ as the eternal high priest ministering in the heavenly sanctuary corresponds to the Orthodox understanding of the Divine Liturgy as participation in the heavenly worship. The patristic commentaries of John Chrysostom and Theodoret of Cyrus shape Orthodox exegesis profoundly.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Heavenly Liturgy
Orthodox theology reads Hebrews' contrast between earthly shadows and heavenly realities (8:1-5; 9:11-12, 24) not as abolishing liturgical worship but as revealing its true nature. The earthly liturgy participates in the heavenly liturgy that Christ eternally celebrates.

### Theosis Through the High Priest
Christ's priesthood enables human participation in the divine nature. By passing through the heavens (4:14), Christ opens a "new and living way" (10:20) for humanity to enter the divine presence — the very goal of theosis.

### The Saints as Cloud of Witnesses
The catalog of faithful ones in chapter 11 and the "cloud of witnesses" in 12:1 support the Orthodox veneration of the saints. These are not distant historical figures but living participants in the communion of saints who surround and encourage the faithful.
