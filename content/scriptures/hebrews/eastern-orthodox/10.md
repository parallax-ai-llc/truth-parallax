## A Call to Persevere

Eastern Orthodoxy interpretation of Hebrews 10 centers on patristic commentary and liturgical theology. The key passages — the law has but a shadow of good things (10:1), a new and living way (10:20), do not shrink back (10:39) — have been read through the lens of theosis, the liturgical life of the Church, and patristic consensus, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

John chrysostom, theodoret of cyrus, and other eastern fathers gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on a call to persevere has shaped Eastern Orthodoxy understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern eastern orthodoxy scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 10 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
