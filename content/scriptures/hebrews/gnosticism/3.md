## Jesus Greater Than Moses

Gnostic interpretation of Hebrews 3 centers on esoteric knowledge and the liberation of the divine spark. The key passages — Jesus worthy of more glory than Moses (3:3), today if you hear his voice (3:7-8), they were unable to enter because of unbelief (3:19) — have been read through the lens of spiritual awakening, the duality of matter and spirit, and hidden meanings, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Ancient Gnostic teachers and modern scholars of Nag Hammadi texts gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on jesus greater than moses has shaped Gnostic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Gnostic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
