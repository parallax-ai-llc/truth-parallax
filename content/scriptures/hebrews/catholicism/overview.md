## Overview

Hebrews is enormously important for Catholic theology, providing the scriptural foundation for the theology of Christ's priesthood, the sacrificial understanding of the Eucharist, and the new covenant. While Protestants read the letter as undermining priestly sacrifice, Catholics understand it as revealing the heavenly reality that the earthly liturgy makes present.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Christ's Eternal Priesthood and the Eucharist
Catholic theology reads Hebrews' presentation of Christ's priestly ministry not as a completed past event but as an ongoing heavenly intercession (7:25). The Eucharist does not repeat Christ's sacrifice but makes his one sacrifice sacramentally present to each generation.

### The Cloud of Witnesses
The "great cloud of witnesses" (12:1) supports Catholic devotion to the saints. The heroes of faith in chapter 11 are not merely historical examples but members of the communion of saints who surround and encourage the Church.

### The Warning Passages and Purgatory
The letter's stern warnings about falling away (6:4-6; 10:26-31) are read alongside its affirmation of divine discipline (12:5-11) within the broader framework of Catholic teaching on purgation and the possibility of losing salvation through mortal sin.
