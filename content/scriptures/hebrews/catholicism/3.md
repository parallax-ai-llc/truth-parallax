## Jesus Greater Than Moses

Catholic interpretation of Hebrews 3 centers on sacred Tradition, the Magisterium, and sacramental theology. The key passages — Jesus worthy of more glory than Moses (3:3), today if you hear his voice (3:7-8), they were unable to enter because of unbelief (3:19) — have been read through the lens of the unity of Scripture and Tradition under the guidance of the Magisterium, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

The church fathers, thomas aquinas, and modern catholic exegetes gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on jesus greater than moses has shaped Catholic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Catholic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
