## Jesus Greater Than Moses

Jewish interpretation of Hebrews 3 centers on Jewish-Christian relations and Second Temple Judaism. The key passages — Jesus worthy of more glory than Moses (3:3), today if you hear his voice (3:7-8), they were unable to enter because of unbelief (3:19) — have been read through the lens of the Jewish roots of the text and its implications for Jewish-Christian dialogue, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Modern Jewish scholars of early Christianity gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on jesus greater than moses has shaped Jewish understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Jewish scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
