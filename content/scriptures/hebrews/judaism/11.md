## The Hall of Faith

Jewish interpretation of Hebrews 11 centers on Jewish-Christian relations and Second Temple Judaism. The key passages — faith is the assurance of things hoped for (11:1), by faith Abraham obeyed (11:8), a great cloud of witnesses (11:39-40) — have been read through the lens of the Jewish roots of the text and its implications for Jewish-Christian dialogue, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Modern Jewish scholars of early Christianity gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on the hall of faith has shaped Jewish understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Jewish scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 11 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
