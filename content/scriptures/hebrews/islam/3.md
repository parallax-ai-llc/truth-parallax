## Jesus Greater Than Moses

Islamic interpretation of Hebrews 3 centers on comparative theology and Qur'anic parallels. The key passages — Jesus worthy of more glory than Moses (3:3), today if you hear his voice (3:7-8), they were unable to enter because of unbelief (3:19) — have been read through the lens of monotheism (tawhid), prophetic tradition, and ethical convergences, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Muslim scholars of comparative religion gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on jesus greater than moses has shaped Islamic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Islamic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
