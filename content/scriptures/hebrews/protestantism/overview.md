## Overview

Hebrews is indispensable for Protestant Christology and soteriology. Its portrayal of Christ as the great high priest who offered himself once for all (7:27; 9:26; 10:10) undergirds the Protestant rejection of the Mass as a re-offering of Christ's sacrifice. The letter's contrast between old and new covenants reinforces the Reformation's emphasis on the sufficiency of Christ's work.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Finished Work of Christ
Protestants emphasize that Christ "sat down at the right hand of God" (10:12), indicating his sacrificial work is complete. This stands against any notion of a continuing or repeated sacrifice in worship.

### The Warning Passages
The five warning passages (2:1-4; 3:7-4:13; 5:11-6:12; 10:26-31; 12:25-29) generate intense debate. Calvinists read them as hypothetical or directed at false believers; Arminians see genuine warnings against apostasy for true believers.

### Faith as Assurance
The definition of faith in 11:1 and the catalog of faithful witnesses in chapter 11 shape Protestant preaching on trust in God's promises despite contrary circumstances.
