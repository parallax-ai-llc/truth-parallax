## Jesus the Great High Priest

Protestant interpretation of Hebrews 5 centers on sola Scriptura and the Reformation heritage. The key passages — every high priest chosen from among men (5:1), a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek (5:6), he learned obedience through suffering (5:8) — have been read through the lens of the authority of Scripture and justification by faith, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Luther, calvin, and subsequent reformed and evangelical scholars gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Hebrews. The chapter's teaching on jesus the great high priest has shaped Protestant understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Protestant scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Hebrews 5 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
