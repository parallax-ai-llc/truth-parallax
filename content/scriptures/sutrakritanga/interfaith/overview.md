## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Sutrakritanga finds in its polemical content not merely a record of ancient disputes but resources for contemporary inter-religious dialogue. The text's engagement with rival views, when read constructively, models a tradition of taking other positions seriously enough to engage with them philosophically.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### From Polemic to Dialogue

While the Sutrakritanga refutes rival doctrines, interfaith interpreters note that its method requires understanding those doctrines accurately before critiquing them. This intellectual rigor can be repurposed as a model for honest inter-religious engagement.

### Shared Ethical Commitments

The text's passionate advocacy for non-violence, truthfulness, and self-restraint identifies values shared across the world's religious traditions. Interfaith readers find common ground in the Sutrakritanga's ethical vision.

### Anekantavada as Dialogue Framework

The Jain principle of many-sidedness, while not yet fully developed in the Sutrakritanga, is implicit in the text's recognition that rival schools grasp partial truths. This principle provides a philosophical foundation for respectful inter-religious dialogue.
