## Overview

The Digambara tradition approaches the Sutrakritanga with respect for its antiquity while recognizing that their canonical tradition differs from the Shvetambara recension. Digambara scholars value the text's vigorous defense of Jain doctrine against heterodox views, reading its polemical passages through the lens of their own stricter interpretive framework.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Doctrinal Rigor

Digambara interpreters emphasize the Sutrakritanga's uncompromising rejection of materialism, hedonism, and fatalism. The text's insistence that only the Jain path leads to liberation resonates with the Digambara emphasis on doctrinal purity and the completeness of the Tirthankara's teaching.

### Ascetic Ideal

The chapters on asceticism are read as supporting the Digambara position that complete renunciation -- including the abandonment of clothing -- is necessary for the highest spiritual attainments. The text's praise of severe austerity aligns with Digambara monastic practice.

### Polemical Tradition

Digambara scholars situate the Sutrakritanga within a broader tradition of philosophical debate, connecting it to later Digambara logicians such as Akalanka and Vidyananda who continued the work of refuting rival philosophies.
