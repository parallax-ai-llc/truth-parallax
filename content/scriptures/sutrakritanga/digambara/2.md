Digambara interpreters emphasize the chapter's teaching that karma is destroyed through severe austerity and complete self-discipline. The path to liberation requires not merely avoiding new karma but actively burning away accumulated karmic matter through tapas -- a process that demands the total renunciation characteristic of Digambara monasticism.
