## Overview

Philosophical interpretation of the Sutrakritanga examines the text's arguments as contributions to systematic philosophy. The text's refutation of rival positions employs logical and dialectical methods that anticipate later developments in Indian epistemology and logic.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Epistemological Foundations

The Sutrakritanga's critique of rival schools rests on epistemological principles -- particularly the Jain theory that reality is multi-faceted and that partial perspectives lead to error. The text demonstrates how attachment to a single viewpoint (ekantavada) produces philosophical distortion.

### Ethical Argumentation

The text presents one of the earliest sustained philosophical arguments for absolute non-violence, grounding ahimsa not merely in sentiment but in a metaphysical understanding of the pervasiveness of life and the karmic consequences of harm.

### Dialectical Method

The Sutrakritanga's method of presenting and refuting rival views anticipates the prasanga (reductio ad absurdum) method that became central to later Indian philosophical debate.
