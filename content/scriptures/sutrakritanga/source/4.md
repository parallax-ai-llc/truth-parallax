---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 4"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 4
---

**1** A monk should not be negligent. Negligence is the root of all evil.

**2** A negligent monk falls from the path. He accumulates karma and is bound to the cycle of birth and death.

**3** A diligent monk progresses on the path. He sheds his karma and moves toward liberation.

**4** Therefore, be vigilant. Be careful. Be mindful at all times.

**5** Reflect on the transitory nature of the world. Nothing lasts forever. Youth fades, strength declines, beauty vanishes, and life comes to an end.

**6** Reflect on the solitude of the soul. The soul comes alone and goes alone. No one accompanies it.

**7** Reflect on the cycle of birth and death. The soul has been wandering in this cycle from beginningless time. It has experienced every form of suffering.

**8** Reflect on the nature of the body. The body is impure. It is made of flesh, blood, bones, and various impurities.

**9** Reflect on the influx of karma. Through careless activities, karmic matter enters the soul and binds it.

**10** Reflect on the means of stopping this influx. Through the practice of the vows and self-restraint, the influx can be stopped.

**11** Reflect on the shedding of karma. Through austerity and meditation, the accumulated karma can be shed.

**12** He who reflects on these things becomes wise. He attains liberation. This is the teaching.
