---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 1"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. The venerable Mahavira spoke thus: I say that all beings are dear to themselves. Therefore one should not injure any being.

**2** Having lived in the world for a long time, the wise and the ignorant alike must die. The soul is not killed when the body is killed.

**3** One should not speak untruth. One should not speak what is harsh. One should not speak what is unprofitable. One should speak only what is true, pleasant, and profitable.

**4** A wise man should not take what is not given. He who takes what is not given commits a sin.

**5** All beings desire happiness and shrink from pain. Life is dear to all. Therefore one should not harm any living being.

**6** The ignorant man thinks pleasure to be happiness. The wise man knows that pleasure leads to suffering.

**7** One should practise self-restraint. One should not yield to anger, pride, deceit, or greed. These are the four great passions that bind the soul.

**8** By renouncing all attachments, a monk attains freedom. By clinging to attachments, a householder is bound.

**9** The world is afflicted by death and decay. The wise do not grieve, for they have understood the nature of the world.

**10** He who has conquered his own self is a true hero. The conquest of oneself is greater than the conquest of all others.

**11** Non-violence is the highest religion. Non-violence is the highest self-restraint. Non-violence is the greatest gift. Non-violence is the best suffering. Non-violence is the greatest sacrifice.

**12** Non-violence is the highest happiness. Non-violence is the greatest truth. Non-violence is the purest conduct. Thus spoke Mahavira, the twenty-fourth Tirthankara.
