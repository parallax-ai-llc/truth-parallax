---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 5"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 5
---

**1** A monk should practise austerity. Austerity is the fire that burns away karma.

**2** External austerity consists of fasting, eating less than one's fill, restricting the variety of food, avoiding tasty food, sitting in a lonely place, and mortifying the body.

**3** Internal austerity consists of expiation, reverence, serving the monks, study, meditation, and abandoning the body.

**4** Of all austerities, meditation is the most powerful. Through meditation, the soul can shed vast amounts of karma in a short time.

**5** There are four kinds of meditation: sorrowful meditation, cruel meditation, righteous meditation, and pure meditation.

**6** Sorrowful meditation arises from the loss of what is dear and from the presence of what is not dear. Cruel meditation arises from the desire to harm others.

**7** These two kinds of meditation lead to bondage. They should be avoided.

**8** Righteous meditation consists of reflecting on the teachings of the Jinas, on the nature of karma, on the structure of the universe, and on the means of liberation.

**9** Pure meditation is the meditation on the nature of the soul itself, free from all karmic impurities. It leads directly to liberation.

**10** He who practises pure meditation destroys all his karma. He attains omniscience.

**11** With the destruction of all karma, the soul rises to the summit of the universe. There it dwells in infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy.

**12** This is liberation. This is the goal of all spiritual practice. This is the teaching of the Jinas.
