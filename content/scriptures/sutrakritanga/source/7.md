---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 7"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The wise man should reflect: "I am all alone. I must bear the consequences of my own actions."

**2** No father, no mother, no brother, no sister, no friend, no companion can share in one's karma.

**3** Each soul is born alone, suffers alone, and dies alone. Each soul must work out its own salvation.

**4** Therefore, why should one commit sin for the sake of others? Why should one accumulate karma for fleeting pleasures?

**5** The pleasures of the senses are momentary. They pass away like a flash of lightning. But the karma they generate lasts for a long time.

**6** The wise man, reflecting on this, renounces worldly pleasures. He devotes himself to the practice of virtue.

**7** He practises non-violence in thought, word, and deed. He speaks only the truth. He does not take what is not given.

**8** He lives a life of chastity. He is free from attachment to worldly possessions.

**9** He endures hardships without complaint. He is patient in the face of adversity.

**10** He is friendly towards all beings. He has compassion for all beings. He rejoices in the virtue of others.

**11** He is equanimous in the face of pleasure and pain, gain and loss, praise and blame, life and death.

**12** Such a man is a true monk. He is on the path to liberation. He will surely attain the goal.
