---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 11"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Renunciation is the gate to liberation. Without renunciation, there is no freedom from bondage.

**2** Renunciation is of two kinds: external and internal. External renunciation is the giving up of worldly possessions and relationships. Internal renunciation is the giving up of attachment and desire.

**3** External renunciation without internal renunciation is fruitless. It is merely a change of external conditions.

**4** Internal renunciation is the true renunciation. It frees the soul from the bondage of karma.

**5** He who renounces internally is free, even while living in the world. He who does not renounce internally is bound, even while living in a forest.

**6** The wise man renounces not out of despair or disappointment, but out of understanding. He sees the transitory nature of worldly pleasures and turns toward the eternal.

**7** He renounces anger, for anger burns the soul. He renounces pride, for pride blinds the soul. He renounces deceit, for deceit corrupts the soul. He renounces greed, for greed enslaves the soul.

**8** He cultivates forgiveness, humility, straightforwardness, and contentment.

**9** He is like a lotus in muddy water — untouched by the impurities around him.

**10** He is like the sky — vast, open, and unaffected by whatever passes through it.

**11** Such a man has truly renounced. He is on the threshold of liberation.

**12** He will soon attain the supreme state. This is the teaching of the Jinas.
