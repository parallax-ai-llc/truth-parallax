---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 9"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Right conduct is the foundation of the spiritual life. Without right conduct, there is no progress on the path.

**2** Right conduct consists in the observance of the five vows and the practice of the supplementary disciplines.

**3** The monk must be vigilant at all times. He must guard his mind against evil thoughts, his speech against evil words, and his body against evil deeds.

**4** He should walk carefully, looking ahead the length of a yoke, so as not to injure any living being.

**5** He should speak carefully, weighing every word, so as not to cause harm through speech.

**6** He should eat carefully, inspecting his food, so as not to consume any living being inadvertently.

**7** He should place and remove things carefully, so as not to crush any living being.

**8** He should dispose of bodily waste carefully, in a place free from living beings.

**9** These are the five kinds of carefulness. He who practises them is called a careful monk.

**10** The careful monk accumulates no new karma. He sheds the old karma through austerity. He moves steadily toward liberation.

**11** The careless monk accumulates new karma. He makes no progress on the path. He remains in the cycle of birth and death.

**12** Therefore, be careful. Be vigilant. This is the teaching of the Jinas.
