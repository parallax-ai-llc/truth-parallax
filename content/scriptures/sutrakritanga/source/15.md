---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 15"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 15
---

**1** Compassion is the hallmark of the wise. He who lacks compassion lacks wisdom.

**2** The wise man sees himself in all beings and all beings in himself. He knows that the same soul dwells in all.

**3** Therefore he does not harm any being. He does not cause any being to be harmed. He does not approve of any being being harmed.

**4** He is friendly towards all beings. He has goodwill towards all beings. He wishes well for all beings.

**5** He forgives those who harm him. He does not bear grudges. He does not seek revenge.

**6** He serves those who are in need. He helps those who are in distress. He comforts those who are in sorrow.

**7** He shares what he has with others. He does not hoard. He does not accumulate more than he needs.

**8** He speaks kind words. He speaks truthful words. He speaks words that bring peace and harmony.

**9** He is patient in adversity. He is humble in prosperity. He is equanimous in all circumstances.

**10** Such a man is a lamp in the darkness. He is a refuge for the weary. He is a friend to all beings.

**11** His compassion is not limited to human beings. It extends to all living creatures — those that walk, those that fly, those that swim, those that crawl.

**12** He who practises universal compassion is on the path to liberation. He is dear to the Jinas. He is a true follower of the Dharma.
