---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 3"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 3
---

**1** A monk who has renounced the world should not return to it. He should be firm in his resolve.

**2** He should endure heat and cold, hunger and thirst, mosquitoes and flies, and the touch of creeping things.

**3** He should not be angry when abused, nor pleased when praised. He should be even-minded in all circumstances.

**4** He should beg for his food without attachment. He should eat to live, not live to eat.

**5** He should not be attached to his begging bowl, his robes, or any other possession. All attachment leads to bondage.

**6** He should practise the five kinds of carefulness: carefulness in walking, carefulness in speaking, carefulness in eating, carefulness in placing and removing things, and carefulness in evacuating.

**7** He should practise the three controls: control of mind, control of speech, and control of body.

**8** He should forgive all beings and seek forgiveness from all beings. He should have friendship towards all beings.

**9** He should not fear death, for the soul does not die when the body dies. Death is merely the separation of the soul from the body.

**10** He who practises all these disciplines with steadfastness and devotion attains liberation. He becomes free from all suffering.

**11** This is the way of the monk. This is the path taught by the Jinas.

**12** He who walks this path reaches the end of sorrow. He reaches the end of sorrow indeed.
