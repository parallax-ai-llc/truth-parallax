---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 8"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 8
---

**1** There are beings who kill and cause others to kill. There are beings who steal and cause others to steal. There are beings who lie and cause others to lie.

**2** These beings, deluded by ignorance, accumulate vast quantities of karma. They suffer in the cycle of birth and death.

**3** The wise man does not kill, nor does he cause others to kill. He does not steal, nor does he cause others to steal. He does not lie, nor does he cause others to lie.

**4** The world is full of violence. Beings kill one another for food, for wealth, for power, for pleasure.

**5** But violence begets violence. He who kills will be killed. He who harms will be harmed. This is the inescapable law of karma.

**6** Therefore, the wise man abstains from violence. He practises non-violence towards all beings — those that are small and those that are great, those that move and those that do not move.

**7** He knows that every being, however small, has a soul. Every soul desires to live. Every soul fears death.

**8** He who deprives a being of its life commits the greatest sin. He who protects the life of a being does the greatest good.

**9** The Jinas have taught: "Do not kill any being. Do not harm any being. Do not cause suffering to any being."

**10** This is the essence of all religion. This is the core of all ethics. This is the foundation of all virtue.

**11** He who practises non-violence in all its fullness — he is a saint. He is a sage. He is a Jina.

**12** He who practises non-violence attains liberation. This is certain. This is the teaching of the omniscient ones.
