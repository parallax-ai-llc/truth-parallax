---
title: "Sutrakritanga - Chapter 10"
scripture: "sutrakritanga"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Knowledge is of five kinds: sensory knowledge, scriptural knowledge, clairvoyance, telepathy, and omniscience.

**2** Sensory knowledge is gained through the five senses and the mind. It is the most common form of knowledge.

**3** Scriptural knowledge is gained through the study of the sacred texts. It depends on sensory knowledge.

**4** Clairvoyance is the direct perception of material objects without the aid of the senses. It arises from the partial destruction of karmas.

**5** Telepathy is the direct knowledge of the thoughts of others. It too arises from the partial destruction of karmas.

**6** Omniscience is the simultaneous knowledge of all objects in all times and places. It arises from the complete destruction of knowledge-obscuring and perception-obscuring karmas.

**7** The knowledge of the ordinary person is limited and often distorted by karmic veils.

**8** The knowledge of the monk increases as he sheds his karmas through austerity and meditation.

**9** The knowledge of the omniscient one is unlimited and undistorted. He knows everything as it truly is.

**10** Wrong knowledge arises from wrong faith. It sees things as they are not. It leads to bondage.

**11** Right knowledge arises from right faith. It sees things as they truly are. It leads to liberation.

**12** Therefore, cultivate right knowledge. Study the teachings of the Jinas. Practise meditation. Attain omniscience.
