## Overview

For the Shvetambara tradition, the Sutrakritanga holds a position of high canonical authority as the second Anga, directly recording the teachings needed to distinguish true dharma from false doctrine. It is studied alongside the Acharanga Sutra as a foundational text for monastic education.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Canonical Context

Shvetambara scholars read the Sutrakritanga within the broader framework of the forty-five text Agamic canon, understanding its polemical content as part of Mahavira's comprehensive teaching program. The text's refutations of rival views are seen as protective measures to guard the community from doctrinal error.

### Textual Preservation

The Shvetambara tradition maintains that their recension of the Sutrakritanga preserves the oldest and most authentic version of the text. The Ardhamagadhi Prakrit language is considered closest to the language Mahavira himself used.

### Monastic Application

The text's teachings on asceticism and discipline are applied within the Shvetambara monastic framework, which permits the wearing of white garments while still demanding rigorous self-discipline and renunciation.
