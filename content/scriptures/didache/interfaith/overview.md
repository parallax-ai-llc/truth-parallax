## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Didache draws on its position at the junction of Jewish and Christian traditions. The Two Ways ethic shares deep roots with Jewish moral teaching and resonates with ethical frameworks across world religions. The text's transformation of Jewish practices (baptism, fasting, prayer) into distinctly Christian forms illustrates the process of religious differentiation that is of interest to scholars and practitioners of all traditions.
