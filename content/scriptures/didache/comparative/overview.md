## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Didache places its Two Ways teaching within the broader tradition of ethical dualism found in the Qumran Community Rule, Zoroastrian moral theology, and Buddhist ethical instruction. The text's ritual instructions invite comparison with Jewish mikveh practices, Hindu purification rites, and Islamic ablution traditions. The community organization reflects patterns of religious authority found across traditions.
