---
title: "Samavayanga Sutra - Chapter 7"
scripture: "samavayanga-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Seven are the fundamental elements taught by the Jinas: the soul, non-soul, influx of karma, bondage of karma, stoppage of influx, shedding of karma, and liberation.

**2** Seven are the types of fear that afflict beings: fear in this life, fear for the next life, fear of suffering, fear of the unexpected, fear of losing possessions, fear of death, and fear of loss of reputation.

**3** Seven are the types of wrong faith: blind faith, faith in false gods, faith in false teachers, faith in false scriptures, doubt about the true path, instability in faith, and praise of those on the wrong path.

**4** Seven are the fields of charity: images of the Jinas, temples, scriptures, monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen.

**5** Seven are the qualities of a true teacher: he is learned, he practises what he teaches, he is free from attachment, he is free from aversion, he is free from delusion, he serves others, and he leads by example.

**6** Seven are the stages of the soul in the descending order: the omniscient, the one with destructive karma removed, the one with conduct-deluding karma removed, the one with faith-deluding karma removed, the one with partial self-control, the one with no self-control but with right faith, and the one with wrong faith.

**7** Seven are the types of conjugal relationships that are prohibited for one who has taken the vow of celibacy.

**8** Seven are the virtues that a wise person cultivates: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, non-possessiveness, humility, and compassion.

**9** Seven are the types of meditation on the nature of the soul: the soul exists, the soul is eternal, the soul is the doer, the soul is the experiencer, liberation is possible, there is a path, and the path has been shown by the Jinas.

**10** Seven are the elements: these seven encompass all of reality as taught by the omniscient ones.
