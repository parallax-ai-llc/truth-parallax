---
title: "Samavayanga Sutra - Chapter 5"
scripture: "samavayanga-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Five are the great vows: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness. These are the foundation of the spiritual life.

**2** Five are the senses: touch, taste, smell, sight, and hearing. Through these five senses, beings experience the world.

**3** Five are the kinds of beings classified by their senses: one-sensed, two-sensed, three-sensed, four-sensed, and five-sensed beings.

**4** Five are the kinds of carefulness: carefulness in walking, carefulness in speaking, carefulness in eating, carefulness in placing and removing things, and carefulness in evacuating waste.

**5** Five are the types of right conduct: equanimity, carefulness, the observance of vows, the practice of austerities, and the conduct of a saint.

**6** Five are the bodies that a soul may possess: the food body, the transformative body, the conveyance body, the fiery body, and the karmic body.

**7** Five are the types of knowledge: sensory knowledge, scriptural knowledge, clairvoyance, telepathy, and omniscience.

**8** Five are the supreme beings worthy of worship: the Arhat, the Siddha, the Acharya, the Upadhyaya, and the Sadhu. Worship of these five brings great merit.

**9** Five are the ethical observances of a householder: non-violence with limitations, truthfulness with limitations, non-stealing with limitations, fidelity, and limiting possessions.

**10** Five are the supplementary vows: limiting the area of activity, limiting articles of daily use, purposeless sins avoidance, meditation, and fasting.
