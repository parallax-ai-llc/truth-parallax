---
title: "Samavayanga Sutra - Chapter 6"
scripture: "samavayanga-sutra"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Six are the substances that constitute the universe: the soul, matter, the principle of motion, the principle of rest, space, and time. These are eternal and uncreated.

**2** Six are the types of living beings: earth-bodied, water-bodied, fire-bodied, air-bodied, plant-bodied, and mobile beings.

**3** Six are the internal austerities: expiation, reverence, service, study, meditation, and renunciation. These purify the soul from within.

**4** Six are the external austerities: fasting, eating less, restricting the variety of food, avoiding tasty food, sitting in a lonely place, and mortifying the body.

**5** Six are the daily duties of a monk: equanimity, praise of the Tirthankaras, reverence to teachers, repentance for transgressions, resolution to avoid transgressions, and abandonment of the body.

**6** Six are the categories of karmic matter that determine the body: the name karma, the life-span karma, the body-making karma, the family karma, the status karma, and the obstructive karma.

**7** Six are the fundamental truths: the soul exists, the soul is eternal, the soul is the doer of its actions, the soul is the experiencer of the fruits of its actions, liberation exists, and there is a path to liberation.

**8** Six are the aspects of the Jain emblem: the arhat, the siddha, the acharya, the upadhyaya, the sadhu, and the dharma.

**9** Six are the forms of purity: purity of the soul, purity of faith, purity of knowledge, purity of conduct, purity of austerity, and purity of charity.

**10** Six are the types of daily meditation: meditation on the transitory nature of the world, meditation on helplessness, meditation on the cycle of transmigration, meditation on being alone, meditation on the separateness of body and soul, and meditation on the impurity of the body.
