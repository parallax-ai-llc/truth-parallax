## Overview

The Samavayanga Sutra (Samavāyāṅga Sūtra) is the fourth of the twelve Angas of the Shvetambara Jain canon. Like the Sthananga Sutra, it employs a numerical method of classification, but its focus is on the convergence or combination (*samavaya*) of multiple factors in doctrinal categories. It provides detailed cosmological, doctrinal, and practical enumerations that complement and extend the material found in other canonical texts.

## Structure

The text is organized into 8 sections covering progressively larger numerical groupings and their combinations:

1. **Section 1 — Cosmological Foundations**: Enumeration of world-structures and time cycles
2. **Section 2 — Categories of Beings**: Classification of living beings across realms
3. **Section 3 — Karmic Combinations**: How karmic factors converge and interact
4. **Section 4 — Doctrinal Enumerations**: Systematic listing of philosophical categories
5. **Section 5 — Ethical Classifications**: Moral categories and their gradations
6. **Section 6 — Monastic Regulations**: Numerical rules for ascetic conduct
7. **Section 7 — Cosmographic Details**: Measurements of the universe
8. **Section 8 — Comprehensive Summaries**: Final enumerations and convergences

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Digambara**: Acknowledge the text's antiquity and systematic value
- **Shvetambara**: Treat it as essential for understanding Jain cosmology
- **Academic**: A key source for Jain cosmological and mathematical thought
- **Comparative**: Offers parallels with Buddhist Abhidhamma numerical methods

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Convergence | How multiple doctrinal factors combine and interact |
| Cosmology | Detailed descriptions of the structure of the universe |
| Enumeration | Systematic numerical classification of all categories |
| Time Cycles | Ascending and descending epochs of cosmic time |
