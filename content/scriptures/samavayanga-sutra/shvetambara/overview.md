## Overview

The Shvetambara tradition regards the Samavayanga Sutra as the fourth Anga, essential for understanding how the various categories of Jain doctrine relate to and combine with one another. It complements the Sthananga Sutra's simple enumerations by showing the dynamic interactions among doctrinal categories.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Canonical Complement

Shvetambara scholars read the Samavayanga Sutra alongside the Sthananga Sutra, treating the two texts as a complementary pair: one enumerating categories, the other showing how those categories converge and interact.

### Cosmological Authority

The text's detailed cosmological measurements -- distances between world-regions, durations of time cycles, numbers of beings in various realms -- are treated as authoritative by Shvetambara scholars and form the basis for Jain cosmographic traditions.

### Doctrinal Integration

The concept of *samavaya* (convergence) helps Shvetambara interpreters understand how seemingly separate doctrinal categories form an integrated whole.
