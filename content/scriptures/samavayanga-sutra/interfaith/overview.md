## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Samavayanga Sutra finds in its cosmological vision a shared human impulse to understand the structure of reality. Every major religious tradition has attempted to map the cosmos, and the Samavayanga Sutra's detailed cosmology provides a distinctive Jain contribution to this universal quest.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Shared Cosmic Wonder

The text's detailed mapping of the universe reflects the same sense of awe and curiosity about creation that motivates cosmological thinking across all traditions -- from Genesis to Buddhist cosmology to modern scientific cosmology.

### Structural Parallels

The Samavayanga Sutra's multi-tiered universe finds structural parallels in Dante's Divine Comedy, Buddhist Pure Lands, Islamic descriptions of the heavens, and Hindu lokas, suggesting universal patterns in human cosmological imagination.

### Ethical Cosmology

Like many religious cosmologies, the Samavayanga Sutra connects cosmic structure to moral reality -- the different realms correspond to different moral states, linking cosmology to ethics in a way shared across traditions.
