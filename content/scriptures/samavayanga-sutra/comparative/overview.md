## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Samavayanga Sutra places Jain cosmological and classificatory thinking alongside similar systems in other traditions. The text's detailed mapping of the universe and its inhabitants invites comparison with Buddhist, Hindu, and other cosmological schemes.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Cosmologies Compared

The Samavayanga Sutra's multi-layered universe with its precise measurements can be compared with Buddhist cosmology (as in the Abhidharmakosa), Hindu puranic cosmology, and even medieval European and Islamic cosmic models.

### Convergence as Method

The text's concept of *samavaya* (convergence of factors) parallels similar analytical methods in other traditions -- Buddhist dependent origination, Vaisheshika samavaya, and systems theory in Western thought.

### Mathematical Traditions

The text's large-number calculations connect to a broader Indian mathematical tradition shared across Jain, Buddhist, and Hindu contexts, revealing a culture of precise quantitative thinking.

## Comparative Framework

| Concept | Jainism (Samavayanga) | Buddhism | Hinduism |
|---------|----------------------|----------|----------|
| Universe structure | Multi-tiered, precise | Three realms | Fourteen lokas |
| Time cycles | Ascending/descending | Kalpas | Yugas |
| Convergence | Samavaya | Dependent origination | Samavaya (Vaisheshika) |
