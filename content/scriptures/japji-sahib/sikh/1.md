The Mool Mantar is the foundational declaration of Sikh theology, encapsulating the entire teaching of Guru Nanak in a single formula: *Ik Onkar, Satnam, Karta Purakh, Nirbhau, Nirvair, Akal Murat, Ajuni, Saibhang, Gur Prasad* — One Universal Creator, whose Name is Truth, Creative Being, Without Fear, Without Enmity, Timeless Form, Unborn, Self-Existent, realized by the Guru's Grace. For the Sikh community, these words are not merely a theological statement but a lived mantra, recited at the beginning of every Sikh prayer and inscribed at the head of every section of the Guru Granth Sahib.

The opening Salok that follows — 'By thinking, one cannot know the True One, even by thinking hundreds of thousands of times' — establishes the fundamental Sikh insight that the divine reality transcends all human intellectual effort. Only through stilling the mind in obedience to the divine will (*Hukam*) can one approach the truth. This sets the stage for the entire Japji Sahib, which unfolds as a progressive meditation on how to live in alignment with the divine order.
