Pauri 9 continues the *Suniai* (Listening) sequence, one of the most beloved passages in the Japji Sahib. In the Sikh devotional context, *Suniai* refers not merely to auditory hearing but to deep, attentive listening to the divine Word (*Shabad*) — a receptive opening of the entire being to the vibration of truth. Guru Nanak enumerates the spiritual blessings that flow from such listening: wisdom, virtue, inner peace, and ultimately liberation from the cycle of suffering.

The Sikh practice of *Kirtan* (devotional singing) and *Shabad* recitation is grounded in this teaching. The Gurdwara (Sikh place of worship) is designed as a space for communal listening — the *Sangat* (congregation) gathers to hear the Guru's Word, and the act of listening with devotion and attention is itself understood as a transformative spiritual practice. For the Sikh devotee, these pauris affirm that the path to God-realization begins not with doing but with being — with the receptive stillness that allows the divine Word to penetrate the heart.
