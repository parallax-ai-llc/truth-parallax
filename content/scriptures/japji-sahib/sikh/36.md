Pauri 36 presents the Saram Khand (Realm of Effort), one of the five spiritual realms (*khands*) that constitute one of the most distinctive and celebrated teachings in the Japji Sahib. For the Sikh community, the five *khands* describe the progressive stages of spiritual consciousness through which the soul journeys on its path to union with the divine. These are not physical locations but states of awareness — increasingly refined modes of experiencing reality as one deepens in devotion, wisdom, and grace.

The Sikh tradition understands the *khands* as a comprehensive map of spiritual development that encompasses and transcends the partial maps offered by other traditions. Each *khand* represents not only a stage of individual spiritual growth but also a dimension of the divine reality itself. The progression from Dharam (duty/righteousness) through Gian (knowledge), Saram (effort/beauty), and Karam (grace/power) to Sach (truth) traces the soul's journey from ethical obedience through intellectual understanding and aesthetic refinement to the experience of divine grace and ultimately the unmediated encounter with truth itself.
