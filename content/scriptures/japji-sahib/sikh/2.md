Pauri 2 introduces the concept of *Hukam* (divine order or will), one of the most central and distinctive concepts in Sikh theology. Guru Nanak declares that all beings and forms come into existence by the *Hukam*, that all actions and destinies unfold within the *Hukam*, and that the recognition of *Hukam* is the key to overcoming the fundamental human problem of *haumai* (ego-self). For the Sikh practitioner, this pauri establishes the essential spiritual orientation: to discern and accept the divine will in every circumstance of life.

The Sikh understanding of *Hukam* differs from fatalism or predestination. It is not a predetermined script that eliminates human agency but rather the recognition that the divine order pervades all reality, and that spiritual liberation comes through aligning one's will with this order rather than asserting the separate ego-self. The practice of *Hukamnama* — opening the Guru Granth Sahib at random to receive the Guru's guidance for the day — reflects this living relationship with the concept of divine will.
