Pauri 28 — Munda Santokh — The Yoga of Contentment — continues the progressive spiritual teaching of the Japji Sahib. In the Sikh devotional context, this pauri is understood as part of Guru Nanak's comprehensive guide to spiritual realization through meditation on the divine Name (*Nam*). The Sikh community recites this pauri as part of the daily *Nitnem* practice, engaging with its teachings not as abstract theology but as lived spiritual guidance.

The themes of this pauri connect to the broader Sikh understanding that spiritual realization is not separate from daily life but is achieved through the integration of devotion, ethical conduct, and service (*seva*) within the framework of the divine order (*Hukam*). Guru Nanak's teaching here reinforces the conviction that every aspect of human experience — from the most mundane to the most exalted — is an arena for the practice of *Nam Simran* (remembrance of the divine Name).
