## Overview

The Japji Sahib occupies the supreme liturgical and theological position within Sikh practice. Recited each morning during *Amrit Vela* (the ambrosial hours before dawn), it is the first prayer in the *Nitnem* and the opening composition of the Guru Granth Sahib. For the Sikh community, the Japji Sahib is not merely a text to be studied but a living spiritual practice — a daily act of remembrance (*Nam Simran*) that attunes the devotee to the divine will (*Hukam*).

The Sikh tradition understands the Japji Sahib as Guru Nanak's comprehensive statement of the path to God-realization. Each pauri builds upon the previous one, tracing the soul's journey from the recognition of divine unity through stages of spiritual deepening to the ultimate experience of *Sach Khand* (the Realm of Truth). The practice of reciting the Japji Sahib with understanding and devotion is considered one of the most powerful means of overcoming *haumai* (ego) and realizing one's essential unity with the divine.
