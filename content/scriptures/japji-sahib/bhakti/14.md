In Bhakti perspective, Pauri 14's emphasis on *Mannai* (accepting/believing) connects to the Sant tradition's central teaching that sincere devotion (*bhakti*) to the divine Name is the one sufficient path to liberation. The Bhakti poets consistently taught that faith (*shraddha*) — not learning, not ritual, not social standing — is the essential qualification for the spiritual life. Guru Nanak's *Mannai* represents this Bhakti conviction in its most developed form.

The progression from *Suniai* to *Mannai* reflects the Bhakti understanding that hearing about the divine naturally leads to loving acceptance when the heart is open. The fruits of *Mannai* described in this pauri — honor, inner clarity, the ability to guide others — are the natural characteristics of the true *bhakta* (devotee) as described throughout the Sant tradition. The devotee who has truly accepted the divine Name becomes a beacon for others, not through formal authority but through the radiance of inner transformation.
