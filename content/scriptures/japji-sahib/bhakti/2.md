In the Bhakti context, Pauri 2's teaching on *Hukam* reflects the Sant tradition's characteristic emphasis on the sovereignty of God and the need for human surrender. The Bhakti poets — Kabir, Ravidas, Namdev — consistently taught that the divine will governs all creation and that liberation comes through accepting this will rather than through ritual performance or intellectual effort. Guru Nanak's formulation of *Hukam* represents a philosophical refinement of this Bhakti intuition, giving it conceptual precision while maintaining its devotional warmth.

The Bhakti reading emphasizes the pauri's emotional and existential dimensions. The recognition of *Hukam* is not merely an intellectual acknowledgment but a heartfelt surrender — the dropping of the ego's pretensions to autonomy and control in the presence of the overwhelming divine reality. This surrender is experienced not as defeat but as liberation, as the soul discovers its true nature in alignment with the divine order.
