The Bhakti tradition reads Pauri 6 — Tirath — Pilgrimage and Purification — within the context of the Sant movement's emphasis on interior devotion to the formless God. Guru Nanak's teaching in this pauri reflects the characteristic Bhakti themes of the primacy of devotion (*bhakti*) over ritual, the accessibility of the divine to all regardless of social status, and the transformative power of the divine Name (*Nam*). The pauri's language and imagery connect it to the broader tradition of Sant poetry in medieval North India.

The Bhakti reading emphasizes the experiential and devotional dimensions of the pauri's teaching. For the Bhakti tradition, the Japji Sahib is not primarily a work of theology or philosophy but a guide to the practice of love — the cultivation of a heart-centered relationship with the divine that transforms the devotee from the inside out.
