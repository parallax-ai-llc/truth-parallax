In the Bhakti context, Pauri 11's emphasis on *Suniai* (listening) connects to the Sant tradition's privileging of *shravan* (hearing) as the first and most accessible form of devotional practice. The Bhakti poets — particularly Kabir and Namdev — consistently taught that hearing the divine Name with love and attention is sufficient for liberation, requiring no special learning, ritual competence, or social status. This radical accessibility is central to the Bhakti revolution: if the path to God begins with simply listening, then it is open to all.

Guru Nanak's enumeration of the fruits of listening in this pauri echoes the Bhakti conviction that devotion to the divine Name (*Nam*) naturally produces all virtues and spiritual attainments. The devotee does not need to pursue wisdom, ethical perfection, or mystical experience as separate goals — they all arise spontaneously from the practice of attentive, loving engagement with the divine Word.
