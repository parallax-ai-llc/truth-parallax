The Bhakti tradition interprets the Karam Khand (Realm of Grace) of Pauri 37 within the broader Sant understanding of spiritual progression through devotion. The five *khands* represent the stages through which the devotee's relationship with God deepens — from initial ethical commitment through knowledge and effort to the experience of grace and finally truth. This progression echoes the Bhakti understanding that devotion naturally produces all other spiritual qualities — moral discipline, wisdom, beauty, power, and ultimately union with the divine.

The Bhakti reading emphasizes that the movement through the *khands* is not a product of human effort alone but of the interplay between personal devotion and divine grace. This resonates with the broader Bhakti dialectic between *sadhana* (spiritual practice) and *kripa* (divine grace) — the devotee must make the effort, but the ultimate transformation comes through God's initiative. Guru Nanak's inclusion of *Karam Khand* (Realm of Grace) as the penultimate stage reflects this Bhakti conviction.
