Pauri 24 — Ant Na Sifti — Endless Praises — offers material for comparative analysis across religious traditions. The themes addressed in this pauri resonate with teachings found in the Sufi, Bhakti, Nath Yogic, and Vedantic traditions, as well as with broader philosophical questions about the nature of the divine, the human condition, and the path to liberation. Comparative study reveals both the shared spiritual heritage of South Asian religious traditions and the distinctive innovations of Guru Nanak's thought.

The pauri's treatment of its central theme invites comparison with analogous passages in the Quran, the Upanishads, the Buddhist Dhammapada, and the Daodejing. Such comparisons illuminate both the universal dimensions of spiritual wisdom and the particular emphases that distinguish Sikh theology from its interlocutors.
