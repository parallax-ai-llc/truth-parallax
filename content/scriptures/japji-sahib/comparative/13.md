The *Mannai* sequence of Pauri 13 invites comparison with the role of faith, belief, and acceptance across religious traditions. The Christian concept of *pistis* (faith) as trust and commitment rather than mere intellectual assent, the Islamic understanding of *iman* (faith) as involving heart, tongue, and action, and the Buddhist notion of *saddha* (faith/confidence) as the foundation of the spiritual path all resonate with Guru Nanak's *Mannai*.

The transition from listening to accepting parallels the Buddhist pedagogical sequence of hearing (*suta*), reflection (*cinta*), and meditation (*bhavana*). Each tradition recognizes that spiritual knowledge must move from external reception through inner conviction to transformative practice. Guru Nanak's contribution is to present this process in language that is at once philosophically rigorous and devotionally warm.
