The *Suniai* sequence of Pauri 11 invites comparison with the emphasis on listening and hearing across religious traditions. The Vedic concept of *shruti* (revealed scripture as 'that which is heard'), the Islamic emphasis on the Quran as a recited and heard text, the Christian injunction 'He who has ears, let him hear,' and the Buddhist practice of hearing the Dharma all point to a cross-traditional recognition that receptive listening is a foundational spiritual practice.

Guru Nanak's treatment is distinctive in its comprehensiveness — these pauris enumerate an exhaustive catalogue of spiritual benefits that flow from genuine listening, from worldly wisdom to ultimate liberation. This echoes the Buddhist teaching that hearing the Dharma is the first stage of wisdom (*suta-maya-panna*), as well as the Sufi understanding that *sama'* (spiritual listening) can trigger transformative mystical states.
