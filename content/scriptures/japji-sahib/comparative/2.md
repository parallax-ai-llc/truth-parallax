The concept of *Hukam* invites comparison with analogous concepts across world traditions: the Stoic *logos* as the rational principle governing the cosmos, the Chinese *Tian Ming* (Mandate of Heaven), the Islamic *qadr* (divine decree), the Jewish concept of divine providence, and the Hindu *dharma* as cosmic order. Each tradition grapples with the relationship between divine sovereignty and human freedom, and each develops distinctive strategies for reconciling the two.

Guru Nanak's treatment of *Hukam* is notable for its emphasis on the experiential and ethical dimensions of the concept. Unlike purely speculative cosmological principles, *Hukam* is presented as something to be recognized and lived. The closest Western parallel may be the Stoic concept of 'living in accordance with nature' (*kata phusin*), where the rational order of the cosmos provides the normative framework for human conduct.
