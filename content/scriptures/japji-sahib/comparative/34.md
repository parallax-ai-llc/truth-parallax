The Dharam Khand (Realm of Righteousness) of Pauri 34 invites comparison with stages of spiritual development across traditions. The five *khands* as a whole resonate with the Sufi *maqamat* (stations of the spiritual path), the Christian mystical stages (purgation, illumination, union), the Buddhist *bhumis* (bodhisattva grounds), and the Kabbalistic *sefirot*. Each tradition maps the soul's journey from initial moral awakening through progressive refinement to ultimate union with or realization of divine/absolute reality.

The specific character of this *khand* reveals both convergences and divergences with other traditions' understandings of the corresponding stage. The five *khands* framework is notable for its inclusion of aesthetic and martial dimensions (particularly in *Saram Khand* and *Karam Khand*) that are less prominent in other traditions' spiritual maps, reflecting the distinctively Sikh integration of beauty, power, and grace in the spiritual life.
