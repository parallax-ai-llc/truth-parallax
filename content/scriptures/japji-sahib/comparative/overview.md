## Overview

In comparative perspective, the Japji Sahib offers remarkably rich material for cross-traditional analysis. Its opening declaration — *Ik Onkar* (One Universal Creator) — resonates with monotheistic affirmations across Abrahamic traditions while its apophatic theology (the divine as beyond all description) connects with mystical traditions from Meister Eckhart to Ibn Arabi to Shankara. The text's emphasis on interiority over ritual mirrors similar movements in Sufi Islam, Quaker Christianity, and Zen Buddhism.

The Japji Sahib's five-realm cosmology (*khands*) invites comparison with other models of spiritual ascent — the Sufi *maqamat*, the Kabbalistic *sefirot*, the Buddhist *bhumis*, and the Christian mystical ladder of John Climacus. Its ethical vision — emphasizing truthful living, compassion, humility, and social justice — provides common ground for interfaith dialogue on the relationship between spiritual realization and moral conduct.
