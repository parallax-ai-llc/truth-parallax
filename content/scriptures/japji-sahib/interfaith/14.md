Pauri 14's teaching on *Mannai* (accepting/believing) addresses one of the most fundamental questions in interfaith dialogue: What is the nature of genuine religious faith? Guru Nanak's answer — that true acceptance of the divine Word transforms every dimension of human existence — resonates with traditions that understand faith not as intellectual belief in propositions but as a total life-orientation toward ultimate reality.

The interfaith significance of the *Mannai* sequence lies in its description of faith's fruits rather than its doctrinal content. The qualities Guru Nanak attributes to the person of genuine faith — wisdom, honor, inner peace, the ability to guide others — are universally recognized as marks of authentic spiritual attainment. This common ground provides a basis for interfaith dialogue that moves beyond doctrinal comparison to the shared practical question: What does genuine faith look like in a human life?
