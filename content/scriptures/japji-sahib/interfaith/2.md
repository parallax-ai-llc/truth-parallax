Pauri 2's teaching on *Hukam* resonates with virtually every religious tradition's understanding of a divine or cosmic order that governs reality. Whether articulated as divine providence, natural law, *dharma*, *Tao*, or cosmic *logos*, the intuition that reality is ordered by a principle greater than individual human will is universal. The Japji Sahib's treatment of this theme is notable for its combination of philosophical depth and practical applicability — *Hukam* is not merely a metaphysical concept but a lived reality to be discerned in daily experience.

In interfaith dialogue, this pauri opens productive conversations about the nature of divine sovereignty, the problem of human suffering, and the relationship between acceptance of the divine will and active engagement with the world. Guru Nanak's *Hukam* avoids both passive fatalism and Promethean self-assertion, offering a middle way that many traditions can recognize as consonant with their own deepest insights.
