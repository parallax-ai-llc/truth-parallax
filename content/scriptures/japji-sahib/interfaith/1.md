The Mool Mantar has become one of the most frequently cited texts in interfaith dialogue, valued for its inclusive vision of the divine that transcends sectarian boundaries. Its description of God as *Nirbhau* (Without Fear) and *Nirvair* (Without Enmity) establishes the divine as fundamentally benevolent and impartial — qualities that all religious traditions aspire to embody. The declaration that God is *Ajuni* (Unborn) and *Akal Murat* (Timeless Form) speaks to the universal human intuition of a reality beyond the flux of time and change.

The opening Salok's gentle dismissal of the claim that any single way of thinking can capture the divine truth creates space for genuine interfaith humility. If the divine cannot be fully grasped by 'thinking hundreds of thousands of times,' then no single tradition can claim a monopoly on religious truth. This foundational insight opens the Japji Sahib to readers of all faiths as an invitation to explore the divine mystery together.
