## Overview

The Japji Sahib has become one of the most widely studied texts in interfaith contexts, valued for its universal spiritual vision that transcends sectarian boundaries. Its opening affirmation — *Ik Onkar*, One Universal Creator — establishes a framework that honors the divine reality beyond all human categories and religious labels. The text's insistence that truth is higher than everything but truthful living is higher still speaks to the shared ethical concerns of all religious traditions.

Interfaith engagement with the Japji Sahib often focuses on its vision of religious diversity as part of the divine order (*Hukam*). Guru Nanak does not argue for the superiority of one path over another but rather for the primacy of sincere devotion, ethical conduct, and humble submission to the divine will — qualities honored across all traditions. The Japji Sahib's cosmology, which envisions countless worlds and forms of life, anticipates a generous, expansive vision of reality that provides ample room for multiple valid perspectives on the divine.
