Pauri 17 — Asankh — The Countless — addresses themes that resonate across the world's religious traditions. The universal dimensions of Guru Nanak's teaching — the reality of the divine, the human capacity for spiritual transformation, and the primacy of inner sincerity over outward form — provide common ground for dialogue between practitioners of different faiths.

Interfaith engagement with this pauri often reveals unexpected convergences between traditions that appear superficially different. The shared human experience of seeking meaning, purpose, and connection to something greater than the individual self provides a foundation for mutual understanding that transcends doctrinal differences. Guru Nanak's inclusive vision — rooted in the conviction that 'there is no Hindu, there is no Muslim' — invites all seekers to engage with the Japji Sahib's wisdom on its own terms.
