Pauri 9's teaching on *Suniai* (listening) speaks to a universal spiritual principle recognized across traditions: that genuine receptivity to truth — whether conceived as divine revelation, natural law, or the voice of conscience — is the foundation of all spiritual growth. In an age of information overload and constant distraction, Guru Nanak's emphasis on deep, transformative listening takes on renewed relevance.

Interfaith dialogue on this pauri often explores the practical dimensions of spiritual listening across traditions — contemplative prayer in Christianity, *dhikr* in Islam, meditation in Buddhism, *hitbodedut* in Judaism — and the shared conviction that such practices cultivate qualities of wisdom, compassion, and inner peace that transcend sectarian boundaries. The universality of Guru Nanak's vision is evident in the fact that the blessings he attributes to listening are valued by all traditions: wisdom, virtue, fearlessness, and liberation from suffering.
