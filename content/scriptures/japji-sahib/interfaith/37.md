The Karam Khand (Realm of Grace) of Pauri 37 speaks to a universal intuition that the spiritual life involves progressive development — a journey from initial awakening through deepening understanding and commitment to ultimate realization. The five *khands* framework provides a map of this journey that, while distinctively Sikh, resonates with the spiritual maps of other traditions and offers a vocabulary for interfaith conversation about the nature and stages of spiritual growth.

Interfaith engagement with this pauri often reveals that practitioners of different traditions recognize their own experience in Guru Nanak's description. A Christian mystic may see the movement from purgation to illumination to union; a Sufi may recognize the stations of the path; a Buddhist may identify parallels with the progressive stages of insight. These convergences suggest that, beneath the diversity of theological languages, there may be a shared structure to the human encounter with the divine.
