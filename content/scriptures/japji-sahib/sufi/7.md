Sufi engagement with Pauri 7 — Jey Jug Charay — The Four Ages — highlights the resonances between Guru Nanak's spiritual vision and the traditions of Islamic mysticism. The themes of this pauri connect to Sufi teachings on the divine names, the spiritual journey (*suluk*), the annihilation of the ego (*fana*), and the cultivation of inner qualities through disciplined practice. The historical connections between the Sikh and Sufi traditions in Punjab lend particular depth to these parallels.

Sufi readers may recognize in this pauri echoes of the great Sufi poets — Rumi, Hafiz, Bulleh Shah — who similarly used poetry to communicate mystical insights that transcend the boundaries of formal religious doctrine. The shared emphasis on the primacy of love, the inadequacy of mere outward observance, and the transformative power of the divine Name connects the Japji Sahib to the broader tradition of Sufi spiritual literature.
