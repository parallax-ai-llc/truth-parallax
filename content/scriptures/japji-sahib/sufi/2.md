The Sufi tradition immediately recognizes in *Hukam* the Arabic theological concept it derives from — *hukm* — which in Islamic theology signifies the divine command that brings all things into being and governs all creation. The Quranic declaration 'Be! And it is' (*Kun fa-yakun*) expresses the same understanding of reality as arising from and governed by the divine will. Sufi readers of this pauri find Guru Nanak engaging directly with the Islamic mystical tradition of surrendering to the divine will (*tawakkul*) as the essence of spiritual life.

The Sufi concept of *rida* (contentment with the divine decree) closely parallels Guru Nanak's teaching that recognition and acceptance of *Hukam* is the path to liberation. Both traditions understand that the ego's resistance to the divine order is the root cause of spiritual suffering, and both prescribe a disciplined practice of surrender that does not entail passive resignation but rather an active alignment of the human will with the divine purpose.
