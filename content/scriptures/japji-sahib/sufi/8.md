Pauri 8 resonates profoundly with the Sufi tradition of *sama'* — spiritual listening or audition — which occupies a central place in Sufi practice. The great Sufi orders, particularly the Chishtiyya (the predominant Sufi order of medieval North India, with which Guru Nanak had direct contact), understood listening to sacred music and poetry as a powerful means of opening the heart to divine reality. The Sufi master Nizamuddin Auliya declared that *sama'* could accomplish in moments what years of ascetic discipline could not.

Guru Nanak's *Suniai* shares this conviction that listening — when practiced with sincerity, attention, and devotion — has the power to transform consciousness. The blessings enumerated in this pauri parallel the Sufi *maqamat* (stations) that the spiritual seeker traverses through disciplined practice. Both traditions understand that true listening is not passive reception but an active, intentional engagement of the whole being with the divine Word.
