Sufi readers recognize in the *Mannai* sequence of Pauri 15 a parallel to the Sufi emphasis on *yaqin* (certainty) as the fruit of spiritual practice. The Sufi tradition distinguishes three levels of certainty: *'ilm al-yaqin* (knowledge of certainty, through hearing), *'ayn al-yaqin* (eye of certainty, through direct vision), and *haqq al-yaqin* (truth of certainty, through identity with the Real). The movement from *Suniai* to *Mannai* corresponds to the movement from the first to the second level — from received knowledge to personal conviction.

The blessings enumerated in this pauri — inner transformation, honor, guidance — parallel the Sufi teaching that the true lover of God (*ashiq*) is transformed in every dimension of being. The Sufi concept of *fana* (annihilation of the ego) resonates with the Sikh understanding that *Mannai* involves the surrender of *haumai* (ego-self) — both traditions teach that genuine acceptance of the divine reality requires the dissolution of the false, separate self.
