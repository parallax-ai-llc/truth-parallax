## Overview

Academic study of the Japji Sahib situates the composition within the broader context of the medieval North Indian Sant tradition, examining its literary, philosophical, and historical dimensions. Scholars analyze the text's relationship to earlier Bhakti and Sufi poetry, its distinctive theological innovations, and its role in the formation of the Sikh community as a distinct religious tradition.

Key areas of scholarly inquiry include the composition's complex cosmology (particularly the five *khands* or realms), its engagement with Nath Yogic, Vedantic, and Islamic philosophical concepts, and the development of its textual tradition from the earliest manuscripts to its canonization in the Adi Granth of 1604. Linguistic analysis reveals a sophisticated multilingual poetics drawing on Punjabi, Braj Bhasha, Sanskrit, Persian, and Arabic vocabulary.
