The Mool Mantar has been the subject of extensive academic analysis as a condensed creedal formula that establishes the theological foundations of Sikhism. Scholars debate its precise parsing, the etymological derivation of key terms such as *Ik Onkar* and *Satnam*, and its relationship to earlier Sant, Sufi, and Nath Yogic theological formulations. W.H. McLeod's influential analysis identified the Mool Mantar as a succinct statement of the *nirguna* (formless) theology that characterizes the Sant tradition, while Arvind-Pal Singh Mandair has explored its philosophical implications through the lens of continental philosophy.

The opening Salok's declaration of the inadequacy of thought (*soch*) to reach the divine establishes an apophatic theology — a 'negative theology' of what God is not — that has parallels in Christian mysticism, Neoplatonism, and Sufi thought. Academic study examines how this apophatic opening frames the subsequent positive theology of *Nam* (divine Name) and *Hukam* (divine will) that unfolds through the 38 pauris.
