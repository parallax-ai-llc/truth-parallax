Pauri 37 belongs to the five *khands* sequence (Pauris 34-38), which has been one of the most extensively analyzed passages in academic Sikh studies. Scholars debate the philosophical sources and precise meaning of the *khands*, with interpretations ranging from stages of individual spiritual development to cosmological realms to modes of divine self-manifestation. The term *khand* itself (from Sanskrit *khanda*, meaning region, section, or realm) leaves open the question of whether these are primarily experiential states or ontological domains.

Academic analysis examines the five *khands* in relation to comparable frameworks in other traditions — the Sufi *maqamat* (stations), the Yogic *chakras*, the Buddhist *bhumis* (bodhisattva stages), and the Christian mystical itinerary. While parallels exist, scholars note that the *khands* framework is distinctively Sikh in its integration of ethical, intellectual, aesthetic, and mystical dimensions of spiritual development under the overarching principle of *Hukam* (divine will).
