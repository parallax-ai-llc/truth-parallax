## Overview

For Christian readers, the Act of Peter raises profound questions about the theology of healing and providence. If Peter can heal his daughter but chooses not to, what does this imply about God's relationship to suffering? The text seems to affirm a strong providentialist position: suffering serves God's purposes, and even miraculous healing may be refused when it would interfere with a greater spiritual good. This position anticipates later Christian debates about the relationship between physical healing and spiritual salvation.
