## Overview

The Act of Peter addresses a question that haunts every religious tradition: why does God allow the righteous to suffer? The text's answer — that suffering can serve a protective and purifying purpose — resonates with theodicy traditions across religions: the Muslim concept of divine testing (ibtila'), the Hindu concept of karma as pedagogy, the Buddhist understanding of suffering as the first Noble Truth that leads to liberation. The paradox of a healing reversed — the power to cure deliberately withheld — challenges simplistic notions of divine blessing in every tradition.
