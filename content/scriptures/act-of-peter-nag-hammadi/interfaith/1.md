## Peter Heals His Daughter — Interfaith Reflection

This section of Act of Peter addresses questions that transcend the boundaries of any single religious tradition. The Act of Peter addresses a question that haunts every religious tradition: why does God allow the righteous to suffer? The text's answer — that suffering can serve a protective and purifying purpose — resonates with theodicy traditions across religions: the Muslim concept of divine testing (ibtila'), the Hindu concept of karma as pedagogy, the Buddhist understanding of suffering as the first Noble Truth that leads to liberation.

The section "Peter Heals His Daughter" speaks from within a specific tradition — Gnostic Christianity of the second and third centuries — but its questions are universal. Every contemplative tradition has grappled with the themes explored here, and the Gnostic answers illuminate dimensions of the human spiritual search that resonate across cultures.

Interfaith readers may discover in this passage both the familiar and the strange — points of contact with their own traditions that create space for dialogue, alongside genuine differences that deepen mutual understanding and respect.
