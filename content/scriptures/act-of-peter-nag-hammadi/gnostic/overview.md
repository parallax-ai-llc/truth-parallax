## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Act of Peter's central paradox — a healing performed and then reversed — illustrates the Gnostic conviction that bodily wholeness is not necessarily spiritual wholeness. Peter's daughter is paralyzed to protect her virginity, suggesting that physical affliction can serve a spiritual purpose and that the preservation of sexual purity is worth any bodily cost. The text reflects the encratite (ascetic) tendencies within some Gnostic communities, where virginity was regarded as the highest form of spiritual discipline.
