## Overview

The Act of Peter is unique among Nag Hammadi texts as a narrative fragment rather than a revelation dialogue, treatise, or prayer. Its relationship to the broader Acts of Peter tradition (particularly the Latin Actus Vercellenses) has been studied for evidence of how apocryphal apostolic narratives circulated and were adapted by different communities. The theology of the text — particularly the reversal of the healing and its justification through the story of Ptolemy — raises questions about early Christian attitudes toward suffering, providence, and the body.
