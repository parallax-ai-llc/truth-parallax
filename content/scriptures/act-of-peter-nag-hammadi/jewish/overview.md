## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Act of Peter a theology of suffering that resonates with certain strands of Jewish thought (the concept of yissurin shel ahavah, 'afflictions of love,' in the Talmud) while diverging sharply from others. The emphasis on virginity as the supreme spiritual value stands in tension with the Jewish commandment to 'be fruitful and multiply' (Genesis 1:28) and the rabbinic affirmation of marriage as a sacred obligation. The text illustrates how early Christian ascetic values diverged from their Jewish roots.
