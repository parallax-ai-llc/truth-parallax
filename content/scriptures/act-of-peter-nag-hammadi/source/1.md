**1.** The Act of Peter. On the first day of the week, which is the Lord's day, the multitudes gathered together, and they brought unto Peter many sick that he might heal them. And one of the sick, who was blind from his youth, cried out and said, "Peter, apostle of Christ, behold, in a dream a vision appeared to me. You were sitting in a high place and before you was a great crowd. And you said to me, 'Be courageous and arise.' And I arose and I was seeing."

Then Peter said to him, "The word that gives light is in you." And he opened his eyes and he saw, and he fell on his face before Peter.

Peter said, "Glory belongs not to me but to the Lord Jesus Christ, through whom all healings come. For I am but a servant and an apostle of the Living One. It is Christ who opens the eyes—not merely the eyes of the flesh, but the eyes of the mind, so that you may perceive the truth that makes all people free."

And the people who witnessed this were amazed and glorified God, saying, "There is one God, the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, to whom be glory forever."
