**3.** Then Peter said to those who had gathered, "Now I must tell you about the suffering of the Lord and the mystery of the cross. For it was not merely a death that he died, but a revelation of what is imperishable.

"When the Savior was upon the cross, he said to me in a mystery, 'Peter, come, I shall reveal to you that which has been hidden from the Aeons. This cross of light is not the wooden cross that you see. And I am not the one upon the tree. The voice that you hear comes from me, but I am above the cross.'

"He said to me, 'Nothing, therefore, of that which they will say of me have I suffered. That suffering which I showed to you and the rest in the dance, I wish it to be called a mystery. For what you are, that I have shown you. But what I am, I alone know, and no one else.'

"Therefore, brothers, let us not look at the outward form of things, but at the inner truth. For the world sees the form, but the elect perceive the mystery. And the mystery is this: that the Living One cannot be conquered by death, and the Light cannot be overcome by darkness."

The Act of Peter.
