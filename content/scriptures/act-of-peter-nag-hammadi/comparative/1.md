## Peter Heals His Daughter — Comparative Perspectives

This section of Act of Peter addresses themes that resonate across the world's religious and philosophical traditions. The Act of Peter's paradox of a healing reversed resonates with traditions worldwide that recognize the spiritual value of affliction.

The section "Peter Heals His Daughter" invites cross-cultural comparison while respecting the irreducible particularity of the Gnostic context. The questions addressed here — about the nature of ultimate reality, the human condition, and the possibility of liberation — are shared by traditions as diverse as Vedanta, Buddhism, Neoplatonism, Kabbalah, and Sufism.

Comparative method at its best illuminates without flattening: genuine parallels create space for mutual understanding, while differences remind us that each tradition develops its insights within a particular historical and cultural matrix.
