## Overview

The Act of Peter's paradox of a healing reversed resonates with traditions worldwide that recognize the spiritual value of affliction. The Hindu concept of tapas (austerity that generates spiritual power), the Buddhist understanding of suffering as a teacher, and the Sufi embrace of hardship as a means of spiritual purification all affirm that physical ease is not identical with spiritual well-being. The text's emphasis on virginity connects to the widespread religious valorization of sexual abstinence, from Hindu brahmacharya to Buddhist celibacy to Vestal Virginity in Roman religion.
