## Overview

Tibetan Buddhist engagement with the Epic of King Gesar brings distinctive interpretive methods and concerns to this sacred text. This tradition reads the text as authoritative scripture within its community of faith.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Vajrayana Framework
Tibetan Buddhist interpreters read the Epic of King Gesar through the lens of tantric Buddhism, understanding its narratives as expressions of enlightened activity and the transformation of negative forces into wisdom.

### Oral Tradition
The Epic of King Gesar is transmitted through a living bardic tradition. Tibetan interpretation emphasizes the spiritual authority of the performer and the sacred nature of the recitation itself.

### Protector Deity Tradition
Within Tibetan Buddhism, figures from the Epic of King Gesar are understood as dharma protectors (*dharmapala*), and the narrative serves liturgical and protective functions.
