Comparative interpretation of the chapter on cultivating devotion places its practical instructions alongside similar guidelines in other traditions. The emphasis on spiritual community resonates with the Christian concept of ecclesia, the Sufi tariqa (order), the Buddhist sangha, and the Jewish havurah. The practice of vairagya (dispassion) finds parallels in the Christian counsels of poverty, chastity, and obedience, the Buddhist practice of nekkhamma (renunciation), and the Sufi concept of zuhd (asceticism).

The chapter raises comparative questions about the universal role of community, discipline, and renunciation in cultivating the spiritual life across traditions.
