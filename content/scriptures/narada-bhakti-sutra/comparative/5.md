Comparative analysis of the concluding chapter places the Narada Bhakti Sutra's vision of liberation through love alongside similar visions in other traditions. The Christian mystical tradition's concept of theosis (divinization), the Sufi concept of baqa (subsistence in God after annihilation of the ego), the Jewish kabbalistic concept of devekut as the soul's return to its divine source, and the Buddhist concept of Buddhahood as the full realization of wisdom and compassion all describe the consummation of the spiritual path in terms that parallel Narada's vision.

The chapter raises the fundamental comparative question of whether these diverse descriptions point to the same ultimate reality experienced through different cultural and theological lenses, or whether the differences in description reflect genuinely different spiritual destinations. This question remains one of the most important and contested in comparative religion.
