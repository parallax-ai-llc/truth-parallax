Comparative study of the chapter on the superiority of devotion examines how different traditions rank the relative importance of love, knowledge, and action in the spiritual life. The Christian debate between faith and works, the Islamic emphasis on submission (islam) as encompassing both inner attitude and outer practice, the Buddhist integration of wisdom and compassion, and the Jewish unity of study and practice all address the question of which spiritual faculty is supreme.

The Narada Bhakti Sutra's unequivocal affirmation of devotion's supremacy invites comparison with traditions that propose more integrative approaches, raising the question of whether a single spiritual quality can encompass all others.
