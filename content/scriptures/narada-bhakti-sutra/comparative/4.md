Comparative study of the forms of divine love places the chapter's typology alongside descriptions of devotional experience in other traditions. The Christian bridal mysticism of Bernard of Clairvaux and John of the Cross, the Sufi poetry of Rumi and Hafiz, and the Jewish mystical concept of devekut (cleaving to God) all describe varieties of intimate relationship with the divine. The Hindu bhakti tradition's explicit classification of these relationships into distinct rasas (flavors) represents a unique contribution to the cross-cultural phenomenology of religious experience.

The chapter illuminates the remarkable similarity of devotional experience across cultures while revealing distinctive theological frameworks for interpreting that experience.
