## Overview

Shaiva engagement with the Narada Bhakti Sutra recognizes the text's universal description of devotion while applying its principles to the worship of Shiva. The Shaiva bhakti tradition, expressed through the Tamil Nayanmars, the Virashaiva vachanakaras, and the Kashmir Shaiva tradition of devotional practice, shares many of the Narada Bhakti Sutra's fundamental convictions about the nature of divine love and the means of cultivating it.

## Interpretive Approach

Shaiva readers transpose the Narada Bhakti Sutra's teachings into a Shaiva key, understanding the text's references to God as pointing to Shiva-consciousness. The text's emphasis on surrender, the transcendence of worldly attachment, and the all-consuming nature of divine love resonates powerfully with the Shaiva devotional poetry of saints such as Manikkavacakar, Basavanna, and Lalleshwari.

## Points of Convergence

The Narada Bhakti Sutra's teaching that devotion transcends all distinctions of caste, learning, and social position finds strong parallels in the Shaiva devotional traditions, particularly in the Virashaiva movement's radical egalitarianism. Both traditions affirm that love of God is the supreme qualification, overriding all social and ritual categories.
