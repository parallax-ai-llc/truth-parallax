Shaiva interpretation of the fruits of devotion understands the chapter's vision of liberation through love as consonant with the Shaiva teaching of jivanmukti (liberation in this life). The perfected Shaiva devotee, like the perfected bhakta described by Narada, embodies divine qualities and radiates spiritual presence. In Kashmir Shaivism, this state is understood as the full recognition of one's identity with Shiva, expressed in and through the world rather than by withdrawal from it.

The chapter's teaching that the devotee who attains supreme love transforms the world through their presence resonates with the Shaiva understanding that the liberated person's actions become expressions of Shiva's creative freedom (svatantrya).
