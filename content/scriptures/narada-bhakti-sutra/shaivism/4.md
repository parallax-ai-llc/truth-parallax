Shaiva engagement with the forms of divine love recognizes parallels between the chapter's descriptions and the rich emotional repertoire of Shaiva devotional poetry. The Tamil Shaiva bhakti tradition describes relationships with Shiva that include servitude, friendship, parental love, and spousal devotion — a range of devotional rasas that mirrors the diversity described in the Narada Bhakti Sutra.

Manikkavacakar's Tiruvachakam and Basavanna's vachanas express forms of divine love that correspond to Narada's descriptions of devotion as all-consuming, self-transcending, and characterized by intense emotional states ranging from ecstasy to anguished longing.
