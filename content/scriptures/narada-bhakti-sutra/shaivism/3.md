Shaiva reading of the chapter on cultivating devotion draws parallels with Shaiva sadhana practices. The emphasis on sat-sanga resonates with the Shaiva tradition's veneration of the guru as the essential guide on the devotional path. The prescription of vairagya aligns with the Shaiva understanding that attachment to the phenomenal world prevents the recognition of one's Shiva-nature.

The Nayanmars' devotional practices — including constant remembrance of Shiva through the panchakshara mantra (Om Namah Shivaya) — parallel the Narada Bhakti Sutra's emphasis on continuous devotional practice as the means of cultivating divine love.
