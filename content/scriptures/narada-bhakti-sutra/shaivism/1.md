Shaiva engagement with the opening chapter transposes Narada's definition of supreme devotion into a Shaiva key, understanding the ultimate object of love as Shiva-consciousness rather than Vishnu. The chapter's description of bhakti as supreme love resonates with the Shaiva devotional poetry of the Nayanmars and the vachanakaras, who describe the devotee's total absorption in Shiva in similar terms of intoxication, wonder, and self-forgetfulness.

The Shaiva reading emphasizes that the love described in this chapter is not merely an emotion but a recognition of one's essential identity with the divine. In Kashmir Shaivism, supreme devotion is understood as the spontaneous expression of the soul's recognition (pratyabhijna) of its own Shiva-nature.
