Shaiva interpretation of the superiority of devotion affirms the chapter's central conviction while situating it within the Shaiva understanding of the relationship between bhakti, jnana, and yoga. The Shaiva Siddhanta tradition agrees that devotion is the supreme path but specifies Shiva as the proper object of devotion. Kashmir Shaivism integrates bhakti with jnana, understanding the highest devotion as the recognition of non-dual Shiva-consciousness.

The chapter's teaching that devotion transcends all distinctions of caste and learning finds powerful support in the Virashaiva tradition, which proclaimed the equality of all Shiva devotees regardless of social position.
