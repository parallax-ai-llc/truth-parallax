Vaishnava interpretation of the concluding chapter on the fruits of devotion celebrates the text's vision of eternal communion with God as the supreme spiritual attainment. The teaching that the perfected devotee becomes an embodiment of divine love, radiating peace and inspiring devotion in others, represents the Vaishnava ideal of the uttama-bhagavata (highest devotee) whose very presence sanctifies the world.

The chapter's description of liberation (moksha) through devotion is understood by Vaishnava commentators not as absorption into an impersonal absolute but as entry into the eternal lila (divine play) of God, where the devotee's individuality is preserved and perfected in an eternal relationship of love.
