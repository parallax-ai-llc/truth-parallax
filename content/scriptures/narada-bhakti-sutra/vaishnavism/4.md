Vaishnava commentary on the forms of divine love reads this chapter through the framework of rasa theology developed by Rupa Goswami and the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition. Narada's description of different modes of devotion — including the love of a servant, a friend, a parent, and a lover — is understood as outlining the five primary rasas (shanta, dasya, sakhya, vatsalya, and madhurya) that characterize the devotee's relationship with God.

The chapter's portraits of great devotees who exemplify different modes of love provide models for the Vaishnava practitioner. The teaching that devotion manifests differently in different devotees reflects the Vaishnava understanding that the devotional relationship is personal, varied, and inexhaustible.
