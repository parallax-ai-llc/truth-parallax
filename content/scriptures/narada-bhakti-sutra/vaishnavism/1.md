Vaishnava interpretation of the opening chapter treasures Narada's definition of bhakti as supreme love (parama prema rupa) for God as the foundational statement of devotional theology. The teaching that bhakti is its own reward — not a means to some other end — establishes the Vaishnava conviction that the loving relationship between the devotee and Krishna/Vishnu is the ultimate spiritual reality. Gaudiya Vaishnava commentators read this chapter through the lens of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu's teaching that the highest love is that which seeks nothing for itself but exists solely for the pleasure of the Beloved.

The chapter's description of the devotee who attains bhakti as becoming intoxicated, stunned, and absorbed in the joy of divine love resonates with the Vaishnava understanding of the transformed consciousness that characterizes the great devotees (mahabhagavatas).
