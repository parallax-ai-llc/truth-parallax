Vaishnava commentary on the superiority of devotion affirms the chapter's central teaching that bhakti transcends and fulfills all other spiritual paths. The Bhagavad Gita's own assertion that the devotee is "the most intimately united with Me in yoga" (6.47) is read as confirming Narada's teaching. Vaishnava interpreters emphasize that the text does not merely rank bhakti above karma and jnana but argues that these paths find their true fulfillment only when integrated with devotion.

The chapter's teaching that God can be attained through devotion alone, without the prerequisites demanded by other paths, establishes the universal accessibility of bhakti — a teaching of particular importance for the Vaishnava movement's democratizing vision of spiritual life.
