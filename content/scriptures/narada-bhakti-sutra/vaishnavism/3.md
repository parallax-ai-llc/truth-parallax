Vaishnava interpretation of the chapter on cultivating devotion treats its practical instructions as a concise manual for the devotional life. The emphasis on sat-sanga (company of the holy) resonates with the Vaishnava conviction that spiritual progress depends on association with advanced devotees. The teaching on vairagya (dispassion) is understood not as world-rejection but as the natural consequence of being absorbed in the superior taste of divine love.

The chapter's prescription of constant remembrance of God (smarana) aligns with the Vaishnava practice of nama-japa (chanting the divine names) as the primary devotional discipline. Vaishnava commentators connect this teaching with the practice of the maha-mantra as taught by Chaitanya Mahaprabhu.
