Advaita interpretation of the concluding chapter reads the fruits of devotion as pointing toward non-dual realization. The chapter's description of the perfected devotee as one who has transcended all worldly concerns and abides in constant communion with God is understood as a description of the jivanmukta (one liberated while living) who has realized the identity of atman and Brahman.

The teaching that the perfected devotee transforms the world through their presence reflects the Advaita understanding that the realized person's equanimity, compassion, and wisdom arise naturally from the direct knowledge of non-dual reality. Liberation through devotion and liberation through knowledge are ultimately one and the same liberation — the recognition of what was always already the case.
