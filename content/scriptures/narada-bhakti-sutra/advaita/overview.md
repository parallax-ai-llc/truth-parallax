## Overview

Advaita Vedanta's engagement with the Narada Bhakti Sutra involves a creative reinterpretation of devotion within the non-dual framework. While Shankara and later Advaita teachers affirm the value of bhakti, they typically understand it as a means to the ultimate goal of jnana (non-dual knowledge) rather than as an end in itself. The devotion described in the Narada Bhakti Sutra is read as directed toward the saguna (qualified) Brahman, which is ultimately transcended in the realization of nirguna (unqualified) Brahman.

## Interpretive Approach

Advaita interpreters read the Narada Bhakti Sutra's descriptions of supreme love as pointing toward the dissolution of the apparent distinction between devotee and God. The highest bhakti, from the Advaita perspective, culminates in the recognition that the lover and the Beloved are ultimately one. The text's emphasis on total surrender and the loss of separate selfhood is read as anticipating the Advaita realization of non-duality.

## Integration

Modern Advaita teachers, including Ramana Maharshi, have affirmed that genuine bhakti and genuine jnana are ultimately identical, converging in the direct realization of the Self. This perspective allows the Narada Bhakti Sutra's devotional teachings to be fully embraced within the Advaita framework.
