Advaita reading of the chapter on forms of divine love reinterprets the various devotional relationships as expressions of the Self's love for itself. Since Brahman alone exists, all forms of love are ultimately the Self's recognition of its own nature in apparent others. The different rasas described by Narada are understood as progressive stages of intimacy that culminate in the non-dual realization where the distinction between devotee and God dissolves.

Modern Advaita teachers such as Ramana Maharshi have affirmed that the deepest devotion and the deepest knowledge are identical: both are the Self's recognition of itself, experienced as infinite love and infinite awareness simultaneously.
