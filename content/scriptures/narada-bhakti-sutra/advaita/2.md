Advaita interpretation of the chapter on the superiority of devotion offers a nuanced reading. While accepting that bhakti is supreme, Advaita understanding ultimately identifies the highest bhakti with jnana. The devotion that surpasses knowledge is not mere emotional attachment to a personal God but the direct, intuitive apprehension of non-dual reality — which is itself the highest form of love. This reading resolves the apparent tension between bhakti and jnana paths.

The chapter's teaching that devotion transcends social distinctions is fully embraced by Advaita, which regards all differences — including the difference between devotee and God — as ultimately illusory superimpositions on the one Brahman.
