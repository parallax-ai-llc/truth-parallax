Advaita Vedanta reads the opening chapter's definition of supreme devotion through the lens of non-dual realization. While the chapter describes bhakti as supreme love for God (parama prema), Advaita interpreters understand this as the love that ultimately dissolves the distinction between lover and Beloved. The highest devotion, in the Advaita reading, is not love of a separate God but the Self's recognition of its own infinite nature, which manifests as boundless love.

Shankara's concept of para bhakti (supreme devotion) aligns bhakti with jnana, suggesting that the devotion Narada describes reaches its fulfillment in the non-dual realization that the devotee and God are not ultimately two. The emotional states described — intoxication, wonder, self-forgetfulness — are read as signs of the ego's dissolution in Brahman-consciousness.
