Advaita engagement with the chapter on cultivating devotion appreciates its practical instructions while reinterpreting them within the non-dual framework. Vairagya (dispassion) is understood as the natural consequence of discriminative knowledge (viveka), which reveals the unreality of worldly attachments. Sat-sanga is valued as association with those who have realized non-dual truth. The practice of constant remembrance of God is read as the continuous awareness of one's own true nature as Brahman.

The chapter's practical prescriptions are thus integrated into the Advaita sadhana of shravana (hearing), manana (reflection), and nididhyasana (meditation) that leads to the direct realization of non-dual truth.
