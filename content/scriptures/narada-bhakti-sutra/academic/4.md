Scholarly study of the chapter on forms of divine love examines its typology of devotional relationships in light of rasa theory and Indian aesthetic philosophy. The chapter's classification of different modes of love has been compared with the rasa theory developed by Rupa Goswami and with the aesthetic categories of Bharata's Natyashastra. Academic analysis examines how the bhakti tradition adapted aesthetic concepts to describe religious experience.

The chapter's portraits of exemplary devotees have been studied as examples of hagiographic idealization, revealing the values and aspirations of the bhakti movement. Scholars compare these portraits with similar idealization of saints and mystics in other religious traditions.
