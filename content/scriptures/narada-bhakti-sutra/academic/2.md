Scholarly study of the chapter on the superiority of devotion examines the philosophical arguments for bhakti's primacy within the broader context of Indian soteriological debates. The chapter's implicit dialogue with the jnana-marga and karma-marga traditions raises important questions about the relationship between emotion, knowledge, and action in Indian philosophical thought. Academic analysis compares these arguments with parallel discussions in the commentaries of Ramanuja, Madhva, and Vallabha.

Scholars note that the chapter's sweeping claims for bhakti's superiority may reflect a polemical context in which devotional movements sought to establish their authority against Advaita and other knowledge-centered traditions.
