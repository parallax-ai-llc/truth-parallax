Academic analysis of the opening chapter examines the philosophical content of Narada's definition of bhakti as supreme love (parama prema rupa). Scholars study the relationship between this definition and earlier treatments of devotion in the Bhagavad Gita, the Bhagavata Purana, and the Shandilya Bhakti Sutra. The chapter's phenomenology of devotional experience — including the emotional states of the perfected devotee — has been analyzed using categories from the philosophy of emotion and the psychology of religion.

Textual scholars debate the chapter's date and relationship to other bhakti texts. The attribution to the sage Narada is understood as a literary convention that lends divine authority to the teaching. The chapter's philosophical vocabulary suggests a date after the systematization of bhakti theology by the medieval acharyas.
