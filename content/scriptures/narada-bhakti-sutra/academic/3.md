Academic analysis of the chapter on cultivating devotion examines its practical prescriptions in light of the broader bhakti movement's social and religious history. The emphasis on sat-sanga (holy company) reflects the institutional structures of devotional communities, including the guru-disciple lineage, the devotional assembly (satsang), and the pilgrimage tradition. Scholars study how these prescriptions shaped the social organization of bhakti communities.

The chapter's treatment of vairagya (dispassion) and its relationship to devotion has been analyzed by scholars of Indian philosophy, who note the distinction between the Narada Bhakti Sutra's understanding of renunciation — motivated by superior taste — and the Advaita tradition's understanding — motivated by discriminative knowledge.
