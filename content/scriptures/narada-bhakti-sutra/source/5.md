---
title: "Narada Bhakti Sutra - Chapter 5"
scripture: "narada-bhakti-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Whoever believes in this auspicious teaching of Narada, whoever has faith in it, becomes endowed with love, attains the Beloved, attains the Beloved.

**2** The devotee who attains supreme love sees the Lord everywhere and in all beings.

**3** He sees no difference between one being and another, for he sees the Lord in all.

**4** He becomes free from all attachments, free from all sorrows, free from all hatred, and he rejoices in the Lord alone.

**5** He who has attained this supreme devotion transcends all dualities, all sorrows, all delusions.

**6** Such devotees, united with God, sanctify their families, their lands, and the whole world.

**7** They sanctify the holy places, they make the scriptures fruitful, they give meaning to all spiritual practices.

**8** Their ancestors rejoice, the gods dance, and the earth is blessed with a protector.

**9** There is no distinction of caste, knowledge, form, family, wealth, profession, or anything else among them.

**10** Because they belong to the Lord, they are His own.

**11** One should not engage in disputation, for there are many differences of opinion and no conclusion can be reached by mere argument.

**12** The doctrines of Bhakti taught by Vyasa, Garga, Shandilya, and other great teachers should be studied and practiced.

**13** He who has faith in this teaching of Narada, who has devotion to the Lord, and who takes refuge in Him — he, indeed, he alone, attains the supreme goal.

**14** Thus has the sage Narada, after destroying the sins of the Kali age, taught the means of crossing over this ocean of worldly existence.

**15** He who studies and practices this Bhakti Sutra is freed from bondage and attains the supreme abode of the Lord.

**16** Thus ends the Narada Bhakti Sutra.
