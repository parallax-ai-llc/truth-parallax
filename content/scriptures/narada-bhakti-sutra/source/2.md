---
title: "Narada Bhakti Sutra - Chapter 2"
scripture: "narada-bhakti-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** As in the case of a palace, of food, and the like — the existence of a thing is known from direct evidence.

**2** The evidence is: one attains peace and supreme joy.

**3** Having attained that, one has no reason to grieve, no reason to hate, no reason to rejoice in sense pleasures, no reason to be eager for anything.

**4** Knowing which, a person becomes as if intoxicated, as if stunned, as if he rejoices in the Atman.

**5** That devotion is not desire-prompted, for it is of the nature of restraint.

**6** Restraint is the consecration of all activities, both social and religious, to the Lord.

**7** There should be exclusive devotion to Him alone, and indifference to everything that is antagonistic to Him.

**8** Exclusive devotion means giving up other refuges and taking refuge in Him alone.

**9** Indifference to what is antagonistic means: performing only those social and religious duties which are favorable to devotion.

**10** One should follow scriptural injunctions even after the dawn of firm faith, lest there be a fall.

**11** Social formalities should also be observed, but only such as are essential; the rest may be left aside.

**12** The authoritative texts on Bhakti should be studied, and the actions enjoined therein should be carried out.

**13** One should give up the objects of pleasure and grief, gain and loss, and similar dualities.

**14** Even a moment should not be wasted without devotion, renouncing all desires.

**15** Ahimsa, truth, purity, compassion, and faith — these should be practiced always.

**16** At all times and in every way, the Lord alone should be worshipped, free from anxiety.

**17** He alone, being praised, quickly manifests Himself and grants direct experience.

**18** The three-fold truth, past, present, and future, is Love alone.
