---
title: "Narada Bhakti Sutra - Chapter 4"
scripture: "narada-bhakti-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** He crosses Maya, he crosses Maya — he who gives up attachment, who serves the great souls, who is free from the sense of "I" and "mine," who attains the company of the Lord.

**2** Who indeed can know the greatness of Bhakti? It is its own proof.

**3** There is no difference of caste, learning, beauty, birth, wealth, or occupation for the devotees of the Lord; for they are His own.

**4** One should not engage in arguments and disputations.

**5** Because there is scope for endless variety in such matters, and mere reasoning cannot lead to finality.

**6** One should study the scriptures relating to devotion and perform the actions prescribed therein.

**7** Free from pleasure, pain, desire, gain, and the like, one should not waste even half a moment, but should utilize every moment in devotion.

**8** One should practice non-violence, truthfulness, purity, compassion, faith, and other such virtues.

**9** Constantly and with all one's heart, the Lord alone should be worshipped, without any anxiety.

**10** When thus glorified, the Lord quickly manifests Himself and grants the devotee direct experience.

**11** In the three forms of truth — past, present, and future — love alone is the supreme means.

**12** Though one in its essence, love for God becomes manifest in eleven forms.

**13** Love of the glory of the Lord's qualities, love of His beauty, love of worship, love of constant remembrance, love of service, love as a friend, love as of a parent, love as of a beloved, love of complete self-surrender, love of total absorption, and love of the pain of separation.

**14** Thus declare the teachers of devotion, without any difference of opinion.
