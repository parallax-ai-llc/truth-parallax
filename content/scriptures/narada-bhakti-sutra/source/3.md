---
title: "Narada Bhakti Sutra - Chapter 3"
scripture: "narada-bhakti-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** That love is of the nature of the highest peace and of the nature of supreme bliss.

**2** It involves no loss and is superior to all other attainments.

**3** On attaining it, one sees only love, hears only love, speaks only of love, and thinks only of love.

**4** Secondary devotion is of three kinds, according to the qualities: Sattvic, Rajasic, and Tamasic; or according to the nature of the aspirant: the afflicted, the seeker of knowledge, the seeker of wealth, and the man of wisdom.

**5** Each succeeding type is superior to the preceding.

**6** Bhakti is the easiest path, compared with all other means of spiritual attainment.

**7** It requires no other proof than its own nature; it is its own proof, being of the nature of peace and supreme bliss.

**8** Yet one should not be heedless, for the obstacles on the path are many.

**9** The company of the great ones is difficult to obtain, difficult to comprehend, but infallible in its effect.

**10** But it is obtained only through the grace of God, for there is no difference between God and His devotees.

**11** One should strive for the company of the holy, indeed, one should strive for the company of the holy.

**12** One must shun evil company by all means.

**13** Because it gives rise to lust, anger, delusion, loss of memory, loss of intellect, and total ruin.

**14** These evils, beginning as ripples, swell into a sea of misery.

**15** Who crosses Maya? He who gives up evil company, who serves the great ones, who is free from the sense of "mine."

**16** Who frequents a solitary place, who cuts the bondage of worldliness, who goes beyond the three gunas, who renounces the desire for acquisition and preservation.

**17** Who renounces the fruit of actions, who renounces actions themselves, and thus transcends the dualities.

**18** Who renounces even the Vedas, and who obtains the uninterrupted flow of love toward God.
