---
title: "Narada Bhakti Sutra - Chapter 1"
scripture: "narada-bhakti-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Now, therefore, we shall expound Bhakti, or Divine Love.

**2** It is of the nature of supreme love towards God.

**3** And it is of the nature of nectar, of immortality.

**4** On attaining which a man becomes perfect, becomes immortal, becomes satisfied.

**5** On attaining which, he desires nothing, grieves not, hates not, does not revel in sense objects, does not become excited by enthusiasm for worldly things.

**6** Knowing which, he becomes intoxicated, becomes still, and rejoices in the Atman alone.

**7** It is not of the nature of lust, because it is of the form of renunciation.

**8** Renunciation is the giving up of all activities, both secular and scriptural.

**9** It consists in dedicating all activities to Him and feeling extreme anguish in forgetting Him.

**10** There are, indeed, examples of such exclusive devotion.

**11** As the Gopis of Vraja had toward Krishna.

**12** Let it not be thought that their love was mere lustful passion, for that is denied.

**13** It would be a denial of the divine nature of their love to call it lust.

**14** Bhakti is superior to karma, jnana, and yoga.

**15** Because it is its own fruit; because the Lord is averse to the proud and is pleased with the humble.

**16** Knowledge of Bhakti is alone sufficient for attaining it, according to some.

**17** Knowledge and Bhakti are mutually dependent, according to others.

**18** But according to the son of Brahma, Bhakti is its own means of accomplishment.
