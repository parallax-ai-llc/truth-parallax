Dvaita interpretation of the concluding chapter on the fruits of devotion celebrates the vision of eternal communion with God as the soul's highest destiny. Unlike the Advaita reading, which sees liberation as the dissolution of the devotee's separate identity, the Dvaita understanding affirms that the perfected devotee retains individual consciousness and enjoys an eternal relationship of love with the Supreme Person.

The chapter's teaching that the perfected devotee transforms the world through their presence is understood as describing the radiating influence of divine grace working through a soul that has become a pure instrument of God's will. Liberation in the Dvaita framework is not absorption but perfection: the soul becomes fully itself in the bliss of eternal devotion.
