## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta provides perhaps the most natural interpretive framework for the Narada Bhakti Sutra, since both affirm the eternal and real distinction between God and the devotee and the supremacy of loving devotion as the means to liberation. Madhvacharya's theology of eternal difference (pancha-bheda) supports the Narada Bhakti Sutra's vision of an everlasting devotional relationship between the soul and God.

## Interpretive Approach

Dvaita interpretation affirms the Narada Bhakti Sutra's definition of bhakti as supreme love (parama prema) without reinterpreting it as a stage on the way to non-dual knowledge. For Dvaita, the devotional relationship described in the text is the ultimate spiritual reality: the soul's love for God is eternal, and liberation consists not in the dissolution of this relationship but in its perfection. The text's descriptions of the devotee's total absorption in God are understood as describing the highest possible spiritual attainment.

## Devotional Practice

The Narada Bhakti Sutra's practical instructions — on holy company, remembrance of God, and the cultivation of virtues conducive to devotion — align closely with the devotional practices prescribed in the Dvaita tradition, providing a concise manual for the cultivation of the devotional life.
