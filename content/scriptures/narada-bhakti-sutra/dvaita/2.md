Dvaita interpretation of the superiority of devotion affirms the chapter's central teaching without qualification. Since Madhva's metaphysics posits an eternal distinction between God and the soul, bhakti — understood as the soul's loving relationship with God — is not a preliminary stage to be transcended but the ultimate spiritual reality. The soul's love for God is not dissolved in non-dual realization but perfected in eternal devotion.

The chapter's argument that devotion surpasses knowledge is read by Dvaita commentators as confirming the primacy of the will and the emotions in spiritual life. Knowledge of God is necessary but not sufficient; love of God, which engages the whole person, is the supreme attainment.
