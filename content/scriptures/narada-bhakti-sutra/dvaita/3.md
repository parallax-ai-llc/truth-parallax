Dvaita engagement with the chapter on cultivating devotion treats its practical instructions as essential elements of the devotional life. The emphasis on sat-sanga aligns with the Dvaita tradition's veneration of the guru lineage as the primary means of spiritual transmission. Vairagya is understood as the natural consequence of tasting the superior bliss of devotion, which makes worldly pleasures pale by comparison.

The chapter's teaching on constant remembrance of God resonates with the Dvaita practice of namasmarana (remembrance of God's names) and the daily devotional disciplines prescribed in the Madhva tradition.
