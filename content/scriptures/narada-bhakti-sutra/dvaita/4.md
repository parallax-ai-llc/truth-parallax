Dvaita commentary on the forms of divine love affirms the eternal diversity of devotional relationships as reflecting the real diversity of souls and their unique relationships with God. Madhva's doctrine of the inherent hierarchy among souls (svarupa-bheda) supports the teaching that different devotees relate to God in different ways according to their innate spiritual nature.

The chapter's portraits of great devotees are read as descriptions of souls who have realized their particular devotional relationship with God to its fullest extent. The diversity of devotional forms is not a deficiency but a reflection of the infinite richness of God's personality and the unlimited ways in which He can be loved.
