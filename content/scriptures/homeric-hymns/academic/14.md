Homeric Hymn 14, addressed to Mother of the Gods, is a shorter hymnic invocation that has attracted significant scholarly attention in the fields of classical philology, Greek religion, and oral poetry studies. Academic analysis examines the hymn's language and formulaic composition in relation to the broader Homeric tradition, its dating and possible geographical origin, and its evidence for ancient Greek religious belief and practice.

Scholars have studied the hymn's relationship to other representations of Mother of the Gods in Greek literature and art, its performance context at religious festivals, and its theological content. The hymn contributes to our understanding of how the ancient Greeks conceptualized Mother of the Gods's nature, powers, and relationship to both the divine order and human communities.
