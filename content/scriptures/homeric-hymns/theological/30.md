As a text composed for performance at religious festivals, the hymn functions as an act of worship — invoking Earth, celebrating the deity's greatness, and establishing the mythological foundations for ongoing devotion.

Theologically, the hymn presents Earth with distinctive attributes and domains, locating the deity within the broader structure of the Greek pantheon and the cosmic order. The hymn's narrative elements — accounts of Earth's origins, deeds, and relationships — serve as theological statements about the nature of divine power and its manifestation in the world.
