Homeric Hymn 31, addressed to Helios, is a shorter hymnic invocation that serves as a primary theological document for understanding ancient Greek conceptions of Helios's nature, powers, and role within the divine order. As a text composed for performance at religious festivals, the hymn functions as an act of worship — invoking Helios, celebrating the deity's greatness, and establishing the mythological foundations for ongoing devotion.

Theologically, the hymn presents Helios with distinctive attributes and domains, locating the deity within the broader structure of the Greek pantheon and the cosmic order. The hymn's narrative elements — accounts of Helios's origins, deeds, and relationships — serve as theological statements about the nature of divine power and its manifestation in the world.
