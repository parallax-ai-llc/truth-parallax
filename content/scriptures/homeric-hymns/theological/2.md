Homeric Hymn 2, addressed to Demeter, is one of the four major Homeric Hymns, an extended narrative poem that serves as a primary theological document for understanding ancient Greek conceptions of Demeter's nature, powers, and role within the divine order. As a text composed for performance at religious festivals, the hymn functions as an act of worship — invoking Demeter, celebrating the deity's greatness, and establishing the mythological foundations for ongoing devotion.

Theologically, the hymn presents Demeter with distinctive attributes and domains, locating the deity within the broader structure of the Greek pantheon and the cosmic order. The hymn's narrative elements — accounts of Demeter's origins, deeds, and relationships — serve as theological statements about the nature of divine power and its manifestation in the world.
