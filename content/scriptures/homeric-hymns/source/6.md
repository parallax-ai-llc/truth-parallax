**Hymn 6: To Aphrodite II**

**1** I will sing of stately Aphrodite, gold-crowned and beautiful, whose dominion is the walled cities of all sea-set Cyprus. There the moist breath of the western wind wafted her over the waves of the loud-moaning sea in soft foam, and there the gold-filleted Hours welcomed her joyously.

**2** They clothed her with heavenly garments: on her head they put a fine, well-wrought crown of gold, and in her pierced ears they hung ornaments of orichalc and precious gold.

**3** Hail, goddess, queen of well-built Salamis and sea-girt Cyprus; grant me a cheerful song.
