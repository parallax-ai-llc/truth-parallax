**Hymn V: To Aphrodite**

**1** Muse, tell me the deeds of golden Aphrodite the Cyprian, who stirs up sweet passion in the gods and subdues the tribes of mortal men and birds that fly in air and all the many creatures that the dry land rears, and all that the sea.

**2** All these love the deeds of rich-crowned Cytherea.

**3** Yet there are three hearts that she cannot bend nor yet ensnare. First is the daughter of Zeus who holds the aegis, bright-eyed Athene; for she has no pleasure in the deeds of golden Aphrodite, but delights in wars and in the work of Ares.

**4** Nor does laughter-loving Aphrodite ever tame in love Artemis, the huntress with shafts of gold; for she loves archery and the slaying of wild beasts in the mountains.

**5** Nor yet does the pure maiden Hestia love Aphrodite's works. She was the first-born child of wily Cronos and youngest too, by will of Zeus who holds the aegis.

**6** But upon all others she has power, and there is none who escapes Aphrodite. Even into the heart of Zeus himself she put sweet desire, Zeus who delights in the thunderbolt, greatest and mightiest of all.

**7** Yet even his wise heart she beguiles at her pleasure, and mates him with mortal women, hidden from Hera. And Zeus put in her heart sweet desire for Anchises, who at that time among the steep hills of many-fountained Ida was tending cattle, and in shape was like the immortals.
