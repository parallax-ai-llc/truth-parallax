**Hymn 33: To the Dioscuri II**

**1** Bright-eyed Muses, tell of the Tyndaridae, the Sons of Zeus, glorious children of neat-ankled Leda, Castor the tamer of horses, and blameless Polydeuces.

**2** When Leda had lain with the dark-clouded Son of Cronos, she bare them beneath the peak of the great hill Taygetus—children who are deliverers of men on earth and of swift-going ships when stormy gales rage over the ruthless sea.

**3** Then the men in the ship call upon the sons of great Zeus with vows of white lambs, going to the highest deck. And the strong wind and the waves of the sea lay the ship under water, but suddenly these two are seen darting through the air on tawny wings.

**4** Forthwith they still the blasts of the cruel winds and calm the waves upon the surface of the white sea: fair signs are they and deliverance from toil. And when the shipmen see them they are glad and have rest from their pain and labour.

**5** Hail, Tyndaridae, riders of swift horses! Now I will remember you and another song also.
