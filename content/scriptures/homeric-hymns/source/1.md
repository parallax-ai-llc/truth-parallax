**Hymn I: To Dionysus**

[The opening of this hymn is lost. Only fragments survive.]

**1** For some say, at Dracanum; and some, on windy Icarus; and some, in Naxos, O Heaven-born, Insewn;

**2** and others by the deep-eddying river Alpheus that pregnant Semele bare you to Zeus the thunder-lover.

**3** And others yet, lord, say you were born in Thebes; but all these lie. The Father of men and gods gave you birth remote from men and secretly from white-armed Hera.

**4** There is a certain Nysa, a mountain most high, blooming with forests, far off in Phoenice, near the streams of Aegyptus.

**5** Hail, child of fair-faced Semele! He who forgets you can in no wise order sweet song.
