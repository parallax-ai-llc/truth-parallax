Homeric Hymn 31, addressed to Helios, is a shorter hymnic invocation that offers valuable material for comparative religious and literary analysis. The hymn's celebration of Helios's attributes and mythological deeds connects to hymnic and mythological traditions across cultures, revealing both shared patterns in how human communities celebrate the divine and the distinctive features of the Greek religious imagination.

Comparative analysis situates the hymn within a broad cross-cultural context: the invocation of Helios and the celebration of the deity's powers parallels devotional practices in Vedic, Mesopotamian, Egyptian, and other ancient traditions. The specific mythological themes associated with Helios — divine birth, establishment of cult, interaction with mortals — resonate with analogous narratives worldwide.
