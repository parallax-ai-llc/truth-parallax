Homeric Hymn 22, addressed to Poseidon, is a shorter hymnic invocation that offers valuable material for comparative religious and literary analysis. The hymn's celebration of Poseidon's attributes and mythological deeds connects to hymnic and mythological traditions across cultures, revealing both shared patterns in how human communities celebrate the divine and the distinctive features of the Greek religious imagination.
