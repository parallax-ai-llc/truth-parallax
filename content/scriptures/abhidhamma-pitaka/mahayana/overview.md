## Overview

Mahayana Buddhism engages with the Abhidharma tradition through the Sarvastivada Abhidharma (preserved in Chinese translation), which provided the philosophical foundation for Mahayana scholastic thought. Vasubandhu's Abhidharmakosa synthesized Sarvastivada analysis and served as a bridge to the Yogachara school.

## Key Themes

Mahayana philosophers both build upon and critique the Abhidharma tradition. The Madhyamaka school critiques the Abhidharma's claim to identify ultimate realities, arguing that even dharmas are empty. The Yogachara school develops the Abhidharma's analysis of consciousness into the eight-consciousness model.

## Relevance to Mahayana

The Abhidharma tradition provides the analytical vocabulary and philosophical rigor that Mahayana philosophy presupposes, even when it transforms or transcends Abhidharma conclusions.
