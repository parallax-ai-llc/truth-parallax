## Overview

The Abhidhamma Pitaka holds a position of supreme importance in the Theravada scholastic tradition. It is regarded as the Buddha's highest teaching -- a systematic analysis of reality in terms of ultimate truths (paramattha dhamma) rather than the conventional language used in the suttas.

## Key Themes

Theravada Abhidhamma study centers on the analysis of consciousness into 89 (or 121) types, the classification of 52 mental factors, and the 28 types of material phenomena. The Patthana's analysis of 24 modes of conditionality is considered the crown jewel of Buddhist philosophy.

## Practice and Study

In Myanmar, the Abhidhamma tradition is particularly strong, with extensive study of the Dhammasangani and commentaries. Abhidhamma analysis informs vipassana meditation practice by providing the detailed map of mental and material phenomena that the meditator observes.
