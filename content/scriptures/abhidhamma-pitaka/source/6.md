---
title: "Abhidhamma Pitaka Section 6: Yamaka (Book of Pairs)"
scripture: "abhidhamma-pitaka"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Roots: Are all wholesome states wholesome roots? No. But all wholesome roots are wholesome states. Are all unwholesome states unwholesome roots? No. But all unwholesome roots are unwholesome states.

**2** The three wholesome roots are: non-greed, non-hatred, and non-delusion. The three unwholesome roots are: greed, hatred, and delusion.

**3** Aggregates: Does the aggregate of material form arise for one who is arising? For one who is being reborn into the five-aggregate plane, the aggregate of material form arises. But for one being reborn into the four-aggregate plane, the aggregate of material form does not arise.

**4** Does the aggregate of consciousness cease for one who is ceasing? For one who is dying, the aggregate of consciousness ceases. But this does not mean the aggregate of consciousness is annihilated; it is succeeded by a new consciousness conditioned by the preceding one.

**5** Sense-bases: Is the eye-base impermanent? Yes. Is everything impermanent the eye-base? No. The eye-base is impermanent, but not all impermanent things are the eye-base. This applies to all specific sense-bases.

**6** Elements: Is the eye-element suffering? Yes, insofar as it is conditioned and impermanent. Is everything that is suffering the eye-element? No. All conditioned things are suffering, but not all conditioned things are the eye-element.

**7** Truths: Is suffering the truth of suffering? Yes. Is the truth of suffering, suffering? Yes. But is the origin of suffering, suffering? The origin of suffering — craving — is itself conditioned and therefore also suffering, yet it is classified as the truth of the origin, not the truth of suffering.

**8** Formations: Does one who has wholesome formations arising also have unwholesome formations arising? At one and the same moment, no. One consciousness-moment has either wholesome or unwholesome formations, not both simultaneously.

**9** Latent tendencies: Does one in whom the latent tendency to sensual lust underlies, also have the latent tendency to aversion underlying? Not necessarily. The latent tendency to sensual lust and the latent tendency to aversion operate in different feeling-contexts: sensual lust underlies pleasant feeling, aversion underlies painful feeling.

**10** Through the method of paired questions — affirmative and negative, converse and inverse — the precise meaning of the terms of the Dhamma is clarified, removing ambiguity, and establishing the exact scope and limits of each category.
