---
title: "Abhidhamma Pitaka Section 1: Dhammasangani (Enumeration of Phenomena)"
scripture: "abhidhamma-pitaka"
chapter: 1
---

**1** What are wholesome states? On an occasion when a thought of the sensuous plane has arisen, accompanied by gladness, connected with knowledge, having as its object a sight, a sound, an odour, a taste, something tangible, or a mental state — on that occasion there is contact, feeling, perception, volition, consciousness.

**2** Therein what is contact? That which is contact, touching, the state of having touched — this is contact.

**3** Therein what is feeling? That which is mental pleasure, mental gladness, the pleasant, the glad experience born of mind-contact — this is feeling.

**4** Therein what is perception? That which is perception, recognizing, the state of having recognized — this is perception.

**5** Therein what is volition? That which is volition, intending, the state of having intended — this is volition.

**6** Therein what is consciousness? That which is mind, mentality, heart, that which is lucid, the mind as sense-base, the mind as faculty, consciousness, the aggregate of consciousness — this is consciousness.

**7** What are unwholesome states? On an occasion when an unwholesome thought of the sensuous plane has arisen, accompanied by displeasure, connected with aversion — on that occasion there is contact, feeling, perception, volition, consciousness, and aversion.

**8** Therein what is aversion? That which is dislike, displeasure, disaffection, ill-will, hostility, harshness, exasperation of the mind — this is aversion.

**9** What are indeterminate states? On an occasion when a thought of the sensuous plane has arisen which is neither wholesome nor unwholesome but indeterminate, a mere reaction to a visible object which has impinged on the eye — on that occasion there is contact, feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness.

**10** These are called indeterminate states: the resultant consciousness of the sensuous plane, neither wholesome nor unwholesome, neither meritorious nor demeritorious.
