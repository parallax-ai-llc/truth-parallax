---
title: "Abhidhamma Pitaka Section 4: Puggalapannatti (Description of Persons)"
scripture: "abhidhamma-pitaka"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Six types of persons are described: the person who is liable to the downward course, the person who is not liable to the downward course, the person who is certain of deliverance, the person who is uncertain, the Stream-enterer, and the Arahat.

**2** What person is liable to the downward course? Here a certain person is possessed of those states, the possession of which makes a person liable to the downward course. This person is called one liable to the downward course.

**3** What person is a Stream-enterer? Here a certain person has abandoned the three fetters: identity view, doubt, and distorted grasp of rules and vows. This person is called a Stream-enterer, no longer liable to the downward course, certain of deliverance, destined for full awakening.

**4** What person is a Once-returner? Here a certain person has abandoned the three fetters, and has attenuated lust, hatred, and delusion. This person is called a Once-returner, who, having come back to this world once more, will make an end of suffering.

**5** What person is a Non-returner? Here a certain person has abandoned the five lower fetters: identity view, doubt, distorted grasp of rules and vows, sensual desire, and ill will. This person is called a Non-returner, who, arising spontaneously in the Pure Abodes, will attain Nibbana there without returning from that world.

**6** What person is an Arahat? Here a certain person, by the destruction of the taints, in this very life enters upon and abides in the taintless deliverance of mind, the deliverance by wisdom, having realized it for himself with direct knowledge. This person is called an Arahat.

**7** What person practises for his own welfare but not for the welfare of others? Here a certain person practises for the subduing of his own lust, hatred, and delusion, but does not encourage others in the subduing of their lust, hatred, and delusion.

**8** What person practises for the welfare of others but not for his own welfare? Here a certain person encourages others in the subduing of their lust, hatred, and delusion, but does not practise for the subduing of his own lust, hatred, and delusion.

**9** What person practises neither for his own welfare nor for the welfare of others? Here a certain person neither practises for the subduing of his own lust, hatred, and delusion, nor encourages others in the same. This person is called the lowest among persons.

**10** What person practises both for his own welfare and for the welfare of others? Here a certain person both practises for the subduing of his own lust, hatred, and delusion, and encourages others in the same. This person is called the highest, the best, the foremost, the supreme, the chief among these four persons.
