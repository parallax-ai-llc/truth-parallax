---
title: "Abhidhamma Pitaka Section 5: Kathavatthu (Points of Controversy)"
scripture: "abhidhamma-pitaka"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Is the person known in the sense of a real and ultimate fact? The Personalist says yes. The Theravadin asks: Is the person known in the same way as a real and ultimate fact is known? If so, the person would be as real as Nibbana. But this cannot be maintained.

**2** The Personalist argues: If there were no person, who is it that realizes Nibbana? Who is it that passes from this world to the next? The Theravadin replies: It is the continuity of mental and material phenomena that accounts for the conventional use of the word "person."

**3** Does the soul exist? Some say: "The soul is a real thing." The Theravadin replies: Is the soul conditioned or unconditioned? If conditioned, it is impermanent. If unconditioned, it cannot perform actions or experience their results.

**4** Is everything impermanent? The Theravadin maintains: All conditioned things are impermanent. Whatever is conditioned, whatever is compounded, has the nature of arising and passing away.

**5** Does an Arahat fall away from Arahatship? Some assert that an Arahat can fall away. The Theravadin maintains: An Arahat does not fall away. One who has destroyed the taints has done so completely and irreversibly.

**6** Is there an intermediate state between death and rebirth? Some maintain there is an intermediate existence. The Theravadin holds that there is no intermediate existence; rebirth-consciousness arises immediately upon the cessation of the death-consciousness.

**7** Is everything produced by past karma? Some say that all experience is the result of past action. The Theravadin maintains: Not all experience is due to past karma. Some feelings arise through the change of seasons, through irregular behavior, through external violence, and through the maturation of karma.

**8** Can a worldling abandon the fetters? The Theravadin maintains: A worldling cannot abandon any of the fetters completely. Only the Noble Ones abandon fetters, each according to their attainment.

**9** Do the four elements exist in the formless realm? Some maintain that material form exists even in the formless realm. The Theravadin holds: In the formless realm there is no material form; that is precisely what defines it as formless.

**10** These and many other points of controversy were settled through careful analysis of the Dhamma, distinguishing what is real and ultimate from what is merely conventional designation, and what is in accordance with the teaching of the Blessed One from what deviates from it.
