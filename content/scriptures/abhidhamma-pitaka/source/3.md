---
title: "Abhidhamma Pitaka Section 3: Dhatukatha (Discussion of Elements)"
scripture: "abhidhamma-pitaka"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Are the aggregates included in the sense-bases? Are the aggregates included in the elements? Some aggregates are included in some sense-bases, and some aggregates are not included in some sense-bases.

**2** The aggregate of material form is included in eleven sense-bases: the eye-base, the ear-base, the nose-base, the tongue-base, the body-base, the visible-form-base, the sound-base, the odour-base, the taste-base, the tangible-base, and a portion of the mental-object-base.

**3** The aggregate of feeling, the aggregate of perception, and the aggregate of mental formations are included in one sense-base: a portion of the mental-object-base.

**4** The aggregate of consciousness is included in two sense-bases: the mind-base and a portion of the mental-object-base.

**5** Are the sense-bases included in the aggregates? The eye-base, the ear-base, the nose-base, the tongue-base, the body-base, the visible-form-base, the sound-base, the odour-base, the taste-base, and the tangible-base — these ten sense-bases are included in one aggregate: the aggregate of material form.

**6** The mind-base is included in one aggregate: the aggregate of consciousness. The mental-object-base is included in all five aggregates, or in one, according to the state concerned.

**7** Are the elements included in the aggregates? The eye-element, the ear-element, the nose-element, the tongue-element, the body-element, the visible-form-element, the sound-element, the odour-element, the taste-element, and the tangible-element — these ten elements are included in the aggregate of material form.

**8** The eye-consciousness-element, the ear-consciousness-element, the nose-consciousness-element, the tongue-consciousness-element, the body-consciousness-element, the mind-element, and the mind-consciousness-element — these seven elements are included in the aggregate of consciousness.

**9** The mental-object-element is included in three aggregates: the aggregate of feeling, the aggregate of perception, and the aggregate of mental formations, and also in a portion of the aggregate of material form.

**10** Thus the aggregates, sense-bases, and elements are mutually inclusive according to their nature, and by understanding their relations, one comes to comprehend the totality of conditioned phenomena.
