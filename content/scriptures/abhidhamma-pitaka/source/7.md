---
title: "Abhidhamma Pitaka Section 7: Patthana (Book of Conditional Relations)"
scripture: "abhidhamma-pitaka"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Root condition: A wholesome root is related to the states associated with it and to the material form produced thereby by root condition. Greed is related to the states associated with greed and to the material form produced by greed-rooted consciousness, by root condition.

**2** Object condition: Visible form is related to eye-consciousness by object condition. Sound is related to ear-consciousness by object condition. All objects of the six senses are related to their respective consciousnesses by object condition.

**3** Predominance condition: Desire-to-act, consciousness, energy, and investigation — these four, when predominant, are related to their associated states and to the material form produced thereby by predominance condition.

**4** Proximity condition: Each preceding state of consciousness is related to the immediately following state of consciousness by proximity condition. The death-consciousness is related to the rebirth-consciousness by proximity condition.

**5** Contiguity condition: Each preceding state of consciousness, which has just ceased, is related to the state of consciousness immediately following by contiguity condition. This is similar to proximity condition but emphasizes the absence of any intervening state.

**6** Co-nascence condition: The four mental aggregates are mutually related to one another by co-nascence condition. The four great elements are mutually related to one another by co-nascence condition. At the moment of rebirth, mind and matter arise together by co-nascence condition.

**7** Mutuality condition: The four mental aggregates are mutually related to one another by mutuality condition. The four great elements are mutually related to one another by mutuality condition. At the moment of rebirth, mind and material form are mutually related by mutuality condition.

**8** Support condition: The four mental aggregates are mutually related to one another by support condition. The four great elements are mutually related to one another by support condition. The eye-base is related to eye-consciousness by support condition. The heart-base is related to mind-element and mind-consciousness-element by support condition.

**9** Decisive support condition: Faith is related to the undertaking of precepts, to the practice of giving, and to the development of meditation by decisive support condition. Wisdom is related to the arising of insight and to the attainment of the paths by decisive support condition.

**10** Kamma condition: Wholesome and unwholesome volitions are related to their resultant aggregates and to kamma-born material form by kamma condition. The twenty-four conditional relations in their entirety constitute the complete system of conditionality, through which all phenomena arise, persist, and cease in dependence upon conditions, with nothing existing independently or in isolation.
