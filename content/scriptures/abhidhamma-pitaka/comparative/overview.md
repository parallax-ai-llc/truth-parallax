## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Abhidhamma Pitaka reveals it as one of the world's great traditions of systematic philosophical analysis. Its detailed treatment of consciousness and mental phenomena invites comparison with Western philosophy of mind, Indian philosophical traditions, and contemporary cognitive science.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Philosophy of Mind

The Abhidhamma's analysis of consciousness can be compared with Aristotelian psychology, Cartesian dualism, phenomenology (Husserl), and process philosophy (Whitehead). The Buddhist analysis of mental events as momentary processes particularly resonates with process philosophy.

### Classification Systems

The Abhidhamma's taxonomic approach parallels Aristotelian categories, Vaisheshika padarthas, and modern scientific classification, revealing universal patterns in how humans organize knowledge about reality.
