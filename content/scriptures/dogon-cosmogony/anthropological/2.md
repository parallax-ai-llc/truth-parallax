Section 2 of the Dogon Cosmogony — The Nommo — is examined by anthropological scholarship within the broader context of the social, cultural, and ritual life of the communities that produced and maintain this tradition. Anthropological analysis draws on ethnographic fieldwork, comparative social analysis, and theoretical frameworks to understand how the content of this section functions within living communities.

Key anthropological questions include the relationship between this section's content and social organization, the role of specialist practitioners in transmitting and interpreting this material, the integration of cosmological knowledge with practical social life, and the dynamics of cultural continuity and change as they affect the tradition represented in the Dogon Cosmogony.
