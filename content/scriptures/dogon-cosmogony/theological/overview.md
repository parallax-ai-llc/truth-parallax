## Overview

Theological engagement with the Dogon Cosmogony approaches the text through the lens of theological reflection on the text's religious and philosophical content. This interpretive tradition emphasizes cosmogony, the nature of the divine, the purpose of human existence, sacrifice, and cosmic order.

Theological analysis of the Dogon Cosmogony engages with the text's profound religious and philosophical content on its own terms, treating it as a serious expression of theological reflection deserving of the same careful attention given to the scriptures of the world's major religions. This approach examines the text's understanding of the divine, the cosmos, humanity, and the relationships among them.

Key theological themes in the Dogon Cosmogony include the nature of divine creativity, the role of sacrifice in sustaining cosmic order, the relationship between the divine and the natural world, the purpose of human existence, and the means by which humans can align themselves with divine intention and cosmic harmony.
