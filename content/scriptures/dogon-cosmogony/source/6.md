---
title: "The Ordering of Society"
scripture: "dogon-cosmogony"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The Nommo established the order of Dogon society. The Hogon, the spiritual leader, sits at the center, as Amma sits at the center of creation.

**2** The village is built in the shape of a human body. The smithy is at the head, the family houses are the chest, the altars are the hands, and the shrines of the ancestors are the feet.

**3** The granary is built in the shape of the cosmic egg, with eight compartments. In each compartment is stored one of the eight seeds that the Nommo brought from heaven.

**4** The smithy is a sacred place, for the smith works with fire and iron, the two great transforming forces. The smith is both feared and respected, for he holds the power of transformation.

**5** The seasons of planting and harvest follow the cycle of the star Sigi Tolo. Every fifty years, when Po Tolo completes its orbit, the great Sigui ceremony is held, renewing the bond between the Dogon and the Nommo.

**6** Thus every aspect of Dogon life reflects the cosmic order established by Amma and the Nommo. The village is a microcosm of the universe, and every action of daily life is a participation in the work of creation.
