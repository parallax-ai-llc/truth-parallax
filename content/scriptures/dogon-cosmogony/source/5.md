---
title: "The Word and Creation"
scripture: "dogon-cosmogony"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The first word was water. The Nommo is the master of water, and through water all life comes into being. Without water there is no life, no speech, no civilization.

**2** The second word was woven into cloth. The Nommo taught the ancestors to weave, and in the weaving, the word was given material form. The pattern of the cloth is the pattern of creation.

**3** The third word was spoken by the drum. The sound of the drum carries the word across distances, through the air, to the ears of those who understand.

**4** Speech and water are one. The moisture of the mouth carries the word. The river carries the word. The rain carries the word. All things that are moist carry the word of Amma.

**5** The word is the seed of creation. Amma spoke, and the universe came into being. The Nommo spoke, and life came into being. The ancestors spoke, and civilization came into being.

**6** Therefore the Dogon honor the word above all things. A word once spoken cannot be taken back. A promise once made must be kept. The word is sacred, for it is the instrument of creation.
