---
title: "The Rebellion of Ogo"
scripture: "dogon-cosmogony"
chapter: 2
---

**1** One of the male twins, Ogo, grew impatient in the cosmic egg. He wished to create before Amma had finished the work of creation.

**2** Ogo tore away a piece of the cosmic egg and descended with it into the void. From this torn piece he attempted to create a world, but because he acted alone, without his twin, the creation was imperfect.

**3** The piece of the egg became the earth, but it was a barren, dry earth, without the moisture of life. Ogo had created disorder where Amma intended order.

**4** Ogo searched for his female twin, but she had been given to the other pair. In his desperation, Ogo committed an act of impurity against the earth.

**5** Amma was angered by Ogo's rebellion. He transformed Ogo into the Pale Fox, Yurugu, who would forever wander the dry earth, seeking his lost twin, never finding peace.

**6** The Pale Fox became the symbol of disorder, of the incomplete, of that which acts without the proper complement. The priests read the tracks of the fox to divine the future.
