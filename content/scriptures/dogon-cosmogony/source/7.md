---
title: "The Cosmic Cycles"
scripture: "dogon-cosmogony"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The universe moves in great cycles. The smallest cycle is the day, from sunrise to sunrise. The next is the year, from planting to planting. The greatest cycle is the Sigui, the fifty-year cycle of Po Tolo.

**2** At the end of each great cycle, the world is renewed. The old is swept away and the new is established. The ceremonies of renewal reconnect the present with the beginning.

**3** The priests know the cycles of the stars. They know when the Pleiades rise and when Sirius appears on the horizon. They know the movements of the planets and the phases of the moon.

**4** This knowledge was given by the Nommo to the first ancestors. It has been passed down through the generations, from priest to priest, in secret, for it is sacred knowledge, not to be shared with the uninitiated.

**5** The Dogon say: We came from the stars. The Nommo came from the region of Sirius, and he brought us the knowledge of the heavens. When we die, our souls return to the stars.

**6** This is the cosmogony of the Dogon, the story of creation from the cosmic egg to the present day. It is told in the great ceremonies, danced in the great dances, and woven into the cloth of the people. It is the truth of the Dogon, and it endures.
