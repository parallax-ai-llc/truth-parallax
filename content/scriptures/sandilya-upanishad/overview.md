## Overview

The Sandilya Upanishad belongs to the Atharva Veda and is named after the ancient sage Sandilya, who appears in the Chandogya Upanishad. It presents a comprehensive system of yoga based on the eight limbs (*ashtanga*) — predating or contemporaneous with Patanjali's formulation — combined with detailed teachings on the subtle body, including the *nadis* (energy channels), *chakras* (energy centers), and the practice of *pranayama* (breath control). The text bridges Upanishadic philosophy with practical yoga instruction.

## Structure

The Sandilya Upanishad consists of three chapters:

1. **Chapter 1 — The Eight Limbs of Yoga**: Yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, and samadhi
2. **Chapter 2 — The Subtle Body**: The nadis, chakras, kundalini, and the practice of pranayama
3. **Chapter 3 — Meditation and Liberation**: The culmination of yogic practice in the realization of Brahman

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: The eight limbs as a devotional discipline oriented toward God
- **Shaivism**: Yoga practice as the means to recognize Shiva within
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Yoga as preparatory purification for non-dual self-knowledge
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Systematic yoga as devotional service under God's guidance
- **Academic**: Important evidence for the early codification of ashtanga yoga
- **Comparative**: Eight-limbed yoga compared with systematic spiritual paths worldwide
