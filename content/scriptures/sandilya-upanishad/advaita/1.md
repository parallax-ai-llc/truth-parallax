For Advaita Vedanta, this section's treatment of the eight limbs of yoga contributes to the cumulative Upanishadic demonstration that Brahman alone is real. The Sandilya Upanishad's teachings serve to generate the discrimination (*viveka*) between the real and the apparent that is the essence of the Advaita path.

Shankara's tradition values these teachings for their power to remove the ignorance (*avidya*) that veils the non-dual self. The knowledge imparted here is not new information but the removal of the misunderstanding that prevented recognition of what was always the case.
