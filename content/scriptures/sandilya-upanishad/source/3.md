---
title: "Sandilya Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "sandilya-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Then Sandilya asked Atharvan: "By what means is the Kaivalya state of the soul attained?"

**2** Atharvan replied: "By knowledge alone is Kaivalya attained. Knowledge is of two kinds: the lower and the higher. The lower knowledge is the knowledge of the Vedas and their branches. The higher knowledge is that by which the imperishable Brahman is attained.

**3** "That imperishable Brahman is not gross, not subtle, not short, not long, not red, not adhesive. It is without shadow, without darkness, without air, without space, without attachment.

**4** "It is without taste, without smell, without eyes, without ears, without speech, without mind, without vigour, without breath, without mouth, without measure, having no within and no without.

**5** "It eats nothing and nothing eats it.

**6** "By the command of that imperishable Being, the sun and the moon are upheld. By the command of that imperishable Being, heaven and earth are upheld.

**7** "By the command of that imperishable Being, moments, hours, days, nights, fortnights, months, seasons, and years all stand apart.

**8** "By the command of that imperishable Being, some rivers flow to the east from the white mountains, others to the west, and in whatever direction each goes.

**9** "That which is unseen, unheard, unknown, and unknowable — that alone is Brahman, and nothing else. He who knows this enters Brahman.

**10** "This Atman is neither born nor does it die. It came from nowhere and has become no one. Unborn, eternal, everlasting, ancient — it is not killed when the body is killed.

**11** "If the killer thinks he kills, if the killed thinks he is killed, both are ignorant. For this one neither kills nor is killed.

**12** "Smaller than the smallest, greater than the greatest, this Self is hidden in the heart of all beings. He who is free from desire, with mind and senses composed, beholds the majesty of the Self and becomes free from sorrow.

**13** "Though sitting still, it travels far. Though lying down, it goes everywhere. Who, save myself, can know this effulgent being who rejoices and rejoices not?

**14** "The wise who know this bodiless Self seated in bodies, this great and all-pervading Atman — they do not grieve.

**15** "This Atman cannot be attained by the study of the Vedas, nor by intelligence, nor by much hearing. He whom the Self chooses, by him alone is the Self attained. The Self reveals His own form to the one who seeks Him.

**16** "He who has not turned away from bad conduct, who is not tranquil, who is not collected, whose mind is not at peace — he cannot attain this Self, even by knowledge.

**17** "When all the desires that dwell in the heart are cast away, then does the mortal become immortal, and even here he attaineth Brahman.

**18** "When all the knots of the heart are cut asunder, then the mortal becomes immortal. This is the teaching.

**19** "There is that ancient path, subtle and extending far, which has been discovered by me and by which the wise, the knowers of Brahman, go to the heavenly sphere after the fall of this body, being freed.

**20** "Om. May the Brahman protect us both. May it nourish us both. May we both acquire strength. May our study be brilliant. May we not cavil at each other. Om. Peace, peace, peace."
