---
title: "Sandilya Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "sandilya-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** "Pratyahara is of two kinds. One is the restraining of the mind from the objects of the senses. The other is the beholding of Atman in all objects everywhere.

**2** "Dharana is of three kinds: fixing the mind upon the Self, bringing the external ether into the space within the heart, and contemplating the five forms of the chosen deity in the five elements.

**3** "Dhyana is of two kinds. The first pertains to the subtle form and the second to the gross form of the object of meditation.

**4** "As the one fire entering the world becomes different in shape according to the objects it consumes, so does the one inner Self of all beings become different according to whatever it enters, and exists also without.

**5** "As the air, the one universal mover, becomes different in shape according to the vessels it enters, so does the one inner Self of all beings become different according to whatever it enters.

**6** "The Yogi should constantly think on the identity of the individual soul and the supreme Self, and should meditate on the all-pervading nature of Brahman.

**7** "Just as a lamp placed inside a pot illumines the pot, so does the Self, dwelling within the body, illumine the whole body.

**8** "Samadhi is the state of identity of the individual Self with the supreme Self, free from all limiting adjuncts — this is the supreme state.

**9** "The Yogi who has attained Samadhi sees no distinction between the individual soul and the supreme Self. He sees the one Atman everywhere.

**10** "He who has realized Atman, which is without beginning and end, which is unchanging, which is beyond the darkness of ignorance — he is freed from the bondage of birth and death.

**11** "The knower of Atman crosses over sorrow. The knower of Atman transcends the illusion of the world. His sins are destroyed and he becomes immortal.

**12** "That which is knowledge, bliss, and truth, which is unchanging, which is complete in itself and ever free — that is Atman. That is to be known. He who knows it becomes liberated.

**13** "This Atman is self-luminous and is separate from the five sheaths. It is the witness of the three states of waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.

**14** "It is distinct from the gross, subtle, and causal bodies. It is the real 'I.' It is the inner being. It is the supreme Brahman. It is all-pervading.

**15** "Brahman is one without a second. It is truth, knowledge, and infinity. It is pure consciousness and bliss. It is without action and without attributes.

**16** "He who meditates upon and realizes this Atman — for him the wheel of birth and death comes to a stop, and he attains immortality.

**17** "This is the supreme secret. This is the highest knowledge. He who knows this — he becomes Brahman. He becomes Brahman indeed." Thus spoke Atharvan to Sandilya.

**18** "This Upanishad is not to be given to one who is not a pupil, nor to one who is not devoted, nor to one who does not serve the guru. It is to be given only to one who is peaceful, self-controlled, and endowed with the six virtues.
