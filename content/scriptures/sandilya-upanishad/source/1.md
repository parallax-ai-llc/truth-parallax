---
title: "Sandilya Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "sandilya-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Sandilya questioned Atharvan thus: "Please tell me about the eight limbs of Yoga which is the means of attaining to Atman."

**2** Atharvan replied: "The eight limbs of Yoga are Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, and Samadhi.

**3** "Yama is of ten kinds: non-violence, truth, non-stealing, continence, compassion, sincerity, patience, firmness, moderate diet, and purity.

**4** "Non-violence is the not causing of pain to any living being at any time through the actions of one's mind, speech, or body.

**5** "Truth is the speaking of words conforming to what one has seen, heard, or known, with the purpose of benefiting beings.

**6** "Non-stealing is the non-appropriation of things belonging to others through the actions of one's mind, speech, or body.

**7** "Continence is the control of the generative organ in all states, through mind, speech, and body.

**8** "Compassion is kindliness toward all beings in all states. Sincerity is the doing of good actions without the thought of the fruits they bring.

**9** "Patience is the bearing of all pleasant and unpleasant things, such as praise and blame, heat and cold. Firmness is the keeping of a steady mind, whether one attains or does not attain what one seeks.

**10** "Moderate diet is the taking of food mixed with ghee and sweet things, leaving one fourth of the stomach empty, and offering the act of eating to the Lord.

**11** "Purity is of two kinds — external and internal. External purity is brought about by earth and water. Internal purity is the cleansing of the mind.

**12** "Niyama is also of ten kinds: penance, contentment, belief in God, charity, worship of God, hearing of spiritual texts, modesty, a discerning intellect, recitation of mantras, and sacrifice.

**13** "Penance is the emaciation of the body through fasts and other observances according to the prescribed rules. Contentment is being satisfied with whatever comes to one unsought.

**14** "Belief in God is the firm conviction of the existence of God drawn from the evidence of the scriptures. Charity is the giving away of one's lawful possessions with a cheerful mind.

**15** "Worship of God is the worshipping of Vishnu, Shiva, and others with pure mind, according to one's power. Hearing of spiritual texts is the study of texts that treat of the liberation of the soul.

**16** "Modesty is the shrinking back from doing actions that are opposed to the rules of the Vedas and of society. A discerning intellect is the faith in the paths laid out by the Vedas.

**17** "Recitation of mantras is the practice of the mantras into which one has been duly initiated by one's spiritual teacher, and which are not opposed to the rules of the Vedas. Sacrifice is the practice of the rites of worship as prescribed by the Vedas.

**18** "Asanas are of many kinds. Of these the chief are Svastikasana, Gomukhasana, Padmasana, Virasana, Simhasana, Bhadrasana, Muktasana, Mayurasana, Sukhasana, Siddhasana, and others.

**19** "Svastika is the sitting with the body erect, placing both the feet between the joints of the thighs and the knees.

**20** "Having mastered the posture, one should practise the restraint of the breath. This is Pranayama. It is of three kinds: expulsion, retention, and inhalation. These together with the pauses between them constitute the complete exercise.
