The Nine Lords of the Night (*Yoalteuctin*) govern the hours of darkness in a repeating nine-day cycle that overlays the twenty day-signs, creating a complex calendrical matrix. These lords — including Xiuhtecuhtli (Fire Lord), Itztli (Obsidian Knife), Piltzintecuhtli (Young Prince), Centeotl (Maize God), Mictlantecuhtli (Death Lord), Chalchiuhtlicue (Water Goddess), Tlazolteotl (Earth Mother), Tepeyollotl (Heart of the Mountain), and Tlaloc (Rain God) — each impart their specific character to the night they govern.

In Mesoamerican cosmology, the night is not simply the absence of day but a positively charged domain with its own spiritual powers and dangers. The Lords of the Night rule the hours when dreams arrive, when spirits move freely, and when the boundaries between the human and supernatural worlds become permeable. Contemporary Nahua curanderos and healers time their most powerful ceremonies to specific nights governed by favorable lords, understanding that healing work conducted under Chalchiuhtlicue's governance draws on water's purifying power, while ceremonies under Mictlantecuhtli's watch engage the forces of death and transformation.

The nine-night cycle creates a perpetual calendar of overlapping influences: each night carries both the quality of its day-sign and the character of its night lord. A night of Coatl (Serpent) governed by Tlaloc produces a very different spiritual environment than a night of Coatl governed by Xiuhtecuhtli. This combinatorial system generates a richness of temporal quality that resists reduction to simple good-or-bad prognostication. Indigenous practitioners trained in the calendar tradition learn to read these combinations with the kind of nuanced judgment that comes only from years of apprenticeship and direct ceremonial experience under Elder guidance.

The Codex Borgia's depiction of the Night Lords also encodes knowledge about nocturnal ecology — the behavior of owls, bats, jaguars, and other creatures of darkness — that reflects centuries of close observation. Night-blooming flowers, the calls of specific birds at particular hours, and the movement of constellations through the night sky all correspond to the governance of specific lords. For communities that maintain close relationships with their local ecosystems, the Night Lords provide a framework for understanding the night as a living, structured environment rather than a void to be feared or ignored.
