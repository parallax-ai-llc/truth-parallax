The Calendar Round — the 52-year cycle produced by the interlocking of the 260-day *tonalpohualli* and the 365-day *xiuhpohualli* (solar calendar) — constitutes the largest regular unit of Mesoamerican time-keeping. The Codex Borgia's treatment of this cycle connects it to the New Fire Ceremony (*Toxiuhmolpilia*), the most dramatic and consequential ritual in the Mexica ceremonial calendar. At the completion of each 52-year bundle (*xiuhmolpilli*), all fires were extinguished, household goods were destroyed, and the world waited in darkness for the priests to drill new fire on the Hill of the Star (*Huixachtlan*).

The New Fire Ceremony expressed the Mesoamerican understanding that the continuation of the world is not guaranteed but must be actively renewed. If the new fire failed to ignite — if the Pleiades did not pass the zenith at the appointed time — the Fifth Sun might end. The ceremony thus dramatized the existential precariousness of cosmic order and the human responsibility for its maintenance. Contemporary indigenous communities that observe Calendar Round completions (the most recent alignments being tracked by various Nahua and Maya calendar traditions) understand these moments as times of heightened spiritual danger and opportunity.

The Codex Borgia's depiction of the Calendar Round encodes the mathematical relationship between the two calendar cycles. Because 260 and 365 share the factor 5, the least common multiple is 18,980 days — exactly 52 solar years or 73 sacred calendar rounds. This mathematical elegance was understood by Mesoamerican astronomers not as mere coincidence but as evidence of intentional cosmic design. The Calendar Round's completion brings the two great time-cycles back into alignment, and this realignment creates a moment of cosmic reset analogous to the creation of a new world-age.

For indigenous communities engaged in calendar revitalization, the Calendar Round provides a framework for long-range temporal awareness that connects personal, communal, and cosmic time. Elders who have witnessed one or more Calendar Round transitions carry special authority as repositories of experience that spans a full cosmic cycle. The ceremony of binding the years — literally tying a bundle of 52 reed-sticks — is both a temporal marker and a spiritual practice that acknowledges the passage of time while affirming the community's commitment to continuing the ceremonial obligations that sustain the world for another cycle.
