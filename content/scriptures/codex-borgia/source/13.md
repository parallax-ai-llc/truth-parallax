---
title: "Codex Borgia - Chapter 13"
scripture: "codex-borgia"
chapter: 13
---

**1** In the count of days and in the reckoning of destinies, the gods established the sacred calendar. Twenty day-signs were given, and thirteen numbers to count with them, making the sacred count of two hundred and sixty days.

**2** Tonatiuh, the Sun God, presides over the first period. His face shines forth from the center of the sacred image, and the four directions radiate from his countenance.

**3** The day-sign Cipactli, the Crocodile, begins the count. It is a day of beginning, of primordial creation, when the earth first rose from the waters.

**4** Each day-sign carries its own power, its own omen, its own patron deity. The wise priests read these signs and counsel the people according to the sacred knowledge.

**5** The gods of rain and maize, of wind and death, of the night sky and the feathered serpent — all are represented in the sacred pages.

**6** Quetzalcoatl, the Feathered Serpent, moves through the panels, bringing wisdom and the breath of life. His is the wind that precedes the rain.

**7** Tezcatlipoca, the Smoking Mirror, stands in opposition, bringing transformation through conflict. His is the obsidian mirror that reveals hidden truths.

**8** Between these great powers the destinies of mortals are woven, as the day-count turns its eternal wheel.
