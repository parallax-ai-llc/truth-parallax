**1** The night lords, the nine Lords of the Night, rule in succession over each night of the tonalpohualli. They cycle endlessly through the count of days.

**2** The first Lord of the Night is Xiuhtecuhtli, the Lord of Fire, who dwells at the center of the hearth. The second is Itztli, the Obsidian Knife, patron of sacrifice.

**3** The third is Piltzintecuhtli, the Young Prince, lord of the hallucinogenic morning glory. The fourth is Centeotl, the Corn God, sustainer of life.

**4** The fifth is Mictlantecuhtli, the Lord of the Dead, ruler of Mictlan, the underworld. The sixth is Chalchiuhtlicue, She of the Jade Skirt.

**5** The seventh is Tlazolteotl, the Filth Goddess, eater of sins, patroness of confession and purification. The eighth is Tepeyollotl, Heart of the Mountain. The ninth is Tlaloc, the Rain God, lord of thunder and lightning.

**6** Each night lord influences the fortune of the day over which he presides. The priests consult the codex to determine which lord rules each night and what offerings must be made.

**7** The pages of the codex depict these lords with their attributes: their regalia, their offerings, their sacred animals, and their directions, painted in the brilliant colors of cochineal red, Maya blue, and obsidian black.
