---
title: "Codex Borgia Section 5"
scripture: "codex-borgia"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The Venus pages of the codex chart the cycle of the morning star and the evening star. Venus, the planet of Quetzalcoatl, appears and disappears in a cycle of 584 days.

**2** When Venus rises as the morning star, after eight days of invisibility in inferior conjunction, its rays are considered dangerous. Warriors born under its influence go forth to battle.

**3** The five Venus periods of 584 days equal eight solar years of 365 days. This great cycle of the Venus round was tracked with precision by the priests.

**4** At each heliacal rising, specific deities are struck by the spears of Venus. The codex depicts the Feathered Serpent hurling his atlatl darts at each of the four cardinal directions.

**5** When Venus is the evening star, Quetzalcoatl descends into Mictlan, the underworld. During his journey through the realm of the dead, the world is in danger.

**6** The return of Venus as the morning star symbolizes the triumph of light over darkness, the resurrection of the Feathered Serpent, and the renewal of the world.
