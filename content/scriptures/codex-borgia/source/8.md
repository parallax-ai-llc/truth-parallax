**1** The great ritual pages depict the ceremonies of the veintenas, the eighteen months of twenty days each that compose the solar year of 360 days, plus the five nemontemi, the unlucky days.

**2** In the first month, Atlcahualo, children are sacrificed to the rain gods Tlaloc and Chalchiuhtlicue, that the rains may come. Their tears are an omen of abundant rainfall.

**3** In the month of Tlacaxipehualiztli, the Flaying of Men, captive warriors are sacrificed and their skins are worn by priests in honor of Xipe Totec, Our Lord the Flayed One, god of spring and renewal.

**4** In the month of Toxcatl, the most beautiful captive, who has been treated as the living image of Tezcatlipoca for an entire year, ascends the temple and is sacrificed. His flutes are broken on the temple steps.

**5** In the month of Panquetzaliztli, the great feast of Huitzilopochtli is celebrated. Runners race through the streets bearing images of the god. Captives are sacrificed at the summit of the Templo Mayor.

**6** The five nemontemi, the empty days at the end of the year, are days of ill omen. No business is conducted, no children are named, and the people fast and pray until the new year begins.
