chapter: 2

**1** The second trecena begins with Ce Ocelotl, One Jaguar, and is governed by Quetzalcoatl, the Feathered Serpent, Lord of Wind and Wisdom, the god who created humanity from the bones of the dead in Mictlan.

**2** The third trecena begins with Ce Mazatl, One Deer, and is governed by Tepeyollotl, the Heart of the Mountain, the jaguar god of echoes, earthquakes, and the interior of the earth.

**3** The fourth trecena begins with Ce Xochitl, One Flower, and is governed by Huehuecoyotl, the Old Coyote, the trickster god of dance, song, and mischief.

**4** The fifth trecena begins with Ce Acatl, One Reed, and is governed by Chalchiuhtlicue, She of the Jade Skirt, goddess of rivers, lakes, and flowing water.

**5** Each trecena brings its own influences and its own patron deity. The priests offer incense, blood, and flowers to each patron at the beginning of the trecena.

**6** The twenty trecenas complete the cycle of 260 days, and then the count begins again, for the sacred calendar has no beginning and no end, turning endlessly like the wheels of time.
