---
title: "Codex Borgia Section 9"
scripture: "codex-borgia"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The journey of the soul through the underworld is depicted in the central fold of the codex. When a person dies, the soul must travel through nine layers of Mictlan before reaching its final rest.

**2** In the first level, the soul crosses a great river with the aid of a dog, the xoloitzcuintli, which was cremated with the dead for this purpose.

**3** In the second level, the soul passes between two mountains that clash together. In the third, the soul crosses a mountain of obsidian. In the fourth, the soul endures the cutting wind of obsidian blades.

**4** In the fifth level, the soul passes through a place where flags flutter. In the sixth, the soul is pierced by arrows. In the seventh, the soul encounters wild beasts that devour hearts.

**5** In the eighth level, the soul crosses narrow ledges of stone. In the ninth and final level, the soul arrives before Mictlantecuhtli and Mictecacihuatl, the Lord and Lady of the Dead.

**6** The soul presents the offerings that were buried or cremated with the body: jade, food, drink, and paper vestments. After four years of wandering through these nine levels, the soul at last dissolves and finds rest.

**7** Warriors who die in battle and women who die in childbirth do not go to Mictlan but ascend to the heavens, accompanying the sun on its daily journey across the sky.
