---
title: "Codex Borgia Section 6"
scripture: "codex-borgia"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The central pages of the codex depict the great cosmic drama of creation and destruction. The five suns, the five ages of the world, are shown in sequence.

**2** The first sun, Nahui Ocelotl (Four Jaguar), was the age ruled by Tezcatlipoca. The people of that age were giants, and they were devoured by jaguars when the sun was destroyed.

**3** The second sun, Nahui Ehecatl (Four Wind), was ruled by Quetzalcoatl. The people were carried away by great winds and transformed into monkeys.

**4** The third sun, Nahui Quiahuitl (Four Rain), was ruled by Tlaloc. The world was destroyed by a rain of fire, and the people were turned into birds.

**5** The fourth sun, Nahui Atl (Four Water), was ruled by Chalchiuhtlicue. A great flood destroyed the world, and the people became fish.

**6** The fifth and present sun, Nahui Ollin (Four Movement), was created at Teotihuacan, when the gods gathered and Nanahuatzin cast himself into the great fire to become the sun. This sun will be destroyed by earthquakes.

**7** The codex records these cosmic cycles to remind the priests and the people that the world is sustained only by the sacrifices offered to the gods, and without these offerings, the fifth sun too shall perish.
