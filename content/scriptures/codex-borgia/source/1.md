**1** The tonalpohualli, the sacred count of days, begins with Ce Cipactli, One Crocodile. The crocodile rests in the primordial waters, and upon its back the earth was formed.

**2** The day signs proceed in a cycle of twenty: Cipactli (Crocodile), Ehecatl (Wind), Calli (House), Cuetzpalin (Lizard), Coatl (Serpent), Miquiztli (Death), Mazatl (Deer), Tochtli (Rabbit), Atl (Water), Itzcuintli (Dog),

**3** Ozomatli (Monkey), Malinalli (Grass), Acatl (Reed), Ocelotl (Jaguar), Cuauhtli (Eagle), Cozcacuauhtli (Vulture), Ollin (Movement), Tecpatl (Flint Knife), Quiahuitl (Rain), Xochitl (Flower).

**4** These twenty signs combine with the numbers one through thirteen to create the sacred calendar of 260 days, the count that governs the fates of all living beings.

**5** Each day bears the influence of its sign and its number. The priests who read the tonalpohualli can determine the destiny of a child born on any day and prescribe the rituals necessary to propitiate the gods.

**6** The first trecena, the count of thirteen days beginning with Ce Cipactli, is governed by Tonacatecuhtli, the Lord of Sustenance, and Tonacacihuatl, the Lady of Sustenance, the primordial pair from whom all creation flows.
