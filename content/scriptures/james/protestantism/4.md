## The Source of Conflict

James 4:1–3 traces interpersonal conflict to disordered desires: "You desire and do not have, so you murder." Protestant interpreters, especially in the Reformed tradition, connect this to the doctrine of indwelling sin — even believers struggle with passions that war against the soul. The passage underscores the ongoing need for sanctification.

## Friendship with the World

James's declaration that "friendship with the world is enmity with God" (4:4) has been a cornerstone of Protestant teaching on worldliness and separation. The call to "submit to God, resist the devil" (4:7) shapes evangelical spiritual warfare theology and the call to personal holiness.

## Against Presumption

The chapter closes with a warning against presumptuous planning: "You do not know what tomorrow will bring" (4:14). Protestant interpreters read this as a call to acknowledge God's sovereignty over all of life, treating every plan with the caveat "if the Lord wills" (4:15). This has influenced Protestant ethics of humility and dependence on divine providence.
