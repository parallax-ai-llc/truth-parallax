## The Danger of the Tongue

James 3:1–12 contains the New Testament's most sustained reflection on the power of speech. The tongue is compared to a small rudder steering a great ship and a tiny spark setting a forest ablaze. Protestant preaching has drawn heavily on this passage, treating the control of speech as a measure of spiritual maturity. James's warning that teachers will be judged more strictly (3:1) has shaped Protestant pastoral theology.

## Two Kinds of Wisdom

James 3:13–18 contrasts "earthly, unspiritual, demonic" wisdom with the "wisdom from above," which is "pure, peaceable, gentle, open to reason." Protestant interpreters read this as a practical test of genuine spirituality: true wisdom is known by its fruit — peace, mercy, and righteousness — not by intellectual sophistication or rhetorical skill.
