## Warning to the Rich

James 5:1–6 contains a prophetic denunciation of wealthy oppressors whose hoarded wealth and unpaid wages "cry out" to the Lord. Protestant interpreters in the social gospel tradition have used this passage to critique economic exploitation. Reformed commentators emphasize that James condemns not wealth itself but its unjust acquisition and selfish hoarding.

## Patience and the Coming of the Lord

James 5:7–11 calls for patient endurance until the Lord's coming, using the farmer waiting for rain and the example of Job. Protestant eschatology connects this to the doctrine of the Second Coming, encouraging believers to persevere in faithfulness while awaiting Christ's return.

## Prayer, Anointing, and Restoration

James 5:13–20 instructs believers to pray in all circumstances, to call the elders for anointing and prayer when sick, and to confess sins to one another. Protestant interpreters affirm the efficacy of prayer while typically distinguishing the anointing described here from the Catholic sacrament. The passage's closing — "whoever brings back a sinner from wandering will save his soul from death" — underscores the Protestant emphasis on mutual accountability and pastoral care.
