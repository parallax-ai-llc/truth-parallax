## The Evil Inclination

James 4:1–3, tracing conflict to disordered desires, parallels the rabbinic concept of the *yetzer hara* (evil inclination). Rabbinic theology teaches that every person possesses both a *yetzer hara* and a *yetzer hatov* (good inclination), and that the study of Torah and the practice of the commandments discipline the evil inclination. James's exhortation to "resist the devil" (4:7) resonates with this rabbinic framework.

## Humility before God

James 4:6–10, with its call to humility and its promise that God will exalt the humble, echoes the Jewish prophetic tradition. Micah 6:8 — "to walk humbly with your God" — captures the same priority. Jewish readers find in James's teaching a prophetic voice consistent with the Hebrew Bible's consistent elevation of humility over pride.

## The Uncertainty of Tomorrow

James 4:13–17 warns against presumptuous planning, echoing Proverbs 27:1: "Do not boast about tomorrow, for you do not know what a day may bring." This recognition of human limitation before divine sovereignty is a constant theme in Jewish wisdom literature and finds expression in the daily Jewish prayer tradition.
