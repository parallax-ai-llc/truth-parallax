## Prophetic Judgment and Economic Justice

James 5:1–6 echoes the Hebrew prophets' condemnation of economic injustice. The cry of the laborers' wages "reaching the ears of the Lord of hosts" (5:4) recalls Isaiah 5:7–9 and Amos 2:6–7. Jewish readers recognize in James the authentic voice of prophetic Judaism, demanding justice for the poor and warning the wealthy of divine judgment.

## Patience and the Example of Job

James 5:7–11 invokes Job as the paradigm of patient endurance. In Jewish tradition, the Book of Job raises the deepest questions about innocent suffering and divine justice. James's summary — "You have seen the purpose of the Lord, how the Lord is compassionate and merciful" (5:11) — reflects the Jewish affirmation that God's justice, though sometimes hidden, ultimately prevails.

## Communal Prayer and Healing

James 5:13–16, with its instructions on communal prayer and confession, resonates with Jewish practices of communal intercession and *teshuvah* (repentance). The confession of sins to one another (5:16) parallels the Jewish tradition of interpersonal forgiveness sought before Yom Kippur. Jewish readers appreciate the communal dimension of James's teaching, which locates healing and restoration within the community of faith.
