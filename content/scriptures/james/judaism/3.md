## The Tongue in Jewish Ethical Teaching

James 3:1–12 on the power of speech has extensive parallels in Jewish tradition. The concept of *lashon hara* (evil speech) is developed extensively in rabbinic literature, particularly by the Chofetz Chaim (Rabbi Israel Meir Kagan). James's comparison of the tongue to a fire that "sets on fire the entire course of life" (3:6) echoes the Talmudic teaching that evil speech kills three persons: the speaker, the listener, and the one spoken about (Arachin 15b).

## Two Kinds of Wisdom

James 3:13–18 contrasts earthly and heavenly wisdom in terms that parallel the Jewish wisdom tradition. The "wisdom from above" that is "pure, peaceable, gentle" resonates with the Torah's description of wisdom as a "tree of life" (Proverbs 3:18). Jewish readers appreciate the practical, ethical focus of James's wisdom teaching, which values moral fruit over intellectual abstraction.
