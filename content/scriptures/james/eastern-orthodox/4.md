## Passions and the Orthodox Struggle

James 4:1–3, which traces conflict to the "passions at war within you," is central to Orthodox ascetic theology. The *pathē* (passions) are understood not as emotions per se but as disordered movements of the soul that draw the person away from God. The Orthodox spiritual life is a therapeutic process of healing these passions through prayer, fasting, and the sacramental life.

## Drawing Near to God

James 4:8 — "Draw near to God, and he will draw near to you" — encapsulates the Orthodox understanding of the spiritual life as mutual approach. God perpetually extends grace, and the human person is called to respond through repentance and prayer. This dynamic of divine initiative and human response defines the Orthodox path of salvation.

## Humility as the Foundation

James 4:6–10, with its call to humility and its promise that God gives grace to the humble, is foundational for Orthodox spirituality. The monastic tradition, from Anthony to Silouan of Athos, identifies humility as the supreme virtue and the prerequisite for every other spiritual gift.
