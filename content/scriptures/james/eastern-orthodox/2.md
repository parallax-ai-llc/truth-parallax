## The Royal Law and the Image of God

Orthodox interpreters read James's condemnation of partiality (2:1–9) in light of the doctrine of the image of God (*imago Dei*). Every person bears the divine image, and to show favoritism based on wealth is to dishonor the Creator. The "royal law" of love (2:8) is understood as the fulfillment of the entire Torah, consistent with Christ's summary of the commandments.

## Faith and Works in Orthodox Soteriology

James 2:14–26 poses no difficulty for Orthodox theology because Orthodoxy has never adopted the forensic model of justification that created the Protestant tension. Salvation is understood as a process of transformation — theosis — in which faith and works are inseparable dimensions of the one movement toward God. The Orthodox Fathers consistently taught that genuine faith naturally produces the fruit of good works.

## The Synergy of Grace and Freedom

James's examples of Abraham and Rahab illustrate the Orthodox principle of synergy: God's grace initiates salvation, but the human person must freely respond through obedient action. Neither faith nor works alone is sufficient; the two cooperate in the single process of salvation, which is ultimately the restoration of the divine likeness in the human person.
