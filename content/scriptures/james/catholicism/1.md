## Trials as Purification

Catholic interpretation reads James's teaching on trials (1:2–4) within the framework of purgative spirituality. Suffering produces not only endurance but purification of the soul, conforming the believer to Christ's own passion. The connection between testing and maturity resonates with the Catholic tradition of redemptive suffering, articulated by John Paul II in *Salvifici Doloris*.

## Asking in Faith

James 1:5–8 on requesting wisdom from God has informed Catholic teaching on prayer. The emphasis on asking "in faith, with no doubting" shapes the Catholic understanding of confident petition rooted in trust in God's goodness. The "double-minded" person who wavers is contrasted with the saints whose single-hearted devotion to God exemplifies mature faith.

## Pure Religion and Social Teaching

James 1:27 — "Religion that is pure and undefiled before God the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their affliction" — has been incorporated into Catholic social teaching as a scriptural mandate for charitable action. Catholic interpretation emphasizes that genuine faith necessarily manifests in works of mercy, not mere interior piety.
