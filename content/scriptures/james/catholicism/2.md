## The Royal Law and Catholic Social Ethics

James 2:1–13, with its condemnation of partiality toward the rich, has been central to Catholic social teaching. The "royal law" of loving one's neighbor (2:8) is understood as the summary of the natural moral law. Pope Francis has drawn on this passage repeatedly in his critique of a "throwaway culture" that discards the poor and vulnerable.

## Faith Perfected by Works

James 2:14–26 is the Catholic Church's premier scriptural text for the inseparability of faith and works. The Council of Trent cited James 2:24 — "a person is justified by works and not by faith alone" — in its decree on justification. Catholic theology teaches that faith is the beginning of salvation, but it must be "faith working through love" (Galatians 5:6), active in charity and good works, to bring justification to completion.

## Abraham's Faith Completed

James 2:22 states that Abraham's faith was "completed" (*eteleiōthē*) by his works. Catholic interpreters read this as evidence that justification is a process of growth, not a one-time legal declaration. The believer's cooperation with grace through works of love brings faith to its perfection, a teaching central to the Catholic understanding of merit.
