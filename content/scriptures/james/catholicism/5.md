## Prophetic Judgment on Wealth

James 5:1–6 contains a prophetic oracle against the rich who exploit laborers. Catholic social teaching has drawn on this passage since Leo XIII's *Rerum Novarum* (1891) to condemn unjust wages and economic exploitation. The cry of the laborers' unpaid wages reaching "the ears of the Lord of hosts" (5:4) is a cornerstone of the Catholic doctrine of economic justice.

## Patient Endurance and the Communion of Saints

James 5:7–11, with its call to patience and its invocation of the prophets and Job as examples, is read within the Catholic framework of the communion of saints. The faithful of every age form one community of patient endurance, and the examples of the saints inspire continued faithfulness.

## Anointing of the Sick

James 5:14–15 — "Is anyone among you sick? Let him call for the elders of the church, and let them pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord" — is the scriptural foundation for the Catholic Sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick. The Council of Trent declared that this sacrament was "instituted by Christ our Lord, intimated indeed in Mark (6:13), but recommended to the faithful and promulgated by James the apostle."

## Confession and Mutual Accountability

James 5:16 — "Confess your sins to one another and pray for one another, that you may be healed" — is cited by Catholic theology in support of the Sacrament of Reconciliation. While the sacramental practice involves confession to a priest, the broader principle of mutual confession and intercession shapes Catholic community life and pastoral care.
