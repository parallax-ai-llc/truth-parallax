## The Tongue and Moral Theology

Catholic moral theology has drawn extensively on James 3:1–12 for its teaching on the sins of speech. The tongue's capacity to "defile the whole body" (3:6) informs the Catholic categorization of sins of speech — detraction, calumny, gossip — as serious moral offenses. The passage underscores the social dimension of sin: words have power to build up or destroy community.

## Wisdom from Above

James 3:13–18 distinguishes between earthly and heavenly wisdom. Catholic interpretation, following Aquinas, connects the "wisdom from above" with the gift of the Holy Spirit enumerated in Isaiah 11:2. This wisdom is not merely intellectual knowledge but a supernatural gift that orders the soul toward God and produces the fruits of peace and righteousness.
