## Worldliness and the Life of the Hereafter

James 4:4 — "friendship with the world is enmity with God" — resonates with the Quranic teaching on the transience of worldly life (*dunya*). The Quran states: "The life of this world is nothing but play and amusement, but far better is the house in the Hereafter" (6:32). Muslim readers find in James a prophetic warning against attachment to material life that echoes the Quran's consistent prioritization of the Hereafter (*akhirah*) over the present world.

## Submission to God

James 4:7 — "Submit yourselves therefore to God" — contains a concept at the very heart of Islam. The word "Islam" itself means submission to God, and the Muslim (*muslim*) is one who submits. Muslim scholars note that James's call to submission, resistance of evil, and humble approach to God describes precisely the Islamic understanding of the believer's relationship to the Creator.

## Human Limitation and Divine Will

James 4:13–15 warns against planning without acknowledging God's will, teaching believers to say, "If the Lord wills, we shall live and do this or that." Muslim readers immediately recognize the Islamic practice of saying *in sha' Allah* ("God willing") before any statement about future plans. This convergence illustrates a shared Abrahamic consciousness of human contingency before divine sovereignty.
