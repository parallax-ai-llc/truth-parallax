## The Power of Speech

James 3:1–12 on the destructive power of the tongue aligns with extensive Islamic teaching on the ethics of speech. The Prophet Muhammad said: "Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day should speak good or remain silent" (Bukhari and Muslim). The Quran warns against backbiting (*ghiba*) and slander (*namima*), comparing backbiting to eating the flesh of one's dead brother (49:12). Muslim readers find in James a prophetic voice consistent with Islamic moral instruction.

## True Wisdom and Its Fruits

James 3:13–18 contrasts earthly and heavenly wisdom. In Islamic tradition, wisdom (*hikmah*) is a divine gift: "He gives wisdom to whom He wills, and whoever has been given wisdom has certainly been given much good" (Quran 2:269). James's description of heavenly wisdom as "peaceable, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy" resonates with the Quranic characterization of the believers as those who are "merciful among themselves" (48:29).
