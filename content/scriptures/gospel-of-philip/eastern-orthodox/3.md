## Christ and Mary Magdalene

Chapter 3 describes Mary Magdalene as the "companion" of Christ. The Eastern Orthodox Church venerates Mary Magdalene as a saint bearing the title "Equal-to-the-Apostles" (*isapostolos*), honoring her as the first witness of the resurrection and a faithful myrrh-bearer. Orthodox tradition celebrates her feast on July 22 and regards her as a model of repentance, devotion, and apostolic witness.

However, the Orthodox reading of Mary Magdalene is grounded in the canonical Gospels and patristic tradition, not in the Valentinian theology of the Gospel of Philip. The "companion" language in Philip serves the Valentinian doctrine of syzygy — the pairing of masculine and feminine emanations — rather than recording historical fact. Orthodox theologians reject the implication that Christ's relationship with Mary Magdalene was anything other than the relationship of a master with a devoted disciple. The Orthodox tradition affirms the dignity and spiritual authority of women — as evidenced by its extensive calendar of women saints — without adopting the Valentinian framework that instrumentalizes gender for cosmological purposes.
