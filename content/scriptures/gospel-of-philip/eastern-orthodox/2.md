## The Sacraments

Chapter 2 enumerates five Valentinian sacraments: baptism, chrism, eucharist, redemption, and the bridal chamber. The Eastern Orthodox Church recognizes seven holy mysteries (*mysteria*), including baptism, chrismation, and the eucharist. The terminological overlap is notable, and Orthodox scholars have observed that Valentinian sacramental practice may have developed in dialogue with — or in imitation of — the liturgical life of the apostolic churches.

However, the theological content of these rites diverges profoundly. Orthodox baptism incorporates the believer into the Body of Christ and the life of the Trinity; Orthodox chrismation seals the gift of the Holy Spirit; the Orthodox eucharist is the true Body and Blood of Christ, offered and received in the divine liturgy as the central act of the Church's worship. In each case, the sacraments are public, ecclesial, and open to all the faithful. The Valentinian sacraments described in Philip, by contrast, are stages of esoteric initiation into a cosmological system, accessible only to the pneumatic elite. The Orthodox understanding of the mysteries is rooted in the incarnation and the goodness of creation, not in escape from the material world.
