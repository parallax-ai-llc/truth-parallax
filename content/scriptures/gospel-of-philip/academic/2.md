## The Sacraments

Chapter 2 is among the most studied sections of the Gospel of Philip, as it provides the most detailed surviving account of Valentinian sacramental practice. The text names five sacraments — baptism, chrism, eucharist, redemption, and bridal chamber — and describes them as a progressive hierarchy. Scholars debate whether these descriptions reflect actual liturgical practices of a Valentinian community or represent theological idealization.

The relationship between these Valentinian rites and emerging "orthodox" Christian sacraments has been extensively analyzed. Hans-Martin Schenke and Jorunn Buckley have argued that Valentinian sacramental practice developed in parallel with proto-orthodox liturgy, sharing common roots in Second Temple Jewish ritual and early Christian worship. The five-sacrament system described here may represent a polemical response to proto-orthodox practice — claiming that the Valentinian community possesses the complete sacramental system of which orthodox Christianity has only a partial version. The question of what the "bridal chamber" sacrament actually involved — literal ritual, meditation, or purely metaphorical description — remains one of the most debated issues in Nag Hammadi scholarship.
