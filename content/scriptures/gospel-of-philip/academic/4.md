## The Bridal Chamber Mystery

Chapter 4 is devoted to the bridal chamber, the supreme sacrament of the Valentinian system. Academic scholarship has approached this topic from multiple angles: history of religions, ritual studies, gender theory, and Platonic philosophy. The central question — what was the bridal chamber? — has produced a spectrum of answers ranging from a literal sexual rite (rejected by most scholars) to a purely metaphorical description of spiritual union, with various intermediate positions involving actual rituals of anointing, blessing, or meditative visualization.

April DeConick's analysis of the bridal chamber as a "mystery of marriage" connects it to both Jewish wedding traditions and Greco-Roman mystery religions, arguing that the Valentinian rite drew on multiple cultural sources to construct a new sacrament of cosmic reunion. Einar Thomassen situates the bridal chamber within the broader Valentinian soteriology: it enacts the reunion of the pneumatic seed with its angelic counterpart, reversing the primordial fall of Sophia. The chapter's identification of the bridal chamber with the Holy of Holies suggests a deliberate appropriation of Temple theology, reinterpreting the most sacred Jewish sacred space as a Valentinian sacramental site.
