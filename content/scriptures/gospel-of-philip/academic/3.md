## Christ and Mary Magdalene

Chapter 3 contains the famous passage describing Mary Magdalene as the "companion" (*koinonos*) of Christ. This section has generated enormous scholarly attention, particularly regarding the meaning of *koinonos* (which can denote partner, companion, or consort), the nature of the "kiss" mentioned in the text, and the significance of the other disciples' jealousy. The Coptic text contains a lacuna at a critical point, and reconstructions vary, affecting interpretation significantly.

Academic analysis situates these passages within the broader context of early Christian debates about the authority of women and the relative status of different apostolic traditions. Karen King, Ann Graham Brock, and Antti Marjanen have argued that the elevation of Mary Magdalene in texts like Philip reflects communities that granted women greater authority, in contrast to the proto-orthodox tendency to marginalize female leadership. Other scholars, including Esther de Boer, caution against reading modern gender categories into ancient texts and emphasize the theological function of Mary Magdalene within Valentinian thought as the embodiment of Sophia restored.
