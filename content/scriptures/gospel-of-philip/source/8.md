---
title: "Gospel of Philip — Section 8: The Perfect Light"
scripture: "gospel-of-philip"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The world came about through a mistake. For he who created it wanted to create it imperishable and immortal. He fell short of attaining his desire.

**2** In this world there is good and evil. Its good things are not good, and its evil things not evil. But there is evil after this world which is truly evil — what is called "the middle." It is death.

**3** While we are in this world it is fitting for us to acquire the resurrection, so that when we strip off the flesh we may be found in rest, and not wander in the middle. For many go astray on the way.

**4** It is better to come into the world when the whole world has fallen than when one has not yet fallen. For one who has come will not be able to remain in the perfect light which is above all.

**5** The perfect light has poured itself over everything and all who are in it have received the chrism. Then the slaves will be set free, and the captives ransomed.

**6** Every plant which my father who is in heaven has not planted will be pulled up. Those who are separated will unite and will be filled. Every one who will enter the bridal chamber will kindle the light, for it burns in the manner of the marriages which are observed, though they happen at night. That fire burns only at night, and is put out. But the mysteries of that marriage are perfected rather in the day and the light. Neither that day nor its light ever sets.

**7** If anyone becomes a son of the bridal chamber, he will receive the light. If anyone does not receive it while he is here, he will not be able to receive it in the other place. He who will receive that light will not be seen, nor can he be detained. And none shall be able to torment a person like this, even while he dwells in the world. And again when he leaves the world, he has already received the truth in the images.

**8** The world has become the Aeon, for the Aeon is fullness for him. This is the way it is: it is revealed to him alone, not hidden in the darkness and the night, but hidden in a perfect day and a holy light.
