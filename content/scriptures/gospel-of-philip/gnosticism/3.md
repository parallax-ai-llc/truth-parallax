## Christ and Mary Magdalene

Chapter 3 contains the Gospel of Philip's most celebrated passages: the description of Mary Magdalene as the "companion" (*koinonos*) of Christ, whom he loved more than the other disciples and used to kiss frequently. For Gnostic readers, this relationship is not scandalous but theologically essential. It embodies the principle of syzygy — the pairing of masculine and feminine emanations — that structures the Pleroma and drives the cosmic drama of fall and restoration.

In Valentinian theology, every Aeon in the Pleroma exists as part of a male-female pair. The separation of these pairs is the origin of deficiency; their reunion is salvation. Christ and Mary Magdalene represent the archetypal syzygy on the earthly plane, modeling the spiritual marriage that each Gnostic will ultimately experience in the bridal chamber. The "kiss" is understood as the transmission of pneumatic (spiritual) substance — the communication of gnosis from the revealer to the receptive soul.

The Gnostic reading of this chapter challenges the marginalization of Mary Magdalene in orthodox tradition and elevates her to the status of primary spiritual partner and recipient of Christ's deepest teachings. Her role is not subordinate but complementary, reflecting the Valentinian conviction that wholeness requires the reunion of what has been divided.
