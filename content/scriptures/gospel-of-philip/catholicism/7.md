## Resurrection and the Living

Chapter 7 teaches that resurrection must be received during one's lifetime, not after death. Catholic teaching, grounded in the Nicene Creed ("I look for the resurrection of the dead and the life of the world to come"), affirms the future bodily resurrection of all the dead. The Catechism of the Catholic Church (988-1019) teaches that at the last day, the bodies of the dead will be reunited with their souls, and that Christ's own bodily resurrection is the cause and model of the general resurrection.

The Church acknowledges a spiritual dimension of resurrection in the present life — baptism is described as a dying and rising with Christ (Romans 6:3-4), and the sacraments communicate the life of the risen Lord. However, this present participation in Christ's resurrection does not replace the future bodily resurrection; it anticipates and guarantees it. The Gospel of Philip's reduction of resurrection to a purely present, spiritual experience eliminates the eschatological hope that is central to Catholic faith and liturgy.
