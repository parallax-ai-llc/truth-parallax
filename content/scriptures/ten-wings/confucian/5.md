## Confucian Reading

The upper Xi Ci Zhuan (Great Commentary) is the most philosophically ambitious of the Ten Wings, articulating a comprehensive Confucian cosmology in which moral cultivation and cosmic order are two aspects of a single reality. The sage, according to the Great Commentary, is one who perceives the patterns (*xiang*) of cosmic change and aligns human affairs with them. The concept of *Taiji* (Supreme Ultimate) is introduced here as the primordial source of the yin-yang dynamic that generates all things.
