## Confucian Reading

The lower Xiang Zhuan extends the symbolic-moral reading to the remaining hexagrams, demonstrating the Confucian principle that nature itself is a moral teacher. The relationship between the trigrams — Water over Earth, Thunder over Mountain, Fire within Wind — is read as modeling the proper relationships within human society and within the individual's own character. The Confucian reader learns to see the natural world as a continuous source of ethical instruction.
