## Confucian Reading

The upper Xiang Zhuan reveals the moral symbolism hidden in the hexagrams' structural images. Each hexagram is composed of two trigrams — representing natural phenomena such as Heaven, Earth, Thunder, Water, Mountain, Wind, Fire, and Lake — and the Xiang Zhuan shows how the relationship between these natural images yields moral instruction. The *junzi* observes the image of the hexagram and derives from it a principle for self-cultivation and ethical conduct.
