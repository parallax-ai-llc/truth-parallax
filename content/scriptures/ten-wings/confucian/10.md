## Confucian Reading

The Za Gua Zhuan offers brief, paired descriptions of the hexagrams that highlight their contrasting qualities. The Confucian reader finds in these terse characterizations a practical tool for moral discernment — the ability to distinguish between superficially similar situations that require fundamentally different responses. The Za Gua's economy of expression reflects the Confucian appreciation for precision in moral judgment and the recognition that wisdom often consists in making fine distinctions.
