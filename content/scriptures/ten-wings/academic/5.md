## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the upper Xi Ci Zhuan examines it as the most philosophically ambitious text in the Ten Wings corpus. Scholars trace its cosmological concepts — *Taiji*, yin-yang, the generation of the myriad things — to earlier philosophical sources and analyze how it synthesizes diverse intellectual traditions (Confucian, proto-Daoist, Yin-Yang school) into a unified cosmological vision. The text's influence on subsequent Chinese philosophy — particularly Neo-Confucianism — is assessed.
