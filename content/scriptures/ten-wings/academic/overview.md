Academic study of the Ten Wings encompasses textual criticism, intellectual history, comparative philosophy, and the history of Chinese hermeneutics. Modern scholarship has largely abandoned the traditional attribution of the Wings to Confucius, dating most of them to the late Warring States and early Han periods on the basis of linguistic analysis, philosophical content, and recently discovered manuscript evidence (particularly the Mawangdui silk manuscripts of 168 BCE and the Shanghai Museum bamboo strips).

Scholars analyze the Ten Wings as a diverse collection reflecting multiple intellectual traditions — Confucian, proto-Daoist, Yin-Yang school, and cosmological correlative thought — that were woven together over several centuries into a unified interpretive apparatus for the *Yijing*. The process by which a Bronze Age divination manual was transformed into a philosophical classic is itself a major topic of scholarly inquiry.
