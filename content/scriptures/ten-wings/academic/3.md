## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the upper Xiang Zhuan examines its distinctive interpretive method — deriving moral instruction from the visual imagery of the hexagram structure. Scholars trace this method to the broader intellectual practice of correlative thinking (*lei*) that characterized late Warring States and early Han Chinese thought. The Commentary's genre — moral maxims derived from natural imagery — is compared with similar genres in other civilizations' wisdom literature.
