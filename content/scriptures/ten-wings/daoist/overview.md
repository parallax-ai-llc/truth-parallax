The Daoist reading of the Ten Wings finds in them a cosmological vocabulary shared between the Confucian and Daoist traditions — particularly the concepts of yin-yang, *Dao*, and the ceaseless transformation of all things. While the Confucian reading emphasizes moral cultivation and social responsibility, the Daoist reading highlights the Wings' descriptions of the natural, spontaneous (*ziran*) patterns of cosmic change and the wisdom of aligning oneself with these patterns through *wu-wei* (non-forceful action).

Daoist interpreters note that the Great Commentary's description of the sage who 'acts in accordance with the Dao' resonates with the Daoist ideal of effortless action — responding to each situation with the precise, minimal intervention that the moment requires. The yin-yang cosmology of the Wings provides the theoretical basis for Daoist practices including internal alchemy (*neidan*), feng shui, and traditional Chinese medicine.
