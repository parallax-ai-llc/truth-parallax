## Daoist Reading

The Daoist reading of the upper Xiang Zhuan appreciates the Commentary on the Images as a guide to learning from nature's spontaneous patterns. The natural images of the hexagrams — mountain, water, thunder, wind — are understood not as symbols requiring moral interpretation but as direct teachers whose lessons are available to anyone who observes them with an empty, receptive mind. The Daoist sage learns from the mountain's stillness, the water's adaptability, and the wind's effortless penetration.
