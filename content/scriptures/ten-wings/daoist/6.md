## Daoist Reading

The lower Xi Ci Zhuan deepens the Daoist reading with its reflections on the nature of change and the sage's relationship to it. The Daoist finds in the Great Commentary's description of the sage who 'follows the *Dao* of Heaven and Earth' a confirmation of the Daoist ideal of *wu-wei* — the effortless action that arises from perfect alignment with the natural order. The passage describing how the sage 'has no fixed mind but makes the mind of the people his mind' resonates with the Daoist concept of the sage as an empty vessel.
