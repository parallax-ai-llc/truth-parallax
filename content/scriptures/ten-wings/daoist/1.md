## Daoist Reading

The Daoist reading of the upper Tuan Zhuan finds in the Commentary on the Judgments a guide to recognizing the natural flow of situations and responding with appropriate, minimal action. Where the Confucian reads moral instruction, the Daoist reads descriptions of the *Dao*'s spontaneous movement through the world — the ceaseless interplay of yin and yang that the wise person observes and follows rather than attempts to control. The favorable and unfavorable judgments are understood not as moral evaluations but as descriptions of the natural consequences of alignment or misalignment with the *Dao*.
