## Daoist Reading

The lower Xiang Zhuan's natural imagery continues to serve the Daoist reading as a manual of *ziran* (naturalness) — the quality of acting in accordance with one's own nature and the nature of the situation. The Daoist reads the Commentary on the Images not as moral instruction but as an invitation to observe how nature itself resolves tensions, balances forces, and achieves harmony without deliberate effort — and to model one's own conduct on these natural processes.
