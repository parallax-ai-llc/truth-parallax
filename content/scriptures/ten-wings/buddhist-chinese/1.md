## Buddhist-Chinese Reading

The Buddhist-Chinese reading of the upper Tuan Zhuan approaches the Commentary on the Judgments through the lens of Buddhist concepts of *karma* (cause and effect) and *pratityasamutpada* (dependent origination). The favorable and unfavorable outcomes described in the hexagram judgments are understood as illustrations of the Buddhist principle that actions have consequences determined by their alignment with the dharma. Chinese Buddhist interpreters found in the Tuan Zhuan a vocabulary for expressing karmic principles in culturally familiar Chinese terms.
