## Buddhist-Chinese Reading

The lower Xi Ci Zhuan's reflections on the nature of change resonated deeply with the Buddhist teaching of *anicca* (impermanence) — the recognition that all conditioned phenomena are in constant flux. Chinese Buddhist interpreters found in the Great Commentary's celebration of change a philosophical ally in their argument against the substantialist assumptions of pre-Buddhist Chinese philosophy. The convergence of *Yijing* change-philosophy and Buddhist impermanence-teaching represents one of the most productive moments in Chinese intellectual history.
