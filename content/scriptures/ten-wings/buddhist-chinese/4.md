## Buddhist-Chinese Reading

The lower Xiang Zhuan continues the Buddhist-Chinese dialogue by providing natural imagery that Chinese Buddhist teachers used to illustrate dharmic principles. The Commentary's observations about the proper response to natural phenomena — stillness before the mountain, adaptability before the water — resonate with Buddhist teachings on meditation (concentration and stillness) and wisdom (fluid responsiveness to each moment). The integration of *Yijing* imagery into Chinese Buddhist discourse represents a distinctive achievement of cultural synthesis.
