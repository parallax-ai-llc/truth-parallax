## Buddhist-Chinese Reading

The lower Tuan Zhuan continues to serve the Buddhist-Chinese reading as a bridge between the *Yijing*'s cosmological wisdom and Buddhist philosophical concepts. The concept of *shi* (timeliness) in the Tuan Zhuan — acting at the right moment in the right way — resonates with the Buddhist concept of *upaya* (skillful means) — the ability to adapt one's teaching and practice to the specific needs of each situation. Chinese Buddhist teachers used this parallel to make Buddhist concepts accessible to a Confucian-educated audience.
