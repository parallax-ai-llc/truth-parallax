## Buddhist-Chinese Reading

The Buddhist-Chinese reading of the upper Xiang Zhuan finds in the Commentary on the Images a vocabulary for expressing the Buddhist teaching that all phenomena are interconnected and mutually conditioning. The natural images of the hexagrams — and the moral responses they inspire — illustrate the Buddhist principle that the observer and the observed arise together in dependence on each other. The Huayan school's concept of the mutual interpenetration of all phenomena found a congenial expression in the Xiang Zhuan's correlative cosmology.
