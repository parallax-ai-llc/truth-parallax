## Buddhist-Chinese Reading

The upper Xi Ci Zhuan is the most philosophically significant Wing for the Buddhist-Chinese reading, as its cosmological concepts provided Chinese Buddhist thinkers with a vocabulary for expressing Mahayana philosophical ideas. The concept of *Taiji* was sometimes identified with the Buddhist *dharmakaya* (truth body), while the yin-yang dynamic was compared to the interplay of *samsara* and *nirvana*, *form* and *emptiness*. The Huayan philosopher Fazang explicitly drew on *Yijing* cosmology in his philosophical writings.
