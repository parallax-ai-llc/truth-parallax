## Comparative Reading

The lower Xiang Zhuan's moral reading of natural symbols invites comparison with the broader cross-cultural phenomenon of symbolic interpretation. The assumption that the physical world carries moral meaning connects with the Jewish *mashal* (parable), the Christian tradition of reading nature allegorically, and the Islamic concept of creation as a divine text requiring interpretation. Comparative analysis examines the philosophical presuppositions underlying these different hermeneutical traditions.
