## Comparative Reading

The lower Xi Ci Zhuan's philosophy of change invites comparison with Heraclitus's *panta rhei* (everything flows), the Buddhist teaching of *anicca* (impermanence), the Hindu concept of cosmic cycles, and Whitehead's process philosophy. Each tradition grapples with the relationship between change and permanence, becoming and being, and the possibility of finding stable ground in a world of constant transformation. The *Yijing*'s distinctive contribution is its positive valuation of change as the expression of cosmic order rather than its negation.
