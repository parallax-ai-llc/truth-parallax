---
title: "Ten Wings - Chapter 5"
scripture: "ten-wings"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Heaven is lofty and honourable; earth is low. Thus the Creative and the Receptive are determined.

**2** In correspondence with this difference between low and high, inferior and superior places are established.

**3** Movement and rest have their definite laws; according to these, firm and yielding lines are differentiated.

**4** Events follow definite trends, each according to its nature. Things are distinguished from one another in definite classes.

**5** In this way good fortune and misfortune come about. In heaven phenomena take form; on earth shapes take form. In this way change and transformation become manifest.

**6** Therefore the eight trigrams succeed one another by turns, as the firm and the yielding displace each other.

**7** Things are aroused by thunder and lightning; they are fertilized by wind and rain. Sun and moon follow their courses, and it is now hot, now cold.

**8** The way of the Creative brings about the male. The way of the Receptive brings about the female.

**9** The Creative knows the great beginnings. The Receptive completes the finished things.

**10** The Creative knows through the easy. The Receptive can do things through the simple.

**11** What is easy, is easy to know; what is simple, is easy to follow. He who is easy to know attains fealty. He who is easy to follow attains works.

**12** He who possesses attachment can endure for long; he who possesses works can become great. To endure is the disposition of the sage; greatness is the field of action of the sage.

**13** By means of the easy and the simple we grasp the laws of the whole world. When the laws of the whole world are grasped, therein lies perfection.

**14** The Book of Changes contains the measure of heaven and earth; therefore it enables us to comprehend the Tao of heaven and earth and its order.

**15** Looking upward, we contemplate with its help the signs in the heavens; looking down, we examine the lines of the earth. Thus we come to know the circumstances of the dark and the light.

**16** Going back to the beginnings of things and pursuing them to the end, we come to know the lessons of birth and death.

**17** The union of seed and power produces all things; the escape of the soul brings about change. Through this we come to know the conditions of outgoing and returning spirits.

**18** Since in this way man comes to resemble heaven and earth, he is not in conflict with them. His wisdom embraces all things, and his Tao brings order into the whole world; therefore he does not err.

**19** He is active everywhere but does not allow himself to be carried away. He rejoices in heaven and has knowledge of fate; therefore he is free of care.

**20** He is content with his circumstances and genuine in his kindness; therefore he can practise love.
