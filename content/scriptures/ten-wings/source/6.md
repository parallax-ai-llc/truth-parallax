---
title: "Ten Wings - Chapter 6"
scripture: "ten-wings"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The Master said: "The Book of Changes, what does it do? The Book of Changes opens things up, accomplishes the task, and encompasses all Ways under heaven."

**2** This and nothing else is what it does. Thus the holy sages used it to penetrate the wills of all under heaven, to determine all fields of action under heaven, and to settle all doubts under heaven.

**3** Therefore the nature of the yarrow stalks is round and spiritual. The nature of the hexagrams is square and wise. The meaning of the six lines changes in order to furnish information.

**4** In this way the holy sages cleansed their hearts, withdrew, and hid in the secret. They brought good fortune and misfortune into common lot with the people.

**5** They were divine, so they foreknew. They were wise, so they stored up experiences. Who is it that could do all this? Only those of old who were sharp of perception and wise, spiritual and clear-sighted — they were the ones.

**6** Therefore they fathomed the Tao of heaven and understood the conditions of men. Thus they invented these divine things in order to meet the needs of the people.

**7** The Master said: "He who knows the Tao of change and transformation knows the action of the gods."

**8** The Book of Changes has four aspects corresponding to the Way of the sages. With regard to speech, we are guided by the judgments; with regard to action, by the changes; with regard to making implements, by the images; with regard to prophecy, by the oracles.

**9** Therefore the superior man, whenever he has to make or to do something, consults the Book of Changes in words, and it receives his communications like an echo.

**10** It is without distance, without remoteness. It brings about all events under heaven. If one is not the most spirit-like man on earth, how can one comprehend its meaning?

**11** The Changes is a book from which one may not hold aloof. Its Tao is forever changing — alteration, movement without rest, flowing through the six empty places; rising and sinking without fixed law.

**12** Firm and yielding transform each other. They cannot be confined within a rule; it is only change that is at work here.

**13** They come and go, following definite laws. Within and without, they teach caution.

**14** They also show care and sorrow and their causes. Though you have no teacher, approach them as you would your parents.

**15** First take up the words, ponder their meaning, and then the fixed rules will reveal themselves. But if you are not the right man, the meaning will not manifest itself to you.
