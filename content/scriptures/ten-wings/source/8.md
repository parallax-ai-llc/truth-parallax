---
title: "Ten Wings - Chapter 8"
scripture: "ten-wings"
chapter: 8
---

**1** In ancient times the holy sages made the Book of Changes thus: they wished to be in accord with the principles of inner nature and of fate.

**2** Therefore they determined the Tao of heaven and called it the dark and the light. They determined the Tao of the earth and called it the yielding and the firm. They determined the Tao of man and called it love and rectitude.

**3** They combined these three fundamental powers and doubled them; therefore in the Book of Changes a sign is always formed by six lines.

**4** The positions are divided into the dark and the light. The firm and the yielding lines occupy these places in alternation. Therefore the Book of Changes has six places which constitute a line figure.

**5** Heaven and earth determine the scene, and the changes take effect within it.

**6** The perfected nature of man, sustaining itself, is the gateway of Tao and of righteousness.

**7** The sage discovers the mysteries of heaven and earth in these representations. In the definiteness of the lines he finds the analogies for all phenomena.

**8** God comes forth in the sign of the Arousing; he brings all things to completion in the sign of the Gentle; he causes creatures to perceive one another in the sign of the Clinging; he causes them to serve one another in the sign of the Receptive.

**9** He gives them joy in the sign of the Joyous; he battles in the sign of the Creative; he toils in the sign of the Abysmal; and he brings them to perfection in the sign of Keeping Still.

**10** All living things owe their beginning to the Creative. Therefore it is heaven. They receive form from the Receptive. Therefore it is the earth.
