## Neo-Confucian Reading

The lower Tuan Zhuan is read by Neo-Confucian interpreters as demonstrating the universality of *li* across all domains of experience. Cheng Yi's famous statement that '*li* is one but its manifestations are many' finds illustration in the varied situations described by the hexagram judgments, each revealing a different aspect of the single cosmic principle. The Neo-Confucian reader approaches these commentaries as exercises in *gewu* (investigation of things) — the systematic study of phenomena to apprehend the principle they embody.
