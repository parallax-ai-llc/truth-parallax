## Neo-Confucian Reading

The Neo-Confucian reading of the upper Xiang Zhuan transforms the Commentary on the Images into a guide for what Zhu Xi called *jing* (reverent attentiveness) — the practice of maintaining constant awareness of moral principle in every situation. The natural images of the hexagrams are understood as expressions of *li* in the natural world, and the *junzi*'s response to these images models the Neo-Confucian ideal of aligning human conduct with cosmic principle through disciplined moral reflection.
