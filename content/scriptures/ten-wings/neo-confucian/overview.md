The Neo-Confucian reading of the Ten Wings, developed by Song Dynasty philosophers such as Zhou Dunyi, Zhang Zai, Cheng Yi, Cheng Hao, and Zhu Xi, finds in the Wings the metaphysical foundations for a comprehensive philosophical system. The concept of *Taiji* (Supreme Ultimate) introduced in the Great Commentary becomes the cornerstone of Neo-Confucian cosmology, interpreted as the primordial principle from which yin-yang dynamics and the myriad things emerge.

Zhu Xi's synthesis — which became the orthodox interpretation for the imperial examination system — reads the Ten Wings as articulating the relationship between *li* (principle/pattern) and *qi* (material force/energy) that structures all reality. The Wings' language about the *Dao* of Heaven, Earth, and Humanity is understood as describing a unified moral-metaphysical order in which ethical cultivation is simultaneously the perfection of one's nature and the realization of cosmic principle.
