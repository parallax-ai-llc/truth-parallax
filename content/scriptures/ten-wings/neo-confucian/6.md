## Neo-Confucian Reading

The lower Xi Ci Zhuan provides the Neo-Confucian tradition with its most important statements about the relationship between metaphysical principle and ethical practice. The passage 'What is above form is called the Dao; what is below form is called a vessel' establishes the fundamental Neo-Confucian distinction between *li* (formless principle) and *qi* (formed matter). The Neo-Confucian reader finds in these passages the philosophical foundation for a worldview that is simultaneously metaphysical and ethical.
