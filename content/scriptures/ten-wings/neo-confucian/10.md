## Neo-Confucian Reading

The Neo-Confucian reading of the Za Gua Zhuan appreciates its terse, paired descriptions as exercises in the discernment of *li* — the perception of the specific principle operating in each situation. The ability to distinguish between hexagrams that appear similar but embody different principles is understood as a model for the moral discernment required in daily life. Zhu Xi's commentaries on the Za Gua emphasize the importance of precise discrimination in both philosophical understanding and ethical judgment.
