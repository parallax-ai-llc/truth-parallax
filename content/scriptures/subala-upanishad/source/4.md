---
title: "Subala Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "subala-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** "But those who depart from this world, having known the Self and these true desires — for them there is freedom in all the worlds.

**2** "Now, in the body, this very Self is the knowing Self. Encased in the heart, surrounded by the sense organs, the Person made of knowledge moves about in the intermediate space. He becomes whatever form he assumes.

**3** "He, being associated with the body, the senses, and the mind, is called the knower of the field. Without any association, he is called the supreme Self.

**4** "This Self, verily, is the lord of all beings, the king of all beings. Just as all the spokes are contained in the hub and the rim of a wheel, even so are all beings, all gods, all worlds, all sense organs, all selves contained in this Self.

**5** "This is the Self, the ruler of all, the lord of all. He does not become greater by good actions nor smaller by evil actions. He is the lord of all, the ruler of beings, the protector of beings.

**6** "He is the bridge that holds these worlds apart so that they do not merge. Him the Brahmins seek to know through the study of the Vedas, through sacrifice, through charity, through penance, through fasting.

**7** "Knowing Him alone, a man becomes a sage. Desiring Him alone as their world, monks renounce their homes.

**8** "Verily, the ancient sages, knowing this, did not desire offspring, for they thought: what shall we do with offspring, we who have this Self, this world?

**9** "They, having risen above the desire for sons, the desire for wealth, and the desire for worlds, led the life of a mendicant. For the desire for sons is the desire for wealth, and the desire for wealth is the desire for worlds; both these are merely desires.

**10** "This Self is 'not this, not this.' It is incomprehensible, for it cannot be comprehended. It is indestructible, for it cannot be destroyed. It is unattached, for it does not attach itself. It is unfettered, it does not suffer, it is not injured. Him who knows this, these two thoughts do not overcome: 'For this reason I did evil,' and 'For this reason I did good.' He overcomes them both. What he has done and what he has not done do not affect him.

**11** "This is the eternal glory of the knower of Brahman: it neither increases nor decreases by action. Therefore one should know the nature of that alone. Finding that, one is not touched by evil action.

**12** "Therefore he who knows this, having become calm, self-controlled, withdrawn, patient, and collected, sees the Self in himself, sees all in the Self. Evil does not overcome him, he overcomes all evil. Evil does not burn him, he burns all evil.

**13** "Free from evil, free from impurity, free from doubt, he becomes a knower of Brahman. This is the world of Brahman, and you have attained it." Thus spoke Brahma to Subala.

**14** "Om. Peace, peace, peace."
