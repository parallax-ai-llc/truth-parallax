---
title: "Subala Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "subala-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** "Now, the resolution of the elements. The earth dissolves into water, water into fire, fire into air, air into ether, ether into the sense organs, the sense organs into the subtle elements, the subtle elements into the primal cause, the primal cause into Brahman.

**2** "There are two forms of Brahman: the gross and the subtle, the mortal and the immortal, the limited and the unlimited, the defined and the undefined.

**3** "The gross form is that which is other than air and ether. It is mortal, it is limited, it is defined. The subtle form is air and ether. It is immortal, it is unlimited, it is undefined.

**4** "Now with respect to the body: the gross form is that which is other than breath and the space within the self. It is mortal, it is limited, it is defined. The subtle form is the breath and the space within the self. It is immortal, it is unlimited, it is undefined.

**5** "The person in the right eye is Indra, and the person in the left eye is Indrani. Their abode of union is the space within the heart. Their food is the red lump within the heart.

**6** "There is a covering within the heart that looks like a lotus. Within that there is a small space. In that small space there is ether. This ether within the heart is as vast as the space outside.

**7** "Within it are contained both heaven and earth, both fire and air, both sun and moon, lightning and stars, whatever exists and whatever does not exist — all that is contained within it.

**8** "If it is asked: when everything is contained in this space within the heart, when old age overtakes the body, or when the body decays, what is it that does not decay?

**9** "The answer is: this space within the heart does not grow old with the ageing of the body, nor does it die with the death of the body. This is the true city of Brahman. In it all desires are contained.

**10** "This is the Self, free from sin, free from old age, free from death, free from grief, free from hunger, free from thirst. Its desire is truth, its resolve is truth. He who departs from this world without having known the Self and these true desires — for him there is no freedom in all the worlds."
