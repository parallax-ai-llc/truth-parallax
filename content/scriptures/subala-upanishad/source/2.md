---
title: "Subala Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "subala-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** "Now then the characteristics of Brahman. It is neither existent nor non-existent, nor both existent and non-existent. It is neither different nor non-different, nor both different and non-different.

**2** "It is not within, it is not without, it is not above, it is not below. It cannot be said to be 'this' or 'not this.'

**3** "It is beyond the range of mind and speech. It is a mass of intelligence and bliss. It is Brahman, the supreme.

**4** "Just as a spider projects and retracts its threads, just as herbs grow on the earth, just as hairs grow on a living body, so from the imperishable arises this universe.

**5** "Brahman expanded by means of austerity, and from it primal matter was produced. From matter came the life force, mind, the elements, the worlds, and from works, immortality.

**6** "He who is omniscient and all-knowing, whose austerity consists of knowledge — from Him are produced this Brahman, name, form, and food.

**7** "Two birds, beautiful of wing, close companions, cling to one common tree. Of the two, one eats the sweet fruit; the other, not eating, watches.

**8** "On the same tree, a person sits grieving, immersed, bewildered by his own impotence. But when he sees the other — the Lord — contented, and knows his glory, then his grief passes away.

**9** "When the seer sees the brilliant maker and lord — that Person who has his source in Brahman — then he is wise, and shaking off good and evil, he reaches the highest unity, free from stain.

**10** "He indeed is life that shines forth in all beings. Understanding this, the wise man goes beyond idle talk. He delights in the Self, he revels in the Self. And having performed his works, he is the greatest of those who know Brahman."
