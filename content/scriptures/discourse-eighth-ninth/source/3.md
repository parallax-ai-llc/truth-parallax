---
title: "Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth Section 3"
scripture: "discourse-eighth-ninth"
chapter: 3
---

**1** My father, from the seventh I send you a greeting. I, the mind, understand. I see the eighth, with the souls that are in it, singing praise to the Father in silence.

**2** For the eighth reveals the ninth, and the mind becomes the servant of the vision. And I know the power that is within me, and the illumination that comes from above.

**3** And the father said: My son, the mystery of the eighth and the ninth cannot be spoken with the tongue of flesh. But the mind that has received the spirit of the divine can behold it.

**4** For the Ogdoad and the Ennead are the places of the true worship. In the seventh, the souls prepare themselves. In the eighth, they behold the truth. In the ninth, they become one with the divine.

**5** I rejoice, my father, because I see the truth. I see the minds receiving nourishment, and the souls singing in joy. And I see the whole pleroma rejoicing.

**6** My father, I see the movement of the All. I see myself in the mind. I see the universe in the mind. I see myself in you. I see the All in you.

**7** My son, behold how the eighth sings praise through the souls, and the angels praise together with them. And the mind sings praise in harmony with all of them.

**8** My father, I too wish to sing praise from within my heart, which is drawn toward you. For you are the boundary of the eighth, and I have received power from you.

**9** Yes, my son. When you become divine, you will see God. For God makes himself visible only to those who have been purified in heart and mind.

**10** The father said: My son, write this discourse in a book. For the initiates will need this teaching. Keep it in the inner sanctuary and reveal it only to the first-born. For this is the mystery of the eighth and the ninth.

**11** I shall write it, my father, and I shall guard it. For you have given me the vision which the tongue cannot speak, the ineffable mystery of the Ogdoad and the Ennead.

**12** The Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth.
