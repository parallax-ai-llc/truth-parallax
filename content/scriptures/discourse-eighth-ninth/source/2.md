---
title: "Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth Section 2"
scripture: "discourse-eighth-ninth"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Let us pray, my father. I call upon you, who rules over the kingdom of power, whose word is birth of light. And his words are immortal. They are eternal and unchanging.

**2** He is the one whose will begets life for the forms everywhere. His nature gives form to substance. By him the souls of the Ogdoad and the Ennead are moved.

**3** Let us pray together in silence. And when they had finished praying, they cried out: Father, Father, Father of the Light, I have seen. I speak. I have seen.

**4** The Father answered: How shall I sing a hymn through this body? For the voice and the tongue which give utterance cannot tell of that which is comprehended only by the mind.

**5** Let us sing a hymn in silence. Let us sing it through the mind. For the mind is the instrument of the eighth.

**6** I see the eighth and the souls that are in it, singing a hymn in silence to the ninth. And I myself prepare to write this discourse in hieroglyphic characters and to inscribe it upon the wall of the temple.

**7** Let him inscribe this discourse upon steles of turquoise, in hieroglyphic characters. And he shall place them in his temple. He shall appoint eight guardians with nine guards.

**8** The male ones on the right are frog-faced and the female ones on the left are cat-faced. And he shall put a square milk-stone at the base and write the name of the discourse upon the turquoise tablet.

**9** My son, it would be proper to write this discourse on steles of turquoise, in hieroglyphic characters. For Mind himself has become overseer of these. Therefore I command that this teaching be carved on stone and that you place it in my sanctuary.

**10** I have called upon you and I have found you. I have been saved through your grace and power. I know you. I know myself, and I worship you, Father of all things.
