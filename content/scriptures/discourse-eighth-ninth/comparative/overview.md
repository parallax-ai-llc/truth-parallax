## Overview

The Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth describes a mystical experience that finds close parallels across the world's contemplative traditions. The progressive ascent through heavenly spheres parallels the Jewish Hekhalot mystic's passage through the seven heavenly palaces, the Islamic mi'raj (Muhammad's night journey through the heavens), and the Tibetan Buddhist's traversal of the bardo realms. The ecstatic dimension — tears, wordless prayer, dissolution of ordinary consciousness — parallels the Sufi experience of hal (mystical state), the Hindu experience of samadhi, and the Christian contemplative's 'prayer of quiet.'
