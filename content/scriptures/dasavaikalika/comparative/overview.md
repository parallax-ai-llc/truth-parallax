## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Dasavaikalika reveals fascinating parallels with monastic rule literature across religious traditions. The text provides rich material for understanding how different traditions organize ascetic life and cultivate spiritual discipline.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Monastic Rules Compared

The Dasavaikalika's prescriptions for daily conduct parallel the Buddhist Vinaya, the Rule of St. Benedict, Hindu dharmasutras for sannyasins, and Sufi manuals of discipline. Each tradition addresses similar practical challenges of communal ascetic life.

### Mindfulness Traditions

The text's emphasis on careful, mindful conduct in every action -- walking, speaking, eating, receiving -- parallels Buddhist sati practice, Christian mindfulness traditions, and Islamic muraqaba.

### Ascetic Ideals

The portrait of the ideal monk in the Dasavaikalika can be compared with Buddhist arhat ideals, Christian monastic perfection, and Hindu sannyasi ideals.

## Comparative Framework

| Practice | Jainism (Dasavaikalika) | Buddhism (Vinaya) | Christianity (Benedict) |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Daily structure | Ten evening topics | Patimokkha recitation | Hours of prayer |
| Food rules | Strict begging rules | Alms round | Communal meals |
| Mindfulness | Care in all action | Sati | Ora et labora |
