---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 7"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The monk should understand the nature of the passions and their progressive stages, for the conquest of the passions is the central task of spiritual practice.

**2** Each passion — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — exists in four degrees of intensity: the most intense, intense, mild, and the mildest.

**3** The most intense degree of anger is like a line carved in stone — it lasts for a very long time and is very difficult to erase.

**4** The intense degree of anger is like a line drawn in sand — it lasts for some time but is eventually erased by the wind.

**5** The mild degree of anger is like a line drawn on water — it appears briefly and then vanishes.

**6** The mildest degree of anger is like a line drawn in air — it appears for only an instant and leaves no trace.

**7** Similarly, the other three passions exist in these four degrees. The monk should work to reduce the intensity of his passions step by step.

**8** First he should overcome the most intense passions. Then the intense. Then the mild. Finally, the mildest. This is the gradual path of spiritual progress.

**9** The complete destruction of all four passions in all four degrees constitutes the twelfth stage of spiritual development — the stage from which omniscience can be attained.

**10** Let the monk not be discouraged by the difficulty of this task. With persistent effort, with the support of the sangha, and with the grace of the teaching, the passions can be conquered.
