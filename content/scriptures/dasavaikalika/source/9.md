---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 9"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The monk should understand the importance of equanimity, Samata, in all circumstances, for equanimity is the hallmark of spiritual maturity.

**2** When praised, the monk should not be elated. When blamed, he should not be depressed. Praise and blame are both transient and do not touch the soul.

**3** When honoured, the monk should not feel proud. When humiliated, he should not feel shame. Honour and humiliation belong to the body and the social persona, not to the soul.

**4** When given delicious food, the monk should not feel pleasure. When given poor food, he should not feel displeasure. All food sustains the body equally.

**5** When in health, the monk should not feel complacent. When in sickness, he should not feel distressed. Both health and sickness are conditions of the body, not of the soul.

**6** When in the company of the wise, the monk should not feel superior. When in the company of the foolish, he should not feel inferior. All souls are equal in their essence.

**7** Equanimity is the steady state of a mind that is free from the disturbance of the passions. It is not indifference but the highest form of engagement.

**8** The equanimous monk responds to every situation with wisdom and compassion, neither drawn by desire nor repelled by aversion.

**9** He is like the deep ocean — waves may rise on its surface, but its depths remain calm and undisturbed.

**10** This equanimity is the fruit of long practice and the sign of genuine spiritual attainment. Let the monk cultivate it daily, in every circumstance, until it becomes his natural state.
