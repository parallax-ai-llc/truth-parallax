---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 5"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 5
---

**1** The monk should understand the proper use of the three instruments of action: body, speech, and mind.

**2** The body should be used for righteous purposes: walking carefully, sitting in meditation, performing acts of service, and enduring hardships with equanimity.

**3** The body should not be used for violence, for sensual indulgence, or for any activity that causes harm to others.

**4** Speech should be used for truthful, kind, and beneficial communication: teaching the dharma, reciting the scriptures, asking forgiveness, and expressing compassion.

**5** Speech should not be used for lying, for harsh words, for gossip, or for any communication that causes harm to others.

**6** The mind should be used for meditation, for the contemplation of the soul, for the study of the scriptures, and for the cultivation of the virtues.

**7** The mind should not be used for plotting harm, for harbouring resentment, for indulging in sensual fantasies, or for any thought that increases the passions.

**8** The proper use of body, speech, and mind generates merit and sheds karma. The improper use generates sin and accumulates karma.

**9** The monk should be constantly aware of how he is using these three instruments. He should correct himself immediately when he notices misuse.

**10** He who has mastered the three instruments of action has mastered himself. He is in control of his own destiny, for it is through these instruments that karma is generated and shed.
