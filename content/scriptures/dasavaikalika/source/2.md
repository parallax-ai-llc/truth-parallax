---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 2"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The monk should understand the five types of beings and should extend his non-violence to all of them.

**2** Earth-bodies are beings whose bodies are composed of earth. They exist in soil, clay, minerals, and stones. They possess only the sense of touch.

**3** Water-bodies are beings whose bodies are composed of water. They exist in rivers, lakes, rain, and dew. They too possess only the sense of touch.

**4** Fire-bodies are beings whose bodies are composed of fire. They exist in flames, lightning, and hot embers. They possess only the sense of touch.

**5** Air-bodies are beings whose bodies are composed of air. They exist in wind, breezes, and storms. They possess only the sense of touch.

**6** Plant-bodies are beings whose bodies are composed of plant matter. They exist in trees, bushes, grasses, and all forms of vegetation. They possess only the sense of touch.

**7** These five types of one-sensed beings are invisible to the ordinary eye, but the wise monk knows of their existence and takes care not to harm them.

**8** He walks carefully, lest he harm earth-bodies. He strains his water, lest he harm water-bodies. He does not kindle fire, lest he harm fire-bodies. He does not fan air, lest he harm air-bodies.

**9** He eats only what is offered and what involves the minimum of harm to plant-bodies. He is aware that every act of eating involves some violence, and he minimizes it.

**10** The monk who extends his compassion to all five types of beings, including the most humble one-sensed creatures, practises the highest form of non-violence.
