---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 8"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The monk should understand the nature of penance and its role in the spiritual life.

**2** Penance is not self-punishment. It is the voluntary acceptance of hardship for the purpose of shedding accumulated karma and strengthening the soul.

**3** The wise monk does not practise penance out of guilt or self-hatred. He practises it out of love for the soul and the desire for its liberation.

**4** The external penances — fasting, reducing food intake, limiting food varieties, giving up delicacies, sitting in lonely places, and bodily mortification — weaken the hold of the body upon the soul.

**5** The internal penances — repentance, reverence, service, study, meditation, and renunciation of the body — strengthen the soul's dominion over the mind.

**6** Both external and internal penances should be practised together, for without internal penance, external penance alone becomes mere physical endurance.

**7** The monk should not compete with others in penance. He should not boast of his austerities. He should practise in secret, known only to himself and his teacher.

**8** The true test of penance is not the severity of the hardship endured but the equanimity with which it is endured. He who suffers with equanimity gains more than he who suffers with complaint.

**9** Penance should be suited to the individual's capacity. It should be neither too severe nor too mild. The guru helps the monk determine the appropriate level of penance.

**10** He who practises penance wisely and consistently sheds his karma steadily, like a tree that sheds its leaves in autumn. In time, the soul stands bare and free, shining with its own light.
