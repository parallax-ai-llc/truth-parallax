---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 3"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The monk should understand the proper manner of begging for alms, for the acceptance of food is a matter of great spiritual significance.

**2** The monk should go out for alms after sunrise and return before sunset. He should not eat during the night, for in darkness, insects may contaminate the food unseen.

**3** He should approach the houses of laypeople with dignity and humility, standing at the door without calling attention to himself.

**4** If food is offered, he should accept it with gratitude. If food is not offered, he should move on without complaint or resentment.

**5** He should not accept food that has been specially prepared for him, for this would make the violence of preparation attributable to the monk.

**6** He should not accept food that is obtained through violence, deceit, or coercion. He should accept only food that is obtained through righteous means.

**7** He should not accept more food than he needs. He should eat only enough to sustain the body, without indulgence or attachment to taste.

**8** Before eating, the monk should inspect the food carefully to ensure that it is free from insects and other living beings.

**9** He should eat in silence, with mindfulness, reflecting upon the purpose of eating — the sustenance of the body for spiritual practice.

**10** The proper manner of accepting alms is itself a form of spiritual practice. It cultivates humility, non-attachment, and compassion in both the monk and the householder who gives.
