---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 6"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The monk should understand the importance of study, Svadhyaya, as a daily practice essential to spiritual progress.

**2** Study consists of five activities: reading the scriptures, asking questions about the meaning, reviewing what has been learned, reciting the texts, and reflecting upon the teaching.

**3** Reading the scriptures acquaints the monk with the teaching of the Jinas. It provides the foundation of right knowledge.

**4** Asking questions about the meaning deepens the monk's understanding. It prevents superficial knowledge and promotes genuine insight.

**5** Reviewing what has been learned strengthens the monk's memory and prevents the teaching from fading. Regular review is essential.

**6** Reciting the texts preserves them for future generations. The oral transmission of the scriptures is a sacred duty of the monastic community.

**7** Reflecting upon the teaching transforms intellectual knowledge into experiential understanding. It is the bridge between knowing and being.

**8** Study should be practised daily, at fixed times, with regularity and discipline. It should not be neglected for any reason.

**9** The monk who neglects study gradually loses his understanding of the path. His practice becomes mechanical, and his progress stalls.

**10** The monk who studies regularly grows in wisdom day by day. His understanding deepens, his practice matures, and his soul approaches liberation.
