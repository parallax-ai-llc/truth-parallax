---
title: "Dasavaikalika - Chapter 1"
scripture: "dasavaikalika"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Dharma is the highest good. Ahimsa, Samyama, and Tapa — non-violence, self-restraint, and austerity — these are the essence of dharma. The mind that is always immersed in dharma feels great joy and bliss.

**2** The monk who understands the nature of the soul and practises the discipline of non-violence has taken the first step on the path of liberation.

**3** Just as a person who has crossed the river does not return to the dangerous current, so the monk who has crossed over to the path of dharma should not return to the life of passion.

**4** The wise monk should be like a bee that takes the nectar from flowers without harming the flower. He should take his food from the householders without causing them any burden.

**5** He should eat only what is given freely, only what is not specially prepared for him, and only during the permitted hours of daylight.

**6** He should not be greedy for food, nor should he be attached to particular tastes. He should eat only to sustain the body for spiritual practice.

**7** The monk should wander from place to place, not staying too long in any one location, lest attachment to a place develop.

**8** He should endure cold, heat, hunger, thirst, and insect bites with equanimity, regarding these hardships as opportunities for the shedding of karma.

**9** He should guard his speech, speaking only what is true, necessary, and kind. He should guard his mind, thinking only what is pure and beneficial.

**10** This is the first lesson of the Dasavaikalika: the foundations of monastic discipline, without which no progress on the path of liberation is possible.
