## Overview

The Digambara tradition recognizes the Dasavaikalika's importance as a monastic manual while maintaining their own more rigorous standards of ascetic practice. The text's emphasis on discipline and mindful conduct resonates with Digambara values, even as Digambara monks follow stricter rules regarding clothing and possessions.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Higher Standard

Digambara interpreters read the Dasavaikalika's prescriptions as a baseline that the Digambara tradition exceeds. Where the text prescribes carefulness in handling clothing and bowls, Digambara monks have renounced these entirely.

### Shared Principles

Despite differences in practice, the underlying principles of the Dasavaikalika -- non-harm, mindfulness, self-restraint, endurance -- are fully shared by the Digambara tradition.

### Ascetic Ideal

The text's portrait of the ideal monk as one who exercises constant vigilance over mind, speech, and body aligns perfectly with the Digambara ascetic ideal.
