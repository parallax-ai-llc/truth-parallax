## The Promise of Return to the Pleroma

Genesis 50, the final chapter of Genesis, recounts Jacob's burial in Canaan, the brothers' fear after their father's death, and Joseph's assurance of forgiveness, concluding with Joseph's death and his command that his bones be carried out of Egypt. For the Gnostic reader, this chapter is eschatological — it addresses the ultimate destiny of the divine sparks imprisoned in matter and the promise that they will be gathered and returned to their origin.

## Jacob's Funeral: The Body Returns

Jacob's elaborate embalming and the great procession to bury him in Canaan (50:1-14) enact the pneumatic's partial return. The body — the material garment — is carried from Egypt back to the land of promise. In Gnostic terms, even the material vessel is restored to the territory associated with divine blessing, removed from the Demiurge's core domain. The forty days of embalming and seventy days of mourning carry numerological significance: forty (the period of testing and transformation) and seventy (the totality of the cosmic order) together represent the full cycle of material existence that must be completed before return is possible.

## "You Meant Evil, But God Meant It for Good"

Joseph's great declaration to his fearful brothers — "As for you, you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good, to bring it about that many people should be kept alive" (50:20) — is the definitive Gnostic reframing of the entire patriarchal narrative. The archonic agents (the brothers, Potiphar, the prison, Pharaoh) acted from ignorance, jealousy, and material motivation. But the transcendent God — operating above and through the archonic system — transformed every act of material evil into an instrument of pneumatic purpose.

This is the Gnostic doctrine of *pronoia* (divine forethought) at its most comprehensive. The Demiurge and his agents believe they act freely, but their actions serve a purpose they cannot perceive. The *Trimorphic Protennoia* from Nag Hammadi expresses this same principle: the transcendent divine works through three descents (thought, speech, and the Word), penetrating the archonic world to rescue the divine sparks.

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Jacob is buried in Canaan as he requested | The pneumatic patriarch's body is restored to the territory of promise |
| The brothers fear Joseph's retribution | The psychic types fear judgment from the pneumatic after the patriarch's death |
| "God meant it for good" | Transcendent *pronoia* operates through archonic evil |
| Joseph forgives and sustains his brothers | The pneumatic preserves the psychic types within the material world |
| Joseph commands his bones be carried out | The pneumatic refuses permanent burial in the material realm |
| Genesis ends in Egypt | The spiritual community remains in the Demiurge's domain, awaiting liberation |

## Joseph's Bones: The Unfinished Return

Joseph's final command — "God will surely visit you, and you shall carry up my bones from here" (50:25) — is the promise that the pneumatic's return is not yet complete. Joseph dies in Egypt. His body remains there, embalmed in a coffin (50:26). The divine spark has completed its work within the material world but the full return — the exodus from the Demiurge's realm — awaits a future act of divine intervention.

This open ending is profoundly Gnostic. Genesis does not conclude with restoration but with the pneumatic community still embedded in matter, sustained by a promise. The gathering of the sparks is underway but incomplete. The soul knows its origin, possesses gnosis of the divine plan, and has received the assurance of return — but the actual return to the Pleroma is deferred. The exodus to come (the book of Exodus) will begin the journey, but in Gnostic eschatology, the ultimate return occurs only at the consummation of all things, when every divine spark has been gathered from the material world and the Pleroma is restored to its original fullness.

## The Final Verse

"So Joseph died, being 110 years old. They embalmed him, and he was put in a coffin in Egypt" (50:26). Genesis ends with a coffin in Egypt. The pneumatic is dead, embalmed, and confined in the material realm. Yet the promise stands. The bones will be carried out. The spark is not extinguished. The return is assured.

## Key Concepts

- **Pronoia**: The transcendent God's forethought operating through archonic evil to accomplish pneumatic purposes.
- **Deferred return**: The pneumatic community remains in the material world, sustained by the promise of future liberation.
- **The bones**: Joseph's command as the insistence that the material remnant of the pneumatic will not remain permanently in the Demiurge's domain.
