## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Genesis represents one of the most radical re-readings of any biblical text in history. Rather than reading Genesis as the story of a benevolent God creating a good world, Gnostic traditions invert the narrative: the creator god (Demiurge) is an ignorant or malevolent being, the material world is a prison, the serpent is a liberator, and humanity's true origin lies in a transcendent divine realm (Pleroma) far above the creator.

## The Gnostic Inversion

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| God is the supreme, good Creator | The Creator (Demiurge/Yaldabaoth) is ignorant or evil |
| Creation is "very good" | The material world is a flawed prison |
| The serpent is Satan/deceiver | The serpent brings salvific knowledge (*gnosis*) |
| The Fall is disobedience | The "Fall" is awakening to divine knowledge |
| Expulsion is punishment | Expulsion is the Demiurge's fear of enlightened humanity |
| Death is consequence of sin | Physical death releases the divine spark |

## Key Gnostic Texts on Genesis

### Nag Hammadi Library (discovered 1945)
- **The Apocryphon of John** (*Secret Book of John*): The most detailed Gnostic cosmogony, rewriting Genesis 1-7
- **On the Origin of the World**: Alternative creation account with Sophia and the archons
- **The Hypostasis of the Archons** (*Reality of the Rulers*): Genesis 1-6 retold from the archons' perspective
- **The Gospel of Philip**: Sacramental interpretation of Eden imagery
- **The Testimony of Truth**: The serpent as Christ figure

### Other Sources
- **Irenaeus, *Against Heresies***: Polemical but detailed account of Valentinian Genesis exegesis
- **Epiphanius, *Panarion***: Descriptions of various Gnostic readings of Genesis

## Gnostic Cosmogony

The Gnostic creation myth, as synthesized from multiple texts:

1. **The Pleroma**: The true, transcendent divine realm of Aeons
2. **Sophia's error**: The lowest Aeon, Sophia, produces offspring without her consort
3. **Yaldabaoth**: The Demiurge, Sophia's malformed offspring, declares "I am God and there is no other" (cf. Isaiah 45:5)
4. **Material creation**: Yaldabaoth and his archons create the material world in ignorance
5. **Adam's creation**: The archons fashion Adam's body, but the divine spark enters from above
6. **Eden as prison**: The Garden is not paradise but a place of ignorance
7. **The serpent as revealer**: Brings *gnosis* that the Demiurge sought to withhold
8. **Humanity's task**: To remember their true divine origin and escape the material world

## Major Gnostic Schools

| School | Founder/Era | Distinctive Reading |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Sethian | 2nd-3rd century | Seth as the spiritual ancestor of Gnostics |
| Valentinian | Valentinus, 2nd century | Sophisticated Pleroma mythology, three types of humans |
| Ophite/Naassene | 2nd century | Serpent (*ophis*) worship, serpent as Christ |
| Mandaean | Pre-3rd century | Ongoing tradition, Adam as first prophet of light |
| Manichaean | Mani, 3rd century | Dualistic cosmology, particles of light trapped in matter |

## Theological Significance

Gnostic readings of Genesis raise fundamental questions:
- **The problem of evil**: If God is good and all-powerful, why does evil exist?
- **The nature of the material world**: Is matter inherently good or a trap?
- **Knowledge vs. obedience**: Is blind obedience or critical knowledge the path to the divine?
- **Authority**: Who has the right to interpret sacred texts?
