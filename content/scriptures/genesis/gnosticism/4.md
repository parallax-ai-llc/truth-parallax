## Cain and Abel

Genesis 4 recounts the first murder — Cain slaying Abel — followed by Cain's exile and the birth of Seth. For Gnostic interpreters, this chapter introduces the fundamental typology of human spiritual natures and establishes the Sethian lineage that would become central to an entire school of Gnostic thought.

## The Orthodox Reading vs. the Gnostic Inversion

| Orthodox Reading | Gnostic Reading |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Cain and Abel are Adam and Eve's sons | Cain is the offspring of the archons or the Demiurge |
| Cain's offering is rejected for lack of faith | The Demiurge arbitrarily favors blood sacrifice |
| Abel is the righteous victim | Abel represents the psychic (soul-level) human |
| Cain's punishment is just | Cain is marked by the Demiurge as a tool of material power |
| Seth continues Adam's line | Seth is the bearer of the divine spark, ancestor of the Gnostics |

## Nag Hammadi Parallels

### The Apocryphon of John (NHC II,1)

The *Apocryphon of John* provides a radical rewriting of Cain and Abel's origins. In this text, Yaldabaoth rapes Eve, producing Cain and Abel (here named Eloim and Yave). These sons are not truly Adam's children but the Demiurge's attempt to implant his own progeny among humanity. This explains why Cain embodies material violence — he is, in his very nature, the offspring of the archontic ruler. Seth, by contrast, is the true child of Adam, born after the spiritual restoration of Eve, and carries the authentic divine spark.

### The Hypostasis of the Archons (NHC II,4)

Similarly, the *Hypostasis of the Archons* attributes Cain's parentage to the archons. The text establishes that the archons' sexual assault on Eve produced the first two sons, while Seth was born "in the manner of the generation that is above" — that is, through the intervention of the true divine power.

### On the Origin of the World (NHC II,5)

This text develops the contrast between the archontic lineage (Cain) and the pneumatic lineage (Seth). Cain's agricultural offering represents the realm of matter and earthly cultivation — the domain of the Demiurge. Abel's pastoral offering, though closer to the psychic, is still within the Demiurge's jurisdiction. Neither brother fully represents the pneumatic reality that only Seth's line will carry.

## The Valentinian Three-Type Anthropology

Valentinian interpreters, as reported by Irenaeus (*Against Heresies* 1.7.5), developed a systematic typology of three human natures based on Genesis 4:

- **Cain** = the *hylic* (material) type: driven by matter, violence, and ignorance; incapable of salvation
- **Abel** = the *psychic* (soul) type: capable of partial righteousness but dependent on the Demiurge's favor; may be saved through faith and works
- **Seth** = the *pneumatic* (spiritual) type: possessing the divine spark, destined for gnosis and return to the Pleroma

This three-type scheme became foundational for Valentinian soteriology and was applied throughout their reading of the Hebrew Bible and the New Testament.

## Key Theological Concepts

**The Mark of Cain (4:15).** Where orthodox tradition reads the mark as divine protection for a sinner, Gnostic interpreters saw it as the Demiurge's brand of ownership — marking Cain as permanently belonging to the material realm. The *mark* is the seal of *hyle*, the indelible stamp of materiality.

**The Birth of Seth (4:25).** Eve's declaration — "God has granted me another offspring in place of Abel" — is read as the decisive moment in Gnostic salvation history. Seth is the "other seed" (*sperma*) placed by the true God, not the Demiurge. The entire Sethian Gnostic tradition takes its name and identity from this figure, understanding themselves as the spiritual descendants of Seth, the carriers of the authentic divine spark through history.

**Cain's City (4:17).** Cain's founding of a city represents the archontic project of civilization: institutions, technologies, and social structures designed to keep humanity embedded in the material world and distracted from the pursuit of gnosis.

## Theological Significance

Genesis 4 is the chapter where Gnostic anthropology crystallizes. The three sons — Cain, Abel, Seth — map onto the three types of human existence: material, psychic, and spiritual. For the Sethians, this chapter establishes their fundamental self-understanding: they are the children of Seth, carrying the divine spark through a hostile material world ruled by archons and populated by the unknowing offspring of the Demiurge.
