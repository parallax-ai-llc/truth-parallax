## Overview

Protestant interpretation of Genesis is shaped by the Reformation principle of *sola scriptura* — that Scripture alone is the ultimate authority for faith and practice. This leads to a distinctive emphasis on the text's plain meaning, while recognizing the diversity of interpretive approaches within Protestantism itself.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Literal-Historical
Many conservative Protestants read Genesis as straightforward historical narrative, emphasizing the literal six-day creation, a historical Adam and Eve, and a global flood.

### Literary-Framework
Other Protestant scholars, particularly in Reformed traditions, see the creation account as employing a literary framework (the "Framework Hypothesis") that organizes the creation days thematically rather than chronologically.

### Evangelical Critical
A growing number of evangelical scholars engage with historical-critical methods while maintaining the theological authority of the text.

## Theological Emphases

- **Original Sin**: Genesis 3 as the foundation for the doctrine of the Fall and total depravity
- **Covenant Theology**: The Abrahamic covenant as the beginning of God's redemptive plan
- **Typology**: Adam as a "type" of Christ (Romans 5:14)
- **Sovereignty of God**: God's sovereign choice in election (Jacob and Esau)
