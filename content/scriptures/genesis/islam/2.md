## Summary: The Islamic Perspective on Adam in the Garden

Genesis 2 provides a second, more intimate account of creation, focusing on Adam's placement in the Garden of Eden, the prohibition against eating from the tree, and the creation of Eve from Adam's rib. Islamic tradition engages deeply with this material, but through the Quranic lens, which reshapes the narrative significantly. In Islam, the garden (*jannah*) in which Adam was placed is a matter of scholarly debate — some hold it was the heavenly Paradise, others a terrestrial garden — but the theological emphasis falls on Adam's role as *khalifah* (vicegerent) and the divine trust (*amanah*) bestowed upon him.

The creation of Hawwa (Eve) is mentioned in the Quran but without the detailed "rib" narrative of Genesis 2:21-22. A hadith in Sahih al-Bukhari does reference woman being created from a rib, but Islamic scholars debate whether this is literal or metaphorical. The Quran's emphasis is on the pair being created from a single soul (*nafs wahidah*, Quran 4:1), stressing equality of origin rather than derivative creation.

## Quranic Parallels

- **Quran 2:30-33** — Allah informs the angels He will place a *khalifah* on earth. The angels question this, but Allah teaches Adam the names of all things, demonstrating his unique knowledge.
- **Quran 2:35** — "And We said, 'O Adam, dwell, you and your wife, in Paradise and eat therefrom in ease and abundance from wherever you will. But do not approach this tree, lest you be among the wrongdoers.'"
- **Quran 7:19** — A parallel command to Adam and his wife about the forbidden tree.
- **Quran 4:1** — "O mankind, fear your Lord, who created you from one soul and created from it its mate and dispersed from both of them many men and women."
- **Quran 15:28-29** — "And when your Lord said to the angels, 'I will create a human being out of clay from an altered black mud. And when I have proportioned him and breathed into him of My spirit, then fall down to him in prostration.'"

## Key Differences from the Biblical Account

| Topic | Genesis 2 | Islamic Interpretation |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|
| God "resting" (2:2-3) | God rested on the seventh day | Categorically rejected; Quran 50:38 |
| Garden location | Eden, a specific earthly location | Debated: heavenly paradise or earthly garden |
| Naming the animals | Adam names the animals (2:19-20) | Adam taught "the names" (*al-asma'*) — broader concept of knowledge (2:31) |
| Eve from a rib | Created from Adam's rib (2:21-22) | Quran says "from one soul"; rib hadith is debated |
| Tree identity | Tree of knowledge of good and evil | The Quran does not specify which tree; identity is irrelevant |
| Purpose of Eve | "Helper" for Adam | Partner from the same soul; emphasis on mutual comfort (*mawaddah*) |

## Views of Major Commentators

**Al-Tabari** records extensive Isra'iliyyat material about Adam's creation, including detailed descriptions of his height, the clay from which he was formed, and the creation of Hawwa. He reports traditions that Hawwa was created from Adam's left rib while he slept, largely following the biblical narrative. However, he frames this within the Quranic context of Adam as khalifah, not merely a gardener.

**Ibn Kathir** is more cautious. He affirms that Hawwa was created from Adam but notes that the Quran's phrasing — "from one soul and created from it its mate" (4:1) — does not specify the mechanism. He cites the rib hadith but warns against embellishing with Isra'iliyyat details not confirmed by revelation. He emphasizes that the purpose of the narrative is to demonstrate God's creative power and Adam's honored status.

**Al-Tha'labi** provides the most elaborate retelling, weaving biblical, rabbinic, and Islamic traditions together. His *Qisas al-Anbiya'* includes descriptions of the garden's rivers, the forbidden tree, and the angels' prostration before Adam. He treats the narrative as a story of divine generosity and human dignity, consistent with the Quranic emphasis on Adam's election (*istifa'*) by God (Quran 3:33).
