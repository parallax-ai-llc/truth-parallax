## Overview

Islam does not regard the Torah (Tawrat) in its current form as fully preserved, but recognizes that it originally contained divine revelation. Many narratives in Genesis have direct parallels in the Quran and Hadith literature. Islamic interpretation of Genesis thus operates through a distinctive lens: the Quranic account is considered the corrective standard against which biblical narratives are evaluated.

## Relationship to the Quran

Key Genesis narratives that appear in the Quran:

| Genesis Narrative | Quranic Parallel | Key Surahs |
|------------------|-----------------|------------|
| Creation of Adam | Adam as khalifah (vicegerent) | Al-Baqarah 2:30-39, Al-A'raf 7:11-25 |
| The Fall | Iblis refuses to bow, Adam's slip | Ta-Ha 20:115-123 |
| Cain and Abel | Qabil and Habil | Al-Ma'idah 5:27-32 |
| Noah and the Flood | Nuh and the Ark | Hud 11:25-49, Nuh 71:1-28 |
| Abraham | Ibrahim, friend of God | Al-Baqarah 2:124-141, Ibrahim 14 |
| Ishmael | Isma'il, sacrifice narrative | As-Saffat 37:99-111 |
| Jacob and Joseph | Ya'qub and Yusuf | Yusuf 12:1-111 |

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Tawhid (Divine Unity)
All narratives are read through the lens of strict monotheism. Any passage suggesting anthropomorphism or divine plurality is reinterpreted or considered a textual corruption (*tahrif*).

### Prophetic Infallibility (*Ismah*)
Prophets are considered sinless or nearly so in Islamic theology. Genesis narratives showing prophets in morally compromising situations (Noah's drunkenness, Lot's daughters, Judah and Tamar) are either reinterpreted or attributed to biblical alteration.

### Isra'iliyyat
Stories from Jewish and Christian sources that entered Islamic tradition. Classical scholars like **Ibn Kathir** and **Al-Tabari** included these but with varying degrees of acceptance:
- **Accepted**: Those confirmed by Quran or Hadith
- **Neutral**: Neither confirmed nor denied — may be narrated without endorsement
- **Rejected**: Those contradicting Islamic principles

## Theological Emphases

- **Adam as khalifah**: Not a "fallen" being but a vicegerent who erred and was forgiven
- **No original sin**: Adam repented and was forgiven (Quran 2:37); sin is not inherited
- **Ibrahim, not Abraham**: The Quran presents Ibrahim as the first *hanif* (pure monotheist), builder of the Kaaba
- **Ishmael over Isaac**: Islamic tradition identifies the son of sacrifice as Isma'il, not Isaac
- **Yusuf (Joseph)**: Surah 12 is called "the best of stories" (*ahsan al-qasas*)

## Major Commentators

| Commentator | Era | Work |
|-------------|-----|------|
| Al-Tabari | 9th-10th century | *Jami' al-Bayan* — encyclopedic tafsir with Isra'iliyyat |
| Ibn Kathir | 14th century | *Tafsir Ibn Kathir* — hadith-based, critical of Isra'iliyyat |
| Al-Qurtubi | 13th century | *Al-Jami' li-Ahkam al-Quran* — legal-theological |
| Al-Tha'labi | 11th century | *Qisas al-Anbiya'* (Stories of the Prophets) |
| Sayyid Qutb | 20th century | *Fi Zilal al-Quran* — modern socio-political reading |
