## Overview

Sunan an-Nasa'i is one of the six canonical hadith collections in Sunni Islam, compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb an-Nasa'i (829–915 CE). The collection is distinguished by its exceptionally rigorous narrator criticism — many scholars consider an-Nasa'i's standards of authentication to be the strictest among the six canonical compilers after al-Bukhari and Muslim. His original work, *al-Sunan al-Kubra*, was later condensed into *al-Sunan al-Sughra* (also known as *al-Mujtaba*), which is the version that circulates as part of the canonical six.

## Structure

The collection is organized thematically to cover the essential domains of Islamic practice and law:

1. **Purification and Prayer** — Wudu, ghusl, tayammum, and the detailed regulations of salah
2. **Mosques and Times** — Rules governing mosques, the adhan, and the prescribed times of prayer
3. **Zakat and Fasting** — Obligatory charity, voluntary alms, and the regulations of Ramadan
4. **Pilgrimage and Jihad** — The rites of Hajj and Umrah, military conduct, and horses
5. **Transactions and Judicial Matters** — Sales, partnerships, oaths, and court procedures

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Sunan an-Nasa'i holds a respected position across Islamic interpretive communities:

- **Sunni Islam**: Recognized for its strict narrator standards and detailed treatment of prayer
- **Shia Islam**: Noted for an-Nasa'i's sympathies toward Ahl al-Bayt, making it of particular interest
- **Sufi traditions**: Valued for hadiths on the spiritual dimensions of worship and purification
- **Salafi movement**: Esteemed for its rigorous commitment to authentic chains of transmission
- **Academic scholarship**: Studied for its contribution to the science of narrator evaluation (*rijal*)

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Strict Isnad Standards | Among the most rigorous narrator criticism in the canonical collections |
| Prayer Regulations | Exhaustive treatment of the rules and etiquette of salah |
| Ritual Purity | Comprehensive coverage of purification in all its forms |
| Legal Precision | Careful delineation of jurisprudential positions |
| Narrator Evaluation | Advanced application of *jarh wa ta'dil* methodology |
