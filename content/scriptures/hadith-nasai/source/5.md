---
title: "Sunan an-Nasa'i - Chapter 5"
scripture: "hadith-nasai"
chapter: 5
---

**1** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever performs Hajj and does not commit any obscenity or transgression, he will return like the day his mother bore him."

**2** It was narrated from Ibn Abbas that the Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "An Umrah in Ramadan is equivalent to a Hajj with me."

**3** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, was asked: "Which deed is best?" He said: "Faith in Allah and His Messenger." It was asked: "Then what?" He said: "Jihad in the cause of Allah." It was asked: "Then what?" He said: "An accepted Hajj."

**4** It was narrated from Aisha that she said: "O Messenger of Allah, we see jihad as the best of deeds. Should we not then do jihad?" He said: "The best jihad for you is an accepted Hajj."

**5** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "From one Umrah to the next is an expiation for the sins committed between them, and an accepted Hajj has no reward except Paradise."

**6** It was narrated from Ibn Abbas that the Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "Hasten to perform Hajj, for none of you knows what may happen to him."

**7** It was narrated from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Alternate between Hajj and Umrah, for they remove poverty and sins just as the bellows removes impurities from iron, gold, and silver."

**8** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, delivered a sermon and said: "O people, Allah has made Hajj obligatory for you, so perform Hajj." A man asked: "Every year, O Messenger of Allah?" He remained silent until the man repeated the question three times. Then the Prophet said: "If I had said yes, it would have become obligatory, and you would not have been able to do it."
