---
title: "Sunan an-Nasa'i - Chapter 2"
scripture: "hadith-nasai"
chapter: 2
---

**1** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "If there was a river at the door of one of you in which he bathed five times each day, do you think any dirt would remain on him?" They said: "Not a trace of dirt would remain." He said: "That is the likeness of the five daily prayers, by which Allah erases sins."

**2** It was narrated from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud that he asked the Prophet, peace be upon him: "Which deed is most beloved to Allah?" He said: "Prayer at its proper time." He asked: "Then what?" He said: "Being dutiful to parents." He asked: "Then what?" He said: "Jihad in the cause of Allah."

**3** It was narrated that Abu Hurairah said: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The first thing for which a person will be brought to account on the Day of Resurrection will be his prayer. If it is complete, that will be good. If it is not complete, Allah the Exalted will say: 'See whether My servant has any voluntary prayers.' If he has voluntary prayers, his obligatory prayers will be completed from them."

**4** It was narrated that Uthman ibn Affan said: I heard the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, say: "Whoever prays the night prayer in congregation, it is as if he had stood the first half of the night. And whoever prays the dawn prayer in congregation, it is as if he had stood the entire night in prayer."

**5** It was narrated that Aisha said: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The two units of prayer before the dawn prayer are better than the world and everything in it."

**6** It was narrated that Abu Hurairah said: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "The most burdensome prayers for the hypocrites are the night prayer and the dawn prayer. If they knew what reward they contain, they would come to them even if they had to crawl."

**7** It was narrated that Anas said: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When one of you feels sleepy while praying, let him lie down until the sleepiness goes away, for when one of you prays while drowsy he may intend to seek forgiveness but ends up cursing himself."

**8** It was narrated that Abu Hurairah said: The Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When the call to prayer is made, Satan turns his back and passes wind loudly so as not to hear the call. When the call ends, he comes back."
