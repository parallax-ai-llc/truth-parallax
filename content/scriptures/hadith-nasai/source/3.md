---
title: "Sunan an-Nasa'i - Chapter 3"
scripture: "hadith-nasai"
chapter: 3
---

**1** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Allah the Exalted said: 'Every deed of the son of Adam is for him, except fasting; it is for Me and I shall reward it.'"

**2** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "When Ramadan begins, the gates of Paradise are opened, the gates of Hell are closed, and the devils are chained."

**3** It was narrated from Sahl ibn Sa'd that the Prophet, peace be upon him, said: "In Paradise there is a gate called Rayyan through which none will enter except those who fast."

**4** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever fasts Ramadan out of faith and seeking reward, his previous sins will be forgiven."

**5** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever stands in prayer during the nights of Ramadan out of faith and seeking reward, his previous sins will be forgiven."

**6** It was narrated from Aisha that the Prophet, peace be upon him, used to strive in the last ten days of Ramadan more than at any other time.

**7** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Whoever does not give up false speech and acting upon it, Allah has no need for him to give up his food and drink."

**8** It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, said: "Fasting is a shield. When one of you is fasting, let him not use obscene language or act ignorantly. If someone fights him or insults him, let him say: 'I am fasting.'"
