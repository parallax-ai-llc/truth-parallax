An-Nasa'i's treatment of zakat and fasting provides Sunni jurisprudence with rigorously authenticated traditions governing these pillars of Islam. The zakat sections detail the types of wealth subject to obligatory charity, the thresholds for each category, and the rates of collection. An-Nasa'i's strict narrator standards give these legal traditions particular weight in jurisprudential debates where the precise wording of a hadith determines the ruling.

The fasting traditions cover the regulations of Ramadan with characteristic precision — the criteria for beginning and ending the fast, the actions that invalidate fasting, the categories of persons exempted, and the expiation required for deliberate violations. An-Nasa'i also records traditions on voluntary fasting, including the Prophet's practice of fasting on alternate days and on specific occasions. Sunni scholars appreciate the reliability of an-Nasa'i's fasting hadiths, using them as secure foundations for legal rulings on this fundamental practice.
