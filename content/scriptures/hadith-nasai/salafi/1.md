Salafi scholars treat an-Nasa'i's purification and prayer traditions with particular confidence because of his strict authentication standards. The detailed prescriptions for ablution and prayer are used to establish the precise prophetic method of worship, with Salafi practitioners seeking to replicate every element of the Prophet's prayer as exactly as possible. An-Nasa'i's exhaustive coverage ensures that virtually every aspect of ritual worship is addressed with authenticated prophetic evidence.

The strictness of an-Nasa'i's narrator criticism means that traditions found in his collection require less additional verification than those in compilations with more inclusive standards. Salafi scholars use his prayer traditions alongside those of al-Bukhari and Muslim to construct a comprehensive description of the Prophet's prayer, treating convergent narrations from these strict compilers as the most reliable basis for practice. Where an-Nasa'i records a detail or variant not found in the two Sahihs, it is evaluated but given significant weight due to the quality of his narrator screening.
