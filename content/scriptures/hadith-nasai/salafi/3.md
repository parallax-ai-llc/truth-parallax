Salafi scholars use an-Nasa'i's zakat and fasting traditions as primary evidence for establishing the authentic prophetic regulations governing these obligations. The zakat traditions are applied rigorously to determine which categories of wealth are subject to charitable taxation, the exact thresholds for each category, and the precise rates of collection. Salafi scholars resist attempts to modernize zakat categories beyond the scope of authenticated prophetic precedent.

The fasting chapters are engaged with characteristic emphasis on separating authentic Sunnah from cultural accretions. An-Nasa'i's strict authentication ensures that the fasting practices derived from his collection rest on solid evidential foundations. Salafi scholars use his traditions to address contemporary fasting questions — such as the use of medical devices during fasting hours and the determination of Ramadan's beginning by astronomical calculation versus physical moon sighting — always prioritizing the apparent meaning of authenticated narrations over modernist reinterpretations.
