Salafi engagement with an-Nasa'i's mosque and prayer time traditions focuses on establishing the prophetic norms for congregational worship and its temporal regulation. The mosque traditions are applied to questions about the proper conduct within mosques, the impermissibility of innovations in mosque design and practice, and the obligation of congregational prayer for men. Salafi scholars use an-Nasa'i's authenticated narrations to distinguish between established prophetic practice and later additions to mosque culture.

The prayer time traditions are employed to determine the exact windows for each daily prayer, with Salafi scholars preferring the most specific and rigorously authenticated narrations as the basis for practice. An-Nasa'i's traditions on the earliest, preferred, and latest times for each prayer are cross-referenced with astronomical calculation to produce accurate prayer schedules. The precision of this approach reflects the Salafi conviction that obedience to God requires exactness in worship, and that the authenticated Sunnah provides the necessary specificity for such precision.
