Sufi interpreters find in an-Nasa'i's exhaustive treatment of purification and prayer a comprehensive guide to the outer practices whose inner dimensions the spiritual seeker progressively discovers. The ablution traditions are read as instructions for both physical and spiritual preparation: each limb washed in ablution corresponds, in Sufi hermeneutics, to a spiritual faculty that must be cleansed of its attachments and distractions before genuine prayer can occur.

An-Nasa'i's unparalleled attention to the details of prayer — every recitation, every posture, every transition — is treasured by Sufi practitioners who understand that mastery of the outward form creates the conditions for inward realization. The Prophet's instruction to "pray as you have seen me pray" is understood as encompassing both the visible and invisible dimensions of worship. As the seeker perfects the outer prayer, the inner prayer — the heart's continuous orientation toward God — gradually awakens. An-Nasa'i's rigorous authentication reassures the Sufi practitioner that the forms being followed are genuinely prophetic.
