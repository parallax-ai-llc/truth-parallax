Sufi engagement with an-Nasa'i's transaction and judicial traditions emphasizes the spiritual integrity that must underlie all worldly dealings. The commercial hadiths are read as tests of the believer's sincerity — the marketplace is a field of spiritual trial where the virtues of honesty, fairness, and detachment are put to practice. Sufi masters teach that one's conduct in business reveals the true state of one's heart more clearly than one's conduct in the mosque.

The judicial traditions speak to the Sufi concept of *taqwa* (God-consciousness) as the foundation of justice. Only the one who is conscious of God's presence in every moment can judge truly, because true judgment requires freedom from the distortions of self-interest, prejudice, and fear. An-Nasa'i's authenticated traditions on the qualities of righteous judges are read as descriptions of the spiritually purified heart — a heart that sees clearly because it has been cleansed of everything that clouds perception. This connection between inner purity and outer justice is a central theme of Sufi ethics.
