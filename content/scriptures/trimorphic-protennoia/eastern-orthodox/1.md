Eastern Orthodox scholars read the first chapter of Trimorphic Protennoia through the lens of patristic theology. The Voice of Protennoia descending into the material world presents an inverse image of Orthodox theophany: in Orthodox theology, God's voice at the Jordan, at Sinai, and at the Transfiguration confirms the goodness of creation and sanctifies the material world. In the Gnostic text, the divine Voice descends to subvert creation and awaken sparks that should never have been trapped in matter.

The chapter's cosmological dualism — its fundamental opposition between the divine realm and the material world — conflicts with the Orthodox doctrine of creation expressed in the Nicene Creed: "Maker of heaven and earth, of all things visible and invisible." Orthodox theology insists that the visible, material world is the good creation of the one true God, not the prison of a malevolent demiurge.
