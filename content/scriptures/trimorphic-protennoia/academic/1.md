Academic analysis of Trimorphic Protennoia's first chapter situates it within the Sethian Gnostic literary tradition. John D. Turner's classification places the text among the "descent" type of Sethian works, alongside the Apocryphon of John and the longer versions of the Gospel of the Egyptians. The Voice descent represents the first stage of a three-stage revelatory pattern that Turner identifies as characteristic of Sethian soteriology.

The chapter's description of Protennoia as hidden, dwelling within all things, and moving through creation without being recognized has been analyzed as an adaptation of Jewish Wisdom traditions. Paul-Hubert Poirier's critical edition notes extensive parallels with Proverbs 8, Sirach 24, and the Wisdom of Solomon, suggesting that the Sethian author drew on Jewish sapiential literature to construct Protennoia's self-revelation.
