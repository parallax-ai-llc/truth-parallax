## Overview

Trimorphic Protennoia (NHC XIII,1) is a Sethian Gnostic text from the Nag Hammadi library describing three descents of the divine First Thought (Protennoia) into the material world. In each descent, Protennoia takes a different form — Voice, Speech, and Word (Logos) — progressively revealing herself to rescue the divine sparks trapped in the material realm. The text is remarkable for its trinitarian-like structure and its close parallels to the Prologue of the Gospel of John.

## Structure

The text divides naturally into three sections corresponding to the three descents:

1. **The First Descent — Voice**: Protennoia descends as a hidden Voice, shaking the foundations of the material world and awakening those who sleep
2. **The Second Descent — Speech**: Protennoia descends as articulate Speech, revealing herself more fully and empowering the elect
3. **The Third Descent — Word/Logos**: Protennoia descends in human form as the Logos, entering the world to liberate souls through baptism and ascent

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The relationship between Trimorphic Protennoia and the Gospel of John's Prologue has generated extensive scholarly discussion. Both texts describe a divine Word/Logos that descends into the world of darkness and is not recognized. Whether the Gnostic text depends on John, John depends on the Gnostic tradition, or both draw on a common source remains one of the most debated questions in Nag Hammadi scholarship.
