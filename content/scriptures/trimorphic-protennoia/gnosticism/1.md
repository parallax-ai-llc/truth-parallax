In the Gnostic reading, the first descent of Protennoia as the Voice represents the initial stirring of divine consciousness within the material world. Protennoia declares herself to be the Thought of the Father, the invisible one who exists in all things. Her Voice penetrates the darkness of the material realm created by the ignorant Demiurge, shaking the foundations of the archons' power and beginning the long process of awakening the divine sparks trapped in human bodies.

The Voice is described as hidden, subtle, and pervasive. She moves through all creation without being recognized by the archons who rule the material world. This hiddenness is essential to the Gnostic understanding of the first descent: the divine presence must work secretly, within the structures of material reality, before it can be openly revealed. The sleeping seeds of Seth begin to stir but do not yet fully awaken.

Gnostic readers understood this chapter as describing their own initial experience of spiritual awakening — the moment when something within them first responded to a call they could not yet name or understand. The Voice of Protennoia is the pre-cognitive stirring of gnosis, the divine Mother's first whisper to her lost children.
