The third and final descent of Protennoia as the Word/Logos represents the climactic moment of Gnostic salvation. Protennoia descends in human form, entering the world as the Logos to complete the liberation of the divine sparks. She describes putting on Jesus as a garment, descending into Hades, and performing the baptism of the five seals that enables the elect to ascend through the heavenly realms to the Pleroma.

This chapter's christological imagery is distinctly docetic: the Logos does not become flesh but wears flesh as a disguise to deceive the archons. The crucifixion is either bypassed or reinterpreted — it is the archons who are defeated, not the divine Logos who suffers. For Gnostic readers, this was not a diminishment of salvation but its proper understanding: the divine cannot truly suffer or die; the appearance of suffering was a stratagem to defeat the rulers of the material world.

The baptism of the five seals described in this chapter served as the culminating ritual for Sethian Gnostic communities. Through this rite, the initiate participates in Protennoia's own descent and ascent, shedding the garments of ignorance and putting on the light-vesture. The five seals represent the complete sealing of the initiate against the powers of the material world, ensuring safe passage through the heavenly spheres to the Father's realm.
