## Chapter 69 — The Names and Deeds of the Fallen Angels

1 Enoch 69 presents the "Son of Man" or "Elect One" as a figure of transcendent, pre-existent glory who reveals divine secrets and executes cosmic judgment. Gnostic interpreters find in this chapter's heavenly redeemer figure an important precursor to the Gnostic concept of the celestial Savior who descends to liberate imprisoned sparks of light.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

The Son of Man in the Parables of Enoch shares significant features with the Gnostic Anthropos (Primal Man) — a pre-existent heavenly being who serves as both revealer and redeemer. Gnostic systems such as those described in the *Apocryphon of John* and the *Trimorphic Protennoia* develop this archetype into a fully articulated mythology of divine descent and salvific revelation.

The pre-existence of the Son of Man — "chosen and hidden before the creation of the world" — resonates with the Gnostic understanding of the Savior as emanating from the divine pleroma before the creation of the material world. This heavenly origin distinguishes the Savior fundamentally from the material realm governed by the demiurge.

## Scholarly Assessment

The Enochic Son of Man traditions represent a crucial link in the chain connecting Jewish apocalypticism to Gnostic Christology. This chapter demonstrates that the concept of a transcendent, pre-existent savior figure was already developing within Jewish theological speculation before being transformed by both orthodox Christian and Gnostic interpreters.
