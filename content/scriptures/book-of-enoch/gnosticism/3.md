## The Regularity of Nature

Gnostic interpreters approach this chapter's themes of cosmic order and divine judgment with characteristic ambivalence, asking whether the order described reflects the true divine will or the Demiurge's imposition of control over a world that is itself a prison.

## Key Themes

### Order as Control
Where other traditions read cosmic regularity as evidence of divine wisdom, Gnostic interpreters may see it as evidence of archontic control. The Demiurge maintains strict order in his creation not out of benevolence but to prevent the divine sparks trapped within from escaping.

### The Inversion Principle
The Gnostic hermeneutical strategy of reading texts "against the grain" opens this chapter to radically different interpretation. What appears as praise of divine order may actually be a description of cosmic imprisonment — the regularity of nature is the predictability of a well-run prison.

## Theological Significance

For Gnostic readers, this chapter demonstrates the fundamental interpretive principle of Gnostic hermeneutics: every text about cosmic order can be read as either praise of divine wisdom or critique of demiurgic control, depending on one's understanding of who created and governs the material world.
