## Chapter 106 — The Birth of Noah

1 Enoch 106 describes the miraculous birth of Noah (chapters 106-107), portraying the child as luminous and otherworldly. Gnostic interpreters find in this narrative a powerful image of the divine spark imprisoned in matter — the pneumatic nature of the true Gnostic manifest even at birth.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

Noah's radiant appearance at birth — his body glowing with light and his eyes illuminating the house — resonates with the Gnostic concept of the divine light-spark (*spinther*) trapped within the material body. In Gnostic anthropology, the pneumatic individual carries within them a fragment of the divine pleroma that distinguishes them fundamentally from the merely psychic or hylic. This chapter's portrayal of Noah as visibly different from ordinary humanity anticipates the Gnostic teaching that those who possess the divine seed are ontologically distinct from the rest of humanity.

## Scholarly Assessment

The Noahic birth narrative illustrates a trajectory of thought that Gnostic interpreters would develop into a comprehensive anthropology. The luminous child who does not belong to the ordinary world of flesh prefigures the Gnostic Savior who descends into matter but remains essentially alien to it — a stranger (*allogenes*) whose true home lies in the realm of pure light above the cosmos of the demiurge.
