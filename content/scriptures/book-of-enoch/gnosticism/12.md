## Enoch's Commission

Gnostic interpreters read this chapter's themes of heavenly vision and divine judgment through their distinctive cosmological framework, asking whether Enoch's visions reveal the true divine realm or merely the highest level of the Demiurge's heaven.

## Key Themes

### Ascent Through the Archons
Enoch's journey through heavenly spaces maps onto the Gnostic soul's ascent through the archontic spheres. Each level of heaven Enoch passes through corresponds to a realm ruled by an archon, and the Gnostic reader asks: does Enoch reach the true Pleroma, or does he stop at the Demiurge's throne?

### The Revealer Figure
Enoch's role as a mediator of heavenly knowledge resonates with the Gnostic concept of the revealer — a figure sent from the true divine realm to communicate salvific *gnosis* to those trapped in matter. Whether Enoch serves this function or is himself deceived by the Demiurge depends on the Gnostic school reading the text.

## Theological Significance

For Gnostic readers, this chapter raises the fundamental question of discernment: can one distinguish between genuine divine revelation and the Demiurge's imitation of it? The answer, in Gnostic tradition, requires *gnosis* — the inner spiritual knowledge that alone can distinguish truth from its most convincing counterfeits.
