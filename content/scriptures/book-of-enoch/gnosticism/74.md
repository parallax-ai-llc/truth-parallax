## Chapter 74 — The Twelve Divisions of the Moon

1 Enoch 74 belongs to the Astronomical Book (chapters 72-82), presenting a cosmological system of celestial governance. Gnostic interpreters find in this material significant resonances with the Gnostic understanding of the cosmos as a structured system of archontic powers governing the material realm.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

The Astronomical Book's detailed system of angelic beings governing celestial portals and luminaries resonates with the Gnostic concept of archons — cosmic rulers who govern the material world. In systems like those described in the *Apocryphon of John* and the *Hypostasis of the Archons*, the visible cosmos is administered by subordinate powers who may obstruct or facilitate the soul's ascent. This chapter's angelic cosmology can be read as a precursor to the elaborate archontic hierarchies of later Gnostic systems.

## Scholarly Assessment

Gnostic scholars note that the Astronomical Book represents an important stage in the development of Jewish cosmological speculation that would later be transformed by Gnostic interpreters. The detailed mapping of celestial portals and angelic governors prefigures the Gnostic concern with cosmic cartography — knowledge of the heavenly topography required for the soul's post-mortem journey through the archontic spheres to the pleroma of divine light beyond the visible cosmos.
