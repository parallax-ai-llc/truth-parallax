## Chapter 103 — The Fate of the Righteous Dead

1 Enoch 103 belongs to the Epistle of Enoch (chapters 91-105), which contains prophetic woes and the Apocalypse of Weeks. Gnostic interpreters find in this section's dualistic ethics and revelatory epistemology important antecedents to Gnostic thought.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

The Epistle's sharp distinction between the righteous who possess revealed knowledge and the sinners who are mired in ignorance prefigures the fundamental Gnostic division between the pneumatics (spiritual ones) who possess *gnosis* and the hylics (material ones) trapped in ignorance. This chapter's insistence that true understanding comes through divine revelation rather than worldly wisdom resonates with the Gnostic emphasis on salvific knowledge communicated through heavenly revealers. The Apocalypse of Weeks' periodization of history into predetermined epochs anticipates the Gnostic understanding of cosmic history as following a divinely ordained pattern of fall and restoration.

## Scholarly Assessment

The Epistle of Enoch represents a crucial transitional text between Jewish apocalypticism and Gnostic soteriology. This chapter's combination of revealed knowledge, ethical dualism, and eschatological reversal demonstrates the apocalyptic matrix from which Gnostic systems of thought would emerge, transforming Jewish categories into a distinctive theology of liberation through knowledge.
