## Chapter 108 — The Concluding Chapter

1 Enoch 108 concludes the Enochic corpus with a final vision of eschatological separation between the righteous and the wicked. Gnostic interpreters read this chapter's dualistic eschatology through the lens of the fundamental Gnostic distinction between those who possess salvific knowledge and those who remain trapped in ignorance.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

This concluding chapter's vision of eternal reward for the righteous and destruction for the wicked resonates with the Gnostic teaching that the pneumatics — those who carry the divine spark and have awakened to *gnosis* — will return to the pleroma of divine light, while those without the divine seed will dissolve into the nothingness from which the demiurge fashioned them. The sharp eschatological dualism of this chapter anticipates the Gnostic transformation of Jewish apocalyptic categories into a metaphysical distinction between spirit and matter.

## Scholarly Assessment

This final chapter of 1 Enoch represents the culmination of the apocalyptic worldview that Gnostic interpreters would radically transform. The text's confidence in ultimate cosmic justice — with the righteous vindicated and the wicked destroyed — provides the ethical and eschatological framework that Gnostic systems would reinterpret through the lens of ontological dualism, replacing moral categories with categories of knowledge and ignorance, light and darkness, spirit and matter.
