## Overview

The Book of Enoch (1 Enoch) is a composite apocalyptic work attributed to the antediluvian patriarch Enoch, who according to Genesis 5:24 "walked with God, and he was not, for God took him." Preserved in its entirety only in Ge'ez (Ethiopic), the text is canonical in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church and has profoundly influenced Jewish and Christian apocalypticism. Fragments in Aramaic were discovered among the Dead Sea Scrolls, confirming the text's antiquity and its importance in Second Temple Judaism.

## Structure

The Book of Enoch comprises five major sections, composed over several centuries (roughly 3rd century BCE to 1st century CE):

1. **The Book of the Watchers (1–36)**: The fall of the angels, their corruption of humanity, Enoch's heavenly journeys
2. **The Book of Parables (37–71)**: The "Son of Man" figure, cosmic judgment, the fate of the righteous and wicked
3. **The Astronomical Book (72–82)**: The solar calendar, the movements of luminaries, cosmological order
4. **The Book of Dreams (83–90)**: Apocalyptic visions including the Animal Apocalypse (a symbolic history of Israel)
5. **The Epistle of Enoch (91–108)**: Ethical exhortation, the Apocalypse of Weeks, final judgment

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Book of Enoch occupies a distinctive position across multiple traditions:

- **Ethiopian Orthodox**: Canonical scripture, integral to the 81-book biblical canon
- **Protestantism**: Non-canonical but studied for its influence on New Testament eschatology, particularly Jude 14–15
- **Catholicism**: Not canonical but recognized as historically significant for understanding Jewish apocalypticism
- **Judaism**: Influential in Second Temple period but excluded from the rabbinic canon
- **Academic**: Essential for reconstructing the diversity of Second Temple Judaism and the origins of Christian apocalypticism
- **Gnosticism**: A source for cosmological and angelological speculation in Gnostic systems

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Fallen Angels / Watchers | Heavenly beings who transgress by descending to earth and corrupting humanity |
| Son of Man | A pre-existent heavenly figure who executes divine judgment |
| Apocalyptic Judgment | The final reckoning in which the wicked are punished and the righteous vindicated |
| Heavenly Journeys | Enoch's guided tours of the cosmos, revealing hidden knowledge |
| Solar Calendar | A 364-day calendar presented as the divinely ordained reckoning of time |
| Theodicy | The problem of evil explained through angelic rebellion and cosmic disorder |
