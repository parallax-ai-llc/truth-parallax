## The Accursed Valley of Judgment

Chapter 27 identifies the valley near Jerusalem as the place where the cursed will be permanently punished while the righteous bless the Lord on the holy mountain. The contrast between the blessed and the cursed is drawn with stark finality.

## Key Themes

### Final Separation
The spatial juxtaposition — the righteous on the mountain, the wicked in the valley — provides a concrete image of final judgment. Protestant interpreters connect this to Jesus' parable of the sheep and the goats (Matthew 25:31-46) and the great chasm of Luke 16:26.

### The Cursed and the Blessed
The language of blessing and cursing echoes Deuteronomy 27-28, where Israel is presented with the choice between obedience and disobedience, blessing and curse. Protestant covenant theologians see this eschatological scene as the ultimate fulfillment of the Deuteronomic covenant structure.

### Mercy within Judgment
Notably, the chapter asserts that this judgment serves a merciful purpose: the cursed are punished "in the sight of the righteous forever" so that the righteous may continually give thanks for God's mercy. Protestant interpreters debate whether this indicates the visibility of punishment (as in Revelation 14:10) or the visibility of justice.

## Theological Significance

This chapter presents the eschatological vision that most directly informed later Protestant doctrines of heaven and hell. The spatial proximity of blessing and curse underscores the Protestant conviction that the final state of every person hangs on their response to God's offer of mercy.
