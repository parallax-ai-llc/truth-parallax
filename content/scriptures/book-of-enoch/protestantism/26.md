## The Holy Mountain and Jerusalem's Valleys

Enoch is taken to the center of the earth and shown a holy mountain (Zion), surrounded by valleys. The angel identifies the deep valley to the east as the place where the cursed will be gathered for judgment — the Valley of Hinnom (Gehenna).

## Key Themes

### Gehenna's Origin
This chapter provides crucial background for the New Testament's use of Gehenna as a place of eschatological punishment. Protestant scholars note that Jesus' references to Gehenna (Mark 9:43-48; Matthew 25:41) draw on a tradition already developed in texts like 1 Enoch 26-27 and 2 Kings 23:10.

### Jerusalem as Cosmic Center
The identification of Jerusalem as the "center of the earth" reflects a widespread ancient conviction (cf. Ezekiel 5:5; 38:12). Protestant interpreters discuss whether this is theological symbolism or literal cosmography, with most favoring the former.

### Topographical Theology
The physical landscape around Jerusalem — Mount Zion, the Kidron Valley, the Valley of Hinnom — is invested with eschatological meaning. Protestant biblical geographers note how real topography becomes a canvas for theological reflection on judgment, blessing, and the final destiny of humanity.

## Theological Significance

For Protestant readers, this chapter demonstrates that the New Testament's use of Gehenna as an image of final punishment was not a Christian innovation but emerged from a developed Jewish tradition associating the physical Valley of Hinnom with eschatological judgment.
