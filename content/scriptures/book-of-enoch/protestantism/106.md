## Chapter 106 — The Birth of Noah

1 Enoch 106 belongs to the appendix concerning the birth of Noah (chapters 106-107), which describes the miraculous circumstances surrounding Noah's birth and Lamech's anxiety about the child's unusual appearance. Protestant scholars treat this material as an important witness to the development of the Noah traditions in Second Temple Judaism.

## Protestant Scholarly Assessment

The account of Noah's extraordinary birth — his luminous appearance and ability to speak at birth — represents haggadic expansion of the Genesis narrative. Protestant interpreters approach this through historical-critical methodology, noting parallels with other ancient Near Eastern birth narratives and recognizing how Jewish communities elaborated biblical stories. This chapter illustrates the interpretive creativity of Second Temple Judaism while remaining clearly non-canonical by Protestant standards.

## Theological Significance

Protestant scholars value this section for demonstrating how pre-Christian Jewish tradition developed the figure of Noah as a type of salvation. The emphasis on Noah's righteousness amid universal corruption anticipates New Testament typology connecting Noah's deliverance through the flood with Christian baptism (1 Peter 3:20-21), though Protestant interpreters maintain the primacy of the biblical account.
