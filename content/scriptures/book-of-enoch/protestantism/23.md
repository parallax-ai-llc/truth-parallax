## The Fire in the West

Enoch sees a great fire blazing in the western regions. The angel guide explains that this fire runs across and around the whole earth — a cosmic element integral to the structure of the world and its coming judgment.

## Key Themes

### Purifying Fire
Protestant interpreters read the western fire as an image of divine judgment that both destroys and purifies. This dual function of fire appears throughout Scripture (Malachi 3:2-3; 1 Corinthians 3:13-15) and became central to Protestant eschatological reflection, particularly in 2 Peter 3:7's vision of the world reserved for judgment by fire.

### Cosmic Geography
The placement of this fire in the west — the direction of sunset and, symbolically, ending and death — carries theological weight. Protestant literary scholars note the consistent association of westward direction with judgment and endings in ancient Near Eastern literature.

### The Luminaries' Path
The fire is connected to the courses of the heavenly luminaries, reinforcing the theme that cosmic elements serve God's purposes. Protestant natural theology finds here an ancient articulation of the principle that the physical universe reflects divine intent.

## Theological Significance

For Protestant readers, this brief chapter contributes to the cumulative portrait of a cosmos saturated with divine purpose and heading toward a definitive judgment. The fire that sustains the luminaries' courses will also serve as the instrument of final reckoning.
