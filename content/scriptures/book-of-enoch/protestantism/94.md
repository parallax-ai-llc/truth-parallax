## Chapter 94 — Woes to the Sinners

1 Enoch 94 belongs to the Epistle of Enoch (chapters 91-105), which contains the Apocalypse of Weeks and a series of prophetic woes against sinners and the wealthy. Protestant scholars engage with this section's strong ethical exhortations and its theology of eschatological reversal.

## Protestant Scholarly Assessment

The Epistle section contains some of the most ethically charged material in the Enochic corpus. The woes against the wealthy and oppressors parallel prophetic denunciations found in Amos, Isaiah, and James. Protestant interpreters note the continuity between this chapter's moral vision and the biblical prophetic tradition, while also recognizing the distinctively apocalyptic framework that promises cosmic reversal rather than historical reform.

## Theological Significance

The Epistle's emphasis on divine justice vindicating the righteous poor resonates with the Reformation's concern for spiritual equality before God. Protestant scholars note that while this text lacks canonical authority, its ethical vision — the condemnation of injustice and the promise of divine vindication — aligns with biblical themes that were central to prophetic preaching and to Jesus' own teaching in the Beatitudes and parables of reversal.
