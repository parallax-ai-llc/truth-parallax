## Chapter 108 — The Concluding Chapter

1 Enoch 108 serves as the final chapter of the compiled Enochic corpus, offering a concluding summary of the fates awaiting the righteous and the wicked. Protestant scholars view this chapter as a later editorial addition that synthesizes the major themes of the preceding sections.

## Protestant Scholarly Assessment

This concluding chapter reiterates the dualistic eschatology that pervades the Enochic literature: the righteous will receive eternal reward and the wicked will face judgment and destruction. Protestant scholars note the clear distinction between the fates of the two groups, which resonates with the New Testament's own teaching on final judgment. The chapter's emphasis on divine justice and the vindication of those who suffered for righteousness anticipates themes found throughout the Gospels and Pauline epistles.

## Theological Significance

While this chapter lacks canonical authority, Protestant interpreters recognize its value in illustrating the theological convictions that shaped Second Temple Judaism. The confidence in post-mortem vindication and the assurance of divine justice reflect beliefs that were part of the broader Jewish matrix from which Christian eschatology emerged, transformed by the resurrection of Christ as the decisive eschatological event.
