## Blessings and Curses

Chapter 5 concludes the introduction with a dual pronouncement: blessings for the righteous and curses for sinners. Protestant interpreters connect this pattern to the covenant blessings and curses of Deuteronomy 28 and the Beatitudes of Matthew 5.

## Key Themes

### Eschatological Blessing
The promise that the elect will "live long" and enjoy peace echoes the covenantal blessings of Deuteronomy while projecting them into an eschatological future. Protestant amillennialists and premillennialists differ on whether such promises are fulfilled spiritually in the church or literally in a future age.

### Wisdom and Light
The righteous will receive "wisdom" and "light" — language that resonates with the Protestant emphasis on Scripture as a "lamp to my feet" (Psalm 119:105) and the Johannine identification of Christ as the light of the world.

### Two-Ways Theology
The sharp distinction between righteous and wicked aligns with the two-ways theology found throughout Scripture (Psalm 1, Matthew 7:13-14) and central to Protestant soteriology's distinction between the saved and the lost.

## Theological Significance

Protestant interpreters find in this chapter a fully formed eschatological hope that predates and informs the New Testament vision of final redemption, confirming that the gospel was anticipated in the intertestamental period.
