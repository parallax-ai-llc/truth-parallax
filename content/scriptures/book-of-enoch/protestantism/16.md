## The Persistence of Evil Spirits

Chapter 16 continues the divine pronouncement, explaining that the evil spirits born from the Watchers' union with human women will continue to afflict humanity through leading people into idolatry, blood sacrifice, and deception until the final judgment.

## Key Themes

### Idolatry's Demonic Origin
The chapter attributes the origin of idolatrous worship to demonic influence — a view that Paul echoes in 1 Corinthians 10:20 ("the things which the Gentiles sacrifice, they sacrifice to demons"). Protestant interpreters see this as evidence of a consistent biblical theology connecting idol worship to demonic deception.

### The Already/Not Yet
Evil spirits are active now but face future destruction — a temporal framework that Protestant scholars recognize as the "already/not yet" structure central to New Testament eschatology. Satan is defeated at the Cross but not yet finally destroyed.

### Sacrificial Distortion
The Watchers' offspring lead humanity into offering blood sacrifices to demons, a distortion of legitimate Israelite sacrifice. Protestant commentators see this as illustrating how sin corrupts even religious impulses, turning worship toward destruction.

## Theological Significance

This chapter completes the Watchers' theological arc by explaining the ongoing effects of their rebellion. Protestant systematic theology draws on this tradition to understand why spiritual warfare remains necessary even after Christ's victory — the demonic realm operates within divinely set boundaries until the eschaton.
