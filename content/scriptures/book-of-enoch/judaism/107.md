## Chapter 107 — Noah's Mission Confirmed

1 Enoch 107 preserves traditions about the miraculous birth of Noah (chapters 106-107), describing his extraordinary appearance and his father Lamech's fearful reaction. Jewish scholars engage with this material as part of the rich haggadic tradition surrounding the patriarchal narratives.

## Jewish Scholarly Analysis

The Noah birth narrative preserved here represents an important strand of Jewish haggadic tradition that developed alongside but independently of the Genesis account. Jewish scholars note parallels with the Genesis Apocryphon from Qumran, which preserves a similar account of Lamech's anxiety about Noah's unusual appearance. This chapter illustrates how Second Temple Jewish communities enriched biblical narratives through imaginative elaboration — a tradition that continued in midrashic literature, including Genesis Rabbah's own expansions of the Noah story.

## Historical Significance

This section is significant for understanding the development of the Noah figure in Jewish tradition. The midrashic and aggadic elaboration of Noah's character — from the terse biblical account to the richly developed figure in rabbinic literature — has roots in traditions like this chapter. The text demonstrates the creative biblical interpretation that characterized Second Temple Judaism and that the rabbis would later systematize in their hermeneutical principles (*middot*).
