## Chapter 108 — The Concluding Chapter

1 Enoch 108 concludes the Enochic corpus with a final statement on the contrasting destinies of the righteous and the wicked. Muslim scholars engage with this chapter's eschatological vision through the comprehensive Quranic teaching on the Day of Judgment and the afterlife.

## Islamic Scholarly Perspective

This concluding chapter's dualistic eschatology — eternal reward for the faithful and eternal punishment for the wicked — resonates strongly with the Quranic vision of Paradise (*Jannah*) and Hellfire (*Jahannam*). The Quran presents the afterlife in vivid detail, describing the gardens of bliss awaiting the righteous (Surah ar-Rahman 55:46-78) and the torment prepared for the unjust (Surah al-Mulk 67:6-11). Muslim scholars note the shared conviction that earthly life is a test (*ibtila'*) whose results are revealed in the hereafter.

## Comparative Observations

Both the Enochic conclusion and Quranic eschatology affirm the moral seriousness of human existence and the reality of divine judgment. While the theological frameworks differ — the Enochic text reflects Jewish apocalyptic categories while the Quran presents its own distinctive eschatological vision — both traditions insist that God's justice will ultimately prevail and that the present moral order will be vindicated in the world to come.
