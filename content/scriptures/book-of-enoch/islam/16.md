## Origin of Evil Spirits

Islamic interpreters read this chapter's themes of heavenly vision and spiritual mediation through the lens of Quranic prophetology and the Islamic tradition of prophetic experience, particularly the Mi'raj (heavenly ascent) of Prophet Muhammad.

## Key Themes

### The Mi'raj Parallel
Enoch's heavenly visions and encounters with the divine presence share structural elements with Muhammad's Night Journey and Ascension (*Isra' wa Mi'raj*). Both involve guided tours of heavenly realms, encounters with angels, and the reception of divine knowledge — suggesting a continuous tradition of prophetic heavenly experience in the Abrahamic faiths.

### Prophetic Intercession
Enoch's intercessory role resonates with the Islamic concept of prophetic intercession (*shafa'ah*), particularly the belief that Muhammad will intercede for his community on the Day of Judgment.

## Theological Significance

For Islamic readers, this chapter illustrates the continuity of prophetic experience across the Abrahamic tradition, with Idris/Enoch's heavenly visions prefiguring the supreme prophetic ascent of Muhammad in the Mi'raj.
