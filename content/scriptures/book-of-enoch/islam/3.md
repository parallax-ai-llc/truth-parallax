## The Regularity of Nature

Islamic interpreters approach this chapter through the figure of Idris (identified with Enoch), one of the prophets mentioned in the Quran. The themes of cosmic order, divine judgment, and natural revelation resonate with Quranic teaching on God's sovereignty over creation and the moral accountability of all beings.

## Key Themes

### Signs in Creation (*Ayat*)
The Quran repeatedly calls attention to natural phenomena as "signs" (*ayat*) of God's existence and power (Q 2:164; 3:190). The chapter's observation of cosmic regularity as evidence of divine governance parallels this Quranic theme of creation as a collection of divine signs pointing toward the Creator.

### Prophetic Wisdom
Islamic tradition credits Idris/Enoch with being among the earliest recipients of divine wisdom, including knowledge of astronomy and natural philosophy. The chapter's attention to cosmic order reflects this tradition of prophetic scientific knowledge.

## Theological Significance

For Islamic readers, this chapter illustrates the universal prophetic message of *tawhid* (divine unity) expressed through the testimony of creation — a theme central to Quranic theology and Islamic natural philosophy.
