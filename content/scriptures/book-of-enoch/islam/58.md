## Chapter 58 — The Third Parable: The Blessed

1 Enoch 58, within the Parables, presents apocalyptic visions of heavenly mysteries and divine judgment. Muslim scholars approach this material as part of the *Isra'iliyyat* tradition, engaging with its prophetic narratives while maintaining the Quran's supremacy as divine revelation.

## Islamic Scholarly Perspective

The apocalyptic themes in this chapter — divine judgment, angelic activity, cosmic secrets — resonate with numerous Quranic passages. The Quran itself contains rich eschatological imagery (Surah al-Waqi'ah 56, Surah al-Qiyamah 75), and Muslim scholars recognize the shared Semitic theological heritage that underlies both the Enochic and Quranic visions.

The figure of Idris, traditionally identified with Enoch in Islamic tradition, is praised in the Quran as "truthful" and "a prophet" whom God "raised to a high station" (Surah Maryam 19:56-57). This Quranic endorsement of Enoch's prophetic status provides a framework for cautious engagement with the wider Enochic literature.

## Theological Parameters

Muslim scholars evaluate the content of this chapter against the criterion of *tawhid* (divine oneness). Elements that affirm God's sovereignty, justice, and mercy are considered consistent with Islamic teaching, while elements that compromise divine unity or elevate created beings to divine status are rejected.
