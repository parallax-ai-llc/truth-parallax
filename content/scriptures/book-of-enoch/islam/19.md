## The Angels' Prison

Islamic interpreters approach this chapter's cosmic geography through the framework of Quranic cosmology and Islamic traditions about the structure of the heavens and the earth, the places of punishment, and the activities of angels.

## Key Themes

### The Seven Heavens
Islamic cosmology affirms the existence of seven heavens (Q 2:29; 67:3), a framework that provides context for understanding the Enochic cosmic journey. The prophetic traditions (*hadith*) about Muhammad's passage through the seven heavens during the Mi'raj share the same cosmological vocabulary.

### Places of Punishment
The Enochic descriptions of places designated for the punishment of rebellious spiritual beings parallel Islamic teaching about Jahannam (Hell) and the punishment of the disobedient. The Quran describes Hell as a place prepared for those who reject God's guidance (Q 3:131).

## Theological Significance

For Islamic readers, this chapter's cosmic geography intersects with the Quranic vision of a morally structured universe where divine justice operates across all realms of existence — from the highest heavens to the deepest places of punishment.
