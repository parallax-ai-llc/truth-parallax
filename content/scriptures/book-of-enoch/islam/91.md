## Chapter 91 — Enoch's Exhortation and the Apocalypse of Weeks

1 Enoch 91 belongs to the Epistle of Enoch (chapters 91-105), containing prophetic woes against the unjust and promises of vindication for the righteous. Muslim scholars engage with this section's ethical vision through the Quranic emphasis on social justice and divine accountability.

## Islamic Scholarly Perspective

The Epistle's denunciation of economic exploitation and its assurance of eschatological justice resonate powerfully with Quranic teachings on social responsibility. The Quran repeatedly warns against dishonest business practices (Surah al-Mutaffifin 83:1-6), the hoarding of wealth (Surah at-Tawbah 9:34-35), and the oppression of the vulnerable. This chapter's moral vision aligns with Islam's emphasis on *'adl* (justice) and *ihsan* (excellence in social relations) as fundamental obligations of the faithful community.

## Comparative Observations

Muslim scholars note the strong parallels between the Epistle's social ethics and the Quranic system of *zakat* (obligatory charity) and care for orphans, widows, and the poor. Both traditions affirm that wealth is a trust from God and that its misuse carries eschatological consequences. The Epistle's apocalyptic framing of social justice — with divine reversal of earthly inequities — mirrors the Quranic promise that the Day of Judgment will reveal the true worth of human actions.
