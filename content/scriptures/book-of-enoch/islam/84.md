## Chapter 84 — Enoch's Prayer After the Dream

1 Enoch 84 is part of the Dream Visions (chapters 83-90), which present an allegorical history of Israel using animal symbolism. Muslim scholars engage with this material through the Quranic understanding of prophetic history and the succession of divine messengers sent to guide humanity.

## Islamic Scholarly Perspective

The Dream Visions' retelling of Israel's history through symbolic narrative resonates with the Quranic presentation of prophetic history as a recurring pattern of divine guidance, human rebellion, and divine judgment. The Quran similarly presents the stories of past prophets — Nuh (Noah), Ibrahim (Abraham), Musa (Moses) — as moral exemplars and warnings (*'ibrah*) for the Muslim community. This chapter's allegorical approach to sacred history reflects a shared conviction that historical events carry theological meaning.

## Comparative Observations

Muslim scholars observe that the Dream Visions' periodization of history parallels the Quranic concept of successive revelations culminating in the final message. While Islam does not accept the Enochic framework, the underlying theology — that God intervenes in history through chosen messengers and that history moves toward a final reckoning — aligns with foundational Islamic beliefs about prophecy (*nubuwwah*) and the Day of Judgment (*Yawm al-Din*).
