## Chapter 107 — Noah's Mission Confirmed

1 Enoch 107 describes the miraculous circumstances surrounding Noah's birth (chapters 106-107). Muslim scholars engage with this material through the Quranic portrayal of Nuh (Noah) as one of the great prophets (*ulu al-'azm*) — the five messengers of greatest resolve.

## Islamic Scholarly Perspective

The Quran presents Nuh as a patient and persistent prophet who preached to his people for 950 years (Surah al-'Ankabut 29:14) before the flood came as divine judgment on the disbelievers. This chapter's account of Noah's miraculous birth, while not found in Islamic sources, reflects the broader tradition of prophetic distinction that Islam affirms — prophets are set apart by Allah from before birth for their divine mission. Muslim scholars note that the Quran's own treatment of prophetic births, particularly that of Isa (Jesus), emphasizes divine intervention in the natural order.

## Comparative Observations

Both the Enochic and Quranic traditions present Noah as a figure of exceptional righteousness chosen by God for a salvific mission. While Islam does not accept the haggadic elaborations found in this chapter, the underlying theology — that God prepares and distinguishes His prophets for their appointed tasks — aligns with the Islamic understanding of prophetic election (*istifa'*) as described throughout the Quran.
