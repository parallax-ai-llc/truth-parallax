## Jerusalem and Surrounding Valleys

Islamic interpreters situate this chapter within the broader tradition of prophetic knowledge about creation's structure that Islam attributes to early prophets, particularly Idris/Enoch. The chapter's cosmological observations resonate with the Quran's invitation to contemplate the signs of God in the natural world.

## Key Themes

### Contemplation of Creation (*Tafakkur*)
The Quran repeatedly exhorts believers to contemplate the created order as a path to knowledge of God (Q 3:191; 88:17-20). The chapter's detailed observation of cosmic features reflects this spiritual discipline of *tafakkur* — reflective contemplation of creation as worship.

### Divine Sovereignty
The orderly cosmos described in this chapter testifies to the absolute sovereignty (*rububiyyah*) of God over all creation — a fundamental principle of Islamic theology that the Quran expresses in passages about God's governance of the heavens and the earth.

## Theological Significance

For Islamic readers, this chapter contributes to the appreciation of Idris/Enoch as a prophet gifted with cosmological knowledge, whose observations of creation's order testify to the same divine unity (*tawhid*) proclaimed by all prophets in the Islamic tradition.
