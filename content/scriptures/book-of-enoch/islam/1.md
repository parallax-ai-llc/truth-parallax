## The Coming Judgment and Prophetic Authority

Islamic interpreters approach 1 Enoch 1 through the identification of Enoch with the Quranic prophet Idris, mentioned in Surah Maryam 19:56-57 and Surah al-Anbiya 21:85-86. The chapter's vision of divine judgment aligns broadly with the Quran's own extensive descriptions of the Day of Judgment (*Yawm al-Qiyamah*).

## Key Themes

### Idris as Prophet
Classical Islamic commentators identify Idris/Enoch as one of the earliest prophets, gifted with knowledge of astronomy, writing, and mathematics. The Enochic tradition's portrayal of Enoch as a recipient of divine revelation is broadly consistent with his Quranic status as "a man of truth, a prophet."

### Eschatological Parallels
The chapter's description of God coming to judge all creation parallels Quranic descriptions of the Last Day: mountains crumbling (Q 56:5-6), the earth shaking (Q 99:1-2), and all humanity assembled before the divine tribunal. Islamic scholars note these convergences as evidence of a shared Abrahamic prophetic tradition.

### Prophetic Continuity
Islam teaches that God sent prophets to every nation (Q 10:47; 16:36), all proclaiming the same essential message of monotheism and judgment. Enoch's prophetic oracle in this chapter fits this paradigm: a universal message of divine judgment delivered by a recognized prophet.

## Theological Significance

For Islamic readers, this chapter confirms the Quranic teaching that prophets before Muhammad proclaimed the reality of divine judgment. The convergence between Enochic and Quranic eschatology supports the Islamic understanding of prophetic continuity across the Abrahamic traditions.
