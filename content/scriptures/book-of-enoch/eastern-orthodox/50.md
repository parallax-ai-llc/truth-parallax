## Chapter 50 — Repentance of the Gentiles

1 Enoch 50, within the Parables, presents visionary content about heavenly realities and eschatological judgment. Eastern Orthodox theology approaches this chapter through the lens of patristic tradition, finding in its apocalyptic imagery authentic spiritual insights that anticipate the fuller revelation in Christ.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

The Orthodox tradition's comfort with apophatic theology — the recognition that God ultimately transcends human categories — prepares Orthodox readers to engage the visionary material in this chapter with both reverence and critical discernment. The apocalyptic genre, with its symbolic language and cosmic scope, resonates with the Orthodox liturgical experience of worship as entry into heavenly reality.

The chapter's emphasis on the revelation of hidden divine mysteries through angelic intermediaries parallels the Orthodox understanding of the liturgy as participation in the angelic worship described in Isaiah 6 and Revelation 4. The Trisagion hymn ("Holy, Holy, Holy") bridges the earthly and heavenly worship that this chapter envisions.

## The Ethiopian Connection

As canonical Scripture in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, this chapter of 1 Enoch carries particular weight within the broader Orthodox world. The Ethiopian preservation of the complete Enochic corpus represents an invaluable contribution to Christian theological heritage.
