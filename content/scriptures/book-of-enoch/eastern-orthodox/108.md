## Chapter 108 — The Concluding Chapter

1 Enoch 108 concludes the Enochic corpus with a final vision of eschatological justice. Eastern Orthodox theology engages with this chapter's dualistic eschatology through the lens of patristic teaching on the Last Things and the ultimate destiny of creation.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

This concluding chapter's vision of eternal reward and punishment is interpreted within the Orthodox tradition through the paradox of divine love experienced differently by the righteous and the wicked. Isaac the Syrian taught that the fire of Gehenna is the fire of God's love — experienced as joy by those who have cultivated receptivity to grace and as torment by those who have hardened their hearts. This chapter's stark eschatological imagery is thus read not as divine vindictiveness but as the inevitable consequence of human freedom in relation to divine love.

## Liturgical Resonance

The themes of final judgment and eternal destiny pervade Orthodox worship, from the hymnography of Meatfare Sunday to the iconographic tradition of the Last Judgment depicted in church narthexes. This chapter's concluding vision of justice finds its deepest expression in the Orthodox prayer for the departed and the confident hope in the resurrection of all flesh.
