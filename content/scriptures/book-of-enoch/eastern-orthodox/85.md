## Chapter 85 — The Second Dream — The Bulls and the Stars

1 Enoch 85 is part of the Dream Visions (chapters 83-90), containing the Animal Apocalypse's allegorical retelling of sacred history. Eastern Orthodox scholars engage with this section's theology of providential history and its anticipation of eschatological renewal.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

The Dream Visions' panoramic view of history as divinely guided toward its eschatological fulfillment resonates with the Orthodox theology of divine economy (*oikonomia*). The Church Fathers — particularly Irenaeus in his concept of *anakephalaiosis* (recapitulation) — understood all of history as moving toward its consummation in Christ. This chapter's symbolic narrative reflects a similar conviction that human history, despite its apparent chaos, unfolds according to divine purpose.

## Liturgical Resonance

The eschatological vision of the Dream Visions — particularly the expectation of a renewed creation and the gathering of the righteous — finds liturgical expression in the Orthodox celebration of Pascha and the Feast of Transfiguration. The transformation of creation anticipated in this chapter's apocalyptic imagery is experienced proleptically in the sacramental life of the Church, where the faithful participate in the age to come.
