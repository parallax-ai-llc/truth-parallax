## Chapter 107 — Noah's Mission Confirmed

1 Enoch 107 describes the miraculous birth of Noah (chapters 106-107), with his radiant appearance and Lamech's astonishment at the child. Eastern Orthodox scholars engage with this material through the lens of theophanic theology and the transfiguration of human nature by divine grace.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

Noah's luminous appearance at birth resonates profoundly with the Orthodox theology of divine light (*phos*). The uncreated light of Tabor, central to Hesychast theology as articulated by Gregory Palamas, represents the divine energies that transfigure human nature. This chapter's description of Noah's radiance can be read as an anticipation of the deification (*theosis*) that Orthodox theology understands as humanity's ultimate vocation — participation in the divine nature (2 Peter 1:4).

## Liturgical Resonance

The Noahic traditions preserved in this chapter find liturgical resonance in the Orthodox blessing of waters at Theophany, which recalls both creation and the flood as moments of divine intervention. Noah's miraculous birth points toward the ultimate miracle of the Incarnation, in which divine and human natures are united in the person of Christ — the true Light that illumines all creation.
