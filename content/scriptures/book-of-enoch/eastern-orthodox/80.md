## Chapter 80 — The Altered Order in the Last Days

1 Enoch 80 belongs to the Astronomical Book (chapters 72-82), presenting the divinely ordained movements of celestial bodies. Eastern Orthodox theology engages with this material through its rich tradition of cosmic liturgy, in which all creation participates in the praise of God.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

The Astronomical Book's vision of cosmic order governed by angelic beings resonates with the Orthodox liturgical understanding of creation as theophany. The Byzantine liturgical tradition celebrates the harmony of the cosmos as reflecting the divine Logos through whom all things were made (John 1:3). This chapter's ordered cosmology anticipates the patristic teaching — developed by Maximus the Confessor and others — that the *logoi* (rational principles) of created things participate in the one Logos.

## Liturgical Resonance

The angelic governance of celestial phenomena described in this chapter finds expression in Orthodox hymnography and iconography. The cosmic liturgy presupposed by the Astronomical Book — in which angels direct the movements of sun, moon, and stars — resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the Divine Liturgy as a participation in the heavenly worship that encompasses all creation in its ceaseless praise of the Holy Trinity.
