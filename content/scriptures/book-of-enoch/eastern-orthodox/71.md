## Chapter 71 — Enoch's Final Vision

1 Enoch 71 presents the exalted "Son of Man" and "Elect One" in eschatological glory. Eastern Orthodox Christology engages profoundly with this material, reading the Enochic Son of Man as a prophetic anticipation of the incarnate Logos who is revealed fully in Jesus Christ.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

Orthodox Christology, grounded in the Chalcedonian definition and the theology of the Seven Ecumenical Councils, understands the Enochic Son of Man as part of the gradual divine pedagogy (*paideia*) through which humanity was prepared for the Incarnation. The pre-existence and heavenly glory attributed to this figure find their complete expression in the Orthodox confession of Christ as consubstantial with the Father.

The theme of theophany — divine manifestation through a glorified human form — resonates with the Orthodox theology of the Transfiguration. The uncreated light revealed on Mount Tabor fulfills the apocalyptic glory that this chapter attributes to the Son of Man.

## Liturgical Resonance

The enthronement imagery in this chapter finds liturgical expression in Orthodox worship, particularly in the hymns and iconography of Christ Pantocrator — the Almighty ruler seated on the throne of glory, surrounded by angelic hosts.
