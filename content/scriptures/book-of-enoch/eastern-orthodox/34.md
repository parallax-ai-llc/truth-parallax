## The Ends of the Earth: West

Eastern Orthodox interpreters read this chapter within Enoch's cosmic survey, finding in its descriptions of heavenly structures and earthly wonders a reflection of the Orthodox conviction that all creation participates in the divine glory and moves toward its eschatological transfiguration.

## Key Themes

### Creation as Theophany
Orthodox theology views the natural world as a continuous revelation of God's glory. The chapter's careful attention to cosmic features and natural wonders reflects this theophanic understanding of creation, which finds expression in Orthodox hymnography and iconographic tradition.

### Eschatological Orientation
The Enochic cosmic journey moves toward a vision of cosmic renewal and divine restoration. This forward-looking orientation resonates with the Orthodox liturgical calendar's eschatological structure, which moves toward the Feast of the Transfiguration and the hope of universal transformation.

## Theological Significance

For Orthodox readers, this chapter contributes to the vision of a cosmos destined for transfiguration — not destruction but renewal — a hope expressed in the Orthodox prayer that God will "make all things new" and in the liturgical celebration of Christ's transfiguration as the promise of creation's ultimate glorification.
