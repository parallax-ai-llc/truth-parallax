## Chapter 84 — Enoch's Prayer After the Dream

1 Enoch 84 belongs to the Dream Visions (chapters 83-90), which contain the Animal Apocalypse — an allegorical history of Israel from creation to the eschatological age. Catholic scholars engage with this section's sophisticated theology of history and its vision of divine providence guiding human affairs toward their appointed end.

## Catholic Theological Engagement

The Animal Apocalypse's panoramic view of history as guided by divine providence resonates with the Catholic theology of salvation history (*Heilsgeschichte*) articulated by theologians from Irenaeus to von Balthasar. This chapter's symbolic retelling of Israel's story through animal imagery demonstrates the same kind of typological and allegorical reading that the Church Fathers employed in their interpretation of the Old Testament.

## Magisterial Context

Catholic biblical scholarship, following *Dei Verbum* and the instruction of the Pontifical Biblical Commission, values the literary and theological creativity of Second Temple Judaism as part of the broader preparation for the Gospel. The Dream Visions' expectation of a messianic age, a final judgment, and a renewed creation anticipate the fullness of revelation that the Church finds in Christ and celebrates in her liturgical life.
