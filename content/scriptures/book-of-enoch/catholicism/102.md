## Chapter 102 — Terrors of the Day of Judgment

1 Enoch 102 is part of the Epistle of Enoch (chapters 91-105), which contains prophetic woes against the wealthy and unjust alongside promises of vindication for the righteous. Catholic scholars find in this section important antecedents to the Church's social teaching and its theology of eschatological justice.

## Catholic Theological Engagement

The Epistle's denunciation of economic injustice and its promise that God will vindicate the oppressed resonate deeply with Catholic social teaching from *Rerum Novarum* to *Laudato Si'*. This chapter's moral vision — that wealth gained through exploitation brings divine condemnation — aligns with the tradition of prophetic social criticism that Catholic teaching has consistently upheld. The preferential option for the poor, articulated at Medellín and Puebla, finds antecedents in this Enochic tradition.

## Magisterial Context

Catholic interpreters situate this chapter within the broader tradition of divine justice that runs from the Hebrew prophets through the Magnificat to the eschatological teachings of Jesus. While the text is not canonical, its ethical witness contributes to the Catholic understanding of how Second Temple Judaism prepared the moral and theological ground for the Gospel's proclamation of the Kingdom of God.
