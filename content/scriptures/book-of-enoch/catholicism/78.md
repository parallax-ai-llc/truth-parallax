## Chapter 78 — The Sun and Moon — Waxing and Waning

1 Enoch 78 forms part of the Astronomical Book (chapters 72-82), presenting a detailed cosmological system governed by divine ordinance. Catholic scholars engage with this material as a witness to the created order reflecting God's wisdom, a theme deeply embedded in Catholic natural law tradition.

## Catholic Theological Engagement

The Astronomical Book's insistence on cosmic regularity under angelic governance resonates with the Catholic tradition of the *lex naturalis* — the natural law written into creation by the Creator. This chapter's ordered cosmology reflects the Thomistic principle that creation participates in divine reason, making the natural world a reliable witness to God's existence and attributes (Romans 1:20). Catholic scholars note that the angelic governance of celestial bodies described here finds echoes in the tradition of angelic stewardship affirmed by Church Fathers including Augustine and Pseudo-Dionysius.

## Magisterial Context

The Pontifical Biblical Commission recognizes the value of intertestamental literature for understanding the development of biblical theology. This chapter's astronomical framework, while reflecting ancient cosmological assumptions, embodies the theological conviction that the universe is purposefully ordered — a conviction central to Catholic teaching on creation and to the dialogue between faith and science encouraged by documents such as *Fides et Ratio*.
