## God's Judgment and Eschatological Renewal

Chapter 10 records the divine response to the Watchers' rebellion: Raphael binds Azazel, Gabriel destroys the Nephilim through internecine warfare, and Michael imprisons Shemihazah until the final judgment. The chapter concludes with a magnificent vision of cosmic renewal.

## Key Themes

### Angelic Agents of Justice
The deployment of named archangels as agents of divine judgment resonates deeply with Catholic angelology. The Church venerates Michael, Gabriel, and Raphael as saints (feast day September 29), and their Enochic roles — warrior, messenger, healer — correspond to their traditional Catholic iconography and devotional functions.

### Purgatorial Imagery
The Watchers' imprisonment "in the valleys of the earth" until the "day of the great judgment" has been compared by Catholic scholars to the doctrine of Purgatory — an intermediate state of punishment that precedes final judgment. While 1 Enoch does not map perfectly onto later Catholic eschatology, the concept of temporal punishment before ultimate resolution contributed to its development.

### The New Creation
The chapter's concluding vision of truth, righteousness, and universal worship anticipates Catholic teaching on the *novissima* (the last things). The renewal of all creation parallels the vision of Revelation 21 and the Catholic hope for the "new heavens and new earth" affirmed in the Catechism (CCC 1042-1050).

## Theological Significance

Catholic readers find in this chapter a comprehensive theology of divine justice that operates through angelic mediation, involves intermediate states of punishment, and culminates in cosmic restoration — all themes that became central to Catholic eschatological doctrine.
