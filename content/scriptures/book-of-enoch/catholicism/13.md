## Enoch's Intercession Rejected

Catholic interpreters read this chapter within Enoch's visionary and intercessory role, finding parallels with Catholic teaching on heavenly mediation, the communion of saints, and the nature of divine revelation through mystical experience.

## Key Themes

### Heavenly Mediation
Enoch's role as intercessor between God and the fallen Watchers resonates with Catholic theology of saintly intercession. While Christ is the unique mediator (1 Timothy 2:5), Catholic teaching holds that the saints participate in his mediatory work — a pattern prefigured by Enoch's heavenly advocacy.

### Mystical Vision
The chapter's visionary content connects to the Catholic mystical tradition, from the Desert Fathers through John of the Cross. The experience of divine revelation through vision and ecstasy is a recognized mode of God's self-communication in Catholic theology.

## Theological Significance

For Catholic readers, this chapter provides early Jewish precedent for the theology of heavenly intercession and mystical vision that became central to Catholic spirituality and doctrinal development.
