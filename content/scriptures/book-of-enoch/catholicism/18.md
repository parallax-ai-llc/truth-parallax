## The Pillars of Heaven

Catholic interpreters read this chapter's cosmic geography as a form of theological cartography, mapping the moral structure of the universe. The journey reveals the places of punishment and reward that correspond to Catholic eschatological teaching about the afterlife's multiple states.

## Key Themes

### Cosmic Moral Order
The chapter's description of cosmic spaces designated for specific purposes — punishment, purgation, reward — resonates with the Catholic understanding of the afterlife as morally differentiated. Heaven, Purgatory, and Hell represent distinct states appropriate to the soul's condition.

### Angelic Guidance
The interpreting angel who guides Enoch through the cosmos prefigures the Catholic teaching on guardian angels and angelic guidance. The Church affirms that angels accompany and guide human beings, a doctrine rooted in texts like this.

## Theological Significance

For Catholic theology, this chapter contributes to the vision of a morally ordered cosmos where every action has eternal consequences — a conviction central to Catholic moral and eschatological teaching.
