## Chapter 108 — The Concluding Chapter

1 Enoch 108 concludes the Enochic corpus with a final affirmation of divine justice — the reward of the righteous and the punishment of the wicked. Catholic scholars view this chapter as a significant witness to the development of eschatological beliefs in Second Temple Judaism.

## Catholic Theological Engagement

This concluding chapter's vision of eternal reward for the righteous and eternal punishment for the wicked aligns with Catholic eschatological teaching on the Four Last Things: death, judgment, heaven, and hell. The chapter's emphasis on the enduring consequences of moral choices resonates with Catholic moral theology's insistence on the eternal significance of human freedom. The text also reflects themes developed in the Catholic doctrine of purgatory insofar as it distinguishes degrees of righteousness and envisions purification.

## Magisterial Context

While not canonical, this chapter's eschatological vision contributes to Catholic understanding of how Jewish eschatological hope evolved toward the fullness of revelation in Christ. The Church's developed teaching on the particular and general judgment, the resurrection of the body, and the life of the world to come represents the fulfillment of the hopes expressed, however imperfectly, in texts like this concluding chapter of 1 Enoch.
