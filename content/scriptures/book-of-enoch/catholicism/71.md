## Chapter 71 — Enoch's Final Vision

1 Enoch 71 presents the "Son of Man" and "Elect One" — figures of supreme eschatological authority who sit on the throne of glory to judge the living and the dead. Catholic scholars engage deeply with this material, seeing in it important precursors to the Church's developed Christology.

## Catholic Theological Engagement

Catholic Christological tradition draws upon multiple streams of Jewish messianic expectation, and the Enochic Son of Man represents one of the most significant. This chapter's portrayal of a pre-existent, heavenly figure who receives worship and exercises divine judgment anticipated — in Catholic understanding — the full revelation of Christ's divine nature.

The Pontifical Biblical Commission has acknowledged the importance of Second Temple Jewish literature for understanding the development of Christological titles. The Son of Man figure in the Parables of Enoch illuminates how the earliest Christians could understand Jesus as both human and transcendent.

## Magisterial Context

While Enoch is not canonical, Catholic biblical scholarship integrates its witness into the broader understanding of salvation history. The themes of this chapter find their fulfillment in Catholic teaching on Christ as judge, king, and pre-existent Word.
