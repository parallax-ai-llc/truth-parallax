## Overview

From the religious Shinto perspective, Prayer 22, Ritual of Lustration, is a living liturgical text whose correct recitation establishes genuine communion between the human community and the kami. Religious interpreters approach this prayer not as a historical artifact but as an active instrument of worship whose power depends on correct performance within the prescribed ritual context.

This prayer addresses fundamental concerns of ritual of lustration, engaging themes that are central to both the Shinto tradition and the broader human experience of seeking divine aid and blessing.

## Key Themes

### Liturgical Efficacy

Religious interpreters understand this prayer as possessing genuine spiritual efficacy when performed correctly by an authorized priest within the proper ritual context. The prayer's power is not metaphorical but real: it establishes a connection between the visible world of human community and the invisible world of the kami, transmitting human gratitude and petition and receiving divine blessing and protection in return.

### Continuity of Tradition

This prayer connects contemporary worshipers to an unbroken tradition of kami worship stretching back to the earliest periods of Japanese civilization. Religious readers emphasize that the prayer's efficacy depends not only on correct performance but on the spiritual lineage within which it is transmitted — a lineage that carries the accumulated merit and authority of generations of faithful practice.

## Key Verses/Passages

Prayer 22 contains several passages of particular significance for religious shinto interpretation. The invocatory formulas, the mythological references, and the specific petitions reveal how the ancient Japanese priesthood understood the nature of divine-human communication and the role of ritual language in maintaining cosmic harmony.

## Commentary Tradition

The religious tradition has engaged with this prayer through its distinctive interpretive lens, finding in its language and structure evidence for broader claims about the nature of kami, the power of ritual, and the relationship between human community and sacred reality. Commentators have paid particular attention to the prayer's archaic linguistic features and its placement within the broader liturgical calendar.
