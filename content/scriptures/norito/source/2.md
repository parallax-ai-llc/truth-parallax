---
title: "Norito - Chapter 2"
scripture: "norito"
chapter: 2
---

**1** I humbly speak before the Sovereign Deities. Hear these words of the Great Purification.

**2** In the High Plain of Heaven, the ancestral gods and goddesses gave their divine command, saying: "Let our august grandchild rule the Land of the Plentiful Reed Plains and the Land of Fresh Rice-ears."

**3** In obedience to this command, the Heavenly Grandchild descended from the heavenly rock-seat, pushing aside the many-layered clouds of Heaven, and cleaving a path through the clouds.

**4** He descended upon the peak of Takachiho in Hyuga, and from there his descendants have ruled this land as the Sovereign.

**5** In the land thus entrusted to the Sovereign, the people committed various transgressions, both heavenly offenses and earthly offenses.

**6** Among the heavenly offenses are: breaking down the ridges of rice paddies, covering up the irrigation ditches, opening the sluices, sowing seeds over the seeds already sown, planting stakes in the fields, flaying animals alive, flaying animals backwards, and defiling the weaving halls.

**7** Among the earthly offenses are: cutting the living skin, cutting the dead skin, suffering from white leprosy, suffering from growths, the sin of a man with his own mother, the sin of a man with his own daughter, the calamity of crawling worms, the calamity of a deity on high, the calamity of birds on high, and the sin of killing animals and using sorcery.

**8** When such offenses are committed, let the priests of Heaven cut off the bottom and cut off the top of heavenly saplings, and make them into a complete array of one thousand offering-tables.

**9** Let them cut off the bottom and cut off the top of heavenly reeds, and slice them into thin strips for expiatory offerings.

**10** Then pronouncing the mighty words of the Heavenly ritual, let the offenses be blown away to the Root-Country, the Bottom-Country.

**11** When they are thus blown away, the Deity of the Swift-Opening, who dwells in the rapids of the swift rivers which fall surging from the high mountains, shall carry them out to the great sea-plain.

**12** When the offenses are thus swept away, the Deity who dwells in the currents of the great sea-plain shall swallow them up.

**13** When the offenses are thus swallowed up, the Deity of the Breath-Blowing shall blow them away to the Root-Country, the Bottom-Country.

**14** When the offenses are thus blown away, the Deity who dwells in the Root-Country shall disperse them and destroy them utterly.

**15** When they are thus destroyed, from this day onward there shall be no offenses. Let all be purified and cleansed, as the wind blows away the morning mists, as the evening breezes disperse the evening mists.
