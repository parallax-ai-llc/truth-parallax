---
title: "Norito - Chapter 3"
scripture: "norito"
chapter: 3
---

**1** I humbly speak before the Great Deity of the Harvest.

**2** In this month, in this day, we offer the first-fruits of the autumn harvest, the rice grown with the blessing of the water from the heavenly springs.

**3** The rice of the long-stemmed and the short-stemmed grain, the rice of the early-ripening and the late-ripening harvest, all are gathered and presented.

**4** We raise high the soaring necks of the countless wine jars, and set in rows the bellies of the countless wine jars.

**5** We offer the produce of the mountains: the sweet herbs and the bitter herbs, the rough things of the mountain and the soft things of the plain.

**6** We offer the produce of the blue sea-plain: the broad-finned and the narrow-finned fishes, the sea-weeds of the deep and the sea-weeds of the shore.

**7** All these things, piled high like a range of hills, we present as offerings to the Great Deity.

**8** In return, may the Great Deity bestow the late-ripening harvest in abundance, that the farmers may bend beneath the weight of the sheaves upon their backs.

**9** May the Great Deity accept these offerings with a tranquil heart, and may the land be blessed with a harvest abundant as the autumn clouds.
