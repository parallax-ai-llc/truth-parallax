## Overview

Academic analysis of Prayer 20, Drought Relief Prayer, examines the text's linguistic features, historical context, and ritual function using the methods of philology, historical criticism, and comparative liturgical studies. Scholars investigate the prayer's date of composition, its relationship to other norito and early Japanese literary texts, and the social and political circumstances that shaped its content.

This prayer addresses fundamental concerns of drought relief prayer, engaging themes that are central to both the Shinto tradition and the broader human experience of seeking divine aid and blessing.

## Key Themes

### Linguistic Analysis

Academic study of this prayer's language reveals archaic grammatical forms, specialized ritual vocabulary, and rhetorical structures that provide evidence for the development of early Japanese religious discourse. The prayer's avoidance of Chinese loanwords (kango) in favor of pure Japanese vocabulary (yamato kotoba) is significant for understanding the conscious construction of a distinctly Japanese sacred register.

### Historical Context

Scholars situate this prayer within the specific historical circumstances of its composition and the broader development of the ritsuryō state's religious institutions. The prayer's content — its references to specific kami, places, and ritual occasions — provides evidence for the political, social, and economic concerns of the period in which it was produced or revised.

## Key Verses/Passages

Prayer 20 contains several passages of particular significance for academic shinto studies interpretation.
