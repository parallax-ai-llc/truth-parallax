---
title: "Engishiki Section 24: The Yin-Yang Divination Methods"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 24
---

The methods of yin-yang divination used by the Bureau of Divination are detailed in this scroll. The fundamental principle is that all phenomena result from the interaction of yin and yang forces and the five elements: wood, fire, earth, metal, and water. Diviners analyze these forces to predict outcomes and prescribe remedies.

Directional taboos form a major part of the yin-yang system. Certain directions are dangerous on certain days, and travel, construction, and other activities must be planned to avoid these inauspicious vectors. The scroll provides tables for calculating which directions are blocked on which days, and prescribes the practice of katatagae, directional avoidance, when an unavoidable journey would take one in a dangerous direction.

The scroll also addresses the use of protective talismans, the performance of purification rituals based on yin-yang theory, and the diagnosis of spiritual ailments through the analysis of the patient's birth date, current directional influences, and other factors. The integration of Chinese cosmological theory with native Japanese religious practices is particularly evident in these procedures, which blend continental learning with indigenous ritual traditions.
