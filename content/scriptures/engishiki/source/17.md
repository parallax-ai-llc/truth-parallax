---
title: "Engishiki Section 17: The Tribute Offerings to Major Shrines"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 17
---

This scroll catalogs the tribute offerings that must be sent to major shrines throughout the provinces. The system of shrine offerings formed a significant part of the government's fiscal obligations, requiring the collection and transport of specific goods from designated areas to hundreds of shrines.

The offerings include textiles of various types and qualities, metals both worked and raw, agricultural products, seafood, and manufactured goods. Each shrine's allocation reflects its rank, its deity's specific preferences as determined by tradition, and the resources available in its region. Mountain shrines might receive different offerings than coastal ones, reflecting local ecology and the nature of the kami worshipped.

The administrative complexity of this system is evident in the detailed transport schedules, the designation of responsible officials, and the penalties for late or incomplete delivery. The scroll also addresses the maintenance of offering materials in storage, the procedures for replacing items damaged in transit, and the accounting methods used to track the enormous flow of goods through the shrine offering system.
