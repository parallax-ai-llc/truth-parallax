---
title: "Engishiki Section 28: The Palace Guard Regulations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 28
---

The Palace Guard regulations establish the procedures for protecting the imperial residence and maintaining order within the capital. Multiple guard units, organized by function and area of responsibility, provide continuous security for the emperor, the palace buildings, and the gates connecting the imperial enclosure to the city beyond.

The scroll specifies the number of guards on each watch, their equipment, their patrol routes, and the signals used to communicate alerts. Guard duty rotations ensure continuous coverage while allowing individual soldiers adequate rest. The procedures for responding to fires, intrusions, and other emergencies are detailed, including the chain of command for authorizing the use of force.

The ceremonial duties of the guards are equally important. They form honor escorts for the emperor's processions, maintain order during court ceremonies, and provide the formal framework within which the ritual life of the palace unfolds. The scroll prescribes their ceremonial dress, their positioning during various types of events, and their role in the elaborate choreography of court life. The guards thus serve both practical security and symbolic functions, embodying the ordered power of the imperial state.
