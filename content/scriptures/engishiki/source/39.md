---
title: "Engishiki Section 39: The Tribute Goods from Western Provinces"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 39
---

The western provinces, politically and culturally closer to the capital, provide a different array of tribute goods reflecting their more developed economies and their connections to continental trade routes. This scroll catalogs the contributions of provinces along the Sanindo, Sanyodo, and Nankaido circuits.

The Inland Sea provinces supply salt, marine products, and the distinctive textiles for which the western regions are famous. The metalworking traditions of Bizen and neighboring provinces are reflected in their tribute of swords, tools, and other iron products. The provinces of Shikoku contribute specialty papers, indigo dye, and tropical products that reach them through maritime trade.

Kyushu's tribute reflects its position as Japan's window to the Asian continent. Dazaifu, the government headquarters in Kyushu, manages both the collection of local tribute and the processing of imported goods that enter Japan through its ports. The scroll specifies the handling of these imported luxury goods, including Chinese silk, Korean ceramics, and Southeast Asian aromatics, as they pass through the tribute system into the court's storehouses.
