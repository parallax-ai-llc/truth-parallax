---
title: "Engishiki Section 23: The Calendar and Astronomical Observations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 23
---

This scroll details the procedures for astronomical observation and calendar compilation. The Onmyoryo maintains a staff of astronomers who observe the movements of celestial bodies, record eclipses and unusual phenomena, and compile the annual calendar. The calendar determines not only the dates of festivals but also the auspicious and inauspicious qualities of each day, information essential for the conduct of all government and personal affairs.

The astronomical instruments and observation platforms are described, along with the schedule of regular observations. Eclipses, comets, meteor showers, and unusual star positions must be reported immediately to the court, as they are considered omens requiring ritual response. The scroll prescribes the ceremonies to be performed during solar and lunar eclipses, including the beating of drums and the recitation of protective prayers.

The calendar system combines Chinese astronomical calculations with Japanese ritual requirements. The determination of intercalary months, the assignment of auspicious and inauspicious qualities to days and directions, and the identification of special days within each month all follow detailed computational procedures preserved in this scroll. Calendar errors are treated as serious offenses, as they can cause rituals to be performed at improper times.
