---
title: "Engishiki Section 41: The Management of State Granaries"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 41
---

Government granaries serve as both fiscal storehouses and emergency reserves. This scroll details the management of the state's rice stores, including procedures for receiving tax rice, maintaining storage conditions, distributing grain for government purposes, and releasing reserves during famines and other emergencies.

The scroll specifies the construction standards for granary buildings, which must protect their contents from moisture, pests, and fire. The procedures for measuring grain as it enters and leaves storage ensure accurate accounting, and the record-keeping requirements create an audit trail from collection through distribution. Different types of rice stores, including regular tax reserves, emergency famine reserves, and seed rice stores, each have their own management procedures.

The distribution of stored rice serves multiple purposes: payment of official salaries, provision of offerings to shrines, supply of military forces, and relief of famine-stricken populations. The authorization procedures for each type of distribution reflect the different levels of urgency and the different budgetary categories involved. The scroll also addresses the inevitable losses from spoilage and pest damage, establishing acceptable wastage rates and the accounting procedures for writing off damaged stores.
