---
title: "Engishiki Section 29: The Court Dress and Vestments Code"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 29
---

Court dress regulations reflect the hierarchical nature of Heian society, with every detail of clothing, color, and accessory conveying information about the wearer's rank, office, and role. This scroll prescribes the garments to be worn for each type of ceremony, from the most formal state occasions to everyday court attendance.

The color system is particularly elaborate. Specific shades of purple, red, green, blue, and other colors are reserved for particular ranks, and unauthorized use of a restricted color is a punishable offense. The scroll specifies the dyes and techniques used to produce each shade, the fabrics appropriate for each season, and the patterns that may be woven or embroidered onto garments of various ranks.

Accessories including headgear, footwear, fans, and ceremonial implements are similarly regulated. The scroll addresses both the aesthetic and practical aspects of court dress, recognizing that proper clothing serves to maintain the visual order of the court while also providing appropriate protection against the elements. Seasonal changes in dress are prescribed with dates for the transition from winter to summer clothing and back, reflecting the Japanese aesthetic sensitivity to the passage of seasons.
