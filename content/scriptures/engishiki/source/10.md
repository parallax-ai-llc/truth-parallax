---
title: "Engishiki Section 10: The Great Purification Ceremony"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 10
---

The tenth scroll expands upon the Great Purification ceremony with additional details and variant procedures. It addresses the purification of the sovereign specifically, which requires a separate ceremony performed by specially designated ritualists. The emperor's purification involves additional offerings and prayers directed to the ancestral deities of the imperial line.

The scroll also prescribes purification procedures for specific forms of pollution. Contact with death requires a period of seclusion and multiple purification rites. The burning of a dwelling requires special prayers to prevent the fire's pollution from spreading. The birth of a child, while joyous, creates ritual pollution that must be addressed before the parents can resume participation in court ceremonies.

The concept underlying these procedures is that pollution, or kegare, is not primarily a moral category but a disruption of the proper spiritual order. It accumulates naturally through the activities of daily life, and without regular removal, it threatens the harmony between the human world and the world of the kami. The Engishiki thus treats purification not as punishment but as essential maintenance of the cosmic order.
