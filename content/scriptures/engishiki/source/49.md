---
title: "Engishiki Section 49: The Regulations for Temple Lands and Monks"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 49
---

This scroll continues the regulation of Buddhist institutions, focusing on the management of temple lands and the conduct of the monastic community. Temple lands, like shrine lands, are exempt from ordinary taxation but subject to specific regulations governing their use and the distribution of their products.

The scroll addresses the problem of unauthorized expansion of temple landholdings, which threatens the tax base by removing land from the rolls. Procedures for registering temple lands, investigating claims of illegal expansion, and returning improperly acquired lands to the tax rolls are prescribed in detail.

The conduct regulations for monks and nuns cover a range of issues from daily discipline to serious offenses. The scroll prescribes penalties for violations of monastic rules, including unauthorized absence from the monastery, engagement in commercial activities, and various forms of moral transgression. The state's interest in monastic discipline reflects both its role as patron and its concern that undisciplined clergy could become sources of social disorder.
