---
title: "Engishiki Section 16: The Kamo Special Festival Procedures"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 16
---

This scroll provides additional procedures for the Kamo Special Festival and related ceremonies. The temporary festivals, performed in response to specific divine communications received through divination, require the mobilization of significant resources on short notice. The scroll addresses the administrative procedures for authorizing these special observances and allocating the necessary materials and personnel.

The scroll details the role of divination in determining when special festivals are needed. The Urabe diviners examine the cracks in heated turtle shell or deer scapula to determine whether current misfortunes are caused by neglected deities, and if so, which deities require attention. The interpretation of these oracular signs follows detailed guidelines preserved in this and related scrolls.

The procedures for responding to divine displeasure include not only offerings and prayers but also practical measures such as the repair of shrine buildings, the restoration of shrine lands that have been encroached upon, and the punishment of officials who have neglected their ritual duties. The scroll thus reveals the reciprocal nature of the relationship between the state and the kami, in which divine favor must be continually earned and maintained.
