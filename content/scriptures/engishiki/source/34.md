---
title: "Engishiki Section 34: The Metalwork and Lacquer Production"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 34
---

Metalworking regulations cover the production of tools, weapons, ceremonial objects, and decorative items by government workshops. This scroll details the operations of the Tanokuchimonodokoro and related bureaus, specifying the techniques, materials, and quality standards for various metal products.

The production of swords and other weapons for the palace guards and provincial forces is prescribed, with specifications for blade length, tempering methods, and mounting styles. Ceremonial metalwork, including mirrors, ritual implements, and decorative fittings for shrine and palace buildings, requires the highest levels of craftsmanship. The scroll specifies the alloys to be used for different purposes and the testing procedures for ensuring quality.

Lacquer production is closely related to metalwork in the Engishiki's administrative framework. The scroll addresses the cultivation of lacquer trees, the harvesting and processing of lacquer sap, and the techniques for applying lacquer to wooden, metal, and textile surfaces. The production of lacquerware for court use and shrine offerings requires multiple coats, careful drying, and often the application of gold or silver decoration. The scroll prescribes the standards of workmanship and the inspection procedures that ensure only the finest work reaches the court.
