---
title: "Engishiki Section 21: The Management of Shrine Lands"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 21
---

The management of shrine lands forms a crucial aspect of the Engishiki's administrative provisions. This scroll establishes the procedures for surveying, registering, and managing the agricultural lands and forests that support shrines throughout the nation. Shrine lands are exempt from ordinary taxation but are subject to their own regulatory framework.

The scroll addresses the recurring problem of encroachment on shrine lands by local magnates and provincial officials. Procedures for investigating and resolving land disputes are prescribed, with penalties for those found to have illegally occupied shrine property. The Jingikan maintains registers of shrine lands and conducts periodic inspections to verify that boundaries are being respected.

The products of shrine lands must be used for specific purposes: the support of priests and shrine attendants, the provision of festival offerings, and the maintenance of shrine buildings and grounds. The scroll prohibits the diversion of shrine land income to private use and establishes accounting procedures to prevent corruption. The management of shrine forests, which provide timber for periodic rebuilding, receives particular attention, with cutting schedules designed to ensure a sustainable supply of building materials.
