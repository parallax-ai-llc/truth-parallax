---
title: "Engishiki Section 27: The Court Music and Dance Ceremonies"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 27
---

Court music and dance, collectively known as gagaku, play an essential role in state ceremonies and religious observances. This scroll details the organization of the court music bureau, the training of musicians and dancers, and the specific performances required for each ceremony and festival.

The repertoire includes pieces derived from Chinese, Korean, and Central Asian traditions as well as indigenous Japanese compositions. The scroll classifies these into Left Music, associated with China and performed in red costumes, and Right Music, associated with Korea and performed in green. Each ceremony requires specific musical pieces performed in prescribed order, with the instruments, tempo, and number of performers all specified.

The training of court musicians follows a hereditary system, with musical families transmitting their art across generations. The scroll specifies the instruments each family is responsible for mastering, the examinations students must pass, and the ranks and privileges of accomplished performers. The preservation of these musical traditions through precise bureaucratic regulation has contributed to the survival of gagaku as the oldest continuously performed orchestral music tradition in the world.
