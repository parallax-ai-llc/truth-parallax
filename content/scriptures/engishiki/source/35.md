---
title: "Engishiki Section 35: The Wood and Construction Regulations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 35
---

This scroll regulates the production of wooden items and the management of government construction projects. The Treasury Bureau's woodworking division produces furniture, containers, architectural fittings, and a vast array of other wooden goods essential to court life. The scroll specifies the types of wood appropriate for each use, the construction techniques to be employed, and the finishing methods that must be applied.

The construction regulations address the building and maintenance of government offices, palace buildings, and shrine structures. The scroll prescribes the dimensions, materials, and architectural styles appropriate for buildings of different ranks and functions. The procedures for the periodic rebuilding of the Ise Shrine, requiring enormous quantities of precisely specified timber, receive particular attention.

The management of government forests is closely linked to construction regulations. The scroll designates specific forests as reserves for particular types of construction timber and establishes cutting schedules designed to ensure sustainable yields. The transportation of timber from mountain forests to construction sites in the capital and provinces requires coordination of labor, waterway transport, and road maintenance, all of which are addressed in these regulations.
