---
title: "Engishiki Section 36: The Pottery and Ceramics Production"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 36
---

Pottery and ceramics production for government use is regulated in this scroll. The official kilns produce a range of wares from simple utilitarian vessels to refined ceremonial pieces. The scroll specifies the clay sources, forming techniques, and firing procedures for different types of pottery, reflecting the sophisticated ceramic technology of early Japan.

The production of ritual vessels for shrine use requires particular attention to purity and form. These vessels must be made from designated clay sources, formed by purified craftsmen, and fired in kilns maintained in ritual cleanliness. Some ritual vessels are used only once and then buried, requiring continuous production to meet demand.

The scroll also addresses the production of roof tiles for government and shrine buildings, storage jars for government granaries and storehouses, and specialty items such as the large vessels used in sake brewing. The training of potters, the maintenance of kilns, and the quality inspection of finished products are all regulated in detail. Provincial kilns producing ceramics as tax payments must meet specific standards, and the scroll establishes the inspection procedures applied to these tributary wares.
