---
title: "Engishiki Section 6: The Monthly Offerings at Ise"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 6
---

The sixth scroll establishes the monthly offering schedule for the Grand Shrine of Ise. Each month has its own distinctive offerings reflecting the agricultural cycle and seasonal resources. In the first month, offerings emphasize fresh greens and early fish. By summer, the offerings shift to include seasonal fruits and special preparations to protect the divine food stores from spoilage.

The scroll specifies which offerings are provided from court revenues, which come from designated shrine lands, and which are supplied as tribute from specific provinces. The logistics of transporting perishable offerings to Ise from distant regions required careful coordination, and the scroll addresses the maintenance of relay stations and the duties of transport officials.

Special monthly rituals accompany the regular offerings. Purification ceremonies for the shrine staff, inspections of the sacred buildings, and maintenance of the shrine forests are all scheduled within the monthly cycle. The scroll also addresses the management of the shrine's sacred horses, the birds kept within the precincts, and the fish raised in sacred ponds, all of which served both practical and symbolic purposes.
