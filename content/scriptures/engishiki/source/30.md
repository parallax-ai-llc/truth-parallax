The Imperial Table provisions detail the food served to the emperor and the procedures for its preparation. The Imperial Kitchen, or Daizenshiki, employs specialists in various culinary arts and maintains strict standards of hygiene and ritual purity. All food served to the emperor must be prepared by purified personnel using ingredients from designated sources.

The scroll specifies the daily menu, which varies by season and follows a rotation designed to provide nutritional balance and culinary variety. The emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients reflects both practical health considerations and aesthetic values. Fish, both freshwater and marine, features prominently, along with rice, vegetables, pickles, and various prepared dishes.

Special feast menus for ceremonies, festivals, and diplomatic occasions are prescribed separately, with each event requiring its own distinctive spread. The logistics of provisioning the imperial table from distant provinces, maintaining food stores, and coordinating the work of multiple kitchen units reveal the enormous complexity of feeding the court. The scroll also addresses the disposal of food remains, which must be handled with care to prevent their use in sorcery directed against the sovereign.
