---
title: "Engishiki Section 26: The Prescriptions and Herbal Remedies"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 26
---

This scroll is a pharmaceutical compendium listing medicinal preparations and their applications. The remedies draw upon both Chinese and Japanese herbal traditions, combining sophisticated pharmaceutical theory with local botanical knowledge. Each entry specifies the ingredients, their preparation methods, the conditions they treat, and the dosages appropriate for patients of different ages and constitutions.

The preparations range from simple herbal infusions to complex compounds requiring multiple ingredients processed through elaborate procedures of drying, grinding, boiling, and fermentation. The scroll addresses the treatment of common ailments such as fevers, digestive disorders, wounds, and skin conditions, as well as more serious conditions including respiratory diseases, internal injuries, and childbirth complications.

The sourcing of ingredients is specified, with certain herbs designated as tribute items from specific provinces known for their quality. The scroll also addresses the preparation of medicines for ritual purposes, including substances used in purification ceremonies and protective preparations distributed during epidemics. The intersection of medical and ritual practice is particularly evident in the treatment of conditions attributed to spiritual causes, where pharmaceutical remedies are combined with prayers and purification rites.
