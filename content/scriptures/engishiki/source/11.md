---
title: "Engishiki Section 11: The Register of Deities Receiving Offerings"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 11
---

The Jinmyocho, the Register of Deities, is one of the most important sections of the Engishiki. This scroll begins the enumeration of all officially recognized shrines and their deities, listing 2,861 shrines housing 3,132 kami. The deities are organized by province and district, beginning with Yamashiro Province surrounding the capital.

Each entry specifies the name of the deity, the shrine where they are venerated, and the level of offerings they receive from the state. The highest-ranked shrines are designated as receiving offerings at the Toshigoi festival, the Tsukinami festivals, and the Ainame festival, while lower-ranked shrines receive offerings only at Toshigoi. Some shrines are further designated as myojin taisha, great shrines of distinguished deities, reflecting their special importance.

This register provides an invaluable snapshot of the religious landscape of early tenth-century Japan. It reveals the geographic distribution of kami worship, the relative importance of different deities, and the extent of state involvement in religious affairs. Many shrines listed in the Jinmyocho continue to exist today, and their inclusion in this ancient register is a source of great prestige.
