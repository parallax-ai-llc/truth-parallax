---
title: "Engishiki Section 4: The Ise Shrine Ceremonies"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 4
---

The fourth scroll is devoted entirely to the ceremonies and administration of the Grand Shrine of Ise, the supreme sanctuary of Amaterasu Omikami, ancestral deity of the imperial line. It prescribes the duties of the Saigu, the Imperial Princess who serves as the chief priestess, and the elaborate rituals of her selection, purification, and journey to Ise.

The scroll details the twenty-year cycle of shrine rebuilding, the Shikinen Sengu, specifying the types of wood, the dimensions of the buildings, and the ritual procedures for transferring the sacred mirror and other divine treasures to the new structures. Every pillar, beam, and thatching element must be prepared according to exact specifications, and the craftsmen must undergo ritual purification before touching the materials.

The daily rituals at Ise are enumerated with precision. Each morning and evening, offerings of rice, water, salt, and other foods must be presented to the deity. The fires for cooking these offerings must be kindled by friction, never from an existing flame. The water must be drawn from a designated sacred well. The priests who perform these duties must maintain strict purity, abstaining from contact with death, sickness, and various polluting substances.
