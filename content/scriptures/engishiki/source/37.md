---
title: "Engishiki Section 37: The Provincial Tax Regulations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 37
---

The provincial tax system provides the economic foundation for the entire apparatus of government described in the Engishiki. This scroll details the types and quantities of goods that each province must provide to the central government, including rice, textiles, metals, and various specialty products.

The tax categories include the so, a rice tax based on land area; the yo, a labor tax that could be commuted to goods; and the cho, a textile tax paid in locally produced fabrics. The scroll specifies the rates for each category, the procedures for assessment and collection, and the mechanisms for transporting tax goods to the capital.

The system accommodates regional variation, with each province's obligations reflecting its particular resources and capabilities. Coastal provinces provide marine products, mountain provinces supply timber and metals, and agricultural regions contribute rice and other crops. The scroll addresses the procedures for adjusting tax obligations in response to natural disasters, crop failures, and other hardships, reflecting a pragmatic recognition that rigid enforcement could be counterproductive.
