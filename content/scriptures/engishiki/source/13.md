---
title: "Engishiki Section 13: The Kasuga and Hirano Festivals"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 13
---

This scroll addresses the procedures for the Kasuga Shrine at Nara, the ancestral shrine of the Fujiwara clan, and the Hirano Shrine in the capital. The Kasuga festivals reflect the enormous political influence of the Fujiwara family, who dominated court politics throughout the Heian period. The offerings to Kasuga are exceptionally generous, befitting the shrine of the most powerful clan's tutelary deities.

The Kasuga festival procedures specify the preparation of the imperial messenger, the decoration of the shrine, and the performance of sacred dances including the kagura. The scroll details the Fujiwara clan's special privileges in relation to the shrine, including their role in appointing priests and their contributions to festival expenses.

The Hirano Shrine, though less politically prominent, is notable for its association with the imperial lineage's connection to the Korean peninsula. Its four deities receive regular state offerings, and its spring festival features distinctive ritual elements including the planting of cherry trees. The scroll prescribes the offerings, the ranks of attending officials, and the special foods and sake to be provided for the festival feast.
