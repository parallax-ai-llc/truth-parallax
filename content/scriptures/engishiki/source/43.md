---
title: "Engishiki Section 43: The Regulations for Barriers and Markets"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 43
---

Barriers and markets are key points of government control over the movement of people and goods. This scroll details the regulations for the barrier stations that control access to the capital region and the market regulations that govern commercial activity in the capital and provinces.

The three great barriers of Suzuka, Arachi, and Fuwa guard the approaches to the Kinai region from the east and north. The scroll specifies the procedures for inspecting travelers, checking their credentials, and controlling the movement of weapons, fugitives, and contraband. The barrier guards operate under strict orders regarding who may pass and under what conditions, with special procedures for noble travelers, imperial messengers, and foreign envoys.

Market regulations address the two official markets in the capital, the East Market and the West Market, as well as provincial markets. The scroll specifies the operating hours, the goods that may be sold, the weights and measures to be used, and the officials responsible for maintaining order and preventing fraud. The regulation of prices, the prohibition of certain transactions, and the procedures for resolving commercial disputes are all addressed, revealing the government's active role in managing economic activity.
