---
title: "Engishiki Section 42: The Regulations for Post Stations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 42
---

The post station system, or ekisei, provides the transportation and communication infrastructure essential to governing the scattered provinces from the capital. This scroll details the location, staffing, and provisioning of post stations along the major routes connecting the capital to the provinces.

Each station maintains a complement of horses, grooms, and support staff, along with lodging facilities for official travelers. The scroll specifies the number and quality of horses to be maintained at each station, the fodder and equipment to be provided, and the procedures for replacing horses that become unfit for service. The speed of official communication depends upon the efficiency of horse exchanges at each station.

The authorization system for using post horses distinguishes between different levels of official urgency. The highest-priority messages, marked with multiple bells on the courier's credential, require the fastest horses and the most rapid service. The scroll prescribes penalties for unauthorized use of post horses and for station masters who fail to maintain their facilities and animals in proper condition. The post station system thus serves as both the nervous system of the state and a significant fiscal obligation on the provinces through which the routes pass.
