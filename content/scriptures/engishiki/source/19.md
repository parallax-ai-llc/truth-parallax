---
title: "Engishiki Section 19: The Appointment of Shrine Officials"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 19
---

The procedures for appointing shrine officials reflect the hierarchical structure of the Heian bureaucratic state. Senior shrine positions are filled by imperial decree, while junior positions are determined by hereditary succession within designated families. This scroll details the ceremonies of appointment, the insignia of office, and the administrative responsibilities of each rank.

The highest shrine officials report to the Jingikan and are subject to the same evaluation system applied to secular bureaucrats. Their performance is assessed annually based on the proper conduct of ceremonies, the maintenance of shrine buildings and grounds, and the management of shrine finances. Outstanding service may be rewarded with promotion or additional land grants, while negligence results in demotion or dismissal.

The scroll addresses the special case of female shrine officials, including the chief priestesses at Ise and Kamo. While most priestly positions are held by men, certain shrines maintain traditions of female service dating to mythological times. The appointment and duties of these women are prescribed with the same precision as those of their male counterparts, reflecting their essential role in maintaining the proper relationship between the human world and the divine.
