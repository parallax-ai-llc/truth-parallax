---
title: "Engishiki Section 3: Prayers for Extraordinary Occasions"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 3
---

This scroll addresses prayers for extraordinary and emergency occasions when the standard ritual calendar proves insufficient. When epidemics sweep through the capital, special purification rites must be performed at the four corners of the city, with offerings cast into rivers to carry away the pollution. When fires threaten the palace, wind-calming rituals are prescribed, invoking the deity Shinatsuhiko.

If foreign envoys arrive bearing unknown spiritual influences, purification barriers must be established and rituals performed to neutralize any harmful forces they might carry. When the emperor falls ill, special prayers are offered to the deity of his birth star, and offerings are sent to shrines associated with healing. The scroll also prescribes the harae purification ceremony for various forms of pollution including contact with death, blood, and certain animals.

The emergency rites for military expeditions specify the prayers to be offered before armies march, the protective talismans to be distributed to commanders, and the thanksgiving rituals to be performed upon victorious return. The scroll concludes with rituals for responding to unusual natural phenomena such as eclipses, comets, and the appearance of unusual animals, all of which require immediate ritual response.
