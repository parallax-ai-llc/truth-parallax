---
title: "Engishiki Section 7: The Toshigoi Festival and Harvest Prayers"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 7
---

The Toshigoi festival, the Spring Prayer for Harvest, is one of the most important ceremonies in the ritual calendar. This scroll details the prayers offered to ensure the success of the year's rice crop. The Nakatomi ritualists deliver the norito prayer simultaneously at shrines throughout the capital, while messengers carry offerings to the 3,132 deities listed in the official divine register.

The prayer text recounts the mythological origins of rice cultivation, tracing it to the heavenly deities who provided the first seeds. It addresses both the heavenly kami and the earthly spirits of field, river, and mountain, asking each to fulfill their role in bringing the crop to fruition. The prayer is notable for its exhaustive enumeration of potential threats, from insects and floods to winds and drought, each of which the kami are asked to prevent.

The scroll specifies the offerings for each rank of shrine, with the highest-ranked shrines receiving silk, cloth, weapons, and sake, while lesser shrines receive proportionally smaller gifts. The coordination required to perform this festival simultaneously at hundreds of locations reflects the administrative sophistication of the early Japanese state and its deep intertwining of governance and ritual.
