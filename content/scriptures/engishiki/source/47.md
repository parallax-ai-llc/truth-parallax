---
title: "Engishiki Section 47: The Procedures for Criminal Investigations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 47
---

Criminal investigation procedures in the Engishiki address both the practical techniques for solving crimes and the ritual dimensions of the justice system. This scroll details the use of divination in criminal cases, the ordeal methods employed to determine guilt or innocence, and the purification requirements that apply to officials involved in criminal proceedings.

The ordeal of boiling water, or kukatachi, is described in detail. The accused plunges a hand into boiling water, and the resulting injuries are examined to determine guilt or innocence. The scroll prescribes the preparation of the ordeal, including the prayers and purification rituals that must precede it, the specifications for the water vessel and heat source, and the criteria for interpreting the results.

The scroll also addresses the use of divination to identify suspects, the procedures for interrogating witnesses, and the ritual cleansing required after executions and other contact with death. The criminal justice provisions of the Engishiki complement the detailed criminal codes found in the Ritsuryo legal compilations, adding a ritual dimension to the state's exercise of judicial authority.
