---
title: "Engishiki Section 14: The Oharano and Matsunoo Festivals"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 14
---

This scroll details the Oharano Shrine, established as the Fujiwara clan's worship site after the capital moved to Kyoto, and the Matsunoo Shrine, one of the oldest shrines in the capital region. The Oharano Shrine mirrors the Kasuga Shrine in its dedications and rituals, serving as a more convenient location for the Fujiwara family to perform their ancestral rites.

The Matsunoo Shrine, associated with the Hata clan who were instrumental in the development of the capital region, venerates a mountain deity linked to sake brewing and water. The festival procedures reflect these associations, with special attention to the quality and quantity of sake offerings. The shrine's spring festival involves a dramatic boat procession along the Katsura River.

Both shrines receive regular state offerings and are served by hereditary priestly families. The scroll prescribes the management of shrine lands, the appointment and conduct of priests, and the resolution of disputes regarding shrine property and privileges. These administrative details reveal how thoroughly the early Japanese state integrated religious institutions into its governmental framework.
