---
title: "Engishiki Section 2: Prayers for Special Festivals"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 2
---

This scroll compiles the prayers and procedures for festivals outside the regular seasonal calendar. The Tsukinami festivals, performed in the sixth and twelfth months, require offerings to 3,132 deities enumerated in the official register. The Kanname festival brings the first fruits of rice from Ise to the court, and requires elaborate preparations including the weaving of special cloth by consecrated women.

The Niiname harvest festival is the most solemn of these special occasions. When performed by a newly enthroned emperor, it becomes the Daijosai, the Great Thanksgiving, in which the sovereign personally offers rice from specially designated fields to Amaterasu Omikami and the assembled deities. The scroll specifies the construction of temporary ritual halls, the selection of the Yuki and Suki rice-field provinces by divination with turtle shell, and the exact sequence of nighttime offerings.

Other special festivals addressed in this scroll include prayers for rain during drought, rituals to calm earthquakes, and ceremonies to avert the evil influences detected through divination. Each festival entry specifies the rank of the officials who must attend, the types and quantities of offerings, and the ritual purity requirements for all participants.
