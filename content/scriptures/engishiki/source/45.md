---
title: "Engishiki Section 45: The Regulations for Imperial Horses"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 45
---

The management of the imperial stables and the broader system of horse provision is detailed in this scroll. Horses serve essential functions in transportation, communication, military operations, and ceremonial display, and the state invests significant resources in their breeding, training, and maintenance.

The imperial stables maintain horses for the emperor's personal use, including riding horses, carriage teams, and ceremonial mounts. The scroll specifies the care and feeding of these animals, the training of grooms and riders, and the procedures for selecting new horses from provincial breeding programs. The ceremonial role of horses in processions and festivals requires animals of specific color, temperament, and training.

The provincial horse-breeding system supplies both the imperial stables and the military. Designated ranches in the eastern and northern provinces maintain breeding herds under the supervision of government officials. The scroll specifies the breeding stock management, the selection criteria for different purposes, and the transport procedures for moving horses from breeding areas to where they are needed. The horse provision system reveals the scale of the logistical challenge facing the early Japanese state in maintaining its transportation and military capabilities.
