---
title: "Engishiki Section 1: Prayers to the Four Seasons"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 1
---

The opening scroll of the Engishiki sets forth the ritual prayers offered to the kami across the four seasons of the year. In spring, prayers are directed to the deities of growth and renewal, beseeching them to bless the rice seedlings and bring abundant rains. The texts prescribe the exact words the Nakatomi ritualists must speak, the offerings of cloth, food, and sake to be presented, and the manner in which the sacred precincts must be purified before each ceremony.

Summer prayers address the threatening forces of pestilence and drought. The rituals invoke the wind deities and water spirits, asking them to temper their power and spare the crops from destruction. Autumn brings the harvest thanksgiving, when the first fruits are offered to the heavenly and earthly kami in gratitude. Winter prayers focus on protection of the imperial house during the dormant season, and on ensuring the cycle of renewal will begin again.

Each seasonal prayer follows a precise formula. The ritualist identifies the deity being addressed, recounts the mythological basis for the deity's authority, presents the offerings with specific ritual gestures, and concludes with words of reverence and petition. Deviation from the prescribed forms is considered dangerous, for improperly performed rituals may offend the kami and bring calamity upon the court and the nation.
