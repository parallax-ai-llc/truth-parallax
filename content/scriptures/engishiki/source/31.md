---
title: "Engishiki Section 31: The Sake Brewing Regulations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 31
---

Sake plays an essential role in Shinto ritual and court ceremony. This scroll details the operations of the Sake Bureau, the Miki no Tsukasa, which produces the sacred sake offered to the kami and the refined beverages served at court functions. The brewing process follows traditional methods that are themselves considered sacred, with specific prayers and rituals accompanying each stage of production.

The scroll specifies the varieties of rice used for brewing, the water sources, the koji mold cultivation methods, and the fermentation schedules for different types of sake. The production of white sake and black sake for the Niiname harvest festival receives particular attention, as these ritual beverages must be prepared according to ancient formulas preserved within the bureau.

Quality control procedures include tasting by senior officials and the maintenance of detailed production records. The distribution of sake to shrines, court events, and government offices is prescribed, with allocations varying by the importance of the occasion and the rank of the recipients. The scroll also addresses the management of the sake storehouses, the training of brewers, and the cultivation of the rice paddies dedicated to sake production.
