---
title: "Engishiki Section 5: The Ise Shrine Offerings and Treasures"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 5
---

This scroll catalogs every offering and treasure to be presented at the Ise Shrine throughout the year. The lists are extraordinarily detailed, specifying the exact quantities of silk cloth in various colors, the number and weight of metal implements, the types and amounts of food offerings, and the ritual vessels in which they must be presented.

The treasures maintained within the shrine include sacred swords, jewels, mirrors, and garments. The scroll prescribes the schedule for replacing these items and the rituals for disposing of the old ones, which must be buried in designated spots within the sacred precincts rather than destroyed. Textile offerings include bolts of silk, hemp, and bark cloth in prescribed lengths and colors.

Metal offerings encompass items of gold, silver, copper, and iron, each crafted to exact specifications. The food offerings are extensive and include rice, sake, various fish and shellfish, seaweed, fruits, and vegetables, all sourced from designated regions and prepared according to strict ritual requirements. The scroll also specifies the clothing, tools, and supplies provided to the shrine priests and attendants as part of their official support.
