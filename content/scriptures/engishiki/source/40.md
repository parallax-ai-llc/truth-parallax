---
title: "Engishiki Section 40: The Tribute Goods from Central Provinces"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 40
---

The central provinces surrounding the capital form the economic heartland of the state, and their tribute contributions reflect both their agricultural productivity and their role as centers of craft production. This scroll details the goods required from the Kinai provinces and the nearby circuits.

Yamashiro, the capital province, provides a wide range of goods including high-quality textiles, refined metalwork, and specialty foods for the court table. Yamato, the ancient heartland of the imperial state, contributes rice from its fertile plains and textile products from its established weaving traditions. The nearby provinces of Settsu, Kawachi, and Izumi each provide their distinctive products.

The proximity of these provinces to the capital simplifies logistics but also increases the frequency and volume of demands upon them. The scroll addresses the special obligations of Kinai provinces, including their role in providing labor for capital construction projects, materials for palace maintenance, and emergency supplies during festivals and state events. The economic relationship between the capital and its surrounding provinces forms the foundation of the Engishiki's fiscal system.
