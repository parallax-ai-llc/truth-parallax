---
title: "Engishiki Section 32: The Imperial Storehouse Management"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 32
---

The Imperial Storehouse system manages the vast quantities of textiles, metals, and other valuables that flow into the court as tax payments and tribute. This scroll details the organization of the Okurazukuri, the procedures for receiving, inspecting, cataloging, and storing these goods, and the authorization procedures for their distribution.

The stores include silk and other textiles from the provinces, metals both precious and practical, lacquerware, ceramics, and various manufactured goods. Each item is inspected upon receipt, graded for quality, and assigned a storage location. The record-keeping system tracks every item from receipt through storage to distribution, creating an audit trail that enables accountability.

The distribution of storehouse goods serves multiple purposes: payment of official salaries, provision of offerings to shrines, gifts to foreign envoys, rewards for meritorious service, and supplies for government offices. The scroll specifies the authorization levels required for different types of distributions, the documentation that must accompany each disbursement, and the penalties for unauthorized access to stored goods. The storehouse system thus forms a crucial component of the government's fiscal infrastructure.
