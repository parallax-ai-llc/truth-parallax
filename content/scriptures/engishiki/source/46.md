---
title: "Engishiki Section 46: The Regulations for Government Cattle"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 46
---

Government cattle serve as draft animals for agriculture, transport, and construction. This scroll details the regulations for maintaining government cattle herds, including breeding programs, veterinary care, and the allocation of animals to various government functions.

The oxen used to pull the aristocracy's carriages are maintained by specialized handlers and must meet exacting standards of size, color, and temperament. The scroll specifies the care, feeding, and training of carriage oxen, and the procedures for replacing animals that become unfit for service. Agricultural oxen, used in the cultivation of government lands, require different management but are equally important to the state's economic functioning.

The scroll also addresses the management of cattle ranches, the prevention and treatment of cattle diseases, and the disposal of animals that die in government service. The ritual implications of cattle death, which creates pollution that must be addressed through purification ceremonies, add complexity to the management task. The cattle regulations, like the horse provisions, reveal the importance of animal husbandry to the functioning of the early Japanese state.
