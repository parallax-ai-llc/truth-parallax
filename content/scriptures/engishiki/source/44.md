---
title: "Engishiki Section 44: The Military Equipment Regulations"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 44
---

Military equipment regulations reflect the state's concern with maintaining adequate defensive capabilities. This scroll details the types, quantities, and specifications of weapons and armor to be maintained in government arsenals and distributed to provincial forces.

The standard armament includes bows, arrows, swords, spears, and armor of various types. The scroll specifies the materials and construction methods for each item, the quality standards that must be met, and the procedures for inspecting and replacing worn or damaged equipment. The quantities to be maintained at each provincial headquarters are prescribed based on the size and strategic importance of the province.

The scroll also addresses the training requirements for soldiers and the procedures for mobilizing forces in response to threats. The system of military conscription, though declining in effectiveness by the tenth century, is described in its official form, with procedures for selecting conscripts, equipping them, and deploying them for guard duty and frontier defense. The military sections of the Engishiki provide important evidence for understanding the defense capabilities of the early Japanese state.
