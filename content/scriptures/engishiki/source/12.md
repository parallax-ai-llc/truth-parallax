---
title: "Engishiki Section 12: The Festivals of the Kamo Shrines"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 12
---

Continuing the Register of Deities, this scroll details the festivals and offerings for the Kamo shrines, among the most important in the capital region. The Kamo Shrines, Upper and Lower, venerate the thunder deity Wakeikazuchi and the ancestral deity Tamayori-hime. Their festivals rank among the most elaborate in the ritual calendar.

The Aoi Matsuri, the Hollyhock Festival, is described in full procedural detail. The imperial messenger's procession from the palace to the shrines requires precise choreography, with specific costumes, horse trappings, and musical accompaniment prescribed for each participant. The hollyhock leaves that give the festival its name must be gathered from designated locations and woven into garlands for the participants and the shrine buildings.

The scroll also addresses the selection and duties of the Saiin, the Imperial Princess who serves as chief priestess of the Kamo Shrines, paralleling the Saigu at Ise. Her purification, installation, and ritual duties are prescribed in detail, as are the arrangements for her residence and attendants. The Kamo priestess tradition continued for centuries as one of the most prestigious religious appointments for women of the imperial family.
