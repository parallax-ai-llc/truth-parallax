---
title: "Engishiki Section 22: The Bureau of Divination Procedures"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 22
---

The Bureau of Divination, the Onmyoryo, plays a central role in the ritual life of the Heian court. This scroll details its structure, personnel, and procedures. The bureau is staffed by specialists in yin-yang theory, astronomy, calendar-making, and divination, who advise the court on auspicious and inauspicious days, directions, and activities.

The Onmyoji, the yin-yang masters, perform divinations to determine the causes of natural disasters, epidemics, and other calamities. Their methods include the analysis of celestial phenomena, the interpretation of unusual natural events, and the performance of rituals to counteract malign influences. The scroll prescribes the training requirements for diviners, who must master texts imported from China and adapted to Japanese conditions.

The bureau's responsibilities extend to the detection and counteraction of curses and sorcery. When illness or misfortune at court is attributed to spiritual attack, the Onmyoji perform diagnostic rituals to identify the source and prescribe countermeasures. The scroll establishes strict regulations on the practice of yin-yang arts outside the bureau, recognizing the potential for abuse of these powerful techniques by unauthorized practitioners.
