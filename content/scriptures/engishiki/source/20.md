---
title: "Engishiki Section 20: The Procedures for Sacred Messengers"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 20
---

Sacred messengers carry offerings and imperial edicts to shrines throughout the provinces. This scroll prescribes the selection, purification, and conduct of these messengers, who serve as the personal representatives of the sovereign in communicating with the kami. The rank of the messenger must correspond to the rank of the shrine being visited, with the most important shrines receiving messengers of the fourth or fifth court rank.

The messenger's journey is itself a ritual act, requiring abstinence from polluting activities and adherence to prescribed routes and schedules. The scroll specifies the prayers to be recited upon departure from the capital, at significant points along the route, and upon arrival at the shrine. The formal presentation of offerings and the recitation of the imperial message follow precise protocols that vary depending on the type of festival and the deity being addressed.

The return journey requires the messenger to report the condition of the shrine, any unusual phenomena observed, and any communications received from the deity through the shrine priests. This intelligence-gathering function made the sacred messenger system an important tool for maintaining central government awareness of conditions in the provinces.
