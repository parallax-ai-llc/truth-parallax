---
title: "Engishiki Section 33: The Textile and Dyeing Procedures"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 33
---

Textile production is one of the most important industries regulated by the Engishiki. This scroll details the operations of the Oribemonodokoro, the Weaving Bureau, and related offices responsible for producing the vast quantities of cloth needed for court dress, shrine offerings, and government operations.

The dyeing procedures are described with remarkable precision, specifying the plants, minerals, and techniques used to produce each color. The famous purple of the highest court ranks requires multiple applications of a dye derived from the gromwell root, a process demanding great skill and patience. Other colors have their own designated dye sources and procedures, each reflecting centuries of accumulated craft knowledge.

The scroll also addresses the weaving of patterned textiles, the production of specialty fabrics for ritual use, and the quality standards applied to cloth produced in the provinces as tax payments. The training of weavers and dyers, the maintenance of looms and other equipment, and the sourcing of raw materials including silk thread, hemp fiber, and dye plants are all regulated in detail. The textile sections of the Engishiki provide invaluable evidence for understanding the material culture and technology of early Japan.
