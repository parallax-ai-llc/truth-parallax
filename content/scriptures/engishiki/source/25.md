---
title: "Engishiki Section 25: The Bureau of Medicine Procedures"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 25
---

The Bureau of Medicine, the Tenyakuryo, is responsible for the health of the court and the training of physicians. This scroll details the bureau's organization, the curriculum for medical students, and the procedures for treating members of the imperial family and court nobility. Medical knowledge combines Chinese pharmaceutical and diagnostic theory with indigenous Japanese herbal traditions.

The bureau maintains gardens for cultivating medicinal herbs and stores of pharmaceutical preparations. The scroll specifies the herbs to be grown, their cultivation methods, and their uses in treatment. Students of medicine undergo a rigorous course of study encompassing the Chinese medical classics, pharmaceutical botany, acupuncture and moxibustion, and diagnostic techniques.

The procedures for treating the emperor and other members of the imperial family are prescribed with particular care. Multiple physicians must be consulted, and their recommendations must be reviewed by senior colleagues before treatment begins. The scroll also addresses the provision of medical care during epidemics, when the bureau's resources must be extended to cover the broader population, and the procedures for quarantine and purification when contagious diseases threaten the capital.
