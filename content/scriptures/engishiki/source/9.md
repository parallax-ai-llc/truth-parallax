---
title: "Engishiki Section 9: The Festival of the Sixth Month Purification"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 9
---

The Festival of the Sixth Month, the Minazuki no Tsugomori no Oharae, is the semiannual Great Purification performed on the last day of the sixth month. This scroll details the elaborate procedures for this ceremony, which aims to cleanse the entire nation of accumulated pollution and transgressions from the first half of the year.

The ceremony takes place at the Suzaku Gate of the palace, where all officials of the fifth rank and above must assemble. The Nakatomi ritualist recites the Great Purification prayer while the Urabe diviners prepare the purification implements. These include human-shaped paper figures onto which the participants breathe their pollutions, cloth strips, hemp, and other materials that will absorb the negative forces.

After the recitation, the purification implements are gathered and cast into a river, symbolically carrying all the accumulated pollution away to the sea. The scroll specifies the materials to be provided from court stores, the ranks and numbers of officials required to attend, and the proper deportment of all participants. A second Great Purification is performed on the last day of the twelfth month, completing the annual cycle of ritual cleansing.
