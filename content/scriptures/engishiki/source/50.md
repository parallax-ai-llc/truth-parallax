---
title: "Engishiki Section 50: The Procedures for Accession and Abdication"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 50
---

The final scroll addresses the most solemn ceremonies of the imperial institution: the accession of a new sovereign and the abdication of the retiring one. These ceremonies define the sacred character of the imperial office and establish the new emperor's relationship with the ancestral deities and the nation.

The accession ceremony begins with the presentation of the imperial regalia: the sacred mirror, the sacred sword, and the sacred jewel. These objects, tracing their origin to the mythological age of the gods, embody the divine mandate of the imperial line. The scroll prescribes the officials who must be present, the prayers to be recited, and the precise sequence of ritual actions that transform a prince into a sovereign.

The Daijosai, the Great Thanksgiving performed by a newly enthroned emperor, is the culmination of the accession process. The scroll details the selection of the rice-field provinces by turtle-shell divination, the construction of the temporary ritual halls, the preparation of the sacred rice and sake, and the nighttime ceremony in which the new emperor personally offers these to Amaterasu Omikami. This most secret and sacred of all court ceremonies establishes the mystical bond between the sovereign and the ancestral deity that legitimates imperial rule and ensures the prosperity of the nation.
