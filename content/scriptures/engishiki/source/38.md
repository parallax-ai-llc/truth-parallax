---
title: "Engishiki Section 38: The Tribute Goods from Eastern Provinces"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 38
---

This scroll details the specific tribute goods required from the eastern provinces. Each province's contributions reflect its local products and resources, providing a detailed picture of the economic geography of eastern Japan in the early tenth century.

The provinces of the Tokaido and Tosando circuits contribute a variety of goods including horse equipment, iron products, hemp textiles, and distinctive regional specialties. Musashi Province provides iron and horse gear, Hitachi supplies silk and hemp, and the northern provinces of Dewa and Mutsu contribute gold, eagle feathers, and animal skins reflecting their frontier character.

The scroll specifies the quantities, quality standards, and delivery schedules for each item, as well as the officials responsible for collection and transport. The eastern tribute system reveals the economic integration of outlying regions into the central government's fiscal framework, while the distinctive products required from each province reflect the still-developing nature of the eastern frontier and its different economic base compared to the western heartland.
