---
title: "Engishiki Section 8: The Norito Ritual Prayers"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 8
---

This scroll preserves the complete texts of the most important norito, the ritual prayers spoken by Nakatomi priests during state ceremonies. These prayers follow a distinctive literary form, opening with honorific address to the assembled deities, recounting the mythological precedent for the ceremony, presenting the offerings with formal language, and concluding with petitions.

The Great Purification prayer, the Oharae no Kotoba, is among the most celebrated. It recounts the mythological origins of purification, describing how the heavenly deities established the practice of cleansing pollution through the descent of Izanagi from the underworld. The prayer catalogs the various forms of transgression and pollution, from agricultural crimes to sorcery, and describes how they are swept away by the river spirits, carried to the sea, and swallowed by the ocean depths.

Other norito preserved in this scroll include prayers for calming wind and rain, for blessing the palace gates, for consecrating new buildings, and for ensuring safe journeys. The language of these prayers is archaic even by tenth-century standards, and the Engishiki preserves linguistic forms that had already passed out of everyday use, making these texts invaluable for understanding early Japanese religious thought and language.
