---
title: "Engishiki Section 18: The Regulations for Shrine Priests"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 18
---

This scroll establishes regulations governing the shrine priests throughout the nation. The hereditary nature of most priesthoods is affirmed, with specific families designated as the traditional custodians of particular shrines. The scroll prescribes the qualifications for priestly appointment, including lineage, age, physical fitness, and freedom from ritual pollution.

Priests are required to maintain strict standards of ritual purity, abstaining from contact with death, serious illness, and various other polluting influences. The periods of abstinence before major ceremonies are specified, ranging from days to weeks depending on the importance of the ritual. Violations of purity regulations are punishable by temporary suspension or permanent removal from priestly duties.

The scroll also addresses the economic support of priests, specifying the lands, stipends, and shares of offerings to which they are entitled. The relationship between shrine priests and provincial governors is defined, with governors responsible for ensuring that local shrines are properly maintained and their priests adequately supported. Disputes between priestly families over succession rights are to be resolved by the Jingikan, the Bureau of Kami Affairs, in consultation with the relevant provincial authorities.
