---
title: "Engishiki Section 48: The Regulations for Buddhist Temples"
scripture: "engishiki"
chapter: 48
---

Buddhist temples, while subject to a separate regulatory framework from Shinto shrines, are nonetheless addressed in the Engishiki because of their integration into the state's ritual system. This scroll details the regulations governing government-sponsored temples, their clergy, and their ceremonial obligations to the state.

The great temples of Nara and the capital, including Todaiji, Kofukuji, and Enryakuji, receive substantial state support in exchange for performing prayers and ceremonies for the protection of the state and the welfare of the sovereign. The scroll specifies the types of Buddhist ceremonies requested by the court, the schedules for sutra recitation, and the offerings provided to temple communities.

The regulation of the Buddhist clergy includes procedures for ordination, the conduct of monks and nuns, and the management of temple lands and property. The scroll addresses the delicate balance between Buddhist institutional autonomy and state control, prescribing the limits of government authority while maintaining the state's right to regulate institutions that receive public support. The interaction between Buddhist and Shinto ceremonial obligations reveals the complex religious landscape of early Japan.
