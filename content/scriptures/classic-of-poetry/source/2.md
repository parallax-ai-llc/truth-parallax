**The Deer Cry**

**1** With notes of a deer calling to one another, they eat the celery of the fields. I have a guest; let me play my lute, and blow my reed organ.

**2** Blow the reed organ, and sound all its pipes. Take up this basket of offerings. The man loves me, and will show me the way of Zhou.

**Cutting Wood**

**3** Cut, cut, cut the wood; and place it on the bank of the river. The waters flow clear and rippling. When you do not sow, nor reap, how do you get the produce of those three hundred farms?

**4** When you do not hunt, nor chase, how do we see the badger skins hanging in your courtyard? That gentleman does not eat the bread of idleness.

**White Flower**

**5** White the flower; yet these plants are not consumed. All among us officers are in danger and distress. Pity us, who are as the gatherers of grass.

**The Fish in the Trap**

**6** The fish are caught in the trap; both yellow-jaws and snout-fish are in it. The princes attended the festive court with their robes beautifully displayed.
