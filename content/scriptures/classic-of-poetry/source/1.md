**Guan Guan (Ospreys)**

**1** Guan guan cry the ospreys, on the islet in the river. The modest, retiring, virtuous young woman — for our prince a good mate she.

**2** Here long, there short, is the duckweed, to the left, to the right, borne about by the current. The modest, retiring, virtuous young woman — waking and sleeping, he sought her.

**3** He sought her and found her not, and waking and sleeping he thought about her. Long he thought; on his side, on his back, he turned, and back again.

**The Peach Tree**

**4** The peach tree is young and elegant; brilliant are its flowers. This young lady is going to her future home, and will order well her chamber and house.

**5** The peach tree is young and elegant; abundant will be its fruits. This young lady is going to her future home, and will order well her house and chamber.

**The Plantain**

**6** Thick grow the plantain; here we gather, gather them. Thick grow the plantain; here we pluck, pluck them.

**7** Thick grow the plantain; here we strip, strip the seeds. Thick grow the plantain; here we rub, rub them out.
