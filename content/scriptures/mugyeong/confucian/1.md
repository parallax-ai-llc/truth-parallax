Confucian interpreters approach Section 1, 'Invocation of the Spirits,' with the complex mixture of critique and recognition that has characterized the Confucian relationship with Korean shamanism throughout the Joseon dynasty and beyond. While Neo-Confucian orthodoxy classified shamanic practices as heterodox superstition (eumsahak/음사학), attentive readers recognize in this section elements that resonate with fundamental Confucian values: the concern for social harmony, the respect for ancestral relationships, and the maintenance of proper order in human affairs.

The tension between Confucian disapproval of shamanic practice and the pragmatic recognition of its social functions has been a persistent theme in Korean intellectual history. Even Confucian scholars who publicly condemned shamanism often privately acknowledged its role in addressing spiritual and emotional needs that the austere rationalism of Neo-Confucian ritual could not fully meet.
