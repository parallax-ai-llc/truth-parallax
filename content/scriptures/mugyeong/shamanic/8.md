Shamanic practitioners understand Section 8, 'Mountain Spirit Ceremonies,' as a living ritual script that can only be fully comprehended through performance. The written text captures the skeleton of what occurs when a mudang enters the sacred space of the gut ceremony and enacts these rites, but the flesh — the vocal power, the drumming rhythms, the movements of the body in trance, the presence of the spirits themselves — belongs to the performed tradition alone.

Within the shamanic framework, this section addresses one of the fundamental dimensions of the mudang's sacred work. The rituals described here have been transmitted through lineages of spirit mothers and spirit daughters for centuries, adapted to regional variations across Korea's provinces while maintaining their essential spiritual purpose. Each mudang brings her own relationship with her personal spirits (momju) to the performance, ensuring that even traditional forms are renewed through each individual's spiritual experience.
