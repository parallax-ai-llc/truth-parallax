Interfaith interpretation of Section 7, 'Rites for the Dead,' approaches these shamanic practices as a valuable contribution to interreligious dialogue and mutual understanding. Rather than evaluating them against the standards of textual or institutional religions, the interfaith tradition receives these practices on their own terms, recognizing in them authentic expressions of human spiritual experience that offer unique perspectives on universal religious questions.

This section's treatment of the relationship between the human and spiritual worlds speaks to concerns shared across religious traditions worldwide: the nature of spiritual reality, the possibility of communication with transcendent beings, the connection between physical and spiritual healing, and the role of specially gifted individuals in mediating between the visible and invisible dimensions of existence.
