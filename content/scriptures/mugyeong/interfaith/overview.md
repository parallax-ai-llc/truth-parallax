## Overview

Interfaith interpretation of the Mugyeong approaches Korean shamanism as a vital participant in global interreligious dialogue. This tradition values the Mugyeong for what it contributes to broader conversations about the nature of spiritual experience, the relationship between healing and religion, the role of women in religious leadership, and the ecological dimensions of indigenous spirituality. Rather than measuring shamanism against the standards of textual religions, the interfaith tradition receives it on its own terms as a valid and valuable spiritual path.

## Contributions to Dialogue

Interfaith readers highlight several distinctive contributions the Mugyeong makes to interreligious understanding: its insistence on direct spiritual experience rather than doctrinal belief, its embodied and performative approach to the sacred, its ecological awareness rooted in reverence for mountains, waters, and natural forces, and its predominantly female leadership in a religious landscape often dominated by male authority.

## Contemporary Relevance

The interfaith tradition finds particular relevance in the Mugyeong's approach to healing, which integrates physical, psychological, social, and spiritual dimensions in ways that anticipate contemporary holistic health movements. The mudang's role as community healer, counselor, and mediator between the living and the dead offers a model of religious leadership that speaks to contemporary longings for more integrated and embodied forms of spiritual practice.
