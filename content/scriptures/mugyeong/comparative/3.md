Comparative analysis of Section 3, 'Preparation of the Gut,' situates the practices described here within the global context of shamanic traditions worldwide. The elements found in this section — the relationship between the practitioner and the spirit world, the techniques of ecstatic engagement, the social function of ritual healing — find parallels across Siberian, Central Asian, Southeast Asian, African, and American indigenous traditions, suggesting both universal patterns in human spiritual experience and the distinctive forms these patterns take in Korean culture.

Mircea Eliade's classic analysis of shamanism as a 'technique of ecstasy' provides one framework for understanding these practices, though subsequent scholars have critiqued the limitations of his universalizing approach. More recent comparative work emphasizes the importance of attending to specific cultural contexts while remaining open to structural similarities that may illuminate the nature of human religious experience itself.
