## Overview

The Mugyeong (巫經, "Shaman Scriptures") represents the collected ritual texts, invocations, and sacred narratives of Korean shamanism (Musok/무속). Unlike scriptures of textual religions, the Mugyeong exists primarily as an oral tradition, transmitted from master mudang (shaman) to apprentice through years of training and spirit possession. The written compilations that exist today reflect scholarly efforts to preserve these living traditions.

## Structure

The Mugyeong is organized into twelve sections reflecting the major dimensions of shamanic practice:

1. **Invocation of the Spirits** (청신/請神): The opening prayers and chants that summon the spirits
2. **The Mudang's Calling** (신병/神病): The experience of spirit sickness and acceptance of the shamanic vocation
3. **Preparation of the Gut** (굿 준비): Ritual purification, altar arrangement, and offering preparation
4. **Descent of the Sinjang** (신장하강/神將下降): The arrival and embodiment of spirit generals
5. **Cleansing of the Household** (가정정화/家庭淨化): Rites for purifying homes and removing malevolent influences
6. **Healing Through the Spirits** (영적치유/靈的治癒): Diagnostic and therapeutic rituals
7. **Rites for the Dead** (진오기/鎭五記): Guiding deceased souls to the afterworld
8. **Mountain Spirit Ceremonies** (산신제/山神祭): Rituals honoring Sansin, the mountain deity
9. **The Dragon King Ritual** (용왕제/龍王祭): Maritime and water-related ceremonies
10. **Divination and Prophecy** (점복/占卜): Methods of reading the will of the spirits
11. **Seasonal Ceremonies** (세시의례/歲時儀禮): Rituals marking the agricultural and lunar calendar
12. **Transmission of the Sacred Way** (도통전수/道統傳授): The passing of shamanic lineage and authority

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Mugyeong has been interpreted across multiple traditions:

- **Shamanic**: The living heart of Korean Musok practice, performed in gut rituals throughout Korea
- **Buddhist-Korean**: Studied for its syncretic incorporation of Buddhist deities and cosmological frameworks
- **Confucian**: Examined for its relationship to ancestor veneration and social ethics
- **Academic**: Analyzed as a repository of pre-literate Korean religious consciousness
- **Interfaith**: Explored for parallels with global shamanic traditions
