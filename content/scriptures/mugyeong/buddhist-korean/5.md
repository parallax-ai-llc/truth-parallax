Buddhist-Korean interpretation of Section 5, 'Cleansing of the Household,' reveals the deep syncretic interweaving of Buddhist and shamanic elements that characterizes Korean religious practice. The practices described in this section incorporate Buddhist cosmological frameworks, devotional elements directed toward bodhisattvas (particularly Gwaneum and Jijang), and soteriological concepts such as karma and merit transfer, all transformed through the distinctive idiom of shamanic ritual performance.

This syncretism reflects over a millennium of mutual influence between Buddhism and indigenous Korean shamanism. Buddhist monks and shamanic practitioners have historically served overlapping social functions — healing, funerary rites, divination, and spiritual counseling — leading to extensive borrowing and adaptation in both directions. The practices in this section exemplify this creative synthesis, presenting Buddhist elements within a fundamentally shamanic ritual structure.
