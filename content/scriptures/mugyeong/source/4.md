---
title: "Mugyeong - Chapter 4"
scripture: "mugyeong"
chapter: 4
---

**1** This chapter teaches concerning the veneration of the mountain spirits and sacred groves.

**2** The mudang speaks: "Listen, O children of the earth, to the wisdom passed down from the ancient ones. The spirits are near, and their voices speak through wind and water, through dream and vision."

**3** In the old times, before the written word, the shamans kept this knowledge in their hearts and passed it from teacher to student through song and sacred dance.

**4** The drum beats the rhythm of the cosmos: the first beat is Heaven, the second beat is Earth, the third beat is the heartbeat of all living things.

**5** The offerings must be prepared with care: the white rice cakes symbolize purity, the fruits of the season represent the bounty of the earth, the wine represents the spirit that flows between worlds.

**6** When the mudang dons the sacred garments and takes up the sacred implements, she becomes the vessel through which the spirits manifest their will.

**7** The people gather in the sacred space, and together they create a bridge of faith and devotion that the spirits may cross.

**8** The spirits say: "We do not ask for much. We ask only that you remember us, that you honour the bonds between the living and the dead, and that you walk in harmony with the natural world."

**9** Those who honour the spirits find that their lives are blessed with good fortune, their families prosper, and their communities are at peace.

**10** Those who forget the spirits find that discord enters their homes, illness visits their bodies, and misfortune follows their endeavours.

**11** Therefore let every person remember the teachings of the ancestors, perform the rites at the proper times, and maintain the sacred connection between Heaven and Earth.

**12** This is the way of the mudang, the ancient way of Korea, the path of harmony between all worlds.
