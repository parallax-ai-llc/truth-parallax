## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Numbers reads the wilderness journey as a type of the Church's pilgrimage through history. The priestly blessing (6:24-26), the sacrificial system, and the ordering of the camp around the Tabernacle all prefigure the Church's sacramental and hierarchical life. The Catechism draws on Numbers for its teaching on intercession, the communion of saints, and the Eucharistic journey.

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **The pilgrim Church**: The wilderness journey prefigures the Church's earthly pilgrimage toward the heavenly promised land (Lumen Gentium 48-51)
- **Priestly blessing**: The Aaronic blessing (6:24-26) is used in Catholic liturgy and expresses God's desire to bless His people
- **Intercessory prayer**: Moses' repeated intercession for rebellious Israel prefigures Christ's intercession and the Church's ministry of prayer
- **The bronze serpent**: A type of the Crucifix — looking upon the lifted serpent for healing anticipates faith in the crucified Christ (CCC 2130)
