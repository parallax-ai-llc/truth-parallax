## Overview

Numbers (*Bamidbar*, "In the wilderness") narrates the formative period of Israel's national identity — the wilderness years between Sinai and the Jordan. Jewish tradition finds in this book some of the Torah's richest narrative and halakhic material: the Priestly Blessing (*Birkat Kohanim*), the Nazirite vow, the sotah ritual, the red heifer (*parah adumah*), and the dramatic stories of the spies, Korah's rebellion, and Balaam.

## Key Interpretive Themes

- **Birkat Kohanim**: The Priestly Blessing (6:24-26) is recited daily in synagogue — the only Torah text spoken directly by kohanim today
- **Meraglim (spies)**: The sin of the spies is connected to Tisha B'Av — "they wept that night" (14:1) established a night of weeping for generations
- **Red heifer**: The *parah adumah* (ch. 19) is the paradigmatic *chok* — a commandment whose reason transcends human understanding
- **Korach**: The archetype of destructive controversy (*machloket she-einah l'shem shamayim*, Pirkei Avot 5:17)
