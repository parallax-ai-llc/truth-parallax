## Overview

While Numbers is not a canonical text in Islam, several of its key narratives appear in the Quran, particularly the story of the spies and Israel's refusal to enter the Promised Land. The Quran presents this episode (Al-Ma'idah 5:20-26) as a paradigmatic example of disobedience to prophetic guidance and its consequences. Musa's patience with his rebellious people is a recurring Quranic theme that resonates throughout Numbers.

| The bronze serpent | Not directly paralleled | — |
| Korah's rebellion | Qarun's punishment | Al-Qasas 28:76-82 |

## Key Interpretive Principles

- **Prophetic patience**: Musa's endurance of his people's complaints and rebellion models *sabr* (patience) for all believers
- **Consequences of disobedience**: The forty-year punishment demonstrates that rejecting divine guidance has real consequences
- **Qarun/Korah**: Identified in the Quran as a wealthy oppressor from Musa's people, punished by being swallowed by the earth
