## Overview

The Hypostasis of the Archons (Reality of the Rulers) is a Gnostic treatise from the Nag Hammadi library (Codex II, Tractate 4) that presents a radical rereading of the Genesis creation narrative. Taking its starting point from Paul's reference to cosmic powers in Ephesians 6:12, the text describes the origin and nature of the archons — the malevolent rulers of the material world. The chief archon, Samael (also called Sakla and Yaldabaoth), arrogantly declares himself the only god, but a heavenly voice corrects him, revealing higher divine realities.

## Structure

The text moves from cosmogony through a retelling of Genesis to an apocalyptic promise:

1. **The Blind God and the Creation of the Material World** (Chapter 1): Samael's arrogant claim to supreme divinity and the creation of the material cosmos
2. **The Garden of Eden Reversed — The Serpent as Instructor** (Chapter 2): A radical inversion of Genesis in which the serpent brings salvific knowledge and the archons are the deceivers
3. **Norea and the Angel — The Promise of Liberation** (Chapter 3): The angel Eleleth reveals to Norea the true nature of the archons and promises their ultimate defeat

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Hypostasis of the Archons offers a striking counter-reading to mainstream biblical interpretation:

- **Gnosticism**: A foundational text that reverses the moral valences of Genesis
- **Catholicism / Eastern Orthodoxy**: Rejected as heretical for its portrayal of the Creator God as a blind, arrogant archon
- **Protestantism**: Not canonical; studied as evidence of early Christian theological diversity
- **Judaism**: Deeply challenging to Jewish theology, as it reinterprets the God of Genesis as a malevolent being
- **Academic**: Important for understanding Gnostic hermeneutics and the reinterpretation of Jewish scripture

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Archons | Malevolent cosmic rulers who imprison humanity in ignorance |
| Samael | The blind chief archon who falsely claims to be the only God |
| Reversed Genesis | The serpent as a liberator and the archons as deceivers |
| Norea | A righteous feminine figure who resists the archons and receives revelation |
| Sophia | The divine Wisdom whose light the archons cannot fully contain |
| Liberation | The promised eschatological defeat of the archons and the freeing of divine sparks |
