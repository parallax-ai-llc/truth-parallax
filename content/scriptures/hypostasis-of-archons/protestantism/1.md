Protestant theology finds chapter 1's identification of the Creator with a blind, arrogant archon to be a fundamental distortion of biblical teaching. The God of Genesis 1 creates with purpose, wisdom, and goodness, declaring each element of creation good. The Hypostasis of the Archons transforms this account into a narrative of cosmic tyranny, in which creation is an act of arrogant self-aggrandizement by an ignorant being. Protestant theologians see this as a misreading that distorts the character of God revealed throughout Scripture.

The text's appeal to Ephesians 6:12 as its starting point illustrates a hermeneutical method that Protestant interpreters find problematic. While Paul does teach that believers struggle against cosmic powers, he never identifies these powers with the Creator God. Protestant hermeneutics insists on reading individual passages within their canonical context, where the "rulers and authorities" are understood as fallen creatures in rebellion against the sovereign God, not as the builders of the material world.
