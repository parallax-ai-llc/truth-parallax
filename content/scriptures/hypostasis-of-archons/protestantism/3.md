Chapter 3 introduces Norea, a figure unknown to the Protestant biblical canon. While some scholars connect her to Naamah of Genesis 4:22, the elaborate narrative built around her — her resistance to the archons, her cry for divine aid, and the angelic revelation she receives — has no canonical basis. Protestant theology evaluates truth claims against Scripture, and the Norea narrative, while imaginative, cannot claim scriptural authority.

The angel Eleleth's promise of the archons' eschatological defeat echoes, in distorted form, the biblical hope for the final victory of God over evil. Protestant eschatology, grounded in texts like Revelation 19-21 and 1 Corinthians 15, anticipates Christ's triumphant return and the renewal of all creation. The Hypostasis of the Archons shares the conviction that evil will be ultimately defeated but places this hope within a cosmological framework that Protestantism rejects — one in which the enemy is not Satan and the fallen powers but the Creator himself.
