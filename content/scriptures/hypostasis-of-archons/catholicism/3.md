The Norea narrative in chapter 3, while presenting a compelling story of resistance and divine aid, operates entirely outside the Catholic canonical and theological framework. Norea does not appear in the Catholic Bible, and the angelic revelation she receives — explaining the archons' origin and promising their defeat — presents a cosmology incompatible with Catholic teaching. The Catholic Church resolves questions about cosmic evil, angelic beings, and eschatological hope through its own authoritative interpretation of canonical Scripture and Sacred Tradition.

Catholic eschatology does share with the Hypostasis of the Archons the conviction that evil will ultimately be defeated, but the frameworks diverge entirely. Catholic hope centers on Christ's second coming, the general resurrection, the last judgment, and the renewal of creation. The Hypostasis of the Archons promises the destruction of the archontic order and the liberation of trapped divine light — a hope that lacks christological grounding and envisions the annihilation rather than the redemption of the material cosmos.
