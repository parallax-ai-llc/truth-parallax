Chapter 3 introduces Norea, one of the most important feminine figures in Gnostic literature. When the archons attempt to dominate her as they dominated Eve's material shadow, Norea cries out to the God of the All for help, and the angel Eleleth descends to reveal the truth about the archons. The Gnostic reader sees in Norea a model of spiritual resistance: she refuses to submit to the archontic powers, recognizes their illegitimate authority, and appeals directly to the true divine realm for aid.

Eleleth's revelation to Norea provides the cosmological knowledge that contextualizes and completes the narrative. The angel explains the origin of the archons, their nature as products of Sophia's error, and their ultimate fate — they will be defeated when the True Human (the eschatological Savior) appears. This promise of liberation is the Gnostic community's eschatological hope: the archontic order is not eternal, and its overthrow is guaranteed by the very structure of divine reality.

The text ends with the assurance that those who possess this knowledge — the Gnostic elect — will be gathered into the imperishable light. The Hypostasis of the Archons thus functions as a complete narrative of cosmic history from the Gnostic perspective: the origin of evil, the means of present resistance, and the certainty of future liberation.
