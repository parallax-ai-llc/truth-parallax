The Orthodox Church's response to chapter 1 is grounded in the Nicene Creed's first article: "I believe in one God, the Father Almighty, Maker of heaven and earth, and of all things visible and invisible." The Hypostasis of the Archons' portrayal of the creator as blind Samael directly contradicts this confession. Orthodox theology maintains that the God who creates is the same God who saves, and that creation is a free act of divine love, not an expression of archontic arrogance.

The text's portrayal of the material world as the product of a blind craftsman also conflicts with the Orthodox understanding of creation as logoi — the rational principles through which the Logos (Christ) structures and sustains all things. Maximus the Confessor taught that every created thing contains a logos, a divine idea that connects it to the Creator and draws it toward its fulfillment. This vision of a creation permeated by divine rationality stands in complete opposition to the Hypostasis's image of a cosmos built by ignorance.
