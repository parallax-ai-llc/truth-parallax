Academic analysis of Chapter 2, 'The Yellow Emperor,' addresses questions of dating, sources, and philosophical interpretation that are central to Liezi scholarship. Scholars examine this chapter's relationship to other early Chinese texts, its possible sources in pre-Han philosophical traditions, and the degree to which it reflects the intellectual concerns of the Wei-Jin period (third to fourth centuries CE) when the received text was likely compiled.

A. C. Graham's influential source-critical study identified different layers within the Liezi, and academic interpreters continue to debate which portions of this chapter represent genuinely ancient material and which reflect later composition or compilation. These textual questions are not merely antiquarian but have direct implications for how the chapter's philosophical content is understood and contextualized within Chinese intellectual history.
