Religious Taoism receives Chapter 1, 'Heaven's Gifts,' as part of the Chongxu zhenjing ('True Scripture of Penetrating Emptiness'), a revealed text with implications for spiritual cultivation. The chapter's teachings are understood not as philosophical abstractions but as descriptions of genuine spiritual realities and attainments that the dedicated practitioner can experience through cultivation.

The religious tradition reads the parables and marvelous accounts in this chapter as testimonies to the transformative power of the Tao. Stories of extraordinary abilities — riding the wind, seeing without eyes, knowing without learning — are received not as fantasies but as descriptions of capacities that arise naturally when the practitioner achieves genuine emptiness and alignment with the Tao.
