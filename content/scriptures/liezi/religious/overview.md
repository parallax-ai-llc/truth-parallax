## Overview

Religious Taoism honors the Liezi as a revealed scripture, bestowing upon it the title Chongxu zhenjing (沖虛真經, "True Scripture of Penetrating Emptiness") during the Tang dynasty. The text's accounts of Lie Yukou riding the wind, its descriptions of paradisiacal realms beyond the ordinary world, and its teachings on emptiness and transformation are received not as philosophical metaphors but as descriptions of genuine spiritual attainments available to the dedicated practitioner.

## Spiritual Cultivation

The religious tradition reads the Liezi's philosophical teachings as guides to actual cultivation practice. The emptiness (xu) that the text describes is understood not merely as a concept but as a state to be achieved through meditation, breath cultivation, and the progressive emptying of desires and attachments that obstruct the practitioner's union with the Tao.

## Transcendent Realms

Religious Taoism takes literally the Liezi's descriptions of extraordinary realms — the mountains of the immortals, the country where people walk on air — understanding them as actual dimensions of reality accessible to those who have achieved sufficient spiritual cultivation. These passages serve as inspiration and aspiration for practitioners on the path of immortality.
