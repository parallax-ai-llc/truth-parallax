Zen interpretation of Chapter 3, 'King Mu of Zhou,' discovers a kindred spirit in a text that unsettles certainties, dissolves boundaries, and points toward a mode of awareness that transcends conceptual thought. The Liezi's characteristic strategies — paradox, reversal, the subversion of expectations — anticipate the Zen koan tradition's use of logical impossibility to trigger awakening beyond the conceptual mind.

Zen readers approach this chapter not as a text to be understood but as a provocation to be experienced. Its parables and paradoxes function in a manner similar to koans: they present the rational mind with something it cannot process, creating an opening for a mode of awareness that transcends logical categories. The Liezi's lightness and humor in addressing the most profound questions mirrors the Zen tradition's own characteristic blend of depth and playfulness.
