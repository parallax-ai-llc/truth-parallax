---
title: "Liezi - Chapter 8"
scripture: "liezi"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Liezi was studying archery and hit the target. He sought instruction from Kuan Yin Tzu.

**2** Kuan Yin Tzu said: Do you know why you hit the target? Liezi replied: No, I do not. Kuan Yin Tzu said: Then it is not yet good enough, and went away.

**3** Liezi practised for three years more, then went to report. Do you know why you hit the target? asked the Master. Yes, said Liezi, I know.

**4** Very well, then, replied the Master; hold fast to this knowledge, and do not let it slip. This applies not only to archery, but to self-cultivation as well.

**5** The Sage does not investigate the sources of things, but contemplates their effects.

**6** Liezi asked Kuan Yin about the Perfect Man: Can the Perfect Man walk beneath the water without difficulty? Can he tread on fire without being burned?

**7** The Perfect Man, replied Kuan Yin, can do these things not through courage, cleverness, determination, or daring, but through maintaining the purity of his original nature.

**8** That which creates form has itself no form, and that which produces sound produces no sound.

**9** Having apprehended this, one enters into a state where material things can no longer obstruct. Such a man dwells within the bounds of the infinite.

**10** Consider the case of a drunken man who falls from a carriage. Although he may be hurt, he does not die. His bones and joints are the same as other men's; yet the injury he receives is different, because his spirit is whole.

**11** If a man can get such concentration from wine, how much more could he get from nature! The Sage takes refuge in nature, and no thing can harm him.
