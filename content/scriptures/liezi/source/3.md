---
title: "Liezi - Chapter 3"
scripture: "liezi"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Chou, King of the Mu dynasty, once made an expedition to the west, ascending the Kun-lun mountains, whence he crossed the Yang-chiu range.

**2** On his return journey, a clever artificer named Yen Shih was presented to him. The King received him in audience, and asked him what he could do.

**3** Yen Shih replied: I will do anything your Majesty commands. But there is something I have already made which I should like to show your Majesty first.

**4** The next day Yen Shih came, bringing with him a life-sized figure which he had fashioned. The King stared at the figure in astonishment.

**5** It walked with rapid strides, moving its head up and down, so that anyone would have taken it for a live human being. The artificer touched its chin, and it began singing, perfectly in tune.

**6** He touched its hand, and it began posturing, keeping perfect time. It went through any number of movements that fancy might happen to dictate.

**7** As the performance was drawing to an end, the figure winked its eye and made advances to the ladies in attendance. This made the King angry.

**8** The artificer immediately took the figure to pieces. It turned out to be only a construction of leather, wood, glue, and lacquer, variously coloured.

**9** Examining it closely, the King found all the internal organs complete: liver, gall, heart, lungs, spleen, kidneys, stomach, and intestines; and over these, muscles, bones, and limbs.

**10** The King tried the effect of taking away the heart, and found that the mouth could no longer speak; he took away the liver, and the eyes could no longer see. The King was delighted.
