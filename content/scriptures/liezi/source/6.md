---
title: "Liezi - Chapter 6"
scripture: "liezi"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Effort said to Destiny: Your achievements are not equal to mine.

**2** Destiny replied: What achievements of yours can compare with mine in the working of things?

**3** Effort said: Longevity and shortness of life, failure and success, rank and wealth, poverty and meanness -- all these I have the power to bestow.

**4** Destiny replied: Peng Tsu was not wiser than Yao or Shun, yet he lived to the age of eight hundred. Yen Yuan was not less talented than the average man, yet he died at the age of thirty-two.

**5** The virtue of Confucius was not less than that of the rulers of States, yet he was reduced to dire straits. The conduct of Chou of Yin was no better than that of the Three Men of Virtue, yet he occupied the throne.

**6** If these results were really your doing, how is it that you allot long life to one and short life to another?

**7** Effort said: If, as you say, I have no control over events, is it not then owing to your management that things turn out as they do?

**8** Destiny replied: The very name Destiny shows that there is no question of management. I simply carry things straight on as they are.

**9** Why should I be biased in favour of longevity or shortness of life, success or failure, rank or meanness, riches or poverty?
