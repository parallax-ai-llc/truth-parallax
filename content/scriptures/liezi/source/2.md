---
title: "Liezi - Chapter 2"
scripture: "liezi"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Huang Ti had been on the throne for fifteen years and was gratified at being the idol of all within the Four Seas, when he began to take thought for his vital essence, and to consider how to nourish his body and regulate his mind.

**2** But in spite of every device, his complexion grew sallow, his senses dulled, and after another fifteen years of this he found himself haggard and wan.

**3** Then he sighed deeply and said: My error has been excessive devotion to my own personality, and the misery I suffer results from over-attention to external objects.

**4** Having recognized this truth, he laid aside the cares of government, dismissed his attendants, removed the hanging bells, reduced the daily fare, and retired to live at leisure in his private apartments.

**5** For three months he abstained from personal intervention in government. Then he fell asleep in the daytime and dreamt that he made a journey to the kingdom of Hua-hsu.

**6** This kingdom was without head or ruler; it simply went on of itself. Its people were without desires or cravings; they simply followed their natural instincts.

**7** They felt neither joy in life nor abhorrence of death; thus they came to no untimely ends. They felt neither attachment to self nor indifference to others.

**8** Water had no power to drown them, nor fire to burn; cuts and blows caused them neither injury nor pain.

**9** Clouds and mist obstructed not their vision; thunder-peals could not stun their ears; physical beauty disturbed not their hearts; mountains and valleys hindered not their steps. They moved about like gods.

**10** When Huang Ti awoke from his dream, he summoned his three Ministers and told them, saying: During the past three months I have been living in quiet, fasting and disciplining my body and mind, and meditating upon the proper way to nourish the body and regulate the mind.
