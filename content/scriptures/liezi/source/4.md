---
title: "Liezi - Chapter 4"
scripture: "liezi"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Confucius was viewing the cataract at Lu-liang. It fell from a height of two hundred and forty feet, and its spray reached a distance of forty li around.

**2** But Confucius spied an old man in the water, and taking him for someone in distress, he sent a disciple along the bank to pull him out.

**3** The old man emerged some hundred yards further on, and strolled along the embankment, with his hair hanging down, singing a song.

**4** Confucius followed and spoke to him. I had thought, he said, that you were a spirit, but now I see you are a man. Pray tell me, is there any way to deal with water?

**5** No, replied the old man, I have no way. I began with what was natural to me, grew up in it, and matured in it. I enter the vortex with the inflowing stream, and emerge with the outflowing one.

**6** I follow the Way of the water, and do not impose any will of my own. That is how I deal with it.

**7** Having been born on dry land, I am at ease on dry land. Having grown up in water, I am at ease in water. Doing it as I do it without knowing how I do it -- that is maturity.
