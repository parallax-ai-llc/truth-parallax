---
title: "Liezi - Chapter 7"
scripture: "liezi"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Yang Chu said: A hundred years is the limit of a long life. Not one in a thousand ever attains it.

**2** Suppose there is one such person. Infancy and feeble old age take almost half of this time.

**3** Rest during sleep at night and waking waste amount to almost half of the remainder. Pain and sickness, sorrow and suffering take off almost half of the rest.

**4** In the ten years and more that is left, I reckon, there is not one moment in which we can be happy, at ease, and free from worry.

**5** This being so, what is life for? What pleasure is there in it? For beauty and abundance, for music and sensual pleasure?

**6** But beauty and abundance cannot always satisfy, and music and sensual pleasure cannot always be enjoyed.

**7** Besides, being warned by punishments, and exhorted by rewards; driven forward by fame, and held back by the law: we are ever engaged in an anxious struggle.

**8** Yang Chu said: The men of old knew that in life they were but sojourners, and that in death they would return. Therefore they did what they liked and did not turn aside from what naturally pleased them.

**9** They were not tempted by fame in their lifetime, but followed their natural bent. They took their course through the world and did not resist their desires.

**10** Yang Chu said: All creatures in life are different, but in death they are all the same. In life, there are clever ones and foolish ones. In death, there is putrefaction and dissolution.

**11** A man may live for ten years or for a hundred years; a virtuous man will die, and a wicked man will die. In life they were Yao and Shun; in death they are rotten bones. Rotten bones are all the same; who shall distinguish between them?
