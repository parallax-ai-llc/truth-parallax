The eschatological teachings in this chapter have been analyzed for their relationship to Egyptian funerary traditions, Platonic philosophy, and Jewish apocalypticism. The detailed description of the soul's post-mortem journey through cosmic spheres, with specific tests and obstacles at each level, resembles Egyptian Book of the Dead traditions in which the deceased must pass through gates and answer questions. This parallel has led some scholars to argue for a specifically Egyptian context for the composition of Pistis Sophia.

The reincarnation doctrine has been compared to Platonic and Orphic traditions of metempsychosis. Scholars debate whether this reflects direct influence or independent development. The text's integration of reincarnation with a Christian soteriological framework — the mysteries provide ultimate liberation from the cycle — represents a distinctive synthesis that is neither purely Christian nor purely Platonic.
