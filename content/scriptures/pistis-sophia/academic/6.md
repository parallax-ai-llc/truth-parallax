The sacramental descriptions in this final chapter have generated significant scholarly interest as evidence for Gnostic liturgical practice. The three baptisms (water, fire, Holy Spirit) are described with enough specificity to suggest real ritual procedures: specific prayers, materials (wine, water, fire, incense), and ritual actions. Jorunn Buckley and others have compared these rites to Mandaean baptismal practices, noting structural similarities that may indicate a shared ritual tradition.

The chapter's anointing ritual, described as the "mystery of spiritual anointing," has been compared to early Christian chrismation practices documented in the Apostolic Tradition and other early liturgical sources. Scholars note that while orthodox and Gnostic communities may have shared certain ritual forms, the theological interpretation of these rites diverged significantly. The academic study of these sacramental descriptions contributes to the broader project of reconstructing the diversity of early Christian worship practices before the standardization of liturgy in the fourth and fifth centuries.
