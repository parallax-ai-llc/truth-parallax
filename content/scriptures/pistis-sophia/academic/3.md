Scholarly analysis of the rescue narrative focuses on its mythological structure and its relationship to other Sophia myths in Gnostic literature. The rescue of Sophia in Pistis Sophia differs from the Valentinian version (in which Sophia's passion is resolved through the production of the Demiurge and the material world) and from the Sethian version (in which Barbelo's descent is more voluntary). The Pistis Sophia version emphasizes Sophia's helplessness and dependence on the savior figure, a soteriology that some scholars describe as more "orthodox" in structure if not in content.

The light-powers' systematic defeat of the archons has been analyzed as a mythological expression of the community's experience of spiritual liberation. The archons may represent social, psychological, or political powers as well as cosmic ones. Scholars debate whether the text's cosmological language functions purely as metaphysics or also as coded social critique.
