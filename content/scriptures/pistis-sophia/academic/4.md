The mysteries of the First Mystery have been studied for what they reveal about the organizational structure and ritual practices of the community that produced Pistis Sophia. The hierarchical arrangement of mysteries suggests a community with graded levels of initiation, possibly modeled on the mystery religions of the Greco-Roman world or on the degrees of holiness in the Jerusalem Temple.

Scholars have compared the mystery system to other ancient initiatory structures, including the Eleusinian mysteries, the Mithraic grades, and the Hermetic corpus. Einar Thomassen's work on Valentinian sacramental practice provides a useful comparative framework. The question of whether Pistis Sophia's mysteries reflect actual ritual practice or ideal theological constructions remains open, though the specificity of the descriptions suggests some basis in real community life.
