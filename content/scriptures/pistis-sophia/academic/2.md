The thirteen repentances of Sophia have received extensive scholarly analysis, particularly regarding their use of the Psalms. Majella Franzmann's detailed study traces each repentance to its Psalm source and analyzes the hermeneutical techniques by which the Gnostic author transforms the meaning of the biblical text. The interpretive method combines allegorical reading with what Franzmann calls "mythological recontextualization" — the Psalm's imagery is read as direct description of Sophia's cosmic situation.

Mary Magdalene's role in this chapter has been central to scholarly discussions of gender and authority in early Christianity. She provides interpretations of ten of the thirteen repentances, outperforming all the male disciples. Karen King's analysis situates this within a broader pattern in Gnostic literature in which Mary represents a model of spiritual authority that challenges the emerging patriarchal structures of orthodox Christianity.
