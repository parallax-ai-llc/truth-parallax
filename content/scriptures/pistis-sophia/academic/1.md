Academic analysis of Pistis Sophia chapter 1 addresses questions of literary structure, dating, and relationship to other Gnostic texts. Carl Schmidt's critical edition identified the Askew Codex as containing four books, with the first three forming a relatively coherent unit and the fourth being a separate composition. The light-vesture scene in chapter 1 establishes the narrative frame for the entire first section: Jesus's post-resurrection instruction of the disciples.

Scholars have debated the text's date, with most placing it in the third century CE, likely the latter half. The elaborate cosmological system described in the light-vesture scene shows development beyond the simpler systems of second-century Gnostic texts like the Apocryphon of John. The specific astronomical and astrological references in the chapter have been studied for what they reveal about the author's scientific and cosmological knowledge.
