The rescue of Sophia from chaos presents Catholic scholars with a soteriology that parallels but fundamentally differs from Catholic teaching on redemption. Catholic soteriology centers on the Incarnation, Passion, and Resurrection of Christ — God entering human history to save humanity from within. The Gnostic rescue of Sophia is a cosmic extraction, a divine intervention that lifts the fallen aeon out of the material realm rather than redeeming the material realm itself.

Catholic scholars note the absence of any concept of bodily resurrection or the redemption of matter in this chapter. Catholic eschatology affirms the resurrection of the body and the renewal of creation; Gnostic soteriology envisions escape from materiality entirely. This difference reflects the fundamental theological divide between Catholic incarnational theology and Gnostic anti-cosmic dualism.
