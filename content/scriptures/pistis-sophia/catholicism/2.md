Catholic scholars are particularly attentive to Sophia's thirteen repentances as an extended meditation on penitence. The Catholic sacramental tradition places great emphasis on confession and penance, and Sophia's persistent cry for forgiveness resonates with this emphasis. However, Catholic theology locates repentance within the sacramental framework of the Church — the penitent confesses to a priest who acts in persona Christi — whereas Sophia's repentance is a cosmic cry directed toward the light itself.

Mary Magdalene's prominence in this chapter interests Catholic scholars in light of the Church's complex relationship with the Magdalene tradition. Pope Gregory I identified her with the sinful woman of Luke 7; modern Catholic scholarship has corrected this identification. Pistis Sophia's portrait of Mary as the wisest disciple contributes to the Catholic re-evaluation of her significance, without granting the Gnostic text doctrinal weight.
