Jewish scholars approach Pistis Sophia chapter 1 as a document that appropriates and transforms Jewish mystical traditions. The scene of Jesus ascending through the heavens and receiving garments of light has parallels in Merkavah (chariot) mysticism, particularly the Hekhalot literature in which the mystic ascends through heavenly palaces and is invested with angelic garments. The Gnostic text likely drew on these Jewish mystical traditions while subordinating them to a christological framework entirely foreign to Judaism.

The concept of a light-vesture also connects to Jewish traditions about the garments of Adam. Rabbinic literature describes Adam and Eve as originally clothed in garments of light (or glory) that were lost at the Fall. The Gnostic text's investiture scene may represent a transformation of this Jewish tradition, with Jesus recovering the light-garments that Adam lost.
