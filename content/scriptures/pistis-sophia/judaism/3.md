Jewish scholars examine the rescue of Sophia with attention to its use of Jewish motifs of divine rescue and redemption. The Hebrew Bible's paradigmatic rescue — the Exodus from Egypt — is a collective, historical liberation that leads to covenant and Torah at Sinai. The Gnostic rescue of Sophia is a cosmic extraction of a single divine figure from the realm of matter, with no covenantal or communal dimensions.

The defeat of the archons and hostile powers in this chapter also draws on Jewish angelological and demonological traditions. The Jewish apocalyptic literature of the Second Temple period describes cosmic battles between angelic and demonic forces, but these battles occur within a framework of divine sovereignty over creation. The Gnostic version pits the true God against a false creator, a dualism that Jewish monotheism does not admit.
