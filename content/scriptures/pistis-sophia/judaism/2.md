Jewish scholars find the chapter's systematic reinterpretation of the Psalms particularly noteworthy. The Psalms are central to Jewish liturgy, recited daily in synagogue worship and on Shabbat. Pistis Sophia's correlation of specific Psalms with episodes in Sophia's cosmic drama represents a radical decontextualization that removes the Psalms from their liturgical and covenantal setting and presses them into service for an alien cosmological narrative.

The figure of Sophia (Wisdom) has Jewish roots in the Hokmah tradition of Proverbs, Job, and Ben Sira. But the Gnostic Sophia's fall from the heavenly realm due to misguided desire transforms the confident, creative Wisdom of Jewish tradition into a tragic figure requiring rescue. Jewish scholars view this transformation as characteristic of the Gnostic inversion of Jewish theology.
