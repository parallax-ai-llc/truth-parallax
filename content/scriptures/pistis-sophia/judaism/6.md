Jewish scholars note that the sacramental practices described in this chapter, while presented within a Christian Gnostic framework, have roots in Jewish ritual practice. Baptismal immersion derives from the mikveh tradition; anointing has precedents in the anointing of priests and kings in the Hebrew Bible; sealing invokes the biblical concept of the mark or sign of covenant (the sign of circumcision, the blood on the doorposts at Passover).

The Gnostic transformation of these Jewish ritual elements illustrates a broader pattern: Jewish practices are preserved in form but radically altered in meaning. The mikveh restores ritual purity within the covenant; the Gnostic baptism transforms the soul's cosmic substance. The anointing marks a person for service within the community; the Gnostic anointing empowers the soul for cosmic ascent. Jewish scholars study these transformations as evidence for the complex relationship between Judaism and the religious movements that emerged from and alongside it.
