## Overview

Pistis Sophia is one of the most extensive surviving Gnostic texts, preserved in the Askew Codex (British Library). Written in Coptic and likely dating to the third century CE, it presents a post-resurrection Jesus instructing his disciples — including prominently Mary Magdalene — about the fall and redemption of the divine aeon Sophia (Pistis Sophia, "Faith Wisdom"), the structure of the cosmos, the mysteries required for salvation, and the fates of souls after death.

## Structure

The text is organized around Jesus's extended post-resurrection teaching, covering six major thematic areas:

1. **The Risen Jesus and the Light-Vesture** (Ch 1): Jesus describes his ascent through the heavens and his investiture with the light-vesture
2. **Sophia's Fall and Her Thirteen Repentances** (Ch 2): The story of Sophia's fall into chaos and her thirteen songs of repentance
3. **The Rescue of Sophia from Chaos** (Ch 3): Jesus rescues Sophia and restores her to her place in the heavenly realm
4. **The Mysteries of the First Mystery** (Ch 4): Teaching about the hierarchies of mysteries and their salvific power
5. **The Fates of Souls After Death** (Ch 5): Detailed descriptions of the post-mortem journey and the punishments and rewards of souls
6. **The Sacraments and Final Mysteries** (Ch 6): The baptisms, seals, and final mysteries that ensure the soul's liberation

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Pistis Sophia is significant across traditions primarily as a historical document. It provides the most detailed surviving account of a Gnostic Sophia myth, offers unique perspectives on early Christian attitudes toward Mary Magdalene's authority, and preserves liturgical and sacramental practices of a Gnostic Christian community.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Sophia's Fall | The divine aeon's descent into chaos through misguided desire |
| Repentance | Sophia's thirteen repentances as a model of spiritual transformation |
| Light and Darkness | The cosmic struggle between the realms of light and the rulers of chaos |
| Post-Resurrection Teaching | Jesus as the revealer of cosmic mysteries after the resurrection |
| Mary Magdalene | Her prominent role as Jesus's primary interlocutor and wisest disciple |
| Sacramental Mysteries | Baptisms, seals, and rituals necessary for the soul's ascent |
