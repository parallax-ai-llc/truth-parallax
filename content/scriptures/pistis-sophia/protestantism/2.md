The chapter's account of Sophia's fall and her thirteen repentances represents the central myth of Pistis Sophia. Protestant scholars analyze this narrative with attention to its systematic reinterpretation of the canonical Psalms. Each of Sophia's repentances is correlated with a Psalm, which is allegorically reread as expressing the cosmic drama of a fallen aeon. Protestant hermeneutics, which emphasizes the historical and grammatical sense of Scripture, identifies this method as a fundamental misreading of the Psalms.

The figure of Mary Magdalene as the primary interpreter of Sophia's repentances has generated significant discussion among Protestant scholars. The canonical Gospels give Mary Magdalene a prominent role as witness to the resurrection, and Protestant historians of early Christianity examine how Gnostic texts like Pistis Sophia extended her role into a model of spiritual authority.
