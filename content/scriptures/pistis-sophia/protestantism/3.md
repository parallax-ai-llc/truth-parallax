Protestant scholars read the rescue of Sophia from chaos with attention to its soteriology. In Protestant theology, salvation is accomplished through Christ's atoning death and resurrection, received by faith alone (sola fide). In Pistis Sophia, salvation is a cosmic rescue operation in which light-powers descend to extract Sophia from chaos — a model that emphasizes divine power rather than sacrificial love, and that operates on a cosmic rather than personal level.

The chapter's systematic defeat of the archons and hostile powers has been compared by Protestant scholars to Colossians 2:15, where Christ "disarmed the rulers and authorities and put them to open shame." The canonical and Gnostic versions share the motif of Christ's triumph over cosmic powers, but the canonical version grounds this triumph in the cross, while the Gnostic version presents it as a display of superior light-power without reference to crucifixion or atonement.
