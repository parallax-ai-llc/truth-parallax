Pistis Sophia is one of the most important texts for understanding Gnostic soteriology, cosmology, and sacramental practice. The text presents a comprehensive account of the Sophia myth — her fall from the Pleroma, her entrapment in chaos by the archons, her thirteen repentances, and her eventual rescue by Jesus as the emissary of the First Mystery. For Gnostic readers, Sophia's journey is the archetype of every soul's experience: falling into the material world, suffering under the powers of ignorance, crying out in repentance, and being rescued through divine knowledge.

One of the most striking features of Pistis Sophia is the prominence of Mary Magdalene. She asks the majority of questions, provides the most interpretations, and is praised by Jesus as the disciple who surpasses all others in understanding. Gnostic communities that honored this text clearly regarded Mary as an authoritative voice, a tradition that challenges the patriarchal structures of emerging orthodox Christianity.

The text's detailed descriptions of baptisms, seals, and mysteries provide invaluable evidence for Gnostic ritual life. These sacraments are presented as essential for the soul's post-mortem journey through the heavenly spheres to the Treasury of Light.
