The final chapter details the sacraments and mysteries that constitute the practical core of Gnostic ritual life as described in Pistis Sophia. Jesus reveals three baptisms — the baptism of water, the baptism of fire, and the baptism of the Holy Spirit — along with the mystery of spiritual anointing and various sealing rites. Each sacrament has specific effects: the water baptism cleanses, the fire baptism purifies, and the Spirit baptism transforms the soul's substance from material to spiritual.

For Gnostic communities, these sacramental descriptions would have served as liturgical guidelines. The specific prayers, invocations, and ritual actions described by Jesus provided the framework for the community's worship life. The sacraments are not merely symbolic but ontologically transformative — they change the nature of the soul, preparing it for its post-mortem ascent.

The chapter concludes with Jesus's final instructions to the disciples, commissioning them to go and share the mysteries with those who are worthy. Mary Magdalene, Peter, and the other disciples receive their final charges. The text ends with a sense of cosmic urgency: the time for receiving the mysteries is limited, and those who delay risk being caught in the cycle of reincarnation when the cosmic order is finally dissolved and the Treasury of Light is sealed.
