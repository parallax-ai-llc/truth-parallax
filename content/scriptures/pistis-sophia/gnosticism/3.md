The rescue of Sophia from chaos is the climactic moment of the Gnostic salvation narrative. Jesus, acting on the command of the First Mystery, sends light-powers down into chaos to lift Sophia out of the darkness and restore her to her rightful place. The archons and the lion-faced Authades are defeated, their false light extinguished, and Sophia is brought up through the successive levels of the cosmos toward the thirteenth aeon.

For Gnostic readers, Sophia's rescue is the prototype of every soul's salvation. Just as Sophia could not free herself from chaos but required a savior sent from the highest realm, so the Gnostic initiate cannot escape the material world through personal effort alone but must receive the light-power through the mysteries and sacraments. The rescue is corporate as well as individual: when Sophia is restored, the entire cosmic order is adjusted and the archons' power diminished.

The chapter's detailed description of the light-powers' descent into chaos and their systematic defeat of the hostile powers would have served as a cosmic map for the Gnostic community, assuring them that the powers of darkness, however threatening, are ultimately subordinate to the First Mystery and will be overcome. Sophia's hymn of thanksgiving upon her rescue expresses the joy and gratitude of the soul that has been brought from darkness into light.
