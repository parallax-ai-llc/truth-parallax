The chapter on the fates of souls after death provides the most detailed eschatological teaching in Pistis Sophia. Jesus describes what happens to souls of varying degrees of spiritual attainment when they die. Souls that have received the mysteries ascend through the heavenly spheres, presenting the appropriate seals and passwords at each level, until they reach the Treasury of Light. Souls that have not received the mysteries are recycled through reincarnation, descending into bodies determined by their karmic condition.

For Gnostic readers, this chapter provided both consolation and warning. The elaborate description of the soul's post-mortem journey assured initiates that the mysteries they had received would protect them. At the same time, the detailed accounts of the punishments awaiting the unrepentant and the uninitiated served as motivation for seeking the mysteries and maintaining ethical conduct.

The chapter's teaching on reincarnation — the soul passing from body to body until it receives the mysteries and achieves liberation — reflects a cosmological framework that distinguishes Pistis Sophia from canonical Christianity. The goal is not resurrection of the body but liberation from embodiment entirely, an escape from the cycle of birth and death into the permanent light of the Treasury.
