Chapter 2 contains the core Gnostic myth of Pistis Sophia: the story of Sophia's fall from the thirteenth aeon and her thirteen repentances. Sophia looked toward the height and saw the light of the Treasury, desiring to ascend to it. But she was deceived by the lion-faced power Authades, who led her down into chaos — the formless depths below the aeons — where she was tormented by the archons and material powers.

Each of Sophia's thirteen repentances is a song of lamentation and petition that Gnostic readers understood as expressing the fundamental condition of the divine spark trapped in matter. Sophia's words — "O Light of lights, in whom I have had faith from the beginning, hear my repentance" — gave voice to the Gnostic initiate's own experience of alienation, suffering, and longing for the divine realm. The thirteen repentances map a complete emotional and spiritual journey from despair through hope to confident expectation of rescue.

The disciples, particularly Mary Magdalene, interpret Sophia's repentances by correlating them with canonical Psalms. This interpretive practice reveals how Gnostic communities read biblical texts: the Psalms are not merely human prayers but cosmic documents that encode the drama of Sophia's fall and restoration. Mary Magdalene's dominant role as interpreter in this chapter establishes her as the disciple who most fully understands the mystery of Sophia's suffering.
