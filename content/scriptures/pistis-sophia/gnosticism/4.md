The mysteries of the First Mystery represent the deepest level of esoteric teaching in Pistis Sophia. Jesus explains to his disciples the hierarchical structure of the mysteries — from the mysteries of the twelve aeons to the mysteries of the First Mystery itself, which is the gateway to the Treasury of Light. Each level of mystery corresponds to a level of cosmic reality and confers specific powers and protections upon the initiate.

For Gnostic readers, this chapter functions as a comprehensive initiation manual. The mysteries are not abstract theological concepts but practical sacramental realities that the initiate must receive to ensure safe passage through the heavenly spheres after death. Without the appropriate mystery for each level, the soul will be stopped by the archons who guard the boundaries between realms.

The chapter also establishes a moral framework that surprised early scholars who assumed Gnosticism was purely antinomian. Jesus teaches that the mysteries have the power to forgive sins, but he also insists that the initiate must practice righteousness. The mysteries are not magical formulas that override moral behavior but sacramental graces that empower and require ethical transformation.
