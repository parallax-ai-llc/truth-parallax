The mysteries of the First Mystery present a system of graded initiation that Orthodox scholars contrast with the Orthodox approach to the sacraments. In Orthodoxy, the mysteries (sacraments) are administered within the life of the Church and are available to all baptized Christians. There is no secret hierarchy of higher and lower mysteries accessible only to advancing initiates. The Eucharist, the central mystery of Orthodoxy, is offered to every baptized, chrismated Christian — not reserved for a spiritual elite.

The chapter's description of mysteries as providing "passwords" for post-mortem cosmic ascent also diverges from Orthodox eschatology. Orthodox teaching on the toll-houses (though debated within Orthodoxy) involves the soul's encounter with demons after death, but the remedy is not secret knowledge or cosmic passwords but a life of repentance, prayer, and participation in the sacraments within the Church.
