The rescue of Sophia from chaos presents Orthodox scholars with a narrative that inverts the Orthodox understanding of salvation. Orthodox soteriology, centered on theosis (deification), teaches that Christ entered the material world to transform it from within — "God became man so that man might become God," in Athanasius's famous formulation. The Gnostic rescue removes Sophia from the material world rather than sanctifying it.

Orthodox scholars note the absence of any ecclesial dimension in the rescue narrative. In Orthodoxy, salvation is worked out within the community of the Church through the sacramental life, prayer, and mutual love. Sophia's rescue is an individual cosmic extraction performed by divine light-powers without reference to any community or liturgical context.
