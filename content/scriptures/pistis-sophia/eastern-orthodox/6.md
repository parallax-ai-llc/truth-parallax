The sacramental descriptions in this final chapter invite comparison with Orthodox liturgical practice. The three baptisms (water, fire, Spirit) differ from the Orthodox practice of a single threefold immersion in the name of the Trinity, followed immediately by Chrismation and first Communion. The Gnostic multiplication of baptismal rites into separate ceremonies with distinct cosmic effects reflects a different understanding of the relationship between ritual and salvation.

Orthodox scholars note that while both Orthodox and Gnostic traditions emphasize the transformative power of the sacraments, the Orthodox mysteries transform the whole person — body and soul — for life in the renewed creation, while the Gnostic sacraments aim to liberate the soul from the body and the material world. This difference crystallizes the fundamental divergence between Orthodox incarnational theology and Gnostic anti-cosmic soteriology.
