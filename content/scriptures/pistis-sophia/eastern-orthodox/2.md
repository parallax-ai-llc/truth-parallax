Orthodox scholars approach Sophia's thirteen repentances through the lens of the Orthodox penitential tradition. The Great Canon of St. Andrew of Crete, sung during Lent, is a sustained hymn of repentance that in some ways parallels Sophia's songs. However, Orthodox repentance (metanoia) is oriented toward the God who created and loves the material world, while Sophia's repentance is a cry to be delivered from the material world itself.

The Orthodox tradition honors Mary Magdalene as "Equal to the Apostles" and recognizes her as the first witness of the Resurrection. Her prominence in Pistis Sophia, while not doctrinally authoritative for Orthodoxy, confirms the tradition's recognition of her significance in early Christian memory. Orthodox scholars note that the Gnostic elevation of Mary does not contradict but rather corroborates the high status she holds in Orthodox hagiography.
