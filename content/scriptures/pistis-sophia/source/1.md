---
title: "Pistis Sophia Section 1"
scripture: "pistis-sophia"
chapter: 1
---

1. It came to pass, after Jesus had risen from the dead, that he spent eleven years speaking with his disciples, teaching them the mysteries of the invisible world.

2. He taught them only as far as the regions of the First Mystery and the region of the First Commandment, and he had not yet revealed the fullness to them.

3. On the fifteenth day of the month of Tybi, the full moon, Jesus sat with his disciples upon the Mount of Olives and said to them:

4. "Rejoice and be glad, for the time has come when I shall speak to you openly, without parables, concerning the mysteries of the Pleroma."

5. While they sat together, a great light-power descended from above, so brilliant that no measure could contain it.

6. The light came forth from the light of lights, and it descended upon Jesus and surrounded him entirely, and he shone exceedingly beyond all measure.

7. The disciples could not see Jesus because of the great light that was upon him. Their eyes were blinded, and they saw only the light.

8. The light-power drew Jesus upward, and he ascended into the height, and the disciples watched in great fear and agitation.

9. Jesus ascended through the heavens, through the regions of the Archons, through the spheres of fate, through the realms of the invisible ones, until he reached the thirteenth Aeon.

10. And in the thirteenth Aeon he found Pistis Sophia, who had fallen from her place in the Pleroma and was weeping in the Darkness.

11. She was below all the twelve Aeons, in a region of chaos, surrounded by the emanations of the Self-Willed One and his lion-faced power.

12. Jesus looked upon Pistis Sophia with compassion, for she was a power of Light that had been deceived and had fallen into the realm of matter.

13. And he determined to help her, to hear her repentances, and to restore her to her rightful place in the Kingdom of Light.
