## Eschatological Liberation: Gnostic and Cathar Visions

The concluding chapter's vision of the final liberation of all souls and the dissolution of the material world parallels the eschatological hopes of ancient Gnostic communities. The Valentinian expectation of the Pleroma's restoration, the Manichaean vision of the final separation of Light and Darkness, and the Sethian hope for the spiritual race's return to the realm of Barbelo all share the conviction that the present cosmic order is temporary and will ultimately give way to a state of pure spiritual existence.

The *Liber de Duobus Principiis*' eschatology is notable for its universalism: all souls will eventually be liberated, regardless of how many incarnations the process requires. This universal salvation stands in contrast to the selective soteriology of some Gnostic systems (which distinguished between pneumatics, psychics, and hylics, with only the first category guaranteed salvation) and to the Catholic doctrine of eternal damnation for the unrepentant.

The parallel with Manichaean eschatology is particularly close. Mani taught that the cosmic process would culminate in a final separation of Light and Darkness, with all divine light-particles liberated from material imprisonment and the Realm of Darkness sealed away forever. The *Liber de Duobus Principiis* envisions an analogous final separation, reinforcing the scholarly hypothesis of some form of historical or conceptual connection between ancient Manichaeism and medieval Catharism.
