## True Creation and Material Fabrication

The fifth chapter draws a sharp distinction between true creation and material fabrication. True creation is the work of the good God: the spiritual realm, the angelic beings, and the souls that properly belong to the kingdom of light. Material fabrication is the work of the evil principle: the physical world with its corruption, decay, and death. The two "creations" are not different aspects of a single divine act but the products of two entirely separate and opposed creative powers.

For Cathar theology, this distinction resolved the apparent contradiction between biblical affirmations of creation's goodness (Genesis 1:31) and the manifest evidence of the world's corruption. The Cathar answer was that Genesis 1 describes the good God's spiritual creation — a creation that is indeed "very good" — while the material world that human beings inhabit is a different creation entirely, the product of the evil principle's malevolent will.

This chapter also addresses the question of the human being's composite nature. Each person is a union of two elements drawn from opposite sources: a soul from the good God and a body from the evil principle. The human condition is therefore one of internal war — the soul straining toward its divine origin while the body drags it deeper into material captivity.
