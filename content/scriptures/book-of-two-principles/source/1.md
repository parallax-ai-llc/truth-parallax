---
title: "Book of Two Principles Section 1"
scripture: "book-of-two-principles"
chapter: 1
---

**1** It is to be demonstrated by manifold proofs that there exist two principles, one of good and one of evil, from which all things that are have their origin and cause.

**2** For it is manifest that the true God, who is perfectly good, could not be the cause or origin of evil, since from a good tree evil fruit cannot come, as the Lord says in the Gospel.

**3** Therefore there must exist another principle, a principle of evil, from which all evil things proceed and have their origin.

**4** That the true God is not the cause of evil is evident from the word of the Apostle James, who says: "Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God; for God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth He any man."

**5** And again it is written: "God is light, and in Him is no darkness at all." If therefore in God there is no darkness, and evil is darkness, then evil cannot come from God.

**6** Furthermore, the Lord said: "A good tree cannot bring forth evil fruit, neither can a corrupt tree bring forth good fruit." Therefore the good God, being a good tree, cannot bring forth evil, which is evil fruit.
