---
title: "Book of Two Principles Section 5"
scripture: "book-of-two-principles"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Concerning free will: some say that God gave free will to His creatures, and that evil entered through the misuse of free will. But this argument does not stand.

**2** For if God is omniscient, He knew before creating that His creatures would misuse their freedom. And if He knew this and still created them, then He is the indirect cause of evil, which contradicts His perfect goodness.

**3** Moreover, if the angels fell through free will alone, without any evil principle to tempt them, whence came the impulse to evil? From nothing? But from nothing, nothing comes.

**4** Therefore there must have been an evil principle already existing, which provided the occasion and the temptation for the fall.

**5** The fall of the angels is thus explained not by free will alone but by the action of the evil principle upon them, drawing them away from the good God and into the world of matter.

**6** And it is for this reason that the good God sent Christ, to recall these fallen souls to their true home in the kingdom of light.
