---
title: "Book of Two Principles Section 4"
scripture: "book-of-two-principles"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Against those who object and say that two principles diminish the power of God, we answer that, on the contrary, it is those who attribute evil to the good God who diminish His goodness and His perfection.

**2** For to say that God created evil, or that He permits evil when He could prevent it, is to impugn either His goodness or His omnipotence.

**3** But we maintain that the good God is perfectly good and perfectly powerful over all that is His, that is, over the spiritual world and over the souls that belong to Him.

**4** The evil principle has power only over matter and over this transient world, and its power shall come to an end when all souls are freed from their bodily prisons.

**5** Therefore we do not diminish God's power by positing two principles; rather, we preserve His perfect goodness, which is His most essential attribute.

**6** For it is better to say that evil comes from an evil principle than to say that evil comes from the good God, for the latter is the greater blasphemy.
