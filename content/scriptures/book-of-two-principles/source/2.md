---
title: "Book of Two Principles Section 2"
scripture: "book-of-two-principles"
chapter: 2
---

**1** That there is another principle besides the true God is shown also from the words of Christ Himself, who says: "The prince of this world cometh, and hath nothing in me."

**2** If the prince of this world is not the true God, and yet he has power and dominion over this world, then there must exist another principle from which his power derives.

**3** Again, the Lord says: "No man can serve two masters." If there were but one principle, there would be but one master. But since the Lord speaks of two masters, it follows that there are two principles.

**4** The Apostle Paul also says: "The god of this world hath blinded the minds of them which believe not." If the god of this world is different from the true God — and he must be, since the true God does not blind minds — then there is another principle.

**5** And in the Prophet Isaiah it is written: "I form the light and create darkness; I make peace and create evil." But this text must be understood correctly: the true God creates the light and the peace, while the evil principle creates the darkness and the evil.

**6** For it would be blasphemy to attribute the creation of evil to the perfectly good God.
