---
title: "Book of Two Principles Section 6"
scripture: "book-of-two-principles"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Concerning the Old Testament: we must distinguish between those parts that come from the good God and those that come from the evil principle.

**2** The Law of Moses, with its commandments concerning sacrifice, war, and the killing of animals, cannot come from the good God, for the good God does not command bloodshed.

**3** When Moses says "Thou shalt not kill," this is from the good God. But when he commands the slaughter of the Canaanites, this is from the god of this world, who is the evil principle.

**4** The Psalms and the Prophets, insofar as they speak of mercy, love, and truth, come from the good God. But insofar as they speak of wrath, vengeance, and destruction, they come from the evil principle.

**5** The New Testament, and especially the words of Christ, come entirely from the good God, for Christ is the messenger of the good God sent to free souls from the prison of matter.

**6** Therefore we accept the Gospels and the Epistles of Paul as our supreme authority, but we read the Old Testament with discernment, separating the wheat from the chaff.
