Catholic commentators read chapter 11's complex political narrative as illustrative of the challenges facing religious leaders who must navigate hostile political environments. Jonathan's ability to maintain Jewish interests amid the shifting alliances of the Seleucid Empire demonstrates the virtue of prudence — the charioteer of the virtues in Catholic moral theology — which enables right judgment about particular circumstances.

The chapter's account of military conflicts and diplomatic maneuvers is interpreted within the Catholic framework of just war theory, which requires legitimate authority, just cause, right intention, and proportionate means. Jonathan's military actions, undertaken to defend Jewish religious freedom and communal survival, meet these criteria in Catholic analysis, providing a historical precedent for the Church's teaching on legitimate defense.
