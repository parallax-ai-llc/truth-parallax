The rededication of the Temple in chapter 4 holds special significance in Catholic interpretation as a type of the Eucharistic liturgy and the consecration of churches. The careful purification of the sanctuary, the crafting of new sacred vessels, and the offering of sacrifice on the restored altar prefigure the Catholic understanding of sacred space and liturgical worship. The eight-day celebration parallels the octave celebrations in the Catholic liturgical calendar.

The Catechism cites the importance of the dedication of places of worship (CCC 1186), and the Maccabean rededication provides an Old Testament model for this practice. Catholic interpreters also note the prophetic dimension: the restored Temple foreshadows the glorified body of Christ, the ultimate Temple (John 2:21), and by extension the Church as the Body of Christ in which God's presence dwells among his people.
