Catholic exegesis reads the zeal of Mattathias as a righteous response to blasphemy, analogous to the zeal of Phinehas (Numbers 25), whom Mattathias himself invokes in his deathbed speech. The Catholic tradition of just war finds in this chapter one of its earliest scriptural foundations: the decision to take up arms in defense of faith and sacred law is presented as both legitimate and praiseworthy.

The Mattathias narrative also illustrates the Catholic principle that religious authority carries with it the duty to resist injustice. His role as a priest who leads both worship and resistance models the Catholic understanding that spiritual and temporal concerns are not separate spheres but interconnected dimensions of the Church's mission. The deathbed exhortation to remember the deeds of the ancestors reflects the Catholic emphasis on sacred tradition as a living source of guidance.
