Orthodox exegesis reads the revolt of Mattathias as a righteous act of zeal inspired by the Holy Spirit, comparing his action to that of the Prophet Elijah against the prophets of Baal. The patriarch's willingness to use force in defense of the faith reflects the Orthodox understanding of holy warfare — not as aggression but as the defense of sacred tradition against desecration. The deathbed testament of Mattathias, recounting the faithfulness of the patriarchs, exemplifies the Orthodox principle that tradition is the living memory of the Church.

The decision to permit fighting on the Sabbath (2:41) is understood by Orthodox interpreters as an exercise of oikonomia — the pastoral flexibility that allows temporary departure from strict observance when the survival of the faith community is at stake. This principle remains operative in Orthodox canonical practice today.
