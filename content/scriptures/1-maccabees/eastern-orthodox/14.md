Orthodox interpreters read chapter 14's celebration of Simon's achievements as a literary icon of the messianic peace that the Church already experiences in its liturgical life and will fully enjoy in the age to come. The description of each person sitting "under his vine and his fig tree" (14:12) echoes the prophetic vision of eschatological peace and is read typologically as pointing to the heavenly banquet.

The formal recognition of Simon's authority by the people, inscribed on bronze tablets in the Temple, illustrates the Orthodox principle of conciliarity (sobornost) — the conviction that legitimate authority is confirmed by the consensus of the faithful. This conciliar model of governance, in which authority is recognized from below rather than imposed from above, remains foundational to Orthodox ecclesiology.
