Orthodox commentators read the alliance with Rome in chapter 8 with the awareness that Rome would later become both the seat of the universal Church and, from the Orthodox perspective, the source of ecclesiological innovations that led to the Great Schism. The Maccabean engagement with Roman power is thus read with a certain ironic awareness of subsequent history.

The chapter illustrates the Orthodox principle of symphonia — the proper relationship between spiritual and temporal authority — while also acknowledging the dangers inherent in such alliances. Orthodox interpreters note that engagement with secular powers is sometimes necessary for the survival of the faith community, but must always be governed by the priority of preserving the integrity of worship and doctrine.
