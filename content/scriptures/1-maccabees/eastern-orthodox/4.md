The rededication of the Temple in chapter 4 is of central importance in Orthodox interpretation, as it prefigures the consecration of Christian churches — one of the most solemn ceremonies in Orthodox liturgical life. The careful purification of the sanctuary, the replacement of defiled stones, and the kindling of the lampstand resonate with Orthodox practices of church consecration, in which the altar is anointed, relics are deposited, and the first Divine Liturgy is celebrated.

The eight-day celebration of the rededication is understood as anticipating the Christian Paschal octave and the eschatological "eighth day" — the age of the resurrection that transcends the seven-day cycle of earthly time. Orthodox hymnography on the feast of the Holy Maccabees draws extensively on this chapter's imagery of light, purification, and joyful worship restored.
