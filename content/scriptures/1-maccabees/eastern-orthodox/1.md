Orthodox interpretation of 1 Maccabees 1 reads the persecution of Antiochus IV Epiphanes as a paradigmatic account of the struggle between the true faith and the spirit of worldly power. The desecration of the Temple and the forced adoption of pagan customs represent, in Orthodox theological understanding, the assault of demonic forces against the people of God — a pattern that recurs throughout salvation history and finds its ultimate expression in the eschatological tribulation.

The faithfulness of those who chose death over apostasy (1:62-63) is commemorated in the Orthodox liturgical calendar and held up as a model for all Christians facing persecution. The Orthodox Church, having endured centuries of persecution under Ottoman rule and Soviet communism, reads this chapter with particular intimacy, recognizing in the Maccabean experience a mirror of its own suffering and steadfast witness.
