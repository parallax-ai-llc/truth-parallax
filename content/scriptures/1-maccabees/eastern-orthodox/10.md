Orthodox interpreters read Jonathan's acceptance of the high priesthood in chapter 10 as a complex development that raises questions about the relationship between canonical legitimacy and providential necessity. While recognizing the irregularity of the Hasmonean priesthood, Orthodox exegesis emphasizes God's sovereignty in working through imperfect human instruments to preserve his people and their worship.

The chapter's account of competing political alliances illustrates the Byzantine concept of oikonomia at the political level — the prudent management of difficult circumstances in service of the greater good. Orthodox interpreters, shaped by centuries of engagement with imperial politics, read Jonathan's diplomatic skill with appreciation while maintaining awareness of the spiritual dangers inherent in political entanglement.
