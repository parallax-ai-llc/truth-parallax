Orthodox exegesis reads Simon's leadership in chapter 13 as a continuation of God's providential care for the covenant community. Simon's combination of military competence, political wisdom, and religious devotion exemplifies what Orthodox theology values as the integration of praxis and theoria — active engagement with the world and contemplative orientation toward God.

The fortification of Jerusalem and the purification of Gazara are read as acts of sanctification — the transformation of profane space into sacred territory. This resonates with the Orthodox understanding of liturgical space as a microcosm of the transfigured creation, where the material world is consecrated and offered back to God through prayer, worship, and faithful stewardship.
