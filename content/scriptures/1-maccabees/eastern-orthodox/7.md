Orthodox exegesis reads the conflict with Alcimus and Nicanor in chapter 7 as a cautionary narrative about the corruption of sacred office. Alcimus, though bearing the title of high priest, acts as an agent of foreign power rather than a shepherd of the faithful — a situation the Orthodox tradition addresses through its theology of episcopal responsibility and accountability. The betrayal of the Hasideans who trusted Alcimus illustrates the danger of naively trusting in institutional authority divorced from authentic holiness.

The defeat of Nicanor and the celebration of his downfall represent the vindication of the faithful community. Orthodox liturgical practice, which commemorates military victories as manifestations of divine intervention (as in the feast of the Triumph of Orthodoxy), finds precedent in the establishment of Nicanor's Day as an annual celebration of God's deliverance.
