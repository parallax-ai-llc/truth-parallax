Gnostic readers interpret the conflict with Alcimus in chapter 7 as an allegory of the false teacher who claims spiritual authority while serving the archontic powers. Alcimus represents the psychic religious leader who maintains the outward forms of worship while betraying the pneumatic content — a figure familiar from Gnostic polemics against the institutional Church.

The defeat of Nicanor symbolizes the triumph of authentic gnosis over the military power of the material world. In Gnostic reading, the true battle is always spiritual rather than physical, and external victories serve as symbols of the inner victory achieved when the pneumatic soul overcomes the illusions imposed by the archons and their earthly agents.
