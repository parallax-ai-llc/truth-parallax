Gnostic readers interpret Simon's leadership in chapter 13 as a new phase in the pneumatic community's journey through the material world. The fortification of cities and the expulsion of hostile forces symbolize the strengthening of the spiritual consciousness and the purification of the inner temple from archontic influences.

Simon's combination of military prowess and religious devotion represents, in Gnostic terms, the integration of practical wisdom and spiritual knowledge that characterizes the mature adept. However, Gnostic interpreters would note that even Simon's achievements remain within the material realm and thus fall short of the ultimate liberation that comes only through full gnosis and the soul's return to the Pleroma.
