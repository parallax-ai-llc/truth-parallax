Gnostic interpreters read Jonathan's political maneuvering in chapter 10 as an allegory of the pneumatic soul's navigation through the competing demands of the archontic powers. The rival Seleucid claimants represent different aspects of the Demiurge's authority, each seeking to co-opt the spiritual being for its own purposes. Jonathan's ability to extract concessions from both sides illustrates the Gnostic adept's skill in using the structures of the material world without being captured by them.

The acceptance of the high priesthood is read ambivalently by Gnostic interpreters — as both a necessary accommodation to material reality and a potential trap that binds the spiritual leader to institutional religion. The tension between inner freedom and outward conformity is a central concern of Gnostic spiritual practice.
