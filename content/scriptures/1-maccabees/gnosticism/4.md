The rededication of the Temple in chapter 4 is of particular significance to Gnostic readers, who interpret it as the restoration of gnosis within the human soul after a period of archontic domination. The purification of the sanctuary — the removal of defiled stones and the kindling of new light — symbolizes the inner purification that occurs when the divine spark is liberated from the encrustations of material existence and false belief.

The eight-day celebration of the rededication resonates with Gnostic numerology, in which the number eight (the Ogdoad) represents the realm beyond the seven planetary archons — the fullness of the Pleroma from which the divine spark originally descended. The Temple, rededicated and illuminated, becomes a symbol of the pneumatic self restored to its original glory.
