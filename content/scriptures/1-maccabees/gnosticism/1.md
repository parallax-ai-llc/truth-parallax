Gnostic interpreters read 1 Maccabees 1 as an allegory of the archontic assault on the divine spark within humanity. The persecution of Antiochus IV — his desecration of the Temple, his prohibition of Torah observance, and his imposition of pagan worship — symbolizes the Demiurge's effort to extinguish gnosis and enslave the pneumatic soul within the prison of material existence. The Temple itself represents the inner sanctum of the human spirit, defiled by the forces of ignorance.

The "abomination of desolation" erected on the altar becomes, in Gnostic reading, a symbol of the false worship imposed by the archons — the substitution of material religion for authentic spiritual knowledge. Those who resist, choosing death over compliance, represent the pneumatic elect who refuse to surrender their divine inheritance to the rulers of this world.
