Gnostic readers interpret chapter 5's military campaigns as allegorical descriptions of the pneumatic soul's effort to rescue the scattered fragments of the divine light imprisoned in various regions of the material world. Each endangered Jewish community represents a portion of the divine spark held captive by local archontic powers, and the rescue missions symbolize the Gnostic redeemer's work of gathering and reuniting the dispersed light.

The distinction between successful and unsuccessful campaigns reinforces the Gnostic principle that liberation can only be accomplished through authentic gnosis and authorized spiritual guidance. The failed attack by unauthorized leaders (5:55-62) illustrates the danger of attempting spiritual liberation without genuine knowledge — a warning against the psychic imitation of pneumatic achievement.
