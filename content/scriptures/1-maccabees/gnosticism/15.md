Gnostic readers interpret chapter 15's renewed threats from Antiochus VII as a reminder that the archontic powers never cease their efforts to recapture and dominate the pneumatic soul. The demand for the return of territories symbolizes the archons' attempt to reclaim the spiritual ground that has been liberated through gnosis, and Simon's resistance represents the awakened soul's refusal to surrender its hard-won freedom.

The perpetual nature of the struggle — new enemies arising as old ones are defeated — illustrates the Gnostic understanding that material existence is characterized by endless conflict. Only the complete transcendence of the material world, achieved through full gnosis and the soul's return to the Pleroma, can bring an end to this cycle of struggle.
