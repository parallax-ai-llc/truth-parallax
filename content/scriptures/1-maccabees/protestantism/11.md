Chapter 11 narrates Jonathan's continued involvement in the internecine conflicts of the Seleucid Empire, playing rival claimants against each other to secure Jewish autonomy. Protestant commentators observe the increasing distance from the original religious motivations of the revolt, as political calculation begins to overshadow covenantal faithfulness.

The chapter's detailed account of treaties, battles, and shifting alliances is read by Protestant interpreters as illustrative of the fallen world's political realities. While acknowledging Jonathan's skill as a leader, Protestant readers detect a cautionary trajectory: the movement that began as a defense of Torah observance is gradually becoming a conventional political dynasty.
