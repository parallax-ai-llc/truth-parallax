The rededication of the Temple described in chapter 4 is recognized by Protestant interpreters as the historical origin of the festival of Hanukkah, which Jesus himself observed (John 10:22). While Protestants do not attach sacramental significance to the Temple's purification, they read this chapter as testimony to God's faithfulness in restoring true worship after a period of desecration.

Protestant commentators have drawn typological connections between the restoration of the Temple and the Reformation's recovery of biblical worship from what the Reformers regarded as medieval corruption. The joy of the people at the rededication is seen as analogous to the spiritual renewal that follows whenever God's people return to authentic forms of worship centered on the Word of God.
