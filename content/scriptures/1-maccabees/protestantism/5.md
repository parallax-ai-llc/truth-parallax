Protestant interpreters read chapter 5's account of Judas's campaigns in Gilead, Galilee, and other regions as demonstrating the protective solidarity of the covenant community. The rescue of endangered Jewish populations from hostile neighbors illustrates the biblical principle of mutual care among God's people, a theme that resonates with Protestant ecclesiology.

The military narrative also raises questions about the ethics of preemptive warfare and the defense of religious minorities — topics that Protestant ethicists have debated extensively. The chapter's matter-of-fact reporting style, free from supernatural embellishment, reinforces the Protestant assessment of 1 Maccabees as a reliable historical document even if not canonical Scripture.
