Chapter 7 introduces the conflict between the Maccabees and Alcimus, a Hellenizing high priest appointed by the Seleucid authorities. Protestant readers see in this episode a clear example of corrupted religious leadership — a theme central to Reformation polemic. The willingness of the Hasideans to trust Alcimus initially, only to be betrayed, illustrates the danger of placing confidence in institutional religious authority.

The defeat and death of Nicanor, celebrated as "Nicanor's Day," demonstrates what Protestant interpreters regard as God's vindication of those who trust in divine rather than human power. The narrative reinforces the Protestant conviction that legitimate religious authority derives not from political appointment but from faithfulness to God's word.
