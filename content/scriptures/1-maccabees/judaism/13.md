Jewish readers view Simon's leadership in chapter 13 as marking the achievement of practical Jewish independence — a milestone celebrated in the text and commemorated in Jewish historical memory. Simon's expulsion of foreign garrisons from Jerusalem and Gazara represents the fulfillment of the Maccabean aspiration for a Jewish polity governed by Jewish law.

The chapter's account of Simon's military and diplomatic achievements is read by Zionist historiographers as a precedent for Jewish sovereignty and self-determination. The establishment of a Jewish government in Jerusalem, with authority over taxation, military affairs, and religious practice, provides a historical model for the kind of political independence that modern Zionism sought to restore after centuries of exile and dispersion.
