Muslim readers interpret the murder of Simon in chapter 16 as a cautionary tale about the destructive power of treachery and ambition — sins that the Quran condemns in the strongest terms. The betrayal by Ptolemy, Simon's own son-in-law, illustrates the Quranic warning that enemies may be found among those closest to us (64:14) and that vigilance against internal corruption is as important as defense against external threats.

The survival of John Hyrcanus and the continuation of the Hasmonean line demonstrate the Islamic conviction that God's plan cannot be thwarted by human wickedness. The Quranic assurance that God will preserve his community and its mission, despite the plots of its enemies, finds resonance in this narrative of divine providence overcoming human treachery.
