Islamic interpreters read chapter 15's account of Antiochus VII's threats as a reminder that oppressive powers persistently seek to dominate and exploit communities of faith. Simon's refusal to surrender Jewish territory, grounded in the claim of ancestral right, resonates with Islamic jurisprudence on the defense of Muslim lands against foreign encroachment.

The chapter's depiction of diplomatic resistance and military preparation illustrates the Islamic principle that communities of faith must be prepared to defend their freedom, while always preferring negotiation and peaceful resolution. The Quranic injunction to "prepare against them whatever you are able of power" (8:60) is understood not as an endorsement of aggression but as a mandate for the legitimate defense of religious and communal rights.
