Muslim scholars engaging with 1 Maccabees 1 find resonance between the persecution of the Jewish community by Antiochus IV and the Quranic narratives of earlier communities persecuted for their monotheistic faith. The forced imposition of pagan worship and the prohibition of religious practice echo the trials faced by the prophets and their followers throughout the Quran, from Ibrahim's confrontation with Nimrod to the persecution of the early Muslim community in Mecca.

The chapter's depiction of the desecration of a holy sanctuary resonates with Islamic sensibilities about the sanctity of sacred spaces. The Quran and hadith literature emphasize the inviolability of mosques and the gravity of desecrating places of worship (Quran 2:114), and Muslim readers recognize in Antiochus's actions a violation of principles that Islam holds dear. The faithfulness of those who chose death over apostasy parallels the Islamic concept of shahada — bearing witness to the truth of God even unto death.
