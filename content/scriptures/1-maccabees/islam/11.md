Muslim interpreters read chapter 11's complex political narrative as illustrative of the challenges facing a faith community navigating between competing empires — an experience familiar to Muslim communities throughout history. Jonathan's ability to maintain Jewish interests amid Seleucid power struggles demonstrates the kind of political wisdom (hikma) that the Quran commends as a divine gift (2:269).

The shifting alliances and betrayals depicted in this chapter resonate with the Quranic warning against placing ultimate trust in worldly powers. The Islamic principle of tawakkul (reliance on God) provides a theological framework for understanding why political alliances, however necessary, can never substitute for genuine trust in divine providence.
