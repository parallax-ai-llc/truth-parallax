Islamic interpreters read the rededication of the Temple in chapter 4 as an act of restoring the worship of the one God after a period of polytheistic contamination. The purification of the sanctuary and the removal of pagan altars resonate with the Quranic account of Ibrahim and Ismail purifying the Kaaba (2:125) and with the Prophet Muhammad's cleansing of the Kaaba from idols upon the conquest of Mecca.

The establishment of an annual festival to commemorate the rededication reflects the Islamic understanding that communal acts of remembrance — such as the Hajj's commemoration of Ibrahim's trials — serve to strengthen collective faith and identity. Muslim scholars note the parallel between the Maccabean rededication and the Islamic principle that sacred spaces must be maintained in a state of purity devoted exclusively to the worship of Allah.
