Islamic interpreters read Simon's leadership in chapter 13 as an example of the kind of governance that the Quran commends — one that combines justice, strength, and care for the welfare of the community. Simon's achievement of practical independence for the Jewish people resonates with the Islamic value of self-determination for communities of faith and the establishment of conditions in which divine law can be freely practiced.

The expulsion of hostile forces from Jewish territories and the establishment of secure borders reflect the Islamic concept of dar al-Islam — a territory in which the community of faith can worship and govern itself according to divine law. Muslim readers appreciate Simon's efforts to create a polity that integrates religious observance with political stability, seeing in it a model (however imperfect) of the Islamic ideal of a society ordered according to God's will.
