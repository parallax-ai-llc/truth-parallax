Muslim scholars reading chapter 14's celebration of Simon's reign note the Quranic parallel in descriptions of righteous kingdoms blessed by God with peace and prosperity. The depiction of each person sitting safely under their vine and fig tree echoes the Quranic vision of paradise and the earthly blessings that attend righteous governance.

The formal recognition of Simon's authority by popular consent resonates with the Islamic principle of bay'ah (oath of allegiance), through which legitimate leadership is confirmed by the community. Muslim political theorists have drawn on this and similar texts to argue that authentic governance in the Abrahamic tradition requires both divine authorization and popular acceptance.
