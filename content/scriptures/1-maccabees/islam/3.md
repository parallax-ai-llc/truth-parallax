Muslim scholars reading chapter 3 appreciate Judas Maccabeus's reliance on prayer and trust in God before entering battle, a practice that mirrors the Prophet Muhammad's own example before the battles of Badr and Uhud. Judas's declaration that victory depends not on the size of one's army but on the will of God (3:18-19) closely parallels the Quranic assurance that "How many a small company has overcome a large company by permission of Allah" (2:249).

The chapter's depiction of a faithful minority prevailing against a powerful pagan empire resonates with the Islamic narrative of the early Muslim community's improbable victories against the Quraysh and other opponents. Muslim readers see in the Maccabean experience a confirmation of the Quranic principle that God supports those who fight for justice and monotheism, regardless of their numerical disadvantage.
