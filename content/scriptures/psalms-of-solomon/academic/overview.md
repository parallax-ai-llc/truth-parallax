## Overview

The Academic study of the Psalms of Solomon examines their historical context in the aftermath of Pompey's conquest of Jerusalem in 63 BCE, their linguistic features suggesting Hebrew composition, and their significance for reconstructing Pharisaic or proto-Pharisaic theology. Scholars analyze the collection's messianic ideology, its polemics against Hasmonean rulers, and its place within the broader tradition of Second Temple Jewish psalmody.
