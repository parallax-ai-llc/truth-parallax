---
title: "Psalms of Solomon Section 8"
scripture: "psalms-of-solomon"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Distress and the sound of war my ear has heard; the sound of a trumpet announcing slaughter and destruction.

**2** The sound of a great people as of an exceeding high wind, as a tempest with a mighty fire sweeping through the wilderness.

**3** And I said in my heart, Surely God judges us. A voice I heard moving toward Jerusalem, the holy city.

**4** My loins were broken at what I heard, my knees tottered; my heart was afraid, my bones were dismayed like flax.

**5** I said, They pursue the way of righteousness in their judgments. I considered the judgments of God since the creation of heaven and earth; I held God righteous in His judgments which have been from of old.

**6** God laid bare their sins in the full light of day; all the earth came to know the righteous judgments of God.

**7** In secret places underground were their iniquities in provoking Him to anger; they wrought confusion, son with mother and father with daughter.

**8** They committed adultery, every one with his neighbor's wife. They concluded covenants among themselves with an oath touching these things. They plundered the sanctuary of God as though there was no avenger.
