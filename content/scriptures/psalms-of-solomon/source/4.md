---
title: "Psalms of Solomon Section 4"
scripture: "psalms-of-solomon"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Why sittest thou, O profane man, in the council of the pious, seeing that thy heart is far removed from the Lord, provoking the God of Israel by transgression?

**2** Extravagant in speech, extravagant in outward seeming beyond all men, is he that is severe in condemning the sinners in judgment.

**3** And his hand is first upon the sinner as though he acted in zeal, and yet he is himself guilty of manifold sins and of wantonness.

**4** His eyes are upon every woman without distinction; his tongue is lying when he makes a contract with an oath.

**5** By night and in secret he sins as though unseen, with his eyes he speaks to every woman of evil compacts.

**6** He is swift to enter every house with cheerfulness as though innocent, while behind the door he commits iniquity.

**7** May God remove from the pious the lot of the hypocrite. May the bones of the flatterers be scattered far from those that fear the Lord. In flaming fire may the slanderous tongue be destroyed far from the pious.
