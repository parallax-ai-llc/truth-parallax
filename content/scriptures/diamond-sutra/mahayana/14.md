Section 14, comprising 18 verses, marks a crucial turning point. The Buddha teaches that a bodhisattva should practice patience without any notion of self, person, being, or life-span. The famous story of the Rishi Kshantivadin, dismembered yet feeling no anger because he had transcended the concept of self, illustrates this teaching.

Mahayana interpretation demonstrates the practical application of emptiness teaching. The perfection of patience is possible precisely because there is no fixed self to be offended. The section's instruction to give rise to a mind that does not abide in anything has been called the single most important instruction in Mahayana Buddhism.
