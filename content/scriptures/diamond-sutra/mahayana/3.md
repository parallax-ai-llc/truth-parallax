Section 3's 8 verses present the bodhisattva's great vow: to liberate all beings while knowing that ultimately no beings are liberated. This paradox stands at the heart of Mahayana soteriology. The bodhisattva vows to save numberless beings yet realizes that 'being' and 'liberation' are empty of inherent existence.

Mahayana commentators see this as establishing the inseparability of wisdom and compassion. The bodhisattva who truly understands emptiness engages more fully with the world, freed from self-centered motivation. The paradox is resolved not intellectually but in the lived practice of the bodhisattva path.
