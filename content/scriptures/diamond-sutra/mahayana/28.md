Section 28, "No Attachment to Merit," with its 10 verses, advances the Diamond Sutra's central Mahayana teaching on emptiness and non-attachment. The passage develops the Prajnaparamita insight that authentic practice requires transcending attachment even to Buddhist concepts.

Mahayana commentators read this section as demonstrating the inseparability of wisdom (prajna) and compassion (karuna). The bodhisattva who realizes emptiness is freed to act with spontaneous compassion, unhindered by conceptual fixations.
