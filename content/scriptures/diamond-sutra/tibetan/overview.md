## Overview

The Diamond Sutra is studied as part of the Prajnaparamita curriculum in Tibetan monastic universities. Tibetan scholars approach it through Madhyamaka philosophy using frameworks from Nagarjuna, Chandrakirti, and their Tibetan commentators.

## Philosophical Analysis

Tibetan commentators analyze the paradoxical negations as Madhyamaka teaching on the two truths. The formula "X is not X, therefore it is called X" distinguishes between conventional existence and lack of inherent existence at the ultimate level.

## Integration with Practice

The Diamond Sutra's teachings on non-attachment support the development of prajna, one of the six perfections. The sutra is studied alongside Madhyamaka treatises and integrated into the graduated path leading to full Buddhahood.
