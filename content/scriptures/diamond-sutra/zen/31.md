Zen reads Section 31, "Views Are Not Views," as a direct challenge to conceptual thinking. The passage's deconstruction of Buddhist categories is the diamond blade of prajna cutting through attachment to fixed ideas.

The section's paradoxical formulations function like koans, not puzzles for intellectual solution but barriers forcing the mind beyond habitual conceptual grasping. The Diamond Sutra's teaching here points to satori: sudden, non-conceptual realization that transforms one's relationship to reality.
