## Overview

The Diamond Sutra occupies a uniquely honored position in Zen. According to the Platform Sutra, the Sixth Patriarch Huineng attained awakening upon hearing: "Develop a mind that does not abide in anything." This established the Diamond Sutra as foundational to the Zen lineage.

## Zen Reading

Zen approaches the Diamond Sutra as a living instrument for cutting through delusion. Its paradoxical formulations function like koans, shattering the discursive mind and opening the way to direct realization.

## Practice Implications

The teaching to develop a non-abiding mind is understood as the essence of meditation practice. In zazen, the practitioner neither grasps at thoughts nor pushes them away. The insistence that there is no fixed dharma to attain liberates practitioners from spiritual materialism.
