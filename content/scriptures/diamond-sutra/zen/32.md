The concluding section contains the Diamond Sutra's famous closing verse: "All conditioned phenomena are like a dream, an illusion, a bubble, a shadow, like dew or a flash of lightning; thus we shall perceive them." Zen holds this verse in the highest regard, not as a philosophical statement about unreality but as direct pointing to the nature of experience.

Zen masters use this verse as a touchstone for practice. Seeing phenomena as dreamlike does not mean dismissing them but perceiving their empty, luminous, impermanent nature directly. The verse encapsulates the entire sutra's teaching in a single breath.
