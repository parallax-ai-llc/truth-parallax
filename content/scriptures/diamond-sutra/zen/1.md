Zen finds profound teaching in Section 1's mundane simplicity. The Buddha rises, puts on his robe, takes his bowl, goes to beg for food, eats, washes his feet, and sits down. This ordinary activity, described without embellishment, is itself the teaching. A Zen master might say: "The entire Diamond Sutra is right here."

The section's lack of supernatural elements resonates with Zen's emphasis on finding awakening in everyday activities. The Buddha does not perform miracles; he simply lives with complete presence. This is the non-abiding mind in action.
