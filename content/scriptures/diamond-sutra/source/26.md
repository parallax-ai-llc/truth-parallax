---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 26"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 26
---

**1** "Subhuti, what do you think? May the Tathagata be perceived by the thirty-two marks of a great man?"

**2** Subhuti answered: "As I understand Buddha's meaning, the Tathagata may not be perceived by the thirty-two marks of a great man."

**3** Whereupon Buddha said: "Subhuti, if the Tathagata may be perceived by such marks, any great imperial ruler would be the same as a Tathagata."

**4** Subhuti then said to Buddha: "World-honoured One, as I understand the meaning of Buddha, the Tathagata may not be perceived by the thirty-two marks."

**5** Thereupon the World-honoured One uttered this verse:

**6** "Who sees Me by form, who seeks Me in sound, perverted are his footsteps upon the Way, for he cannot perceive the Tathagata."
