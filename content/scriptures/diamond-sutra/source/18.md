---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 18"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 18
---

**1** "Subhuti, what do you think? Does the Tathagata possess the human eye?"

**2** "He does, World-honoured One."

**3** "Does the Tathagata possess the divine eye?"

**4** "He does, World-honoured One."

**5** "Does the Tathagata possess the gnostic eye?"

**6** "He does, World-honoured One."

**7** "Does the Tathagata possess the eye of transcendent wisdom?"

**8** "He does, World-honoured One."

**9** "Does the Tathagata possess the Buddha eye of omniscience?"

**10** "He does, World-honoured One."

**11** "Subhuti, what do you think? Has the Buddha taught that the sand grains of the Ganges are really sand grains?"

**12** "Yes, World-honoured One, the Tathagata has taught that they are."

**13** "Subhuti, what do you think? If there were as many Ganges rivers as the sand grains of the Ganges and there was a Buddha-land for each sand grain in all those Ganges rivers, would those Buddha-lands be many?"

**14** "Many indeed, World-honoured One!"

**15** Buddha said: "Subhuti, however many living beings there are in all those Buddha-lands, though they have manifold natures and minds, the Tathagata understands them all. Wherefore? Because the Tathagata teaches that all these natures and minds are not really natures and minds; they are merely called natures and minds.

**16** "Subhuti, it is the same concerning the past, the present, and the future: mind is ungraspable."
