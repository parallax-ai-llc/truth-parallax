---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 28"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 28
---

**1** "Subhuti, if a Bodhisattva filled untold millions of worlds with the seven treasures and gave them all in charity, his merit thereby accrued would not equal that of one who, having realised that all things are egoless, attained perfect self-control. And why, Subhuti? Because merit is not to be received by a Bodhisattva."

**2** Subhuti said: "World-honoured One, how can you say that a Bodhisattva is not to receive merit?"

**3** "Subhuti, a Bodhisattva should not hanker after merit on account of his good actions. Therefore it is said: Merit is not to be received."
