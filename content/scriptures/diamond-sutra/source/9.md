---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 9"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1** "Subhuti, what do you think? Does a disciple who has entered the Stream of the Holy Life say within himself: I obtain the fruit of a Stream-entrant?"

**2** Subhuti said: "No, World-honoured One. Wherefore? Because Stream-entrant is merely a name. There is no stream-entering. The disciple who pays no regard to form, sound, odour, taste, touch, or any quality, is called a Stream-entrant."

**3** "Subhuti, what do you think? Does an adept who is subject to only one more rebirth say within himself: I obtain the fruit of a Once-to-be-reborn?" Subhuti said: "No, World-honoured One. Wherefore? Because Once-to-be-reborn is merely a name. There is no passing away nor coming into existence. He is merely called Once-to-be-reborn."

**4** "Subhuti, what do you think? Does a venerable one who will never more be reborn as a mortal say within himself: I obtain the fruit of a Non-returner?" Subhuti said: "No, World-honoured One. Wherefore? Because Non-returner is merely a name. There is really no non-returning. Hence the designation Non-returner."

**5** "Subhuti, what do you think? Does a holy one say within himself: I have obtained Perfective Enlightenment?" Subhuti said: "No, World-honoured One. Wherefore? Because there is no such condition as that called Perfective Enlightenment. World-honoured One, if a holy one of Perfective Enlightenment said to himself 'such am I,' he would necessarily partake of the idea of an ego-entity, a personality, a being, or a separated individuality."

**6** "World-honoured One, when Buddha declares that I excel amongst holy men in the Yoga of perfect quiescence, in dwelling in seclusion, and in freedom from passions, I do not say within myself: I am a holy one of Perfective Enlightenment, free from passions. World-honoured One, if I said within myself: Such am I; you would not declare: Subhuti finds happiness abiding in peace, in seclusion in the midst of the forest. This is because Subhuti abides nowhere: therefore he is called Subhuti, Joyful-Abider-in-Peace, Dweller-in-Seclusion-in-the-Forest."
