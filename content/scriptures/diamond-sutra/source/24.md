---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 24"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 24
---

**1** "Subhuti, if a good man or good woman were to take three thousand galaxies of worlds and grind them to powder, and if those atoms of dust were piled together, do you think that would be a great collection?"

**2** Subhuti replied: "Great, indeed, World-honoured One! Wherefore? If such a collection of atoms of dust were really such, the Buddha would not call it a collection of atoms of dust. And when the Tathagata speaks of atoms of dust, it is not really such, but is merely called atoms of dust. And, World-honoured One, when the Tathagata speaks of three thousand galaxies of worlds, these are not really worlds; they are merely called worlds.

**3** "Wherefore? If worlds were really worlds, that would be a case of a composite unity; but what the Tathagata calls a composite unity is not really such, but it is merely called a composite unity."

**4** "Subhuti, a composite unity is just a conventional way of speaking. But in fundamental nature it is beyond verbal expression. Only common people of dull faculties fasten upon it."
