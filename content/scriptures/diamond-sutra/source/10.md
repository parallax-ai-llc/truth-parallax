---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 10"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Buddha said to Subhuti: "What do you think? In the remote past when the Tathagata was with Dipankara Buddha, did He have any degree of attainment in the Good Law?"

**2** "No, World-honoured One. When the Tathagata was with Dipankara Buddha He had no degree of attainment in the Good Law."

**3** "Subhuti, what do you think? Does a Bodhisattva set in order any Buddha-field?"

**4** "No, World-honoured One. Wherefore? Because setting in order a Buddha-field is not really such; it is merely called setting in order a Buddha-field."

**5** Buddha said: "Therefore, Subhuti, all Bodhisattvas, lesser and great, should develop a pure, lucid mind, not depending upon sound, flavour, touch, odour, or any quality. A Bodhisattva should develop a mind which alights upon nothing whatsoever; and so should he establish it."

**6** "Subhuti, this may be likened to a human frame as large as the mighty Mount Sumeru. What do you think? Would such a body be great?"

**7** Subhuti replied: "Great indeed, World-honoured One. This is because Buddha has explained that no body is called a great body."
