---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 15"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 15
---

**1** "Subhuti, if on the one hand, a good man or a good woman receives, retains, studies, and recites this Discourse, and, on the other hand, is despised, that person who in a previous life committed sins deserving degradation into a lower realm of existence, and who in this present life is thus despised, will thereby have wiped out the sins of a previous existence, and will attain the Consummation of Incomparable Enlightenment.

**2** "Subhuti, I recall the infinitely long past before Dipankara Buddha. There were eighty-four thousand myriads of multimillions of Buddhas, and to all these I made offerings and served them without the least trace of fault.

**3** "Nevertheless, if anyone in the last period, in the last five hundred years, receives, retains, studies, and recites these teachings, the merit so attained eclipses that which I obtained through serving all those Buddhas — a hundred-fold and more, to any extent that figures or any comparisons or similitudes of any kind will fail to represent.

**4** "Subhuti, if I fully detailed the merit gained by good men and good women coming to receive, retain, study, and recite this Discourse in the last period, my hearers would be filled with doubt and might not believe. Subhuti, you should know that the significance of this Discourse is beyond conception; likewise the fruit of its reward is beyond conception."
