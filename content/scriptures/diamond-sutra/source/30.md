---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 30"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 30
---

**1** "Subhuti, if a good man or good woman ground an infinite number of galaxies of worlds to dust, would the resulting minute particles be many?"

**2** Subhuti replied: "Many indeed, World-honoured One! Wherefore? If such particles were really existing, the Buddha would not call them particles of dust. And when the Tathagata speaks of particles of dust, it is not really of particles He speaks, for they are merely called particles of dust.

**3** "World-honoured One, even the three thousand galaxies of worlds which the Tathagata speaks of, are not really worlds; for they are merely called worlds. Wherefore? If the worlds were really there, that would suggest a composite unity. But any composite unity of which the Tathagata speaks is not really such, for it is merely called a composite unity.

**4** "Subhuti, a composite unity is nothing more than a convenient way of speaking."
