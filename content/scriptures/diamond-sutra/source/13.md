---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 13"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 13
---

**1** At that time Subhuti addressed Buddha, saying: "World-honoured One, by what name shall this Discourse be known, and how shall we receive it and retain it?"

**2** Buddha answered: "Subhuti, this Discourse should be known as The Diamond of Transcendent Wisdom — thus should you receive and retain it. Subhuti, what is the reason herein? According to the Buddha-teaching the Perfection of Transcendent Wisdom is not really such. 'Perfection of Transcendent Wisdom' is just the name given to it.

**3** "Subhuti, what do you think? Has the Tathagata a teaching to enunciate?"

**4** Subhuti replied: "The Tathagata has nothing to teach."

**5** "Subhuti, what do you think? Would there be many molecules in the composition of three thousand galaxies of worlds?"

**6** Subhuti said: "Many, indeed, World-honoured One!"

**7** "Subhuti, the Tathagata declares that all these molecules are not really such; they are called molecules. Furthermore, the Tathagata declares that a world is not really a world; it is called a world.

**8** "Subhuti, what do you think? May the Tathagata be perceived by the thirty-two physical peculiarities of an outstanding sage?"

**9** "No, World-honoured One, the Tathagata may not be perceived by them. Wherefore? Because the Tathagata has explained that the thirty-two physical peculiarities of an outstanding sage are not really such; they are called the thirty-two physical peculiarities of an outstanding sage."

**10** "Subhuti, if on the one hand, a good man or a good woman sacrifices as many lives as the sand grains of the Ganges, and on the other hand, anyone receives and retains even only four lines of this Discourse, and teaches and explains them to others, the merit of the latter will be the greater."
