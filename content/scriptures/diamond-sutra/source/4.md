---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 4"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** "Furthermore, Subhuti, in the practice of charity a Bodhisattva should be detached. That is to say, he should practise charity without regard to appearances, without regard to form, without regard to sound, smell, taste, touch, or any quality of any kind."

**2** "Subhuti, thus should the Bodhisattva practise charity without attachment. Wherefore? In such a case his merit is incalculable."

**3** "Subhuti, what do you think? Can you measure all the space extending eastward?" "No, World-honoured One, I cannot."

**4** "Then can you, Subhuti, measure all the space extending southward, westward, northward, or in any other direction, including nadir and zenith?" "No, World-honoured One, I cannot."

**5** "Well, Subhuti, equally incalculable is the merit of the Bodhisattva who practises charity without any attachment to appearances. Subhuti, Bodhisattvas should persevere one-pointedly in this instruction."
