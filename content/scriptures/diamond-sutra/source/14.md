---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 14"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Upon the occasion of hearing this Discourse, Subhuti had an interior realisation of its meaning and was moved to tears. He said: "It is a most precious thing, World-honoured One, that you should deliver this supremely profound Discourse.

**2** "Never have I heard such an exposition since of old my eye of wisdom first opened. World-honoured One, if anyone after hearing this Discourse is not filled with alarm or awe or dread, be it known that such a one is of remarkable achievement.

**3** "World-honoured One, the First Perfection, the Perfection of Patience, is declared by the Tathagata to be no perfection; such is merely a name.

**4** "Wherefore? Because, World-honoured One, when the Prince of Kalinga severed the flesh from my limbs and body, I had at that time no idea of an ego-entity, a personality, a being, or a separated individuality. Wherefore? Because then, when my limbs were being dismembered, had I cherished any of these ideas, feelings of anger and hatred would have been aroused.

**5** "World-honoured One, I remember, in the distant past, during my five hundred previous lives, I was an ascetic practising patience. Even then I had no such arbitrary conceptions as an ego-entity, a personality, a being, or a separated individuality.

**6** "Therefore, Subhuti, Bodhisattvas should leave behind all phenomenal distinctions and awaken the thought of the Consummation of Incomparable Enlightenment by not allowing the mind to depend upon notions evoked by the sensible world — by not allowing the mind to depend upon notions evoked by sounds, odours, flavours, touch-contacts, or any qualities. The mind should be kept independent of any thoughts which arise within it.

**7** "If the mind depends upon anything it has no sure haven. This is why Buddha teaches that the mind of a Bodhisattva should not accept the appearances of things as a basis when exercising charity. Subhuti, as Bodhisattvas practise charity for the welfare of all living beings they should do it in this manner.

**8** "The Tathagata teaches that all phenomenal appearances are not really such. He also teaches that all living beings are not really beings. Subhuti, the Tathagata is He who declares that which is true; He who declares that which is fundamental; He who declares that which is ultimate. He does not declare that which is deceitful, nor that which is monstrous. Subhuti, that Truth to which the Tathagata has attained is neither real nor unreal.

**9** "Subhuti, if a Bodhisattva practises charity with a mind attached to formal notions he is like unto a man groping sightless in the gloom; but a Bodhisattva who practises charity with a mind detached from any formal notions is like unto a man with open eyes in the radiant glory of the morning, to whom all kinds of objects are clearly visible."
