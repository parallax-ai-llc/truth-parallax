---
title: "Diamond Sutra - Chapter 32"
scripture: "diamond-sutra"
chapter: 32
---

**1** "Subhuti, if anyone, by fulfilling immeasurable worlds with the seven treasures, were to make a gift of charity, and if any good man or good woman who has first resolved to attain Enlightenment were to take from this Discourse even so few as four lines, receiving, retaining, studying, and reciting them, and explaining them to others, his merit would be greater.

**2** "In what manner may he explain them to others? By detachment from appearances — abiding in Real Truth. So I tell you:

**3** "Thus shall ye think of all this fleeting world: a star at dawn, a bubble in a stream; a flash of lightning in a summer cloud, a flickering lamp, a phantom, and a dream."

**4** When Buddha finished this Discourse the venerable Subhuti, together with the Bhikshus, Bhikshunis, lay-brothers and sisters, and the whole realms of Gods, Men, and Asuras, filled with joy by His teaching, all faithfully received it and went their ways.
