Section 14, "Beyond Perception," offers material for comparative study across traditions. The method of deconstructing fixed concepts parallels negative theology in Christianity, the neti neti method of the Upanishads, and deconstructive strategies in Western philosophy.

Comparative analysis reveals convergences and differences. While the Diamond Sutra shares with mystical traditions the insight that ultimate reality exceeds concepts, its Buddhist framework of dependent origination and absence of inherent existence distinguishes it from traditions discovering a positive absolute beyond negations.
