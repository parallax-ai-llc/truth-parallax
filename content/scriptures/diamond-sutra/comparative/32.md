The Diamond Sutra's closing verse, comparing conditioned phenomena to dreams, illusions, bubbles, and shadows, invites comparison with similar imagery across traditions. The Hindu concept of maya, Plato's cave allegory, and Zhuangzi's butterfly dream all suggest that ordinary perception does not capture the deepest nature of reality.

However, unlike Hindu maya veiling Brahman or Plato's shadows contrasting with eternal Forms, the Diamond Sutra's dream metaphor points to emptiness of inherent existence in all phenomena. The sutra does not posit a higher reality behind the dream but reveals the dream-like quality as itself the deepest truth.
