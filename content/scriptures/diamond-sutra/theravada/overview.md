## Overview

The Diamond Sutra falls outside the Theravada Pali canon. However, its themes of non-attachment and deconstruction of fixed views resonate with Theravada teachings in the Atthakavagga and Parayanavagga of the Sutta Nipata.

## Points of Connection

Theravada scholars find common ground in the emphasis on non-clinging and the danger of attachment to views (ditthi). The Pali canon's teaching that the Dhamma is like a raft to be abandoned after crossing parallels the Diamond Sutra's insistence that teachings must not become objects of attachment.

## Critical Assessment

While sharing common ground with early Buddhist non-attachment, the Diamond Sutra's Mahayana framework, including the bodhisattva ideal and cosmic merit teachings, goes beyond what the Pali canon authorizes. The sutra remains an important point for Theravada-Mahayana dialogue.
