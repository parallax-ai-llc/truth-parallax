Theravada scholars engaging with Section 31, "Views Are Not Views," find connection with the Pali canon's teachings on non-attachment and the danger of clinging to views. The core teaching that fixed views obstruct liberation echoes passages in the Sutta Nipata's Atthakavagga.

The philosophical method of deconstructing concepts parallels Theravada vipassana practice of examining phenomena to see their impermanent, suffering, and not-self nature. Theravada readers can appreciate the Diamond Sutra's radical non-attachment as an extension of principles in the earliest Buddhist teachings.
