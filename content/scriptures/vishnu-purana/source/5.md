---
title: "Vishnu Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "vishnu-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Parashara said: I shall now describe the story of Prahlada, the great devotee of Lord Vishnu, whose faith could not be shaken by any power on earth.

**2** Prahlada, the son of the demon king Hiranyakashipu, was devoted to Lord Vishnu from his very birth. Even as a child, he chanted the Lord's name and taught his fellow students the path of devotion.

**3** Hiranyakashipu, who hated Vishnu, tried every means to turn his son from his devotion. He had him thrown from cliffs, trampled by elephants, bitten by serpents, and burnt in fire.

**4** But Prahlada emerged unharmed from every ordeal, for the Lord protected him. His faith grew stronger with each trial, and he said: "The Lord is everywhere. He is in this pillar and in this atom."

**5** When Hiranyakashipu struck a pillar in rage, demanding to see Vishnu, the Lord burst forth as Narasimha, the Man-Lion, and slew the demon at twilight, on the threshold, upon His lap — thus fulfilling the conditions of Brahma's boon.

**6** The Lord embraced Prahlada and said: "Ask any boon." Prahlada said: "I desire no boon for myself. But if Thou art pleased with me, free my father from his sins."

**7** The Lord said: "Not only your father but twenty-one generations of your ancestors are purified by your devotion. Such is the power of a true devotee."

**8** Prahlada said: "O Lord, grant me this one boon — that my devotion to Thee may never waver, regardless of any circumstance, in any birth."

**9** The Lord said: "So be it. You are the greatest of My devotees. Your fame shall endure as long as the world endures."

**10** He who hears the story of Prahlada with faith and devotion obtains the Lord's protection. He is freed from all fears and attains the supreme state.
