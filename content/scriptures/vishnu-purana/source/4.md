---
title: "Vishnu Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "vishnu-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Parashara said: I shall now describe the nature of the Lord's divine play and His relationship with His devotees.

**2** The Lord said: "I am the friend of the devoted. I am the protector of the surrendered. I am the guide of the seeker. I am the refuge of the afflicted."

**3** "He who worships Me with exclusive devotion, who thinks of Me at all times, who sees Me in all things — to him I give what he lacks and preserve what he has."

**4** "Even the worst sinner, if he worships Me with undivided devotion, must be considered righteous, for he has rightly resolved."

**5** "He soon becomes a righteous soul and attains lasting peace. O Arjuna, declare it boldly: My devotee never perishes."

**6** "Those who take refuge in Me, though they be of lower birth, women, merchants, even labourers — they too reach the supreme goal."

**7** The devotee who has surrendered to the Lord is like a child in the arms of its mother. The child does not worry about the path; the mother carries it safely to the destination.

**8** The Lord's grace is like the rain that falls equally on all — on the mountain and the valley, on the garden and the wasteland. But only the prepared soil brings forth flowers.

**9** The preparation of the soil is the purification of the heart. Through worship, through service, through study, through meditation — the heart is made ready for the Lord's grace.

**10** He who has received the Lord's grace sees the world with new eyes. He sees the Lord in the sunrise and the sunset, in the flower and the stone, in the saint and the sinner. For him, all is the Lord.
