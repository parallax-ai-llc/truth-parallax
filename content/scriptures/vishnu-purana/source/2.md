---
title: "Vishnu Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "vishnu-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Parashara said: The earth is divided into seven continents and seven oceans. At the centre stands the golden mountain Meru, around which all the celestial bodies revolve.

**2** The continent of Jambudvipa surrounds Mount Meru. It is divided into nine divisions, of which Bharatavarsha is the most sacred, being the land of karma.

**3** In Bharatavarsha alone can beings perform actions that lead to liberation. Even the gods desire to be born here, for here alone the soul can progress toward the ultimate goal.

**4** The Lord Vishnu, out of compassion for the beings of Bharatavarsha, incarnates here again and again to protect dharma and to guide souls toward liberation.

**5** The seven upper worlds are Bhur, Bhuvar, Svar, Mahar, Jana, Tapa, and Satya. Each is more refined and blissful than the one below.

**6** The seven lower worlds are Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Rasatala, Talatala, Mahatala, and Patala. They are the abodes of the demons and the serpent beings.

**7** Beyond all these worlds is Vaikuntha, the eternal abode of Vishnu, which is self-luminous, imperishable, and beyond the reach of material nature.

**8** He who attains Vaikuntha never returns to the cycle of birth and death. He dwells eternally in the presence of the Lord, enjoying infinite bliss.

**9** The universe undergoes cycles of creation and dissolution, each lasting billions of years. But the Lord remains unchanged through all these cycles, the eternal witness of all.

**10** He who understands the structure of the universe and the nature of the Supreme Lord who pervades it is freed from the illusion of separateness and attains the supreme state.
