---
title: "Vishnu Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "vishnu-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Maitreya said: "Master, I have heard from you the whole of the sacred teachings. Now tell me of the creation of the world, and of the nature of the Supreme Lord."

**2** Parashara said: Glory to the unchangeable, holy, eternal, supreme Vishnu, of one universal nature, the mighty over all. Glory to Him who is Hiranyagarbha, Hari, and Shankara, the creator, preserver, and destroyer of the world.

**3** Glory to Vasudeva, the liberation of whose worshippers from the bonds of repeated birth is the aim of the Vedas. Glory to that supreme Vishnu whose nature neither gods nor sages can comprehend.

**4** In the beginning, there was neither existence nor non-existence. There was no atmosphere, no sky beyond. The Supreme Being alone existed, self-luminous, eternal, and infinite.

**5** From that Supreme Being arose Pradhana, the primary unmanifest nature. From Pradhana arose Mahat, the great principle. From Mahat arose Ahankara, the principle of self-consciousness.

**6** From Ahankara arose the five subtle elements, the five gross elements, the ten senses, and the mind. From these the cosmic egg was formed.

**7** Within that egg, the Lord Vishnu entered as the inner self. He is the soul of the universe, the support of all creation, the foundation upon which everything rests.

**8** He who knows Vishnu as the supreme cause, the creator and destroyer of the universe, who is devoid of all imperfections — he attains the highest goal.

**9** The wise describe the Supreme by the names Vishnu, Brahman, Paramatman, and Bhagavan. These are not different beings but different names of the one Supreme.

**10** He is called Vishnu because He pervades all things. He is called Brahman because He is vast. He is called Paramatman because He is the Supreme Self. He is called Bhagavan because He possesses all perfections.
