---
title: "Vishnu Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "vishnu-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Parashara said: I shall now describe the royal dynasties that ruled the earth in accordance with dharma.

**2** From the sun was born the solar dynasty, in which appeared the great kings Ikshvaku, Mandhatri, Harishchandra, Sagara, Bhagiratha, Dilipa, Raghu, Dasharatha, and Rama.

**3** Rama, the ideal king, the embodiment of dharma, ruled Ayodhya for eleven thousand years. In His reign, there was no disease, no sorrow, no injustice.

**4** From the moon was born the lunar dynasty, in which appeared the great kings Pururavas, Nahusha, Yayati, Puru, Bharata, Kuru, Shantanu, and the Pandavas.

**5** The Pandavas — Yudhishthira, Bhima, Arjuna, Nakula, and Sahadeva — upheld dharma against the forces of injustice and won the great war of Kurukshetra with the grace of Lord Krishna.

**6** Krishna, the Supreme Lord Himself, incarnated in the lunar dynasty. He was the charioteer of Arjuna, the speaker of the Bhagavad Gita, the protector of the righteous.

**7** The glory of these dynasties lies not in their power and wealth but in their devotion to dharma. They ruled for the welfare of their subjects, guided by the teachings of the sages.

**8** In the age of Kali, these dynasties have passed away, and kings no longer rule by dharma. But the Lord continues to protect the righteous through His grace and through the teachings of the scriptures.

**9** He who reads the histories of these great dynasties, who remembers their devotion to dharma, who follows their example — he is blessed in this world and the next.

**10** The purpose of these histories is not merely to record the past but to inspire the present. The deeds of the righteous kings are the dharma of all time.
