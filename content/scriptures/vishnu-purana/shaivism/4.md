## Overview

Shaiva readers approach the fourth Ansha's genealogical content as largely historical rather than sectarian material. The dynastic lists record the succession of kings, many of whom were patrons of both Vaishnava and Shaiva traditions. Shaiva interpreters note that many kings in these genealogies were devoted to Shiva.

The Shaiva perspective contextualizes these genealogies within a broader understanding of kingship that includes the Shaiva concept of the king as a representative of Shiva's sovereign power on earth. The ideal of dharmic kingship described in the text is compatible with Shaiva royal theology, which emphasizes the king's role as maintainer of cosmic order in Shiva's name.

Shaiva interpreters also note the Kali Yuga prophecy at the end of this book, reading the decline of dharma as part of Shiva's cosmic cycle of dissolution (samhara) that precedes renewal.

## Key Themes

- **Shaiva Royal Patrons**: Kings in the genealogy who supported Shaiva traditions
- **Kingship as Shiva's Mandate**: The sovereignty of kings as derived from Shiva
- **Historical Pragmatism**: Reading genealogies as historical records rather than sectarian claims
- **Dissolution and Renewal**: The Kali Yuga prophecy as part of Shiva's cosmic cycle
- **Royal Shaivism**: The tradition of Shaiva royal worship alongside Vaishnava devotion

## Key Verses and Passages

### Shaiva Kings

Shaiva interpreters identify various kings in the genealogical lists who were historically associated with Shaiva worship, demonstrating that royal legitimacy was not exclusively tied to Vaishnava devotion.

### The End of the Kali Age

The prophecy of the end of the Kali Yuga is read by Shaivas as pointing to Shiva's destructive function that precedes cosmic renewal rather than specifically to a Vaishnava avatar.

## Commentary Tradition

Shaiva engagement with genealogical material tends to focus on historical and political dimensions rather than theological interpretation, treating the dynastic lists as shared Hindu heritage rather than exclusively Vaishnava property.
