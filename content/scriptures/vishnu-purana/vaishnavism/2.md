## Overview

The second Ansha of the Vishnu Purana presents an elaborate cosmography describing the structure of the earth and the universe. For Vaishnavas, this geographical and cosmological description is not merely informational but reveals the extent of Vishnu's dominion — every continent, ocean, mountain, and celestial realm exists within and is sustained by Vishnu.

The book describes the seven concentric continents (dvipas) surrounded by seven oceans of different liquids, the seven netherworlds (patalas), and the celestial spheres above. For Vaishnava interpreters, this ordered cosmic structure reflects Vishnu's nature as the supreme organizer and sustainer of all reality.

The detailed geographical descriptions, including the central mountain Meru and the various regions of Jambudvipa (the central continent identified with the known world), are understood by Vaishnavas as revealing the sacred geography within which Vishnu's worship and dharmic activity take place.

## Key Themes

- **Vishnu's Cosmic Sovereignty**: The entire universe as Vishnu's domain
- **Sacred Geography**: Physical space as infused with divine presence
- **Cosmic Order**: The structured universe reflecting Vishnu's organizing intelligence
- **Pilgrimage Theology**: The sanctity of particular places within the cosmic framework
- **Celestial Hierarchy**: The ordering of heavenly realms under Vishnu's authority

## Key Verses and Passages

### The Seven Dvipas (2.1-4)

Vaishnava commentators note that the seven-continent cosmography demonstrates the comprehensiveness of Vishnu's creation. Each dvipa has its own forms of worship, all ultimately directed toward the one supreme Vishnu.

### Mount Meru (2.2)

The central cosmic mountain Meru is understood by Vaishnavas as the axis of creation, with Vaikuntha (Vishnu's abode) at the pinnacle of the cosmic hierarchy.

## Commentary Tradition

Vaishnava commentators typically treat the cosmographic material as simultaneously literal and symbolic — describing real cosmic structures while also mapping the spiritual journey from the material world to Vishnu's supreme abode.
