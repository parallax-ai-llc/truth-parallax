## Overview

The Vaishnava tradition regards the Vishnu Purana as one of its most authoritative Puranic texts. For Vaishnavas, this Purana articulates the fundamental theological vision of Vishnu as the Supreme Being — the creator, sustainer, and destroyer of the cosmos who periodically descends in avatara form to restore dharma.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Vishnu as Para Brahman
Vaishnavas read the Vishnu Purana as a revelation of Vishnu's nature as the highest reality (Para Brahman). The text's opening invocation to Vishnu as the cause of creation, preservation, and dissolution is taken as a direct theological statement of Vishnu's ontological supremacy.

### Avatar Theology
The detailed account of Krishna's life in Book 5 is read as demonstrating the full divinity of the avatar — not merely an empowered being but Vishnu himself in human form. The Vishnu Purana's avatar narratives serve as a key scriptural basis for Vaishnava devotional practice.

### Bhakti as the Highest Path
Vaishnavas emphasize the text's passages on devotion (bhakti) as the most accessible and effective means of liberation, superseding jnana (knowledge) and karma (ritual action) as ultimate paths.

## Theological Emphases

- **Para Vasudeva**: Vishnu as the transcendent Supreme Person beyond all material qualities
- **Lakshmi-Narayana**: The eternal relationship between Vishnu and his consort Lakshmi as a model for divine grace
- **Nama-smarana**: The repetition and remembrance of Vishnu's names as a primary spiritual practice
- **Prapatti**: Complete surrender to Vishnu as the means of liberation
