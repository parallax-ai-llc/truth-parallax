## Overview

The Vishnu Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas and among the most important texts in the Puranic corpus. Traditionally attributed to the sage Parashara, it is structured as a dialogue between Parashara and his disciple Maitreya. The text is considered one of the earliest and most systematic of the Puranas, likely reaching its present form between the 1st and 4th centuries CE.

The Vishnu Purana is notable for its relatively concise and well-organized presentation of Vaishnava theology, cosmology, genealogy, and dharma. Unlike many other Puranas that grew through centuries of accretion, the Vishnu Purana maintains a coherent narrative structure centered on the supremacy and all-pervasive nature of Vishnu.

## Structure

The text is divided into six Anshas (books):

1. **Ansha 1 — Creation**: Cosmogony, the nature of Vishnu, the origin of the universe, and the genealogy of the gods
2. **Ansha 2 — Earth and the Cosmos**: Geography of the earth, the structure of the seven continents and oceans, the netherworlds, and celestial realms
3. **Ansha 3 — Dharma and the Vedas**: The Vedic traditions, the institution of castes and stages of life, and the duties of dharma
4. **Ansha 4 — Royal Dynasties**: The solar and lunar dynasties of kings from Manu to the Kali age
5. **Ansha 5 — Life of Krishna**: The life, exploits, and teachings of Krishna as an avatar of Vishnu
6. **Ansha 6 — Dissolution**: The dissolution of the universe, the nature of liberation, and the cycles of cosmic time

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Vishnu Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: A foundational theological text establishing Vishnu's supreme sovereignty over all creation
- **Shaivism**: Acknowledged as an important Purana though reinterpreted to accommodate Shaiva theology
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited by Shankaracharya and later Advaitins for cosmological and philosophical passages
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Central to Madhvacharya's theological system as evidence for Vishnu's supremacy
- **Academic**: A key source for understanding the development of Hindu sectarian theology and Puranic literature

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Vishnu's Supremacy | Vishnu as the ultimate reality pervading all existence |
| Cosmological Cycles | The creation, preservation, and dissolution of the universe in recurring cycles |
| Avatara Doctrine | The descents of Vishnu in various forms to restore cosmic order |
| Dharma | The duties and ethical obligations of individuals within the social and cosmic order |
| Moksha | Liberation from the cycle of rebirth through devotion to and knowledge of Vishnu |
