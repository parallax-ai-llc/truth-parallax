## Overview

A comparative approach to the Vishnu Purana examines its themes, narratives, and theological structures alongside those found in other religious and mythological traditions worldwide. This perspective illuminates both the distinctive contributions of Hindu Puranic thought and the shared patterns of religious imagination across cultures.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Cosmological Parallels
The Vishnu Purana's cyclical cosmology — with its repeating ages (yugas), periodic destructions (pralayas), and rebirths of the universe — offers a striking contrast to the linear cosmologies of Abrahamic traditions. However, parallels exist with Stoic cosmology, Buddhist cosmic cycles, and Mesoamerican time systems.

### Incarnation and Avatara
The avatara doctrine finds comparisons with incarnation theology in Christianity, though significant differences remain. The Christian incarnation is a unique, unrepeatable event, while Vishnu descends repeatedly. Comparisons can also be drawn with bodhisattva theology in Mahayana Buddhism.

### Flood Narratives
The Vishnu Purana's account of Matsya (the fish avatar) saving Manu from a great flood invites comparison with Mesopotamian (Gilgamesh), biblical (Noah), and Greek (Deucalion) flood narratives.

## Theological Emphases

- **Cyclical vs. Linear Time**: Hindu yugas compared with eschatological frameworks elsewhere
- **Divine Accessibility**: The avatar concept compared with theophany and incarnation in other traditions
- **Cosmic Order**: Dharma and rita compared with Ma'at (Egypt), Asha (Zoroastrianism), and Tao
- **Sacred Geography**: Puranic cosmography compared with other sacred spatial systems
