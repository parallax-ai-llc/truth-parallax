## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta, founded by Madhvacharya (1238-1317 CE), regards the Vishnu Purana as one of the most authoritative Puranic texts. For Dvaitins, this Purana provides direct and unambiguous evidence for the supreme independence (svatantrya) of Vishnu and the eternal, real distinction between God, souls, and the material world.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Literal Supremacy of Vishnu
Dvaitins read the Vishnu Purana's declarations of Vishnu's supremacy as literal theological truths, not metaphorical or provisional statements. When the text declares Vishnu to be the cause of all, this is taken as an ontological claim about the real hierarchy of being.

### Pancha-bheda (Five-fold Difference)
The Dvaita reading emphasizes the five eternal distinctions: between God and souls, God and matter, soul and matter, one soul and another, and one material entity and another. These distinctions are permanent and never dissolve, even in liberation.

### Tattvavada (Realism)
Dvaitins insist on the reality of the world described in the Vishnu Purana. The cosmological accounts are not maya or illusion but describe real structures of a real universe created and sustained by a real personal God.

## Theological Emphases

- **Svatantrya**: Vishnu's absolute independence — he depends on nothing else for his existence or actions
- **Bhimakaya**: The supreme strength and power of Vishnu as demonstrated through his avatars
- **Taratamya**: The graded hierarchy of beings, from Brahma and Vayu down to insentient matter
- **Aparoksha Jnana**: Direct, unmediated knowledge of Vishnu as the goal of spiritual practice
- **Eternal Servitude**: The liberated soul remains eternally distinct from and subordinate to Vishnu
