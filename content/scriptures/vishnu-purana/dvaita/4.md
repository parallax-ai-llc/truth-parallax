## Overview

Dvaita Vedanta reads the genealogical records of the fourth Ansha as historical evidence for Vishnu's ongoing governance of the world through righteous kingship. The dynastic lists are taken as substantially historical, demonstrating the real working of Vishnu's providence in human affairs.

For Dvaitins, the genealogies are particularly important because they establish the historical lineages through which Vishnu's key avatars — Rama and Krishna — appeared. The reality of these genealogies supports the reality of the avatars themselves, against any interpretation that would reduce them to myth or allegory.

Madhvacharya's own genealogical work in the Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya draws extensively on Puranic genealogies, including those in the Vishnu Purana. For Dvaita, the historical dimension of divine activity is not secondary to the philosophical but is itself a primary mode of revelation.

## Key Themes

- **Historical Providence**: Vishnu's real governance visible in dynastic succession
- **Avatara Lineages**: The genealogies as establishing the historical context for divine descents
- **Dharmic History**: History as the working out of Vishnu's moral governance
- **Taratamya among Kings**: The hierarchical ranking of kings based on their devotion to Vishnu
- **Scriptural Historicity**: The genealogies as evidence for the historical reliability of Puranic accounts

## Key Verses and Passages

### The Rama Lineage

Dvaita interpreters trace the solar dynasty to Rama with particular care, emphasizing that each link in the genealogical chain is a historical person chosen by Vishnu's providence to prepare the lineage for his avatar.

### The Krishna Lineage

The lunar dynasty genealogy leading to Krishna and the Yadavas is read similarly, with each generation contributing to the divine plan.

## Commentary Tradition

Madhvacharya's Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya provides extensive genealogical analysis that draws on the Vishnu Purana alongside the Mahabharata and other Puranas, establishing a comprehensive Dvaita historical chronology.
