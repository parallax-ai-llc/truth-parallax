## Overview

Advaita Vedanta interprets the Vishnu Purana through the lens of non-dualism, reading the text's theological affirmations about Vishnu as descriptions of nirguna Brahman — the attributeless, non-dual Absolute. For Advaitins, the Purana's devotional framework is a valid but ultimately provisional approach to the highest truth, which transcends all personal conceptions of the divine.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### Vishnu as Saguna Brahman
Advaita interpreters understand the Vishnu of the Purana as saguna Brahman — Brahman with attributes — which serves as a practical focus for worship and meditation. The ultimate reality, however, is nirguna Brahman, beyond all names and forms.

### Cosmology as Vivarta
The Vishnu Purana's elaborate cosmology is read through the doctrine of vivarta — apparent transformation. The universe does not truly emerge from Brahman but appears to do so through the power of maya (cosmic illusion).

### Liberation through Knowledge
While the text emphasizes bhakti, Advaitins interpret the ultimate message as pointing toward jnana (knowledge) — the realization that the individual self (atman) is identical with Brahman.

## Theological Emphases

- **Non-duality**: The apparent multiplicity of the universe is ultimately illusory
- **Adhyasa**: Superimposition of attributes onto the attributeless Brahman
- **Vyavaharika and Paramarthika**: The distinction between conventional and ultimate levels of reality
- **Jivanmukti**: Liberation while still embodied through the knowledge of one's true nature
