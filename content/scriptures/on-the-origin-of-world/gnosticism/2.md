Chapter 2 narrates the emergence of the Demiurge through the interaction of Pistis Sophia with the primordial chaos. Unlike the Apocryphon of John, where Sophia's unauthorized emanation directly produces Yaldabaoth, On the Origin of the World presents a more complex process in which Sophia's light interacts with pre-existing chaotic matter to generate the ignorant creator. For the Gnostic reader, this variation enriches the understanding of how divine light becomes entangled with darkness.

Pistis Sophia's role is ambiguous — she is both the source of the Demiurge's power (since he derives his existence from her light) and the being whose divine authority ultimately limits and judges him. Her rebuke of Yaldabaoth's arrogant self-declaration echoes the corrective voice in the Hypostasis of the Archons and the Apocryphon of John, reinforcing the pan-Sethian tradition that the Demiurge's claim to sole divinity is immediately and authoritatively refuted.

The Gnostic community draws from this chapter both an explanation for the Demiurge's power (he possesses stolen divine light) and an assurance of his ultimate defeat (the source of his power is also the agent of his judgment). Pistis Sophia functions as a bridge between the transcendent Pleroma and the fallen material world, ensuring that the divine plan for the recovery of lost light will ultimately be fulfilled.
