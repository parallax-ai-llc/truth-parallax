The eschatological conclusion of On the Origin of the World provides the Gnostic community with its ultimate hope: the complete destruction of the archontic order and the liberation of all divine light trapped in matter. The text describes a cosmic conflagration in which the material world and its rulers are consumed, and the light particles that have been imprisoned since Sophia's interaction with chaos are finally restored to their proper place in the divine fullness.

This vision of total cosmic destruction and reconstitution differs from the eschatology of mainstream Christianity, which anticipates the renewal and transformation of creation rather than its annihilation. For the Gnostic reader, however, the destruction of the material world is not a tragedy but a liberation — the final dissolution of the prison that has held divine sparks captive. The archons' power is temporary and will end when the divine plan reaches its fulfillment.

The chapter concludes with the assurance that the Gnostic elect — those who have received gnosis and recognized their true divine origin — will participate in this cosmic restoration. The text thus serves as both a warning (the material order is destined for destruction) and a promise (those who possess gnosis will be gathered into the imperishable light). This eschatological framework gives coherence and urgency to the Gnostic path.
