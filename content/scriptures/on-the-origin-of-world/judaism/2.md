The Pistis Sophia of chapter 2 is derived from the Jewish personification of Wisdom (Hokhmah), a figure prominently featured in Proverbs 8, Job 28, Sirach 24, and the Wisdom of Solomon. In these Jewish texts, Wisdom is God's companion in creation, delighting in the inhabited world and calling human beings to righteousness. The Gnostic transformation of this figure into a tragic being whose interaction with chaos produces the ignorant Demiurge represents a radical subversion of the Jewish Wisdom tradition.

Jewish scholars emphasize that the biblical Wisdom figure functions to affirm the goodness and rationality of creation — Wisdom is the principle through which God orders the world. On the Origin of the World inverts this function: Sophia's involvement in cosmogony produces catastrophe rather than order. This inversion is theologically significant because it takes a Jewish tradition designed to celebrate God's creative wisdom and deploys it to explain the origin of cosmic evil — a purpose directly opposed to its original function.
