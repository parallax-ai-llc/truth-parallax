The Pistis Sophia narrative in chapter 2 has been the subject of detailed comparative analysis with parallel accounts in the Apocryphon of John, the Hypostasis of the Archons, and the separate text Pistis Sophia (Askew Codex). Scholars have identified both shared mythological motifs (Sophia's interaction with lower realms, the Demiurge's arrogant self-declaration, the corrective heavenly voice) and significant variations in narrative detail and theological emphasis. These comparisons have been central to attempts to reconstruct the development and diversification of the Sophia myth within Gnostic tradition.

The text's version of the demiurgic myth is distinctive in its emphasis on the process by which the Demiurge acquires and exercises power. The detailed account of Yaldabaoth's organization of his realm — the creation of subordinate archons, the structuring of celestial spheres — provides scholars with important data for reconstructing the Gnostic understanding of cosmic governance. The relationship between these archontic hierarchies and the planetary deities of Greco-Roman religion has been explored by scholars including Michael Williams and April DeConick.
