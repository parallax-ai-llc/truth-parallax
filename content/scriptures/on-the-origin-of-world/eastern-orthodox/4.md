Orthodox eschatology is characterized by the expectation of cosmic transfiguration rather than annihilation. The icon of the Transfiguration — Christ radiant with uncreated light on Mount Tabor — serves as the paradigm for creation's ultimate destiny: the material world will be illuminated and perfected by divine glory, not destroyed. On the Origin of the World's vision of cosmic conflagration and the elimination of the archontic order lacks this transformative hope.

The Orthodox hope for the resurrection of the body further distinguishes its eschatological vision from the text's. The Paschal liturgy proclaims, "Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life." This promise of bodily resurrection and the renewal of all creation stands in complete contrast to a text that envisions the destruction of the material order. For the Orthodox tradition, the last things are not about escape from matter but about matter's final and complete participation in the divine glory — the fulfillment of what the incarnation inaugurated.
