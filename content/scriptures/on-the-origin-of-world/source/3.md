---
title: "On the Origin of the World - Chapter 3"
scripture: "on-the-origin-of-world"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Now when Pistis Sophia desired to cause the thing that had no spirit to be formed into a likeness and to rule over matter and over all her forces, there first appeared from the waters a ruler, lion-like in appearance, androgynous, having great authority within him and not knowing whence he had come into being.

**2** Now when Pistis Sophia saw him moving about in the depth of the waters, she said to him: "Youth, pass through to here," whose equivalent is "Yaldabaoth." Since that day, there appeared the principle of verbal expression, which reached the gods and the angels and mankind.

**3** Now the prime parent, Yaldabaoth, since he possessed great authorities, created heavens for each of his offspring through verbal expression — created them beautiful, as dwelling places — and in each heaven he created great glories, seven times excellent. Thrones and mansions and temples, and also chariots and virgin spirits up to an invisible one and their glories.

**4** Now all this he fashioned after the pattern of those that were before him. And when he saw the creation which surrounds him and the multitude of the angels around him which had come forth from him, he said to them: "I am a jealous God, and there is no other God beside me."

**5** But by announcing this he indicated to the angels who attended him that there exists another God. For if there were no other one, of whom would he be jealous?
