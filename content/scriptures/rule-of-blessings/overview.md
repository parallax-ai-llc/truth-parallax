## Overview

The *Rule of Blessings* (1QSb, also known as 1Q28b) is an appendix to the Community Rule (1QS) discovered in Cave 1 at Qumran. It contains a series of liturgical blessings to be pronounced over various figures in the eschatological community — the faithful members, the high priest, the priests of Zadok, and the Prince of the Congregation (a messianic figure). The text provides crucial evidence for understanding the Qumran community's eschatological expectations and their vision of the ideal community that would exist at the end of days.

The Rule of Blessings is significant for its combination of priestly and royal messianic expectations. The blessings envision a restored Israel led by both a priestly messiah and a royal messiah (the Prince of the Congregation), working together in a divinely ordered community that fulfills the promises of the Hebrew prophets.

## Structure

The five sections correspond to blessings over different figures:

1. **Section 1**: Blessing over the faithful members of the covenant community
2. **Section 2**: Blessing over the high priest (the priestly messiah)
3. **Section 3**: Blessing over the priests of the sons of Zadok
4. **Section 4**: Blessing over the Prince of the Congregation (the royal messiah)
5. **Section 5**: Concluding benedictions and eschatological vision

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Essene**: Rule of Blessings as a document of the community's self-understanding and eschatological hope
- **Judaism**: Witness to the diversity and creativity of Second Temple Jewish thought and practice
- **Protestantism**: Illuminates the Jewish background of the New Testament and early Christian theology
- **Catholicism**: Part of the salvation-historical development that prepared the way for Christ
- **Eastern Orthodox**: Anticipation of the liturgical and mystical realities fulfilled in the Church
- **Academic**: Primary source for understanding Second Temple Jewish religion, literature, and social organization
