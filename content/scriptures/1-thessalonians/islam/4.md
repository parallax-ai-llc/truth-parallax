## Living to Please God and the Coming of the Lord

Islamic interpretation of 1 Thessalonians 4 centers on comparative theology and Qur'anic parallels. The key passages — sanctification and sexual purity (4:3-5), the dead in Christ rise first (4:16), caught up in the clouds (4:17) — have been read through the lens of monotheism (tawhid), prophetic tradition, and ethical convergences, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Muslim scholars of comparative religion gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 1 Thessalonians. The chapter's teaching on living to please god and the coming of the lord has shaped Islamic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Islamic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 1 Thessalonians 4 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
