## Overview

1 Thessalonians holds a central place in Protestant eschatology, particularly in discussions about the rapture and the second coming of Christ. The letter's vivid description of believers being "caught up" to meet the Lord (4:17) has generated extensive debate among premillennialists, amillennialists, and postmillennialists. Luther and Calvin both treated this letter as a model of apostolic pastoral care.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Rapture and the Second Coming
Dispensationalist Protestants find in 4:13-18 a description of a pretribulation rapture distinct from Christ's visible return. Reformed Protestants generally read this passage as describing the single event of Christ's return at the end of the age.

### Sanctification
Protestant interpreters emphasize God's initiative in sanctification (4:3; 5:23) while debating whether entire sanctification is achievable in this life. Wesleyan and Reformed traditions diverge sharply here.

### Comfort in Grief
The pastoral warmth of Paul's words about the dead in Christ (4:13-18) remains a staple of Protestant funeral liturgies and grief ministry.
