## Paul's Ministry Among the Thessalonians

Jewish interpretation of 1 Thessalonians 2 centers on Jewish-Christian relations and Second Temple Judaism. The key passages — gentle as a mother (2:7), working night and day (2:9), word of God at work (2:13) — have been read through the lens of the Jewish roots of the text and its implications for Jewish-Christian dialogue, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Modern Jewish scholars of early Christianity gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of 1 Thessalonians. The chapter's teaching on paul's ministry among the thessalonians has shaped Jewish understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Jewish scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how 1 Thessalonians 2 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
