## Overview

Gnostic interpreters of 1 Thessalonians read the letter through the lens of spiritual awakening and liberation from the material world. The eschatological imagery of the Lord's descent, the archangel's call, and the believers' ascent (4:16-17) was interpreted as describing the soul's liberation from the realm of the archons.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Spiritual Ascent
The description of believers being "caught up in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air" (4:17) was read by Gnostics not as a future physical event but as a symbol of the pneumatic soul's ascent through the celestial spheres, passing beyond the archons who govern the material cosmos.

### Awakening from Sleep
Paul's language about those who "sleep" and the call to be "awake" (5:4-8) resonated with the central Gnostic motif of awakening from ignorance. The spiritual person (*pneumatikos*) is one who has been aroused from the sleep of material existence to recognize the divine spark within.

### The True God Beyond the Demiurge
The call to turn from idols to the "living and true God" (1:9) was reinterpreted by Gnostics as turning from the false gods of the material world — including the demiurge — to the transcendent, unknown God beyond all creation.
