## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of 1 Thessalonians emphasizes the communal and liturgical dimensions of Paul's teaching, reading the letter within the broader framework of theosis (divinization) and the Church's eschatological hope. The prayer that God sanctify believers "wholly — spirit, soul, and body" (5:23) resonates with the Orthodox understanding of salvation as the transformation of the whole person.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Theosis and Sanctification
The call to holiness in 1 Thessalonians is read through the Orthodox lens of theosis — participation in the divine nature. Sanctification is not merely moral improvement but genuine transformation through the energies of the Holy Spirit.

### Liturgical Use
Passages from 1 Thessalonians, especially 4:13-18, feature prominently in Orthodox funeral services and memorial prayers. The liturgical context shapes how the faithful encounter and internalize Paul's words about death and resurrection.

### Communal Life
Orthodox interpretation stresses that Paul addresses the community as a whole, not isolated individuals. The exhortations to mutual encouragement (5:11), respect for leaders (5:12-13), and communal prayer reflect the Orthodox emphasis on the ecclesial nature of Christian existence.
