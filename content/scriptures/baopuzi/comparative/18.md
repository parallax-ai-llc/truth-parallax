Comparative interpretation of Inner Chapter 18, 'On Earth Travel,' examines Ge Hong's teachings alongside parallel traditions worldwide. The practices and principles described in this chapter invite comparison with Western Hermetic and alchemical traditions, Indian rasayana (alchemical medicine), and the broader category of transformative spiritual practices that seek to transcend the limitations of ordinary human existence through systematic cultivation.

Comparative scholars note both striking parallels and significant differences between Ge Hong's approach and analogous traditions elsewhere. The aspiration toward physical immortality through the transmutation of substances finds echoes in the Western Philosopher's Stone tradition and the Indian quest for amrita (the nectar of immortality), while the specific methods, cosmological frameworks, and social contexts differ dramatically.
