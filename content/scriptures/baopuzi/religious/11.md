Religious Taoism receives Inner Chapter 11, 'On Immortal Medicines,' as practical instruction for the cultivation of immortality. Within the framework of organized Taoist practice, this chapter's teachings are understood not as philosophical speculation but as tested methods transmitted through legitimate lineages of masters and disciples. The religious tradition values Ge Hong's text for its detailed, specific guidance — the precision of its instructions reflecting genuine esoteric knowledge rather than theoretical abstraction.

The practices described in this chapter have been incorporated into the broader repertoire of Taoist cultivation across multiple schools and lineages. While later developments in internal alchemy (neidan) would reinterpret much of Ge Hong's external alchemical language in terms of inner energy cultivation, the fundamental principles articulated here continue to inform Taoist spiritual practice.
