## Overview

Religious Taoism receives the Baopuzi as one of the foundational scriptures of the alchemical and Shangqing traditions. Ge Hong's detailed accounts of elixir recipes, meditation techniques, talismanic arts, and ritual procedures constitute a comprehensive manual of Taoist cultivation practice. The text occupies a pivotal position in the history of religious Taoism, bridging the early Celestial Masters tradition and the later flowering of the Shangqing and Lingbao schools.

## Alchemical Practice

The religious tradition values the Baopuzi's detailed treatment of external alchemy (waidan), particularly the gold elixir (jindan) described in chapter 4. While later Taoist tradition would reinterpret alchemical language in terms of internal energy cultivation (neidan), the Baopuzi's descriptions of laboratory procedures, mineral preparations, and ritual protocols preserve the original alchemical tradition in its most systematic early form.

## Ritual Framework

Beyond alchemy, the religious tradition draws on the Baopuzi's extensive treatment of ritual practice: the use of talismans (fu) and registers (lu), methods of communicating with celestial beings, techniques of visualization and meditation, and protocols for creating sacred space. These practices continue to inform Taoist ritual to the present day.
