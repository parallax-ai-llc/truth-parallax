Confucian interpretation of Inner Chapter 8, 'On Expelling Evil,' engages with Ge Hong's distinctive effort to integrate Confucian moral values with Taoist spiritual cultivation. Unlike earlier Taoist thinkers who defined themselves in opposition to Confucianism, Ge Hong consistently argues that Confucian virtues — loyalty, filial piety, benevolence, righteousness — constitute the necessary moral foundation without which technical cultivation practices cannot succeed.

Confucian readers find in this chapter evidence of a synthetic vision that honors both traditions: the Confucian insistence on moral cultivation as the foundation of all self-improvement, and the Taoist aspiration toward transcendence of ordinary human limitations. Whether this synthesis succeeds philosophically remains a matter of debate, but its historical significance as an early attempt at Confucian-Taoist integration is widely recognized.
