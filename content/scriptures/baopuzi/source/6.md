## Wei Zhi (Subtle Purport)

The subtle purport of the Way cannot be conveyed through words alone. It must be transmitted from master to student through direct experience.

The ancient masters did not write down their most important teachings. They passed them orally to those students who had proven their sincerity through years of devoted practice. This was not secrecy for its own sake but recognition that the deepest truths cannot be captured in written form.

The written texts serve as guides and reminders, but they are not the teaching itself. The teaching is the living transmission between master and student.

Consider: A book on swimming describes the strokes and the breathing, but reading the book does not make one a swimmer. One must enter the water. So too with the Way: reading about it does not make one a practitioner. One must practice.

The subtle purport includes the understanding of correspondences: How the macrocosm corresponds to the microcosm. How the movements of the stars correspond to the movements of energy in the body. How the cycle of the seasons corresponds to the cycle of cultivation.

These correspondences are not arbitrary but reflect the fundamental unity of all things. The same patterns repeat at every scale, from the smallest particle to the largest constellation.

He who perceives these correspondences has grasped the subtle purport. He sees the unity behind the diversity, the pattern behind the chaos, the stillness behind the motion. This perception transforms not only his understanding but his entire being.
