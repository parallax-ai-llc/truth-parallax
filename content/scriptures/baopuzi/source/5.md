## Zhi Li (The Ultimate Principle)

The ultimate principle underlying all practice is this: Preserve the One. The One is the unity of all things. It is the original nature, the primordial qi, the undifferentiated wholeness that existed before the separation of yin and yang.

He who can preserve the One can ward off all harm. He who loses the One is vulnerable to every danger. Disease enters when the One is dispersed. Death comes when the One departs entirely.

The method of preserving the One: Concentrate the mind upon the center. Do not allow it to scatter among the ten thousand things. The mind that is concentrated is powerful. The mind that is scattered is weak.

When the mind is concentrated, the vital energy gathers. When the vital energy gathers, the spirit brightens. When the spirit brightens, understanding deepens. When understanding deepens, the Way is perceived.

This is a graduated process, not an instantaneous achievement. The practitioner begins with simple exercises in concentration and gradually advances to deeper states. Each stage prepares for the next.

The greatest obstacle to preserving the One is desire. Desire scatters the mind, disperses the vital energy, and dims the spirit. Therefore the practitioner must reduce his desires -- not eliminate them entirely, which is impossible, but reduce them to the minimum necessary for life.

The second obstacle is doubt. The practitioner who doubts the Way will not practice with sincerity. He who does not practice with sincerity will not achieve results. He who does not achieve results will doubt more. This is a vicious cycle that can only be broken by faith.
