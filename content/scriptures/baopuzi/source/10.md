## Ming Ben (Illuminating the Root)

The root of all cultivation is sincerity. Without sincerity, all practice is empty form. With sincerity, even imperfect practice bears fruit.

The root is also the mind. The mind governs the body as the ruler governs the state. When the mind is ordered, the body is healthy. When the mind is disordered, the body falls ill.

The mind must be illuminated -- that is, it must be brought to a state of clarity in which it can perceive its own workings. Most men are unconscious of their own mental processes. They think without knowing that they think. They desire without knowing that they desire. They fear without knowing that they fear.

The practice of illumination: Sit quietly. Observe the thoughts as they arise. Do not follow them. Do not suppress them. Simply observe. Over time, the mind becomes transparent to itself. It sees its own patterns and habits. This seeing is itself the beginning of transformation.

When the mind sees a harmful pattern -- a tendency toward anger, a habit of grasping, a disposition toward anxiety -- it can begin to release it. Not through force of will, but through simple awareness. What is seen clearly can be released naturally.

The root and the branches: The mind is the root. The body is the branch. Thoughts are the root. Actions are the branches. If you attend to the root, the branches will take care of themselves.

The Master says: Do not chase after the branches. Return always to the root. This is the essence of all cultivation.
