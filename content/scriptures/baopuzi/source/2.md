## Lun Xian (On Immortals)

Some say that immortals do not exist. I say: How do they know? The world is vast and the ages are long. Because a man has not seen a thing with his own eyes, does that prove it does not exist?

The ant does not know the ox. The mayfly does not know the seasons. A creature that lives for one day cannot conceive of a creature that lives for a thousand years. Yet this does not mean the thousand-year creature does not exist.

The ancients record many cases of men who achieved immortality through the practice of the Way. Guangchengzi instructed the Yellow Emperor. Pengzu lived for eight hundred years. These are not idle tales but the testimony of generations.

There are different grades of immortals. The highest ascend to heaven in broad daylight, their bodies transformed. The middle grade wander among the famous mountains and sacred groves. The lowest grade die and shed their bodies, but their spirits endure.

The methods of achieving immortality are various: Some practice breath cultivation. Some refine elixirs. Some observe dietary restrictions. Some perform meritorious deeds. The common thread is the accumulation of spiritual merit through long and consistent practice.

The skeptic says: If immortality is possible, why has no one I know achieved it? I answer: The practice is difficult and demands decades of unwavering commitment. Most men lack the patience and the sincerity. The rarity of achievement does not prove the impossibility of the goal.
