## Xia Lan (A Broad View)

Taking a broad view of the practices and teachings gathered in this work, certain principles emerge as fundamental.

First: The Way is real. It is not a metaphor or a philosophical abstraction. It is a living force that pervades all things and can be directly experienced by those who practice correctly.

Second: Cultivation requires time. There are no shortcuts. The practitioner who expects quick results will be disappointed. The Way unfolds gradually, like the growth of a tree. You cannot see it grow from day to day, but over the years the sapling becomes a great tree.

Third: Moral cultivation is inseparable from spiritual cultivation. The man who seeks spiritual power without moral foundation will come to harm. The accumulation of moral merit -- through acts of kindness, generosity, honesty, and compassion -- is as essential as any breathing exercise or elixir practice.

Fourth: A teacher is necessary. The Way has too many pitfalls for a solo practitioner to navigate safely. Even the most talented student needs guidance from someone who has traveled the path before.

Fifth: The integration of knowledge and practice. Knowledge without practice is sterile. Practice without knowledge is blind. The complete practitioner studies widely and practices diligently.

Sixth: Perseverance. Most practitioners fail not because they lack talent but because they lack perseverance. The Way tests the practitioner's commitment at every turn. Only those who persist through difficulties will reach the goal.
