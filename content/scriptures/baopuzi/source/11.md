## Xian Yao (Elixirs of Immortality)

The elixirs of immortality are many. Each has its own properties, its own method of preparation, and its own effects.

The five mineral elixirs: Cinnabar, realgar, orpiment, sulphur, and magnetite. Each corresponds to one of the five phases and one of the five organs. Cinnabar corresponds to fire and the heart. Magnetite corresponds to water and the kidneys.

The herbal elixirs: Lingzhi mushroom, pine resin, sesame, wolfberry, and ginseng. These are less potent than the mineral elixirs but also less dangerous. They can be consumed over long periods to gradually transform the body.

The dietary elixirs: Certain grains, fruits, and seeds, prepared in specific ways and consumed according to specific schedules. These are the mildest and safest methods, accessible to all practitioners.

The method of grain avoidance: By gradually reducing one's dependence on ordinary food and substituting special preparations, the practitioner lightens his body and sharpens his senses. This practice must be undertaken gradually, over months and years.

The dangers of elixir practice: Improperly prepared elixirs can cause illness or death. This is why a qualified teacher is essential. The student who attempts to prepare elixirs without proper instruction puts his life at risk.

Even properly prepared elixirs will not work without the foundation of moral merit and spiritual cultivation. The elixir is a tool, not a magic charm. It works only in conjunction with a complete program of self-cultivation.
