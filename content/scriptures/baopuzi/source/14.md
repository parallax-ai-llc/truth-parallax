## Qin Qiu (Diligent Seeking)

The Way does not come to those who wait idly. It must be sought diligently, with the whole heart.

The practitioner must be like a man whose hair is on fire -- he cannot rest until the fire is extinguished. He must seek the Way with that same urgency, that same single-mindedness.

The methods of diligent seeking: Rise early. Practice breathing exercises at dawn. Study the texts in the morning. Meditate at midday. Practice physical cultivation in the afternoon. Review the day's progress in the evening.

This schedule is demanding, but the Way is not achieved by those who practice casually. Half-hearted practice produces half-hearted results. Wholehearted practice produces complete results.

The practitioner must also seek out teachers and fellow practitioners. The Way is difficult to travel alone. A community of seekers provides support, encouragement, and correction.

When seeking a teacher, look for these qualities: Has the teacher achieved visible results in his own practice? Does the teacher live simply and without ostentation? Does the teacher demand sincerity rather than payment? Does the teacher speak from experience rather than from books?

The false teacher can be recognized by the opposite qualities: He claims achievements he cannot demonstrate. He lives extravagantly. He demands payment. He speaks from theory rather than from practice.

The Master says: Seek diligently but seek wisely. Do not follow every attractive teaching. Test all claims against your own experience. Trust what works and discard what does not.
