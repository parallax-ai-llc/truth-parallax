## Chang Xuan (Expounding the Mysterious)

The Way of the highest mystery is the root of all creation. It existed before Heaven and Earth. It is silent and void, standing alone without change, reaching everywhere without peril.

Those who study the Way must begin with an open mind. If the mind is cluttered with preconceptions, the Way cannot enter. If the mind is agitated with desires, the Way cannot be perceived.

The Way is not far from man. It is present in every breath, every heartbeat, every thought. Yet most men pass through their entire lives without perceiving it. They are like fish who do not know they are surrounded by water.

The ancients who practiced the Way did not seek it in distant places. They found it within themselves. They cultivated stillness and the Way revealed itself. They practiced emptiness and the Way filled them.

The mysterious is not opposed to the ordinary. It is the depth of the ordinary. The surface of water appears flat and featureless, but beneath it lies a world of infinite complexity. So too with the Way: it appears simple but contains infinite depth.

He who grasps the mysterious can prolong his life, preserve his health, avoid danger, and achieve clarity of mind. He does not seek these things directly -- they are the natural consequences of holding fast to the Way.

The Master Who Embraces Simplicity says: Do not be discouraged by the vastness of the Way. Begin with what is near. Begin with yourself. Begin today.
