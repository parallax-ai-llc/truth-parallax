## Deng She (Climbing and Traversing)

When the practitioner enters the mountains to cultivate, he must know the arts of climbing and traversing. The mountains are home to wild beasts, venomous snakes, and spiritual beings both benevolent and malevolent.

The practitioner must carry protective talismans. He must know the proper incantations for warding off harm. He must understand the signs of the mountain -- which paths are safe, which are dangerous, where fresh water can be found, which plants are edible.

The proper days for entering the mountains: Avoid inauspicious days. Choose days that correspond to the practitioner's own birth chart. Enter when the weather is clear and the omens are favorable.

The arts of traversing include: Walking on difficult terrain. Crossing rivers. Climbing steep slopes. Navigating through dense forest. These are practical skills that every mountain practitioner must possess.

But traversing also has a spiritual dimension. The mountain itself is a living being, with its own qi, its own moods, its own character. The practitioner must learn to read the mountain's energy and move in harmony with it.

When the practitioner finds a suitable site for cultivation, he should examine its feng shui. The site should have clean water, shelter from wind, exposure to sunlight, and a feeling of tranquility. The qi of the site should be clear and vibrant.

The Master says: The mountains are both the school and the home of the practitioner. Learn to live in them with respect and humility, and they will provide everything you need.
