## Dui Su (Responding to Common Objections)

The common objections to the pursuit of the Way are many. I will address them one by one.

Objection: If the Way is real, why do learned scholars reject it? Answer: Because learned scholars study texts and debate theories. The Way is not found in texts or theories but in practice. The scholar who has never practiced cannot know the Way, any more than the man who has never tasted wine can know its flavor.

Objection: If immortality is possible, the world would be overrun with immortals. Answer: Immortality is exceedingly difficult to achieve. For every thousand who attempt it, perhaps one succeeds. The rarity of success explains the rarity of immortals.

Objection: If the Way can prolong life, why did the sages of antiquity die? Answer: Some chose to die rather than continue in a corrupted world. Others had achieved the Way but deliberately concealed their immortality. Still others practiced the Way for other purposes -- wisdom, virtue, governance -- and did not specifically seek longevity.

Objection: The pursuit of immortality is selfish. Answer: The man who achieves the Way benefits all beings. His understanding illuminates the world. His practice demonstrates the truth. His very existence is a proof that human nature can transcend its ordinary limits.

The Master Who Embraces Simplicity says: Do not let the objections of the ignorant deter you from the pursuit of truth. The moth flies toward the flame and the fish snaps at the bait, but the wise man is not guided by the moth or the fish.
