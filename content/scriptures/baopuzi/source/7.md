## Sai Nan (Removing Difficulties)

The practitioner of the Way faces many difficulties. These must be addressed and removed, one by one, or progress is impossible.

The first difficulty: Finding a teacher. True teachers are rare. Many who claim to teach the Way are charlatans. The sincere student must search diligently and test every teacher against the standards of the ancients.

The second difficulty: Obtaining the proper texts. The essential texts of the Way have been scattered, lost, or deliberately hidden. Some exist only in fragmentary copies. The student must gather them patiently over many years.

The third difficulty: Securing the proper materials for practice. The ingredients for the elixir, the proper environment for meditation, the tools for cultivation -- these require resources that many practitioners lack.

The fourth difficulty: Maintaining sincerity over time. The Way requires decades of consistent practice. Most men begin with enthusiasm and abandon the practice within a few years. Only those with iron determination succeed.

The fifth difficulty: Accumulating sufficient merit. The practitioner who has not accumulated merit through good deeds will not succeed in his cultivation, no matter how technically proficient his practice may be.

The sixth difficulty: Avoiding the interference of demons and spirits. As the practitioner advances, he becomes visible to spiritual beings who may attempt to obstruct his progress. He must be armed with talismans and protective methods.

The Master says: Do not be deterred by these difficulties. Each one can be overcome. The journey of a thousand li begins with a single step.
