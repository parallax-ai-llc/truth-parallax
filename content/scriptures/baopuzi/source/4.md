## Jin Dan (The Golden Elixir)

The golden elixir is the supreme method of achieving transcendence. Among all the practices of the Way, none is more powerful than the refinement of the elixir.

The principle of the golden elixir: Gold does not corrode. Cinnabar can be transformed repeatedly without being exhausted. When these substances are refined through the proper methods and ingested, they transform the body from within, replacing the mortal flesh with imperishable substance.

The preparation of the elixir requires: a secluded place, far from human habitation. Pure ingredients, obtained at the proper time. The correct furnace and vessels. Precise knowledge of temperatures and durations. And above all, complete sincerity of heart.

There are nine stages of refinement. Each stage produces an elixir of greater potency. The first transformation requires nine days. The second, more. By the ninth transformation, the elixir is complete.

But the golden elixir is not merely a physical substance. It is also a metaphor for the internal transformation of the practitioner. The furnace is the body. The fire is the breath. The ingredients are the vital essences of the body itself.

The external elixir and the internal elixir correspond to each other. The practitioner who works only with the external substances but neglects the internal cultivation will not succeed. The body and spirit must be refined together.

The pursuit of the golden elixir requires patience measured in years and decades. There are no shortcuts. The practitioner who seeks quick results will achieve nothing.
