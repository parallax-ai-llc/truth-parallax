## Bian Wen (Distinguishing Questions)

A questioner asks: "If the Way is truly effective, why are there so few who achieve it?"

I answer: The Way is like a great mountain. The peak is accessible to all who climb, but most men look up from the base and turn back, declaring the peak unreachable. It is not the mountain that bars them but their own unwillingness to climb.

A questioner asks: "Are the stories of the ancient immortals true?"

I answer: Some are true. Some have been embellished over time. Some are entirely fabricated. The wise student distinguishes between them by examining the sources, comparing the accounts, and testing the methods described.

A questioner asks: "Can the Way be practiced while living in the world, or must one withdraw to the mountains?"

I answer: The Way can be practiced anywhere. The mountains provide solitude and freedom from distraction, which aid the practice. But many of the greatest adepts practiced in cities and courts. The essential requirement is inner stillness, which can be maintained in any environment by one who has cultivated it sufficiently.

A questioner asks: "Is it necessary to have a teacher?"

I answer: A teacher is almost always necessary. The Way has many pitfalls and false paths. Without a guide who has traveled the road before, the student will waste years in fruitless effort or, worse, come to harm.

The Master says: Questions are good. They indicate a mind that is seeking. But eventually, one must stop asking and start practicing.
