## Shi Zhi (Releasing Stagnation)

Stagnation is the enemy of cultivation. When qi is stagnant, the body falls ill. When the mind is stagnant, understanding ceases. When the spirit is stagnant, the Way recedes.

The causes of stagnation: Excess in eating and drinking. Excess in sexual activity. Excess in work. Excess in emotion. Any excess causes the qi to accumulate where it should not and to be deficient where it should not.

The methods of releasing stagnation:

Breathing exercises: Inhale slowly through the nose. Hold the breath. Exhale slowly through the mouth. The inhalation gathers qi; the holding distributes it; the exhalation expels the stale and turbid.

Physical exercises: The body must be moved regularly. Stretching, bending, twisting -- these movements open the channels through which qi flows. A body that is sedentary will become stagnant.

Dietary regulation: Eat moderately. Avoid heavy meats and strong flavors. Favor grains, vegetables, and herbs. Eat at regular times. Do not eat when angry or anxious.

Mental cultivation: Still the mind through meditation. Release worries, resentments, and anxieties. These emotions create blockages in the flow of qi as surely as a dam blocks a river.

The Master says: Stagnation is not a single event but a gradual accumulation. It must be countered by daily practice, not occasional effort. The man who practices every day will never become stagnant. The man who practices only when illness strikes is treating the symptom, not the cause.
