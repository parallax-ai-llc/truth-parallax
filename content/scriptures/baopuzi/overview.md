## Overview

The Baopuzi (抱朴子, "The Master Who Embraces Simplicity") is a major work by Ge Hong (283–343 CE), one of the most important figures in the history of Chinese alchemy and religious Taoism. The text is divided into Inner Chapters (Neipian) and Outer Chapters (Waipian). The twenty Inner Chapters, which constitute the esoteric core of the work, present a systematic treatment of Taoist practices aimed at achieving physical immortality, including external alchemy (waidan), meditation, talismanic arts, and pharmacological cultivation.

## Structure

The twenty Inner Chapters address the theory and practice of immortality cultivation:

1. **Discourse on Immortality** (畅玄): Defense of the reality of immortality against skeptics
2. **On Learning** (论仙): The importance of study and the transmission of secret knowledge
3. **On Grasping the Fundamentals** (对俗): Essential principles of cultivation practice
4. **The Gold Elixir** (金丹): Recipes and methods for the supreme alchemical elixir
5. **The Supreme Purity** (至理): The highest principles underlying all cultivation
6. **On the Subtlety of the Tao** (微旨): The subtle meanings of the Tao as applied to practice
7. **On Ingesting Medicines** (塞难): Herbal and mineral medicines for longevity
8. **On Expelling Evil** (释滞): Methods of protection against malevolent spirits and influences
9. **On the Way of the Master** (道意): The relationship between master and disciple
10. **On Understanding the Interior** (明本): Interior visualization and meditation practices
11. **On Immortal Medicines** (仙药): Pharmacology of immortality substances
12. **On Questions and Answers** (辨问): Dialogues addressing common objections
13. **On Numinous Gems** (极言): Sacred objects and their spiritual properties
14. **On Worldly Affairs** (勤求): The relationship between worldly engagement and spiritual cultivation
15. **On Examining Merits** (杂应): The role of moral merit in achieving immortality
16. **On Yellow and White** (黄白): Alchemical transmutation of metals
17. **On Ascending and Transforming** (登涉): Techniques for spiritual ascent and transformation
18. **On Earth Travel** (地真): Methods of safe travel through dangerous terrain
19. **On Encountering Calamities** (遐览): Navigating spiritual dangers and trials
20. **On Dispelling Confusions** (祛惑): Final resolution of doubts about the Taoist path

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Baopuzi has been interpreted across multiple traditions:

- **Religious Taoism**: A foundational text of the alchemical tradition and the Shangqing school
- **Philosophical Taoism**: Examined for its synthesis of Taoist philosophy with practical cultivation
- **Confucian**: Engaged for Ge Hong's explicit effort to harmonize Taoist and Confucian values
- **Academic**: Studied as a primary source for the history of Chinese science, alchemy, and medicine
- **Comparative**: Analyzed alongside Western alchemy, Indian rasayana, and other transformative traditions

| Merit | The inseparability of moral cultivation from alchemical practice |
| Secrecy | The proper transmission of esoteric knowledge from master to disciple |
| Synthesis | The integration of Confucian ethics with Taoist spiritual practice |
| Pharmacology | The systematic study of minerals, herbs, and substances for cultivation |
