Zen interpretation of Inner Chapter 20, 'On Dispelling Confusions,' looks beyond the elaborate alchemical and technical apparatus to discover a spiritual core that resonates with Zen's own concerns. The title 'Baopuzi' — 'The Master Who Embraces Simplicity' — points toward a vision of cultivation that, at its deepest level, involves a return to the simple, uncarved nature that both Taoism and Zen recognize as the ground of authentic being.

Zen readers approach this chapter with the recognition that all spiritual techniques are ultimately means rather than ends — skillful methods (upaya) that point beyond themselves toward a transformation that cannot be manufactured but only received. The chapter's specific teachings, whatever their technical content, serve the deeper purpose of returning the practitioner to an original simplicity that precedes and transcends all technique.
