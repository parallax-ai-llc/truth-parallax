Academic analysis of Inner Chapter 16, 'On Yellow and White,' examines this portion of the Baopuzi through the lenses of intellectual history, history of science, and religious studies. Scholars situate the chapter's teachings within the broader context of Eastern Jin dynasty thought, tracing Ge Hong's sources in earlier Han-dynasty fangshi (recipe master) traditions and examining his influence on subsequent Taoist developments.

The academic tradition attends to the chapter's empirical dimensions: the observable chemical and pharmacological phenomena described alongside religious and metaphysical claims. Joseph Needham and subsequent historians of Chinese science have identified in passages such as these evidence of genuine proto-scientific experimentation embedded within a religious framework, raising important questions about the relationship between scientific and religious modes of knowledge in pre-modern China.
