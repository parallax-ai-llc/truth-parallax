Philosophical Taoism reads Inner Chapter 15, 'On Examining Merits,' as an exploration of how the philosophical principles established by Laozi and Zhuangzi can be translated into concrete practice. Ge Hong's distinctive philosophical contribution lies in his insistence that the Tao is not merely a concept for contemplation but a reality that can be practically engaged through systematic cultivation. This chapter's treatment of its subject demonstrates the unity of theory and practice that defines Ge Hong's philosophical vision.

Philosophical interpreters examine how this chapter's specific teachings relate to the broader framework of Taoist natural philosophy. Ge Hong's reasoning consistently draws on analogies with observable natural phenomena to support his claims, constructing arguments that blend empirical observation with metaphysical speculation in ways that reveal the distinctive character of Chinese natural philosophy.
