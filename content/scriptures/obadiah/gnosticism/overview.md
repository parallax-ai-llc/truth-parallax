## Overview

Gnostic interpretation of Obadiah engages the Esau-Jacob typology that underlies the Edom oracle. In Gnostic anthropology, humanity is divided into types — pneumatic (spiritual), psychic (soul-oriented), and hylic (material). Esau/Edom, the hairy, earthly, hunter-warrior, naturally maps onto the hylic type, while Jacob/Israel represents the pneumatic seeker of divine knowledge.

## The Twin Archetype

The brother-against-brother dynamic in Obadiah becomes, in Gnostic reading, a cosmic allegory. Edom's mountain fortress represents the false security of material existence — impressive to the senses but ultimately illusory. The oracle's declaration that Edom will be "brought down" despite dwelling among the stars (v. 4) can be read as the inevitable collapse of material power before spiritual reality. The Valentinian division of humanity into three types finds a biblical anchor in the contrasting fates of the twin brothers' descendants.

## The Demiurge's Justice

Obadiah's God — wrathful, retributive, declaring "as you have done, it shall be done to you" — fits the Gnostic portrait of the Demiurge: a deity of strict, mechanical justice rather than transcendent mercy. The concluding declaration that "the kingdom shall be the LORD's" is ambiguous in a Gnostic frame. If this LORD is the Demiurge, the promise is merely a reassertion of archontic control. If a deeper reading is possible, the "kingdom" hints at the true divine sovereignty that transcends the Demiurge's petty territorial disputes between favored and disfavored nations.
