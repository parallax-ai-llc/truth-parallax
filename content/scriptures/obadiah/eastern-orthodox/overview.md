## Overview

Eastern Orthodox interpretation of Obadiah, shaped by the Greek patristic tradition, reads the oracle against Edom as a spiritual lesson about pride, betrayal, and the certainty of divine justice. The Fathers consistently moved beyond the historical circumstances to draw out the universal moral and spiritual significance of Edom's downfall.

## Patristic Heritage

**Cyril of Alexandria** interpreted Obadiah's oracle as addressing not only historical Edom but all peoples and individuals who exalt themselves against God and his purposes. The mountain strongholds of Edom (Petra and its environs) symbolize the false security of those who trust in worldly power rather than divine grace. **Theodoret of Cyrus** emphasized the kinship dimension — Edom's sin was doubly grave because it involved betrayal of a brother nation, violating the fundamental bond of shared ancestry.

## Theological Significance

Orthodox theology reads Obadiah's judgment within the framework of divine pedagogy. Judgment is not vindictive but reveals the spiritual consequences of pride and hardness of heart. The concluding declaration that "the kingdom shall be the LORD's" (v. 21) is understood eschatologically as pointing toward the universal reign of Christ, which the Orthodox Church anticipates in every celebration of the Divine Liturgy. The overthrow of Edom's arrogance prefigures the ultimate triumph of humility — the kenotic pattern of Christ's self-emptying and exaltation that stands at the heart of Orthodox Christology.
