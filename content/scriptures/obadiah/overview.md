## Overview

Obadiah is the shortest book in the Hebrew Bible, consisting of a single chapter of twenty-one verses. The prophet Obadiah ("servant of the LORD") delivers an oracle against Edom, the nation descended from Esau, Jacob's twin brother. The historical backdrop is most likely the Babylonian destruction of Jerusalem in 586 BCE, when the Edomites aided the invaders and exploited Judah's downfall.

## Structure

1. **Judgment on Edom** (vv. 1–9): God will bring down Edom despite their fortified mountain strongholds and alliances
2. **Edom's Crimes Against Judah** (vv. 10–14): A catalogue of Edom's treachery during Jerusalem's fall — standing aloof, looting, cutting off fugitives
3. **The Day of the LORD and Restoration** (vv. 15–21): Cosmic reversal in which Edom and all hostile nations face judgment while Israel is restored, culminating in the declaration that "the kingdom shall be the LORD's"

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Judaism**: Edom became a cipher for Rome and later oppressive empires in rabbinic literature; Obadiah is traditionally identified as an Edomite convert
- **Christianity**: The book illustrates divine justice against those who harm God's people and prefigures the ultimate triumph of Christ's kingdom
- **Islam**: The prophetic theme of divine retribution against arrogant nations mirrors Quranic accounts of destroyed civilizations (*qawm*)
- **Gnosticism**: The sibling rivalry of Jacob and Esau and the judgment of the material-oriented Edom can be read as the conflict between pneumatic and hylic natures

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Divine Justice | God holds nations accountable for their actions, especially against the vulnerable |
| Pride and Downfall | Edom's arrogance in their mountain fortresses leads to their destruction |
| Brotherhood Betrayed | The violation of kinship bonds between Esau's and Jacob's descendants |
| Day of the LORD | Universal judgment that encompasses all nations |
| Kingdom of God | The ultimate establishment of divine sovereignty over all the earth |
