Islamic engagement with Baruch 5 would affirm the chapter's vision of divine restoration while situating it within Islamic eschatological expectations. The transformation of the landscape for the return of God's people resonates with the Quranic descriptions of the Day of Judgment, when the earth will be transformed (Quran 14:48) and the faithful will enter gardens of paradise.

Muslim readers would note that while the specific focus on Jerusalem differs from Islamic eschatology, the broader principle that God fulfills divine promises and restores justice is central to Quranic theology. The chapter's confidence that God's word will be accomplished mirrors the Islamic trust in God's qadr (decree) and the certainty that divine promises, including the promise of resurrection and judgment, will be fulfilled.
