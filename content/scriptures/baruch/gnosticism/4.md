A Gnostic reading of Baruch 4 would identify the personified Jerusalem with Sophia, the divine mother who weeps for her lost children, the pneumatic souls scattered throughout the material world. Sophia's fall, her descent from the Pleroma, is the cosmic event that results in the scattering of divine sparks into matter. Her maternal lament is the ongoing cry of the divine feminine for the return of what has been lost.

The chapter's promise that the children will be gathered and the oppressor punished would be read as the Gnostic eschatological hope: the divine sparks will return to the Pleroma, and the archonic powers that imprisoned them will be destroyed. The Demiurge and his servants, who have oppressed the pneumatic community, will face the judgment of the true God. Sophia's grief will be turned to joy when the last divine spark is recovered and the Pleroma is restored to its original fullness.
