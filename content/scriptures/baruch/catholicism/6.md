Catholic interpretation of Baruch 6 (the Letter of Jeremiah) reads the sustained polemic against idolatry as a catechetical text that reinforces the First Commandment. The letter's detailed arguments against the worship of idols, demonstrating their powerlessness and absurdity, support the Catholic teaching that worship belongs to God alone and that any diversion of worship to creatures or objects constitutes a grave sin.

The Catholic tradition extends the letter's critique beyond ancient idol worship to all forms of idolatry, including the worship of money, power, pleasure, and self. The Catechism (CCC 2112-2114) treats idolatry broadly as 'divinizing what is not God,' and the Letter of Jeremiah provides the scriptural foundation for this teaching. The letter's pastoral urgency, warning the exiles not to be seduced by the impressive appearance of pagan worship, remains relevant in Catholic catechesis on the dangers of spiritual compromise.
