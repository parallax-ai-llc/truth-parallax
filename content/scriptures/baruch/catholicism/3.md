Catholic interpretation of Baruch 3 reads the wisdom poem as a prophetic text pointing toward Christ. The identification of wisdom with the Torah given to Israel anticipates the New Testament identification of Christ as the Wisdom of God incarnate (1 Corinthians 1:24, Colossians 1:15-20). The verse 'She appeared on earth and lived among men' (3:38) has been read by Catholic Fathers, including Irenaeus and Augustine, as a prophecy of the Incarnation.

The chapter's survey of nations seeking wisdom without finding it supports the Catholic teaching on the natural human desire for God (desiderium naturale) articulated in the Catechism (CCC 27-30). Humanity searches for wisdom, but true wisdom comes only as God's gift. The Catholic reading connects this to the theology of grace: wisdom cannot be achieved by human effort alone but must be received from God, who has revealed it definitively in Christ.
