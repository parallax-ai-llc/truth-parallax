Catholic interpretation of Baruch 1 reads the chapter as inspired scripture that establishes the historical and liturgical context for the book's penitential theology. The exiles' response to Baruch's reading, their weeping, fasting, and collection of offerings for the Temple, illustrates the Catholic teaching on the proper response to awareness of sin: contrition, prayer, and concrete acts of reparation.

The chapter's portrayal of the exiles maintaining their spiritual connection with Jerusalem and the Temple resonates with the Catholic understanding of the Church as a universal communion that transcends geographical boundaries. The sending of funds to support Temple worship prefigures the Catholic practice of supporting the Church's universal mission through charitable giving. Catholic exegesis reads the chapter as establishing a model of communal piety that continues in the Church's penitential seasons.
