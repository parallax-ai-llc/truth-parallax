Catholic interpretation of Baruch 5 reads the vision of a glorified Jerusalem as a prophecy of the eschatological Church, the heavenly Jerusalem described in Revelation 21. The instruction to Jerusalem to 'put on the robe of righteousness' and the 'diadem of divine glory' is read as prefiguring the Church's ultimate glorification at the end of time.

The chapter is read in the Catholic Lectionary during the Second Sunday of Advent in Year C, where its message of joyful hope and divine restoration complements the Gospel reading's call to prepare the way of the Lord. Catholic exegesis connects the flattening of mountains and filling of valleys in Baruch 5:7 to the similar imagery in Isaiah 40:4 and Luke 3:5, creating a continuous thread of prophetic hope from the Old Testament through John the Baptist to the coming of Christ.
