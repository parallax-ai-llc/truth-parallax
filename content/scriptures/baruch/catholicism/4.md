Catholic exegesis of Baruch 4 reads the personification of Jerusalem as a mother mourning her children within the Catholic typological framework that sees Jerusalem as a figure of the Church and of the Blessed Virgin Mary. The maternal imagery, with Jerusalem weeping for her exiled children and encouraging them to hope, resonates with the Catholic understanding of the Church as Mater Ecclesia (Mother Church) and Mary as Mother of the faithful.

The chapter's message of consolation, promising that God will restore what has been lost, aligns with Catholic eschatological hope. The Advent liturgy, which prepares for the coming of Christ, draws on this prophetic tradition of waiting and hope. The chapter appears in the Catholic Lectionary during Advent, where its themes of exile, longing, and promised restoration set the stage for the celebration of the Incarnation.
