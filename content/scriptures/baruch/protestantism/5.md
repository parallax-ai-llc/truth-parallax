Baruch 5 concludes the prophetic consolation section with a vision of Jerusalem restored and glorified. The city is told to put on the robe of righteousness and the diadem of divine glory, and to look east and west for the return of her scattered children. The language closely parallels Isaiah 40-66 and Psalm 126.

Protestant readers note the chapter's eschatological vision, which anticipates the New Testament hope of a new creation and a heavenly Jerusalem (Revelation 21). While the specific promises are addressed to historical Jerusalem, the theological pattern of exile followed by glorious restoration becomes a framework for understanding the Christian hope of resurrection and renewal. Protestant interpreters value this chapter as an illustration of the prophetic hope that finds its ultimate fulfillment in Christ.
