Orthodox interpretation of Baruch 3 reads the wisdom poem as a prophetic witness to the Logos. The verse 'She appeared on earth and lived among men' (3:38) is interpreted by the Orthodox Fathers as a prophecy of the Incarnation, anticipating the Prologue of John's Gospel: 'The Word became flesh and dwelt among us' (John 1:14). This christological reading is central to the Orthodox reception of this chapter.

The chapter's teaching that wisdom is God's exclusive gift, not discoverable by human effort, resonates with the Orthodox emphasis on divine revelation as the source of all true knowledge. The apophatic tradition in Orthodox theology, which insists that God is ultimately beyond human comprehension, finds support in the chapter's assertion that no human wisdom can discover the way to God without divine initiative. Wisdom comes as gift, as grace, as the self-revelation of the transcendent God.
