Orthodox exegesis of Baruch 4 reads Jerusalem's maternal lament and consolation within the typological framework that connects Jerusalem, the Theotokos, and the Church. The image of a mother mourning her children while encouraging them to hope in God resonates with Orthodox veneration of the Theotokos as the mother who suffers at the cross yet trusts in the resurrection.

The chapter's movement from lament to hope mirrors the Orthodox liturgical movement from the penitential seasons through Holy Week to the joy of Pascha. Orthodox hymnography, particularly the Lamentations of Holy Saturday (Epitaphios), draws on the tradition of maternal mourning that finds expression in this chapter. The promise of restoration and the return of the scattered children anticipates the Orthodox eschatological hope of universal gathering and the transfiguration of all creation.
