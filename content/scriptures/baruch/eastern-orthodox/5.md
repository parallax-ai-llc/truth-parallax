Orthodox interpretation of Baruch 5 reads the vision of Jerusalem's glorification through the lens of the Orthodox theology of transfiguration and divine light. The command to Jerusalem to clothe herself in God's glory connects to the Orthodox doctrine of theosis, in which the faithful are transformed by participation in the divine energies. The glorified Jerusalem represents the eschatological Church, radiant with uncreated light.

The chapter's imagery of mountains leveled and valleys filled for the return of God's people resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the cosmic scope of salvation. Orthodox theology insists that salvation encompasses not only human souls but all of creation, which will be transfigured in the age to come. The vision of Baruch 5, in which even the landscape is transformed for the sake of God's people, illustrates this comprehensive eschatological hope.
