Orthodox interpretation of Baruch 6 (the Letter of Jeremiah) reads the polemic against idolatry with careful attention to the distinction between idols and icons that is central to Orthodox theology. The Seventh Ecumenical Council (787) established that the veneration of icons is not idolatry, because icons are not worshipped as gods but honored as windows into the divine reality they depict. The Letter of Jeremiah's critique applies to objects falsely believed to possess divine power, not to sacred images that point beyond themselves.

Orthodox exegesis thus reads the Letter as a critique of the fundamental error of idolatry: confusing the creature with the Creator. The letter's sustained demonstration that idols cannot see, hear, speak, or act serves the Orthodox teaching that only the living God deserves worship (latreia), while created images may receive veneration (proskynesis) insofar as they direct the worshipper to God. The letter remains a valuable catechetical text for distinguishing true worship from its counterfeits.
