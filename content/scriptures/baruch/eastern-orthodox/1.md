Orthodox interpretation of Baruch 1 reads the chapter as inspired scripture that models the Church's practice of communal repentance. The exiles' response to Baruch's reading, weeping and fasting together, reflects the Orthodox liturgical experience of Great Lent, when the Church gathers for penitential services and the reading of prophetic texts that call the community to repentance.

The chapter's emphasis on maintaining connection with the Temple and the worshipping community, even from exile, resonates with the Orthodox understanding of the Church as a eucharistic community that transcends geographical boundaries. Orthodox Christians in diaspora find in this chapter a model for maintaining liturgical and spiritual fidelity far from their traditional homelands, a theme of particular significance in the modern Orthodox experience.
