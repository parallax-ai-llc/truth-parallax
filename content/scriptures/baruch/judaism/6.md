Jewish scholarly analysis of Baruch 6 (the Letter of Jeremiah) examines it as a Second Temple Jewish polemic against idolatry that extends the canonical tradition of prophetic critique found in Isaiah 40-44 and Jeremiah 10. The letter's systematic dismantling of claims for idol power reflects the ongoing Jewish concern with maintaining strict monotheism in pagan environments.

The letter provides evidence for the specific challenges faced by Jewish communities in the Hellenistic period, where impressive pagan temples and cult images could exert social and cultural pressure on minority Jewish communities. The rabbinic tradition continued this polemic in the Mishnah (Avodah Zarah), which discusses the proper Jewish response to pagan religious objects and practices. The Letter of Jeremiah thus occupies an important position in the development of Jewish anti-idolatry literature from the prophets through the rabbis.
