Chapter 70 is read in religious Taoism as guidance for the practitioner navigating the challenges of the spiritual path. The chapter's themes of humility, compassion, and the renunciation of aggression are understood as essential virtues for the adept who seeks to harmonize with the Tao. The three treasures mentioned in the text — compassion, frugality, and humility — are venerated in religious Taoist ethics as the foundation of a virtuous life that conduces to spiritual realization.

In the monastic tradition of Quanzhen Taoism, chapter 70 informs the daily discipline of monks and nuns who have renounced worldly life to devote themselves entirely to spiritual cultivation. The chapter's counsel against striving and competition shapes the monastic ideal of a simple, contemplative life in which the practitioner gradually dissolves the barriers between self and Tao through sustained practice of meditation, scripture recitation, and virtuous conduct.
