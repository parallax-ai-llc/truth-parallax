Chapter 31 is revered in religious Taoism as a teaching about the Tao's manifest presence in the natural world and the human body. The Shangqing (Highest Clarity) tradition interprets the chapter's cosmological imagery as descriptions of the celestial realms that the meditating adept can visit through visualization practices. The gods and spirits who inhabit these inner landscapes are understood as emanations of the Tao, and communion with them is a stage on the path to immortality.

In the ritual context of religious Taoism, chapter 31 informs the structure and intention of ceremonies performed by Taoist priests (daoshi). The chapter's themes of non-interference, naturalness, and the power of emptiness shape the priestly ideal of serving as a transparent vessel for the Tao's transformative energy. The priest does not act from personal will but channels the Tao's power for the benefit of the community, healing the sick, purifying sacred spaces, and guiding the dead to their proper resting place.
