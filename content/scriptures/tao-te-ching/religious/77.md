The closing chapters of the Tao Te Ching, including chapter 77, hold special significance in religious Taoism as Lao Tzu's final teachings before his legendary departure through the western pass. Religious practitioners understand these chapters as the sage's most concentrated and essential instructions, distilled from a lifetime of spiritual realization. The chapter's themes are read as a summation of the entire path of Taoist cultivation, from initial awakening to final liberation.

Religious Taoist communities continue to recite and meditate on chapter 77 as part of their living spiritual practice. The text is not treated as a historical artifact but as a perennially fresh revelation whose meaning deepens as the practitioner advances in cultivation. Each reading reveals new layers of significance, and the chapter's words serve as a constant companion on the long journey from ordinary consciousness to the deathless state of union with the Tao.
