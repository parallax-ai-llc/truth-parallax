Religious Taoists receive chapter 5, 'Heaven and Earth Are Impartial,' as a sacred teaching revealed by the deified Lao Tzu for the spiritual liberation of humanity. The chapter's imagery is understood as describing interior states accessible through meditation, breath cultivation, and ritual practice. Where philosophical interpreters see abstract metaphysics, religious practitioners find a map of the spiritual journey from ordinary consciousness to union with the Tao.

In the liturgical tradition of religious Taoism, chapter 5 is chanted as part of daily devotional practice, its words understood to carry transformative power beyond their semantic content. The rhythmic recitation of the text is itself a form of spiritual cultivation, harmonizing the practitioner's breath and intention with the Tao's creative energy. Commentaries from the Celestial Masters tradition and the Quanzhen school interpret the chapter's symbols as references to specific stages of inner alchemical transformation.
