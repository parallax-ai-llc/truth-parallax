---
title: "Tao Te Ching - Chapter 29"
scripture: "tao-te-ching"
chapter: 29
---

**1** If any one should wish to get the kingdom for himself, and to effect this by what he does, I see that he will not succeed. The kingdom is a spirit-like thing, and cannot be got by active doing. He who would so win it destroys it; he who would hold it in his grasp loses it.

**2** The course of things is such that what was in front is now behind; what warmed anon we freezing find. Strength is of weakness oft the spoil; the store in ruins mocks our toil.

**3** Hence the sage puts away excessive effort, extravagance, and easy indulgence.
