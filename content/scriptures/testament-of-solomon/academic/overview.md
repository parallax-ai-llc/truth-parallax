## Overview

The Academic study of the Testament of Solomon examines the text as a primary source for understanding ancient demonology, the social practice of magic and exorcism, and the interaction between Jewish, Christian, and Greco-Roman traditions in late antiquity. Scholars analyze the text's literary structure, its relationship to magical papyri and amulets, the development of the Solomonic legend, and the social settings in which demon-cataloguing and exorcism rituals were practiced.
