## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Testament of Solomon examines the text's demonological system alongside parallel traditions. The Islamic tradition preserves extensive traditions about Sulayman's (Solomon's) authority over the jinn, providing a direct parallel. Mesopotamian exorcism texts offer antecedents for the interrogation-and-binding format. Broader comparative analysis connects the text to Buddhist, Hindu, and Chinese traditions of spirit subjugation.
