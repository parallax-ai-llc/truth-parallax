Madhva reads Yajnavalkya's teaching as affirming God's sovereign call. When Vishnu draws a soul toward liberation, no social obligation takes precedence. The immediacy of renunciation reflects the urgency of devotion — the recognition that human life is precious and should not be wasted on pursuits that do not lead to God.

For Dvaita, sannyasa is not an escape from the world but a total commitment to divine service. The renunciant gives up worldly duties not because they are meaningless but because a higher duty — exclusive devotion to Vishnu — supersedes them.
