## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita school reads the Jabala Upanishad's teaching on renunciation as pointing to the highest form of devotion to Vishnu — the complete dedication of one's life to God's service. Sannyasa is not the negation of the world but the positive commitment to serve the Lord without the distractions of worldly obligations.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, the sannyasi does not renounce because the world is unreal but because exclusive devotion to God requires freedom from competing duties. The Jabala's teaching that renunciation can be taken at any time is read as affirming the primacy of God's call — when the Lord draws a soul toward Himself, no social convention should obstruct the response. Avimukta is the sacred space of God's unfailing presence, available not only in Varanasi but wherever the devotee turns the heart toward Vishnu.
