Madhva reads the sannyasi's prescribed conduct as the life of one completely dedicated to God's service. The lack of possessions is not poverty but freedom — freedom to serve Vishnu without distraction. The equanimity in praise and blame reflects the devotee's focus on God's opinion alone, not on human judgment.

The Dvaita sannyasi's life, while outwardly similar to the Advaita monk's, has a fundamentally different inner orientation: it is directed not toward self-knowledge but toward God-service. Every aspect of the sannyasi's conduct — eating, moving, resting — is an act of worship.
