Madhva's tradition reads the discussion of sacred marks as affirming the importance of both external and internal signs of devotion. While internal devotion is primary, external marks serve as reminders of one's commitment and as testimony to others. The Dvaita sannyasi maintains specific external marks that identify him as a servant of Vishnu.

The teaching that internal marks supersede external ones does not, for Dvaita, eliminate external marks but establishes their proper context: they are meaningful only when they express genuine internal devotion.
