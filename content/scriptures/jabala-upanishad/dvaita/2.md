Madhva reads the sannyasa mantras as solemn vows of dedication to God. The renunciant is not negating the world but consecrating the remaining life to Vishnu's exclusive service. The mantras express the soul's recognition that all prior duties have been fulfilled in the decision to devote everything to God.

The formality of the mantras is significant for Dvaita: renunciation is a real act with real consequences, not merely a change in attitude. The mantras establish a covenant between the soul and God, binding the renunciant to a life of devotion.
