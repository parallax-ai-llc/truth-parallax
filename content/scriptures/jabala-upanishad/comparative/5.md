The tension between external and internal religious marks in Section 5 parallels debates across traditions. The Christian discussion of circumcision of the heart versus the flesh (Romans 2:28-29), the Islamic distinction between external and internal *jihad*, and the Buddhist debate over monastic rules versus direct realization all address the same fundamental question: does spiritual transformation require external signs?

The renunciant's abandonment of social markers (sacred thread, hair tuft) parallels the Buddhist monk's head-shaving, the Christian tonsure, and the Sufi's shedding of worldly garments. In each case, the removal of social markers symbolizes death to the old identity and rebirth into a new spiritual one.
