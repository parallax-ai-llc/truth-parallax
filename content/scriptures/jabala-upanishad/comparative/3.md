The sannyasi's life described in Section 3 — wandering, begging for food, possessing nothing — closely parallels the life of the early Buddhist monk (*bhikkhu*), the Jain ascetic, the early Christian desert father, and the wandering Franciscan friar. The radical simplicity of the renunciant lifestyle appears across traditions as the outward expression of inner freedom.

The emphasis on equanimity in all conditions compares with the Stoic ideal of *apatheia* (freedom from passion), the Buddhist concept of *upekkha* (equanimity), and the Christian mystical concept of indifference (*Gelassenheit* in Meister Eckhart). The instruction to wander without a fixed home resonates with Jesus's saying, "The Son of Man has nowhere to lay his head" (Matthew 8:20).
