The Jabala's teaching on Avimukta — the sacred city where God is always present and where liberation is guaranteed — parallels concepts of sacred space across traditions. Jerusalem as the city of God's dwelling in Judaism, the heavenly Jerusalem of Christian Revelation, Mecca as the house of God in Islam, and Bodh Gaya as the Buddha's place of awakening all express the idea that certain places are charged with special spiritual significance.

The internalization of Avimukta — moving it from an external city to an internal point of meditation — mirrors the Christian mystics' transition from physical pilgrimage to interior journey, the Sufi teaching on the *qalb* (spiritual heart) as the true Ka'ba, and the Buddhist understanding that the Pure Land is the purified mind.
