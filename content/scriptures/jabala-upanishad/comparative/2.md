The ritualization of renunciation through formal vows and mantras parallels ordination and profession ceremonies across traditions. Christian monastic vows (poverty, chastity, obedience), Buddhist ordination ceremonies (*upasampadā*), and Jain renunciation rites (*diksha*) all involve formal verbal declarations that mark the transition from worldly to renunciant life.

The declaration of harmlessness (*abhaya*) in the sannyasa mantras echoes the Buddhist precept of *ahimsa*, the Jain great vow of non-violence, and the Hippocratic oath's principle of "do no harm." The universal impulse to ritualize life-transitions through spoken formulas reflects the human understanding of words as performative — creating new realities through their utterance.
