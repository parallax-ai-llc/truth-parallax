Yajnavalkya's teaching that renunciation should be immediate upon the arising of detachment parallels the urgency found in other renunciant traditions. Jesus's call to his disciples — "Follow me, and let the dead bury their dead" (Matthew 8:22) — expresses the same radical priority of spiritual calling over social obligation. The Buddha's departure from his palace, leaving family and kingdom behind, embodies the same principle.

The tension between social duty and spiritual calling that the Jabala addresses is universal: it appears in the Christian debate over active versus contemplative life, the Islamic discussion of Sufi withdrawal, and the Confucian critique of Buddhist monasticism.
