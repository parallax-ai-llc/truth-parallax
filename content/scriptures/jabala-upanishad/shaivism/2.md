The mantras of sannyasa in Section 2 represent the verbal dissolution of the old identity — the shedding of social roles, ritual obligations, and worldly attachments. Shaiva interpreters read these mantras as the articulation of what has already occurred inwardly: the recognition that one's true identity is Shiva, not the social self with its duties and roles.

The ritualized cutting of the sacred thread and the extinguishing of the domestic fires symbolize the end of the ritual self and the emergence of the free self — consciousness no longer defined by its social functions.
