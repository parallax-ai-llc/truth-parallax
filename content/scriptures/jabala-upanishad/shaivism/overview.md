## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find in the Jabala Upanishad a direct connection to Shiva through the teaching on *Avimukta* — the sacred space that is "never forsaken" by the Lord. Avimukta is traditionally identified with Varanasi (Kashi), the city sacred to Shiva, where liberation is granted to all who die within its boundaries. The Upanishad's teaching on sannyasa resonates with the Shaiva tradition of the wandering ascetic who has renounced all worldly ties in pursuit of Shiva-consciousness.

## Theological Emphases

The Jabala's instruction that the sannyasi should wear no marks except the internal marks of wisdom aligns with the Shaiva emphasis on inner realization over external ritual. Avimukta as a spiritual principle — the place within that is never abandoned by consciousness — mirrors the Shaiva teaching that Shiva dwells eternally in the heart. The renunciant who realizes Avimukta attains liberation because they have recognized the ever-present divine within.
