Avimukta is supremely significant for Shaivism as the sacred space of Shiva's eternal presence. Varanasi — also called Kashi ("the city of light") — is Shiva's city, and the teaching that it is "never forsaken" by the divine means that Shiva's consciousness is eternally present there. Those who die in Varanasi receive liberation directly from Shiva, who whispers the *taraka mantra* (mantra of liberation) in their ears.

The internalization of Avimukta — locating it between the eyebrows — connects the external sacred geography to the inner landscape of meditation. The Shaiva practitioner carries Varanasi within, recognizing that the space of Shiva's presence is not limited to any physical location but is the very nature of consciousness.
