The final section declares that complete renunciation leads to liberation — the recognition of one's identity with the absolute. For Shaiva interpreters, this means the recognition that one's consciousness has always been Shiva. The renunciation described here is not merely the giving up of possessions but the letting go of all limited self-identifications.

The liberated sannyasi described in Section 6 is the Shaiva *jivanmukta* — one who is liberated while still alive, moving through the world without attachment because they have recognized that consciousness is never bound.
