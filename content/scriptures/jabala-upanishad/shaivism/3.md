The conduct described in Section 3 — wandering, possessing nothing, remaining equanimous — embodies the Shaiva ideal of the *avadhuta*, the completely liberated being who has transcended all social conventions. The Shaiva renunciant is not following rules but expressing the natural state of consciousness freed from identification with the body and its needs.

The instruction to maintain equanimity in praise and blame, comfort and discomfort, reflects the Shaiva teaching that liberated consciousness is unaffected by external conditions. The renunciant's wandering mirrors the freedom of consciousness itself — unlimited, unattached, and at home everywhere.
