Section 5's teaching on sacred marks resonates with the Shaiva tradition's ambivalent relationship with external religious symbols. While Shaivism has its own marks (*tripundra*, *rudraksha*), the deeper teaching is that the true mark of the renunciant is the internal recognition of Shiva. The ashes (*vibhuti*) that Shaiva sannyasis wear symbolize the burning away of all worldly attachments in the fire of knowledge.

The Jabala's privileging of internal over external marks aligns with the *avadhuta* tradition in Shaivism, which holds that the truly liberated being is beyond all conventions, including religious ones.
