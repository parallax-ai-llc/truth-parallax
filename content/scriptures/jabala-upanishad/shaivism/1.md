Yajnavalkya's declaration in Section 1 that renunciation should be taken at the moment of genuine detachment resonates with the Shaiva tradition of spontaneous awakening. The recognition of one's true nature as Shiva does not wait for the completion of social duties — it erupts in consciousness like lightning, and the awakened person naturally lets go of all that obscures the infinite.

The Shaiva tradition of the wandering *avadhuta* — the utterly free renunciant who has transcended all conventions — finds its scriptural foundation in this teaching. The Jabala authorizes the radical freedom of the one who has recognized consciousness as unlimited.
