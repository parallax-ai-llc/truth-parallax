Section 1's teaching is foundational for Advaita: Yajnavalkya declares that the moment *vairagya* (dispassion) arises, one should renounce. Shankara cited this passage to justify his own early renunciation and to establish sannyasa as the proper external condition for the pursuit of *brahma-vidya* (knowledge of Brahman). For Advaita, external renunciation supports but does not constitute liberation — it removes the distractions that prevent clear self-inquiry.

The teaching that sannyasa can be taken from any ashrama (stage of life) was revolutionary in its context, freeing spiritual seekers from the rigid life-stage requirements of orthodox Brahmanical law.
