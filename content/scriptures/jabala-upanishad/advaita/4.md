For Advaita, Avimukta represents the self that is never forsaken — the unchanging witness-consciousness that remains present through all states and all circumstances. The identification of Avimukta with the space between the eyebrows points to the *ajna chakra*, the center of intuitive knowledge where the self is most directly recognized.

Shankara, who spent significant time in Varanasi, connected the external sacred geography with the internal teaching: the true Avimukta is not a physical location but the self itself, which can never be lost or abandoned because it is the very basis of all experience. Liberation is the recognition that one has always been in Avimukta — the self was never truly forsaken.
