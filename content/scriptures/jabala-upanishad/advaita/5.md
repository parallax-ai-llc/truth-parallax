For Advaita, Section 5's teaching on sacred marks demonstrates the progressive internalization that characterizes the path to liberation. External marks — the sacred thread, the tuft of hair, specific garments — belong to the realm of social identity and ritual status. The sannyasi who has recognized the self as Brahman no longer needs external symbols to define an identity that has been transcended.

Shankara's tradition, however, maintained certain external marks (ochre robes, staff, water pot) as conventional supports for the renunciant life, while insisting that the essential mark is internal knowledge.
