Section 6 presents the Advaita ideal of liberation: the sannyasi who has renounced all attachments, both external and internal, recognizes the self as Brahman and is freed from the cycle of birth and death. This is *jivanmukti* — liberation while still alive — the hallmark of Advaita soteriology.

For Shankara, the final teaching of the Jabala confirms that sannyasa and self-knowledge together constitute the complete path: renunciation provides the conditions, and knowledge provides the content, of liberation. The two are inseparable.
