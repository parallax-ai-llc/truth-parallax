For Advaita, Section 3's description of the renunciant's conduct provides the practical framework for the life of self-inquiry. Possessing nothing, having no fixed abode, and maintaining equanimity — these conditions free the mind from the distractions that prevent clear recognition of the self as Brahman.

Shankara's monastic order (*dashanami*) derives its codes of conduct from this and related Upanishadic passages. The external simplicity of the sannyasi's life creates the conditions for the internal simplicity of non-dual awareness.
