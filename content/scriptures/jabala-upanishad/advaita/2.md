For Advaita, the sannyasa mantras in Section 2 are the external expression of internal *viveka* (discrimination). The renunciant declares: "I have renounced" — meaning the identification with the body, the social role, and the ritual performer has been dissolved through knowledge. The mantras mark the transition from the life of action (*karma*) to the life of knowledge (*jnana*).

Shankara's sannyasa tradition preserves these mantras as essential elements of the renunciation ceremony, connecting each modern Advaita sannyasi to the Upanishadic source.
