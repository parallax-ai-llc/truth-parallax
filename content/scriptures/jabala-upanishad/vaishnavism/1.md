Section 1 presents Yajnavalkya's foundational declaration on renunciation: the day one truly becomes detached, one should renounce — whether as a student, householder, forest-dweller, or at any time. Vaishnava commentators read this as affirming that when the soul awakens to its relationship with God, no worldly obligation should delay the response. Yajnavalkya's teaching mirrors Krishna's assurance in the Gita (18.66): "Abandon all duties and surrender to Me alone."

The immediacy of renunciation reflects the Vaishnava understanding that divine grace operates on its own schedule. When God calls, the devotee must respond, regardless of social convention or personal convenience.
