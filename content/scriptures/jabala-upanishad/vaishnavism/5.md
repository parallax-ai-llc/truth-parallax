Section 5 discusses the external and internal marks of the renunciant. The Jabala teaches that the true sannyasi may abandon external marks (sacred thread, tuft of hair) and rely only on internal marks — knowledge, devotion, and detachment. Vaishnava commentators read this as affirming that true devotion is an inner disposition, not dependent on external symbols, though some Vaishnava traditions maintain specific external marks (*tilaka*, *tulasi mala*) as expressions of internal devotion.

The teaching that internal marks supersede external ones supports the Vaishnava emphasis on the heart's orientation toward God as the essential criterion of spiritual life.
