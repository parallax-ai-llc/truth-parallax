## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Jabala Upanishad's teaching on renunciation as pointing to the highest form of devotion — complete surrender (*prapatti*) to the Supreme Lord. The Upanishad's declaration that one may renounce at any moment is understood as affirming that when genuine detachment arises through awareness of God, formal stage-of-life requirements become secondary to the soul's urgent call toward the divine.

## Theological Emphases

Ramanuja and later Vaishnava teachers recognized a form of sannyasa that was not mere world-negation but positive dedication to God's service. The Jabala's teaching on *Avimukta* — the sacred place never abandoned by the divine — is read as referring not only to Varanasi but to the heart of the devotee, which God never abandons. The sannyasi's renunciation of ritual fires is reinterpreted as the culmination of a life of offering everything to Vishnu.
