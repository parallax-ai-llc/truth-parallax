Section 3 describes the sannyasi's way of life: wandering without a fixed home, eating only what is offered, possessing nothing, and maintaining equanimity in all circumstances. Vaishnava commentators read this as the life of one who has surrendered everything to God — the renunciant depends entirely on divine providence, trusting that the Lord who sustains all beings will sustain the one who has given up everything for Him.

The sannyasi's radical simplicity mirrors the Vaishnava ideal of total dependence on God (*sharanagati*). Having renounced all worldly supports, the renunciant demonstrates through their life that God alone is sufficient.
