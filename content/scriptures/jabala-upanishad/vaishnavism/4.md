Section 4 teaches about *Avimukta* — literally "the never-forsaken" — which is identified with both the sacred city of Varanasi and the space between the eyebrows where the divine dwells. Vaishnava commentators read Avimukta as the place where Vishnu is always present — the sacred geography of God's unceasing presence, whether understood as an external pilgrimage site or the internal sanctuary of the heart.

The teaching that one who knows Avimukta attains liberation confirms the Vaishnava understanding that consciousness of God's presence is the means to freedom. Whether in Varanasi or in meditation, the devotee who recognizes that the Lord never abandons the heart has found the true sacred city.
