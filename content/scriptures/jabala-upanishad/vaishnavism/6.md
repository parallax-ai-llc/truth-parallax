Section 6 concludes with the teaching that the sannyasi who has truly renounced — externally and internally — attains liberation. Vaishnava commentators read this as the fruit of complete surrender to God. The renunciant who has given up everything — possessions, desires, even the sense of being the doer — enters into the Lord's eternal presence.

The final teaching confirms that renunciation is not an end in itself but a means to the highest goal: eternal communion with Vishnu. The sannyasi's liberation is not the dissolution of the self but its perfection in relationship with God.
