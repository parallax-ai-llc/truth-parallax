Section 5's discussion of sacred marks provides data on the social negotiation of renunciant identity in ancient India. The tension between external and internal marks reflects the broader debate about whether sannyasa is a social institution (requiring visible markers) or a purely interior state. Olivelle argues that this tension was never fully resolved and continues to shape Hindu renunciant traditions.

The abandonment of the sacred thread at sannyasa is particularly significant, as the thread (*upavita*) was the primary marker of Brahmanical identity. Giving it up symbolized a radical step beyond caste and social status.
