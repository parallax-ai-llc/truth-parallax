## Overview

The Jabala Upanishad is a primary source for the history of the sannyasa institution in India. Patrick Olivelle's groundbreaking study of the *Samnyasa Upanishads* identifies the Jabala as one of the earliest texts to provide scriptural legitimation for renunciation as a formal institution with specific mantras, procedures, and codes of conduct.

## Research Themes

Scholars debate the Jabala's dating — estimates range from the 4th century BCE to the early centuries CE. The text's radical claim that sannyasa can be taken from any stage of life was controversial within Brahmanical orthodoxy, which generally required the completion of householder duties before renunciation. The teaching on Avimukta connects the Upanishad to the growing importance of Varanasi as a pilgrimage center and raises questions about the relationship between philosophical renunciation and sacred geography.
