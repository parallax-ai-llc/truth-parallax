Section 2's sannyasa mantras represent some of the earliest liturgical texts for the renunciation ceremony. Olivelle notes their formulaic character — they follow patterns established in Vedic ritual while radically subverting the ritual framework by ending all ritual obligations. The mantras include declarations of harmlessness toward all beings (*abhaya*), which connect the sannyasa tradition to the growing ethic of *ahimsa* (non-violence) in the late Vedic period.

The preservation of exact mantras suggests that the Jabala served as a practical handbook for renunciation ceremonies, not merely a philosophical text.
