The Avimukta teaching in Section 4 provides important evidence for the growing sacred geography of Varanasi in the late Vedic and early post-Vedic period. Diana Eck's study of Varanasi traces the city's identification as a *tirtha* (sacred crossing point) and its association with liberation (*moksha*) through texts like the Jabala.

The dual identification of Avimukta with both an external city and an internal point (between the eyebrows) reflects the characteristic Upanishadic strategy of internalizing sacred geography — transforming physical pilgrimage into meditative practice. This internalization serves both to validate the external sacred site and to transcend dependence on it.
