Section 1's declaration that sannyasa can be taken from any ashrama represents a significant departure from mainstream Dharmashastra literature, which typically prescribed a sequential progression through the four life-stages (student, householder, forest-dweller, renunciant). Olivelle argues that this radical position reflects a period of tension between the householder-oriented orthodoxy and the growing prestige of renunciant movements.

The attribution of this teaching to Yajnavalkya — the Upanishadic sage most associated with the primacy of knowledge over ritual — lends it significant authority within the Vedic tradition.
