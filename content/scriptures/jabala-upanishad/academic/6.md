Section 6's brief conclusion summarizes the Jabala's soteriology: renunciation leads to liberation. Scholars note the text's practical character — it is less concerned with metaphysical speculation than with establishing the legitimacy and procedures of sannyasa as an institution. The final teaching serves as a summation and motivational conclusion: the rigors of renunciation are justified by the supreme goal of liberation.

The Jabala's influence on subsequent Hindu tradition is enormous: it provided the scriptural foundation for the sannyasa orders that have been central to Hindu religious life for over two millennia.
