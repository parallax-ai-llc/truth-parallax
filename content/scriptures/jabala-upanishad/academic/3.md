Section 3's code of conduct for the renunciant provides important ethnographic data about the early sannyasa institution. The prescription of wandering, alms-begging, and possession of only minimal equipment (a water pot, staff, and loincloth) establishes the basic pattern that persists in Hindu renunciant orders to the present day.

Olivelle notes that the Jabala's prescriptions are relatively austere compared to later sannyasa texts, which often elaborate extensive rules and permit more possessions. The emphasis on equanimity in all circumstances reflects Upanishadic philosophical ideals being translated into practical behavioral guidelines.
