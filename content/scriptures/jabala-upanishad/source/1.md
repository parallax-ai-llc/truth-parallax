---
title: "Jabala Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "jabala-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Brihaspati asked Yajnavalkya: "What is the means of attaining Kaivalya, the state of absolute isolation and freedom?"

**2** Yajnavalkya said: "First one should live as a Brahmacharin with the teacher, then become a householder, then a forest-dweller, and then renounce all."

**3** "Or one may renounce from any of these stages directly. Whether one has completed the stages or not, on the very day that dispassion arises, one should renounce."

**4** "The Sannyasin should give up the sacred thread, the tuft of hair, the study of the Vedas, and all external rituals. He should carry only a water-pot, a staff, and a loincloth."

**5** "He should beg his food from all castes without distinction, eating only enough to sustain the body. He should sleep under a tree, in a temple, or on the bank of a river."

**6** "The Paramahamsa, the supreme ascetic, has no external marks. He wanders like a child, like a madman, like a ghost. He may or may not carry the staff. He may or may not wear the loincloth."

**7** "For the Paramahamsa, there is no rule and no prohibition. He has transcended all duties, all distinctions, all conventions. He abides in Brahman alone."

**8** "The Paramahamsa sees the same Self in a clod of earth, in a stone, and in gold. He looks upon a Brahmana and an outcast with the same eye. He is the same in honour and dishonour."
