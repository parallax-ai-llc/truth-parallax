---
title: "Jabala Upanishad - Chapter 5"
scripture: "jabala-upanishad"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Atri asked Yajnavalkya: "How may I know the Self which is without a covering, which is bliss itself?"

**2** Yajnavalkya replied: "The Self is to be known through the teaching 'Not this, not this' — Neti, neti. The Self is that which remains when everything that is 'not the Self' has been negated."

**3** "The Self is not the body, not the senses, not the mind, not the intellect, not the ego. It is not the waking state, not the dream state, not the state of deep sleep."

**4** "When all these are negated, what remains is the Self — pure consciousness, self-luminous, infinite, and blissful. That is the Atman. That is Brahman."

**5** "The Self cannot be known as an object of knowledge, for it is the eternal subject, the knower of all. He who seeks to know the Self must become the Self."

**6** "As a man cannot see his own eye but sees through his eye, so the Self cannot be known as an object but is known as the very consciousness by which all else is known."

**7** "He who has realized the Self knows: 'I am all this. I am Brahman. I am the infinite. I am the eternal.' For him, there is nothing other than the Self."

**8** "This is the highest teaching. He who understands it is freed from all bondage. He attains the supreme state of Brahman."
