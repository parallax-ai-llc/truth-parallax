---
title: "Jabala Upanishad - Chapter 6"
scripture: "jabala-upanishad"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Yajnavalkya said: "Now I shall teach you the order of the Paramahamsas, those who have attained the highest state of renunciation."

**2** "The Paramahamsas are: Samvartaka, Aruni, Svetaketu, Durvasas, Ribhu, Nidagha, Jadabharata, Dattatreya, Raivataka, and others like them."

**3** "These great ones move about freely, without any fixed abode, without any external marks of renunciation. They have gone beyond all social conventions."

**4** "They do not distinguish between pure and impure food. They eat what comes to them, like fire that consumes everything without being tainted."

**5** "They are established in Brahman at all times. Whether they appear to be acting or not acting, they are ever absorbed in the Self."

**6** "For them, there is no birth and no death, no bondage and no liberation, no merit and no sin. They have transcended all dualities."

**7** "He who thus renounces everything, who abides in the Self alone, who has no desire for anything in the three worlds — he is the true Paramahamsa."

**8** "Such a one attains the supreme Brahman. He is never born again. This is the secret teaching of the Jabala Upanishad. He who knows this, he who truly knows this, attains liberation. Om, peace, peace, peace."
