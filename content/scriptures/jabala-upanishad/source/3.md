---
title: "Jabala Upanishad - Chapter 3"
scripture: "jabala-upanishad"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Yajnavalkya said: "The city of Varanasi is the city of Brahman in the body. It is the space between the eyebrows, the junction of the nose and the brow."

**2** "In that city, the Avimukta — the never-forsaken — abides. The Lord Shiva, who never forsakes His devotees, dwells there as the inner light."

**3** "He who knows this Avimukta, who meditates upon it, is never forsaken by the supreme knowledge. He attains liberation."

**4** "When the vital breaths depart from the body, the Lord Shiva teaches the departing soul the mantra of liberation — the Taraka mantra — the mantra that carries one across."

**5** "By that mantra the soul is liberated. This is why Varanasi is called the place of liberation, for the liberation that occurs there is the liberation that occurs in the body through knowledge."

**6** "The true Varanasi is not a place on the map but the state of consciousness in which the Self is realized. He who has realized the Self dwells in Varanasi wherever he may be."

**7** "Thus the Avimukta is the sacred space within, where the Lord forever dwells. He who meditates upon this inner sacred space attains the supreme state."

**8** "This is the secret teaching of the Jabala. He who knows it becomes a Paramahamsa. He attains the supreme Brahman."
