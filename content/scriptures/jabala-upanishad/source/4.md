---
title: "Jabala Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "jabala-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Janaka, the king of Videha, approached Yajnavalkya and asked: "O sage, tell me about the sacred ash, the Bhasma, and its significance."

**2** Yajnavalkya said: "The Sannyasin should smear his body with sacred ash, for the ash represents the imperishable. All that is perishable is reduced to ash, but the ash itself remains."

**3** "The ash symbolizes the truth that all material forms are transient. The body will one day be reduced to ash, but the Self, like the ash, is indestructible."

**4** "By smearing the body with ash, the Sannyasin constantly reminds himself of the impermanence of the body and the permanence of the Self."

**5** "The sacred ash also represents the fire of knowledge that has consumed the fuel of ignorance. The wise man, whose ignorance has been burnt by the fire of knowledge, wears the ash as a sign of his liberation."

**6** "The three lines of ash on the forehead represent the three states of consciousness — waking, dreaming, and deep sleep — that have been transcended by the knower of Brahman."

**7** "They also represent the three gunas — sattva, rajas, and tamas — that have been transcended. He who wears the three lines has gone beyond all qualities."

**8** "Thus the sacred ash is the mark of the renunciant, the symbol of transcendence, the sign of one who has realized the imperishable Self."
