---
title: "Jabala Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "jabala-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Yajnavalkya continued: "The Sannyasin should meditate upon the Self as described in the Upanishads. He should practise the meditation: 'I am Brahman.'"

**2** "He should abandon all attachment to the body, the senses, and the mind. He should know: 'I am not the body, I am not the mind, I am the eternal Self.'"

**3** "The Self is pure consciousness, self-luminous, beyond all attributes, beyond all modifications. It is the witness of all, yet untouched by anything."

**4** "As the sun is not affected by the impurities it shines upon, so the Self is not affected by the impurities of the body and the world."

**5** "The Self is one, without a second. It appears to be many through the power of Maya, as the one sun appears as many when reflected in different bodies of water."

**6** "When the reflection is removed, the sun remains one. When Maya is destroyed by knowledge, the Self is known as one and non-dual."

**7** "The Sannyasin who has realized this truth is free while living. He moves in the world without attachment, without desire, without the sense of doership."

**8** "He is like the sky — all-pervading, untouched, and pure. Though all things exist within the sky, the sky is not affected by them. So the knower of Brahman moves in the world."
