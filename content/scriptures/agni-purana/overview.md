## Overview

The Agni Purana is one of the most encyclopedic of the eighteen Mahapuranas, narrated by Agni (the fire god) to the sage Vasishtha. It functions less as a continuous narrative and more as a vast compendium covering an extraordinarily wide range of subjects — from cosmology and mythology to grammar, medicine, prosody, rhetoric, law, martial arts, and temple architecture.

The Agni Purana is generally dated to the 8th-11th centuries CE, though its encyclopedic character suggests it was compiled from diverse sources over a long period. Its breadth of coverage makes it invaluable for understanding medieval Indian knowledge systems, and it has been described as a medieval Hindu encyclopedia.

## Structure

The text contains approximately 383 chapters that can be grouped into twelve thematic sections:

1. **Section 1 — Avatars of Vishnu**: Accounts of Vishnu's ten major incarnations
2. **Section 2 — Cosmology and Geography**: The structure of the universe, earth, and sacred places
3. **Section 3 — Royal Dynasties**: Genealogies of solar and lunar royal lineages
4. **Section 4 — Ritual and Worship**: Temple construction, image installation, and worship procedures
5. **Section 5 — Grammar and Lexicography**: Sanskrit grammar, word formation, and vocabulary
6. **Section 6 — Prosody and Poetics**: Meters, rhetorical figures, and the theory of rasa
7. **Section 7 — Dharmashastra**: Law, ethics, inheritance, and social duties
8. **Section 8 — Medicine and Science**: Ayurvedic medicine, treatments, and herbal remedies
9. **Section 9 — Military Science**: Warfare, weapons, strategy, and martial arts
10. **Section 10 — Yoga and Meditation**: Yogic practices, mantras, and spiritual disciplines
11. **Section 11 — Astrology and Divination**: Celestial phenomena, omens, and astrological calculations
12. **Section 12 — Liberation and Eschatology**: The nature of moksha and the dissolution of the universe

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Agni Purana holds significance across multiple Hindu traditions:

- **Vaishnavism**: Valued for its detailed accounts of Vishnu's avatars and Vaishnava ritual procedures
- **Shaivism**: Engaged for its sections on Shaiva worship, mantra science, and temple architecture
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Cited for its philosophical sections on the nature of Brahman and the paths to liberation
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: Referenced for its avatar narratives and its systematic treatment of Vaishnava theology
- **Academic**: An indispensable source for studying medieval Indian encyclopedic knowledge and cultural synthesis
