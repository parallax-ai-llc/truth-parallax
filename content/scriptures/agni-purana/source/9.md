---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 9"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the science of karma, the law of action and its fruits, by which the fate of all beings is determined.

**2** Every action, whether of body, speech, or mind, produces its fruit. Good actions produce good results, evil actions produce evil results. This is the eternal law of karma.

**3** The fruits of action may be experienced in this life or in future lives. No action is ever lost; every action bears its fruit in due course.

**4** Actions are of three kinds: Sanchita, the accumulated karma of all past lives; Prarabdha, the portion of past karma that has begun to bear fruit in this life; and Kriyamana, the karma being created in this present life.

**5** Sanchita karma can be destroyed by the knowledge of the Self. Prarabdha karma must be experienced; even the knower of Brahman must undergo it until the body falls. Kriyamana karma is neutralized by performing all actions as an offering to the Lord.

**6** The soul transmigrates from body to body, carrying with it the subtle impressions of its past actions. According to the nature of its accumulated karma, it is born in higher or lower forms of life.

**7** The soul that has accumulated merit is born in the worlds of the gods, or as a human being in favourable circumstances. The soul that has accumulated sin is born in the lower worlds, or as an animal, or in unfavourable circumstances.

**8** But all these births, whether high or low, are within the realm of samsara, the cycle of birth and death. True liberation comes only through the knowledge of the Self.

**9** He who performs all actions without attachment, offering the fruits of his actions to the Supreme Lord, is not bound by karma, even though he engages in action.

**10** The wise man acts in the world without attachment, like a lotus leaf that remains untouched by the water upon which it floats. Such a one attains liberation even while living in the body.
