---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 6"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the science of Yoga, by which the seeker may control the mind and attain union with the Supreme Self.

**2** Yoga is the cessation of the modifications of the mind. When the mind is still, the Self shines forth in its own light, pure and unchanging.

**3** The eight limbs of Yoga are: Yama, restraint; Niyama, observance; Asana, posture; Pranayama, breath control; Pratyahara, withdrawal of the senses; Dharana, concentration; Dhyana, meditation; and Samadhi, absorption.

**4** By practising Yama, the seeker cultivates non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness. These are the foundations of spiritual life.

**5** By practising Niyama, the seeker cultivates purity, contentment, austerity, study of the scriptures, and surrender to the Supreme Lord.

**6** Asana is the firm and comfortable posture in which the seeker sits for meditation. The body should be held steady, the spine erect, the mind calm.

**7** Pranayama is the regulation of the breath. By controlling the breath, the seeker gains control over the vital forces and thereby over the mind.

**8** Pratyahara is the withdrawal of the senses from their objects, like a tortoise drawing its limbs within its shell. When the senses are withdrawn, the mind becomes fit for concentration.

**9** Dharana is the fixing of the mind upon a single point. Dhyana is the continuous flow of awareness toward that point. Samadhi is the state in which the distinction between the meditator, the act of meditation, and the object of meditation dissolves.

**10** In Samadhi, the seeker directly perceives the Supreme Self, which is pure consciousness, infinite bliss, and eternal freedom. This is the goal of Yoga, this is the goal of all spiritual practice.
