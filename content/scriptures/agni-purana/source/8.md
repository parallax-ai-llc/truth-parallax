---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 8"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 8
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the science of dharma, the eternal law that sustains all creation.

**2** Dharma is that which upholds, sustains, and supports the world. It is the moral order of the universe, the law of righteousness, the foundation of all social and spiritual life.

**3** The ten characteristics of dharma are: steadfastness, forgiveness, self-control, non-stealing, purity, restraint of the senses, wisdom, learning, truthfulness, and freedom from anger.

**4** He who practises these ten virtues attains the highest good. He who neglects them falls into suffering and darkness.

**5** The duties common to all men are: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, purity, and restraint of the senses. These are the universal duties, applicable to all regardless of their station in life.

**6** The specific duties vary according to one's order of life and one's station in society. But the universal duties are paramount and must never be abandoned.

**7** Non-violence is the highest dharma. He who practises non-violence in thought, word, and deed attains the highest merit. He becomes dear to all beings.

**8** Truthfulness is the foundation of all dharma. The world rests upon truth. The gods are pleased by truth. By truth the sun shines and the wind blows.

**9** He who is established in truth — his words never fail to come true. By the power of truth, he attains whatever he desires.

**10** The essence of all dharma is this: do not do unto others that which, if done to thee, would cause thee pain. This is the eternal law of righteousness.
