---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the creation of the universe as narrated by the Supreme Lord to Brahma at the beginning.

**2** Before creation, there was only Brahman, the one without a second, self-luminous, infinite, and eternal. From Brahman arose the desire to create, and from that desire the universe came into being.

**3** The Lord first created the cosmic waters, and into the waters He placed His seed. From that seed arose a golden egg, brilliant as a thousand suns.

**4** Within that golden egg, Brahma, the creator, was born. For one divine year he remained within the egg, and then by his mere thought he split it into two halves.

**5** From the two halves of the egg arose heaven and earth. Between them he established the sky, the eight directions, and the eternal abode of the waters.

**6** From his mind he created the ten Prajapatis, the progenitors of all creatures: Marichi, Atri, Angiras, Pulastya, Pulaha, Kratu, Vasishtha, Daksha, Bhrigu, and Narada.

**7** He created the gods, the demons, the human beings, the animals, the birds, the reptiles, the fish, the trees, the herbs, and all living creatures.

**8** He established the four Vedas, the sacred rituals, the moral laws, and the social orders for the governance of creation.

**9** He created Time, with its divisions of moments, hours, days, months, seasons, and years, and through Time the wheel of creation turns ceaselessly.

**10** The Lord who creates this universe also preserves it during its existence and dissolves it at the end of the cosmic cycle, only to create it again. Thus the cycle of creation is without beginning and without end.
