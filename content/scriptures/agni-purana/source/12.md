---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 12"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 12
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the nature of liberation, the supreme goal of all spiritual practice.

**2** Liberation is the release from the cycle of birth and death, the cessation of all suffering, the attainment of the infinite bliss of the Supreme Self.

**3** Four paths lead to liberation: Jnana Yoga, the path of knowledge; Bhakti Yoga, the path of devotion; Karma Yoga, the path of selfless action; and Raja Yoga, the path of meditation.

**4** Through Jnana Yoga, the seeker discriminates between the real and the unreal, between the eternal Self and the transient world, and attains the direct knowledge of Brahman.

**5** Through Bhakti Yoga, the seeker surrenders wholly to the Supreme Lord with love and devotion, and by the Lord's grace attains liberation.

**6** Through Karma Yoga, the seeker performs all actions without attachment to their fruits, offering all actions to the Supreme Lord, and thereby purifies the mind and attains liberation.

**7** Through Raja Yoga, the seeker controls the mind through the practice of the eight limbs of Yoga and attains the state of Samadhi, in which the Self is directly realized.

**8** All four paths lead to the same goal. The seeker should choose the path that is most suited to his temperament and follow it with sincerity and perseverance.

**9** He who has attained liberation is free from all desire, all fear, all attachment. He sees the same Self in all beings and all beings in the Self. He is established in eternal peace and bliss.

**10** Thus I have described to you, O Vasishtha, the essence of all knowledge. He who hears this Purana with faith and devotion, who recites it and contemplates its meaning, is freed from all sins and attains the supreme abode. Thus ends the Agni Purana.
