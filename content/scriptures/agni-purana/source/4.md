---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 4"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the duties of the four orders of life, by following which a man attains prosperity in this world and liberation in the next.

**2** The first order is that of the student, Brahmacharya. He should live with his teacher, study the Vedas, practise self-control, tend the sacred fire, and serve his teacher with devotion.

**3** The student should be celibate, speak the truth, avoid intoxicants, eat simple food, and sleep upon the ground. His mind should be fixed upon learning.

**4** The second order is that of the householder, Grihastha. He should marry according to the sacred rites, maintain the household fires, perform the daily sacrifices, and earn his livelihood by righteous means.

**5** The householder should give to the poor, feed the guests, honour the gods, the ancestors, and the sages. He is the support of all the other three orders.

**6** The third order is that of the forest-dweller, Vanaprastha. Having seen his children's children, he should retire to the forest, subsist on fruits and roots, practise austerities, and devote himself to meditation.

**7** The forest-dweller should study the Upanishads, contemplate the nature of the Self, endure heat and cold with equanimity, and gradually withdraw from all worldly attachments.

**8** The fourth order is that of the renunciant, Sannyasa. He should give up all possessions, all ties of family and society, and wander as a mendicant, devoted solely to the realization of Brahman.

**9** The renunciant should be free from anger, envy, and attachment. He should look upon all beings with equal vision, seeing the same Self in all.

**10** He who follows the duties of his order with sincerity and devotion attains the highest good. He who neglects them falls into darkness and suffering.
