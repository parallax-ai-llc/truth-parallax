---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 10"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the sacred places of pilgrimage, where the merit of worship is multiplied a thousandfold.

**2** Prayaga, the confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and the invisible Sarasvati, is the king of all pilgrimage sites. He who bathes there during the sacred month is freed from all sins.

**3** Varanasi, the city of Shiva, is the abode of liberation. He who dies in Varanasi attains moksha, for Shiva himself whispers the mantra of liberation into the ear of the dying.

**4** Gaya is the place where offerings to the ancestors are most efficacious. He who performs the shraddha ceremony at Gaya liberates his ancestors for seven generations.

**5** Pushkara is the sacred lake of Brahma. He who bathes there with faith and performs the prescribed rituals attains the world of Brahma.

**6** Kurukshetra is the field of dharma, where the great battle was fought and where Krishna spoke the Gita. The merit of performing austerities there is immeasurable.

**7** The banks of all sacred rivers — the Ganga, Yamuna, Sarasvati, Narmada, Godavari, Krishna, and Kaveri — are places of pilgrimage. He who bathes in their waters with faith is purified.

**8** But the greatest pilgrimage is the pilgrimage within. He who controls his senses, speaks the truth, practises non-violence, and sees the Self in all beings — he has visited all the sacred places.

**9** He whose mind is pure, whose heart is filled with devotion, who sees the Lord in all things — for him every place is a sacred place, every moment is auspicious.

**10** The external pilgrimage purifies the body; the internal pilgrimage purifies the mind. He who practises both attains the highest state of liberation.
