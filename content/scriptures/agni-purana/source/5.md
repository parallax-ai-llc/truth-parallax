---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 5"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the science of worship, by which the devotee may attain communion with the Supreme Lord.

**2** The devotee should rise before dawn, bathe in pure water, and perform his morning devotions facing the east. He should meditate upon the Supreme Lord with a concentrated mind.

**3** The image of the Lord should be installed with sacred rites and worshipped daily with flowers, incense, lights, food, and water.

**4** The sixteen offerings of worship are: invocation, offering a seat, washing of feet, offering water for washing hands, offering water for sipping, bathing, clothing, investiture with the sacred thread, offering sandal paste, offering flowers, offering incense, offering a lamp, offering food, prostration, circumambulation, and dismissal.

**5** The mantra is the means of invoking the divine presence. Each deity has a specific mantra, and through the repetition of the mantra with devotion, the deity manifests in the heart of the worshipper.

**6** Om is the supreme mantra, the source of all mantras. It represents Brahman, the Absolute. By meditating on Om, the seeker attains the highest state.

**7** The yantra is the geometric diagram which represents the deity and serves as a focus for meditation. The mandala is the sacred circle within which the worship is performed.

**8** The devotee should practise japa, the repetition of the divine name, with a rosary of 108 beads. The name of the Lord is the most powerful means of purification in this age of Kali.

**9** Through worship, the impurities of the mind are removed, the knots of the heart are loosened, and the devotee attains the vision of the Supreme Lord.

**10** The highest worship is the constant remembrance of the Lord in all activities, seeing His presence in all beings, and offering all actions to Him as a sacrifice.
