---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 11"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Agni said: I shall now describe the science of giving, by which a man attains merit in this world and the next.

**2** The gift of food is the greatest gift, for food sustains life. He who feeds the hungry acquires the merit of performing all sacrifices.

**3** The gift of knowledge is even greater than the gift of food, for knowledge leads to liberation. He who teaches the Vedas and the sacred sciences to a worthy student attains imperishable merit.

**4** The gift of fearlessness is the highest gift. He who protects the life of a creature in danger, who gives shelter to the afflicted, who saves the persecuted — he attains the world of the gods.

**5** A gift should be given at the proper time, to a worthy recipient, in a clean place, with faith and respect. A gift given without respect bears no fruit.

**6** The gift of gold purifies the giver. The gift of land ensures prosperity for generations. The gift of a cow leads to heaven. The gift of the Vedas leads to liberation.

**7** He who gives with a pure heart, without expectation of return, without pride or ostentation — his gift bears fruit a hundredfold.

**8** He who gives reluctantly, with attachment, expecting praise or reciprocation — his gift bears little fruit, for it is tainted by selfishness.

**9** The highest form of giving is the giving of oneself — the surrender of the ego, the offering of all one's actions, thoughts, and possessions to the Supreme Lord.

**10** He who has given up the sense of "mine" and "I," who has offered everything to the Lord, who sees all beings as the Lord's own — he has given the greatest gift, and he is truly free.
