---
title: "Agni Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "agni-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The sages, headed by Shaunaka, once approached the great Suta and asked him: "O blessed one, tell us the Purana which was narrated by the god of fire, Agni, to the sage Vasishtha."

**2** Suta said: I shall relate to you that excellent Purana which Agni narrated to Vasishtha, which destroys all sins and grants all desires.

**3** Vasishtha once approached Agni and asked: "O lord of fire, relate to me the knowledge of the Supreme Being, the means of attaining liberation, and the duties of men."

**4** Agni said: I shall relate to you that which Vishnu, the supreme lord of all, once taught to Brahma at the beginning of creation, and which Brahma in turn taught to the gods and sages.

**5** In the beginning there was only the Supreme Being, without a second. From Him arose the unmanifest Prakriti, and from Prakriti arose Mahat, the great principle of intelligence.

**6** From Mahat arose Ahankara, the principle of individuation. From Ahankara arose the five subtle elements: sound, touch, form, taste, and smell.

**7** From the five subtle elements arose the five gross elements: ether, air, fire, water, and earth. From these the entire universe was created.

**8** The Supreme Lord Vishnu, who is the cause of creation, preservation, and dissolution, pervades all things. He is the inner self of all beings, the eternal witness.

**9** He who knows the Supreme Lord as the one without a second, as the cause of all causes, as the infinite and eternal — he is freed from the bonds of birth and death.

**10** This knowledge was first imparted by Vishnu to Brahma, by Brahma to the sages, and by the sages to mankind. He who hears it with faith attains liberation.
