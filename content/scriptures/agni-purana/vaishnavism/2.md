The Agni Purana's cosmological and geographical section is read by Vaishnavas as a map of Vishnu's sovereign presence pervading every level of creation. Ramanuja's Vishishtadvaita framework treats the concentric structure of the universe — the seven dvipas, the seven oceans, and the vertical hierarchy from Patala to Satyaloka — as the body (*sharira*) of Vishnu, who is the inner controller (*antaryamin*) of every cosmic region. The sacred geography described here, including the rivers, mountains, and pilgrimage sites, is understood not merely as physical landscape but as the Lord's self-manifestation in spatial form, each site sanctified by His presence or the *lila* of His incarnations.

Madhva's Dvaita interpretation emphasizes the hierarchical ordering of the cosmos as reflecting Vishnu's absolute sovereignty (*svatantrya*) and the dependent nature (*paratantrya*) of all created beings. The placement of Vishnu's realm above even Brahma's Satyaloka confirms the ontological supremacy of the Supreme Person. For the Chaitanya school, the geographical descriptions of sacred rivers like the Ganga and Yamuna carry additional devotional significance — these are understood as flowing from the Lord's own form, making pilgrimage to them a direct encounter with divine grace (*kripa*). The section's encyclopedic scope reflects the Bhagavata teaching that nothing exists independent of Vishnu's sustaining power.
