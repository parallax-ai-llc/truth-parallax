The Agni Purana's dharmashastra section, covering law, ethics, inheritance, and social duties, is read by the Vaishnava tradition through the lens of the Bhagavad Gita's teaching on *svadharma* — the performance of one's prescribed duties as an offering to Vishnu. Ramanuja understands the entire system of *varnashrama-dharma* as Vishnu's ordained structure for spiritual progress, where each person's social role becomes a mode of devotional service when performed with consciousness of the Lord. The legal prescriptions on inheritance, property, and dispute resolution are not secular regulations but expressions of *rita* (cosmic order), which Vishnu sustains through His will.

Madhva's Dvaita school reads this section as confirming the reality and moral seriousness of worldly action — because the individual soul is genuinely distinct from God and genuinely responsible for its choices, the dharmic prescriptions carry real weight. Neglecting one's duties is not merely a social failing but a spiritual offense against Vishnu's established order. The Chaitanya tradition, while acknowledging the validity of *varnashrama* duties at the conventional level, ultimately subordinates the entire dharmashastra to the principle of *bhagavata-dharma* — the religion of devotion that transcends caste and social station. As the Bhagavata Purana declares, devotion to the Lord is the essence (*sara*) from which all dharmic prescriptions derive their authority.
