The Agni Purana's Ayurvedic section, detailing herbal remedies, diagnostic methods, and therapeutic treatments, is read by Vaishnavas as evidence of Vishnu's comprehensive care for embodied souls. The Lord Dhanvantari — an avatar of Vishnu who emerged from the churning of the cosmic ocean bearing the nectar of immortality — is regarded as the divine source of medical knowledge. Ramanuja's Vishishtadvaita theology sees the body as part of God's *sharira* (body), making its care a form of stewardship that supports the soul's spiritual journey. Maintaining bodily health through Ayurveda enables the devotee to pursue sustained devotional practice (*sadhana*).

Madhva's tradition reads the medical prescriptions as demonstrating Vishnu's concern for the practical welfare of His dependents — the Lord provides not only spiritual liberation but material sustenance and healing. The Chaitanya school, while primarily focused on transcendent devotion, acknowledges that bodily health serves *bhajana* (worship), and that the herbs and minerals described here are manifestations of Vishnu's creative energy (*shakti*) placed in nature for human benefit. The Puranic framework integrates medical science into a theological worldview where every form of knowledge ultimately points back to the Lord's omniscient providence.
