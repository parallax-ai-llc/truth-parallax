The Agni Purana's section on royal dynasties traces the Surya Vamsha (Solar Dynasty) and Chandra Vamsha (Lunar Dynasty) in a manner that Vaishnava commentators read as demonstrating Vishnu's ongoing intervention in human history. The Solar Dynasty, which includes Rama, and the Lunar Dynasty, which includes Krishna and the Pandavas, are not merely genealogical records but theological evidence that Vishnu incarnates within specific lineages to guide humanity. Ramanuja's tradition reads these genealogies as confirming that the Lord's *avatara* activity follows a purposeful pattern — He appears in royal families so that His actions carry political and social consequence, not merely personal salvation.

Madhva's interpretation stresses the doctrine of *taratamya* (gradation) evident in how certain kings flourish through devotion to Vishnu while others fall through neglect of dharma. The rise of Sagara, Dilipa, and Raghu in the Solar line demonstrates how royal prosperity depends on alignment with Vishnu's will, while the Lunar Dynasty's turbulent history — particularly the Kaurava-Pandava conflict — illustrates the devastating consequences of abandoning divine guidance. The Chaitanya tradition draws attention to the Yadu lineage as the chosen vessel for Krishna's appearance, reading the genealogical chain as a preparation across generations for the supreme manifestation of divine love (*prema*) in human form.
