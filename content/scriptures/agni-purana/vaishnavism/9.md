The Agni Purana's treatment of military science — covering battle formations, weaponry, strategy, and the duties of warriors — is interpreted by Vaishnavas through the paradigm of the Bhagavad Gita, where Krishna Himself instructs Arjuna in the dharma of righteous warfare. Ramanuja's reading holds that the kshatriya's martial duty is a legitimate mode of *nishkama-karma* (desireless action) when performed as service to the Lord's cosmic order. The detailed knowledge of arms and tactics presented here is understood as Vishnu's provision for the protection of dharma — the Lord empowers kings and warriors to maintain the social stability within which devotional life can flourish.

Madhva's Dvaita interpretation connects the military science to Vishnu's own warrior manifestations — as Rama wielding the divine bow Sharnga, as Krishna guiding Arjuna's chariot at Kurukshetra, and as the future Kalki who will appear armed to destroy adharma at the end of the age. The divine weapons (*divyastra*) described in this section are understood as condensed forms of Vishnu's cosmic power entrusted to righteous warriors. The Chaitanya tradition, while emphasizing that pure devotion transcends all social duties, acknowledges that martial dharma performed in service to the Lord represents *karma-yoga* as Krishna taught it — action without selfish motive, dedicated entirely to the divine will.
