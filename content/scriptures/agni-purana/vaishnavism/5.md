The Agni Purana's inclusion of Sanskrit grammar and lexicography within a Puranic text is theologically significant for Vaishnavas. The tradition holds that sacred language itself is a manifestation of Vishnu's creative power — the *shabda-brahman* (word-absolute) through which the Lord articulates the structure of reality. Ramanuja's Vishishtadvaita framework reads the grammatical analysis of Sanskrit as revealing the inherent relationship between name (*nama*) and the named (*nami*), paralleling the inseparable connection between Vishnu and His creation. Correct understanding of language thus becomes a prerequisite for correct understanding of scripture, and ultimately of God Himself.

Madhva's tradition places particular emphasis on the precision of language for theological discourse, holding that verbal testimony (*shabda-pramana*) is the highest means of knowing God. The Agni Purana's treatment of word formation, etymology, and semantic analysis is read as Vishnu's own provision for the accurate transmission of Vedic truth. The Chaitanya school finds devotional significance in the science of divine names (*nama-tattva*), understanding the grammatical structure of names like Narayana, Vasudeva, and Krishna as encoding theological truths about the Lord's nature, activities, and relationships with His devotees.
