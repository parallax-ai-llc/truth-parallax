---
title: "Muktika Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "muktika-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om! At the end of the recitation of the Ramayana, Hanuman asked Sri Rama: "O Rama, tell me about the nature of the Atman and the means of liberation."

**2** Sri Rama replied: "I shall tell thee a wonderful secret. The knowledge of the Atman is the means of liberation. I shall tell thee about the Muktika — the science of liberation."

**3** Hanuman asked: "O Rama, what are the Upanishads? How many are they? What do they teach? Which among them leads to liberation?"

**4** Sri Rama replied: "There are one hundred and eight Upanishads. The Mandukya alone is sufficient for the liberation of the aspirant. If knowledge is not attained by it, then study the ten principal Upanishads."

**5** "These ten are: Isha, Kena, Katha, Prashna, Mundaka, Mandukya, Taittiriya, Aitareya, Chandogya, and Brihadaranyaka."

**6** "If liberation is not attained even through these, then study the thirty-two Upanishads. If even then liberation is not attained, study all one hundred and eight Upanishads."

**7** "Among the Upanishads of the Rig Veda are: Aitareya, Kaushitaki, Nadabindu, Atmaprabodha, Nirvana, Mudgala, Aksha-malika, Tripura, Saubhagya-lakshmi, and Bahvricha."

**8** "Among the Upanishads of the Shukla Yajur Veda are: Isha, Brihadaranyaka, Jabala, Hamsa, Paramahamsa, Subala, Mantrika, Niralamba, Trisikhi-brahmana, Mandala-brahmana, Advaya-taraka, Paingala, Bhikshuka, Turiyatita, Adhyatma, Tarasara, Yajnavalkya, Shatyayani, and Muktika."

**9** "Among the Upanishads of the Krishna Yajur Veda are: Katha, Taittiriya, Brahma, Kaivalya, Shvetashvatara, Garbha, Narayana, Amritabindu, Amritanada, Kalagni-rudra, Kshurika, Sarvasara, Shukarahasya, Tejobindu, Dhyanabindu, Brahmavidya, Yogatattva, Dakshinamurti, Skanda, Shariraka, Yogashikha, Ekakshara, Akshi, and Avadhuta."

**10** "Among the Upanishads of the Sama Veda are: Kena, Chandogya, Aruneya, Maitrayani, Maitreyi, Vajrasuchika, Yogachudamani, and Vasudeva."

**11** "Among the Upanishads of the Atharva Veda are: Prashna, Mundaka, Mandukya, Atharva-shiras, Atharva-shikha, Brihajjabala, Nrisimha-tapaniya, Narada-parivrajaka, Sita, Sharabha, Mahanarayana, Ramarahasya, Ramatapaniya, Sandilya, Paramahamsaparivrajaka, Annapurna, Surya, Atma, Pashupata, Parabrahma, Tripuratapini, Devi, Bhavana, Ganapati, and Dattatreya."

**12** "These one hundred and eight Upanishads are the essence of the Vedas. He who studies them all is freed from all bonds and attains the supreme Brahman."
