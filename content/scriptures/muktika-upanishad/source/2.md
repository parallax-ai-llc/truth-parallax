---
title: "Muktika Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "muktika-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Hanuman then asked: "O Rama, what is the nature of liberation? Is it of one kind or many kinds?"

**2** Sri Rama replied: "Liberation is of four kinds: Salokya, Samipya, Sarupya, and Sayujya."

**3** "Salokya is living in the same world as the Lord. Samipya is dwelling near the Lord. Sarupya is having the same form as the Lord. Sayujya is complete union with the Lord."

**4** "But the liberation spoken of in the Upanishads is Kaivalya — absolute independence, the state in which the Atman alone shines in its own nature, free from all adjuncts."

**5** "This Kaivalya is attained by the knowledge of Brahman alone. Brahman is Sat-Chit-Ananda — Existence, Consciousness, and Bliss — without parts, without qualities, without action, without fault."

**6** "The universe is but a superimposition upon Brahman, like the serpent upon the rope. When the rope is known, the serpent disappears. When Brahman is known, the universe disappears."

**7** "The means to this knowledge are: discrimination between the real and the unreal, dispassion toward the enjoyments of this world and the next, the sixfold virtues beginning with tranquility, and an intense desire for liberation."

**8** "When these four qualifications are present, one should approach a teacher who is well-versed in the scriptures and established in Brahman."

**9** "From the teacher one should hear the truth, then reflect upon it, and then meditate upon it. Through these three — hearing, reflection, and meditation — one attains the direct knowledge of Brahman."

**10** "When the knowledge of Brahman arises, ignorance is destroyed, and with it all bondage. The knower of Brahman attains the highest."

**11** "He who studies these one hundred and eight Upanishads attains the knowledge of Brahman. He is not born again. He attains Kaivalya."

**12** "Om! Thus has Sri Rama declared the Muktika, the garland of liberation, to Hanuman. He who studies this Muktika Upanishad is freed from all sins and attains the supreme goal."

**13** Om! Thus ends the Muktika Upanishad, as contained in the Shukla Yajur Veda. Om Shanti, Shanti, Shanti.
