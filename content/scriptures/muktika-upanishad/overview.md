## Overview

The Muktika Upanishad ("Upanishad of Liberation") belongs to the Shukla Yajur Veda and holds a unique position in the Upanishadic corpus as the text that establishes the canon of 108 Upanishads. Cast as a dialogue between Rama and Hanuman, the text lists all 108 Upanishads by name, classifies them according to the four Vedas, and teaches that the study of all 108 leads to complete liberation (*muktika*). The Upanishad also discusses the nature of liberation itself and the relative importance of the different Upanishadic texts.

## Structure

The Muktika Upanishad consists of two sections:

1. **Section 1 — The Canon of 108 Upanishads**: Rama's listing and classification of all Upanishads
2. **Section 2 — The Nature of Liberation**: The teaching on liberation through Upanishadic study and realization

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Vaishnavism**: Rama as the divine teacher establishing the scriptural canon
- **Shaivism**: The 108 Upanishads include major Shaiva texts as part of the revealed canon
- **Advaita Vedanta**: The Mandukya alone is sufficient for liberation; the 108 provide the complete teaching
- **Dvaita Vedanta**: The scriptural canon as God's provision for the souls' liberation
- **Academic**: The primary source for the Upanishadic canon and its classification
- **Comparative**: Canon formation compared across scriptural traditions worldwide

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
