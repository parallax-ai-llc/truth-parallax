title: "Fudoki Section 4: Hizen Province"
scripture: "fudoki"
chapter: 4
---

The Hizen Fudoki records the geography and legends of the province in northwestern Kyushu, a region whose proximity to the Asian continent shaped its history, culture, and mythology. The text preserves traditions reflecting centuries of contact with Korea and China, making it essential for understanding early Japan's international connections.

The mythological narratives of Hizen emphasize the region's role as a frontier zone where Japanese civilization encountered foreign influences. Stories describe the arrival of continental visitors, both human and divine, who brought new technologies, crops, and religious practices. The deity of the Yodo Shrine is said to have arrived from across the sea, and several place names are traced to Korean or Chinese origins.

The text describes the hot springs of Hizen with particular enthusiasm, notably the famous springs at Ureshino and other sites that remain popular today. These springs are described not only for their healing properties but also as sites of divine manifestation, where deities revealed themselves to humans through the miraculous appearance of hot water from the earth.

The province's role in national defense receives attention, reflecting Kyushu's vulnerability to continental military threats. The text describes the construction of fortifications, the maintenance of signal fire stations along the coast, and the readiness of local forces to respond to invasion. The great wall of Mizuki, built after the defeat at the Battle of Baekgang in 663, is referenced as evidence of the region's military significance.

The agricultural and natural resources of Hizen are cataloged with attention to the province's distinctive products, including ceramics produced from local clays, marine products from the extensive coastline, and agricultural goods from the fertile plains. The text notes the presence of foreign communities and their contributions to local culture, providing evidence for the cosmopolitan character of early Kyushu.
