title: "Fudoki Section 1: Hitachi Province"
scripture: "fudoki"
chapter: 1
---

The Hitachi Fudoki records the geography, myths, and customs of one of the great eastern provinces. The name Hitachi itself is explained through a narrative of the rising sun striking the land directly, making it a place of constant sunlight and exceptional fertility. The text opens with an account of Emperor Kotoku's decree ordering the compilation of provincial records, establishing the bureaucratic context for the literary and mythological content that follows.

The province's districts are enumerated with careful attention to etymology. The name of each district is traced to a mythological event, a natural feature, or a founding act by a deity or legendary figure. Kashima District takes its name from the kami enshrined there, a deity of thunder and martial power whose shrine would become one of the most important in eastern Japan. The text describes the shrine's sacred grove, its spring rituals, and the annual festival at which provincial officials and local people gather.

The myths of Hitachi emphasize the taming of the eastern wilderness. Stories recount how deities and imperial princes subdued the indigenous Emishi people and established agricultural settlements. One famous passage describes the giant Daidarabocchi, whose footprints formed lakes and whose actions shaped the landscape. These tales serve both to explain geographic features and to legitimize imperial authority over the eastern provinces.

The natural resources of Hitachi receive detailed attention. The text catalogs the province's rivers, mountains, plains, and coastal features, noting the fish, animals, and plants found in each area. Hot springs are described with attention to their healing properties. The agricultural potential of the land is assessed, with notes on soil quality, water availability, and the crops most suited to each district. The Hitachi Fudoki thus combines mythological narrative with practical geographic information, reflecting the dual purposes of the fudoki genre.
