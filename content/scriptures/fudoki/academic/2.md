Academic study of the Harima Province (播磨国) Fudoki applies the methodologies of historical geography, philology, archaeology, and religious studies to the records from modern Hyogo Prefecture. Scholars examine this provincial gazetteer as a product of the ritsuryō state's administrative apparatus, as a record of oral traditions captured at a specific historical moment in the eighth century CE, and as a source for understanding the social, economic, and religious conditions of Nara-period provincial Japan.

The Harima Province (播磨国) records present geographical and mythological records reflecting the cultural traditions of the Inland Sea region. Textual scholars analyze the relationship between the surviving text and the original provincial submission, the degree to which local traditions were filtered through the perspectives of the literati who compiled them, and the identification of different chronological layers within the text. Archaeological evidence from the region provides material correlates for the textual record, enabling a more complete reconstruction of eighth-century provincial life.
