Academic study of the Bungo Province (豊後国) Fudoki applies the methodologies of historical geography, philology, archaeology, and religious studies to the records from modern Oita Prefecture. Scholars examine this provincial gazetteer as a product of the ritsuryō state's administrative apparatus, as a record of oral traditions captured at a specific historical moment in the eighth century CE, and as a source for understanding the social, economic, and religious conditions of Nara-period provincial Japan.

The Bungo Province (豊後国) records present accounts from eastern Kyushu preserving distinctive local kami traditions and geographical lore. Textual scholars analyze the relationship between the surviving text and the original provincial submission, the degree to which local traditions were filtered through the perspectives of the literati who compiled them, and the identification of different chronological layers within the text. Archaeological evidence from the region provides material correlates for the textual record, enabling a more complete reconstruction of eighth-century provincial life.
