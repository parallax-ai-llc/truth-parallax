Confucian interpretation of the Bungo Province (豊後国) Fudoki reads this provincial gazetteer within the framework of proper governance and administrative virtue. The imperial edict commanding its compilation reflects Confucian ideals of rational administration: a virtuous ruler must know the conditions, resources, and customs of every province in order to govern wisely. The records from modern Oita Prefecture demonstrate the application of Chinese-influenced bureaucratic rationality to the task of understanding and ordering the Japanese realm.

The Bungo Province (豊後国) records present accounts from eastern Kyushu preserving distinctive local kami traditions and geographical lore. Confucian readers note the administrative dimensions of the text: its systematic recording of agricultural production, natural resources, and population — information essential for effective governance. At the same time, the text's extensive mythological content reveals the limits of bureaucratic rationality in a culture where divine narrative and administrative record coexist as complementary ways of knowing the land.
