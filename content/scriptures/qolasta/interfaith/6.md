## Interfaith Reading

Interfaith engagement with Qolasta Section 6, "Sabbath and Festival Prayers," invites participants from diverse religious backgrounds to encounter the Mandaean prayer tradition and to discover resonances with their own experiences of worship, ritual, and devotion. The Qolasta's treatment of Sabbath and Festival Prayers addresses dimensions of human spiritual life that transcend religious boundaries — the need for purification, the desire for divine protection, the experience of grief and hope, and the longing for connection with the sacred source of life.

Reading this section in an interfaith context encourages participants to reflect on how their own traditions address similar liturgical needs. How do different communities pray at the bedside of the sick or the grave of the departed? How is water used sacramentally across traditions? What role does sacred language play in mediating the divine presence? The Qolasta's answers to these questions — rooted in the ancient Mandaean reverence for the World of Light and the living water of the *yardna* — enrich the interfaith conversation by introducing a voice that is both deeply particular and surprisingly universal.

## Key Themes

- Universal liturgical needs addressed through Mandaean specificity
- Water as a cross-traditional symbol of purification and renewal
- The human experience of life transitions as common ground for dialogue
- Honoring minority liturgical traditions in the interfaith conversation
- The role of beauty, chant, and sensory experience in worship

## Key Passages

> "I stand in the presence of the Great Life and offer my prayer. May the light that flows from the World of Light purify my heart and strengthen my soul."

This prayer, with its themes of divine presence, purification, and spiritual strengthening, resonates across traditions. Christian participants may hear echoes of the Lord's Prayer; Muslims may recognize the spirit of *du'a*; Jews may recall the *Amidah*'s opening; and Hindus may think of *puja* offerings. These resonances create bridges for interfaith understanding.

## Commentary Tradition

Interfaith engagement with the Qolasta is a growing field, facilitated by scholars and community leaders who bring Mandaean texts into interreligious dialogue. The increasing presence of Mandaean diaspora communities in Western countries has created new opportunities for interfaith encounter.
