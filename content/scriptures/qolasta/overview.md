## Overview

The Qolasta (Mandaic: "Praised" or "Anthology of Praises"), also known as the Canonical Prayerbook of the Mandaeans, is the central liturgical text of the Mandaean religion. This comprehensive collection of prayers, hymns, blessings, and ritual instructions accompanies every major ceremony in Mandaean religious life — from the weekly *masbuta* (baptism) to the elaborate *masiqta* (death ceremony), from marriage rites to priestly ordination. Written in classical Mandaic, the Qolasta is the living voice of Mandaean worship, recited by priests and community members in ceremonies that have been performed continuously for nearly two millennia.

The Qolasta's prayers are addressed to the Great Life (*Hiia Rbia*), the supreme divine reality, and to the *uthras* (celestial beings of light) who serve as intermediaries between the World of Light and the material world. The liturgical language is characterized by elaborate praise, cosmic imagery, and repeated invocations of light, purity, and divine protection. Every prayer situates the worshipper within the larger cosmic drama of Light versus Darkness, connecting the most intimate moments of human life — birth, marriage, illness, death — to the eternal processes of the heavenly realm.

E. S. Drower's critical edition and English translation (1959) made the Qolasta accessible to the scholarly world for the first time, and it remains the standard reference. The text continues to be used in Mandaean communities in Iraq, Iran, and the global diaspora, serving as the indispensable handbook of Mandaean worship.

## Structure

The Qolasta is organized into fifteen main sections according to liturgical function:

1. **Baptismal Prayers**: For the *masbuta* ceremony in living water
2. **Daily Prayers**: Morning and evening devotions
3. **Death Ceremony**: Prayers for the *masiqta* and the soul's ascent
4. **Life Cycle Rites**: Marriage, ordination, and other ceremonies
5. **Festival and Sabbath**: Prayers for sacred times
6. **Hymns and Doxologies**: Songs of praise to the World of Light
7. **Protective and Penitential**: Prayers for healing, protection, and repentance
8. **Cosmic and Commemorative**: Creation hymns and prayers for the departed

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Mandaean**: The essential liturgical handbook, used in every religious ceremony
- **Academic**: A primary source for the study of Mandaic language, liturgical history, and ritual practice
- **Comparative**: Offers rich parallels to Jewish, Christian, and Zoroastrian liturgical traditions
- **Gnostic**: Contains some of the most elaborate Gnostic liturgical compositions to survive
- **Jewish**: Illuminates the liturgical practices of ancient baptismal communities related to Judaism
- **Interfaith**: Presents a living prayer tradition that speaks to universal human spiritual needs
