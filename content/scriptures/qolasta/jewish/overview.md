## Overview

Jewish scholarly engagement with the Qolasta focuses on the text's liturgical parallels with Jewish prayer traditions and what these reveal about the shared religious heritage of Judaism and Mandaeism. The Qolasta's use of Aramaic, its patterns of blessing and praise, its invocations of divine attributes, and its ritual concern with purity through immersion all resonate with Jewish liturgical practice, suggesting a common matrix in the diverse religious world of late Second Temple Judaism.

The parallels between the Qolasta's baptismal prayers and Jewish *mikveh* blessings, between the Qolasta's daily prayer cycle and the Jewish *shacharit/mincha/maariv* structure, and between the Qolasta's prayers for the dead and Jewish *kaddish* and *yizkor* traditions invite careful comparative analysis. Jewish scholars examine whether these parallels reflect direct historical connection (a shared origin in Jewish baptismal communities), parallel development from a common cultural and linguistic base, or later mutual influence in the Mesopotamian context where both communities coexisted for centuries.
