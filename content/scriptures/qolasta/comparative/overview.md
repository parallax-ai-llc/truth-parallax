## Overview

The Qolasta offers extraordinarily rich material for comparative liturgical study. Its prayers for baptism, marriage, death, and daily devotion invite comparison with analogous liturgical traditions across the world's religions — the Jewish *siddur* and *machzor*, the Christian *Book of Common Prayer* and Eastern Orthodox liturgies, the Islamic *salat* and *du'a* traditions, and the Zoroastrian *yasna* ceremonies. These comparisons reveal both shared patterns in how human communities structure their encounters with the sacred and distinctive features that mark Mandaean worship as unique.

The Qolasta's baptismal prayers are of particular comparative interest, as they represent the most elaborate surviving liturgy of ritual immersion from the ancient world. Scholars compare these prayers with Jewish *mikveh* blessings, Christian baptismal rites (especially the early Syriac traditions described in the *Didascalia* and the writings of Ephrem), and the lustration ceremonies described in Zoroastrian texts. The Qolasta's death liturgy (*masiqta* prayers) is compared with the Tibetan *Bardo Thodol*, the Egyptian *Book of the Dead*, and Christian funeral rites, revealing diverse strategies for ritually accompanying the soul in its post-mortem journey.
