---
title: "The Story of Maca Uisa"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 17
---

**1** Maca Uisa was a minor huaca, but his story is told because it teaches an important lesson about the relationship between huacas and the people.

**2** Maca Uisa demanded excessive offerings from the people. He wanted more llamas, more chicha, more coca than the people could afford.

**3** The people complained to Pariacaca. Pariacaca said: A huaca who takes too much from his people is no better than a thief. He must be moderate.

**4** Maca Uisa was chastened and reduced his demands. From that time, the offerings to each huaca were fixed by custom and could not be increased without the consent of the community.

**5** This story shows that even the gods must be just, and that the relationship between the people and their huacas is one of mutual obligation.
