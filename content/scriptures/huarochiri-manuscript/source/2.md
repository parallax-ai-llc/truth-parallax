---
title: "The Life of Cuniraya Viracocha"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Cuniraya Viracocha, in the very ancient times before Pariacaca existed, used to walk about in the form of a very poor and wretched man. His cloak and tunic were torn and tattered.

**2** Some people who did not recognize him called him a lousy beggar. Yet he was the one who shaped all the villages. Just by speaking, he made the terraces and their irrigation channels.

**3** And by throwing down a flower of a cane called pupuna, he made the irrigation channels function perfectly from their sources.

**4** This Cuniraya Viracocha once fell in love with a woman named Cavillaca, a beautiful virgin whom all the huacas and villas desired but who rejected them all.

**5** By a trick, he caused her to eat his seed, hidden in a lucuma fruit. She became pregnant without ever having been with a man.

**6** When the child was a year old, she set it on the ground and told it: Go to your father. The child crawled to Cuniraya, who sat in his poor rags at the edge of the assembly. Cavillaca was horrified and fled with the child toward the sea.
