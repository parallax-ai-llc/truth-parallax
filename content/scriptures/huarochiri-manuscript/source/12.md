---
title: "The Festivals of Pariacaca"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 12
---

**1** At the great festival of Pariacaca, the people gathered from all the surrounding communities. Each ayllu came with its offerings: llamas, guinea pigs, chicha, coca, and fine textiles.

**2** The procession ascended the mountain in a prescribed order. The people of Concha came first, then the people of Checa, then the others, each in their ancient order of precedence.

**3** At the summit, the priests performed the sacrifice. The blood of the llamas was sprinkled on the stones of the shrine. The fat was burned as an offering, and its smoke rose to the sky.

**4** For five days the people feasted and danced. The old men told the stories of Pariacaca. The young men competed in games and contests of skill.

**5** Then the people descended the mountain and returned to their homes, carrying with them the blessing of Pariacaca for the coming season.
