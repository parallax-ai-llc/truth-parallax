---
title: "The Story of the Conquest of the Yunca"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 13
---

**1** The yunca, the people of the lowlands, were different from the highland peoples. They worshipped different huacas and followed different customs.

**2** When Pariacaca conquered the highlands, the yunca resisted. They fought with the power of their own huacas, but Pariacaca's rain and hail overcame their fire and heat.

**3** The yunca were pushed down to the coastal valleys. Some submitted to Pariacaca's rule; others maintained their independence and their own gods.

**4** The boundary between the highlands and the lowlands was a sacred boundary, marked by the great snake that Pariacaca had placed to guard against Huallallo Carhuincho.

**5** Trade between the highlands and the lowlands continued despite the rivalry. The highlanders brought potatoes and wool; the lowlanders brought fish and cotton.
