---
title: "How the Idols of Old Were, and How They Warred Among Themselves"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 1
---

**1** In very ancient times, there were huacas named Yanañamca and Tutañamca. We do not know which of these came first.

**2** In those times, the world was in darkness. There was no sun. Whoever was stronger lived by preying upon the weaker. The people lived in misery, fighting among themselves.

**3** In those days, the people said: Whoever among us finishes dancing first shall be as the sun, giving light to all. They danced all night.

**4** It was the one named Pariacaca who was destined to become the great huaca, but he had not yet been born. Before him was Cuniraya Viracocha, who wandered the land in the form of a poor man.

**5** These are the ancient stories that the people of Huarochiri have told from the beginning, and we write them down now so that they will not be forgotten.
