---
title: "The Inca and the Local Huacas"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 29
---

**1** The relationship between the Inca and the local huacas was one of accommodation. The Inca recognized the power of Pariacaca and made offerings at his shrine.

**2** In return, Pariacaca was said to have supported the Inca in their wars of conquest. His rain and hail helped the Inca armies defeat their enemies.

**3** Some of the local huacas resisted the Inca. They were defeated and their shrines were destroyed. But those who cooperated were honored and their worship was maintained.

**4** The priests of Pariacaca served alongside the priests of the sun. They performed their ceremonies at the same festivals, though the sun god was given precedence.

**5** This system endured until the coming of the Spanish, who destroyed both the Inca and the local systems of worship.
