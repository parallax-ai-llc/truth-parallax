---
title: "The Coming of the Christians"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 30
---

**1** When the Christians came, they destroyed the shrines and forbade the worship of the huacas. They smashed the stone images and burned the sacred objects.

**2** The priests were persecuted. Those who continued to perform the ancient ceremonies were punished with imprisonment and whipping.

**3** But the people did not forget. In secret, they continued to make offerings to Pariacaca and to the other huacas. They hid their sacred objects and performed their ceremonies at night.

**4** The Christian priests said: Your gods are demons. There is only one God, the God of the Christians. But the people said: Our gods are not demons. They give us water and food. Without them, we cannot live.

**5** The old ways and the new ways existed side by side, sometimes in open conflict, sometimes in uneasy accommodation. The people adapted, as they had always adapted, but they did not forget.
