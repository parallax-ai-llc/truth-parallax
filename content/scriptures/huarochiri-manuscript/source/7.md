---
title: "The War Between Pariacaca and Huallallo Carhuincho"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Pariacaca and his brothers climbed to the summit of the mountain where Huallallo Carhuincho dwelt. Huallallo was a fire god, and he sent great flames against them.

**2** Pariacaca and his brothers sent rain and hail and lightning. For five days the battle raged: fire against water, heat against cold, the old god against the new.

**3** Pariacaca prevailed. His rain extinguished Huallallo's fire. His hail buried Huallallo's territory. His lightning struck Huallallo again and again.

**4** Huallallo fled to the lowlands, to the jungle on the eastern side of the mountains. Pariacaca pursued him and drove him deep into the forest.

**5** Then Pariacaca set a great snake to guard the boundary, so that Huallallo could never return to the highlands. And Pariacaca took his place as the lord of the mountains.

**6** The people of the highlands began to worship Pariacaca. They made offerings to him of llamas and guinea pigs and chicha. They built his shrine on the mountain and held festivals in his honor.
