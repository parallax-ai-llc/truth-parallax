---
title: "Cuniraya's Pursuit"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Cuniraya Viracocha ran after Cavillaca, calling out: Sister, wait! Look at me! I am truly beautiful! He threw off his rags and revealed his golden clothing, and the whole world was illuminated by his beauty.

**2** As he ran, he met the condor and asked: Brother, where is that woman? The condor replied: She is very near. You will find her. Cuniraya said: You shall live long. You shall eat the dead of the wild animals and those that men kill. Whoever kills you shall die.

**3** He met the fox and asked the same question. The fox said: She is already far away. You will never find her. Cuniraya cursed the fox: Even when you walk afar, people shall hate you and call you a thief. When they kill you, they shall throw you away without caring.

**4** He met the parrot, who gave bad news. He cursed it: You shall screech and screech, and when people hear you, they shall say: There is that enemy. And they shall chase you.

**5** He met the puma, who said: She is very near. Cuniraya blessed it: You shall eat the llamas of those who sin. When people kill you, they shall wear your skin at festivals, and your fame shall endure forever.

**6** At last Cuniraya reached the sea. Cavillaca and her child had already turned to stone, two rocks standing in the ocean at Pachacamac. They stand there to this day.
