---
title: "The Sacred Springs"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 25
---

**1** The sacred springs of Huarochiri were numerous. Each one had its own story, its own huaca, and its own ceremonies.

**2** The springs were the sources of the irrigation channels. Without them, the fields could not be watered, and the people could not survive.

**3** The most important spring was the one at the foot of Pariacaca's mountain. It was said to flow with the water of Pariacaca himself, and its water had healing properties.

**4** The priests cleaned the spring channels every year in a great communal effort. All the people participated, and the work was accompanied by singing and dancing.

**5** To pollute a spring was one of the gravest offenses. The punishment was severe: the offender was banished from the community and his fields were confiscated.
