---
title: "The Story of Tutayquiri"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 27
---

**1** Tutayquiri was the ancestor huaca of the Checa people. He was a warrior who conquered the territory for his people in the ancient times.

**2** Tutayquiri came from the east, leading his people through many hardships. He fought against the existing inhabitants and defeated them through both military skill and divine power.

**3** When he had conquered the territory, he divided it among his followers. Each family received its land and its water rights according to its service in the conquest.

**4** Tutayquiri then turned to stone, and the stone became the principal shrine of the Checa. The people made offerings to it before every important undertaking.

**5** The story of Tutayquiri justified the territorial claims of the Checa. By telling his story, they asserted their right to the land they occupied.
