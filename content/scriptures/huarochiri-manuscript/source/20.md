---
title: "The Marriage Customs"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 20
---

**1** Marriage among the people of Huarochiri followed strict customs established by the ancestors. A man could not marry a woman of his own ayllu. He must seek a wife from another community.

**2** The families of the bride and groom negotiated the terms. The groom's family provided llamas, cloth, and chicha to the bride's family.

**3** The wedding ceremony was held at the bride's home. The couple exchanged gifts, and the priest spoke the ancient words of blessing.

**4** After the wedding, the couple lived with the bride's family for one year, working in their fields. After one year, they moved to the groom's community.

**5** If the marriage was unhappy, the couple could separate. The children stayed with the mother's family, and the man returned to his own community.
