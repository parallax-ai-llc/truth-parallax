---
title: "The Story of Llocllayhuancupa"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 19
---

**1** Llocllayhuancupa was a powerful huaca associated with storms and floods. The people feared him and made offerings to keep him appeased.

**2** It is said that Llocllayhuancupa was the child of Pariacaca and a woman of the lowlands. He inherited his father's power over water but used it destructively.

**3** When Llocllayhuancupa was angry, he sent floods that destroyed the irrigation channels and swept away the crops. The people had to rebuild every year.

**4** The priests of Llocllayhuancupa performed special ceremonies to calm his anger. They sacrificed black llamas and poured chicha into the rivers.

**5** The story of Llocllayhuancupa teaches that the forces of nature can be both creative and destructive, and that the people must maintain their relationship with these forces through constant vigilance and ceremony.
