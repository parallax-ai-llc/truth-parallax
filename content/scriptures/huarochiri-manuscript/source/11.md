---
title: "The Story of Huatiacuri"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 11
---

**1** Huatiacuri was the son of Pariacaca. Before his father's full emergence, Huatiacuri wandered the land as a poor man, eating only roasted potatoes, which is why he was called Huatiacuri, the potato eater.

**2** He came upon a great lord named Tamtañamca, who was terribly ill. No healer could cure him, despite his wealth and power.

**3** Huatiacuri overheard two foxes talking. One told the other the cause of the illness: Tamtañamca's wife had committed adultery, and two serpents were eating the house from within.

**4** Huatiacuri went to Tamtañamca and said: I can cure you, but you must give me your daughter in marriage. Tamtañamca agreed, and Huatiacuri revealed the wife's sin and killed the serpents. The lord was healed.

**5** But the daughter's husband was jealous and challenged Huatiacuri to a series of contests. Through the power inherited from Pariacaca, Huatiacuri won every contest, humiliating the rich man.

**6** This story teaches: the poor man who has the truth is more powerful than the rich man who lives a lie.
