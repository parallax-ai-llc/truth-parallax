---
title: "The Agricultural Calendar"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 22
---

**1** The agricultural calendar of Huarochiri was governed by the movements of the stars and the sun. The Pleiades marked the beginning of the planting season.

**2** When the Pleiades rose large and bright, it foretold a good harvest. When they rose small and dim, it foretold drought.

**3** The solstice was observed with great ceremony. At the June solstice, the sun reached its northernmost point and began its return. The people celebrated with feasting and sacrifice.

**4** The irrigation schedule followed the agricultural calendar. Water was released into the channels at the proper time, and the planting was done in a prescribed order: first the communal fields, then the fields of the huacas, then the private fields.

**5** Those who planted outside the proper time were punished. The agricultural calendar was sacred, and to violate it was to violate the order established by Pariacaca.
