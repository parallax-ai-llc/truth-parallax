---
title: "The Origins of the Peoples"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 10
---

**1** Each ayllu, each community, told the story of its own origin. Some said they emerged from caves, some from lakes, some from mountains.

**2** The people of Concha said they descended from a condor. The people of Checa said they descended from a puma. Each people had its own ancestor and its own huaca.

**3** When Pariacaca conquered the region, each people was assigned its territory. The boundaries were fixed by the gods and could not be changed without their permission.

**4** The system of irrigation was established according to the will of Pariacaca. Each community received water in turn, and the schedule was sacred. To steal another's water was a grave sin.

**5** The people lived by the order established by Pariacaca: farming their fields, tending their llamas, worshipping their huacas, and maintaining the ancient ceremonies.
