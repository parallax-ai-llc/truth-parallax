---
title: "The Story of the Lake of Yansa"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 23
---

**1** The Lake of Yansa was a sacred body of water near the village of Concha. It was said that a powerful huaca dwelt in its depths.

**2** The people of Concha made offerings to the lake at planting time and harvest time. They threw in gold, silver, coca, and sea shells.

**3** It is said that once a year, at midnight, a golden light could be seen rising from the lake. Those who saw it were blessed with good fortune.

**4** No one was permitted to fish in the lake or to wash in its waters. It was a sacred place, set apart from daily use.

**5** The story tells how a man once tried to drain the lake to find the treasures at the bottom. Pariacaca sent a flood that drowned the man and restored the lake to its original state.
