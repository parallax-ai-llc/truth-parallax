---
title: "The Enduring Traditions"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 31
---

**1** These stories were told by the elders of Huarochiri and written down by Francisco de Avila so that they might be preserved.

**2** Whether the reader believes these stories or not, they are the testimony of a people. They tell how the people understood the world, how they organized their society, how they related to the forces of nature.

**3** The huacas may be gone, the shrines may be destroyed, but the stories remain. And in the stories is the memory of a people who lived in these mountains for many generations.

**4** The water still flows from the springs of Pariacaca. The mountains still stand where the gods stood. The land remembers, even if the people are told to forget.

**5** This is the manuscript of Huarochiri, the most complete record of the beliefs of the Andean peoples before the coming of the Europeans. It is a treasure beyond price, and it must be preserved for all time.
