---
title: "The Customs of the Checa"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 26
---

**1** The Checa were one of the principal communities of Huarochiri. Their customs and traditions were particularly well preserved by their elders.

**2** The Checa traced their origin to a puma that emerged from the earth at the sacred site of Tutayquiri. From this puma descended the founding ancestors of the Checa people.

**3** The Checa had their own huaca, their own festivals, and their own customs, but they also participated in the worship of Pariacaca as the supreme huaca of the region.

**4** The elders of the Checa were the principal informants for this manuscript. They remembered the old stories and the old customs with great precision.

**5** The Checa prided themselves on their faithfulness to the ancient traditions. Even as the world changed around them, they preserved the memories of the past.
