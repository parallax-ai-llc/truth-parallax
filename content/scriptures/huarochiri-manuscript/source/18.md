---
title: "The Rituals of the Dead"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 18
---

**1** When a person died, the body was wrapped in fine cloth and placed in a sitting position. Food and chicha were placed beside the body.

**2** For five days the family mourned. They did not eat salt or chili. They did not wash or comb their hair. They wept and sang the songs of mourning.

**3** After five days, the body was carried to the burial place. In some communities, the dead were placed in caves; in others, in stone-lined tombs.

**4** The people believed that the dead continued to live in another world, similar to this one. They needed food and drink and clothing, just as in life.

**5** At the annual festival of the dead, the mummies of the ancestors were brought out and seated in the plaza. Food and chicha were offered to them, and the people danced and sang in their honor.
