---
title: "The Water Ceremonies"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 15
---

**1** The water ceremonies were the most important rituals of the year. Without water, the fields would not produce, and the people would starve.

**2** At the beginning of the irrigation season, the priest went to the source of the water channel and made offerings to the huaca of the spring.

**3** He sacrificed a guinea pig and sprinkled its blood on the stones. He poured chicha into the water and scattered coca leaves upon the ground.

**4** Then the water was released into the channels. The first water was considered sacred, and no one was allowed to bathe in it or wash clothing in it.

**5** The distribution of water followed the ancient schedule established by Pariacaca. Each community received its share in turn, and the schedule could not be altered without the consent of all.
