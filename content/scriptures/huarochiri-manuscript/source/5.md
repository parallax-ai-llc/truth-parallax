---
title: "The Birth of Pariacaca"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Now we shall tell how Pariacaca was born. In those days there was a huaca named Tamtañamca, who was very powerful. He had five eggs on the mountain called Condorcoto.

**2** From these five eggs, five falcons emerged. These falcons became five men. They were Pariacaca and his brothers.

**3** When they were born, they already knew everything that Tamtañamca and Huallallo Carhuincho had done. Huallallo Carhuincho was a fearsome god who demanded human sacrifice and ate human flesh.

**4** Pariacaca was angry at the wickedness of Huallallo Carhuincho. He resolved to drive him out and to establish a better order.

**5** But first, Pariacaca wanted to know the character of the people. He went among them disguised as a poor man, to see who was generous and who was cruel.
