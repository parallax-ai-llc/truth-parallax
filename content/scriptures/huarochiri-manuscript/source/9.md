---
title: "Chaupiñamca"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 9
---

**1** Now we shall tell of Chaupiñamca, a female huaca who was the sister of Pariacaca. She dwelt at Mama, and she had five sisters.

**2** Chaupiñamca was worshipped by the women. She had power over fertility and sexuality. At her festivals, the women danced lewdly and sang songs of desire.

**3** It is said that Chaupiñamca had many lovers among the huacas. She was powerful and beautiful, and many sought her favor.

**4** At her festival, the women competed to see who could dance the longest. The one who endured was considered blessed by Chaupiñamca.

**5** Chaupiñamca was also the guardian of the irrigation channels in her territory. She ensured that the water flowed to the fields of those who honored her.
