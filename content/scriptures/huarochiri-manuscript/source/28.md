---
title: "The Coming of the Inca"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 28
---

**1** When the Inca came to Huarochiri, they found a people already organized under their own huacas and their own customs.

**2** The Inca did not destroy the local huacas. Instead, they incorporated them into the Inca system. Pariacaca was recognized as a great huaca, second only to the Inca sun god, Inti.

**3** The Inca required the people of Huarochiri to contribute labor for imperial projects: road building, temple construction, and military service. But they allowed the people to continue their own worship.

**4** The Inca built a road through Huarochiri and established tambos, way-stations, along the route. They also built a temple to the sun, but it did not replace the shrine of Pariacaca.

**5** Under the Inca, the people of Huarochiri prospered. The irrigation systems were improved, new lands were brought under cultivation, and trade increased.
