---
title: "How Pariacaca's Children Spread Across the Land"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 14
---

**1** Pariacaca had five sons, and each went to a different region to establish the worship of their father.

**2** One son went to the north, one to the south, one to the east, and one to the west. The fifth remained at the sacred mountain to guard the shrine.

**3** Each son brought water to the region where he settled. They created springs and rivers, and they taught the people the proper ceremonies.

**4** In this way, the worship of Pariacaca spread across the entire region of Huarochiri, and every community acknowledged him as the supreme huaca.

**5** But each community also maintained the worship of its own local huaca, for the local huacas cared for the daily needs of the people, while Pariacaca watched over all from his mountain.
