---
title: "The Feast and the Flood"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 6
---

**1** Pariacaca, in the form of a poor man, came to a village where the people were holding a great feast. He sat among them, but no one offered him food or drink.

**2** Only one woman took pity on him. She brought him chicha to drink and food to eat. Pariacaca thanked her and said: In five days, something terrible will happen to this village. You must leave with your family and go to the high ground.

**3** Five days later, Pariacaca ascended to the mountain top. From there he sent down a great flood. Torrents of red and yellow water poured from the mountains.

**4** The flood swept away the village and all its people. Only the woman who had been kind to the poor man was saved, with her family.

**5** From the mountaintop, Pariacaca looked down at the destruction. He said: This is what happens to those who forget generosity, who despise the poor, who feast while others go hungry.

**6** Then Pariacaca went to confront Huallallo Carhuincho, to drive him out and to take his place as the supreme huaca of the region.
