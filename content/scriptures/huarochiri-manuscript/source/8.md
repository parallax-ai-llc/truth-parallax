---
title: "The Worship of Pariacaca"
scripture: "huarochiri-manuscript"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The people of Huarochiri worshipped Pariacaca at the great shrine on the mountain. Three times a year they made pilgrimage to his sacred site.

**2** They offered llamas, guinea pigs, chicha, coca leaves, and fine cloth. The priests performed the ceremonies according to the ancient rites.

**3** At the great festival, the people danced and sang for five days. They retold the stories of Pariacaca's birth, his conquest of Huallallo, and his gifts to the people.

**4** Pariacaca gave the people water. He caused the springs to flow and the irrigation channels to fill. Without his favor, the crops would wither and the animals would die.

**5** The people said: Pariacaca is our father. He gives us water and life. We must honor him always, or the water will cease to flow.
