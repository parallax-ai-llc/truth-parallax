Chapter 21 of the Huarochirí Manuscript — Chaupiñamca — The Female Deity — holds deep significance for contemporary indigenous communities connected to this tradition. Indigenous engagement with this chapter emphasizes its continuing relevance as living spiritual knowledge, its role in sustaining cultural identity and ceremonial practice, and its authority as a sacred text that communicates essential truths about the relationship between humans, the natural world, and the divine.

Indigenous readers and practitioners approach this chapter not as an artifact of the past but as a continuing source of guidance, identity, and spiritual power.
