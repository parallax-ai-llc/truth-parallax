Mark 4's parables are appreciated in Islamic tradition as examples of prophetic wisdom teaching. The Quran itself employs parables (*amthal*) extensively — 'God sets forth parables for people so that they may reflect' (Quran 14:25). The agricultural imagery of sowing and harvest resonates with Quranic metaphors of planting and divine provision.

The calming of the storm (4:35-41) is understood as a miracle demonstrating God's power exercised through his prophet. Muslim interpreters would not read the disciples' question — 'Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?' — as pointing toward Jesus' divinity but rather as recognizing the extraordinary prophetic authority granted to him by God. The miracles of nature attributed to prophets in Islamic tradition include Moses parting the sea and Solomon commanding the wind, establishing a pattern within which Jesus' mastery over nature fits naturally.
