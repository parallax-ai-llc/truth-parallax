Mark 2's controversies about forgiveness, Sabbath, and fasting are read by Muslim scholars as evidence of Jesus' prophetic authority to teach and reform religious practice. The claim to forgive sins (2:5-7) is understood not as a divine prerogative exercised by Jesus personally but as a declaration made by a prophet authorized to convey God's forgiveness — much as Muhammad declared God's mercy and forgiveness throughout the Quran.

The Sabbath disputes (2:23-28) are of interest because Islam does not observe a Sabbath rest. Friday (*Jumu'ah*) is the day of congregational prayer but not a day of rest in the Jewish or Christian sense. Muslim commentators see Jesus' flexible attitude toward Sabbath regulations as consistent with the Quranic critique that excessive legalism was imposed upon the Children of Israel as a punishment for their disobedience (Quran 4:160, 6:146).
