Mark 11's account of Jesus' entry into Jerusalem and the temple action is read by Muslim scholars as a prophetic challenge to corrupt religious authority. The cleansing of the temple (11:15-19) is understood as a prophet's righteous protest against the exploitation of worship for commercial gain — a theme resonant with Muhammad's own purification of the Kaaba from idols upon the conquest of Mecca.

The cursing of the fig tree (11:12-14, 20-25) is interpreted as a prophetic symbolic action — a warning to those who display outward religiosity without bearing spiritual fruit. This theme is well attested in the Quran, which warns against hypocrisy (*nifaq*) and empty ritual (Quran 2:8-20, 4:142, 107:4-7). Jesus' challenge to the religious authorities about the source of his authority (11:27-33) reflects the prophetic pattern of confrontation with established power that runs through Islamic salvation history.
