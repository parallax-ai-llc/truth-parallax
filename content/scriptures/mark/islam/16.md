Mark 16's resurrection narrative is interpreted by Islamic theology through the lens of the Quran's teaching that God raised Jesus to himself: 'God raised him up to Himself' (Quran 4:158). Islam affirms that Jesus is alive — not dead and risen, but taken up without dying. The empty tomb, in this reading, is evidence not of resurrection from death but of Jesus' bodily assumption into heaven.

The Longer Ending's account of Jesus appearing to his followers and then ascending to heaven (16:19) is partially consistent with the Islamic view: Jesus was indeed raised and will return at the end of time. However, Islam rejects the intervening narrative of death and resurrection. The commission to preach the gospel 'to all creation' (16:15) is understood by Muslim scholars as a mission that was legitimate for its time but has been superseded by the universal mission of Muhammad, the Seal of the Prophets (Quran 33:40), whose message is intended for all humanity until the Day of Judgment.
