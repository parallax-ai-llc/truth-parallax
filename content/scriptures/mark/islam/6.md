Mark 6's feeding of five thousand is recognized by Muslim scholars as a miracle with Quranic parallels. The disciples' request for a heavenly table is echoed in Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:112-115), where the disciples ask Isa for a table spread from heaven — 'O Jesus, Son of Mary, can your Lord send down to us a table from the sky?' God grants this request as a sign, but warns of punishment for those who disbelieve afterward.

The rejection at Nazareth (6:1-6) fits the Quranic pattern of prophetic rejection. The Quran repeatedly states that prophets were mocked and rejected by their own peoples (Quran 6:10, 15:11, 36:30). Jesus' statement that a prophet has no honor in his own country resonates with Islamic prophetology. The death of John the Baptist (6:17-29) at Herod's hands exemplifies the persecution of prophets — a theme central to the Quran's narrative of salvation history, where the Children of Israel are criticized for killing and mistreating their prophets (Quran 2:61, 3:21, 3:112).
