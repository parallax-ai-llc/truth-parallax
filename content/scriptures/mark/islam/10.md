Mark 10's teachings on marriage, children, and wealth resonate with Islamic values in several respects. Jesus' affirmation of the sanctity of marriage aligns with the Quran's teaching that God created spouses for mutual comfort and placed between them love and mercy (Quran 30:21). However, Islam permits divorce under regulated conditions and allows polygyny (up to four wives under strict conditions of justice — Quran 4:3), distinguishing it from the more restrictive position attributed to Jesus.

Jesus' welcoming of children (10:13-16) is consistent with the Islamic emphasis on compassion toward children and the prophetic traditions (*hadith*) in which Muhammad showed particular tenderness to children. The encounter with the rich man (10:17-31) reflects themes central to Islamic ethics: wealth is a trust (*amanah*) from God, and attachment to worldly possessions can impede spiritual progress. The Quran warns that 'your wealth and your children are but a trial' (Quran 64:15).
