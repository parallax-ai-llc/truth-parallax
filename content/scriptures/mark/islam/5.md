Mark 5's healing miracles — the Gerasene demoniac, the hemorrhaging woman, and the raising of Jairus' daughter — are understood in Islamic tradition as demonstrating the extraordinary *mu'jizat* granted to Isa. The Quran specifically affirms that Jesus raised the dead by God's permission (Quran 3:49, 5:110), making the Jairus narrative broadly consistent with Islamic belief.

The Gerasene demoniac's encounter with Jesus illustrates prophetic authority over the *jinn* — beings of smokeless fire whose existence is affirmed in the Quran (Quran 15:27, 55:15). Solomon's authority over the jinn (Quran 27:17, 34:12-14) provides the primary Islamic paradigm for prophetic mastery over spiritual beings. Muslim scholars would emphasize that this authority is delegated by God, not inherent in the prophet himself. The healing of the hemorrhaging woman through touching Jesus' garment does not imply divine power residing in Jesus but rather God's decision to channel healing through his chosen prophet.
