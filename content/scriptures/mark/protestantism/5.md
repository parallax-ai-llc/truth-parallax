Mark 5 presents three dramatic demonstrations of Jesus' power: over demons, disease, and death. The Gerasene demoniac (5:1-20) illustrates the comprehensive nature of Jesus' authority. Protestant missionaries have found in this passage a paradigm for spiritual liberation. The man's request to follow Jesus is refused — instead, he is sent to testify in his own region, a model for local witness.

The intertwined stories of Jairus' daughter and the hemorrhaging woman (5:21-43) display Mark's characteristic 'sandwich' technique. The woman's twelve years of suffering and the girl's twelve years of age are connected by more than coincidence. Protestant interpreters emphasize the role of faith: 'Daughter, your faith has made you well' (5:34) and 'Do not fear, only believe' (5:36). These passages have been central to Protestant discussions of the relationship between faith and healing, with cessationists and continuationists drawing different conclusions about their applicability today.
