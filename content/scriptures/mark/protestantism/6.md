Mark 6 narrates Jesus' rejection at Nazareth, the mission of the Twelve, the death of John the Baptist, and the feeding of five thousand. The Nazareth rejection (6:1-6) includes the striking statement that Jesus 'could do no mighty work there' because of their unbelief — Protestant interpreters debate whether this reflects a genuine limitation or a sovereign decision not to act where faith is absent.

The feeding of the five thousand (6:30-44) is the only miracle recorded in all four Gospels. While Catholic and Orthodox interpreters read strong Eucharistic symbolism, Protestant commentators tend to emphasize the literal miracle as evidence of Jesus' messianic identity, echoing Moses and the manna. Jesus' walking on the water (6:45-52) and the disciples' hardened hearts (6:52) continue the theme of the disciples' persistent failure to understand — a motif that Protestant interpreters read as both a historical observation and a pastoral warning about the difficulty of faith.
