## Overview

Protestant interpretation of Mark has been profoundly shaped by the modern recognition of Markan priority — the scholarly consensus that Mark was the first Gospel written and served as a source for Matthew and Luke. This insight, emerging from German Protestant scholarship in the nineteenth century, gave Mark a new prominence after centuries of relative neglect. Luther himself had ranked Mark below Matthew and John, but subsequent Protestant scholarship elevated Mark as the earliest witness to Jesus' ministry.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Redaction Criticism
Protestant scholars like Willi Marxsen pioneered redaction criticism of Mark, examining the evangelist's theological purposes in selecting and arranging material. Mark's emphasis on the cross and suffering was seen as addressing a community facing persecution, possibly in Rome during or after the Neronian period.

### Narrative Theology
More recent Protestant interpreters read Mark as a carefully crafted narrative, attending to its literary devices — the intercalation ("sandwich") technique, irony, the motif of misunderstanding — as carriers of theological meaning.

### Evangelical Approaches
Conservative Protestant scholars emphasize Mark's historical reliability, treating the Gospel as an eyewitness-based account rooted in Petrine testimony, following the patristic tradition recorded by Papias.

## Theological Emphases

- **Theology of the Cross**: Mark's portrayal of a suffering Messiah resonates deeply with Luther's *theologia crucis*
- **Discipleship and Failure**: The disciples' repeated failure offers both warning and comfort — grace is available even to those who abandon Jesus
- **The Messianic Secret**: Wrede's theory that Jesus commanded secrecy about his identity raised questions about the relationship between the historical Jesus and the Christ of faith
- **The Ending of Mark**: The abrupt ending at 16:8 (the women flee in fear) has generated extensive debate about whether the original ending was lost or whether Mark intentionally ended with fearful silence
