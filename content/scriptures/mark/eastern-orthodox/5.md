Mark 5 reveals Christ's power over the three great enemies of humanity: demonic possession, chronic illness, and death itself. The Gerasene demoniac (5:1-20) provides a dramatic account of liberation from demonic bondage. Orthodox theology reads this as a paradigm of Christ's saving work: the demoniac is restored to his right mind, clothed, and sent to proclaim what God has done — an image of baptismal transformation.

The healing of the hemorrhaging woman (5:25-34) and the raising of Jairus' daughter (5:35-43) display the life-giving power of Christ that flows even through physical contact. Orthodox sacramental theology draws on such passages to affirm that God works through material means — water, oil, bread, wine, and the touch of the faithful. The raising of the young girl with the words 'Talitha koum' anticipates the general resurrection, when Christ will call all the dead to rise.
