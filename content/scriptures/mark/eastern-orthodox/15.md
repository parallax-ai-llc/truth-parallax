Great Friday in the Orthodox Church is a day of fasting, mourning, and solemn liturgical commemoration. Mark 15 narrates the events that are reenacted liturgically: the trial before Pilate, the scourging, the way of the cross, and the crucifixion. The Royal Hours of Great Friday include readings from all four Gospels, placing Mark's account within the full canonical testimony.

The cry of dereliction (15:34) is understood not as the Father abandoning the Son but as Christ descending into the depths of human suffering and God-forsakenness in order to fill all things with his presence. The tearing of the temple veil (15:38) signals the end of the separation between God and humanity — the way to the Holy of Holies is now open through Christ. The burial by Joseph of Arimathea is commemorated in the Epitaphios procession of Great Friday evening, when the icon of the burial shroud is carried through the streets.
