Mark 10 addresses marriage, children, wealth, and sacrificial service. Jesus' teaching on the indissolubility of marriage (10:2-12) is upheld in Orthodox canon law, though the Orthodox Church, following the principle of *oikonomia* (pastoral economy), permits up to three marriages, with the second and third accompanied by penitential rites. This approach balances the ideal of indissolubility with the pastoral reality of human brokenness.

The blessing of children (10:13-16) supports the Orthodox practice of baptizing and communing infants — the kingdom belongs to such as these, and therefore they are not excluded from the Church's sacramental life. The encounter with the rich young man (10:17-31) has inspired the monastic vocation in Orthodoxy. St. Anthony the Great heard this passage read in church and immediately gave away his possessions, inaugurating the monastic movement. The teaching on service (10:42-45) — 'the Son of Man came not to be served but to serve' — defines the Orthodox understanding of hierarchical authority as diakonia.
