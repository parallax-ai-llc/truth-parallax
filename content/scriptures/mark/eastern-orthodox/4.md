Orthodox interpretation of Mark 4 attends to the parables as revelations of the Kingdom of God already present in the Church. The Parable of the Sower (4:1-20) is read in light of the liturgical proclamation of the Word — the seed is sown in every Divine Liturgy through the reading of Scripture and the homily. The different soils represent the varying dispositions of the faithful, and the call is to cultivate the heart through prayer, fasting, and the sacraments.

The calming of the storm (4:35-41) has deep resonance in Orthodox iconography and hymnography. The boat on the sea is an ancient image of the Church navigating the turbulent waters of history. Christ's presence in the boat — even when apparently asleep — assures the faithful that the Church will not be overwhelmed. The disciples' question, 'Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?' (4:41), invites the reader into the mystery of Christ's divine-human identity.
