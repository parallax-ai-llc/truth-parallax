The resurrection of Christ — Pascha — is the central event of the Orthodox liturgical year and the foundation of all Orthodox theology. Mark 16 narrates the discovery of the empty tomb by the myrrh-bearing women, who came to anoint Jesus' body. The young man's proclamation — 'He has risen; he is not here' (16:6) — is the Paschal announcement that resounds through the midnight Paschal Liturgy: 'Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life.'

The myrrh-bearing women are honored on the second Sunday after Pascha. Their faithfulness contrasts with the disciples' absence and models the courage of faith. The instruction to tell the disciples 'and Peter' (16:7) reveals the mercy of the risen Christ, who restores even those who denied him. Orthodox tradition accepts the Longer Ending (16:9-20) as canonical Scripture, and the Great Commission (16:15-16) grounds the Church's missionary imperative.
