Mark 14 contains the events of Holy Thursday and the beginning of Great Friday in the Orthodox liturgical cycle. The anointing at Bethany (14:3-9) is commemorated on Holy Wednesday, when the Church sings of the sinful woman who anointed Christ. The Mystical Supper (14:22-25) is the institution of the Eucharist — the central act of Orthodox worship. The words 'This is my body' and 'This is my blood' are understood as effecting a real transformation of the bread and wine into the Body and Blood of Christ, though Orthodoxy does not employ the Western term 'transubstantiation.'

The agony in Gethsemane (14:32-42) reveals the full humanity of Christ — his fear, sorrow, and submission to the Father's will. Orthodox theology emphasizes that Christ's human will is real and free, as defined by the Third Council of Constantinople (681). Peter's denial (14:66-72) is a paradigm of repentance: his bitter weeping leads to restoration, modeling the sacrament of Confession.
