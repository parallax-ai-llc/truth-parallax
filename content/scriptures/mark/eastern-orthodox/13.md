Orthodox interpretation of Mark 13 is eschatological but not speculative. The Orthodox Church teaches that the precise timing of Christ's return is unknown and unknowable, and it resists the detailed end-times chronologies that characterize some Western traditions. The discourse is read as a call to vigilance, faithfulness, and patient endurance rather than as a blueprint for calculating dates.

The 'abomination of desolation' (13:14) is understood historically as referring to the Roman destruction of the temple, while also pointing to the final apostasy that will precede Christ's Second Coming. The Orthodox Creed affirms that Christ 'will come again in glory to judge the living and the dead,' and this expectation shapes the entire liturgical life of the Church. The exhortation to watch (13:33-37) is embodied in the Orthodox practice of vigil services, particularly the Paschal Vigil.
