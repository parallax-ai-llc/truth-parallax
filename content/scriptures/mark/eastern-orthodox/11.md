Mark 11 narrates the triumphal entry into Jerusalem, commemorated on Palm Sunday with the distribution of palms and the procession of the faithful. Orthodox liturgical texts interpret the entry as the coming of the King to his city and his temple — a royal Christological event that simultaneously announces the passion. The children's cries of 'Hosanna' are echoed in the Cherubic Hymn of the Divine Liturgy.

The cursing of the fig tree (11:12-14, 20-25) is read as a prophetic judgment on fruitless worship — a warning that applies to every generation of believers. The cleansing of the temple (11:15-19) signals the end of the old sacrificial order and its replacement by the sacrifice of Christ. Orthodox theology sees the Eucharist as the fulfillment of all temple worship, offered not in a single location but throughout the world wherever the Divine Liturgy is celebrated.
