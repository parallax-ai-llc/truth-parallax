Mark 12 contains teachings that have shaped Orthodox theology and ethics. The Parable of the Wicked Tenants (12:1-12) is understood as tracing the history of salvation from the prophets to Christ, with the cornerstone rejected by the builders becoming the foundation of the Church. Orthodox hymnography for Holy Week draws extensively on this parable.

The dialogue about the greatest commandment (12:28-34) establishes the foundation of Orthodox moral theology: love of God and love of neighbor are inseparable. The widow's offering (12:41-44) embodies the Orthodox understanding of sacrificial giving — it is not the amount but the disposition of the heart that matters before God. The Fathers teach that true worship requires the offering of one's whole self, not merely surplus resources.
