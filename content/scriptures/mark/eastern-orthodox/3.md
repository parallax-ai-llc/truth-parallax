Mark 3 presents the formation of the Twelve and the intensification of opposition. The appointment of the apostles (3:13-19) is understood in Orthodox ecclesiology as the foundation of apostolic succession — the unbroken chain of ordination from the apostles through the bishops to the present day. The Twelve represent the reconstitution of the twelve tribes, signaling the renewal of the people of God.

The Beelzebul controversy (3:22-30) reveals the cosmic dimension of Christ's ministry — the binding of the strong man and the plundering of his house describe the overthrow of demonic powers. Orthodox theology, following the Fathers, sees the incarnation itself as the decisive moment in this cosmic battle. The teaching about the true family of Jesus (3:31-35) is handled carefully in Orthodox tradition, which honors the Theotokos (Mary, the God-bearer) as the supreme example of doing the will of God.
