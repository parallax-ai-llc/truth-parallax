Mark 7 addresses inner purity versus external observance. Orthodox interpretation distinguishes between the human traditions Jesus criticizes (the Corban practice that circumvented the commandment to honor parents) and the Holy Tradition of the Church, which is the faithful transmission of apostolic teaching guided by the Holy Spirit through the Ecumenical Councils and the consensus of the Fathers.

The healing of the Syrophoenician woman's daughter (7:24-30) demonstrates that the grace of God extends beyond ethnic boundaries. The healing of the deaf and mute man (7:31-37) with the word 'Ephphatha' is incorporated into the Orthodox baptismal rite. The opening of ears and tongue symbolizes the spiritual illumination that comes through baptism and chrismation — the newly baptized person is enabled to hear the Word of God and confess the faith.
