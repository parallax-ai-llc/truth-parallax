Mark 2 presents controversies that Orthodox interpreters read through the lens of Christ's divine authority exercised within the economy of salvation. The forgiveness of the paralytic's sins (2:1-12) demonstrates that the Son of Man possesses divine authority on earth — a power that the Orthodox Church understands as transmitted through the apostles to the bishops and priests in the sacrament of Confession.

The Sabbath controversies reveal Christ as Lord of the Sabbath, not abolishing the law but fulfilling it. Orthodox theology maintains that the Sabbath rest finds its fulfillment in the Lord's Day — Sunday, the day of the resurrection — which becomes the primary day of worship. The image of the bridegroom (2:19-20) resonates in Orthodox hymnography, particularly the Bridegroom Matins services of Holy Week, where Christ comes as the bridegroom of the Church.
