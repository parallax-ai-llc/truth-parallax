The Transfiguration (9:2-8) is among the most theologically significant events in Orthodox Christianity, celebrated as a great feast on August 6. The uncreated light that shone from Christ on Mount Tabor is central to Orthodox theology, particularly the Palamite teaching that the light of the Transfiguration is the uncreated energy of God — not a created symbol but a real participation in the divine glory. St. Gregory Palamas defended this teaching against Barlaam of Calabria in the fourteenth century, and the Councils of 1341 and 1351 vindicated his position.

The presence of Moses and Elijah represents the Law and the Prophets bearing witness to Christ. The divine voice — 'This is my beloved Son; listen to him' — confirms the Christological dogma. The healing of the epileptic boy (9:14-29) reveals the necessity of prayer and fasting for spiritual combat. The teaching on humility (9:33-37) and the warning against scandal (9:42-48) shape Orthodox monastic and ethical teaching.
