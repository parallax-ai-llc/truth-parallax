Mark 6 contains the feeding of the five thousand, which Orthodox interpreters read as a Eucharistic anticipation. The actions of Jesus — taking, blessing, breaking, giving — mirror the Eucharistic liturgy. St. John Chrysostom drew explicit parallels between this miracle and the Divine Liturgy, where Christ continues to multiply his body and blood for the nourishment of the faithful.

The rejection at Nazareth (6:1-6) illustrates the mystery of human freedom to resist grace. Jesus' walking on the water (6:45-52) reveals his divine nature — in the Old Testament, it is God alone who walks on the sea (Job 9:8). The disciples' hardened hearts (6:52) are a warning against spiritual complacency. Orthodox ascetical theology teaches that even those closest to Christ can fail to perceive his glory without the purification of the heart through prayer and repentance.
