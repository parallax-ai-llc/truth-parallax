Catholic interpretation of Mark 4 emphasizes the ecclesial dimension of the parables. The Parable of the Sower (4:1-20) is read as describing the Church's mission of evangelization — the Word is sown through preaching, catechesis, and the sacraments, and its fruitfulness depends on the disposition of the hearers. The Second Vatican Council's decree *Ad Gentes* draws on this imagery for the theology of mission.

The parables of the seed growing secretly (4:26-29) and the mustard seed (4:30-32) illuminate the Catholic understanding of the Kingdom of God as already present in the Church yet still growing toward its eschatological fullness. The calming of the storm (4:35-41) has been read ecclesiologically since the Fathers: the boat is the Church, the storm is persecution, and Christ's presence guarantees that the Church will not be overcome.
