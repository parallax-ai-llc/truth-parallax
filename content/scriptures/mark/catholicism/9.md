The Transfiguration (9:2-8) is one of the great feasts of the Catholic liturgical calendar, celebrated on August 6. The appearance of Moses and Elijah alongside the glorified Christ is understood as representing the Law and the Prophets bearing witness to Christ. Peter's suggestion to build three tents (9:5) is read as a well-intentioned but misguided attempt to domesticate the divine encounter — the voice from the cloud redirects attention to Christ alone.

The healing of the epileptic boy (9:14-29) and Jesus' statement that 'this kind cannot be driven out by anything but prayer' (9:29, with some manuscripts adding 'and fasting') has been important for Catholic ascetical theology. The chapter's teaching on humility (9:33-37) and the seriousness of scandal (9:42-48) shapes Catholic moral theology, particularly the obligation to avoid leading others into sin.
