Mark 2 presents five controversy stories that Catholic interpreters read as revealing Jesus' divine authority, which he transmits to the Church. The healing of the paralytic (2:1-12) is particularly significant: Jesus' declaration 'your sins are forgiven' grounds the Catholic sacrament of Reconciliation, affirming that the power to forgive sins has been given to the Church through apostolic succession.

The call of Levi (2:13-17) illustrates Christ's outreach to sinners, a pattern continued in the Church's mission. The Sabbath controversies demonstrate that Jesus, as Lord of the Sabbath, has authority to reorder religious practice — an authority Catholic theology sees as exercised through the Magisterium. The image of the bridegroom (2:19-20) connects to the nuptial theology of Christ and the Church developed by the Fathers and reiterated in Ephesians 5.
