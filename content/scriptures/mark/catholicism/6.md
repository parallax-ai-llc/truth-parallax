Mark 6 contains the feeding of the five thousand (6:30-44), which Catholic tradition reads as a Eucharistic anticipation. Jesus takes bread, blesses, breaks, and gives — the four Eucharistic actions that will be repeated at the Last Supper (14:22). Pope Benedict XVI drew attention to this passage as revealing the Eucharistic structure of Jesus' entire ministry. The setting in the wilderness echoes the manna, connecting the Eucharist to the entire salvation history of Israel.

The rejection at Nazareth (6:1-6) includes the reference to Jesus as 'the son of Mary' and mentions his 'brothers and sisters.' Catholic interpretation, following Jerome and the broader patristic tradition, understands *adelphoi* as referring to cousins or close relatives, preserving the doctrine of Mary's perpetual virginity. The sending of the Twelve (6:7-13) with authority over unclean spirits and instructions for itinerant ministry provides a model for the apostolic mission of the Church.
