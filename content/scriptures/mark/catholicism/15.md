Mark 15 narrates the trial, crucifixion, death, and burial of Jesus — the events commemorated throughout Holy Week in Catholic liturgy. The Stations of the Cross, though drawing on multiple Gospel accounts, find much of their scriptural basis in Mark's spare, powerful narrative. The crucifixion account is read during the Liturgy of the Passion on Good Friday.

The cry of dereliction (15:34) is understood in Catholic theology not as the Father actually abandoning the Son but as Christ's experience of the full weight of human sin and separation from God. The tearing of the temple veil (15:38) signifies the opening of access to God through Christ's sacrifice — the foundation of the new priesthood and the new worship. The centurion's confession (15:39) demonstrates that faith in Christ is available to all peoples. Joseph of Arimathea's burial of Jesus (15:42-47) establishes the reality of Christ's death, a necessary precondition for the truth of the resurrection.
