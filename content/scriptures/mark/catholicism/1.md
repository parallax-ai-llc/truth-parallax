Catholic interpretation of Mark 1 emphasizes the continuity between John the Baptist's ministry and the sacramental life of the Church. Jesus' baptism in the Jordan is understood not as a personal need for purification but as the sanctification of the waters themselves — a theme echoed in the Blessing of Waters at Epiphany. The descent of the Spirit and the Father's voice constitute a Trinitarian theophany, the revelation of the three divine Persons that grounds Catholic Trinitarian dogma.

The calling of the first disciples (1:16-20) establishes the pattern of apostolic vocation that the Catholic Church sees as the foundation of its ordained ministry. Peter's prominence from the outset — called first, his house serving as Jesus' base of operations — supports the Catholic understanding of Petrine primacy. The exorcisms and healings demonstrate Christ's authority over evil, a power that the Church continues to exercise through the sacrament of anointing and the ministry of exorcism.
