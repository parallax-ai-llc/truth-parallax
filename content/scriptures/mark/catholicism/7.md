Mark 7 addresses the relationship between divine commandment and human tradition — a passage that requires careful handling in Catholic interpretation, given the Catholic emphasis on Tradition alongside Scripture. Catholic scholars distinguish between the 'traditions of the elders' criticized by Jesus (which were human additions that contradicted God's will) and the Sacred Tradition of the Church (which faithfully transmits apostolic teaching under the guidance of the Holy Spirit).

The declaration about clean and unclean foods (7:19) is understood as Jesus exercising his messianic authority to fulfill and transcend the ceremonial law. The encounter with the Syrophoenician woman (7:24-30) reveals the universal scope of God's salvation, extending beyond Israel to the Gentiles. Catholic missiology sees in this passage a foreshadowing of the Church's universal mission. The healing of the deaf man with the word 'Ephphatha' (7:31-37) is incorporated into the baptismal liturgy, where the celebrant touches the ears and mouth of the candidate, praying that they may hear and proclaim the Gospel.
