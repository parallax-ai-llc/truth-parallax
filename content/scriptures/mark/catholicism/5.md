Mark 5 presents three mighty acts that Catholic interpreters understand as prefiguring the sacramental ministry of the Church. The Gerasene demoniac (5:1-20) illustrates Christ's power over evil, continued in the Church's rite of exorcism. The restoration of the possessed man to community and right mind anticipates the reconciliation effected through the sacrament of Penance.

The hemorrhaging woman (5:25-34) touches Jesus' garment and is healed — Catholic theology sees in this a prototype of sacramental contact, the healing power that flows through material mediation. The raising of Jairus' daughter (5:35-43) demonstrates Christ's power over death. The command 'Talitha koum' is preserved in Aramaic, lending the narrative an eyewitness quality that Catholic tradition connects to Petrine testimony transmitted through Mark.
