Mark 10 addresses the sacrament of marriage, the dignity of children, evangelical poverty, and sacrificial service. Jesus' teaching on divorce (10:2-12) is foundational for the Catholic doctrine of the indissolubility of marriage: 'What God has joined together, let not man separate' (10:9). This teaching, upheld by the Council of Trent, means that a valid sacramental marriage between baptized persons cannot be dissolved by any human authority.

The blessing of children (10:13-16) supports the Catholic practice of infant baptism: if children can receive the kingdom, they can receive its sacramental sign. The encounter with the rich young man (10:17-31) has inspired the evangelical counsels — poverty, chastity, and obedience — that define consecrated religious life in the Catholic Church. Jesus' promise that those who leave everything for his sake will receive a hundredfold 'now in this time' (10:30) has been read as describing the richness of community life in religious orders.
