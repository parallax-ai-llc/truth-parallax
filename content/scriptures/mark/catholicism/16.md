Mark 16 presents the empty tomb and, in the Longer Ending (16:9-20), resurrection appearances and the Great Commission. Catholic biblical scholarship, following the Pontifical Biblical Commission and modern text criticism, acknowledges that the Longer Ending was likely not part of Mark's original composition. Nevertheless, the Council of Trent included Mark 16:9-20 in the canonical text, and these verses are read in the liturgy.

The women at the tomb — Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James, and Salome — are the first witnesses to the resurrection. Catholic tradition honors these women as models of faithful discipleship. The young man's instruction to tell the disciples 'and Peter' (16:7) singles out Peter for special attention, which Catholic interpreters connect to his future role as head of the apostolic community. The Longer Ending's commission to 'go into all the world and proclaim the gospel to the whole creation' (16:15) has served as a missionary mandate throughout Catholic history.
