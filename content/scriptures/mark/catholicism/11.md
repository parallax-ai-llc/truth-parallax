Mark 11 narrates Jesus' entry into Jerusalem, celebrated on Palm Sunday in the Catholic liturgical year. The procession with palms, the cries of 'Hosanna,' and the messianic symbolism of the colt are reenacted in parish liturgies worldwide. Catholic interpreters read the triumphal entry as the coming of the King to his temple — a royal and priestly act that inaugurates the events of Holy Week.

The cleansing of the temple (11:15-19) is understood as a prophetic action declaring the end of the old sacrificial system and its replacement by the sacrifice of Christ. The fig tree's withering (11:20-25) leads to teaching on faith and prayer, including the requirement of forgiveness as a condition for prayer. Catholic moral theology connects this to the sacrament of Reconciliation: one cannot approach God in prayer while harboring unforgiveness.
