Catholic interpretation of Mark 13 is governed by the principle articulated in the Catechism: the Church's eschatology is oriented toward the Second Coming of Christ while acknowledging that the precise time and manner remain unknown. The discourse is read as addressing both the historical destruction of Jerusalem (70 CE) and the final events preceding Christ's return.

The 'abomination of desolation' (13:14) is interpreted variously as the Roman destruction of the temple and as a future manifestation of the Antichrist. Catholic eschatology, shaped by the Catechism (nn. 675-677), teaches that the Church must pass through a final trial before Christ's return. The exhortation to watchfulness (13:33-37) grounds the Catholic emphasis on readiness through the sacramental life — regular participation in the Eucharist, frequent confession, and prayer as the means of staying spiritually alert.
