Mark 14 contains events of supreme sacramental and devotional significance for Catholic faith. The anointing at Bethany (14:3-9) inspires both the theology of extravagant worship and the understanding that devotion to Christ transcends pragmatic calculation. The institution of the Eucharist (14:22-25) — 'This is my body... This is my blood of the covenant' — is the scriptural foundation for the Catholic doctrine of transubstantiation, defined at the Fourth Lateran Council (1215) and reaffirmed at Trent.

Gethsemane (14:32-42) reveals the fully human dimension of Christ's suffering. Catholic spirituality has developed rich traditions of meditating on the agony in the garden — the Sorrowful Mysteries of the Rosary, the Holy Hour devotion. Peter's denial (14:66-72) is read as demonstrating both human frailty and the possibility of repentance. The Catholic tradition emphasizes that Peter's restoration (implied in the young man's message at the tomb, 16:7) models the sacrament of Reconciliation.
