Mark 7 is one of the most intensely debated chapters in Jewish-Christian dialogue. The dispute over handwashing (7:1-13) concerns the Pharisaic practice of ritual handwashing before meals — a practice not required by the Torah but developed as an extension of priestly purity to everyday life. Jewish scholars emphasize that this was a matter of ongoing debate within Judaism itself; Jesus' critique resembles positions found within other Jewish groups.

The Corban controversy (7:9-13) involves a genuine halakhic issue: can a vow dedicating property to the Temple override the obligation to support one's parents? Rabbinic literature records similar debates (m. Nedarim 9:1), and the Rabbis ultimately agreed with the position attributed to Jesus — parental obligations take precedence. Mark's editorial comment that Jesus 'declared all foods clean' (7:19) is read by Jewish scholars as a later Christian interpretation imposed on an intra-Jewish dispute about the scope of purity law.
