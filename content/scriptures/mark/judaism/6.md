Mark 6 provides important evidence for the social world of Galilean Judaism. The rejection at Nazareth (6:1-6) reflects the proverb about a prophet without honor in his own country — a motif found in Jewish tradition (cf. Jeremiah's reception). The reference to Jesus as 'the carpenter' and 'the son of Mary' provides rare data about his social status. Jewish scholars note that artisan status placed Jesus in the lower-middle class of Galilean society.

The death of John the Baptist (6:17-29) at the hands of Herod Antipas is confirmed by Josephus (*Antiquities* 18.5.2), who attributes John's execution to Herod's fear of his political influence. The feeding of five thousand (6:30-44) evokes the manna tradition and Elisha's multiplication miracle (2 Kings 4:42-44). Jewish scholars read this as Jesus positioning himself within the prophetic tradition of Israel, not outside it.
