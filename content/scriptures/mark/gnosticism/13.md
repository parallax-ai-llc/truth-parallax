Mark 13's apocalyptic discourse is read by Gnostic interpreters as describing the dissolution of the Demiurge's cosmos. The destruction of the temple (13:1-2) signals the end of the Demiurge's worship system. The cosmic signs — the sun darkened, the moon not giving light, the stars falling (13:24-25) — describe the collapse of the archonic spheres that form the prison of material existence.

The 'abomination of desolation' (13:14) represents the ultimate manifestation of archonic power before its overthrow. The appearance of false christs and false prophets (13:21-22) describes the Demiurge's final attempt to deceive the elect through counterfeit spiritual authority. The coming of the Son of Man 'in clouds with great power and glory' (13:26) represents the final revelation of the Pleroma, breaking through the cosmic barriers permanently. The call to watchfulness (13:33-37) is addressed to the pneumatics who must remain alert to the movements of gnosis and avoid falling back into the sleep of ignorance.
