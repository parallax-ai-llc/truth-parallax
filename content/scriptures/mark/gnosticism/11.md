Mark 11's temple actions are interpreted by Gnostic readers as a direct assault on the Demiurge's central institution. The temple is the house of the Demiurge — the place where the god of this world receives worship. Jesus' cleansing of the temple (11:15-19) is not a reform but a symbolic destruction, anticipating the ultimate dissolution of the Demiurge's order. The accusation that it has become 'a den of robbers' refers to the archons who steal worship from the true God by directing it to the Demiurge.

The cursing of the fig tree (11:12-14, 20-25) symbolizes the barrenness of the Demiurge's religious system — it produces leaves (outward observance) but no fruit (gnosis). The withering of the tree represents the eschatological judgment on the entire cosmic order established by the ignorant creator. The teaching on faith that can move mountains (11:23) is read as a promise that gnosis has the power to dismantle the material cosmos itself.
