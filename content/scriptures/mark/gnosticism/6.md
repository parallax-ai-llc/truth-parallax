Mark 6's rejection at Nazareth (6:1-6) illustrates the Gnostic theme that the bearer of gnosis is rejected by the world of ignorance. The people of Nazareth judge Jesus by his material identity — his family, his trade — unable to perceive the heavenly being concealed within the human form. This is the fundamental error of the hylic and psychic types: they perceive only surfaces, not the hidden spiritual reality.

The feeding of five thousand (6:30-44) is understood as the multiplication of gnosis — spiritual nourishment that satisfies without being depleted. The walking on water (6:45-52) reveals Jesus' transcendence over the material elements. The disciples' failure to understand — their hearts were 'hardened' (6:52) — illustrates the difficulty of receiving gnosis even for those within the inner circle. Gnostic interpreters saw this as evidence that the Twelve themselves required further initiation beyond what is recorded in the canonical Gospels.
