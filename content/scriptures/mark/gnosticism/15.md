The crucifixion narrative in Mark 15 is the passage most thoroughly reinterpreted by Gnostic readers. Docetic Christology holds that the heavenly Christ did not truly suffer — the material body of Jesus may have hung on the cross, but the divine being had already departed. The cry of dereliction — 'My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?' (15:34) — is read as the moment of separation: the earthly Jesus cries out because the heavenly Christ has withdrawn, leaving only the material shell.

The tearing of the temple veil (15:38) is the single most important detail for Gnostic interpreters of the passion. The veil separating the Holy of Holies from the outer temple represents the cosmic barrier between the Pleroma and the material world. Its tearing 'from top to bottom' signals that the barrier has been permanently breached — not by the Demiurge but by the true God. The centurion's confession (15:39) is ironic: a servant of the archonic power (Rome) inadvertently speaks the truth about the heavenly being who has just escaped the material world through apparent death.
