Mark 10 presents teachings on marriage, wealth, and social status that Gnostic interpreters read through the lens of their cosmology. The teaching on marriage (10:2-12) was received differently by different Gnostic groups: ascetic Gnostics (like the Encratites) rejected marriage as participation in the Demiurge's reproductive scheme, while libertine Gnostics (like some Carpocratians) treated all material institutions, including marriage, as morally indifferent.

The blessing of children (10:13-16) — 'unless you become like a child' — is interpreted as a call to recover the original innocence of the divine spark before its entanglement in the material world. The rich man's refusal to part with his possessions (10:17-22) illustrates the psychic type's bondage to the material world. The healing of blind Bartimaeus (10:46-52) represents the opening of spiritual sight — the transition from ignorance to gnosis. His casting away of his garment (10:50) symbolizes the shedding of the material body that blinds the divine spark.
