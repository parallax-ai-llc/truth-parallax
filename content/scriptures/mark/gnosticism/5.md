Gnostic interpreters find rich material in Mark 5's three demonstrations of power. The Gerasene demoniac (5:1-20) harbors a 'Legion' of demons — the term itself suggests the archonic armies that populate the Gnostic cosmos. The demons' recognition of Jesus as 'Son of the Most High God' (5:7) reveals their awareness that a being from beyond the material cosmos has entered their domain. Their destruction in the sea represents the ultimate fate of the archons.

The hemorrhaging woman (5:25-34) is read through the lens of Valentinian theology as representing Sophia, who suffered for twelve Aeons before being healed by contact with the Savior. This interpretation is attributed to Marcus the Valentinian by Irenaeus (*Against Heresies* 1.3.3). The raising of Jairus' daughter (5:35-43) demonstrates the power of gnosis to awaken those who are spiritually dead — 'she is not dead but sleeping' (5:39) describes the condition of the divine spark trapped in matter, awaiting the call to awakening.
