Mark 4 is the most important chapter for Gnostic interpretation of Mark, because it explicitly distinguishes between public parables and private mystery. The statement 'To you has been given the secret of the kingdom of God, but for those outside everything is in parables' (4:11) is the foundational proof text for Gnostic claims of esoteric apostolic tradition. The seed parables describe the dissemination of gnosis in a hostile world ruled by archonic powers.

The Parable of the Sower (4:1-20) is read as describing the different responses to gnosis: the path represents the hylic (material) people from whom the archons steal all spiritual awareness; the rocky ground represents the psychics who receive gnosis with joy but lack the depth to sustain it; the thorns represent those entangled in material concerns; and the good soil represents the pneumatics who receive and bear fruit from the divine seed. The calming of the storm (4:35-41) demonstrates the heavenly Christ's mastery over the elements — the wind and sea, which are under the archons' dominion, obey the voice from the Pleroma.
