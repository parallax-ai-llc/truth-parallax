Mark 16's empty tomb is the ultimate vindication of the Gnostic reading. The tomb is empty because the material body has been dissolved — the divine spark has returned to the Pleroma. The 'young man dressed in a white robe' (16:5) is not merely an angel but an emissary from the realm of light, wearing the garment of glory that characterizes beings of the Pleroma.

The abrupt ending at 16:8 — 'they said nothing to anyone, for they were afraid' — is profoundly significant for Gnostic interpreters. The women's silence explains why the deepest truths of the Gospel were not publicly transmitted but preserved in secret tradition. This is the origin point of the esoteric lineage that Gnostic teachers claimed: the true Gospel was not the public proclamation but the hidden teaching transmitted privately from the risen Christ to select disciples. The so-called Secret Gospel of Mark, whatever its authenticity, represents precisely this claim — that the canonical text is only the outer shell of a deeper, secret Gospel accessible only to the initiated.
