## The Necessity of Ongoing Reform

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah 13 reads the backsliding and re-reform as evidence that the Church is *semper reformanda* — always in need of renewal. The Council of Trent and the Second Vatican Council both represent the Catholic Church's commitment to ongoing self-examination and reform.

## Key Themes

The return of abuses during Nehemiah's absence illustrates that reform is not a one-time event but an ongoing process. Catholic tradition recognizes that every generation must receive, live, and transmit the faith anew. The expulsion of Tobiah from the temple (13:4-9) reflects the Catholic practice of maintaining the integrity of sacred spaces.

## Theological Significance

- **Semper reformanda**: The Church constantly needs renewal
- **Vigilance**: Reform requires sustained attention and enforcement
- **Sacred space**: The temple's integrity must be maintained
- **Recurring challenges**: Each generation faces similar temptations
