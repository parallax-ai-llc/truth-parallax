## Discernment Against Deception

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah 6 highlights the virtue of discernment — the ability to distinguish true from false spiritual counsel. Nehemiah's resistance to manipulation by false prophets models the Catholic principle of *discretio spirituum* (discernment of spirits).

## Key Themes

Nehemiah's refusal to be distracted from his mission (6:3) models the Catholic virtue of perseverance. His recognition of the false prophet Shemaiah (6:12) illustrates the need for discernment, a gift cultivated through prayer and formation. The completion of the wall in fifty-two days demonstrates that God's work, faithfully pursued, reaches its appointed end.

## Theological Significance

- **Discernment of spirits**: True and false spiritual counsel must be distinguished
- **Perseverance**: Faithful commitment to one's vocation resists distraction
- **False prophecy**: Not all religious authority is genuine
- **Divine completion**: God brings His work to fulfillment
