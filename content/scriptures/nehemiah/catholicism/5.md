## Catholic Social Teaching in Nehemiah

Nehemiah 5 provides one of the strongest Old Testament foundations for Catholic social teaching. Nehemiah's confrontation of economic exploitation anticipates the Church's encyclical tradition on social justice, from *Rerum Novarum* to *Fratelli Tutti*.

## Key Themes

### Usury Condemned

The lending at interest to fellow community members violates Torah law. Catholic moral theology has historically condemned usury, and the Church's social encyclicals consistently defend the poor against exploitative financial practices.

### Preferential Option for the Poor

Nehemiah's intervention on behalf of the indebted poor reflects what Catholic social teaching calls the "preferential option for the poor" — the principle that the needs of the most vulnerable must receive priority.

### Leadership as Service

Nehemiah's refusal of the governor's food allowance (5:14-18) models the Catholic understanding of authority as service. The Second Vatican Council's *Lumen Gentium* describes bishops as servants of the faithful.

## Theological Significance

- **Social justice**: Economic exploitation within the community is sinful
- **Preferential option**: The poor have first claim on communal resources
- **Authority as service**: Leaders serve rather than exploit
- **Prophetic confrontation**: Injustice must be publicly challenged
