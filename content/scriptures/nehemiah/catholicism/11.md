## Consecrated Presence in the City

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah 11 reads the resettlement of Jerusalem through the lens of consecrated life and urban mission. Those who volunteer to live in the holy city model the vocation of religious communities who dedicate themselves to specific locations and ministries.

## Key Themes

The casting of lots and the voluntary sacrifice of those who move to Jerusalem reflect the Catholic understanding of vocation: some are called to particular places and ministries that require personal sacrifice for the common good.

## Theological Significance

- **Vocation**: God calls individuals to specific places and ministries
- **Sacrifice**: Faithful service often requires personal inconvenience
- **Urban mission**: Cities need intentional Christian presence
- **Consecrated life**: Dedicated service in a particular place
