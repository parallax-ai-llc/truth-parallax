## The Covenant as Canonical Commitment

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah 10 reads the covenant pledge as an analogy for the canonical commitments that structure Catholic life: baptismal promises, religious vows, and the obligations of Church membership.

## Key Themes

The specific commitments — regarding marriage, Sabbath, tithes, and temple service — correspond to the canonical obligations that shape Catholic practice. The signatures of leaders, followed by the community's assent, reflect the Catholic principle that authority proposes and the faithful respond.

## Theological Significance

- **Canonical obligations**: Faith is expressed through specific, binding commitments
- **Hierarchical structure**: Leaders commit publicly before asking others to follow
- **Practical holiness**: Covenant faithfulness is measured in daily practices
- **Communal worship**: Supporting the house of God is a collective duty
