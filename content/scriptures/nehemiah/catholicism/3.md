## The Church Built by Many Hands

Catholic interpretation of the builders list sees a model of the Church's diverse ministries. Every member contributes according to their gifts and station, united in the common work of building up the Body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12).

## Key Themes

The chapter illustrates the Catholic principle of subsidiarity: each section of wall is assigned to the nearest group, ensuring that responsibility is distributed according to proximity and capacity. The diversity of builders — priests, goldsmiths, perfumers, merchants — reflects the universal call to service.

The refusal of the Tekoite nobles (3:5) stands as a warning against those who place personal dignity above communal service. Catholic social teaching insists that privilege entails responsibility.

## Theological Significance

- **Diverse ministries**: Every vocation contributes to the Church's mission
- **Subsidiarity**: Responsibility is distributed at the appropriate level
- **Universal call**: All baptized persons share in the Church's building work
- **Accountability**: Refusal to serve is noted and recorded
