## Nehemiah's Mission and the Building of the Church

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah 2 sees in Nehemiah's royal commission and wall inspection a type of the Church's mission: authorized by the King of Kings, the Church surveys the world's brokenness and undertakes the work of restoration.

## Key Themes

### Royal Authorization

Nehemiah receives letters of authorization from Artaxerxes. Catholic ecclesiology emphasizes that the Church's mission is authorized by Christ Himself (Matthew 28:18-20) and transmitted through apostolic succession.

### Prudent Assessment

Nehemiah's nighttime inspection models the virtue of prudence — one of the four cardinal virtues in Catholic moral theology. The Catechism (CCC 1806) teaches that prudence guides the practical judgment of every virtue.

### Communal Response

"Let us rise up and build" — the community's response to Nehemiah's call reflects the Catholic understanding of the Church as the People of God, called to build up the Body of Christ through active participation.

## Theological Significance

- **Authorized mission**: The Church acts under Christ's commission
- **Prudence**: Wise assessment precedes effective action
- **Communal participation**: Building the Church is everyone's responsibility
- **Opposition expected**: The Church militant faces resistance
