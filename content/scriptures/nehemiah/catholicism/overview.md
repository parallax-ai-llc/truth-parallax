## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah emphasizes the ecclesial and liturgical dimensions of the restoration. The rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls symbolizes the Church's ongoing work of building up the Body of Christ, while the covenant renewal ceremony prefigures the liturgical and sacramental life of the Catholic community.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Ecclesiological
The restoration of Jerusalem as a walled, governed city with defined membership parallels the Catholic understanding of the Church as a visible, structured institution with clear boundaries and authority.

### Liturgical
The great Torah-reading ceremony (chapter 8) and the subsequent covenant (chapters 9-10) are read as prototypes of the Liturgy of the Word and the communal commitment that flows from it.

### Social Teaching
Nehemiah's economic reforms (chapter 5) resonate with Catholic social teaching on the preferential option for the poor, just wages, and the obligation of the powerful to protect the vulnerable.

## Theological Emphases

- **Visible Church**: Jerusalem's walls symbolize the Church's visible structure
- **Liturgical worship**: Torah reading prefigures the Liturgy of the Word
- **Social justice**: Economic reform flows from covenant commitment
- **Leadership and authority**: Nehemiah exercises legitimate governance for the common good
