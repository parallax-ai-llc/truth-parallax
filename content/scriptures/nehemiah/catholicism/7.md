## The Community Register

Catholic interpretation of Nehemiah 7 sees the genealogical register as an analogy for the Church's visible membership. Catholic ecclesiology insists that the Church is a visible society with definable membership, not merely an invisible communion of believers.

## Key Themes

The discovery and use of the register (7:5) illustrates the Catholic value of institutional memory and archival records. The Church's baptismal registers, sacramental records, and canonical documentation serve the same function: establishing who belongs to the community and maintaining continuity across generations.

## Theological Significance

- **Visible Church**: The community of faith has definable membership
- **Institutional memory**: Records preserve identity across generations
- **Administrative grace**: God guides organizational decisions
- **Continuity**: The present community is linked to its origins
