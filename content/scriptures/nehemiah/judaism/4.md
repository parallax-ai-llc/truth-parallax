## Defense and Faith

Jewish interpretation of Nehemiah 4 addresses the balance between *bitachon* (trust in God) and *hishtadlut* (human effort). Nehemiah's formula — "We prayed to our God and set a guard" (4:9) — is frequently cited in Jewish discussions of this fundamental tension.

## Key Themes

The combination of prayer and armed defense reflects the halakhic principle that one may not rely on miracles (*ein somchin al ha-nes*). Jewish law permits and requires self-defense while maintaining trust in divine protection. The enemies' mockery parallels historical experiences of antisemitic ridicule.

## Theological Significance

- **Bitachon and hishtadlut**: Trust and effort work together
- **Self-defense**: Jewish law permits and requires defense of the community
- **Historical pattern**: Jewish communities have frequently faced mockery and threat
- **Communal solidarity**: The community shares both danger and defense
