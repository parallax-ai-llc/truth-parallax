## The Builders: Community Responsibility

Jewish interpretation reads the builders list as illustrating the principle that all members of the community share responsibility for communal welfare. The Talmudic concept of *kol Yisrael arevim zeh bazeh* (all Israel are responsible for one another) finds concrete expression in this cooperative building effort.

## Key Themes

The participation of diverse groups — priests, goldsmiths, perfumers — illustrates that communal obligation transcends professional and class boundaries. The refusal of the Tekoite nobles (3:5) is noted as a failure of communal responsibility. Jewish ethical tradition emphasizes that privilege entails greater, not lesser, obligation.

## Theological Significance

- **Mutual responsibility**: All community members share the communal burden
- **Diverse contribution**: Every skill serves the common good
- **Noblesse oblige**: Privilege demands greater service, not less
- **Communal building**: The community constructs its own identity through shared work
