## Settling the Holy City

Jewish interpretation of the resettlement reflects the enduring principle of *yishuv ha-aretz* (settling the Land of Israel) as a religious obligation. The casting of lots to determine who would live in Jerusalem reflects the conviction that dwelling in the holy city is both a privilege and a sacrifice.

## Key Themes

The Talmud (Ketubot 110b-111a) discusses the mitzvah of living in the Land of Israel and particularly in Jerusalem. Those who volunteer to live in Jerusalem are praised because the holy city, while spiritually supreme, lacked the economic advantages of rural life.

## Theological Significance

- **Yishuv ha-aretz**: Settling the Land is a religious obligation
- **Jerusalem's primacy**: The holy city holds supreme spiritual status
- **Sacrificial service**: Living in Jerusalem requires personal sacrifice
- **Divine lot**: God's providence guides communal decisions
