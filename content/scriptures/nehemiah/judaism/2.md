## Rebuilding the Walls of Jerusalem

Jewish interpretation of Nehemiah 2 focuses on the practical aspects of rebuilding Jerusalem, a city that holds unparalleled significance in Jewish consciousness. The chapter's account of the nighttime inspection and the rallying of the people reflects the practical piety that characterizes Jewish approaches to communal obligation.

## Key Themes

Nehemiah's request to Artaxerxes illustrates how Jewish leaders in the diaspora navigated the relationship between imperial authority and communal autonomy. The night inspection shows careful assessment before action. The opposition of Sanballat and Tobiah prefigures the ongoing challenges of rebuilding Jewish communal life in the face of external hostility.

## Theological Significance

- **Practical piety**: Assessment and planning serve divine purposes
- **Diaspora diplomacy**: Navigating imperial power for communal benefit
- **Jerusalem's priority**: Rebuilding the holy city is a sacred obligation
- **Community mobilization**: Effective leadership inspires collective action
