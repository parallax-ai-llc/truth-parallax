## Economic Ethics and Torah Law

Jewish interpretation of Nehemiah 5 engages with the Torah's prohibition of usury (*neshekh*) among fellow Jews (Exodus 22:24, Leviticus 25:35-37, Deuteronomy 23:20-21). Nehemiah's confrontation of the wealthy illustrates the practical application of these commandments.

## Key Themes

The exploitation described violates multiple Torah prohibitions. The Mishnah (Bava Metzia 5:1-11) elaborates extensive halakhic regulations against usury in all its forms. Nehemiah's public confrontation and demand for restitution model the rabbinic principle that communal leaders must enforce economic justice.

## Theological Significance

- **Prohibition of usury**: Torah law forbids exploitative lending among Jews
- **Communal leadership**: Leaders must enforce economic fairness
- **Restitution**: Wrong must be publicly corrected
- **Economic ethics**: Financial dealings are subject to religious law
