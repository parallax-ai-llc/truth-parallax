## The Communal Covenant

Jewish interpretation of the covenant reads it as an act of communal self-legislation — the people voluntarily binding themselves to specific mitzvot. This practice anticipates the later rabbinic institution of *takkanot* (communal enactments) by which Jewish communities adapted Torah law to changing circumstances.

## Key Themes

The specific commitments — regarding intermarriage, Shabbat observance, the sabbatical year, and temple support — address the most pressing challenges facing the post-exilic community. Jewish communal governance has always included the power to enact binding regulations for the welfare of the community.

## Theological Significance

- **Communal legislation**: The community can bind itself to specific observances
- **Takkanot**: Rabbinic enactments extend and apply Torah principles
- **Shabbat priority**: Sabbath observance defines Jewish identity
- **Communal responsibility**: Supporting communal worship is obligatory
