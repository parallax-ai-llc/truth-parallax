## Nehemiah's Prayer and the Islamic Tradition of Du'a

Islamic interpretation reads Nehemiah's prayer through the lens of *du'a* (supplication). The Prophet Muhammad taught that du'a is "the essence of worship" (Tirmidhi), and Nehemiah's prayer exemplifies the qualities of effective supplication: sincerity, humility, confession, and specific petition.

## Key Themes

The prayer's focus on Jerusalem (*al-Quds*) resonates with Islam's own reverence for the holy city. The Quran identifies al-Masjid al-Aqsa as a blessed precinct (Quran 17:1), and Nehemiah's distress at the city's condition reflects a concern shared across Abrahamic traditions. The four-month period of prayer before action models the Islamic emphasis on spiritual preparation through *du'a* and *dhikr* (remembrance of God).

## Theological Significance

- **Du'a**: Supplication is the essence of worship
- **Al-Quds**: Jerusalem holds sacred significance in Islam
- **Spiritual preparation**: Prayer precedes effective action
- **Corporate responsibility**: Leaders pray on behalf of their communities
