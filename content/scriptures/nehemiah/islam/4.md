## Patience Under Opposition

Islamic interpretation reads the opposition through the lens of *sabr* (patience) and *tawakkul* (trust in God). The Quran promises: "Indeed, with hardship comes ease" (94:5-6), and Nehemiah's experience confirms this promise.

## Key Themes

The combination of prayer and armed defense (4:9) parallels the Islamic concept of preparing for *jihad* (struggle) while maintaining trust in God. The Quran commands: "Prepare against them whatever you are able of power" (8:60) while also teaching that "victory is not except from Allah" (3:126).

## Theological Significance

- **Sabr**: Patience under trial is a cardinal Islamic virtue
- **Preparation and trust**: Human effort and divine reliance work together
- **Communal defense**: Protecting the community is a collective obligation
- **Divine promise**: Hardship is followed by ease
