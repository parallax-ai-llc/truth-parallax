## Overview

The figure of Nehemiah does not appear directly in the Quran, but the book's themes — the rebuilding of Jerusalem, the governance of a religious community, and the enforcement of divine law — connect to significant Islamic concerns. Jerusalem (*al-Quds*) holds a sacred place in Islam as the site of al-Masjid al-Aqsa and the destination of the Prophet Muhammad's Night Journey (*Isra*).

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Historical
Muslim historians like al-Tabari include the Persian-period restoration in their universal histories, drawing on biblical and Jewish sources (Isra'iliyyat) to reconstruct the history of Bani Isra'il.

### Theological
The theme of community governance under divine law resonates with the Islamic concept of *shari'ah* — the comprehensive legal framework governing Muslim life. Nehemiah's enforcement of Torah observance parallels the Islamic emphasis on implementing divine law in communal life.

## Theological Emphases

- **Al-Quds**: Jerusalem's sacred significance in Islam
- **Governance**: Community leadership under divine law
- **Communal discipline**: Enforcing religious observance
- **Bani Isra'il**: The Children of Israel's ongoing story as Quranic lesson
