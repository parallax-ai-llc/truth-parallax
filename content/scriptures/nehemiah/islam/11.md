## Sacred Geography and Al-Quds

Islamic interpretation of the resettlement emphasizes the sacred geography of Jerusalem. The city's holiness in Islam is connected to al-Masjid al-Aqsa and the Prophet Muhammad's Night Journey (*Isra'* — Quran 17:1).

## Key Themes

The dedication of individuals to living in Jerusalem reflects the Islamic understanding that holy places require the sustained presence of worshipping communities. The concept of *waqf* (religious endowment) in Islamic law preserves sacred spaces for perpetual communal use.

## Theological Significance

- **Sacred geography**: Jerusalem holds irreducible holiness
- **Waqf**: Sacred spaces are preserved for perpetual worship
- **Sacrifice**: Living in holy places may require personal sacrifice
- **Divine appointment**: Sacred geography is divinely established
