## Prohibition of Riba and Economic Justice

Islamic interpretation of Nehemiah 5 connects directly to the Quran's prohibition of *riba* (usury/interest). The Quran declares: "Allah has permitted trade and has forbidden interest" (2:275). Nehemiah's confrontation of usurious lending resonates powerfully with Islamic economic ethics.

## Key Themes

The exploitation described in Nehemiah 5 violates principles shared by Islamic and Jewish law. The Islamic prohibition of *riba* is comprehensive, extending to all forms of interest-based lending. Nehemiah's personal example of refusing the governor's allowance models the Islamic concept of *zuhd* (asceticism) in leadership.

## Theological Significance

- **Riba prohibited**: Usurious lending is forbidden by divine law
- **Economic justice**: Financial systems must protect the vulnerable
- **Ascetic leadership**: Leaders should live simply
- **Prophetic confrontation**: Injustice must be publicly challenged
