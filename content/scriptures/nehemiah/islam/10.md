## Covenant and Shari'ah

Islamic interpretation sees the covenant's specific commitments as an analogy for *shari'ah* — the comprehensive Islamic legal framework governing all aspects of life. The voluntary adoption of binding religious obligations reflects the Muslim's submission to divine law.

## Key Themes

The specific commitments regarding marriage, Sabbath, and temple support parallel the Islamic regulation of marriage (*nikah*), worship (*ibadah*), and charitable giving (*zakat*). The voluntary nature of the covenant reflects the Islamic principle that "there is no compulsion in religion" (Quran 2:256).

## Theological Significance

- **Shari'ah**: Comprehensive divine law governs all of life
- **Voluntary submission**: True religion is freely embraced
- **Practical commitment**: Faith is expressed through specific obligations
- **Community discipline**: Religious observance is communally maintained
