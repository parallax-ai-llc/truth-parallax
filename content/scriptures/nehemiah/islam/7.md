## Community Identity

Islamic interpretation sees the genealogical register as reflecting the Quran's teaching that God created humanity as nations and tribes "that you may know one another" (49:13). Identity serves the purpose of mutual recognition, not superiority.

## Key Themes

The register establishes communal identity without implying supremacy. Islamic theology affirms that God favored the Bani Isra'il (2:47) while also teaching that ultimate honor comes through piety, not lineage (49:13).

## Theological Significance

- **Identity without supremacy**: Lineage serves recognition, not pride
- **Divine favor**: God chooses whom He wills for particular roles
- **Community records**: Maintaining identity across generations
- **Universal principle**: True honor comes from piety

- Quran 49:13 and 2:47.
