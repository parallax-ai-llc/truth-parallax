## Sacred Geography and Habitation

Orthodox interpretation of the resettlement reads it through the lens of sacred geography — the conviction that certain places carry particular spiritual significance. Jerusalem's need for inhabitants reflects the Orthodox understanding that holy places require the sustained presence of worshipping communities.

## Key Themes

The Orthodox emphasis on monastic communities in holy places (Mount Athos, Jerusalem, Sinai) reflects this same principle. The voluntary sacrifice of those who move to Jerusalem models the monastic call to leave worldly comfort for sacred service.

## Theological Significance

- **Sacred geography**: Holy places require worshipping communities
- **Monastic parallel**: Voluntary dedication to a sacred place
- **Communal sacrifice**: Some are called to difficult postings
- **Holy presence**: The faithful sanctify the places they inhabit
