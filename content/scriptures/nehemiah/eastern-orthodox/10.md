## The Canonical Commitment

Orthodox interpretation of the covenant reads it as an analogy for the canonical life of the Church — the body of ecclesiastical law that governs Orthodox communal practice. The specific commitments regarding marriage, Sabbath, and temple support correspond to the canonical regulations that structure Orthodox life.

## Key Themes

The community's voluntary self-binding to specific practices reflects the Orthodox understanding of canon law as the community's self-discipline under the Spirit's guidance. The canons are not imposed externally but adopted by the Church in council.

## Theological Significance

- **Canonical life**: The community structures itself through voluntary commitments
- **Conciliar authority**: Decisions emerge from communal discernment
- **Practical discipline**: Faith is expressed through specific practices
- **Communal obligation**: Supporting worship is everyone's responsibility
