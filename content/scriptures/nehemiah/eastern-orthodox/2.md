## The Night Vision and the Call to Build

Orthodox interpretation of Nehemiah's nighttime inspection reads it as a type of spiritual discernment — the soul surveying its own ruins before beginning the work of restoration. The call "Come, let us build" echoes the Church's invitation to participate in the divine work of renewal.

## Key Themes

Nehemiah's secret inspection models the Orthodox practice of spiritual self-examination before confession. The transition from assessment to action reflects the Orthodox integration of contemplation and work. The opposition of Sanballat and Tobiah represents the spiritual forces that resist every genuine work of restoration.

## Theological Significance

- **Self-examination**: Honest assessment precedes genuine renewal
- **Synergeia**: Divine and human effort cooperate in restoration
- **Spiritual opposition**: Resistance is expected and overcome through faith
- **The call to build**: Every Christian participates in the Church's construction
