## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox Church reads Nehemiah within the framework of the divine economy — God's providential plan for salvation unfolding through the rebuilding of Jerusalem. The book's integration of physical reconstruction, liturgical renewal, and communal repentance reflects the Orthodox understanding that salvation encompasses the whole of human life.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Typological
The rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls prefigures the building of the Church. The Fathers saw in Nehemiah's leadership a type of Christ, who rebuilds the ruined walls of human nature through the Incarnation and Resurrection.

### Liturgical
The great assembly of chapter 8, where Ezra reads the Torah and the people respond with worship and weeping, is read as a prototype of the Orthodox liturgical assembly.

### Ascetical
Nehemiah's combination of fasting, prayer, and vigorous action models the Orthodox integration of contemplation and work — the unity of *theoria* and *praxis*.

## Theological Emphases

- **Divine economy**: God directs all historical events toward salvation
- **Theosis through action**: Practical work, done prayerfully, participates in divine life
- **Communal repentance**: Corporate confession leads to genuine renewal
- **Liturgical formation**: The community is shaped by its worship
