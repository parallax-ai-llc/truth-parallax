## The Liturgical Procession

Orthodox interpretation reads the wall dedication as a great liturgical procession — a form of worship deeply embedded in Orthodox practice. The two choirs processing in opposite directions around the walls create a liturgical embrace of the city, sanctifying the space through movement, music, and prayer.

## Key Themes

Orthodox worship regularly employs processions: the Great Entrance with the Eucharistic gifts, the procession around the church building on Pascha night, and the cross processions on feast days. Nehemiah 12's dedication is a biblical prototype of these practices. The joy "heard far away" (12:43) anticipates the cosmic dimension of Orthodox worship.

## Theological Significance

- **Liturgical procession**: Movement is an essential element of worship
- **Sanctification of space**: Sacred space is created through liturgical action
- **Cosmic worship**: Earthly celebration echoes heavenly praise
- **Joy as testimony**: Authentic worship radiates outward
