## Kenotic Leadership and Economic Justice

Orthodox interpretation of Nehemiah 5 highlights the theme of *kenosis* (self-emptying) in leadership. Nehemiah's refusal of the governor's food allowance (5:14-18) models the self-emptying love that Christ demonstrated and that Orthodox theology calls all leaders to imitate.

## Key Themes

The exploitation of the poor by the wealthy violates the Orthodox understanding of *koinonia* (communion). Economic life, in Orthodox social theology, must reflect the self-giving love of the Trinity. Nehemiah's confrontation of the exploiters reflects the prophetic tradition that Orthodox theology affirms as integral to the Church's witness.

## Theological Significance

- **Kenotic leadership**: Self-emptying love characterizes true authority
- **Koinonia**: Economic life must reflect Trinitarian communion
- **Prophetic witness**: Confronting injustice is part of the Church's mission
- **Philanthropy**: Love of neighbor includes economic justice
