## Discernment of Spirits

Orthodox interpretation of Nehemiah 6 reads the chapter as a lesson in *diakrisis* (discernment of spirits). Nehemiah's ability to distinguish true from false counsel reflects the Orthodox ascetical tradition's emphasis on spiritual discernment as a gift cultivated through prayer, fasting, and spiritual direction.

## Key Themes

The false prophet Shemaiah (6:10-13) illustrates the Orthodox teaching that spiritual deception can come through seemingly religious channels. The tradition of consulting a spiritual father (*gerontas* / *staretz*) helps guard against such deception. Nehemiah's focused perseverance (6:3) models the Orthodox virtue of steadfastness in one's calling.

## Theological Significance

- **Diakrisis**: Discernment is a vital spiritual gift
- **False prophecy**: Deception can wear religious garments
- **Steadfastness**: Perseverance in one's calling resists distraction
- **Spiritual direction**: A trustworthy guide helps navigate deception
