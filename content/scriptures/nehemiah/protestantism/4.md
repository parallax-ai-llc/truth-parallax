## Opposition Intensified

Nehemiah 4 records the escalation of external opposition — from mockery to conspiracy to armed threat. Nehemiah's response models the Protestant integration of prayer and practical vigilance.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Mockery (4:1-3)

Sanballat and Tobiah mock the builders: "If a fox goes up on it, he will break down their stone wall!" Protestant interpreters note that ridicule is often the first weapon against God's work. The appropriate response is prayer, not counter-mockery (4:4-5).

### The Conspiracy (4:7-8)

When mockery fails, the adversaries plot armed attack. Nehemiah responds with the famous combination: "We prayed to our God and set a guard" (4:9). This verse is a cornerstone of Protestant teaching on the relationship between faith and action.

### Armed Builders (4:16-18)

The workers build with one hand and hold a weapon with the other. Protestant interpreters apply this to spiritual warfare: believers must simultaneously build the Kingdom and defend against attack.

### "Our God will fight for us" (4:20)

Nehemiah's rallying cry expresses the Protestant conviction that spiritual battles are ultimately won by God, even though human effort is required.

## Theological Significance

- **Prayer and vigilance**: Faith does not preclude practical defense
- **Spiritual warfare**: Building and battling go together
- **Corporate solidarity**: The community shares both work and danger
- **Divine warrior**: God fights for His people
