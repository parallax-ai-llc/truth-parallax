## The Register of the Community

Nehemiah 7 largely parallels Ezra 2, containing the genealogical list of those who returned from exile. Nehemiah discovers this record and uses it to organize the restored community.

## Key Themes

### The Importance of Records (7:5)

"Then my God put it into my heart to assemble the nobles and the officials and the people to be enrolled by genealogy." Protestant interpreters note that God directs even administrative decisions. Church governance and record-keeping are spiritual activities.

### Continuity of Identity

The list establishes the legitimate composition of the covenant community. Protestant ecclesiology recognizes the importance of church membership and the visible identification of who belongs to the believing community.

### Stewardship and Administration

The detailed financial records at the chapter's end (7:70-72) reflect faithful stewardship. Protestant teaching affirms that financial administration in God's work requires transparency and accountability.

## Theological Significance

- **Divine guidance in administration**: God directs organizational work
- **Community identity**: Knowing who belongs to the covenant community matters
- **Historical continuity**: The present community inherits the past's promises
- **Financial transparency**: Sacred funds require honest management
