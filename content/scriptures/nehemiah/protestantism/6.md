## Plots and Perseverance

Nehemiah 6 records the most sophisticated attacks against Nehemiah personally: invitations to negotiate (a trap), false accusations, and hired false prophets. Nehemiah's discernment and perseverance provide a masterclass in resisting manipulation.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Invitation Trap (6:1-4)

Sanballat and Geshem invite Nehemiah to a meeting in the plain of Ono. Nehemiah recognizes the trap and refuses four times with the iconic response: "I am doing a great work and I cannot come down" (6:3). Protestant interpreters celebrate this as a model of focus and refusal to be distracted from God-given priorities.

### The Open Letter (6:5-9)

An unsealed letter publicly accusing Nehemiah of planning rebellion is designed to intimidate. Nehemiah's response is prayer: "Now strengthen my hands" (6:9). Protestant teaching on spiritual warfare emphasizes that false accusations should be met with prayer, not panic.

### The False Prophet (6:10-14)

Shemaiah attempts to lure Nehemiah into the temple, suggesting he hide from assassins. Nehemiah discerns this as a hired scheme to discredit him. Protestant theology of discernment teaches that not every spiritual-sounding counsel comes from God.

### The Wall Completed (6:15-16)

The wall is finished in fifty-two days. Even the enemies recognize "this work had been accomplished with the help of our God" (6:16).

## Theological Significance

- **Focus**: Refusing distraction from God's calling
- **Discernment**: Testing spiritual counsel against God's character
- **Prayer under pressure**: Turning threats into occasions for prayer
- **Divine vindication**: God's work speaks for itself when completed
