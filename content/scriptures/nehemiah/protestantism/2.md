## The Royal Commission

Nehemiah 2 records the pivotal transition from prayer to action. Nehemiah secures the king's permission, travels to Jerusalem, and conducts a secret nighttime inspection of the ruined walls.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### Before the King (2:1-8)

Artaxerxes notices Nehemiah's sadness — dangerous in the court, where gloom before the king could be fatal. Nehemiah's "arrow prayer" (2:4) before answering is a model of integrating prayer into daily life. Protestant interpreters celebrate this: prayer need not be limited to formal settings.

### The Night Inspection (2:12-16)

Nehemiah surveys the ruins secretly before making his plans known. Protestant leadership teaching draws lessons from this: wise leaders assess situations privately before making public commitments. Information precedes proclamation.

### "Come, let us build" (2:17-18)

Nehemiah's address to the people combines honest assessment of the problem with confident testimony of God's provision. The response — "Let us rise up and build" — illustrates how godly leadership inspires corporate action.

### Opposition Begins (2:19-20)

Sanballat, Tobiah, and Geshem mock the project. Nehemiah's response — "The God of heaven will give us success" — models Protestant confidence in divine sovereignty over human opposition.

## Theological Significance

- **Prayer in the moment**: Arrow prayers integrate faith into daily decisions
- **Strategic planning**: Faith and wisdom work together
- **Inspiring leadership**: Honest assessment combined with confident faith
- **Confidence under opposition**: Trust in God rather than human resources
