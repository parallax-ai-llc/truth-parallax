## The Covenant Pledge

Nehemiah 10 records the community's formal covenant commitment — a signed pledge to observe specific Torah requirements. Protestant interpreters read this as an act of corporate commitment that follows genuine repentance.

## Key Themes

### The Signatories (10:1-27)

Leaders, Levites, and priests sign the covenant first, followed by the rest of the community. Protestant church governance recognizes that leaders must commit publicly before asking others to follow.

### Specific Commitments (10:28-39)

The covenant addresses concrete practices:

| Commitment | Verses | Significance |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| No intermarriage | 10:30 | Maintaining covenant identity |
| Sabbath observance | 10:31 | Honoring God's created order |
| Sabbath year | 10:31 | Economic justice through debt release |
| Temple support | 10:32-39 | Funding communal worship |

### "We will not neglect the house of our God" (10:39)

The covenant closes with this comprehensive pledge. Protestant interpreters apply this to the local church: faithful commitment includes financial support, regular attendance, and active participation.

## Theological Significance

- **Public commitment**: Faith requires concrete, specific pledges
- **Corporate accountability**: The community holds itself to stated standards
- **Practical obedience**: Covenant faithfulness is measured in daily practices
- **Worship support**: Maintaining the house of God is a communal obligation
