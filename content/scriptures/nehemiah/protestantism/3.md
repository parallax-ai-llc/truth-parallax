## The Builders of the Wall

Nehemiah 3 provides a detailed account of who built which section of the wall. Protestant interpreters read this as a theology of cooperative service, where every member of the community contributes to the common work.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### The Priestly Beginning (3:1)

Eliashib the high priest begins the work at the Sheep Gate. Protestant commentators note that spiritual leaders should be first to participate in communal labor, not exempt from it.

### Diverse Workers (3:1-32)

The list includes priests, Levites, goldsmiths, perfumers, merchants, and district rulers. Women participate (3:12). Protestant theology of vocation sees this as affirming that all legitimate work serves God's purposes.

### The Nobles of Tekoa (3:5)

"But their nobles would not stoop to serve their Lord." This negative note highlights those who refused to participate. Protestant interpreters draw a pointed contrast: faithfulness is measured by willingness to serve, regardless of social status.

### Each Before Their Own House (3:10, 23, 28-30)

Many builders work on sections near their own homes. Protestant practical theology observes that faithful service often begins in one's immediate vicinity — the principle of proximate responsibility.

## Theological Significance

- **Cooperative service**: The whole community participates in God's work
- **Vocational diversity**: Every skill and trade contributes
- **Leadership by example**: Spiritual leaders serve alongside the people
- **Proximate responsibility**: Serve where you are, with what you have
