## Populating the Prison City

Gnostic interpretation reads the resettlement of Jerusalem as the forced habitation of the Demiurge's primary cultic center. The casting of lots — attributed to divine guidance — is actually the archontic system assigning souls to their positions within the material order.

## Key Themes

Those who "volunteer" to live in Jerusalem are praised for their dedication, but from a Gnostic perspective, they are volunteering for deeper imprisonment. The holy city is the Demiurge's capital, and living there intensifies one's involvement in the material cult.

## Theological Significance

- **Urban imprisonment**: Jerusalem is the Demiurge's capital
- **Forced assignment**: Lots represent archontic allocation of souls
- **False honor**: Praising volunteers for deeper imprisonment
- **Sacred geography inverted**: The "holy" city is the center of material bondage
