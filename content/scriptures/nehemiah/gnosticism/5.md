## The Demiurge's Economic System

Gnostic interpretation reads the economic crisis as inherent to the material system. The exploitation of the poor by the rich is not an aberration but a feature of the archontic order, where material resources are distributed unequally to maintain hierarchical control.

## Key Themes

Nehemiah's reforms rearrange the economic system without questioning its fundamental assumptions. True liberation, in Gnostic thought, requires transcending the material economy entirely, not merely redistributing resources within it.

## Theological Significance

- **Systemic exploitation**: Economic injustice is built into the material order
- **Surface reform**: Rearranging the system does not change its nature
- **Material attachment**: Both rich and poor are bound by material concerns
- **Transcendent liberation**: True freedom lies beyond the economic system
