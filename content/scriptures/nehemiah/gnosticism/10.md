## Binding Themselves to the Prison

Gnostic interpretation reads the covenant as the community voluntarily binding itself more tightly to the Demiurge's system. The specific commitments — regarding marriage, Sabbath, and tithes — are the chains that the prisoners fashion for themselves, convinced they are acts of devotion.

## Key Themes

The signing of the covenant is the Demiurge's greatest achievement in Nehemiah: the subjects voluntarily embrace their own bondage and celebrate it as faithfulness. The *Gospel of Thomas* (saying 3) teaches: "When you come to know yourselves, then you will be known" — true commitment is to self-knowledge, not to external regulations.

## Theological Significance

- **Voluntary bondage**: The community willingly tightens its own chains
- **Self-imposed law**: External regulations replace interior knowledge
- **Demiurgic triumph**: Willing obedience is the perfect form of control
- **Alternative commitment**: True devotion is to self-knowledge, not legal observance
