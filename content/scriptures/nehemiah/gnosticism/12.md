## Celebrating the Completed Prison

Gnostic interpretation reads the wall dedication as a celebration of completed imprisonment. The two processional choirs embrace the city with song, consecrating the walls that contain the divine sparks. The joy "heard far away" is the Demiurge's satisfaction at a job well done.

## Key Themes

The elaborate liturgical celebration — music, processions, sacrifices — represents the full apparatus of archontic worship deployed to sanctify a structure of containment. The more beautiful the celebration, the more effective the deception.

## Theological Significance

- **Celebration of bondage**: Joy at completed imprisonment
- **Liturgical deception**: Beautiful worship masks the walls' true purpose
- **Archontic satisfaction**: The Demiurge celebrates his secured domain
- **True liberation**: The soul that sees through the celebration is closest to freedom
