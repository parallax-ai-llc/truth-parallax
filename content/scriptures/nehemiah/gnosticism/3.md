## Building the Prison Together

Gnostic interpretation reads the builders list as a catalogue of those who unknowingly participate in constructing their own prison. The cooperation of priests, merchants, and artisans illustrates how the Demiurge's system co-opts diverse human talents for the maintenance of material bondage.

## Key Themes

The enthusiasm of the builders is tragically ironic: they celebrate their own imprisonment. The Gnostic text *On the Origin of the World* describes how the archons organize creation through hierarchy and assigned roles — precisely the structure described in Nehemiah 3.

## Theological Significance

- **Cooperative bondage**: The community builds its own prison
- **Co-opted talents**: Human gifts serve archontic purposes
- **Hierarchical control**: Assigned sections reinforce the system's structure
- **Tragic irony**: Enthusiasm for one's own imprisonment
