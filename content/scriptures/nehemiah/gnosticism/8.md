## The Reading of the Demiurge's Law

Gnostic interpretation reads the great Torah-reading assembly as the reinforcement of the Demiurge's legal code. The people's tears upon hearing the law reflect their recognition of its demands — but from a Gnostic perspective, the appropriate response is not obedience but awakening to the law's true nature as a system of spiritual bondage.

## Key Themes

The *Testimony of Truth* critiques those who follow the Torah without understanding its spiritual meaning. The people's joy after initial weeping represents accommodation to bondage — finding comfort within the prison rather than seeking escape. The recovery of the Feast of Tabernacles restores a festival that celebrates dwelling in temporary structures — a fitting metaphor for material existence.

## Theological Significance

- **Legal bondage**: Torah reading reinforces the Demiurge's control
- **Misplaced tears**: Weeping at the law's demands misses the deeper truth
- **Accommodation**: Finding joy in bondage is the Demiurge's ultimate triumph
- **Temporary dwelling**: Sukkot inadvertently reveals the provisional nature of material existence
