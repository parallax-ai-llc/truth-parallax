## Overview

Gnostic traditions did not produce commentaries on Nehemiah, but the book's central themes — wall building, communal governance, and Torah enforcement — are deeply problematic from a Gnostic perspective. The rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls represents, in Gnostic reading, the construction of a more secure prison for the divine sparks trapped in material bodies. The enforcement of Torah observance reinforces the Demiurge's legal system that binds rather than liberates.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Anti-Demiurgic
The walls of Jerusalem are read not as protection but as containment. The Demiurge seeks to keep his worshippers within a defined space, subject to his laws and his cult. Physical walls mirror the cosmic boundaries the archons established to prevent the pneumatic sparks from ascending to the Pleroma.

### Critique of Law
Nehemiah's enforcement of Sabbath observance, tithing, and marriage regulations represents the tightening of legal control. Gnostic texts like the *Testimony of Truth* criticize those who follow the Law without understanding its spiritual meaning.

## Theological Emphases

- **Walls as imprisonment**: Physical walls mirror cosmic boundaries
- **Legal bondage**: Torah enforcement binds rather than liberates
- **Material governance**: Political authority serves the Demiurge
- **Hidden liberation**: True freedom is interior, not external
