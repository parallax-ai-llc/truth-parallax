## The Register of the Imprisoned

Gnostic interpretation reads the genealogical register as a catalogue of souls bound to the material realm. The careful enumeration of families and their places reflects the archontic system's obsessive organization — every soul accounted for, every divine spark tracked and contained.

## Key Themes

The Demiurge, like any warden, keeps careful records of his inmates. The genealogical register serves this function, ensuring that no soul escapes the system unnoticed. True identity, in Gnostic thought, is not found in earthly lineage but in the soul's origin in the Pleroma.

## Theological Significance

- **Soul registry**: The Demiurge tracks every imprisoned spark
- **False identity**: Earthly lineage masks true spiritual origin
- **Archontic organization**: Careful records serve the system of control
- **True origin**: The pneumatic self originates in the Pleroma, not in genealogy
