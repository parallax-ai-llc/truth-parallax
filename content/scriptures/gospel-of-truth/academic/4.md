## Rest in the Father

The concluding chapter of the Gospel of Truth describes the final rest (*anapausis*) of the pneumatic beings in the Father's Pleroma. Scholars have analyzed the concept of *anapausis* in relation to Jewish sabbath theology, Platonic concepts of return to the One, and the broader Valentinian eschatological framework. The term appears in other Nag Hammadi texts (the Gospel of Thomas, logion 50) and in patristic descriptions of Valentinian teaching, suggesting it was a key technical term in the Valentinian vocabulary.

The chapter's apparent universalism — its suggestion that all pneumatic beings will return to the Father — has been debated in relation to the more exclusivist soteriology of other Valentinian texts, which divide humanity into pneumatic (spiritual), psychic (soul-like), and hylic (material) categories, with only the pneumatic guaranteed salvation. Some scholars argue that the Gospel of Truth represents an early, more inclusive phase of Valentinian thought, before the tripartite anthropology was fully developed. Others suggest that the text's universalism applies only to pneumatic beings, with the fates of psychic and hylic persons left unaddressed. The chapter's function as literary closure has also been studied: the text ends with a return to its beginning, creating a circular structure that mirrors the cosmological drama of emanation and return.
