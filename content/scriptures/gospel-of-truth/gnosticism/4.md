## Rest in the Father

The concluding chapter of the Gospel of Truth describes the final state of the pneumatic beings who have received gnosis: rest (*anapausis*) in the Father within the Pleroma. For Gnostic readers, this is the telos of the entire cosmic drama — the return of all emanated beings to their source and the dissolution of the deficiency that produced the material world. The chapter employs the language of sabbath rest, reinterpreting the Genesis creation narrative as an allegory of emanation and return.

The rest described here is not the cessation of activity but the fullness of being — the condition of existing in complete knowledge of and union with the Father. The material world, produced by Error and sustained by ignorance, will simply cease to be relevant once all pneumatic sparks have returned to the Pleroma. This is not annihilation but completion: the deficiency is filled, the gap is closed, and the Pleroma is restored to its original wholeness.

The chapter concludes with a vision of cosmic reconciliation that distinguishes the Gospel of Truth from more pessimistic Gnostic texts. There is no eternal punishment, no permanent division of humanity into saved and damned. The tone is one of serene confidence that the Father's will — the reunification of all that belongs to him — will inevitably be accomplished. This universalist tendency has led scholars to compare the Gospel of Truth favorably with the more exclusivist soteriology of other Valentinian texts.
