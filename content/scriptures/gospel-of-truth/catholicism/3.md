## Knowledge and Return

Chapter 3 describes salvation as the dissolution of the fog of Error through knowledge of the Father, enabling the lost to "return" to their origin in the Pleroma. Catholic theology affirms that knowledge of God is integral to salvation — the Catechism teaches that "the desire for God is written in the human heart" (CCC 27) — but insists that this knowledge is mediated through the Church, the sacraments, and Sacred Scripture, not through private esoteric illumination.

The Catholic understanding of salvation is communal, sacramental, and moral. The Church teaches that salvation involves justification (the remission of sins and the infusion of sanctifying grace), sanctification (the growth in holiness through the sacraments and virtuous living), and glorification (the beatific vision in the life to come). The Gospel of Truth's reduction of salvation to an interior awakening — the recognition that one belongs to the Father — bypasses the moral and sacramental dimensions that the Catholic tradition considers essential. The text's vision of Error dissolving naturally when knowledge arrives lacks the Catholic emphasis on the necessity of grace, the sacraments, and the mediation of the Church.
