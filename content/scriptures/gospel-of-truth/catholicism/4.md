## Rest in the Father

The concluding chapter describes the final rest of the pneumatic beings in the Father's Pleroma. Catholic eschatology anticipates the particular judgment at death, the general resurrection, the Last Judgment, and the beatific vision — the face-to-face encounter with God that constitutes the ultimate fulfillment of human existence (1 Corinthians 13:12, CCC 1023-1029). While the concept of "rest in the Father" resonates with the Catholic hope for eternal life, the Valentinian framework differs in crucial respects.

The Gospel of Truth envisions rest as the dissolution of the deficiency and the reabsorption of emanated beings into the Pleroma — a process that appears to eliminate individual identity in the fullness of the divine. Catholic teaching, by contrast, affirms that the blessed retain their individual identity in the beatific vision: the communion of saints is a communion of persons, not a dissolution into undifferentiated divine substance. Furthermore, the Catholic tradition teaches the reality of judgment and the possibility of eternal separation from God — a note absent from the Gospel of Truth's serene universalism.
