---
title: "Gospel of Truth — Section 3: The Living Book"
scripture: "gospel-of-truth"
chapter: 3
---

**1** There was manifested in their heart the living book of the living — the one written in the thought and the mind of the Father, and which from before the foundation of the totality was within his incomprehensibility.

**2** This is the book which no one was able to take, since it remains for the one who will take it to be slain. No one could have become manifest from among those who believed in salvation unless that book had intervened.

**3** For this reason the merciful one, the faithful one, Jesus, was patient in accepting sufferings until he took that book, since he knows that his death is life for many.

**4** Just as in the case of a will which has not yet been opened — for the wealth of the deceased master of the house is hidden — so also in the case of the totality, which lay hidden while the Father of the totality was invisible, being something which was from himself, from whom all spaces come forth.

**5** For this reason Jesus appeared; he put on that book; he was nailed to a tree; he published the edict of the Father on the cross. O such great teaching! He draws himself down to death though life eternal clothes him.

**6** Having stripped himself of the perishable rags, he put on imperishability, which no one can possibly take away from him. Having entered the empty spaces of terrors, he passed through those who were stripped naked by oblivion, being knowledge and perfection, proclaiming the things that are in the heart of the Father, in order to teach those who will receive teaching.

**7** But those who are to receive teaching are the living who are inscribed in the book of the living. It is about themselves that they receive instruction, receiving it from the Father, turning again to him.

**8** Since the perfection of the totality is in the Father, it is necessary for the totality to ascend to him. Then, if one has knowledge, he receives the things that are his own and draws them to himself.

**9** For he who is ignorant is in need, and what he lacks is great, since he lacks that which will make him perfect. Since the perfection of the totality is in the Father and it is necessary for the totality to ascend to him and for each one to receive the things which are his own, he enrolled them in advance, having prepared them to give to those who came forth from him.
