Confucian interpretation of Mencius 7, the concluding chapter, presents the culmination of Mencius's moral philosophy in the teaching on "exhausting the heart-mind" (jin xin). Mencius teaches that by fully developing the capacities of the heart-mind, one comes to know one's nature, and by knowing one's nature, one comes to know Heaven. This chain of knowledge links the innermost dimension of personal experience to the ultimate moral reality of the cosmos.

The chapter contains Mencius's classification of moral achievement from the good person through the trustworthy, the beautiful, the great, and the sagely to the divine—a progression that establishes the Confucian understanding of moral development as an ascending scale of realization. The chapter concludes the Mencius with the conviction that moral perfection is possible for every human being, given the innate goodness of human nature and the availability of the Way through sustained cultivation.
