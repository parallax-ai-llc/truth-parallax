Confucian interpretation of Mencius 5 examines the concept of Heaven's mandate (tian ming) through discussions of historical exemplars. The chapter addresses the complex question of how Heaven selects rulers and how the mandate is transmitted, with Mencius arguing that Heaven's will is expressed through the acceptance of the people rather than through direct divine communication. This teaching grounds political legitimacy in popular consent understood through a moral lens.

The chapter's discussions of the sage Shun's relationship with his morally deficient family illustrate the Confucian commitment to maintaining familial obligations even under extreme circumstances. Mencius argues that the sage's filial piety is tested precisely when the parent is unworthy, establishing that Confucian filial devotion is unconditional and not contingent on the parent's moral character.
