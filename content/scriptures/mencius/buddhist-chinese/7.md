Chinese Buddhist interpretation of Mencius 7 reads the teaching on "exhausting the heart-mind" as a contemplative method parallel to Buddhist meditation. The chain from knowing the heart-mind to knowing one's nature to knowing Heaven mirrors the Buddhist progression from mindfulness (sati/nian) to insight into the nature of mind (vipassana/guan) to the realization of ultimate reality (nirvana/niepan). Both traditions teach that the deepest knowledge is achieved not through external investigation but through the thorough exploration of one's own mental experience.

The chapter's classification of moral attainment from the good to the divine invites comparison with the Buddhist stages of the bodhisattva path (bhumis). Both traditions describe a progressive deepening of moral realization that culminates in a transformation so complete that the person becomes, in some sense, more than human—the Confucian shen ren (divine person) and the Buddhist fo (buddha) representing the highest possibilities of human development.
