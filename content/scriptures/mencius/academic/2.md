Academic study of Mencius 2 focuses on the concepts of the flood-like qi and the four moral sprouts as contributions to moral psychology and ethical theory. Scholars of Chinese philosophy have analyzed the flood-like qi as an early theory of moral motivation that bridges the gap between knowledge and action, addressing the perennial philosophical problem of weakness of will (akrasia). The concept suggests that moral knowledge becomes effective only when it is accompanied by a transformed affective-somatic state.

The four sprouts doctrine has attracted extensive comparative analysis, with scholars comparing Mencius's moral sentimentalism to the theories of Hume, Smith, and Hutcheson. The thought experiment of the child at the well has been evaluated as a philosophical argument, with scholars debating whether it successfully demonstrates the innateness of moral feelings or merely shows that certain responses are universal without establishing that they are innate rather than culturally conditioned.
