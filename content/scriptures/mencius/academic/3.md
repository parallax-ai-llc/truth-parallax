Academic interpretation of Mencius 3 analyzes the polemics against Mohism and Yangism as evidence for the intellectual diversity of the Warring States period and the competitive dynamics among philosophical schools. The recovery of Mohist texts and the recent discovery of manuscripts related to the Yangist position have enabled scholars to evaluate Mencius's representations of rival views for accuracy and fairness, finding both genuine engagement and strategic mischaracterization.

The chapter's economic teachings, particularly the well-field system, have been studied by economic historians as evidence for ancient Chinese agrarian practices and as an early contribution to the theory of distributive justice. Scholars debate whether the well-field system represents an actual historical institution, an idealized reconstruction, or a utopian proposal, and they analyze its implications for understanding the relationship between economic organization and moral cultivation in Confucian thought.
