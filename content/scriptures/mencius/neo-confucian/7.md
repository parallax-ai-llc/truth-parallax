Neo-Confucian interpretation of Mencius 7 reads the teaching on "exhausting the heart-mind" as the culmination of the Neo-Confucian program of moral-metaphysical development. Zhu Xi interprets jin xin as the complete realization of the heart-mind's capacity to apprehend principle. When the heart-mind is fully developed, it grasps the principle in all things, which is to know one's nature (the principle endowed by Heaven), which in turn is to know Heaven (the source of all principle). This chain constitutes the ascending movement from individual moral psychology to universal metaphysics.

Wang Yangming's alternative reading of this chapter emphasizes that the heart-mind and principle are one (xin ji li), so that exhausting the heart-mind is not a matter of investigating external things but of fully realizing the innate moral knowing (liangzhi) that is already present in every person. This disagreement over Mencius 7 encapsulates the fundamental debate between the Cheng-Zhu and Lu-Wang schools that defined Neo-Confucian philosophy.
