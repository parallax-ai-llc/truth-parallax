Neo-Confucian interpretation of Mencius 5 reads the discussions of Heaven's mandate through the framework of principle's operation in history. Zhu Xi interprets tian ming not as the arbitrary will of a personal deity but as the necessary operation of principle in the political sphere: when a ruler governs in accordance with principle, the mandate naturally rests with him; when he deviates, the mandate transfers to one who better embodies principle. The people's acceptance serves as the empirical indicator of Heaven's judgment because the people's heart-mind naturally responds to the presence or absence of principle in governance.

The discussions of sage exemplars are read as case studies in the relationship between principle and historical circumstance. Shun's ability to maintain filial piety toward his unworthy parents demonstrates the power of principle to operate even under the most adverse conditions, when a person of exceptional moral endowment refuses to allow the turbidity of circumstances to obscure the demands of heavenly principle.
