Neo-Confucian reading of Mencius 4 interprets the Way of the former kings as the historical realization of heavenly principle in governance. Zhu Xi reads the sage-kings not merely as moral exemplars but as persons in whom principle was so fully actualized that their governance expressed the natural order of the cosmos. Their institutions and practices were not arbitrary inventions but manifestations of the same principle that structures the natural world.

The chapter's teaching on moral influence is given metaphysical grounding through the Neo-Confucian understanding of the sage's qi. When the sage's vital energy is completely purified and governed by principle, it radiates outward and transforms the qi of those in proximity. This is not mere metaphor but a description of the actual mechanism by which moral transformation occurs, grounding the Confucian teaching on government by example in the metaphysics of principle and vital energy.
