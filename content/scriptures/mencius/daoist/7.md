Daoist reading of Mencius 7 engages with the teaching on "exhausting the heart-mind" as approaching but ultimately missing the Daoist insight. Mencius's chain—from knowing the heart-mind to knowing one's nature to knowing Heaven—resembles the Daoist movement from self-knowledge to alignment with the Dao, but differs in its insistence on deliberate moral effort. The Daoist sage knows Heaven not by exhausting the heart-mind through moral cultivation but by emptying the heart-mind of all moral content and allowing the Dao to operate through it without obstruction.

The chapter's classification of moral achievement from the good person to the divine person is viewed by Daoists as a typically Confucian ranking system that imposes hierarchy on what should be the equality of all beings within the Dao. The highest category, the "divine" (shen) person, approaches the Daoist ideal only if understood as one who has transcended all moral categories, not as one who has perfected them. The Daoist sage is not the most virtuous person but the most natural one.
