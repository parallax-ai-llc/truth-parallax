---
title: "Mencius - Chapter 4"
scripture: "mencius"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Mencius said: "The compass and square produce perfect circles and squares. The sage produces the perfection of human relations."

**2** "He who desires to be a sovereign should fulfill the duties of a sovereign. He who desires to be a minister should fulfill the duties of a minister."

**3** "They have only to imitate Yao and Shun. He who does not serve his sovereign as Shun served Yao, does not respect his sovereign. He who does not govern his people as Yao governed his, injures his people."

**4** Mencius said: "The people are the most important element in a nation; the spirits of the land and grain are the next; the sovereign is the lightest."

**5** "Therefore to gain the peasantry is the way to become sovereign; to gain the sovereign is the way to become a prince of a state; to gain the prince of a state is the way to become a great officer."

**6** "When a prince endangers the altars of the spirits of the land and grain, he is changed, and another is appointed in his place."

**7** "When the sacrificial victims have been perfect, when the millet in its vessels has been pure, when the sacrifices have been offered at their proper seasons, and yet droughts ensue and floods come — then the spirits of the land and grain are changed, and others appointed in their place."

**8** Mencius said: "Benevolence subdues its opposite just as water subdues fire. Those who nowadays practice benevolence do it as if with one cup of water they could save a whole wagon-load of fuel which was on fire."

**9** "When the water does not overcome the fire, they say: 'Water cannot overcome fire.' This provides the greatest aid to those who are not benevolent."

**10** "The final result will be the loss of benevolence altogether."

**11** Mencius said: "The five kinds of grain are considered the best of all seeds, but if they are not ripe, they are not even as good as cockle. So it is with benevolence; the value of it depends entirely upon its being brought to maturity."

**12** Mencius said: "Yi, in teaching men to shoot, drew the bow to its full extent. The learner must also draw the bow to its full extent. The master carpenter, in teaching others, uses the compass and square; the learner must also use the compass and square."

**13** Mencius said: "When Heaven is about to confer a great responsibility on any man, it will exercise his mind with suffering, subject his sinews and bones to hard work, expose his body to hunger, put him to poverty, place obstacles in the paths of his deeds."

**14** "So it stimulates his mind, hardens his nature, and supplies his incompetencies."

**15** "Men for the most part err, and are afterwards able to reform. They are distressed in mind and perplexed in their thoughts, and then they arise to vigorous reformation."

**16** "If a state has not families attached to the laws and worthy counsellors within, and if abroad there are not hostile states and external perils, such a state will generally come to ruin. Then we know that sorrow and calamity bring life, and ease and pleasure bring death."
