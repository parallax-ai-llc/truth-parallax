---
title: "Mencius - Chapter 7"
scripture: "mencius"
chapter: 7
---

**1** Mencius said: "He who has exhausted all his mental constitution knows his nature. Knowing his nature, he knows Heaven."

**2** "To preserve one's mental constitution, and nourish one's nature, is the way to serve Heaven."

**3** "When neither a premature death nor long life causes a man any double-mindedness, but he waits in the cultivation of his personal character for whatever issue — this is the way in which he establishes his Heaven-ordained being."

**4** Mencius said: "There is an appointment for everything. Let a man receive submissively what may be correctly ascribed thereto."

**5** "Therefore, he who has the true idea of what is Heaven's appointment will not stand beneath a precipitous wall."

**6** "Death sustained in the discharge of one's duties may correctly be ascribed to the appointment of Heaven. Death under handcuffs and fetters cannot correctly be so ascribed."

**7** Mencius said: "When we get by our seeking and lose by our neglecting — in that case seeking is of use to getting, and the things sought for are those which are in ourselves."

**8** "When the seeking is according to the proper course, and the getting is only as appointed — in that case the seeking is of no use to getting, and the things sought are without ourselves."

**9** Mencius said: "All things are already complete in us. There is no greater delight than to be conscious of sincerity on self-examination."

**10** "If one acts with a vigorous effort at the law of reciprocity, when he seeks for the realization of perfect virtue, nothing can be closer than his approximation to it."

**11** Mencius said: "A man with definite aims to be accomplished may be compared to one digging a well. To dig the well to a depth of seventy-two cubits, and stop without reaching the spring — is to throw away the well."

**12** Mencius said: "Benevolence is the distinguishing characteristic of man. As embodied in man's conduct, it may simply be called the path of duty."

**13** Mencius said: "Confucius ascended the eastern hill, and Lu appeared to him small. He ascended the T'ai mountain, and all beneath the heavens appeared to him small."

**14** "So he who has contemplated the sea finds it difficult to think anything of ordinary waters, and he who has wandered in the gate of the sage finds it difficult to think anything of the words of others."

**15** "There is a method of contemplating water — it is necessary to look at it as flowing in a stream. The sun and moon, having their brightness, their light at once illumines every crevice to which it reaches."

**16** "The flowing water is a thing which does not proceed till it has filled the hollows in its course. The student who has set his mind on the Way, and does not carry it out to completion, does not penetrate the divine brilliance."

**17** Mencius said: "That whereby man differs from the lower animals is but small. The mass of people cast it away, while superior men preserve it."

**18** "Shun clearly understood the multitude of things, and closely observed the relations of humanity. He walked along the path of benevolence and righteousness; he did not need to pursue benevolence and righteousness."

**19** Mencius said: "The great man does not think beforehand of his words that they may be sincere, nor of his actions that they may be resolute — he simply speaks and does what is right."

**20** Mencius said: "The great man is he who does not lose his child's-heart."
