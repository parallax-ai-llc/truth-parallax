---
title: "Mencius - Chapter 5"
scripture: "mencius"
chapter: 5
---

**1** Wan Chang asked Mencius: "Was it the case that Yao gave the throne to Shun?" Mencius said: "No. The sovereign cannot give the throne to another."

**2** "Then who gave the throne to Shun?" Mencius replied: "Heaven gave it to him."

**3** "Did Heaven confer it on him with specific injunctions?" Mencius replied: "No. Heaven does not speak. It simply showed its will by his personal conduct and his conduct of affairs."

**4** "How was it shown by his personal conduct and his conduct of affairs?" Mencius said: "The sovereign can present a man to Heaven, but he cannot make Heaven give that man the throne. A prince can present a man to the sovereign, but he cannot cause the sovereign to make that man a prince."

**5** "A great officer can present a man to his prince, but he cannot cause the prince to make that man a great officer."

**6** "Yao presented Shun to Heaven, and Heaven accepted him. He presented him to the people, and the people accepted him."

**7** "Therefore I say, Heaven does not speak. It simply indicated its will by his personal conduct and his conduct of affairs."

**8** Wan Chang said: "I venture to ask how it was that Yao presented Shun to Heaven, and Heaven accepted him; and that he exhibited him to the people, and the people accepted him."

**9** Mencius said: "He caused him to preside over the sacrifices, and all the spirits were well pleased with them — thus Heaven accepted him. He caused him to preside over the conduct of affairs, and affairs were well administered, and the people reposed under him — thus the people accepted him."

**10** "Heaven gave the throne to him. The people gave it to him. Therefore I said: The sovereign cannot give the throne to another."

**11** "Shun assisted Yao in the government for twenty and eight years. This was more than man could have done. It was Heaven."

**12** "After the death of Yao, when the three years' mourning was completed, Shun withdrew from the son of Yao to the south of South River."

**13** "The princes of the kingdom, however, resorting to court, went not to the son of Yao, but they went to Shun. Litigants went not to the son of Yao, but they went to Shun. Singers sang not the praises of the son of Yao, but they sang the praises of Shun."

**14** "Therefore I said it was Heaven that gave him the throne. It was after these things that he went to the middle kingdom, and occupied the seat of the son of Heaven."

**15** "If he had, before these things, taken up his residence in the palace of Yao, and had applied pressure to the son of Yao, it would have been an act of usurpation, and not the gift of Heaven."

**16** "This sentiment is expressed in the words of the Great Declaration: 'Heaven sees according as my people see; Heaven hears according as my people hear.'"
