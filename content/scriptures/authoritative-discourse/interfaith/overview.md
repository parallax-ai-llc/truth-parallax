## Overview

The Authoritative Discourse addresses a universal human challenge: how to discern what is genuinely valuable in a world full of attractive counterfeits. The marketplace metaphor speaks to every spiritual tradition that warns against attachment to worldly goods — the Buddhist teaching on detachment, the Hindu concept of vairagya (dispassion), the Islamic practice of zuhd (ascetic detachment), the Christian counsel of evangelical poverty. The text's message transcends its Gnostic context: true freedom comes not from acquiring more but from the discernment to recognize what is worth having.
