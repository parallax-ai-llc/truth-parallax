**2.** The adversaries of the soul are many. They are the invisible powers of the flesh: desire, fear, the love of money, vainglory, ignorance, and foolishness. These all wage war against the soul.

She is like a fish caught in a net. She struggles but cannot free herself. She is like a merchant seeking to sell his goods; the robbers wait for him along the road. She is like a bride who has been abducted from her bridegroom and given to bandits.

But the soul who has received the word of the Father—she gathers strength within herself. She becomes like a warrior who puts on armor. She takes up the weapons of the Logos: understanding, endurance, mercy, patience, and love of truth.

The adversaries wage war against her, but she defeats them. She tramples upon their heads. She overcomes desire by self-control, and she overcomes ignorance by knowledge, and she overcomes envy by goodness. The powers of the flesh cannot stand against her, for the power of the Logos is within her.

And when she has conquered all the adversaries, then the soul rises upward. She ascends past the powers and the principalities, and she enters the bridal chamber of the Father. She receives her rest, and she dwells in that place forever.
