**1.** The soul—her nature, her companions, her conduct—these things the Authoritative Discourse teaches.

The soul has been cast into the body. She came into being by the will of the Father, and he gave her to the body. From the first she was with the Father, dwelling in that place, before she was cast down into this place. But because she had been cast down, she forgot her origin.

She sank into the body and became entangled in matter. The adversaries laid hold of her and brought her into the house of poverty. They gave her hatred and jealousy and envy and desire and ignorance. She was bound in these passions and she forgot the place from which she had come.

But the Father, because he is good and because he loved the soul, sent to her the Logos, the Word of Truth. He wished that she should turn again toward him and remember her origin. For the invisible soul of righteousness has been in the body since the day she was cast down.

The Logos became a physician for the soul. He healed her from the passions which the adversaries had placed upon her. He brought her to the bridal chamber and gave her back her former beauty.
