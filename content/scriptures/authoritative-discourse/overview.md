## Overview

The Authoritative Discourse (NHC VI,3), also known as Authentikos Logos, is an allegorical treatise describing the soul's journey through the material world, where she is beset by deceptive "merchants" — the archonic powers who peddle false goods (pleasures, honors, and material attachments) designed to trap the soul in ignorance. The soul, however, possesses an innate capacity for discernment and, through spiritual struggle, is able to reject the merchants' wares, fast from worldly attachments, and ultimately return to the divine realm from which she came. The text shares the feminine personification of the soul found in the Exegesis on the Soul but is more focused on the metaphor of the spiritual marketplace and the language of spiritual combat. It is generally dated to the second or third century CE.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | The Soul Enters the World | The soul's descent into the material world and her encounter with the powers that seek to deceive her |
| 2 | The Merchants and Their Wares | The archons are described as merchants selling worthless goods — material pleasures designed to trap the soul |
| 3 | The Soul's Struggle and Discernment | The soul engages in spiritual warfare, fasting from worldly attachments and discerning truth from deception |
| 4 | Victory and Return to the Father | The soul's triumph over the merchants and her return to the divine realm |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: Read as a practical guide to the soul's liberation from the material world's deceptions
- **Academic**: Studied for its extended marketplace metaphor, its relationship to other soul-journey texts, and its rhetoric
- **Comparative**: Offers parallels to the Buddhist concept of attachment, Platonic soul-ascent, and Sufi allegories of the soul's journey
- **Christian**: Engages with Christian themes of spiritual warfare (Ephesians 6) and fasting as spiritual discipline
- **Jewish**: Draws on prophetic imagery of false prophets and the distinction between true and counterfeit wisdom
- **Interfaith**: Addresses the universal human experience of discerning what is genuinely valuable from what is seductively worthless

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Deceptive Merchants | The archons are traders selling worthless material goods to trap the soul |
| Spiritual Discernment | The soul's innate capacity to distinguish truth from deception is the key to liberation |
| Fasting and Renunciation | Fasting from worldly attachments is the practical means of spiritual freedom |
| The Soul's Return | The goal of the soul's journey is return to her original divine home |
