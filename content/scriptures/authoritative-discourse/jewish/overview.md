## Overview

Jewish readers find in the Authoritative Discourse resonances with the prophetic tradition's warnings against false prophets and counterfeit wisdom. Jeremiah's critique of prophets who 'speak visions of their own minds, not from the mouth of the Lord' (Jeremiah 23:16) and Proverbs' contrast between Wisdom and the 'strange woman' (Proverbs 7) both employ the metaphor of discerning true from false. The text's concern with spiritual discernment parallels the rabbinic emphasis on distinguishing the yetzer ha-tov (good inclination) from the yetzer ha-ra (evil inclination).
