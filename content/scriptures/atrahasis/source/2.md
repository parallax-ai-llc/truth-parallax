**Tablet II**

**1** Six hundred years, less than six hundred, passed,
**2** and the country became too wide, the people too numerous.
**3** The country was as noisy as a bellowing bull.
**4** The god grew restless at their racket.

**5** Enlil had to listen to their noise.
**6** He addressed the great gods:
**7** "The noise of mankind has become too much.
**8** I am losing sleep over their racket.

**9** Cut off food supplies to the people!
**10** Let the vegetation be too scant for their hunger!
**11** Let Adad hold back his rain.
**12** Below, let the flood not rise from the deep.

**13** Let the wind blow and parch the ground.
**14** Let the clouds thicken, but hold back the rain.
**15** Let the field diminish its yield.
**16** Let Nisaba seal up her breast.

**17** Let there be no happiness for the people."

**18** They cut off food supplies to the people.
**19** The vegetation became too scant for their hunger.
**20** Adad held back his rain.
**21** Below, the flood did not rise from the deep.

**22** The field diminished its yield.
**23** Nisaba sealed up her breast.
**24** The dark fields became white,
**25** the broad plain choked with salt.

**26** In the first year they ate stored grain.
**27** In the second year they suffered depletion.
**28** In the third year their features were distorted by hunger.
**29** In the fourth year their long legs became short,
**30** their broad shoulders became narrow;
**31** they walked hunched in the street.

**32** In the fifth year, the daughter would eye the mother
**33** when the mother opened the door.
**34** The daughter would watch the scales at the sale of the mother.
**35** In the sixth year they served up the daughter for dinner.
**36** They served up the son for food.

**37** One house consumed another.
**38** Their faces were coated with malt, like dead skin.
**39** The people were living on the edge of death.

**40** They took advice; they attended the assembly.
**41** They addressed Atrahasis, the exceedingly wise:
**42** "We have called upon you in assembly.
**43** You know how to find the right word.
**44** Your speech is wisdom. Save us!

**45** What is to be done?"

**46** Atrahasis opened his mouth and spoke to the assembly:
**47** "Organize the whole land.
**48** Let heralds proclaim in the land a loud call.
**49** Do not worship your gods,
**50** do not pray to your goddesses.

**51** Seek the door of the god of rain, Adad.
**52** Lay baked loaves at his door.
**53** May the flour-offering reach him.
**54** May he be shamed by the gift
**55** and lift his hand."

**56** They organized the whole land.
**57** Heralds proclaimed in the land a loud call.
**58** They did not worship their gods.
**59** They did not pray to their goddesses.

**60** They sought the door of Adad.
**61** They laid baked loaves at his door.
**62** The flour-offering reached him.
**63** He was shamed by the gift.

**64** In the morning he rained down a mist,
**65** and furtively he rained down a dew in the night.
**66** The fields bore grain by stealth.
**67** The famine lifted, the hunger ended.

**69** But again six hundred years, less than six hundred, passed,
**70** and the country became too wide, the people too numerous.
**71** The country was as noisy as a bellowing bull.
**72** The god grew restless at their racket.

**73** Enlil had to listen to their noise.
**74** He addressed the great gods:
**75** "The noise of mankind has become too much.
**76** I am losing sleep over their racket.

**77** Let there be plague upon the people."

**78** Namtar let loose his assault.
**79** The plague spread. People lay sick.
**80** Like a storm it blew upon them.
**81** The swift could not escape,
**82** the strong could not hold it back.

**83** They took advice; they attended the assembly.
**84** They addressed Atrahasis:
**85** "What is to be done?"

**86** Atrahasis opened his mouth and spoke:
**87** "Build a house for the god Namtar.
**88** Lay baked loaves at his door.
**89** May the flour-offering reach him.
**90** May he be shamed by the gift
**91** and lift his hand."

**92** They built a house for Namtar.
**93** The flour-offering reached him.
**94** He was shamed by the gift.
**95** The plague withdrew from them.

**96** The gods assembled in counsel.
**97** Enlil was furious with the gods:
**98** "You have again let the people multiply!
**99** They are more numerous than before!
**100** I am losing sleep over their racket."

**101** Enlil spoke to the assembly of the gods:
**102** "Take away their food.
**103** Let Anu and Adad guard heaven above.
**104** Let Sin and Nergal guard the middle earth.

**105** The bolt, the bar of the sea,
**106** let Ea guard together with his creatures.
**107** He shall hold back the flood of the deep."

**108** Anu and Adad guarded heaven above.
**109** Sin and Nergal guarded the middle earth.
**110** The bolt, the bar of the sea,
**111** Ea guarded together with his creatures.

**112** [Gap in text—another cycle of deprivation follows]

**113** The people did not diminish;
**114** they became even more numerous than before.
**115** Enlil opened his mouth to speak,
**116** addressed the gods, his brothers:
**117** "The racket of mankind has become too great.
**118** I cannot sleep for their noise.
**119** Enough! Let the flood sweep over them all.
**120** Let us destroy mankind altogether!"
