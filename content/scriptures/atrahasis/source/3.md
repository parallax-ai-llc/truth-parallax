**Tablet III**

**1** Atrahasis opened his mouth
**2** and addressed his lord:
**3** "Indicate to me the meaning of the dream.
**4** Let me find out its purpose."

**5** Enki opened his mouth and addressed his servant:
**6** "You say, 'What am I to seek?'
**7** Observe the message that I will speak to you:
**8** Wall, listen to me!

**9** Reed wall, observe all my words!
**10** Destroy your house, build a boat,
**11** reject possessions and save living things.
**12** The boat that you build—

**13** Roof it over like the Apsu.
**14** So that the sun shall not see inside it.
**15** Let it be roofed over above and below.
**16** The tackle should be very strong,
**17** let the pitch be tough, and so give the boat strength.

**18** I will make rain fall on you here,
**19** a wealth of birds, a hamper of fish."

**20** He opened the water clock and filled it.
**21** He told him of the coming of the flood for the seventh night.

**22** Atrahasis received the message.
**23** He assembled the elders to his gate.
**24** Atrahasis opened his mouth and spoke,
**25** addressed the elders:

**26** "My god does not agree with your god.
**27** Enki and Enlil are angry with each other.
**28** They have expelled me from the land.
**29** Since I have always revered Enki,
**30** he told me this.

**31** I cannot live in your city.
**32** I cannot set my feet on Enlil's earth.
**33** I must go down to the Apsu and live with my lord Enki.
**34** He will make wealth rain upon you—

**35** a profusion of fowl, a hamper of fish.
**36** In the morning he will pour down upon you
**37** a rain of wheat-cakes,
**38** in the evening a rain of wheat."

**39** He set builders to work on the boat.
**40** The carpenter carried his axe,
**41** the reed-worker carried his stone.
**42** The child carried the pitch,
**43** the poor man brought what was needed.

**44** [Gap in text]

**45** Everything that he had he loaded on it.
**46** Everything that he had of silver he loaded on it.
**47** Everything that he had of gold he loaded on it.
**48** Everything that he had of living things he loaded on it.

**49** All his kith and kin he sent up on board.
**50** The cattle of the field, the wild creatures of the field,
**51** the craftsmen he sent up on board.

**52** Shamash set him a time:
**53** "In the morning he will pour down upon you
**54** a rain of wheat-cakes,
**55** in the evening a rain of wheat.
**56** Enter the boat and seal up your door."

**57** That stated time arrived.
**58** In the morning he poured down a rain of wheat-cakes,
**59** in the evening a rain of wheat.
**60** He watched the appearance of the weather.
**61** The weather was frightful to behold.

**62** He entered the boat and sealed his door.
**63** While he was closing up his boat,
**64** Adad was roaring in the clouds.
**65** The winds were furious as he set forth.

**66** He cut the mooring rope and released the boat.

**67** [Gap in text]

**68** Anzu was tearing at the sky with his talons.
**69** The land was broken like a pot.
**70** All day long the South Wind blew,
**71** quickly blowing, the flood came.
**72** Like a battle-force it passed over the people.

**73** No man could see his fellow.
**74** They could not be recognized in the destruction.
**75** The flood bellowed like a bull.
**76** The wind howled like a screaming eagle.
**77** The darkness was dense.
**78** The sun was gone.

**79** [Gap in text]

**80** The noise of the flood made the gods afraid.
**81** Enki was beside himself,
**82** seeing his sons before him.
**83** Nintu, the great lady,
**84** gnashed her teeth with her lips.

**85** The great gods, the Anunnaki,
**86** were starving and thirsty.
**87** The goddess saw it, weeping.
**88** The midwife of the gods, the wise Mami:

**89** "Let the day become dark,
**90** let it become gloom again!
**91** In the assembly of the gods,
**92** how did I agree to the destruction?

**93** Did I bear my people
**94** that they might fill the sea like fish?"

**95** The gods wept with her over the Anunnaki.
**96** With her they sat, weeping.
**97** Their lips were feverish with thirst.

**98** For seven days and seven nights
**99** came the deluge, the storm, the flood.

**100** Where it had laid waste the land,
**101** and destroyed all living things,
**102** the flood was stilled. The sea grew calm.

**103** He opened a vent and fresh air fell upon his face.
**104** He looked at the sea; the stillness had set in.
**105** All the human beings had turned to clay.
**106** The landscape was as level as a flat roof.

**107** He opened a vent and light fell on his cheek.
**108** Bowing low, he sat and wept.
**109** Tears streamed down the sides of his nose.
**110** He looked around; the expanse of the sea was desolate.

**111** Then he released a dove to go free.
**112** The dove went off, but came back to him.
**113** There was no resting place, so it returned.

**114** Then he released a swallow to go free.
**115** The swallow went off, but came back to him.
**116** There was no resting place, so it returned.

**117** Then he released a raven to go free.
**118** The raven went off and saw the receding of the waters.
**119** It ate, it flew about, it bobbed and croaked,
**120** and did not return.

**121** Then he sent out offerings in all directions.
**122** He set up an offering on the mountain top.
**123** He set out seven and seven cult vessels.
**124** Into the fire beneath he poured reeds, cedar, and myrtle.

**125** The gods smelled the savour.
**126** The gods smelled the sweet savour.
**127** The gods gathered like flies over the offering.

**128** When at last the great goddess arrived,
**129** she lifted up the great jewels which Anu had made to her liking:
**130** "You gods here present, as surely as this lapis lazuli
**131** is on my neck, I shall be mindful of these days
**132** and never forget them.

**133** Let the gods come to the offering,
**134** but Enlil shall not come to the offering,
**135** because he was not reasonable,
**136** and brought about the flood,
**137** and consigned my people to destruction."

**138** When at last Enlil arrived
**139** and saw the boat, Enlil was furious.
**140** He was filled with anger against the gods:
**141** "Has some living soul escaped?
**142** No man was to survive the destruction!"

**143** Ninurta opened his mouth and said to warrior Enlil:
**144** "Who could do this but Enki?
**145** Only Enki knows every device."

**146** Enki opened his mouth and spoke to warrior Enlil:
**147** "You are the wisest of the gods—
**148** how, O how could you be so unreasoning
**149** as to bring about the flood?

**150** On the sinner lay his sin,
**151** on the transgressor lay his transgression.
**152** But be lenient, lest all be destroyed;
**153** be patient, lest all be undone.

**154** Instead of your causing the flood,
**155** let the lion rise and diminish the people.
**156** Instead of your causing the flood,
**157** let the wolf rise and diminish the people.

**158** Instead of your causing the flood,
**159** let famine occur to waste the land.
**160** Instead of your causing the flood,
**161** let Erra rise and mow down the people."

**162** [Gap in text]

**163** Enlil crossed over into the boat.
**164** He took Atrahasis by the hand and led him up.
**165** He led his wife up and had her kneel beside him.
**166** He touched their foreheads, standing between them, to bless them:

**167** "In the past, Atrahasis was mortal.
**168** Now, Atrahasis and his wife
**169** shall become like us gods.
**170** Atrahasis shall dwell far away,
**171** at the Mouth of the Rivers."

**172** They took him far away
**173** and settled him at the Mouth of the Rivers.

**174** But then Enki and Enlil together devised a new plan:
**175** they created barrenness among the women,
**176** they decreed that the Pashittu-demon should snatch the baby from the lap of its mother.
**177** They established the ugbabtu-women, the entu-women, and the egisitu-women,
**178** and they were taboo, and so cut off childbearing.
