**Tablet I**

When the gods instead of man
Did the work, bore the loads,
The gods' load was too great,
The work too hard, the trouble too much,
The great Anunnaki made the Igigi
Carry the workload sevenfold.
Anu their father was king,
Their counsellor warrior Enlil,
Their chamberlain was Ninurta,
Their canal-controller Ennugi.
They took the box of lots,
Cast the lots; the gods made the division.
Anu went up to heaven,
Enlil took the earth for his people,
The bolt which bars the sea
Was given to far-sighted Enki.

When Anu had gone up to heaven,
And the gods of the Apsu had gone below,
The Anunnaki of heaven
Made the Igigi bear the workload.
The gods had to dig out canals,
Had to clear channels, the lifelines of the land,
The Igigi had to dig out canals,
Had to clear channels, the lifelines of the land.
The gods dug out the Tigris river
And then dug out the Euphrates.

For 3,600 years they bore the excess,
Hard work, night and day.
They groaned and blamed each other,
Grumbled over the masses of excavation:
"Let us confront our chamberlain,
And get him to relieve us of our hard work!
Come, let us carry the lord,
The counsellor of the gods, the warrior, from his dwelling.
Come, let us carry Enlil,
The counsellor of the gods, the warrior, from his dwelling."

Then Alla made his voice heard
And spoke to the gods his brothers:
"Come! Let us carry
The counsellor of gods, the warrior, from his dwelling.
Come! Let us carry Enlil,
The counsellor of gods, the warrior, from his dwelling.
Now, cry battle!
Let us mix fight with battle!"
The gods listened to his speech,
Set fire to their tools,
Put aside their spades for fire,
Their loads for the fire-god,
They flared up. When they reached
The gate of warrior Enlil's dwelling,
It was night, the middle watch,
The house was surrounded, the god had not realized.
It was night, the middle watch,
Ekur was surrounded, Enlil had not realized.

Kalkal observed it and was disturbed.
He slid the bolt and watched.
Kalkal roused Nusku,
They listened to the noise of the Igigi.
Then Nusku roused his master,
Made him get out of bed:
"My lord, your house is surrounded,
A rabble is running around your door!
Enlil, your house is surrounded,
A rabble is running around your door!"

Enlil had weapons brought to his dwelling.
Enlil made his voice heard
And spoke to the vizier Nusku:
"Nusku, bar your door,
Take your weapons and stand before me."
Nusku barred his door,
Took his weapons and stood before Enlil.
Nusku made his voice heard
And spoke to the warrior Enlil:
"O my lord, your face is sallow as tamarisk!
Why do you fear your own sons?
O Enlil, your face is sallow as tamarisk!
Why do you fear your own sons?
Send for Anu to be brought down to you,
And for Enki to be fetched into your presence."

He sent for Anu to be brought down to him,
Enki was fetched into his presence.
Anu king of heaven was present,
Enki king of the Apsu attended.
With the great Anunnaki present,
Enlil got up and the case was put.
Enlil made his voice heard
And spoke to the great gods:
"Is it against me that they have risen?
Shall I do battle?
What did I see with my own eyes?
A rabble was running around my door!"

Anu made his voice heard
And spoke to the warrior Enlil:
"Let us find out the reason
Why the Igigi surround your door."
Anu made his voice heard
And spoke to Nusku:
"Nusku, open your door,
Take your weapons,
And in the assembly of all the gods
Bow, then stand and tell them:
'Your father Anu,
Your counsellor warrior Enlil,
Your chamberlain Ninurta,
And your canal-controller Ennugi
Have sent me to say:
Who is in charge of the rabble?
Who is in charge of the fighting?
Who declared war?
Who ran to the door of Enlil?'"

Nusku opened his door,
Took his weapons,
In the assembly of all the gods
He bowed, then stood and delivered the message:
"Your father Anu,
Your counsellor warrior Enlil,
Your chamberlain Ninurta,
And your canal-controller Ennugi
Have sent me to say:
Who is in charge of the rabble?
Who is in charge of the fighting?
Who declared war?
Who ran to the door of Enlil?"

"Every single one of us gods declared war!
We have put a stop to the digging.
The load is excessive, it is killing us!
Our work is too hard, the trouble too much!
So every single one of us gods
Has agreed to complain to Enlil."

Enki made his voice heard
And spoke to the great gods:
"Why do we blame them?
Their work was too hard, their trouble was too much.
Every day the earth resounded.
The warning signal was loud enough,
We kept hearing the noise.
There is a task to be done:
Belet-ili the womb-goddess is present,
Let the womb-goddess create offspring,
And let man bear the load of the gods!"
They called the goddess, asked
The midwife of the gods, wise Mami:
"You are the womb-goddess, to be the creator of mankind!
Create a mortal, that he may bear the yoke!
Let him bear the yoke, the work of Enlil,
Let man bear the load of the gods!"

Nintu made her voice heard
And spoke to the great gods:
"It is not proper for me to make him.
The work is Enki's;
He makes everything pure!
If he gives me clay, then I will do it."
Enki made his voice heard
And spoke to the great gods:
"On the first, seventh, and fifteenth of the month
I shall make a purification, a bath.
Let one god be slaughtered.
And let the gods be purified by immersion
In his flesh and his blood.
Let Nintu mix clay
With his flesh and his blood.
Let that same god and man
Be thoroughly mixed in the clay.
Let us hear the drumbeat forever after,
Let a ghost come into existence from the god's flesh,
Let her proclaim it as his living sign,
And let the ghost exist so as not to forget the slain god."
They answered "Yes!" in the assembly,
The great Anunnaki who assign the fates.

On the first, seventh, and fifteenth of the month
He made a purification, a bath.
Ilawela who had intelligence
They slaughtered in their assembly.
Nintu mixed clay
With his flesh and blood.
She saw it later in the living creature,
From the flesh of the god there was a ghost.
She proclaimed it as his living sign,
The ghost existed so as not to forget the slain god.
After she had mixed that clay,
She called up the Anunnaki, the great gods.
The Igigi, the great gods,
Spat spittle upon the clay.
Mami made her voice heard
And spoke to the great gods:
"I have carried out perfectly
The work that you ordered of me.
You have slaughtered a god together with his intelligence.
I have relieved you of your hard work,
I have imposed your load on man.
You have bestowed noise on mankind.
I have undone the fetter and granted freedom."
They listened to this speech of hers,
And were freed from anxiety, and kissed her feet:
"We used to call you Mami
But now your name shall be Mistress of All Gods."

**Tablet II**

He was wise, he performed his task daily,
The land grew wide, the people became numerous,
The land was bellowing like a bull.
The god was disturbed by their uproar.
Enlil heard their noise
And addressed the great gods:
"The noise of mankind has become too intense for me,
With their uproar I am deprived of sleep.
Let there be a plague upon the people."

Enki counselled Atrahasis,
His voice was heard through the reed wall:
"Wall, listen to me!
Reed wall, observe all my words!
Dismantle your house, build a boat,
Reject possessions, and save living things.
The boat that you build
Shall be roofed over like the Apsu,
So that the sun cannot see inside it!
Let it be roofed over above and below.
The tackle should be tough,
The bitumen should be firm and so give the boat strength.
I will rain down upon you here
An abundance of birds, a profusion of fishes."

Atrahasis opened his mouth
And spoke to Enki his lord:
"I have never built a boat,
Draw for me a design on the ground
That I may see the design and build the boat."
Enki drew a design on the ground:
"Following the design which I have drawn upon the ground:
Let the structure be 120 cubits each way,
Let the height be as the breadth."

He invited his people
To a feast.
He put his family on board.
They were eating, they were drinking.
But he himself was in and out,
He could not sit, could not crouch,
For his heart was broken and he was vomiting gall.
The outlook of the weather changed.
Adad roared in the clouds.
The wind became hostile as he arose,
He cut the mooring rope and released the boat.

**Tablet III**

Adad's rain-clouds gathered,
The storm swept over the peoples like a battle.
One person could not see another,
They could not recognize each other in the catastrophe.
The deluge bellowed like a bull,
The wind resounded like a screaming eagle.
The darkness was dense, the sun was gone,
The flood swept over the land like a battle.

It rained for seven days,
Seven nights the deluge attacked,
The vast flood-weapon swept the land.
After seven days the deluge
Had swept the land like a battle.
Like a woman in the travail of birth,
The sea grew quiet, the tempest was still.
The flood ceased.
He opened a vent and fresh air wafted on his face.
He looked about and all was silence.
All the human beings had turned to clay.
The landscape was as level as a flat roof.
He bowed low, sat down, and wept,
Tears running down his face.
He looked about at the face of the water in all directions—
On each of the fourteen regions
There emerged a region of land.
The boat lodged on Mount Nimush.
Mount Nimush held the boat, allowing no sway.

He released a dove, and it went forth but
Came back; there was no resting-place for it and it returned.
Then he released a swallow, and it went forth but
Came back; there was no resting-place for it and it returned.
He released a raven, which went forth,
And seeing that the waters had diminished,
It eats, it scratches, it bobs, but does not circle back to him.

Then he sent forth everything to the four winds
And offered a sacrifice.
He set out incense-offerings on the peak of the mountain.
Seven and seven cult-vessels he set in place,
And into the fire beneath he poured reeds, cedar, and myrtle.
The gods smelled the savor,
The gods smelled the sweet savor,
The gods gathered like flies about the sacrifice.

Enlil arrived
And saw the boat and was furious.
He was filled with rage at the Igigi gods:
"Where did a living being escape?
No man was to survive the destruction!"

Enki made his voice heard
And spoke to the warrior Enlil:
"You are the sage of the gods, warrior,
How could you so senselessly bring about the flood?
Lay upon the sinner his sin,
Lay upon the transgressor his transgression,
But be lenient, lest he be cut off,
Be patient, lest he be displaced.
Instead of your bringing on the flood,
Would that a lion had risen up to diminish the human race!
Instead of your bringing on the flood,
Would that a wolf had risen up to diminish the human race!
Instead of your bringing on the flood,
Would that famine had occurred to lay low the land!
Instead of your bringing on the flood,
Would that Erra had risen up to lay low the land!"

Enlil went up into the boat,
Grasping Atrahasis by the hand, he led him out.
He led his wife out and made her kneel by his side.
He touched their foreheads and, standing between them, he blessed them:
"Previously Atrahasis was a human being,
But now Atrahasis and his wife shall become like unto us gods.
Atrahasis shall reside far away, at the mouth of the rivers."
They took Atrahasis far away
And placed him at the mouth of the rivers.
