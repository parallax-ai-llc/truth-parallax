## Overview

Archaeological engagement with the Atrahasis brings distinctive interpretive methods and concerns to this foundational text. This approach illuminates dimensions of the text that may be less visible from other perspectives.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Material Culture
Archaeological interpreters examine the Atrahasis in light of material evidence — artifacts, inscriptions, architectural remains, and other physical evidence that illuminates the world from which the text emerged.

### Historical Reconstruction
Archaeological evidence helps scholars reconstruct the historical contexts described in or presupposed by the Atrahasis, distinguishing between literary representation and historical reality.

### Comparative Epigraphy
The study of inscriptions and written records from the Atrahasis's cultural milieu provides crucial context for understanding its language, concepts, and historical references.
