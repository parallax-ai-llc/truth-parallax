## Overview

Eastern Orthodox reading of Titus emphasizes the apostolic origins of episcopal governance, the sacramental reality of baptism, and the transformative power of grace. The letter's instructions to Titus as Paul's delegate in Crete are understood as establishing patterns of church life that continue in Orthodox ecclesial practice.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Apostolic Delegation and Episcopal Ministry
Titus's appointment of elders under Paul's authority is read as a prototype of episcopal succession. The Orthodox Church sees in this delegation the pattern by which apostolic authority is transmitted through ordination from generation to generation.

### Baptism as Regeneration
The "washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit" (3:5) is understood sacramentally: baptism truly effects new birth and the gift of the Spirit. This passage is read during the Orthodox baptismal liturgy.

### Grace and Theosis
The great grace passage (2:11-14) is read through the lens of theosis: divine grace does not merely forgive but transforms, training believers to participate ever more fully in the divine nature.
