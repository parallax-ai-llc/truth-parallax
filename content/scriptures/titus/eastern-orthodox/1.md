## Appointing Elders in Crete

Eastern Orthodoxy interpretation of Titus 1 centers on patristic commentary and liturgical theology. The key passages — appoint elders in every town (1:5), holding firm to the trustworthy word (1:9), they profess to know God but deny him (1:16) — have been read through the lens of theosis, the liturgical life of the Church, and patristic consensus, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

John chrysostom, theodoret of cyrus, and other eastern fathers gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Titus. The chapter's teaching on appointing elders in crete has shaped Eastern Orthodoxy understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern eastern orthodoxy scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Titus 1 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
