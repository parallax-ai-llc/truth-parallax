## The Grace of God and Good Works

Eastern Orthodoxy interpretation of Titus 3 centers on patristic commentary and liturgical theology. The key passages — the washing of regeneration (3:5), justified by his grace (3:7), devote themselves to good works (3:8) — have been read through the lens of theosis, the liturgical life of the Church, and patristic consensus, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

John chrysostom, theodoret of cyrus, and other eastern fathers gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Titus. The chapter's teaching on the grace of god and good works has shaped Eastern Orthodoxy understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern eastern orthodoxy scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Titus 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
