## The Grace of God and Good Works

Islamic interpretation of Titus 3 centers on comparative theology and Qur'anic parallels. The key passages — the washing of regeneration (3:5), justified by his grace (3:7), devote themselves to good works (3:8) — have been read through the lens of monotheism (tawhid), prophetic tradition, and ethical convergences, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Muslim scholars of comparative religion gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Titus. The chapter's teaching on the grace of god and good works has shaped Islamic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Islamic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Titus 3 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
