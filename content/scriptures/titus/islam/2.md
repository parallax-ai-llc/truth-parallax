## Sound Doctrine and Godly Living

Islamic interpretation of Titus 2 centers on comparative theology and Qur'anic parallels. The key passages — teach what accords with sound doctrine (2:1), the grace of God has appeared (2:11), our great God and Savior Jesus Christ (2:13) — have been read through the lens of monotheism (tawhid), prophetic tradition, and ethical convergences, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Muslim scholars of comparative religion gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Titus. The chapter's teaching on sound doctrine and godly living has shaped Islamic understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Islamic scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Titus 2 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
