## The Grace of God and Good Works

The reminder to "be submissive to rulers and authorities" (3:1) is understood by Gnostic readers as a practical survival strategy rather than a theological endorsement of earthly power. The pneumatic community, small and vulnerable in the Demiurge's world, must avoid unnecessary confrontation with the archons' human representatives. Outward compliance shields the inner work of gnosis from persecution. The instruction to "speak evil of no one" and "show perfect courtesy toward all people" (3:2) reflects the pneumatic understanding that even hylics and psychics carry dormant divine sparks that may one day awaken.

The confession that "we ourselves were once foolish, disobedient, led astray, slaves to various passions and pleasures" (3:3) describes the pre-gnostic condition of every pneumatic soul. Before the awakening of gnosis, even those carrying the divine spark lived in the fog of archontic deception, driven by the material impulses the Demiurge built into the flesh-body. The "washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit" (3:5) is not water baptism but the pneumatic rebirth that occurs when gnosis floods the consciousness, washing away the accumulated sediment of material identification. This Spirit was "poured out on us richly through Jesus Christ our Savior" (3:6), meaning the divine aeon's descent into matter released the Pleroma's light-substance into the archontic cosmos, making gnosis available to all who possess the capacity to receive it.

Being "justified by his grace" and becoming "heirs according to the hope of eternal life" (3:7) positions the pneumatic as a rightful citizen of the Pleroma, an heir whose inheritance was stolen by the Demiurge's cosmic usurpation. The instruction to "avoid foolish controversies, genealogies, dissensions, and quarrels about the law" (3:9) warns against engagement with the Demiurge's theological system on its own terms. These debates are traps: the more energy the pneumatic invests in arguing about the law, the more deeply entangled the soul becomes in the archontic framework. The directive to "have nothing more to do with" a divisive person after two warnings (3:10) protects the pneumatic community from those who, whether through hylic nature or archontic infiltration, would fragment the fellowship and disrupt the transmission of gnosis.
