## Sound Doctrine and Godly Living

Protestant interpretation of Titus 2 centers on sola Scriptura and the Reformation heritage. The key passages — teach what accords with sound doctrine (2:1), the grace of God has appeared (2:11), our great God and Savior Jesus Christ (2:13) — have been read through the lens of the authority of Scripture and justification by faith, generating rich theological reflection across centuries of commentary.

Luther, calvin, and subsequent reformed and evangelical scholars gave significant attention to this chapter, exploring how its themes illuminate the broader message of Titus. The chapter's teaching on sound doctrine and godly living has shaped Protestant understanding of the Christian life, ecclesiology, and eschatology in important ways.

Modern Protestant scholarship continues to engage this chapter with fresh exegetical insight, examining how Titus 2 speaks to contemporary theological and ethical questions while remaining grounded in the interpretive traditions that have sustained the community of faith.
