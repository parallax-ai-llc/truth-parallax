## Overview

Titus is a significant epistle in the New Testament canon, addressing themes of good works, sound doctrine, grace that have generated extensive theological reflection across Christian traditions and beyond. The letter's teaching has shaped doctrine, ethics, and worship across two millennia of interpretation.

## Structure

The letter comprises 3 chapters:

1. **Chapter 1**: Appointing Elders in Crete (16 verses)
2. **Chapter 2**: Sound Doctrine and Godly Living (15 verses)
3. **Chapter 3**: The Grace of God and Good Works (15 verses)

## Cross-Traditional Significance

Titus generates rich cross-traditional engagement:

- **Protestantism**: Emphasizes good works and sound doctrine as central theological themes
- **Catholicism**: Reads the letter through the lens of sacred tradition and sacramental theology
- **Eastern Orthodoxy**: Interprets the text within patristic commentary and liturgical practice
- **Judaism**: Examines the letter's Jewish roots and implications for Jewish-Christian relations
- **Islam**: Engages with ethical parallels while critically assessing Christological claims
- **Gnosticism**: Reads esoteric and spiritual meanings beneath the surface of the text
