## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Titus emphasizes the apostolic appointment of elders, the sacramental significance of baptism, and the inseparability of faith and works. The letter provides important support for Catholic teaching on ordained ministry, baptismal regeneration, and the moral life.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Episcopal Authority
Titus's commission to "appoint elders in every town" (1:5) is read as evidence of apostolic delegation and the origins of episcopal governance. The authority to appoint leaders derives from apostolic commission, supporting the Catholic model of hierarchical church structure.

### Baptismal Regeneration
The reference to "the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit" (3:5) is a primary Catholic proof text for the doctrine of baptismal regeneration — that baptism truly effects spiritual rebirth, not merely symbolizing it.

### Grace and Good Works
Catholic exegesis reads Titus 2:11-14 and 3:4-8 as affirming that salvation originates in grace but necessarily produces good works. This reading supports the Catholic understanding that faith and works cooperate in the economy of salvation.
