Acts 4 records the arrest and trial of Peter and John before the Sanhedrin and the community's response in prayer. Peter's declaration that Jesus is the "stone that was rejected by you, the builders, which has become the cornerstone" (4:11) is a foundational text in Catholic ecclesiology — Christ is the cornerstone, and the Church is built upon him through the apostolic foundation (cf. Ephesians 2:20).

Peter's statement that "there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved" (4:12) is affirmed in Catholic teaching as the basis for the necessity of Christ for salvation, while the Church also recognizes that God's salvific will extends to those who, through no fault of their own, do not know Christ (*Lumen Gentium* 16). This balanced reading distinguishes Catholic interpretation from both exclusivist and relativist positions.

The community's prayer (4:24-31) and the description of their common life (4:32-37) have profoundly influenced Catholic social teaching. The sharing of possessions — "no one said that any of the things that belonged to him was his own" (4:32) — is cited in papal encyclicals as a model of the universal destination of goods, a principle foundational to Catholic social doctrine. The Catechism (CCC 952) connects this passage to the communion of saints and the sharing of spiritual goods.
