Acts 21 describes Paul's journey to Jerusalem and his arrest. Gnostic interpreters read Paul's determination to go to Jerusalem despite prophetic warnings (21:4, 10-14) as a descent into the archontic realm — the city of the Demiurge's Temple, the center of the old dispensation. Paul's willingness to participate in Temple purification rites (21:23-26) is viewed as an accommodation to the psychic church's attachment to material religion.

The Jerusalem church's report that many believers are "zealous for the law" (21:20) confirms, in Gnostic reading, that the Jerusalem community remained trapped in the Demiurge's system. The Law is the Demiurge's instrument of control; zeal for the Law is zeal for spiritual bondage. The tension between Paul's freedom from the Law (as expressed in his epistles) and his accommodation to it (as portrayed in Acts) reflects the fundamental conflict between gnosis and institutional religion.

Paul's arrest by the mob (21:27-36) is read as the archontic powers seizing the pneumatic messenger. The false accusation — that Paul brought a Gentile into the Temple (21:28) — illustrates the Demiurge's concern with material boundaries and purity distinctions. In the Gnostic vision, the true Temple has no barriers; the divine presence is accessible to all who possess the spark, regardless of ethnic or ritual status.
