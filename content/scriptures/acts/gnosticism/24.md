Acts 24 describes Paul's trial before Felix. The prosecution's characterization of Paul as a "ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes" (24:5) is read by Gnostic interpreters as the archontic power's attempt to categorize and control the pneumatic movement. Labels and classifications — "sect," "heresy," "Nazarene" — are instruments of institutional control. The true pneumatic transcends all categories.

Paul's defense — affirming continuity with the faith of the fathers while claiming that the real issue is the resurrection (24:14-21) — is read on two levels by Gnostic interpreters. The exoteric level affirms historical continuity; the esoteric level recognizes that "resurrection" (*anastasis*) in Pauline thought (as the Valentinians understood it) refers not to the reanimation of physical bodies but to the spiritual awakening of the divine spark — the resurrection that has already occurred in the life of the pneumatic.

Felix's interest and procrastination (24:24-27) represent the psychic response to gnosis: intellectually intrigued but unwilling to surrender the attachments that bind one to the material world. The psychic person stands at the threshold of illumination but cannot cross it because he remains attached to power, wealth, and worldly advantage. Felix's hope for a bribe (24:26) is the material world's pathetic attempt to purchase what can only be received as a gift.
