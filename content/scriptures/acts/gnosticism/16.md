Acts 16 describes the second missionary journey, including the Macedonian call and events at Philippi. The Spirit's guidance of Paul — forbidding certain directions, opening others through a vision (16:6-10) — is affirmed in Gnostic reading as authentic pneumatic direction. The Spirit operates through inner illumination and visionary experience, not through institutional channels. Paul's sensitivity to the Spirit's leading marks him, in Gnostic perspective, as a genuine pneumatic even within the canonical narrative.

The conversion of Lydia (16:14) — whose heart the Lord "opened" — is read as an awakening of the divine spark within. The language of opening corresponds to the Gnostic imagery of the sealed soul that must be unlocked through gnosis. Lydia's openness to Paul's message indicates a pre-existing pneumatic receptivity; she was already one of the elect, and Paul's preaching merely triggered her conscious awakening.

Paul and Silas' imprisonment and the miraculous earthquake (16:25-26) are rich in Gnostic symbolism. The prison is the material world; the chains are the bonds of ignorance; the earthquake is the pneumatic power that shatters material constraints. The opened doors and loosened bonds represent the soul's liberation through gnosis. The jailer's question — "What must I do to be saved?" (16:30) — receives a canonical answer ("Believe in the Lord Jesus"), but Gnostic readers would substitute: "Know yourself, and you will know the All."
