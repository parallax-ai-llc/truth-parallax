Acts 7 contains Stephen's speech before the Sanhedrin, a comprehensive retelling of Israel's history. The Orthodox Church venerates Stephen as the Protomartyr — the first Christian witness to die for the faith — and commemorates him on December 27, the day after the Nativity. His speech demonstrates the Orthodox hermeneutical principle that all of Scripture points toward Christ: Abraham, Joseph, and Moses are types whose lives anticipate the coming of the Righteous One.

Stephen's assertion that "the Most High does not dwell in houses made by hands" (7:48) is not read in Orthodox theology as a rejection of sacred architecture. Rather, it corrects the reduction of God's presence to a single location. The Orthodox temple is understood as an image of heaven on earth — its iconography, incense, and liturgy create a space where heaven and earth meet. But God is not confined there; the temple's purpose is to orient the worshiper toward the God who fills all things.

Stephen's martyrdom (7:54-60) is the paradigm of Christian witness in Orthodox tradition. His vision of the heavens opened and Christ standing at the right hand of God (7:55-56) is an eschatological moment — a glimpse of the glory that awaits all the faithful. His prayer for his persecutors (7:60) mirrors Christ's own prayer from the cross and demonstrates the perfection of love that is the fruit of theosis. The Orthodox tradition of praying for enemies and forgiving those who cause harm is rooted in Stephen's example.
