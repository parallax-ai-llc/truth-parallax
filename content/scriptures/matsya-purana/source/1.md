---
title: "Matsya Purana - Chapter 1"
scripture: "matsya-purana"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The great sage Manu, the progenitor of mankind, was once performing austerities on the bank of the river Virini.

**2** While he was offering water oblations, a small fish came into his hands along with the water.

**3** The fish spoke to Manu, saying: "O great King, protect me! I am afraid of the larger fish who will devour me. If you save me, I shall save you in return."

**4** Manu said: "Who art thou that speakest with a human voice? And how wilt thou save me?"

**5** The fish replied: "I am Vishnu, the Supreme Lord, who has taken the form of a fish for the salvation of the world. A great deluge will soon overwhelm the earth. I shall save thee and all living creatures from the flood."

**6** Manu placed the fish in a jar, but it grew so large that he had to transfer it to a tank, then to a lake, and finally to the ocean.

**7** The fish said: "O Manu, when the great flood comes, gather together the seven Rishis, the seeds of all plants, and pairs of all living creatures. Place them in a great boat which I shall provide."

**8** "When the waters rise and cover the earth, tie the boat to my horn with the great serpent Vasuki as a rope. I shall guide you through the flood to safety."

**9** Manu did as the fish commanded. When the deluge came, the waters covered the mountains and all the land was submerged.

**10** The great fish appeared, its golden body stretching across the ocean. Manu tied the boat to its horn, and the fish guided them through the raging waters.

**11** For many years the boat was borne upon the waters. At last the fish brought them to the peak of the Himalayas, where the waters began to recede.

**12** The fish said: "I am Vishnu, who has assumed this form to preserve creation. The Puranas which I shall now narrate contain the knowledge of dharma, artha, kama, and moksha. Listen with devotion."

**13** Thus begins the Matsya Purana, narrated by Lord Vishnu in the form of a fish to Manu, the father of mankind.
