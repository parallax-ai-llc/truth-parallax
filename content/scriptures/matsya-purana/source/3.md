---
title: "Matsya Purana - Chapter 3"
scripture: "matsya-purana"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Fish spoke: Now hear, O Manu, the teaching concerning the duties of kings and the principles of righteous governance.

**2** In the ancient times, the great sages gathered upon the sacred mountain and inquired of Brahma concerning the duties of men and the path to liberation.

**3** Brahma said: "The Supreme Lord has ordained different duties for different ages, castes, and stages of life. By following one's own dharma with devotion, a man attains the highest good."

**4** The sages asked: "What is the highest dharma, O Lord of Creation?"

**5** Brahma replied: "The highest dharma is devotion to the Supreme Being, who pervades all things and sustains all things. By his grace alone is liberation attained."

**6** "The path of devotion is open to all beings, regardless of birth, caste, or station in life. Whoever calls upon the Lord with a sincere heart shall be heard."

**7** "Yet the duties of one's station must not be neglected. The householder must care for his family, the king must protect his subjects, the sage must pursue knowledge and teach others."

**8** "When dharma is upheld, the rains fall in their proper season, the harvests are abundant, and the people live in peace and prosperity."

**9** "When dharma is neglected, drought and famine follow, disease and war afflict the people, and the social order is dissolved."

**10** "Therefore let every being follow the path of righteousness, worship the Supreme Lord with devotion, and serve others with compassion. This is the essence of all the scriptures."

**11** The Fish said: "This teaching, which I have narrated to you, O Manu, is the essence of the Matsya Purana. Whoever hears it with faith and recites it with devotion shall be freed from all sins and shall attain the supreme abode of Vishnu."

**12** Thus spoke the Lord in the form of a fish, and Manu received the teaching with gratitude and transmitted it to his descendants for the welfare of all beings.
