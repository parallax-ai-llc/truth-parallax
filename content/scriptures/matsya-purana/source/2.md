---
title: "Matsya Purana - Chapter 2"
scripture: "matsya-purana"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Fish spoke: O Manu, hear now the account of creation, as it was in the beginning.

**2** Before creation, there was only the Supreme Brahman, without form, without attributes, beyond the reach of mind and speech. He alone existed, one without a second.

**3** When the time for creation came, the Supreme Being willed: "Let me become many." From his thought arose the cosmic waters, and upon the waters he placed the seed of creation.

**4** That seed became a golden egg, brilliant as a thousand suns. Within the egg the Creator, Brahma, was born.

**5** Brahma remained within the egg for a divine year. Then he divided the egg into two halves: the upper half became the heavens, the lower half became the earth.

**6** Between them he placed the atmosphere, and the eight directions, and the eternal abode of the waters.

**7** From his mind he created the seven great Rishis, and from various parts of his body he created the four varnas: the Brahmanas from his mouth, the Kshatriyas from his arms, the Vaishyas from his thighs, and the Shudras from his feet.

**8** He created day and night, the months and the seasons, and the great cycle of the yugas through which time revolves.

**9** The four yugas are: Krita, Treta, Dvapara, and Kali. In the Krita Yuga, dharma stands upon four legs, and all beings are righteous. In each succeeding age, one leg of dharma is lost.

**10** In the Kali Yuga, dharma stands upon one leg only, and the world is filled with strife, hypocrisy, and suffering. Yet even in the Kali Yuga, liberation is possible through devotion to the Supreme Lord.

**11** The Fish said: Know that all creation proceeds from the Supreme Being and returns to him at the end of each cosmic cycle. He is the beginning, the middle, and the end of all things.
