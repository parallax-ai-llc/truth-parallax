---
title: "Thanksgiving Hymns - Chapter 5"
scripture: "thanksgiving-hymns"
chapter: 5
---

**1** I thank Thee, O Lord, for Thou hast enlightened me through Thy truth. In Thy marvellous mysteries, and in Thy lovingkindness to a man of vanity, and in the greatness of Thy mercy to a perverse heart, Thou hast granted me knowledge.

**2** Who is like Thee among the gods, O Lord, and who is according to Thy truth? And who, when he is judged, shall be righteous before Thee?

**3** For no spirit can reply to Thy rebuke nor can any withstand Thy wrath.

**4** Yet Thou bringest all the sons of Thy truth to cleansing before Thee, purifying them from their sins by the greatness of Thy goodness, and by the multitude of Thy mercy, to cause them to stand before Thee forever and ever.

**5** For Thou art an eternal God; all Thy ways are determined for ever and ever, and there is none other beside Thee. And what is a man of Nought and Vanity that he should understand Thy marvellous mighty deeds?

**6** From the dust Thou hast taken me, and from clay I have been shaped. In what way shall dust and ashes reply, and how understand Thy designs?

**7** From a source of darkness is the nature of man, and from a fountain of darkness have his beginnings proceeded. Iniquity and sin belong to his youth.

**8** And the whole nature of man — in guilt he walks all the length of his days. And were it not for Thy goodness, man could not be made righteous.

**9** And how should I set a step unless Thou hadst traced the way? Without Thy guidance, how could my foot be set upon the path? There is no strength in my right foot, and there is no standing without Thy might.

**10** Behold, Thou art the Prince of gods and King of the glorious ones, Lord of every spirit and Ruler of every creature. Without Thee nothing is done, and nothing is known without Thy will.

**11** Beside Thee there is nothing, and nothing can compare with Thee in strength; in the presence of Thy glory there is nothing, and Thy might is without price.

**12** Who among Thy great and marvellous creatures can stand in the presence of Thy glory?

**13** How then can he who returns to his dust? For Thy glory's sake alone hast Thou made all these things.

**14** Blessed art Thou, O Lord, God of mercy and rich in grace, for Thou hast made known Thy wisdom unto Thy servant, that he may know all these things.

**15** Blessed art Thou, who hast created the spirits of knowledge, to establish Thy glory. Blessed art Thou, who art long-suffering, abundant in truth and rich in mercy.

**16** Blessed art Thou, for in Thy wisdom Thou hast appointed the counsel of truth, and in Thy power Thou hast ordained the mighty deeds, and in Thy knowledge Thou hast established all things.
