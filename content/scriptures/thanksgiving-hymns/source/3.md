---
title: "Thanksgiving Hymns - Chapter 3"
scripture: "thanksgiving-hymns"
chapter: 3
---

**1** I thank Thee, O Lord, for Thou hast placed my soul in the bundle of the living, and hast hedged me against all the snares of the Pit.

**2** For violent men have sought after my soul, but they did not lay hold on me when Thou didst set my feet upon the rock.

**3** For Thou hast established my edifice on rock, and everlasting foundations serve me for my ground, and all my walls are a tried wall which nothing can shake.

**4** And Thou, O my God, hast given me to the weary as a holy counsel; Thou hast established me in Thy Covenant, and my tongue is as that of Thy disciples.

**5** But the spirit of error has no word, and all the sons of guilt are dumb. For those who spread wickedness have locked their lips before an unjust tongue.

**6** But with broken teeth the Lord has silenced the young lions, and Thou hast locked the jaws of the lioness.

**7** And I was like a man forsaken, and there was no help for me; for my sorrow blossomed into bitterness, and incurable pain that would not cease.

**8** My spirit was engulfed within me and my soul failed me even unto death. My strength departed from my frame, and my heart ran out like water, and my flesh melted like wax, and the strength of my loins was turned to fear.

**9** My arm was torn from its socket, and I could not move my hand. My foot was caught in shackles, and my knees were dissolved like water.

**10** I could take neither pace nor step; heaviness replaced my light footstep. I stumbled and there was no strength in me.

**11** But Thou, O God, hast put into my heart the counsel of truth and understanding, and wisdom and knowledge and mighty valour.

**12** And Thou hast given me authority over the hidden things, and strength has been established in me. And the abundance of Thy forgiveness is with my steps, and the multitude of Thy mercy is with the judgment of me.

**13** And until I am old Thou wilt care for me; for my father knew me not, and my mother abandoned me to Thee.

**14** For Thou art a father to all the sons of Thy truth, and as a woman who tenderly loves her babe, so dost Thou rejoice in them; and as a foster-father bearing a child in his lap, so carest Thou for all Thy creatures.

**15** Who shall be accounted just in Thy judgment? No man can answer Thy rebuke, for all the works of righteousness are as nothing before Thee.

**16** And the truth is known only by the grace of God, and only by His goodness is man made righteous. And by the abundance of His mercy are all the sinners pardoned, and by the multitude of His grace are they established in His truth.
