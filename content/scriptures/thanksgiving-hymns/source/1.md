---
title: "Thanksgiving Hymns - Chapter 1"
scripture: "thanksgiving-hymns"
chapter: 1
---

**1** I thank Thee, O Lord, for Thou hast redeemed my soul from the pit, and from the Sheol of Abaddon Thou hast raised me up to everlasting height.

**2** I walk on limitless level ground, and I know there is hope for him whom Thou hast shaped from dust for the everlasting Council.

**3** Thou hast cleansed a perverse spirit of great sin that it may stand with the host of the Holy Ones, and that it may enter into community with the congregation of the Sons of Heaven.

**4** Thou hast allotted to man an everlasting destiny amidst the spirits of knowledge, that he may praise Thy Name in a common rejoicing and recount Thy marvels before all Thy works.

**5** And yet I, a creature of clay, what am I? Kneaded with water, what is my worth and my power? For I stood in the realm of wickedness and my lot was with the damned.

**6** The soul of the poor one was carried about in great tribulation, and miseries of suffering accompanied my steps.

**7** When all the traps of the pit were opened, and all the snares of wickedness were spread, and the net of the damned was upon the face of the waters;

**8** When all the arrows of the pit flew without cease, and were shot without hope of return; when the line fell upon judgment, and the lot of anger upon the forsaken;

**9** When the outpouring of fury was upon the hypocrites, and it was a time of wrath for all worthlessness;

**10** When the torrents of Belial burst their high banks — torrents that are like fire devouring all their shores, a fire that consumes until there be no more;

**11** That destroys every tree, green and barren, on their banks; that lashes with whirlwinds of flame until none that drink there are left — a fire that consumes the foundations of clay and the tract of dry land;

**12** The bases of the mountains are given over to burning, and the roots of flint become torrents of pitch. And it devours to the Great Abyss.

**13** And the torrents of Belial pour into Abaddon, and the creatures of the Abyss make an uproar amid the roaring of mud and mire.

**14** The earth cries aloud at the ruin which is wrought in the world, and all its creatures shriek; and all who are upon it go mad and melt in utter ruin.

**15** For God thunders with the roar of His might, and His holy dwelling resounds with the truth of His glory; and the host of heaven utter their voice, and the eternal foundations melt and shake.

**16** And the war of the heavenly warriors scours the world and turns not back until the full end decreed forever; there is nothing like it.
