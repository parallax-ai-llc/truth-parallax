## Knowledge and Revelation: The Mysteries of God

Chapter 2 celebrates the community's access to divine mysteries (*razei pele*) — hidden knowledge revealed by God to the Teacher and through him to the community. The hymns express gratitude for illumination that sets the community apart from all others: "You have opened my ears to wondrous mysteries, though I am a creature of clay."

For the Essene community, this revelatory knowledge was not merely intellectual but constitutive of their identity. To know God's mysteries was to be chosen by God; ignorance of these truths was a mark of those outside the covenant. The hymns' language of "opening the ears" and "illuminating the heart" describes a divine transformation that enabled the elect to perceive what remained hidden to the rest of Israel.

The emphasis on revealed knowledge also undergirded the community's interpretive authority. Their distinctive readings of Scripture — the pesher interpretations that found contemporary meaning in ancient prophecy — were grounded in the conviction that God had revealed to the Teacher the true meaning of the prophets' words. This chapter's hymns provided the theological foundation for that hermeneutical confidence.
