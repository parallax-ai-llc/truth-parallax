**1** The file clerk is a mechanism of the mind which selects the data the auditor needs. The auditor asks for material and the file clerk presents it.

**2** The file clerk should be trusted. It will present the material in the order in which it should be addressed. The auditor should not attempt to direct the case against the file clerk's selections.

**3** In processing, engrams are approached from the earliest accessible incident on the time track. Earlier engrams must be reduced before later engrams can be fully addressed.

**4** The basic-basic is the earliest engram on the entire time track. Reaching and reducing the basic-basic produces the most dramatic improvements.

**5** Chains of engrams exist when later incidents of similar content are laid down on top of earlier ones. The chain must be traced back to its earliest link for full resolution.

**6** When an engram is properly reduced, the preclear experiences a release of emotion. He may laugh, feel suddenly lighter, or experience a sudden insight into the pattern of his aberrations.

**7** The erasure of an engram is permanent. Once fully reduced, it does not return. The aberrations and psychosomatic ills it caused disappear and do not recur.
