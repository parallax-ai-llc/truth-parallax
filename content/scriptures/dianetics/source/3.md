**1** The reactive mind operates on the principle of everything in an engram equals everything in an engram. This is the basic computation of the reactive mind.

**2** An engram is a complete recording of a moment of physical pain and unconsciousness. It is recorded by the reactive mind, not by the analytical mind, which shuts down during moments of pain.

**3** The engram is a recording which is made below the level of consciousness. When a man is knocked unconscious, the reactive mind records every perception present during the period of unconsciousness.

**4** These recordings are stored as engrams and have command power over the individual. They are not memories in the ordinary sense. They are complete recordings with full force.

**5** An engram can contain all perceptions of the incident: sight, sound, smell, taste, organic sensation, pain, and the content of speech heard during the period.

**6** The spoken content of an engram acts as a command upon the analytical mind when the engram is restimulated. This command power is literal and absolute.

**7** The individual does not know the source of these commands. He acts upon them without knowing why, attributing his actions to rational motives when in fact he is being driven by the reactive mind.
