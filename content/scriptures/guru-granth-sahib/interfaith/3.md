## Interfaith Reading

An interfaith reading of Bhagat Bani — Hymns of the Saints approaches its 25 verses as a testimony to the possibility of genuine spiritual recognition across religious boundaries. The Guru Granth Sahib's unique compilation — incorporating Hindu, Muslim, and Sikh voices within a single canonical scripture — challenges the assumption that authentic religious truth is confined within the boundaries of a single tradition. This section contributes to the interfaith conversation by demonstrating that profound spiritual insights can be honored without erasing the distinctive identity of each contributor.

The themes expressed in these verses speak to concerns shared across religious traditions: the nature of ultimate reality, the meaning of human existence, the path to spiritual liberation, the ethical imperatives of justice and compassion, and the relationship between inner spiritual life and outward social engagement. Interfaith readers discover in the Guru Granth Sahib not a syncretistic merging of traditions but a respectful gathering of diverse voices united by a shared orientation toward the divine.

For contemporary interfaith practitioners, this section offers both inspiration and practical wisdom. The Guru Granth Sahib demonstrates that deep commitment to one's own spiritual tradition need not entail rejection of truth wherever it is found. Its model of inclusive scripture-making invites reflection on how religious communities today might honor the insights of others while maintaining the integrity of their own paths.
