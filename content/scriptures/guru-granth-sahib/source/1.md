---
title: "Asa di Var - The Ballad of Hope"
scripture: "guru-granth-sahib"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Asa di Var, composed by Guru Nanak, is recited in the early morning. It speaks of the nature of God and the path of righteousness.

**2** From the True Lord came the air, and from the air came water. From water He created the three worlds and infused His light into every heart.

**3** The True One was there from the beginning. The True One is now also. The True One ever shall be. O Nanak, the True One shall always be.

**4** By thinking, He cannot be reduced to thought, even by thinking hundreds of thousands of times. By remaining silent, inner silence is not obtained, even by remaining lovingly absorbed deep within.

**5** He Himself creates and He Himself destroys. He Himself bestows and He Himself takes away. He who created the creation watches over it. O Nanak, no one can challenge His command.

**6** He created the world with one word. From that word, hundreds of thousands of rivers began to flow. What power do I have to describe the magnificence of His creation?

**7** I am not worthy to make even one sacrifice to Him. Whatever pleases Him is the only good done. You are forever and ever the Formless One, O Lord.
