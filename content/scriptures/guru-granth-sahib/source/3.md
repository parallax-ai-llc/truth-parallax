---
title: "Anand Sahib - The Hymn of Bliss"
scripture: "guru-granth-sahib"
chapter: 3
---

**1** Anand Sahib, composed by Guru Amar Das, the third Guru, is the Song of Bliss, recited at all Sikh ceremonies of joy.

**2** O my mind, remain always with the Lord. Remain always with the Lord, O my mind, and all sufferings will be forgotten.

**3** He will accept you as His own, and all your affairs will be resolved. The Almighty Lord is all-powerful. Why should we forget Him?

**4** O my mind, the Lord is ever with you. Through the Guru's teachings, enjoy the Lord's love. Leave behind all the frauds and deceits, and O my mind, remain always with the Lord.

**5** O beloved saints, know that the true merchandise is the Lord's Name. Blessed is the merchant who deals in it. His stores are filled with the Name.

**6** Wherever I look, I see the Lord. Without the True Guru, no one finds Him. But through the Guru, God is revealed within the heart.

**7** O Nanak, the Lord Himself is the player. He Himself is the spectator. He Himself is the director. He Himself watches the show.
