## Overview

The Holy Piby, sometimes called the "Black Man's Bible," was written by Robert Athlyi Rogers of Anguilla between 1913 and 1917 and published in 1924. It is one of the foundational texts of the early Rastafari and Afro-Caribbean spiritual movements, presenting a radical reinterpretation of biblical narrative centered on the divine election of Black people and the spiritual primacy of Ethiopia. Although not universally accepted across all Rastafari mansions, the Holy Piby played a crucial role in shaping the theological and political consciousness that gave rise to the Rastafari movement in Jamaica.

## Structure

The Holy Piby is divided into four books:

1. **Book 1 — Athlyi and the Host of Heaven**: Visionary encounters and divine revelations establishing the prophetic authority of Athlyi Rogers
2. **Book 2 — Aggregation**: The establishment and mission of the Ethiopian Church as a unifying institution for people of African descent
3. **Book 3 — The Facts of the Apostles**: Accounts of the apostles' missionary work among the African diaspora
4. **Book 4 — Precepts and Commandments**: Moral and ethical instructions for the community of the faithful

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Rastafari**: A foundational proto-Rastafari text that articulated the theological vision of Black divine election and Ethiopian primacy
- **Academic**: A key document for the study of Afro-Caribbean religious movements, diaspora theology, and anti-colonial spirituality
- **Comparative**: Illuminates the process by which colonized peoples reinterpret dominant religious narratives to affirm their own dignity and destiny
- **Ethiopian Orthodox**: Engages with the Ethiopian Christian heritage as a symbol of African spiritual authenticity and independence
- **Protestant**: Raises questions about biblical interpretation, canonical authority, and the social context of prophetic claims
- **Interfaith**: Offers a powerful witness to the intersection of religion, racial justice, and liberation

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Black Divine Election | The teaching that Black people are chosen by God for a special destiny |
| Ethiopianism | Ethiopia as the spiritual homeland and symbol of African greatness |
| Prophetic Authority | The visionary experience of Athlyi Rogers as the basis for a new dispensation |
| Diaspora Solidarity | The call for unity among people of African descent worldwide |
| Moral Law | Ethical commandments oriented toward communal righteousness and self-determination |
