## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Holy Piby engages with Robert Athlyi Rogers's prophetic text from the distinctive vantage point of the Comparative tradition. This perspective brings particular attention to the text's claims of divine revelation, its theology of Black election, its engagement with Ethiopian heritage, and its vision of communal righteousness. The Comparative interpretation illuminates both the Holy Piby's internal theological logic and its significance within broader religious and cultural contexts.
