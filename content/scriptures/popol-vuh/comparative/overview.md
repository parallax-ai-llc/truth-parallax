## Overview

The Popol Vuh offers extraordinarily rich material for comparative mythology and comparative religion. Its creation narrative — featuring multiple failed attempts to create humanity, culminating in the successful creation from maize — invites comparison with creation accounts from around the world, including Genesis, the Enuma Elish, the Vedic Purusha Sukta, and various indigenous creation narratives. The Hero Twins' descent to the underworld and their triumph over the Lords of Death parallels the descent narratives of Mesopotamian (Inanna/Ishtar), Greek (Orpheus, Persephone), and other traditions.

Comparative analysis reveals both shared mythological patterns (the cosmic tree, the flood, the culture hero, the descent to the underworld) and the distinctive features of the Maya mythological imagination: the centrality of maize as the substance of human creation, the emphasis on divination and calendrical knowledge, the concept of multiple creations and destructions, and the intimate connection between cosmic mythology and political legitimacy.
