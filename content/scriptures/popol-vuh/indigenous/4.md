The conception of the Hero Twins begins when Xquic, a maiden of Xibalba, approaches the calabash tree bearing One Hunahpu's skull. The skull spits into her hand, and she conceives Hunahpu and Xbalanque. This scene carries profound significance in K'iche' understanding of how life emerges from the realm of death. Xquic's name means "Blood Woman" or "Blood Moon," and her pregnancy represents the transfer of vital force (*k'ux*) from the underworld back to the surface of the earth.

Xquic's subsequent trial before the Lords of Xibalba, who demand proof of her pregnancy through sacrifice, introduces themes of feminine courage and resourcefulness. She substitutes a ball of tree sap and cochineal for her heart, deceiving the death lords and escaping to the surface world. For K'iche' women's traditions, Xquic exemplifies the capacity of mothers to navigate hostile powers to protect their children. Her journey from Xibalba to the surface mirrors the passage through pregnancy and birth itself — a descent into vulnerability followed by emergence with new life.

Upon reaching the surface, Xquic must prove herself to One Hunahpu's mother, Xmucane, who is skeptical of her claim. Xmucane sends her to harvest a field of maize that contains only a single stalk. Through prayer, Xquic calls upon the guardian spirits of food — the spirits of cacao, of maize, of sustenance — and the single stalk multiplies to fill her net. This episode establishes the principle that the land responds to those who approach it with proper ceremony and intention. It is not mere agricultural technique but spiritual relationship that produces abundance.

The birth and early childhood of the Hero Twins is marked by adversity. Their half-brothers, One Monkey and One Artisan (sons of One Hunahpu by an earlier wife), resent the newcomers and attempt to harm them. The twins endure mistreatment with patience, drawing on their innate connection to the divine plan. K'iche' Elders teach this section as a lesson in perseverance: those born for a great purpose will face jealousy and opposition, but their gifts cannot be suppressed. The twins' musical and artistic talents also emerge during this period, connecting creative expression to sacred calling in K'iche' thought.
