---
title: "Popol Vuh - Chapter 4"
scripture: "popol-vuh"
chapter: 4
---

**1** And so they tried to make a body again. "Let us try again. Already the dawn draws near. Let us make a provider, a nurturer. How else can we be invoked and remembered on the face of the earth? We have already tried with our first creatures, but we could not make them praise and venerate us."

**2** "So let us try to make obedient, respectful beings who will nourish and sustain us," they said. So then was the making, the modeling of people out of mud, out of earth.

**3** But it didn't work out. It was just separating, just crumbling, just dissolving, just softening, just disintegrating. Its head was not well formed. Its face could only look in one direction. Its face was lopsided. It couldn't look around. It talked at first, but senselessly. It was quickly dissolving in the water.

**4** "It won't last," said the makers. "It seems to be dwindling away, so let it just dwindle. It can't walk and it can't multiply, so let it be merely a thought," they said.

**5** So they broke it down and dismantled what they had built and what they had modeled. And then they said: "What is there for us to make that would turn out well, that would succeed in keeping our days and praying to us?" they said.
