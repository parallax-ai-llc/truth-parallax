## Overview

The Popol Vuh presents a sophisticated theology of creation, sacrifice, and cosmic renewal. The divine creators — Heart of Sky (Huracan) and Sovereign Plumed Serpent (Q'uq'umatz/Kukulkan) — create the world through dialogue and deliberation, seeking beings capable of worshipping them, remembering their names, and keeping the sacred calendar. The theological vision is one of reciprocity: the gods need human worship and remembrance just as humans need divine sustenance and guidance.

The theology of sacrifice is central to the Popol Vuh. The Hero Twins' self-sacrifice in Xibalba — allowing themselves to be killed in order to be reborn and ultimately defeat the Lords of Death — establishes the pattern of sacrificial renewal that underlies Maya religious practice. The creation of humans from maize reinforces this theology: just as maize must be planted, die in the ground, and be reborn as a new plant, so human life participates in the cosmic cycle of death and renewal.
