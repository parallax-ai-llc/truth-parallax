## Mahayana Interpretation

This section presents the cosmic Buddha Vairocana and the lotus treasury world-system (*Padmagarbha-lokadhatu*), an infinite ocean of worlds arranged in a vast lotus formation, each world containing its own Buddha and assembly. Within the Mahayana tradition, this passage is understood as a direct expression of the Buddha's enlightened vision of reality — the world as seen from the perspective of omniscient awareness, where every particle contains the totality and the totality is expressed in every particle.

The Huayan school developed its entire philosophical system from passages such as this one, articulating the fourfold dharmadhatu: the realm of phenomena (*shi*), the realm of principle (*li*), the non-obstruction of principle and phenomena (*lishi wu'ai*), and the non-obstruction of phenomena with phenomena (*shishi wu'ai*). This fourfold framework allows the Huayan thinkers to account for both the ultimate unity and the conventional diversity of experience.

The practical implications of this teaching are profound: if every phenomenon contains the totality, then practice and realization are not separate, the ordinary and the sacred are not two, and every moment of experience is the complete expression of Buddhahood. This understanding underpins the Mahayana confidence that enlightenment is not distant but immediately available.
