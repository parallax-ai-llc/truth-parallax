## Tibetan Buddhist Reading

This section presents the Buddha's initial awakening display, in which infinite worlds are adorned with wondrous phenomena as the Tathagata sits beneath the Bodhi tree, his enlightenment simultaneously pervading every realm of existence. Tibetan scholars engage with this material primarily through the Madhyamaka and Yogacara philosophical frameworks, reading the Avatamsaka's visionary content as an expression of the two truths — conventional and ultimate — in their non-dual unity.

The Dashabhumika section of the Avatamsaka is particularly significant in Tibetan Buddhism, where the ten bodhisattva stages form a central organizing structure for the path to Buddhahood. Tibetan commentators such as Tsongkhapa integrate the Avatamsaka's account of the bhumis with the Madhyamaka understanding of emptiness and the Yogacara analysis of consciousness to produce a comprehensive map of spiritual development.

In the Nyingma tradition, the Avatamsaka's vision of the dharmadhatu resonates with the Dzogchen teaching that all phenomena are the spontaneous display (*lhun grub*) of rigpa (pure awareness). The entire cosmos, as described in the Avatamsaka, is understood as the self-manifestation of primordial awareness — infinite, luminous, and without obstruction.
