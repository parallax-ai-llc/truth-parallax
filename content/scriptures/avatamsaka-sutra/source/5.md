---
title: "Avatamsaka Sutra Section 5: Entering the Dharma Realm (Gandavyuha)"
scripture: "avatamsaka-sutra"
chapter: 5
---

**1** At that time, the youth Sudhana, having been inspired by the Bodhisattva Manjushri, set out on a great pilgrimage, seeking spiritual teachers throughout the land. Manjushri told him: "Go south, good son, and seek out spiritual friends. A spiritual friend is the very foundation of the Bodhisattva path."

**2** Sudhana visited the bhikshu Megashri on Mount Sugriva. Megashri said: "I have attained the light of the Dharma by which I see all the Buddhas of the ten directions. I see them on the mountain peaks, in the valleys, in the forests, everywhere." He directed Sudhana to seek further teachers.

**3** Sudhana visited the bhikshuni Simhavijurmbhita, who said: "I have attained the liberation called 'All Lions' Yawn.' When I teach the Dharma, all who hear it become free from fear and attain fearlessness."

**4** Sudhana visited the physician Megha, who said: "I know the science of medicine that heals all diseases. But the greatest disease is the disease of the defilements, and the greatest medicine is the Dharma."

**5** Sudhana visited the night goddess Vasanti, who said: "I have cultivated this liberation for as many aeons as atoms in untold Buddha-lands. Wherever there are beings who suffer in the darkness of night, I appear to comfort them and reveal the light of the Dharma."

**6** Sudhana visited the youth Shilpabhijna, who said: "Through mastery of the arts and sciences, I am able to teach beings according to their inclinations. Some are drawn to architecture, some to writing, some to music — through each art, I reveal the way of the Bodhisattva."

**7** Sudhana visited the elder Saradhvaja, who said: "I practice the contemplation of the Buddha. In every thought-moment, I see all the Buddhas of the past, present, and future, in all their lands, teaching all beings."

**8** Sudhana visited King Anala, who said: "I appear to punish evildoers, but in truth I do not harm anyone. Through the appearance of punishment, beings are frightened away from evil and turned toward the good. My seeming wrath is nothing but compassion."

**9** After visiting fifty-three teachers in all — monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, boys, girls, gods, spirits, and kings — each revealing a different gate of liberation, Sudhana came at last to the tower of the Bodhisattva Maitreya.

**10** Maitreya opened the doors of his tower, and Sudhana entered. Inside, he saw the entire cosmos reflected in every part of the tower, world-systems within world-systems without end. Then Maitreya said: "Go now to the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra." Before Samantabhadra, Sudhana saw in every pore of the Bodhisattva's body innumerable universes, and in each universe the complete career of a Bodhisattva from first aspiration to supreme enlightenment. Thus the pilgrimage of Sudhana was complete.
