---
title: "Avatamsaka Sutra Section 4: The Vows of Samantabhadra"
scripture: "avatamsaka-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The great Bodhisattva Samantabhadra addressed the assembly and said: "Even if all the Buddhas of the ten directions were to speak for as many aeons as atoms in an inconceivable number of Buddha-lands, they could not fully describe the merits and virtues of the Tathagata."

**2** "Those who wish to perfect these merits and virtues should cultivate ten vast vows. What are the ten? First, to pay homage and respect to all Buddhas. Second, to praise the Tathagatas. Third, to make abundant offerings. Fourth, to repent misdeeds and evil karma."

**3** "Fifth, to rejoice in others' merits and virtues. Sixth, to request the Buddhas to teach the Dharma. Seventh, to request the Buddhas to remain in the world. Eighth, to follow the teachings of the Buddhas at all times. Ninth, to accommodate and benefit all sentient beings. Tenth, to transfer all merits and virtues universally."

**4** "In paying homage to all Buddhas: in every land, in every atom, in every particle of dust, there are Buddhas as numerous as atoms in all the lands. Before each and every Buddha, I appear in bodies as numerous as atoms in all the lands, and with profound reverence of body, speech, and mind, I pay homage to them all."

**5** "In making offerings: with the finest flowers, the finest garlands, music, perfumes, parasols, canopies, and other adornments, in quantities as vast as oceans, I make offerings to all the Buddhas. But the greatest offering of all is the offering of practice: the offering of practice according to the Dharma surpasses all material offerings."

**6** "In repenting misdeeds: whatever evil karma I have created in the past, on account of beginningless greed, hatred, and delusion, through my body, speech, and mind, I now repent and reform all of it."

**7** "In accommodating all sentient beings: throughout the oceans of future ages, I shall accommodate all sentient beings. Toward all beings I shall practice as if they were my own parents, my own teachers, my own family. I shall be a doctor to the sick, a guide to those who have lost their way, a light to those in darkness."

**8** "In transferring all merits: all the merit I accumulate, from making offerings and paying homage, from repenting and rejoicing, from requesting teaching and requesting the Buddhas to remain — all this merit I transfer to all sentient beings throughout the dharma-realm and the realm of empty space."

**9** "If anyone should hear these great vows and give rise to faith, that person will never retreat from supreme enlightenment. If anyone should recite and uphold these vows, that person will leave behind all evil destinies and be free from all demons and obstacles."

**10** "At the moment of death, when all faculties deteriorate and all relatives depart, when all power and status are left behind, only these vows of Samantabhadra will accompany one, guiding one forward in every moment, and in an instant one will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss."
