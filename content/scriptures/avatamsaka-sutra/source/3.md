---
title: "Avatamsaka Sutra Section 3: The Ten Practices and Ten Dedications"
scripture: "avatamsaka-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Bodhisattva cultivates ten practices: the practice of giving joy, the practice of beneficial action, the practice of non-opposition, the practice of indomitability, the practice of non-confusion, the practice of good manifestation, the practice of non-attachment, the practice of that which is difficult to attain, the practice of good teaching, and the practice of truth.

**2** In the practice of giving joy, the Bodhisattva becomes a great giver: giving wealth, giving Dharma, giving fearlessness. Whatever beings need, the Bodhisattva provides, never seeking reward, never harboring regret.

**3** In the practice of beneficial action, the Bodhisattva upholds pure moral conduct, not for the sake of personal liberation but for the benefit of all sentient beings. The Bodhisattva takes the suffering of all beings upon oneself.

**4** In the practice of non-opposition, the Bodhisattva cultivates patience. When reviled, the Bodhisattva does not revile in return. When struck, the Bodhisattva does not strike back. The Bodhisattva endures all hardship for the sake of beings.

**5** In the practice of indomitability, the Bodhisattva exerts great effort in the cultivation of all virtues. For countless aeons the Bodhisattva strives without weariness, without discouragement, without turning back.

**6** The ten dedications are: the dedication of saving all beings, the indestructible dedication, the dedication equal to all Buddhas, the dedication reaching all places, the dedication of inexhaustible treasuries of merit, the dedication of all wholesome roots, the dedication equally adapting to all beings, the dedication with the character of true suchness, the unbound liberated dedication, and the dedication equal to the cosmos.

**7** Through the first dedication, the Bodhisattva dedicates all merit to the salvation of all beings without exception, including those in the lowest hells, praying that they may be released from suffering and attain supreme enlightenment.

**8** Through the dedication equal to all Buddhas, the Bodhisattva dedicates merit in the manner of all past, present, and future Buddhas, following their way of dedication, their intention, their scope, and their method.

**9** Through the dedication with the character of true suchness, the Bodhisattva realizes that all dharmas are of the nature of suchness, and dedicates all merit without grasping at any dharma as real, without abandoning any being as unworthy.

**10** Through the dedication equal to the cosmos, the Bodhisattva makes a dedication as vast as the cosmos itself, as enduring as the cosmos, as inexhaustible as the cosmos — that all beings may attain liberation throughout all time and in all places.
