---
title: "Avatamsaka Sutra Section 1: The Wonderful Adornments of the Leaders of the World"
scripture: "avatamsaka-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, at the site of enlightenment, having just realized true awareness. The ground was solid and firm, made of diamond, adorned with exquisite jewel discs and myriad precious flowers, with pure clear crystals.

**2** The ocean of characteristics of the Tathagata is boundless; infinite wisdom is universally able to enlighten the world. The marks and embellishments of the body are equal in number to sentient beings; all appear before beings according to their kind.

**3** The body of the Buddha emits great beams of light, and each beam of light contains innumerable Buddhas. The Buddhas appear in all the worlds of the ten directions, showing the practices of Bodhisattvas in their various stages.

**4** In every atom of every land, the Bodhisattvas fill the cosmos, each one cultivating the vows and practices of universal good, each one adorned with all merits and virtues.

**5** All things in the universe arise from the mind. The mind is like a master painter who can paint all the worlds. The five aggregates all arise from the mind. There is nothing in the world that is not created by the mind.

**6** As is the mind, so is the Buddha. As is the Buddha, so are sentient beings. The mind and the Buddha and sentient beings — these three are without distinction. All Buddhas know that everything arises from the mind.

**7** In every single particle of dust throughout the universe there are inconceivable numbers of Buddha-lands. In each land there is a Buddha, and each Buddha is surrounded by an ocean-wide assembly of Bodhisattvas.

**8** The realm of reality is one. The Buddhas appear within it in response to beings. Though the lands where they appear are different, the empty sky is one and has no diversity.

**9** The Buddha's body is like empty space, without substance, without boundaries. Appearing in all worlds for the sake of beings, this is the way of the universal body of the Tathagata.

**10** The virtues of the Buddha are as boundless as the sky is vast, appearing before all sentient beings, and the causes and conditions of all are cleared.
