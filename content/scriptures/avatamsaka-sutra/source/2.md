---
title: "Avatamsaka Sutra Section 2: The Ten Stages (Dasabhumika)"
scripture: "avatamsaka-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The Bodhisattva Vajragarbha entered samadhi and, arising from samadhi, spoke thus: "Children of the Buddha, there are ten stages of the Bodhisattva. What are the ten? The first is the stage of Joy, the second the stage of Purity, the third the stage of Radiance."

**2** "The fourth is the stage of Blazing Wisdom, the fifth the stage of Difficult to Conquer, the sixth the stage of Facing Forward, the seventh the stage of Far-reaching, the eighth the stage of Immovable, the ninth the stage of Good Intelligence, and the tenth the stage of Cloud of Dharma."

**3** "The Bodhisattva of the first stage, the Stage of Joy, gives rise to immeasurable joy, having first aroused the mind of supreme enlightenment. Reflecting on the Buddhas, reflecting on the Dharma, reflecting on the Bodhisattvas, the mind becomes filled with great joy."

**4** "The Bodhisattva of the first stage cultivates ten great vows: to honor and serve all Buddhas, to uphold and propagate the Dharma, to attend the assemblies of all Buddhas, to practice all Bodhisattva conduct, to ripen all sentient beings."

**5** "To perceive all Buddha-lands, to purify one's own land, to enter the great vehicle with one mind, to benefit all beings wherever one goes, and to attain supreme enlightenment together with all beings."

**6** "At the second stage, the Stage of Purity, the Bodhisattva naturally fulfills the ten wholesome courses of action and purifies moral conduct. The Bodhisattva reflects: 'Beings are suffering because of unwholesome action. I shall teach them the way of purity.'"

**7** "At the third stage, the Stage of Radiance, the Bodhisattva gains the light of the Dharma. Having contemplated the impermanence and suffering of all conditioned things, great compassion arises. The Bodhisattva attains the four meditations and the four immeasurable minds."

**8** "At the sixth stage, the Stage of Facing Forward, the Bodhisattva contemplates dependent origination in both forward and reverse order and realizes that all things are like illusions, like dreams, like reflections, like echoes, empty and without self-nature."

**9** "At the eighth stage, the Stage of Immovable, the Bodhisattva attains the acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. No effort is needed; all virtues flow spontaneously, as water flows downward without effort."

**10** "At the tenth stage, the Stage of Cloud of Dharma, the Bodhisattva sits on a great lotus throne in a splendid palace. The Buddhas of the ten directions emit light that enters the crown of the Bodhisattva's head, and the Bodhisattva receives the consecration of a Dharma-king, ready to become a fully enlightened Buddha."
