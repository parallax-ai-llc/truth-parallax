## Overview

Catholic interpretation of the Song of Solomon has been profoundly shaped by the mystical tradition. St. Bernard of Clairvaux's 86 sermons on the Song (he only reached chapter 3!) established the dominant Catholic reading: the Song depicts the soul's mystical union with God. This was extended by St. John of the Cross and St. Teresa of Avila, who made the Song central to Carmelite spirituality.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Mystical-Allegorical
The primary Catholic tradition: the Bridegroom is Christ (or God), and the Bride is the individual soul or the Church. The Song narrates the stages of mystical ascent — purgation, illumination, union.

### Marian
In Catholic devotion, the Bride has also been identified with Mary. Several Song passages appear in the liturgy for Marian feasts: "You are all beautiful, my love" (4:7) is applied to the Immaculate Conception.

### Theology of the Body
John Paul II's *Theology of the Body* recovered the literal, nuptial meaning alongside the allegorical, seeing human sexual love as a genuine icon of divine love.

## Theological Emphases

- **Bridal Mysticism**: The soul as bride of Christ
- **Stages of Prayer**: The Song maps the mystical journey
- **Sacramental Marriage**: Human love as a sign of divine love
- **Beauty and Goodness**: The celebration of bodily beauty within a sacramental framework
