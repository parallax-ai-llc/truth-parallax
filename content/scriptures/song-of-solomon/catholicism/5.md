## The Beloved Knocks

Catholic interpretation of Song of Solomon 5 draws on the rich mystical tradition — from Origen through Bernard of Clairvaux to John of the Cross — reading the poem's love imagery as an allegory of the soul's journey toward union with God.

## Key Themes

### Beloved and Myrrh

The chapter's imagery of beloved and myrrh is read in the Catholic mystical tradition as depicting stages of the spiritual life. The soul moves from initial desire through purification toward the transforming union described by the great Carmelite mystics.

### Search

The theme of search has been developed extensively in Catholic bridal mysticism. St. Bernard of Clairvaux's sermons explore every nuance of the beloved's relationship, finding in each detail a lesson for the soul's communion with God.

### Daughters Of Jerusalem and Description

The imagery of daughters of Jerusalem and description is read sacramentally: physical beauty and desire are not rejected but elevated as signs of the spiritual realities they represent.

## Marian Connections

Catholic liturgy applies several images from the Song to the Blessed Virgin Mary, who is seen as the perfect embodiment of the Bride's receptivity to divine love. The *Tota Pulchra* tradition draws on the Song's praise of beauty.

## Theological Significance

Song of Solomon 5 contributes to the Catholic understanding that the spiritual life is fundamentally a love story. The soul is created for union with God, and the longing expressed in the Song is the deepest truth about the human condition.
