## The Seal of Love

Song of Solomon 8 contains the book's theological climax: "Set me as a seal upon your heart... for love is strong as death" (8:6). Catholic theology reads this as the definitive statement about the nature of love — human and divine.

## Key Verses and Interpretations

### "Set me as a seal upon your heart" (8:6)

Pope Benedict XVI, in *Deus Caritas Est* (2005), cited the Song of Solomon as evidence that *eros* and *agape* are not opposed but united. The seal represents permanent, irrevocable love — both the sacramental bond of marriage and God's eternal covenant with humanity.

### "Love is strong as death" (8:6)

St. John of the Cross interpreted this verse as the mystical death that precedes union with God: love must be as strong as death because the soul must die to self before it can live fully in God.

The *shalhevet-yah* — possibly "flame of the Lord" — connects love to its divine source. Catholic theology affirms that all genuine love participates in God's love: "God is love" (1 John 4:8).

### "Many waters cannot quench love" (8:7)

This verse grounds the Catholic theology of perseverance: authentic love endures through suffering, persecution, and death. The martyrs exemplify this love.

| Catholic Doctrine | Connection |
|------------------|-----------|
| Sacramental marriage | The seal of permanent, exclusive love |
| *Deus Caritas Est* | Eros and agape united |
| Mystical death | Love as strong as death (John of the Cross) |
| Martyrdom | Many waters cannot quench |

## Theological Significance

Song of Solomon 8 declares that love is ultimately divine in origin and indestructible in nature. Catholic theology finds here the foundation for its teaching on marriage, mysticism, and the nature of God Himself.
