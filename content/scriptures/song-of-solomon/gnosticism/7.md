## The Dance of Mahanaim

Gnostic interpreters read Song of Solomon 7 as an allegory of the soul's journey from material imprisonment toward reunion with the divine — the bridal chamber theology that was central to Valentinian and other Gnostic traditions.

## Key Themes in Gnostic Reading

### Dance and Beauty

The chapter's imagery of dance and beauty is read as depicting stages of the soul's awakening. The beloved represents the divine element — whether the True God, the angelic syzygy, or the light of gnosis — that the imprisoned soul seeks.

### Desire

The theme of desire mirrors the Gnostic narrative of Sophia's fall and return. Every movement of seeking in the Song reflects the cosmic drama of divine elements separated and reunited.

### Vineyard and Love

The imagery of vineyard and love is reinterpreted through the lens of the Pleroma: the garden is the fullness of the divine realm, and beauty is the radiance of the true Light beyond the Demiurge's counterfeit creation.

## Theological Significance

For Gnostic readers, Song of Solomon 7 is part of the great narrative of gnosis: the soul's recognition of its divine origin, its longing for the Pleroma, and its eventual reunion with the Beloved through knowledge. The Song is the Gnostic gospel hidden within the Hebrew Bible.
