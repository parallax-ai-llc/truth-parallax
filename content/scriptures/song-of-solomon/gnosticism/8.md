## The Bridal Chamber Restored

Song of Solomon 8 provides the climactic imagery for the Gnostic sacrament of the bridal chamber: "Set me as a seal upon your heart" (8:6) and "love is strong as death" (8:6) become the proclamations of gnosis triumphant over the archons.

## Key Verses and Gnostic Interpretation

### "Set me as a seal upon your heart" (8:6)

The *Gospel of Philip* (NHC II,3) teaches that the bridal chamber is the sacrament in which the soul is reunited with its angelic counterpart — sealed forever against the archons' power. The "seal" of Song 8:6 is the pneumatic seal of gnosis, the mark that identifies the soul as belonging to the Pleroma.

### "Love is strong as death" (8:6)

In Gnostic soteriology, gnosis overcomes death — not physical death but the spiritual death of ignorance. The *Treatise on the Resurrection* (NHC I,4) teaches that the Gnostic has already overcome death through knowledge: "Do not think the resurrection is an illusion. It is no illusion, but it is truth."

### *Shalhevet-yah* — The divine flame (8:6)

The fire of gnosis — the *ophis* (serpent) of the Ophites, the light of the Pleroma — burns within the soul, consuming the darkness of material ignorance. This is the true "flame of God": not the Demiurge's jealous fire but the transcendent light.

| Song 8 Image | Gnostic Reading |
|-------------|----------------|
| Seal upon the heart | The pneumatic seal against the archons |
| Love strong as death | Gnosis conquering spiritual death |
| Flame of God | The light of the Pleroma |
| Many waters cannot quench | Material forces cannot extinguish the divine spark |
| "My vineyard is mine" | The soul reclaims its divine nature |

## Theological Significance

Song of Solomon 8 is, for Gnostic readers, the culmination of the soul's journey: the bridal chamber entered, the seal affixed, the divine spark reunited with its origin. Love — understood as gnosis — has conquered death and the archons, and the soul is free.
