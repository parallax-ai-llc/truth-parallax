## The Beloved Knocks

Protestant interpretation of Song of Solomon 5 engages with the poem's rich imagery through both allegorical and literal approaches, finding in its love poetry either a depiction of Christ's love for the Church or a celebration of the God-given gift of marital love.

## Key Themes

### Beloved and Myrrh

The chapter's imagery of beloved and myrrh has been read allegorically as representing aspects of the believer's relationship with Christ, or literally as the language of human romantic love in all its intensity.

### Search

The theme of search reflects the Song's characteristic movement between presence and absence, seeking and finding — a pattern that Protestant interpreters connect to the life of faith.

### Daughters Of Jerusalem and Description

The imagery of daughters of Jerusalem and description draws on the natural world of ancient Israel to express the beauty and desirability of the beloved, affirming the goodness of physical beauty and desire within the covenant of love.

## Interpretive Approaches

| Approach | Reading |
|----------|---------|
| Allegorical | Christ and the Church |
| Literal | Celebration of marital love |
| Typological | Human love as a type of divine love |
| Lyric | The poetry's emotional and aesthetic power |

## Theological Significance

Song of Solomon 5 contributes to the Protestant understanding that love — both human and divine — is a gift to be celebrated, not a temptation to be feared. The Reformation's recovery of marriage as a positive good finds support in the Song's unashamed celebration of desire and devotion.
