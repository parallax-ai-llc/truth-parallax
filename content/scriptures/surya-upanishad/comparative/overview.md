## Overview

The Surya Upanishad's identification of the sun with the supreme reality places it within the universal tradition of solar religion and light symbolism. Sun worship appears in virtually every ancient civilization, and the philosophical identification of divine light with consciousness or ultimate reality is a recurring theme in mystical traditions worldwide.

## Comparative Themes

The Surya Upanishad's solar theology compares with the Egyptian worship of Ra/Aten, the Zoroastrian identification of Ahura Mazda with light, Plato's analogy of the Good with the sun (*Republic* 508-509), and the Christian identification of Christ as "the light of the world" (John 8:12). The Gayatri's prayer for divine illumination parallels the Christian prayer "Lighten our darkness" and the Buddhist aspiration for the "light of wisdom." The inner sun of meditation connects to the Eastern Orthodox experience of the Taboric Light and the Quaker concept of the Inner Light.
