## Overview

The Surya Upanishad reflects the continuity between ancient Vedic solar religion and later Upanishadic philosophy. The Rig Veda's worship of the sun — as Surya, Savitri, Mitra, and other solar deities — was central to the earliest Vedic religion, and the Surya Upanishad philosophizes this solar theology by identifying the sun with Brahman.

## Research Themes

Scholars note that the Surya Upanishad preserves elements of the ancient Saura (sun-worship) tradition that was once a major religious movement in India. The Gayatri mantra, which the text prominently features, is the most widely recited mantra in Hinduism and serves as a bridge between Vedic ritualism and Upanishadic contemplation. The text's dating is uncertain but likely falls in the early centuries CE.
