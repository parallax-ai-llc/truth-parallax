## Overview

Shaiva interpreters read the Surya Upanishad's solar theology as pointing to the inner sun of consciousness — Shiva as the self-luminous awareness that illuminates all experience. The external sun is a symbol of the internal light that Kashmir Shaivism identifies with Shiva's *prakasha* (luminosity) — the fundamental nature of consciousness as self-illuminating.

## Theological Emphases

The Upanishad's teaching that the sun is Brahman is read through the Shaiva lens as affirming that consciousness (symbolized by light) is the ultimate reality. The Gayatri meditation becomes a practice of recognizing the inner light of awareness as identical with the cosmic light that illuminates all. Meditation on the inner sun is a central practice in many Shaiva traditions.
