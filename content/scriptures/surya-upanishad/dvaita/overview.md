## Overview

Madhva reads the Surya Upanishad as teaching that the sun deity is a dependent being who reflects the glory of the Supreme Lord Vishnu. The sun's brilliance is real but derived — it shines because Vishnu empowers it. The identification of Surya with Brahman is read as pointing to Vishnu who dwells within and empowers the sun.

## Theological Emphases

For Dvaita, solar worship through the Gayatri and other mantras is legitimate because the sun is a real deity who mediates Vishnu's light to the world. But the sun itself is not the supreme being — it is a servant of Vishnu, and meditation on the sun should ultimately lead the devotee to the recognition of Vishnu as the source of all light and all being.
