## Overview

Vaishnava commentators read the Surya Upanishad's identification of the sun with Brahman as pointing to Vishnu, who is the inner light of the sun. The Rig Veda declares that Vishnu's highest step is the realm of the sun, and the Surya Upanishad confirms this by teaching that the solar deity is a manifestation of the supreme Brahman, whom Vaishnavas identify as Vishnu-Narayana.

## Theological Emphases

The Gayatri mantra — "We meditate on the divine light of the sun; may it illuminate our intellects" — is read by Vaishnavas as a prayer to Vishnu in His solar form. The sun's role as the illuminator of the physical world symbolizes Vishnu's role as the illuminator of all consciousness. Meditation on Surya is thus a form of Vishnu worship.
