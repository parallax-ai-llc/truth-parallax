## Overview

For Advaita Vedanta, the Surya Upanishad's identification of the sun with Brahman is a meditation on the self-luminous nature of consciousness. Just as the sun illuminates the world without needing another light to illuminate it, Brahman — pure consciousness — is self-luminous (*svayam-prakasha*), illuminating all experience without itself being illuminated by anything else.

## Theological Emphases

The sun serves as the most powerful natural symbol for the Advaita teaching that Brahman is self-luminous awareness. The Gayatri meditation — requesting the divine light to illuminate the intellect — is read as a prayer for the removal of ignorance so that the self-luminous Brahman, already present, can be recognized. The Surya Upanishad thus uses solar symbolism to teach the fundamental Advaita truth that consciousness illuminates all but is illuminated by nothing.
