---
title: "Surya Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "surya-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Om. Now we shall expound the Surya Upanishad. The sun is the Self of the world, of all that moves and does not move.

**2** From the sun indeed all these beings are born, and having been born, they are sustained by the sun. They are dissolved in the sun at the time of dissolution. Therefore the sun is Brahman.

**3** Aditya is truly the Self. The sages declared: "Aditya is Brahman." Aditya, verily, is the supreme light.

**4** The orb of the sun is the door to truth, the entrance to the imperishable. The golden vessel covers the face of truth. Remove it, O sun, that I may behold the truth, the Dharma.

**5** O Pushan, the sole traveler, O controller, O sun, offspring of Prajapati — spread thy rays and gather them in. I see thy most auspicious form. The Person that is in the sun — I am He.

**6** The sun is Brahman — this is the teaching. Om, the ethereal space within the heart, in that is this Person consisting of mind, immortal, golden.

**7** The sun illumines all the worlds — the highest, the middle, and the lowest. It is Brahman, the all, the best.

**8** He who worships the sun as Brahman, to him pleasant words proceed. He rejoices. He rejoices indeed.

**9** Now this is the Gayatri mantra dedicated to the sun: "Om, let us meditate on the glory of that divine being, the sun, who illumines all, who recreates all, from whom all proceed, to whom all must return, whom we invoke to direct our understanding aright in our progress toward His holy seat."

**10** He who chants this mantra is purified of all great sins. He who meditates on the sun at dawn, at noon, and at evening, is freed from the sins committed during those three periods.
