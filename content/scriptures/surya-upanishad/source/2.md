---
title: "Surya Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "surya-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** He who knows this becomes one with the sun. The syllable Om is the sun. He who knows this becomes the sun himself.

**2** The sun is the Self of all beings. He who knows this truth, that the sun is the Self, meditates on it, and worships it — he crosses over death. He crosses over death indeed.

**3** The sun gives heat. The sun gives rain. The sun gives life. The sun gives light. Without the sun there is no life, no warmth, no light in the world.

**4** As the spider sends forth and draws in its thread, as plants sprout from the earth, as hair grows on the body of a living person — so does everything in the universe arise from the imperishable Brahman.

**5** The sun is the soul of both the moving and the unmoving beings. He encompasses all beings. He is both within and without all beings.

**6** He who knows the Self as the sun, that seer, that sage, becomes one with the supreme light, having passed beyond darkness.

**7** He who knows this meditation on the sun, he is freed from great fears. He is freed from the fear of death.

**8** The sun is the first cause. The sun is the visible form of the supreme. He who worships the sun as the supreme Brahman, to him comes all glory, all prosperity.

**9** This Surya Upanishad is the secret doctrine. He who studies it daily becomes pure. He obtains the fruit of the study of all the Vedas. He obtains the fruit of bathing in all the sacred waters.

**10** He who reads this Upanishad once becomes free from all sins. He who reads it a second time obtains the state of Brahman. He who reads it a third time is never again born in this world of suffering. He reaches the supreme abode. He reaches the supreme abode indeed.

**11** Om. Peace, peace, peace.
