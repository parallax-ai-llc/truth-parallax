## Overview

The Eastern Orthodox tradition approaches the Book of Jubilees with particular nuance, given its close relationship with the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, where Jubilees holds canonical status. While not part of the standard Orthodox biblical canon, Jubilees is valued as a witness to ancient interpretive traditions consistent with patristic theology.

## Canonical Considerations

The Book of Jubilees is not included in the standard Orthodox Old Testament canon. However, the Orthodox tradition maintains a more fluid understanding of canonical boundaries than Western Christianity. The Ethiopian Orthodox Church, an Oriental Orthodox communion, includes Jubilees in its broader canon of 81 books.

## Patristic Connections

Orthodox scholars note significant continuities between Jubilees and patristic interpretation:

- **Typological reading**: Jubilees' retelling of Genesis employs typological methods similar to those of the Church Fathers
- **Angelology**: The elaborate angelic hierarchy resonates with Orthodox theology of the heavenly powers
- **Liturgical time**: The sacred calendar emphasis connects to Orthodox liturgical theology
- **Theosis implications**: The portrayal of human-divine interaction aligns with Orthodox soteriology

## The Ethiopian Connection

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church preserves the only complete manuscript tradition of Jubilees in Ge'ez. This preservation reflects the broader Ethiopian Orthodox commitment to maintaining ancient Jewish-Christian textual traditions.
