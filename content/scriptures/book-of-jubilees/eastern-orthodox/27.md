## Chapter 27 — Jacob Receives the Blessing

Jubilees 27 expands the Jacob narrative with emphasis on priestly blessing and covenant continuity. Orthodox interpreters value this chapter's attention to the transmission of sacred knowledge across generations, a theme central to Orthodox theology of Holy Tradition.

## Orthodox Theological Perspective

The Orthodox tradition approaches this chapter with the interpretive richness characteristic of patristic exegesis. Rather than treating the text as merely historical, Orthodox scholars discern theological patterns that illuminate the mystery of God's *oikonomia* (divine economy) — the gradual unfolding of salvation through successive covenants.

The chapter's cosmological vision, with its emphasis on angelic hierarchy and divine order, resonates with the Orthodox understanding of creation as a living icon of divine beauty. The liturgical dimensions of the text anticipate the Orthodox experience of worship as participation in the heavenly liturgy.

## Connection to Ethiopian Orthodox Tradition

This chapter is particularly significant within the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, which preserves Jubilees as canonical Scripture in Ge'ez. The Ethiopian liturgical tradition reflects many of the calendar and ritual concerns expressed in this passage.
