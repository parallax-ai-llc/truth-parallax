## Genealogical Expansion

Jubilees 4 substantially expands the Genesis 4-5 genealogies by supplying names for the wives of the patriarchs — names absent from the Hebrew Bible. Academic scholars note that these additions serve the theological purpose of demonstrating that the earliest generations married within their own kin group, thus retroactively endorsing the book's strict prohibition against exogamy. This strategy of filling narrative gaps to support legal positions exemplifies the rewritten Bible method.

## Enoch Traditions

The chapter's treatment of Enoch is significant for the history of apocalyptic literature. Jubilees presents Enoch as the first to learn writing, astronomy, and calendar reckoning — traditions that connect with the Astronomical Book of 1 Enoch. Scholars such as James VanderKam have argued that Jubilees draws on early Enochic traditions while integrating them into a Mosaic framework, reflecting a synthesis of previously distinct scribal traditions.

## Chronological Framework

The precise jubilee-based chronology establishes a system of 49-year periods that structures all of history. This chronological scheme serves both historiographic and eschatological purposes, enabling the calculation of the expected date of divine intervention.
