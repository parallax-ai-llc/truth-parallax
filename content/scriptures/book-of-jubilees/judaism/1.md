## Chapter 1 — Prologue on Sinai

Jubilees 1 presents a dramatic expansion of the Sinai theophany, claiming that the Angel of the Presence dictated to Moses not only the Torah but an entire hidden history of creation. Jewish scholars recognize this as a characteristic Second Temple strategy for legitimating new interpretive traditions by grounding them in Mosaic authority.

## Jewish Scholarly Analysis

This chapter exemplifies the genre of *rewritten Bible* — a distinctly Jewish literary form in which biblical narrative is retold with interpretive expansions, harmonizations, and legal extrapolations. The technique visible here anticipates later midrashic methods while differing in important respects: Jubilees presents its expansions as direct divine revelation rather than human interpretation.

The halakhic material in this chapter, while not authoritative in rabbinic Judaism, provides evidence for legal traditions that circulated in pre-Rabbinic Jewish communities. The discovery of Jubilees fragments at Qumran confirmed its importance within at least one major Jewish movement of the Second Temple period.

## Relationship to Rabbinic Tradition

While rabbinic Judaism did not preserve Jubilees, many traditions found in this chapter resurfaced in later midrashic literature, suggesting a shared reservoir of interpretive tradition that predates the Rabbinic-sectarian split.
