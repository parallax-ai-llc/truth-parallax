## Chapter 15 — Covenant of Circumcision

Jubilees 15 centers on the covenant of circumcision, presented as an eternal ordinance inscribed on the heavenly tablets. Gnostic interpreters engage this chapter as a test case for the relationship between bodily regulation and spiritual reality.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

Circumcision is perhaps the paradigmatic case of what Gnostic interpreters regard as archontic body-regulation. Jubilees 15 presents circumcision not merely as a sign of covenant but as an ontological marker: the uncircumcised belong to "the children of destruction" and are destined for annihilation. This absolutist claim — that a bodily modification determines cosmic destiny — epitomizes the Demiurge's governance through flesh. The pneumatic spark within the human being is entirely ignored; what matters to the God of Jubilees is the state of the body.

Gnostic thinkers, particularly Paul's Gnostic interpreters and the Valentinians, explicitly repudiated the soteriological significance of circumcision, arguing that the "circumcision of the heart" or the "spiritual circumcision" — the cutting away of material attachments — superseded and annulled the Demiurge's fleshly ordinance. Jubilees 15's extreme insistence that this law was written on the heavenly tablets before creation reveals, for Gnostic readers, the Demiurge's strategy of projecting his material regulations back onto the transcendent realm, claiming Pleromatic authority for what is fundamentally a bodily discipline.

The promise of Isaac in this chapter — the birth of the covenantal heir — illustrates the Gnostic theme of the pneumatic seed's transmission through specific lineages. Isaac will carry the spiritual inheritance, while Ishmael will not. This principle of spiritual election by lineage has parallels in the Valentinian classification of humans into *pneumatic*, *psychic*, and *hylic* categories — predetermined spiritual natures that determine one's relationship to *gnosis*.

## Scholarly Assessment

Michael Williams's influential study *Rethinking "Gnosticism"* identifies the critique of bodily ritual as a consistent marker of Gnostic hermeneutics applied to Jewish scripture. Jubilees 15's elevation of circumcision to a cosmic ordinance represents the maximum expression of what Gnostic interpreters understood as the Demiurge's investment in bodily governance — the very extremity of the claim invited the Gnostic counter-reading that the true God cares nothing for flesh.
