## Chapter 48 — Exodus and Passover

Jubilees 48 presents the Exodus events with Mastema playing a central and explicit role — he assists the Egyptian sorcerers against Moses, attempts to kill Moses on the way to Egypt, and is ultimately bound by the angels during the Passover night. This chapter is the climactic confrontation between the pneumatic liberator and the chief archon.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

Jubilees 48 is the most explicitly Gnostic-compatible chapter in the entire book. Mastema's role is fully archontic: he empowers Pharaoh's magicians to resist Moses, he attempts to murder Moses en route to his divine mission, and he works to strengthen Pharaoh's resolve against releasing the Israelites. This is the chief archon deploying every instrument in his arsenal — magical power, physical violence, and psychic manipulation — to prevent the pneumatic community's liberation from the material world.

The plagues, in Gnostic reading, represent the systematic dismantling of the Demiurgic cosmos. Each plague targets an element of the created order — water, livestock, the sky, the firstborn — revealing that the material world the Demiurge built is fragile and can be undone by a higher power. The God who sends the plagues operates at a level above the Demiurge's creation, capable of unmaking what the world-ruler made. For Gnostic interpreters, this distinction — between the God who can destroy creation and the Demiurge who built it — points toward the transcendent deity beyond the cosmic architect.

Mastema's binding during the Passover night is the archon's definitive defeat — temporary within Jubilees' narrative but permanent in Gnostic eschatological hope. The chief archon is restrained so that the pneumatic community can escape. The Passover lamb's blood on the doorposts, in Gnostic reading, is a pneumatic sign — a marker that identifies the souls to be freed from archontic governance. The destroying angel passes over those who bear the sign of *gnosis*, striking only those who remain under the archon's jurisdiction.

The Red Sea crossing — the final escape from Egypt — enacts the soul's passage through the last archontic barrier. The waters that close over Pharaoh's army represent the material world collapsing behind the escaping pneumatic community, sealing the archons' domain while the liberated souls pass through to freedom.

## Scholarly Assessment

Birger Pearson's seminal work on Gnosticism and Judaism identifies Jubilees 48 as the single most important pre-Gnostic text for understanding the development of archontic mythology. Mastema's explicit role as cosmic antagonist during the Exodus — empowering the Egyptian establishment, attempting to kill the liberator, and being bound during the liberation event — provides a virtually complete template for the Gnostic drama of the soul's escape from archontic captivity through the intervention of a transcendent power.
