## Chapter 35 — Rebecca's Final Words

Jubilees 35 presents Rebecca's final words, her attempts to mediate peace between Jacob and Esau, and her deathbed appeals to both sons. Gnostic interpreters read this chapter as the Sophia figure's last efforts to reconcile the pneumatic and hylic dimensions before departing the material world.

## Gnostic Interpretive Lens

Rebecca's role in Jubilees 35 as peacemaker between Jacob and Esau represents Sophia's mediating function within the cosmos. The feminine wisdom figure stands between the pneumatic soul (Jacob) and the hylic being (Esau), attempting to create a modus vivendi within the Demiurge's divided creation. Yet the peace she brokers is temporary and fragile — as the Gnostic Sophia discovers, the fundamental opposition between spirit and matter cannot be resolved within the material order; it can only be transcended beyond it.

Rebecca's deathbed scene is one of the most emotionally rich passages in Jubilees, and Gnostic interpreters read it as the departure of wisdom from the material world. When Sophia withdraws — when the feminine divine presence leaves the earthly realm — the remaining figures are left without the perceptive intelligence that could discern spiritual realities behind material appearances. After Rebecca's death, the Jacob-Esau conflict will escalate into open warfare, because the wisdom that mediated between them is no longer present.

The oath Rebecca extracts from Esau — that he will not harm Jacob — attempts to impose a moral constraint on the hylic nature. But oaths belong to the Demiurgic legal framework; they bind through fear of punishment rather than through genuine transformation. Gnostic interpreters recognize that moral constraints cannot change the hylic being's fundamental nature — Esau will eventually break any oath, because oaths operate at the *psychic* level while Esau's nature is *hylic*, beneath the reach of moral governance.

## Scholarly Assessment

Silke Petersen's research on the Sophia tradition from Jewish Wisdom literature through Gnostic mythology has identified Rebecca as a figure who embodies key Sophia attributes: perceptive intelligence, mediating activity, and eventual departure from the material realm. Jubilees 35's portrait of Rebecca's final words and death provided Gnostic interpreters with a narrative of Sophia's withdrawal that closely parallels the Valentinian myth of Sophia's departure from the Pleroma — in both cases, the feminine wisdom figure's absence leaves the material world impoverished.
