## Cosmic Judgment and Purity

The Essene community read Jubilees 6 ("Noah's Sacrifice and Covenant") as confirming their understanding of divine judgment against impurity and transgression. The flood narrative and its aftermath demonstrated that God would not tolerate violations of the created order — a principle the community applied to the corrupted Jerusalem Temple and its illegitimate priesthood. The text's account of supernatural evil reinforced the Essene dualistic worldview.

## Mastema and the War of Light and Darkness

The chapter's treatment of demonic forces, particularly the figure of Mastema (Prince of Enmity), provided scriptural foundation for the community's developed demonology as expressed in the War Scroll and the Community Rule. The Essene understanding of cosmic warfare between the spirits of light and darkness drew heavily on Jubilees' account of the origin and persistence of evil spirits after the flood.

## Calendar and Festival Ordinances

The legal material in this section — including festival dates and sacrificial regulations — confirmed the community's distinctive calendar and ritual practice. Every detail aligned with the 364-day solar calendar, demonstrating that correct worship had been revealed from the earliest period of human history and faithfully preserved by the righteous remnant.
