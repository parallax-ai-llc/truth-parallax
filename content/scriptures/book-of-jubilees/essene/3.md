## Purity from the Beginning

The Essene reading of Jubilees 3 emphasizes the text's derivation of purification laws from the creation narrative itself. The periods of Adam's and Eve's separation before entering the Garden correspond exactly to the Levitical purification periods, demonstrating that purity law is not merely Mosaic legislation but a cosmic principle established at creation. This understanding reinforced the community's rigorous purity practices.

## The Garden as Sanctuary

Essene interpreters understood the Garden of Eden as a prototype of the Temple sanctuary. The text's description of Adam offering incense upon leaving the Garden connects primordial worship with the community's own liturgical practice. Since the Jerusalem Temple had been defiled by incorrect calendar observance and illegitimate priests, the Essene community understood their own worship as the true continuation of Edenic and patriarchal practice.

## The Origin of Evil

The account of the fall and the subsequent loss of animal speech illustrated the cosmic consequences of transgression. For the Essene community, this narrative reinforced the absolute necessity of obedience to divine law as revealed in the heavenly tablets.
