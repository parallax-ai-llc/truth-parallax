## Divine Revelation and Community Authority

The Essene community at Qumran received Jubilees' prologue as an authoritative account of the Sinai revelation, supplementing the Torah with essential information about the correct calendar and legal practice. The Angel of the Presence's dictation from the heavenly tablets validated the Essene conviction that their distinctive practices were not innovations but recoveries of primordial divine law. The prologue's prediction that Israel would "forget all of my law" and follow the Gentile calendar justified the Essene separation from the Jerusalem Temple.

## Creation and the Calendar

Jubilees' creation account, with its careful enumeration of created entities and its institution of the sabbath as a cosmic ordinance, provided the Essene community with scriptural grounding for their 364-day solar calendar. The text's insistence that the angels observe the sabbath in heaven confirmed the Essene belief that their community lived in fellowship with the angelic host. The precise correspondence between the creation account and the solar calendar demonstrated that correct time-reckoning was built into the fabric of creation itself.

## Angelic Fellowship

The text's detailed angelology — the creation of multiple classes of angels, each with specific functions — informed the Essene understanding of their community as existing alongside and in communion with the heavenly beings. The Songs of the Sabbath Sacrifice and other Qumran liturgical texts reflect this conviction, which finds its scriptural basis in Jubilees' account of the angelic creation.
