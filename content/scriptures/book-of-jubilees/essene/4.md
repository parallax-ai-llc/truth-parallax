## Righteous Genealogy

The Essene community read Jubilees 4 as establishing the principle of endogamous marriage from the earliest generations. The careful naming of patriarchal wives — all from within the family line — provided authoritative precedent for the community's strict prohibition against marriage with outsiders. The sectarian understanding of "Israel" as the community of the renewed covenant made this genealogical exclusivism directly applicable to their own marriage practices.

## Enoch and Revealed Knowledge

The chapter's portrait of Enoch as the recipient of astronomical and calendrical knowledge resonated deeply with the Essene community's self-understanding. The community possessed and studied the Enochic writings alongside Jubilees, and they understood both as transmitting the same revealed knowledge about the correct ordering of time. Enoch's translation to the Garden of Eden to "write condemnation and judgment of the world" prefigured the eschatological role the community assigned to itself.

## The Jubilee Chronology

The 49-year jubilee periods structuring human history provided the community with a framework for calculating the approach of the eschaton, reinforcing their conviction that they lived in the final age.
