## Abraham in Ethiopian Sacred History

Ethiopian Orthodox tradition reads Jubilees 22 ("Abraham's Blessing of Jacob") as providing essential details about Abraham's life that supplement and complete the Genesis account. Abraham's rejection of idolatry, his covenant with God, and his faithful obedience are central to Ethiopian Christian understanding of the continuity between the Old and New Testaments. The patriarch's example of faith is regularly invoked in Ethiopian homiletic tradition.

## Canonical Integration

The Ethiopian Orthodox Church reads Jubilees alongside Genesis, treating both as complementary scriptural witnesses to the Abrahamic covenant. The additional narrative details, chronological specifications, and legal prescriptions found in Jubilees are accepted as divinely revealed information that enriches the biblical record. This integration reflects the broader Ethiopian canonical principle of inclusivity.

## Theological Significance

The chapter's theological themes — election, covenant faithfulness, and divine promise — resonate with Ethiopian Orthodox Christology and ecclesiology. Abraham's covenant is understood as a stage in the progressive revelation that culminates in Christ, and the Ethiopian church's claim to Abrahamic heritage through the Solomonic dynasty connects national identity with scriptural narrative.
