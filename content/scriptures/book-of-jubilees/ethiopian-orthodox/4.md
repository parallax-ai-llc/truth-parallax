## Patriarchal Genealogy as Sacred Record

Ethiopian Orthodox tradition receives the genealogical expansions in Jubilees 4 — including the names of the patriarchs' wives — as genuine historical information revealed through divine inspiration. These details complete the record of sacred history and demonstrate God's providential guidance of the chosen lineage from Adam through Seth to Noah. The Ge'ez text preserves these names as part of the canonical historical record.

## Enoch in Ethiopian Tradition

The chapter's treatment of Enoch holds particular significance for the Ethiopian Orthodox Church, which also canonizes 1 Enoch. Jubilees' account of Enoch as scribe, astronomer, and witness to divine judgment coheres with the fuller Enochic portrait in 1 Enoch, and Ethiopian interpreters read both texts as complementary revelations about this pivotal figure. Enoch's translation to Paradise prefigures the ultimate destiny of the righteous.

## The Continuity of Revelation

The genealogical framework demonstrates the unbroken chain of divine revelation from Adam through the patriarchs to Moses, confirming the Ethiopian Orthodox understanding that the Bible contains a continuous and coherent sacred history. Jubilees' additional details do not contradict but supplement the canonical Genesis account.
