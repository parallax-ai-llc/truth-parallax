## The Law and the Jubilee

Ethiopian Orthodox tradition reads Jubilees 46 ("The Death of Jacob and Joseph") as concluding the book's comprehensive revelation of divine law and sacred history. The chapter's legal prescriptions — including sabbath and festival regulations — are received as part of the authoritative Mosaic revelation, complementing the legislation found in the Pentateuch. The Ethiopian church's distinctive observance of both Saturday and Sunday sabbath finds support in these passages.

## Canonical Completeness

As part of the broader Ethiopian biblical canon — which includes texts not found in other Christian canons — Jubilees 46 contributes to the church's comprehensive understanding of divine revelation. The Ethiopian Orthodox approach to canon formation emphasizes inclusivity and the preservation of ancient traditions, and Jubilees represents one of the most significant texts unique to this canonical tradition.

## Living Tradition

The chapter continues to function as living scripture within Ethiopian Orthodox worship, preaching, and theological education. Seminary students study Jubilees alongside other canonical texts, and its teachings inform both scholarly theology and popular piety. The book's integration into Ethiopian Christian life demonstrates the practical significance of canonical status.
