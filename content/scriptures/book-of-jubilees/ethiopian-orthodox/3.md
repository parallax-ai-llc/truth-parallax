## The Garden and Sacred History

Ethiopian Orthodox interpreters read Jubilees 3 as providing essential details about humanity's original state and fall. The text's specification of the periods before Adam and Eve entered the Garden — and their correspondence with purification periods — is received as revealed chronological data that illuminates both the creation narrative and Levitical legislation. These details are integrated into the church's comprehensive sacred history.

## The Role of Eve

The chapter's treatment of Eve and the purification periods has shaped Ethiopian Orthodox understanding of the relationship between creation, gender, and liturgical law. The church's distinctive practices regarding women's participation in worship and purification after childbirth draw in part on Jubilees' primordial narrative, which grounds these practices in the very structure of creation.

## The Fall and Redemption

Ethiopian Orthodox theology reads the fall narrative in light of the church's soteriology. The loss of Edenic perfection — including the animals' loss of speech — demonstrates the cosmic scope of the fall and, by implication, the cosmic scope of the redemption accomplished in Christ, a typological reading that connects Old Testament narrative with New Testament fulfillment.
