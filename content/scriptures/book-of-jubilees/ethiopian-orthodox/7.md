## Sacred History and Divine Judgment

Ethiopian Orthodox tradition reads Jubilees 7 ("Noah's Sons and Their Inheritance") as an authoritative supplement to the Genesis account, providing revealed details about the flood, its causes, and its aftermath. The text's explanation of the Watchers' sin and God's judgment illuminates the theological significance of the flood as divine response to the corruption of creation. These traditions are integrated into the church's comprehensive understanding of salvation history.

## The Ge'ez Textual Tradition

As the only complete version of Jubilees exists in Ge'ez, the Ethiopian Orthodox Church serves as the primary custodian of this text. The chapter's preservation in numerous Ethiopic manuscripts — some dating to the fourteenth and fifteenth centuries — testifies to the book's continuous importance within Ethiopian Christian literary culture. Textual scholars study these manuscripts to establish the most reliable reading of the text.

## Liturgical and Moral Application

Ethiopian Orthodox preachers and commentators draw on the flood narratives to illustrate God's justice and mercy. The preservation of Noah and his family through the flood prefigures baptismal salvation, a typological reading shared with other Christian traditions but given particular authority in Ethiopian theology by Jubilees' canonical status.
