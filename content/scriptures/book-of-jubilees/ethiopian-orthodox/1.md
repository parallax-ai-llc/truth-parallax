## The Heavenly Revelation

Ethiopian Orthodox tradition reads the opening of Jubilees as a genuine account of divine revelation on Mount Sinai. The Angel of the Presence dictates to Moses the contents of the "heavenly tablets," providing an authoritative supplement to the Torah's own account of creation and the earliest generations. This framing device establishes the entire book as divinely warranted scripture, and Ethiopian Orthodox interpreters accept it as such, integrating Jubilees' additional details about creation and the patriarchs into their understanding of sacred history.

## Creation and the Sabbath

Jubilees' account of creation emphasizes the institution of the sabbath as a cosmic ordinance observed by the angels in heaven before it was commanded to Israel. Ethiopian Orthodox theology draws on this passage to affirm the sabbath's universal and eternal significance. The text's statement that God sanctified Israel to keep the sabbath "together with the angels" reinforces the Ethiopian Orthodox understanding of worship as a participation in the heavenly liturgy.

## The First Generations

The text's retelling of Adam and Eve, Cain and Abel, and the line of Seth adds narrative details absent from Genesis — including specific dates, the names of wives, and the origin of sacrificial practice. Ethiopian Orthodox interpreters receive these additions as revealed information that fills gaps in the canonical account, providing a more complete record of humanity's earliest history.
