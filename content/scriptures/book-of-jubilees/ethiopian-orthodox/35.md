## The Patriarchal Narratives

Ethiopian Orthodox tradition receives Jubilees 35 ("Rebekah's Plea to Jacob and Esau") as canonical scripture that enriches the Jacob cycle with divinely revealed details absent from Genesis. The text's treatment of Jacob's family, his conflicts, and his covenant faithfulness provides material for homiletic and theological reflection within the Ethiopian church. Jacob's life exemplifies the providential guidance that characterizes all of sacred history.

## Ge'ez Literary Heritage

The chapter forms part of the Ge'ez literary heritage that distinguishes Ethiopian Christianity from other Christian traditions. Ethiopian scribes copied and preserved these texts for centuries, and the manuscript tradition reveals a careful attention to textual accuracy that reflects the community's reverence for Jubilees as holy scripture. Variant readings among manuscripts attest to a living scribal tradition.

## Moral and Spiritual Instruction

Ethiopian Orthodox interpreters draw moral and spiritual lessons from the patriarchal narratives, reading them typologically in light of the New Testament. The themes of blessing, election, reconciliation, and divine justice that pervade the Jacob cycle are applied to the life of the church and the individual believer through preaching, commentary, and liturgical reading.
