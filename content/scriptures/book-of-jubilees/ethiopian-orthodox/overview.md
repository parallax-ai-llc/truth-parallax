## Overview

The Book of Jubilees is canonical scripture in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, preserved in Ge'ez as part of the broader 81-book biblical canon. Ethiopian Orthodox tradition regards the text as a genuine revelation given to Moses on Mount Sinai, complementing the Torah with additional chronological, legal, and narrative detail. The book's emphasis on the solar calendar, the antiquity of the sabbath, and the patriarchal origins of Israel's festivals informs Ethiopian Orthodox liturgical practice and theological reflection.

## Interpretive Approach

Ethiopian Orthodox interpreters read Jubilees as an authoritative expansion of Genesis and Exodus, offering divinely revealed details absent from the canonical Pentateuch. The text's insistence that the patriarchs observed the sabbath, circumcision, and the festivals before Sinai supports the Ethiopian Orthodox conviction that God's law is eternal and was known to the righteous from the beginning of creation. The angelic mediation of the revelation to Moses is understood as consistent with the biblical pattern of divine communication through heavenly messengers.

## Liturgical and Calendrical Significance

Jubilees' 364-day solar calendar has historically influenced Ethiopian calendar reckoning and the dating of feasts. The text's detailed chronological framework, which maps the entire narrative of Genesis onto a system of jubilee cycles, provides Ethiopian Orthodox scholars with a precise timeline of sacred history that integrates patriarchal, Mosaic, and eschatological eras into a single providential scheme.
