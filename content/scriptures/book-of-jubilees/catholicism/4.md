## The Patriarchal Blessings

Catholic interpreters engage Jubilees' expanded account of the patriarchal blessings as a witness to the typological reading of the patriarchal narratives that became central to patristic exegesis. The blessings of Levi and Judah in particular are read as prefigurations of the priestly and royal dimensions of Christ's messianic office. Jubilees' emphasis on the eternal character of these blessings supports the Catholic understanding of the Old Testament as a coherent preparation for the New.

## The Priestly Vocation

Jubilees' detailed account of Levi's ordination and priestly instruction contributes to Catholic reflection on the origins and nature of the priestly vocation. The text's presentation of the priesthood as a divinely ordained institution, established through patriarchal blessing and transmitted through a specific lineage, resonates with Catholic theology of the sacramental priesthood as a divinely instituted office with apostolic succession.

## Family and Moral Instruction

Jubilees' portrayal of Jacob as a father who instructs his children in righteousness and covenant faithfulness reflects a vision of the family as the primary unit of moral and religious formation. Catholic interpreters find in this portrait support for the Catholic teaching on the domestic church (ecclesia domestica) and the responsibility of parents to transmit the faith to the next generation.
