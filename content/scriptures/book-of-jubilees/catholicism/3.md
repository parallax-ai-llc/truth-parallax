## Abraham as Model of Faith and Obedience

Catholic interpreters find in Jubilees' portrait of Abraham a vision of faith that integrates trust and obedience — precisely the synthesis that Catholic theology has traditionally maintained against a faith-alone reading of the Abrahamic narrative. The text's Abraham is both a man of faith and a scrupulous observer of divine law, combining interior conviction with exterior practice. This portrait resonates with the Catholic understanding of justification as involving both faith and the works of grace.

## The Aqedah as Sacrificial Typology

Jubilees' expanded account of the Binding of Isaac, with Mastema's challenge and Abraham's unwavering obedience, has been read by Catholic interpreters as a type of Christ's passion. The text's dating of the Aqedah to the month of Nisan (the month of Passover and Easter) strengthens the typological reading, connecting Abraham's willing sacrifice of his son to God's offering of his own Son. Catholic liturgical and theological tradition has long drawn on this typology, and Jubilees provides additional Second Temple evidence for its antiquity.

## Separation from Idolatry

Jubilees' vivid account of Abraham's youthful struggle against the idolatry of his family and culture resonates with the Catholic missionary tradition and its emphasis on conversion as a decisive break with false worship. The text's narrative of Abraham burning the house of idols and choosing the true God provides a paradigm for the radical transformation that Catholic theology associates with baptismal conversion.
