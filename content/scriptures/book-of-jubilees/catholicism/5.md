## Passover and Eucharistic Typology

Catholic interpreters read Jubilees' detailed Passover legislation in light of the typological relationship between the Passover lamb and Christ's sacrifice. The text's insistence on specific ritual requirements — the lamb without blemish, the blood on the doorposts, the consumption within the household — provides additional Second Temple evidence for the liturgical context from which the Last Supper and the Eucharist emerged. The precision of Jubilees' Passover halakhah illuminates the ritual world that shaped early Christian sacramental theology.

## The Cosmic Dimension of the Exodus

Jubilees' introduction of Mastema into the Exodus narrative — as the force behind the Egyptian magicians and the adversary of Moses — gives the Exodus a cosmic dimension that Catholic theology recognizes in its own reading of salvation history. The Exodus as a victory over not merely human but spiritual oppressors prefigures the Catholic understanding of Christ's redemptive work as a triumph over sin, death, and the powers of evil.

## Sabbath and the Lord's Day

The concluding sabbath legislation provides Catholic scholars with valuable context for understanding the early Church's transition from sabbath to Lord's Day observance. The text's elevation of the sabbath as the supreme sign of the covenant helps explain both the Jewish attachment to sabbath practice and the significance of the Christian claim that Christ's resurrection on the first day inaugurated a new creation and a new ordering of sacred time.
