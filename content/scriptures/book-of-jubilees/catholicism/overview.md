## Overview

Catholic scholarship approaches the Book of Jubilees as a significant Jewish pseudepigraphon that, while not part of the Catholic biblical canon, provides important context for understanding the development of biblical interpretation and theological traditions that influenced early Christianity.

## Canonical Status

The Catholic Church does not include Jubilees in its canon, though it maintains a broader Old Testament canon than Protestantism (including the deuterocanonical books). Jubilees is classified among the pseudepigrapha. However, some early Church Fathers cited it, and the Ethiopian Orthodox Church — in communion with Rome on many doctrinal matters — considers it canonical.

## Patristic Reception

Several early Church Fathers demonstrated awareness of Jubilees:

- **Epiphanius of Salamis** cited the book in his *Panarion*
- **Jerome** was aware of traditions found in Jubilees
- The book's angelology influenced early Christian theological development

## Liturgical and Theological Parallels

Catholic scholars note several areas where Jubilees illuminates Catholic tradition:

- **Angelic mediation**: Jubilees' elaborate angel hierarchy resonates with Catholic angelology
- **Sacred calendar**: The emphasis on liturgical time parallels Catholic liturgical sensibility
- **Tradition alongside Scripture**: Jubilees' claim of oral Mosaic tradition parallels Catholic teaching on Sacred Tradition
