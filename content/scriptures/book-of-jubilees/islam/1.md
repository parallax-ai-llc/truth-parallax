## Chapter 1 — Prologue on Sinai

Jubilees 1 describes a divine revelation to Musa (Moses) at Sinai mediated through an angel. Islamic scholars engaging with this chapter through the *Isra'iliyyat* framework note the parallel with the Quranic account of revelation through Jibril (Gabriel), while maintaining that the Quran represents the final, uncorrupted divine message.

## Islamic Scholarly Perspective

Muslim scholars approach this chapter within the *Isra'iliyyat* tradition, which permits cautious engagement with Jewish and Christian narratives that do not contradict Quranic teaching. The prophetic narratives found here are evaluated against the Quranic criterion: what aligns with the Quran may be accepted, what contradicts it is rejected, and what is neither confirmed nor denied may be transmitted without judgment.

The chapter's emphasis on divine sovereignty, prophetic faithfulness, and moral accountability resonates with core Quranic themes (*tawhid*, *risalah*, *akhirah*). However, the specific legal prescriptions and calendar system reflect a pre-Islamic Jewish context that Islam supersedes with its own comprehensive legal and liturgical framework.

## Comparative Observations

The angelic mediation described in this chapter invites comparison with the Islamic understanding of *wahy* (revelation) transmitted through Jibril. Both traditions affirm that divine knowledge reaches humanity through angelic intermediaries, though the theological frameworks differ significantly.
