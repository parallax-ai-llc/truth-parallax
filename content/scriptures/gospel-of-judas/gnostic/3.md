## The Sethian Cosmogony

The cosmogonic revelation constitutes the theological center of the text. Jesus discloses to Judas the structure of the divine realm, beginning with the Great Invisible Spirit and proceeding through the emanation of Barbelo, the Self-Generated (Autogenes), the four luminaries (Harmozel, Oroiael, Daveithai, Eleleth), Adamas, Seth, and the seventy-two luminaries who produce 360 firmaments.

For Gnostic readers, this passage locates the Gospel of Judas squarely within the Sethian tradition. The cosmogonic schema closely parallels the Apocryphon of John, the Holy Book of the Great Invisible Spirit (Gospel of the Egyptians), and Zostrianos — creating an intertextual web that would have been recognizable to initiated readers. The emergence of Saklas ("fool") and Nebro ("rebel") as the creators of the material world and humanity establishes the demiurgical framework essential to Sethian soteriology: the world is not the creation of the true God but of ignorant, rebellious angels.

The figure of Adamas as a heavenly archetype — the "first human" who exists in the divine realm before any material creation — signals the Gnostic anthropology that undergirds the text. Human beings (at least those of the pneumatic race) contain a spark of Adamas's divine light, trapped in bodies fashioned by Saklas. The entire soteriological drama turns on the liberation of this divine spark from its material imprisonment.
