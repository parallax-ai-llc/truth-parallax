Islamic interpretation of Jeremiah 49 reads the oracles against the five nations as demonstrating God's universal sovereignty — a principle that the Quran articulates with particular force. The judgment of Ammon, Edom, Damascus, Kedar, and Elam shows that no nation or tribal confederation stands outside God's moral governance. The Quranic formula "How many a generation have We destroyed before them" (Quran 19:74) encompasses all these peoples within the pattern of divine judgment on human arrogance and disobedience.

The oracle against Edom (49:7-22), with its critique of Edom's famous wisdom, connects in Islamic tradition to the Quranic teaching that human knowledge is always subordinate to divine knowledge. Surah Al-Kahf (18:109) declares that "if the sea were ink for the words of my Lord, the sea would be exhausted before the words of my Lord were exhausted." Edom's wisdom, however celebrated among the nations, is finite and fallible before the infinite knowledge of God. The destruction of Teman and Bozrah demonstrates that intellectual achievement provides no protection against divine judgment.

The oracle against Kedar and Hazor (49:28-33), concerning the Arabian tribes, has particular resonance in Islamic tradition. While these pre-Islamic Arabian peoples are not directly connected to the Prophet Muhammad's lineage, their mention in the biblical prophetic corpus confirms the Islamic understanding that Arabia was within the scope of prophetic concern long before Muhammad's mission. The judgment on Kedar and Hazor illustrates the Quranic principle that God's dealings with the Arabian peoples, like His dealings with all peoples, are governed by the same moral standards of justice and accountability.
