The de-creation imagery of Jeremiah 4:23-26 — the earth returning to *tohu vavohu* — is read by Gnostic interpreters as a revelation of the material world's fundamental instability. The Demiurge's creation, fashioned in ignorance as a flawed imitation of the Pleroma, contains within itself the seeds of its own dissolution. The language of de-creation does not describe a punishment imposed from outside but the inevitable collapse of a cosmos built on a faulty foundation. The *Apocryphon of John* teaches that the material world was never "good" in any ultimate sense; Jeremiah's de-creation vision confirms that even the prophet sensed its fragility.

The command to "circumcise your hearts" (4:4) is read by Gnostic interpreters as a call to cut away the material accretions that obscure the divine spark within. Physical circumcision — the mark of the Demiurge's covenant — operates on the flesh, but the circumcision of the heart operates on the inner being where the pneumatic seed resides. Gnostic soteriology understands salvation as a process of stripping away the layers of material and psychic conditioning that prevent the divine spark from recognizing its origin in the Pleroma.

The prophet's personal anguish (4:19) — "My anguish, my anguish!" — is interpreted in Gnostic reading as the suffering of the pneumatic soul trapped between two worlds. Jeremiah is bound to the Demiurge's system as a prophet of Israel, yet his anguish reveals a deeper awareness that something is profoundly wrong with the world he inhabits. This is the existential condition of the Gnostic before full awakening: the sense that the material world is not home, that suffering is not merely incidental but structural, woven into the fabric of the Demiurge's creation.
