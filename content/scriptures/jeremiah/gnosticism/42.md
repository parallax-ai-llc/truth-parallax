The remnant's false request for guidance (42:1-6) is read by Gnostic interpreters as illustrating the condition of the *hylic* (material) consciousness: the appearance of seeking truth while remaining fundamentally committed to the material world's logic of self-preservation. The Valentinian classification of humanity into pneumatics, psychics, and hylics finds expression in this chapter — the community's inability to receive and act upon divine guidance reveals them as operating at the hylic or, at best, psychic level of awareness.

The ten-day delay before God's response represents, in Gnostic reading, the interval between the call of the Pleroma and the soul's capacity to receive it. The *Trimorphic Protennoia* describes the divine voice calling repeatedly into the darkness of matter, waiting for the pneumatic seeds to awaken. But unlike the pneumatic who recognizes the voice and responds, the community in Jeremiah 42 hears the words but cannot receive their meaning — they lack the spiritual capacity (*nous*) to translate hearing into obedience.

Jeremiah's discernment of the people's hypocrisy (42:19-22) exemplifies the pneumatic's ability to perceive the interior reality behind material appearances. The prophet sees through the community's outward piety to their inward rebellion, just as the Gnostic revealer in the *Apocryphon of John* perceives the Demiurge's true nature behind his claims of sole divinity. This capacity for spiritual discernment — seeing the hidden truth — is the essence of *gnosis* itself.
