## Overview

A comparative approach to the Meshaf Resh situates this text within the broader context of world religious literature, identifying parallels, contrasts, and mutual influences across traditions. Offers parallels with Zoroastrian, Gnostic, and ancient Mesopotamian creation narratives. This comparative perspective enriches understanding both of the Meshaf Resh itself and of the religious traditions with which it is brought into dialogue.

Key areas of comparative inquiry include cosmological narratives, theological concepts, ethical frameworks, ritual practices, and soteriological visions. The comparative study of the Meshaf Resh contributes to a deeper appreciation of both the universal themes in human religious experience and the distinctive contributions of Yazidi Faith to the world's spiritual heritage.
