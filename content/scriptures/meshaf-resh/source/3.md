---
title: "Meshaf Resh - Chapter 3"
scripture: "meshaf-resh"
chapter: 3
---

**1** After this God sent down to the earth one hundred and four books. Of these, He sent down to the people thirty books with Adam, and the Psalms with David, and the Torah with Moses, and the Gospel with Jesus.

**2** But the other peoples altered and corrupted their books. The Yezidis alone received the truth and kept it.

**3** The Yezidis are the beloved of Melek Taus. They do not speak the name of Shaytan, for in that name is one of the seventy-two names of God, and they do not wish to take it in vain.

**4** They do not wear blue, for it is the sacred color of Melek Taus. They do not eat lettuce, for the name of it resembles the name of one of their holy ones. They do not eat pumpkin, for it is associated with the suffering of their people.

**5** In their worship they face the sun, for the sun is the throne of Melek Taus upon the earth, and the light is his glory.

**6** Three times a day they pray: at sunrise, at noon, and at sunset. And the prayer at sunrise is the most important, for it greets the coming of the light.

**7** At prayer they turn toward the sun and say: "O God, Thou art the great, the glorious, the mighty! From Thee comes all light and all goodness."

**8** The sacred pilgrimage of the Yezidis is to the valley of Lalish, where the tomb of Sheikh Adi lies. For Sheikh Adi was the great saint of the Yezidis, and his spirit abides there still.

**9** In the valley of Lalish there are springs of sacred water, and groves of ancient trees, and temples carved in the rock.

**10** Every year in autumn the Yezidis gather at Lalish for the feast of the Assembly. And they light lamps and sing hymns and make their offerings.

**11** And the peacock, which is the symbol of Melek Taus, is honored above all creatures. For the peacock is beautiful and proud, and its tail feathers contain the colors of the rainbow, which is the sign of God's covenant.

**12** These are the ways of the people of Melek Taus, as handed down from the ancients.
