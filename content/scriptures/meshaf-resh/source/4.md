---
title: "Meshaf Resh - Chapter 4"
scripture: "meshaf-resh"
chapter: 4
---

**1** Concerning the laws and ordinances: the Yezidis are forbidden to marry outside their caste, for each of the three castes — the Murids, the Sheikhs, and the Pirs — shall keep its lineage pure.

**2** Every Yezidi shall have a Brother or Sister of the Hereafter. These spiritual companions share the bond of eternal friendship.

**3** The Sheikh shall be the teacher of the faith. The Pir shall be the guardian of the traditions. The Murid shall be the faithful worshipper.

**4** When a child is born, it is baptized in the waters of the White Spring at Lalish, or in water brought from that spring.

**5** Circumcision is practiced, and the child is presented to a spiritual guardian who becomes its godparent in the faith.

**6** At death, the body is washed and clothed in white. The Sheikh reads the prayers of the dead. The body is placed in the earth with the face turned toward the east, toward the rising sun.

**7** Fasting is observed at appointed times. In the month of December, a three-day fast is kept in honor of the sun. In the month of the Assembly, the pilgrims fast before the feast.

**8** The five daily duties of the Yezidi are: prayer, fasting, pilgrimage, almsgiving, and loyalty to the community.

**9** No Yezidi shall reveal the sacred mysteries to outsiders. The inner teachings are transmitted from master to pupil, from Sheikh to Murid, in the sacred trust.

**10** The sacred objects are the Sanjak, the peacock standard of Melek Taus, of which there are seven, one for each of the seven regions of the earth.

**11** Once a year, the Sanjaks are carried through the Yezidi communities, and the faithful make their offerings and receive blessings.

**12** These are the ordinances given by Melek Taus through Sheikh Adi, preserved by the Yezidi people in faith and obedience.
