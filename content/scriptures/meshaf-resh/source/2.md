---
title: "Meshaf Resh - Chapter 2"
scripture: "meshaf-resh"
chapter: 2
---

**1** And Melek Taus descended to the earth and found it trembling and unsteady. He went about it, and from his own radiance he made it firm.

**2** He adorned it with beautiful flowers, trees, and plants. He created also the beasts and the birds and the creatures of the sea.

**3** Then God created Adam from earth, water, air, and fire. He placed in him a soul by His power. Then He commanded the angel Jibra'il to take Adam into Paradise.

**4** And God said to Adam: "Thou mayest eat of all the trees, but of wheat thou must not eat." For God knew that wheat was the cause of all trouble.

**5** After a hundred years, Melek Taus said to God: "How will Adam multiply and where is his posterity, if he does not eat of the grain?"

**6** And God said: "I have put the whole matter into thy hands." Then Melek Taus went and told Adam: "Hast thou eaten of the grain?" Adam answered: "No, God has forbidden me."

**7** Then Melek Taus said unto him: "Eat of the grain, and all shall be well with thee." And Adam ate of the grain, and immediately his belly swelled.

**8** But Melek Taus drove him out of the garden, and left him. Adam was troubled because his belly was swollen, for he had no outlet. God sent a bird to him which pecked open an outlet, and Adam was relieved.

**9** Then Jibra'il was absent from Adam for a hundred years. And Adam was sorrowful.

**10** Then God commanded Jibra'il to create Eve from under the left shoulder of Adam. And Eve was created, and she was his companion.

**11** And Adam and Eve bore two children, male and female. The male was called Shehr ibn Jaber, and the female was called his sister.

**12** Then there arose a dispute between Adam's children as to whether there were among them those who were the progeny of Adam only, without Eve.

**13** And it was decided by a test. Each child placed a gift in a sealed jar, and the jars were buried. And after forty days, the jars were opened. And from the jar of the son came forth a beautiful child, but from the jar of the daughter came forth only corruption.

**14** Thus it was known that from the son of Adam came the Yezidi people, the chosen of Melek Taus.
