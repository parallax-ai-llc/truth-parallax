## Sikh Reading

The Sikh community's relationship with the Chandi Charitar is complex and debated. The composition retells the Hindu *Puranic* narratives of the goddess Chandi (Durga) and her cosmic battles against demonic forces, but through a Sikh theological lens that interprets Chandi not as a Hindu deity but as a manifestation of the one formless God's power (*Shakti*). The martial valor celebrated in the Chandi Charitar informs the Khalsa spirit of courage and fearlessness.

Sikh scholars who attribute the Chandi Charitar to Guru Gobind Singh understand it as a deliberate use of mythological narrative to inspire martial courage in the Khalsa — the Guru appropriating existing cultural stories to shape the warrior identity of the Sikh community. The text's vivid battle scenes and celebration of heroic valor serve as spiritual preparation for the real-world struggles the Khalsa would face.
