## Sikh Reading

The Jaap Sahib is the most liturgically important composition in the Dasam Granth for the Sikh community. Recited as part of the daily *Nitnem* (morning prayers) and central to the *Amrit Sanskar* (Khalsa initiation ceremony), the Jaap Sahib is a hymn of 199 verses praising the formless, timeless God (*Akal Purakh*) through an extraordinary litany of divine names and attributes drawn from Sanskrit, Persian, Arabic, and Braj Bhasha.

The Sikh community receives the Jaap Sahib as Guru Gobind Singh's supreme expression of devotion to the one God who transcends all names, forms, and religious categories. Each verse adds another facet to the infinite divine reality, building a cumulative portrait of God as simultaneously beyond all description and present in every aspect of creation. The practice of reciting the Jaap Sahib is understood as both an act of worship and a spiritual discipline that expands the devotee's consciousness of the divine.
