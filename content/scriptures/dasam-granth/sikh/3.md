## Sikh Reading

The Sikh community receives the Bachitar Natak as Guru Gobind Singh's own account of his divine mission — a first-person narrative in which the Guru describes his pre-birth existence in the divine realm, his commission by God to establish righteousness on earth, and the events of his earthly life, including the founding of the Khalsa. The Bachitar Natak is read as spiritual autobiography, establishing Guru Gobind Singh's identity as a divinely commissioned warrior-saint.

The narrative's account of the Guru's lineage — tracing the succession of Sikh Gurus as a continuous divine mission — provides the theological framework for understanding the relationship between the ten human Gurus and the eternal Guru manifested in the Guru Granth Sahib. The Bachitar Natak's martial spirit and its emphasis on righteous resistance to tyranny continue to shape Sikh identity and self-understanding.
