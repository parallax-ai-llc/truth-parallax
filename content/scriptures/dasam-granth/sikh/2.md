## Sikh Reading

The Sikh community receives Akal Ustat as Guru Gobind Singh's philosophical masterpiece — a sustained meditation on the nature of God as the Immortal (*Akal*), the Timeless One who exists beyond birth, death, and the cycle of creation and destruction. The composition explores the paradox of divine transcendence and immanence: God is utterly beyond human comprehension yet present in every atom of creation.

Portions of the Akal Ustat are recited in Sikh worship, and its theology informs the Sikh understanding of *Akal Purakh* (the Timeless Being) as the fundamental reality underlying all existence. The text's most celebrated passage declares that the temple and the mosque, Hindu worship and Muslim prayer, are all directed toward the same God — a statement of theological inclusivism that shapes Sikh attitudes toward other religions.
