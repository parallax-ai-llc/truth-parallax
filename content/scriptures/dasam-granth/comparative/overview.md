## Overview

From a comparative perspective, the Dasam Granth represents a remarkable example of inter-traditional literary and theological engagement. The text's retelling of Hindu *Puranic* narratives — stories of Chandi, Rama, Krishna, and other figures — through a Sikh theological lens demonstrates how one tradition can creatively appropriate and reinterpret the sacred stories of another without simple acceptance or rejection.

The Dasam Granth's martial theology invites comparison with other traditions that affirm the righteousness of armed resistance: the concept of *jihad* in Islam, the Just War tradition in Christianity, the *dharma yudh* (righteous war) in Hindu thought, and the warrior ethos of Japanese Bushido. The text's integration of devotional piety with martial valor challenges the common assumption that spirituality and warfare are inherently opposed.
