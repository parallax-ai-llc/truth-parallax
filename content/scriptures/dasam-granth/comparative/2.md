## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Akal Ustat examines its philosophical theology alongside other traditions' reflections on divine transcendence and immanence. The text's treatment of God as simultaneously beyond all categories and present in all things echoes Christian mysticism (Meister Eckhart), Islamic Sufism (Ibn Arabi's *wahdat al-wujud*), and Advaita Vedanta (Shankara's Brahman).

Comparative theologians note the Akal Ustat's explicit inclusivism — its declaration that diverse forms of worship all reach the same God — as a distinctive contribution to the global conversation about religious pluralism. The text goes beyond mere tolerance to affirm a substantive theological unity underlying religious diversity.
