## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Jaap Sahib reveals a composition that consciously draws upon the divine-name traditions of Hinduism, Islam, and indigenous Indic spirituality. The hymn's multilingual litany of divine attributes invites comparison with the Ninety-Nine Names of God (*al-asma al-husna*) in Islam, the *Vishnu Sahasranama* (Thousand Names of Vishnu) in Hinduism, and the divine-name mysticism of Jewish Kabbalah.

Comparative scholars note that the Jaap Sahib's theological strategy — using names from multiple traditions to point toward a God who transcends all names — represents a distinctive approach to the problem of religious pluralism. Rather than claiming that one tradition's names are correct and others false, the Jaap Sahib suggests that all genuine names of God are partial reflections of an infinite reality.
