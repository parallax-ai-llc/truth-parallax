## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Zafarnama places it alongside other letters of moral and spiritual resistance written by religious leaders to political authorities: Martin Luther King Jr.'s "Letter from Birmingham Jail," Dietrich Bonhoeffer's prison letters, Mahatma Gandhi's correspondence with British officials, and the Apostle Paul's prison epistles.

Comparative scholars note the Zafarnama's distinctive combination of political protest, theological argument, and literary artistry. The Guru's decision to address Aurangzeb in Persian — the language of the Mughal court — and to ground his critique in Islamic principles demonstrates a sophisticated strategy of internal criticism that challenges the emperor on his own terms.
