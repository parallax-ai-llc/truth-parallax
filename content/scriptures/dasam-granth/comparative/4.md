## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Chandi Charitar examines the phenomenon of one religious tradition retelling the sacred narratives of another. The Sikh retelling of Hindu goddess mythology parallels similar inter-traditional appropriations: Christian reinterpretation of Hebrew Bible narratives, Islamic retelling of Biblical stories in the Quran, and Buddhist adaptation of Hindu cosmological frameworks.

Comparative scholars note that the Chandi Charitar does not merely repeat Hindu mythology but transforms it — subordinating the goddess to the one formless God of Sikh theology and redirecting the narrative's spiritual energy from goddess worship to Khalsa martial identity. This process of appropriation and transformation illuminates the dynamics of inter-traditional influence across religious boundaries.
