## Comparative Reading

The comparative study of the Bachitar Natak places it alongside other prophetic autobiographies and divine-commission narratives: Muhammad's call in the Cave of Hira, Moses' encounter at the burning bush, Paul's Damascus road experience, and the Bab's declaration. Each narrative establishes the prophet's authority through a direct encounter with the divine, and the Bachitar Natak's account of Guru Gobind Singh's pre-birth commission represents a distinctive variant of this universal pattern.

Comparative scholars note the Bachitar Natak's integration of cosmic mythology with political history — the divine plan encompassing both the metaphysical realm and the specific military conflicts of late seventeenth-century Punjab. This fusion of cosmic and historical narrative distinguishes the Bachitar Natak from prophetic calls that focus primarily on spiritual transformation.
