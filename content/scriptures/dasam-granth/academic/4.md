## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the Chandi Charitar examines it as a case study in inter-traditional literary appropriation. The composition retells narratives from the *Markandeya Purana* and the *Devi Mahatmya* — foundational texts of Hindu Shakta tradition — in Braj Bhasha, raising questions about authorship, audience, and theological purpose.

Scholars debate whether the Chandi Charitar was composed by Guru Gobind Singh himself or by court poets working under his patronage. The text's literary quality, its mastery of the *bir ras* (heroic sentiment) in Braj poetics, and its sophisticated reinterpretation of Hindu mythology through Sikh monotheism demonstrate the high literary culture of the Guru's court at Anandpur Sahib.
