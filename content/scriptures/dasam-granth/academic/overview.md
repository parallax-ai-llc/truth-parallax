## Overview

Academic study of the Dasam Granth has been dominated by the authorship question: which portions of the text were actually composed by Guru Gobind Singh, and which were contributed by court poets or added by later compilers? Scholars employ linguistic analysis, stylistic comparison, and manuscript study to distinguish between texts likely authored by the Guru and those attributed to poets in his circle.

The linguistic diversity of the Dasam Granth — spanning Braj Bhasha, Hindi, Persian, Punjabi, and Sanskrit — presents significant challenges for textual criticism. Academic consensus generally attributes the Jaap Sahib, Akal Ustat, Bachitar Natak, Zafarnama, and certain other compositions to Guru Gobind Singh, while regarding the extensive *Puranic* retellings (Chandi Charitar, Chaubis Avtar, etc.) as more likely the work of court poets working under the Guru's patronage. The Dasam Granth also provides invaluable evidence for the literary culture of the Guru's court at Anandpur Sahib.
