## Academic Reading

Academic analysis of the Jaap Sahib examines its remarkable multilingual composition and its theological architecture. The hymn systematically draws upon the divine-name traditions of multiple religious cultures — Sanskrit epithets from Hindu theology, Arabic and Persian names from Islamic tradition, and Braj Bhasha devotional vocabulary — creating a litany of praise that transcends any single linguistic or religious tradition.

Scholars note the Jaap Sahib's sophisticated use of *via negativa* (apophatic theology) alongside *via positiva* (cataphatic theology): God is described both through an abundance of positive attributes and through the systematic negation of all limiting categories. This double movement — affirming and denying simultaneously — places the Jaap Sahib within a global tradition of apophatic mysticism that includes Pseudo-Dionysius, Maimonides, and the Sufi concept of *tanzih* (divine transcendence).
