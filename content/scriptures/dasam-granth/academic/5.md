## Academic Reading

Academic study of the Zafarnama examines it as a remarkable document of political theology, Persian literary art, and historical witness. Written in the *masnavi* form (rhyming couplets) of classical Persian poetry, the letter demonstrates Guru Gobind Singh's mastery of the literary conventions of the Mughal court — the very culture against which he was fighting.

Scholars analyze the Zafarnama's rhetorical strategy: the Guru addresses Aurangzeb not as a foreign enemy but as a fellow servant of God who has failed to live up to his own religious principles. By quoting the Quran and invoking Islamic concepts of justice and divine accountability, the Guru turns the emperor's own religion against him — a brilliant act of political and theological argumentation.
