## Overview

The Sufi reading of the Dasam Granth focuses on its Persian-language compositions, particularly the Zafarnama — Guru Gobind Singh's extraordinary letter to the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. Written in elegant Persian verse, the Zafarnama engages with Islamic literary and theological conventions, invoking the name of God (*Allah*) and drawing upon the Persian poetic tradition that Sufis claim as their own.

Sufi scholars note the remarkable theological resonance between the Dasam Granth's concept of the formless, nameless God (*Akal Purakh*) and the Sufi understanding of *tawhid* (divine unity) and the *dhat* (divine essence) that transcends all names and attributes. The Guru's willingness to invoke God in Islamic terminology — while maintaining Sikh theological independence — demonstrates a spiritual generosity that Sufi practitioners find deeply sympathetic.
