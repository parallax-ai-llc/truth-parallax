## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reading of the Akal Ustat finds remarkable resonance with Islamic mystical theology. The composition's treatment of divine transcendence — God as beyond all names, forms, and categories — echoes the Sufi concept of *tanzih* (divine incomparability), while its affirmation of God's presence in all things resonates with *tashbih* (divine similarity) and the Sufi teaching of *wahdat al-wujud* (unity of being).

Sufi scholars are particularly drawn to the Akal Ustat's declaration that Hindu and Muslim worship reach the same God, finding in this statement a parallel to the Sufi conviction — expressed by poets like Rumi and Hafiz — that divine love transcends the boundaries of religious law and sectarian identity. The philosophical depth of the Akal Ustat demonstrates that Guru Gobind Singh was deeply conversant with the Sufi intellectual tradition.
