## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reading of the Zafarnama engages with the letter's remarkable deployment of Islamic theological concepts and Persian literary conventions. The Guru's invocation of God using Islamic names, his references to the Quran, and his appeal to the principles of Islamic justice demonstrate a deep and respectful familiarity with Islam that Sufi scholars find profoundly moving.

Sufi readers are particularly struck by the Zafarnama's moral courage — the willingness of a defeated military leader to address the most powerful emperor in the world with absolute spiritual authority. The Sufi tradition values the *faqir* (spiritual poor one) who stands before worldly power without fear, and in Guru Gobind Singh's letter, Sufi scholars recognize the voice of such a spiritual warrior — one whose moral authority derives not from political or military power but from unwavering commitment to truth and justice.
