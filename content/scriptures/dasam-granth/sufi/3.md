## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reading of the Bachitar Natak engages with its concept of the divine warrior — one commissioned by God to fight for justice in the temporal world. Sufi scholars find resonance with the Islamic concept of the *mujaddid* (renewer of the faith) and the Sufi understanding of spiritual warfare (*jihad al-nafs*) as the inner struggle against the ego, though the Bachitar Natak affirms the legitimacy of outer, physical warfare in defense of righteousness.

The text's Persian and Arabic vocabulary, its invocations of God using Islamic divine names, and its engagement with Mughal political reality demonstrate Guru Gobind Singh's deep familiarity with the Islamic world. Sufi readers find in the Bachitar Natak a spiritual warrior whose commitment to justice transcends religious boundaries.
