## Sufi Reading

The Sufi reading of the Jaap Sahib finds deep resonance with the Islamic tradition of *dhikr* (remembrance of God through the recitation of divine names). The hymn's Arabic and Persian divine names — including variations of *Rahman* (Compassionate), *Rahim* (Merciful), and other Quranic attributes — demonstrate Guru Gobind Singh's familiarity with Islamic theology and his willingness to invoke God in the language of Islam.

Sufi scholars note the Jaap Sahib's affinity with the Sufi understanding that the divine names are not merely labels but windows into the infinite divine reality. The practice of reciting the Jaap Sahib — with its rhythmic repetition and cumulative intensity — parallels the Sufi practice of *dhikr*, in which the repetition of divine names induces states of spiritual concentration and divine awareness.
