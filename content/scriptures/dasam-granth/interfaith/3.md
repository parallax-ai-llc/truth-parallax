## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Bachitar Natak highlights its theology of righteous resistance as a contribution to the global conversation about religion, violence, and justice. The text's insistence that armed struggle is legitimate only as a last resort, after all peaceful means have been exhausted, resonates with Just War theories in Christianity, the Islamic principles of *jihad*, and Gandhian concepts of righteous resistance.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Bachitar Natak a challenging voice that refuses to separate spiritual devotion from political engagement. The text's vision of a warrior-saint commissioned by God to defend the oppressed challenges both militant extremism and passive acquiescence to injustice, offering a middle path of principled, disciplined resistance rooted in divine love.
