## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Akal Ustat highlights its celebrated inclusivism as a theological foundation for interreligious understanding. The composition's declaration that the temple and the mosque, the Vedas and the Quran, are all pathways to the same God represents one of the most explicit statements of theological pluralism in any sacred text.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Akal Ustat both a theological framework and a spiritual practice for interreligious engagement. The text's insistence that God transcends all religious categories — while being genuinely accessible through all sincere worship — challenges exclusivism without dissolving the distinctive identities of particular traditions.
