## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Zafarnama highlights the letter as one of history's most remarkable examples of interreligious moral discourse. A Sikh Guru, writing in Persian, invokes Islamic theological principles to hold a Muslim emperor accountable for injustice — demonstrating that moral truth transcends religious boundaries and that the ethical principles of one tradition can be used to critique practitioners of that same tradition.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Zafarnama a model for prophetic interreligious engagement — not dialogue for its own sake but the deployment of shared moral principles in the service of justice. The letter demonstrates that genuine interreligious understanding can become a powerful instrument for social transformation, challenging those who abuse religious authority or violate the ethical principles of their own faith.
