## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Jaap Sahib highlights its multilingual, multi-traditional praise of the one God as a powerful resource for interreligious worship and dialogue. The hymn's deliberate use of divine names from Sanskrit, Arabic, Persian, and Braj Bhasha traditions demonstrates that the one God can be authentically praised in the languages and concepts of multiple religions.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Jaap Sahib a practical model for theocentric pluralism — the conviction that the diverse religions of the world, for all their differences, point toward the same ultimate divine reality. The hymn does not erase religious distinctions but transcends them, affirming that the God praised by Hindus, Muslims, and Sikhs — under different names and through different forms — is one and the same infinite, formless, timeless Being.
