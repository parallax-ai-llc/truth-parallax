## Interfaith Reading

The interfaith reading of the Chandi Charitar examines the text's creative engagement with Hindu mythology as a model for interreligious literary and theological exchange. The composition demonstrates that one tradition can engage deeply with the sacred narratives of another — retelling, reinterpreting, and transforming them — without either wholesale adoption or dismissive rejection.

Interfaith practitioners find in the Chandi Charitar evidence that sacred stories belong not to single traditions but to the broader human spiritual heritage. The Sikh retelling of Hindu goddess mythology, filtered through monotheistic theology and directed toward martial spiritual formation, illustrates the creative possibilities of interreligious engagement at the level of narrative and imagination.
