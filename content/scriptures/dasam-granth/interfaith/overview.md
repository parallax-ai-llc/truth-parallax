## Overview

The interfaith reading of the Dasam Granth highlights the text's remarkable engagement with multiple religious traditions — Hindu, Islamic, and Sikh — as a resource for interreligious dialogue. The Dasam Granth demonstrates that deep theological commitment to one tradition (Sikhism) is compatible with respectful, creative engagement with the sacred stories and literary forms of other traditions (Hindu *Puranic* narratives, Persian Islamic poetry).

Interfaith practitioners find in the Dasam Granth a model of theological generosity — the willingness to find divine truth expressed in the languages, stories, and literary conventions of traditions other than one's own. The Zafarnama's invocation of God in Islamic terms, and the Chandi Charitar's retelling of Hindu mythology through Sikh theology, demonstrate that religious boundaries need not be barriers to spiritual recognition and mutual enrichment.
