## Bhakti Reading

The Bhakti reading of the Bachitar Natak notes its transformation of the bhakti tradition's typically devotional and pacifistic ethos into a martial spirituality. While the bhakti saints generally taught non-violence and inner transformation, Guru Gobind Singh's spiritual autobiography integrates devotional love for God with the willingness to fight and die for righteousness.

Bhakti scholars see in the Bachitar Natak a creative extension of the bhakti principle that true devotion demands total surrender to the divine will — including, when necessary, the sacrifice of one's life in the service of justice. The text demonstrates that bhakti devotion is not inherently quietistic but can inspire heroic action when God commands it.
