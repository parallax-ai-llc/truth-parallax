## Bhakti Reading

The Bhakti reading of the Zafarnama appreciates the letter's devotional foundation — the trust in God's justice and sovereignty that sustains the Guru's moral courage in the face of devastating personal and military loss. The letter's opening invocation of the one God and its pervasive theology of divine justice reflect the bhakti conviction that ultimate reality is personal, just, and compassionate.

Bhakti scholars note that the Zafarnama extends the bhakti tradition's concept of surrender (*prapatti* or *sharanagati*) into the political realm: the Guru surrenders not to the emperor but to God, and from this surrender draws the strength to challenge earthly injustice. The letter demonstrates that bhakti devotion is not escapist mysticism but a source of moral power that can confront even the most formidable political authority.
