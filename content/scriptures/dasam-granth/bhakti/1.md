## Bhakti Reading

The Bhakti reading of the Jaap Sahib situates it within the tradition of devotional praise that extends from the Vedic hymns through the Alvars and Nayanars to the medieval *sant* poets. The hymn's emotional intensity, its accumulation of divine attributes, and its posture of humble adoration before the infinite God all reflect the bhakti tradition's central conviction that love and praise are the highest forms of worship.

Bhakti scholars note that the Jaap Sahib's insistence on God's formlessness (*nirguna*) aligns it with the *nirguna bhakti* tradition of Kabir and Guru Nanak, while its exuberant, multilingual praise of divine attributes introduces a *saguna* dimension — not by ascribing form to God but by affirming that the formless God is knowable through an infinity of qualities. This creative tension between form and formlessness is characteristic of the Sikh bhakti tradition.
