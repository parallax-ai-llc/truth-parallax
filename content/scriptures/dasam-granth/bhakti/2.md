## Bhakti Reading

The Bhakti reading of the Akal Ustat appreciates its philosophical depth while noting its continuity with the devotional tradition of *nirguna bhakti*. The composition's insistence on God's formlessness, timelessness, and transcendence echoes the teaching of Kabir, Ravidas, and Guru Nanak, while its literary sophistication and philosophical ambition represent a new level of theological articulation within the bhakti tradition.

Bhakti scholars note that the Akal Ustat maintains the emotional core of devotional practice — awe, love, and surrender before the divine — even as it engages in rigorous philosophical argument. The text demonstrates that *nirguna bhakti* is not merely a simple, unlettered piety but a tradition capable of sustaining sophisticated theological reflection.
