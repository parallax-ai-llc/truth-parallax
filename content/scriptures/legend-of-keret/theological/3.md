The third tablet raises the theological question of sacred kingship in crisis. If the king is 'son of El,' can he be mortal? Can he fail? Yassib's challenge articulates a theology of conditional kingship: the king's divine status does not exempt him from the obligations of justice. When the king fails to protect the weak, his right to rule is forfeit.

El's healing of Keret resolves the crisis at the divine level but does not address the political challenge. The theological message is dual: the king's relationship with El is real (El heals him) but does not override the king's obligation to his people. Sacred kingship does not mean unchecked power.
