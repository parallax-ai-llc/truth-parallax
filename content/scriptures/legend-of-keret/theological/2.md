The second tablet presents a theology of divine promise — El's blessing of Keret with progeny echoes the patriarchal promises of Genesis (15:5, 22:17). The theological emphasis is on divine generosity: El does not merely restore what Keret lost but promises abundance beyond imagining. Yet the unfulfilled vow to Athirat introduces a theological complication: divine favor is conditional, and broken promises to the gods have consequences.

The tension between El's unconditional blessing and the conditional obligation of the vow creates a theological problem that the third tablet will bring to crisis: can divine favor coexist with human failure to honor divine obligation?
