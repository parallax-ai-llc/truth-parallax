KTU 1.14 was found in the house of the high priest at Ras Shamra, along with the Baal Cycle and the Tale of Aqhat. The tablet is attributed to the scribe Ilimilku, who also copied the other major Ugaritic narratives. The tablet's archaeological context — a priestly library — suggests the text served cultic or educational functions.

The detailed military instructions in El's dream speech — the mustering of troops, the preparation of provisions, the march to Udum — have been compared to actual military administrative texts from Ugarit and other Late Bronze Age sites, suggesting that the literary description draws on genuine military practice.
