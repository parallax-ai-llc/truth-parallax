The march to Udum has prompted scholarly attempts to identify the city with a real Late Bronze Age site. Proposals have included Edom, Udummu (in the Amarna letters), and various sites in Transjordan, but no identification is certain. The military details — troop mustering, siege tactics, negotiation terms — correspond to known Late Bronze Age military practice as documented in the Amarna letters and Hittite military texts.

The vow to Athirat connects to archaeological evidence of Asherah worship at Ugarit and across the Levant. Inscriptions from Kuntillet Ajrud ('YHWH and his Asherah') and numerous clay figurines attest to the widespread veneration of this goddess.
