Keret's dream-theophany — El appearing to a sleeping king with specific instructions — parallels several important biblical scenes:
- **Solomon's dream at Gibeon** (1 Kings 3:5-15): God appears to the king in a dream and offers a gift
- **Jacob's dream at Bethel** (Genesis 28:12-17): A divine revelation during sleep that changes the dreamer's destiny
- **The Annunciation patterns**: Divine messengers delivering instructions for obtaining a child

The dream as a medium of divine communication is one of the most widespread motifs in ancient Near Eastern religion, attested in Mesopotamian, Egyptian, Hittite, and biblical texts. The Keret text provides one of the most fully developed examples.
