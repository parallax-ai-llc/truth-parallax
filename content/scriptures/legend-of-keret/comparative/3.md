Keret's illness and the challenge to his kingship parallel several biblical narratives:
- **Job**: A righteous man struck by illness who maintains his faithfulness
- **Hezekiah**: A king miraculously healed by God (2 Kings 20:1-11) — the closest structural parallel
- **David and Absalom**: A king challenged by his own son for failing in his duties (2 Samuel 15-18)

Yassib's accusation — that Keret has failed to protect the widow and orphan — echoes the language of social justice found across ancient Near Eastern royal ideology and in biblical prophetic literature (Isaiah 1:17, Psalm 72:12-14). The king's obligation to the vulnerable is a universal theme of ancient kingship.
