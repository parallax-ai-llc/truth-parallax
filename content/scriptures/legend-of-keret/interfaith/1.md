The image of God appearing to a grieving king in a dream resonates across religious traditions:
- **Judaism**: God appears to Solomon in a dream (1 Kings 3); Jacob's dream of the ladder (Genesis 28)
- **Christianity**: Joseph receives divine guidance through dreams (Matthew 1:20, 2:13)
- **Islam**: Ibrahim's dream of sacrificing his son (Quran 37:102); the Prophet's night journey
- **Hinduism**: Arjuna receives divine revelation from Krishna on the battlefield

The first tablet of Keret speaks to the universal human experience of encountering the sacred in moments of deepest vulnerability — when grief has stripped away all pretense and the heart is finally open.
