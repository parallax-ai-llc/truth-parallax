The tension between sacred authority and social obligation resonates across every religious tradition:
- **Judaism**: The prophets challenge kings who oppress the poor (Amos 5:11-12, Isaiah 10:1-2)
- **Christianity**: Jesus's challenge to religious leaders who 'devour widows' houses' (Mark 12:40)
- **Islam**: The caliph's obligation to justice is a fundamental principle of Islamic political theology
- **Buddhism**: The concept of the *Cakkavatti* (righteous king) whose rule depends on maintaining dharma
- **Confucianism**: The Mandate of Heaven can be withdrawn from an unjust ruler

The Legend of Keret's third tablet presents perhaps the earliest literary dramatization of this universal principle: power without justice forfeits its legitimacy.
