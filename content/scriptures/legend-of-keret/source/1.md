---
title: "Legend of Keret - Chapter 1"
scripture: "legend-of-keret"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The abode of Keret was destroyed, the dwelling of the king was laid waste. The house that had seven brothers, eight sons of one mother, was no more.

**2** Keret, the noble one, was bereft of offspring. His lawful wife had departed, his rightful spouse had gone away. The woman he had married had withdrawn, she that bore his children had left him.

**3** A third of his kin had died of pestilence, a fourth had fallen to disease. A fifth was gathered by Resheph, a sixth perished by the sea. A seventh fell by the sword.

**4** Keret saw his offspring destroyed, wholly and entirely destroyed was his royal house. His posterity had utterly perished, his succession had been completely ruined.

**5** He entered his chamber and wept, he repeated his lamentation and shed tears. His tears poured down like shekels to the ground, like pieces of five upon his bed.

**6** As he wept, sleep overcame him. As he shed tears, slumber fell upon him. Sleep prevailed over him and he lay down. Slumber overcame him and he reclined.

**7** And in his dream El descended, in his vision the Father of Man came down. And El drew near and asked: "What ails Keret that he weeps? What troubles the Gracious One, the Lad of El, that he sheds tears?"

**8** "Does he desire the kingship of the Bull, his father? Or does he desire dominion like the Father of Man?"

**9** Keret answered: "What need have I of silver and gold, of a holding of land and of servants forever? Of three horses and a chariot from the stable of a handmaid's son?"

**10** "Grant me the sons I have not. Grant me the offspring I lack."

**11** And El, the Bull, his father, replied: "Enough of weeping, Keret. Enough of shedding tears, O Gracious One, Lad of El."

**12** "Wash thyself and make thyself ruddy. Wash thy hands to the elbow, thy fingers up to the shoulder. Enter into the shade of the pavilion."

**13** "Take a lamb of sacrifice in thy hand, a kid of sacrifice in thy right hand. Take thy food, all of it. Take a sacrificial bird."

**14** "Pour wine from a silver cup, honey from a golden cup. Go up to the top of the tower, go up to the top of the tower, mount the shoulder of the wall."

**15** "Raise thy hands to heaven, sacrifice to the Bull, thy father El. Honor Baal with thy sacrifice, the Son of Dagan with thine offering."

**16** "Then let Keret descend from the housetop. Let him prepare food for the city, wheat for the town. Let him bake bread for five months, provisions for six months."

**17** "Let the host be provisioned and let it go forth. Let the vast host be provisioned. Let the host be three hundred myriads, serfs without number, archers beyond reckoning."

**18** "They shall march in thousands like the rain, and in myriads like the spring showers. After two they shall march by the third, after three by the fourth. The lone man shall close his house, the widow shall lock herself in."

**19** "The sick man shall carry his bed, the blind man shall be led. Even the newly wed shall go forth. He shall lead his wife to another, his beloved to a stranger."

**20** "They shall go forth like locusts to the field, like grasshoppers to the border of the desert. March a day and a second, a third and a fourth day, a fifth and a sixth day."

**21** "Lo, at sunrise on the seventh day thou shalt come to Udm the Great, even to Udm the Well-Watered. Stay in the fields and encamp."

**22** "Send then a messenger to Pabil the king: 'The message of Keret the Noble: Thus says Keret the Gracious One: Give me the Lady Hurriya, the fairest of thy first-born.'"

**23** "'Whose grace is as the grace of Anat, whose beauty is as the beauty of Astarte, whose eyebrows are lapis lazuli, whose eyes are jeweled bowls. Give her to me that she may bear offspring for Keret, children for the Lad of El.'"
