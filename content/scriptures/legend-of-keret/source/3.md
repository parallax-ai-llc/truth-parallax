---
title: "Legend of Keret - Chapter 3"
scripture: "legend-of-keret"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The Lady Hurriya wept. She shed tears in the chamber of the king. She entered and wept before Keret, in the presence of the king she shed tears.

**2** "In thy life, our father, we rejoiced. In thy immortality we were glad. Like a dog in thy house we are become, like a cur at thy door."

**3** "Shall the father die as mortals die? Shall the court of Keret be given over to weeping, a dwelling where the Gracious One dwelt to mourning?"

**4** "Father, wilt thou die as men die? Or shall the court of the Gracious One become a place for the dirge of women?"

**5** "How can it be said: Keret is the son of El, the offspring of the Kindly One and the Holy? Or do gods die? Does the offspring of the Kindly One not live?"

**6** And Keret the Noble answered: "My daughter, weep not for me. Do not wail for me with wailing. Call thy brother Ilhu, whose perception is swift, whose understanding is keen."

**7** The Lady Hurriya called her brother Ilhu. "Come, let us weep for our father. He is stricken upon his bed, Keret our lord is ill."

**8** And Ilhu went forth to the gods. He lifted his voice unto heaven. He cried aloud to El the Bull: "O El, wilt thou not grant healing for Keret? Shall not the son of El live?"

**9** And the tears of Ilhu ran down like shekels to the ground. He wept before El in the assembly of the gods.

**10** El called to the assembly of the gods: "Who among the gods can cast out the sickness? Who can drive away the disease?" Seven times he asked, but none among the gods answered.

**11** Then El, the Bull, the Kindly One, said: "Sit, my sons, upon your seats, upon your thrones of dominion. I myself will act. I will create a being. I will create a remover of illness, a driver-away of disease."

**12** El filled his hands with clay. He fashioned a female being, Sha'tiqat. He said to Sha'tiqat: "Go forth. With thy hand drive out the sickness. With thy fingers drive away the disease. Go to Keret the Noble and heal him."

**13** Sha'tiqat went forth. She flew over a hundred cities, she flew over a multitude of towns. She came to the house of Keret. She entered and wept. She passed through the chamber.

**14** Death sat upon his face. She drove Death from his head. She washed his body clean of sweat. She opened his appetite for food, his desire for meat.

**15** Death departed from Keret. The sickness was driven away. The Lady Hurriya gave command. She prepared the feast. She slaughtered an ox.

**16** And Keret sat upon his throne. He sat upon his royal seat. The Lady Hurriya sat at his right hand. And Keret ate and was refreshed. His spirit returned to him.

**17** And Keret called to the Lady Hurriya: "Hear, O Lady Hurriya, prepare a lamb that I may eat, a fatling for my sustenance." And she obeyed.

**18** Then Yassib, the son of Keret, went within himself, and his heart instructed him. He drew near to his father and said:

**19** "Hear, O Keret the Noble. Listen and incline thine ear. While bandits raid, thou dost not drive them off. While enemies invade, thou dost not repel them."

**20** "Thy hand has become lax. Thou dost not judge the case of the widow. Thou dost not adjudicate the cause of the broken in spirit. Thou dost not drive out those who plunder the poor."

**21** "Before thee thou dost not feed the fatherless, nor behind thee the widow. Sickness is as thy companion, disease as thy intimate."

**22** "Descend from thy kingship that I may be king, from thy dominion that I may sit therein."

**23** And Keret the Noble answered: "May Horon break, O my son, may Horon break thy head, Astarte the Name of Baal thy skull. Mayest thou fall in the prime of thy years."

**24** But El protected the life of Keret. For Keret was the servant of El, the attendant of the Kindly One. The king sat upon his throne, and he ruled the land in justice.

**25** And all the days of Keret were long, and he reigned in Khubur, and the land was at peace. And the Gracious One, the Lad of El, was praised in the assembly of the gods, yea, he was praised forever.
