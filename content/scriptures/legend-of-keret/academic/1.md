The first tablet opens with Keret's devastating grief: he has lost his entire family — seven brothers, one by one, to various catastrophes. Keret weeps in his chamber until sleep overcomes him, and El appears to him in a dream, asking why he weeps. El instructs Keret to prepare a military expedition to Udum to obtain Huraya, daughter of King Pabel, as his wife. The instructions are extraordinarily detailed, specifying sacrifices, troop mustering, the march route, and the terms of negotiation.

Greenstein's translation has refined understanding of the dream-theophany scene, emphasizing the intimate, paternal tone of El's interaction with Keret. The detailed military instructions have been analyzed as reflecting actual Late Bronze Age military practice.
