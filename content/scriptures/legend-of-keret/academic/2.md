The second tablet narrates the execution of El's instructions: Keret marshals his army, marches to Udum, lays siege to the city, and obtains Huraya as his wife. A significant episode involves Keret's vow to the goddess Athirat (Asherah) during the march — a vow he will later fail to fulfill, with serious consequences. The tablet concludes with a divine banquet at which El blesses Keret with the promise of numerous progeny, including the birth of a special son.

Parker's analysis emphasizes the folktale elements of this section: the impossible army (so vast it covers the landscape), the three-day siege, and the divine blessing of fertility all belong to the narrative conventions of royal legend rather than historical chronicle.
