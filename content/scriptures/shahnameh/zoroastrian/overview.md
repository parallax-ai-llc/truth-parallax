## Overview

Zoroastrian engagement with the Shahnameh brings distinctive interpretive methods and concerns to this sacred text. This tradition reads the text as authoritative scripture within its community of faith.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Asha and Druj
Zoroastrian interpreters read the Shahnameh through the fundamental cosmic opposition between asha (truth/righteousness) and druj (falsehood/chaos), which pervades the narrative.

### The Farr (Divine Glory)
The concept of *khvarenah* (farr) — the divine glory that legitimizes righteous kingship — is central to Zoroastrian interpretation of the Shahnameh.

### Eschatological Reading
Zoroastrian tradition understands the Shahnameh's narrative arc in light of the cosmic drama leading to the *frashokereti* (renovation/making wonderful) at the end of time.
