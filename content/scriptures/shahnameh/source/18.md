---
title: "Shahnameh - Chapter 18"
scripture: "shahnameh"
chapter: 18
---

**1** Hear now the tale of Bizhan and Manizha, as told in the Book of Kings, the story of Iran from the beginning of time to the coming of the new age.

**2** The world is full of wonders, and the tale of kings is the greatest wonder of all. For in their deeds we see the hand of fate and the turning of fortune's wheel.

**3** The throne of power is never secure. He who sits upon it today may lie beneath it tomorrow. For the world turns, and what was high is brought low, and what was low is raised up.

**4** The wise king governs with justice and mercy. He listens to the counsel of the wise and does not let anger cloud his judgment.

**5** The brave warrior fights not for glory alone but for the protection of the innocent and the defence of the righteous.

**6** The earth has seen many kings and many warriors, but those who are remembered are those who served justice and spoke the truth.

**7** For fame is not won by the sword alone but by the heart that beats for what is right. The name of the just endures when the name of the tyrant is forgotten.

**8** Ahriman, the evil one, is always at work in the world, sowing discord, spreading falsehood, tempting men with power and pleasure.

**9** But the light of Ahura Mazda shines through the darkness, guiding those who seek the truth and strengthening those who fight for the good.

**10** This is the lesson of the Shahnameh: that the struggle between good and evil is eternal, but in the end, the light prevails. Let those who have ears to hear, hear. Let those who have eyes to see, see.
