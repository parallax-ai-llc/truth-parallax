---
title: "Shahnameh - Chapter 2"
scripture: "shahnameh"
chapter: 2
---

**1** After Kayumars came Hushang, his grandson, who was wise and just. He founded the Peshdadian dynasty and taught mankind the arts of civilization.

**2** It was Hushang who first discovered fire. One day, as he cast a stone at a serpent, the stone struck a rock and sparks flew forth.

**3** From this he learned the secret of fire, and he thanked the Creator. He established a feast in honour of fire, and this is the festival of Sadeh.

**4** Hushang taught men to draw water from rivers by digging channels, and thus irrigation came into the world.

**5** He separated iron from stone and taught the arts of the smith. He made axes and saws and showed men how to build.

**6** He taught the sowing of grain and the harvesting of crops, so that men might eat bread instead of raw herbs and roots.

**7** He divided the beasts: the wild from the tame. He taught men to keep flocks and herds, and from their wool and skins to make clothing.

**8** When Hushang died, his son Tahmuras came to the throne. He was called "the Binder of Demons," for he rode upon Ahriman himself, having made the evil one his steed.

**9** Tahmuras defeated the demons and bound them. In exchange for their freedom, they taught mankind the art of writing in thirty different scripts.

**10** Thus from the conquest of evil came the gift of knowledge, and the wisdom of the ancients was preserved in written form.
