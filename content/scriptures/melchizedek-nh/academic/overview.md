## Overview

Melchizedek (NHC IX,1) is studied for its place within the broader Melchizedek tradition attested across multiple ancient sources: Genesis 14:18-20, Psalm 110:4, Hebrews 7, the Dead Sea Scrolls' 11QMelchizedek (which portrays Melchizedek as a heavenly being who executes divine judgment), and 2 Enoch 71-72 (which describes Melchizedek's miraculous birth). The Nag Hammadi text adds a Sethian dimension to this tradition, integrating Melchizedek into the Gnostic cosmic drama. The heavily damaged condition of the manuscript limits scholars' ability to reconstruct the full argument.
