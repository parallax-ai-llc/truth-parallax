## Overview

For Gnostic readers, Melchizedek presents a powerful vision of the priestly warrior-redeemer who receives divine revelation, participates in the defeat of the archons, and will return at the end of time to wage a final battle against the cosmic powers. The identification of Melchizedek with Jesus Christ — not as a simple equation but as a complex typological relationship in which each illuminates the other — reflects the Sethian interest in figures who bridge the gap between the divine and the material realms. Melchizedek's role as priest 'without father or mother, without genealogy' (Hebrews 7:3) makes him the ideal Gnostic figure: one whose origin lies beyond the material world and its archontic genealogies.
