## Overview

The figure of Melchizedek — the priest-king who serves as mediator between the divine and human realms — finds parallels in priestly mediator traditions across cultures. The Zoroastrian Saoshyant (the eschatological savior who will lead the final battle against evil), the Hindu avatar tradition (the divine incarnating in human form to restore cosmic order), the Buddhist bodhisattva (who delays final liberation to serve others), and the Islamic Mahdi (the eschatological leader who will restore justice) all share features with the Gnostic Melchizedek's role as warrior-priest-redeemer.
