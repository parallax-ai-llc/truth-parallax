## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Nag Hammadi Melchizedek a transformation of a figure who appears briefly in Genesis 14:18-20 as the priest-king of Salem who blesses Abraham. In Second Temple Judaism, Melchizedek underwent significant development: Psalm 110:4 made him a priestly archetype, and 11QMelchizedek from the Dead Sea Scrolls elevated him to a heavenly being who executes divine judgment on Belial and his spirits. The Gnostic text continues this trajectory of elevation, integrating Melchizedek into the Sethian cosmic drama as a warrior-priest with an eschatological mission.
