Danel's mourning rituals — breaking open the eagles, cursing the cities, seven years of lamentation — parallel mourning traditions across the ancient world:
- **David's lament for Absalom** (2 Samuel 18:33): A father's devastating grief for a lost son
- **Achilles' mourning for Patroclus** (Iliad XXIII): Extended, ritualized grief including funeral games
- **Egyptian mourning practices**: The elaborate funerary rituals for the dead

Paghat's quest for vengeance parallels traditions of female avengers:
- **Electra and Orestes**: Siblings who avenge their father's murder
- **Judith**: A woman who infiltrates the enemy camp and kills the general (Book of Judith)
- **Jael**: A woman who kills the enemy commander through deception (Judges 4-5)
