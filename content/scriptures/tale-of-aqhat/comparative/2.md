Aqhat's refusal of immortality is one of the great comparative passages in ancient literature:
- **Gilgamesh**: Gilgamesh rejects Ishtar's advances (Tablet VI), citing her treatment of previous lovers — the closest parallel in tone and consequence
- **Odysseus and Calypso**: Odysseus chooses mortal Penelope over immortal Calypso (Odyssey V)
- **Achilles' choice**: Glory and death vs. long life without fame (Iliad IX)
- **Ecclesiastes**: 'The living know that they will die' (9:5) — the same philosophical acceptance

In each case, a human figure confronts the boundary between mortality and immortality and, in some sense, chooses mortality — asserting the value of a genuinely human life over an artificial extension.
