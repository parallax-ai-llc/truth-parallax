Aqhat's acceptance of mortality resonates with traditions that affirm the value of a genuinely human life:
- **Buddhism**: The acceptance of impermanence (*anicca*) as the foundation of wisdom
- **Judaism**: 'Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom' (Psalm 90:12)
- **Islam**: The acceptance of God's decree (*qadr*) including death
- **Existentialism**: Heidegger's concept of authentic existence as 'being-toward-death'

Anat's unjust murder of the truth-telling Aqhat resonates with traditions of the righteous suffering for speaking truth to power — from the biblical prophets to Socrates to Al-Hallaj. The second tablet of Aqhat affirms that truth, even when it costs everything, is more valuable than immortality obtained through self-deception.
