The granting of a child to a pious, childless parent speaks to one of the most universal human experiences — the longing for children and the gratitude when that longing is fulfilled:
- **Judaism**: The barrenness-to-fertility motif runs through Genesis (Sarah, Rebekah, Rachel) and Samuel (Hannah)
- **Christianity**: The Annunciation as the ultimate divine gift of a child
- **Islam**: Ibrahim and Sarah, Zakariya and his wife — the Quran emphasizes God's power to grant children
- **Hinduism**: The Ramayana opens with Dasharatha's desperate desire for sons

The first tablet of Aqhat speaks to this universal experience with particular tenderness: Danel's patience, El's compassion, and the joy of Aqhat's birth are rendered with warmth before the coming tragedy.
