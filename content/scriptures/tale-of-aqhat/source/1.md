---
title: "Tale of Aqhat - Chapter 1"
scripture: "tale-of-aqhat"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The tale of Aqhat, son of Danel. Danel, the man of healing, the hero, the man of the god of Harnam, sat in the entrance of the gate, beneath the mighty tree on the threshing floor.

**2** He judged the cause of the widow; he adjudicated the case of the fatherless.

**3** For seven days Danel offered sacrifice to the gods, presenting offerings of food and drink to El and to Baal, praying for a son.

**4** On the seventh day, Baal approached El, the Father of the Gods, on behalf of Danel, saying: "Danel has no son. Grant him a son, O El, Father of Years."

**5** El heard the plea of Baal and blessed Danel, saying: "By my life, Danel shall have a son. He shall have an heir who shall set up the stelae of his ancestral gods."

**6** Danel returned to his house and his wife conceived. And in the fullness of time she bore a son, and they called his name Aqhat.

**7** The child grew and became strong. And Danel rejoiced, for he had an heir to carry on his name.

**8** Now Kothar-wa-Khasis, the divine craftsman, came to Danel's house bearing a gift — a bow of surpassing beauty and arrows of great power.

**9** Danel gave the bow to his son Aqhat, and the youth was greatly pleased. With the bow he hunted in the fields and brought back game for his father's table.

**10** The fame of the bow of Aqhat spread throughout the land, and even the goddess Anat heard of its beauty and desired it for herself.
