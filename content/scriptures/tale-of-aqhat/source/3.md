---
title: "Tale of Aqhat - Chapter 3"
scripture: "tale-of-aqhat"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The land withered and the crops failed, for the blood of the innocent had been shed. Danel looked up and saw the eagles circling overhead.

**2** He cursed the eagles: "May Baal break the wings of the eagles! May they fall at my feet! I will split open their stomachs and see if there be fat and bone, fat and blood, the remains of my son."

**3** Baal broke the wings of the eagles, and they fell at Danel's feet. He searched their stomachs but found nothing.

**4** Then he saw Samal, the mother of eagles. He cursed her and Baal broke her wings. In her stomach he found the fat and bone, the remains of Aqhat.

**5** He took the remains and buried them, weeping and mourning: "May the gods crush him who slew my son!"

**6** Danel returned to his palace and for seven years he wept for his son Aqhat.

**7** Then Pughat, the daughter of Danel, sister of Aqhat, arose. She washed herself with sea-dew, she rouged herself with rouge derived from the sea, whose source is a thousand fields away.

**8** She put on a hero's garment beneath her woman's garment. She placed a dagger in her sheath and a sword upon her.

**9** She went forth to avenge the blood of her brother, to seek out Yatpan the warrior who had slain Aqhat.

**10** And so the tale tells of the courage of Pughat, the faithfulness of Danel, and the price of hubris before the gods. For the gods are just, and the blood of the innocent is never forgotten.
