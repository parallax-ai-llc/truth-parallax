---
title: "Tale of Aqhat - Chapter 2"
scripture: "tale-of-aqhat"
chapter: 2
---

**1** Anat, the Maiden, the goddess of war, came to Aqhat as he sat feasting and said: "Give me thy bow, O hero. Name thy price and I shall pay it."

**2** Aqhat answered: "I will give thee the finest timber from the Lebanon, I will give thee the choicest tendons from wild bulls, and Kothar-wa-Khasis shall make thee a bow."

**3** Anat said: "Ask for life, O Aqhat, son of Danel. Ask for life and I will grant it thee. I will make thee immortal, like the gods."

**4** Aqhat replied: "Do not deceive me, O Maiden. For a youth, thy lies are loathsome. What does a man obtain in the end? What does a mortal obtain at the last?"

**5** "Glaze will be poured upon my head, quickite upon my crown. The death of all men I shall die, and I shall surely die."

**6** "And furthermore, the bow is a weapon for warriors. Shall women now hunt with it?"

**7** Anat was greatly angered. She went to El, the Father of the Gods, and demanded vengeance. El would not act against the righteous youth.

**8** But Anat plotted in her heart. She took Yatpan, the warrior, and sent him against Aqhat in the form of an eagle.

**9** As Aqhat sat eating in the field, the eagles descended upon him. Among them was Yatpan, who struck Aqhat and took his life.

**10** The bow fell into the sea and was broken. And the blood of Aqhat cried out from the earth.
