The third tablet narrates the aftermath of Aqhat's murder: Danel discovers that his son has been killed when the crops fail and eagles circle overhead. He systematically breaks open the eagles until he finds Aqhat's remains in the belly of the mother eagle. Danel buries his son, curses the three cities near the site of the murder, and mourns for seven years. His daughter Paghat then takes up the quest for vengeance, disguising herself and seeking out Yatpan. The tablet breaks off before the resolution.

Scholarly debate about the missing ending has been extensive: does Paghat succeed? Is Aqhat restored to life? The text's fragmentary condition makes these questions unanswerable, though Margalit and de Moor have proposed various reconstructions.
