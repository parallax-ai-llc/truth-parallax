The gate-sitting judge described in the opening of Aqhat corresponds to an important social institution attested at Ugarit and across the ancient Near East. The city gate complex — a multi-chambered architectural feature found at virtually every excavated Levantine city — served as the primary venue for legal proceedings, commercial transactions, and public assembly. At Ugarit, the gate area has been partially excavated, revealing the kind of built environment the text describes.

The divine bow crafted by Kothar-wa-Khasis connects to the metallurgical and weapons-manufacturing industries documented at Ugarit, where bronze and copper workshops have been excavated. The composite bow was the most sophisticated weapon of the Late Bronze Age, requiring skilled craftsmanship to produce.
