The mourning rituals described in the third tablet — tearing garments, gashing flesh, pouring dust on the head, seven-year mourning periods — correspond to funerary practices documented in Ugaritic ritual texts and attested archaeologically across the Levant. The *marzihu* feast, a funerary banquet institution documented in Ugaritic administrative texts, may provide the institutional context for the mourning described.

The cursing of the three cities near Aqhat's death site has prompted attempts at geographic identification, though no scholarly consensus has been reached. The eagle imagery connects to the well-documented presence of griffon vultures and other raptors in the Levantine landscape — birds whose association with death and carrion-eating makes them natural literary symbols for the aftermath of violent death.
