The bow at the center of the narrative connects to Late Bronze Age weapons technology. Composite bows — constructed from horn, wood, and sinew — were among the most sophisticated military technologies of the period. Archaeological evidence of bow components has been found at sites across the Levant, and representations of archers with composite bows appear in Egyptian and Mesopotamian art of the period.

The eagle that serves as Yatpan's disguise connects to the significance of raptors in Ugaritic and broader Levantine culture. Eagle bones and depictions appear in cultic contexts, and the association between eagles and divine power is well attested in ancient Near Eastern iconography.
