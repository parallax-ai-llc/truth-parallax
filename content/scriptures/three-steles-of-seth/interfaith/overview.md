## Overview

The Three Steles of Seth demonstrates a truth recognized across all contemplative traditions: that praise is not merely expression but transformation. To praise the divine is to participate in the divine; to direct one's attention upward through praise is to be drawn upward into the reality one addresses. This understanding of praise as ascent is shared by the Sufi who recites the divine names, the Hindu devotee who chants the Thousand Names of Vishnu, the Buddhist who recites the Nembutsu, and the Christian monk who prays the Psalms. The Three Steles embodies this universal contemplative insight in a specifically Sethian form.
