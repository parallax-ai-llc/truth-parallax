## Overview

The Three Steles of Seth provides the clearest evidence of Sethian liturgical practice in the Nag Hammadi library. The explicit instructions for communal recitation — including directions about how to chant the hymns and what postures to adopt — allow scholars to reconstruct elements of Sethian communal worship. John Turner has argued that the three steles correspond to the three levels of the Sethian metaphysical hierarchy (Autogenes, Barbelo, Invisible Spirit) and that the hymnic ascent mirrors the philosophical ascent described in Allogenes and Zostrianos.
