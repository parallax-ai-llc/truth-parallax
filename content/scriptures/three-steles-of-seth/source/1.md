---
title: "Three Steles of Seth - Chapter 1"
scripture: "three-steles-of-seth"
chapter: 1
---

**1** The revelation of Dositheos about the three steles of Seth, the father of the living and unshakable race, which he saw and understood. And after he had read them, he remembered them.

**2** And he gave them to the elect, just as they were inscribed there.

**3** Many times I joined in giving glory with the powers, and I became worthy of the immeasurable majesties.

**4** The first stele of Seth: I bless thee, Father Geradamas, I, thine own son Emmacha Seth, whom thou didst beget without begetting, as a blessing of our God.

**5** For I am thine own son, and thou art my mind, O my Father. And I, I have sown and begotten; but thou hast seen the majesties.

**6** Thou hast stood as one who is imperishable. I bless thee, Father. Bless me, Father.

**7** It is because of thee that I exist; it is because of God that thou dost exist. Because of thee I exist in the presence of God. Thou art light, seeing light.

**8** Thou hast revealed lights. Thou art Mirotheas; thou art my Mirothoe. I bless thee as God.

**9** I bless thy divinity. Great is the good self-begotten one that stood, the God who had already stood.

**10** Thou didst come in goodness; thou hast appeared, and thou hast revealed goodness.

**11** I shall utter thy name, for thou art a first name. Thou art unbegotten. Thou hast appeared in order that thou mightest reveal the eternal ones.

**12** Thou art he who is. Therefore thou hast revealed the really truly existent ones. Thou art he who is uttered by a voice, but by mind art thou glorified.

**13** Thou art he who has dominion everywhere. Therefore the perceptible world also knows thee because of thee and thy seed.

**14** Thou art merciful. And thou art from another people, and the other people is over another people.

**15** And now thou art from another people, and the other people is over another people. Thou art merciful, for thou art eternal.

**16** And thy people dwell above every people and race. I bless thee, for I am able. I am he who is good of thy beholding.
