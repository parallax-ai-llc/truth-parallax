## Overview

The Three Steles of Seth (NHC VII,5) is a Sethian liturgical text presenting three hymns of praise (steles) attributed to Seth and transmitted by Dositheos. Each stele is addressed to a member of the Sethian divine triad — Autogenes (the Self-Generated), Barbelo (the first emanation), and the Invisible Spirit (the highest God) — and each represents a progressively higher stage in a contemplative ascent. The three steles were to be used liturgically: the text concludes with instructions for communal recitation, including directions for how the hymns should be chanted and the postures to be adopted. This makes the Three Steles one of the few Nag Hammadi texts that provides explicit evidence of Sethian communal worship and contemplative practice. The text is generally dated to the late second or third century CE.

## Structure

| Stele | Title | Content |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Praise to Autogenes | Hymn addressed to the Self-Generated One (Autogenes), celebrating the generation of the divine realm and the salvation of Seth's seed |
| 2 | Praise to Barbelo | Hymn addressed to Barbelo, the first emanation of the Invisible Spirit, celebrating her role as mother of the aeons and source of life |
| 3 | Praise to the Invisible Spirit | Hymn addressed to the supreme God, the Invisible Spirit who is beyond all predication and known only through silence |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: A primary witness to Sethian liturgical practice and contemplative ascent through doxological prayer
- **Academic**: Critical for understanding Sethian communal worship, the relationship between theology and liturgy, and the influence of Platonic ascent philosophy
- **Comparative**: Offers parallels to contemplative ascent practices in Sufism, Kabbalah, Hindu bhakti, and Christian mysticism
- **Christian**: Illuminates the broader context of early Christian hymnody and contemplative prayer
- **Jewish**: Parallels the Hekhalot hymns sung by the merkabah mystic at each stage of heavenly ascent
- **Interfaith**: Demonstrates the universal practice of prayer and praise as a means of ascending toward the divine

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Liturgical Ascent | The three steles map a progressive contemplative ascent from the Self-Generated to the Unknowable One |
| Doxological Prayer | Praise is not merely expression but a vehicle of transformation — the worshipper becomes what they praise |
| Sethian Triad | Autogenes, Barbelo, and the Invisible Spirit constitute the three levels of divine reality |
| Communal Worship | The text provides instructions for collective recitation, implying a worshipping community |
