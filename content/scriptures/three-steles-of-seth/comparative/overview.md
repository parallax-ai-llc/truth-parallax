## Overview

The Three Steles of Seth's progressive ascent through hymnic praise parallels contemplative prayer traditions worldwide. The Sufi practice of dhikr (remembrance of God through repeated invocation) as a means of ascending through spiritual states, the Hindu bhakti tradition of devotional chanting as a path to union, the Buddhist practice of mantra recitation as a vehicle of meditation, and the Christian monastic tradition of the Divine Office (chanting the Psalms as a form of continuous prayer) all share the conviction that structured praise can be a vehicle of spiritual ascent.
