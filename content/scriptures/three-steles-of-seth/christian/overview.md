## Overview

For Christian readers, the Three Steles of Seth illuminates the broader context of early Christian hymnody and contemplative prayer. The early church practiced psalmody, hymn-singing, and liturgical prayer as forms of worship and spiritual ascent (Colossians 3:16, Ephesians 5:19). The Sethian pattern of ascending praise — from lower to higher levels of the divine — parallels the Christian monastic tradition of the Divine Office and the Ignatian Exercises' movement from meditation to contemplation to union.
