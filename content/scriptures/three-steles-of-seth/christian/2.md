## Second Stele: Praise to Barbelo — Christian Engagement

For Christian readers, this stele of Three Steles of Seth offers both challenge and opportunity for theological reflection. For Christian readers, the Three Steles of Seth illuminates the broader context of early Christian hymnody and contemplative prayer.

The section "Second Stele: Praise to Barbelo" engages with themes that remain central to Christian theology, though it develops them in directions that diverge from creedal orthodoxy. The divergence itself is instructive: by encountering a coherent alternative theology that works with much of the same scriptural and theological raw material, Christian readers can deepen their understanding of their own tradition's distinctive commitments.

The patristic response to Gnostic teachings — from Irenaeus's systematic refutation to Clement of Alexandria's more nuanced engagement — demonstrates that the early church took the Gnostic challenge with profound seriousness.
