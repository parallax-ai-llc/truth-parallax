## Overview

Jewish readers find in the Three Steles of Seth a striking parallel to the Hekhalot literature's descriptions of hymns sung by the merkabah mystic at each stage of heavenly ascent. The Hekhalot texts describe the mystic passing through seven heavenly palaces, singing praises at each level to the angelic beings and ultimately to God. The Three Steles' ascending pattern of praise — from Autogenes through Barbelo to the Invisible Spirit — mirrors this structure, suggesting a possible genetic or analogical relationship between Sethian and Jewish merkabah liturgical traditions.
