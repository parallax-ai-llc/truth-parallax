## First Stele: Praise to Autogenes — Jewish Perspectives

Jewish readers encounter in this stele of Three Steles of Seth evidence of how Jewish traditions were appropriated and transformed in the Gnostic context. Jewish readers find in the Three Steles of Seth a striking parallel to the Hekhalot literature's descriptions of hymns sung by the merkabah mystic at each stage of heavenly ascent.

The section "First Stele: Praise to Autogenes" draws on Jewish sources — Genesis narratives, prophetic imagery, wisdom traditions, and possibly early mystical speculation — but deploys them within a framework that diverges from rabbinic Judaism. This transformation is not simple distortion but a creative engagement with traditions that remained fluid and contested in the second and third centuries.

Reading this passage against rabbinic parallels illuminates both shared concerns and fundamental divergences — revealing what was at stake theologically when Jewish concepts entered Gnostic systems.
