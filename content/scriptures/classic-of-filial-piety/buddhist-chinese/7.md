The trinity chapter's claim that filial piety connects heaven, earth, and humanity invites Buddhist comparison with the doctrine of dependent origination (pratityasamutpada). While the Confucian framework posits a moral connection between the cosmic and human realms, Buddhism teaches that all phenomena are interconnected through the web of causes and conditions. Chinese Buddhist commentators acknowledged the Confucian insight while arguing that the Buddhist understanding of interdependence is more comprehensive, encompassing all beings rather than focusing exclusively on the parent-child relationship.

This chapter illustrates both convergence and divergence in the two traditions' cosmological thinking.
