Chinese Buddhist readers engage with the five punishments chapter by drawing parallels with Buddhist karma theory. While the Confucian system relies on external punishment for unfilial conduct, Buddhism teaches that unfilial behavior naturally produces negative karmic consequences across multiple lifetimes. The Buddhist understanding of moral causation thus reinforces the Confucian concern with filial behavior while grounding it in a universal metaphysical framework rather than social convention.

This chapter illustrates the Buddhist strategy of supporting Confucian moral conclusions while providing different justifications.
