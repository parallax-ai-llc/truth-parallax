Chinese Buddhist engagement with the Son of Heaven's chapter focuses on the compatibility of Buddhist and Confucian models of enlightened rulership. Buddhist thinkers such as Huiyuan argued that the ideal ruler governs not only by Confucian moral example but also by supporting the sangha and the dharma, creating conditions for spiritual liberation alongside social harmony. The Buddhist chakravartin (wheel-turning king) model was presented as complementary to the Confucian ideal.

The chapter's teaching on the ruler's transformative influence finds a Buddhist parallel in the bodhisattva's skillful means (upaya), which adapt the teaching to the needs of different beings.
