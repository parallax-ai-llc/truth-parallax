Chinese Buddhist interpretation of the remonstrance chapter finds an important parallel in the Buddhist concept of right speech (sammavaca). Just as the Confucian tradition teaches that the filial son must speak truth to a parent who errs, Buddhism teaches that compassionate speech sometimes requires pointing out wrongdoing. The Buddhist approach emphasizes that remonstrance must be motivated by compassion (karuna) rather than judgment.

This chapter demonstrates shared ground between Confucian moral courage and Buddhist compassionate truthfulness.
