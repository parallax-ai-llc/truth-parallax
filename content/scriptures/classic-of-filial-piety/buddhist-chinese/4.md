Chinese Buddhist readers note the ministers' chapter's emphasis on loyalty and service while reinterpreting these values through a Buddhist lens. The Buddhist understanding of service extends beyond the human realm: the bodhisattva serves all sentient beings with the same devotion that the Confucian minister shows the ruler. This reframing allows Buddhism to honor the Confucian ideal of loyal service while transcending its social limitations.

The chapter's prescriptions for proper conduct find parallels in the Buddhist vinaya (monastic code), which similarly regulates dress, speech, and comportment as aids to spiritual cultivation.
