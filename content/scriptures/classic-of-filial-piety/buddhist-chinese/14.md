The exhortation chapter's emphasis on sustained practice resonates with the Buddhist understanding of spiritual cultivation as a gradual process. Chinese Buddhist commentators drew parallels between the Confucian discipline of daily filial practice and the Buddhist practice of maintaining precepts, performing daily devotions, and cultivating mindfulness. Both traditions recognize that moral transformation requires sustained effort over time.

The chapter provides common ground for the Chinese Buddhist argument that Confucian moral discipline prepares the practitioner for the deeper transformations of Buddhist practice.
