Chinese Buddhist engagement with the cosmic influence chapter reinterprets its claims through the doctrine of merit transfer (parinamana). While the Confucian understanding locates cosmic efficacy in filial piety specifically, Buddhism teaches that all virtuous action generates merit that can influence the spiritual realm. The Ullambana Sutra's teaching that the merit of the sangha can rescue ancestors from lower realms represents a specifically Buddhist version of the cosmic influence described in this chapter.

The chapter illustrates how Chinese Buddhism adapted Confucian concepts to serve its own soteriological purposes.
