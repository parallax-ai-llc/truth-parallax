Chinese Buddhist interpretation of the amplification chapter's moral extension parallels the Buddhist practice of expanding loving-kindness (metta) meditation from oneself to family, friends, neutral persons, enemies, and ultimately all beings. The Confucian method of extending filial love outward shares the Buddhist conviction that compassion must be cultivated through progressive stages. Chinese Buddhist commentators noted this parallel while arguing that the Buddhist practice is more comprehensive, extending to all sentient beings rather than stopping at the boundaries of human society.

The chapter reveals structural similarities between Confucian and Buddhist approaches to moral cultivation.
