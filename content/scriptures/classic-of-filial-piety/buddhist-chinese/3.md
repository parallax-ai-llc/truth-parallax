Buddhist interpretation of the feudal lords' chapter draws parallels between the text's emphasis on moderation and the Buddhist middle way. The warnings against arrogance and excess echo the Buddha's own teaching that the spiritual life avoids the extremes of self-indulgence and self-mortification. Chinese Buddhist commentators found in such passages evidence that Confucian moral wisdom, while not reaching the ultimate truth of the Buddha's teaching, shared important common ground with Buddhist ethics.

This chapter illustrates the Chinese Buddhist strategy of harmonization, which acknowledged the validity of Confucian morality as a foundation for Buddhist spiritual practice.
