Chinese Buddhist engagement with the common people's chapter finds particular resonance with the Buddhist lay ideal. The teaching that ordinary people should follow natural patterns and manage resources responsibly aligns with the Buddhist emphasis on right livelihood and moderation. Chinese Buddhist thinkers argued that lay Buddhists could fulfill both Confucian and Buddhist moral requirements simultaneously, practicing filial piety in the family while cultivating compassion and wisdom through Buddhist practice.

This chapter provided common ground for the Buddhist argument that the dharma enriches rather than undermines the moral life of ordinary Chinese families.
