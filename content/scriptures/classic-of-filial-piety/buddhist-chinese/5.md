Chinese Buddhist interpretation of the scholars' chapter highlights the tension between scholarly filial piety and monastic renunciation. Buddhist apologists argued that the monk who leaves the household life does not abandon filial devotion but transforms it. By dedicating merit from spiritual practice to one's parents and seeking their liberation from samsara, the Buddhist practitioner fulfills filial obligations at the deepest possible level.

The Ullambana (Ghost Festival) tradition, in which monastics transfer merit to deceased ancestors, represents a practical synthesis of Buddhist soteriology and Confucian filial piety.
