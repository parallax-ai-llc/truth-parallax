Chinese Buddhist reading of the governance chapter reinterprets the Confucian ideal of moral governance through the lens of the dharma-protecting king. Buddhist political theory, developed through texts such as the Sutra of the Golden Light, teaches that the righteous king governs by supporting the sangha, disseminating the dharma, and practicing the paramitas (perfections). This model incorporates filial governance while adding the specifically Buddhist dimension of spiritual leadership.

The chapter's vision of a society transformed by the ruler's virtue finds a Buddhist parallel in the pure land ideal, where the Buddha's merit creates conditions for universal liberation.
