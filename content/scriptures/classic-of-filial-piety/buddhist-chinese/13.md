Chinese Buddhist engagement with the perfect virtue chapter draws parallels between the Confucian sage's effortless virtue and the bodhisattva's spontaneous compassion. Both traditions teach that the highest moral attainment transcends deliberate effort, arising naturally from a transformed character. Chinese Buddhist commentators found in this convergence evidence that the Confucian moral ideal, while incomplete from a Buddhist perspective, points toward the same ultimate reality that Buddhist practice seeks to realize.

This chapter supports the syncretic Chinese Buddhist position that Confucian sagehood and Buddhist enlightenment, while differing in depth, share common characteristics.
