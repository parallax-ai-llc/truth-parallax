Chinese Buddhist engagement with the sages' chapter addresses the role of music and ritual in moral cultivation, drawing parallels with Buddhist liturgical traditions. Chinese Buddhism developed elaborate chanting practices, dharani recitation, and ritual ceremonies that served functions analogous to the Confucian use of music for moral transformation. Buddhist commentators argued that Buddhist liturgy not only harmonizes emotions but also purifies karma and generates merit.

The chapter's vision of the sages as moral educators resonated with the Buddhist understanding of the Buddha as the supreme teacher of gods and humans.
