Chinese Buddhist interpretation of the chapter on serving the ruler draws on the Buddhist concept of the bodhisattva-ruler who serves all beings through enlightened governance. While the Confucian understanding of loyal service focuses on the minister-ruler relationship, Buddhism expands the concept of service to encompass all sentient beings. The Buddhist official serves the ruler not merely from loyalty but as a means of benefiting the people and creating conditions for spiritual practice.

This chapter reveals the capacity of Chinese Buddhism to incorporate Confucian political values within a broader soteriological framework.
