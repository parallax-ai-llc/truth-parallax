Chinese Buddhist interpretation of the practice chapter finds in the three aspects of filial devotion — daily service, mourning, and sacrifice — parallels with Buddhist devotional practice. Daily service to parents is analogous to the bodhisattva's constant service to all beings. Mourning finds its Buddhist counterpart in merit dedication for the deceased. And ancestral sacrifice is reinterpreted through the Buddhist understanding of transferring merit across the boundary between the living and the dead.

The chapter's emphasis on sincerity underlying ritual form resonates with the Buddhist teaching that the efficacy of practice depends on the quality of one's intention (cetana).
