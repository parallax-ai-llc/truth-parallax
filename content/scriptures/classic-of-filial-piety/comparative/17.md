Comparative analysis of the chapter on serving the ruler examines the relationship between private virtue and public duty across traditions. The Platonic concept of justice as each person fulfilling their proper function, the Hindu concept of svadharma (one's own duty), and the Confucian vision of loyal service rooted in filial piety all address the integration of personal moral life with political obligation.

This chapter invites reflection on how different traditions balance the claims of family, state, and the individual conscience.
