Comparative analysis of the five punishments chapter examines the relationship between moral exhortation and legal enforcement across traditions. The biblical integration of moral law and civil law in the Torah, the Islamic sharia's comprehensive moral-legal framework, and the Hindu dharmashastra's integration of ethics and law all address the question of whether moral obligations should be legally enforced. The Confucian position, which treats unfilial conduct as the most serious criminal offense, represents a particularly strong integration of moral and legal domains.

This chapter invites reflection on the proper relationship between morality and law across cultural contexts.
