## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Classic of Filial Piety places its teachings on filial devotion in dialogue with similar themes across world religious and philosophical traditions. The biblical commandment to "honor thy father and mother," the Quranic emphasis on kindness to parents, and various indigenous traditions' reverence for elders all provide points of comparison.

## Interpretive Approach

Comparative scholars examine what is distinctive about the Confucian formulation: the systematic extension of filial piety from family to state, the integration of filial devotion with cosmic order, and the emphasis on remonstrance as a component of true filial love. These features distinguish the Xiaojing from other traditions' treatments of parent-child obligations and reveal the specifically Confucian contribution to global ethical thought.

## Global Significance

The Classic of Filial Piety offers contemporary comparative ethics a robust framework for thinking about intergenerational obligation, the relationship between family and state, and the moral foundations of care ethics. Its vision of filial piety as encompassing not just obedience but active moral engagement challenges individualist assumptions common in Western ethical thought.
