Comparative study of the trinity chapter examines the Confucian vision of cosmic moral order alongside similar concepts in other traditions. The Hindu concept of rita (cosmic order), the Egyptian concept of ma'at, the Stoic notion of natural law, and the biblical understanding of creation as moral order all assert a connection between human ethics and cosmic structure. The Confucian contribution is to identify filial piety specifically as the principle linking human and cosmic orders.

This chapter illuminates the widespread human impulse to locate moral values within the structure of reality itself.
