Comparative study of the Son of Heaven's chapter examines the relationship between ruler and parent across traditions. The ancient Egyptian concept of pharaoh as father of the people, the Hindu ideal of the dharmaraja (righteous king), and the Roman notion of the emperor as pater patriae all draw parallels between political authority and parental care. The Confucian formulation is distinctive in making the ruler's actual filial piety, rather than merely his paternal role, the foundation of political legitimacy.

This chapter illuminates cross-cultural patterns in the sacralization of political authority through family metaphors.
