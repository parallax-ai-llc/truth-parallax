Comparative interpretation of the feudal lords' chapter places its prescriptions for noble conduct alongside similar teachings in other traditions. Aristotle's doctrine of the mean, the Buddhist middle way, and the biblical wisdom literature's warnings against pride all counsel moderation in those who hold power. The Confucian emphasis on differentiating filial duties by social rank finds no exact parallel elsewhere but invites comparison with the Hindu varnashrama system, which similarly assigns different duties to different social positions.

This chapter raises comparative questions about the relationship between social hierarchy and moral obligation.
