Comparative interpretation of the perfect virtue chapter examines ideals of moral perfection across traditions. The Aristotelian phronimos, the Daoist sage, the Buddhist arahant, and the Christian saint all represent culturally specific models of completed moral development. The Confucian ideal of effortless virtue through filial perfection is distinctive in grounding moral excellence in the domestic sphere rather than in ascetic withdrawal, mystical experience, or philosophical contemplation.

This chapter reveals the diversity of human understandings of what constitutes the highest moral achievement.
