Comparative interpretation of the common people's chapter explores how different traditions moralize the labor of ordinary people. The biblical concept of work as both divine mandate and consequence of the Fall, the Buddhist emphasis on right livelihood, and the Islamic concept of work as worship (ibada) all invest everyday labor with moral significance. The Confucian formulation is distinctive in linking agricultural labor directly to filial devotion, making the act of feeding one's parents the primary moral meaning of work.

This chapter raises comparative questions about the dignity of labor and the relationship between economic activity and moral life.
