Comparative interpretation of the cosmic influence chapter examines beliefs about the moral efficacy of human action across traditions. The Hindu concept of punya (merit), the Buddhist doctrine of karma, the Christian understanding of grace, and the Islamic concept of barakah (blessing) all assert connections between moral action and cosmic consequences. The Confucian teaching that filial piety moves heaven and earth represents a specific instance of this widespread belief in the cosmic significance of moral conduct.

This chapter reveals the nearly universal human conviction that moral action has consequences beyond the immediately visible.
