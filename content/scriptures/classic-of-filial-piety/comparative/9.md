Comparative interpretation of the sages' chapter examines the role of music and ritual in moral education across traditions. The Platonic discussion of music's moral effects in the Republic, the Psalms' integration of worship and moral formation, and the Hindu understanding of raga as spiritual practice all assign moral and spiritual significance to music. The Confucian vision of sage-kings who use music and ritual to harmonize society reflects a broader cross-cultural recognition of the arts as instruments of moral formation.

This chapter illuminates shared human insights about the relationship between aesthetic experience and ethical development.
