Neo-Confucian interpretation of the governance chapter emphasizes the concept of transformative moral influence (jiaohua). The ruler's filial piety does not merely set a good example but actually transforms the moral qi of the realm. Zhu Xi's commentary draws parallels between the ruler's filial governance and the sage's capacity to bring the cosmic order into proper alignment through personal cultivation.

This chapter illustrates the Neo-Confucian conviction that political reform must begin with the ruler's inner moral transformation.
