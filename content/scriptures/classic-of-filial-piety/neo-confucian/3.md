Neo-Confucian interpretation of the feudal lords' chapter emphasizes the metaphysical grounding of moderation. The text's warnings against arrogance and extravagance are read as prescriptions for maintaining the proper balance of principle and vital energy. When principle governs, conduct is naturally moderate; when desire predominates, excess follows. The Neo-Confucian reading thus transforms social advice into metaphysical instruction.

Zhu Xi notes that the feudal lords' position requires particular vigilance because their power and wealth constantly tempt them to deviate from principle. Filial piety serves as the anchor that keeps them aligned with the Way.
