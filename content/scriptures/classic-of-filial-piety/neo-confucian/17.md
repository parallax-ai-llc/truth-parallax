Neo-Confucian readers interpret the chapter on serving the ruler through the concept of the unity of loyalty (zhong) and filial piety (xiao). Since both express the same heavenly principle in different relational contexts, the transition from filial devotion to political loyalty is not a transfer of allegiance but a widening of the same moral commitment.

The chapter supports the Neo-Confucian ideal of the scholar-official whose public service is continuous with his private virtue.
