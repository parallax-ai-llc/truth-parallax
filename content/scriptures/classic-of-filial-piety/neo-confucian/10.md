Neo-Confucian commentary on the practice chapter emphasizes the three aspects of filial devotion — daily service, mourning, and sacrifice — as progressive stages of moral cultivation. Each stage demands deeper sincerity and more complete alignment of inner feeling with outer form. Zhu Xi interprets the requirement of genuine feeling as reflecting the Neo-Confucian doctrine that moral effort must engage both the heart-mind (xin) and bodily practice.

The chapter's insistence on authentic feeling underlying ritual form addresses the central Neo-Confucian concern with making reverence (jing) the foundation of all practice.
