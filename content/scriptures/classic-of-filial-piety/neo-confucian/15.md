The remonstrance chapter is of particular importance for Neo-Confucian interpreters because it demonstrates the priority of principle over convention. When a parent or ruler acts contrary to principle, the duty to remonstrate takes precedence over the duty to obey. Zhu Xi reads this chapter as confirming that filial piety is ultimately loyalty to principle rather than loyalty to persons.

This interpretation is crucial for Neo-Confucian political theory, which grounds the legitimacy of political authority in moral principle rather than hereditary succession.
