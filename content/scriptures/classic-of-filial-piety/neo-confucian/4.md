The Neo-Confucian reading of the ministers' chapter connects the parallel between serving parents and serving the ruler to the doctrine of the investigation of things (gewu). By carefully examining the principle inherent in the filial relationship, the minister discovers the same principle operating in the political relationship. This process of moral reflection transforms social convention into metaphysical insight.

Neo-Confucian commentators emphasize that the minister's proper dress and speech are not mere formalities but external expressions of inner alignment with heavenly principle.
