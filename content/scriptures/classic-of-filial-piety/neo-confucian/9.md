Neo-Confucian readers find in the sages' government chapter confirmation of their understanding of music and ritual as expressions of heavenly principle. The sages' use of music to harmonize emotions reflects the Neo-Confucian theory that proper music aligns the qi of the listener with cosmic principle, while ritual orders the relationships that constitute the social expression of that principle.

The chapter's integration of aesthetic and moral culture supports the Neo-Confucian vision of civilization as the full expression of li in human social life.
