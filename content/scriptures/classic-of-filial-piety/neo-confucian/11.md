Neo-Confucian interpretation of the five punishments chapter locates the severity of unfilial conduct in its violation of heavenly principle. Since filial piety expresses the most fundamental pattern of cosmic order, its transgression represents the deepest possible departure from the Way. Zhu Xi's commentary emphasizes that the punishment for unfilial behavior reflects not arbitrary social convention but the natural consequences of acting against the grain of reality.

This chapter supports the Neo-Confucian argument that moral law and natural law are ultimately identical.
