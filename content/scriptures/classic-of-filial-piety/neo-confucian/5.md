Neo-Confucian interpretation of the scholars' filial piety chapter integrates the text's teachings with the broader educational program of the Four Books. The scholar who transfers filial love to political service demonstrates the Neo-Confucian principle that all genuine knowledge leads to action. Wang Yangming's unity of knowledge and action (zhixing heyi) finds support in this chapter's teaching that understanding filial piety and practicing it are inseparable.

The chapter's vision of scholarly filial piety as encompassing both family devotion and public service reflects the Neo-Confucian ideal of the scholar-official who unites moral cultivation with political responsibility.
