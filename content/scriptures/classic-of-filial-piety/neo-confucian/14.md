Neo-Confucian interpretation of the exhortation chapter emphasizes that practice is the necessary complement to understanding. Zhu Xi's doctrine of the investigation of things requires that theoretical knowledge be completed by practical engagement. The chapter's detailed prescriptions for daily filial conduct provide concrete occasions for the practice of reverence (jing) that Neo-Confucians regard as the foundation of all moral development.

The emphasis on habitual practice reflects the Neo-Confucian understanding that virtue is formed through the sustained effort of aligning one's qi with heavenly principle.
