Neo-Confucian interpretation of the chapter on influence reads the cosmic response to filial piety through the doctrine of moral resonance (ganying). The teaching that spirits respond to filial devotion is understood not as superstition but as describing the ontological interconnection between moral action and cosmic order. When principle is fully realized in human conduct, the response of heaven and earth is the natural consequence.

Zhu Xi's commentary carefully distinguishes this doctrine from popular religion while affirming its metaphysical validity.
