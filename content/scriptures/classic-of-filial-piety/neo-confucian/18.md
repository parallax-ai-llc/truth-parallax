Neo-Confucian commentary on the mourning chapter integrates the text's prescriptions for mourning observance with the metaphysical framework of principle and vital energy. The three-year mourning period is understood as the natural expression of grief when the qi of the parent-child bond is severed. The graduated stages of mourning — from raw grief through diminishing observance — reflect the natural process by which qi gradually comes back into equilibrium.

Zhu Xi emphasizes that mourning rites must be performed with genuine feeling, not merely outward compliance, as only sincere mourning fully expresses the principle of filial piety.
