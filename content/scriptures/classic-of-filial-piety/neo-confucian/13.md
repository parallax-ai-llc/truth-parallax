Neo-Confucian commentary reads the amplification of perfect virtue through the lens of quiet sitting (jingzuo) and moral introspection. The perfect virtue described in this chapter is not an extraordinary achievement but the natural result of sustained attention to the principle of filial piety as it manifests in everyday life. Wang Yangming would argue that this perfect virtue is already present in the innate moral knowledge of every human being, needing only to be uncovered rather than constructed.

The chapter reflects the Neo-Confucian emphasis on gradual cultivation as the path to moral perfection.
