Neo-Confucian readers interpret the amplification of the essential Way through the concept of moral extension (tui). The process of extending reverence for one's father to respect for others, and filial love to broader social benevolence, illustrates the Neo-Confucian method of expanding the circle of moral concern by recognizing the same principle operating in ever wider contexts.

Zhu Xi's commentary connects this chapter to the Great Learning's program of self-cultivation extending to the ordering of the family, governing the state, and bringing peace to the world.
