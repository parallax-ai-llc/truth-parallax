Neo-Confucian readers interpret the common people's filial piety through the lens of the principle that heavenly li is equally present in all human beings regardless of social position. The teaching that commoners must follow the patterns of heaven and earth is read as affirming that even the simplest agricultural labor participates in the cosmic order when performed with proper moral awareness.

This chapter demonstrates the Neo-Confucian conviction that moral cultivation is not reserved for the educated elite but is accessible to all through the universal experience of filial love.
