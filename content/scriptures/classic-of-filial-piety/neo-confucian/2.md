Neo-Confucian readers interpret the Son of Heaven's filial piety through the concept of the ruler as the embodiment of heavenly principle in the political order. The emperor's moral cultivation is not merely personal but cosmological: when the ruler's inner principle is properly aligned, the qi of the empire flows harmoniously. Zhu Xi's commentary emphasizes that the Son of Heaven's filial piety must be understood as the supreme expression of ren (humaneness), extending from the family to encompass all under heaven.

This chapter illustrates the Neo-Confucian conviction that political authority is legitimate only when grounded in moral self-cultivation.
