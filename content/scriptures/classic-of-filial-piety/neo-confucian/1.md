Neo-Confucian interpretation reads the opening chapter's declaration that filial piety is the root of virtue through the metaphysical framework of principle (li) and vital energy (qi). Zhu Xi understands xiao as the expression of heavenly principle in the most intimate human relationship. The statement that the body is received from one's parents is read not merely as a biological fact but as reflecting the Neo-Confucian understanding that human nature (xing) is the manifestation of cosmic principle in individual form.

Wang Yangming's school emphasizes the innate knowledge (liangzhi) of filial love, arguing that the infant's natural attachment to its parents demonstrates that moral knowledge is not acquired but inborn. The Xiaojing's opening chapter thus provides evidence for the Neo-Confucian doctrine of original goodness.
