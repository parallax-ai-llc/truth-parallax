Scholarly study of the exhortation chapter examines the text's pedagogical strategy of combining philosophical argument with practical prescription. Academic analysis reveals the rhetorical structure of the Xiaojing, which alternates between establishing theoretical principles and providing concrete guidance for daily conduct. This combination of theory and practice reflects the Confucian educational philosophy that moral knowledge is inseparable from moral action.

The chapter's pedagogical approach has been compared with similar strategies in other educational traditions, including Aristotelian, Buddhist, and Stoic moral education.
