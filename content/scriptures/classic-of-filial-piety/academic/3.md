Academic treatment of the feudal lords' chapter examines its social context within the early imperial transition from feudal to bureaucratic government. Scholars note that the chapter's prescriptions may reflect idealized memories of Zhou dynasty aristocratic culture rather than contemporary Han practice. The emphasis on moderation and avoidance of arrogance served as a critique of real-world aristocratic behavior, suggesting that the text had a reformist as well as a legitimating function.

Comparative studies place these prescriptions alongside similar moral advice for rulers in Egyptian, Mesopotamian, and Greek wisdom literature.
