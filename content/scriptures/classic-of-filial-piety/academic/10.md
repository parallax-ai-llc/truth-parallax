Academic analysis of the practice chapter examines the three aspects of filial devotion — daily service, mourning, and sacrifice — as social institutions with specific historical contexts. Scholars note that the prescriptions for mourning and sacrifice reflect elite practices that varied significantly across regions and periods. The standardization of these practices through the Xiaojing represents a process of cultural unification that served the interests of centralized imperial governance.

Anthropological studies compare these filial practices with ancestor veneration traditions in other cultures, revealing both universal patterns and culturally specific formations.
