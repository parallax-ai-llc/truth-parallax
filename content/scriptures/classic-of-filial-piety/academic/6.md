Academic analysis of the common people's chapter examines the extension of filial piety ideology to the agrarian majority. Scholars observe that the chapter's integration of agricultural labor with filial duty served to moralize the peasant economy, presenting subsistence farming as a form of cosmic participation. This ideological move had practical consequences: by framing agricultural work as filial duty, the text discouraged social mobility and reinforced the existing economic order.

Comparative studies note similar strategies in other agrarian civilizations, where ruling ideologies moralized manual labor to maintain social stability.
