Academic analysis of the perfect virtue chapter examines the Confucian ideal of effortless virtue and its relationship to concepts of moral perfection in other traditions. Scholars note that the description of virtue that transforms without conscious effort bears structural similarities to the Daoist concept of wu wei and to Aristotelian accounts of the fully virtuous person for whom right action has become second nature.

The chapter's portrait of perfect virtue has been studied by moral psychologists and virtue ethicists as an example of how traditions conceptualize the end point of moral development.
