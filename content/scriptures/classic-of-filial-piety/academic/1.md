Academic analysis of the opening chapter examines its claim that filial piety is the foundation of all virtue in light of the text's probable composition during the early Han dynasty (3rd-2nd century BCE). Scholars note that the attribution of this teaching to Confucius himself likely reflects the Confucian movement's strategy of investing its core values with the authority of the sage. The dialogue between Confucius and Zengzi — a disciple particularly associated with filial piety — serves as a literary device for presenting Han-era Confucian ideology as ancient wisdom.

Textual critics observe differences between the jinwen (modern script) and guwen (old script) recensions in the treatment of this opening chapter, suggesting editorial revision over time.
