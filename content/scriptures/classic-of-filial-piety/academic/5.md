Scholarly study of the scholars' chapter examines the social construction of the shi (scholar) class and the role of filial piety ideology in defining their identity. Academic historians note that the chapter's teaching on transferring filial devotion to political loyalty served the interests of the centralized state by ensuring that educated officials would channel their moral energies into government service rather than independent criticism.

The chapter reflects the tension within Confucian thought between the scholar as moral exemplar and the scholar as loyal servant, a tension that would generate significant political controversy throughout Chinese history.
