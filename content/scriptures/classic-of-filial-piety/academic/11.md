Scholarly analysis of the five punishments chapter examines the relationship between moral exhortation and legal enforcement in early Chinese governance. The claim that unfilial conduct is the most serious of all offenses reflects the legal codification of Confucian values, a process that began in the Han dynasty and continued through subsequent dynasties. Academic legal historians have documented cases of prosecutions for unfilial behavior, revealing how the Xiaojing's moral teachings were translated into criminal law.

The chapter raises important questions about the relationship between moral education and legal coercion in Confucian political thought.
