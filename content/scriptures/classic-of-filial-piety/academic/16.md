Academic study of the cosmic influence chapter examines its claims about the response of spirits to filial devotion within the framework of Han correlative cosmology. Scholars analyze the concept of ganying (stimulus-response) as a comprehensive theory of cosmic interconnection that unified moral, political, and natural phenomena. The chapter's teachings reflect the intellectual context of the early Han, when Dong Zhongshu and other thinkers developed systematic correlative cosmologies.

Recent scholarship has reassessed these cosmological claims not as superstition but as sophisticated attempts to theorize the relationship between human action and natural order.
