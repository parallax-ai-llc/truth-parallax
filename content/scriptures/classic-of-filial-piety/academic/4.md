Academic analysis of the ministers' chapter examines the text's role in defining the ideal of the Confucian official. Scholars observe that the parallels drawn between family devotion and political loyalty served to naturalize hierarchical relationships by grounding them in the parent-child bond. This strategy, which Keith Knapp terms "the politics of filial piety," made political obedience appear as natural and inevitable as filial love.

Historical studies trace the practical impact of these teachings on bureaucratic culture, showing how filial piety rhetoric shaped expectations for official conduct across Chinese dynastic history.
