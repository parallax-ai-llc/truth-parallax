Academic study of the mourning chapter examines the three-year mourning period as a social institution with specific historical origins and functions. Scholars such as James Watson and Evelyn Rawski have studied mourning practices in China from anthropological and historical perspectives, showing how the idealized prescriptions of the Xiaojing related to actual practice across different periods, regions, and social classes.

The chapter's treatment of mourning raises important questions about the relationship between ritual prescription and lived experience, and about the role of funerary practices in maintaining social cohesion and political order.
