Academic scholarship on the trinity chapter examines its cosmological claims within the broader context of Han correlative cosmology. The teaching that filial piety connects heaven, earth, and humanity reflects the Han synthesis of Confucian ethics with cosmological speculation, creating a unified system in which moral, political, and natural orders mirror each other. Scholars such as Sarah Queen and Michael Puett have analyzed how this cosmological framework served to legitimate political authority and social hierarchy.

The chapter represents the high point of the text's ideological ambitions, claiming not merely moral but ontological status for filial piety.
