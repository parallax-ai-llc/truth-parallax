Academic analysis of the remonstrance chapter examines its significance for Confucian political theory. The teaching that filial sons must remonstrate with erring parents, and loyal ministers with erring rulers, represents a crucial qualification of the apparent demand for unconditional obedience. Scholars have traced the long history of remonstrance in Chinese political culture, from the advice literature of the Zhou dynasty through the memorial system of the imperial period.

The chapter has received particular attention from scholars interested in the potential for critical political thought within the Confucian tradition.
