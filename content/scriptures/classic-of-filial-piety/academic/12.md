Academic treatment of the amplification chapter examines the logical structure of the Confucian argument for extending filial piety to the political realm. Scholars have debated whether this extension is justified or represents a fallacy of misplaced analogy. Critics such as Mozi challenged the Confucian strategy of grounding universal moral obligations in particular family relationships, arguing that impartial concern (jian'ai) provides a more coherent ethical foundation.

The chapter's argumentative strategy has been compared with Aristotelian arguments about the naturalness of the political community and with social contract theories in Western political philosophy.
