## Overview

The Classic of Filial Piety (Xiaojing) is one of the thirteen classics of Confucian literature, presenting a dialogue between Confucius and his disciple Zengzi on the nature and practice of filial piety (xiao). Traditionally attributed to Confucius himself or compiled by Zengzi, the text elevates filial piety from a simple family virtue to the foundational principle of all ethics, governance, and cosmic harmony. Its 18 chapters systematically unfold the meaning of filial devotion across every level of society, from the Son of Heaven to the common people.

## Structure

The 18 chapters progress from general principles to specific applications:

| Chapters | Theme |
|----------|-------|
| 1-3 | The scope and universal importance of filial piety |
| 4-7 | Filial piety differentiated by social rank |
| 8-9 | The governance of filial piety and the role of sages |
| 10-12 | Proper conduct, the five punishments, and amplifying virtue |
| 13-15 | Broad application, remonstrance, and the response of nature |
| 16-18 | Influence, mourning, and serving one's ruler |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

The Classic of Filial Piety has exercised enormous influence across East Asian civilization:

- **Confucian**: The primary textual authority on filial piety as the root of all virtue
- **Neo-Confucian**: Integrated into the metaphysical framework of principle (li) and human nature
- **Daoist**: Engaged critically regarding the tension between natural affection and prescribed duty
- **Buddhist-Chinese**: A focal point in debates about monastic renunciation and family obligations
- **Academic**: Studied for its role in shaping family ethics and political ideology across East Asia

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Xiao (孝) | Filial piety as the root of all virtue and the foundation of governance |
| Hierarchy | Differentiated duties of filial piety according to social position |
| Remonstrance | The duty to correct a parent or ruler who errs |
| Cosmic Harmony | Filial piety as the principle that unites heaven, earth, and humanity |
| Governance | The ruler's filial devotion as the model for the entire kingdom |
| Mourning | Proper observance of mourning rites as the culmination of filial duty |
