The chapter on exhortation to practice emphasizes that filial piety must be actively cultivated rather than merely understood intellectually. Confucian readers note the text's insistence on daily practice: serving parents with reverence at meals, supporting them in illness, expressing grief at their death, and making offerings at their grave. These concrete prescriptions reflect the Confucian conviction that virtue is formed through habitual action.

The exhortation reminds readers that filial piety is not a theoretical principle but a lived practice requiring sustained effort and attention.
