Confucian commentary on serving the ruler emphasizes the parallel between filial devotion and loyal service. The text teaches that the person who truly loves his parents will not dare to hate others, and the person who truly reveres his parents will not dare to despise others. This extension of filial sentiment to all relationships, and especially to the ruler-minister bond, provides the Confucian justification for political loyalty grounded in moral principle.

The chapter's vision of the ideal minister as one who serves the ruler with the same devotion he shows his parents makes political service an extension of familial love.
