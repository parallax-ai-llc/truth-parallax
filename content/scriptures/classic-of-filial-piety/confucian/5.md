The scholars' filial piety chapter applies the principle of filial devotion to the class of educated officials who serve both family and state. Confucian interpretation emphasizes that scholarly filial piety requires transferring the service given to a father to the service of one's ruler and the service given to a mother to the service of one's ruler's wife. This transference demonstrates that filial piety is not limited to biological relationships but extends to all relationships characterized by authority and care.

The chapter's teaching that the scholar who serves his ruler with filial loyalty will be ennobled and his parents honored reflects the Confucian ideal of harmonizing personal devotion with public achievement.
