Confucian interpretation of the chapter on the five punishments treats it as a warning about the consequences of unfilial conduct. The text teaches that among the three thousand offenses covered by the five categories of punishment, none is greater than being unfilial. This extreme formulation reflects the Confucian conviction that filial piety is the foundation of all moral order: to violate it is to undermine the very basis of human civilization.

The chapter's severity serves a pedagogical purpose, impressing upon readers the absolute centrality of filial devotion in the Confucian moral hierarchy.
