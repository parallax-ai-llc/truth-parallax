Confucian commentary on the ministers' and officers' filial piety stresses the connection between serving one's parents and serving one's ruler. The text teaches that the same virtues of reverence, loyalty, and attentiveness that characterize filial devotion also define exemplary public service. For Confucian readers, this parallel demonstrates that family and state are not separate spheres but concentric circles of the same moral order.

The requirement that ministers maintain proper dress, speech, and conduct reflects the Confucian conviction that outward forms both express and cultivate inner dispositions. Ritual propriety in public life is an extension of the reverence first learned at the family altar.
