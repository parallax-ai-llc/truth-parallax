The government of the sages chapter extends the scope of filial piety to encompass music, rites, and the entire civilizing project. Confucian interpreters see here the integration of aesthetic and moral culture: the sages used music to harmonize the people's emotions and rites to order their social relationships, both rooted in the filial sentiment of reverence and gratitude.

This chapter demonstrates the Confucian understanding that filial piety is not merely a domestic virtue but the generative principle of civilization itself. The sages' achievement was to channel the natural love between parent and child into the creation of a harmonious social order.
