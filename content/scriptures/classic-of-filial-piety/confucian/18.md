The final chapter on mourning for parents describes the proper observance of the three-year mourning period, including fasting, wailing, and the graduated stages of mourning dress. Confucian interpreters regard this chapter as the culmination of the text, showing that filial piety extends beyond death and encompasses the rituals that maintain the bond between the living and the dead.

The three-year mourning period mirrors the three years of helpless infancy during which parents devoted themselves to their child's care, creating a symmetry of devotion that spans the entire arc of the parent-child relationship.
