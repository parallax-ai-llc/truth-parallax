This chapter teaches that filial piety moves the spirits of heaven and earth, bringing blessings and averting disasters. Confucian interpreters understand this as describing the mysterious efficacy (shen) of sincere moral practice. The filial person's devotion creates a resonance (ganying) with the cosmic order, drawing forth a response from the spiritual realm.

The teaching reflects the Confucian worldview in which moral action and natural phenomena are not separate domains but aspects of a single interconnected reality.
