Confucian readers find in this chapter the most universal expression of filial piety, applied to the common people who constitute the vast majority of society. The teaching that even ordinary people must follow the patterns of heaven and earth, using the land's resources carefully and moderating expenditures, grounds everyday labor in cosmic principle.

The insistence that filial piety for commoners means attending to agricultural seasons and managing household resources responsibly reflects the Confucian conviction that moral life encompasses the most mundane activities. Even the act of tilling the soil becomes an expression of filial devotion when performed with the awareness that one labors to nourish and support one's parents.
