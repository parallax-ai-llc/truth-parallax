The chapter differentiating the feudal lords' filial piety emphasizes moderation and propriety as the distinctive marks of noble filial conduct. Confucian readers note the text's warning against arrogance and extravagance — virtues particularly necessary for those who hold power and wealth. The feudal lord who practices filial piety preserves his position not through force but through the moral authority that comes from humble devotion.

This chapter reflects the Confucian understanding that different social positions require different expressions of the same underlying virtue. Filial piety is not a uniform prescription but a principle that must be adapted to one's circumstances, always maintaining the balance between fulfilling obligations and avoiding excess.
