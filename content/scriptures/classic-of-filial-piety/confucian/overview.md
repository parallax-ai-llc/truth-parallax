## Overview

The Classic of Filial Piety occupies a foundational position in the Confucian tradition, where filial piety is regarded as the root of all virtue and the basis of social harmony. Confucian interpreters from Zengzi onward have treated the text as Confucius's definitive statement on the primacy of xiao, which extends from serving one's parents to governing the empire.

## Interpretive Approach

Traditional Confucian reading emphasizes the text's teaching that filial piety is not merely obedience but encompasses reverence, care, remonstrance, and the perpetuation of one's family's good name. The chapter on remonstrance (Chapter 15) is particularly important, establishing that true filial piety requires correcting a parent who acts wrongly rather than blindly following orders.

## Contemporary Relevance

Modern Confucian scholars continue to find in the Classic of Filial Piety resources for addressing contemporary questions about elder care, intergenerational responsibility, and the foundations of civic virtue. The text's vision of ethical life as rooted in family relationships remains a distinctive contribution to global moral discourse.
