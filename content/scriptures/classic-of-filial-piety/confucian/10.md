Confucian reading of this chapter emphasizes the three aspects of filial practice: serving parents with deep love and reverence in daily life, observing proper mourning with genuine grief, and making ancestral sacrifices with solemnity and care. The teaching that the filial person serves parents according to these three principles encapsulates the Confucian vision of filial piety as encompassing all of life, from daily conduct to the rituals that connect the living with the dead.

The chapter's insistence on genuine feeling underlying ritual form addresses the persistent Confucian concern with authenticity. Mere outward compliance without inner devotion is not true filial piety.
