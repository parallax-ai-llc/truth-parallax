---
title: "Classic of Filial Piety - Chapter 8"
scripture: "classic-of-filial-piety"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The Master said, "Formerly, the illustrious kings served their fathers with filial piety, and therefore they served Heaven with intelligence. They served their mothers with filial piety, and therefore they served Earth with discrimination."

**2** "They pursued the orderly sequence of seniority and inferiority, and therefore they secured the regulation of the relations between superiors and inferiors throughout the kingdom."

**3** "When Heaven and Earth were served with intelligence and discrimination, their spiritual influences shone forth brightly."

**4** "Therefore even the Son of Heaven must have one whom he honours — that is his father. He must have those to whom he concedes precedence — they are his elder brothers."

**5** "In the ancestral temple he serves them with the utmost reverence, showing that he does not forget his parents. He cultivates his person and is careful of his conduct, fearing lest he should disgrace his predecessors."

**6** "When he appears in the ancestral temple, full of reverence, his filial piety and obedience reach to the spiritual intelligences. To the spiritual beings of heaven and earth, the filial piety of the sage is responsive."

**7** "It is said in the Book of Poetry: 'From the west to the east, from the south to the north, there was not a thought but did him homage.'"
