---
title: "Classic of Filial Piety - Chapter 3"
scripture: "classic-of-filial-piety"
chapter: 3
---

**1** "To be above others, and yet free from pride and arrogance; to occupy a position of honour, and yet free from peril: by such observances, however high the position, the holder of it will not be overthrown. This is the filial piety of the princes of states."

**2** "To be cautious in conduct and not daring to be neglectful; to be moderate in expenditure, and yet not wasteful; to love the people as children, and to have compassion for the labouring classes — when all this is done, the people rejoice in the government."

**3** "It is said in the Book of Poetry: 'A prince who is fearful and cautious, as if he were on the brink of a deep abyss, as if he were treading on thin ice!'"
