The trinity chapter's assertion that filial piety connects heaven, earth, and humanity receives a characteristically Daoist reinterpretation. While Confucians read this as elevating filial piety to cosmic principle, Daoists note that the Dao itself is prior to and more fundamental than any specific human virtue. The real unity of heaven, earth, and humanity exists independently of filial piety; the Confucian error, from a Daoist perspective, is to locate cosmic significance in a particular moral practice rather than in the undifferentiated Way that precedes all distinctions.

Yet the chapter's vision of a unified cosmos resonates with the Daoist understanding of all things as manifestations of the Dao.
