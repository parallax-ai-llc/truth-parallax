Daoist interpretation of the cosmic influence chapter reframes its claims within a naturalistic understanding of resonance. While Confucians interpret the response of spirits to filial devotion as confirming the moral structure of the cosmos, Daoists understand resonance (ganying) as a natural phenomenon that operates through the Way rather than through specifically moral action. All sincere action, not merely filial devotion, produces cosmic resonance.

The Daoist reading broadens the Confucian claim while questioning its exclusive focus on filial piety as the operative principle.
