The exhortation chapter's emphasis on sustained effort and deliberate practice contrasts sharply with the Daoist approach to moral cultivation. While Confucians regard disciplined practice as essential for developing virtue, Daoists argue that effort-based cultivation produces artificial rather than genuine goodness. The Zhuangzi's image of the crooked tree that survives because it is useless suggests that the most natural virtue is precisely that which does not strive to be virtuous.

Daoist readers treat this chapter as illustrating the limitations of the Confucian moral program.
