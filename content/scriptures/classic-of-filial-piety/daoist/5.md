The scholars' filial piety chapter's teaching on transferring devotion from parents to ruler strikes Daoist readers as a problematic conflation of natural affection with institutional loyalty. The Zhuangzi's portrayal of Cook Ding, who follows the natural grain of reality rather than imposed structures, suggests an alternative model of skillful engagement that does not require the artificial framework of transferred loyalties.

Daoist interpretation notes that the scholar who truly understands the Way would serve neither parent nor ruler from obligation but would act with natural care for all beings.
