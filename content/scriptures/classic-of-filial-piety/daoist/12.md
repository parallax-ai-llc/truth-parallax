Daoist interpretation of the amplification chapter notes the Confucian strategy of moral extension with some sympathy but also skepticism. While the idea of extending natural affection outward has parallels with the Daoist vision of universal care, the Confucian method of deliberate, step-by-step extension contrasts with the Daoist understanding that compassion for all beings arises naturally when one's ego-centered perspective is dissolved.

The Daoist critique is not of the goal — universal benevolence — but of the method: calculated extension versus spontaneous realization.
