Daoist interpretation of the Son of Heaven's filial piety highlights the tension between the Confucian ideal of moral rulership and the Daoist ideal of wu wei governance. While the Xiaojing teaches that the emperor's filial devotion transforms the people, Daoist readers note that the most effective governance occurs when the ruler refrains from intervention. The Daoist ruler does not model specific virtues for imitation but creates conditions in which the people's natural goodness can express itself spontaneously.

The chapter's claim that the emperor's virtue reaches "the four seas" is read by Daoists as an overestimation of the power of deliberate moral effort.
