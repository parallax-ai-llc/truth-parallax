Daoist interpretation views the ministers' chapter's elaborate prescriptions for proper dress, speech, and conduct as precisely the kind of artificial regulation that the Zhuangzi parodies. The Daoist sage does not need to be told how to speak or dress because authentic conduct arises naturally from inner alignment with the Way. The chapter's detailed instructions suggest, from a Daoist perspective, that the Confucian system replaces genuine spontaneity with rule-following.

Yet Daoist readers acknowledge that in a world where natural simplicity has been lost, such prescriptions may serve as provisional guides.
