Daoist readers find the feudal lords' chapter's emphasis on moderation and avoidance of excess closer to Daoist sensibilities than other chapters. The warning against arrogance and extravagance echoes the Laozi's counsel that "the sage discards the excessive, the extravagant, the extreme." However, the Daoist understanding of moderation differs fundamentally from the Confucian: where the Xiaojing prescribes moderation as a duty, Daoism sees it as the natural result of aligning oneself with the Way.

The chapter inadvertently illustrates the Daoist point that conscious moral striving can become its own form of excess.
