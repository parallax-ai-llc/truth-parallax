Daoist readers interpret the chapter on serving the ruler as the most problematic in the text, since it most directly equates filial devotion with political loyalty. The Zhuangzi's portraits of sages who refuse official appointment — preferring to drag their tails in the mud like turtles — represent the Daoist alternative to the Confucian vision of loyal service. For Daoists, authentic care for others may require withdrawing from political structures rather than serving within them.

This chapter crystallizes the fundamental disagreement between Confucian political engagement and Daoist withdrawal.
