Daoist interpretation of the practice chapter's three aspects of filial devotion acknowledges the genuine human feelings underlying the prescriptions while questioning their formalization. The love and reverence shown to parents in daily life, when arising naturally, reflects the Dao's inherent care for all beings. However, the detailed prescriptions for mourning and sacrifice risk converting natural grief into ritualized performance.

The Daoist reading highlights the paradox that the very attempt to ensure authentic filial feeling through prescribed forms may undermine the spontaneity that makes such feeling genuine.
