Daoist readers find the chapter on perfect virtue most amenable to Daoist reinterpretation. The description of effortless virtue that transforms others without conscious intent parallels the Daoist concept of de (virtue/power) as the natural influence of one who embodies the Way. If the Confucian sage's virtue truly operates without deliberate effort, then it approximates the Daoist ideal of wu wei.

This chapter reveals an unexpected convergence between Confucian and Daoist understandings of the highest moral attainment.
