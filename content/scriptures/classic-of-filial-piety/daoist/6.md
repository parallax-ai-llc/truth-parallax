Daoist readers respond most sympathetically to the chapter on the common people's filial piety because of its emphasis on following natural patterns and seasonal rhythms. The teaching that commoners should work according to the patterns of heaven and earth aligns with the Daoist understanding of wu wei as acting in harmony with natural processes rather than against them.

The chapter's integration of agricultural labor with filial devotion reflects a worldview closer to Daoist naturalism than the more hierarchical chapters. From the Daoist perspective, the farmer who follows the seasons embodies a more authentic relationship with the Way than the ruler who consciously models virtue.
