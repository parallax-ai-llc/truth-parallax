Daoist interpretation of the governance chapter questions whether moral governance through filial example is truly superior to the effortless governance of the Daoist sage-ruler. The Laozi teaches that "the best ruler is one whose existence the people barely know," suggesting that even benevolent moral influence represents an excessive intervention. The Daoist ideal of governance allows the people to flourish according to their own nature without the pressure of moral exemplification.

The chapter reveals the fundamental difference between Confucian and Daoist political philosophy: active moral leadership versus creative non-interference.
