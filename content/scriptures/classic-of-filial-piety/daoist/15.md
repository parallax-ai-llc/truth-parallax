The remonstrance chapter receives a nuanced Daoist reading. On one hand, the duty to correct a parent or ruler who acts wrongly demonstrates a Confucian commitment to moral truth over mere social conformity — a value Daoists share. On the other hand, the very framework of remonstrance presupposes a hierarchical relationship that Daoists question. The Daoist sage does not remonstrate because the sage does not occupy a position of subordination; instead, the sage's presence naturally influences others toward the Way.

The chapter reveals both common ground and irreconcilable differences between the two traditions.
