Daoist readers approach the sages' government chapter with particular interest because of its treatment of music. While the Confucian understanding sees music as a tool for moral transformation, Daoists regard the most profound music as that which arises spontaneously from nature — the "music of heaven" described in the Zhuangzi. The Confucian sages' deliberate use of music to shape the people's emotions is seen as an inferior substitute for the natural harmonies that prevail when the Way is followed.

The chapter's praise of ritual is similarly reinterpreted: true ritual, for Daoists, is the spontaneous order of nature, not the prescribed ceremonies of human convention.
