---
title: "Nihon Shoki - Chapter 6"
scripture: "nihon-shoki"
chapter: 6
---

**1** After his expulsion from Heaven, Susa no Wo no Mikoto descended to the place of Tori-kami, at the head-waters of the River Hi, in the land of Izumo.

**2** At this time he heard a sound of weeping. He went in search of the sound, and found an old man and an old woman who had placed a young girl between them, and were weeping.

**3** He asked them, saying: "Who are ye, and why do ye weep?"

**4** They answered: "We are earthly deities. I am Ashi-nadzu-chi, and my wife's name is Te-nadzu-chi. This girl is our daughter, whose name is Kushi-nada-hime."

**5** "The reason of our weeping is that formerly we had eight daughters. But they have been devoured year by year by an eight-forked serpent, and now the time approaches for this girl likewise to be devoured. There is no means of escape, and therefore we weep."

**6** Susa no Wo asked: "What is this serpent's form like?"

**7** They answered: "Its eyes are as red as the akakagachi; it has one body with eight heads and eight tails. On its body grow moss and cryptomeria and cypress trees. Its length extends over eight valleys and eight hills. If you look at its belly, it is all constantly bloody and inflamed."

**8** Then Susa no Wo said to the old man: "If this is thy daughter, wilt thou give her to me?"

**9** He replied: "With reverence, but I know not thine august name."

**10** He answered: "I am the elder brother of the Sun-Goddess, and have now descended from Heaven."

**11** Then the old couple said: "If that is so, with reverence we will offer her to thee."

**12** So Susa no Wo took the young girl and changed her into a many-toothed close-comb which he stuck in his august hair-bunch. He bade the old couple brew sake of eight-fold brewing, and to make a fence with eight gates, each gate having a platform, and on each platform a sake-vat, each vat filled with the eight-fold sake.

**13** When all was prepared, the eight-forked serpent came, and dipping a head into each vat, drank the sake. It became intoxicated and lay down to sleep.

**14** Thereupon Susa no Wo drew his ten-span sword and cut the serpent to pieces. The River Hi ran with blood.

**15** When he cut the middle tail, the edge of his sword was slightly notched. He thrust in the point of his sword and split it open and looked, and there was a sharp great sword within.

**16** This sword he named Kusa-nagi no Tsurugi, the Herb-quelling Sword. This is the sword which is now at Atsuta in Owari.

**17** Then Susa no Wo built a palace at Suga in Izumo. At this time clouds rose up. He made a song, saying: "Many clouds rise -- the clouds which come forth -- a manifold fence -- for the newly-weds to retire within -- a manifold fence they form -- that manifold fence!"

**18** This was the first thirty-one-syllable verse ever composed.
