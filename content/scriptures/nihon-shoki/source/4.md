---
title: "Nihon Shoki - Chapter 4"
scripture: "nihon-shoki"
chapter: 4
---

**1** At this time Susa no Wo no Mikoto said: "I wish before I go, to take leave of my elder sister." But when he ascended to Heaven, the mountains and rivers all shook, and every land and country quaked.

**2** Amaterasu, alarmed at his coming, said: "My younger brother's ascent is surely not with any good intent. It must be that he wishes to wrest my land from me."

**3** So she bound up her hair into knots and tied up her skirts into the form of trousers. She slung on her back a quiver holding a thousand arrows and one holding five hundred arrows, and on her arm she drew a dread loud-sounding elbow-pad, and brandishing her bow, she stamped her feet firmly into the hard earth.

**4** She asked him, saying: "Wherefore dost thou ascend hither?"

**5** Susa no Wo no Mikoto answered and said: "From the beginning my heart has not been black. But as the parents have commanded me to proceed to the Nether-Land, I wish before I go to see my elder sister for a little while."

**6** Thereupon Amaterasu said: "If that is so, how shall I know that thy intentions are honest and bright?"

**7** Susa no Wo answered and said: "Let us swear oaths together, and produce children."

**8** So they stood on opposite sides of the Tranquil River of Heaven, and took an oath together. First Amaterasu took Susa no Wo's ten-span sword and broke it into three fragments, and rinsing them in the True-Well of Heaven, with a crunching noise bit them to pieces, and blew them away.

**9** From the mist of her breath three goddesses were born. Then Susa no Wo took the five hundred jewels strung upon the august necklace of Amaterasu, and rinsing them in the True-Well of Heaven, with a crunching noise bit them to pieces, and blew them away.

**10** From the mist of his breath five gods were born.

**11** After this Susa no Wo's behaviour was exceedingly rude. He broke down the ridges between the rice-paddies of Amaterasu and filled in the ditches. He flayed a piebald colt of Heaven backwards and flung it into the weaving hall.

**12** All the gods said: "Susa no Wo's conduct is extremely outrageous." But Amaterasu bore with him.
