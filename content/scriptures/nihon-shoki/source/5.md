---
title: "Nihon Shoki - Chapter 5"
scripture: "nihon-shoki"
chapter: 5
---

**1** After this, the Sun-Goddess Amaterasu, terrified by Susa no Wo's violence, opened the Rock-Cave of Heaven, and entered therein, and shut the door, and made it fast.

**2** Then the whole Plain of Heaven was obscured, and all the Central Land of Reed-Plains was darkened.

**3** Because of this, constant night prevailed, and the cries of the myriad deities were like the buzzing of summer flies; and myriad portents of woe all arose.

**4** Therefore the eight hundred myriad deities assembled in a divine assembly in the bed of the Tranquil River of Heaven, and considered what plan to devise.

**5** Then Omohi-kane no Kami, with profound thought and far-reaching counsel, caused to be gathered the long-singing birds of eternal night, and made them utter their prolonged cries to one another.

**6** They made the smith Ama-tsu-ma-ra to fashion a mirror of the stars of Heaven. They caused Tamanoya no Mikoto to string together eight hundred curved jewels.

**7** They made prayer-offerings and set up sacred sakaki trees adorned with jewels, mirrors, and cloth offerings, and the assembled gods recited grand liturgies.

**8** Then Ame no Uzume no Mikoto, ancestress of the Sarume chieftains, took in her hand a spear wreathed with Eulalia grass, and standing before the door of the Rock-Cave of Heaven, performed a divine dance.

**9** She became divinely possessed, exposed her breasts, and pushed down her skirt-string to her private parts. Then the Plain of High Heaven shook, and the eight hundred myriad deities laughed together.

**10** Amaterasu, hearing this, thought it strange. She opened the Rock-door a narrow space and said from within: "Since I have shut myself up, I thought that all the Plain of Heaven and the Central Land of Reed-Plains would be in darkness. How then is it that Ame no Uzume dances and all the gods laugh?"

**11** Then Ame no Uzume said: "We rejoice and are glad because there is a deity more illustrious than thou."

**12** While she was speaking, Ame no Ko-yane no Mikoto and Futo-tama no Mikoto pushed forward the mirror and showed it to the Sun-Goddess.

**13** The Sun-Goddess, more and more astonished, gradually came forth from the door and gazed upon it. Then the god Ta-jikara-wo, who was standing hidden, took her august hand and drew her out.

**14** Forthwith they drew a rope behind her, called the shimenawa, and said: "Thou must not go back further in than this."

**15** So the Sun-Goddess came forth again, and Heaven and Earth once more were light.
