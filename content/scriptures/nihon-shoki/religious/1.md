## The Birth of the Kami

Volume 1 of the Nihon Shoki recounts the most sacred moment in Shinto cosmology: the emergence of the first kami from the primordial chaos. When heaven and earth separated, three kami appeared spontaneously in the High Plain of Heaven (*Takamagahara*) — beings of pure creative power who set in motion the unfolding of the divine world.

For Shinto believers, this is not mere mythology but the revelation of the deepest nature of reality. The *musubi* (creative-becoming) power that generates the kami continues to animate all life. Shrine rituals reenact this primordial creativity through offerings, purification, and festival celebrations.

The chapter establishes the foundational Shinto teaching that the kami are not separate from the natural world but are its sacred dimension. The cosmos is alive with divine presence — every mountain, river, and tree participates in the ongoing work of creation begun in this first moment.

Motoori Norinaga emphasized that the creation account should be received with awe and reverence rather than rationalized. The mystery of origins (*hajime*) transcends human intellectual categories and calls for a response of wonder (*mono no aware*).
