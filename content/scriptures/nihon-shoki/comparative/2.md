The Izanagi-Izanami narrative invites rich comparative analysis. The divine couple who create through union parallels cosmogonic pairs across world mythology: Rangi and Papa (Maori), Geb and Nut (Egyptian), Ouranos and Gaia (Greek). In each case, the generative union of complementary cosmic forces produces the world.

The descent to the underworld is one of the most widespread mythological motifs. Izanagi's journey to Yomi closely parallels Orpheus's descent for Eurydice, Inanna/Ishtar's descent to the netherworld, and Demeter's search for Persephone. The common element — a taboo that is violated, resulting in permanent separation — suggests deep structural patterns in human mythological thinking about death.

The creation of islands by stirring the ocean with a cosmic implement finds close parallels in South and Southeast Asian traditions, particularly the Hindu *Samudra Manthana* (Churning of the Ocean of Milk). Obayashi Taryō argued this motif entered Japan through Austronesian cultural channels, predating Chinese influence.

The birth of kami from Izanagi's bodily purification — Amaterasu from an eye, Susanoo from the nose — parallels the Hindu account of deities emerging from Purusha's body (Rig Veda 10.90) and the Norse giant Ymir.
