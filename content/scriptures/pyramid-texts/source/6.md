**Utterances 213-246: The Cannibal Hymn**

**1** The sky is overcast, the stars are darkened, the celestial expanses quiver, the bones of the earth-god tremble.

**2** The planets stand still when they see the King rising as a spirit, a god who lives on his fathers, who feeds on his mothers.

**3** The King is the bull of the sky, who conquers at will, who lives on the being of every god, who eats their entrails, even of those who come with their bodies full of magic from the Island of Flame.

**4** The King is equipped. He has assembled his spirits. The King has appeared as this Great One, a lord of those who serve.

**5** He sits with his back to Geb, and it is the King who judges with Him-whose-name-is-hidden on this day of slaying the Oldest Ones.

**6** The King is a possessor of offerings who knots the cord and who himself prepares his meal. The King is one who eats men and lives on gods.
