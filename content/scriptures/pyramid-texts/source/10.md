**Utterances 337-374: The King and the Sun God**

**1** O Ra-Atum, this King comes to you, an imperishable spirit. Your son comes to you. This King comes to you.

**2** May you cross the sky, united in the dark. May you rise in the horizon, in the place where it is well with you.

**3** Ra lives, the tortoise dies! Ra lives, the tortoise dies! You who are in the sun's mansion, your arm is grasped by Ra.

**4** You shall not die, for you are bound for the horizon with Ra. The arm of the King is grasped by Ra.

**5** O Ra, do not go alone, without the King! O Ra, do not depart without the King! The King's place is before you.
