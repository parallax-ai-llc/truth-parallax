## Overview

The Hermetic tradition regards the Pyramid Texts as the most ancient source of the esoteric wisdom later attributed to Hermes Trismegistus. These utterances, inscribed in the most sacred of spaces — the royal burial chamber — are read as preserving the pristine teachings of the Egyptian priesthood about the soul's ascent to divine union.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Ascent of the Soul
The pharaoh's ascent through the heavens is read as a template for the Hermetic ascent of the soul through the planetary spheres, as described in the Poimandres (Corpus Hermeticum I).

### Sacred Architecture
The pyramid itself is understood as a theurgic instrument — a structure designed to facilitate the soul's transformation and ascent. The placement of texts within the chambers encodes a spatial theology of spiritual progression.

### Stellar Mysticism
The association of the pharaoh with the imperishable stars reflects a stellar mysticism that Hermeticists see as the foundation of later astrological and astronomical wisdom.
