## Hermetic Reading

Utterance Group 9, "The Ferryman of the Gods," addresses the king's identification with celestial powers and his ascent through the heavenly regions. The Pyramid Texts, as the oldest extensive religious literature in human history (c. 2400-2300 BCE), represent for Hermetic interpreters the earliest stratum of the prisca theologia — the primordial wisdom tradition that Hermes Trismegistus embodied and that Marsilio Ficino believed predated even Moses.

## The Prisca Theologia and Egyptian Origins

Renaissance Hermeticists following Ficino and Pico della Mirandola regarded Egypt as the fountainhead of all sacred wisdom. The Pyramid Texts confirm this intuition: their elaborate cosmology, ritual technology, and doctrine of divine ascent prefigure the philosophical Hermeticism of the Corpus Hermeticum by two millennia. Garth Fowden's *The Egyptian Hermes* traces the historical continuity from Old Kingdom mortuary religion through the temple culture of Ptolemaic Egypt to the Hermetic circles of Roman Alexandria.

## Correspondence: As Above, So Below

The utterances in this group encode the principle of correspondence that the Emerald Tablet formulates as "that which is above is like that which is below." The king's ascent mirrors the ascent through the seven planetary spheres in Corpus Hermeticum I.24-26, where the soul sheds its accumulated material accretions. The pyramid itself — its geometry orienting the deceased toward the circumpolar stars — is a physical embodiment of the Hermetic principle that terrestrial forms reflect celestial archetypes.

## Spiritual Alchemy

The transformation described in these utterances corresponds to the sublimation — the elevation of volatile essences from the base material. The king's body, preserved through mummification (the material basis), undergoes spiritual transmutation through the utterances' performative power. This is the original form of the Great Work that later alchemists encoded in chemical metaphor: the transformation of the leaden mortal condition into the gold of divine immortality.

## Pneuma and Divine Nous

The utterances' invocations work through what Hermetic philosophy calls pneuma — the divine breath-spirit that permeates all creation. The ancient Egyptian concept of the *ba* (soul-spirit) corresponds to the Hermetic pneuma, while the *akh* (glorified spirit) corresponds to the nous that has been purified through the ascent. Peter Kingsley's *Ancient Philosophy, Mystery, and Magic* traces these connections between Egyptian, Pythagorean, and Hermetic pneumatology.
