## Overview

An interfaith reading of the Pyramid Texts discovers in these oldest of religious texts themes and concerns that transcend their ancient Egyptian context. The universal human longing for transcendence, the belief in life after death, and the conviction that the divine and human can meet — these resonate across the world's religious traditions.

## Key Interfaith Themes

### Resurrection and Afterlife Hope
The Pyramid Texts' central concern — that the dead king shall rise again — echoes across traditions:
- **Christianity**: The resurrection of Christ and the hope of bodily resurrection
- **Islam**: Resurrection on the Day of Judgment
- **Zoroastrianism**: The Frashokereti and bodily resurrection
- **Hinduism**: The immortality of the atman

### Ascension to the Divine
The pharaoh's ascent to the heavens parallels:
- **Christianity**: Christ's Ascension and the saints' journey to heaven
- **Islam**: Muhammad's Mi'raj (Night Journey)
- **Hinduism**: The soul's ascent to Brahmaloka
- **Judaism**: Elijah's ascent in the chariot of fire

### Sacred Speech and Ritual Power
The belief that uttered words can effect cosmic change connects to:
- **Judaism**: The creative power of divine speech in Genesis
- **Hinduism**: The power of mantras and Vedic recitation
- **Islam**: The miraculous nature of Quranic recitation
