---
title: "Kitab-i-Iqan - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kitab-i-iqan"
chapter: 2
---

**1** And now, concerning His words: "And He shall send His angels." By angels is meant those who, reinforced by the power of the spirit, have consumed, with the fire of the love of God, all human traits and limitations, and have clothed themselves with the attributes of the most exalted Beings and of the Cherubim.

**2** That holy Being said: "We are all from God, and unto Him shall we return." In their every action they were guided by the light of God, and their every thought was centered on Him.

**3** It is clear and evident that by the words "the sun shall be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven" is not meant the sun and moon that provide light to the peoples of the world. It hath been in truth a spiritual sun and moon and stars that have ever been guiding mankind.

**4** By the terms "sun" and "moon" mentioned in the writings of the Prophets of God, is not meant solely the sun and moon of the visible universe. Nay rather, manifold are the meanings they have intended for these terms. In every instance they have attached to them a particular significance.

**5** Thus, by the "sun" in one sense is meant those Suns of Truth Who rise from the dayspring of ancient glory, and fill the world with a liberal effusion of grace from on high. These Suns of Truth are the universal Manifestations of God in the worlds of His attributes and names.

**6** Even as the visible sun that assisteth, as decreed by God, the true One, in the development of all earthly things, such as the trees, the fruits, and colours thereof, the minerals of the earth, and all that may be witnessed in the world of creation, so do the divine Luminaries, by their loving care and educating influence, cause the trees of divine unity, the fruits of His oneness, the leaves of detachment, the blossoms of knowledge and certitude, and the myrtles of wisdom and utterance, to exist and be made manifest.

**7** Thus it is that through the rise of these Luminaries of God the world is made new, the waters of everlasting life stream forth, the billows of loving-kindness surge, the clouds of grace are gathered, and the breeze of bounty bloweth upon all created things.

**8** It is the warmth that these Luminaries of God generate, and the undying fires they kindle, which cause the light of the love of God to burn fiercely in the heart of humanity. It is through the abundant grace of these Symbols of Detachment that the Spirit of life everlasting is breathed into the bodies of the dead.

**9** Assuredly the peoples of the world are, one and all, immersed in the sea of God and His bounties. None is left without His grace.

**10** Know verily that the purpose underlying all these symbolic terms and abstruse allusions, which emanate from the Revealers of God's holy Cause, hath been to test and prove the peoples of the world; that thereby the earth of the pure and illuminated hearts may be known from the perishable and barren soil.

**11** From time immemorial such hath been the way of God amidst His creatures, and to this testify the records of the Sacred Scriptures. Only those will attain to the knowledge of the Word of God that have turned unto Him, and repudiated the manifestations of Satan.

**12** Thus doth God prove His servants. Thus is the coin of the true distinguished from the counterfeit, and the humble from the arrogant. Could ye but perceive it, He verily speaketh the truth, and leadeth the way.

**13** Now, concerning the question whether the true Sovereign possesseth any earthly sovereignty or not, and if so, why He doth not assert His authority through outward means, know thou that the dominion of these Sovereigns is not confined merely to this earthly plane.

**14** Their spiritual ascendancy over all peoples of the earth is abundantly proved by the sovereignty of Him Who is the King of Kings. The very purpose of the existence of these Luminaries of the world is the advancement of mankind, the quickening of the souls of the children of men.

**15** Strive therefore, O my brother, to grasp the meaning of "Assistance," and count it as among those things that pertain unto the spirit. Consider: What manner of sovereignty and what kind of dominion doth God exercise?

**16** Know thou of a certainty that by sovereignty is meant the all-encompassing, all-pervading power which is inherently exercised by the Qaim whether or not He appear to the world clothed in the majesty of earthly dominion.

**17** This is solely because the people in every age have failed to understand the meaning of sovereignty and have attached to it a significance which it doth not possess.

**18** Nay, by sovereignty is meant that sovereignty which in every dispensation resideth within, and is exercised by, the person of the Manifestation, the Daystar of Truth. That sovereignty is the spiritual ascendancy which He exerciseth to the fullest degree over all that is in heaven and on earth.

**19** Such is the depth of the grace vouchsafed unto you that nought that hath been created can excel it. Happy is the man that hath understood this truth, and woe betide him that hath failed to attain thereto.

**20** Ponder upon the things which have been mentioned, perchance thou mayest quaff the Sealed Wine through the power of the Lord of all men and attain unto Him Who is the Desire of all nations.

**21** Verily He shall gather together all men beneath His shadow, and shall lead them to the living waters. This is indeed that which the divine Scriptures do promise, and of this hath the Pen of the Most High given its assurance.

**22** Peace be upon him that inclineth his ear unto the melody of the Mystic Bird calling from the Sadratu'l-Muntaha! Glorified be our Lord, the Most High!

**23** May the peace and blessings of God be upon those who have recognized His truth, and acknowledged His sovereignty, and may His curse be upon those who have committed that which hath caused the Repositories of His knowledge and wisdom to complain.

**24** We have now established, to thine eyes, the truth of Our Cause, and have proved unto thee that which was prophesied. Thus hath the Daystar of certitude shone forth from the horizons of the Book unto them that have eyes to see, and ears to hear.

**25** In these days, praise be to God, the Pen of Glory hath disclosed the mystery of all things. Not a single letter of the Scriptures hath remained uninterpreted, and not a single allusion in all the books of former times hath remained unexplained.
