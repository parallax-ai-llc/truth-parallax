---
title: "Kitab-i-Iqan - Chapter 1"
scripture: "kitab-i-iqan"
chapter: 1
---

**1** In the name of our Lord, the Exalted, the Most High. No man shall attain the shores of the ocean of true understanding except he be detached from all that is in heaven and on earth.

**2** Sanctify your souls, O ye peoples of the world, that haply ye may attain that station which God hath destined for you and enter thus the tabernacle which, according to the dispensations of Providence, hath been raised in the firmament of the Bayan.

**3** The essence of these words is this: they that tread the path of faith, they that thirst for the wine of certitude, must cleanse themselves of all that is earthly, their ears from idle talk, their minds from vain imaginings, their hearts from worldly affections, their eyes from that which perisheth.

**4** They should put their trust in God, and, holding fast unto Him, follow in His way. Then will they be made worthy of the effulgent glories of the sun of divine knowledge and understanding, and become the recipients of a grace that is infinite and unseen.

**5** Inasmuch as it hath been clearly shown that only those who are initiated into the divine mysteries can comprehend the melodies uttered by the Bird of Heaven, it is therefore incumbent upon every one to seek enlightenment from the illumined in heart and from the Treasuries of divine mysteries regarding the indications of the revealed Word.

**6** This is the food that conferreth everlasting life upon the pure in heart. This is the bread of which it is said: "Lord, send down upon us Thy bread from heaven." This bread shall never be withheld from them that deserve it, nor can it ever be exhausted. It groweth everlastingly from the tree of grace.

**7** It is incumbent upon thee, by the permission of God, to cleanse the eye of thine heart from the things of the world, that thou mayest realize the infinitude of divine knowledge, and mayest behold Truth so clearly that thou wilt need no proof to demonstrate His reality, nor any evidence to bear witness to His truth.

**8** O affection of the Merciful! O warmth of the Blessed! He that was hidden from the eyes of men is revealed, girded with sovereignty and power. Open the eye of thine heart, that thou mayest see Him and with thy whole being celebrate His praise.

**9** Consider the past. How many, both high and low, have, at all times, yearningly awaited the advent of the Manifestations of God in the sanctified persons of His chosen Ones. How often have they anticipated His coming, how frequently have they offered their prayers that the breeze of divine mercy might blow, and the promised Beauty step forth from behind the veil of concealment.

**10** And whensoever the portals of grace did open, and the clouds of divine bounty did rain upon mankind, and the light of the Unseen did shine above the horizon of celestial might, they all denied Him, and turned away from His face, the face of God Himself.

**11** Reflect, what could have been the motive for such deeds? What could have prompted such behaviour towards the Revealers of the beauty of the All-Glorious? Whatever in days gone by hath been the cause of the denial and opposition of those people hath now led to the perversity of the people of this age.

**12** To maintain that the testimony of Providence was incomplete, that it hath therefore been the cause of the denial of the people, is but open blasphemy. How far from the grace of the All-Bountiful and from His loving providence and tender mercies it is to single out a soul from amongst all men for the guidance of His creatures, and, on one hand, to withhold from Him the full measure of His divine testimony, and, on the other, inflict severe retribution on His people for having turned away from His chosen One.

**13** Nay, the manifold bounties of the Lord of all beings have, at all times, through the Manifestations of His divine Essence, encompassed the earth and all that dwell therein. Not for a moment hath His grace been withheld, nor have the showers of His loving-kindness ceased to rain upon mankind.

**14** Consequently, such behaviour can be attributed to naught save the petty-mindedness of such souls as tread the valley of arrogance and pride, are lost in the wilds of remoteness, walk in the ways of their idle fancy, and follow the dictates of the leaders of their faith.

**15** The traditions and beliefs of each of these groups testify to the truth of the words that have been uttered. These traditions are too well known to require further elucidation; as they are known to all, reference unto them is not necessary.

**16** Among the Prophets was Noah. For nine hundred and fifty years He prayerfully exhorted His people and summoned them to the haven of security and peace. None, however, heeded His call. Each day they inflicted on His blessed person such pain and suffering that no one believed He could survive.

**17** How frequently they denied Him, how malevolently they cast their aspersions against Him! Thus it hath been revealed: "And as often as a company of His people passed by Him, they derided Him. To them He said: Though ye scoff at us now, we will scoff at you hereafter even as ye scoff at us."

**18** Long afterward, He several times promised victory to His companions and fixed the hour thereof. But when the hour struck, the divine promise was not fulfilled. This caused a few among the small number of His followers to turn away from Him, and to this testify the records of the best-known books.

**19** And when the appointed hour arrived, the storm raged, and the ark sailed upon the face of the waters. Those who rejected Him were drowned, while those who believed in His signs and acknowledged His sovereignty were saved.

**20** And after Noah the light of the countenance of Hud shone forth above the horizon of creation. For well-nigh seven hundred years, according to the sayings of certain historians, He exhorted the people to turn their faces and draw nearer unto the Ridvan of the divine presence.

**21** What showers of afflictions rained upon Him, until at last His adjurations bore the fruit of increased rebelliousness, and His assiduous endeavours resulted in the wilful blindness of His people.

**22** And after Hud the Beauty of the face of Salih shone forth from the veils of concealment. He arose and summoned the people to the river of everlasting life. They, however, rebelled against Him and flung themselves far from Him, until He at last breathed His last.

**23** And after Him there appeared from the Ridvan of the Eternal, the Invisible, the holy person of Abraham. He it was Who in the fire was raised up. God made the fire to be for Him a light and a protection. And after Him We raised up Moses and Aaron. Such was their fate that Pharaoh and his hosts could not but seek to slay them.

**24** Judge fairly: Were these people to be chastised because they refused to recognize the divine Messengers? And if so, why should God punish a people for following the very leaders whom He Himself had set over them? Yea, if we be fair, we must acknowledge that all the people of every age and nation were led by their divines, and followed their guidance.

**25** Hast thou not heard of the sayings of old? It hath been revealed: "The roads to God are as many as the breaths of His creatures." And yet they have strayed from His path, and in all ages have rejected His Messengers and Prophets. How many souls, how many lives, have been wasted, and what blood hath been shed!
