## Overview

For Baha'is, the Kitab-i-Iqan is the foundational theological treatise of their Faith — the text that establishes the intellectual and spiritual framework for understanding Baha'u'llah's claim and the principle of progressive revelation. Written before Baha'u'llah's public declaration of his mission, it lays the groundwork by demonstrating through reason, scripture, and spiritual insight that God's revelation is continuous and ongoing.

## Key Interpretive Principles

### The Hermeneutics of Certitude
The Iqan establishes a distinctive hermeneutical method: scriptural prophecies about the "end times," resurrection, and return are to be understood symbolically rather than literally. This principle, applied to the Quran, the Gospels, and the Hebrew Bible, demonstrates that the apparent non-fulfillment of prophecy is actually its fulfillment in spiritual rather than material terms.

### The Station of the Manifestation
Baha'u'llah articulates the concept of the Manifestation of God — a being who is neither God incarnate nor merely human but a perfect mirror reflecting divine attributes. All the prophets share this station, which explains both their unity and their apparent differences.

### Prerequisites for Spiritual Search
The Iqan describes the qualities required of the true seeker: detachment, purity of heart, independence of investigation, and willingness to abandon inherited assumptions. These prerequisites apply to seekers in every age confronting a new revelation.
