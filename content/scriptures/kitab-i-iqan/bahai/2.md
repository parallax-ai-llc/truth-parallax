## Part Two (The New Revelation)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the establishment of the Bab's station and the principles by which a new Manifestation of God can be recognized, including the qualities of the true seeker and the proofs of divine authority.

Part Two of the Iqan establishes the theological framework for recognizing a Manifestation of God. Baha'u'llah articulates the proofs of divine authority — including the Manifestation's innate knowledge, the transformative power of his words, the constancy of his followers in the face of persecution, and the ultimate triumph of his cause despite opposition.

For Baha'is, this section provides both the intellectual justification for accepting Baha'u'llah's claim and the spiritual method for deepening one's certitude. The qualities of the 'true seeker' described here — detachment, purity of heart, willingness to abandon preconceptions — constitute a spiritual discipline that Baha'is strive to embody.

The concept of the Manifestation of God as a 'perfect mirror' reflecting divine attributes — neither God incarnate nor merely human — establishes a distinctive Baha'i Christology and prophetology that resolves, for Baha'is, the apparent contradictions between the claims of different religious founders.
