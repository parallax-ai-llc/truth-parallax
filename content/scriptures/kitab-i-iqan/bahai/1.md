## Part One (Proof from Past Scriptures)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the interpretation of past scriptures — Quran, Gospels, and Hebrew Bible — to demonstrate that prophetic language is symbolic and that divine revelation is continuous, using examples of how each new Manifestation was rejected by the followers of the previous one.

For Baha'is, Part One of the Iqan provides the hermeneutical key to understanding all scripture. Baha'u'llah demonstrates through meticulous analysis that the prophecies of the Hebrew Bible, the Gospels, and the Quran concerning the 'end times' are fulfilled spiritually, not materially. The 'sun being darkened' refers to the eclipse of religious leadership; the 'stars falling' symbolizes the decline of religious scholars; 'resurrection' is spiritual awakening rather than physical rising from graves.

This interpretive framework liberates the Baha'i reader from literalist expectations while deepening reverence for all scriptures. Every sacred text is understood as divinely revealed truth whose inner meaning transcends any single historical application. The apparent contradictions between religions dissolve when their prophecies are read symbolically.

Baha'u'llah also establishes the pattern of opposition that greets every new Manifestation: the Jewish leaders rejected Jesus, the Christian world rejected Muhammad, and each community clung to its literal reading of prophecy to justify its rejection. This pattern serves as both historical analysis and warning to Baha'is themselves.
