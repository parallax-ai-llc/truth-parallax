## Part Two (The New Revelation)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the establishment of the Bab's station and the principles by which a new Manifestation of God can be recognized, including the qualities of the true seeker and the proofs of divine authority.

Part Two offers the theological foundation for the Baha'i approach to interfaith relations: if all Manifestations of God share a single station and purpose, then the religions they founded are not competing claims to truth but successive chapters in a single divine story. This 'unity of the prophets' teaching provides a framework for interfaith engagement that is neither exclusivist nor relativist.

The description of the true seeker's qualities — detachment, humility, independent investigation, willingness to be transformed — offers a model for interfaith dialogue itself. Productive dialogue requires the same virtues: the ability to listen without defensiveness, to consider unfamiliar ideas with openness, and to allow genuine encounter to change one's understanding.

For interfaith participants, Part Two raises the question of whether authentic religious pluralism requires a meta-framework (such as progressive revelation) or whether it is better served by acknowledging irreducible differences while seeking common ground. The Iqan offers one compelling answer to this perennial question.
