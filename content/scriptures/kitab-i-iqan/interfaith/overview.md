## Overview

The Kitab-i-Iqan is perhaps the most significant Baha'i text for interfaith dialogue because it directly engages with the scriptures and prophetic traditions of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Its argument for the essential unity of all divine revelation, while controversial, opens productive conversations about how different traditions understand scripture, prophecy, and the nature of religious truth.

## Key Themes for Dialogue

### Unity of the Prophets
The Iqan's teaching that all Manifestations of God share a single purpose — the education and spiritual advancement of humanity — offers a framework for interfaith understanding that goes beyond mere tolerance to affirm the divine origin of all major religions. This challenges both exclusivism and relativism.

### The Challenge of Recognition
The Iqan's analysis of why people reject new prophets — clinging to tradition, literal reading of prophecy, following religious leaders rather than seeking independently — resonates across traditions. Every religion was once "new" and faced opposition from the established order.

### Hermeneutical Humility
The Iqan implicitly calls for hermeneutical humility — the recognition that no single community's interpretation of scripture exhausts its meaning. This principle, if accepted, transforms interfaith dialogue from apologetic debate into collaborative exploration.

## Key Questions for Dialogue

| Question | Traditions Engaged |
|----------|-------------------|
| Can revelation be progressive? | Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Baha'i |
| Is prophecy symbolic or literal? | All Abrahamic traditions |
| How do we recognize truth? | Universal question |
| Is unity possible without uniformity? | All traditions |
