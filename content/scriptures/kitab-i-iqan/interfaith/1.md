## Part One (Proof from Past Scriptures)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the interpretation of past scriptures — Quran, Gospels, and Hebrew Bible — to demonstrate that prophetic language is symbolic and that divine revelation is continuous, using examples of how each new Manifestation was rejected by the followers of the previous one.

Part One of the Iqan models a form of interfaith engagement that is both respectful and challenging. By treating the Hebrew Bible, the Gospels, and the Quran as equally authoritative scriptures with a unified purpose, Baha'u'llah demonstrates what it means to take multiple traditions seriously on their own terms while discovering a larger pattern.

The text's analysis of why each community rejected the next prophet — attachment to literal interpretation, reliance on clerical authority, unwillingness to investigate independently — offers a productive mirror for all religious communities. Every tradition has been both the rejector and the rejected at different points in history.

For interfaith dialogue, Part One raises the central question: is it possible to honor the truth of multiple scriptures without reducing them to a lowest common denominator? The Iqan's answer — progressive revelation — offers one framework, though participants from other traditions may propose alternatives.
