## Part One (Proof from Past Scriptures)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the interpretation of past scriptures — Quran, Gospels, and Hebrew Bible — to demonstrate that prophetic language is symbolic and that divine revelation is continuous, using examples of how each new Manifestation was rejected by the followers of the previous one.

Part One engages directly with Quranic verses and Islamic theological concepts. Baha'u'llah reinterprets Quranic eschatological passages — the Day of Judgment, the resurrection, the signs of the Hour — in spiritual terms, arguing that these have been fulfilled in the advent of the Bab.

From a mainstream Islamic perspective, this hermeneutical approach is problematic. While *ta'wil* (esoteric interpretation) has a place in Islamic exegesis, particularly in Shia and Sufi traditions, the dominant view holds that Quranic descriptions of the Day of Judgment refer to real, physical events that will occur at the end of time. The Iqan's thoroughgoing spiritualization of these prophecies exceeds what most Muslim scholars regard as legitimate interpretation.

However, scholars note that Baha'u'llah's method has roots in the Ismaili tradition of *ta'wil* and in the Shaykhi school of Shia Islam from which the Babi movement emerged. The question is whether these precedents authorize the conclusions the Iqan draws.
