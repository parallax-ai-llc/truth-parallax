## Part Two (The New Revelation)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the establishment of the Bab's station and the principles by which a new Manifestation of God can be recognized, including the qualities of the true seeker and the proofs of divine authority.

Part Two's assertion that the Bab is a legitimate Manifestation of God directly challenges mainstream Islamic theology. The proofs Baha'u'llah adduces — the Bab's innate knowledge, the power of his verses, the steadfastness of his followers — mirror traditional Islamic arguments for Muhammad's prophethood (*dala'il an-nubuwwa*), raising the uncomfortable question of whether these criteria, if valid for Muhammad, might apply to others.

The concept of the Manifestation of God, while drawing on Islamic prophetology, introduces theological categories unfamiliar to mainstream Islam. The idea that the Manifestation is a 'perfect mirror' of divine attributes goes beyond the Islamic understanding of prophets as chosen human beings who receive revelation (*wahy*).

For Islamic scholars, the fundamental issue remains: does the principle of *khatm an-nubuwwa* preclude the very possibility of such a figure appearing after Muhammad? The Iqan's argument that 'seal' means culmination rather than termination remains unpersuasive to mainstream Islamic theology.
