## Overview

From an Islamic perspective, the Kitab-i-Iqan represents a radical reinterpretation of Quranic eschatology and the concept of prophetic finality. Its argument that scriptural prophecies should be read symbolically rather than literally challenges mainstream Sunni and Shia hermeneutical traditions, even while employing methods familiar from Islamic exegetical history.

## Key Interpretive Issues

### Quranic Exegesis
The Iqan interprets numerous Quranic verses — particularly those concerning the Day of Judgment, resurrection, and the signs of the Hour — in spiritual rather than physical terms. While symbolic interpretation has precedent in the Sufi tradition of *ta'wil* (esoteric exegesis), the Iqan's application of this method to eschatological prophecy goes beyond what most Islamic scholars accept.

### Prophetic Finality
The Iqan directly addresses the concept of *khatm an-nubuwwa* (the seal of prophethood), arguing that Muhammad is the "seal" of a prophetic cycle but not the end of divine communication. This interpretation remains unacceptable to mainstream Islamic theology.

### Methodological Parallels
Despite theological disagreements, scholars note that the Iqan's interpretive methods have roots in Islamic tradition — particularly in Shia *ta'wil*, Ismaili hermeneutics, and Sufi approaches to the inner meaning of scripture. The question is whether these methods are legitimately applied to the conclusions the Iqan draws.
