## Part One (Proof from Past Scriptures)

This part of the Kitab-i-Iqan addresses the interpretation of past scriptures — Quran, Gospels, and Hebrew Bible — to demonstrate that prophetic language is symbolic and that divine revelation is continuous, using examples of how each new Manifestation was rejected by the followers of the previous one.

Scholars analyze Part One as a masterwork of cross-scriptural hermeneutics unprecedented in its systematic scope. Baha'u'llah draws on Shia exegetical methods — particularly *ta'wil* (esoteric interpretation) — but applies them in a novel cross-traditional framework, interpreting Jewish, Christian, and Islamic scriptures within a single unified argument.

Christopher Buck has shown that the Iqan's interpretive method combines elements of traditional Islamic *tafsir* with original innovations. The text's engagement with biblical passages reveals an author familiar with the Bible not only through Quranic references but through direct textual knowledge — a notable achievement in the nineteenth-century Middle Eastern context.

The rhetorical structure of Part One follows a cumulative logic: by demonstrating that the followers of each previous religion rejected the next Manifestation for the same reasons, Baha'u'llah builds an inductive argument that applies equally to contemporary Muslim rejection of the Bab. The academic reader notes both the elegance of this argument and its apologetic function.
