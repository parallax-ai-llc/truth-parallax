Chapter 2 deepens the paradox: Brahman is known to those who do not claim to know it, and unknown to those who think they know it (2.3). Vaishnavas read this as teaching humility before the infinite nature of God — Vishnu's reality always exceeds human comprehension. The devotee who recognizes the limits of human knowledge is closer to truth than the philosopher who claims to have defined God through concepts.

The chapter concludes by stating that Brahman is realized "in every state of consciousness" (2.4) — in every flash of awareness, Brahman is glimpsed. Vaishnavas interpret this as the omnipresence of the Lord in every moment of experience. The instruction to seek Brahman through each moment of awareness supports the Vaishnava practice of constant remembrance (*smarana*) of the Lord in all activities.
