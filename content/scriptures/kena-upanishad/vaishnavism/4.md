Chapter 4 introduces Uma Haimavati — the daughter of the Himalayas — as the one who reveals Brahman's identity to Indra. When Indra, king of the gods, approaches the mysterious Yaksha, it vanishes. In its place appears Uma, who tells Indra: "That was Brahman. It is through Brahman's victory that you achieved your glory." This revelation transforms Indra's understanding.

Vaishnavas identify Uma with the Lord's divine energy (*Shakti*) or with Lakshmi, who mediates between the Supreme Lord and the souls seeking Him. Her role as revealer underscores the Vaishnava teaching that knowledge of God comes through God's own grace, not through independent human effort. The chapter concludes by declaring that Brahman is known as *Tadvanam* — "that which is worthy of all adoration" — directing the aspirant toward devotional worship as the means of realization.
