## Overview

Vaishnava interpreters read the Kena Upanishad as demonstrating Vishnu's supremacy as the power behind all cosmic functions. The opening question — "By whom is the mind directed?" — is answered by pointing to the Supreme Lord who enables all faculties while remaining beyond their grasp. The parable of the gods' humiliation in Chapters 3-4 illustrates the Vaishnava teaching that even the greatest celestial beings are subordinate to Vishnu.

## Theological Emphases

The Kena Upanishad's teaching that Brahman cannot be known through ordinary cognition (2.3: "It is known to those who do not know it") is read by Vaishnavas as affirming the necessity of divine grace for God-realization. Human effort alone — including the mighty powers of Agni, Vayu, and Indra — cannot reach God. Only when Uma (identified with Lakshmi or Shakti) reveals Brahman's identity do the gods attain understanding, demonstrating that knowledge of God comes through God's own self-disclosure.
