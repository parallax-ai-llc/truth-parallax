Chapter 3 tells the parable of Brahman's appearance before the gods after their victory over the demons. The gods, proud of their triumph, fail to recognize that it was Brahman who secured their victory. Brahman appears as a mysterious *Yaksha* (spirit) to humble them. Agni, the fire god, approaches first and boasts of his power to burn anything — but cannot burn a single blade of grass placed before him by the Yaksha.

Vaishnavas read this parable as a powerful demonstration of divine sovereignty. Even the greatest cosmic powers — fire, wind, thunder — derive their capacity entirely from Vishnu. When separated from His enabling will, they are powerless. The gods' failure to recognize Brahman mirrors the universal human tendency to claim credit for capacities that are actually gifts from God. True wisdom begins with acknowledging complete dependence on the Lord.
