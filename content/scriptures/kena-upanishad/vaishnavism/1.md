The Kena Upanishad opens with the fundamental question: "By whom (*kena*) is the mind directed? By whom does breath function? By whom is speech impelled?" Vaishnava interpreters answer: by Vishnu, the Supreme Lord who is the inner controller (*antaryamin*) of all faculties. The mind thinks, the eyes see, and the ears hear only because the Lord empowers them from within. Without His enabling power, no faculty can function.

The chapter's teaching that Brahman is "the ear of the ear, the mind of the mind" (1.2) is read by Vaishnavas as describing Vishnu's relationship to the individual soul — He is the power behind every power, the consciousness behind every act of consciousness. This does not mean the soul is identical with God but that the soul's every capacity is derived from and dependent upon divine grace.
