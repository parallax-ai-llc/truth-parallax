Madhva reads the opening question of the Kena Upanishad — "By whom is the mind directed?" — as a straightforward inquiry about the Supreme Controller. The answer is Vishnu, who directs every faculty as the inner ruler (*antaryamin*) of all souls. Unlike Advaita's reading, which identifies Brahman with the subject of experience itself, Dvaita maintains that Brahman is a separate, supreme agent who enables the soul's faculties from within without being identical to them.

The verse "That which is not expressed by speech, but by which speech is expressed" (1.5) is read by Dvaita as affirming God's transcendence — human language cannot adequately describe Vishnu's infinite nature, though it can point toward Him through scriptural revelation. God is beyond speech not because He is an impersonal awareness but because His personal qualities are infinitely rich and inexhaustible by finite description.
