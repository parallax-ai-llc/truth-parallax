Madhva identifies Uma in Chapter 4 as a manifestation of Vishnu's energy who functions as a mediator between God and the gods. Her revelation to Indra demonstrates that knowledge of God is always mediated — it comes through scripture, tradition, and divine grace, never through independent rational inquiry alone.

The chapter's conclusion that Brahman is *Tadvanam* — "the object of supreme adoration" — is taken literally by Dvaita as directing all beings toward devotional worship of Vishnu. The teaching that Brahman is realized through *tapas* (austerity), *dama* (self-control), and *karma* (ritual action) establishes the practical disciplines necessary for devotional life. For Dvaita, these are not mere preliminaries to be transcended but permanent aspects of the soul's loving service to God.
