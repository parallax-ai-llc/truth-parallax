## Overview

Madhva's Dvaita reading of the Kena Upanishad emphasizes God's absolute transcendence over all created beings, including the most powerful gods. The opening question about who directs the mind and senses is answered definitively: Vishnu alone is the supreme controller who empowers all faculties while remaining forever beyond their reach.

## Theological Emphases

The parable of the gods (Chapters 3-4) is particularly important for Dvaita because it demonstrates the *taratamya* (hierarchical ordering) of beings. Agni, Vayu, and Indra — among the highest gods — cannot even identify Brahman, let alone match His power. This establishes God's infinite superiority over all souls and deities. The verse "It is known to those who do not know it" (2.3) is read not as a paradox about non-dual awareness but as affirming that God's nature always exceeds human comprehension — the truly wise know the limits of their knowledge.
