---
title: "Kena Upanishad - Chapter 1"
scripture: "kena-upanishad"
chapter: 1
---

**1** By whom willed and directed does the mind light on its objects? By whom commanded does life first move? By whom willed do men utter this speech? What god directs the eye and ear?

**2** It is the ear of the ear, the mind of the mind, the speech of speech, the breath of breath, and the eye of the eye. When freed from the senses the wise, on departing from this world, become immortal.

**3** There the eye goes not; speech goes not, nor the mind. We know not, we understand not, how one would teach it.

**4** Other it is, indeed, than the known, and moreover above the unknown. Thus we have heard from those of old who taught us this.

**5** That which is not expressed by speech and by which speech is expressed; that alone know as Brahman, not that which people here adore.

**6** That which thinks not by mind, and by which, they say, mind is thought; that alone know as Brahman, not that which people here adore.

**7** That which sees not by the eye, and by which one sees the eyes; that alone know as Brahman, not that which people here adore.

**8** That which hears not by the ear, and by which the ear is heard; that alone know as Brahman, not that which people here adore.

**9** That which breathes not by the breath, and by which breath is drawn; that alone know as Brahman, not that which people here adore.
