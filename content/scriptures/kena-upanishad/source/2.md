---
title: "Kena Upanishad - Chapter 2"
scripture: "kena-upanishad"
chapter: 2
---

**1** If thou thinkest "I know It well," but little indeed dost thou know the form of the Brahman. That of It which is thou, that of It which is among the gods, this thou hast to think of.

**2** I think It is known. I do not think that I know It well, and yet I know that It is not unknown to me. He among us who knows It best, knows It; he does not think that he does not know It, and yet he knows that It is not unknown.

**3** He by whom It is not thought, by him It is thought; he by whom It is thought, knows It not. It is not understood by those who understand It; It is understood by those who do not understand It.

**4** When It is known through every state of cognition, it is rightly known, for by it one attains immortality. By the Self one obtains strength; by knowledge one obtains immortality.

**5** If a man know this here, that is the true end of life; if he does not know this here, then there is great destruction. The wise, having realized Brahman in every being, depart from this world and become immortal.
