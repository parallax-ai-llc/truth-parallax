---
title: "Kena Upanishad - Chapter 4"
scripture: "kena-upanishad"
chapter: 4
---

**1** She replied, "It is Brahman. It is through the victory of Brahman that you have thus become great." After that he knew that It was Brahman.

**2** Therefore these gods, viz. Agni, Vayu, and Indra, are, as it were, above the other gods, for they touched It nearest, for they and especially Indra first knew It was Brahman.

**3** Therefore Indra is, as it were, above the other gods, for he touched It nearest, for he first knew It was Brahman.

**4** Of Brahman there is this teaching: It is like that which is seen when lightning flashes, or like the winking of the eye. This is its teaching with regard to the gods.

**5** Now with regard to the Self: It is that which seems to move as mind, and by it imagination remembers again and again. That thought is called Tadvanam, and it should be worshipped as Tadvanam. All beings love him who knows this.

**6** The disciple asked: "Sir, tell me the Upanishad." The teacher replied: "The Upanishad has been told to thee. We have told thee the Upanishad of Brahman."

**7** The feet on which that Upanishad stands are austerity, self-restraint, and work; the Vedas are all its limbs; truth is its abode.

**8** He who knows this Upanishad shakes off all evil and becomes established in the most excellent, unlimited, heavenly world. Yea, he becomes established therein.

**9** He who thus knows this, shakes off all sin and becomes established in the infinite and blissful Brahman. Yea, he becomes established therein.
