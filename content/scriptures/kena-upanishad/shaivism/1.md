The Kena Upanishad's opening question — "By whom is the mind directed?" — points directly to what Kashmir Shaivism calls the fundamental mystery of consciousness. The mind, senses, and breath all function, but what is the power that animates them? The Shaiva answer is *chit* — pure awareness, Shiva Himself — who is the eternal subject behind every act of cognition, perception, and vital function.

The teaching that Brahman is "the ear of the ear, the mind of the mind" (1.2) aligns with the Shaiva doctrine of *svaprakasha* — self-luminous consciousness that illuminates all experience without itself being illuminated by anything else. Just as light reveals objects but is not revealed by them, consciousness enables all knowing but cannot be made into a known object. This chapter establishes the epistemological foundation for the Shaiva path of recognition (*pratyabhijna*).
