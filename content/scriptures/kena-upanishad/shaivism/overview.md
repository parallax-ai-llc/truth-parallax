## Overview

Shaiva interpreters find the Kena Upanishad particularly congenial to their philosophical framework. The teaching that Brahman is the consciousness behind all mental and sensory functions aligns precisely with Kashmir Shaivism's central doctrine: Shiva is pure awareness (*chit*) that illuminates all experience while remaining beyond objectification.

## Theological Emphases

The role of Uma (Chapter 4) as the one who reveals Brahman's identity to the gods is especially significant in Shaiva thought. Uma is Shakti — the dynamic, revelatory power of consciousness. That the gods cannot recognize Brahman without her intervention demonstrates the essential role of Shakti in making the transcendent knowable. In Kashmir Shaivism, *vimarsha* (self-reflective awareness, Shakti) is inseparable from *prakasha* (luminosity, Shiva) — knowledge requires both the light of consciousness and the power of recognition.
