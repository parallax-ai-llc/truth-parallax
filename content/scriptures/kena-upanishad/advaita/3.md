Shankara reads the parable of the gods in Chapter 3 as illustrating the fundamental Advaita teaching that all power belongs to Brahman alone. The gods' claim to independent capacity represents the universal error of *ahamkara* (ego-sense) — attributing to the individual self powers that belong to the non-dual Brahman. Agni's inability to burn a blade of grass demonstrates that fire has no inherent power of its own.

For Advaita, this parable applies not only to cosmic deities but to every individual. The sense "I see," "I hear," "I think" is a superimposition (*adhyasa*) of agency upon the ego, which is itself a superimposition upon the one Brahman. When this superimposition is recognized as such, what remains is Brahman alone — the sole reality and sole agent. The gods' experience of powerlessness is a step toward this recognition.
