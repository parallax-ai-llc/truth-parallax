The Kena Upanishad's opening question — "By whom is the mind directed?" — resonates with fundamental questions asked across philosophical and mystical traditions. The Greek philosopher Anaxagoras posited *nous* (mind) as the principle ordering all things, while the Stoics identified *logos* (reason) as the rational principle pervading the cosmos. In the Abrahamic traditions, God is the creator and sustainer of all cognitive faculties — "The hearing ear and the seeing eye, the Lord has made them both" (Proverbs 20:12).

The Upanishad's distinctive contribution is its recursive structure: the power behind the mind is not another mind but the ground of all mental activity. This anticipates the phenomenological insight of Husserl — that consciousness is always "consciousness of" something but is itself not an object — and resonates with the Buddhist teaching that awareness (*vijnana*) cannot be grasped by itself.
