## Overview

The Ziji has attracted significant scholarly attention across multiple disciplines, including religious studies, textual criticism, history of religions, and comparative philosophy. Studied for its literary form, historical claims, and relationship to Buddhist hagiography. Academic inquiry into this text encompasses philological analysis, historical contextualization, and comparative methodology, seeking to understand the text both on its own terms and within the broader landscape of religious literature and thought.

Scholars have examined the textual transmission history, the relationship between oral and written traditions, the cultural and political contexts of composition, and the philosophical and theological arguments presented. The Ziji continues to generate new scholarly insights as methodological approaches evolve and previously unavailable comparative materials become accessible.
