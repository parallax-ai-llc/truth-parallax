Chapter 6 of the Ziji, addressing sangha, holds particular significance within the Bon tradition. The themes of monastic community, ordination are central to Bon theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Bon tradition, this chapter's treatment of sangha is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on monastic community, ordination inform Bon self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
