Chapter 10 of the Ziji, addressing compassion, holds particular significance within the Bon tradition. The themes of universal love, bodhicitta are central to Bon theology and practice, and this chapter has been a focus of traditional interpretation and spiritual reflection.

Within Bon tradition, this chapter's treatment of compassion is understood not merely as historical or theoretical but as a living source of guidance for faith and practice. The teachings on universal love, bodhicitta inform Bon self-understanding and continue to shape communal identity and spiritual aspiration.
