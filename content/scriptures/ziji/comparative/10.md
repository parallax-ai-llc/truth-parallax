A comparative reading of Chapter 10 of the Ziji situates its treatment of compassion within the broader context of world religious literature. The themes of universal love, bodhicitta find parallels and contrasts in multiple traditions, offering rich material for cross-cultural analysis.

Comparative scholars have identified resonances between this chapter's teachings and similar discussions in other scriptures and philosophical texts. These parallels illuminate both universal patterns in human religious thought and the distinctive contributions of Bon to the world's understanding of compassion.
