A comparative reading of Chapter 3 of the Ziji situates its treatment of education within the broader context of world religious literature. The themes of learning, mastery find parallels and contrasts in multiple traditions, offering rich material for cross-cultural analysis.

Comparative scholars have identified resonances between this chapter's teachings and similar discussions in other scriptures and philosophical texts. These parallels illuminate both universal patterns in human religious thought and the distinctive contributions of Bon to the world's understanding of education.
