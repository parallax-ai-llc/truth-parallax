---
title: "Ziji Section 7: The Dzogchen Teachings"
scripture: "ziji"
chapter: 7
---

Dzogchen, the Great Perfection, is the highest teaching of the Bon tradition. It is called the Great Perfection because in the natural state of mind, everything is already perfect, complete, and fully realized. There is nothing to add and nothing to remove. The only obstacle is the failure to recognize what is already present.

The Dzogchen teachings are divided into three series. The Mind Series introduces the practitioner to the nature of mind through analytical investigation, leading to the direct experience that the mind's essence is emptiness, its nature is clarity, and its energy is unceasing compassion. The Space Series emphasizes the inseparability of emptiness and appearance, training the practitioner to recognize that all phenomena, without exception, are the spontaneous display of the primordial state.

The Secret Instruction Series, the most direct of the three, introduces the practitioner to the natural state through the direct pointing-out instruction of a realized master. In this introduction, which is called rigpa or pure awareness, the practitioner recognizes their own nature as identical with the primordial state described in the first chapter of this text. This recognition is not an intellectual understanding but a direct, non-conceptual experience that transforms the practitioner's entire relationship with reality.

The practice of Dzogchen after the initial recognition consists simply in maintaining and deepening that recognition through all activities of daily life. The practitioner learns to rest in the natural state during sitting meditation and to carry that recognition into walking, eating, speaking, and all other activities. Gradually, the recognition becomes continuous, and the distinction between meditation and daily life dissolves.

The fruition of Dzogchen practice is the rainbow body, in which the physical body dissolves into light at the time of death, leaving behind only the hair and nails. This extraordinary achievement has been documented throughout the history of the Bon tradition and serves as the ultimate demonstration that the physical body is nothing other than the crystallization of awareness, which returns to its luminous nature when all obscurations have been purified.
