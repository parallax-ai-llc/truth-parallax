---
title: "Ziji Section 3: The Master Tonpa Shenrab"
scripture: "ziji"
chapter: 3
---

The great master Tonpa Shenrab Miwoche descended from the celestial realms to bring the teachings of Bon to the beings of this world. Born as a prince in the land of Olmo Lungring, a mystical country situated to the west of Tibet and shaped like an eight-petaled lotus, he renounced his royal inheritance to dedicate his life to the liberation of all sentient beings.

Olmo Lungring is described as a land of perfection, where the teachings of Bon have always been known and practiced. At its center rises Mount Yungdrung Gutseg, the nine-stacked swastika mountain, from whose summit four rivers flow toward the four cardinal directions, nourishing the surrounding lands. This sacred geography mirrors the structure of the mandala and represents the ideal order that the Bon teachings seek to establish in the world.

Tonpa Shenrab's life is recounted in three levels of biographical literature. The brief account, the Dodu, summarizes his twelve great deeds. The medium account, the Zermig, tells his life story in greater detail across sixty-one chapters. The extensive account, the Ziji, which is this very text, presents the most complete narrative of his life, teachings, and activities in twelve volumes.

Among his twelve great deeds, Tonpa Shenrab's descent from the celestial realms, his teaching of the nine ways, his conquest of the demon prince Khyabpa Lagring, and his final passing into nirvana are the most celebrated. His confrontation with the demon prince is particularly significant, for it represents the victory of wisdom over ignorance, of compassion over cruelty, and of the Bon teachings over the forces that seek to keep beings trapped in suffering.

Tonpa Shenrab is not merely a historical figure but the manifestation of enlightened awareness in human form. His teachings continue to be transmitted through an unbroken lineage of realized masters who have preserved them through centuries of political upheaval, persecution, and exile.
