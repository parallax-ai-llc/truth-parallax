---
title: "Ziji Section 9: The Cosmology of Bon"
scripture: "ziji"
chapter: 9
---

The Bon cosmology describes a universe of immense scope and complexity, populated by countless classes of beings dwelling in multiple realms of existence. At the center of the world system rises Mount Meru, the cosmic axis, surrounded by concentric rings of mountains and oceans and the four continents oriented to the cardinal directions.

The six realms of cyclic existence encompass the full range of samsaric experience. The three higher realms are those of the gods, who enjoy immense pleasure but are ultimately subject to suffering when their merit is exhausted; the jealous gods, who possess great power but are tormented by envy and conflict; and the humans, who alone possess the perfect balance of pleasure and pain necessary to motivate spiritual practice.

The three lower realms are those of the animals, who are driven by ignorance and instinct; the hungry ghosts, who are tormented by insatiable craving; and the hell beings, who experience intense suffering as the result of extremely negative actions. These realms are not permanent abodes but temporary stations through which consciousness migrates according to the momentum of its accumulated actions.

Beyond these six realms, the Bon cosmology describes pure lands created by the compassion and wisdom of enlightened beings. These pure lands serve as ideal environments for spiritual practice, free from the obstacles and distractions of samsaric existence. The most important of these in the Bon tradition is the pure land of Shenla Okar, the Buddha of Compassion, to which practitioners may aspire to be reborn through the power of prayer and virtuous action.

The cosmological teachings serve a practical purpose in the Bon tradition. By contemplating the vastness of the universe and the innumerable beings who inhabit it, the practitioner develops the boundless compassion necessary for the path. By understanding the mechanisms of karma and rebirth that govern movement through the six realms, the practitioner generates the motivation to practice diligently and to help all beings achieve liberation.
