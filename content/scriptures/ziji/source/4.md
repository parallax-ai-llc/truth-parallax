---
title: "Ziji Section 4: The Teachings on Impermanence"
scripture: "ziji"
chapter: 4
---

All conditioned phenomena are impermanent. This is the first of the fundamental contemplations that turn the mind toward the path of liberation. The practitioner is urged to contemplate impermanence not merely as an intellectual concept but as a lived reality that penetrates every aspect of experience.

The external world, which appears so solid and enduring, is in constant flux. Mountains erode, rivers change their courses, civilizations rise and fall. The seasons cycle endlessly, each day bringing changes both subtle and dramatic. Nothing in the external world remains the same from one moment to the next, yet beings cling to the appearance of stability as if it were the truth.

The body, which serves as the basis for this precious human life, is equally impermanent. From the moment of birth, it moves inexorably toward death. The young grow old, the healthy fall ill, the strong become weak. No amount of wealth, power, or knowledge can prevent the body's eventual decay. The practitioner is urged to contemplate their own mortality daily, not from morbid fascination but to generate the urgency needed to practice the teachings while the opportunity exists.

The mind itself, the stream of consciousness that experiences all phenomena, is impermanent in its momentary manifestations even as its fundamental nature remains unchanging. Thoughts arise and dissolve like waves on the ocean, emotions surge and subside like weather patterns, perceptions shift and transform with each passing instant. The practitioner learns to observe this flux without grasping or aversion, allowing each moment of experience to arise, display itself, and dissolve naturally.

The contemplation of impermanence is not a cause for despair but for liberation. When the grip of attachment loosens, when the illusion of permanence is seen through, the mind naturally relaxes into its own fundamental nature, which is beyond birth and death, beyond arising and ceasing. Impermanence is the doorway through which the practitioner enters the timeless awareness that is the heart of the Bon teachings.
