---
title: "Ziji Section 11: Death and the Intermediate States"
scripture: "ziji"
chapter: 11
---

The Bon teachings on death and dying are among the most detailed and practical guides to the dying process found in any spiritual tradition. They describe the stages of death, the intermediate states that follow, and the methods by which a trained practitioner can navigate these states toward liberation or favorable rebirth.

As death approaches, the elements that compose the body dissolve in a specific sequence. Earth dissolves into water, and the dying person experiences a sensation of heaviness and sinking. Water dissolves into fire, and the bodily fluids dry up. Fire dissolves into wind, and the body's warmth withdraws from the extremities. Wind dissolves into consciousness, and the breathing ceases. Each dissolution is accompanied by specific outer signs visible to those attending the dying person and inner signs experienced by the dying person themselves.

After the cessation of breath, consciousness enters the intermediate state, or bardo. The first bardo is the bardo of the moment of death, in which the fundamental luminosity of mind manifests in its naked form. For a practitioner who has trained in recognizing the nature of mind, this is the supreme opportunity for liberation. By recognizing this luminosity as their own true nature, the practitioner achieves enlightenment at the moment of death.

If this recognition does not occur, consciousness enters the bardo of reality, in which the peaceful and wrathful deities manifest as projections of the practitioner's own mind. These visions can be terrifying or blissful, but in either case, the practitioner who has been trained recognizes them as self-display and maintains the recognition of the natural state.

If liberation is not achieved in the bardo of reality, consciousness enters the bardo of becoming, in which the force of accumulated karma propels it toward rebirth. The practitioner who has been trained can direct this process, choosing a favorable rebirth that will provide the best conditions for continuing spiritual practice. The teachings provide detailed instructions for recognizing the signs of each potential realm of rebirth and for directing consciousness toward the most beneficial destination.
