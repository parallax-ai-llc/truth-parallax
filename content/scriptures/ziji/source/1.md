---
title: "Ziji Section 1: The Primordial State"
scripture: "ziji"
chapter: 1
---

In the beginning, before the arising of samsara and nirvana, there existed the unconditioned base, the primordial state of pure awareness. This base is without beginning and without end, without center and without periphery. It is the natural condition of mind before the arising of conceptual thought, the luminous emptiness from which all phenomena emerge and into which all phenomena dissolve.

The Bon teachings call this primordial state the Mother Luminosity, the fundamental ground from which the entire display of existence arises. It is not a void or a nothingness but a dynamic potentiality, pregnant with all possible manifestations yet itself unchanging and unborn. The realized masters of the Bon tradition recognize this state as their own true nature, the face they had before they were born.

From this primordial ground, through the spontaneous arising of creative energy, the three dimensions of being manifest: the dimension of absolute truth, the dimension of perfect enjoyment, and the dimension of manifest emanation. These three dimensions are not separate from the base but are its natural expression, like the rays that emanate from the sun without being separate from it.

The ignorance that gives rise to samsaric existence begins when awareness fails to recognize its own nature. Like a person who looks into a mirror and does not recognize their own face, awareness becomes fixated on its own projections and takes them to be external, independent realities. This initial moment of non-recognition is the root of all suffering, all confusion, and all wandering through the six realms of cyclic existence.

The teachings of the Ziji reveal the path by which awareness can recognize its own nature and return to the primordial state that was never truly lost. This recognition is not the attainment of something new but the uncovering of what has always been present, like clearing clouds from the sky to reveal the sun that never ceased to shine.
