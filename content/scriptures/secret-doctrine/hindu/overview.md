The Hindu reading of the Secret Doctrine recognizes in the Stanzas of Dzyan a creative reinterpretation of Vedantic and Puranic cosmological concepts. The cosmic night (*pralaya*) and cosmic day (*manvantara*) of the Stanzas directly correspond to the Hindu concept of the cyclic creation and dissolution of the universe, while the emanation of the cosmos from the Absolute echoes the Vedantic teaching on Brahman as the source and substance of all existence.

Hindu scholars have offered both appreciative and critical assessments of Blavatsky's use of Hindu concepts. Some have praised her for bringing Hindu cosmological wisdom to Western attention at a time when India's intellectual heritage was widely dismissed; others have noted distortions, decontextualizations, and the subordination of Hindu ideas to a framework that is ultimately Neoplatonic rather than authentically Vedantic.
