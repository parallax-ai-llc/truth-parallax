## Hindu Reading

The Hindu reading of the first Stanza recognizes in its description of cosmic dissolution a creative reinterpretation of the Vedantic and Puranic concept of *mahapralaya* — the great dissolution at the end of Brahma's life-span. The imagery of darkness filling the boundless all echoes the Nasadiya Sukta (Rig Veda 10.129): 'There was neither non-existence nor existence then; there was neither the realm of space nor the sky beyond.' Blavatsky's description captures something of the Vedantic intuition of the Absolute beyond all categories, though Hindu scholars note her departure from strict Vedantic categories.
