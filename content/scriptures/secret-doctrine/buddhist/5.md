## Buddhist Reading

The fifth Stanza's concept of elemental evolution finds some resonance with the Buddhist analysis of materiality into the four great elements (*mahabhuta*): earth, water, fire, and air (with space sometimes added as a fifth). However, the Buddhist treatment of the elements is primarily analytical rather than cosmogonic — the elements are categories for analyzing present experience rather than stages in a temporal process of cosmic evolution. The Buddhist reader notes this important difference while appreciating certain parallels.
