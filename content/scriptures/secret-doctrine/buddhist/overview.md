The Buddhist reading of the Secret Doctrine engages critically with Blavatsky's claim that the Stanzas of Dzyan derive from an esoteric Buddhist text. No such text has been identified in any Buddhist canonical collection, and scholars of Buddhism have noted significant divergences between the Stanzas' cosmology and mainstream Buddhist philosophical positions — particularly the Stanzas' apparent affirmation of an Absolute that generates the universe, which conflicts with the Buddhist teaching of *shunyata* (emptiness) and *pratityasamutpada* (dependent origination).

Nevertheless, certain elements of the Secret Doctrine do reflect genuine Buddhist concepts — the cyclic nature of cosmic time, the progressive evolution of consciousness, and the ultimately illusory nature of the material world. The Buddhist reader may find in the Stanzas a creative, if heterodox, engagement with Buddhist ideas that is more interesting as a document of Buddhist-Western encounter than as an expression of authentic Buddhist teaching.
