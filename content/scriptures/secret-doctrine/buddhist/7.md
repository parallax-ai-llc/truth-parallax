## Buddhist Reading

The seventh Stanza's account of humanity's spiritual origins is examined critically from the Buddhist perspective, which does not teach a single moment of spiritual 'awakening' for the human race but rather a beginningless process of cyclic existence in which individual beings have always possessed the potential for enlightenment. The concept of external beings (*Manasaputras*) implanting the mind in humanity conflicts with the Buddhist emphasis on self-reliance and the Buddha's injunction to 'be a lamp unto yourself.' Nevertheless, the Mahayana concept of bodhisattvas who postpone their own liberation to assist all beings offers a partial parallel.
