## Buddhist Reading

The Buddhist reading of the first Stanza critically examines its description of the cosmic night in relation to Buddhist cosmological concepts. While Buddhist cosmology does recognize cyclic phases of cosmic contraction and expansion, the Stanza's description of an Absolute that persists through the dissolution raises concerns from the Buddhist perspective. The concept of a permanent, underlying reality conflicts with the fundamental Buddhist teaching of *shunyata* (emptiness) — the absence of any permanent, self-existing substance. The Buddhist reader approaches this Stanza with respectful skepticism.
