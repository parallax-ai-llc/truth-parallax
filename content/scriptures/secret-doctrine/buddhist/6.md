## Buddhist Reading

The sixth Stanza's planetary chain concept is assessed in relation to the elaborate Buddhist cosmology of world-systems (*lokadhatu*), which describes multiple world-realms at different levels of materiality inhabited by beings of varying degrees of spiritual development. While the specific concept of a seven-globed chain is not found in Buddhist sources, the broader idea of a multi-layered cosmos with interpenetrating levels of existence does have Buddhist parallels, particularly in Mahayana descriptions of the buddha-fields (*buddhakshetra*).
