## Comparative Reading

The sixth Stanza's concept of the earth's place in a chain of worlds invites comparison with other traditions' accounts of cosmic geography: Buddhist cosmology's Mount Meru and surrounding world-systems, Hindu Puranic cosmography, Kabbalistic descriptions of the four worlds, and indigenous cosmologies that place the earth within a larger spiritual geography. Comparative analysis examines how different traditions understand the relationship between the visible world and the invisible realms that surround and interpenetrate it.
