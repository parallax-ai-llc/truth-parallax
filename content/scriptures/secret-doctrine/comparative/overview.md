Comparative study of the Secret Doctrine examines Blavatsky's ambitious attempt to demonstrate the underlying unity of the world's cosmogonic traditions. The Stanzas of Dzyan are compared with their claimed source traditions — Vedantic cosmology, Buddhist *abhidharma*, Kabbalistic *seder hishtalshelut* (chain of emanation), and Neoplatonic procession — to assess both the accuracy of Blavatsky's representations and the genuineness of the parallels she identifies.

Scholars of the 'perennial philosophy' tradition (Aldous Huxley, Huston Smith) have found in the Secret Doctrine a pioneering attempt at the kind of cross-traditional synthesis they advocate, while critics note the selectivity and sometimes inaccuracy of Blavatsky's comparative method. The Secret Doctrine remains a provocative case study in the possibilities and pitfalls of comparative theology.
