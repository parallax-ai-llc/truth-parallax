The Theosophical reading of the Secret Doctrine receives the Stanzas of Dzyan as authentic transmissions from the Masters of Wisdom (Mahatmas) — advanced spiritual beings who have guided humanity's evolution from behind the scenes of history. For the Theosophist, the Secret Doctrine is not merely a philosophical text but a revelation of the fundamental structure of reality, communicated through H.P. Blavatsky as the appointed agent of the Masters.

The Stanzas describe the process by which the Absolute — the unknowable, infinite ground of all being — emanates the universe through successive stages of spiritual and material evolution. This cosmogony is understood as the key to all world religions, which are seen as partial, exoteric expressions of the one esoteric truth preserved by the Masters. The Theosophical student approaches the Secret Doctrine as a lifetime study, finding in its often opaque symbolism successive layers of meaning that reveal themselves as the student's consciousness develops.
