## Theosophical Reading

The Theosophical reading of the first Stanza contemplates the state of cosmic rest (*pralaya*) — the period between universal cycles when the manifest cosmos has been reabsorbed into the Absolute. Blavatsky describes this as the 'Night of Brahma,' when 'Darkness alone filled the Boundless All.' The Theosophist understands this not as mere nothingness but as the infinite potential of the Absolute — the unconditioned ground of all being that precedes and exceeds every manifestation. The Stanza's symbolic language — the 'Great Breath,' the 'Eternal Parent wrapped in her Ever-Invisible Robes' — is received as authentic descriptions of metaphysical realities that transcend ordinary human comprehension.
