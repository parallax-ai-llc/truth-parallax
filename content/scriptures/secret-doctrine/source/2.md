**1** The Stanzas of Dzyan form the basis of the present work. These stanzas give an abstract formula for the Evolution of the Universe.

**2** Stanza I: "The Eternal Parent, wrapped in her ever-invisible robes, had slumbered once again for seven eternities."

**3** "Time was not, for it lay asleep in the infinite bosom of duration."

**4** "Universal Mind was not, for there were no beings to contain it."

**5** "The seven ways to bliss were not. The great causes of misery were not, for there was no one to produce and get ensnared by them."

**6** "Darkness alone filled the Boundless All, for Father, Mother, and Son were once more one, and the Son had not yet awakened for the new wheel and his pilgrimage thereon."

**7** "The seven sublime Lords and the seven Truths had ceased to be, and the Universe, the son of Necessity, was immersed in Paranishpanna, to be outbreathed by that which is, and yet is not."

**8** "Nothing existed. Neither being nor non-being."

**9** "Alone the one form of existence stretched boundless, infinite, causeless, in dreamless sleep; and life pulsated unconscious in universal space."

**10** This is the description of Pralaya — the state of universal dissolution that precedes every new cycle of manifestation.
