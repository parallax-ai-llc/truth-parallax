## Academic Reading

Scholars examine the second Stanza's cosmogonic imagery in relation to contemporary 19th-century scientific and philosophical debates about the origins of the universe. Blavatsky's concept of the 'Germ of Life' is compared with the nebular hypothesis in astronomy and with vitalist theories in biology. The Stanza's language is analyzed for its debts to Romantic natural philosophy (Schelling, Hegel) and to the Hindu concept of *hiranyagarbha* (the golden cosmic egg).
