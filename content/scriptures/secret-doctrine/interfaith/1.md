## Interfaith Reading

The first Stanza's vision of cosmic dissolution and rest opens interfaith dialogue about how different traditions understand the ultimate origin and destiny of the universe. The image of a state before creation — whether described as the 'Night of Brahma,' the *tohu va-vohu* ('formless void') of Genesis, or the Buddhist concept of cosmic contraction — is a remarkably widespread motif that suggests a shared human intuition about the mystery that precedes and encompasses all existence.
