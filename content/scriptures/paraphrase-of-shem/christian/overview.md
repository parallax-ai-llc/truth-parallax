## Overview

For Christian readers, the Paraphrase of Shem challenges the biblical creation account at the most fundamental level. Where Genesis describes creation by a single, good God who declares his work 'very good,' the Paraphrase describes the cosmos as the product of a three-way cosmic conflict in which the material world is an unfortunate mixture to be unmixed. The concept of light trapped in matter resonates distantly with the Johannine theme of light shining in darkness (John 1:5) but develops it in a direction that denies the goodness of creation.
