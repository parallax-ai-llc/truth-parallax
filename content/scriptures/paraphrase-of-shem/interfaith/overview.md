## Overview

The Paraphrase of Shem addresses what may be the deepest question in religious thought: why does evil exist? The text's answer — that the material world results from a primordial conflict between eternal principles of Light and Darkness — represents the dualistic solution to the problem of evil, a solution that appears in various forms across traditions: Zoroastrian dualism, Manichaean cosmogony, aspects of Kabbalistic thought (the sitra achra, the 'other side'), and the universal human experience of light and darkness as fundamental spiritual categories.
