## Overview

The Paraphrase of Shem (NHC VII,1) is one of the longest and most complex cosmogonic texts in the Nag Hammadi library. It presents a revelation to Shem — identified as the eldest son of Noah — in which he is shown the origin of the cosmos from the interaction of three primordial principles: Light (above), Darkness (below), and Spirit (between). The cosmogonic drama begins when Darkness, moved by envy, gazes upward and mixes with Spirit, creating the material world as a confused blend of spiritual and dark elements. The rest of the text describes the progressive liberation of the light particles trapped in this mixture through a series of revelations, descents of salvific figures (including Derdekeas, a Christ-like revealer), and cosmic transformations. The text culminates in the final separation of Light from Darkness and the restoration of the original cosmic order. Hippolytus of Rome may refer to this text (or a closely related one) in his account of a Sethian system in *Refutatio* 5.19-22.

## Structure

| Section | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 1 | The Three Roots: Light, Darkness, Spirit | The primordial state of the three principles before the cosmogonic crisis |
| 2 | The Primordial Conflict | Darkness's upward gaze triggers the mixing of Spirit and Darkness |
| 3 | The Formation of the World | The material world emerges from the confused mixture of the three principles |
| 4 | The Entrapment of Light | Light particles are scattered throughout the material world, trapped in material forms |
| 5 | The Coming of the Savior | Derdekeas descends to begin the process of liberating the trapped light |
| 6 | The Process of Liberation | The progressive extraction of light from matter through revelation and cosmic events |
| 7 | The Final Separation | The complete separation of Light from Darkness and the restoration of the original order |

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Gnostic**: A major cosmogonic text presenting a distinctive three-root system that differs from both Valentinian and standard Sethian models
- **Academic**: Studied for its unique cosmogony, its relationship to Hippolytus's reports, and its complex literary history
- **Comparative**: Offers parallels to Zoroastrian and Manichaean light-darkness dualism and to Hindu cosmogonic texts
- **Christian**: Raises questions about the diversity of creation accounts in early Christian-adjacent movements
- **Jewish**: Transforms the Genesis figure of Shem into a receiver of primordial cosmogonic revelation
- **Interfaith**: Addresses universal questions about the origin of evil, the nature of matter, and the possibility of cosmic redemption

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Three Primordial Roots | The cosmos originates from three eternal principles: Light, Darkness, and Spirit |
| Cosmic Mixture | The material world results from the illegitimate mixing of the three principles |
| Entrapped Light | Particles of divine light are scattered throughout matter, awaiting liberation |
| Final Separation | Salvation is the complete separation of light from darkness, restoring the original order |
