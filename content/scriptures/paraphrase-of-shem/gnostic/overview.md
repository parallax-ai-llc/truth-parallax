## Overview

For Gnostic readers, the Paraphrase of Shem presents a distinctive cosmogonic vision in which the cosmos originates from the interaction of three eternal principles — Light, Darkness, and Spirit — rather than from the fall of a single divine figure (Sophia or the Logos). The drama begins when Darkness, envious of the Light above, gazes upward and mixes with Spirit, creating the confused material world. The Gnostic reader understands this as an account of how divine light came to be entrapped in matter and how the revealer Derdekeas descends to initiate the progressive liberation of the scattered sparks.
