## Overview

The Paraphrase of Shem's three-principle cosmogony invites comparison with dualistic creation accounts worldwide. The Zoroastrian conflict between Ahura Mazda (Light) and Angra Mainyu (Darkness) provides the closest structural parallel, and scholars have long debated whether Gnostic dualism derives from Iranian sources. The Manichaean cosmogony — in which the Kingdom of Light is invaded by the Kingdom of Darkness, trapping particles of light in matter — develops a similar theme with greater systematic rigor. Hindu Samkhya philosophy's account of the interaction between purusha (consciousness/spirit) and prakriti (matter) offers a non-mythological parallel to the Gnostic three-root scheme.
