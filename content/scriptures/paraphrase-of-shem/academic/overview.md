## Overview

The Paraphrase of Shem is one of the most complex and debated texts in the Nag Hammadi library. Its three-root cosmogony (Light, Darkness, Spirit) differs from both Valentinian and standard Sethian systems, making its classification difficult. Hippolytus of Rome may refer to a related text in his account of a 'Sethian' system (Refutatio 5.19-22), though the correspondence is not exact. Scholars have debated the text's relationship to Manichaean cosmogony, which also features a primordial conflict between Light and Darkness, though the role of Spirit as a third principle is distinctive.
