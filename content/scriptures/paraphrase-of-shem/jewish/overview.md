## Overview

Jewish readers encounter in the Paraphrase of Shem a radical alternative to the Genesis creation account. The transformation of Shem — the eldest son of Noah and ancestor of the Semitic peoples — from a Genesis figure into the recipient of cosmogonic revelation follows the typical Gnostic pattern of appropriating biblical characters for non-biblical purposes. The three-root cosmogony, with its eternal Darkness existing alongside and in conflict with Light, contradicts the Jewish affirmation of God as the sole creator (Isaiah 45:7: 'I form the light and create the darkness').
