## Overview

Sunni appreciation for the Risala al-Qushayriyya centers on its demonstration that authentic Sufism is fully compatible with Sunni orthodoxy. Qushayri, himself a distinguished Ash'ari theologian and Shafi'i jurist, wrote the Risala partly to counter criticism from scholars who saw Sufism as incompatible with normative Islam, and partly to correct Sufis who had abandoned the standards of Islamic law.

## Interpretive Approach

Sunni readers value Qushayri's insistence that every genuine Sufi master was also a faithful observer of Islamic law. The biographical section demonstrates this by highlighting the legal learning and orthodox practice of each master. The Risala thus serves as evidence that Sufism, properly understood, enhances rather than undermines Sunni religious practice.

## Significance

The Risala played a crucial role in the "sunni-fication" of Sufism — the process by which mystical practice was integrated into mainstream Sunni Islam. Together with Ghazali's Ihya (composed a generation later), the Risala helped establish the consensus that the inner life of the heart is not a heterodox addition to Islam but its essential spiritual dimension.
