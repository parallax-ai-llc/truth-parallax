Interfaith reading of Risala al-Qushayriyya section 1, "The Lives of the Sufi Masters," explores how the spiritual teachings presented here resonate across religious boundaries. The themes of this section — the journey of the soul, the disciplines of the spiritual life, the relationship between effort and grace — are concerns shared by contemplative practitioners in every tradition.

Interfaith readers find in Qushayri's systematic presentation a common vocabulary for discussing the inner life that transcends doctrinal differences. While the theological framework is distinctively Islamic, the experiential realities it describes — the turning of the heart, the struggle with self-deception, the taste of divine presence — are recognized by practitioners of prayer and meditation across the world's religions.
