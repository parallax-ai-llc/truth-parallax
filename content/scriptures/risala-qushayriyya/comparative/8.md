Comparative reading of Risala al-Qushayriyya section 8, "Love and Longing," places Qushayri's systematic account of the mystical path in dialogue with parallel traditions of contemplative practice. The spiritual concepts and experiences described in this section find analogues in Christian mystical theology, Buddhist meditation theory, Hindu yoga, and other contemplative traditions.

These parallels illuminate both shared structures and irreducible differences. The human encounter with transcendence appears to generate similar experiential patterns across cultures — stages of purification, moments of illumination, and the ultimate challenge of integrating ordinary life with transcendent awareness. Yet the theological frameworks within which these experiences are interpreted differ profoundly, shaping how practitioners understand and respond to what they undergo.
