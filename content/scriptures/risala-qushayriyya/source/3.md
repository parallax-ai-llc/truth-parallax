## On Spiritual Struggle

**1.** Spiritual struggle is the combat of the soul against its own lower tendencies. It is the inner jihad, which the Prophet called "the greater jihad."

**2.** The Prophet returned from a battle and said: "We have returned from the lesser jihad to the greater jihad." They asked: "What is the greater jihad?" He said: "The struggle against the self."

**3.** The self is the greatest enemy, for it knows your weaknesses from within. The external enemy can be seen and avoided; the internal enemy is always present.

**4.** The tools of spiritual struggle are four: reducing food, reducing sleep, reducing speech, and reducing social interaction. Through these reductions, the self is weakened and the spirit is strengthened.

**5.** Abu Ali al-Daqqaq said: "Whoever adorns his outward with spiritual struggle, God will adorn his inward with witnessing."

**6.** The struggle is not against the self's existence but against its tyranny. The self is not to be destroyed—it is to be tamed, trained, and redirected.

**7.** The wild horse, once broken, is the noblest of mounts. The self, once tamed, is the most faithful of servants.

**8.** The spiritual struggle is hardest at the beginning. Once the self has been accustomed to discipline, it submits willingly, and what was once painful becomes pleasurable.

**9.** Sahl al-Tustari said: "The first step on the path is the struggle against the self, and the last step on the path is the annihilation of the self in God."

**10.** Do not be discouraged by failure, for failure is part of the struggle. The soldier who falls and rises again is more honored than the soldier who never fought.

**11.** The masters said: "Whoever thinks he can reach God without struggle is dreaming. And whoever thinks he can reach God through struggle alone is presumptuous."

**12.** Struggle is necessary, but it is not sufficient. The final step is grace—the gift of God that descends upon the struggling heart and completes what effort alone could not.

**13.** Abu Yazid said: "I struggled against my self for thirty years before I found anything. Then one night, I gave up the struggle, and in that surrender, I found everything."

**14.** This does not mean that the thirty years were wasted. Without the thirty years of struggle, the surrender would not have been possible. The struggle prepared the ground; the grace planted the seed.

**15.** O seeker, struggle with patience and trust in God's mercy, for the mercy is greater than the struggle, and the One who commands the struggle is the same One who grants the victory.
