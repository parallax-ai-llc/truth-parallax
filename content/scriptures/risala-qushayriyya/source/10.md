## On the Stations of Love and Gnosis

**1.** Love is the supreme station on the path to God. All other stations are steps leading to it, and all other virtues are branches growing from it.

**2.** God says: "He loves them and they love Him." The love of the servant for God is a response to God's love for the servant, for God loved first.

**3.** Al-Junayd was asked about love. He said: "The annihilation of the lover in the attributes of the Beloved." This means that the lover's own qualities disappear, replaced by the qualities of the One he loves.

**4.** Sahl al-Tustari said: "Love is to obey the Beloved and to abandon everything that distracts from Him."

**5.** Dhu al-Nun said: "Love is a fire in the heart that burns everything except the will of the Beloved."

**6.** Abu Yazid said: "The lovers of God are those whom God has stripped of their own attributes and clothed in His attributes, so that when you look at them, you see Him."

**7.** The stations of love are many: inclination, attachment, devotion, passion, and annihilation. At each station, the lover gives up more of himself and receives more of the Beloved.

**8.** At the station of annihilation, the lover no longer exists as a separate being. He has been absorbed in the Beloved as the moth is absorbed in the flame.

**9.** But the masters cautioned against speaking too freely about annihilation, for the tongue cannot convey what the heart experiences in this station. Words about annihilation are like words about the taste of honey to one who has never tasted it.

**10.** Gnosis—ma'rifa—is the intimate knowledge of God that comes through love. It is not the knowledge of the scholar, which comes through study, but the knowledge of the lover, which comes through union.

**11.** The gnostic knows God not through books or arguments but through direct experience. He has tasted what others only describe.

**12.** Ma'ruf al-Karkhi said: "Gnosis is that you know God with your heart, not with your tongue."

**13.** The gnostic is silent about what he knows, for what he knows cannot be spoken. If he speaks, he speaks in metaphor and allusion, and only the one who shares his experience understands.

**14.** The end of the path is the beginning. The gnostic returns to the beginning—to the declaration "There is no god but God"—and finds that it contains everything he has been seeking.

**15.** For in the end, there is only God: the Beloved, the Lover, and the Love are one. The journey, the traveler, and the destination are one. The seeker, the sought, and the seeking are one. And in this unity, all questions are answered, all longings are fulfilled, and all separations come to an end.
