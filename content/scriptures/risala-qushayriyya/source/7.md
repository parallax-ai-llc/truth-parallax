## On Silence and Stillness

**1.** Silence is the garden of reflection, and stillness is the soil in which wisdom grows. The one who does not know when to be silent does not know when to speak.

**2.** The masters valued silence highly, for most sins enter the world through the tongue, and most distractions enter the heart through noise.

**3.** Al-Funayd said: "The foundation of Sufism is built upon five things: eating little, seeking refuge in God, fleeing from people, remembering God, and being silent."

**4.** Silence is of three kinds: the silence of the tongue, the silence of the heart, and the silence of the spirit.

**5.** The silence of the tongue is to refrain from speech. The silence of the heart is to refrain from inner chatter. The silence of the spirit is to rest in the presence of God without any movement, inward or outward.

**6.** The third silence is the silence of the saints, and it is the most eloquent speech, for in that silence, God speaks, and the servant listens.

**7.** Abu Bakr al-Warraq said: "When the servant is silent, three things pray for him: his heart, his faith, and his knowledge. But when he speaks, three things may testify against him."

**8.** Stillness is the sister of silence. The body that is always moving is the body of one whose heart is always agitated. The body that is still reflects a heart that has found peace.

**9.** The masters practiced stillness as a discipline. They would sit for hours in one position, not because they were inactive, but because their inner activity had reached a depth that required outer stillness.

**10.** The one who cannot sit still cannot pray deeply, for deep prayer requires the stilling of the body so that the heart can soar.

**11.** Do not confuse stillness with laziness. The stillness of the mystic is the stillness of the loaded bow, not the stillness of the empty room.

**12.** In stillness, the heart hears what the noise of the world drowns out: the quiet voice of God, the whisper of the spirit, the murmur of meaning beneath the surface of things.

**13.** Dhun-Nun said: "Silence is the language of the learned, as speaking is the language of the ignorant."

**14.** Practice silence daily. Set aside time in which you do not speak, do not read, do not listen—simply be. In that being, you will discover a Presence that words could never reveal.

**15.** And when you break your silence, let your words be few, purposeful, and true. The word that emerges from silence carries a weight that the word born of noise can never possess.
