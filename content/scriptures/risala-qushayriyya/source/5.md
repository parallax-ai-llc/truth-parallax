## On Piety and Scrupulousness

**1.** Piety is the guarding of the heart and the limbs from everything that displeases God. It is the armor of the believer on the battlefield of the world.

**2.** God says: "The most honored of you in the sight of God is the most pious." Piety is the only standard of rank in the divine estimation.

**3.** Scrupulousness is a higher degree of piety. Piety avoids the clearly forbidden; scrupulousness avoids even the doubtful.

**4.** The Prophet said: "Leave what causes you doubt for what does not cause you doubt." The scrupulous man is the one who leaves the doubtful, even when the scholars permit it.

**5.** Ibrahim ibn Adham said: "Scrupulousness is to leave every doubtful thing. And the scrupulousness of the scrupulous is to leave what is not doubtful, for fear that it may contain what is doubtful."

**6.** The degrees of piety are three: to avoid the forbidden, to avoid the doubtful, and to avoid excess in the lawful. The third is the piety of the saints.

**7.** The first degree protects the religion. The second protects the heart. The third protects the spirit.

**8.** Dhu al-Nun said: "The sign of piety is that when something doubtful comes before you, your heart recoils from it without deliberation."

**9.** This natural recoiling is the result of long practice. In the beginning, the scrupulous man must deliberate and examine. In the end, his heart recognizes the doubtful instinctively.

**10.** Be scrupulous in what you eat, for the body is built from what it consumes. If the building material is lawful, the building is sound. If it is forbidden, the building is rotten.

**11.** Be scrupulous in what you earn, for the source of income colors every transaction that follows. The money earned through injustice corrupts every purchase made with it.

**12.** Be scrupulous in what you say, for the tongue is the most difficult of the limbs to control, and the sins of the tongue are the most numerous.

**13.** Be scrupulous in what you look at, for the gaze is the gateway to the heart, and what enters through the gaze settles in the heart.

**14.** The masters said: "If you are scrupulous, God will open for you the door of knowledge. If you are heedless, He will close it."

**15.** Piety is not the joyless restriction that the world imagines. It is the liberation of the heart from all that weighs it down, and the opening of the soul to all that lifts it up.
