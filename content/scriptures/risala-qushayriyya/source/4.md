## On Seclusion and Withdrawal

**1.** Seclusion is the practice of withdrawing from the company of people in order to devote oneself entirely to the company of God.

**2.** The masters differed on whether seclusion is superior to social engagement. Some said seclusion is better because it protects the heart from the distractions and diseases of society. Others said engagement is better because it provides opportunities for patience, generosity, and service.

**3.** The truth is that the best practice depends on the state of the seeker. The beginner who has not yet tamed his self may benefit more from seclusion. The advanced who has tamed his self may benefit more from engagement.

**4.** Ibrahim ibn Adham said: "I withdrew from people not because I despised them but because I despised my own behavior among them."

**5.** The danger of social interaction is that it invites the diseases of the tongue—backbiting, gossip, flattery, and argumentation—and the diseases of the heart—envy, ostentation, and competition.

**6.** The danger of seclusion is that it invites self-delusion, spiritual pride, and the neglect of the duties one owes to others.

**7.** The ideal is inner seclusion combined with outward engagement. The saint who has achieved this walks among people while his heart is with God.

**8.** Al-Junayd said: "The Sufi is with people outwardly and with God inwardly." This is the perfection of seclusion—to be alone with God in the midst of the crowd.

**9.** When you practice physical seclusion, set a limit to it. Forty days was the practice of Moses on Mount Sinai, and many of the masters adopted this period as the standard retreat.

**10.** During seclusion, occupy yourself with the remembrance of God, the recitation of the Quran, prayer, and reflection. Do not waste the time of seclusion in idle thought.

**11.** The solitude of the body is valuable, but the solitude of the heart is essential. The one who is physically alone but mentally in the marketplace has not achieved seclusion.

**12.** Abu Sa'id al-Kharraz said: "The true retreat is the retreat of the heart from all that is not God."

**13.** The fruit of genuine seclusion is clarity of heart. When the water is still, the reflection is clear. When the heart is still, the reflection of God is clear.

**14.** Return from seclusion refreshed and with greater compassion for people, for the one who returns from God's company without increased love for God's creatures has not truly been in God's company.

**15.** The purpose of seclusion is not to escape the world but to return to it with renewed vision—to see God in every face and to serve God in every creature.
