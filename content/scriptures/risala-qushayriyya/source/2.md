## On Repentance

**1.** Repentance is the first station on the path to God, for the journey cannot begin until the traveler turns from the wrong direction to the right one.

**2.** God says: "Turn to God in repentance, all of you, O believers, that you may succeed." Repentance is not for the sinners alone—it is for the believers as well.

**3.** The Prophet himself asked God's forgiveness seventy times a day—or in some reports, a hundred times. If the most perfect of men repents constantly, how much more should we?

**4.** The masters distinguished between the repentance of the common people, which is repentance from sins; the repentance of the elect, which is repentance from heedlessness; and the repentance of the elect of the elect, which is repentance from seeing anything other than God.

**5.** Sahl al-Tustari said: "Repentance is to never forget your sin." Meaning that the awareness of one's sinfulness should be a constant companion, preventing pride and complacency.

**6.** Others said: "Repentance is to forget your sin entirely." Meaning that the servant should be so absorbed in God's mercy that the memory of sin does not distract him.

**7.** These two sayings are not contradictory: the first is for the beginner, who needs the humility that comes from remembering his sin. The second is for the advanced, who has gone beyond the self-consciousness of sin to the God-consciousness of mercy.

**8.** The conditions of repentance are three: to stop the sin, to regret having committed it, and to resolve never to return to it. If the sin involved the rights of another person, a fourth condition is added: to restore those rights.

**9.** Abu Ali al-Daqqaq said: "Repentance is of three kinds: the first is repentance, the second is returning, and the third is cleansing. Repentance belongs to the believers, returning belongs to the saints, and cleansing belongs to the prophets."

**10.** The believer repents from sin. The saint returns from heedlessness. The prophet is cleansed from the perception of otherness.

**11.** Do not delay repentance, for delay itself is a sin. The one who says "I will repent tomorrow" has added the sin of procrastination to his other sins.

**12.** Al-Junayd said: "Repentance has three meanings: the first is remorse, the second is the resolve to abandon sin, and the third is the righting of wrongs."

**13.** The sign of true repentance is that you find the sin more hateful after repenting than you found it pleasant before. If the sin still attracts you, your repentance is incomplete.

**14.** God says: "God loves those who repent and loves those who purify themselves." The repentant servant is not merely forgiven—he is loved.

**15.** And this is the great mercy: that the very act of turning away from sin and turning toward God is itself a gift from God, for no one turns to God unless God has first turned to him.
