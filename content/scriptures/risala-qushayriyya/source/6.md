## On Renunciation

**1.** Renunciation is to turn away from the world, not with the body but with the heart. The true renunciant may possess the world, but the world does not possess him.

**2.** Al-Junayd said: "Renunciation is the emptying of the hand of possessions and the emptying of the heart of wanting possessions."

**3.** The masters distinguished between the renunciation of the common, which is to leave what is forbidden; the renunciation of the elect, which is to leave what is excessive in the lawful; and the renunciation of the elect of the elect, which is to leave everything that distracts from God.

**4.** Abu Sulayman al-Darani said: "Do not consider it renunciation to leave the world and then wish for it in your heart. True renunciation is when the world leaves your heart."

**5.** The sign of true renunciation is equanimity: the renunciant does not rejoice when wealth comes to him, nor does he grieve when it departs. He is the same in poverty and abundance.

**6.** Yahya ibn Mu'adh said: "Renunciation is to give up three things: the world, people, and the self. To give up the world is the beginning. To give up people is the middle. To give up the self is the end."

**7.** The renunciation of the self is the hardest of all, for the self clings to itself more tightly than it clings to any worldly thing.

**8.** The renunciant does not condemn those who enjoy the world, for he knows that the world was created to be used, not condemned. He simply does not need it.

**9.** Sari al-Saqati said: "The renunciant does not desire the world, and the knower does not desire the next world—he desires only God."

**10.** This is the highest renunciation: to renounce even Paradise, not because Paradise is undesirable, but because the lover desires only the Beloved, not the Beloved's gifts.

**11.** The Prophet possessed the world when it came to him and gave it away when it was needed. He was neither attached nor averse. This is the model of renunciation.

**12.** Do not make renunciation a source of pride, for the man who is proud of his renunciation has merely exchanged one idol for another.

**13.** The goal of renunciation is freedom. The heart that is free from attachment is free for God. The heart that is occupied with the world has no room for the Real.

**14.** As Rabi'a al-Adawiyya said: "I have made You the Companion of my heart, but my body is available to those who desire to see me. My body is friendly with the guest, but the Beloved of my heart is the guest of my soul."

**15.** Renounce all that separates you from God, and you will find that what remains is sufficient—indeed, it is everything.
