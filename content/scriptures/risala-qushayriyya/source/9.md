## On Hope and Longing

**1.** Hope is the expectation of God's mercy, and longing is the burning desire to be near Him. Together, they form the upward pull that draws the soul toward its Lord.

**2.** Abu Ali al-Daqqaq said: "Fear drives the servant, and hope leads him, and love carries him."

**3.** Hope is not the lazy expectation of one who does nothing and expects everything. It is the hope of the farmer who has planted his field and awaits the rain—he has done his part and trusts God to do His.

**4.** The masters said: "Hope without action is delusion. Action without hope is despair. The believer is the one who acts and hopes."

**5.** Yahya ibn Mu'adh said: "A single atom of hope is preferable to a thousand years of worship, for hope is a bridge to God and worship without hope is a wall."

**6.** Longing is a higher station than hope. The hopeful man hopes for God's favor. The longing man burns for God's presence. Hope is cool; longing is fire.

**7.** The Prophet said: "O God, I ask You for the longing to meet You." If the Prophet asked for longing, it is because longing is not a natural state—it is a gift from God.

**8.** When longing seizes the heart, nothing in the world can satisfy it. The longing man wanders through the world like a lost child looking for its mother—nothing consoles him until he finds what he seeks.

**9.** Abu Yazid said: "The longing of the knower for God is greater than the longing of the lover for the beloved, for the knower has tasted what he longs for, and the taste makes the longing more intense."

**10.** This is the paradox of spiritual longing: the closer you get, the more intense the longing becomes. It is not the longing of absence but the longing of presence—the longing for more of what you have already found.

**11.** The sign of longing is restlessness. The longing heart cannot rest in any station, for every station is a veil before the next. It presses on, driven by a fire that no station can extinguish.

**12.** Dhu al-Nun said: "The lover is the one whose longing never ceases, whose thirst is never quenched, and whose journey never ends."

**13.** But the journey of longing is itself a joy, for the one who longs for God is in God's company even in the longing. The longing is the proof of the connection.

**14.** God says to the one who longs for Him: "If you long for Me, I long for you more. If you seek Me, I have already found you."

**15.** O seeker, let your longing be your guide, for it points in the direction of the Beloved. Follow it past every distraction, past every station, past every veil, until you arrive at the Presence that your heart has always known.
