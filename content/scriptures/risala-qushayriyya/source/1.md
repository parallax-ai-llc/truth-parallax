## On the Science of Sufism and Its Foundations

**1.** In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. Know that God Most High has singled out from among His servants a group whom He has chosen for His companionship and selected for His love.

**2.** He has purified their hearts from the darkness of heedlessness and illuminated them with the light of direct witnessing. These are the Sufis, and their science is the science of the heart.

**3.** The foundation of this science is the Book of God and the practice of His Messenger, peace be upon him. Any mysticism that contradicts the Book and the practice is falsehood.

**4.** The early masters of this path—al-Junayd, Sahl al-Tustari, Abu Yazid al-Bistami, Dhu al-Nun al-Misri—were all people of the outward law before they became people of the inward reality.

**5.** The outward and the inward are two wings of one bird. The bird that has only one wing cannot fly.

**6.** The Sufis said: "Whoever does not memorize the Quran and study the traditions of the Prophet has no authority in this matter, for our path is bound by the Book and the practice."

**7.** Al-Junayd said: "Our path is bound by the fundamentals of the Book and the practice. Whoever has not memorized the Quran, has not written down the traditions, and has not studied jurisprudence is not fit to be followed."

**8.** The science of Sufism is the science of the states and stations of the heart on its journey to God. A state is what descends upon the heart without effort; a station is what is achieved through effort and practice.

**9.** The states include contraction, expansion, awe, intimacy, longing, and bewilderment. The stations include repentance, patience, gratitude, trust, contentment, and love.

**10.** The difference between a state and a station is that the state comes and goes, while the station, once achieved, remains as long as the servant maintains it.

**11.** The goal of the Sufi path is not the acquisition of miraculous powers or extraordinary states—it is the purification of the heart and the direct knowledge of God.

**12.** The masters said: "If you see a man walking on water or flying in the air, do not be impressed until you have examined his adherence to the sacred law."

**13.** Many have been deceived by extraordinary states, thinking them signs of spiritual advancement, when in truth they may be tests or even distractions.

**14.** The true sign of spiritual advancement is the refinement of character: increased humility, increased generosity, increased patience, and increased love.

**15.** This treatise is written to preserve the teachings of the early masters and to warn against the deviations that have crept into the path. May God guide us all to the straight way.
