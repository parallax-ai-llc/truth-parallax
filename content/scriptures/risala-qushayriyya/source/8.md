## On Fear and Reverence

**1.** Fear of God is the foundation of piety and the beginning of wisdom. The Prophet said: "The beginning of wisdom is the fear of God."

**2.** But the fear of the masters is not the fear of punishment—it is the fear of separation from the Beloved. The lover does not fear the whip; he fears the beloved's turning away.

**3.** Al-Junayd said: "Fear is the anticipation of punishment until it arrives. And the fear of the knowers is the fear that they may be veiled from God."

**4.** There are three degrees of fear: the fear of the common, which is fear of punishment; the fear of the elect, which is fear of the reckoning; and the fear of the elect of the elect, which is fear of the veil.

**5.** Dhu al-Nun wept and was asked: "Do you weep for fear of the Fire?" He said: "The Fire is nothing compared to the veiling. What is the Fire compared to being separated from God?"

**6.** Reverence is a higher station than fear. The one who fears avoids punishment. The one who reveres is awed by Majesty. Fear drives the servant away from sin; reverence draws the servant toward God.

**7.** Abu Sulayman said: "Everything has an adornment, and the adornment of worship is reverence."

**8.** The worshipper without reverence moves his body but his heart is absent. The worshipper with reverence is fully present—trembling, humble, and aware of the Majesty before him.

**9.** The sign of fear is that the servant restrains himself from sin even when no one is watching. The sign of reverence is that the servant is in awe of God even when he is alone.

**10.** The masters said: "If fear does not prevent you from sin, it is not real fear—it is merely anxiety."

**11.** True fear transforms behavior. The fearful man does not merely feel afraid—he changes. He leaves what he was doing and turns to what he should be doing.

**12.** But fear must be balanced with hope, for fear alone produces despair, and despair is a greater sin than the sin that caused it.

**13.** The Prophet said: "If the believer knew the full extent of God's punishment, he would never hope for Paradise. And if the disbeliever knew the full extent of God's mercy, he would never despair of it."

**14.** The perfection of fear is to reach the station of reverence, where fear is transformed into awe, and awe becomes a form of love.

**15.** For in the end, the servant who truly fears God will love God, and the servant who truly loves God will fear to displease Him—and in this circle of fear and love, the heart finds its eternal home.
