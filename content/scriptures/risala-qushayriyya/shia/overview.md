## Overview

Shia engagement with the Risala al-Qushayriyya focuses on its spiritual psychology and ethical teachings while adapting its theological framework. The Shia tradition of irfan (mystical gnosis) shares many of the Risala's concerns with the stations and states of the spiritual path, and Shia scholars have drawn on Qushayri's systematic presentation while grounding it in the teachings of the Ahl al-Bayt.

## Interpretive Approach

Shia readers appreciate Qushayri's preservation of early Sufi teachings, many of which parallel sayings attributed to Imam Ali, Imam Husayn, and Imam Sajjad. The Risala's framework of stations and states provides a useful map of the spiritual journey that can be adapted to a Shia context by supplementing Qushayri's sources with narrations from the Imams.

## Significance

The Risala demonstrates that the fundamental structures of Islamic mystical experience — repentance, struggle, trust, love, knowledge, and annihilation — transcend sectarian boundaries. Shia scholars of irfan have found in the Risala a valuable resource for articulating the inner dimensions of the faith taught by the Imams.
