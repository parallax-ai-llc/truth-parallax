Shia interpretation of Risala al-Qushayriyya section 1, "The Lives of the Sufi Masters," engages with Qushayri's spiritual teachings through the lens of the Ahl al-Bayt's guidance. The spiritual concepts and practices described in this section find parallels in the sayings of the Imams, particularly in the prayers of Imam Sajjad's Sahifa and the wisdom traditions attributed to Imam Ali.

Shia scholars of irfan have found in this section valuable resources for understanding the stages of the spiritual path. While adapting the theological framework to Shia principles, they recognize that the experiential core of the teachings — the soul's journey through stations of purification toward knowledge of God — represents a shared Islamic spiritual heritage that transcends sectarian differences.
