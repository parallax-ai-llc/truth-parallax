---
title: "Sibylline Oracles - Chapter 1"
scripture: "sibylline-oracles"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Beginning with the generation first of mortal men, down to the very last, I will prophesy all things, what things were first, what things shall yet befall the world through the impiety of men.

**2** O God most blessed, who art in the heights of heaven, who sittest upon cherubim, I pray thee, give me rest, who have received the truth.

**3** For my heart within me is weary, and my spirit trembles. But I will speak all things that God commands me to speak unto men.

**4** O men, who bear the form shaped after God's image, why do ye wander in vain, and walk not in the straight path, ever mindful of the immortal Creator?

**5** There is one God, sole ruler, ineffable, who dwells in heaven, self-sprung, unseen, who sees all things Himself, and whom no mortal eye can see.

**6** He is not gold or silver wrought by hand of man; He is not stone or any crafted thing. He dwells beyond the vault of heaven.

**7** Being alone He beholds all things, and Him no hand of man has made, nor can the mind of mortal man conceive His form.

**8** He shows to none a clear image of Himself, yet all may see Him through His works: the strength of winds, the brightness of the sun, the rain, the earth, the waves of ocean, mountains, rivers, springs.

**9** All these the one God made, and all that lives and grows beneath the wide heaven's dome.

**10** O men, children of vanity, do not provoke the great God's anger. Cast aside your swords and knives, your wars and murders.

**11** Wash your whole body in the living rivers, stretch your hands to heaven, and ask forgiveness for your former deeds.

**12** God will grant repentance and will not destroy. He will stay His wrath again if ye all practise in your hearts the precious virtue of right worship.

**13** But if ye obey me not, ye wicked ones, but love impiety and receive all these things with evil ears, there shall be throughout all the world a great burning fire upon you.

**14** This is the word of God to mortal men, spoken through the Sibyl, His humble servant.
