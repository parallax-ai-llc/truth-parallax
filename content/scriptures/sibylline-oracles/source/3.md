---
title: "Sibylline Oracles - Chapter 3"
scripture: "sibylline-oracles"
chapter: 3
---

**1** O exalted one, who possessest a heavenly home, who hast the eternal firmly established abode, I beseech thee give me a little rest from hymning.

**2** For my heart within me is weary. But again I will speak the words from God, words for all mortals.

**3** God bids me prophesy again and speak. Woe to the men of the land of Italy, woe to the great and lawless race.

**4** In the time of the fifth generation of men shall arise the dominion of Egypt. The reign of the Macedonians shall come, and a fearful overlord from the west.

**5** He shall ravage the earth and overthrow all, and shall come to the height of power and destroy even the princes.

**6** Then from Asia shall come a great king, a fiery eagle, who shall cover all the land with foot soldiers and horsemen, and shall cut up all the ways and lay waste all things.

**7** And then God shall send from the sun a king who shall stop all the earth from evil war, killing some and imposing oaths of loyalty on others; and he shall not do all these things by his own counsel but in obedience to the good decrees of the great God.

**8** And then the people of God shall be strong again, and they shall be guides of life to all mortals.

**9** But why does my mind bid me tell these things also? From the first generation unto the last I will prophesy all things in turn, whatsoever have been from the beginning, whatsoever are, and whatsoever shall yet befall the world through the impiety of men.

**10** First God bids me declare how the world came to be. And thou, crafty mortal, understand that the Most High is the eternal, everlasting God, who made the light and darkness, who made heaven and earth and sea and all that is therein.

**11** He fashioned again the form of man in His own image, ordaining his nature by the four-lettered name, even Adam, the first formed.

**12** O blessed shall that man be, and blessed that woman, who having kept all these things shall live in the time to come, having hope in the One Great God.
