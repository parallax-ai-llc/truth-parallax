## Overview

The Interfaith reading of the Sibylline Oracles explores the collection as an ancient model of interfaith engagement. By placing monotheistic theology in the mouth of a pagan prophetess, the Jewish and Christian authors of the Oracles created a literary space where the boundaries between traditions became permeable. The Church Fathers' embrace of the Sibyl as a legitimate witness to divine truth illustrates how prophetic authority could transcend religious boundaries in the ancient world.
