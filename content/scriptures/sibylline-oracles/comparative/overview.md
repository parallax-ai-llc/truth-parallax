## Overview

The Comparative reading of the Sibylline Oracles examines how Jewish and Christian authors appropriated the pagan Sibylline tradition to create a unique form of cross-cultural religious literature. This approach compares the Oracles' apocalyptic visions with Persian, Egyptian, and Mesopotamian eschatological traditions, and explores the broader phenomenon of prophetic borrowing across religious boundaries.
