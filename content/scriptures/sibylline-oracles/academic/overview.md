## Overview

The Academic study of the Sibylline Oracles examines this composite collection as a primary source for understanding the literary strategies of Hellenistic Judaism, early Christian apologetics, and the interaction between pagan, Jewish, and Christian cultures in the ancient Mediterranean. Scholars analyze the dating, provenance, and redaction history of each book, the social settings that produced them, and their reception by patristic authors who cited the Sibyl as a pagan witness to monotheistic truth.
