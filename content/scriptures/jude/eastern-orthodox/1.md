## Tradition Beyond Scripture

Jude's citation of 1 Enoch and the Assumption of Moses exemplifies the Orthodox understanding that the Church's tradition is broader than the biblical canon. The Holy Spirit guides the Church's reception of truth from many sources, and the inspired apostolic author's use of extra-canonical material demonstrates the living, dynamic nature of sacred tradition. Orthodox theology has always been comfortable with a broader understanding of tradition than that permitted by strict Protestant *sola Scriptura*.

## The Fallen Angels and Cosmic Struggle

Jude 6, describing angels who abandoned their position and are kept "in eternal chains under gloomy darkness," informs Orthodox angelology and the understanding of the cosmic spiritual struggle. The Christian life, in the Orthodox tradition, is lived within a cosmic drama involving angelic and demonic beings. The ascetic tradition's emphasis on spiritual warfare finds scriptural support in Jude's vivid description of the fallen angels.

## The Doxology

Jude 24–25 is one of the great doxologies of the Orthodox liturgical tradition, expressing the Church's confidence in God's power to preserve the faithful and present them "blameless before the presence of his glory with great joy." This Paschal confidence — rooted in Christ's victory over death — is the fundamental orientation of Orthodox worship, and the doxology of Jude gives it sublime expression.
