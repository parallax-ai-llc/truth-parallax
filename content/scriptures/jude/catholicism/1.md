## The Faith Once Delivered

Jude 3 speaks of "the faith once for all delivered to the saints." Catholic theology identifies this with the *depositum fidei* — the complete body of revealed truth entrusted to the apostles and transmitted through apostolic succession. The Magisterium's role is to guard, interpret, and transmit this deposit faithfully. The letter's urgency about defending this faith supports the Catholic conviction that the Church bears a solemn responsibility for doctrinal integrity.

## Judgment and Extra-Canonical Traditions

Jude's use of 1 Enoch (vv. 14–15) and the Assumption of Moses (v. 9) demonstrates that the inspired author drew on traditions beyond the strict canonical boundaries. Catholic theology affirms that the Holy Spirit guided this use, and that truth can be found in traditions that are not themselves canonical. This principle supports the broader Catholic understanding that Scripture and Tradition together form the one deposit of the Word of God.

## The Doxology and Divine Preservation

Jude 24–25 affirms God's power "to keep you from stumbling." Catholic theology reads this as an expression of divine preserving grace, which is always available to the faithful through the sacraments, prayer, and the communion of saints. The doxology's ascription of glory "before all time and now and forever" reflects the Catholic understanding of God's eternal plan of salvation, which encompasses past, present, and future.
