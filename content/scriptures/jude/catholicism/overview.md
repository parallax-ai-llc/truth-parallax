## Overview

Catholic interpretation of Jude emphasizes the letter's defense of apostolic tradition, its use of extra-canonical sources, and its teaching on divine judgment. The call to contend for "the faith once for all delivered to the saints" (v. 3) supports the Catholic understanding of the deposit of faith — the body of revealed truth entrusted to the Church and transmitted through apostolic succession. Jude's citation of 1 Enoch and the Assumption of Moses also raises important questions about the boundaries of inspired Scripture and the value of sacred tradition.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### The Deposit of Faith
Jude 3 speaks of "the faith once for all delivered to the saints," which Catholic theology identifies with the *depositum fidei* — the complete body of revealed truth that the Church guards, interprets, and transmits. This passage supports the Catholic conviction that revelation is complete in the apostolic era and that the Church's task is faithful transmission and authentic interpretation, not innovation.

### Extra-Canonical Sources
Jude's citation of 1 Enoch (vv. 14–15) and apparent allusion to the Assumption of Moses (v. 9) raises questions about the canon. Catholic theology affirms that the inspired author can draw on non-canonical sources without thereby conferring canonical status on those sources. The Holy Spirit guided the author's selection and use of these traditions for the purpose of his inspired message.

### The Doxology
Jude 24–25 affirms God's power "to keep you from stumbling" and "to present you blameless." Catholic theology reads this as an affirmation of divine preserving grace, available through the sacramental life and the prayers of the Church. The doxology is used in Catholic liturgy as an expression of confident hope in God's faithfulness.

## Theological Emphases

- **Deposit of Faith**: The faith delivered once for all as the Church's sacred trust
- **Tradition and Scripture**: The value of extra-canonical traditions within the inspired text
- **Judgment**: The certainty of divine judgment on apostasy and moral corruption
- **Doxology**: God's power to preserve the faithful until the day of glory
