## Overview

Jude's polemic against false teachers who pervert grace into license may be directed against proto-Gnostic opponents. The letter's description of intruders who "deny our only Master and Lord, Jesus Christ" (v. 4) and engage in immoral behavior while claiming spiritual authority matches the profile of certain Gnostic-influenced groups. Jude's appeal to established tradition and its vigorous defense of moral boundaries represent the kind of proto-orthodox response that would eventually marginalize Gnostic Christianity.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Libertine Gnosticism
Jude's false teachers who turn grace into license (v. 4) may represent a libertine form of proto-Gnosticism. Some Gnostic groups, particularly the Carpocratians, taught that the pneumatic was above the moral law and that indulgence in bodily pleasures had no effect on the divine spark. Jude's vigorous condemnation of moral license in the name of spiritual freedom targets precisely this kind of claim.

### The Watchers Tradition
Jude 6, drawing on the 1 Enoch tradition of fallen angels, engages a mythological tradition that Gnostic cosmology would develop in its own direction. The Gnostic archons — cosmic rulers who imprison the divine sparks — bear a family resemblance to the Enochic Watchers who transgressed their boundaries. However, Jude uses the tradition to warn against transgression, while Gnostic texts use similar traditions to explain the origin of the material cosmos.

### Contending for Institutional Orthodoxy
Jude 3 — "contend for the faith once for all delivered to the saints" — represents an appeal to fixed, authoritative tradition that stands in tension with the Gnostic claim to ongoing revelation and hidden knowledge. The Gnostic response to such appeals was that the apostolic tradition, while valuable for psychics, was incomplete without the secret teaching transmitted to the pneumatic elite.

## Theological Emphases

- **Anti-Libertine Polemic**: The rejection of moral license based on spiritual claims
- **Fixed Tradition**: The faith "once for all delivered" against claims of continuing revelation
- **Cosmic Judgment**: Divine judgment on angelic and human rebellion
- **Moral Boundaries**: The inseparability of correct doctrine and moral conduct
