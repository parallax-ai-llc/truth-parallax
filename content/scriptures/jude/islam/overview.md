## Overview

Islamic engagement with Jude focuses on the letter's themes of false prophets, divine judgment, and moral corruption — all of which have strong parallels in Quranic teaching. The letter's vivid examples of divine judgment on the disobedient resonate with the Quran's recurring narratives of destroyed peoples (*qawm*) who rejected their prophets. While Muslim scholars do not accept the letter's canonical status, its moral and eschatological concerns align closely with Islamic teaching.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### False Prophets and Corrupted Teaching
Jude's denunciation of those who "crept in unnoticed" and "pervert the grace of our God into sensuality" (vv. 3–4) resonates with the Quranic concern about those who corrupt divine teaching for personal gain. The Quran warns: "Among them are unlettered ones who do not know the Scripture except in wishful thinking, and they are only assuming" (2:78). Both traditions recognize the persistent danger of false teachers who distort revealed truth.

### Divine Judgment on the Disobedient
Jude's three examples of judgment — Israel in the wilderness, the fallen angels, and Sodom — parallel Quranic narratives of divine punishment. The Quran narrates the destruction of Pharaoh's army, the people of Lut (Lot), the people of 'Ad and Thamud, and others as warnings to subsequent generations. Muslim readers find in Jude a shared theological conviction: God is patient but ultimately just, and persistent disobedience meets certain punishment.

### The Angelic Tradition
Jude 6, describing angels who left their proper station, engages traditions about fallen angels that have parallels in Islamic angelology. While Islamic theology generally teaches that angels are incapable of disobedience (with Iblis/Satan being a jinn, not an angel), the concept of cosmic beings who rebel against God's order is present in both traditions.

## Theological Emphases

- **False Teachers**: The corruption of divine teaching as a persistent danger
- **Divine Judgment**: The certainty of punishment for unrepentant disobedience
- **Prophetic Warning**: Jude's exhortation as consistent with the prophetic tradition
- **Moral Accountability**: The connection between doctrinal corruption and moral license
