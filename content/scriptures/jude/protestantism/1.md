## Contend for the Faith

Jude 3 — "I found it necessary to write appealing to you to contend for the faith that was once for all delivered to the saints" — is one of the most cited verses in Protestant apologetics. The "faith" is understood as the objective content of apostolic teaching, and "contending" involves both positive proclamation and vigorous defense against distortion. This verse has been the motto of countless Protestant movements defending doctrinal orthodoxy.

## The False Teachers Described

Jude 4–16 provides a vivid portrait of false teachers who have infiltrated the community. They "pervert the grace of our God into sensuality" (v. 4), "defile the flesh, reject authority, and blaspheme the glorious ones" (v. 8), and follow the path of "Cain," "Balaam's error," and "Korah's rebellion" (v. 11). Protestant interpreters read this as a comprehensive catalog of the marks of false teaching: moral license, rejection of legitimate authority, and greed.

## The Great Doxology

Jude 24–25 brings the letter to a climax of praise: "Now to him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you blameless before the presence of his glory with great joy — to the only God, our Savior, through Jesus Christ our Lord, be glory, majesty, dominion, and authority, before all time and now and forever." This doxology is among the most beloved in Protestant worship, affirming God's sovereign power to preserve the faithful through every trial and to bring them safely to glory.
