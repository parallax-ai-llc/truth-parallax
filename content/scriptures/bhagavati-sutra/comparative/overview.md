## Overview

Comparative interpretation of the Bhagavati Sutra reveals illuminating parallels with dialogue literature and philosophical traditions across cultures. The text's treatment of karma, cosmology, and the soul provides rich material for cross-traditional analysis.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Dialogue Traditions

The Mahavira-Gautama dialogue format parallels the Buddhist suttas (Buddha-disciple dialogues), Platonic dialogues, Upanishadic conversations, and Talmudic discussions. Each tradition uses dialogue to explore truth through questioning.

### Karma Theories

The Bhagavati Sutra's materialist karma theory -- karma as physical substance binding to the soul -- contrasts sharply with Buddhist karma as mental intention and Hindu karma as moral law, illuminating fundamental differences in Indian metaphysics.

### Cosmological Models

The text's detailed cosmology can be compared with Buddhist Abhidhamma cosmology, Hindu puranic geography, and Zoroastrian cosmic geography, revealing distinctive approaches to mapping reality.

## Comparative Framework

| Concept | Jainism (Bhagavati) | Buddhism | Hinduism |
|---------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Teaching method | Dialogue | Discourse/dialogue | Dialogue/commentary |
| Karma | Material substance | Mental volition | Cosmic law |
| Soul | Eternal, individual jiva | No permanent self | Atman = Brahman |
