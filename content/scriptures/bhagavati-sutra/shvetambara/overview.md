## Overview

For the Shvetambara tradition, the Bhagavati Sutra is the crown jewel of the Agamic canon -- the largest, most comprehensive, and most philosophically rich of the twelve Angas. Its dialogue format between Mahavira and Indrabhuti Gautama is treasured as the most intimate record of the Tirthankara's teaching.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Supreme Canonical Authority

Shvetambara scholars regard the Bhagavati Sutra as the most authoritative single source for Jain doctrine. When doctrinal questions arise, this text is often the first reference consulted.

### The Dialogue Tradition

The Mahavira-Gautama dialogue is seen as establishing a model for the guru-disciple relationship central to Jain monastic life. Gautama's questions represent the genuine doubts of a sincere seeker, and Mahavira's answers represent perfect omniscient knowledge.

### Encyclopedic Scope

The text's coverage of karma theory, cosmology, soul analysis, ethics, and rival doctrines makes it an encyclopedic reference for the entire range of Jain thought.
