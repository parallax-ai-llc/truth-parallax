## Overview

The Bhagavati Sutra (Bhagavatī Sūtra), also known as the Vyakhyaprajnapti ("Exposition of Explanations"), is the fifth and largest of the twelve Angas of the Shvetambara Jain canon. It is presented as a series of dialogues between Lord Mahavira and his chief disciple Indrabhuti Gautama, covering an extraordinary range of philosophical, cosmological, and doctrinal topics. It is arguably the most important single text for understanding classical Jain thought.

## Structure

The text is organized into 12 major sections (shatakas), each containing multiple dialogues:

1. **Section 1 — On the Soul**: Nature, types, and conditions of jivas
2. **Section 2 — On Karma**: Binding, duration, and fruition of karmic matter
3. **Section 3 — On Cosmology**: Structure of the universe and its inhabitants
4. **Section 4 — On Rebirth**: Mechanisms of transmigration
5. **Section 5 — On Knowledge**: Types of cognition and perception
6. **Section 6 — On Time and Space**: Nature of temporal and spatial categories
7. **Section 7 — On Ethics**: Moral conduct and its consequences
8. **Section 8 — On Asceticism**: Forms and fruits of austerity
9. **Section 9 — On Liberation**: Conditions for achieving moksha
10. **Section 10 — On Rival Doctrines**: Responses to other philosophical schools
11. **Section 11 — On Cosmographic Details**: Detailed descriptions of world-realms
12. **Section 12 — On the Tirthankaras**: Lives and teachings of the ford-makers

## Cross-Traditional Significance

- **Digambara**: Respect its antiquity while maintaining their own doctrinal interpretations
- **Shvetambara**: Consider it the richest single source of Mahavira's teachings
- **Academic**: Essential for reconstructing early Jain philosophy and Indian intellectual history
- **Comparative**: The dialogue format parallels Buddhist suttas and Upanishadic discussions

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Dialogue | Teaching through questions and answers between Mahavira and Gautama |
| Karma Theory | The most detailed early Jain analysis of karmic mechanics |
| Cosmology | Comprehensive mapping of the Jain universe |
| Soul Theory | Extensive analysis of the nature and states of the jiva |
| Philosophical Debate | Engagement with rival Indian schools |
