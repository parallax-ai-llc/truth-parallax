## Overview

The Digambara tradition respects the Bhagavati Sutra's claim to record Mahavira's dialogues while maintaining that their own doctrinal sources preserve the most authentic version of the Tirthankara's teachings. Digambara scholars engage with the text's philosophical content, particularly its detailed karma theory and soul analysis.

## Key Interpretive Approaches

### Mahavira's Teaching Authority

Digambara interpreters value the dialogue format as reflecting the authentic teaching method of Mahavira -- responding to specific questions with precise doctrinal answers. The text's authority derives from its attribution to the omniscient Tirthankara.

### Strict Karma Theory

The Bhagavati Sutra's detailed analysis of karmic mechanics is read through the Digambara lens of strict determinism in karmic processes. Every aspect of bondage and liberation follows precise laws that the text systematically expounds.

### Philosophical Engagement

Digambara scholars particularly value the sections where Mahavira engages with rival philosophical positions, seeing these as models for the later Digambara tradition of rigorous philosophical debate.
