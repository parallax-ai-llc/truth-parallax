---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 2: On Karma and Its Varieties"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 2
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, how many kinds of karma are there?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, karma is of eight kinds: knowledge-obscuring karma, perception-obscuring karma, feeling-producing karma, deluding karma, life-determining karma, body-determining karma, status-determining karma, and obstructing karma."

**2** "Knowledge-obscuring karma is that which prevents the soul from knowing things as they are. Just as a veil covers the face and prevents one from seeing, so this karma covers the soul's natural omniscience."

**3** "Perception-obscuring karma is that which prevents the soul from perceiving correctly. It obscures the soul's natural capacity for infinite perception, just as clouds obscure the light of the sun."

**4** "Feeling-producing karma is of two kinds: that which produces pleasant feeling and that which produces unpleasant feeling. By this karma, the soul experiences pleasure and pain in its various existences."

**5** "Deluding karma is the most dangerous of all karmas, for it prevents the soul from perceiving the truth. It is of two kinds: faith-deluding karma, which prevents right faith, and conduct-deluding karma, which prevents right conduct. As a man intoxicated with liquor cannot distinguish right from wrong, so the soul obscured by deluding karma cannot distinguish truth from falsehood."

**6** "Life-determining karma determines the span of life in each existence. Body-determining karma determines the body — whether one is born as a celestial being, a human being, an animal, or a hellish being. Status-determining karma determines whether one is born in high or low circumstances."

**7** "Obstructing karma is that which obstructs the soul's natural capacity for infinite energy. It is like a treasurer who, though possessing wealth, prevents its use. By this karma, even when the soul encounters an opportunity for giving, for gaining, or for enjoyment, it is prevented from doing so."

**8** "O Gautama, the influx of karma is caused by the activities of mind, speech, and body. When these activities are accompanied by passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — the karma that flows in is of the binding kind. When these activities are free from passions, the karma that flows in is quickly shed."

**9** "The binding of karma depends on four factors: the nature of the karma, its duration, its intensity, and the quantity of karmic particles bound. The passions determine the intensity and duration; the type of activity determines the nature and quantity."

**10** "The cessation of karmic influx is called samvara. It is achieved through right conduct, through austerities, through carefulness, through the observance of vows, and through the practice of equanimity. The shedding of already accumulated karma is called nirjara, and it is achieved through austerities and meditation."
