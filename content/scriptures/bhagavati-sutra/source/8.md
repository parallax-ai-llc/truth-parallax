---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 8: On the Nature of Knowledge"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 8
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, how many kinds of knowledge are there?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, knowledge is of five kinds: sensory knowledge, scriptural knowledge, clairvoyant knowledge, telepathic knowledge, and omniscient knowledge."

**2** "Sensory knowledge is that which is obtained through the senses and the mind. It includes the knowledge gained through seeing, hearing, smelling, tasting, and touching, as well as the knowledge gained through reasoning and memory."

**3** "Scriptural knowledge is that which is obtained through the study of the scriptures, through the words of the teachers, through inference, and through analogy. It depends upon sensory knowledge as its basis."

**4** "These two kinds of knowledge—sensory and scriptural—are called indirect knowledge, for they depend upon external means. They can be erroneous if the senses are defective or if the reasoning is faulty."

**5** "Clairvoyant knowledge is the direct knowledge of material things without the aid of the senses. It perceives objects at a distance, objects hidden from sight, and objects in the past and future. It is limited in scope, perceiving only material forms."

**6** "Telepathic knowledge is the direct knowledge of the thoughts of other minds. It perceives the mental states—the thoughts, desires, and intentions—of other beings. It too is limited, for it perceives only thoughts, not all reality."

**7** "Omniscient knowledge is the direct, complete, and perfect knowledge of all substances, all qualities, and all modifications—past, present, and future. It is unlimited and infinite. It is the knowledge of the Kevalin, the liberated one."

**8** "These five kinds of knowledge exist as attributes of the soul. They are not acquired from outside; they are inherent in the soul's nature. But they are obscured by knowledge-obscuring karma, and they manifest only when that karma is partially or fully destroyed."

**9** "Sensory knowledge and scriptural knowledge manifest when the relevant knowledge-obscuring karma is partially suppressed. Clairvoyant knowledge and telepathic knowledge manifest when more karma is destroyed. Omniscient knowledge manifests only when all knowledge-obscuring karma is completely destroyed."

**10** "False knowledge also exists. When knowledge-obscuring karma operates in a perverse manner, it produces wrong knowledge—wrong sensory knowledge, wrong scriptural knowledge, and wrong clairvoyant knowledge."

**11** "The difference between right knowledge and wrong knowledge is this: right knowledge sees things as they are; wrong knowledge sees things as they are not. Right knowledge leads to liberation; wrong knowledge leads to further bondage."

**12** "Therefore, O Gautama, the seeker of liberation should cultivate right knowledge with diligence. He should study the scriptures under a qualified teacher, practise meditation, and gradually purify the soul until the full light of omniscience dawns."
