---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 3: On the Categories of Living Beings"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 3
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, how are living beings classified?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, living beings are classified according to the number of their senses, the nature of their bodies, and their capacity for reason."

**2** "Beings with one sense possess only the sense of touch. They are earth-bodied, water-bodied, fire-bodied, air-bodied, and plant-bodied beings. These beings experience pleasure and pain through touch alone. They are the most numerous of all living beings."

**3** "Among one-sensed beings, earth-bodied beings dwell in earth, clay, sand, stones, minerals, and metals. Water-bodied beings dwell in water, dew, fog, ice, and hail. Fire-bodied beings dwell in flames, burning coals, lightning, and heat. Air-bodied beings dwell in winds, storms, cyclones, and breezes."

**4** "Plant-bodied beings are of two kinds: those having individual bodies and those having common bodies. Individual-bodied plants each house a single soul. Common-bodied plants, such as tubers, bulbs, and roots, house many souls in a single body. For this reason, the wise are careful not to harm plants unnecessarily."

**5** "Beings with two senses possess touch and taste. These include worms, leeches, earthworms, and shellfish such as cowries and conches. Beings with three senses possess touch, taste, and smell. These include ants, lice, fleas, and centipedes."

**6** "Beings with four senses possess touch, taste, smell, and sight. These include flies, bees, wasps, butterflies, and moths. Beings with five senses possess touch, taste, smell, sight, and hearing."

**7** "Five-sensed beings are of two kinds: those with mind and those without mind. Those with mind possess the capacity for reasoning, reflection, and moral discernment. These include human beings, celestial beings, infernal beings, and certain animals."

**8** "Those without mind, though possessing five senses, lack the capacity for reasoning and moral choice. They act by instinct alone. Many animals belong to this category."

**9** "O Gautama, among all beings, the human birth is the most precious, for only in the human state does the soul possess the full capacity for spiritual endeavour. The celestial beings are too absorbed in pleasure, the infernal beings too overwhelmed by suffering, and the animals too governed by instinct."

**10** "Therefore the wise, having obtained this rare human birth, devote themselves to the practice of right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct, knowing that this opportunity may not come again for countless ages. They practise non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-attachment, which are the five great vows of the path to liberation."
