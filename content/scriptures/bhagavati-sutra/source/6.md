---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 6: On the Universe and Its Structure"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 6
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, what is the nature of the universe?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, the universe is without beginning and without end. It was not created by any god or supreme being. It exists by its own nature, governed by its own laws."

**2** "The universe is composed of six eternal substances: soul, matter, the medium of motion, the medium of rest, space, and time. These six substances have always existed and will always exist. They are neither created nor destroyed."

**3** "Matter is of two kinds: atomic and aggregate. Atoms are the smallest indivisible particles of matter. When atoms combine, they form aggregates. All physical objects, including karmic matter, are aggregates of atoms."

**4** "The medium of motion makes movement possible, as water makes the movement of fish possible. The medium of rest makes rest possible, as the shade of a tree makes rest possible for a traveller. Without these two media, neither motion nor rest would be possible."

**5** "Space provides room for the existence of all substances. It is infinite, but the occupied universe is finite, surrounded by infinite empty space."

**6** "Time is of two kinds: absolute time and conventional time. Absolute time is a substance that assists in the modification of other substances. Conventional time is measured by the changes we observe—the succession of moments, hours, days, and years."

**7** "The universe has the shape of a man standing with arms akimbo. It is divided into three parts: the lower world, the middle world, and the upper world."

**8** "The lower world consists of seven hells, each darker and more painful than the one above it. In the lowest hell, beings suffer the most intense pain. The infernal beings there have long lifespans measured in oceans of time."

**9** "The middle world is a disc-shaped region in the centre of the universe. It contains innumerable continents and oceans arranged in concentric rings. The innermost continent is Jambudvipa, where human beings dwell."

**10** "The upper world consists of heavenly realms arranged in ascending tiers. The celestial beings who dwell there enjoy great pleasures, but their existence is also impermanent. Above all the heavens is the Siddha-loka, the abode of liberated souls."

**11** "The universe undergoes cycles of progress and decline, called Utsarpini and Avasarpini. In each cycle, twenty-four Tirthankaras appear to teach the path of liberation. We are now in the declining phase of the current cycle."

**12** "The universe is self-sustaining and self-governing. No external agency is needed to create it, maintain it, or destroy it. The natural order of karma governs the fate of all living beings within it."
