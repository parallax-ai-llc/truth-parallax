---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 12: On Liberation and the Liberated Soul"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 12
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, what is the state of the liberated soul?" The Blessed One said: "Gautama, the liberated soul has destroyed all eight kinds of karma. It possesses in their fullness the four infinities: infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy."

**2** "The liberated soul knows all things simultaneously—all substances, all their qualities, all their modes, past, present, and future—without any instrument of knowledge, without any sense organ, without any medium. Its knowledge is direct, unmediated, and complete."

**3** "The liberated soul perceives all things simultaneously. There is nothing hidden from it, nothing obscured. Its perception encompasses the entire universe in a single act of awareness."

**4** "The liberated soul experiences infinite bliss. This bliss is not dependent on any external object. It is the soul's own nature, revealed in its fullness when all obscuring karma has been removed."

**5** "The liberated soul possesses infinite energy. But this energy is not exercised in any worldly activity. The liberated soul does not act, does not move, does not speak. It simply exists in its own perfection."

**6** "Lord, where does the liberated soul abide?" The Blessed One said: "Upon shedding all karma, the soul, freed from the weight of matter, rises naturally to the top of the universe, to the Siddha-loka. There it remains, forever beyond the cycle of birth and death."

**7** "The liberated soul has no body, no senses, no mind in the ordinary sense. It exists as pure consciousness, pure awareness, pure bliss. It occupies the last space-point of the universe."

**8** "Lord, can the liberated soul ever fall back into bondage?" The Blessed One said: "Never. Liberation, once attained, is eternal and irreversible. The liberated soul has destroyed the very seeds of karma. There is no cause for new bondage to arise."

**9** "Lord, do all souls ultimately attain liberation?" The Blessed One said: "All souls have the potential for liberation, for all souls possess inherently the four infinities. But not all souls will attain liberation in any given cycle of time. Some souls, called abhavya, lack the capacity in their present condition. But the teaching is offered to all."

**10** "The path to liberation is not easy. It requires the practice of severe austerity, the conquest of all passions, the observance of all vows, the cultivation of complete equanimity. It requires lifetimes of dedicated practice."

**11** "But the fruit is beyond all comparison. No pleasure of the senses, no joy of the heavens, no power of the gods can compare with the infinite bliss of the liberated soul. All worldly pleasures are as nothing compared to liberation."

**12** "Therefore, Gautama, let the wise strive for liberation. Let them take refuge in right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct. Let them follow the path of the Jinas, the Conquerors, the Tirthankaras. For liberation is the supreme goal, the highest attainment, the final resting place of the soul."
