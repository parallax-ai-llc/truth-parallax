---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 1: On Existence and Non-Existence"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 1
---

**1** Thus have I heard: The Blessed One, the venerable Mahavira, the great hero, the omniscient one, the all-seeing one, was dwelling at Champa, in the garden of the palace of King Kunika, under the ashoka tree, surrounded by the community of monks and nuns, by the community of laymen and laywomen.

**2** The venerable Gautama, the chief disciple, approached the Blessed One, circumambulated him three times from left to right, paid homage to him, and standing at a respectful distance, spoke thus:

**3** "Lord, is the soul eternal or non-eternal?" The Blessed One replied: "O Gautama, the soul is eternal in some respects and non-eternal in other respects. In its substance, the soul is eternal; in its modifications, the soul is non-eternal. This is the doctrine of manifold aspects."

**4** "Lord, is the world eternal or non-eternal?" The Blessed One replied: "O Gautama, from the point of view of substance, the world is eternal. From the point of view of modification, the world is non-eternal. The wise do not assert a one-sided view."

**5** "Lord, what is the nature of existence?" The Blessed One replied: "O Gautama, existence is of two kinds: substantial existence and modal existence. Substantial existence means the permanence of the substance through all its changes. Modal existence means the arising and perishing of particular states."

**6** "Every entity has three aspects: origination, decay, and permanence. At every moment, a thing originates in a new mode, decays from its old mode, and persists in its essential substance. This threefold nature is the mark of reality."

**7** "The ignorant see only one aspect and quarrel among themselves, saying 'It is' or 'It is not.' But the wise, seeing all three aspects simultaneously, attain to the truth. This is the doctrine of non-absolutism, the anekantavada."

**8** "O Gautama, there are six substances that constitute reality: souls, matter, the medium of motion, the medium of rest, space, and time. These six substances are eternal in their existence, though they undergo constant modification."

**9** "Matter consists of atoms and aggregates of atoms. The atom is the ultimate indivisible unit of matter. Aggregates are formed by the combination of atoms through the qualities of dryness, moisture, heat, and cold."

**10** "The soul and matter interact through karma. The soul, by its activities of mind, speech, and body, attracts karmic matter, which binds to it and determines its future conditions. This is the beginning of bondage, and the cessation of this attraction is the beginning of liberation."
