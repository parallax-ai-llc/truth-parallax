---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 10: On Right Conduct"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 10
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, what is right conduct?" The Blessed One said: "Right conduct is the observance of the five great vows with body, speech, and mind. It is the path to the destruction of karma and the attainment of liberation."

**2** "The five great vows are: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-attachment. These are to be observed without any exception by those who have renounced the world."

**3** "The vow of non-violence: I renounce all violence toward living beings, whether subtle or gross, whether moving or unmoving. I shall not myself kill, nor cause others to kill, nor consent to the killing of any being."

**4** "The vow of truthfulness: I renounce all speech that is untrue, whether uttered in anger, in greed, in fear, or in jest. I shall speak only what is true, what is beneficial, and what is measured."

**5** "The vow of non-stealing: I renounce the taking of anything not given, whether in a village or in a forest, whether small or great. I shall not take, nor cause others to take, nor consent to the taking of what is not given."

**6** "The vow of celibacy: I renounce all sexual activity, whether with gods, with humans, or with animals. I shall not engage in, nor cause others to engage in, nor consent to any sexual activity."

**7** "The vow of non-attachment: I renounce all attachment to objects, whether small or great, whether living or non-living. I shall not attach, nor cause others to attach, nor consent to attachment."

**8** "For householders who cannot observe these five great vows in their fullness, there are five lesser vows: limited non-violence, limited truthfulness, limited non-stealing, limited celibacy in the form of fidelity, and limited non-attachment in the form of limiting possessions."

**9** "In addition to the vows, right conduct includes the practice of the three guptis—carefulness of mind, speech, and body—and the five samitis—carefulness in walking, speaking, eating, placing and removing objects, and disposing of waste."

**10** "The purpose of all these practices is the same: to stop the influx of new karma and to shed old karma. Every act of carefulness is a step toward liberation."

**11** "Right conduct without right faith is ineffective. Right conduct without right knowledge is blind. But when right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct are combined—this is the path to liberation, the ratnatraya, the three jewels."

**12** "He who follows this path, steadfast in the five vows, careful in the three guptis and five samitis, endowed with right faith and right knowledge—he shall in time destroy all karma and attain the supreme state of the Siddha."
