---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 11: On the Tirthankaras"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 11
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, who are the Tirthankaras?" The Blessed One said: "The Tirthankaras are the ford-makers, those who establish the tirtha—the community of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen. They are the Omniscient Ones who reveal the path of liberation."

**2** "In each half-cycle of time, twenty-four Tirthankaras appear. In the present descending half-cycle, the first was Rishabhadeva, also called Adinatha, the First Lord. The last was I, Mahavira, the Great Hero."

**3** "Rishabhadeva appeared when mankind needed guidance. He taught the arts of living—agriculture, crafts, writing—and also the path of renunciation. He was a king who renounced his kingdom, practised great austerities, and attained omniscience."

**4** "After Rishabhadeva came Ajitanatha, Sambhavanatha, Abhinandananatha, Sumatinatha, Padmaprabha, Suparshvanatha, Chandraprabha, Pushpadanta, Shitalanatha, Shreyamsanatha, and Vasupujya."

**5** "Then came Vimalanatha, Anantanatha, Dharmanatha, Shantinatha, Kunthunatha, Aranatha, Mallinatha, Munisuvrata, Naminatha, Neminatha, Parshvanatha, and finally, I, Mahavira."

**6** "Each Tirthankara, before his final birth, accumulated immense merit through innumerable previous lives. Each practised the twenty qualities that lead to tirthankara-nature: right faith undisturbed by others, right faith from one's own reasoning, hunger for liberation, compassion, and many others."

**7** "Each Tirthankara attained omniscience by the destruction of the four destructive karmas: knowledge-obscuring, perception-obscuring, deluding, and obstructive karma. Upon attaining omniscience, each established the fourfold community."

**8** "The teaching of all Tirthankaras is the same in essence: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-attachment are the path. Right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct are the three jewels. The soul's liberation is the goal."

**9** "Yet the Tirthankaras differ in their external circumstances—their height, their lifespan, their lineage, their number of followers—according to the conditions of the time in which they appear."

**10** "In the time of Rishabhadeva, human lifespan was vast and the world was in a state of great prosperity. In my time, the Mahavira's time, human lifespan is short and the world is in decline. Yet the teaching remains the same."

**11** "After the passing of the Tirthankara, the teaching is maintained by the ganadharas, the chief disciples, and after them by the succession of monks and teachers, until in time the teaching is lost and the cycle renews."

**12** "Those who follow the teaching of the Tirthankaras with sincere devotion, who practice the vows, who cultivate right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct—they shall cross the ocean of worldly existence and attain liberation, even in this age of decline."
