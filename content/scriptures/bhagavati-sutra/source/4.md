---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 4: On the Soul and Its Modifications"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 4
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, how many kinds of souls are there?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, souls are of two kinds: worldly souls and liberated souls. Worldly souls are bound by karma; liberated souls are free from all karma."

**2** "Worldly souls are further of two kinds: those that are mobile and those that are immobile. The immobile souls are of five kinds: earth-bodied, water-bodied, fire-bodied, air-bodied, and plant-bodied."

**3** "Mobile souls are of four kinds: those with two senses, those with three senses, those with four senses, and those with five senses. Beings with two senses include worms, leeches, and shellfish. Beings with three senses include ants, lice, and bugs."

**4** "Beings with four senses include flies, bees, and butterflies. Beings with five senses include fish, birds, animals, celestial beings, infernal beings, and human beings."

**5** "Among five-sensed beings, some are rational and some are irrational. The rational beings possess mind in addition to the five senses. The irrational beings have five senses but lack the capacity for reasoning."

**6** "The soul in its pure state possesses infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. But due to the covering of karmic matter, these qualities are obscured."

**7** "As the sun is obscured by clouds yet retains its nature of radiance, so the soul is obscured by karma yet retains its nature of consciousness. When the clouds are dispersed, the sun shines forth; when karma is destroyed, the soul shines forth in its true nature."

**8** "The soul is neither created nor destroyed. It is eternal. It has existed from beginningless time. Only its conditions change—from bondage to liberation, from ignorance to knowledge."

**9** "The soul occupies the body it inhabits, pervading it entirely. In a small body, the soul contracts; in a large body, the soul expands. Like a lamp whose light fills whatever room it is placed in, the soul pervades whatever body it occupies."

**10** "Each soul is distinct and individual. The liberation of one soul does not cause the liberation of another. Each soul must work out its own salvation through right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct."
