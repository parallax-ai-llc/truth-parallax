---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 9: On the Stages of Spiritual Development"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 9
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, what are the stages through which the soul passes on its way to liberation?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, there are fourteen stages of spiritual development, called Gunasthanas, through which the soul ascends from the lowest state of delusion to the highest state of omniscience."

**2** "The first stage is that of wrong belief. Here the soul is completely deluded. It does not know its own true nature. It identifies itself with the body and the mind. It pursues worldly pleasures, not knowing that they are the source of suffering."

**3** "The second stage is that of the lingering taste of right belief. Here the soul has caught a glimpse of truth but has not yet firmly established itself in right belief. It oscillates between right and wrong belief."

**4** "The third stage is that of mixed belief, where right and wrong belief alternate. The fourth stage is that of unsteady right belief—right belief is present but may still be lost."

**5** "The fifth stage is that of partial self-discipline. Here the soul has attained right belief and has begun to practise the vows, but only partially. It observes the lesser vows of the householder."

**6** "The sixth stage is that of complete self-discipline with occasional lapses. The soul has taken the great vows of the ascetic but sometimes fails to observe them perfectly due to negligence."

**7** "The seventh stage is that of complete self-discipline without negligence. Here the soul is perfectly vigilant in the observance of the vows. There is no lapse, no carelessness."

**8** "The eighth, ninth, and tenth stages involve the progressive suppression of the passions. In the eighth stage, the soul begins to weaken its passions through meditation. In the ninth, it advances further. In the tenth, the subtlest passions are being subdued."

**9** "The eleventh stage is that of suppressed passions. Here all passions are suppressed, but they have not yet been destroyed. They lie dormant and may arise again."

**10** "The twelfth stage is that of destroyed passions. Here all the passions that lead to bondage have been permanently destroyed. The soul can never again fall from this stage."

**11** "The thirteenth stage is that of omniscience with activity. Here the soul has destroyed all knowledge-obscuring, perception-obscuring, deluding, and obstructive karmas. It possesses infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. Yet it still possesses a body and performs bodily activities."

**12** "The fourteenth stage is that of omniscience without activity. This is the final moment of embodied existence. The soul sheds the remaining non-destructive karmas, abandons the body, and rises to the summit of the universe, where it abides forever as a Siddha—a perfected being, free from all karma and all embodiment."
