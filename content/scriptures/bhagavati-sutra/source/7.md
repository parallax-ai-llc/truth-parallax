---
title: "Bhagavati Sutra (Viyahapannatti) - Chapter 7: On Non-Violence"
scripture: "bhagavati-sutra"
chapter: 7
---

**1** The venerable Gautama asked: "Lord, what is the highest dharma?" The Blessed One said: "O Gautama, non-violence is the highest dharma. Non-violence is the highest self-restraint. Non-violence is the highest gift. Non-violence is the highest penance."

**2** "Non-violence is the highest truth. Non-violence is the highest forgiveness. Non-violence is the highest humility. Non-violence is the highest meditation."

**3** "All breathing, existing, living, sentient creatures should not be slain, nor treated with violence, nor abused, nor tormented, nor driven away. This is the pure, eternal, unchangeable law, which the wise ones who know the world have proclaimed."

**4** "A man should wander about treating all creatures in the world so as he himself would be treated. The suffering that would be caused to oneself should not be caused to any other being."

**5** "Violence is done not only by one's own hand. It is also done by ordering others to commit violence, and by approving of violence committed by others. All three are equally sinful."

**6** "Violence is of three kinds: physical violence, violence by speech, and mental violence. Physical violence includes killing, injuring, and binding. Violence by speech includes harsh words, slander, and falsehood. Mental violence includes ill-will, contempt, and malice."

**7** "He who is unmindful while walking may crush insects and small creatures beneath his feet. He who is unmindful while speaking may utter words that wound others. He who is unmindful in thought may harbour ill-will toward others. Therefore mindfulness is the root of non-violence."

**8** "The ascetic who observes the great vow of non-violence must be careful in walking, careful in speaking, careful in eating, careful in placing and removing things, and careful in disposing of waste."

**9** "Even the intention to cause harm is violence. Even wishing ill upon another being is violence. The wise one guards not only his body and speech but also his mind from all forms of violence."

**10** "Some say: 'Plants have no consciousness, therefore killing them is not violence.' But the Blessed One has taught that plants too have souls—they are one-sensed beings, possessing the sense of touch. To harm them unnecessarily is also violence."

**11** "Others say: 'Violence is permitted for the sake of sacrifice.' But the Blessed One has taught that no sacrifice that involves the killing of living beings can be righteous. The true sacrifice is the sacrifice of one's passions."

**12** "He who has conquered his own passions has conquered the world. He who lives without causing harm to any being—he is the true hero, the true warrior, the true conqueror. This is the teaching of the Jinas."
