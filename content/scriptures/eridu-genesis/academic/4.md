The fourth section describes the divine assembly's decision to send the flood. The text is heavily damaged at this point, and the gods' specific motivation for destroying humanity remains unclear. The Atrahasis epic, which elaborates the same tradition, identifies human noise (*huburu*) as the cause — humans have become too numerous and too loud, disturbing the gods' rest. Whether the Eridu Genesis shared this rationale cannot be confirmed from the surviving text.

The divine council (*puhrum*) is a well-attested institution in Mesopotamian religious thought. Decisions of cosmic significance — including the flood — are presented as collective judgments rather than unilateral decrees. The roles of specific deities (Anu, Enlil, Enki) in the deliberation have been reconstructed primarily from the parallel Atrahasis tradition.
