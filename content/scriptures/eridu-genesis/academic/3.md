The third section covers the antediluvian period — the era between the founding of cities and the flood. This section is poorly preserved, but the Sumerian King List provides a parallel tradition of antediluvian kings who reigned for extraordinary periods (tens of thousands of years each). Scholars have debated the significance of these impossible reign lengths: Jacobsen suggested they reflect a belief in a qualitatively different era before the flood, while others have proposed numerical symbolism or corruption of the original figures.

The antediluvian king tradition connects the Eridu Genesis to the broader literary-historical framework of Mesopotamian chronography. The ten antediluvian kings of the Sumerian King List have been compared to the ten pre-flood patriarchs of Genesis 5, suggesting a shared literary convention if not direct borrowing.
