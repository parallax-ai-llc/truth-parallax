The fifth section narrates the core flood episode: Ziusudra is warned of the coming deluge and survives. The text is badly damaged at the crucial moment of warning, but the Atrahasis parallel suggests that Enki (who had sworn not to reveal the council's decision) communicates the warning indirectly — perhaps speaking to a reed wall rather than to Ziusudra directly.

Ziusudra's name means 'life of long days' or 'he who found long life,' connecting him to the Akkadian Atrahasis ('exceedingly wise') and Utnapishtim ('he found life') — all epithets rather than personal names, suggesting a shared tradition of the flood survivor as an archetypal figure. The Berossus tradition (3rd century BCE) preserves the name as Xisouthros.
