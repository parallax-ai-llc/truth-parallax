The creation of humanity — why we exist and what we are made of — is perhaps the most universal question addressed by religious traditions. The Eridu Genesis offers one answer (humans exist to serve the gods), which can be set in dialogue with others:

- **Judaism**: Humans are created in God's image (*tzelem Elohim*) to be partners in creation
- **Christianity**: Humanity is created for relationship with God and stewardship of creation
- **Islam**: Humans are God's vicegerents (*khalifah*) on earth, entrusted with responsibility
- **Hinduism**: The Purusha Sukta presents creation as cosmic sacrifice; humans arise from the divine body
- **Buddhism**: The question of origins is set aside in favor of addressing the reality of suffering

The diversity of these answers reflects not contradiction but the richness of human reflection on the deepest question of all.
