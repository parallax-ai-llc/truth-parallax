The concept of an antediluvian age — a period of primordial civilization before a catastrophic break — resonates across religious traditions:

- **Judaism**: The pre-flood patriarchs (Genesis 5) live for hundreds of years, suggesting a more vital connection to God
- **Christianity**: The pre-fall state of Eden and the pre-flood world represent stages of progressive decline
- **Hinduism**: The four *yugas* describe a cyclical decline from the perfect *Satya Yuga*
- **Buddhism**: The concept of declining *dharma* ages (*mappo* in Japanese Buddhism)
- **Islam**: The pre-flood prophets as figures of primordial purity

Across traditions, the memory of a better past serves as both a lament for what was lost and a hope for what might be restored.
