The flood survivor — the one righteous person preserved from universal destruction — is a figure who transcends cultural boundaries:

- **Judaism**: Noah's righteousness in a corrupt generation (Genesis 6:9) — the principle that one righteous person can be the seed of renewal
- **Christianity**: Noah as a type of Christ — the one through whom creation is saved; the ark as a type of the Church
- **Islam**: Nuh as one of the great prophets, whose patience and faithfulness model the prophetic vocation
- **Hinduism**: Manu's rescue by Vishnu in the form of a fish — divine grace manifested through divine initiative

Across traditions, the flood survivor embodies the hope that catastrophe is not the final word — that faithfulness, however isolated, can preserve the future.
