The reward of the flood hero — Ziusudra's deification and placement in paradise — raises questions about the relationship between human merit and divine grace that every tradition addresses:

- **Judaism**: Noah receives a covenant but remains mortal; the concept of *olam ha-ba* (the world to come) develops later
- **Christianity**: The promise of resurrection and eternal life through Christ — not earned by merit alone but granted through grace
- **Islam**: Paradise (*Jannah*) as the reward of the faithful, described in vivid concrete terms (Quran 47:15)
- **Buddhism**: Nirvana as the cessation of suffering — not a place but a state of liberation
- **Hinduism**: *Moksha* as release from the cycle of rebirth — union with or proximity to the divine

The Eridu Genesis contributes the earliest known literary expression of the hope that transcends death: the hope that extraordinary faithfulness can lead to a life beyond this life.
