Dilmun, where Ziusudra receives eternal life, has been identified with the island of Bahrain and the adjacent Arabian coast. Archaeological excavations on Bahrain have revealed a sophisticated Bronze Age civilization with extensive burial mounds (some 170,000 tumuli), temple complexes, and evidence of the freshwater springs that the literary tradition associates with Dilmun's paradisiacal character.

The Barbar Temple complex on Bahrain has been interpreted as a temple to Enki, the god most closely associated with Dilmun in Sumerian tradition and the likely deity who warned Ziusudra of the flood. Trade contacts between Dilmun, Mesopotamia, and the Indus Valley are well documented archaeologically, confirming that the Sumerian literary tradition's knowledge of Dilmun was grounded in real commercial and cultural contact.
