The archaeological evidence for a historical flood in Mesopotamia has been debated since Leonard Woolley's discovery of a flood deposit at Ur in 1929. Similar deposits have been identified at Shuruppak (Tell Fara) and Kish, though they date to different periods and represent local flooding events rather than a single catastrophic deluge.

The most significant flood deposit at Shuruppak dates to approximately 2900 BCE (Early Dynastic I period). Since Shuruppak is named in the text as one of the antediluvian cities and is the home of the flood hero in the Gilgamesh tradition, some scholars have suggested that a particularly severe flood at Shuruppak may have provided the historical kernel for the flood tradition.
