The Eridu Genesis is preserved on tablet CBS 10673, excavated at Nippur in the 1890s by the University of Pennsylvania expedition. The tablet's archaeological context — the scribal quarter of Nippur, where thousands of literary, lexical, and administrative texts were found — indicates that the text was part of the scribal curriculum. No other copies of the Eridu Genesis have been identified, making CBS 10673 uniquely important.

The city of Eridu, which the text identifies as the first city, has been extensively excavated. The site (Tell Abu Shahrain, southern Iraq) reveals a continuous sequence of temple foundations dating back to the Ubaid period (c. 5400 BCE), providing archaeological confirmation of Eridu's extreme antiquity and religious importance.
