The antediluvian king tradition is preserved not only in the Eridu Genesis but in multiple copies of the Sumerian King List, the most important being the Weld-Blundell Prism (Ashmolean Museum, Oxford). This prism, dating to the Isin-Larsa period, lists antediluvian kings and their reign lengths before noting: 'Then the flood swept over.'

The historical reality behind the antediluvian traditions remains debated. The cities named are real sites, and the Early Dynastic period (c. 2900–2350 BCE) provides a plausible historical context for the emergence of these traditions. Some scholars have connected the tradition of a pre-flood civilization with archaeological evidence of Ubaid-period settlement across southern Mesopotamia.
