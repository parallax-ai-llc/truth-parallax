The five cities named in the Eridu Genesis are all real archaeological sites in southern Mesopotamia. Eridu (Tell Abu Shahrain) has been excavated most extensively, revealing the sequence of eighteen superimposed temples that confirms its exceptional antiquity. Bad-tibira (Tell al-Madineh), Larak (Tell al-Wilayah), Sippar (Tell Abu Habbah), and Shuruppak (Tell Fara) have all been identified and partially excavated.

Shuruppak is of particular interest as the home of Ziusudra, the flood hero. Excavations at Tell Fara revealed a significant flood deposit (a layer of water-laid silt) separating Early Dynastic occupation levels, which some scholars have connected to the historical memory behind the flood tradition.
