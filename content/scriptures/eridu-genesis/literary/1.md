The opening section of the Eridu Genesis employs the 'when not yet' (*ud re-a*) formula that characterizes Sumerian cosmogonic literature. This literary device establishes a primordial setting by negation — describing what did not yet exist — before introducing the positive act of creation. The technique creates a sense of emptiness that the narrative then fills, a literary strategy also found in Genesis 1:2 ('the earth was without form and void') and the Enuma Elish ('When on high the heaven had not been named').

Despite the fragmentary condition of the text, the surviving lines reveal a structured progression from cosmic conditions to the specific act of human creation, suggesting a deliberate literary architecture that moves from the general to the particular.
