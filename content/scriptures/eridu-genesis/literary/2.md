The catalogue of the five antediluvian cities functions as both a literary and a historical device. The list format — city name, patron deity, assignment of kingship — creates a rhythm of establishment and order that contrasts with the chaos of the flood to come. This literary contrast between ordered creation and catastrophic destruction is a structural principle shared with the Atrahasis and the biblical primeval history.

The city catalogue also serves a legitimizing function: by naming specific cities and their divine patrons, the text anchors cosmic mythology in geographic reality, blending the genres of myth and chronicle.
