The divine council scene serves as the narrative's turning point, transforming the story from a tale of origins and civilization into a tale of crisis and survival. The council's decree introduces dramatic tension: the audience knows what is coming, and the question becomes not whether the flood will happen but whether anyone will survive.

The council scene is a literary convention found across Mesopotamian mythological texts (Atrahasis, Enuma Elish, the Baal Cycle). Its function is to locate cosmic events within a framework of deliberation and decision, presenting catastrophe as the result of a process rather than a whim.
