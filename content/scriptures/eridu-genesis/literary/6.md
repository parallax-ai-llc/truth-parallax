The final section brings the narrative full circle, from creation through civilization through destruction to a new beginning. The literary structure of the Eridu Genesis is thus revealed as a pattern of establishment-crisis-resolution that becomes foundational to subsequent literature: the Atrahasis, the Gilgamesh flood narrative, and the biblical primeval history all follow the same arc.

The image of Ziusudra in Dilmun — the single survivor of the old world, now dwelling in paradise — serves as both an ending and a bridge. The story of the old world is complete, but the story of the new world (our world) is just beginning. This technique of ending a cosmic narrative with a figure who connects the mythic past to the present is a sophisticated literary strategy found across ancient traditions.
