The antediluvian king list functions as a literary bridge between the creation of civilization and the flood. By establishing a period of stable, divinely ordered kingship, the text creates the conditions for the flood's dramatic impact: what is destroyed is not chaos but order; what is lost is not nothing but civilization itself.

The enormous reign lengths serve a literary purpose beyond historical memory: they stretch time to create a sense of deep antiquity, establishing the pre-flood world as qualitatively different from the present — a technique also used in the genealogies of Genesis 5.
