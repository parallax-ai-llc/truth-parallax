The flood narrative is the text's climactic set piece. Despite the tablet's damaged condition, the surviving fragments convey the catastrophic scale of the event: 'all the windstorms, exceedingly powerful, attacked as one' and 'the flood sweeps over the cult-centers.' The contrast between the orderly world established in the first sections and the overwhelming chaos of the flood creates the poem's primary dramatic arc.

Ziusudra emerges as a literary figure of contrast — the single point of order amid universal chaos, the one who survives because of his relationship to the divine. This narrative function — the righteous survivor who bridges the old world and the new — becomes one of the most enduring character types in world literature.
