The divine council's decision to destroy humanity appears across flood traditions in varied forms:

- **Atrahasis**: The gods send the flood because humanity's noise prevents them from sleeping
- **Genesis**: God sends the flood because human wickedness is great and 'every inclination of the thoughts of the human heart was only evil continually' (6:5)
- **Gilgamesh XI**: The flood is sent, but Ea/Enki later criticizes its indiscriminate nature
- **Greek (Deucalion)**: Zeus sends the flood because of human impiety

The contrast between the Mesopotamian reason (noise) and the biblical reason (moral corruption) reveals fundamentally different theological frameworks — one focused on divine comfort, the other on divine justice.
