The antediluvian period — a golden age of extraordinary lifespans before the flood — is a shared motif across ancient Near Eastern traditions. The ten antediluvian kings of the Sumerian King List parallel the ten pre-flood patriarchs of Genesis 5 (Adam through Noah). Both traditions assign supernatural lifespans to these figures, though the Sumerian numbers are vastly larger.

The concept of a pre-flood golden age appears in other traditions as well: the Greek myth of the Golden Age under Kronos, the Hindu concept of the *Satya Yuga* (age of truth) as the first and longest of the four cosmic ages, and the Zoroastrian tradition of Yima's kingdom as a period of perfection before corruption.
