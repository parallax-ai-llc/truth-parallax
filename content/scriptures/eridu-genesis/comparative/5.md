Ziusudra's survival of the flood places him in a worldwide tradition of flood survivors:

| Tradition | Survivor | Warning Source | Vessel | Outcome |
|-----------|----------|---------------|--------|----------|
| Sumerian | Ziusudra | God (Enki?) | Boat | Deification |
| Akkadian | Atrahasis/Utnapishtim | Enki via reed wall | Boat | Immortality |
| Biblical | Noah | God (YHWH) | Ark | Covenant |
| Greek | Deucalion | Prometheus | Chest | Repopulation |
| Hindu | Manu | Vishnu as fish | Boat | New creation |

The consistency of the pattern — divine warning, vessel construction, survival, post-flood favor — across independent traditions has generated extensive scholarly discussion about whether the motif reflects shared cultural memory, diffusion from a single source, or universal experience of catastrophic flooding.
