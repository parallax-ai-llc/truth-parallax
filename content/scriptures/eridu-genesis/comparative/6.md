The deification of Ziusudra finds direct parallels in the Akkadian tradition, where Utnapishtim receives immortality from the gods and dwells in a remote paradise. The biblical tradition diverges: Noah receives a covenant (Genesis 9:8-17) but remains mortal. The Greek tradition splits the difference: Deucalion repopulates the earth but does not receive immortality.

The concept of a human achieving divine status through extraordinary piety or service appears across traditions:
- **Chinese**: The sage kings who achieve heavenly status
- **Hindu**: Mortals who achieve *moksha* or divine status through extreme devotion
- **Greek**: Heracles achieves apotheosis through his labors
- **Christianity**: The Ascension of Christ and the promise of theosis (Eastern Orthodox tradition)
