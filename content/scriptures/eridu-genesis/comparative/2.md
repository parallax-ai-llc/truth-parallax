The founding of the first cities in the Eridu Genesis represents a theology of civilization — the idea that urban life is not a human invention but a divine establishment. This concept invites comparison with other traditions:

- **Biblical**: Cain builds the first city (Genesis 4:17), but in a context of exile and violence — a strikingly negative view of urbanization
- **Greek**: Athens is founded by the goddess Athena; Rome by Romulus under divine auspices
- **Chinese**: The sage kings (Yao, Shun, Yu) establish civilization under the Mandate of Heaven
- **Hindu**: Cities are established by divine kings in the *Puranas*

The contrast between the Sumerian tradition (cities as divine gift) and the biblical tradition (the first city built by a murderer) reveals fundamentally different attitudes toward urban civilization.
