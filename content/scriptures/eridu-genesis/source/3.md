**Section III: The Divine Decision**

**1** In those days the people multiplied;
**2** the land bellowed like wild oxen.
**3** The god was disturbed by their commotion.
**4** Enlil heard their noise.

**5** He spoke to the great gods:
**6** "The noise of mankind has become too great.
**7** With their noise they prevent my sleep.
**8** Let food be cut off from the people.

**9** In their bellies, let the greens be too few.
**10** Above, let Adad make his rain scarce.
**11** Below, let the flood water not rise from its source.
**12** Let the wind blow and parch the ground.

**13** Let the clouds thicken but not produce rain.
**14** Let the field diminish its yield.
**15** Let there be no joy."

**16** [Gap in text]

**17** The people did not diminish;
**18** they were more numerous than before.

**19** Again the god was disturbed by their noise.
**20** Enlil heard their clamour
**21** and said to the great gods:
**22** "The noise of mankind has become too great.
**23** With their noise they prevent my sleep."

**24** [Gap in text]

**25** The great gods decided upon the flood.
**26** They swore an oath among themselves:
**27** An swore, Enlil swore,
**28** Ninkhursag swore, Enki swore.

**29** But Enki, god of wisdom and sweet waters,
**30** was troubled in his heart.
**31** He did not reveal the decision of the assembly,
**32** but he devised a plan.

**33** He spoke not to any man,
**34** but he spoke to the reed wall:
**35** "Reed wall, reed wall! Wall, wall!
**36** Reed wall, hear my words!
**37** Wall, heed my speech!

**38** Man of Shuruppak, son of Ubara-Tutu,
**39** build a house, construct a boat!
**40** Forsake possessions, save life!
**41** Cause the living creatures to go up into the boat."
