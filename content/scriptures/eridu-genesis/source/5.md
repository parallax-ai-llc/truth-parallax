**Section V: The Flood**

**1** All the windstorms, exceedingly powerful, attacked as one.
**2** The flood swept over the cult centres.
**3** For seven days and seven nights
**4** the flood swept over the land.

**5** The huge boat was tossed about
**6** by the windstorms on the great waters.

**7** Then Utu came forth,
**8** who sheds light on heaven and earth.

**9** Ziusudra opened a window of the huge boat.
**10** Utu, the hero, brought his rays into the giant boat.

**11** Ziusudra, the king,
**12** prostrated himself before Utu.
**13** The king slaughtered an ox, slaughtered a sheep.

**14** [Gap in text]

**15** The storm ceased.
**16** The flood was over.
**17** The great waters receded.
**18** Ziusudra looked upon the land.

**19** Silence had set in.
**20** All mankind had returned to clay.
**21** The landscape was as level as a flat roof.

**22** Ziusudra opened a vent.
**23** Light fell upon his face.
**24** He bowed low and sat, weeping.
**25** Tears ran down his face.

**26** He looked out in all directions.
**27** In the expanse of the sea, there was nothing.
**28** No land was visible.

**29** [Gap in text]

**30** Then a mountain emerged.
**31** On the mountain the boat came to rest.
**32** Ziusudra stepped onto the mountain.

**33** He set out offerings in all directions.
**34** He sacrificed an ox and a sheep.
**35** The sweet savour rose to heaven.
**36** The gods smelled the savour.
