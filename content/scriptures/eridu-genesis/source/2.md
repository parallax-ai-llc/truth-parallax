**Section II: Kingship and Civilization**

**1** After kingship had descended from heaven,
**2** after the exalted crown and the throne of kingship
**3** had descended from heaven,
**4** he perfected the rites and the exalted divine powers.

**5** He founded the five cities in pure places,
**6** called their names, apportioned them as cult centres.

**7** The first of these cities, Eridu, he gave to Nudimmud, the leader.
**8** The second, Badtibira, he gave to the Prince and the Sacred One.
**9** The third, Larak, he gave to Endurbilhursag.
**10** The fourth, Sippar, he gave to the hero Utu.
**11** The fifth, Shuruppak, he gave to the Lady of Life.

**12** After the names of these cities had been called
**13** and the allotments of the divine powers had been fixed—

**14** the river, the—
**15** the people set up their dwellings
**16** in the lowlands of the plain.

**17** [Gap in text]

**18** At that time Enki, who dwells in Eridu,
**19** the god of sweet waters and wisdom,
**20** at that time did Enki establish the arts of civilization:

**21** The art of the scribe, the art of the smith,
**22** the art of the builder, the art of the weaver,
**23** the art of the farmer, the art of the shepherd—
**24** all the arts he brought into being.

**25** Kingship was in Eridu.
**26** In Eridu, Alulim became king; he ruled 28,800 years.
**27** Alalgar ruled 36,000 years.
**28** Two kings ruled 64,800 years.

**29** Eridu was abandoned;
**30** its kingship was carried to Badtibira.
**31** In Badtibira, Enmenluanna ruled 43,200 years.
**32** Enmengalanna ruled 28,800 years.
**33** The divine Dumuzi, the shepherd, ruled 36,000 years.

**34** Three kings ruled 108,000 years.
**35** Badtibira was abandoned;
**36** its kingship was carried to Larak.

**37** In Larak, Ensipadzidanna ruled 28,800 years.
**38** One king ruled 28,800 years.
**39** Larak was abandoned;
**40** its kingship was carried to Sippar.

**41** In Sippar, Enmeduranna became king; he ruled 21,000 years.
**42** One king ruled 21,000 years.
**43** Sippar was abandoned;
**44** its kingship was carried to Shuruppak.

**45** In Shuruppak, Ubara-Tutu became king; he ruled 18,600 years.
**46** One king ruled 18,600 years.

**47** Five cities: eight kings ruled 241,200 years.
**48** Then the flood swept over.
