**Section IV: Ziusudra Builds the Boat**

**1** Ziusudra, being king, being a priest of—
**2** fashioned, being humble and obedient,
**3** reverently he—

**4** attending daily, constantly he—
**5** bringing forth all kinds of dreams, he—
**6** uttering the name of heaven and earth, he—

**7** [Gap in text]

**8** Ziusudra, standing at its side, listened.
**9** "Stand by the wall at my left side!
**10** At the wall I will say a word to you—
**11** take my word!

**12** Give ear to my instructions:
**13** By our hand a rainstorm—
**14** will be sent upon the land.
**15** It will destroy the seed of mankind.

**16** This is the decision,
**17** the word of the assembly of the gods,
**18** the command of An and Enlil.
**19** Its rule, its reign—

**20** A flood will sweep over the cult centres.
**21** To destroy the seed of mankind
**22** is the decision, the word of the assembly of the gods."

**23** [Gap in text—Ziusudra builds the boat]

**24** All the windstorms, exceedingly powerful, attacked as one.
**25** At the same time, the flood swept over the cult centres.

**26** After, for seven days and seven nights,
**27** the flood had swept over the land,
**28** and the huge boat had been tossed about
**29** by the windstorms on the great waters,

**30** Utu came forth, who sheds light on heaven and earth.
**31** Ziusudra opened a window of the huge boat.

**32** The hero Utu brought his rays into the giant boat.
**33** Ziusudra, being king,
**34** prostrated himself before Utu,
**35** the king slaughtered an ox, slaughtered a sheep.
