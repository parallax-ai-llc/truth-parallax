**Section I: The Creation of Man**

[The Eridu Genesis, also known as the Sumerian Creation Myth, is preserved on a fragmentary tablet. The opening is largely destroyed.]

**1** When An, Enlil, Enki, and Ninhursag
**2** had fashioned the dark-headed people,
**3** vegetation luxuriated from the earth,
**4** animals, four-legged creatures of the plain,
**5** were artfully brought into existence.

**6** [Gap in text—approximately 36 lines missing]

**7** After the kingship had descended from heaven,
**8** after the exalted crown and throne of kingship
**9** had descended from heaven,
**10** the divine rites and the exalted powers were perfected,
**11** the bricks of the cities were laid in holy places,

**12** their names were called and the allotments were distributed.

**13** The first of the cities, Eridu, was given to the leader Nudimmud.
**14** The second, Badtibira, was given to the Nugig.
**15** The third, Larak, was given to Pabilsag.
**16** The fourth, Sippar, was given to the hero Utu.
**17** The fifth, Shuruppak, was given to Sud.

**18** When these cities had been called by name
**19** and the allotments had been distributed,

**20** the people dug the canals—
**21** the pure canals.
**22** The people established the clean basins
**23** alongside the canals—
**24** the pure canals.

**25** The people established irrigation
**26** and the plough worked the land,
**27** the land brought forth the grain.
