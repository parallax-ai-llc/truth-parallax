## Overview

The Eridu Genesis raises fundamental theological questions about human origins, divine purpose, and the meaning of catastrophic destruction. Though fragmentary, the text presents a coherent theological vision: humanity is created for a purpose (to serve the gods), civilization is divinely ordered, and yet the divine assembly retains the power to unmake what it has created. The survival of Ziusudra introduces the theme of divine mercy tempering divine judgment.

## Key Theological Themes

### The Purpose of Human Creation
Unlike the biblical account in which humanity is created in God's image, the Eridu Genesis presents human creation in functional terms: humans exist to serve the gods, to build their temples, and to maintain the cult. This utilitarian theology of creation is consistent across Mesopotamian tradition.

### Kingship as Divine Order
The descent of kingship from heaven (*nam-lugal an-ta ed-de-a-ba*) establishes political authority as part of the cosmic order. The founding of the first five cities represents not merely human achievement but divine planning — each city assigned to a patron deity.

### The Theology of the Flood
The divine assembly's decision to destroy humanity raises the question of theodicy: why destroy what you have created? The fragmentary state of the text makes the gods' motivation unclear, but the Atrahasis tradition (which elaborates the same story) suggests that human noise or overpopulation disturbed the gods.

### Divine Mercy
Ziusudra's survival implies that righteousness can avert total destruction — a theological principle that echoes across traditions from Abraham's intercession for Sodom to the Buddhist bodhisattva ideal.

## Theological Concepts

| Concept | Expression in the Text |
|---------|----------------------|
| Purposeful creation | Humanity created to serve the gods |
| Cosmic order | Cities and kingship established by divine decree |
| Divine sovereignty | The assembly can decree destruction |
| Mercy within judgment | One righteous figure survives |
| Deification | Ziusudra's reward suggests human transcendence is possible |
