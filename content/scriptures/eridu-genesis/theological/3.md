The antediluvian period represents a theological concept of primordial civilization — a time when the connection between gods and humans was more direct, kings reigned for millennia, and the world operated under different conditions than the present age. This theology of decline — from a better past to a diminished present — is a recurring pattern across traditions.

The extraordinary lifespans of the antediluvian kings suggest that proximity to the divine origin conferred vitality and longevity. The flood functions as a cosmic boundary: after it, lifespans shorten, the divine-human connection weakens, and the world takes on its familiar form.
