The descent of kingship from heaven — *nam-lugal an-ta ed-de-a-ba* ('when kingship had descended from heaven') — is one of the most important theological statements in Sumerian literature. Kingship is not a human invention or a social contract; it is a divine institution, part of the cosmic order. Each city receives not only a human king but a divine patron, establishing a parallel governance between heaven and earth.

This theology of divinely ordained political authority has parallels across traditions — from the biblical anointing of kings to the Chinese Mandate of Heaven to the Hindu concept of *rajadharma* (the king's sacred duty). The Eridu Genesis locates this theology at the very beginning of human history.
